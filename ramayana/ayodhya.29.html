Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 29
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha implores upon Rama to allow her to come to forests, and says that she heard through some foretellers that she has to live in forests for some time, when she was with her father's place. But Rama is not interested to make Seetha's life penurious in forests and once again tries to pacify her. He asks her to stay back at Ayodhya. For that Seetha laments to become lonely parted with her husband.

Sloka 1

etat tu vacanam śrutvā sītā rāmasya duhkhitā |
prasakta aśru mukhī mandam idam vacanam abravīt || 2-29-1

1. sīta = ṣeetha; shrutvātu = heard; etat = these; vachanam = words; rāmasya = of Rama; duḥkhitā = distressed; prasaktāshrumukhī = having the face wet with tears; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; mandam = slowly.

Seetha was distressed to hear these words of Rama and spoke these words slowly, with her face with tears.

Sloka 2

ye tvayā kīrtitā doṣā vane vastavyatām prati |
guṇān iti eva tān viddhi tava sneha puraḥ kṛtān || 2-29-2

2. viddhi = know; ye = which; doshhāḥ = disadvantages; kīrtitāḥ = mentioned; tvayā = by you; vastavyatām prati = about dwelling; vane = in the forest; tān = those; guṇānityeva = to be advantages; tava sneha puraskṛitān = if your affection is placed before them.

"know all those disadvantages you mentioned about dwelling in the forest become advantages to me, if your affection is placed before them."

Sloka 3

mṛgāḥ simhā gajāścaiva śārdūlāḥ śarabhāstathā |
pakṣiṇaḥ sṛmarāścaiva ye cānye vanacāriṇaḥ || 2-29-3
adṛṣṭapūrvarūpatvātsarve te tava rāghava |
rūpam dṛṣṭvāpasarpeyurbhaye sarve hi bibhyati || 2-29-4

3;4. rāghava = Oh; Rama! mṛigāḥ = Antelopes; simhāḥ = lions; gaṇāshchaiva = elephants; shārdūlāḥ = tigers; tathā = and; sharabhāḥ = sarabhas(a legendary animal with eight legs and said to be more powerful than the lion); pakshhiṇaḥ = birds; sṛīmarāshchaiva = yaks; te sarve = all those; anye = others; ye = which; vanachāriṇaḥ = roam in the forest; apasarpcyuḥ = run away; dṛishhṭvaiva = after seeing; rūpam = your form; tava adṛishhṭapūrva rūpatvāt = since they have never seen your figure; before; bhaye = when there is cause for fear; sarve = all; bibhyati hi = would have indeed fear.

"Oh Rama! Antelopes, lions, elephants, tigers, sarabhas(legendary animal with eight legs), birds, yaks and all others which roam in the forest, run away after seeing your form, since they have never seen your figure before. When there is cause for fear, who would not have fear?"

Sloka 5

tvayā ca saha gantavyam mayā guru jana ājñayā |
tvad viyogena me rāma tyaktavyam iha jīvitam || 2-29-5

5. rāma = Oh; Rama! gurujanāJṇjayā = as per the command of the elders; mayā cha = I also; gantavyam = should go; tvayāsaha = along with you; tvadviyogena = by separation from you; jīvitam = life; me = to me; tyaktavyam = is to be abandoned; iha = here.

"Oh, Rama! As per the command of the elders, I also should go along with you. ṃy life is to be abandoned here, if I were separated from you."

Sloka 6

na ca mām tvat samīpastham api śaknoti rāghava |
surāṇām īśvaraḥ śakraḥ pradharṣayitum ojasā || 2-29-6

6. rāghava = Oh; Rama! shakraḥ api = even Devendra; īshvaraḥ = who is the lord; surāṇām = of celestials; na shaknoti = cannot; ojasā = by his strength; pradarshhiyitum = over power; mām = me; tvatsamīpasthām = who is in your vicinity.

"Oh Rama! Even Devendra the Lord of celestials, by his strength, cannot over power me, who is in your vicinity."

Sloka 7

pati hīnā tu yā nārī na sā śakṣyati jīvitum |
kāmam evam vidham rāma tvayā mama vidarśitam || 2-29-7

7. yā nārī = which woman; patihīnā = is without husband; sā = she; na shakshhyati = should not be able; jīvitum = to live; rāma = Oh; Rama! kāmam = verily; evam vidham = such a thing; vidarshitam = was taught; mama = to me; tvayā = by you.

"Oh Rama! Verily such a thing was taught to me by you, that a woman disunited from her husband should not be able to survive."

Sloka 8

atha ca api mahā prājña brāhmaṇānām mayā śrutam |
purā pitṛ gṛhe satyam vastavyam kila me vane || 2-29-8

8. mahā prāJṇja = Oh; the very wide man! atha chāpi = besides; purā = formerly; pitṛigṛihe = in my father's house; brāhmaṇānām = brahmana's word; shrutam = was heard; mayā = by me; vastavyam kila = to be stayed; satyam = really; vane = in forest; me = to me.

"Oh Rama, the very wise! Besides, formerly in my father's house it was heard by me from the month of brahmanas that I was destined to stay really in a forest."

Sloka 9

lakṣaṇibhyo dvijātibhyaḥ śrutvā aham vacanam gṛhe |
vana vāsa kṛta utsāhā nityam eva mahā bala || 2-29-9

9. mahābala = Oh; man of great strength! aham = I; shrutvā = after hearning; purā = formerly vachanam = the word; dvijātibhyaḥ = from brahmanas; lakshhaṇibhyaḥ = who can interpret marks on the boday; nityameva = always; vanavāsakṛitotsāhā = I was enthusiastic to live in a forest.

"Oh Rama the man of great strength! Ever since I heard this word from brahmanas who can interpret marks on the body, I was always eager to stay in a forest."

Sloka 10

ādeśo vana vāsasya prāptavyaḥ sa mayā kila |
sā tvayā saha tatra aham yāsyāmi priya na anyathā || 2-29-10

10. priya = Oh; Rama the beloved! saḥ ādeshaḥ = that prediction; vanavāsasya = of dwelling in the forst prāptavyaḥ kila = is to be realised by me; aham = I sā = as such; yāsyāmi = can go; tatra = to forest; tvayā saha = along with you; na = not; anyathā = otherwise.

"Oh, Rama the beloved! That prediction of dwelling in the forst is to be realised by me. I must accordingly accompany you to the forest, it cannot be otherwise."

Sloka 11

kṛta ādeśā bhaviṣyāmi gamiṣyāmi saha tvayā |
kālaḥ ca ayam samutpannaḥ satya vāg bhavatu dvijaḥ || 2-29-11

11. bhavishhyāmi = I shall become; kṛitādeshā = the one who has fulfilled brahmana's word; gamishhyāmi = I shall come; tvayā saha = along with you; ayam = this; kālashcha = time also; samntpannaḥ = has come; bhavatn dvijaḥ = let brahmana become; bhavatu dvijaḥ = let brahmana become; satyavāk = true to his word.

"I shall be the one who has fulfilled the brahmana's word. I shall accompany you to the forest. The time has come for brahmana's words to come true."

Sloka 12

vana vāse hi jānāmi duhkhāni bahudhā kila |
prāpyante niyatam vīra puruṣaiḥ akṛta ātmabhiḥ || 2-29-12

12. vīra = Oh; valiant prince! abhijānāmi = I know; vanavāse = that in the forest; duḥkhāni = sufferings; bahudhākila = are many indeed; niyatam = certainly; prāpyante = be obtained; purushhaiḥ = by men; akṛitātmabhiḥ = having unformed minds.

"Oh valiant prince! I know that in the forest, sufferings indeed are many and are invariably obtained by men having unformed minds."

Sloka 13

kanyayā ca pitur gehe vana vāsaḥ śrutaḥ mayā
bhikṣiṇyāḥ sādhu vṛttāyā mama mātur iha agrataḥ || 2-29-13

13. mayā = by me; kanyayā = who was unmarried girl; pituḥ gehe = in my father's horse; vanavāsaḥ = dwelling in a forest; iha = now; shrutaḥ = was heard; bhikshhiṇyāḥ = from a female medicant; sādhuvṛittāyāḥ = who was having good manners; agvataḥ = in the presence; mama mātuḥ = of my mother

"While I was an unmarried girl, the prediction about my dwelling the forest was heard by me in my father's house from a female mendicant having good manners, in the presence of my mother."

Sloka 14

prasāditaḥ ca vai pūrvam tvam vai bahu vidham prabho |
gamanam vana vāsasya kānkṣitam hi saha tvayā || 2-29-14

14. prabho = Oh; Lord! pūrvam = In the past; tvam = you; prasādiashcha vai = have been pleased really; bahutitham = for many a time vanavāsasya gamanam = for the tour of a forest; kāṇkshhitam hi = as desire by me indeed; tvayā saha = with you.

"Oh Lord! In the past, you have been really pleased for many a time to take me along with you indeed for the tour of a forest, as longed by me."

Sloka 15

kṛta kṣaṇā aham bhadram te gamanam prati rāghava |
vana vāsasya śūrasya caryā hi mama rocate || 2-29-15

15. rāghava = Oh; Rama! bhadvam = many all be well; te = with you! aham = I kṛitakshhaṇā = am waiting; gamanam prati = for a journey; vanavāsasya = of the forest;charyā = a work-out; shūrasya = of adventurist; rochate hi = is really delightful mama = to me.

"Oh, Rama! ṃay all be well with you! I am waiting for a journey to the forest. A work-out of adventurist is really delightful to me."

Sloka 16

śuddha ātman prema bhāvādd hi bhaviṣyāmi vikalmaṣā |
bhartāram anugaccantī bhartā hi mama daivatam || 2-29-16

16. shuddhātman = Oh; the pure-minded! prema bhāvāt hi = by loving devotion; anugachchhantī = following; bhartāram = you; the husband; bhavishhyāmi = I shall become; vikalmashhā = sinless; mama = to me; bhartā = husband; daivatam hi = is supreme deity.

"Oh, the pure-minded! Following my husband with loving devotion, I shall become sin-less; for husband is the supreme deity to me."

Sloka 17

pretya bhāve api kalyāṇaḥ samgamaḥ me saha tvayā |
śrutir hi śrūyate puṇyā brāhmaṇānām yaśasvinām || 2-29-17
iha loke ca pitṛbhir yā strī yasya mahā mate |
adbhir dattā svadharmeṇa pretya bhāve api tasya sā || 2-29-18

17;18. pretya bhāve.api = even after death; kalyāṇaḥ = auspicions; saṇgamaḥ = union; me = to me; tvayā saha = with you; puṇyā = meritoins; shrutiḥ = words of Veda; brahmaṇām = by brahmanas; shrūyate hi = being heard indeed; mahāmate = Oh; the great minded! yā strī = which woman; iha loke = in this world; dattā = was given away; yasya = to who; adbhiḥ = with water; pitṛibhiḥ = by her parents; svadharmeṇa = according to the moral code; tasya = to him; sā = she(belongs) pretya bhāve.api = even after death.

"Even after my death, I shall stay united with you. On this subject, the meritorious words of brahmanas is being heard thus:- In this world, which woman was given away to whom by parents with water according to the moral code binding on them, that woman belongs to him alone even after death.

Sloka 19

evam asmāt svakām nārīm suvṛttām hi pati vratām |
na abhirocayase netum tvam mām kena iha hetunā || 2-29-19

19. kena hetunā = by what reason; iha = now; tvam = you; nābhirochayase = are not willing; evam = thus; netum = to take; asmāt = from this place; mām = me; suvṛittām = having good conduct; pativratām = devoted to her husband; svakām nārīm = the woman belonging to you.

"By what reason now are you thus not willing to take me, the woman having good conduct, devoted to her husband and belonging to you, from this place?"

Sloka 20

bhaktām pati vratām dīnām mām samām sukha duhkhayoh |
netum arhasi kākutstha samāna sukha duhkhinīm || 2-29-20

20. kākutthsa = Oh; the seion of Kakustha! arhasi = you ought to; netum = take; mām = me; bhaktām = who is a devotee; pativratām = devoted to husband; dīnām = who is distressed; samām = who is equal; sukhaduḥkhayoḥ = in pleasure and pain; samāna sukhaduḥkhanīm = sharing in your joys and sorrows.

"Oh Rama, the seion of Kakutsa! You ought to take me, who is a devotee, so devoted to husband, who is distressed who feels alike in pleasure and pain and shares your joys and sorrows."

Sloka 21

yadi mām duhkhitām evam vanam netum na ca iccasi |
viṣam agnim jalam vā aham āsthāsye mṛtyu kāraṇāt || 2-29-21

21. āsthāsye = I shall take resort to; vishham = poison or; agnim = fire or; jalamvā = water; mṛityukāraṇāt = for dying; nachaichchhasiyadi = if you are not willing; netum = to take; mām = me; duḥkhitām = afflicted as I am; evam = as above.

"I shall take resort to poison or fire or water for dying, if you are not willing to take me, afflicted as I am, as above."

Sloka 22

evam bahu vidham tam sā yācate gamanam prati |
na anumene mahā bāhus tām netum vijanam vanam || 2-29-22

22. sā = she; evam = thus yāchate = requested; tam = him; bahuvidham = in many ways; gamanam prati = about setting out(to the forst) mahābāhuḥ = Rama; the long armed; nāmnumane = did not agree; netum = to take; tām = her; vanam = to forest; vijanam = which is a solitary place.

Eventhough she requested him in many ways about setting out to the forest, he did not agree to take her to the forest, which is a solitary place.

Sloka 23

evam uktā tu sā cintām maithilī samupāgatā |
snāpayantī iva gām uṣṇaiḥ aśrubhir nayana cyutaiḥ || 2-29-23

23. sā maithili = that ṣeetha; uktā = who was spoken; evam = thus; samupāgatā = got; chintām = anguish; gām = with earth; snāpayantīva = as though wet; ushhṇaiḥashrubhiḥ = by burning tears; nayanachyntaiḥ = trickling down from her eyes.

After hearing Rama's words, seetha was sorrowful, moistening with earth as it were with burning tears, trickling down her eyes.

Sloka 24

cintayantīm tathā tām tu nivartayitum ātmavān |
tāmroṣṭhīṃ sa tadā sītām kākutstho bahv asāntvayat || 2-29-24

24. saḥ kakutthsaḥ = that Rama; ātmavān = the prudent man; tadā = then; asāntvayat = pacified; bahu = in many ways; nivartiyutum = to avert;tām sītām = that ṣeetha; tāmroshhṭhīm = with red lips; chintayantīm = who was sorrowful; tathā = as above.

Rama the prudent man then pacified in many ways, to avert that Seetha with red lips, who was sorrowful as above.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekona triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 29th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda in glorious Ramayana, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
