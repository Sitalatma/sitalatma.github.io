Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda

Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 56
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sampāti hears about Jatāyu's death when Angada cites it while lamenting for their misfortune. He praises that Jatāyu is better off than the vanara-s for he encountered Ravana and attained martyrdom. On listening his brother's name and news of his death, Sampāti, who firstly wanted to gluttonise all the vanara-s, seeks their help to lower him down from mountain heights, to listen more of his brother Jatāyu.


Sloka 1

upaviṣṭāḥ tu te sarve yasmin prāyam giri sthale |
harayo gṛdhra rājaḥ ca tam deśam upacakrame || 4-56-1
sāṃpātiḥ nāma nāmnā tu cira jīvī vihaṃgamaḥ |
bhrātā jaṭāyuṣaḥ śrīmān prakhyāta bala pauruṣaḥ || 4-56-2

1, 2. te sarve harayaḥ = those, all, monkeys; yasmin giri sthale = at which, mountain, tableland; prāyam upaviṣṭāḥ tu = self-immolation, sat down; tam desham = to that, area; cira jīvī = long, lived [sempiternal]; gṛidhra rājaḥ = eagle, kingly; nāmnā sāmpātiḥ nāma = by name, ṣampāti, known as; jaṭāyuṣaḥ bhrātā = Jatāyu's, brother; shrīmān = celebrated one; prakhyāta bala pauruṣaḥ = one renowned, for forcefulness, aggressiveness; vihamgamaḥ upacakrame = sky-flyer [eagle,] arrived there.

At which mountain's tableland those monkeys sat down for self-immolation, to that area the elder brother of Jatāyu, a kingly eagle known as Sampāti by name, one renowned one for his forcefulness and aggressiveness, and a celebrated and sempiternal sky-flying eagle has arrived. [4-56-1, 2]

Sloka 3

kaṃdarāt abhiniṣkramya sa vindhyasya mahāgireḥ |
upaviṣṭān harīn dṛṣṭvā hṛṣṭātmā giram abravīt || 4-56-3

3. saḥ = he [ṣampāti]; mahā gireḥ = from great-mountain; vindhyasya = of Vindhya; kandarāt = from cave; abhi niṣ kramya = on coming out; upaviṣṭān harīn dṛiṣṭvā = those who sat down, monkeys, on seeing; hṛiṣṭa atmā giram abravīt = gladdened, at heart, words, spoke.

On coming out of the cave of the great-mountain Vindhya, Sampāti is gladden at heart to see the monkeys sitting down there, and he spoke these words. [4-56-3]

Sloka 4

vidhiḥ kila naram loke vidhānena anuvartate |
yathā ayam vihito bhakṣyaḥ cirāt mahyam upāgataḥ || 4-56-4

4. loke = in world; vidhiḥ = fate / god; vidhānena = procedurally [fatefully]; naram = after human; anuvartate kila = follows, indeed; yathā = why because; cirāt = long-awaited; mahyam vihitaḥ = to me, fated; ayam bhakṣyaḥ upa āgataḥ = this, food, came nigh of me.

"Whether it is good or bad, fate indeed follows humans in this world procedurally, why because, this fated and long-waited food has now come nigh of me... [4-56-4]

Sloka 5

paraṃparāṇām bhakṣiṣye vānarāṇām mṛtam mṛtam |
uvāca etat vacaḥ pakṣī tān nirīkṣya plavaṃgamān || 4-56-5

5. vānarāṇām paramparāṇām = of vanara-s, in the series; mṛitam mṛitam = dead one, on dying - whoever is dead while fasting, or, whomever I kill sequentially; bhakṣiṣye = I wish to eat; tān plavamgamān nirīkṣya = them, fly-jumpers, on seeing; pakṣī = bird ṣampāti; etat vacaḥ uvāca = these, words spoke.

"I wish to eat them frugally, killing one by one and eating one by one, preying on one on one day, and then preying upon the other on the other day..." thus that bird spoke those words on seeing the massy fly-jumpers. [4-56-5]

▼

Here some translate this as 'I wish to eat the one by one as and when that monkey falls dead, then the next, on its falling dead...' In such a case, Angada would not have bothered or feared for this eagle, because it wants to vulture upon a cadaver. No need to fear if death occurs prior to their becoming prey to Sampāti. He feared to become the prey of eagle, whereby their sacred immolation is also going to fail along with other failures like searching Seetha, transgressing timeframe, hiding in Black Hole etc. Besides, a vulture is the most unpredictable bird, if it comes to it food.

Sloka 6

tasya tat vacanam śrutvā bhakṣa lubdhasya pakṣiṇaḥ |
aṃgadaḥ param āyasto hanūmaṃtam atha abravīt || 4-56-6

6. bhakṣa lubdhasya = food, greedy [gormandising]; tasya pakṣiṇaḥ tat vacanam shrutvā = that, bird's, that, word, on hearing; atha = then; angadaḥ param āyastaḥ = Angada, becoming highly, breathless; hanūmantam abravīt = to ḥanuma, spoke.

On hearing the words of that gormandising bird, Angada became highly breathless and then spoke to Hanuma. [4-56-6]

Sloka 7

paśya sītā - gṛdhrā - apadeśena sākṣāt vaivasvato yamaḥ |
imam deśam anuprāpto vānarāṇām vipattaye || 4-56-7

7. sītā apadeshena = ṣeetha, in the name of; [or, gṛidhrā apadeshena = eagle, in the name of]; vaivasvataḥ yamaḥ = ṣun's son, Yama, the ṭerminator; sākṣāt = manifestly; vānarāṇām vipattaye = for monkeys', fatality; imam desham anuprāptaḥ = to this, area, arrived; pashya = look.

"Look! In the name of an eagle, the son of the Sun, Yama, the Terminator, has manifestly arrived at this area for the fatality of monkeys... thus ill luck is still haunting us... [4-56-7]

▼

Some mms have the name of Seetha compared to Yama, saying that Yama come in the name of Seetha, while some say it is Sampāti. Here Sampāti is taken as the manifestation of Death, rather than Seetha.

Sloka 8

rāmasya na kṛtam kāryam na kṛtam rāja śāśanam |
harīṇām iyam ajñātā vipattiḥ sahasā āgatā || 4-56-8

8. rāmasya kāryam na kṛitam = Rama's, task, not, done; rāja shāshanam na kṛitam = king's, decree, not, done; by us; harīṇām = for monkeys; iyam a jñātā vipattiḥ = this, not, known, catastrophe; sahasā āgatā = abruptly, bechanced.

"We fulfilled neither Rama's task, nor the decree of our king, but this unknown catastrophe has abruptly bechanced for the monkeys... [4-56-8]

Sloka 9

vaidehyāḥ priya kāmena kṛtam karma jaṭāyuṣā |
gṛdhra rājena yat tatra śrutam vaḥ tat aśeṣataḥ || 4-56-9

9. vaidehyāḥ priya kāmena = for Vaidehi, to please [to do good,] wishing to; gṛidhra rājena = eagle, by kingly; jaṭāyuṣā = by Jatāyu; tatra = there [in Janasthāna]; yat karma kṛitam = which, deed, is done; tat = that - deed of confronting Ravana; a sheṣataḥ = without, remainder [completely]; vaḥ shrutam = by you al, heard.

"You all have heard in its entirety what deed the kingly Jatāyu has done wishing to do good to Vaidehi... [4-56-9]

Sloka 10

tathā sarvāṇi bhūtāni tiryak yoni gatāni api |
priyam kurvanti rāmasya tyaktvā prāṇān yathā vayam || 4-56-10

10. tathā = like that; tiryak yoni gatāni api = in animal, uterus, gone in [birthed in animality,] even; sarvāṇi bhūtāni = all, beings; prāṇān tyaktvā = lives. o forgoing; rāmasya priyam kurvanti = for Rama, agreeable things, are doing; yathā vayam = as with, us.

"Like that all beings, even those birthed in animality, are doing what that is agreeable to Rama even on forgoing their own lives, as with us... [4-56-10]

Sloka 11

anyonyam upakurvanti sneha kāruṇya yantritāḥ |
tataḥ tasya upakāra artham tyajata ātmānam ātmanā || 4-56-11

11. sneha kāruṇya yantritāḥ = camaraderie, concern, by instinct; [tiryak yoni gatāni api = in animal, uterus, birthed in, even]; anyonyam upakurvanti = mutually, succouring; tataḥ = therefore; tasya = for his [Rama's]; upakāra artham = succour, for purpose of; ātmānam ātmanā tyajata = one's self [soul,] by oneself [soulfully,] may leave off.

"Even animals will be mutually succouring by their instinct of concern and camaraderie, therefore, let each of us leave off our souls, soulfully... [4-56-11]

Sloka 12

priyam kṛtvā hi rāmasya dharmajñena jaṭāyuṣā |
rāghava arthe pariśrāntā vayam saṃtyakta jīvitāḥ || 4-56-12
kāṃtārāṇi prapannāḥ sma na ca paśyāma maithilīm |

12, 13a. dharmajñena jaṭāyuṣā = right, knowing [conscientious,] by Jatāyu; rāmasya priyam kṛitvā hi = for Rama, likable [act,] is done, isn't it; vayam = we [too]; rāghava arthe = = Raghava, in the cause of; parishrāntā = overtired; samtyakta jīvitāḥ = on leaving [the fear of,] our lives - without caring for our lives; kāntārāṇi prapannāḥ sma = in forests, obtained, we have; maithilīm na ca pashyāma = ṃaithili, not, even, we saw.

"Conscientious Jatāyu has done a deed much liked by Rama, and we too have been trekking the forests without caring even for our lives in the cause of Rama, but we have not seen ṃaithili... [4-56-12, 13a]

Sloka 13

sa sukhī gṛdhra rājaḥ tu rāvaṇena hato raṇe |
muktaḥ ca sugrīva bhayāt gataḥ ca paramām gatim || 4-56-13

13b, 13c. raṇe rāvaṇena hataḥ = in combat, by Ravana, one who is killed; saḥ gṛidhra rājaḥ tu = he that, eagle, kingly one, on his part; sukhī = a happy [soul]; sugrīva bhayāt muktaḥ ca = from ṣugreeva, fear of, relieved, also; paramām gatim gataḥ ca = ṣublimity, on Avenue, departed, also.

"He that kingly eagle Jatāyu who is killed by Ravana is a happy soul, and he is even relieved from the fear of Sugreeva as he departed on the Avenue of Sublimity... [4-56-13b, c]

▼

Sugreeva is not only the sovereign of monkeys but to all animal worlds also, and even to all birds of Aves kingdom, nidificatis aves, apes, oves...  as Virgil would say. So slain by Ravana, Jatāyu is absolved of sin and fear of Sugreeva, as well. ṃaheshvara Tīrtha.

Sloka 14

jaṭāyuṣo vināśena rājño daśarathasya ca |
haraṇena ca vaidehyāḥ saṃśayam harayo gatāḥ || 4-56-14

14. jaṭāyuṣaḥ vināshena = Jatāyu's, by the ending; rājñaḥ dasharathasya ca = king, Dasharatha's [end,] also; vaidehyāḥ haraṇena ca = of Vaidehi, purloin, also; harayaḥ samshayam gatāḥ = monkeys, in doubt [predicament,] piled into.

"By the ending of Jatāyu and by the end of Dasharatha, also by the purloin of Vaidehi, all of the monkeys are piled into a predicament... [4-56-14]

▼

Vividly: 'If Jatāyu is not killed Seetha would not be abducted, even if Seetha is abducted, should Jatāyu be living he might have detailed that abduction, and Rama might not have come to Kishkindha, but would have gone to Lanka straightaway. At least, if Dasharatha is not dead he would have called Rama and others back to Ayodhya, thus the question of Seetha's abduction does not arise. Because these two are dead, Seetha is abducted and their deaths became a deadly predicament for us... the monkeys...'

Sloka 15

rāma lakṣmaṇayoḥ vāsām araṇye saha sītayā |
rāghavasya ca bāṇena vālinaḥ ca tathā vadhaḥ || 4-56-15
rāma kopāt aśeṣāṇām rākṣasām ca tathā vadham |
kaikeyyā vara dānena idam ca vikṛtam kṛtam || 4-56-16

15, 16. kaikeyyā vara dānena = to Kaikeyi, boon, by giving; sītayā saha = ṣeetha, along with; araṇye = in forest; rāma lakṣmaṇayoḥ = of Rama, Lakshmana; vāsām = dwelling; tathā = as a result; rāghavasya bāṇena = by Raghava's, arrow; vālinaḥ vadhaḥ ca = Vali's, slaying, also; tathā = as a result; rāma kopāt = Rama, by fury; a sheṣāṇām = without, leftovers; rākṣasām vadham ca = of demons, eliminating, also; idam vi kṛitam ca = this, wrong, deed [quandary,] also; kṛitam = is done [bechanced.]

"Just by giving boons to Kaikeyi, the quandaries like Rama's dwelling in forests along with Seetha and Lakshmana, as a result Rama's fury eliminating complete demons of Janasthāna, and as a result Raghava's arrow exterminating Val, have come to pass." Thus Angada is lamenting. [4-56-15,16]

Sloka 17

tat asukham anukīrtitam vaco
bhuvi patitān ca nirīkṣya vānarān |
bhṛśa cakita matiḥ mahāmatiḥ
kṛpaṇam udāhṛtavān sa gṛdhrarājaḥ || 4-56-17

17. anu kīrtitam = eulogised; a sukham = not, comfortable; tat vacaḥ = that, word - that information; [shrutvā = on hearing]; bhuvi patitān vānarān nirīkshya = on ground, fallen [lay recumbent,] at monkeys, on observing; mahāmatiḥ = high, minded; saḥ gṛidhra rajaḥ [rāṭ] = he, eagles, king; bhṛisha = highly; cakita = perturbed; matiḥ = with mind; kṛipaṇam udāhṛitavān = sorrowfully, spelled out - said word to the wise.

On observing the monkeys who lay recumbent on ground and on hearing them eulogising the death of Jatāyu he that king of eagles Sampāti is highly perturbed at that uncomfortable information about Jatayu, and that high minded Sampāti sorrowfully spelled out this word. [4-56-17]

▼

Some versions of Valmiki Ramayana end this chapter with this verse and continue next verses in the next chapter.

Sloka 18

tat tu śrutvā tadā vākyam aṃgadasya mukha udgatam |
abravīt vacanam gṛdhraḥ tīkṣṇa tuṇḍo mahāsvanaḥ || 4-56-18

18. tīkṣṇa tuṇDaḥ = sharp [shredder,] billed; mahā svanaḥ = thunderously, voicing; gṛidhraḥ = eagle; angadasya mukha udgatam = from Angada's, mouth, came out; tadā = thus; tat vākyam shrutvā = that, sentence, on hearing; vacanam abravīt = sentence, said.

That shredder-billed eagle Sampāti said this sentence voicing thunderously on hearing words voiced by Angada. [4-56-18]

Sloka 19

ko ayam girā ghoṣayati prāṇaiḥ priyatarasya me |
jaṭāyuṣo vadham bhrātuḥ kaṃpayan iva me manaḥ || 4-56-19

19. me to me; prāṇaiḥ priya tarasya = by lives, precious, more than; bhrātuḥ jaṭāyuṣaḥ vadham = of [my] bother, Jatāyu's, about slaying; me manaḥ kampayan iva = my, heart, to quake, as though; girā ghoṣayati = by speech, who announces; ayam kaḥ = this one, who is.

"Who speaks to announce as though to quake my heart that Jatayu, the more precious brother of mine than my own lives, is slain? [4-56-19]

Sloka 20

katham āsīt janasthāne yuddham rākṣasa gṛdhrayoḥ |
nāmadheyam idam bhrātuḥ cirasya adya mayā śrutam || 4-56-20

20. janasthāne = in Janasthāna; rākshasa gṛidhrayoḥ = between demon, eagle; yuddham = combat; katham āsīt = how, occasioned; bhrātuḥ idam nāmadheyam = of brother this, name; mayā adya = by me, today; cirasya shrutam = after a long time, heard.

"How a combat occasioned between that eagle and a demon in Janasthāna, and how am I hearing this name of my brother after a long time... [4-56-20]

Sloka 21

iccheyam giri durgāt ca bhavadbhiḥ avatāritum |
yavīyaso guṇajñasya ślāghanīyasya vikramaiḥ || 4-56-21
ati dīrghasya kālasya parituṣṭo asmi kīrtitanāt |

21, 22a. giri durgāt = from mountain, soars; bhavadbhiḥ ava tāritum = by you, to down, lowered; iccheyam = I wish to; guṇa jñasya = virtue, knower - straightforward; vikramaiḥ shlāghanīyasya = by valour, praiseworthy; yavīyasaḥ = of younger brother; ati dīrghasya kālasya = after much, long, time; kīrtitanāt = by [your] praising; parituṣṭaḥ asmi = gladdened, I am.

"I wish you to get me down from these mountain soars. After a long time I have heard about my younger brother who is straightforward, praiseworthy for his valour, and I am glad that too spoke of him praisefully... [4-56-21, 22a]

Sloka 22

tat iccheyam aham śrotum vināśam vānara ṛṣabhāḥ || 4-56-22
bhrātuḥ jaṭāyuṣaḥ tasya janasthāna nivāsinaḥ |

22b, 23a. vānara ṛiṣabhāḥ = oh, vanara, bullish ones - best ones; tat = thereby; aham = I; janasthāna nivāsinaḥ = in Janasthāna, dweller - when he was in bhrātuḥ = [as he is my] brother; tasya jaṭāyuṣaḥ = of his, of Jatāyu; vināsham shrotum iccheyam = of death, to listen, I wish to.

"Thereby, oh, best vanara-s, I wish to listen about the death of my brother Jatāyu when he was in Janasthāna... [4-56-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

tasya eva ca mama bhrātuḥ sakhā daśarathaḥ katham || 4-56-23
yasya rāmaḥ priyaḥ putro jyeṣṭho guru jana priyaḥ |

23b, 24a. guru jana priyaḥ rāmaḥ = for doyen, people, pleasant [creditable to,] Rama; yasya = whose [Dasharatha's]; priyaḥ putraḥ jyeṣṭhaḥ = dear, son, eldest one; dasharathaḥ = [such a] Dasharatha; mama bhrātuḥ tasya eva ca sakhā = my, bother, his [to Jatāyu,] alone, also, friend; katham [mṛitam] = how, [died.]

"Whose eldest and dear son is Rama, the creditable one for doyens, that Dasharatha is the friend of my brother Jatayu/ But how did Dasharatha pass away? [4-56-23b, 24a]

Sloka 24

sūrya aṃśu dagdha pakṣatvāt na śaknomi visarpitum |
iccheyam parvatāt asmāt avatartum arindamāḥ || 4-56-24

24b, c. arindamāḥ = oh, enemy-chasteners; sūrya amshu dagdha pakṣatvāt = by ṣun's, rays, burnt, owing to such wings; vi sarpitum = to widely, spread - lessened wing-spread; na shaknomi = not, capable I am; [tasmāt = therefore]; asmāt parvatāt = from this, mountain; ava tartum = down, climb; iccheyam = I wish to.

"I am incapable to spread my wings widely as they are burnt by Sun's rays, hence oh, enemy-chasteners, I wish you to lower me down from this mountain..." Thus Sampāti sought for the help of monkeys. [4-56-24b, c]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe ṣaṭ paṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 56th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
