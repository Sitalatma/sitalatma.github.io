Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 6
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana recalls the over-powering of Lanka and discovering of Seetha by Hanuman. He calls a meeting of his ministers and invites suggestions from them in view of the latest situation of a probable attack of Rama and his army on the City of Lanka.

Sloka 1

laṃkāyām tu kṛtam karma ghoram dṛṣṭvā bhava āvaham |
rākṣasa indro hanumatā śakreṇa iva mahātmanā || 6-6-1
abravīd rākṣasān sarvān hriyā kiṃcid avān mukhaḥ |

1. dṛiṣṭvā = having seen; bhayāvaham = the fearful; ghoram = and the terrible; karma = act; kṛitam = done; hanumatā = by ḥanuman; shakreṇa iva = as Devendra (King of celestials); hamātmanā = the powerful; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka; rākshasendraḥ = Ravana; avān mukhaḥ = had his face down; kimchit = a little; hriyā = and spoke; sarvān = to all; rākshhasān = the demons (as follows):

Having seen the fearful and the terrible act in Lanka done by Hanuma, like Devendra the powerful King of celestials, Ravana with his face descended a little with shame, spoke to the demons as follows:

Sloka 2

dharṣitā ca praviṣṭā ca laṃkā duṣprasahā purī || 6-6-2
tena vānara mātreṇa dṛṣṭā sītā ca jānakī |

2. tena = by him; vānara mātreṇa = who was only a monkey; dushhprāsahā = the irresistible; laṇkapurī = City of lanka; pravishhṭācha = was entered into; dharshhitā cha = and was over-powered; sītā = ṣeetha; jānakī = the daughter of Janka; dṛishhṭām cha = was also seen (by him).

"By him, who was only a monkey, the irresistible City of Lanka was entered into and was over-powered. Seetha the daughter of Janaka was also discovered by him.

Sloka 3

prasādo dharṣitaḥ caityaḥ pravarā rākṣasā hatāḥ || 6-6-3
āvilā ca purī laṃkā sarvā hanumatā kṛtā |

3. chaityaḥ prāsādaḥ = the thousand pillared building used as a sanctuary; dharshhitaḥ = was assaulted; hanumatā = by ḥanuma; pravarāḥ = the most excellent; rākshhāsāḥ = demons; hatāḥ = were killed; sarvā = the entire; laṇkā purī = City of Lanka; kṛitā = was made; āvilā = turbid.

The thousand-pillared building used as a sanctuary was assaulted by Hanuma. Our most excellent demons were killed. The entire City of Lanka was made turbid.

Sloka 4

kim kariṣyāmi bhadram vaḥ kim vā yuktam anantaram || 6-6-4
ucyatām naḥ samartham yat kṛtam ca sukṛtam bhavet |

4. bhadram = prosperity; vaḥ = to you! Kim = what; karishhyāmi = shall I do? Kim vā = what thing; yuktam = appropriate thing; anantaram = (is to be done) afterwards? Uchyatām = let it be spoken; yat = that which is samantham = well-answering; bhavet = and which becomes; sukrutam = well-done.

ṃay prosperity befall on you! What shall I do? What appropriate thing is to be done next? Please speak that which is well-answering and that, if attempted becomes well-done.

Sloka 5

mantra mūlam hi vijayam prāhur āryā manasvinaḥ || 6-6-5
tasmād vai rocaye mantram rāmam prati mahābalāḥ |

5. mahābalāḥ = Oh; people of great strength! manasvinaḥ = wise-men; pravadanti = say; vijayam = that victory; mantramūlam = is dependent on good forethought; tasmāt vai = just for that reason; rochaye = I desire; mantram = a deliberation; rāmamprati = about Rama.

"Oh, people of great strength! Wise-men say that victory is dependent on good forethought. Verily for that reason, I desire a deliberation about Rama."

Sloka 6

trividhāḥ puruṣā loke uttama adhama madhyamāḥ || 6-6-6
teṣām tu samavetānām guṇa doṣam vadāmi aham |

6. trīvidhāḥ = there are three types; purushhāḥ = of men; loke = in the world; uttamādhama madhyamāḥ = namely; the foremost; the middle most and the lowest; aham = I; vadāmi = am telling; teshhām = their; samavetānām = inherent; guṇadoshhā = merits and defects.

"There are three types of men in the world, namely the foremost the middle most and the lowest. I am telling their inherent merits and defects."

Sloka 7

mantribhir hita samyuktaiḥ samarthair mantra nirṇaye || 6-6-7
mitrair vā api samāna arthair bāndhavair api vā hitaiḥ |
sahito mantrayitvā yaḥ karma ārambhān pravartayet || 6-6-8
daive ca kurute yatnam tam āhuḥ puruṣa uttamam |

7-8. yaḥ = he who; pravartayet = carries through; karmārmabhān = beginning of undertaking; mantrayitvā = after consulting; sahitaḥ = collectively with; hitasmyuktaiḥ = or with ministers; samarthaiḥ = efficient; mantra nirṇaye = in advising decisions; mitrairvāpi = or with friends; samanārthaiḥ = having common; bāndhavairāpi = or with relatives; adhikāḥ = additionally; kurute = or who does; yatnam = endeavour; daivecha = in the matter of providence too; tam = him; āhuḥ = (the wise) call; purushhottamam = as the foremost among men.

"The wise call him as the foremost among men, who carries through the beginning of any undertaking after consulting those who are wedded with welfare of others, or with ministers who are efficient in conferring decisions or with friends having common interests or additionally with relatives or who try to get a favour from Providence too."

Sloka 9

eko artham vimRśed eko dharme prakurute manaḥ || 6-6-9
ekaḥ kāryāṇi kurute tam āhur madhyamam naram |

9. vimṛishet = (ḥe who) deliberates; artham = about an activity; ekaḥ = solitarily; prakurute = applies; manaḥ = his mind; dharme = on law and justice; ekaḥ = all by himself; kurute = performs; kāryāṇi = the workds; eakḥ = singly; āhuḥ = they call; tam naram = that man; madhyamam = as mediocre.

"The wise men call that man as mediocre, who deliberates about an activity solitarily, applies his mind on law and justice all by himself and performs works singly."

Sloka 10

guṇa doṣāv aniścitya tyaktvā daiva vyapāśrayam || 6-6-10
kariṣyāmi iti yaḥ kāryam upekṣet sa nara adhamaḥ |

10. yaḥ = ḥe who; nanishchitya = does not determine; guṇadoshhā = the merits and demerits; vyaktma = clearly; daivavyapāshreyaḥ = having recourse to providence; upekshhet = neglects; kāryam = his duty; karishhyāmi iti = telling simply �I shall do'; saḥ = he; narādhamaḥ = is the lowest among men.

"He who does not determine the merits and demerits of an act clearly, having recourse to Providence and neglects his duty by simply telling �I shall do it', he is the lowest among men."

Sloka 11

yathā ime puruṣā nityam uttama adhama madhyamāḥ || 6-6-11
evam mantro api vijñeya uttama adhama madhyamaḥ |

11. yathā = how; ime = those; purushhāḥ = men; nityam = are always; uttamādhama madhyāḥ = either good; bad or mediocre; mantro.api = even the resolution in thought is; evam = thus; viJṇeyaḥ = known; uttama adhama madhyamaḥ = as good; bad or mediocre."

"In which manner these men are categorized always as good, bad or mediocre, so also the resolution in thought is classified as good bad or mediocre."

Sloka 12

aikamatyam upāgamya śāstra dṛṣṭena cakṣuṣā || 6-6-12
mantriṇo yatra nirastās tam āhur mantram uttamam |

12. mantraiṇaḥ = the thinkers; chakshhushhā = on a view; shāstradṛiṣṭena = perceived by scriptures; upāgamya = obtain; aikamatyam = consensus; niratāḥ = and are satisfied; tam = that; mantram = resolution of thought; āhuḥ = (the wise) say; uttamam = as the excellent one.

"The wise say that resolution of thought is excellent, in which the thinkers, on a view perceived by scriptures, obtain consensus and are satisfied with it."

Sloka 13

bahvyo api matayo gatvā mantriṇo hi artha nirṇaye || 6-6-13
punar yatra ekatām prāptaḥ sa mantro madhyamaḥ smṛtaḥ |

13. gatvāpi = even after obtaining; bahvīḥ = several kinds; matiḥ = of opinions; yatra = where; arthanirṇayaḥ = the decision of a matter; mantriṇām = by the counselors; punaḥ = once more; prāptaḥ = gets; ekatām = to a consensus; saḥ = that; mantraḥ = decision; smṛitaḥ = is called; madhmaḥ = mediocre.

"Only after deliberating several kinds of opinions, that decision of a matter obtained finally by a consensus of the counselors, is called mediocre."

Sloka 14

anyonya matim āsthāya yatra sampratibhāṣyate || 6-6-14
na ca aikamatye śreyo asti mantraḥ so adhama ucyate |

14. saḥ = that; mantraḥ = resolution; uchyate = is said to be; adhamaḥ = worst; yatra = in which; sampratibhāshhyate = arguments are made; āsthāya = emplying; anyouya matim = one or the other divergent opinions; nāsti = (in which) there is no; shriyaḥ = advantage; aikamatye = even after a consensus.

"That resolution is said to be worst in which several arguments are made, employing one or the other divergent opinions and in which there is no advantage even after a consensus."

Sloka 15

tasmāt sumantritam sādhu bhavanto mantri sattamāḥ || 6-6-15
kāryam sampratipadyantām etat kṛtyatamam mama |

15. tasmāt = for that reason; bhavantaḥ = you; matisattamāḥ = having excellent intellect; sampritipadyantām = arrive at; sumantritam = a well-thought out; sādhu = rightful; kāryam = action; etat = this; mama = to me; matam = is earnestly; kṛityam = to be done."

"For that reason, you, having excellent intellect, arrive at a well-thought out rightful action. To me, this is earnestly to be done."

Sloka 16

vānarāṇām hi vīrāṇām sahasraiḥ parivāritaḥ || 6-6-16
rāmo abhyeti purīm laṃkām asmākam uparodhakaḥ |

16. rāmaḥ = Rama; parivāritah = surrounded by; sahasraiḥ = thousands of; dhirāṇām = brave; vānarāṇām = monkeys; abhyeti = is coming; laṇ^kām purīm = to the City of Lanka; uparodhakaḥ = for besieging; asmākam = us.

"Rama surrounded by thousands of courageous monkeys, is coming to the City of Lanka, for the purpose of besieging us."

Sloka 17

tariṣyati ca suvyaktam rāghavaḥ sāgaram sukham || 6-6-17
tarasā yukta rūpeṇa sānujaḥ sabala anugaḥ |

17. rāgahavaḥ = Rama; yukta rūpeṇa = by his befitting; tarasā = strength; suvyaktam = can certainly; tarishhyati cha = cross; sāgaram = the ocean; sukham = easily; sāmjaḥ = with his brother; sabalāmgaḥ = along with his army and companions.

"Rama, by his be-fitting strength, can certainly cross the ocean easily, with his brother along with his army and his companions."

Sloka 18

samudramucchoṣayati vīryeṇānyatkaroti vā|| 6-6-18
tasminn evam gate kārye viruddhe vānaraiḥ saha |
hitam pure ca sainye ca sarvam sammantryatām mama || 6-6-19

18-19. samudram uchchhoshhayati = (ḥe may) get the ocean dried up; karoti = (ḥe may) do; anyadvā = any other thing; vīryeṇa = by his valour; tasmin = that; kārye = act; viruddhe = of enemity; vānaraiḥ saha = with monkeys; evam vidhe = (having commenced) in this way; sammantryatām = advise me; sarvam = everything; hitam = that is good; pure = for the city; mama sainye = and for my army.

"He may even get the ocean dried up. He may do any other thing by his valour. The aforesaid act of enmity with monkeys having commenced in this way, advise me everything that is good for the city and my army."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ṣaṣṭhaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 6th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
