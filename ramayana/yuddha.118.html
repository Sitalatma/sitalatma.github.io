Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 118
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The fire-god appears in person from the burning pyre, carrying Seetha in his arms and restores her to Rama, testifying to her purity. Rama later joyfully accepts her.

Sloka 1

etacchrutvā śubhaṃ vākyaṃ pitāmahasamīritam |
aṅkenādāya vaidehīmutpapāta vibhāvasuḥ || 6-118-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; etat shubham vākyam = these auspicious words; pitāmaha samīritam = spoken by Brahma (creator); vibhāvasuḥ = the fire-god; utpapāta = came up; ādāya = taking; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; aṇ^kena = in his arms.

Hearing the foregoing auspicious words of Brahma (the creator), the fire-god came up, taking Seetha in his arms.

Sloka 2

vidhūyātha citāṃ tāṃ tu viadehīṃ havyavāhanaḥ |
uttasthau mūrtimānāśu gṛhītvā janakātmajām || 6-118-2

2. vidhūya tām chitām = shaking off that funeral pile; gṛihitvā = and taking; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; janakātmajam = the daughter of Janaka (in his arms); havyavāhanaḥ = the fire-god; atha = forthwith; uttasthau = sprang up; āshu = at once; marurtimān = in a personified form.

Shaking off that funeral pile and taking that Seetha, the daughter of Janaka in his arms, the fire-god forthwith sprang up at once in a personified form.

Sloka 3

taruṇādityasaṃkāśāṃ taptakāñcanabhūṣaṇām |
raktāmbaradharāṃ bālāṃ nīlakuñcitamūrdhajām || 6-118-3
akliṣṭamālyābharaṇāṃ tathārūpāmaninditām |
dadau rāmāya vaidehīmaṅke kṛtvā vibhāvasuḥ || 6-118-4

3-4. kṛitvā = bearing; aṇ^ke = in his arms; bālā viadehīm = the youthful ṣeetha; taruṇāditya samkāsham = who was shining brightly as the rising sun; taptakāṇ^chana bhūṣhaṇām = was decked in ornaments of refined gold; raktāmbaradharām = and attired in a red robe; nīlakuṇ^chita mūrdhajām = and wore dark curly hari; akliṣhṭa mālyābharaṇām = who was further adorned with ornaments of flowers which had not abraded (on her entering the fire and coming out of it); aninditām = who was absolutely beyond reproach; tathārūpam = and looked just the same (as she did while entering the flames); vibhāvasuḥ = the fire-god; dadau = restored (her); rāmāya = to Rama.

Bearing in his arms the youthful Seetha, who was shining brightly as the rising sun, was decked in ornaments of refined gold, attired in a red robe and wore dark curly hair, who was further adorned with ornaments of flowers, which had not abraded (on her entering the fire and coming out of it), who was absolutely beyond reproach and looked just the same (as she did while entering the fire and coming out of it), who was absolutely beyond reproach and looked just the same (as she did while entering the flames), the fire-god restored her to Rama.

Sloka 5

abravīttu tadā rāmaṃ sākṣī lokasya pāvakaḥ |
eṣā te rāma vaidehī pāpamasyāṃ na vidyate || 6-118-5

5. tadā = then; pāvakaḥ = the fire-god; lokasya sākṣhī = the witness of the world; abravīt tu = spoke; rāmam = to Rama (as follows); eṣhā = here; te vaidehī = is your ṣeetha; na vidyate pāpam = no sin exists; asyām = in her.

Then, the fire-god, the witness of the whole world, spoke to Rama as follows "Here is your Seetha. No sin exists in her."

Sloka 6

naiva vācā na manasā naiva buddhyā na cakṣuṣā |
suvṛttā vṛttaśauṇḍīryaṃ na tvāmatyacaracchubhā || 6-118-6

6. shubhā = this auspicious lady; suvṛittā = whose character has been good; na atyacharat = has never been unfaithful; tvām = to you; vṛittashauṇ^Dīryam = who are endowed with strength of character; vāchā = either by word; na manasā = nor by mind; naiva buddhyā = nor even by intellect; na chakṣhuṣhā = nor by her glances.

"This auspicious lady, whose character has been good, has never been unfaithful to you who are endowed with strength of character either by word or by mind or even by intellect or by her glances."

Sloka 7

rāvaṇenāpanītaiṣā vīryotsiktena rakṣasā |
tvayā virahitā dīnā vivaśā nirjanādvanāt || 6-118-7

7. virahatā tvayā = separated from you; eṣhā dīnā vivashā = this miserable and helpless lady; apavītā = was taken away; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; rakṣhasā = the demon; vīryotsiktena = who was arrogant of his valour; nirjanāt vanāt = from a lonely hermitage.

"Separated from you, this miserable and helpless lady, was taken away by Ravana the demon, who was arrogant of his valour, from a lonely hermitage."

Sloka 8

ruddha cāntaḥpure guptā tvaccittā tvatparāyaṇā |
rakṣitā rākṣasībhiśca ghorābhirghorabuddhibhiḥ || 6-118-8

8. tvachchittā = (ṭhis ṣeetha) fixing her mind upon you; tvatparāyaṇā = and having you as her final attainment; ruddhā = was detained; antaḥpure = in the gynaecium; guptā = and hidden there; rakṣhitā = (she) was guarded; ghorābhiḥ = rākṣhasībhiḥ = by frightful female-demons; ghorabuddhibhiḥ = with horrible intellect.

"This Seetha, who was fixing her mind upon you ad looking on you as her final attainment, was detained in the gynaecium and hidden there. She was guarded by rightful female-demons with horrible intellect."

Sloka 9

pralobhyamānā vividhaṃ tarjyamānā ca maithilī |
nācintayata tadrakṣastvadgatenāntarātmanā || 6-118-9

9. maithilī = ṣeetha; tvadgatea antarātmanā = whose mind was directed towards you; nāchintayata = ignored; tat = that; rakṣhaḥ = demon; pralobhyamānā = even though allured; tarjyamānā = and frightened; vividham = through various means.

"Seetha, whose mind was directed towards you, ignored that demon even though allured and frightened through various means."

Sloka 10

viśuddhabhāvāṃ niṣpāpāṃ pratigṛhṇīṣva maithilīm |
na kiṃcirabhidhātavyā ahamājñāpayāmi te || 6-118-10

10. pratigṛihṇīṣhva = take back; maithilīm = ṣeetha; niṣhpāpam = who is sinless; vishuddha bhāvām = with a pure character; na abhidhātavyā = she should not be told; kimchit = anything (harsh); aham = I; ājJṇāpayāmi = (hereby) command; te = you.

"Take back Seetha, who is sinless, with a pure character. She should not be told anything harsh. I hereby command you."

Sloka 11

tataḥ prītamanā rāmaḥ śrutvaivaṃ vadatāṃ varaḥ |
dadhyau muhūrtaṃ dharmātmā bāṣpavyākulalocanaḥ || 6-118-11

11. tataḥ = then; shrutvā = having heard; evam = thus; rāmaḥ = Rama; varaḥ = the excellent; vadatām = among the eloquent; dharmātmā = whose mind was sent on virtue; prītamanāḥ = with a pleasant mind; bāṣhpa vyākula lochanaḥ = with his eyes entirely filled with tears of joy; dadhyau = reflected; muhūrtam = for a while.

Rama, the excellent among the eloquent, whose mind was set on virtue, with a pleasant mind to hear the aforesaid speech, reflected for a while, his eyes, entirely filed with tears of joy.

Sloka 12

evamukto mahātejā dhṛmānuruvikramaḥ |
uvāca tridaśaśreṣṭhaṃ rāmo dharmabhṛtāṃ varaḥ || 6-118-12

12. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; dhṛitimān = the courageous; rāmaḥ = Rama; uruvikramaḥ = of great prowess; dharma bhṛitām varaḥ = the foremost among those upholding the virtue; uvācha = spoke; tridasha shreṣhṭham = to the fire-god; the best of gods.

Hearing those words, the courageous Rama of great prowess and the foremost of those upholding the virtue, replied to the fire-god, the best of gods.

Sloka 13

avaśyaṃ cāpi lokeṣu sītā pāvanamarhati |
dīrghakāloṣitā hīyaṃ rāvaṇāntaḥpure śubhā || 6-118-13

13. sītā = ṣeetha; avashyam = certainly; arhat = deserves; pāvanam = this purefactory ordeal; lokeṣhu = in the eyes of the people; iyam shubhā = (in as much as) this blessed woman; dīrgha kāloṣhitā hi = had resided for a long time; rāvaṇāntaḥ pure hi = indeed in the gynaecium of Ravana.

"Seetha certainly deserves this pure factory ordeal in the eyes of the people in as much as this blessed woman had resided for a long time indeed in the gynaecium of Ravana.

Sloka 14

bāliśo bata kāmātma rāmo daśarathātmajaḥ |
iti vakṣyati māṃ loko jānakīmaviśodhya hi || 6-118-14

14. lokaḥ = the world; vakṣhyati = would chatter; mām = against me; iti = saying that; bata = Alas!; rāmaḥ = Rama; dasharātmajaḥ = the son of Dasaratha; bālishaḥ = was really foolish; kāmaratmā = and that his mind was dominated by lust; avishodhya jānakīm hi = without indeed examinig ṣeetha with regard to her chastity.

"The world would chatter against me, saying that Rama, the son of Dasaratha, was really foolish and that his mind was dominated by lust, if I accept Seetha without examining her with regard to her chastity."

Sloka 15

ananyahṛdayāṃ bhaktāṃ macattaparivartinīm |
ahamapyavagacchāmi maithilīṃ janakātmajām || 6-118-15

15. ahamapi = I also; avagachchhāmi = know; maithilīm = (that) ṣeetha; janakātmajām = the daughter of Janaka; machchittaparivartinīm = who revolves in my mind; bhaktām ananya hṛidayām = is undivided in her affection to me.

"I also know that Seetha, the daughter of Janaka, who ever revolves in my mind, is undivided in her affection to me."

Sloka 16

imāmapi viśālākṣīṃ rakṣitāṃ svena tejasā |
rāvaṇo nātivarteta vela miva mahodadhiḥ || 6-118-16

16. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; nātivarteta = could not violate; imām vishālākṣhīm = this wide-eyed woman; rakṣhitām = protected as she was; svena tejasā = by her own splendour; mahodadhiḥ iva = any more than a sea; velām = would transgress (its bounds).

"Ravana could not violate this wide-eyed woman, protected as she was by her own splendour, any more than an ocean would transgress its bounds."

Sloka 17

pratyayārthaṃ tu lokānāṃ trayāṇām satyasaṃśrayaḥ |
upekṣe cāpi vaidehīṃ praviśantīṃ hutāśanam || 6-118-17

17. pratyayārtham = in order to convince; trayāṇām lokānām = the three worlds; satya samshrayaḥ = I; whose refuge is truth; upekṣhechāpi = ignored; viadehīm = ṣeetha; pravishantīm = while she was entering; hutāshanam = the fire.

"In order to convince the three worlds, I, whose refugee is truth, ignored Seetha while she was entering the fire."

Sloka 18

na ca śaktaḥ suduṣṭatmā manasāpi hi maithilīm |
pradharṣayitumaprāpyāṃ dīptāmagniśikhāmiva || 6-118-18

18. suduṣhṭātmā = the evil-minded Ravana; na cha shaktaḥ = was not even able; pradharṣhayitum = to lay his violent hands; manasāpi = even in thought; aprāpyām maithilīm = on the unobtainable ṣeetha; dīptām = who was blazing; agnishikhāmiva = like a flaming tongue of fire.

"The evil-minded Ravana was not able to lay his violent hands, even in thought, o the unobtainable Seetha, who was blazing like a flaming tongue of fire."

Sloka 19

neya marhati caiśvaryaṃ rāvaṇāntaḥpure śubhā |
ananyā hi mayā sītā bhāskareṇa prabhā yathā || 6-118-19

19. iyam shubhā = this auspicious woman; nārhati = could not (give way) aishvaryam = to the sovereignty; rāvaṇāntaḥ pure = existing in the gynaecium of Ravana; sītā = in as much as ṣeetha; ananyāhi = is not different; mayā = from me; prabhā yathā = even as sunlight; bhāskareṇa = (is not different) from the sun.

"This auspicious woman could not give way to the sovereignty, existing in the gynaecium of Ravana, in as much as Seetha is not different from me, even as sunlight is not different from the sun."

Sloka 20

viśuddhā triṣu lokeṣu maithilī janakātmajā |
na vihātuṃ mayā śakyā kīrtirātmavatā yathā || 6-118-20

20. maithilī = ṣeetha; janakātmajā = the daughter of Jankā; vishuddhā = is completely pure in her character; triṣhu lokeṣhu = in all the three worlds; na shakyā = and can no longer be; vihātum = renounced; mayā = by me; kīrtiḥ yathā = as a good name (cannot be cast aside); ātmavatā = by a prudent man.

"Seetha, the daughter of Janaka, is completely pure in her character, in all the three worlds and can no longer be renounced by me, as a good name cannot be cast aside by a prudent man."

Sloka 21

avaśyaṃ ca mayā kāryaṃ sarveṣāṃ vo vaco hitam |
snigdhānāṃ lokanāthānāmevaṃ ca vadatāṃ hitam || 6-118-21

21. hitam = the salutary; vachaḥ = worlds; vaḥ sarveṣhām = of all of you; lokanāthām = the guardians of the world; snighdhānām = who are affectionate; evam = thus; vadatām = speaking; avashyam = certainly; kāryam = are to be carried out; mayā = by me.

The salutary advice of you all, the affectionate guardians of the world, who are saying what is conducive to our good, must be certainly carried out by me."

Sloka 22

ityevamuktvā vijayī mahābalaḥ |
praśasyamānah svakṛtena karmaṇā |
sametya rāmaḥ priyayā mahāyaśāḥ |
sukhaṃ sukhārho.anubabhūva rāghavaḥ || 6-118-22

22. uktvā = saying; ityevam = thus; sametya = and getting reunited; priyayā = with her beloved (ṣeetha); vijayī = the victorious; mahāyashāḥ = and highly illustrious; rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = a scion of Raghu dynasty; mahābalaḥ = who was endowed with a great strength; sukhārhaḥ = and deserved with a great strength; sukhārhaḥ = and deserved happiness; prashasyamānaḥ = and was being glorified; karmaṇā = by his feats; svakṛitena = performed by own self; anubabhūva = experienced; sukham = joy.

Saying thus and getting reunited with her beloved Seetha, the victorious and highly illustrious Rama, a scion of Raghu dynasty, who was endowed with a great strength and deserved happiness and was being glorified by his exploits, performed by his own self, experienced joy.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe aṣṭādaśādhika śatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 118th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
