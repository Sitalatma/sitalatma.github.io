Introduction

Seetha was shocked, fell unconscious at the sight of Rama's head and bow. She regained consciousness and asked Ravana to kill her also, so that she would join her husband in heaven. ṃeanwhile, Ravana is abruptly called away by an emissary and after consultation with his ministers, prepares his army for action against the forces of Rama.

1; 2; 3. dṛishhṭva = seeing; tat = that; shiraḥ = head; tat = that; uttamam = excellent; kārmukamcha = bow; sugrīvapratisamsargam = (hearing about Rama's) friendly union with ṣugreeva; ākhyātam = as appraised; hanūmatā = by ḥanuman; nayane = the eyes; mukhavarṇamcha = facial complexion; sadṛisham = resembling; bhartuḥ = (those of) her husband; tat = that; mukham = face; kesham = the hair; keshānta deshamcha = the expanse of his fore-head; tam = that; shubham = beautiful; chūDā maṇimcha = jewel worn on the top of his head; abhijṇ^āya = and recognizing; sarvaiḥ = all; etaiḥ = these; abhijṇ^ānaiḥ = marks of proof; sā sītā = that ṣita; suduḥkhitā = was very much afflicted with sorrow ; kroshantī = crying; kurarī yathā = like an osprey; vijagarhecha = and abused; kaikeyīm = Kaikeyi(as follows):

ṣeetha saw the illusory head and bow. ṣhe heard Ravana narrating about Rama's friendly relationship with Sugreeva, as earlier apprised by Hanuman. Recognizing that head as that of Rama, with a proof resembling her husband's eyes, facial complexion, hair, expanse of his forehead and the beautiful jewel worn on the top of his head, she was very much afflicted with sorrow, cried like an osprey and abused Kaikeyi who was the originator of the present calamity (as follows):

Sloka 4

kulam utsāditam sarvam tvayā kalaha śīlayā || 6-32-4

4. kaikeyī = O;Kaikeyi ;bhava = = be; sakāmā = an accomplisher of your desires; ayam = this Rama; kula nandanaḥ = causing joy to the family;hato = was killed; sarvam = the entire;kulam = race; utsāditam = has been destroyed; tvayā = by you; kalaha shīlayā = of quarrelsome nature.

Kaikeyi! Be a fulfiller of your craving! ṭhis Rama, causing a joy to the family, has been slain. The entire race has been destroyed by a woman of squabbling nature.

Sloka 24

aśoka vanikām tyaktvā mantriṇām darśanam yayau || 6-32-38

38. shrutvā = hearing; etat = the aforesaid; rākshhasa prativeditam = communication of the demon; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; tyaktvā = left; ashokavanikām = the garden of Ashoka; yayou = and accorded; darshanam = his audience; mantriṇām = to the ministers.

ḥearing the aforesaid communication of the demon, Ravana left the garden of Ashoka and bestowed audience to his ministers.

Sloka 39

sa tu sarvam samarthya eva mantribhiḥ kRtyam ātmanaḥ |
sabhām praviśya vidadhe viditvā rāma vikramam || 6-32-39

39. saḥ = he; samarthaiva = deliberated; ātmanaḥ = with his; mantribhiḥ = ministers; sarvam = about the entire things; kṛityam = to be done; pravishya = entered; sabhām = the council chamber; viditvā = and understanding; rāmavikramam = about the strength of the Rama; vidhadhe = arranged for what was to be done.

ḥe deliberated with his ministers as to what action in its entirety to be pursued and entered the council-chamber and issued his commands in accordance with the knowledge he possessed of Rama's forces.

Sloka 40

antardhānam tu tat śīrṣam tac ca kārmukam uttamam |
jagāma rāvaṇasya eva niryāṇa samanantaram || 6-32-40

40. niryāṇa samanantarameva = soon after the departure; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; tat shīrshham = that head; tatuttamam = and that distinguished; kārmukamcha = bow; jagāma = attained; antardhānam = disappearance.

ṣoon after the departure of Ravana, the illusory head and bow had vanished.

Sloka 41

rākṣasa indras tu taiḥ sārdham mantribhir bhīma vikramaiḥ |
samarthayām āsa tadā rāma kārya viniścayam || 6-32-41

41. tadā = then; rākshhasendrastu = Ravana; the king of demons; sārtham bhīma vikramaiḥ = along with his highly powerful;mantribhiḥ = ministers; samartha yāmāsa = decided; rāmakāryavinishchayam = on the measures he would adopt against Rama.

ṭhen, Ravana the king of demons in consultation with his highly powerful ministers, decided on the measures he would adopt against Rama.

Sloka 42

avidūra sthitān sarvān bala adhyakṣān hita eṣiṇaḥ |
abravīt kāla sadRśo rāvaṇo rākṣasa adhipaḥ || 6-32-42

42. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākshhasādhipaḥ = the king of demons; kālasadṛishaḥ = resembling Yama; the god of death; abravīt = addressed; sarvān = all; balādhyakshhān = the generals; hitaishhiṇaḥ = devoted to his interests; avidūrasthitān = standing nearby.

Ravana the king of demons, resembling Yama the god of death, addressed all the generals standing nearby, devoted to his interests as follows:

Sloka 43

śīghram bherī ninādena sphuṭa koṇa āhatena me |
samānayadhvam sainyāni vaktavyam ca na kāraṇam || 6-32-43

43. bherīninādena = by the sound of a drum; sphuṭam = audibly; koṇa hatena = beaten with a drumstick; shīghram = immediately; samānayadhvam = summon; me = my; sainyāni = forces; nachavaktavyam = without expecting; kāraṇam = a reason.

"By a sound of a drum beaten audibly with a drum stick, immediately summon all my forces. Do not tell them the reason for which I am calling."

Sloka 44

tatas tathā iti pratigṛhya tad vaco |
stadaiva dūtāḥ sahasā mahādbalam |
samānayaṃsḥ caiva samāgatam ca te |
nyavedayan bhartari yuddha kānkṣiṇi || 6-32-44

44. tataḥ = then; dūtāḥ = the messengers; pratigṛihya = answered; tathā iti = "ṣo be it" ; tadvachaḥ = obedient to his words; sahasā = instantly; samānayana = gathered together; mahat = a huge; balam = army; tadaiva = then itself; nyavedayan = and informed; bharatari = their lord; yuddhakāṇ^kchhiṇi = longing for fight; samāgatam = that they had assembled.

ṭhen, the messengers answered, "ṣo be it" obedient as they were to his words and instantly gathered a huge army together and informed their lord longing for fight, that they had since assembled the army.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvtriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 32nd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
