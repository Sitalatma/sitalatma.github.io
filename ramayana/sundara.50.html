Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 50
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

As instructed by Ravana, Prahasta his chief minister inquires of Hanuma as to who he was and also his motive in destroying the pleasure-garden as also killing the demons. In reply, Hanuma says that he destroyed the pleasure-garden in order that he might be taken captive and dragged to the presence of Ravana, whom he was eager to see and was forced in self-defense to kill those who stood in his way. Finally, he declares himself to be a messenger of Rama and adds that, though he was incapable of being bound by a missile presided over by Brahma, he surrendered to it only in order to see Ravana in person.


Sloka 1

tam udvīkṣya mahābāhuḥ pinga akṣam purataḥ sthitam |
roṣeṇa mahatā āviṣṭo rāvaṇo loka rāvaṇaḥ || 5-50-1
sa rājā roṣa tāmra akṣaḥ prahastam mantri sattamam |

1. saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = ṭhat Ravana; mahābāhuḥ = the long armed; lokarāvaṇaḥ = who causes the people to cry; udvīkṣya = looking up; tam piṇ^gākṣam = at that mokey; sthitam = standing; purataḥ = in front of him; mahatā kopena āviṣṭah = affected with violent anger; shaṇ^kāhṛitmā = with his mind seized with suspicion; dadhyau = speenlated; kapīndram = about ḥanuma the formost of monkeys; āvṛitam = who was invested; tejasā = with splendour.

ṭhat Ravana, the long-armed, who caused people to cry, looking up at that monkey standing in front of him, was affected with violent anger, having his mind seized with suspicion, speculated (as follows) about Hanuma, the jewel among the monkeys, who was invested with splendour.

Sloka 2

kimeṣa bhagavānnandī bhavetsākṣādihāgataḥ || 5-50-2
yena śapto.asmi kailāse mayā saṃcālite purā |
so.ayam vānaramūrtiḥ syātkiṃsvidbāṇo mahāsuraḥ || 5-50-3

2; 3. Kailāse = (when) ṃount Kailasa; samchālite = was moved; mayā = by me; kim bhavet = whether (he is); sākṣāt = actually; bhagavān = the divine; nandī = bull; āgataḥ = who came; iha = here; yena = and by whom; shaptaḥ asmin = I was cursed; purā = long ago; saḥ ayam = or he; syāt kim svit = in whether; mahāsuraḥ bāṇaḥ = the great demon named Bana; vānara mūrtiḥ = in the form of a monkey.

"When ṃount Kailasa was moved by me, is it the same ṇandi the divine bull (attendant of Siva) which cursed me long ago and came here now in this form or whether he is Bana the great demon (son of Bali) came here in the form of a monkey."

Sloka 4

sa rājā roṣatāmrākṣaḥ prahastam mantrisattamam |
kāla yuktam uvāca idam vaco vipulam arthavat || 5-50-4

4. saḥ rājā = that king; roṣa tām rākṣaḥ = enraged with red-hot eyes; uvācha = spoke; idam vachaḥ = these words; kālavat = connected with that time; arthavat = meaningful; avipulam = and concise; prahastam = to Prahasta; mantrisattāmam = his chief of ministers.

ṭhat king Ravana, becoming enraged with red hot eyes, spoke the following concise and meaningful words relevant to that occasion, to Prahasta, his chief of ministers.

Sloka 5

durātmā pṛcchyatām eṣa kutaḥ kim vā asya kāraṇam |
vana bhange ca ko asya artho rākṣasīnām ca tarjane || 5-50-5

5. pṛichchhyatām eṣaḥ durātmā = let this wicked one be asked; kutaḥ = from where did he come; kim kāaṇam = for what reason; atra = at this place; kaḥ arthaḥ = and for what purpose; asya = for him; tarjane = to frighten; rākṣasīnām = the female-demons; vana bhaṇgecha = in destroying the garden.

"Ask this wicked monkey from where did he come, for what reason did he come to this place and for what purpose did he frighten the female-demons in destroying our garden."

Sloka 6

matpurīmapradhṛṣyām vāgamane kiṃ prayojanam |
āyodhane vākiṃ kāryam pṛcchyatāmeṣa durmatiḥ ||5-50-6

6. pṛichchhyatām eṣaḥ durmatiḥ = let this evil-minded monkey be asked; kim prayojanam = on what purpose; āgamane = in coming; matpurīm = to my City; apradhṛiṣyām = which is invincible; kim kāryam = and on what pursuit; āyodhanevā = in doing the battle.

"Ask this evil-minded monkey as to what really is his purpose in coming over to my invincible City and what pursuit he is doing this battle."

Sloka 7

rāvaṇasya vacaḥ śrutvā prahasto vākyam abravīt |
samāśvasihi bhadram te na bhīḥ kāryā tvayā kape || 5-50-7

7. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; prahastaḥ = Prahasta; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words (to ḥanuma); samāshvasihi = take courage!; bhadram te = ḥappiness to you!; bhīḥ na kāryā = not to be frightened; tvayā = by you!; kape = O monkey.

ḥearing the words of Ravana, Prahasta spoke the following words to ḥanuma, "ṭake courage! Happiness to you! You need not get frightened, O monkey!"

Sloka 8

yadi tāvat tvam indreṇa preṣito rāvaṇa ālayam |
tattvam ākhyāhi mā te bhūt bhayam vānara mokṣyase || 5-50-8

8. vānara = O monkey!; tvam preṣitaḥ yadi tāvat = If you are sent; indreṇa = by Indra; rāvaṇālayam = to the abode of Ravana; ākhyāhi = tell (me); tattvam = the truth; mā bhūt = let there not be; bhayam = fear; te = for you; mokṣyase = you will be released.

"O monkey! If Indra has sent you to the abode of Ravana, tell me the truth. Do not have fear. You will be released."

Sloka 9

yadi vaiśravaṇasya tvam yamasya varuṇasya ca |
cāru rūpam idam kṛtvā oravuṣṭo naḥ purīmimām || 5-50-9
viṣṇunā preṣito vā api dūto vijaya kānkṣiṇā |

9. tvam = you; praviṣṭaḥ = the one who has entered; imam purim = this city; naḥ = of ours; kṛitvā = assuming; idam = this; chārarūpam = guise of a spy; tvam = (are) you; vaishrvaṇasya = belonging to Kubera the lord of riches; yamasya = or belonging to Yama the god of death; varuṇasya = or belonging to Varuna the Lord of the sea; preṣitovāpi yadi = or whether sent; viṣnunā = by Vishnu; the lord of preservation; dūtaḥ = as a messenger; vijayakāmkṣiṇā = longing for a victory?

"Are you, the one who has entered our city, assuming the guise of a spy, sent by Kubera the god of riches or by Yama the god of death or by Varuna the lord of the sea or whether sent by Vishnu the lord of preservation, as a messenger, longing for a victory?"

Sloka 10

na hi te vānaram tejo rūpa mātram tu vānaram || 5-50-10
tattvataḥ kathayasva adya tato vānara mokṣyase |

10. vānara = O monkey!; te = your; rūpamadtramtu = form alone; vānaram = (is that of) a monkey; tejaḥ = (your) splendour; na hi = is not indeed; vānaram = that of a monkey; adya = ṇow; kathayasva = tell (me); tattvataḥ = actually; mokṣase = you will be released; tataḥ = thereafter.

"O monkey! Your form alone is that of a monkey. Your splendour indeed is not that of a monkey. Now, tell me the truth. Thereupon, you will be released."

Sloka 11

anṛtam vadataḥ ca api durlabham tava jīvitam || 5-50-11
athavā yan nimittaḥ te praveśo rāvaṇa ālaye |

11. tava vadataḥ = If you tell; anṛitam = a lie; jīvitam = your survival; durlabham = will be difficult; athavā = or (tell me); yannimittam = for what purpose; te praveshaḥ = is your entry; rāvaṇālaye = into Ravana's abode."

"If you tell a lie, your survival will be difficult. Or else, tell me the purpose of your entry into Ravana's abode."

Sloka 12

evam ukto hari varaḥ tadā rakṣo gaṇa īśvaram || 5-50-12
abravīn na asmi śakrasya yamasya varuṇasya vā |
dhanadena na me sakhyam viṣṇunā na asmi coditaḥ || 5-50-13
jātiḥ eva mama tu eṣā vānaro aham iha āgataḥ |

12; 13. evan = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; harishreṣṭaḥ = ḥanuma the foremost of monkeys; tadā = then; abravīt = spoke; rakṣogaṇeshvaram = to Ravana the Lord of demons; (as follows); nāsmi = I am not (a messenger); shakrasya = of Indra; yamasya = or of Yama; varuṇasye = or of Varuna; me = to me; na shakhyam = there is no friendship; dhanadena = with Kubera the god of riches; na = nor; choditaḥ = instigated; viṣṇunā = by Vishnu; eṣā = this; mame eva = is just my; jātiḥ = form of existence; aham = I; vānaraḥ = am a monkey; āgataḥ = who came; iha = here.

ḥearing the words of Prahasta, ḥanuma spoke to Ravana the Lord of demons as follows, "I am not a messenger of Indra or Yama or Varuna. I have no friendship with Kubera the god of riches, nor am I instigated by Vishnu. This is just my form of existence, by my very birth. I am a monkey who came here."

Sloka 14

darśane rākṣasa indrasya durlabhe tat idam mayā || 5-50-14
vanam rākṣasa rājasya darśana arthe vināśitam |

14. darshane = the sight; rākṣasendrasya = of the king of demons; durlabhe = difficult to be obtained; taidam = Intent on that; darshanārthe = object of sight; rākṣasarājasya = of the king of demons; vanam = the garden; vināshitam = was destroyed; mayā = by me.

"ṭhe sight of Ravana the King of demons is difficult to be obtained. Intent on that object (of sight of Ravana), the garden was destroyed by me."

Sloka 15

tataḥ te rākṣasāḥ prāptā balino yuddha kānkṣiṇaḥ || 5-50-15
rakṣaṇa artham ca dehasya pratiyuddhā mayā raṇe |

15. te balinaḥ rākṣasāḥ = those strong demons; tataḥ = then; prāptāḥ = came; yuddha kāṇ^kṣiṇah = with a desire to fight; rakṣaṇārtham tu = for the protection; dehasya = of my body; pratiyuddhāḥ = they were attacked; raṇe = in battle; mayā = by me.

"ṭhen, those strong demons came with a desire to fight with me. Just for protecting my body, I had to attack them in battle."

Sloka 16

astra pāśaiḥ na śakyo aham baddhum deva asuraiḥ api || 5-50-16
pitāmahāt eva varo mama api eṣo abhyupāgataḥ |

16. aham = I; na shakyaḥ = am incapable; baddhum = of being bound; astrapāshaiḥ = by missiles and nooses; devāsurairapi = even by celestials and demons; eṣaḥ varaḥ = this boon; abhyupāgataḥ = came; mamāpi = to me too; pitāmahādeva = from Brahma only.

"I am incapable of being bound by missiles and nooses even by celestials and demons. This boon came to me also from Brahama only."

Sloka 17

rājānam draṣṭu kāmena mayā astram anuvartitam || 5-50-17
vimukto aham astreṇa rākṣasaiḥ tu atipīḍitaḥ |
kevacidrājakāryeṇa samprāpto.asmi tavāntikam || 5-50-18

17; 18. mayā = by me; draṣṭukāmena = with a desire to see; rājānam = you the king; astram anuvartitam = the missile was obeyed; mayā = by me; abhipīDitaḥ tu = after being bound; rākṣasaiḥ = by the demons; aham = I; vimuktohi = was indeed released; astreṇa = from the missile; samprāptaḥ asmi = I came; tava antikam = to your vicinity; kenachit rājakāryeṇa = on some king's duty.

With a desire to see you the king, I surrendered to the missile. ṣoon after bound by the demons, I was indeed released from the missile. I came to your presence, prompted by some mission of Rama."

Sloka 19

dūto.ahamiti vijñeyo rāghavasyāmitaujasaḥ |
śrūyatām cāpi vacanam mama pathyamidam prabho || 5-50-19

19. aham = I; vijṇeyaḥ = am to be known; dūtāḥ iti = as a messenger; rāghavasya = of Rama; amitoujasaḥ = possessing an unlimited splendour; prabho = O Lord of demons!; idam = let this; pathyam = beneficial; mama vachām = word of mine; shrūjatām cha = be heard (by you).

"O, Lord of demons! Know me to be a messenger of Rama who is possessing I am unlimited splendour. I am telling you a beneficial word. Listen to it."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe paṅcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 50th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
