Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 85
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana suggests Rama to send Lakshmana to kill Indrajit, even before Indrajit completes the sacrificial fire in the sanctuary of Nikumbhila. Rama then asks Lakshmana to proceed with the battle along with armies of Sugreeva, Hanuma, Jambavan and Vibhishana. Lakshmana proceeds to Nikumbhila along with Hanuma, Vibhishana and Jambavan to kill Indrajit. Lakshmana accompanied by Vibhishana, Angada and Hanuma penetrates that heterogeneous army of the enemies at the sanctuary of Nikumbhila.

Sloka 1

tattasya vacanaṃ śrutvā rāghavaḥ śokakaśmitaḥ |
nopadhārayate vyaktaṃ yaduktaṃ tena rakṣasā || 6-85-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; tasya = of Vibhishana; rāghavaḥ = Rama; shoka kashmitaḥ = agonized with grief; nopadhārayate = could not comprehend; vyaktam = clearly; yat uktam = that which was spoken; tena rakṣhasā = by that demon.

Hearing those words of Vibhishana, Rama, agonized as he was with grief, could not understand clearly what was spoken by that demon.

Sloka 2

tato dhairyamavaṣṭabhya rāmaḥ parapuraṃjayaḥ |
vibhīṣaṇamupāsīnamuvāca kapisaṃnidhau || 6-85-2

2. tataḥ = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; parapuramjayaḥ = the conqueror of the cities of enemies; avaṣhṭabhya = leaning upon; dhairyam = his courage; uvācha = spoke; vibhīṣhaṇam = to Vibhishana; upāsīnam = sitting nearby; kapisamnidhau = and before the presence of the monkeys.

Then, Rama the conqueror of the cities of enemies, leaning upon his courage, spoke to Vibhishana who was sitting nearby and in front of the monkeys (as follows):

Sloka 3

nairṛtādhipate vākyaṃ yaduktaṃ te vibhīṣaṇa |
bhūyastacchrotumicchāmi brūhi yatte vivakṣitam || 6-85-3

3. naiṛitādhipate = O lord of demons!; ichchhāmi = I wish; shrotum = to hear; bhūyaḥ = again; yat vākyam = those words; te = of you; uktam = spoken; ichchhāmi = I desire; shrotum = to hear; bhūyaḥ = again; tat = that; yat = which; te vivakṣhitam brūhi = you intend to tell (me).

"O lord of demons! I wish to hear again those of the words spoken by you. I desire to hear again, whatever you intend to tell me."

Sloka 4

rāghavasya vacaḥ śrutvā vākyaṃ vākyaviśāradaḥ |
yattatpunaridaṃ vākyaṃ babhāṣe.atha vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-85-4

4. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = those words; rāghavasya = of Rama; udāhṛitam = spoken; sudīnavat = very sadly; saḥ vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; vākya vishāradaḥ = who was skilled in expression; punaḥ = again; babhāṣhe = spoke; idam vākyam = the following words; yatnāt = gently.

Hearing those words of Rama, spoken very sadly, that Vibhishana who was skilled in expression, again spoke his words gently as follows:

Sloka 5

yathājñaptaṃ mahābāho tvayā gulmaniveśanam |
tattathānuṣṭhitaṃ vīra tvadvākyasamanantaram || 6-85-5

5. mahābāho = O the long armed!; vīra = O the valiant one!; yathā = how; gulmaniveshanam = the marshalling of the troops; āJṇaptam = was ordered; tvayā = by you; tat = the same; anuṣhṭhitam = was done; tathā = accordingly; tvadvākya samanantaram = and immediately after your words.

"O the long armed hero! Immediately after your orders, the marshalling of the troops was done by me in the manner as enjoined by you."

Sloka 6

tānyanīkāni sarvāṇi vibhaktāni samantataḥ |
vinyastā yūthapāścaiva yathāanyāyaṃ vibhāgaśaḥ || 6-85-6

6. sarvāṇi = all; tāni = those; anīkāni = armies; vibhaktāni = were divided; samantataḥ = and positioned at all places; yūthapashchaiva = even the commanderes of those divisions; vinyastāḥ = wee positioned; vibhāgashaḥ = and distributed; yathānyāyam = as per their ranks.

"All those armies were divided and positioned at all places. Even the commanders of those divisions were arrayed and distributed according to their ranks."

Sloka 7

bhūyastu mama vijñāpyaṃ tacchṛṇuṣva mahāprabho |
tvayyakāraṇasamtapte samtaptahRidayā vayam || 6-85-7

7. mahāprabho = O great lord!; māma = to me; bhūyaḥ viJṇāpyam = there is more to be reported; tat shṛiṇuṣhva = listen to it; tvayi akāraṇa samtapte = while you are lamenting causelessly are lamenting; vayam = we; samtapta hṛidayāḥ = are painful at our hearts.

"O great lord! I have to report to you something further. Listen to it. While you are lamenting without any cause, we feel painful at our hearts."

Sloka 8

tyaja rājannimaṃ śokaṃ mithyāsaṃtāpamāgatam |
tadiyaṃ tyajyatāṃ cintā śatruharṣavivardhinī || 6-85-8

8. rājan = O king!; tyaja = abandon; imam = this; shokam = lamentation; mithyā santāpam = and false grief; āgatam = which came (to you); tat = therefore; iyam chintā = let this worry; shatruharṣhavivardhinī = which augments the pleasure of the adversaries; tyajyatām = be abandoned.

"O king! Abandon this lamentation and false grief which came to you. Let your worry, which augments the pleasure of your adversaries be therefore abandoned."

Sloka 9

udyamaḥ kriyatāṃ vīra harṣaḥ samupasevyatām |
prāptavyā yadi te sītā hantavyāśca niśācarāḥ || 6-85-9

9. vīra = O hero!; te sītā prāptavyā yadi = if you want to get back ṣeetha; nishācharāḥ hantavyāshcha = if the demons are to be destroyed; udyamaḥ kriyatām = raise up for the occasion; harṣhaḥ samupasevyatām = keep yourself cheerful.

"O hero! If you want to get back Seetha and if the demons are to be destroyed, raise up for the occasion. Keep yourself cheerful."

Sloka 10

raghunandana vakṣyāmi śrūyatāṃ me hitaṃ vacaḥ |
sādhvayaṃ yātu saumitrirbalena mahatā vṛtaḥ || 6-85-10
nikumbhilāyāṃ saṃprāptaṃ hantuṃ rāvaṇimāhave |
dhanurmaṇḍalanirmuktairāśīviṣaviṣopamaiḥ || 6-85-11

10; 11. raghunandana = O Rama!; vakṣhyāmi = I will tell you; me hitam vachaḥ = let my benign words; shrūyatām = be heard; ayam saumitriḥ = this Lakshmana; vṛitaḥ mahatā balena = together with a large army; sādhu yātu = has to go immediately; hantum = to kill; rāvaṇim = Indrajit; nikumbhilāyām samprāptam = who reached ṇikumbhila; āṣhīviṣhaviṣhopamaiḥ = with arrows tantamount to serpentine poison; dhanurmaṇ^Dala nirmuktaiḥ = released from a snake-like bow; āhave = in battle.

"O Rama! Listen to my benign words I tell you. This Lakshmana together with a large army should go soon to kill Indrajit, who reached Nikumbhila, with arrows as deadly as the serpentine poison released from his snake-like bow in battle."

Sloka 12

tena vīreṇa tapasā varadānātsvayaṃbhuvaḥ |
astraṃ brahmaśiraḥ prāptaṃ kāmagāśca turaṃgamāḥ || 6-85-12

12. vīreṇa tena = by the valiant Indrajit; tapasā = by his penance; prāptam = was obtained; astram = a weapon; brahmashiraḥ = called Brahmashira; varadānāt = by way of a gift; svayambhuvaḥ = from Brahma; turangamāḥ = and horses; kānngāḥ = coursing at his will.

"By his penance, Indrajit obtained a weapon called Brahmashira, by way of a gift from Brahma and also horses, which are coursing at his will."

Sloka 13

sa eṣa saha sainyena prāptaḥ kila nikumbhilām |
yadyuttiṣṭhetkṛtaṃ karma hatānsarvāṃśca viddhi naḥ || 6-85-13

13. saḥ eṣhaḥ = that Indrajit as such; sainyena saha = along with his army; prāptaḥ kila = is reported to have reached; nikumbhilām = ṇikumbhila; karmakṛitam = after performing his sacrificial act uttiṣhṭhedyadi = if he comes forth; viddhiḥ = know (that); sarvān = all; naḥ = of us; hatān = as destroyed!

"That Indrajit, as such along with his army, is reported to have reached Nikumbhila. If he comes forth after completing his sacrificial act, know that all of us as killed."

Sloka 14

nikumbhilāmasaṃprāptamahutāgniṃ ca yo ripuḥ |
tvāmātāyinaṃ hanyādindraśatro sa te vadhaḥ || 6-85-14
varo datto mahābāho sarvalokeśvareṇa vai |
ityevaṃ vihito rājanvadhastasyaiṣa dhīmataḥ || 6-85-15

14; 15. varaḥ = a boon; dattaḥ = was given; sarva lokeshvareṇa = by Brahma; tasya dhīmataḥ = to that intelligent demon; itvevam = thus; indrajito = O Indrajit!; yaḥ ripuḥ = which enemy; hanyāt = strikes; tvām = you; ātatāyinam = having your bow drawn; asamprāptam = before not arriving; nikumbhilām = at ṇikumbhila; ahutāgnimcha = or before offering your oblations to the sacred fire; saḥ = he alone; vadhaḥ = is the killer; te = to you; mahābāho = O the long armed!; eṣhaḥ = this; vadhaḥ = killing; vihitaḥ = is ordained.

"A boon was given by Brahma to that intelligent demon as follows: "O Indrajit! That enemy of yours, who strikes while you are marching with your bow drawn, and while you have not arrived at Nikumbhila, or even while you have not offered oblations to the sacred fire, will prove to be the cause of your killing."

Sloka 16

vadhāyendrajito rāma saṃdiśasva mahābala |
hate tasmin hataṃ viddhi rāvaṇam sasuhṛdgaṇaṃ || 6-85-16

16. mahābala rāma = O mighty Rama!; samdishasva = give your command; vadhāya = for killing; indrajitaḥ = of Indrajit; tasmin hate = while he is killed; viddhi = know (that); rāvaṇam = Ravana; sa suhṛidgaṇam = along with his associate-troops; hatam = as killed.

"O mighty Rama! Give your command for killing of Indrajit. When he is killed, know that Ravana along with his associate troops also as killed."

Sloka 17

vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā rāmo vākyamathābravīt |
jānāmi tasya raudrasya māyāṃ satyaparākrama || 6-85-17

17. shrutvā = hearing; vibhīṣhaṇa vachaḥ = the words of Indrajit; rāmaḥ = Rama; atha = then; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; satyaparākrama = O Vibhishana of true prowess!; jānāmi = I know; māyām = the conjuring trick; raudrasya tasya = of that terrific demon.

Hearing the words of Indrajit, Rama then spoke the following words:"O Vibhishana of true prowess! I know the conjuring trick of that terrific demon."

Sloka 18

sa hi brahmastravitprājño mahāmāyo mahābalaḥ |
karotyasamjñān saṃgrāme devānsavaruṇānapi || 6-85-18

18. saḥ = that Indrajit; brahmāstravit = knows how to employ Brahma's missile; prājJṇaḥ = intelligent; mahāmāyaḥ = has several conjuring tricks; mahābalaḥ = mighty; karoti = he can make; asamJṇān = unconscious; devānapi = even the celestials; savaruṇān- along with Varuna the king of gods; samgrāme = in battle.

"That Indrajit is skilled in the use of the mystic missile presided over by Brahma. He is intelligent. He is a master of several conjuring tricks. He is so mighty that he can render unconscious in battle even the gods along with Varuna the king of gods."

Sloka 19

tasyāntarikṣe carataḥ sarathasya mahāyaśaḥ |
na gatirjñāyate vīra sūryasyevābhrasaṃplave || 6-85-19

19. mahāyashaḥ vīraḥ = O highly illustrious hero!; tasya = his; gatiḥ = movement; charataḥ = while drifting; antarikṣhaḥ = i the sky; sarathasya = along with his chariot; na Jṇāyate = is not known; suryasyeva = as the movement of the sun (is not known); abhrasamplave = under a dense mass of clouds.

"O highly illustrious hero! While he drifts in the sky, ascending his chariot, his movement is not known to others, as the sun is not seen beneath a dense mass of clouds."

Sloka 20

rāghavastu riporjñatvā māyāvīryaṃ durātmanaḥ |
lakṣmaṇaṃ kīrtisaṃpannamidaṃ vacanamabravīt || 6-85-20

20. jJṇātvā = having known; māyāvīryam = the toughness of the conjuring tricks; durātmanaḥ ripoḥ = of the evil-minded adversary; rāghavaḥ = Rama; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; lakṣhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; kīrti sampannam = endowed with glory.

Having known the toughness of the conjuring tricks of the evil-minded adversary, Rama spoke to the illustrious Lakshmana as follows:

Sloka 21

yadvānarendrasya balaṃ tena sarveṇa saṃvṛtaḥ |
hanūmatpramukhaiścaiva yūthapaiḥ saha lakṣmaṇa || 6-85-21
jāmbavenārkṣapatinā sahasainyena saṃvṛtaḥ |
jahi taṃ rākṣasasutaṃ māyābalasamanvitam || 6-85-22

21; 22. lakṣhmaṇa = O Lakshmana!; samvṛitaḥ = accompanied; tena = by that; sarveṇa = entire; vānarendrasya yat balam = army of ṣugreeva; yūthapashchaiva saha = along with the army = leaders; hanūmatpramukhaiḥ = with ḥanuma as their head; jāmbavena samvṛitaḥ = and accompanied by Jambavan; ṛikṣhapatinā = the lord of bears; sahasainyena = together with his army; jahi = kill; tam rākṣhasasutam = Indrajit; that prince of demons; māyābalasamanvitam = rich in the power of conjuring tricks.

"O Lakshmana! You, accompanied by that entire army which stands at the disposal of Sugreeva, along with troop-commanders with Hanuma as their head; and protected by Jambavan the lord of bears, who will be accompanied by his army, kill that Indrajit the prince of demons, rich in the power of conjuring tricks."

Sloka 23

ayaṃ tvāṃ sacivaiḥ sārdhaṃ mahātmā rajanīcaraḥ |
abhijñastasya māyānāṃ pṛṣṭhato.anugamiṣyati || 6-85-23

23. mahātmā = the great souled; ayam rajanīcharaḥ = Vibhishana; abhiJṇaḥ = the knower; tasya māyānām = of his conjuring tricks; sachivaiḥ sārdham = along with his counsellors; anugamiṣhyati = will follow; pṛiṣhṭhataḥ = behind; tvām = you.

"The great souled Vibhishana, who knows all the conjuring tricks of Indrajit, along with his counsellors, will follow behind you."

Sloka 24

rāghavasya vacaṃ śrutvā lakṣmaṇaḥ savibhīṣaṇaḥ |
jagrāha kārmukaśreṣṭhamatyadbhutaparākramaḥ || 6-85-24

24. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; rāghavasya = of Rama; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; atyadbhuta parākramaḥ = of highly wonderful prowess; savibhīṣhaṇaḥ = along with Vibhishana; jagrāha = took hold; kārmuka shreṣhṭham = of his excellent bow.

Hearing the words of Rama, Lakshmana of highly wonderful prowess, along with Vibhishana, took hold of his excellent bow.

Sloka 25

saṃnaddhaḥ kavacī khaḍgī saśaro vāmacāpabhṛt |
rāmapādāvupaspṛśya hṛṣṭaḥ saumitrirabravīt || 6-85-25

25. hṛiṣhṭaḥ- the rejoicing; saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; samnaddhaḥ = who was prepared for the combat; kavachī = covered with armour; khaDgī = wielding a sword; sa sharaḥ = and arrows; vāmachāpa bhṛit = wearing a bow in his left hand; upaspṛishya = having touched; rāmapādau = Rama's feet; abravīt = spoke (as follows):

The rejoiced Lakshmana who was prepared for the combat, clad in an armour, wielding a sword and arrows, wearing his bow in his left hand and having touched Rama's feet in salutation, spoke as follows:

Sloka 26

adya matkārmukonmuktāḥ śarā nirbhidya rāvaṇim |
laṅkāmabhipatiṣyanti haṃsāḥ puṣkariṇīmiva || 6-85-26

26. adya = today; sharāḥ = the arrows; matkārmukonmuktāḥ = released from my bow; nirbhidya = piercing; rāvaṇim = Indrajit; abhipatiṣhyanti = will drop towards; laṇ^kām = Lanka; hamsāḥ iva = as swans; puṣhkarīm = (descend into) a lotus-pond.

"Today, the arrows released from my bow, piercing Indrajit, will drop into the City of Lanka, as swans descend into a louts-pond.

Sloka 27

adyeva tasya raudrasya śarīraṃ māmakāḥ śarāḥ |
vidhamiṣyanti bhittvā taṃ mahācāpaguṇacyutāḥ || 6-85-27

27. adyaiva = now itself; māmakāḥ sharāḥ = my arrows; mahāchāpaguṇa chyutāḥ discharged from the bow-string of the great bow; bhittvā = splitting; tasya raudrasya sharīram = his terrific body; tam vidhamiṣhyati = will blow him away.

"Now itself, my arrows discharged from the bow-string of this great bow, duly splitting his terrific body, will blow him away."

Sloka 28

evamuktvā tu vacanam dyutimān bhrāturagrataḥ |
sa rāvaṇivadhākāṅkṣī lakṣmaṇastvaritaṃ yayau || 6-85-28

28. evam uktvā = thus speaking; bhrātuḥ agrataḥ = in front of his brother; saḥ dyutimān lakṣhmaṇaḥ = that resplendent Lakshmana; yayau = sallied forth; tvaritam = quickly; rāvaṇi vadhākāṇkṣhī = with an intent to kill Indrajit.

Thus speaking in front of his brother, that resplendent Lakshmana, quickly marched forward, with an intent to kill Indrajit.

Sloka 29

so.abhivādya guroḥ pādau kṛtvā cāpi pradakṣiṇaṃ |
nikumbhilāmabhiyayau caityaṃ rāvaṇipālitam || 6-85-29

29. abhivādya = offering salutation; guroḥ pādau = to his brother's feet; kṛitvā = and doing; pradakṣhiṇam cha = circumambulation too; saḥ = that Lakshmana; abhiyayau = went; chaityam = to the sancctuary; nikumbhilām = called ṇikumbhila; rāvaṇipālitām = protected by Indrajit.

Offering salutation to the feet of his brother and doing circumambulation too, that Lakshmana went to the sanctuary named Nikumbhila, which was protected by Indrajit.

Sloka 30

vibhīṣaṇena sahito rājaputraḥ pratāpavān |
kṛtasvastyayano bhrātrā lakṣmaṇastvarito yayau || 6-85-30

30. kṛita svastyayanaḥ = having obtained blessings; bhrātrā = from his brother; pratāpavān lakṣhmaṇaḥ = the glorious Lakshmana; rājaputraḥ = the prince; tvaritaḥ = quickly; yayau = sallied forth; vibhīṣhaṇena sahitaḥ = along with Vibhishana.

Having obtained the blessings from his brother, the glorious Lakshmana, the prince, quickly sallied forth along with Vibhishana.

Sloka 31

vānarāṇām sāsraistu hanumān bahubhirvaṛtaḥ |
vibhīṣaṇaśca sāmātyo tadā lakṣmaṇamanvagāt || 6-85-31

31. tadā = then; hanumān = ḥanuma; vṛitaḥ = accompanied; bahubhiḥ sahasraiḥ = by many thousands; vānarāṇām = of monkeys; vibhīṣhaṇashcha = and Vibhishana sāmātyaḥ = along with his counsellors; lakṣhmaṇam anvagāt = went behind Lakshmana.

Then, Hanuma accompanied by many a thousand of monkeys and Vibhishana along with his counsellors went behind Lakshmana.

Sloka 32

mahatā harisainyena savegamabhisaṃvṛtaḥ |
ṛkṣarājabalaṃ caiva dadarśa pathi viṣṭhitam || 6-85-32

32. abhisamvṛitaḥ = (ṭhat Lakshmana) surrounded; savegam = quickly; mahatā harisainyena = by a large army of monkeys; dadarsha = saw; ṛikṣharāja balamchaiva = also the army of Jambavan; viṣhṭhitam = stationed; pathi = in the way.

That Lakshmana, quickly surrounded by a large army of monkeys, saw the army of Jambavan also stationed in the way.

Sloka 33

sa gatvā dūramadhvānaṃ saumitrirmitranandanaḥ |
rākṣasendrabalaṃ dūrādapaśyadvyūhamāśritam || 6-85-33

33. gatvā = going; dūram = a long; adhvānam = distance; saḥ saumitriḥ = that Lakshmana; mitranandanaḥ = the delight to his friends; apashyat = saw; rākṣhasendrabalam = the army of Ravana; āshritam = ranged; vyūdham = in battle-array; dūrāt = from a dsitance.

After covering a long distance, that Lakshmana, the delight to his friends, saw the army of Ravana, ranged in battle-array, from a distance.

Sloka 34

sa taṃ prāpya dhanuṣpāṇirmāyāyogamariṃdamaḥ |
tasthau brahmavidhānena vijetuṃ raghunandanaḥ || 6-85-34

34. prāpya = duly arriving at ṇikumbhila; saḥ = raghunandanaḥ = that Lakshmana; arimdamaḥ = the foe-conqueror; tasthau = stood; dhanuṣhpāṇiḥ = bow in hand; vijetum = to conquer; tam = that Indrajit; māyāyogam = possessing conjuring tricks; brahmavidhānena = in conformity with Brahma's ordinance.

Duly arriving at Nikumbhila, that foe-conquering Lakshmana stood, bow in hand, to conquer that Indrajit, the possessor of conjuring tricks, in conformity with Brahma's ordinance.

Sloka 35

vibhīṣaṇena sahito rājaputraḥ pratāpavān |
aṅgadena ca vīreṇa tathānilasutena ca || 6-85-35

35. pratāpavān = the valiant; rājaputraḥ = Lakshmana; (tasthau = stood); sahitaḥ vibhīṣhaṇena = along with Vibhishana; vīreṇa aṇ^gadena = the heroic Angada; tathā = and; anilasutena = ḥanuma; the son of wind-god.

The valiant Lakshmana stood there along with Vibhishana, the heroic Angada and Hanuma, the son of wind-god.

Sloka 36

vividhamamalaśastrabhāsvaraṃ ta |
ddhvajagahanaṃ gahanaṃ mahārathaiśca |
pratibhayatamamamprameyavegaṃ |
timiramiva dviṣatāṃ balaṃ viveśa || 6-85-36

36. vivesha = (Lakshmana) entered; dviṣhatām balam = the army of enemies; vividham = which was of many kinds; amala shastra bhāsvaram = resplendent with spotless weapons; dhvajagahanam = thick with ensigns; mahārathaishcha = and abounded with huge chariots; gahanam = exceedingly formidable; pratibhayatamam = very terrific; aprameya vegam = with unimaginable swiftness; timaramiva = even as one would enter a thick veil of darkness.

Lakshmana penetrated that heterogeneous army of enemies, resplendent with spotless weapons, thick with ensigns and abounded with huge chariots, exceedingly impenetrable, very terrific, with unimaginable swiftness, even as one would enter a thick veil of darkness.
