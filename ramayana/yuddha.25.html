Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 25
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

While Rama crossed over to the other shore of the sea, Ravana instructs his ministers, Suka and Sarana to enter the enemy's army in disguise and to bring all the required information about them. When Suka and Sarana entered the enemy's ranks, Vibhishana recognises and captures them. But Rama sets them free. Going back to Ravana, they explain the might strength of the enemy's ranks and exhort him to restore Seetha to Rama.

Sloka 1

sabale sāgaram tīrṇe rāme daśaratha ātmaje |
amātyau rāvaṇaḥ śrīmān abravīt śuka sāraṇau || 6-25-1

1. rāme = (While) Rama; dasharathātmaje = the son of Dasaratha; tīrṇe = crossed; sāgaram = the ocean; sabale = along; with his army; shrīmān = the illustrious; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; abravīt = spoke; shukasāraṇau = to ṣhuka and sarana; amātyau = his ministers (as follows)

While Rama, the son of Dasaratha, crossed the ocean along with his army, the illustrious Ravana spoke to Shuka and Sarana, his ministers as follows:

Sloka 2

samagram sāgaram tīrṇam dustaram vānaram balam |
abhūta pūrvam rāmeṇa sāgare setu bandhanam || 6-25-2

2. samagram = the entire; bala = army; vānaram = of monkeys; tīraṇam = crossed; dustaram = the impassable; sāgaram = ocean; abhūtapurvam = the unprecedented; setubandhanam = construction of the bridge; sāgare = across the ocean (was done); rāmeṇa = by Rama.

"The entire army of monkeys crossed the impassable ocean. The construction of the bridge, unprecedented, across the ocean was executed by Rama."

Sloka 3

sāgare setu bandham tu na śraddadhyām kathaṃcana |
avaśyam ca api samkhyeyam tan mayā vānaram balam || 6-25-3

3. na shraddhadhyām = I am not able to believe; kathamchana = under any circumstances; tam = that; setubandham = construction of the bridge; sāgare = across the ocean; avashyam = certainly; tat = that; vānaram balam = army of monkeys; samkhyeyamcha = is worth to be conduted (carried weight); mayā = by me.

"I am not able to believe under any circumstances that a pass-way has been constructed across the ocean. Certainly, that army of monkeys is worth to be counted (carried weight) by me."

Sloka 4

bhavantau vānaram sainyam praviśya anupalakṣitau |
parimāṇam ca vīryam ca ye ca mukhyāḥ plavam gamāḥ || 6-25-4
mantriṇo ye ca rāmasya sugrīvasya ca sammatāḥ |
ye pūrvam abhivartante ye ca śūrāḥ plavam gamāḥ || 6-25-5
sa ca setur yathā baddhaḥ sāgare salila arṇave |
niveśaḥ ca yathā teṣām vānarāṇām mahātmanām || 6-25-6
rāmasya vyavasāyam ca vīryam praharaṇāni ca |
lakṣmaṇasya ca vīrasya tattvato jñātum arhatha || 6-25-7

4; 5; 6; 7. bhavantau = both of you; anupalakṣhitau = unperceived (to others); pravishya = enter; sainyam = the army; vānaram = of monkeys; arhathaḥ = and ought; ṇ^ātum = to know; tattvataḥ = actually; parimāṇamcha = the quantum of the army; vīryamcha = their prowess; ye = as to which; plavaṇ^gamāḥ = monkeys; mukhyāḥ = are important ones; ye = which; mantriṇaḥ = ministers; rāmasya = of Rama; sugrīvasya cha = and of ṣugreeva; sangatāḥ = have come together; ye = which; abhivartante = are abiding; pūrvam = in front; ye = which; plavaṇgamā = monkeys; shūrāḥ = are valiant; yathā = how; saḥ = that; setuḥ = bridge; baddhaḥ = was constructed; sāgare = across the ocean; salilārṇave = full of water; yathā = how; nivesham = the encamping (is done); teṣām vānarāṇām = for those monkeys; mahātmānām = the great souled; vyavasāyamcha = the determination; vīryam = the strength; praharaṇānicha = and the striking senses; rāmasya = of Rama; vīrasya = and of the heroic; lakshmaṇasya = Lakshmana.

"Become unrecognisable to others, enter the army of monkeys and make out the real quantum of their army, their prowess, as to which monkeys are important among them, which ministers of Rama and Sugreeva have come together, which monkeys are abiding in front, which monkeys are valiant, how that bridge was constructed across the ocean full of water, how the encamping is done for those great-souled monkeys; the determination, strength and the striking senses of Rama and of the heroic Lakshmana."

Sloka 8

kaḥ ca senā patis teṣām vānarāṇām mahāojasām |
etaj jñātvā yathā tattvam śīghram agantum arhathaḥ || 6-25-8

8. kaḥ = who; senāpatiḥ = is the chief of army; teṣām = of those; mahātmanām = high-souled; vānarāṇām = monkeys; ṇ^ātvā = knowing; tatcha = that also; yathātattvam = actually; shīghram = and quickly; arhathaḥ = you ought; āgantum = to come.

"Knowing also accurately who the chief of Army of those high-souled monkeys is, you have to come back quickly."

Sloka 9

iti pratisamādiṣṭau rākṣasau śuka sāraṇau |
hari rūpa dharau vīrau praviṣṭau vānaram balam || 6-25-9

9. pratipamādiṣṭau = having thus been commanded; vīrau = the valiant; rākṣasau = demons; shukasāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; harirūpadhārau = both possessing the form of monkeys; praviṣṭau = entered; bala = (that) army; vānaram = of monkeys.

Having thus been commanded by Ravana, the valiant demons Shuka and Sarana , both in the disguise of monkeys, entered that army of monkeys.

Sloka 10

tatas tad vānaram sainyam acintyam loma harṣaṇam |
samkhyātum na adhyagaccetām tadā tau śuka sāraṇau || 6-25-10

10. tataḥ = thereafter; tau = those; shuka sāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; tadā = then; nādhyagachchhetām = were not able; samkhyātum = to count; sainyam = the army; tat = of those; vānaram = monkeys; achintyam = which was inconceivable; lomaharṣaṇam = and causing their hair to stand erect.

Shuka and Sarana were not able to count then that inconeviable army of monkeys, causing their hair to stand on end.

Sloka 11

tat sthitam parvata agreṣu nirdareṣu guhāsu ca |
samudrasya ca tīreṣu vaneṣu upavaneṣu ca || 6-25-11
taramāṇam ca tīrṇam ca tartu kāmam ca sarvaśaḥ |
niviṣṭam niviśac caiva bhīma nādam mahābalam || 6-25-12
tadbalārṇavamakṣobhyam dadṛśāte niśācarau |

11; 12. tat = that army; sthitam = was stationed; parvatāgreṣu = on the tops of mountains; nirjhareṣu = round about the water-falls; guhāsucha = in the caves; tīreṣu = on the shores; samudrasya = of the ocean; vaneṣu = in the woodlands; upavaneṣu = and in the gardens; taramāṇam = (It was either in) the process of crossing the ocen; tīrṇam cha = or had crossed it; tartukāmamcha = or was intending to cross it; sarvashaḥ = in its entirety; niviṣṭam = (It had either) encamped; nivishachchaiva = or was still encamping; bhīma nādam = making a terrible noise; nishācharau = the two demons; dadṛiṣāte = saw; mahābalam = that very stong; akṣobhyam = and impeterbable; tadbalārṇavam = sea of army.

That army was stationed on the tops of mountains, round about the waterfalls, in the caves, on the sea-shores, in the woodlands and in the gardens. It was either in the process of crossing the ocean, or was intending to cross it in its entirety. It had either encamped or was still encamping, making a terrible noise. The two demons saw that very strong and imperturbable sea of army.

Sloka 13

tau dadarśa mahātejāḥ praccannau ca vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-25-13
ācacakṣe atha rāmāya gṛhītvā śuka sāraṇau |

13. vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; mahātejāḥ = with great splendour; dadarshau = beheld; tau = both of them; praticchannau = in disguise; saḥ = ḥe; gṛihītva = laid hold of; shuka sāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; ācacakṣe = and said; rāmāya = to Rama (as follows)

Vibhishana with great splendour beheld both of them in disguise. He caught hold of Shuka and Sarana and told Rama as follows:

Sloka 14

tasyaitau rākṣasendrasya mantriṇau śuksāraṇau || 6-25-14
laṃkāyāḥ samanuprāptau cārau para puram jayau |

14. parapuramjaya = O; Rama the conqueror of hostile citadels!; etau = these; shukasāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; mantriṇau = the two mīsters; tasya rākṣasendrasya = of that Ravana; samanuprāptau = and came; cārau = as spies; laṇkāyāḥ = from Lanka.

"O, Rama the conqueror of hostile citadels! These two demons are Shuka and Sarana, the ministers of Ravana and they came here as spies from Lanka."

Sloka 15

tau dṛṣṭvā vyathitau rāmam nirāśau jīvite tadā || 6-25-15
kṛta anjali puṭau bhītau vacanam ca idam ūcatuḥ |

15. tau = both of the them; dṛiṣṭvā = having seen; vāmam = Rama; vyathitau = were trembled; tathā = and ; nirāshau = were without hope; jīvite = for their lives; bhītau = were frightened; chedam = and; ūchatu = spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words; kṛitāṇ^jalipuṭau = having joined their palms.

Both of them, having seen Rama, were trembled with fear, without any hope for their lives and having joined their palms, spoke the following words:

Sloka 16

āvām iha āgatau saumya rāvaṇa prahitāv ubhau || 6-25-16
parijñātum balam kṛtsnam tava idam raghu nandana |

16. raghunandana = O; Rama! ṣaumya = O; excellent man! āvām ubhau = we both; rāvaṇaprahitau = as sent by Ravana; āgatau = came; iha = here; parijJṇātum = to know; idam = this; tat = and that; sarvam = entire; balam = army.

"O, excellent man! O, Rama! We both of us, as sent by Ravana, came here together to know everything about your entire army."

Sloka 17

tayos tad vacanam śrutvā rāmo daśaratha ātmajaḥ || 6-25-17
abravīt prahasan vākyam sarva bhūta hite rataḥ |

17. rāmaḥ = Rama; dasharathātmajaḥ = the son of Dasaratha; rataḥ = who was interested; sarvabhūtahite = in the welfare of all being; shrutvā = after hearing; tat = those; vachanam = words; tayoḥ = of them both; prahasan = simingly; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words.

Hearing their aforesaid words, Rama the son of Dasaratha who was interested in the welfare of all beings, smilingly spoke the following words:

Sloka 18

yadi dṛṣṭam balam kṛtsnam vayam vā susamīkṣitāḥ || 6-25-18
yathā uktam vā kṛtam kāryam candataḥ pratigamyatām |

18. sarvam = (ḥas) the entire; balam = army; dṛiṣṭam = yadi = been seen? vayam susamīkṣitaḥ vā = have we been seen well? kāryam kṛitam vā = has the operation been accomplished; yathoktam = as instructed? pratigamyatām = you may go back; chandataḥ = according to your own wish.

"Has the entire army been observed by you? Have you observed us well? Have you accomplished the task, as entrusted to you? If so, you may go back now according to your free will."

Sloka 19

atha kiṃcidadṛṣṭam vā bhūyastaddraṣṭumarhathaḥ || 6-25-19
vibhīṣano vā kārtsnyena punaḥ saṃdarśayiṣyati |

19. atha = or; adṛiṣṭam vā = even if you have not seen; kimchit = something; arhathaḥ = you ought; draṣṭum = to see; tat = that; bhūyaḥ = also; vā = otherwise; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; samdarṣayati = will show you; punaḥ = again; kārtsnyena = entirely.

"Even if you have not seen something you may see it now also. Otherwise, Vibhishana will show it to you again entirely."

Sloka 20

na cedam graham ṇam prapya bhetavyam jīvitam prati || 6-25-20
vyastaśastrau gṛhītau ca na dūtau vadhamarhataḥ |

20. prāpya = having obtained; idam = this; grahaṇam = seizure; nabhetavyam = you need not fear; jivitam prati = about your life; gṛihītau = the seized; ddutau = messengers; nyastashastrau = without weapons; arhataḥ = deserve; na vadham = no killing.

"You, having been caught, need not fear about your life. The captured messengers holding no weapons indeed deserve no killing."

Sloka 21

praccannau ca vimuñcaemau cārau rātriṃca rāvubhau || 6-25-21
śatrupakṣasya satatam vibhīṣaṇa vikarṣiṇau |

21. vibhiṣaṇa = O; Vibhishana! satatam = always; vikarṣaṇau = draw; a bow-string; shatrupakṣasya = on the opponents; vimuJṇca = set free; ubhau = the two; rātrimaharau = deomns; chārau = who are spies; pracchanau = in disguise.

"O, Vibhishana! Always try to draw a bow-string on the opponents and not on these two demons. Set them both, who are spies in disguise, free."

Sloka 22

praviśya nagarīm laṃkām bhavadbhyām dhanada anujaḥ || 6-25-22
vaktavyo rakṣasām rājā yathā uktam vacanam mama |

22. pravishya = after entering; laṇ^kām nagarīm = the city of Lanka; dhanadānujaḥ = Ravana the younger half-brother of Kubera the god of riches; rājā = and the king; rakṣasām = of demons; vaktavyaḥ = be told; bhavadbhyām = by you; yathoktam = as told; mama vachanam = in my words.

"When you return to the city of Lanka, repeat my words faithfully to Ravana the younger half-brother of Kubera and the king of demons."

Sloka 23

yad balam ca samāśritya sītām me hṛtavān asi || 6-25-23
tad darśaya yathā kāmam sasainyaḥ saha bāndhavaḥ |

23. yat = that; balam = force; samāshritya = on which you relied; hṛitavān asi = when you were taking away; sītām = ṣeetha; darshaya = display; yathākāmam = at your will; tat = that force; sasainyaiḥ = along with your troops; sahabāndhavaḥ = and with your allies.

"That force on which you relied when you were taking away Seetha from me, display that force at your will along with your troops and allies."

Sloka 24

śvaḥ kāle nagarīm laṃkām saprākārām satoraṇām || 6-25-24
rākṣasam ca balam paśya śarair vidhvaṃsitam mayā |

24. shvaḥ = tomorrow; kālye = at break of day; pashya = you will see; laṇ^kā nagarīm = the city of Lanka; saprākārām- with its ramparts; satoraṇām = and arches; balam = (as well as) the army; rakṣasām = of demons; vidhavamsitām = demolised; mayā = by me; shariaḥ = by my arrows.

"Tomorrow, at break of day, you will see my arrows demolish the city of Lanka with its ramparts and arches as well as the army of demons."

Sloka 25

khodham bhīmamaham mokṣye balam dhāraya rāvaṇa || 6-25-25
śvaḥ kāle vajravān vajram dānaveṣv iva vāsavaḥ |

25. rāvaṇa = O; Ravana! shvaḥ = tomorrow; kālye = at break of day; aham = I; mokṣyāmi = shall cast; bhīmam = my terrific; krodham = andger; tvayi = on you; sasainye = with your army; vajram iva = as casting a thunderbolt; dānaveṣu = on demons; vāsavaḥ = Indra the Lord of Celestials; vajravān = wielding the thunder-bolt.

"O, Ravana! Tomorrow, at break of day I will cast my dreadful anger on you and your army as Indra the Lord of celestials wielding a thunderbolt casts the thunderbolt on demons."

Sloka 26

iti pratisamādiṣṭau rākṣasau śuka sāraṇau || 6-25-26
jayeti pratinandyainam rāghavam dharmavatsalam |
āgamya nagarīm laṃkām abrūtām rākṣasa adhipam || 6-25-27

26; 27. iti = thus; pratisamādiṣṭau = commanded; shuka sāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; rākṣasau = the demons; pratinandya = admired; iti = that; jaya = "ṃay you be Victorious!" enam = on this; rāghavam = Rama; dharmavatsalam = who was intent on righteousness; āgamya = reached; laṇ^kām nagarīm = the city of Lanka; abrūtām = and spoke; rākṣasādhipam = to Ravana.

Receiving this command, the two demons, Shuka and Sarana, admiring his justice, cried out: "ṃay you be victorious!", reached the city of Lanka and spoke to Ravana as follows:

Sloka 28

vibhīṣaṇa gṛhītau tu vadha arhau rākṣasa īśvara |
dṛṣṭvā dharma ātmanā muktau rāmeṇa amita tejasā || 6-25-28

28. rākṣaseshvara = O; king of demons! vibhiṣaṇagṛihītau = we were seized by Vibhishana; vadhārtham = with the intention of killing us; rāmeṇa = but by Rama; dharmātmanā = the pious minded; amita tejasā = whose valour is immeasureable; muktau = we were released; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing.

"O, king of demons! We were seized by Vibhishana with the intention of killing us. But, Rama, the pious minded wose valour is immeasurable, seeing us, set us free."

Sloka 29

eka sthāna gatā yatra catvāraḥ puruṣa ṛṣabhāḥ |
loka pāla upamāḥ śūrāḥ kṛta astrā dṛḍha vikramāḥ || 6-25-29
rāmo dāśarathiḥ śrīmāml lakṣmaṇaḥ ca vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
sugrīvaḥ ca mahātejā mahāindra sama vikramaḥ || 6-25-30
ete śaktāḥ purīm laṃkām saprākārām satoraṇām |
utpāṭya samkrāmayitum sarve tiṣṭhantu vānarāḥ || 6-25-31

29; 20; 31. yatra = where; chatvāraḥ = the four; puruṣarṣabhāḥ = eminent persons; lokapālasamāḥ = who are equal to the protector of the worlds; shūrāḥ = the valiant ones; kṛitāstrāḥ = skilled in the use of weapons; dṛiDhavikramāḥ = and of proven prowess; rāmaḥ = Rama; dāsharathiḥ = son of Dasartha; shrīmān = the illustrious; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; mahātejaḥ = the great resplendent; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; sugrīvashca = and ṣugreeva; mahendra samavikramaḥ = whose valour is equal to that of Indra the Lord of celestials; ekasthānagatāḥ = are stationed at the same place; ete = they; utpāṭya = having plucked up; laṇ^kāmpurīm = the city of Lanka; saprākārām = with its ramparts; satoraṇām = and arches; shaktāḥ = are able; samkrāmayitum = to transplant it elswhere; sarve = (even if) all; vānarāḥ = (the) monkeys; tiṣṭhantu = keep aloof.

"Where the four eminent persons who are equal to the protector of the worlds, the valiant ones skilled in the use of weapons and of proven prowess namely Rama the son of Dasaratha, the illustrious Lakshmana, the great resplendent Vibhishana and Sgreeva whose valour is equal to Indra the Lord of celestials, arestationed at the same place, they can pluck up the city of Lanka with its ramparts and arches and transplant it elswhere, even if all the monkeys keep aloof."

Sloka 32

yādṛśam tasya rāmasya rūpam praharaṇāni ca |
vadhiṣyati purīm laṃkām ekas tiṣṭhantu te trayaḥ || 6-25-32

32. tat = such; rāmasya = is Rama's; rūpam = from; yādṛiṣham = and such; praharaṇānicha = are his weapons; ekaḥ = that he can alone; vadhiṣyati = overthrow; laṇ^kāmpurim = the city of Lanka; te trayaḥ = his three companions; tiṣṭhantu = standing by.

"Such is Rama's form and such are his weapons, that he can alone overthrow the city of Lanka, his three other companious standing by."

Sloka 33

rāma lakṣmaṇa guptā sā sugrīveṇa ca vāhinī |
babhūva durdharṣatarā sarvair api sura asuraiḥ || 6-25-33

33. sā vāhinī = that army; rāmalakṣmaṇaguptā = protected by Rama and Lakshmana; sugreeveṇaca = as well as by sugreeva; babhūva = are; durdharṣatarā = exceedingly unconquerable; sarvaiḥ = by all; surāsuraiḥ api = the gods and demons even.

"That army protected by Rama and Lakshmana as well as Sugreeva, are exceedingly unconquerable even by all the gods and demons."

Sloka 34

prahṛṣṭa rūpā dhvajinī vana okasām |
vanaukasām samprati yoddhum iccatām |
alam virodhena śamo vidhīyatām |
pradīyatām dāśarathāya maithilī || 6-25-34

34. samprati = now; dhvajinī = the army; vanaukasām = of the monkeys; mahātmanām = the minghty ones; icchatām = who are inclined; yoddhum = to fight; prahṛiṣṭayodhāḥ = has cheerful warriors; alaim = enough; virodhena = of hte enmity; shamaḥ = (Let) peace; vidhīyatām = be made; pradīyatām = restore; maithilī = ṣeetha; dāsharathāya = to Rama.

"Now, the army of the mighty monkeys, who are readily inclined to fight, consists of cheerful warriors. ṃae peace, insted of enmity with them. Restore Seetha to Rama."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe pañcaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 25th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
