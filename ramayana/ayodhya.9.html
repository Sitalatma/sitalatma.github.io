Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 9
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter describes ṃanthara's success in turning Kaikeyi's mind away from good towards evil.

Sloka 1

evamuktā tu kaikeyī kopena jvalitānanā |
dīrghamuṣṭam viniḥśvasya mantharāmidam abravīt || 9-2-1

1. uktā = having been spoken; evam = thus; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; jvalita ānanā = having a burning face; kopena = with anger; viniḥshvasya = sighed; dīrgham = lengthily; ushhṇam = heatedly; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; mantharām = to ṃanthara.

After hearing those words, Kaikeyi with her face burning with anger, had a long and hot sigh and spoke to ṃanthara as follows:

Sloka 2

adya rāmamitaḥ kṣipraṃ vanaṃ prasthāpayāmyaham |
yauvarājye ca bharataṃ kṣipramevābhiṣecaye || 9-2-2

2. adya = now; aham = I; prasthāpayāmi rāmam = shall make Rama to travel; kshhipram = quickly; vanam = to forest; itaḥ = from here; abhishhechayāmi bharatam cha = I shall also get Bharata anointed; youvarājye = for princely kingdom; kshhiprameva = even quickly.

"Now itself, I shall send Rama quickly to forest. I shall get Bharata anointed for princely kingdom immediately."

Sloka 3

idaṃ tvidānīṃ saṃpaśya kenopāyena manthare |
bharataḥ prāpnuyādrājyaṃ na tu rāmaḥ kathaṃcana|| 9-2-3

3. manthara = O ṃanthara! kena upāyena = what is the means by which; bharataḥ = Bharata; prāpūyāt = will get; rājyam = kingdom; rāmaḥ tu = but Rama; na = (will) not (get); kathamechana = by any method; sampasya = see; idam = this; idānīm = now.

"O ṃanthara! What is the means by which Bharata will get the kingdom but Rama will not get it by any method. Think about it now."

Sloka 4

evamuktā tayā devyā mantharā pāpadarśinī |
rāmārthamupahiṃsantī kaikeyīmidamabravīt || 9-2-4

4. uktā = after being spoken; evam = thus; devyā = by the queen Kaikeyi; mantharā = ṃanthara; pāpadarshinī = the sinful thinker; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi; upahimsantī = destroying; ramārtham = Rama's benefit.

After hearing her words, the sinful thinking ṃanthara, spoke thus to Kaikeyi to destroy Rama's benefit of kingdom.

Sloka 5

hantedānīṃ pravakṣyāmi kaikeyi śrūyatāṃ ca me |
yathā te bharato rājyaṃ putraḥ prāpsyati kevalam || 9-2-5

5. kaikeyi = O Kaikeyi! hanta = (I am) very glad! pravakshhyāmi = I will tell; yathā = how; te putraḥ = your son; bharataḥ = Bharata; kevalam = only; prāpsyati = will get; rājyam = kingdom; me shrūyatāmcha = let I be listened.

"O Kaikeyi! I am very glad. I shall tell you the means by which your son Bharata only will get the kingdom. Listen to it!"

Sloka 6

kiṃ na smarasi kaikeyi smarantī vā nigūhase |
yaducyamānamātmārthaṃ mattastvaṃ śrotumicchasi || 9-2-6

6. kaikeyi = O Kaikeyi! tvam yat ichchhati = you are desiring; shrotam = to hear; mattaḥ = from me; uchyamānam = the thing to be stated; ātmānam = for your benefit; na smarasi kim = are you not remebering it; smarantī = though remembering; nigūhase vā = (are you) hiding?

"Oh, Kaikeyi! You desire to hear from me the means for your benefit. Have you forgotten about it or are you hiding, even if you have remembered it?"

Sloka 7

mayocyamānaṃ yadi te śrotuṃ cchndo vilāsini |
śrūyatāmabhidhāsyāmi śrutvā caitad vidhīyatām || 9-2-7

7. vilāsini = o lady full of charms! chhandaḥ yadi = If it is desired; te = by you; shrotum = to hear; uchyamānam = telling mayā = by me; abhidhāsyāmi = I shall tell you; shrūyatām = let it be heard; shrutvā = after hearing; vidhīyatām etad cha = let it be acted upon also.

"O the lady of charms, Kaikeyi! If you desire to hear this from my mouth, I shall tell you. Listen to me. Let it be acted upon after listening to me."

Sloka 8

śrutvaivaṃ vacanaṃ tasyā mantharāyāstu kaikayi |
kiṃcidutthāya śayanātsvāstīrṇādidamabravīt || 9-2-8

8. shruttvaiva = after hearing; evam = these; vachanam = words; mantharāyāḥ = of ṃanthara; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; utthāya = rose; kiJṇchit = a little; svāstīrṇāt shayanāt = from well laid bed; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these words.

After hearing ṃanthara's words, Kaikeyi slightly rose from the well-laid bed and spoke the following :

Sloka 9

kathaya tvaṃ mamopāyaṃ kenopāyena manthare |
bharataḥ prāpnuyādrajyaṃ na tu rāmaḥ kathaṃcana || 9-2-9

9. manthare = o ṃanthara! tvam = you; kathaya = tell; mama = me; kena upāyena = by what means; bharataḥ = Bharata; prāpnuyāt = will get; rājyam = kingdom; rāmaḥ tu = but Rama; na = (will) not (get); kathamchana = by any count; upāyam = that trick.

"O ṃanthara! Tell me the trick by which Bharata will get the kingdom and Rama will not get it under any circumstances."

Sloka 10

evamuktā tayā devyā mantharā pāpadarśinī |
rāmārthamupahiṃsantī kubjā vacanamabravīt || 9-2-10

10. uktā = after being spoken; evam = thus; tayā devyā = by that queen Kaikeyi; kubjā = the hunch backed; mantharā = ṃanthara; pāpadarshinī = the sinful thinker; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; upahimsantīm = to destroy; rāmārtham = Rama's benefit.

After hearing Kaikeyi's words, the sinful thinking, hunch backed ṃanthara, spoke thus, with an intention to destroy Rama's benefit of kingdom.

Sloka 11

tava daivāsure yuddhe saharājarṣibhiḥ patiḥ |
agacchattvāmupādāya devarājasya sāhyakṛt || 9-2-11
diśamāsthāya vai devi dakṣiṇāṃ daṇḍakān prati |
vaijayantamiti kyātaṃ puraṃ yatra timidhvajaḥ || 9-2-12

11;12. devi = O queen! yuddhe = in a battle; devāsure = between celestials and demons tava patiḥ = your husband; upādāya = taking; tvām = you; rājarshhibhiḥ saha = along with holy kings; agachchhat = went; sāhyakṛiti = to help; devarājasya = king Devendra; puram = to the city; khyātam = famously; vaijayantamiti = called Vaijayanta; yatra = where; timidhvajaḥ = ṭimidhvaja (resided); āsthāya = getting into; dakshhiṇām disham = southern direction; daṇdakān prati = towards forest of Dandaka.

"O Queen! Once upon a time, when there was a battle between celestials and demons, your husband Dasaratha went along with you and other holy kings to help Indra in battle, to a famous city called Vaijayata in which the demon Timidhvaja lived in a southern direction in the forest of Dandaka."

Sloka 13

sa śambara iti khyātaḥ śatamāyo mahāsuraḥ |
dadau śakrasya saṃgrāmaṃ devasaṅighairanirjitaḥ || 9-2-13

13. saḥ mahāsuraḥ = ṭhat great demon; khyātaḥ = famous; shambaraḥ = as shambara; shatamāyaḥ = who had several magic effects; anarjitaḥ = not being defeated; devasanghaiḥ = by celestrial groups; dadau = gave; sangrāmam = battle; shakrasya = to Devendra.

"That great demon famously called shambara, who had several magic effects, defeated all the celestials and conferred battle upon Devendra."

Sloka 14

tasmin mahati saṃgrāme puruṣān kṣatavikṣatān |
rātrau prasuptān ghnanti sma tarasāsādya rākṣasāḥ || 9-2-14

14. tasmin mahati sangrāme = in that great battle; rākshhasāḥ = demons; āsādya = approached; tarasā = by force; ghnantisma = and killed; purushān = men; kshhatavikshhatān = who were beaten by arrows; prasuptān = and those sleeping; rātrau = in the night.

"In that great battle, demons approached and forcefully killed men who were injured by arrows and also those who were sleeping at night."

▼

Comment : It is not a virtuous practice according to scriptures, to kill injured people and defenceless people sleeping at night. Demons do not follow such scriptures.

Sloka 15

tatrākaronmahāyuddhaṃ rājā daśaratha stadā |
asuraiśca mahābāhuḥ śastraiśca śakalīkṛtaḥ || 9-2-15

15. tadā = then; dasharathaḥ rājā = king Dasaratha; akarot = created; mahāyuddham = great battle; tatra = there; mahābāhuḥ = the long armed Dasaratha; shakalīṛitaḥ cha = was made into pieces; shastraiaḥ = with weapons; asuraiḥ cha = by demons.

"Then, king Dasaratha fought a great battle there. With their weapons, demons made into pieces the long armed Dasaratha."

Sloka 16

apavāhya tvayā devi saṃgrāmānnaṣṭacetanaḥ |
tatrapi vikṣataḥ śasraiḥ patiste rakṣitastvayā || 9-2-16

16. devi = O queen! nashhṭa chetanaḥ = Dasaratha; who became unconscious; rakshhitaḥ = was saved; tvayā = by you; apavāhya = by taking him away; sangrāmāt = from battle field; tatrāpi = there also; te patiḥ = your husband; vikshhataḥ = who was beaten; shastraiḥ = by weapons; rakshhitaḥ = was saved; tvayā = by you.

"O Queen! You saved the life of your unconscious husband by taking him away from the battle field. There also, you again saved him, when beaten by demons with their weapons."

Sloka 17

tuṣṭena tena dattau te dvau varau śubhadarśane |
satvayoktaḥ patirdevi yadeccheyaṃ tadā varau || 9-2-17
gṛhṇīyāmiti tattana tadhetyuktaṃ mahātmanā |

17. shubhadarshane = O Kaikeyi with auspicious appearance! tushhṭena = after getting delighted; tena = by him; dattau = were given; te = to you; dvou = two; varau = boons; saḥ patiḥ = that husband; uktaḥ = was told; tvayā = by you; gṛihṇīyām iti = that I shall take; varau = boons; yadā tadā = as and when; ichchheyam = desired by me; uktam = said; tena mahātmanā = by that great souled Dasaratha; tathā iti = let it be; tat = that.

"O Kaikeyi with auspicious appearance! Getting delighted, he gave two boons to you. Then, you said, " I shall ask you whenever I require them". He then agreed to it."

Sloka 18

anabhijñā hyahaṃ devi tvayaiva kathitā purā || 9-2-18
kathaiṣā tava tu snehānmanasā dhāryate mayā |
rāmābhiṣekasaṃbhārānnigṛhya vinivartaya || 9-2-19

18;19. devi = o queen! aham = I; anabhiṇJyāhi = do not know anything about this matter; purā = previously; eshhā kathā = this story; kathitā = was told; tvayaiva = by you only; dhāryate = being kept; mayā = by me; manasā = in mind; snehāt tu = due to friendship; vinivartaya = stop; rāmābhishheka sambhārān = arragnments of Rama's anointment; nigṛihya = by force.

"O Queen! I do not know what had happened there. You yourself told this story previously to me. I am keeping this in my mind because of my friendship with you. Hence, you have to stop, by force, the arrangements being made for Rama's coronation."

Sloka 20

tau varau yāca bhartāraṃ bharatasyābhiṣecanam |
pravrājanaṃ tu rāmasya tvaṃ varṣāṇi caturdaśa || 9-2-20

20. tvam = you; yācha = ask; bhartāram = your husband; tau = those (two); varau = boons; bharatasya = Bharata's abhishhechanam = anointment (and); rāmasya = Rama's; pravrājanam = sending to exile; chaturdasha varshhāṇi = for fourteen years.

"You ask your husband as two boons, anointing Bharata for princely kingdom and sending Rama to exile for fourteen years."

Sloka 21

caturdaśa hi varṣāṇirāme pravrājite vanam |
prajābhāvagatasnehaḥ sthiraḥ putro bhaviṣyati || 9-2-21

21. pravrājite = if you send; rāme = Rama; chaturdashavarshhāṇi = for fourseen years; vanam = to forest; putraḥ = your son; prajābhāvagatasnehaḥ = will get intimate association in the people's hearts; sthiraḥ bhavishhyati = and will become stable.

If you send Rama to forest for fourteen years, your son Bharata will get intimate association in the hearts of the people and will get stabilised in kingdom.

Sloka 22

krodhāgāraṃ praviśyādya kṛdddhevāśvapateḥ sute |
śeṣvānantarhitāyāṃ tvaṃ bhūmau malinavāsinī || 9-2-22

22.asvapetḥ sute = oh; Kaikeyi the daughter of Asvapati! adya = now; pravishya = enter; krodhāgāram = the room of wrath; dṛiddheva = as if angry; sheshhva = and lie down; bhūmau = on floor; anantarhitāyām = without any spread; malinavāsinī = and wearing soiled clothes.

Oh, kaikeyi! Now, enter the room of wrath as if angry with him and lie down on floor without any spread underneath and wearing soiled clothes.

Sloka 23

māsmainaṃ pratyudīkṣethā mācaina mabhibhāṣathāḥ |
rudantī cāpi taṃ dṛṣṭvā jagatyāṃ śokalālasā || 9-2-23

23. dṛishhṭvā = after seeing; tam = that Dasaratha; shokalālasā = be sorrowful; rudantī = and by weaping; māsma pratyudīkshhethāḥ = do not look face to face; enam = at him; māchaiva abhibhāshhatāḥ = nor talk; face to face; enam = to him.

As soon as you see Dasaratha, be sorrowful and keep on weeping, without looking at him and without talking to him face to face.

Sloka 24

dayitā tvaṃ sadā bharturatra me nāsti saṃśayaḥ |
tvatkṛte sa mahārājo viśedapi hutāśanam || 9-2-24

24. tvam = you; sadā = always; dayitā = dear one; bhartuḥ = to husband; nāsti = no; samsayaḥ = doubt; me = to me; atra = about this; saḥ mahārājaḥ = that king; vishedapi = will even enter; hutāshanam = fire; tvatkṛite = for your sake.

You are always dear to your husband. I have no doubt about it. King Dasaratha will even jump into a fire for your sake.

Sloka 25

na tvāṃ krodhayituṃ śaktona kruddhāṃ pratyudīkṣitum|
tava priyārthaṃ rājā hi prāṇānapi parityajet || 9-2-25

25. rājā = the king na shaktaḥ = is not capable; tvām krodhayitum = of making you angry; na = not able; dīkshhitum = to see; kruddhām = you with anger; parityajet hi = he will give up; prāṇānapi = even life; tava priyārtham = for your love.

The king is not capable of making you angry. He is not even able to see you with anger. He will give up his life for your love.

Sloka 26

na hyatikramituṃ śaktastava vākyaṃ mahīpatiḥ |
mandasvabhāve buddhyasva saubhāgyabalamātmanaḥ || 9-2-26

26. manda svabhāve = oh; foolish one! mahīpatiḥ = king; nashaktaḥ hi = will not transgress; tava = your; vākyam = words; buddhyastva = you know; ātmanaḥ = your; saubhāgya balam = abundant strength.

Oh, foolish one! The king will not be able to transgress your word. You realise your abundant strength.

Sloka 27

maṇimuktaṃ suvarṇāni ratnāni vividhāni ca |
dadyāddhaśaratho rājā mā sma teṣu manaḥ kṛthāḥ || 9-2-27

27.dasharathaḥ rājā = king Dasaratha; dadyāt = may give; maṇi muktam = diamonds; pearls; suvarṇāmi = gold; vividhāni = various; ratnānicha = precious stones; mākRithāḥ = do not keep; manaḥ = mind; teshhu = in them.

King Dasaratha may offer you diamonds, pearls, gold and various other precious stones. Do not care for them.

Sloka 28

yau tau daivāsure yuddhe varau dśaratho.adadāt |
tau smāraya mahābhāge so.artho mātvāmatikramet || 9-2-28

28. mahābhāge = oh Kaikeyi; the great fortunate one! smāraya - remind; dasarathaḥ = Dasaratha; daivāsurayuddhe = in battle between celestials; and demons; you - which; tau = those famous; varau = boons; adadāt = were given; tau = those boons; arthaḥ - benefit; mātikramet = not to cross. tvā; = you.

Oh, Kaikeyi, the great fortunate! you remind Dasaratha of those boons given by him to you in that battle between celestials and demons. See that your benefit does not get thwarted.

Sloka 29

yadā tu te varaṃ dadyātsvayamutthāpya rāghavaḥ |
vyavasthāpya mahārājaṃ tamimaṃ vṛṇuyā varam || 9-2-29

29. yadā = when; rāghavaḥ = Dasaratha; svayam = himself; utthāpya = will raise you from the floor; dadyāt = and give; te = you; varam = the boon; mahārajam vyavasthāpya = after making the king steady; vriṇuyāḥ = ask; imam varam = this boon.

Dasaratha himself will raise you from the floor and offer you the boon. Thus , after making him steady, you ask for this boon.

Sloka 30

rāmaṃ pravrājayāraṇye nava varṣāṇi pañca ca |
bharataḥ kriyatāṃ rājā pṛthivyāḥ pārthivarṣabha || 9-2-30

30. pārthivarshhabha = oh; king! pravrājaya = send to exile; rāmam = Rama; aranye = in forest; nava panchacha varshhāṇi = for fourteen years; bharataḥ kriyatām = let Bharata be made; rājā = as king; prithivyāḥ = to this earth.

"Oh, king! send Rama to forest for fourteen years. Let Bharata be made as king of this earth".

Sloka 31

caturdaśa hi varṣāṇi rāme pravrājite vanm |
rūḍhaśca kṛtamūlaśca śeṣaṃ sthāsyati te sutaḥ || 9-2-31

31. rāmaḥ = Rama; pravrājite = having been sent; vanam - to forest; chaturdashavarshhāṇi = for fourteen years; te sutaḥ = your son; rūdhaḥ cha = will stand firmly; kritamūlaḥ cha = having laid roots and ; sthāsyati = will stay; sheshhaṃ = for remaining period.

Rama having been sent to forest for fourteen years, your son will stand firmly, having laid his roots and will stay in power for the remaining period.

Sloka 32

rāmapravrājanaṃ caiva devi yācasva taṃ varam |
evaṃ siddhyanti putrasya sarvārthāstava bhāmini || 9-2-32

32. devi = oh; queen! yāchasva = ask; tam = him; rāmapravrājanamchaiva = of sending Rama to forest also; varam = as a boom; evam = thus; tava = your; putrasya = son's; sarvārthāḥ = all benefits; siddhyanti = will be accomplished.

Oh, Kaikeyi! Ask Dasaratha of sending Rama to forest also as a boon. Thus, your son will accomplish all the desired benefits.

Sloka 33

evaṃ pravrājitaścaiva rāmo.arāmo bhaviṣyati |
bharataśca hatāmitrastava rājā bhaviṣyati || 9-2-33

33. evam = thus; pravrājitaḥ = by sending to exile; rāmaḥ = Rama; bhavishhyati = will become; arāmaḥ = not amiable to people; tava = your; bharatascha = Bharata; bhavishhyati = will become; rājā = king; hatāmitrascha = the enemies having been annihilated.

Thus , by sending Rama to exile, he will not become amiable to people. Your Bharata will become king, after the enemies having been annihilated.

Sloka 34

yena kālena rāmaśca vanātpratyāgamiṣyati |
tena kālena putraste kṛtamūlo bhaviṣyati || 9-2-34
sugṛhītamanuṣyaśca suhṛdbhiḥ sārdhamātmavān |

34. yena kālena = by which time; rāmaḥ cha = Rama; pratyāgamishhyati = returns; vanāt = from forest; tena kālena = by that time; te = your; ātmavān = wise; putraḥ = son; sugṛihīta manushhyaḥ = having drawn people to his fold; suhridbhiḥ = and friends also; bhavishhyati = will become; kṛitamūlaḥ = one by whom roots are established.

When Rama returns from forest, your wise son will accumulate friends and draw people to his fold, thus establishing roots himself.

Sloka 35

prāptakālaṃ nu manye.ahaṃ rājānaṃ vītasādhvasā || 9-2-35
rāmābhiṣekasaṃkalpānnigṛhya vinivartaya |

35. manye = I think; praptakālam tu = that it is the appropriate time; te = to you; vīta sādhvasā = after bequeathing fear; nigrihya = and by insistence; rājānam vimivartaya = make the king turn aside; rāmābhishheka sankalpāt = his desire to anoint Rama.

This is the appropriate time for you. Be fearless and by your insistence, make the king turn aside his effort to anoint Rama.

Sloka 36

anarthamartharūpeṇa grāhitā sā tatastayā || 9-2-36
hṛṣṭā pratītā kaikeyī mantharāmidamabravīt |

36.sā kaikeyī = that kaikeyi; grahitā = having been taught; anartham = unworthy matter; artharūpeṇa = in the form of a worthy matter; tayā = by that ṃanthara; tataḥ = afterwards; hṛisṭā = having delighted; pratītā = and understood; abravīt = spoke; mantharām = to ṃanthara; idam = these words.

Kaikeyi, being brain-washed an unworthy matter as the most worthy matter by ṃanthara, accepted her well and having delighted, spoke to her as follows:-

Sloka 37

sā hi vākyena kubjāyāḥ kiśorīvotpathaṃ gatā |
kaikeyī vismayaṃ prāptā paraṃ paramadarśanā || 9-2-37

37. sā kaikeyi = ṭhat Kaikeyi; paramadarshanā = the good natured; param vismayam gatā = was very much surprised; vākyena = by words of; kubjāyāḥ = the hunch backed ṃanthara; kishorīva = and like a small girl; gatā = got; utpatham = into a wrong path.

Though of right thinking in nature, Kaikeyi was very much surprised to hear ṃanthara's words and like a small girl, got into a wrong path.

Sloka 38

kubje tvāṃ nābhijānāmi śreṣṭhāṃ śreṣṭhabhidhāyinīm |
pṛthivyāmasi kubjānāmuttamā buddhinirṇaye || 9-2-38

38. kubje = oh; ṃanthara the hunch backed! nābhijānāmi = I have not recognised; tvām = you; shreshhṭām = as the best; shreshhṭābhidhayinīm = in telling good things; uttamāsi = you are the best; kubjānām = among the hunch backed; pṛithivyām = on earth; buddhinishchaye = in deciding intellectually.

Oh, ṃanthara! You are telling very good things. Till now, I have not recognised you as this good. You are the best among the hunch-backed on this earth in making intellectual decisions.

Sloka 39

tvameva tu mamārtheṣu nityayuktā hitaiṣiṇī || 9-2-39
nāhaṃ samavabuddhyeyaṃ kubje rājñaścikīrṣitam |

39. kubje = oh; ṃanthara; tvameva = you only; nityayuktā = are always interested; mama = in my; artheshhu = well being; hitaishhiṇī = and wishing my benefit; aham = I; nasamavabuddhyeyam = would not have been aware of; rāGyaḥ = kings's; chikīrshhitam = intending action.

Oh, ṃanthara! you always show interest in my well being and wish for my benefit. But for you, I would not have been aware of the king's intentions.

Sloka 40

santi duḥsaṃsthitāḥ kubjā vakrāḥ paramadāruṇāḥ || 9-2-40
tvaṃ padmamiva vātena sannatā priyadarśanā |

40. santi = there are kubjāḥ = hunch backed persons;duḥ.samsthitāḥ = those with bad body construction; vakrāḥ = crooked ones; paramadāruṇāḥ = extremely dreadful; tvam = you; priyadarshanāḥ = appear lovely; padmamiva = like lotus; samnatā = bent; vatena = by breeze.

There are so many hunch backed persons in the world. Their bodily construction is very bad. They are crooked and dreadful. But you appear lovely, as a lotus flower bent by breeze.

Sloka 41

uraste.abhiniviṣṭaṃ vai yāvat skandhātsamunnatam || 9-2-41
adhastāccodaraṃ śātaṃ sunābhamiva lajjitam |

41. te uraḥ = your chest; abhinivisṭam = appears equally on both sides; samunnatam = and in lofty; yāvatskandāt = upto the shoulders; adhastāt = below it is; sunābham = good movelled; udaramcha = stomach; shātam = is lien; lajjitam iva = as though it is shy.

Your chest is appearing equally on both sides and is loftily extended unto the shoulder. Below it is the stomach with an excellent navel, which is lean as though it is shy of seeing the loftiness of chest.

Sloka 42

paripūrṇaṃ tu jaghanaṃ supīnau ca payodharau || 9-2-42
vimalendusamaṃ vaktramaho rājasi manthare |

42. manthare = oh; ṃanthara! paripurunam = with perfect; jaghanam = hips; supīnou = with well rounded; payodharou = chest; vaktram = face; vimalendu samam = equal to untainted moon; aho = her; rājasi = shining you are!

Oh, ṃanthara! with perfect hips and well rounded breasts and with your face like an untainted moon you are shining well.

Sloka 43

jaghanaṃ tava nirghuṣṭaṃ raśanādāmaśobhitam || 9-2-43
jaṅaghe bh^iśamupanyaste pādau cāpyāyatāvubhau |

43. tava = your; jaghanam = waist; rashanā dāma shobhitam = shining with golden belt; nirghusṭam = is making sound; jaṇ^ghe = leg muscles; bhṛisham = very well; upanyaste = rounded up; ubhou = both; pādou cha = feet also; āyatou = are long.

Oh, ṃanthara! your waist decorated with golden belt is making sound. Your leg-muscles are well rounded and your feet are tall.

Sloka 44

tvamāyatābhyāṃ sakthibhyāṃ manthare kṣaumavāsinī |
agrato mama gacchantī rājahaṃseva bhāsase|| 9-2-44

44. manthare = oh; ṃanthara! tvam = you; rājase = are splendid; rājahamsīva = like a royal swan; gachchhantī = walking; agrataḥ = in front; mama = of me; āyatābhyām = with long; sakthibhyām = thighs; kshouma vāsinī = wearing silk saree.

Oh, ṃanthara! you are splendid like a royal swan walking before me, with long thighs are wearing a silk sari.

Sloka 45

āsanyāḥ śambare māyāḥ sahasramasurādhipe || 9-2-45
sarvāstvayi niviṣṭāstā bhūyaścānyāḥ sahasraśaḥ |

45. yāḥ = which; sahasram māyāḥ = thousand magic effects āsan = lying; shambare = in shambara; asurādhipe = the lord of demons; tāḥ sarvāḥ = all those; bhūyaḥ = and further; sahasrashaḥ = thousands of anyāshcha = others nivisṭāḥ = are lying; tvayi = in you.

In addition to all those thousand magic effects lying in shambara, the lord of demons, another further thousands of magic effects are lying in you.

Sloka 46

tavedaṃ sthagu yaddīrghaṃ rathaghoṇamivāyatam || 9-2-46
matayaḥ kṣatravidyāśca māyāścātra vasanti te |

46. yat = which; tava = your; idam = this; dīrgham = long; sthagu = hunch; āyatam rathaghaṇamiva = looking like long apex of a chariot; atra = in it; vasanti = are dewelling; te = your; matayaḥ = thoughts; kshhatravidyāḥ cha = royal arts; mayāḥ cha = and magic effects.

In your long hunch, which looks like an apex of a chariot, are dwelling your various thoughts, royal arts and magic effects.

Sloka 47

atrate pratimokṣyāmi mālāṃ kubje hiraṇmayīm || 9-2-47
abhiṣikte ca bharate rāghave ca vanaṃ gate |

47. kubje = oh; ṃanthara! rāgahave = Rama; gate = goes to; vanam = forest; bharate cha = and Bharata; abhishikte = gets anointed; pratimokshyāmi = adore; atra = this huch back; mālām = with garland; hiraṇmayīm = made of gold.

Oh ṃanthara! When Rama goes to forest and Bharata gets kingdom, I shall adore this hunch back with a golden garland.

Sloka 48

jātyena ca suvarṇena suviṣṭaptena manthare || 9-2-48
labdhārthā ca pratītā ca lepayiṣyāmi te sthagu |

48. manthara = oh; ṃanthara! labdārthācha = after getting the benefit; pratītācha = and having delighted lapayishhyāmi = I shall anoint; te sthagu = your hunch back; suvisṭaptena = with well refined; suvarṇena = gold; jātyena = of good class.

Oh, ṃanthara! After accomplishing the benefit desired by me, I shall, with delight, get your hunch back anointed with well refined gold of good quality.

Sloka 49

mukhe ca tilakaṃ citraṃ jātarūpamayaṃ śubham || 9-2-49
kārayiṣyāmi te kubje śubhānyabharaṇāni ca|

49.kubje = oh; ṃanthara! kārayishhyāmi = I shall get made; shubhāni = beautiful;ābharaṇāmicha = ornaments; te mukhe = and for your face; shubham = auspicious; tilakam = dots; chitram = of variety; jātarūpamayam = in pure gold.

Oh, ṃanthara! I shall get beautiful ornaments made and for your forehead auspicious dots of variety done of pure gold.

Sloka 50

paridhāya śubhe vastre devateva cariṣyasi || 9-2-50
caṃdramāhvayamānena mukhenāpratimānanā|
gamiṣyasi gatiṃ mukhyāṃgarvayantī dviṣajjane || 9-2-51

50;51. charishhyasi = you will be moving around; devateva = like an angel; paridhāya = wearing; subhe = beautiful; vastre = clothes; gamishhyasi = you will get; mukhyām = important; gatim = position; dvishhajjane = amidst adversaries; garvayantī = with pride; mukhena = with face; apratimānanā = incomparably; āhvayamānena = competing with; chandram = moon.

You will be moving around like an angel, wearing beautiful clothes. You will achieve an important position in the eyes of your adversaries with pride and with your face, incomparably competing with moon.

Sloka 52

tavāpi kubjāyāḥ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitāḥ |
pādau paricariṣyanti yathaiva tvaṃ sadā mama || 9-2-52

52. kubjāḥ = the hunch backed women; sarvābharanṇa bhushhitāḥ = adorned with all ornaments; sadā = always; paricharashhyanti = will do service; tava pādou api = at your feet also; yathaiva = as how; kibjāyāḥ = as hunch backed; tvam = you; mama = to me.

The hunch backed women, adorned with all ornaments, will always do service, at your feet, as how as hunch-backed women, adorned with all ornaments, will always do service at your feet, as how as hunch-backed you are doing it to me.

Sloka 53

iti praśasyamānā sā kaikeyīmidamabravīt |
śayānāṃ śayane śubhre vedyāmagniśikhāmiva || 9-2-53

53.sā = that ṃanthara; iti = in this way; prashasyamānā = being praised; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyim shānām = who was lying; shubhre shayane = in a clean couch;agni sihāmiva = like a flame of five; vedyām = in a sacrificial altar.

ṃanthara in this way being praised spoke these words to Kaikeyi, who was lying in a clean couch like a flame of fire upon the sacrificial altar.

Sloka 54

gatodake setubando na kalyāṇi vidhīyate |
uttiṣṭha kuru kalyāṇaṃ rājānamasudarśaya|| 9-2-54

54. kalyaṇi = Oh Kaikeyi; the auspicious one! gatodake = in a place where water has flown out; stubandhaḥ = coustructing a dam; navidhīyate = is not made; uttisṭa = rise; kuru = do; kalyāṇam = the auspicious act; anudarshaya = show your influence; rājānam = to king.

Oh, Kaikeyi the auspicious one! When water has already flown out, there is no use constructing a dam. Now rise and do this purposeful action. Show your influence to the king.

Sloka 55

tathā protsāhitā devī gatā mantharayā saha |
krodhāgāraṃ viśālākṣī saubhāgyamadagarvitā || 9-2-55
anekaśatasāhasraṃ muktāhāraṃ varāṅganā |
avamucya varārāṇi śubhānyābharaṇāni ca || 9-2-56
tato hemopamā tatra kubjāvākyavaśaṃgatā |
saṃviśya bhūmau kaikeyī mantharāmidamabravīt || 9-2-57

55;56;57. kubjāvākyavashamgatā = by surrendering to the words of the hunch backed ṃanthara; tathā = thus; protsāhitā = having been encouraged; kaikeyī = Kakeyi; vishalākshhī = who was having wide eyes; soubhāgyamadagarvitā = who was proud of intoxicating beauty; varāṇ^ganā = a gifted woman; devī = a queen; avamuchya = removed; muktāhāram = pearl necklace; anekashatasāhasram = worth in lacs varārhaṇi = greatly valued; shubbāni = auspicious; ābharaṇāni = ornaments; gatvā = entered; krodhāgāram = chamber of wrath; mantharayāpaha = along with ṃanthara; tataḥ = afterwards; savishya = lied; bhūmou = on the floor; tatra = there; hemopamā = like gold; abravīt = spoke; mantharām = to ṃanthara; idam = these words.

By surrendering to the words of ṃanthara and having been thus encouraged by her, Kaikeyi who was of wide eyes, who was proud of her intoxicating beauty, who was a gifted woman and a queen, removed pearl necklace worth in lacs and other great valuable auspicious ornaments from her body, entered the chamber of wrath along with ṃanthara, lied on the floor there like a golden wire and spoke to ṃanthara as follows:

Sloka 58

iha vā māṃ mṛtāṃ kubje ṇṛpāyāvedayiṣyasi |
vanaṃ tu rāghave prāptebharataḥ prāpsyati kṣitim|| 9-2-58

58. kubje = oh; ṃanthara! rāghave = Rama; prāpte = having reached; vanamtu = forest; bharataḥ = Bharata; prāpsyasi = shall get; kshhitim = the earth; vā = or ese; āvedayishhyasi = inform; nṛipāya = king Dasaratha; mām = about me; mṛitām mām = as dead person; iha = here.

"After Rama's going to the forest, Bharata shall get the kingdom. Or else, you inform the king that I died here itself".

Sloka 59

na suvarṇena me hyartho na ratnairna ca bhūṣaṇaiḥ |
eṣa me jīvitasyānto rāmo yadyabhiṣicyate || 9-2-59

59.suvarṇena = gold; na arthaḥ = is of no use; me = to me; na = nor; ratnaiḥ = diamonds; na = nor; bhūshhaṇaiḥ cha = ornaments; ramaḥ abhishhichyate yadi = If Rama gets anointed for kingdom; eshhaḥ = that itself; antaḥ = is end; me jīvitasya = of my life.

Neither gold nor diamonds nor ornaments are of any use to me. on which day Rama gets anointed for the kingdom, the same day it will be the end of my life.

Sloka 60

atho punastāṃ mahiṣīṃ mahīkṣito |
vacobhiratyartha mahāparākramaiḥ |
uvāca kubjā bharatasya mātaraṃ |
hitaṃ vaco rāmamupetya cāhitam || 9-2-60

60. atho = afterwards; kubjā = ṃanthara; punaḥ = again; uvācha = spoke; tām bharatasya mātaram = to that mother of Bharata; mahishhīm = and wife; mahīkshhitaḥ = of king; vachobhiḥ = who was talking; atyarthamahāparakramaiḥ = very seriously; vachaḥ = the words; hitam = whcih are beneficial to her; ahitam = but not beneficial; rāmam upetya = in relation to Rama.

Afterwards, that ṃanthara again spoke to the seriously talking mother of Bharata that is Kaikeyi the following words which were beneficial to her and not beneficial in relation to Rama.

Sloka 61

prapatsyate rājyamidaṃ hi rāghavo |
yadi dhruvaṃ tvaṃ sa sutā ca tapsyase |
ato hi kalyāṇi yatasva tattathā |
yathā sutaste bharato.abhiṣekṣyate || 9-2-61

61. rāghavaḥ prapatsyate yadi = If Rama gets; idam = this; rājyam = kingdom; tvam = you; sasutā = along with your son; tapsyase = get destroyed; dhruvam = it is certain; kalyāṇi = oh; the auspicious one! ataḥ = hence; yathā = in what way; te sutaḥ = your son; bharataḥ = Bharata; abhishhekshhyate = will get anointed ; yatasva = try; tathā = in that way; tat = that action.

If Rama becomes king, you and your son will be destroyed. Oh, Kaikeyi of auspicious qualities! Hence you try for coronation of your son, Bharata

Sloka 62

tathātividdhā mahiṣi tu kubjayā |
samāhatā vāgiṣubhirmuhurmuhuḥ |
vidhāya hastau hṛdaye.ativismitā |
śśaṃsa kubjāṃ kupitā punaḥ punaḥ || 9-2-62

62. samāhatā = having hit; tathā = thus; vāgishhubhiḥ = by arrows of words; kubjayā = by the hunch backed ṃanthara; muhurmuhuḥ = again and again; mahishhī = Kaikeyi; viddhā = felt sad; kupitā = angry; ativismitā = was very much surprised; vidhāya = keeping; hastou = hands; hṛidaye = on heart; shamshasa = praised; kubjām = the hunch backed one; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again.

Having thus hit by arrows of words employed by ṃanthara again and again, Kaikeyi felt sad, was angry with the king, by keeping hands on her heart, was surprised with the cleverness of ṃanthara and praised her time and again.

Sloka 63

yamasya vā māṃ viṣayaṃ gatāmito |
niśāmya kubje prativedayiṣyasi |
vanaṃ gate vā sucirāya rāghave |
samṛddhakāmo bharato bhaviṣyati || 9-2-63

63. kubje = oh; ṃanthara; prativedayishhyasivā = either you have to inform; nishāmya = having seen; mām = me; gatām = going; yamasya vishayam = to the world of death; itaḥ = from here; bharataḥ bhavishhyativā = or Bharata becomes; samṛiddha kāmaḥ = fulfiller of desire; rāghave gate = when Rama left for ; vanam = forest.

If Rama does not go to forest from here, I do not long for any mattresses or garlands or sandal paste or eye-liner or drink or food or even the loaf here.

Sloka 64

ahaṃ hi vai nāstaraṇāni na srajo |
na candanaṃ nāñjanapānabhojanam |
na kiṃcidicchāmi na ceha jīvitaṃ |
na cedito gacchati rāghavo vanam || 9-2-64

64. rāghavaḥ na gachchhatichet = If Rama does not go to forest; itaḥ = from here; aham = I; na ichchhāmi = do not desire for; kinchit = any; āstaraṇāmi = mattresses; na = nor; chandanam = sandal paste; na = nor; anjanam = eye-liner; na = nor; pāna bhojanam = drink or food; na = nor; jīvitamcha = life even.

If Rama does not go to forest from here, I do not long for any mattresses or garlands or sandal paste or eye-liner or drink or food or even the life here.

Sloka 65

athaitaduktvā vacanaṃ sudāruṇaṃ |
ndhāya sarvābharaṇāni bhāminī |
asaṃvṛtāmāstaraṇena medinīṃ |
tadādhiśiśye patiteva kinnarī || 9-2-65

65. bhaminī = that Kaikeyi; uktvā = uttering; etat vachanam = these words; sudāruṇam = which are cruel; atta = afterwards; nidhāya = removing and keeping at one place; sarvābharaṇāni = all ornaments; tadā = then; adhishishye = lied down; medinīm = on floor; asamvritām = uncovered; āstaraṇena = by mattress; patitua kinnarī iva = like a fallen angel called kinnara.

Kaikeyi, thus uttering cruel words, having removed all ornaments, lied on the floor without a mattress like a fallen angel.

Sloka 66

udīrṇasaṃrambhatamovṛtānanā |
tadāvamuktottamamūlyabhūṣaṇā |
narendrapatnī vimanā babhūva sā |
tamovṛtā dyauriva magnatārakā || 9-2-66

66.sā = Kaikeyi; narendra patnī = wife of the king; avanuktottamamūlya bhūshaṇā; having removed valued ornaments; udīrṇasamrambhatamovṛitānanā = having her face covered by the darkness of extreme anger; vimanāḥ = with depressed mind; babhūva = was; dyouriva = like sky; magnatārakā = with missing stars; tamovṛitā = surrounded by gloom.

Kaikeyi, having removed all adornments, having her face covered by the darkness of extreme anger and with her depressed mind, looked like a starless sky in a gloomy night.

- - -

|| ityārṣe srimadrāmayaṇe ādikāvye ayodhya kāṇde navama sargaḥ ||

Thus completes the ninth sarga of Ayodhya Kanda of Srimad Ramayana.
