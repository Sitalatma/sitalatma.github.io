Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

Introduction

Seetha, on beholding Rama and Lakshmana, was absorbed in various thoughts like, "The sooth sayers had prophesied that I should never be widowed and would bear sons. How could their prediction become untrue?" However, Trijata reassures her, stating good reasons for Rama and Lakshman to be still alive and cheering her up, takes her back to Ashoka grove.

Sloka 1

bhartāram nihatam dṛṣṭvā lakṣmaṇam ca mahā balam |
vilalāpa bhṛśam sītā karuṇam śoka karśitā || 6-48-1

1. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; nihatam = the slain; bhartāram = husband; mahābalam = and the exceedingly strong; lakshmaṇamcha = Lakshmana; sītā = ṣeetha; shoka karshitā = was emaciated with grief; bhṛisham = very much; vilalāpa = and lamented; karuṇtam = pitiably.

Seeing her husband and the mighty Lakshmana having been killed, Seetha was very much emaiated through grief and lamented pitiably (as follows):

Sloka 2

ūcur lakṣaṇikā ye mām putriṇy avidhavā iti ca |
te asya sarve hate rāme ajnānino anṛta vādinaḥ || 6-48-2

2. ye = which; lākṣaṇikāḥ = interpretors of marks or signs ( the sooth-sayers); ūchuḥ = predicted; mām = me; putriṇī = to be having children; avidhaveti cha = and without widowhood; te = those; jJṇāninaḥ = knowledgeable persons; adya = now; anṛitavādinaḥ = (are proved to be) liers; rāme = (since) Rama; hate = has been killled.

"The sooth sayers, reading the marks and signs on my body, prophesized that I shall bear sons and never be widowed. Now that Rama has been slain, their words have proved to be untrue."

Sloka 3

yajvano mahiṣīm ye mām ūcuh patnīm ca satriṇaḥ |
te adya sarve hate rāme ajnānino anṛta vādinaḥ || 6-48-3

3. rāme = ṣince Rama; hate = is slain; sarve = all; te jJṇāninaḥ = those astrologers; ye = who; ūchuḥ = predicted; mām patnīm = that I should be the companion; sattriṇaḥ = of a sattra sacrifice; mahiṣīm = and the consort; yajvanaḥ = of the performer of great sacrifices; adya = now; anṛitavādinaḥ = have become utterers of falsehood.

"Since Rama is slian, all those astrologers, who predicted that I shold be the companio of a sattra sacrifice and the consort of the performer of great sacrifices, now have proved to be utteres of falsehood."

Sloka 4

vīra pārthiva patnī tvam ye dhanyā iti ca mām viduḥ |
te adya sarve hate rāme ajnānino anṛta vādinaḥ || 6-48-4

4. adya = now; rāme = that Rama; hate = has been slain; sarve = all; te = those; jṇāninaḥ = sooth-sayers; ye = who; viduḥ = predicted; bhartṛipūjitām = that I should be honoured by my husbaned; vīrapārthiva patnīnām = and by the wives of warriors and kings; anṛita vādinaḥ = are proved not to have spoken truly.

"Now that Rama has been slain, all those sooth-sayers who predicted that I should be honoured by my husband as well as the wives of warriors and kings, are proved to be liars."

Sloka 5

ūcuḥ saṃśravaṇe ye mām dvijāh kārtāntikāh śubhām |
te adya sarve hate rāme ajnānino anṛta vādinaḥ || 6-48-5

5. adya = now; rāme = that Rama; hate = has beeen slain; sarve = all; te = those; jJṇāninaḥ = astrologers; kārtānikāḥ dvijāḥ = among the wise brahmins; ye = who; samshravaṇe = openly; ūchuḥ = foretold; shubham = happiness; māmi = for me; anṛitavādinaḥ = are proved to have spoken falsely!;

"Now that Rama has been slain, all those astrologers among the wise brahmins. Who openly foretold that I should remain happy with my husband are proved to have spoken falsely!"

Sloka 6

imāni khalu padmāni pādayor yaih kila striyaḥ |
adhirājye abhiṣicyante nara indraih patibhih saha || 6-48-6

6. padmāni khalu = by the marks of lotus indeed; imāni = on these; pādayoḥ = my feet; yaiḥ = by which; kulastriyaḥ = hight-born women; abhiṣichyante = are consecreated; ādhirājye = for an empire; patibhiḥ saha = with their husbands; narendraḥ = and kings.

"Yet I bear the marks of lotus on my soles by virtue of which high-born women are consecrated on an imperial throne with their husbands and lords."

Sloka 7

vaidhavyam yānti yair nāryo alakṣaṇair bhāgya durlabhāḥ |
na ātmanas tāni paśyāmi paśyantī hata lakṣaṇā || 6-48-7

7. na pashyāmi = I do not find; tāni = tohse; alakṣaṇaiḥ = marks of ill-fortune; yaiḥ = which; yānti = betoken; vaidhavyam- widowhood; nāryaḥ = in women; bhāgya durlabhāḥ = who are ill-starred; pashyantī = and as I examine; hata lakṣaṇā = all the auspicious signs appear to be rendered void; ātmanaḥ = for me.

"I do not find those marks of ill-fortune which betoken widowhood in women who are ill-starred and as I examine, all the auspicious signs appear to be rendered void for me."

Sloka 8

satyāni imāni padmāni strīṇām uktvāni lakṣaṇe |
tāny adya nihate rāme vitathāni bhavanti me || 6-48-8

8. lakṣaṇaiḥ = (ṭhese) marks; padmāni = of the lotus; uktāni = said to be; satyanāmāni = good angury; strīṇām = for women; (by the wise men); tāni = those marks; me = of mine; bhavanti = have become; vitathāni = meaningless; adya = now; rāme = that Rama; nihate = is slain.

"Those marks of the lotus said to be good angury for women by wise men, have become meaningless, now that Rama is slain."

Sloka 9

keśāh sūkṣmāh samā nīlā bhruvau ca asamgate mama |
vṛtte ca alomaśe janghe dantāś ca aviralā mama || 6-48-9

9. mam = my; keshāḥ = hair; sūkṣmāḥ = is fine; nīlāḥ = black in clour; samāḥ = and smooth; bruvoucha = my eye-brows too; asamhate = are dis-united; mama = my; jaṇ^ghe = shanks; vṛitte = are well-rounded; aromake = and hair-less; dantāshcha = my teeth; aviralāḥ = are contiguous.

"ṃy hair is fine, black in colour and smooth. ṃy eye-brows are disunited. ṃy shanks are hair-less and well rounded. ṃy teeth are contiguous, without any gaps between them."

Sloka 10

śankhe netre karau pādau gulphāv ūrū ca me citau |
anuvṛttā nakhāh snigdhāh samāś ca angulayo mama || 6-48-10

10. mama = ṃy; shaṇkhe = temples; netre = eyes; karau = arms; pādau = feet; gulphau = ankles; ūru = thighs; samau = are homogenous; chitau = and wll-proportioned; aṇgulayaḥ = the fingers; anuvṛittanakhāḥ = have well-rounded nails; snigdhāḥ = which are glossy; samāshcha = and having a right length.

"ṃy temples, eyes, arms, feet, ankles and thights are homogenous and well-proportioned. ṃy fingers have well-rounded and glossy nails, having a right lenght."

Sloka 11

stanau ca aviralau pīnau mama imau magna cūcukau |
magnā ca utsanginī nābhih pārśva uraskam ca me citam || 6-48-11

11. māmakau = my; stanau = breasts; aviralau = are close to each other; pīnau = fully developed; magna chūchukau = and have depressed nipples; nābhiḥ = my navel; magnā = is deeply; utsedhinī = indented; me = my; pārshvoraskamcha = flanks and bossom; chitam = are well-formed.

"ṃy breasts are close to each other, fully developed and have depressed nipples. ṃy navel is deeply idented. ṃy flanks and bossom are well-formed."

Sloka 12

mama varṇo maṇi nibho mṛdūny anga ruhāṇi ca |
pratiṣṭhitām dvadaśabhir mām ūcuh śubha lakṣaṇām || 6-48-12

12. mama = my; varṇaḥ = complexion; maṇinibhaḥ = has the sheen of a pearl; aṇga ruhāṇicha = the hair on my skin; mṛidūni = are soft; ūchuḥ = It is said; mām = of me; as endowed with auspicious signs; pratiṣṭhitām = in that I touched the ground; dvādashabhiḥ = with my twelve limbs ( vix. the ten toes and two soles).

"ṃy complexion has the sheen of a pearl. The hair on my skin are soft. It is said of me as endowed with auspicious signs, in that I touched the ground with my twelve limbs (viz. the ten toes and two soles).

Sloka 13

samagra yavam accidram pāṇi pādam ca varṇavat |
manda smitā ity eva ca mām kanyā lakṣaṇikā viduḥ || 6-48-13

13. kanyālakṣaṇikāḥ = those who interpret the marks of youthful maidens; viduḥ = spoke; mām = of me; iti = that; pāṇipādamcha = my hands and feet; varṇavat = are rosy; samagrayavam = fully provided with marks resembling a barley-corn; achchhidram = devoid of space between the fingers and toes; mandasnitā = and my smile is gentle.

"Those who interpret the marks of youthful maidens spoke of me that my hands and feet are rosy, fully provided with marks each resembling a barley corn, devoid of space between my fingers and toes and that my smile is gentle."

Sloka 14

adhirājye abhiṣeko me brāhmaṇaih patinā saha |
kṛta anta kuśalair uktam tat sarvam vitathī kṛtam || 6-48-14

14. tat sarvam = all that; uktam = was told; brāhmaṇaiḥ = by brahmins; kṛitānta kushalaiḥ = who were skilled in sooth-saying; me = of me; ādhi rājyābhiṣekaḥ = that I should be conscrated on the imperial throne; patinā saha = along with my husband; vitathīkṛitam = proved to be in vain.

"All that was told by brahmins well-versed in sooth-saying, of me that- I should be consecrated on the imperial throne along with my husband -proved to be in vain."

Sloka 15

śodhayitvā jana sthānam pravṛttim upalabhya ca |
tīrtvā sāgaram akṣobhyam bhrātarau goṣpade hatau || 6-48-15

15. shodayitvā = having searched; janasthānam = all the resorts of men; upalabhyacha = received; pravṛittim = the tidings of my fate; tīrtvā = and crossed; akṣobhyam = the impassable; sāgaram = ocean; bhrātaram = those two brothers; hatau = have perished; goṣpade = in the imprint of a cows hoof (a small brothers)

"Having searched all the resorts of men, received the tidings of my fate, and crossed the impassable ocean, those two brothers have perishe din the imprint of a cow's hoof ( a small puddle)."

Sloka 16

nanu vāruṇam āgneyam aindram vāyavyam eva ca |
astram brahma śiraś caiva rāghavau pratyapadyatām || 6-48-16

16. rāghavau = Rama and Lakshmana; pratya padyatām nanu = surely knew the use of; vāruṇam = the arrows of Varuna; āgneyam = Agni; aindram = Indra; vāyavyameva cha = and Vayu; brahmashiraḥ astram cha = as also the Brahmashira weapon.

"Rama and Lakshmana surely knew the use of the arrows of Varuna, Agni, Indra and Vayu, as also the Brahmashira weapon."

Sloka 17

adṛśyamānena raṇe māyayā vāsava upamau |
mama nāthāv anāthāyā nihatau rāma lakṣmaṇau || 6-48-17

17. māyayā = by means of magic arts; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; mama = my; nāthau = protectors; vāsavopamau = who are equal to Indra; nihatau = have been slain; adṛishya mānena = by an invisible foe; raṇe = in battle; anāthāyāḥ = and I am now bereft of any support.

"By means of magic arts, an invisible foe has slain Rama and Lakshmana, my protectors, who are equal to Indra in battle and I am now bereft of any support."

Sloka 18

na hi dṛṣṭi patham prāpya rāghavasya raṇe ripuḥ |
jīvan pratinivarteta yady api syān mano javaḥ || 6-48-18

18. prāpya = having reached; dṛiṣṭipatham = the range of sight; rāghavasya = of Rama; raṇe = in a combat; na ripuḥ = no enemy; pratinivartate hi = could return; jīvam = alive; syādyadyapi = even though; manojavaḥ = (he were endowed with) the swiftness of thought.

"Having reached the range of sight of Rama in a combat, no enemy could return alive, eventhough he were endowed with the swiftness of thought."

Sloka 19

na kālasya atibhāro asti kṛta antaś ca sudurjayaḥ |
yatra rāmah saha bhrātrā śete yudhi nipāthitaḥ || 6-48-19

19. nāsti = there is no; atibhāraḥ = great burden; kālasya = for death; yatra = as; rāmaḥ = Rama; bhrātrā saha = along wtih his brother; shete = are lying; nipātitaḥ = struck down; yudhi = on the battle-field; kṛitāntashcha = fate; sudurjayaḥ = is inexorable.

There is no burden too heavy for death to shoulder, as Rama along with his brother are lying struck down on the battle-field. Fate is inexorable."

Sloka 20

na aham śocāmi bhartāram nihatam na ca lakṣmaṇam |
na ātmānam jananī ca api yathā śvaśrūm tapasvinīm || 6-48-20

20. na shochāmi = I do not repent; rāma = for Rama; lakṣmaṇam cha = or Lakshmana; mahāratham = the great chariot-warīor; na = nor; ātmānam = for myself; na = nr; jananīm chāpi = my mother even; yathā tathā = by so much; shashrūm = as my mother-īn-law; tapasvinīm = the unfortunate one.

"I do not repent so much for Rama and Lakshmana or for myself or even my mother but for my unfortunate mother-in-law, Kausalya."

Sloka 21

sā hi cintayate nityam samāpta vratam āgatam |
kadā drakṣyāmi sītām ca rāmam ca saha lakṣmaṇam || 6-48-21

21. sā tu = Kausalya for her part; nityam = forever; chintayate = remains absorbed in the thought; kadā = when; drakṣyāmi = shall I see; lakṣmaṇamcha = Lakshmana; sītāmcha = and ṣeetha; sarāghavam = with Rama; āgatam = returned (to Ayodhya); samapta vratam = having completed his vow ( of remaining in exile in the forest for fourteen years)?

"Kausalya for her part, forever remains absorbed in the thought 'When shall I see Lakshmana and Seetha with Rama returned (to Ayodhya) having completed his vow (of remaining in exile in the forest for fourteen years)?"

Sloka 22

paridevayamānām tām rākṣasī trijaṭā abravīt |
mā viṣādam kṛthā devi bhartā ayam tava jīvati || 6-48-22

22. trijaṭā = ṭrijata; rākṣasī = the demoness; abravīt = spoke; tām = to that ṣeetha; paridevayamānām = who was thus lamenting (as follows); devi = O; god-like lady!; mā kṛithāḥ = do not; viṣādam = despair; tava = your; bhartā = husband; ayam = this Rama; jīvati = is living.

The demoness Trijata spoke to that lamenting Seetha as follows : "O, the god-like lady! Do not despair. Your husband, Rama is still living."

Sloka 23

kāraṇāni ca vakṣyāmi mahānti sadṛśāni ca |
yathā imau jīvato devi bhrātarau rāma lakṣmaṇau || 6-48-23

23. devī = O godlike lady!; vakṣyāmi = I will tell; mahānti = mighty; sadṛishāni = and cogent; kāraṇānicha = reasons; yathā = how; imau = these; bhrātarau = two brothers; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; jīvataḥ = are living.

"O, godlike lady! I will tell you mighty and cogent reasons, how these two brothers Rama and Lakshmana are living."

Sloka 24

na hi kopa parītāni harṣa paryutsukāni ca |
bhavanti yudhi yodhānām mukhāni nihate patau || 6-48-24

24. patau = ṭhe leader; nihate = having been lost; mukhāni = the faces; yodhānām = of the soldiers; yudhi = on the field of battle; na bhavanti hi = are never seized; kopaparītāni = by anger; harṣapryutsukāni = (nor are they) animated with joy.

"The leader having been lost, the faces of the soldiers on the field of battle are never seized by anger nor are they animated with joy."

Sloka 25

idam vimānam vaidehi puṣpakam nāma nāmataḥ |
divyam tvām dhārayen na idam yady etau gaja jīvitau || 6-48-25

25. vaidehi = O; ṣeetha!; idam vimānam = this aerial car; divyam = celestial as it is; puṣpakam nāma called Pushpaka; nāmataḥ = by name; na dhārayat = would not have brought; tvām = you; (here); yadi etau = if those two heroes; gata jīvitau = have lost their lives.

"O, Seetha! This aerial car called Pushpaka by name, celestial as it is, would not have brought you here, if those two heroes have lost their lives."

Sloka 26

hata vīra pradhānā hi hata utsāhā nirudyamā |
senā bhramati samkhyeṣu hata karṇā iva naur jale || 6-48-26

26. senā = an army; hata vīra pradhānā = that sees its valiant commander fall; gatotsāhā = is bereft of courage; bhramati = and wanders; nirudyamā = aimlessly about; samkhyeṣu = on the battle field; nauḥ iva = like a ship; hata karṇā = which has broken its rudder; jale = in water.

"An army that sees its valiant commander falll, is bereft of courage and wanders aimlessly about on the battle field, like a ship which has broken its rudder in water."

Sloka 27

iyam punar asambhrāntā nirudvignā tarasvinī |
senā rakṣati kākutsthau māyayā nirjitau raṇe || 6-48-27

27. iyam = this; senā punaḥ = army for its part; asambhrāntā = is neither confused; nirudvignā = nr perturbed; rakṣati = and is guarding; kākutsthsau = the two scious of kakutstha; Rama and Lakshmana; niveditau = this is being pointed out; mayā = by me; prītyā = on account of my affection for you; tapasvini = O lady given to austerities!.

"This army for its part is neither confused nor perturbed and is guarding Rama and Lakshman. I am pointing this out to you on account of my affection for you, O lady given to austerities!"

Sloka 28

sā tvam bhava suvisrabdhā;anumānaih sukha udayaiḥ |
ahatau paśya kākutsthau snehād etad bravīmi te || 6-48-28

28. bhava = be; savisrabdhā = reassured; sukhodayaiḥ = by these auspicious; anumānaiḥ = omens; pashya = behold; kākutsthau = Rama and Lakshmana the scions of kakutstha; ahatau = who are not dead; bravīmi = I am telling; te = you; etad = this; snehāt = on account of my affection for you.

"You as such, be reassured by these auspicious omens. Behold Rama and Lakshman who are not dead. I am telling you this, on account of my affection for you."

Sloka 29

anṛtam na ukta pūrvam me na ca vakṣye kadācana |
cāritra sukha śīlatvāt praviṣṭā asi mano mama || 6-48-29

29. maithili = O; ṣeetha!; anṛitam = falsehood; na ukta pūrvam = has not been uttered before; me = by me; nacha rakṣyami = nor do I utter too now; praviṣṭā asi = you have entered; mam = my; mamaḥ = mind; chāritrasukha shīlatvāt = by your conduct and natural gaiety.

"O, Seetha! I have not spoken falsehood before, nor shall I ever do so. By your conduct and natural gaiety, you have found a place in my heart!"

Sloka 30

na imau śakyau raṇe jetum sa indrair api sura asuraiḥ |
etayor ānanam dṛṣṭvā mayā ca āveditam tava || 6-48-30

30. surāsurairapi = even celestials and demons; sendraiḥ = along with Indra; na shakyau = are unable; jetum = to defeat; imau = these two heroes; raṇe = in battle; dṛiṣṭvā = having observed; tādṛishan = such; darshanam = a sight; āveditam = it was communicated; tava = to you; mayā = by me.

"Even celestials and demons along with Indra cannot vanquish there two heroes in battle. This is what I have observed and communicated to you."

Sloka 31

idam ca sumahac cihnam śanaih paśyasva maithili |
nihsamjnāv apy ubhāv etau na eva lakṣmīr viyujyate || 6-48-31

31. maithili = O ṣeetha!; pashyasva = see; idam = this; sumahat = a very great; chitram = marvel!; (see how); patitau = fallen; sharaiḥ = (under those) shafts; visamjṇ^au = and deprived of their senss; lakṣmīḥ = their beauty; naiva muṇehati = has not desrted; etau = them.

"O Seetha!; See this, a great marvel !; See how, fallen under those shafts and deprived of their senses, thier beauty has not deserted them."

Sloka 32

prāyeṇa gata sattvānām puruṣāṇām gata āyuṣām |
dṛśyamāneṣu vaktreṣu param bhavati vaikṛtam || 6-48-32

32. prāyeṇa = generally; vaktreṣu = the faces; puruṣāṇām = of men; gata sattvānām = who lost their lives; gatāyuṣām = and whose vital power has vanished; bhavati = with be; dṛishyamāieeṣu = appearing; param = with an appalling; vaikṛitam = alteration.

"Generally, the faces of those who lost their lives and whose vital power has vanished, will be appearing with an appalling alteration".

Sloka 33

tyaja śokam ca duhkham ca moham ca janaka ātmaje |
rāma lakṣmaṇayor arthe na adya śakyam ajīvitum || 6-48-33

33. janakātmaje = O; ṣeetha!; tyaja = Banish; shokam = your grief; duḥkham cha = pain; mohamcha = and ignorance; rāmalakṣmaṇayoḥ arthe = on account of Rama and Lakshman; na shakyam = It is impossible; adya = now 9with the splendour in their faces); (they) ajīvitum = do not live.

"O, Seetha! Banish your grief, pain and ignorance on account of Rama and Lakshmana. It is impossible, now with the splendour seen in their faces, for Rama and Lakshmana to die."

Sloka 34

śrutvā tu vacanam tasyāh sītā sura sutā upamā |
kṛta anjalir uvāca idam evam astv iti maithilī || 6-48-34

34. shrutvā = hearing; tasyāḥ = her; vachanam = words; sīta = ṣeetha; surasutopamā = who resembled a daughter of gods; maithilī = but the daughter of ṃithila kingdom; kṛitāṇjaliḥ = with joined palms; uvācha = exclaimed; iti emām = in this way; evam astu = may it be so."

Hearing her words, Seetha, the daughter of ṃithila kingdom resembling a daughter of gods, with joined palms exclaimed, "ṃay it be so."

Sloka 35

vimānam puṣpakam tat tu samivartya mano javam |
dīnā trijaṭayā sītā lankām eva praveśitā || 6-48-35

35. samnivartya = after duly sending back; tat = that; puṣpakam vimānam = aerial car; Pushpaka; manojavam = sorrowfu; sītā = ṣeetha; pravashitā = was caused to enter; laṇ^kāmeva = Lanka once more; tirjaṭayā = by ṭrijata.

After sending back that aerial car, Pushpaka which was as swift as the thought, the sorrowful Seetha was caused by Trijata to enter Lanka once more.

Sloka 36

tatas trijaṭayā sārdham puṣpakād avaruhya sā |
aśoka vanikām eva rakṣasībhih praveśitā || 6-48-36

36. tataḥ = thereafter; sā = ṣeetha; trijaṭayā sārdham = along with ṭrijata; avaruhya = who descended; puṣpakāt = from Pushpaka the aerial car; praveshitāa = were caused to enter; ashokavanikāmeva = Ashoka grove again; rākṣasībhiḥ = by the female demons.

Thereafter, Seetha along with Trijata who descended from Pshpaka the aerial car, were caused by the female demons, to enter the Ashoka grove again.

Sloka 37

praviśya sītā bahu vṛkṣa ṣaṇḍām |
tām rākṣasa indrasya vihāra bhūmim |samprekṣya saṃcintya ca rāja putrau |
param viṣādam samupājagāma || 6-48-37

37. pravishya = entering; tām = that Ashoka grove; bahuvṛikṣaṇDām = planted with a multitude of trees; vihāra bhūmim = the sporting ground; rākṣasendrasya = of Ravana; samchintya cha = recalling; rājaputrau = the two princes; samprakṣya = who she had just seen; sītā = ṣeetha; upāja = gave way; param viṣādam = to extreme grief.

Entering that Ashoka grove, planted with a multitude of trees, sporting ground of Ravana and recalling the two princes who she had just seen, Seetha gave way to extreme grief.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyane ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe aṣṭacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 48th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
