Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 44
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana sends Jambumali, son of Prahasta, to capture Hanuma. Jambumali attacks Hanuma with his numerous arrows. Hanuma hurls a big rock towards Jambumali, but it fails to kill him. Then, Hanuma throws a big sal tree towards Jambumali and the latter succumbs to it.


Sloka 1 Verse Locator

saṃdiṣṭo rākṣasa indreṇa prahastasya suto balī |
jambu mālī mahādamṣṭro nirjagāma dhanuḥ dharaḥ || 5-44-1
rakta mālya ambara dharaḥ sragvī rucira kuṇḍalaḥ |
mahān vivṛtta nayanaḥ caṇḍaḥ samara durjayaḥ || 5-44-2
dhanuḥ śakra dhanuḥ prakhyam mahat rucira sāyakam |
visphārayāṇo vegena vajra aśani sama svanam || 5-44-3

1; 2; 3. jambumālī = Jambumali; mahādamṣṭraḥ = the one with great tusks; dhanurdharaḥ = wielder a bow; raktamālyāmbaradharaḥ = the one who wears crimson garlands and clothes; sragvī = wearing a chaplet; ruchikuṇDalaḥ = the one who wears charming ear-rings; mahān = having a gigantic body; vivṛttanayanaḥ = having round and open eyes; chaṇṭDaḥ = a demon causing diseases; balī = a strong demon; prahastasya sutaḥ = and the so of Prahasta; sandiṣṭaḥ = commanded; rākṣasendreṇa = by Ravana; nirjagāma = started; visphārāṇaḥ = stretching; vegena = with rashness; mahat dhanuḥ = his big bow; shakradhanuḥ prakhyam = resembling a rain bow; ruchirasāyakam = with splendourous arrows; vajrāshanisamaprabham = which brilliance was similar to that of lightning and produced a sound like that of a thunder.

Jambumali, the demon with great tusks, wielding a bow, donning crimson garlands and clothes, wearing beautiful chaplet and charming ear-rings, possessing a gigantic body, having round and open eyes, the demon causing diseases, unconquerable in battle, a strong demon and the son of Prahasta, commanded by Ravana, started stretching with rashness his big bow resembling a rain bow garnished splendorous arrows and which brilliance was similar to that of a lightening and produced a sound like that of a thunder.

Sloka 4

tasya visphāra ghoṣeṇa dhanuṣo mahatā diśaḥ |
pradiśaḥ ca nabhaḥ caiva sahasā samapūryata || 5-44-4

4. mahatā visphāra ghoṣeṇa = the tumultuous sound out of the twanging; tasya dhanuṣaḥ = of that bow; sahasā = soon; samapūryata = filled up; dishaḥ = the quarters; pradishashchaiva = the intermediate points; nabhashchaiva = and even the sky.

ṭhe tumultuous sound created out of the twanging of that bow, soon filled up the quarters, intermediate points and the sky.

Sloka 5

rathena khara yuktena tam āgatam udīkṣya saḥ |
hanūmān vega sampanno jaharṣa ca nanāda ca || 5-44-5

5. udīkṣye = seeing; tam = him; āgatam = who arrived; rathena = by a chariot; kharayuktena = yoked with donkeys; saḥ hanumān = that ḥanuma; vega sampannaḥ = endowed with swiftness; jaharṣa cha = was thrilled with joy; nanādacha = and made a noise too.

ṣeeing him, arriving by a chariot yoked with donkeys, that ḥanuma who was endowed with swiftness, was thrilled with joy and made a noise too.

Sloka 6

tam toraṇa viṭankastham hanūmantam mahākapim |
jambu mālī mahābāhuḥ vivyādha niśitaiḥ śaraiḥ || 5-44-6

6. mahābāhuḥ = the long-armed; jambumālī = Jambumali; vivyādha = transfixed; nishitaiḥ = sharp; sharaiḥ = arrows; tam hanumantam = into that ḥanuma; mahākapim = the great monkey; toraṇa viṭaṇ^kastham = who was standing on the top of the archy door-way.

ṭhe long-armed Jambumali transfixed sharp arrows into that great monkey, ḥanuma who was standing on the top of the archy door-way.

Sloka 7

ardha candreṇa vadane śirasi ekena karṇinā |
bāhvoḥ vivyādha nārācaiḥ daśabhiḥ tam kapi īśvaram || 5-44-7

7. vivyādha = (ḥe) transfixed; tam kapīshvaram = that ḥanuma; the leader of monkeys; vadane = in the face; ardha chandreṇa = with an arrow with a crescent-shaped head; shirasi = on the head; ekena karṇinā = with an arrow with an ear-shaped top; bāhvoḥ = and in the arms; dashabhiḥ = with ten; vārāchaiḥ = arrows made fully of steel.

ḥe transfixed ḥanuma the leader of monkeys in the face with an arrow with a crescent-shaped head, on the head with an arrow having an ear-shaped top and in the arms with ten steel arrows.

Sloka 8

tasya tat śuśubhe tāmram śareṇa abhihatam mukham |
śaradi iva ambujam phullam viddham bhāskara raśminā || 5-44-8

8. tat = that; tāmram = crimson; mukham = face; abhihatam = hit; shareṇa = by an arrow; shushubhe = shone; phullam ambujamiva = like a full-blown lotus; viddham = penetrated; bhāskara rashminā = by the ray of the sun; sharadi = in autumn.

ṭhat crimson face of ḥanuma, hit by an arrow, shone like a full-blown lotus touched by a sun's ray in autumn.

Sloka 9

tattasya raktam raktena rañjitam śuśubhe mukham |
yathākāśe mahāpadmaṃ siktaṃ candanabindubhiḥ || 5-44-9

9. tat = that; mukham = face; tasya = of ḥanuma; raktam = which was naturally crimson in colour; raṇ^jitam = smeared; raktena = with blood; shushubhe = beamed; mahāpadmamyathā = like a large louts; ākāshe = in the sky; siktam = sprinkled; chandana bindubhiḥ = with drops of sandal.

ṭhat face of ḥanuma, which was naturally crimson in colour, smeared with blood, beamed like a large lotus in the sky sprinkled with drops of sandal.

Sloka 10

cukopa bāṇa abhihato rākṣasasya mahākapiḥ |
tataḥ pārśve ativipulām dadarśa mahatīm śilām || 5-44-10

10. mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; bāṇābhihataḥ = struck by the arrows; chukopa = was enraged; rākṣasasya = of the demon; tataḥ = and then; dadarsha = saw; pārshve = by his side; ativipulām shilām = a big rock; mahatīm = of a very large measure.

ḥanuma, struck by those arrows, was enraged with that demon and then saw by his side, a big rock of a very large measure.

Sloka 11

tarasā tām samutpāṭya cikṣepa balavat balī |
tām śaraiḥ daśabhiḥ kruddhaḥ tāḍayāmāsa rākṣasaḥ || 5-44-11

11. samutpāṭya = uplifting; tām = that rock; tarasā = with strength; balī = the strong ḥanuma; chikṣepa = hurled (it); balavat = with force; kruddhaḥ = the enraged; rākṣasaḥ = demon; tāDayāmāsa = struck; tām = that rock; dashabhiḥ = with ten; sharaiḥ = arrows.

Uplifting that rock with strength, the athletic ḥanuma hurled it with force. ṭhe enraged demon then struck back that rock with ten arrows.

Sloka 12

vipannam karma tat dṛṣṭvā hanūmān caṇḍa vikramaḥ |
sālam vipulam utpāṭya bhrāmayāmāsa vīryavān || 5-44-12

12. vīryavān = the powerful; hanumān = ḥanuma; chaṇ^Da vikramah = having terrific prowess; dṛiṣṭvā = in vain; utpāṭya = and having uprooted; vipulam = a very large; sālam = sal tree; bhrāmayāmāsa = whirled it around.

ṣeeing that work in vain, the powerful ḥanuma of terrific prowess, uprooted a very large sal tree and whirled it around.

Sloka 13

bhrāmayantam kapim dṛṣṭvā sāla vṛkṣam mahābalam |
cikṣepa subahūn bāṇān jambu mālī mahābalaḥ || 5-44-13

13. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; mahābalam kapim = the mighty ḥanuma; bhrāmayantam = whirling around; sāla vṛikṣam = the sal tree; mahābalaḥ = the exceedingly strong; jambumālī = Jambumali; chikṣepa = discharged; subahūn = numerous bāṇān = arrows.

ṣeeing the mighty ḥanuma, whirling around the sal tree, the exceedingly strong Jambumali discharged numerous arrows.

Sloka 14

sālam caturbhiḥ ciccheda vānaram pancabhiḥ bhuje |
urasi ekena bāṇena daśabhiḥ tu stana antare || 5-44-14

14. chichheda = (ḥe) splitted; sālam = sal tree; chaturbhiḥ = with four arrows; vānaram bhuje = the arms of ḥanuma; paṇ^chabhiḥ = with five arrows; urasi = in the chest; ekena bāṇena = with one arrow; stanāntare = in the space between the breasts; dashabhiḥ = with ten arrows.

ḥe splitted sal tree with four arrows and struck the arms of ḥanuma with five arrows, in the chest with one arrow and in the space between the breast wit ten arrows.

Sloka 15

sa śaraiḥ pūrita tanuḥ krodhena mahatā vṛtaḥ |
tam eva parigham gṛhya bhrāmayāmāsa vegitaḥ || 5-44-15

15. saḥ = ḥanuma; sharaiḥ pūrita tanuḥ = with his body full of arrows; mahatā krodhena = was highly enraged; gṛihya = and taking; tam parighameva = the same iron rod; vṛitaḥ vegitaḥ = and clasping it swiftly; bhrāmayāmāsa = whirled it around.

ḥanuma, with his body full of arrows, was highly enraged and taking the same iron rod as well as clasping it swiftly, whirled it around.

Sloka 16

ativego ativegena bhrāmayitvā bala utkaṭaḥ |
parigham pātayāmāsa jambu māleḥ mahāurasi || 5-44-16

16. bhrāmayitvā = spinning; parigham = the iron rod; ativegena = with a great speed; balatkaṭaḥ = ḥanuma; richly endowed with strength; ativegaḥ = and highly agitated; pātayāmāsa = threw (it); mahorasi = on the broad chest; jambumālaḥ = of Jamubumali.

ṣpinning the iron rod with a great speed, ḥanuma, richly endowed with strength and highly agitated, threw it on the broad chest of Jamubumali.

Sloka 17

tasya caiva śiro na asti na bāhū na ca jānunī |
na dhanuḥ na ratho na aśvāḥ tatra adṛśyanta na iṣavaḥ || 5-44-17

17. tasya = that Jambumali's; shiraḥ chaiva = head; nāsti = was not; tatra = there; na = now; jānunī = his knees; na = nor; dhanuḥ = his bow; na = nor; rathaḥ = his chariot; nā dṛishyanta = ashvāḥ = nor his horses were seen; na = nor iṣavaḥ = his arrows.

ṭhat Jambumali's head was not there, nor his arms, nor his knees, nor his bow, nor his chariot and nor his horses were seen nor his arrows.

Sloka 18

sa hataḥ tarasā tena jambu mālī mahārathaḥ |
papāta nihato bhūmau cūrṇita anga vibhūṣaṇaḥ || 5-44-18

18. hataḥ = (ṭhus) struck; tena = by ḥanuma; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; jambumālī = Jambumali; chūrṇitān^ga vibhūṣaṇaḥ = with his limbs and ornaments crushed to powder; sahasā = was instantly; nihataḥ = killed; papāta = and fell; bhūmau = on the ground.

ṭhus struck by ḥanuma, the mighty Jambumali, with his limbs and ornaments crushed to powder, was instantly killed and fell dead on the ground.

Sloka 19

jambu mālim ca nihatam kimkarān ca mahābalān |
cukrodha rāvaṇaḥ śrutvā kopa samrakta locanaḥ || 5-44-19

19. shrutvā = hearing; jambumālimcha = Jambumali; nihatam = having been killed; mahābalān kimkarānshcha = as also the might kimbaras; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; chukrodha = was angry; kopa samrakta lochanah = with his red enraged eyes.

ḥearing Jambumali having been killed, as also the mighty Kimkaras, Ravana was angry with his red enraged eyes.

Sloka 20

sa roṣa samvartita tāmra locanaḥ |
prahasta putre nihate mahābale |
amātya putrān ativīrya vikramān |
samādideśa āśu niśā cara īśvaraḥ || 5-44-20

20. mahābale = (ṣince) the mighty; prahastaputre = son of Prahasta; nihate = was killed; saḥ nishāchareshvaraḥ = that Ravana; roṣasamvartita tāmra lochanaḥ = rolling his red eyes with rage; āshu = immediately; samādidesha = ordered; amātya putrān = the sons of ministers; ativīrya vikramān = having too much of strength and prowess; (to go to the battle).

ṣince the mighty son of Prahasta was killed, that Ravana rolling his red eyes with rage, immediately ordered the sons of ministers possessing high strength and prowess, to go to the battle.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe catuścatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 44th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
