Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 19
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Valmiki describes the plight of Seetha in Ashoka garden in detail.


Sloka 1

tasminneva tataḥ kāle rājaputrī tvaninditā |
rūpayauvanasampannaṃ bhūṣaṇottamabhūṣitam || 5-19-1
tato dṛṣṭvaiva vaidehī rāvaṇam rākṣasādhipam |
prāvepata varārohā pravāte kadalī yathā || 5-9-2

1; 2. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; tasmin kāle eva = at that time;vaidehī = ṣeetha; rājaputrī = a princess; aninditā = without any blame; varārohā = of excellent limbs; prāvepata = shook; kadalī yathā = like a plantain tree; pravāte = in wind; dṛishhṭvaiva = on seeing; rāvaṇam = Ravana; rākshasādhipam = the king of Rakshasas; rūpa yauvana sampannam = endowed with beauty and youth; bhūshaṇotāma bhūshhitam = adorned with excellent ornaments.

ṭhereafter at that time ṣeetha a princess without any blame of excellent limbs shook like a plantain tree in wind on seeing Ravana the king of rakshasas endowed with beauty and youth adorned with excellent ornaments.

Sloka 3

ācchādyodaramūrubhyāṃ bāhubhyāṃ ca payodharau |
upaviṣṭā viśālākṣī rudantī varavarṇinī || 5-19-3

3. vishālākshī = ṭhe wide eyed ṣeetha; varavarṇinī = with excellent color; āchchhādya = covering; udaram = stomach; ūrubhyām = with thighs; payodharau = breasts; bāhubhyām = with hands; upavisshṭā = sat down; rudantī = crying.

ṭhe wide eyed ṣeetha with excellent color covering stomach with thighs and breasts with hands sat down crying.

Sloka 4

daśagrīvastu vaidehīm rakṣitāṃ rākṣasīgaṇaiḥ |
dadarśa dīnām duḥkhārtāṃ nāvaṃ sannāmivārṇave || 5-19-4

4. dashagrīvastu = Ravana; dadarsha = saw; vaidehīm = ṣeetha;rakshitām = protected; rākshasīgaṇaiḥ = by batches of ogres; dīnām = (looking) miserable; duḥkhātām = being struck with grief; nāvamiva = like a ship; sannām = in danger; ārṇave = in the sea;

Ravana saw ṣeetha protected by batches of ogres looking miserable being struck with grief like a ship in danger in the sea.

Sloka 5

asaṃvṛtāyāmāsīnām dharaṇyām saṃśitavratām |
cināṃ prapatitāṃ bhūmau śākhāmiva vanaspateḥ || 5-19-5

5. āsīnām = (ḥanuma saw ṣeetha) sitting down;asamvṛitām = on uncovered; dhāraṇyām = land;samṣhitavratān = with a firm resolve; vanaspateḥ shākhāmiva = like a branch of a tree; prapatitām = fallen down; bhumau = on earth; chinnam = being torn (from the tree).

ḥanuma saw ṣeetha sitting down on uncovered land, with a firm resolve, like a branch of a tree fallen down on earth being torn from the tree.

Sloka 6

malamaṇḍanacitrāṅgīm maṇḍanārhāmamaṇḍitām |
mṛṇālī paṅkadigdheva vibhāti na vibhāti ca || 5-19-6

6. malamaṇDana chitrāṇ^gīm = (ḥanuma saw ṣeetha) With a strange body with dirt as an ornament; maṇDanārhām = suitable to decorate with ornaments; amaṇDittam = without any ornaments;vibhāti = shining; mṛiṇālīva = like a lotus stem; paṇ^kadigdhā = being covered with mud; na vibhāti cha = and also not shining

ḥanuma saw ṣeetha with a stange body with dirt as ornament, who although suited to decorate with ornaments was without ornaments. Although She was shining like a lotus stem covered with mud, She was not shining also.

Sloka 7

samīpam rājasimhasya rāmasya viditātmanaḥ |
saṅkalpahayasamyuktairyāntīmiva manorathaiḥ || 5-19-7

yāntīmiva = (ṣeetha) Was as though going;samīpam = near; rāmasya = to Rama; rājasimhasya = a lion among kings; viditātmanaḥ = with a famous personality; manorathaiḥ = with desires as chariot; samkalpahayasamyuktaiḥ = tied with thoughts as horses

ṣeetha was as though going near to Rama a lion among kings with a famous personality with desires as chariot tied with thoughts as horses.

Sloka 8

śuṣyantīm rudatīmekāṃ dhyānaśokaparāyaṇām |
duḥkhasyāntamapaśyantīṃ rāmāṃ rāmamanuvratām || 5-19-8

8. rāmām = (ṣeetha) a pleasant one; shhuṣhyanttim = was emaciated; rudatīm = was crying; ekām = being alone; dhyānashokaparāyaṇām = having surrendered to ḥer thoughts and sorrow; apashyantīm = not seeing; antam = the end; dukhasya = of grief;anuvratām = following the will; rāmam = of Rama.

ṣeetha a pleasant one, was emaciated, was crying, being alone having surrendered to ḥer thoughts and sorrow not seeing the end of grief, following the will of Rama.

Sloka 9

ceṣṭamānāṃ tathāviṣṭāṃ pannagendravadhūmiva |
dhūpyamānām graheṇeva rohiṇīṃ dhūmaketunā || 5-19-9

9. tathā = And also; pannagendravadhūmiva = (ṣeetha) was like great she-serpant; cheshhṭamānām = writhing;āvishhṭām = under the spell of an incantation;rohiṇīmiva = like Rohini; dhūpyamānām = being fumigated; dhūmaketunā grahena = by a smoke colored planet called Ketu.

And also ṣeetha was like a great she-serpant writhing under the spell of an incantation. ṣhe was like Rohini being fumigated by a smoke colored planet called Ketu.

Sloka 10

vṛttaśīlakule jātāmācāravati dhārmike |
punaḥ saṃskāramāpannāṃ jātāmiva ca duṣkule || 5-19-10

10. jātām = Being born; dhārmike = in a virtuous; āchāravati = traditional; vṛitta shīla kule = family with good conduct and character; āpannām = getting; samskāram = married; punaḥ = again; jātāmiva = (ṣeetha was) like being born; dushhkule = in a bad family

Being born in a virtuous traditional family with good conduct and character and getting married, Seetha appeared as if She was born in a bad family.

Sloka 11

abhūtenāpavādena kīrtīm nipatitāmiva |
amnāyānāmayogena vidyāṃ praśithilāmiva || 5-19-11

11. kīrtimiva = (ṣeetha was) like fame;nipatitām = fallen down;abhūtena = (due to) false; apavādena = blame;vidyāmiva = like education; prashithitām = runied; ayogena = (due to) non conformity ;āmnāyānām = (of) learning by heart.

ṣeetha was like fame fallen down due to false blame. ṣhe was like education ruined due to lack of learning by heart.

Sloka 12

sannāmiva mahākīrtiṃ śraddhāmiva vimānitām |
prajñāmiva parikṣīṇāmāśāṃ pratihatāmiva || 5-19-12

12. sannam iva = (ṣeetha was) like deteriorated; mahākīrtim = great fame; shraddāmiva = like faith; vimānitām = insulted; praJṇāmiva = like an understanding; parikshīṇām = grown feeble; āshāmiva = like hope; pratihatām = dulled.

ṣeetha was like deteriorated great fame,like insulted faith,like an understanding grown feeble, like dulled hope.

Sloka 13

āyatīmiva vidhvastāmājñāṃ pratihatāmiva |
dīptāmiva diśaṃ kāle pūjāmapahṛtāmiva || 5-19-13

13. āyatīmiva = (ṣeetha was) like expectation; vidhvastām = destroyed; ājJṇāmiva = like an order; pratihatām = flouted; dīptām dishāmiva = like a blazing quarter; kāle = during a period of destruction; pūjāmiva = like a worship; avahṛitām = stolen;

ṣeetha was like an expectation destroyed, like an order flouted; like a blazing quarter during a period of destruction, like a worship stolen.

Sloka 14

padminīmiva vidhvastāṃ hataśūrāṃ camūmiva |
prabhāmiva tamodhvastāmupakṣīṇāmivāpagām || 5-19-14

14. padminīmiva = (ṣeetha was) like a lotus; vidhvastām = destroyed; chamūmiva = like an army; hata shurām = with killed warriors; prabhāmiva = like light; tamodhvastām = destroyed by darkness; apāgāmiva = like a river; upakshīṇām = vanished.

ṣeetha was like a lotus destroyed, like an army with killed warriors, like light destroyed by darkness, like vanished river.

Sloka 15

vedīmiva parāmṛṣṭāṃ śāntāmagniśikhāmiva |
paurṇamāsīmiva niśāṃ rāhugrastendumaṇḍalām || 5-19-15

15. vedīmiva = (ṣeetha was) like an altar; parāmṛishhṭām = desecrated; agnishikhāmiva = like a tongue of fire; shantām = gone out; paurṇamāsīm nishāmiva = like a full moon night; rāhugrastendumaṇDalām = with moon devoured by Rahu.

ṣeetha was like an altar desecrated, like a tongue of fire gone out, like a full moon night with moon devoured by Rahu.

Sloka 16

utkṛṣṭaparṇakamalāṃ vitrāsitavihaṅgamām |
hastihastaparāmṛṣṭamākulāṃ padminīmiva || 5-19-16

16. padminīmiva = (ṣeetha was) like a lotus pond; ākulām = agitated; utKṛishhṭapaṇakamalām = with torned out leaves and lotuses; hastihastaparāmṛishhṭām = (being) roughly treated by the trunk of an elephant.

ṣeetha was like a lotus pond agitated with torned out leaves and lotuses, being roughly traeated by the trunk of an elephant.

Sloka 17

patiśokāturāṃ śuṣkāṃ nadīṃ visrāvitāmiva |
parayā mṛjayā hīnām kṛṣṇapakṣaniśāmiva || 5-19-17

17. patishokāturām = Grieved for husband; nadīmiva = (ṣeetha was) like a river; shushhkām = dried up; visrāvitām = (with water) caused to flow out; hīnām = lacking; parayā mṛijayā = best washing; kṛishhṇa paksha nishāmiva = like a new moon night.

Grieved for husband, ṣeetha was like a river which has dried uo with water caused to flow out lacking best washing, was like a new moon night.

Sloka 18

sukumārīm sujātāṅgīṃ ratna garbhagṛhocitām |
tapyamānāmivoṣṇena mṛṇālīmaciroddhṛtām || 5-19-18

18. sukumārīm = (ṣeetha was) a delicate one; sujātaṇ^gīm = with well built limbs; ratnagarbhagṛihochitām = accustomed to houses full of diamonds; tapyamānām = being tormented; ushhṇena = by heat; mṛiṇālīmiva = like a lotus fibre; achiroddhṛitām = uprooted short time back.

ṣeetha was a delicate one with well built limbs, accustomed to houses full of diamonds, being tormented by heat, like a lotus fibre uprooted short time back.

Sloka 19

gṛhītāmālditāṃ stambhe yūthapena vinākṛtām |
niḥśvasantīṃ suduḥkhārtāṃ gajarājavadhūmiva || 5-19-19

19. gajarājavadhūmiva = (ṣeetha was) Like a she-elephant; gṛihītām = captured; āLitām = tied; stambhe = to a pillar; kṛitena = made; yūdhapena vinā = (to be) without the leader of herd; niḥshvasantīm = sighing; suduḥkhartām = very much tormented by grief.

ṣeetha was like a she-elephant captured and tied to a pillar, made to be without the leader of herd, sighing, very much tormented by grief.

Sloka 20

ekayā dīrghayā veṇyā śobhamānāmayatnataḥ |
nīlayā nīradāpāye vanarājyā mahīmiva || 5-19-20

20. dīrghayā = with a long; ekayā = single; veṇyā = plait;shobhamānām = shining; ayatnataḥ = without effort; mahīmiva = (ṣeetha) was like the earth; nīlayā vanarājyā = with dark groups of trees; nīradāpāye = at the end of rainy season.

With a long single plait, shining without effort, ṣeetha was like the earth with dark groups of trees at the end of rainy season.

Sloka 21

upavāsena śokena dhyānena ca bhayena ca |
parikṣīṇāṃ kṛśāṃ dīnāmalpāhārāṃ tapodhanām || 5-19-21

21. parikshīṇām = wasted; kRishām = emaciated; upavāsena = due to fasting; shokena = due to sorrow; dhyānena = due to thought; bhayena cha = and due to fear; alpāhārām = (ṣeetha) was with limited food; tapodhanām = (and) with austerity as wealth.

Wasted, emaciated due to fasting, due to sorrow, due to thought and due to fear, ṣeetha was with limited food and with austerity as wealth.

Sloka 22

āyācamānām duḥkhārtāṃ prāñjaliṃ devatāmiva |
bhāvena raghumukhyasya daśagrīvaparābhavam || 5-19-22

22. devatāmiva = (ṣeetha was) like a celestial being; duḥkhārtām = tormented by grief; bhavena = by thought; prāṇ^jalim = with joined palms; ayāchamānām = desiring; dashagrīva parābhavam = defeat of Ravana; raghu mukhyasya = (at the hands of) ṣri Rama.

ṣeetha was like a celestial being, tormented by grief and by thought with joined palms, desiring the defeat of Ravana at the hands of Sri Rama

Sloka 23

samīkṣamāṇāṃ rudatīmaninditāṃ |
supakṣmatāmrāyataśuklalocanām |
anuvratāṃ rāmamatīva maithilīṃ |
pralobhayāmāsa vadhāya rāvaṇaḥ || 5-19-23

23. rāvanaḥ = Ravana; pralobhayāmāsa = (was trying) to allure; vadhāya = (and threatening) death ( to ṣeetha) ;samīkshamāṇām = who was looking at every direction; rudatīm = crying; aninditām = without blame; supakshmatāmrāyata shuklalochanām = with good eye lids and reddish and white eyes;atīva anuvratām = with an excessive vow to follow; rāmam = ṣri Rama;

Ravana was trying to allure and threatening death to ṣeetha who was looking at every direction, crying, without blame, with good eye lids and reddish and white eyes, with an excessive vow to follow Sri Rama.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ekonaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 19th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
