Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 23
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter describes the ogre women speaking to Seetha about her marrying Ravana.


Sloka 1

ityuktvā maithilīṃ rājā rāvaṇaḥ śatrurāvaṇaḥ |
saṃdiśya ca tataḥ sarvā rākṣasīrnirjagāma ha || 5-23-1

1. rājā = the king; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; shatrurāvaṇaḥ = who makes enemies to cry; iti uktvā = thus speaking; maithilīm = to ṣeetha; tataḥ = thereafter; saṃdishya = also ordering; sarvāḥ = all; rākshasīḥ = ogre women; nirjagāma = went away.

ṭhe king Ravana who makes enemies to cry thus speaking to ṣeetha thereafter also ordering all ogre women went away.

Sloka 2

niṣkrante rākṣasendra tu punarantaḥpuraṃ gate |
rākṣasyo bhīmarūpāstāḥ sītāṃ samabhidudruvuḥ || 5-23-2

2. rākshasendre = Ravana; nishhkrānte = leaving; gate = obtaining; punaḥ = again; antaḥpuram = gynaeceum; tāḥ rākshasyaḥ = those ogres; bhīmarūpāḥ = with horrible appearances; samabhidudruvuḥ = went running; sītām = to ṣeetha.

After Ravana left and obtained gynaeceum, those ogres with horrible appearances went running to Seetha.

Sloka 3

tataḥ sītāmupāgamya rākṣasyaḥ krodhamūrcitāḥ |
paraṃ puruṣayā vācā vaidehīmidamabravan || 5-23-3

3. tataḥ = thereafter; upāgamya = reaching; sītām = ṣeetha; rākshasyaḥ = ogre women; krodhamūrchitāḥ = swooned with anger; vāchā = (with a) speech; param = (which was) very; parushhayā = harsh; abruvan = spoke; idam = this (word); vaidehīm = to ṣeetha.

ṭhereafter reaching ṣeetha ogre women swooned with anger and with a very harsh speech spoke these words to Seetha.

Sloka 4

paulastyasya variṣṭhasya rāvaṇasya mahātmanaḥ |
daśagrīvasya bhāryātvaṃ sīte na bahumanyase || 5-23-4

4. sīte = O ṣeetha!; na bahumanyase = you are not respecting; bhāryātvam = becoming wife; rāvaṇAsya = of Ravana; paulastasya = born in the family of Paulastya; varidhhṭhasya = best among men; mahātmanaḥ = a great soul; dashagrrivasya = with ten heads;

"O ṣeetha! You are not respscting becoming wife of Ravana born in the family of Paulastya, best among men, a great soul with ten heads."

Sloka 5

tatasvaekajaṭā nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt |
amantyra krodhatāmrākṣī sītāṃ karatalodarīm || 5-23-5

5. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; ekajaṭā nāma = named Ekajata; krodhatāmrākshī = with red eyes due to anger; āmantrya = calling; sītām = ṣeetha; karatalodarīm = with stomach that can be held by a palm of the hand; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named Ekajata with red eyes sue to anger calling ṣeetha with stomach that can be held by palm of the hand, spoke these words.

Sloka 6

prajāpatīnām ṣaṇṇaṃ tu caturtho yaḥ prajāpatiḥ |
mānaso brahmaṇaḥ putraḥ pulastya iti viśrutaḥ || 5-23-6

6. aṇṇām prajāpatīnām = among the six Prajapathis; yaḥ = whichever; putraḥ = son;chaturthaḥ prajapatiḥ = the fourth Prajapati; mānasaḥ = born by the thought; brahmaṇaḥ = of Lord Brahma; vishrutaḥ = (he is) famous; pulastyaḥ iti = as Pulastya.

"Among th six Prajapatis, whichever son, the fourth Prajapati, born by the thought of Lord Brahma ,he is famous as Paulastya."

Sloka 7

pulastyasya tu tejasvī mahārṣirmānasaḥ sutaḥ |
nāmnā sa viśravā nāma prajāpatisamaprabhaḥ || 5-23-7

7. nāmnā = By the name; vishravā nāma = famous as Visrava; prajāpati samaprabhaḥ = with a radiance equalling that of Lord Brahma; saḥ tejasvī maharshhiḥ = that glorious sage; sutaḥ = is the son; mānasaḥ = (born) from the thought; pulastasya = of Pulastya.

By the name, famous as Visrava, with a radiance equalling that of Lord Brahmathat glorious sage is the son born from the thought of Pulastya.

Sloka 8

tasya putro viśālākṣi rāvaṇaḥ śatrurāvaṇaḥ |
tasya tvaṃ rākṣasendrasya bhāryā bhavitumarhasi || 5-23-8
mayoktaṃ cārusarvāṅgi vākyam kiṃ nāmamanyase |

8. vishākshī = O wide eyed one!; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; shatrurāvaṇaḥ = who makes enemies to cry; tasya = is his (Visrava's); putraḥ = son; tvam = you; arhasi = are suited; bhavitum = to become; tasya = Ravana's; bhāryā = wife; chārusarvāṇgī = O one with all beautiful limbs!; kim = why; nānumanyase = will you not agree; vākyam = words; uktam = spoken; mayā = by me.

"O wide eyed one! Ravana who makes enemies to cry, is Visrava's son. You are suited to become Ravana's wife. O one with all beautiful limbs! Why will you not agree words spoken by me."

Sloka 9

tato harijaṭā nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt || 5-23-9
vivartya nayane kopānmārjārasadṛśekṣaṇā |

9. tataḥ = ṭhereafter;rākshasī = an ogre woman; harijaṭā nāma = named ḥarijata; mārjārasadṛishekshaṇā = with eyes like those of a cat; vivartya = turned around; nayane = eyes; kopāt = due to anger; abravīt = (and) spoke; vākyam = (these) words.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named ḥarijata with eyes like those of a cat turned around eyes due to anger and spoke these words.

Sloka 10

yena devāstrāyastriṃśaddevarājaśca nirjatāḥ || 5-23-10
tasya tvaṃ rākṣasendrasya bhāryā bhavitumarhasi |

10. yena = by whom; trastriṃshat = thirty three; devāḥ = Devas; devarājashcha = and the king of Devas; nirjitāḥ = have been conquered; tvam = you; arhasi = are suited; bhavitum = to become; bhāryā = the wife; tasya rākshasendrasya = of that lord of ogres.

"By whom thirty three Devas and the king of Devas have been conquered, you are suited to become the wife of that lord of ogres.

Sloka 11

tatastu praghasā nāma rākṣasī krodhamūrchitā || 5-23-11
bhartsayanī tadā ghoramidaṃ vacanamabravīt |

11. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; praghasā nāma = named Praghasa; krodhamūrchhitā = swooning with anger; tadā = then; bhartsayantī = frightening (ṣeetha) ; abravīt = spoke; idam = this; ghoram = horrible (word).

ṭhereafter an ogre named Praghasa swooning with anger and then frightening ṣeetha, spoke this horrible word.

Sloka 12

vīryotsiktasya śūrasya samgrāme na nivartinaḥ || 5-23-12
balino vīryayuktasya bhāryātvam kiṃ na lapsyase |

12. kim = why; na lapsyase = (you) will not get; bharyātvam = wife-hood (of Ravana); vīryotsiktasya = (who is) proud of his might; shūrasya = valiant; anivartinaḥ = who will not turn back; saṃgrāmeshhu = in wars; balinaḥ = strong; vīryayuktasya = together with valour.

"Why will you not get wifehood of Ravana who is proud of his might, valiant, who will not turn back in wars, who is strong together with valour."

Sloka 13

priyāṃ bahumatāṃ bhāryāṃ tyaktvā rājā mahābalaḥ || 5-23-13
sarvāsāṃ ca mahābhāgāṃ tvāmupaiṣyati rāvaṇaḥ |

13. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; mahābalaḥ = with great might; tyaktvā = leaving;priyām = dear; bahumatām = respectable; bhāryām = wife; sarvāsām = who among all wives; mahābhāgām = is most fortunate; upaishhyati = can obtain; tvām = you.

"ṭhe mighty Ravana can leave dear respectable wife who among all wives is most fortunate and can obtain you."

Sloka 14

samṛddham strīsahasreṇa nānāratnopaśobhitam || 5-23-14
antaḥpuraṃ samutsṛjya tvāmupaiṣyati rāvaṇaḥ |

14. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; samutsṛijya = leaving; antaḥpuram = gynaeceum; samṛiddham = filled; strī sahasreṇa = with thousand women; nānā ratnopashobhitam = shone by various best things; upaishhyati = can obtain; tvām = you.

"Ravana leaving gynaeceum filled with thousand women and shone by various best things can obtain you.

Sloka 15

anyā tu vikaṭā nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt || 5-23-15
asakṛddevatā yuddhe nāgagandharvadānavāḥ |
nirjitāḥ samare yena sate pārśvamupāgataḥ || 5-23-16

15; 16. anyā = another; rākshasī tu = ogre woman; vikaṭā nāma = named Vikata; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; yena = by whom; asakṛit = a number of times; yuddhe = in war; devatāḥ = Devas; samare = in war; nāgagandharvadānavāḥ = ṇagas; Gandharvas and Danavas; nirjiāḥ = have been conquered; saḥ = that Ravana; āgataḥ = has come; te pārsvam = to your side.

Another ogre woman named Vikata spoke these words: "By whom a number of times in war Devas, in war Nagas, Gandharvas and Danavas have been conquered that Ravana has come to your side."

Sloka 17

tasya sarvasamṛddhasya rāvaṇasya mahātmanaḥ |
kimadya rākṣasendrasya bhāryātvaṃ neccha.adhame || 5-23-17

17. adhame = O vile one; kim = why; adya = now; nechchhase = are you not desring; bhāryātvam = wifehood; tasya rāvaṇasya = of such Ravana; sarvasamṛiddhasya = fulfilled in all respects; mahātmanaḥ = great soul; rākshasendrasya = lord of ogres.

"O vile one! Why are you not desiring wifehood of such Ravana, fulfilled in all respects, who is a great soul and lord of ogres.

Sloka 18

tatastu durmukhī nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt |
yasya sūryo na tapati bhīto yasya ca mārutaḥ || 5-23-18
na vāti smāyatāpāṅge kiṃ tvaṃ tasya na tiṣṭhasi |

18. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; durmukhī nāma = named Durmūkhi; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; āyatā pāṇ^ge = O one with long cornered eyes! bhītaḥ = fearing; yasya = whom; sūryaḥ = sun; na tapati = will not consume by heat; bhītaḥ = fearing; yasya = whom; mārutaḥ = wind; na vāti sma = will not blow; tasya = to that one; kim = why; tvam = are you; na tiṣhṭhasi = not favourable.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named Durmukhi spoke these words: " O one with long eyes! Fearing whom the sun will not comsume by heat, fearing whom the win will not blow, to that one why are you not favourable."

Sloka 19

puṣṭavṛṣṭaṃ ca taravo mumucuryasya vai bhayāt || 5-23-19
śailāśca subhru pānīyam jaladāśca yadecchati |
tasya nairṛtarājasya rājarājasya bhāmini || 5-23-20
kiṃ tvaṃ na kuruṣe buddhiṃ bhāryārthe rāvaṇasya hi |

19; 20. subhru = O one with good eyebrows!; bhaminī = O ṣeetha!; bhayāt = by the fear; yasya = of whom; taravaḥ = trees; mumuchuḥ = shed; pushhpavṛishhṭim = a shower of flowers; yadā ichchhati = whenever desired; shailāshcha = mountains; jaladāshcha = and clouds; panīyam = (will shed) water;kim = why; tvam = you; na kurushhe = are not making; buddhim = mind; bāryārdhe = to be the wife; tasya rāvaṇasya = of such Ravana; nairṛita rājasya = king of ogres; rājarājasya = (and) king of kings;

"O one with good eyebrows! O ṣeetha! By the fear of whom trees shed a shower of flowers, whenever Ravana desired mountains and clouds will shed water, why you are not making mind to be the wife of such Ravana, who is the king of ogres and king of kings."

Sloka 21

sādhu te tattvato devi kathitaṃ sādhu bhāmini || 5-23-21
gṛhāṇa susmite vākyamanyathā na bhaviṣyasi |

21. susmite bhāminī = O ṣeetha with good smile; devī = O princess! gṛihāṇa = understand; sādhu vākyam = kind words; te kathitam = spoken to you; sādhu = well; tattvataḥ = truly.

"O ṣeetha with a good smile! O princess! Understand kind words spoken to you well and truly."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe trayoviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 23rd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
