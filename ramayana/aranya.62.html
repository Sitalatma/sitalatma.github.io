Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 62
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama bewails for Seetha as an ambivert, ambivalent romantic epical hero and asks Lakshmana to return to Ayodhya, as Rama is certain to perish without Seetha. He thinks his agony aloud, weighing pros and cons of his situation.


Sloka 1

sītām apaśyan dharmātmā śoka upahata cetanaḥ |
vilalāpa mahābāhū rāmaḥ kamala locanaḥ || 3-62-1

1. dharmātmā = virtue-souled one; mahābāhū = long armed one; kamala locanaḥ = lotus, eyed one; rāmaḥ = Rama; sītām a pashyan = ṣeetha, not, being able to see; shoka upahata cetanaḥ = anguish, marred, sagacity; vilalāpa = bewailed.

In not seeing Seetha that virtue-souled Rama's sagacity is marred by his anguish and he bewailed with his lotus-like eyes that are reddening like a pair of lotuses, raising is long arms. [3-62-1]

▼

Some mms use the word kāma in the compound shoka upahata cetanaḥ thus it becomes kāma upahata cetana and then it means 'his sagacity is marred by desire for Seetha...' and because of the adjacency of word dharmātmā 'righteous one...' 'a righteous desire is no sin to desire for... hence his desire for Seetha is righteous...' Then mahābāhū denotes that he is wailing raising his long arms, and kamala locana is indicative of redness of lotuses, thus his eyes are reddening by his wailing.

Sloka 2

paśyan iva ca tām sītām apaśyan madana arditaḥ |
uvāca rāghavo vākyam vilāpa āśraya durvacam || 3-62-2

2. madana arditaḥ rāghavaḥ = by Love-god, cowed down, Raghava; tām sītām = at her, at ṣeetha; a pashyan = [even if] not, seeing; pashyan iva = seeing, as though; vilāpa āshraya dur vacam = wail, sheltered [pampered,] badly [inarticulately,] speech [articulacy - because his speech took shelter of waling he is articulating inarticulately with a speech that is pampered by that wailing instinct; [ittham = this way]; uvāca vākyam = said, words [vented his heart.]

Even if Raghava is not able to see Seetha in his presence he started talking to her in a kind of inarticulate wailing, as wailing pampered his articulacy because his speech took shelter of wailing instinct, and as he is cowed down by Love-god, and thus he started to vent out his heart in this way. [3-62-2]

▼

Here the poet is starting the sixth phase among the ten phases called manmadha avasthāḥ 'phases of pangs of love,' and this one is called a + rati 'non-indulgence, the ambivalence, the ambiversion...' of the romantic hero, in romantic epical poetry.

Sloka 3

tvam aśokasya śākhābhiḥ puṣpa priya tarāa priye |
avṛṇoṣi śarīram te mama śoka vivardhinī || 3-62-3

3. priye = oh, dear; tvam = you are; puṣpa priya tarāa = by flowers, fascinated, very much; mama shoka vi vardhinī = [that which] to me, anguish, highly, amplifying one; ashokasya shākhābhiḥ = with Ashoka tree, branches [which are in full bloom]; te sharīram = you, body; āvṛiṇoṣi = veiling yourself [now.]

"Flowers fascinate you very much, my dear, hence you veil yourself with the full bloomed branches of Ashoka tree, but that alone is amplifying my anguish because you both have presently became tormentors. [3-62-3]

▼

We may recount the idea reg. Ashoka flowers expressed at 3-60-17.

Sloka 4

kadalī kāṇḍa sadṛśau kadalyā saṃvṛtā ubhau |
ūrū paśyāmi te devi na asi śaktā nigūhitum || 3-62-4

4. devi = oh, lady; kadalī kāṇDa [sknadha] sadṛishau = banana plant's, stalk [trunk,] that likens to; kadalyā samvṛitā = by banana plants [grove,] lapped in; te ubhau ūrū = your, both, thighs; pashyāmi = I caught sight of; ni gūhitum = inside, cover them in; shaktā na asi = possible, not, you are [inapt].

"ṃaybe, both your thighs liken to the stalks of banana plants, thinking so, now you have lapped them in grovy banana plants, but I can distinguish which is which, thus now I caught sight of them, oh, lady, you are inapt at least to cover them from me. [3-62-4]

Sloka 5

karṇikāra vanam bhadre hasaṃtī devi sevase |
alam te parihāsena mama bādhāvahena vai || 3-62-5

5. devi = oh, lady; bhadre = oh, glorious lady; hasantī = facetiously; karṇikāra vanam sevase = Karnikāra [trees with flowers,] in boscage, you glorying in; mama bādha āvahena [ā vahena] = to me, worry, bringing, carrying [wafting]; te parihāsena alam vai = your, facetiousness, is enough, really.

"Oh, lady, you are facetiously glorying in the boscage of fully bloomed Karnikāra trees which is really wafting worry to me, rather than the fragrance of those flowers oh, glorious lady, enough, enough is this facetiousness of yours. [3-62-5]

Sloka 6

viśeṣeṇa āśramasthāne hāso ayam na praśasyate |
avagacchāmi te śīlam parihāsa priyam priye || 3-62-6
āgaccha tvam viśālākṣī śūnyo ayam uṭajaḥ tava |

6, 7a: visheṣeṇa = especially; āshrama sthāne = [like] hermitage, in a place; ayam = this [sort of]; hāsaḥ = raillery; na prashasyate = not, merited; priye = oh, dear; te = your; parihāsa priyam shīlam = humour, loving, disposition; avagacchāmi = I am aware of [state of mind, your humour]; vishālākshī = oh, wide-eyed one; tvam = you; āgaccha = come on; tava ayam uṭajaḥ shūnyaḥ = your, this, cottage, is empty.

"I am aware of your humour, lady, and I know that you are jocose, but in a place like hermitage unmerited is this sort of raillery, even if it is good-natured. Hence, you come back, oh, wide-eyed one, your cottage is empty. [3-62-6, 7a]

▼

Be it a cottage or a palace, minus a housewife, it cannot be called a 'home' na gṛiham gṛiha iti āhuḥ gṛihiṇī gṛiham ucyate | gṛiham tu gṛihiṇī hīnam araṇyam sadṛishamatam || ṃaha Bharata, 12-144-6; bhāryāhīnam gṛihasthasya shūnyam eva gṛiham bhavet | 'a house is a house when housewife is there, otherwise it amounts to a forest...' 'a house to the householder is a void, if the housewife is not there...'

Sloka 7

su vyaktam rākṣaiḥ sītā bhakṣitā vā hṛtā api vā || 3-62-7
na hi sā vilapaṃtam mām upasampraiti lakṣmaṇa |

7b, 8a. lakshmaṇa = oh, Lakshmana; sītā = ṣeetha; su vyaktam = very, evidently; rākshaiḥ = by demons; bhakshitā vā = gorged up, either; hṛitā api vā = abducted, perhaps, or; sā = she; vilapantam mām = whiny, at me; na = not; upasampraiti [upa sam pra eti = to nearby, well, easily, coming] = returning; hi = indeed.

"Oh, Lakshmana, very evidently demons have either gorged up Seetha, or perhaps abducted her, because she is not returning to me who am whiny indeed for her. [3-62-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8

etāni mṛga yūdhāni sa aśru netrāṇi lakṣmaṇa || 3-62-8
śaṃśanti iva hi me devīm bhakṣitām rajanīcaraiḥ |

8b, 9a. lakshmaṇa = oh, Lakshmana; sa ashru netrāṇi = those that are - with, with tears, eyes - with tearful eyes; etāni mṛiga yūdhāni = these, deer, mobs of; me devīm = my, lady is; rajanīcaraiḥ = by nightwalkers; bhakshitām = gluttonised; shamshanti iva hi = explaining, as if, indeed.

"Indeed these teary-eyed mobs of deer look as if to explain that nightwalkers have gluttonised my lady. [3-62-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

hā mama ārye kva yātā asi hā sādhvi vara varṇini || 3-62-9
hā sa kāmā adya kaikeyī devi me adya bhaviṣyati |

8b, 9a. hā = ha; mama ārye = my, graceful one; adya = now; kva yātā asi = to where, strayed, have you; hā sādhvi = ha, chastely lady; vara varṇini = oh, best, complexioned one; hā = ha; me = my; devi kaikeyī = lady [queen mother, dowager,] Kaikeyi; sa kāma = with [fulfilled,] ambition; adya bhaviṣyati = now, she will be.

"Ha! ṃy graceful lady, to where you have strayed now... Ha! chastely and best complexioned lady, now the ambition of my queen mother Kaikeyi will be fulfilled, as I breath my last owing to your straying... [3-62-8b, 9a]

Sloka 10

sītāyā saha niryāto vinā sītām upāgataḥ || 3-62-10
katham nāma pravekṣyāmi śūnyam antaḥ puram mama |

9b, 10a. sītāyā saha nir yātaḥ = with ṣeetha, along, out, I came - to exile; vinā sītām upa āgataḥ = without, ṣeetha, towards, arriving - go back to Ayodhya; shūnyam = empty; mama antaḥ puram = my, inside, palace [palace-chambers]; katham = how; nāma = [in all but] name; pravekshyāmi = enter [step in.]

"I have come to forests with Seetha and have to go back to Ayodhya without Seetha. How, in all but name, can I step into an oblivion called my palace-chambers? [3-62-9b, 10a]

Sloka 11

nirvīrya iti loko mām nirdayaḥ ca iti vakṣyati || 3-62-11
kātaratvam prakāśam hi sītā apanayanena me |

10b, 11a. lokaḥ mām = populace, of me; nir vīrya iti = [a person] without, vigour, thus as; nir dayaḥ ca = without, pity, also; iti vakshyati = thus, they say [denounce]; sītā apa nayanena = in ṣeetha's, away, leading - regarding abduction; me = my; kātaratvam = timidity [ineptitude]; prakāsham hi = will be self-evident, indeed.

"People will denounce me as a vigourless and pitiless person, and my ineptitude will indeed be self-evident, for Seetha is led away from me by some tactical being... [3-62-10b, 11a]

Sloka 12

nivṛtta vana vāsaḥ ca janakam mithila adhipam || 3-62-12
kuśalam paripṛcchantam katham śakṣe nirīkṣitum |

11b, 12a. nivṛitta vana vāsaḥ ca = on completing, forest, living, also; kushalam paripṛicchantam = wellbeing [of all,] one who asks after [me]; mithila adhipam = ṃithila's, king; janakam = to Janaka; nirīkshitum = to gaze at [have the face]; katham shakshe = how, I am capable [how can I stand.]

"When the king of ṃithila Janaka asks after the wellbeing of all the three of us after the completion of forest living, how do I have the face to stand him? [3-62-11b, 12a]

Sloka 13

videha rajo nūnam mām dṛṣṭvā virahitam tayā || 3-62-13
sutā vināśa saṃtapto mohasya vaśam eṣyati |

12b, 13a. videha rajaḥ = Videha's, king; tayā virahitam = her [ṣeetha,] without; mām dṛiṣṭvā = me, on seeing; sutā vināsha santaptaḥ = daughter, by perishing, distraught; mohasya vasham eṣyati = of perplexity, preponderance, goes under; nūnam = it is definite.

"On seeing me without Seetha the king of Videha will be distraught by the perishing of his daughter, and he defiantly goes under the preponderance of perplexity... [3-62-12b, 13a]

Sloka 14

athavā na gamiṣyāmi purīm bharata pālitam || 3-62-14
svargo api hi tayā hīnaḥ śūnya eva mato mama |

13b, 14a. athavā = instead; bharata pālitam purīm = by Bharata, ruled, to city [Ayodhya]; na gamiṣyāmi = not, I prefer to go; tayā hīnaḥ = her, without; svargaḥ api = heaven, even; mama = to me; shūnya eva hi = void, thus, indeed; mataḥ = [is my] belief.

"Instead, I prefer not to go to the city Ayodhya that is ruled by Bharata, because it must be comforting to one and all under his rulership, but not to me as Seetha will not be with me... else if, that end of my life occurs now and if I were to go to heaven, even that heaven will be a void to me without her... I believe so... [3-62-13b, 14a]

Sloka 15

tat mām utsṛjya hi vane gaccha ayodhyā purīm śubhām || 3-62-15
na tu aham tām vinā sītām jīveyam hi kathaṃcana |

14b, 15a. tat = hence; mām vane utsṛijya = me, in forest, forsaking; shubhām ayodhyā purīm gaccha = to auspicious, city, to Ayoidhya, [you Lakshmana] you go back; aham tu = I, on my part; tām sītām vinā = her, that ṣeetha, without; kathamcana = in anyway; na jīveyam hi = not, I live on, isn't it.

"Hence, Lakshmana, you go back to that auspicious city Ayodhya forsaking me in forests, because I have no existence without Seetha, isn't it! [3-62-14b, 15a]

Sloka 16

gāḍham āśliṣya bharato vācyo mat vacanāt tvayā || 3-62-16
anujñāto asi rāmeṇa pālaya iti vasuṃdharām |

15b, 16a. bharataḥ gāDham āshliṣya = Bharata, [on your] tightly, hugging [him]; mat vacanāt tvayā = my, according to word, by you; vācyaḥ = is tellable - Bharata is to be said this; vasundharām pālaya iti = the earth, you reign, thus; rāmeṇa anuGYātaḥ asi = by Rama, authorised, you are.

"On tightly hugging Bharata you shall tell him these words as I have said, 'Rama authorises you to reign the earth...' [3-62-16]

Sloka 17

aṃbā ca mama kaikeyī sumitrā ca tvayā vibho || 3-62-17
kausalyā ca yathā nyāyam abhivādyā mama ajñayā |
rakṣaṇīyā prayatnena bhavatā sā ukta kāriṇā || 3-62-18

17b, 18. vibho = oh, efficacious Lakshmana; mama ambā = my, mother; kaikeyī sumitrā ca kausalyā ca = Kaikeyi, ṣumitra, also, Kausalya, also; tvayā = by you; mama aGYayā = by my, order; yathā nyāyam = as per, justification [justifiably]; abhivādyā = you on revering them; you tell them my good bye; ukta kāriṇā = one who does whatever said to him - by Rama, namely Lakshmana; such a you are you say; bhavatā = by you; prayatnena = effortfully; sā = she [Kausalya]; rakshaṇīyā = is to be protected.

"Oh, efficacious Lakshmana, on revering my mothers Kaika, Sumitra, and Kausalya justifiably you tell them my good bye, and you as the one who effectuates whatever is assigned to you, you have to effortfully protect my mother Kausalya by doing whatever she says. [3-62-17b, 18]

Sloka 19

sītāyāḥ ca vināśo ayam mama ca amitra sūdana |
vistareṇa jananyā vinivedya tvayā bhavet || 3-62-19

19. a mitra sūdana = un, friendly, subjugator of; sītāyāḥ ca = ṣeetha's, also; mama ca = mine, also; ayam = this; vināshaḥ = perish; tvayā = by you; jananyā = to mothers; vistareṇa = in detail; vi nivedya = clearly, be informed; bhavet = it shall be.

"Oh, the subjugator of unfriendly, Lakshmana... you shall clearly inform in detail about this perish of Seetha, also that of mine, to our mothers. [3-62-19]

Sloka 20

iti vilapati rāghavo tu dīno
vanam upagamya tayā vinā su keśyā |
bhaya vikala mukhaḥ tu lakṣmaṇo api
vyathita manā bhṛśam āturo babhūva || 3-62-20

20. dīnaḥ = dejected one; rāghavaḥ = Raghava; vanam upagamya = forest, on nearing [searchingly he neared every corner of forest]; su keshyā = with good, tresses - lady with best hair-locks; tayā vinā = her, without - missing from him; iti vilapati = thus, bewailing; lakshmaṇaḥ api = Lakshmana, even; bhaya vi kala mukhaḥ = by fear, without, lustre, faced [whey-faced]; vyathita manā = frantic, hearted; bhṛisham āturaḥ babhūva = highly, overwrought, he became.

Thus Raghava, the dejected, neared every corner of the forest in his search, and bewailed because that lady with best plaits, Seetha, is not found and missing from him. Even Lakshmana became whey-faced, frantic-hearted, highly overwrought, by the fear of uncertainty looming large on them. [3-62-20]

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe dvi ṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 62nd chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the first Epic poem of India
