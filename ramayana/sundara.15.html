Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 15

Introduction

Hanuma continues his search in the Ashoka garden for Seetha. He sees an emaciated woman surrounded by demonesses. From Her radiance and manner of clothing etc, Hanuma recognizes Her to be Seetha.


Sloka 1

sa vīkṣamāṇaḥ tatrastho mārgamāṇaḥ ca maithilīm |
avekṣamāṇaḥ ca mahīm sarvām tām anvavaikṣata || 5-15-1

1. saḥ = that ḥanuma; tatrastaḥ = being there; vīkṣamāṇaḥ = seeing(and); mārgamāṇaḥ = searching; maithilīm = for ṣeetha; avekshamāṇaḥ = observing; sarvām = all; tām mahīm = that land; anvavaikṣata = explored (the garden).

ṭhat ḥanuma being there, seeing and searching for ṣeetha, observing all that land, explored the garden.

ṭhat ḥanuma being there observed closely that Ashoka garden shone by ṣantanaka creepers, ṣantana trees with heavenly aroma and juice, well decorated in all directions equalling the garden of Nandana, surrounded by animals and birds, congested with mansions and palaces, resounded with the notes of cuckoos, decorated with wells having golden water lilies, lotuses and with many chairs and carpets; with many sub-terrain houses, beautiful trees, with trees with flowers of all seasons and with fruit, with the radiance of raising sun, by the glory of ashoka trees in bloom, as though radiant as though being made to be with branches without leaves, by hundreds of birds perching again and again, Ashoka trees with wonderful flowers as head decoration, with flowers spread till the roots destroying leaves, with weight of groups of flowers as touching the earth with karnolāra trees; in blossom with Kimkusa trees in full blossom.

Sloka 9

sa deśaḥ prabhayā teṣām pradīpta iva sarvataḥ |
pumnāgāḥ sapta parṇāḥ ca campaka uddālakāḥ tathā || 5-15-9
vivṛddha mūlā bahavaḥ śobhante sma supuṣpitāḥ |

9. teshaḥ prabhyayā = by the radiance of those trees; saḥ deshaḥ = that region; pradīpta iva = was as though lit; sarvataḥ = in all directions; pumnāgāḥ = Punnāga trees; viv^iddha mūlaḥ = with fattened roots; supuṣpitāḥ = and in full blossom; sapta parṇāḥ ca = saptaparna trees; tathā campaka uddālakāḥ = campaka and Uddalaka trees; shobhante sma = were shining

By the radiance of those trees that region was as though lit in all directions. Punnāga trees with flatted roots and in full blossom, Saptaparna trees Campaka and Uddalaka trees were shining.

Sloka 10

śāta kumbha nibhāḥ kecit kecid agni śikha upamāḥ || 5-15-10
nīla anjana nibhāḥ kecit tatra aśokāḥ sahasraśaḥ |

10. tatra = there (in that garden of Ashoka); sahasrashaḥ ashokāḥ = (there were) thousands of Ashoka trees; kecit = some; shāta kumbha nibhāḥ = equalling gold; kecit = some; agni shikha upamāḥ = equalling fire; kecit = some; nīla anjana nibhāḥ = equalling the precious stone ṇīla and Anjana.

ṭhere in that garden of Ashoka there were thousands of Ashoka trees some equalling gold, some equalling fire, some equalling the precious stone Nīla and Anjana.

Like the garden of ṇandana, a celestial garden, wonderful like Caitraratham, a garden of Kubera, surpassing all, unfathomable, an excellent one, a beautiful one consisting of glory together with flowers like clusters of stars, like a second sky wonderful with flowers, like hundreds of diamonds, like a second ocean with flowers of all seasons, spread with trees having the smell of honey, beautiful with groups of animals of various sounds, diffused with many smells with an auspicious soul pleasing smell.

ṭhat monkey warrior ḥanuma saw not far away in that Ashoka garden, in the middle, a lofty temple filled with fragrance like a second Gandhamadana ṃountain, standing by a thousand pillars, white like ṃount Kailāsa with stairs made of corals with platforms made of refined gold as though radiant with glory, clear and because of being tall as though scraping the sky.

Sloka 18

18b-19. tataḥ = thereafter; dadarsha = ḥanuma saw (ṣeetha); malina samvītām = wearing a soiled garment; samāvṛitām = surrounded; rākṣasībhiḥ = by raksha women; upavāsa kṛishām = emaciated due to fasting; dīnām = looking miserable; niḥshvasantīm = sighing; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; amalām candra rekhām iva = like the pure crescent moon; shukla pakṣa ādau = at the beginning of a bright fortnight;

ṭhereafter ḥanuma saw ṣeetha wearing a soiled garment, surrounded by rakshasa women, emaciated due to fasting, looking miserable, sighing again and again, like the pure crescent moon at the beginning of a bright fortnight.

Sloka 20

20. rūpeṇa = with a form; manda prakhyāyamānena = that slowly came out; vibhāvasoḥ shikhām iva = like a tongue of fire; rucira prabhām = of beautiful radiance; pinaddhām = covered; dhūma jālena = by smoke.

With a form that slowly came out like a tongue of fire, of beautiful radiance covered by smoke.

Sloka 21

21. samvītām = covered; ekena = by a single; uttama vāsasā = best cloth; pītena = of yellow colour; kliṣṭena = which was wrinkled; analamkārām = without any decorations; padminīm iva = like a lotus plant; vipadmām = without lotuses; sapankām = together with mud.

Covered by a single best cloth of yellow colour, which was wrinkled, without any decorations like a lotus plant without lotuses, together with mud.

Sloka 22

Bashful, tortured by sorrow worn out in a pitiable condition like the constellation Rohini, pressed by the planet mars.

Sloka 23

With a face full of tears in a pitiable condition, emaciated due to not taking food, immersed in thought with gloom, always in a pitiable state, surrendering to sorrow.

Sloka 24

ṇot seeing dear people, seeing groups of demonesses, like a female deer loosing her own herd surrounded by group of hounds.

Sloka 25

25-26a. veṇyā = with a braid; ekayā nīla nāga ābhayā = with the radiance of a single black serpent; gatayā = reaching; jaghanam = hinder part; mahīmiva = like the earth; nīlayā = with dark green; vanarājyā = groups of trees; nīradāpāye = during the end of rainy season; sukha arhām = deserving of comforts; duhkha samtaptām = tormented by grief; vyasanānām akovidām = not accustomed to calamities.

With a braid with the radiance of a single black serpent reaching hinder part, like the earth with dark green groups of trees during the end of rainy season, deserving of comforts, tormented by grief, not accustomed to calamities.

Sloka 26

26b-27a. samīkṣya = observing; tām = that woman; vishāla akṣīm = with wide eyes; adhikam malinām = very untidy; kṛishām = emaciated; tarkayām āsa = ḥanuma thought (that); sīteti = this is indeed ṣeetha; upapādibhiḥ kāraṇaiḥ = on logical grounds.

Observing that woman with wide eyes, very untidy, emaciated, ḥanuma thought that this is indeed Seetha on logical grounds.

Sloka 27

ṭhen, while being abducted by that rakshasa with the ability to assume desired form in whatever way, She was seen, this woman has the same form.

Sloka 28

28b-29. sītām = (ḥanuma saw) ṣeetha; pūrṇa candra ānanām = with a face like full moon; subhrūm = with beautiful eyebrows; cāru vṛitta payo dharām = with graceful rounded breasts; prabhayā = by the radiance; kurvantīm = making; sarvāḥ dishaḥ = all directions; vitimirāḥ = without darkness; devīm = goddess like; nīla keshīm = with black hair; bimba oṣṭhīm = with lips like bimba fruit; sumadhyām = with a good waist; supratiṣṭhām = very firm; padma palāsha akṣīm = with eyes like lotus petals; manmathasya ratim yathā = like Rati the consort of god of love.

ḥanuma saw ṣeetha with a face like full moon, with beautiful eyebrows, with graceful rounded breasts, by the radiance making all directions without darkness, goddess like with black hair, with lips like bimba fruit, with a good waist, very firm, with eyes like lotus petals, like Rati the consort of god of love.

Sloka 30

As the light of full moon, dear to all world, like an ascetic woman leading austere life sitting on bare ground with a good body.

Sloka 31

ṣighing a lot like the consort of lord of serpents in fright, not radiant by sorrow which was great and extensive.

Sloka 32

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

Sloka 37

Wearing a coat of dust, pitiable one, although suited for decorations undecorated, like the radiance of the moon, the lord of stars, covered by black clouds.

ṣeeing ṣeetha like knowledge in ruin for want of repeated study, that ḥanuma's mind got repeatedly doubtful.

Sloka 39

ḥanuma recognized with great sorrow ṣeetha not decorated, like a sentence which got a different meaning being devoid of culture.

Sloka 40

40. samīkṣya = seeing; tām rāja purtrīm = that princess; vishāla akṣīm = the wide eyed one; aninditām = blamless one; tarkayāmāsa = (ḥanuma) reasoned; sīteti = her to be ṣeetha; upapādayan kāraṇaiḥ = for the following reasons.

ṣeeing that princess, the wide eyed one, blameless one, ḥanuma reasoned her to be ṣeetha for the following reasons.

Sloka 41

vaidehyā yāni ca angeṣu tadā rāmo anvakīrtayat |
tāni ābharaṇa jālāni gātra śobhīni alakṣayat || 5-15-41
sukṛtau karṇa veṣṭau ca śva damṣṭrau ca susaṃsthitau |
maṇi vidruma citrāṇi hasteṣv ābharaṇāni ca || 5-15-42
śyāmāni cira yuktatvāt tathā saṃsthānavanti ca |

41;42-43a. tadā = then; alakṣayat = (ḥanuma) saw; yāni = whatever; ābharaṇa jālāni = groups of jewellery; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha; angeṣu = on various parts; rāmaḥ = Rama; anvakīrtayat = spoke; tāni = those; gātra shobhīni = charming ḥer limbs; sukṛitau = well made; karṇa veṣṭau ca = earrings; susamsthitau = well fitting; sva damṣṭrau ca = svadamstras; ābharaṇāni ca = and ornaments; maṇi vidruma citrāṇi = wonderful with diamonds; corals; hasteṣu = on the limbs of ḥer hands; cira yuktatvāt = belonging to body for long time; shyāmāni = blackened; samsthānavanti ca = and making marks (on body).

ṭhen ḥanuma saw whatever groups of jewellery of ṣeetha on various parts Rama spoke, those charming Her limbs, well made earrings, well fitting Svadamstras and ornaments wonderful with diamonds on the limbs of Her hands, belonging to body for long time, blackened and making marks on body.

Sloka 43

tāni eva etāni manye aham yāni rāmo anvakīrtayat || 5-15-43
tatra yāni avahīnāni tāni aham na upalakṣaye |
yāni asyā na avahīnāni tāni imāni na saṃśayaḥ || 5-15-44

43b-44. aham manye = I think; etāni = all these; tāni eva = to be same; yāni = about which; rāmaḥ anvakīrtayat = Rama spoke; tatra = among them; yāni = whatever; avahīnāni = have been worn out; tāni = those; aham = I; na upalakṣaye = will not see; yāni = whatever; asyāḥ = (jewels) of ḥer; na avahīnāni = are not worn out; tāni imāni = these are they; na samshayaḥ = no doubt.

"I think all these to be same about which Rama spoke; among them whatever have been worn out those I will not see; whatever jewels of Her are not worn out these are they - no doubt."

Sloka 45

pītam kanaka paṭṭa ābham srastam tad vasanam śubham |
uttarīyam naga āsaktam tadā dṛṣṭam plavam gamaiḥ || 5-15-45

45. tat uttarīyam vastram = that upper garment; pītam = yellow; kanaka paṭṭa ābham = with a shine like a golden plate; subham = auspicious one; srastam = being dropped; dṛiṣṭam = had been seen; naga āsaktam = caught in a tree; tadā = then; plavam gamaiḥ = by vanaras.

ṭhat upper garment yellow with a shine like a golden plate, auspicious one, being dropped had been seen caught in a tree then by vanaras.

Sloka 46

bhūṣaṇāni ca mukhyāni dṛṣṭāni dharaṇī tale |
anayā eva apaviddhāni svanavanti mahānti ca || 5-15-46

46. apaviddhāni = dropped; dharaṇī tale = on the earth; anayā eva = by ḥer; bhūṣaṇāni ca = ornaments also; mahānti mukhyāni = great and important; svanavanti = making sound; dṛiṣṭāni = have been seen.

"Dropped on the earth by ḥer, ornaments also great and important, those making sound have been seen."

Sloka 47

idam cira gṛhītatvād vasanam kliṣṭavattaram |
tathā hi nūnam tad varṇam tathā śrīmad yathā itarat || 5-15-47

47. idam vasanam = this cloth; cira gṛihītatvād = being worn for a long time; kliṣṭavattaram = has worn out a lot; tathā hi = even then; nunam = definitely; tat varṇam = it is of the same colour; yathā = like; itaram = the other (upper garment); tathā = in the same way; shrīmat = (this is) glorious.

"ṭhis cloth being worn for a long time has worn out a lot, even then definitely it is of the same colour, like the other upper garment; in the same way this is glorious."

Sloka 48

iyam kanaka varṇa angī rāmasya mahiṣī priyā |
pranaṣṭā api satī yasya manaso na praṇaśyati || 5-15-48

48. iyam = this ṣhe; kanaka varṇa angī = of golden colour; rāmasya piryā mahiṣī = is indeed Rama's dear wife; yā = who ever (ṣeetha); satī = the chaste one; pranaṣṭā api = even though not seen; na praṇashyati = is not gone; asya manasaḥ = from Rama's heart.

"ṭhis ṣhe of golden colour is indeed Rama's dear wife; who ever ṣeetha the chaste one, even though not seen, is not gone from Rama's heart."

Sloka 49

iyam sā yat kṛte rāmaḥ caturbhiḥ paritapyate |
kāruṇyena ānṛśaṃsyena śokena madanena ca || 5-15-49
strī pranaṣṭā iti kāruṇyād āśritā iti ānṛśaṃsyataḥ |
patnī naṣṭā iti śokena priyeti madanena ca || 5-15-50

49-50. sā iyam = this is ṣhe; yat kṛite = for whose sake; rāmaḥ = Rama; paritapyate = is tormented; caturbhiḥ = in four ways; kāruṇyena = from compassion; ānṛishamsyena = from pity; shokena = from grief; madanena = from love; pranaṣṭā strī iti = (thinking that) a woman has disappeared; kāruṇyena = from compassion; āshritā iti = depending on him; ānṛishamsyataḥ = from pity; naṣṭā patnī iti = wife has been lost; shokena = from grief; priyā iti = a dear one; madanena = from love.

"ṭhis is ṣhe for whose sake Rama tormented in four ways from compassion from pity from grief from love; (thinking that) a woman has disappeared from compassion, depending on him from pity, wife has been lost from grief, a dear one from love."

Sloka 51

asyā devyā yathā rūpam anga pratyanga sauṣṭhavam |
rāmasya ca yathā rūpam tasya iyam asita īkṣaṇā || 5-15-51

51. yathā = in whatever way; asyāḥ devyāḥ = (is) this lady's; rūpam = appearance; anga pratyanga sauṣṭhavam = elegance of major and minor limbs; rāmasya ca = in the same way is Rama's; yathā = in whatever way; tasya rūapam = ḥis appearance; iyam asita īkṣaṇā = (in the same way is) this black-eyed one's appearance.

"In whatever way is this lady's appearance, elegance of major and minor limbs, in the same way is Rama's. In whatever way His appearance, in the same way is this black-eyed one's appearance."

Sloka 52

asyā devyā manaḥ tasmimḥ tasya ca asyām pratiṣṭhitam |
tena iyam sa ca dharma ātmā muhūrtam api jīvati || 5-15-52

52. asyāḥ devyāḥ = this lady's; manaḥ = heart; tasmin = is in Rama; tasya = ḥis heart; asyām ca pratiṣṭhitam = is firmly in ḥer; tena = for that reason; iyam = she; dharmātmā sa ca = that noble Rama; muhūrtam api jīvati = are able to live even for a moment.

"ṭhis lady's heart is in Rama, ḥis heart is firmly in ḥer, for that reason ṣhe and that noble Rama are able to live even for a moment."

Sloka 53

duṣkaram kurute rāmo imām matta kāśinīm |
sītām vinā mahā bāhuḥ muhūrtam api jīvati || 5-15-53

53. prabhuḥ rāmaḥ = lord Rama; ānayā hīnaḥ = losing ḥer; duṣkaram kuṛitavān = has done an impossible task; dhārayati yat = in that ḥe still preserved; ātmanaḥ deham = his body; nāvasīdati iti yat = in that ḥe did not die; shokena = from grief.

"Lord Rama losing ḥer has done an impossible task in that ḥe still preserved ḥis body, in that He did not die from grief."

Sloka 54

evam sītām tadā dṛṣṭvā hṛṣṭaḥ pavana sambhavaḥ |
jagāma manasā rāmam praśaśaṃsa ca tam prabhum || 5-15-54

54. pavanasambhavaḥ = ḥanuma; tadā = then; dṛiṣtvā = seeing; sītām = ṣeetha; hṛiṣṭaḥ = became happy; evam = thus; jagāma = and went; manasā = by mind; rāmam = to Rama; prashashamsa ca = and also praised; tam prabhum = that lord.

ḥanuma, then seeing ṣeetha became happy thus and went by mind to Rama and also praised that Lord.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe pañcadaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 15th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
