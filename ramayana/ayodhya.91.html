Introduction

Having been instructed by Bharadwaja to bring the army to his place, Bharata allowed his army also to come to the hermitage. Then, Bharadwaja invokes Visvakarma, the divine architect and requests him to arrange for a befitting hopsitality to Bharata,

Sloka 1

kṛta buddhim nivāsāya tathaiva sa muniḥ tadā |
bharatam kaikayī putram ātithyena nyamantrayat || 2-91-1

1. kṛita buddhim = (ṣeeing that he) had resolved; nivāsāya = to halt; tatraiva = at that place; saḥ = that; muniḥ = sage; tadā = then; nyamantrayat = prepared to offer; bharatam = Bharata; kaikeyīputram = the son of Kaikeyi; ātithyena = the full honours due to a guest.

Seeing he had resolved to halt at that place for the night, the sage Bharadwaja prepared to offer Bharata the son of Kaikeyi the full honours due to a guest

Sloka 2

abravīd bharataḥ tu enam nanu idam bhavatā kṛtam |
pādyam arghyam tathā ātithyam vane yad ūpapadyate || 2-91-2

2. bharatastu = Bharata on his part; abravīt = said; enam = to Bharadwaja; pādyam = water to wash my feet; arghyam = and hands; kṛitam name = have indeed been presented; bhavatā = by you; tathā = and; idam ātithyam = this hositality fitting for a guest; yat = which; upapadyate = is available; vane = in a forest (was made available to me).

Then, Bharata said to Bharadwaja as follows: "Have you not presented me with water to wash my hands and feet and offered me this hospitality fitting for a guest in this forest?"

Sloka 3

atha uvāca bharadvājo bharatam prahasann iva |
jāne tvām prīti samyuktam tuṣyeḥ tvam yena kenacit || 2-91-3

3. atha = thereafter; bharadwājaḥ = Bharadwaja; prahasanniva = smilingly; uvācha = spoke; bharatam = to Bharata (as follows); jāne = I know; tvām = you; prītisamyuktam = are of a friendly disposition; tvam = you; tuṣyeḥ = are please; yena kena chit = with whatever is offered to you.

Then, Bharadwaja smilingly replied to Bharata as follows: "I know you possess a friendly disposition and you are pleased with whatever is offered to you."

Sloka 4

senāyāḥ tu tava etasyāḥ kartum igcchāmi bhojanam |
mama pritir yathā rūpā tvam arho manuja ṛṣabha || 2-91-4

4. manujarṣabha = O the excellent among men!ichchhami = I wish; kartum = to offer; bhojanam = food; tava = to your; etasyāḥ = this; senāyāḥ = army; arhaḥ = you ought to ; (fulfil); mama = my; prītiḥ = satisfaction; yathārūpātathā = in every suitable way.

"O, Bharata the excellent among men! I wish to offer food to your army. You ought to fulfil my satisfaction in every suitable way."

Sloka 5

kim artham ca api nikṣipya dūre balam iha āgataḥ |
kasmān na iha upayāto asi sabalaḥ puruṣa ṛṣabha || 2-91-5

5. puruṣarṣabha = O; warrior!; kimartham = why; āgataḥ = did you come; iha = here; nikṣipya = keeping; balam = your army; dūre = at a distance?; kasmāt = why; na upayātaḥ asi = did you not come; sabalaḥ = with your army; iha = here?

"O, warrior! Why did you come here, leaving your army at a distance? Why did you not come to my place with your army?"

Sloka 6

bharataḥ pratyuvāca idam prānjaliḥ tam tapo dhanam |
sasainyo na upayāto asmi bhagavan bhagavad bhayāt || 2-91-6

6. bharataḥ = Bharata; prāṇjaliḥ = with joined palms; pratyuvācha = replied; tam = to that Bharadwaja; tapodhanam = enriched with austerity; idam = these words; bhagavan = O; venerable one!; nopato.asmi = I have not come; sasainyaḥ = with the army; bhagavadbhayāt = for fear (of displeasing) you.

Bharata, with joined palms, replied to Bharadwaja who was rich with austerity in the following words: "O, venerable one! I have not come here with my army for the fear of displeasing you."

Sloka 7

rājñā ca bhagavannityaṃ rājaputreṇa vā sadā |
yatnataḥ parihartavyā viṣayeṣu tapasvinaḥ || 2-91-7

7. bhagavan = O; venerable one!; rājJṇācha = either by the king; rāja putreṇa = or by the prince; tapasvinaḥ = ascetics; parihartavyaḥ = are to be kept away at a distance; yatnataḥ = deliberately; sadā = ever; viṣayeṣu = in their own lands.

"O, Venerable One! Either the king or the prince should always keep away from the places occupied by ascetics"

Sloka 8

vāji mukhyā manuṣyāḥ ca mattāḥ ca vara vāraṇāḥ |
pragcchādya mahatīm bhūmim bhagavann anuyānti mām || 2-91-8

8. bhagavan = O; ḥoly one!; vāji mukhyāḥ = spirited horses; manuṣyāshcha = men; vara vāraṇāshcha = rare elephants; mattāḥ = in rut; prachchhādya = covering; mahatīm = an immense; bhūmim = area; anuyānti = are accompanying; mām = me

"O, Holy one! Spirited horses, men and rare elephants in rut covering an immense area are accompanying me."

Sloka 9

te vṛkṣān udakam bhūmim āśrameṣu uṭajāmḥ tathā |
na hiṃsyur iti tena aham eka eva āgataḥ tataḥ || 2-91-9

9. na himsyuḥ = let them not damage; vṛikṣān = the trees; uṭajān = huts; bhūmim = and ground; tathā = and; (defile); udakam = the waters; āshrameṣu = of hermitages; iti te = in this thought; aham = I; samāgataḥ = came; eka eva = alone.

"Let them not damage the trees, huts and ground and defile the waters of hermitages' - in this thought, I came alone."

Sloka 10

ānīyatām itaḥ senā ity ājnaptaḥ parama ṛṣiṇā |
tathā tu cakre bharataḥ senāyāḥ samupāgamam || 2-91-10

10. tatastu = thereafter; bharataḥ = Bharata; ājṇaptaḥ = having been commanded; paramarṣiṇā = by that great sage; iti = thus; senā anīyātam = 'Let the army be brought; itaḥ = here'; chakre = allowed; senayāḥ = the army's samupāgamam = arrival.

Thereafter Bharata, having been commanded by that great sage to bring the army there, allowed the army's arrival to the hermitage.

Sloka 11

agni śālām praviśya atha pītvā apaḥ parimṛjya ca |
ātithyasya kriyā hetor viśva karmāṇam āhvayat || 2-91-11

11. atha = thereafter; pravishya = entering; agnishālām = the fire-sanctuary (the place where the sacrificial fire is kept); pītvā = sipping; āpaḥ = water; parimṛijyacha = and wiping the lips; kriyāhatoḥ = and in order to fulfil his duties; ātithyasya = towards the guests; āhvayat = (he) invoked; vishva karmāṇam = visva Karma (the architect of gods)

ṃeanwhile, entering the fire-sanctuary (the place where the sacrificial fire is kept), sipping water and wiping the lips, Bharadwaja invoked Visvakarma (the architect of gods) in order to fulfil his duties towards the guests (as follows)

Sloka 12

āhvaye viśva karmāṇam aham tvaṣṭāram eva ca |
ātithyam kartum igcchāmi tatra me samvidhīyatām || 2-91-12

12. aham = I; ichchhāmi = wish; kartum = to offer; ātithyam = hospitality to the guests; āhvaye = I summon; vishvakarmāṇam = vishvakarma; tvaṣṭāram eva cha = who is also the divine carpenter; samvidhīyatām = let arrangements be made; tatra = in that connection; me = for me.

"I wish to offer hospitality to the guest I summon vishvakarma who is also the divine carpenter. Let arrangements be made in that connection for me."

Sloka 13

ahvaye lokapālāṃ strīn devān śakramukhāṃstathā |
ātithyaṃ kartumiccāmi tatra me saṃvidhīyatām || 2-91-13

13. tathā = and; āhvaye = I invoke; trīn devān = the three gods; lokapālān = the guardians of the worlds; shakramukhān = with Indra as their king; ichchhāmi = I wish; kartum = to offer; ātithyam = hospitality to the guests; samvidhīyatām = let arrangements be made; tatra = in this connection ; me = for me.

"I further invoke the three gods (Yama, Varuna and Kubera) the guardians of the worlds with Indra as their king. I wish to offer hospitality to the guests. Let arrangements be made in this connection for me."

Sloka 14

prāk srotasaḥ ca yā nadyaḥ pratyak srotasa eva ca |
pṛthivyām antarikṣe ca samāyāntu adya sarvaśaḥ || 2-91-14

14. yāḥ nadyaḥ = Let the rivers; prāk srotasashcha = which flow towards the east; pratyak srotasa evacha = and those which flow towards the west; pṛithivyām = across the earth; antarikṣecha = and in the aerial region; adya = now; saha āyāstu = come together here; sarvashaḥ = from all quarters

"Let the rivers which flow towards the east and those which flow towards the west, across the earth and in the aerial region now come together here from all quarters."

Sloka 15

anyāḥ sravantu maireyam surām anyāḥ suniṣṭhitām |
aparāḥ ca udakam śītam ikṣu kāṇḍa rasa upamam || 2-91-15

15. anyāḥ = (Let) some rivers; sravantu = flow with; maireyam = ṃaireya ( a kind of wine made from date palms etc); anyāḥ = and some others (flow with) suniṣṭhitām = highly; aparāshcha = and some others; sravantu = flow with; shītam = cool; udakam = water; ikṣukāṇDarasopamam = with a taste of sugar cane.

"Let some rivers flow with ṃaireya ( a kind of wine made from date palms etc) some others flow with highly refined spirituous liquor and some others flow with cool water with a taste of sugarcane."

Sloka 16

āhvaye deva gandharvān viśvā vasu hahā huhūn |
tathaiva apsaraso devīr gandharvīḥ ca api sarvaśaḥ || 2-91-16

16. āhvaye = I invoke; devagandharvān = the celestials; celstial musicians; vishvāvasu hāhā huhūn = Vishvavasu ḥaha and ḥuhu; tathaiva = and apsarasaḥ = the nymphs; devīḥ = belonging to celestial race; gandharvīshchāpi = and also belonging to celestial musicians race sarvashaḥ = from all regions.

"I invoke the celestials and celestial musicians Vishvavasu, Haha and Huhu as also Apsaras (nymphs) belonging to the celestial and celestial musicians' race from all regions.

Sloka 17

ghṛtācīm atha viśvācīm miśra keśīm alambusām |
nāgadantāṃ ca hemāṃ ca himāmadrikṛtasthalām || 2-91-17

17. atha = And; (I invoke); ghṛitāchīm = the celstial nymphs called Ghritachi; vishvāchīm = Vishvachi; mishra kesīm = ṃishra Kesi; alambusām = Alambusa; nāgadantāmcha = ṇagadanta; hemāmcha = and ḥema; himām = and ḥima; adrikṛita sthadām = who has an abode made in mountains.

"I invoke the celestial nymphs called ghritachi, Vishvachi, ṃishra Kesi, Alambusa, Nagadanta and Hema as also Hima who has an abode made in mountains."

Sloka 18

śakram yāḥ ca upatiṣṭhanti brahmāṇam yāḥ ca bhāminīḥ |
sarvāḥ tumburuṇā sārdham āhvaye saparigcchadāḥ || 2-91-18

18. āhvaye = I invoke; yoṣitaḥ = the fair women; yāshcha = who; upatiṣṭhanti = are attending on; shakram = Indra the Lord of celestials; yāshcha = and those who are (are attending on); brahmāṇam = Brahma the Lord of creation; sarvāḥ = all of them; saparichchhadāḥ = with their external appendages (like musical instruments); tumburuṇāsārtham = along with (their teacher) ṭumburu.

"I invoke the fair women who are attending on Brahma the Lord of creation - all of them with their external appendages (like musical instruments) along with (their teacher) Tumburu."

Sloka 19

vanam kuruṣu yad divyam vāso bhūṣaṇa patravat |
divya nārī phalam śaśvat tat kauberam iha eva tu || 2-91-19

19. tat = (Let) that; divyam vanam = beautiful garden; kuruṣu = in the region of northern Kuru; kauberam = presided over by Kubera; shashvat = always; vāsobhūṣaṇa patravat = with its leaves which; sarve = as raiment and ornaments; divya nārīphalam = with its fruits in the form of heavenly damsels; etu = appear; iha = in this place.

"Let that beautiful garden in the region of northern Kuru, presided over by Kubera (the god of riches) always with its leaves which serve as raiment and ornaments, with its fruits in the form of heavenly damsels appear in this place."

Sloka 20

iha me bhagavān somo vidhattām annam uttamam |
bhakṣyam bhojyam ca coṣyam ca lehyam ca vividham bahu || 2-91-20

20. (ṃay); bhagavān = the blessed; somaḥ = moon god( the deity presiding over annual plants); vidhattām = furnish; me = me; iha = at this place; uttamam = excellent; annam = food; bahu = f many; vividham = Varieties; bhakṣyam = confections; bhojayam = sweets; choṣyam = sances; lchyamcha = and syrups

"ṃany the blessed moon-god (the duty presiding over annual plants) furnish me at this place, excellent food of every variety, confections, sweets, sauces and syrups."

Sloka 21

vicitrāṇi ca mālyāni pādapa pracyutāni ca |
surā ādīni ca peyāni māṃsāni vividhāni ca || 2-91-21

21. (ṃay the blessed moon-god furnish me); vichitrāṇi = many-coloured; mālyānicha = flowers; pādapa prachyutāni = which have just fallen from trees; peyāni = drinks; surādīni = like wine and others; vividhāni = and various kinds; māmsāni cha = of meat.

"ṃay the blessed moon-god furnish me many-coloured flowers which have just fallen from the flower-plants or trees, the drinks like wine and others as also various kinds of meat."

Sloka 22

evam samādhinā yuktaḥ tejasā apratimena ca |
śikṣā svara samāyuktam tapasā ca abravīn muniḥ || 2-91-22

22. muniḥ = that sage Bharadwaja; samādhinā = with his intense meditation; apratimena = having unequal; tejasācha = splendour; yuktaḥ = and with; tapasācha = austerity; abravīt = uttered; evam = thus; shīkṣa svara samāyuktam = in tones according to the science which deals with proper articulation and pronunciation of Vedic texts.

That sage Bharadwaja, with his intense meditation, possessing unequal splendour and with austerity, spoke as follows, in tones befitting proper articulation and pronunciation of the scriptural texts:

Sloka 23

manasā dhyāyataḥ tasya prān mukhasya kṛta anjaleḥ |
ājagmuḥ tāni sarvāṇi daivatāni pṛthak pṛthak || 2-91-23

23. tasya = (While) that sage Bharadwaja; kṛitāṇ^jale = with joined palms; dhyātaḥ = was meditating; manasā = with intelligence; prāṇmukhasya = turning his face towards the east; sarvāṇi = all; tāni daivatāni = those celestials; ājagmuḥ = arrived; pṛithak pṛithak = one by one.

While that sage Bharadwaja, with joined palms, was meditating with absorption in the east, all those celestials came one by one.

Sloka 24

malayam durduram caiva tataḥ sveda nudo anilaḥ |
upaspṛśya vavau yuktyā supriya ātmā sukhaḥ śivaḥ || 2-91-24

24. tataḥ = then; anilaḥ = a breeze; upaspṛishya = passing over; malayam = the mountain of ṃalaya; darduramchaiva = and Dardura; vavau = bean to blow; svedanudaḥ = removing sweat; yuktvā = by its influence; supriyātmā = which was pleasant in nature; sukhaḥ = comfortable; shivaḥ = and gracious

Then, a pleasant comfortable and gracious breeze, passing over the mountains of ṃalaya and Dardura, began to blw, removing sweat by its influence.

Sloka 25

tato abhyavartanta ghanā divyāḥ kusuma vṛṣṭayaḥ |
deva dundubhi ghoṣaḥ ca dikṣu sarvāsu śuśruve || 2-91-25

25. tataḥ = thereafter; divyāḥ = wonderful; ghanāḥ = clouds; abhyavarṣantaḥ = let loose; kusuma vṛiṣṭayaḥ = a rain of flowers; divya dundubhi ghoṣashcha = while the sound of divine gongs; shushrave = could be heard; sarvāsu = in every; dikṣu = quarter

Thereafter wonderful clouds let loose a rain of flowers, while the sound of divine gongs could be heard on every side.

Sloka 26

pravavuḥ ca uttamā vātā nanṛtuḥ ca apsaro gaṇāḥ |
prajagur deva gandharvā vīṇā pramumucuḥ svarān || 2-91-26

26. uttamāḥ = (to the) ṃelodious; prasavuḥ = rustling; vātāshcha = of the wind; apsarogaṇāḥ = troops of Apsaras( divine nymphs); nanṛitushcha = danced; devagandharāḥ = the celestials and celestial musicians; jagushcha = sang; vīṇāḥ = vina's stringed musical instruments; pramumuchuḥ = sent forth; svarān = their tones.

Troops of Apsaras (divine nymphs) danced to the melodious rustling of the wind. The clestials and the celestial musicians sang. Vinas, stringed musical instruments transmitted their tunes.

Sloka 27

sa śabdo dyām ca bhūmim ca prāṇinām śravaṇāni ca |
viveśa uccāritaḥ ślakṣṇaḥ samo laya guṇa anvitaḥ || 2-91-27

27. ṣakṣṇaḥ = sweet; layasamanvitaḥ = and harmonious; saḥ shabdaḥ = sounds; uchcharitaḥ = emitted; samaḥ = smoothly; vivesha = entered; dyāmcha = the sky; bhūmimcha = earth; shravaṇānicha = and the ears; prāṇinām = of beings.

Sweat and harmonious sounds emitted smoothly, entered the sky earth and the ears of beings.

Sloka 28

tasminn uparate śabde divye śrotra sukhe nṛṇām |
dadarśa bhāratam sainyam vidhānam viśva karmaṇaḥ || 2-91-28

28. tasmin (when) those; divye shabde = celestial melodies; uparate = ceased; shrotrasukhe = so pleasingly heard; nṛiṇām = by human beings; bhāratam = Bharata's; sainyam = army; dadarsha = saw; vidhānam = the wonderful creations; vishvakarmaṇaḥ = of Visvakarma.

When those celestial melodies ceased so pleasingly heard by the human ears, Bharata's army saw the wonderful creations of Visvakarma.

Sloka 29

babhūva hi samā bhūmiḥ samantāt panca yojanam |
śādvalair bahubhiḥ cannā nīla vaidūrya samnibhaiḥ || 2-91-29

29. samā = A levelled; bhūmiḥ = land; paṇ^ca yojanā = of approximately twenty miles; samantāt = around; babhūva hi = became; chhanna = covered; bahubhiḥ shādvalaiḥ = by many carpets of grass; nīla vaiDhūrya sannibhaiḥ = dark as emerald.

A levelled land of approximately twenty miles on all sides, became covered by many carpets of grass, dark as emerald.

Sloka 30

tasmin bilvāḥ kapitthāḥ ca panasā bīja pūrakāḥ |
āmalakyo babhūvuḥ ca cūtāḥ ca phala bhūṣaṇāḥ || 2-91-30

30. tasmin = At that place; bilvāḥ = Bilva; kapitthashcha = Kapitha; panasāḥ = Panasa; bīja pūrakaḥ = Citron; amalakyaḥ = amalaki; chūtāshcha = and mango trees; phala bhūṣaṇāḥ = laden with fruit; babhūvUḥ = were there.

At that place, Bilva, Kapittha, Panasa, Citron Amalaki and ṃango Trees laden with fruit appeared.

Sloka 31

uttarebhyaḥ kurubhyaḥ ca vanam divya upabhogavat |
ājagāma nadī divyā tīrajair bahubhir vṛtā || 2-91-31

31. vanam = A forest; divyopabhogāt = of divine enjoyments; uttarebhyaḥ kurubhyaḥ = from the tearitory of northern Kuru's; divyā = and a divine; nadī = river; kṛitā = shaped; bahubhiḥ = with various; tīrajaiḥ = trees on its bank; ājagāma = appeared (there)

A forest of divine enjoyments from the territory of northern Kuru's along with a river shaped with various trees on its bank appeared there.

Sloka 32

catuḥ śālāni śubhrāṇi śālāḥ ca gaja vājinām |
harmya prāsāda samghātāḥ toraṇāni śubhāni ca || 2-91-32

32. shubhrāṇi = ṣplendid; chatushshālāni = square mansions; shālāshcha = with stables; gaja vājinām = for elephants and horses; (as well as); shubhāni = resplendent; harmya prāsāda samyukta toraṇāni = gates with watch-towers flanked by turrets (could be seen).

Splendid square mansions with stables for elephants and horses as well as resplendent gates with watch-towers flanked by turrets were seen.

Sloka 33

sita megha nibham ca api rāja veśma sutoraṇam |
śukla mālya kṛta ākāram divya gandha samukṣitam || 2-91-33
catur asram asambādham śayana āsana yānavat |
divyaiḥ sarva rasair yuktam divya bhojana vastravat || 2-91-34
upakalpita sarva annam dhauta nirmala bhājanam |
klṛpta sarva āsanam śrīmat svāstīrṇa śayana uttamam || 2-91-35

33; 34; 35. rāja veshma chāpi = even a royal palace; (emerged); sita megha nibham = dazzling as a cloud; sutoraṇam = pierced by splendid arches; shukla mālya kṛitākāram = hung with white garlands; divya gandha samukṣitam = filled with fragrance of celestial perfumes; chaturashram = forming a perfect quadrangle; asambādham = spacious; shayanāsana yānavat = and furnished with couches; seats and palanquins; divyaiḥ = (supplied with) ambrosial; sarva rasaiḥ = drinks of every kind; divya bhojana vastravat = magnificent attire and food of every variety; upakalpita sarvānnam = well-prepared eatables of every description; dhauta nirmala bhājanam = in cleaned vessels free from dirt; klupta sarvāsanam = all kinds of seats arranged in order; svāstīrṇa shayanottamam = with superb couches duly covered with rich carpets; shrīmat = looked charming.

A royal palace emerged, dazzling as a cloud, pierced by splendid arches, hung with white garlands, filled with fragrance of celestial perfumes, forming a perfect quadrangle, spacious, furnished with couches seats and palanquins, supplied with ambrosial drinks of every kind as well as magnificent attire and food of every variety, well-prepared eatables of every description in cleaned vessels free from dirt and all kinds of seats arranged in order looked charming with superb couches duly covered with rich carpets.

Sloka 36

praviveśa mahā bāhur anujnāto maharṣiṇā |
veśma tad ratna sampūrṇam bharataḥ kaikayī sutaḥ || 2-91-36

36. mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; bharataḥ = Bharata; kaikeyīsutaḥ = the son of Kaikeyi; anṇ^ātaḥ = on the invitation; maharṣiṇā = of the great sage; pravivesha = entered; tat veshma = that palace; ratna sampūrṇam = filled with precious gems.

The mighty armed Bharata the son of Kaikeyi on invitation by the great sage, entered that palace filled with precious gems.

Sloka 37

anujagmuḥ ca tam sarve mantriṇaḥ sapurohitāḥ |
babhūvuḥ ca mudā yuktā tam dṛṣṭvā veśma samvidhim || 2-91-37

37. sarve = all; mantriṇaḥ = the ministers; sapurohitāḥ = together with priests; anujagmushcha = accompanied; tam = Bharata; dṛiṣṭvā = and on seeing; tam = that; veshma samvidhim = excellent get-up of the palace; babhūvushcha = they became; yuktāḥ = filled; mudā = with delight.

All the ministers together with priests accompanied Bharata and on entering that excellent get-up of the house, were filled with delight

Sloka 38

tatra rāja āsanam divyam vyajanam catram eva ca |
bharato mantribhiḥ sārdham abhyavartata rājavat || 2-91-38

avagāhya sutīrthāmḥ ca hradān sa utpala puṣkarān || 2-91-79
nīla vaidūrya varṇāmḥ ca mṛdūn yavasa saṃcayān |
nirvāpa artham paśūnām te dadṛśuḥ tatra sarvaśaḥ || 2-91-80

76; 77; 78; 79; 80. ta = those soldiers; dadṛishaḥ = saw; tatra = there; sarvashaḥ = all around; dana dhāvana samchayān = heaps of twigs used for cleaning the teeth; shuklām = with white; mshumatashchāpi = brushes; shuklān = white; chandana kalkāmshcha = sandal pastes; avatiṣṭhataḥ = stored; samudgeṣu = in caskets; parimṛiṣṭhām = polished; darpaṇāncha = mirrors; samchayān chāpi = piles; vāsasām = of clothes; sahasrashaḥ = thousands; yugmānicha = of pairs; pādukopānahām = of shoes and sandals; āṇjanīḥ = caskets containing collyrium for the eyes; kamkatān = combs; kūrchān = brushes; vastrāṇi cha = raiments; dhanūmṣicha = bows; marmatrānāni = protectors of vitals; chitrāṇi = strange; shayanāni = couches; āsanāni = and seats; pratipāa hradān = drinking ponds; kharoṣṭragaja vājinām = for donkeys; camels; elephants and horses; hradān = lakes; avagāhya sutīrthān = with good stairs for descent; sotapalapuṣkaran = with water lilies and lotuses; ākāshavarṇapratimān = with sky-blue colour; svachchhatoyān = with clear water; sukhaplavān- offering a comfortable bath; mṛidūn = and soft; yavasa samohayān = grasses; nirvāpārthān = useful for feeding; pashūnām = the animal; nīpa vaiDhurya varṇāmshcha = having colour of ṇipa trees and of cat's eye gems.

Those soldiers saw there heaps of twings used for cleaning the teeth with white brushes at their tips, white sandal pastes stored in caskets, polished mirrors, piles of clothes, thousands of pairs of shoes and sandals, caskets containing collyrium for the eyes; combs, brushed, raiments bows, protectors of vitals, strange couches and seats, drinking ponds for donkeys camels elephants and horses, lakes with good stairs for descent having water-lilies and lotuses with sky-blue colour, with clear water offering a comfortable bath and soft grasses having a colour of Nipa tree and of cat's eye gems useful for feeding animals.

Nipa tree: Nanclea cadamba

Sloka 81

vyasmayanta manuṣyaste svapnakalphaṃ tadadbhutaṃ |
dṛṣṭvā.atithyaṃ kṛtaṃ tādṛgbharatasya mahārṣiṇā 2-91-81

81. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tat = that; adbhutam = wonderful; tādṛik = and such; svapnakalpam = a dream-like; ātithyam = hospitality; kṛitam = offered; maharṣiṇā = by the great sage Bharadwaja; te manuṣyāḥ = those men; vyasmayanta = were surprised.

Seeing such a wonderful and dreamlike hospitality offered by the great sage Bharadwaja, all those men were surprised.

Sloka 82

ity evam ramamāṇānām devānām iva nandane |
bharadvāja āśrame ramye sā rātrir vyatyavartata || 2-91-82

82. rama māṇānām = while they were enjoying; ityevam = thus; ramye = in that charming; bharadwājāshrame = hermitage of Bharadwaja; devānāmiva = like celestials; (enjoying); nanadane = in the garden of nandana; sā rātriḥ = that night; vytyavartata = had elapsed.

While they were enjoying thus in that charming hermitage of Bharadwaja, in the same manner as celestials enjoy the garden of Nandna, that night had elapsed.

Sloka 83

pratijagmuḥ ca tā nadyo gandharvāḥ ca yathā āgatam |
bharadvājam anujnāpya tāḥ ca sarvā vara anganāḥ || 2-91-83

83. anujJṇāpya = having been permitted; bharadvājam = by Bharadwaja; tāḥ nadyaḥ = those rivers; gandharvāshcha = the celestial musicians; sarvāḥ = (and) all; tāḥ = those; varāṇganāshcha = beautiful women; pratijagmushcha = went back; yathāgatam = by the same way they came.

Having been permitted by Bharadwaja, those rivers, the celestial musicians and all those beautiful women went back by the same way they came.

Sloka 84

tathaiva mattā madira utkaṭā narāḥ |
tathaiva divya aguru candana ukṣitāḥ |
tathaiva divyā vividhāḥ srag uttamāḥ |
pṛthak prakīrṇā manujaiḥ pramarditāḥ || 2-91-84

84. narāḥ = the soldiers; madirotkaṭāḥ = intoxicated with spirituous liquor; tathaiva mattāḥ = were likewise excited with joy; tathaiva = Like wise; divyāgaru chandanokṣitāḥ = they were drenched in charming aloes and sandal paste; pramarditāḥ = crushed; prakīrṇāḥ = and scattered; pṛithak = at a distance; vividhāḥ = various kinds; divyāh = of beautiful; sraguttamāḥ = and excellent garlands; tathaiva = were there likewise.

The soldiers, intoxicated with spirituous liquor, were likewise excited with joy. Likewise, the men were drenched in charming aloes and sandal paste. Various kinds of excellent and charming garlands were there, crushed and garlands were there, crushed and scattered at distances, likewise.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ekanavatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 91st chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
