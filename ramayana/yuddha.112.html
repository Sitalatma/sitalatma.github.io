Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 112
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

ṃatali, Indra's charioteer leaves the battle-field and returns to his heaven. Then, Rama instructs Lakshmana to arrange for the installation of Vibhishana on the throne of Lanka. Lakshmana arranges to get sea-water from some monkey-chiefs and sprinkles it on vibhishana, to instal him as the King of Lanka, in the presence of the demons there. Then, Rama sends his message to Seetha through Hanuma.

Sloka 1

te rāvaṇavadhaṃ dṛṣṭvā devagandharvadānavāḥ |
jagmuḥ svaiḥ svai rvimānaiste kathayantaḥ śubhāḥ kathāḥ || 6-112-1

1. dṛiṣhṭvā = ḥaving seen; rāvaṇa vadham = the destruction of Ravana; te = those; devagandharvadānavāḥ = gods; Gandharvas the celestial musicians and the demons; jagmuḥ = went away; svaiḥ svaiḥ vimānaiḥ = in their respective aerial cars; te kathayantaḥ = narrating as they were; shubhāḥ kathāḥ = about the auspicious narratives.

Having seen the destruction of Ravana, those gods, celestial musicians and demons, while chatting about the auspicious narrative, went away in their respective aerial cars.

Sloka 2

rāvaṇasya vadhaṃ ghoraṃ rāghavasya parākramam |
suyuddhaṃ vānarāṇāṃ ca sugrīvasya ca mantritam || 6-112-2
anurāgaṃ ca vīryaṃ ca saumitre rlakṣmaṇasya ca |
pativratātvam sītāyā hanūmati parākramam || 6-112-3
kathayanto mahābhāgā jagmurhṛṣṭa yathāgatam |

2-3. kathayantaḥ = chatting themselves about; ghoram vadham = the awful destruction; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rāghavasya parākramam = the prowess of Rama; vānarāṇām suyuddham = the admirable combat of the monkeys; sugrivasya mantritam = the counsel of ṣugreeva; anurāgam cha vīryam cha = the affection and the valour; lakṣhmaṇasya = of Lakshmana; saumitreḥ = the son of ṣumitra; pativratātvam sītāyāḥ = the loyalty of ṣeetha to her husband; hanumati parākramam = and the strength; mahābhāgāḥ = the illustrious gods and others; of ḥanuma; hṛiṣhṭāḥ = were rejoiced; jagmuḥ = and returned; yathāgatam = as they had come.

chatting themselves about the awful destruction of Ravana, the terrific prowess of Rama, the admirable combat of the monkeys, the counsel tendered by Sugreeva, the affection and valour of Lakshmana; the son of Sumitra; the loyaltyof Seetha to her husband and about the strength of Hanuma, the illustrious celestials and others rejoicingly returned as they had come.

Sloka 4

rāghavastu rathaṃ divyamindradattaṃ śikhiprabham || 6-112-4
anujñāya mahābāhurmātaliṃ pratyapūjayat |

4. anujJṇāya = taking leave; divyam ratham = of the celestial chariot; indradattam = which had been given by Indra the lord of celestials; shikhiprabham = and which = shone like fire; mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; rāghavastu = Rama; pratya pūjayat = respectfully saluted; mātalim = ṃatali.

Taking leave of the celestial chariot, which had been given by Indra the lord of celestials and which shone like fire, the mighty armed Rama respectfully saluted ṃatali.

Sloka 5

rāghaveṇābhyanujñāto mātaliḥ śakrasārathiḥ || 6-112-5
divyaṃ taṃ tathamāsthāya divamevotpapāta ha |

5. abhyanujJṇātaḥ = as assented; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; mātaliḥ = ṃatali; shakrasārathiḥ = Indra's charioteer; āsthāya = mounting; divyam tam ratham = that celestial chariot; utpapāta = ascended; diṇameva = to the heaven itself.

As assented by Rama, ṃatali Indra's charioteer, mounting that celestial chariot, ascended to the heaven itself.

Sloka 6

tasmiṃstu divamāruḍhe sarathe rathināṃ varaḥ || 6-112-6
rāghavaḥ paramaprītaḥ sugrīvaṃ pariṣasvaje |

6. tasmin = when that matali; ārūDhe = ascended; divam = to heaven; sarathe = along with his chariot; rāghavaḥ = Rama; varaḥ = the foremost one; rathinām = among the chariot-warriors; paramaprītaḥ = was extremely pleased; pariṣhasvaje = and embraced; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva.

When ṃatali ascended to heaven along with his chariot, Rama, the foremost one among the chariot-warriors was highly rejoiced and embraced Sugreeva.

Sloka 7

parṣvajya ca sugrīvaṃ lakṣmaṇenābhivāditaḥ || 6-112-7
pūjyamāno harigaṇairājagāma balālayam |

7. pariṣhvajya cha = having embraced; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; abhivāditaḥ = and having been greeted; lakṣhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; pūjyamānaḥ = and having been honoured; harigaṇaiḥ = by the monkey-troops; ājagāma = (Rama) came; balālayam = to the camp where the army had been stationed.

Having embraced Sugreeva greeted by Lakshmana and having been honoured by the monkey-troops. Rama came to the camp where the army had been stationed.

Sloka 8

athovāca sa kākutsthaḥ samīpaparivartinam || 6-112-8
saumitriṃ sattvasaṃpannaṃ lakṣmaṇaṃ śubhalakṣaṇam |

8. atha = then; saḥ kākutthsaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; (as follows) lakṣhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; saumitrim = the son of ṣumitra; sattva sampannam = endowed as he was with strength; shubhalakṣhaṇam = and having auspicious marks; samīpa parivartanam = who stayed nearby.

Then, Rama spoke (as follows) to Lakshmana, son of Sumitra, who was endowed with strength and having auspicious marks, who stayed nearby.

Sloka 9

vibhīṣaṇamimaṃ saumya laṅkāyāmabhiṣecaya || 6-112-9
anuraktaṃ ca bhaktaṃ ca tathā pūrvopakāriṇam |

9. saumya = O the benevolent Lakshmana!; abhiṣhechaya = consecrate; laṇ^kāyām = on the throne of Lanka; imam vibhīṣhaṇam = this Vibhishana; anuraktam = to whom everyone is attached; bhaktam cha = a loyal person; tathā = and; pūrvopakāriṇam = the one who has formerly done a service to us.

"O the benevolent Lakshmana! Consecrate on the throne of Lanka, this Vibhishana to whom everyone is attached, a loyal person as he is and the one who has formerly done a service to us."

Sloka 10

eṣa me paramaḥ kāmo yadimaṃ rāvaṇānujam || 6-112-10
laṅkāyāṃ saumya paśyeyamabhiṣiktaṃ vibhīṣaṇam |

10. saumya = O gentle one! eṣhaḥ = this; me = is my; paramaḥ = paramount; kāmaḥ = desire; yat = that; pashyeyam = I should see; imam vibhīṣhaṇam = this Vibhishana; rāvaṇānujam = the brother of Ravana; abhiṣhiktam = being consecrated for throne; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka.

"O gentle one! This s my paramount desire that I should see this Vibhishana, the brother of Ravana being consecrated for throne in Lanka."

Sloka 11

evamuktastu saumitrī rāghaveṇa mahātmanā || 6-112-11
tathetyuktvā susaṃhṛṣṭaḥ sauvarṇam ghaṭamādade |

11. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; mahātmanā = by the great souled; rāghaveṇa = Rama; saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; uktvā tatheti = saying; "so it be"; susamhṛiṣhṭaḥ = and rejoicingly; ādade = procured; sauvarṇam = a golden; ghaṭam = pot.

Hearing the words of the great soled Rama, Lakshmana replied, 'so it be' and rejoicingly procured a golden pot.

Sloka 12

taṃ ghaṭaṃ vānarendrāṇām haste dattvā manojavān || 6-112-12
vyādideśa mahāsattvaḥ samudrasalilaṃ tadā |

12. mahāsattvaḥ = that mighty Lakshmana; tadā = then; dattvā = placing; tam = that; ghaṭam = pot; haste = in the hands; vānerendrāṇām = of the monkey-chiefs; vyādidesha = instructed; manojavān = them; who were as swift as thought; samudra salilam = (to bring) sea-water.

That mighty Lakshmana, then placing that pot in the hands of the monkey-chiefs, instructed them, who were as swift as thought, to bring sea-water.

Sloka 13

atiśīghram tato gatvā vānarāste manojavāḥ || 6-112-13
āgatāstu jalaṃ gṛhya samudrādvānarottamāḥ |

13. tataḥ = thereupon; te vānarottamāḥ = those excellent monkeys; manojavāḥ = with the swiftness of thought; gattvā = moving; atishīghram = very quickly; āgatāḥ = returned; gṛihya = having obtained; jalam = the water; samudrāt = from the sea.

Thereupon, those excellent monkeys, with the swiftness of thought, moving very quickly from that place, returned, having obtained the water from the sea.

Sloka 14

tatastvekaṃ ghaṭaṃ gṛhya saṃsthāpya paramāsane || 6-112-14
ghaṭena tena saumitrirabhyaṣiñcadvibhīṣaṇam |
laṅkāyāṃ rakṣasāṃ madhye rājānaṃ rāmaśāsanāt || 6-112-15
vidhinā mantradṛṣṭena suhṛdgaṇasamāvṛtaḥ |

14-15. tataḥ = thereafter; saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; suhṛidgaṇa samāvṛitaḥ = together with his friends; gṛihya = taking; ekam = one; ghaṭam = pot; vibhīshaṇam samsthāpya = making Vibhishana to sit; paramāsane = on the throne; abhyaṣhiṇ^chat = sprinkled (him); tena ghaṭena = (with sea-water) from that pot; vidhinā = in accordance with the rule (in Vedic scriptures); rakṣhasām madhye = in the middle of the demons; rājānām = to make him the king; laṇ^kāyām = for the kingdom of Lanka; rāma shāsanāt = as directed by Rama.

Thereupon, Lakshmana together with his friends, taking one pot and making Vibhishana to sit on the throne, sprinkled him with sea-water from that pot in accordance with the rule prescribed in Vedic scriptures, while the demons were witnessing the ceremony, to make him the king for Lanka, as directed by Rama.

Sloka 16

abhyaṣiñcaṃstadā sarve rākṣasā vānarāstathā || 6-112-16
praharṣamatulaṃ gatvā tuṣṭuvū rāmameva ha |

16. tadā = then; sarve rākṣhasāḥ = all the demons; tathā = as well as; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; abhyaṣhiṇ^chan = consecrated; (Vibhishana); gatvā = having obtained; atulam praharṣham = extra ordinary delight; tuṣhṭuvuḥ = (they) eulogized; rāmeva hi = Rama indeed.

Then, all the demons as well as the monkeys consecrated Vibhishana. Having gained extra ordinary delight, they indeed eulogized Rama.

Sloka 17

tasyāmātyā jahṛṣire bhaktā ye cāsya rākṣasāḥ || 6-112-17
dṛṣṭvābhiṣktaṃ laṅkāyāṃ rākṣasendraṃ vibhīṣaṇam |

17. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana; rākṣhasendram = the lord of demons; abhṣhiktam = consecrated; laṇ^kāyām = for the throne of Lanka; tasya = his; amātyāḥ = (four) counsellors; ye rākṣhasāḥ = and those demons; asya bhaktāḥ = who were devoted to him; jahṛiṣhire = were rejoiced.

Seeing Vibhishana the lord of demons consecrated for the throne of Lanka, his four counsellors who were always together with him and such of those demons who were devoted to him, were rejoiced.

Sloka 18

rāghavaḥ pramāṃ prītiṃ jagāma sahalakṣmaṇaḥ || 6-112-18
sa tadrājyaṃ mahatprāpya rāmadattaṃ vibhīṣaṇaḥ |

18. rāghavaḥ = Rama; saha lakṣhmaṇaḥ = together with Lakshmana; jagāma = obtained; paramām = great; prītim = delight; saḥ = that; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; prāpya = having gained; tat mahat rājyam = that great kingdom; rāma dattam = bestowed on him by Rama; (was also greatly delighted).

Rama together with Lakshmana were greatly delighted. That Vibhishana, having gained that great kingdom bestowed on him by Rama, was also greatly delighted.

Sloka 19

prakṛtīḥ sāntyayitvā ca tato rāmamupāgamat || 6-112-19
dadhyakṣatān modakāṃśca lājāḥ sumanasastathā |
ājahruratha saṃtuṣṭāḥ paurāstasmai niśācarāḥ || 6-112-20

19-20. sāntvayitvā = after consoling; prakṛitīḥ = his people; tataḥ = (Vibhishana) then; upāgamat = approached; rāmam = Rama; tataḥ = then; nishācharāḥ = the demons; paurāḥ = who were residing in the city; samtuṣhṭāḥ = were quite delighted; ājahruḥ = and brought; tasmaiḥ = to him; (by way of presents) dadhi = curds; akṣhatān = unbroken grains of rice; modakāmshcha = sweets shaped like balls; lājāḥ = parched grains of unhusked rice; tathā = and; sumanasaḥ = flower also.

After consoling his people, Vibhishana then sought to presence of Rama. Now, the demons who were residing in the city were quite delighted and brought to him (by way of presents) curds, unbroken grains of rice, sweets shaped like balls, parched grains of unhusked rice and flowers too.

Sloka 21

sa tān gṛhītvā durdharṣo rāghavāya nyavedayat || 6-112-21
māṅgalyaṃ maṅgalaṃ sarvaṃ lakṣmaṇāya ca vīryavān |

21. gṛihītvā = accepting; tān = them; vīryavān = the valiant; saḥ = Vibhishana; durdharṣhaḥ who was unconquerable; nyavedayat = offered; sarvam = all; māṇ^galyam = those auspicious objects; rāghavāya = to Rama; lakṣhmaṇāya = and to Lakshmana; mangalam = happily.

Accepting them, the valiant and unconquerable Vibhishana happily offered all those auspicious objects to Rama and Lakshmana.

Sloka 22

kṛtakāryaṃ samṛddhārthaṃ dṛṣṭvā rāmo vibhīṣaṇam || 6-112-22
pratijagrāha tatsarvaṃ tasyaiva priyakāmyayā |

22. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana; kṛita kāryam = who had accomplished his act; samṛiddhārtham and increased his resources; rāmaḥ = Rama; pratijagrāha = accepted; tat = it; sarvam = all; tasya priya kāmyayā eva = with a sole desire to show gracefulness to him.

Seeing Vibhishana, who had accomplished his act and increased his resources, Rama accepted it all, with a sole desire to show gratefulness to him.

Sloka 23

tatah śailopamaṃ vīraṃ prāñjaliṃ praṇataṃ sthitam || 6-112-23
uvācedaṃ vaco rāmo hanūmantaṃ plavaṅgamam |

23. tāḥ = thereupon; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; idam vachaḥ = the following words; vīra hanūmantam = to the valiant;plavaṇ^gamam = the monkey; shailopamam = who was equal in size to a mountain; sthitam = and who was standing; praṇatam = humbly; prāṇ^jalim = with his palms joined together for salutation.

Thereupon, Rama spoke the following words to the valiant Hanuma the monkey who was equal in size to a mountain and who was standing in humility, with his hands joined in salutation.

Sloka 24

anujznāpya mahārājamimaṃ saumya vibhīṣaṇam || 6-112-24
praviśya nagarīṃ laṅkāṃ kauśalaṃ brūhimaithilīm |

24. saumya = O the benevolent one!; anujJṇāpya = taking permission from; imam vibhīṣhaṇam = this Vibhishana; māhārājam = the great king; praveshya = and entering into; laṇ^kām nagarim = the City of Lanka; brūhi = inform; kaushalam = about our welfare; maithilīm = to ṣeetha.

"O the benevolent one! Taking permission from this Vibhishana, the great king and entering into the City of Lanka, inform about our welfare to Seetha."

Sloka 25

vaidehyai māṃ kuśalinaṃ sugrīvaṃ ca salakṣmaṇam || 6-112-25
acakṣva vadatāṃ śreṣṭha rāvaṇaṃ ca hataṃ raṇe |

25. shreṣhṭha vadatām = O ḥanuma; the proficient one in speech!; āchakṣhva = tell; vaidehyai = ṣeetha; (that); mām = myself; sa lakṣhmaṇam = along with Lakshmana; sugrīvam cha = and ṣugreeva; kushalinam = are well; rāvaṇam = and that Ravana; hatam = had been killed; raṇe = in battle.

"O Hanuma, the proficient one in speech! Inform Seetha that myself together with Lakshmana and Sugreeva are well and that Ravana had been killed in battle."

Sloka 26

priyametadudāhṛtya vaidehyastvaṃ harīśvara || 6-112-26
pratigṛhya ca saṃdeśamupāvartitumarhasi |

26. harīshvara = O the master of monkeys!; udāhṛitya = making clear; etat = this; priyam = favourite news; vaidehyāḥ = to ṣeetha; arhasi = you ought; upāvartitum = to return; pratigṛihya = taking back; samdesham = her message.

"O Hanuma the master of monkeys! ṃaking clear this favourite news to Seetha, you ought to return, taking back her message."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvādaśādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 112th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
