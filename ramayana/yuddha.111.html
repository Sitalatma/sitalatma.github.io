Introduction

ṃandodari stares at the dead body of Ravana and laments, recollecting the strength and power of Ravana. But, she realizes that Rama, who killed Ravana, is none other than Vishnu the lord of maintenance of the world. She further laments that Ravana has done a sinful deed in abducting Seetha and that he has faced the consequence in the form of his death; in the hands of Rama. She describes the charming personality and the unconquerable prowess of Ravana, but expresses her surprise how he has been killed by a mortal like Rama. ṃandodari's co-wives console her. ṃeanwhile, Rama asks Vibhishana to perform obsequies to Ravana. Initially, Vibhishana refuses to perform the last rites, but when Rama convinces him, he begins to perform the funeral of Ravana. After the funeral is over, Vibhishana comes and joins Rama, who along with Lakshmana, Sugreeva and others experience happiness for having destroyed their enemy.

agnayo dīpyamānāste tadādhvaryusamīritāḥ || 6-111-112
śaraṇābhigatāh sarve purastattasya te yayuḥ |

112. te = those; agnayaḥ = sacrificial fires; dīpyamānāḥ = were ignited; adhvaryu samīritāḥ = and re-animated; as they were; by Adhvaryu priests the performers of the sacrificial act; tadā = at that time; te = those fires; sharaṇābhigatāḥ = were contained in earthen pots; yayuḥ = went; purastāt = in front; tasya = of Ravana's body.

Those sacrificial fires were ignited and re-animated, as they were, by Adhvaryu priests, the performers of the sacrificial act, at that time. Those sacrificial fires were contained in earthen pots and went in front of Ravana's body.

Sloka 113

antaḥpurāṇi sarvāṇi rudamānāni satvaram || 6-111-113
pṛṣṭhato.anuyayustāni plavamānāni sarvataḥ |

113. tāni sarvāṇi = all those; antaḥpurāṇi = women of the gynaecium; rudamānāni = while weeping; annyayuḥ pṛiṣhṭhataḥ satvanam = followed at his heels with quick paces; plavamānāni sarvataḥ = stumbling as they were; on all sides.

All those women of the gynaecium, while weeping, followed at his heels with quick paces, stumbling, as they were, on all sides.

Sloka 114

rāvaṇam prayate deśe sthāpya te bhṛśaduḥkhitāḥ || 6-111-114
citāṃ candanakāṣṭhaiśca padmakośīrasaṃvṛtām |
brāhmyā saṃvartayāmāsū rāṅkavāstaraṇāvṛtām || 6-111-115
vartate vedavihito rājño vai paścimaḥ kratuḥ |

114-115. sthāpya = keeping; rāvaṇam = (the body of ) Ravana; prayate deshe = on a consecrated spot; (Vibhishana and others); bhṛisha duḥkhitāḥ = who were very much afflicted with grief; samvartayāmāsuḥ chitām = piled up a pyre; brāhmyā = which was sacred; chandana kāṣhṭhaishcha = with logs of sandalwood; padma koshīra samvṛitām = moistened with perfumes called Padmaka and Koshira; rāṇka vāstaraṇāvṛitam = covered with the skin of black antelopes; vartate = turned about; pashchima kratuḥ = to perform the obsequies; vedavihitaḥ = in accordance = with the Vedic rites; rājJṇaḥ = in honour of the king.

Keeping the body of Ravana on a consecrated spot, Vibhishana and others, who were very much afflicted with grief, piled up a sacred pyre, with logs of sandalwood, moistened with perfumes called Padmaka and Koshira and covered with the skin of black antelopes, turned about to perform the obsequies in accordance with Vedic rites in honour of the king.

Sloka 116

pracakrū rākṣasendrasya pitṛmedhamanuttamam || 6-111-116
vediṃ ca dakṣiṇaprācyāṃ yathāsthānaṃ ca pāvakam |

116. prachakruḥ = (ṭhey) performed; pitṛimedham = the ancestral oblations; rāvaṇasya = to Ravana; anuttamam = in a superb way; vedimcha = (ṭhey constructed) an altar; dakṣhiṇaprāchyām = in the south-east (of the funeral pile); pāvakam yathāsthānam = and placed the sacred fire in its proper place.

They performed the ancestral oblations to Ravana in a superb way. They constructed an altar in the south-east the funeral pile) and placed the sacred fire in its proper place.

Sloka 117

pṛṣadājyena saṃpūrṇaṃ sruvaṃ skandhe pracikṣipuḥ || 6-111-117
pādayoḥ śakaṭaṃ prādurūrvoścolūkhalaṃ tadā |

117. prachikṣhipuḥ = (ṭhey) poured; sruvam = a small wooden ladle; sampūrṇam = full; vṛiṣhadājyena = of ghee mixed with curds; skande = on his shoulders; prāduḥ = and placed; shakaṭam = a cart; pādayaḥ = at his feet; tadā = and then; ulūkhalam = a wooden mortar; ūrvoḥ cha = at his thighs.

They poured a ladle-full of ghee mixed with curds on his shoulders, placed a cart at his feet and then a wooden mortar at his thighs.

Sloka 118

dārupātrāni sarvāṇi araṇiṃ cottarāraṇim || 6-111-118
dattvā tu musalaṃ cānyaṃ yathāsthānaṃ vicakramuḥ |

118. dattvā = having set; sarvāṇi = all; dārupātrāṇi = the wooden vessels; araṇim = the lower piece of wood used for kindling fire at a sacrifice; uttarāraṇim = and the upper wood (which is rotated at great spped; on to lower one; to produce fire by friction); musalam = the wooden pestle used in the sacrifice; anyamcha = and other things; yathāsthānam = at their proper place; vichakramuḥ = they circled around the funeral pyre.

Having set at their proper place, all the wooden vessels (used in Agnihotra), the lower piece of wood used for kindling fire at a sacrifice and the upper piece of wood (which is rotated at great speed on to lower one, to produce fire by friction), the wooden pestle and other things used in the sacrifice, they circled around the funeral pyre.

Sloka 119

śāstradṛṣṭena vidhinā maharṣivihitena ca || 6-111-119
tatra medhyaṃ paśuṃ hatvā rākṣasendrasya rākṣasāḥ |
paristaraṇīkāṃ rājñoghṛtāktāṃ samaveśayan || 6-111-120

119-120. vidhena maharṣhivihitena = according to ordinance laid down by eminent sages; shāstradṛiṣhṭena = and according to the rules viewed in the Vedas; hatvā = having sacrificed; medhyam pashum = a goat fit for sacrifice; tatra = at that spot; rākṣhasāḥ = the demons; samaveshayan paristaraṇikām = spread the limbs of the dead goat; ghṛitāktām = dampened with ghee; rājJṇaḥ = on Ravana; rākṣhasendrasya = the king of demons.

According to ordinance laid down by eminent sages and according to the rules viewed in Vedas, having sacrificed a goat fit for sacrifice at that spot, the demons dampened with ghee on Ravana the King of demons.

Sloka 121

gandhairmālyairalaṃkṛtya rāvaṇaṃ dīnāmānasāḥ || 6-111-121
vibhīṣaṇasahāyāste vasttraiśca vividhairapi |
lājairavakiranti sma bāṣpapūrṇamukhāstathā || 6-111-122

121-122. alamkṛitya = having decorated; rāvaṇam = the body of Ravana; gandhaiḥ = with perfumes; mālyaiḥ = garlands; tathā vividhaiḥ vastraiḥ = and various kinds of clothes; te = those demons; vibhīṣhaṇa sahāyāḥ = accompanied by Vibhishana; dīnamānasāḥ = who were distressed in mind; tathā = and who were distressed in mind; tathā = and bāṣhpapūrṇa mukhāḥ = and with their faces bathed in tears; avākirantisma = poured; lājaiḥ = parched grains of rice.

Having decorated the body of Ravana with perfumes, garlands and various kinds of clothes; those demons accompanied by Vibhishana, distressed as they were in their minds, poured parched grains of rice, with their faces bathed in tears.

Sloka 123

sa dadau pāvakan tasya vidhiyuktan vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
snātvā caivārdravastreṇa tilān darbhavimiśritān || 6-111-123
udakena ca saṃmiśrān pradāya vidhipūrvakam |
pradāya codakaṃ tasmai mūrdhnā cainaṃ namasya ca || 6-111-124
tāḥ striyoanunayāmāsa sāntvamuktvā punaḥ punaḥ |
gamyatāmiti tāḥ sarvā viviśurnagaraṃ tataḥ || 6-111-125

123-125. saḥ vibhīshaṇah = that Vibhishana; dadau pāvakam = set fire; tasya = to Ravana; vidhiyuktam = according to the rules in scriptures; snātvā = washing himself; pvadāya = and offering; ārdravastreṇa = in his wet clothes; vidhipūrvakam = according to scriptural ordinance; tilān = sesame seeds; sammishritān = mixed; udakena cha = with water; darbha mishritān = as well as blades of sacred Kusha grass; namasyacha = and offering salutation; enam = to Ravana; mūrdhanā = with his head; anunayāmāsa = and entreated; tāḥ striyaḥ = those women; gamyatām iti = to return; sāntvayitvā = consoling them; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; tataḥ = then; tāḥ sarvāḥ = all of them; nagaram vivashuḥ = returned to the city.

That Vibhishana set fire to Ravana, according to the rules in the scriptures. Washing himself and offering in his wet clothes, according to scriptural ordinance, sesame seeds mixed with water, as well as blades of Kusha grass and offering obeisance to Ravana by bowing his head, Vibhishana entreated those women to return, consoling them again and again. Then, all of the returned to the City.

Sloka 126

praviṣṭāsu ca sarvāsu rākṣasīṣu vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
rāmapārśvamupāgamya tadātiṣṭhadvinītavat || 6-111-126

126. strīṣhu = (While) those women; praviṣhṭāsu = were re-entering; purīm = the city; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; rākṣhasendraḥ = the lord of demons; upāgamya = on approaching; rāmapārshvam = Rama's vicinity; atiṣhṭhat = remained standing; tadā = then; vinītavat = with humility.

When those women were re-entering the City, Vibhishana the lord of demons on approaching Rama's vicinity then remained standing there with humility.

Sloka 127

rāmoapi saha sainyena sasugrīvaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |
harṣan lebhe ripuṃ hatvā yathā vṛtraṃ vajradharo yathā || 6-111-127

127. hatvā = having destroyed; ripum = the enemy; ramo.api = Rama too; lebhe harṣham = experience happiness; sainyena saha = along with his army; sasugrīvaḥ = as well as with ṣugreeva; salakṣhmanaḥ = and Lakshmana; vajradhara yathā = even as Indra; the wielder of thunderbolt; vṛitram = did on destroying Vritra the demon.

Having destroyed the enemy, Rama too experienced happiness, along with his army as well as with Sugreeva and Lakshmana, even as Indra the wielder of thunderbolt did on destroying Vritra the demon.

Sloka 128

tato vimuktvā saśaraṃ śarāsanaṃ |
mahendradattaṃ kavacaṃ ca tanmahat |
vimucya roṣaṃ ripunigrahāttato |
rāmaḥ susaumyatvamupāgato.ariha || 6-111-128

128. tataḥ = thereupon; vimuchya = taking off; sharāsanam = his bow; sasharam = along with his arrows; mahat tat kavacham = and that great armour; mahendradattam = endowed to him by Indra; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; arihā = the annihilator of his enemies; vimuktvā = abandoning; roṣham = his anger; ripunigrahāt = because of the defeat of his enemy; tataḥ = then; upāgataḥ = obtained; susaumyatvam = charming benevolence.

Thereupon, taking off his bow and arrows as also that great armour endowed to him by Indra, Rama the annihilator of his enemies, taking off his anger too, because of the enemy's defeat, then obtained a charming benevolence.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekādaśādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 111th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
