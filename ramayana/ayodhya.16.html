Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 16
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter, Sumantra arrives at Rama's palace to fetch him to royal court. Rama starts to the royal court to meet his father. On the way Rama observes the jubiliant city.

Sloka 1

sa tat antaḥ pura dvāram samatītya jana ākulam |
praviviktām tataḥ kakṣyām āsasāda purāṇavit || 2-16-1
prāsa kārmuka bibhradbhir yuvabhir mṛṣṭa kuṇḍalaiḥ |
apramādibhir eka agraiḥ svanuraktaiḥ adhiṣṭhitām || 2-16-2

1;2. saḥ = that sumantra; purāṇavit = who knew the ancient sacred works; samatītya = passed over; tad = that; janākulam = crowded; antaḥpura dvāram = palace entrance; tataḥ = then; āsasāda = reached secluded; kakshhyām = inner apartment; adhishhṭhitām = guarded by; yūabhiḥ = youth; prāsakārmukabibhradbhiḥ = wearing bows and darts; mṛisṭakundalaiḥ = heaving polished ear-rings; apramādibhiḥ = who were vigilant; ekāgraiḥ = attentive; svanuraktaiḥ = and devoted to the king.

That Sumantra, who knew the ancient sacred works, passed over the crowded entrance of the palace and reached the secluded inner apartment. In that area of the inner apartment, some youths , wearing darts and bows, were on security-duty with due vigilance and attention. They were all greatly devoted to the king and were wearing polished ear-rings.

Sloka 3

tatra kāṣāyiṇo vṛddhān vetra pāṇīn svalamkṛtān |
dadarśa viṣṭhitān dvāri stry adhyakṣān susamāhitān || 2-16-3

3. tatra = there; dadarsha = (he)saw; vṛiddhān = aged; stryadhyakshhān = palace supervisors; kāshhayiṇaḥ = with orange coloured clothes; svalankṛitān = well-adorned themselves; vetrapāṇīn = having vanes in their hands; susamāhitān = well composed; vishhṭitān = being posted; dvāri = in door-ways.

There he saw aged palace-supervisors wearing orange coloured clothes, adorning themselves well, having canes in their hands, well composed and being posted at door-ways.

Sloka 4

te samīkṣya samāyāntam rāma priya cikīrṣavaḥ |

4. te sarve = all of them; rāmapriyachikīrshhavaḥ = the well-wishers of Rama; samīkshhya = saw; samāyāntam = him coming; utpatitāḥ = raised;āsanabhyaḥ = from their seats; sahasā = all at once; sasambhramāḥ = hurriedly.

All of them, the well-wishers of Rama, saw that Sumantra arriving and raised from their seats hurriedly at once.

Sloka 5

prativeditam ājñāya sūtam abhyantaram pituḥ |
tatra eva ānāyayām āsa rāghavaḥ priya kāmyayā || 2-16-5

5. vimītātmā = humble minded; sūtaputraḥ = ṣumantra; uvācha = spoke; tān = to them; ākhyāta = "ṃake known; rāmāya = to Rama; kshhipram = immediately; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; tishhṭati = is standing; pradikshhaṇaḥ = with revernece; dvāri = at the entrance.

That humble-minded Sumantra spoke to them thus, "Tell Rama immediately that Sumantra is waiting with reverence at the entrance"

Sloka 6

te rāmamupasamgamya bhartuḥ priyacikīrṣavaḥ |
sahabhāryāya rāmāya kṣipramevācacakṣire || 2-16-6

6. te = they; priyachikīrshhavaḥ = the well wishers; bhartuḥ = of master upasangamya = approached; rāmam = Rama; kshhiprameva = quickly; achachakshhire = informed; ramāya = to Rama; sahabhāryāya = who was with his wife.

They, the well-wishers of their master, approached Rama who was with his wife and immediately informed him.

Sloka 7

prativeditamājñāya sūtamabhyamtaram pituḥ |
tatraivānāyayāmāsa rāghavaḥ piyakāmyayā || 2-16-7

7. aṇ^yāya = knowing; sūtam = ṣumantra; abhyantaram = who was intimate; pituḥ = to father; prativeditam = being informed by them ; rāgahava = Rama; priyakāmyayā = who desired to show kindness; anāyayamāsa = called him in ; tatraiva = there only.

Knowing that Sumantra, who was intimate to his father, came, Rama who desired to show kindness, called him in there.

Sloka 8

tam vaiśravaṇa samkāśam upaviṣṭam svalamkṛtam |
dādarśa sūtaḥ paryanke sauvaṇo sa uttarac cade || 2-16-8
varāha rudhira ābheṇa śucinā ca sugandhinā |
anuliptam para ardhyena candanena param tapam || 2-12-9
sthitayā pārśvataḥ ca api vāla vyajana hastayā |
upetam sītayā bhūyaḥ citrayā śaśinam yathā || 2-16-10

8;9;10. tam = that; sūtaḥ = ṣumantra; dadarsha = saw; tam = that Rama; upavishhṭam = sitting; vaishravaṇa sankāsham = like Kubera; sauvarṇe paryanke = on a golden couch; sūttarachchade = with the best of cloth = covering; svalankṛitam = well adorned himself; anuliptam = smeared ; parārdhyena chandanena = by excellent sandal; shuchinā = purely; sugandhinā = smelling like; varāharudhirābheṇa = blood of a boar; upetam = endowed; sītayāchāpi = with ṣita; sthitayā = standing; pārshvataḥ = by the side; vālavajanahastayā = with chowrie in her hand; parantapam = Rama. the formentor of enemies; shashinamyathā = was like moon; chitrayā = with chitra star.

That Sumantra saw Rama sitting like Kubera on a golden couch with the best cloth-covering. He adorned himself well and had the body anointed with sandal perfume, purely smelling like blood of a boar. With Sita, standing on his side with chowrie in her hand, Rama was shining like moon with chitra star.

Sloka 11

tam tapantam iva ādityam upapannam sva tejasā |
vavande varadam bandī niyamajño vinītavat || 2-16-11

11. vandi = ṣumantra; vinayajJṇḥ = who knew pokite conduct; vavande = respectfully saluted; tam = that Rama; vimītavat = who was refined; upapannam = endowed with; svateja sa = hi own brilliance;tapantam = shining; ādityam iva = like sun; varadam = granting wishes.

Sumantra, who knew polite conduct, offered salutation to Rama, who was refined with his own brilliance, shining like the sun and the fulfiller of desires.

Sloka 12

prānjalis tu sukham pṛṣṭvā vihāra śayana āsane |
rāja putram uvāca idam sumantraḥ rāja satkṛtaḥ || 2-16-12

12. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; rājasatkṛitaḥ = who was treated with respect by Rama; vuācha = spoke; idam = these words; praṇ^jaliḥ = with folded hands; rājaputram = to that prince; sumukham = with beautiful face; vihārashayanāsane = and who was seated on a swinging sofa.

Sumantra, who was treated with respect by Rama spoke thus with folded hands to that prince with beautiful face and who was seated on a swinging sofa.

Sloka 13

kausalyā suprabhā deva pitā tvam draṣṭum iccati |
mahiṣyā saha kaikeyyā gamyatām tatra māciram || 2-16-13

13. rāmaḥ = Rama; kausalyā suprajā = the good offspring of Kausalya! pitā = your father; mahishhyā kaikeyyāsaha = along with Queen Kaikeyi; ichchhati = wish; drasṭum = to see; tvām = you; gamyatām = let you go; tatra = there; māchiram = wtihout delay.

"Oh Rama, the good offspring of Kausalya! Your father and the Queen Kaikeyi want to see you. Go there soon."

Sloka 14

evam uktaḥ tu samhṛṣṭaḥ nara simho mahā dyutiḥ |
tataḥ sammānayām āsa sītām idam uvāca ha || 2-16-14

14. narasimhaḥ = that best of men; mahādyutiḥ = with great brilliance; samhṛisṭaḥ = was pleased; uktaḥ = to be told; evam = thus; tataḥ = them; sammanayāmāsa = honoured him; uvācha ha = spoke; idam = these words; sītām = to ṣita.

That best of men with great brilliance was pleased to hear those words, then honoured him and spoke thus to Sita.

Sloka 15

devi devaḥ ca devī ca samāgamya mad antare |
mantreyete dhruvam kiṃcit abhiṣecana samhitam || 2-16-15

15. devi = Oh; ṣita! devashcha = the king; devīcha = and the queen; samāgamya = together; mantrayate = are thinking; kinchit = of something; madantare = about me; abhishhachana samhitam = relating to coronation; dhṛivam = certain.

"Oh Sita! The king and the queen together are thinking of something about me, relating to coronation. It is certain."

Sloka 16

lakṣayitvā hi abhiprāyam priya kāmā sudakṣiṇā |
saṃcodayati rājānam mad artham madira īkṣaṇā || 2-16-16

16. asitekshhaṇā = the black-eyed Kaikeyi; sudakshhiṇā = who is dexterous; priyakāmā = friendly disposed; lakshhayitva = perceived; abhiprāyam = the opinion; sanchodayati = inciting; rājānam = the king; madartham = for my sake.

"Then black-eyed Kaikeyi who is dexterous and friendly disposed, has perceived the king's opinion and is inciting him for my sake"

Sloka 17

sā prahṛṣṭā mahārājam hitakāmānuvartinī |
jananī cārthakāmā me kekayādhipatessutā || 2-16-17

17. kekayādhipateḥ sutā = "Kaikeyi; the daughter of king Kekaya; jananīcha = and my mother; sā = she; anuvartinī = is following; mahārajam = the king; prahṛishhṭā = is delighted; hitakāma = desirous of my benefit; me arthakāmā = wishing my prosperity".

"Kaikeyi, my mother is following the king's intentions with great pleasure, desirous of my benefit and prosperity"

Sloka 18

diṣṭyā khalu mahārājo mahiṣyā priyayā saha |
sumamtram prāhiṇoddūta marthakāmakaram mama || 2-16-18

18. mahārajaḥ = the king; priyayā saha = along with wife; mahishhyā = the queen; khalu = certainly; dishhtyā = by luch; prāhiṇot = sent; sumantram = ṣumantra; mama arthakāmakaram = who fulfils my desires and wishes; dūtam = as messenger.

"It is certainly our luck that king and queen have sent Sumantra, who fulfils my desires and wishes, as messenger".

Sloka 19

yādṛśī pariṣat tatra tādṛśo dūtāagataḥ |
dhruvam adya eva mām rājā yauvarājye abhiṣekṣyati || 2-16-19

19. yādṛishī = of which sort of tatra parishhat that sort of ; dūtaḥ = messenger; āgataḥ = came; dhṛivam = certainly; adyaiva = today itself; rājā = the king; abhishhekshhyati = will anoint; mām = me; youvarājye = for the right of succession to kingdom.

"A worthy messenger has come who is truly representing the assembly there. It is certain that today itself the king will anoint me for the right of succession to kingdom".

Sloka 20

hanta śīghram itaḥ gatvā drakṣyāmi ca mahī patiḥ |
saha tvam parivāreṇa sukham āssva ramasya ca || 2-16-20

20. hanta = Alas! gatvā = (I shall) go; shīghram = immediately; itaḥ = from here; drakshhyāmi cha = and see; mahipatim = the king; tvam = you; āsva = stay; sukham = happily; parivāreṇa saha = with the circle of friends; ramasva cha = and enjoy yourself.

"I shall go immediately and see the king. You stay happily with the circle of friends and enjoy yourself."

Sloka 21

pati sammānitā sītā bhartāram asita īkṣaṇā |
ādvāram anuvavrāja mangalāni abhidadhyuṣī || 2-16-21

21. sītā = ṣita; patisammānitā = treated with respect by husband; asitekshhaṇā = having black eyes; anuvavrāja = followed; bhartāram = husband; ā dvāram = upto door; abhidadhyushhī = thinking; mangalāni = of auspicious things.

Sita, treated with respect by her husband and having black eyes, followed her husband up to the door, thinking of auspicious things in her mind.

Sloka 22

rājyam dvijātibhirjuṣṭam rājasūyābhiṣecanam |
kartumarhati te rājā vāsavasyeva lokakṛt || 2-16-22

22. rājā = king Dasaratha; te = to you; rājyam = kingdom; jushhṭam = being served; dvijātibhiḥ = by brahmanas; vasavasyeva = like to Devendra; lokakṛit = by Brahma; arhati = to perform; rājasūyābhishhehanam = great sacrifice at coronation of king.

"King Dasaratha will give you kingdom, being served by brahmanas, which will invest you with eligibility to perform the great sacrifice at the coronation ceremony like Brahma to Devendra."

Sloka 23

dīkṣitam vratasampannam varājinadharam śucim |
kuraṅgapāṇim ca paśyantī tvām bhajāmyaham || 2-16-23

23. aham = I; bhajāmi = shall enjoy; pashyantī = seeing; tvām = you; dīkshhtam = initiated; vratasampannam = for perfect religious act of austerity; varājinadharam = wearing excellent antelope-hide; shuchim = being pure; kuraṇ^ga shṛiṇ^gapāṇimcha = and bearing a deer-horn in hand.

"I shall be delighted to see you, initiated for the perfect religious act of austerity, wearing excellent antelope-hide, being pure and bearing a deer-horn in hand".

Sloka 24

pūrvām diśam vajradharo dakṣiṇām pātu te yamaḥ |
varuṇaḥ paścimāmāśām dhaneśastūttarām diśam || 2-16-24

24. te = your; pūrvam disham = eastern direction; vajradharaḥ = Indra the god of rain; dakshhiṇām = south; yamaḥ = yama the god of death; pashchimāmashām = western direction; varunaḥ = varuna the regent of ocean; uttarām disham tu = northern direction; dhaneshaḥ = Kubera; the god of riches; pātu = protect.

"ṃay Indra the god of rain in the east, Yama the god of death in the south, Varuna the regent of the ocean in the west and Kubera the god of riches and treasure in the north, protect you".

Sloka 25

atha sītāmanujñāpya kṛtakautukamagaldaḥ |
niścakrāma sumantreṇa saha rāmo niveśanāt || 2-16-25

25. atha = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; kṛitakantuka mangalaḥ = adorned as in an auspicious ceremony; anujJṇāpya = got permission; sītām = from ṣita; nishchakrāma = started; niveshanāt = from house; sumantreṇa saha = along with ṣumantra.

Rama, adorned as in an auspicious festivity, took permission from Sita and started from house along with Sumantra.

Sloka 26

parvatādiva niṣkramya simho giriguhāśayaḥ |
lakṣmaṇam dvāriso.apaśyat prahvañjalipuṭam sthitam || 2-16-26

26. saḥ = that Rama; mishhkramya = came out of the house; parvatāt iva = like from the mountain; simhah = lion; firigulāshayaḥ = residing in mountain cave; apashyat = saw; lakshhmaṇam = Lakshmana; sthitam = staying; dvāri = at the door; prahvāJṇjalipuṭam = bowing with the palms of hand joined and put to forehead as a mark of respect.

That Rama who came out, like a lion residing in a mountain cave from out of the mountain, saw Lakshmana standing at the door, bowing with palms of the hand joined and put to the forehead as a mark of respect.

Sloka 27

27;28. atha = then; sah that; rājananadanaḥ = prince; purushhavyāghraḥ = the best of men; samāgachchhat = met; suhṛijjanaiḥ = the friends madhya kakashhyāyām = in the middle chamber; dṛishhṭvā = saw; sarvān = all; arthinaḥ = people desiring to behold him; sametya = approached nearer; pratinandyacha = and greated; āruroha = mounted; rathothamam = excellent chariot; rājitam = shining; pāvaka sankāsham = like fire; vyyāghram = covered by tiger's skin

Then, that prince the best of men, met his friends in the middle chamber. He saw all the people who came there to behold him, approached nearer to them, greeted them and mounted the excellent chariot, which was resplendent like fire and covered with tiger's skin.

Sloka 29

meghanādamasambādham maṇihemavibhūśitam |
muṣṇantam iva cakṣūmṣi prabhayā hema varcasam || 2-16-29
kareṇu śiśu kalpaiḥ ca yuktam parama vājibhiḥ |
hari yuktam sahasra akṣo ratham indraiva āśugam || 2-16-30

29;30. ratham = the chariot; meghanāda = sounding like thunder; asambādham = not congested; maṇihemavibhūshhitam = decorated with gold and jewels; mushhṇantamiva = as it was stealing; chakshhūmshhi = the eyes; prabhayā = by splendour; marevarchasam = like brilliance of ṃeru mountain; paramavājabhiḥ = excellent horses; kareṇu shishukalpaiḥ = excellet horses; kareṇu shishukalpaiḥ = looking like infantine elephants; yuktam = were fastened; āshugam = moving quickly; rāghavaḥ = Rama; jvalitaḥ = shining; shriyā = with prosperity; āsthāya = mounted; hariyuktam iva = what like chariot with horses; indraḥ = of Indra; the god of sky; sahasrākshhaḥ = having thousand eyes; prayayau = went forth; tūrṇam = quickly.

That chariot was sounding like thunder. It was not congested. It was decorated with gold and jewels. Its splendour was like that of ṃeru mountain, stealing the eyes. Excellent horses looking like infantine elephants were tied to the chariot, which was moving quickly. That Rama shining with splendour like Indra, the lord of sky, mounted such a chariot and went forth quickly.

Sloka 31

31. shrīmān saḥ = that splendid chariot; svanavān = having sound; parjanya iva = like rain cloud; ākāshe = in the sky; abhinādayan = making sound; niryayau = started; niketāt = from the house; chandramā iva = like moon; mahābhrāt = from mighty cloud.

That splendid chariot, sounding like rain-cloud in the sky, came out of the house like moon coming out of a mighty cloud.

Sloka 32

Lakshmana, Rama's younger brother mounted the chariot in the back-side and protected Rama with umbrella and chamara fan.

Sloka 33

Then, the crowds started dispersing from all sides generating tumultuous hallowing sound.

Sloka 34

Thereupon, hundreds and thousands of important people mounted on excellent horses and soldiers mounted on elephants, then followed Rama.

Sloka 35

Valiant persons, dressed in armour adorned with sandal and aloe perfumes, wearing swords and bows, declaring the arrival of Rama, went in front of Rama to receive him.

Sloka 36

At that time, sounds of musical instruments, sounds of praising hymns of panagyrists and lion-like roars of valiant persons were heard on the way.

Sloka 37

harmyavātāyanasthābhirbhūṣitābhiḥ samantataḥ || 2-16-37

While Rama, the annihilator of enemies, was going, well-adorned women standing in porticoes of palaces, sprinkled flowers on him from all sides.

Sloka 38

Women of exquisitely beautiful form standing in palaces and on ground were offering salutations to Rama with the best words with a desire to please Rama.

Sloka 39

"Oh, Rama, who creates happiness to mother! Have successful journey! Your mother Kausalya will be certainly delighted to see you obtaining the paternal kingdom."

Sloka 40

40. tāḥ = those; nāryaḥ = women; amanyantahi = thought; sītām = ṣita; rāmasya hṛidayapriyām = who is dear to Rama's heart; varām = is better; sīmantīm = woman; sarvasīmantībhyaḥ = than all women.

Those women thought that Sita, who is dear to Rama's heart, is better woman than all women.

Sloka 41

tayā sucaritam devyā purā nūnam mahattapaḥ || 2-16-41
rohiṇīva śaśāṅkena rāmasamyogamāpa yā |

41. yā = which ṣita; rahiṇī iva = like Rohini star; shashāṇkena = with moon; āpa = has obtained; rāmasmyogam = union with Rama; tayā devyā = by such ṣita; mahat = great; tapaḥ = penance; mūnam = indeed; sucharitam = was performed well; purā = in former times.

"As Rohini star stays together with the moon, Sita having obtained Rama as husband must have certainly performed a great penance in former times."

Sloka 42

iti prāsādaśṛṅgeṣu pramadābhirnarottamaḥ || 2-16-42
śuśrāva rājamārgasthaḥ priyā vāca udāhṛtāḥ |

42. narottamaḥ = Rama; the best of men; rājamārgasthaḥ = who was in the royal road; sushrāva = heard; priyāḥ vāchaḥ = pleasent words; udāhṛitāḥ = spoken; iti = thus; pramadābhiḥ = by women; prāsādasṛingeshhu = who were in turrets.

While going through the royal road, Rama heard those pleasant words spoken thus by women standing in turrets.

Sloka 43

sa rāghavaḥ tatra kathā pralāpam |
śuśrāva lokasya samāgatasya |
ātma adhikārā vividhāḥ ca vācaḥ |
prahṛṣṭa rūpasya pure janasya || 2-16-43

43. tadā = then; saḥ = that; rāghavaḥ = Rama;sushrāva = heard; pralapān = words; lokasya = of people; samāgatasya; gathered; tatra = there; vāchaḥ cha = and also words; vividhāḥ = variously; ātmādhikārāḥ = pertaining to himself; pure janasya = by citizens; prahṛishhṭarūpasya = who were much delighted.

Then, Rama heard the words of people gathered there and also the words variously pertaining to himself, by citizens.

Sloka 44

eṣa śriyam gaccati rāghavo adya|
rāja prasādāt vipulām gamiṣyan |
ete vayam sarva samṛddha kāmā|
yeṣām ayam no bhavitā praśāstā || 2-16-44

44. rāja prasādāt = by king's propitiousness; eshhaḥ = this; rāghavaḥ = Rama; gamishhyan = who will obtain; vipulām = the earth; adya = now; gachchhati = will gain; shriyam = wealth; yeshhām naḥ = for which of us; ayam = he; bahvitā = will become; prashastā = ruler; ete vayam = such of us; sarvasamṛidhdhakāmā = will have fulfilled all desires.

"By the king's propitiousness, this Rama today is going to obtain this earth and the wealthy kingdom. All our desires are going to be fulfilled because Rama will become the ruler."

Sloka 45

lābho janasya asya yad eṣa sarvam |
prapatsyate rāṣṭram idam cirāya |
sa ghoṣavadbhiḥ ca hayaiḥ sanāgaiḥ |
purahsaraiḥ svastika sūta māgadhaiḥ || 2-16-45

45. yat = since when; eshhaḥ = this Rama; pravatsyate = will get; sarvam idam rāshhṭram = this entire state; chirāya = for a long time(since then); lābhaḥ = benefit; asya janasya = for these people; asmin mamjādhipe = when this Rama becomes king; kashchit = anyone; jātu = at anytime; na pashyet hi = will not see kichana = anything; apriyam = unkindly; na = nor; duḥkham = grief.

"If Rama rules this state for a long time, it will be a great benefit to the people. If he becomes a king none can see dislike and sorrow at any time."

Sloka 46

sa ghoṣavadbhiśca hayaiḥ sanāgaiḥ |
purassaraiḥ svastikasūtamāgadhaiḥ |
mahīyamānaḥ pravaraiḥ ca vādakaiḥ |
abhiṣṭutaḥ vaiśravaṇo yathā yayau|| 2-16-46

46. sūtamāgadhaiḥ = bards and panegyrists; svastika = invoking blessings; purassaraiḥ = moving in front; mahīyamānaḥ = praising with great respect; abhishhṭutaḥ = eulogised; pravaraiḥ vādyakaiḥ = by the most excellent instrumentalists; saḥ = that Rama; vaishrāvaṇotatha = like Kubera the god of weatlh; yayau = went; hayaiḥ = with horses; sanāgaiḥ = together with elephants; ghoshhavadbhiḥ = having resounds.

While bards and panegyrists were moving in front praising with great respect, invoking blessings and eulogised by the most excellent instrumentalists, Rama, like Kubera the god of wealth, went with resounding horses and elephants.

Sloka 47

kareṇu mātanga ratha aśva samkulam |
mahā jana oghaiḥ paripūrṇa catvaram |
pabhūtaratnam bahupaṇyasaṃcayam |
dadarśa rāmo vimalam mahāpatham || 2-16-47

47. rāmaḥ = Rama; dadarsha = saw; mahāpatham; great royal road; vimalam = which was spotless; prabhūta ratnam = having abundant diamonds; bhaupaṇya sanchayam = havin abundant diamonds; bahupaṇya sanchayam = having many vendible commodities; kareṇu mātaṇ^ga rathāshva sankulam = full of female and male elephants; chariots and horses; paripūrṇa chatvaram = the road junctions were filled; janaughaiḥ = with multitude of people.

Rama saw the great royal road which was spotlessly clean, having diamonds and various vendible commodities. It was full of male and female elephants, horses and chariots. There was abundance of people gathered at road junctions.

- - -

|| iti śrimad Ramayane ṣoḍaśa sargaḥ ||

Thus completes sixteenth sarga of Ayodhya Kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
