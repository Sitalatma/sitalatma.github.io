Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 58
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

As per the orders of king Dasaratha, Sumantra delivers the parting messages of Rama and Lakshmana. Rama sends positive messages to Kausalya and Bharata but Lakshmana sends angry messages to Dasaratha.

Sloka 1

pratyāśvastaḥ yadā rājā mohāt pratyāgataḥ punaḥ |
thājuhāva tam sūtam rāma vṛtta anta kāraṇāt || 2-58-1

1. atha = thereafter; rājā = the king; yadā = when; pratyāshvastaḥ = recovered; mohāt = from loss of consciousness; punaḥ = and again; pratyāgataḥ = returned to his breath; (then) ājuhāva = called; tam sūtam = that charioteer; rāma vṛittantā kāraṇāt = for the purpose of getting the report on Rama.

Thereafter, the king when recovered from loss of consciousness and again returned to his breath, he called that charioteer for the purpose of getting the report on Rama.

Sloka 2

tadā sūto mahārāja kṛtāñjalirupasthitaḥ|
rāmameva anuśocantaṃ duḥkhaśokasamanvitam || 2-58-2
vṛddham parama samtaptam nava graham iva dvipam |
viniḥśvasantam dhyāyantam asvastham iva kunjaram || 2-58-3

2-3. atha = then; sūtaḥ = sumantra; kṛitāJṇjaliḥ = who joined his palms in reverence; upasthitaḥ = approached; mahārāja = the monarch; anushochantam = who was repenting; rāmameva = about Rama alone; duhkha shoka samanvitam = filled with pain and sorrow; vṛiddham = who was advanced in age; parama samtaptam = who was greatly anguished; vinihshvasantam = who was sighing; dvipam iva = like an elephant; nava graham = which was newly caught; dhyāyantam = who was thinking something; kunjaram iva = like an elephant; asvastham = which is sick.

Then, Sumantra, who joined his palms in reverence, approached the monarch who was repenting about Rama alone, filled with pain and sorrow, advanced in age, greatly anguished, sighing like a newly caught elephant and reflecting on something as a sick elephant.

Sloka 4

rājā tu rajasā sūtam dhvasta aṅgam samupasthitam |
aśru pūrṇa mukham dīnam uvāca parama ārtavat || 2-58-4

4. rājā = the king; parama ārtavat = having great distress; uvācha = spoke; sūtam = to ṣumantra; dhvasta aṇ^gam = whose body was covered; rajasā = with dust; ashru pūrṇa mukham = whose face was filled with tears; dīnam = who was miserable; samupasthitam = and who approached near him.

The king, having great distress, spoke to Sumantra, whose body was covered with dust, his face filled with tears, who was miserable and who approached near him (as follows):

Sloka 5

kva nu vatsyati dharma ātmā vṛkṣa mūlam upāśritaḥ |
so atyanta sukhitaḥ sūta kim aśiṣyati rāghavaḥ || 2-58-5

5. sūta = Oh; ṣumantra; kva nu = where; dharmātmā = can Rama the virtuous man; vatsyati = reside; upāshritaḥ = taking refuge; vṛikṣa mūlam = at the root of a tree? Kim = what; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; atyanta sukhitaḥ = who enjoyed very many comforts; ashiṣyati = can eat?

"Oh, Sumantra! Where can Rama the virtuous man abide, taking refuge at the root of a tree? What that Rama, who enjoyed very many pleasantries, can eat now?"

Sloka 6

duḥkhasyānucito duḥkham sumantra śayanocitaḥ |
bhūmi pāla ātmajo bhūmau śete katham anāthavat || 2-58-6

6. sumantra = Oh ṣumantra; katham = how can; bhūmi pāla ātmajo = Rama the prince; shayanochitaḥ = who was habituated to good beds; shete = sleep; duḥkham = with discomfort; bhūmau = on a floor; anāthavat = like an orphan?

"Oh, Sumantra! How can Rama the prince, who was habituated to good beds, sleep with such discomfort on a floor like an orphan?"

Sloka 7

yam yāntam anuyānti sma padāti ratha kuṇzjarāḥ |
sa vatsyati katham rāmaḥ vijanam vanam āśritaḥ || 2-58-7

7. yam = with whom; yāntam = while journeying; padāti ratha kuṇJjarāḥ = foot-soldiers; chariots and elephants; anuyānti sma = sued to follow; katham = how; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; āshritaḥ = can take shelter; vatsyati = and stay; vijanam = in a deserted; vanam = forest?

"Foot soldiers, chariots and elephants used to follows whenever Rama was marching ahead. How such a man can take shelter and stay in a forest devoid of people?"

Sloka 8

vyāldaiḥ mṛgaiḥ ācaritam kṛṣṇa sarpa niṣevitam |
katham kumārau vaidehyā sārdham vanam upasthitau || 2-58-8

8. katham = how; kumārau = Rama and Lakshmana; vaidehyā sārdham = along with ṣeetha; upasthitau = reached; vanam = the forest; ācaritam = being frequented by; vyāLaiḥ = wild; mṛigaiḥ = animals; kṛiṣṇa sarpa niṣevitam = and haunted by black snakes?

"How Seetha Rama and Lakshmana entered forest filled with wild animals and black snakes?"

Sloka 9

sukumāryā tapasvinyā sumantra saha sītayā |
rāja putrau katham pādaiḥ avaruhya rathāt gatau || 2-58-9

9. sumantra = Oh ṣumantra! Katham = how; rājaputrau = those princes; sītayā saha = with ṣeetha; sukumāryā = the delicate; tapasvinyā = and the poor lady; gatau = went; pādaiḥ = on foot; avaruhya = after descending; rathāt = from the chariot?

"Oh, Sumantra! How those princes with Seetha the delicate and the poor lady went on foot, after descending from the chariot?"

Sloka 10

siddha arthaḥ khalu sūta tvam yena dṛṣṭau mama ātmajau |
vana antam praviśantau tāv aśvināv iva mandaram || 2-58-10

10. sūta = Oh; ṣumantra! Ye = by you; mama = my; ātmajau = sons; dṛiṣṭau = have been seen; pravishantau = entering; vana antam = the boundary of the forest; mandaram iva = as (entering) the region of ṃandara mountain; ashvināv = by the two divinities of Aswini; tvam = you; siddha arthaḥ khalu = are indeed accomplished.

"Oh, Sumantra! You have seen my sons entering the forest, as though divinities of Aswini had entered the ṃountain-range of ṃandara. You are indeed lucky."

Sloka 11

kim uvāca vaco rāmaḥ kim uvāca ca lakṣmaṇaḥ |
sumantra vanam āsādya kim uvāca ca maithilī || 2-58-11

11. sumantra = Oh; ṣumantra! Aasādya = after reaching; vanam = the forest; kim = what; vachaḥ = words; rāmaḥ = (did) Rama; uvācha = speak; kim = what did; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; uvācha = speak; kim cha = and what; maithilī = (did) ṣeetha; uvācha = speak?

"Oh, Sumantra! After reaching the forest, what words did Rama speak? What did Lakshmana speak? What did Seetha speak?"

Sloka 12

āsitam śayitam bhuktam sūta rāmasya kīrtaya |
jīviṣyāmyahametena yayātiriva sādhuṣu || 2-58-12

12. sūta = Oh; charioteer; kīrtaya = tell (me); asitam = about sitting; shayitam = sleeping; bhuktam = and eating; rāmasya = of Rama; etena = by this; aham = I; jīvishhyāmi = shall survive; sādhushhu iva = as among saints; yayātiḥ = Yayati (lived).

"Oh, charioteer! Tell me where Rama sat, slept and took food. By hearing these things, I shall survive, as Yayati survived in the company of saints."

Comment: King Yayati, when doomed to fall from heaven requested Indra to cast his lot with saints. He was accordingly sent down to a spot on the earth, where four ascetics- Astaka Pratardana, Vasuman and King Sibi had been practicing austerities, and had discourse with them- ṃahabharata, Adi Parva.

Sloka 13

iti sūtaḥ nara indreṇa coditaḥ sajjamānayā |
uvāca vācā rājānam sa bāṣpa parirbaddhayā || 2-58-13

13. choditaḥ = as asked; iti = thus; narendreṇa = by the king; saḥ sūtaḥ = that charioteer; uvācha = spoke; rājānam = to the emperor; vāchā = in a voice; sajjamānayā = quivering; bāṣpa paribaddhayā = and choked with tears.

As asked thus by the king, Sumantra spoke to the emperor in a voice quivering and choked with tears (as follows).

Sloka 14

abravīn mām mahā rāja dharmam eva anupālayan |
anjalim rāghavaḥ kṛtvā śirasā abhipraṇamya ca || 2-58-14

14. mahārāja = Oh; emperor; rāghavaḥ = Rama; anupālayan = keeping up; dharmameva = the prescribed course of conduct; kṛitvā = making; anjalim = salutation with joined palms; abhipraṇamya cha = and bowing in reverence; shirasā = with his head (to you); abravīt = spoke; mām = to me.

"Oh, emperor! Rama, keeping up the prescribed course of conduct, making salutation with joined palms and bowing his head in reverence to you, spoke to me as follows:"

Sloka 15

sūta madvacanāt tasya tātasya vidita ātmanaḥ |
śirasā vandanīyasya vandyau pādau mahātmanaḥ || 2-58-15

15. sūta = Oh; charioteer; pādau = the feet; tasya tātasya = of my father; vidita ātmanaḥ = one with remarkable intellect; mahātmanaḥ = one with mighty soul; vandanīyasya = and one to be respectfully greeted; vandyau = are to be saluted; shirasā = with my head; madvacanāt = through my words.

"Oh, charioteer! Tell my salutations to my father, the one with a remarkable intellect, a mighty soul and the one to be respectfully greeted."

Sloka 16

sarvam antaḥ puram vācyam sūta madvacanāttvayā |
ārogyam aviśeṣeṇa yathā arham ca abhivādanam || 2-58-16

16. sūta = Oh; charioteer; vāchyam = It is to be spoken; tvayā = by you; madvachanāt = as my word; sarvam = to the entire; antaḥ puram = gynaeceum; avisheṣeṇa = without any disparity; ārogyam = about their health; yathārham = and appropriate; abhivādanam ca = salutations too.

Oh, charioteer! Enquire about the health of all the people in the gynaeceum without any disparity and offer my appropriate salutations to them.

Sloka 17

mātā ca mama kausalyā kuśalam ca abhivādanam |
apramādam ca vaktavyā brūyāścaimidam vacaḥ || 2-58-17

17. mama = my; mātā = Kausalya; vaktavyā = is to be told; kushalam = about my welfare; abhivādanam ca = about my salutations; apramādam ca = and about my alertness; brūyāḥ = tell; idam = these; vachashcha = words also.

ṃy mother Kausalya is to be told about my welfare, about my offering salutations to her and about my meticulousness.

Sloka 18

dharmanityā yathākālamagnyagāraparā bhava |
devi devasya pādau ca devavat paripālaya || 2-58-18

18. devī = Oh; godly lady; bhava = be; dharmanityā = righteous always; agnyagāraparā = and be interested in offering sacrifices in the house of fire-worship; yathā kālam = as per the prescribed timings; paripālaya = nurture; pādau ca = the feet; devasya = of the King Dasaratha; devavat = like with a god.

"Oh, godly lady! Be always righteous and be interested in offering worship and sacrifices as per the prescribed timings in the House of Fire worship. Nurture the feet of the Lord Dasaratha, like with a god."

Sloka 19

abhimānam ca mānam ca tyaktvā vartasva mātṛṣu |
anu rājāna māryām ca kaikeyīmamba kāraya || 2-58-19

19. amba = Oh; mother; vartasva = behave; mātṛiṣu = towards other mothers; tyaktvā = abounding; abhimānam ca = self-conceit; mānam ca = and indignation; kāraya = make; āryām = the venerable; kaikeyīm = Kiakeyi; rājānam anu = agreeable to the king.

"Oh, mother! Behave towards my other mothers, abandoning self-conceit and indignation. ṃake the venerable Kaikeyi, agreeable to the king."

Sloka 20

kumāre bharate vṛttirvartitavyāca rājavat |
arthajyeṣṭhā hi rājāno rājadharmamanusmara || 2-58-20

20. bharate = in respect of Bharata; kumāre = your son; vartitavyā = follow; vṛittiḥ = a respectable behaviour; rājavat = as with a king; rājānaḥ = kings; artha jyeṣṭhāḥ hi = are indeed sovereign in substance; anusmara = remember; rājadharmam = the rules; relating to kings.

"In respect of Bharata your son, follow a respectable behaviour as with a king. Kings are indeed sovereign in substance. Remember the rules relating to kings."

Sloka 21

bharataḥ kuśalam vācyo vācyo mad vacanena ca |
sarvāsva eva yathā nyāyam vṛttim vartasva mātṛṣu || 2-58-21

21. bharataḥ = Bharata; vācyaḥ = is to be asked; kushalam = about his well-being; vācyaḥ ca = It is to be told also; madvachanena = as my word; vartasva = follow; vṛittim = good behaviour; eva = indeed; yathānyāyam = judiciously; sarvāsu = towards all; mātṛiṣu = mothers.

"Bharata is to be enquired about his well-being and he is also to be informed as follows: "Follow good behaviour indeed judiciously towards all your mothers."

Sloka 22

vaktavyaḥ ca mahā bāhur ikṣvāku kula nandanaḥ |
pitaram yauvarājyastho rājyastham anupālaya || 2-58-22

22. mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed Bharata; ikṣvāku kula nandanaḥ = the son of Ikshvaku race; vaktavyaḥ ca = is to be told (as follows) yauvarājyasthaḥ = being installed in the office of prince Regent.

"The mighty armed Bharata the son of Ikshvaku race, is to be told as follows: �Being installed in the office of Prince Regent, attend to your father who still stays in the throne'."

Sloka 23

atikrāntavayā rājā māsmainam vyavarorudhaḥ |
kumārarājye jīva tvam tasyaivājñpravartanām || 2-58-23

23. rājā = the King; atikrāntavayāḥ = has crossed his age; māsmainam vyavarorudhaḥ = do not depose; enam = him; āJṇāpravartanāt = by proceeding according to the orders; tasyaiva = of the king; tvam = of; jīva = live; kumāra rājye = as a prince."

"The king has crossed his age. But do not depose him on that score. By proceeding according to the orders of the king, you live as a prince."

Sloka 24

abravīccāpi mām bhūyo bhṛśamaśrūṇi vartayan |
māteva mama mātā te draṣṭavyā putragardhinī || 2-58-24

24. vartayan = shedding; ashrūṇi = tears; bhṛisham = profusely; bhūyaḥ = (he) again; abravīcchāpi = spoke; mām = to me (as follows); mām mātā = my mother; putragardhinī = who is very much longing her son; draṣṭavyā = is to be looked after; te = by you; māteva = as your mother.

"Shedding tears profusely, Rama again spoke to me as follows : �ṃy mother, who is very much longing her son, is to be looked after by you as if she is your mother'."

Sloka 25

iti evam mām mahārāja bṛvann eva mahā yaśāḥ |
rāmaḥ rājīva tāmra akṣo bhṛśam aśrūṇi avartayat || 2-58-25

25. mahāraja = Oh; emperor; rāmaḥ = Rama; mahāyashāḥ = of most beautiful appearance; rājīva tāmrākshhaḥ = with red eyes resembling red lotus flowers; bruvanneva = while even uttering; ityeva = thus; mām = to me; bhṛisham = amply; avartata = shed; ashrūṇi = tears.

"Oh, emperor! Rama of the most beautiful appearance with red eyes resembling red lotus flowers, while even uttering these words to me profusely shed tears."

Sloka 26

lakṣmaṇaḥ tu susamkruddho nihśvasan vākyam abravīt |
kena ayam aparādhena rāja putraḥ vivāsitaḥ || 2-58-26

26. lakshmaṇastu = but Lakshmana; susamkruddhaḥ = was very angry; nishshvasan = and breathing a sigh; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; kena = on which; aparedhena = offence; ayam = this; rājaputraḥ = prince; vivāsitaḥ = was expelled from his home'"

"But Lakshmana was very angry and breathing a sigh, spoke to me as follows: �On which offence this prince was expelled from home?'"

Sloka 27

rājñā tu khalu kaikeyyā laghu tvāśritya śāsanam |
kṛtam kāryamakāryam vā vayam yenābhipīḍitāḥ || 2-58-27

27. rājJṇā tu = the king even; āshritya = took shelter under; laghu = the wretched; shāsanam = decree; kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi; kṛitam = and did; akāryam = an act not to be done; kāryam = as though it is a proper act; yena = by which; vayam = we; abhipīDitaḥ = are tormented.

"The king even took shelter under a wretched decree of Kaikeyi and performed an act not to be done, as though it is a proper deed, for which we are now tormented."

Sloka 28

yadi pravrājitaḥ rāmaḥ lobha kāraṇa kāritam |
vara dāna nimittam vā sarvathā duṣkṛtam kṛtam || 2-58-28

28. lobha kāraṇa kāritam = either instigated by passion; vara dāna nimittam vā = or with the intention of giving boons; rāmaḥ = Rama; pravrājitaḥ yadi = was sent to exile; dushhkṛitam = an infamous deed; kṛitam = was done; sarvathā = by all means."

"Even if this was done for the good pleasure of the king or through the will of God, I do not see any justification for Rama's abandonment."

Sloka 29

idam tāvadyathākāmamīśvarasya kṛte kṛtam |
rāmasya tu parityāge na hetum upalakṣaye || 2-58-29

29. idam tāvat = even if this; kṛitam = was done; yathākāmam = for the good pleasure of the kin; īshvarasya kṛita = or through the will of god; na upalakshhye = I do not see; hetum = any justification; rāmasya = for Rama's parityāge = abandonment.

"Even if this was done for the good pleasure of the king or through the will of god, I do not see any justification for Rama's abandonment."

Sloka 30

asamīkṣya samārabdham viruddham buddhi lāghavāt |
janayiṣyati samkrośam rāghavasya vivāsanam || 2-58-30

30. rāghavasya = Rama's; vivāsam = exile; viruddham = an illegal decision; samārabdham = undertaken; buddhi lāghavāt = whether due to levity of judgment; asamīkshhya = or due to lack of reflection; janayishhyati = will raise; samkrosham = intensive anguish.

"Rama's exile, an illegal decision undertaken whether due to levity of judgment or due to lack of reflection, will raise countless protests."

Sloka 31

aham tāvan mahā rāje pitṛtvam na upalakṣaye |
bhrātā bhartā ca bandhuḥ ca pitā ca mama rāghavaḥ || 2-58-31

31. aham = I; na upalakshhaye = do not see; pitṛitvam = father-hood; mahārāja = in the emperor; mama = for me; rāghavaḥ = Rama; bhrātā ca = is brother; bhartā ca = lord; bandhuḥ ca = relative and; pitā = father.

"I do not consider the emperor as my father. For me, Rama is the brother, lord, relative and my father."

Sloka 32

sarva loka priyam tyaktvā sarva loka hite ratam |
sarva loko anurajyeta katham tvā anena karmaṇā || 2-58-32

32. tyaktvā = leaving; sarva loka priyam = (Rama) the beloved of all people; ratam = and who is interested; sarva lokahite = in the welfare of all mankind; katham = how would; sarva lokaḥ = all the people; anurajyeta = be interested; tvam = in you; anena karmaṇā = by this act?

"Leaving Rama the beloved of all people and who is interested in the welfare of all human beings, how would all these people would be interested in you by this act of yours?"

Sloka 33

sarvaprajābhirāmam hi rāmam pravrājya dhārmikam |
sarvalokam virudhyemam katham rājā bhaviṣyasi || 2-58-33

33. pravrājya = by banishing; rāmam = Rama; sarvaprajābhirāmam = who is delightful to entire people; dhārmikam = and virtuous; virudhya = and opposing; imam sarva lokam = all this entire world; katham = how; bhavishhyasi = will you stay; rājā = as a king?

"By banishing Rama who is delightful to entire people and who is a virtuous man and by opposing all this entire world, how will you stay as a king?"

Sloka 34

jānakī tu mahā rāja niḥśvasantī tapasvinī |
bhūta upahata cittā iva viṣṭhitā vṛṣmṛtā sthitā || 2-58-34

34. mahārāja = "Oh king; tapasvinī = the wise; jānakī tu = ṣeetha for her part; niḥshvasantī = stood sighing; bhūta upahata cittā = with mind like one possessed; vṛiṣmṛitā sthitā = forgetting her own existence there; viṣṭhitā = stood motionless.

"Oh, King! The wise Seetha for her part with her mind like one possessed and forgetting her own existence, stood sighing and static.

Sloka 35

adṛṣṭa pūrva vyasanā rāja putrī yaśasvinī |
tena duhkhena rudatī na eva mām kiṃcit abravīt || 2-58-35

35. rāja putrī = that princess ṣeetha; yashasvinī = the illustrious one; adṛiṣṭa pūrva vyasanā = having not seen such misfortune earlier; na eva abravīt = could not even tell; kimcit = anything; mām = to me; rudatī = weeping (as she was); tena duḥkhena = with that unpleasantness.

"The illustrious Seetha having not seen such misfortune earlier, could not even to speak me anything, weeping as she was with that uneasiness."

Sloka 36

udvīkṣamāṇā bhartāram mukhena pariśuṣyatā |
mumoca sahasā bāṣpam mām prayāntam udīkṣya sā || 2-58-36

36. udīkṣya = seeing; mām = me; parishuṣyatā = with emaciated; mukhena = face; udvīkṣamāṇā = perceiving; bhartāram = her husband; sahasā = soon; mumoca = shed down; bāṣpam = tears.

"Seeing me going back, Seetha with emaciated face, perceiving her husband, soon shed down tears."

Sloka 37

tathaiva rāmaḥ aśru mukhaḥ kṛta anjaliḥ |
sthitaḥ abhaval lakṣmaṇa bāhu pālitaḥ sthitaḥ |
tathaiva sītā rudatī tapasvinī |
nirīkṣate rāja ratham tathaiva mām || 2-58-37

37. tathaiva = In the same way; rāmaḥ = Rama; ashru mukhaḥ = with tears on his face; kṛita anjaliḥ = and with joined palms; sthitaḥ abhaval = stood; lakṣmaṇa bāhu pālitaḥ = shielded by the arms of Lakshmana; tathaiva = In the same way; tapasvinī = the miserable; sītā = ṣeetha; rudatī = weeping; nirīkṣate = was seeing; rāja ratham = the royal chariot; tathaiva = and; mām = me."

"In the same way, Rama with tears on his face and with joined palms, stood being shielded by the arms of Lakshmana. The miserable Seetha in the same manner was weeping and seeing the royal chariot and me."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe aṣṭapañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 58th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
