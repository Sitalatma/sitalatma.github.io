Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 16
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hanuma is grief stricken seeing the plight of Seetha, in that garden being surrounded by ogresses. He becomes firm in mind that She is indeed Seetha, based on Her auspicious bodily marks.

Sloka 1

praśasya tu praśastavyām sītām tām hari pumgavaḥ |
guṇa abhirāmam rāmam ca punaḥ cintā paro abhavat || 5-16-1

1. hari pumgavaḥ = the best among vanaras; prashasya = praising; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; prashastavyām = who is fit to be praised; rāmam ca = and also (praising) Rama; guṇa abhirāmam = pleasing with ḥis virtues; cintā paraḥ abhavat = became immersed in thought; punaḥ = again.

ṭhe best among vanaras praising that ṣeetha who is fit to be praised, and also praising ṣri Rama pleasing with His virtues, became immersed in thought again.

Sloka 2

sa muhūrtam iva dhyātvā bāṣpa paryākulekṣaṇaḥ |
sītām āśritya tejasvī hanumān vilalāpa ha || 5-16-2

2. saḥ hanumān = ṭhat ḥanuma; tejasvī = the radiant one; muhūrtam iva dhyātvā = thinking for a moment; vilalāpa ha = cried; sītām āshritya = about ṣeetha; bāṣpa paryākulekṣaṇaḥ = (and) became with eyes tormented with tears.

ṭhat ḥanuma the radiant one thinking for a moment, cried about ṣeetha and became with eyes tormented with tears.

Sloka 3

mānyā guru vinītasya lakṣmaṇasya guru priyā |
yadi sītā api duhkha ārtā kālo hi duratikramaḥ || 5-16-3

3. mānyā = respectable; lakṣmaṇasya = to Lakshmana; guru vinītasya = instructed by teachers; sīta api = ṣeetha also; guru priyā = dear to teachers; duhkha ārtā yadi = if ṣhe is stricken with grief; kālaḥ = destiny; duratikramaḥ hi = is indeed difficult to withstand.

"Respectable to Lakshmana, instructed by teachers, ṣeetha is also dear to teachers; if ṣhe is stricken with grief, destiny is indeed difficult to withstand."

Sloka 4

rāmasya vyavasāyajñā lakṣmaṇasya ca dhīmataḥ |
na atyartham kṣubhyate devī gangā iva jalada āgame || 5-16-4

4. vyavasāyajñā = (knowing) effort; rāmasya = of Rama; dhīmataḥ lakṣmaṇasya ca = and of sagacious Lakshmana; devī = ṣeetha; na kṣubhyate = is not worried; atyartham = a lot; gangā iva = like Ganga; jalada āgame = in the rainy season.

"Knowing the effort of ṣri Rama and of sagacious Lakshmana, ṣeetha is not worried a lot, like Ganga in the rainy season."

Sloka 5

tulya śīla vayo vṛttām tulya abhijana lakṣaṇām |
rāghavo arhati vaidehīm tam ca iyam asita īkṣaṇā || 5-16-5

5. rāghavaḥ = Rama; arhati = is suited; vaidehīm = to ṣeetha; tulya shīla vayo vṛittām = with ḥer well matched character age and conduct; tulya abhijana lakṣaṇām = with well matched pedigree and characteristics; iyam = this; asitekṣaṇā = black-eyed ṣeetha; tam arhati = is also suited to ḥim.

"ṣri Rama is suited to ṣeetha with ḥer well matched character, age and conduct; with well matched pedigree and characteristics this black-eyed Seetha is also suited to Him."

Sloka 6

tām dṛṣṭvā nava hema ābhām loka kāntām iva śriyam |
jagāma manasā rāmam vacanam ca idam abravīt || 5-16-6

6. tām dṛiṣṭvā = seeing that ṣeetha; nava hema ābhām = with the radiance of new gold; shriyam iva = like the goddess Laksmi; loka kāntām = delightful to the world; jagāma = (ḥanuma) went; manasā = by heart; rāmam = to ṣri Rama; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam ca = these words also.

ṣeeing that ṣeetha with the radiance of new gold, like the goddess Laksmi, delightful to the world, Hanuma went by heart to Sri Rama spoke these words also.

Sloka 7

asyā hetor viśāla akṣyā hato vālī mahā balaḥ |
rāvaṇa pratimo vīrye kabandhaḥ ca nipātitaḥ || 5-16-7

7. asyāḥ hetoḥ = for the sake of this ṣeetha; vishāla akṣyāḥ = the wide eyed one; mahābalaḥ vālī = Vali of great strength; hataḥ = has been killed; kabandhaḥ ca = Kabanda also; rāvaṇa pratimaḥ = equalling to Ravana; vīrye = in strength; nipātitaḥ = has been felled.

"For the sake of this ṣeetha, the wide eyed one, Vali of great strength has been killed, Kabanda also equalling Ravana in strength has been felled."

Sloka 8

virādhaḥ ca hataḥ samkhye rākṣaso bhīma vikramaḥ |
vane rāmeṇa vikramya mahā indreṇa iva śambaraḥ || 5-16-8

8. vane = in the forest; samkhye = during war; virādhaḥ = Virada; bhīma vikramaḥ = of horrible prowess; hataḥ = has been killed; rāmeṇa = by Rama; vikramya = by valour; shambaraḥ iva = like ṣambara; mahā indreṇa = by ṃahendra.

"In the forest during war Virada of horrible prowess has been killed by Rama by valour, like ṣambara by ṃahendra."

Sloka 9

catur daśa sahasrāṇi rakṣasām bhīma karmaṇām |
nihatāni jana sthāne śarair agni śikha upamaiḥ || 5-16-9

9. jana sthāne = at Janastāna; catur dasha sahasrāṇi = fourteen thousands; rakshasān = of raksasas; bhīma karmaṇām = of terrible deed; nihatāni = have been killed; agni shikha sharaiḥ = by arrows equalling tongues of fires.

"At Janastāna fourteen thousands of rakshasas of terrible deed have been killed by arrows equalling tongues of fires."

Sloka 10

karaḥ ca nihataḥ samkhye triśirāḥ ca nipātitaḥ |
dūṣaṇaḥ ca mahā tejā rāmeṇa vidita ātmanā || 5-16-10

10. rāmeṇa = by Rama; vidita ātmanā = with a famed mind; Kharashca nihataḥ = Khara has been killed; trishirāḥ ca nipātitaḥ = ṭrisira also has been fell down; mahātejāḥ dūṣaṇaḥ ca = Dūshana with great glory also (has been killed); samkhye = in war.

"By Rama with a famed mind, Khara has been killed, ṭrisira also has been fell down, Dūshana with great glory also has been killed in war."

Sloka 11

aiśvaryam vānarāṇām ca durlabham vāli pālitam |
asyā nimitte sugrīvaḥ prāptavān loka satkṛtam || 5-16-11

11. asyāḥ = for ḥer; nimitte = sake; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; prāptavān = obtained; aishvaryam = wealth; vānarāṇām = of Vanaras; vāli pālitam = ruled by Vali; durlabham = difficult to be obtained; loka satkṛitam = and worshiped by the world.

"For ḥer sake ṣugreeva obtained wealth of vanaras ruled by Vali difficult to be obtained and worshiped by the world."

Sloka 12

sāgaraḥ ca mayā krāntaḥ śrīmān nada nadī patiḥ |
asyā hetor viśāla akṣyāḥ purī ca iyam nirīkṣitā || 5-16-12

12. asyāḥ heto = for ḥer sake; vishāla akṣyāḥ = with wide eyes; sāgaraḥ ca = the ocean; nada nadī patiḥ = lord of rivers and streams; krāntaḥ = has been crossed; mayā = by me; iyam = this; purī ca = city also; nirīkṣitā = has been explored.

"For the sake of ḥer, with wide eyes, the ocean, lord of rivers and streams, has been crossed by me; this city also has been explored."

Sloka 13

yadi rāmaḥ samudrāntām medinīm parivartayet |
asyāḥ kṛte jagat ca api yuktam iti eva me matiḥ || 5-16-13

13. yadi = if; rāmaḥ = Rama; asyāḥ kṛite = for ḥer sake; parivartayet = turns upside down; medinīm = earth; samudrāntām = with the ocean at the end; jagat ca api = and the entire universe; yuktam iti = it is fair; eva me matiḥ = this is my opinion.

"If ṣri Rama for ḥer sake turns upside down the earth with the ocean at the end, and the entire universe it is fair, this is my opinion."

Sloka 14

rājyam vā triṣu lokeṣu sītā vā janaka ātmajā |
trailokya rājyam sakalam sītāyā na āpnuyāt kalām || 5-16-14

14. triṣu lokeṣu = among three worlds; rājyam vā = kingdom or; sītā vā = ṣeetha; janakātmaja = the daughter of Janaka; sakalam trailokya rājyam = all the kingdom of three worlds; na āpnuyāt = will not approach; sītāyāḥ kalām = a sixteenth part of ṣeetha.

"Among three worlds, if it is a choice between kingdom or ṣeetha, the daughter of Janaka, all the kingdom of three worlds will not approach a sixteenth part of Seetha."

Sloka 15

iyam sā dharma śīlasya janakasya mahātmanaḥ |
sutā maithilarājasya sītā bhartṛdṛḍha vratā || 5-16-15

15. iyam = ṭhis ṣhe; sā sītā = is that ṣeetha; sutā = the daughter; janakasya maithilarājasya = of king Janaka of ṃithila; mahātmanaḥ = the great soul; dharma shīlasya = and noble one; bhartṛidṛiDavrata = with a firm resolution towards husband.

"ṭhis ṣhe is that ṣeetha, the daughter of king Janaka of ṃithila, the great soul, and noble one with a firm resolution towards husband."

Sloka 16

utthitā medinīm bhittvā kṣetre hala mukha kṣate |
padma reṇu nibhaiḥ kīrṇā śubhaiḥ kedāra pāṃsubhiḥ || 5-16-16

16. kṣetre hala mukha kṣate = while a field was being ploughed; utthitā = (ṣhe) rose up; medinīm bhittvā = splitting the earth; kīrṇā = covered; shubhaiḥ kedāra pāmsubhiḥ = by auspicious dust of field; padma reṇu nibhaiḥ = equalling pollen.

"While a field was being ploughed ṣhe rose up splitting the earth covered by auspicious dust of field equalling pollen."

Sloka 17

vikrāntasya ārya śīlasya samyugeṣu anivartinaḥ |
snuṣā daśarathasya eṣā jyeṣṭhā rājño yaśasvinī || 5-16-17

17. eṣā = ṣhe is; yashasvinī = the famed; jyeṣṭhā snuṣā = eldest daughter-in-law; rājñaḥ dasharathasya = of king Dasaratha; vikrāntasya = valorous one; anivartinaḥ = not turning back; samyugeṣu = in the battle.

"ṣhe is the famed eldest daughter-in-law of king Dasaratha the valorous one, not turning back in the battle."

Sloka 18

dharmajñasya kṛtajñasya rāmasya vidita ātmanaḥ |
iyam sā dayitā bhāryā rākṣasī vaśam āgatā || 5-16-18

18. iyam = this ṣhe; dayitā bhāryā = dear wife; rāmasya = of Rama; dharmajñasya = the righteous one; kṛitajñasya = grateful one; vidita ātmanaḥ = knower of ṣelf; āgatā = has obtained; rākṣasī vasam = capture of demonesses.

"ṭhis ṣhe the dear wife of Rama the righteous one, grateful one, knower of ṣelf has obtained capture of demonesses."

Sloka 19Verse Locator

sarvān bhogān parityajya bhartṛ sneha balāt kṛtā |
acintayitvā duhkhāni praviṣṭā nirjanam vanam || 5-16-19
samtuṣṭā phala mūlena bhartṛ śuśrūṣaṇā parā |
yā parām bhajate prītim vane api bhavane yathā || 5-16-20
sā iyam kanaka varṇa angī nityam susmita bhāṣiṇī |
sahate yātanām etām anarthānām abhāginī || 5-16-21

19-21. yā = whoever ṣeetha; parityajya = abandoning; sarvān bhogān = all comforts; bhartṛi sneha balākṛitā = forced by the love towards husband; acintayitvā = not thinking; duhkhāni = (about) sorrows; praviṣṭā = entering; nirjanam vanam = forest unpopulated with humans; samtuṣṭā = being happy; phala mūlena = with fruits and roots; bhartṛi shushrūṣaṇā parā = devoted to the service of ḥer husband; vane api = even in forest; bhajate = is getting; parām prītim = great happiness; bhavane yathā = like in a palace; kanaka varṇa angī = the one with golden limbs; nityam susmita bhāṣiṇī = always with a smiling talk; anarthānām abhāginī = not suited to misfortunes; sā iyam = that this ṣeetha; sahate = is bearing; etām yātanām = all this suffering.

"Whoever ṣeetha abandoning all comforts, forced by the love towards husband not thinking about sorrows, entering forest unpopulated with humans, being happy with fruits and roots, devoted to the service of Her husband, even in forest is getting great happiness like in a palace, the one with golden limbs, always with a smiling talk, not suited to misfortunes - that this Seetha is bearing all this suffering."

Sloka 22

imām tu śīla sampannām draṣṭum iccati rāghavaḥ |
rāvaṇena pramathitām prapām iva pipāsitaḥ || 5-16-22

22. rāghavaḥ = Rama; draṣṭum icchati = likes to see; imām = this ṣeetha; sīlasampannām = who is endowed with excellent character; rāvaṇena pramathitām = and tormented by Ravana; pipāsitaḥ prapām iva = like a thirsty one for a place where water is available freely.

"ṣri Rama likes to see this ṣeetha who is endowed with excellent character and tormented by Ravana, like a thirsty one for a place where water is available freely."

Sloka 23

asyā nūnam punar lābhād rāghavaḥ prītim eṣyati |
rājā rājya paribhraṣṭaḥ punaḥ prāpya iva medinīm || 5-16-23

23. asyāḥ lābhād = by ḥer gain; punaḥ = again; rāghavaḥ = Rama; nūnam = definitely; eṣyati = will get; prītim = happiness; rājā iva = like a king; rājya paribhraṣṭaḥ = who lost kingdom; prāpyaḥ punaḥ = getting back; medinīm = the land.

"By ḥer gain again ṣri Rama definitely will get happiness like a king who lost kingdom getting back the land."

Sloka 24

kāma bhogaiḥ parityaktā hīnā bandhu janena ca |
dhārayati ātmano deham tat samāgama kānkṣiṇī || 5-16-24

24. parityaktā = left; kāma bhogaiḥ = by coveted enjoyments; hīnā = lacking; bandhu janena ca = relatives also; dhārayati = (ṣeetha) is keeping; deham = body; tat samāgama kānkṣiṇī = desirous of meeting with Rama.

"Left by coveted enjoyments, lacking relatives also ṣeetha is keeping body desirous of meeting with Sri Rama."

Sloka 25

na eṣā paśyati rākṣasyo na imān puṣpa phala drumān |
ekastha hṛdayā nūnam rāmam eva anupaśyati || 5-16-25

25. eṣā = ṣhe; na pashyati = is not seeing; rākṣasyaḥ = demonesses; na = not; puṣpa phala drumān = flowers; fruits or trees; ekastha hṛidayā = with a single heart; rāmam eva anupashyati = is meditating only on ṣri Rama; nūnam = this is sure.

"ṣhe is not seeing demonesses, not flowers, fruits or trees and with a single heart is meditating only on Sri Rama - this is sure."

Sloka 26

bhartā nāma param nāryā bhūṣaṇam bhūṣaṇād api |
eṣā hi rahitā tena śobhana arhā na śobhate || 5-16-26

26. bhartā nāma = husband is; hi = indeed; param bhūṣaṇam = the greatest adornment; nāryāḥ = for a woman; bhūṣaṇād api = greater than jewellery; eṣā = this ṣeetha; shobhana arhā = though deserving of decoration; na shobhate = is not looking charming; tena rahitā = without ṣri Rama.

"ḥusband is indeed the greatest adornment for a woman greater than jewellery; this ṣeetha though deserving of decoration, is not looking charming without Sri Rama."

Sloka 27

duṣkaram kurute rāmo hīno yad anayā prabhuḥ |
dhārayati ātmano deham na duhkhena avasīdati || 5-16-27

27. rāmaḥ = ṣri Rama; hīnāḥ = (although) bereft; anayā = of ḥer; prabhuḥ = that Lord ṣri Rama; kurute = is doing; duṣkaram = an impossible task; dhārayati iti yad = by retaining; ātmanaḥ deham = ḥis body; na avasīdati = not being depressed; duhkhena = by sorrow.

"ṣri Rama although bereft of ḥer that Lord Rama is doing an impossible task by retaining ḥis body and not being depressed by sorrow."

Sloka 28

imām asita keśa antām śata patra nibha īkṣaṇām |
sukha arhām duḥkhitām j~ātvā mama api vyathitam manaḥ || 5-16-28

28. j~ātvā = knowing; imām = ḥer; asita kesha antām = with black ended hairs; shata patra nibha īkṣaṇām = with lotus like eyes; sukha arhām = deserving of comforts; duḥkhitām = in sorrow; mama manaḥ api = my mind also; vyathitam = is gloomy.

"Knowing ḥer with black ended hairs, with lotus like eyes, deserving of comforts, and in sorrow, my mind also is gloomy."

Sloka 29

kṣiti kṣamā puṣkara samnibha akṣī |
yā rakṣitā rāghava lakṣmaṇābhyām |
sā rākṣasībhir vikṛta īkṣaṇābhiḥ |
samrakṣyate samprati vṛkṣa mūle || 5-16-29

29. yā = whoever (ṣeetha); kṣiti kṣamā = with earth like patience; puṣkara samnibha akṣī = with eyes resembling lotuses; rakṣitā = was protected; rāghava lakṣmaṇābhyām = by ṣri Rama and Lakshmana; sā = that ṣeetha; samprati = now; samrakṣyate = is being guarded; rākṣasībhiḥ = by ogresses; vikṛita īkṣaṇābhiḥ = with crooked eyes; vṛikṣa mūle = at the base of a tree.

"Whoever ṣeetha with earth-like patience, with eyes resembling lotuses, was protected by ṣri Rama and Lakshmana, that Seetha now is being guarded by ogresses with crooked eyes at the base of a tree."

Sloka 30

hima hata nalinī iva naṣṭa śobhā |
vyasana paramparayā nipīḍyamānā |
saha cara rahitā iva cakra vākī |
janaka sutā kṛpaṇām daśām prapannā || 5-16-30

30. naṣṭa shobhā = with lost glory; hima hata nalinī iva = like a lotus plant hit by snow; nipīDyamānā = being tormented; vyasana paramparayā = by a series of calamities; janaka sutā = ṣeetha; prapanna = obtained; kṛipaṇām dashām = a pitiable condition; cakra vākī iva = like a chakravaka bird; saha cara rahitā = without a companion.

"With lost glory like a lotus plant hit by snow, being tormented by a series of calamities, ṣeetha obtained a pitiable condition like a chakravaka bird without a companion."

Sloka 31

asyā hi puṣpa avanata agra śākhāḥ |
śokam dṛḍham vai janayati aśokāḥ |
hima vyapāyena ca śītaraśmiḥ |
rabhyutthito na eka sahasra raśmiḥ || 5-16-31

31. ashokāḥ = Ashoka trees; puṣpa avanata agra shākhāḥ = with bent branches due to flowers; janayanti = are giving rise to; shokam = gloom; dṛiDham = a lot; asyāḥ = to ḥer; hima vyapāyena = due to melting of snow; abhyutthitaḥ = the risen up; sītarashmiḥ ca = moon also; na eka sahasra rashmiḥ = with thousands of rays (is causing grief).

"Ashoka trees with bent branches due to flowers are giving rise to gloom a lot to ḥer; Due to melting of snow the risen up moon also with thousands of rays is causing grief."

Sloka 32

iti evam artham kapir anvavekṣya |
sītā iyam iti eva niviṣṭa buddhiḥ |
saṃśritya tasmin niṣasāda vṛkṣe |
balī harīṇām ṛṣabhaḥ tarasvī || 5-16-32

32. balī = the mighty one; harīṇām ṛiṣabhaḥ = the best among vanaras; tarasvī = quick one; kapiḥ = ḥanuma; iti evam = thus; anvavekṣya = observed; artham = the subject; iyam sītā = this is ṣeetha; iti eva = thus; niviṣṭa buddhiḥ = with a firm mind; niṣasāda = sat; tasmin vṛikṣe = at that tree; samshritya = leaning against (it).

ṭhe mighty one, the best among vanaras, quick one, ḥanuma thus observed the subject: "ṭhis is ṣeetha!" - thus with a firm mind sat at that tree, leaning against it.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ṣoḍaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 16th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
