Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 101
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama laments over the uncertain condition of Lakshmana. He calls Sushena, the monkey-physician. Sushena tests Lakshmana and says he is still alive. Sushena then requests Hanuma to go all the way to Himalayas and bring the required herbs to treat Lakshmana. Hanuma proceeds to ṃount ṃahodara and failing to identify the herbs there, carries the mountain-peak itself and brings it to Sushena. Lakshmana regains consciousness, after getting treated with that herb by Sushena.

Sloka 1

śaktyā nipātitaṃ dṛṣṭvā rāvaṇena balīyasā |
lakṣmaṇaṃ samar śūraṃ śoṇitaughapariplutam || 6-101-1
sa dattvā tumulan yuddhaṃ rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ |
visṛjaneva bāṇaughānsuṣeṇan vākyamabravīt || 6-101-2

1; 2. dṛiṣhṭyā = seeing; shūram = the valiant; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; shoṇitau ghapariphitam = who was bathed in a stream of blood; samare = in battle; nipātitam = when overthrown; shaktyā = with a spear; balīyasā rāvaṇena = by the mighty Ravana; saḥ = Rama; dattvā = giving; tumulam = a tumultuous; yuddham = fight; durātmanaḥ rāvaṇaḥ = with the evil-minded Ravana; visṛijanneva = and even while releasing; bāṇaughān = a multitude of arrows; abravīt = spoke; suṣheṇam = to ṣushena; idam = the following words:

Seeing the valiant Lakshmana bathed in a stream of blood, when struck down in battle with a spear by the mighty Ravana and having given a tumultuous fight, to the evil-minded Ravana, Rama spoke as follows to Sushena, even while releasing a multitude of arrows (at Ravana).

Sloka 3

eṣa rāvaṇavegena lakṣmaṇaḥ patitaḥ kṣitau |
sarpavadveṣṭate vīro mama śokamudīrayan || 6-101-3

3. eṣhaḥ = this; vīraḥ = heroic; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; patitaḥ = who fell down; bhuvi = on earth; rāvaṇa vīryeṇa = on account of Ravana's prowess; udīrayan = increasing; shokam = my sorrow; cheṣhṭate = is wiggling; bhūmau = on the ground; sarpavat = like a serpent.

"This heroic Lakshmana, falling down on earth yielding to Ravana's prowess, is wiggling like a serpent, causing an increase in grief to me."

Sloka 4

śoṇitārdramiman vīraṃ prāṇairiṣṭataraṃ mama |
paśyato mama kā śaktiryoddhuṃ paryākulātmanaḥ || 6-101-4

4. kā = what; shaktiḥ = energy (is there); mama = for me; paryākulātmanaḥ = disturbed in mind; yauddhum = to fight; pashyataḥ = while seeing; imam = this; vīram = valiant Lakshmana; mama priyataram prāṇaiḥ = the most beloved to me as dear as life; shoṇitārdram = bathed in blood?

"ṃy energy to fight is failing, even as I see this valiant Lakshmana, who is dearer to me than life, bathed in blood, my mind being greatly disturbed."

Sloka 5

ayan sa samaraślāghī bhrātā me śubhalakṣaṇaḥ |
yadi pañcatvamāpannaḥ prāṇairme kin sukhena vā || 6-101-5

5. kim = of what use; prāṇaiḥ = is life; sukhena vā = or happiness; me = to me; yadi saḥ ayam me bhrātā = if this famous brother of mine; shubha lakṣhaṇaḥ = who is endowed with auspicious bodily marks; samarashlāghī = and always speaks highly of war; āpannaḥ = obtain; paṇ^chatvam = his death?

"Of what use is life or happiness to me, if this illustrious brother of mine, who is endowed with auspicious bodily marks and who always speaks highly of war, meets with his death?"

Sloka 6

lajjatīva hi me vīryaṃ bhraśyatīva karāddhanuḥ |
sāyakā vyavasīdanti dṛṣṭirbāṣpavaśan gatā || 6-101-6

6. me = my; vīryam = valour; lajjatīva = is feeling shy; as it were;dhanuḥ = my bow; bhrashyatīva = is falling down; karāt = from my hand; sāyakāḥ = my arrows; vyavasīdanti = are dropping down; dṛiṣhṭiḥ = my vision; gatā = got; bāṣhpavasham = overcome with tears.

"ṃy valour is feeling shy, as it were. ṃy bow seems to fall down from any hand. ṃy arrows are dropping down. ṃy vision has been overcome with tears."

Sloka 7

avasīdanti gātrāṇi svapnayāne nṛṇāmiva |
cintā me vardhate tīvrā mumūrṣāpi ca jāyate || 6-101-7

7. gātrāṇi = my limbs; avasīdanti = seem to sink down; nṛiṇām iva = like those of men; svapnayāne = in sleep; chintā = tension; vartate = is; tīvrā = growing; me = in me; jāyate mumūrṣhāpi = I even wish to die.

"ṃy limbs seem to sink down, like those of men in sleep. I have a serious tension, growing in me. I even wish to die."

Sloka 8

bhrātaraṃ nihatan dṛṣṭvā rāvaṇena durātmanā |
viṣṭanntaṃ tu duḥkhārtaṃ marmaṇyabhihataṃ bhṛśam || 6-101-8

8. bhrātaram = my brother; abhihatam = has been struck down; durātmanā = by the evil-minded; rāvaṇena = Ravana; abhihatam bhṛisham = seriously wounded; marmaṇi = in his vital parts; duḥkhārtam = afflicted with agony; viṣhṭanantam = and grooming.

"ṃy brother has been struck down by the evil-minded Ravana, seriously wounded in his vital parts, afflicted with agony and groaming."

Sloka 9

rāghavo bhrātaraṃ dṛṣṭya priyaṃ prāṇaṃ bahiścaram |
duḥkhena mahatāviṣṭo dhyānaśokaparāyaṇaḥ || 6-101-9

9. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; priyam = his beloved; bhrātaram = brother; prāṇam = who was his very life-breath; bahishcharam = moving outside; rāghavaḥ = Rama; āviṣhṭaḥ = was stricken; mahatā duḥkhena = with excessive agony; dhayāna shoka parāyaṇaḥ = gave himself up to anxiety and grief.

Seeing his beloved brother, who was his very life-breath moving outside, Rama was afflicted with excessive agony and gave himself up to anxiety and grief.

Sloka 10

paran viṣādamāpanno vilalāpākulendriyaḥ |
na hi yuddhena me kāryaṃ naiva prāṇairna sītayā || 6-101-10

10. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; bhrātaram = his brother; nihatam raṇapāmsuṣhu = lying wounded in the dust of the battle-field; āpannaḥ = he got into; param = extreme; viṣhādam = despondency; vilalāpa = and lamented (as follows); ākulendriyaḥ = confused in mind; as he was.

Seeing Lakshmana his brother lying wounded in the dust of the battle-field, he got into extreme despondency and lamented (as follows), confused in mind, as he was:

Sloka 11

vijayo.api hi me śūra na priyāyopakalpate |
acakṣurviṣayaścandraḥ kāṃ prītiṃ janayiṣyati || 6-101-11

11. shūra = O the valiant Lakshmana!; vijayaḥ.api = even victory; na priyāya upakalpate = is not making a pleasure; me = for me; kām = what; prītim = pleasure; (can) chandraḥ = the moon; janayiṣhyati = create; achakṣhurviṣhayaḥ = for a man who has lost his vision?

"O the valiant Lakshmana! Even victory cannot create a pleasure for me. What pleasure can the moon create for a man who has lost his vision?"

Sloka 12

kiṃ me yuddhena kiṃ prāṇairyuddhakāryaṃ na vidyate |
yatrāyaṃ nihataḥ śete raṇamūrdhani lakṣmaṇaḥ || 6-101-12

12. kim me = what purpose of mine will be served by fighting; kim prāṇaiḥ = or even by life?; ayam lakṣhmaṇaḥ = this Lakshmana; shete = lies; nihataḥ = killed; yatra raṇamūrdhani = in forefront of battle here; na vidyate = I have no; yuddhakāryam = use for the war.

"What purpose of mine will be served by fighting or eve by life? When this Lakshmana lies killed in the forefront of battle here, I have no use for the war."

Sloka 13

yathaiva māṃ vanaṃ yāntamanuyāti mahādyutiḥ |
ahamapyupayāsyāmi tathaivainaṃ yamakṣayam || 6-101-13

13. yathaiva = in the same way as; mahādyutiḥ = the very glorious Lakshmana; amyāti = followed; mām = me; yāntam = when I was going; vanam = to the forest; ahamapi = I too; tathaiva = will in the same way; upayāsyāmi = follow; enam = him; yamakṣhayam = to the abode of Yama the Lord of Death.

"In the same way as the very glorious Lakshmana followed me When I was going to the forest, I too will in the same way follow him to the abode of Yama the lord of Death."

Sloka 14

iṣṭabandujano nityaṃ māṃ sa nityamanuvrataḥ |
imāmavasthāṃ gamito rākṣasaiḥ kūṭayodhibhiḥ || 6-101-14

14. saḥ = Lakshmana; iṣhṭa bandhujanaḥ = who is having his beloved relatives; anuvrataḥ = and who is ardently devoted; mām = to me; nityam = forever; gamitaḥ = is obtained; imām = this; avasthām = condition; rākṣhasaiḥ = by the demons; kūṭayodhibhiḥ = who fight treacherously.

"The demons, who fight treacherously, created this condition to Lakshmana, who loved his relatives and who was ardently devoted forever to me."

Sloka 15

deśe deśe kalatrāṇi deśe deśe ca bāndhavāḥ |
taṃ tu deśaṃ na paśyāmi yatra bhrātā sahodaraḥ || 6-101-15

15. kalatrāṇi = wives may be obtained; deshe deshe = everywhere; bāndhavāḥ = relatives (can be had) deshe deshe cha = everywhere; na pashyāmi = I do not find; bhrātā = brother; sahodaraḥ = born of the same womb; yatra tam desham tu = as such a place; wherever.

"Wives may be obtained ever where. Relatives can be had every where. However, I do not find a brother, born of the same womb, at such a place whatesoever."

Sloka 16

ityevaṃ vilapantaṃ taṃ śokavihvalitendriyam |
viveṣṭamānaṃ karuṇamucchvasantaṃ punaḥ punaḥ || 6-101-16

16. (ṭhe monkeys saw) tam = that Rama; vilapantam ityevam = thus lamenting; shoka vihvalitendriyam viveṣhṭamānam = with his mind reeling and distressed in grief; karuṇam = pitiably; uchchhvasantam = sighing his breath; punaḥ punaḥ = repeatedly.

The monkeys saw that Rama, thus lamenting with his mind reeling and distressed in grief and pitiably sighing his breath repeatedly.

Sloka 17

kiṃ nu rājye durdharṣalakṣmaṇena vinā mama |
kathaṃ vakṣyāmyahaṃ tvambāṃ sumitrāṃ putravatsalām || 6-101-17

17. kim nu = of what use; rājyena = is the kingdom; mama = for me; durdharṣha lakṣmaṇena vinā = without Lakshmana; who was difficult to over power?; aham katham akṣhyāmi = what can I tell; sumitrām = to ṣumitra; putravatsalāmambām = the mother; who was fond of her son.

"Of what use is the kingdom for me, without Lakshmana, who was difficult to over power? What can I tell to Sumitra, the mother who was so fond of her son?"

Sloka 18

upālambhaṃ na śakṣyāmi soḍhuṃ dattaṃ sumitrayā |
kiṃ nu vakṣyāmi kausalyāṃ mātaraṃ kiṃ nu kaikayīm || 6-101-18

18. na shakṣhyāmi = I shall not be able; soDhum = to bear; upālambham = the reproach; dattam = which will be given; sumitrayā = by sumitra; kim nu vakṣhyāmi = what shall I say; kausalyām = to Kausalya; mātaram = the mother; kimnu = and what shall I say; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi?

"I shall not be able to bear the reproach, which will be levelled by Sumitra. What shall I pay to kausalya the mother or what shall I say to Kaikeya?"

Sloka 19

bharataṃ kiṃ nu vakṣyāmi śatrughnaṃ ca mahābalam |
saha tena vanaṃ yāto vinā tenāgataḥ katham || 6-101-19

19. kim nu vakṣhyāmi = what shall I say; bharatam = to Bhrata; mahābalam shatrughnam = or to the mighty ṣhatrughna; (when they ask me); katham = how; āgataḥ = I can back; tena vinā = without Lakshmana; yātaḥ = eventhogh I went; vanam = to the forest; tena saha = along with him?

"What shall I say to Bharata or to the mighty Shatrughna, when they ask me, how I came back without Lakshmana even though I went to the forest along with him?"

Sloka 20

ihaiva maraṇaṃ śreyo na tu bandhuvigarhaṇam |
kiṃ mayā duṣkṛtaṃ karma kṛtamanyatra janmani || 6-101-20
yena me dhārmiko bhrātā nihataścāgrataḥ sthitaḥ |

20. maraṇam = death; shreyaḥ = is better; ihaiva = here only; tu = but; na = not; bandhuvigarhaṇam = a reproaches by one's own people; kim = what; duṣkṛitam karma = sinful act; kṛitam = was done; mayā = by me; anyatra janmani = in another life; yena = due to which; dhārmikaḥ me bhrātā = my righteous brother; nihataḥ = having been killed; sthitaḥ = lies; agrataḥ = before me.

"It is better to die at this place only, rather than hear the reproaches of one's own people. What sinful act was done by me in a previous birth due to which my righteous brother lies killed before me?

Sloka 21

hā bhrātarmanujaśreṣṭha śūrāṇām pravara prabho || 6-101-21
ekākī kiṃ nu māṃ tyaktvā paralokāya gacchasi |

21. hā = Alas!; bhrātaḥ = O brother; manuja shroṣhṭha = the best of men!; pravara = O the foremost; shūrāṇām = among the valiant men!; prabho = O the powerful man!; tyaktvā = deserting; mām = me; kim nu = why; gachchhasi = are you going; paralokāya = to the other world; ekākī = alone?

"Alas! O the powerful brother, the best of men! O the foremost among the valiant men! Why are you going to the other world, deserting me?"

Sloka 22

vilapantaṃ ca māṃ bhrātaḥ kimarthaṃ nāvabhāṣase || 6-101-22
uttiṣṭha paśya kiṃ śeṣe dīnaṃ māṃ paśya cakṣuṣā |

22. bhrātaḥ = O brother!; kim = why; nāvabhāṣhase = don't you talk; mām = to me; vilapantam = who is lamenting?; uttiṣhṭha = rise; pashya = and see!; kim = why; sheṣhe = are you lying down? pashya = see; dīnam = the miserable; mām = me; chakṣhuṣhā = with your own eyes.

"O brother! Why don't you talk to me, even though I am lamenting? Rise and see! Why are you lying down? See me, by opening your own eyes, miserable as I am."

Sloka 23

śokārtasya pramattasya parvateṣu vaneṣu ca || 6-101-23
viṣaṇṇasya mahābāho samāśvāsayitā mama |

23. mahābāho = O the mighty armed!; samāshvāsayitā = you have been indeed consolign; mama = me; viṣhaṇṇasya = when I felt low-spirited; pramattasya = and listless; shokāntasya = stricken with grief as I was; parvateṣhu = in mountain; vaneṣhu = and forests.

"O the mighty armed! You have been indeed comforting me, whenever stricken with grief, I roamed listlessly among mountains and forests or felt low-spirited."

Sloka 24

rāmamevaṃ bruvāṇaṃ tu śokavyākulitendriyam || 6-101-24
āśvāsayannuvācedaṃ suṣeṇaḥ paramaṃ vacaḥ |

24. āshvāsayan = comforting; rāmam = Rama; shoka vyākulitendriyam = whose mind was full of grief; evam = and thus; bruvāṇam = speaking (lamenting); suṣheṇaḥ = ṣushena; uvācha = addressed; idam paramam vachaḥ = the following excellent words.

Comforting Rama, whose mind was full of grief and who was thus lamenting, Sushena, for his part, addressed the following excellent words:

Sloka 25

tyajemāṃ naraśārdūla buddhiṃ vaiklabyakāriṇīm || 6-101-25
śokasaṃjananīṃ cintāṃ tulyāṃ bāṇaiścamūmukhe |

25. navashārdūla = O the excellent among men!; tyaja = give up; buddhim = this notion; vaiklabyakāriṇīm = which causes despondency in you; imām chintām = this anguish; shoka samjananīm = which gives rise to grief; tulyām bāṇaiḥ = and is as piercing as arrows; chamūmukhe = in the forefront of battle.

"O the excellent among men! Give up this notion, which causes despondency in you, this anguish which gives rise to grief and is as piercing as arrows, in the forefront of battle."

Sloka 26

naiva pañcatvamāpanno lakṣmaṇo lakṣmivardhanaḥ || 6-101-26
na hysya vikṛtaṃ vaktraṃ na ca śyāmatvamāgatam |

26. lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; lakṣhmivardhanaḥ = the augmenter of prosperity; naina āpannaḥ = has not got; paṇ^chatvam = into death; asya = his; vaktram = countenance; na vikṛitam hi = is not changed; nāgatam shyāmatvam = nor has it became dark.

"Lakshmana, the augmenter of prosperity, has not got into death. His countenance is not changed, nor has it become dark."

Sloka 27

suprabhan ca prasannaṃ ca mukhamasyābhilakṣyate || 6-101-27
padmaraktatalau hastau suprasanne ca locane |

27. asya mukham = Let his face; nirīkṣhyatām = be seen; suprabham = with a good brightness; suprasannam cha = and very much placid; hastau = ḥis hands; padmapatra talau = are having palms; resembling the petals of a lotus; lochane cha = ḥis eyes too; suprasanne = are very bright.

"His face may be seen with a good brightness and very much placid. The palms of his hands are resembling the petals of a lotus. His eyes too are very bright."

Sloka 28

nedṛśaṃ dṛśyate rūpaṃ gatāsūnāṃ viśāṃ pate || 6-101-28
viṣādaṃ mā kṛthā vīra saprāṇo.ayamariṃdama |

28. vishāmpate = O king!; rūpam = the appearance; gatāsūnām = of the dead; nadṛishyate = does not look; īdṛisham = like this; vīra = O hero; arimdama = the annihilator of enemies!; ayam = ḥe; saprāṇaḥ = is all with his life; viṣhādam mākṛithāḥ = do not worry|

"O king! The appearance of the dead does not look like this. O hero, the annihilator of enemies! He is all with his life. Do not worry."

Sloka 29

ākhyāti tu prasuptasya srastagātrasya bhūtale || 6-101-29
socchvāsaṃ hṛdayaṃ vīra kampamānaṃ muhurmuhuḥ |

29. muhurmuhuḥ = the repeatedly; kampamānam = beating; hṛidayam = heart; sochchhvāsam = with the take of the breath; (of Lakshmana); prasuptasya bhutale = lying on the earth's surface; fast asleep; sraptagātrasya = with his limbs relaxed; ākhyāti = are telling; vīra = O the gallant prince!.

"The repeatedly beating heart with the signs of breath of Lakshmana, lying on the earth's surface, fast asleep, with his limbs relaxed are telling it, O the gallant prince!"

Sloka 30

evaṃ na vidyate rūpan gatāsūnān viśāṃ pate || 6-101-30
samīpasthamuvācedaṃ hanūmantaṃ mahākapim |

30. uktvā = having addressed; vachaḥ = the words; evam = thus; rāghavam = to Rama; suṣheṇaḥ = ṣushena; mahāprājJṇaḥ = the highly learned one; uvācha = spoke; idam = these words; hanūmantam = to ḥanuma; mahākapim = the great monkey; samīpastham = who was standing nearby.

Having addressed the words thus to Rama, the highly learned Sushena spoke the following words to Hanuma the great monkey, who was standing nearby:

Sloka 31

saumya śīghramito gatvā śailamoṣadhiparvatam || 6-101-31
pūrvan hi kathito yoasau vīra jāmbavatā śubhaḥ |
dakṣiṇe śikhare tasya jātāmoṣadhimānaya || 6-101-32
viśalyakaraṇī nāma viśalyakaraṇīn śubhām |
saṃjīvakaraṇīṃ vīra saṃdhānīṃ ca mahauṣadhīm || 6-101-33
saṃjīvanārthaṃ vīrasya lakṣmaṇasya mahātmanaḥ |

31; 32; 33. gatvā = going; itaḥ = from this place; shīghram = quickly; saumya = O gentle one!; shailam = to the mountain; ouṣhadhiparvatam = called Oushadhi ṃountain; kathitaḥ = which was already narrated; tava = to you; pūrvam = previously; jānbavatā = by Jambavan; vīra = O brave one!; ānaya = bring; iha = here; samjīvanārtham = for restoring to consciousness; vīrasya mahātmanaḥ lakṣhmaṇasya = the great souled and the heroic Lakshmana; mahauṣhadhim vishalyakaraṇīm(1) = the precious herb Vishalya karani; nāmnā = by name; jātām = which has sprung up; dakṣhiṇe shikhare = on its southern peak; tathā = and; sāvarṇa karaṇīm(2) = savarngakarani; sanjīvakaraṇīm(3) = ṣanjiva karani; mahauṣhadhīm samdhānīm cha(4) = and the precious herb ṣamdhanakarani.

"Proceeding from this place with full speed, O gentle one!, to the mountain called Oushadhi (ṃahodaya), which was already described to you previously by Jambavan, O brave one! Bring here for restoring the great-souled and heroic Lakshmana to consciousness, the precious herb Vishalyakarani (1) by name which was sprung up on its southern peak, Savarnakarnani (2), Samjivakarani(3) and the precious herb, Samdhanakarani (4)."

(1) Vishalyakarani: A herb credited with the virtue of expelling an arrow /other weapons from the body, healing the wound and relieving pain. (2) Savarnakarani: Another herb supposed to possess the property of counteracting the discolouration caused by a wound, burns etc. and restoring the original colour of the skin. (3) Samjivakarani: Another herb believed to possess the virtue of bringing back an unconscious person to consciousness. (4) Samdhani: A herb credited with the property of joining a fractured bone.

Sloka 34

ityevamukto hanumāngatvā cauṣadiparvatam || 6-101-34
cintāmabhyagamacchrīmānajānaṃstā mahauṣadhīḥ |

34. ityevam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; shrīmān = the illustrious; hanumān = ḥanuma; gatvā = having gone; ouṣhadhiparvatam = to mount Oushadha; ajānam = could not identify; tāḥ = those; mahauṣhadhīḥ = precious herbs; abhyagamat = became; chintām = thoughtful.

Hearing those words, Hanuma, having sprung to ṃount Oushadhi, could not identify those precious herbs and became thoughtful.

Sloka 35

tasya buddhiḥ samutpannā māruteramitaujasaḥ || 6-101-35
idameva gamiṣyāmi gṛhītvā śikharaṃ gireḥ |

35. tasa māruteḥ = to that ḥanuma; amitoujasaḥ = whose strength was unlimited; buddhiḥ = the thought; samutpannā = arouse; (as follows:); gamiṣhyāmi = I will go; gṛihītvā = taking; gireḥ shikharameva = this mountain-peak itself.

The following thought arose to that Hanuma, whose strength was unlimited: "I will go back, taking this mountain-peak itself."

Sloka 36

asmiṃstu śikhare jātāmoṣadhiṃ tāṃ sukhāvahām || 6-101-36
pratarkeṇāvagacchāmi suṣeṇo hyevamabravīt |

36. pratarkeṇa = by conjecture; avagachchhāmi = I understand; tām = that; sukhāvahām = delightful; oṣhadhi = herb; jātām = must have sprouted; asmin shikhare = in this mountain-peak; suṣheṇaḥ = ṣushena; abravīt hi = indeed has said; evam = so.

"By conjecture, I understand that the delightful herb must have sprouted in this mountain-peak. Sushena indeed has said so."

Sloka 37

agṛhya yadi gacchami viśalyakaraṇīmaham || 6-101-37
kālātyayena doṣaḥ syādvaiklabyaṃ ca mahadbhavet |

37. aham gachchhāmi yadi = If I go; ag^ihya = without taking; vishalyakaraṇīm = Vishalyakarani; doṣhaḥ = harm; syāt = may come; kālātyayena = through passage of time; mahat cha = and a great; vaiklabyam = perplexity; bhavet = may arise.

"If I go without taking Vishalyakarani, harm may come through passage of time and a great perplexity may arise."

Sloka 38

iti sañcintya hanumāngatvā kṣipraṃ mahābalaḥ || 6-101-38
āsādya parvataśreṣṭhaṃ triḥ prakramya gireḥ śiraḥ |
plullanānātarugaṇaṃ samutpāṭya mahābalaḥ || 6-101-39
gṛhītvā hariśārdūlo hastābhyāṃ samatolayat |

38; 39. iti samchitya = thus thinking; hanumān = ḥanuma; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; harishārdūlaḥ = and excellent of monkeys; gatvā = having gone; kṣhipram = quickly; āsādya = and approaching; parvata shreṣhṭham = that excellent mountain; gṛihītvā = and taking; gireḥ shikharam = that mountain-peak; hastābhyām = with his arms; prakampya = shaking it well; triḥ = for three times; samutpāṭya = uprooting; pullanānātarugaṇam = the mountain-peak; which was endowed with many trees in bloom; samatolayat = lifted it up; mahābalaḥ = so mighty as he was.

Thinking thus, the mighty Hanuma the excellent of monkeys, having gone quickly and approaching that excellent mountain, taking that mountain-peak with his arms, shaking it violently for three times and uprooting the mountain-peak, which was endowed with many trees in bloom, lifted it up, so mighty as he was.

Sloka 40

sa nīlamiva jīmūtaṃ toyapūrṇam nabhastalāt || 6-101-40
utpapāta gṛhītvā tu hanūmāñśikharan gireḥ |

40. gṛihītvā = taking; gireḥ shikharam = that mountain-peak; nīlam toyapūrṇam jīmūtam iva = which looked like a dark rainy cloud; saḥ hanūmān = that ḥanuman; utpapāta = jumped up; nabhastalāt = into the sky.

Taking that mountain-peak, which looked like a dark rainy cloud, that Hanuman jumped up into the sky.

Sloka 41

samāgamya mahāvegaḥ saṃnyasya śikharaṃ gireḥ || 6-101-41
viśramyakiṃciddhanumān suṣeṇamidamabravīt |

41. samāgamya = coming; mahāvegaḥ = with a great speed; hanumān = ḥanuma; samnyasya = putting down; gireḥ shkharam = the mountain-peak; kimchit vishramya = and resting for a while; abravīt = spoke; idam = the following words; suṣhaṇam = to ṣushena.

Arriving with a great speed, Hanuma putting down the mountain-peak and resting for a while, spoke the following words to Sushena:

Sloka 42

oṣadhīrnāvagachāmi tā ahan haripuṅgava || 6-101-42
tadidan śikharan kṛtsnaṃ girestasyāhṛtaṃ mayā |

42. haripungava = O the excellent of monkeys!; aham = I; nāvagachchhāmi = could not identify; tāḥ = those; oṣhadhīḥ = herbs; tat idam kṛitsnam = that and this entire; tasya gireḥ shikharam = mountain-peak; āhṛitam = has been brought; mayā = by me.

"O the excellent of monkeys! I have brought the entire mountain-peak, as I could not identify those herbs."

Sloka 43

evam kathayamānaṃ taṃ praśasya pavanātmajam || 6-101-43
suṣeṇo vānaraśreṣṭho jagrāhotpāṭya cauṣadhīḥ |

43. prashasya = eulogizing; pavanātmajam = ḥanuma; evam kathamānam = who was thus narrating; suṣheṇaḥ = ṣushena; vānara shreṣhṭhaḥ = the excellent of monkeys; utpāṭya = pulling out; oṣhadhīḥ = the herbs; jagrāha = took hold of them.

Eulogizing Hanuma, who was thus narrating his trip, Sushena, the excellent of monkeys, pulling out the herbs, took hold off them.

Sloka 44

vismitāstu babhūvuste sarve vānarapuṃgavāḥ || 6-101-44
dṛṣṭvā hanūmataḥ karma surairapi suduṣkaram |

44. sarve te vānarapungavāḥ = all those monkey-chiefs; surairapi = and even the gods; babhūvuḥ vismitāḥ = were surprised; dṛiṣhṭvā = in seeing; duṣhkaram karma = that most difficult feat; hanūmataḥ = of ḥanuma.

All those monkey-chiefs and even the gods were surprised to see that most difficult feat of Hanuma.

Sloka 45

tataḥ saṅkṣodayitvā tāmoṣadhin vānarottamaḥ || 6-101-45
lakṣmaṇasya dadau nastaḥ suṣeṇaḥ sumahādyutiḥ |

45. tataḥ = then; samkṣhodayitvā = crushing; tām = that; oṣhadhim = herb; suṣheṇaḥ = ṣushena; vānarottamaḥ = the excellent of monkeys; sumahādyutiḥ = having a great splendour; dadau = administered (it); lakṣhmaṇasya = to Lakshmana; nastaḥ = through the nose.

Then, crushign that herb, Sushena the excellent of monkeys, having a great splendour, administered it through Lakshmana's nose.

Sloka 46

saśalyaḥ sa samāghrāya lakṣmaṇaḥ paravīrahā || 6-101-46
viśalyo virujaḥ śīghramudatiṣṭhanmahītalāt |

46. samāghrāya = duly inhaling it well; saḥ lakṣhmaṇaḥ = that Lakshmana; paravīrahā = the annihilator of adversaries; sashalyaḥ = having the spear lodged in his body; udatiṣhṭhat = raised; shīghram = quickly; mahītalāt = from the earth's surface; vishalyaḥ = rid as he was of the spear; virujaḥ = and his pain.

Duly inhaling it well, that Lakshmana, the annihilator of adversaries, having the spear lodged in his body, raised quickly from the earth's surface rid as he was of the spear and his pain.

Sloka 47

tamutthitaṃ tu harayo bhūtalātprekṣya lakṣmaṇam || 6-101-47
sādhu sādhviti suprītāḥ suṣeṇaṃ pratyapūjayan |

47. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam lakṣhmaṇam = that Lakshmana; utthitam = raisen; bhūtalāt = from the ground; harayaḥ = the monkeys; suprītāḥ = were over-joyed; pratyapūjayan = and applauded; lakṣhmaṇau = Lakshmana; sādhu sādhu iti = saying; "Excellent! Excellent!"

Seeing that Lakshmana risen from the ground, the mokneys were overjoyed and applauded Lakshmana, saying, "Excellent, excellent!".

Sloka 48

ehyehītyabravīdrāmo lākṣmaṇaṃ paravīrahā || 6-101-48
sasvaje snehagāḍhan ca bāṣpaparyākulekṣaṇaḥ |

48. rāmaḥ = Rama; paravīrahā = the destroyer of enemies; abravīt = said; lakṣhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; iti = saying; ehi ehi = "come; come!" sasvaje = and embraced him; snehagāDham cha = firmly with affection; bāṣhpaparyākulekṣhaṇaḥ = with his eyes clouded with tears.

Rama, the destroyer of enemies, said to Lakshmana, "come, come!" and embraced him firmly with affection, with his eyes clouded with tears.

Sloka 49

abravīcca pariṣvajya saumitrin rāghavastadā || 6-101-49
diṣṭyā tvān vīra paśyāmi maraṇātpunarāgatam |

49. pariṣhvajya = after embracing; saumitrim = Lakshmana; rāghavaḥ = Rama; tadā = then; abravīchcha = said to him; diṣhṭyā = luckily; vīra = O valiant one; pashyāmi = I see; tvām = you; punaḥ āgatam = returned; maraṇāt = from death.

After embracing Lakshmana, Rama then said to him: "Luckily, O valiant one, I see you here, returned from death."

Sloka 50

na hi me jīvitenārthaḥ sītayā ca jayena vā || 6-101-50
ko hi me jīvitenārthastvayi pañcatvamāgate |

50. na hi arthaḥ = there is indeed no purpose; me = of mine; jīvitena = by me my own life; sītayā = nor of ṣeetha; vijayenavā = nor of victory; tvaye = If you; āgate = have attained; paṇ^chatvam = demise; vada = tell me; kaḥ = what; arthaḥ = significane; (is there); tena = by it; me = to me?

"There is no purpose of mine, by me own life nor of Seetha nor of victory. If you have attained demise, tell me what significnace is there for it to me?"

Sloka 51

ityevan vadatastasya rāghavasya mahātmanaḥ || 6-101-51
khinnaḥ śithilayā vācā lakṣmaṇo vākyamabravīt |

51. mahātmanaḥ rāghavah = (while) the great souled Rama; vātaḥ = was speaking ityevam = thus; khinnaḥ = the exhausted; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the following words; shithilayā vāchā = in a feeble tone.

While the great souled Rama was speaking thus, the exhausted Lakshmana, in a feeble tone, spoke the following words:

Sloka 52

tāṃ pratijñāṃ pratijñāya purā satyaparākrama || 6-101-52
laghuḥ kaścidivāsattvo naivan vaktumihārhasi |

52. satya parākramā = O brother; true to your promise!; na arhasi = you ought not; vaktum = to speak; evam = like this; iha = now; kashchidiva = like somebody; asattvah = who has no courage; laghuḥ = and who is weak; pratijJṇāya = having taken; tām pratijJṇām = that vow (of killing Ravana); purā = before.

"Having solemnly taken that pledge (of killing Ravana) before, O brother true to your promise, you ought not to speak as you have done like a weak and courageless man."

Sloka 53

na pratijñān hi kurvanti vitathāṃ sādhavoanagha || 6-101-53
lakṣmaṇan hi mahattvasya pratijñāparipālanam |

53. satyavādinaḥ = those who speak the truth; na kurvanti hi = do not make; pratijJṇām = their promise; vitathām = futile; pratijJṇapālanam = fulfilling their pledge; lakṣhaṇam hi = is indeed the attribute; mahattvasya = of the moral amplitude.

"There is no purpose of mine, by me own life nor of Seetha nor of victory. If you have attained demise, tell me what significance is there for it to me?"

Sloka 54

nairāśyamupagantun te tadalaṃ matkṛteanagha || 6-101-54
vadhena rāvaṇasyādya pratijñāmanupālaya |

54. anagha = O the faultless one!; te = to you; upagantum cha nairāshyam = getting into hopelessness too; matkṛite = for my sake; nālam = is not befitting; anupālaya = fulfill; pratijJṇām = your promise; vadhena = by killing; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; adya = now.

"O the faultless one! Getting into homelessness for my sake is not befitting of you. Fulfill your promise now, by killing Ravana."

Sloka 55

na jīvanyāsyate śatrustava bāṇapathan gataḥ || 6-101-55
nardatastīkṣṇadaṃṣṭrasya sinhasyeva mahāgajaḥ |

55. shatruḥ = An enemy; gataḥ = who stands; tava bāṇa patham = within the path of your arrows; na yāsyate = cannot turn out; jīvan = alive; mahāgajaḥ iva = as a huge elephant; nardataḥ simhasya = (which gets into the path) of a roaring lion; tīkṣhṇa damṣhṭrsya = having terrible tusks.

"An enemy who falls victim to your arrows, cannot turn out alive, as a huge elephant which gets into the path of a roaring lion possessing terrible tusks."

Sloka 56

ahan tu vadhamicchāmi śīghramasya durātmanaḥ || 6-101-56
yāvadastaṃ na yātyeṣa kṛtakarmā divākaraḥ |

56. aham = I; ichchhāmi = wish for; vadham = the death; asya durātmanaḥ = of this evil-minded fellow; shīghram = instantly; yāvat = even before; eṣhaḥ = this; divākaraḥ = sun; kṛita karmā = having finished his task; na yāti = does not sink; astam = below the horizon.

"I, for my part, wish to see the death of this evil-minded fellow instantly, even before this sun, having finished his task, does not sink below the horizon."

Sloka 57

yadi vadhamicchasi rāvaṇasya saṃkhye |
yadi ca kṛtāṃ hi tavecchasi pratijñām |
yadi tava rājasutābhilāṣa ārya |
kuru ca vaco mama śīgramadya vīra || 6-101-57

57. ārya = O venerable; vīra = hero!; ichchhasiyadi = If you wish; vadham = the killing; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; samkhye = in battle; kṛitām ichchhasiyadi tava pratijJṇām = if you want to fulfil your promise; tava rāajasutābhilāṣhaḥ yadi = and if you feel affectionate towards ṣeetha; kurucha = you do; mama vachaḥ = as I tell you; shīghram = soon; adya = now.

"O venerable hero! If you wish to kill Ravana in battle, if you wish to fulfill your promise and if you feel affectionate towards Seetha, do as I tell you soon and now."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 101st chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
