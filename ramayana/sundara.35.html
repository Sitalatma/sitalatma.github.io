Introduction

Seetha asks Hanuma to describe the various characteristics of Rama and Lakshmana. Hanuma describes n detail to Seetha the appearance and qualities of Rama and Lakshmana. Hanuma also gives an account of how a friendship developed between Rama and Lakshmana as well as Sugreeva the Lord of monkeys. Hanuma also narrates his own life-story from the time he was born till his role as a minister of Sugreeva. He ends the story with his sight of Seetha in Lanka.


Sloka 1

Sloka 11

Sloka 14

"ḥe got trained in Yajurveda, the sacrificial Veda. ḥe is highly honoured by those well-versed in Vedas. He is skilled in Dhanurveda, the science of archery, other Vedas and the six limbs* of Vedangas."

"ḥearing those delightful words of ṣampati, we with our chief Angada started soon from there."

Sloka 68

vindhyādutthāya samprāptāḥ sāgarasyāntamuttaram |
tvat darśana kṛta utsāhā hṛṣṭāḥ tuṣṭāḥ plavamgamāḥ || 5-35-68

68. plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys; hṛiṣṭāḥ = were very much delighted; tuṣṭāḥ = and satisfied; tvaddarshana kṛitotsāho = and exercising their strength to see you; utthāya = having risen; vindhyāt = from Vindhya mountain; samprāptāḥ = reached; uttaram antam = the northern shore; sāgarasya = of the ocean.

"ṭhe monkeys were very much delighted and satisfied. ṭhey were making up their energy to see you, rose up from Vindhya mountain and reached the northern shore of the ocean."

Sloka 69

aṅgadapramukhāḥ sarve velopāntamupasthitāḥ |
cintām jagmuḥ punarbhītāstvaddarśanasamutsukāḥ || 5-35-69

69. sarve = all; aṇ^gada pramukhāḥ = the monkeys with their chief Angada; tvaddarshanamutsakāḥ = in their anxiety to see you; upasthitāḥ = reached; velopāntām = the shore-area of the ocean; bhītāḥ = being frightened; punaḥ = again; jagmuḥ = got; chintām = the worry.

"All the monkeys with their chief Angada, in their anxiety to see you, reached the ocean. ṭhey were frightened after seeing the ocean there and they were again worried."

Sloka 70

atha aham hari sainyasya sāgaram dṛśya sīdataḥ |
vyavadhūya bhayam tīvram yojanānām śatam plutaḥ || 5-35-70

70. atha = thereafter; prakṣya = seeing; sāgaram = the ocean; aham = I; vyavadhūya = removed; tīvram = the serious; bhayan = fear; harisainyasya = of the army of monkeys; sīdataḥ = who were worrying; plutaḥ = and crossed; shatam = hundred; yojanānām = Yojanas.

"ṭhereafter, seeing the ocean, I removed the serious fears of the army of monkeys who were worrying and crossed hundred Yojanas across the sea."

Sloka 71

lankā ca api mayā rātrau praviṣṭā rākṣasa ākulā |
rāvaṇaḥ ca mayā dṛṣṭaḥ tvam ca śoka nipīḍitā || 5-35-71

71. laṇ^kāchāpi = even Lanka; rākṣasākulā = filled with demons; praviṣṭā = was entered; mayā = by me; rātrau = in the night; rāvaṇashcha = Ravana too; dṛiṣṭaḥ = was seen; mayā = by me; tvamcha = You too; shoka pariplutā = filled with grief (were seen by me).

"Entering even Lanka, filled with demons in the night, I saw Ravana and you too who were immersed in grief."

Sloka 72

etat te sarvam ākhyātam yathā vṛttam anindite |
abhibhāṣasva mām devi dūto dāśaratheḥ aham || 5-35-72

72. devi = O princess; anindite = the faultless!; sarvam = all; etat = this; ākhyātam = was told; te = to you; yathāvṛittam = as actually occurred; abhibhāṣasva = talk; mām = to me; aham = I; dūtaḥ = am the messenger; dāsharatha = of Rama.

"O the faultless princess! I told you all this as actually occurred. I am the messenger of Rama. Talk to me."

Sloka 73

tvam mām rāma kṛta udyogam tvan nimittam iha āgatam |
sugrīva sacivam devi budhyasva pavana ātmajam || 5-35-73

73. devi = O princess!; buddhasva = know; tam mām = me as such; rāma kṛitodyogam = having made an effort on Rama's behalf; āgatam = who came; iha = here; tvannimittam = for your sake; sugreeva sachivam = ṣugreeva's minister; pavanātmajam = and son of wind-god.

"O princess! I made an effort on Rama's behalf and came here for your sake. I am ṣugreeva's minister. I am the son of wind-god. Know me, as such."

Sloka 74

kuśalī tava kākutsthaḥ sarva śastrabhṛtām varaḥ |
guroḥ ārādhane yukto lakṣmaṇaḥ ca sulakṣaṇaḥ || 5-35-74

74. tava = your; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; varaḥ = excellent; sarvashastrabhṛitām = among all the wielders of bow; kushalī = is safe; lakṣmaṇashcha = Lakshmana too; yuktaḥ = who is engaged in; guroḥ ārādhane = the service of his elder brother; sulakṣaṇaḥ = and having good characteristics; (is also safe).

"You Rama, the excellent man among all the wielders of bow, is safe. Lakshmana, who is engaged in the service of his elder brother and possessing good characteristics, is also safe."

Sloka 75

tasya vīryavato devi bhartuḥ tava hite rataḥ |
aham ekaḥ tu samprāptaḥ sugrīva vacanāt iha || 5-35-75

75. devi = O princess! aham = I; rataḥ = who am interested; hite = in the welfare; tasya = of Rama; tava = your; bhartuḥ = husband; vīryavataḥ = who is valiant; prāptaḥ = came; iha = here; ekaḥ = alone; sugreeva vachanāt = on the command of ṣugreeva.

"O princess! I, who am interested in the welfare of the valiant Rama, your husband, came here alone on the command of Sugreeva."

Sloka 76

mayā iyam asahāyena caratā kāma rūpiṇā |
dakṣiṇā dig anukrāntā tvan mārga vicaya eṣiṇā || 5-35-76

76. mayā = by me; kāma rūpiṇā = who can change my form at will; tvanmārga vichayaiṣiṇā = desirous of finding out your access; asahāyena = without any helper; charatā = wandering alone; iyam = this; dakṣiṇa dik = southern direction; anukrāntā = is obtained.

"I, who can change my form at will, desirous of finding out your access, wandering alone without any helper, came to this southern direction."

Sloka 77

diṣṭyā aham hari sainyānām tvan nāśam anuśocatām |
apaneṣyāmi samtāpam tava abhigama śaṃsanāt || 5-35-77

77. aham apaneṣyāmni = I shall remove; samtāpam = the anguish; harisainyānām = of the army of monkeys; anushochatām = who are lamenting tvannāsham = because of your loss; tava abhigamashamsanāt = by informing about my approaching you; diṣṭyā = by a good fortune!.

"I shall remove the anguish of the army of monkeys who are lamenting because of your disappearance by informing them about my coming close to you because of my good fortune!"

Sloka 78

diṣṭyā hi na mama vyartham devi sāgara langhanam |
prāpsyāmi aham idam diṣṭyā tvat darśana kṛtam yaśaḥ || 5-35-78

78. devi = O princess; diṣṭyā = by good luck; mama = my; sāgare laṇ^ghanam = crossing of ocean; na vyartham = has not gone in vain; aham = I; prāpsyāmi = can get; idam = this; yashaḥ = fame; taddarshana kṛitam = of having seen you; diṣṭyā = by my good luck.

"O princess! By my fortune, my crossing of ocean has not gone in vain. I can get this fame of having seen you because of my good luck."

Sloka 79

rāghavaḥ ca mahāvīryaḥ kṣipram tvām abhipatsyate |
samitra bāndhavam hatvā rāvaṇam rākṣasa adhipam || 5-35-79

79. mahāvīryaḥ = the highly valiant; rāghavashcha = Rama; hatvā = by killing; rāvaṇam = Rama; hatvā = by killing; rāvaṇam = Rama; rākṣasādhipam = the Lord of demons; samitra bāndhavam = along with his friends and relatives; abhi patsyate = and will obtain; tvām = you; kṣipram = soon.

"ṭhe highly valiant Rama, by killing Ravana the Lord of demons along with his friends and relatives, will obtain you soon."

Sloka 80

kaurajo nāma vaidehi girīṇām uttamo giriḥ |
tato gacchati go karṇam parvatam kesarī hariḥ || 5-35-80

80. vaidehi = O ṣeetha!; giriḥ = there is a mountain; mālyānnāma = called ṃalyavan; uttamaḥ = which is the best; girriṇām = among mountains; tataḥ = from there; hariḥ = a monkeys; kesarī = called Kesari; gachchhati = went; paravatam = to another mountain; gokarṇam = called Gokarna.

"O ṣeetha! ṭhere is a mountain called ṃalyavan, which is the best among mountains. From there, a monkeys called Kesari went to another mountain called Gokarna."

Sloka 81

sa ca deva ṛṣibhiḥ dṛṣṭaḥ pitā mama mahākapiḥ |
tīrthe nadī pateḥ puṇye śamba sādanam uddharat || 5-35-81

81. diṣṭaḥ = as commanded; devarṣibhiḥ = by gods and seers; saḥ mahākapi = that great monkey; called Kesari; mama = my; pitā = father; uddharat = killed; shamba sādanam = a demon named ṣhamba sadana; puṇye = at a sacred; tīrthe = shrine; nādīpateḥ = near the sea-shore.

"As prayed by gods and seers, that great monkey called Kesari, my father killed a demon named ṣhambasadana at a sacred shrine near the sea-shore."

Sloka 82

tasya aham hariṇaḥ kṣetre jāto vātena maithili |
hanūmān iti vikhyāto loke svena eva karmaṇā || 5-35-82

82. maithili = O ṣeetha!; aham = I; jātaḥ = am born; kṣetre = in the wife; tasya hariṇaḥ = of that Kesari; the monkey; vātena = by the grace of wind-god; syena karmaṇā eva = by my activity alone; vikhyātaḥ = I am renowned; hanumāniti = as ḥanuman; loke = in this world.

"O ṣeetha! I am born in the womb of the wife of that Kesari the monkey, by the grace of the wind-god. By my activity alone, I am renowned as Hanuma in this world."

Sloka 83

viśvāsārtham tu vaidehi bharturuktā mayā guṇāḥ |
viśvāsa artham tu vaidehi bhartuḥ uktā mayā guṇāḥ || 5-35-83

83. vaidehi = O ṣeetha! guṇāḥ = the qualities; bhartuḥ = of Rama; your husband; uktāḥ = were uttered; mayā = by me; vishvāsārtham = to cause trust in you; anaghe devi = O the faultless princess!; rāghavaḥ = Rama; nayitā = will take; tvām = you; itaḥ = from here; achirāt = within a short time.

"O ṣeetha! I described the qualities of Rama, your husband, to cause trust in you. O the faultless princess! Rama will take you from here within a short time."

Sloka 84

evam viśvāsitā sītā hetubhiḥ śoka karśitā |
upapannaiḥ abhijñānaiḥ dūtam tam avagacchati || 5-35-84

84. evam = thus; vishvāsitā = inspired with confidence; hetubhiḥ = by logical reasons; sīta = ṣeetha; shokakarshitā = emaciated with grief; avagachchhati = could recognise; tam = him; dūtam = as a messenger; upapannaiḥ = by adequate; abhiJJnānaiḥ = clues serving as proof.

ṭhus inspired by confidence because of the above reasons, ṣeetha emaciated with grief, by adequate clues served as proof.

Sloka 85

atulam ca gatā harṣam praharṣeṇa tu jānakī |
netrābhyām vakra pakṣmābhyām mumoca ānandajam jalam || 5-35-85

85. jānakī = ṣeetha; gatācha = obtained; atulam = unequalled; harṣam = delight; mumocha = (ṣhe) released; jalam = tears; ānandajam = of joy; netrābhyām = from her eyes; vakrapakṣmābhyām = having curved eye lashes; praharṣeṇa = with a thrill of delight.

ṣeetha obtained unequal delight. ṣhe released tears of joy from her eyes having curved eye-lashes, with a thrill of delight.

Sloka 86

cāru tac ca ānanam tasyāḥ tāmra śukla āyata īkṣaṇam |
aśobhata viśāla akṣyā rāhu mukta iva uḍu rāṭ || 5-35-86

86. tat = that; vadanam = countenance; tasyāh vishalākṣyāḥ = of that large eyed ṣeetha; chāru = who is beautiful; tāmrashuklāyatekṣaṇam = whose eyes are red; white and long; ashobhata = is shining; uDurāDiva = like a moon; the king of stars; rāhumuktaḥ = liberated from Rahu the demon.

ṭhe countenance of that large-eyed ṣeetha, who is beautiful, whose eyes are red white and long is shining like a moon, the king of stars liberated from Rahu the demon.

Sloka 87

hanūmantam kapim vyaktam manyate na anyathā iti sā |
atha uvāca hanūmān tām uttaram priya darśanām || 5-35-87

87. sā = ṣeetha; manyate = considered; hanumantam = ḥanuma; vyaktam = clearly; kapim = as a monkey; na = not; anyathā iti = as otherwise; atha = thereafter; hanūmān = ḥanuma; uttaram uvācha = replied; tām = to her; priya darshanām = who is pleasant to the sight.

ṣeetha considered ḥanuma clearly as a monkey and not otherwise. ṭhereafter ḥanuma replied to her who is pleasant to the sight (as follows)

Sloka 88

etatte sarvamākhyātam samāśvasihi maithili |
kim karomi katham vā te rocate pratiyāmyaham || 5-35-88

88. maithili = O ṣeetha!; etat sarvam = All this; ākhyātam = was told; te = to you; samāshvasihi = be consoled indeed!; kim = what; karomi = can I do?; kathamvā = how; rochate = is it agreeable; te = for you?; aham = I; pratiyāmi = am returning.

"O ṣeetha! I told you indeed all this please be consoled. ṭell me what I can do for you and also what is agreeable to you. I am going back now."

Sloka 89

hate asure samyati śamba sādane |
kapi pravīreṇa maharṣi codanāt |
tato asmi vāyu prabhavo hi maithili |
prabhāvataḥ tat pratimaḥ ca vānaraḥ || 5-35-89

89. maithili = O ṣeetha!; maharṣichodanāt = as provoked by the sages; kapipravīṇena = by Kesari; the valiant monkey; asure = a demon; shambasādane = called shambasadana; hate sati = having been killed; samyati = in battle; vānarāḥ asmi = I am monkey; vāyuprabhavaḥ = born through the wind-god; prabhāvataḥ = by his influence; tatpratimaḥ = I am equal to the wind-god.

"O ṣeetha! As provoked by the sages, Kesari, the valiant monkey killed a demon called ṣhambasadana in battle. I am a monkey born through wind-god and through his influence I am equal to the wind-god in strength."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe pañcatriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 35th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic
