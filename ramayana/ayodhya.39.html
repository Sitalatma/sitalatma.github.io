Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 39
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sumantra arranges for a chariot for the travel of Rama, Seetha, and Lakshmana at the orders of Dasharatha. Kingly ornaments are obtained for Seetha. Rama seeks blessings from his mother Kausalya and others too. Kausalya's parting advises to Seetha.


Sloka 1

rāmasya tu vacaḥ śrutvā muni veṣa dharam ca tam |
samīkṣya saha bhāryābhī rājā vigata cetanaḥ || 2-39-1
na enam duhkhena samtaptaḥ pratyavaikṣata rāghavam |
na ca enam abhisamprekṣya pratyabhāṣata durmanāḥ || 2-39-2

1;2. shrutvā = hearing; rāmasya = Rama's; vachaḥ = word; samīkshhyacha = and seeing; tam = him; muniveshhadharam = disguised as hermit; rājā = the king; bhāryābhiḥ saha = with his wives; vigatachetanaḥ = lost their consciousness; samtaptaḥ = consumed; duhkhena = by grief; na pratyavekshhata = he could not behold; evam = thus; rāghavam = Rama; durmanāh = being troubled in mind; na pratyabhāshhata cha = he could not reply; abhisamprekshhya = seeing in the direction of enam = him(Rama).

Hearing Rama's words and seeing him clad in a garb of hermit, king Dasaratha with his wives fell unconscious. Consumed by grief, he could not look towards Rama. Troubled in mind as he was, the king could not speak to Rama by seeing into his face.

Sloka 3

sa muhūrtam iva asamjño duhkhitaḥ ca mahī patiḥ |
vilalāpa mahā bāhū rāmam eva anucintayan || 2-39-3

3. saḥ = mahīpatiḥ = that king; mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; asamJṇa iva = was unconscious; muhūrtam = for a moment; duḥkhitaschcha = felt distressed and; vilalāpa = repented(in various ways) anuchintayan = thinking of rāman eva = Rama alone.

The mighty armed Dasaratha was unconscious for some time, distressed and repented in various ways thinking of Rama alone.

Sloka 4

manye khalu mayā pūrvam vivatsā bahavaḥ kṛtāḥ |
prāṇino hiṃsitā vā api tasmāt idam upasthitam || 2-39-4

4. pūrvam = "previously; bahavaḥ = many people; kṛitāh = were made; vivatsāḥ = separated from their children; mayā = by me; vāpi = or; prāṇinaḥ = living beings; himsitāḥ = were killed; tasmāt; hence; manye khalu = I think; idam = this; upasthitam = has come."

"I think that previously many people were separated from their children by me or in any case many living beings were killed. Hence, this (calamity) has befallen me."

Sloka 5

na tu eva anāgate kāle dehāc cyavati jīvitam |
kaikeyyā kliśyamānasya mṛtyur mama na vidyate || 2-39-5
mo aham pāvaka samkāśam paśyāmi purataḥ sthitam |
vihāya vasane sūkṣme tāpasa āccādam ātmajam || 2-39-6

5;6. jīvitam = life; nachyavati = does not decamp; dehāt = from the body; tveva = surely; anāgate = without arrival; kāle = of time; nṛityuḥ = death; na vidyate = does not claim; mama = me; klishyamānasya = who am being tormented; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; pashyāmi = seeing; ātmajam = my son; pāvakasamkāsham = efflugent as fire; purataḥ sthitam = standing before me; tāpasāchchhādanam = clad in the robes of an ascetic; vihāya = having cast off; sūkshhme = fine; vasane = garments.

"life does not decamp from the body surely without the arrival of time. Death does not claim me, even being tormented by Kaikeyi and eventhough I behold my son, efflugent as fire, standing before me clad in the robes of an ascetic having cast off his fine garments."

Sloka 7

ekasyāḥ khalu kaikeyyāḥ kṛte ayam kliśyate janaḥ |
sva arthe prayatamānāyāḥ saṃśritya nikṛtim tvimām || 2-39-7

7. ayam = janaḥ = all these people; klishyate = are suffering; kṛite = on account; ekasyāḥ = of only; kaikeyyāḥ = Kaikeyi; samshritya = who sought refuge; nikṛitim = in chicanery; prayatamānāyāḥ = with an implicit intent; svārthe = of selfishness.

"All these people are suffering on account of only Kaikeyi, who sought refuge in chincanery, with an implicit intent of selfishness"

Sloka 8

evam uktvā tu vacanam bāṣpeṇa pihita īkṣṇaha |
rāma iti sakṛd eva uktvā vyāhartum na śaśāka ha || 2-39-8

8. uktvā = having uttered; evam = these; vachanam = words; uktvā = and saying; rāmeti = "Oh Rama!"; sakṛideva = only once; na shashāka ha = he was not able; vyāhārtum = to speak; pihitendriyaḥ = his organs of voice being choked; bhāshhpena = by tears.

Having uttered these words and saying "Oh, Rama!" only once, he was not able to speak any more, his organs of voice being choked by tears.

Sloka 9

samjñām tu pratilabhya eva muhūrtāt sa mahī patiḥ |
netrābhyām aśru pūrṇābhyām sumantram idam abravīt || 2-39-9

9. pratilabhyevor = regaining; samJṇām = consciousness; muhūrtāt = after a moment; mahīpatiḥ = the king; netrābhyām = with his eyes; ashrupūrṇābhyām = filled with tears; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; sumantram = to ṣumantra.

Regaining consciousness after a moment, the king, with his eyes filled with tears, spoke these words to Sumantra:

Sloka 10

aupavāhyam ratham yuktvā tvam āyāhi haya uttamaiḥ |
prāpaya enam mahā bhāgam itaḥ jana padāt param || 2-39-10

10. tvam = you; āyāhi = come; ratham = (with a) chariot; oupavāhyam = fit for driving; yuktvā = yoked; hayottamaiḥ = with excellent horses; prāpaya = and take; evam = this; mahābhāgam = highly distinguished prince; param = beyond; itaḥ = this; janapadāt = territory.

"You come with a chariot, fit for driving, yoked with excellent horses and take this highly distinguished prince beyond this territory."

Sloka 11

evam manye guṇavatām guṇānām phalam ucyate |
pitrā mātrā ca yat sādhur vīraḥ nirvāsyate vanam || 2-39-11

11. yat = since; sādhuḥ = a pious; vīraḥ = and a valiant (son); nirvāsyate = is being sent to exile; pitrā = by father; mātrācha = and mother; manye = I think; evam = such; uchate = is said (to be); phalam = the reward; guṇānām = of virtues; guṇavatām = of the virtuous.

"Since a pious and a valiant son is being sent to exile by father and mother, I think such is said to be the reward of virtues of the virtueous."

Sloka 12

rājño vacanam ājñāya sumantraḥ śīghra vikramaḥ |
yojayitvā āyayau tatra ratham aśvaiḥ alamkṛtam || 2-39-12

12. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; āJṇāya = obeying; vachanam = the words; rāJṇa = of the king; shīghra vikramaḥ = walked away quickly; āyayau = and arrived; tatra = there; yojayitvā = fitted; ashvaiḥ = with horses; ratham = a chariot; alakṛitam = duly decorated.

Sumantra obeying the words of the king, walked away quickly and arrived there fitted with horses, a chariot duly decorated.

Sloka 13

tam ratham rāja putrāya sūtaḥ kanaka bhūṣitam |
ācacakṣe anjalim kṛtvā yuktam parama vājibhiḥ || 2-39-13

13. sūtaḥ = the charioteer; āchachakshhe = told; rājaputrāya = the prince; aṇjalim kṛitvā = with joined palms; tam ratham = about that chariot; kanaka bhushhitam = decorated by gold; yuktam = fitted; parama vājibhiḥ = with superb horses.

The charioteer told the prince with joined palms about that chariot decorated by gold, fitted wih superb horses.

Sloka 14

rājā satvaram āhūya vyāpṛtam vitta saṃcaye |
uvāca deśa kālajño niścitam sarvataḥ śuci || 2-39-14

14. deshakālaJṇam = who knew proper place and time; nishchitam = who had a decisive attitude; suhuchim = who was clear; sarvatah = by all means; satvaram = quickl; āhūya = called; vyāpṛitam = an officr; vittasamchaye = in the treasury; uvācha = spoke(as follows)

The king, who knew what should be done at a proper place and time and was clear by all means, quickly called an officer placed in charge of the treasury and spoke in a decisive tone (as follows);-

Sloka 15

vāsāṃsi ca mahā arhāṇi bhūṣaṇāni varāṇi ca |
varṣāṇi etāni samkhyāya vaidehyāḥ kṣipram ānaya || 2-39-15

15. kshhipram = quickly; ānaya = bring; vaidehyāḥ = for ṣeetha; vāsāmsicha = clothings; mahārṇāni = of great worth; bhūshhaṇānicha = and ornaments; varāṇi = of high quality; samkhyāya = considering; etāni = (all) these; varshhāṇi = years (that ṣeetha has to spend in exile).

"Quickly bring for Seetha, clothings of great worth and ornaments of high quality, taking into consideration all these years (that Seetha has to spend in exile)

Sloka 16

nara indreṇa evam uktaḥ tu gatvā kośa gṛham tataḥ |
prāyaccat sarvam āhṛtya sītāyai kṣipram eva tat || 2-39-16

16. tataḥ = after; uktaḥ = being spoken; evam = thus; narendreṇa = by the king; yatvā (he) went; koshagṛiham = to the treasury; āhṛitya = brought; sarvam = all; tat = that; samameva = in a lot; prāyachchhat = (and) gave; sītāyai = to ṣeetha.

After thus spoken by the king, the officer went to the treasury, brought all that in a lot and gave to Seetha.

Sloka 17

sā sujātā sujātāni vaidehī prasthitā vanam |
bhūṣayām āsa gātrāṇi taiḥ vicitraiḥ vibhūṣaṇaiḥ || 2-39-17

17. prasthitā = setting out(as she was); vanam = to the forest; sā vaidehī = that ṣeetha; sujātā = of noble birth; bhūshhayāmāsa = adorned; sujātāni = (her) beautiful; gātrāṇi = limbs; vibhūshhaṇaiḥ = with jewels; vichitraiḥ = which were wonderful.

Setting out as she was to the forest, Seetha of noble birth adorned her beautiful limbs with those wonderful jewels.

Sloka 18

vyarājayata vaidehī veśma tat suvibhūṣitā |
udyataḥ aṃśumataḥ kāle kham prabhā iva vivasvataḥ || 2-39-18

18. vaidehi = ṣeetha; suvibhūshhitā = beautifully adorned; vyarājayata = illumined; tat veshma = that palace; ahamiva = as the sky; kāle = in the morning; prabhā = (by) radiance; vivasvataḥ = of a sun; udyataḥ = rising.

Seetha, beautifully adorned as she was, illumined that palace, as the sky in a morning is illuminated by a rising sun.

Sloka 19

tām bhujābhyām pariṣvajya śvaśrūr vacanam abravīt |
anācarantīm kṛpaṇam mūdhni upāghrāya maithilīm || 2-39-19

19. parishhvajya = embracing; bhujābhyām = in her arms; maithilīm = that princess of ṃithila; anācharantīm = who never behaved; kṛipaṇam = in self-pity; shvashrūḥ = her mother in law(Kausalya); upāghrāya = and smelling; mūrdhni = her head(as a token of affection); abravīt = spoke as follows:

Embracing in her arms that princess of ṃithila, who never behaved in self-pity, and smelling her head(as a token of affection), her mother-in-law(Kausalya) spoke as follows:

Sloka 20

asatyaḥ sarva loke asmin satatam satkṛtāḥ priyaiḥ |
bhartāram na anumanyante vinipāta gatam striyaḥ || 2-39-20

20. asatyaḥ = dishonest; striyaḥ = women; satkṛitāḥ = eventhough adored; satatam = all the time; priyaiḥ = by their husbands; mānumanyate = cease to esteem; bhartāram = their husband; vinipātagatam = who has befallen into evil days; asmin sarvaloke = throughout this world.

"Dishonest women, eventhough adored all the time by their husbands, cease to esteem their husband who has come to pass into evil days, throughout this world."

Sloka 21

eṣa svabhāvo nārīṇāmanubhūya purā sukham |
alpāmapyāpadam prāpya duṣyanti prajahatyapi || 2-39-21

21. anubhūya = having enjoyed; sukham = happiness; purā = in the past; dushhyanti = (they) become spoiled; prajahatyapi = and even desert(their husband) prāpya = on obtaining; alpāmapi = even the least; āpadam = misfortune; eshhaḥ = such; svabhāvaḥ = is the nature; nāsīṇām = of (bad) woman.

"Having enjoyed happiness in the past, they become spoiled and even desert their husband, on obtaining even the least misfortune: such is the nature of (bad) women."

Sloka 22

asatyaśīlā vikṛtā durrgāhyāhṛdayāstathā |
yuvatyaḥ pāpasamkalpāḥ kṣaṇamātrādvirāgiṇaḥ || 2-39-22

22. yuvatyaḥ = young women; pāpa samkalpāḥ = who are evil-minded; asatya shīlāḥ = are untruthful in their disposition; vikṛitāḥ = affected by passion; tathā = and; durgrāhya hṛidayāḥ = their essence; incomprehensible; virāgiṇaḥ = they get aversion; kshhaṇamātrāt = within a moment.

"Young women, who are evil-minded, are untruthful in their disposition, are affected by passion and their essence, incomprehensible. They get aversion within a moment."

Sloka 23

na kulam na kṛtam vidyā na dattam nāpi samgrahaḥ |
strīṇām gṛhṇāti hṛdayamanityahṛdayā hi tāḥ || 2-39-23

23. nakulam = neither a noble descent; na kṛitam = nor an accomplishment; vidyā = nor learning; na dattam = nor a gift; nāpi samgrahaḥ = nor even entertainment; grihṇāti = can capture; hṛidayam = the heart; strīṇām = of women; tāḥ = they are; anitya hṛidayāhi = unstable their heart indeed.

Neither a noble descent, nor an accomplishment, nor learning, nor a gift nor even entertainment can capture the heart of women. They are unstbale of their heart indeed!"

Sloka 24

sādhvīnām hi sthitānām tu śīle satye śrute śame |
strīṇām pavitram paramam patireko viśiṣyate || 2-39-24

24. te = but; strīṇām = established; shīle = in good conduct; satye = truthfulness; shrute = sacred learning; shame = and mental quictness; patiḥ = their husband; ekaḥ = alone; paramam = is the most; pavitram = sacred entity; vishishhyate = the best of all.

"But, for women established in good conduct, truthfulness, sacred learning and mental quietness, their husband alone is the most sacred entity, the best of all."

Sloka 25

sa tvayā na avamantavyaḥ putraḥ pravrājitaḥ mama |
tava daivatam astu eṣa nirdhanaḥ sadhano api vā || 2-39-25

25. mama = my; saḥ putraḥ = this son; pravrājitaḥ = who is sent to forest; nāvamantavyaḥ = should not be despised; tvayā = by you; sadhanopi vā = even if he is with riches or; nirdhanaḥ = without riches; astu eshhaḥ = let him be; diavam = a deity; tava = to you.

"ṃy son who is being sent to the forest should not be despised by you. Even if he is invested with riches or without riches, let him be a deity to you."

Sloka 26

vijñāya vacanam sītā tasyā dharma artha samhitam |
kṛta anjalir uvāca idam śvaśrūm abhimukhe sthitā || 2-39-26

26. viJṇāya = perceiving; tasyaḥ = her; vachanam = words; dharmārthasamhitam = endowed with essence of righteousness; kṛitāṇJalaiḥ = and joining her palms; sītā = ṣeetha; uvācha = replied; shvashrūm = to her mother-in-law; sthitām = standing; abhimukhe = in front of her; idam = as follows:

perceiving her words, endowed with essence of righteousness and joining her palms, Seetha replied to her mother-in-law standing in front of her, as follows:-

Sloka 27

kariṣye sarvam eva aham āryā yad anuśāsti mām |
abhijñā asmi yathā bhartur vartitavyam śrutam ca me || 2-39-27

27. aham = i; karishhye = shall do; sarvameva = just all; yathā = as; āryā = your venerable self; anushāshi = instructs; mām = me; abhiJṇā = I know; yathā = how; vartitavyam = it is to be behaved; bhartuḥ = towards my husband; asmi = I; me = for me; shrutamcha = have also heard(about it)

"I shall do just all that, as your venerable self instructs me. I know how I should behave towards my husband. I have also heard about it earlier."

Sloka 28

na mām asaj janena āryā samānayitum arhati |
dharmāt vicalitum na aham alam candrāt iva prabhā || 2-39-28

28. āryā = your venerable self; na arhati = is not worthy; samānayitum = of equating; mām = me; asjjanena = with evil women; nālam = I am unable; vichalitum = to deviate; dharmāt = from virtue; chandrāmiva = even as moon; prabhā = the moonlight.

"Your venerable self is not worthy of equating me with evil women. I am unable to deviate from virtue, even as a moon from the moonlight"

Sloka 29

na atantrī vādyate vīṇā na acakraḥ vartate rathaḥ |
na apatiḥ sukham edhate yā syāt api śata ātmajā || 2-39-29

29. vīṇā = Vina(Indian lute); na vādyate = does not resonate; atantrī = without chords; rathaḥ = chariot; na vartate = does not move; achakraḥ = without wheels; yā = which woman; apatiḥ = without husband; syādapi = even perhaps; shatātmajā = belssed with hundred children; na edheta = will not live in comfor; sukham = happily.

"Vina (Indian lute) does not resonate without chords. chariot does not move without wheels. Now can a wife bereft of her husband, even if blessed with hundred children, will not lie happily in comfort."

Sloka 30

mitam dadāti hi pitā mitam mātā mitam sutaḥ |
amitasya hi dātāram bhartāram kā na pūjayet || 2-39-30

30. pitā = "A father; dadātihi = indeed gives; mitam = a limited extent; mātā = A mother; mitam = in a limited measure; sutah = A son; mitam = in moderation; kā = which woman; na pūjayet = would not adore; bhartāram = her husband; dātāram = the bestower; amitasya = of unlimitedness."

"A father indeed gives a limited extent, a mother in a limited measure and a son in moderation. Which woman, then, would not adore her husband, the bestower of unlimitedness!"

Sloka 31

sā aham evam gatā śreṣṭhā śruta dharma para avarā |
ārye kim avamanyeyam strīṇām bhartā hi daivatam || 2-39-31

31. shruta dharma varāvarā = "having heard about the special and ordinary duties(of a wife); shreshhṭhā = from the most excellent women; kim = how; sā aham = can I; evam gatā = despise(my husband?); ārye = Oh; venerable self! bhartā = husband; daivatam hi = is indeed a deity; strīṇām = to women."

"Having heard about the special and ordinary duties of a wife from the most excellent women, how can I, as such, despise my husband, Oh venerable lady? Husband is indeed a deity to women."

Sloka 32

sītāyā vacanam śrutvā kausalyā hṛdayam gamam |
śuddha sattvā mumoca aśru sahasā duhkha harṣajam || 2-39-32

32. shrutvā = hearing; sītāyāḥ = ṣeetha's vachanam = words; hṛidayaṇgamam = which touched her heart; kausalyā = Kausalya; shuddhasattvā = of pure mind; sahasā = suddenly; mumocha = shed; ashru = tears; duḥkha harshhajam = born of agony and delight.

Hearing Seetha's words, which touched her heart, Kausalya of pure mind suddenly started shedding tears born of agony and delight.

Sloka 33

tām prānjalir abhikramya mātṛ madhye atisatkṛtām |
rāmaḥ parama dharmajño mātaram vākyam abravīt || 2-39-33

33. rāmaḥ = Rama; parama dharmātmā = the most virtuous man; prāṇjaliḥ = joining his palms; abhikramya = approached; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; tām mātaram = to that mother; atisatkṛitām = who is highly respected; mātṛimadhye = among his mothers.

Rama, the most virtuous man, joining his palms, approached and spoke these words to his mother, who is highly respected among all his mothers:

Sloka 34

amba mā duhkhitā bhūs tvam paśya tvam pitaram mama |
kṣayo hi vana vāsasya kṣipram eva bhaviṣyati || 2-39-34

34. amba = Oh; mother! tvam = you; mā bhūḥ = do not; duḥkhitā = grieve; tvam = you; pashya = look after; mama pitaram = my father; kshhayaḥ = the end; vanavāsasya = of exile; bhavishhyati = will come; kshhiprameva = rather soon.

"Oh, mother! You do not grieve. You look after my father. The end of exile will come rather soon."

Sloka 35

suptāyāḥ te gamiṣyanti nava varṣāṇi panca ca |
sā samagram iha prāptam mām drakṣyasi suhṛd vṛtam || 2-39-35

35. nava paṇchacha = fourteen; varshhāṇi = years; gamishhyanti = will elapse; te = (while) you; suptāyāḥ = are asleep; sā = you as such; drakshhyasi = will see; mām = me; samprāptam = duly arrived; iha = here; samagram = in my entire being; suhṛidvṛitam = surrounded by my well- wishers.

"Fourteen years will elapse, while you are asleep. you as such, will see me, duly arrived here in my entire being, surrounded by my well-wishers."

Sloka 36

etāvad abhinīta artham uktvā sa jananīm vacaḥ |
trayaḥ śata śata ardhā hi dadarśa avekṣya mātaraḥ || 2-39-36

36. uktvā = uttering; vachaḥ = words; etāvat = of such quality; abhinītārtham = end appropriate meaning; āvekshhya = refelected again; dadarsha = (and) gazed at; trayaḥ shata shatārdhāḥ = his three hundred fifty; mātaraḥ cha = mothers also.

Uttering the aforesaid words of such quality and appropriate meaning to his mother, Rama thought once again and gazed at his three hundred and fifty step-mothers.

Sloka 37

tāḥ ca api sa tathaiva ārtā mātṛṛr daśaratha ātmajaḥ |
dharma yuktam idam vākyam nijagāda kṛta anjaliḥ || 2-39-37

37. saḥ = that; dasharathātmajaḥ = Rama; kṛitāṇjaliḥ = joined his palms; tāḥ = to those; mātṛīrapi = mothers also; ārtāḥ = who were distressed; tathaiva = in the same way; nijagāda = and spoke; idam vākyam = these words; dharmayuktam = which were in consonance with righteousness.

Rama joined his palms and saluted those step mothers also, who were too distressed in the same way and spoke these words which were in consonance with the spirit of righteousness.

Sloka 38

samvāsāt paruṣam kiṃcit ajñānāt vā api yat kṛtam |
tan me samanujānīta sarvāḥ ca āmantrayāmi vaḥ || 2-39-38

38. samanujānīta = excuse; me = me; tat = that; yat = for which reson; kiṇchit = a little; parushham = wrong; kṛitam = was done; aJṇānādvā = due to ignorance or; samvāsāt = or because of our living together; āmantrayāmi = I take leave of; sarvāḥ = all; vaḥ = of you.

"Please excuse me, if I have done any mistake either becuase of our living together or due to ignorance on my part. I now take leave of you all."

Sloka 39

vacanam rāghavasyaitaddharmayuktam samāhitam |
śuśruvu stāḥ striyam sarvāḥ śokopahatacetasaḥ || 2-39-39

39. sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ = those; striyaḥ = women; shokopahata chetanaḥ = whose mind was overpowered with grief; shushruvuḥ = heard; etat = these; vachanam = words; dharmayuktam = which were in consonance with righteousnes; samāhitam = the cool submission; rāghavasya = of Rama.

All those women, whose mind was overpowered with grief, heard the aforesaid cool submission of Rama, which was in consonance with righteousness.

Sloka 40

jajñe atha tāsām samnādaḥ krauncīnām iva nihsvanaḥ |
mānava indrasya bhāryāṇām evam vadati rāghave || 2-39-40

40. atha = after; rāghave = Rama; vadati = has spoken; evam = thus; sannādaḥ = an outery; nisvaḥ iva = resembling the wail; krauṇchīnām = of female cranes; jaJṇe = arose; tāsam = (from) those; mānavendrasya bhāryāṇām = wives of Dasaratha.

While Rama was speaking thus, an outery, resembling the wail of female cranes arose from those wives of Dasaratha.

Sloka 41

muraja paṇava megha ghoṣava |
ddaśaratha veśma babhūva yat purā |
vilapita paridevana ākulam |
vyasana gatam tat abhūt suduhkhitam || 2-39-41

41. yat = which; dasharatha veshma = Dasaratha's palace; pūrvā = earlier; murajapaṇavamegha ghoshhavat = was resounding with tomtoms; large drum and ṃeghas(musical instruments which sound like rumbling of clouds); tat = it; abhūt = became; vilapita paridevanākulam = filled with wails and cries; vyasanagatam = fall on evil days.

Dasaratha' s palace, which was earlier resounding with tomtoms, large drums and ṃeghas( musical instruments which sound like rumbling of clouds) was now filled with wails and cries and fallen on evil days.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe eko na catvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 39th chapter of ayodhya Kanda in glorious Valmiki Ramayana, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
