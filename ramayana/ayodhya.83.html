Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 83
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Leaving Ayodhya early next morning, accompanied by family-priests, ministers, archers, elephants, cavalry, artisans, army and citizens and covering a long distance, Bharata arrives at the bank of Ganga River at Sringaberapura ruled by guha and, encamping the army there, breaks journey for offering libations to his deceased father on the bank of the Ganaga and also to give rest to the retinue.

Sloka 1

tataḥ samutthitaḥ kālyam āsthāya syandana uttamam |
prayayau bharataḥ śīghram rāma darśana kānkṣayā || 2-83-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; bharataḥ = Bharata; samutthitaḥ = rose up; kālayam = at day-break; āsthāya = mounted; syandanottamam = on an excellent chariot; prayayau = and procedded; shīghra = quickly; rāmadarshanakāṇkshayā = with a desire to see Rama.

Bharata rose up at day-break, mounted on excellent chariot and went quickly with a desire to see Rama.

Sloka 2

agrataḥ prayayus tasya sarve mantri purodhasaḥ |
adhiruhya hayaiḥ yuktān rathān sūrya ratha upamān || 2-83-2

2. sarve = all; mantra purodhasaḥ = the ministers and priests; adhirūhya = ascended; rathān = chariots; yuktām = yoked; hayaiḥ = with horses; sūrya rathopamān = resembling those of the sun; prayayuḥ = and went; agrataḥ = ahead; tasya = of him.

All the ministers and priests ascended, chariots yoked with horses, resembling those of the sun, and went ahead of Bharata.

Sloka 3

nava nāga sahasrāṇi kalpitāni yathā vidhi |
anvayur bharatam yāntam ikṣvāku kula nandanam || 2-83-3

3. nava nāgasrāṇi = nine thousand elephants; kalpitāni = well-arranged; yathāvidhi = suitably; anvayuḥ = accompanied; yāntam = the moving; bharatam = Bharata; ikshvāku kulanandanam = the joy of Ikshvaku dynasty.

Nine thousand elephants suitably duly arranged escorted Bharata the joy of the Ikshvaku dynasty, on his journey.

Sloka 4

ṣaṣṭhī ratha sahasrāṇi dhanvino vividha āyudhāḥ |
anvayur bharatam yāntam rāja putram yaśasvinam || 2-83-4

4. ṣaṣṭiḥ = sixty; ratha sahasrāṇi = thousand chariots; dhanvinaḥ = men wielding bows; vividhāyudhāḥ = and various kinds of weapon; anvayuḥ = followed; yashasvinam = the illustrious; rājaputram = prince; bharatam = Bharata; yāntam = on his journey.

Sixty thousand chariots filled with archers, furnished with weapons of various kinds, followed the illustrious prince Bharata.

Sloka 5

śatam sahasrāṇi aśvānām samārūḍhāni rāghavam |
anvayur bharatam yāntam rāja putram yaśasvinam || 2-83-5

5. shatam = a hundred; sahasrāṇi = thousad; ashvānām samārūDhāni = cavalry; anvayuḥ = accompanied; yāntam = the moving; bharatam = Bharata; rāghavam = born in Raghu dynasty; satyasandham = who was true to his promise; jitendriyam- and who subdued his senses.

A hundred thousand cavalry accompanied the moving Bharata, born in Raghu dynasty, who was true to his promise and who subdued his senses.

Sloka 6

kaikeyī ca sumitrā ca kausalyā ca yaśasvinī |
rāma ānayana samhṛṣṭā yayur yānena bhāsvatā || 2-83-6

6. kaikeyī cha = Kaikeyi; sumitrācha = ṣumitra; yashashvinī = and the illustrious; kausalyācha = Kausalya; yayuḥ = rode; bhāsvatā yānena = in a resplendent chariot; ramānayana samhṛiṣṭāḥ = delighted as they were at the thought of the return of Rama.

Kaikeyi, Sumitra and the illustrious Kausalya rode in a resplendent chariot, delighted as they were at the thought of Rama's return.

Sloka 7

prayātāḥ ca ārya samghātā rāmam draṣṭum salakṣmaṇam |
tasya eva ca kathāḥ citrāḥ kurvāṇā hṛṣṭa mānasāḥ || 2-83-7

7. ārya samghātāshcha = the whole body of noble men; hṛiṣṭamānasāḥ = with jubiliant hearts; kurvāṇāḥ = narrating; chitrāḥ = marvelous; kathāḥ = stories; tasyaiva = of Rama himself; prayātāḥ = wnt; draṣṭum = to behold; rāmam = Rama; salakshamaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana.

The whole body of noble men with their jubiliant hearts followed Bharata to see Rama again as also Lakshmana, recounting the marvelous exploits of that hero to each other saying:

Sloka 8

megha śyāmam mahā bāhum sthira sattvam dṛḍha vratam |
kadā drakṣyāmahe rāmam jagataḥ śoka nāśanam || 2-83-8

8. kadā = when; drakṣyāmahe = shall we see; mahābāhum = the mighty armed prince; maghashyāmam = of the hue of a dark cloud; sthira sattvam = whose courage is unshakable; dṛiDhavratam = of inflexible purpose; rāmam = Rama; shokanāshanam = who banished sorrow; jagataḥ = from the earth?

"When shall we see the mighty armed prince, of the hue of dark cloud, whose courage is unshakable, of inflexible purpose Rama, who banished sorrow from the earth?"

Sloka 9

dṛṣṭaeva hi naḥ śokam apaneṣyati rāghavaḥ |
tamaḥ sarvasya lokasya samudyann iva bhāskaraḥ || 2-83-9

9. rāghavaḥ = (As soon as) Rama; dṛiṣṭa eva = is seen; apaneṣyatihi = he will indeed remove; naḥ = our; shokam = sorrow; tamaḥ iva = like darkness; sarvasya = of the entire; lokasya = world (is removed); samudyan = at the rising; bhāskaraḥ = sun.

"Beholding Rama, our entire sorrow will indeed be dispelled, as darkness of the entire earth is dispelled at the rising of the sun."

Sloka 10

iti evam kathayantaḥ te samprahṛṣṭāḥ kathāḥ śubhāḥ |
pariṣvajānāḥ ca anyonyam yayur nāgarikāḥ tadā || 2-83-10

10. te = those; nāgarikāḥ janāḥ = citizens; kathayantaḥ = recounting; shubhāḥ = the auspicious; kathāḥ = stories; ityevam = as aforesaid; pariṣvajānāshcha = embracing; anyonyam = each other; samprahṛiṣṭāḥ = joyfully; yayuḥ = went along.

Thus did the citizens of Ayodhya recount happily of Rama's glorious deeds, and they embraced each other as they went along.

Sloka 11

ye ca tatra apare sarve sammatā ye ca naigamāḥ |
rāmam prati yayur hṛṣṭāḥ sarvāḥ prakṛtayaḥ tadā || 2-83-11

11. ye = which; apare = others; sammatāḥ = who were held in high esteem; tatra = there; ye = which; naigamāḥ = merchants; tathā = and; sarvāḥ = all; prakṛitayaḥ = common people; yayuḥ = proceeded; hṛiṣṭāḥ = merrily; rāmam = to join Rama.

Those others who were held in high esteem, merchants and all common people proceeded merrily to join Rama.

māyūrakāḥ krākacikā rocakā vedhakāḥ tathā |
danta kārāḥ sudhā kārāḥ tathā gandha upajīvinaḥ || 2-83-13

snāpaka āccādakā vaidyā dhūpakāḥ śauṇḍikāḥ tathā || 2-83-14

12; 13; 14; 15. ye = which; kechit = some; maṇikārāḥ = lapidaries; shobhanāḥ = good; kumbhakārāshcha = potters; ye = which; shastropajīvanaḥ = those who lived by manufacture of arms; sūtrakarma kṛitashchaiva = carpenters; māyūrakāḥ = those who made various articles from peacock-feathers; krākachikāḥ = sawyers; rochakāḥ = workers in artificial ornaments; vedhakāḥ = those who pierced gems; pearls etc; tathā = and dantakārāḥ = those who made articles of ivory; sudhākarāḥ = those who prepared lime; tathā = and; gandhopajīvanaḥ = those who lived by perfumery; prakhyātāḥ = highly reputed; suvarnakārāḥ = goldsmiths; tathā = and; kambaladhāvakāḥ = weavers of blankets and other wollen goods; snāpakoṣṇodakāḥ = those who provided hot water baths for others; vaidyāḥ = physicians; shauṇ^Dikāḥ = distillers and venders of spirituous liquors; dhūpakāḥ = fumigaters; rajakāḥ = washer-men; tunnavāyāshcha = tailors; grāmaghoṣamahattarāḥ = chiefs of villages and hamlets; shailūṣāḥ = dancers; strībhiḥ saha = with their women-folk; tathā = and; kaivartakāḥ = fisher men; yayuḥ = started on the journey.

Some lapidaries, skilled potters, those who lived by manufacture of arms, carpenters, those who made various articles from peacock-feather, sawyers, workers in artificial ornaments, those who pierced gems, pearls etc, those who made articles of ivory, those who lived by perfumery, highly reputed goldsmiths, weavers of blankets and other wollen goods, those who provided hot-water baths for other, physicians, distillers and vendors of spirituous liquors, fumigators, washer-men, tailors, chiefs of villages and hamlets, dancers with their women-folk and fishermen started on the journey.

Sloka 16

samāhitā vedavido brāhmaṇā vṛtta sammatāḥ |
go rathaiḥ bharatam yāntam anujagmuḥ sahasraśaḥ || 2-83-16

16. sahasrashaḥ = thousands; brāhmaṇāḥ = of Brahmanas; samāhitāḥ = well-composed; vṛitta sammatāḥ = renowned for their virtuous conduct; veda vidaḥ = versed in sacred scriptures; yāntam = riding; gorathaiḥ = on bullock-carts; anujagmuḥ = accompanied; bharatam = Bharata.

Thousands of Brahmanas, well-composed, renowned for their virtuous conduct, versed in sacred scriptures and riding on bullock-carts, accompanied Bharata.

Sloka 17

suveṣāḥ śuddha vasanāḥ tāmra mṛṣṭa anulepanāḥ |
sarve te vividhaiḥ yānaiḥ śanaiḥ bharatam anvayuḥ || 2-83-17

17. te sarve = all of them; suveṣāḥ = attired in; shuddhavasanāḥ = clean and fair raiment; tāmra mṛiṣṭānulepanāḥ = anointed with pure red sandal-paste; vividhaiḥ = mounted on various kinds; yānaiḥ = of vehicles; anvayuḥ = allowed; bharatam = Bharata; shanaiḥ = trangquilly.

All of them, attired in clean and fair raiment, anointed with pure red sandal-paste, mounted on various kinds of vehicles, followed Bharata tranquilly.

Sloka 18

prahṛṣṭa muditā senā sānvayāt kaikayī sutam |
bhrāturānayane yāntam bharatam bhrātṛvatsalam || 2-83-18

18. prahṛiṣṭamuditāḥ = exceedingly pleased and cheerful; sā senā = that army; anvayāt = escorted; kaikeyīsutam = the son of Kaikeyi (Bharata); bhrātṛivatsalam = who had affection for his brother; yāntam = who had set out; sānvayāt = along with hs family; ānayane = to bring; bhrātuḥ = his brother.

Exceedingly pleased and cheerful, that army escorted the son of Kaikeyi- Bharta who was affectionate towards his brother and who had set out along with his family to bring his brother, Rama.

Sloka 19

te gatvā dūramadhvānam ratham yānāśvakuñjaraiḥ |
samāsedustato gaṅgām śṛṅgiberapuram prati || 2-83-19
yatra rāmasakho vīro guho jñātigaṇairvṛtaḥ |
nivasatyapramādena deśam tam paripālayan || 2-83-20

19; 20. te = they; gatvā = having gone; dūram = a long; adhrānam = course; ratha yānāshva = kuṇ^jaraiḥ = on chariots; carts; horses and elephants; samāseduḥ = reached; gaṇgām = the River Ganga; shṛiṇ^giberapuram prati = close to the city of ṣhrigaberapura; yatra = where; vīraḥ = the valiant; guhaḥ = Guha; rāmasakhaḥ = a bosom friend of Rama; vṛitaḥ = along with; j^ṇātigaṇaiḥ = multitude of his relatives; paripālayan = was ruling; tam = that; desham = region; apramādena = carefully.

Having gone for a considerable distance in their chariots, carts, horses and elephants, they reached the River Ganga close to the city of Shringaberapura, where the valiant Guha, a bosom friend of Rama along with multitude of relatives was ruling that region carefully.

Sloka 21

upetya tīram gaṅgāyāścakramākairalaṅkatam |
vyavatiṣṭhata sā senā bharatasya anuyāyinī || 2-83-21

21. sā senā = that army; bharasya = anuyāyinī = which was accompanying Bharata; upetya = arrived at; tīram = the bank; gaṇ^gāyāḥ = of Ganga River; alaṇ^kṛitam = beautified; chakravākaiḥ = with charka birds; vyavātiṣṭhata = and was stationed there.

That army, which was accompanying Bharata, arrived at the bank of Ganga River, looking beautiful as it was with charka* birds and stopped there.

chakra bird: zoological name= Anas casarca.

Sloka 22

nirīkṣya anugatām senām tām ca gangām śiva udakām |
bharataḥ sacivān sarvān abravīd vākya kovidaḥ || 2-83-22

22. nirīkshya = seeing; senām = the army; anugatām = which accompanied him; tām gaṇ^gām cha = and that River Ganga; shivodakām = with propitious waters; bharataḥ = Bharata; vākya kovidaḥ = the master of words; abravīt = spoke; sarvam = to all; sachivān = the ministers (as follows):

Seeing the army behind, which accompanied him and the River Ganga in front with its propitious waters, Bharata who was skillful in words, spoke to all his officers (as follows):

Sloka 23

niveśayata me sainyam abhiprāyeṇa sarvaśaḥ |
viśrāntaḥ pratariṣyāmaḥ śvaidānīm mahā nadīm || 2-83-23

23. abhi prāyeṇa = It is my will; me sainyam = that my army; niveshayata = should take rest; sarvataḥ = by all means; vishrāntāḥ = after taking rest; idāmim = now; pratariṣyāmaḥ = we shall cross; nadīm = the river; shvaḥ = tomorrow.

"It is my will that my army should take rest by all means. When they are refreshed we shall cross the river tomorrow."

Sloka 24

dātum ca tāvad iccāmi svar gatasya mahī pateḥ |
aurdhvadeha nimitta artham avatīrya udakam nadīm || 2-83-24

24. avatīrya = descending into; nadīm = the river; ichchhami = I wish; datum cha = to make offering; udakam = of sacred water; ourdhvadehanimittārtham = for the infinitive spirit relating to the state after death; mahīpateḥ = of the king; svargatasya = who has gone to heaven.

"ṃeanwhile, descending into the river, I desire to make offering of the sacred water as libation for the infinite spirit relating to the state after death, to the king Dasaratha who has gone to heaven."

Sloka 25

tasya evam bruvataḥ amātyāḥ tathā iti uktvā samāhitāḥ |
nyaveśayaṃs tāmḥ candena svena svena pṛthak pṛthak || 2-83-25

25. evam = thus; bruvataḥ = spoken; tasya = by him; amātyāḥ = the officers; uktvā = replied; samāhitāḥ = attentively; tatheti = "Be it so"; pr^ithak = and each; nyaveshayan = encamped; svena svena = their respective; tām = forces; chhandena = in accord with his pleasure.

Hearing these words from Bharata, the officers attentively answered, "Be it so" and each of them encamped their respective forces in accord with his pleasure.

Sloka 26

niveśya gangām anu tām mahā nadīm |
camūm vidhānaiḥ paribarha śobhinīm |
uvāsa rāmasya tadā mahātmano |
vicintayāno bharataḥ nivartanam || 2-83-26

26. niveshya = having encaped; vidhānaiḥ = mentienlously; chamūve = the army; paribarhashobhinīm = which looked lovely with its surrounding equipment (like tents; furnishings etc); gaṇ^gām anu = along Ganga; tām = that; mahānadīm = great river; bharataḥ = Bharata; uvāsa = halted; tadā = then; vichintayānaḥ = reflecting; nivartanam = how to bring back; mahātmanaḥ = the magnanimous; rāmasya = Rama.

Having meticulously arranged for encampment of the army which looked lovely along Ganga that great river with the surrounding equipment (like tunts, furnishings etc), Bharata halted then, reflecting as to how to bring back the magnanimous Rama.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe tryaśītitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 83rd chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
