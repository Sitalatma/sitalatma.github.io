Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 103
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

A ferocious battle ensues between Rama and Ravana, hurling thousands of arrows on each other. Each one intercepts the other's arrows. Rama speaks harsh words to Ravana for having carried away Seetha from Janasthana-forest to Lanka and vows that he will soon dispatch him surely to the world of Death. Rama then pours down streams of arrows on Ravana. Rama more vehemently torments Ravana with his sharp arrows and mystic missiles, till Ravana becomes helplessly weak and confused. Ravana's charioteer carries away Ravana in the chariot, camly and slowly away from the battle-front.

Sloka 1

sa tu tena tadā krodhātkākutsthenārdito raṇe |
rāvaṇaḥ samaraślāghī mahākrodhamupāgamat || 6-103-1

1. saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; samara shlāghī = boasting of his fight; arditaḥ = who was tormented; bhṛisham = much; kākutsthena = by Rama; krodhāt = in fury; tadā = then; upāgamat = flew into; mahākrodhāt = a highly enraged state.

Tormented by Rama in fury, that Ravana then for his part, who was boasting of his fight, flew into a great rage.

Sloka 2

sa dīptanayano roṣāccāpamāyamya vīryavān |
abhyardayatsusaṅkruddho rāghavaṃ paramāhave || 6-103-2
bāṇadhārā sahasraistu sa toyada ivāmbarāt |
rāghavan rāvaṇo bāṇaistaṭākamiva pūrayat || 6-103-3

2; 3. Udyamya = raising; chāpam = his bow; dīptanayanaḥ = his eyes blazing; amarṣhāt = with rage; rāghavam samkruddhaḥ = extremely enraged with Rama as he was; paramāhave = in that great battle; vīraḥ = the valiant Ravana; vīryavān = of prowess; abhyardayat = continued to oppress; pūrayan = by covering; rāghavam = Rama; bāṇadhāvāsahasraiḥ = with thousands of streams of arrows; saḥ toyadaḥ iva = as a rainy cloud; (would fill) taṭākam = a pond; (with thousands of arrow-like torrents; ambarāt = from the sky.

Raising his bow, his eyes blazing with anger, extremely enraged as he was with Rama in that great battle, the valiant Ravana of prowess continued to oppress, by covering Rama with thousands of streams of arrows, as a rainy cloud would fill a pond with thousands of arrow-like torrents from the sky.

Sloka 4

pūritaḥ śarajālena dhanurmuktena sanyuge |
mahāgiririvākampyaḥ kākustho na prakampate || 6-103-4

4. pūritaḥ = covered; sharajālena = by a multitude = of arrows; dhanurmuktena = discharged from the bow; samyuge = in battle; kākutsthaḥ = Rama; na kampate = did not wince; mahāgiririva = like a large mountain; akampyaḥ = which was unshakable.

Covered by a multitude of arrows discharged from Ravana's bow in battle, Rama did not wince, like a large mountain which was unshakable.

Sloka 5

sa śaraiḥ śarajālāni vārayansamare sthitaḥ |
gabhastīniva sūryasya pratijagrāha vīryavān || 6-103-5

5. vīryavān = ṭhe valiant; saḥ = Rama; sthitaḥ = stood; vārayan = interrupting; sharajālāni = the torrents of arrows; sharaiḥ = with his own arrows; samyuge = in the battle-field; pratjagrāha = and accepted them; gabhastīniva = as rays; sūrya = of the sun.

The valiant Rama stood interrupting the torrents of arrows with his own arrows in the battle-field and endured them as rays of the sun.

Sloka 6

tataḥ śarasahasrāṇi kṣiprahasto niśācaraḥ |
nijaghānorasi kruddho rāghavasya mahātmanaḥ || 6-103-6

6. tataḥ = then; kruddhaḥ = the enraged; nishāchaiaḥ = Ravana; kṣhipra hastaḥ = of brisk hand; nijaghāna = struck; shara sahasrāṇi = thousands of arrows; urasi = into the breast; mahātmanaḥ = of the great souled Rama.

Then, the enraged Ravana of brisk hand, struck thousands of arrows into the breast of the great-souled Rama.

Sloka 7

sa śoṇita samādigdhaḥ samare lakṣmaṇāgrajaḥ |
dṛṣṭaḥ phulla ivāraṇye sumahānkiṃśukadrumaḥ || 6-103-7

7. lakṣhmaṇāgrajaḥ = Rama; shoṇita samādigdhaḥ = bathed in blood; samare = in the battle-field; dṛiṣhṭaḥ = appeared; sumahān kimshuka drumaḥ = like a very big Kimshuka tree; phullaḥ = with bloom; araṇye = in a forest.

Rama, bathed in blood in the battle-field, appeared like a very big Kimshuka tree with bloom in a forest.

Sloka 8

śarābhighātasanrabdhaḥ soapi jagrāha sāyakān |
kākutsthaḥ sumahātejā yugāntādityavarcasaḥ || 6-103-8

8. sharābhighāta samrabdhaḥ = enraged at the impact of the arrows; saḥ kākutsthaḥ = that Rama; sumahātejaḥ = of very great splendour; abhijagrāha = took hold of; sāyakān = arrows; yugāntāditya tejasaḥ = which shone like the sun at the time of the dissolution of the world.

Enraged at the impact of the arrows, that Rama of very great splendour , took hold of arrows which shone like the sun at the time of dissolution of the world.

Sloka 9

tatoanyonyan susanrabdhāvubhau tau rāmarāvaṇau |
śarāndhakāre samare nopālakṣayatān tadā || 6-103-9

9. tau ubhau rāma rāvaṇau = Rama and Ravana; susamrabdhau = who were quite enraged; tataḥ anyonyam nopalakṣhayatām = could not thereupon see each other; tadā = at that time; samara = in the battle-field; sharāndhakāre = which was shrouded in darkness by the arrows.

Both Rama and Ravana who were see each other at that time in the battle-field, which was shrouded in darkness by the arrows.

Sloka 10

tataḥ krodhasamāviṣṭo rāmo daśarathātmajaḥ |
uvāca rāvaṇan vīraḥ prahasya paruṣaṃ vacaḥ || 6-103-10

10. prahasya = bursting into laughter; krodhasamāriṣhṭaḥ = though filled with anger; vīraḥ = the valiant; rāmaḥ = Rama; dasharathātmajaḥ = the son of Dasaratha; uvācha = spoke; paruṣham vachaḥ = the following harsh words; rāvaṇam = to Ravana.

Bursting into laughter, though filled with anger, the valiant Rama, the son of Dasaratha spoke the following harsh words to Ravana.

Sloka 11

mama bhāryā janasthānādajñānādrākṣasādhama |
hṛtā te vivaśā yasmāttasmāttvaṃ nāsi vīryavān || 6-103-11

11. rākṣhasādhama = O the worst of demons!; yasmāt = since; mama bhāryā = my wife; hṛitā = was taken away; te = by you; janasthānāt = from Janasthana; vivashā = helpless as she was; ajṇānāt = when I was unaware; tasmāt = hence; tvam = you; nāsi = are not; vīryavān = a person of prowess.

"O the worst of demons! Since you took away my helpless wife without my notice from Janasthana, hence you are not a person of prowess."

Sloka 12

mayā virahitān dīnān vartamānāṃ mahāvane |
vaidehīṃ prasabhan hṛtvā śūroahamiti manyase || 6-103-12

12. hṛitvā = having taken away; prasabham = by force; dīnām = the miserable; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; vartamānām = while she was staying; mahāvane = in the great forest; virahitām = away from me; manyase = am a champion."

"Having taken away by force the miserable Seetha while she was staying in the forest away from me, you think: �I am a champion'."

Sloka 13

strīṣu śūra vināthāsu paradārābhimarśake |
kṛtvā kāpuruṣan karma śūroahamiti manyase || 6-103-13

13. kṛitvā = having done; karma = an act; kāpuruṣham = of cowardly persons; paradārābhimarshaka = of laying your hands on another's wife; shūra = posing as a hero; strīṣhu = in relation to women; vināthāsu = without a protector; manyase = you think; iti = that; aham = I; shūraḥ = am a champion.

"Having done an act of cowardly persons of laying your hands on another's wife, posing as a hero in relation to women without a protector, you think: "I am a champion'."

Sloka 14

bhinnamaryāda nirlajja cāritreṣvanavasthita |
darpānmṛtyumupādāya śūroahamiti manyase || 6-103-14

14. nirlajja = O shameless person; bhinna maryāda = who have broken the bounds of morality; anavasthita = and are unstable; chāritreṣhu = of customs; upādāya = having laid hold; darpāt mṛityum = through vanity of death (in the form of ṣeetha); manasye = you think; iti = that; aham shūraḥ = �I am a champion'.

"O shameless person, who have broken the bounds of morality and are unstable of customs, having laid hold through vanity of death (in the form of Seetha) you think �I am a champion'."

Sloka 15

śūreṇa dhanadabhrātrā balaiḥ samuditena ca |
ślāghanīyan yaśasyan ca kṛtaṃ karma mahattvayā || 6-103-15

15. shlāghanīyam = indeed a praise worthy; mahat = great; yashasyam cha = and glorious; karma = act; kṛitam = has been performed; tvayā = by you shūreṇa dhanada bhrātrā = a valiant brother of Kubera; the god of wealth; balaiḥ samuditena = rich in strength!.

"Indeed a praiseworthy, great and glorious act has been performed by you, a valiant brother of Kubera, the god of wealth, rich in strength!"

Sloka 16

utsekenābhipannasya garhitasyāhitasya ca |
karmaṇaḥ prāpnuhīdānīn tasyādya sumahatphalam || 6-103-16

16. prāpnuhi = reap; sumahat = a stupendous; phalam = fruit; idānīm = now; adya = today; tasya karmaṇaḥ = for that act; abhipannasya = done; utsekena = with vanity; garhitasya = which is contemptible; ahitasya = and noxious.

"Reap now and today the stupendous fruit of that noxious and contemptible act, perpetrated through sheer vanity."

Sloka 17

śūro.ahamiti cātmānamavagacchasi durmate |
naiva lajjāsti te sītān coravadvyapakarṣataḥ || 6-103-17

17. durmate = O evil minded one!; avagachchhasi = you think; iti = that; aham = �I; shūraḥ = am a champion; ātmānām = yourself!; naiva asti lajjā = there is no shame; te = to you; vyapakarṣhataḥ = for having taken away; sītām = ṣeetha; chauravat = like a thief.

"O evil-minded one! You think: �I am a champion' yourself! Shame did not stand in your way at all, for having taken away Seetha like a thief."

Sloka 18

yadi matsaṃnidhau sītā dharṣitā syāttvayā balāt |
bhrātaran tu kharaṃ paśyestadā matsāyakairhataḥ || 6-103-18

18. syādyadi sītā = If ṣeetha were; dharṣhitā = laid hands upon; tvayā = by you; matsammidhau = in my presence; pashyeḥ = you would have surely seen; bhrātaram = your brother; kharam = Khara; tadā = then; hataḥ = when killed; matsāyakaiḥ = with my arrows.

"If Seetha were laid hands upon by you in my presence, you would have surely seen your brother Khara at that very moment when killed with my arrows."

Sloka 19

diṣṭyāsi mama duṣṭātmaṃścakṣurviṣayamāgataḥ |
adya tvān sāyakaistīkṣṇairnayāmi yamasādanam || 6-103-19

19. mandātman = O stupid fellow!; diṣhṭyā = thank heaven!; āgataḥ asi = you have come; chakṣhurviṣhayam = within the range of my sight; nayāmi = I will dispatch; tvām = you; yamasādanam = to the world of Death; tīkṣhṇaiḥ sāyakaiḥ = by my sharp arrows; adya = today.

"Thank heaven, O stupid fellow, you have come within the range of my sight. I will dispatch you to the world of Death, by my sharp arrows, today."

Sloka 20

adya te maccharaiśchinnan śiro jvalitakuṇḍalam |
kravyādā vyapakarṣantu vikīrṇan raṇapānsuṣu || 6-103-20

20. jvalitakuṇ^Dalam = with blazing ear-rings; te shiraḥ = let your head; raṇapāmsuṣhu = lying on the dust-laden battlefield; vyapakarṣhantu = be carried away; kravyādāḥ = by beasts of prey; chhinnam = after having been chopped off; machharaiḥ = by my arrows; adya = today.

"Let your head, having blazing ear-rings lying on the dust-laden battle-field, be carried away by beasts of prey, after being chopped off by my arrows today."

Sloka 21

nipatyorasi gṛdhrāste kṣitau kṣiptasya rāvaṇa |
pibantu rudhiran tarṣādbāṇaśalyāntarothitam || 6-103-21

21. rāvaṇa = O Ravana!; gṛidhrās = (Let) vultures; nipatya = fly down; te urasi = on your breast; kṣhiptasya = when wounded by shooting with my arrows and fallen; kṣhitau = on the ground; pibantu = and drink; rudhiram = the blood; bāṇashalyāntotthitam = oozing out from the orifices caused by the impact of pointed arrow-tips.

"O Ravana! Let vultures fly down on your wounded breast when you have been thrown down on the ground and oozing out from the orifices caused by the impact of my pointed arrow-tips."

Sloka 22

adya madbāṇābhinnasya gatāsoḥ patitasya te |
karṣantvantrāṇi patagā garutmanta ivoragān || 6-103-22

22. te antrāṇi = (let) your bowels; patitasya = when you have fallen down; gatāsoḥ = dead; madbāṇa bhinnasya = when torn out by my arrows; adya = today; karṣhantu = be dragged; patagāḥ garutmanaḥ = by birds and vultures; uragāmiva = as serpents are dragged.

"Let birds (such as crows and vultures) tear out your bowels, as eagles would drag serpents when you fall down dead when pierced by my arrows today."

Sloka 23

ityevan sa vadanvīro rāmaḥ śatrunibarhaṇaḥ |
rākṣasendran samīpasthaṃ śaravarṣairavākirat || 6-103-23

23. ityevam vadan = thus speaking; vīraḥ = the valiant; rāmaḥ = Rama; shatrunibarhaṇaḥ = the annihilator of enemies; avākirat = poured out; sharavarṣhaiḥ = streams of arrows; rākṣhasendram = on Ravana; samīpastham = who was in the vicinity.

Thus speaking, the valiant Rama, the annihilator of enemies, poured out streams of arrows on Ravana, who was in the vicinity.

Sloka 24

babhūva dviguṇan vīryaṃ balaṃ harṣaśca sanyuge |
rāmasyāstrabalan caiva śatrornidhanakāṅkṣiṇaḥ || 6-103-24

24. vīryam = the prowess; balam = the strength; harṣhashcha = the enthusiasm; astrabalam chaiva = and the stamina of the arms; rāmasya = of Rama; babhūva dviguṇam = became two-fold; shatroḥ nidhana kāṇkṣhiṇaḥ = when he longed for the death of his enemy; samyuge = in battle.

The prowess, the strength, the enthusiasm and the stamina of arms of Rama became two-fold, when he longed for the death of his enemy in battle.

Sloka 25

prādurbabhūvurastrāṇi sarvāṇi viditātmanaḥ |
praharṣācca mahātejāḥ śīghrahastataroabhavat || 6-103-25

25. viditātmanaḥ = to Rama; the learned self; sarvāṇi = all kinds; astrāṇi = of mystic missiles; prādurbhūvuḥ = came to light in his mind; praharṣhāt = from his excessive enthusiasm; mahātejāḥ = Rama of extra ordinary energy; abhūt = became; shīghrahasta taraḥ = all the more swift-handed.

All kinds of mystic missiles came to light in the mind of Rama the learned self and in his excessive enthusiasm, Rama of extra-ordinary energy became all the more swift-handed.

Sloka 26

śubhānyetāni cihnāni vijñāyātmagatāni saḥ |
bhūya evārdayadrāmo rāvaṇan rākṣasāntakṛt || 6-103-26

26. vijṇ^āyā = recognizing; etāni = these; shubhāni chihnāni = good omens; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; rākṣhasāntakṛit = the destroyer of demons; ārdayan = tormented; rāvaṇam = Ravana; bhūyatteva = even more vehemently.

Recognizing those good omens, Rama, the destroyer of demons, tormented Ravana even more vehemently.

Sloka 27

harīṇān cāśmanikaraiḥ śaravarṣaiśca rāghavāt |
hanyamāno daśagrīvo vighūrṇahṛdayoabhavat || 6-103-27

27. hanyamānaḥ = struck; ashmanikaraiḥ = by volleys of stones; harīṇām = of the monkeys; sharavarṣhāt = and the showers of arrows; rāghavāt = of Rama; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; abhavat = felt; vighūrṇa hṛidayaḥ = bewildered at heart.

While being struck by volleys of stones hurled by the monkeys and the showers of arrows coming from Rama, Ravana felt bewildered at heart.

Sloka 28

yadā ca śastraṃ nārebhe na vyakarṣaccharāsanam |
nāsya pratyakarodvīryan viklavenāntarātmanā || 6-103-28

28. yadā shastram nārabhe = he could no longer take up weapons; na chakarṣha sharāsanam = nor stretch his bow; na pratyakarot = nor reacted; asya vīryam = to Rama's prowess; viklabena antarātmanā = on account of his mind being confused.

Ravana could no longer take up weapons, nor stretch his bow, nor reacted to Rama's prowess - on account of his mind being confused.

Sloka 29

kṣiptāścāpi śarāstena śastrāṇi vividhāni ca |
na raṇārthāya vartante mṛtyukāleabhivartataḥ || 6-103-29

29. sharāḥ = the arrows; āshu = swiftly; kṣhiptāḥ = hurled; tena = by him; shastrāṇicha = and the mystic missiles; vividhāni = of different kinds (employed by him); na vartante = did not turn to be; raṇāthāya = of any use in battle; mṛityu kālaḥ = (as) his time of death abhyavartata = approached.

As the time of his death approached, the arrows swiftly hurled and the various kinds of missiles employed by Ravana did not turn to be of any use in battle.

Sloka 30

sūtastu rathanetāsya tadavasthaṃ nirīkṣya tam |
śanairyuddhādasambhānto rathan tasyāpavāhayat || 6-103-30

30. sūtastu = the charioteer for his part; rathanetā = driving the chariot; asya = of Ravana; nirīkṣhya = seeing; tam = him; tadavastham; having that plight; asambhrāntaḥ = without getting excited; apavāhayat = carried off; tasya = his; ratham = chariot; shanaiḥ = slowly; yuddhāt = from the battle-front.

Seeing Ravana reduced to such a plight, the charioteer driving the chariot, for his part, without getting excited, calmly and slowly carried off his chariot away from the battle-front.

Sloka 31

rathaṃ ca tasyātha javena sārathi |
rnivārya bhīmaṃ jaladasvanaṃ tadā |
jagāma bhītyā samarānmahīpatiṃ |
nirastavīryaṃ patitaṃ samīkhya || 6-103-31

31. atha = thereupon; samīkṣhya = seeing; mahīpatim = Ravana; his king; nirastavīryam = having hopelessly dropped his energy; patitam = and looked sunken; sārathiḥ = the charioteer; tadā = then; nivārya = diverting; bhīmam ratham = his terrific chariot; javena = with speed; jaladasvanam = which was rumbling like a cloud; jagāma = went; samarāt = from the battle-filed; bhītya = in dismay.

On seeing Ravana, the king sunk down; hopelessly bereft of energy, the charioteer diverting in haste the chariot of Ravana, which was rumbling like a cloud, thereupon sneaked away from the battle-field in dismay.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe tryadhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 104th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
