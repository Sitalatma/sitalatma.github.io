Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 50
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction


ṃonkeys enter Riksha cavity, an unsightly and gloomy one, during their search in southern direction. While groping in that dark they loose time set by Sugreeva. Finally, they see luminosity and extraordinarily built mansions, woodlands, trees and lakes. Thereupon they catch sight of an elderly sainted lady and they all approach her asking after the legend of that cavity.


Sloka 1

saha tārā aṃgadābhyām tu saṃgamya hanumān kapiḥ |
vicinoti ca vindhyasya guhāḥ ca gahanāni ca || 4-50-1

1. kapiḥ hanumān = monkey, ḥanuma; tārā angadābhyām saha = Lt. ṭara, Angada and others, along with; sangamya = associated with; vindhyasya guhāḥ ca gahanāni ca = Vindhya's, caves, also, thick forests, also; vicinoti ca = searched, also.

Associated with Angada, Lt. Tara and others, the monkey Hanuma searched the caves and thick forests of Vindhya Range. [4-50-1]

Sloka 2

siṃha śārdūla juṣṭāḥ ca guhāḥ ca paritaḥ tathā |
viṣameṣu naga indrasya mahā prasravaṇeṣu ca || 4-50-2

2. tathā = then; paritaḥ = all around; simha shārdūla juṣṭāḥ ca = by lions, tigers, jammed in, also; guhāḥ ca = caves, also; naga indrasya = of mountain, king [ṃt. Vindhya]; viṣameṣu = in crags; mahā pra sravaṇeṣu ca = towering, rapids, also; [vicinoti sma = he has searched.]

Hanuma has searched in the caves of that kingly ṃt. Vindhya, which are jammed in with lions and tigers, and even at the towering rapids. [4-50-2, 3a]

Sloka 3

āseduḥ tasya śailasya koṭim dakṣiṇa pascimām |
teṣām tatra eva vasatām sa kālo vyatyavartata || 4-5-3

3. tasya shailasya = that, mountain's; dakshiṇa paschimām koṭim = on south, west, peak; āseduḥ = reached; teṣām tatra eva vasatām = for them, thereat, alone, on staying [overstaying]; sa kālaḥ vyatyavartata = that, time, swiftly elapsed.

They reached the southwest peak of that mountain during their search and the time set by Sugreeva swiftly lapsed while they are overstaying thereat that ṃt. Vindhya alone.

Sloka 4

sa hi deśo duranveṣyo guhā gahanavān mahān |
tatra vāyu sutaḥ sarvam vicinoti sma parvatam || 4-50-4

3b. 4. guhā gahanavān mahān saḥ deshaḥ = with caves, impenetrable forests, extensive one, that place; dur anveṣyaḥ hi = not, searchable, indeed; vāyu sutaḥ = Air's, son [ḥanuma]; tatra sarvam parvatam = there, everywhere, on mountain; vicinoti sma = he is searching.

Though that extensive province is indeed an unsearchable one with its impenetrable caves and forests, yet Hanuma, the son of Air-god, searched everywhere on that mountain. [4-50-3b, 4]

Sloka 5

paraspareṇa rahitā anyonyasya avidūrataḥ |
gajo gavākṣo gavayaḥ śarabho gandhamādanaḥ || 4-50-5
maindaḥ ca dvividaḥ caiva hanumān jāṃbavān api |
aṃgado yuva rājaḥ ca tāraḥ ca vanagocaraḥ || 4-50-6
giri jāla āvṛtān deśān mārgitvā dakṣiṇām diśam |
vicinvantaḥ tataḥ tatra dadṛśuḥ vivṛtam bilam || 4-50-7
durgam ṛkṣa bilam nāma dānavena abhirakṣitam |

5, 6, 7, 8a. gajaḥ gavākṣaḥ gavayaḥ sharabhaḥ gandhamādanaḥ = Gaja, Gavāksha, Gavaya, ṣharabha, Gandhamādana; maindaḥ ca dvividaḥ caiva = ṃainda, also, Dvivida, also thus; hanumān jāmbavān api = ḥanuma, Jāmbavanta, even; yuva rājaḥ angadaḥ ca = crown, prince, Angada, also; vana gocaraḥ tāraḥ ca = forest-mover, Lt. ṭara, also; paraspareṇa rahitā = mutually, without - distancing from one another; anyonyasya = in mutuality; a vi dūrataḥ = not, very, far-flung - one another; giri jāla āvṛitān deshān = mountains, mesh of, encompassed by, province; mārgitvā = having searched; tataḥ dakṣiṇām disham vicinvantaḥ = then, southern, direction, on searching; tatra = there; dānavena abhi rakshitam = by a demon, well-guarded; durgam = impassable one; ṛiksha bilam nāma = Riksha, cavity, known as; vi vṛitam bilam = very, wide, cavity; dadṛishuḥ = they observed.

Without pressing one another closely, yet not distancing from one another largely, Gaja, Gavāksha, Gavaya, Sharabha, Gandhamādana, also thus ṃainda, Dvivida, and Hanuma, and even Jāmbavanta, crown prince Angada, also the forest-mover Lt. Tara have searched that province in southern direction that is encompassed with interlocked mountains, and during their search there they have observed a wide-opened and impassable cavity known as Riksha cavity which is well guarded by a demon called ṃaya. [4-50-5, 6, 7, 8a]

Sloka 8

kṣut pipāsā parītāḥ tu śrāntāḥ tu salila arthinaḥ || 4-50-8
avakīrṇam latā vṛkṣaiḥ dadṛśuḥ te mahā bilam |

8b, 9a. kṣut pipāsā parītāḥ = hunger, thirst, wrapped up in [invaded by]; shrāntāḥ = fatigued; salila arthinaḥ = water, cravers of; te = they - the monkeys; latā vṛikṣaiḥ avakīrṇam = with climbers, trees, enwrapped; mahā bilam dadṛishuḥ = capacious, cavity, they beheld.

Thirst and hunger invading them they are fatigued and craving for water, and such as they are they beheld that capacious cavity which is enwrapped in climbers and trees. [4-50-8, 9a]

Sloka 9

tatra krauncāḥ ca haṃsāḥ ca sārasāḥ ca api niṣkraman || 4-50-9
jala ārdrāḥ cakravākāḥ ca rakta aṃgāḥ padma reṇubhiḥ |

9b,10a. tatra = there; krauncāḥ ca hamsāḥ ca sārasāḥ ca = Kaunca waterfowls, also, swans, also, ṣārasa waterfowls, also; jala ārdrāḥ = by water, drenched; padma reṇubhiḥ = with lotus, pollen; rakta angāḥ reddened, with bodies; cakravākāḥ ca api = Cakravaka waterfowls, also, even; niṣkraman = exiting; [dadṛishuḥ = they saw.]

There they have seen the swans and the waterfowls like Kraunca, Sārasa, and even Cakravaka waterfowls exiting from the cavity with their bodies drenched in water and reddened with the dapples of lotuses' pollen. [4-50-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

tataḥ tat bilam āsādya sugandhi duratikramam || 4-50-10
vismaya vyagra manaso babhūvuḥ vānararṣabhāḥ |

10b, 11a. tataḥ = then; vānara ṛiṣabhāḥ = vanara-s, bullish [best] ones; sugandhi = fragranced [cave]; dur ati kramam = not, to over, step [un-enterable anywise]; tat bilam āsādya = that, cavity, on nearing; vismaya vyagra manasaḥ = with amaze, dismayed, at heart [disheartened with amazement]; babhūvuḥ = they became.

When those best vanara-s have neared that fragrant and impermeable cavity they are disheartened with amazement. [4-50-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11

saṃjāta pariśaṃkāḥ te tat bilam plavaga uttamāḥ || 4-50-11
abhyapadyanta saṃhṛṣṭāḥ tejovanto mahābalāḥ |

11b, 12a. tejovantaḥ = highly resplendent ones; mahā balāḥ = great-mighty ones; such as they are; te = those; plavaga uttamāḥ = fly-jumpers, the best; sanjāta pari shankāḥ = evoked, general, suspicion - a high suspicion is evoked in them; sam hṛiṣṭāḥ = rejoicing; tat bilam = that, to cavity; abhi āpadyanta = towards, draw near.

Though a suspicion in general is evoked in them, as they presumed that cavity to be Naraka, The Hell of Yama, or Pātāla, the Netherworld of Emperor Bali, those highly resplendent and great mighty fly-jumpers have neared it, rejoicing for the availability of water in there. [4-50-11b, 12a]

Sloka 12

nānā sattva samākīrṇām daitya indra nilaya upamam || 4-50-12
durdarśam iva ghoram ca durvigāhyam ca sarvaśaḥ |

12b, 13a. nānā sattva samākīrṇām = diverse, beings, dispersed with; daitya indra nilaya upamam = demon's, emperor's [Emperor Bali,] residence, similar; dur darsham iva = not, to see [unsightly,] like; ghoram = hideous; sarvashaḥ = from anywhere; dur vi gāhyam ca = impossible, verily, to enter [un-enterable cavity,] also.

That hideous cavity is dispersed with diverse beings, similar to the residence of the emperor of demons, namely Emperor Bali, in Pātāla, the Netherworld, and it is unsightly and impermeable from anywhere. [4-50-12b, 13a]

Sloka 13

tataḥ parvata kūṭa ābho hanumān māruta ātmajaḥ || 4-50-13
abravīt vānarān ghorān kāntāra vana kovidaḥ |

13b, 14a. tataḥ = then; parvata kūṭa ābhaḥ = mountain, top, similar in gloss; kāntāra vana kovidaḥ = about inscrutable, forests, a mastermind; māruta ātmajaḥ hanumān = Air-god's, son, ḥanuma; ghorān [sarvān] vānarān abravīt = to formidable ones [or, to all of the,] Vanara-s, spoke to.

The son of Air-god Hanuma whose glossiness is like that of a mountaintop and who is a mastermind in scrutinising every inscrutable forest, then spoke to all of the formidable Vanara-s. [4-50-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

giri jāla āvṛtān deśān mārgitvā dakṣiṇām diśam || 4-50-14
vayam sarve pariśrāṃtā na ca paśyāma maithilīm |

14b, 15a. vayam sarve = we, all; giri jāla āvṛitān deshān = mountains, meshes, enmeshed, places; dakṣiṇām disham = southern, direction; mārgitvā = having searched; pari shrāntā = overly, fatigued; maithilīm = ṃaithili; na ca pashyāma = not, also [yet,] seen.

"We all have searched the southern quarter including those places that are enmeshed with meshes of mountains, and we are overly fatigued, but ṃaithili is unnoticed. [4-50-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

asmāt ca api bilāt haṃsāḥ krauncāḥ ca saha sārasaiḥ || 4-50-15
jala ārdrāḥ cakravākāḥ ca niṣpatanti sma sarvaśaḥ |
nūnam salilavān atra kūpo vā yadi vā hradaḥ || 4-50-16
tathā ca ime bila dvāre snigdhāḥ tiṣṭhanti pādapāḥ |

15b, 16, 17a. asmāt bilāt = from this, cavity; hamsāḥ sārasaiḥ saha = swans, along with, also, ṣārasa waterfowls; krauncāḥ ca = Kraunca birds, also; jala ārdrāḥ cakravākāḥ ca = water, drenched, Cakravaka waterfowls, also; sarvashaḥ niṣpatanti sma = from all over, out falling - coming out, they are; atra = there - in cavity; salilavān = having water; kūpaḥ vā = a wellspring, or; yadi vā hradaḥ = otherwise, or, a pond; nūnam = certainly; shall be there; tathā = like that; ime = these; bila dvāre pādapāḥ = cavity, at doorway, trees are; snigdhāḥ tiṣṭhanti = glisten, they are - they are verdant.

"Swans along with waterfowls like Sārasa, Kruanca, and water drenched Cakravaka birds are coming out of this cavity from all over, and even the trees available at its doorway are verdant. Certainly there must be a wellspring, or otherwise a pond with water." Thus Hanuma spoke to all monkeys. [4-50-15b, 16, 17a]

Sloka 17

iti uktāḥ tat bilam sarve viviśuḥ timira āvṛtam || 4-50-17
acandra sūryam harayo dadṛśū roma harṣaṇam |

17b. 18a. iti uktāḥ = thus, they are spoken [by ḥanuma]; sarve = all of them; timira āvṛitam = by darkness, enfolded in; tat bilam vivishuḥ = that, cavity, they entered; harayaḥ = monkeys; a candra sūryam = devoid of, moon, sun [unilluminated by]; roma harṣaṇam = hair, raising [cavity]; dadṛishū = they saw.

When Hanuma spoke to them thus as a kind of assurance, all those monkeys entered the cavity and found it as a sunless or moonless cavity, enfolded in hair-raising darkness. [4-50-17b, 18a]

Sloka 18

niśamya tasmāt siṃhāḥ ca tān tān ca mṛga pakṣiṇaḥ || 4-50-18
praviṣṭā hari śārdūlā bilam timira āvṛtam |

18b, 19a. hari shārdūlā = monkeys, tigerly ones; tasmāt = therefrom - coming from cavity; simhāḥ ca = lions, also; tān tān ca = those, those - various, also; mṛiga pakshiṇaḥ = animals, birds; nishamya = on seeing; timira āvṛitam = by obscurity, shrouded; bilam praviṣṭāḥ = cavity, they entered.

Those tigerly monkeys have also seen lions and various other animals and birds exiting therefrom, and entered that cavity which is enshrouded in obscurity. [4-50-18b, 19a]

Sloka 19

na teṣām sajjate dṛṣṭiḥ na tejaḥ na parākramaḥ || 4-50-19
vāyoḥ iva gatiḥ teṣām dṛṣṭiḥ tam api vartate |

19b, 20a. teṣām = for them; dṛiṣṭiḥ na sajjate = eyesight, not, fit enough [became unfit, ineffectual / obstructed]; tejaḥ na = their vigour, no; parākramaḥ na = valorousness, no; teṣām gatiḥ = their, permeation; vāyoḥ iva = air [gust,] like; dṛiṣṭiḥ tamasi vartate = eyesight, in gloominess, actuated.

Their eyesight, or their vigour, or their valorousness is rendered ineffectual, and their permeation is just like the directionless air as their eyesight is thwarted in gloominess.

Or

Despite of the gloominess of that cavity, unobstructed is their eyesight, or their vigour or their valorousness, and their permeation is like that of the gust of air as their eyesight is actuated even in darkness. [4-50-19, 20a]

Sloka 20

te praviṣṭāḥ tu vegena tat bilam kapi kuṃjarāḥ || 4-50-20
prakāśam ca abhirāmam ca dadṛśuḥ deśam uttamam |

20b, 21a. tat bilam vegena praviṣṭāḥ tu = that, cavity, speedily, on entering [going further,] but; te kapi kunjarāḥ = those, monkeys, elephantine; prakāsham ca = luminous [phosphorescent, visible in darkness,] also; abhirāmam ca = lovely, also; uttamam desham dadṛishuḥ = a best, place, they saw.

But on their going further into that cavity speedily, those elephantine monkeys beheld a best place that is luminous, as well as lovely. [4-50-20b, 21a]

Sloka 21

tataḥ tasmin bile bhīme nānā pādapa saṃkule || 4-50-21
anyonyam saṃpariṣvajya jagmur yojanam aṃtaram |

21b, 22a. tataḥ = then; anyaḥ anyam sam pariṣvajya = each, to each, tightly, bracing [hand in hand]; bhīme = in awful - cavity; nānā pādapa sankule = various, trees, compacted with; tasmin bile = in that, cavity; yojanam antaram jagmuḥ = a yojana, interstice, advanced further.

In that awful cave that is compacted with various trees, then they advanced further an interstice of yojana distance, hand in hand lest one may miss the other. [4-50-21b, 22a]

Sloka 22

te naṣṭa saṃjñāḥ tṛṣitāḥ saṃbhrāṃtāḥ salila arthinaḥ || 4-50-22
paripetur bile tasmin kaṃcit kālam atandritāḥ |

22b, 23a. naṣṭa sanjñāḥ = lost, track of; tṛiṣitāḥ = thirsty; sam bhrāntāḥ = highly perplexed; salila arthinaḥ = water, cravers; te = those; a tandritāḥ = without, becoming weary - unwearyingly; tasmin bile = in that, cavity; kancit kālam = for some, time [for a good while]; pari petuḥ = over, hopped [hopped over.]

Though they are craving for water, highly perplexed, and lost track of their destination or any waterway, and yet hopeful of their mission they unwearyingly hopped in that cavity for a good while. [4-50-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

te kṛśā dīna vadanāḥ pariśrāntāḥ plavaṃgamāḥ || 4-50-23
ālokam dadṛśuḥ vīrā nirāśā jīvite yadā |

23b, 24a. kṛishā = enervated; dīna vadanāḥ = sad, faced; parishrāntāḥ = overly tired; such as they are; te = they; vīrāḥ plavangamāḥ = brave, fly-jumpers; yadā = when; jīvite nirāshāḥ = of life, despaired; then; ālokam dadṛishuḥ = a seeable thing [luminescence,] they perceived.

Those fly-jumpers are presently enervated and overly tired, and sad faced, and when those brave ones are despaired of their life, just then they perceived a luminescence. [4-50-23b, 24a]

24b, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29a. saumyāḥ = amenable - monkeys; tataḥ = then; tam desham āgamya = at that, province, on arriving; vi timiram vanam = without, shadow, woodland; dīpta vaishvānara prabhān = irradiated, ritual-fire like, in irradiance; kāncanān vṛikṣān = golden, trees; sālān tālān tamālān ca = ṣāla, ṭāla [Palmyra,] ṭamāla [trees], also; punnāgān vanjulān dhavān = Punnāga, Vanjula, Dhava [trees]; campakān = Campaka-s; nāga vṛikṣān ca = ṇāga, trees, also; puṣpitān karṇikārān ca = flowered, Karnikāra, also; kāncanaiḥ citraiḥ stabakaiḥ = golden, amazing, with bunches of lowers; tathā = like that; raktaiḥ kisalayaiḥ āpīDaiḥ = with reddish, leaflets, as towering diadems; latābhiḥ ca = with climbers, also; hema ābharaṇa bhūṣitaiḥ = golden, with jewellery, decorated with; taruṇa āditya sankāshān = tender, sun, similar in shine; vaidūryamaya vedikān = wholly lapis gem's, with pedestals; vapuṣā vibhrājamānān hiraṇmayān pādapān ca = by their bodies, glittering, with golden, trees, also; nīla vaidūrya varṇāḥ ca = blue, lapis lazuli gems, in hue, also; patagaiḥ = with birds; āvṛitāḥ = encircling [flying around]; bāla arka sannibhaiḥ = tender, sun, similar in shine; kāncanaiḥ = golden ones; mahadbhiḥ = broad [with petals]; padmaiḥ vṛitā = with lotuses, encompassed by; padminīḥ = with lotus lakes [or, lotus-creepers,] dadṛishuḥ = they beheld.

Those amenable monkeys on arriving at that province then beheld a shadowless woodland with golden trees which in irradiance is like the irradiated Ritual-fire. They beheld Sāla, Tāla [Palmyra,] Tamāla trees and some flowered trees like Punnāga, Vanjula, Dhava, Campaka, Nāga, also Karnikāra trees. The bunches of their flowers are golden and amazing, leaflets are reddish, and like that the climbers enwreathing at their tops are like their towering diadems and since those trees have golden fruits, they look as though decorated with golden jewellery. Those golden-bodied tress are shining forth like tender sun and their pedestals are completely studded with lapis gems. The birds encircling those trees are in the hue of lapis lazuli gems. The lotus lakes are encompassed with golden lotuses with broad petals, which in shine are similar to tender sun. [4-50-24b, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29a]

Sloka 29

jātarūpamayaiḥ matsyaiḥ mahadbhiḥ ca atha pankajaiḥ || 4-50-29

Then they beheld there the lotus-lakes containing serene waters and stocky lotuses and burly fishes that are golden. [4-50-29b, 30a]

Sloka 30

kāṃcanāni vimānāni rājatāni tathā eva ca || 4-50-30
tapanīya gavākṣāṇi muktā jāla āvṛtāni ca |
haima rājata bhaumāni vaidūrya maṇimanti ca || 4-50-31
dadṛśuḥ tatra harayo gṛha mukhyāni sarvaśaḥ |

30b, 31, 32a. harayaḥ = monkeys; tatra = there; kāncanāni = golden ones; tathā eva ca = like that, also; rājatāni = silver ones; vimānāni = having domes; tapanīya gavākṣāṇi = [ore purified by melting] golden, with windows; muktā jāla āvṛitāni ca = pearly, laceworks, covered by, also; haima rājata bhaumāni = golden, silver, having multi-stories; vaidūrya maṇimanti ca = lapis, gem-studded, also; gṛiha mukhyāni = mansions, choicest ones; sarvashaḥ = everywhere; dadṛishuḥ = they beheld.

There the monkeys beheld choicest mansions everywhere made out of gold and silver, some with golden and some with silver domes, while some with golden and some with silver multi-stories, but all are studded with lapis gems with golden windows covered with laceworks of pearls. [4-50-30b-32a]

Sloka 32

puṣpitān phalino vṛkṣān pravāla maṇi saṃnibhān || 4-50-32
kāṃcana bhramarān caiva madhūni ca samantataḥ |

32b, 33a. puṣpitān phalinaḥ = flowered, fruited; pravāla maṇi sannibhān = red corals, rubies, similar in shine; vṛikṣān = trees; kāncana bhramarān caiva = golden, honeybees, also, thus; madhūni ca = honeys, also; samantataḥ = everywhere; [dadṛishuḥ = they beheld.]

They have also seen everywhere flowered and fruited trees that are similar in shine to red corals and rubies, and golden honeybees, as well as honeys. [4-50-32b, 33a]

Sloka 33

maṇi kāṃcana citrāṇi śayanāni āsanāni ca || 4-50-33

hema rajata kāṃsyānām bhājanānām ca rāśayaḥ || 4-50-34

33b, 34. te = they; maṇi kāncana citrāṇi = gems, gold, amazingly crafted; vividhāni vishālāni = diverse, capacious; shayanāni āsanāni ca = beds, seats, also; hema rajata kānsyānām = of golden, silver, bell-metal; bhājanānām rāshayaḥ = utensils, stacks of; samantataḥ dadṛishuḥ = everywhere, they beheld.

They beheld everywhere diverse and capacious beds and seats that are amazingly crafted with gemstones and gold, and also stacks of utensils made of gold, silver and bell metal. [4-50-33b, 34]

Sloka 35

agurūṇām ca divyānām caṃdanānām ca saṃcayam |
śucīni abhyavahārāṇi mūlāni ca phalāni ca || 4-50-35

35. divyānām agurūṇām = with heavenly, aloe vera substance; candanānām sancayam = sandalwood sticks, stockpiles; shucīni abhyavahārāṇi [abhi ava hṛi] = depurated, [to throw down - swallowable victuals]; mūlāni ca phalāni ca = tubers, also, fruits, also; [dadṛishuḥ te = saw, they.]

They saw the stockpiles of heavenly aloe vera substances and sandalwood sticks and depurated eatables, tubers and fruits. [4-50-35]

Sloka 36

mahā arhāṇi ca pānāni madhūni rasavanti ca |
divyānām ambarāṇām ca mahā arhāṇām ca saṃcayān || 4-50-36
kaṃbalānām ca citrāṇām ajinānām ca saṃcayān |

36, 37a. mahā arhāṇi ca = top-graded, also; pānāni = soft-drinks; rasavanti madhūni ca = luscious, honeys, also; divyānām = divinely; mahā arhāṇām = finest quality; ambarāṇām sancayān = clothing, heaps of; citrāṇām kambalānām ca = exotic ones, blankets, also; ajinānām ca sancayān = deerskins, sheaves, also - they saw.

They have also seen top-grade soft drinks, luscious honeys, heaps of finest quality clothing, exotic blankets and sheaves of deerskins. [4-50-36, 37a]

Sloka 37

tatra tatra vinyastān dīptān vaiśvānara prabhān || 4-50-37
dadṛśuḥ vānarāḥ śubhrān jātarūpasya saṃcayān |

37b, 38a. vānarāḥ = Vanara-s; tatra tatra vinyastān = there, there, kept - set up; dīptān vaishvānara prabhān = glowing, ritual-fire, with glow; shubhrān = undefiled; jātarūpasya sancayān = gold's, mounds of; dadṛishuḥ = they observed.

Vanara-s have also observed mounds of undefiled gold stockpiled here and there glowing with the glow of ritual fire. [4-50-37b, 38a]

Sloka 38

tatra tatra vicinvanto bile tatra mahā prabhāḥ || 4-50-38
dadṛśuḥ vānarāḥ śūrāḥ striyam kāṃcit adūrataḥ |

38b, 39a. mahā prabhāḥ shūrāḥ vānarāḥ = of high, resplendence, valiant ones, Vanara-s; tatra bile = in that, cavity; tatra tatra vicinvantaḥ = there, there, when searching; a dūrataḥ = not, far-off; kāmcit = someone; striyam = a lady; dadṛishuḥ = they perceived.

When searching here and there in that cavity those valiant vanara-s of high resplendence have perceived some lady rather not far from them. [4-50-38b, 39a]

Sloka 39

tām ca te dadṛśuḥ tatra cīra kṛṣṇa ajina ambarām || 4-50-39
tāpasīm niyata āhārām jvalaṃtīm iva tejasā |

39b, 40a. te tatra = they, there; cīra kṛiṣṇa ajina ambarām = jute- cloth, deer, skin, attired in; niyata āhārām = one with controlled, diet; tejasā jvalantīm iva = by her luminescence [of asceticism,] one who is glowing, as with; tāpasīm tām dadṛishuḥ = a sainted lady, at her, they saw.

They saw a sainted lady there who is attired in jute-cloths and who is on a controlled diet and who is like a luminiferous entity by her own luminescence of asceticism. [4-50-38b, 39a]

Sloka 40

vismitā harayaḥ tatra vyavatiṣṭanta sarvaśaḥ |
prapaccha hanumān tatra kā asi tvam kasya vā bilam || 4-50-40

40b, 40c. harayaḥ vismitāḥ = monkeys, are wondered; tatra sarvashaḥ vyavatiṣṭanta [vi ava tiṣṭanta ] = there, all about, stood [still]; tatra hanumān prapaccha = there [in that matter,] ḥanuma, asked about; tvam kā asi = you, who, are; bilam kasya vā = cavity, whose, either.

In wonderment the monkeys stood still all over, and in the matter of her identity Hanuma asked her, "Who are you? Whose cavity is this, either? [4-50-40b, 40c]

Sloka 41

tato hanūmān giri sannikāśaḥ
kṛta aṃjaliḥ tām abhivādya vṛddhām |
papraccha kā tvam bhavanam bilam ca
ratnāni ca imāni vadasva kasya || 4-50-41

41. tataḥ = then; giri sannikāshaḥ = mountain, similar in shine; hanūmān = ḥanuma; kṛita anjaliḥ = making, palm-fold; tām vṛiddhām = her, at aged lady; abhivādya = on reverencing; papraccha = asked; tvam kā = you, who [are]; bhavanam bilam ca = mansion, cavity, and; imāni ratnāni ca = these, jewels, also; kasya = whose; vadasva = [please] speak.

Then Hanuma whose shine is similar to that of a mountain making palm-fold and reverencing that aged lady asked, "Who are you? Whose is this cavity, or the mansion or these jewels? Please speak." [4-50-41]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe paṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 50th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
