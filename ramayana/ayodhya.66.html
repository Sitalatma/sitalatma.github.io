Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 66
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Kausalya reproaches Kaikeyi for her wrong deeds, while weeping profusely for her husband's death. The chamberlains reverently remove Kausalya from the place of the king's body, which they later keep in a trough filled with oil. The ministers and other functionaries return to their abodes, when the night fell in darkness.

Sloka 1

tam agnim iva saṃśāntam ambu hīnam iva arṇavam |
hataprabham iva ādityam svargatham prekṣya bhūmipam || 2-66-1
kausalyā bāṣpa pūrṇa akṣī vividham śoka karśitā |
upagṛhya śiraḥ rājñaḥ kaikeyīm pratyabhāṣata || 2-66-2

1;2. prekshhya = ṣeeing; tam pārthivam = that king; svargastham = who was dead; agnimiva = like fire; samshāntam = which is fully extinguished; ārṇavamiva = like an ocean; ambuhīnam = bereft of water; ādityamiva = like the sun; hataprabham = dimmed in luster; kausalya = Kausalya; upagṛihya = holding; rāG^Yaḥ = the king's; shiraḥ = head; bāshhpapūrṇākshhī = having her eyes filled with tears; shokakarshhitā = who was stricken with grief; vividham = of various sorts; abhāshhata = spoke; kaikeyīm prati = to Kausalya (as follows:

Seeing the aforesaid deceased king, who looked like a fully extinguished fire, a waterless ocean and a lusterless sun, Kausalya holding the king's head on her lap with her eyes full of tears and who was stricken with grief of various sorts, spoke to Kausalya as follows:

Sloka 3

sakāmā bhava kaikeyi bhunkṣva rājyam akaṇṭakam |
tyaktvā rājānam eka agrā nṛśaṃse duṣṭa cāriṇi || 2-66-3

3. Kaikeyi = " O Kaikeyi; nṛishanse = the cruel one; dushhṭachāriṇi = and the evil doer! Bhava = you be there; sakāma = having fulfilled your desires; tyaktvā = deserting; rājānam = the king; bhuṇ^jvā = you can enjoy;rājyam = the kingdom;ekāgrā = undisturbed; akaṇtakam = and with out any hindrance."

"O Kaikeyi the cruel one and the doer of evils! You be there, having fulfilled your desires. Deserting the king, you can enjoy the kingdom undisturbed and without any hindrance.

Sloka 4

vihāya mām gataḥ rāmaḥ bhartā ca svar gataḥ mama |
vipathe sārtha hīnā iva na aham jīvitum utsahe || 2-66-4

4. rāmaḥ = Rama; vihāya = has left; mām = me; gataḥ = and gone (to the forest); mama = ṃy; bharatācha = husband also; svargataḥ = has gone to heaven; aham = I; na utsahe = do not wish; jīvitum = to live; sārtha hīnena = as left behind by a caravan; vipathe = in a wrong road"

"Rama has left me and gone to the forest. ṃy husband too has left for heaven. I do not wish to live, as one left behind by a caravan in a wrong road."

Sloka 5

bhartāram tam parityajya kā strī daivatam ātmanaḥ |
iccej jīvitum anyatra kaikeyyāḥ tyakta dharmaṇaḥ || 2-66-5

5. kāstrī = which woman; ichchhet = would wish; jīvitum = to survive; parityajya = leaving; ātmanaḥ tam bhartāram = her that husband; daivatam = who was divine?; kaikeyyāḥ anyatra = except Kaikeyi; tyakda dharmaṇaḥ = who was kicked off righteousness."

"Which woman would wish to survive, leaving her husband who was divine, except Kaikeyi who kicked off righteousness?"

Sloka 6

na lubdho budhyate doṣān kim pākam iva bhakṣayan |
kubjā nimittam kaikeyyā rāghavāṇān kulam hatam || 2-66-6

6. lubdaḥ = a greedy person; na buddhyate = cannot understand; doshhān = one's faults. Bhakshhayanniva = like one who eats; kimpākam = a forbidden dish; kubjānimiḥan = Due to that hump-backed woman; kulam = the race; rāghavāṇām = of Raghu dynasty; hitam = has been destroyed; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi."

"A greedy person cannot understand one's faults, like one who eats a forbidden dish. Due to that hump- backed woman, Kaikeyi has ruined the race of Raghu dynasty."

Sloka 7

aniyoge niyuktena rājñā rāmam vivāsitam |
sabhāryam janakaḥ śrutvā patitapsyati aham yathā || 2-66-7

7. shrutvā = hearing; rāmam = that Rama; sabhāryam = along with his wife; vivāsitam = have been sent to exile; rāG^yā = by the king; myuktena = coerced; aniyoge = to do an unworthy act; janakaḥ = Janaka; paritapsyati = will profusely lament; aham yathā = as I do."

"Hearing Rama along with his wife have been sent to exile by the king as he was coerced as he was (Kaikeyi) to do an unworthy act, Janaka will profusely lament as I do."

Sloka 8

sa māmanāthām vidhavām nādya jānāti dhārmikaḥ |
rāmaḥ kamala patra akṣo jīva nāśam itaḥ gataḥ || 2-66-8

8. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; dharmikaḥ = the pious man; kamala patrākshhaḥ = having eyes like lotus leaves; gataḥ = having got; nāsham = disappeared; itaḥ = from here; jīvam = even though; surviving; adya = now; na jānāti = does not know; mām = me; anādham = to have become support-less; vidhavām = widow."

"Rama the pious man who is possessed of like lotus leaves, and who has disappeared from here even though living, now does not know me to have because a support -less widow.

Sloka 9

videha rājasya sutā tahā sītā tapasvinī |
duhkhasya anucitā duhkham vane paryudvijiṣyati || 2-66-9

9. tathā = even so; sītā = ṣita; sutā = the daughter; videha rājasya = of king of Videha; tapasvinī = the pitiable lady; anuchitā = and who is unworthy; duḥkhasya = of lamentation; parudvijishhyati = will get frightened; duḥkham = with grief; vane = in the forest."

"Even so, Sita, the daughter of king of Videha, the pitiable lady and who is unworthy of lamentation, will get frightened with grief in the forest.

Sloka 10

nadatām bhīma ghoṣāṇām niśāsu mṛga pakṣiṇām |
niśamya nūnam saṃstrastā rāghavam saṃśrayiṣyati || 2-66-10

10. santraptā = greatly alarmed; nishamya = to hear; bhimaghoshhaṇām = fearfully crying; mṛigapakshhiṇām = wild animals and birds; nadatām = making their noises; nishāsu = in nights; samshrayishhyaḥ = she will take refuge; nūnam = surely; rāghavam = with Rama."

"Greatly alarmed to hear the fearful cries of wild animals and birds making their noises in nights, surely she will cling herself to Rama.

Sloka 11

vṛddhaḥ caiva alpa putraḥ ca vaidehīm anicintayan |
so api śoka samāviṣṭaḥ nanu tyakṣyati jīvitam || 2-66-11

11. saḥapi = Janaka too; vṛiddhashchaiva = who is aged; alpa putrashcha = and who was not having sons; anuchintayan = will be thinking; vaidehīm = of ṣita; shokamāvishhṭaḥ = over whelmed with grief; tyakshhyatinanu = and will surely abandon; jīvitum = his life."

"Janaka too who is aged, and who is not having sons, will be thinking of Sita, over whelmed with grief and will surely abandon his life.

Sloka 12

sāhamadyaiva diṣṭāntam gamiṣyāmi pativratā |
idam śarīramāliṅgya pravekṣyāmi hutāśanam || 2-66-12

12. adyaiva = today itself; sā aham = I too; pativratā = in devotion to my husband; gamishhyāmi = will get; dishhṭāntam = my appointed end; pravakshhyāmi = I shall enter; hutāsham = the fire; āliṇ^gya = duly embracing; idam = this; sharīram = body of my husband"

"Today itself, I too in devotion to my husband, will meet my appointed end. I shall enter the fire, duly embracing this body of my husband.

Sloka 13

tām tataḥ sampariṣvajya vilapantīm tapasvinīm |
vyapaninyuḥ suduhkha ārtām kausalyām vyāvahārikāḥ || 2-66-13

13. vyāvakāri kāḥ = chamberlains; vyapanyuḥ = reverently removed; tataḥ = from that place; tām kausalyā = that Kausalya; tapasvinīm = who was unfortunate; suduḥkhārtām = who was in the excessof her grief; samparishhvajya = and closely embracing her dead husband.

The chamberlains reverently removed from that place that unfortunate Kausalya, who was in excess of her grief and was closely embracing her dead husband.

Sloka 14

taila droṇyām atha amātyāḥ samveśya jagatī patim |
rājñaḥ sarvāṇi atha ādiṣṭāḥ cakruḥ karmāṇi anantaram || 2-66-14

14. atha = thereafter; amātyāḥ = the ministers; ādishhṭāḥ = assigned; samveshya = kept; jagatīpatim = the king; tailadroṇyām = in an oil through; chakruḥ = and did; sarvāṇi = all karmāṇi = the acts; anantaram = that were to be done thereafter; rāṇ^gaḥ = for the king.

The ministers assigned for the job kept the king in an oil trough and did all the acts that were to be done therafter.

Sloka 15

na tu samkalanam rājño vinā putreṇa mantriṇaḥ |
sarvajñāḥ kartum īṣus te tataḥ rakṣanti bhūmipam || 2-66-15

15. mantriṇaḥ = the ministers; sarvaG^yāḥ = who were knowing all such matters; na īshhuḥ = were not willing; kartum = to do; samkalanam = cremation; rāG^yaḥ = for the king; vinā putreṇa = in the absence of his sons; tataḥ = and that is why; rakshhantu = preserved; bhūmipam = the king.

The ministers who were knowing all such matters were not willing to do cremation for the king in the absence of his sons and that is why preserved the king's body .(in an oil through).

Sloka 16

taila droṇyām tu sacivaiḥ śāyitam tam nara adhipam |
hā mṛtaḥ ayam iti jñātvā striyaḥ tāḥ paryadevayan || 2-66-16

16. jJṇātrā = knowing; tam narādhipam = that the king; shāyitam = also laid down; taila droṇyām = in an oil through; tāḥ = those ; striyaḥ = women; puryadevayan = cried; iti = thus: "hā = Alas! Ayam = ḥe; mṛitaḥ = is dead!"

Seeing that the king was laid down in an oil through by the ministers those women in the gynācium cried "Alas! He is dead!"

Sloka 17

bāhūn udyamya kṛpaṇā netra prasravaṇaiḥ mukhaiḥ |
rudantyaḥ śoka samtaptāḥ kṛpaṇam paryadevayan || 2-66-17

17. mukhaiḥ = with their faces; netra prasravaṇaiḥ = with eyes flowing with tears; kṛipaṇāḥ = the miserable women; udyamya = lifted up; bāhūn = their arms; rudantyaḥ = and while weeping; shokasantaptāḥ = stricken with grief; kṛipaṇā = pitiably; paryadevayā = lamented as follows:-

Crying their faces with eyes flowing with tears, the miserable women through up their arms, stricken with grief as they were and pitiably lamented as follows:

Sloka 18

hā mahārāja rāmeṇa satatam priyavādinā |
vihīnāḥ satyasandhena kimartham vijahāsi naḥ || 2-66-18

18. "hā mahārāja = Alas! Great king! Vivīnāḥ = when we are deprived; rāmeṇa = of Rama; priya vādinā = who speaks lovingly; satatam = always; satya sandhena = and keeps up his promise; kimartham = why; vijahāsi = have you left; naḥ = us?"

"Alas! Great king! When we are already deprived of Rama who speaks lovingly and who always keeps up his promise, why have you left us?"

Sloka 19

kaikeyyā duṣṭabhāvāyā rāghaveṇa viyojitāḥ |
katham patighnyā vatsyāmaḥ samīpe vidhavā vayam || 2-66-19

19. viyojitā = far in distance; rāghaveṇa = Rama; katham = how; vayam = shall we; vidhāvaḥ = without our husband; vatyāyaḥ = = live; samīpe = in proximity; kaikeyyāḥ = to Kaikeyi; dushhṭabhāvayāḥ = of perverse nature; patighnāḥ = and who killed her husband?"

"Far in distance from Rama, how shall we without our husband, live in proximity to Kaikeyi of wretched nature, who killed her husband?

Sloka 20

sa hi nāthaḥ sadāsmākam tava ca prabhurātmavān |
vanam rāmo gataḥ śrīmān vihāya nṛpatiśriyam || 2-66-20

20. saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; nāthaḥ = who was a protector; sadā = always; asmākam = to us; tava cha = and to you; prabhuḥ = a capable person; ātmavān = a prudent man; shrīmān = an eminent man; gataḥ hi = has indeed gone; vanam = to the forest; vihāya = living; nṛipatishriyam = the loving kingdom."

"Rama, a protector to you and all of us as well as a capable, prudent and an eminent man, has indeed gone on exile to the forest, leaving this lovely kingdom.

Sloka 21

tvayā tena ca vīreṇa vinā vyasanamohitāḥ |
katham vayam nivatsyāmaḥ kaikeyyā ca vidūṣitāḥ || 2-66-21

21. katham = how; vayam = can we; vidūshhitāḥ = by Kaikeyi; vyasana mohihatā = who is stupefied; by evil passion; nivatsyāmaḥ = live; tvayā vinā = and without that heroic Rama."

"How can we, being disgraced by Kaikeyi, and stupefied by evil passion, live without you and the heroic Rama."

Sloka 22

yayā tu rājā rāmaśca lakṣmaṇaśca mahābalaḥ |
sītayā saha samtyktāḥ sā kamanyam na hāsyati || 2-66-22

22. yayā = by which Kaikeyi; rājā = the king; rāmashcha = Rama; lakshmaṇashcha = Lakshmana; mahābalah = of great strength; along with ṣita; samtyaktāḥ = were abandoned; sā = by her; kam = whom; anyam = else; na hāsyati = cannot be abandoned?"

"Whom else will Kaikeyi by whom have been abandoned the king as well as Rama along with Lakshmana great strength and Sita- not abandon?

Sloka 23

tā bāṣpeṇa ca samvītāḥ śokena vipulena ca |
vyaveṣṭanta nirānandā rāghavasya varastrīyaḥ || 2-66-23

23. bāshhpeṇa = with tears; vipulena = with excessive; shokenacha = agony; rāghavasya = Dasaratha's; tāḥ varastriyaḥ = those excellent wives; sam vī tāh = together in arrow; vyaveshhṭanta = rolled on the ground; nirānandāḥ = sorrowful as they were."

"With tears and excessive agony, those excellent Dasaratha's wives together in a row, rolled on the ground, sorrowful as they were."

Sloka 24

niśā nakṣatra hīnā iva strī iva bhartṛ vivarjitā |
purī na arājata ayodhyā hīnā rājñā mahātmanā || 2-66-24

24. ayodhayā purī = the city of Ayodhya; hīnā = bereft; rāG^yā = of the king Dasaratha; mahātmanā = the high soled; nārāJata = was splendour less; nisheva = as a night; chandra vihīnā = without moon; strīva = and as a woman; bhartṛivihīnā = without husband."

The city of Ayodhya, bereft of the high-soled king Dasaratha, was splendour-less, as a night without moon and as awoman without husband.

Sloka 25

bāṣpa paryākula janā hāhā bhūta kula anganā |
śūnya catvara veśma antā na babhrāja yathā puram || 2-66-25

25. bāshhpa paryākula janā = people disturbed with tears; hāhābhūta kulāṇ^ganā = with virtuous women raising cries of distress; shūnyachatvara veshmantā = its cross roads and entrances of horses looking desolate ; (the city of Ayodhya); na babhrāja = did not shine; yathāpuram = as before.

With people disturbed with tears, with virtuous women raising cries of distress and with its cross -roads and entrances of houses looking desolate, the city of Ayodhya did not shine as before.

Sloka 26

gata prabhā dyaur iva bhāskaram vinā |
vyapeta nakṣatra gaṇā iva śarvarī |
nivṛttacāraḥ sahasā gato raviḥ |
pravṛttacārā rājanī hyupasthitā || 2-66-26

26. narādhipe = (while)the king; gate tu = has gone; tridivam = to heaven; shokāt = due to misery; nṛipāṇga nāsu = the king's wives; mahītalasthāsucha = were lying on the ground; raviḥ = the sun; sahasā = quickly; gataḥ = went away; nivṛiḥachāyāḥ = retreating its movement. rajanī = ṭhe night; upāsthitā hī- = approaches; pravṛiḥachārā = darkness having proceeded to spread.

While the king has gone to heaven due to grief and while the king's wives were lying on the ground , the sun suddenly went away retreating its movement. The night approached, with its darkness having proceeded to spread throughout.

Sloka 27

ṛte tu putrāddahanam mahīpate |
rnarocayante suhṛdaḥ samāgatāḥ |
itīva tasmin śayane nyaveśaya |
nvicintya rājānamacintya darśanam || 2-66-27

27. suṛidaḥ = the friends and relatives; samāgatāḥ = who came there; na rochayante = did not like; mahīpateḥ = the king's; dahanam = cremation; putrātṛite = in the absence of his sons; itivā = ṭhus; vichintya = thinking; nyaveshayan = kept; rājānam = the king; achintya darshanam = of inconceivable sight; tasmin shayane = in that resting place.

The friends and relatives who came there, did not like the king's cremation in the absence of his sons. Thus thinking, they kept the king of inconceivable sight in that resting place(oil- trough)

Sloka 28

gataprabhā dyauriva bhāskaram vinā |
vyapetanakṣatragaṇeva śarvarī |
purī babhāse rahitā maha ātmanā |
na ca asra kaṇṭha ākula mārga catvarā || 2-66-28

28. rahitā = deserted by; mahātmanā = the high-soled Dasaratha; asrakaṇṭhākula mārgachatvāra = with its roads and cross-roads filled with people choked with tears in their throats; purī = the city of Ayodhya; dyouriva = looking like sky; bhāskaram vinā = without sun; sharvarīva = and like night; vyapeta nakshhātragaṇā = with its multitude of stars disappeared ; nababhāse = did not shine; gata prabhā = with its light having gone away.

Deserted by the high-soled Dasaratha, with its roads and cross roads filled with people choked with tears in their throats, the city of Ayodhya, looking like sky without sun and night with its multitude of stars disappeared, did not shine with its light having gone away.

Sloka 29

narāḥ ca nāryaḥ ca sametya samghaśo |
vigarhamāṇā bharatasya mātaram |
tadā nagaryām nara deva samkṣaye |
babhūvur ārtā na ca śarma lebhire || 2-66-29

29. tadā = then; naradeva samkshhaye = while the king was dead; narāshcha = men; nāryashcha = and women; nagaryām = in the city; samatya = converged; samghashah = in groups; vigarhamāṇāḥ = abusing; bharatasya = Bharata's;mātaram = mother; babhūvaḥ = become; ārtāḥ = disturbed; nachalebhiva = and did not obtain; sharma = happiness.

After the king's death, men and women in the city converged in groups, abusing Bharata's mother, became disturbed and did not obtain happiness.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ṣṭṣṣṭitama sargaḥ

Thus completes 66th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
