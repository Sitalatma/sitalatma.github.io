Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 98
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Bharata instructs Shatrughna and Guha along with some troops each to search for Rama and Lakshmana in the forest. Bharata, personally, along with his ministers citizens and priests, proceed on foot to some other side of the forest for the search. He goes through the forest for some time. Climbing up a sala tree, Bharata observes a plume of smoke at a distance and conjectures that place as Rama's hermitage. Bharata quickly proceeds with quick steps, along with Guha, to see Rama.

Sloka 1

niveśya senām tu vibhuḥ padbhyām pādavatām varaḥ |
abhigantum sa kākutstham iyeṣa guru vartakam || 2-98-1

1. saḥ = that Bharata; vibhuḥ = the mighty; varaḥ = and the excellent; pādavatām = among men (those having feet); niveshya = (after) encamping; senām = the army; iyeshha = sought; abhigantum = to approach; kākutthsam = Rama; guruvartakam = who was abiding his; padbhyām = by foot.

After encamping the army, the mighty Bharata the excellent among men, sought to approach on foot, Rama who was complying with the words of his father.

Sloka 2

niviṣṭa mātre sainye tu yathā uddeśam vinītavat |
bharato bhrātaram vākyam śatrughnam idam abravīt || 2-98-2

2. niviṣṭa mātre = ṣoon after encamping; sainye = the army; vinītavat = in a well-behaved manner; yathoddesham = according to the guidelines; bharataḥ = Bharata; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; shatrughnam = to ṣhatrughna.

Soon after encamping the army in a well-behaved manner per the guidelines, Bharata spoke the following words to Shatrughna:

Sloka 3

kṣipram vanam idam saumya nara samghaiḥ samantataḥ |
lubdhaiḥ ca sahitair ebhiḥ tvam anveṣitum arhasi || 2-98-3

3. saumyaḥ = O; gentle brother!; tvam = you; narasaṇ^ghaiḥ = along with a troop of men; lubdhaishcha sahitaiḥ = and along with trackers; arhasi = ought; anveṣitum = to search; samantataḥ = on all sides; idam anam = of this forest.

"O, gentle brother! You, along with a troop of men and trackers, ought to search on all sides of this forest."

Sloka 4

guho jñātisahasreṇa śaracāpāsidhāriṇā |
samanveṣatu kākutthsamasmin parivṛtaḥ svayam || 2-98-4

4. guhaḥ = (Let) Guha; svayam = himself; samanveṣutu = search; kākutthsau = for Rama and Lakshmana; asmin = in this forest; parivṛitaḥ = accompanied; j^ṇāti sahasreṇa = by his multitude of kinsmen; shara chāpāsi dhāriṇā = wielding arrows; bows and swords.

"Let Guha search for Rama and Lakshmana in this forest, himself accompanied by a multitude of his kinsmen, duly wielding their bows, arrows and swords."

Sloka 5

amātyaiḥ saha pauraiśca gurubhiśca dvijātibhiḥ |
vanaṃ sarvaṃ cariṣyāmi padbhyāṃ parivṛtaḥ svayam || 2-98-5

5. svayam = I; too; parivṛitaḥ = encircled; amātyaiḥ saha = along with ministers; parveshcha = citizens; gurubhishcha = priests; dvijātibhiḥ = and Brahmanas; chariṣyāmi = shall move; padbhyām = by foot; sarvam = in the entire; vanam = forest.

"I too, surrounded by the ministers, citizens, priests and Brahmanas; shall personally go round the entire forest on foot.

Sloka 6

yāvan na rāmam drakṣyāmi lakṣmaṇam vā mahā balam |
vaidehīm vā mahā bhāgām na me śāntir bhaviṣyati || 2-98-6

6. shāntiḥ = peace; na bhaviṣyati = will not come; me = to me; yāvat = until which time; na drakṣyāmi = I do not see; rāmam = Rama; mahābalam = the immensely strong; lakṣmaṇam vā = Lakshmana or; mahābhāgam = the illustrious; vaidehīm vā = ṣeetha.

"Peace will not be with me until I do not see Rama or the very mighty Lakshmana or the illustrious Seetha."

Sloka 7

yāvan na candra samkāśam drakṣyāmi śubham ānanam |
bhrātuḥ padma palāśa akṣam na me śāntir bhaviṣyati || 2-98-7

7. shāntiḥ = peace; na bhaviṣyati = will not be; me = with me; yāvat = until which time; na drakṣyāmi = I do not see; shubham = the auspicious; ānanam = face; bhrātuḥ = of my elder brother; chandra samkāsham = resembling the moon; padma palāshakṣam = having eyes like lotus = leaves.

"Peace will not be with me until I do not see the auspicious face of my elder brother, resembling the moon and having eyes bearing resemblance to lotus-leaves.

Sloka 8

yāvan na caraṇau bhrātuḥ pārthiva vyanjana anvitau |
śirasā dhārayiṣyāmi na me śāntir bhaviṣyati || 2-98-8

8. shāntiḥ = peace; na bhaviṣyati = will not be; me = with me; yāvat = until which time; na dhārayiṣyāmi = I do not hold; shirasā = on my head; charaṇau = the soles; bhrātuḥ = of my elder brother; pārthiva vyaṇ^janānvitau = bearubg ritak ubsugbua;

"Peace will nt be with me until I do not hold firmly on my head, the soles of my elder brother, bearing royal insignia.

Sloka 9

yāvan rājye rājya arhaḥ pitṛ paitāmahe sthitaḥ |
abhiṣeka jala klinno na me śāntir bhaviṣyati || 2-98-9

9. shāntiḥ = peace; na bhaviṣyati = will not be; me = with me; yāvat = until which time; rājyārhaḥ = my elder brother eligible for the crown; na sthitaḥ = is not installed; rājya = in the kingdom; pitṛipaitāmahe = which was derived from father and grandfather; abhiṣeka jalaklinnaḥ = and consecrated by sprinkling water (on his head).

"Peace will not come to me until my elder brother, who is eligible for the crown, is not installed in the kingdom of Ayodhya, which was derived from our father and grand father and consecrated by sprinkling water on his head (as a symbol of crowing the kingdom)."

Sloka 10

siddhārthaḥ khalu saumitriryaścandravimalovamam |
mukhaṃ paśyati rāmasya rājīvākṣaṃ mahādyuti || 2-98-10

10. saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; yaḥ = who; pashyati = is seeing; mukham = the face; rāmasya = of Rama; mahā dyuti = having a great luminance; chandra vimalopamam = resembling the bright moon; rājīvakṣam = and having lotus = eyes; siddhārthaḥ khalu = is indeed an accomplished man.

"Lakshmana, who is seeing regularly the face of Rama having a great luminance resembling the bright moon and having lotus eyes, is indeed an accomplished man."

Sloka 11

kṛta kṛtyā mahā bhāgā vaidehī janaka ātmajā |
bhartāram sāgara antāyāḥ pṛthivyā yā anugagcchati || 2-98-11

11. mahābhāgā = the illustrious; vaidehī = ṣeetha; janakātmajā = the daughter of Janaka; yā = who; anugachchhati = is following; (Rama); bhartāram = the Lord; pṛithivyāḥ = of the earth; sāgarāntayāḥ = extending to the sea; kṛita kṛityā = has fulfilled her object.

"Seetha, the illustrious daughter of Janaka, who is following the steps of Rama the Lord of the earth extending to the sea, has fulfilled her object."

Sloka 12

subhagaḥ citra kūṭo asau giri rāja upamo giriḥ |
yasmin vasati kākutsthaḥ kubera iva nandane || 2-98-12

12. asau = that; subhagaḥ = enchanting; chitrakūṭaḥ giriḥ = mountain of chitrakuta; girirājopamaḥ = resembles the King of mountains; yasmin = on which; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; vasati = is residing; kuberaḥ iva = as Kubera the Lord of riches; nanadane = is living in the garden of nandana."

"That enchanting mountain of chitrakuta resembles the King of ṃountains on which Rama is residing, as Kubera the Lord of riches is living in the garden of Nandana."

Sloka 13

kṛta kāryam idam durgam vanam vyāla niṣevitam |
yad adhyāste mahā tejā rāmaḥ śastrabhṛtām varaḥ || 2-98-13

13. kṛitakāryam = Blessed; idam = is this; durgam = dense; vanam = forest; vyāla niṣevitam = inhabited by wild animals; yat = where; mahātejāḥ = the great warrior; varaḥ = the excellent; shastrabhṛitām = among the wielders of rāmaḥ = Rama; adhyāste = dwells;

"Blessed is this dense forest, inhabited by wild animals, where Rama, the great warrior and the excellent man among the wielders of weapons, dwells."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyakāṇḍe aṣṭanavatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 98th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
