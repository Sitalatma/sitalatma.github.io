chapter [Sarga] 2
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter introduces us to the glorious city of Lanka and Hanuma's first encounter with it. The geography around the island where Lanka is located is described herein. The chapter also depicts the initial reaction of Hanuma on seeing the city and his initial thoughts about the feasibility of the task of overcoming the Rakshasas and Ravana to reunite Sri Rama with mother Seetha. As the book progresses, we shall see how Hanuma overcomes these thoughts and moves toward achieving this task.


Sloka 1

1. saḥ = that ḥanuma; mahābalaḥ = one with great strength; atikramya = crossed; anādhrishhyam = the insurmountable; sāgaram = ocean; svasthaḥ = (stayed) healthy (without any physical tiredness); dadarsha ha = and viewed; laṇ^kām = the city of Lanka; sthitām = situated; trikūṭa sikhare = on the peak of ṃount ṭrikuta.

ṭhat ḥanuma with great strength crossed the insurmountable ocean without becoming tired and viewed the city of Lanka located on the peak of ṃount Trikuta.

Sloka 2

Comment : Being showered by flowers is considered auspicious.

Sloka 3

yojanānāṃ śataṃ śrīmāṃstīrtvāpyuttamavikramaḥ |
aniḥsvasan kapistatra na glānimadhigacchati || 5-2-3

3. shrīmān = the glorious; kapiḥ = ḥanuma; uttama vikramaḥ = with the best courage; tīrtvāpi = even though crossing; shatam = a hundred; yojanānām = yojanas; aniḥshvan = was without a sigh; na adhigachhati = (and) did not obtain; glānim = tiredness; tatra = there.

ṭhe glorious ḥanuma with the best courage, even though crossing a hundred yojanas, was without a sigh and did not obtain any tiredness.

Comment : This verse and others clearly mention that Hanuma crossed an ocean of hundred yojanas. At the present time the shore to shore distance between southern tip of India and Northern tip of Sri Lanka is around sixty miles. Even with a measure of 2.5 miles per yojana, hundred yojanas translate to 250 miles. To explain this anomaly, we need to consider that around the time of Ramayana, the distance was lot more than the current sixty miles. This is in sync with the current theories by geologists that the ocean between India and the current Sri Lanka had been replaced by land mass over a period of time. ṃost of the land to the south of current Raghunatha Pura had been covered by landmass in antiquity. Also according to Ramayana, the mountains Trikuta, Lamba and Suvela were to the north of Lanka, but currently the mountains are found in the southern part of Lanka. This suggests that the northern parts of current Lanka were submerged in water during the Ramayana period.

ekāmekaśca paśyeyaṃ rahite janakātmajām || 5-2-38

38. katham = how; kāryam = (can) the task; viditātmanaḥ rāmsya = of ṣri Rama; the knower of self; na vinashyet = not be destroyed; pashyeyam ekashcha = (ḥow) can I alone see; janakātmajām = ṣeetha; ekām = alone; rahite = in a place devoid of people.

"ḥow can the task of ṣri Rama, one who knows self, not be destroyed? ḥow can I alone see ṣeetha alone in a place devoid of people?."

Sloka 39

bhūtāścārthā vipadyante deśakālavirodhitāḥ |
viklabaṃ dūtamāsādya tamaḥ sūryodaye yathā || 5-2-39

39. āsādya = obtaining; dūtam = a messenger; viklabam = with a gloomy mind; deshakālavirodhitāḥ = being against the place and time; bhūtāḥ = ready; arthāḥ = tasks; vipadyante = get harmed; tamaḥ yathā = like darkness (getting destroyed); sūryodaye = at dawn.

"With a mediator who has an unsteady and gloomy mind working against time and place, tasks that are otherwise ready to succeed get harmed, like the darkness that gets destroyed at dawn".

Sloka 40

arthānarthāntare buddhirniścitāpi na śobhate |
ghātayanti hi kāryāṇi dūtāḥ paṇḍitamāninaḥ || 5-2-40

40. nishchitāḥ buddhirapi = even a decided mind; na shobhate = will not shine; arthānarthāntare = (when vacillating) between gain and loss; dūtāḥ = messengers; paṇDitamāninaḥ = who are respected by scholars; ghātayanti hi = will also harm; kāryāṇi = tasks (due to their arrogance).

"Even a decided mind will not shine when thinking about gain and loss. ṃediators who are respected by scholarly people will also harm tasks due to their arrogance."

Sloka 41

na vinaśyetkathaṃ kāryaṃ vaiklabyam na kathaṃ bhavet |
laṅghanaṃ ca samudrasya kathaṃ nu na vṛthā bhavet || 5-2-41

41. katham = how; kāryam = the task (at hand); na vinashyet = will not be harmed; katham = how; vaiklabyam = gloominess; na bhavet = will not be got; katham nu = how; laJṇghanam cha = crossing; samudrasya = of the ocean; na bhavet = will not become; vṛidhā = waste.

"ḥow will the task at hand be not harmed? ḥow will my mind not get gloominess? ḥow will not the crossing of ocean go waste?."

Sloka 42

mayi dṛṣṭe tu rakṣobhī rāmasya viditātmanaḥ |
bhavedvyarthamidaṃ kāryaṃ rāvaṇānarthamicchataḥ || 5-2-42

42. mayi dṛishhṭe tu = If I am seen; rakshobhiḥ = by rakshasas; idam kāryam = this task; rāmasya = of ṣri Rama; viditātmanaḥ = one with famed mind; icchataḥ = who desires; rāvaṇānartham = the slaying of Ravana; bhavet = will become; vyartham = wasteful.

"If I were to be seen by rakshasas, this task of ṣri Rama, with a famed mind, who desires the slaying of Ravana will become wasteful."

Sloka 43

na hi śakyaṃ kvacit sthātumavijñātena rākṣasaiḥ |
api rākṣasarūpeṇa kimutānyena kenacit || 5-2-43

43. na shakyam hi = it is not possible; sthātum = to be; kvachit = in any place; rākshasa rūpeṇāpi = with the form of a rakshasa; aviṇJātena = not being known; rākshaiḥ = by rakshasas; kimuta = what to say; anyena kenachit = (about) any other form.

"It is not possible to be in Lanka even in the form of a rakshasa. What to say about being there in any other form?"

Sloka 44

vāyurapyatra nājñātaścarediti matirmama |
na hyastyaviditaṃ kiṃcidrākṣasānāṃ balīyasām || 5-2-44

44. iti = this; mama matiḥ = is my thought; vāyurapi = even wind; na charet = cannot pass; atra = here; aṇJgnātaḥ = without being known; nāsti hi kiṃchit = there is nothing; aviditam = not known; balīyasām rākshasānām = to the mighty rakshasas.

"ṃy thought goes thus: Even wind cannot pass here without being known. ṭhere is nothing not known to the might rakshasas."

Sloka 45

ihāhaṃ yadi tiṣṭhāmi svena rūpeṇa saṃvṛtaḥ |
vināśamupayāsyāmi bharturarthaśca hīyate || 5-2-45

45. aham tishhṭhāmi yadi = if I stay; iha = here; svena rūpeṇa saṃvṛitaḥ = consisting of my usual form; upayāsyāmi = I can obtain; vināsham = destruction; arthashcha = the task; bhartuḥ = of Lord ṣri Rama; hīyate = will also be harmed.

"If I stay here with my usual form, I can be destroyed. ṭhe task of the Lord will also be harmed."

Sloka 46

tadahaṃ svena rūpeṇa rajanyāṃ hrasvatāṃ gataḥ |
laṅkāmabhipatiṣyāmi rāghavasyārthasiddhaye || 5-2-46

46. tat = for that (reason); aham = I; gataḥ = will obtain; hrasvatām = smallness; svena rūpeṇa = in my usual form; abhipatishhyāmi = and will enter; laṇ^kām = Lanka; rajanyām = at night; artha siddhaye = for the success of the task; rāghavasya = of ṣri Rama.

"For that reason, I will become small in my appearance and will enter Lanka at night for the success of the task of Sri Rama."

Sloka 47

rāvaṇasya purīṃ rātrau praviśya sudurāsadām |
vicinvan bhavanaṃ srvaṃ drakṣyāmi janakātmajām || 5-2-47

47. pravishya = entering; sudurāsadām = the unapproachable; purīm = city; rātrau = at night; vichinvan = searching; sarvam bhavanam = all the mansion; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; drakshyāmi = I can see; janakātmajām = ṣeetha.

"Entering the unapproachable city of Lanka at night and searching all the mansion of Ravana, I can see Seetha."

Sloka 48

iti saṃcintya hanumān sūryasyāstamayaṃ kapiḥ |
ācakāṃkṣe tato vīro vaidehyā draśanotusakaḥ || 5-2-48

48. vīraḥ kapiḥ = the courageous monkey; hanumān = ḥanuma; iti = thus; saṃchintya = thinking; darshanotsukaḥ = interested in discovery; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha; tadā = then; āchakāṃkshe = desired; sūryasya astamayam = the sun set.

ṭhe courageous ḥanuma thus thinking and being interested in discovering ṣeetha, then desired the sun to set.

Sloka 49

sūrye cāstaṃ gate rātrau dehaṃ saṃkṣipya mārutiḥ |
vṛṣadaṃśakamātraḥ san babhūvādbhutadarśanaḥ || 5-2-49

49. sūrye astaṃgate cha = After the sun had set; mārutiḥ = ḥanuma; saṃkshipya = reduced; deham = body; rātrau = at night; vṛishhadaṃshaka mātraḥ san = with a body size equal to that of a cat; babhūva = became; adhuta darshanaḥ = a wonderful sight (to behold).

After the sun had set, ḥanuma reduced ḥis body at night with a size equal to that of a cat and became a wonderful sight to behold.

Sloka 50

pradoṣakāle hanumāṃstūrṇamutplutya vīryavān |
praviveśa purīṃ ramyāṃ suvibhaktamahāpathām || 5-2-50

50. vīryavān = the courageous; hanumān = ḥanuma; tūrṇam = quickly; utplutya = flew; pradoshhakāle = during the evening time; pravivesha = (and) entered; ramyām = beautiful; purīm = city of Lanka; suvibhaktamahāpathām = with well divided main pathways.

ṭhe courageous ḥanuma quickly flew during evening time and entered the beautiful city with well divided main pathways.

Sloka 51

prāsādamālāvitatāṃ stambhaiḥ kāñcanarājataiḥ |
śātakumbhamayairjālairgandharvanagaropamām || 5-2-51
saptabhaumāṣṭabhaumaiśca sa dadarśa mahāpurīm |
talaiḥ sphatikasaṃkīrṇaiḥ kārtasvaravibhūṣitaiḥ || 5-2-52

51; 52. saḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; mahāpurīm = (that) great city; prāsāda mālā vitatām = filled with series of mansions; kāṇ^chana rājataiḥ = (with) golden hued; sthambhaiḥ = pillars; jālaiḥ = and windows; gandharvanagaropamām = equal to the city of Gandharvas; saptabhaumāshhṭabhaumai cha = (consisting of) seven and eight storied houses; talaiḥ = (with) top portions; sphaṭika saṃkīrṇaiḥ = inlaid with crystals; kārtasvaravibhūshhitaiḥ = and decorated with gold.

ḥanuma saw that great city filled with series of mansions, golden hued pillars and window lattices, which was equal to the city of Gandharvas, consisting of seven and eight storied buildings with their top portions studded with crystals and pearls and decorated with gold.

Sloka 53

vaiḍūryamaṇicitraiśca muktājālavibhūṣitaiḥ |
talaiḥ śuśubhire tāni bhavanānyatra rakṣasām || 5-2-53

53. atra = there (in that city of Lanka); bhavanāni = the houses; rakshasām = of rakshasas; shushubhire = shone; talaiḥ = with top portions; vaiDūryamaṇi chitraiḥ = painted by cats-eyes and emeralds; muktājālavibhūshhitaiḥ = decorated by groups of pearls.

ṭhere in that city of Lanka, the houses of rakshasas shone with top portions painted by cats-eyes and emeralds and decorated by groups of pearls.

Sloka 54

kāñcanāni ca citrāṇi toraṇāni ca rakṣasām |
laṅkāmuddyotayāmāsuḥ sarvataḥ samalaṃkṛtām || 5-2-54

54. kāṇ^chanāni = golden; toraṇāni = archways; chitrāṇi = with strange hues; uddyotayāmāsuḥ = illuminated; samalaṃkṛitām = well decorated; laṇ^kām = city of Lanka; sarvataḥ = in all directions.

Golden archways with strange hues illuminated the well decorated city of Lanka in all directions.

Sloka 55

acintyāmadbhutākārāṃ dṛṣṭvā laṅkāṃ mahākapiḥ |
āsīdviṣṇḍo hṛṣṭaśca vaidehyā darśanotsukaḥ || 5-2-55

55. mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; achintyām = the unimaginable; laṇ^kām = city of Lanka; adbhutākārām = with a wonderful appearance; darshanotsukaḥ = eager in discovery; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha; āsīt = became; vishhaṇṇaḥ = sad; hṛishhṭashcha = and happy.

ḥanuma on seeing the unimaginable city of Lanka with a wonderful appearance became sad thinking about the prospect of capturing Lanka and also happy being eager to see Seetha.

Sloka 56

sa pāṇḍurāviddhavimānamālinīṃ |
mahārhajāmbūnadajālatoraṇām |
yaśasvinīṃ rāvaṇabāhupālitāṃ |
kṣapācarairbhimabalaiḥ samāvṛtām || 5-2-56

56. saḥ = ḥanuma (saw Lanka); pāṇDurāviddhavimānamālinīm = with white; closely built series of buildings; mahārhajāmbūnadajālatoraṇām = having golden windows and doorways of great value; yashasvinīm = of great fame; samāvṛitām = consisting; kshapācharaiḥ = of ogres; bhimmabalaiḥ = of great strength; rāvaṇabāhupālitām = ruled by the hands of Ravana.

ḥanuma saw that city of Lanka of great fame, with white, closely built buildings having golden windows and doorways of great value, consisting of ogres of great strength and ruled by the hands of Ravana.

Sloka 57

candro.api sācivyamivāsya kurvaṃ |
stārāgaṇairmadhyagato virājan |
jyotsnāvitānena vitatya loka |
muttiṣṭhate naikasahasraraśmiḥ || 5-2-57

57. naikasahasrarashmiḥ chandro~pi = ṃoon with many thousands of rays; tārā gaṇaiḥ = together with a retinue of stars; madhyagataḥ = obtaining the center of stars; virājan = shining; vitatya = (and) covering; lokam = the earth; jyotsnā vitānena = with canopy of moon-light; uttishhṭhate = rose up; kurvanniva = as though performing; sāchivyam = help; asya = to ḥim (ḥanuma) . saḥ hari pravīraḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; chandram = the moon; udgachchhamānam = rising up; shaṇJkhaprabham = with a glow of a conch; vyavabhāsamānam = shining; kshīramṛiṇāla varṇam = with milky white hue like a lotus-fiber; haṃsamiva = like a swan; poplūyamānam = swimming; sarasi = in a lake.

ṭhe shining moon too rose up with its many thousands of rays, as though performing help to ḥanuma, being at the middle of its retinue of stars and covering the earth with a canopy of light. That Hanuma saw the moon rising up with a glow of a conch shell, shining with a milky white hue of a lotus-fiber, like a swan swimming in a lake.

Sloka 58

śaṅkhaprabhaṃ kṣīramṛṇālavarṇa |
mudgacchamānaṃ vyavabhāsamānam |
dadarśa candraṃ sa haripravīraḥ |
ploplūyamānaṃ sarasīva haṃsam || 5-2-58

58. saḥ haripravīraḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; chandram = the moon; udgachha mānam = who was rising up; shaṇ^kha prabham = with the splendour of a couch-shell; vyavabhāsamānam = shining; kṣhīramṛiṇāla varṇam = in white colour as milk and lotus-fibre; hamsamiva = and looking like a swan; poplūyamānam = swimming; sarasi = in a lake.

ṭhat ḥanuma saw the moon, who was rising up in the sky with the splendour of a couch-shell, shining in white colour as milk or lotus-fibre, and looking like a swan swimming in a lake.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe dvitīyaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 2nd chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
