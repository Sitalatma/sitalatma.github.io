Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 8
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter describes Pushpaka in glorious and amazing detail.


Sloka 1

sa tasya madhye bhavanasya saṃsthitam |
mahadvimānam maṇivajracitritam |
prataptajāmbūnadajālakṛtrimam |
dadarśa vīraḥ pavanātmajaḥ kapiḥ || 5-8-1

1. saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuma; vīraḥ = the courageous one; pavanātmajaḥ = the son of Lord Vayu; dadarsha = saw; mahat vimānam = a great aerial plane; samsthitam = standing; madhye = in the middle; tasya bhavanasya = of that building; maṇivajrachitritam = with a surprising hue due to diamonds and gems; prataptajāmbūnadajālakṛitrimam = decorated by series of refined gold.

That Hanuma the courageous one, the son of Lord Vayu, saw the great aerial plane standing in the middle of that building with a surprising hue, due to diamonds, and gems decorated by series of refined gold.

Sloka 2

tadaprameyāpratikārakṛtrimam |
kṛtam svayam sādhviti viśvakarmaṇāḥ |
divam gatam vāyupathapratiṣṭhitam |
vyarājatādityapathasya lakṣmivat || 5-8-2

2. tat = that plane; vyarājata = shone; lakshmavat = like symbol; ādityapathasya = for solar path; vāyupathapratishhṭhatam = standing in the aerial path; divamgatam = obtaining in the sky; kṛitam = manufactured; svyam vishvakarmaṇā = by Vishvakarma himself; sādhviti = and praised by him; aprameyāpratikārakṛitrimam = one without comparison in beauty.

That plane shone like symbol for solar path standing in the aerial path obtaining the sky. ṃanufactured by Vishvakarma himself and praised by him as one without comparison in beauty.

Sloka 3

na tatra kiṃcinna kṛtam prayatnato |
na tatra kiṃcinna maharharatnavat |
na te viśeṣā niyatāḥ sureṣvapi |
na tatra kiṃcinna mahāviśeṣavat || 5-8-3

3. tatra = In that plane; na kimchit na kṛitam = there is not even a small thing that is not made; prayatnataḥ = with effort; tatra = in that; na kimchit = there is nothing; na mahārharatnavat = without the best diamonds; te visheshhāḥ = those characteristics; naniyatāḥ = definitely are not; sureshhvapi = in Devas also; tatra = in that; kimchit na = there is nothing; na mahāvisheshhavat = that is not great significance.

In that plane there is not even a small thing that is not made with great effort, in that there is nothing without the best diamonds, those characteristics definitely are not in Devas also, in that there is nothing that is not of great significance.

Sloka 4

tapahsamādhānaparākramārjitam |
manaḥsamādhānavicāracāriṇam |
anekasaṃsthānaviṣeṣanirmitam |
tatastatastulyaviśeṣadarśanam || 5-8-4

4. tapḥsamādhānaparākramārjitam = one that has been obtained by austerities and by prowess; manaḥsamādhānavichārachāriṇam = one that moves about by the thoughts of concentrated mind; anekasamsthānavisheshhanirmitam = made from various significant parts; tulyavisheshhanadarshanam = with an appearance of parts of equal significance; tataḥ tataḥ = (collected from) here and there

One that has been obtained by austerities and by prowess, one that moves about by thoughts of concentrated mind, made from various significant parts with an appearance of parts of equal significance, collected from here and there from all over the world.

Sloka 5

viśeṣamālambya viśeṣasaṃsthitam |
vicitrakūṭam bahukūṭamaṇḍitam |
mano.abhirāmam śaradndunirmalam |
vicitrakūṭam śikharam gireryathā || 5-8-5

5. ālambya = obtaining; visheshham = a special construction; visheshhasamsthitam = stood as a special object; vichitrakūṭam = like a mountain with wonderful peaks; bahukūṭamaṇDitam = adorned by many peaks; mano.abhirāmān = attractive to soul; sharadindunirmalam = calm like an autumnal moon; shikaram yadā = like the peak; gireḥ = of a mountain; vichitrakūṭam = with other wonderful small peaks.

Obtaining a special construction as a special object, like a mountain with wonderful peaks adorned by many peaks, attractive to soul, calm like an autumnal moon, like the peak of a mountain with other wonderful small peaks.

Sloka 6

vahanti yam kuṇḍaśobhitānanā |
mahāśanā vyomacarā niśācarāḥ |
vivṛttavidhvastaviśālalocanā |
mahājavā bhūtagaṇāḥ sahasraśaḥ || 5-8-6

6. (ḥanuma saw that aerial plane) yam = which; nishācharāḥ = rakshasas; mahāshanāḥ = who were great eaters; kunDalashobhitānanāḥ = with a face beautified by earrings ;vyomacharāḥ = who roamed around in the sky; sahasrashaḥ būtagaṇāḥ = (and) thousands of genī; nivṛittavidhvastavishālalochanāḥ = with round eyes; crooked eyes and wide eyes; mahājavāḥ = capable of great speed; vahanti = carried.

Hanuma saw that aerial plane which rakshasas who were great eaters, with a face beautified by earnings, who roamed around in the sky and thousands of genī with round eyes, crooked eyes and wide eyes capable of great speed carrying it.

Sloka 7

vasantapuṣpotkaracārudarśanam |
vasantamāsadapi kāntadarśanam |
sa puṣpakam tatra vimānamuttamam |
dadarśa tadvānaravīrasattamaḥ || 5-8-7

7. saḥ = that ḥanuma; vanaravīrasattamaḥ = the best among Vanara warriors; dadarsha = saw; tatra = there; uttamam vimānam = a best plane; pushhpakam = with the name of Pushpaka; vasantapushhpotkarachārudarshanam = with a more beautiful appearance than a group of flowers during spring; kāntadarshanam = a more attractive appearance; vasantamāsādapi = than even the spring season.

That Hanuma the best among Vanara warriors saw there a best plane with the name of Pushpaka with a more beautiful appearance than a group of flowers during spring, a more attractive appearance than even the spring season.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye sundara kāṇḍe aṣṭamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 8th chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
