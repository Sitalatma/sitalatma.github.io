Introduction

Seetha agrees to the instructions given by Anasuya on the duties of a devoted wife. At the end of the conversation with Seetha, Anasuya requests her to ask for a boon, and highly pleased with her, Anasuya bestows heavenly flowers and ornaments on her. Anasuya then requests Seetha to relate to her the story of her marriage with Rama. Then Seetha relates that story to Anasuya in detail.

Sloka 1

sā tu evam uktā vaidehī anasūyān asūyayā |
pratipūjya vaco mandam pravaktum upacakrame || 2-118-1

1. sā vaidehī = that ṣeetha; anasūyā = without any jealousy; evam = thus; uktā = spoken; anasūyayā = by Anasuya; pratipūjya = with full reverence to; vachaḥ = her word; upachakrame = and began; pravaktum = to speak; mandam = gently (as follows):

Anasuya having spoken thus, Seetha without any jealousy, full of reverence for her, addressed her gently as follows:

Sloka 2

na etad āścaryam āryāyā yan mām tvam anubhāṣase |
viditam tu mama apy etad yathā nāryāḥ patir guruḥ || 2-118-2

2. na = It is not; āshcharyan = a surprise; yat tvam = that you; āryāyāḥ = a venerable woman like you; abhibhāṣate = talk; mām = to me; etat = like this; viditam tu = It is known; mamāpi = to me too; etat = this acclamation; yathā = how; patiḥ = a husband; guruḥ = is a respectable person; nāryāḥ = for a woman.

"It is not a surprise that a venerable woman like a you, talk to me like this, for, I know well that a husband is a respectable person for a woman."

Sloka 3

yady apy eṣa bhaved bhartā mama ārye vṛtta varjitaḥ |
advaidham upavartavyaḥ tathā apy eṣa mayā bhavet || 2-118-3

3. ārye = O; venerable woman!; yadyapi = even if; eṣaḥ = this; mama bhartā = my husband; bhavet = be; vṛitta arjitaḥ = without fortune; eṣaḥ = he; tathāpi = even the; advaidham = should unhesitatingly; upachartavyaḥ = be obeyed; mayā = by me.

"O, venerable woman! Even if my husband be without fortune, he should unhesitatingly be obeyed by me."

Sloka 4

kim punar yo guṇa ślāghyaḥ sānukrośo jita indriyaḥ |
sthira anurāgo dharma ātmā mātṛ vartī pitṛ priyaḥ || 2-118-4

4. kim punaḥ = how much more; yaḥ = if he be; gunaḥ shlāghyaḥ = renowned for his virtues; sāmkroshaḥ = compassionate; jitendriyaḥ = master of my heart; sthirānurāgaḥ = who is ever affectionate; dharmātmā = a religious person; priyaḥ = and manifests the tenderness; mātṛivat = of a mother; pitṛivat = and a father (to me).

"How much more, if he be renowned for his virtues, compassionate, master of my heart, who is ever affectionate, a religious person, manifests the tenderness of a mother and a father to me."

Sloka 5

yām vṛttim vartate rāmaḥ kausalyāyām mahā balaḥ |
tām eva nṛpa nārīṇām anyāsām api vartate || 2-118-5

5. mahābalaḥ = the exceedingly strong; rāmaḥ = Rama; vartate = bears; tāmeva = himself; a nyāsām = to all other; nṛipanārīṇāmapi = Queens; yām vartate = as he exercises; vṛittim = his behaviour; kausalyāyām = towards Kausalya.

The exceedingly strong Rama bears himself to all other Queens, as he exercises his behaviour towards his mother, Kausalya."

Sloka 6

sakṛd dṛṣṭāsu api strīṣu nṛpeṇa nṛpa vatsalaḥ |
mātṛvad vartate vīro mānam utsṛjya dharmavit || 2-118-6

6. vīraḥ = the valiant Rama; nṛipavatsalaḥ = who has affection towards Dasaratha; dharmavit = and who knows righteousness; vartate = behaves; mātṛivat = like with a mother; strīṣvapi = towards even the women; dṛiṣṭāsu = who were seen; nṛipaṇa = by Dasaratha; sakṛit = even once; utsṛijya = free from; mānam = all sense of importance.

"The valiant and pious Rama who is devoted to Dasartha and free from all sense of importance treats all those women as his mothers on whom Dasaratha has even once bestowed a single glance."

Sloka 7

āgagcchantyāḥ ca vijanam vanam evam bhaya āvaham |

7. āgachchhantyāḥ = while departing; evam = thus; bhayāvaham = for a fearful; vijanam = and lonely; vanam = forest; dhṛitam = I am hṛidaye = in my heart; tat = that; mahat = great message; samāhitam = imparted; me = to me; shvashrā = by my mother-in-law.

"While departing for the lonely and fearful forest, my mother-in-law imparted a great message to me, which I have inscribed in my heart."

Sloka 8

prāṇi pradāna kāle ca yat purā tu agni samnidhau |
anuśiṣṭā jananyā asmi vākyam tad api me dhṛtam || 2-118-8

"What my mother taught me when witnessed by the fire, earlier at the time of my marriage with Rama, I shall always remember them."

Sloka 9

"O, virtuous woman! By your words, all that is being renewed. No austerity, other than obedience to one's husband is decreed for a woman."

Sloka 10

sāvitrī pati śuśrūṣām kṛtvā svarge mahīyate ||
tathā vṛttiḥ ca yātā tvam pati śuśrūṣayā divam || 2-118-10

10. sāvitrī = ṣavitri*; mahīyate = is highly honoured; svarge = in heaven; kṛitvā = for having done; patishushrūṣām = service to her lord; tvam cha = you too; tathā vṛithiḥ = having such a conduct; yātā = will proceed; divam = to heaven; pati shushrūṣayā = by obedience to your husband.

Savitri* is now highly honoured in heaven, for having served her lord faithfully heaven, You too, having followed this, will proceed to heaven, in showing obedience to your husband."

SAvitri was the celebrated wife of Prince Satyavan.

Sloka 11

variṣṭhā sarva nārīṇām eṣā ca divi devatā |
rohiṇī ca vinā candram muhūrtam api dṛśyate || 2-118-11

11. eṣā rohiṇī = ṭhis goddess Rohini; variṣṭhā = the excellent; sarva narīṇām = among all the women; na dṛishyate = is not seen; chandram vinā = without the moon; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment; divi = in the sky.

"This goddess Rohini, the excellent among all women, is not seen without the moon even for a single moment in the sky."

Sloka 12

"Such excellent women, devoted to their husbands, and by their meritorious deeds, are highly honoured in heaven."

Sloka 13

Hearing the words of Seetha, Anasuya was very much pleased, kissed on Seetha's forehead and spoke delightfully (as follows)

Sloka 14

niyamair vividhair āptam tapo hi mahad asti me |
tat saṃśritya balam sīte candaye tvām śuci vrate || 2-118-14

14. sīte = O; ṣeetha; shuchi smite = with a bright smile!; asti hi = there is indeed; mahat = a great; tapaḥ = merit; āptam = acquired; me = by me; vividhaiḥ = in virtue of my various; niyamaiḥ = pious observances; samshritya = taking shelter; tat balam = of that power; chhandaye = I wish to confer a boon; tvām = on you.

"O, Seetha with a bright smile! There is a great merit acquired by me in virtue of my various pious observances. Through that power, I wish to confer a boon on you."

Sloka 15

upapannam ca yuktam ca vacanam tava maithili |
prītā ca asmy ucitam kim te karavāṇi bravīhyaham || 2-118-15

15. maithili = O; ṣeetha!; tava rachanam = your words; upapannam = are suited for the occasion; manojJṇamcha = and are charming too; asmi = I am; prītā = satisfied; bravīhi = tell; me = me; kim = what; uchitam good; aham karavāṇi = I may do; te = for you.

"O, Seetha! Your words are suitable for the occasion and are charming too. I am satisfied. Tell me what good I may do for you."

Sloka 16

tasyāstadvacanam śrutvā vismitā mandavismayā |
kṛtam ity abravīt sītā tapo bala samanvitām || 2-118-16

16. shrutvā = hearing; tadvachanam = those words; tasyāḥ = of Anasuya; sītā = ṣeetha; vismitā = was surprised; mandavismayā = and with gentle smile; abravīt = spoke; tapobala samanvitam = to Anasuya who was richly endowed with the power of askesis; (as follows): kṛitamiti = "Everything stands fulfilled (by your grace)."

Hearing those words of Anasuya, Seetha was surprised and with a gentle smile spoke to her, who was richly endowed with the power of askesis, saying; "Everything stands fulfilled (by your grace).

Sloka 17

sā tu evam uktā dharmajnā tayā prītatarā abhavat |
saphalam ca praharṣam te hanta sīte karomyaham || 2-118-17

17. evam = thus; uktā = spoken; tayā = by that ṣeetha; sā = that Anasuya; dharmajṇā = the pious minded; abhavat = became; prīta tarā = more delighted; sīte = O; ṣeetha!; hanta = Alas!; aham = I; karomi = will create; praharṣam = a great joy; te = for you; saphalam = which will be to your advantage.

Thus spoken by Seetha, the pious-minded Anasuya became more delighted and said: "O, Seetha! Alas! I will create a great joy for you, which will be to your advantage."

Sloka 18

idam divyam varam mālyam vastram ābharaṇāni ca |
anga rāgam ca vaidehi mahā arham anulepanam || 2-118-18
mayā dattam idam sīte tava gātrāṇi śobhayet |
anurūpam asamkliṣṭam nityam eva bhaviṣyati || 2-118-19

18; 19. sīte = O; ṣeetha; vaidehi = the daughter of Videha kingdom!; idam = here are; divyam = divine; varam = gifts; mālyam = a garland; vastram = apparel; ābharaṇānicha = jewels; aṇ^ga rāgamcha = scented cosmetic; mahārham = and a rare; anulepanamcha = cream; idam = these are all; dattam = given; mayā = by me; shobhayet = to adorn; tava = your; gātrāṇi = limbs; nityameva = It wll be ever; anurūpam = worthy of you; bhaviṣyati = and will remain; asamkliṣṭam = intact (even after constant use).

"O, Seetha, the daughter of Videha kingdom! Here are divine gifts: a garland, an apparel, jewels, a scented cosmetic and rare body-cream. These are all given by me to adorn your limbs. They will be ever worthy of your and will remain in tact (even after constant use)."

Sloka 20

śobhayiṣyāmi bhartāram yathā śrīr viṣṇum avyayam || 2-118-20

20. janakātmaje = O; ṣeetha! liptāṇ^gī = your body; anointed; divyena = with this celestial; aṇ^garāgeṇa = cosmetic; shobhayiṣyasi bhartāram = will cause yourhusabdn to look beautiful; yathā = as; shrīḥ = Lakshmi (the goddess of fortune and beauty); (does); avyayam = the imperishable; viṣṇum = Vishnu (the lord of preservation).

"O, Seetha! Your body, anointed with these heavenly cosmetics, will cause your husband to look beautiful, as Lakshmi (the goddess of fortune and beauty) does the imperishable Vishnu (the Lord of Preservation)."

Sloka 21

maithilī pratijagrāha prīti dānam anuttamam || 2-118-21

21. sā maithilī = that ṣeetha; pratijagrāha = accepted; vastram = the apparel; aṇgarāgam cha = the scented cosmetic; bhūṣaṇāni = the jewels; tathā = and; srajaḥ = the garlands; anuttamam = as unsurpassed prītidānam = gifts of love.

Seetha accepted the apparel, the scented cosmetic, the jewels and the garlands as unsurpassed gifts of love.

Sloka 22

śliṣṭa anjali puṭā dhīrā samupāsta tapo dhanām || 2-118-22

Having accepted those gifts of love, the illustrious Seetha with joined palms, sat near that female ascetic.

Sloka 23

To inquire about a lovely tale of once upon a time, Anasuya who was firm in austerity, started asking the following words to Seetha who was sitting near her in that manner.

Sloka 24

24. sīte = O; ṣeetha! kila = It is so said that; tvam = you; prāptā = were received as a wife; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; yashasvinā = the illustrious man; svayamvare = through a process of your self-choosing; iti = this; kathā = report; upāgatā = has reached; me = my; shrutim = ear.

"O, Seetha! It is so said that you were won by the illustrious Rama through a process of your self-choosing (svayamvara)*. This report has reached my ear."

*Svayamvara= the ceremony of self-choice, when a princess chooses her own consort from the royal suitors assembled, by placing a garland around his neck.

Sloka 25

"O, Seetha! I wish to hear that narrative in detail. Hence, tell me that tale in full as you experienced it."

Sloka 26

Then, Seetha obediently answered, saying "Hear me" and began to truly narrate that tale of Svayamvara to the virtuous woman, Anasuya."

Sloka 27

"A king of ṃithila kingdom, named Janaka who was valiant and a knower of righteousness, was devoted to the duty of his warrior class and was ruling the earth in a fitting manner."

Sloka 28

"While he was furrowing a territory of land, holding a plough in his hand, it is so said that I came forth, splitting up the land, as a daughter of that king."

Sloka 29

Sloka 30

"Placing me personally in his lap with affection, that childless king Janaka called one as his daughter, and was very fond of me since then."

Sloka 31

Sloka 32

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

Sloka 36

Sloka 37

37. jJṇātvā = knowing; mām = me; ayonijām = to be the one not emerged from a mother's womb; mahīpālaḥ = the king; vichintayan = after a deep reflection; nādhyagachchhat = was unable to find; anurūpam cha = a suitable; sadṛisham = and worthy; patim = husband; mama = for me.

Sloka 38

tasya buddhir iyam jātā cintayānasya samtatam |
svayam varam tanūjāyāḥ kariṣyāmi iti dhīmataḥ || 2-118-38

38. tasya = to him; dhīmataḥ = the wiseman; chintayāmasya = after reflecting (thus); santatam = constantly; jātā = it has come; iyam buddiḥ = to his mind; iti = thus; kariṣyāmi = "I shall arrange; svayamvaram = for a process of self-choosing marriage; tanūjayāḥ = for my daughter."

"After reflecting thus deeply, the thought came to him, 'I shall inaugurate a Svayamvara, a process of self-choosing marriage, for my daughter."

Sloka 39

mahā yajne tadā tasya varuṇena mahātmanā |
dattam dhanur varam prītyā tūṇī ca akṣayya sāyakau || 2-118-39

39. tadā = In ancient days; mahāyajJṇe = on the occasion of a great sacrifice; mahātmanā = by the high-souled; varuṇena = Varuna; the rain-god; tasya = to him; dattam = was given; dhanurvaram = an excellent bow; tūṇīcha = with two quivers; akṣaya sāyakau = that should never lack arrows; prītyā = with affection.

"In ancient days, Janaka received with affection from Varuna the rain-god, an excellent bow with two quivers that should never lack arrows."

Sloka 40

asaṃcālyam manuṣyaiḥ ca yatnena api ca gauravāt |
tan na śaktā namayitum svapneṣu api nara adhipāḥ || 2-118-40

40. gauravāt = because of heavy weight; tat = that bow; asamchālyam = which could not be lifted; manuṣyaiḥ = by men; yatnenāpi = even with effort; narādhiparaḥ = and the kings; na shaktāḥ = were unable; namayitum = to bend (it); svapneṣvapi = even in their dreams.

"That bow was so heavy in weight that no man could lift it up nor any of the kings were bale to bend it even in their dreams."

Sloka 41

tad dhanuḥ prāpya me pitrā vyāhṛtam satya vādinā |
samavāye nara indrāṇām pūrvam āmantrya pārthivān || 2-118-41

41. me pitrā = by my father; satyavādinā = who speaks truth; āmantrya = having called pārthivān = the princes; pūrvam = first; vyāhṛitam = informed; samavāye = in their meeting; tat dhanuḥ = about that bow; prāpya = to be lifted.

"ṃy truthful father called all the princes first and informed them in a meeting about the bow to be lifted."

Sloka 42

idam ca dhanur udyamya sajyam yaḥ kurute naraḥ |
tasya me duhitā bhāryā bhaviṣyati na saṃśayaḥ || 2-118-42

42. yaḥ narah = whichever man; udyamya = lifts; idam dhanuḥ = this bow; kurute sajyamcha = and strings it; tasya = to him; me duhitā = my daughter; bhaviṣyati = will become; bhāryā = a consort (to him); na = there is no; samshayaḥ = doubt.

"Whoever is able to lift up and string this bow, I will bestow my daughter in marriage on him. There is no doubt about it."

Sloka 43

tac ca dṛṣṭvā dhanuḥ śreṣṭham gauravād giri samnibham |
abhivādya nṛpā jagmur aśaktāḥ tasya tolane || 2-118-43

43. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tat dhanuḥ shreṣṭham- that excellent bow; girisannibham = resembling a mountain; gauravāto = from the point of view of its heavy weight; asaktāḥ = and bieng unable; tolane = to life; tasya = it; nṛipāḥ = the princes; abhivādhya = offered salutation to it; jagmuḥ = and went away.

"Seeing that excellent bow, resembling a mountain in weight, and being unable to lift it up, the princes offered salutation to it and went away."

Sloka 44

sudīrghasya tu kālasya rāghavo ayam mahā dyutiḥ |
viśvāmitreṇa sahito yajnam draṣṭum samāgataḥ || 2-118-44
lakṣmaṇena saha bhrātrā rāmaḥ satya parākramaḥ |

44. sudīrghasya kālasya = after a very long time; ayam rāmaḥ = this Rama; rāghavaḥ = born in Raghu dynasty; mahā dyutiḥ = with a great splendour; satya parākramaḥ = possessing a true valour; lakṣmaṇena saha = along with Lakshmana; bhrātrā = his brother; vishvāmitreṇa sahitaḥ = together with Visvamitra the sage; samāgataḥ- came; draṣṭum = to behold; yajṇam = a sacrifice.

"After a very long time, this Rama born in Raghu dynasty with a great splendour possessing a true valour, along with his brother Lakshmana together with a sage Visvamitra, came to witness a sacrifice."

Sloka 45

viśvāmitraḥ tu dharma ātmā mama pitrā supūjitaḥ || 2-118-45
provāca pitaram tatra rāghavo rāma lakṣmaṇau |

45. dharmātmā = the pious souled; vishvāmitrastu = Visvamitra; supūjitaḥ = having been worshipped well; mama pitrā = by my father;bhrātarau = about the two brothers; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; tatra = there.

"The pious-souled Visvamitra, having been received well by my father said to my father, (as follows):

Sloka 46

sutau daśarathasya imau dhanur darśana kānkṣiṇau || 2-118-46
dhanurdarśaya rāmāya rājaputrāya daivikam |

46. imau = these two boys; sutau = the sons; dasharathasya = of Dasaratha; dhanurdarshana kāṇ^kṣiṇau = wish to see that bow; darshaya = show; dhanuḥ = the bow; daivikam = coming from gods; rāmāya = to Rama; rājaputrāya = the prince.

"These two boys, the sons of Dasaratha wish to see that bow. Show that bow, coming from gods, to Rama the prince."

Sloka 47

ity uktaḥ tena vipreṇa tad dhanuḥ samupānayat || 2-118-47
nimeṣa antara mātreṇa tad ānamya sa vīryavān |
jyām samāropya jhaṭiti pūrayām āsa vīryavān || 2-118-48

47; 48. iti = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; tena viprena = by that Brahmana; tatodhanuḥ upānayat = (Janaka) caused that bow to be brought there; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; vīryavān = and the valiant Rama; ānamya = bent; tat = that bow; nimeṣāntara meatreṇa = merely within an instant; jhaṭiti = and soon; samāropya = stringed the bow; jyām = with the bows-cord; pūrayāmāsa = ad drew the bow to the full.

"Hearing the words of Vivamitra, Janaka caused the bow to be brought there. The mighty and the valiant Rama bent that bow merely within an instant and immediately stringed the bow with the bow-cord and drew the bow to the full."

Sloka 49

tena pūrayatā vegān madhye bhagnam dvidhā dhanuḥ |
tasya śabdo abhavad bhīmaḥ patitasya aśaner iva || 2-118-49

49. tena = by that Rama; pūrayatā = who was drawing the bow to the full; dhanuḥ = the bow; bhagnam = was broken; dvidhāḥ = into two; madhye = in the middle vegāt = due to that jerk; tasya = its; bhīmaḥ = terrific; shabdaḥ = sound; abhavat = was; patitasya ashaneriva = like that of a falling thunder-bolt.

"While Rama was drawing the bow to the full, the bow was broken into two in the middle due to the resultant jerk. The terrific sound then created was like that of a falling thunderbolt."

Sloka 50

tato aham tatra rāmāya pitrā satya abhisaṃdhinā |
udyatā dātum udyamya jala bhājanam uttamam || 2-118-50

50. tataḥ = then; tatra = and there; pitrā = by my father; satyābhisandhinā = who was true to his promise; nishchitā = it was decided; dātum = to give; aham = me; rāmāya = to Rama; udyamya = offering him; jalabhājanam = of jar of pure water.

"Then and there, my father true to his promise decided to bestow me on Rama, offering him a jar of pure water."

Sloka 51

dīyamānām na tu tadā pratijagrāha rāghavaḥ |
avijnāya pituḥ candam ayodhyā adhipateḥ prabhoḥ || 2-118-51

51. tadā = then; rāghavaḥ = Rama; na tu pratijagrāha = did not consent to accept; dīyamānām = me who was being offered to him; avijJṇāya = withut knowing; chhandam = the opinion; pituḥ = of his father; prabhoḥ = the Lord; ayodhyādhipateḥ = and the king of Ayodhya.

"But Rama did not consent to accept my hand till the will of his father, the Lord and the king of Ayodhya had been made known to him."

Sloka 52

tataḥ śvaśuram āmantrya vṛddham daśaratham nṛpam |
mama pitrā aham dattā rāmāya vidita ātmane || 2-118-52

52. tataḥ = thereupon; mama pitrā = by my father; shvashuram = my father -in-law; vṛiddham = and the aged; dasharatham nṛipam = king Dasaratha; āmantrya = was invited; aham = I; dattā = was given; rāmāya = to Rama; viditātmane = the knower of the self.

Thereupon, my father-in-law and the aged Dasaratha was invited to ṃithila by my father and with his approval, I was bestowed on Rama, the knower of the self."

Sloka 53

mama caiva anujā sādhvī ūrmilā priya darśanā |
bhārya arthe lakṣmaṇasya api dattā pitrā mama svayam || 2-118-53

53. dattā = was given; mama pitrā = by my father; svayam = personally; mama = my; anujā = younger sister; ūrmilā = Urmila; sādhvī = the virtuous lady; priyadarshanā = of lovely looks; bhāryārthe = as a consort; lakṣmaṇasyāpi = to Lakshmana.

"ṃy younger sister Urmila, the virtuous lady of lovely looks was given as a consort to Lakshmana by my father himself."

Sloka 54

evam dattā asmi rāmāya tadā tasmin svayam vare |
anuraktā ca dharmeṇa patim vīryavatām varam || 2-118-54

54. tadā = then; asmi = I was; dattā = given; evam = thus; rāmāya = to Rama; tasmin svayamvare = in that; svayamvara; a process of self-choosing marriage; asmi = I became; anuraktā = devoted; dhameṇa = by my good works; patim = to my husband; param = who is excellent; vīryavatām = among men of strength.

"I was given thus to Rama in that Svayamvara, a process of self-choosing marriage. I became devoted, by my good works, to my husband who is excellent among men of strength."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe aṣṭādaśottaraśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 118th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
