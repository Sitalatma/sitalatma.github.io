Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 30
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dispatched by the king Ravana to ascertain the strength of the army in the opposition-camp, Shardula the demon-spy acquaints his master with the important leaders of the monkeys.

Sloka 1

tatastamakṣobhya balam laṃkā adhipataye carāḥ |
suvele rāghavam śaile niviṣṭam pratyavedayan || 6-30-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; charāḥ = the spies; pratyavedayan = informed; laṇ^kādhipate = Ravana; tam = about that; rāghavam = Rama; akshobhyabalam = with his unperturbable army; suvela shaile = on ṣuvela ṃountain.

Thereafter, the spies informed Ravana about Rama, with his unperturbable army, having encamped on Suvela ṃountain.

Sloka 2

cārāṇām rāvaṇaḥ śrutvā prāptam rāmam mahābalam |
jāta udvego abhavat kiṃcit śārdūlam vākyam abravīt || 6-30-2

2. shrutvā = hearing; chārāṇām = from the spies; prāptam = about the arrival; mahābalam = of the exceedingly strong; rāmam = Rama; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; abhavat = became; kimchit = a little; jātodvegaḥ = perturbed; abravīt = (and) spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; shardūlam = to ṣhardula.

Hearing from the spies about the arrival of Rama, the exceedingly strong man, Ravana became a little perturbed and spoke to Shardula as follows:

Sloka 3

ayathāvac ca te varṇo dīnaḥ ca asi niśā cara |
na asi kaccid amitrāṇām kruddhānām vaśam āgataḥ || 6-30-3

3. nishāchara = O; demon! ṭe = your; varṇaḥ = complexion; ayathāvat cha = is not so as it should be; asi = you (look); dīnashcha = miserable too; na āgataḥ kachchit = I hope that you have not fallen; vasham = a victim; kruddhānām = to the enraged; amitrāṇām = enemies.

O, demon! Your complexion is not so as it should be. You look miserable too. I hope that you have not fallen a victim to the enraged monkeys."

Sloka 4

iti tena anuśiṣṭas tu vācam mandam udīrayat |
tadā rākṣasa śārdūlam śārdūlo bhaya vihvalaḥ || 6-30-4

4. iti = thus; anushiṣṭaḥ = questioned; tena = by Ravana; shārdūlaḥ = ṣhardula; bhaya vihvalaḥ = afflicted with fear; tadā = then; mandam = sluggishly; udīrayat = spoke; vācham = (these) words; rākshasa shārdūlam = to Ravana the tiger among demons.

Thus questioned by Ravana, Shardula afflicted with fear, then sluggishly spoke the following words to Ravana the tiger among demons:

Sloka 5

na te cārayitum śakyā rājan vānara pumgavāḥ |
vikrāntā balavantaḥ ca rāghaveṇa ca rakṣitāḥ || 6-30-5

5. rājan = O; king! ṭe = those; vānara puṇgavāḥ = excellent monkeys; vikrāntāḥ = bold; balavantashcha = strong; rakṣitāḥ cha = and protected; rāghavaṇa = by Rama; na shakyāḥ = are not capable of being; chārayitum = spied upon.

"O, king! Those strong, bold and excellent monkeys protected by Rama are not capable of being spied upon."

Sloka 6

na api sambhāṣitum śakyāḥ sampraśno atra na labhyate |
sarvato rakṣyate panthā vānaraiḥ parvata upamaiḥ || 6-30-6

6. na shakyāḥ = they are not capable of being; sambhāṣitumapi = talked with; na labhyate = nor can be found anything; samprashna = by interrogating; atra = with them; sarvataḥ = the entire; panthāḥ = path; rakshyate = is being protected; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys; parvatopamaiḥ = looking like mountains.

"They are not capable of being talked with and nothing can be found in interrogating with them. The entire path is being protected by the monkeys looking like mountains."

Sloka 7

praviṣṭa mātre jñāto aham bale tasminn acārite |
balād gṛhīto bahubhir bahudhā asmi vidāritaḥ || 6-30-7

7. aham = I; jJṇātaḥ = was identified; (when); tasmin = that; bale = army; praviṣṭamātre = was merely penetrated into (by me); achārite = and began to be examined; gṛihītaḥ = I was seized; balata = forcibly; rakshobhiḥ = by the demons (forming the retinue of Vibhishana); asmi = I was; vichāritaḥ = investigated (by them); babhudhāḥ = in various ways.

"I was identified when that army was merely penetrated into (by me) and began to be examined. I was seized forcibly by the demons (forming the retinue of Vibhishana), who investigated me in various ways."

Sloka 8

jānubhir muṣṭibhir dantais talaiḥ ca abhihato bhṛśam |
pariṇīto asmi haribhir balavadbhir amarṣaṇaiḥ || 6-30-8

8. abhihataḥ = I was beaten; bhṛisham = very much; jāmbhiḥ = with knees; muṣṭibhiḥ = fists; dantaiḥ = teeth; talaishcha = and palms; amarṣaṇaiḥ = by the enraged; haribhiḥ = monkeys; asmi = I was; pariṇītaḥ = paraded round; balamdhye = in the middle of the army.

"I was beaten on all sides with knees, fists, teeth and palms by the enraged monkeys. I was also paraded round in the midst of their army."

Sloka 9

pariṇīya ca sarvatra nīto aham rāma saṃsadam |
rudhira ādigdha sarva ango vihvalaḥ calita indriyaḥ || 6-30-9

9. pariṇīya = having been paraded; sarvatra = all around; aham = I; nītaḥ = was taken; rāmasamsadi = to the court of Rama; rudhirādidighdhasarvāṇ^gaḥ = all my limbs was anointed with blood; vihvalaḥ = being trembled; chalitendriyaḥ = my senses were disturbed.

"Having been paraded all around thus, I was finally taken to the court of Rama. All my limbs were bleeding and Rama. All my limbs were bleeding and I was trembling, with my senses disturbed."

Sloka 10

haribhir vadhyamānaḥ ca yācamānaḥ kṛta anjaliḥ |
rāghaveṇa paritrāto jīvāmi ha yadṛccayā || 6-30-10

10. vadhyamānaḥ = while being plagmed; haribhiḥ = by the monkeys; yāchamānaḥ = and asked for protection; kṛitaṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms; paritrātaḥ = I was saved; yard^ichchhayā = fortunately; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; māmeti cha = saying "stay! ṣtay!".

"While being plagued by the monkeys and asked for protection with joined palms, I was saved fortunately by Rama, saying �stay!stay!'

Sloka 11

eṣa śailaiḥ śilābhiḥ ca pūrayitvā mahāarṇavam |
dvāram āśritya laṃkāyā rāmas tiṣṭhati sāyudhaḥ || 6-30-11

11. eṣaḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; pūrayitvā = has filled; mahārṇavam = the sea; shailaiḥ = with boulders; shilābhishcha = and rocks; āshritya = taking up his position; dvāram = at the gates; laṇ^kāyāḥ = of Lanka; tiṣṭhati = and stands there; sāyndhaḥ = well equipped with weapon.

"Rama has filled the sea with boulders and rocks, taking up his position at the gates of Lanka and stands there, well-equipped with weapons."

Sloka 12

garuḍa vyūham āsthāya sarvato haribhir vṛtaḥ |
mām visṛjya mahātejā laṃkām eva abhivartate || 6-30-12

12. visṛijya = having released; mām = me; mahātejajāḥ = Rama of great splendour; āsthāya = arranged; (the army); garuDa vyūham = in the form of Garuda (an eagle); vṛitaḥ = (ḥe is) encircled; sarvataḥ = on all sides; haribhiḥ = by monkeys; abhivartate = and is approaching; laṇ^kāmeva = towards Lanka.

"Having released me, Rama of great splendour arranged the army in the form of Garuda (an eagle). He is encircled on all sides by the monkeys and is approaching towards Lanka."

Sloka 13

purā prākāram āyāti kṣipram ekataram kuru |
sītām ca asmai prayacca āśu suyuddham vā pradīyatām || 6-30-13

13. purā āyāti = he may reach; prākāram = the ramparts; kuru = do; ekataram = one thing or the other; kshipram = quickly; vā = or; prayāchchha = give away; sītām = ṣeetha; āshu = immediately; pradīyatām vāpi = or even give him; yuddham = a battle.

"Before he reaches the ramparts, do one thing or the other quickly or give away Seetha immediately or even offer him battle."

Sloka 14

manasā samtatāpa atha tat śrutvā rākṣasa adhipaḥ |
śārdūlasya mahad vākyam atha uvāca sa rāvaṇaḥ || 6-30-14

14. shrutvā = having heard; tat = those words; saḥ = that; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākshasādhipaḥ = the Lord of demons; tadā = then; prekshya manasā = reflected; tat = on it; atham = and thereafter; uvācha = spoke; sumahat vākyam = the significant words; shārdūlam = to shardula.

Having heard those words, Ravana the lord of demons reflected on it and thereafter spoke the following significant words to Shardula.

Sloka 15

yadi mām pratiyudhyeran deva gandharva dānavāḥ |
na eva sītām pradāsyāmi sarva loka bhayād api || 6-30-15

15. naira pradāsyāmi = I will not give away; sītām = ṣeetha; yadi = even if; deva gandharva dānavāḥ = the celestials or Gandharvas the celestial musicians or the demons; pratiyudhyerannapi = were to fight again; mām = me; sarva loka bhayādapi = or even under danger from all beings.

"I will not give away Seetha even if the celestials or Gandharvas the celestial musicians or the demons were to fight against me or even under danger from all beings."

Sloka 16

evam uktvā mahātejā rāvaṇaḥ punar abravīt |
cāritā bhavatā senā ke atra śūrāḥ plavam gamāḥ || 6-30-16

16. mahātejāḥ = the heroic; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; evam = thus; uktvā = uttered; punaḥ = and again; abravīt = spoke (as follows); senā = (has) the army; charitā = been espied; bhavatā = by you? Ke = who; shūrāḥ = are the valiant; plavaṇ^gamāḥ = monkeys; atra = among them?

After uttering the aforesaid words, the heroic Ravana again spoke as follows: "Has the army been espied by you? Who are the valiant monkeys among them?"

Sloka

kīdṛśāḥ kim prabhāvāḥ ca vānarā ye durāsadāḥ |
kasya putrāḥ ca pautrāḥ ca tattvam ākhyāhi rākṣasa || 6-30-17

17. sūrata = O; the virtuous; saumya = gentle man!; kimprabhāḥ vānarāḥ = what radiance do the monkeys have; ye = who; durāsadāḥ = an difficult to be conquered? kīdṛishāḥ = of what kind? Kasya = whose; putrāḥ = sons (are they?) pautrashcha = whose grandsons are they? ṭvam = you; ākhyāhi = describe; tat = it.

"O, the virtuous gentle Shardula! What radiance do the monkeys, who are difficult to be conquered possess of? what kind? Whose sons and grandsons are they? You tell me that information."

Sloka 18

tathātra pratipatsyāmi jñātvā teṣām bala abalam |
avaśyam bala samkhyānam kartavyam yuddham iccatā || 6-30-18

18. jJṇātvā = after knowing; teṣām = their; balābalam = strength and weakness; pratipatsyāmi = I will decide; atra = in this matter; tathā = accordingly; kartavyam khalu = Is it not necessary; samkhyānam = to count; avashyam = clearly; ichchhatām = those who are yearning; yuddham = for battle?

"After knowing their strengths and weakness, I will decide in this matter accordingly. Is it not necessary to count clearly those who are really yearning to fight the battle?"

Sloka 19

atha evam uktaḥ śārdūlo rāvaṇena uttamaḥ caraḥ |
idam vacanam ārebhe vaktum rāvaṇa samnidhau || 6-30-19

19. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; shārdūlaḥ = ṣhardula; uttamaḥ charaḥ = the excellent spy; atha = then; ārebhe = started; vaktum = to tell; idam = these; vachanam = words; rāvaṇa samvidhau = for Ravana's clearance.

Hearing Ravana's words, Shardula the excellent spy then started to narrate these words for Ravana's information:

Sloka 20

atha ṛkṣa rajasaḥ putro yudhi rājan sudurjayaḥ |
gadgadasya atha putro atra jāmbavān iti viśrutaḥ || 6-30-20

20. atha = certainly; rājā = king ṣugreeva; putraḥ = the son; ṛiksharajasaḥ = of Riksharajasa; durāsadaḥ = is difficult to be conquered; yudhi = in battle; atra = here; sutraḥ = is the so; gadgadasya = of Gadgada; vishrutaḥ = famously; jāmbavāniti = called as Jambavan.

"Certainly, king Sugreeva, the son of Riksharajasa, is difficult to be conquered in battle. Here is the son of Gadgada, famously called as Jambavan."

Sloka 21

gadgadasya eva putro anyo guru putraḥ śata kratoḥ |
kadanam yasya putreṇa kṛtam ekena rakṣasām || 6-30-21

21. asyaḥ = the other one; putraḥ = is the son; gadgadasyaiva = of the same Gadagada. (another one); guruputraḥ = is the son of Brihaspati; the teacher; shatakratoḥ = of Indra the god of celestials; (called Kesari); yasya = by whose; putreṇa = son (ḥanuman); ekena = alone; kadanam = the destroyal; rakshasām = of demons; kṛitam = was done.

"The other one is the son of the same Gadgada. Another one is the son of Brihaspati the teacher of Indra the lord of celestials, called Kesari by whose son Hanuman the demons were destroyed."

Sloka 22

suṣeṇaḥ ca api dharma ātmā putro dharmasya vīryavān |
saumyaḥ soma ātmajaḥ ca atra rājan dadhi mukhaḥ kapiḥ || 6-30-22

22. rājan = O; king! Atra = here is; suṣeṇashcha = ṣushena; putraḥ = the son; dharmasya = of Yama the lord of Death; vīryavān = who is valiant; dharmātmā = and virtuous minded; dadhimukhaḥ kapiḥ = and a monkey called Dadhimukha; somātmajaḥ = who is the son of moon; saumyaḥ = and cool-minded.

"O, king! Here is Sushena the son of Yama the Lord of Death who is valiant and virtuous, as well as a monkey called Dadhimukha, the cool-minded, who is the son of moon."

Sloka 23

sumukho durmukhaḥ ca atra vega darśī ca vānaraḥ |
mṛtyur vānara rūpeṇa nūnam sṛṣṭaḥ svayambhuvā || 6-30-23

23. sumukhaḥ = ṣumukha; durmukhashchaiva = Durmukha; vānaraḥ = and monkey called; vegadarshīcha = called Vegadarshi; nūnam = are surely; mṛityuḥ = the death; sṛiṣṭaḥ = created; vānara rapeṇa = in the form of monkeys; svayambhuvā = by Brahma the Lord of creation.

"Sumukha, Durmukha and a monkey called Vegadarshi are surely the personifications of death in the form of monkeys, created by Brahma, the Lord of creation."

Sloka 24

putro huta vahasya atha nīlaḥ senā patiḥ svayam |
anilasya ca putro atra hanūmān iti viśrutaḥ || 6-30-24

24. atra = here is; nīlaḥ = ṇila; senāpatiḥ = the army-general; svayam = himself; putraḥ = the son; hutavahasya = of Agni the Lord of Fire; atra = here is; anilasya putraḥ = the son of Vayu the Lord of Wind; vishrutaḥ = well-know; hanumāniti = as ḥanuman.

"Here is Nila the army-general himself, the son of Agni the Lord of Fire. Here is the son of Vayu the Lord of Wind well- known as Hanuman."

Sloka 25

naptā śakrasya durdharṣo balavān angado yuvā |
maindaḥ ca dvividaḥ ca ubhau balināv aśvi sambhavau || 6-30-25
putrā vaivasvatasya atra panca kāla antaka upamāḥ |
gajo gava akṣo gavayaḥ śarabho gandha mādanaḥ || 6-30-26
daśa vānarakoṭyaśca śūrāṇām yuddhakāṅgkṣiṇām |
śrīmatām devaputrāṇām śeṣam nākhyātumutsahe || 6-30-27

25; 26; 27. yuvā = the young; an^gadaḥ = Angada; shakrasya = Indra's; naptā = grandson; balavān = the stron; durdharṣaḥ = and invincible; ubhau = both; balinau = the mighty; maindashacha = ṃainda; dvividashcha = and Dvivida; ashvisambhavau = born of the two divinities of Ashvin*; atha = and; gaja = Gaja; gavākṣaḥ = Gavaksha; gavayaḥ = Gavaya; sharabhaḥ = ṣharabha; gandhamādhanaḥ = and Gandhamadhana; paṇ^cha = the five; putrāḥ = sons; vaivasvatasya = of Yama the God of Death; kālāntkopamā = resembling Yama at the time of dissolution of the world; dasha = ten; vānarakoṭyashcha = crores of monkeys; shūrāṇām = the valiant ones; yuddhakāṇkshiṇām = who are yearning for battle (are here); na utsahe = I cannot venture; ākhyātum = to tell; sheṣām = about remaining; devaputrāṇām = sons of god; shrīmatām = the glorious.

"The young Angada, Indra's grandson, the strong and invincible one, both the mighty ṃainda and Dvinda born of the two divinties of Ashvin* as well as Gaja, Gavaksha, Gavaya, Sharabha and Gandhamadhana the five sons of Yama the God of Death all of them resembling Yama at the time of dissolution of the world, the valiant ten crores of monkeys who are yearning for battle are all here. I cannot venture to tell about the remaining glorious sons of god."

The two divinities of Ashvin are said to appear in the sky before the dawn in a golden carriage drawn by horses or birds, they bring treasures to men and avert misfortune and sickness; they are considered as the physicians of heaven.

Sloka 28

putro daśarathasyeṣa simhasamhanano yuvā |
dūṣaṇo nihato yena kharaśca triśirāstathā || 6-30-28

28. eṣaḥ = this; yuvā = young man; (is Rama); dasharathasya = Dasaratha's; putraḥ = son; simha samhananaḥ = who is well-built like a lion; yena = by whom; dūṣaṇaḥ = Dushana; nihataḥ = was killed; tathā = as also; kharashcha = Khara; trishirāḥ = and ṭrishira.

"This young man is Rama, Dasartha's son, who is well-built like a lion, by whom Dushana ws killed as also Khara and Trishana.

Sloka 29

nāsti rāmasya sadṛśo vikrame bhuvi kaścana |
virādho nihato yena kabandhaścāntakopamaḥ || 6-30-29

29. nāsti kashchana = there is no one; bhuvi = on earth; sadṛishaḥ = who is equal; vikrame = in prowess; rāmasya = to Rama; yena = by whom; virādho = Viradha; nihataḥ = was killed; kabandhashcha = as also Kabandha; antakopamaḥ = who was equal to Yama.

"There is no one on earth who is equal in prowess to Rama, by whom Viradha was killed as also Kabandha who was equal to Yama."

Sloka 30

vaktum na śakto rāmasya guṇān kaścinnaraḥ kṣitau |
janasthānagatā yena tāvanto rākṣasā hatāḥ || 6-30-30

30. na shaktaḥ naraḥ kashchit = ṇo man is able; kṣitau = on this earth; vaktum = to tell; guṇān = the qualities; rāmasya = of Rama; yena = by whom; tāvantaḥ = all rākshasaḥ = the demons; janasthānagatāḥ = staying in Janasthana; hatāḥ = were killed.

"No man on this earth is able to narrate the qualities of Rama, by whom all the demons staying in Janasthana were killed."

Sloka 31

lakṣmaṇaścātra dharmātmā mātaṅgavāmivarṣabhaḥ |
yasya bāṇapatham prāpya va jīvedapi vāsavaḥ || 6-30-31

31. prāpya = having stood; yasya = on whose; bāṇapatham = path of arrows; vāsavaḥ api = even Indra the Lord of celestials; na jīvet = could not survive; lakshmaṇashcha = that Lakshamna; dharmātmā = the virtuous man; ṛiṣabhaḥ iva = resembling the most excellent of elephants; mātaṇ^gānām = among elephants.

"Here too is the virtuous Lakshamana, resembling the most excellent elephant among elephants, in the path of whose arrows, even Indra the Lord of celestials himself could not survive

Sloka 32

śveto jyotir mukhaḥ ca atra bhāskarasya ātma sambhavau |
varuṇasya ca putro atha hema kūṭaḥ plavam gamaḥ || 6-30-32
viśva karma suto vīro nalaḥ plavaga sattamaḥ |
vikrānto vegavān atra vasu putraḥ sudurdharaḥ || 6-30-33

32; 33. shveta = ṣweta; jyotirmukhashcha = and Jyotirmukha; ātmasambhavau = the sons; bhāskarasya = of the sun-god; plavaṇ^gamaḥ = a monkey called; hemakūṭaḥ = ḥemakuta; anyaḥ putraḥ = another son; varuṇasya = of Varuna; nalaḥ = ṇala; vishvakarmasutaḥ = the son of Vishvakarma; vīraḥ = the strong; plavṇ^gama sattamaḥ = and the best of monkeys; saḥ = (as well as) that; durdharaḥ = Durdhara; vasuputraḥ = the son of Vasus; vikrāntaḥ = the mighty; vegavān = and the swift; atra = are here.

"Sweta and Jyotirmukha, the sons of the sun-god, a monkey called Hemakuta, another son of Varuna, Nila the son of Visvakarma the strong and the best of monkeys, as well as that mighty and swift Durdhara the son of Vasus are all here."

Sloka 34

rākṣasānām variṣṭhaḥ ca tava bhrātā vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
parigṛhya purīm laṃkām rāghavasya hite rataḥ|| 6-30-34

34. variṣṭhashcha = there is the prince; rākṣasānām = of demons; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; tava = your; bhrātā = brother; prtigṛihya = who having secured; laṇ^kām purīm = the city of Lanka; (as a prospective gift from Rama); rataḥ = remains devoted; hite = to be good; rāghavasya = of Rama.

"There is the prince of demons Vibhishana, your brother, who having secured the city of Lanka (as a prospective gift from Rama) remains devoted to the good of Rama."

Sloka 35

iti sarvam samākhyātam tava idam vānaram balam |
suvele adhiṣṭhitam śaile śeṣa kārye bhavān gatiḥ || 6-30-35

35. iti = thus; samākhyātam = has been; described; idam = about this; sarvam = entire; vānaram balam = army of monkeys; adhiṣṭhitam = stationed; suvele shaile = on the ṃount ṣuvela; bhavān = It is for you; gatiḥ = to decide; sheṣakārye = what remains to be done!.

"Thus, I have described about the entire army of monkeys stationed on the ṃount Suvela. Now, it is for you to decide what remains to be done!"

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 30th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
