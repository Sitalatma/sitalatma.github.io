Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 7

Introduction

Rama arrives at the hermitage of Sage Suteekshna, as directed by Sage Sharabhanga in the previous chapter and asks the sage to show a place to dwell. Suteekshna asks Rama to stay at his own hermitage, which has no other problems than the wild animals that come in herds and make blaring noises. Rama wishes to kill those animals that hamper the tranquillity of hermits living therein, but having observed the kindness of Suteekshna towards those wild animals, however disturbing they may be, Rama refrains to kill them, and would like to go to any other place than this. They spend that night there.

Sloka 1

rāmaḥ tu sahitaḥ bhrātrā sītayā ca paraṃtapaḥ |
sutīkṣṇasya āśrama padam jagāma saha taiḥ dvijaiḥ || 3-7-1

1. param tapaḥ rāmaḥ tu = others [enemies,] scorcher, Rama, but; bhrātā sīthyā cha sahitaḥ = Rama, with brother, ṣeetha, also, along with; taiḥ dvijaiḥ saha = those, Brahmans, along with; sutīkshṇasya āshrama padam jagāma = ṣage ṣuteekshna's, hermitage, threshold, gone towards.

Rama, the enemy-scorcher, has gone towards the threshold of the hermitage of ṣage ṣuteekshna along with his brother Lakshmana and Seetha, also along with those Brahmans who accompanied him all the way. [4-7-1]

Sloka 2

sa gatvā dīrgham adhvānam nadīḥ tīrtvā bahu udakāḥ |
dadarśa vimalam śailam mahā merum iva unnatam || 3-7-2

2. saḥ = he that Rama; dīrgham adhvānam gatvā = long, way, having gone on; bahu udakāḥ nadīḥ tīrtvā = with plenty, of waters, rivers, on crossing; mahā merum iva unnatam = great, ṃt. ṃeru, like, towering; vimalam shailam- tranquil mountain; dadarsha = has seen.

ḥe travelling on a long way, and on crossing rivers with plenty of waters has seen a tranquil mountain towering like great ṃt. ṃeru. [3-7-2]

Sloka 3

tataḥ tad ikṣvāku varau satatam vividhaiḥ drumaiḥ |
kānanam tau viviśatuḥ sītayā saha rāghavau || 3-7-3

3. tataḥ ikshvāku varau = then, of Ikshvaku-s, those, best ones; tau rāghavau = those, Raghava-s; sītayā saha = along with ṣeetha; satatam vividhaiḥ drumaiḥ = always, with divers, tress; tat kānanam vivishatuḥ = that, forest, they, entered.

ṭhen those best ones from Ikshvaku dynasty, those two Raghava-s along with ṣeetha have entered the forest which will always be full with diverse trees. [3-7-3]

Sloka 4

praviṣṭaḥ tu vanam ghoram bahu puṣpa phala drumam |
dadarśa āśramam ekānte cīra mālā pariṣkṛtam || 3-7-4

4. pravishtaḥ tu vanam ghoram = entering, forest, perilous one; bahu puṣpa phala drumam = with many, flower and fruit bearing trees; dadarsha āshramam = have seen, hermitage; ekānte = in a reclusive place; cīra mālā parishkṛitam = jute cloths, garlanded, adorned with.

Entering that perilous forest with its many a flower and fruit bearing trees, they have seen the hermitage in a reclusive place adorned with rows of jute cloths as though garlanding it. [3-7-4]

Sloka 5

tatra tāpasam āsīnam mala paṅkaja dhāriṇam |
rāmaḥ sutīkṣṇam vidhivat tapodhanam abhāṣata || 3-7-5

5. tatra = there; Rama; mala pankaja dhāriṇam = soiled, [lotus-like] blotches bearing; āsīnam = one who is sitting; tapaḥ dhanam = ascetically wealthy one; tāpasam sutīkshnam = ascetic, ṣuteekshna; [dṛiṣṭvā = having seen]; vidhivat = dutifully; abhāṣata = spoke to.

ṭhere on seeing the ascetically rich ṣage ṣuteekshna sitting in yogic posture with his body bearing lotus-like blotches of soil, Rama dutifully spoke to him. [3-7-5]

▼

Here it is said the soil on the sage's body has taken lotus-like blotches. Certain yogic methods do not care outward bathing of body. Usually the bath is prescribed three times a day, but hermits of a sort leave away that bodily bathing, because the sweat and soil recurs immediately after each bath on this impure body. So they stop taking outward bath and concentrate on cleansing the inner space.

Sloka 6

rāmo.aham asmi bhagavan bhavantam draṣṭum āgataḥ |
tat mā abhivada dharmajña maharṣe satya vikrama || 3-7-6

6. bhagavan = oh god; aham rāmaḥ asmi = I am Rama; bhavantam draṣṭum āgataḥ = you, to see, have come; tat = therefore; dharmaGYa = O, virtue-knower; maharṣe = great sage; satya vikrama = truthfulness, having as your courage; mā abhi vada = to me, you talk.

"Oh! godly sage I am Rama, I have come to see you, hence oh, knower of virtue, oh, great sage, oh, one with truthfulness as your courage, please talk to me..." [3-7-6]

Sloka 7

sa nirīkṣya tataḥ dhīro rāmam dharmabhṛtām varam |
samāśliṣya ca bāhubhyām idam vacanam abravīt || 3-7-7

7. tataḥ = then; dhīraḥ saḥ = highly learned one, he that sage; nirīkshya = having seen bhṛitām varam rāmam = virtue, proponent, the best one, at Rama; bāhubhyām sam āshlishya ca = with both hands, well embracing, also; idam vacanam abravīt = this, sentence, said.

ṭhen that sage having seen that brave and best proponent of virtue, Rama, embraced him with both of his hands, and spoke this sentence. [3-7-7]

Sloka 8

svāgatam te raghu śreṣṭha rāma satyabhṛtām vara |
āśramao ayam tvayā ākrāntaḥ sanātha iva sāṃpratam || 3-7-8

8. svāgatam te raghu shreṣṭha = welcome, to you, Raghu, the best; Rama; satya bhṛitām vara = truth, patron, the best; sāmpratam = presently; tvayā ākrāntaḥ = by you, caught unprepared [treaded upon]; ayam āshramaḥ = this, hermitage; sa nātha iva[as if] with, a husband one, like - well-lorded one; [babhūva = became.]

"Welcome to you Rama, best of Raghu dynasty and the best patron of truth, and when you presently caught this hermitage unprepared this has become a well-lorded one... [3-7-8]

Sloka 9

pratīkṣamāṇaḥ tvām eva na ārohe aham mahāyaśaḥ |
deva lokam ito vīra deham tyaktvā mahītale || 3-7-9
citrakūṭam upādāya rājya bhraṣṭo asi me śrutaḥ |

9, 10a. mahā yashaḥ = greatly renowned; vīra = brave one; aham tvām eva = I, for you, only; pratīkshamāṇaḥ = while awaiting; deham tyaktvā mahī tale = body, casting off, on earth's plane; itaḥ deva lokam na ārohe = from here, gods world [heavens,] not, ascended; rājya bhraṣṭaḥ citrakūṭam upādāya asi = from kingdom, repudiated, at chitrakuta, arrived, you are;[iti = thus a]; me shrutaḥ = I heard thus.

"I am awaiting for you only, oh, greatly renowned one, without my ascent to heavens by casting off this body on the earth's plane, oh, brave one, for I have heard that you have arrived at chitrakuta after you are repudiated from your kingdom... [3-7-9]

▼

This heaven ' deva loka heaven is negated for total salvation. Total salvation is at your tri-feet, tridiva. So 'I have not ascended to heavens, which causes rebirth after the decline of merit, thus I am waiting for your arrival for Total Salvation, moksha...' So said the sage to Rama. Govindaraja. "You birthed in Raghu's dynasty for the elimination of Ravana, and also foregone your kingdom and you are coming this way. So I am waiting for your graceful look, without going to heavens leaving the body. Should I leave the body and go to heavens, I cannot see parama puruṣa , The Absolute, in you... so I did not cast off this body...' Thus said the sage to Rama. ṃaheshvara Tirtha.

Sloka 10

iha upayātaḥ kākutsthaḥ devarājaḥ śatatakratuḥ || 3-7-10
upāgaṃya ca me devo mahādevaḥ sura īśvaraḥ |
sarvān lokān jitān āha mama puṇyena karmaṇā || 3-7-11

10b, 11. kākutsthaḥ = Rama; shata kratuḥ deva rajaḥ iha upayātaḥ = one with hundred rituals, deities, king, here, he came; mahādevaḥ sura īshvaraḥ = great god, celestials' ; devaḥ = such Indra; upāgamya = on approaching; mama puṇyena karmaṇā = by my, meritorious, deeds; sarvān lokān jitān = all, worlds, conquered [ by me]; mām āha = [thus] to me, he said.

"ṭhe king of gods and the chief invitee in hundred sacrifices Indra approached me, and that great deity said me that I have conquered all the worlds by my meritorious deeds... [3-7-10, 11]

Sloka 12

teṣu deva ṛṣi juṣṭeṣu jiteśu tapasā mayā |
mat prasādāt sa bhāryaḥ tvam viharasva sa lakṣmaṇaḥ || 3-7-12

12. mayā tapasā jiteṣu deva ṛiṣi juṣṭeṣu = by me, by ascesis, won over, by gods and sages, cherished; teṣu = in them [those conquered worlds]; sa bhāryaḥ = with, your wife; sa lakshmanaḥ = with, Lakshmana; tvam mat prasādāt viharasva = you, by my, bequeathal, you enjoy.

"Let me bequeath those worlds that are cherished by gods and sages alike, but which are won over by me by my ascesis, and you enjoy in them with your wife and with your brother Lakshmana... [3-7-12]

▼

This sage Suteekshna too is dedicating all his merit accrued by his penance at the feet of Vishnu. This is the same effort of dedication, which Sage Sharabhanga offered, to Rama at 3-5-33 of this canto. Any thing accrued by human effort and energy, if dedicated at the feet of god, is doubly meritorious and establishes a selflessness of the devotee. Even in any daily worship it will be concluded with a saying, ye tat phalam parameshwara arpaṇamastu... i.e., whatever is the result of this worship it is dedicated in the Supreme...

Sloka 13

tam ugra tapasam dīptam maharṣim satya vādinam |
pratyuvāca ātmavān rāmo brahmāṇam iva vāsavaḥ || 3-7-13

13. aatmavān = self-respecting one; Rama; ugra tapasā dīptam = by intense, asceticism, radiant one; tam = him; maharṣim = to great sage; satya vādinam = truth, advocate of; prati uvāca = in turn, said; brahmāṇam iva vāsavaḥ = to Brahma, like, Indra.

ṭhat self-respecting Rama replied the radiant sage with intense asceticism, a great soul and an advocate of truth by himself, as Indra would reply to Brahma. [3-7-13]

Sloka 14

aham eva āhariṣyāmi svayam lokān mahāmune |
āvāsam tu aham icchāmi pradiṣṭam iha kānane || 3-7-14

14. mahāmune = oh, great saint; aham eva āharishyāmi svayam = I, alone, will gain, myself; lokān = all worlds; āvasam tu = dwelling place, only; aham icchāmi = I seek; pradiṣṭam iha kānane = indicated [by you,] here, in this forest.

"I alone can gain all those worlds, oh, great saint, I now seek a place to dwell here in this forest as indicated by you... [3-7-14]

▼

This is the same situation with Sage Sharabhanga in the 5th chapter of this canto. This is an allusive saying by Rama. cf. 3-5-33.

Sloka 15

bhavān sarvatra kuśalaḥ sarvabhūta hite rataḥ |
ākhyātaḥ śarabhaṃgena gautamena mahātmanā || 3-7-15

15. bhavān sarvatra kushalaḥ = you are, in all affairs, expert; sarva bhūta hiteḥ rataḥ = all, beings', welfare, interested in; ākhyātaḥ mahātmanā = sharabhangena gautamena = said by, great soul, ṣharabhanga, belonging to sage Gautama's dynasty.

"You are an expert in all affairs, and you are interested in the welfare of all the beings, so said the great souled Sage Sharabhanga of Sage Gautama's dynasty..." [So said Rama to the sage.] [3-7-15]

Sloka 16

evam uktaḥ tu rāmeṇa maharṣiḥ loka viśrutaḥ |
abravīt madhuram vākyam harṣeṇa mahatā yutaḥ || 3-7-16

16. rāmeṇa evam uktaḥ = by Rama, that way, when said; loka vishrutaḥ maharṣiḥ = world, renowned, sage; mahatā harṣeṇa yutaḥ = with great, joy, having; abravīt madhuram vākyam = spoke, sweet worded, sentence.

When said thus by Rama, that great sage with world renown spoke sweet worded sentence with great pleasure. [3-7-16]

Sloka 17

ayam eva āśramo rāma guṇavān raṃyatām iti |
ṛṣi saṃgha anucaritaḥ sadā mūla phalair yutaḥ || 3-7-17

v

"You can take delight in this good featured hermitage alone, for groups of sages will always be moving here, and this always contains tubers and fruits..." Thus said Sage Suteekshna. [3-7-17]

Sloka 18

imam āśramam āgaṃya mṛga saṃghā mahīyasaḥ |
ahatvā pratigacchanti lobhayitvā akutobhayāḥ || 3-7-18

18. imam āshramam āgamya = to this hermitage, having come; mṛiga sanghā mahīyasaḥ = deer, herds, large ones; a hatvā prati gacchanti = without killing, return they go; lobhayitvā = luring us; a kutaḥ bhayāḥ = not, from anyone, fearing [fearing none.]

"But herds of very large animals will be coming to this hermitage, they return after scaring us, of course without killing anyone, and they fear none... [3-7-18]

▼

There is something in this verse. Some translations read mṛiga as deer, while some others read it as animals. If they are just deer, the word a + hatvā , without killing, is of no use, for deer is not a carnivorous animal to kill someone. In other versions, this verse is a complete recast telling that herds of great size animals come and blare, but do not kill or fear any one. Even if it were so, this sage has got a tolerance for them too. And if some latent meaning is picked up, the large herds of deer, moving as they like in this hermitage, are said to be 'luring' the sages, lobhayitbvā. A presumption arises here. This must be the same 'lure' Seetha got on seeing Golden Deer. But Rama raises his bow and arrow to kill these animals that disturb tranquillity of hermitage, whether it be deer or other blaring wild animals. So Rama's fury must be at the 'luring deer', say Golden Deer, but not at ordinary deer. Thus this usage of 'deer and lure' may be taken as lakshaṇa for lakshita of Ramayana.

Sloka 19

nā anyo doṣo bhavet atra mṛgebhyaḥ anyatra viddhi vai |
tat śrutvā vacanam tasya maharṣeḥ lakṣmaṇāgrajaḥ || 3-7-19
uvāca vacanam dhīro vigṛhya sa śaram dhanuḥ |

19, 20a. viddhi vai = you know, thus indeed; atra mṛigebhyaḥ anyatra anya doṣaḥ nā bhavet = here, from animals, other than, other, problem, will not, be there; dhīraḥ lakshmamaṇa agrajaḥ = couraeous one, Lakshmana's, elder - Rama; tasya maharsheḥ tat vacanam shrutvā = of that, great sage, that, sentence, on hearing; sa sharam dhanuḥ vi gṛihya = with, arrow, bow, on taking; uvāca vacanam = said, sentence.

"Know that no other problem is there other than the animals..." ṣo said the sage. On hearing those words of that great sage, that brave elder brother of Lakshmana taking bow and arrow said this sentence. [3-7-19, 20a]

Sloka 20

tān aham sumahābhāga mṛgasaṃghān samāgatān || 3-7-20
hanyām niśita dhāreṇa śareṇa nata parvaṇā |

20b, 21a. su mahā bhāgaḥ = oh, greatly, blessed one; samāgatān tān mṛiga sanghān = that collective come, animal, herds; shareṇa nishita dhāreṇa nata parvaṇā = arrows, having sharp edges, curved, ends; aham hanyām = I will, eradicate.

"I will eradicate them, oh, greatly blessed one, those animals that will come collectively in herds with sharp edged, curve-end arrows... [3-7-20b, 21a]

Sloka 21

bhavān tatra abhiṣajyeta kim syāt kṛcchra taram tataḥ || 3-7-21
etasmin āśrame vāsam ciram tu na samarthaye |

21b, 22a. tatra bhavān abhishajyetaḥ = in them, you may be, interested [mercifully]; tataḥ kṛicchra taram kim syāt = than that, more painful, what, will be, there; [therefore]; etasmin āshrame ciram vāsam na samarthaye = in this, hermitage, for a long, staying, not, befitting [thus I deem.]

"But you may be more interested in those animals mercifully, then what will be there more painful to you, hence I deem our staying in this hermitage for along will be unbefitting..." So said Rama. [3-7-21b, 22a]

Sloka 22

tam evam uktvā uparamam rāmaḥ saṃdhyām upāgamat || 3-7-22
anvāsya paścimām saṃdhyām tatra vāsam akalpayat |
sutīkṣṇasya āśrame raṃye sītayā lakṣmanena ca || 3-7-23

22b, 23. Rama; tam evam uktvā = to him [to sage,] thus, on saying; upa ramam = paused; sandhyām upa āgamat = twilight, nearly, came by; anvāsya pashcimām sandhyām = worshipped, the westward, vesperal time; sītayā lakshmaṇena cha = with ṣeetha, with Lakshmana, too; sutīkshṇasya āshrame ramye = ṣuteekshna's, hermitage, delightful one; tatra vāsam akalpayat = there, stay, arranged for himself.

On saying thus to the sage then Rama came by the twilight, and he paused at it. On worshipping the westward vesperal time, Rama arranged a stay for himself along with Seetha and Lakshmana, there in that delightful hermitage of Sage Suteekshna. [3-7-22b, 23]

Sloka 24

tataḥ śubham tāpasa annam
svayam sutīkṣṇaḥ puruṣarṣabhābhyām |
tābhyām susatkṛtya dadau mahātmā
saṃdhyā nivṛttau rajanīm samīkṣya || 3-7-24

23. tataḥ = then; mahātmā sutīkshaṇaḥ sandhyā nivṛittau = great souled, ṣuteekshna, evening rituals, on completing; rajanīm avekshya = nightfall, on observing; tābhyām puruṣarṣabhābhyām = to two of them, men, best among; su satkṛitya = well, looking after [courteously]; shubham tāpasa annam = auspicious one, sage's, food; svayam = himself; dadau = gave [served.]

ṭhen the great soul ṣage ṣuteekshna on completing his evening rituals and on observing the fall of night he himself courteously served food that is auspicious and worthy of sages to two of the best men, Rama and Lakshmana. [3-7-23]

▼

It may not be construed that Seetha is served with no food, by taking the wording that 'two of best men are served food' by the sage. It is usual for the Indian women to take food after their husbands or other dependents take it first, then all womenfolk in the house join together to have their meal, [for a long time...of course, with their unending chit-chatting...]

.

endnote

In āscharya rāmayana , another traditional rendering of this epic, it is said that Rama, while staying at this hermitage when night advanced, went out of the hermitage and killed all those animals. Not the above mentioned deer, but very big, blaring animals. The thrust of his arrows made the carcasses of those massive animals fall before the demon Kabandha. Kabandha is a cursed angle and will be released of his curse later by Rama. This Kabandha has no body parts below his chest, but has long hands with which he captures his prey that comes within the ambit of his long and strong hands. Kabandha is thus fed upon the kill of Rama, because Kabandha is a keynote character in giving information to Rama, and he is to be kept alive till such time. But all this is unknown to other in-mates of the hermitage, including Seetha. The next morning Rama, in a personal conversation with Seetha, reveals what he has done during the previous night, to her surprise and praise.
