Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 63
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dadhimukha comes to Sugreeva and reports that ṃadhuvana, the grove abounding in honey, has been damaged by monkeys headed by Angada and Hanuma. When Lakshmana in the vicinity enquires Sugreeva about the matter, Sugreeva says that by hearing about the jubilation of the monkeys, it can be concluded that Seetha has been traced out. Assuaging Dadhimukha, Sugreeva asks him to send without delay, Hanuma, Angada and others to him, so as to hear about their efforts in tracing out Seetha.


Sloka 1

tato mūrdhnā nipatitam vānaram vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |
dṛṣṭvā eva udvigna hṛdayo vākyam etat uvāca ha || 5-63-1

1. dṛiṣhṭvaiva = just after seeing; vānaram = Dadhimukha; nipatitam = who fell; mūrdhnā = touching the head (on his feet); vānaram ṛiṣhabhaḥ = ṣugreeva the chef of monkeys; tataḥ = there; uvācha ha = spoke; etat = these; vākyam = words; udvigna hṛidayaḥ = with an agitated mind.

Just on seeing Dadhimukha, who fell touching his feet with his head, ṣugreeva the chief of monkeys, with an agitated mind, spoke the following words:

Sloka 2

uttiṣṭha uttiṣṭha kasmāt tvam pādayoḥ patito mama |
abhayam te bhavet vīra satyam eva abhidhīyatām || 5-63-2

2. vīra = O valiant monkeys!; uttiṣhṭha uttiṣhṭha = stand up; rise!; kasmāt = for what reason; tvam = have you; patitaḥ = fallen; mama pādayoḥ = on my feet?; te abhayam bhavet = I grant you immunity from fear; sarvameva abhidhīyatām = Let the entire truth be spoken!.

"O valiant monkey! ṣtand up, rise! Why have you fallen on my feet? I grant you immunity from fear. Let the entire truth be spoken!"

Sloka 3

sa tu viśvāsitaḥ tena sugrīveṇa mahātmanā |
utthāya ca mahāprājño vākyam dadhi mukho abravīt || 5-63-3

3. vishvāsitaḥ = thus inspired with confidence; mahātmanā tena sugreevaṇa = by that high-souled ṣugreeva; saḥ sumahā prāJṇaḥ dadhimukhaḥ = that vastly intelligent Dadhimukha; utthāya = rising up; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words:

ṭhus inspired by the high-souled ṣugreeva with confidence, that highly intelligent Dadhimukha, rising up, spoke the following words:

Sloka 4

na eva ṛkṣa rajasā rājan na tvayā na api vālinā |
vanam nisṛṣṭa pūrvam hi bhakṣitam tat tu vānaraiḥ || 5-63-4

4. rājā = O king!; vanam = ṃadhuvana; naiva nispṛiṣṭa pūrvam hi = was not indeed set free previously at any time; ṛikṣha rājasā = by Riksharajas; your father; na = nor; tvayā = by you; na = nor vālina api = by Vali eve; tachcha = It; bhakṣhitam = has been consumed; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys.

"O king! ṃadhuvana was indeed not set free previously at any time by Riksharajas, your father, or by you, or by even Vali. That ṃadhuvana has been consumed away by the monkeys."

Sloka 5

ebhiḥ pradharṣitāḥ ca eva vāritā vana rakṣibhiḥ |
madhūni acintayitvā imān bhakṣayanti pibanti ca || 5-63-5

5. vānarāḥ = the monkeys; pradharṣhitāḥ cha eva = when frightened; ebhiḥ vana rakṣhibhiḥ = by these guards of the grove; achintayitvā = disregarding; imān = then; bhakṣhayanti = (they) continue to eat (the fruits); pibanti cha = and drink; madhūni = the honey.

"When these guards of the grove obstructed the monkeys, they disregarded the guards and continue to eat the fruits and drink the honey too."

Sloka 6

śiṣṭam atra apavidhyanti bhakṣayanti tathā apare |
nivāryamāṇāḥ te sarve bhruvau vai darśayanti hi || 5-63-6

6. apavidhyanti = some are throwing away; shiṣhṭam = the remnant (after drinking) of the honey; atra = there; tathā = and; apare = some others; bhakṣhayanti = are consuming (that remnant); nivāryamāṇāḥ = (when) prevented (by us); te sarve = all of them; darshayanti hi = are indeed showing; bhruvaḥ = their eye-brows.

"ṣome are throwing away a remnant of honey, after drinking it there and some others are consuming that remnant. When prevented by us, all of them are indeed exhibiting their eye-brows to us (in anger)."

Sloka 7

ime hi samrabdhatarāḥ tathā taiḥ sampradharṣitāḥ |
vārayanto vanāt tasmāt kruddhaiḥ vānara pumgavaiḥ || 5-63-7

7. ime = (when) these guards of the grove; samrabdhatarāḥ = are more angry; tathā = and; vārayantaḥ = keeping them off; tasmāt vānāt = from that grove; sampradharṣhitāḥ = they were threatened; taiḥ kruddhaiḥ vānarapuṇ^gavaiḥ = by those enraged monkeys.

"When these guards of the grove became angry and tried to keep them off from the grove, those enraged monkeys threatened them."

Sloka 8

tataḥ taiḥ bahubhiḥ vīraiḥ vānaraiḥ vānara ṛṣabhāḥ |
samrakta nayanaiḥ krodhādt harayaḥ sampracālitāḥ || 5-63-8

8. vānara ṛiṣhabhāḥ = O chief of monkeys!; taiḥ vīraiḥ bahubhiḥ vānaraiḥ = eyes; by those valiant monkeys in large numbers; samrakta nayanaiḥ = having red-eyes; harayaḥ = these monkeys; tataḥ = then; pravichālitāḥ = were driven away; krodhāt = with anger.

"O chief of monkeys! ṭhen, those valiant monkeys in large numbers, having their red eyes in anger, drove away these monkeys."

Sloka 9

pāṇibhiḥ nihatāḥ kecit kecij jānubhiḥ āhatāḥ |
prakṛṣṭāḥ ca yathā kāmam deva mārgam ca darśitāḥ || 5-63-9

9. kechit = some; nihatāḥ = were struck down; pāṇibhiḥ = with their hands; kechit = some; āhatāḥ = were beaten; jānubhiḥ = with their knees; prakṛiṣhṭāḥ = being dragged; yathā kāmam = as liked by them; darshitāḥ cha = they were tossed into; deva mārgam = air.

"ṭhose monkeys struck some of the guards with their hands. ṭhey beat some of them with their knees. They dragged some of them and tossed them into air."

Sloka 10

evam ete hatāḥ śūrāḥ tvayi tiṣṭhati bhartari |
kṛtsnam madhu vanam caiva prakāmam taiḥ prabhakṣyate || 5-63-10

10. tvayi tiṣhṭhati bhartari = while you are in the position of a king; ete = these; shūrāḥ = valiant guards; hatāḥ = were beaten; taiḥ = by those monkeys; kṛitsnam = the entire; madhu vanam chaiva = ṃadhuvana too; prakāmam prabhakṣyate = is being eaten completely.

"While you are in the position of a king, those monkeys have beaten these valiant guards and are eating away the entire ṃaduvana too."

Sloka 11

evam vijñāpyamānam tu sugrīvam vānara ṛṣabham |
apṛcchat tam mahāprājño lakṣmaṇaḥ para vīrahā || 5-63-11

11. tam sugrīvam = to that ṣugreeva; vānara ṛṣhabham = the excellent of monkeys; vijJṇāpyamānam = who was being reported; evam = thus; (by Dadhimukha); lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; para vīrahā = the killer of hostile heroes; mahāprājJṇaḥ = and a very wise man; apṛichchhat = asked (as follows):

Lakshmana, the killer of hostile heroes and a very wise man, asked that ṣugreeva, the excellent of monkeys (as follows) while Dadhimukha was thus reporting to him:

Sloka 12

kim ayam vānaro rājan vanapaḥ pratyupasthitaḥ |
kam ca artham abhinirdiśya duhkhito vākyam abravīt || 5-63-12

12. rājam = O king!; kim = why; ayam vānaraḥ = has this monkey; vanapaḥ = the garden-protector; pratyupasthitaḥ = come (here)?; abhinirdishya = pointing out; kam = what; artham = matter; duḥkhitaḥ = has this monkey in grief; abravīt = spoken; vākyam = some words (to you)?

"O king! Why has this monkey the garden-protector, come here?' Pointing out what matter has this monkey in grief, spoken these words to you?"

Sloka 13

evam uktaḥ tu sugrīvo lakṣmaṇena mahātmanā |
lakṣmaṇam pratyuvāca idam vākyam vākya viśāradaḥ || 5-63-13

13. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; mahātmanā lakṣhmaṇena = by the high souled Lakshmana; sugrīvaḥ vākyam vishāradaḥ = the eloquent ṣugreeva; pratyuvācha = replied; laKṣhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; idam = these; vākyam = words.

ḥearing the words of the high-souled Lakshmana, the eloquent ṣugreeva replied to him as follows:

Sloka 14

ārya lakṣmaṇa samprāha vīro dadhi mukhaḥ kapiḥ |
angada pramukhaiḥ vīraiḥ bhakṣitam madhu vānaraiḥ || 5-63-14
vicitya dakṣiṇāmāśāmāgatairharipuṅgavaiḥ |

14. ārya lakṣhmaṇa = O esteemed Lakshmana!; dadhi mukhaḥ = Dadhimukha; vīraḥ = the valiant; kapiḥ = monkey; samprāha = is informing; hari puṇ^gavaiḥ = that by the foremost of monkeys; āgataiḥ = who returned; vichitya = having searched (for ṣeetha); dakṣhiṇām āshām = from southern direction; aṇ^gada pramukhaiḥ vīraiḥ = accompanied by Angada and other heroes; madhu = honey; bhakṣitam = was consumed.

"O esteemed Lakshmana! Dadhimukha, the valiant monkey is informing that the foremost of monkeys, who returned from the southern direction, after searching for Seetha as also Angada and other heroes consumed honey."

Sloka 15

na eṣām akṛta kṛtyānām īdṛśaḥ syāt upakramaḥ || 5-63-15
āgataiśca pramathitam yathā madhuvanam hi taiḥ |
dharṣitaṃ ca vanam kṛtsnamupayuktam ca vānaraiḥ || 5-63-16

15; 16. yathā = in which manner; taiḥ āgataiḥ = by those returned; vānaraiḥ = monkeys; kṛitsnam vanam = the entire grove; pramathitam = was destroyed; dharṣhitam = overpowered; upayuktam = and used; (tathā = seeing that manner); eṣhām = these monkeys; na syāt = would not have indulged; īdṛishaḥ upakramaḥ = in such an exploit; akṛita kṛityānām = had they not accomplished my purpose.

"By seeing the manner in which these returned monkeys overpowered, destroyed and behaved towards ṃadhuvana, we can deduce that the monkeys would not have indulged in such an exploit, had they not accomplished my purpose."

Sloka 17

vanam yathā abhipannam taiḥ sādhitam karma vānaraiḥ |
dṛṣṭā devī na saṃdeho na ca anyena hanūmatā || 5-63-17

17. yadā = when; te = those monkeys; abhipannāḥ = have overpowered; anam = ṃadhuvana; (then); karma = the task; sādhitam = has been accomplished; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys; devī = ṣeetha; dṛiṣhṭā = was found; na sandehaḥ = there is no doubt; na = none; anyena = other; hanumatā = than ḥanuma.

"When those monkeys have overpowered ṃadhuvana, then it means that our task has been accomplished by the monkeys. Seetha was found. There is no doubt. None other than Hanuma must have seen Seetha."

Sloka 18

na hi anyaḥ sādhane hetuḥ karmaṇo asya hanūmataḥ |
kārya siddhiḥ hanumati matiḥ ca hari pumgava || 5-63-18
vyavasāyaḥ ca vīryam ca śrutam ca api pratiṣṭhitam |

18. na hi anyaḥ = there is indeed none other; hanūmataḥ = than ḥanuma; hetuḥ = who is the executor; in accomplishing; asya karmaṇaḥ = this work; tasmin vānarapuṇ^gave = In that ḥanuma; the foremost among the monkeys; kārya siddhiḥ = accomplishment of any object; matiḥ cha iva = intelligence; vyavasāyāḥ cha = strenuous effort; vīryam cha = vigour; shrutam cha api = and learning; pratiṣhṭhitam = are well established in his life.

"ṭhere is none other than, ḥanuma who is the executor in accomplishing this work. In that Hanuma, the foremost among the monkeys, capacity to accomplish any object; intelligence; strenuous, vigour effort; and learning are well established in his life."

Sloka 19

jāmbavān yatra netā syāt angadasya bala īśvaraḥ |
hanūmān ca api adhiṣṭhātā na tasya gatiḥ anyathā || 5-63-19

19. yatra = where; jāmbavān = Jambavan; netā syāt = is a leader; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; aṇ^gadashcha = Angada also (is a leader); hanumāmshcha = and ḥanuma; adhiṣhṭhātā = is an administrator; gatiḥ = the progress; tasya = of that work; na anyathā = cannot be otherwise.

"Where Jambavan as well as Angada are leaders and ḥanuma is an administrator, the progress of that work cannot be as otherwise."

Sloka 20

angada pramukhaiḥ vīraiḥ hatam madhu vanam kila || 5-63-20
vāritāḥ sahitāḥ pālāḥ tathā jānubhiḥ āhatāḥ |

20. madhuvanam hatam kila = It is so reported that ṃadhuvana has been destroyed; vīraiḥ = by the heroes; aṇ^gada pramukhaiḥ = with Angada as the chief; vārayantaḥ cha = the garden guards who wee preventing it; sahitāḥ = together; tadā = then; āhatāḥ = were beaten up; jānubhiḥ = with knees.

"It is so reported that ṃadhuvana has been destroyed by the heroes with Angada as the chief and the garden-guards who were together preventing the damage, were beaten up with knees."

Sloka 21

etat artham ayam prāpto vaktum madhura vāg iha || 5-63-21
nāmnā dadhi mukho nāma hariḥ prakhyāta vikramaḥ |

21. vaktum = to tell; etat artham = this matter; madhura vāk = in sweet words; hariḥ = the monkeys; dadhimukho nāma nāmnā = renowned by Dadhimukha by name; prakhyāta vikramaḥ = with a celebrated prowess; prāptaḥ = has come; iha = here.

"ṭo tell this matter in sweet words, the monkey renowned by Dadhimukha by name, with his celebrated prowess, has come here."

Sloka 22

dṛṣṭā sītā mahābāho saumitre paśya tattvataḥ || 5-63-22
abhigamya yathā sarve pibanti madhu vānarāḥ |

22. mahābāho = O the long-armed; saumitre = Lakshmana!; sītā = ṣeetha; dṛiṣhṭā = was found; pashya = perceive; tattvataḥ = the truth; pashya = see (it); tathā = and; sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; abhigamya = meeting together; pibanti = are drinking; madhu = honey.

"O the long-armed Lakshmana! Perceive the truth that ṣeetha has been seen and see that all monkeys, getting together, are drinking honey."

Sloka 23

na ca api adṛṣṭvā vaidehīm viśrutāḥ puruṣa ṛṣabha || 5-63-23
vanam dātta varam divyam dharṣayeyuḥ vana okasaḥ |

23. puruṣha ṛiṣhabha = O Lakshmana; the foremost among men!; vishrutāḥ = (if) the renowned; vana okasaḥ = monkeys; adṛiṣhṭvā = have not seen; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; na dharṣhayeyuḥ = (they) would not have abused; vanam = the grove; datta varam = which was granted as a boon; divyam = and is charming.

"O Lakshmana, the foremost among men! If the renowned monkeys have not seen ṣeetha, they would not have abused the charming grove; which was granted as a boon."

Sloka 24

tataḥ prahṛṣṭo dharma ātmā lakṣmaṇaḥ saha rāghavaḥ || 5-63-24
śrutvā karṇa sukhām vāṇīm sugrīva vadanāc cyutām |
prāhṛṣyata bhṛśam rāmo lakṣmaṇaḥ ca mahāyaśāḥ || 5-63-25

24; 25. tataḥ = then; dharma ātmā = the righteous; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; saha rāghavaḥ = together with Rama; prahṛiṣhṭaḥ = felt delighted; shrutvā = hearing; vāṇīm = the words; chyutām = coming; sugrīva vadanāt = from the mouth of ṣugreeva; karṇa sukhām = pleasant for the ears; rāmaḥ = Rama; prāhṛiṣhyata = was rejoiced; mahābalaḥ = the mighty lakṣmaṇashcha = Lakshmana too; bhṛisham = was very much; prāhṛiṣhyate = rejoiced.

ṭhen, the righteous Lakshmana, together with Rama, felt delighted. ḥearing the words coming from the mouth of Sugreeva, which were pleasant for the ears, Rama was rejoiced. The mighty Lakshmana too was very much rejoiced.

Sloka 26

śrutvā dadhi mukhasya idam sugrīvaḥ tu prahṛṣya ca |
vana pālam punaḥ vākyam sugrīvaḥ pratyabhāṣata || 5-63-26

26. shrutvā = hearing; idam = these words; dadhimukhasya = of Dadhimukha; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva with a charming neck; samprahṛiṣhya = was very much pleased; punaḥ = and further; pratyabhāṣhataḥ = spoke; vākyam = the following words; vana pālam = to the guardian of the grove.

ḥearing those words of Dadhimukha, ṣugreeva with a charming neck, was very much pleased and further spoke to Dadhimukha, the guardian of the grove (as follows):

Sloka 27

prīto asmi saumya yat bhuktam vanam taiḥ kṛta karmabhiḥ |
marṣitam marṣaṇīyam ca ceṣṭitam kṛta karmaṇām || 5-63-27

27. saḥ aham = I; prītaḥ asmi = am pleased; yat = that; vanam = ṃadhuvana; bhuktam = has been enjoyed; taiḥ = by those monkeys; kṛita karmabhiḥ = who have accomplished their act; cheṣhṭitam = the act (damage to the grove); kṛita karmaṇām = done by those monkeys who have accomplished their purpose; marṣhaṇīyam = is fit to be forgiven; marṣhitam = and it is pardoned.

"I am happy that ṃadhuvana has been enjoyed by those monkeys, who have accomplished their act. Since the act (damage to the grove) done by those monkeys, who have accomplished their purpose is fit to be forgiven, I pardon them.

Sloka 28

icchāmi śīghram hanumat pradhānān |
śākhā mṛgān tān mṛga rāja darpān |
draṣṭum kṛta arthān saha rāghavābhyām |
śrotum ca sītā adhigame prayatnam || 5-63-28

28. rāghavābhyām saha = I; along with Rama and Lakshmana; shīghram = soon; ichchhami = desire; draṣhṭum = to see; tān = those; shākhā mṛigān = monkeys; hanumat pradhānān = with ḥanuma as their chief; mṛiga rāja darpān = having the pride of a lion; kṛita arthān = and who attained their object; shrotum cha = and to hear; prayatnam = about their effort; sītā adhigame = in finding out ṣeetha.

"I, together with Rama and Lakshmana, soon desire to see those monkeys, with ḥanuma as their chief, who having acquired the pride of a lion and who attained his purpose; and also to hear about their effort in finding out Seetha."

Sloka 29

prītisphītākṣau samprahṛṣṭau kumārau |
dṛṣṭvā siddhārthau vānarāṇām ca rājā |
aṅgaiḥ samhṛṣṭaiḥ karmasiddhim viditvā |
bāhvoorāsannām so.atimātram nananda || 5-63-29

29. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; kumārau = these princes; prītisphītākṣhau = with their delighted expansive eyes; samprahṛiṣhṭau = who are thrilled with joy; siddhārthau = and who accomplished their aim; saḥ = that; vānarāṇām rājā = king of the monkeys; atimātram nananda = was very much pleased; viditvā = knowing; karmasiddhim = that accomplishment of their work; āsannām = is coming close; bāhvoḥ = to their hands; aṇ^gaiḥ = and with his limbs; samhṛiṣhṭaiḥ = thrilled with joy.

ṣeeing Rama and Lakshmana with their eyes filled with ecstatic delight, the princes, who were thrilled with joy and who accomplished their aim, Sugreeva that king of the monkeys was very much delighted, knowing that accomplishment of their work is coming close to their hands and his limbs were thrilled with joy.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe triṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 63nd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
