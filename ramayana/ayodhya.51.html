Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 51
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Guha requests Lakshmana to take rest, duly expressing his readiness to guard Rama and his consort by keeping awake the whole night. Lakshmana expressed his unwillingness to lie down in the presence of his elder brother and sister in law and preferred to remain awake. He spends night talking with Guha, voicing grief for his royal father as well as his affectionate mothers.

Sloka 1

tam jāgratam adambhena bhrātur arthāya lakṣmaṇam |
guhaḥ samtāpa samtaptaḥ rāghavam vākyam abravīt || 2-51-1

1. guhaḥ = Guha; santāpa santaptaḥ = distressed with anguish; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; tam Lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; rāghavam = who was born in Raghu dynasty; jāgratam = who kept awake; adambhena = bereft of hypocrisy; arthāya = for the sake of; bh

Distressed with anguish (to see Rama and Sita lying on the ground) Guha said to Lakshmana, the scion of Raghu, who kept awake, through sincere love, for the protection of his brother Rama.

Sloka 2

iyam tāta sukhā śayyā tvad artham upakalpitā |
pratyāśvasihi sādhv asyām rāja putra yathā sukham || 2-51-2

2. iyam = "ṭhis is; sukhā = a comfortable; upakalpitā = made; tvadartham = for you; tāta = Oh friend; pratyāshvasihi = relax; sādhu = well; yathāsukham = comfortably; asyām = on it; rājaputra = Oh; prince!"

"Here is a comfortable bed made for you, my friend! Relax well comfortably on it, Oh, prince!"

Sloka 3

ucitaḥ ayam janaḥ sarvaḥ kleśānām tvam sukha ucitaḥ |
gupti artham jāgariṣyāmaḥ kākutsthasya vayam niśām || 2-51-3

3. sarvaḥ = All; ayam janaḥ = these people; uchitaḥ = are habituated; kleshānām = to hardships. tvam = You; sukhochitaḥ = are habituated to comforts. Guptyartham = For the protection; kākutstsya = of Rama; vayam = we; jāgarishhyāmaḥ = shall keep awake; nishām = (this) night."

"All of us are habituated to hardships. You are habituated to comforts. We shall keep awake this night, for the protection of Rama"

Sloka 4

na hi rāmāt priyataraḥ mama asti bhuvi kaścana |
bravīmy etat aham satyam satyena eva ca te śape || 2-51-4

4. kashchana nāstihi = " ṇone is; priyatamaḥ = dearer; mama = to me; rāmāt = than Rama; bhuvi = in this world. aham = I; bravīmi = speak; etat = this; satyam = truth; shape = and swear; te = to you; satyenaina = by truth."

"For, none is dearer to me than Rama in this world. I speak the truth and swear to you by truth."

Sloka 5

asya prasādāt āśaṃse loke asmin sumahad yaśaḥ |
dharma avāptim ca vipulām artha avāptim ca kevalām || 2-51-5

5. āshamse = I hope; sumahat = abundant; yashaḥ = acclaim; asmin loke = in this world; dharma vāptimcha = acquisition of ṣupreme merit; vipulām = (and) extensive; arthāvāptimcha = acquirment of wealth; prasādāt = by the grace; kevalam = alone; asya = of Rama.

"I hope to acquire abundant acclaim and supreme merit in this world as also full reward of wealth, by the sole grace of Rama"

Sloka 6

so aham priya sakham rāmam śayānam saha sītayā |
rakṣiṣyāmi dhanuṣ pāṇiḥ sarvataḥ jñātibhiḥ saha || 2-51-6

6. saḥ aham = "As such; I; G^yātibhiḥ = along with my kindred; rakshhishhyāmi = shall protect; priya sakham = my dear friend; rāmam = Rama; shayānam = who is sleeping; sītayā saha = with ṣita; sarvataḥ = in every way; dhanushhpāṇiḥ = with bow in hand."

"As such, I along with my kindred, shall protect my dear friend Rama who is reposing with Sita in every way, with bow in hand."

Sloka 7

na hi me aviditam kiṃcit vane asmimḥ carataḥ sadā |
catur angam hi api balam sumahat prasahemahi || 2-51-7

7. "nahi kimchit = ṇothing is indeed; aviditam = un known; asmin vane = in theforest; me = to me; charataḥ = where I wander; sadā = continually. prasahemahi = We are able to withstand; sumahat = a vast; balam api = army too; chaturaṇ^gam = comprising four parts (elephants; chariots; horsemen; and foot soldiers)."

"Nothing is indeed unknown in this forest to me, where I wander continually. We can able to withstand even a vast army too, comprising of four parts (elephants, chariots, cavalry, and infantry)."

Sloka 8

lakṣmaṇaḥ tam tadā uvāca rakṣyamāṇāḥ tvayā anagha |
na atra bhītā vayam sarve dharmam eva anupaśyatā || 2-51-8

8. tadā = then; lakshhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; uvācha = replied; tam = to Guha; (as follows): anagha = "Oh; sinless Guha! rakshhyamāṇaḥ = Being protected; tvayā = by you; dharmameva = who keep your duty alone; anupashyatā = in view; vayam sarve = all of us are ; na bhītāḥ = not afraid; atre = in this land."

Then, Lakshmana replied to Guha as follows: "Oh, sinless Guha! Being protected by you, who keep your duty alone in view, all of us are fearless in this land."

Sloka 9

katham dāśarathau bhūmau śayāne saha sītayā |
śakyā nidrā mayā labdhum jīvitam vā sukhāni vā || 2-51-9

9. dāsharathou = "when Rama; the son of Dasaratha; shayāne = is lying; bhūmou = on the ground; sītayā saha = along with ṣita; katham = how; shakyā = is it possible; mayā = to me; nidrā = the sleep; jīvitam vā = or the enjoyment of life; sukhāni vā = or the amenities of life?"

"When Rama, the son of Dasaratha is lying on the ground with Sita, how is it possible for me to sleep or to enjoy the pleasures of life?"

Sloka 10

yo na deva asuraiḥ sarvaiḥ śakyaḥ prasahitum yudhi |
tam paśya sukha samviṣṭam tṛṇeṣu saha sītayā || 2-51-10

10. "pashya = ṣee; tam = that Rama; yaḥ = who; na shakyaḥ = cannot be; prasehitum = vanquished;yudhi = in combat; sarvaiḥ = by all; devāsuraiḥ = the gods and demons; sukha samvishhṭam = sleeping profoundly; tṛiṇeshhu = on the grass; sītayā saha = along with ṣita."

"See that Rama, who cannot be vanquished in combat even by the gods and demons combined, now sleeping profoundly on the grass along with Sita."

Sloka 11

yo mantra tapasā labdho vividhaiḥ ca pariśramaiḥ |
eko daśarathasya eṣa putraḥ sadṛśa lakṣaṇaḥ || 2-51-11
asmin pravrajitaḥ rājā na ciram vartayiṣyati |
vidhavā medinī nūnam kṣipram eva bhaviṣyati || 2-51-12

11;12. asmin = this Rama as such; yaḥ = who; sadṛisha lakshhaṇaḥ = is endowed with characteristics alike; dasarathasya = of Dasaratha; ekaḥ = uniquely; ishhṭaḥ = liked; putraḥ = the son; labdhaḥ = obtained (by Dasaratha); mantra tapasā = by virtue of his charity of sacred texts and austerities; vividhaiḥ = (and) by various; parishramaishcha = undertakings; (in the form of sacrificial performances);pravrājite = has gone to exile;rājā = the king; na vartayishhyati = will not live; chiram = long; medinī = (and) the earth; nūnam = will surely; bhavishhyati = become; vidhavā = widowed; kshhiprameva = forthwith.

When Rama- who is endowed with similar characteristics as Dasaratha, uniquely beloved, the son obtained by Dasaratha by virtue of his chanting of several sacred texts and austerities and by various undertakings (in the form of sacrificial performances) has gone to exile, the king will not live long and the earth will become surely widowed forthwith.

Sloka 13

vinadya sumahā nādam śrameṇa uparatāḥ striyaḥ |
nirghoṣa uparatam tāta manye rāja niveśanam || 2-51-13

13. vinadya = having cried out; sumahara nādam = in a high-pitched tone; striyaḥ = the women; shrameṇa = exhausted; uparatāḥ = will have fallen silent; ataḥ = for that reason; manye = I think; nirghoshhoparatam = a profound stillness reigns; rājaniveshanam = in the palace.

Having cried out in a high-pitched tone, the women having exhausted, will have fallen silent and I am sure that a profound stillness reigns in the palace.

Sloka 14

kausalyā caiva rājā ca tathaiva jananī mama |
na āśaṃse yadi jīvanti sarve te śarvarīm imām || 2-51-14

14. nāshamse = I do not expect; kausalyāchaiva = Kausalya; rājācha = the king; tathaiva = and; mama jananī = my mother; te sarve = all of them; yadi jīvanti = to remain alive; imām = this; sharvarīm = night.

"I do not expect Kausalya, Dasaratha and my mother all of them to remain alive for this night."

Sloka 15

jīved api hi me mātā śatrughnasya anvavekṣayā |
tat duhkham yat tu kausalyā vīrasūr vinaśiṣyati || 2-51-15

15. anvavekshhayā = by looking for; shatrughnasya = ṣatrughna; me mātā = my mother; jīvedapi hi = may indeed stay alive. tat = It will be; duḥkham = painful; Kausalyā = (that) Kausalya; vīrasūḥ = who has given birth to a hero; yat vinashishhyati = may perish.

"By looking forward to meet Satrughna, my mother might even stay alive. But it will be painful if Kausalya who has given birth to a heroic son, dies."

Sloka 16

anurakta jana ākīrṇā sukha āloka priya āvahā |
rāja vyasana saṃsṛṣṭā sā purī vinaśiṣyati || 2-51-16

16. anuraktajanākīrṇā = filled with devoted people; sukhā = a source of joy; lokapriyāvahā = and which brought pleasure to the world; sā purī = that city of Ayodhya; rāja vyasana samsṛishhṭā = when seized with sorrow over the king's death; vinashishhyati = will perish.

That city of Ayodhya, filled with devoted people, hitherto a source of joy and which brought pleasure to the world, when seized with agony over the king's death, will perish.

Sloka 17

katham putram mahātmānam jyeṣṭham priyamapasyataḥ |
śarīram dhārayuṣyānti prāṇā rājño mahātmanaḥ || 2-51-17

17. katham = how; apashyataḥ = not seeing; putram = his son; mahātmānam = the magnanimous; jyeshhṭam = and the first born; prāṇāḥ = will the vital airs ; dhārayishhyanti = be maintained; sharīram = in the body; rāG^yaḥ = of the king; mahātmanaḥ = the generous?"

"How, in the absence of his magnanimous and the first born son, will the vital airs in the body of the generous king be maintained?"

Sloka 18

vinaṣṭe nṛpatau paścātkausalyā vinaśiṣyati |
anantaram ca mātā.api mama nāśamupaiṣyati || 2-51-18

18. vinashhṭe = after the death; nṛipatou = of the king; kausalyā = Kausalya; vinashishhyati = will die; pashchāt = from behind. anantaramcha = Afterwards; mama mātā.api = my mother also; upaishhyati = will obtain; nāsham = death."

"After the death of king, Kausalya will die. ṃy mother also will die thereafter.

Sloka 19

atikrāntam atikrāntam anavāpya manoratham |
rājye rāmam anikṣipya pitā me vinaśiṣyati || 2-51-19

19. anavāpya = not attaining; manoratham = his desire( to enthrone Rama) atikrāntamatikrāntam = going astray again and again; anikshhipya = and without installing; rāmam = Rama; rājye = in the kingdom; me pitra = my father; vinashishhyati = will perish."

"Having failed to install Rama in the kingdom, failed forever, which was the most cherished wish of his heart, my father will leave this world.

Sloka 20

siddha arthāḥ pitaram vṛttam tasmin kāle hi upasthite |
preta kāryeṣu sarveṣu saṃskariṣyanti bhūmipam || 2-51-20

20. siddhārthāḥ = those who are fortunate; samskarishhyanti = will consecrate; vṛttam = deceased; pitaram = father; bhūmipam = and the king; sarveshhu pretakāryeshhu = in the course of all funeral rites; tasmin kale = (when) the hour; vyupasthite = has struck."

"Those who are fortunate will consecrate our deceased father and the king in the course of all funeral rites, when the hour has struck.

Sloka 21

ramya catvara saṃsthānām suvibhakta mahā pathām |
harmya prasāda sampannām gaṇikā vara śobhitām || 2-51-21
ratha aśva gaja sambādhām tūrya nāda vināditām |
sarva kalyāṇa sampūrṇām hṛṣṭa puṣṭa jana ākulām || 2-51-22
ārāma udyāna sampannām samāja utsava śālinīm |
sukhitā vicariṣyanti rāja dhānīm pitur mama || 2-51-23

21;22;23. sukhitāḥ = the happy people; vicharishhyanti = will move; rājadhānīm = in the capital; mama pituḥ = belonging to my father; ramya chatvara samsthānām = with its quadrangular places allocated at lovely sites; suvibhakta mahāpathām = well- aligned; wide roads; harmya prāsāda sampannām = rich in mansions of well-to-do men and temples; gaṇikāvara shobhitām = adorned with the foremost of courtesans; rathāshvagaja sambādhām = its chariots; horses and elephants that obstruct the roads; tūrya nāda vināditām = the musical instruments that resound there; sarva kalyāṇa sampūrṇām = full of all blessings; hṛishhṭa pushhṭa janākulām = crowded with merry and well- fed men; ārāmodyāna sampannāam = well- provided with gardens and royal parks; samājotsava shālinīm = (and) bright with festivities carried on under the patronage of associations.

People will joyfully move in the capital belonging to my father and the city with its quadrangular places allocated at lovely sites, and well- aligned roads, rich in mansions of well-to-do men, temples and royal palaces adorned with the foremost of courtesans, its chariots horses and elephants that obstruct the roads, the musical instruments that resound there- full of all blessings and crowded with merry and well fed men, well- provided with gardens and royal parks and bright with festivities carried on under the patronage of associations.

Sloka 24

api jīveddhaśaratho vanavāsātpunarvayam |
pratyāgamya mahātmānamapi paśyema suvratam || 2-51-24

24. dasharathaḥ api jīvet = Will Dasaratha remain alive? pratyāgamya = After returning; vanavāsāt = from exile; vayam api pashyema = can we see; mahātmānam = the high-soled king; suvratam = of noble vows; punaḥ = again?

Will Dasaratha remain alive? After returning from exile, can we see the high-soled king Dasaratha of noble vows again?

Sloka 25

api satya pratijñena sārdham kuśalinā vayam |
nivṛtte vana vāse asminn ayodhyām praviśemahi || 2-51-25

25. nivṛittavanavāse = after completion of the exile in the forest; vayamapi praveshemahi = can we enter; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; kushalinā = safely; asmin = with Rama; satyapratiG^ena sārdham = who is faithful to his promises.

Can we safely return to Ayodhya, after completion of the exile in the forest along with Rama who is faithful to his promises?

Sloka 26

paridevayamānasya duhkha ārtasya mahātmanaḥ |
tiṣṭhataḥ rāja putrasya śarvarī sā atyavartata || 2-51-26

26. mahātmanaḥ = (while) the high-soled; rāja putrasya = Lakshmana;paridevayāmānasya = thus lamenting; tishhṭataḥ = stood on guard; duḥkhārtasya = afflicated with anguish as he was; sā sharvarī = that night; atyavartata = rolled away.

While the high-soled Lakshmana thus lamenting, stood on ground, afflicted with anguish as he was, that night rolled away.

Sloka 27

tathā hi satyam bruvati prajā hite |
nara indra putre guru sauhṛdāt guhaḥ |
mumoca bāṣpam vyasana abhipīḍitaḥ |
jvarā āturaḥ nāgaiva vyathā āturaḥ || 2-51-27

27. narendra putre = (while) Lakshmana (son of Dasaratha); prajāhite = who was concerned with the welfare of the people; bruvati = was speaking; satyam = the truth; tathā = thus; gurusouhṛidāt = out of his affection for his elder brother (Rama); guhaḥ = Guha; vyathāturaḥ = overcome with agony; vyasanābhi pīditah = oppressed with adversity; mumocha = shed; bāshhpam = tears; nāgāḥ iva = like an elephant; jvarāturaḥ = tormented with fever.

While Lakshmana (son of Dasaratha), who was concerned with the welfare of the people; was thus speaking the truth out of his affection for his elder brother (Rama), Guha, overcome with agony and oppressed with adversity shed tears like an elephant tormented with fever.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekapaṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 51th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
