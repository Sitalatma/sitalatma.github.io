Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Kishkindha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 45
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama orders ten of the monkey-generals to search for the whereabouts of Indrajit. But Indrajit arrested the approach of those monkeys, by means of his arrows. Both Rama and Lakshmana were transfixed by Indrajit with a net work of serpentine arrows into the vital parts of Rama and Lakshmana and they fall down on the battle-ground in a bath of blood. Seeing those two scions of Raghu in that state, the monkeys give way to utter despondence.

Sloka 1

sa tasya gatim anviccan rāja putrah pratāpavān |
dideśa atibalo rāmo daśa vānara yūthapān || 6-45-1

1. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; raja putraḥ = the son of Dasaratha; pratāpavān = the powerful man; ati balaḥ = and a very strong man; didesha = directed; dasha = ten; vānarayuthapān = monkey-generals; anvichchhan = to search; tasya = his; gatim = course of movement.

That very strong and powerful Rama the son of Dasaratha ordered ten monkey-generals to search for the whereabouts of Indrajit.

Sloka 2

dvau suṣeṇasya dāyādau nīlam ca plavaga ṛṣabham |
angadam vāli putram ca śarabham ca tarasvinam || 6-45-2
dvinatam jāmbavantam ca sānuprastham mahā balam |
ṛṣabham ca ṛṣabha skandham ādideśa param tapaḥ || 6-45-3

2; 3. paramtapaḥ = Rama; who torments his enemies; ādidesha = ordered; dvau = both; dāyādau = the sons; suṣeṇasya = of ṣushena; nīlamcha = ṇila; plavagādhipam = the chief of monkeys; aṇgadam = Andaga; vāliputram = the son of Vali; tarasvinam = the strong; sharabhamcha = ṣharabha; dvividam cha = Divivda; hanūmantam = ḥanuman; mahābalam = the very strong; sānuprastham = ṣamprastha; ṛiṣabham cha = Rishabha; ṛiṣabha skandham = and Rishabhaskandha.

Rama the scourger of his enemies ordered both the sons of Sushena, Nila the chief of monkeys, Angada the son of Vali, the stron Sharabha, Dvivida, Hanuman, the very strong Sanuprastha, Rishabha and Rishabha skandha.

Sloka 4

te samprahṛṣṭā harayo bhīmān udyamya pādapān |
ākāśam viviśuh sarve mārgāmāṇā diśo daśa || 6-45-4

4. te sarve harayaḥ = all those monkeys; samprahṛiṣṭāḥ = thrilled with enthusiasm; udyamya = lifted; bhīmān = terrific; pādapān = trees; mārgamāṇāḥ = searching; dasha = in ten; dishaḥ = directions; vivishuḥ = entered; ākāsham = the air.

All those monkeys, thrilled with enthusiasm, flung into the air brandishing huge trunks of trees in order to explore the ten regions.

Sloka 5

teṣām vegavatām vegam iṣubhir vegavattaraiḥ |
astravit parama astreṇa vārayām āsa rāvaṇiḥ || 6-45-5

5. rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; the son of Ravana; astravit = who was skilled in the use of magic weapons; iṣubhiḥ = by means of his arrows; vegavattaraiḥ = with great speed (released) parama astraiḥ = from the most excellent of bows; vārayāmāsa = arrested; teṣām = their; vegavatām = impetuous; vegam = outbreak.

Indrajit, the son of Ravana, who was skilled in the use of magic weapons, by means of his arrows with great speed, released from his most excellent of bows, arrested the impetuous outbreak of the monkeys.

Sloka 6

tam bhīma vegā harayo nārācaih kṣata vikṣatāḥ |
andha kāre na dadṛśur meghaih sūryam iva āvṛtam || 6-45-6

6. harayaḥ = the monkeys; bhīmavegāḥ = of terrific bound nārāchaiḥ kṣata vigrahāḥ = whose bodies were cruelly pierced by those shafts; na dadṛishaḥ = were unable to see; tam = Indrajit; andhakāre = in the darkness; sūryamiva = as the sun; (is obscured); āvṛitam = when veiled; meghaiḥ = in clouds.

Those monkeys of terrific bound, whose bodes were cruelly pierced by those shafts, were unable to see Indrajit in the darkness, as the sun is obscured when veiled in clouds.

Sloka 7

rāma lakṣmaṇayor eva sarva marma bhidah śarān |
bhṛśam āveśayām āsa rāvaṇih samitim jayaḥ || 6-45-7

7. rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; the son of Ravana; samitimjayaḥ = victorious in battle; āveshayāmāsa = caused to enter; sharān = the arrows; sarva dehabhidaḥ = which split up the flesh; bhṛisham = in great measure; rāma lakṣmaṇayoreva = into Rama and Lakshmana exactly.

Indrajit, the victorious in battle, transfixed Rama and Lakshmana with those arrows that lacerated their flesh in great measure.

Sloka 8

nirantara śarīrau tu bhrātarau rāma lakṣmaṇau |
kruddhena indrajotā vīrau pannagaih śaratām gataiḥ || 6-45-8

8. indrajitā = by Indrajit; kruddhena = the enraged demon; ubhau = both; tau = those; vīrau = warriors; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; nirantara sharīrau = whose bodies were densely transfixed with arrows; pannagaiḥ = by serpants; gataiḥ = which obtained; sharatām = the form of arrows.

The bodies of both those warriors, Rama and Lakshmana were densely transfixed with serpentine arrows by the enraged Indrajit.

Sloka 9

tayoh kṣataja mārgeṇa susrāva rudhiram bahu |
tāv ubhau ca prakāśete puṣpitāv iva kiṃśukau || 6-45-9

9. rudhiram = blood; susrāva = flowed; bahu = profusely; tayoḥ = from their; kṣata mārgeṇa = wound-marks; ubhau = both; tau = of them; prakāshitau = shone; kimshukau iva = like Kimshuka trees; puṣpitau = in following.

Blood flowed from the wound-marks of both Rama and Lakshmana and both of them shone like Kimshuka trees in flowring.

Sloka 10

tataḥ paryanta rakta akṣo bhinna anjana caya upamaḥ |
rāvaṇir bhrātarau vākyam antardhāna gato abravīt || 6-45-10

10. tataḥ = at that instant; paryanta raktākṣaḥ = his eyes inflamed; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; Ravana's son; bhīnnaṇ^jana chayopamaḥ = which resembled a mass of collyrium mixed with oil; antardhāna gataḥ = though still invisible; abravīt = spoke; vākeyam = the following words; bhrātarau = those two brothers.

At that instant, though still invisible, Indrajit, Ravana's son, with his inflamed eyes, which resembled a mass of collyrium mixed with oil, spoke the following words to those two brothers.

Sloka 11

yudhyamānam anālakṣyam śakro api tridaśa īśvaraḥ |
draṣṭum āsāditum vā api na śaktah kim punar yuvām || 6-45-11

11. yudhyamānam = when I enter into combat; anālakṣyam = making myself invisible; shakro.api = even Indra; tridasheshvaraḥ = the lord of celestials; na shaktaḥ = is not bale; draṣṭum = to see; āsāditum vāpi = or approach (me); kim punaḥ = how much less; yuvām = you two!.

"When I enter into combat, making myself invisible, even Indra the lord of celestials is not able to see or approach me. How much less, you two!"

Sloka 12

prāvṛtāv iṣu jālena rāghavaukanka patriṇā |
eṣa roṣa parīta ātmā nayāmi yama sādanam || 6-45-12

12. rāghavau = O; Descendents of Raghu!; prāvṛitau = having imprisoned you; aviṣujālena = in this net work of arrows; kaṇ^kapatriṇā = furnished with heron's feathers; eṣaḥ = this I; roṣa parītātmā = yielding myself up to the violence of my wrath; nayāmi = am about to dispatch you; yamasādanam = to the region of Yama the Lord of Death.

"O, Descendents of Raghu! Having imprisoned you in this net work of arrows furnished with heron's feathers, I, yielding myself up to the violence of my wrath, am about to dispatch you to the region of Yama the Lord of Death."

Sloka 13

evam uktvā tu dharmajnau bhrātarau rāma lakṣmaṇau |
nirbibheda śitair bāṇaih prajaharṣa nanāda ca || 6-45-13

13. uktvā = speaking; evam = thus; bhrātarau = to the brothers; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; dharmajJṇau = who wee aware of righteousness; (Indrajit); nirbibheda = pierced (them); shitaiḥ = with pointed; bāṇaiḥ = arrows; nanādacha = and shouted too; prajaharṣa = exultantly.

Speaking thus to the brothers Rama and Lakshmana, who were aware of righteousness, Indrajit pierced them with pointed arrows and shouted too exultantly.

Sloka 14

bhinna anjana caya śyāmo visphārya vipulam dhanuḥ |
bhūyo bhūyah śarān ghorān visasarja mahā mṛdhe || 6-45-14

14. bhinnāṇ^jana chaya shyāmaḥ = Indrajit; who was as black as a heap of shattered collyrium; visphārya = stretching; vipulam = his immense dhanuḥ = now; visasarja = discharged; ghorān = formidable; sharān = arrows; bhūya eva = even once more; mahā mṛidhe = in that great fight.

Indrajit, who was as black as a heap of shattered collyrium, stretching his immense bow, discharged formidable arrows even once more, in that great fight.

Sloka 15

tato marmasu marmajno majjayan niśitān śarān |
rāma lakṣmaṇayor vīro nanāda ca muhur muhuḥ || 6-45-15

15. vīraḥ = that warrior; Indrajit; marma jJṇaḥ = who was aware of the vital parts; muhurmuhuḥ = set up a continual; nanāda cha = shouting; majjayan = digging; nishitān = sharp; sharān = arrows; rāmalakṣmaṇayoḥ = marmasu = into the vital parts of Rama and Lakshmana.

That warrior, Indrajit, who was aware of their vital parts, set up a continual shouting, digging sharp arrows into the vital parts of Rama and Lakshmana.

Sloka 16

baddhau tu śara bandhena tāv ubhau raṇa mūrdhani |
nimeṣa antara mātreṇa na śekatur udīkṣitum || 6-45-16

16. tau ubhau = those two princes; raṇamūrdhāni = in the forefront of battle; baddhau tu shara bandhena = bounded by the net of arrows; nimeṣāntara mātreṇa = in the twinkling of an eye; na shekatuḥ = became incapable; avekṣitum = of even looking up.

Those two princes, in the forefront of battle, bounded by that net work of arrows in the twinkling of an eye, became incapable of even looking up.

Sloka 17

tato vibhinna sarva angau śara śalya ācitāv ubhau |
dhvajāv iva mahā indrasya rajju muktau prakampitau || 6-45-17
tau sampracalitau vīrau marma bhedena karśitau |
nipetatur mahā iṣvāsau jagatyām jagatī patī || 6-45-18

17; 18. marma bhedena = pierced in their vital parts; karshitau = exhausted; kṛitau = made; sharashalya achitau = covered by heads of arrows; vibhinna sarvāṇ^gau = all over the different limbs; tau = those two; maheṣvāsau = wielding mighty arches; jagatī patī = who were the lords of the earth; nipetutuḥ = fell; jagatyām = to the earth; samprachalitau = shaking violently; mahendrasya dhvajāmiva = like a pair of flag-staffs raised in honour of Indra the Lord of celestials; rajju muktau = and freed from their chords.

Pierced in their vital parts, exhausted, and covered all over with heads of arrows, those two mighty and courageous archers fell to the earth, they who were the lords of the earth, shaking violently like a pair of flag-staffs in honour of Indra the Lord celestials and freed from their raised of chords.

Sloka 19

tau vīra śayane vīrau śayānau rudhira ukṣitau |
śara veṣṭita sarva angāv ārtau parama pīḍitau || 6-45-19

19. tau vīrau = those warriors; shayanau = lying; vīra shayane = on that heroes' bed; rudhirokṣitau = bathed in blood; sharaveṣṭita sarvāaṇ^gau = all their limbs bristling with arrows; parama pīDitau = and extremely injured; ārtau = felt distress.

Those warriors, Rama and Lakshmana, lying on that heroes' bed (on the battle-ground), bathed in blood, all their limbs bristling with arrows and extremely injured, felt distressed.

Sloka 20

na hy aviddham tayor gātram babhūva angulam antaram |
na anirbhinnam na ca astabdham ā kara agrād ajihmagaiḥ || 6-45-20

20. na babhūva = there was not; aṇ^gula mātram = a finger's breadth; antaram = of space; tayoḥ = on their; gātre = bodies; ākārāgrāt = from the tips of their fingers to the end of their feet; anirbhinnam = that was not lacerated; na = nor; astabdham = not implanted; aviddham = and not pierced; ajihmagaiḥ = by those arrows.

There was not a finger's breadth on their bodies form the tips of their fingers to the end of their feet that was not lacerated, implanted and pierced by those arrows.

Sloka 21

tau tu krūreṇa nihatau rakṣasā kāma rūpiṇā |
asṛk susruvatus tīvram jalam prasravaṇāv iva || 6-45-21

21. nihatau = struck down; krūreṇa = by that ferocious; rakṣasā = demon; kāma rūpiṇā = able to change his shape at will; tīvram = the hot; asṛik = blood; susruvatuḥ = gushed forth; tau = from both Rama and Lakshmana; jalam iva = as water; prasravaṇau = from a spring.

Struck down by that ferocious demon, who was able to change his shape at will, the hot blood gushed forth from both Rama and Lashmana, as water from a spring.

Sloka 22

papāta prathamam rāmo viddho marmasu mārgaṇaiḥ |
krodhād indrajitā yena purā śakro vinirjitaḥ || 6-45-22

22. rāmaḥ = Rama; papāta = fell; prathamam = first; marmasu = his vital parts; viddhaḥ = pierced; mārgaṇaiḥ = by arrows; krodhāt = of the wrathful; indrajitā = Indrajit; yena = by whom; shakraḥ = Indra the Lord of celestials; purā = had been formerly; vinirjitaḥ = vanquished.

Rama fell first, his vital parts pierced by arrows of the wrathful Indrajit, who had formerly vanquished Indra the Lord of celestials.

Sloka 23

rukmapuṅkheḥ prasannāgrairadhogatibhirāśugaiḥ |
nāracair ardha nārācair bhallair anjalikair api || 6-45-23
vivyādha vatsa dantaiś ca simha damṣṭraih kṣurais tathā |

23. (Indrajit); vivyādha = pierced; (Rama) (by arrows); rukma puṇ^khaiḥ = with golden shafts; prasanna agraiḥ = with sharp points; adhogatibhiḥ = which had downward movement; āshugaiḥ = which go fast; nārachaiḥ = ṇarachas; ardha nārāchaiḥ = Demi-naraches; bhallaiḥ = Bhallas (with wide tips); aṇjalikairapi = Anjalis; vatsa dantaishcha = Vatsadantas; simha damṣṭraiḥ = ṣimhadantas; tathā = and; kṣuraiḥ = those shafts like unto razors.

Indrajit pierced Rama by arrows with golden shafts, with sharp points, which had downward movement, which go fast, Narchas, Demi-narachas, Bhallas (with wide tips), Anjalis, Vatsa dantas, Simha dantas and those shafts like unto razors.

Sloka 24

sa vīra śayane śiśye vijyam ādāya kārmukam || 6-45-24
bhinna muṣṭi parīṇāham triṇatam rukma bhūṣitam |

24. āvidhya = throwing asunder; kārmukam = his bow; vijyam = string-less; bhinna muṣṭiparīṇāham = detached from the hold of his fist; trinatam = and which was bent at three places; rukma bhūṣitam = adorned with gold; saḥ = that Rama; shishye = lied down; vīrashayane = on heroes bed (on the battle-ground).

Throwing asunder his bow bent at three places, adorned with gold, with its string loosened away and detached from the hold of his fist, Rama lied down on the battle-ground.

Sloka 25

bāṇa pāta antare rāmam patitam puruṣa ṛṣabham || 6-45-25
sa tatra lakṣmaṇo dṛṣṭvā nirāśo jīvite abhavat |

25. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; puruṣarṣabham = Rama the excellent among men; patitam = fallen; tatra = there; bāṇa pātāntare = at a distance of a range of an arrow; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; abhavat = became; nirāshaḥ = the one without any hope; jīvite = in life.

Seeing Rama the excellent man fallen, at a distance of an arrows range, Lakshman became hopeless about his own life.

Sloka 26

rāmam kamalapatrākṣaṃ śarabandhaparikṣatam || 6-45-26
śuśoca bhrātaraṃ dṛṣṭvā patitam dharaṇītale |

26. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing bhrātaram = his elder brother; rāmam = Rama; kamala patrākṣam = with his eyes resembling lotus-leaves; patitam = having fallen; dharaṇītale = on the ground; sharabandha parikṣatam = wounded by a net work of arrows; (Lakshmana); shushocha = lamented.

Seeing his elder brother Rama, with his eyes resembling lotus-leaves, having fallen on the ground, wounded as he was by a net work of arrows, Lakshmana felt sad.

Sloka 27

harayaścāpi tam dṛṣṭvā samtāpam paramaṃ gatāḥ || 6-45-27
śokārtāścukruśurghoramaśrupūritalocanāḥ |

27. dṛiṣṭvā = beholding; tam = that Rama; harayashchāpi = the monkeys also; gatāḥ = obtained; paramam = great; samtāpam = grief; ashrupūrita lochanāḥ = with their eyes filled in tears; shokārtāḥ = afflicted by sorrow; ghoram = and terribly; chakmshuḥ = wept.

Beholding that Rama, the monkeys too were in great grief and wept terribly, with their eyes filled in tears, being afflicted as they were by sorrow.

Sloka 28

baddhau tu vīrau patitau śayānau |
tau vānarāh samparivārya tasthuh |
samāgatā vāyu suta pramukhyā |
viṣadam ārtāh paramam ca jagmuh || 6-45-28

28. te vānarāḥ = ṭhose monkeys; vāyusuta pramukhyāḥ = with ḥanuman in first place; samāgatāḥ = gathered at one place; tasthuḥ = and stood; samparivārya = surrounding; tau = both those Rama and Lakshman; baddhau = bound by a net work of arrows lying down; vīrashayane = on the battle-ground; jagmuḥ cha = (they) ārtāḥ = were disturbed; jagmuḥ cha = and obtained; paramam = a great; viṣādam = grief.

Those monkeys with Hanuman in first place gathered at a place and stood surrounding Rama and Lakshmana, who wee bound by a net work of arrows and lying down on the battle ground. They were disturbed and afflicted sorrow.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe pañcacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 45th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
