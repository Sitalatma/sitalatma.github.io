Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 47
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana summons some female demons including Trijata and instructs them to take Seetha in Pushpaka-plane to the battle-front and show Rama and Lakshmana alleged to have been killed by Indrajit. Accordingly, the female-demons take Seetha in Pushpaka-plane and show her Rama and Lakshmana lying unconscious on a bed of arrows in the battle-field. Imagining Rama and Lakshmana to have been dead, Seetha bursts into sobs.

Sloka 1

tasmin pratipraviṣṭe lankām tu kṛta arthe rāvaṇa ātmaje |
rāghavam parivārya ārtā rarakṣur vānara ṛṣabhāḥ || 6-47-1

1. tasmin = (while) that; rāvaṇātmaja = Indrajit; the son of Ravana; kṛitārthe = accomplished his purpose; praviṣṭe = and entered; laṇ^kāyām = Lanka; vānaraṣabhāḥ = the leading mokeys; atha = then; parivārya = having surrounded; rāghavam = Rama; rarakṣuḥ = and protected him.

Indrajit, the son of Ravana having returned to Lanka, his purpose accomplished, the leading monkeys surrounded Rama in order to watch over him.

Sloka 2

hanūmān angado nīlah suṣeṇah kumudo nalaḥ |
gajo gava akṣo gavayah śarabho gandha mādanaḥ || 6-47-2
jāmbavān ṛṣabhah sundo rambhah śata balih pṛthuḥ |
vyūḍha anīkāś ca yattāś ca drumān ādāya sarvataḥ || 6-47-3
vīkṣamāṇā diśah sarvās tiryag ūrdhvam ca vānarāḥ |
tṛṇeṣv api ca ceṣṭatsu rākṣasā;iti menire || 6-47-4

2; 3; 4. hanuman = ḥanuman; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; ṇīlaḥ = ṇila; suṣeṇaḥ = ṣushena; kumudaḥ = Kumuda; nalaḥ = ṇala; gajaḥ = Gaja; gavākṣaḥ = Gavaksha; panasaḥ = Panasa; mahā hariḥ = the mighty monkey; sānaprasthaḥ = ṣanuprastha; jāmbavān = Jambavan; ṛiṣabhaḥ = Rishabha; sunaḥ = ṣunda; rambhaḥ = Rmaba; shatabaliḥ = ṣhatabali; pṛithuḥ = Prithu; vānarāḥ = (these) monkeys; ādāya = armed with; drumān = trees; vyūDhānīkāḥ = reorganized their ranks; sarvataḥ = on all sides;yathāshcha = alert; vīkṣamāṇāḥ = surveyed; dishaḥ = the quarters of the sky; ūrdhvam cha = upwards; tiryak = and horizontally; sarvataḥ = and on every side; tṛiṇeṣvapi = and even if a grass; cheṣṭatsu = stirred; menire = (they) exclaimed; rākṣasāḥ iti = "It is a demon!"

Hanuman, Angada, Nila, Sushena, Kumuda, Nala, Gaja, Gavaksha, Panasa, Sanuprastha and the mighty Jambavan with Sunda, Rambha, Shatabali and Prithu all these monkeys, armed with trees, reorganized their ranks, stood alert, surveyed the quarters of the sky up and down and on every side and, even if a grass stirred, they exclaimed, "It is a demon!"

Sloka 5

rāvaṇaś ca api samhṛṣṭo visṛjya indrajitam sutam |
ājuhāva tatah sītā rakṣaṇī rākṣasīs tadā || 6-47-5

5. rāvaṇashchāpi = Ravana too; samhṛiṣṭaḥ = full of joy; visṛijya = dismissed; sutam = his son; indrajitam = Indrajit; tataḥ = and thereafter; ājuhāva = summoned; rākṣasīḥ = the female-demons; sītā rākṣaṇīḥ = who guarded ṣeetha; tadā = then.

Ravana, meanwhile, full of joy, dismissed his son Indrajit and thereafter summoned the female demons who guarded Seetha.

Sloka 6

rākṣasyas trijaṭā ca api śāsanāt tam upasthitāḥ |
tā uvāca tato hṛṣṭo rākṣasī rākṣasa īśvaraḥ || 6-47-6

6. shāsanāt = as per his orders; rākṣasyaḥ = the female-demons; trijaṭā chāpi = along with ṭrijata; upasthitāḥ = appeared; tam = before him; tataḥ = then; hṛiṣṭaḥ = the rejoiced; rākṣasādhipaḥ = Ravana; uvācha = spoke; tāḥ rākṣasādhipaḥ = Ravana; uvācha = spoke; tāḥ rākṣasīḥ = to those female-demons (as follows)

Following his orders, the female-demons along with Trijata appeared before him. Then, the rejoiced Ravana said to them as follows:

Sloka 7

hatāv indrajitā ākhyāta vaidehyā rāma lakṣmaṇau |
puṣpakam ca samāropya darśayadhvam hatau raṇe || 6-47-7

7. ākhyāta = tell; vaidehyāḥ = ṣeetha; rāma lakṣmaṇau = that Rama and Lakshmana; hatau = have been killed; indrajitā = by Indrajit; samāropya = having made to ascend; tat = that; puṣpakam = aeroplane; Pushpaka; darshayadhvam = show; hatau = Rama and Lakshmana; killed; raṇe = in battle.

"Tell Seetha that Rama and Lakshmana have been killed by Indrajit. Take her in Pushpaka the aeroplane and show her Rama and Lakshman who were killed in battle."

Sloka 8

yad āśrayād avaṣṭabdho na iyam mām upatiṣṭhati |
so asyā bhartā saha bhrātrā nirasto raṇa mūrdhani || 6-47-8

8. yadāshrayāt = on whose shelter; iyam = she; avaṣṭabdhā = feels proud; na upatiṣṭhate = and does not come near; mām = me; saḥ = that; asyāḥ bhartāḥ = her husband; nihataḥ = was killed; bhrātrā saha = along with his brother; raṇamūrdhani = in the battle-front.

"Her husband, the one depending on whom rendered her so proud that she refused to be united with me, lies there killed with his brother in the battle-front."

Sloka 9

nirviśankā nirudvignā nirapekṣā ca maithilī |
mām upasthāsyate sītā sarva ābharaṇa bhūṣitā || 6-47-9

9. sītā = ṣeetha; maithilī = the princess of ṃithila; sarvābharaṇa bhūṣitā = adorned herself with all kinds of ornaments; upasthāsyate = willl submit before; mām = me; nirvishankā = without any apprehension; nirudvignā = without grief; nirapekṣā = and without any hope of reunion.

"From now on, free from anxiety, grief and expectation of reunion, Seetha the princess of ṃithila kingdom, adorned in all her jewels, will submit herself to me."

Sloka 10

adya kāla vaśam prāptam raṇe rāmam salakṣmaṇam |
avekṣya vinivṛtta āśā na anyām gatim apaśyatī || 6-47-10
anapekṣā viśālākṣī māmupasthasyate svayam |

10. avekṣya = beholding; rāmam = Rama; salakṣmaṇam = with Lakshmana; gatam = having obtained; kālavasham = the dominion of death; adya = today; sā = she; vishālākṣī = the large-eyed woman; vinivṛittā = will return; upasthāsyate = and submit before; mām = me; svayam = herself; apashyatī = without finding; anyām = any other; gatim = haven; anapekṣā = and hoping for nothing else .

"Beholding Rama and Lakshmana fallen under the sway of death on the battle-field today, finding no other haven and hoping for nothing else, the large-eyed Seetha will voluntarily seek refuge with me!"

Sloka 11

tasya tad vacanam śrutvā rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 6-47-11
rākṣasyas tās tathā ity uktvā prajagmur yatra puṣpakam |

11. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = that command; tasya rāvaṇasya = of that Ravana; durātmanaḥ = the curel demon; tāḥ rākṣasya = those female-demons; uktvā = having replied; tathā iti = "so it be"; jagmuḥ vai = and went; yatra = there; where; puṣpakam = Pushpaka-plane was there.

Hearing these words of the cruel Ravana, they all replied, "Be it so" and went to where the Pushpaka chariot was.

Sloka 12

tataḥ puṣpakam ādaya rākṣasyo rāvaṇa ājnayā || 6-47-12
aśoka vanikāsthām tām maithilīm samupānayan |

12. tataḥ = thereafter; rākṣasyaḥ = the female-demons; ādāya = brought; puṣpakam = the aerial car; Pushpaka; rāmaṇājJṇayā = as per the instructions of Ravana; samupānayan = and carried it near; tām = tat; maithilīm = ṣeetha; ashokavanikā; samsthām = who was staying in Ashoka grove.

Thereafter, the female-demons brought the aerial car, Pushpaka as per the instructions of Ravana and carried it nearer to Seetha who was staying in Ashoka-grove.

Sloka 13

tām ādāya tu rākṣasyo bhartṛ śoka parāyaṇām || 6-47-13
sītām āropayām āsur vimānam puṣpakam tadā |

13. tadā = then; rākṣasyaḥ = the female demons; ādāya = brought; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; bhartṛishoka parājitām = who was afflicted with grief for her husband; āropayāmāsuḥ = and made to ascend; puṣpakam vimānam = Pushpaka -plane.

The female-demons brought Seetha who was afflicted with grief for her husband and made her to ascend Pushpaka-plane.

Sloka 14

tataḥ puṣpakam āropya sītām trijaṭayā saha || 6-47-14
jagmurdarśayituṃ tasyai rākṣasyo rāmalakṣmaṇau |

14. tataḥ = then; rākṣasyaḥ = the female-demons; trijaṭayāsaha = along with ṭrijata; āropya sītām = made ṣeetha to ascend; puṣpakam = Pushpaka-plane; jagmuḥ = and proceeded; darshayitum = to show; tasyai = her; rāma lakṣmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana.

The female-demons along with Trijata made Seetha to ascend Pushpaka-plane and proceeded to show her Rama and Lakshmana.

Sloka 15

rāvaṇo akārayal lankām patākā dhvaja mālinīm || 6-47-15
prāghoṣayata hṛṣṭaś ca lankāyām rākṣasa īśvaraḥ |
rāghavo lakṣmaṇaś caiva hatāv indrajitā raṇe || 6-47-16

15; 16. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣaseshvaraḥ = the king of demons; hṛiṣṭaḥ = thrilled with rapture; kārayāmāsa = made; laṇ^kām = Lanka; patāka dhvaja mālinīm = garlanded with flags and banners; prāghoṣayata = and caused a proclamation; (to be made in Lanka); announcing that; rāghavaḥ = Rama; lakṣmaṇashcha = and Lakshmana; hatau = had been slain; raṇe = in battle; indrajitā = by Indrajit.

Ravana the king of demons, thrilled with rapture, caused Lanka to be garlanded with flags and banners and arranged a proclamation to be made in Lanka announcing that Rama and Lakshmana had been slain by Indrajit in battle.

Sloka 17

vimānena api sītā tu gatvā trijaṭayā saha |
dadarśa vānarāṇām tu sarvam sinyam nipātitam || 6-47-17

17. sītā = ṣeetha; trijaṭayā saha = along with ṭrijata; gatvā = went; vimānena = by that plane; dadarsha = and saw; sarvam = all; vānarāṇām sainyam = the monkey-troops; nipātitam = who had been slain.

Seetha along with Trijata, transported by that plane, saw all the monkey-troops who had been slain.

Sloka 18

prahṛṣṭa manasaś ca api dadarśa piśita aśanān |
vānarāṃś ca api duhkha ārtān rāma lakṣmaṇa pārśvataḥ || 6-47-18

18. dadarsha = (ṣeetha) saw; pishitāshanān = the demons; prahṛiṣṭamanasaḥ = who were delighted at heart; vānarāmshcha = and monkeys; atiduḥkhārtān = who were disturbed with immense grief; rāmalakṣmaṇa pārshvataḥ = by the side of Rama and Lakshmana.

Seetha saw the demons who were delighted at heart and monkeys disturbed with grief, standing round Rama and Lakshmana.

Sloka 19

tataḥ sītā dadarśa ubhau śayānau śata talpayoḥ |
lakṣmaṇam caiva rāmam ca visamjnau śara pīḍitau || 6-47-19
vidhvasta kavacau vīrau vipraviddha śara āsanau |
sāyakaiś cinna sarva angau śara stambhamayau kṣitau || 6-47-20

19; 20 tataḥ = then; sītā = ṣeetha; dadarsha = beheld; ubhau vīrau = those two warriors; rāmam cha = Rama; lakṣmaṇamchaiva = and Lakshmana; shayanau = lying; shara pīDitau = pierced with arrows; visamjJṇau = unconscious; shara pīDitau = riddled with weapons; vidhvasta kavachau = their armour shattered; vipraviddha sharāsanau = their bows thrown at a distance; chhinna sarvāṇ^gau = their entire body transfixed; sāyakaiḥ = by darts; sharastambamayau = on a bed of arrows; kṣitau = on the ground.

Then, Seetha beheld those two warriors Rama and Lakshmana lying unconscious on the ground on a bed of arrows, their limbs pierced with arrows, riddled with weapons, their armour shattered, their bows cast aside at a distance and their entire body transfixed by darts.

Sloka 21

tau dṛṣṭvā bhrātarau tatra pravīrau puruṣa ṛṣabhau |
śayānau puṇḍarīkākṣau kumārāviva pāvakī || 6-47-21
śaratalpagatau vīrau tathābhūtau nararṣabhau |
duhkha ārtā subhṛśam sītā karuṇam vilalāpa ha || 6-47-22

21; 22. dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing; tau bhrātarau = those two brothers; pravīrau = who were filled with valour; puṇDarīkākṣau = having lotus-eyes; puruṣarṣabhau = the excellent of men; shayanau sharatalpagatau = lying stretched on a bed of arrows; tatra = there; tathā bhūtau = in that wretched plight; kumārāviva = like the two sons; pāvakī = of the fire-god (shakha and Vishakha) (lying on a bed of reeds); sītā = ṣeetha; vilalāpaha = wailed; karuṇam = piteously; subhṛisham duḥkhartā = stricken with a great agony.

On seeing those two brothers, who were filled with valour, having lotus-eyes, the excellent of men, lying streteched on a bed of arrows there in that wretched plight like the two sons of the fire-god (Shkha and Vishakha) lying on a bed of reeds, Seetha wailed piteously, stricken as she was with a great agony.

Sloka 23

bhartāramanavadyāṅgī lakṣmaṇam cāsitekṣaṇā |
prekṣya pāṃsuṣu ceṣṭantau ruroda janakātmajā || 6-47-23

23. asitekṣaṇā = the dark-eyed; janakātmajā = ṣeetha the daughter of Janaka; anavadyāṇgī = with faultless limbs; prekṣya = beholding; bharatāram = her lord; lakṣmaṇam cha = and Lakshmana; cheṣṭantau = lying pāmsuṣu = in the dust; ruroda = burst into sobs.

The dark-eyed Seetha the daughter of Janaka with her faultless limbs, beholding her lord and Lakshmana lying in the dust, burst into sobs.

Sloka 24

sā bāṣpa śoka abhihatā samīkṣya |
tau bhrātarau deva sama prabhāvau |
vitarkayantī nidhanam tayoh sā |
duhkha anvitā vākyam idam jagāda || 6-47-24

24. samīkṣya = witnessing; tau bhrātarau = those brothers; devasuta prabhāvau = the prominent sons of god; sā = that ṣeetha; sabāṣpa shokābhihatā = struck by grief with tears; vitarkayantī = believing; tayoḥ = their; nidhanam = death; jagāda = spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words; duḥkhānvitā = possessed with grief.

Witnessing those brothers, the prominent sons of god, Seetha afflicted with tearful sorrow, believing their death, spoke with grief the following words.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe saptacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 47th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
