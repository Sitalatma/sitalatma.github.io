Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 20
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama approaches his mother Kausalya to inform her of the calamity. Kausalya on hearing the news, faints. On recovering, she laments in the most grevious tones to Rama.

Sloka 1

tasmiṃstu puruṣvyāghre niṣkrāmati kṛtāñjalau |
ārtaśacdo mahān jajñe strīṇāma ntaḥpure tadā || 2-20-1

1. tadā = ṭhen; tasmin = that Rama; purushha vyāaghre = the best of men; nishhkrāmati = While leaving; kṛitānjalou = after offering salutation; srīṇām = among women; antaḥpura = in the palace; jajJṇe = there was; mahān = great ārtashsbdaḥ = cry of pain.

While Rama, the best of men was leaving, after offering salutation to his father, women in the palace were weaping loudly.

Sloka 2

kṛtyeṣvacoditaḥ pitrā sarvasyāntaḥ purasya ca |
gatiryaḥ śaraṇam cāsīt sa roomo.adya pravatsyati || 2-20-2

2. yaḥ = which Rama; achoditaḥ = without being prompted; pitrā = by father kṛityeshhu = in actions; āsīt = became; gatiḥ = the recourse; sharaṇamcha = and protector; sarvasya = to entire; anta purasya = palace; saḥ rāmaḥ = such Rama; pravatsyati = is going to exile; adya = now.

Which Rama, without being told by his father, used to do necessary activities and protect the entire palace, is about to go to exile now".

Sloka 3

kausalyāyām yathā yukto jananyām vartate sadā |
tathaiva vartate.asmāsu janmaprabhṛti rāghavaḥ || 2-20-3

3. rāghavaḥ = Rama; janma prabhṛiti = since he was born; vartate = was behaving; tathaiva = in the same way; asmāsu = towards us; yathā = as how; yuktaḥ = attentive; sadā = always; kausalyām = towards Kausalya; jananyām = who was the mother.

"Rama, since he was born, was showing the same attention to us, as he was showing to his mother Kausalya."

Sloka 4

na krudhyatyabhiśasto.api krodhanīyāni varjayan |
kṛddhān prasādayan sarvān sa ito.adya pravatsyati || 2-20-4

4.varjayan = used to avoid; krodhayāni = actions which created anger; prasādayani = soothed; sarvān = all; kruddhān = who were angry; na kruddhyati = was not furious; abhishasto api = even if abused; saḥ = ṣuch Rama Pravatsyati = is going to exile; adya = to day; itaḥ = from here.

"He used to avoid actions which created anger and soothed all those who were angry. Rama was not furious with those who abused him. Such Rama is going today to exile."

Sloka 5

abhuddhirbata no rājā jīvalokam caratyayam |
yo gatim sarvabhūtānām parityajati rāghavam || 2-20-5

5. yaḥ = Which Dasaratha parityajati = by abonding rāghavam = Rama gatim = who was helpful sarvabhūtānām = to all beings ayam = this naḥ rajahā = our king abuddhiḥ = who is stupid; charati = is offending jīvalokam = all beings."

"Our stupid king by abandoning Rama who was helpful to all beings , is offending all the beings."

Sloka 6

iti sarvā mahiṣyastā vivatsā iva dhenavaḥ |
patimācukruśuścaiva sasvaram cāpi cukruśuḥ || 2-20-6

6. iti = ṭhus sarvāḥ = all tāḥ = those mahishhaḥ = king's consorts chukrushuḥcha api = were weaping sasvaram = with sound; dhenavaḥ = who lost their calves āchukrushushcha eva = also abused patim = husband.

Thus, all the king consorts while weaping loudly, as cows which lost their Calves, abused their husband .

Sloka 7

sa hi cāntaḥ pure ghoramārtaśabdham mahīpatiḥ |
putraśokābhisantaptaḥ śrutvā vyālīyatāsane || 2-20-7

7. shrutva = After hearing ārta shabdam = the distressful sounds yhoram = which was horrible; antaḥpura = in the palace mahīpatiḥ = king Dasaratha vyālīyata = clung; āsane = to the seat putra shokābhisantaptaḥ = with extreme anguish for his son.

After hearing the terrific distressful crying sounds in the palace, Dasaratha clung to the seat, with extreme anguish for his son.

Sloka 8

rāmaḥ tu bhRśam āyastaḥ nihśvasann iva kunjaraḥ |
jagāma sahitaḥ bhrātrā mātur antaḥ puram vaśī || 2-20-8

8. rāmastu = Rama; be it so; bhRisham = greatly āyastaḥ = hurt; niḥshvasan = sighed kuṇ^jaraḥiva = like elephant; vashī = subdued the senses jagāma = went antaḥpuram = to the palace mātuḥ = of mother bhrātrā sahitaḥ = along with brother.

Rama, who was greatly hurt, sighed like an elephant, subdued his senses and went to his mother's palace along with Lakshmana

Sloka 9

so apaśyat puruṣam tatra vRddham parama pūjitam |
upaviṣṭam gRha dvāri tiṣṭhataḥ ca aparān bahūn || 2-20-9

9. saḥ = Rama apashyat = saw parama pūjitam = much-worshipped vriddham purusham = old man upavishṭam = sitting gṛihadvāri = at the gateway; bahūm = many aparāmshcha = others tishṭataḥ = standing tatra = there.

Rama saw a much worshipped old man sitting at the gateway of the house and many other standing there.

Sloka 10

dṛṣṭvai tu tadā rāmaṃ te sarve sahasotthitāḥ |
jayena jayatām śreṣṭham vardhayanti sma rāghavam || 2-20-10

10. tadā = ṭhen; dṛiṭvaiva = after seeing rāmam = Rama te sarve = all of them utthitāḥ = rose up sahasā = quickly vardhayantisma = exhilarating their spirits jayena = by uttering "ḥail! Glory!" rāghavam = to Rama jayatam shreshṭam = the most triumphant man.

After seeing Rama, all of them rose quickly and greeted Rama the most triumphant man, by uttering the words "ṃay you have increased success!"

Sloka 11

praviśya prathamām kakṣyām dvitīyāyām dadarśa saḥ |
brāhmaṇān veda sampannān vRddhān rājñā abhisatkRtān || 2-20-11

11. saḥ = ṭhat Rama pravishya = entered prathamām kakshyām = the first enclosure; dadarsha = saw dvitīyām = at the second gate vriddhāan = old brāhmaṇān = brahmanas; veda sampannān = perfect in Vedas abhisatkRitān = honoured rāGyā = by king.

Rama crossed the first gate and saw old Brahmanas who were perfect in Vedas and duly honoured by the king.

Sloka 12

praṇamya rāmaḥ tān vRddhāṃs tRtīyāyām dadarśa saḥ |
striyo vRddhāḥ ca bālāḥ ca dvāra rakṣaṇa tatparāḥ || 2-20-12

12. rāmaḥ = Rama pranamya = saluted tān = those viprān = brahmanas; dadarsha = saw vṛiddhāḥ = old striyaḥ = women tathā = and balāḥ = girls dvāra rakshaṇa tatparāḥ = who were interested in guarding the gate tṛitīyāyām = the third enclosure.

Rama offered salutation to those brahmanas and saw old women and girls, guarding the gate in the third enclosure.

Sloka 13

vardhayitvā prahRṣṭāḥ tāḥ praviśya ca gRham striyaḥ |
nyavedayanta tvaritā rāma mātuḥ priyam tadā || 2-20-13

13. tadā = ṭhen tāḥ = those striyaḥ = women; prahṛishṭāḥ = having been glad; vardhayatvā = greeted Rama with word of success; tvaritāḥ = quickly pravishya = entered gṛiham = house; nyavedayasta = informed rāmamātuḥ = Kausalya; the mother of Rama priyam = the pleasing news.

Then those women, having been delighted, greeted Rama with good word of success, quickly entered the house and informed Kausalya about the lovely arrival of Rama .

Sloka 14

kausalyā api tadā devī rātrim sthitvā samāhitā |
prabhāte tu akarot pūjām viṣṇoh putra hita eṣiṇī || 2-20-14

14. tadā = at that time; kausalyā = Kausalya sthitvā = having stayed samāhitā = steadfast rātrim = all the night; akarot = performed pujām = worship vishhnoḥ = to Vishnu prabhāte = at dawn putrahi taishhiṇī = for the welfare of the son.

At that time, Kausalya having spent the whole night with steadfastness, who performing worship to Vishnu,at dawn, for the welfare of her son.

Sloka 15

sā kṣauma vasanā hRṣṭā nityam vrata parāyaṇā |
agnim juhoti sma tadā mantravat kRta mangalā || 2-20-15

15. sā = Kausalya vrata parāyaṇā = who was interested to pracise religious vows nityam = regularly; kshhouma vesanā = was wearing white silk sari kṛita maṇ^galā = made up of auspiciousness; hṛishhṭā = thrilling with rapture; juhotisma = was doing sacrificial ceremony agnim = in fire mantravat = reciting vedic hymns.

Kausalya, who was interested to practise religious vows regularly was appearing auspiciously by wearing a white silk sari and gladly performing sacrificial ceremony in a sacred fire, by reciting vedic hymns.

Sloka 16

praviśya ca tadā rāmaḥ mātur antaḥ puram śubham |
dadarśa mātaram tatra hāvayantīm huta aśanam || 2-20-16

16. tadā = ṭhen raAmaḥ = Rama provishya = entered shubham = auspicious mātuḥ antaḥpuram = mother's apartment; dadarsha = saw mātaram = mother hāvayantīm = performing sacrificial ceremony hutāshanam = in sacred fire tatra = there.

Then Rama entered the auspicious mother's apartment and saw his mother performing sacrificial ceremony in sacred fire there.

Sloka 17

devakāryanimittam ca tatrāpaśyat samudyatam|
dadhyakṣatam ghṛtam caiva modakān haviṣastadā || 2-20-17
lājān mālyāni śuklāni pāyasam kṛsaram tathā |
samidhaḥ pūrṇakumbhāṃścha dadarśa raghunaṃdanaḥ || 2-20-18

17;18. tatra = ṭhere; raghunandanaḥ = Rama apashyat = saw samudyatam = articles of worship kept ready devakāryanimiḥam = for the purpose of sacred ceremony; dadhyakshhatam = curd;unbroken rice; gṛitam chaiva = clarified butter modakān = sweet meats; tathā = and havishhaḥ = things fit for oblation lājān = fried grain; shuklāni = white mālyāni = garlands; pāyasam = rice boiled in milk; kṛisaram = mixture of rice and peas with a few spices; samidhaḥ = sacrificial sticks pūrṇa kumbhāshcha = vessels full of water.

There, Rama saw the articles of worship kept ready for the purpose of the sacred ceremony like curd, unbroken rice, clarified butter, sweet meats, things fit for oblation, fried grain, garlands made of white flowers, rice boiled in milk, mixture of rice and peas with a few spices, sacrificial sticks, vessels full of water etc.

Sloka 19

tām śuklakṣaumasamvītām vratayogena karśitām |
tarpayantīm dadarśādbhiḥ devatām devavarṇinīm || 2-20-19

19. dadarsha = saw tām = Kausalya shukla kshhouma samvītām = who was wearing white silk sari; karshitām = became lean vratayogena = because of observance of vow; deva varṇinīm = having appearance of an angel devatām = gods abdhiḥ = with water.

Kausalya, who became lean because of observance of vows, was shining like an angel with her white silk sari and was satiating gods by presenting to them libations by water.

Sloka 20

sā cirasya ātmajam dRṣṭvā mātR nandanam āgatam |
abhicakrāma samhRṣṭā kiśoram vaḍavā yathā || 2-20-20

20. dr^ishṭvā = by seeing ātmajam = her son; āgatam = who came chirasya = after a long time; mātṛi nandanam = who gave delight to his mother; sā = Kausalya samhRishhṭā = was glad abhichakrama = approached; by moving in front; kishoram yathā = like a young colt; badabā = by a female horse.

By seeing her son who came after a long time and who gave delight to mother, Kausalya was pleased and approached towards him, like a female horse approaching her young colt.

Sloka 21

sa mātaramabhikrāntāmupasamgṛhya rāghavaḥ |
pariṣvaktaśca bāhubhyāmupāgrātaśca mūrdhani || 2-20-21

21. rāmaḥ = Rama upa samgṛihya = offered salutation by touching the feet abhikrāntam = of approaching mātaram = mother; parishhvaktaḥ = was embraced bāhubhyām = by her arms; upāghrātashcha = was smelt mūrdhani = in his head.

Rama offered salutation to the approaching mother by touching her feet. Then, she took him into her arms and smelt his head.

Sloka 22

tam uvāca durādharṣam rāghavam sutam ātmanaḥ |
kausalyā putra vātsalyāt idam priya hitam vacaḥ || 2-20-22

22. kausalya = Kausalya vuvācha = spoke idam = these priyahitham = pleasing and salutary vachaḥ = words putravātsalyāt = due to affection on son ātmanaḥ = her sutam = son tam rāghavam = that Rama durādharshham = who was unassailable

Kausalya spoke these pleasing words affectionately to her son Rama, who was unassailable by enemies.

Sloka 23

vRddhānām dharma śīlānām rājarṣīṇām mahātmanām |
prāpnuhi āyuḥ ca kīrtim ca dharmam ca upahitam kule || 2-20-23

23. prāpnuhi = obtain āyushcha = long life; kīrtimeha = glory; dharmamcha = and duty upahitam = engrained kule = in your race; vriddhānam = as with the aged; dharmashhīlānām = the great souled; rājarshhīṇām = the rayol sages.

"Obtain long life and glory, as obtained by the aged , the virtuous, the great souled and the royal sages .Protect the righteousness engrained in your race"

Sloka 24

satya pratijñam pitaram rājānam paśya rāghava |
adya eva hi tvām dharma ātmā yauvarājye abhiṣekṣyati || 2-20-24

24. rāghava = Oh; Rama! pashya = see rājānam = the king pitaram = your father; satya pratiG^yam = who is having true promise adyaiva = ṭoday itself; dharmātmā = that virtuous man abhishhekshhyati = will install tvām = you youvarājye = as successor to kingdom.

"Oh,Rama ! King Dasaratha ,your father is a man of true promise. Today itself, you will be installed as successor to the kingdom by your virtuous father."

Sloka 25

dattamāsanamālabhya bhojanena nimantritaḥ |
mātaram rāghavaḥ kiṃcit prasārya anjalim abravīt || 2-20-25

25. rāghavaḥ = Rama nimantritaḥ = was called bhojanena = for food; ālabhya = touched āsanam = the seat dattam = offered; prasārya = raised kinchit = slightly aṇjalim = joined palms; abravīt = spoke mātaram = to mother.

Kausalya asked Rama to take food , but Rama just touched the seat offered by her and after performing salutation to her, spoke thus to his mother.

Sloka 26

sa svabhāva vinītaḥ ca gauravāc ca tadā ānataḥ |
prasthito daṇḍakāraṇyamāpraṣṭumupacakrame || 2-20-26

26. tadā = ṭhen saḥ = that Rama ; svabhāva vinītashcha = being humble in nature; nataḥcha = and modest gouravāt = due to respect for mother; prasthtaḥ = setting fourth journey daṇd^akāraṇy^am = to Dandaka forest; upakrame = was set about āprasṭum = to seek permission.

That Rama , being humble in nature , became still modest due to respect for his mother and was set about to ask her permission before setting forth his journey to Dandaka forest.

Sloka 27

devi nūnam na jānīṣe mahad bhayam upasthitam |
idam tava ca duhkhāya vaidehyā lakṣmaṇasya ca || 2-20-27

27. devī = oh; mother! na jānīshhe = you do not know mahat = -great bhayam = dismay upasthitam = is approaching nūnam = indeed. idam = ṭhis duḥkhāya = brings grief tavācha = to you ; vaidehyā = to ṣita lakshhmaṇ^asyacha = and to Lakshmana.

"Oh,mother! You do not know that a great dismay is approaching now. It brings grief to you, to Sita and to Lakshmana"

Sloka 28

gamiṣye daṇḍakāraṇyam kimanenāsanena me |
viṣṭarāsanayogyo hi kālo.ayam māmupasthitaḥ || 2-20-28

28. gamishhye = while going daṇ^dakārṇy^am = to Dandaka forest; kim = why anena = ṭhis seat me = for me? ayam = ṭhis kālaḥ = time upasthitaḥ = has appeared; visṭarāsanayogyaḥ = which is fit for a seat made of kusa grass.

"I am going to Dandaka forest. Why this seat for me? Time has come for me to sit on a seat made of Kusha grass."

Sloka 29

caturdaśa hi varṣāṇi vatsyāmi vijane vane |
madhu mūla phalaiḥ jīvan hitvā munivad āmiṣam || 2-20-29

29. vatsyāmi = I shall live vane = in forest; vijane = bereft of people munivat = like sage chaturdasha = fourteen varshhāṇi = years hitvā = leaving off āmishham = meat; jīvan = living madhu mūlaphalaiḥ = with honey; roots and fruits.

"I shall live in a solitary forest like a sage for fourteen years, leaving off meat and living with roots, fruits and honey".

Sloka 30

bharatāya mahā rājo yauvarājyam prayaccati |
mām punar daṇḍaka araṇyam vivāsayati tāpasam || 2-20-30

30. mahārajaḥ = the great king prayachhati = is giving bharatāya = to Bharata youvarājyam = the succession to kingdom. mām punaḥ = ṭo me; however; tāpasam = as a sage nivāsayati = making me stay daṇ^dakāraṇye = in forest

"The great king is giving to Bharata the succession to kingdom and to me, however, he is making me a sage to stay in the forest of Dandaka."

Sloka 31

sa ṣṭcāaṣṭau ca varṣāṇi vatsyāmi vijane vane |
āsevamāno vanyāni phalamūlaiśca cartayan || 2-20-31

31. saḥ = such of myself; āsevamānaḥ = has to satisfy vanyāni = with things existing in the forest; vartayam = live phala nūlai = with roots and fruits vatsyāmi = reside in vijane vane = forest; of people shaṭcha = ashhṭoucha = for fourteen varshhāṇi = years.

"I have to satisfy with the things existing in the forest and subsist with roots and fruits in a solitary forest for fourteen years."

Sloka 32

sā nikṛttaiva sālasya yaṣṭiḥ paraśunā vane |
papāta sahasā devī devateva divaścyutā || 2-20-32

32. sā devī = ṭhat queen papāta = fell on floor sahasā = all at once yashṭiḥ iva = like the branch sālasya = of the tree nikRitta = cut down parashunā = by axe vane = in the forest; devatena = as angel chyutā = dropping down divaḥ = from heaven

The queen Kausalya fell on the floor all at once like the branch of a tree, cut down by an axe and as an angel dropping down from heaven.

Sloka 33

tām aduhkha ucitām dRṣṭvā patitām kadalīm iva |
rāmaḥ tu utthāpayām āsa mātaram gata cetasam || 2-20-33

33. rāmaḥ = Rama dṛishṭvā = saw tām mātaram = that mother aduḥkhochitām = who was fit to be from sorrow; utthāpayāmāsa = lifted up her gatachetanam = who lost consciousness; patitām = who fell on the floor kadaLīmiva = like a plantain tree.

Seeing Kausalya who was fit to be free from sorrow, Rama lifted up her, who had fallen unconsciously on the floor like a plantain tree.

Sloka 34

upāvRtya utthitām dīnām vaḍavām iva vāhitām |
pāṃśu guṇṭhita sarva agnīm vimamarśa ca pāṇinā || 2-20-34

34. vi mamarsha = Rama touched pāṇinā = with hand dīnām = miserable Kausalya; pāmsukuṇṭhita sarvāṇ^gīm = whose limbs were covered with fragrant powder badamāmiva = like a female horse; vāhitām = made to be carried of load; utthitām = rose upāvṛittya = after rolling from side to side.

Rama touched with his hand that miserable Kausalya,whose limbs were covered with fragrant powder and who rose like a loaded horse from the floor on which it rolled from side to side.

Sloka 35

sā rāghavam upāsīnam asukha ārtā sukha ucitā |
uvāca puruṣa vyāghram upaśRṇvati lakṣmaṇe || 2-20-35

35. sā = ṭhat kausalya = sukhochita = who was fit for happiness asukhārtā = but afflicted with sorrow; uvācha = spoke rāghavam = to Rama purushhavyāghram = the best of men upāsīnam = who was sitting nearby; lakshhmaṇ^e = while Lakshmana upashṛiṇvati = was hearing.

Kausalya, who was fit for happiness but was afflicted with grief, spoke thus to Rama the best of men who was sitting nearby, while Lakshmana was hearing.

Sloka 36

yadi putra na jāyethā mama śokāya rāghava |
na sma duhkham ataḥ bhūyaḥ paśyeyam aham aprajā || 2-20-36

36. putra = oh;son! rāghavā = Rama! jayethāḥ yadi = If you were not born shokāya = to cause grief mama = to me aham = I nasma pashyeyam = would not have seen bhūyaḥ = greater duḥkham = grief ataḥ = than this aprajāḥ = without progeny.

"Oh, Rama! I would not have felt this much grief if I were childless .You are born only to produce sorrow to me."

Sloka 37

ekaeva hi vandhyāyāḥ śoko bhavati mānavaḥ |
aprajā asmi iti samtāpo na hi anyaḥ putra vidyate || 2-20-37

37. putra = Oh;son! na vidyate = ṭhere is no anyaḥ = other santāpaḥ = grief vandhyāyāḥ = to a barren women bhavati hi = ṭhere is indeed ekaḥ = one shokaḥ eva = only grief mānasaḥ = relating to mind iti = that asmi aprajāḥ "I have no children"

"Oh, son! There is no other grief to a barren woman except the only worry that "I have no sons".

Sloka 38

na dRṣṭa pūrvam kalyāṇam sukham vā pati pauruṣe |
api putre vipaśyeyam iti rāma āsthitam mayā || 2-20-38

38. rāmaḥ = oh; Rama na dṛishṭa pūrvam = earlier was not seen sukham vā = happiness or kalyāṇam = prospering pate pourushhe = during my husband's manliness. asthitam = It was believed mayā = by me iti = that api pashyeyam = can I see putre_api = at least in my son

"Oh, Rama! I have not seen any happiness or prosperity earlier, while my husband is in power. I believed that I could see them at last when my son comes to power."

Sloka 39

sā bahūni amanojñāni vākyāni hRdayaccidām |
aham śroṣye sapatnīnām avarāṇām varā satī || 2-20-39

39. sā aham = such a person as myself; varā satī = who is a better and virtuous wife; bahūni = so many amaroG^Yāni = disagreeable vākyāni = words sapatnīnām = of fellow wives; avarāṇām = who are inferior to me; hṛidayachhidām = who pierce the heart.

"I have to hear so many disagreeable words from my fellow-wives who are inferior to me and who pierce my heart with their words though I am a better and virtuous wife among them."

Sloka 40

ataḥ duhkhataram kim nu pramadānām bhaviṣyati |
tvayi samnihite api evam aham āsam nirākRtā || 2-20-40

40. kim nu = which one bhavishhyati = will be duḥkhataram = more sorrowful pramadānām = to women ataḥ = than yādṛishaḥ = which -anantakaḥ = unending vilāpashcha = and dissolving shokaḥ = grief mama = to me

"Which one will be more sorrowful to women than this unending and consuming grief that occurred to me now?"

Sloka 41

tvayi sannihite.apyevamahamāsaṃ nirākṛtā |
kim punaḥ proṣite tāta dhruvam maraṇam eva me || 2-20-41

41. tāta = Oh; father(a term of affection addressed to a junior) tvayi sannihite api = Even when you are nearby; aham = I nirākṛitā āsam = was rejected evam = thus kim punaḥ = what to tell proshhite = when you go to exile. maraṇam = death dhṛivam is certain me = to me

"Even when you are near by, I am being rejected. What to tell after you leave for exile? Death indeed is certain to me."

Sloka 42

atyantanigṛhītāsmi bharturnitymatantritā |
parivāreṇa kaikeyyā samā vāpyathavā.avarā || 2-20-42

42. asmi = Iam; nityam ;always atyanta nigṛihītā = held down heaving bhartuḥ = by husband; atantritā = without independence samāva = equal to athavā = or avarā = lower than parivāreṇa = servants kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi

"ṃy husband always held me down, without giving me any independence and treat me equal to or even lower than the servants of Kaikeyi"

Sloka 43

yo hi mām sevate kaścit atha vā api anuvartate |
kaikeyyāḥ putram anvīkṣya sa jano na abhibhāṣate || 2-20-43

43. yaḥ = kashchit = Anyone whatsoever sevate = serves mām = me athava = or anuvartate = follows saḥjanaḥ = that person na abhibhāshhate hi = will not indeed speak anvīkshhya = after seeing kaikeyyāḥ = Kaikeyi's putram = son

Those who serve me now and those who follow me, will not speak to me, after seeing Bharata".

Sloka 44

nityakrodhatayā tasyāḥ kathaṃ nu kharavādi tat |
kaikeyyā vadanam draṣṭum putra śakṣyāmi durgatā || 2-20-44

44. putra = oh; son! durgatā = ṃyself; the miserable one! katham nu = how shakshhyāmi = can I be able drashhṭum = to see adanam = face tasyāḥ kaikeyyāḥ = of that Kaikeyi khara vādi = who talks hurtingly nitya krodhatayā ;because of the continuous anger

Oh, son! How can I, the miserable one, see the face of Kaikeyi who talks hurtingly because of her continuous anger."

Sloka 45

daśa sapta ca varṣāṇi tava jātasya rāghava |
asitāni prakānkṣantyā mayā duhkha parikṣayam || 2-20-45

45. rāghava = Oh; Rama! āsitāni = sat down mayā = by me dasha saptacha = for seventeen varshhā ṇi = years tava jātasya = after your second birth of your thread ceremony prākāṇkshhantyā = with hope of duḥkha parikshhantyām = disappearance of troubles

"Oh,Rama! I have been waiting for seventeen years after your second birth of thread ceremony, with the hope that my troubles will disappear at one time or the other."

Sloka 46

tadakṣayam mahādduḥkham notsahe sahitum ciram |
viprakāram sapatnīnāmevam jīrṇāpi rāghava || 2-20-46

46. rāghava = oh; Rama! tat = for that reason; evam = in the way jīr ṇāpi = even if old aged; notsahe = I do not wish sahitum = to bear viprakāram = insult sapatnīnām = of co-wives; akshhayam = un-ending mahat = great du ḥkham = sorrow chiram = for long time

Oh, Rama! In this old age, I cannot bear insult from co-wives and this unending sorrow for a long time.

Sloka 47

apaśyantī tava mukham paripūrṇaśaśiprabham |
kṛpaṇā vartayiṣyāmi katham kṛpaṇajīvikām || 2-20-47

47. katham = ḥow kṛipaṇā = the pitiable me; vartayishhyāmi = can roll on kṛipaṇa jīvikām = this miserable life apashhyantī = without seeing tava = your mukham = face; paripūrṇa sashiprabham = radiating like full moon

"How can I the pitiable one, spend this miserable life without seeing your face radiating like full moon."

Sloka 48

upavāsaiḥ ca yogaiḥ ca bahubhiḥ ca pariśramaiḥ |
duhkham samvardhitaḥ mogham tvam hi durgatayā mayā || 2-20-48

48. tvam = you samvardhitaḥ = were nourished mogham = in vain mayā = by me durgatayā = the unfortunate one; upavāsaishcha = and painful voes.

"You were nourished in vain by me ,the important one by fasts, by meditations, by various difficult and painful vows ."

Sloka 49

sthiram tu hṛdayam manye mama idam yan na dīryate |
prāvṛṣi iva mahā nadyāḥ spṛṣṭam kūlam nava ambhasā || 2-20-49

49. idam = ṭhis; mama = my hṛidayam = heart yat = for which reason na dīryate = is not breached kūlam iva = like bank mahānadyā = of great river spṛishhṭam = struck navām bhasā = by new water prāvṛishhi = in rainy season; manye = I think it sthhiram = as strong.

"ṃy heart is not breached, as bank of a great river, struck by new water in monsoon, by hearing this news.That is why, I feel it is strong."

Sloka 50

mama eva nūnam maraṇam na vidyate |
na ca avakāśo asti yama kṣaye mama |
yad antako adya eva na mām jihīrṣati |
prasahya simho rudatīm mRgīm iva || 2-20-50

50. na vidyate = ṭhere is no maraṇam = death nāsti = ṭhere is no nūnam = indeed. avakāshaḥ = place; mama = to me yamakshhaye = in the abode of yama. yat = for which reason; antakaḥ = yama; the god of death najihīrshhati = is not wishing to carry off mām = me adyaiva = now itself prasahya = forcibly.

"There is no death to me, indeed .I have no place in the abode of god yama who presides over the spirits of the dead .That is why, Yama is not forcingly carrying me off like a lion carrying off a wailing antelope."

Sloka 51

sthiram hi nūnam hṛdayam mama āyasam |
na bhidyate yad bhuvi na avadīryate |
anena duḥkhena ca deham arpitam |
dhruvam hi akāle maraṇam na vidyate || 2-20-51

51. anena duḥkhena = By the grief; deham = body arpitam = is pierced. nūnam = Indeed mama = my hṛidayam = heart sthhiram = which is hard āyasam = is made of iron. yat = for which reason na bhidyate = it is not broken nāvadīryate = it has not fallen into pieces; bhuvi = on the floor na vidyate = ṭhere is no akāle = untimely = maraṇam = death dhṛivam = It is certain.

"This grief is inserted into my body. Even then, my heart is stable. It has not broken into pieces and fallen on the floor .It is indeed made of iron. There is no untimely death, it is certain"

Sloka 52

idam tu duḥkham yad anarthakāni me|
vratāni dānāni ca samyamāḥ ca hi |
tapaḥ ca taptam yad apatya kāraṇāt |
suniṣphalam bījam iva uptam ūṣare || 2-20-52

52. idam = ṭhis is duḥkham = sad anarthakānīti = to speak about waste of me = my vratāni = religious voes dānāmicha = charities samyamāshcha = and restraints. yat = which tapaḥ = austerity taptam = is performed apatya kāraṇāt = for the sake of offspring; sunishhphalam = is completely in vain bījam iva = as seed uptam = sown ūshhare = in saline soil.

"I feel bad since all my religious vows, charities, restraints have all gone waste.The austerity I performed for the sake of of -spring has gone in vain, as a seed sown in a saline soil."

Sloka 53

yadi hi akāle maraṇam svayā iccayā |
labheta kaścit guru duḥkha karśitaḥ |
gatā aham adya eva pareta saṃsadam |
vinā tvayā dhenur iva ātmajena vai || 2-20-53

53. kaschit = If a person guruduḥkhakarshitaḥ = in great distress labheta yadi = can get akāle = premature maraṇam = death svayā = ichchhayā = out of his own will; aham = I gatā = would have got pareta sampadam = glory of the dead adyaiva = now itself tvayā vinā = without you dhenuriva = like cow ātmajenavina = without calf

"If a man in great distress can get premature death out of his own will, I being separated from you ,would have attain the glory of the dead now itself like cow without the calf."

Sloka 54

athāpi kim jīvita madya me vṛthā |
tvayā vinā cndranibhānanaprabha |
anuvrajiṣyāmi vanam tvayaiva gauḥ |
sudurbalā vatsamivānukāṅkṣayā || 2-20-54

54. athāpi = ṃoreover; chandranibhānana prabha = oh; Rama! With your brilliant face shining like moon! kim = what me = my jīvitam = life tvayāvinā = without you! vṛithā = waste indeed. adya = ṇow anuvrajishhyāmi = Ican accompany tvayaiva = you only vanam = to the forest; vatsam iva = like with calf sudurbalā = a completely weak cow gouḥ = cow anukāṇkshhayā = with compassion

"ṃoreover, what is the use of life? Oh, Rama! With your brilliant face shining like moon! ṃy life is useless without you. I shall accompany you to the forest like a weak cow going behind it's calf"

Sloka 55

bhṛśam asukham amarṣitā tadā |
bahu vilalāpa samīkṣya rāghavam |
vyasanam upaniśāmya sā mahat |
sutam iva baddham avekṣya kimnarī || 2-20-55

55. tadā = ṭhen; sā = that Kausalya samīkshhya = looked at rāghavam = Rama; upanishāmya = beholden with mahat = great vyasanam = calamity; avekshhya = saw sutam = son badhham = tied up; vilāpa = lamented bhṛisham = very much bahu = in many ways asukham = uneasily amarshhitā = with anger kinnarīva = like kinnara woman.

Then, that Kausalya looked at Rama to whom a great calamity has occurred, saw her son bound by grief and lamented very much in various ways like a kinnara woman.

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe viṃśaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes the twentieth canto of Ayodhya Kanda in srimad Ramayana.
