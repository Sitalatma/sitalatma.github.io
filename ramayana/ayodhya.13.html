Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 13
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter, Sage Valmiki describes the bitter agony of Dasaratha on hearing to the adamant Kaikeyi's words.

Sloka 1

atat arham mahā rājam śayānam atathā ucitam |
yayātim iva puṇya ante deva lokāt paricyutam || 2-13-1
anartha rūpā siddha arthāabhītā bhaya darśinī |
punar ākārayām āsa tam eva varam anganā || 2-13-2

1;2. aṇ^ganā = that Kaikeyi; anartha rūpā = who was a manifesting of worthlessness ; siddhārthā = who accomplished her desire; abhīta = who was without fear; bhayadarshinī = who exhibited fear; ākārayāmāsa = informed ; tameva varam = those boons; punaḥ = again; mahārājan = to Dasaratha; atadarsham = who was unsuitable for it; atathochim = who was not habitated to such a situation; shayānam = who was lying down on floor; yayātimiva = as king Yayati; parichyutam = who dropped; devalokāt = from heaven; puṇyānte = after exhausting his merit.

Dasaratha was not suitable for such an unfortunate condition nor was he habituated to such a situation. He was lying down on the floor, as king Yayati who fell down on earth fter exhausting his merit in the region of heaven. Kaikeyi who was a manifestation of worthlessness who accomplished her desire, who was fearlessly exhibiting her fearful form, asked for those boons again in a loud voice, to Dasaratha who was in such a bad mood situation.

Sloka 3

tvam katthase mahā rāja satya vādī dRḍha vrataḥ |
mama ca imam varam kasmāt vidhārayitum iccasi || 2-13-3

3. mahāragja = Oh; king!; tvam = you; katthase = boast satyavādī = I speak truth; dṛiDhavrataḥ = I am persistent in promise; kasmat = why; ichhasi = do you desire; vidhārayitum = to object; imam varam = this boon; mama = of mine.

"Oh, king! You always boast yourself, saying "I speak truth. I am persisted in my promise." Now, why are you objecting to my boon?"

Sloka 4

evam uktaḥ tu kaikeyyā rājā daśa rathaḥ tadā |
pratyuvāca tataḥ kruddho muhūrtam vihvalann iva || 2-13-4

4. tadā = then; evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; rājā dasharathā = King Dasaratha; krudhhaḥ = became angry; vihvalanniva = turned out delirious; muhūrtam = for a moment; tataḥ = and thereafter; pratyuvācha = again spoke.

After thus spoken to by Kaikeyi, Dasaratha became angry, turned out delirious for a moment and again spoke as follows:

Sloka 5

mRte mayi gate rāme vanam manuja pumgave |
hanta anārye mama amitre rāmaḥ pravrājitaḥ vanam || 2-13-5

5. anārye = Oh; the vulgar one! mama amitre = you; my enemy!; bhava = become; sukhinī = happy; sakāmā = after your desire is fulfilled; manuja puṇ^gave rāme vanamgate = when Rama the best among men leaves for forest; mayi mṛite = and when I die; hanta = alas!

"Oh, the vulgar one! You, my enemy! You want to be happy after your desire is fulfilled when Rama the best among men leaves for the forest and when I die thereafter. Alas!"

Sloka 6

svarge.api khalu rāmasya kuśalaṃ daivatairahm |
pratyādeśādabhihitaṃ dhārayiṣye kathaṃ bata || 2-13-6

6. katham khalu = ḥow indeed aham = I; dhārayishhye = get bit perceived; daivataiḥ = by celestials; svarge api = in heaven also; rāmasya = about Rama's; kushalam = well-being; abhihitam = spoken; pratyādeshāt = obscuringly; bata = alas!

"When celestials ask for me about Rama's well-being after I reach heaven, how can I indeed convince them that he is well obscuringly the actual fact?"

Sloka 7

kaikeyyāḥ priyakāmena rāmaḥ pravrājito mayā |
yadi satyam bravīmy etat tat asatyam bhaviṣyati || 2-13-7

7. bravīmiyadi = If I tell ; etat = this; satyam = truth; rāmaḥ = Rama; pravājitaḥ = was sent; vanam = to forest; mayā = by me; priya kāmena = desirous of showing kindness; kaikeyyā = to Kaikeyi; tat = that; bhavishhyati = will become; asatyam = untruth.

"If I tell without obscuring the fact that I sent Rama to exile desirous of showing kindness to Kaikeyi, nobody will believe me."

Sloka 8

aputreṇa mayā putraḥ śrameṇa mahatā mahān |
rāmo labdho mahābāhuḥ sa kathaṃ tyajyate mayā || 2-13-8

8. shrameṇa = with much effort; mayā = by me; aputreṇa = the childless; mahān = the great; mahābāhuḥ = powerful man; rāmaḥ = Rama; labhaḥ = was obtained; putraḥ = as son; katham = how; mayā = by me; saḥ = such Rama; tyajyate = can be abandoned?

"With much effort I, the childless begot the great and powerful Rama as my son. How can I abandon such Rama?"

Sloka 9

śūrśca kṛtavidyaśca jitakrodhaḥ kṣamāparaḥ |
kathaṃ kamalapatrākṣo mayā rāmo vivāsyate || 2-13-9

9. katham = how; rāma = Rama; shūraḥ = who is valiant; krita vidyashcha = who is knoledgeable; jitu krodhaḥ = whose anger has been defeated; kshhamāparaḥ = who is solely devoted to patience; kamala patrākshhaḥ = who has eyes like lotus petals ; vivasyate = be exiled; mayā = by me?

"How can I send away Rama who is valiant and knowledgeable, who has subdued anger, who has forbearance and who has eyes like lotus-petals".

Sloka 10

kathamindīvaraśyāmaṃ dīrghabāhuṃ mahābalam |
abhirāmamahaṃ rāmam preṣayiṣyāmi daṇḍakān || 2-13-10

10. katham = ḥow; aham = I; preshhayishhyāmi = can send; rāmam = Rama; indīvarashyāmam = who is dark blue in colour like a blue lotus; dīrghabahūm = who is long-armed; mahābalam = who is very strong; abhirāmam = who is graceful; daṇDakān = to Dandaka forest?

"How I can I send Rama, who is dark blue in colour like a blue lotus, who is long-armed , who is very strong and graceful, to Dandaka forest?"

Sloka 11

sukhānāmucitasyaiva duḥkhairanucitasya ca |
duḥkhaṃ nāmānupaśyeyaṃ kathaṃ rāmasya dhīmataḥ || 2-13-11

11. uchitasya = one who is accostomed to; sukhānām = comforts; anuchitāsya = who is not habituated to; duḥkhaiḥ = difficulties; dhīmatataḥ = who is sensible; katham nāma = ḥow; anupashyeyam = can I see; rāmasya = (such)Rama's ; duḥkham = trouble ?

"Rama is accustomed to comforts. He is not accustomed to difficulties. How can I imagine such sensible Rama in a troublesome situation?"

Sloka 12

yadi duḥkhamakṛtvādya mama saṃkramaṇaṃ bhavet |
aduḥkhārhasya rāmasya tataḥ sukhamavāpnu yām || 2-13-12

12. avāpnuyām = I shall attain ; sukham = happiness; sankramaṇam bhavet = if death occurs; mama = to me; adya = now; tataḥ = so that; aduḥkhārhasya rāmasya to difficulties; duḥkham = misery; akṛitvā = is not created.

ṣloka 13

nṛśaṃse pāpasaṃkalpe rāmaṃ styaparākramam |
kim vipriyeṇa kaikeyi priyaṃ yojayase mama || 2-13-13
akīrtiratulā loke dhruvaḥ paribhavaśca me |

13. nṛishamse = Oh; cruel one! pāpasankalpi = one with sinful thoughts! kaikeyī = Oh; Kaikeyi! kim = why; yojayase = are you instigating; vipriyeṇa = offence; rāmam = to Rama; priyam = who is beloved; mama = to me; satyaparākramam = who is truly heroic ? atulā = uneaqualled; akīrtiḥ = ill-fame; parābhavashcha = and humiliation; dhṛivaḥ = is certain; me = to me; loke = in the world.

"Oh, cruel one! One with sinful thoughts! Oh, kaikeyi! why are you planning to do harm to Rama who is beloved to me and who is truly heroic? I shall certainly get unequalled ill fame and humiliation in this world".

Sloka 14

tathā vilapataḥ tasya paribhramita cetasaḥ || 2-13-14
astam abhyagamat sūryo rajanī ca abhyavartata |

14. tasya = that Dasaratha; tathā = thus; vilapataḥ = while lamenting ; paribhramita chetasaḥ = with disturbed mind; rajani = night; abhijavartatacha = also came; astamagamat = after setting of; sūryaḥ = sun.

While Dasaratha was thus lamenting with his disturbed mind, there was approach of night with setting of the sun.

Sloka 15

sā tri yāmā tathā ārtasya candra maṇḍala maṇḍitā || 2-13-15
rājño vilapamānasya na vyabhāsata śarvarī |

15. rāgYaḥ = to king dasaratha; tathā = thus; vilāpamānusya = lamenting; ārtasya = painfully; sā sharvarī = that night ; triyāmā = at nine hours(three yamas) ; navyabhāsata = was not shining; chandramaṇdalamaṇDitā = eventhough it was adorned with circular moon.

To Dasaratha, who was thus lamenting painfully, the night seemed to be dark even though it was adorned with charming circular moon.

Sloka 16

tathaiva uṣṇam vinihśvasya vRddho daśaratho nRpaḥ || 2-13-16
vilalāpa ārtavad duhkham gagana āsakta locanaḥ |

16. vṛiddhaḥ = ṭhe aged; dasarathaḥ nṛipaḥ = king Dasaratha; vilalāpa = was lamenting; duḥkham = painfully; ārtavāt = as afflicted with disease; vinishshvasya = sighed; ushhṇam = hotly; gaganāsakta lochanaḥ = having his eyes fixed on sky.

The aged Dasaratha was lamenting painfully as though afflicted with a disease, with lot and hard breaths, having his eyes fixed on the sky.

Sloka 17

na prabhātam tvayā iccāmi mayā ayam racitaḥ anjaliḥ || 2-13-17
athavā gamyatām śīghram na aham iccāmi nirghRṇām |

17. bhadra nishe = Oh; auspicious night; nakshhatra bhūshhaṇe = decorated with stars! na ichchāmi = I do not wish; prabhātam = day-break tvayā = by you; kṛiyatām = make; dayā = mercy; me = to me; ayam = these; aṇ^jali = folded hands; rachitaḥ = are formed; mayā = by me.

"Oh, auspicious night, decorated with stars! do not wish you to do break into a dawn. Have mercy on me. I pray with folded hands."

Sloka 18

atha vā gamyatāṃ śīghraṃ nāhamicchāmi nirghṛṇām || 2-13-18
nRśaṃsām kaikeyīm draṣṭum yat kRte vyasanam mahat |

18. athavā = Otherwise; gamyatām = to be gone; (go away) ; shīghram = quickly.; aham = I; nechchhāmi = do not want; drasṭum = to see; kaikeyīm = kaikeyi; nirghRi^ṇām = who is shameless; nṛishamsām = cruel; yatkṛite = for whose reason; mahat = great; vyasanam = calamity occured.

"Otherwise, Oh, Night! Leave away quickly. I do not went to see Kaikeyi, who is shameless, cruel and for whose reason this great calamity occurred"

Sloka 19

evam uktvā tataḥ rājā kaikeyīm samyata anjaliḥ || 2-13-19
prasādayām āsa punaḥ kaikeyīm ca idam abravīt |

19. rājā = king; uktvā = spoke; evam = this; tataḥ = thereafter; prasa dayāmāsa = beseeched; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi; samyatāṇjaliḥ = with folded hands; punaḥ = again; abravīt = spoke; idam cha = this word also; Kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi

The king spoke like this and beseeched kaikeyi with folded hands. He again spoke this word to Kaikeyi.

Sloka 20

sādhu vRttasya dīnasya tvad gatasya gata āyuṣaḥ | 2-13-20
prasādaḥ kriyatām devi bhadre rājño viśeṣataḥ |

20. bhadre = Oh; auspicious ; devi = queen!; sādhuvṛittasya = I am well conducted man; dīnasya = dejected person; tvadgatasya = one who sought refuse in you; gatā yushhaḥ = old man; visheshhataḥ = especially; rāGyaḥ = king; prasādaḥ kriyatām = Let favour be done.

"Oh, auspicious queen! I am a well conducted man. I am dejected and seeking refuse in you. I am an old man and especially a king. Be kind to me."

Sloka 21

śūnyena khalu suśroṇi mayā idam samudāhRtam || 2-13-21
kuru sādhu prasādam me bāle sahRdayā hi asi |

21. sushroṇi = Oh; the well hipped!; idam = All this; na khalusamudātiṛitum = is not indeed addressed to ; shūnye = vacūm!(the sky); bāle = Oh; young woman!; kuru = do; prasādam = favour; me = to me; sādhu = well; asihi = you are indeed; sahṛidayā = good hearted!

"Oh, well-hipped one! I hope that whatever I told, has not merged in the sky. Oh! young woman, be kind to me . You are good-hearted."

Sloka 22

prasīda devi rāmo me tvaddhattaṃ rājyamavyayam || 2-13-22
labhatāmasitāpāṅge yaśaḥ paramavāpnuhi |

22. devi = Oh; queen!; asitāpāṇ^ge = with dark outer corner of eyes; prasīda = Be kind; rāmaḥ labhatām = Let Rama obtain; me rājyam = my kingdom; tvaddattam = given by you; avāpnuti = obtain; param = great; yashaḥ = fame.

"Oh, queen with dark outer corner of eyes be kind. You your self give my kingdom to Rama. Thus, you will obtain great fame."

Sloka 23

mama rāmasya lokasya gurūṇāṃ bharatasya ca || 2-13-23
priyametadguruśroṇi kuru cārumukhekṣaṇe |

23. gurushroṇe = Oh; the broad hipped; charumukhekshhaṇā = with beautiful face and eyes; kuru = do; etat = this; priyam = which will be pleasing; mama = to me; rāmasya = to Rama; lokasya = to the world; gurūṇām = to priests bharatasyacha = and to Bharata.

"Oh, broad hipped, with beautiful face and eyes! do this. It will be pleasing to me, to Rama, to the world, to priests and to Bharata."

Sloka 24

viśuddha bhāvasya su duṣṭa bhāvā |
dīnasya tāmrāśrukalasya rājñḥ |
śrutvā vicitram karuṇam vilāpam |
bhartur nRśaṃsā na cakāra vākyam || 2-13-24

24. sudushhta bhāva = that too bad-tempered; nṛishamsā = cruel woman; vishuddha bhāvasya = that cruel woman; shrutvā = after hearing; vishuddha bhāvasya = the pure hearted; dīnasya = the depressed; bhartuḥ = the husband; tāmrekshhaṇasyashru kalasya = with red eyes filled with tears; karuṇam vilāpam = lamenting pitifully; vichitram = in a strange way ; nachakāra = could not follow; vākyam = the words; rāGyaḥ = of king.

Hearing the king, who was her pure-hearted husband and who was pitifully lamenting strangely with red eyes filled with tears, that bad tempered cruel woman did not follow his words.

Sloka 25

tataḥ sa rājā punar eva mūrcitaḥ |
priyām atuṣṭām pratikūla bhāṣiṇīm |
samīkṣya putrasya vivāsanam prati |
kṣitau visamjño nipapāta duhkhitaḥ || 2-13-25

25. samīkshha = seeing; priyām = wife; pratikūla bhashhinīm = who is talking unpleasantly; putrasya vivāsanam prati = about sending the son to forest; atushhtām = and who is dissatisfied; saḥ rājā = that king; duḥkhitaḥ = was distressed; tataḥ = and therafter; mūrchchhitaḥ = fainted; punareva = again; visamGyaḥ = losing consciousness; nipapāta = fell down; kshhitau = on floor.

Seeing his discontented wife talking unpleasantly about sending Rama to forest, that king was distressed, fainted again and fell down unconscious on the floor.

Sloka 26

itīva rājño vythitasya sā niśā |
jagāma ghoraṃ svasato manasvinaḥ |
vibodhyamānaḥ pratibodhanaṃ tadā |
nivārayāmāsa sa rājasattamaḥ || 2-13-26

26. itīva = In this way; vyathitasya = the distressed; manasvinaḥ = self-respected; rājJṇyaḥ = king; shvasataḥ = sighing; ghoram = terribly; sa a nishā = that night; jagāma = exhausted.; rājasattamaḥ = ṭhat excellent king; vibodhyamāvaḥ = having been awakened; tadā = then; nivārayāmāsa = prevented; vibodhanam = the awakening.

While the distressed and self-respected king was sighing terribly in the way, that night came to an end. In the dawn, bards and singers started to awaken him. But the excellent king prevented them to do.

- - -

|| ityārśe srīmadrāmāyane ādikāvye ayodhyakānde trayodaśaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes thirteenth sarga of Ayodhya kanda in Srimad Ramayana, the first poem.
