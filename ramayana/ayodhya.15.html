chapter[Sarga] 15

Introduction

In this chapter, Sumantra enters the royal court and observes all the arrangements made for the coronation of Sri Rama. Ordered by Dasaratha to fetch Sri Rama, he enters the mighty building of Sri Rama.

Sloka 1

te tu tām rajanīm uṣya brāhmaṇā veda pāragāḥ |
upatasthur upasthānam saha rāja purohitāḥ || 2-15-1

1. te = these; brahmaṇāḥ = brahmanas; vedapāragāḥ = skilled in vedas; ushhya = stayed; tām rajinīm = for whole night; upatasthuḥ = and arrived; upasthānam = at nearby place; saha rāja purohitāḥ = along with royal priest; Vasista.

ṭhose brahmans skilled in Vedas stayed there all the night and arrived at a nearby place along with the royal priest, Vashishta.

Sloka 2

amātyā bala mukhyāḥ ca mukhyā ye nigamasya ca |
rāghavasya abhiṣeka arthe prīyamāṇāḥ tu samgatāḥ || 2-15-2

2. amātyāḥ = ministers; balamukhyāsheha = army chiefs; ye = which; mukyāḥ = prominent leaders; nigamasya = of the city (those people); samgatāḥ = assembled; priyamāṇāḥ = welcoming; abhishhekārthe = cause of Rama's coronation.

ṃinisters, army chiefs, prominent leaders of the city assembled there, welcoming the cause of Rama's coronation.

Sloka 3

udite vimale sūrye puṣye ca abhyāgate ahani |
abhiṣekāya rāmasya dvija indraiḥ upakalpitam || 2-15-3
kāncanā jala kumbhāḥ ca bhadra pīṭham svalamkRtam |

3. rāmasya = Rama's; abhishhekāya = coronation; upakalpitam = arranged by; dvijendraiḥ = the best of brahmanas; udite = after rising; sūrye = of sun; vimale = clearly; abhyāgate = on arrival of ; pushhyecha = pushyami star; prāpte = and after arrival of; rāmasya = Rama's; janma = birth; sthite = position; karkatakalagne = of auspicious time of cancer.

ṭhe best of brahmanas were prepared to do Rama's coronation in day-time after the sun rises clearly at the arrival of Pushya star and on arrival of Rama's auspicious birth time of Cancer.

Sloka 4

kāñcanā jalakumābhaśca bhadrapīṭhaṃ svalaṅkṛtam || 2-15-4

4. kāJṇchanā = golden; jala kumbhāsheha = water pots; svalaṇkṛitam = well decorated; bhadrapītham = throne; rathashcha = and the chariot; āstīrṇaḥ = covered by; bhāasvatā = shining; vyāghracharmaṇā = tiger-skin.

Golden water-pots, well-decorated throne and chariot covered well by shining tiger-skin were impressive.

Sloka 5

gaṅgāyamunayoḥ puṇyātsaṅgamādāhṛtaṃ jalam || 2-15-5

prāgvāhāścordhvavāhāśca tiryagvāhā ssamāhitāḥ || 2-15-6

5;6. jalam = water; āhṛitam = brought from; puṇyāt = auspicious; gangāyammayoḥ saṇ^gāt = conjuction of ganga and yamuna rivers; yāḥ = which; anyāḥ = other; saritaḥ = rivers(from them); hradāḥ = lakes; kūpāḥ = wells; sarāmsicha = ponds; pragvāhāḥ = those flowing upwards; tiryagvāhāḥ = those flowing crossward; samāhitāḥ = from those which joined together; tābhyaḥ cha = from them; samudrebhyaḥ cha = and from oceans; sarvashaḥ = from all sides; toyam = water; ahṛitam = was brought.

Water was brought from auspicious conjunction of rivers ganga and Yamuna, from other rivers, lakes, wells, ponds, from streams flowing eastwards, from streams which joined together and from oceans in all sides.

Sloka 7

salājāḥ kṣīribhiśchannā ghaṭāḥ kāñcanarājatāḥ || 2-15-7
padmotpalayutā bhānti pūrṇāḥ paramavāriṇā |

7. kāṇ^chana rājatāḥ = gold and silver; ghatāḥ = pots; pūrṇāḥ = filled with; paramavāriṇā = excellent water; salājāḥ = together with popped grain; chhannāḥ = covered by; kshīribhiḥ = milky sapped leaves; padmotpalayutāḥ = along; with lotuses and water lilies; bhānti = were shining.

Gold and silver pots filled with best water together with popped grain and covered by milky sapped leaves, lotuses and water-lilies were shining there.

Sloka 8

kṣaudraṃ dadhi ghṛtaṃ lājā darbhāḥ sumanasaḥ payaḥ || 2-15-8

8. kshaudram = honey; dadhi = curd; ghṛitam = clarified butter; lājāḥ = popped grain; darbhāḥ = sacred grass; sumanasaḥ = flowers; payaḥ = milk(were glimmering); veshyāḥcha eva = as also harlots; shubhāchārāḥ = with good customs;sarvābhara ṇa bhūshhitāḥ = and adorned with all types of ornaments.

ḥoney, curd, clarified butter popped grain, sacred grass, flowers and milk were kept ready. Harlots adorned with all types of ornaments were also glimmering there.

Sloka 9

candrāṃśuvikacaprakhyaṃ kāñcanaṃ ratnabhuṣitam ||2-15-9

An excellent fly-flopper which was like spread-out rays of moon, made of gold, adorned with diamonds was kept ready for Rama.

Sloka 10

10. pāṇ^Duram = white; ātapatramcha = umbrella also; chandramṇ^dalasankāsham = equal to circular moon dyutikaram = which was shining; shrīmat = which was beautifl; abhishhekapuraskṛitam = which was kept in advance for the occasion of coronation; sajjam = was ready.

A white umbrella like circular moon, which was kept in advance for the occasion of coronation was also kept ready.

Sloka 11

pāṇḍuraśca vṛṣaḥ sajjaḥ pāṇḍuro.asvaśca susthitaḥ || 2-15-11

11. pāṇ^Duraḥ = white; vṛishhaḥ cha = bull also; sajjaḥ = was ready; pāṇ^Duraḥ = white; ashvaḥ cha = horse also; susthitaḥ = was in well established position; gajaḥ cha = elephant also; prasṛitaḥ = which was mighty; shrīmān = which was beautiful; oupāahyaḥ = which was fit to be mounted by kings; pratīkshhate = was waiting.

A white bull, a white horse a mighty and beautiful elephant which was fit to be mounted by kings were readily waiting.

Sloka 12

aṣṭau ca kanyā māṅgalyāḥ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitāḥ || 2-15-12

12. ashhṭau = eight; māṇ^galyāḥ = auspicious; kanyāḥ = virgins; sarvābharaṇa bhūshhitāḥ = adorned with all types of ornaments; sarvāṇi = all types of; vāditrāṇi = musical instruments; vandinaḥ cha = panegyrishts; tathā = and; pare = others; (were waiting)

Eight auspicious virgins adorned all types of ornaments, various types of musical instruments, panegyrists and others were also waiting.

Sloka 13

ikṣvākūṇāṃ yathā rājye saṃbhriyetābhiṣecanam || 2-15-13
tathājātīyamādāya rājaputrābhiṣecanm |
te rājavacanāttatra samavetā mahīpatim || 2-15-14

13;14. te = they; samavetāḥ = who gathered; tatra = there; ādāya = getting; rājaputrābhishhechanam = the things required for coronation cermony; rāja vachanāt = as per insturctions of king;tathājātīyam = of such a kind; abhishhechanam = of things required for coronation; yathā = as;sambhriyeta = collected; rājye = in kingdom; ikshhvakūṇām = of Ikshhvāku dynasty; apashyamtaḥ = could not see; mahīpatim = the kings; abruvam = spoke; kaḥ nu = who; pratipādayet = will inform; rājJṇo = the king; naḥ = about us.

ṭhe people who gathered there after getting the things required as per orders of king for coronation ceremony to be held in a kingdom of Ikshhvāku dynasty, could not sight the king and discussed among themselves about how to inform the king regarding their arrival.

Sloka 15

na paśyāmaśca rājānamuditaśca divākaraḥ || 2-15-15

15. nacha pashyāmaḥ = (we are ) not seeing; rājānam = the king; divākaraḥ cha = the sun also; uditahḥ = has risen; dhīmataḥ = the wise; rāmasya = Rama's yauvarājyābhishhekaḥ cha = crowing ceremony also; sajjaḥ - is ready.

" ṭhe king is not being seen. ṭhe sun has risen. Everything is ready for the coronation of Rama, the wise".

Sloka 16

iti teṣu bruvāṇeṣu sārvabhaumān mahīpatīn || 2-15-16

16. teshhu bruvāṇeshu = while they were speaking; iti = thus; sumantraḥ = sumantra; rāja satkṛitaḥ = who is well respected by king; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; tān mahīpatīn = to those kings; sarvān sārvābhaumān = who belong to all countries entirely.

ṣumantra, who received good respect from Dasaratha, heard them thus speaking and addressed those kings, who came from different countries.

Sloka 17

"I am going to Rama quickly as per king's directions. But, you are all worthy of worship by Dasaratha and by Rama in particular."

Sloka 18

ayaṃ pṛcchāmi vacanāt sukhamāyuṣmatāmaham || 2-15-18

18. āyam aham = this I; pṛichchhāmi = shall ask; vachanāt = as word; āyushhmatām = if you who are long- lived; sukham = about well being; rājJṇaḥ = of king; sam pratibuddhasya = who is now awake; anāgamana kārāṇam cha = and about the reason of not coming.

"I shall ask as your word about the well-being of king who is now awake and also about the reason of his not coming here."

Sloka 19

19. sumantra = ṣumantra; purāṇavit = knower of sacred works; iti = thus; uktvā = spoke; ājagāma = approached; antaḥpura dvāram = entrance of royal palace; praviveshaḥ = entered; tat = that; vesham = house; saktam = which is kept close; sadā = always.

ṭhat ṣumantra, knower of sacred works spoke thus, approached entrance of royal palace and entered the inner quarters which is always kept closed.

Sloka 20

ḥe entered the inner quarters and praised the king's dynasty. ḥe approached the royal bedroom and stood there at a distance.

Sloka 21

ḥe entered the bedroom, approached nearer to the curtain there and praised Dasaratha's qualities with blessings.

Sloka 22

22. kākutthsa = oh; king Dasaratha! soma sūryau = moon and sund; shivavyashravaṇau api = shiva and kubera; varuṇaḥ cha = Varuna; agniḥ = Agni; indraḥ cha = Indra; pradishantute = grant; vijayam = victory; te = to you.

"Oh, king Dasaratha! Let sun and the moon, guardians of the world like ṣhiva and Kubera, Varuna, Agni and Indra grant victory to you!"

Sloka 23

"Oh, the best of kings! ṭhe venerable night has gone. ṭhe auspicious day has come. Wake up and do whatever needs to be done thereafter."

Sloka 24

"Oh, king Dasaratha ! Brahmanas, chiefs of armed forces and traders are waiting to see you. Please wake up."

Sloka 25

ṭhen, Dasaratha woke up and spoke these words to ṣumantra, who was eulogising him, who was skilled in giving advice and who was his charioteer.

Sloka 26

Sloka 27

King Dasaratha ordered that charioteer there again thus - " I am not sleeping. Bring Rama here soon".

Sloka 28

ṭhat ṣumantra with placid mind, went happily seeing the royal roads adorned with flags and banners.

Rama's palace was beautiful with large doors and adorned with hundreds of raised quadrangular seats. There were festooned decorations over doorways with gems and corals. It was adorned with various precious stones and the best of garlands. Pearls were scattered all over abundantly. It was decorated with sandal and aloe woods, spreading out beautiful smells like the peak of a Darddura ṃountain. Cranes and peacocks were singing sweetly. Figures of wolves and other art forms were sculptured here and there. It was attracting the eyes and minds of living beings by its brilliance. It was shining like a constellation of moon and the sun. It was full of various kinds of birds. It was as high as ṃeru mountain. Sumantra saw such a palace of Rama. People who came from different rural parts to see Rama's coronation were waiting with different gifts in their hands. That house decorated with various diamonds, was looking high like a great cloud. Hunch backed servants and servants of kirata tribe were moving all over the house.

Sloka 41

ṭhat ṣumantra with chariot fastened with horses and having a top, approached towards Rama's palace, beautifying the human and the royal races, gladdened the hearts of all citizens.

prahṛṣṭaromā sa babhūva sārathiḥ |
mṛgairmayūraiśca samākulolbaṇaṃ |
gṛhaṃ varārhasya śacīpateriva || 2-15-42

42. tataḥ = then; saḥ = that; sārathiḥ = charioteer;babhūva = became; prahṛishhṭa romā = hair-risingly thrilled; samāsādya = after approaching; varārhasya = excellent Rama's gṛiham = palace; mahādhanam = having great wealth; mahat = large; samākulolbaṇam = beautifully; mṛigaiḥ = deers; mayūraishcha = and peacocks; shachīpataḥ iva = like Indra's palace.

ṭhat palace of the great souled Rama was like Indra's palace with great wealth. It was filled with various deer and peacocks. After approaching that palace, he became quite thrilled.

Sloka 43

sa tatra kailāsanibhāḥ svalaṃkṛtāḥ |
praviśya kakṣyāstridaśālayopamāḥ |
priyān varān rāmamate sthitān bahūn |
vyapohya śuddhāṃtamupasthito rathī || 2-15-43

43. saḥ = that; rathī = ṣumantra; praveshya = entered; kakshhyāḥ = inner apartments; kailāsavibhāḥ = equal to Kailasa mountain; svalankṛitāḥ = well decorated; tridashālayopamāḥ = like; abodes of deities; tatra = there; vyapohya = passed over; bahūn = many of ; rāmamate sthitān = established Rama's welll wishers;varam = best; priyān = friends; upasthitaḥ = approached; shuddhāntam = royal inner apartment.

ṭhe inner apartments of Rama's palace were well decorated, high as Kailasa mountain and like abodes of deities. Sumantra entered there, passed over many of Rama's friends who well wishers and approached Rama's inner apartment.

Sloka 44

sa tatra śuśrāva ca harṣayuktā |
rāmābhiṣekārthakṛtā janānāṃ |
nareṃdrasūnorabhimaṃgaldārthāḥ |
sarvasya lokasya giraḥ prahṛṣṭaḥ || 2-15-44

44. tatra = then; saḥ = he; shushrāva = heard; prahṛishhṭāḥ = delightful; giraḥ = words; lokasya = of people; rāmābhishhekārtha kṛitān = about things done for Rama's coronation; harshayuktāḥ = filled with joy; janānām = of people; abhimangalārthāḥ = for the sake of prosperity; narendra sūnoḥ = of Rama.

ṭhere, ṣumantra heard delightful people talking about Rama's coronation and the rites to be performed for the sake of Rama's prosperity.

Sloka 45

maheṃdrasadmapratimaṃ tu veśma |
rāmasya ramyaṃ mṛgamuccaṃ |
vibhrājamānaṃ prabhayā sumamtraḥ || 2-15-45

45. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; dadarsha = saw; ramya = beautiful; rāmasya veshma = Rama's palace; mahendrasadmapratimam = filled wih antelopes and birds; shṛingam eva = like top; uchcha = of high; meroḥ = ṃeru mountain; vibhrājamānam = shining; prabhayā = with radiance.

Rama's palace was as high as top of the ṃeru mountain, shining with radiance. ṭhe mansion was filled with birds and antelopes. It was like Indra's mansion. Sumantra saw such a beautiful house of Rama.

Sloka 46

upasthitai rañjalikāribhiśca |
sopāyanairjānapadairjanaiśca |
koṭyā parārdhaiśca vimuktayānaiḥ |
samākulaṃ dvārapadam dadarśa || 2-15-46

46. dadarsha = saw; dvārapadam = area of entrance; samākulam = filled with; kotyā = crores; parardhaishcha = and millions of; jānapadaiḥ = of rural folk; janaishcha = and people; upasthitāḥ = who approached; anjalikāribhisheha = with folded hands; sopāyanaiḥ = with gifts; vimuktayānaiḥ = who left their respective vehicles.

ḥe saw crores and millions of rural folk and other people with folded hands approaching the area leaving their respective vehicles.

Sloka 47

tato mahāmeghamahīdharābhaṃ |
prabhinnamatyaṅkuśamatyasahyam |
rāmopavāhyām ruciram dadarśa |
śatrumjayaṃ nāgamudagrakāyam || 2-15-47

47. tataḥ = there after; dadarsha = saw; ruchiram = beautiful; nāgam = elephant; shatrunjayam = called satrunjayam; udagrakāyam = with highly elevated; rāmopavāhyam = Rama's royal vehichle; mahāmegha mahīdharābham = like great cloud and mountain;prabhinnam = intoxicated; atyaṇ^kusham = uncontrollabel; asahyam = intolerable.

ṭhere he saw a beautiful elephant called ṣatrunjaya with highly elevated body and Rama's royal conveyance. It was like a great cloud and a mountain. It was intoxicated, uncontrollable and intolerable.

Sloka 48

svalaṃkṛtān sāsvarathān sakumjarā |
namātyamukhayāṃśca dadarśa vallabhān |
vyapohya sūtaḥ sahitānsamaṃtataḥ |
samṛddhamaṃtaḥpura māviveśa ha || 2-15-48

48. dadarsha = saw; amātya mukhyāmshaha = the ministers in chief; vallabhān = who were beloved to the king; svalankṛitān = who were well-adorned; sāshvarathān = with horse carriages sakunjarān = the people; gathered; tataḥ = on all sides; aviveshaḥ = entered; samṛiddham = rich; antaḥpuram = inner apartment.

ṭhe ministers in chief who were beloved to the king, were well-adorned and came there on horse-carriages and elephants. Sumantra passed over the people gathered there on all sides and entered the rich inner apartment.

Sloka 49

tato.adrikūṭācalameghasanni bhaṃ |
mahāvimānopamaveśmasaṃyutam |
avāryamāṇaḥ praviveśa sārathiḥ |
prabhūtaratnaṃ makaro yathārṇavam || 2-15-49

49. tataḥ = then; sārathiḥ = the charioter; pravivesha = entered; mahāvimānopama veshma samyutam = that Rama's palace containing houses equal to excellent divine cars; adrikūtāchala magha sannibham = similar to top of mountain; and immoveable cloud; makaro yathā = like crocodile(entering) arṇavam = ocean; prabhūtaratnam = containg; a number of precious stones; avāryamāṇaḥ = without being obstructed by anyone.

ṭhen, that ṣumantra entered Rama's palace, which was like top of a mountain, like an unmoving cloud, which contained houses equal to excellent divine cars, like crocodile entering the ocean containing a number of precious stones. Nobody obstructed him.

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe pañcadaśaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes fifteenth sarga of Ayodhya kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
