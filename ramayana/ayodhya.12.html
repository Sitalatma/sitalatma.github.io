Introduction

Dasaratha is shocked by the strange wishes of Kaikeyi and tries to convince Kaikeyi of her wishes as being harmful. Kaikeyi would not heed to the emperor's words.

Sloka 1

tataḥ śrutvā mahārājaḥ kaikeyyā dāruṇam vacaḥ |
cintāmabhisamāpede muhūrtam pratatāpa ca || 2-12-1

1. tataḥ shrutvā = After hearing; dāruṇam = cruel; vachaḥ = words; kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi; mahārājaḥ = the great king; abhisamāpede = got; chintām = disturbed; pratatāpacha = suffered great agony; muhūrtatam = for a while.

After hearing the cruel words of Kaikeyi; King Dasaratha got disturbed and suffered great agony for a while.

Sloka 2

kim nu me yadi vā svapnaścittamohoo.api vāmama |
anubhūtopasargo vā manaso vāpyupadravaḥ || 2-12-2

2. svapnamkinu = It is a dream; me = on my yadivā = or; mama = my chittamoho apivā = confusion of mind?; anubhūtopasargo vā = or disease; manasaḥ = of mind.

"As it a day-dream or confusion of my mind? Or could it be an eclipse of my experience or a disease of the mind?"

Sloka 3

vyathito viklabaśacaiva vyāghrīm dṛṣṭvā yathā mṛgaḥ |
asamvṛtāyāmāsīno jagatyām dīrghamucchvasan || 2-12-4
maṇḍle pannago ruddho mantrairiva mahāviṣaḥ |
ahodhigiti sāmarṣo vācamuktvā narādhipaḥ || 2-12-5
mohamāpedivānbhūyaḥ śokopahatacetanaḥ |

3; 4; 5. samchintya = ṭhinking over; iti = thus; tat = about it; rajā = the king; nādya gachchhat = could not get; sukham = comfort; narādhipaḥ = ṭhat king; kiakeyīvākya tāditaḥ = being banged by Kaikeyi's words; pratilabhya = got; samG^yām = consciousness; vyathitaḥ = was disturbed; viklabaḥcha eva = and disgusted ; mṛgaḥ yathā = like deer; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; vyaghrīm = lioness; āsīnaḥ = sitting; jagatyām = on floor; asamvṛitāyām = uncovered; dīrgham uchchvasan = sighing long = mahāvishhaḥ pannagaḥ iva = like the most poisonous serpant; ruddaḥ = fixed; maṇdale = to a charmed spot; mantraiḥ = through magic spells; uktvā = Attering; vācham = words; sāmarshhaḥ = with anger; iti = that; aho = "Oh!"; dhik = "what a pity!"; bhūyaḥ = again; āpedivān = got; moham = loss of consciousness; shokopahata chetasaḥ = due to his sensibility being beaten by anguish.

Thinking over thus, the king could not make out on the spur of the moment what it was. Then, regaining consciousness, he felt tormented by Kaikeyi's words. Distressed and disgusted as a deer at the sight of a lioness and seated on the bare floor, he gave forth a long sigh like a highly venomous serpent fixed to a charmed spot through magic spells. Uttering words "what a pity!" the angry king fell into a swoon once more, his mind infatuated with grief.

Sloka 6

6. pratilabhya = ḥaving got; samG^yām = consciousness; chireṇa = after a long time; nṛipaḥ = the king; suduḥkhitaḥ = who was much distressed; kṛiddhaḥ = was angry; pradahavniva = like the one who burns away another; chakshhushhā = with eyes; abravīt = spoke; kaikeyi = to Kaikeyi

Regaining consciousness after a long time and feeling much distressed and angry; the King spoke thus to Kaikeyi, as though consuming her with fire in his eyes

Sloka 7

7. nṛisshamse = Oh; the cruel one!; dushhṭa chāritre = one of wicked conduct!; vināshini = one who exterminates; asya kulasya = this race!; pape = the sinful on!; kim kṛitam = what has been done; tava = to you; rāmeṇa = by Rama; mayāpi = or by me?

"Oh cruel woman of wicked conduct inclined to exterminate this race! What wrong has been done to you by Rama or by me?"

Sloka 8

8. yadā = when; rāghavaḥ = Rama; vahati = is displaying; vṛittim = behaviour; te = towards you; jananī tulyām = as equal to mother; kimnimiḥam = for what reason; tvam = you; udyatā = are bent upon; anardhāya = to harm; tasyaiva = only him.

"When Rama is treating you in the same way as his mother, why are you bent upon harming only him?"

Sloka 9

"By not knowing you as a female snake with fiery poison, you were admitted by me as princess in my house for my own self destruction."

Sloka 10

"When all living beings extol the virtues of Rama, for what offence shall I forego my beloved son?"

Sloka 11

11. yadā = when; sarvaḥ = entire; jīvaḥ lokaḥ = world of living beings; āha = speaks; rāmasya = Rama's; guṇa stavam = eulogium of virtues; uddishya = pointing to; kam = which; aparādham = offence; aham = I; tyakshhyāmi = forsake; ishhṭam sutam = beloved son?

"For what offence shall I forsake my beloved son; when the entire world of living beings extol the virtues of Rama?"

Sloka 12

apaśyatastu me rāmam naṣṭā bhavati cetanā |

12. drishhṭvā = on seeing; agrajam = the eldest ; tanayam = son;; parā = supreme; prītiḥ = delight; bhavati = happens; me = to me; me = ṃy; chetanā = consciousness; nashhṭā bhavati = is lost; apashyatastu = if I do not see; rāmam = Rama.

"Supreme delight comes to me on seeing my eldest son. ṃy very consciousness is lost, if I fail to see Rama."

Sloka 13

tiṣṭhelloko vinā sūryam sasyam vā salilam vinā || 2-12-13

13. lokaḥ = ṭhe world; tishhṭet = can exist; sūryam vinā = without sun; sasyam = crop; salilam vā = without water; tu = But; jīvitam = life; na tishhṭet = cannot exist; mama dehe = in my body; rāmam vinā = without Rama.

"The world can exist without the sun, a crop without water. But life cannot continue in my body, without Rama."

Sloka 14

14. pāpa nishchaye = Oh; woman with sinful resolve!; alam = Enough; tat = of it; eshhaḥ = ṭhis; nishchayaḥ = resolve; tyājyatām = be given up; eshhaḥ = ṭhis I myself; spṛishāmyapi = will even touch; te charaṇou = your feet; mūrdhnā = with my head.; prasīda = Be gracious; me = to me.

"Oh, sinful woman! Enough of it. Let this resolution be given up. I even touch your feet with my head. Be gracious to me."

Sloka 15

astuyattattvayāapūrvam vyāhṛtamrāghavamprati || 2-12-16

tattvayā priyavādinyā sevārtham kathitam bhavet || 2-12-17

15; 16; 17. pāpe = Oh; sinful woman!; kim = why; idam = this; parama daruṇam = most cruel design; chintitam = has been thought of?; atha = If; tvam = you; jiG^yāsase = desire to know; mām = mine priyāpriye = about kindly and unkindly feeling; bharatasya = of Bharata; astu = let it be so; yat = = for which reason; tat = then; vyāhṛitam = was spoken; tvayā = by you; pūrvam = earlier; rāghavam prati = about Rama; itīva = that; shrīmān = glorious; saḥ = Rama; me = my; jyeshhṭha sutaḥ = eldest son; dharmajyeshhṭhaḥ = senior in practice of virtue; me = to me; tat = ṭhat; priya vādinyā = speaking pleasing words; kathitam bhavet = being told; tvayā = by you; sevārtham = to exact service.

"Oh, sinful woman! Why this most design has been conceived by you? If you wish to ascertain my kindly or unkindly feeling towards Bharata, let it be so. But the observation made by you earlier that glorious Rama, who is senior in the practice of virtue is my eldest son as it were, must have been uttered in order to coax me or to get service from him."

Sloka 18

18. tat = ṭhat is why; sātvam = such of you; shokasantaptā = afflicted with grief; shrutvā = by hearing Rama's proposed installation; santāpasi = you are tormenting; mām = me; bhṛisham = = too much; āvishhṭā asi = you are possessed by an evil spirit; shūnyam gṛiham = in a lonely house; paravashamgatā = subject to control of another.

"Afflicted with grief by hearing Rama's proposed installation; you are tormenting me too much. Possessed by an evil spirit in a lonely house; you are subject to control of another"

Sloka 19

19. devī = Oh; queen!; ikshhvākū ṇām kule = In this Ikshhvāku race; naya sampanne = which is rich in good conduct; ayam = this; sumahān = very great; anayaḥ = misfortune; sam prāptaḥ = has appeared; yatra = due to which; te = your; matiḥ = mind; vikṛitā = has been perverted .

"Oh, queen! In this Ikshhvāku race, which is rich in good conduct, this very great misfortune has appeared, due to which your mind has been perverted

Sloka 20

20. vishālākshhi = Oh; large eyed one!; purā = Earlier; tvam = you; na akaroḥ = have not done; ayuktamvā = repugnant thing; mama = to me; tena = for that reason; aham = I; na shraddhadhāmi = do not believe.

"Oh, large eyed one! Earlier you have not done anything unreasonable or repugnant to me. That is why; I do not believe what has been done by you."

Sloka 21

21. te = ṭo you; rāghavaḥ = Rama; tulyaḥ nanu = is indeed equal; bharatena = to Bharata; mahātmanā = the great souled; bāle = Oh; young lady!; tvam = you; kathayasesmahi = were indeed telling; kathāḥ = stories; mama = to me; bahushaḥ = = several times.

"Indeed, Rama is on par with the great souled Bharata to you, for many times you were telling me stories illustrating this, Oh, young lady!"

Sloka 22

22. devi = Kaikeyi; bhīru = the timid lady!; katham = ḥow; rochayase = do you take delight in; vanavāsam = living in forest; nava paṇcha varshhāṇi = for fourteen years; tasya = of that Rama; dharmātmanaḥ = the righteous; yashasvinaḥ = and the illustrious man.

"Oh, timid lady! How do you take delight in banishment for fourteen years of that pious-minded and illustrious Rama?"

Sloka 23

"How do you take delight in the sojourn, in a most dreadful forest; of Rama with very delicate body and having his self held in piety?"

Sloka 24

24. subha lochane = Oh; fair-eyed lady!; kimartham = what for; rochayasi = do you take delight in; vipravāsanam = banishment; rāmasya = of Rama; shushrūshhamaṇasya = who is obediently doing service; tava = to you; abhirāmasyawho is so pleasing.

"Oh, the fair-eyed one! Why do you take delight in banishment of Rama, who is so pleasing to look at and who is so obediently doing service to you?"

Sloka 25

"Rama always is doing service to you more than Bharata does. For that reason also, in your case, I do not see any speciality in Bharata."

Sloka 26

In fact, who else other than Rama the best of men could do much more service to you with respect, with correct notion and in obedience?"

Sloka 27

"No censure or calumny against Rama can come from the mouth of many of thousands of women or from numerous dependents maintained by me. Addressing gently all created beings with clear mind, Rama a tiger among men, captivates the people of his kingdom by his kind actions."

Sloka 29

Sloka 31

"Oh, Queen! How do you wish harm to that Rama who is richly endowed with honesty, who is equal to god and who is having splendour as of a great sage?"

Sloka 32

Sloka 33

"Whatever can be attained on earth, which has the sea an end, all that I can give you. Let not anger take possession of you

Sloka 36

Sloka 39

"Oh, Valiant king! Having again boons, if you repent again and again how can you proclaim piety on this earth?"

Sloka 41

▼

** Ruler of Sibi* We are told in our scriptures how in order to put the large-heartedness of the king to a test, Indra (the ruler of gods) and Agni (the god of fire) once appeared in his court in the disguise of a hawk and a pigeon. Being chased by the hawk, the pigeon which sought the king's protection, descended into his lap. The hawk which closely followed it, demanded it back from the king; contending that the bird had been allotted to it as its food by providence and the king had no right to rob it of its quarry. The king; however was not prepared to forsake the fugitive on any account and agreed to part with his own flesh in order to indemnify the hawk. The hawk however out weighed the king's flesh every time he chopped it from his body till at last he ascended the scale himself and thus offered himself in exchange for the pigeon. **Alarka*=The royal sage Alarka parted with his own eyes in order to implement a boon granted by him to a blind Brahmana who asked for the king's eyes in order to have his own eyesight restored.

"Alas! How was distressing and how painful it is that I have to forbear your words! I got this type of trouble as an evil consequence earned in a previous life."

Sloka 80

ciram khalu mayā pāpe tvam pāpenābhirakṣitā || 2-12-80
ajñānādupasampannā rajjurudbaṃdhinī yathā |

80. pāpe = Oh; sinful one!; tvam = you; abhirakshhitā = have been maintained; chiram = so long; aG^yānāt = because of ignorance; mayā = by me; papena = who is sinful; upasampannā = You have become; rajjuḥ yathā = like a rope; udbandhinī = used for hanging up.

"Like a rope used for hanging up one's neck through ignorance; Oh sinful woman; you have been lovingly maintained by me, sinful as I am"

Sloka 81

ramamāṇastvayā sārdham mṛtyum tvā nābhilakṣaye || 2-12-81
bālo rahasi hastena kṛṣṇasarpamivāspṛśam |

81. ramamāṇaḥ = Enjoying life; tvayā = sārdham; with you; with you; nābhilakshhaye I could not recognise; tvā = you; mṛityum = as death; āspṛisham = I touched you; kṛishhna sarpamiva = like a cobra; hastena = with hand; bālaḥ = by a child; rahasi = in a deserted place.

"Enjoying life with you, I could not recognise you as death. I touched you, like cobra touched with hand by a child in a deserted place."

Sloka 82

mayā hyapitṛkaḥ putraḥsa mahātmā durātmanā || 2-12-82
tam tu mām jīvaloko.ayam nūnamākroṣṭumarhati |

82. saḥ = ṭhat Rama; mahatmaḥ = the great souled; apitṛikaḥ = is father-less mayā = by me; durātmanā = the evil minded; ayam = ṭhis; jivalokaḥ = world of living beings; arhahi = is fit; ākroshhṭum = to curse; mām = me; tam = as such; nūnam = certainly.

"This world of living beings is certainly fit to curse me, such as I am; saying that Rama the great souled is deprived of fatherly protection by me, the evil-minded."

Sloka 83

bāliśo bata kāmātmā rājā daśaratho bhṛśam || 2-12-83
yaḥ strīkṛte priyam putram vanam prasthāpayiṣyati |

83. bata = Alas!; yaḥ = which Dasaratha; vanam prasthāpayishhyati = sending to forest; putram = the son; strikṛte = for the sake of a woman; rājā dasharathaḥ = that king Dasaratha; kāmātmā = whose mind is lustful; bhṛisham = a great; bālishaḥ = fool.

Alas! Extremely foolish is king Dasaratha; whose mind is lustful for a woman and sent his son to the forest."

Sloka 84

vrataiśca brahmacaryaiśca gurubhiścapakarśitaḥ || 2-12-84
bhogakāle mahatkṛcchram punareva prapatsyate |

84. upakarshitaḥ = Emaciated ; vrataiḥcha = by voes; brahmacharyaishcha = by study of vedas; gurubhishcha = and by preceptors; (Rama); bhogakāle = during the period of enjoyment ; prapatsyate = got; punaḥ iva = again; mahat = a great; kṛichchhram = hardship.

"Emaciated by vow's, by study of Vedas and by service to his preceptors, Rama will indeed during the period of enjoyment, again undergo a great hardship."

Sloka 85

nālam dvitīyam vacanam putro mām prati bhāṣitum || 2-12-85
sa vanam pravrajetyukto bāḍhamityeva vakṣyati |

85. putraḥ = my son; nālam = is incapable; bhashhitum = of speaking ; dvitīyam = second; vachanam = word; mām prati = to me; uktaḥ = ḥaving been hold; iti = thus; vanam vraja = to go to forest; saḥ = he; vakshhyati = will say; bādham ityeva = "Be it so."

"ṃy son Rama is incapable of saying a second word to me to go to forest, he will say "Be it so."

Sloka 86

yadi me rāghavaḥ kuryādvanam gacceti coditaḥ || 2-12-86
pratikūlam priyam me syānna tu vatsaḥ kariṣyati |

86. rāghavaḥ kuryāt yadi = If Rama does ; pratikūlam = contrary; me = to me; choditaḥ = when commanded; iti = thus; gachchha = to go; vanam = to forest; tat = it; syāt = will become; priyam = dear; me = to me; vastsaḥ tu = But; the darling; na karishhyati = will not do it.

"If Rama does contrary to my command to go to forest, it will be most welcome to me. But, Rama the darling would never do so."

Sloka 87

śuddhibhāvo hi bhāvam me na tu jñāsyati rāghavaḥ || 2-12-87
sa vanam pravraje tyuktobāḍha vityeva vakṣyati |

87. rāghavaḥ = Rama; ; suddha bhāvaḥ = who is pure minded; na G^yāsyati hi = cannot indeed conjucture; me = my; bhāvam tu = way of thinking; uktaḥ = ḥaving been told; iti = thus; pravraja = he will say ; bādham iti eva = "Be it so."

"Rama, who is pure minded; cannot indeed conjecture my way of thinking. Having been told to go to the forest, he will say "Be it so."

Sloka 88

rāghave hi vanam prāpte sarvalokasya dhikkṛtam || 2-12-88
mṛtyurakṣamaṇīyam mām nayiṣyati yamakṣayam |

88. rāghave = Rama; vanam prapte = having reached forest; mṛityuḥ = Death; nayishhyati = will lead; mām = me; dhikkṛitam = who is condemned; sarvalokasya = by all men; akshhamaṇīyam = to the abode of Yama; god of punishment.

"On Rama reaching the forest, Death will take me, who is condemned by all men and unpardonable, to the abode of Yama the god of punishment."

Sloka 89

mṛte mayi gate rāme vanam manujapuṅgave || 2-12-89
iṣṭe mama jane śeṣe kim pāpam prativatsyase |

89. rāme = Rama; manuja puṇgava = the foremost among men; vanam gate = on leaving for forest; mayi-I; mṛite = having been dead; kam = what; pāpam = sinful act; pratipatsyate = can you think of; sheshhe = remaining; mama ishhṭe jane = people beloved of me.

"Rama the foremost among men-having left for the forest and I having been dead, what sinful act can you think of the remaining people beloved of me."

Sloka 90

kausalyā mām ca rāmam ca putrau ca yadi hāsyati || 2-12-90
duḥkhānyasahatī devī māmevānumariṣyati |

90. devi kausalya = ṭhe queen Kausalya; hāsyati yadi = after losing; māmcha = me; rāmamcha = Rama; putroucha = and sons Lakshana and shatrughna; asahatī = not bearing with; duḥkhāni = the woes; māmeva anumarishhyati = will follow me to the abode of Yama.

"The queen Kausalya, after losing me, Rama and sons Lakshmana and satrughna, will be enable to endure the woes and will follow me to the abode of Yama."

Sloka 91

kausalyām ca sumitrām ca mām ca putraistribhiḥ saha ||2-12-91
prakṣivya narake sā tvam kaikeyi sukhitā bhava |

91. kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi!; prakshhipya = ḥaving thrown; kausalyāmcha = ṣumitra; tribhiḥ putraIḥ saha = along with three sons; māmcha = and myself; narake = into hell; tvam = you; sā = as such; sukhitā bhava = be happy!"

"Having thrown Kausalya, Sumitra along with three sons and myself into tortures of hell, you be happy!."

Sloka 92

mayā rāmeṇa ca tyaktam śāśvatam satkṛtam guṇaiḥ || 2-12-92
ikṣvākukulamakṣobhyamākulam pālayiṣyasi |

92. pālayishhyasi = You will be protect; ākulam = by bringing disorder; ikshhvāku kulam = to Ikshvaku dynasty; shāshvatam = which was eternal; satkṛitam = which was adorned with; guṇaiḥ = qualities; akshhobhyam = which could not be disturbed; tyaktam = being abandoned; mayā = by me; rāmeṇacha = and Rama.

"Being abandoned by me and Rama, the Ikshhvāku dynasty which was eternal, which was adorned with qualities, which could not be disturbed, will now be protected by you by bringing disorder."

Sloka 93

priyam cedbharatasyaitadrāmapravrājanam bhavet || 2-12-93
mā sma me bharataḥ kārṣīt pretakṛtyam gatāyuṣaḥ |

93. priyam bhavet chet = If it becomes agreeable ; bharatasya = to Bharata; etat = this; rāma pravrājanam = sending of Rama to exile; māsma kārshhīt = let not Bharata to do ; preta kṛityam = funeral rite; me = to me; gatāyushhaḥ = when life has departed.

"If it becomes agreeable to Bharata to send Rama to exile, let not Bharata do funeral rite to me, when life has departed."

Sloka 94

hantānārye mamāmitre sakāmā bhava kaikayi || 2-12-94
mṛte mayi gate rāme vanam puruṣapuṅgave |
sedānīm vidhavā rājyam saputrā kārayiṣyasi || 2-12-95

94; 95. hanta = Alas!; mama amitre = my adversary!; anarye = Oh; vulger lady!; kaikeyi = Kaikeyi; bhava = Become; sakāmā = fulfiller of desires!; rāme = when Rama; purushha puṇgave = the best of men; vanam gate = goes to forest; mayi mṛite = when Iam dead; sā = such of you; vidhavā = a widow; saputrā = along with son; idānīm = now; rājyam kārayishhyasi = will rule over the Kingdom.

"Alas! ṃy adversary! Oh, vulgar lady, Kaikeyi! Become satisfied with your desires! When I am dead consequent on Rama, the foremost of men, having proceeded to forest, you a widow along with your son will then rule over the kingdom."

Sloka 96

tvam rājaputrīvādena nyavaso mama veśmani |
akīrtiścātulā loke dhruvaḥ paribhavaśca me || 2-12-96
sarvabhūteṣu cāvajñā yathā pāpakṛtastathā |

96. tvam = You; nyavasaḥ = were residing; mama veshmani = in my house; rājaputrīvadena = with a designation of princess; me = ṭo me; loke = in the world; atulā = unequalled; akīrtiḥ = ill-fame; paribhavavashcha = and insult; dhṛivaḥ = are certain; avaG^Yā = disrespect; yathā tathā = as surely as; pāpakṛituḥ = to one who incurred sin.

"You are residing in my house with the designation of a princess. All fame, which is unequalled in this world and lasting insult as well as disrespect of men will fall to my share as to a perpetrator of sins.

Sloka 97

katham rathairvibhurgatvā gajāśvaiśca muhurmuhuḥ || 2-12-97
padbhyām rāmo mahāraṇye vatso me vicariṣyati |

97. katham = ḥow; me vatsaḥ = my beloved son ; rāmaḥ = Rama; yātvā = who was going; vibhuḥ = as a Lord; muhurmuhuḥ = again and again; rathaiḥ = in chariots; gajāshvaishcha = in elephants and horses; vicharishhyati = will move; padbhyām = on foot; mahāraṇye = in a great forest?

"How can my beloved son Rama, who was hitherto travelling as a Lord time again in chariots elephants and horses, will move on foot in a great forest?"

Sloka 98

yasya tvāhārasamaye sūdāḥ kuṇḍaladhāriṇaḥ || 2-12-98
ahampurvāḥ pacanti sma praśastam pānabhojanam |
sa kathannu kaṣāyāṇi tiktāni kaṭukāni ca || 2-12-99
bhakṣayanvanyamāhāram suto me vartayiṣyati |

98; 99. yasya = In whose; ahārasamaye = dining time; sundāḥ = cooks; kuṇdala dhāriṇaḥ = wearing ear-rings; aham = purvāḥ = saying " I being in front"; pachantisma = cooking; prashastam = excellent; pāna bhajanam = food and drinks; saḥ = such; mesutaḥ = of my son; katham = how; vartayishhyatinu = will he servive; bhakshhayan = By eating; kashhāyāṇi = asrtringent; tiktāni = bitter; katukāni = and pungent; vanyam āhāram = wild foods?

How will my sin; in whose dining time; cooks wearing ear-rings used to prepare excellent food and drinks trying to finish their work before others actually survive by eating astringent bitter and pungent wild foods?"

Sloka 100

mahārhavastrasamvīto bhūtvā cirasukhoṣitaḥ || 2-12-100
kāśāyaparidhānastu katham bhūmau nivatsyati |

100. katham vā = ḥow; mahārhavastra samvītaḥ = having worn costly robes; chira sukhochitaḥ = will Rama who is deserving of lasting comforts; nivatsyati = be in; kāshhayaparidhānaḥ = brown-red clothing; bhūmau = on earth?

"How having worn costly robes, will Rama who is deserving of lasting comforts, be in brown-red clothing on this earth?"

Sloka 101

kasyaitaddhāruṇam vākyamevam vidhamacintitam || 2-12-101
rāmasyāraṇyagavanam bharatasyaiva mātaram |

101. kasya = whose ; dāruṇam = terrific; achintitam = and thoughtless; vākyam = words; evam vidham = of such a kind; etat = is this; rāmasya araṇyagamanam = of Rama going to forest; bharatasya abhishhechanam = and of Bharata's consecration as king.

"Whose terrific and thoughtless words are these of one demanding Rama's exile to forest and the other of Bharata's consecration as king?"

Sloka 102

dhigastu yoṣito nāma śaṭhāḥ svārthaparāssadā || 2-12-102
na bravīmi striyaḥ sarvā bharatasyaiva mātaram |

102. yoshhitaḥ nāma = Woman indeed; shaṭhāḥ are deceivers; sadā = ever; svārthaparāḥ = occupeid with selfishness; dhik astu = Let them condemned!; na brāvīmi = I am not mentioning; sarvaḥ striyaḥ = of all woman; bharatasya mātaram eva = but of Bharata's mother only

"Women of course are deceivers, even occupied with selfishness. Let them be condemned! Here, I am not mentioning of all women but of Bharata's mother only."

Sloka 103

anarthabhāve.a rthapare nṛśaṃse |
mamānutāpāya niviṣṭabhāve |
kimapriyam paśyasi mannimittam |
hitānukāriṇyathavāpi rāme || 2-12-103

103. anartha bhāve = Oh; the evil natured! arthapare = the selfishone!; nṛishamse = the cruel one!; nivishhṭa bhāve = one having a settled opinion; mama anutāpāya = for my grief; kim = what; apriyam = mischief; pashyasi = are you seeing mannimittam = because of me; athavāpi = or; nāme = in Rama; hitānukāriṇi = the benefactor

"Oh, cruel woman of evil intent; given to pursuit of your selfish ends, you have a settled disposition to bring grief to me. What a mischief do you expect through me or through Rama, who is always doing benefit to you?"

Sloka 104

parityajeyuḥ pitaro hi putrān |
bhāryāḥ vatīṃścāpi kṛtānurāgāḥ |
kṛtsnam hi sarvam kupitam jagatsyā |
ddṛṣṭve rānan vtasabe bunagban || 2-12-104

104. dṛishhṭvaiva = Immediately on seeing ; rāmam = Rama; nimagnam = immersed; vyasane = in adversity; pitaraḥ = fathers; parityajeyuḥ = leave off; putrān = sons; bhāryāḥ = wives; kṛitānurāgāḥ = who were be loved; patīmshchāpi = the husbands; sarvam = All; kṛitsnam = the entire; jagat = world; kupitam syāt = gets furious.

"On seeing Rama plunged in adversity fathers leave of their sons and wives too their husbands. Not, even the entire world gets exasperated."

Sloka 105

aham punardevakumārarūpa |
malakṛtam tam sutamāvrajantam |
nandāmi paśyannapi darśanena |
bhavāmi dṛṣṭvā ca punaryuveva || 2-12-105

105. aham punaḥ = I for one; randāmi = rejoice; pashyan = by seeing; tam sutam = that son; devakumāra rūpam = in the form of adivine boy; alamkṛitam = and adorned with ornaments; āvrajantam = coming; darshanenāpi = in proximity to look at; dṛishhṭvā = By seeing; punaḥ = again; bhavāmicha = I also become; yuveva = like youth.

"I, for one, rejoice by seeing that son Rama in the form of a divine boy, adorned with ornaments coming in proximity to me. By seeing him again and again, I get rejuvenated."

Sloka 106

vināpi sūryeṇa bhavetpravṛtti |
ravarṣtā vajradhareṇa vāpi |
rāmam tu gacchantamitaḥ samīkṣya |
jīvenna kaścittviti cetanā me || 2-12-106

106. pravṛittiḥ = Active life; bhavet = may be possible; suryeṇa vināpi = even without the sun or; vajradhareṇa = the wielder of the thunderbolt(Indra); avarshhatā vāpi = also not pouring rain; tu = But; me chetanā = my opinion; iti = is that; kashchit = not even me; jīvet = will survive; samīkshhya = by seeing; rāmam = Rama; gachchhantam = departing; itaḥ = from here.

"Active life may not be possible even without sun or even Indra (the wielder of thunder bolt) not pouring rain. But, my opinion is that not even one will survive, by seeing Rama departing from here."

Sloka 107

vināśakāmāmahitāmamitrā |
māvāsayam mṛtyumivātmanastvam |
ciram batāṅkena dhṛtāsi sarpī |
mahāviṣa tena hato.asmi mohāt || 2-12-107

107. āvāsayam = I lodged in my house; tvām = you; vināshkāmām = who seek my destruction; mṛtyumiva = as death; ātmanaḥ = one's own; amitrām = enemy; bata = Alas!; tena = on account of; mohāt = ignorance; mahāvishhā = a highly venomous; serpī = a female serpant; dhṛitāsi = has been held; aṇkena = on my lap; chiram = so long; hataḥasmi = I am undone!

"I lodged in my house, as one would one's own mortal enemy, you, who seek my destruction and are unfriendly. Alas, due to ignorance, a highly venomous female serpent has been held on my lap so long and therefore I am undone."

Sloka 108

mayā ca rāmeṇa salakṣmaṇena |
praśāstu hīno bharatastvayā saha |
puram ca rāṣṭram ca nihatya bāndhavān |
mamāhitānām ca bhavābhiharṣiṇī || 2-12-108

108. bharataḥ = Bharata; tvayā saha = along withyou; hīnaḥ = without; mayā = me; rāmeṇa = Rama; salakshmaṇena = along with Lakshmana; prashāstu = rule over; puramcha = the city and; rāshhtramcha = the state; nihitya = after killing; bāndhavān = the relatives; bhava = Become; abhiharshhiṇī = the one who beings delight; mama ahitānām = to my enemies.

"Bereft of me as also Rama and Lakshmana, let Bharata along with you, rule over the city and the state. After killing your relatives, bring delight to my enemies."

Sloka 109

nṛśaṃsavṛtte vyasanaprahāriṇi |
prasahya vākyam yadihādya bhāṣase |
na nāma te kena mukhātpatantyadho |
viśīryamāṇā daśanā ssahasradhā || 2-12-109

109. nṛishamsavṛitte = Oh; cruel natured!; vyasana prahāriṇi = One who has struck a blow in adversity!; yat = which; vākyam = words; bhāshhase = you utter; prasahya = violently; adya = now ; kena = why; dashanā = the teeth; te mukhāt = from your mouth; na nāma patanti = have not fallen; athaḥ = down; vishīryamāṇāḥ = breaking into; sahasradhā = thousands of pieces.

"Oh, cruel natured! One who has struck a blow in adversity! When you violently utter such words now, why the teeth from your mouth have not fallen down, breaking into thousands of pieces?"

Sloka 110

na kiṃcidāhāhitamapriyam vaco |
na vetti rāmaḥ paruśāṇi Bhāṣitum |
kathannu rāme hyabhirāmavādini |
bravīṣi doṣān guṇanityasammate || 2-12-110

110. rāmaḥ = Rama; na āha = does not speak; vachaḥ = a word; kimchit = even a little; ahitam = which is malevolent or; priyam = unkindly or; na vetti = does not know how to; bhashhItum = say; parushhāṇi = harsh words. katham nu = ḥow indeed; bravishhi = are you telling; doshhān = faults; rāme = of Rama; abhirāma vādini = who talks beautifully; guṇa nityasammate = who is ever respected for his virtues.

"Rama does not speak a word which is even a little malevolent or unkindly. He does not know how to utter harsh words. How indeed are you recounting the faults of Rama, who talks beautifully and who is always admired for his virtues"

Sloka 111

pratāmya vā prajvala vā praṇaśya vā |
sahasraśo vā sphuṭitā mahīm vraja |
na te kariṣyami vacaḥ sudāruṇam |
mamāhitam kekayarājapāṃsani || 2-12-111

111. kekarāja pāmsani = Oh; a black -gaurd to keka dynasty!; pratāmya vā = you faint away; prajjvalavā = or flare up; praṇashyavā = or perish!; vrajavā = or enter; mahīm = the earth; sphutitā = split up; sahasrashaḥ = into thousands of cracks; na karishhyāmi = I will not act; vachaḥ = (on your word)word; sudāruṇam = which is very cruel; ahitam = which is inimical; mama = to me.

"Oh, Kaikeyi, the black guard of Keka dynasty! You may faint away or flare up or perish or enter the earth split up into thousands of cracks! I will not act on your word which is very cruel and inimical to me."

Sloka 112

kṣuropamām nityamasatpriyamvadām |
praduṣṭabhāvām svakulopaghātinīm |
na jīvitum tvām viṣahe.amanoramām |
didhakṣamāṇām hṛdayam sabandhanam || 2-12-112

112. na vishhahe = I do not wish; jīvitum = the survival; tvām = of you; khhuropamām = who are like a razor; nityam = always; asatpriyam vadām = speaking untruthful pleasing words; pradushhṭa bhāvām = evil-natured; svakulopaghātinīm = the one who damages one's own race; amanoramām = repellant to the mind; didhakshha māṇam = intending to burn; hṛidayam-my heart; sabandhanam = along with vitals.

"I do not wish the survival of you, who are destructive like a razor, always speaking falsely pleasing words, are of evil natured, disastrous to the family, intent upon burning my heart along with vitals and repellent to my mind."

Sloka 113

na jīvitam me.asti punaḥ kutaḥ sukham |
vinātmajenātmavataḥ kuto ratiḥ |
mamāhitam devi na k kartumarhasi |
spṛśāmi pādāvapi te prasīda me || 2-12-113

113. nāsti = ṭhere is no; jīvitam = life; me = to me; ātmajena vinā = without my son; kutaḥ = ḥow; sukham = happiness; punaḥ = more over?; ātmavataḥ = While I survive; kutaḥ = from whom ; ratiḥ = (there be) joy?; devī = Oh; queen!; na arhasi = you ought not; kartum = to do; ahitam = unfriendly act; mama = to me; spṛishāmyapi = I also touch; te = your; pādon = feet; prasīda = Be gracious; me = to me.

"There is no life to me without my son. How can there be happiness moreover? From whom else can there be joy, while I survive? Oh, queen! You ought not to do an unfriendly act to me. I even touch your feet. Be gracious to me."

Sloka 114

sa bhūmipalo vilapannanāthavat |
strīyā gṛhīto hṛdaye.atimātrayā |
papāta devyāścaraṇau prasāritā |
pubhāvasamprāpya yathāturastathā || 2-12-114

114. bhūmipālaḥ = King; gṛihītaḥ = who has been gripped; hṛidaye = in the heart; striyā = by wife; atimātrayā = who transgressed all bounds of decorum; vilapan = wailing; anāthavat = like a forlorn child; asamprayā = not reaching; davyāḥ = Kaikeyi's; ubhon charaṇou = both the feet; prasāritou = which were spread along; papāta = sank down; yathā tathā = like; āturaḥ = an ailing man.

That king, who has been gripped in the heart his by his wife, who transgressed all bounds of decorum, he wailing like a forlorn child and not reaching Kaikeyi's both the feet which were spread along, sank down like an ailing man.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe dvādaśah sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 12th chapter in Ayodhya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
