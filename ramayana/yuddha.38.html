Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI :Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 38
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama expresses his desire to Vibhishana and Sugreeva to halt on Suvela mountain for that night, and to envision the City of Lanka. Rama along with Lakshmana, Vibhishana, Sugreeva and his army ascend the mountain and witness the beauty of Lanka. They halt on that mountain-resort for the night.

Sloka 1

sa tu kṛtvā suvelasya matim ārohaṇam prati |
lakṣmaṇa anugato rāmah sugrīvam idam abravīt || 6-38-1
vibhīṣaṇam ca dharmajnam anuraktam nizā caram |
mantrajnam ca vidhijnam ca zlakṣṇayā parayā girā || 6-38-2

1; 2. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; lakshhmaṇānugataḥ = who was followed by Lakshmana; kṛitvā = having made up; matim = his mind; ārohaṇam prati = to ascend; suvelasya = ṃount ṣuvela; abravīt = spoke; idam this; shalakṣṇayā = with gentle; parayā = and prime; girā = voice; sugrīvam = to ṣugreeva; vibhiṣaṇamcha = and to Vibhishana; dharmaJJṇam = the knower of what is right; nishācharam = a demon; anuraktam = devoted to him; mantrajJṇam = experienced in counsel; vidhijJṇam- and the knower of prescribed rules.

Rama, who was followed by Lakshmana, having made up his mind to ascend ṃount Suvela, spoke in a gentle and prime voice to Sugreeva and to Vibhishana who was the knower of what is right, a demon devoted to him who was experienced in counsel and a knower of prescribed rules (as follows):

Sloka 3

suvelam sādhu zaila indram imam dhātu zataiz citam |
adhyārohāmahe sarve vatsyāmo atra nizām imām || 6-38-3

3. adhyārohāmahe = we shall ascend up on high; imam = this; suvelam shailendram = ṣuvela mountain; chitam = shining; sādhu = well; dhātushataiḥ = with hundreds of minerals; sarve = all of us; vatsyāmahe = will stay; atra = on this mountain; imam = for this; nishām = right.

"We shall ascend up on high this Suvela mountain, shining well with hundreds of minerals. All of us will stay on this mountain for this night."

Sloka 4

lankām ca ālokayiṣyāmo nilayam tasya rakṣasah |
yena me maraṇa antāya hṛtā bhāryā durātmanā || 6-38-4
yena dharmo na vijnāto na vṛttam na kulam tathā |
rākṣasyā nīcayā buddhyā yena tad garhitam kṛtam || 6-38-5

4; 5. ālokayiṣyāmaḥ cha = we shall see; laṇ^kām = Lanka; nilayam = the abode; tasya rākṣasaḥ = of that demon; Ravana; yena = by whom; durātmanā = the evil minded; bhāryā = my wife; hṛitā = was taken away; maraṇāntāya = for the sake of his own death; yena = by whom; dharmaḥ = virtue; navijJṇātaḥ = was not understaood; vṛittam = good character; na = was not known; tathā = and; kulam = noble lineage; na = was not seen; yena = by whom ; tat garhitam = that contemptible action; kṛitam = was done; nīchayā = with an inferior; rākṣasyā buddhyā = demoniacal mind.

"We shall see Lanka the abode of that demon, Ravana the evil-minded by whom my wife was taken away for the sake of his own death, by whom virtue was not understood, good character was not known and noble lineage was not seen and by whom that contemptible act was done with an inferior demoniacal mind."

Sloka 6

yasmin me vardhate roṣah kīrtite rākṣasa adhame |
yasya aparādhān nīcasya vadham drakṣyāmi rakṣasām || 6-38-6

6. me = ṃy; roṣaḥ = anger; vartate = is enhancing; kīrtite = on hearing the name; tasmin = of such; rākṣascadhame = worst demon; aparādhāt = Due to the offence; yasya = of whom; nīchasya = the lowest; drakṣyāmi = I shall see; vadham = the destroyer; rakṣasām = of the demons.

"ṃy anger is increased on hearing the name of that demon. Because of the offence committed by him, I shall see that all these demons are destroyed."

Sloka 7

eko hi kurute pāpam kāla pāza vazam gataḥ |
nīcena ātma apacāreṇa kulam tena vinazyati || 6-38-7

7. gataḥ = approaching; kālapāshavasham = and falling into the power of trap of death; ekaḥ = one; kurute = will do; pāpam = an evil act; tena = by him; nīchena = the vilest; ātmāpachāreṇa = because of his offence; kulam = his lineage; vinashyati = is destroyed.

"Falling into that power of trap of death, one does an evil act. Because of the offence committed by him the vilest, his lineage is destroyed."

Sloka 8

evam sammantrayann eva sakrodho rāvaṇam prati |
rāmah suvelam vāsāya citra sānum upāruhat || 6-38-8

8. rāmaḥ = Rama; evam = thus; sammantrayanneva = thinking; rāvaṇamprati = of Ravana; sakrodhaḥ = with anger; āsādya = approached; upāruthat = and ascended; suvelam = the mountain of ṣuvela; chitrasānum = with its conspicuous summits.

Rama, thus thinking of Ravana in anger, approached and ascended the mountain of Suvela with its conspicuous summits.

Sloka 9

pṛṣṭhato lakṣmaṇa ca enam anvagaccat samāhitaḥ |
sazaram cāpam udyamya sumahad vikrame rataḥ || 6-38-9
tam anvarohat sugrīvah sāmātyah savibhīṣaṇah |

9. lakṣmaṇashcha = Lakshmana too; rataḥ = delighted in; vikrame = his valour; samāhitaḥ = and stead fasteness; udyamya = keeping ready; sumahat = his mighty; chāpam = bow; sasharam = wtih arrows; anragachchhat = accompanied; pṛiṣṭhataḥ = behind; evam = this Rama; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; sāmātyaḥ = together with his ministers; savibhīṣaṇaḥ = along with vibhishana; anvārohat = ascended by following; tam = him.

Lakshamana too who took delight in his own valour and steadfastness, keeping ready his mighty bow with arrows, followed behind Rama Sugreeva along with his ministers and Vibhishana ascended the mountain, by following him.

jāmbavāṃśca suṣeṇaśca ṛṣabhaśca mahāmatiḥ |

10; 11; 12; 13. hanumān = ḥanuman; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; nīlaḥ = ṇila; maindaḥ = ṃainda; dvivida evacha = Dvivida; gajaḥ = Gaja; gavākṣaḥ = Gavaksha; gavayaḥ = Gavaya; sharabhaḥ = ṣharabha; gandhamādanaḥ = Gandhamadana; panasaḥ = Panasa; kumudashchaiva = Kumuda; haraḥ = ḥara; rambhashcha = Rambha; yūthapaḥ = the chief of the troop; jāmbavāmshcha = Jambavan; suṣeṇashcha = sushena; mahāmatiḥ = the greatly wise; ṛiṣebhashcha = Rishabha; durmukhashcha = Durmukha; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; shatabaliḥ = shatabali; kapiḥ = the monkey; anyecha bahavo shatashaḥ = and other many hundreds of; te = those; vānarāḥ = monkeys; etecha = here; shīghragāminaḥ = which are fast moving; girichāriṇaḥ = which can wander easily on mountains; vāyuvega pravaṇāḥ = with a speed akin to that of wind; adhyārohanta = ascended; tam = that; suvelamgirim = ṣuvela mountain; yatra rāghavaḥ = where Rama was there.

Hanuman, Angada, Nila, ṃainda, Dvivida, Gaja, Gavaksha, Gavaya, Sharabha, Gandhamadana, Panasa, Kumuda, Hara, Rambha the chief of the troop, Jambavan, sushena, the greatly wise Rishabha, Durmukha of great splendour, Shatabali the monkey and other hundreds of monkeys which are fast-moving, which can wander easily on mountains with a speed akin to that of wind, ascended that Suvela mountain which Rama ascended.

Sloka 14

te tv adīrgheṇa kālena girim āruhya sarvatah || 6-38-14
dadṛzuh zikhare tasya viṣaktām iva khe purīm |

14. te tu = they; adīrghena kālena = within no long time; ārūhya = having ascended; girim = the mountain; sarvataḥ = from all sides; shikhare = and at the apex; tasya = of it; dadṛishaḥ = saw; purīm = the City of Lanka; vishhaktāmiva = which appeared hanging; khe = in the sky.

Having ascended the mountain from all sides within no long time, they saw at its apex, the city of Lanka which appeared as though it was hanging in the sky.

Sloka 15

tām zubhām pravata dvārām prākāra vara zobhitām || 6-38-15
lankām rākṣasa sampūrṇām dadṛzur hari yūthapāh |

15. hariyūthapāḥ = the chiefs of monkey-troops; dadṛishaḥ = saw; tām shubhām laṇ^kām = that beautiful City of Lanka; pravaradvāram = having excellent gates; prākāravara shobhitām = enriched with exquisite ramparts; rākṣasa sampūrṇām = and pervaded with demons.

The chiefs of monkey-troops saw that beautiful City of Lanka, furnished with excellent gates, enriched with exquisitc ramparts and pervaded all over with demons.

Sloka 16

prākāra caya saṃsthaiz ca tathā nīlair nizā caraih || 6-38-16
dadṛzus te hari zreṣṭhāh prākāram aparam kṛtam |

16. te = those; harishreṣṭhāḥ = excellent monkeys; dadṛishuḥ = saw; aparam = another; prākāram = rampart; kṛitam = formed; nīlashcha rākshasaiḥ = our of the black demons; prākara vara samsthaiḥ tathā = so standing together on that exquisite rampart.

Those excellent monkeys saw another rampart formed out of the black demons so standing together scribble on that existing exquisite rampart.

Sloka 17

te dṛṣṭvā vānarāh sarve rākṣasān yuddha kānkṣiṇah || 6-38-17
mumucur vipulān nādāṃs tatra rāmasya pazyatah |

17. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; rākṣasān = the demons; yuddhakāṇkṣiṇaḥ = craving for war; sarve te vānarāḥ = all those monkeys; mumuchuḥ = made; vividhān = various kinds; nādān = of sounds; (while); tasya = that; rāmasya = Rama; pashyataḥ = was witnessing.

Seeing the demons who were craving for war, all those monkeys made various types of noises, while Rama was witnessing the scene.

Sloka 18

tato astam agamat sūryah saṃdhyayā pratiranjitah || 6-38-18
pūrṇa candra pradīpā ca kṣapā samabhivartate |

18. tataḥ = then; sūryaḥ = the sun; pratiraṇ^jitaḥ = reddened; sandhyayā = by evening twillight; astam āgamat = wnet down; kṣapācha = (and) the night; pūrṇachandra pradīptā = shining with full moon; samativartata = arrived.

Then, the sun reddened with evening-twillight went down and the night shining with full moon arrived.

Sloka 19

tataḥ sa rāmo hari vāhinī patir |
vibhīṣaṇena pratinandya satkṛtaḥ |
salakṣmaṇo yūthapa yūtha samvṛtaḥ |
suvela pṛṣṭhe nyavasad yathā sukham || 6-38-19

19. tataḥ = thereafter; saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; sa lakshhmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana; harivāhinīpatiḥ = and ṣugreeva the Lord of monkeys; yūthapa yūtha samyutaḥ = together with troops and captains of troops; pratinandya = greeted welcoming (and); satkṛitaḥ = treated hospitably; vibhīṣaṇena = by Vibhishana; nyavasat = stayed; yathāsukham = happily; suvela pṛiṣṭhe = on the mountain resort of ṣuvela

Thereafter, Rama along with Lakshmana as well as Sugreeva the Lord of monkeys together with troops and captions of troops, duly greeted welcomingly and treated hospitably by Vibhishana, halted happily on the mountain resort of Suvela.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe aṣṭātriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 38th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
