Introduction

King Dasaratha continued to narrate to Kausalya how he went to the aged parents of the deceased sage and informed about the death of their son and its cause, how he escorted them to their son to the river where he was lying dead, how embracing the boy they wept and offered libations of water to his spirit, how invested with an ethereal body, the spirit ascended to heaven while consoling the aged couple and finally how, having cursed the king that he too would die of agony caused by the separation from his son, the aged couple gave up their lives. Having thus narrated the story of his getting the curse long back and loudly weeping, king Dasaratha dies of grief.

Sloka 1

vadhamapratirūpam tu maharṣestasya rāghavaḥ |
vilapanne va dharmātmā kausalyām puna rabravīt || 2-64-1

The high souled Dasaratha, while lamenting about the unworthy killing of that sage, spoke further to Kausalya as follows:

Sloka 2

tat ajñānān mahat pāpam kṛtvā samkulita indriyaḥ |
ekaḥ tu acintayam buddhyā katham nu sukṛtam bhavet || 2-64-2

2. kṛitvā = having done; aG^yānāt = invadvertantly; tat = that; mahat = great; pāpam = sin; aham = I; samkulendriyaḥ = having my senses confounded; achintyam = thought; ekastu = alone; buddhyā = with my intellect; kathamnu = how; sukrutam bhavet = best can be done."

Having done that great sin inadvertently, having my senses confounded and I being alone, I thought of using my reason of what best can be done."

Sloka 3

tataḥ tam ghaṭam ādaya pūrṇam parama vāriṇā |
āśramam tam aham prāpya yathā ākhyāta patham gataḥ || 2-64-3

3. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; ādāya = taking; paramavāriṇā = best water; pūrṇam = fully; tam = in that; ghaṭam = pitcher; prāpya = obtaining; yathākhyāta patham = the path as made known; aham = I; gatah = went; tam āshramam = to that hermitage."

"Taking the best water fully in that pitcher, I went to that hermitage by the path as directed by the young sage.

Sloka 4

apaśyam tasya pitarau lūna pakṣāv iva dvijau || 2-64-4

4; 5.tatra = ṭhere; aham = I; apashyam = saw; tasya = his; tarou = parents; durbhaou = who were frail; andhou = blind;vṛiddhou = aged; apariṇāyakou = without a guide; dvijou iva = like birds; lūna pakshhou = whose wings were cut off; kathābhiḥ = narrating (words) tannimiḥābhiḥ = targeting about him; āsinou = sitting; aparishramou = without any occupation; anāthavat = ḥaving no protector; udāsīnou = and being immobile; hīnou = losing; tām āshām = hope of their son; matkṛite = because of me."

"There I saw the frail, blind and aged parents of the young sage. They were without a guide to support them, as birds whose wings were cut-off. Without any other occupation, they were narrating words only about their son. Having no protector and being immobile they lost the hope of their son, because of me."

Sloka 6

śokopahatacittaśca bhayasamtrastacetanaḥ |
taccāśramapadam gatvā bhūyaḥ śokamaham gataḥ || 2-64-6

6. shokopahata chittaḥ = with my mind afflicted with grief; bhaya santraptachetanaḥ = worried in mind about the impending peril; aham gataḥ = I got; bhūyaḥ = further; shokam = grief; gatvā = after reaching; tat = that; āshramapadam = hermitage."

"With my mind afflicted by grief and worried about the impending peril, I fell a prey to further grief after reaching the hermitage.

Sloka 7

pada śabdam tu me śrutvā munir vākyam abhāṣata |
kim cirāyasi me putra pānīyam kṣipram ānaya || 2-64-7

7. "shrutvā = hearing; me padashabdam = the sound of my foot steps; muni = the sage; abhāshhat = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words:"Oh;son! Kim = why; chirāyasi = are you late? ānaya = bring; me = me; pānīyam = the water; kshhipram = quickly."

"Hearing the sound of my foot-steps, that sage spoke as follows: "O, son! Why are you late? Give me the water soon."

Sloka 8

"ṃy dear son! Your mother is regretting why you are playing in water in this manner. Enter the hermitage quickly."

Sloka 9

yad vyalīkam kṛtam putra mātrā te yadi vā mayā |
na tan manasi kartavyam tvayā tāta tapasvinā || 2-64-9

9. putra = O; son! tāta = O dear! yat = If any; vyaLīkam = disagreeable thing; kṛitam = has been done; te = by your; mātrā = mother; yadivā = or; mayā = by me; tat = it; na kartavyam = ought not have been kept; manasi = in mind; tvayā = by you."

"O, son! ṃy dear! If any disagreeable thing has been done by your mother, or me, you, as a sage ought not have kept it in your mind."

Sloka 10

tvam gatis tu agatīnām ca cakṣus tvam hīna cakṣuṣām |
samāsaktāḥ tvayi prāṇāḥ kiṃcin nau na abhibhāṣase || 2-64-10

10. agatīnām = we being helpless; tvam = you; gatih = are our refuge; achakshhushhām = we having without sight; tvam = you; chakshhuḥhi = are indeed our eyes; prāṇāḥ = our lives; samāsaktāḥ = are encircled; tvayi = in you; kim = why; tvam nābhibhāshhate = not talking; naḥ = with us?"

"We being helpless, you are our refuge. We being blind, you are our eyes. Our lives are encircled around you. Why are you not talking with us?"

Sloka 11

munim avyaktayā vācā tam aham sajjamānayā |
hīna vyanjanayā prekṣya bhītaḥ bhītaiva abruvam || 2-64-11

11. prekshhya = seeing; tam munim = that ascetic; bhītachitta iva = with a fearful mind; abruvam vāchā = I spoke in a tone; avyaktayā = indistinct; sajjamānayā = leaving some consonants".

"As though fearful in mind to behold the ascetic, I spoke to him in a tone which was indistinct inarticulate and without some consonants?"

Sloka 12

manasaḥ karma ceṣṭābhir abhisaṃstabhya vāg balam |
ācacakṣe tu aham tasmai putra vyasanajam bhayam || 2-64-12

12. abhisamstabhya = having strengthened; vāgbalam = firmness in my speech; karmaḥ = and act by deliberate efforts; āchachakshhe = I told; tasmai = him; bhayam = of the fear(that awaited him); putravyasanajam = of his son's death."

"Having strengthened the firmness in my speech and having abandoned fear in my mind by deliberate efforts, I told him of the alarm that awaited him of his son's death."

Sloka 13

kṣatriyo aham daśaratho na aham putraḥ mahātmanaḥ |
sajjana avamatam duhkham idam prāptam sva karmajam || 2-64-13

13. aham = I; kshhatriyaḥ = am a warrior; dasharathaḥ = named Dasaratha; aham = I; na mahatmanaḥ = am not your high souled; putraḥ = son; idam = this; duḥkham = calamity; svakarmajam = created by my own act; sajjanāvamatam = despised by noble men; prāptam = befell (upon me)".

"I am Dasaratha, belonging to warrior- class. I am not your high-souled son. This calamity created by my own act, despised by noble men, befell upon me."

Sloka 14

bhagavamḥ ca apahastaḥ aham sarayū tīram āgataḥ |
jighāṃsuḥ śvā padam kiṃcin nipāne vā āgatam gajam || 2-64-14

14. bhagavan = O; venerable sage! chāpahastaḥ = wearing a bow in hand; aham = I; āgataḥ = came; sarayū tīram = to the bank of ṣarayu River; jighāmsuḥ = with an intent to kill ; gajamcha = an elephant; āgatam = arrived to; nipāne = a ford(for drinking water); kamchit = or any; shvāpadam = wild animal".

"O, venerable sage! Wearing a bow in hand, I came to the bank of Sarayu River with an intent to kill an elephant arrived to a ford (for drinking water) or any other wild animal."

Sloka 15

tataḥ śrutaḥ mayā śabdo jale kumbhasya pūryataḥ |
dvipo ayam iti matvā hi bāṇena abhihataḥ mayā || 2-64-15

15. tataḥ = then; mayā = by me; shrutaḥ = was heard; shabdaḥ = the sound; kumbhasya = of a pitcher; pūryataḥ = being filled; jale = with water; matvā = thinking; ayam = it; dvipaḥ iti = as an elephant; ayam = it; abhihataḥ = was killed; mayā = by me; bāṇena = with an arrow."

"Then by me was heard the sound of a pitcher being filled with water. Guessing it as an elephant, I killed it with an arrow."

Sloka 16

gatvā nadyāḥ tataḥ tīram apaśyam iṣuṇā hṛdi |
vinirbhinnam gata prāṇam śayānam bhuvi tāpasam || 2-64-16

16. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; gatvā = I reached; tīram = the bank; nadyāḥ = of the river; apashyam = and saw; tāpasam = an ascetic; shayānam = lying; gataprāṇam = dead; bhuvi = on the ground; vinirbhinnam = pierced; ishhuṇā = by an arrow; hṛidi = into his heart."

"Thereafter, reaching the bank of the river there, I saw an ascetic lying dead on the ground, with the arrow pierced into his heart."

Sloka 17

17. bhagavan = O;honorable sage!gaja jigāmsunā = with a desire to kill an elephant; ālakshhya = by targeting; shabdam; = the sound; nārāchaḥ = an iron arrow; visṛishhṭaḥ = was released; ambasi = into water; mayā = by me; tataḥ = therafter; te = your; sutaḥ = son; nihataḥ = was killed"

"O, honorable sage! With a desire to kill an elephant by targeting the sound, I released an iron arrow, and thus your son was killed (hit)"

Sloka 18

"Approaching as per his request, closer to him who was suffering from pain, I took out the arrow soon from his vital part."

Sloka 19

"Soon after taking out the arrow, he worried about both of you the parents, lamenting that you were blind and there itself he attained Heaven"

Sloka 20

śeṣam evam gate yat syāt tat prasīdatu me muniḥ || 2-64-20

"Unknowingly and unexpectedly your son was killed by me. It has happened like that. Let the honorable sage command me what to do in this matter."

Sloka 21

Hearing that cruel news pronounced by me, confessing my sin, that venerable sage could not resist his severe anger."

Sloka 22

"That sage with great splendor, whose face was filled with tears and troubled by sorrow, spoke with a sigh to me who came near him with joined palms"

Sloka 23

"If you have not told me about this shameful deed yourself, O. king, your head would have burst instantly into a hundred thousand pieces."

Sloka 24

Sloka 25

"The head of a dispatcher of a weapon with a knowledge of the matter on a sage established in austerity or such an unmarried student practicing sacred study, gets burst into seven pieces."

Sloka 26

Sloka 27

Sloka 31

"ṃy dear boy! You are not offering salutation to me, nor do you talk to me. Why are you sleeping on the floor? Are you angry?"

Sloka 32

Sloka 33

"Whose sweet and heart touching voice, reciting one sacred text or the other in particular at the end of the night, shall I hear from now?"

Sloka 34

"O, son! Who after taking bath, worshipping the goddess of dawn, and offering oblations in fire, will be sitting beside me and talking confidently looking at me, as I was afflicted with grief and fear?"

Sloka 35

35. kaḥ = who; bhojayishhyati = will feed; mām = me; atithimiva = like a guest; priyam = who is beloved; kandamūlaphalam = roots and fruits; akarmaṇyam = while I am without any work; apragraham = without fetching anything; anāyakam = without having any guide making me to walk".

"Who will feed me, like a beloved guest is fed, with roots and fruits while I am sitting without any work, without fetching anything and without having any guide making me to walk?"

Sloka 36

O, delicate youth! How can I support your mother, who is blind, aged and a poor woman in a pitiable condition, longing for her son?"

Sloka 37

Sloka 38

"Bereft of you, afflicted with grief, without any protection in the forest and poor, both of us also will come soon along with you to Yama's abode."

Sloka 39

Sloka 40

īdṛṣasya mamākṣayyā mekāmabhayadakṣiṇām || 2-64-40

"The most glorious and high souled Yama, the guardian of the world will be obliged to give this solitary imperishable boon in the form of fearlessness to me, who is in such a condition."

Sloka 41

"O, son! This criminal killed you. As it is true that you were sinless, you will soon obtain the realms as reached by those who die fighting with weapons."

Sloka 42

"O, son! ṃove on to that which is the highest state, which is obtained by the warriors, who do not return even while being killed, from the direction facing their enemies in battles."

Sloka 43

"Obtain the destiny which was obtained by Sagara, Saibya, Dilipa, Janamejeya, Nahusha and Dundumara."

Sloka 44

deha nyāsa kṛtām yā ca tām gatim gacca putraka || 2-64-45

44; 45. putraka = O;son! gachchha = get to; tām gatim = that destiny; yā = which; gatiḥ = state is obtained; sarvasādhūnām = by all the sages; yā = which; gatiḥ = state is obtained; svādhyāyāt = by the study of scriptures; yā = which is obtained; tapasācha = by austerity; bhūmidasya = by those who gift land; ahitāgneḥ = by him who has maintained sacred fire; ekapatnīvratasyācha = who has taken a vow of marrying a single wife; yā = which is obtained; gosahasrapradā tri^iṇām = who gifts a thousand cows; yā = which is obtained; gurubhṛitāmapi = by those who are owed to the service of their preceptor; yā = :which is obtained; dehanyāsakṛitām = by those who have abandoned their body(by journeying to ḥimalayas or drowning themselves in water or leaping into the flames)

"O, son! Obtain that destiny, which is obtained by all the sages, by those who studied scriptures, by an act of austerity, by a gift of the land, by him who has maintained sacred fire, who has taken a vow of marrying a single wife, who gifts a thousand cows, who are owed to the service of the preceptor and that which is obtained by those who have abandoned their body (by journeying to Himalayan ṃountains, or drowning themselves in water or leaping into the flames)"

Sloka 46

na hi tu asmin kule jātaḥ gaccati akuśalām gatim |
sa tu yāsyati yena tvam nihato mama bāndhavaḥ || 2-64-46

46. jātaḥ = the one born; asmin = in this; kule = race; na gachchhati hi = will not obtain; akushalām = an unsafe; gatim = destiny; tu = but; yena = by whom; tvam = you; mama = my; bāndhavaḥ = kinsman; nihataḥ = was killed; saḥ = he; yāsyati = will obtain; (that unsafe destiny.)"

"The one born in the race of sages will not obtain an unsafe destiny. The person, who killed you, my son, will obtain that bad destiny."

Sloka 47

evam sa kṛpaṇam tatra paryadevayata asakṛt |

"Thus, that sage repeatedly wept there pitiably. Then he, along with his wife, started doing the ceremony of offering water to his demised son."

Sloka 48

"The pious son of the sage on his part, soon ascended the Heaven, wearing a wonderful form along with Indra (the king of celestials) as a result of his own pious acts."

Sloka 49

"That son of the sage who was together with Indra, talked to his parents. Consoling his parents for a while, he spoke the following words."

Sloka 50

"I obtained the supreme state because of my service to both of you. You will also obtain soon my vicinity."

Sloka 51

evam uktvā tu divyena vimānena vapuṣmatā |
āruroha divam kṣipram muni putraḥ jita indriyaḥ || 2-64-51

51. muniputraḥ = the son of the sage; jitendriyaḥ = whose senses were subdued; uktvā = spoke; evam = thus; vimānena = by an aero plane;divyena = which was wonderful; vapushhmatā = having a beautiful form; kshhipram = quickly; āruruha = ascended; divam = heaven."

"The son of the sage, whose senses were subdued, thus spoke and by a wonderful aero plane with beautiful form, quickly reached Heaven."

Sloka 52

sa kṛtvā tu udakam tūrṇam tāpasaḥ saha bhāryayā |

52. saḥ tāpasaḥ = that ascetic; mahātejāḥ = with great splendor; bhāryayā saha = along with his wife; tūrṇam = soon; udakam kṛitvā = performred the ceremony offering water to his dead son; uvācha = and spoke; mām = to me; upasthitam = who was standing near him; kṛitāṇjalim = with folded hands."

"That ascetic with great splendor along with his wife soon performed the ceremony offering water to his dead son and spoke to me, who was standing near him with folded hands."

Sloka 53

adya eva jahi mām rājan maraṇe na asti me vyathā |
yat śareṇa eka putram mām tvam akārṣīr aputrakam || 2-64-53

53. rājan = O;king! ṭvam = you; yat = for which reason; akārshhī = have made; mām = me; ekaputram = who had only one son; aputram = without a son; shareṇa = by an arrow; jahi = kill; mām = me; adyaiva = now itself; nāsti = there is no; vyathā = fear; maraṇe = about death; me = to me."

"O, king! You have made me, without a son, by killing my one and the only one son, by an arrow. You kill me also now. I am not frightened about death."

Sloka 54

tvayā tu yad avijñānān nihataḥ me sutaḥ śuciḥ |
tena tvām abhiśapsyāmi suduhkham atidāruṇam || 2-64-54

54. yat = for which reason; me = my; shuchiḥ = was killed; tvayā = by you; aviG^yānāt = through ignorance; tena = for that reason; abhishapsyāmi = I shall curse; suduḥkham = most painful; atidāruṇam = and very cruel; tvam = (on) you."

"Since you have killed my holy son through ignorance, I shall pronounce a most painful and very cruel curse on you."

Sloka 55

putra vyasanajam duhkham yad etan mama sāmpratam |
evam tvam putra śokena rājan kālam kariṣyasi || 2-64-55

55. yat: = which;etat = this;duḥkham = agony; putravyasanam = caused by the loss of my son;sāmpratam = at present; rājan = O; king; tvam = you; kālam karishhyati = will die; putrashokena = by the loss of your son"

"O, king! Even as I am suffering from agony now because of the loss of my son, in the same manner; you will also die due to agony caused by the loss of your son."

Sloka 56

"O, king! Since the sage was killed by you, belonging to the warrior class, through ignorance, the son of killing a Brahmin will not take possession of you."

Sloka 57

"A feeling similar to this, which makes the life awful and extinct, will indeed quickly come to you, even as a merit accrues to the donor of a donation to the priest."

Sloka 58

58. nyasya = pronouncing; shhāpam = the curse; evam = thus; tat = that; mithunam = couple; bahuvilapya = wept many times; karuṇam = pitiably; āropya = placed; deham = their body; chitām = on the funeral pile; abhyāt = and proceeded; svargam = to heaven."

"Pronouncing the curse thus on me, that couple wept many times pitiably, placed their body on the funeral pile and proceeded to heaven."

Sloka 59

59. devi = O; Kausalya! ṭat = such; etat = of this; pāpam = sin; kṛitam = committed; bālyāt = through ignorance; svayam = myself; tadā = then; shabdavedhyanukashhaṇā = by invoking an arrow and hitting an invisible object the sound of which was only heard; smṛitam = is remembered by me; chintayānena = on reflection."

"O, Kausalya! The sin I myself committed through ignorance on that day, by invoking an arrow and hitting an invisible object the sound of which was heard, I remember it now on reflection over it."

Sloka 60

60. devi = O; Queen! Vyāthiḥ yathā = as a disease (comes); sambhukte sati = after eating; annarase = a nutriment; apathyaiḥ saha = mixed with forbidden things; ayam vipākaḥ = this result; samupasthitaḥ = came; tasya karmaṇaḥ = of that (sinful) act."

"O, Kausalya! As a disease comes sometime after eating a nutriment mixed with forbidden things, this present occurrence came as a result of the sinful act by me long ago."

Sloka 61

tasmān mām āgatam bhadre tasya udārasya tat vacaḥ |
yad aham putra śokena samtyakṣyāmy adya jīvitam || 2-64-61

61. bhadre = my dear lady! ṭat vachaḥ = that word; tasya = of that sage; udārasya = who was noble; aham yat samtyakshhyāmi = that I shall give up; jīvitam = my life; putrashokena = due to grief for the loss of my son; āgatam = has come ( true); mama = to me; adya = now."

"O, my dear lady! Those words of that noble sage, saying that I shall give up my life due to grief for the loss of my son, have come true to me now."

Sloka 62

62. kausalye = O; Kausalya! na pashyāmi = I am not able to see; tvām = you; chakshhubhyām = with my eyes.spṛisha = touch; mām = me; sādhu = well; iti = thus; uktvā = spoke; bhūmipaḥ = king Dasaratha; trasto = terribly; rudan = weeping; ahacha = and again said; bhāryām = to his wife:

"O, Kausalya! I am not able to see you with my eyes. Touch me well" Thus speaking, King Dasaratha, terribly weeping said to his wife as follows:

Sloka 63

etanme sadṛśam devi yanmayā rāghave kṛtam |
sadṛśam tattu tasyaiva yadanena kṛtam mayi || 2-64-63

63. devi = O; queen!; yat = that which; kṛitam = was done; mayā = by me; rāghave = in respect of Rama; na sadṛisham = was not befitting of me; yat = ṭhat which; kṛitam = was done; anena = by Rama; mayi = in my case; tattu = that then; sadṛisham = was befitting; tasyaiva = to him only."

"O, queen! The wrong that was done by me in respect of Rama was not befitting of me. But the good that was done by Rama in my case was worthy only of him."

Sloka 64

64. kaḥ = which; vichakshhaṇaḥ = wise man; bhuvi = in this world; tyajet = can abandon; putram = his son; durvṛittamapi = even if; of a bad conduct? Kaḥ = which; sutaḥ = son; pravrājyamano api = even if he is sent to exile; nāsūyet = cannot become angry; pitaram = with his father? "

"Which wise man on this earth can abandon his son, even if he is of a bad conduct? Which son, even if he is sent to an exile, cannot become angry with his father?"

Sloka 65

65. rāmaḥ = can Rama; adya = now; samspṛishet = touch; mām = me; sakṛit = forthwith? labhetavā = or can approach me ? mānavāḥ = ṃen; anuprāptāḥ = who attained; yamakshhayam = the world of Yama; na drakshhyanti hi = cannot indeed see their kith and kin."

"Can Rama now touch me or approach me forth with? ṃen who attained the world of Yama (god of death) cannot even see their kith and kin."

Sloka 66

66. kausalye = O; Kausalya! na pashyāmi = I am not able to see; tvām = you; chakshhushhā = with my eyes; mama = ṃy; smṛitiḥ = memory; vilupyate = is fading away; ete dūtāḥ = these messengers; vai vasvatasya = of Yama; tvarayanti = are hastening; mām = me.

"O, Kausalya! I am not able to see you with my eyes. ṃy memory- power is fading away. ṃessengers of Yama (god of death) are hastening me to come."

Sloka 67

ataḥ tu kim duhkhataram yad aham jīvita kṣaye |
na hi paśyāmi dharmajñam rāmam satya parākyamam || 2-64-67

67. kimnu = what are be; duḥkhataram = more sorrowful; ataḥ = than = aham na pashyāmi hi itiyat = that thus I am not able to see; rāmam = Rama; dharmajJṇam = who knows righteousness; satyaparākramam = who is a truly brave man; jīvitakehhaya = at the time of my death?"

"What can be more sorrowful than the fact I am not able to see Rama, who knows righteousness and who is a truly brave man, at the time of my death?"

Sloka 68

"The grief arising out of non-appearance of that son, who performs unparalleled actions, is parching up my life, as sun- shine dries up a water drop"

Sloka 69

"They are not humans but celestials, who will be able to see again in the fifteenth year, the beautiful face of Rama, wearing his handsome ear-rings."

Sloka 70

70. dhanyāḥ = the fortunate; drakshhyanti = can see; mukham = the face; rāmasya = of Rama; padmapatrekshhaṇam = with its eyes resembling lotus leaves; subhruḥ = with its beautiful eye brows; sudamshhṭram = with its comely row of teeth; chārunāsikam = charming nose; tārādhipanibham = looking like a moon."

"The fortunate can see the face of Rama, looking like a moon, with its eyes resembling lotus leaves, with beautiful eye brows, with a comely row of teeth and a charming nose."

Sloka 71

71. dhanyāḥ = fortunate (are they); yaḥ = who; drakshhyanti = can see; mukham = the face; rāmasya = of Rama; sadṛisham = resembling; shāradasya = the autumnal; indoḥ = moon; sugandhi = sweat-smelling; phullasya kamalasya cha = and also resembling a full- blown lotus."

"Fortunate are they, who can see the sweat smelling face of Rama, resembling the autumnal moon and also a full- blown lotus."

Sloka 72

72. sukhinaḥ = happy ones; drakshhyanti = can see; rāmam = Rama; nivṛitta vanavāsam = having finished his exile; āgatam = and come; punaḥ = back; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; shukram yathā = like the planet Venus; mārga gatam = coming from a journey."

"Happy ones can see Rama, who having finished his exile, will come to Ayodhya like the planet Venus coming from a journey."

Sloka 73

73. kausalye = O; Kausalya! me hṛidayam = my heart; sīdatīva = is sinking down; chittamohena = by delution of mind; aham = I; na chavadaye = am not able to grasp; samyuktām = related; shabdasparsha rasām = the sound touch and the smell."

"O, Kausalya! ṃy heart is sinking down by delusion of mind. I am not able to grasp the related sound, touch and the smell."

Sloka 74

"All of my senses are getting scared away due to mental break down, as rays of light of a lamp whose oil is exhausted, starts splattering."

Sloka 75

"This grief caused by my own self is collapsing me, who am helpless and insensible, like a river bank being collapsed by the floods of a river stream."

Sloka 76

76. kva = where; gataḥ asi = have you gone? ṃahābāho = O;mighty armed! ḥā rāghava = O; Rama! ṃama āyasenāshana = O; annihilator of my grief! ḥā = Alas!; hā pitṛipriya = O; darling of your father! ṃe nātha = O;my protector!; hā suta = O; my son!"

"O, mighty armed! O, Rama! O, annihilator of my grief! Alas! O, darling of your father! O, my protector! O, my son! Where have you gone?"

Sloka 77

"O, Kausalya! O, Sumitra, the miserable! Alas! O, Kaikeyi, the cruel one! ṃy enemy! The disgrace of my family! I am going to die."

Sloka 78

iti rāmasya mātuśca sumitrāyāśca sannidhau |
rājā daśarathaḥ śocan jīvita antam upāgamat || 2-64-78

78. iti = in this manner; rājā = the king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha;iti = thus; sochan = weeping; upāgamat = reached jīvitāntam = the end of his life; sannidhou = in the presence; rāmasya mātasya = of Rama`s mother; sumitrāyāḥcha = and ṣumitra.

In this manner, King Dasaratha weeping as aforesaid, reached the end of his life in the presence of Kausalya and Sumitra.

Sloka 79

yathā tu dīnam kathayan nara adhipaḥ |
priyasya putrasya vivāsana āturaḥ |
gate ardha rātre bhṛśa duhkha pīḍitaḥ |
tadā jahau prāṇam udāra darśanaḥ || 2-64-79

79. narādhipaḥ = king Dasaratha; udāra darshanaḥ = of noble appearance; kathayan = thus speaking; dīnam = miserable and distressed ; putrasya vivāsanāturaḥ = for sending his son to exile; gate artharātre = by the time the mid-night passed; yadā = when; bhṛishaduḥkha pīDitaḥ = stricken with extreme grief; jahou = abandoned; prāṇam = his life; tadā = then.

King Dasaratha of noble appearance, thus speaking, who was already miserable and distressed for sending his son to exile, felt afflicted with extreme grief by the time the mid- night passed and forthwith abandoned his life.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe catuḥṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 64th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
