Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 65
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

At the dawn of the next day, panergysts and servants arrived at the palace to serve the king. Some women, who were empowered to go near the bed, detected the king's death and started crying loudly. Kausalya and Sumitra suddenly woke up from their slumber and came to know of the king's death. All the wives of Dasaratha wept miserably. The gynaecium was filled with men and women who felt bewildered with fear and restlessness; when the king met with his appointed end.

Sloka 1

atha rātryām vyatītāyām prātar eva apare ahani |
vandinaḥ paryupātiṣṭhaṃs tat pārthiva niveśanam || 2-65-1
sūtāḥ paramasaṃskārāḥ maṅgaldāścoottamaśrutāḥ |
gāyakāḥ stutiśīlāśca nigadantaḥ pṛthak pṛthak|| 2-65-2

1; 2. atha = thereafter; rātryām vyatītāyām = while the night passed away; prātaḥ eva = at the dawn itself; apare = of the following; ahari = day; paryupātishhṭhan = there approached; tat = that; pārthiva niveshanam = residence of the emperor; paramasamskārāḥ = highly cultured; uttamashrutāḥ = excellently learned; sūtāḥ = bards; maṇgaLāshcha = who were pronouncers of benedictions; stutishīlāḥ = and whose custom was to eulogize the king; gāyakāḥ = as well as singers; vandinaḥ = and panegyrists; nigadantaḥ = pronouncing ; pṛithak pṛithak = in different styles.

While the night passed away, there arrived at the residence of the emperor, at the dawn of the next day, highly cultured and excellently learned bards who were pronouncers of benedictions and whose custom was to eulogize the king, as well as singers and panegyrists uttering recitations in different styles.

Sloka 3

rājānam stutām teṣāmudāttābhihitāśiṣām |
prāsādābhogavistīrṇaḥ stutiśabdo hyavartata || 2-65-3

3. teshhām = their; stutishabdaḥ = sounds of praises; udattābhitāshishhā = uttering benedictions in a loud voice; stuvatām = and pronouncing accolades; rājānan = of the king; āvartatahi = pervaded; prāsādābhogavistīrṇaḥ = in full; the interior area of the palaces.

Their sounds of praises, uttering benedictions in a loud voice and pronouncing accolades of the king, pervaded in full the interior area of the palace.

Sloka 4

tatastu stuvatām teṣām sūtānām pāṇivādakāḥ |
avadānānyudāhṛtya pāṇivādā navādayan || 2-65-4

4. tataḥ = then; teshhām sūtānām = while those bards; stuvatām = were uttering praises; pāṇivādakāḥ = some others who make noises of claps; avādayān = pronounced claps; udāhṛitya = illustrating; avadānāmi = the glorious acts (of Dasaratha).

While those bards are uttering praises, others who make a bang of claps, patted the claps, eulogizing the previous glorious acts of Dasaratha.

Sloka 5

tena śabdena vihagāḥ pratibuddhā visasvanuḥ |
śākhāsthāḥ pañjarasthāśca ye rājakulagocarāḥ || 2-65-5

5. ye = which; vihagāḥ = birds; rājakula gocharāḥ = dwelling in the royal palaces; shākhāsthāḥ = on the branches of trees; panjarasthāḥcha = and in cages; pratibuddhāḥ = were awakened; tena shabdena = by that sound; visasvanuḥ = began to twitter.

The birds dwelling in royal palaces perched on the branches of trees and others in cages were awakened by that sound and began to twitter.

Sloka 6

vyāhṛtāḥ puṇyśabdāśca vīṇānām cāpi nissvanāḥ |
āśīrgeyam ca gāthānām pūrayāmāsa veśma tata || 2-65-6

6. puṇya shabdāshcha = the auspicious words; vyāhṛtāḥ = uttered; nissvanāḥcha = and the notes; vīṇānām = of Vina; āshīrgeyamcha = as well as blissful songs; gāthānām = of the metric verses; pūrayāmāsa = filled; tat = that; veshma = palace.

The auspicious words uttered by bards parrots etc and the notes of Vina as well as blissful songs of the metric verses filled that palace.

Sloka 7

tataḥ śuci samācārāḥ paryupasthāna kovidaḥ |
strī varṣa vara bhūyiṣṭhāupatasthur yathā puram || 2-65-7

7. tataḥ = then; yathā puram = as before; shuchi samāchārāḥ = those who maintain pure practices; paryupasthāna kovidāḥ = and those who were skilled in the services to the king; strīvarshhavarabhūyishhṭhāḥ = most of whom were women and eunuchs ; upatasthuḥ = arrived to serve(the king).

Then, as before, persons who maintain pure practices and those who were skilled in the services to the king, most of whom were women and eunuchs, arrived there to serve the king.

Sloka 8

hari candana sampṛktam udakam kāncanaiḥ ghaṭaiḥ |
āninyuḥ snāna śikṣā ājñā yathā kālam yathā vidhi || 2-65-8

8. snāna shikshhājJṇāḥ = those who took care of royal ablutions; āninyuḥ = brought; udakam = water; harichandana samvṛiktam = spinkled with yellow sandal; kāṇchanaiḥ = in golden; ghaṭaiḥ = pitchers; yathākālam = in accordance with the appropriate time; yathāvidhi = and according to the set procedures.

Those who took care of royal ablutions brought water sprinkled with yellow sandal in golden pitchers in accordance with appropriate time and the set procedures.

Sloka 9

mangala ālambhanīyāni prāśanīyān upaskarān |
upaninyus tathā api anyāḥ kumārī bahulāḥ striyaḥ || 2-65-9

9. tathā = in that manner; striyaḥapi = women too; kumarībahuLāḥ = most of whom were virgins; upaninyuḥ = brought; maṇgaLālamba nīyāni = all that were pleasing to touch prāshanīyān = eatables; upaskarān = decorations and ornaments.

In that manner, women too most of whom were virgins brought all that were pleasing to the touch, eatables, decorative clothing and ornaments.

Sloka 10

sarvalakṣaṇasampannam sarvam vidhivadarcitam |
sarvam suguṇalaksmīvattadbhabhūvābhihārikam || 2-65-10

10. sarvam = all; tat = that; abhihārikam = which was brought near him; abhūt = was; sarva lakshhaṇasampannam = rich with all auspicious characteristics; architam = was offered with reverence; vidhivat = as per prescribing scriptures; sarvam = all that; suguṇa lakshhmīvat = was extremely graceful.

All that was brought near him was endowed with all auspicious characteristics offered with reverence as per prescribed scriptures. All that was extremely graceful.

Sloka 11

tataḥ sūryodayam yāvatsarvam parisamutsukam |
tasthāvanupasamprāptam kim svidityupaś || 2-65-11

11. tataḥ = thereafter; sarvam = all that retinue; parisamutsukam = greatly excited; anupasamprāptam = without coming near to the king; upashaṇkitam = with a suspicion; kim svit iti = as to what was it; tasthou = stayed thus; sūryodayam yāvat = till sun rise.

All that retinue, greatly excited as they were, without coming near to the king and with a suspicion as to what was it, stayed thus till sunrise.

Sloka 12

atha yāḥ kosala indrasya śayanam pratyanantarāḥ |
tāḥ striyaḥ tu samāgamya bhartāram pratyabodhayan || 2-65-12

12. atha = then; yāḥ striyaḥ = which women; anantarāḥ = have no hindrance; shayanam prati = towards the bed; kosalendrāya = of Dasaratha; tāḥ tu = those women only; samāgamya = approached; bhartāram = their lord; pratyabodhayan = and woke him up.

The women who were empowered to go near the bed of Dasaratha went in proximity to him and woke him up.

Sloka 13

tathāpyucitavṛttāstā vinayena nayena ca |
na hyasya śayanam spṛṣṭvā kim cidapyupalebhire || 2-65-13

13. tathāpi = even so; tāḥ = those women; uchitavṛttāḥ = having proper conduct; vinayena = by their modesty; nayena = (and) by their prudence; spṛishhṭvā = touched; shayanam = the bed; asya = of Dasaratha; na upalebhire = and could not detect; kimchit api = anything either.

Even so, those modest and prudent women with proper conduct, touched Dasaratha's bed and could not detect any sign of life whatsoever in him.

Sloka 14

tāḥ strīyaḥ svapnaśīlajñāsceṣṭāsaṃcalanādiṣu |
tā vepathu parītāḥ ca rājñaḥ prāṇeṣu śankitāḥ || 2-65-14
pratisrotaḥ tṛṇa agrāṇām sadṛśam saṃcakampire |

14. tāḥ strīyaḥ = those women; svapnashīlajJṇāḥ = knew the condition of a body in sleep; cheshhṭā samchalanādishhu = by its gestures; movements and others; tāḥ = those women as such; shaṇkitaḥ = were apprehensive; rājJṇaḥ = of the king`s; prāṇeshhu = life; vepathuparītāḥcha = possessed of tremor; samchakāshire = and were revealing; pratisrotastṛiṇāgrāṇām = like tips of reeds standing against a stream.

Those women knew the condition of a body in sleep, by its gestures, movements and others. As such, they were possessed of tremor apprehensive as they were about the king's life and they resembled like tips of reeds standing against a stream.

Sloka 15

atha samvepamanānām strīṇām dṛṣṭvā ca pārthivam || 2-65-15
yat tat āśankitam pāpam tasya jajñe viniścayaḥ |

15. atha = now; dṛishhṭvācha = on seeing; pārthivam = the king; tasya = its; vinishchayaḥ = certainty; jaG^ye = arose; sandehamānānām = in the minds of doubting; strīṇām = women; yat = of which; pāpam = calamity; āshaṇkitam = that was apprehended.

Now, seeing the king, certainty arose in the minds of those doubting women about the calamity (of death) that was apprehended.

Sloka 16

kausalyā ca sumitrā ca putraśokaparājite || 2-65-16
prasupte na prabudhyete yathā kālasamanvite |

16. putra shoka parājite = overpowered by grief for their sons; prasupte = the slumbering; kausalyācha = Kausalya; sumitrācha = and ṣumitra; na prabudhyate = did not wake up; kālasamanvite yathā = as if they were completely possessed of death.

Overpowered by grief for their sons, the slumbering Kausalya and Sumitra did not wake up, as though they were completely overtaken by death.

Sloka 17

niṣprabhā ca vivarṇā ca sannā śokena sannatā || 2-65-17
na vyarājata kausalyā tāreva timirāvṛtā |

17. kausalya = Kausalya; nishhprabhācha = bereft of luster; vivarṇā = pale faced; sannā = emaciated; shokena = by grief; sannatā = and dispirited; na vyarājata = did not shine brightly; tāreva = like a star; timirāvṛitā = hidden away in darkness.

Kausalya bereft of luster, pale faced, emaciated with grief and dispirited, did not shine brightly (as before) like a star hidden away in darkness.

Sloka 18

kausalyānantaram rājñaḥ sumitrā tadantanaram || 2-65-18
na sma vibhrājate devī śokāśrululitānanā |

18. devī = the queen; kausalyā = Kausalya; anantaram = immediately close; rājJṇaḥ = to the king; sumitrā = and sumitra; tadanantaram = on her side; na sma vibhrājate = was not shining (as before); shokāshru lulitānanā = with her face ruffled by tears of grief.

The queen Kausalya, immediately close to the king and with Sumitra on her side, was not shining as before with her face ruffled by tears of grief.

Sloka 19

te ca dṛṣṭvā tathā supte śubhe devyau ca tam nṛpam || 2-65-19
suptame vodgataprāṇamantaḥ puramanyata |

19. antaḥpuram = the gynaecium; dṛishhṭvā = having seen; te devyon shubhe = those distinguished queens; supte = sleeping; tam = and that; nṛipam = king; tathā = in that manner; āmanyata = thought; udgataprāṇam = that the king had lost his life; suptameva = while merely sleeping.

The persons in the gynaecium, having seen those distinguished queens in slumber and also the king in that manner, thought that the king had lost his life while just sleeping.

Sloka 20

tataḥ pracukruśur dīnāḥ sasvaram tā vara anganāḥ || 2-65-20
kareṇavaiva araṇye sthāna pracyuta yūthapāḥ |

20. tataḥ = then; tāḥ varāṇganāḥ = those beautiful women; dīnaḥ = miserably; prachukrushuḥ = cried loudly; sasvaram = with their voice; kareṇavaḥ iva = like female elephants crying; sthāna prachyuta yūthapāḥ = removed from their place; araṇye = in the forest.

Then, those beautiful women miserably cried loudly with their voice, like female elephants crying when their group leader an elephant was removed from their place in the forest.

Sloka 21

tāsām ākranda śabdena sahasā udgata cetane || 2-65-21
kausalyā ca sumitrāca tyakta nidre babhūvatuḥ |

21. kausalyācha = Kausalya and; sumitrācha = ṣumitra; sahasā = suddenly; udgatachetane = obtained consciousness; tāsām ākranda shabdena = by their sound of cries; babhūvatuḥ = and became; tyakta nidre = relieved of sleep.

Kausalya and Sumitra suddenly obtained consciousness by the sound of their cries and woke up from sleep.

Sloka 22

kausalyā ca sumitrā ca dṛṣṭvā spṛṣṭvā ca pārthivam || 2-65-22
hā nātha iti parikruśya petatur dharaṇī tale |

22. kausalyācha = Kausalya; sumitrācha = and ṣumitra; dṛishhṭvā = on seeing; spṛishhṭvācha = and touching; pārthivam = the king; parikrushya = cried; hānāthā iti = as �O; lord!' patatuḥ = and fell; dharaṇītala = on the ground.

Kausalya and Sumitra, on seeing and touching king Dasaratha, cried �O, Lord!' and fell on the ground.

Sloka 23

sā kosala indra duhitā veṣṭamānā mahī tale || 2-65-23
na babhrāja rajo dhvastā tārā iva gagana cyutā |

23. veshhṭamānā = rolling; mahītale = on the floor; rajodhvastā = and covered by dust; sā = that; kausalyendra duhitā = daughter of Kosala king; Kausalya; na babhrāja = was not shining; tāreva = like a star; chyutā = slipped down; gaganāt = from the sky.

Rolling on the floor and covered by dust, Kausalya the daughter of Kosala king was not shining as before and resembled a star falling down from the sky.

Sloka 24

nṛpe śāntaguṇe jāte kausalyām patitām bhuvi || 2-65-24
āpaśyaṃstāḥ striyaḥ sarvā hatām nāgavadhūmiva |

24. nṛipe = the king; jāte = became; shāntaguṇe = one whose virtues were destroyed; ie. Deceased; tāḥstriyaḥ = all the women; apashyan = saw; kausalya = Kausalya; patitām = who fell; bhuvi = on the ground; nāgavadhū miva = like a female elephant; hatām = which was killed (and felled on ground).

While the king was deceased, all the women saw Kausalya who fell on the floor appearing as a female elephant, which was killed and felled on the ground.

Sloka 25

tataḥ sarvā narendrasya kaikeyīpramukhāḥ striyaḥ || 2-65-25
rudantyaḥ śokasantaptā nipeturgatacetanāḥ |

25. tataḥ = thereafter; kaikeyīpramukhāḥ = Kaikeyi and others; sarve = all; narendrasya = the king`s; striyaḥ = women; rudantyaḥ = were weeping; shokasantaptāḥ = being afflicted by grief; nipetuḥ = fell; gatachetanāḥ = unconscious.

Kaikeyi and all other wives of king Dasaratha were weeping afflicted by grief and fell unconscious.

Sloka 26

tābhiḥ sa balavānnādaḥ krośantībhiranudrutaḥ || 2-65-26
yena sphītīkṛto bhūyastadgṛham samanādayat |

26. balavān = great; saḥnādaḥ = that noise; anudrutaḥ = = supplemented; tābhiḥ = by those women; kroshantībhiḥ = wailing; sphītīkṛitaḥ = was made greater; bhūyaḥ = more; yena = by which; tat = that; gṛiham = house;

That great noise was supplemented by those wailing women, bloated by which, it rendered the house all the more noisy.

Sloka 27

tat samuttrasta sambhrāntam paryutsuka jana ākulam || 2-65-27
sarvataḥ tumula ākrandam paritāpa ārta bāndhavam |
sadyo nipatita ānandam dīna viklava darśanam || 2-65-28
babhūva nara devasya sadma diṣṭa antam īyuṣaḥ |

27; 28. tatsadma = that gynoecium; naradevasya = of the king; īyushhaḥ = who met; dishhṭāntam = the appointed end (death); santrapta sambhrānta paryutsaka janākulam = was filled with people bewildered with fear and restlessness; tumulākrandam = with tumultuous wailing; sarvatāḥ = all around; paritāpārtabāndhavan = having relatives disturbed by sorrow; nipatitānandam = with its happiness withered away; sadyaḥ = suddenly; babhūva = and became; dīnaviklaba darshanam = a sight of misery and despondency.

That gynaecium of the king who met his appointed end was filled with people who felt bewildered with fear and restlessness, with tumultuous wail all around, and crowded with relatives disturbed by sorrow, with happiness withered away in it suddenly and became a sight of misery and despondency.

Sloka 29

atītam ājñāya tu pārthiva ṛṣabham |
yaśasvinam samparivārya patnayaḥ |
bhṛśam rudantyaḥ karuṇam suduhkhitāḥ |
pragṛhya bāhū vyalapann anāthavat || 2-65-29.

29. ājJṇāya = knowing; yashasvinam = the glorious; pārthivarshhabham = king of excellence; atītam = had passed away; (his wives); samparivārya = surrounded him; suduḥkhitāḥ = with great sorrow; karuṇam = pitiably; rudantyaḥ = wailing; bhrusham = profusely; vylapan = and lamented; pragṛihya = grasping; bāhum = their hands; (one another); anāthavāt = like orphans.

Knowing that the glorious king of excellence had passed away, the king's wives surrounded him with great sorrow; pitiably wailing profusely and lamented, grasping their hands one another, like orphans.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe pañcaṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 65th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
