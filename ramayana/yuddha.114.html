Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 114
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama sends Vibhishana to bring Seetha to his presence. Vibhishana brings Seetha to Rama's presence. Seetha casts her looks on the moon-like face of Rama, her beloved husband.

Sloka 1

tamuvāca mahāprajñamabhigamya plavaṅgamaḥ |
rāmaṃ vacanamarthajño varaṃ sarvadhanuṣmatām || 6-114-1

1. saḥ mahāprājJṇaḥ = that highly intelligent; plavangamaḥ = ḥanuma; abhivādya = having offered his salutation; uvācha = spoke; tam rāmam = to that Rama; varam sarvadhanuṣhmatām = who was excellent among all the wielders of the bow; kamala patrākṣham = and whose eyes resembled the louts-petals.

Having offered his salutation to that Rama, who was excellent among all the wielders of the bow and whose eyes resembled the lotus-petals, that highly intelligent Hanuma spoke to him as follows:

Sloka 2

yannimitto.ayamārambhaḥ karmaṇāṃ ca phalodayaḥ |
tāṃ devīṃ śokasantaptāṃ maithilīṃ draṣṭumarhasi || 6-114-2

2. arhasi = you ought; draṣhṭum = to see; tām devīm = that divine lady; shoka samtaptām = who is consumed by grief; yannimittaḥ = for whose sake; ayam = this; ārambhaḥ = undertaking; karmaṇām = of acts (was done); yaḥ = and which; phalodayaḥ = has borne fruit.

"You ought to see Seetha that divine lady who is consumed by grief, for whose sake this course of actions was undertaken and which has (now) borne fruit."

Sloka 3

sā hi śokasamāviṣṭā bāṣpaparyākulekṣaṇā |
maithilī vijayaṃ śrutvā tava harṣamupāgamat || 6-114-3

3. shrutvā = hearing; vijayam = the news of your victory; sā maithilī = that ṣeetha; shoka samāviṣhṭā = stricken as she was with grief; abhikāṇkṣhati = is (now) longing; draṣhṭum = to see; tvām = you; bāṣhpa paryākulekṣhaṇā = her eyes filled with tears of joy.

"Hearing the news of your victory, that Seetha, stricken as she was with grief, is now longing to see you, her eyes filled with tears of joy."

Sloka 4

pūrvakātpratyayāccāhamukto viśvastayā tayā |
bhartāraṃ draṣṭumicchāmi kṛtārthaṃ sahalakṣmaṇam || 6-114-4

4. tayā = by her; vishvastayā = who has trust (in me); pratyayāt = because of confidence which had been inspired by me; pūrvakāt = on a former occasion; aham = I; uktaḥ = was spoken (as follows); 'ichchhāmi = I desire; draṣhṭum = to see; bhartāram = my husband; kṛitārtham = who has accomplished his purpose; saha lakṣhmaṇam = together with Lakshmana.

"By her, who has trust in me because of confidence which has trust in me because of confidence which had been inspired me on a former occasion, I was spoken as follows: 'I desire to see my husband, who has accomplished his purpose, together with Lakshmana.'"

Sloka 5

evamukto hanumatā rāmo dharmabhṛtāṃ varaḥ |
agacchatsahasā dhyānamāsīdbāṣpapariplutaḥ || 6-114-5

5. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; hanumatā = by ḥanuma; rāmaḥ = Rama; varaḥ = who was the foremost; dharmabhṛitām = among the supporters of righteousness; īṣhat bāṣhpa pariplutaḥ = was shightly overwhelmed with tears; sahasā = and suddenly; āgachchhat dhyānam = became a bit thoughtful.

Hearing Hanuma's words, Rama who was the foremost among the supporters of righteousness, was a little over whelmed with tears and suddenly became a bit thoughtful.

Sloka 6

dīrghamuṣṇaṃ ca niśvasya medinīm avalokayan |
uvāca meghasaṅkāśaṃ vibhīṣaṇamupasthitam || 6-114-6

6. viniḥ shvasya dīrgham uṣhṇam = Drawing a deep and warm breath; avalokayam = and casting his looks; jagatīm = on the ground; uvācha = he spoke (as follows); vibhīṣhaṇam = to Vibhishana; meghasamkāsham = who closely resembled a cloud in hue; upasthitan = and who was standing nearby.

Drawing a deep and warm breath and casting his looks on the ground, he spoke (as follows) to Vibhishana, who closely resembled a cloud in hue and who was standing nearby.

Sloka 7

divyāṅgarāgāṃ vaidehīṃ divyābharaṇabhūṣitām |
iha sītāṃ śiraḥsnātāmupasthāpaya māciram || 6-114-7

7. upasthāpaya = bring; iha = here; sītām = ṣeetha; shiraḥ snānam = after she has bathed her head; divyāṇ^garāgām = has been anointed with charming cosmetics; divyābharaṇa bhūṣhitām = and adorned with beautiful jewels; mā chiram = let there be no delay.

"Bring here Seetha, after she has bathed her head, has been anointed with charming cosmetics and adorned with beautiful jewels. Let there be no delay."

Sloka 8

evamuktastu rāmeṇa tvaramāṇo vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
praviśyāntaḥpuraṃ sītāṃ strībhiḥ svābhiracodayat || 6-114-8

8. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmena = by Rama; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana; pravishya = entering; antaḥpuram = the gyaecium; tvaramāṇaḥ = in haste; achodayat = communicated his presence; sītām = to ṣeetha; svābhiḥ strībhiḥ = through his own women.

Hearing the words of Rama, Vibhishana, after entering the gynaecium in haste, communicated his presence to Seetha, through his own women.

Sloka 9

tataḥ sītāṃ mahābhāgāṃ dṛṣṭvovāca vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
mūrdhni baddhāñjaliḥ śrīmānvinīto rākṣaseśvaraḥ || 6-114-9

9. tataḥ = then; shrīmān = the glorious; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; rākṣhaseshvaraḥ = the king of demons; dṛiṣhṭvā = after seeing; māhābhāgām = the highly fortunate; sītām = ṣeetha; baddhāṇ^jaliḥ = with his palms joined; mūrdhni = over his head; vinītaḥ = humbly; uvācha = spoke (as follows):

Then, the glorious Vibhishana the king of demons, after seeing the highly fortunate Seetha, with his palms joined over his head, humbly spoke to her as follows:

Sloka 10

divyāṅgarāgā vaidehī divyābharaṇabhūṣitā |
yānamāroha bhadraṃ te bhartā tvāṃ draṣṭumicchati || 6-114-10

10. vaidehi = O ṣeetha!; divyāṇgarāgāḥ = anointed with charming cosmetics; divyābharaṇa bhūṣhitāḥ = and adorned with beautiful jewels; bhadram te = if you please; āroha = mount; yānam = on the vehicle; bhartā = your husband; ichchhati = wants; draṣhṭum = to see; tvām = you.

"O Seetha! Anointed with charming cosmetics and adorned with beautiful jewels if you please, mount on the vehicle. Your husband wants to see you."

Sloka 11

evamuktā tu vaidehī pratyuvāca vibhīṣaṇam |
asnātā draṣṭumicchāmi bhartāraṃ rākṣasādhipa || 6-114-11

11. evam = thus; uktā = spoken; vaidehī = ṣeetha; pratyuvācha = replied; vibhīṣhaṇam = to Vibhishana (as follows): rākṣhaseshvara = O king of demons!; ichchhāmi = I wish; draṣhṭum = to see; bhartāram = my husband; asnātvā = even without taking the bath.

Hearing the words of Vibhishana, replied as follows: "O king of demons! I wish to see my husband, even without taking my bath."

Sloka 12

tasyāstadvacanaṃ śrutvā pratyuvāca vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
yathāhaṃ rāmo bhartā te tattathā kartumarhasi || 6-114-12

12. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; tasyāḥ = of ṣeetha; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; pratyuvācha = replied; arhasi = you ought; kartum = to do; yathā tathā = in whatever manner; rāmaḥ = Rama; te bhartā = your husband; āha = mentioned; tat = that.

Hearing those words of Seetha, Vibhishana replied as follows: "You ought to do the bidding of Rama, your husband, as he has enjoined you to do."

Sloka 13

tasya tadvacanaṃ śrutvā maithilī bhrātṛdevatā |
bhartṛbhaktivratā sādhvī tatheti pratyabhāṣata || 6-114-13

13. shrutvā = hearing; tat rachanam = those words; tasya = of Vibhishana; sādhvī = the virtuous; maithilī = ṣeetha; patidevatā = regarding her husband as a divinity; vṛitā = endowed as she was; bhartṛi bhaktyā = with a devotion to her husband; pratyabhāṣhata = replied; tatheti = "so it be!"

Hearing those words of Vibhishana, the virtuous Seetha, regarding her husband as a divinity, endowed as she was with a devotion to her husband, said in reply, "So t be!"

Sloka 14

tataḥ sītāṃ śiraḥsnātāṃ yuvatībhiralaṅkṛtām |
mahārhābharaṇopetāṃ mahārhāmbaradhāriṇīm || 6-114-14
āropya śibikāṃ dīptāṃ parārdhyāmbarasaṃvṛtām |
rakṣobhirbahubhirguptāmājahāra vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-114-15

14-15. tataḥ = then; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; ājahāra = brought; sītām āropya = prevailing upon ṣeetha to ascend; dīptām shibikām = a shining palanquin; parārghyāmbara samvṛitām = covered with an exceedingly valuable cloth; guptām = and guarded; bahubhiḥ rākṣhasaiḥ = by numerous demons; shirāsnātām = after she has bathed her head; samyuktām pratikarmaṇā = after making use of toilet and other cleansing facilities; mahārhāmbaradhāraṇīm = had put on very costly robes; mahārhābharaṇopetām = and had been adorned with exceedingly valuable jewels.

Prevailing upon Seetha to ascend a shining palanquin, covered with an exceedingly valuable cloth and guarded by numerous demons, after she had bathed her head and cleansed her body, had put on costly robes and had been adorned with exceedingly valuable jewels, Vibhishana then brought her to the presence of Rama.

Sloka 16

so.abhigamya mahātmānaṃ jñātvābhidhyānamāsthitam |
praṇataśca prahṛṣṭaśca prāptāṃ sītāṃ nyavedayat || 6-114-16

16. abhigamya = approaching; mahātmānam = the great-souled Rama; āsthitam = who was absorbed in thought; jJṇātāpi = even after coming to know that ṣeetha had arrived; saḥ = vibhishana; praṇataḥ cha = offered his obeisance; prahṛiṣhṭashcha = and with full of great joy; nyavedayat = announced to him; sītām prāptām = that ṣeetha had arrived.

Approaching the great-souled Rama, who was absorbed in thought, even after coming to know that Seetha had arrived, Vibhishana offered his obeisance and with full of great joy, announced to him that Seetha had arrived.

Sloka 17

tāmāgatāmupaśrutya rakṣogṛhaciroṣitām |
harṣo dainyaṃ ca roṣaśca trayaṃ rāghavamāviśat || 6-114-17

17. upashrutya = hearing; tām āgatām = that ṣeetha had arrived; rakṣhogṛiha chiroṣhitām = after living long in the abode of a demon; rāghavam = Rama; āvishat = was filled; harṣhaḥ = with jo; roṣhashcha = indignation; dianyam = and felt miserable (too); trayam = all the three (at once).

Hearing that Seetha had arrived after living long in the abode of a demon, Rama was filled with joy, indignation and felt miserable too all the three emotions at the same time.

Sloka 18

tataḥ pārśvagataṃ dṛṣṭvā savimarśaṃ vicārayan |
vibhīṣaṇamidaṃ vākyamahṛṣṭo rāghavo.abravīt || 6-114-18

18. ahṛiṣhṭaḥ = feeling unhappy; vichārayan = on considering; savimarsham = with deep thought; (the question of ṣeetha having come in a palanquin; all the way); rāghavaḥ = Rama; tataḥ = then; abravīt = spoke; idam vachaḥ = the following words; dṛiṣhṭvā = looking at; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana; pārshvagatam = who was by his side.

Feeling unhappy on considering with deep thought, the question of Seetha having come in a palanquin, all the way, Rama then spoke the following words, looking at Vibhishana who was beside him.

Sloka 19

rākṣasādhipate saumya nityaṃ madvijaye rata |
vaidehī saṃnikarṣaṃ me śīghraṃ samupagacchatu || 6-114-19

19. saumya rākṣhasādhipate = O the gentle king of demons; nityam rata = who is forever intent; madvijaya = on my victory! vaidehī = (Let) ṣeetha; samupagachchhatu = duly seek; me samnikaṣham = my presence; shīghram = quickly.

"O the gently king of demons, who is forever intent on my victory! Let Seetha duly seek my presence quickly."

Sloka 20

sa tadvacanamājñāya rāghavasya vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
tūrṇamutsāraṇe yatnaṃ kārayāmāsa sarvataḥ || 6-114-20

20. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; tasya rāghavasya = of that Rama; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; dharmavit = who knew what was right; utsāraṇam kārayāmāsa = began to disperse the people; tatra = there; tūṇam = quickly.

Hearing those words of that Rama, Vibhishana, who knew what was right, began to disperse the crowd there quickly.

Sloka 21

kañcukoṣṇīṣiṇastatra vetrajharjharapāṇayaḥ |
utsārayantaḥ puruṣāḥ samantātparicakramuḥ || 6-114-21

21. kaṇ^chakoṣhṇīṣhiṇaḥ = demons wearing jackets and turbans; vetrajarjana pāṇayaḥ = their hands carrying staffs which made a jingling sound; samantāt parichakramuḥ = walked all round; tatra = there; utsārayantaḥ = dispersing; tān = those; yodhān = warriors.

Demons wearing jackets and turbans, their hands carrying staffs which made a jingling sound, walked there all round, dispersing those warriors.

Sloka 22

ṛkṣāṇāṃ vānarāṇāṃ ca rākṣasānāṃ ca sarvataḥ |
vṛndānyutsāryamāṇāni dūramutsasṛjustataḥ || 6-114-22

22. vṛindāni = crowds; ṛikṣhāṇām = of bears; vānarāṇām cha = monkeys; rākṣhasānām cha = and demons; utsārya māṇāni = dispersed; sarvashaḥ = on all sides; utthasthuḥ = bounced; dūram = for a distance; antataḥ = from their nearness (to the palanquin).

Crowds of bears, monkeys and demons, dispersed on all sides, bounced for a distance, from their nearness to the palanquin.

Sloka 23

teṣāmutsāryamāṇānāṃ sarveṣāṃ dhvanirutthitaḥ |
vāyunodvartamānasya sāgarasyeva nisvanaḥ || 6-114-23

23. teṣhām utsāryamāṇānām = while they were being dispersed (in that way); abhūt = there was; sumahān svanaḥ = a very great sound; sāgarasya niḥsvanaḥ iva = resembling the roar of sea; uddhūyamānasya = lashed; vāyunā = by a storm.

While those warriors were being driven away, there was a very great sound, resembling the roar of a sea, lashed by a storm.

Sloka 24

utsāryamāṇāṃstāndṛṣṭvā samantājjātasambhramān |
dākṣiṇyāttadamarṣācca vārayāmāsa rāghavaḥ || 6-114-24

24. dṛiṣhṭyā = seeing; utsāryamāṇān = them being dispersed on all sides; jātasambhramān = excited; rāmaḥ = Rama; atha = then; vārayāmāsa = stopped; tat = it; dākṣhiṇyāt = out of kindness (for those who were being driven away); amarṣhāt = and resentment (at the behaviour of the demons who were dispersing them).

Seeing them being dispersed on all sides excited, Rama then stopped that operation of those who were dispersing them, out of kindness (for those who were being driven away) and resentment (at the behaviour of the demons who were dispersing them).

Sloka 25

saṃrabdhaścābravīdrāmaścakṣuṣā pradahanniva |
vibhīṣaṇaṃ mahāprājñaṃ sopālambhamidaṃ vacaḥ || 6-114-25

25. rāmaḥ = Rama; samrabdhaḥ cha = enraged as he was; chakṣhuṣhā pradahanniva = with his looks as though burning; abravīt = spoke idam vachaḥ = the follwoing words; sopālambham = with a reproach; mahāprājJṇam vibhīṣhaṇam = to the highly intelligent Vibhishana.

The enraged Rama, consuming the demons with his looks as it were, Rama spoke the following reproaching words to the highly intelligent Vibhishana

Sloka 26

kimarthaṃ māmanādṛtya kṛśyate.ayaṃ tvayā janaḥ |
nivartayainamudyogaṃ jano.ayaṃ svajano mama || 6-114-26

26. kimartham = why; ayam janaḥ = these people; klishyate = are harassed; tvayā = by you; anādṛitya = disregarding; mām = me?; nivartaya = stop; udyogam = this exertion; ayam = these; janaḥ = people; svajanaḥ = are my own people.

"Why disregarding me, are these people harassed by you? Stop this exertion. They are my own people."

Sloka 27

na gṛhāṇi na vastrāṇi na prākārāstiraskriyāḥ |
nedṛśā rājasatkārā vṛttamāvaraṇaṃ striyaḥ || 6-114-27

27. gṛihāṇi = An apartment; na = is not; āvaraṇam = a thing that protects; striyāḥ = a woman; na = nor; vastrāṇi = robes; na = nor; prākārāḥ = compound-walls; na = nor; tiraskṛiyā = the concealments; na = nor; īdṛishāḥ = such; rāja satkārāḥ = royal honours; vṛittam = ḥer character is her shield.

"An apartment is not a thing that protects a woman, nor robes, nor compound-walls, nor concealments nor such royal honours. Her character is her shield."

Sloka 28

vyasaneṣu na kṛcchreṣu na yuddhe na svayaṃ vare |
na kratau no vivāhe ca darśanaṃ duṣyate striyaḥ || 6-114-28

28. stiyāḥ = A woman; darshanam = becoming visible; vyasaneṣhu = in times of a clamity; na dūṣhyate = is not condemned; na = nor; kṛichchheṣhu = in battles; svayamvare = in self-choosing of a husband by a princess at a public assemly of suitors; na = nor; kratua = in sacrificial ceremonies; na vā = nor; vivāhe = in marriage functions.

"A woman becoming visible to public in times of a calamity is not condemned in difficult situations, nor in battles, nor in self-choosing of a husband by a princess at a public assembly of suitors, nor in sacrificial ceremonies nor in marriage-functions."

Sloka 29

saiṣā yuddhagatā caiva kṛcchre mahati ca sthitā |
darśane.asyā na doṣaḥ syānmatsamīpe viśeṣataḥ || 6-114-29

29. sā eṣhā = the yonder ṣeetha; vipadgatā chaiva = is in distress; sthitā = and beset; mahati = with a great; kṛichchhre = difficulty; nāsti = there is no; doṣhaḥ = fault; ayāḥ darshane = in her becoming visible in public; visheṣhātaḥ = particularly; matsamīpe = in my presence.

"The younder Seetha is in distress and beset with a great difficulty. There is no fault in her appearance in public, particularly in my presence."

Sloka 30

visṛjya śibikāṃ tasmātpadbhyāmevopasarpatu |
samīpe mama vaidehīṃ paśyantvete vanaukasaḥ || 6-114-30

30. tasmāt = that is why; upasarpatu = let her come; padbhyāmeva = on foot alone; utsṛijya = leaving; shibikām = the palanquin; vanaukasaḥ = let these monkeys; pashyantu = see; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; mama samīpe = in my presence.

"That is why, let her come on foot alone, leaving the palanquin there. Let these monkeys see Seetha in my presence."

Sloka 31

evamuktastu rāmeṇa savimarśo vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
rāmasyopānayatsītāṃ saṃnikarṣaṃ vinītavat || 6-114-31

31. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmeṇa = by Rama; savimarshaḥ = the intelligent; vibhīṣhaṇah = Vibhishana; vinītavat = with decency; upānayat samnikarṣham sītām = brought ṣeetha to the vicinity; rāmasya = of Rama.

Hearing the words of Rama the intelligent Vibhishana, with decency, brought Seetha to Rama's vicinity.

Sloka 32

tato lakṣmaṇasugrīvau hanūmāṃśca plavaṅgamaḥ |
niśamya vākyaṃ rāmasya babhūvurvyathitā bhṛśam || 6-114-32

32. nishamya = hearing; vākyam = the words; rāmasya = of Rama; lakṣhmaṇa sugrīvau = Lakshmana; ṣugreeva; hanūmāmshcha = and ḥanuma; plavangamaḥ = the monkey; tataḥ = then babhūvuḥ = became; bṛisham = very much; vyatitau = perturbed.

Hearing the words of Rama, Lakshmana Sugreeva and Hanuma the monkey then felt very much perturbed.

Sloka 33

kalatranirapekṣaiśca iṅgitairasya dāruṇaiḥ |
aprītamiva sītāyāṃ tarkayanti sma rāghavam || 6-114-33

33. dāruṇaiḥ iṇ^gitaiḥ = from the pitiless facial features; asya = of Rama; kalatra nirapekṣhaiḥ = showing indifference to his consort; tavkayanti sma = they conjectured; rāghavam iva = as if Rama; aprītam = had a displeasure; sītāyām = towards ṣeetha.

From the pitiless facial features of Rama, showing indifference to his consort, they conjectured as if Rama had some displeasure towards Seetha.

Sloka 34

lajjayā tvavalīyantī sveṣu gātreṣu maithilī |
vibhīṣaṇenānugatā bhartāraṃ sābhyavartata || 6-114-34

34. sā = that; maithilī = ṣeetha; for her part; avalīyanti = shrinking; gātreṣhu sveṣhu = into her limbs; lajjayā = with modesty; abhyavartata = approached; bhartāram = her husband; anugatā = followed; vibhīṣhaṇena = by Vibhishana.

Seetha, for her part, shrinking into her limbs with modesty, approached her husband, duly followed by Vibhishana.

Sloka 35

vismayācca praharṣācca snehācca paridevatā |
udaikṣata mukhaṃ bhartuḥ saumyaṃ saumyatarānanā || 6-114-35

35. saumya tarānanā = the pleasant faced ṣeetha; patidevatā = who considered her husband as the divinity; udaikṣhata = saw; saumyam = the charming; mukham = face; bhartuḥ = of her husband; vismayāchcha = with a surprise; praharṣhāchcha = with a surprise; praharṣhāchcha = rejoice; snehāchcha = and affection.

The pleasant-faced Seetha, who considered her husband as the divinity, saw the charming face of her husband, with a surprise, rejoice and affection.

Sloka 36

atha samapanudanmanaḥklamaṃ sā |
suciramadṛṣṭamudīkṣya vai priyasya |
vadanamuditapūrṇacandrakāntaṃ |
vimalaśaśāṅkanibhānanā tadāsīt || 6-114-36

36. atha = then; sā = that ṣeetha; vimala shashāṇ^kanibhānanā = with her face resembling the bright moon; tadānīm = at that time; udīkṣhya = seeing; vadanam = the face; priyasya = of her beloved husband; adṛiṣhṭam = which had not been seen; suchiram = for a long time; uditachandra pūrṇa kāntam = and which was charming like the rising full-moon; apanudat = and dispelled; manaḥ klamam = her mental fatigue.

Seeing the face of her beloved husband, which had not been seen for a long time and which was charming like the rising full moon, she forth dispelled her mental fatigue.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe caturdaśādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 114th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
