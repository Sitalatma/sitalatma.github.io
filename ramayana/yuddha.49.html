Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 49
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Returning to consciousness, Rama laments over the plight of Lakshmana, who was lying unconscious. The foremost of monkeys too were plunged in grief and despondency on seeing Rama lamenting as aforesaid. In the meantime, Vibhishana approaches Rama and the monkeys flee away, mistaking him as Indrajit.

Sloka 1

ghoreṇa śara bandhena baddhau daśaratha ātmajau |
niśvasantau yathā nāgau śayānau rudhira ukṣitau || 6-49-1
sarve te vānara śreṣṭhāh sasugrīvā mahā balāḥ |
parivārya mahātmānau tasthuh śoka pariplutāḥ || 6-49-2

1; 2. baddhau = bound; ghoreṇa = by that formidable; sharabandhena = net work of arrows; mahātmanau = the high-souled; dasharātmajau = sons of Dasaratha (Rama and lakshmana); shayānau = lay; rudhirokṣitau = bathed in blood; niḥshvasantau = breething; nāgau yathau = like serpants; sarve = all; te vānarashreṣṭhaḥ = those foremost of monkeys; mahābalāh = who were exceedingly stron; sa sugrīvāḥ = including ṣugreeva; shokapariplutāḥ = plunged in grief; tasthuḥ = were standing; parivārya = round; (the two illustrious warriors).

Bound by that formidable network of arrows, the two high-souled sons of dasaratha lay bathed in blood, breathing like serpants. All those foremost of monkeys who were exceedingly strong, including Sugreeva, plunged in grief, were standing around those two illustrious warriors.

Sloka 3

etasminn antere rāmah pratyabudhyata vīryavān |
sthiratvāt sattva yogāc ca śaraih saṃdānito api san || 6-49-3

3. etasmin antare = in the meanwhile; vīryavān = the mighty; rāmaḥ = Rama; sthiratvāt sattvayogāt cha = by virtue of his hardihood and native strength; pratyabuddhata = awoke from his sworn; samdānito.api shāiḥ = despite the shifts that held him captive.

In the meanwhile, the mighty Rama by virtue of his hardihood and native strength, awoke from his swwon, despite the shafts that held him captive.

Sloka 4

tato dṛṣṭvā sarudhiram viṣaṇṇam gāḍham arpitam |
bhrātaram dīna vadanam paryadevayad āturaḥ || 6-49-4

4. tataḥ = then; dṛiṣṭvā = beholding; bhrātaram = his brother; sarudhiram = discharging blood; niṣaṇṇam = unconcious; gāDham = firmly; arpitam = thrown on the floor; dīnavadanam = and with a melancholic face; paryadevayat = (Rama) lamented; āturaḥ = full of grief (thus):

Then, beholding his brother, bleeding unconscious, firmly thrown on teh floor, and his features changed, Rama full of grief lamented thus:

Sloka 5

kim nu me sītayā kāryam kim kāryam jīvitena vā |
śayānam yo adya paśyāmi bhrātaram yudhi nirjitam || 6-49-5

5. kim nu kāryam = of what use; sītayā = is the recovery of ṣeetha; kim kāryam vā = or of what use; jīvitena = is even life itself; me = to me; yaḥ = since; pashyāmi = I am seeing; bhrātaram = my brother; adya = now; shayānam = lying; nirjitam = subdued; yudhi = in the fihgt.

"Of what use to me is the recovery of Seetha or even life itself, since my brother now lying, before my eyes, has been struck down in teh fight?"

Sloka 6

śakyā sītā samā nārī prāptum loke vicinvatā |
na lakṣmaṇa samo bhrātā sacivah sāmparāyikaḥ || 6-49-6

6. shakyā = It can be possible; vichinvatā = if I were to look for her; (to find); nārī = a consort; sītāsamā = equal to ṣeetha; martyaloke = in the world of mortals; na = but not; bhrātā = a brother; sachivaḥ = a friend; sāmparāyikaḥ = and a comrade in a hostile war; lakṣmaṇaḥ samaḥ = such as Lakshmana.

"It can be possible, if I were to look for her, to find a consort equal to Seetha in this world of mortals but not a brother, a friend and a comrade in a hostile war, such as Lakshmana!"

Sloka 7

parityakṣyāmy aham prāṇān vānarāṇām tu paśyatām |
yadi pancatvam āpannah sumitra ānanda vardhanaḥ || 6-49-7

7. āpannaḥ yadi = If he has returned; paṇchatvam = to the five elements; sumitrānanda vardhanaḥ = he; the increaser of ṣumitra's joy; aham = I; parityakṣyāmi = will yield up; prāṇān = my life-breaths; vānaraṇām = (while) the monkeys; pashyatām = stand looking on.

"If Lakshmana returned to the five elements, he the increaser of Sumitra's joy, I will yield up my life-breaths while the monkeys stand looking on."

Sloka 8

kim nu vakṣyāmi kausalyām mātaram kim nu kaikayīm |
katham ambām sumitrāṃca putra darśana lālasām || 6-49-8

8. kim nu vakṣyāmi = what shall I say; mātaram = to my mother; kausalyām = Kausalya?; kim nu = what shall I tell; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi?' katham = and how can I talk; ambām = to my mother; sumitrām = ṣumitra; putra darshanalālasām = who is keenly sighing for the sight of her son.

"What shall I say to my mother, Kausalya or to Kaikeyi? How can I talk to my mother Sumitra, sighing for the sight of her son?"

Sloka 9

vivatsām vepamānām ca krośantīm kurarīm iva |
katham āśvāsayiṣyāmi yadi yāsyāmi tam vinā || 6-49-9

9. katham = ḥow; āshvāsayiṣyāmi = shall I console; (ṣumitra); vepamānām cha = trembling; kroshantīm = and crying out; kurarīmiva = like an osprey; vivatsām = bereft of her son; Lakshmana; yāsyāmi yadi = if I return (to Ayodhya); tam vinā = without Lakshmana.

"How shall I console Sumitra, trembling and crying out like an osprey, bereft of her son, if I return to Ayodhya without lakshmana?"

Sloka 10

katham vakṣyāmi śatrughnam bharatam ca yaśasvinam |
mayā saha vanam yāto vinā tena āgatah punaḥ || 6-49-10

10. katham = how; vakṣyāmi = shall I tell shatrughnam = ṣhatrughna; yashashvinam = and the illustrious; bharatam = Bharata; aham = (when) I; āgataḥ = return; tena vinā = without Lakshmana; yātaḥ = who followed; vanam = to the forest; mayasaha = along with me?

"How shall I tell Shatrughna and the illustrious Bharata when I return without Lakshmana, who followed to the forest along with me?"

Sloka 11

upālambham na śakṣyāmi soḍhum bata sumitrayā |
iha eva deham tyakṣyāmi na hi jīvitum utsahe || 6-49-11

11. bata = Alas!; na shakṣyāmi = I should not be able; soDhum = to endure; upālambham = the reproaches; sumitrayā = of ṣumitra; tyakṣyāmi = I will leave; deham = my body; ihaiva = here itself; na utsahe = I do not wish; jīvitum = to live.

"Alas! I should not be able to endure Sumitra's reproaches. I will leave my body here itself. I do not wish to continue living."

Sloka 12

dhig mām duṣkṛta karmāṇam anāryam yat kṛte hy asau |
lakṣmaṇah patitah śete śara talpe gata asuvat || 6-49-12

12. dhik = woe; mām = unto me; duṣkṛita karmāṇam = to my wicked deed anāryam = and to my lack of nobility; matkṛite = through my fault; asau = this; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; pātitaḥ = was befallen; shete hi = and lies indeed; sharatalpe = on a bed of arrows; gatāsuvat = as me who has yielded up his life!

"Woe unto me to my wicked deed and to my lack of nobility. Through my fault, Lakshmana has fallen and lies indeed on a bed of arrows as on who has yielded up his life!"

Sloka 13

tvam nityam suviṣaṇṇam mām āśvāsayasi lakṣmaṇa |
gata asur na adya śaknoṣi mām ārtam abhibhāṣitum || 6-49-13

13. lakṣmaṇa = O; Lakshmana!; tvam = you āshvāsayasi = used to console; mām = me; nityam = always; suviṣaṇṇam = whenever I was in a great sorrow; gatāsuḥ = you; having lost your life; nāsi = are not; shaktaḥ = able; abhibhāṣitum = to talk; mām = to me; ārtam = afflicted with grief.

"O, Lakshmana! You always used to consoe me whenever I was in a great sorrow. You, having lost your life now are not able to allay my sufferings with your words."

Sloka 14

yena adya bahavo yuddhe rākṣasā nihatāh kṣitau |
tasyām eva kṣitau vīrah sa śete nihatah paraiḥ || 6-49-14

14. tvam yena = You; who; yuddhe = in this battle; adya = today; nihatāḥ = struck down; bahavaḥ = innumerable; rākṣasāaḥ = demons; kṣitau = to the earth; sheṣe = are fallen; nihataḥ = pierced; sharaiḥ = by darts; shūraḥ = like a hero; tasyāmeva = on the self same field.

"You, who in this battle today, struck down inummerable demons to the earth, are fallen, pierced by darts, like a hero on the self same field."

Sloka 15

śayānah śara talpe asmin sva śoṇita pariplutaḥ |
śara jālaiś cito bhāti bhāskaro astam iva vrajan || 6-49-15

15. shayānaḥ = lying; asmin sharatalpe = on this bed of arrows; shoṇitapariplutaḥ = bathed in blood; chitaḥ = you are piled up; sharajālaiḥ = in a heap of arrows; saḥ = you; as such; bhāsi = look; bhāskaraḥ iva = like the sun; vrajan = setting behind; astam = the horizon.

"Lying on this bed of arrows, bathed in blood, you are piled up; sharajālaiḥ= in a heap of arrows; saḥ= you, as such; bhāsi= look; bhāskaraḥ= iva= like the sun; vrajan= setting behind; astam= the horizon.

Sloka 16

bāṇa abhihata marmatvān na śaknoty abhivīkṣitum |
rujā ca abruvato hy asya dṛṣṭi rāgeṇa sūcyate || 6-49-16

16. bāṇābhihata marmatvāt = your vital parts having been pierced with arrows; na shaknoti = you are not able even; abhibhāṣitum = to speak (now); abruvato.api = eventhough you are not speaking; asya rujā = your agony; sūchyate = is disclosed; dṛiṣṭirāgeṇa = by the redness of your eyes.

"Your vital parts having been pierced with arrows, you are not able even to speak now. Eventhough you not speaking, your agony is disclosed by the redness of your eyes."

Sloka 17

yathā eva mām vanam yāntam anuyāto mahā dyutiḥ |
aham apy anuyāsyāmi tathaiva enam yama kṣayam || 6-49-17

17. anuyāsyāmi = I shall follow; enam = him; yamakṣayam = to the region of Yama; yathaiva = as; mahadyutuḥ = that illustrious warrior anuyātaḥ = accompanied; mām = me; ahamapi = when I; yāntam = retired; vanam = to the forest.

"I shall follow him to the region of Yama, as that illustrious warrior accompanied me when I retired to the forest."

Sloka 18

iṣṭa bandhu jano nityam mām ca nityam anuvrataḥ |
imām adya gato avasthām mama anāryasya durnayaiḥ || 6-49-18

18. nityam = ḥe who ever; iṣṭa bandhu janaḥ = loved his own kinsfolk; anuvrataḥ = and was filled with devotion; mām = for me; gataḥ = got; imām = this; avasthām = state; durnayaiḥ = to which the misdeeds; mama = of me; (have brought him); wretched that I am !

"He who loved his own kinsfolk and was filled iwth devotion for me, got this state to which my misdeeds have brought him, wretched that I am!"

Sloka 19

suruṣṭena api vīreṇa lakṣmaṇenā na saṃsmare |
paruṣam vipriyam vā api śrāvitam na kadācana || 6-49-19

19. na samsmare = I do not remember; shrāvitam = to have heard; kadāchana = at any time; paruṣam = harsh; vipriyam = and hateful words; lakṣmaṇena = by lakshmana; vīreṇa = the valiant man; suruṣṭenāpi = even if he is deeply provoked.

"I do not remember to have heard any harsh or hateful words from that valiant Lakshmana, even if he is deeply provoked."

Sloka 20

visasarja eka vegena panca bāṇa śatāni yaḥ |
iṣv astreṣv adhikas tasmāt kārtavīryāc ca lakṣmaṇaḥ || 6-49-20

20. yaḥ = he who; visasarja = was able to loose; paṇcha bāṇa shatāni = five hundred arrows; eka vegena = in one shot; tasmāt = such; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; adhikaḥ = was surpassing; kārtavīryāt cha = Kartavirya himself; iṣvastreṣu = in the science of archery.

"Lakshmana, who was able to loose five hundred arrows in one shot, was surpassing kartavirya himself in the science of archery."

Sloka 21

astrair astrāṇi yo hanyāt śakrasya api mahātmanaḥ |
so ayam urvyāmhatah śete mahā arha śayana ucitaḥ || 6-49-21

21. saḥ ayam = this Lakshmana; yaḥ = who; mahārha shayanochitaḥ = was accustomed to a rich couch; astraiḥ = and who by his arrows; hanyāt = severe; astrānapi = the weapons even; shakrasya = of Indra; mahātmanaḥ = the mighty one; shete = is lying; hataḥ = slain; urvyām = on the earth.

"This Lakshmana, who was accustomed to a rich couch and who by his arrows severe the weapons the mighty Indra the Lord of celestials, is lying slain on the ground."

Sloka 22

tac ca mithyā pralaptam mām pradhakṣyati na saṃśayaḥ |
yan mayā na kṛto rājā rākṣasānām vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-49-22

22. mithyā pralaptam = (ṭhose) vain words; na samshayaḥ = without doubt; pradhakṣyati = will consume; mām = me; yat tat = since; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; na kṛitaḥ = was not made; rajā = the king; rākṣasānām = of demons; mayā = by me.

"Those vain words uttered by me, will ndoubtedly consume me since I have not made Vibhishana the king of demons."

Sloka 23

asmin muhūrte sugrīva pratiyātum ito arhasi |
matvā hīnam mayā rājan rāvaṇo abhidraved balī || 6-49-23

23. sugrīva = O; ṣugreeva!; arhasi = you ought; pratiyātum = to return; asmin = this; muhūrte = moment; itaḥ = from here; (since); matvā = knowing (you are); hīnam = without; mayā = me; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; abhibhaviṣyat = will overcome (you); rājan = O; king!.

"O, Sugreeva! You ought to return at once from here since knowing that you are bereft of my support, Ravana will overcome you, O king!"

Sloka 24

angadam tu puras kṛtya sasainyah sasuhṛj janaḥ |
sāgaram tara sugrīva punas tena eva setunā || 6-49-24

24. sugrīva = O; ṣugreeva!; tara = re-cross; samudram = the sea; sasainyam = with you army; puraskṛitya = keeping Angada in front; aṇgadam puraskṛitya = keeping Angada in front; parichchhadam = with your followers; nīlena cha = ṇila; nalenacha = and ṇala.

"O, Sugreeva! Recross the sea with your army, keeping Angada in front and with your followers, Nila and Nala."

Sloka 25

kṛtam hanumatā kāryam yad anyair duṣkaram raṇe |
ṛkṣa rājena tuṣyāmi go lāngūla adhipena ca || 6-49-25

25. tuṣyāmi = I am fully satisfied; sumahat = by the great; karma = military exploit; kṛitam hi = acccomplished indeed; ṛikṣarajena = by Jambavan; the king of Bears; golāṇgūladhipena = and the general of the monkeys; yat = which; duṣkaram = was impossible; anyaiḥ = to any other; raṇe = in battle.

"I am fully satisfied by the great military exploit, done by Jambavan the king of Bears and the General of the ṃonkeys, which was impossible to any other in battle."

Sloka 26

angadena kṛtam karma maindena dvividena ca |
yuddham kesariṇā samkhye ghoram sampātinā kṛtam || 6-49-26

26. karma = A (great) act; kṛitam = was done; aṇ^gadena = by Angada; maindena = ṃainda; dvividena = and Dvivida; ghoram = A terrible; yuddham = combat; kṛitam = was done; kesariṇā = by Kesari; sampātinā = and ṣampati; samkhye = in the battle-field.

"A great act was done by Angada, ṃainda and Dvivida. A terrible combat was done by Kesari and Sampati in the battle-field."

Sloka 27

gavayena gava akṣeṇa śarabheṇa gajena ca |
anyaiś ca haribhir yuddham mad ārthe tyakta jīvitaiḥ || 6-49-27

27. gavayena = by Gavaya; gavākṣeṇa = Gavaksha; sharabheṇa = ṣharabha; gajena = Gaja; anyaiḥ = and other; haribhishcha = mokeys; tyakta jīvitaiḥ = who are willing to sacrifice their lives; madarthe = for me; yuddham = the battle was carried on.

"By Gavaya, Gavaksha, Sharabha, Gaja and other monkeys, who are willing to sacrifice their lives for me, the battle was carried on."

Sloka 28

na ca atikramitum śakyam daivam sugrīva mānuṣaiḥ |
yat tu śakyam vayasyena suhṛdā vā param tapa || 6-49-28
kṛtam sugrīva tat sarvam bhavatā adharma bhīruṇā |

28. sugrīva = O; ṣugreeva!; na cha shakyam = It is not possible; mānushaiḥ = for mortals; atikramitum = to avoid; daivam = their destiny; sugrīva = O; ṣugreeva; paramtapa = the tormentator of enemies!; dharma bhīruṇā = fearing to fail in your duty; kṛitam = it was done; tvayā = by you; sarvam = all; tat = that; yatha = which suhṛidā = a friend; vayasyena = and a comrade; shakyam = could do.

"O, Sugreeva! It is not possible for mortals to avoid their destiny. O, Sugreeva the tormentat of enemies! Fearing to fail in your duty, you have done all that which a friend and a comrade could do."

Sloka 29

mitra kāryam kṛtam idam bhavadbhir vānara ṛṣabhāḥ || 6-49-29
anujnātā mayā sarve yathā iṣṭam gantum arhatha |

29. vānararṣabhāḥ = O; foremost of monkeys!; idam = this; mitra kāryam = friendly act; kṛitam = has been done; bharadbhiḥ = by you; sarve = all of you; anujJṇātāḥ = are being permitted mayā = by me; arhatha = you ought; gantum = to go; yatheṣṭam = where it seems best to you!.

"O. foremost of ṃonkyes! You have accomplished all this due to your friendship. I take leave of you all; go where it seems best to you!"

Sloka 30

śuśruvus tasya te sarve vānarāh paridevitam || 6-49-30
vartayām cakrur aśrūṇi netraih kṛṣṇa itara īkṣaṇāḥ |

30. sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; kṛiṣṇatarekṣaṇāḥ = the tawny eyed one; ye = who; shushruvaḥ = heard; tasya = that Rama's; paridevitam = lamentation; ashrūṇi vartayām chakrire = allowed tears to fall netraiḥ = from their eyes.

All the tawny eyed monkeys, who heard Rama's lament thus, allowed tears to fall from their eyes.

Sloka 31

tataḥ sarvāṇy anīkāni sthāpayitvā vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-49-31
ājagāma gadā pāṇis tvarito yatra rāghavaḥ |

31. tataḥ = thereafter; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; sthāpayitvā = having establied order; sarvāṇi = in all; a nīkāni = the ranks; gadā pāṇih = mace in his land; ājagāma = come; tvaritam = quickly; yatra = where; rāghava = Rama (was then).

ṃeanwhile, Vibhishana having established order in all the ranks, with mace in his hand, came quickly to Rama.

Sloka 32

tam dṛṣṭvā tvaritam yāntam nīla anjana caya upamam || 6-49-32
vānarā dudruvuh sarve manyamānās tu rāvaṇim |

32. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = him; nīlāṇjana chayopamam = who resembled a mass of collyrium; tvaritam yāntam = hastening; (towards them thus); sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; manyamānāḥ = thinking; (him) rāvaṇim = to be Indrajit; the son of Ravana; dudruvuḥ = fled away.

Seeing him, who resembled a mass of collyrium, hastening towards them thus, all the monkeys thinking him to be Indrajit the son of Ravana, fled away.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekonapañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 49th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
