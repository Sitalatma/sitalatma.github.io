Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam

Canto 7: The Science of God

CHAPTER TWELVE

The Perfect Society: Four Spiritual Classes

This chapter particularly describes the brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี and the person in the vānaprastha - วานปฺรสฺถ - วานัปรัสถะ stage, and it also gives a general description of the four āśramas - อาศฺรมสฺ - อาศระมัส — brahmacarya, gṛhastha, vānaprastha - พฺรหฺมจรฺย, คฺฤหสฺถ, วานปฺรสฺถ - พรัหมะจัรยะ, คฤหัสถะ, วานัปรัสถะ and sannyāsa. - สนฺนฺยาสฯ - สันนยาสะฯ In the previous chapter, the great saint Nārada Muni has described the varṇa - วรฺณ - วัรณะ institution of society, and now, in this chapter, he will describe the stages of spiritual advancement in the four āśramas, - อาศฺรมสฺ, - อาศระมัส, which are known as brahmacarya, gṛhastha, vānaprastha - พฺรหฺมจรฺย, คฺฤหสฺถ, วานปฺรสฺถ - พรัหมะจัรยะ, คฤหัสถะ, วานัปรัสถะ and sannyāsa. - สนฺนฺยาสฯ - สันนยาสะฯ

The brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี should live under the care of the true spiritual master, giving him sincere respect and obeisances, acting as his menial servant, and always carrying out his order. The brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี should engage himself in spiritual activities and study the Vedic literature under the direction of the spiritual master. According to the brahmacarya - พฺรหฺมจรฺย - พรัหมะจัรยะ system, he should dress with a belt, deerskin, and matted hair and should bear a daṇḍa, - ทณฺฑ, - ทัณฑะ, waterpot and sacred thread. He should collect alms daily in the morning, and in the evening whatever alms he has collected he should offer to the spiritual master. A brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี should accept prasāda - ปฺรสาท - ประสาทะ upon the order of the spiritual master, and if the spiritual master sometimes forgets to order the disciple to eat, the disciple should not take prasāda - ปฺรสาท - ประสาทะ on his own initiative; rather, he should fast. The brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี should be trained to be satisfied with eating what is absolutely necessary, he should be very expert in executing responsibilities, he should be faithful, and he should control his senses and try to avoid the association of women as far as possible. A brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี should very strictly abstain from living with women and should not meet with gṛhasthas - คฺฤหสฺถสฺ - คฤหัสถัส and those too addicted to women. Nor should a brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี speak in a lonely place with a woman.

After completing one’s education as a brahmacārī - พฺรหฺมจารี - พรัหมะจารี in this way, one should give dakṣiṇā, - ทกฺษิณา, - ทักษิณา, an offering of gratitude, to one’s guru, - คุรุ, - คุรุ, and then one may leave for home and accept the next āśrama - อาศฺรม - อาศระมะ — the gṛhastha - คฺฤหสฺถ - คฤหัสถะ-āśrama - คฺฤหสฺถ-อาศฺรม - คฤหัสถะ-อาศระมะ — or else one may continue in the brahmacarya-āśrama - พฺรหฺมจรฺย-อาศฺรม - พรัหมะจัรยะ-อาศระมะ without adulteration. The duties for the gṛhastha - คฺฤหสฺถ - คฤหัสถะ-āśrama - คฺฤหสฺถ-อาศฺรม - คฤหัสถะ-อาศระมะ and brahmacarya-āśrama, - พฺรหฺมจรฺย-อาศฺรม, - พรัหมะจัรยะ-อาศระมะ, as well as the duties for sannyāsīs, - สนฺนฺยาสีสฺ, - สันนยาสีส, are prescribed in the śāstras. - ศาสฺตฺรสฺฯ - ศาสตรัสฯ A gṛhastha - คฺฤหสฺถ - คฤหัสถะ is not meant to enjoy sex life without restriction. Indeed, the whole purpose of Vedic life is to become free from sexual indulgence. All the āśramas - อาศฺรมสฺ - อาศระมัส are recognized for spiritual progress, and therefore although the gṛhastha-āśrama - คฺฤหสฺถ-อาศฺรม - คฤหัสถะ-อาศระมะ gives a kind of license for sex life for a certain time, it does not allow unrestricted sex life. Therefore, in gṛhastha life also, there is no illicit sex. A gṛhastha should not accept a woman for sexual enjoyment. Wasting semen is also illicit sex.

After the gṛhastha - คฺฤหสฺถ - คฤหัสถะ-āśrama - คฺฤหสฺถ-อาศฺรม - คฤหัสถะ-อาศระมะ is another āśrama, - อาศฺรม, - อาศระมะ, known as vānaprastha - วานปฺรสฺถ - วานัปรัสถะ, - วานปฺรสฺถ, - วานัปรัสถะ, which is midway between gṛhastha and sannyāsa. - สนฺนฺยาสฯ - สันนยาสะฯ A person in the vānaprastha - วานปฺรสฺถ - วานัปรัสถะ order is restricted in eating food grains and forbidden to eat fruits that have not ripened on the tree. Nor should he cook food with fire, although he is allowed to eat caru, - จรุ, - จะรุ, grains that have been offered in a sacrificial fire. He may also eat fruits and grains that have grown naturally. Living in a thatched cottage, the vānaprastha should endure all kinds of heat and cold. He should not cut his nails or hair, and he should give up cleaning his body and teeth. He should wear tree bark, accept a daṇḍa, - ทณฺฑ, - ทัณฑะ, and practice life in the forest, taking a vow to live there for twelve years, eight years, four years, two years or at least one year. At last, when because of old age he can no longer perform the activities of a vānaprastha, - วานปฺรสฺถ, - วานัปรัสถะ, he should gradually stop everything and in this way give up his body.

Material consciousness, along with the goal of thought, should be merged in the individual living being, and the demigods acting under the modes of material nature should be merged, along with the perverted living being, into the Supreme Being. The earth should be merged in water, water in the brightness of the sun, this brightness into the air, the air into the sky, the sky into the false ego, the false ego into the total material energy, the total material energy into the unmanifested ingredients [the pradhāna feature of the material energy], and at last the ingredient feature of material manifestation into the Supersoul.

Translation

When all the material designations have thus merged into their respective material elements, the living beings, who are all ultimately completely spiritual, being one in quality with the Supreme Being, should cease from material existence, as flames cease when the wood in which they are burning is consumed. When the material body is returned to its various material elements, only the spiritual being remains. This spiritual being is Brahman and is equal in quality with Parabrahman.

Purport

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Seventh Canto, Twelfth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled “The Perfect Society: Four Spiritual Classes.”
