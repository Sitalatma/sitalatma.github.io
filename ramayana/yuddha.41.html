Introduction

Rama advises Sugreeva not to repeat such reckless acts in future on the ground of he being a king. Then Rama tells Lakshmana about the various evil portents he had seen. Rama commands the monkey-generals to besiege all the four gates of Lanka. He himself along with Lakshmana protects the army, besieging the northern gate being guarded by Ravana. Then, Rama sends Angada to Ravana, to expostulate him and to bring him back to reason. Angada tries his best to convince Ravana, but in vain. Ravana commands his attendants to seize Angada. Angada shakes out those attendants who tried to seize him ascends the roof of Ravana's palace, tramples ad crushes it down by his strength. Angada finally returns to Rama, who was stationed in the midst of the monkey-forces.

79. rāmaḥ = Rama; Kausalya nandārdhanaḥ = who augments the joy of Kausalya; rāghavaḥ = and the one born in Raghu dynasty; aha = speaks (thus); tvām = to you; nṛishamsa = O; ruthless demon!; niṣpatya = come forth; yudhyasva = and enter into combat; bhava = prove; puruṣaḥ = to be the representative of your race.

"Rama who is born in Raghu dynasty and who augements the joy of Kausalya speaks thus to you, �O, ruthless demon! Come forth and enter into combat. Prove to be the representative of your race!' "

Sloka 80

hantā asmi tvām saha amātyam saputra jnāti bāndhavam |
nirudvignās trayo lokā bhaviṣyanti hate tvayi || 6-41-80

80. hantāsmi = I shall kill tvām = you; sahāmātyam = along with your ministers; saputra jJṇāti bāndhavaḥ = sons; cousins and relatives; tvam = you; hate = being dead; trayaḥ = the three; lokāh = worlds; bhaviṣyanti = will be; nirudvignāḥ = rid of fear.

" �I shall kill you along with your ministers, sons, cousins and other relatives. You being dead, all the three worlds will be rid of fear.'"

Sloka 81

deva dānava yakṣāṇām gandharva uraga rakṣasām |
śatrum adya uddhariṣyāmi tvām ṛṣīṇām ca kaṇṭakam || 6-41-81

81. adya = now; uddhariṣyāmi = I shall uproot out; tvam = you; kaṇ^ṭakamcha = who are a thorn; ṛiṣīṇām = to sages; shatrum = and enemy; deva dānava yakṣāṇām = to celestials; demons; semi-divine beings; gandharvoraga rakṣasām = celestial musicians; serpent-gods and ogres.

" �Now, I shall uproot you, a thorn to sages and an enemy to celestials, demons, semi-divine beings, celestial musicians, serpent-gods and ogres'."

Sloka 82

vibhīṣaṇasya ca aiśvaryam bhaviṣyati hate tvayi |
na cet satkṛtya vaidehīm praṇipatya pradāsyasi || 6-41-82

82. na pradāsyasi chet = If you do not restore; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; satkṛitya = honourably; praṇipatya = by bowing respectfully before me; tvayi = you; hate = ae slain; aishvaryam = and the kingdom; bhaviṣyati = will belong; vibhīṣaṇasya = to Vibhishana.

"If you do not restore Seetha honourably, by bowing respectfully before me, you are slain and Vibhishana will become the king.'"

Sloka 83

ity evam paruṣam vākyam bruvāṇe hari pumgave |
amarṣa vaśam āpanno niśā cara gaṇa īśvaraḥ || 6-41-83

83. hari puṇ^gave = (While) the lion among monkeys; Angada; bruvāṇe = was speaking; ityevam = thus; paruṣam = in harsh; vākyam = words; nishācharagaṇeshvaraḥ = Ravana the Lord of the demon-tribe; āpannaḥ = prevailed; amarṣa vasham = on the influence of anger.

Hearing these harsh words from Angada the lion among the monkeys, Ravana the Lord of the demon-tribe was infuriated.

Sloka 84

tataḥ sa roṣa tāmra akṣah śaśāsa sacivāṃs tadā |
gṛhyatām eṣa durmedhā vadhyatām iti ca asakṛt || 6-41-84

84. tataḥ = thereafter; saḥ = Ravana; āpannaḥ = who obtained; roṣam = wrath; tadā = then; asakṛit = respeatedly; shasāsa = commanded; sachivān = his attendants; iti = thus; durmedhāḥ = (Let) this stupid monkey; gṛihyatām = be seized; vadhyatām = and killed.

Then, the enraged Ravana repeatedly commanded his attendants, saying: "Let this stupid monkey be seized and put to death."

Sloka 85

rāvaṇasya vacah śrutvā dīpta agni sama tejasaḥ |
jagṛhus tam tato ghorāś catvāro rajanī carāḥ || 6-41-85

85. tataḥ = then; shrutvā = hearing; rāvaṇasya = Ravana's vachaḥ = words; chatvāraḥ = four; ghorāḥ = terrible; rajanīcharāh = demons; jagṛihuḥ = seized; tam = Angada; tejasā = who; in his splendour; dīptāgnimiva = resembled a blazing torch.

Hearing Ravana's words, four terrible demons seized Angada who, in his splendour, resembled a blazing torch.

Sloka 86

grāhayām āsa tāreyah svayam ātmānam ātmanā |
balam darśayitum vīro yātu dhāna gaṇe tadā || 6-41-86

86. tadā = then; tāreyaḥ = Angada the son of ṭara; ātmavān = the prudent; vīraḥ = and the heroic monkey; svayam = voluntarily; grāhayāmāsa ātmānām = allowed himself to be seized; darshayitum = to display; balam = his prowess; yātudhānagaṇe = before the host of demons.

Then, the prudent and heroic Angada the son of Tara voluntarily allowed himself to be seized, in order to display his prowess before the host of demons.

Sloka 87

sa tān bāhu dvaye saktān ādāya patagān iva |
prāsādam śaila samkāśam utpāpāta angadas tadā || 6-41-87

87. tadā = then; ādāya = seizing; patagāniva = like unto birds; tān = those attendants; bāhudvayā = saktān = entangled in both of his arms; utpapāta = he leapt on; prāsādam = to the palace; shaila sankāsham = that resembled like a mountain.

Then, seizing like unto birds those attendants clung to his arms, Angada leapt on to the palace that resembled like a mountain.

Sloka 88

tesyotpatanavegena nirdhūtāstatra rākṣasāḥ |
bhumau nipatitāh sarve rākṣasa indrasya paśyataḥ || 6-41-88

88. sarve = all those four; rākṣasāḥ = demons; nirdhūtāḥ = were shaken out; tasya = by Angada's utpatana vegena = impetuous leap; nipatitāḥ = and they fell; bhūmau = on the ground; tatra = there; pashyataḥ = under the eyes; rākṣasendrasya = of the king of demons.

All those four demons were shaken out by Angada's impetuous leap and fell on the ground there under the eyes of their king.

Sloka 89

tataḥ prāsāda śikharam śaila śṛngam iva unnatam |
tat paphāla tadā ākrāntam daśagrīvasya paśyataḥ || 6-41-89

89. tataḥ = thereafter; pratāpavān = the glorious; vāli putraḥ = Angada the son of Vali; chakrāma = ascended; prāsāda shikharam = the roof of the palace; rākṣasendrsya = of Ravana; shailashṛigamiva = which equaled the summit of a mountain; unnatam = in height.

Thereafter, the glorious Angada the son of Vali ascended the roof of Ravana's palace, which equaled the summit of a mountain in height.

Sloka 90

paphāla ca tadākrāntaṃ daśagrīvasya paśyataḥ |
purā himavataḥ śṛṅgaṃ vajreṇeva vidāritam 6-41-90

90. tadākrāntam = that roof of the palace; trampled by Angada; paphālacha = crumbled; himātaḥ shṛiṇ^gamiva = as a peak of ḥimalayan range; vidāritam = was shattered; purā = long ago; vajreṇa = by lightening; dashagrīvasya = before Ravana's pashyatah = gaze.

That roof of the palace, trampled by Angada, crumbled - as a peak of Himalayan range was shattered long ago by lightening - before Ravana's gaze.

Sloka 91

bhanktvā prāsāda śikharam nāma viśrāvya ca ātmanaḥ |
vinadya sumahā nādam utpapāta vihāyasā || 6-41-91

91. bhaṇKtvā = after destroying; prasāda shikharam = the roof of the palace; nāma vishrāvyacha ātmanaḥ = proclaiming his name; vinadya = roaring; sumahā nādam = with a very great noise; utpapāta = rose; vihāyasā = into the air.

After destroying the roof of the palace, Angada proclaimed his name and with a triumphant roar, rose into the air.

Sloka 92

vyathayan rākṣasān sarvān harṣayaṃścāpi vānarān |
sa vānarāṇāṃ madhye tu rāmapārśvamupāgataḥ 6-41-92

92. vyathayan = causing restlessness; sarvān = to all; rākṣasān = the demons; harṣayam shchāpi = but delight; vānarāṇām = to the monkeys; (Angada); upāgataḥ = approached; rāma pārshvam = close to Rama; madhye = in the middle; vānarāṇām = of the monkeys.

Causing restlessness to all the demons but delight to the monkeys, Angada approached close to Rama, who was stationed in the middle of the monkey-forces.

Sloka 93

rāvaṇas tu param cakre krodham prāsāda dharṣaṇāt |
vināśam ca ātmanah paśyan nihśvāsa paramo abhavat || 6-41-93

93. prāsāda dharṣaṇāt = because of the destruction of the palace; rāvaṇastu = Ravana on his part; chakre = got; param = extreme; krodham = anger; pashyan = foreseeing; ātmānaḥ = his own; vināsham = destruction; abhavat = he became; niḥ shvāsa paramaḥ = a desponded demon.

Because of the destruction of his palace, Ravana on his part got extremely angry. Foreseeing his own destruction, he became a desponded demon.

Sloka 94

rāmas tu bahubhir hṛṣṭair ninadadbhih plavam gamaiḥ |
vṛto ripu vadha ākānkṣī yuddhāya eva abhyavartata || 6-41-94

94. vṛitaḥ = surrounded; bahubhiḥ plavaṇgamaiḥ = by many monkeys; vinadadbhiḥ = roaring; hṛiṣṭaiḥ = with delight; rāmastu = Rama on his part; abhivartata = was proceeding ahead; yuddhāyaiva = for the battle already; ripuvadhā kāṇ^kṣī = with an intent to wipe out the enemy.

Surrounded by many monkeys, roaring with delight, Rama on his part was proceeding ahead already for the battle, with an intent to wipe out the enemy.

Sloka 95

suṣeṇas tu mahā vīryo giri kūṭa upamo hariḥ |
bahubhih samvṛtas tatra vānaraih kāma rūpibhiḥ || 6-41-95

95. suṣeṇastu = now ṣushena; mahāvīryaḥ = the most valiant; hariḥ = girikūṭopamaḥ = was like the head of the mountain; tatra = there; bahubhiḥ = samvṛitaḥ = surrounded by; innumerable; vānaraiḥ = monkeys; kāmarūpibhiḥ = who were able to change their form at will.

Now, Sushena the highly valiant monkey, was stationed there like the head of the mountain surrounded by innumerable monkeys, who were bale to change their form at will.

Sloka 96

sa tu dvārāṇi sarvāṇi sugrīva vacanāt kapiḥ |
paryākramata durdharṣo nakṣatrāṇi iva candramāḥ || 6-41-96

96. ṣugreeva vachanāt = under the order of ṣugreeva; saḥ kapiḥ = that monkey; durdharṣaḥ = the invincible; samyamya = was patrolling; dvārāṇi = the gates; paryakrāmata = and wandering; nakṣatrāṇiva = like among stars; chandramāḥ = the moon.

Under the order of Sugreeva, the invincible monkey, Sushena was patrolling the gates and wandering like a moon among the stars.

Sloka 97

teṣām akṣauhiṇi śatam samavekṣya vana okasām |
lankām upaniviṣṭānām sāgaram ca ativartatām || 6-41-97
rākṣasā vismayam jagmus trāsam jagmus tathā apare |
apare samara uddharṣādd harṣam eva upapedire || 6-41-98

97; 98. samavekṣya = seeing; akṣauhiṇi shatam = hundreds of divisions; teṣām = of those; vanaukasām = monkeys; abhivartatām = encamped; laṇ^kām upaniviṣṭānām = under the walls of Lanksa; sāgaram cha = (marshaled on the shores) of the sea; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; jagmuḥ = got; vismayam = amazed; tathā = and; apare = some others; jagmuḥ = got; tathā = and; apare = some others; jagmuḥ = got; tāsam = terror-struck; apare = (while) others; trāsam = terror-struck; apare = (While) others; harṣāt = overjoyed; samara = at the prospect of fighting; upapedire = leapt; harṣameva = even in exultation.

Seeing hundreds of divisions of those monkeys encamped under the walls of Lanka, marshaled on the shores of the sea, the demons were amazed and some others were terror-struck while others, overjoyed at the prospect of fighting, leapt even in exultation.

Sloka 99

kṛtsnam hi kapibhir vyāptam prākāra parikha antaram |
dadṛśū rākṣasā dīnāh prākāram vānarī kṛtam || 6-41-99
hāhākāramakurvanta rākṣasā bhayamāgatāḥ |

99. dīnaḥ = the miserable; rakṣasāḥ = demons; dadṛishuḥ = saw; kṛitsnam = the entire; vyāptam = extensive; prākāra parikhāntaram = space between the walls and the moat; vānarīkṛitam = being occupied by the monkeys; prākāram = like under unto a second rampart; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; āgataḥ = got; bhayam = a panic; akurvanta = and cried out; hāhākāram = "woe! Alas!"

Those miserable demons saw the entire extensive space between the walls and the moat being occupied by the monkeys, like unto a second rampart. The demons cried out, "Woe! Alas!" in panic.

Sloka 100

tasmin mahā bhīṣaṇake pravṛtte |
kolāhale rākṣasa rājadhānyām |
pragṛhya rakṣāṃsi mahā āyudhāni |
yuga anta vātā;iva samviceruh || 6-41-100

100. pravṛitte = as a result; tasmin = of that; mahā bhīṣaṇake = appalling; kolāhale = tumult; rākṣasa rājadhānyām = in the capital city of demons; rakṣāmsi = the demons; pragṛihya = seized hold of; mahāyudhāni = their great weapons; samvicheruḥ = and sallied forth; yugāntā rātāḥ iva = like the winds that blow at the dissolution of the worlds.

As a result of that appalling tumult in the capital city of the demons, the soldiers of Ravana seized hold of their great weapons and sallied forth like the winds that blow at the dissolution of the worlds.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 41st chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
