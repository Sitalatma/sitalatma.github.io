Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda


Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 41
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The ladies of the royal gymnasium lament over the exile of Rama and others. The citizens of Ayodhya also are reduced to a miserable plight. All Nature mourns at Rama's departure.


Sloka 1

tasmiṃstu puruṣavyāghre viniryāti kṛtāñjalau |
ārtaśabdo.atha samjajñe strīṇāmantahpute tadā || 2-41-1

1. tadā = then; tasmin = that Rama; purushhavyāghre = the tiger among men; kṛitāṇjalou = having paid obeisance with joined palms; viniryāti = departed; atha = moreover ; ārtha shabdaḥ = a great cry of distress ;samjaG^ye = arose ;strīṇām = among women ; antaḥpure = of inner apartments.

As Rama the tiger among men, having paid obeisance with joined palms departed, a great cry of distress arose among women of inner apartments.

Sloka 2

anāthasya janasya asya durbalasya tapasvinaḥ |
yo gatim śaraṇam ca āsīt sa nāthaḥ kva nu gaccati || 2-41-2

2. kvanu = Where; saḥ nāthaḥ = is he the Lord; gachchhati = going; yaḥ = who; gataḥ = was the path ; āsīt = who became; sharaṇamcha = the protector ;asya Janasya = of these people; anāthasya = who are defenseless ; durbalasya = weak; tapasvinaḥ = and unhappy."

Where is he going, who was the path, who became the protector of the people, who are now defenseless weak and unhappy?"

Sloka 3

na krudhyati abhiśastaḥ api krodhanīyāni varjayan |
kruddhān prasādayan sarvān sama duhkhaḥ kva gaccati || 2-41-3

3. kvachit = where; gataḥ = has he gone; kṛidhyati = who was not angry; abhishapto. api = even if accused ; varjayan = who resolved; krodhanīyāni = every misunderstanding; prasādayan = who propitiated; sarvān = all those; kruddhān = who were angry; samaduḥkaḥ = by sharing their suffering?"

"Where has he gone, who was not angry even when provoked, who resolved every misunderstanding and who propitiated all those who were angry by sharing their suffering?

Sloka 4

kausalyāyām mahā tejā yathā mātari vartate |
tathā yo vartate asmāsu mahātmā kva nu gaccati || 2-41-4

4. yaḥ = which Rama; mahātejaḥ = having great splendor; yathā = in which manner; vartate = was treating; kausalyāyām = Kausalya; mātari = his mother; tathā = in the same manner; vartate = was treating; Asmāsu = us; kvanu = where; mahātmā = that great-souled Rama; gachchhati = has gone?"

That illustrious and great-souled Rama, who treated us with the same reverence he paid to his own mother, where has he gone?"

Sloka 5

kaikeyyā kliśyamānena rājñā saṃcoditaḥ vanam |
paritrātā janasya asya jagataḥ kva nu gaccati || 2-41-5

5. kvanu = Where; gachchhati = has he gone; paritrātā = (he) the protector; asya = of these; janasya = people; jagataḥ = and the world; samachoditaḥ = driven; vanam = to the forest; rāG^yā = by the king; klishyamānena = tormented; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi"

"Where has he gone, he the protector of the people and the whole world, driven to the forest by the king, goaded on by Kaikeyi? ''

Sloka 6

aho niścetano rājā jīva lokasya sampriyam |
dharmyam satya vratam rāmam vana vāso pravatsyati || 2-41-6

6. rājā = the king; nishchetanaḥ = is a senseless man. Aho = Alas! pravastyati = he has sent away from home; vanavāse = to dwell in forest; rāmam = Rama; sampriyam = the very beloved man; dharmyam = the pious minded; satyavratam = strictly truthful .

"Alas! In his folly, the king has banished the virtuous the beloved and the strictly truthful Rama to the forest!"

Sloka 7

iti sarvā mahiṣyaḥ tā vivatsāiva dhenavaḥ |
ruruduḥ caiva duhkha ārtāḥ sasvaram ca vicukruśuḥ || 2-41-7

8. iti = thus; sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ mahishhyaḥ = those queens; rurudushchaiva = shed their tears; duhkhārtāḥ = in grief; dhenavaḥ iva = like unto cows; vivatsāḥ = deprived of their calves; vichukrushuḥ = cried out; sasvaram = in loud noise.

Thus did all the queens lament, like unto cows deprived of their calves and in there affection cried out loudly.

Sloka 8

sa tam antaḥ pure ghoram ārta śabdam mahī patiḥ |
putra śoka abhisamtaptaḥ śrutvā ca āsīt suduhkhitaḥ || 2-41-8

8. saḥ mahīpatḥ = that king; putrashokābhisantaptaḥ = profoundly afflicted by the departure of his son; āsīt = became; suduḥkhitaḥ = increasingly depressed; shrutvā = after hearing; tam = that; ghoram = terrible; ārta shabdam = crying sound; antaḥpure = in the private apartments.

Hearing those terrible crying sounds in the private apartments, the king profoundly afflicted by the departure of his son, became increasingly depressed.

Sloka 9

na agni hotrāṇi ahūyanta sūryaḥ ca antar adhīyata |
vyasṛjan kavalān nāgā gāvo vatsān na pāyayan || 2-41-9

9. agnihotrāṇi = sacrificial fires; nāhūyanta = were not performed; gṛihamethinaḥ = house-holders; nāpachan = did not cook their food.; prajāḥ = people; na ākurvan = did not do; kāryam = (their )act .sūryashcha = even the sun antaradhīyata = disappeared .

On the departure of Rama to the forest, the sacred fires were no longer performed, no house holder prepared food, the people did not do their acts and even the sun went into eclipse.

Sloka 10

vyasṛjan kabalānnāgā gāvo vatsānna pāyayan |
putram prathamajam labdhvā jananī nābhyanandata || 2-41-10

10. nāgāḥ = Elephants; vyasṛijan = threw out; kabalān = the morsels of food (they had taken into their mouths ) gāvaḥ = Cows; na pāyayan = did not give suck; vatsān = to their calves; jananī = mother; nābhyanandata = did not rejoice; labdhvā = for having got; putram = a son; prathamajam = born for the first time .

Elephants threw out the morsels of food they had taken into their mouths. Cows did not give suck to their calves. ṃother did not rejoice, for having got a son born for the first time.

Sloka 11

triśankur lohita angaḥ ca bṛhaspati budhāv api |
dāruṇāḥ somam abhyetya grahāḥ sarve vyavasthitāḥ || 2-41-11

11. trishankuḥ = ṭrisanku; lohitāṇ^gashcha = Lohitanga; Brihaspati budhāvapi = Brihaspati as also Budha; sarve = all; grahāḥ = planets; dāruṇāḥ = assumed harshness; abhyetya = got; vyavasathitāḥ = stayed; samam = with the moon.

Trisanku, Lohintanga, Brihaspati as also Budha and all other planets assumed a menacing aspect and got stayed with the moon.

Sloka 12

nakṣatrāṇi gata arcīmṣi grahāḥ ca gata tejasaḥ |
viśākhāḥ ca sadhūmāḥ ca nabhasi pracakāśire || 2-41-12

12. nakshhatrāṇi = ṣtars; gatārchīmshhi = ceased to twinkle; grahāshcha = planets; gatachetasaḥ = were deprived of their splendour; vishākhāstu = ṭhe stars of Visakha; prachakāshire = appeared; nabhasi = in the heaven; sadhūmāḥ = veiled in mist.

Stars ceased to twinkle. Planets were deprived of their splendour. The stars of Visakha appeared in the heaven, veiled in the mist.

Sloka 13

kālikānilavegena mahodadhirivotthitaḥ |
rāme vanam pravrajite nagaram pracacāla tat || 2-41-13

13. mahodadhiḥ = ṭhe great ocean; utthitaḥ = rose; kālikānilavegena = as by a speed of a long-lasting tempest; rāme = (while) Rama; pravrajite = went; vanam = to the forest; tat nagaram = that city; prachachāla = was shaken.

The great ocean rose, as by a speed of a long-lasting tempest. While Rama went to the forest, that city was shaken.

Sloka 14

diśaḥ paryākulāḥ sarvā stimireṇeva samvṛtāḥ |
na graho nāpi nakṣatraṃ pracakāśe na kiṃcana || 2-41-14

14. sarvāḥ = All; dishaḥ = the cardinal points;paryākulāḥ = having been disturbed;samvṛitāḥiva = were like covered timireṇa = by darkness ; grahāḥ = planets; naprakāshe = did not become visible; na = nor; nakshhatramapi = even the stars; na = nor; kimcha = any other.

All the cardinal points, having been disturbed, were covered by darkness. Planets did not become visible, nor the stars nor any other thing in heaven.

Sloka 15

akasmān nāgaraḥ sarvo jano dainyam upāgamat |
āhāre vā vihāre vā na kaścit akaron manaḥ || 2-41-15

15. sarvāḥ = All; janaḥ = the people; nāgaraḥ = belonging to the city; akasmāt = suddenly; upāgamat = became; dainyam = apathetic. na kashchit = ṇo one; akarot = made; manaḥ = mind; āhāre vā = in either food; vihāre vā = or in pastime.

All the people of the city suddenly became apathetic. No one was interested in either food or in pastime.

Sloka 16

śokaparyāyasantaptaḥ satataṃ dīrghamucchvasan |
ayodhyāyām janaḥ sarvaḥ śuśoca jagatīpatim || 2-41-16

16. sarvāḥ = All; janaḥ = the people; ayodhyāyām = in Ayodhya; shokaparyāyasamtaptaḥ = afflicted by grief in succession; satatam = with a perpetual;dīrgham = long;uchchhvasan = sighs;shushocha = bewailed;jagatīpatim = about(the departure of)the Lord of the earth.

All the people of Ayodhya, afflicted by grief in succession, heaved profound and burning sighs and bewailed about the departure of the Lord of the earth.

Sloka 17

bāṣpa paryākula mukho rāja mārga gataḥ janaḥ |
na hṛṣṭaḥ lakṣyate kaścit sarvaḥ śoka parāyaṇaḥ || 2-41-17

17. janaḥ = ṭhe people; rājamārga gataḥ = in the king's way; bhāshhpa paryākula mukhaḥ = were having distressed faces filled with tears. nakashchit = ṇo one; lakshhyate = appeared; hṛishhṭaḥ = rejoicing.sarvāḥ = All; shoka parāyaṇaḥ = were possessed by grief.

The people in the king's way were wearing distressed faces filled with tears. No one appeared rejoicing. All were possessed by grief.

Sloka 18

na vāti pavanaḥ śītaḥ na śaśī saumya darśanaḥ |
na sūryaḥ tapate lokam sarvam paryākulam jagat || 2-41-18

18. pavanaḥ = ṭhe wind;na vāti = was not blowing; shītaḥ = coolly; shashī = ṭhe moon; na = did not; soumyadarshanaḥ = appear beneficient; sūryaḥ = the sun; na tapate = did not give out warmth; lokam = to the earth.sarvam = ṭhe entire; jagat = world; paryākulam = was in disorder.

The wind was not blowing coolly. The moon did not appear beneficient. The sun did not give out warmth to the earth. The entire world was in disorder

Sloka 19

anarthinaḥ sutāḥ strīṇām bhartāraḥ bhrātaraḥ tathā |
sarve sarvam parityajya rāmam eva anvacintayan || 2-41-19

19. sutāḥ = ṣons; bhartāraḥ husbands; anarthinaḥ = did not ask for; strīṇām = the women; tathā = and; bhrātaraḥ = brothers; anarthinaḥ = did not enquire (of their sisters); sarve = all; parityajaya = gave up; sarvam = everything; anvachintayan = thinking of; rāmam eva = Rama alone.

Sons did not ask for their mothers and husbands did not ask for their wives. Brothers did not enquire about their sisters. All gave up every thing and thought of Rama alone.

Sloka 20

ye tu rāmasya suhṛdaḥ sarve te mūḍha cetasaḥ |
śoka bhāreṇa ca ākrāntāḥ śayanam na juhus tadā || 2-41-20

20. tadā = ṭhen; rāmasya = Rama's; ye = which; suhrudaḥ = ownfriends; te sarve = all of them; mūdhachetasaḥ = bewildered in mind; ākrāntāḥ = overwhelmed; shoka bhāreṇa = by the weight of their agony; na jahuḥ: did not abandon; shayanam = their beds.

Then, all of Rama's own friends were bewildered in mind and the weight of their agony overwhelmed them. They did not leave their beds.

Sloka 21

tataḥ tu ayodhyā rahitā mahātmanā |
puraṃdareṇa iva mahī saparvatā |
cacāla ghoram bhaya bhāra pīḍitā |
sanāga yodha aśva gaṇā nanāda ca || 2-41-21

21. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; ayodhyā = the city of Ayodhya; rahitā = bereft of; mahātmanā = the great souled Rama; mahīva = like the earth; saparvatā = with mountains; rahitā = abandoned by; purandhareṇa = Indra; the lord of rain; chachāla = was shaken;ghoram = terribly;bhaya shoka pīditā = afflicted by fear and grief;sanāgayodhāshvagaṇāḥ = the city filled with elephants; warriors and horses;nanādacha = made a roaring sound.

Afflicted by fear and grief, the earth with its mountains, deprived of that great souled Rama, suffered a terrible convulsion, as when it is abandoned by Indra the lord of rain, and a great uproar reigned in the city which was filled with elephants, warriors and horses.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 41th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
