Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

: Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 22
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Khara, the brother of Shūrpanakha starts out to wage war with Rama with fourteen thousand demons and with their unusual arsenal. This is the first major move of demons towards Rama that leads to the ultimate battle at the end of epic.

Sloka 1

evam ādharṣitaḥ śūraḥ śūrpaṇakhyā kharaḥ tataḥ |
uvāca rakṣasām madhye kharaḥ kharataram vacaḥ || 3-22-1

1. tataḥ = then; shūrpaṇakhyā = by ṣhūrpanakha; evam rakṣasām madhye ādharṣitaḥ = this way, demons, among, disparaged; shūraḥ kharaḥ = valiant, Khara; kharaḥ = infuriated one; khara taram vacaḥ = hurtful, highly, words; uvāca = spoke.

Thus when Shūrpanakha disparaged that valiant Khara amidst demons he is infuriated and spoke these highly hurtful words. [3-22-1]

Sloka 2

tava apamāna prabhavaḥ krodho ayam atulo mama |
na śakyate dhārayitum lavaṇa aṃbha iva ulbaṇam || 3-22-2

2. tava apamāna prabhavaḥ = your, disgrace, originating from; mama atulaḥ ayam krodhaḥ = my, limitless, this, fury; ulbaṇam lavaṇa ambha iva = highly [bitter,] salt, water, like; dhārayitum na shakyate = to contain, not, possible.

"This fury of mine originating from the disgrace brought on you is like the highly bitter salt water that is impossible to contain when ingested. [3-22-2]

Sloka 3

na rāmam gaṇaye vīryān mānuṣam kṣīṇa jīvitam |
ātma duścaritaiḥ prāṇān hato yo adya vimokṣyati || 3-22-3

3. mānuṣam kṣīṇa jīvitam rāmam = human, one with dwindled, lifetime, Rama will be; vīryāt na gaṇaye = by [his] valour, not, I do not reckon - I do not care; yaḥ = which Rama; ātma duḥ caritaiḥ = by his own, bad, doings; hataḥ = slain; adya prāṇān vi mokṣyati = now, lives, is going to give up.

"I do not care the valour of that Rama for he is a human with dwindled lifetime, now going to give up his life slain by his own misdoings. [3-22-3]

Sloka 4

bāṣpaḥ saṃdhāryatām eṣa saṃbhramaḥ ca vimucyatām |
aham rāmam saha bhrātrā nayāmi yama sādanam || 3-22-4

4. eṣa bāṣpaḥ = these, tears, hold back; samdhāryatām = hold back; sambhramaḥ ca vimucyatām = despair, too, cast of; aham rāmam bhrātrā saha = I, Rama, brother, along with; nayāmi yama sādanam = send, to ṭerminator's, abode.

"Control your tears and cast off your despair too, for I will send Rama to the abode of the Terminator along with his brother. [3-22-4]

Sloka 5

paraśvadha hatasya adya manda prāṇasya bhū tale |
rāmasya rudhiram raktam uṣṇam pāsyasi rākṣasi || 3-22-5

5. rākṣasī = oh, demoness; adya = now; bhū tale = to earth's, surface; parashvadha hatasya = by axe, hew down; manda prāṇasya = one who is with slowed down, lives - short-lived Rama; rāmasya raktam uṣṇam rudhiram = Rama's, red, hot, blood; pāsyasi = you will swill.

"Now, you demoness, you will swill that Rama's red hot blood when that short-lived one is hewed down to earth with an axe." So Khara assured Shūrpanakha. [3-22-5]

Sloka 6

sā prahṛṣṭvā vacaḥ śrutvā kharasya vadanāt cyutam |
praśaśaṃsa punar maurkhyāt bhrātaram rakṣasām varam || 3-22-6

6. sā kharasya vadanāt cyutam = she, frm Khara's, face - mouth, fallen; vacaḥ shrutvā = words, on hearing; pra hṛiṣṭvā = very, gladdened; maurkhyāt = by stupidity; bhrātaram = about brother; rakṣasām varam = among demons, [you are] the best; punaḥ pra shashamsa = again - further, highly praised.

On hearing the words plunged from the mouth of Khara she is very much gladdened and in all her stupidity she further praised her brother highly saying, "you are the best among all the demons..." [3-22-6]

Sloka 7

tayā paruṣitaḥ pūrvam punar eva praśaṃsitaḥ |
abravīt dūṣaṇam nāma kharaḥ senā patim tadā || 3-22-7

7. tayā pūrvam paruṣitaḥ = by her, earlier, denounced; punaḥ eva prashamsitaḥ = again, thus, praised; kharaḥ = Khara [is overjoyed]; tadā = then; dūṣaṇam nāma senā patim = Dūshana, named, to army, commander; abravīt = said - ordered.

Though she denounced him earlier she alone praised him now for which Khara is overjoyed, and then he ordered his army commander, namely Dūshana. [3-22-7]

Sloka 8

caturdaśa sahasrāṇi mama citta anuvartinām |
rakṣasām bhīma vegānām samareṣu anivartinām || 3-22-8

8. mama citta anuvartinām = my, will, followers of; bhīma vegānām = of frightful, hustle; samareṣu = in combats; a+ni vartinām = not, return, coming [unretreating]; such of those; rakṣasām = demons; catur dasha sahasrāṇi = four, ten, thousand [fourteen thousand]; are there.

"Those who are the followers of my will, whose hustle will be frightful and who will be unretreating in combats, such of those demons are there in fourteen thousand numbers, aren't they. [3-22-8]

Sloka 9

nīla jīmūta varṇānām loka hiṃsā vihārāṇām |
sarva udyogam udīrṇānām rakṣasām saumya kāraya || 3-22-9

8. mama citta anuvartinām = my, will, followers of; bhīma vegānām = of frightful, hustle; samareṣu = in combats; a+ni vartinām = not, return, coming [unretreating]; such of those; rakṣasām = demons; catur dasha sahasrāṇi = four, ten, thousand [fourteen thousand]; are there.

"And oh, gentle Dūshana, those domineering demons are with dark-clouds like complexions, and they are the pamperers of genocide, as such, cause them for a belligerency, all at once. [3-22-9]

▼

The very sight of these black cloud like demons will be murderous to humans, as Rama exclaimed about Tataka. If the on looking humans are not dead at first sight of these demons, then these demons can pamper in killing humans

Sloka 10

upasthāpaya me kṣipram ratham saumya dhanūṃṣi ca |
śarān ca citrān khaḍgāṃ ca śaktī ca vividhāḥ śitāḥ || 3-22-10

10. saumya = oh, gentle one; me = for me; ratham = chariot; dhanūmṣi sharān ca = bows, arrows, also; citrān khaDgām ca = amazing, swords, also; vi vidhāḥ shitāḥ shaktī ca = diverse, sharp, power bolts, also; kṣipram upa sthāpaya = promptly, before me, be positioned.

"For me, oh, gentle one, promptly position my chariot before me along with bows and arrows, amazing swords and diverse sharp power bolts. [3-22-10]

Sloka 11

agre niryātum icchhāmi paulastyānām mahātmanām |
vadhārtham durvinītasya rāmasya raṇa kovida || 3-22-11

11. raṇa kovida = oh, war, expert Dūshana; dur vinītasya = of evil, minded one; rāmasya vadha artham = Rama's, for elimination, purpose of; mahātmanām paulastyānām great-souled ones, of all Paulastya clansmen [demon race]; agre = ahead of; niryātum icchāmi = to start, I wish to.

"Oh, war pert Dūshana, I wish to start ahead of all the demons of ours, who are the clans-demons of the great souled Paulastya, for the purpose of elimination of that evil-minded Rama." So said Khara to Dūshana. [3-22-11]

▼

For this ṃaheshvara Tīrtha says: rāmasya agre paulastyānām rākṣhasānām vadhārtham niryātum icchāmi which gives meaning: "Before Rama I wish to present all the demonic clansmen of Sage Paulastya, for the purpose of elimination, for which I am starting ahead as a starter of this elimination process..."

Sloka 12

iti tasya bruvāṇasya sūrya varṇam mahāratham |
sat aśvaiḥ śabalaiḥ yuktam ācacakṣe atha dūṣaṇaḥ || 3-22-12

12. tasya = his, Khara's; iti bruvāṇasya = thus, while saying; atha dūṣaṇaḥ = then, Dūshana; sūrya varṇam mahā ratham = sun, coloured [dazzle,] great, chariot; shabalaiḥ = variegated in colours; sat ashvaiḥ yuktam = very good, horses, along with [yoked]; ācacakṣe = informed - heralded.

While Khara is saying thus Dūshana heralded saying, "Here comes the great chariot with sun's dazzle, yoked with very good horses of variegated colours." [3-22-12]

Sloka 13

tam meru śikhara ākāram tapta kāṃcana bhūṣaṇam |
hema cakram asaṃbādham vaidūryamaya kūbaram || 3-22-13
matsyaiḥ puṣpaiḥ drumaiḥ śailaiḥ candra sūryaiḥ ca kāṃcanaiḥ |
mā.ṃgalyaiḥ pakṣi samghaiḥ ca tārābhiḥ ca samāvṛtam || 3-22-14
dhvaja nistriṃśa saṃpannam kiṃkiṇī vara bhūṣitam |
sat aśva yuktam saḥ amarṣāt āruroha kharaḥ tadā || 3-22-15

13, 14, 15. saḥ kharaḥ = he, that Khara; meru shikhara ākāram = like ṃt. ṃeru's, ridge, in shape; tapta kāncana bhūṣaṇam = molten, gold, embellished in; hema cakram = golden, wheeled; a sam bādham = not, verily, inconvenient [not congested, having enough room, seating capacity]; vaidūryamaya kūbaram = with studded-gems, shaft of yoke; kāncanaiḥ = with golden [with artefacts of gold, like]; matsyaiḥ puṣpaiḥ drumaiḥ shailaiḥ = with fishes, flowers, trees, mountains; candra sūryaiḥ ca = moon, sun, also; māngalyaiḥ pakṣi samghaiḥ ca = auspicious one, bird, flocks; tārābhiḥ ca = with stars, also; sam ā vṛitam = all over, encompassed; dhvaja nistrinsha sampannam = flags, swords, endowed with; kinkiṇī vara bhūṣitam = jingle-bells, best ones, decorated with; sat ashva yuktam = best, horses, yoked with; [ratham = chariot] tadā = then; amarṣāt āruroha = with rancour, mounted.

That chariot which has a roomy cabin with its rooftop like that of the ridge of ṃt. ṃeru, body embellished in gold, wheels golden, gem-studded yoke-to-carriage shaft, all around encompassed with golden artefacts like fishes, flowers, trees, mountains, sun, moon, and auspicious birds flocks and stars, and even decorated with jingle-bells, endowed with flags, swords, and best horses, and such as it is, Khara embarked on it with all his rancour. [3-22-13, 14, 15]

Sloka 16

kharaḥ tu tān mahat sainyām ratha carma āyudha dhvajān |
niryāta iti abravīt prekṣya dūṣaṇaḥ sarva rākṣasān || 3-22-16

16. kharaḥ tu = Khara, but; mahat sainyām = great, army; ratha carma [varma] āyudha dhvajān = which has - chariots, skins, [shields,] weaponry, flags; tān sarva rākṣasān = them, all, demons; prekshya dūṣaṇaḥ = on seeing - on overseeing, Dūshana; niryāta iti = proceed, thus as; abravīt = said - charged.

On overseeing that great army with chariots, skins shields, weaponry and flags, and even at Dūshana too, Khara charged all those demons saying "proceed." [3-22-16]

Sloka 17

tataḥ tad rākṣasam sainyam ghora carma āyudha dhvajam |
nirjagāma jana sthānāt mahānādam mahājavam || 3-22-17

17. tataḥ = then; ghora carma [varman] āyudha dhvajam = having ghoulish, skins, [shields] weaponry, flags; tat rākṣasam sainyam = that, demonic, force; mahā nādam = with blatant, blare; mahā javam = with hasty, foray; janasthānāt nirjagāma = from Janasthāna, issued forth.

Then that demonic force with their ghoulish skin shields, weaponry and flags, issued forth from Janasthāna, blaring blatantly and foraying dashingly. [3-22-17]

Sloka 18

mudgaraiḥ paṭṭiśaiḥ śūlaiḥ sutīkṣṇaiḥ ca paraśvadhaiḥ |
khaḍgaiḥ cakraiḥ ca hastasthaiḥ bhrājamānaiḥ sa tomaraiḥ || 3-22-18
śaktibhiḥ parighaiḥ ghoraiḥ atimātraiḥ ca kārmukaiḥ |
gadā asi musalaiḥ vajraiḥ gṛhītaiḥ bhīma darśanaiḥ || 3-22-19
rākṣasānām sughorāṇām sahasrāṇi caturdaśa |
niryātāni jana sthānāt khara citta anuvartinām || 3-22-20

18. gṛihītaiḥ = taken; hasta sthaiḥ = in hands, carrying [wielding]; mudgaraiḥ paṭṭishaiḥ shūlaiḥ = maces, dart-bolts, spears; su tīkṣṇaiḥ ca = very, [razor-] sharp ones, also; parashvadhaiḥ khaDgaiḥ cakraiḥ ca = axes, swords, discs, also; bhrājamānaiḥ tomaraiḥ shaktibhiḥ = flashing ones, power-darts, with power-bolts; ghoraiḥ parighaiḥ = dreadful ones, iron-maces; ati mātraiḥ kārmukaiḥ = un, usual ones, bows; gadā asi musalaiḥ = clubs, scimitars, pounding-shafts; bhīma darshanaiḥ = that are calamitous, in look; vajraiḥ = with thunderbolt [like weapons]; khara citta anuvartinām = Khara's, whim, followers; su ghorāṇām = very, fiendish one; rākṣasānām = of demons; catur dasha sahasrāṇi = four, ten, thousand, [army of fourteen thousand]; janasthānāt niryātāni = from Janasthāna, started out.

Wielding maces, dart-bolts, and spears, and also razor-sharp axes, swords and discs, and dreadful power-darts and power-bolts that are flashing, iron maces, unusual bows, clubs, scimitars and pounding-shafts, and even thunderbolt like weapons that are calamitous in look, that army which comprises fourteen thousand fiendish demons that are the followers of Khara's whim, has started from Janasthāna. [3-22-18, 19, 20]

▼

Earlier batch which was sent against Rama consisted fourteen demons and now it is an army of fourteen thousand demons.

Sloka 21

tān tu nirdhāvato dṛṣṭvā rākṣasān bhīma darśanam |
kharasya atha rathaḥ kiṃcit jagāma tat anantaram || 3-22-21

21. nir dhāvataḥ = out, rushing; bhīma darshanam = of diabolic, appearance; tān rākṣasān dṛiṣṭvā = thgem, demons, on seeing; kimcit tat anantaram = = a little, that, after - at their behind; atha = then; kharasya rathaḥ jagāma = Khara's, chariot, proceeded.

On seeing the outrush of those demons of diabolic appearance, then Khara's chariot also proceeded a little later at their behind. [3-22-21]

Sloka 22

tataḥ tān śabalān aśvān tapta kāṃcana bhūṣitān |
kharasya matam ājñāya sārathiḥ paryacodayat || 3-22-22

22. tataḥ = then; sārathiḥ = charioteer; tapta kāncana bhūṣitān = molten [polished] golden, adorned with; shabalān tān ashvān = colourful, them, horses; kharasya matam ājñāya = Khara's, notion, knowing [according to]; pari acodayat = set spurs to horses - speedily charioted.

Then the charioteer set spurs on those colourful horses that are adorned in polished gold according to the notions of Khara. [3-22-22]

Sloka 23

saṃcodito rathaḥ śīghram kharasya ripu ghātinaḥ |
śabdena āpūrayāmāsa diśaḥ sa pradiśaḥ tathā || 3-22-23

23. tathā = that way; ripu ghātinaḥ = enemy, destroyer; kharasya = of Khara; shīghram coditaḥ = quickly - impetuously, driven; saḥ rathaḥ = that, chariot; dishaḥ = quarters, sa pra dishaḥ = with, inter, quarters; shabdena āpūrayāmāsa = with noise, started to swell.

When the chariot of enemy destroyer Khara is driven that impetuously, it has started to swell earth's four cardinal quarters and inter-quarters with turbulence. [3-22-23]

Sloka 24

pravṛddha manyuḥ tu kharaḥ khara svare
ripoḥ vadha artham tvarito yathā aṃtakaḥ |
acūcudat sārathim unnadan punar
mahābalo megha iva aśma varṣavān || 3-22-24

24. pra vṛiddha manyuḥ = speedily, escalating, rancour; khara svare = having harsh - thunderous, voice; kharaḥ = Khara; ripoḥ vadha artham = enemy, killing, intending to; antakaḥ yathā = ṭerminator, as with; tvaritaḥ = hastening; ashma varṣavān = having stones, storm; mahā balaḥ = overwhelming; megha iva = a cloud, like; sārathim = at charioteer; punaḥ = again - repeatedly; ut nadan = loudly, shouting at; acūcudat [a cū cudat] = hurried up.

He whose voice is thunderous and whose rancour escalated speedily, and who is hastening forward like the Terminator intending to destroy his enemy, that Khara repeatedly hurried the charioteer shouting at him loudly like a overwhelming cloud that is going to storm stones. [3-22-24]

---

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe dvā viṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 22nd chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
