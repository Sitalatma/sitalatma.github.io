Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

: Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 19
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Shūrpanakha narrates her woeful story to her brother Khara ncriminating Seetha in particular. She wants Khara to wage a war so that she could drink Seetha's blood. Khara in order to appease his sister sends fourteen demons to eliminate Rama.

Sloka 1

tām tathā patitām dṛṣṭvā virūpām śoṇita ukṣitām |
bhaginīm krodha saṃtaptaḥ kharaḥ papracchha rākṣasaḥ || 3-19-1

1. tathā = then; patitām = she who fell [before Khara]; vi rūpām = misshapen; shoṇita ukṣitām = by blood, soaked; tām bhaginīm dṛiṣṭvā = that, sister, on seeing; rākṣasaḥ kharaḥ = demon, Khara; krodha sam taptaḥ = by anger, well, burnt; papraccha = asked.

On seeing her who fell before him in a misshapen and blood-soaked condition, demon Khara is all stewed up and asked her. [3-19-1]

Sloka 2

uttiṣṭha tāvat ākhyāhi pramoham jahi saṃbhramam |
vyaktam ākhyāhi kena tvam evam rūpā virūpitā || 3-19-2

2. uttiṣṭha = rise up; ākhyāhi tāvat = tell, firstly; pramoham sambhramam jahi = fluster, flurry, put away; tvam kena evam rūpā virūpitā = you, by whom, this way, by form, disfigured; vyaktam ākhyāhi = clearly, tell.

"Rise up, first tell me clearly putting away your flurry and fluster, who disfigured your form in this way. [3-19-2]

Sloka 3

kaḥ kṛṣṇa sarpam asīnam āśī viṣama nāgasam |
tudati abhisamāpannam a.ṃguli agreṇa līlayā || 3-19-3

3. abhi sam āpannam = nearby, well, available - that has chanced in close proximity; āshī viṣam = in fangs, with venom; an āgasam = without, offence - inoffensive; asīnam = sitting - slouched down; kṛiṣṇa sarpam = black, cobra; kaḥ līlayā anguli agreṇa tudati = who, for sport, by finger, tip, poking.

"Who for sport is poking his fingertip at a venomously fanged black-cobra that has chanced in his close proximity but slouching down inoffensively. [3-19-3]

Sloka 4

kāla pāśam samāsajya kaṇṭhe mohāt na jānate |
yaḥ tvām adya samāsādya pītavān viṣam uttamam || 3-19-4

4. yaḥ adya = who, now; tvām samāsādya = you, having reached - on harrowing you; uttamam viṣam pītavān = best [deadly,] poison, gulped; [kaḥ = who is he]; kāla pāsham = ṭime's, noose; mohāt = foolishly; kaṇṭhe samāsajya = around neck, bracing; na jānate = not, [he is able to] know - heedless.

"Who on harrowing you is now heedless about his gulping a deadly poison, or about his foolish bracing the noose of Time around his own neck. [3-19-4]

Sloka 5

bala vikrama saṃpannā kāmagā kāma rūpiṇī |
imām avasthām nītā tvam kena aṃtaka samā gatā || 3-19-5

5. bala vikrama sampannā = boldness, braveness, gifted with; kāma gā = whim, goer - a whimsical rover; kāma rūpiṇī = by wish, guise changer; antaka samā = ṭerminator, coequal; gatā = having gone; kena imām avasthām tvam nītā = by whom, this kind of, plight, you are, lead into.

"You are gifted with boldness and braveness, a whimsical rover, a guise changer by your wish, a coequal of Terminator, but to whom you have gone and by whom you are led into this kind of plight. [3-19-5]

Sloka 6

deva gandharva bhūtānām ṛṣīṇām ca mahātmanām |
ko ayam evam mahāvīryaḥ tvām virūpām cakāra ha || 3-19-6

6. deva gandharva bhūtānām ṛiṣīṇām ca mahātmanām = among gods, celestials, other beings, sages, even, high-souled ones; kaḥ ayam evam mahā vīryaḥ = who, is he, that kind of, highly intrepid individual; tvām virūpām cakāra ha = you, shapeless, rendered into, indeed.

"Who is that kind of highly intrepid warper, either from gods, or from celestials or from high-souled sages who indeed rendered you shapeless. [3-19-6]

Sloka 7

na hi paśyāmi aham loke yaḥ kuryāt mama vipriyam |
amareṣu sahasrākṣam mahaendram pākaśāsanam || 3-19-7

7. loke yaḥ mama vipriyam kuryāt = in world, who, to me, displeasure, he who causes; such a one; amareṣu sahasra akṣam mahaendram pāka shāsanam = among divinities, thousand, eyed one, Indra, Pāka [demon] controller; aham na hi pashyāmi = I, do not, indeed, see - find.

"Indeed, I do not find anyone who causes displeasure to me in this world, even among divinities including the thousand eyed Indra, the controller of demon Pāka. [3-19-7]

Sloka 8

adya aham mārgaṇaiḥ prāṇān ādāsye jīvitāṃtagaiḥ |
salile kṣīram āsaktam niṣpiban iva sārasaḥ || 3-19-8

8. adya aham = now, I; salile āsaktam kṣīram = in water, mingled with, milk; niṣ piban sārasaḥ iva = drinking to dregs, swan, as with; jīvita antagaiḥ mārgaṇaiḥ = life, terminating, with arrows; prāṇān ādāsye = lives [of that miscreant,] take away.

"Now, I will take away the lives of that miscreant with my arrows that are terminators of lives, as a swan would drink milk to dregs even if it is mingled in water, duly separating milk from water. [3-19-8]

Sloka 9

nihatasya mayā saṃkhye śara saṃkṛtta marmaṇaḥ |
saphenam rudhiram kasya medinī pātum icchhasi || 3-19-9

9. mayā sankhye nihatasya = by me, in combat, he who is drop dead; shara samkṛitta marmaṇaḥ = by arrows, totally cut-off, having crucial organs; kasya = whose; sa phenam rudhiram = with, froth, blood; medinī pātum icchasi = earth, to quaff, wishes to.

"Whose frothy blood is it that the earth wishes to quaff when I cut off his crucial organs by my arrows and drop him dead in a combat. [3-19-9]

Sloka 10

kasya patrarathāḥ kāyāt māṃsam utkṛtya saṃgatāḥ |
prahṛṣṭā bhakṣayiṣyanti nihatasya mayā raṇe || 3-19-10

10. mayā raṇe hatasya = by me, in war, who is slain; kasya kāyāt = whose, from body; patra rathāḥ sangatāḥ = winged, chariots [birds,] pressing round; pra hṛiṣṭā = gladly; māmsam = flesh; ut kṛitya = up, lifting - rip to pieces; bhakṣayiṣyanti = wish to eat.

"Whose body is it from which birds pressing round gladly wish to wring flesh to pieces for eating when I slay him in war. [3-19-10]

Sloka 11

tam na devā na gaṃdharvā na piśācā na rākṣasāḥ |
mayā apakṛṣṭam kṛpaṇam śaktāḥ trātum iha āhave || 3-19-11

11. iha = now; āhave mayā apakṛiṣṭam = in battle, by me, easily hauled [on ground]; tam kṛipaṇam = that, villain; trātum = to redeem; devāḥ na shaktāḥ = gods, not, capable; pishācā na = evil spirits, not; gandharvā na = celestials, not; rākṣasāḥ na = demons, not.

"Now, when I haul him easily on the ground in a battle that villain will be unredeemable either by gods, celestials, evil spirits, or by demons. [3-19-11]

Sloka 12

upalabhya śanaiḥ saṃjñām tam me śaṃsitum arhasi |
yena tvam durvinītena vane vikramya nirjitā || 3-19-12

12. dur vinītena = by bad, mannered one [miscreant]; yena tvam = by whom [which,] you are; vikramya vane nir jitā = on attacking, in forest, won out - worsted; tvam shanaiḥ sanjñām upalabhya = you, slowly, sensitivity, on regaining; me shamsitum arhasi = to me, to tell, apt of you.

"It will be apt of you to gather your sensitivity slowly and tell me as to which miscreant attacked and worsted you in the forest." Thus Khara spoke to Shūrpanakha. [3-19-12]

Sloka 13

iti bhrātur vacaḥ śrutvā kruddhasya ca viśeṣataḥ |
tataḥ śūrpaṇakhā vākyam sabāṣpam idam abravīt || 3-19-13

13. bhrātuḥ = brother's; visheṣataḥ kruddhasya = especially, of that one who is rancorous; iti vacaḥ shrutvā = this kind of, words, on hearing; tataḥ shūrpaṇakhā = then, ṣhūrpanakha; sa bāṣpam = with, tears; idam vākyam abravīt = this, sentence, said.

On hearing this kind of words of her brother, especially who is rancorous, then Shūrpanakha spoke these words, tearfully. [3-19-13]

Sloka 14

taruṇau rūpa saṃpannau sukūmārau mahābalau |
puṇḍarīka viśālākṣau cīra kṛṣṇa ajina aṃbarau || 3-19-14
phala mūla aśinau dāntau tāpasau brahmacāriṇau |
putrau daśarathasya āstām bhrātarau rāma lakṣmanau || 3-19-15

14, 15. taruṇau rūpa sampannau = in adulthood, look, enriched [good-looking] sukūmārau mahābalau = gentle, great-mighty; puṇDarīka vishāla akṣau = lotus like, broad, eyed; cīra kṛiṣṇa ajina ambarau = jute-cloths, deerskin, dressed in; phala mūla ashinau = fruits, tubers, eaters [subsistent]; dāntau tāpasau = composed, sagacious; brahmacāriṇau [dharma cāriṇau] = celibate [righteousness, followers of]; putrau dasharathasya = sons, of Dasharatha; rāma lakshmanau = Rama, Lakshmana; bhrātarau = brothers; āstām = are there.

"Those two are in adulthood, good-looking, gentle yet greatly mighty, eyes broad like lotuses, dressed in jute-cloths and deerskin, subsistent on fruits and tubers, composed, sagacious and celibate, and they are the sons of Dasharatha, namely Rama and Lakshmana. [3-19-14,15]

------

▼

Here, when asked by Khara 'who mutilated you?' Shūrpanakha should have normally reported that, 'sons of some king Dasharatha namely Rama and Lakshmana did this' But not telling so she starts her narration with an odd appreciation of Rama's aspects. Some of them are as below:

taruṇau rūpa sampannau Though they scarred her on her nose and ears she did not loose her heart for them. This taruṇau rūpa sampannau is in accordance with Rama's aspect which will be sadaiva priya darshanaḥ   'the same pleasant persona at any time' as in Bala Kanda at 1-1-16, as said by Sage Narada to Valmiki. This is the same situation with Hanuma when Seetha asks him to indicate some aspects of Rama so as to believe Hanuma. There Hanuma starts with rāmaḥ kamala patra akshaḥ sarva sattva manoharaḥ - sundara 35-8. 'Rama is lotus-petal eyed one' Further people in sleep, intoxicated or infuriated conditions, will speak their heart supramatta kupitānām bhāva jñānam dṛiṣṭaḥ So Shūrpanakha who is also in a sleep like daze, she came out with her soulful feelings about Rama.

This narration has threefold meaning. 1] Through the eyes of a lustful female; 2] To belittle Rama and Lakshmana's prowess only to extol Kara's pride; 3] To deride Khara's valour so that he gets incited to fight Rama and Lakshmana.

1] Through the eyes of a lustful female:

taruṇau 'came of age.' kanyām varayate rūpam Women's first criterion towards a man is age. But this wording is in dwivacana 'in duel number' so it belongs to both Rama and Lakshmana. Then the question comes 'is she leaning towards both of them?' Yes. She makes eyes at both. First she approached Rama, but he advised her to approach Lakshmana. Lakshmana in turn advises her to approach Rama, thus she got interest in both. This is as per anyonya sadṛishau vīrau   'those darers are similar to one another.' Rama and Lakshmana may be different among themselves, but to the onlookers they appear as one reality. Further, she has hate-thinking for both of them also. She says that 'this situation occurred due to both of them' as at 3-19-18. How can she fall after both at a time? She can, with 'either-or-single-or-both' syndrome because she is a demoness, and unseen are such brothers with their glorious aspects, so far by her.

rūpa sampannau 'their aspects are ravishing' The syndrome is that angāt angāt sambhavasi 'offshoots derive parent's aspects.' So ṃanmatha, the Love-God derived all his aspects from Vishnu. Then it is imaginable how much more that Vishnu might be in his lovely aspect. So Rama is with niratishaya rūpa samṛiddhi   'sublime features, endowed with' that which can be borrowed even by ṃanmatha.

sukumārau 'delicate' like flowers. Rama is said to equal a delicate flower. asīta puṣpa samkāsham Rama's body itself is a flower that which can be cherished by all. Or su = excellent; ku =of earth; mārau = ṃanmatha-s' 'two ṃanmatha-s on earth' ṃanmatha, is only one god of love and without any form or body parts, but these two are having excellent forms and are evident on earth before eyes as per the attribute of kandarpa iva mūrtimān 'he has a form like that of Love-god'

Then her bother Khara may ask 'then why we demons shall hanker after some flimsy, fragile, feeble creatures?' For that she says mahā balau 'greatly vigorous.' They have un-depleting vigour of manhood, which is what required by an aged demoness.

puṇDarīka vishāla akshau 'white-lotus, broad, eyed' 'Their eyes are not like ours while our eyes are samrakta nayanā ghorā 'bloodshot eyes of terrible look'

Or, unlike tamo guṇa udreka 'ever persisting with stolid constituent of nature' and unlike rajo guṇodreka 'ever energetic with sprightly constituent of nature' -- their eyes are sattva guṇa sampanna 'serene with the pure constituent of nature' Though Rama says that he ātmānām mānuṣyam manye 'in my soul I consider I am human' but as per shruti / scripture, yathākapyāsam puṇDarīkam evam akshiṇī 'though his outward aspect is hiding his real Being, his eyes are telling the truth of His supernal nature', hence Rama is other-worldly.

Or his white broad eyes are comparable with kshīra samudra ṃilky-ocean, his abode, with its unfathomable depths, so is his heart.

Or mahā balau puṇDarīka vishāla akshau 'Great mighty and white broad eyed ones' who by their great might annihilate vice and if the evildoer submits himself, their white eyes alone give salvation with a broad-mind. sukumārau puṇDarīka vishāla akshau 'ṃanmatha-like, white, broad eyed'

If it is said that these two are Love-gods on earth, then it may be asked 'where are the flowery arrows of that Love-god to these two?' But the arrows of these earthly Love-gods are their eyes and glances. The lotus is but one among the five arrows of ṃanmatha. Even Seetha under her direly distressed situation in Lanka says that 'gods, celestials, great-souls et al are sanctified with the look of this lotus-petal eyed one' dhanyā devāḥ sa gandhavāḥ siddhāḥ ca paramarṣyaḥ | mama pashyanti ye nādhama rāmam rājīva locanam || sundara 26-41. This verse takes its place in gayatri ramayaṇa as its 14th verse out of total 27 verses.

Further they are excelling only by their nature but not by their dressings and outfits for they are just clad in cīra kṛishṇa ajina dharau 'jute-cloths, deerskin wearers of' ṃahākavi Kalidasa says in his abhijñāna shākuntalam: says iyam [shakuntala] adhika manojñā valkalena api tanvī that, 'this Shakuntala is pleasant though clad in jute-cloths' kimavahi madhurāṇām maṇDananam na akṛitīnām 'nothing can beautify beautiful people' etc. Though not adorned by any extra special ornamentation they by their nature are attractive, as discerned by sages at 3-1-13 of Aranya Kanda rūpa samhanam lakshmīm saukumārya suveṣatam as well by this Shūrpanakha or even by Ravana in part II of Yuddha Kanda, in 99th chapter 12th verse, who also extols these lotus eyes and the aspect of Rama on his first sight at Rama as padma patra vishāla aksham dīrgha bāhum arindamam

2] To belittle Rama and Lakshmana's prowess only to extol Kara's pride.

Should Khara ask in fear 'who is that great-mighty one who mutilated a sister of a great demon like me? Thereby my fear is growing about my own existence.' Then Shūrpanakha's reply is like this, in order to remove Khara's fear of Rama:

taruṇau  'youngsters, boys' for his own father Dasharatha tells Vishvamitra that these are ūna Sodasha varshau; na yuddha yogyatam asya apashyāmi in Bala Kanda. Shūrpanakha that way tells Khara 'brother Khara... these two Rama and Lakshmana are boyish you need not care them, ' Further yauvane viṣayaisiṇām 'boys in young age bother more for enjoymentï -- they are bothered to enjoy life and are not yet fit for a war, so you can war with them'

rūpa sampannau 'just by getups they are brilliant, but lack in courage to confront you' kanyā kāmayet rūpam 'girls cherish charming ones' 'They fall after girls only with their charming aspects that are unworthy to withstand war, so you can war with them'

sukumārau 'delicate or fragile, or flimsy,' because they are sons of some king, grown up in palace chambers, unlike us who grow in ravines; and they eat nonsensically delectable food, unlike us who happily eat raw-flesh. So, wherefrom they can tolerate your bloodshot glances, let alone your arrows...'

mahā.abalu = mahā + a + balau   'very, without, strength; very weak fellows';

furthermore mahā abalau; mahatī rakshaṇīyā abalā yayostu 'a lady, namely Seetha, is to be protected by them. She has become a string fastened around their necks. Then how do they, the sentinels of a woman, can fight you...'

puṇDarīka vishalā akshau 'paled, broadened, eyed' 'Their eyes paled and wide-open awaiting the results of their scarring my nose and ears, and their eyes popped up in daze, then what can they do to you...'

Then Khara may ask, "it is good you have said that they are hapless etc., but they may now muster up some army and fight me back then what?'

For this she says, cīra kṛishṇa ajina dharau 'wearers of jute-cloths and deerskin' And furthering her argument she says, 'how can they afford an army for they themselves have nothing special to wear. That too one of those items, namely the jute-cloth, is begged from a tree that is sessile, and the other, the deerskin, is begged from a deer, that is mobile.  They themselves are shuttling in between mobile and sessile how can they confront you.'

Further, this god, say this Rama, has no other special work or duty, except shuttling between mobile or sessile beings or matter, begging their produce, and beg them to recognise him.

3] To deride Khara's valour so that he can be incited to fight.

Her words also extol the capabilities of Rama and Lakshmana in warring and said to have addressed Khara this way in order to enhance his self-pride.

tarunau; yauvane vijigīsuṇām 'youthful ones are interested in triumphs' 'Because they are the seekers of triumphs, they will definitely triumph over you, you better do not go there and get defeated'

rūpa sampannau; rūpyate anena; asādhāraṇa niratishaya rūpa visheṣatvāt 'with an unimaginable stance' They are known for their stance from their childhood in eliminating Tataka, Subāhu etc., gatvā saumitri sahito nā vijitya ivartate 'on going with Lakshmana, he [Rama] does not return without conquering' yashasaḥ ca eka bhājanam : Tara in Kishkindha, 14-20. 'for glory [Rama is] the only resort.' 'So many praises are said about them and hence they fight you out, better do not go'

If Khara asks, 'how can they fight me, such a mighty one?'

For this she says, sukumārau 'they are dextrous' 'with their dexterity they easily eliminate you'

mahā balau 'greatly mighty' 'Rama is mightily by his shoulder-strength' and also by the use of mahat shabda his might is by his tenacity of mind and ideation to eliminate foes. Or mahā balau 'greatly sturdy.' Though he wars for a long his vigour does not deplete for he uses two vidya-s: bala, atibala 'by the twin principles taught by Vishvamitra his strength does not deplete.

puṇDarīka vishāla akshau 'white-lotus, broad, eyed' His broad white-lotus like eyes does neither contract in anger nor expand in exclamation, as usually happen to anyone who sees an object of interest with unnecessary widening of eyes prīti visphārita īkshaṇam

Next if it be asked 'are they ready for a fight' for which she says 'they are already clad in cīra kṛishṇa ajina dharau 'jute-cloth, deerskin, clad in' They have tightened their girdle-cloths and are armoured in shield like deerskin. Further they also have arrows and knives with which I am scarred so better not to go there and get your throat slit like my nose and ears, for you are a shameless, incapable demon still sitting here as I go on narrating the incident like a story'

Sloka 16 Verse Locator

gandharva rāja pratimau pārthiva vyanjana anvitau |
devau vā dānavau - mānuṣau - vā tau na tarkayitum utsahe || 3-19-16

16. gandharva rāja pratimau = gandharva [celestial,] kings, equalling; pārthiva vyanjana anvitau = kingly, aspects, having; tau = those two; devau vā dānavau [- mānuṣau -] vā = gods, or, demons [ humans] or; tarkayitum na utsahe = to differentiate, not, I am disposed to.

"Having all the aspects of kings they equal the kings of gandharva celestials, and I am ill-disposed to differentiate whether they two are gods or demons. [3-19-16]

▼

Some mms have this dānavau demons as mānuṣau, humans. While Khara, sending his demons to eliminate the brothers, says that they are just humans as at verse 22 in this chapter itself. So Shūrpanakha evasively told a lie to his brother that she could not distinguish between their own race and that of others. Khara for his part believes that neither demons nor gods can hurt his race.

Sloka 17 Verse Locator

taruṇī rūpasaṃpannā sarvāabharaṇa bhūṣitā |
dṛṣṭā tatra mayā nārī tayor madhye sumadhyamā || 3-19-17

" I have seen a curvaceous lady between those two, a ripely beauty, rich in comeliness, adorned with lots of ornaments. [3-19-17]

▼

She is baiting Khara with the beauty of Seetha. But this does not incite Khara like Ravana. She also plays the same card there with Ravana after the failure of this Khara and other brothers.

Sloka 18 Verse Locator

tābhyām ubhābhyām saṃbhūya pramadām adhikṛtya tām |
imām avasthām nītā aham yathā anāthā satī tathā || 3-19-18

18. tām pramadām adhikṛitya = that, lady, owing to; tābhyām ubhābhyām = by them, the two; sambhūya = together; aham = I am; a nāthā satī yathā = without, protector / husband, [insecure] female, as with; tathā = likewise; imām avasthām nītā = this sort of, situation, led into.

"Owing to that woman I am led into this sort of situation, together by those two, as with an insecure female. [3-19-18]

▼

The escapist words may be observed. She is not telling that she alone went to eat away Seetha, but she is holding Seetha responsible for all this and she is evasive to show any action of Seetha as the cause of trouble. She is simply twisting the episode towards her side.

Sloka 19 Verse Locator

tasyāḥ ca anṛju vṛttāyāḥ tayoḥ ca hatayor aham |

"Staying in the van of war I wish to drink the frothy blood of that guileful Seetha along with that of those two, Rama and Lakshmana, when they are killed in war. [3-19-19]

Sloka 20 Verse Locator

Sloka 21 Verse Locator

iti tasyām bruvāṇāyām catur daśa mahābalān |
vyādideśa kharaḥ kruddho rākṣasān aṃtakopamān || 3-19-21

21. tasyām iti bruvāṇāyām = by her, like that, while saying; kharaḥ kruddhaḥ = Khara, vengefully; mahā balān antaka upamān = great, mighty ones, ṭerminator, similar; catur dasha rākṣasān vyādideshaḥ = four, ten, [fourteen,] demons, summoned.

While she is saying like that Khara vengefully summoned fourteen great mighty demons that are similar to the Terminator. [3-19-21]

Sloka 22 Verse Locator

mānuṣau śastra saṃpannau cīra kṛṣṇa ajina aṃbarau |
praviṣṭau daṇḍakāraṇyam ghoram pramadayā saha || 3-19-22

22. shastra sampannau = weapons, possessors of; cīra kṛiṣṇa ajina ambarau = jute-cloths, black, deerskin, clothed in; mānuṣau = two humans; pramadayā saha = young woman, along with; ghoram daṇDaka araṇyam praviṣṭau = ghastly, Dandaka, forest, got into.

"Wielding weapons and wearing jute-cloths and deerskin two humans have got into ghastly forest of Dandaka along with a woman of age." Thus Khara is ordering the fourteen demons. [3-19-22]

Sloka 23 Verse Locator

tau hatvā tām ca durvṛttām upāvartitum arhatha |
iyam ca rudhiram teṣām bhaginī mama pāsyati || 3-19-23

23. tau = them two; dur vṛittām tām ca = = with bad, conduct [treacherous,] her, also; hatvā = on killing; upa āvartitum arhatha = nearby, turning back, you shall [you shall return]; mamaiyam bhaginī = my, this, sister; teṣām rudhiram pāsyati = their, blood, she drinks.

"You shall return to me only on killing those two and also that treacherous woman, and their blood this sister of mine will drink. [3-19-23]

Sloka 24

manoratho ayam iṣṭo asyā bhaginyā mama rākṣasāḥ |
śīghram saṃpadyatām gatvā tau pramathya sva tejasā || 3-19-24

24. rākṣasāḥ = oh, demons; sva tejasā = by own, vigour; tau shīghram pramathya = them two, quickly, on stamping out; asyā mama bhaginyā = her, my, sister's; ayam iṣṭaḥ manaḥ rathaḥ = that, fanciful, heart's, desire; gatvā sam padyatām = having gone, le it be achieved.

"Oh, demons, on going there swiftly stamp them out by you own vigour, thus let the fanciful hearty desire of my sister be achieved. [3-19-24]

▼

On these 23, 24 verses ṃaheshvara Tīrtha says: iyam bhaginī 'this my sister' pramathya 'killing me' mama rudhiram pāsyati 'my blood she drinks' teṣām [samīpam gatvā] to them, you nearing' sva tejasā shīghreṇa 'by your own valour, quickly' tau to them Rama and Lakshmana; dur vṛittām Seetha of impossible, behaviour [to be followed by others]; hatvā; hana himsā gatyoḥ knowing her: because hana indicates 'knowing also; so you know that Seetha, meaning 'be absolved by her' apāvartitum arhatha 'return, you may' to heavens, as all these demons are celestials earlier to their curse.

Sloka 25

yuṣmābiḥ nirhato dṛṣṭvā tau ubhau bhrātau raṇe |
iyam prahṛṣṭā muditā rudhiram yudhi pāsyati || 3-19-25

25. yuṣmābiḥ = by you; tau ubhau bhrātau raṇe = them, two, brothers, in war; nir hataḥ dṛiṣṭvā = flatly, finished off, on seeing; iyam pra hṛiṣṭā = she, well, gladdened; yudhi = in war; muditā = amusingly; rudhiram pāsyati = blood, will drink.

"On seeing your finishing those two brothers flatly in a war she this sister of mine will be gladdened, and then she drinks their blood in war, amusingly. [3-19-25]

▼

This sister of mine would like to drink my blood. So you go to Rama and Lakshmana and then know that Seetha, who has an impossible behaviour. And be absolved by her for all of our sins. And then you may return. Not to Khara but their return is to heavens. The endnote may please be seen. ṃaheshvara Tīrtha.

Sloka 26

iti pratisamādiṣṭā rākṣasāḥ te catur daśa |
tatra jagmuḥ tayā sārdham ghanā vāteritāḥ yathā || 3-19-26

26. iti = in this way; prati samādiṣṭā = ordered; te catur dasha rākṣasāḥ = they, four, ten - fourteen, demons; tayā sārdham = her [ṣhūrpanakha,] along with; vāta īritāḥ ghanā yathā = by gale, called [glided,] dark-clouds, as with; tatra jagmuḥ = there, went.

In this way when Khara ordered those fourteen demons, they have gone to Panchavati along with Shūrpanakha like black-clouds glided by gales. [3-19-26]

Sloka 27

tatastu te tam samudarga tejasam
tathāpi tīkṣṇa pradarā niśācarā |
na śekur enam sahasā pramarditum
vanadvipā dīptvam iva agnim uthitam || 3-19-27

27. tataḥ tathā = then, thus; te nishā carā = those, nightwalkers; tīkshṇa pradarāḥ = with sharp, arrows; vana dvipāḥ = forest, elephants; uthitam dīptvam agnim iva = uprisen, blazing, fire, like; samut arga tejasam = one who has uprisen, peaking, resplendence; tam enam = that, him Rama; sahasā pra marditum = quickly, to completely, trounce; na shekuḥ = not, capable.

But then, though the nightwalker wielding sharp arrows have become ineffectual to trounce Rama like the wild elephants which will become ineffectual to overcome the blazingly uprising wildfire. [3-19-27]

▼

This last verse is mismatching with the episode. This is not found in some publications of Gorakhpur versions but found in others and even in the Eastern recension. The import of this verse is that though the demons have gone to Rama but they could not assault him, which actually happens in next episode. However this verse is also included here as per the text of chaukambha Vidyā Bhavan, Ksashi, and according to Eastern recension.

The curse of Khara and Dūshana

 

ṃaheshvara Tīrtha notes that God Shiva cursed Khara and pardoned saying that he will be released from curse by Rama of Dasharatha. tasmādyaḥ krūra rūpeṇa dehena haratā amṛitam | viṣhṇur dāsharathir bhūtvā mokṣhayiṣhyati suvrata | In the sheṣa dharma of ṃaha Bharata in the discourse between Bhīshma and Yudhistira it is said that sage Yajnavalkya has three sons. They are chandrakanta, ṃahāmedha and Vijaya. These three Brahmans became Khara, Dūshana, and Trishira by the curse of Shiva. The first one chandrakanta is Khara. And the disciples of the three Brahman scholars are fourteen and they also became Brahma-demons, who are now being sent. On listening Shūrpanakha Khara came to know that Vishnu arrived in the form of Rama and thus Khara wanted to get release from the curse by Rama, and hence firstly he sends the fourteen demon-disciples, and later he and his brother also follow the suit.
