Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 25
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha calls out Rama and others and bursts into a wail not being able to endure the threats of ogre women.


Sloka 1

atha tāsāṃ vadantīnāṃ paruṣaṃ dāruṇaṃ bahu |
rākṣasīnāmasaumyānāṃ ruroda janakātmajā || 5-25-1

1; atha = thereafter; tāsām = (as) those; rākshasīnām = ogre women; asaumyānām = with a cruel nature;vadantīnām = were speaking; bahu = a lot; parushham = harshly; dāruṇam = (and) horribly;janakātmajā = ṣeetha; ruroda = cried.

As those ogre woman with a cruel nature were speaking a lot harshly and horribly, ṣeetha cried.

Sloka 2

evamuktā tu vaidehī rākṣasībhirmanasvinī |
uvāca paramatrastābāṣpagadgadayā girā || 5-25-2

2. evam = thus; uktā = being spoken to; rākshasībhiḥ = by ogre women; vaidehī = ṣeetha; manasvinī = with a good mind; paramatrastā = being frightened a lot; bāshhpa gadgadayā girā = with a voice indistinct due to tears; uvācha = spoke (as follows).

ṭhus being spoken to by ogre women, ṣeetha with a good mind being frightened a lot spoke as follows with a voice indistinct due to tears.

Sloka 3

na mānuṣī rākṣasasya bhāryā bhavitumarhati |
kāmaṃ khādata māṃ sarvā na kariṣyāmi vo vacaḥ || 5-25-3

3. mānushhī = a human woman; na arhati = is not suited; bhavitum = to become; bhāryā = a wife; rākshasasya = of an ogre; sarvāḥ = all of you; khādata = eat; mām = me; kāmam = according to your desire;na karishhyāmi = I will not do; vaḥ = (according to) your; vachaḥ = words.

"A human woman is not suited to become a wife of an ogre. All of you eat me according to your desire. I will not do according to your words."

Sloka 4

sā rākṣasīmadhyagatā sītā surasutopamā |
na śarma lebhe duḥkhārtā rāvaṇena ca tarjitā || 5-25-4

4. rākshsaīmadhyagatā = being in the middle of ogre women; tarjitā = being frightened; rāvaṇena cha = by Ravana also; surasutopamā = like a daughter of gods; sā sītā = that ṣeetha; na lebhe = did not get; sharma = comfort; duḥkhārtā = being tormented by grief.

Being in the middle of ogre women, being frightened by Ravana also, like a daughter of gods, that Seetha did not get comfort being tormented by grief.

Sloka 5

vepate smādhikaṃ sītā viśantī vāṅgamātmanaḥ |
vane yathā paribhraṣṭā mṛgī kokairivārditā || 5-25-5

5. mṛigīva = like a fawn; vane = in a forest; yūdhaparibhrashhṭā = lost from its herd; arditā = tormented; kokaiḥ = by wolves; vishantīva = (ṣeetha was) as though sinking (into); ātmanaḥ = own; aṇ^gam = body; vepate sma = (and) shook; adhikam = a lot.

Like a fawn in a forest lost from its herd and tormented by wolves, ṣeetha was as though sinking into own body and shook a lot.

Sloka 6

sā tvaśokasya vipulāṃ śākhāmālambya puṣpitām |
cintayāmāsa śokena bhartāraṃ bhagnamānasā || 5-25-6

6. sā tu = that ṣeetha; bhagna mānasā = with a broken heart; shokena = due to grief; ālambya = holding; pushhpitām shākhām = a flowering branch; vipulām = which was wide; ashokasya = of Ashoka tree; chintayāmāsa = thought; bhartāram = about husband.

ṭhat ṣeetha with a broken heart due to grief, held a wide flowering branch of Ashoka tree and thought about Her husband.

Sloka 7

sā snāpayantī vipulau stanau netrajalasravaiḥ |
cintayantī na śokasya tadāntamadhigacchati || 5-25-7

7. tadā = then; sā = that ṣeetha; snāpayantī = washing; vipulau stanau = ḥer wide breasts; netra jala sravaiḥ = with a flow of tears from eyes; chintayantī = (and) thinking; na adhigachchhati = did not get; antam = end; shokasya = of sorrow.

ṭhen that ṣeetha washing ḥer wide breasts with a flow of tears from eyes and thinking, did not get end of sorrow.

Sloka 8

sā vepamānā patitā pravāte kadaldī yathā |
rākṣasīnāṃ bhayatrastā vivarṇavadanābhavat || 5-25-8

8. sā = that ṣeetha; vepamānā = was shaking; kadaLī yathā = like a plantain tree; patitā = fallen down; pravāte = in an exceedingly windy place; rākshasī bhaya saṃtrastā = trembling with fear of the ogre women; abhavat = became; vivarṇavadanā = with a pale face.

ṭhat ṣeetha was shaking like a plantain tree fallen down in an exceedingly windy place, trembling with fear of the ogre women, and became with a pale face.

Sloka 9

tasyāḥ sā dīrghavipulā vepantyā sītayā tadā |
dadṛśe kampinī veṇī vyāldīva parisarpatī || 5-25-9

9. sā kampinī veṇī = that moving plait; dīrgha vipulā = which was wide and long; tasyāḥ paramstriyāḥ = of that best woman; vepantyāḥ = trembling; dadṛishe = was seen (to be); vyāLīva = like a serpant;parisarpatī = in motion.

ṭhat moving plait, which was wide and long, of that best woman was seen to be like a serpant in motion.

Sloka 10

sā niḥśvasantī duḥkhārtā śokopahatacetanā |
ārtā vyasṛjadaśrūṇi maithilī vilalāpa ca || 5-25-10

10. sā maithilī = that ṣeetha; duḥkhārtā = tormented by grief; shokopahata chetanā = with a mind hit by sorrow; ārtā = struck by calamity; niḥshvasantī = was sighing; nyasṛijat = let down; ashrūṇi = tears; vilalāpa cha = and also cried.

ṭhat ṣeetha tormented by grief, with a mind hit by sorrow, struck by calamity was sighing and let down tears and also cried.

Sloka 11

hā rāmeti ca duḥkhārtā hā punarlakṣmaṇeti ca |
hā śvaśru mama kausalye hā sumitreti bhāminī || 5-25-11

11. bhāminī = ṣeetha; duḥkhārtā = being tormented by grief (said) ; hā rāmeti = Oh! Rama! punaḥ = again; hā lakshmaṇeti = Oh! Lakshmana; hā kausalye = Oh! Kausalya; mama shvashru = my mother-in-law; hā sumitreti = Oh! ṣumitra.

ṣeetha being tormented by grief said : "Oh! Rama!" and again "Oh! Lakshmana!","Oh! Kausalya!, my mother-in-law" "Oh! Sumitra!"

Sloka 12

lokapravādaḥ satyo.ayaṃ paṇḍitaiḥ samudāhṛtaḥ |
akāle durlabho mṛtyuḥ striyā vā puruṣasya vā || 5-25-12
yadāhamevaṃ krūrābhī rākṣasībhirihārditā |
jīvāmi hīnā rāmeṇa muhūrtamapi duḥkhitā || 5-25-13

12; 13. aham = I; evam = thus; ārditā = being tormented; krūrābhiḥ rākshasībhiḥ = by cruel ogre women; iha = here; hīnā = lacking; rāmeṇa = Rama; duḥkhārtā = being tormented by grief; yadā = when; jīvāmi = I am living; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment; mṛityuḥ = death; akāle = at wrong time; striyāḥ vā = to woman; purushhasya = or to a man; durlabhaḥ = is hard; samudāhṛitaḥ = (thus) being said;lokapravādaḥ = the popular saying in the world; satyaḥ = is true.

"I thus being tormented by cruel ogre women here lacking Rama, being tormented by grief, when I am living even for a moment,' death at wrong time to woman or to a man is hard' - thus being said this popular saying in the world is true."

Sloka 14

eṣālpapuṇyā kṛpaṇā vinaśiṣyāmyanāthavat |
samudramathye nauḥ pūrṇā vāyuvegairivāhatā || 5-25-14

14. eshhā = this me; alpapuṇyā = with small merit;kṛipaṇā = wretched; anāthavat = like an orphan; vinashishhyāmi = will perish; nauḥ iva = like a ship; samudramadhye = in the middle of the ocean; āhatā = being hit; vāyuvegaiḥ = by the speed of wind.

"ṭhis me with small merit,wreched, like an orphan, will perish like a ship in the middle of the ocean being hit by the speed of wind."

Sloka 15

bhartāraṃ tamapaśyantī rākṣasīvaśamāgatā |
sīdāmi khalu śokena kūlaṃ toyahataṃ yathā || 5-25-15

15. apashyantī = not seeing; bhartāram = my husband; tam = that Rama;āgatā = obtaining; rākshasī vasham = control of ogre women; tīram yathā = like a bank; toyahatam = hit by water; sīdāmi khalu = I am verily perishing; shokena = with sorrow.

"ṇot seeing my husband that Rama and obtaining control of ogre women like a bank hit by water I am verily perishing with sorrow."

Sloka 16

taṃ padmadaldapatrākṣaṃ siṃhavikrāntagāminam |
dhanyāḥ paśyanti me nāthaṃ kṛtajñaṃ priyavādinam || 5-25-16

16. dhanyāḥ = (only) fortunate; pashyanti = see; tam = that; me = my; nātham = husband; padmadaLapatrāksham = with eyes like petals of an expanded lotus; siṃha vikrānta gāminam = walking with the gait of a lion; kṛitajJṇam = correct in conduct; priyavādiam = pleasant talker;

"Only fortunate see that my husband with eyes like petals of an expanded lotus, walking with the gait of a lion, correct in conduct and a pleasant talker."

Sloka 17

sarvathā tena hīnāyā rāmeṇa viditātmanā |
tīkṣṇaṃviṣamivāsvādyadurlabhaṃ mama jīvitam || 5-25-17

17. mama = to me; hīnāyāḥ = lacking; tena = that Rama; viditātmanā = with a famous soul; āsvādyeva = like after eating; tīkshṇam vishham = a pungent poison; jīvitam = life; sarvathā = in all ways; durlabham = is hard.

"ṭo me lacking that Rama with a famous soul, like after eating a pungent poison life in all ways is hard."

Sloka 18

kīdṛśaṃ tu mahāpāpaṃ mayā janmāntare kṛtam |
yenedaṃ prāpyate duḥkhaṃ mayā ghoraṃ sudāruṇaṃ || 5-25-18

18. yena = by what; mayā = by me; prāpyate = obtaining; idam = this; duḥkham = grief; ghoram = which is horrible;sudāruṇam = very dreadful; kīdṛisham = what kind of; mahā pāpam = great sin;kṛitam = has been done; mayā = by me; janmāntare = in an other life.

"By what this horrible and very dreadful grief is obtained by me, what kind of great sin had been done by me in another life."

Sloka 19

jīvitaṃ tyaktuṃicchāmi śokena mahatā vṛtā |
rākṣasībhiśca rakṣyantyā rāmo nāsādyate mayā || 5-25-19

19. vṛitā = together; mahatā shokena = with great grief; ichchhāmi = I desire; tyaktum = to give away; jīvitum = life; kāmaḥ = (this) desire; na avāpyate = is not obtainable; mayā = by me; surakshitā = I am well protected; rākshasībhiḥ = by ogre women.

"ṭogether with great grief, I desire to give away my life. ṭhis desire is not obtainable by me. I am well protected by ogre women."

Sloka 20

dhigastu khalu mānuṣyaṃ dhigastu paravaśyatām |
na śakyaṃ yatparityaktumātmacchandena jīvitam || 5-25-20

20. dhik astu = fie; mānushhyam = upon human condition; dhik astu = fie; paravashyatām = upon being in the control of others; yat = by what reason; ātmachchhandena = according to own desire; na shakyam = it is not possible; parityaktum = to give up; jīvitam = life.

"Fie upon human condition! Fie upon being in the control of others. By what reason it is not possible according to own desire to give up life."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe pañcaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 25th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
