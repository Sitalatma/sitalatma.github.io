Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 84
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana comes to Lakshmana and beholds Rama having fallen into a swoon and lying in the lap of Lakshmana. Lakshmana informs Vibhishana that on hearing the words of Hanuma saying that Seetha was killed by Indrajit, Rama fell into a swoon. Telling Rama the secret of conjuring trick practiced by Indrajit in killing an illusory image of Seetha, Vibhishana assures him of Seetha being still alive and urges him to send Lakshmana with an army to the sanctuary of Nikumbhila.

Sloka 1

rāmamāśvāsayāne tu lakṣmaṇe bhrātṛvatsale |
nikṣipya gulmānsvasthāne tatrāgacchadvibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-84-1

1. lakṣhmaṇe = (While) Lakshmana; bhrātṛivatsale = who was fond of his brother; āshvāsamāne = was consoling; rāmam = Rama; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; nikṣhipya = keeping; gulmān = the division of his army; svasthāne = in their respective positions; āgachchhat = arrived; tatra = there.

While Lakshmana, who was so fond of his brother, was consoling Rama, Vibhishana, keeping the troops of simian army in their respective positions, arrived there.

Sloka 2

nānāpraharaṇairvīraiścaturbhiḥ sacivairvṛtaḥ |
nīlāñjanacayākārairmātaṅgairiva yūthapaḥ || 6-84-2
so.abhigamya mahātmānaṃ rāghavaṃ śokalālasaṃ |
vānarāṃścaiva dadṛśe bāṣpaparyākulekṣaṇān || 6-84-3

2; 3. saḥ = that Vibhishana; abhivṛitaḥ = surround; chaturbhiḥ vīraiḥ = by four warriors; nānāpraharaṇaiḥ = wielding many types of weapons; yūthapaiḥ = who were chiefs of troops; nīlāṇjana chākāraiḥ = who looked like heaps of black collyrium; mātaṇ^gaiḥ iva = and who were appearing like elephants; abhigamya = approaching; mahātmānam = the great souled; rāghavam = Lakshmana; shokalālasam = who was totally absorbed in sorrow; dadṛishe = saw; vānarāmshchāpi = even the monkeys; bāṣhpaparyākulekṣhaṇān = having their eyes filled with tears.

That Vibhishana, surrounded by four warriors, wielding many types of weapons, and who were chiefs of troops, looking like heaps of black collyrium and who were appearing like elephants, approached Lakshmana who was totally engrossed in sorrow and saw the monkeys also, whose eyes were filled with tears.

Sloka 4

rāghavaṃ ca mahātmānamikṣvākukulanandanam |
dadarśa mohamāpannaṃ lakṣmaṇasyāṅkamāśritam || 6-84-4

4. dadarsha cha = (Vibhishana) also saw; mahātmānam = the great souled Rama; ikṣhvākukulanandanam = the delight of Ikshvaku dynasty; moham āpannam = fallen into a swoon; āshritam = and resting on; aṇ^kam = the lap; lakṣhmaṇasya = of Lakshmana.

Vibhishana also saw the great-souled Rama, the delight of Inkshvaku dynasty fallen into a swoon and resting on the lap of Lakshmana.

Sloka 5

vrīḍitaṃ śokasantaptaṃ dṛṣṭvā rāmaṃ vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
antarduḥkhena dīnātmā kimetaditi so.abravīt || 6-84-5

5. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vrīDitam = the embarrassed; rāmam = Rama; shokasantaptam = tormented with grief; saḥ vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; dīnātmā = distressed in spirits; duḥkhena = with agony; antaḥ = inside; abravīt = asked him; kim etat iti = "What is this?"

On seeing the embarrassed Rama, tormented with grief, that Vibhishana, distressed as he was in spirits, with agony inside, asked him, "What is this?"

Sloka 6

vibhīṣaṇa mukhaṃ dṛṣṭvā sugrīvaṃ tāṃśca vānarān |
uvāca lakṣmaṇo vākyamidaṃ bāṣpapariplutaḥ || 6-84-6

6. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vībhīshaṇamukham = Vibhishana's face; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; tān vānarāmshcha = and those mokeys; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; bāṣhpa pariplutaḥ = with tears pouring down; uvācha = spoke; idam = thse words; mandārtham = with splendid meaning.

Fixing his gaze on Vibhishana's face and looking at Sugreeva and those monkeys, Lakshmana with tears pouring down, spoke the following words, with a splendid meaning:

Sloka 7

hatāmindrajitā sītāmiha śrutvaiva rāghavaḥ |
hanūmadvacanātsaumya tato mohamupāgataḥ || 6-84-7

7. saumya = O gentle ṣir!; shrutvaiva = just after hearing; hanumadvachanāt = from the words of ḥanuma; iti = that; sītā = ṣeetha; hatā = was killed; indrajitā = by Indrajit; rāghavah = Rama; tataḥ = then; upāgataḥ = entered into; moham = a swoon.

"O gentle Sir! Just on hearing through the words of Hanuma, saying that Seetha was killed by Indrajit, Rama entered into a swoon."

Sloka 8

kathayantaṃ tu saumitriṃ saṃnivārya vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
puṣkalārthamidaṃ vākyaṃ visaṃjñaṃ rāmamabravīt || 6-84-8

8. samnivārya = Intercepting; saumitram = Lakshmana; kathayantam = who was thus narrating; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; abravīt = spoke; idam = the following; puṣhkalārtham = amply meaningful; vākyam = words; rāmam = to Rama; visamJṇjam = who was unconscious.

Intercepting the narrating Lakshmana in the middle, Vibhishana spoke the following amply meaningful words to Rama, who was in an unconscious state.

Sloka 9

manujendrārtarūpeṇa yaduktastvaṃ hanūmatā |
tadayuktamahaṃ manye sāgarasyeva śoṣaṇam || 6-84-9

9. aham = I; manye = think; yat tat = that which; tvam = you; uktaḥ = have been told; hanūmatā = by ḥanuma; ārtarūpeṇa = with a sorrowful temper; manujendra = Oruler of men; ayuktam = is absurd; shoṣhaṇam iva = as the drying up; sāgarasya = of the sea.

"I hold that which you have been told by Hanuma with a sorrowful temper, O ruler of men, to be absurd as the drying up of the sea."

Sloka 10

abhiprāyaṃ tu jānāmi rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ |
sītāṃ prati mahābāho na ca ghātaṃ kariṣyati || 6-84-10

10. mahābāho = O; the long-armed!; jānāmi = I know; abhiprāyam = the design; durātmanaḥ rāvaṇasya = of the evil-mined Ravana; sītām prati = towards ṣeetha; na kariṣhyati = ḥe would never do; ghātam = the killing (of ṣeetha).

"O the long armed! I know the design of the evil-minded Ravana towards Seetha. He would never do the killing of Seetha."

Sloka 11

yācyamānaḥ subahuśo mayā hitacikīrṣuṇā |
vaidehīmutsṛjasveti na ca tatkṛtavānvacaḥ || 6-84-11

11. yāchyamānaḥ = eventhough begged; mayā = by me; hitachikīrṣhuṇā = the well-wisher; subahushaḥ = several times; utsṛijasva iti = to give up; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; na kṛitavān = he did not heed; tat vachaḥ = that advice.

"Eventhough begged by me, the well-wisher, several times to give up Seetha, he did not heed that advice."

Sloka 12

naiva sāmnā na bhedena na dānena kuto yudhā |
sā draṣṭumapi śakyeta naiva cānyena kena cit || 6-84-12

12. na shakyeta = It is neither possible; draṣhṭum api = even to see; sā = her; sāmnā = by conciliation; na dānena = nor by bestowing gifts; na bhedena = nor by sowing dissention; kutaḥ = how is it possible; yudhā = by a war?; naiva = It is not possible; anyena kenachit = by any other strategy.

"It is neither possible even to see her by recourse to conciliation nor by bestowing gifts nor by sowing dissention. How is it possible then by a war? By any other strategy, it is impossible."

Sloka 13

vānarānmohayitvā tu pratiyātaḥ sa rākṣasaḥ |
māyāmayīṃ mahābāho tāṃ viddhi janakātmajām| 6-84-13

13. saḥ rākṣhasaḥ = that Indrajit; pratiyātaḥ = retracted; mohayitvā = by hoodwinking; vānarān = the monkeys; mahābāho = O long armed!; viddhi = know; tām jānakātmajām = that ṣeetha (who was seemingly killed by him); māyāmayīm = as unreal.

"That Indrajit for his part retracted, by hoodwinking the monkeys. O the long armed! Know that Seetha (who was seemingly killed by him) as illusory."

Sloka 14

caityaṃ nikumbhilāṃ nāma yatra homaṃ kariṣyati |
hutavānupayāto hi devairapi savāsavaiḥ || 6-84-14
durādharṣo bhavatyeṣa saṅgrāme rāvaṇātmajaḥ |

14. prāpya = after reaching; chaityam = a sanctuary; nikumbhilāmnāma = by name; ṇikumbhila; eṣhaḥ rāvaṇātmajaḥ = this Indrajit; homam kariṣhyati = will pour oblations into the sacred fire; hutavān upayātaḥ = on returning after pouring oblations into the sacred fire (Indrajit); bhavati durādharṣhaḥ = will prove difficult to be overcome; samgrāme = in battle; devairapi = even by celestials; sa vāsavaiḥ = including Indra.

"After reaching a sanctuary by name Nikumbhila, this Indrajit will pour oblations into the sacred fire. On returning after pouring oblations into the sacred fire, Indrajit will prove difficult to be overcome in battle, even by celestials including Indra."

Sloka 15

tena mohayatā nūnameṣā māyā prayojitā || 6-84-15
vighnamanvicchatā tāta vānarāṇāṃ parākrame |

15. nūam = certainly; eṣhā = this; māyā = conjuring trick (in the form ṣeetha); prayojitā = was exhibited; tena = by him; mohayatā = who was employing magical art; anvichchhatā = expecting; vighnam = interruption; parākrame = by the prowess; vānarāṇām = of the monkeys; tatra = there.

"Certainly, this conjuring trick (in the form of killing an illusory living effigy of Seetha) was exhibited by him, who was employing his magical art, expecting interruption by the prowess of the monkeys there."

Sloka 16

sasainyāstatra gacchāmo yāvattanna samāpyate || 6-84-16
tyajemaṃ naraśārdūlamithyā santāpamāgatam |
sīdate hi balaṃ sarvaṃ dṛṣṭvā tvāṃ śokakarśitam || 6-84-17

16; 17. yāvat = before which time; tat = that act of offering oblations in the sacred fire; na samāpyate = is not completed; (within that time); gachchhāma = we shall go; tatra = there; sasainyāḥ = along with an army; narashārdūla = O the foremost of men!; tyaja = give up; enam = this; mithyā santāpam = improper grief; āgatam = has come upon (you); dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; tvām = you; shokakarshitam = tormented by grief; sarvam = all; balam = the army; sīdatehi = is indeed sinking into despondency.

"While the sacrificial performance is not ended, we shall proceed to that place, along with an army. O the foremost of men! Give up this improper grief, which has come upon you. Seeing you tormented with grief, the entire army is sinking into despondency."

Sloka 17

iha tvaṃ svastha hṛdayastiṣṭha sattvasamucchritaḥ |
lakṣmaṇaṃ preṣayāsmābhiḥ saha sainyānukarṣibhiḥ || 6-84-18

18. tvam = you; tiṣhṭha = stay; iha = here; svasthahṛidayaḥ = confident at heart; sattvasamuchchhritaḥ = and exalted in courage; preṣhaya = send; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; asmābhiḥ saha = with us; sainyānukarṣhibhiḥ = who are summoning an army.

"Very exalted as you are in courage, stay you here, confident at heart, and send Lakshmana with us, who are taking an army along with us."

Sloka 19

eṣa taṃ naraśārdūlo rāvaṇiṃ niśitaiḥ śaraiḥ |
tyājayiṣyati tatkarma tato vadhyo bhaviṣyati || 6-84-19

19. eṣhaḥ = this Lakshmana; narashārdūlaḥ = the eminent prince; rāvaṇim tyājayiṣhyati = can make Indrajit abandon; tat = that; karma = act of sacrifice; nishitaiḥ sharaiḥ = by his sharp arrows; tataḥ = then; bhaviṣhyati vadhyaḥ = he will be susceptible to be killed.

"This Lakshmana, the eminent prince, can make Indrajit abandon that act of sacrifice, by employing his sharp arrows. Then, he will be susceptible of being killed."

Sloka 20

tasyaite niśitāstīkṣṇāḥ patripatrāṅgavājinaḥ |
patatriṇa ivāsaumyāḥ śarāḥ pāsyanti śoṇitam || 6-84-20

20. ete sharāḥ = these arrows; nishitāḥ = which are sharp; tīkṣhaṇāḥ = fiery; patripatrāṇ^ga vājinaḥ = with a speed equal to the speed of the wings of a bird; pāsyanti = can drink; tasya shoṇitam = his blood; asaumyāḥ patatriṇaḥ iva = like unlovely birds.

"Lakshmana's arrows, which are sharp and fiery, with a speed equal to the wings of a bird, can drink his blood like cruel birds."

Sloka 21

tatsandiśa mahābāho lakṣmaṇaṃ śubhalakṣaṇam |
rākṣasasya vināśāya vajraṃ vajradharo yathā || 6-84-21

21. mahābāho = O the long-armed one!; tat = therefore; samdisha = dispatch; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; shubhalakṣhaṇaḥ = having auspicious body-marks; vajradhvaḥ yathā = as Indra the lord of celestials; vajram (employed) the thunderbolt; vināshāya = for the destruction; rākṣhasasya = of Indrajit.

"O the long-armed one! Therefore, dispatch Lakshmana having auspicious body-marks even as Indra the lord of celestials employed the thunderbolt to kill Indrajit."

Sloka 22

manujavara na kālaviprakarṣo |
ripunidhanaṃ prati yatkṣamo.adya kartum |
tvamatisṛja riporvadhāya bāṇīm |
asurapuronmathane yathā mahendraḥ || 6-84-22

22. manujavara = O the best of men!; yat = for which reason; na kṣhamaḥ = it is not appropriate; kartum kāla viprakarṣhaḥ = to delay; ripunidhanam prati = the killing of the enemy; adya = at this time; (for that reason); tvam atisṛija vāṇīm = you grant permission in your words; ripoḥ vadhāya = to destroy the enemy; mahendraḥ yathā = as Indra; divijaripoḥ madhane = (hurl his thunderbolt) to destroy the enemies of celestials.

"O the best of men! It is not appropriate to delay the killing of the enemy any further now. Therefore, grant permission to Lakshmana to destroy the enemy, as Indra would hurl his thunderbolt to destroy the enemies of celestials."

Sloka 23

samāptakarmā hi sa rākṣasendro |
bhavatyadṛśyaḥ samare surāsuraiḥ |
yuyutsatā tena samāptakarmaṇā |
bhavetsurāṇāmapi saṃśayo mahān || 6-84-23

23. saḥ rākṣhasaṛiṣhabhaḥ = that foremost among demons; samāpta karma = having completed the sacrifice; adṛishyaḥ bhavati hi = he will indeed be invisible; surāsuraiḥ = to celestials and demons; samara = in battle; surāṇāmapi = even the celestials; bhavet mahā samshayaḥ = feel a great doubt; tena = of him; samāptakarmaṇā = who has completed the sacrifice; yuyutsatā = and has an intent to fight.

"If that Indrajit, the foremost of demons completes the sacrifice, he would not be visible even to the celestials and the demons in battle. If he come with a desire to fight, after ending that sacrifice, the celestials also will feel a great suspicion on their own lives."

Sloka 24

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe caturaśītitamaḥ sargaḥ
