Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 11
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana goes again to the assembly hall, to hold consultations with his ministers, othere important demons and Vibhishana.

Sloka 1

sa babhuva kṛśo rājā maithilīkāmamohitaḥ |
asanmānācca suhṛdām pāpaḥ pāpena karmaṇāḥ || 6-11-1

1. saḥ = that; pāpaḥ = sinful; rājā = king of demons; maithilīkāmamohitaḥ = infatuated by his desire for ṣeetha; babhūva = became; kṛishaḥ = emaciated; asanmānācca = despising; suhṛidām = the kind-hearted; pāpena karmaṇā = and by his sinful action.

That sinful Ravana, infatuated by his desire for Seetha, became emaciated by his sinful action and by his despising of well-disposed people like Vibhishana.

Sloka 2

atītasamaye kāle tasminve yudhi rāvaṇaḥ |
amātyaiśca suhṛdbhiśca prāptakālamamanyata || 6-11-2

2. tasmi = that; kāle = occasion; yudhi = for war; vai = indeed; atīta samaye = having gone by; rāvama = Ravana; amanyata = thought of consultation with; amātyaishcha = his ministers and; suhṛidbhishca = well-wishers; prāpta kāle = to be a fit-time.

Though the occasion for war was lacking Ravana thought of consulting with his ministers and well-wishers then to be a fit time.

Sloka 3

sa hemajālavitatam maṇividrumabhūṣitam |
upagamya vinītāśvamāruroha mahārtham || 6-11-3

3. upagamya = approaching; mahāratham = a great chariot; hemajālavitatam = covered by a golden net; maṇividruma bhūshhitam = adorned with gems and corals; vinītāshvam = and tied with trained horses; saḥ = he; āruruha = ascended (it).

Approaching a great chariot, covered by a golden net, adorned with gems and corals and yoked with trained horses, Ravana mounted on it.

Sloka 4

tamāsthāya rathaśreṣṭho mahāmeghasamasvanam |
prayayau rakṣasām śreṣṭho daśagrīvaḥ sabhām prati || 6-11-4

4. dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; shreṣṭhaḥ = the excellent; rakṣasām = among demons; āsthāya = mounted; tam = that; rathashreṣṭham = most beautiful chariot; mahāmeghasmasvanam = with a rattling sound resembling the rumbling of a large cloud; prayayau = and preceeded; sabhām prati = towards the assembling hall.

Ravana the excellent among demons ascended that most beautiful chariot, with a rattling sound resembling the rumbling of a large cloud and then proceeded towards the assembly-hall.

Sloka 5

asicarmadharā yodhāḥ sarvāyudhadharāstataḥ |
rākṣasā rākṣasendrasya purastātsampratasthire || 6-11-5

5. tataḥ = then; yodhāḥ = warriors; as carmadharāḥ = holding swords and shields; rākṣasāḥ = and demons; sarvāyudhadharāḥ = wearing all tpes of weapons; sampratasthire = marched; purastāt = in front; rākṣasendrasya = of that king of demons.

Warriors holding swords and shield as well as demons wearing all types of weapons, marched in front of Ravana.

Sloka 6

nānāvikṛtaveṣāśca nānābhūṣaṇabhūṣitāḥ |
pārśvataḥ pṛṣṭhataścainam parivārya yayustadā || 6-11-6

6. nānā vikṛita veṣāshca = 9demons) wearing all types of unusual dresses; nānābharaṇa bhūshhitāḥ = and adoring various kinds of jewellery; tadā = then; yayuḥ = marched; parivārya = surrounding; enam = him; pārshvataḥ = from sides; pṛiṣṭhatashcaiva = and even from behind.

Demons wearing all types of unusual dresses and adoring various kinds of jewellery then marched, duly surrounding him from sides and even behind.

Sloka 7

rathaiścātirathā śīghram mataiśca varavāraṇaiḥ |
amātpeturdaśagrīvamākrīḍadbhiśca vājibhiḥ || 6-11-7

7. atirathāḥ = great chariot-warriors; rathaiḥ = in chariots; varavāraṇaiḥ = on excellent elephants; mattaiḥ = in rut; vājibhishca = and horses; ākrīDadbhiḥ = sportively coming in different gaits; anūtpetuḥ dashagrīvam = rushed after Ravana; shīghram = quickly.

Great chariot-warriors in chariots, on excellent elephants in rut and horses supportively coming in different gaits, rushed after Ravana quickly.

Sloka 8

gadāparighahastāśca śaktitomarapāṇayaḥ |
paraśvathadharāścānye tathānye śūlapāṇayaḥ || 6-11-8

8. gadāparigha hastāshca = with maces and iron bars in their hands; shaktitomarapāṇayaḥ = with javelins and iron clubs in their hands; parishvathadharāḥ = wearing axes; anye = some others; shūlapāṇayaḥ = with darts in theirs hands.

Some had maces and iron bars in their hands. Some had javelins and iron clubs. Some were holding axes. Some others had darts in their hands.

Sloka 9

tatastūryasahasrāṇām samjajñe niḥsvano mahān |
tumulaḥ śaṅkhaśabdaśca sabhām gaccati ravaṇe || 6-11-9

9. tataḥ = thereafter; rāvaṇe = as Ravana; gachchhati = was going; sabhām = to the assembly hall; mahān = loud; niḥsvanaḥ = blare; tūryasahasrāṇām = of thousands of trumpets = tumulaḥ = and tumultuous; shaṇkhashabdashca = noise of couches; samjaṇ^e = were produced.

As Ravana was proceeding to the assembly-hall, loud blare of thousands of trumpets along with tumultuous noise of conches were produced.

Sloka 10

sa nemighoṣeṇa mahānsahasābhininādayan |
rājamārgam śriyā juṣṭam pratipede mahārathaḥ || 6-11-10

10. saḥ = that; mahān = excellently; mahārathaḥ = great chariot; abhininādayau = making noise; nemighoṣeṇa = of the sound of the wheels; sahasā = soon; pratipade = obtained; rājamārgam = the royal high way; juṣṭam = possessed; shriyā = of beauty.

That excellently great chariot, making noise of the sound of its wheels, soon entered the beautiful royal high-way.

Sloka 11

vimalam cātapatram ca pagṛhītamaśobhata |
pāṇḍuram rākṣasendrasya pūrṇastāradhipo yathā || 6-11-11

11. ātapatram = the umbrella; vimalam = which was spot-lessly; pāṇDuram = white; ashobhata = shone; pūrṇa tārādhipoyathā = like a full moon; pragṛihītam = was held; rākṣasandraya = (on the head) of Ravana.

The spotlessly white umbrella, held on Ravana's head, shone like a full-moon.

Sloka 12

hemañjarigarbhe ca śuddhasphaṭikavigrahe |
cāmaravyajane tasya rejatuḥ savyadakṣiṇe || 6-11-12

12. tasya savyadakṣiṇe = on his left and right sides; chāmaravyajane = two fans of yak's tails; shuddha sphaṭika vigrahe = with crystal handles; hemamaṇjarigarbhe = and golden fringes; regatuḥ = were shining.

On his left and right sides, two fans of yak's tails with crystal handles and golden fringes were shining.

Sloka 13

te kṛtāñjalayaḥ sarve rathastham pṛthivīsthitāḥ |
rākṣsā rākṣasaśreṣṭham śirobhistam vavandire || 6-11-13

13. sarve = all; te rākṣasāḥ = those demons; vavandire = offered theri salutation; shirobhiḥ = (by bending) their heads; kṛitāṇjalayaḥ = and by joining their palsm; tam = to him; rākṣasashreṣṭam = the best of demons; rathastham = mounted on the chariot.

All those demons standing on ground offered their salutation by bending their heads and joining their palms to his the best of demons, mounted on the chariot.

Sloka 14

rākṣanaiḥ stūyamānaḥ san jayāśīrbhirariṃdamaḥ |
asasāda mahātejāḥ sabhām viracitām tadā || 6-11-14

14. tadā = then; arindamaḥ = Ravana the harasser of enemies; san = possessing; mahātejaḥ = great splendour; stūyamānaḥ = while being praised; rākṣasaiḥ = by demons; jayashīrbhiḥ = throught cheers of victory; āsasāda = approached; virachitām = the arranged; sabhām = assembly.

Ravana the harasser of enemies, possessing great splendor, while being praised by demons through cheers of victory, approached that organized assembly.

Sloka 15

suvarṇarajatāstīrṇām viśuddhasphaṭikāntarām |
virājamāno vapuṣā rukmapaṭṭottaraccadām || 6-11-15
tām piśācaśataiḥ ṣaḍbhirabhiguptām sadāprabhām |
praviveśa mahātejāḥ sukṛtām viśvakarmaṇā || 6-11-16

15;16. mahātejāḥ = Ravana with great splendour; virājamānaḥ = with shining brightly; napuṣā = body; pravivesha = entered; tām = that assembly hall; suvarṇarajatāstīrṇām = viṣuddhasphaṭikāntarām = whose intereior was wrought with pure crystal; rukmapaṭṭottaraccadām = carpeted with skins of deer; abhiguptām = guarded by shāDbhiḥ pishāca shataiḥ = six hundred evil spirits; sadāprabhām = ever shining; sukṛitām = and well- build; vishvakarmaṇā = by Visvakarma.

Ravana with great splendour, with his body shining brightly, entered that assembly hall, which was paved with gold and silver, whose interior was wrought with pure crystal, carpeted with skins of deer, guarded by six hundred evil spirits, ever shining and well-constructed by Visvakarma.

Sloka 17

tasyām sa vaidūryamayam priyākājinasamvṛtam |
mahatsopāśrayam bheje rāvaṇaḥ paramāsanam || 6-11-17

17. saḥ = that; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; tasyām = in that hall; bheje = occupied; mahat = big; paramāsanam = (and) excellent throne; vaidūryamayam = embedded with jewels (cat's eye gems); priyakājinasamvṛitam = covered with skin of deer called Priyaka; sopāshrayam = (and) furnished with pillows.

Ravana, that hall, occupied a big and excellent throne embedded with jewels (cat's eye gems), covered with skin of deer called Priyaka and furnished with pillows.

Sloka 18

tataḥ śaśāseśvaravaddūtān laghuparākramān |
samānayata me kṣipramihaitān rākṣasāniti || 6-11-18
kṛtyamasti mahājjāne kartavyamiti śatrubhiḥ |

18. tataḥ = thereafter; īshvaravat = as a king; iti = thus; dūtān = his messengers; laghuparākramān = who were mering fast; asti = there is; mahat = a gigantic; kṛityam = work; jāne = I know; kartvayam iti = what is being done; shatrubhiḥ = by enemies; samānayata = bring; iha = here; etān = these; rākṣasān = demons; kṣipram = quickly.

Thereafter Ravana, as a king, ordered his messengers, who were moving fast as follows: "There is a gigantic work before us. I know what the enemies are doing. Therefore, bring all the demons here quickly."

Sloka 19

rākṣasāstadvacaḥ śrutvā laṅkāyām paricakramuḥ || 6-11-19
anugehamavasthaya vihāraśayaneṣu ca |
udyāneṣu ca rakkṣaṃsi codayanto hyabhītavat || 6-11-20

19; 20. shrutvā = hearing;tadvacaḥ = those words; rākṣasāḥ = the (messenger) demons; chodayantaḥ = impelling; rakṣāmsi = the demons; avasthāya = staying; vihāra shayaneṣu = in entertainment or in places bed-rooms; udyāneṣuca = or in gardens; paricakramuḥ = went; abhītavat = without fear; anugchām = to every house; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka.

Hearing those words, the messenger impelled the demons staying in entertainment places, in bedrooms or in gardens and went without fear to every house in Lanka.

Sloka 21

te rathān rucirāneke dṛptāneke dṛḍhān hayān |
nāganeke.adhiruruhurjagmuścaike padātayaḥ || 6-11-21

21. adhiruruhuḥ = mounted; te = those demons; eke = some; ruchirān = beautiful; rathān = chariots; eke = some; dṛiptān = wild; dṛiDhān = and strong hayān = horses; eke = some; nāgān = elephants; eke = some; jagmuh = wnet; padātayaḥ = by foot.

Some of the demons mounted beautiful chariots; some mounted wild and strong horses and some mounted on elephants. Some others went on foot.

Sloka 22

sā purī paramākīrṇā rathakuñjaravājibhiḥ |
sampatadbhirviruruce garutmadcirivāmabaram || 6-11-22

22. sā purī = that city; paramākīrṇā = extremely crowded; rathā kuṇjara vājibhiḥ = with chariots; elephants and horses; sampatadbhiḥ = rushing togeher fast; viruruche = appeared; ambaramiva = like the sky; garutmadbhiḥ = by the birds.

That city, extremely crowded with chariots elephants and horses rushing fast together, appeared like birds rushing fast in the sky.

Sloka 23

te vāhanānyavasthāpya yānāni vividhāni ca |
sabhām padbhiḥ praviviśuḥ simhā giriguhāmiva || 6-11-23

23. avasthāpya = parking; vāhanāni = horses; elephants; vividhāni = and various; yānānicha = kinds of vehicles like chariots; te = they; pravivishuḥ = entered; padbhiḥ = by foot; sabhām = the assembly-hall; giriguhāmiva = like a mountain-cave; simhāḥ = by lions.

Parking horses, elephants and various kinds vehicles like chariots, they entered by foot the assembly-hall as a mountain-cave is entered by lions.

Sloka 24

rājñḥ pādau gṛhītvā tu rājñā te pratipūjitāḥ |
pīṭheṣvanye bṛsīṣvanye bhūmau kecidupāviśan || 6-11-24

24. gṛihītvā = touching; pādau = the feet; rāṇ^aḥ = of the king; pratipūjitāḥ = and being greeted in turn; rājJṇā = by the king; anye = some; upāvishan = sat down; pīṭheṣu = on seats; anye = some others; bṛīṣu = on mats(of Kusa grass); kechit = and others; bhūmau = on the ground.

Touching the feet of the king and greeted in turn by the king, some sat down on seats, some others on mats (of Kusa grass) and others on the ground.

Sloka 25

te sametya sabhāyām vai rākṣasā rājaśāsanāt |
yathārhamupatasthuste rāvaṇam rākṣasādhipam || 6-11-25

25. sametya = congregating; sabhāyām = in the assembly-hall; rāja shāsanāt = at the king's command; te rākṣasāḥ = those demons; te = they; upasthuḥ = took their position; yathārham = (each) according to their rank; rāvaṇam (round) Ravana; rākṣasādhipam = the king of demons.

Congregating in the assembly hall at the king's command, those demons took their position round Ravana the king of demons, each according to their rank.

Sloka 26

mantriṇaśca yathāmukhyā niścitārtheṣu paṇḍitāḥ |
amātyāśca guṇopetāḥ sarvajñā buddhidarśanāḥ || 6-11-26
samīyustatra śataśaḥ śūrāśca bahavastathā |
sabhāyām hemavarṇāyām sarvārthasya sukhāya vai || 6-11-27

26;27. mantriṇashca = ministers; paṇditāḥ = who were scholars; nishchitārtheshhu = well-versed in deciding actions; guṇopetāḥ = those endowed with good qualities; sarvajJṇāḥ = those who were all-knowing; amātyāshca = king's ministers; buddhidarshanāḥ = who could perceive things by their power of intellect; tathā = and; bahuvaḥ = many; shūrāḥ = warriors; shatashaḥ = in hundreds; samīyuḥ = gathered; yathāmukhyāḥ = according to their importance; sabhāyām = in assembly hall; hemavarṇāyām- wrapped with gold; sukhāyavai = for felicity; sarvārthasya = of all actions.

ṃinisters who were scholars well versed in deciding action those endowed with good qualities, those who were all-knowing, king's ministers who could perceive things by their power of intellect and many warriors in hundreds, gathered according to their importance in that assembly hall, wrapped with gold, for providing felicity in all actions.

Sloka 28

tato mahātmā vipulam suyugyam |
ratham varam hemavicitritāṅgam |
śubham samāsthāya yayau yaśasvī |
vibhīṣaṇaḥ saṃsadamagrajasya || 6-11-28

28. tataḥ = then; vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; mahātmā = the hight-souled; yashasvī = and the inllustrious; āsthāya = ascended; varam = the excellent; ratham = chariot; vipulam = which was spacious; suyugyam = yoked with good horses; hemavichitritāṇ^gam = its various parts decked with gold; shubham = and auspicious; yayau = and drove; samsadam = towards assembly-hall; agrajasya = of his elder brother; Ravana.

The high-souled and illustrious Vibhiṣhana ascended an excellent auspicious and spacious chariot yoked with good horses with its various parts decked in gold and drove towards the assembly-hall of Ravana his elder brother.

Sloka 29

sa pūrvajāyāvarajaḥ śaśaṃsa |
nā mātha paścāccaraṇau vavande |
śukaḥ prahastaśca tathaiva tebhyo |
dadau yathārham pṛthagāsanāni || 6-11-29

29. atha = thereafter; saḥ = that; avarajaḥ = younger brogher; shashamsa = told; nāma = his name; pashchāt = then; vavande = saluted; charaṇau = the feet; shukaḥ = ṣuka; prahastashcha = and Prahasta; tathaiva; (saluted) likewise (Ravana); dadau = gave; āsanāni = seats; tebhyaḥ = to them; pṛithak = separately; yathārham = according to their rank.

Then, that younger brother Vibhishana told Ravana his name and offered salutation to the feet. Suka and Prahasta followed suit. Ravana gave them suitable seats separately.

Sloka 30

suvarṇanānāmaṇibhuṣaṇānām |
suvāsasām saṃsadi rākṣasānām |
teṣām parārthyagurucandanānām |
srajām ca gandhāḥ pravavuḥ samantāt || 6-11-30

30. gandhāḥ = the aroma; parārthyāguru chandanānām = of the best aloe and sandal paste; teṣām rakṣasānām = painted on those demons; srajāmca = and of garlands; suvarṇanānāmaṇi = of gold and gems; suvāsasām = and attired in good clothes; pravavuḥ = diffused; samantāt = all round; samsadi = in the assembly-hall.

The aroma of the best aloe and sandal paste painted on the persons as also of garlands worn round the necks of those demons duly adorned with ornaments of gold and gems and attired in excellent clothes diffused all round in the assembly-hall.

Sloka 31

na cukruśurnānṛtamāha kaści |
tsabhāsado nāpi jajalpuruccaiḥ |
saṃsiddhārthaḥ sarva evogravīryā |
bhartuḥ sarve dadṛśuścānanam te || 6-11-31

31. sabhāsadaḥ = the assembled persons; na chukrushuḥ = did not yell loudly; na kashchit = ṇo one; āha = uttered; anṛitam = untruth; na jajulpuḥ api = not even talked; uchchaiḥ = loudly; sarve = all of them; samsiddhārthāḥ = were ready for action; ugravīryāḥ = and were endowed with terrible energy; te sarve = all of them; dadṛishuḥ = perceived; bhartuḥ = their lord's ānām = face.

The assembled persons there did not yell loudly. None uttered untruth or even talked vociferously. All of them were ready for action and were endowed with terrible energy. All of them sat, perceiving their lord's face.

Sloka 32

sa rāvaṇaḥ śastrabhṛtām manasvinām |
mahābalānām samitau manasvī |
tapyām sabhāyām prabhayā cakāśe |
madhye vasūnāmiva vajrahastaḥ || 6-11-32

32. tasyām sabhāyām = In that assembly; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; manasvī = full of mind; chakashe = shone; samitau = prabhayā = with brilliance; samitau = among the assmeblage; mahābalānām = of those demons possessing great strength; vajrahastaḥ iva = like Devendra (the Lord of heaven); madhye = amidst; vasūnām = Vasus (a class of gods)

In that assembly, that Ravana, full of mind, shone with brilliance among the assemblage of those demons possessing great strength, like Devendra (the Lord of heaven) amidst Vasus (a class of gods)

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekādaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 11th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
