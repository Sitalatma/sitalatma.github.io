Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 100
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama gives instruction to Bharata as regards the duties of a king and the polity under an ideal monarchy under the pretext of enquiring about the welfare of his father and others.

Sloka 1

jaṭilaṃ cīravasanaṃ prāñjaliṃ patitaṃ bhuvi |
dadarśa rāmo durdarśaṃ yugānte bhāskaraṃ yathā || 2-100-1

1. rāmaḥ = Rama; dadarsha = saw; (Bharata); jaṭilam = with matted locks; chīravasanam = wearing bark-robes; prāṇ^jalim = and with joined palms; patitam = lying; bhuvi = on the ground; durdarsham = incapable of being seen; bhāskaram yathā = as the sun; yugānte = at the end of the world-period.

Rama saw Bharata, with matted locks, wearing bark-robes and, with joined palms, lying on the ground, incapable of being seen as the sun at the time of the dissolution of the world.

Sloka 2

kathaṃ cidabhivijñāya vivarṇavadanaṃ kṛśam |
bhrātaraṃ bharataṃ rāmaḥ parijagrāha bāhunā || 2-100-2

2. rāmaḥ = Rama; katham chit = with some difficulty; abhiJ^ṇāya = recognized; bharatam = Bharata; vivarṇa vadanam = whose face became pale; kṛisham = who was emaciated; parijagrāha = and took; bhrātaram = his brother; bāhunā = by the arm.

Rama recognized with some difficulty his brother Bharata, whose face became pale and who was emaciated, and took him by the arm.

Sloka 3

āghrāya rāmaḥ tam mūrdhni pariṣvajya ca rāghavaḥ |
anke bharatam āropya paryapṛgcchat samāhitaḥ || 2-100-3

3. āghrāya = smelling; mūrdhni = the crown of his head; āropya = placing him; an^ke = on his lap; rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = born in Raghu dynasty; pariṣvajya cha = embraced; tam bharatam = that Bharata; paryapṛichchhat = and enquired (as follows); samāhitaḥ = with an absorbent mind.

Smelling the crown of his head, placing him on his lap, Rama who was born in Raghu dynasty embraced Bharata and with an absorbent mind, enquired of him as follows:

Sloka 4

kva nu te abhūt pitā tāta yad araṇyam tvam āgataḥ |
na hi tvam jīvataḥ tasya vanam āgantum arhasi || 2-100-4

4. tāta = my darling!; kvam = where; abhūt = is; te pitā = your father; yat tvam = (that) you; āgataḥ = have come; araṇyam = to the forest?; tasya = ḥe; jīvitaḥ = being alive; tvam = you; na arhasi hi = ought not have; āgatum = to come; vanam = to the forest.

"ṃy darling! Where is our father, that you have come to the forest? He, being alive, you ought not have to come to the forest."

Sloka 5

cirasya bata paśyāmi dūrād bharatam āgatam |
duṣpratīkam araṇye asmin kim tāta vanam āgataḥ || 2-100-5

5. pashyāmi = I see; bharatam = Bharata; chirasya = after a long time; āgatam = who comes; dūrāt = from a far-off distance; duṣpratīkam = wearing a mournful face; asmin araṇye = into this forest; bata = Alas!; tāta = ṃy darling!; kim = why; āgataḥ = have you come; vanam = to the forest?.

"I see you after a long time, coming from a far-off distance, wearing a mournful face into this forest Alas! Why have you come to the forest, my darling?"

Sloka 6

kacciddhārayae tāta rājā yattvamihāgataḥ |
kaccinna dīnaḥ sahasā rājā lokāntaraṃ gataḥ || 2-100-6

6. tāta = my dear brother!; kachchiddhārayate rājā = Is the king alive? yat tvam = (that) you; āgataḥ = have come; iha = here?; dīnaḥ rājā = (I hope) the miserable king; na gataḥ kachchit = has not indeed departed; lokāntaram = to the other world; sahasā = all of a sudden.

"ṃy dear brother! Is the king alive, that you have come here? I hope the miserable king has not indeed departed to the other world, all of a sudden."

Sloka 7

kaccitsaumya nate rājyaṃ bhraṣṭhaṃ bālasya śāśvatam |
kaccichuśrūṣase tāta pitaraṃ satyavikramam || 2-100-7

7. saumya = O; gentle brother!; (I hope); shāshvatam = the permanent; rājyam = kingdom; na bhraṣṭam kachchit = has in no way suffered; te = from your; bālasya = youthful inexperience; tāta = ṃy darling!; shushrūṣate kachchit = Are you rendering service; pitaram = to our father; satyavikramam = who is truly valiant?

"O, gentle brother! I hope the eternal kingdom has in a no way suffered from your youthful experience. ṃy darling! Are you rendering service to our father, who is truly valiant?"

Sloka 8

kaccid daśaratho rājā kuśalī satya samgaraḥ |
rāja sūya aśva medhānām āhartā dharma niścayaḥ || 2-100-8

8. kachchit = I hope that; rājā dasharathaḥ = the king Dasaratha; kushalī = is well; satyasangaraḥ = he who is true to his promise; āhartā rāja sūyāshva medhānām = he who performs Rajasuya and Ashvamedha sacrifices; dharma nishchayaḥ = and he who has a righteous resolve.

"I hope that the King Dasaratha is well, he who is true to his promise, he who performs Rajasuya and Ashvamedha Sacrifices and he who has a righteous resolve."

Sloka 9

sa kaccid brāhmaṇo vidvān dharma nityo mahā dyutiḥ |
ikṣvākūṇām upādhyāyo yathāvat tāta pūjyate || 2-100-9

9. tāta = ṃy darling! pūjyate kachchit = are you treating respectfully; yathāvat = as before; saḥ = that; upādhyāyaḥ = preceptor; ikṣvākūṇām = of the Ikshvakus; brāhmaṇaḥ = the one who knows the sacred scriptures; vidvān = the learned; dhamanityaḥ = who constantly keeps up the virtues; mahādyutiḥ = and who has a great splendour.

"ṃy darling! Are you treating respectfully as before that preceptor of the Ikshvakus, the one who knows the sacred scriptures, the one who knows the sacred scriptures, the learned who constantly keep up the virtues and he who has a great splendour?"

Sloka 10

sā tāta kaccic ca kausalyā sumitrā ca prajāvatī |
sukhinī kaccid āryā ca devī nandati kaikayī || 2-100-10

10. tāta = ṃy darling!; kachchit = I hope; sā kausalyā = that Kausalya; sumitrācha = and ṣumitra; prajāratī = having good offspring; sukhinī = are happy; kachchit = I hope; āryā = the venerable; devī = queen; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; nandati = is rejoicing.

"ṃy darling! I hope that Kausalya and Sumitra having good offspring are happy. I hope the venerable queen Kaikeyi is rejoicing."

Sloka 11

kaccid vinaya sampannaḥ kula putro bahu śrutaḥ |
anasūyur anudraṣṭā satkṛtaḥ te purohitaḥ || 2-100-11

11. kachchit = I hope that; purohitaḥ = the preceptor; vinayasampannaḥ = who is rich in humility; kulaputraḥ = a son of a noble family; bahushrutaḥ = who has a knowledge of many scriptures; anasūyuḥ = an unenvious person; anudraṣṭā = and full of insight; satkṛitaḥ = is duly honoured; te = by you.

"I hope that the preceptor (Suyajgna the son of Vasishta); who is rich in humility, a son of a noble family, who has a knowledge of many scriptures an unenvious person and who is full of insight, is duly honoured by you."

Sloka 12

kaccid agniṣu te yukto vidhijno matimān ṛjuḥ |
hutam ca hoṣyamāṇam ca kāle vedayate sadā || 2-100-12

12. kachchit = I hope that; vidhijṇ^aḥ = a brahmin who is versed in the traditions; matimān = who is intelligent; ṛiyuḥ = and just; yuktaḥ = employed; te = in your; agniṣu = sacred fires; sadā = always; vedayate = informs you; kāle = in time; hutamcha = and about a sacred fire having been; hoṣyamāṇamcha = or going to be fed with oblations.

"I hope that a brahmin who is versed in the traditions, who is intelligent and just, employed in your sacred fires, always informs you in time, about a sacrificial fire having been or going to be fed with oblations."

Sloka 13

kacciddevān pitṛūn bhṛtvāngurūn pitṛsamānapi |
vṛddhāṃśca tāta vaidyāṃśca brāhmaṇāṃścābhimanyase || 2-100-13

13. tāta = ṃy darling!; kachchit = I hope that; abhimanyase = you are holding in high esteem; devān = the gods; pitṛīn = your ancestors; bhṛityān = dependents; gurūn = and teachers; vṛiddhamshcha = who are aged; pitṛisamān api = as your father too; vaidyāmshcha = the doctors; brāhmanāmshcha = and the brahmins.

"ṃy darling! I hope that you are holding in high esteem the gods, your ancestors, dependents and teachers of your father's age the doctors and the brahmins."

Sloka 14

iṣu astra vara sampannam artha śāstra viśāradam |
sudhanvānam upādhyāyam kaccit tvam tāta manyase || 2-100-14

14. tāta = O; my darling!; kacchit = I hope that; tvam = you; manyase = treat with due respect; (in archery); upādhyāyam = your teacher; sudhanvānam = ṣudhanva; ishhvastra vara sampannam = who is furnished with the most excellent arrows and darts; artha shāstra vishāradam = well-versed in political economy.

"O, my darling! I hope that you treat with due respect; Sudhanva, your teacher in archery, who is furnished with the most excellent arrows and darts and well-versed in political economy."

Sloka 15

kaccid ātma samāḥ śūrāḥ śrutavanto jita indriyāḥ |
kulīnāḥ ca ingitajnāḥ ca kṛtāḥ te tāta mantriṇaḥ || 2-100-15

15. kachchit = I hope that; mantriṇaḥ = ministers; shūrāḥ = who are valiant; ātma samāḥ = like you; shrutavantaḥ = learned; jitendriyāḥ = masters of their senses; kulīnāshcha = of noble birth; iṇ^gitaj^ṇāshcha = and and skilled in interpreting internal sentiments by external gesture; kṛitāḥ = are assigned; te = to you.

"I hope that ministers who are valiant like you, learned, masters of their senses of noble birth and skilled interpreting internal sentiments by external gesture, are assigned to you."

Sloka 16

mantro vijaya mūlam hi rājnām bhavati rāghava |
susamvṛto mantra dharair amātyaiḥ śāstra kovidaiḥ || 2-100-16

16. vijaya mūlan = the source of victory; rāj^ṇām = for kings; bhavatihihi = in deed coures; samvṛitaḥ = from a concealed; mantraḥ = counsel; amātyaiḥ = by ministers; shāstra kovidaiḥ = well-versed in political sciences; mantradharaiḥ = and who can conceal their thoughts within themselves; rāghava = O; Bharata!.

"The source of victory for kings indeed comes from a concealed counsel by ministers, who are well-versed in political sciences and who can hide their thoughts within themselves."

Sloka 17

kaccin nidrā vaśam na eṣi kaccit kāle vibudhyase |
kaccin ca apara rātriṣu cintayasy artha naipuṇam || 2-100-17

17. naiṣī kashchit = I hope you do not; nidrā vasham = fall a prey to excess of sleep; prabhudhyase kachchit = and wake-up; kāle = at appropriate time; chintayati kachchit = i hope you contemplate; apara rātreṣu = during the later half of the night; arthanaipuṇam = adroitness of an action.

"I hope you do not fall a prey to excess of sleep and do wake up at appropriate time. I hope you contemplate during the later half of the night, about the adroitness of an action."

Sloka 18

kaccin mantrayase na ekaḥ kaccin na bahubhiḥ saha |
kaccit te mantrito mantro rāṣṭram na paridhāvati || 2-100-18

18. kachchit = I hope that; na mantrayase = you do not deliberate; ekaḥ = alone; na kachchit = nor indeed; bahubhissaha = together with = numerous men; kachchit = I hope; te mantraḥ = your decision; mantritaḥ = arrived at by you through deliberation; na paridhāvati = does not flow; rāṣṭram = to the public.

"I hope that you do not deliberate alone nor indeed with numerous men. I hope your decision arrived at by you through such deliberation does not flow to the public (even before it is carried out)".

Sloka 19

kaccid artham viniścitya laghu mūlam mahā udayam |
kṣipram ārabhase kartum na dīrghayasi rāghava || 2-100-19

19. rāghava = O; Bharata!; kachchit = I hope that; ārabhase = you launch; kartam = to excente; kṣipram = soon; vinishchitya = considering your interest; artham = an undertaking; mahodayam = which has great benefit; laghumūlam = with least cost; na dīrghayasi = and do not delay it.

"O, Bharata! I hope considering your interest fully, you lanuch an undertaking, which has maximum benefit with minimum coast and indeed do not delay it further."

Sloka 20

kaccit tu sukṛtāny eva kṛta rūpāṇi vā punaḥ |
viduḥ te sarva kāryāṇi na kartavyāni pārthivāḥ || 2-100-20

20. kachhit = I hope that; pārthivā = other kings; viduḥ = know; te = your; sarva kāryāṇi = entire undertaking; sukṛitānyeva = only after they have been successfully completed; punah = and; kṛita rūpāni vā = which have taken shape; na = and do not get to know; kartavyāni = your proposed undertakings.

"I hope the other kings know your entire undertakings only after they have been successfully completed as well as those which have taken a shape, but not your proposed undertakings."

Sloka 21

kaccin na tarkair yuktvā vā ye ca apy aparikīrtitāḥ |
tvayā vā tava vā amātyair budhyate tāta mantritam || 2-100-21

21. tātaḥ = ṃy darling!; kachchit = I hope that; na budhyate = it is not known (by others); antritam = the discussion; yuktyāvā = by a strategem; tarkaiḥ = or by enquiry; ye chāpi = or by any other strategies; aparikīrtitā = no mentioned; tvayā rā = by you; tava amātyairvā = or by your ministers.

"ṃy darling! I hope that others are not knowing, by their enquiries or strategies or by any other approaches not mentioned, the details of discussions you make with your ministers."

Sloka 22

kaccit sahasrān mūrkhāṇām ekam igcchasi paṇḍitam |
paṇḍito hy artha kṛgcchreṣu kuryān nihśreyasam mahat || 2-100-22

22. kachchit = I hope; ichhasi = you solicit for; ekam = one; paṇ^Ditam = wise man; sahasrāt = rather than for a thousand; mūrkhānām = stupids; (for); paṇ^Ditaḥ = a wise man; kuryāt = can do; mahat = a great; nishreyasam = advantageous thing; artha kṛichchhreṣu = in difficult matters.

"I hope you solicit for one wise man rather than for a thousand stupids for, a wise man can be of a great help to you in difficult matters."

Sloka 23

sahasrāṇy api mūrkhāṇām yady upāste mahī patiḥ |
atha vā apy ayutāny eva na asti teṣu sahāyatā || 2-100-23

23. yadyapi = even if; mahīpatiḥ = a king; upāste = employs; sahasrāṇi = thousands; athavā = or; āyutānyeva = tens of thousands; mūrkhāṇām = of fools; na asti = there is no; sahāyatā = helpfulness; teṣu = in them.

"Even if a king employs thousands or tens of thousands of fools, they will not be helpful to him."

Sloka 24

eko apy amātyo medhāvī śūro dakṣo vicakṣaṇaḥ |
rājānam rāja mātram vā prāpayen mahatīm śriyam || 2-100-24

24. ekaḥ amātyo. api = even one minister; medhāvī = who is wise; shūraḥ = valiant; dakṣaḥ = efficient; vichakṣaṇaḥ = and sagacious; prāpayet = causes to secure; mahatīm = a great; shriyam = prosperity; rājānam = to the king; rājamātram vā = or to one who enjoys royal authority.

"Even one wise, valiant sagacious and efficient minister alone can cause to secure a great prosperity to the king or to one who enjoys royal authority."

Sloka 25

kaccin mukhyā mahatsu eva madhyameṣu ca madhyamāḥ |
jaghanyāḥ ca jaghanyeṣu bhṛtyāḥ karmasu yojitāḥ || 2-100-25

25. kachchit = I hope that; mukhyāḥ = superior; bhṛityāḥ = servants; yojitāḥ = are assigned; mahatsu = superior; karmasu eva = works only; madhyamāḥ = mediocre servants; madhyameṣu cha = in mediocre works; jaghanyāḥ tu = and inferior servants; jaghanyeṣu = in inferior works.

"I hope that superior servants are assigned superior works only, mediocre servants in mediocre works and inferior servants in inferior works."

Sloka 26

amātyān upadhā atītān pitṛ paitāmahān śucīn |
śreṣṭhān śreṣṭheṣu kaccit tvam niyojayasi karmasu || 2-100-26

26. achchit = i hope; tvam = you; niyojayasi = are appointing; amātyān = (those) ministers; upadhātītān = who are incorruptible; pitṛipaitāmahān = born of the fathers and forbears of good family; shuchīn = who are full of integrity; shreṣṭhān = and eminent ones; karmasu = in matters; shreṣṭhesu = of great importance.

"I hope you are appointing those ministers, who are eminent incorruptible, born of the fathers and for bears of good family and who are full of integrity in matters of great importance."

Sloka 27

kaccinnogreṇa daṇḍena bhṛśamudvejitaprajam |
rājyaṃ tavānujānanti mantriṇaḥ kaikayīsuta || 2-100-27

27. kaikeyī suta = o; Bharata!; kachhit = I hope; tava = your; mantriṇaḥ = ministers; nānujānanti = do not watch as mere witnesses; bṛisham udvejita prajam = (while) your subjects tremble with great fear; rājyam = (in your) kingdom; ugre ṇa daṇ^Dena = by your stern scepter.

"O, Bharata! I hope your ministers do not watch as mere witnesses, while your subjects in the kingdom wielding tremble with great fear, under your inflexible wielding of the scepter."

Sloka 28

kaccit tvām na avajānanti yājakāḥ patitam yathā |
ugra pratigrahītāram kāmayānam iva striyaḥ || 2-100-28

28. kachchit = I hope; yājakāh = those who perform the sacrifice; nāvajānanti = do not hold you in contempt; ugra pratigrahītāram = as one who accepts terrible gifts; patitam yathā = as one who is fallen; strīyaḥ iva = as women; (hold); kāmayānam = of those lustful men.

"I hope those who perform the sacrifice do not hold you in contempt, as one who accepts terrible gifts; as one who is fallen, as women hold in contempt of those highly lustful men."

Sloka 29

upāya kuśalam vaidyam bhṛtya saṃdūṣaṇe ratam |
śūram aiśvarya kāmam ca yo na hanti sa vadhyate || 2-100-29

29. yaḥ = he who; na hanti = does not slay; vaidyam = a physician; upāya kushalam = skilled in ways and means of aggravating a disease; bhṛityu samdūṣaṇe ratam = a servant intent on bringing disgrace; shūram = and a valiant warrior; aishvarya kāmam cha = seeking kingly power; saḥ = he (himself); hanyate = is slain.

"He, who does not slay a physician skilled in ways and means of aggravating a disease, a servant intent on bringing disgrace and a valiant warrior seeking kingly power, is *himself) slain by them."

Sloka 30

kaccidd hṛṣṭaḥ ca śūraḥ ca dhṛtimān matimān śuciḥ |
kulīnaḥ ca anuraktaḥ ca dakṣaḥ senā patiḥ kṛtaḥ || 2-100-30

30. kachchit = I hope; senāpatiḥ = an army-chief; hṛiṣṭashcha = who is happy man; shūrashcha = a valiant man; matimān = a wise man; dhṛitimān = a courageous man; shuchiḥ = of good conduct; kulīnashcha = one who is born in a good family; anuraktashcha = who is beloved by his subordinates; dakṣaḥ = and one who is efficient; kṛitaḥ = is selected.

"I hope an army-chief, who is cheerful, wise, courageous, valiant, well-behaved, born in a good family, who is beloved by his subordinates and efficient, is selected by you."

Sloka 31

balavantaḥ ca kaccit te mukhyā yuddha viśāradāḥ |
dṛṣṭa apadānā vikrāntāḥ tvayā satkṛtya mānitāḥ || 2-100-31

31. kachchit = I hope; te = those; mukhyāḥ = important warriors; belaventaḥ = who are exceedingly strong; yuddhavishāradāḥ = skilled in war-fare; dṛiṣṭāpadānā = whose excellent actions were seen before; vikrāntāḥ = and the most courageous men honoured; mānitāḥ = and respected; tvayā = by you.

"I hope those warriors, who are excellent strong, skilled in war-face, whose excellent actions were seen before and the most courageous ones are duly honoured and respected by you."

Sloka 32

kacid balasya bhaktam ca vetanam ca yathā ucitam |
samprāpta kālam dātavyam dadāsi na vilambase || 2-100-32

32. kachchit = I hope; samprāpta kālam = at the proper time; dadāsi = you are giving; balasya = your army; bhaktam cha = the daily provisions; dātavyam = to be given; yathochitam = and suitable; vetanamcha = salary; na vilambase = without any delay.

"I hope you are regularly giving your army, the daily provisions and the suitable salary to them, without any delay."

Sloka 33

kāla atikramaṇe hy eva bhakta vetanayor bhṛtāḥ |
bhartuḥ kupyanti duṣyanti so anarthaḥ sumahān smṛtaḥ || 2-100-33

33. kālātikramaṇāt = (when) there is delay; bhaktavetanayoḥ = in giving bread and wages; bhṛitāḥ = the servants; kupyanti = become incensed; bhartuḥ = against their master; duṣyanti = and become corrupt; saḥ = (and) that; smṛitāḥ = is said to be; sumahān = a great; anarthaḥ = unfortunate occurrence.

"When there is delay in giving bread and wages, the servants become incensed against their master and become corrupt; and that is said to be a great unfortunate occurrence."

Sloka 34

kaccit sarve anuraktāḥ tvām kula putrāḥ pradhānataḥ |
kaccit prāṇāmḥ tava artheṣu samtyajanti samāhitāḥ || 2-100-34

34. kachchit = I hope; sarve = all; pradhānataḥ = the foremost; kulaputrāḥ = descendents of your race (kshatriyas); anuraktāḥ = are devoted; tvām = to you; kachchit santyajanti = (and) do they lay down; prāṇān = their lives; tava artheṣu = for your sake; samāhitāḥ = steadfastly?

"I hope all the foremost descendents of your race (kshatriyas) are devoted to you and do they lay down their lives steadfastly for your sake?"

Sloka 35

kaccij jānapado vidvān dakṣiṇaḥ pratibhānavān |
yathā ukta vādī dūtaḥ te kṛto bharata paṇḍitaḥ || 2-100-35

35. bharata = O; Bharata!; kachchit = I hope that; paṇ^Ditaḥ = a knowledgeable man; jānapadaḥ = living in the country; vidvān = a wise man; dakṣiṇaḥ = a skilled person; pratibhānavān = endowed with presence of mind; yathoktavādī = the one who knows how to speak to the point; kṛitaḥ = is selected; dūtaḥ = ad an ambassador; te = by you.

"I hope that a knowledgeable man, living in your own country, a wise man a skilled person endowed with presence of mind and the one who knows how to speak to the point, is selected as an ambassador by you."

Sloka 36

kaccid aṣṭādaśāny eṣu sva pakṣe daśa panca ca |
tribhiḥ tribhir avijnātair vetsi tīrthāni cārakaiḥ || 2-100-36

(9) Adversity brought about by human agencies: officials, thieves, enemies, king's favourites and king himself, when acutated by greed. (10)Twenty types of monarchs (who are not worth-negotiating with):1. a king who is yet a child. 2. Aged. 3. Who has been ailing for a long time. 4. who has been ostracised by his own kith and kin. 5. ho is characterized by a cowardly attitude. 6. who is surrounded by cowards. 7. who is greedy. 8. has greedy associated. 9. who has estranged his ministers and others. 10. who confers with fickle-minded persons 11. who speaks ill of divine begins and brahmins; 12. who is extremely indulged in sensuous pleasures and luxuries; 13. who is ill-fated; 14. a fatalist (who believes that all things are pre-determined or subject to fate); 15. who is afflicted by famine and; 16. by military reverses; 17. who (mostly) remains away from home; 18. who has numerous enemies; 19. who is in the clutches of adverse times and; 20. who is not devoted to truth and piety.

Sloka 71

mantribhistvaṃ yathoddiṣṭaiścaturbhistribhireva vā |
kaccitsamastairvyastaiśca mantraṃ mantrayase mithaḥ || 2-100-71

71. kachchit = I hope that; tvam = you; mantrayate = consult with; chaturbhiḥ = four; tribhireva vā = or three; mantribhiḥ = ministers; mantram = a proposal yathoddiṣṭaiḥ = as mentioned in scriptures; samastaiḥ = collectively; vyastaishcha = and singly; mithaḥ = in secret

"I hope that you consult with three or four ministers as mentioned in scriptures any proposal collectively and singly with each of them in secret."

Sloka 72

kaccitte saphalā vedāḥ kaccitte saphalāḥ kriyāḥ |
kaccitte saphalā dārāḥ kaccitte saphalaṃ śrutam || 2-100-72

72. saphalāḥ kachchit = do you find advantages; te = in you; vedāḥ = study of Vedas?; kachchit te kriyāḥ = are your acts; saphalāḥ = productive of fair results?; saphalāḥ kachchit = do you benefit; te dārāḥ = from the company of your consorts?' te shrutam kachchit = ḥas your learning; saphalam = been fruitful?

"Do you find advantages in your study of Vedas? Are your acts, production of fair results? Do you benefit from the company of your consorts? Has your learning been fruitful?"

Sloka 73

kaccideṣaiva te buddhiryathoktā mama rāghā |
āyuṣyā ca yaśasyā ca dharmakāmārthasaṃhitā || 2-100-73

73. rāghava = O; Bharata!; kachchit = I hope that; te buddhiḥ = your conviction; eṣaiva = is the same; yathā uktā = as mentioned (in the foregoing verses) mama = by me; samhitā = which is conducive; āyuṣyā = to long life; yashasyācha = fame; dharma kāmārtha = religious merit; enjoyment and wealth.

"O, Bharata! I hope that your conviction, is the same as mentioned by me in the foregoing verses, which is conducive to long life, fame, religious merit, enjoyment and wealth."

Sloka 74

yāṃ vṛttiṃ vartate rāto yāṃ canaḥ prapitāmahāḥ |
tāṃ vṛttiṃ vartase kaccidyāca satpathagā śubhā 2-100-74

74. vartase kachchit = Do you follow; tām = that; vṛittam = common practice; yām = which; tātaḥ = our fore father; vartate = follows; yām = and which; naḥ = our; prapitāmahāḥ = forefathers observed; yācha = and which; satpathagā = is in accord with the path of the virtuous; shubhā = and which is distinguished (in itself).

"Do you follow the common practice, which our fore fathers observed and which is in accord with the path of the virtuous and which is distinguished in itself."

Sloka 75

kaccit svādu kṛtaṃ bhojyameko nāśnāsi rāghava |
kaccidāśaṃsamānebhyo mitrebhyaḥ samprayacchasi 2-100-75

75. rāghava = O Bharata!; kachchit = I hope that; ekaḥ nāshnāpi kaschit = you alone do not eat; svādukṛitam = nicely made; bhojyam = eatable; samprayachchasi = and do you give (it); mitrebhyaḥ = to your friends; āsham samānebhyaḥ = who wish to receive the same.

"I hope you do not eat by yourself nicely made eatable and do you share it with your friends, who seek it?"

Sloka 76

avāpya kṛtsnāṃ vasudhāṃ yathāva |
ditaśchyutaḥ svargamupaiti vidvān || 2-100-76

76. rājātu = A king; mahā matiḥ = who is very wise; vidvān = and learned; avāpya = having obtained; kṛitsnām = the entire; vasudhām = earth; daṇ^Dadharaḥ = a administering justice; prajānām = to the people; pālayitvā = and having ruled; dharmeṇa = by righteousness; yathāvat = properly; chyutaḥ = when detached; itaḥ = from this mortal body; upaitihi = and indeed obtains; svargam = heaven.

"A wise and learned king, having obtained and ruled the entire earth, properly by righteousness and by administering justice to the people, indeed ascends to heaven when detached from the mortal body."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe śatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 100th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
