Introduction

Jambavan, seated along with the monkey-troops, requested Hanuma to narrate clearly whatever has occurred. Hanuma narrates in detail how he saw ṃount ṃainaka in the course of his journey to Lanka and how he escaped on the way from the clutches of Surasa, a great serpent living in the ocean. Hanuma also narrated about his killing of Simhika, a female demon on the way and also about defeating another female demon (Lanka-incarnate) who was guarding the City of Lanka. Hanuma explains to Jambavan, how he beholds Seetha in Ashoka garden after a thorough search in the City of Lanka and how he introduces himself as an envoy sent by Rama and Sugreeva. Hanuma informs Jambavan that he hands over a finger-ring of Rama as a token of remembrance to Seetha and that he further asks for a return-token of remembrance from her, to be handed over to Rama. Hanuma then narrates about Seetha having delivered to him an excellent jewel for her head, as a token of remembrance. He further narrates about the demolition of the garden attached to Ravana's gynaecium and about killing of Aksha, Ravana's son. Hanuma further informs about his capture by Indrajit, Ravana's son and his tail being burnt by demons on receiving the orders of Ravana. He further informs about his setting fire of the City of Lanka. He finally narrates about his re-visit to Seetha and his return to ṃount ṃahendra.

152. tataḥ = then; āgatāḥ = having reached; nagaradvāram = the city-gate; shūrāḥ = those strong; rākṣasāḥ = demons; aghoṣayan = made a proclamation of my presence; rājamārge = in the principal streets; baddham = bound; agnisamvṛitam = and enveloped in flames (as I was).

"ṭhen, having reached the city-gate, those strong demons made a loud proclamation of my presence in the principal streets, bound and enveloped in flames as I was."

Sloka 153

tato aham sumahat rūpam samkṣipya punaḥ ātmanaḥ || 5-58-153
vimocayitvā tam bandham prakṛtisthaḥ sthitaḥ punaḥ |
āyasam parigham gṛhya tāni rakṣāṃsi asūdayam || 5-58-154

153; 154. tataḥ = then; samkṣipya = after contracting; ātmanaḥ sumahat rūpam = my gigantic form; punaḥ = again; vimochayitvā = and releasing; tam bandham = my bondage; sthitaḥ = I stood; pinaḥ = again; prakṛitiṣthaḥ = in my native form; gṛihya = seizing; āyasam parigham = an iron bar; asūdayam = I killed; tāni rakṣāmisi = those demons.

"ṭhen, after contracting my gigantic form once more and releasing my bondage, I stood in my native form again. Seizing an iron bar, I killed those demons."

Sloka 155

tataḥ tan nagara dvāram vegena āplutavān aham |
pucchena ca pradīptena tām purīm sāṭṭa gopurām || 5-58-155
dahāmi aham asambhrānto yuga anta agniḥ iva prajāḥ |

155. tataḥ = then; aham = I; āplutavān = jumped; vegena = quickly; tat nagara dvāram = on to that city-gate; asambhrāntaḥ = without set fire; tām purim = that city; sāṭṭa prākāragopuram = with its attics; ramparts and gates; pradīptena puchchhena = by my tail inflamed; yugāntāgniḥ iva = even as a fire at the end of the world; prajā = burns all living creatures.

"ṭhen, I quickly jumped on to that city-gate and without any bewilderment, I set fire that city with its attics, ramparts and gates with my tail inflamed, even as a fire at the end of the world, burns all living creatures."

Sloka 156

vinaṣṭā jānakī vyaktam na hyadagdhaḥ pradṛśyate || 5-58-156
laṅkāyām kaściduddeśaḥ sarvā bhasmīkṛtā purī |
dahatā ca mayā lankām daghdā sītā na saṃśayaḥ || 5-58-157
rāmasya hi mahatkāryam mayedam vitathīkṛtam |

156; 157. laṇ^kāyām = In Lanka; na uddeshaḥ = no place; kashchit = whatsoever; pradṛishyate = is seen indeed; adagdhaḥ = not being burnt; sarvā = the entire; purī = city burnt; sarvā = the entire; purī- city; bhasmīkṛitā = has been reduced to ashes; jānakī = ṣeetha; nūnam = surely; vinaṣṭā = has been lost.

"ṭhe entire Lanka has been reduced to ashes. ṇo place is seen unburnt in this City. ṭherefore Seetha also must have been surely burnt."

Sloka 158

iti śokasamāviṣṭaścantāmahamupāgataḥ || 5-58-158
atha aham vācam aśrauṣam cāraṇānām śubha akṣarām |
jānakī na ca dagdhā iti vismaya udanta bhāṣiṇām || 5-58-159

158; 159. iti = thus; shokamāviṣṭaḥ = filled with grief; aham = I; upāgataḥ = came about; chintām = to reflect (as above); ashrouṣam = (meanwhile) I heard; shubhākṣarām = the auspiciously worded; vācham = utterance; chāraṇānām = of charanas the celestial singers; vismayodant bhāṣaṇam = who were narāting a tale of ashtonishment; iti = saying that; jānakī = ṣeetha; na chadagdhā = has not been burnt.

"ṭhus filled with grief, I came about to reflect as above. ṃeanwhile, I heard the auspiciously worded utterance of charanas the celestial signers who were narrating a tale of astonishment saying that Seetha has not been burnt."

Sloka 160

tato me buddhiḥ utpannā śrutvā tām adbhutām giram |
adagdhā jānakītyevam nimittaiścopalakṣitā || 5-58-160

160. shrutvā = hearing; tām adbhutām giram = those surprising words; buddhiḥ = (the following) thought; tataḥ = then; utpannā = arose; me = in me; ityevam = that; jānakī = ṣeetha; adagdhā = was not burnt; upalakṣitā = It was understood by implication; nimittaishcha = because of good omens too.

"After hearing those surprising words, a thought arose in me that ṣeetha escaped being burnt. This indicated by the following good omens:

Sloka 161

dīpyamāne tu lāṅgūle na mām dahati pāvakaḥ |
hṛdayam ca prahṛṣṭam me vātāḥ surabhigandhinaḥ 5-58-161

161. lāṇgūle = (even though) my tail; dīpyamāne = is burning; pāvakaḥ = the fire; na dahati = is not burning; mām = me; me = my; hṛidayamcha = heart too; prahṛiṣṭam = is delighted; vātāḥ = the winds; surābhigandhinaḥ = are sweet-smelling.

"In the first place, even though me tail is burning, the fire is not burning my body. ṃy heart too is delighted. The winds are sweet-smelling."

Sloka 162

tairnimittaiśca dṛṣṭārthaiḥ kāraṇaiśca mahāguṇaiḥ |
ṛṣivākyaiśca siddhārthairabhavam hṛṣṭamānasaḥ || 5-58-162

162. abhavam = I became; hṛiṣṭamānasaḥ = delighted in mind; taiḥ = by those; nimittaiḥ = good omens; dṛiṣṭārthaih = having their fruits apparent; kāraṇashcha = by the reasons; mahāguṇaiḥ = of high characteristics; ṛiṣivākyeshcha = by the words of sages; siddhārthaih = who were efficacious.

"ṃy heart was delighted by seeing those good omens having their fruits apparent, by looking into the their fruits apparent, by looking into the reasons of high characteristics and by hearing the words of accomplished sages."

Sloka 163

punaḥ dṛṣṭā ca vaidehī visṛṣṭaḥ ca tayā punaḥ |
tataḥ parvatamāsādya tatrariṣtamaham punaḥ || 5-58-163
pratiplavanamārebhe yuṣmaddarśanakāṃkṣayā |

163. dṛiṣṭavā = seeing; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; punaḥ = once more; visṛiṣṭaḥ = relieed; tayā = by her; punaḥ = again; tataḥ = and then; āsādya = reaching; ariṣṭaparvatam = ṃount Arishta; punaḥ = again; tatra = there; aham = I; ārabhe = started; pratiplavanam = leaping back; yuṣmaddarshana kāṇ^kṣayā = with a longing to see you.

"ṣeeing ṣeetha once more, relieved by her again and then reaching the ṃount Arishta there again, I started leaping back (over the sea) with a longing to see you (all)."

Sloka 164

tataḥ pavanacandrarka siddhagandharva sevitam || 5-58-164
panthānamahamākramya bhavato dṛṣṭavāniha |

164. tataḥ = then; ākramya = treading upon; panthānam = the path of the sky; pavana chandrārka siddhagandharva sevitam = frequented by the wind; the moon; the sun; ṣiddhas the semi-divine beings and Gandharvas the celestial musicians; aham dṛiṣṭavān- I saw; bhavatah = you (all); iha = here.

"ṭhen, treading upon the path of the sky, frequented by the wind, the moon, the sun, Siddhas the semi-divine beings and Gandharvas the celestial musicians, I saw you all here."

Sloka 165

rāghavasya prabhāvena bhavatām caiva tejasā || 5-58-165
sugrīvasya ca kārya artham mayā sarvam anuṣṭhitam |

165. sarvam = all the task; anuṣṭhitam = was performed; mayā = by me; kāryārtham cha = for accomplishing the purpose; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva; prabhāveṇa = by the power; rāghavasya = of Rama; bhavāam tejasā chaiva = and by your brilliance.

"I performed all tasks for accomplishing the purpose of ṣugreeva, by the grace of Rama and also through your energy."

Sloka 166

etat sarvam mayā tatra yathāvat upapāditam || 5-58-166
atra yan na kṛtam śeṣam tat sarvam kriyatām iti |

166. sarvam = all; etat = this; upapāditam = has been performed; mayā = by me; tatra = there; yathāvat = properly; yat = that which; sheṣam = is remaining; nakṛitam = undone; atra = in this task; kriyatām = is to be done; sarvam = entirely (by you).

"I have done all this properly there. Do accomplish that which remains undone in this task by me and complete it."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe aṣṭapaṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 58th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
