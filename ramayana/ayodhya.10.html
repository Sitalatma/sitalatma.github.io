Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 10
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter, Kaikeyi enters the house of wrath. Dasaratha tries to pacify her anger.

Sloka 1

vidarśitā yadā devī kubjayā pāpayā bhṛśam |
tadā śete sma sā bhūmau digdhaviddheva kinnarī || 2-10-1

1. yadā = when; vidarshitā = being wrongly preached; kubjayā = by the hunch backed manthara; bhṛisham pāpayā = who was the most sinful person; tadā = then; sā devī = that kaikeyi; shetesma = lied down; bhumau = on floor; kinnarīva = like a kinnara woman; digdhaviddhā = beaten by poisoned arrow.

Having been thus wrongly preached by that sinful ṃanthara. Kaikeyi lied down on the floor as a Kinnara woman beaten by a poisoned arrow.

Sloka 2

niścitya manasā kṛtyam sā samyagiti bhāminī |
mantharāyai śnaḥ sarvamāccakṣe vicakṣaṇā || 2-10-2

2. sā bhāminī = that lady; vichakshhaṇā = skilful; nishchitya = after deciding; samyak = well; manasā = in mind; iti = thus; kṛityam = what ought to be done; āchachakshae = said; sarvam = all; mantharāyai = to ṃanthara; shanaiḥ = slowly.

The skilful Kaikeyi, after deciding in mind what ought to be done told it all slowly to ṃanthara.

Sloka 3

sā dīnā niścayaṃ kṛtvā mantharāvākyamohitā |
nāgakanyeva niḥsvasya dīrghamuṣṇaṃ ca bhāminī || 2-10-3
muhūrtaṃ cintayāmāsa mārgamātmasukhāvaham |

3. mantharāvākyamohitā = stupefied by the words of ṃanthara; sā bhāminī = that Kaikeyi; dīna = became dejected; nishchayam kṛitvā = made firm resolution; niḥshvasya = sighed; dīrgham = deeply; ushhṇamcha = and warmly; nāgakanyeva = like maiden-serpant; chintayāmāsa = thought; muhūrtam = for a moment; mārgam = about the way; ātmasukhāvaham = which can lead to her happiness.

Being stupefied by the words of ṃanthara, that Kaikeyi became dejected, made firm resolve, with deep and warm sigh, like a maiden-serpent and thought for that moment about the ways which can lead to her happiness.

Sloka 4

sā suhṛccārthakāmā ca taṃ niśamya suniścayam || 2-10-4
babhūva paramaprītā siddhiṃ prāpyeva mantharā |

4.sā mantharā = that ṃanthara; suhṛichcha = who is desirous of getting benefit; nishamya = heard; tam sunishchayam = of that firm resolution; babhūva = became; paramaprītā = very much pleased; prapyeva = as if achieved; siddhim = success.

ṃanthara, who is Kaikeyi's companion and who is desirous of getting benefit, heard of Kaikeyi's firm resolve and became very much pleased as if she achieved success.

Sloka 5

atha sā ruṣitā devī samyakkṛtvā viniścayam || 2-10-5
saṃviveśābalā bhūmau niveśya bhṛkuṭiṃ mukhe |

5. atha = afterwards; sā abala = that lady; devī = the queen; rushhitā = being angry; samyak = well; kṛitvā = made; vinishchayam = determination; savivesha = lied down; bhūmau = on floor; niveshya = keeping; bhrukutim = eyebrows contracted; mukhe = in face.

Kaikeyi, being angry and well determined, lied down on the floor with eye brows, knitted in her face.

Sloka 6

tataścitrāṇi mālyāni divyānyābharaṇāni ca || 2-10-6
apaviddhāni kaikeyyā tāni bhūmiṃ prapedire |

6.tataḥ = thereafter; tāni = those; chitrāṇi = chaplets; divyāni = beautiful; ābharaṇāni = ornaments; apaviddhām = which were thrown away; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; prapedire = reached; bhūmim = the floor.

Thereafter, she threw away her colourful chaplets and beautiful ornaments on to the floor.

Sloka 7

tayā tānyapaviddhāni mālyānyābharaṇāni ca || 2-10-7
aśobhayanta vasudhāṃ nakṣatrāṇi yathā nabhaḥ |

7. tāni = those; mālyāni = chaplets; ābharāṇicha = and ornaments; apaviddhāni = which were thrown away; tayā = by her; ashobhayanta vasudhām = were making the earth shine; yathā = as; nakshhatrāṇi = stars; babhaḥ = in the sky.

Those chaplets and ornaments which were thrown away by her were making the earth shine, as stars to the sky.

Sloka 8

krodhāgāre nipatitā sā babhau malināmbarā || 2-10-8
ekaveṇīṃ dṛḍhaṃ baddhvā gatasattveva kinnarī |

8. baddhvā = tying; ekaveṇīm = single braid of; dṛiDham = tightly; malināmbarā = wearing soiled clothes; sā = that Kaikeyi; nipatitā = lying down; krodhāre = in chamber of wrath; babhau = shone(was); kinnarī eva = like kinnara woman.

Tying her single braid of hair tightly over her head and wearing soiled clothes, that Kaikeyi lying down in the chamber of wrath was looking like a Kinnara woman lying down dead.

Sloka 9

ājñāpya tu mahārājo rāghavasyābhiṣecanm || 2-10-9
upasthāsamanujñāpya praviveśa niveśanm |

9. mahārājaḥ tu = but king Dasaratha; ājJṇāpya = ordered for; rāghavasya = Rama's; abhishechanam = coronation; anujJṇāpya = took permission; upasthānam = from the nearest; pravivesha = entered; nivehsanam = the palace.

King Dasaratha ordered for various arrangements to be made for Rama's coronation, took permission to leave from the elders who were present there and entered his palace.

Sloka 10

adya rāmābhiṣeko vai prasiddha iti jajñivān || 2-10-10
priyārhaṃ priyamākhyātuṃ viveśāntaḥpuraṃ vaśī |

10. vashī = Dasaratha who kept all activities under his control; vivesha = entered into; antaḥ puram = inner chambers; ākhyātum = to tell; priyam = the good news; priyārham = to Kaikeyi who was a worthy woman to be told good news.

king Dasaratha, who kept all activates under his control entered into the inner chambers to tell the good news of Rama's coronation to Kaikeyi who was a worthy woman to be told the good news first (thinking that she has not yet heard the news).

Sloka 11

sa kaikeyyā gṛhaṃ śreṣṭhaṃ praviveśa mahāyaśāḥ || 2-10-11
pāṇḍurābhramivākāśaṃ rāhuyuktaṃ niśākaraḥ |

11. saḥ = that Dasaratha; mahāyashāḥ = with great glory; pravivesha = entered; shreshhṭam = most excellent; kaikeyyāgṛiham = Kaikeyi's house; nishākaraḥ ākāshamiva = as the moon into sky; pāṇDurābhram = with white clouds; rāhuyuktam = containing Rahu; the demon.

That Dasaratha with vast glory entered Kaikeyi's chambers as moon enters the sky with white clouds.

latāgṛhaiścitragṛhaiścampakāśokaśobhitaiḥ || 2-10-13
dāntarājata sauvarṇavedikābhissamāyutam |
nityapuṣpaphalairvṛkṣairvāpībhiścopaśobhitam || 2-10-14
dāntarājatasauvarṇaiḥ saṃvṛtaṃ paramāsanaiḥ |
vividhyairannapānaiścha bhakṣyaiścavi vidhairapi || 2-10-15

12;13;14;15;16. mahārājaḥ = the great king; rājā = Dasaratha; pravishya = entering; tat = that; svam = his; Riddhimat = magnificient; antaḥ puram = palace;shukabarahiṇa samyuktam = containing parrots and peacocks; krauncha hamsa rutāyutam = filled with sounds of birds like curlews and swans; vāditraravasanghushhtam = resonant with sounds of musical instruments; kubjā vāmanikāyutam = consisting of short and hunch backed maid servants; champakāshoka shobhitai = with lovely champaka and ashoka trees; latāgṛihaiḥ = with bowers surrounded by creapers; chitragṛihaiḥ cha = with painted rooms; dāntarājata souvarṇa; vedikābhiḥ = with altars built with ivory; silver and gold;samāyutam = endowed with vsikshhaiḥ = trees; nitya pushhpa phalaiḥ = always yielding flowers and fruits; vāpībhiḥ cha = and with wells; upashobhitam = adorned with paramāsanaiḥ = beautiful seats; dāntarājata sauvarṇaiḥ = of ivory; silver and gold; upashobhitam = adorned with vividhaiḥ = various; aunapānaiḥ cha = foods and drinks; vividhaiḥ = various; bhakshhaiḥ cha = snacks; upapannam = endowed with; bhūshhitaiḥ = beautifully adorned; mahārhaiḥ = worthy women; tridivopamam = looking like heaven; nadadarsha = could not find; priyam = beloved; kaikeyīm = Kaikeyi; shayauottame = on the best couch.

King Dasaratha entered his magnificent palace but could not find his beloved kaikeyi on her best couch there. Peacocks and parrots were being reared in that palace, sounds of birds like curlews, and swans were heard around. The place was resonant with sounds of musical instruments. Short and hunch-backed maid-servants were moving here and there. There were bowers surrounded with creepers and lovely champaka and Ashoka trees. There were painted rooms. There were altars built with ivory, silver and gold. There were trees yielding flowers and fruits in all seasons and wells in the middle. There were beautiful seats constructed with ivory, silver and gold. Various types of food, drinks and snacks were made available. That palace was looking like heaven with beautifully adorned women.

Sloka 17

sa kāmabalasaṃyukto ratyarthaṃ manujādhipaḥ || 2-10-17
apaśyan dayitāṃ bhāryāṃ papraccha viṣasāda ca |

17. kāmabalasamyuktaḥ = being possessed of power of lust; saḥ = that ; manujādhipaḥ = king; ratyartham = with object of sexual pleasure; vishhasāda = was distress; apashyam = in not seeing; dayitām = beloved; bhāryām = wife; paprachachha = and asked also.

Being possessed of power of lust, that king who came with object of sexual pleasure, was distressed in not finding his beloved wife and enquired the people there.

Sloka 18

na hī tasya purā devī tāṃ veldāmatyavartata || 2-10-18
na ca rājā gṛhaṃ śūnyaṃ praviveśa kadācana |

18. purā = in former times; devī = queen Kaikeyi; na atyavartatahi = did not transgress; tasya tām veLām = such of his time; rājā = the king; na pravivesha cha = did not enter also; shūnyam = empty; gṛiham = house; kadāchana = at any time.

In former times, Kaikeyi did not keep away herself at that kind of hour, nor the king enter her chambers at any time without her presence.

Sloka 19

tato gṛhagato rājā kaikeyīṃ paryapṛcchata || 2-10-19
yathāpuramavijñāya svārthalipsumapaṇḍitām |

19. tataḥ = thereafter; rājā = the king; gṛihagataḥ = who entered the house; paryapṛichchata = enquired; yathā puram = as before; avijJṇāyakaikeyīm apaṇDitām = about without kuching foolish Kaikeyi; swārtha lipsum = who desired to get her selfish ends.

After entering the house, king Dasaratha enquired with the aid-servants there as before, without knowing about foolish Kaikeyi who desired to get her selfish ends.

Sloka 20

pratīhārī tvathovāca saṃtrastā tu kṛtāñjaliḥ || 2-10-20
deva devī bhṛśaṃ kṛddhā krodhāgāramabhidrutā |

20. atta = then; santrastā = fearful; pratīhārī = female door keeper; kṛitāJṇjaliḥ = folding hands in supplication; uvācha = said; deva = "Oh; king!; devī = the queen; abhidrutā = ran; krodhāgāram = to chamber of wrath; bṛisham kruddhā = after becoming too much angry".

Then, a female door-keeper fearfully folding her hands in supplication, said: "Oh, king! The queen ran to her chamber of wrath with much anger".

Sloka 21

pratīhāryā vacaḥ śrutvā rājā paramadurmanāḥ || 2-10-21
viṣasāda punarbhuyo lulitavyākulendhriyaḥ |

21. rāja = the king; paramadurmanāḥ = having much disturbed mind; lulitavyākulendriyaḥ = with wavering and agitated senses; punaḥ = again; vishhasāda = was dejected; bhūyaḥ = more; shrutvā = after hearing; pratīhāryāḥ = door keeper's; vachaḥ = words.

That Dasaratha having much disturbed mind already for the absence of Kaikeyi there, became more dejected after hearing the words of door-keeper.

Sloka 22

tatratāṃ patitāṃ bhūmau śayānāmatathocitām || 2-10-22
pratapta iva duḥkhena so.apaśyajjagatīpatiḥ |

22. jagatīpatiḥ = Lord of the earth; saḥ = that Dasaratha; apashyat = saw; tām = her; patitām = lying down; bhūmau = on floor; tatra = there; shayānām = sleeping; atathochitām = in such and improper condition; praptaḥ eva = like burning; duḥkhena = with grief.

King Dasaratha, after seeing Kaikeyi lying down on floor in the chamber of wrath, became tormented with grief.

Sloka 23

sa vṛddhastaruṇīṃ bhāryāṃ prāṇebhyo.api garīyasīm || 2-10-23
apāpaḥ pāpasaṅkalpāṃ dadarśa dharaṇītale |
latāmiva viniṣkṛttāṃ patitāṃ deva tāmiva || 2-10-24
kinnarīmiva nirdhūtāṃ cyutāmapsarasaṃ yathā |
māyāmiva paribhraṣṭāṃ hariṇīmiva saṃyatām || 2-10-25

23;24;25. vṛiddhaḥ = the aged; apāpaḥ = sinless; saḥ = Dasaratha; dadarsha = saw; bhāryām = wife; taruṇīm = a youthful woman; garīyasīm = greater; prāṇebhyao api = than life; pāpasankalpām = with a desire to do sin; latāmiva = like creeper; vinishhkṛittām = completely uprooted; devatāmiva = like angel; patitām = fallen; kinnarīmiva = like Kinnara woman; nirdhūtām = thrown down; apsarasam yathā = like Apsarasa; chytām = expelled; mayāmiva = like illusionism; paribhrashhṭām = dropped off; hariṇīmiva = like female deer; samyatām = tied up.

That Dasaratha, who was sinless, a youthful woman dearer than his life, lying down on the floor with an intent to do sin. At that time, she was looking like a completely uprooted creeper, like a fallen angel, like a thrown down Kinnara woman, like an expelled divine dancer Apsarasa, like a dropped off illusionism and like a female deer tied up in a net.

Sloka 26

kreṇumiva digdhena viddhāṃ mṛgayunā vane |
mahāgaja invāraṇye snehātparimamarśa tām || 2-10-26

26. mahāgajaḥ iva = like great elephant; araṇye = in a forest; kareṇumiva = a female elephant; viddhām = wounded; dighena = by poisoned arrow; vane = in the grove; parimarsha = (he) touched; tām = her; snehāt = affectionately.

That Dasaratha touched Kaikeyi affectionately, as a great elephant in a forest touches a female elephant which is wounded by poisoned arrow.

Sloka 27

parimṛśya ca pāṇibhyāmabhisaṃtrastacetanaḥ |
kāmī kamalapatrākṣīmuvāca vanitāmidam || 2-10-27

27. kāmī = possessed with infatuation; abhisantrasta chetanaḥ = having fearful mind; parimṛishya = touched; kamala patrākshhīm = lotus-eyed; vanitām = woman; pāṇibhyām = with hands; uvācha = spoke; idam = these words.

That Dasaratha, possessed with infatuation and having fearful mind, touched that lotus-eyed woman with his hands and spoke the following words.

Sloka 28

na te.ahamabhijānāmi krodhamātmani saṃśritam |
devi kenābhiśaptāsi kena vāsi vimānitā || 2-10-28
yadidaṃ mamam duḥkhāya śeśe klyāṇi pāṃsuṣu |

28. devī = "Oh queen; kalyāṇi = of auspicious qualities! aham = I; na abhijānāmi = do not know; te = your; krodham = anger; samshṛitam = in habited; ātmani = in me; kena = by whom; abhishaptā asi = you are cursed; kenavā = or by whom; vimānitā asi = you are insulted; idam mama duḥkhāyā = this has become cause for my pain; yat seshhe = that you are resting; pāmsushhu = on dirty floor."

"Oh queen, of auspicious qualities! I do not know that you are angry with me. Who has cursed or disrespected you? I am pained to see you resting in this way on a dirty floor."

Sloka 29

bhūmau śeṣe kimarthaṃ tvaṃ mayi kalyāṇacetasi |
bhūtopahatacitteva mama cittapramāthinī || 2-10-29

29. mayi kalyāna chetasi = when I have a good sense of mind; kimartham = why; tvam = you; mama chiḥa pramāthinī = hurt my mind; seshhe = by resting; bhūmau = on floor; bhūtopahata chitteva = like one who is possessed of a devil.

"when I am here to expend all good to you, why are you hurting my mind by lying down on the floor like the one who is possessed of a devil."

Sloka 30

santi me kuśalā vaidyāstvabhituṣṭāśca sarvaśaḥ |
sukhitāṃ tvāṃ kariṣyanti vyādhimācakṣva bhāmini || 2-10-30

30. bhāmini = Oh; Kaikeyi! me = my; vaidyāḥ = physicians; santi = are there; kushalāḥ = who are skilled; saravashaḥ abhishhṭutāḥ cha = and who are enlogised on all sides; karishhyanti = who can made; tvām = you; sukshitām = healthy; achakshhva = tell; vyādhim = the sickness.

"There are so many royal physicians who are skilled and famous on all sides. They can make you healthy. Tell me your sickness".

Sloka 31

kasya vā te priyaṃ kāryaṃ kena vā vipriyaṃ kṛtam |
kaḥ priyaṃ labhatāmadya ko vā sumahadapriyam || 2-10-31

31. kasyavā = to whom; priyam = favour; kāryam = ought to be done; te = by you? kena vā = by whom; vipriyam = offence; kṛitam = was done? katt = who; labhatām = has to get; priyam = favour; adya = now? kovā = who; sumahat = great; apriyam = offence?'

"To whom do you want a favour to be done? who has offended you? Tell me to whom I have to extend a favour now and to whom I have to do a great offensive act."

Sloka 32

mā rodīrmā ca kārṣistvaṃ devi saṃpariśoṣaṇam || 2-10-32
avadhyo vadhyatāṃ ko vā ko vā vadhyo vimucyatām |
daridraḥ ko bhavedāḍhyo dravyavānvāpyakiñcanaḥ || 2-10-33

32;33. devi = " oh; queen! tvam = you; mā rodīḥ = do not weep; mā cha kārshhīḥ = nor make ; samparishoshhaṇam = body dry up; kovā = who; avadhyaḥ = exempt from death; vadhyatām = is to be killed?; kovā = who; vadhyaḥ = worthy of killing; vimuchyatām = is to be released? kovā = who; daridraḥ = a poor person; bhavet = to become; ādhyaḥ = wealthy? dravyavānvāpi = or a rich man; akiṇ^ichanaḥ = destitute.

"Oh, Kaikeyi! Do not weep and get your body dried up. If you tell, I shall kill a person who is exempt from death or shall release a person who is worthy to be killed. I shall make a poor man prosperous or a rich man, destitute".

Sloka 34

ahaṃ caiva madīyāśca sarve tava vaśānugāḥ |
na te kiṃcidabhiprāyaṃ vyāhantumahamutsahe || 2-10-34

34. ahamchaiva = I and; sarvexha = all; madīyāḥ = my people; tava vashāmgāḥ = are submissive to you; aham = I; na utsahe = do not try; vyāhantum = to foil; kiṇ^chit = even a little of; te = your; abhiprāyam = intention.

"I and all my people are submissive to you. I do not wish to foil even a little of your intention".

Sloka 35

ātmano jīvitenāpi bruhi yanmanasecchasi |
balamātmani jānantī na māṃ śaṅkitumarhasi || 2-10-35
kariṣyāmi tava prītiṃ sukṛtenāpi te śape |

35. brūhi = tell; yat = that which; ichhasi = you desire; manasā = from mind; jīvitenāpi = even by life; na arhasi shaṇ^kitum = to suspect; mām = me; jānantī = as you knew; balam = your strength; ātmani = over me; shape = I keep my oath; sikṛitenāpi = even by righteousness; te = to you; karishhye = I shall do; tava = your; rītim = liking.

"Tell me that which is desired by your mind to be achieved even at the cost of my life. You need not suspect me, as you very well know your authority over me. I keep my oath on my righteousness that I shall certainly do whatever is liked by you".

Sloka 36

yāvadāvarta te cakraṃ tāvatī me vasundharā || 2-10-36
prācīnāḥ sindhusauvīrāḥ saurāṣṭrā dakṣiṇāpathāḥ |
vaṅgāṅgamagadhā matsyāḥ samṛddhāḥ kāśikosalāḥ || 2-10-37

36;37. me vasundharā = my earth; yāvat tāvati = is as long as; chakram = chariout-wheel; āvartate = revolves; prachīnāḥ = eastern countries; sindhu sauvīrāḥ = ṣindhu; ṣauviva countries; saurāshhtrāḥ = ṣaurastra; dakshhiṇāpathāḥ = sounthern countries; vaṇ^gāṇ^ga magadhāḥ = Vanga; Anga and ṃagadha countries; matsyāḥ = the country of ṃatsya; kāshi kausalāḥ = kasi and Kausala countries; samṛiddhāḥ = are all affluent.

ṃy jurisdiction over this earth stretches out as much to the extent as to the extent a chariot-wheel revolves. Eastern countries, Sindhu, Sauvīra and Saurastra countries, as well as countries in the south, Vanga, Anga, ṃagadha and ṃatsya countries, Kasi and Kausala countries are all full of riches.

Sloka 38

tatra jātaṃ bahudravyaṃ dhanadhānya majāvikam |
tato vṛṇīṣva kaikeyi yadyattvaṃ manasecchasi || 2-10-38

38. tatra = there; dhana dhānyam = gold and grain; ajāvikam = sheep and goats; bahu = various; dravyam = goods; jātam = are produced; kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi; yadyat = which; tataḥ = fo those; ichhasi = do you want; Vṛiṇīshhva = to acquire; manasā = from the mind.

In those countries, gold and grain, sheep and goats and all types of goods are produced. Oh, Kaikeyi! which of the goods do you want to acquire?

Sloka 39

kimāyāsena te bhīru uttiṣṭottiṣṭa śobhane |
tatvaṃ me brūhi kaikeyi yataste bhayamāgatam || 2-10-39
tatte vyapanayiṣyāmi nīharamiva rśmivān |

39. bīru = "oh; fearful one! kim = what use; āyāsena = by exertion; te = to you; shobhane = oh; beautiful woman! uttishhṭha; uttishhṭha = get up; get up! kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi! brūhi = tell; me = me; tat = that; yataḥ = from where; bhayam = fear; āgatam = came; te = to you; vyapena yisshhyāmi = I shall remove; te = your; tat = that source of fear; nīhāramiva = as mist; rashmivān = by sund.

"Oh, fearful one! why are you exerted like this? oh, beautiful lady? Get up get up! Oh, Kaikeyi1 Tell me the source of your fear. I shall remove that source as sun sets aside the mist."

Sloka 40

tathoktā sā samāsvastā vaktukāmā tadapriyam || 2-10-40
paripīḍayituṃ bhūyo bhartāramupacakrame |

40. samāshvastā = consoled by; tathā uktā = thus spoken words; sā = she; vaktukāmā = intending to; tat apriyam = that unkindly word; upachakrame = was begining; paripīdayitum = harass; bharatāram = husband; bhūyaḥ = further.

Consoled by hearing the words of Dasaratha, Kaikeyi intending to tell unkindly words like Rama's exile was beginning to harass her husband further, by her words.

- - -

|| ityārṣe srimadrāmāyaṇe addikāvye ayodhya kāṇde daśama sargaḥ ||

Thus completes tenth sarga of Ayodhya Kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
