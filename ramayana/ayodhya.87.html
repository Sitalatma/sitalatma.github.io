Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 87
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hearing Guha's words about Rama, Bharata fell unconscious. Bharata eases himself thereafter and asks Guha to report further details about Rama. Guha informs that eventhough he offered various kinds of flood to Rama, the latter refused it. He adds that Rama Seetha and Lakshmana observed fasting for that night. Guha also informs Bharata that Lakshmana laid a bed of grass for Rama as well as Seetha to sleep theron and Lakshmana stood guarding Rama during the night, along with Guha and his kinsmen.

Sloka 1

guhasya vacanam śrutvā bharato bhṛśam apriyam |
dhyānam jagāma tatra eva yatra tat śrutam apriyam || 2-87-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; bhṛisham the most; apriyam = disagreeable; vachanam = words; gahasya = of guha; bharataḥ = Bharata; jagāma = got; dhyānam = into contemplation; tatraiva = directing his mind on Rama; yatra = of whom; tam = those; apriyam = unpleasant words; shrutam = were heard.

Hearing those most disagreeable words from Guha, Bharata began to think deeply, directing his mind on Rama, of whom those unpleasant words were heard.

Sloka 2

sukumāro mahā sattvaḥ simha skandho mahā bhujaḥ |
puṇḍarīka viśāla akṣaḥ taruṇaḥ priya darśanaḥ || 2-87-2
pratyāśvasya muhūrtam tu kālam parama durmanāḥ |
papāta sahasā totrair hṛdi viddha iva dvipaḥ || 2-87-3

2;3. sukumāraḥ = that gentle; mahāsattvaḥ = extremely courageous; mahābhujaḥ = and the mighty armed; taruṇaḥ = young prince; simhaskandhaḥ = whose shoulders resembled a lion's; puṇ^Darīka vishālākshhaḥ = his large eyes opening like a lotus in flowering; priyadarshanaḥ = having pleasing appearance; muhūrtakālam tu = mometarily; pratyāshvasya = sighed; paramadurmanāḥ and in his extreme anguish; papāta = sank to the ground; sahasā = suddenly; dvipaḥ iva = like an elephant; atividdhaḥ = that has been wounded in the heart; totraiḥ = with goads.

That gentle, extremely courageous and the mighty armed young prince, whose shoulders resembled a lion's his large eyes opening like a flowering lotus and having pleasing appearance, momentarily sighed and in his extreme anguish, sank to the ground suddenly like an elephant that has been wounded in the heart with goads.

Sloka 4

bharatam murcchitam druṣṭvā vivarṇavadano guhaḥ |
babhūva vyathitastatra bhūmikampe yathā drumaḥ || 2-87-4

4. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; bharatam = Bharata; mūrchhitam = unconscious; guhaḥ = Guha; vivarṇavadanaḥ = growing pale; babhūva = became; vyathitaḥ = shaken; tatra = there; yathā drumaḥ = like a tree; bhūmikampe = during an earth-quake.

Seeing Bharata unconscious, Guha growing pale, shook like a tree during an earthquake.

Sloka 5

tad avastham tu bharatam śatrughno anantara sthitaḥ |
pariṣvajya ruroda uccair visamjnaḥ śoka karśitaḥ || 2-87-5

5. shatrughnaḥ = ṣhatrughna; anantara sthitaḥ = who stood near; pariṣvajya = taking in his arms; bharatam = Bharata; tadavastham = who was in that stae; visamjJṇaḥ = becoming non-pulsed; ruroda = cried; uchchaiḥ = loudly; shokakarshitaḥ = overcome with grief."

Shatrughna, who stood near, taking Bharata, who was in that unconscious state in his arms, becoming non-pulsed, cried loudly, overcome as he was with grief.

Sloka 6

tataḥ sarvāḥ samāpetur mātaro bharatasya tāḥ |
upavāsa kṛśā dīnā bhartṛ vyasana karśitāḥ || 2-87-6

6. tataḥ = then; sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ = those; bharatasya = Bharata's mātaraḥ = mothers; uparāshakṛishāḥ = emaciated through fasting; dinnāḥ = extremely wretched; bhartṛivyasena karshitāḥ = being unable to find consolation for the sad end of their lord; samāpetaḥ = rushed (towards Bharata).

Then, all the queens, emaciated through fasting, extremely miserable, being unable to find consolation for the sad end of their lord, rushed towards Bharata.

Sloka 7

tāḥ ca tam patitam bhūmau rudantyaḥ paryavārayan |
kausalyā tu anusṛtya enam durmanāḥ pariṣasvaje || 2-87-7

7. tāshcha = all of them; rudatyaḥ = sobbing paryavārayan = surrounded; tam = Bharata; patitam = who fell; bhūmau = on the ground; kausalyāḥ tu = Kausalya on her part; durmanāḥ = torn with grief; anusṛitya = ebnding over; enam = him; parishhasvaje = embraced him.

All of them, sobbing, surrounded Bharata who fell on the ground. Kausalya on her part, torn with grief and bending over him, embraced him.

Sloka 8

vatsalā svam yathā vatsam upagūhya tapasvinī |
paripapragccha bharatam rudantī śoka lālasā || 2-87-8

8. vatsalā = the affectionate Kausalya; shoka lālasā = agitated with grief; tapasvinī = and looking miserable; upagūhya = pressing him to her bossom; vatsam yathā = as to a calf; svam = by its own mother-cow; paripaprachchha = questioned; bharatam = Bharata ( in the following words)

That affectionate Kausalya, agitated as she was with grief and looking miserable, pressing him to her bosom as a cow its own calf, questioned Bharata (in the following words)

Sloka 9

putra vyādhir na te kaccit śarīram paribādhate |
adya rāja kulasya asya tvad adhīnam hi jīvitam || 2-87-9

9. putra = O; son!; kachchit vyādhi na paribādhate = I hope that no ailment afflicts; te sharīram = your body; adya = now; jīvitam = the life; asya = of this; rājaknlasya = royal race; tvadadhīnam hi = is indeed dependent on you.

"O, son! I hope that no ailment afflicts your body. Now, the life of this royal race is indeed dependent on you."

Sloka 10

tvām dṛṣṭvā putra jīvāmi rāme sabhrātṛke gate |
vṛtte daśarathe rājni nātha ekaḥ tvam adya naḥ || 2-87-10

10. putra = O; son!; rāme = Rama; sa bhrātṛike = along with his brother Lakshmana; gate = hoas gone (to the forest); rājJṇi = the king; dasharathe = Dasaratha; vṛiṣṭvā = looking on you; tvam = you; ekaḥ = alone; nāthaḥ = are the protector; naḥ = for us; adya = now.

"O, son! Rama along with Lakshman has gone to the forest. The king Dasaratha is dead. I survive, looking on you. Now, you alone are the protector for us."

Sloka 11

kaccin na lakṣmaṇe putra śrutam te kiṃcid apriyam |
putra vā hy ekaputrāyāḥ saha bhārye vanam gate || 2-87-11

11. putra = O; son!; kachchit = na shrutam = I hope it is not heard; te = by you; kimchit = any; apriyam = unpleasnt news; lakshmaṇe = about Lakshman; putre vā = or about Rama my son; eka putrāyāḥ = my only son; gate = who left; vanam = for the forest; sahabhārye = along with his wife.

"I hope you have not heard any unpleasant news about Lakshmana or about Rama my one and the only son who left for the forest along with his wife."

Sloka 12

sa muhūrtam samāśvasya rudann eva mahā yaśāḥ |
kausalyām parisāntvya idam guham vacanam abravīt || 2-87-12

12. saḥ = that Bharata; mahā yashāḥ = of great renown; samāshvasya = easing himself well; muhūrtam- within a moment; parisāntvya = consoled; kausalyām = kausalya; rudanneva = and while still weaping; abravīt = spoke; idam = these vachanam = words; guham = to Guha.

Bharata of great renown, easing himself well within a moment, consoled Kausalya and while still weeping, spoke to Guha (as follows)

Sloka 13

bhrātā me kva avasad rātrim kva sītā kva ca lakṣmaṇaḥ |
asvapat śayane kasmin kim bhuktvā guha śaṃsa me || 2-87-13

13. guha = O; Guha!; kva = where; me bhrātā = did my brother; avasat = halt; rātrau = in the night?; kva = where; sītā = did ṣeetha (half in the night)?; kva = where; lakshhmaṇaḥ cha = did Lakshmana (halt in the night)?; bhuktvā = eating; kim = what; kasmin = and on which; shayane = couch; asvapat = did he repose?; shamasa = tell; me = me.

"O, guha! Tell me where did Rama my brother as also Seetha and Lakshmana halt in the night? On what couch and eating what, did he repose?

Sloka 14

so abravīd bharatam pṛṣṭo niṣāda adhipatir guhaḥ |
yad vidham pratipede ca rāme priya hite atithau || 2-87-14

14. saḥ guhaḥ = that Guha; nishhādādhipatiḥ = the Lord of ṇishadas; hṛiṣṭaḥ = thrilling with rapture; abravīt = spoke; bharatam = to Bharata; yadvidham = about the manner in which; pratipade = he arranged for (feeding and reposing) priya hite = his beloved and kind; atithau = guest; rāme = Rama.

Guha, the Lord of Nishadas, thrilling with rapture, spoke to Bharata about the manner in which he made arrangements for (feeding and reposing) his beloved and kind guest, Rama.

Sloka 15

annam ucca avacam bhakṣyāḥ phalāni vividhāni ca |
rāmāya abhyavahāra artham bahu ca upahṛtam mayā || 2-87-15

15. annam = food; uchchāvacham = of various kinds; bhakshhāḥ = drinks; vividhāni = and different variets; phalāni cha = of fruits; upahṛitam = were brought; bahu = repeatedly; mayā = by me; rāmāya = to the presence of Rama; abhyavahārārtham = for the purpose of his consumption.

"Various kinds of food and drink and different varieties of fruits were brought repeatedly by me to the presence of Rama for the purpose of his consumption."

Sloka 16

tat sarvam pratyanujnāsīd rāmaḥ satya parākramaḥ |
na hi tat pratyagṛhṇāt sa kṣatra dharmam anusmaran || 2-87-16

16. rāmaḥ = Rama; satya parākramaḥ = the truely courageous man; pratyannjJṇāsīt = refused; tatsarvam = all of them; anusmaran = and remembering; kṣatradharmam = his warrior's duty; saḥ = he; na tu pratyagṛihṇāt = could not accept; tat = it.

Rama, the truly courageous man, refused all of them. Reminding himself of his warrior's statute, he could not accept it.

Sloka 17

na hy asmābhiḥ pratigrāhyam sakhe deyam tu sarvadā |
iti tena vayam rājann anunītā mahātmanā || 2-87-17

17. rājan = O; Bharata!; vayam = we; anunītāḥ = were pacified; tena = by that; mahātmanā = high-souled Rama; iti = saying thus; 'sakhe = O; friend!; na pratigrāhyam = nothing should be received; deyam = but be given; saradā = always; asmābhiḥ = by us.

"O, Bharata! We were pacified by that high-souled Rama by saying thus: ' O, friend! We are not allowed to receive anything we must always be givers."

Sloka 18

lakṣmaṇena samānītam pītvā vāri mahā yaśāḥ |
aupavāsyam tadā akārṣīd rāghavaḥ saha sītayā || 2-87-18

18. rāghavaḥ = Rama; mahāyashaḥ = of great glory; tadā = then; sītayāsaha = along with ṣeetha; pītvā = drank; vāri = the water; samānītam = brought; lakshmaṇena = by Lakshmana; akārshhit = and pulled himself; oupavāsyam = to fasting.

"Rama, of great glory, then along with Seetha drank the water brought by Lakshman as observed fasting."

Sloka 19

tataḥ tu jala śeṣeṇa lakṣmaṇo apy akarot tadā |
vāg yatāḥ te trayaḥ saṃdhyām upāsata samāhitāḥ || 2-87-19

19. tataḥ = thereafter; tadā = at that time; lakṣmaṇo.api = Lakshmana also; akarot = drank; jalasheṣeṇa = the remainder of water; te trayaḥ = and those three; samupāsata = practised; vāgyatāḥ = silence; samhitā = together; sandhyām = in that evening-twillight

"Thereafter Lakshmana also drank the left-over water and those three practised silence together in that evening-twilight.

Sloka 20

saumitriḥ tu tataḥ paścād akarot svāstaram śubham |
svayam ānīya barhīmṣi kṣipram rāghava kāraṇāt || 2-87-20

20. tataḥ pashchāt = thereafter; saumitristu = Lakshmana; svayam = personally; ānīya = brought; barhīmshi = a layer of Kusa grass; akarot = and arranged; shubham = a suitably; svāstaram = good bed; rāghava; kāraṇāt = for Rama; kṣipram = immediately.

"Lakshmana then personally brought a layer of Kusa grass and arranged a fittingly nice bed with it for Rama immediately."

Sloka 21

tasmin samāviśad rāmaḥ svāstare saha sītayā |
prakṣālya ca tayoḥ pādāu apacakrāma lakṣmaṇaḥ || 2-87-21

21. rāmaḥ = Rama; sītayā saha = along with ṣeetha; samāvishat = settled dwon on; tasmin = that; svāstare = bed; prakṣālaya = after washing; tayoḥ = their; pādau = feet; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; apachakrāma = wnet away to a distance.

"Rama, along with Seetha, settled down on that bed. After washing their feet, Lakshmana went away to a distance."

Sloka 22

etat tad ingudī mūlam idam eva ca tat tṛṇam |
yasmin rāmaḥ ca sītā ca rātrim tām śayitāu ubhau || 2-87-22

22. etat = here; tat = at that; ingudī mūlam = foot of Ingudi ṭree; idameva = on just this; tṛiṇamcha = grass; yasmin = on which; ubhau = both; rāmashcha = Rama and; sītā = ṣeetha; shayitau = slept; tām rātrim = that night.

"Here at this fort of Ingndi Tree, on just this grass, on which both Rama and Seetha slept that night."

Sloka 23

niyamya pṛṣṭhe tu tala angulitravān |
śaraiḥ supūrṇāu iṣudhī param tapaḥ |
mahad dhanuḥ sajyam upohya lakṣmaṇo |
niśām atiṣṭhat parito asya kevalam || 2-87-23

23. lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshman; paramtapaḥ = who torments his enemy; talāngulītravān = wearing a glove of Goha skin; niyamya = strapping; pṛiṣṭhe = on his back; iṣudhī = quivers; supūrṇau = full of; sharaiḥ = arrows; upohya = and wielding; mahat = a great; dhanuḥ = bow; sajyam = with string; atiṣṭhat kevalam = remained walking alone; paritaḥ = round; asya = Rama; nishām = during the night.

"Lakshmana, the scourge of his foe, wearing a glove of goha skin, strapping two quivers full of arrows on either side of his back and wielding a great stringed bow, remained on his part walking round Rama during that night."

Sloka 24

tataḥ tu aham ca uttama bāṇa cāpadhṛk |
sthito abhavam tatra sa yatra lakṣmaṇaḥ |
atandribhir jnātibhir ātta kārmukair |
mahā indra kalpam paripālayamḥ tadā || 2-87-24

24. tataḥ = thereafter; ahamtu = I on my part; tadā = at that time; uttama bāṇa chāpa dhṛit = wearing excellent arrows and a bow; jṇ^ātibhiḥ = along with my kinsment; atandribhiḥ = who were free from lassitude; ātta kārmukaiḥ = and carrying bows; abhavam = becoming; sthitaḥ = remained; tatra = there; yatra = where; lakṣmanaḥ = Lakshama; paripālayan = was guarding mahendra kalpam = Rama; who vied with Indra.

"Wearing excellent arrows and a bow along with my vigilant kinsmen carrying their bows, I on my part remained near Lakshman, who was guarding Rama who vied with Indra the Lord of celestials."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe saptāśītitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 87th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
