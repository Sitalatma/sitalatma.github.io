Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 70
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The messengers entered the city of Rajagriha and meet the king Kekeya and prince Yudhajit. They request Bharata to start for Ayodhya urgently. Bharata seeks permission from king Kekeya his maternal uncle. King Kekeya gives permission to Bharata to leave for Ayodhya and bestows him various gifts. Bharata and Satrughna along with men. elephants and horses start his journey for Ayodhya.

Sloka 1

bharate bruvati svapnam dūtāḥ te klānta vāhanāḥ |
praviśya asahya parikham ramyam rāja gṛham puram || 2-70-1
samāgamya tu rājñā ca rāja putreṇa ca arcitāḥ |
rājñaḥ pādau gṛhītvā tu tam ūcur bharatam vacaḥ || 2-70-2

1; 2. bharate = (while)Bharata; bruvati = was telling; svapnam = about the dream; te dūtaḥ = those messengers; klāntavāhanāḥ = whose horses were tired; pravishya = entered; ramyam = beautiful; puram = city; rājagṛiham = called Rajagriha; asahyaparigham = which has too big a palace-gate; samāgamya = met; rājJṇācha = the king Kekeya; rājaputreṇa = and the prince Yudhajit; architāḥ = were received by them hospitably; gṛihītvā = grasped(in salutation); pādou = the feet; rāṇ^gaḥ = of the king Kekeya; ūchuḥ = and spoke; tam bharatam = to that Bharata; vachaḥ = (the following) words:-

While Bharata was narrating his dream, the messengers whose horses were tired, entered the beautiful city called Rajagriha, which has too big a palace-gate and met the king Kekeya and the prince Yudhajit, who received them hospitably. They saluted the feet of the king Kekeya and spoke the following words to Bharata:

Sloka 3

purohitaḥ tvā kuśalam prāha sarve ca mantriṇaḥ |
tvaramāṇaḥ ca niryāhi kṛtyam ātyayikam tvayā || 2-70-3

3. purohitaḥ = the family priest; prāha = was asking; tvām = your; kushalam = welfare; sarve = All; mantriṇashcha = the ministers also; (were asking about your welfare); niryāhi = come forth indeed; tvaramāṇaḥcha = quickly. atyayikam = ṭhere is an urgent; kṛityam = work; tvayā = with you."

"Vasistha, the family-priest and all the ministers were asking about your welfare. Come forth indeed quickly. There is an urgent work with you."

Sloka 4

imāni ca mahārhāṇi vastrābharaṇāni ca |
pratigṛhya viśālākṣa mātulasya ca dāpaya || 2-70-4

4. vishālākshha! O; Bharata the wide-eyed man! Pratigṛihya = ṭake; imāni = these; mahārhāṇi = very valuable; vastrāṇi = clothes; ābharaṇānicha = and jewels; dāpaya = and arrange to give; mātulasya = to your maternal uncle."

"O, Bharata the wide-eyed man! Take these very valuable clothes and jewels and give them to your maternal uncle"

Sloka 5

atra viṃśati koṭyaḥ tu nṛpater mātulasya te |
daśa koṭyaḥ tu sampūrṇāḥ tathaiva ca nṛpa ātmaja || 2-70-5

5. nṛipātmaja = O; prince! Atra = in these; vimshatikoṭyaḥ = a hundred crores; nṛipataḥ = are to be given to the king Kekeya; tathaivacha = and like wise; sampūrṇāḥ = a full; dashakoṭyastu = ten crores ; te = your; mātulasya = maternal uncle."

"O, prince! In these jewels and clothes, a worth of hundred crores is to be given to the king Kekeya and likewise a worth of ten crores in full to your maternal uncle."

Sloka 6

pratigṛhya ca tat sarvam svanuraktaḥ suhṛj jane |
dūtān uvāca bharataḥ kāmaiḥ sampratipūjya tān || 2-70-6

6. bharataḥ = Bharata; svanuraktaḥ = who had a great affection; suhṛijjane = towards his kind hearted relatives; pratigṛihya = took; tat sarvam = all that; tān dūtān samprati pūjya = honored those messengers greatly; kāmaiḥ = with gifts of their liking; uvācha = and spoke (as follows):

Bharata, who had a great affection towards his kind hearted relatives, took all that, honored these messengers profusely with gifts of their liking and spoke(as follows) :

Sloka 7

kaccit sukuśalī rājā pitā daśaratho mama |
kaccic ca arāgatā rāme lakṣmaṇe vā mahātmani || 2-70-7

7. kachchit = "I hope that; mama pitā = my father; rājā dasharathaḥ = king Dasaratha; sukushalī = is quite safe; ārogyotā = there is health; rāme = in Rama; mahātmani = and in the high soled; lakshhmaṇecha = Lakshmana."

"I hope that king Dasaratha, my father is quite safe. I also hope that Rama and the high-soled Lakshmana are well."

Sloka 8

āryā ca dharma niratā dharmajñā dharma darśinī |
arogā ca api kausalyā mātā rāmasya dhīmataḥ || 2-70-8

8. kausalya = (Is) Kausalya; āryācha = the venerable lady; dharmaniratā = who is intent on piety; dharmaṇ^ga = who knows righteousness; dharmadarshinī = who visualizes righteousness; mātā = and mother; rāmasya = of Rama; dhīmataḥ = the wise; api arogā = also healthy?"

"Is Kausalya, the venerable lady, who is intent on virtue, who knows righteousness, who visualizes goodness and who is the mother of the wise Rama, also well? "

Sloka 9

kaccit sumitrā dharmajñā jananī lakṣmaṇasya yā |
śatrughnasya ca vīrasya sārogā ca api madhyamā || 2-70-9

9. kachchit = I hope that; sā sumitrāpi = that ṣumitra; yā = who; dharmaG^yā = knows righteousness; jananī = who is the mother; lakshhmaṇasya = of Lakshmana; shatrughnasyacha = and of ṣatrughna; vīrasya = the valiant; madhyamā = and our middle mother; arogā = is healthy."

"I hope that Sumitra, who knows righteousness, who is the mother of Lakshmana as well as the heroic Satrughna and our middle mother, is well."

Sloka 10

ātma kāmā sadā caṇḍī krodhanā prājña māninī |
arogā ca api kaikeyī mātā me kim uvāca ha || 2-70-10

10. kaikeyi = (Is) Kaikeyi; ātmakāmā = who loves herself; sadā = forever; chaṇDī = fiery lady; krodhanā = of wrathful nature; prāG^yamāninī = priding herself to be highly intelligent; me mātā = and my mother; api arogā = also healthy? kim = What; uvāchaha = did she say?"

"As Kaikeyi, who loves herself, a forever fiery lady of wrathful nature, who prides herself to be highly intelligent and my mother, also well? What did she say?"

Sloka 11

evam uktāḥ tu te dūtā bharatena mahātmanā |
ūcuḥ sampraśritam vākyam idam tam bharatam tadā || 2-70-11

11. uktvāḥ = having been spoken; evam = thus; bharatena = by Bharata; mahātmanā = the high soled; te dūtāḥ = those messengers; tadā = then; ūchuḥ = spoke; idam vachaḥ = these words; saprashrayam = respectfully; tam bharatam = to that Bharata:

On hearing the words of the high soled Bharata, those messengers then respectfully spoke to Bharata as follows:

Sloka 12

kuśalāḥ te nara vyāghra yeṣām kuśalam iccasi |
śrīśca tvām vṛṇute padmā yujyatām cāpi te rakaḥ || 2-70-12

12. "naravyāghra = O; tiger among men! Yeshhām = whose; kushalam = welfare; ichchhasi = do you desire; te = they; kushalāh = are safe; shrīḥ = the goddess of prosperity; padmā = holding a lotus in her hand; vṛiṇute = awaits; tvām = you; te = Let your; rathaḥ = chariot; yujyatāmchāpi = be made ready."

"O, tiger among men! Those, of whom you do enquire, are all well. The goddess of prosperity, holding a lotus in her hand, awaits you. Let your chariot be made ready."

Sloka 13

bharataḥ ca api tān dūtān evam uktaḥ abhyabhāṣata |
āpṛcce aham mahā rājam dūtāḥ samtvarayanti mām || 2-70-13

13. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; (by the messengers); bharatashchāpi = Bharata also; abhyabhāshhata = spoke; tān dūtān = to those messengers(as follows); "aham = I; apṛichchhe = will get permission; mahārājam = from the monarch; (saying); "dūtāh = the messengers; samtvarayanti = are hastening; mām = me."

Hearing the words Bharata spoke to the messengers as follows: "I will get permission from the monarch(to leave for Ayodhya) saying that the messengers are hastening me up."

Sloka 14

evam uktvā tu tān dūtān bharataḥ pārthiva ātmajaḥ |
dūtaiḥ saṃcoditaḥ vākyam mātāmaham uvāca ha || 2-70-14

14. bharataḥ = Bharata; pārthivātmajaḥ = the prince; samchoditah = being asked to move quickly; dūtaiḥ = by the messengers; evam = thus; uktvā = spoke; tān dūtān = to the messengers; uvāchaha = and uttered; vākyam = these words; mātāmaham = to his maternal uncle:

Bharata the prince having been asked to move quickly (to Ayodhya) by the messengers, spoke as aforesaid to those messengers and uttered the following words to his maternal uncle:

Sloka 15

rājan pitur gamiṣyāmi sakāśam dūta coditaḥ |
punar api aham eṣyāmi yadā me tvam smariṣyasi || 2-70-15

15. rājan = O; king! dūtachoditāh = asked by the messengers to move quickly; gamishhyāmi = I am going; pituḥ = to my father's; sakāsham = presence. eshhyāmi = I shall come; punarapi = again; yadā = whenever; tvam = you; smarishhyasi = recall; me = me."

"O, king! Requested by the messengers to come to Ayodhya urgently, I am going to my father's presence. I shall again come, whenever you recall me."

Sloka 16

bharatena evam uktaḥ tu nṛpo mātāmahaḥ tadā |
tam uvāca śubham vākyam śirasy āghrāya rāghavam || 2-70-16

16. tadā = then; nṛipaḥ = the king Kekeya; mātā mahaḥ = the maternal uncle; uktaḥ = having been spoken; bharatena = by Bharata; evam = as aforesaid; āghrāya = smelling; shirasi = the head; tām rāghavam = of that Bharata; uvācha = spoke; shubham = these auspicious ; vākyām = words:

Hearing the words of Bharata, the king Kekeya, his maternal uncle smelled (as a token of affection) the head of Bharata and spoke the following auspicious words:

Sloka 17

gacca tāta anujāne tvām kaikeyī suprajāḥ tvayā |
mātaram kuśalam brūyāḥ pitaram ca param tapa || 2-70-17

17. gachchha = go; tāta = my dear child! anujāne = I am permitting; tvām = you; tvayā = by you; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; suprajāḥ = has a good son; paramtapa = O; the scourge of foes! Brūyāḥ = Ask; kushalam = the welfare; mātaram = of your mother; pitaracha = and father."

"Go, my dear child! I am permitting you. Kaikeyi is fortunate to have a good son like you. Bear my greetings to your mother and father."

Sloka 18

purohitam ca kuśalam ye ca anye dvija sattamāḥ |
tau ca tāta mahā iṣvāsau bhrātaru rāma lakṣmaṇau || 2-70-18

18. tāta = my dear child! Brūhi = Ask for; kushalam = welfare; purohitam = of the family priest; ye = which; anye = other; dvijasattamāḥ = foremost of Brahmins; maheshhvāsou = wielders of great bows; tou = those; bhrātarou = brothers; rāma lakshmaṇou = Rama and Lakshmana."

" ṃy dear child! Pay obeisance to your family priest, the other illustrious Brahmins and your brothers Rama and Lakshmana the wielders of bows."

Sloka 19

tasmai hasti uttamāmḥ citrān kambalān ajināni ca |
abhisatkṛtya kaikeyo bharatāya dhanam dadau || 2-70-19

19. kaikeyaḥ = the king Kekeya; abhisatkṛitya = treating him hospitably; dadou = gave; tasmai = that; bharatāya = Bharata; hastyuttamān = the best of elephants; chitrān = variously colored; kambaLān = woolen clothes; ajināni = antelope skins; dhanam = and riches(gold;silver vessels etc)

The king Kekaya, treating him hospitably gave that Bharata the best of elephants, woolen clothes of varied colors, antelope skins and riches (gold and silver vessels etc).

Sloka 20

rukma niṣka sahasre dve ṣoḍaśa aśva śatāni ca |
satkṛtya kaikeyī putram kekayo dhanam ādiśat || 2-70-20

20. kekeyaḥ = the king Kekeya; satkṛitya = in honor of him; ādishat = gave; dhanam = as gifts; dve = two; rukma nishhkasahasre = thousand coins of gold; shhoDasha = sixteen; ashvashatānicha = hundred horses.

The king Kekeya generously gave as gifts to Bharata, two thousands coins of gold and sixteen hundred horses.

Sloka 21

tathā amātyān abhipretān viśvāsyāmḥ ca guṇa anvitān |
dadāv aśva patiḥ śīghram bharatāya anuyāyinaḥ || 2-70-21

21. tathā = likewise; ashvapatiḥ = aswapati; dadou = gave; anuyāyinaḥ = some attendants; abhipretān = who were dear; vishvāsyān = reliable; guṇānvitān = endowed with virtues; amātyān = and inmates of his palace; bharatāya = to Bharata.

Likewise, Aswapati sent some attendants, who were dear, reliable, endowed with virtues and inmates of his palace with Bharata (to accompany him on the return journey)

Sloka 22

airāvatān aindra śirān nāgān vai priya darśanān |
kharān śīghrān susamyuktān mātulo asmai dhanam dadau || 2-70-22

22. mātulaḥ = his maternal uncle; dadou = gave; dhanam = as presents; nāgān = elephants; airāvatān = of Airavata race; aindrashirān = born in Indrasira mountain range; priya darshanān = and which were charming to behold; kharān = and mules; shīghrān = which could walk quickly; susamyuktān = and were well trained.

Yuddhajit, his maternal uncle gave him as gifts, elephants of Airavata race born in Indrasira mountain-range and which were charming to behold as well as mules which could walk quickly and were well trained

Sloka 23

antaḥ pure atisamvṛddhān vyāghra vīrya bala anvitān |
damṣṭra āyudhān mahā kāyān śunaḥ ca upāyanam dadau || 2-70-23

23. dadou = (he) gave; upāyanam = as a gift; shunashcha = dogs also; ati samvṛiddhān = which were well nourished; antaḥpura = in the gynaecium; vyāghre vīrya balānvitān = which had vitality and strength equal to tigers; mahākāyān = which were large bodied; damshhṭrā yudhān = and which used tusks as weapons.

He gave as a gift, large bodied dogs, which were well nourished in the gynoecium, possessing strength and vitality of a tiger and which were using their tusks as weapons.

Sloka 24

sa mātāmaham āpṛccya mātulam ca yudhā jitam |
ratham āruhya bharataḥ śatrughna sahitaḥ yayau || 2-70-24

24. dhanam = the wealth of gifts; dattam = bestowed by; kekayendra = the king Kekeya; nābhyanandata = did not rejoice; saḥ = that; bharataḥ = Kaikeyi's son; gamanatvarayā = who was in a hurry to go; tadā = then.

The wealth of gifts bestowed by the king Kekeya did not rejoice Bharata, Kaikeyi's son, who was then in a hurry to go.

Sloka 25

babhūva hyasya hṛdate cintā sumahatī tadā |
tvarayā cāpi dūtānām svapnasyāpi ca darśanāt || 2-70-25

25. tvarayāchāpi = due to goading; dūtānām = of messengers; darshanāchchāpi = and visualization; svapnasya = of the dream; sumahatī = a very big; chintā = worry; babhūva hi = was formed; asya hṛidaye = in his heart; tadā = then.

Due to goading of messengers and visualization of the dream, a very big worry was formed at that time in his heart.

Sloka 26

sa svaveśmābhyatikramya naranāgaśvasamvṛtam |
prapede sumahacchrīmān rājamārgamanuttamam || 2-70-26

26. shrīmān = the glorious; saḥ = Bharata; abhyatikramya = crossed; svaveshma = his palace; sumahat = which was very big; nara nāgāshva savṛitam = augmented with men; elephants and horses; prapede = and entered; anuttamam = the excellent; rājamārgam = royal high way.

The glorious Bharata crossed his very big palace, augmented with men elephants and horses and entered the excellent royal high way.

Sloka 27

abhyatītya tato.apaśyadantaḥ puramudāradhīḥ |
tatastadbharataḥ śrīmānāviveśānivāritaḥ || 2-70-27

27. udāradhīh = the sagacious Bharata; abhyatītya = crossed the royal highway; apashyat = as saw; antaḥpuram = the gynaecia; tatah = thereafter; anivaritaḥ = unhindered; shrīmān = the glorious; bharataḥ = Bharata; āvivesha = entered; tat = that gynoecia.

The sagacious Bharata crossed the royal high way and perceived the gynoecia. Then, the glorious Bharata entered those gynoecia.

Sloka 28

sa mātā mahamāpṛccya mātulam ca yudhājitam |
rathamāruhya bharataḥ śatrughnasahito yayau || 2-70-28

28. saḥ bharataḥ = that Bharata; āpṛichchhya = took leave of; mātāmaham = his maternal grand father; mātulam = and his maternal uncle; yudhājit = Yuddhajit; yayon = and set out; āruhya = mounted; ratham = on chariot; ṣatrughna sahitah = along with ṣatrughna.

Bharata took leave of his maternal grandfather and also Yudhajit, his maternal uncle and set out on a chariot (for Ayodhya) along with Satrughna.

Sloka 29

rathān maṇḍala cakrāmḥ ca yojayitvā paraḥ śatam |
uṣṭra go aśva kharaiḥ bhṛtyā bharatam yāntam anvayuḥ || 2-70-29

29. paraḥ shatān = with more than hundred; rathān** = chariots; ratna vichitrāmshcha = variegated with jewels; yojayitvā = yoked to; ushhṭra goshva balaih = camels; bullocks; horses; and mules; bhṛityāḥ = servants; anvayuḥ = followed; bharatam = Bharata; yāntam = as he departed.

With more than hundred chariots variegated with jewels yoked to camels, bullocks, horses and mules as also servants followed Bharata as he departed.

Instead of "rathān maṇdalachakrāmshcha", "rathān ratnavichitrāmshcha" in the old version is taken -as the clause "chariots with round wheels " appears to be meaningless.

Sloka 30

balena guptaḥ bharataḥ mahātmā |
saha āryakasya ātma samaiḥ amātyaiḥ |
ādāya śatrughnam apeta śatrur |
gṛhāt yayau siddhaiva indra lokāt || 2-70-30

30. guptaḥ = being protected; balena = by the army; mahātmā = the high soled; bharatah = Bharata; apeta shatruḥ = who was free from enemies; ādāya = and taking; shatrughnam = shatrughna; amātyaiḥ saha = along with companies; ātmasamaih = good enough to him; yayon = started; gṛihāt = from the palace; āryakasya = of his venerable maternal uncle; indralokādiva = as from the world of celestials; siddhah = a ṣiddha(a semi divine being of great purity and perfection and said to possess supernatural faculties).

Being protected by the army, the high soled Bharata, who was free from enemies, taking Shatrughna along with other companions good enough to him, started from the palace of his venerable maternal uncle, as a Siddha(a semi- divine being of great purity and perfection and said to possess supernatural faculties) would start from Indraloka( the world of celestials).

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe saptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 70th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
