Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 33
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Stepping down from the tree, Hanuma approached Seetha and asked her to clarify him whether she is the wife of Rama. Seetha then starts to narrate her antecedents and the story of Rama along with circumstances under which she along with Rama and Lakshmana went to Dandaka forest as per the command of King Dasaratha. She also informs Hanuma about her snatching away of Ravana from the forest.


Sloka 1

so.avatīrya drumāttasmādvidrumapratimānanaḥ |
vinītaveṣaḥ kṛpaṇaḥ praṇipatyopasṛtya ca || 5-33-1
tām abravīn mahātejā hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
śirasi añjalim ādhāya sītām madhurayā girā || 5-33-2

1; 2. saḥ hanumān = that ḥanuma; vidruma prati māna naḥ = with coral-coloured countenance; mahā tejā = very bright; mārutātmajaḥ = and the so of wind-god; vinīta veṣaḥ = with a humble look; kṛipaṇaḥ = and feeble; upasṛitya = approached; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; praṇipatya = offered his salutation; ādhāya = by keeping; aṇ^jalim = his joined palms; shirasi = on his head; abravīt = and spoke; (the following); madhurayā = sweet; girā = words.

ṭhat ḥanuma, with coral-coloured face, very bright, the son of wind-god, wearing a humble and feeble look, approached Seetha by stepping down from the tree and offered her his salutation by keeping his joined palms on his head and spoke the following sweet words:

Sloka 3

kā nu padma palāśa akṣī kliṣṭa kauśeya vāsinī |
drumasya śākhām ālambya tiṣṭhasi tvam aninditā || 5-33-3

3. padma palāshakṣi = O Lady with your eyes resembling lotus leaves!; kliṣṭa kaushaya vāsini = wearing worn-out sila cloth; anindita = O the irreproachable one! tiṣṭhasi = you have stood ālambya = taking by the hand; shākhām = a branch; drumasya = of the tree; kānu = who are you?

"O Lady with your eyes resembling lotus leaves, wearing a worn out silken cloth! O, the irreproachable one! You have stood, holding by the hand a branch of the tree who are you?"

Sloka 4

kim artham tava netrābhyām vāri sravati śokajam |
puṇḍarīka palāśābhyām viprakīrṇam iva udakam || 5-33-4

4. vāri shokajam = tears of sorrow; sravati = are gushing down; tava = from you; netrābhyām = eyes; udakam = as water; viprakirṇam = scattered down; puṇDarīka palāshābhyām = from two lotus petals; kimartham = why?

"ṭears of sorrow are gushing down from your eyes, as water is scattered down from two lotus-petals. Why?"

Sloka 5

surāṇām asurāṇām ca nāga gandharva rakṣasām |
yakṣāṇām kimnarāṇām ca kā tvam bhavasi śobhane || 5-33-5

5. shobhane = O auspicious lady!; kā = In whom; tvam = are you; bhavasi = born; surāṇām = whether in celestials; asurāṇām vā = or in ogres; nāga gandharvarakṣasām = or in serpent-demons or in celestial musicians or in demons; yakṣāṇām = or in semi-divine being; kimnarāṇām = or in mythical beings.

"O auspicious lady! In whom are you born-whether in celestials or in ogres or in serpent-demons or in celestial musicians or in demons or in semi-divine beings or in mythical beings?"

Sloka 6

kā tvam bhavasi rudrāṇām marutām vā vara ānane |
vasūnām vā vara ārohe devatā pratibhāsi me || 5-33-6

6. varānane = O Lovely faced woman!; kā = who; bhavasi = are ; tvam- you; rudrāṇām = among eleven Rudras; who; marutām vā = or among ṃaruts the storm-gods; vasūnām = or among eight Vasus; the beneficient gods?; varārohe = O handsome lady with fine hips!; me = to me; pratibhāsi = you appear; devatā = as a deity.

"O lovely-faced woman! Who are you among eleven Rudras who bestow strength, or among ṃaruts the storm-gods or among eight Vasus the beneficent gods? O Handsome lady with fine hips! You appear to me as a deity."

Sloka 7

kim nu candramasā hīnā patitā vibudha ālayāt |
rohiṇī jyotiṣām śreṣṭhā śreṣṭhā sarva guṇa anvitā || 5-33-7

7. rohiṇīkimvā = Are you Rohini; shreṣṭha sarva guṇānvitā = endowed with all excellent qualities; patitā = fallen; vibudhālayāt = from heaven; hīnā = left behind; chandramasā = by moon; shreṣṭhā = excellent; jyotiṣām = among stars.

"Are you Rohini, endowed with all the best qualities, fallen from heaven, left behind by moon and excellent among stars."

Sloka 8

kopāt vā yadi vā mohāt bhartāram asita īkṣaṇā || 5-33-8
vasiṣṭham kopayitvā tvam na asi kalyāṇi arundhatī |

8. asitekṣaṇe = O dark eyed lady!; tvam nāsi = are you not; kalyāṇī = the auspicious; arundhati = Arundhati; kopayitvā = who irritated; vasiṣṭham = the sage Vasishta; bhartāram = her husband; kopādvā yadivā = either by anger.

"O dark-eyed lady! Are you not the auspicious Arundhati, who irritated her husband, Vasishta the sage either by anger or by error?"

Sloka 9

ko nu putraḥ pitā bhrāta bhartā vā te sumadhyame || 5-33-9
asmāl lokāt amum lokam gatam tvam anuśocasi |

9. sumadhyame = O the slender waisted lady!; tvam = you; anushochasi = seem to be lamenting; gatam = (about someone) who has gone; asmāt = from thus; lokāt = world; amum lokam = to the other world; te = your; putraḥ son; pitā = father; bhrātā = brother; bhartā vā = or your husband?

"O the slender waisted lady! You seem to be lamenting over someone who died is he your son, or father or brother or husband."

Sloka 10

rodanādatiniḥśvāsādbhūmisaṃsparśanādapi || 5-33-10
na tvām devīmaham manye rājñaḥ saṃjñāvadhāraṇāt |

10. rodanāt = for you lamenting; atiniḥ shvāsāt = for you long sighs; bhūmi sam sparshanādapi = also for your touching of the earth; samjJṇāvadharaṇāt = for holding the signs; rājJṇaḥ = of a king; aham = I; na manye = do not think; tvām = you; devīm = as a divine woman.

"You are lamenting. You are sighing a lot. You are touching the earth. You have royal insignia in you. According to these things, I do not consider you as a divine woman."

Sloka 11

vyañjanāni hi te yāni lakṣaṇāni ca lakṣaye || 5-33-11
mahiṣī bhūmi pālasya rāja kanyā asi me matā |

11. yāni = which; vyaṇ^janāni = marks on your person; te lakṣaṇāmcha = and your form; lakṣaye = I see; (according to that); me = to me; matā = (your are) believed to be; mahiṣī = a queen; bhūmipālasya = of an emperor; rāja kanyācha = and a king's daughter.

"Based on the marks on your person and your bodily characteristics, I believe you to be a queen of an emperor and the daughter of a king."

Sloka 12

rāvaṇena jana sthānāt balāt apahṛtā yadi || 5-33-12
sītā tvam asi bhadram te tan mama ācakṣva pṛcchataḥ |

12. asi yadi tvam = If you; sītā = are ṣeetha; apahṛitā = stolen; balāt = by force; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; janasthānāt = from Janasthana; āchakṣya = tell; tat = it; mama = to me; pṛichchhataḥ = who is asking; bhadram te = if you please."

"If you are ṣeetha stolen per force by Ravana from Janasthana, tell it to me who is asking if you please!"

Sloka 13

yathā hi tava vaidainyam rūpam cāpyatimānuṣam || 5-33-13
tapasā cānvito veṣastvaṃ rāmamahiṣī dhruvam |

13. yathā = according to; tava = your; dainyam = miserable state; rūpam cha = the (beautiful) figure; atimānuṣam = which is super-human; veṣaḥ = your costume; anvitaḥ = endowed with; tapasā = austerity; dhruvam = surely; tvam = you are; rāma mahiṣī = Rama's wife."

"According to your miserable state, the super-human beautiful figure and your costume endowed with austerity, surely you are Rama's wife."

Sloka 14

sā tasya vacanam śrutvā rāma kīrtana harṣitā || 5-33-14
uvāca vākyam vaidehī hanūmantam druma āśritam |

14. shrutvā = hearing; tasya = his; vachanam = words; sā vaidehī = that ṣeetha; rāma kīrthana harṣitā = was delighted of Rama's mention; uvācha = and spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; hanumantam = to ḥanuma; drumāshritam = who was dwelling on a tree.

ḥearing his words, ṣeetha was delighted of Rama's mention and spoke the following words to ḥanuma, who was dwelling on a tree.

Sloka 15

pṛthivyāṃ rājasiṃhānāṃ mukhyasya viditātmanaḥ || 5-33-15
snuṣā daśarathasyāham śatrusainyapratāpinaḥ |

15. aham = I; snuṣā = am the daughter-in-law; dasharathasya = of Dasaratha; mukhyasya = the eminent; rāja simhānām = among the excellent kings; pṛithi vyām = on earth; viditātmanaḥ = who understands the nature of the spirit; shatrusainya pratāpinaḥ = and who torments the army of the adversary.

"I am the daughter-in-law of Dasaratha, eminent among the excellent kings on earth, who understands the nature of the spirit and who torments the army of the adversary."

Sloka 16

duhitā janakasya aham vaidehasya mahātmanaḥ || 5-33-16
sītā ca nāma nāmnā aham bhāryā rāmasya dhīmataḥ |

16. aham = I; duhitā = am the daughter; janakasya = of Janaka; vaidehasya = the king of Videha kingdom; mahātmanaḥ = having a noble nature; ṣeetha; nāmnā = by name; bhāryā = and wife; rāmasya = of Rama; dhīmataḥ = the wise man.

"I am the daughter of the noble-natured Janaka, the king of Videha kingdom. I am know.

Sloka 17

samā dvādaśa tatra aham rāghavasya niveśane || 5-33-17
bhunjānā mānuṣān bhogān sarva kāma samṛddhinī |

17. aham = I; (stayed); tatra = there; rāghavasya = in Rama's; niveshane = house; dvādasha = for twelve; samāḥ = years; bhuJṇānā = enjoying; bhogān = the worldly pleasures; mānuṣān = belonging to mankind; sarvakāma samṛiddhinī = and fulfilling all the desires.

"I stayed in Rama's house there for twelve years, enjoying the worldly pleasures belonging to human kind and fulfilling all my desires."

Sloka 18

tataḥ trayodaśe varṣe rājyena ikṣvāku nandanam || 5-33-18
abhiṣecayitum rājā sa upādhyāyaḥ pracakrame |

18. tataḥ = thereafter; trayodashe varṣe = in the thirteenth year; rājā = the king Dasaratha; sopādhyāyaḥ = along with his preceptors; prachakrame = started; abhiṣechayitum = to; anointment; rājyena = of; the kingdom; ikṣvākunandanam = to Rama; a celebrity of Ikshvaku dynasty.

"ṭhereafter, in the thirteenth year, King Dasaratha along with his preceptors started to perform anointment of the kingdom to Rama, a celebrity of Ikshvaku dynasty."

Sloka 19

tasmin sambhriyamāṇe tu rāghavasya abhiṣecane || 5-33-19
kaikeyī nāma bhartāram devī vacanam abravīt |

19. tasmin = (While) that; abhiṣechane = anointment; rāghavasya = of Rama; sambhriyamāṇe = was being arranged; devī = a queen; kaikeyī nāma = called Kaikeyi; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = the (following) words; bhartāram = to Dasaratha; her husband.

"While that anointment of Rama was being arranged, a queen called Kaikeyi spoke the following words to Dasaratha, her husband.

Sloka 20

na pibeyam na khādeyam pratyaham mama bhojanam || 5-33-20
eṣa me jīvitasya anto rāmo yadi abhiṣicyate |

20. rāmaḥ yadi abhiṣichyate = If Rama gets anointed; na pibeyam = I shall not drink water; na khādeyam = I shall not eat; mama = my; bhojanam = food; pratyaham = daily; eṣaḥ = this is; antaḥ = the end; me = to my; jīvitasya = life.

"If Rama gets anointed for the kingdom, from now onwards, I shall neither drink water nor take my food daily. This is the end to my life."

Sloka 21

yat tat uktam tvayā vākyam prītyā nṛpati sattama || 5-33-21
tac cen na vitatham kāryam vanam gacchatu rāghavaḥ |

21. nṛipatisattama = O the excellent king!; yat = which; tat vākyam = that word; uktam = was uttered; tvayā = by you; prītyā = with affection; tat yadi = and if that; na kāryam = for waste; rāghavaḥ = (let) Rama; gachchatu = go; vanam = to the forest.

"O the excellent king! If that word of pledge was uttered affectionately by you and if it is not going to be a waste, let Rama go to the forest."

Sloka 22

sa rājā satya vāg devyā vara dānam anusmaran || 5-33-22
mumoha vacanam śrutvā kaikeyyāḥ krūram apriyam |

22. shrutvā = hearing; krūram = the cruel; apriyam = and the disagreeable; vachanam = words; kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi; anusmaram = and recollecting; varadānam = the boon given; devyāḥ = to the queen; saḥ rājā = that king; satyarāk = who was true to his word; mumoha = became unconscious.

"ḥearing the cruel and the disagreeable words of Kaikeyi and recollecting the boon given to the queen, King Dasaratha who was true to his word, fell unconscious.

Sloka 23

tataḥ tu sthaviro rājā satya dharme vyavasthitaḥ || 5-33-23
jyeṣṭham yaśasvinam putram rudan rājyam ayācata |

23. tataḥ = then; sthaviraḥ = the aged; rājā = king; vyavasthitaḥ = who was firmly established; satye = in truth; dharme = and righteousness; rudan = while weeping; ayāchat = asked for ; rājyam = the kingdom; yashasvinam = from his illustrious; jyeṣṭham = eldest; putram = son.

"ṭhen, the aged king, who was firmly established in truth and righteousness, regretfully asked his illustrious eldest son to give back the kingdom, which he bestowed him earlier."

Sloka 24

sa pituḥ vacanam śrīmān abhiṣekāt param priyam || 5-33-24
manasā pūrvam āsādya vācā pratigṛhītavān |

24. shrīmān = the glorious; saḥ = that Rama; pūrvam = first; āsādya = admitted; pituḥ = his father's; vachanam = words; manasā = in mind; param priyam = more dearer; abhiṣekāt = than his anointment to the kingdom; pratigṛihītavān = and accepted (them); vāchā = with his word.

"ṭhat glorious Rama first admitted his father's words in mind, considering them more dearer than his anointment to the kingdom and accepted them with his word."

Sloka 25

dadyān na pratigṛhṇīyān na brūyat kiṃcit apriyam || 5-33-25
api jīvita hetoḥ hi rāmaḥ satya parākramaḥ |

25. rāmaḥ = Rama; satya parākramaḥ = the truly courageous man; dadyāt = (always) gives (to others) na pratigṛihṇīyāt = and does not take (from others); na brūyāt = (ḥe) does not speak; kimchit = even a little; apriyam = of disagreeable word; api jīvita hetorvā = even for the cause of his life.

"ṭhe truly courageous Rama always gives anything to others and does not take anything from others. He does not speak just a little of a disagreeable word even for the cause of his life."

Sloka 26

sa vihāya uttarīyāṇi mahāarhāṇi mahāyaśāḥ || 5-33-26
visṛjya manasā rājyam jananyai mām samādiśat |

26. vihāya = leaving behind; mahārhāṇi = greatly valuable; uttarīyāṇi = outer garments; (and wearing garments made of bark); saḥ = that Rama; mahāyashāḥ = the highly illustrious one; visṛijya = gave up; rājyam = his kingdom; manasā = intentionally; samādishat = (and) assigned; mām = me; jananyai = to his mother.

"Leaving behind his greatly valuable outer garments and wearing garments made of bark, that highly illustrious Rama gave up his kingdom willingly and assigned me to his mother."

Sloka 27

sā aham tasya agrataḥ tūrṇam prasthitā vana cāriṇī || 5-33-27
na hi me tena hīnāyā vāsaḥ svarge api rocate |

27. hīnāyāḥ = bereft; tena = of him; vāsaḥ = dwelling; svarge.api = even in heaven; na rochate hi = does not indeed give pleasure; sā aham = I; as such; vana chāriṇī = (decided) to proceed to the forest; tūrṇam = and soon; prasthitā = was ready to start; agrataḥ = (even) ahead; tasya = of that Rama.

"ṭo me bereft of him, residing even in heaven does not indeed give pleasure. I, as such, decided to proceed to the forest and soon was ready to start even ahead of Rama."

Sloka 28

prāg eva tu mahābhāgaḥ saumitriḥ mitra nandanaḥ || 5-33-28
pūrvajasya anuyātrā arthe druma cīraiḥ alamkṛtaḥ |

28. saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; mahābhāgaḥ = the illustrious man; mitra nandanaḥ = the rejoice of his friends; anuyātrārthe = for the purpose of following; pūrvajasya = of his elder brother; alamkṛitaḥ = adorned; prāgeva = even before (me).

"Adorned with clothes of bark, the illustrious Lakshmana, the rejoice of his friends, got ready to follow his elder brother even before (me)."

Sloka 29

te vayam bhartuḥ ādeśam bahu mānya dṛḍha vratāḥ || 5-33-29
praviṣṭāḥ sma purāt dṛṣṭam vanam gambhīra darśanam |

29. bahumānya = obeying respectfully; ādesham = the command; bhartuḥ = of Lord Dasaratha; vayam = we; te = as such; dṛiDhavratāḥ = firm in our resolution; praviṣṭāḥ = smaḥ = entered; vanam = the forest; adṛiṣṭam = not seen; purā = before; gambhīra darshanam = with its deep and profound appearance.

"Obeying respectfully the command of Lord Dasaratha, we as such with a firm resolution, entered the forest, which we have never seen before and which was deep and profound in appearance."

Sloka 30

vasato daṇḍaka araṇye tasya aham amita ojasaḥ || 5-33-30
rakṣasā apahṛtā bhāryā rāvaṇena durātmanā |

30. aham = I; bhāryā = the wife; tasya = of Rama; amitaujasaḥ = of great vigour; vasataḥ = dwelling; daṇ^Dakāraṇya = in the forest of Dandaka; apahṛitā = was taken away; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; rakṣasā durātmanā = the evil-minded demon.

While Rama of great vigour was dwelling in the forest of Dandaka, the evil-minded demon, Ravana took me, Rama's wife, away."

Sloka 31

dvau māsau tena me kālo jīvita anugrahaḥ kṛtaḥ || 5-33-31
ūrdhvam dvābhyām tu māsābhyām tataḥ tyakṣyāmi jīvitam |

31. me = to me; dvau = two; māsau = months; jīvitānugrahaḥ = of favour of survived; kṛitaḥ = have been given; tena = by him; tataḥ = for that reason; ūrdhvam = after; dvābhyām = two; māsābhyām = months; tyakṣyāmi = I shall give up; jīvitam = my life.

"A period of two months of survival-benefit has been given to me by him. For that reason, after the said two months, I shall give up my life."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe trayastriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 33rd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic
