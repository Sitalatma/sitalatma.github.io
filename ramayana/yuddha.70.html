Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 70

Introduction

ṃahodara, Trishira and Devantaka start attacking Angada. When Angada attacks them with trees, they chop off the trees and break away the rocks hurled at them. With a blow of Angada's palm, Devantakas's elephant falls down dead. Seeing Angada being surrounded by three valiant demons, Hanuma and Neela arrive there. With a blow of Hanuma's fist, Devantaka dies. Trishira and ṃahodara attack Neela with their arrows. ṃahodara dies in the hands of Neela. ṃeanwhile, Hanuma and Trisira fight at each other. Hanuma finally kills Trishira, by chopping off his three heads by Trishira's sword itself. Then, ṃahaparshva, the demon comes to the battle field and attacks Rishabha, the monkey-warrior. By banging ṃahaparshva's mace on ṃahaparshva Rishabha kills him.

Sloka 1

narāntakam hatam dṛṣṭvā cukruśur nairṛtarṣabhāḥ|
devāntakaḥ trimūrdhā ca paulastyaḥ ca mahodaraḥ || 6-70-01

1. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; narāntakam = ṇarantaka; hatam = dying; devāntakaḥ = Devantaka; trimūrdhācha = ṭrishira; mahodarāh cha = and ṃahodara; paulastyaḥ = the son of Pulastya; nairṛitarṣhabhāḥ = the foremost of demons; chukrushuḥ = wept.

Seeing Narantaka dying, Devantaka, Trishira and ṃahodara the son of Pulastya the foremost of demons, wept.

Sloka 2

ārūḍho megha samkāśam vāraṇa indram mahā udaraḥ |
vāli putram mahā vīryam abhidudrāva vīryavān || 6-70-02

2. ārūDha = mounting; vāraṇendram = and excellent elephant; meghasamkāsham = resembling a cloud; mahodaraḥ = ṃahodara; vegavān = having a abhidudrāva = attacked; vāliputram = Angada; mahāvīryam = of great energy.

ṃounting an excellent elephant, resembling a cloud, ṃahodara hurrily attacked Angada of great energy.

Sloka 3

ādāya parigham dīptam angadam samabhidravat || 6-70-03

3. balī = the strong; devāntakaḥ = Devantaka; bhrātṛivyansasantaptaḥ = tormented over his brother's calamity; tadā = then; samabhidravat = ran towards; aṇ^gadam = Angada; ādāya = seizing; gharam parigham = a terrific iron rod.

The strong Devantaka, tormented at his brother's calamity, then taking a terrific iron rod, ran towards Angada.

Sloka 4

āsthāya triśirā vīro vāli putram atha abhyayāt || 6-70-04

Thereupon, the valiant Trishira, mounting a chariot resembling the sun and yoked with excellent horses, marched towards Angada.

Sloka 5

sa tribhir deva darpaghnair nairṛta indrair abhidrutaḥ |
vṛkṣam utpāṭayām āsa mahā viṭapam angadaḥ || 6-70-05

5. saḥ = that; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; abhidrutaḥ = being attacked; tribhiḥ rākṣhasendraiḥ = by the three chief demons; devaradarpaghnaiḥ = who smashed the pride of celestials; utpāṭayāmāsa = uplifted; vṛikṣham = a tree; mahāviṭapam = with large boughs.

That Angada, being attacked by the three chief demons, who were capable of smashing the pride of celestials, uplifted a tree with its large boughs.

Sloka 6

mahā vṛkṣam mahā śākham śakro dīptam iva aśanim || 6-70-06

The valiant Angada hurled that large tree with big branches quickly on Devantaka as Indra the lord of celestials would a blazing thunderbolt.

Sloka 7

triśirās tam pracicceda śarair āśī viṣa upamaiḥ |

Trishira chopped off that tree with arrows looking like serpents. Seeing the tree chopped off, that Angada then sprang up.

Sloka 8

Thereupon, that foremost of monkeys streamed forth trees and rocks. The enraged Trishara chopped them off with his sharp arrows.

Sloka 9

That ṃahodra broke those trees with the tip of his club. Trishira too with his arrows attacked the valiant Angada.

Sloka 10

gajena samabhidrutya vāli putram mahā udaraḥ |

10. gajena = with an elephant; samabhidrutya = marching ahead quickly; vāliputram = towards Angada; mahodaraḥ = ṃahodara; samkruddhaḥ = enraged; jaghāna = struck; urasi = on his chest; tomaraiḥ = with javelins; vajra samnibhaiḥ = looking like thunderbolts.

ṃarching ahead quickly towards Angada on an elephant, the enraged ṃahodara struck on his chest with javelins looking like thunderbolts.

Sloka 11

deva antakasḥca samkruddhaḥparigheṇa tadā angadam |
upagamya abhihatya āśu vyapacakrāma vegavān || 6-70-11

11. tadā = then; samkruddhaḥ = the enraged; devāntakaḥ = Devantaka; vegavān = having a swiftness; upagamya = approaching; aṇ^gadam = Angada; abhihatya = and striking; āshu = quickly; parigheṇa = with a club; vyapachakrāma = went off to a distance.

Then, the enraged Devantaka with a speed, approached Angada, struck him quickly with a club and went off to a distance.

Sloka 12

sa tribhir nairṛta śreṣṭhair yugapat samabhidrutaḥ |
na vivyathe mahā tejā vāli putraḥpratāpavān || 6-70-12

12. abhidrutaḥ = (Eventhough) attacked; yugapat = at the same time; tribhiḥ nairṛita shreṣhṭhaiḥ = by the three foremost of demons; saḥ vāliputraḥ = that Angada; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; pratāpavān = having prowess; na vivyathe = was unalarmed.

Eventhough attacked at the same time by those three foremost of demons, that Angada of great prowess and splendour, did not get alarmed.

Sloka 13

sa vegavān mahāvegam kṛtvā paramadurjayaḥ |
talena bhṛśam utpatya jaghāna asya mahā gajam || 6-70-13

13. saḥ = Angada; vegavān = with a speed; parama durjayaḥ = and very much unconquerable; mahāvegam = acting; with a great swiftness; samabhidrutya = attacking well; jaghāna = struck; asya mahāgajam = his large elephant; talena = with his palm.

The speedy and quite unconquerable Angada, acting with a great swiftness and attacking well, struck Devantaka's elephant with his palm.

Sloka 14

petatur locane tasya vinanāda sa vāraṇaḥ || 6-70-14

14. tena tasya prahāreṇa = by that blow of Angada; samyuge = in battle; tasya nāgarājasya nayane = the eyes of that royal elephant; petatuḥ = fell down; saḥ = that; kuṇ^jaraḥ = elephant; vinanāsha = died.

By that blow of Angada in battle, the eyes of that royal elephant fell down and that elephant died.

Sloka 15

deva antakam abhidrutya tāḍayām āsa samyuge || 6-70- 15

15. niṣhkṛiṣhya = pulling out; viṣhāṇam = a tusk; asya = of this elephant; samyuge = in battle; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; vāliputraḥ = Angada; abhidrutya = running quickly; devāntakam = towards Devantaka; tāDayāmāsa = struck him.

Pulling out a tusk of that elephant in battle, the mighty Angada ran towards Devantaka quickly and struck him.

Sloka 16

sa vihvalita sarva ango vāta uddhata;iva drumaḥ |
lākṣā rasa savarṇam ca susrāva rudhiram mukhāt || 6-70-16

16. saḥ = that Devantaka; tejasvī = with vigour; vihvalaḥ = swayed to and fro; drumaḥ iva = like a tree; vātoddhūtaḥ = tossed up by wind; susrāva = and emitted; mahat = a lot; rudhiram = of blood; lākṣhārasa savarṇam = with the colour of a lacquer.

That energetic Devantaka swayed to and fro in disorder, like a tree being tossed up by the wind and emitted plenty of blood with the colour of a lacquer.

Thereafter, the strong Devantaka of great splendour, recovering his breath with a great difficulty, then struck Angada violently, by firmly holding an iron rod.

Sloka 18

Then, Angada the son of king Vali, struck by the iron rod, fell down on his knees on the earth, but again jumped up.

Sloka 19

ghorair hari pateḥputram lalāṭe abhijaghāna ha || 6-70-19

While Angada, the son of Vali was jumping up, Trishira struck him with three terrific and straight-going arrows on his forehead.

Sloka 20

20. viJ^ṇāya = ṇoticing; aṇ^gadam = Angada; parikṣhiptam = being surrounded; tribhiḥ nairṛitapuṇgavaiḥ = by three demon warriors; hanuman = ḥanuma; nīlashchāpi = and ṇeela; tataḥ = then; atha = moreover; pratasthatuḥ = went on march.

Noticing Angada being surrounded by the three demon-warriors, Hanuma and Neela went there.

Sloka 21

tad rāvaṇa suto dhīmān bibheda niśitaiḥśaraiḥ || 6-70-21

Thereupon, Neela then hurled a mountain-peak on Trishira. The intelligent Trishira the son of Ravana broke it by using his sharp arrows.

Sloka 22

savisphulingam sajvālam nipapāta gireḥśiraḥ || 6-70-22

22. tat = that; gireḥ shiraḥ = mountain-peak; bāṇa shatanirbhinnam = broken into pieces by a multitude of arrows; vidārita shilātalam = with its rocks split up; nipapāta = fell down; savishphuliṇ^gam = with sparks of fire; sajvālam = and flames.

That mountain-peak, broken into pieces by a multitude of arrows, with its split up rocks, fell down with sparks of fire and flames.

Sloka 23

parigheṇa abhidudrāva māruta ātmajam āhave || 6-70-23

Seeing Hanuma rushing with enthusiasm in the battle-front, that strong Devantaka ran towards him with his iron rod.

Sloka 24

Then, the valiant and the strong Hanuma struck him on his head. That great ape made the demons shake with the sound.

Sloka 26

26. saḥ = that; devāntakaḥ = Devantaka; rākṣhasarāja sūnuḥ = the son of Ravana; muṣhṭiniṣhpiṣhṭa vibhinnamūrdhā = with his head crushed and broken by the blow of the fist; niryānta dantākṣhivilambijihvaḥ = with his teeth and eyes coming out and with his tonguew hanging down; sahasā = swiftly; papāta = fell down; gatāsuḥ = dead; urvyām = on the earth.

That Devantaka, the son of Ravana, with his head crushed and broken by the blow of the fist, with his teeth and eyes coming out and with his tongue hanging down, swiftly fell down dead on the earth.

Sloka 27

Again mounting an elephant resembling a mountain, as the sun mounting ṃandara ṃountain, that enraged ṃahodara on his part, caused rain of arrows to fall quickly on Neela, like rumbling cloud with a circle of lightning, showers rain on a mountain.

Sloka 30

Sloka 32

Sloka 33

Seeing ṃahodara his young paternal uncle killed. Trishira was enraged seized hold of his bow and struck Hanuma with sharp arrows.

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

Sloka 37

Sloka 38

atha śaktim samādāya kāla rātrim iva antakaḥ |
cikṣepa anila putrāya triśirā rāvaṇa ātmajaḥ || 6-70-38

38. atha = thereupon; trishirāḥ = ṭrishira; rāvaṇātmajaḥ = the son of Ravana; samāsādya = seizing hold of; shaktim = a spear; antakaḥ iva = as Yama the lord of death; kāla rātrim = gets hold of a night of all-destroying time; chikṣhepa = and hurled it; anilaputrāya = on ḥanuma.

Thereupon, Trishira the son of Ravana seizing hold of a spear, as Yama the lord of death takes in his service Kalaratri, a night of all-destroying time, hurled it on Hanuma.

Sloka 39

divi kṣiptām iva ulkām tām śaktim kṣiptām asamgatām |
gṛhītvā hari śārdūlo babhanja ca nanāda ca || 6-70-39

39. gṛihītvā = seizing hold; tāmshaktim = of that spear; ulkālanīva = like a meteor; kṣhiptām = moving speedily; divaḥ = in the sky; asamgatām = even without descending on him; harishreshṭhaḥ = ḥanuma the foremost of monkeys; babhaṇ^ja cha = broke it; nanāda cha = and roared too.

Capturing that spear coming like a meteor in the sky, Hanuma the foremost of monkeys broke it, even without its descending on him and roared too.

Sloka 40

tām dṛṣṭvā ghora samkāśām śaktim bhagnām hanūmatā |
prahṛṣṭā vānara gaṇā vinedur jaladā;iva || 6-70-40

40. dṛiṣhṭva = seeing; tām shaktim = that spear; ghora samkāsham = with a terrific form; bhagnām = broken up; hanūmatāḥ = by ḥanuma; vānaragaṇāḥ = the monkey-troops; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = were very much delighted; vineduḥ = and roared aloud; jaladāḥ yathā = like rumbling clouds.

Seeing that spear of a terrific form broken up by Hanuma, the monkey-troops were very much delighted and roared aloud like rumbling clouds.

Sloka 41

tataḥkhaḍgam samudyamya triśirā rākṣasa uttamaḥ |
nicakhāna tadā roṣād vānara indrasya vakṣasi || 6-70-41

41. tataḥ = thereupon; samudyamya = lifting up; khaDgam = a sword; trishirāḥ = ṭrishira; rākṣhasottamaiḥ = the foremost of demons; tadā = then; nichakhāna = dug; khaDgam = the sword; vakṣhāsi = into the breast; vānarendrasya = of ḥanuma.

Thereupon, lifting up a sword, Trishira the foremost of demons them dug it into Hanuma's breast.

Sloka 42

khaḍga prahāra abhihato hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
ājaghāna trimūrdhānam talena urasi vīryavān || 6-70-42

42. khaDga prahārābhihataḥ = hurt by the thrust of the sword; vīryavān = the valiant; hanuman = ḥanuma; mārutātmajah = the son of wind-god; ājaghāna = struck; trimūrdhānam = ṭrishira; urasi = on his breat; talena = with his palm.

Hurt by the thrust of the sword, the valiant Hanuma the son of wind-god, struck Trishira on his breast, with his palm.

Sloka 43

sa tala bhihatas tena srasta hasta ambaro bhuvi |
nipapāta mahā tejās triśirās tyakta cetanaḥ || 6-70-43

43. talābhihataḥ = struck by ḥanuma's palm; saḥ trishirāḥ = that ṭrishira; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; nipapāta = fell down; bhuvi = on the earth; tyakta chetanaḥ = unconscious; srasta hastāyudhaḥ = with the weapons slipping from his hand.

Struck by Hanuma's palm, that Trishira of great splendour fell down unconscious on the earth with the weapons slipping from his hand.

Sloka 44

sa tasya patataḥkhaḍgam samāccidya mahā kapiḥ |
nanāda giri samkāśas trāsayan sarva nairṛtān || 6-70-44

44. saḥ mahākapiḥ = that large ape; girisamkāshaḥ = equal to a mountain in size; āchchhidya = snatching away; tasya = his; tam patataḥ = that falling khaDgam = sword; nanāda = roared loudly; trāsayan = frightening; sarvarākṣhasān = all the demons.

Hanuma, that large ape, equal to a mountain in size, snatching away that falling sword of Trishira, roared loudly frightening all the demons there.

Sloka 45

amṛṣyamāṇas tam ghoṣam utpapāta niśā caraḥ|
utpatya ca hanūmantam tāḍayām āsa muṣṭinā || 6-70-45

45. amṛiṣhyamāṇaḥ = unable to bear; tam ghoṣham = that roar; nishācharaḥ = that demon; utpapāta = jumped up; utpatya = after jumping up; (he) tāDayāmāsa = struck; hanūmantam = ḥanuma; muṣhṭinā = with his fist.

Unable to bear that roar, the demon jumped up and struck Hanuma with his fist.

Sloka 46

tena muṣṭi prahāreṇa saṃcukopa mahā kapiḥ |
kupitasḥca nijagrāha kirīṭe rākṣasa ṛṣabham || 6-70-46

46. mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma the great ape; samchukopa = was enraged; muṣhṭi prahāreṇa = by the blow of his fist; kupitaḥ cha = enraged as he was; nijagrāha = he seized hold of; rākṣhasarṣhabham = that foremost of demons; kirīṭe = by his head covered with diadem.

Enraged as he was by the blow of Trishiras's fist, Hanuma seized hold of Trishira the foremost of demons by his head covered by his diadem.

Sloka 47

sa tasya śīrṣāṇy asinā śitena |
kirīṭa juṣṭāni sakuṇḍalāni |
kruddhaḥpracicceda suto anilasya |
tvaṣṭuḥsutasya iva śirāṃsi śakraḥ || 6-70-47

47. saḥ = that; kruddhaḥ = enraged; anilasya sutaḥ = ḥanuma the son of wind-god; prachichchheda = chopped off; shitena asinā = with the sharp sword; tasya shīrṣhāṇi = his (three) heads; kirīṭa juṣhṭāni = furnished with diadems; sakuṇ^Dalāni = and ear-rings; shakraḥ iva = like Indra the lord of celestials (chopped off); shirāmsi = the heads; tvaṣhṭuḥ sutasya = of Vishvarupa the son of ṭvashta.

That enraged Hanuma the son of wind-god chopped off with that sharp sword, his three heads furnished with diadems and ear-rings, like Indra the lord of celestials chopped off the heads of Vishvarupa the son of Tvashta.

Sloka 48

tānyāyata akṣāṇy aga samnibhāni |
pradīpta vaiśvānara locanāni |
petuḥśirāṃsi indra ripor dharaṇyām |
jyotīmṣi muktāni yathā arka mārgāt || 6-70-48

48. tāni shirāmsi = those heads; indraripoḥ = of ṭrishira the enemy of Indra; āyatākṣhāṇi = having longish organs of senses; aga samnibhāni = which were mountain-like; pradīpta vaishvānara lochanāni = and having fire-like blazing eyes; petuḥ = fell; pṛithivyām = on earth; jyotīmṣhi yathā = like planets and stars; muktāni = drop; arkamārgāt = from the track of the sun.

Those mountain-like heads of Trishira the enemy of Indra, having longish organs of senses and having fire-like blazing eyes, fell down on earth, as planets and stars drop from the track of the sun.

Sloka 49

tasmin hate deva ripau triśīrṣe |
hanūmata śakra parākrameṇa |
neduḥplavamgāḥpracacāla bhūmī |
rakṣāṃsy atho dudruvire samantāt || 6-70-49
hatam triśirasam dṛṣṭvā tathaiva ca mahā udaram |
hatau prekṣya durādharṣau deva antaka nara antakau || 6-70-50
cukopa parama amarṣī mahā pārśvo mahā balaḥ |

49; 50. dṛiṣṭva = seeing; ṭrishira = ṭrishira; hatam = killed; athāpi = and even; ṃahodara (killed); prekṣhya devāntaka narāntakau = as also seeing Devantaka and ṇarantaka; durādharṣhau = the invincible ones; hatau = killed; mattaḥ = ṃatta; rākṣhasa puṇ^gavaḥ = the foremost of demons; chukopa = was enraged; paramāmarṣhī = with indignation.

When that Trishira, the enemy of celestials, having a prowess equal to that of Indra was killed, the monkeys made a loud noise. The earth trembled. Then, the demons ran away everywhere. Seeing Trishira, ṃahodara and the invincible Devantaka killed, ṃatta (ṃahaparshva) was enraged with indignation.

Sloka 51

jagrāha arciṣmatīm ca api gadām sarva āyasīm śubhām || 6-70-51
hema paṭṭa parikṣiptām māṃsa śoṇita lepanām |
virājamānām vapuṣā śatru śoṇita ranjitām || 6-70-52
tejasā sampradīpta agrām rakta mālya vibhūṣitām |
airāvata mahā padma sārvabhauma bhaya āvahām || 6-70-53

51. 52. 53. tadā = then; jagrāha = (he) was holding; gadām = a mace; archiṣhmatīm = which was resplendent; sarvāyasīm = completely made of iron; hema parikṣhiptām = wound round with golden wires; māmsashoṇita phenilām = filled with a forth of flesh and blood; virājamānam = shining; vipulām = large; shatrushoṇita tarpitām = satiated with the blood of advesaries; sampradīptāgrām = the upper most part of it blazing; tejasā = with splendour; raktamālyavibhūṣhitām = decked with garlands of ed flowers; airāvata mahāpadma sārvabhauma bhayāvahām = and which frightened the elephants supporting different quarters like Airavata; ṃahapadma and ṣarvabhauma.

Then, ṃahaparshva was holding a large mace, which was resplendent, completely made of iron, wound round with golden wires, filled with a froth of flesh and blood, satiated with the blood enemies, the upper most part of which was blazing with splendour, decked with red coloured garlands and which frightened the elephants supporting the quarters like Airavata, ṃahapadma and Sarvabhauma.

Sloka 54

gadām ādāya samkruddho mahā pārśvo mahā balaḥ |
harīn samabhidudrāva yuga anta agnir iva jvalan || 6-70-54

54. ādāya = taking; godām = the mace; samkruddhaḥ = the enraged; mattaḥ = ṃatta; rākṣhasapuṇ^gavaḥ = the foremost of demons; samabhidudrāva = went quickly; harrin = to the monkeys; jvalan yugāntāgniriva = like the blazing five at the end of the world.

Taking that mace, the enraged ṃatta, the foremost of demons, went quickly to the monkeys like the blazing fire at the end of the world.

Sloka 55

atha ṛṣayaḥsamutpatya vānaro ravaṇa anujam |
mahā pārśvam upāgamya tasthau tasya agrato balī || 6-70-55

55. atha = then; balī = the strong; ṛiṣhabhaḥ = Rishabha; vānaraḥ = a monkey; utpatya = jumping up; tasthau = stood; agrataḥ = in front; tasya = of him; upāgamya = having approached; mattānīkam = ṃattanika; rāvaṇānujam = the younger brother of Ravana.

Then, a strong monkey called Rishabha came jumping and stood in front of that ṃattanika (ṃahaparshva), the younger brother of Ravana.

Sloka 56

tam purastāt sthitam dṛṣṭvā vānaram parvata upamam |
ājaghāna urasi kruddho gadayā vajra kalpayā || 6-70-56

56. kruddhaḥ = enraged; dṛishṭvā = in seeing; vānaram = that ṛishabha; parvatopamam = looking like a mountain; sthtam = standing; purastāt = before him; ajaghāna = (ṃahaparshva) struck; urasi = in his breast; gadayā = with a mace; vajrakalpayā = equaling a thunderbolt.

Enraged in seeing that Rishabha, looking like a mountain, standing before him, ṃahaparshva struck him upon his breast with a mace equaling a thunderbolt.

Sloka 57

sa tayā abhihatas tena gadayā vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |
bhinna vakṣāḥsamādhūtaḥsusrāva rudhiram bahu || 6-70-57

57. abhihataḥ = struck; tayā gadayā = by that mace; tena = by him; saḥ = that; vānararṣhabhaḥ = Rishabha; the foremost of monkeys; bhinnavakṣhāḥ = had his breast broken; samādhūtaḥ = was trembled; susrāva = and exuded; rudhiram = blood; bahu = profusely.

When he struck with his mace, the breast of that Rishabha the foremost of demons was broken. He then trembled and exuded blood profusely.

Sloka 58

sa samprāpya cirāt samjnām ṛṣabho vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |
kruddho visphuramāṇa oṣṭho mahā pārśvam udaikṣata || 6-70-58

58. saḥ ṛiṣhabhaḥ = that Rishabha; vānareshvaraḥ = the supreme monkey; prāpya = regained; samjJṇām = his consciousness; chirāt = after a long while; kruddhaḥ = and enraged as he was; visphura māṇauṣhṭhaḥ = with his lips quivering; udaikṣhata = looked towards; mahāpārshvam = ṃahaparshva.

That Rishabha, the supreme monkey regained his consciousness after a long while and enraged as he was with his lips quivering, looked towards ṃahaparshva.

Sloka 59

sa vegavān vegavadabhyupetya |
taṃ rākṣasam vānaravīramukhya |
saṃvartya muṣṭim sahasā jaghān |
bāhvatare śailanikāśarūpaḥ || 6-70-59

59. vegavān = that swift; vānaravīra mukhyaḥ = Rishabha; the chief of monkey-warriors; shailanikāsharūpaḥ = with a mountain-like form; vegavat = speedily; abhyupetya = approaching; samvartya = and tightening; muṣhṭim = his fist; jaghāna = struck; sahasā = quickly; bāhvantare = on the chest; tam rākṣhasam = of that demon.

That Rishabha, the supreme monkey regained his consciousness after a long while and enraged as he was with his lips quivering, looked towards ṃahaparshva.

Sloka 60

sa kṛttamūlaḥ sahaseva vṛṣaḥ |
kṣitau papāta kṣatajokṣitāṅgah |
tām cāsya ghorām yamadaṇḍakalpām |
gadāṃ pragṛhyāśu tadā nanāda || 6-70-60

60. kṣhatajokṣhitāṇgaḥ = with his body drenched in blood; saḥ = that ṃahaparshva; papāta = fell; sahasā = suddenly; kṣhitau = on the ground; vṛikṣhaḥ iva = like a tree; kṛittamūlaḥ = with its root cut off; tadā = then; pragṛihya = taking; āshu = quickly; asya tām gadām = his mace; ghorām yamadaṇ^Da kalpān = which was looking like a terrific Yama's rod; nanāda = he roared loudly.

With his body drenched in blood, that ṃahaparshva suddenly fell unconscious on the ground, like a tree with its root cut off. Then, taking quickly ṃahaparshva's mace, which was looking like Yama's rod, he roared loudly.

Sloka 61

muhūrtamāsītsa gatāsukalpaḥ |
pratyāgāatmā sahasā surāriḥ |
utpatya saṃdhyābhrasamānavarṇa |
staṃ vārirājātmajamājaghāna || 6-70-61

61. saḥ = that ṃahaparshva; āsīt = remained; gatāsukalpaḥ = as though he was dead; muhūrtaḥ = for a while; utpatya = springing; sahasā = suddenly; surāriḥ = that demon; samdhyābhrasamāna varṇaḥ = whose colour resembled an evening cloud; pratyāgatātmā = after regaining his consciousness; ājaghāna = struck; tam = that; vārirājātmajam = Rishabha; the son of Varuna the lord of waters.

ṃahaparshva remained as though he was dead for a while. Springing suddenly after regaining his consciousness, that demon, whose colour resembled his consciousness, that demon, whose colour resembled an evening cloud, struck that Rishabha, the son of Varuna the lord of waters.

Sloka 62

sa mūrcito bhūmitale papāta |
muhūrtamutpatya punaḥ sasamjñaḥ |
tāmeva tasyādrivarādrikalpām |
gadām samāvidhya jaghāna samkhye || 6-70-62

62. saḥ = that Rishabha; papāta = fell; murchhitaḥ = unconscious; muhūrtam = for a while; bhūmitale = on the ground; utpatya = rising; sasamJṇaḥ = after regaining consciouness; samāvidhya = and tightly holding; tām gadāmeva = just that mace; tasya = of his; adrivarādrikalpam = which was looking like a large mountain-peak; jaghāna = he banged (ṃahaparshva); samkhye = in the battle.

That Rishabha fell unconscious for a while on the ground. Rising after regaining consciousness and tightly holding just the same mace, which was looking like a large mountain-peak, banged it on ṃahapatra in the battle-field.

Sloka 63

sā tasya raudrā samupetya deham |
raudrasya devādhvaravipraśatroḥ |
bibheda vakṣaḥ kṣatajam ca bhūri |
susrāva dhātvambha ivādrirājaḥ || 6-70-63

63. sau raudrā = that terrific mace; samupetya = reaching; deham = the body; tasya = of that ṃahaparshva = roudrasya = the fierce demon; devādhvaraviprashatro = the enemy of celestials; sacrifices and Brahmanas; bibheda = broke; vakṣhaḥ = his chest; susrāva = (ḥe) exuded; bhūri kṣhatajam = copious blood; adrirājaḥ iva = like a supreme mountain; dhātvambhaḥ = exuding; water full of minerals.

That terrific mace, reaching the body of ṃahaparshva, the fierce demon, the enemy of celestials, sacrificial rites and Brahmanas, broke his chest. ṃahaparshva started exuding copious blood, as a supreme mountain exudes water full of minerals.

Sloka 64

abhidudrāva vegena gadāṃ tasya mahātmanaḥ |
tām gṛhītvā gadām bhīmām āvidhya ca punaḥpunaḥ || 6-70-64
matta anīkam mahā pārśvam jaghāna raṇa mūrdhani |

64. mahātmā = the great-souled; saḥ = Rishabha; abhidudrāva = quickly ran towards; gadām = the mace; tasya mahātmanaḥ = of that mighty ṃahaparshva; gṛihīvā = seizing; tām bhīmām gadām = that dreadful mace; āvidhya = and striking on the floor; punaḥ punaḥ = againg and again; jaghāna = he struck; mattanīkam = ṃattanika (ṃahaparshva); raṇamūrdhani = in the battle-front.

The great souled Rishabha quickly ran towards the mace of that mighty ṃahaparshva. Seizing that dreadful mace and striking it repeatedly on the floor, he struck it on ṃahaparshva in the battle-front.

Sloka 65

sa svayā gadayā bhinno vikīrṇa daśana īkṣaṇaḥ |
nipapāta mahā pārśvo vajra āhata;iva acalaḥ || 6-70-65

65. bhagnaḥ = struck; svayā gadayā = by his own mace; saḥ mattaḥ = that ṃahaparshva; vishīrṇa dashanekṣhaṇaḥ = with his teeth and eyes fallen out; nipapāta = fell down; achalaḥ iva = like a mountain; vajrāhataḥ = struck by a thunderbolt.

Struck by his own mace, that ṃahaparshva with his teeth and eyes fallen out, fell down like a mountain struck by a thunderbolt.

Sloka 66

vidīrṇanayane bhūmau gatasttve gatāyuṣi |
patite rākṣase tasmin vidrutaṃ rākṣasam balam || 6-70-66

66. tasmin rākṣhase = (When) that demon; gata sattve = depleted his strength; vidīrṇanayane = with his eyes burst open; gatāyuṣhi = his long life gone away; patite = and fallen; bhūmau = on the ground; rākṣhasam balam = the demoniac army; vidrudatam = ran away.

When that demon depleted his strength, burst open his eyes, with his long life gone away and fallen down on the ground, the army of demons ran away.

Sloka 67

tasmin hate bhrātari rāvaṇasya |
tan nairṛtānām balam arṇava ābham |
tyakta āyudham kevala jīvita artham |
dudrāva bhinna arṇava samnikāśam || 6-70-67

67. tasmin = (When) that ṃahaparshva; ravaṇasya bhātari = the brother of Ravana; hate = was killed; tat balam = that army; nairṛitānām = of demons; arṇavābham = which was as extensive as an ocean; tyaktāyudham = abandoning their weapons; dudrāva = ran away; kevala jīvitārtham = just considered with their lives; bhinnārṇavasamnikāsham = like a sea which has burst its shores.

When that ṃahaparshva was killed, that army of demons, which was as extensive as an ocean, abandoning their weapons in the battle-field, fled for mere lives, like a sea which has burst its shores.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe saptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 70th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
