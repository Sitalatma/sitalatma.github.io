Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 63
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dasaratha woke up with anxious thoughts. Recalling his earlier sin, he starts recounting the story of a young ascetic to Kausalya. He says that while he was Prince Regent of Ayodhya, he went out for hunting in a forest one day and heard the sound of a hermit-boy filling his pitcher with water by suberging it into Sarayu River. ṃistaking the gurgling sound for trumpeting of an elephant the prince hit the boy with an arrow, which went deep into the body and mortally wounded him. On approaching the victim, he discovered the fatal blunder and tendered his heart felt apologies to the hermit boy, who asked him to extract the arrow from his body and inform his parents. The boy died soon after the arrow was drawn out from the body.

Sloka 1

pratibuddho muhur tena śoka upahata cetanaḥ |
atha rājā daśarathaḥ sa cintām abhyapadyata || 2-63-1

1. atha = then; saḥ rājā = that king; pratibuddhaḥ = awakened; muhūrtena = after some time; shokopahata chetanaḥ = with mind affiliated with grief; abhyapadyata = got into; chintām = anxious thought.

The king, awakened after some time, with his mind afflicted with grief, got into an anxious thinking.

Sloka 2

rāma lakṣmaṇayoḥ caiva vivāsāt vāsava upamam |
āviveśa upasargaḥ tam tamaḥ sūryam iva āsuram || 2-63-2

2. vivāsāt = due to the exile; rāma lakṣmaṇayoḥ = of Rama and Lakshmana; upasargaḥ = a great calamity; āvivesha = befell; tam = on that Dasaratha; vāsavopamam = who is equal to Indra; sūryamiva = like sun (seized); tamaḥ = by obscurity; asuram = which is attributed to the demon Rahu.

Due to banishing of Rama and Lakshmana, a great calamity befell on Dasaratha who was equal in strength to Indra like sun seized by obscurity, which is attributed to the demon Rahu.

Sloka 3

sabhārye nirgate rāme kausalyām kosaleśvaraḥ |
vivakṣurasitāpāṅgām smṛvā duṣkṛtamātmanaḥ || 2-63-3

3. rāme = after Rama; nirgate sati = having departed (to the forest); sabhārye = along with his wife; kosaleshvaraḥ = Dasaratha the king of Kosala; smṛitvā = recalling ātmanaḥ = his; duṣkṛitam = sin; vivakṣuḥ = wanted to speak; kausalyām = to Kausalya; astāpāṇgām = whose eye-corners were dark in colour.

After Rama along with his wife departed to the forest, Dasaratha the king of Kosala recalling his earlier sin, wanted to tell Kausalya, whose eye-corners were dark in colour all about it.

Sloka 4

sa rājā rajanīm ṣaṣṭhīm rāme pravrajite vanam |
ardha rātre daśarathaḥ saṃsmaran duṣkṛtam kṛtam || 2-63-4

4. rāme = after Rama; pravrājite = was sent into exile; vanam = to the forest; rājā = king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; ṣaṣṭhīm = on the sixth; rajanīm = night; arthavātra = at mid-night; samsmavan = was recalling; duṣkṛitam = his sin; kṛitam = done (long ago).

After Rama was sent into exile to the forest, King Dasaratha, on the sixth night at mid-night, was recalling his sin done (long ago).

Sloka 5

sa rājā putraśokārtaḥ smaran duṣkṛtamātmanaḥ |
kausalyām putra śoka ārtām idam vacanam abravīt || 2-63-5

5. saḥ rājā = that king; putrashokārtaḥ = afflicted by grief for his son; smaram = recalling; ātmanaḥ = his; duṣkṛitam = sin; abravīt = spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words; kausalyām = to Kausalya; putrashokārtām = who was suffering from sorrow for her son.

King Dasaratha, afflicted by grief for his son, recalling his sin, spoke the following words to Kausalya who was suffering from sorrow for her son.

Sloka 6

yad ācarati kalyāṇi śubham vā yadi vā aśubham |
tat eva labhate bhadre kartā karmajam ātmanaḥ || 2-63-6

6. kalyāṇi = O; blessed lady!; bhadre = O; gracious one!; yat = whatever; kartā = a doer of an act; shubham vā = either good; yadi vā = or; ashubham = bad; kurute = does; labhate = (he) obtains; tadeva = it surely; ātmanaḥ = his own; karmajam = result.

"O, blessed lady! O gracious one! A doer reaps surely the fruit of his own deeds corresponding to the nature of work either good or bad, of that which he does!"

Sloka 7

guru lāghavam arthānām ārambhe karmaṇām phalam |
doṣam vā yo na jānāti sa bālaiti ha ucyate || 2-63-7

7. yaḥ = who; ārambhe = while undertaking; karmaṇām = actions; najānāti = does not know; gurulāghavam = the relative value; arthānām = of their utilities phalam = (or) their fruit; doṣam vā = (or) their defect; saḥ = he; uchoyate ha = is surely called; bālaḥ iti = as a boy (fool).

"He who does not consider, while undertaking actions, the relative value of their fruits as well as the utilities or defects accruing from them, is surely called a fool."

Sloka 8

kaścit āmra vaṇam cittvā palāśāmḥ ca niṣincati |
puṣpam dṛṣṭvā phale gṛdhnuḥ sa śocati phala āgame || 2-63-8

8. kashchit = anyone; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; puṣpam = their flowers; gṛidhnuḥ = and greedily desirous of; phale = their; fruits; niṣiṇchati = nourishes; palāshran = Palasa trees; chhittvā = by cutting off; āmravanam = mango groves; saḥ = he; shochati = will come to grief; phalāgame = after arrival of fruit."

"Anyone, who after seeing their (charming and big) flowers, greedily desires their (large and luscious) fruits and nourishes Palasa trees, by cutting off a mongo grove, he will come to grief after bearing of their fruit."

Sloka 9

avijñāya phalam yo hi karma tvevānudhāvati |
sa śocetphalaveldāyām yathā kiṃśukasecakaḥ || 2-63-9

9. yaḥ = who; avijJṇāya = without knowing; phalam = the fuit; anudhāvati = follows running; karmatu eva = towards action; saḥ = he kimshukaschakaḥ iva = as a nourisher of Kimsuka trees; phalavelāyām = at the time of fruit-bearing; shochet = will come to grief.

"He who without knowing the result goes running towards action, will come to grief as a nourisher of Kimsuka trees coming to grief at the time of their fruit-bearing."

Sloka 10

so aham āmra vaṇam cittvā palāśāmḥ ca nyaṣecayam |
rāmam phala āgame tyaktvā paścāt śocāmi durmatiḥ || 2-63-10

10. saḥ aham = I as such; nyaṣachayam = nourished; palashān cha = Palasa trees; chhitvā = by cutting off; āmravaṇam = mango grove. durmatiḥ = being wicked-minded; tyaktvā = leaving; rāmam = Rama; phalāgame = at the time of arrival of fruit; shochāmi = I am lamenting; pashchāt = afterwards.

"I nourished Palasa trees, by cutting off mango grove. Being wicked-minded in abandoning Rama at the time of arrival of fruit, I am lamenting thereafter."

Sloka 11

labdha śabdena kausalye kumāreṇa dhanuṣmatā |
kumāraḥ śabda vedhī iti mayā pāpam idam kṛtam || 2-63-11

11. kausalye = O Kausalya!; mayā = by me; labdhashabdena = who has obtained a name; kumāraḥ iti = as a youth; shabdavedhī = shooting a heard; but unseen object; dhanuṣmatā = (and) wielding a bow; idam = this; pāpam = sin; kṛitam = was done; kumāreṇa = while I was young."

"This sin was done by me while I was young and wielding a bow. At that time, I was famous as a young man, who can shoot heard (but unseen) object by an arrow, O Kausalya!"

Sloka 12

tat idam me anusamprāptam devi duhkham svayam kṛtam |
sammohāt iha bālena yathā syāt bhakṣitam viṣam || 2-63-12

12. tatidam = such of this; duḥkham = grief; svayam kṛitam = obtained by my own accord; samanuprāptam = has befallen; me = to me; yathā syāt = perhaps as; viṣam = a poison; bhakṣitam = eaten; bālena = by a boy; sammohāt = in ignorance; iha = in this world; devi = O; Kausalya!"

"This grief, obtained by my own accord, has befallen me, as by a boy in ignorance eating poison in the world; O, Kausalya!"

Sloka 13

yathānyaḥ puruṣaḥ kaścitpalāśairmoohito bhavet |
evam mama api avijñātam śabda vedhyamayam phalam || 2-63-13

13. yathā = ḥow; kashchit = some; anyaḥ = other; puruṣaḥ = man; bhavet = became; mohitaḥ = disillusioned; phalāshaiḥ = by Palasa trees; evam = in the same manner; phalam = the (correct) result; shabdavedhyamayam = of hitting a target by sound only; apravijJnatam = was not known accurately; mayāpi = by me also."

"How the other man became disillusioned by Palasa trees, I also became disillusioned in the same manner, without knowing accurately the correct result of hitting a target by means of sound only."

Sloka 14

devy anūḍhā tvam abhavo yuva rājo bhavāmy aham |
tataḥ prāvṛḍ anuprāptā mada kāma vivardhinī || 2-63-14

14. devi = O; Kausalya!; tvam = you; abhavaḥ = were; anūDhā = unmarried (till then); aham = I; bhavāmi = was; yuvarājaḥ = a prince Regent; tataḥ = then; anuprāptā = came; prāvṛiṭ = the rainy season; madakāma vivardhinī = which enhanced excitement and desire (for hunting).

"O, Kausalya! You were unmarried till then and I was the Prince Regent. Then came the rainy season which enhanced excitement and desire (for hunting)."

Sloka 15

upāsyahi rasān bhaumāṃs taptvā ca jagad aṃśubhiḥ |
pareta ācaritām bhīmām ravir āviśate diśam || 2-63-15

15. raviḥ = the ṣun; upāsya = partaking; rasān = the fluid; bhaumān = relating to earth; taptvācha = and warming; jagat = the world; amshubhiḥ = with its rays; āvishat = entered; bhīmām = the terrible; disham = (southern) quarter; paretācharitām = haunted by spirits.

"The sun, partaking fluids from earth and warming the world with its rays, entered the terrible southern quarter haunted by spirits."

Sloka 16

uṣṇam antar dadhe sadyaḥ snigdhā dadṛśire ghanāḥ |
tataḥ jahṛṣire sarve bheka sāranga barhiṇaḥ || 2-63-16

16. uṣṇam = heat; antardadhe = disappeared; sadyaḥ = suddenly; snigdhāḥ = resplendent; ghanāḥ = clouds; dādṛishire = were seen; tataḥ = thereafter; sarve = all; bhekasāraṇga barihiṇaḥ = frogs; cuckoos and peacocks; kajṛiṣire = were full of joy."

"Heat disappeared suddenly. Resplendent clouds appeared in the sky. All frogs, cuckoos and peacocks were full of joy."

Sloka 17

klinnapakṣottarāḥ snātāḥ kṛccrādiva vatatriṇaḥ |
vṛṣṭivātāvadhūtāgrān pādapānabhipedire || 2-63-17

17. patatriṇaḥ = birds; klinnapakṣottarāḥ = with moistened top of their wings; snātāḥ = af if bathed; kṛichchrāt iva = with great difficulty; abhipadire = reached; pādapān = the trees; vṛiṣṭivātāvadhūtāgrān = the tops of which were being shaken off by rain and wind."

"Birds with moistened top of their wings as if they bathed, reached with great difficulty the trees, the tops of which were shaken off by rain and wind."

Sloka 18

patitena ambhasā cannaḥ patamānena ca asakṛt |
ābabhau matta sārangaḥ toya rāśir iva acalaḥ || 2-63-18

18. chhannaḥ = enveloped; ambhasā = by water; patamānena = falling; asakṛit = again and again; patitena = (on the water already) fallen; achalaḥ = the mountain; mattasārṇgaḥ = with antelopes in rut; ababhau = glowed; toyarāshiriva = like an ocean.

"Enveloped by water falling again and again on the water already fallen, the mountain with its antelopes in rut, glowed like an ocean."

Sloka 19

pāṇḍurāruṇavarṇāni srootāṃsi vimalānyapi |
susruvurgiridhātubhyaḥ sabhasmāni bhujaṅgavat || 2-63-19

19. srotāmsi = the water-streams; vimalānyapi = thought crystal clear; susrusuḥ = flowed; bhujaṇ^gavat = like serpents; pāṇDurāruṇa varṇāni = with white and red colours; sabhasmāni = along with ashes; giridhātubhyaḥ = or mountain-minerals

"Although the water-streams were crystal clear, they flowed in serpentine courses, with white and red colours because of their admixture with ashes and mountain-minerals on the way."

Sloka 20

ākulāruṇatoyāni srootāṃsi vimalānyapi |
unmārgajalavāhīni babhūvurjaladāgame || 2-63-20

20. srotāmsi = the water-streams; vimalānyapi = even though crystal-clear; babhūvuḥ = became; jalagāmini ādulāruṇa toyāmi = churned with reddish water; unmārgajalogāmini = carrying water by a wrong way; jaladāgame = after the onset of rain.

"The water-streams, which were crystal-clear till then, became churned with reddish waters, carrying water by a wrong way, after the onset of rain."

Sloka 21

tasminn atisukhe kāle dhanuṣmān iṣumān rathī |
vyāyāma kṛta samkalpaḥ sarayūm anvagām nadīm || 2-63-21

21. vyāyāma kṛita samkalpaḥ = with a wish to do (ḥunting as) an exercise; tasmin kāle = in that season; atisukhe = which was most comfortable; anvagām = I went along; sarayūnadīm = ṣarayu River; rathī = going in a chariot; dhanuṣmān; wearing a bow; ishhumān = and arrows.

"With a wish to do hunting as an exercise in that most comfortable season, I went along Sarayu River, in a chariot, wearing bow and arrows."

Sloka 22

nipāne mahiṣam rātrau gajam vā abhyāgatam nadīm |
anyam vā śvā padam kaṃcij jighāṃsur ajita indriyaḥ || 2-63-22
tasmiṃstatrāhamekānte rātrau vivṛtakārmukaḥ |

22. tatra = there; aham = I; ajitendriyaḥ = without my senses subdued; jihāmsuḥ = with an intent to kill; mahiṣam = a (wild) buffalo; gajamvā = or an elephant; anyam shvāpadam vā = or any other wild animal; abhyāgatam = coming; nadīm = the river; rātrau = in the night; tasmin = at that place; nipāme = for drinking water; vivṛita kārmukaḥ = I was ready with my bow; ekānte = at a solitary place.

"Without my senses under control and with an intent to kill there a (wild) buffalo or an elephant or any other wild animal coming in the night at that place for the purpose of drinking water, I was ready with my bow at a solitary place."

Sloka 23

tatrāham samvṛtam vanyam hatavāṃstīramāgatam || 2-63-23
anyam cāpi mṛgam hiṃsram śabdam śrutvābhu pāgatam |

23. shrutvā = hearing; shabdam = the sound; aham = I; hatavān = killed; samvṛitam = turning towards (it); vanyam = a wild animal; to; tīram āgata = which came to bank (of ṣarayu River); anyam = (and) another; hrimsram = violent; mṛigam cha = animal too; abhyupāgatam = which arrived; tatra = there.

"Hearing the sound, I killed turning towards it, a wild animal, which came to the bank of Sarayu River and another violent beast too which came there."

Sloka 24

atha andha kāre tu aśrauṣam jale kumbhasya paryataḥ || 2-63-24
acakṣur viṣaye ghoṣam vāraṇasya iva nardataḥ |

24. atha = thereafter; andha kāre = in darkness; achakṣur viṣaye = and not within the reach of the eyes; ashrauṣam = I heard; ghoṣham = the sound; kumbhasya = of a pitcher; pūryataḥ = being filled up; jale = in water; nardataḥ vāraṇasyeva = like that of an elephant."

"ṃeanwhile, in that darkness and not within the react of the eye, I heard the sound of a pitcher being filled up, the sound of which appeared like that of an elephant."

Sloka 25

tataḥ aham śaram uddhṛtya dīptam āśī viṣa upamam || 2-63-25
śabdam prati gajaprepsurabhilakṣya tvapātayam |

25. tataḥ = then; gajaprepsuḥ = with an intent to obtain the elephant; aham = I; uddhṛitya = drew out; abhilakṣya = targeted; shabdam prati = towards that sound; apātayam = and released; sharam = an arrow; dīptam = shining; ashīviṣopamam = and equal to a serpent.

"With an intent to hit that elephant, I drew out a shining arrow resembling a serpent, targeted towards the direction sound and discharged it."

Sloka 26

amuncam niśitam bāṇam aham āśī viṣa upamam || 2-63-26
tatra vāg uṣasi vyaktā prādur āsīd vana okasaḥ |
hā hā iti patataḥ toye bāṇābhihatamarmaṇaḥ || 2-63-27

26; 27. aham = I; anuṇeham = discharged nishitam = a sharp; bāṇam = arrow; ashīviṣopamam = resembling a serpent; tatra = there; uṣasi = at dawn; prādurāsīt = arose; vyaktā = a distinct; vāk = voice; hā hā iti = uttering 'Ah! Ah!' vanaukasaḥ = of an inhabitant of forest; patataḥ = dropping down; toye = in water; bāṇābhīhatamarmaṇaḥ = his vital part hit by the arrow.

"I released a sharp arrow, resembling a serpent. There, at dawn, arose a distinct voice uttering 'Ah! Ah!' of a forest-inhabitant, dropping down in water after his vital part was hit by an arrow."

Sloka 28

tasminnipatite bāṇe vāgabhūttatra mānuṣī |
katham asmad vidhe śastram nipatet tu tapasvini || 2-63-28

28. tasmin = (while) that; bāṇe = arrow; nipatite = was attacking; mānushhu = a human; vāk = voice; abhūt = was manifest; tatra = there; katham = how; shastram = a weapon; nipatettu = did rush upon; tapasvini = an ascetic; asmadvidhe = like me?

"While that arrow was attacking a human voice there was heard, saying, 'Oh, how a weapon did rush upon an ascetic like me?' "

Sloka 29

praviviktām nadīm rātrāv udāhāraḥ aham āgataḥ |
iṣuṇā abhihataḥ kena kasya vā kim kṛtam mayā || 2-63-29

29. aham = I; āgataḥ = came; nadīm = to the river; pravivektām = which is away from habitation; udākāraḥ = to carry water; rātrau = in the night; kena = by whom; abhihataḥ = I was hit; iṣhuṇā = by an arrow? vā = on the other hand; kim = what; iṛitam = was done; mayā = by me; karya = to any one?

'I came to the river, which is away from habitation, in the night to fetch water. By whom I was hit by an arrow? On the other hand what wrong have I done to anyone?"

Sloka 30

ṛṣer hi nyasta daṇḍasya vane vanyena jīvataḥ |
katham nu śastreṇa vadho mad vidhasya vidhīyate || 2-63-30

30. ṛishhe = to a sage; madvidhasya = like me; nyastadaṇdasya = of having laid down the road; jīvataḥ = and living; vanyena = on fruits and roots of wild plants; vane = in the forest; katham na = how; vadhaḥ = a killing; shastreṇa = by an arrow;vidhīyate = is enjoined?

"To a seer like me, of having laid down violence and living a fruits and roots of wild plants in the forest, how a killing by an arrow is enjoined?"

Sloka 31

jaṭā bhāra dharasya eva valkala ajina vāsasaḥ |
ko vadhena mama arthī syāt kim vā asya apakṛtam mayā || 2-63-31

31. kaḥ = who; syāt = is desirous of; vadhena = in killing; mama = of me; jaṭābhārudharasyām = wearing a mass of locks of hair; valka lājina vāsasaḥ = with bark of trees and deer-skin as my clothing? kim vā = what; apakṛitam = harm; was done; asya = to him; mayā = by me?"

"Who is desirous of killing me, wearing a mass of locks of hair and clad with bark of trees and deer-skin? What harm was done to him by me?"

Sloka 32

evam niṣphalam ārabdham kevala anartha samhitam |
na kaścit sādhu manyeta yathaiva guru talpagam || 2-63-32

32. kevālānarthasamhitam = concerning only with a useless objective; niṣphalam = a vain act; ārabdham = is undertaken; evam = in this way; na kashchit = no one; manyate = will admire him; as for instance who violates his teacher's bed; sādhu = as good.

"A vain act concerning only with a useless objective is undertaken in this manner. No one will admire him, as for instance like any man seeking the bed of his preceptor's wife, as good."

Sloka 33

naham tathā anuśocāmi jīvita kṣayam ātmanaḥ |
mātaram pitaram ca ubhāv anuśocāmi mad vidhe || 2-63-33

33. aham = I; nānushochāmi = do not worry; tathā = that much; ātmanaḥ = of my; jīvitakṣayam = destroyed of life; madvadhe = In the case of my death;; anushochāmi = I worry; ubhau = both; mātaram = for my mother; pitāramcha = and father.

"I do not worry that much of my death. I worry both for my mother and father, in the case of my death."

Sloka 34

tat etān mithunam vṛddham cira kālabhṛtam mayā |
mayi pancatvam āpanne kām vṛttim vartayiṣyati || 2-63-34

34. mayi apanne = while I obtain my death; kāmi = which; vṛittim = livelihood; etat = this; vṛiddham = elderly; mithunam = couple; chirakālabhṛitam = maintained since a long time; mayā = by me; vartayiṣyati = will follow?"

'By what means of livelihood this elderly couple, maintained since a long time by me, support themselves when I obtain my death?'

Sloka 35

vṛddhau ca mātā pitarāv aham ca eka iṣuṇā hataḥ |
kena sma nihatāḥ sarve subālena akṛta ātmanā || 2-63-35

35. vṛddhau = the aged; mātāpitā = mother and father; ahamca = me too; hataḥ = are killed; ekeṣaṇā = by a single arrow; kena = By whom; akṛitātmanā = a man without self-control; subālena = and very much a fool; sarve = all of us; nihatāḥ sma = are killed?

"I and my aged parents are killed by a single arrow. By a man without self control and being very much a fool, all of as are virtually killed."

Sloka 36

tam giram karuṇām śrutvā mama dharma anukānkṣiṇaḥ |
karābhyām saśaram cāpam vyathitasya apatat bhuvi || 2-63-36

36. shrutvā = ḥearing; tāmgiram = those words; karaṇām = which were pitiable; karābhyām = from the hands; mama = of me; dharmānukāṇkṣiṇaḥ = who is intent on righteousness; vyathitasya = and having anguish; anpatat = fell; chāpam = the bow; sasharam = with arrows."

"Hearing those pitiable words and since I was always intent on righteousness, I felt distressed. The bow and arrow fell down from my hands to the ground."

Sloka 37

tasyāham karuṇam śrutvā niśi lālapato bahu |
sambhrānatḥ śokavegena bhṛśamāsa vicetanaḥ || 2-63-37

37. shrutvā = hearing; tasya = his; karuṇam = pathetic cry; lālavataḥ = weeping; bahu = very much; nishi = during the night; asa = I became; vichetasaḥ = unconscious; bhṛisham = frequently; sambhrāntaḥ = utterly bewildered ( as I was); shokavegena = due to flare-up of grief."

"Hearing his pathetic cry, weeping profusely during the night, I became unconscious frequently, utterly bewildered as I was, due to flare-up of my grief."

Sloka 38

tam deśam aham āgamya dīna sattvaḥ sudurmanāḥ |
apaśyam iṣuṇā tīre sarayvāḥ tāpasam hatam || 2-63-38
avakīrṇajaṭābhāram praviddhakalaśodakam |
pāsuśoṇitadigdhāṅgam śayānam śalyapīḍitam || 2-63-39

38; 39. dīnasattvaḥ = Distressed in mind; sudurshānāḥ = and with much sorrowful feeling; aham = I; āgamya = reached; tam desham = that place; apashya = and saw; tāpasam = the ascetic; hatam = hit; iṣuṇā = by arrow; tīre = at the bank; sarayvāḥ = of ṣarayu River; avukīrṇajaṭābhāram = with his tresses of hair scattered; praviddhakalashodakam = his pitcher of water thrown asunder; pāmskushoṇitadigdhāṇ^gam = having his limbs anointed with sand and blood; shayānam = lying down; shalyapīDitam = hurt by the arrow.

"Distressed in mind and with much sorrowful feeling, I reached that place and saw the ascetic, hit by the arrow at the bank of Sarayu River, with his tresses of hair scattered, his pitcher of water thrown asunder, having his limbs anointed with sand and blood lying down as he was, hurt by the arrow."

Sloka 40

sa mām udvīkṣya netrābhyām trastam asvastha cetasam |
iti uvāca vacaḥ krūram didhakṣann iva tejasā || || 2-63-40

40. udvīkṣya = looking up; netrābhyām = with his eyes; didhakṣinnivatejasā = as if going to scorch with his glory; mām = at me; trastam = who stood terrified; asvathachetasam = and sick in mind; saḥ = he; uvācha = spoke; iti = thse; krūram = harsh(words); tataḥ = then.

Looking up with his eyes, as if going to scorch with his glory, at me who stood terrified and sick in mind, he spoke those harsh words then:

Sloka 41

kim tava apakṛtam rājan vane nivasatā mayā |
jihīrṣiur ambho gurv artham yad aham tāḍitaḥ tvayā || 2-63-41

41. jihīrṣuḥ = who was carrying; ambhaḥ = water; rājan = O; king!; aham = I; gurvartham = for my parents; tāDitaḥ = was hit; tvayā = by you; kim = what; apakṛitam = harm was done; tava = to you; mayā = by me; nivasatā = who is residing; vane = in a forest?"

"O, king! I, who was carrying water for my parents, was hit by you. What was the harm done to you by me who is residing in a forest?"

Sloka 42

ekena khalu bāṇena marmaṇi abhihate mayi |
dvāv andhau nihatau vṛddhau mātā janayitā ca me || 2-63-42

42. abhihate = being hit; marmaṇi = on my secret part; ekena bāṇena = by a single arrow; nihatā khalu = you; virtually killed; dvau = both; me = my; mātā = mother; janayitācha = and father; andhau = who are blind; vṛiddhau = and aged.

'Being hit on my secret part by a single arrow, you virtually killed both my mother and father, who are blind and aged.'

Sloka 43

tau nūnam durbalāv andhau mat pratīkṣau pipāsitau |
ciram āśā kṛtām tṛṣṇām kaṣṭām saṃdhārayiṣyataḥ || 2-63-43

43. tau = both of them; durbalau = weak; andhau = blind; pipāsitau = and thirsty; smadhāvayiṣyataḥ = might have regrained; tṛiṣṇām = theri thirst; kaṣṭām = with difficulty; āshākṛitām = and attended with expectation; chiram = since long; nūnam = I am sure.

"Both of them, weak blind and thirsty, might have refrained their thirst with difficulty and waited with expectation of my arrival since long. I am sure."

Sloka 44

na nūnam tapaso vā asti phala yogaḥ śrutasya vā |
pitā yan mām na jānāti śayānam patitam bhuvi || 2-63-44

44. nūnam = surely; tapasovā = religious austerity; shrutasya vā = or sacred knowledge; nāsti phalayogaḥ = have not borne fruit; yat = because; pitā = my father; na jānāti does not know; mām = about me; patitam = having fallen down; shayānam = and lying ; bhūmi = on the ground.

'Surely, our (mine or father's) religious austerity or sacred knowledge have not borne fruit because my father does not have information about me, having fallen down and lying on the ground.'

Sloka 45

jānann api ca kim kuryāt aśaktir aparikramaḥ |
cidyamānam iva aśaktaḥ trātum anyo nago nagam || 2-63-45

45. jānannapi = even if he knows; kim = what; ashaktiḥ = my disabled (father); aparikramaḥ = who is unable to walk around; kuryāt = can do? anyaḥ naga iva = like one tree; ashaktaḥ = is unable; trātum = to protect; nagam = a tree; chhidyamānam = being cut off.

"What my disabled father, unable to walk around can do, even if he knows like a tree unable to protect another tree which is being cut off?"

Sloka 46

pitus tvam eva me gatvā śīghram ācakṣva rāghava |
na tvām anudahet kruddho vanam vahnir iva edhitaḥ || 2-63-46

46. rāghava = O; Dasaratha!; gattvā = going; shīghram = soon; tvameva = you alone; āchakṣva = tell me pituḥ = my father; nānudahet = ḥe will not scroch; tvām = you; kruddhaḥ = by anger; vanamiva = as a forest (in scorched); edhitaḥ = by flared-up; vahniḥ = fire

'O, Dasaratha! Going there soon, you alone tell my father; so that he will not scorch you by his anger, as a forest is scorched by a flared-up fire.'

Sloka 47

iyam eka padī rājan yataḥ me pitur āśramaḥ |
tam prasādaya gatvā tvam na tvām sa kupitaḥ śapet || 2-63-47

47. rājan = O; king!; yataḥ = on which side; āshramaḥ = the hermitage; me pituḥ = of my father (on that side); iyam = this; ekapadī = foot-path (will go); gattvā = after going ( there); tvam = you; prasādaya = obtain graciousness; tam = from him; kupitaḥ = being angry; saḥ na shapet = let him not execrate; tvām = you."

'O, king! This foot-path will lead you to my father's hermitage. After going there, seek his graciousness, lest he should get angry and execrate you."

Sloka 48

viśalyam kuru mām rājan marma me niśitaḥ śaraḥ |
ruṇaddhi mṛdu sa utsedham tīram ambu rayo yathā || 2-63-48

48. rājan = O; king!; kuru = make; mām = me; vishalyam = free from the arrow-head; nishitaḥ = the sharp; sharaḥ = arrow; ruṇadhhi = torments; me marma = my vital part; mṛidu = which is delicate; amburayaḥ yathā = in the same way as a river current (corrodes); tīram = the sandy bank sotsedham = which is at height.

'O, king! Extract this arrow-head from my body. This sharp arrow torments my delicate vital part in the same way as a river-current corrodes the sandy bank, which is at a height.'

Sloka 49

saśalyaḥ kliśyate prāṇairviśalyo vinaśiṣyati |
iti māmaviśaccintā tasya śalyāpakarṣaṇe || 2-63-49

49. asya shalyāpakarṣaṇe = while I was extracting the arrow-head from him; iti = this; chintā = thought; avishat = entered; mām = me; sashalyaḥ = with arrow-head in his body; klishyat = he will suffer; prāṇaiḥ = with his lives; vishalyaḥ = without the arrow-head; vinashiṣyat = he will die.

"While extracting the arrow-head from him, this thought entered my mind. With arrow-head in his body he will suffer, even though surviving. But after extracting the arrow-head, he will die."

Sloka 50

duḥkhitasya ca dīnasya mama śokāturasya ca |
lakṣyāmāsa hṛdaye cintām munisuta stadā || 2-63-50

50. tadā = then; munisutaḥ = the son of the sage; lakṣayāmāsa = perceived; chintām = the worry; hṛidaye = in the heart; mama = of myself; duḥkhitasya = who was lamenting; dīnasya = pitiable; shokāturasya = suffering from anguish.

"Then, the son of the sage perceived worry in the heart of myself, who was lamenting pitiably, suffering from anguish."

Sloka 51

tāmyamānaḥ sa mām duḥkhāduvāca paramārtavat |
sīdamāno vivṛttāṅgo veṣṭamāno gataḥ kṣayam || 2-63-51

51. sīdamānaḥ = sinking into despondency; tāmyamānaḥ = and being in distress; vivṛittāngaḥ = distorting the limbs in agony; veṣṭamānaḥ = coiling round himself; kṣayam = the end (of life); paramārtavat = extremely pained; saḥ = he; uvācha = spoke; mām = to me; kṛichchhrant = with great difficulty.

Sinking into despondency with distress, distorting his limbs in agony, coiling round himself on the floor with extreme pain and having approached the end of his life, he spoke to me (as follows) with great difficulty.

Sloka 52

saṃstabhya dhairyeṇa sthiracitto bhavāmyaham |
brahmahatyākṛtam pāpam hṛdayādapanīyatām || 2-63-52

52. samstabhya = suppressing; shokam = my grief; dhairyeṇa = with firmness; aham = I; bhavāmi = am becoming; sthirachittaḥ = steady; tāpam = (let the) torment; hṛidayāt = in your heart; brahmahatyākṛitam = caused by the thought of your having killed a Brahmana; apanīyatām = be removed.

'Suppressing my grief with firmness, I am becoming stable-minded. Let the torment in your heart, caused by the thought of your having killed Brahmana be removed.'

Sloka 53

na dvijātir aham rājan mā bhūt te manaso vyathā |
śūdrāyām asmi vaiśyena jātaḥ jana pada adhipa || 2-63-53

53. rājan = O; king; janapadādhipā = the ruler of the country! aham = I; na = am not; dvijātiḥ = a Brahmana; mābhūt vyathā = let there be no agony; manasaḥ = in your mind; asmi = I am; jātaḥ = born; shūdrāyām = = through a ṣudra woman; vaishyena = by Vysya.

'O, king the ruler of the country! I am not a Brahmana. Let there be no agony in your mind. I am born through a Sudra woman by a Vysya.

Sloka 54

iti iva vadataḥ kṛccrāt bāṇa abhihata marmaṇaḥ |
vighūrṇato viceṣṭasya vepamācasya bhūtale || 2-63-54
tasya tu ānamyamānasya tam bāṇam aham uddharam |
tasya tvānamyamānasya tam bāṇāmahamuddharam || 2-63-55

54; 55. itīva = In this manner; vadataḥ = speaking; kṛichchhāt = with great difficulty; bāṇābhihata marmaṇaḥ = who was hit in his vital part by an arrow; vicheṣṭasya = exerting himself; vepamānasya = trembling; ānamyamānasya = sinking; aham = I; uddharam = drew out; tam bāṇam = that arrow; tasya = from him; saḥ tapaodhanaḥ = that sage; udvīkṣya = looked up; mām = towards me; samtrastaḥ = in fear; jahau = and relinquished; prāṇān = his lives.

"While he was speaking in that manner with great difficulty, his vital part having been hit by an arrow and was rolling on the ground, now exerting himself, now trembling and sinking, I drew out that arrow from him. That sage looked up towards me in fear and relinquished his life."

Sloka 56

jala ārdra gātram tu vilapya kṛccān |
marma vraṇam samtatam uccasantam |
tataḥ sarayvām tam aham śayānam |
samīkṣya bhadre subhṛśam viṣaṇṇaḥ || 2-63-56

56. bhadre = O; my dear Kausalya! samīkṣaya = seeing; tam = him; jalārdragātram = with his body drenched in water; vilapya = who wept; kṛichchhāt = with anguish; uchchhvasantam = sighing his breath; samtatam = unceasingly; marmavraṇam = with injury in his vital part; shayānam = lying down; sarayvām = in the River ṣarayu; asmi = I became; bhṛisham = very much; viṣaṇṇaḥ = grief-stricken.

"O, my dear Kausalya! Seeing him, with his body drenched in water, weeping with anguish, unceasingly sighing his breath, with injury in his vital part and lying down in River Sarayu as he was, I became very much grief-stricken."
