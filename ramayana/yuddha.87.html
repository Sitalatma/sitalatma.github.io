Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 87
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana takes Lakshmana to the place where indrajit is performing the sacrificial rite. He advises Lakshmana to destroy Indrajit even before he finishes the sacrificial rite at a banyan tree. Indrajit sees Vibhishana there and starts talking harsh words to him, saying that he has ditched him by bringing Lakshmana to that place. Vibhishana replies that because of Ravana's vices, he has left him and joined Rama's side. He further adds that Indrajit and his army will not survive under the range of Lakshmana's arrows.

Sloka 1

evamuktvā tu saumitriṃ jātaharṣo vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
dhanuṣpāṇinamādāya tvaramāṇo jagāma saḥ || 87-6-1

1. evam = thus; uktvā = speaking; saumitrim = to lakshmana; saḥ vibhiṣhaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; tam dhanuṣhpāṇim ādāya = taking him who held a bow in his hand; jagāma = went; jāta harṣhaḥ = rejoiced; tvaramāṇaḥ = hurriedly.

After speaking to him as aforesaid, Vibhishana taking Lakshmana, who held a bow in his hand, felt rejoiced and hastened away.

Sloka 2

avidūraṃ tato gatvā praviśya ca mahadvanam |
darśayāmāsa tatkarma lakṣmaṇāya vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 87-6-2

2. gatvā = going; avidūram = not very distant; tataḥ = from there; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; pravishya = having entered; mahat vanam = an extensive thicket; adarshayata = showed; lakṣhmaṇāya = to Lakshmana; tat karma = that place where Indrajit was going to pour oblations into the sacred fire.

ṃoving not very distant from there and entering an extensive thicket, Vibhishana showed to Lakshmana, the place where Indrajit was going to pour oblations into the sacred fire.

Sloka 3

nīlajīmūtasaṅkāśaṃ nyagrodhaṃ bhīmadarśanam |
tejasvī rāvaṇabhrātā lakṣmaṇāya nyavedayat || 87-6-3

3. tejasvī = the glorious; rāvaṇa bhrātā = Vibhishana; nyavedayat = showed; bhīmadarshanam = a terribly looking; nyagrodham = banyan tree; nīlajīmūtasamkāsham = looking like a black cloud.

The glorious Vibhishana showed a terribly looking banyan tree, appearing like a blace cloud (and spoke as follows).

Sloka 4

ihopahāraṃ bhūtānāṃ balavānrāvaṇātajaḥ |
upahṛtya tataḥ paścātsaṅgrāmamabhivartate || 87-6-4

4. upahṛitya = after offering; upahāram = oblations; bhūtānām = to the spirits; iha = here; balavān = the mighty; rāvaṇātmajaḥ = Indrajit; abhivartate = proceeds; samgrāmam = to the battle; pashchāt = afterwards.

"After offering oblations to the spirits at this place, the mighty Indrajit proceeds to the battle afterwards."

Sloka 5

adṛśyaḥ sarvabhūtānāṃ tato bhavati rākṣasaḥ |
nihanti samare śatrūnbadhnāti ca śarottamaiḥ || 87-6-5

5. tataḥ = thereupon; rākṣhasaḥ = (this) demon; bhavati = becomes; adṛishyaḥ = invisible; sarva bhūtānām = to all the beings; nihanti = kills; shatrūn = the enemies; samare = in battle; badhnāti = and binds; sharottamaiḥ = with excellent arrows.

"Thereupon, this demon becomes invisible to any one, kills some enemies in battle and binds some with his excellent arrows."

Sloka 6

tamapraviṣṭaṃ nyagrodhaṃ balinaṃ rāvaṇātmajam |
vidhvaṃsaya śaraistīkṣṇaiḥ sarathaṃ sāśvasārathim || 87-6-6

6. vidhvamsaya = destroy; tam = that; balinam rāvaṇātmajam = mighty Indrajit; saratham = with his chariot; sāshvasārathim = along with its horses and charioteer; dīptaiḥ sharaiḥ = by your blazing arrows; apraviṣhṭam = while he has not reached; nyagrodham = the banyan tree.

"Destroy that mighty Indrajit, along with his chariot, horses and charioteer, by releasing your blazing arrows, even while he has not reached the banyan tree."

Sloka 7

tathetyuktvā mahātejāḥ saumitrirmitranandanaḥ |
babhūvāvasthitastatra citraṃ visphārayandhanuḥ || 87-6-7

7. mahātejāḥ = the greatly brilliant; saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; mitranandanaḥ = a delight of his friends; tatheti = saying 'ṣo be it'; babhūva avasthitaḥ = took his stand; tatra = there chitram dhanuḥ visphārayan = twanging his wonderful bow at full length.

That greatly brilliant Lakshmana, a delight of his fiends, saying 'So be it' took his stand there, twanging his bow at full length.

Sloka 8

sa rathenāgnivarṇena balavānrāvaṇātmajaḥ |
indrajitkavacī khaḍgī sadhvajaḥ pratyadṛśyata || 87-6-8

8. saḥ balavān indrajit = that mighty Indrajit; rāvaṇātmajah = the son of Ravana; kavachī = wearing an armour; khaDgī = and a sword; sadhvajaḥ = distinguished by his ensign; pratyadṛishyata = appeared; rathena in his chariot; agnivarṇena = having a colour of fire.

That Indrajit, the son of Ravana, clad in armour, armed with a sword, and distinguished by his ensign, appeared in a fire-coloured chariot.

Sloka 9

tamuvāca mahātejāḥ paulastyamaparājitam |
samāhvaye tvāṃ samare samyagyuddhaṃ prayaccha me || 87-6-9

9. mahātejāḥ = that highly splendid Lakshmana; uvācha = spoke; tam = to that; paulastya = Indrajit the scion of sage Pulastya; aparājitam = who had never been conquered before (as follows):;samāhvaye = I am inviting; tvām = you; samare = for a fight; prayachchha = give; yuddham = battle; me = to me; samyak = in a right manner.

That highly splendid Lakshmana spoke to that Indrajit, the scion of Sage Paulastya, who had never been conquered before (as follows); "I am inviting you for a fight. Give battle to me in a right manner."

Sloka 10

evamukto mahātejā manasvī rāvaṇātmajaḥ |
abravītparuṣaṃ vākyaṃ tatra dṛṣṭvā vibhīṣaṇam || 87-6-10

10. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; (by Lakshmana); rāvaṇātmajaḥ = Indrajit; mahātejāḥ = with a great brilliance; manasvī = and high mindedness; dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana; tatra = there; abravīt = spoke; paruṣham = (the following) harsh; vākyam = word.

Thus spoken by Lakshmana, Indrajit with a great brilliance and high mindedness, seeing Vibhishana there, spoke the following harsh words to him:

Sloka 11

iha tvaṃ jātasaṃvṛddhaḥ sākṣādbhrātā piturmama |
kathaṃ druhyasi putrasya pitṛvyo mama rākṣasa || 87-6-11

11. rākṣhasa = O demon!; tvam = you; jāta samvṛiddhaḥ = are born and brought up; iha = here; sākṣhāt = directly; bhrātā = a brother; mama pituḥ = to my father; pitṛivyaḥ = and an uncle; mama = to me; katham = how; druhyati = can you be hostile; putrasya = to a son?.

"O demon! You are born and brought up here. You are a direct brother to my father and a paternal uncle to me. How can you be hostile to a son?

Sloka 12

na jñātitvaṃ na sauhārdaṃ na jātistava durmate |
pramāṇaṃ na ca sodaryaṃ na dharmo dharmadūṣaṇa || 87-6-12

12. durmati = O perverted fellow!; dharmadūṣhaṇa = an abuser of righteousness!; Jṇātitvam = consanguinity; na pramāṇam = is not a norm; tava = to you; na sauhārdram = nor friendliness; na jātiḥ = nor pride of birth; na saudaryam = nor brotherly feeling; na dharmaḥ = nor a right conduct.

"O perverted fellow, an abuser of righteousness! Consanguinity is not a norm for you, nor friendliness, nor pride of birth, nor a brotherly feeling nor a right conduct."

Sloka 13

śocyastvamasi durbuddhe nindanīyaśca sādhubhiḥ |
yastvaṃ svajanamutsṛjya parabhṛtyatvamāgataḥ || 87-6-13

13. durbuddhe = O evil-minded one!; tvam = you; asi = are; shochyaḥ = pitiable; nindanīyashcha = and deserve to be reproached; sādhubhiḥ = by the virtuous; yaḥ utsṛijya = (in that) having abandoned; svajanam = your own kind; tvam āgataḥ = you have entered into; parabhṛityatvam = the service of our enemy.

"O evil-minded one! You are pitiable and deserve to be reproached by the virtuous, in that having abandoned your onw kind, you have entered into the service of our enemy."

Sloka 14

naitacchithilayā buddhyā tvaṃ vetsi mahadantaram |
kva ca svajanasaṃvāsaḥ kva ca nīcaparāśrayaḥ || 87-6-14

14. tvam = you; na vatsi = are not recognising; mahat = the great; antaram = difference; shithilayā buddhyā = with your feeble mind; etat = in this manner; kva cha = where; svajana samvāsaḥ = is living together with one's own kindred; kvacha = and where; nīcha parāshrayaḥ = is taking refuge iwth low kind of enemies?

"You are not recognizing the great difference because of your feeble mind. Where is living together with one's own kindred and where is taking refuge with low kind of enemies?

Sloka 15

guṇavānvā parajanaḥ svajano nirguṇo.api vā |
nirguṇaḥ svajanaḥ śreyānyaḥ paraḥ para eva saḥ || 87-6-15

15. parajanaḥ = opponents; guṇavānvā = may be virtuous; svajanaḥ = one's own kindred; nirguṇo.api = may not be virtuous; nirguṇaḥ svajanaḥ = the unvirtuous kindred; shreyān = is preferable; yaḥ paraḥ = he who is an enemy; saḥ = he; para eva hi = is indeed just an enemy.

"Opponents may be virtuous. One's own kindred may not be virtuous. But, a relation, though bereft of merit is preferable. An enemy is, indeed, ever an enemy."

Sloka 16

yaḥ svapakṣaṃ parityajya parapakṣaṃ niṣevate |
sa svapakṣe kṣayaṃ prāpte paścāttaireva hanyate || 87-6-16

16. yaḥ = he who; parityajya = having abandoned; svapakṣham = his onw side; niṣhevate = and serves; parapakṣham = the side of an adversary; saḥ = he; prāpte = having obtained; kṣhayam = the destruction; svapakṣhe = of his own side; hanyate = would be killed; pashchāt = thereafter; taireva = only be them.

"He who, abandoning his own side, takes sides with adversary, is killed, after his own knsmen are destroyed just by those people of the other side."

Sloka 17

niranukrośatā ceyaṃ yādṛśī te niśācara |
svajanena tvayā śakyaṃ paruṣaṃ rāvaṇānuja || 87-6-17

17. rāvaṇānuja = O Indrajit; nishāchara = the demon! niranukroshatā = the ruthlessness; iyam yādṛishī te = thus shown by you; pauruṣham = and the manliness exhibited by you (in bringing Lakshmana here); shakyam could be possible; tvayam = only by you; svajanena = our own kindred.

"O Indrajit, the demon! The ruthlessness thus shown by you and the manliness exhibited by you (in bringing Lakshmana here) could be possible only by you, our own kindred."

Sloka 18

ityukto bhrātṛputreṇa pratyuvāca vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
ajānanniva macchīlaṃ kiṃ rākṣasa vikatthase || 87-6-18

18. iti = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; bhrātṛiputreṇa = by his brother's son; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; pratyuvācha = replied; rākṣhasa = O demon!; kim = why; vikatthase = do you speak perversely; ajānanniva = as though you do not know; machchhīlam = my nature.

Hearing the words of Indrajit, his brother's son, Vibhishana replied: "O demon! Why do you speak perversely, as though you are not aware of my nature."

Sloka 19

rākṣasendrasutāsādho pāruṣyaṃ tyaja gauravāt |
kule yadyapyahaṃ jāto rakṣasāṃ krūrakarmaṇām || 87-6-19
guṇo.ayaṃ prathamo nṛiṇāṃ tanme śīlamarākṣasaṃ |

19. asādho = O the wicked one!; rākṣhasendrasuta = O Indrajit!; gauravāt = out of respect; tyaja = abandon; pauruṣhyam = the harshness; aham jātaḥ yadyapi = even if I am born; rakṣhasām kulam = in a demoniac race; krūra karmaṇām = doing cruel acts; me shīlam = my nature (of goodness); yaḥ prathamaḥ guṇaḥ nṛīṇām = which is the prime virtue of human beings; tat = that; arākṣhasam = is not demoniacal.

"O Indrajit the wicked one! Atleast out of respect for the elders, abandon your harshness. Even if I am born in a demoniac race, which does cruel acts, my nature is goodness, which is prime virtue of human beings and it is not demoniacal."

Sloka 20

na rame dāruṇenāhaṃ na cādharmeṇa vai rame || 87-6-20
bhrātrā viṣamaśīlena kathaṃ bhrātā nirasyate |

20. aham = I; na rame = do not delight; dārunena = in cruel acts; na rame vai = nor rejoice; adharmeṇa cha = in virtuous acts; katham = how; bhrātā = can a brother; nirasyate = be driven away; bhrātrā = by his brother; viṣhamashilo.api = even if the former's nature is different.

"I neither delight in cruel acts, nor rejoice in unvirtuous acts. How can a brother be driven away by his brother, even if the former's nature is different?"

Sloka 21

dharmātpracyutaśīlaṃ hi puruṣaṃ pāpaniścayam || 87-6-21
tvaktvā sukhamavāpnoti hastādāśīviṣaṃ yathā |

21. tyaktvā = renouncing; puruṣham = a man; pāpanishschayam = of sinful resolve; prachyuta shīlam = whose conduct has deviated; dharmāt = from righeousness; avāpnoti = one attains; sukham = happiness; ashīvisham yathā = as (one shakes off) a venomous serpent; hastāt = from his hand.

"Renouncing a man of sinful resolve hose conduct has deviated from righteousness, one attains happiness, as one shakes off a venomous serpent from his hand."

Sloka 22

parasvaharaṇe yuktaṃ paradārābhimarśām || 87-6-22
tyājyamāhurdurātmānaṃ veśma prajvalitaṃ yathā |

22. āhuḥ = (the wise men) say that durātmanam = an evil-natured fellow; yuktam = who is intent; para svaharaṇe = on taking away other's property; paradārābhimarshakm = and lustfully touches another's wife; tyājyam = is worth shunning; prajvalitam veshmayathā = as one abandons a house in flames.

"The wise men say that an evil-natured fellow, who is intent on taking away other's property and lustfully touches another's wife, is worth shunning, as one abandons a house in flames."

Sloka 23

parasvānāṃ ca haraṇaṃ paradārābhimarśanam || 87-6-23
suhṛdāmatiśaṅkāṃ ca trayo doṣāḥ kṣayāvahāḥ |

23. trayaḥ = three; doṣhāḥ = faults; kṣhayāvahāḥ = produce destruction; parasvānāmharaṇam = taking away other's property; para dārābhi marshanam = lustfully touching another's wife; suhṛidām atishaṇ^kācha = and excessive distrust with one's friends.

"Three faults produce destruction. Taking away other's property, lustfully touching another's wife and excessive distrust with one's friends."

Sloka 24

maharṣīṇāṃ vadho ghoraḥ sarvadevaiśca vigrahaḥ || 87-6-24
abhimānaśca kopaśca vairitvaṃ pratikūlatā |
ete doṣā mama bhrāturjīvitaiśvaryanāśanāḥ || 87-6-25
guṇānpracchādayāmāsuḥ parvatāniva toyadāḥ |

24; 25. ete doṣhāḥ = these faults; jīvitaishvaryanāshanāḥ = which destroy one's life and lordship; ghoraḥ vadhaḥ maharṣhīṇām = terribly killing the great sages; sarvadevaiḥ vigrahaḥ cha = waging war against all celestials; abhimānashcha = arrogance; roṣhaḥ cha = becoming angry very easily; vairitvam cha = quarrelsomeness; pratikūlatā = ill-will; prachchhādayāmāsaḥ = have concealed; guṇān = the good qualities; mama bhrātuḥ = of my elder brother; toyadāḥ iva = as clouds (conceal); parvatāniva = (the view of) mountains.

"These faults which destroy one's life and lordship killing great sages terribly, waging war against all celestials, arrogance, becoming angry very easily, quarrelsomeness, ill-will have concealed the good qualities of my elder brother, as the clouds conceal the view of mountains."

Sloka 26

doṣairetaiḥ parityakto mayā bhrātā pitā tava || 87-6-26
neyamasti purī laṅkā na ca tvaṃ na ca te pitā |

26. etaiḥ doṣhaiḥ = because of these faults; tava pitā = your father; bhrātā = and my brother; parityaktaḥ = has been abandoned; mayā = by me; na = neither will; iyam laṇ^kā purī = this City of Lanka; asti = will exist (any longer); na tvam cha = nor you; na te pitācha = nor your father.

"Because of these vices, Ravana, your father and my brother, has been abandoned by me. Neither will this City of Lanka will exist any longer nor you nor your father."

Sloka 27

atimānī ca bālaśca durvinītaśca rākṣasa |
baddhastvaṃ kālapāśena brūhi māṃ yadyadicchasi || 87-6-27

27. rākṣhasa = O demon!; tvam = you; mām brūhi = can talk to me; yat yat ichchhasi = as you like; atimānashcha = you are highly arrogant; bālashcha = foolish; durvinītashcha = and indisciplined; kālapāshena baddhaḥ = and caught; as you are; in the noose of Death.

"O demon! You can talk to me as you like, since you are arrogant, foolish, indisciplined and caught, as you are, in the noose of Death."

Sloka 28

adya te vyasanaṃ prāptaṃ kimiha tvaṃ tu vakṣyasi |
praveṣṭuṃ na tvayā śakyo nyagrodho rākṣasādhama || 87-6-28

28. rākṣhasādhama = O the worst of demons!; yat = for which reason; uktavān = you spoke; paruṣham = harsha words; mām = about me; adya = today; prāptam = you got; vyasanam = a calamity; iha = here; na shakyam = it is not possible; tvayā = for you; pravaṣhṭum = to enter; nyagrodham = the banyan tree.

"O the worst of demons! You got this calamity here because you spoke harsh words to me today. You cannot reach the banyan-tree any longer."

Sloka 29

dharṣayitvā tu kākutsthau na śakyaṃ jīvituṃ tvayā |
yudhyasva naradevena lakṣmaṇena raṇe saha || 87-6-29
hatastvaṃ devatā kāryaṃ kariṣyasi yamakṣaye |

29. na shakyam = It is not possible; tvayā = for you; jīvitum = to survive; dharṣhayitvā = on attacking; kākutthsam = Lakshmana; yuddhyasva = you fight; lakṣhmaṇena saha = with Lakshmana; naradevana = the lord of men; raṇe = in the battle; hataḥ = being killed; tvam = you; kariṣhyasi = will do; devatākāryam = sacred work; yamakṣhaya = in the abode of Yama; the god of Death.

"It is not possible for you to survive, on attacking Lakshmana. You fight with Lakshmana, the lord of men, in the battle-field. After being killed, you will do sacred work in the abode of Yama, the god of Death."

Sloka 30

nidarśayasvātmabalaṃ samudyataṃ |
kuruṣva sarvāyudhasāyakavyayam |
na lakṣmaṇasyaitya hi bāṇagocaraṃ |
tvamadya jīvansabalo gamiṣyasi || 87-6-30

30. kuruṣhva = do; sarvāyudhasāyaka vyayam = spend all your weapons and arrows; nidarshayitvā = by showing; samudyatam ātmabalam = your own augmented strength; etya = reaching; bāṇa gocharam = the range of arrows; lakṣhmaṇasya = of Lakshmana; tvam nagammiṣyasihi = you will not indeed go; jīvan = alive; adya = today; sabalaḥ = along with your army.

"Do spend away all your weapons and arrows, by showing your own augmented strength. Reaching the range of Lakshmana's arrows, you will not indeed return alive with your army today."
