Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 28
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction


Rama eulogises the rainy season for a heavenly elixir is showered for all the living beings. This is one among the other eulogies to seasons. On this narration about rainy season, there are extensive commentaries by the ancient commentators as rainy season occupies a prime ordeal status among other seasons, and some of them are included here.


Sloka 1

sa tadā vālinam hatvā sugrīvam abhiṣicya ca |
vasan mālyavataḥ pṛṣṭe rāmo lakṣmaṇam abravīt || 3-28-1

1. vālinam hatvā = Vali, on eliminating; sugrīvam abhiṣicya ca = ṣugreeva, anointment, also; tadā = ensuing; mālyavataḥ pṛiṣṭe = ṃt. ṃalayavata - Prasavana, on upland of - mountainside; vasan = while residing; saḥ rāmaḥ lakṣmaṇam abravīt = he, Rama, to Lakshmana, spoke.

Rama spoke to Lakshmana while residing on the mountainside of ṃt. ṃalayavata, also known as ṃt. Prasavana, ensuing the elimination of Vali and enthronement of Sugreeva. [3-28-1]

Sloka 2

ayam sa kālaḥ saṃprāptaḥ samayo adya jala āgamaḥ |
saṃpaśya tvam nabho meghaiḥ saṃvṛtam giri saṃnibhaiḥ || 3-28-2

2. saḥ = that - time, of which we thought; ayam = is this; kālaḥ = time; sam prāptaḥ = has come to pass; adya jala āgamaḥ samayaḥ = now, waters, coming [rains,] season; giri sannibhaiḥ = mountain, similar; meghaiḥ = with clouds; samvṛitam = fully covered; nabhaḥ = sky; tvam sampashya = you, see.

"This is that time of which we thought and it has come to pass. Now it is rainy season. You see the sky fully covered with mountainous clouds. [3-28-2]

Sloka 3

nava māsa dhṛtam garbham bhāskārasya gabhastibhiḥ |
pītvā rasam samudrāṇām dyauḥ prasūte rasāyanam || 3-28-3

3. dyauḥ = heaven - sky; bhāskārasya gabhastibhiḥ = with sun's, sunrays; samudrāṇām rasam pītvā = of oceans, essence, on guzzling; nava māsa dhṛitam = nine, months, after sustaining; rasāyanam garbham = of elixir [of life,] to the pregnancy; prasūte = is giving birth.

"Sustaining a nine-month pregnancy, impregnated through the Sun with his sunrays guzzling the essence of oceans, the heaven is giving birth to the elixir of life. [3-28-3]

▼

The atmosphere bears pregnancy of rain water drawing through the sunrays, keeping that essence in air consolidating them to become watery clouds for a nine month period from the month of kārtīka to āṣāDha. That is roughly from November to July. Then it delivers rainwater, the elixir of life, in the month of shrāvaṇa. It is an extract of six rasa-s elixirs, for the utilisation of various crops.

▼

atra meghāḥ sūrya rashmi marut ādibhiḥ samudram udakam gṛihītvā garbhavt kārtīka māsa prabhṛiti nava māsa dhṛitam sasyānām ShaD rasa sampatti hetu bhūtam shuddham udakam shrāvaṇe māsi muncati it sūcitam -- dharmākūtam

Sloka 4

śakyam aṃbaram āruhya megha sopāna paṃktibhiḥ |
kuṭaja arjuna mālābhiḥ alaṃkartum divākaram || 3-28-4

4. megha sopāna panktibhiḥ = with cloud's, stairs, flight of; ambaram āruhya = sky, climbing up; divā karam = day, maker - sun can be; kuṭaja arjuna mālābhiḥ = with wild-jasmines, Arjuna-flowers, garlands; alankartum shakyam = to adorn, it is possible - in these days.

"It is very likely to climb up the flight of stairs of clouds to bedeck the sun with the garlands of white wild-jasmines, and red Arjuna flowers, in these days. [3-28-4]

▼

Vishnu or Narayana is located in the solar orb in the aspect of sun, calle sūrya nārayaṇa and he is to be adored in these days of rain before any bodily activity like eating or drinking is undertaken. dhyeyaḥ sadā savitṛi maṇDala madhya vartī nārāyaṇam and this Vishnu is a 'decoration-happy' deity alankāra priyo vishṇuḥ and thus He can easily be reached by the stepladder formation of clouds and he can be garlanded easily. To do so a wife must be at his side sa patnyā vishālākshyā nārāyanam upāgamat 'adore that Narayana along with your wife...' and now if Rama were to adore that Supreme, Seetha is not at his side. Govindaraja.

Sloka 5

saṃdhyā rāga utthitaiḥ tāmraiḥ aṃteṣu adhika pāṇḍuraiḥ |
snigdhaiḥ abhra paṭa cchedaiḥ baddha vraṇam iva aṃbaram || 3-28-5

5. sandhyā rāga utthitaiḥ = sunset time, with the hue of, extending - mottled with; tāmraiḥ = with redness; anteṣu adhika pāṇDuraiḥ = at edges, much, whitish - pure white; snigdhaiḥ = with soft; abhra paṭa cchedaiḥ = cloud, [called] cloth, pieces - bandages; ambaram = sky; baddha vraṇam iva = bandaged, gash, like.

"Those clouds with pure white edges, and with reddish hue of sunset extending on them, look like soft pieces of cloth bandaged on the gashes of sky. [3-28-5]

▼

The sky's parturition in the form of rain is just over and she is bandaged with cotton-like whitish and softish clouds, while the colour of spilt blood is still appearing on those dressings borrowed from the colour of sunset.

Sloka 6

manda māruta niḥśvāsam saṃdhyā caṃdana raṃjitam |
āpāṇḍu jaladam bhāti kāma āturam iva aṃbaram || 3-28-6

6. manda māruta niḥ shvāsam = slothful, breezes, out, breathing - as sighs; sandhyā candana ranjitam = sunset, sandal-paste, tinged with; āpāṇDu = with very white - blanched; jaladam = clouds; floating on her face; ambaram = sky is; kāma āturam iva = one with - love, longing, as if; bhāti = appearing.

"Slothful breezes as her sighs, sunset tingeing her as sandal paste, the sky with blanched clouds on her face appears like the one sighing for love. [3-28-6]

▼

The birthing of rain is over and to give next birth to next rain, the sky is ready as a wooable female with sighs and daubing of sandal pastes to cool off her yearning for love.

Sloka 7

eṣā gharma parikliṣṭā nava vāri pariplutā |
sītā iva śoka saṃtaptā mahī bāṣpam vimuṃcati || 3-28-7

7. gharma pari kliṣṭā = by searing-summer, overly, agonised; nava vāri pari plutā = by new, waters, thoroughly, flooded - wetted; eṣā mahī = this, earth; shoka santaptā = by grief, fully, searing; sītā iva = ṣeetha, like; bāṣpam vi muncati = earth, tears, verily - implacably, outpouring.

"Overly agonised with the searing-summer, but thoroughly wetted with new waters, this earth looks like Seetha, both implacably outpouring tears. [3-28-7]

▼

The earth that becomes heated in summer vents out hot vapours on the fall of first rains releasing its agony of heat. The first act of humans to vent out agony is to shed tears. Now the earth is shedding humid tears and she is like Seetha who is also in the same predicament of earth, as the heat called the atrocity of Ravana sears Seetha.

Sloka 8

megha udara vinirmuktāḥ karpūra dala śītalāḥ |
śakyam aṃjalibhiḥ pātum vātāḥ ketaki gandhinaḥ || 3-28-8

8. megha udara vinirmuktāḥ [vi nir muktaḥ - vātāḥ] = from cloud's, womb, verily, out, freed [breezes; karpūra [kalhāra] dala shītalāḥ = minty-camphor [yellow camphor,] [or, red-lotuses] leaves, with coolness of; ketaki gandhinaḥ = Ketaki plant [Pandanus odora tissimus, ṃogra,] with perfume of; vātāḥ = breezes; anjalibhiḥ pātum shakyam = with palms-bowl, to draught, possible to.

"Freed from the wombs of clouds, cool as the leaves of minty-camphor, perfumed with the fragrance of ṃogra plants, such as these breezes are it is possible to have a draught of them with the bowl of palms. [3-28-8]

Sloka 9

eṣa phulla arjunaḥ śailaḥ ketakaiḥ adhivāsitaḥ |
sugrīva iva śānta ariḥ dhārābhiḥ abhiṣicyate || 3-28-9

9. phulla arjunaḥ = fully bloomed, with Arjuna trees' flowers; ketakaiḥ = with Ketaka trees' flowers; abhi vāsitaḥ = further, fragranced; eṣa shailaḥ = this, mountain; shānta ariḥ = is silenced, whose enemy; sugrīva iva = ṣugreeva, like; dhārābhiḥ abhiṣicyate = downpours, anointing.

"This mountain with fully bloomed Arjuna flowers, further fragranced by Ketaka flowers is being anointed with downpours on a par with Sugreeva, who is anointed when his enemy is silenced. [3-28-9]

▼

This ṃountain Prasavana is full with its blooming Arjuna flowers, like the colourful garlands on the chest of Sugreeva. It is further fragranced with Ketaka flowers, like the fragrant items brought for Sugreeva's anointment. As with Sugreeva who after silencing his enemy Vali is anointed on time with the pouring down of all kinds of waters from golden flasks, this is mountain after silencing its enemy called sun-sear, is also being anointed by rainy downpours.

Sloka 10

megha kṛṣṇa ajina dharā dhārā yajña upavītinaḥ |
māruta āpūrita guhāḥ prādhītā iva parvatāḥ || 3-28-10

10. megha kṛiṣṇa ajina dharā = clouds, black, deerskin, donning; dhārā yajña upavītinaḥ = showers, ritual, mantled with - wearing sacred thread; māruta āpūrita guhāḥ = by breeze, replete with, caves; parvatāḥ = mountains; pra adhītā iva = well, studying - reciting ones [young-scholars,] are like.

"Donning clouds as black deer skins, torrents as sacred-threads, and caves replete with breezes as vocals, the mountains look like young-scholars reciting Veda-s. [3-28-10]

▼

The duty of young scholars of Vedic studies is to wear black deerskin and sacred thread as their initiation into Vedic studies. In these rainy days, there will be no other activity to them than sitting tight to recite their texts. The caves of mountains are its own mouths and they are replete with air and air gives out a din, and even the Vedic recitation gives out a din of its own kind. Thus, the mountains appear to be like humble young-scholars of Veda-s, voicing out their chanting like the hum of the air in caves.

▼

 atra parvatānām kṛiṣhṇa ajina yajñopavīta ādi dharma kalpena adhyetṛiu sādṛishya pratipādanāt adhyetṛiūṇām brahmacāriṇām kṛiṣhṇa ajina yajñopavīta ādi dhāraṇam dharma iti uktam - dk.

Sloka 11

kaśābhiḥ iva haimībhiḥ vidyudbhiḥ iva tāḍitam |
antaḥ stanita nirghoṣam savedanam iva aṃbaram || 3-28-11

12. haimībhiḥ kashābhiḥ iva = with golden, whips, as if; vidyudbhiḥ = by flashes of lightening; [ambaram] tāDitam iva = [sky is] whipped, as though; stanita = rattle of thunder; nir ghoṣam = out letting; ambaram = sky is; antaḥ sa vedanam iva = internally, with, throes, as though having - internally suffering with them.

"While the flashes of lightning are whipping the sky as if they are the golden whips, it is letting out rattle of thunders holding out against its inly throes. [3-28-11]

Sloka 12

nīla megha āśritā vidyut sphuraṃtī pratibhāti me |
sphuraṃtī rāvaṇasya aṃke vaidehī iva tapasvinī || 3-28-12

12. nīla megha āshritā = blue, cloud, hingeing on; sphurantī vidyut = squirming, lightening; rāvaṇasya anke sphurantī = in Ravana's, grasp, squirming; tapasvinī vaidehī iva = penitent - sorry state, Vaidehi, as if; pratibhāti me = appears to be, to me.

"That lightning hingeing on the blue cloud is squirmy, to me it appears like woeful Vaidehi squirming in the grasp of Ravana. [3-28-12]

Sloka 13

imāḥ tā manmathavatām hitāḥ pratihatā diśaḥ |
anuliptā iva ghanaiḥ naṣṭa graha niśā karāḥ || 3-28-13

13. manmathavatām hitāḥ = for lovers having beloveds, [the precincts are] advantageous; pratihatā = [precincts are] marred [unclear]; naṣṭa graha nishā karāḥ = lost, planets, night, maker - moon; imāḥ tāḥ dishaḥ = these, they, directions; ghanaiḥ anuliptā iva = with clouds, begrimed, as though.

"For those who are with their beloveds these precincts are advantageous, as their directions, as to which east and which is west, are unclear; and to loverless ones like me they are disadvantageous, because the same precincts have their planets and even their moon lost, and they look as though begrimed with dark-clouds. [3-28-13]

Sloka 14

kvacit bāṣpa abhisaṃruddhān varṣa āgama samutsukān |
kuṭajān paśya saumitre puṣṭitān giri sānuṣu |
mama śoka abhibhūtasya kāma saṃdīpanān sthitān || 3-28-14

14. saumitre = oh, ṣoumitri; kvacit = somewhere; bāṣpa = tears / droplets of vapour; abhisamruddhān [abhi sam ruddhān] = thickly, covered; varṣa āgama samutsukān = of rainy season, by arrival of, feeling happy; giri sānuṣu sthitān = on mountain, terraces, standing; puṣṭitān = flowered ones; mama shoka abhibhūtasya = my, sorrow, verily, one who is let down; kāma samdīpanān = passion, inciters [plants on mountain]; such plants; kuṭajān pashya = wild-jasmines, you see.

"Feeling happy with the arrival of rain wild jasmine plants standing somewhere on the terraces of mountain are flowered, and they are thickly covered with droplets of vapour, as if they are the tears of happiness hovering round the flower like faces of ladyloves at the arrival of their lovers, and such plants are becoming inciters of passion in me, who am let down by my sorrow for my ladylove. [3-28-14]

▼

While some natural beings are enthused to take delight in the process of nature, brahma anubhava santuṣṭha, or Supreme Self, some others are refuting it. Thus my passion is increasing to bring back those perverts onto correct path.

Sloka 15

rajaḥ praśāṃtam sa himo adya vāyuḥ
nidāgha doṣa prasarāḥ praśāṃtāḥ |
sthitā hi yātrā vasudhā adhipānām
pravāsino yāṃti narāḥ sva deśān || 3-28-15

15. adya = now; rajaḥ prashāntam = dust, is quietened; vāyuḥ sa himaḥ = breeze, together with, chill; nidāgha doṣa prasarāḥ = summer's, vile, pervasions; prashāntāḥ = quietened; vasudhā adhipānām = of land, lords- kings; yātrā = expeditions - conquests; sthitā hi = came to a halt, indeed; pravāsinaḥ narāḥ sva deshān yānti = in outstation, those men who are, one's, own, to countries, they are going - returning.

"In these days the dust is quietened, breeze is chilly, vile pervasions of summer are also quietened, conquests of kings indeed came to a halt, and the people in outstation are returning to their own countries. [3-28-15]

▼

This verse speaks about the one who is blessed by the Supreme Being by way of His beneficence in the form of rain. For such a blessed soul rajaḥ which also means rajo guṇa, 'over-activism...' apart from the meaning of 'dust..' is silenced. This is one among the three attributes sattva, rajaḥ, tamo guṇā-s 'purity, activity, stolidity...' and thus rajaḥ prashāntam 'the quality of over-activity is quietened, along with the quality of stolidity...' thus, only 'purity' is evident in him in his worldly activities. sa himo vāyuḥ 'with, coldness, is air...' the word 'air' denotes 'breathing humans' and those blest human's heart breaths a cool breeze, rather than fumes and fires, that which is the attitude of a person who is dominated by rajo guna 'attribute of over-activity...' Thus, they the blessed souls have no evil effects of searing or scorching of summer. The compound nigadha doṣa... prashantāḥ means worldly affairs, are silenced, where vile of summer is the personified vile of worldly affairs. And even their worldly expeditions to conquer, to possess, and to become significant etc., are stalled for the blessed souls sthitāḥ... adhipānām. They have nothing to do here on earth because they are immigrants onto earth. So, they are returning to their own country, quarters, or abode, pravāsino... sva deshān. Their own place sva desha is vaikunṭha. Thus the divine rain blesses them with salvation.

Sloka 16

saṃprasthitā mānasa vāsa lubdhāḥ
priya anvitāḥ saṃprati cakravākaḥ |
abhīkṣṇa varṣa udaka vikṣateṣu
yānāni mārgeṣu na saṃpatanti || 3-28-16

16. samprati = presently; [hamsāḥ = swans]; mānasa vāsa lubdhāḥ = at Lake ṃānasa, to live, eager to; sam prasthitā = readily, started to journey; cakravākaḥ priya anvitāḥ = Cakravaka [ruddy gees,] with beloved one, conjoined with; abhīkṣṇa varṣa udaka vikṣateṣu = oftentimes, by rainy, waters, verily battered; mārgeṣu = in roadways; yānāni = vehicles; na sam patanti = not, verily, falling - not moving ahead.

"Presently swans have started journey to Lake ṃanasa eager to live there, but the Cakravaka birds, ruddy gees, that are together with their beloveds are not departing, and vehicles are not taking to road as the rainwater has battered the roadways oftentimes. [3-28-16]

▼

The swans do not like muddy waters so they aspire the clear waters of Lake ṃānasa in Himalayas. The great sages, averse to muddy worldly affairs, are also called 'swans, supreme swans' hamsa, parama hamsa for they wander all over acquiring and disseminating spirituality to world. But they take break in rainy season in a vow called cātur māsyam and contemplate in their own manas 'mind' about the Supreme Being. If the swans tend to go to Himalayan ṃānasa Lake, these sagely-swans tend to gain entry into their own mind in search of Supreme. And, like swans that can search and separate milk from water to drink milk alone, the sages can search for their being. The compound sam prasthitā, indicates a journey towards Supreme. And priya anvitā indicates involvement in worldly affairs, nominally indicated here as 'together with their lovers'. Contrarily, those ruddy gees bound by attachments do not flee away, but they will stick to their worldly interests. Thus, this rainy season is sagely, to enable the seekers to seek the Supreme, without any bickerings due from the world.

Sloka 17

kvacit prakāśam kvacid aprakāśam
nabhaḥ prakīrṇā aṃbu dharam vibhāti |
kvacit kvacit parvata saṃniruddham
rūpam yathā śānta mahārṇavasya || 3-28-17

17. pra kīrṇā ambu dharam = widely bestrewn with, water, carriers - clouds; kvacit = somewhere; prakāsham = bright; kvacit = somewhere else; a prakāsham = not, bright; nabhaḥ = sky; kvacit kvacit = somewhere, somewhere else - hear and there; parvata sam niruddham = with mountains, verily, dammed up; shānta mahā arṇavasya = peaceful [tideless,] vast, of ocean; rūpam yathā = in aspect, as with; vi bhāti = well, gleaming.

"Widely bestrewn with clouds the sky is bright somewhere, and not so bright somewhere else, thus it is gleaming well with an aspect of vast but tideless ocean that is by far dammed up with cloudlike mountains. [3-28-17]

▼

The sky, or heaven, is also called Supreme Being, according to the saying kham brahma 'Sky is Brahman.' This is apparently shiny at places where the clouds of ignorance are not hovering on the minds of the discerners, and it is unshiny and unapparent at other places because of the gloom in the mind of the beholder about the disposition of that sky, or Supreme Being.

Sloka 18

vyāmiśritam sarja kadaṃba puṣpaiḥ
navam jalam parvata dhātu tāmram |
mayūra kekābhiḥ anuprayātam
śaila apagāḥ śīghrataram vahanti || 3-28-18

18. shaila apa gāḥ = from mountains, water, goings - mountain rapids; sarja kadamba puṣpaiḥ = with ṣarja, with wild-jasmine, flowers; vyā mishritam = well, coalesced; parvata dhātu tāmram = with mountain's, ores, became coppery; mayūra kekābhiḥ = with peacock's, screeches; anu prayātam = at behind, transiting; such a; navam jalam = new, water; shīghra taram vahanti = swiftly, in higher degree, carrying away - flowing.

"Coalesced with Sarja and Wild-jasmine flowers, and also with the coppery ores of the mountain, the new waters are swiftly transiting towards ocean, while screams of peacocks haunting them. [3-28-18]

▼

The flowers and mineral ores are mingling in waters and flowing as one with the waters. This is indicative of purposeful devotion, sātvika guṇa prathāna bhakti 'devotion in its pureness.' The sattva guṇa from the standpoint of mind is sukha 'devotion in pleasure....' and the rajo guṇa is 'pain...' thus that sort of devotion is rajo guṇa prathāna bhakti is 'devotion in pain...' Thus in pleasure or pain devoutness is evident. Apart from these two, there are some simple devotees. They do not have any higher values/purposes in view, except to extol, praise, and sing for the Ultimate. Thus, the screaming and screeching peacocks indicate such simple devotees. Govindaraja.

Sloka 19

rasa ākulam ṣaṭpada saṃnikāśam
prabhujyate jaṃbu phalam prakāmam |
aneka varṇam pavana avadhūtam
bhūmau patati āmra phalam vipakvam || 3-28-19

19. rasa ākulam = lusciousness, full of; ṣaṭ pada sam nikāsham = six, footed [honeybee,] nearly, equalling - that resemble; jambu phalam = Jambu tree's, fruit - rose-apple; pra kāmam = much, delightedly; pra bhujyate = well, devoured aneka varṇam = diversely, coloured - discoloured; vi pakvam = verily, ripened - over-ripe, putrefied; āmra phalam = mango, fruit; pavana ava dhūtam = by wind, down, heaved; bhūmau patati = on ground, falling;.

"The fruits of rose-apple that resemble honeybees are delightedly devoured as they are highly luscious, but the mango fruits that are discoloured and putrefied are falling to ground heaved down by wind. [3-28-19]

▼

The falling of fruits on their own by the gusts of wind is indicative of the fall of fruits for a true devotee, and to him the ripened fruit is made available. Though Rose apple is an insignificant fruit it is consumed. It has a medicinal property in this rainy season. And though mango is a rich fruit it is not the season to consume it. So it is not picked now. Thus it is becoming a wasted fruit. Fruits of actions are therefore according to actions but not according to their richness or otherwise.

Sloka 20

vidyut patākāḥ sa balāka mālāḥ
śailendra kūṭa ākṛti saṃnikāśāḥ |
garjanti meghāḥ samudīrṇa nādā
matta gajendrā iva saṃyugasthāḥ || 3-28-20

20. vidyut patākāḥ = electric-charges, as [fluttering] flags; sa balāka mālāḥ = with, cranes, as garlands; shaila indra kūṭa ākṛiti sam nikāshāḥ = mountain, lordly, pinnacles, in build, very, similar to; such; meghāḥ = clouds; samyuga sthāḥ = in a fight, standing up to; matta = vigorous; gaja indrā iva = elephant, lordly ones; as with; sam udīrṇa nādā = with verily, high-pitched, voice; garjanti = are thundering.

"Electric-charges as their fluttering flags of war, flights of cranes as their garlands of gallantry, those clouds very similar in their build to the pinnacles of lordly mountains are thundering with very high pitched voices like invigorated lordly elephants standing up to a fight. [3-28-20]

▼

The towering mountains, or the haughty elephants stand tall among all, and their thunderous grumblings are akin to the rumblings of Vedic scholars who stand tall to establish Vedic tenets, though they come to fistfights with opponents.

Sloka 21

varṣa udaka āapyāyita śādvalāni
pravṛtta nṛtta utsava barhiṇāni |
vanāni nirvṛṣṭa balāhakāni
paśya aparāhṇeṣu adhikam vibhānti || 3-28-21

21. varṣa udaka āapyāyita shādvalāni = by rain, water, satiated, pasturelands; pravṛitta nṛitta utsava barhiṇāni = stimulated, for dance, carnivals, of peacocks; nir vṛiṣṭa balāhakāni = completely, rained, by clouds; vanāni = woodlands; aparāhṇeṣu = in afternoons; adhikam vi bhānti = extremely, well, splendorous; pashya = you behold them.

"When clouds have completely rained on the greenery pasturelands are satiated with rainwater and peacocks are stimulated to dancing carnivals, thus it is extremely splendorous in afternoons, behold it. [3-28-21]

41. gajāḥ = elephants - are moving where; pramatta samnādadita barhiṇāni = voluptuously, well, screaming, peahens; sa shakragopa akula shādvalāni = with, red-beetles, overspread, pasturelands; nīpa arjuna vāsitāni = by ṇīpa, Arjuna flowers, fragranced; su ramyāṇi vana antarāṇi = in verily, pleasant, forest, interiors; caranti = [elephants are] moving.

"Elephants are moving about the very pleasant interiors of forests where peahens are voluptuously screaming, pasturelands are overspread with red-beetles, and where they are fragranced with the flowers of Nīpa and Arjuna trees. [3-28-41]

Sloka 42

nava aṃbu dhāra āhata kesarāṇi
drutam parityajya saroruhāṇi |
kadaṃba puṣpāṇi sa kesarāṇi
navāni hṛṣṭā bhramarāḥ pibanti || 3-28-42

42. hṛiṣṭā bhramarāḥ = joyful, honeybees; nava ambu dhāra āhata kesarāṇi = with new, water, streams, lashed, fibrils; saroruhāṇi = lotuses; drutam parityajya = quickly, leaving off; sa kesarāṇi = lotuses that are - with, fibrils; navāni = new ones; kadamba puṣpāṇi pibanti = Kadamba, flowers, quaffing.

"Lashed are the fibrils of lotuses with the downpour of new waters, hence quickly leaving them off the joyful honeybees are quaffing the Kadamba flowers with new fibrils. [3-28-42]

Sloka 43

mattā gajendrā muditā gavendrā
vaneṣu vikrāṃtatarā mṛgendrāḥ |
ramyā nagendrā nibhṛtā narendrāḥ
prakrīḍito vāri dharaiḥ surendraḥ || 3-28-43

43. gaja indrā mattā = elephants, lordly ones, are ruttish; vaneṣu = in forests; gava indrā muditā = bulls, lordly ones, are delighted; mṛiga indrāḥ vikrānta tarā = of animals, lordly ones - lions, are triumphant, to a better degree; naga indrā ramyā = mountains, lordly ones, are heart-pleasing; nara indrāḥ = peoples, lords of; nibhṛitā = withdrawing - from conquests; sura indraḥ = gods, lord; vāri dharaiḥ pra krīDitaḥ = with water, carries [clouds,] well, playing.

"In forests lordly elephants are ruttish, ideal bulls that are delighted, peerless lions are triumphant to a better degree, lofty mountains are heart-pleasant, lords of people are withdrawing from their campaigns, and the lord of gods, Indra, is playing well with water giving clouds. [3-28-43]

Sloka 44

meghāḥ samud bhūta samudra nādā
mahājala oghaiḥ gagana avalaṃbāḥ |
nadīḥ taṭākāni sarāṃsi vāpiḥ
mahīm ca kṛtsnām apavāhayanti || 3-28-44

44. mahā jala oghaiḥ = with great, waters, streams; sam ud dhūta samudra nādā = verily, over, thrown - spurning, ocean's, boom; gagana avalambāḥ = on skies, dangling - soaring; such as they are, the; meghāḥ = clouds; nadīḥ taṭākāni sarāmsi vāpiḥ = rivers, lakes, pools, tanks; kṛitsnām mahīm ca = entire, earth, also; apa vāhayanti = making to outflow - inundating.

"The clouds having torrential water are soaring the sky, and spurning the booms of oceans with their rumbles they are inundating rivers, lakes, pools, tanks and even the earth. [3-28-44]

Sloka 45

varṣa pravegā vipulā patanti
pravānti vātāḥ samudīrṇa vegāḥ |
pranaṣṭa kūlāḥ pravahanti śīghram
nadyo jalam vipratipanna mārgāḥ || 3-28-45

45. vipulā varṣa pravegā patanti = substantially, rains, speedily, falling; samudīrṇa vegāḥ vātāḥ pravānti = intensified, in speediness, gales, are blowing; pra naṣṭa kūlāḥ = verily, damaged - eroded, having riverbanks; vi pratipanna mārgāḥ = contradictory, having their course - out of ordinary course, far afield; nadyaḥ = rivers; shīghram jalam pravahanti = speedily, water, conducting.

"Rains are falling substantially and speedily, gales are blowing intensified in their speediness, while rivers are conducting water speedily over their eroded riverbanks and far afield of their course. [3-28-45]

Sloka 46

naraiḥ narendrā iva parvatendrāḥ
surendra nītaiḥ pavana upanītaiḥ |
ghana aṃbu kumbhaiḥ abhiṣicyamānā
rūpam śriyam svām iva darśayanti || 3-28-46

46. parvata indrāḥ = mountain, lordly; sura indra nītaiḥ = gods, lord - by Indra, brought - sent; pavana upa nītaiḥ = by Air-god, to near, wafted; ghana ambu kumbhaiḥ = cloud, water, with pots of; naraiḥ = by people; nara indrā iva = people's, lord, like; abhiṣicyamānā = while being anointed; rūpam = aspect [of mountains]; svām shriyam iva = one's own, grandeur, as if; darshayanti = showing.

"Lords of people will be anointed by people with water carried in pots on their shoulders, and then those kings show their propitious aspect of a humanly king with applied divinity, whereas the lords of mountains are being anointed with the water sent directly by Indra and hitherto wafted straight by Air-god in the pots called black-clouds, thus these lordly mountains are standing alone as if showing their grandeur and divinity on earth. [3-28-46]

Sloka 47

ghana upagūḍham gaganam na tārā
na bhāskaro darśanam abhyupaiti |
navaiḥ jala oghaiḥ dharaṇī vitṛptā
tamo viliptā na diśaḥ prakāśāḥ || 3-28-47

47. gaganam ghana upa gūDham = sky is, by clouds, closely, enshrouded; bhāskaraḥ darshanam na abhyupaiti = sun, to see, not, getting around - imperceivable; tārā na = stars, not - seen; dharaṇī navaiḥ jala oghaiḥ = earth, with new, water, torrents; vi tṛiptā = fully, satiated; tamaḥ vi liptā dishaḥ na prakāshāḥ = by obscurity, verily, swathed, precincts, not, illuminated.

"Sky is closely enshrouded with clouds, neither sun nor stars are getting around for a glimpse, earth is fully satiated with the torrents of new water, enswathed in obscurity the precincts are unilluminated... [3-28-47]

▼

The statement of 'obscurity and illumination' is suggestive of the Vedic import tamasomā jyotirgamaya 'from darkness lead me unto light...' When the earth itself is satisfied with the gift of nature the earthlings are still hankering after inaccessible desires, thus they cycle through the karmic cycle.

Sloka 48

mahānti kūṭāni mahī dharāṇām
dhārā vidhautāni adhikam vibhānti |
mahā pramāṇaiḥ vipulaiḥ prapātaiḥ
mukta kalāpaiḥ iva laṃbamānaiḥ || 3-28-48

48. mahī dharāṇām = by earth, born ones - of mountains; dhārā vi dhautāni = by torrents, clearly washed; mahānti kūṭāni = paramount, pinnacles; lambamānaiḥ = pendulous; mukta kalāpaiḥ iva = pearl's, necklaces, as if; mahā pramāṇaiḥ vipulaiḥ pra pātaiḥ = of large, volume - voluminous, widespread, that pitches down - torrential rains; adhikam vi bhānti surpassingly, verily, shining forth.

"The paramount pinnacles of the mountains are thoroughly washed by the torrential rains and are shining forth surpassingly, and the voluminous and widespread torrents that are pitching down are like the pearly necklaces pendulous on the chests of mountains. [3-28-48]

Sloka 49

śailopala praskhalamāna vegāḥ
śailottamānām vipulāḥ prapātāḥ |
guhāsu saṃnādita barhiṇāsu
hārā vikīryanta iva avabhānti || 3-28-49

49. shaila upala pra skhalamāna vegāḥ = on mountain, stones, verily, falling, speedily; vipulāḥ prapātāḥ = widespread, torrents; shaila uttamānām = on mountain, lofty ones; samnādita barhiṇāsu = that are with - highly screaming, peacocks; in such; guhāsu = caves; hārā vi kīryanta iva ava bhānti = lavalieres, strewn around, like, well, gleaming.

"Washing the mountain stones speedily the torrents on the lofty mountains are widespread and entering the caves, in which peacocks are highly screaming, and thus they are in the gleam of lavalieres strewn around. [3-28-49]

▼

The mountain rapids are running helter-skelter on the mountain washing the boulders speedily. But the boulders according to their nature obstruct these rapids. When obstructed they have the only recourse to enter the nearest cave. The caves by then are already full with screaming and colourful peacocks. Adding to the lustre of those peacock's variegated colours, the pearly-white water has also entered the caves. Then these three, pearly water, colourful peacocks, blackish caves are giving out sparkles of their own, like necklaces studded with many colourful gems, and if many such pendants are strewn around, each of its studded stone emits its own colour.

Sloka 50

śīghra pravegā vipulāḥ prapātā
nirdhauta śṛṃga upatalā girīṇām |
muktā kalāpa pratimāḥ patanto
mahā guha ustsaṃga talaiḥ dhriyante || 3-28-50

50. shīghra pravegā = having high, speed; vipulāḥ = wide ones; girīṇām = of mountains; nir dhauta shṛinga upa talā = on neatly, washing, on crest of mountains, nearby, planes; muktā kalāpa pratimāḥ = pearly, necklaces, in shape; patantaḥ = that are falling; prapātā = waterfalls; mahā guha utsanga talaiḥ = cavernous, caves, in recess's [surfaces,] planes; dhriyante = wearing.

"On neatly washing the crests of wide mountains and their nearby planes, the high-speeded waterfalls are falling like pearl-necklaces and the mountains are wearing them at the planes of their cavernous caves. [3-28-50]

▼

The mountains are noblemen in simile. The mountain rapids are pearly-necklaces firstly falling around their necks, and then drooping onto their chests, thus culminating at their locket-position, i.e., at the caves, on the chest of mountains. Those caves are glittering with the colours of peacocks, pearly water and blackish caves. Thus the utsanga 'heart of hearts...' these noblemen's hearts of hearts are covered with such necklaces and lockets.

Sloka 51

suratām arda vicchinnāḥ svarga strī hāra mauktikāḥ |
patanti ca atulāḥ dikṣu toya dhārāḥ samantataḥ || 3-28-51

51. suratām arda vicchinnāḥ = in lovemaking, in friction, broke apart; svarga strī hāra mauktikāḥ = heavenly, females', strings, of pearls; atulāḥ toya dhārāḥ = excellent, water, streams; samantataḥ dikshu = all over, in directions; patanti = are falling.

"Like the strings of pearls of heavenly females plopping down when broken in the friction of lovemaking, excellent streams of water are falling all over and in all directions. [3-28-51]

Sloka 52

vilīyamānaiḥ vihagaiḥ nimīladbhiḥ ca paṃkajaiḥ |
vikasantyā ca mālatyā gato astam jñāyate raviḥ || 3-28-52

52. vilīyamānaiḥ vihagaiḥ = by crouching in - returning to nests, of birds; nimīladbhiḥ pankajaiḥ ca = doubling up, with lotuses, also; vikasantyā mālatyā ca = blooming up, of Jasmine flowers, also with; raviḥ astam gataḥ jñāyate = sun, into dusk, gone, known to be.

"With the return of birds to nests, doubling up of lotuses, and blooming up jasmines, the sun is know to have gone into dusk. [3-28-52]

Sloka 53

vṛttā yātrā narendrāṇām senā pathi eva vartate |
vairāṇi caiva mārgāḥ ca salilena samīkṛtāḥ || 3-28-53

53. nara indrāṇām yātrā vṛittā = people's, lords - of kings, campaigns, reversed; senā pathi eva vartate = armies, on path, only, remaining - marooned; salilena = by water; vairāṇi caiva mārgāḥ ca = conflicts, and also, roadways; samīkṛitāḥ = evened out - neutralised.

"Reversed are the campaigns of kings as the armies are marooned and thus rainwater has neutralised conflicts and roadways. [3-28-53]

Sloka 54

māsi prauṣṭhapade brahma brāhmaṇānām vivakṣatām |
ayam adhyāya samayaḥ sāmagānām upasthitaḥ || 3-28-54

54. prauṣṭhapade māsi = Bhādrapada, in month; brahma = for Vedic-studies; vivakṣatām = having concern; sāma gānām = ṣama Veda, those that sing; brāhmaṇānām = for such Brahmans; ayam adhyāya samayaḥ = this one, learnable, time; upasthitaḥ = has come forth.

"The month of Bhādrapada, August-September, is the time for learning the singing of Sama Veda, and this is the time that has come for those Brahmans who concern themselves for learning the singing of Sama Veda. [3-28-54]

▼

During this time a vow called cātur māsya vrata will be observed by all adherers of Veda-s, and during this time young celibates are initiated into Vedic studies which commences on bhādrapada shukla tṛitīya 'third day in the white-fortnight of this Bhādrapada month.'

Sloka 55

nivṛtta karma āyatano nūnam saṃcita saṃcayaḥ |
āṣāḍhīm abhyupagato bharataḥ kosala adhipaḥ || 3-28-55

55. nivṛitta karma āyatanaḥ = one who has carried out, affairs, domestic; sancita sancayaḥ = garnering, goods; kosala adhipaḥ bharataḥ = Kosala's, king, Bharata; āṣāDhīm = full moon day - pertaining to āṣādha month; abhyupagataḥ = reached - undertaken; nūnam = definitely.

"On carrying out all the domestic affairs and completely garnering the goods for rainy season, Bharata, the king of Kosala, might have definitely undertaken the 'four-month-vow' on the full moon day of aṣāDha month. [3-28-55]

Sloka 56

nūnam āpūryamāṇāyāḥ sarayvā vadhate rayaḥ |
mām samīkṣya samāyāntam ayodhyāyā iva svanaḥ || 3-28-56

56. ā pūrya māṇāyāḥ = with water - being fully filled; sarayvā = of River ṣarayu; rayaḥ = swashing; sam āyāntam = verily, on coming back; mām samīkṣya = me, on beholding; ayodhyāyā svanaḥ iva = Ayodhya's, commotion, as with; nūnam vardhate = definitely, must be increasing.

"Definitely the River Sarayu must be overfilling and its swashing must be mounting as with the commotion of Ayodhya on beholding my returning to city. [3-28-56]

▼

Ayodhya and its people used to create hubbubs whenever Rama used to return to city after his short trips to outside. Such is the affection of the inmates of Ayodhya to Rama, and now Rama is reminiscing that hubbub of people and swashing of Sarayu, as both soundalike.

Sloka 57

imāḥ sphīta guṇā varṣāḥ sugrīvaḥ sukham aśnute |
vijita ariḥ sa dāraḥ ca rājye mahati ca sthitaḥ || 3-28-57

57. vi jita ariḥ = one who has - verily, conquered, enemy - ṣugreeva on conquering Vali; sa dāraḥ ca = with, wife, also; mahati rājye sthitaḥ ca = in an admirable, kingdom, instituted; such; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; sphīta guṇā imāḥ varṣāḥ = that have - manifold, merits - inbuilt facilities, these, rains - in rainy season; sukham ashnute = luxuries, he partakes - he luxuriate in.

"This rainy season as such has manifold inbuilt facilities for self-contained entertainment, so Sugreeva must be luxuriating in them, besides, revelling for conquering his enemy, regaining his wife, and instituting himself as a king of an admirable kingdom. [3-28-57]

Sloka 58

aham tu hṛta dāraḥ ca rājyāt ca mahataḥ cyutaḥ |
nadī kūlam iva klinnam avasīdāmi lakṣmaṇa || 3-28-58

58. lakṣmaṇa = Lakshmana; hṛita dāraḥ ca = with stolen, wife; mahataḥ rājyāt cyutaḥ ca = from a great, kingdom, fallen - toppled from; such as I am; aham tu = I am, on my part - as for me; klinnam nadī kūlam iva = wetted - toppled over, river, bank, like; avasīdāmi = I am sinking down.

"As for me, Lakshmana, I am the one who is toppled from a great kingdom, whose wife is stolen, such as I am, I am toppling down like a riverbank that is toppled over. [3-28-58]

Sloka 59

śokaḥ ca mama vistīrṇo varṣāḥ ca bhṛśa durgamāḥ |
rāvaṇaḥ ca mahān śatruḥ apāram pratibhāti me || 3-28-59

59. mama shokaḥ ca vistīrṇaḥ = my, anguish, is also, boundless; varṣāḥ ca bhṛisha dur gamāḥ = rains, also, definitely, not, passable - pathways are impassable; rāvaṇaḥ ca mahān shatruḥ = Ravana is, but, formidable, enemy; me = to me; a pāram = not, possible to cross over - not, negotiable - insurmountable obstacles; pratibhāti = appears, to me.

"Also my anguish is boundless, the rains have definitely made the pathways impassable, my enemy Ravana is a formidable one, thus these three appear to me as insurmountable obstacles. [3-28-59]

Sloka 60

ayātrām caiva dṛṣṭvā imām mārgām ca bhṛśa durgamān |
praṇate caiva sugrīve na mayā kiṃcit īritam || 3-28-60

60. sugrīve praṇate caiva = ṣugreeva is, very, humble, also thus; imām = this; a yātrām = not, possible for a campaign; bhṛisha durgamān = highly, impassable; mārgām ca = pathways, also; dṛiṣṭvā = on visualising; mayā kimcit na īritam = by me, anything, not, uttered.

"Though Sugreeva is very humble to embark on a campaign, visualising the rainy season as an impossible one for any campaign, and even the pathways are rendered highly impassable I have not said anything to him in the affirmative. [3-28-60]

Sloka 61

api ca ati parikliṣṭam cirāt dāraiḥ samāgatam |
ātma kārya garīyastvāt vaktum na icchāmi vānaram || 3-28-61

61. api ca = even, so; ati pari kliṣṭam = much, entirely, difficulties - that ṣugreeva has undergone; cirāt dāraiḥ samāgatam = after a long while, with wife, united with; vānaram = to such vanara; ātma kārya garīyastvāt = our, work, owing to loftiness of due to magnitude involved in our task; vaktum na icchāmi = to speak [insist,] not, I desired.

"Even Sugreeva has undergone many difficulties in their entirety, after a long while he is united with his wife, and even owing to the magnitude involved in our task of tracking down Ravana I did not desire to insist on that monkey. [3-28-61]

Sloka 62

svayam eva hi viśramya jñātvā kālam upāgatam |
upakāram ca sugrīvo vetsyate na atra saṃśayaḥ || 3-28-62

62. sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; vishramya = after respite; kālam upa āgatam jñātvā = time's, closely, coming in, on realising; upakāram ca = requital, also; svayam eva vetsyate hi = on his own, thus, will know, isn't it; atra samshayaḥ na = = in that, doubt, is not there.

"After a respite Sugreeva will realise on his own that the time has come, and he will also realise the he has something to requite, isn't it! So, there needn't be any doubt in that matter. [3-28-62]

Sloka 63

tasmāt kāla pratīkṣo aham sthito asmi śubha lakṣaṇa |
sugrīvasya nadīnām ca prasādam abhikāṃkṣayan || 3-28-63

63. shubha lakṣaṇa = oh, one with felicitous aspect, Lakshmana; tasmāt = therefore; aham = I am; sugrīvasya nadīnām ca = of ṣugreeva, of rivers, also; prasādam abhikānkshayan = generosity, expectant of; sthitaḥ kāla pratīkṣaḥ asmi = abiding, time, looking forward to, I am.

"I therefore stay abidingly looking forward for the time, oh, Lakshmana with felicitous aspects, expectant of the generosity of Sugreeva and of these rivers. [3-28-63]

Sloka 64

upakāreṇa vīro hi pratikāreṇa yujyate |
akṛtajño apratikṛto hanti sattvavatām manaḥ || 3-28-64

64. upakāreṇa = one who extends - succour; viraḥ tu = valiant one, on his part; pratikāreṇa yujyate = with requital, conjoined - indebted with; a kṛitajñaḥ = un, faithful one; further; a pratikṛitaḥ = who does not, requite; satvavatām manaḥ = those with soft, hearts; hanti = wounds.

"A valiant one who is succoured will be indebted with some requital on his part, but besides being unfaithful, if one who does not make requital for all the favours done in his interest, he will be wounding the hearts of soft-hearted people." Rama said so to Lakshmana, keeping Sugreeva in view. [3-28-64]

Sloka 65

atha evam uktaḥ praṇidhāya lakṣmaṇaḥ
kṛta aṃjaliḥ tat pratipūjya bhāṣitam |
uvāca rāmam svabhirāma darśanam
pradarśayan darśanam ātmanaḥ śubham || 3-28-65

65. lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; tat eva = that, alone - what Rama said about their waiting for cessation of rains; yuktam praṇidhāya = as candid, visualising; kṛita anjali = making, adjoined-palms; tat bhāṣitam pratipūjaya = that, what is said by Rama, honouring - praisefully; ātmanaḥ shubham darshanam pradarshayan = his own, opportune, viewpoint, to clarify; sva abhi rāma darshanam = one with his - own, best, delightful, aspect - Rama; rāmam uvāca = to Rama, said.

Visualising what Rama said about their waiting for pre-winter season to be candid, and praisefully making palm-fold at that determination of Rama, and even to clarify his own opportune viewpoint, Lakshmana spoke to Rama who by his own aspect is a delightful one for a ken. [3-28-65]

Sloka 66

yat uktam etat tava sarvam īpsitam
nara indra kartā nacirā hari īśvaraḥ |
śarat pratīkṣaḥ kṣamatām imam bhavān
jala prapātam ripu nigrahe dhṛtaḥ || 3-28-66

66. narendra = oh, lord of people; yathā uktam = as, said; like that; vānaraḥ = vanara - ṣugreeva; na cirāt tu = not, lately, but; tava īpsitam = by you, desired; etat sarvam kartā = everything, completely, he will be doing; ripu nigrahe = enemy, in disciplining - elimination; dhṛitaḥ bhavān = steadfadt, as you are; sharat pratīkshaḥ = autumn, while awaiting; imam = this; jala pra pātam = waters, heavy, falling - rainy season; kshamatām = you may bear with.

"Oh, lord of people, Sugreeva will be effectuating everything you have desired and said, not later but soon, hence awaiting autumn you may please bear up these heavy rainfalls, besides bearing with the idea of disciplining the enemy." So said Lakshmana to Rama. [3-28-66]

▼

The last three verses are a repeat of 45, 46, and 47th verses of ch 27 of this Kishkindha kānda. ṃany of the verses in this canto bear the blemish of interpolations.

Thus, this is the 28th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
