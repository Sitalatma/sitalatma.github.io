Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 20
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

As per the advice of Sardula, Ravana sends Shuka to Sugriva as an ambassador. The monkeys harass him, who came to their place as a spy. But because of Rama's gracefulness, Shuka comes out of danger. Sugriva gives a fitting reply to Ravana's message sent through Shuka. When monkeys again try to bind him and harass him, Rama prevents them from killing him.

Sloka 1

tato niviṣṭām dhvajinīm sugrīveṇābhipālitām |
dadarśa rākṣaso.abhyetya śārdūlo nāma vīryavān || 6-20-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; vīryavān = an energic;; rākshhasaḥ = demon; shārdūlo nāma = named ṣhardula; abhyetya = came; dadarsha = and saw; dhvajinīm = army; abhipālitam = ruled; sugriveṇa = by ṣugriva; nivishhṭām = which was drawn up in battle array.

Thereafter, an energetic demon named Shardula came and saw the army, commanded by Sugriva, which was drawn up in a battle array.

Sloka 2

cāro rākṣasarājasya rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ |
tām dṛṣṭvā sarvato.avyagram pratigamya sa rākṣasaḥ || 6-20-2
āviśya laṅkām vegena rājānamidamabravīt |

2. saḥ rākshhasaḥ = that demon; chāraḥ = a spy; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rākshhasarājasya = the king of demons; durātmanaḥ = the evil natured; avyagraḥ = coolly; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam = that army; sarvataḥ = from all sides; pratigamya = returned; vegena = speedily; āvishya = entered; laṇkām = Lanka; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these words; rājānam = to the king.

That demon, the spy of Ravana the evil natured king of ogres, coolly observed that army from all sides, speedily returned to Lanka and spoke to their king as follows:

Sloka 3

eṣa vai vānararkṣagho laṅkām samabhivartate || 6-20-3
agādhaścāprameyaśca dvitīya iva sāgaraḥ |

3. agādhashcha = boundless; aprameyashcha = and immeasurable; dvitīyaḥ sāgaraḥ iva = like a second ocean; eshhaḥ = this; vānararkshhoughaḥ = multitude of monkeys and bears; samabhivartate = is approaching ; laṇkām = Lanka.

"Boundless and immeasurable like another ocean, this multitude of monkeys and bears is approaching Lanka.".

Sloka 4

putrau daśarathasyemau bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau || 6-20-4
uttamau rūpasampannau sītāyāḥ padamāgatau |
etau sāgaramāsādya samniviṣṭau mahādyutī || 6-20-5

4; 5. imou = these; bhrātarou = brothers; rāmalakshhmaṇou = Rama and Lakshmana; putrou = the sons; dasharathasya = of Dasaratha; uttamou = the excellent men; rūpasampannou = endowed with beauty; mahādyuti = very much dignified men; āgatou = have come; padam = in the cause; sītāyāḥ = of sita; sannivishhṭā = and settled in camp; āsadya = after reaching; sāgaram = the sea.

"These brothers Rama and Lakshmana, the sons of Dasaratha, the excellent and the most dignified men endowed with beauty have arrived in the cause of Sita and settled in a camp after reaching the sea shore" .

Sloka 6

balam cākāśamāvṛtya sarvato daśayojanam |
tattvabhūtam mahāraja kṣipram veditumarhasi || 6-20-6

6. mahārāja = O; monarch! Balamcha = the army; āvṛitya = is employed around; ākāsham = a space; āyatam = measuring; sarvataḥ = on all sides; dashayojanam = ten yojanas(ten miles);archasi = you are fit; veditum = to ascertain; kshhipram = quickly; tattvabhūtam = this truth.

"O, monarch! Their army is employed around a radius of ten yojanas (eighty miles)on all sides. You have to ascertain this truth quickly"

Sloka 7

tava dūtā mahārāja kṣipramarhanti veditum |
upapradānam sāntvam vā bhedo vātra prayujyatām || 6-20-7

7. mahārajaḥ = O; monarch! ṭava = your; dūtāḥ = spies; arhanti = are fit; veditum = to know; kshhipram = quickly; atra = under such circumstances; sāntvamvā = either conciliation;upapradānam = or an act of giving away;bhedovā = or sowing dissention (in the enemy's ranks); prayujyatām = may be employed.

"O, monarch! Your other spies also ought to ascertain the fact quickly. Under such circumstances, either conciliation or an act of giving away or sowing dissention (in the enemy's ranks) may be employed."

Sloka 8

śārdūlasya vacaḥ śrutvā rāvaṇo rākṣaseśvaraḥ |
uvāca sahasā vyagraḥ sampradhāryārthamātmanaḥ || 6-20-8
śukam nāma tadā rakṣo vākyamarthavidām varam |

8. shrutvā = hearing; shārdūlasya = shardula's; vachaḥ = words; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākshhaseshvaraḥ = the lord of demons; sahasā = immediately; vyāghraḥ = disturbed; sampradhārya = determined; ātmānaḥ = his; artham = course of action; tadā = and then; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = words; rakshhaḥ = to a demon; shukam nāma = by name ṣuka; varam = the foremost among; arthavidām = those who know their duty.

Hearing Shardula's words Ravana the lord of demons was perturbed and immediately determined his cause of action. Then he spoke the following words to a demon by name, Shuka the foremost among those who know their duty.

Sloka 9

sugrīvam brūhi gatvāśu rājānam vacanānmanu || 6-20-9
yathāsaṃdeśamaklībam śalksṇayā parayā girā |

9. gatvā = going; āshu = quickly; brūhi = speak; aklībam = fearlessly; rājānam = to the king; sugrīvam = ṣugriva; mama = as my; vachanāt = words; yatha samdesham = according to my command; parayā = in an excellent; shlakshhṇayā = and gentle; girā = voice:

"Going quickly, speak fearlessly to king Sugriva on my behalf, according to my command, in an excellent and gentle voice, as follows:"

Sloka 10

tvam vai mahāraja kulaprasūto |
mahābalaścarkṣarajaḥsutaśca |
na kaścanārthastava vāstyanartha |
stathāpi me bhrātṛsamo harīśa || 6-20-10

10. mahārāja = O; great king! tvam = you; kulaprasūtaḥ vai = are indeed born in a noble family; mahābalashcha = possessing great strength; rikshharajaḥ sutashcha = are a son of Riksharaja; nāsti = no; arthaḥ = gain; kashchana = whatsoever; anarthaḥ = nor any harm; tava = to you(from this battle); harīsha = O; lord of monkeys; tathāpi = nevertheless; bhrātrusamah = you are like a brother; me = to me.

O, great king! You are indeed born in a noble family, possessor of a great strength and are a son of Riksharaja. No gain whatsoever or any harm will accrue to you (from this battle) .O, lord of monkeys! Nevertheless, you are like a brother to me."

Sloka 11

aham yadyaharam bhāryām rājaputrasya dhīmataḥ |
kim tatra tava sugrīva kiśkindhām prati gamyatām || 6-20-11

11. sugriva = O; ṣugriva; kim = what (does it matter); tava = to you; tatra = there; aham aharamyadi = if I have embezzled; bhāryām = the wife ; rājaputrasya = of prince Rama; dhīmataḥ = the wise? Gamyatām = return; kishhkindhām prati = to Kishkinda.

"What does it matter to you, if I have embezzled the wife of a wise prince? (Therefore) return to Kishkindha"

Sloka 12

na hīyam haribhirlaṅkā prāptum śakyā katham cana |
devairapi sagandharvaiḥ kim punarnaravānaraiḥ || 6-20-12

12. iyam laṇkā = this Lanka; na shakyāhi = cannot indeed; prāptum = be acquired; haribhiḥ = by monkeys; kathamchana = in any way. (It cannot be reached) devairapi = even by celestials; sagandharvaiḥ = together with Gandharvas; kim punaḥ = what to tell; nara vānaraiḥ = of reaching by men and monkeys?

"This Lanka cannot be reached by monkeys in any way. It cannot be acquired even by celestials and Gandharvas (celestial musicians), why to talk about men and monkeys?"

Sloka 13

sa tadā rākṣasendreṇa saṃdiṣṭo rajanīcaraḥ |
śuko vihamgamo bhūtvā tūrṇamāplutya cāmbaram || 6-20-13

13. tadā = then; shukaḥ = ṣhuka; sah = that; rajanīcharaḥ = demon; samdishhṭah = heralded; rākshhasendreṇa = by Ravana; bhūtvā = become; vihaṇgamaḥ = a bird; āplutyacha = and flew; tūrṇam = quickly; ambaram = into the sky.

Then Shuka the demon heralded by Ravana turned himself into the form of a bird and quickly flew into the sky.

Sloka 14

sa gatvā dūramadhvānamuparyupari sāgaram |
saṃsthito hyambare vākyam sugrīva madimabravīt || 6-20-14

14. saḥ = that ṣhuka; gatvā = going; adhvānam = in the sky; dūram = for a distance; uparyupari sāgaram = continuously over the ocean; samsthitaḥ = and stationed; ambare = in the sky(itself); abravīt = spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words; sugrīvam = to ṣugriva.

Proceeding in the sky for a distance continuously over the ocean, Shuka then stood up in the sky itself and spoke these words to Sugriva:

Sloka 15

sarvamuktam yathādiṣṭam rāvaṇena durātmanā |
tatprapayantam vacanam tūrṇamāplutya vānarāḥ || 6-20-15
prāpadyanta tadā kṣipram loptum hantum ca muṣṭibhiḥ |

15. yathā = As was ; ādishhṭam = directed; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; durātmanā = the wicked; sarvam = all that; uktam = was told; prāpayantam = while receiving ; tat = those; vachanam = words; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; āplutya = jumped up; prāpadyanta = reached him; tadā = at that moment; kshhipram = quickly; tūrṇam = and speedily; loptum = to tear off his wings; hantumcha = and to strike him; mushhṭibhiḥ = by their fists.

Shuka repeated all the words the wicked Ravana had told him to say to Sugriva. As he was still speaking, the monkeys bounded into the air to reach him at that moment quickly to tear off his wings and to smite him by their fists.

Sloka 16

sarvaiḥ plavaṇgaiḥ prasabham nigṛhīto niśācarah || 6-20-16
gaganādbhūtale cāśu pratigṛhyāvatāritaḥ |

16. nigṛihītaḥ = the attacked; nishhācharaḥ = demon; prasabham = was forcibly; pratigṛihya = seized; sarvaiḥ = by all; plavaṇgaiḥ = monkeys; āshu = and immediately; avatāritaḥ = brought down; bhūtale = to the ground; gaganāt = from the sky.

The demon thus attacked was forcibly seized by all the monkeys and immediately brought down to the ground from the sky.

Sloka 17

vānaraiḥ pīḍyamānastu śuko vacanamabravīt || 6-20-17
na dūtān ghnanti kākutthsa vāryantām sādhu vānarāḥ |

17. shukaḥ = ṣhuka; pīDyamānaḥ = being harassed; vānaraiḥ = by monkeys; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; kakutthsa = O; Rama! Dūtān = messengers; naghnanti = are not killed; vānarāḥ = let the monkeys; vāryantām = be restrained; sādhu = properly.

Shuka being harassed by monkeys spoke the following words: "O, Rama! ṃessengers are not to be killed. Let your monkeys be restrained properly."

Sloka 18

yastu hitvā matam bhartuḥ svamatam samprabhāṣate || 6-20-18
anuktavādī dūtaḥ sann sa dūto vadhamarhati |

18. yaḥ = who; hitvā = abandon; matam = the intent; bhartuḥ = of his lord; samprabhāshhate = will speak; svamatam = about his own intent; dūtaḥ sau = through being a messenger; saḥ dūtaḥ = that messenger; anukta vādī = who speaks which was not told; arhati = is fit; vadham = for killing

"That messenger, who by abandoning the opinion of his king will speak about his own opinion though he being a messenger and he who on the other hand speaks that which was not told to him, that messenger is indeed fit to be killed."

Sloka 19

śukasya vacanam rāmaḥ śrutvā tu paridevitam || 6-20-19
uvāca mā vadhisṭeti ghnataḥ śākhāmṛgarṣabhān |

19. shrutvā = hearing; shukasya = ṣhuka's; vachanam = words; paridevitam = and his complaint; rāmaḥ = Rama;uvāchām = spoke; iti = thus; shakhā mṛigarshhabhān = to the foremost of monkeys; ghnatah = who were beating; mā vadhishhṭa = "do not kill."

Hearing Shuka's complaining words, Rama spoke to the foremost of monkeys, who were beating him as aforesaid, saying "Do not kill him"

Sloka 20

sa ca patralaghudbhūtvā haribhirdarśite.abhaye || 6-20-20
antarikṣe sthito bhūtvā punarvacana mabravīt |

20. darshite = perceiving; abhaye = no fear; haribhiḥ = from the monkeys; saḥ cha = he; bhūtvā = became; patra laghuḥ = light winged; bhūtvā = virtually; sthitaḥ = standing; antarikshhe = in the sky; abravīt = and spoke; vachanam = these words; punaḥ = again:

Perceiving no fear from monkeys, Shuka became light winged, virtually standing in the sky and spoke again the following words.

Sloka 21

sugrīva sattvasampanna mahābalaparākrama || 6-20-21
kim mayā khalu naktavyo rāvaṇo lokarāvaṇaḥ |

21. sugrīva = O;ṣugriva; sattvasampannaḥ = rich in courage; mahābalaparākrama = possessing strength and great valor; kim = what; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; lokarāvaṇah = who causes the world to cry; vaktvyaḥ = is to be told; mayā = by me?

"O, Sugriva, rich in courage and possessing great strength and valor! What am I to tell Ravana, whose nature is to cause world to cry?"

Sloka 22

sa evamuktaḥ plavagādhipastadā |
plavaṅgamānāmṛṣabho mahābalaḥ |
uvāca vākyam rajanīcarasya |
cāram śukam śuddha madīnasattvaḥ || 6-20-22

22. evam = thus; uktvā = being told; mahābalaḥ = the mighty strong; saḥ = ṣugriva; plavagādhipaḥ = the king of monkeys; plavaṇgamānām Rishhabhaḥ = and the foremost among the monkeys; tadā = then; adīna sattvaḥ = with a merciless mind; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = these words; shuddham shukam = to the blame less ṣhuka; rajanīcharasya = Ravana's; chāram = spy:

Hearing the aforesaid words, the mightily strong Sugriva the king of monkeys and the foremost among them, with a merciless mind then spoke to the blame less Shuka, Ravana's spy, as follows:

Sloka 23

sa me.asi mitram va tathānukampyo |
na copakartāsi na me priyo.api |
ariśca rāmasya sahānubandha |
stato.asi vālīva vadhārha vadhyaḥ || 6-20-23

23. vadhārha = O; Ravana; worthy of killing! ṇa asi = you are not; me = my; mitram = friend; tathā = and; na = you are not; anukampyaḥ = worthy of sympathy; na asi = you are not; upakartācha = the person who helps; na api = you are not; priyaḥ = beloved; me = to me; (you are); rāmasya = Rama's; ariḥ = enemy; tataḥ = therefore; asi = you are; vadhyaḥ = enemy to be killed; sahānubandhaḥ = along with your associates; vālina = like Vali.

"O, Ravana, worthy of killing! You are not my companion. You are not worthy of sympathy. You are not the person who helped me in any way. I do not like you, as you are Rama's enemy. Hence, You are worthy to be killed like Vali, along with your associates"

Sloka 24

nihanmyaham tvām sasutam sabandhum |
sajñātivargam rajanīcareśa |
laṅkām ca sarvām mahatā balena |
sarvaiḥ kariṣyāmi sametya bhasma || 6-20-24

24. rajanīcharesha = O; the king of demons! aham = I; nihanmi = am killing; tvām = you; sasutam = along with your sons; sabandhum = along with your relatives; sa jṇ^āti vargam = along with your kinsfolk; sametya = together; mahatā balena = with my great army; karishhyāmi = I will make; sarvām = the entire; laṇkām = Lanka; sarvaḥ = along with all of you; bhasma = to ashes.

"O, the king of demons! I am killing you along with your sons, relatives and other kinsfolk. Arriving with a great army, I will reduce the entire Lanka and all of you to ashes."

Sloka 25

na mokṣyase rāvaṇa rāghavasya |
sarvaiḥ sahendraidapi mūḍha guptaḥ |
antarhataḥ sūryapatham gato.api|
tathaiva pātālamanupraviṣṭaḥ || 6-20-25
girīśapādambujasamgato vā |
hato.asi rāmeṇa śānujastvam || 6-20-26

25; 26. rāvaṇa = O; Ravana; mūDha = the stupid! ṭvam = you; na mokshhyase = will not be left; sahānujaḥ = along with your younger brother; rāghavasya = by Rama; guptaḥ api = even if protected; sarvaiḥ = by all; sahendraiḥ = including Indra; antarhitaḥ = even if disappeared; gatovā = or obtained; sūryapatham = the solar path; tathaiva = and so also; anupravishhṭaḥ = if you entered; pātalam = the nethermost subterranean region; gīrīsha pādāmmbuja sangatovā = or even if approached the lotus feet of ṣhiva( the lord of Kailasa mountain).

"O, stupid Ravana! You along with your younger brother will not be left unkilled by Rama, even if you are protected by all including Indra (the lord of celestials) or even if you have disappeared (by virtue of conjuring trick) or obtained, the solar orbit or entered the nethermost subterranean region or even if approached the lotus feet of Shiva (the lord of Kailasa mountain)"

Sloka 27

tasya te triṣu lokeṣu na piśācam na rākṣasam |
trātāramanupaśyāmi na gandharvam na cāsuram || 6-20-27

27. na anupashyāmi = I do not see; trātāram = any protector; te = to you; tasya = as such; trishhu lokeshhu = in the three worlds; na = nor do I see; pishācham = a devilish being; na = nor; rākshhasam = a demon; na = nor; gandharvam = a Gandhrva (celestial musician); na = nor; asuramcha = an ogre.

"I do not see any protector to you as such in the three worlds, nor do I see any devilish being or a demon or a Gandharva (celestial musician) or an ogre coming forward to protect you."

Sloka 28

avadhīstvam jarāvṛddham gṛdhrārājam jaṭāyuṣam |
kim mate rāmasāmnidhye sakāśe lakṣmaṇasya ca || 6-20-28
hṛtā sītā viśālākṣi yām tvam gṛhya na budhyase |

28. tvam = you; avadhīḥ = killed; jaṭāyushham = Jatayu; gṛidhrarājam = the king of vultures; jarāvṛiddham = and who was senior due to old age; kimnu = why; visālākshhī = the wide eyed; sītā = ṣita; nahṛitā = was not taken away; rāma sānnidhye = in the presence of Rama; sakāshe = and in the presence; lakshhmaṇa = of Lakshmana; tvam = you; gṛihya = having seized her; na budhyase = do not recognize her; yām = what she is.

"You killed Jatayu, the king of vultures and who was senior to you in old age. Why the wide-eyed Sita was not taken away in the presence of Rama and Lakshmana? Having made her captive, you do not recognize her for what she is."

Sloka 29

mahābalam mahātmānam durādharṣam surairapi || 6-20-29
na budhyase raghuśreṣṭham yaste prāṇān hariṣyati |

29. na budhyase = you are unaware; mahābalam = how much strong; mahātmānam = high soled; durādarshham = and irresistible; surai rapi = even to celestials; Raghu shreshhṭam = is this excellent man of Raghu dynasty; yaḥ = who; harishhyati = shall deprive; te = you; prāṇān = of your lives.

"You are unaware how strong, high soled and irresistible even to celestials, is this Rama the excellent man of Raghu dynasty, who shall deprive you of your life."

Sloka 30

tato.abravīdvālisuto.apyaṅgado harisattamaḥ || 6-20-30
vāyam dūto mahāprājña cārakaḥ pratibhāti me |

30. tataḥ = thereafter; angadaḥ = Angada; vālisutaḥ = the son of Vali; harisattamaḥ = and the foremost of the monkeys; abravīt = spoke; (as follows) mahā prajṇ^a = O; ṣugriva the highly intelligent! ayam = ḥe; na = is not; dūtaḥ = an ambassador; pratibhāti = he appears; me = to me; chārakaḥ = as a spy.

Thereafter, Angada the son of Vali and the foremost of the monkeys spoke as follows: "O, Sugriva the highly intelligent! He is not an ambassador. He appears to me as a spy."

Sloka 31

tulitam hi balam sarvamanena tava tiṣṭhatā || 6-20-31
gṛhyatām māgamallaṅkā metaddhi mama rocate |

31. sarvam = all; balam = our army; tulitam hi = indeed has been evaluated; anena = by him; tishhṭhatā = standing here; gRi^hyatām = let him be made captive; mā gamāt = le him not return; laṇkām = to Lanka; etat hi = this indeed; rochate = finds favor; mama = with me.

"All our army has been indeed evaluated by him, standing here. Let him be made captive. Let him not return to Lanka. This indeed finds favor with me."

Sloka 32

tato rājñā samādiṣṭāḥ samutpatya valīmukhāḥ || 6-20-32
jagṛhuśca babandhuśca vilapantamanāthavat |

32. tataḥ = then; samādishhṭāḥ = commanded; rājṇ^ā = by the king ṣugriva; valīmukhāḥ = the monkeys; samutpatya = jumped up; jagṛihushcha = seized; babandhushcha = and bound him; anāthavat = who without defence; vilapantam = wailed loudly.

Commanded then by the king Sugriva, the monkeys jumped up, seized and bound him, who without defence, wailed loudly.

Sloka 33

śukastu vānaraiśca ṇḍaistatra taiḥ samprapīḍitaḥ || 6-20-33
vyācukrośa mahātmānam rāmam daśarathātmajam |

33. samprapīDitāh = harassed; taiḥ vānaraiḥ = by those monkeys; chaṇ^Daiḥ = who were fierce; tatra = there; shukastu = ṣhuka on his part; vyāchukrosha = cried loudly; rāmam = to Rama; mahātmānam = the high soled; dasharathātmajam = son of Dasaratha(as follows):

Harassed by those monkeys there, Shuka on his part cried loudly towards Rama, the high soled son of Dasaratha as follows:

Sloka 34

lupyete me balātpakṣau bhidyete me tathākṣiṇī || 6-20-34
yām ca rātrim mariṣyāmi jāye rātrim ca yāmaham |
etasminnantre kāle manmayā cāśubham kṛtam || 6-20-35
sarvam tadupapadyethā jahyam cedyadi jīvitam |

34;35. me = my; pakshhou = wings; lupyete = are being pulled out; balata = forcibly; tathā = and; me akshhiṇī = my eyes; bhidyete = are being pierced; jahyām chedyadi = if I abandon; jīvitam = my life; yat = which; ashhubham = sin; kṛitam = done; mayā = by me; ekasmin antare kale = between this period; aham = I; jāye = was born; yāmcha = on which; rātrim = night; yāmcha = and on which; rātrim = night; marishhyāmi = I shall die; tat sarvam = all that; upapadyethāḥ = would fall to your share.

"ṃy wings are being pulled out forcibly. ṃy eyes are being pierced. If I die, all the sins incurred by me between my birth and my death would fall to your share."

Sloka 36

nāghātayattadā rāmaḥ śrutvā tatparidevitam || 6-20-36
vānarānabravīdrāmo mucyatām dūta āgataḥ |

36. tadā = then; shrutā = hearing; tat = that; paridevanam = lamentation; rāmaḥ = Rama; na aghātayat = did not cause his killing; rāmah = Rama; abravīt = told; vānarām = Vanaras; muchyatām = let him be forced; āgataḥ = as he came; dūtaḥ = as an ambassador.

Hearing that lamentation of Shuka, Rama did not allow his killing by the monkeys. Rama ordered monkeys to release him, as he came as an ambassador.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye uddhakāṇḍe viṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 20th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
