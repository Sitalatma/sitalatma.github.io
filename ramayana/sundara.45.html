Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 45
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The seven sons of Prahasta surrounded by a great army rush in speedy chariots upon Hanuma, who is standing on the archy gate way. They discharge a shower of arrows on Hanuma. Then, Hanuma crushes all the enemies, using his palms, feet, fists, nails, chest and thighs only as his weapons. After killing those demons, Hanuma climbs up the archy door-way and takes up his position there again.


Sloka 1 Verse Locator

tataḥ te rākṣasa indreṇa coditā mantriṇaḥ sutāḥ |
niryayuḥ bhavanāt tasmāt sapta sapta arci varcasaḥ || 5-45-1
mahābala parīvārā dhanuṣmanto mahābalāḥ |
kṛta astrā astravidām śreṣṭhāḥ paraspara jaya eṣiṇaḥ || 5-45-2
hema jāla parikṣiptaiḥ dhvajavadbhiḥ patākibhiḥ |
toyada svana nirghoṣaiḥ vāji yuktaiḥ mahārathaiḥ || 5-45-3
tapta kāncana citrāṇi cāpāni amita vikramāḥ |
visphārayantaḥ samhṛṣṭāḥ taḍidvanta iva ambudāḥ || 5-45-4

1; 2; 3; 4. tataḥ = thereafter; te = those; sapta = seven; sutāḥ = sons; mantriṇaḥ = of his chief minister; saptārchirochiṣaḥ = having a lustre similar to that of fire; mahābalaparīvārāḥ = surrounded by a great army; dhanuṣmantaḥ = wielding their respective bows; mahābalāḥ = possessing a great strength; kṛitāstrāḥ = well; trained in archery; astravidām shreṣṭhaḥ = the best among knowers of weapons; paraspara jayaiṣiṇaḥ = wishing victory reciprocally; amita vikramāḥ = and having an immense prowess each; choditāḥ = directed; rākṣasendreṇa = by Ravana; niryayuḥ = started; samhṛiṣṭāḥ = highly delighted; tasmāt = from that; bhavanāt = palace; mahārathaiḥ = in great chariots; vājiyuktaiḥ = yoked with horses; (the chariots) toyadasvananirghoṣaiḥ = were having a noise similar to that of an autumnal cloud; hemajāla parikṣiptaiḥ = overlaid with aprotective armour of gold; dhvajadbhiḥ = surmounted by banners; patākibhiḥ = decorated with smaller flags; visphārayantaḥ = stretching; chāpāni = their bows; tapta kāṇchana chitrāṇi = inlaid with refined gold and looking colourful; taṭitvantaḥ ambudāḥ iva = like clouds accompanied by flashes of lightning.

ṭhereafter, those seven sons of his chief minister having a lustre similar to that of fire, surrounded by a great army, wielding their respective bows, possessing a great strength, well-trained in archery and the best among knower of weapons, wishing a victory reciprocally, having an immense prowess each, and as directed by Ravana, started highly delighted from that palace, in great chariots yoked with horses. Those chariots were having a noise similar to that of an autumnal cloud, overlaid with a protective armour of gold, surmounted by banners and decorated with smaller flags. They were stretching their bows inlaid with refined gold and looking colourful like clouds accompanied by flashes of lightning.

Sloka 5

jananyaḥ tāḥ tataḥ teṣām viditvā kimkarān hatān |
babhūvuḥ śoka sambhrāntāḥ sabāndhava suhṛt janāḥ || 5-45-5

5. tataḥ = thereupon; viditvā = coming to know; kimkarān = that Kimkaras (themselves); hatān = were killed; teṣām = their; janyastu = mothers on their part; sa bāndhava suhṛijjanāḥ = together with their relatives and friends; babhūvuḥ = became; shoka sambhrāntāḥ = tormented with grief.

Coming to know that Kimkaras themselves were killed their mothers together with their relatives and friends were tormented with grief.

Sloka 6

te paraspara samgharṣāḥ tapta kāncana bhūṣaṇāḥ |
abhipetuḥ hanūmantam toraṇastham avasthitam || 5-45-6

6. te = those sons of chief minister; taptakāṇchana bhūṣaṇāḥ = wearing ornaments made of pure gold; abhipetuḥ = rushed; hanumantam = towards ḥanuma; avasthitam = who was ready for battle; toraṇastham = and who stood on the archy door-way motionless.

ṭhose sons of chief minister, wearing ornaments made of pure gold, rushed towards ḥanuma, who was ready for battle and who stood on the archy gate-way motionless.

Sloka 7

sṛjanto bāṇa vṛṣṭim te ratha garjita nihsvanāḥ |
vṛṣṭimanta iva ambhodā viceruḥ nairṛta ṛṣabhāḥ || 5-45-7

7. te nairṛitāmbudāḥ = those demons looking like clouds; ratha garjita niḥsvanāḥ = diffusing thunder-like sounds of their chariots; sṛijantaḥ = creating; bāṇavṛiṣṭim = a shower of arrows; vicheruḥ = moved in different directions; vṛiṣṭimantaḥ ambhodāḥ = resembling rainy clouds.

ṭhose demons looking like clouds, diffusing thunder-like sounds of their chariots and discharging a shower of arrows, moved in different directions resembling rainy clouds in the monsoon.

Sloka 8

avakīrṇaḥ tataḥ tābhiḥ hanūmān śara vṛṣṭibhiḥ |
abhavat samvṛta ākāraḥ śaila rāḍ iva vṛṣṭibhiḥ || 5-45-8

8. tataḥ = then; avakīrṇaḥ = covered by; tābhiḥ sharavṛiṣṭibhiḥ = those showers of arrows; hanumān = ḥanuma; abhavat = became; shailarāDiva = like a mountain-king; samvṛitākāraḥ = concealed by incessant showers.

ṭhus covered by those showers of arrows, ḥanuma became invisible even as a mountain-king is concealed by incessant showers.

Sloka 9

sa śarān vancayāmāsa teṣām āśu caraḥ kapiḥ |
ratha vegān ca vīrāṇām vicaran vimale ambare || 5-45-9

9. vicharan = roaming about; vimale = in a cloudless; ambare = sky; saḥ āshucharaḥ = that fast-footed; kapiḥ = monkey; moghayāmāsa = made useless; sharān = the arrows; ratha vegamcha = and the speed of the chariots; teṣām vīrāṇām = of those virile demons.

Roaming about in a cloudless sky, that fast-footed monkey made the arrows and the speedy chariots of those virile demons, useless.

Sloka 10

sa taiḥ krīḍan dhanuṣmadbhiḥ vyomni vīraḥ prakāśate |
dhanuṣmadbhiḥ yathā meghaiḥ mārutaḥ prabhuḥ ambare || 5-45-10

10. krīDan = playing; taiḥ = with those demons; dhanuṣmadbhiḥ = wielding a bow each; vyomni = in the sky; saḥ vīraḥ = that hero; prakāshate = shone; prabhuḥ mārutaḥ = loke the Lord of Wind; meghaiḥ = (playing with) clouds; dhanuṣmadbhiḥ = brandishing a rainbow.

Playing with those demons wielding a bow each in the sky, that hero shone like the Lord of Wind, playing with clouds brandishing a rain bow.

Sloka 11

sa kṛtvā ninadam ghoram trāsayan tām mahācamūm |
cakāra hanumān vegam teṣu rakṣahsu vīryavān || 5-45-11

11. kṛitvā = making; ghoram = a terrific; ninadam = roar; vīryavān = the valiant; saḥ = ḥanuma; trāsayan = and frightening; tām = that; mahā chamūm = great army; chakāra vegam = rushed; teṣu rakṣassu = on those demons.

ṃaking a terrific roar and frightening that great army, the valiant ḥanuma rushed on those demons.

Sloka 12

talena abhihanat kāṃścit pādaiḥ kāṃścit param tapaḥ |
muṣṭinā abhyahanat kāṃścin nakhaiḥ kāṃścit vyadārayat || 5-45-12

12. paramtapaḥ = that ḥanuma; the annihilator of enemies; abhyahanat = struck; kāmshchit = some; talena = with palms; kāmschhit = some; pādaiḥ = with his feet; kāmshchit = and some; muṣṭinā = with his fists; vyadārayat = (ḥe) tore down; kāmshchit = some; nakhaiḥ = with his nails.

ṭhat ḥanuma, the annihilator of enemies, struck some with his palms, some with his feet and some others with his fists. He tore down some others with his nails.

Sloka 13

pramamātha urasā kāṃścit ūrubhyām aparān kapiḥ |
kecit tasya eva nādena tatra eva patitā bhuvi || 5-45-13

13. kapiḥ = ḥanuma; pramamātha = crushed; kāmshchit = some; urasā = with his chest; aparān = and some others; ūrubhyām = with his thighs; kechit = some; patitāḥ = fell down; bhuvi = on the ground; tatvaiva = there itself; tasya minādena = by his roar.

ḥanuma crushed some with his chest and some others with his thighs. ṣome fell down there itself on the ground, just by the roar made by Hanuma.

Sloka 14

tataḥ teṣv avapanneṣu bhūmau nipatiteṣu ca |
tat sainyam agamat sarvam diśo daśa bhaya arditam || 5-45-14

14. teṣu = (ṣeeing) those demons; uvasanneṣu = dying; nipatiteṣu cha = and falling; bhūmau = on the ground; sarvam = all; tat = that (remaining); sainyam = army; agamat = fled; dasha dishaḥ = in ten (different) directions; bhayārditam = afflicted with fear.

ṣeeing those demons dying and falling on the ground, all the remaining army, afflicted with fear, fled in ten different directions.

Sloka 15

vineduḥ visvaram nāgā nipetuḥ bhuvi vājinaḥ |
bhagna nīḍa dhvajac catraiḥ bhūḥ ca kīrṇā abhavat rathaiḥ || 5-45-15

15. nāgāḥ = elephants; vineduḥ = trumpeted; visvām = with a wrong accent; vājinaḥ = ḥorses; nipetuḥ = fell down; bhuvi = on the ground; bhūshcha = even the earth; kīrṇā abhavat = was filled with; rathaiḥ = chariots; bhagna nīDa dhvajachchhatraiḥ = which had their seats; banners and canopies broken.

ṭheir elephants trumpeted with a wrong accent. ḥorses fell down on the ground. Even the earth was filled with chariots which had their seats, banners and canopies broken.

Sloka 16

sravatā rudhireṇātha sravantyo darśitāḥ pathi |
vividhaiśca svarairlaṅkā nanāda vikṛtam tadā || 5-45-16

16. sravantyaḥ = streams; sravatā rudhireṇa = flowing with blood; darshitā = were seen; pathi = on the way; atha = thereafter; tadā = then; laṇ^kā = Lanka; nanāda = resounded; vikṛitam = horribly; vividhaiḥ svaraiḥ = with various kinds of voices.

ṭhereafter, streams flowing with blood were seen on the way. Lanka resounded with various kinds of horrible voices.

Sloka 17

sa tān pravṛddhān vinihatya rākṣasān |
mahābalaḥ caṇḍa parākramaḥ kapiḥ |
yuyutsuḥ anyaiḥ punaḥ eva rākṣasaiḥ |
tat eva vīro abhijagāma toraṇam tat || 5-45-17

17. saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuma; vīraḥ = the hero; mahābalaḥ = the exceedingly strong one; chaṇ^Da parākramaḥ = having terrible prowess; vinihatya = killing; tān = those; pravṛiddhān rākṣasān = arrogant demons; yuyutsuḥ = and being desirous of a combat; anyaiḥ rākṣasaiḥ = with the other demons; abhijagāma = reached; punareva = yet again; tam toraṇameva = that archy door way itself.

ṭhat heroic and mighty ḥanuma having a terrible prowess, killing those arrogant demons and being desirous of a combat with the other demons, again reached that archy door way itself.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe pañcacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 45th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
