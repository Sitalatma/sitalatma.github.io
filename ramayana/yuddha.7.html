Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 7
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The demons inspire Ravana with confidence and eulogise his army's strength. They suggest that Indrajit alone is enough to kill the army of monkeys together with Rama.

Sloka 1

iti uktā rākṣasa indreṇa rākṣasāste mahābalāḥ |
ūcuḥ prānjalayaḥ sarve rāvaṇam rākṣasa īśvaram || 6-7-1
dviṣtpakṣhmavijñāya nītibāhyāstvabuddhayaḥ |

1-2a. uktāḥ = after spoken; iti = thus; rākṣasa indreṇa = by Ravana; te rākṣasāste = those demons; mahābalaḥ = who were very strong; nītibāhyāḥ = who were without moral behaviour; abuddhayaḥ = who were stupid; sarve = all prāṇjalayaḥ = who lifted up their joined palms; avijJṇāya = ignorant; dviṣatpakshham = of the enemy's side; ūchuḥ = spoke; rāvaṇam = to Ravana; rākṣaseshvaram = the king of demons.

ḥearing the words of Ravana, those demons who were strong, stupid and without moral conduct, all raising up their joined palms and spoke to Ravana the king of demons, without knowing the strengths of their enemy's side (as follows):

Sloka 2

rājan parigha śakti ṛṣṭi śūla paṭṭasa samkulam || 6-7-2
sumahan no balam kasmād viṣādam bhajate bhavān |

2b-3a. rājan = Oh; king; naḥ balam = our army; parigha shakti ṛiṣṭi shūla paṭṭasa samkulam = equipped with iron bars; javelins; double-edged swords; darts and sharp-edged spears; sumahat = is very great; kasmāt = why; bhavān bhajate = are you getting; viṣādam = worry?

"Oh, king! Our army, equipped with iron bars, javelins, double-edged swords, darts and sharp-edged spears, is very great. Why are you getting worried?"

Sloka 3

tvayā bhogavatīm gatvā nirjatāḥ pannagā yudhi || 6-7-3
kailāsa śikhara āvāsī yakṣair bahubhir āvṛtaḥ |
sumahat kadanam kṛtvā vaśyas te dhanadaḥ kṛtaḥ || 6-7-4

3b-4. gatvā = after proceeding; bhogavatīm = to Bhogavati city (the abode of ṇagas in Patala; one of the seven regions under the earth); pannagāḥ = the serpents; nirjatāḥ = were defeated; tvayā = by you; dhanadaḥ = Kubera (the bestower of riches); kailāsa shikhara āvāsī = who resides on the peak of ṃount Kailasa; āvṛitaḥ = surrounded by; bahubhiḥ = many; yakshhaiḥ = Yakshas (a class of demi-gods); kṛitvā = having done; sumahat = a very great; kadanam = battle; vashyaḥ = surrendered; te = to you.

"After proceeding to Bhogavati city (the abode of ṇagas in Patala one of the seven regions under the earth), the serpents there were defeated by you. Kubera (the bestower of riches) who resides on the peak of ṃount Kailasa, surrounded by many yakshas (a class of demi-gods) surrendered to you after doing a great battle."

Sloka 5

sa mahāīśvara sakhyena ślāghamānas tvayā vibho |
nirjitaḥ samare roṣāl loka pālo mahābalaḥ || 6-7-5

5. vibho = "Oh; Lord; roṣāl = by your rage; saḥ = that Kubera; shlāghamānaḥ = who was praising himself; maheshvara sakhyena = for his friendship with Lord ṣhiva (the ṣupreme Lord); loka pālaḥ = a guardian of the world; mahābalaḥ = with great strength; nirjitaḥ = was defeated samare = in battle; tvayā = by you.

"Oh, Lord! By your rage, Kubera who was boasting himself for his friendship with Lord Shiva (the Supreme Lord) and said to be a guardian of the world possessing great strength, was defeated by you in battle."

Sloka 6

vinihatya ca yakṣaughān vikṣobhya ca vigṛhya ca |
tvayā kailāsa śikharād vimānam idam āhṛtam || 6-7-6

6. vinihatya = throwing down; yakṣaughān = a group of Yakshas; vikṣobhya = and taking them into prison; idam = this; vimānam = aerial car (Pushpak); āhṛitam = was procured; tvayā = by you; kailāsa sikharāt = from ṃount Kailasa.

"ṭhrowing down a group of Yakshas, after causing a flutter among them and taking them in prison, this aerial car (Pushpak) was procured by you from ṃount Kailasa."

Sloka 7

mayena dānava indreṇa tvad bhayāt sakhyam iccatā |
duhitā tava bhāryā arthe dattā rākṣasa pumgava || 6-7-7

7. rākṣasa pumgava = Oh; the best of demons; mayena = by ṃaya; dānava indreṇa = the lord of demons; duhitā = his daughter (ṃandodari); dattā = was give; tava = to you; bharyā arthe = as wife; tvad bhayāt = due to fear from you; icchatā = and desiring; sakhyam = friendship.

"Oh, the best of ogres! By ṃaya, the lord of demons, ṃandodari his daughter was given to you in marriage, due to fear from you and duly desiring your friendship."

Sloka 8

dānava indro madhur nāma vīrya utsikto durāsadaḥ |
vigṛhya vaśam ānītaḥ kumbhīnasyāḥ sukha āvahaḥ || 6-7-8

8. mahābāho = Oh; mighty armed; kumbhīnasyāḥ = Kumbhinasa's; sukha āvahaḥ = husband; dānava indraḥ = a king of demons; vīrya utsiktaḥ = who was proud of his valour; durāsadaḥ = and who was difficult to be defeated; ānītaḥ = was taken into; vasham = (your) subjugation; vigṛihya = having warred against him.

Oh, mighty armed! ṃadhu Kumbhinasa's (your sister's) husband, another ruler of demons, who was proud of his valour and who was difficult to be defeated, was taken into your subjugation, your having won a battle against him."

Sloka 9

nirjitās te mahābāho nāgā gatvā rasā talam |
vāsukis takṣakaḥ śankho jaṭī ca vaśam āhṛtāḥ || 6-7-9

9. mahābāho = Oh; mighty armed! Gatvā = after going; rasā talam = to Rasatala (the penultimate subterranean region); nāgāḥ = the serpent-demons; vāsukiḥ = Vasuki; takṣakaḥ = ṭakshaka; shankhaḥ = ṣankha; jaṭī ca = and Jati; nirjitāḥ = were conquered; te = by you; āhṛitāḥ = and were captivated; vasham = as your subject.

"Oh, mighty armed! After moving down to rasatala (the penultimate subterranean region), the you conquered serpent-demons Vasukim Takshaka, Saukha and Jati were conquered by you and captivated as your subjects."

Sloka 10

akṣayā balavantaḥ ca śūrā labdha varāḥ punaḥ |
tvayā samvatsaram yuddhvā samare dānavā vibho || 6-7-10
sva balam samupāśritya nītā vaśam arim dama |
māyāḥ ca adhigatās tatra bahavo rākṣasa adhipa || 6-7-11

10-11. arindamau = Oh; annihilator of enemies; rākṣasa adhipa = commander of demons; vibho = Oh; Lord; yuddhvā = after performing battle; samvatsaram = for a year; tvayā = by you; dānavāḥ = demons; akṣayāḥ = who were beyond destruction; balavantaḥ ca = mighty and; shūrāḥ = valiant; punaḥ = and; labdhavarāḥ = who obtained boons; vasham nītāḥ = were defeated; samupāshritya = taking resort; sva balam = to your own strength; samare = in the combat; bahvyaḥ = several; māyāshcha = fascinating tricks also; adhigatāḥ = were obtained from them.

"Oh, annihilator of enemies, Commander of demons and the Lord! You after performing a battle for a year by you mighty demons who were beyond destruction, who were valiant and who obtained boons (for immortality) were defeated using your own strength in combat. Several fascinating tricks also were learnt from them."

Sloka 12

śūrāḥ ca balavantaḥ ca varuṇasya sutā raṇe |
nirjitās te mahābāga catur vidha bala anugāḥ || 6-7-12

12. mahābhāga = Oh; the illustrious; varuṇasya = Varuna's; sutāḥ = sons; shūrāḥ ca = the valiant ones; balavantaḥ cha = the strong ones; catur vidha bala anugāḥ = who were followed by an army having four limbs (viz. elephants; chariots; cavalry; and infantry); nirjitāḥ = were defeated; te = by you; raṇe = in a battle.

"Oh, illustrious king! Varuna's sons, valiant strong and who were followed by an army having four limbs (viz. elephants, chariots, cavalry and infantry) were defeated by you in a battle."

Sloka 13 Verse Locator

mṛtyu daṇḍa mahāgrāham śālmali dvīpa maṇḍitam |
kālapāśamahāīcim yamakimkarapannagam || 6-7-13
mahājvareṇa durdharṣam yamalokamahārṇavam |
avagāhya tvayā rājan yamasya bala sāgaram || 6-7-14
jayaḥ ca viplulaḥ prāpto mṛtyuḥ ca pratiṣedhitaḥ |
suyuddhena ca te sarve lokās tatra sutoṣitāḥ || 6-7-15

13-15. rājan = Oh; king; avagāhya = after entering; yamalokamahārṇavam = a great ocean in the shape of Yama's realm; mṛityu daṇDa mahāgrāham = with a huge alligator in the form of Death's rod of punishment; shālmali druma maṇDitam = adorned with silk cotton trees (bristling with thorns to be used as weapons); kālapashamahāvīcim = turbulent with a huge wave in the shape of the noose of Kala (ṭime spirit); yamakimkarapannagam = with serpents in the form of Yama's servants; durdharṣam = and difficult to be conquered; mahā jvareṇa = on account of mighty Jvara (spirit presiding over fevers); sāgaram = (and churning) the sea; bala sāgaram = in the form of Yama's forces; viplulaḥ = a great; jayaḥ ca = victory; prāptaḥ = was obtained; mṛityuḥ ca = and Death; pratiṣedhitaḥ = warded off; tvayā = by you; sarve = all lokaḥ = the people; tatra = there; sutoṣitāḥ = were delighted; te suyuddhena = by your good battle.

Oh, king! After entering a great ocean in the shape of Yama's realm, with a huge alligator in the form of death's rod of punishment, adorned with silk-cotton trees (bristling with thorns to be used a weapons), turbulent with a huge wave in the shape of noose of Kala (Time Spirit), with serpents in the form of Yama's servants and difficult to be conquered on account of mighty Jvara (Spirit presiding over fevers), and churning the sea in the form of Yama's forces, a great victory was obtained and death warded off by you. All the people there were delighted to see your good battle."

Sloka 16

kṣatriyair bahubhir vīraiḥ śakra tulya parākramaiḥ |
āsīd vasumatī pūrṇā mahadbhir iva pādapaiḥ || 6-7-16

16. vasumatī = the earth; āsīt = was; pūrṇā = filled; madadbhiḥ pādapaiḥ iva = as with big trees; bahubhiḥ = with many; kṣatviyaiḥ = warriors; vīraiḥ = who were strong; shakra tulya parākramaiḥ = with a courage equal to that of Devendra (Lord of celestials).

"ṭhe earth was filled, like with big trees, with many warriors who were strong with a courage equal to that Devendra (Lord of celestials).

Sloka 17

teṣām vīrya guṇa utsāhair na samo rāghavo raṇe |
prasahya te tvayā rājan hatāḥ parama durjayāḥ || 6-7-17

17. rājan = Oh; king; rāghavaḥ = Rama; na = was not; samaḥ = equal; teṣām = to them; raṇe = in combat; vīrya guṇa utsāhaiḥ = by their valour; excellence and energy; te = those warriors; samo raṇe durjayāḥ = who were difficult to be overcome in a battle; prasahya = were over powered; hatāḥ = and killed; tvayā = by you."

"Oh, King! Rama was not equal to them in combat, by their valour excellence and energy. Those warriors, who were difficult to be overcome in battle, were overpowered and killed by you."

Sloka 18

tiṣṭha vā kim mahārāja śrameṇa tava vānarān |
ayameko mahāraja indrajit kṣapayiṣyati || 6-7-18

18. mahārāja = Oh; emperor; vā = or; tiṣṭha = you stay; kim = why; shrameṇa = by exertion; tava = to you? ṃahārāja = Oh; emperor! Ayam = this; indrajit = Indrajit; ekaḥ = alone; kshapayiṣyati = can destroy; vānarān = the monkeys.

Oh, emperor! Why exertion to you? You remain here. Oh, emperor! ṭhis Indrajit alone can destroy the monkeys."

Sloka 19

anena hi mahārāja maheśvaramanuttamam |
iṣṭvā yajñam varo labdho loke paramadurlabhaḥ || 6-7-19

19. mahāraja = Oh; emperor; iṣṭvā = after performing; anuttamam = an excellent; yajJṇam = sacrifice; mahesvaram = to propitiate ṃahesvara (the great Lord; shiva); varaḥ = a boon; parama durlabhaḥ = very rare to be obtained; loke = in the world; labdhaḥ hi = was indeed acquired; anena = by him."

"Oh, emperor! After performing an excellent sacrifice to propitiate ṃahesvara (the great lord shiva), a boon very rare to be obtained in the world, was acquired by him."

Sloka 20 Verse Locator

śaktitomaramīnam ca vinikīrṇāntraśaivalam |
gajakaccapasambādhaṃśvamaṇḍūkasamkulam || 6-7-20
rudrādityamahāgrāham marudvasumahoragam |
rathaśvagajatoyaugham padātipulinam mahat || 6-7-21
anena hi samāsādya devānām balasāgam |
gṛhīto daivatapatirlaṅkām cāpi praveśitaḥ || 6-7-22

20-22. samāsādya = Approaching; mahat = the huge; balasāgaram = ocean of army; devānām = celestials; shaktitomaramīnam ca = whose fist are lances and spheres; vinikīrṇāntrashaivalam = and guts thrown asunder as duck-weeds; gajakacchapasambādhām = whose turtles are the elephants; ashvamanDūka samkulam = whose frogs are the teeming horses; rudrādityamahāgrāham = infested with alligators in the form of (eleven) Rudras (gods of destruction) and (twelve) Adityas (sons of Aditi); marudvasumahoragam = with huge serpents in the form of (forty nine) maruts (winds gods) and (eight) Vasus; rathashvagajatoyaugham = having chariots; horses and elephants for its volume of waters; padātipulinam = the sandy banks its infantry; daivatapatiḥ = Devendra (Lord of celestials); gṛihītaḥ = was captured; anena = by him; praveshitaḥ cāpi = and was impelled to enter; laṇ^kām = Lanka (as a detenu).

"Approaching that huge ocean of army of celestials, whose fish are lanes and spheres, whose guts thrown asunder as duck-weeds, whose turtles are the elephants, whose frogs are the teeming horses, infested with alligators in the form of (eleven) Rudras (gods of destruction) and (twelve) Adityas (sons of Aditi), with huge serpents in the form of (forty nine) ṃaruts (wind gods) and (eight) Vasus, having chariots horses and elephants for its volume of waters and the sandy elephants for its volume of waters and the sandy banks its infantry, Devendra (Lord of celestials) was captured by him and was impelled to come to Lanka (as a detenu).

Sloka 23

pītāmahaniyogācca muktaḥ śambaravṛtrahā |
gatastriviṣṭapam rājan sarvadevanamaskṛtaḥ || 6-7-23

23. rājan = Oh; king; muktaḥ = having been released; pītāmahaniyogācca = by the orders of Lord Brahma (your grand-father); shambaravṛitrahā = Devendra who killed demons called ṣhambara and Vritra; sarvadevanamaskṛitaḥ = and who was adored by all celestials; gataḥ = went (back); tritviṣṭapam = to heaven.

"Oh, king! ḥaving been released by the orders of your grand father Brahma, Devendra who killed demons called Shambara and Vrita and who was adored by all celestials, went back to heaven."

Sloka 24

tameva tvam mahārāja visṛjendrajitam sutam |
yāvadvānarasenām tām parāmām nayati kṣhayam || 6-7-24

24. mahārāja = Oh; emperor; tvam = you; visṛija = send; tam = that; indrajitameva = Indrajit alone; tām = that; vānara senām = army of monkeys; parāmām = together with Rama; yāvadnayati = will be brought; kṣayam = to an end.

"Oh, emperor! You send that Indrajit alone, so that the army of monkeys together with Rama will be brought to an end."

Sloka 25

rājan na āpad ayuktā iyam āgatā prākṛtāj janāt |
hṛdi na eva tvayā kāryā tvam vadhiṣyasi rāghavam || 6-7-25

25. rājan = Oh; king; iyam = this; ayuktā = unbefitting; āpad = danger; āgatā = has come; janāt = from a man; prākṛitāj = who is ordinary; naiva kāryā = It is not to be kept; tvayā = by you; hṛidi = in mind; tvam = you; vadhiṣyasi = will kill; rāghavam = Rama.

"Oh, king! ṭhis unbefitting danger has come froma a man who is ordinary. It is not to be kept by you in mind, you will kill Rama."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye saptamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 7th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
