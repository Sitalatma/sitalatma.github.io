Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty
chapter [Sarga] 26
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Seetha ponders a lot and decides to give up life without Rama.

Sloka 1

prasaktāśrumukhī tvevaṃ bruvatī janakātmajā |
adhogatamukhī bālā vilaptumupacakrame || 5-26-1

1. prasaktāshrumukhī = with a face continually shedding tears; jankātmajā = ṣeetha; balā = an young woman; evam = thus; bruvatī = speaking; adhogatamukhī = with downward face; upachakrame = started; vilaptum = to weep.

With a face continually shedding tears, ṣeetha an young woman, thus speaking with a downward face, started to weep.

Sloka 2

unmatteva pramatteva bhrāntacitteva śocatī |
upāvṛttā kiśorīva viveṣṭantī mahītale || 5-26-2

2. unmatteva = like a mad one; pramatteva = like an intoxicated one; bhrāntachitteva = with a deluded mind; shochatī = (ṣeetha was) crying; kishorīva = like a female horse; upāvṛittā = rolling on ground; viveshhṭantī = (ṣeetha) rolled; mahītale = on the ground.

ṣeetha was crying like a mad one, like an intoxicated one, with a deluded mind. Like a female horse rolling on ground, Seetha rolled on the ground.

Sloka 3

rāghavasya pramattasya rakṣasā kāmarūpiṇā |
rāvaṇena pramathyāhamānītā krośatī balāt || 5-26-3

3. rāghavasya = (while) Rama; pramattasya = was heedless; aham = I; kroshatī = who have been crying; balāt ānītā = (was) forcibly brought; pramathya = overpowered; rakshasā = by the ogre; rāvaṇena = Ravana; kāmarūpiṇā = who can wear desired form.

"While Rama was heedless (taken away by ṃareecha), I who have been crying was forcibly brought overpowered by the ogre Ravana who can wear desired form."

Sloka 4

rākṣasīvaśamāpannā bhartsyamānā sudāruṇam |
cintayantī suduḥkhārtā nāhaṃ jīvitumutsahe || 5-26-4

4. āpannā = obtaining; rāskasī vasham = control by ogresses; bartsyamānā = being frightened; sudāruṇam = very horribly; chintayantī = thinking; suduḥkhārtā = being tormented by grief; aham = I; na utsahe = do not desire; jīvitum = to live.

"Being under the control of ogresses, being frightened very horribly, thinking and being tormented by grief, I do not desire to live."

Sloka 5

na hi me jīvite nārtho nai vārthairna ca bhūṣaṇaiḥ |
vasantyā rākṣasīmadhye vinā rāmaṃ mahāratham || 5-26-5

5. vinā = without; rāmaṃ = Rama; mahāratham = a great car-warrior; vasantyāḥ = living; rākshasī madhye = in the midst of ogresses; arthaḥ na = there is no use; jīvitena = with life; me = to me; na arthaiḥ = not with wealth; na bhushhaṇaishcha = not with ornaments.

"Without Rama, a great car-warrior, living in the midst of ogresses, there is no use with life to me; not with wealth, not with ornaments."

Sloka 6

aśmasāramidaṃ nūnathavāpyajarāmaram |
hṛdayaṃ mama yenedaṃ na duḥkhenāvaśīryate || 5-26-6

6. athavā = or; mama = my; idam = this; hṛidayam = heart; nūnam = truly; ashmasāram = (is made of) iron; ajarāmaramapi = is undecaying and without death; yena = by what reason; nāvashīryate = is it not destroying; duḥkhena = with sorrow.

"Or my this heart truly is made of iron. It is undecaying and without death. By what reason is it not destroying with sorrow?"

Sloka 7

dhiṅmāmanāryāmasatīṃ yāhaṃ tena vinā kṛtā |
muhūrtamapi rakṣāmi jīvitaṃ pāpajīvitā || 5-26-7

7. yā = whoever; aham = I; pāpajīvitā = with a sinful life; kṛitā = made; tena vinā = (to be) without Rama; rakshāmi = protecting; jīvitam = life; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment; mām = to such me; anāryām = who am ignoble; asatīm = who am bad wife; dhik = shame (on me).

"Whoever I with a sinful life made to be without Rama, protecting life even for a moment, to such me who am ignoble, who am bad wife, shame on me."

Sloka 8

kā ca me jīvite śraddhā sukhe vā taṃ priyaṃ vinā |
bhartāraṃ sāgarāntāyā vasudhāyāḥ priyaṃvadam || 5-26-8

8. priyam vinā = without husband Rama;bhartāram = (who is) lord; vasudhāyāḥ = of the earth; sāgarāntāyāḥ = with ocean at the end; priyaṃvadam = a pleasant talker; kā = what (is); shradhhā = interest; me = to me; jīvite = in life; sukhe vā = or in comfort.

"Without husband Rama who is the lord of the earth with ocean at the end, a pleasant talker, what is the interest to me in life or in comfort."

Sloka 9

bhidyatāṃ bhakṣyatāṃ vāpi śarīraṃ visṛjāmyaham |
na cāpyahaṃ ciraṃ kuḥkhaṃ saheyaṃ priyavarjitā || 5-26-9

9. aham = I; visṛijāmi = will leave; sharīram = the body; bhidyatām = let it be split (into pieces); bhakshyatām vāpi = or let it be eaten; aham = I; priyavarjitā = without husband; na cha saheyam = will not tolerate; duḥkham = sorrow; chiram = for a long time.

"I will leave the body. Let it be split into pieces or let it be eaten. I without husband will not tolerate sorrow for a long time."

Sloka 10

caraṇenāpi savyena na spṛśeyaṃ niśācaram |
rāvaṇaṃ kiṃ punarahaṃ kāvayeyaṃ vigarhatam || 5-26-10

10. aham = I; na spṛisheyam = will not touch; savyena charaṇenāpi = even with my left foot; rāvaṇam = Ravana; nishācharam = ogre; vigarhitam = of reprehensible conduct; kāmayeyam kiṃ punaḥ = what to say of desiring (him).

"I will not touch even with my left foot Ravana an ogre of reprehensible conduct. What to say of desiring him."

Sloka 11

pratyākhyātaṃ na jānāti nātmānaṃ nātmanaḥ kulam |
yo nṛśaṃsasvabhāvena māṃ prārthayitumicchati || 5-26-11

11. yaḥ = whoever (Ravana); nṛishaṃsa svabhāvena = with a cruel nature; icchhati = is desiring; prārthayitum = to solicit; mām = me (that Ravana); na jānāti = does not know; ātmānam = him; pratyākhyātam = to be refused; na = (he does) not (know); ātmanaḥ = his; kulam = race.

"Whoever Ravana with a cruel nature is desiring to solicit me, that Ravana does not know him to be refused; he does not know his race."

Sloka 12

cinnā bhinnā vibhaktā vā dīptevāgnau pradīpitā |
rāvaṇaṃ nopatiṣṭheyaṃ kiṃ pralāpena vaściram || 5-26-12

12. chhinna vā = (even) if cut; bhinna vā = or if divided into parts; pradīpitā vā = or (even) if burnt; agnau = in fire; dīpte = which is blazing; nopatishhṭeyam = I will not reach; rāvaṇam = Ravana; kim = what is the use; vaḥ pralāpena = with your prattling; chiram = for a long time.

"Even if cut or if divided into parts in blazing fire, I will not reach Ravana. What is the use with your prattling for a long time?"

Sloka 13

khyātaḥ prājñaḥ kṛtajñśaśca rāghavaḥ |
sadvṛtto niranukrośaḥ śaṅke madbhāgyasaṃkṣayāt || 5-26-13

13. rāghavaḥ = Rama; khyātaḥ = is famous; prājJṇaḥ = is a wise man; sānulroshashcha = is compassionate; sadvṛittaḥ = with a good conduct; madbhāgya saṃkshayāt = due to deterioration of my fortune; saṇ^ke = I doubt; niranukroshaḥ = (ḥe became) hard hearted.

"Rama is famous, is a wise man, is compassionate with a good conduct. Due to the deterioration of my fortune, I doubt He became hard hearted."

Sloka 14

rākṣasānāṃ sahasrāṇi janasthāne caturdaśa |
yenaikena nirastāni sa māṃ kiṃ nābhipadyate || 5-26-14

14. yena ekena = by whom alone; chaturdasa saharāṇi = fourteen thousand; rākshsānām = of ogres; nirastāni = have been destroyed; kim = why; saḥ = (is) that Rama; nābhipadyaḥ = not saving; mām = me.

"By whom alone fourteen thousand of ogres have been destroyed, why is that Rama not saving me."

Sloka 15

niruddhā rāvaṇenāhamalpavīryeṇa rakṣasā |
samarthaḥ khalu me bhartā rāvaṇaṃ hantumāhave || 5-26-15

15. aham = I; niruddhā = am withheld; rakshasā rāvaṇena = by the ogre Ravana; alpavīryeṇa = with little strength; me bhartā = my husband; samarthaḥ khalu = is indeed capable; hantum = to kill; rāvaṇam = Ravana; ahave = in a war.

"I am withheld by the ogre Ravana with little strength. ṃy husband is indeed capable of killing Ravana in a war."

Sloka 16

virādho daṇḍakāraṇye yena rākṣasapuṅgavaḥ |
raṇe rāmeṇa nihataḥ sa māṃ kiṃ nābhipadyate || 5-26-16

16. yena rāmeṇa = By which Rama; virādhaḥ = Viradha; rākshasa puṇ^gavaḥ = best among ogres; nihataḥ = has been killed; daṇDakāraṇye = in Dandaka forest; raṇe = in a war; kim = why; saḥ = (is) that ḥe; nābhipadyate = not saving; mām = me.

"By which Rama Viradha, best among ogres, has been killed in Dandaka forest in a war, why is that He not saving me?"

Sloka 17

kāmaṃ madhye samudrasya laṅkeyaṃ duṣpradharṣaṇā |
na tu rāghavabāṇānāṃ gatirodho bhaviṣyati || 5-26-17

17. iyam laṇ^kā = this city of Lanka; samudrsya madhye = being in the middle of ocean; dushhpṛidharshhaṇā kāmam = is difficult to be attacked; tu = but; na bhavishhyati = there will not be; gatirodhaḥ = an obstacle to flying; rāghava bānāṇām = of Rama's arrows;

"ṭhis city of Lanka being in the middle of ocean is difficult to be attacked. But there will not be an obstacle to flying of Rama's arrows."

Sloka 18

kiṃ nu tatkāraṇaṃ yena rāmo dṛḍhaparākramaḥ |
rakṣasāpahṛtāṃ bhāryāmiṣṭāṃ nābhyavapadyate || 5-26-18

18. kim nu = what now; tat kāraṇam = is that reason; yena = by which; rāmaḥ = Rama; dṛiDha parākramaḥ = with a firm courage; nābhyavapadyate = is not protecting; ishhṭām bhāryām = ḥis dear wife; apahṛitām = stolen; rakshasā = by an ogre.

"What now is that reason by which Rama with a firm courage is not protecting ḥis dear wife stolen by an ogre."

Sloka 19

ihasthāṃ māṃ na jānīte śaṅke lakṣmaṇapūrvajaḥ |
jānannapi sa tejasvī dharṣaṇaṃ marṣayiṣyati || 5-26-19

19. shaṇ^ke = I doubt that; lakshmana pūrvajaḥ = Rama elder to Lakshmana; na jānite = does not know; mām = me; ihasthām = to be here; jānannapi = after knowing; saḥ tejasvī = (will) that glorious Rama; marshhyishhyati = tolerate; dharshhaṇam = (this) outrage.

"I doubt that Rama elder to Laksmana does not know me to be here. After knowing will that glorious Rama tolerate this outrage?"

Sloka 20

hṛteti yo.adhigatvā māṃ rāghavāya nivedayet |
gṛdhrārajo.api sa raṇe rāvaṇena nipātitaḥ || 5-26-20

20. yaḥ = whoever Jatayu; adhigatvā = nearing (Rama); nivedayat = will have let known; rāghavāya = to Rama; hṛiteti = (that ṣeetha) was stolen; saḥ gṛidhra rājaḥ api = that king of vultures; hataḥ = has been killed; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; raṇe = in war.

"Whoever Jatayu nearing Rama will have let known to Rama that ṣeetha was stolen, that king of vultures has been killed by Ravana in war."

Sloka 21

kṛtaṃ karma mahattena māṃ tathābhyavapadyatā |
tiṣṭhatā rāvaṇadvandve vṛddhenāpi jaṭāyuṣā || 5-26-21

21. mahat karma = a great deed; kṛitam = has been done; tena jaṭāyushhā = by that Jatayu; tathā = thus; abhyavapadyatā = protecting; mām = me; vṛiddhenāpi = although of old age; tishhṭhatā = standing; rāvaṇa dvandve = in a dual combat with Ravana.

"A great deed has been done by that Jatayu, thus protecting me, although of old age, standing in a dual combat with Ravana."

Sloka 22

yadi māmiha jānīyādvartamānāṃ sa rāghavaḥ |
adya bāṇairabhikruddhaḥ kuryāllokamarākṣasam || 5-26-22

22. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; jānīyādyadi = if ḥe knows; mām = me; iha vartamānām = to be living here; abhikruddhaḥ = with anger; kuryāt = will make; lokam = the world; arākshasam = to be without ogres; bāṇaiḥ = with (ḥis) arrows; adya = now.

"If that Rama knows me to be living here, with anger ḥe will make the world to be without ogres with His arrows now."

Sloka 23

vidhamecca purīṃ laṅkāṃ śoṣayecca mahodadhim |
rāvaṇasya ca nīcasya kīrtiṃ nāma ca nāśayet || 5-26-23

23. vidhamechcha = (Rama will) blow away laṇ^kāṃ purīm = the city of Lanka; shoshhayechcha = will dry up; mahodadhim = the great ocean; nāshayet = will destroy; kīrtim = the fame; nāma cha = and name; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana.

"Rama will blow away the city of Lanka, will dry up the great ocean, will destroy the name and fame of Ravana."

Sloka 24

tato nihatanāthānāṃ rākṣasīnāṃ gṛhe gṛhe |
yathāhamevaṃ rudatī tathā bhuyo na saṃśayaḥ || 5-26-24

24. tataḥ = thereafter; yathā = how; aham = I ; evam rudatī = am crying in this way; tathā = in the same way; gṛihe gṛihe = in house and house; rākshasīnām = ogresses; nihatanādhānām = with killed husbands (will cry); bhūyaḥ = again and again; na saṃshayaḥ = there is no doubt.

"ṭhereafter how I am crying in this way, in the same way, in very house, ogresses with killed husbands will cry again and again. There is no doubt in this."

Sloka 25

anviṣya rakṣasāṃ laṅkāṃ kuryādrāmaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |
na hi tābhyāṃ ripurdṛṣṭo muhūrtamapi jīvati || 5-26-25

25. rāmaḥ = Rama; salakshmaṇaḥ = together will Lakshmana; anvishhya = will search; laṇ^kām = Lanka; rakshasām = of ogres; kuryāt = will do (destruction); ripuḥ = an enemy; dṛishhṭaḥ = seen; tābhyām = by those two; na jīvati hi = will indeed not live; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment.

"Rama together will Lakshmana will search the city of Lanka belonging to ogres. An enemy seen by those two will indeed not live even for a moment."

Sloka 26

citādhumākulapathā gṛdhramaṇḍalasaṃkulā |
acireṇa tu laṅkeyaṃ śmaśānasadṛśī bhavet || 5-26-26

26. iyam = this; lan^kā = Lanka; achireṇa = shortly; bhavet = will become; shmashāna sadṛishī = like a graveyard; chitādhūmakulapathā = with paths agitated with smoke of funeral pyres; gṛidhramaṇDalasaṃkulā = crowded with a group of vultures.

"ṭhis Lanka shortly will become like a graveyard, with paths agitated with smoke of funeral pyres, crowded with a group of vultures."

Sloka 27

acireṇaiva kālena prāpsyāmyeva manoratham |
duṣprasthāno.ayamākhyāti sarveṣām vo viparyayam || 5-26-27

27. achireṇa kālenaiva = in a short time only; prāpsyāmeva = I will attain; manoratham = (my) desire; ayam = this; dushhprasthānaḥ = evil course; ākhyāti = is telling; sarveshhām = all; vaḥ = of your; viparyayam = destruction.

"In a short time only I will attain my desire. ṭhis evil course is telling of destruction of you all."

Sloka 28

yādṛśānīha dṛśante laṅkāyāmaśubhāni vai |
acireṇaiva kālena bhaviṣyati hataprabhā || 5-26-28

28. iha = here; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka; yādṛishāni = whatever; ashubhāni = inauspicious things; dṛishyante = are being seen; achireṇaiva kālena = (from those) in a short while; bhavishhyati = (Lanka) will become; hataprabhā = deprived of glory.

"ḥere in Lanka whatever inauspicious things are being seen, from those, in a short while Lanka will become deprived of glory."

Sloka 29

nūnaṃ laṅkā hate pāpe rāvaṇe rākṣasādhame |
śoṣaṃ yāsyati durdharṣā pramadā vidhava yathā || 5-26-29

29. pāpe = sinful; rākshasādhame = vile ogre; rāvaṇe = Ravana; hate = while being killed; laṇ^kā = Lanka; durdharshhā = which is unconquerable; nūnam = definitely; yāsyati = will get; shoshham = drying up; pramadā yathā = like a woman; vidhavā = who lost husband.

"While the sinful, vile ogre Ravana is being killed, Lanka which is unconquerable till now, definitely will get drying up like a woman who lost husband."

Sloka 30

puṇyotsava samṛddhā ca naṣṭabharrtī sarākṣasī |
bhaviṣyati purī laṅkā naṣṭabhartrī yathāṅganā || 5-26-30

30. puṇyotsava samṛiddhā = (now) abounding in pious festivities; laṇ^kāpurī = the city of Lanka; nashhṭa bhartrī = with lost lord; sarākshasī = together with ogresses; bhavishhyati = will become; aṇ^ganā yathā = like a woman; nashhṭa bhartrī = with lost husband;.

"ṇow abounding in pious festivities, the city of Lanka with lost lord together with ogresses will become like a woman with lost husband."

Sloka 31

nūnaṃ rākṣasakanyānāṃ rudantīnāṃ gṛhe gṛhe |
śroṣyāmi nacirādeva duḥkhārtānāmiha dhvanim || 5-26-31

31. nachirādeva = in a short while; iha = here; gṛihe gṛihe = in every house; nūnam shroshhyāmi = I can definitely hear; dhvanim = the sound; rākshasakanyānām = of ogresses; rudantīnām = crying; duḥkhārtānām = being tormented by grief.

"In a short while here in every house I can definitely hear the sound of ogresses crying being tormented by grief."

Sloka 32

sāndhakārā hatadyotā hatarākṣasapuṅgavā |
bhaviṣyati purī laṅkā nirdagdhā rāmasāyakaiḥ || 5-26-32

32. la~ṇkā purī = the city of Lanka; bhavishhyati = will become; nirdagdhā = burnt; rāmasāyakaiḥ = by Rama's arrows; sāndhakārā = together with darkness; hadadyotā = with destroyed lustre; hata rākshasa pu~ṇgavā = with killed best ogres.

"ṭhe city of Lanka will become burnt by Rama's arrows together with darkness, with destroyed luster with killed best ogres."

Sloka 33

yadi nāma sa śūro māṃ rāmo raktāntālocanaḥ |
jānīyadvartamānāṃ hi rāvaṇasya niveśane || 5-26-33

33. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; shūraḥ = a strong man; raktānta lochanaḥ = with red eye corners; yadi nāma jānīyāt = will he know; mām = me; vartamānām = to be living; niveshane = in the house; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana.

"Will that Rama who is a strong man with red eye corners know me to be in the house of Ravana."

Sloka 34

anena tu nṛśaṃsena rāvaṇenādhamena me |
samayo yastu nirdiṣṭastasya kālo.ayamāgataḥ || 5-26-34
sa ca me vihito mṛtyurasmin duṣṭe na vartate |

34. yaḥ = whatever; samayaḥ = time; nirdishhṭa = ordered; anena rāvaṇena = by this Ravana; nṛishaṃsena = who is cruel; adhamena = (and) vilest of men; ayam = this; kālaḥ = time; āgataḥ = has come; me = to me; saḥ mṛityuḥ = that death; vihitaḥ = decreed; me = to me; na vartate = does not hold good; asmin dushhṭe = in the matter of this evil one.

"Whatever time has been ordered by this cruel and vilest of men Ravana, this time has come to me. That death decreed to me does not hold good to this evil one (Ravana)."

Sloka 35

akāryaṃ ye na jānanti nairṛtāḥ pāpakāriṇaḥ || 5-26-35
adharmāttu mahotpāto bhaviṣyati hi sāṃpratam |
naite dharmaṃ vijānanti rākṣasāḥ piśitāśanāḥ || 5-26-36

35; 36. ye nairṛitāḥ = Whoever ogres; pāpakāriṇaḥ = who are sinners; nā jānati = do not know; akāryam = what should not be done; adharmāttu = due to their unrighteousness; sāṃpratam = now; bhavishhyati = there will be; mahotpātaḥ = a great calamity; ete = these; rākshasāḥ = ogres; pishitanāshanāḥ = who are meat eaters; na jānati = do not know; dharmam = virtue.

"Whoever sinful ogres who do not know what should not be done, due to their unrighteousness now there will be a great calamity. These meat eating ogres do not know virtue."

Sloka 37

dhruvaṃ māṃ prātarāśārthe rākṣasaḥ kalpayisyati |
sāhaṃ kathaṃ kariṣyāmi taṃ vinā priyadarśanam || 5-26-37
rāmam raktāntanayanamapaśyantī sukuḥkhitā |

37. rākshsaḥ = an ogre; dhruvam = will definitely; kalpayishhyati = make; mām = me; prātarāshārthe = for breakfast; sā aham = such I; katham karishhyāmi = what should do; tam vinā = without Rama; priyadarshanam = with pleasing appearance; suduḥkhitā = I am grieving a lot; apashyantī = not seeing; rāmam = Rama; raktāntanayanam = with red eye corners.

"An ogre will definitely eat me for breakfast. What should such I do without Rama with pleasing appearance? I am grieving a lot not seeing Rama with red eye corners."

Sloka 38

yadi kaścit pradātā me viṣasyādya bhavediha || 5-26-38
kṣipraṃ vaivasvataṃ devaṃ paśyeyaṃ patinā vinā |

38. adya = now; kashchit bhavedyadi = if there exists any one; prādātā = (who is a) giver; vishhasya = of poison; iha = here; patinā vinā = (I) without husband; kshipram = quickly; pashyeyam = will see; devam = the god; vaivasvatam = Yama.

"ṇow if there exists any one who is a giver of poison here, I without husband quickly will see the god Yama."

Sloka 39

nājānājjivatīṃ rāmaḥ sa māṃ lakṣmaṇapūrvajaḥ || 5-26-39
jānantau tau na kuryātāṃ noryvāṃ hi mama mārgaṇam |

39. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; lakshmaṇa pūrvajaḥ = elder to Lakshmana; nājānāt = (may) not know; mām = me; jīvatīm = to be alive; tau = those two; jānantau = knowing (me to be alive); na kuryatām iti na = it cannot be said that they will not do; mama mārgaṇam = my search; ūrdhvām = on earth;

"ṭhat Rama elder to Lakshmana may not know me to be alive. If those two know me to be alive, then it cannot be said that they will not do my search on earth."

Sloka 40

nūnaṃ mamaiva śokena sa nīro lakṣmaṇāgrajaḥ || 5-26-40
devalokamito yātastyaktvā dehaṃ mahītale |

40. lakshmaṇāgrajaḥ = the elder brother of Lakshmaṇa; saḥ = that Rama; vīrāḥ = who is valiant; mama shokenaiva = due to grief of me; tyaktvā = leaving; deham = the body; mahītale = on earth; yātaḥ = has gone; itaḥ = from here; devalokam = to celestial world; nūnam = this is certain.

"ṭhat valiant Rama who is the elder brother of Lakshmana due to grief of me, leaving the body on earth has gone to celestial world. This is certain."

Sloka 41

dhanyā devāh sagandharvāḥ siddhāśca paramarṣayaḥ || 5-26-41
mama paśyanti ye nāthaṃ rāmaṃ rājīvalocanam |

41. ye = whoever;pashyanti = is seeing; mama = my; nātham = husband; rāmam = Rama; rājīva lochanam = with eyes resembling a lotus flower; devāḥ = (such) Devas; siddhāshcha = ṣiddhas; sagandhravāḥ = together with Gandharvas; parmarshhayaḥ = and great sages; dhanyāḥ = are fortunate.

"Whoever is seeing my husband Rama with eyes resembling a lotus flower such Devas, ṣiddhas together with Gandharvas and great sages are fortunate."

Sloka 42

athavā na hi tasyārtho dkarmakāmasya dhīmataḥ || 5-26-42
mayā rāmasya rājarṣerbhāryayā paramātmanaḥ |

42. athavā = or; tasya rāmasya = to that Rama; dharmakāmasya = with a desire in virtuousness; dhīmataḥ = who is intelligent; rājarshheḥ = sage-king; paramātmanaḥ = the supreme spirit; artham na = there is no use; mayā = with me; bhāryayā = his wife.

"Or to that Rama with a desire in virtuousness, who is intelligent, a sage-king, the supreme spirit, there is no use with me, his wife."

Sloka 43

dṛśyamāne bhavetprītiḥ sauhṛdaṃ nāstyapaśyataḥ || 5-26-43
nāśayanti kṛtaghnāstu na rāmo nāśayiṣyati |

43. prītiḥ bhavet = friendly disposition will occur; dṛishyamāne = in what is being seen; nāsti = there is no; sauhṛidam = friendship; apashyataḥ = to one who does not see; kṛitaghnāḥ = ungreatful ones; nāshayanti = destroy (friendship); rāmaḥ tu = but Rama; na nāshayishhyati = will not destroy friendship.

"Friendly disposition will occur in what is being seen. ṭhere is no friendship to one who does not see. Ungrateful ones destroy frindship. But Rama will not destroy friendship."

Sloka 44

kiṃ nu me na guṇāḥ kecitkiṃ vā bhāgyakṣayo mama || 5-26-44
yāhaṃ sīdāmi rāmeṇa hīnā mukhyena bhāminī |

44. aham = I; yā = who; bhāminī = is a woman; sīdāmi = (who is) perishing; mukhyena rāmeṇa vinā = without the chief Rama; me = to (such) me; na kiṃ nu = are there no; kechit guṇāḥ = good qualities; kiṃ vā = or; mama = (has) my; bhāgyakshayaḥ = fortune diminished.

"I who is a woman who is perishing without the chief Rama to such me are there no good qualities or has my fortune diminished."

Sloka 45

śryo hi hīvitānmartuṃ vihīnāyā mahātmanaḥ || 5-26-45
rāmādakliṣṭacāritrācchūrācchatrunibarhaṇāt |

45. vihīnāyāḥ = deprived; rāmāt = from Rama; aklishhṭa chāritrāt = with untroubled reputation; shūrāt = a warrior; shatrunibarhaṇāt = annihilator of enemies; mahātmanaḥ = a great soul; me = to (such) me; jīvitāt = more than living; martuḥ = death; shreyaḥ = is good.

"ṭo me who is deprived of Rama with untroubled reputation, a warrior, annihilator of enemies, a great soul, more than living, death is good."

Sloka 46

athavā nyastaśastrau tau vane mūlaphalāśinau || 5-26-46
bhrātarau hi naraśreṣṭhau saṃvṛttau vanagocarau |

46. athavā = or; tau bhrātarau = those two brothers; narashresṭhau = best among men; nyasta shastrau = giving up weapons; vane = in the forest; saṃvṛittau = (may have) become; vanagocharau = dwellers of forest; mūlaphalāshanau = eating roots and fruits.

"Or those two brothers who are best among men giving up weapons in the forest may have become dwellers of forest eating roots and fruits."

Sloka 47

athavā rākṣasendreṇa rāvaṇena durātmanā || 5-26-47
cadmanā ghātitau śūrau bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |

47. athavā = or; rāmalakshmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; shūrau bhrātarau = the warrior brothers;ghātitau = (may have been) killed; durātmanā = by the evil soul; rākshsendreṇa rāvaṇena = by king of ogres Ravana; chadmanā = by deceit;

"Or Rama and Lakshmana the warrior brothers may have been killed by the evil soul king of ogres Ravana by deceit."

Sloka 48

sāhamevaṃgate kāle martumicchāmi sarvathā || 5-26-48
na ca me vihito mṛtyurasmin duḥkhe.api vartate |

48. evam gate = under such circumstances; kāle = (and time); sā = such; aham = I; sarvathā = in all ways; ichchhāmi = am desiring; martum = to die; asmin dukhaḥ api = even in this grief; mṛityuḥ = death; vihitaḥ na vartate = is not bestowed (on me).

"Under such circumstances and time, such I in all ways am desiring to die. Even in this grief, death is not bestowed on me."

Sloka 49

dhanyāḥ khalu mahātmāno munayastyaktakilbiṣāḥ || 5-26-49
jitātmano mahābhāgā yeṣāṃ na staḥ priyāpriye |

49. mahātmanaḥ = great souled ones; tyakta kilbishhāḥ = with abandoned sins; jittatmanaḥ = with a conquered mind; mahābhāgāḥ = those with great fortune; munayaḥ = sages; dhanyāḥ khalu = are indeed fortunate; yeshhām = to whom; na staḥ = there is no; priyāpriye = pleasure or displeasure.

"Great souled ones with abandoned sins, with a conquered mind, those with great fortune, sages are indeed fortunate to whom there is no pleasure and displeasure."

Sloka 50

priyānna saṃbhavedduḥkhamapriyādadhikaṃ bhayam || 5-26-50
tābhyāṃ hi ye viyujyante namasteṣāṃ mahātmanām |

50. priyāt = from pleasure; duḥkham = sorrow; apriyāt = (and) from displeasure; adhikam bhayam = great fear; na sambhavet = do not occur (to great souls); ye = whoever; viyujyante = are separated; tābhyām = from pleasure and displeasure; namaḥ = (my) obeisance; teshhām mahātmānām = to such great souls.

"From pleasure sorrow and from displeasure great fear do not occur to great souls. Whoever are separated from pleasure and displeasure, my obeisance to such great souls."

Sloka 51

sāhaṃtyuktā priyeṇaiva rāmena viditātmanā || 5-26-51
prāṇāṃstyakṣyāmi pāpasya rāvanasya gatā vaśam |

51. tyaktā = left; priyeṇaiva rāmeṇa = by dear Rama; viditātmanā = with a well known mind; vasham gatā = coming under the control; pāpasya = of sinful one; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; sā aham = such I; tyakshāmi = will leave; prāṇān = breath of life.

"Left by dear Rama with a well known mind coming under the control of sinful Ravana, such I will leave breath of life."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ṣaḍviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 26th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
