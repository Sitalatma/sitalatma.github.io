Introduction

Bharata asserts that he does not know how the exile of Rama, Sita and Lakshmana has occurred. Bharata and Satrughna go to Kausalya's place. Kausalya speaks harsh words to Bharata, saying that Kaikeyi his mother procured for him the kingdom as desired by him the kingdom as desired by him. Bharata politely explains, on a number of oaths, his own innocence over the matter, Kausalya comes to know Bharata's heart, fondly takes him to her lap and weeps distressfully.

Sloka 1

so.amātyamadhyebharato jananīmabhyakutsayat |

That valiant Bharata, having come to himself a long time on obtaining consciousness, saw his mother whose eyes were filled pitiably with tears, reproached her mother in the midst of the ministers and said as follows:-

"I never desired for the kingdom nor consulted about this matter with even my mother. I was not aware of the proposed coronation, which has of the been contemplated by king Dasaratha; since I was staying indeed in a distant land, along with Satrughna."

Sloka 4

"I was neither aware of the banishment of Rama to the forest nor about how the exile of Lakshmana and Seetha have occurred."

Recognising the voice of Bharata the noble man, crying out in that manner, Kausalya spoke the following words to Sumitra:

"Bharata, the son of Kaikeyi, the doer terrific deeds arrived. I went to see that far-sighted Bharata."

Sloka 7

That kausalya who became pale with her demeanor, dirty and emaciated, speaking to Sumitra as aforesaid, became agitated and absent minded and proceeded to Bharata's place.

Bharata, the younger brother of Rama on his part, along with Shatrughna started out to Kausalya's place.

Seeing Kausalya who was afflicted with grief, who fell unconscious (on the way) who was crying and who was an honorable woman with a noble mind. Shatrughna and Bharata wept in sorrow, approached her painfully and embraced her.

Sloka 10

Kausalya who was very much in sorrow , spoke the following words to Bharata: "you, who have a desire for kingdom, have got this invincible kingdom ."Alas! By the cruel deed of Kaikeyi, it was obtained by you soon!"

Sloka 12

"The ruthless Kaikeyi condemned my son to wear garments of bark and sent him to dwell in the forest: What merit has she gained from this?"

Sloka 13

13. kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; arhati = is opt; prasthāpayitum = to send; māmapi = me too; kshhipram = without delay(to the place); yatra = where; me = my; sumahāyashāḥ = illustrious; sutaḥ = son; hiraṇya nābhaḥ = with a golden navel(the distinguished mark of Vishnu the god of creation).

"Kaikeyi ought to send me too without delay to the place where my illustrious son with a golden novel (the distinguished mark of Vishnu the god of creation) is there."

Sloka 14

"Otherwise, I for my own satisfaction accompanied by Sumitra, placing in front(carried by Brahmana) the sacred fire*** will happily set out to the place Raghava has gone."

Sloka 15

"Otherwise, you yourself ought to conduct me now, if you so desire, to the place where my son, the tiger among men, is living as an ascetic."

Sloka 16

"This vast kingdom, endowed with elephants, horses and chariots and stock piled with grains and riches, has been made over to you by her."

Sloka 17

Sloka 18

His mind being perplexed and having lost his consciousness, Bharata then coming his senses, wailed in many ways and fell at the feet of the queen.

Sloka 19

Then, Bharata with folded hands, replied as follows to Kausalya who was encircled by afflictions and weeping as aforesaid in many ways:

Sloka 20

"O, noble woman! Why do you reproach me, sinless as I am, for those things that I do not know anything? Nay, you know well of the greatest affection I have for Rama."

Sloka 21

Sloka 22

Sloka 23

Sloka 24

"with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, to him let that sin incur as to those who seek to harm a king who protects his people as if they are his own children."

Sloka 26

Sloka 27

Sloka 28

"ṃay that perverse wretch, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, forget the subtle meanings of the scriptures taught with care by a wise teacher."

Sloka 29

Sloka 30

Sloka 32

Sloka 34

Sloka 36

Sloka 37

rāja strī bāla vṛddhānām vadhe yat pāpam ucyate |
bhṛtya tyāge ca yat pāpam tat pāpam pratipadyatām || 2-75-37

37. yat = which; pāpam = sin; uchyate = was proclaimed; vadhe = in killing; raja strībāla vṛiddhānām = a king ; a woman; a child or an aged person; yat = which; pāpam = sin( was proclaimed); bhṛityāgacha = in abandouning the dependents; (let him with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile); pratipadyatām = obtain; tat = the same; pāpam = sin.

"That sin which was proclaimed in killing a king a woman, a child or an aged person or in abandoning one's dependents let him (with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile) obtain the same sin."

Sloka 38

lākṣayā madhumāṃsena lohena ca viṣeṇa ca |
sadaiva bibhṛyādbhṛtyān yasyāryo.asumate gataḥ || 2-75-38

38. (ṃay he)yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; obtain sin) bibhṛiyāt = of nurturing; sadaiva = always; bhṛityān = one's wife; children; and other dependents; through sale; lākshhayā = of lacquer; madhumāmsena = liquor; and flesh; lohena = iron; vishheṇa = and poison.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, obtain, tat= the sin of nurturing his wife children and other dependents through sale of lacquer, liquor, flesh, iron or poison."

Sloka 39

samgrāme samupoḍhe sa śatrupakṣbhayamkare |
palāyāmāno vadhyeta yasyāryo.anume gataḥ || 2-75-39

39. saḥ = may he; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; obtain the sin); palāyamānaḥ = of escaping; vadhyeta = and getting killed; samgrāme = at a time where) a battle; satrupakshhabhayamkare = inspiring fear upon the enemy's side; samupoDhe = has commanded.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, obtain the sin of being killed while fleeing, at a time when a battle inspiring fear upon enemy's side has commanded."

Sloka 40

kapālapāṇiḥ pṛthivīmaṭatām cīrasamvṛtaḥ |
bhiksamāṇo yathonmatto yasyāryo.anumate gatah || 2-75-40

40. saḥ = may he; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; aṭatām = wandar about; pṛithivīm = the world; unmattoyathā = as a mad man; kapālapāṇiḥ = with a wooden bowl in his hand; chīrasamvṛitaḥ = dressed in rags; bhikshhamāṇaḥ = and begging for alms.

ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, wander about the world, as a mad man with a wooden bowl in his hand, dressed in rags and begging for alms."

Sloka 41

pāne prasakto bhavatu strīṣvakṣeṣu ca nityaśaḥ |
kāmkrodhābhibhūtastu yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-41

41. may he; yasya = with whose; counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has departed; bhavatu = be; kāmakrodhābhibhūtaḥ = given over to the infatuation = and anger; nityashaḥ = and constantly prosaktaḥ = involved; pane = in intoxicvated drinks; srtīshhu = women; akshheshucha = and dice.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has departed, be given over to infatuation and anger and be constantly involved in wine women and dice."

Sloka 42

yasya dharme mano bhūyādadharmam sa niṣevatām |
apātravarṣī bhavatu yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-42

42. manaḥ = may the mind; asya = of the man; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; mābhūyāt = never take delight; dharma = in righteousness; saḥ = Let him; nishhevatām = indulge; adharmam = in un righteousness; bhavatu = and become; apātravarshhi = distributor of gifts to the undeserving.

ṃay the mind of man with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, never take delight in righteousness? Let him indulge in un righteousness and become a distributor of gifts for the undeserving."

Sloka 43

saṃcitānyasya vittāni vividhāni sahasraśaḥ |
dasyubhirvipralupyantām yaśyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-43

43. yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; asya = let him; vividhhāni = various; vittāni = riches; samchitāni = accumulated; sahasrashaḥ = in thousands; vipralupyantām = be snatched away ; dashyubhiḥ = by robbers.

"With whose counsel , my elder brother has gone to exile, let his various kinds of riches got accumulated in thousands , be snatched away by robbers."

Sloka 44

ubhe saṃdhye śayānasya yat pāpam parikalpyate |
tac ca pāpam bhavet tasya yasya āryo anumate gataḥ || 2-75-44

44. yat = which; pāpam = sin; parikalpyate = was proclaimed; tasya = on him; shayanāsya = who sleeps; ubhe sandhye = during both the twillights; tat = let him; pāpam = sin; bhavet = be obtained; by him; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile.

"That sin which was proclaimed on him who sleeps during both the twilights, let that sin be obtained by him, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile."

Sloka 45

yad agni dāyake pāpam yat pāpam guru talpage |
mitra drohe ca yat pāpam tat pāpam pratipadyatām || 2-75-45

45. yat = which; pāpam = sin; agnidāyaka = is in an arsonist; yat = which; pāpam = sin; gumtalpage = is in a violator of his teacher's bed; yat = which; pāpam = sin; mitradrohe = is in cheating a friend; tat = let that; pāpam = sin; pratipadyatām = be obtained (to him on whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile?)

"Let him obtains that sin, which attaches to a man who practices arson, to a man who violets his teacher's bed and to a man who cheats his friend."

Sloka 46

devatānām pitṛṛṇām ca mātā pitros tathaiva ca |
mā sma kārṣīt sa śuśrūṣām yasya āryo anumate gataḥ || 2-75-46

46. saḥ = may he; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; (incure that sin); māsmakārshhīt = of not doing; shushrūshhām = service; devatānām = to the gods; pitṛīṇām = to the manes; tathaivāha = and like wise; matāpitroḥ = to his mother and father.

"ṃay he, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur that sin of not doing service to be god, to the manes and likewise to his mother and father."

Sloka 47

satām lokāt satām kīrtyāḥ saj juṣṭāt karmaṇaḥ tathā |
bhraśyatu kṣipram adya eva yasya āryo anumate gataḥ || 2-75-47

47. ṃay he)yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother ; gataḥ = has gone to exile; bhrashyatu = forfeit; kshhipram = soon; adyaiva = and now; karmaṇaḥ = = from the act; sajjushhṭāt = of the righteous; lokāt = from the region; satām = obtained;by good man; tathā = and ; kīrtyāḥ = from illustriousness.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, forfeit soon and now from the region obtained by virtuous man, forfeit the illustriousness enjoyed by good men and the righteous actions."

Sloka 48

apāsya mātṛśuśrūṣāmanarthe so.avatiṣṭhatām |
dīrghabāhurmahāvakṣā yasyāryo.asumate gataḥ || 2-75-48

48. (ṃay he)yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; dīrghabāhuḥ = that long armed; mahāvakshhā = and broad-chested; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; apāsya = fail to give; mātṛishushrūshhokam = due reverence to his mother; avatishhṭhatām = and be condemned; anarthe = to idleness.

"ṃay he with whose counsel, that long armed and broad chested elder brother has gone to exile, fail to give due reverence to his mother and be condemned to idleness."

Sloka 49

bahuputro daridraśca jvararogasamanvitaḥ |
sa bhūyātsatatakleśī yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-49

49. saḥ = may he; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone exile; bahubhṛityaḥ = possess many dependents; daridraḥ = be without resources; jvararoga samanvitaḥ = and be ; satatakleshī = forever in distress.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, possess many dependents be without resources, be undermined with fever and disease and be forever in distress."

Sloka 50

āśāmāśam samānānām dīnānāmūrdhvacakṣuṣām |
ārthinām vitathām kuryādyasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-50

50. (may he) yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; incur the sin; kuryāt = of making; vitathām = the hope; arthinām = of the destitute; dīnānām = the very miserable ones; ūrdhva chakshhushhām = having their eyes looking up; āshamsamānānām = and seeking for alms.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin of falsifying the hopes of highly miserable destitute, who having their eyes looking up seek for alms."

Sloka 51

māyayā ramatām nityam paruṣaḥ piśuno.aśuciḥ |
rājzno bhīta stvadharmātmā yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-51

51. (may he) yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; incur the sin; ramatām = of staying; bhītaḥ = in fear; rājṇ^aḥ = of the king; nityam = forever; māyayā = and in conceit; parushhaḥ = cruel man;pishunaḥ = a back biter; ashuchiḥ pishunaḥ = dishonest man.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin of staying in fear of the king forever as a cunning man, a cruel man, a back biter, a dishonest man and an unrighteous man."

Sloka 52

ṛtusnātām satīm bhāryāmṛtukālānurodhinīm |
ativarteta duṣṭātmā yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-52

52. dushhṭātmā = may the evil minded man; yasya = with whose anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile;(incur the sin); ativarteta = of ignoring; satīm = his chaste; bhāryām = wife; ṛitusnātām = who bathed after her course of menstruation( and so prepared for sexual intercourse) ṛtukālānurodhinīm = having regard for the season favorable for procreation.

"ṃay the evil minded man, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin of ignoring his chaste wife, who having bathed after her course of menstruation. approaches him for union having regard for the season favorable for procreation."

Sloka 53

dharmadārān parityajya paradārānni ṣevatām |
tyaktadharmaratirmūḍho yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-53

53. mūDhaḥ = may that stupid; yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; incur the sin) nishhevatām = of courting; paradārān = the consort of another; parityajya = disdaining; dharma dārān = his lawful wife; tyaktadharmaratiḥ = and having left his love for piety.

"ṃay that stupid with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin of courting the consort of another disdaining his lawful wife and having forsaken his love for piety."

Sloka 54

vipralu ptaprajātasya duṣkṛtam brāhmaṇasya yat |
tadeva pratipadyeta yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-54

54. (may he)yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; rāryḥ my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; pratipadyatām = obtain; tadeva = even that; dushhkrutam = sin; yat = which (is obtained); brāhmanaṇasya = by a Brahmin; vipraluptaprajātasya = who is issue less.

"ṃay he, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur that sin, which is obtained by a Brahmana who is issue less."

Sloka 55

pānīyadūṣake pāpam tathaiva viṣadāyake |
yattadekaḥ sa labhatām yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-55

55. yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; labhate = incur; yat = which; pāpam = sin; pānīyadushhake = of a person polluting the drinking water; ekaḥ = himself alone; tathaiva = and likewise; vishhadāyake = of a person who administers person.

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin that is attached to a person polluting the drinking water and likewise to a person who administers poison."

Sloka 56

brāhmaṇāyodyatām pūjām vihantu kaluṣendriyaḥ |
bālavatsām ca gām dogdu yasyaryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-56

56. (ṃay he ) yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; (incur the sin)vihantu = of disturbing; kalushhendriaḥ = with wicked senses; pūjām = a worship; udyatām = undertaken; brāhmaṇāya = to a Brahmana; dogdhu = and a milking; gām = a cow; bālavatsām = having a young calf(of ages less than ten years).

"ṃay he with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, incur the sin attached to a person disturbing the worship going to be offered to a Brahmana (by another) and to milking a cow having a young calf. " (of age less than ten days)

Sloka 57

tṛṣṇārtam sati pānīye vipralambhena yojayet |
labheta tasya yatpāpam yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-57

57. tasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; yatpāpam = let that sin; labhate = be obtained; yojayet vipralambena = of disappointing; tṛishhṇārtam = a person oppressed with thirst; sati pānīye = when water was available.

"To him, with whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, let that sin be obtained of disappointing a person oppressed with thirst, even though water was available."

Sloka 58

bhaktyā vivadamāneṣu mārgamāśritya paśyataḥ |
tasya pāpena yujyeta yasyāryo.anumate gataḥ || 2-75-58

58. yasya = with whose; anumate = counsel; āryaḥ = my elder brother; gataḥ = has gone to exile; pāpena = let the sin; yajyeta = be accorded; tasya = to him; (who) āshritya = standing; mārgam = on the road; pashyataḥ = and merely witnessing; bhktyā = between two divided groups (without trying to make peace between them.)

"With whose counsel my elder brother has gone to exile, let the sin be accorded to him, who while standing on the road, merely witnesses a dispute between two divided groups without trying to make peace between them."

Sloka 59

vihīnām pati putrābhyām kausalyām pārthiva ātmajaḥ |
evam āśvasayann eva duhkha ārtaḥ nipapāta ha || 2-75-59

59. evam = whilein that manner; āshvāsayāneva = even consoling; kausalyām = Kausalya; vihīnām = bereft of; patiputrābhyām = her husband and sin; pārthivātmajaḥ = the Prince Bharata ; nipapāta ha = fell down; duḥkhārtaḥ = depressed with anguish.

"While thus counseling Kausalya, who was bereft of her husband and son, the prince Bharata fell down, depressed as he was with anguish."

Sloka 60

tathā tu śapathaiḥ kaṣṭaiḥ śapamānam acetanam |
bharatam śoka samtaptam kausalyā vākyam abravīt || 2-75-60

60. kausalya = Kausalya; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the following words; bharatām = to Bharata; shokasamtaptam = who was tormented with grief; achetanam = and was quite distraught; shapamānam = who just uttered; kashhṭaiḥ = formidable; shapathaiḥ = imprecations; tathātu = in that manner.

Kausalya spoke the following words to Bharata who was tormented with grief, was quite distraught and who just uttered formidable imprecations in the aforesaid manner:

Sloka 61

mama duhkham idam putra bhūyaḥ samupajāyate |
śapathaiḥ śapamāno hi prāṇān uparuṇatsi me || 2-75-61

61. putra = O; son! idam duḥkham = this grief; samupajāyate = is added; bhūyaḥ = further; mama = to me; shapamānaḥ = by uttering; shapathaiḥ = the curses; uparuṇatisihi = you are indeed afflicting; me = my; prāṇān = spirits.

"O, son! ṃy grief is further enhanced. By uttering the curses, you are indeed racking my spirits."

Sloka 62

diṣṭyā na calitaḥ dharmāt ātmā te saha lakṣmaṇaḥ |
vatsa satya pratijño me satām lokān avāpsyasi || 2-75-62

62. vatsa = O; son! dishhṭyā = fortunately; te = your; ātmā = mind; satyapratiJ^ṇaḥ = endowed as it is with nobility; nachalitaḥ = has not moved ; dharmāt = from righteousness; avāpsyasi = you will obtain; lokam = the world; satām = of the blessed; saha lakshhmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana.

"O ,son! Fortunately, your mind as it is endowed with nobility, has not swerved from righteous path. You will obtain the world of the blessed, along with Lakshmana."

Sloka 63

ityuktvā cāṅkamānīya bharatam bhrātṛvatsalam |
pariṣvajya mahābāhum ruroda bhṛśaduḥkhitā || 2-75-63

63. uktvā = speaking; iti = thus; bharatam = to Bharata; bhrātru vatsalam = who was devoted to his brother Rama; ānīya = she has taken; mahābāhum = the long armed Bharata; aṇkam = to her lap; parishhvajya = embraced him; ruroda = and wept; bhṛishaduḥkhitā = very much; afflicted with grief.

Thus speaking to Bharata who was devoted to his brother Rama, she took the valiant Bharata to her lap, embraced him and wept aloud , as she was very much afflicted with grief.

Sloka 64

evam vilapamānasya duhkha ārtasya mahātmanaḥ |
mohāc ca śoka samrodhāt babhūva lulitam manaḥ || 2-75-64

64. vilapamānaḥ = the great soled Bharata; duḥkhārtasya = distraught on account of grief; babhūva = felt himself; mohāchcha = about tp swoon; manaḥ = with his mind; lulitam = crushed; shokasamrodhāt = by the burden of grief.

Lamenting thus, the great soled Bharata distraught on account of grief, felt himself about to swoon, with his mind crushed by the burden of grief.

Sloka 65

lālapyamānasya vicetanasya |
pranaṣṭa buddheḥ patitasya bhūmau |
muhur muhur nihśvasataḥ ca dīrgham |
sā tasya śokena jagāma rātriḥ || 2-75-65

65. lālapyamānasya = lamenting thus; vichetanasya = being unconscious; praṇashhṭabuddheḥ = with a lost intellect; patitasya = lying fallen; bhūmou = on the ground; niḥshvasataḥ = again and again; rātriḥ = that night; jagāma = passed; shokena = with grief; tasya = to Bharata.

To Bharata that night passed with grief in lamenting thus laying fallen unconscious on the ground, sighing warmly again and again with his power of discernment having been lost.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe pañcasaptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 75nd chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
