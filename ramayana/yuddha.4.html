Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 4
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

After hearing the report of Hanuma, Rama fixes an auspicious hour for the departure of his forces to Lanka and perceives good omens. The Army reaches the shores of the sea.

Sloka 1

śrutvā hanūmato vākyam yathāvad anupūrvaśaḥ |
tato abravīn mahātejā rāmaḥ satya parākramaḥ || 6-4-1

1. rāmaḥ = Rama; mhātejāḥ = a very bright man; satyaparākramaḥ = a true warrior; yathāvat = duly; shrutvā = having heard; vākyam = the words; hanumataḥ = of ḥanuman; anupūrashaḥ = from the beginning; tataḥ = thereafter; abravīt = spoke (as follows).

Rama, a very bright man and a true warrior, having duly heard the words of Hanuman from the beginning, thereafter spoke as follows:

Sloka 2

yām nivedayase laṃkām purīm bhīmasya rakṣasaḥ |
kṣipram enām vadhiṣyāmi satyam etad bravīmi te || 6-4-2

2. mathisya = I shall destroy; kshhipram = quickly; purīm = the city; laṇ^kām = of Lanka; bhīmasya = of the terrible; rakshasaḥ = ogre; yām = of which; nivedayase = you inform; bravīmi = I am telling; te = you; etat = this; satyam = really.

"I shall destroy quickly Lanka, the city of that terrible ogre (Ravana), of which you just informed I am really telling this."

Sloka 3

asmin muhūrte sugrīva prayāṇam abhirocaye |
yukto muhūrto vijayaḥ prāpto madhyam divā karaḥ || 6-4-3

3. ṣugreeva = Oh; ṣugreeva! Abhirochaya = be pleased; prayāṇam = for our march; asmin = at this; muhūrte = moment; yukto = a suitable; muhūrtaḥ = moment; vijayaḥ = for success; divākaraḥ = the sun; prāptaḥ = reached; madhyam = the mid-day.

"Be pleased to approve our march at this moment, a suitable moment for success. The sun reached the mid-day.

Sloka 4

sītām gṛtvā tu tadyātu kvāsau yāsyati jīvitaḥ |
sītā śrutvābhiyānam me āśāmeṣyati jīvite || 6-4-4
jīvitānte.a mṛtam spṛṣṭvā pītvā viṣamivāturaḥ |

4. tat = let that ogre; yātu = go (to his abode); hṛitam = after kidnapping; sītām = ṣeetha; kva = where; asau yāsyati = will he go; jīvitaḥ = alive? shrutvā = hearing; me = of my; abhiyānam = march; sītā = ṣeetha; yāsyati = will get back; āshām = her hope; āshām = her hope; jīvite = in life; spṛisṭvā iva = like touching; amṛitam = ambrosia; āturaḥ = by a sick man; pītvā = having drunk; vishham = poison; jīvitānte = at the end of his life."

"Let that ogre go (to his abode) after kidnapping Seetha. Where will he go alive? Hearing of my march to Lanka, Seetha will get back her hope in life, like a sick man having drunk poison touches ambrosia at the end of his life."

Sloka 5

uttarā phalgunī hi adya śvas tu hastena yokṣyate || 6-4-5
abhiprayāma sugrīva sarva anīka samāvṛtāḥ |

5. adya = this; uttarā phalgunīhi = northern planet of Phalguni; yokshhyate = will be in conjunction; hastena = with the ḥasta star; shvastu = tomorrow; abhiprayāma = let us depart; sarvānīkasamāvṛitaḥ = with all the troops accompanying us; sugrīva = Oh; ṣugreeva!

"This northern planet of Phalguni will be in conjunction with the Hasta star tomorrow. Hence, let us depart today itself with all the troops accompanying us, Oh, Sugreeva!"

Sloka 6

nimittāni ca dhanyāni yāni prādur bhavanti me || 6-4-6
nihatya rāvaṇam sītām ānayiṣyāmi jānakīm |

6. pashyāmi = by seeing; nimittāni = the omens; yāni = which; prādurbhavanti = are becoming visible ; ānayishhyāmi = I shall bring; sītām = ṣeetha; jānakīm = the daughter of Janaka; nihatya = by killing; rāvaṇam = Ravana.

"By seeing the omens which are becoming visible, I deduce that I shall bring back Seetha the daughter of Janaka, by killing Ravana."

Sloka 7

upariṣṭādd hi nayanam sphuramāṇam idam mama || 6-4-7
vijayam samanuprāptam śaṃsati iva mano ratham |

7. idam = this; mama = my; nayanam = eye; sphuramāṇam = which is twitching; uparishhṭāt = is proclaiming as it were; manoratham = my desire; vijayam = of victory; samanuprāptam = coming nearer."

"ṃy eye which is twitching on the upper lid, is proclaiming as it were, my desire of victory coming nearer."

Sloka 8

tato vābararāheba kajśmaṇena supūjitaḥ || 6-4-8
uvāca rāmo dharmātmā punarapyarthakovidaḥ |

8. tataḥ = ṭhen; rāmaḥ = Rama; dharmātmā = the virtuous man; arthakovidaḥ = versed in moral law; supūjitāḥ = well adored; vānararājena = by ṣugreeva the king of monkeys; lakshmaṇena = and Lakshmana; punarapi = again; uvācha = spoke (as follows).

Then Rama the virtuous man versed in moral law, who was well-adored by Sugreeva the king of monkeys and Lakshmana, again spoke as follows:-

Sloka 9

agre yātu balasya asya nīlo mārgam avekṣitum || 6-4-9
vṛtaḥ śata sahasreṇa vānarāṇām tarasvinām |

9. nīlaḥ = (let) ṇila; vṛitaḥ = accompanied by; shatasahasreṇa = a hundred thousand; tarasvinām = strength; vānarāṇām = of monkeys; yātu = go; agre = before; asya = this; balasya = army; avekshhitum = to explore; mārgam = the way.

"Let general Nila accompanied by strength of hundred thousand warriors go before the army, to explore the way."

Sloka 10

phala mūlavatā nīla śīta kānana vāriṇā |
pathā madhumatā ca āśu senām senā pate naya || 6-4-10

10. nīla = Oh; ṇila; senāpate = the army-general! naya = steer; senām = the army; āshu = speedily; pathā = by the path; phalamūlavatā = abound with fruits and roots; shītakānanavāriṇā = cool woods and fresh water; madhumatā = and honey.

"Oh Nila the chief of Army! Steer the army speedily by the path, abound with fruits and roots, cool woods and fresh water and honey."

Sloka 11

dūṣayeyur durātmānaḥ pathi mūla phala udakam || 6-4-11
rākṣasāḥ parirakṣethās tebhyas tvam nityam udyataḥ |

11. rākshhāsāḥ = the demons; durātmanaḥ = who are evil minded; dūshhayeyuḥ = will spoil; mūlaphalodakam = the roots; fruits and water; pathi = in the path; tvam = you; nityam = always; udyataḥ = try; parirakshhedhāḥ = to protect; tebhyaḥ = from them.

"The evil-minded demons may spoil the roots, fruits and water in the path-way. You always try to be on you guard."

Sloka 12

nimneṣu vana durgeṣu vaneṣu ca vana okasaḥ || 6-4-12
abhiplutya abhipaśyeyuḥ pareṣām nihatam balam |

12. vanaukasaḥ = (let) the monkeys; abhiplutya = jump; nimneshhu = into low grounds; vanadurgeshhu = into places made inaccessible by forest-grores; vaneshhu cha = and into thickets; abhipashyeyuḥ = and notice; balam = the army; pareshhām = of the enemies; nihitam = stationed there.

"Let the monkeys jump into law grounds, into places made inaccessible by forest-grores and into thickets and notice whether any rival forces are stationed there."

Sloka 13

yattu phalgu balam kiṃcittadatraivopapadyatām || 6-4-13
etaddhi kṛtyam ghoram no vikrameṇa prayujyatām |

13. yat kimchit = whatever little; phalgu = of feeble; balam = force; tat = let it; upapadyatāt = be present; atraiva = here itself; etat = this; naḥ = our; kṛityam = operation; ghoram hi = is indeed dreadful; prayujyatām = let is be discharged; vikrameṇa = daringly."

"Let whatever little of feeble forces stay back in Kishkindha, as our operation will indeed be dreadful. It has to be discharged daringly."

Sloka 14

sāgara ogha nibham bhīmam agra anīkam mahābalāḥ || 6-4-14
kapi simhā prakarṣantu śataśo atha sahasraśaḥ |

14. kapisimhāḥ = let the best of monkeys; mahābalāḥ = with great strength; shatashaḥ = in hundreds; atha = and; sahasrashaḥ = in thousands; prakarshhantu = lead; bhīmam = the formidable; agrānīkam = front of the army; sāgaraughanibham = which is akin to an oceanic stream."

"Let the best of monkeys with great strength in hundreds and thousands lead the formidable of the army, which is akin to an oceanic stream."

Sloka 15

gajaḥ ca giri samkāśo gavayaḥ ca mahābalaḥ || 6-4-15
gava akṣaḥ ca agrato yāntu gavām dṛptā iva ṛṣabhāḥ |

15. gajashcha = (Let) Gaja; girisamkāshaḥ = who is equal to a mountain; gavayashcha = Gavaya; mahābalaḥ = a very strong man; gavākshhashcha = and Gavaksha; yātu = march; agrataḥ = in front; gavām iva = as in front of cows; dṛipataḥ = a majestic; ṛishhabhaḥ = full.

"Let Gaja, equal to a mountain, Gavaya a very strong warrior and Gavaksha march in front, as a majestic bull marches in front of a cow-herd."

Sloka 16

yātu vānara vāhinyā vānaraḥ plavatām patiḥ || 6-4-16
pālayan dakṣiṇam pārśvam ṛṣabho vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |

16. ṛishhabhaḥ vānaraḥ = Let the monkey called Rishabha; plavatām patiḥ = lord of the simians; vānararshhabhaḥ = and the best of the primates; yātu = march forward; pālayan = guarding; dakshhinam = the right; pārshvam = side; vānaravāhinyāḥ = of the army of simians.

"Let the monkey called Rishabha, Lord of the simians and the best of the primates march forward, duly guarding the right side of the army of the simians."

Sloka 17

gandha hastī iva durdharṣas tarasvī gandha mādanaḥ || 6-4-17
yātu vānara vāhinyāḥ savyam pārśvam adhiṣṭhitaḥ |

17. gandhamādanaḥ = Let Gandhamādna; tarasvī = with a strength; durdharshhaḥ = unconquerable; gandhahastīva = like an elephant in rut; yātu = proceed; adhishhṭhitaḥ = duly guarding; savyam = the left; pārshvam = side; vānaravāhinyāḥ = of the army of monkeys.

"Let Gandhamadana, with a strength unconquerable like an elephant in rut, proceed duly guarding the left side of the army of monkeys."

Sloka 18

yāsyāmi bala madhye aham bala ogham abhiharṣayan || 6-4-18
adhiruhya hanūmantam airāvatam iva īśvaraḥ |

18. aham = I; adhiruhya = mounted; hanūmantam = on (the shoulders of) ḥanuman; airāvatamiva = like on Airavata; īshvaraḥ = Indra; yāsyāmi = will march; balamadhye = in the centre of my troops; abhiharshhayan = cheering balangham = the multitude of army.

"I myself, mounted on the shoulders of Hanuman, like Indra on Airavata, will march in the centre of my troops, duly cheering the multitude of army."

Sloka 19

angadena eṣa samyātu lakṣmaṇaḥ ca antaka upamaḥ || 6-4-19
sārvabhaumena bhūta īśo draviṇa adhipatis yathā |

19. eshhaḥ lakshhmaṇashcha = Let this Lakshmana; antakopamaḥ = like of the lord of Death; samyātu = march; aṇgadena = on the shoulders of Angada; sārvabhaumena yathā = like on an elephant called ṣarvabhauma; draviṇādhipatiḥ = Kubera the lord of Riches; bhuteshaḥ = and the sovereign of beings.

"Let this Lakshmana, resembling the lord of Death, march on the shoulders of Angada like Kubera the lord of riches and the sovereign of beings marches on an elephant called Sarvabhauma."

Sloka 20

jāmbavāmḥ ca suṣeṇaḥ ca vega darśī ca vānaraḥ || 6-4-20
ṛkṣa rājo mahāsattvaḥ kukṣim rakṣantu te trayaḥ |

20. mahāsattvaḥ = let the highly strong; jāmbavānshcha = Jambavan; ṛiksharājaḥ = the lord of the bears; sushhenashcha = ṣushena; vānarāshcha = and the monkey; vegadarshhī = called Vegadarshi; te = those; trayaḥ = three; rakshhantu = guard; kukshim = the middle part.

Let the highly strong, Jambavan with Sushena and the monkey called Vegadarshi, all three, guard the middle part of the army."

Sloka 21

rāghavasya vacaḥ śrutvā sugrīvo vāhinī patiḥ || 6-4-21
vyādideśa mahāvīryān vānarān vānara Rṣabhaḥ |

21. shrutvā = having heard; vachaḥ = the words; rāghavasya = of Rama; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; vāhinīpatiḥ = commander of the forces; vānararshhabhaḥ = the lion among monkeys; mahāvīryaḥ = with great valour; vyādidesha = gave orders; vānarām = to the monkeys.

"Hearing the words of Rama, Sugreeva with great valour, the commander of forces and the lion among monkeys gave orders to the monkeys accordingly."

Sloka 22

te vānara gaṇāḥ sarve samutpatya yuyutsavaḥ || 6-4-22
guhābhyaḥ śikharebhyaḥ ca āśu pupluvire tadā |

22. tadā = then; sarve = all; te = those; vanaragaṇāḥ = troops of monkeys; mahaujanāḥ = with great speed; samutpatya = together raised up; āshu = and quickly; pupluvire = bounced; guhābhyaḥ = from caves; shikharebhyashcha = and peaks of mountains.

Then, all those troops of monkeys with great speed together raised up and quickly bounced from caves and mountain-tops.

Sloka 23

tato vānara rājena lakṣmaṇena ca pūjitaḥ || 6-4-23
jagāma rāmo dharma ātmā sasainyo dakṣiṇām diśam |

23. tataḥ = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; dharmātmā = the virtuous man; pujitaḥ = treated respectfully; vānara rājena = by ṣugreeva; lakshmaṇena cha = and Lakshmana; jagāma = went; dakshhinam disham = towards southern direction; sa sainyaḥ = along with army.

Thereafter Rama the virtuous man, treated respectfully by Sugreeva and Lakshmana, moved towards southern direction, along with the army.

Sloka 24

śataiḥ śata sahasraiḥ ca koṭībhir ayutair api || 6-4-24
vāraṇābhiḥ ca haribhir yayau parivRtas tadā |

24. tadā = at that time; yayau = (Rama) went; parivṛitaḥ = surrounded; haribhiḥ = by monkeys; vāraṇābhaiḥ = looking like elephants; shataiḥ = (numbering) the hundreds; shatasahasraiḥ = hundreds of thousands; kotibhishcha = and in crores.

At that time, Rama went surrounded by monkeys, looking like elephants, numbering in hundreds, hundreds of thousands and crores.

Sloka 25

tam yāntam anuyāti sma mahatī hari vāhinī || 6-4-25
hṛṣṭāḥ pramuditāḥ sarve sugrīveṇa abhipālitāḥ |

25. sā = that; mahatī = extensive; harivāhinī = army of monkeys; anuyāntī = followed; tam = Rama; yāntam = who was marching (along) sarve = all those (monkeys) pālitāḥ = maintained; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; hṛishhṭāḥ = were rejoicing; pramuditāḥ = with delight

That extensive army of monkeys followed Rama who was marching in the lead. All those monkeys maintained by Sugreeva were rejoicing with delight.

Sloka 26

āplavantaḥ plavantaḥ ca garjantaḥ ca plavam gamāḥ || 6-4-26
kṣvelanto ninadantaḥ ca jagmur vai dakṣiṇām diśam |

26. plavangamāḥ = the monkeys; plavantaḥ = jumping; āplavantaḥ = overwhelmingly; garjantashcha = with roaring sound; kshhveLantaḥ = jest fully; ninadantashcha = playing (musical instruments) jagmuḥ vai = marched; dakshhinamdisham = towards southern direction.

The monkeys, jumping overwhelmingly with roaring sound and jest fully playing musical instruments (like trumpets) marched towards southern direction.

Sloka 27

bhakṣayantaḥ sugandhīni madhūni ca phalāni ca || 6-4-27
udvahanto mahāvṛkṣān manjarī punja dhāriṇaḥ |

27. bhakshhayantaḥ = eating; sugandhīni = good-smelling; madhūni = honeys; phalānicha = and fruits; udvahantaḥ = carrying; mahāvṛikshhān = large branches; maJṇjarīpuJṇjadhāriṇaḥ = bearing clusters of blossoms in multitude.

They marched on, eating good-smelling honeys and fruits and carrying large branches bearing clusters of blossoms in multitude.

Sloka 28

anyonyam sahasā dṛṣṭā nirvahanti kṣipanti ca || 6-4-28
patantaḥ ca utpatanti anye pātayanti apare parān |

28. dṛiptāḥ = wild monkeys; nirvahanti = would lift up; kshhipanti = and throw; anyonyam = one another; sahasā = all of a sudden; anye = some others; patntaḥ cha = were hanging down and utpatanti = flying upwards; apare = some others; pātayanti = were throwing down; parān = others.

Wild monkeys would lift up and throw one another all of a sudden. Some others were hanging down and flying upwards. Some other monkeys were throwing down others.

Sloka 29

rāvaṇo no nihantavyaḥ sarve ca rajanī carāḥ || 6-4-29
iti garjanti harayo rāghavasya samīpataḥ |

29. harayaḥ = monkeys; samīpataḥ = nearer; rāghavasya = to Rama; iti garjanti = were roaring thus; naḥ = to us; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; nihantavyaḥ = is worthy of killing; rajanīcharāshcha = and also demons; sarve = entirely.

ṃonkeys close to Rama were thus shouting, "To us, Ravana is worthy of killing and also the entire demons."

Sloka 30

purastād ṛṣabhho vīro nīlaḥ kumuda eva ca || 6-4-30
pathānam śodhayanti sma vānarair bahubhiḥ saha |

30. ṛishhbhaḥ = Rishabaha; nīlaḥ = ṇila; kumuda eva cha = and also Kumuda; vīraḥ = the courageous; bahubhiḥ vānaraiḥ = along with many monkeys; shodhayanti = were clearing up; paṇthānam = the path; purastāt = ahead.

Rishabha, Nila, and the courageous Kumuda along with many monkeys were clearing up the path ahead.

Sloka 31

madhye tu rājā sugrīvo rāmo lakṣmaṇa eva ca || 6-4-31
bahubhir balibhir bhīmair vRtāḥ śatru nibarhaṇaḥ |

31. rājā = the king; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; rāmaḥ = Rama; lakshmaṇa eva cha = and Lakshmana; shatrunibarhaṇāḥ = the destroyers of enemies; vṛitaḥ = were moving; madhye = in the centre; bahubhiḥ = along with many monkeys; balibhiḥ = which are robust; bhīmāḥ = and terrible.

Sugreeva the king of monkeys, Rama and Lakshmana the destroyers of enemies were moving in the centre along with many robust and terrible monkeys.

Sloka 32

hariḥ śata balir vīraḥ koṭībhir daśabhir vṛtaḥ || 6-4-32
sarvām eko hi avaṣṭabhya rarakṣa hari vāhinīm |

32. vīraḥ = the heroic; shatabaliḥ = ṣatabali; hariḥ = the monkey; vṛitaḥ = who was accompanied by; dashabhiḥ = ten; kotibhiḥ = crores (of monkeys) avashhṭabhya = standing firmly; ekaḥ = alone; abhirakshhati = guarded; harivāhinīm = the army of monkeys.

The heroic monkey satabali who was accompanied by ten crores of monkeys, standing alone firmly, guarded the whole army of monkeys.

Sloka 33

koṭī śata parīvāraḥ kesarī panaso gajaḥ || 6-4-33
arkaḥ ca atibalaḥ pārśvam ekam tasya abhirakṣati |

33. kesarī = Kesari; kotīshataparīvāraḥ = with a retinue of a hundred crore; pansaḥ = Panasa; gajaḥ = Gaja; arkashcha = and Arka; bahubhiḥ = along with many monkeys; abhirakshhati = were protecting; ekam = one; pārshvam = flank (of that army).

Kesari with a retinue of a hundred crore, Panasa, Gaja and Arka along with many monkeys were protecting one flank of that army.

Sloka 34

suṣeṇo jāmbavāmḥ caiva ṛkṣair bahubhir āvṛtaḥ || 6-4-34
sugrīvam purataḥ kṛtvā jaghanam samrarakṣatuḥ |

34. sushheṇaḥ = ṣushena; jāmbavashchaiva = and Jambavanta; āvṛitaḥ = surrounded; bahubhiḥ = by many; ṛikshhaiḥ = bears; kṛitvā = keeping; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; purataḥ = in front; samrarakshhatuḥ = protected; jaghanam = the hinder part of the army.

Keeping Sugreeva in front, Sushena and Jambavanta surrounded by many bears, protected the hinder part of that army.

Sloka 35

teṣām senā patir vīro nīlo vānara pumgavaḥ || 6-4-35
sampatan patatām śreṣṭhas tad balam paryapālayat |

35. nīlaḥ = ṇila; teshhām = their; senāpatiḥ = chief of the army; vīraḥ = the brave; vānarapuṇ^gavaḥ = the best among monkeys; samyataḥ = the self-controlled; shreshhṭhah = the foremost; charatām = among movable beings; paryapālayat = was protecting in every direction; tat = that; balam = army.

Nila their chief of the army, the brave and the best among monkeys, the self controlled and the foremost among movable beings, was protecting atha army in every direction.

Sloka 36

valīmukhaḥ prajaṅghaśca jambho.atha rabhasaḥ kapiḥ || 6-4-36
sarvataḥ ca yayur vīrās tvarayantaḥ plavam gamān |

36. valīmūkhaḥ = Valimukha; prajaṇghashcha = Prajangha; jambhaḥ = Jambha; atha = and; rabhasaḥ = Rabhasa; kapiḥ = the monkey; yayuḥ = were moving; sarvataḥ = on all sides; tvarayantaḥ = urging forward; plavaṇ^gamān = the monkeys.

Valimukha, Prajangha, Jambha and Rabhasa the monkey were moving on all sides, urging the monkeys forward.

Sloka 37

evam te hari śārdūlā gaccanto bala darpitāḥ || 6-4-37
apaśyaṃs te giri śreṣṭham sahyam druma latā yutam |
sāgara ogha nibham bhīmam tad vānara balam mahat || 6-4-38

37;38. te = those; harishārdūlaḥ = foremost among the monkeys; baladarpitāḥ = proud of their strength; evam = thus; gachchhantaḥ = marching; apashyanta = saw; sahyam = the ṣahya mountain; girishreshhṭam = the best of mountains; girishatāyutam = joined with many small mountains; sarāmsi = lakes; suphullāni = fully abounding in flowers; varāṇi = eminent; taṭākāni cha = ponds.

Those foremost among the monkeys, proud of their strength, thus marching with the army, saw the Sahya mountain the best of mountains joined with many small mountains, lakes fully abounding in flowers and eminent ponds.

Sloka 39

rāmasya śāsanam jñātvā bhīmakopasya bhītavat |
varjayannagarābhyāśāṃstathā janapadānapi || 6-4-39
sāgaraughanibham bhīmam tadvānarabalam mahat |
nihsasarpa mahāghoṣam bhīma vega iva arṇavaḥ || 6-4-40

39;40. tat = that; mahat = large; vānarabalam = army of monkeys; bhīmam = terrific; sāgaraughanibham = like an ocean-flood; jJṇyātvā = cognizing; shāsanam = the command; rāmasya = of Rama; bhīma kopasya = which is frightfully enraged; bhītavat = having terror-stricken; varjayat = abandoning; nagarābhyāshān = the vicinity of towns; tathā = and; janapadānapi = and even villages; niḥsasarpa = marched; ārṇavam iva = like an ocean; mahāghoram = highly terrific; mahāghoshham = with great hoaring sound.

That large army of monkeys, terrific like an ocean-flood cognizing the command of Rama which is frightfully enraging, having terror-stricken, abandoning the vicinity of towns and even villages, marched like a highly dreadful ocean with a great hoaring sound.

Sloka 41

anila uddhūtam ākāśe pravalgatam iva ūrmibhiḥ |

122;123. mahātmanaḥ = the high-souled; te = those (monkeys) dadṛishuḥ = saw; vātāhata jalāshayam = the sea lashed with winds; ratnaughajalasamnādam = resonating with abundance of precious stones and water; vishhaktamiva = as in a grip; vāyunā = of hurricane; utpatantam = rising high; kruddhamiva = as if enraged; yādogaṇasamākulam = filled with a number of aquatic creatures; aniloddhūtam = tossed by them; ākāshe = in the air; pralapantam iva = by seemingly murmering; ūrmibhiḥ = waves.

Those high-souled monkeys saw the sea lashed with winds, resonating with abundance of precious stones and water, rising high as if enraged in a grip of hurricane, filled with a number of aquatic creatures and tossed by them in the air by seemingly murmuring waves.

Sloka 124

tato vismayāmāpannā harayo dadṛśuḥ sthitāḥ || 6-4-124
brānta ūrmi jala samnādam pralolam iva sāgaram |

124. tataḥ = then; sthitāḥ = the standing; harayaḥ = monkeys; āpannaḥ = were struck; vismayam = with astonishment; dadṛishuḥ = (and) saw; sāgaram = the sea; pralolamiva = seemed moving; bhrāntormijālasamnādam = full of resonance produced by a multitude of dashing waves rolling to and fro.

The standing monkeys were struck with amazement to see the ocean seemed moving, full of resonance produced by a multitude of dashing waves rolling to and fro.

ityārśe śrrimadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe caturthaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 4th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
