Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda


Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 42
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The dust raised by Rama's chariot having disappeared for a while, Dasaratha feels dejected and fell insensible on ground. When Kaikeyi comes forward to support him he scolds and asks her not to touch him. Kausalya then lifts him up and persuades him to return to their house. His personal attendants take him to Kausalya's apartments and seeing him plunged in grief, Kausalya seats herself beside him and began to lament in various ways.


Sloka 1

yāvat tu niryataḥ tasya rajo rūpam adṛśyata |
na eva ikṣvāku varaḥ tāvat samjahāra ātma cakṣuṣī || 2-42-1

1. yāvat = so long as; rajorūpam = the appearance of dust; adṛishyata = was seen; tasya = (while) Rama; niryataḥ = was going; tāvat = so long; ikshhvākuvaraḥ = Dasaratha the best of Ikshvakus; naiva samjahāra = did not withdraw; ātmachakshhushhī = his eyes.

As long as the dust raised by the wheels of Rama's chariot could be seen, so long did the king not withdraw his gaze from it.

Sloka 2

yāvad rājā priyam putram paśyati atyanta dhārmikam |
tāvad vyavardhata iva asya dharaṇyām putra darśane || 2-42-2

2. yāvat = As long as; rājā = the king; pashyati = was seeing; priyam = his beloved; putram = son; atyanta dhārmikam = who was exceedingly virtuous; tāvat = so long; dharaṇyām = the dust in earth; vyavardhateva = appeared to be raising; asya putra darshane = for the purpose of seeing his son.

As long as the king was seeing his beloved son who was exceedingly virtuous, so long did the dust in the earth appear to be rising for the purpose of seeing his son.

Sloka 3

na paśyati rajo api asya yadā rāmasya bhūmipaḥ |
tadā ārtaḥ ca viṣaṇṇaḥ ca papāta dharaṇī tale || 2-42-3

3. yadā = When; rajo api = the dust assuredly; asya rāmasya = of the Rama;na pashyati = was no longer seen; tadā = then; bhūmipaḥ = the king; ārtaḥcha = afflicted by; vishhaṇṇaḥcha = dejection; papāta = fell; dharaṇītale = on ground.

When the dust raised by Rama's chariot was no longer visible, the unfortunate king afflicted by dejection, fell insensible on ground.

Sloka 4

tasya dakṣiṇam anvagāt kausalyā bāhum anganā |
vāmam ca asya anvagāt pārśvam kaikeyī bharata priyā || 2-42-4

4. kausalya = Kausalya; aṇganā = his wife; anvāgāt = took place; tasya dakshhiṇam bāhum = on his right side .kaikeyīcha = Kaikeyi ;sumadhyamā = of charming limbs; anvagāt = took her place; vāmam = on the left; pārshvam = side.

When Dasaratha fell down, Kausalya his wife took her place on his right side to lift him up and Kaikeyi of charming limbs took her place on his left side.

Sloka 5

tām nayena ca sampanno dharmeṇa nivayena ca |
uvāca rājā kaikeyīm samīkṣya vyathita indriyaḥ || 2-42-5

5. rājā = ṭhe king; sampannaḥ = endowed with; nayena = wisdom; dharmeṇa = virtue; vinayenacha = and discretion; samīkshhya = seeing; tām kaikeyīm = that Kaikeyi; uvācha = said to her; vyathitendriyaḥ = in his agitated senses.

The king endowed with wisdom virtue and discretion seeing Kaikeyi beside him, said to her in great agitation as follows:

Sloka 6

kaikeyi mā mama angāni sprākṣīs tvam duṣṭa cāriṇī |
na hi tvām draṣṭum iccāmi na bhāryā na ca bāndhavī || 2-42-6

6. kaikeyi = Oh;Kaikeyi! tvam = you dushṭachāriṇī = of wicked conduct; mā sprākshhī = do not touch; mama = my; aṇgāni = limbs; nahi ichchhāmi = I do not want; drashhṭum = to see; tvām = you; na bhāryā = are not my wife; na = nor; bāndhavī = a relative

"Oh, wicked Kaikeyi! Do not touch my limbs. I do not wish to see you .You are no longer my wife nor a relative."

Sloka 7

ye ca tvām upajīvanti na aham teṣām na te mama |
kevala artha parām hi tvām tyakta dharmām tyajāmy aham || 2-42-7

7. aham = I; na = am not; ye cha = of those; anujivanti = who are; dependent ;tvām = on you; na = nor; te = are they; mama = on me. aham = I; tyajāmi = discord; tvām = you; tvaktadharmām = who left righteousness; kevalārthaparām = and interested in avariciousness alone.

"I am not of those who are dependent on you nor are they anything to me. I discord you, by whom righteousness has been discorded and who are solely interested in avariciousness."

Sloka 8

agṛhṇām yac ca te pāṇim agnim paryaṇayam ca yat |
anujānāmi tat sarvam asmiml loke paratra ca || 2-42-8

8. tatsarvam = All that; āgṛhṇāmitiyat = by taking; te = your; pāṇim = hand; paryaṇayamchayat = circumabubulating; agnim = the sacred fire; anujānāmi = I renounce; asmin loke = in this world; paratracha = and the next.

All that contract which I entered into by taking your hand and circumambulating the sacred fire, I now renounce in this world and the next. "

Sloka 9

bharataḥ cet pratītaḥ syāt rājyam prāpya idam avyayam |
yan me sa dadyāt pitr artham mā mā tat dattam āgamat || 2-42-9

9. syātchet = In case; bharataḥ = Bharata; pratititaḥ = is cheerful man; prāpya = after obtaining; idam rājyam = this kingdom ; avyayam = which is imperishable; yat = what ever; dadyāt = is given ; me = to me; pitrartham = in the form of obsequial rites; māgamat = let it reach; mām = me.

"If Bharata feels cheerful for having got this kingdom which is imperishable, let the obsequial rites performed to me after my death do not reach me"

Sloka 10

atha reṇu samudhvastam tam utthāpya nara adhipam |
nyavartata tadā devī kausalyā śoka karśitā || 2-42-10

10. atha = ṭherafter;kausalya = Kausalya; devī = the queen; shokakarshitā = overwhelmed with grief; samutthāpya = lifted up; tam narādhipam = that king; reṇusamuddhvastam = who was covered with dust; tadā = then; nyavartata = returned (home).

ṃeanwhile Kausalya the queen overwhelmed with grief, lifted up the king who was covered with dust and returned to her house.

Sloka 11

hatvā iva brāhmaṇam kāmāt spṛṣṭvā agnim iva pāṇinā |
anvatapyata dharma ātmā putram saṃcintya tāpasam || 2-42-11

11. dharmātma = ṭhe virtuous Dasaratha ; samchinthya :thinking deeply of; putram = his son ;rāghavam = Rama; anvatapyata = was desolated; hatveva = as one who has slain; brahmaṇam = a Brahmin; kāmāt = intentionally; spṛishhṭvā = or as touching; agnim = fire ; pāṇinā = by hand.

The virtuous monarch, thinking deeply of his son Rama, was so desolated as one who has slain a brahmin intentionally or as touching the fire by hand.

Sloka 12

nivṛtya eva nivṛtya eva sīdataḥ ratha vartmasu |
rājño na atibabhau rūpam grastasya aṃśumataḥ yathā || 2-42-12

12. nivṛityaiva nivṛityaiva = ṭurning again and again ;rathavartmasu = towards the track of the chariot; rūpam = the countenance ;rāG^yaḥ = of the king ;sīdataḥ = in grief ;nāti babhou = lost its radiance ; amshumataḥyathā = like sun ;grastasya = when swallowed by Rahu .*

Turning again and again towards the track of the chariot, the countenance of the king in grief lost its radiance like sun losing its radiance when swallowed by Rahu.

▼

*-Rahu- the inauspicious planet sent to swallow the sun at the time of eclipse.

Sloka 13

vilalāpa ca duhkha ārtaḥ priyam putram anusmaran |
nagara antam anuprāptam buddhvā putram atha abravīt || 2-42-13

13. duhkhārtāḥ = Afflicted by grief; anusmaran = thinking of ; priyam = his beloved; putram = son ;vilalāpacha = he lamented;.atha = ṭhereafter; buddhvā = knowing ; putram = his son;anuprāptam = reaching ; nagarāntam = the end of the city ; abravīt = he said :

Afflicted by grief, thinking of his beloved son he lamented. Knowing that his son reached the end of the city, he said as follows:

Sloka 14

vāhanānām ca mukhyānām vahatām tam mama ātmajam |
padāni pathi dṛśyante sa mahātmā na dṛśyate || 2-42-14

14. padāni = ṭhe marks of hooves; mukhyānām = oh excellent ; vāhanām = horses;vahatām = which carried;mama = my;ātmajam = child ;tam = that Rama ;dṛshyante = were seen; pathi = on the way; saḥ = ḥe ; mahātmā = the great souled; na dṛishyate = is not seen .

"The marks of hooves of excellent horses which carried away my child are seen on the way. But he the great souled one is not being seen"

Sloka 15

sa nūnam kvacit eva adya vṛkṣa mūlam upāśritaḥ |
kāṣṭham vā yadi vā aśmānam upadhāya śayiṣyate || 2-42-15
utthāsyati ca medinyāḥ kṛpaṇaḥ pāṃśu guṇṭhitaḥ |
vinihśvasan prasravaṇāt kareṇūnām iva ṛṣabhaḥ || 2-42-16

15;16. yaḥ = ḥe who; shete = slept; sukheshhu = in comfort ;upadhāneshhu = on coverings ;chandana rūshhitaḥ = sprinkled with the essence of sandalwood; vījyamāna = refreshed by fans over him ; mahārhābhiḥ = by the most deserving ;strībhiḥ = women; saḥ = that Rama ;mama sutottamaḥ = the best of all my sons ;nūnam = will from now on ;upāshritaḥ = has to take refuge ;vṛikshhamūlam = at the foot of a tree ;kvachideva = somewhere ;shayishhyate = lying down; kāshhṭam vā = on the trunk ; yadivā = or ; upadhāya = rest on; āshmānam = a stone."

"He who slept on comfort on coverings sprinkled with the essence of sandalwood and refreshed by fans waved over by him by the most deserving women, Rama the best of all my sons , will from now on , has to take refuge at the foot of a tree somewhere, lying down on its trunk or rest on a stone."

Sloka 17

utthāsyati ca medinyāḥ kṛpaṇaḥ pāṃsuguṇṭhitaḥ |
viniḥsvasan prasravaṇātka reṇūnāmi varṣbhaḥ || 2-42-17

17. kṛipeṇaḥ = ṭhe unfortunate one; utthāsyaticha = will wake up; medinyāḥ = from the hard earth; pāmshugunDitaḥ = stained with dust ;vivasvasan = sighing;ṛishhabhaḥ = like a bull elephant ;the lord of ; kareṇūnām = female elephants; prasravaṇāt iva = (rising from)the side of a hill."

"The unfortunate one will wake up from the hard earth stained with dust, sighing like a bull-elephant rising from the side of a hill"

Sloka 18

drakṣyanti nūnam puruṣā dīgha bāhum vane carāḥ |
rāmam utthāya gaccantam loka nātham anāthavat || 2-42-18

18. nūnam = from today; purushhāḥ = the men ; vanecharāḥ = who are the inhabitants of the forest; drakshhyanti = will behold ; dīrghabāhum = that long armed ; lokanātham = lord of the world; rāmam = Rama ; utthāya = rising from the ground ;gachchhantam = and wandering around ; anāthavat = like an orphan."

From today the inhabitants of the forest will behold that long armed lord of the world, Rama, rising from the ground and wandering around like an orphan"

Sloka 19

sakāmā bhava kaikeyi vidhavā rājyam āvasa |
kaṇṭakākramaṇa klāntāvanamadya gamiṣyati || 2-42-19

19. sā = ṭhat ṣita; sukhasadochitā = who was always accustomed to comforts; ishhṭā = the beloved; sutā = daughter; janakasya = of king Janaka ; adya = now ; gamishhyanti = will wander; vanam = in the forest; kaṇṭakākramaṇaklāntā = exhausted through the thorny undergrowth."

"The daughter of Janaka, who was always accustomed to comforts, will wander exhausted in the forest through the thorny undergrowth "

Sloka 20

anabhijñā vanānām sā nūnam bhayamupaiṣyati |
śvāpadānrdhitam śrutvā gamibhīram romaharṣṇam || 2-42-20

20. sā = ṣita ;anabhiG^yā = who was not accustomed ;vanānām = to woodlands ;nūnam = from now on; upaishhyati = will obtain ;bhayam = fear; shrutvā = hearing ;shvāpadā narditam = the roaring sound of wild beasts; gambhīram = which was thundering; romaharshhaṇam = causing hair to stand on end.

"Sita who was not earlier accustomed to woodlands, from now on will live in terror, hearing the roaring sound of wild beasts ,which was thundering ."

Sloka 21

sakāmā bhavakaikeyi vidhavā rājya māvasa |
na hi tam puruṣa vyāghram vinā jīvitum utsahe || 2-42-21

21. Kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi! Bhava = be; sakāmā = your desire fulfilled! āvasa = Dwell ; rājyam = in the kingdom ;vidhavā = as a widow; na utsahe hi = I do not indeed wish; jīvitum = to live; tamvinā = without Rama ; purushhavyāghram = the tiger among men."

"Oh, Kaikeyi! Let your desire be fulfilled! Dwell in the kingdom as a widow. I do not indeed wish to survive without Rama the tiger among men."

Sloka 22

iti evam vilapan rājā jana oghena abhisamvṛtaḥ |
apasnātaiva ariṣṭam praviveśa pura uttamam || 2-42-22

22. ityevam = ṭhus; vilapam = lamenting; rājā = the king; abhisamvṛitaḥ = surrounded; janoughena = by his people ; pravivesha = entered ; purothamam = Ayodhya the best of cities; arishhṭam iva = like enering a disastrous house; apasnātaḥ = by the one who has taken the funeral bath.

Thus lamenting, the king surrounded by his people, entered Ayodhya the best of cities like entering a disastrous house by one who has taken the funeral bath.

Sloka 23

śūnya catvara veśma antām samvṛta āpaṇa devatām |
klānta durbala duhkha ārtām na atyākīrṇa mahā pathām |
tām avekṣya purīm sarvām rāmam eva anucintayan |
vilapan prāviśad rājā gṛham sūryaiva ambudam || 2-42-23

23. āvekshhya = seeing; tām = that; sarvām = entire; purīm = city ;shūnya chatvara veshmāntām = with its road junctions and mansions deserted ; samvṛitāpaṇa devatām = with its shops and temples closed ; klānta durbala duḥkhārtām = with people exhausted with fatigue and plunged in affliction ; nātyākīrṇe mahāpathām = its highways not very crowded ; rājā = the king ;prāvishat = entered ;vilāpan = wailing ;anuchintayan = thinking; rāmameva = of Rama alone;ambudam iva = as the cloud ;sūryaḥ = by a sun.

Seeing the entire city with its road-junctions and mansions deserted, its shops and temples closed, with its people exhausted with fatigue and plunged in affliction; its highways not very crowded ,the king entered his palace wailing and thinking of Rama alone as the sun enters a cloud.

Sloka 24

mahā hradam iva akṣobhyam suparṇena hṛta uragam |
rāmeṇa rahitam veśma vaidehyā lakṣmaṇena ca || 2-42-24

24. rahitam = Bereft; rāmeṇa = of Rama;vaidehyā = ṣita ;lakshhmaṇenacha = and Lakshmana ;veshma = the palace ;mahāhradam iva = (looked) like a big pool ; akshhobhyam = rendered unperturbed; hṛitoragam = emptied of snakes ;suvarṇena = by Garuda.

Bereft of Rama Sita and Lakshmana, the palace looked like a big pool rendered unperturbed due to emptying of snakes in it by Garuda*

▼

*Garuda was a mythical bird (chief of feathered race, enemy of the serpent race)

Sloka 25

atha gadgadaśabdastu vilapanmanujādhipaḥ |
uvāca mṛdumandhārtham vacanam dīna masvaram || 2-42-25

25. atha = ṭhereafter; vilapan = the lamenting ;vasudhādhipaḥ = monarch ; uvācha = spoke ; gadgada shabdaḥ = in trembling accents; mṛidu = in soft; dīnam = pitiful ; asvaram = indistinct ; mandārtham = and not clearly intelligible ;vachanam = words .

The lamenting monarch spoke in trembling accents and in soft, pitiful, indistinct and not clearly intelligible words (as follows):

Sloka 26

kausalyāyā gṛham śīghram rāma mātur nayantu mām |
iti bruvantam rājānam anayan dvāra darśitaḥ || 2-42-26

26. nayantu = ṭake; mām = me; shīghram = quickly; gṛiham = to the apartment ; kausalyāyāḥ = of Kausalya ;rāma mātuḥ = the mother of Rama; na anyatra = ṇowhere else ; mama hṛidayasya = my heart ;bhavishhyati hi = will indeed find; ashvāsaḥ = solace ."

"Take me quickly to the apartment of Kausalya the mother of Rama. Nowhere else my heart will find solace "

Sloka 27

iti bruvantam rājāna manayan dvārdarśinaḥ |
kausalyāyā gṛham tatra nyaveśyata vinītavat || 2-42-27

27. dvāradarshinaḥ = the attendants; anayan = took; rājānam = the king ;bruvantam = who was speaking; iti = thus ;kausalyāḥ = to Kausalya's; gṛiham = house . tatra = there; vinītavat = (he was ) gently; nyaveshyata = laid .

The attendants took the king who was speaking as aforesaid, to Kausalya's house and there he was gently laid (on a coach).

Sloka 28

tataḥ tatra praviṣṭasya kausalyāyā niveśanam |
adhiruhya api śayanam babhūva lulitam manaḥ || 2-42-28

28. tataḥ = ṭhen; pravishhṭasya = having entered; kausalyāḥ = Kausalya's; niveshanam = apartment; tasya = his; manaḥ = heart; babhūva = became; lulitam = agitated; adhiruhyāpi = even though laid on ;shayanam = a coach .

Having entered Kausalya's apartment and even though laid on a coach, his heart became agitated.

Sloka 29

putradvayavihīnam ca snuṣayāpi vivarjitam |
apaśyadbhavanam rājā naṣṭacandhramivāmbaram || 2-42-29

29. putradvaya vihīnam = bereft of two sons; snushhayāpi = and the daughter in law also; vivarjitam = having left; rājā = the king; apashyat = saw; bhavanam = the house; ambaram iva = as a sky; nashhṭochandram = without a moon .

Bereft of his two sons and his daughter -in-law also having deserted, the king saw that house devoid of charm as a sky without a moon.

Sloka 30

tac ca dṛṣṭvā mahā rājo bhujam udyamya vīryavān |
uccaiḥ svareṇa cukrośa hā rāghava jahāsi mām || 2-42-30

30. dṛishhṭvā = Looking at; tat = that house; udyamya = and lifting up; bhujam = his arm; vīryavān = the powerful; mahārājaḥ = monarch; chukrosha = cried ; uchchaiḥsvareṇa = in loud voice; hā rāghava = Oh ;Rama! jahāsi = you have left ; mām = us(both your mother and I).

Looking at that palace and lifting up his arm, the powerful monarch cried in a loud voice, " Oh, Rama! You have left us both, your mother and I."

Sloka 31

sukhitā bata tam kālam jīviṣyanti nara uttamāḥ |
pariṣvajantaḥ ye rāmam drakṣyanti punar āgatam || 2-42-31

31. bata = Alas! Ye = ṭhose; narottamaḥ = jewels among men; jīvishhyanti = who will survive; tam kālam = the term; drakshhyanti = and see; rāmam = Rama;punaḥ āgatam = come back ;parishhvajantaḥ = and embracing him ;sukhitāḥ = will be happy .

"Alas! Those jewels among men who will survive the term of fourteen years, seeing Rama come back and embrace him, will be happy indeed."

Sloka 32

atha rātryām prapannāyām kālarātryāmiātmanaḥ |
ardharātre daśarathah kausalyāmidamabravīt || 2-42-32

32. atha = thereupon ;rātrayām = the night ;prasannām = having fallen; kāla rātryāmiva = (the night) resembling the hour of death ;ātmanaḥ = to him; dasarathaḥ = Dasaratha; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words ;kausalyām = to Kausalya; artharātre = at that midnight

Thereupon, the night having fallen, the night resembling the hour of death to him, Dasaratha spoke these words to Kausalya when the midnight struck :

Sloka 33

rāmam me.anugatā dṛṣṭiradyāpi na nivartate |
na tvām paśyāmi kausalye sādhu mām pāṇinā spṛśa || 2-42-33

33. kausalye = "Oh;Kausalya! me dṛishhṭiḥ = ṃy sight ;anugatā = extinguished along with ; rāmam = Rama;na nivartate = and not turned out. adyāpi = Even now; na pashyāmi = I cannot see; tvā = you;sādhu = properly ;spṛisha = touch ;mām = me ;pāṇinā = with your hand."

"Oh, Kausalya! ṃy sight has extinguished along with Rama and not returned. Even now, I am not able to see you properly. Touch me with your hand."

Sloka 34

tam rāmam eva anuvicintayantam |
samīkṣya devī śayane nara indram |
upa upaviśya adhikam ārta rūpāupa |
vinihśvasantī vilalāpa kṛccram || 2-42-34

34. samīkshhya = perceiving; tam narendram = that monarch; shayane = on the coach ;anuvichintayantam = absorbed in the thought ;rāmam eva = of Rama alone; devī = Kausalya; adhikam ārtarūpā = appeared very sad ;upāvishya = and sitting down beside him ;viniḥshvasantī = began to sigh; vilalāpa = and lament; kṛichchham = with distressed look.

Perceiving that monarch on the coach absorbed in the thought of Rama alone, Kausalya appeared very sad and sitting beside him, began to sigh and lament with a distressed look.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe dvicatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 42nd chapter in the Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
