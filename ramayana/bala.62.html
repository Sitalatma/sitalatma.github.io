Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

chapter [Sarga] 62
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Sage Vishvamitra curses his sons as they defy his orders and accords two esoteric hymns to Shunashepa, for chanting them in the Vedic-ritual of Ambarīsha. Shunashepa gets longevity on chanting those hymns. Thus Vishvamitra not only creates another universe as in Trishanku's episode, he even accords longevity, or even deathlessness to mortals by his ascetic power. Such as he is, he is the mentor of Rama, and hence Sage Shatānanda informs Rama about the capabilities of his own mentor, Vishvamitra, in these many episodes.

Sloka 1

śhunaḥśhepam naraśhreṣhṭha gṛhītvā tu mahāyaśhāḥ |
vyaśhrāmat puṣhkare rājā madhyāhne raghunaṃdana || 1-62-1

1. narashreṣṭha = oh, best among men, Rama; raghuna.ndana = oh, Raghu's, legatee; mahāyashāḥ rājā = highly renowned one, that king Ambarīsha; shunaḥshepam gṛihītvā = ṣhunashepa, on taking; madhya ahne = in mid, day - at noontime; puṣkare vyashrāmat = at holy lakeside, took rest.

"Oh, Rama, the best one among men and the legatee of Raghu, on taking Shunashepa that highly renowned king Ambarīsha took rest at noontime on the lakeside of Holy Lake." Thus Sage Shatānanda continued the legend of Shunashepa, as a part of Vishvamitra's legend. [1-62-1]

Sloka 2

tasya viśhramamāṇasya śhunaḥśhepo mahāyaśhāḥ |
puṣhkaram jyeṣhṭham āgaṃya viśhvāmitram dadarśha ha || 1-62-2
tapyantam ṛṣhibhiḥ sārtham mātulam parama āturaḥ |

2, 3a: tasya = his [Ambarīsha]; vishramamāṇasya = while resting; mahāyashāḥ shunaḥshepaḥ = highly brilliant, ṣhunashepa; parama āturaḥ = with high, anxiety; jyeṣṭham puṣkaram āgamya = elder [main one,] holy lakeside, having come; ṛiṣibhiḥ sārtham tapyantam = [other] sages, along with, performing ascesis; mātulam vishvāmitram dadarsha ha = maternal uncle, at Vishvamitra, he [ṣhunashepa] saw, indeed.

"While the king Ambarīsha is taking rest that highly brilliant Shunashepa came to the lakeside of main Holy Lake with high anxiety, and there he indeed saw his maternal uncle Sage Vishvamitra who is performing ascesis along with other sages. [1-62-2, 3a]

Sloka 3

viṣhaṇṇa vadano dīnaḥ tṛṣhṇayā cha śhrameṇa cha || 1-62-3
papāta anke mune rāma vākyam cha idam uvācha ha |

3b, 4a. rāma = oh, Rama; tṛiṣṇayā ca shrameṇa ca = by thirst, also, by strain, also; viṣaṇṇa vadanaḥ = sulky, faced; dīnaḥ = became pitiable; [āshu = immediately]; mune anke papāta = in saint Vishvamitra's, in flank [lap,] fell down; idam vākyam uvāca ha = this, sentence, said, indeed.

"Shunashepa became pitiable and sulky faced by strain and thirst, oh, Rama, and he immediately fell in the lap of saint Vishvamitra saying this sentence. [1-62-3b, 4a]

Sloka 4

na me asti mātā na pitā jñātayo bāndhavāḥ kutaḥ || 1-62-4
trātum arhasi mām sauṃya dharmeṇa munipuṃgava |

4b, 5a. saumya munipungava = oh, peaceable, sage; me mātā na asti = to me, mother, not, is there; pita na = father, nor; jñātayaḥ bāndhavāḥ kutaḥ = cousins, relatives, wherefore; mām dharmeṇa trātum arhasi = me, according to saintliness, to protect, apt of you.

" 'I have no mother or a father to save me. Then wherefore cousins or relatives will be there to protect me. Oh, peaceable saint the eminent, it will be apt of you to protect me according to saintliness. [1-62-4b, 5a]

Sloka 5

trātā tvam hi naraśhreṣhṭha sarveṣhām tvam hi bhāvanaḥ || 1-62-5
rājā cha kṛtakāryaḥ syāt aham dīrgha āyuḥ avyayaḥ |
svarga lokam upāśhnīyām tapaḥ taptvā hi anuttamam || 1-62-6

5b, 6. narashreṣṭha = oh, best one among men - illustrious sage; tvam sarveṣām trātā hi = you are, to each and every one, saviour, isn't it; tvam bhāvanaḥ hi = you are, apologist - upholder - guardian angel, isn't it; rājā ca kṛita kāryaḥ syāt = king Ambarīsha, also, achieved, [of his] purpose, let him be; aham dīrgha āyuḥ = I, with long, life; a vyayaḥ = not, spent on becoming imperishable; an uttamam tapaḥ taptvā hi = un, excelled, ascesis, on performing, indeed; svarga lokam upāshnīyām = heavenly, worlds, I wish to enjoy.

" 'Oh, illustrious sage, you alone are the saviour to each and every one, isn't it! You alone are the guardian angel, isn't it! Hence, let the purpose of the king Ambarīsha be achieved, and let longevity come to me, and I on becoming imperishable and indeed on performing an unexcelled ascesis, I wish to enjoy in heavenly worlds. [1-62-5b, 6]

Sloka 7

sa me nātho hi anāthasya bhava bhavyena chetasā |
pitā iva putram dharmāatman trātum arhasi kilbiṣhāt || 1-62-7

7. a nāthasya = for not, protected one; me = to me; saḥ = such as you are; [tvam = you]; bhavyena cetasā hi = with providential, sentiment, indeed; nāthaḥ bhāva = [my] providence, you become; dharmāatman = oh, virtue-souled one; pitā putram iva = father, for son, as with; kilbiṣāt trātum arhasi = from misfortune, to protect, apt of you.

" You shall be my providence with a providential sentiment as I stand unprotected, and oh, virtue souled one, it will be apt of you to protect me from misfortune, like a father protecting his own son.' Thus Shunashepa appealed to Vishvamitra. [1-62-7]

Sloka 8

tasya tat vachanam śhrutvā viśhvāmitro mahātapāḥ |
sāntvayitvā bahu vidham putrān idam uvācha ha || 1-62-8

8. mahā tapāḥ vishvāmitraḥ = of higher ascesis, Vishvamitra; tasya tat vacanam shrutvā = his [of ṣhunashepa,] that, sentence, on hearing; bahu vidham sāntvayitvā = in many, a way, having pacified; putrān idam uvāca ha = to [his] sons, this, said, indeed.

"On hearing that sentence of Shunashepa and on pacifying him in many ways, Vishvamitra of higher ascesis, indeed said this to his sons. [1-62-8]

Sloka 9

yat kṛte pitaraḥ putrān janayanti śhubha arthinaḥ |
para loka hita arthāya tasya kālo ayam āgataḥ || 1-62-9

9. shubha arthinaḥ pitaraḥ = positivity, desirers of, parents; yat kṛite = for what, reason; para loka hita arthāya = in other, world, welfare, for the purpose of; putrān janayanti = sons, engender; tasya = its [that reason's]; ayam kālaḥ āgataḥ = this [is that,] time, [that] has come.

" 'For what reason parents engender sons, desiring positivity and for the purpose of welfare in the other worlds, this is the time that has come for fulfilling that reason. [1-62-9]

Vividly: 'A father's ambition in begetting sons is to do something good and positive to the society in the present world and when departed a right place is acquired in heavens through these sons, by their yearly death-day rituals etc., and hence you do some good in saving this boy from premature death and earn an apt place for me in heavens...'

Sloka 10

ayam muni suto bālo mattaḥ śharaṇam ichchhati |
asya jīvita mātreṇa priyam kuruta putrakāḥ || 1-62-10

10. muni sutaḥ ayam bālaḥ = sage's, son, this, youngster; mattaḥ = from me; sharaṇam icchati = shelter, aspires; putrakāḥ = oh, sons; asya = to him; jīvita mātreṇa = life, just [by giving]; priyam kuruta = satisfaction, be given.

" 'This youngster is the son of sage and he aspires shelter from me. Hence, oh, sons, give him satisfaction just by giving life to him. [1-62-10]

Sloka 11

sarve sukṛta karmāṇaḥ sarve dharma parāyaṇāḥ |
paśhu bhūtā narendrasya tṛptim agneḥ prayachchhata || 1-62-11

11. sarve = all of you; su kṛita karmāṇaḥ = well, done, pious deeds; sarve dharma parāyaṇāḥ = you all, in probity, have abidance; narendrasya = of the king; pashu bhūtā = [ritual] animal, on becoming; agneḥ tṛiptim prayacchata = to Fire-god, appeasement, you bestow.

" 'You all have done very good pious deeds and you all abide by probity. Hence, you bestow appeasement to Fire-god on your becoming the ritual-animals of king Ambarīsha in lieu of this boy Shunashepa. [1-62-11]

Sloka 12

nāthanān cha śhunaḥśhepo yajñaḥ cha avighnato bhavet |
devatāḥ tarpitāḥ cha syuḥ mama cha api kṛtam vachaḥ || 1-62-12

12. shunaḥshepaḥ nāthanān ca = ṣhunashepa will be, with protectors, also; yajñaḥ ca a vighnataḥ bhavet = Vedic-ritual, also, un, impeded, it will become; devatāḥ tarpitāḥ ca syuḥ = gods, oblated, also, they will be; mama vacaḥ ca api kṛitam = mine, word, also, even, actualised.

" 'As a result, Shunashepa will have protectors, Vedic-ritual will be unimpeded, gods will be oblated, and my word too will be actualised.' Thus Vishvamitra said to his sons. [1-62-12]

Sloka 13

muneḥ tu vachanam śhrutvā madhuṣhyanda ādayaḥ sutāḥ |
sa abhimānam naraśhreṣhṭha sa līlam idam abruvan || 1-62-13

13. nara shreṣṭha = oh, man the best, Rama; madhuṣyanda ādayaḥ sutāḥ tu = ṃadhu syanda, and other, sons - of Vishvamitra, on their part; muneḥ vacanam shrutvā = sage's, saying, on hearing; sa abhimānam = with, haughtiness; sa līlam = with, disparage; idam abruvan = this, said.

"But on hearing the saying of the sage, oh, Rama, the best of men, ṃadhushyanda and the other sons of Vishvamitra said this, haughtily and disparagingly. [1-62-13]

Sloka 14

katham ātma sutān hitvā trāyase anya sutam vibho |
akāryam iva paśhyāmaḥ śhva māṃsam iva bhojane || 1-62-14

14. vibho = oh, lordly [father]; ātma sutān hitvā = your own, sons, on leaving off [sacrificing]; anya sutam katham trāyase = other's, son, how, you save; bhojane shva māmsam iva = in dinner, dog's, meat, as [good as]; a kāryam iva pashyāmaḥ = wrong, doing, we see [we deem.]

" 'On sacrificing your own sons how can you save another's son, oh, lordly father, we deem this as a wrongdoing and as good as dog's meat in a dinner.' Thus the sons of Vishvamitra replied their father. [1-62-14]

Vishvamitra will be nagged by almost all, including his sons, on this 'dog-meat-eating.' There is a parable in ṃaha Bharata that Vishvamitra once tried to eat dog's meat when he did not get any food, but caught red-handedly. Later this has become the curse-theme to Vishvamitra when he cursed Vashishta's sons. At the present juncture also he curses his own sons with the same theme.

Sloka 15

teṣhām tat vachanam śhrutvā putrāṇām munipuṃgavaḥ |
krodha saṃrakta nayano vyāhartum upachakrame || 1-62-15

15. muni pungavaḥ = sage, the eminent; teṣām putrāṇām tat vacanam shrutvā = their, of sons, that, saying, on listening; krodha samrakta nayanaḥ = with fury, reddening, eyes; vyāhartum upacakrame = to speak [to curse,] started to.

"On listening that saying of his sons that eminent sage Vishvamitra started to curse them while fury reddened his eyes. [1-62-15]

Sloka 16

niḥsādhvasam idam proktam dharmāt api vigarhitam |
atikraṃya tu mat vākyam dāruṇam roma harṣhaṇam || 1-62-16

16. mat vākyam ati kramya = my, word, [you all] on over, stepping - on transgressing; dharmāt api = according to probity, even; vi garhitam = verily, recriminatory; dāruṇam = abhorrent; roma harṣaṇam = hair-raising; idam = this - in this way; niḥsādhvasam = impudently; pra uktam = verily, said - pertly replied me.

" 'You all have not only transgressed my word, but pertly replied me in an impudent manner which is abhorrent and hair-raising, and recriminatory according to probity. [1-62-16]

Sloka 17

śhva māṃsa bhojinaḥ sarve vāsiṣhṭhā iva jātiṣhu |
pūrṇam varṣha sahasram tu pṛthivyām anuvatsyatha || 1-62-17

17. sarve = you all; shva māmsa bhojinaḥ = dog's, meat, while subsisting on; pūrṇam varṣa sahasram tu = complete, for years, thousand, but; pṛithivyām = on earth; vāsiṣṭhā iva = Vashishta's [sons,] like; jātiṣu = in race of [ṃustika-s]; anuvatsyatha = whirl around.

" 'You all will be whirling around the earth totally for a thousand years taking birth in the race that subsists on dog's meat, like the sons of Vashishta.' Thus Vishvamitra cursed his sons. [1-62-17]

Sloka 18

kṛtvā śhāpa samāyuktān putrān munivaraḥ tadā |
śhunaḥśhepam uvācha ārtam kṛtvā rakṣhām nirāmayām || 1-62-18

18. muni varaḥ = saint, the best; putrān shāpa samāyuktān = sons, curse, bounden by; kṛitvā = on making - on cursing; tadā = then; nirāmayām rakṣām kṛitvā = unharmed, invulnerability, on making; ārtam shunaḥshepam uvāca = pitiable one, to ṣhunashepa, spoke.

"On making his sons bounden by curse, then that best saint spoke to the pitiable Shunashepa, on making unharmed invulnerability to him with sanctifying hymns. [1-62-18]

Sloka 19

pavitra pāśhair baddho rakta mālya anulepanaḥ |
vaiṣhṇavam yūpam āsādya vāgbhiḥ agnim udāhara || 1-62-19

19. vaiṣṇavam yūpam āsādya = Vishnu's, sacrificial post, on attaining [when you are fastened]; pavitra pāshaiḥ baddhaḥ = by sacred, fastener, when fastened; rakta mālya anulepanaḥ = with red, garlands, smeared with [red] paste; such as you are you; vāgbhiḥ = with word [with hymns I am going to tell]; agnim udāhara = to Fire-god, you address [you praise him.]

" 'When you are fastened with sacred fastener to the sacrificial post of Vishnu, smeared with red paste and garlanded with red garlands, you praisefully address the Fire-god with the words I going to impart to you in Vedic hymns. [1-62-19]

Sloka 20

ime cha gāthe dve divye gāyethā muni putraka |
aṃbarīṣhasya yajñe asmin tataḥ siddhim avāpsyasi || 1-62-20

20. muni putraka = oh, saint's, son; ambarīṣasya asmin yajñe = in of Ambarīsha, in that, Vedic-ritual; ime dve divye gāthe = these, two, divine, songs [hymns]; gāyethā = be sung [ chanted]; tataḥ siddhim avāpsyasi = then, aspiration, you will obtain.

" 'These two divine hymns, oh, son of saint, shall be chanted in the Vedic-ritual of Ambarīsha, then you will obtain your aspiration.' Thus Vishvamitra taught two Vedic hymns to the boy. [1-62-20]

Sloka 21

śhunaḥśhepo gṛhītvā te dve gāthe susamāhitaḥ |
tvarayā rāja siṃham tam aṃbarīṣham uvācha ha || 1-62-21

21. shunaḥshepaḥ = ṣhunashepa; su samāhitaḥ = very, attentively; te dve gāthe gṛihītvā = those, two, hymns, having taken; rāja simham = to king, the lion; tam ambarīṣam = to him, to Ambarīsha; tvarayā [gatvā] = instantly [on going to]; uvāca ha = spoke, indeed.

"Shunashepa having taken those two hymns from Vishvamitra very attentively has instantly gone to that lion-king Ambarīsha and indeed spoke to him. [1-62-21]

Sloka 22

rāja siṃha mahābuddhe śhīghram gachchhāvahe vayam |
nivartayasva rājendra dīkṣhām cha samudāhara || 1-62-22

22. mahābuddhe = oh, highly intellectual one; rāja simha = king, the lion; [sadaḥ = to your ritual place]; vayam shīghram gacchāvahe = we, promptly, go to; rājendra = oh, king the best; dīkṣām nivartayasva = pledge, apply yourself to; [iti = this way]; samudāhara = said.

" 'Oh, king the lion, let us go promptly to your ritual place, oh, best king, you may apply yourself to your pledge in completing the ritual, with me as its sacrificial animal,' thus the boy said. [1-62-22]

Sloka 23

tat vākyam ṛṣhi putrasya śhrutvā harṣha samanvitaḥ |
jagāma nṛpatiḥ śhīghram yajña vāṭam atandritaḥ || 1-62-23

23. nṛipatiḥ = king; ṛiṣi putrasya tat vākyam shrutvā = of sage, son's, that, sentence, on listening; harṣa samanvitaḥ = , gladness, along with [gladdened]; a tandritaḥ = not, lazily - spiritedly; yajña vāṭam = to ritual, shed [hall]; shīghram jagāma = immediately, proceeded.

"On listening that sentence of the son of sage, the king Ambarīsha is gladdened and proceeded to the ritual hall immediately and spiritedly. [1-62-23]

Sloka 24

sadasya anumate rājā pavitra kṛta lakṣhaṇam |
paśhum rakta aṃbaram kṛtvā yūpe tam samabandhayat || 1-62-24

24. rājā = king; sadasya anumate = officiators', by permission of; tam = him [ṣhunashepa]; pavitra kṛita lakṣaṇam = sanctity, prepared, with features [got the boy is prepared with bodily features of sanctity]; rakta ambaram pashum = with red, cloth, as ritual animal; kṛitvā = made [clad him in red clothes]; yūpe sam abandhayat = to sacrificial post, securely fastened.

"The king with the permission of officiators of ritual got the boy prepared as a ritual animal with sanctified bodily features and clad him in red clothes and got him securely fastened to the sacrificial post. [1-62-24]

Sloka 25

sa baddho vāgbhiḥ agryābhiḥ abhituṣhṭāva vai surau |
indram indra anujam chaiva yathāvat muni putrakaḥ || 1-62-25

25. baddhaḥ saḥ muni putrakaḥ = when tied, he, saint's, son - ṣhunashepa; indram = Indra; indra anujam caiva = Indra's, brother [Upendra,] as well as; surau = these two gods; agryābhiḥ = with superior ones; vāgbhiḥ = with words [two hymns]; yathāvat = as per [scriptures]; abhituṣṭāva vai = highly pleased, indeed.

"When Shunashepa is tied to ritual post he immensely pleased two gods, namely Indra and Upendra as well, with those two hymns he got from Vishvamitra. [1-62-25]

Sloka 26

tataḥ prītaḥ sahasra akṣho rahasya stuti toṣhitaḥ |
dīrgham āyuḥ tadā prādāt śhunaḥśhepāya rāghava || 1-62-26

26. rāghava = oh, Raghava; tataḥ = then; rahasya stuti toṣitaḥ = esoteric, by laudation, who is satisfied; sahasra akṣaḥ = thousand, eyed god Indra; prītaḥ = is gladdened; tadā = then; shunaḥshepāya = for ṣhunashepa; dīrgham āyuḥ prādāt = long, life [longevity,] bestowed.

"Then the Thousand-eyed Indra who is satisfied with esoteric laudation is gladdened, and oh, Raghava, then he bestowed longevity to Shunashepa. [1-62-26]

Sloka 27

sa cha rājā naraśhreṣhṭha yajñasya cha samāptavān |
phalam bahu guṇam rāma sahasrākṣha prasādajam || 1-62-27

27. narashreṣṭha = oh, best one among men, Rama; rāma = oh, Rama; saḥ rājā ca = he, that king, also; sahasrākṣa prasāda jam = ṭhousand-eyed Indra's, grace, caused by; bahu guṇam = many, fold; yajñasya phalam ca sam āptavān = Vedic-ritual, fruits of, also, well, obtained.

"Oh, Rama, the best one among men, he that king Ambarīsha also obtained the fruits of that Vedic-ritual in manyfold, resulted from the grace of Thousand-eyed Indra. [1-62-27]

Sloka 28

viśhvāmitro api dharmātmā bhūyaḥ tepe mahātapāḥ |
puṣhkareṣhu naraśhreṣhṭha daśha varṣha śhatāni cha || 1-62-28

28. narashreṣṭha = oh, best one among men Rama; dharmātmā = virtue-soled sage; mahā tapāḥ = great ascetic; vishvāmitraḥ api = Vishvamitra, even; puṣkareṣu = at ḥoly place; dasha varṣa shatāni ca = ten, years, hundred, also; bhūyaḥ tepe = again, performed ascesis.

"Oh, Rama, the best among men, even the virtue-souled great ascetic Vishvamitra again performed ascesis at the same Holy lakeside for another thousand years." Thus Sage Shatānanda continued the narration of Vishvamitra's legend. [1-62-28]

...

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye bāla kāṇḍe dvi ṣhaṣhṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 62nd chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
