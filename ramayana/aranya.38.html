Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 38
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

ṃāreecha continues his good word to Ravana and he dissuades him from abducting Seetha. His advice is that the dalliance with other's wives is the greatest sin, more so, with Rama's wife as Rama's valour is irrefutable. ṃāreecha narrates how Rama hurt him while safeguarding Vishwamitra's Vedic-ritual, when Rama was still a young boy.


Sloka 1

kadācit api aham vīryāt paryaṭan pṛthivīm imām |
balam nāga sahasrasya dhārayan parvatopamaḥ || 3-38-1
nīla jīmūta saṃkāśaḥ tapta kāṃcana kuṇḍalaḥ |
bhayam lokasya janayan kirīṭī parigha āyudhaḥ || 3-38-2
vyacaram daṇḍaka araṇyam ṛṣi māṃsāni bhakṣayan |

1. kadācit = at one time; aham api = I, even; vīryāt = with valour; imām pṛithivīm paryaṭan = this, earth, while roving; nāga sahasrasya balam dhārayan = elephants, thousand, strength, bearing; parvata upamaḥ = mountain, similar - I am; nīla jīmūta samkāshaḥ = blackish, cloud, in sheen - I am; tapta kāncana = burnt [refined,] golden; kuṇDalaḥ = ear-knobs - myself having; kirīṭī = wearing crown; parigha āyudhaḥ = bludgeon, as weapon; lokasya bhayam janayan = to world, terror, causing; ṛiṣi māmsāni bhakṣayan = sage's, fleshes, while eating; daṇDaka araṇyam vyacaram = Dandaka, forests, I was rambling.

"At one time even I was on the rove around this earth, taking pride in my valour, bearing the strength of a thousand elephants, mountainous in my size and black-cloudlike in my sheen, wearing ear-knobs and a crown made out of refined gold, with a bludgeon as my weapon, and such as I was, I rambled Dandaka forest causing terror to the world, and eating the fleshes of sages." Thus ṃāreecha started to narrate his experience with Rama. [3-38-1, 2]

Sloka 3

viśvāmitro atha dharmātmā mat vitrasto mahāmuniḥ || 3-38-3
svayam gatvā daśaratham narendram idam abravīt |

3b, 4a. atha dharmātmā vishvāmitraḥ = then, virtue-souled, Vishvamitra; mat = of me; vi trasta = highly, scared; svayam = in person; nara indram dasharatham gatvā = to people's king, to Dasharatha, on going to him; mahāmuniḥ = great-saint; idam abravīt = this, spoke.

"The virtue-souled great-saint Vishvamitra who was highly scared of me, then on going to Dasharatha in person that great-saint spoke this to that lord of people. [3-38-3b, 4a]

Sloka 4

ayam rakṣatu mām rāmaḥ parva kāle samāhitaḥ || 3-38-4
mārīcāt me bhayam ghoram samutpannam nareśvara |

4b, 5a. nara īshvara = oh, people's, lord; ayam mārīcāt = from this, ṃāreecha; me ghoram bhayam = to me, awesome, dismay; sam utpannam = verily, chanced - befallen; rāmaḥ samāhitaḥ = Rama, becoming alert; parva kāle = at opportune, time of ritual; mām rakṣatu = me, let him safeguard.

" 'An awesome dismay has befallen owing to this ṃāreecha, oh, lord of people Dasharatha, let Rama safeguard me becoming alert at the opportune time of the Vedic-ritual. [3-38-4b 5a]

Sloka 5

iti evam ukto dharmātmā rājā daśarathaḥ tadā || 3-38-5
pratyuvāca mahābhāgam viśvāmitram mahāmunim |

5b, 6a. iti evam uktaḥ = thus, that way, one who is spoken to - Dasharatha; dharma atmā rājā dasharathaḥ tadā = virtue-souled, king, Dasharatha, then; mahābhāgam mahāmunim vishvāmitram = to great-fortunate, to great-saint, Vishvamitra; prati uvāca = in turn, spoke - replied.

"When he is spoken in that way, then that virtue-souled king Dasharatha replied that great-fortunate and great-saint Vishvamitra in this way. [3-38-5b, 6a]

Sloka 6

ūna dvādaśa varṣo ayam akṛta astraḥ ca rāghavaḥ || 3-38-6
kāmam tu mama yat sainyam mayā saha gamiṣyati |

6b, 7a. ayam [bālaḥ] rāghavaḥ = this, [boy,] Rama is; ūna dvādash varṣaḥ = less than, twelve, years - in age; a kṛita astraḥ ca = not, performed - unpractised, weapons, also; mama yat sainyam = mine, which, army - whatever army is there; with that; kāmam mayā saha gamiṣyati = if need be, me, along with, will march on.

" 'This boy Rama is less than twelve years in his age, unproficient in the use of weapons, and if need be, whatever army of mine is there it will march on along with me instead of Rama. [3-38-6b, 7a]

▼

In Bala Kanda the age of Rama as said by Dasharatha to Vishvamitra is ūna SoDasa varṣaḥ ' 'less than sixteen years.' Here ṃāreecha is saying that Rama is less than twelve. ṃaheshvara Tīrtha says that ṃāreecha lessened the age of Rama only to intimidate Ravana.

Sloka 7

balena caturaṃgeṇa svayam etya niśācaram || 3-38-7
vadhiṣyāmi muniśreṣṭha śatrum tava yathā īpsitam |

7b, 8a. muni shreṣṭha = oh, sage, the eminent; caturangeṇa balena = with fourfold, forces; [aham = I]; svayam etya = in person, having come; tava shatrum = your, enemy; yathā īpsitam = as, desired [or, manasā īpsitān = as your heart, desires - whomever you indicate for elimination; nishācaram = such - nightwalkers; vadhiṣyāmi = I will kill.

" 'And having come in person with fourfold forces, oh eminent sage, I myself will eradicate that nightwalker whomever you indicate as an enemy of yours." So said Dasharatga to Vishvamitra. [3-38-7b, 8a]

▼

In the fourfold army the four categories of army units are chariots, elephants, cavalry, and foot-soldiers.

Sloka 8

] evam uktaḥ sa tu munī rājānam idam abravīt || 3-38-8
rāmāt na anyat balam loke paryāptam tasya rakṣasaḥ |

8b, 9a. evam uktaḥ = thus, who is spoken; saḥ tu munī = he, on his part, that sage Vishvamitra; rājānam idam abravīt = to king, this, said; rāmāt anyat balam = apart from Rama, other, force; tasya rakṣasaḥ = to him, to that demon; loke = in world; na paryāptam = not, enough for - can counterbalance.

"When the sage Vishvamitra is spoken thus, then he said to the king, 'No other force in the world than Rama can counterbalance that demon. [3-38-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

devatānām api bhavān samareṣu abhipālakaḥ || 3-38-9
āsīt tava kṛte karma triloka viditam nṛpa |

9b, 10a. nṛipa = oh, king; bhavān = you are; samareṣu = in war; devatānām api abhipālakaḥ = for gods, even [no doubt,] a defender of; tava kṛite karma = by you, undertaken, exploit; triloka viditam āsīt = in tri-world, well-known, is there.

" 'No doubt, you are a defender of gods in wars, and oh, king, the exploits you have undertaken on behalf of gods are well-known in the triad of worlds. [3-38-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

kāmam asti mahat sainyam tiṣṭatu iha paraṃtapa || 3-38-10
bālo api eṣa mahātejāḥ samarthaḥ tasya nigrahe |
gamiṣye rāmam ādāya svasti te astu paraṃtapaḥ || 3-38-11

10b, 11. paran tapaḥ = oh, enemy-flamer; mahat sainyam asti [astu] = remarkable, army, there is; kāmam = maybe, if it is there, let it be there; iha tiṣṭatu = here, let it sit tight; mahātejāḥ = great-resplendent one; eṣa bālaḥ api = he is, a boy, even if; tasya nigrahe samarthaḥ = his - ṃāreecha's, in forestalling, he is competent; parantapa = oh, enemy-inflamer; rāmam ādāya gamiṣye = Rama, on taking, I wish to go; te svasti astu = to you, safety, let there be.

" 'ṃaybe your army is a remarkable one, oh, enemy-inflamer, let it sit tight here alone, and even if this great resplendent one is still a boy he alone is competent to forestall that ṃāreecha, hence oh, enemy-subjugator, let good betide you, I wish to take him alone along with me.' [3-38-10b, 11]

Sloka 12

iti evam uktvā sa muniḥ tam ādāya nṛpātmajam |
jagāma parama prīto viśvāmitraḥ svam āśramam || 3-38-12

12. saḥ muniḥ vishvāmitraḥ = he, sage, Vishvamitra; iti evam uktvā = thus, that way, on saying; nṛipa ātma jam = king's, own, son; tam = him - Rama; ādāya = taking along; parama prītaḥ = highly, gladdened; svam āshramam jagāma = to his own, to hermitage, went away.

"Thus that way on saying he that sage Vishvamitra returned to his own hermitage highly gladdening to take the prince Rama along with him. [3-38-12]

Sloka 13

tam tadā daṇḍakāraṇye yajñam uddiśya dīkṣitam |
babhūva upasthito rāmaḥ citram visphārayan dhanuḥ || 3-38-13

13. tadā rāmaḥ daṇDakāraṇye = then, Rama, in Dandaka forest; yaGYam uddishya dīkṣitam = Vedic-ritual, concerning, who entered into vow; tam = him - at Vishvamitra; upa sthitaḥ = nearby staying; citram dhanuḥ visphārayan = amazing, bow, twingeing out; abhūva = he was there.

"Then in Dandaka forest Rama twingeing his amazing bow stayed nearby the sage Vishvamitra who entered the vow of Vedic-ritual. [3-38-13]

▼

There is a difference in mentioning the forest as Dandaka. Bala Kanda says that Vishvamitra performed ritual in north and ṃāreecha is now saying that as Dandaka forest.

Sloka 14

ajāta vyaṃjanaḥ śrīmān bālaḥ śyāmaḥ śubhekṣaṇaḥ |
eka vastra dharo dhanvī śikhī kanaka mālayā || 3-38-14
śobhayan daṇḍakāraṇyam dīptena svena tejasā |
adṛśyata tadā rāmo bāla candra iva uditaḥ || 3-38-15

14. tadā = at that time; a jāta vyanjanaḥ = one with - un, born, identities of maleness - like moustaches etc.; shrīmān = providential one - Rama; shyāmaḥ = peacock-bluish - complexioned; shubha īkṣaṇaḥ = magnificent, in looks; eka vastra dharaḥ = one, cloth, wearing; dhanvī = handling bow; shikhī = having hair-locks; kanaka mālayā = with golden, pendant; dīptena svena tejasā = with radiating, of his own, with radiance; daṇDakāraṇyam shobhayan = Dandaka, forest, irradiating; such a; rāmaḥ bālaḥ = Rama, is a boy; uditaḥ bāla candra iva = uprisen, baby, moon, [new moon,] like; adṛishyata = then, is seen, appeared.

"At that time, yet unborn are the identities of adulthood like moustache on his face, and that providential one was magnificent in looks with a peacock-blue complexion, wearing a single cloth, locks of hair, and golden locket, and wielding a bow, and he was irradiating the Dandaka forest with a radiating radiance of his own, and then he appeared like the just risen baby-moon. [3-38-14, 15]

▼

The identities of adulthood are the discardable things like moustaches, beard, etc. These are natural to humans or to birthed beings. But the mythological entities do not have them. As a young scholar, the boys are supposed to wear simply, hence 'one-piece-clothing' is said to be the dress of Rama. This suggests that he is not wearing any shield or armour to keep him safe. In Bala Kanda, at the outset of the journey of Rama and Lakshmana with Vishvamitra, it is said that they are wearing finger-covers made of certain lizard skin, which means to say that their fingers are very tender in using arrows. His mothers delicately arranged the locks of hair, because he is still 'boyish' to her. ṃore so, he is with a 'boyish' ornament, say a single pendant, not verily embellished with princely jewellery. He appeared like some providential 'boy' hitherto unseen, with the height of a peacock, with the complexion of peacock-neck-blue, and moving about like a noble peacock. This boyish-Rama peacock is not screaming, yet his bow is twanging; leave alone the screeching and screaming.

▼

Tṭhe 'new-moon' metaphor to Rama suggests that he has only waxing and no waning in him, uttarottara abhivṛiddhi and also that he is by birth an antagonist of tamas 'the darkness,' or darkish activities, like the moon whose activity is only to illumine the night. Further, he has a likeable persona to any, even to the present demon ṃāreecha, and even to Ravana. When Ravana sees Rama for the first time in war, Ravana praisefully acknowledges Rama's personality. Besides, Rama's gleam is like that of a baby-moon, against the backdrop of a thickish, darkish, blackish sky, called the forest.

Sloka 16

tato aham megha saṃkāśaḥ tapta kāṃcana kuṇḍalaḥ |
balī datta varo darpāt ājagāma āśrama aṃtaram || 3-38-16

16. tataḥ = then; megha samkāshaḥ = cloud, in similarity [in massiveness]; tapta kāncana kuṇDalaḥ = burnt [purified,] golden, with ear-knobs; balī datta varaḥ = forceful, given, boon's - one who is given boons by Brahma; aham = I, such as I am; darpāt = pridefully; āshrama antaram ājagāma = hermitage's, interior, arrived.

"And I who was like a massive black-cloud, wearing golden ear-knobs, already a forceful one further reinforced with the boon of Brahma, I pridefully entered into the interior of Vishwamitra's hermitage. [3-38-16]

▼

The golden ear-knobs on the lobes of ears are either indicative of the knowledge one possess or the wealth factor. ṃāreecha refers to these ear ornaments more than once suggesting that 'wealth or wisdom is nothing to Rama, but righteousness alone will work out.' Because Ravana did not give an ear for the suggestions of ṃāreecha, his Lanka, built with refined gold, will be burnt to ashes in Sundara Kanda.

Sloka 17

tena dṛṣṭaḥ praviṣṭo aham sahasā eva udyata āyudhaḥ |
mām tu dṛṣṭvā dhanuḥ sajyam asambhrāntaḥ cakāra ha || 3-38-17

17. udyata āyudhaḥ = one with lifted up, weapon; praviṣṭaḥ = on who entered; aham = I am; sahasā eva = quick, thus; tena dṛiṣṭaḥ = by him - by Rama, noticed; saḥ mām tu dṛiṣṭvā = he, me, on seeing; a sam bhrāntaḥ = not, at all, excited - unexcitedly; dhanuḥ sa jyam = bow, with, bowstring; cakāra ha = made - strung, aimed, indeed.

"Rama quickly noticed me on my very entry with an uplifted weapon, and on seeing me he unexcitedly strung bowstring to his bow. [3-38-17]

Sloka 18

avajānan aham mohāt bālo ayam iti rāghavam |
viśvāmitrasya tām vedim abhyadhāvam kṛta tvaraḥ || 3-38-18

18. aham mohāt = I, unmindfully; ayam bālaḥ iti = he is, a boy, thus as; rāghavam = about Raghava; ava jānan = without, judging - trivialising; kṛita tvaraḥ = making, haste; vishvāmitrasya tām vedim = of Vishvamitra, that, towards Fire-altar; abhya dhāvam = towards, ran.

"Unmindfully trivialising him as, 'this Raghava is just a boy,' I made haste and ran towards that Fire-altar of Vishvamitra. [3-38-18]

Sloka 19

tena muktaḥ tato bāṇaḥ śitaḥ śatru nibarhaṇaḥ |
tena aham tāḍitaḥ kṣiptaḥ samudre śata yojane || 3-38-19

19. tataḥ = then; tena = by him; shatru nibarhaṇaḥ = enemy, destroying - arrow; shitaḥ bāṇaḥ muktaḥ = acute, arrow, is released; aham tena tāDitaḥ = I am, by it, who is hit; shata yojane samudre kṣiptaḥ = hundred, yojana-s [afar,] in ocean, plunged.

"He then released an acute arrow that eliminates the enemy, and hit by it I am plunged into the ocean which was a hundred yojana-s off. [3-38-19]

Sloka 20

na icchatā tāta mām hantum tadā vīreṇa rakṣitaḥ |
rāmasya śara vegena nirasto bhrānta cetanaḥ || 3-38-20

20. tāta = oh, dear boy; tadā mām hantum na icchatā = then, me, to kill, [he] did not wish; vīreṇa rakshitaḥ = by valiant Rama, saved [I was]; rāmasya shara vegena = Rama's, by arrow's, thrust; bhrānta cetanaḥ = bewildered, [is my] animation; nir astaḥ = without, base - I was on my last legs.

"That Rama did not wish to kill me then, and I am thus saved by that valiant one, oh, dear boy Ravana, but the thrust of Rama's arrow bewildered my animation and I was on my last legs. [3-38-20]

Sloka 21

pātito aham tadā tena gaṃbhīre sāgara aṃbhasi |
prāpya saṃjñām cirāt tāta laṃkām prati gataḥ purīm || 3-38-21

21. tāta = oh, boy; tadā = that way; aham = I was; gambhīre sāgara ambhasi = abyssal, ocean, in waters; tena = by him; pātitaḥ = one who is flung; cirāt sanGYām prāpya = after long time, consciousness, on gaining; lankām purīm = to Lanka, city; prati gataḥ = towards, went.

"That way I who had been flung into the abyssal water of the ocean by him gained consciousness after a long time and proceeded towards the city of Lanka. [3-38-21]

Sloka 22

evam asmi tadā muktaḥ sahāyāḥ te - śāyāstu - nipātitāḥ |
akṛta astreṇa rāmeṇa bālena akliṣṭa karmaṇā || 3-38-22

22. tadā = at that time; a kṛita astreṇa = not, practised [elementary,] in use of arms; a kliṣṭa karmaṇā = un, complicated [effortless,] in deeds; bālena rāmeṇa = by boy, by Rama; evam muktaḥ asmi = thus, released, I am; te sahāyāḥ [tu] nipātitāḥ = those, helpmates, [on their part,] are killed.

"At that time, that one with elementary knowledge in the use of weaponry, but who can undertake deeds in an effortless manner, such a boy, such a Rama spared me, albeit killing them that were my helpmates in that escapade. [3-38-22]

Sloka 23

tat mayā vāryamāṇaḥ tvam yadi rāmeṇa vigraham |
kariṣyasi āpadam ghorām kṣipram prāpya na śiṣyasi || 3-38-23

23. tat = thereby; mayā vāryamāṇaḥ = by me, being proscribed; tvam rāmeṇa vigraham = you, with Rama, confrontation; kariṣyasi yadi = do - make for, if; kṣipram ghorām āpadam = quickly, disastrous, calamity; prāpya nashiṣyasi = on getting, you ruin yourself.

"Thereby, if you wish to make for any confrontation with Rama, despite of my proscribing you, you will ruin yourself on getting a disastrous calamity. [3-38-23]

Sloka 24

krīḍā rati vidhijñānām samāja utsava śālinām |
rakṣasām caiva saṃtāpam anartham ca āhariṣyasi || 3-38-24

24. krīDā rati vidhi GYānām = sporting, flirting, methods, knowers; samāja utsava shālinām = socialising, frolicking, with a conduct of; rakṣasām caiva = to such demons, also thus; santāpam anartham ca = distress, disaster, also; āhariṣyasi = you derive.

"The demons are methodical in sporting and flirting, thus by their own conduct they are also socialising and frolicking, and to them you derive distress and disaster. [3-38-24]

Sloka 25

harmya prāsāda saṃbādhām nānā ratna vibhūuṣitām |
drakṣyasi tvam purīm laṃkām vinaṣṭām maithilī kṛte || 3-38-25

25. harmya prāsāda sambādhām = with skyscrapers, palaces, dense with; nānā ratna vibhū ṣitām = with, many, gemstones, decorated; such a city; purīm lankām = city, Lanka; tvam = you; maithilī kṛite = ṃaithili, for the sake of; vi naṣṭām = as completely shattered [city]; drakṣyasi = you will see.

"Dense with skyscrapers and palaces, and decorated with many a gemstone is that city Lanka, but you will see it completely shattered down, for the sake of that ṃaithili. [3-38-25]

Sloka 26

akurvanto api pāpāni śucayaḥ pāpa saṃśrayāt |
para pāpaiḥ vinaśyanti matsyā nāga hrade yathā || 3-38-26

26. pāpāni = sins; a kurvantaḥ api = not, committed, even if; shucayaḥ = sinless ones; pāpa samshrayāt = with sinners, by interdependence - tie-up; nāga hrade = in snakes, lake of; matsyā yathā = fish, as with; para pāpaiḥ = with other's, sins; vinashyanti = get ruined.

"Even if with uncommitted sins, the sinless will get ruined owing to their interdependence with other sinners, like the fish in a lake with snakes. [3-38-26]

Sloka 27

divyacaṃdanadigdhāṃgāndivyāabharaṇabhūṣitān -yadvā-
divya caṃdana digdha aṃgān divya ābharaṇa bhūṣitān |
drakṣyasi abhihatān bhūmau tava doṣāt tu rākṣasān || 3-38-27

27. divya candana digdha angān = divine, sandal-paste, bedaubed, with limbs; divya ābharaṇa bhūṣitān = divine, ornaments, beautified with; at such; rākṣasān = at demons; tava doṣāt tu = for your, blunder, owing to; bhūmau abhihatān drakṣyasi = to earth, trampled out, you are going to see.

"Bedaubed with divine sandal-paste, beautified with divine ornaments are the limbs of the demons now, perchance you will be seeing them trampled out on ground for the blunder of yours. [3-38-27]

Sloka 28

hṛta dārān sa dārān ca daśa vidravato diśaḥ |
hata śeṣān aśaraṇān drakṣyasi tvam niśācarān || 3-38-28

28. hata sheṣān = after killing, remaining ones; nishā carān = nightwalkers; hṛita dārān = reft of [not abduction, but abandoned,] wives; sa dārān ca with wives, also - along with wives; a sharaṇān = without, protector; dasha dishaḥ = to ten, directions; vi dravataḥ = verily, running away - fleeing; tvam drakṣyasi- you, will be seeing.

"ṃaybe, you will have to see the fleeing of the nightwalkers surviving after the carnage in all ten directions, some abandoning their wives and some along with their wives, only for lack of a protector. [3-38-28]

Sloka 29

śara jāla parikṣiptām agni jvālā samāvṛtām |
pradagdha bhavanām laṃkām drakṣyasi tvam asaṃśayam || 3-38-29

29. shara jāla parikṣiptām = arrows, enmesh, encircled by; agni jvālā samāvṛitām = by torch's, tongues, enfolded by; pra dagdha bhavanām = utterly, gutting down, buildings; such a; lankām = at Lanka; tvam drakṣyasi = you, will see; a samshayam = without, doubt.

"Without a doubt you will be seeing Lanka encircled with an enmesh of arrows, enfolded in the tongues of torches, while her buildings are utterly gutting down. [3-38-29]

Sloka 30

para dāra abhimarṣāt tu na anayat pāpa taram mahat |
pramadānām sahasrāṇi tava rājan parigrahe || 3-38-30

30. para dāra abhimarṣhā tu = other's, wife, dalliance, but; pāpa taram = sin, higher in degree - gravest; mahat = outrageous [error]; ānayat na = another, is not there; rājan = oh, king; pramadānām sahasrāṇi tava parigrahe = ladies, thousands, by you/your, seized [or, married.]

"Oh, king, there is no other sin that is outrageous and gravest than the dalliance with another's wife and, besides, you already have a thousand wives, isn't so! [3-38-30]

Sloka 31

bhava sva dāra nirataḥ sva kulam rakṣa rākṣasa |
mānam vṛddhim ca rājyam ca jīvitam ca iṣṭam ātmanaḥ || 3-38-31

31. rākṣasa = oh, demon; sva dāra nirataḥ bhava = in your own, wife, delighted in, you become; sva kulam rakṣa = your own, ethnos, safeguard; mānam vṛiddhim ca rājyam ca = dignity, affluence, also, kingdom, also; ātmanaḥ iṣṭam jīvitam ca = for yourself, dear, [your] life, also; [raksha = safeguard.]

"Abide by your own wife and be delighted with her, oh, demon, safeguard your own ethnos, thus safeguard dignity, affluence, and kingdom of demons, thereby safeguard even your own dear life. [3-38-31]

Sloka 32

kalatrāṇi ca saumyāni mitra vargam tathaiva ca |
yadi icchasi ciram bhoktum mā kṛthā rāma vipriyam || 3-38-32

32. saumyāni kalatrāṇi ca = beautiful ones, wives, also; tathā eva ca = also thus; mitra vargam = friends, circle; ciram bhoktum = for a long time, to enjoy; icchasi yadi = you desire, if; rāma vi priyam mā kṛithā = to Rama, un, desirable, do not, do.

"If you desire to take pleasure with your beautiful wives and with your friend's circles for a long time, do not do anything undesirable to Rama. [3-38-32]

Sloka 33

nivāryamāṇaḥ suhṛdā mayā bhṛśam
prasahya sītām yadi dharṣayiṣyasi |
gamiṣyasi kṣīṇa balaḥ sa bāndhavo
yama kṣayam rāma śara ātta jīvitaḥ || 3-38-33

33. su hṛidā = with a good, heart; mayā bhṛisham nivāryamāṇaḥ = by me, considerably, being prevented; sītām prasahya dharṣayiṣyasi yadi = ṣeetha is, aggressively, intimidated, if [you wish to]; kṣīṇa balaḥ = one with diminished, army; sa bāndhavaḥ = with, kinsfolk; rāma shara ātta jīvitaḥ = Rama's, arrow, taken away, life - one with such removed life - dead; yama kṣayam gamiṣyasi = to Yama [the ṭerminator's,] world, you will go.

"If you wish to intimidate Seetha aggressively despite of my considerable preventing in all my good-heartedness towards you, then your army will fade away, and you with your kinsfolk will emigrate to the world of Yama, the Terminator, for your life will be taken away by the arrow of Rama." Thus, ṃāreecha spoke to Ravana. [3-38-33]

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe aṣṭā triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 38th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
