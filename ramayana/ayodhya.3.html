Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 3
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dasaratha, glad after hearing the words of assembled people, requests Vasishta and Vamadeva to start arrangements for the anointing ceremony of Rama. Vasishta and Vamadeva order the officers present there to make all arrangements, describing them in detail. Dasaratha then orders Sumantra to bring Rama to the assembly. After letting Rama know of his desire, Dasaratha then advises Rama on how to rule the kingdom for the benefit of the people.

Sloka 1

teṣāmañjalipadmāni pragṛhītāni sarvaśaḥ |
pratigṛhyābravīdrājā tebhyaḥ priyahitaṃ vacaḥ || 2-3-1

1. rājā = King (Dasaratha); pragṛihītāni = obtained; teshhām = their; aJṇjalipadmāni = salutation (with folded hands) resembling lotus buds; sarvashaḥ = by all means; pratigṛihyā = accepted (that); abravīt = (and) spoke; tebhyaḥ = to them; priyahitam = pleasing and good; vachaḥ = sentence.

King Dasaratha, accepted their salutations with folded hands resembling lotus buds and spoke good and agreeable words to them as follows:-

Sloka 2

aho.asmi paramaprītaḥ prabhāvaścātulo mama |
yanme jyeṣṭhaṃ priyaṃ putraṃ yauvarājyastha micchatha || 2-3-2

2. aho = Oh!; yat ichchhatha = why you desire; me = my; jyeshhṭham = elder; priyam = beloved; putram = son; yauvarājyastham = as prince; paramaprītaḥ asmi = (for that)I am very pleased; mama = my; prabhāvashcha = fortune is also; atulaḥ = without comparision.

"Oh! I am very much pleased and incomparable is my fortune in that you desire my beloved elder son to be anointed as prince. "

Sloka 3

iti pratyarcya tān rājā brāhmaṇānida mabravīt |
vasiṣṭhaṃ vāmadevaṃ ca teṣāmevopaśṛṇvatām || 2-3-3

3. rājā = King Dasaratha; iti = thus; pratyarchya = respected; tān = them; abravīt = spoke; idam = this (sentence); teshhām upashṛiṇvatām eva = while they were listening; vasishhṭham = to Vasishta; Vāmadevaṃ cha = Vāmadeva and; brāhmaṇān = other brahmanas.

King Dasaratha, thus duly returning the honor done by them, spoke while they were listening, to Vasishta, Vamadeva and other Brahmans as follows:

Sloka 4

caitraḥ śrīmānayaṃ māsaḥ puṇyaḥ puṣpitakānanaḥ |
yauvarājyāya rāmasya sarvamevopakalpyatām || 2-3-4
rājñastūparate vākye janaghoṣo mahānabhūt |

4. "ayam = this; chaitraḥ māsaḥ = month of chaitra; pushhpitakānanaḥ = has flowering forests; shrīmān = glorious; puṇyaḥ = auspicious; sarvam eva upakalpyatām = let all arrangements be made (for anointment of Rama as prince)"; uparate = while completing; rājJṇaḥ = the king's; vākye = words; abhūt = there was; mahān = great; janaghoshhaḥ = uproar ( due to applause) from people.

"This is the glorious and auspicious month of chaitra, in which the forests bloom with flowers. Let all arrangements be made for the coronation of Rama as prince." While these words of the king were being completed, there was a great delightful uproar of the people.

Sloka 5

śanaistasmin praśānte ca janaghoṣe janādhipaḥ || 2-3-5
vasiṣṭhaṃ muniśārdūlaṃ rājā vacanamabravīt |

5. tasmin = that; janaghoshhe = uproar of people; shanaiḥ = gradually; prashānte = subsided; janādhipaḥ = lord of the people; rājā = King Dasaratha; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; vasishhṭham = to Vasista; munishārdūlam = the best of sages.

When their applause gradually subsided, King Dasaratha spoke the following words to the best of sages, Vasishta.

Sloka 6

abhiṣekāya rāmasya yatkarma saparicchadam || 2-3-6
tadadya bhagavan sarvamājñāpayitu marhasi |

6. bhagavan = Oh divine Vasishta!; arhasi = you are suitable; adya = now; ājJṇāpayitum = to order for; sarvam = all; tat karma yat = the traditional ceremonial which (needs) to be done; saparichchhadam = including the accessories (that are needed );abhishhekāya rāmasya = for the anointing ceremony of Rama.

"Oh Divine Vasishta! Be pleased to order now for the traditional ceremonial that is to be done and various accessories needed for the anointing ceremony of Rama."

Sloka 7

tacchrutvā bhūmipālasya vasiṣṭho dvijasattamaḥ || 2-3-7
ādideśāgrato rājñaḥ sthitān yuktān kṛtāñjalīn |

7. vasishhṭhaḥ = Vasishta; dvijasattamaḥ = the best of brahmanas; shrutvā = heard; tat = that (sentence); bhūmipālasya = of king Dasaratha; ādidesha = ordered; yuktān = the officers; sthitān = who were there; kṛitāJṇjalīn = with folded hands; agrataḥ = before; rājJṇaḥ = the king.

Vasishta, the best of Brahmans, hearing the words of king Dasaratha, ordered the officers who stood there with folded hands before the king, as follows: -

Sloka 8

suvarṇādīni ratnāni balīn sarvauṣadhīrapi || 2-3-8
śuklamālyāṃśca lājāṃśca pṛthakca madhusarpiṣī |
ahatāni ca vāsāṃsi rathaṃ sarvāyudhānyapi || 2-3-9
caturaṅgabalaṃ caiva gajaṃ ca śubhalakṣaṇam |
cāmaravyajane śvete dhvajaṃ chatraṃ ca pāṇḍuram || 2-3-10
śataṃ ca śātakumbhānāṃ kumbhānāmagnivarcasām |
hiraṇyaśṛṅgamṛṣabhaṃ samagraṃ vyāghracarma ca || 2-3-11
upasthāpayata prātaragnyagāraṃ mahīpateḥ |

8;9;10;11. upasthāpayata = gather; agnyagāram = in the sacred fire house; mahīpate = of the king; prātaḥ = at dawn; suvarṇādīni = gold and the like; ratnāni = diamonds; balīn = things needed to perform pooja (worship); sarvaushhadhīḥ api = various herbs; shuklamālyān cha = white floral garlands; lājān cha = corn; madhusarpishhī = honey and clarified butter; pṛithak = separately; ahatāni vāsāṃsi cha = new clothes; ratham = chariot; sarvāyudhānyapi = all weapons; chaturaṇ^gabalaṃcha iva = the four divisions of army; gajam cha = elephant; shubhalakshaṇam = with good signs; shvete = white; chāmaravyajane = fans made from the skin of an animal called chamari; dhvajam = pillar (or) staff ; pāṇDuram = white; chhatraṃ cha = umbrella; shataṃ cha = one hundred; shātakumbhānām = golden hued; kumbhānām = pots; agnivarchasām = brilliant like fire; ṛishhabham = bull; hiraṇyashṛiṇ^gam = with golden horns; samagram = complete; vyāghracharma cha = tiger skin.

"Gather the following in the sacred fire house of the king at dawn: gold and the like, diamonds, things needed to perform worship, various herbs, white floral garlands, corn, honey and clarified butter in separate vessels, new clothes, chariot, all weapons, the four divisions of army, an elephant with auspicious signs, white fan, flag staff, white umbrella, one hundred golden pots with brilliant shine, bull with gilded horns and a complete tiger skin".

Sloka 12

yaccānyatkiñcideṣṭavyaṃ tatsarvamupakalpyatām | 2-3-12
astaḥpurasya dvārāṇi sarvasya nagarasya ca |
candanasragbhirarcyantāṃ dhūpaiśca ghrāṇahāribhiḥ || 2-3-13

12;13. anyat = (if) other; yat cha kiJṇchit = even small thing; eshhṭavyam = is required; upakalpyatām tat sarvam = let all of it be arranged; dvārāṇi = doorways; antaḥpurasya = of royal palace; sarvasya nagarasya cha = of entire city also; archyantām = be worshipped; chandanasragbhiḥ = with sandal paste and floral garlands; ghrāṇahāribhiḥ = attractive smelling; dhūpaiḥ = incense.

"If any other small things are required, arrange for all of them. Let the doorways of the royal palace and those of the entire city be worshipped with sandalwood paste, floral garlands and good smelling incense."

Sloka 14

praśastamannaṃ guṇavaddhadhikṣīropasecanam |
dvijānāṃ śatasāhasre yatprakāmamalaṃ bhavet || 2-3-14

14. prashastam = fine; annam = rice; guṇavat = of good quality; dadhi kshīropasechanam = with yoghurt and milk; prakāmam = completely; alaṃ bhavet = sufficient for; shatasāhasre = one hundred thousand; dvijānām = brahmanās.

"Let fine rice of good quality with milk and yogurt, fully sufficient for one hundred thousand Brahmans be arranged."

Sloka 15

satkṛtya dvijamukhyānāṃ śvaḥ prabhāte pradīyatām |
ghṛtaṃ dadhi ca lājāśca dakṣiṇāścāpi puṣkalāḥ || 2-3-15

15. pradīyatām = let (that rice) be given; satkṛitya = with respect; shvaḥ = tomorrow; prabhāte = at dawn; dvijamukhyānām = to important Brahmans; ghṛitam = (along with) clarified butter; dadhi cha = yoghurt; lājāḥ cha = corn and; pushhkalāḥ = lot of; dakshiṇāshchāpi = gifts also.

"Let that rice be given with due respect to important Brahmans tomorrow at dawn; along with clarified butter, yogurt, corn, and lot of gifts."

Sloka 16

sūrye.abhyuditamātre śvo bhavitā svastivācanam |
brāhmaṇāśca nimantryantāṃ kalpyantāmāsanāni ca || 2-3-16

16. bhavitā = there will be; svastivāchanam = chorus invocating well being of all; shvaḥ = tomorrow; abhyuditamātre = soon after the rise of; sūrye = sun; brāhmaṇāḥ cha = brahmanas; nimantryantām = be summoned; āsanāni cha = and seats; kalpyantām = be arranged.

"There will be an invocation for the well being of all, tomorrow at sun rise. Let brahmanas be invited and suitable seats be arranged for them."

Sloka 17

ābadhyantāṃ patākāśca rājamārgaśca sicyatām |
sarve ca tāldāvacarā gaṇikāśca svalaṃkṛtāḥ || 2-3-17
kakṣyāṃ dvitīyāmāsādya tiṣṭhantu nṛpaveśmanaḥ |

17. patākāḥ cha ābadhyantām = let flags be hung; rājamārgaḥ cha sichyatāṃ cha = let royal roads be made wet; sarve = (let)all; svalaṃkṛitāḥ = well dressed; tāLāvacharāḥ = musicians; gaṇikāḥ cha = dancers; āsādya = get; dvitīyām = second; kakshyām = enclosure; nṛipaveshmanaḥ = of royal palace; tishhṭhantu = to stay.

"Let the flags be hung and the royal highways be sprinkled with water. Let well dressed dancers and musicians wait in second enclosure in the royal compound."

Sloka 18

devāyatanacaityeṣu sānnabhakṣāḥ sadakṣiṇāḥ || 2-3-18
upasthāpayitavyāḥ syurmālyayogyāḥ pṛthak pṛthak |

18. devāyatanachaityeshhu = in temples and at road junctions; yogyāḥ = deserving people; upasthāpayitavyāḥ syuḥ = be gathered; sānna = with rice; bhakshāḥ = eatables; sadakshiṇāḥ = with presents; mālya = garlands; pṛithak pṛithak = separately.

"As worshipping is to be done in temples and at road junctions, let some deserving people gather there with rice, eatables, presents and garlands separately."

Sloka 19

dīrghāsibaddhā yodhāśca sannaddhā mṛṣṭavāsasaḥ || 2-3-19
mahārājāṅgaṇaṃ sarve praviśantu mahodayam |

19. sarve = (let) all the; yodhāshcha = warriors also; pravishastu = enter; mahodayam = well developed; mahārājāṇ^gaṇam = front yard of royal palace; dīrghāsibaddhāḥ = wearing long swords; sannaddhāḥ = wearing armors; mṛishhṭavāsasaḥ = wearing washed clothes.

"Let all the warriors enter the well developed front yard of the royal palace with long swords, armors and clean clothing."

Sloka 20

evaṃ vyādiśya viprau tau kriyāstatra suniṣṭhitau || 2-3-20
cakratuścaiva yaccheṣaṃ pārthivāya nivedya ca |

20. tau = those; viprau = wisemen - Vasishta and Vamadeva; sunishhṭhitau = with good attention; evam = thus; vyādishya = ordered; kriyāḥ = things to be done; tatra = there; nivedya cha = informed; pārthivāya = the king Dasaratha; chakratuḥ cha eva = also performed; yat sheshham = (those things) that remained.

Those wise men Vasishta and Vamadeva attentively ordered for all those things to be done there, informed that to king Dasaratha and performed the remaining things also.

Sloka 21

kṛtamityeva cābrūtāmabhigamya jagatpatim || 2-3-21
yathoktavacanaṃ prītau harṣayuktau dvijarṣabhau |

21. prītau = satisfied; harshhayuktau = with pleasure; dvijarshhabhau = the best of brahmanas; Vasishta and Vamadeva; abhigamya = approached; jagatpatim = king Dasaratha - the lord of world; abrūtām = and told him; kṛitamityeva = that all had been done; yathoktavachanam = as per his words.

Pleased and satisfied with the arrangements made Vasishta and Vamadeva approached king Dasaratha and told him that all had been done according to his words.

Sloka 22

tataḥ sumantraṃ dyutimān rājā vacanamabravīt || 2-3-22
rāmaḥ kṛtātmā bhavatā śīghramānīyatāmiti |

22. tataḥ = afterwards; dyutimān = radiant; rājā = king Dasaratha; abravīt = spoke; iti = thus; sumantram = to ṣumantra; vachanam = these words; rāmaḥ = Rama; kṛitātmā = who has a disciplined intellect; anīyatām = be brought; bhavatā = by you; shīghram = immediately.

Afterwards, Dasaratha spoke to Sumantra "Let Rama, who has a disciplined intellect, be brought by you immediately."

Sloka 23

sa tatheti pratijñāya sumantro rājaśāsanāt || 2-3-23
rāmaṃ tatrānayāṃcakre rathena rathināṃ varam |

23. saḥ = that; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; pratijJṇāya = spoke; tathā iti = that would be done accordingly; ānayāṃchakre = brought; rāmam = Rama; varam = the best among; rathināṃ = charioteers; tatra = there; rathena = in a chariot; rāja shāsanāt = as commanded by the king.

As commanded by the king, Sumantra brought there, Rama, the best of charioteers, in a chariot.

Sloka 24

atha tatra samāsīnāstadā daśarathaṃ nṛpam || 2-3-24
prācyodīcyāḥ pratīcyāśca dākṣiṇātyāśca bhūmipāḥ |
mlecchāścāryāśca ye cānye vane śailāntavāsinaḥ || 2-3-25
upāsāñcakrire sarve taṃ devā iva vāsavam |

24;25. atha = afterwards (after ṣumantra left); bhūmipāḥ = kings; prāchyodīchyāḥ = belonging to east; north; pratīchyāḥ cha = and belonging to west; dākshiṇātyāḥ cha = and belonging to south; mlechchhāḥ cha = those belonging to the country of ṃlechha; āryāḥ cha = and belonging to the country of AryāVarta; vane = in forest areas; anye = others; ye = who are; shailāntavāsinaḥ = staying in hilly areas; sarve = all; samāsīnāḥ = sat; tatra = there; tadā = then; upāsāJṇchakrire = attended on; tam = that; dasaratham nrṛipam = king Dasaratha; vāsavamiva = as to Devendra; devāḥ = by celestials.

Afterwards the kings belonging to east, west, north, south as well as those belonging to ṃlechha kingdom, Aryā Vartha kingdom and those ruling in hill and forest areas sat there and attended on King Dasaratha as celestials attended on Devendra.

Sloka 26

teṣāṃ madhye sa rājarṣirmarutāmiva vāsavaḥ || 2-3-26
prāsādastho rathagataṃ dadarśāyānta mātmajam |

26. saḥ = that; rājarshhiḥ = the sage and the king Dasaratha; madhye = in the midst; teshhāṃ = of them; vāsavaḥ = like Devendra; maruthām = in the midst of celestials; dadarsha = saw; prāsādasthaḥ = staying in the palace; ātmajam = his son Rama; āyāntam = arriving; rathagatham = in a chariot.

The sage king Dasaratha, sitting in the midst of those kings like Devendra in the middle of the celestials, saw from the palace itself, his son Rama arriving in a chariot.

Sloka 27

gandharvarājapratimaṃ loke vikhyātapauruṣam || 2-3-27
dīrgha bāhuṃ mahasattvaṃ mattamātaṅgagāminam |
candrakāntānanaṃ rāmamatīva priyadarśanam || 2-3-28
rūpaudāryaguṇaiḥ puṃsāṃ dṛṣṭicittāpahāriṇam |
gharmābhitaptāḥ parjanyaṃ hlādayantamiva prajāḥ || 2-3-29
na tatarpa samāyāntaṃ paśyamāno narādhipaḥ |

27;28;29. gandharva rāja pratimam = equal to king of Gandharvas; vikhyāta paurushham = has famous valiance; loke = in the world; dīrgha bāhum = has long arms; mahāsattvam = has great strength; mattamātaṇ^ga gāminam = has a gait like that of a vigorous elephant; chandrakāntānanam = has enchanting face like the moon; atīva = very; priyadarshanam = pleasant to behold; rūpaudāryaguṇaiḥ = with looks (and his) magnanimity (and) virtues; dṛishhṭi chittāpahārakam = (he had) stolen the eyes and minds; pumsām = of men; narādhipaḥ = king Dasaratha; na tatarpa = was not satiated; pashyamānaḥ = in seeing; rāmam = Rama; samayāntam = coming; parjanyam iva = like cloud; hlādayantam = gladdening; prajāḥ = to people; gharmābhitaptāḥ = scorched by summer heat.

Rama was as beautiful as the king of Gandharvas. His valiance was world famous. He had great strength and walked like a vigorous elephant. He had long arms, and with his enchanting face, he looked like the moon. He stole the eyes and minds of the people by his looks, generosity and virtues. King Dasaratha was thus not satiated enough in seeing Rama, coming like a cloud that gladdens people by relieving them from the scorching summer heat.

Sloka 30

avatārya sumantrastaṃ rāghavaṃ syandanottamāt || 2-3-30
pituḥ samīpaṃ gacchantaṃ prāñjaliḥ pṛṣṭhato.anvagāt |

30. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; avatārya tam rāghavam = helped that Rama to alight; syandanottamāt = from the excellent chariot; prāJṇjaliḥ = (and) with folded hands; anvagāt = followed; pṛishhṭhataḥ = behind; gachchhantam = (Rama) going; samīpam = close to; pituḥ = his father.

Sumantra helped Rama to get down from the excellent chariot and with folded hands followed behind Rama, who approached his father.

Sloka 31

sa taṃ kailāsaśṛṅgābhaṃ prāsādaṃ narapuṅgavaḥ || 2-3-31
āruroha nṛpaṃ draṣṭuṃ saha sūtena rāghavaḥ |

31. saḥ = that; rāghavaḥ = Rama; narapuṇ^gavaḥ = the best among men; āruroha = ascended; tam = that; prāsādam = palace; kailāsa shṛiṇ^gābham = which is equal to ṃount Kailāsa; sūtena saha = along with the driver of chariot; drashhṭum = to see; nṛipam = the king Dasaratha.

Rama, the best among men, ascended the steps of the palace, which resembled ṃount Kailāsa, along with Sumantra, to see the king Dasaratha.

Sloka 32

sa prāñ^jalirabhipretya praṇataḥ piturantike || 2-3-32
nāma svaṃ śrāvayan rāmo vavandhe caraṇau pituḥ |

32. saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; prāJṇ^jaliḥ = with folded hands; abhipretya = approached; antike = nearer to; pituḥ = his father; praṇataḥ = bowed down; shrāvayan = mentioning; svam = his own; nāma = name; vavande = offered obeisance; pituḥ = (to) his father's; charaṇau = feet.

Rama with folded hands approached his father, bowed down mentioning his own name and offered salutations to his father's feet.

Sloka 33

taṃ dṛṣṭvā praṇataṃ pārśve kṛtāñjalipuṭaṃ nṛpaḥ || 2-3-33
gṛhyāñjalau samākṛṣya sasvaje priyamātmajam |

33. nṛipaḥ = king Dasaratha; gṛihya = caught; anjalau = by his joined hands; tam = that; priyam = beloved; ātmajam = son; praṇatam = who was (standing) humbly; pārshve = by his side; kṛitānjalipuṭam = with folded hands; samākṛishhya = drew him nearer and; sasvaje = embraced.

King Dasaratha took into his embrace that beloved son who was standing humbly beside him with folded hands.

Sloka 34

tasmai cābhyuditaṃ divyaṃ maṇikāñcanabhūṣitaṃ || 2-3-34
dideśa rājā ruciraṃ rāmāya paramāsanam |

34. rājā = king Dasaratha; didesha = gave; tasmai rāmāya = for that Rama; paramāsanam = great throne; abhyuditam = which is high; divyam = excellent; ruchiram = beautiful; maṇikānchana bhūshhitam = decorated with gold and diamonds.

King Dasaratha gave Rama a great throne, which was high, excellent and beautiful, decorated with gold and diamonds.

Sloka 35

tadāsanavaraṃ prāpya vyadīpayata rāghavaḥ || 2-3-35
svayeva prabhayā merumudaye vimalo raviḥ |

35. rāghavaḥ = Rama; prāpya = got; tat = that; āsanavaram = great throne; vyadīpayata = (and) caused it to shine; svayā = with his own; prabhayā = brightness; merumiva = as to ṃeru ṃountain; udaye = in the morning; vimalaḥ = by spot less; raviḥ = sun.

Rama, occupying that great throne, caused it to shine with his own brightness, just as the spotless sun in the morning causes ṃeru mountain to shine with brilliance.

Sloka 36

tena vibhrājatā tatra sā sabhābhivyarocata || 2-3-36
vimalagrahanakṣatrā śāradī dyaurivendunā |

36. tatra = there; tena = by that Rama; vibhrājatā = shining; sā = that; sabhā = assembly; abhivyarochata = shone more; inudunā iva = as by the moon; shāradī = the autumn; dyauḥ = sky; vimalagrahanakshatrā = with the dimless planets and stars.

Rama by his brilliance made that assembly to shine, just as the moon shines the clear autumnal sky, with the dimless stars and planets.

Sloka 37

taṃ paśyamāno nṛpati stutooṣa priyamātmajam || 2-3-37
alaṅkṛtamivātmānamādarśatalasaṃsthitam |

37. nṛipaḥ = king Dasaratha; tutoshha = was delighted; pasyamānaḥ = seeing; tam = that; priyam = beloved; ātmajam = son; ātmanamiva = as seeing oneself; alankṛitam = adorned; ādarshatalasamsthitam = in a mirror.

King Dasaratha was as delighted in seeing his beloved son, as one delights in seeing oneself adorned, in a mirror.

Sloka 38

sa taṃ sasmitamābhāṣya putraṃ putravatāṃ varaḥ || 2-3-38
uvācedaṃ vaco rājā devendramiva kāśyapaḥ |

38. saḥ = that; rāja = king Dasaratha; varaḥ = best; putravatām = among those who have sons; sasmitam = smilingly; ābhāshhya = conversed with; tam = that; putram = son; uvācha = spoke; idam = these; vachaḥ = words; devendramiva = as to Devendra; kāsyapaḥ = (by) kāsyapa.

King Dasaratha, the best among those who have sons, smilingly conversed with his son, and addressed Rama as follows, as Kasyapa does to (his eldest son) Devendra (the ruler of gods).

Sloka 39

jyeṣṭhāyāmasi me patnyāṃ sadṛśyāṃ sadṛśaḥ sutaḥ || 2-3-39
utpannastvaṃ guṇaśreṣṭho mama rāmātmajaḥ priyaḥ |

39. rāma = oh; Rama! tvam = you; asi = are; sadṛishaḥ = worthy; sutaḥ = son; utpannaḥ = born; me = to my; sadṛishyām = worthy; jyeshhṭhāyām = elder; patnyām = wife; guṇashreshhṭhaḥ = being excellent in virtues; priyaḥ = (you are) beloved; ātmajaḥ = son; mama = to me.

"Oh Rama! You are an exemplary son born to my exemplary elder wife. Being excellent in virtues, you are beloved son to me."

Sloka 40

yatastvayā prajāścemāḥ svaguṇairanurañjitāḥ || 2-3-40
tasmāttvaṃ puṣyayogena yauvarājyamavāpnuhi |

40. yataḥ = Why; tvayā = by you; imāḥ = these; prajāḥ = people; anuraJṇjitāḥ = were cherished; svaguṇaiḥ = by your good qualities; tasmāt = for that reason; tvam = you; avāpnuhi = get; yauvarājyam = princely kingdom; pushhyayogena = on the day of Pushyami (when the moon appears in conjunction with the constellation Pushya).

"O, Rama! As you with your good qualities cherished these people, you get the princely kingdom on the day of Pushyami star (when the moon appears in conjunction with the constellation Pushya).."

Sloka 41

kāmatastvaṃ prakṛtyaiva vinīto guṇavānasi || 2-3-41
guṇavatyapi tu snehātputra vakṣyāmi te hitam |

41. putra = Oh; son!; prakṛityaiva = by nature; tvam asi = you are; kāmataḥ = very much; vinītaḥ = humble; guṇavān = virtuous man; guṇavatyapi = even if virtuous; because of my; snehāt = friendship; vakshyāmi = I can tell; te = to you; hitam = good (words).

"Oh, son! By nature, you are very humble and virtuous. Even then, because of my friendship with you, I can tell you this for your benefit."

Sloka 42

bhūyo vinayamāsthāya bhava nityaṃ jitendriyaḥ || 2-3-42
kāmakrodhasamutthāni tyajethā vyasanāni ca |

42. āsthāya = try to; bhava = become; bhūyaḥ = (even) more; vinayam = humble; nityam = always; jitendriyaḥ = (remain as) one who has victory over senses; tyajethāḥ = leave; vyasanāni cha = bad habits; kāmakrodha samutthāni = born of desire and wrath.

"Be even more humble and always keep the senses under control. Leave the bad habits born out of desire and wrath."

Sloka 43

parokṣayā vartamāno vṛttyā pratyakṣayā tathā || 2-3-43
amātyaprabhṛtīḥ sarvāḥ prakṛtīścānurañjaya |

43. vṛityā = By conduct; parokshayā = indirectly; tathā = and; vartamānaḥ = operating; pratyakshayā = directly; anuraJṇjaya = keep happy; amātya prabhṛitīḥ = (people) like ministers and others.

"Through direct and indirect means, keep ministers and others happy."

Sloka 44

koṣṭhāgārāyudhāgāraiḥ kṛtvā sanni cayān bahūn || 2-3-44
tuṣṭānuraktaprakṛtiryaḥ pālayati medinīm |
tasya nandanti mitrāṇi labdhvā.amṛtamivā.amarāḥ || 2-3-45
tasmāttvamapi cātmānaṃ niyamyaivaṃ samācara |

44;45. amarāḥ iva = like celestials; labdhvā = (who were happy after) obtaining; amṛitam = the nectar; tasya = his; mitrāṇi = friends; nandanti = are delighted with him; yaḥ = who; pālayati = rules; medinīm = the earth; kṛitvā = by making; bahūn = many; sannichayān = good storage; koshhṭhāgarāyudhāgāraiḥ = with granaries and armories; tushhṭānuraktaprakṛitiḥ = with delightful and happy people; tasmāt = hence; tvamapi = you too; ātmānam = (your)self; samāchara = act; evam = like this.

"Like the celestials becoming happy after obtaining the nectar, friends of a king (ruler of earth) are delighted when he fills the granaries and armories to the brim, making the common people delightful and happy. Hence, you too act like this."

Sloka 46

tacchrutvā suhṛdastasya rāmasya priyakāriṇaḥ || 2-3-46
tvaritāḥ śīghramabhyetya kausalyāyai nyavedayan |

46. tasya = that; rāmasya = Rama's; suhṛidaḥ = friends; shrutvā = after hearing; tat = that; tvaritāḥ = hurriedly; priyakāriṇaḥ = intending to do good; shīghram = immediately; abhyetya = came; kausalyāyai = to Kausalya; nyavedayan = (and) informed (her of the good news).

The well -wishers of Rama, after hearing those words, intending to do good to Kausalya, immediately came to her and informed the matter to her.

Sloka 47

sā hiraṇyaṃ ca gāścaiva ratnāni vividhāni ca || 2-3-47
vyādideśa priyākhyebhyaḥ kausalyā pramadottamā |

47. pramadottamā = the best among women; sā = that; kausalyā = Kausalya; vyādidesha = gave away; hiraṇyaṃ cha = gold; gāḥ chaiva = cows and; vividhāni = different types of; ratnānicha = diamonds also; priyākhyebhyaḥ = to those who informed the good news.

The best among women, Kausalya gave away gold, cows and different types of diamonds to those who informed her good news.

Sloka 48

athābhivādya rājānaṃ rathamāruhya rāghavaḥ || 2-3-48
yayau svaṃ dyutimadveśma janaughaiḥ pratipūjitaḥ |

48. atha = afterwards; rāghavaḥ = Rama; abhivādya = salutated; rājānam = to king Dasaratha; āruhya = (and) alighted; ratham = the chariot; yayau = (and) went; svam veshma = for his house; dyutimat = (which was) splendid; pratipūjitaḥ = after being worshipped; janaughaiḥ = by the crowds.

Rama, after offering salutations to king Dasaratha, ascended the chariot and went to his splendid house after being worshipped by the crowds on the way.

Sloka 49

te cāpi paurā nṛpatervacasta |
cchrutvā tadā lābhamiveṣṭamāśu |
narendramāmantrya gṛhāṇi gatvā |
devān samānarcuratiprahṛṣṭāḥ || 2-3-49

49. tadā = then; te = those; paurāshchāpi = citizens also; shrutvā = listened (to); tat = those; vachaḥ = words; nṛipateḥ = of the king; ishhṭam lābhamiva = seeing their dearest wish gratified;; ati prahṛishhṭāḥ = became very happy; āmantrya = took leave; nareṃdram = of king Dasaratha; āshu = immediately; samānarchuḥ = worshipped; devān = the gods; gatvā = going; gṛihāṇi = to their houses.

Having heard those words of the king, seeing their dearest wish gratified, the citizens took leave of the King Dasaratha and returned to their homes full of joy, in order to give thanks and render homage to the Gods.

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadvālmīkirāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyakāṇḍe tṛtīya sargaḥ ||

Thus completes 3rd chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
