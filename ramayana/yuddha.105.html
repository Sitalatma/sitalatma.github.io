Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 105
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sage Agastya advises Rama to recites the 'Aditya Hridaya', a collection of verses in praise of the sun-god, with a view to gaining victory.

Sloka 1

tato yuddhapariśrāntaṃ samare cintayā sthitam |
rāvaṇaṃ cāgrato dṛṣṭvā yuddhāya samupasthitam || 6-105-1
daivataiśca samāgamya draṣṭumabhyāgato raṇam |
upagamyābravīdrāmamagastyo bhagavān ṛṣiḥ || 6-105-2

1; 2. upagamya = approaching; rāmam = Rama; sthitam = standing; chintayā = absorbed in thought; samare = in the battle-field; yuddha parishrāntām = exhausted as he was by the fight; dṛiṣhṭvā = and seeing; rāvaṇam = Ravana; samupasthitam = who came in proximity; agrataḥ = facing him; yuddhāya = duly; prepared for a battle; bhagavān = the glorious; agastya ṛiṣhiḥ = sage;; Agastya; abhyāgataḥ = who had come; samāgamya = together; daivataiḥ = with the gods; draṣhṭum = to perceive; raṇam = the battle; tataḥ = then; abravīt = spoke (as follows).

Approaching Rama standing absorbed in fight in the battle-field, exhausted as he was by the fight, and seeing Ravana who came nearby, facing him duly prepared for the battle, the glorious sage, Agastya, who had come together with the gods, to perceive the battle, then spoke as foolows:

Sloka 3

rāma rāma mahābāho śṛṇu guhyaṃ sanātanam |
yena sarvānarīnvatsa samare vijayiṣyase || 6-105-3

3. rāma rāma = O Rama; mahābāho = the mighty armed!; shruṇu = hear; sanātanam guhyam = the follwoing eternal secret; yena by which; vijayiṣhyase = you can conquer; sarvān arīn = all the enemies; samare = in battle; vatsa = my child!.

"O Rama, the mighty armed! Hear the following eternal secret, by which you can conquer all the enemies in battle, my child!"

Sloka 4

ādityahṛdayaṃ puṇyaṃ sarvaśatruvināśanam |
jayāvahaṃ japennityamakṣayyaṃ paramaṃ śivam || 6-105-4
sarvamaṅgalamaṅgalyaṃ sarvapāpapraṇāśanam |
cintāśokapraśamanamāyurvardhanamuttamam || 6-105-5

4; 5. āditya hṛidayam = 'Aditya ḥridaya' (which is intended to propitiate Brahma; installed in the heart of the orb of the sun); japet = is to be muttered; nityam = at all times; (ṭhis hymn in praise of the sun); puṇyam = is holy; sarva shatruvināshanam = which can destroy all the adversaries; jayāvaham = which can bestow victory; akṣhyayyam = giving undecaying prosperity; paramam shivam = supremely auspicious; sarva maṇ^gala maṇ^galayam = the blessing of all blessings; sarva pāpa praṇāshanam = root out all sins; chintā shoka prashamanam = allay all anxiety and grief; uttamam = excellent; āyurvardhanam = and prolong life.

ṣloka 6

raśmimantaṃ samudyantaṃ devāsuranamaskṛtam |
pūjayasva vivasvantaṃ bhāskaraṃ bhuvaneśvaram || 6-105-6

6. punjayasva = you worship (the sun-god); bhuvaneshvaram = the ruler of the worlds; rashmimantam = who is crowned with the rays; samudyantam = who appears at the horizon; devāsmanamaskṛitam = who is greeted by the gods and the demons alike; vivasvantam = shining forth covering all other lights; bhāskaram = and brings light to the world.

"Worship the sun-god, the ruler of the worlds, who is crowned with the rays, who appears at the horizon, who is greeted by the gods and the demons alike, shines forth; covering up all other lights and brings light to the world."

Sloka 7

sarvadevātmako hyeṣa tejasvī raśmibhāvanaḥ |
eṣa devāsuragaṇān lokān pāti gabhastibhiḥ || 6-105-7

7. eṣhaḥ = he is; sarva devātmakaḥ = the embodiment of all gods; tejasvī = full of glory; rashmibhāvanaḥ = and the reflector of rays; eṣhaḥ = ḥe; pāti = protects; devāsura gaṇān = the multitude of gods and demons; lokān = as well as their worlds; (by his rays).

"He is the embodiment of all gods, full of glory and the reflector of rays. He protects the multitude of gods and demons as well as their worlds by his rays."

Sloka 8

eṣa brahmā ca viṣṇuśca śivaḥ skandaḥ prajāpatiḥ |
mahendro dhanadaḥ kālo yamaḥ somo hyapāṃpatiḥ || 6-105-8

8. eṣhaḥ = ḥe; brahmācha = is Brahma the creator; viṣhṇushcha = Vishnu; the protector of the universe; shivaḥ = Lord ṣhiva the god of destruction; skandaḥ = ṣkanda (son of Lord ṣhiva); prajapatiḥ = Prajapati (the ten lords of created beings viz. ṃarichi; Atri; Angirasa; Paulastya; Pulaka; Kratu; Vasishta; Daksha; Bhrigu; ṇarada); mahendraḥ = Indra the ruler of gods; dhanadaḥ = Kubera; teh bestower of riches; kālaḥ = Kala; the ṭime-spirit; yamaḥ = Yama; the god of retribution; somaḥ = ṣoma the moon-god; apāmpatiḥ = Varuna; the ruler of the waters.

"He is Brahma the creator, Vishnu the protector of the Universe, Lord Shiva the god of destruction, Skanda (son of Lord Shiva), Prajapati (the ten lords of creted beings), Indra the ruler of gods, Kubera the bestower of riches, Kala the Time-spirit, Yama the god of retribution, Soma the moon-god and Varuna the ruler of the waters."

Sloka 9

pitaro vasavaḥ sādhyā aśvinau maruto manuḥ |
vāyurvahniḥ prajāḥ prāṇa ṛtukartā prabhākaraḥ || 6-105-9

9. pitarah = the Pitrus (ṃanes); vasavaḥ = the eight Vasns; sādhyāḥ = the twelve ṣadhyas; ashvinau = the two Ashvins = the physicians of gods; marutaḥ = the fortynine ṃaruts (wind-gods); manuḥ = ṃanu ( a progenitor of the human race); vāyuḥ = Vayu; the wind-god; vahniḥ = the fire-god; prajāḥ = the created beings; prāṇaḥ = the life-breath of the universe; ṛitikartā = the source of the seasons; prabhākaraḥ = and store-house of light.

"The Pitrus (manes), the eight Vasus, the twelve Sadhyas, the two Ashvins the physicians of gods, the fortymine ṃaruts (wind-gods), ṃanu (a progenitor of the human race), Vayu the wind-god, the fire-god, the created beings, the life-breath of the universe, the source of teh seasons and the store-house of light."

Sloka 10

ādityaḥ savitā sūryaḥ khagaḥ pūṣā gabhastimān |
suvarṇasadṛśo bhānurhiraṇyaretā divākaraḥ || 6-105-10

10. ādityaḥ = an off-spring of Aditi; savitā = the progenitor of all; sūryaḥ = ṣurya; the sun-god and the provocator of acts in people; khagaḥ = the courser in the sky; pūṣhā = the nourisher of all with rain; gabhastimān = the possessor of rays; suvarṇa sadṛishaḥ = the golden; bhānuḥ = the brilliant; hiraṇya retāḥ = having golden seed; divākraḥ = and the maker of the day.

"An off-spring of Aditi, the progenitor of all, Surya the sun-god and the provocator of acts in people, the courser in the sky, the nourisher of all iwth rain, the possessor of rays the golden, the brilliant, having golden seed whose energy constitutes the seed of the universe and the maker of the day."

Sloka 11

haridaśvaḥ sahasrārciḥ saptasaptirmarīcimān |
timironmadhanaḥ śambhustvaṣṭā mārtaṇḍako.aṃśumān || 6-105-11

11. haridashvaḥ sapta saptiḥ = he has seven green horses; marīchimān = full of rays; timironmathanaḥ = the destroyer of darkness; shambhuḥ = the source of happiness; tvaṣhṭā = maker of thinness; mārtaṇ^Dakaḥ = the infuser of life in the lifeless cosmic egg; amshumān = and radiant.

"He has seven green horses, is thousand-rayed, full of rays, the destroyer of darkness, the source of happiness, maker of thinness, the infuser of life in the lifeless cosmic egg and radiant."

Sloka 12

hiraṇyagarbhaḥ śiśirastapano.ahaskaro raviḥ |
agnigarbho.aditeḥ putraḥ śaṅkhaḥ śiśiranāśanaḥ || 6-105-12

12. hiraṇyagarbhaḥ = (ḥe is) a golden foetus; shishiraḥ = maker of coolness; tapanaḥ = creator of heat; ahaskāḥ = the bringer of a day; raviḥ = the one who is eulogized by all; agnigarbhaḥ = pregnant with fire; aditoḥ putraḥ = the son of Aditi; shaṇ^khaḥ = the one who pacifies himself in the evening; shishiranāshanaḥ = and the destroyer of frost.

"He is a golden foetus, maker of coolness, creator of heat, the bringer of a day, the one who is eulogized by all, pregnant with fire, the son of Aditi, the one who pacifies himself in the evening and the destroyer of frost."

Sloka 13

vyomanāthastamobhedī bugyajuḥ sāmapāragaḥ |
ghanavṛṣṭirapāṃ mitro vindhyavīthīplavaṃgamaḥ || 6-105-13

13. vyomanāthaḥi = (ḥe is) the lord of the sky; tamobhedī = disperser of darkness; ṛigyajuḥ sāmapāragaḥ = the master of the three Vedas (Rik; ṣama and Yajur); ghana vṛiṣhṭiḥ = the sender of thick rain; mitraḥ = the friend; apām = of water; vindhyavīthīplavangamaḥ = and the one who courses swiftly in the path of the sky.

"He is the lord of the sky, the disperser of darkness, the master of the three Vedas (Viz. Rik, Sama and Yajur), the sender of thick rain, the friend of water and the on who courses swiftly in the path of the sky."

Sloka 14

ātapī maṇḍalī mṛtyuḥ piṅgalaḥ sarvatāpanaḥ |
kavirviśvo mahātejā raktaḥ sarvabhavodbhavaḥ || 6-105-14

14. ātapī = (ḥe is) the radiator of heat; maṇ^Dalī = and adorned with a circle of rays; mṛityuḥ = ḥe is death (himself); piṇ^galaḥ = tawny of hue; sarva tāpanaḥ = giver of warmth to all; kaviḥ = (ḥe is) omniscent; vishvaḥ = all-formed; mahātejāḥ = endowed with extra-ordinary splendour; raktaḥ = the one who has an affection in all; sarvabhavodbhavaḥ = and the source of all evolutes.

"He is the radiator of heat and adrned with a circle of rays. He is death himself, tawny of hue, a giver of warmth to all, omniscent, all-formed, endowed with extra-ordinary splendour, the one who has an affection in all and the source of all evolutes."

Sloka 15

nakṣatragrahatārāṇāmadhipo viśvabhāvanaḥ |
tejasāmapi tejasvī dvādaśātmannamo.stu te || 6-105-15

15. nakṣhatra grahatārāṇām adhipaḥ = the controller of all lunar mansions; planets and stars; vishvabhāvanaḥ = the creator of all; tejasāmapi tejasvī = the resplendent among the splendid; dvādashātman = O god; appearing in twelve forms (in the shape of twelve months in a year); te namaḥ astu = hail to you!.

"He is the controller of all lunar mansions, planets and stars, the creator of all and the resplendent among the splendid. O god, appearing in twelve forms (in the shape of twelve months in a year), hail to you."

Sloka 16

namaḥ pūrvāya giraye paścimāyādraye namaḥ |
jyotirgaṇānāṃ pataye dinādhipataye namaḥ || 6-105-16

16. namaḥ = ḥail to you; pūrvāya giraye = in the form of eastern mountain namaḥ = and hail; pashchimāya adraye = to the western mountain; namaḥ = ḥail; yotirgaṇānām pataye = to the lord of hosts of luminaries; namaḥ = hail; dinādhipataye = to the lord of the day.

"Hail to you in the form of eastern mountain and hail to the western mountain. Hail to the lord of host of luminaries and hail to the lord of the day."

Sloka 17

jayāya jayabhadrāya haryāśvāya namo namaḥ |
namo namaḥ sahasrāṃśo ādityāya namo namaḥ || 6-105-17

17. namaḥ jayāya = hail to you; the giver of victory; namaḥ jayabhadrāya = hail to you; the joy born of victory!; namaḥ harvashvāya = hail to you; the god having green horses; namaḥ sahasrāmshaḥ = hail to you; having thousands of rays!; namaḥ namaḥ ādityāya = hail; hail to you the son of Aditi!

"Hail to you, the giver of victory, hail to you, the joy born of victory! Hail to you, the god with green horses, hail to you, having thousands of rays! Hail, hail to you the son of Aditi!

Sloka 18

nama ugrāya vīrāya sāraṅgāya namo namaḥ |
namaḥ padmaprabodhāya pracaṇḍāya namo.astu te || 6-105-18

18. namaḥ ugrāya = hail to you; the fierce one! namaḥ vīrāya = hail to you; the valiant one!; namaḥ sāraṇ^gāya = hail to you; the one with variegated colours!; namaḥ padma prabodhāya = hail to you; the awakener of the lotus!; te namaḥ tu prachaṇ^Dāya = hail to you the furious one!

"Hail to you, the fierce one, hail to you the valiant one! Hail to you, the one with variegated colours! Hail to you, the awakener of the louts, hail to you, the furious one!"

Sloka 19

brahmeśānācyuteśāya sūryāyādityavarcase |
bhāsvate sarvabhakṣāya raudrāya vapuṣe namaḥ || 6-105-19

19. namaḥ = hail to you; brahmeshānāchyuteshāya = the ruler of Brahma; Lord ṣhiva and Lord Vishnu; the infallible; sūryāya = the sun-god; āditya varchase = the light indwelling the solar orb; bhāsvate = the resplendent one; sarva bhakṣhāya = the devourer of all; raudrāya vapuṣhe = appearing in teh form of Rudra.

"Hail to you, the ruler of Brahma, Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu the infallible, the sun-god, the light indwelling the solar orb, the resplendent one, the devourer of all, appearing in form of Rudra."

Sloka 20

tamoghnāya himagnāya śatrughnāyāmitātmane |
kṛtaghnaghnāya devāya jyotiṣāṃ pataye namaḥ || 6-105-20

20. namaḥ = ḥail to you; tamoghnāya = the dispeller of darkness; himaghnāya = the destroyer of cold; shatrughnāya = teh exterminator of foes; amitātmane = the one whose extent is immeasurable; kṛitaghnaghnāya = the destroyer of the ungreateful; devāya = the god; jyotiṣhām pataye = who is the ruler of all lights.

"Hail to you, the dispeller of darkness, the destroyer of cold, the exterminator of foes, the one whose extent in immeasurable, the destroyer of the ungrateful, the god, who is the ruler of all lights."

Sloka 21

taptacāmīkarābhāya haraye viśvakarmaṇe |
namastamobhinighnāya rucaye lokasākṣiṇe || 6-105-21

21. namaḥ = ḥail to you; tapta chāmīkarābhāya = possessing the lustre of refined gold; haraye = the dispeller of ignorance; vishvakarmaya = the architect of the universe; tamobhinighnāya = the uprooter of darkness; ruchaye = the splendour incarnate; lokasākṣhiṇe = the onlooker of the world!

"Hail to you, possessing the lustre of refined gold, the dispeller of ignorance, the architect of the universe, the uprooter of darkness, the splendour incarnate, the onlooker of the world!'"

Sloka 22

nāśayatyeṣa vai bhūtaṃ tadeva sṛjati prabhuḥ |
pāyatyeṣa tapatyeṣa varṣatyeṣa gabhastibhiḥ || 6-105-22

22. eṣhaḥ vai prabhuḥ = this sun; the lord alone; nāshayati = destroys; tadeva sṛijati = and brings into existence; bhūtam = all the beings; eṣhaḥ = he; tapati pāyati = radiates heat; gabhastiḥ = by his rays; eṣhaḥ = ḥe; varṣhati = sends the rain.

"This sun, the lord alone destroys and brings into existence all the brings. He radiates heat by his rays. He sends the rain."

Sloka 23

eṣa supteṣu jāgarti bhūteṣu pariniṣṭhitaḥ |
eṣa caivāgnihotraṃ ca phalaṃ caivāgnihotriṇām || 6-105-23

23. pariniṣhṭhitaḥ = planted in all created beings; eṣhaḥ = he; jāgarti = remains awake; supteṣhu = when they have fallen asleep; eṣhaḥ chaiva = he alone; agnihotram cha = is the oblation to the fire-god; phalam cha = and the fruit; agnihotriṇām = attained by those who pour such oblations.

"Planted in all created beings, he remains awake, when they have fallen asleep. He alone is the oblation to the fire-god and the fruit attained by those who pour such oblations."

Sloka 24

devāśca kratavaścaiva kratūnāṃ phalameva ca |
yāni kṛtyāni lokeṣu sarveṣu paramaprabhuḥ || 6-105-24

24. devāḥ = (ḥe comprises) all the gods; kratavashchaiva = as well as the sacrifices; kratavashchaiva = as well as the sacrifices; kratūnām phalamevacha = as also the fruit of sacrifices; parama prabhuḥ = he is the supremely competent one; kṛityāmi = of all activities; yāni = which are found; sarveṣhu lokeṣhu = in all the worlds.

"He comprises all the gods, as well as the sacrifies as also the fruit of all sacrifices. He is a supremely competent one of all activities, which are found in all the worlds."

Sloka 25

enamāpatsu kṛcchreṣu kāntāreṣu bhayeṣu ca |
kīrtayan puruṣaḥ kaścinnāvasīdati rāghava || 6-105-25

25. rāghava = O Rama!; na kashchit puruṣhaḥ = no individual; kīrtayan = glorifying; enam = this sun-god; āpatsu = in distresses; kṛichchheṣhu = in difficulties; kāntāreṣhu = in the wood; bhayeṣhu = in times of peril; avasīdati = comes to grief.

"O Rama! No individual glorifying this sun-god, in distresses in difficulties; in the woods or in times of peril, comes to grief."

Sloka 26

pūjayasvainamekāgro devadevaṃ jagatpatim |
etattriguṇitaṃ japtvā yuddheṣu vijayiṣyasi || 6-105-26

26. pūjayasva = worship; enam = this sun-god; jagatpatim = the lord of the universe; devadevam = and the god of all gods; ekāgraḥ = with undivided attention; japtyā = muttering; etat = this praise; triguṇetam = three times; vijayiṣhyasi = you will come out victorious; yuddheṣhu = in battles.

"Worship this sun-god, the lord of the universe and the god of all gods with undivided attention. ṃuttering this praise three times, you will come out victorious in battles."

Sloka 27

asmin kṣaṇe mahābāho rāvaṇaṃ tvaṃ vadhiṣyasi |
evamuktvā tato.agastyo jagāma sa yathāgatam || 6-105-27

27. tvam = you; vadhiṣhyasi = will be able to kill; rāvaṇam = Ravana; asmin kṣhaṇe = at this very moment; mahābāho = O the mighty armed!; evam uktvā = saying so; agastya = the sage; Agastya; tataḥ = thereupon; jagāma = left; yathāgatam = as in the same way as he had come.

"You will be able to kill Ravana at this very moment, O mighty armed one!" Saying so, the sage, Agastya, thereupon, left as in the same way as he had come.

Sloka 28

etacchrutvā mahātejā naṣṭaśoko.abhavattadā |
dhārayāmāsa suprīto rāghavaḥ prayatātmavān || 6-105-28

28. shrutvā = hearing; etat = this; mahātejāḥ = Rama; of extraordinary energy; tadā = then; abhavat = became; naṣhṭa shokaḥ = bereft of anguish; suprītaḥ = feeling greatly delighted; rāghavaḥ = Rama; dhārayāmāsa = retained in memory; (that hymn of praise of the sun-god); prayatātmavān = with a devoted mind.

Hearing this, Rama of extra ordinary energy, then became bereft of anguish. Feeling greatly delighted, Rama retained that hymn in his memory with a devoted mind.

Sloka 29

ādityaṃ prekṣya japtvā tu paraṃ harṣamavāptavān |
trirācamya śucirbhūtvā dhanurādāya vīryavān || 6-105-29
rāvaṇaṃ prekṣya hṛṣṭātmā yuddhārthaṃ samupāgamat |
sarvayatnena mahatā vadhe tasya dhṛto.abhavat || 6-105-30

29; 30. āchamya = sipping the water; triḥ = thrice; shuchiḥ bhūtvā = and getting purified; prekṣhya = looking intently; ādityam = on the orb of the sun; japtvā = and repeating this prayer; vīryavān = the valiant Rama; avāptavān = obtained; param = a great; harṣham = rejoice; ādāya = seizing hold; dhanuḥ = of his bow; prekṣhya = and seeing; rāvanam = Ravana; (Rama); hṛiṣhṭātmā = felt delighted in mind; samupāgamat = and advanced; yuddhārtham = for the fight; abhavat dhṛitaḥ = he stood voed; tasya vadhe = to kill Ravana; mahatā sarva yatnena = with an intense and all-sided effort.

Sipping the water thrice and getting purified looking intently on the orb of the sun and repeating this prayer, the valiant Rama obtained a great rejoice. Seizing hold of his bow and seeing Ravana, Rama felt delighted in mind and marched forward for the fight. He stood voed to kill Ravana, with an intense and all-sided effort.

Sloka 31

atha raviravadannirīkṣya rāmaṃ |
muditamanāḥ paramaṃ prahṛṣyamāṇaḥ |
niśicarapatisaṃkṣayaṃ viditvā |
suragaṇamadhyagato vacastvareti || 6-105-31

31. nirīkṣhya = seeing; rāmam = Rama; raviḥ = the sun-god; atha = thereupon; muditamanāḥ = with a delighted mind; param prahṛiṣhyamāṇaḥ = and becoming most rejoiced; viditvā = on foreseeing; nishicharapatisamkṣhayam = Ravana's death; avadat = spoke; vachaḥ = the words; iti = saying; tvara = "ḥsten up"; suragaṇamadhyagataḥ = standing as he was in teh middle of a troop of celestials.

Seeing Rama with a delighted mind, and becoming most exhildrated on foreseeing the death of Ravana, the sun-god, standing in the middle of a troop of celestials, exclaimed, "Hasten up."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe pañcottaraśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 105th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
