chapter [Sarga] 34

Introduction

Rama comes to see his father Dasharatha and Dasharatha summons Sumantra to fetch all his wives to hither, and asks Rama to stay with him at least for a night. But Rama resolved in his vow, persuades his father to be peaceful. Dasharatha's anguish intensifies and he swoons at the departure of Rama.


Sloka 1

tataḥkamalapatrākṣaḥ śyāmo nirupamo mahān |
uvāca rāmastam sūtaṃ piturākhyāhi māmiti || 2-34-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; kamalapatrākshhaḥ = having eyes resembling lotus leaves; shyāmaḥ = the dark-brown; coloured man; mahān = the mighty man; nirupamaḥ = beyond compare; uvācha = spoke; tam sūtam = to that charioteer; iti = thus; ākhyāhi = "ṭell; mām = about me; pituḥ = to father.

Thereafter, the lotus-eyes Rama, who was dark-brown in complexion and great beyond compare, spoke thus to the charioteer, "Tell about me to my father."

Sloka 2

sa rāma preṣitaḥ kṣipram samtāpa kaluṣa indriyaḥ |
praviśya nṛpatim sūtaḥ nihśvasantam dadarśa ha || 2-34-2

2. saḥ sūtaḥ = that charioteer; santāparkalushhendriyaḥ = with his senses disturbed by grief; rāma preshhitaḥ = having been sent by Rama; kshhipram = quickly; pravishya = entered; dadarsha ha = and saw; nṛipatim = the king; niḥshvasantam = heaving sighs.

Quickly entering inside, when sent by Rama, the charioteer saw the king having sighs, his senses disturbed by grief.

Sloka 3

uparaktamivādityaṃ bhasmacchannamivānalam|
taṭākamiva nistoyamapaśyajjagatīpatim|| 2-34-3

3. apashyat = (he) saw;; jagatīpatim = the king; ādityam iva = resembling a sun; uparaktam = eclipsed; analam iva = like fire; bhasmachchhannam = covered by ash; tatākam iva = as a lake; nistoyam = without water.

He saw the king resembling an eclipsed sun, like fire covered by ash, as a lake without water.

Sloka 4

ālokya tu mahā prājñaḥ parama ākula cetasam |
rāmam eva anuśocantam sūtaḥ prānjalir āsadat || 2-34-4

4. sūtaḥ = the charioteer; mahāprāJṇaḥ = a great intellectual; ālokyatu = saw; anushochantam = the repenting; varamākula chetasam = mind greatly agitated; āsadat = approached; prāṇ^jalaiḥ = with joined pams.

The charioteer, a great intellectual, saw Dasaratha repenting with his mind greatly agitated and approached him with joined palms.

Sloka 5

tam vardhayitvā rājānam sūtaḥ pūrvam jayāśiṣā|
bhayaviklabayā vācā mandayā ślakṣṇamabravīt || 2-34-5

5. sūtaḥ = the charioteer; pūrvam = first; vardhayitvā = inspiring; tam rājānam = the king; jayāshishhā = with blessings of triumph; abravīt = spoke; mandayā = in slow; shlakshhṇam = and sweet; vachā = words; bhaya viklabayā = overcome with fear.

The charioteer, first inspiring the king with blessings of triumph, spoke thus in a slow and sweet voice, overcome with fear.

Sloka 6

ayam sa puruṣa vyāghra dvāri tiṣṭhati te sutaḥ |
brāhmaṇebhyo dhanam dattvā sarvam caiva upajīvinām || 2-34-6

6. te sutaḥ = your son; purushhavyāghraḥ = tiger among men; saḥ ayam = as such; dattvā = having given away; sarvam = all; dhanam = wealth; brāhmaṇebhyaḥ = to brahmanas; upajīvināmchaiva = and dependents; tishhṭati = is standing; dvāri = at the gate.

"Your son, the tiger among men, having given away all his wealth to brahmanas and dependents, is waiting at the gate."

Sloka 7

sa tvā paśyatu bhadram te rāmaḥ satya parākramaḥ |
sarvān suhṛdāapṛccya tvām idānīm didṛkṣate || 2-34-7

7. bhadram = auspiciousness; te = to you! saḥ rāmaḥ = (let) that Rama; satya parākramaḥ = who is truly brave; pashyatu = see; tvā = you; āpṛichchhya = after bidding farewell; sarvam suhṛidaḥ = to all friends; didṛikshhate = (he) wants to see; tvām = you; idānīm = now.

"ṃay auspiciousness betide you! Let that Rama, who is unfailingly brave, see you. After bidding farewell to all friends, he wants to see you now."

Sloka 8

gamiṣyati mahā araṇyam tam paśya jagatī pate |
vṛtam rāja guṇaiḥ sarvaiḥ ādityam iva raśmibhiḥ || 2-34-8

8. jagatīpate = Oh; king! gamishhyati = (ḥe) is seeting out; mahāraṇyam = to a great forest; pashya = see; tam = that Rama; ādityamiva = who is like sun; rashmibhiḥ = with rays of light; vṛitam = displaying; rājaguṇaiḥ = royal qualities.

"Oh, king! Rama is setting out to a great forest. See him who is resembling a sun with rays of light, displaying royal qualities."

Sloka 9

sa satya vādī dharma ātmā gāmbhīryāt sāgara upamaḥ |
ākāśaiva niṣpanko nara indraḥ pratyuvāca tam || 2-34-9

9. saḥ narendraḥ = that king Dasaratha; satya vādī = who speaks truth; dharmātmā = a virtuous man; sāgaropamaḥ = who is like an ocean; gāmbhīryāt = by his depth of character; nishhpaṇkaḥ = who is blemish-less; ākāsha iva = like a sky; pratyuvācha = replied; tam = to him(thus).

That king Dasaratha, who speaks truth, whose mind is virtuous, who is like an ocean by his depth of character and who is blemish less like a sky, replied to Sumantra as follows:

Sloka 10

sumantra ānaya me dārān ye kecit iha māmakāḥ |
dāraiḥ parivṛtaḥ sarvaiḥ draṣṭum iccāmi rāghavam || 2-34-10

10. sumantra = Oh; ṣumantra! ye kachit = whoever; māmakāḥ = my wives; iha = are here; anaya = bring; me dārān = such of my wives; parivṛitaḥ = surrounded by; sarvaiḥ = all; dāraiḥ = wives; ichchhāmi = I want; drashhṭum = to see; dhārmikam = the virtuous Rama.

"Oh, Sumantra! being all my wives, who are here. Surrounded by all of them, I want to see the virtuous Rama."

Sloka 11

so antaḥ puram atītya eva striyaḥ tā vākyam abravīt |
āryo hvayati vo rājā gamyatām tatra māciram || 2-34-11

11. saḥ = ḥe; atītyaiva = after entering; antaḥ puram = the gynaecium; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; tāḥ striyaḥ = to those women; āryāḥ = "Oh; the venerable ladies! rājā = the king; hvayati = is calling; vaḥ = you; gamyatām = go; tatra = there; mā chiram = without delay.

Sumantra, after entering the gynaecium, spoke these words to those women as follows: "Oh, the venerable ladies! The king is calling you. Go there without delay.

Sloka 12

evam uktāḥ striyaḥ sarvāḥ sumantreṇa nṛpa ājñayā |
pracakramus tat bhavanam bhartur ājñāya śāsanam || 2-34-12

12. sarvāḥ = all; stiyaḥ = women; uktāḥ = spoken; evam = thus; sumantreṇa = by ṣumantra; nṛipāJṇayā = as per king's orders; prachakramaḥ = moved; tadbhavanam = to his house; āJṇāya = after knowing; shāsanam = the instruction; bhartuḥ = of their husband.

All those women, asked thus by Sumantra as per the king's orders, went to his palace, after knowing the instructions of their husband.

Sloka 13

ardha sapta śatāḥ tāḥ tu pramadāḥ tāmra locanāḥ |
kausalyām parivārya atha śanaiḥ jagmur dhṛta vratāḥ || 2-34-13

13. atha = thereafter; tāḥ = those; ardha sapta shatāḥ = three hundred and fifty; pramadāḥ = women; dhṛitavratāḥ = steadfast in their vow (of devotion to their husband); tāmra lochanāḥ = having red eyes; parivārya = encircling; kausalyām = Kausaly; jagmuḥ = went; shanaiḥ = slowly.

Encircling Kausalya, three hundred fifty women, steadfast in their vow(of devotion to their husband), with their eyes reddened, went there slowly.

Sloka 14

āgateṣu ca dāreṣu samavekṣya mahī patiḥ |
uvāca rājā tam sūtam sumantra ānaya me sutam || 2-34-14

14. āgateshhu = after arrival;dāreshhu = of wives; rājā = Dasaratha; mahīpatih = the lord of the earth; samavekshhya = looked; tam sūtam = towards that chariorteer; uvācha = and spoke(thus); sumantra = Oh; ṣumantra! ānaya = bring; me sutam = my son.

After arrival of his wives, king Dasaratha spoke to that charioteer as follows, "Oh, Sumantra! Bring my son here."

Sloka 15

sa sūtaḥ rāmam ādāya lakṣmaṇam maithilīm tadā |
jagāma abhimukhaḥ tūrṇam sakāśam jagatī pateḥ || 2-34-15

15. tadā = then; saḥ sūtah = that charioteer; ādāya = took; rāmam = Rama; lakshhmaṇam = Lakshmana; maithilīm = and ṣeetha; tūrṇam = speedily; jagāma = went; abhimukhaḥ = towards; jagatīpataḥ = the king's sakāsham = presence.

Taking Rama, Lakshmana and Seetha, that charioteer then speedily went to the king's presence.

Sloka 16

sa rājā putram āyāntam dṛṣṭvā dūrāt kṛta anjalim |
utpapāta āsanāt tūrṇam ārtaḥ strī jana samvṛtaḥ || 2-34-16

16. saḥ rājā = that king; strījana samvṛitaḥ = surrounded by women; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; durāt = from a distance; putram = his son; ayāntam = coming; kṛitāṇjalim = with folded hands; ārtaḥ = being distressed; utpapāta = rose; āsanāt = from his seat; tūrṇam = swiftly.

King Dasaratha surrounded by women, seeing from a distance his son coming with folded hands, distressed as he was, rose swiftly from his seat.

Sloka 17

so abhidudrāva vegena rāmam dṛṣṭvā viśām patiḥ |
tam asamprāpya duhkha ārtaḥ papāta bhuvi mūrcitaḥ || 2-34-17

17. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; rāmam = Rama; saḥ = Dasaratha; vishāmpatiḥ = the ruler of people; abhidadrāva = ran towards him; vegena = speedily; asamprāpya = without reaching; tam = him; papāta = fell; bhuvi = on the floor; mūrchhitaḥ = senseless; duḥkhārtaḥ = afflicted with sorrow.

Seeing Rama, King Dasaratha ran speedily towards him. But without reaching him he fell on the floor senseless, afflicted as he was with sorrow.

Sloka 18

tam rāmaḥ abhyapātat kṣipram lakṣmaṇaḥ ca mahā rathaḥ |
visamjñam iva duhkhena saśokam nṛpatim tadā || 2-34-18

18. tadā = tehn; rāmaḥ = Rama; lakshhmaṇashche = and Lakshmana; mahārathaḥ = an eminent car-warrior; kshhipram = quickly; abhyapatat = approached; tam nṛipatim = that king; visamJṇam iva = who seemed senseless; duḥkhena = with grief; sashokam = through agony.

Then, Rama and Lakshmana an eminent car-warrior, quickly approached that king who seemed unconscious with full of grief as he was, through agony.

Sloka 19

strī sahasra ninādaḥ ca samjajñe rāja veśmani |
hāhā rāma iti sahasā bhūṣaṇa dhvani mūrcitaḥ || 2-34-19

19. samjaJṇe = generated; sahasā = all at once; rājaveshmani = from the royal palace; strīsahasraninādashcha = sound of thousands of women crying; hā hā rāma iti = "Alas! Alas! Oh Rama !" bhūshhaṇa dhvanimūrchhitaḥ = mixed with the sound of tinkling of ornaments.

Sounds of thousands of women crying "Alas! Alas! Oh Rama!" generated all at once from the royal palace, mixed with the sound of tinkling ornaments.

Sloka 20

tam pariṣvajya bāhubhyām tāv ubhau rāma lakṣmaṇau |
paryanke sītayā sārdham rudantaḥ samaveśayan || 2-34-20

20. tau = those; ubhau = both; rāmalakshhmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; sītayā sārdham = along with ṣeetha; parishhvajya = held; tam = Dasaratha; bāhubhyām = in their arms; rudanta = and crying; samaveshayan = laid him; paryaṇke = in the couch.

Holding Dasaratha in their arms and crying, both Rama and Lakshmana along with Seetha laid him on the couch.

Sloka 21

atha rāmaḥ muhūrtena labdha samjñam mahī patim |
uvāca prānjalir bhūtvā śoka arṇava pariplutam || 2-34-21

21. atha = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; bhūtvā = becoming; praṇjaliḥ = one with folded hands; uvācha = spoke(as follows) mahīpatim = to king Dasaratha; labdha samJṇam = who got consciousness; muhūrtena = within a short time; shokārṇava pariplutam = imundated in a sea of sorrow.

Then, Rama with folded hands spoke thus to king Dasaratha who got consciousness within a short time and who was inundated in a sea of sorrow.

Sloka 22

āpṛcce tvām mahā rāja sarveṣām īśvaraḥ asi naḥ |
prasthitam daṇḍaka araṇyam paśya tvam kuśalena mām || 2-34-22

22. mahāra = Oh Emperor! āpṛichchhe = I am seaking permission; tvām = from you; asi = you are; īshvaraḥ = the lord; sarveshhām = to all; naḥ = of us; tvam = you; pashya = see; kushalena = auspiciously; mām = towards me; prasthitaḥ = setting out; daṇdakāraṇyam = to the forest of Dandaka.

"Oh, emperor! I take leave of you, who are the lord of all of us. See auspiciously towards me, who am about to leave for Dandaka forest."

Sloka 23

lakṣmaṇam ca anujānīhi sītā ca anveti mām vanam |
kāraṇaiḥ bahubhis tathyaiḥ vāryamāṇau na ca iccataḥ || 2-34-23

23. anujānīhi = permit; lakshhmaṇamcha = Lakshmana also; sītācha = ṣeetha too; anveti = is accompanying; mām = me; vanam = to the forest; vāryamāṇau = even if prevented; bahubhiḥ = by many; tathyaiḥ = true; kāranaiḥ = reasons; nachaichchhataḥ = these two are not agreeing.

"Permit Lakshmana also and Seetha too who is accompanying me to the forest. Even if prevented(by me0 on many true reasons, these two are not agreeing to stay behind"

Sloka 24

anujānīhi sarvān naḥ śokam utsṛjya mānada |
lakṣmaṇam mām ca sītām ca prajāpatir iva prajāḥ || 2-34-24

24. utsṛijya = giving up; shokam = grief; mānada = Oh; the bestower of honour! anujānīhi = grant leave; sarvān = to all; naḥ = of us; lakshhmaṇam = Lakshmana; māmeha = myself; sitāmcha = and ṣeetha; prajāḥ iva = as to his sons; prajāpatiḥ = Brahma.

"Giving up grief, Oh the bestower of honour, grant leave to all of us, Lakshmana myself and Seetha as Brahma(the lord of creation) did to his sons(sanaka and his three brothers who intended to go to forest for practising austerities)."

Sloka 25

pratīkṣamāṇam avyagram anujñām jagatī pateḥ |
uvāca rarjā samprekṣya vana vāsāya rāghavam || 2-34-25

25. samprekshhya = gazing; rāghavam = at Rama; avyagram = who is unruffled; pratīkshhamāṇam = awaiting; anuJṇyām = permission; jagatīpateḥ = of the king; vanavāsāya = stay in the forest; rājā = the king Dasaratha; uvācha = spoke(thus).

Gazing at Rama who is unruffled, awaiting permission of the king for stay in the forest, the king Dasaratha spoke thus:

Sloka 26

aham rāghava kaikeyyā vara dānena mohitaḥ |
ayodhyāyāḥ tvam eva adya bhava rājā nigṛhya mām || 2-34-26

26. rāghava = Oh; Rama! aham = I; mohitaḥ = was stupefied; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; varadānena = through a boon; adya = now; nigṛihya = by confirning; mām = me; tvameva = you alone; bhava = be; rājā = the king; ayodhyāyāḥ = of Ayodhya.

"Oh, Rama! I was stupefied by Kaikeyi through a boon. Now, by confining me, be you the king of Ayodhya."

Sloka 27

evam uktaḥ nṛpatinā rāmaḥ dharmabhṛtām varaḥ |
pratyuvāca anjalim kṛtvā pitaram vākya kovidaḥ || 2-34-27

27. rāmaḥ = Rama; varaḥ = the best man; dharma bhṛitām = in supporting righteousness; vākyakovidaḥ = who is proficient in speech; uktaḥ = after being spoken; evam = thus; nṛipatinā = by the king; pratyuvācha = replied; pitaram = to his father; aṇjalim kṛitvā = after making salutation with joined palms.

When told thus by the king, Rama the best man in supporting righteousness and who is proficient in expression, replied( as follows) to his father, after making salutation with joined palms.

Sloka 28

bhavān varṣa sahasrāya pṛthivyā nṛpate patiḥ |
aham tu araṇye vatsyāmi na me kāryam tvayā anṛtam || 2-34-28

28. nṛipate = Oh; King! bhavān = you be; patiḥ = the lord; pṛithivyāḥ = of the earth; varshhasahasrāya = for thousand years; aham tu = but I; vatsyāmi = shall stay arṇye = in the forest; me = for me; na kāryam = do not make; anṛitam = untruth; tvayā = of you.

"Oh, king! You be the ruler of the earth for thousand years. But, I for my part, will stay in the forest. For my sake, do not generate untruth about you."

Sloka 29

nava pañca ca varṣāṇi vanavāse vihṛtya te |
punaḥ pādau grahīṣyāmi pratijñānte narādhipaḥ || 2-34-29

29. narādhipa = Oh; ruler of men! vihṛitya = having strolled; vanavāse = in the forest; nava paṇchācha = for fourteen; vashhāni = years; pratiJṇānte = after fulfillingthe promise; grahishhyāmi = I shall clasp; te = your; pādau = feet; punaḥ = once more.

"Oh, ruler of men! Having strolled in the forest for fourteen years, I shall clasp your feet once more after fulfilling my promise."

Sloka 30

rudannāha priyam putraṃ satyapāśena saṃyataḥ |
kaikeyyā codyamānstu mitho rājā tamabravīt || 2-34-30

30. chodyamānastu = prompted; mithaḥ = secretly; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; ārtaḥ = the distressed; rājā = king; samyataḥ = tied up; sata pāshena = by fetters of truth; abravīt = said; rudan = weeping; tam = to that; priyam putram = beloved son.

Prompted secretly by kaikeyi, the distressed king, tied up by fetters of truth, said weeping to that beloved son.

Sloka 31

śreyase vṛddhaye tāta punar āgamanāya ca |
gaccasva ariṣṭam avyagraḥ panthānam akutaḥ bhayam || 2-34-31

31. tāta = Oh; darling! gachchhasva = proceed; shreyase = for welfare; vṛiddhaye = for advancement; punaḥāgamanāyacha = and for coming again; panthānam = let your path; arishhṭam = be auspicious; avyagram = undistracted; akutobhayam = and fearless from any quarter.

"Oh, darling! Proceed for welfare, for advancement and for coming again. Let your path of travel be auspicious, undistracted and fearless from any quarter."

Sloka 32

na hi satyātmanastāta dharmābhimanasastava |
vinivarta yituṃ buddhi śakyate raghunandana || 2-34-32

32. tāta = Oh; darling! raghunandana = Oh; Rama; the seion of Raghu! satyātmanaḥ = your mind being given to righteousness; tava = your; buddhiḥ = decision; na shakya hi = cannot be; vinivartayitum = reversed.

"Oh, darling, the seion of Raghu! Truthful as you are by nature and your mind being given to righteousness, your decision cannot be reversed."

Sloka 33

adya tu idānīm rajanīm putra mā gacca sarvathā |
mātaram mām ca sampaśyan vasa imām adya śarvarīm || 2-34-33

33. putra = Oh; son! adya = today; idānīm = now; rajanīm = night; mā gachchha = do not go; sarvathā = by all means; ekāha darshaenāpi = even by seeing for one day; aham = I; charāmitāvat = may live; sādhu = happily.

"Oh, son! Do not leave in any case today, now at night time. Even by seeing you for one day, I may live happily."

Sloka 34

mātaraṃ mām ca sampaśyan vasemāmadya śarvarīm |
tarpitaḥ sarvakāmaistvam svaḥ kāle sādhayiṣyasi || 2-34-34

34. sampashyan = looking at mātaram = your mother; māmcha = and myself; vasa = stay; imām = this; sharvarīm = might; adya = today; tarpitaḥ = satiated; sarva kāmaiḥ = by all desired objecs; tvam = you; sādhayishhyasi = may leave; shvaḥ = tommorrow; kāle = at dawn.

"Looking at your mother and myself, stay at least for this night today. Satiated by all the desired objects, you may leave tomorrow at dawn."

Sloka 35

duṣkaram kriyate putra sarvathā rāghava tayā |
matpriyārtham priyāṃstyaktvā yadyāsi vijanam vanam || 2-34-35

35. putra = Oh; son; rāghava = Rama! dushhkaram = an incorrigible thing; sarvathā = in every respect; kriyate = is being done; tvayā = by you; yat = since; mat priyārtham = for my pleasure; yāsi = you are going; vanam = to the forest; vijanam = a solitary place; tyaktvā = leaving; priyān = beloved ones.

"Oh Rama, my son! An incorrigible thing is being done by you since for my pleasure you are going to the forest a solitary place, leaving beloved ones."

Sloka 36

na caitanme priyam putra śape satyena rāghava |
channayā chalitastvasnu struyā channāgnikalpayā || 2-34-36

36. putra = Oh; son; rāghava = Rama! etat = this; nacha = is not; priyam = agreeable; me = to me; shape = I swear; satyena = by my faith; asmi = I was; chhalitaḥ = cheated; striyā = by the woman; chhannayā = who had concealed intentions; chhannāgni kalpayā = resembled fire covered with ashes.

"Oh Rama my son! Your exile is not agreeable to me. I was cheated by Kaikeyi who had concealed intentions and resembled fire with ashes."

Sloka 37

pañcanā yā tu labdhā me tāṃ tvam nistartumicchasi |
anayā vṛttasādinyā kaikeyyā.abhipracoditaḥ || 2-34-37

37. athiprachoditaḥ = incited by; anayā = this; kaikeyyā = Kaikeyi; vṛittasādinyā = who has destroyed established customs; tvam = you; ichchhasi = wish; mistartum = to redeem; me = my; yāvaṇchanā = which deceit; tām = that; labdhā = was derived.

Incited by Kaikeyi who has destroyed established customs, you wish to redeem my deceit, derived by me from her.

Sloka 38

na caitadāścaryatamam yattajjyeṣṭhassuto mama |
apānṛtakatham putra pitaram kartumicchsi || 2-34-38

38. putra = Oh; son! jyeshhṭaḥ sutaḥ = as eldest son; mama = of min; ichhasi iti yat = that which you wish; kartum = to make; pitaram = your father; apānṛitakatham = as on ewho does not have false promises; tat etat = this; na = is not āshcharyatamam = a great surprise.

"It is not a great surprise, my son, that you my eldest son should seek to make your father as one who does not have false promises."

Sloka 39

atha rāmaḥ tathā śrutvā pitur ārtasya bhāṣitam |
lakṣmaṇena saha bhrātrā dīno vacanam abravīt || 2-34-39

39. atha = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; bhrātā lakshhmaṇena saha = along with his brother lakshman; shrutvā = hearing; bhāshhitam = the words; pituḥ = of his father; tathā = thus; ārtasya = depressed; dīnaḥ = was upset; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words.

After hearing these words of their father who was depressed, Rama and Lakshmana were upset. Then Rama spoke these words.

Sloka 40

prāpsyāmi yān adya guṇān ko me śvastān pradāsyati |
apakramaṇam eva ataḥ sarva kāmaiḥ aham vṛṇe || 2-34-40

40. yān = which; guṇām = wordly pleasures prāpsyāmi = I can get; adya = today; tān = those; kaḥ = who; pradāsyati = will offer; me = to me; shvaḥ = tomorrow? ataḥ = therfore; aham = I; vṛiṇe = desire; apakramaṇameva = only to get away; sarvaka = in lien of all enjoyments.

Even if these worldly pleasures are obtained today, who will offer those things to me tomorrow? Therefore I desire only to get away in lieu of all these enjoyments.

Sloka 41

iyam sarāṣṭrā sajanā dhana dhānya samākulā |
mayā visṛṣṭā vasudhā bharatāya pradīyatām || 2-34-41

41. iyam = vasudhā = this earth; visṛishhṭā = being relinquished; mayā = by me; sa rāshhṭrā = which includes various provinces; sajanā = filled with people; dhana dhānya samākulā = inbued with riches and foodgrains; pradīyatām = may be given; bharatāya = to Bharata.

"This earth being relinquished by me; which included various provinces, filled with people, imbued with riches and food grains, may be given to Bharata."

Sloka 42

vanavāsakṛtā buddhirna ca me.adya caliṣyati |
yastuṣṭena varo dattaḥ kaikeyyai varada tvayā || 2-34-42
dīyatām nikhilenaiva satyastvam bhava pārthiva |

42. adya = now; nachalishhayati = I cannot budge; me buddhiḥ = from my decision; vanavāsa kṛitā = made about my stay in the forest; pārthiva = Oh; king; varada = the bestower of boons! tvayā = by you; tushhṭena = who were pleased; dattaḥ = to give; yaḥ varaḥ = which boon; kaikeyyai = to Kaikeyi; dīyatām = let it be given; nikhilenaina = completely.

"I cannot budge now from my decision made about my stay in the forest. Oh king the bestower of boons! You were pleased to give a boon to Kaikeyi and let it be fulfilled completely. Be you a truthful man."

Sloka 43

aham nideśam bhavato yathoktamanupālayan || 2-34-43
caturdaśa samā vatsye vane vanacaraiḥ saha |

43. aham = I; yathoktam = as promised; anupālayan = fulfilling; bhavata = your; nidesham = command; vatsye = shall live; vane = in the forest; vanacharaiḥ saha = along with forest-dewellrs; chaturdasha = for fourteen; samāḥ = years.

"I , as promised, fulfilling your command, shall live in the forest along with forest-dwellers for fourteen years."

Sloka 44

mā vimarśo vasumatī bharatāya pradīyatām || 2-34-44
na hi me kāmkṣitam rājyam sukhamātmani vā priyam |
yathā nideśam kartum vai tavaiva raghunandhana || 2-34-45

44;45. mā = Oh; do not have; vimarshaḥ = hesitation; vasumatī pradīyatām = let the kingdom be given; bharatāya = to Bharata; raghunandana = Oh; king! me = to me; rājyam = either kingdom; sukhamvā = or happiness; nahi = indeed is not; kāṇkshhitam = desired; ātmano = by nature; kartum eva = only doing; tava = your; yathānidesham = command as instructed; priyam vai = is dear to me.

"Oh, king! Do not have any hesitation in offering the kingdom to Bharata. Neither kingdom nor happiness indeed is desired by my nature. It is dear to me only to do your command as directed."

Sloka 46

apagaccatu te duhkham mā bhūr bāṣpa pariplutaḥ |
na hi kṣubhyati durdharṣaḥ samudraḥ saritām patiḥ || 2-34-46

46. te duḥkham apagachchhatu = let your sorrow disappear! mā bhūḥ = do not be; bhāshhpa pariplutaḥ = overwheled with tears; samudraḥ = the ocean; patiḥ = the lord; saritām = of rivers; durdharshhaḥ = which is difficult to assail; na kshhubhyatihi = is not indeed agitated.

"Let your sorrow disappear! Do not be overwhelmed with tears. The ocean, the lord of rivers which is difficult to assail, indeed does not get agitated."

Sloka 47

na eva aham rājyam iccāmi na sukham na ca maithilīm |
tvām aham satyam iccāmi na anṛtam puruṣa ṛṣabha || 2-34-47

47. aham = I; naiva ichchhāmi = do not even desire; rājyam = kingdom; na = nor; sukham = happiness; na = nor; maithilīmcha = ṣeetha too; naiva = nor even; sarvān = all; imān = these; kāmān = enjoyments; naiva = nor; jīvitam = life.

"I do not crave for kingdom or happiness nor again for Seetha nor even all these enjoyments nor for heaven nor even for life."

Sloka 48

tvāmaham satyamicchāmi nānṛtam puruṣarṣabha |
pratyakṣam tava satyena sukṛtena ca te śape || 2-34-48

48. purushharshhabha = Oh; jewel among men! ahma = I; ichchhami = wish; tvām = you; satyam = to be a truthful man; na = not; anṛitam = a fallaceous man; tava = In you; pratyakshham = presence; satyena = by truth; sukṛitena = and by virtue; shape = I swear; te = to you.

"Oh, Jewel among men! I wish you to be a truthful mean, not a fallacious man. I swear to you in your presence by truth and by virtue."

Sloka 49

na ca śakhyam mayā tāta sthātum kṣaṇamapi prabho |
sa śokam dhrārayasvemam na hi me.asti viparyayaḥ || 2-34-49

49. tāta = Oh; father! prabho = Oh; lord! mayā = by me; nashakyam = it is not possible; sthātum = to stay; khhaṇamapi = even for a moment; saḥ = you as such; dhārayasva = should contian; evam = this; shokam = grief; nāstihi = there is indeed no; viparyayaḥ = going back; me = for me.

"It is not possible for me, Oh father, to stay on even for a moment. Therefore, contain this grief, Oh Lord, for there is no going back upon my word."

Sloka 50

arthito hyasmi kaikeyyā vanam gaccheti rāghava |
mayā coktaṃ prajāmīti tatsatyamanupālaye || 2-34-50

50. rāghava = Oh; Dasaratha the seion of Raghu! asmihi = I was indeed; arthitaḥ = asked; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; iti = as follows: gachchha = go; vanam = to the forest." uktamcha = It was also replied; mayā = by me; prajāmi iti = that I would go; anupālaye = I shall redeem; tat = that; satyam = pledge.

"Oh Dasaratha the seion of Raghu! I was indeed asked by Kaikeyi to go to the forest. It was also replied by me that I would go. I shall redeem that pledge."

Sloka 51

mā cotkaṇthāṃ kṛthā deva vane raṃsyāmahe vayam |
praśāntahariṇākīrṇe nānāśakuninādite || 2-34-51

51. deva = Oh; king! mākṛithāḥ = do not feel; utkaṇṭhām = anxious; vayam = we; ramsyāmahe = shall sport; vane = in the forest; prashānta hariṇākīrṇe = flocked with peaceful deers; nānāshakuninādite = rendered noisy by birds of various types.

"Oh, king! Do not feel anxious. We shall sport in the forest, flocked with peaceful deer and rendered noisy by birds of various types."

Sloka 52

pitā hi daivatam tāta devatānāmapi smṛtam |
tasmāddaivatamityeva kariṣyāmi piturvacaḥ || 2-34-52

52. tat = Oh; father! smṛitamhi = it was said indeed; pitā = father; daivatam = is the god; devatānāmapi = even for celestials; tasmāt = therefore; karishhyāmi = I shall do; pituḥ = father's; vachaḥ = word; daivatam ityeva = considering it s divine.

"Oh, father! It was indeed said that even for celestials, father is the god. Therefore, I shall carry out the father's word, considering it as divine."

Sloka 53

caturdhaśasu varṣeṣu gateṣu narasattama |
punardrakṣyasi mām prāptam santāpo.ayam vimucyatām || 2-34-53

53. narasattama = Oh; best among men! prāptam = coming; gateshhu = after passing away; chaturdasha = fourteen; varshheshhu = years; drakshhyasi = you will see; mām = me; ayam samtāpaḥ vimuchyatām = let this grief be given up.

"Oh father, the best among men! You will see me, when I come back after elapsing fourteen years. Let this grief be given up."

Sloka 54

yena saṃstambhanīyo.ayam sarvo bāṣpagaldo janaḥ |
sa tvam puruṣaśārdūla kimartham vikriyām gataḥ || 2-34-54

54. purushha shārdūla = Oh; tiger among men! saraḥ = all; ayam janaḥ = these people; bhāshhpagaLaḥ = whoare bathed with tears; samstambhanīyaḥ = are to be consoled by you; saḥ tva = such of you; kimartham = why; gataḥ = got; vikriyām = perturbation?

"Oh, tiger among men! Why have you got perturbed-you by whom all these people bathed in tears, ought to be consoled?"

Sloka 55

puram ca rāṣṭram ca mahī ca kevalā |
mayā nisṛṣṭā bharatāya dīyatām |
aham nideśam bhavataḥ anupālayan |
vanam gamiṣyāmi cirāya sevitum || 2-34-55

55. puramcha = let this city; rāshhṭramcha = province; kevalā mahīcha = and whole of this earth; nishṛishhṭā = left; mayā = by me; dīyatām = be given; bharatāya = to Bharata; aham = I; anupālayan = following; bhavataḥ = your; nidesham = instruction; gamishhyāmi = shall proceed; vanam = to the forest; sevitum = to dwell in it; chirāya = for long.

"Let this city, province and whole of this earth left by me, be given to Bharata. I for one following your instructions, shall proceed to the forest to dwell in it for long."

Sloka 56

mayā nisṛṣṭām bharataḥ mahīm imām |
saśaila khaṇḍām sapurām sakānanām |
śivām susīmām anuśāstu kevalam |
tvayā yad uktam nṛpate yathā astu tat || 2-34-56

56. nṛipate = Oh; king! imāma mahīm = let this earth; sashaila shhaṇdām = with multitude mountains; sapurām = comprising of cities; sakānanām = with their gardens; nisṛishhṭām = relinquished; mayā = by me; anushāstu = be ruled over; bharataḥ kevalam = barely by Bharata; susīmām = within bounds; shirām = of well being; yat = that which; uktam = is told; tvayā = by you; tat = let it; astu = be so; tathā = thus.

"Oh, king! Let this earth with multitude of mountains, comprising of cities with their gardens relinquished by me, be ruled over barely by Bharata, within the bounds of righteousness. Let the word of honour given by you(to Kaikeyi) come true."

Sloka 57

na me tathā pārthiva dhīyate mano |
mahatsu kāmeṣu na ca ātmanaḥ priye |
yathā nideśe tava śiṣṭa sammate |
vyapaitu duhkham tava mat kṛte anagha || 2-34-57

57. pārthiva = Oh; king; anagha = the faultlessman! yathā = how; memanaḥ = my mind; dhīyate = is shanding firmly; tava nirdeshe = to your command; shishhta sammate = approved by the wise; tathā = in that manner; na = it is not; mahatsu = on haughty; kāmeshhu = enjoyments; na = it is not; ātanaḥ = on my own; priyeḥ = pleasures; matkṛite = for my sake; vyapaitu duḥkham = let your agony disappear.

"Oh, the faultless king! ṃy mind is not directed on haughty physical enjoyments or sensuous pleasures which is approved by the wise. Let your agony disappear, for my sake."

Sloka 58

tat adya na eva anagha rājyam avyayam |
na sarva kāmān na sukham na maithilīm |
na jīvitam tvām anṛtena yojayan |
vṛṇīya satyam vratam astu te tathā || 2-34-58

58. anagha = Oh the sinless king! tat = therefore; adya = now; yojayan = connecting; tvām = you; anṛitena = with untruth; naiva vṛitena = I just cannot yearn; rājyam = for kingdom; na = nor desirous; sarva kāmān = of various enjoyment; na = nor; sukham = for happiness; na = now; maithilīm = ṣeetha; na = nor; jīvitam = life; te = (let) your; vratam = voe; tathā = thus; astu = become; satyam = true.

Oh, the sinless king! Therefore, now associating you with untruth, I just do not desire for kingdom, nor all the sensual enjoyments, nor happiness nor existence nor even Seetha. Let your pledge(given to Kaikeyi) prove true."

Sloka 59

phalāni mūlāni ca bhakṣayan vane |
girīmḥ ca paśyan saritaḥ sarāṃsi ca |
vanam praviśya eva vicitra pādapam |
sukhī bhaviṣyāmi tava astu nirvṛtiḥ || 2-34-59

59. bhavishhyāmi = I shall be; sukhī = happy; pravishyaiva = entering; vanam = the forest; vichitra pādapam = filled with; bhakshhayan = eating; phalāni = fruits; mūlanicha = and tubers; pushyan = and lakes; vane = in the forest; astu = let there be; nirvṛitiḥ = satisfaction; tava = to you.

"I shall be happy, entering the forest filled with wonderful trees, eating fruits and tubers as well as seeing mountains rivers and lakes in the forest. Let there be satisfaction for you.

Sloka 60

evam sa rājā vyasanābhipannaḥ |
śokena duḥkhena ca tāmyamānaḥ |
āliṅgya putram suvinaṣṭasamjño |
moham gato naiva ciceśṭa kiṃcit || 2-34-60

60. āliṇgya = embracing; putra = his son; saḥ rājā = that king Dasaratha; evam = who had thus; vyasanābhipannaḥ = fallen on evil days; tāmyamānaḥ = was tormented; shokena = with grief; duḥkhena = and distress; mahomgataḥ = got fainted; suvinashhṭa samJṇaḥ = fully losing consciousness; naiva chicheshhṭa = not moving; kimchit = even a little.

Embracing his son, that king Dasaratha, who had thus fallen on evil days, was tormented with grief and distress, got fainted, fully losing his consciousness and not moving even a little.

Sloka 61

devyastataḥ samruruduḥ sametā |
stām varjayitvā naradevapatnīm |
rudan sumantro.api jagāma mūrchām |
hā hā kṛtam tatra babhūva sarvam || 2-34-61

61. tataḥ = then; varjayitvā = leaving out; naradevapatnīm = Kaikeyi the wife of king Dasaratha; devyaḥ = the queens; sametāḥ = assembled there; samruduḥ = began to cry; sumantro.api = ṣumantra also; jagāma = got; mūrchhām = the faint; rudan = while weeping; sarvam = everything; tatra = there; babhūva = became; hāhākṛitam = loud lamenting.

Then, all the queens assembled there except Kaikeyi began to cry. Sumantra also fell into a swoon, while weeping. Everything there became loud lamenting.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe catur triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 34th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda in glorious Ramayana, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
