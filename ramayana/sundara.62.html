Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 62

Introduction

The monkeys enter ṃadhuvana and partake the honey in plenty, as permitted by Hanuma and Angada. Singing and dancing as if in a drunken state, the monkeys were obstructed by those guarding the grove. The terrific monkeys warded off the guards, nay, they even dragged them by their knees and tossed them up in the air. The guards then went to Dadhimukha, the chief of the garden-guards and complained him of the matter. Dadhimukha along with his followers appears on the scene and invade the monkeys with trees and rocks. Angada thrashed Dadhimukha with his arms and made him fall on the ground. Dadhimukha along with his followers then proceed to Kishkindha to report the matter to him. Dadhimukha bows down and touches Sugreeva's feet as a mark of salutation.


Sloka 1

tān uvāca hari śreṣṭho hanūmān vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |
avyagra manaso yūyam madhu sevata vānarāḥ || 5-62-1
ahamāvārayiṣyāmi yuṣmākam paripanthinaḥ |

1. hanumān = ḥanuma; vānararṣabhaḥ = like a bull among the monkeys; harishreṣṭhaḥ and the foremost among them; uvācha = spoke; tān = to them (as follows); vānarāḥ = O monkeys!; yūyam = you; sevata = cherish; madhu = the honey; avyagramanasaḥ = with a cool mind; aham = I; āvārayiṣyāmi = will keep off; yuṣmākam = your; paripanthinaḥ = opposer who is standing in your way.

ḥanuma, like a bull among the monkeys and the foremost among them spoke to them as follows: "cherish the honey with a cool mind, O monkeys! I will keep off your opposer who is standing in your way."

Sloka 2

śrutvā hanumato vākyam harīṇām pravaro angadaḥ || 5-62-2
pratyuvāca prasanna ātmā pibantu harayo madhu |

2. shrutvā = hearing; vākyam = the words; hanumataḥ = of ḥanuma; prasannātmā = the gracious minded; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; pratyuvācha = repeated the following words; harayaḥ = (Let) the monkeys; pibantu = drink; madhu = the honey.

ḥearing the words of ḥanuma, the gracious minded Angada the principal among the monkeys repeated the words: "Let the monkeys drink the honey."

Sloka 3

avaśyam kṛta kāryasya vākyam hanumato mayā || 5-62-3
akāryam api kartavyam kim anga punaḥ īdṛśam |

3. vākyam = the words; akāryam api = though improper; kṛita kāryasya = who has accomplished his work; to be obliged; avashyam = certainly; mayā = by me; kim aṇ^ga = wherefore then; īdṛisham punaḥ = moreover; on such an occasion?

"ṭhe words, though improper of ḥanuma who had accomplished his task, are to be obliged certainly by me. Wherefore then, moreover, on such an occasion?"

Sloka 4

andagasya mukhāt śrutvā vacanam vānara ṛṣabhāḥ || 5-62-4
sādhu sādhv iti samhṛṣṭā vānarāḥ pratyapūjayan |

4. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; mukhāt = coming from the mouth; aṇ^gadasya = of Angada; vānarāḥ = those monkeys; vānarṣhabhāḥ = who were like bulls among the monkeys; samhṛiṣṭāḥ = were delighted; pratya pūjayan = and answered with reverence; sādhu sādhv iti = saying 'good; good!'

ḥearing the words coming from the mouth of Angada, those monkeys who were like bulls among the monkeys were delighted and answered with reverence, saying 'good, good!'

Sloka 5

pūjayitvā angadam sarve vānarā vānara ṛṣabham || 5-62-5
jagmuḥ madhu vanam yatra nadī vega iva drutam |

5. pūjayitvā = reverencing; aṇ^gadam = Angada; vānarā ṛiṣhabham = the foremost among the monkeys; sarve vānarāḥ = all the monkeys; jagmuḥ = went; yatra = to the place where; madhu vanam = ṃadhuvana; the grove abounding in honey was there; nadī vegaḥ iva = like rush of a river; drumam = towards a tree.

Reverencing Angada the foremost among the monkeys, all the monkeys rushed towards ṃadhuvana, the grove abounding in honey, like the rush of a river towards a tree.

Sloka 6

te prahṛṣṭā madhu vanam pālān ākramya vīryataḥ || 5-62-6
atisargāc ca paṭavo dṛṣṭvā śrutvā ca maithilīm |
papuḥ sarve madhu tadā rasavatphalamādaduḥ || 5-62-7

6; 7. dṛiṣhṭvā = (because ḥanuma) had seen ṣeetha; shrutvā = and others had heard of her; atisargācha cha = and because of grant of permission from Angada; te sarve paṭavaḥ = all those gifted monkeys; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = having entered; madhuvanam = ṃadhuvana the grove; ākramya pālān = and overpowering themselves over the guards; vīryataḥ = with their prowess; vapuḥ = drank; madhu = the honey; tadā = then; ādaduḥ = took; rasavat phalam = juicy fruits.

Because ḥanuma had seen ṣeetha and others had heard of her and because of the grant of permission from Angada, all those gifted monkeys entered ṃadhuvana the grove, overpowered the guards with their prowess, drank honey and the partook juicy fruits there.

Sloka 8

utpatya ca tataḥ sarve vana pālān samāgatāḥ |
tāḍayanti sma śataśaḥ saktān madhu vane tadā || 5-62-8

8. tadā = then; sarve = all those monkeys; utpatya = having jumped; vanapālān = on the guards of the grove; samāgatān = who came; saktān = together; shatashaḥ = in hundreds; tataḥ = and then; tāDayanti = banged them.

ṭhen, all those monkeys jumped on the guards of the grove, who came there in hundreds together and banged them.

Sloka 9

madhūni droṇa mātrāṇi bahubhiḥ parigṛhya te |
pibanti sahitāḥ sarve bhakṣayanti tathā apare || 5-62-9

9. sarve = all those monkeys; sahitāḥ = together; parigṛihya = taking; drone mātrāṇi* madhūni = vessels of honey; bāhubhiḥ = in their arms; pibanti = drank them; apare = some others; nighnanti sma = banged (those who obstructed them).

All those monkeys together took vessels of honey in their arms and drank them. ṣome others banged those who obstructed them.

Drona= A wooden vessel containing 1024 ṃushtis/fists of honey.

Sloka 10

kecit pītvā apavidhyanti madhūni madhu pingalāḥ |
madhu ucciṣṭena kecic ca jaghnuḥ anyonyam utkaṭāḥ || 5-62-10

10. kechit = some monkeys; madhu piṇ^galāḥ = with their skin-colour; as yellow as honey; pītvā = having drunk; madhūni = vessels of honey; pravidhyanti = were throwing (some honey) away; kechichcha = some others; madhu uchchhiṣhṭhena = with remnant of honey after drinking; jagmuḥ = whipped up; anyonyam = one another; utkaṭāḥ = with excessive intoxication.

ṣome monkeys, with their skin-colour as yellow as honey, after drinking vessels of honey, also threw some honey away. Some others, with the remnant of honey in their arms, after drinking, whipped up one another with their excessive intoxication.

Sloka 11

apare vṛkṣa mūleṣu śākhām gṛhya vyavasthitaḥ |
atyartham ca mada glānāḥ parṇāni āstīrya śerate || 5-62-11

11. apare = some others; gṛihya = seizing; shākhām = a branch of a tree; vyavasthitāḥ = settled; vṛikṣa mūle = at the foot of a tree; atyartham mada glānāḥ = those who were highly intoxicated; sherate = laid down; āstīrṇa parṇāni = spreading leaves.

ṣome others, seizing a branch of a tree, stood rooted at the foot of a tree. ṣome, who were highly intoxicated, laid down spreading leaves on the ground.

Sloka 12

unmatta bhūtāḥ plavagā madhu mattāḥ ca hṛṣṭavat |
kṣipanti api tathā anyonyam skhalanti api tathā apare || 5-62-12

12. plavagāḥ = monkeys; madhu mattāḥ = intoxicated with the drinking of honey; unmatta bhūtāḥ = became inebriated; kṣhipanti = and were pushing; anyonyam = one another; hṛiṣhṭavat = cheerfully; tathā = and; apare = some others; skhalanti cha = began to stumble.

ṭhose monkeys, who were intoxicated by drinking the honey, became inebriated and started pushing one another cheerfully and some others began to stumble.

Sloka 13

kecit kṣveḍān prakurvanti kecit kūjanti hṛṣṭavat |
harayo madhunā mattāḥ kecit suptā mahī tale || 5-62-13

13. kechit = some; prakurvanti = were making; kṣhveDān = lion's roars; kechit = some; hṛiṣhṭavat = with joy; kūjanti = whistled like birds; kechit harayaḥ = some; madhunā = with honey; suptāḥ = slept; mahī tale = on the floor.

ṣome were roaring like lions. ṣome, with joy, whistled like birds. ṣome monkeys, inebriated as they were by drinking honey, simply slept on the floor.

Sloka 14

kṛtvā kecirdhasantyanye kecitkurvanti cetarat |
kṛtvā kecidvadantyanye kecidbudhyanti cetarat || 5-62-14

14. kṛitvā = doing something or other; kechit = some; hasanti = were laughing; anye kechit = some others; kurvanti = were doing; itarat = something else; kechit = some; vadanti = were telling; kṛitvā = what they were doing; anye kechit = some others; budhyanti = were understanding; itarat = something else.

Doing something or other, some were laughing. ṣome others were doing something else. Some were telling what they were doing, while some others were understanding some things else.

Sloka 15

ye api atra madhu pālāḥ syuḥ preṣyā dadhi mukhasya tu |
te api taiḥ vānaraiḥ bhīmaiḥ pratiṣiddhā diśo gatāḥ || 5-62-15

15. madhu pālāḥ = the honey-keepers; ye = who; syuḥ = were there; preṣhyāḥ = as messengers; dadhimukhasya = of Dadhimukha; te.api = they also; atra = in that ṃadhuvana; pratiṣhiddhāḥ = were warded off; bhīmaiḥ vānaraiḥ = by the terrific monkeys; gatāḥ = and went; dishaḥ = towards different directions.

ṭhe honey-keepers, who were there as messengers of Dadhimukha in that ṃadhuvana, were warded off by the terrific monkeys and they fled to different directions.

Sloka 16

jānubhiḥ ca prakṛṣṭāḥ ca deva mārgam ca darśitāḥ |
abruvan parama udvignā gatvā dadhi mukham vacaḥ || 5-62-16

16. prakṛiṣṭāḥ = dragged; jānubhiḥ = by their knees; pradarshitāḥ = those honey-keepers who were shown; deva mārgam = the air; parama udvignāḥ = were very much depressed; gatvā = and having gone; dadhimukham = to Dadhimukha; abruvan vachaḥ = spoke the (following) words.

Dragged by their knees and tossed up in the air by the monkeys, those honey-keepers were very much depressed, went to Dadhimukha and complained as follows:

Sloka 17

hanūmatā datta varaiḥ hatam madhu vanam balāt |
vayam ca jānubhiḥ kṛṣṭā deva mārgam ca darśitāḥ || 5-62-17

17. data varaiḥ hanūmatā = by the monkeys; as they were granted a boon by ḥanuma; madhu vanam = ṃadhuvana; hatam = was destroyed; balāt = violently; vayamcha = we; kṛiṣhṭāḥ = were dragged; jānūbhiḥ = by our knees; darshitāḥ = and were shown; deva mārgam = the air.

"ṭhe monkeys, as permitted by ḥanuma, violently destroyed ṃadhuvana. We were dragged by our knees and further tossed up in the air."

Sloka 18

tato dadhi mukhaḥ kruddho vanapaḥ tatra vānaraḥ |
hatam madhu vanam śrutvā sāntvayāmāsa tān harīn || 5-62-18

18. shrutvā = hearing; madhu vanam = (that) ṃadhuvana; hatam = was damaged; dadhimukhaḥ = Dadhimukha; vanapaḥ = the grove-protector; tatra = there; kruddhaḥ = was enraged; tataḥ = and thereafter; sāntvayāmāsa = consoled; tān harīn = those monkeys.

ḥearing that ṃadhuvana was damaged, Dadhimukha, the grove-protector there was enraged and thereafter consoled those monkeys (as follows):

Sloka 19

iha āgacchata gacchāmo vānarān atidarpitān |
balena āvārayiṣyāmo madhu bhakṣayato vayam || 5-62-19

19. āgachchhata iha = come here; gachchhāma = let us go; vārayiṣhyāmaḥ = let us prevent; balena = by our force; vānarān = the monkeys; baladarpitān = who are proud of their strength; bhakṣhayataḥ = and who are consuming; madhu = the honey.

"Come here let us go there and prevent by our force, the monkeys who are arrogant of their strength and are consuming the honey."

Sloka 20

śrutvā dadhi mukhasya idam vacanam vānara ṛṣabhāḥ |
punaḥ vīrā madhu vanam tena eva sahitā yayuḥ || 5-62-20

20. shrutvā = hearing; idam = these; vachanam = words; dadhimukhasya = of Dadhimukha; vānara ṛiṣhabhāḥ = the foremost among the monkeys; vīrāḥ = who display heroism; sahasā = quickly; yayaḥ = went; punaḥ = again; madhu vanam = to ṃadhuvana; tena eva = along with that Dadhimukha.

ḥearing these words of Dadhimukha, the foremost of those monkeys who displayed their heroism, quickly went again to madhuvana along with him.

Sloka 21

madhye ca eṣām dadhi mukhaḥ pragṛhya sumahātarum |
samabhyadhāvat vegenā te ca sarve plavamgamāḥ || 5-62-21

21. pragṛihya = grasping; tarum = a tree; dadhimukhaḥ = Dadhimukha; samabhyadhāvat = ran; vegenā = with speed; eṣhām madhye = towards the middle of those monkeys; sarve = all; te = those; plavaṇ^gamāḥ cha = monkey-followers too (ran).

Grasping a tree, Dadhimukha speedily took up his position in the middle of those monkeys in ṃadhuvana. All his follower-monkeys also ran with him.

Sloka 22

te śilāḥ pādapān ca api pāṣāṇān ca api vānarāḥ |
gṛhītvā abhyāgaman kruddhā yatra te kapi kunjarāḥ || 5-62-22

22. te kruddhāḥ vānarāḥ = those enraged monkeys; gṛihītvā = taking; shilāḥ = rocks; pādapan cha api = trees; parvatāmshchāpi = and even mountains; abhyāgaman = went; yatra = were; te = those; kapi kuṇjarāḥ = foremost of monkeys (were there).

ṭhose enraged monkeys, taking rocks, trees and even mountains, went to the place wehre those foremost of monkeys were there.

Sloka 23

te svāmi vacanam vīrā hṛdayeṣv avasajya tat |
tvarayā hi abhyadhāvanta sāla tāla śilā āyudhāḥ || 5-62-23

23. te = those; vīrāḥ = heroic monkeys; avasajya = keeping; hṛidayeṣhu tat svāmi vachanam = the words of their master in mind; tvarayā = briskly; abhyadhāvanta = ran; sāla tāla shilā āyudhāḥ = with ṣala trees; palm trees and rocks as their weapon.

ṭhose heroic monkeys, keeping the words of their master in mind, briskly ran with ṣala trees, palm trees and rocks as their weapons.

Sloka 24

vṛkṣasthān ca talasthān ca vānarān bala darpitān |
abhyakrāmanta te vīrāḥ pālāḥ tatra sahasraśaḥ || 5-62-24

24. tat = then; vīrāḥ = the valiant; pālāḥ = guards of the grove; sahasrashaḥ = running; in thousands; abhyakrāman = invaded; vānarān = the monkeys; vṛiṣasthān cha = who were on trees; talasthān cha = at the foot of the trees; bala darpitān = and who wee arrogant of their strength.

ṭhen, the valiant guards of the grove running in thousands invaded the monkeys who were on trees, at the foot of the trees and who were arrogant of their strength.

Sloka 25

atha dṛṣṭvā dadhi mukham kruddham vānara pumgavāḥ |
abhyadhāvanta vegena hanūmat pramukhāḥ tadā || 5-62-25

25. atha = thereupon; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; dadhimukham = Dadhimukha; kruddham = enraged; hanumat pramukhāḥ = ḥanuma eminent monkeys; tadā = then; abhyadhāvanta = ran towards him; vegena = with speed.

ṣeeing Dadhimukha coming with anger, ḥanuma along with eminent monkeys ran towards him with speed.

Sloka 26

tam savṛkṣam mahābāhum āpatantam mahābalam |
āryakam prāharat tatra bāhubhyām kupito angadaḥ || 5-62-26

26. kupitaḥ = the enraged; aṇgadaḥ = Angada; tatra = there; prāharat = struck; bāhubhyām = with his arms; tam = that Dadhimukha; āryakam = the maternal uncle of his father; mahābalam = who was endowed with extraordinary night; mahābāhum = having long arms; āpatantam = and rushing with speed; savṛikṣham = with a tree in hand.

ṭhe enraged Angada struck there with his arms, that long-armed Dadhimukha the maternal uncle his father, who was endowed with extraordinary might and who was rushing with speed with a tree in his hand.

Sloka 27

mada andhaḥ a na veda enam āryako ayam mama iti saḥ |
atha enam niṣpipeṣa āśu vegavat vasudhā tale || 5-62-27

27. mada andhaḥ cha = Blinded with arrogance; saḥ = that Angada; na veda = could not remember; enam = that being; iti = as; ayam = he (who was) mama āryakaḥ = the maternal uncle of is father; atha = then; niṣhpipeṣha = (he) crushed; enam = him; āshu = quickly; vegavat = and with speed; vasudhātale = on the ground.

Blinded with arrogance, that Angada did not show any mercy to him on the score of his being the maternal uncle of his father and caused him at once to fall down and began to crush him against the ground.

Sloka 28

sa bhagna bāhuḥ vimukho vihvalaḥ śoṇita ukṣitaḥ |
mumoha sahasā vīro muhūrtam kapi kunjaraḥ || 5-62-28

28. saḥ vīraḥ = that valiant; kapi kuṇ^ijaraḥ = elephant among the monkeys; vihvalaḥ = who was afflicted; sabhagna bāhūrubhujaḥ = with his broken arms; thighs and shoulders; shoṇito kṣhitaḥ = and drenched in blood; sahasā = soon; mumoha = lost his consciousness; muhūrtam = for a moment.

ṭhat Dadhimukha, the valiant elephant among the monkeys, who was afflicted with his broken arms, thighs and shoulders, drenched as he was in blood, lost his consciousness for a moment.

Sloka 29

sa samāśvasya sahasā samkruddho rājamātulaḥ |
vānarānvārayāmāsa daṇḍena madhumohitān || 5-62-29

29. saḥ rājamātulaḥ = that Dadhimukha; ṣugreeva's maternal uncle; sahasā = quickly; samāshvasya = recovering his breath; samkruddha = was enraged; vārayāmāsa = resisted; vānarān = those monkeys; madhumohitān = who were infatuated because of drinking of honey; daṇ^Dena = by a reprimand.

ṭhat Dadhimukha, ṣugreeva's maternal uncle, quickly recovering his breath, was enraged and resisted those monkeys, who were highly intoxicated because of drinking of honey, by a proper reprimand.

Sloka 30

sa kathaṃcit vimuktaḥ taiḥ vānaraiḥ vānara ṛṣabhaḥ |
uvāca eka antam āgamya bhṛtyān tān samupāgatān || 5-62-30

30. kathamchit = somehow; vimuktaḥ = released; taiḥ vānaraiḥ = by those monkeys; saḥ vānara ṛiṣhabhaḥ = that foremost among the monkeys; āshritya = took shelter; ekāntam = at a solitary place; uvācham = and spoke; svān = to his; bhṛityān = messengers; samupāgatān = who came there.

ṣomehow released by those monkeys that Dadhimukha the foremost among the monkeys, went to a solitary place and spoke to his messengers who came there (as follows):

Sloka 31

ete tiṣṭhantu gacchāmo bhartā no yatra vānaraḥ |
sugrīvo vipula grīvaḥ saha rāmeṇa tiṣṭhati || 5-62-31

31. ete tiṣhṭhantu = let these monkeys stay here; gachchhāmaḥ = we will go; yatra = to the place where; sugreevaḥ = ṣugreeva; naḥ = our; bhartā = lord; vānaraḥ = the monkey; vipula grīvaḥ = having a thick neck; rāmeṇa saha = along with Rama; tiṣhṭhati = are staying.

"Let these monkeys stay here. We will go to the place where the thick-necked ṣugreeva, our lord of the monkeys stays together with Rama."

Sloka 32

sarvam caiva angade doṣam śrāvayiṣyāmi pārthiva |
amarṣī vacanam śrutvā ghātayiṣyati vānarān || 5-62-32

32. pārthive shrāvayiṣyāmi = I will make the king hear; sarvam = all; doṣam = the misdeed; aṇ^gada = of Angada; shrutvā = hearing; vachanam = my words; vānarān ghātayiṣyati amarṣī = the king; enraged; will get the monkeys killed.

"I will make known to the king, all the misdeed of Angada. ḥearing my words, the enraged king will get the monkeys killed."

Sloka 33

iṣṭam madhu vanam hi etat sugrīvasya mahātmanaḥ |
pitṛ paitāmaham divyam devaiḥ api durāsadam || 5-62-33

33. etat madhuvanam = this ṃadhuvana; iṣṭam hi = is indeed cherished; mahātmanaḥ pārthivasya = by the high-souled king ṣugreeva; pitṛi paitāmaham = and it derived from his father and grand-father; divyam = (It is ) so charming; durāsadam = and is dangerous to be approached; devaiḥ api = even by celestials.

"ṭhis ṃadhuvana is indeed cherished by our high-souled king, ṣugreeva and it derived from his father and grand father. It is so charming, but is dangerous to be approached even by celestials."

Sloka 34

sa vānarān imān sarvān madhu lubdhān gata āyuṣaḥ |
ghātayiṣyati daṇḍena sugrīvaḥ sasuhṛj janān || 5-62-34

34. saḥ sugrīvaḥ = that ṣugreeva; ghatayiṣhyati imān sarvān vānarān sasuhṛij janān = can get all these monkeys together with their companions killed; daṇ^Dena = as a punishment; (those monkeys are) madhulubhān = lustful of honey; gata āyuṣaḥ = and their longevity got reduced.

"ṭhe longevity of these monkeys got reduced, as they were very much lustful of honey. That Sugreeva can get these monkeys together with their companions, killed as a matter of punishment."

Sloka 35

vadhyā hi ete durātmāno nṛpa ājñā paribhāvinaḥ |
amarṣa prabhavo roṣaḥ saphalo no bhaviṣyati | 5-62-35

35. ete = these; durātmanaḥ = evil-minded beings; nṛipa ājJṇā paribhāvinaḥ = who disregarded the royal command; vadhyāḥ hi = are fit to be capitally punished; naḥ roṣhaḥ = our anger; amarṣa prabhavaḥ = springing from our impatience; bhaviṣhyati = will become; saphalaḥ = fruitful.

"ṭhese evil-minded beings, who disregarded the royal command, are fit to be capitally punished. Our wrath, springing from our impatience, will indeed become fruitful."

Sloka 36

evam uktvā dadhi mukho vana pālān mahābalaḥ |
jagāma sahasā utpatya vana pālaiḥ samanvitaḥ || 5-62-36

36. evam = thus; uktvā = speaking; vanapālan = to the garden-guards; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; dadhimukhaḥ = Dadhimukha; sahasā = quickly; jagāma = went; utpatya = springing up all at once; samanvitaḥ = together; vana pālaiḥ = with the garden-guards.

ṭhus speaking to the garden-guards, the mighty Dadhimukha quickly springing up all at once, went (to Kishkindha) together with the garden-guards.

Sloka 37

nimeṣa antara mātreṇa sa hi prāpto vana ālayaḥ |
sahasra aṃśu suto dhīmān sugrīvo yatra vānaraḥ || 5-62-37

37. saḥ vana ālayaḥ = that Dadhimukha = the monkey; prāptaḥ = reached yatra = where; dhīmān sugrīvaḥ vānaraḥ = that intelligent monkey; sugreeva (was there); nimeṣha antara mātreṇa = within an interval of only a moment.

ṭhat Dadhimukha, the monkey, reached the place where that ṣugreeva, the intelligent monkey was there, within an interval of only a moment.

Sloka 38

rāmam ca lakṣmaṇam caiva dṛṣṭvā sugrīvam eva ca |
sama pratiṣṭhām jagatīm ākāśān nipapāta ha || 5-62-38

38. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; rāmam cha = Rama; lakṣhmaṇam chaiva = Lakshmana; sugrīvam eva cha = and ṣugreeva; nipapāta = (Dadhimukha) descended; sama pratiṣhṭhām jagatīm = to a well-leveled ground; ākāshāt = from the sky.

ṣeeing Rama, Lakshmana and ṣugreeva, Dadhimukha descended to a level ground, from the sky.

Sloka 39

sa nipatya mahāvīryaḥ sarvaiḥ taiḥ parivāritaḥ |
hariḥ dadhi mukhaḥ pālaiḥ pālānām parama īśvaraḥ || 5-62-39
sa dīna vadano bhūtvā kṛtvā śirasi ca añjalim |
sugrīvasya śubhau mūrdhnā caraṇau pratyapīḍayat || 5-62-40

39; 40. saḥ = mahāvīryaḥ hariḥ dadhimukhaḥ = that Dadhimukha; the monkey of a great prowess; parivāritaḥ = surrounded; sarvaiḥ taiḥ pālaIḥ = by all those garden-guards; parama īshvāḥ = and the supreme lord; pālānām = of those guards; bhūtvā dīnavadanaḥ = keeping a sad face; kṛitvā aṇ^jalim = making respectful salutation; shirasi = with his head; samnipatya = falling down; paryapīDayat = touched; mūrdhnā = with his head; shubhe charaṇau = the auspicious feet; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva.

ṭhat Dadhimukha of a great prowess, the supreme lord of the guards, surrounded by all those garden-guards keeping a sad face, made a respectful salutation to Sugreeva with his head, by falling down and touching with his head, the auspicious feet of Sugreeva.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe dviṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 62nd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
