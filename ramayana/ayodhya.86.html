Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 86
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Guha reports to Bharata, how Rama and his party broke their journey at Shringaverapura and narrate to him the dialogue that took place between Lakshmana and himself during that night. Guha further tells him how Rama and Lakshmana matted their hair into a thick mass crossed the holy river with Seetha and left for the hermitage of Sage Bharadwaja.

Sloka 1

ācacakṣe atha sadbhāvam lakṣmaṇasya mahātmanaḥ |
bharatāya aprameyāya guho gahana gocaraḥ || 2-86-1

1. atha = thereafter; guhaḥ = Guha; gahanagocharaḥ = the foremost dweller; āchachakshhe = told; bharatāya = Bharata; aprameyāya = who was having incomprehensible qualities; sadbhāvam = about the quality of goodness; lakshhmanasya = of Lakshmana; mahātmanah = the high soled.

Then Guha the foremost dweller reported Bharata, who was having incomprehensible qualities, about the quality of goodness of Lakshmana(as follows):

Sloka 2

tam jāgratam guṇair yuktam vara cāpa iṣu dhāriṇam |
bhrātṛ gupty artham atyantam aham lakṣmaṇam abravam || 2-86-2

2. aham = I; abravam = spoke; tam lakshhmanām = to that Lakshmana; yuktam = endowed with; guṇaiḥ = virtues; shara chāpāsidhāriṇam = who wielded arrows; bow and a sword; jāgratam = and was awake; atyantam = very much; bhrātṛI gupyartham = for the sake guard of his brother.

"I spoke as follows to that Lakshmana, who is endowed with virtues and wielding arrows bow and a sword and who was keeping a vigil for the safe guard of his brother."

Sloka 3

iyam tāta sukhā śayyā tvad artham upakalpitā |
pratyāśvasihi śeṣva asyām sukham rāghava nandana || 2-86-3

3. tāta = O dear broter! Rāghavanandana = a delight to those born in Raghu dynasty! Iyam = ḥere is; sukhā = a comfortable; shayyā = bed; upakalpitā = made ready ; tvadartham = for you; pratyāshvasihi = be comfortable; sheshhva = rest; sukham = peacefully; asyām = on this.

"O, dear brother! Here is a comfortable bed made ready for you. Feel yourself at ease. Rest peacefully on this, O, delight of the Raghu race!"

Sloka 4

ucito ayam janaḥ sarve duhkhānām tvam sukha ucitaḥ |
dharma ātmamḥ tasya gupty artham jāgariṣyāmahe vayam || 2-86-4

4. dharmātman = o; virtuous prince! sarvaḥ = all ayam these; janaḥ = people; uchitaḥ = are habitual; duḥkhānām = to hardships; tvam = you; sukhochitaḥ = are used to comfort; vayam we; jāgarishhyāmahe = shall keep awake; gupyartham = for the safe guard; tasya = of Rama.

"All my people are habituated for hardships, o virtuous prince! You are used to comfort. Hence ,we shall keep awake for the safe guard of Rama."

Sloka 5

na hi rāmāt priyataro mama asti bhuvi kaścana |
mā utsuko bhūr bravīmy etad apy asatyam tava agrataḥ || 2-86-5

5. kaschana nāstihi = ṇone is indeed; priyataraḥ = more dear; mama = to me; rāmāt = than Rama; bhuvi = on earth; atha = moreover; mā bhūḥ = do not become; utsukaḥ = restless; bhavīmi = I am telling; etat = this; satyam = truthful; vākyam = sentence; agrataḥ = before; tava = you.

"None is more dear to me than Rama on this earth. ṃoreover, do not become restless. I am telling this truth in your presence."

Sloka 6

asya prasādād āśaṃse loke asmin sumahad yaśaḥ |
dharma avāptim ca vipulām artha avāptim ca kevalām || 2-86-6

6. prasādāt = by the grace; asya = of that Rama; āshamse = I wish to attain; sumahat = a very great; yashaḥ = glory; asmin loke = in this earth; vipulām = an extensive; dharmāvyāptimcha = acquisition of righteousness; kevalou = unalloyed; arthakāmoucha = wealth and enjoyment.

"By the grace of Rama, I wish to attain a very great renown in this earth, an extensive acquisition of righteousness, an unalloyed wealth and enjoyment."

Sloka 7

so aham priya sakham rāmam śayānam saha sītayā |
rakṣiṣyāmi dhanuṣ pāṇiḥ sarvaiḥ svair jnātibhiḥ saha || 2-86-7

7. aham = I; saḥ = as such; dhanushhpāṇiḥ = with a bow in my hand; saha = along with; svaiḥ = my; jṇ^ātibhiḥ = relatives; rakshhisyāmi = will guard; priyasakham = my dear friend; rāman = Rama; shayānam = who slept ; sītayāsaha = together with ṣeetha.

"I as such with a bow in my hand, along with my relatives will guard my dear friend Rama, who slept together with Seetha."

Sloka 8

na hi me aviditam kiṃcid vane asmimḥ carataḥ sadā |
catur angam hy api balam prasahema vayam yudhi || 2-86-8

8. me = to me; sadā = who forever; charataḥ = wander; asmin vane = in this forest; na kimchit hi = nothing is indeed; aviditam = unknown; vayam = we; prasahema hi = can defeat; chaturaṇgam balam api = even an army consisting of all the four limbs; yudhi = in battle.

"Nothing is indeed unknown to me, who forever wander in this forest. We can defeat even an army consisting of all the four limbs in battle."

Sloka 9

evam asmābhir uktena lakṣmaṇena mahātmanā |
anunītā vayam sarve dharmam eva anupaśyatā || 2-86-9

9. evam = thus; uktam = spoken; asmābhiḥ = by us; sarve = all; vayam = of us; anunītāḥ = were politely replied; lakshhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; mahātmanā = the high soled; anupashyatā = who perceives; dharmameva = righteousness alone.

"Thus spoken as aforesaid by us the high soled Lakshmana, who perceives righteousness alone,politely replied to all of us( as follows)

Sloka 10

katham dāśarathau bhūmau śayāne saha sītayā |
śakyā nidrā mayā labdhum jīvitam vā sukhāni vā || 2-86-10

10. katham = how; shakyā = is it possible; labdhum = to obtain; nidrā = sleep; jīvitamvā = or even life ; sukhānivā = or comforts; mayā = by me; while) dāsharatha = Rama; sītayāsaha = along with ṣeetha; shayane = is reposing; bhūmou = on the floor?

"How is it possible for me to get a sleep, or even life or comforts when Rama is reposing on the floor along with Seetha?"

Sloka 11

yo na deva asuraiḥ sarvaiḥ śakyaḥ prasahitum yudhi |
tam paśya guha samviṣṭam tṛṇeṣu saha sītayā || 2-86-11

11. guha = O; Guha!; yaḥ = which Rama; na shakyaḥ = cannot be; prasahitum = conquered; yudhi = in battle; sarvaiḥ = by all; devāsuraiḥ = the celestials and demons; pashya = see; tam = that Rama; sītāyā saha = along with ṣeetha; samvishhṭam = who has reposed; tṛiṇeshhu = on blades of grass.

"O, Guha! Behold that Rama, who cannot be conquered in a battle even by all the celestials and demons, sleeping on the blades of grass along with Seetha."

Sloka 12

mahatā tapasā labdho vividhaiḥ ca pariśramaiḥ |
eko daśarathasya eṣa putraḥ sadṛśa lakṣaṇaḥ || 2-86-12

12. eshhaḥ = this Rama; ekaḥ = is an excellent; putraḥ = son; sadṛisha lakshhaṇaḥ = having attributes equal to those; dasarathasya = of Dasaratha; labdhaḥ = who obtained him; mahatā = by a great; tapasā = penance; vividhiḥ = and by facing various; parishramaishcha = troubles.

"This Rama is an excellent son, having qualities equal to those of Dasaratha. The king obtained Rama by performing a great penance and after facing various troubles."

Sloka 13

asmin pravrājite rājā na ciram vartayiṣyati |
vidhavā medinī nūnam kṣipram eva bhaviṣyati || 2-86-13

13. asmin = when he; pravrajite = having been to exiled; rājā = the king Dasaratha; navaratryishhyati = will not survive; chiram = for long; medinī = this earth; bhavishhyati = will be; nūnam = surely; vidhavā = without a husband; kshhiprameva = soon.

"When Rama having seen exiled, the king Dasaratha will not survive for long.This earth will surely be without a husband soon."

Sloka 14

vinadya sumahā nādam śrameṇa uparatāḥ striyaḥ |
nirghoṣa uparatam nūnam adya rāja niveśanam || 2-86-14

14. striyaḥ = the women; (in the gynoecium's; vinadya = having cried; sumahānādam = with agreat noise; uparatāḥ = would have ceased crying; shramena = out of fatigue; adya = now; nirghoshhaḥ = the noise; nūnam = surely; virtaḥ = would have stopped; rājanivashane = in the royal palace.

"The women in the gynoecium's, having cried with a great noise, would have ceased their crying out of fatigue. Now, the great noise would have stopped in the royal palace by now."

Sloka 15

kausalyā caiva rājā ca tathā eva jananī mama |
na āśaṃse yadi te sarve jīveyuḥ śarvarīm imām || 2-86-15

15. nāshamse = I do not think; kausalyachaiva = that Kausalya; rājācha = king Dasaratha; tathaiva = and; mamajananī = my mother ṣumitra; yadi = whether; tesarve = all of them; jīveyuḥ = will survive; imām = in this; rajanīm = night.

"I do not expect whether all of them, like Kausalya Dasaratha and even my mother Sumitra will survive at all till this night."

Sloka 16

jīved api hi me mātā śatrughnasya anvavekṣayā |
duhkhitā yā tu kausalyā vīrasūr vinaśiṣyati || 2-86-16

16. anvavekshhyā = by beholding; shatrughnasya = of ṣatrughna; me mātā = my mother; jīvedapicha = may even survive; ; sātu = but she; yā = that; kausalya = Kausalya; vīrasūḥ = who gave birth to the eminent Rama; vinashishhyati = will die; duḥkhitā = of sorrow.

"By beholding Shatrughna, my mother perhaps may even survive. However, the afflicted Kausalya who gave birth to the eminent Rama will surely die."

Sloka 17

atikrāntam atikrāntam anavāpya mano ratham |
rājye rāmam anikṣipya pitā me vinaśiṣyati || 2-86-17

17. me pitā = ṃy father; dasaratha; anavāpya = having not obtained; manoratham = his wish; atikrāntam atikrāntan = over steppingly and over stappingly; anikṣipya = and having not been able to install; rāmam = Rama; rājye = in the kingdom; vinashiṣyati = will die.

Without realising his long-cherished wish and failing to install Rama in the kingdom of Aodhya, my father Dasaratha will expire."

Sloka 18

siddha arthāḥ pitaram vṛttam tasmin kāle hy upasthite |
preta kāryeṣu sarveṣu saṃskariṣyanti bhūmipam || 2-86-18

18. tasminkāle = (when) that time; upasthite = comes; samskariṣyanti = (those) who will perform; sarveṣu = all; preta kāryeṣu = theobsequial rites; pitaram; of my father; bhūmipam = the king Dasaratha; vṛittam = who expired; siddhārthāḥ = are indeed they who accomplished their purpose.

"Those who have accomplished their purpose (Shatrughna and others) will perform all the funeal honours to king Dasaratha, my father when the time comes for it."

Sloka 19

ramya catvara saṃsthānām suvibhakta mahā pathām |
harmya prāsāda sampannām sarva ratna vibhūṣitām || 2-86-19
gaja aśva ratha sambādhām tūrya nāda vināditām |
sarva kalyāṇa sampūrṇām hṛṣṭa puṣṭa jana ākulām || 2-86-20
ārāma udyāna sampūrṇām samāja utsava śālinīm |
sukhitā vicariṣyanti rāja dhānīm pitur mama || 2-86-21

19; 20; 21. vichariṣyanti = (ṭhey) will inhabi;t sukhitāḥ = joyfully; mama pituḥ = my father; rājadhānīm = capital city; ramyachatvara samsthānām = with its pleasant cross roads and meeting places; suvibhakta mahāpathām = with its main roads well laid-out; harmya prāsāda sampannām = sumptous with large houses and lofty mansions; sarva ratna vibhūshhitām = and all the gems with which they are encrusted; gajāshva ratha sambādhām = with the elephants horses and chariots that abound there; tūryanāda vināditām = the sound of trumpets with which it resonates; sarva kalyāna sampūrṇām = the many diversions it contains; hṛiṣṭa janākulām = its prosperous and well-fed people; ārāmodyāna sampūrṇām = its luxurious gardens and parks;samājotsava shalinīm = the assemblies and festivals that continuously take place in it.

"They will joyfully inhabit my father capital City, with its pleasant cross roads and meeting places, with its main roads well laid out, sumptuous with large houses and lofty mansions, with all the gems they are encrusted, with elephants horses and chariots that abound there, the sound of trumpets with which the city resonates, the many diversions it contains, its properous and well-fed people, its luxurious gardens and parks as well as the assmeblies and festivals that continuously take place inti."

Sloka 22

api satya pratijnena sārdham kuśalinā vayam |
nivṛtte samaye hy asmin sukhitāḥ praviśemahi || 2-86-22

22. vayam api pravishamahi = shall we indeed entere Ayodhya; sukhitāḥ = happily; kushalinā sārtham = along with that virtuous Rama; satya pratijJṇena = who is true to his promise; nivṛitte = when he completes; asmin = this; samaye = term ( of exile)?

Shall we enter Ayodhya happily along with that virtuous Rama, who has truly redeemed his promise, when he completed his term of exile?

Sloka 23

paridevayamānasya tasya evam sumahātmanaḥ |
tiṣṭhato rāja putrasya śarvarī sā atyavartata || 2-86-23

23. tasya sumahātmanaḥ = (While) that magnanimous; rājaputrasya = Prince; paridevayamānasya = was lamenting; evam = thus; tiṣṭataḥ = stnading guard; sā shārvarī = that night; atyavartata = passed.

While that magnamimous prince was lamenting thus, standing guard, the night passed.

Sloka 24

prabhāte vimale sūrye kārayitvā jaṭā ubhau |
asmin bhāgīrathī tīre sukham samtāritau mayā || 2-86-24

24. sūrye = (When) the sun; prabhāte = rose; vimale = brightly; ubhau = the two of them; got kārayitvā jaṭāḥ = got their locks of hair matted; asmin = at the banks of River Ganga; samtāritau = and were ferried; sukham = safely; mayā = by me.

"When the sun rose brightly in the next morning Rama and Lakshmana got their locks of hair matted into a thick mass at the banks of River Ganga and they were safely ferried by me."

Sloka 25

jaṭā dharau tau druma cīra vāsasau |
mahā balau kunjara yūthapa upamau |
vara iṣu cāpa asi dharau param tapau |
vyavekṣamāṇau saha sītayā gatau ||2-86-25

25. tau = those; paramtapau = Rama and Lakshamana; mahābalau = possessing great strength; jaṭādharau = wearing matted locks of hair; drumachīravāsasau = and clothes made of bark; kuṇ^jara yūtha popamau = like unto the leaders of a herd of elephants; vareṣu chāpāsidharau = wielding excellent bow; arrows and sword; vyapekṣamāṇau = looking back at me; gatam = went away; sītāyā saha = with ṣeetha.

"Rama and Lakshmana, who were capable of tormenting their enemies, possessing great strength, wearing matted locks of hari and clothes made of bark, like unto the leaders of herd of elephants, wielding excellent bow, arrows and sword, looking back at me, went away with Seetha.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ṣaḍaśītitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 86th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
