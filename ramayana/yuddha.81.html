Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 81
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Placing an illusory live image of Seetha in his chariot, Indrajit along with his army enters the battle-field. Hanuma with his army of monkeys march in front to face Indrajit in battle. While Hanuma and his army are watching, Indrajit pulls Seetha by her hair and unsheathed his sword. Indrajit kills the illusory living image of Seetha, with his sharp sword.

Sloka 1

vijñāya tu manastasya rāghavasya mahātmanaḥ |
saṃnivṛtyāhavāttasmātpraviveśa puraṃ tataḥ || 2-81-1

1. viJṇāya = knowing; manaḥ = the mind; tasya mahātmanaḥ rāghavasya = of that great-souled Rama; saḥ = Indrajit; tataḥ = then; nivṛitya = turning away from; tasmāt āhavāt = that battle; praviesha = entered; puram = the city.

Guessing the mind of that great-souled Rama, Indrajit turning away from that battle, entered the City.

Sloka 2

so.anusmṛtya vadhaṃ teṣāṃ rākṣasānāṃ tarasvinām |
krodhatāmrekṣaṇaḥ śūro nirjagāma mahādyutiḥ || 2-81-2

2. atha = thereafter; saḥ shūraḥ = that valiant; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; anusmṛitya = recalling; vadham = the killing; teṣhām = of those; tarasvinām = energetic; rākṣhasānām = demons (like Kumbhakarna and others); nirjagāma = sallied forth (for the fight); krodhatāmrekṣhaṇaḥ = with red-hot eyes in anger.

Then, recalling the killing of those energetic demons like Kumbhakarna and others, that valiant Indrajit sallied forth (for the fight) with red-hot eyes in anger.

Sloka 3

sa paścimena dvāreṇa niryayau rākṣasairvṛtaḥ |
indrajittu mahāvīryaḥ paulastyo devakaṇṭakaḥ || 2-81-3

3. saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; sumahāvīryaḥ = of very great prowess; devakaṇ^ṭakaḥ = the adversary of gods; poulastyaḥ = and who was born in Pulastya-dynasty; vṛitaḥ = surrouded; rākṣhasaiḥ = by demons; niryayau = came forth; pashchimena dvareṇa = from the western gate.

That Indrajit, of very great prowess, the adversary of gods and who was born in Sage Pulstya's dynasty, accompanied by an army of demons, came forth from the western gate.

Sloka 4

indrajittu tato dṛṣṭvā bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |
raṇāyābhyudyatau vīrau māyāṃ prāduṣkarottadā || 2-81-4

4. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vīrau bhrātarau = the two valiant brothers; rāmalakṣhmaṇau = Rama and lakshmana; abhyudyatau = who were prepared; raṇāya = for the battle; indrajit tu = Indrajit for his part; tadā = then; prāduṣhkarot = demonstrated; māyām = his conjuring tricks.

Seeing the two valiant brothers, Rama and Lakshamana, who were prepared for the encounter, Indrajit for his part, then started to demonstrate his conjuring tricks.

Sloka 5

indrajittu rathe sthāpya sūtāṃ māyāmayīṃ tadā |
balena mahatāvṛtya tasyā vadhamarocayat || 2-81-5

5. sthāpya = placing; māyāmayīm sītām = an illusory image of ṣeetha; rathe = on his chariot; indrajit tu = Indrajit for his part; mahatā = and a huge; balena āvṛitya = army surrounding it; tadā = then; ārochayat = intended; tasyāḥ vadham = to kill her.

Placing an illusory image of Seetha on his chariot and a large army surrounding the chariot, Indrajit intended to kill her.

Sloka 6

mohanārthaṃ tu sarveṣāṃ buddhiṃ kṛtvā sudurmatiḥ |
hantuṃ sītāṃ vyavasito vānarābhimukho yayau || 2-81-6

6. sudurmatiḥ = the exceedingly evil-minded Indrajit; buddhim kṛitvā = making up his mind; sarveṣhām mohanārtham = to purposefully confuse all; vyavasitaḥ = by playing a trick; hantum = to kill; sītām = ṣeetha; yayau = sallied forth; vānarābhimukhaḥ = in the direction of the monkeys.

ṃaking up his mind to purposefully confuse all, by playing a trick to kill Seetha's illusory image, the exceedingly evil-minded Indrajit sallied forth towards the direction of the monkeys.

Sloka 7

taṃ dṛṣṭvā tvabhiniryāntaṃ nagaryāḥ kānanaukasaḥ |
utpeturabhisaṅkruddhāḥ śilāhastā yuyutsavaḥ || 2-81-7

7. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam = him; abhiniryāntam = coming forth towards their direction; sarve te = all those; kānanaukasaḥ = monkeys; abhisamkruddhāḥ = were enraged; utpetuḥ = bounced towards him; shilāhastāḥ = with rocks in their hands; yuyutsavaḥ = and with an intent to fight.

Seeing him coming forth towards their direction, all those monkeys were enraged and bounced towards him, with rocks in their hands and with an intent to fight.

Sloka 8

hanūmānpuratasteṣāṃ jagāma kapikuñjaraḥ |
pragṛhya sumahacchṛṅgaṃ parvatasya durāsadam || 2-81-8

8. hanūmān = ḥanuma; kapikuṇ^jaraḥ = the foremost among the monkeys; pragṛihya = taking; sumahat = a very large; parvatasya shṛiṇgam = mountain-peak; sudurāsadam = which was dangerous to be reached by others; jagāma = went; purataḥ teṣhshām = in front of them.

Hanuma, the foremost among the monkeys, taking hold of a very large mountain-peak, which was difficult to be reached by others, marched in front.

Sloka 9

sa dadarśa hatānandāṃ sītām indrajito rathe |
ekaveṇīdharāṃ dīnāmupavāsakṛśānanām || 2-81-9
parikliṣṭaikavasanāmamṛjāṃ rāghavapriyām |
rajomalābhyāmāliptaiḥ sarvagātrairvarastriyam || 2-81-10

9. saḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; sītām = ṣeetha; rāghava priyām = the wife of Rama; vara striyam = the best among women; hatānandām = bereft of joy; eka veṇīdharām = wearing only a single braid of hair; dīnām = looking miserable; upavāsakṛishānanām = with her face emaciated due to fasting; parikliṣhṭaika vasanām = wearing a single worn-out clothing; amṛijām = unadorned; sarvagātraiḥ = and with all her limbs; āliptaiḥ = covered; rajomalābhyām = with dust and dirt; indrajitaḥ = in Indrajit's rahte = chariot.

That Hanuma saw Seetha, the wife of Rama, the best among women, bereft of joy, wearing only a single braid of hair, looking miserable, with her face emaciated due to fasting, dressed in a single worn-out clothing, unadorned and with all her limbs covered with dust and dirt; in the chaiot of Indrajit.

Sloka 11

tāṃ nirīkṣya muhūrtaṃ tu maithilīm adhyavasya ca |
bāṣpaparyākulamukho hanūmānvyathito.abhavat || 2-81-11

11. nirīkṣhya = on seeing; tām = her; muhūrtam = for a moment; adhyavasya cha = (ḥanuma) ascertained her; maithilīm = as ṣeetha; babhūva (viṣhaṇṇaḥ) = and became dejected; sā = that; janakātmajā = ṣeetha; achiradṛiṣhṭā his = was indeed seen not long ago; tena = by him.

On seeing her for a moment, Hanuma ascertained her as Seetha and became dejected. That Seetha was indeed seen by him, not long ago.

Sloka 12

abravīttāṃ tu śokārtāṃ nirānandāṃ tapasvinām |
dṛṣṭvā rathe stitāṃ sītāṃ rākṣasendrasutāśritām || 2-81-12
kiṃ samarthitamasyeti cintayansa mahākapiḥ |
saha tairvānaraśreṣṭhairabhyadhāvata rāvaṇim || 2-81-13

12; 13. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tām = her; shokārtām = stricken with grief; nirāndām = bereft of joy; tapasvinīm = looking distressed; dīnām = and miserable; rākṣhasendrasutashritām = sitting beside Indrajit; mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; abravīt = wondered; kim samarthitam iti = as to what his intention might be; chintayan = thus thinking; taiḥ vānarashreṣhṭhaiḥ saha = (he) along with those leaders of monkeys; abhyadāvata = rushed; rāvaṇim = towards Indrajit.

Seeing her stricken with grief bereft of joy, looking distreseed, miserable and sitting under the sway of Indrajit, Hanuma wondered as to what his intention might be. Thus thinking, he along with those leaders of mnkeys, rushed towards Indrajit.

Sloka 14

tadvānarabalaṃ dṛṣṭvā rāvaṇiḥ krodhamūrchitaḥ |
kṛtvā viśokaṃ nistriṃśaṃ mūrdhni sītāṃ parāmṛśat || 2-81-14

14. dṛiṣhṭvā = beholding; tat vānarabalam = that army of monkeys; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; krodha mūrchhitaḥ = was excited with anger; kṛitvā = and making; nistrimsham = his sword; vikosham = sheathless; ākarṣhayat = and pulled; sītām = ṣeetha; mūrdhni = (by the hair) on her head.

Beholding that army of monkeys, Indrajit was excited with anger, pulled Seetha by the hair on her head and unsheathed his sword.

Sloka 15

taṃ striyaṃ paśyatāṃ teṣāṃ tāḍayāmāsa rāvaṇiḥ |
krośantīṃ rāma rāmeti māyayā yojitāṃ rathe || 2-81-15

15. teṣhām pashyatām = while those monkeys were seeing; rākṣhasaḥ = the demon; tāDayāmāsa = began to strike; tām striyam = that woman; yojitām = who was placed; rathe = in the chariot; māyayā = by the dint of his conjuring trick; kroshantīm = and was crying; rāma rāmeti = "Rama! Rama!".

While those monkeys were seeing, demon began to strike that woman who was placed in the chariot by the dint of his conjuring trick and who was crying "Rama! Rama!"

Sloka 16

gṛhītamūrdhajāṃ dṛṣṭvā hanūmāndainyamāgataḥ |
duḥkhajaṃ vārinetrābhyāmutsṛjanmārutātmajaḥ || 2-81-16

16. dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; gṛihīta mūrdhajām = her seized by the hair (by Indrajit); hanūmān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; dainyam āgataḥ = was confounded with misery; utsṛijat = and shed; vāri = tears; netrābhyām = from his eyes; duḥkhajam = in agony.

On seeing her seized by the hair Hanuma the son of wind-god was confounded with affliction and shed tears, in agony from his eyes.

Sloka 17

tām dṛṣṭvā cārusarvāṅgīṃ rāmasya mahiṣīṃ priyām |
abravītparuṣaṃ vākyaṃ krodhādrakṣodhipātmajam || 2-81-17

17. dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; tām = her; priyām = the beloved; mahiṣhīm = wife; rāmasya = of Rama; chāru sarvāṇ^gīm = with all her charming limbs; krodhāt = (ḥanuma) angrily; abravīt = addressed; paruṣham vākyam = the following harsh words; rākṣhodhipātmajam = to Indrajit.

On seeing Seetha with all her charming limbs, the beloved wife of Rama, Hanuma angrily addressed the foolowing harsh words to Indrajit:

Sloka 18

durātmannātmanāśāya keśapakṣe parāmṛśaḥ |
brahmarṣīṇāṃ kule jāto rākṣasīṃ yonimāśritaḥ || 2-81-18

18. durātman = O; evil-minded one!; jātaḥ = born; brahmarṣhīṇām kule = in a dynasty of Brahmanical sages; āshritaḥ yonim = but belonging to the race; rākṣhasīm = of demons; parāmṛishaḥ = you touched; keshapakṣhe = a lock of her hair; ātmanāshāya = for your own ruin.

"O evil minded one! Born in a dynasty of Brahmanical sages, but belonging to demoniacal race, you touched a lock of her hair, for your hair, for your own ruin."

Sloka 19

dhiktvāṃ pāpasamācāraṃ yasya te matirīdṛśī |
nṛśaṃsānārya durvṛtta kṣudra pāpaparākrama || 2-81-19
anāryasyedṛśaṃ karma ghṛṇā te nāsti nirghṛṇa |

19. nṛishamsa = O cruel one!; anārya = O vulgar one!; durvṛitta = O mean fellow!; kṣhudra = O wicked demon!; pāpaparākrama = having sinful prowess!; nirghṛiṇa = O pitiless one!; tvām dhik = woe be to you; pāpa samācharam = of sinful conduct!; yasya īdṛishī = in whom such; matiḥ = a resolve; te = of you; īdṛisham karma = such an act; anāryasya = is worthy of a barbarian; gṛiṇāa te nāsti = there is no pity in you!.

"O cruel, vulgar and mean fellow! O wicked demon of sinful prowess! O pitiless fellow! Woe be to you, of sinful conduct, in whom such a resolve has taken place. Such an act is worthy of a barbarian. There is no pity in you."

Sloka 20

cyutā gṛhācca rājyācca rāmahastācca maithilī || 2-81-20
kiṃ tavaiṣāparāddhā hi yadenāṃ hiṃsi nirdaya |

20. nirdaya = O pitiless one!; maithilī = ṣeetha; chyutā = came away; gṛihāchcha = from her house; rājyāchcha = from her kingdom; rāma hastāchcha = and from the protection of Rama; hamsi yat enām = you are killing her; kim aparāddhāhi = what wrong; eṣhā = has ṣeetha; tava = (done) to you?

"O pitiless one! Seetha came away from her house, from her kingdom and from her huband's (Rama's) protection. What wrong has Seetha done to you, that you are killing her?"

Sloka 21

sītāṃ ca hatvā na ciraṃ jīviṣyasi kathaṃ cana || 2-81-21
vadhārhakarmaṇānena mama hastagato hyasi |

21. hatvā = by killing; sītām = ṣeetha; na jīviṣhyasi = you will not survive; chiram = for long; kathamchana = in any way; vadhārha = O demon; deserving of death!; tena karmeṇa = by your act; mama hastagataḥ asi hi = you indeed fell into my hands.

"By killing Seetha, you will not survivie for long in any way. O demon, desrving of death! By your act, you indeed fell into my hands."

Sloka 22

ye ca strīghātināṃ lokā lokavadhyaiśca kutsitāḥ || 2-81-22
iha jīvitamutsṛjya pretya tānpratilapsyase |

22. utsṛijya = abandoning; jīvitam = your life; iha = in this world; pretya = after death; prati lapsyase = you shall descend; ye ān lokāḥ = to those worlds; strīghātinām = which are the lot of the killers of women; kutsitāḥ lika vadhyeṣhu = and are condemned even by thse who deserved to be killed by the peple.

"Abandoning your life in this world, you shall, after death, descend to those worlds which are the lot of the killers of women and are condemned even by those who deserved to be killed by the people."

Sloka 23

iti bruvāṇo hanumānsāyudhairharibhirvṛtaḥ || 2-81-23
abhyadhāvata saṅkruddho rākṣasendrasutaṃ prati |

23. hanumān = ḥanuma; iti = thus; bruvāṇaḥ = speaking; vṛitaḥ = surrounded; haribhiḥ = by monkeys; sāyudhaiḥ = with their weapons; susamkruddhaḥ = and very much enraged; abhyadhāvat = rushed; rākṣhasendrasutamprati = towards Indrajit.

Thus speaking, Hanuma surrounded as he was by monkeys with their weapons and very much enraged, rushed headlong towards Indrajit.

Sloka 24

āpatantaṃ mahāvīryaṃ tadanīkaṃ vanaukasām || 2-81-24
rakṣasāṃ bhīmavegānāmanīkena nyavārayat |

24. anīkam = the army; bhīma kopānām = of terrible rage; rakṣhasām = of demons; nyavārayat = intercepted tam anīkam = that army; vanaukasām = of monkeys; mahāvīryam = possessing a high prowess; āpatantam = and advancing towars them.

The army of demons of terrible rage intercepted that army of mokeys possessing a high prowess and advancing towards them.

Sloka 25

sa tāṃ bāṇasahasreṇa vikṣobhya harivāhinīm || 2-81-25
hariśreṣṭhaṃ hanūmantamindrajitpratyuvāca ha |

25. vikṣhobhya = stirring up; tām hari vāhinīm = that army of monkeys; bāṇa sahasreṇa = with a multitude of his arrows; saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; pratyuvācha ha = replied; hanumantam = to ḥanuma; harishreṣhṭham = the foremost of monkeys (as follows).

Stirring up that army of mokeys with a multitude of his arrows, Indrajit replied to Hanuma, the foremost of mokeys as follows:

Sloka 26

sugrīvastvaṃ ca rāmaśca yannimittamihāgatāḥ || 2-81-26
tāṃ haniṣyāmi vaidehīmadyaiva tava paśyataḥ |

26. yannimittam = for whose sake; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; tvam cha = yourself; rāmashcha = and Rama; āgatāḥ = came; iha = here; vadhiṣhyāmi = I am going to kill; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; adyaiva = now itself; tava pashyataḥ = while you stand looking on.

"For whose sake, Sugreeva, yourself and Rama came here, I am going to kill Seetha now itself, while you stand looking on."

Sloka 27

imāṃ hatvā tato rāmaṃ lakṣmaṇaṃ tvāṃ ca vānara || 2-81-27
sugrīvaṃ ca vadhiṣyāmi taṃ cānāryaṃ vibhīṣaṇam |

27. vānara = O monkey!; hatvā imām = after killing her; vadhiṣhyāmi = I will make a short work of; rāmam = Rama; lakṣhmaṇamcha = Lakshmana; tvām cha = yourself too; sugrīvamcha = ṣugreeva; anāryam tam vibhīṣhaṇam cha = and that vulgar Vibhishana; tataḥ = thereafter.

"O monkey! After killing her, I will make a short work of Rama, Lakshmana, yourself too, Sugreeva and that vulgar Vibhishana thereafter."

Sloka 28

na hantavyāḥ striyaśceti yadbravīṣi plavaṅgama || 2-81-28
pīḍā karamamitrāṇāṃ yatsyātkartavyameta tat |

28. plavamgama = O monkey!; yat = (for) what; bravīṣhi = you have said; iti = that; striyaḥ = women; na hantavyāḥ = are not to be killed; (hear my reply); yat = that which; pīDākaramsyāt = causes tormentation; amitrāṇām = to te enemies; tat = that; kartavyameva = indeed needs to be done.

"O monkey! What you have said just now viz. that women are not to be killed, is correct. But that which causes tormentation to the enemies indeed needs to be done."

Sloka 29

tamevamuktvā rudatīṃ sītāṃ māyāmayīṃ tataḥ || 2-81-29
śitadhāreṇa khaḍgena nijaghānendrajitsvayam |

29. evam = thus; uktvā = having spoken; tam = to ḥanuma; indrajit = Indrajit; svayam = personally; nijaghāna = killed; tām māyāmayīm sītām = that illusory ṣeetha; rudatīm = who had been sobbing (all the while); shitadhāreṇa = with sharp-edged; khaDgena = sword.

Having spoken thus to Hanuma, Indrajit personally killed that illusory Seetha, who ahd been sobbing all the while, with his sharp-edged sword.

Sloka 30

yajñopavītamārgeṇa chinnā tena tapasvinī || 2-81-30
sā pṛthivyāṃ pṛthuśroṇī papāta priyadarśanā |

30. chchhinnā = ṣplit asunder; tena = by him; yaJṇopavīta mārgeṇa = diagonally (as the way of wearing a sacred thread over the left shoulder and hanging down under the right by the three twice-born castes); sā tapasvinī = that miserable lady; pṛithushreṇī = of broad hips; priyadarshanā = and pleasant to the sight; papāta = fell down; pṛithivyām = on the ground.

Split asunder by him diagonally, that miserable lady of broad hips and pleasant to the sight, fell down on the ground.

Sloka 31

tāmindrajitstriyaṃ hatvā hanūmantamuvāca ha || 2-81-31
mayā rāmasya paśyemāṃ kopena ca niṣūditām |
eṣā viśastā vaidehī niṣphalo vaḥ pariśramaḥ || 2-81-32

31; 32. hatvā = after killing; tām = that; striyam = woman; indrajit = Indrajit; uvācha ha = told; hanumantam = ḥanuma (as follows); pashya = see; imām = this; rāmasya priyām = wife of Rama; shastraniṣhūditām = has been killed with a weapon; mayā = by me; eṣhā = this; vaidehī = ṣeetha; vishastā = has been killed; vaḥ = your; parishramaḥ = labour; niṣhphalaḥ = is in vain.

After killing that woman, Indrajit told Hanuma as follows: "See, I killed Rama's wife with my weapon. This Seetha is dead and all your labour is in vain."

Sloka 33

tataḥ khaḍgena mahatā hatvā tām indrajitsvayam |
hṛṣṭaḥ sa rathamāsthāya vinanāda mahāsvanam || 2-81-33

33. hatvā = after killing; tām = her; svayam = personally; mahatākhaDgena = with a large sword; saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; tataḥ = then; āsthāya = sitting on; ratham = his chariot; hṛiṣhṭhaḥ = pleasurefully; nanāda cha = roared; mahāsvanam = with a high sound.

After killing her with a large sword personally, that Indrajit then sitting on his chariot, pleasurefully roared loudly.

Sloka 34

vānarāḥ śuśruvuḥ śabdamadūre pratyavasthitāḥ |
vyāditāsyasya nadatastaddurgaṃ saṃśritasya tu || 2-81-34

34. vānarāḥ = the monkeys; pratyavasthitāh = standing; adūre = in vicinity; shushruvuḥ = heard; shabdam = his roar; nadataḥ = even as he shouted; vyāditāsyasya = with his mouth wide open; samshritya = while comfortably sitting; tat durgam = in that tower of the aerial chariot; which was actually difficult of access for others.

The monkeys standing in vicinity heard his roar, even as he shouted with his mouth wide open, while comfortable sitting in that tower of the aerial chriot, which was actually difficult of access for others.

Sloka 35

tathā tu sītāṃ vinihatya durmatiḥ |
prahṛṣṭacetāḥ sa babhūva rāvaṇiḥ |
taṃ hṛṣṭarūpaṃ samudīkṣya vānarā |
viṣaṇṇarūpāḥ samabhipradudruvuḥ || 2-81-35

35. vinihatya = killing; sītām = ṣeetha; tathā = in that way; sah = that; durmatiḥ = evil-minded; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; babhūva = became; prahṛiṣhṭa chetāḥ = exceedingly glad; samudīkṣhya = seeing; tam = him; hṛiṣhṭa rūpam = exceedinly plesed; vānarāḥ = the mokeys; viṣhaṇṇarūpāḥ = looked dejected; sahasā = and suddenly; abhidudruvuḥ = ran away.

After killing Seetha in that way, the evil-minded Indrajit became exceedingly glad. Seeing him exceedingly pleased, the monkeys looked dejected and suddenly ran away.
