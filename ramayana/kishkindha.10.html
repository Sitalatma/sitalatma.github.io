Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 10
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sugreeva informs Rama that Vali could not be appeased in spite of repeated requests as he is resolved to accept Sugreeva as an enemy. Vali banishes Sugreeva from the kingdom with the same thinking.


Sloka 1

tataḥ krodha samāviṣṭam samrabdham tam upāgatam |
aham prasādayān cakre bhrātaram hita kāmyayā || 4-10-1

1. tataḥ aham = then, I; prasādayān cakre = begged of him; krodha samāviṣṭam = by anger, enveloped; samrabdham = capricious one; upāgatam = who arrived; tam bhrātaram = him, brother; hita kāmyayā = well-being, desiring.

"Then desiring both of our well-being I have begged of my brother who is enveloped in anger and behaving capriciously. [4-10-1]

Sloka 2

diṣṭyā asi kuśalī prāpto nihataḥ ca tvayā ripuḥ |
anāthasya hi me nāthaḥ tvam eko anātha nandanaḥ || 4-10-2

2. anātha ānandanaḥ = oh, orphan's, delight Rama; diṣṭyā tvayā ripuḥ nihataḥ = luckily, by you, enemy, is eliminated; kushalī prāptaḥ asi = safely, returned, you are; anāthasya me tvam ekaḥ nāthaḥ = to an orphaned one, to me, you, alone, are the protector.

"Oh, orphan's, delight Rama, I addressed him like this, "Luckily you have eliminated the enemy and returned safely, and to an orphaned one like me you alone are the protector. [4-10-2]

Sloka 3

idam bahu śalākam te pūrṇa candram iva uditam |
chatram sa vāla vyajanam pratīcchasva mayā dhṛtam || 4-10-3

3. te = to you; mayā dhṛitam = by me, held; bahu shalākam = with many, spikes; uditam pūrṇa candram iva = arisen, full, moon, like; sa vāla vyajanam = with, [accompanying] fur, fans; idam chatram pratīcchasva = this, [regal] umbrella, you accept.

Let this regal umbrella unfolded like a full moon, with its many of its spikes, and with accompanying fur-fans may please be accepted while I hold it for you. [4-10-3]

Sloka 4

ārtasya atha bilā dvāri sthitaḥ saṃvatsaram nṛpa |
dṛṣṭvā ca śoṇitam dvāri bilāt ca api samutthitam || 4-10-4
śoka saṃvigna hṛdayo bhṛśam vyākulita indriyaḥ |
apidhāya bila dvāram śaila śṛṅgeṇa tat tadā || 4-10-5
tasmāt deśāt apākramya kiṣkindhām prāviśam punaḥ |

4, 5, 6a. nṛipa = oh, king; ārtaḥ = saddened [I was]; samvatsaram = for one year; bila dvārī sthitaḥ = at that, cave's entrance, I stayed; atha bilāt samutthitam shoṇitam dvāri dṛiṣṭvā = then, from cave, started to flow, blood, at entrance, having seen; shoka samvigna hṛidayaḥ = sadness, engulfed, with a heart; bhṛisham vyākulita indriyaḥ = much, perturbed, senses; tadā tat bila dvāram = then, that, cave's, entrance; shaila shringeṇa apidhāya = with a hill, top, having covered; tasmāt deshāt apākramya = from that, place, moving away; punaḥ kiṣkindhām prāvisham = again, Kishkindha, I entered.

"Saddened I was after staying at the entrance of the cave for one year, oh, king, and on seeing the blood that started to flow from out the cave in the entrance my heart is engulfed in sadness and my senses perturbed very much, and then covering that cave's entrance with a hilltop, and departing from that country, I re-entered Kishkindha. [4-10-4, 5, 6a]

Sloka 6

viṣādāt iha mām dṛṣṭvā pauraiḥ maṃtribhir eva ca || 4-10-6
abhiṣikto na kāmena tanme kṣantum tvam arhasi |

6b, 7a. viṣādāt iha mām dṛiṣṭvā = with grief, [on my coming] here, me, on seeing; pouraiḥ mantribhiḥ eva ca = citizens, ministers, alike, also; abhishikhtaḥ = crowned me; na kāmena = not, [at my] desire; tat me kshantum tvam arhasi = therefore, me, to pardon, apt of you.

"On seeing me returning with grief, the citizens and ministers alike crowned me, but that is not at my desire, therefore it is apt of you pardon me. [4-10-6b, 7a]

Sloka 7

tvam eva rājā mānārhaḥ sadā ca aham yathā purā || 4-10-7
rājabhāve niyogaḥ ayam mama tvat virahāt kṛtaḥ |
sa amātya paura nagaram sthitam nihata kaṇṭakam || 4-10-8

7b, 8. mānārhaḥ rājā tvam eva = estimable, king, you, alone; aham ca purā yathā = I will be, as earlier, as I was; mama ayam raja bhāve niyogaḥ tvat virahāt kṛitaḥ = my, this, in kingship, establishment, with your, unavailability, it is done; sa amātya paura nagaram = with, ministers, citizens, capital; nihata kantakam = eliminated, thorniness; sthitam = is there.

"As an estimable one you alone are the king, and I will be as I was, and establishing me in kingship is owing to your unavailability, but the capital with citizens and ministers is kept up without thorniness. [4-10-7b, 8]

Sloka 9

nyāsa bhūtam idam rājyam tava niryātayāmi aham |
mā ca roṣam kṛthāḥ saumya mama śatru niṣūdana|| 4-10-9

9. nyāsa bhūtam = custodial, one[kingdom]; idam rājyam = this, kingdom; tava = to you; niryātayāmi = returning; aham = I am; shatru niṣūdana = oh enemies, eliminator; saumya = oh, gentle one; mama = in my respect; roṣam = anger / hostility; mā kṛithāḥ = do not, you make.

"Oh, gentle one, I am now returning this custodial kingdom to you, oh, enemy eliminator, you need not be hostile towards me. [4-10-9]

Sloka 10

yāce tvām śirasā rājan mayā baddho ayam aṃjaliḥ |
balāt asmin samāgamya maṃtribhiḥ pura vāsibhiḥ || 4-10-10
rājabhāve niyukto aham śūnya deśa jigīṣayā |

10, 11a. rājan = oh king; shirasā tvām yāce = with head bowed, you, I beg; mayā ayam anjaliḥ baddhaḥ = by me, this, [prayerful] palm-fold, is made; mantribhiḥ pura vāsibhiḥ = by the ministers, capital, dwellers; samāgamya = coming together; shūnya desha jigīshayā = empty [kingless,] kingdom, to have control on; aham = I was; asmin = to this [kingship]; rajabhāve = in kingship; balāt = forcibly; niyuktaḥ = nominated.

"I beg you with my bowed head and with my prayerful palm-fold, the ministers and the city dwellers have collectively and forcibly nominated me to the kingship, only to keep the kingless kingdom under control. [4-10-10, 11a]

Sloka 11

snigdham evam bruvāṇam mām sa vinirbhartsya vānaraḥ || 4-10-11
dhik tvām iti ca mām uktvā bahu tat tat uvāca ha |

11b, 12a. saḥ vānaraḥ = he, that monkey; evam snigdham bruvāṇam = this way, politely, while I was saying; mām nirbhartsya = me, on threatening; dhik = fie; tvām = upon you; iti ca = thus, also; mām uktvā = to me said; tat tat = that, that, [expletives]; bahu = many; uvāca ha = he said, indeed.

"While I was speaking that politely he threatened me and indeed used many expletives starting from 'fie, fie, upon you.' and the like. [4-10-11b, 12a]

Sloka 12

prakṛtīḥ ca samānīya maṃtriṇaḥ caiva sammatān || 4-10-12
mām āha suhṛdām madhye vākyam parama garhitam |

12b, 13a. prakṛitīḥ ca = people, also; sammatān = venerable ones; mantriṇaḥ caiva = ministers, also, thus; samānīya = on summoning; suhṛidām madhye = friends, amongst; mām = to me; parama garhitam vākyam = very, abusive, words; āha = he said / hurled.

"And summoning people and venerable ministers he hurled very abusive words at me among friends. [4-10-12b, 13a]

Sloka 13

viditam vo mayā rātrau māyāvī sa mahāasuraḥ || 4-10-13
mām samāhvayata kruddho yuddha kāṃkṣī tadā purā |

13b, 14a. purā mahāasuraḥ māyāvī = earlier, the gigantic, ṃāyāvi; kruddhaḥ = rancorous; yuddha kānkshī = duel, desiring one; tadā rātrau = in that, night; mām = me; samāhvayata = invited; vaḥ = that; viditam = you are aware of.

"You all are aware that earlier the gigantic and rancorous demon ṃāyāvi invited me desiring a duel in that night. [4-10-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

tasya tad bhāṣitam śrutvā niḥsṛtaḥ aham nṛpāalayāt || 4-10-14
anuyātaḥ ca mām tūrṇam ayam bhrātā sudāruṇaḥ |

14b, 15a. tasya tat = his, that; bhāṣitam = saying [calling for duel]; shrutvā = on hearing; niḥsṛitaḥ = went out; aham = I am; nṛipa ālayāt = from royal, palace su dāruṇaḥ = very, hazardous one; ayam bhrātā = this, brother; anuyātaḥ ca = followed, also; tūrṇam = quickly; mām = me.

"On hearing his call for a duel I went out of the royal palace, and this very hazardous brother of mine quickly followed me. [4-10-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

sa tu dṛṣṭvā eva mām rātrau sa dvitīyam mahābalaḥ || 4-10-15
prādravat bhaya saṃtrasto vīkṣya āvām samupāgatau |
abhidrutaḥ tu vegena viveśa sa mahābilam || 4-10-16

15b, 16. mahābalaḥ = formidable one; saḥ = that demon; rātrau sa dvitīyam mām dṛiṣṭvā eva = in night, with, a second one, me, on seeing, just by; pra adravat = he ran away; samupāgatau āvām vīkshya = those that reached nearly, at us, on seeing; bhaya samtrastaḥ = fear, fraught by; vegena abhidrutaḥ = speedily, he ran; saḥ = he; mahābilam = great, cavity; vivesha = entered.

"And that formidable demon ṃāyāvi ran away as he was fraught with fear on seeing me with a second one at my side, and he speedily entered a great cavity of earth on seeing us two nearly reaching him. [4-10-15b, 16]

Sloka 17

tam praviṣṭam viditvā tu sughoram sumahad bilam |
ayam ukto atha me bhrātā mayā tu krūra darśanaḥ || 4-10-17

17. tam = him; praviṣṭam = entered into; viditvā tu = on knowing; su ghoram = great, precarious; sumahat bilam = great cavity; atha = then; krūra darshanaḥ = cruel to look; ayam me bhrātā = this, brother, of mine; mayā uktaḥ by me, said.

"On knowing the demon's entry into that great precarious cavity, I said to this one, this cruel looking brother of mine. [4-10-17]

Sloka 18

ahatvā na asti me śaktiḥ prati gantum itaḥ purīm |
bila dvāri pratīkṣa tvam yāvat enam nihanmi aham || 4-10-18

18. a hatvā = without, killing; itaḥ = from here; purīm = to capital; prati gantum = return to go; me = to me; shaktiḥ = power; na asti = not, there; aham = I am; enam = him, the demon; yāvat = till; nihanmi = I kill; tvam = you; bila dvāri = at cavity's, entrance; pratīksha = wait.

"ṃy power will not let me return from here to the capital without killing that demon, hence you wait at this cavity's entrance, till I kill that demon and come out of the cavity. [4-10-18]

Sloka 19

sthito.ayam iti matvā aham praviṣṭaḥ tu durāsadam |
tam me mārgayataḥ tatra gataḥ saṃvatsaraḥ tadā || 4-10-19

19. sthitaḥ = stayed; ayam iti = he thus; matvā aham = believing, I have; pravishtaḥ tu = entered, thus; durāsadam = impenetrable cave; tadā = then; tatra = there; tam = him, that demon; mārgamānasya = while searching; me = for me; gataḥ = elapsed; samvatsaraḥ = one year.

"Believing that he stayed at the entrance of the cavity I entered that impenetrable cavity, and then in searching for that demon there one year elapsed. [4-10-19]

Sloka 20

sa tu dṛṣṭo mayā śatruḥ anirvedāt bhayāavahaḥ |
nihataḥ ca mayā sadyaḥ saḥ sarvaiḥ saha bandhubhiḥ || 4-10-20

20. bhayāavahaḥ = terrified one; saḥ shatruḥ = he that, enemy; mayā = by me; a nirvedāt = without, worry - effortlessly; dṛiṣṭaḥ = is seen; saḥ sarvaiḥ bandhubhiḥ saha = he, all of his, relatives, along with; mayā = by me; sadyaḥ nihataḥ = immediately, killed.

"I have seen that terrified enemy of mine effortlessly, and I killed him immediately along with all of his relatives. [4-10-20]

Sloka 21

tasya āsyāt tu pravṛttena rudhiraugheṇa tad bilam |
pūrṇam āsīt durākrāmam svanataḥ tasya bhūtale || 4-10-21

21. bhūtale = on ground; svanataḥ = [fallen] yelling; tasya āsyāt tu = from his, mouth, but; pravṛittena, rudhira aughena = emanating, by blood, gushes of; tat bilam pūrṇam = that, cavity, is filled up; dur ākrāmam āsīt = impossible, to tread, it has become.

"That cavity has become impassable, since it is fully filled with the gushes of blood emanating from the mouth of that demon who fell yelling onto ground. [4-10-21]

Sloka 22

sūdayitvā tu tam śatrum vikrāntam tam aham sukham |
niṣkrāmam na eva paśyāmi bilasya pihitam mukham || 4-10-22

22. aham = I; tam shatrum vikrāntam = that, enemy, who was invading; sukham = easily; sūdayitvā = having killed; nishkrāmam = exit; na eva = not, thus; pashyāmi = saw; bilasya pihitam mukham = cavity's, closed, face.

"On my killing that invading enemy easily, I could not see any exit from that cavity as its mouth was closed. [4-10-22]

Sloka 23

vikrośamānasya tu me sugrīva iti punaḥ punaḥ |
yataḥ prativaco nāsti tataḥ aham bhṛśa duḥkhitaḥ || 4-10-23

23. sugrīva iti = oh ṣugreeva, thus; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; vi kroshamānasya = while I loudly shouted; me = to me; yataḥ = what for; prativacaḥ = reply; na asti = not, there; tataḥ = by that; aham = I am; bhr^isha duhkhitaḥ = very, saddened.

"Wherefore I did not get a reply though I repeatedly shouted, 'Sugreeva, oh, Sugreeva.' thereby I was very saddened. [4-10-23]

Sloka 24

pāda prahāraiḥ tu mayā bahubhiḥ paripātitam |
tataḥ aham tena niṣkramya pathā puram upāgataḥ || 4-10-24

24. mayā = by me; bahubhiḥ = with many; pāda prahāraiḥ = with foot, poundings; paripātitam = [lidded hilltop] is smashed; tataḥ = then; aham = I have; tena pathā = by that, way; nishkramya = exited; punaḥ upāgataḥ = again, arrived here.

"With my foot I pounded and smashed that lidded hilltop, and from there I exited that way and arrived here. [4-10-24]

Sloka 25

tatra anena asmi samruddhaḥ rājyam mṛgayata ātmanaḥ |
sugrīveṇa nṛśaṃsena vismṛtya bhrātṛ sauhṛdam || 4-10-25

25. ātmanaḥ = for himself; rājyam = kingdom; mṛigayata = fantasizing; nṛishamsena anena = by cruel one, by this one; vismṛitya bhrātṛi sauhṛidam = forgetting, brotherly, good heartedness - clannishness; [aham = I was]; tatra = there; samruddhaḥ = trammelled; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; asmi = I was.

"As such this cruel fantasist of kingdom trammelled me in there forgetting the clannishness." So said Vali to all the courtiers. [4-10-25]

Sloka 26

evam uktvā tu mām tatra vastreṇa ekena vānaraḥ |
tadā nirvāsayāmāsa vālī vigata sādhvasaḥ || 4-10-26

26. vānaraḥ = that monkey; vālī = Vali; tatra = in there, in court; mām = to me; evam uktvā = thus, saying; vigata sādhvasaḥ = gone [leaving off,] ruth; tadā = then; vastreṇa ekena = clothing, one only; nirvāsayāmāsa = started to render me homeless - banished me.

"On saying thus that monkey ruthlessly banished me, then and there in the court itself, who am with a single clothing on my body. [4-10-26]

Sloka 27

tena aham apaviddhaḥ ca hṛta dāraḥ ca rāghava |
tat bhayāt ca mahīm sarvān krāntavān sa vana arṇavām || 4-10-27

27. Rāghava; tena aham = by him, I am; apaviddhaḥ ca = thrown away, also; hṛita dāraḥ ca = stolen, wife, also; tat bhayāt ca = by his, fear, alone; sa vana arṇavā = withal, forests, oceans sarvān = all over the; mahīm = earth; krāntāvān = I have roamed.

"Thus he threw me away and even stole my wife, oh, Rama, and owing to his fear alone I roamed all over the earth with its forests and oceans. [4-10-27]

Sloka 28

ṛśyamūkam giri varam bhāryā haraṇa duḥkhitaḥ |
praviṣṭo asmi durādharṣam vālinaḥ kāraṇāntare || 4-10-28

28. bhāryā haraṇa duḥkhitaḥ = wife, by stealing, I who am saddened; kāraṇa antare = by another reason; vālinaḥ durādharṣham = by Vali, impenetrable one; giri varam = mountain, safest; Rishyamūkam = ṃt. Rishyamuka; praviṣṭaḥ asmi = entered, I have;

"I who am saddened by stealing my wife have entered this safest mountain Rishyamuka, which is impenetrable for Vali by another reason. [4-10-28]

Sloka 29

etat te sarvam ākhyātam vaira anukathanam mahat |
anāgasā mayā prāptam vyasanam paśya rāghava || 4-10-29

29. Rāghava; etat mahat vaira anukathanam = all this, notable, enmity, account of; sarvam te ākhyātam = all to you, is reported; pashya = you may see - examine; anāgasā = without fault; mayā prāptam = by me, attained; vyasanam = dire straits.

"All this notable account of enmity is reported to you, oh, Raghava, and you may examine the dire straits befallen on me at no fault of mine. [4-10-29]

Sloka 30

vālinaḥ ca bhayāt tasya sarvaloka bhayāpaha |
kartum arhasi me vīra prasādam tasya nigrahāt || 4-10-30

30. sarva loka bhayā apahā = to all, worlds, fear, remover - oh, Rama; vālinaḥ ca = of Vali, only; tasya bhayāt = from that, fear; me = to me; prasādam = invulnerability; vīra = oh, valiant one; tasya nigrahāt = him, rein [him] in; kartum arhasi = to bestow, apt of you.

"Oh, Rama, the fear-remover of all the worlds, it is apt of you to bestow me invulnerability from the fear of Vali, and oh, valiant one, as well as to rein him in." Sugreeva requested Rama thus. [4-10-30]

Sloka 31

evam uktaḥ sa tejasvī dharmajño dharma saṃhitam |
vacanam vaktum ārebhe sugrīvam prahasan iva || 4-10-31

31. evam uktaḥ = thus, one who is said - Rama; tejasvī = resplendent one; dharmajñaḥ = virtuous one; saḥ = he, that Rama; prahasan iva = laughing off, as though; dharma samhitam vacanam = in virtue, abiding, sentence; sugrīvam = to ṣugreeva; vaktum = to speak; ārebhe = started.

When he is requested thus by Sugreeva, that virtuous and resplendent Rama started to tell Sugreeva, the words that abide by virtue, as though trivializing the task on hand. [4-10-31]

Sloka 32

amoghāḥ sūrya saṃkāśā niśitā me śarā ime |
tasmin vālini durvṛtte patiṣyanti ruṣa anvitāḥ || 4-10-32

32. amoghāḥ = admirable; sūrya samkāshā = sun-like; rusha anvitāḥ = [my] wrath, mingled with; nishitāḥ = sharp; mama = my; ete = these; sharāḥ = arrows; durvṛitte = evil minded one; tasmin vālini = on that, Vali; patiṣyanti = will fall.

"Admirable and sun-like burners are these arrows of mine that are now mingled with my wrath, and they are bound fall on that evil minded Vali. [4-10-32]

Sloka 33

yāvat tam na hi paśyeyam tava bhārya apahāriṇam |
tāvat sa jīvet pāpātmā vālī cāritra dūṣakaḥ || 4-10-33

33. tava = your; bhārya apahārinam = wife, stealer; tam = him; yāvat = till which time; na pashyeyam = not, I see; tāvat = only till then; pāpātmā cāritra dūshakaḥ = evil minded, history, abuser; saḥ vālī jīvet = he, that, Vali, will live.

"He that stealer of your wife, that evil-minded abuser of history lives as long as I descry. [4-10-33]

Sloka 34

ātma anumānāt paśyāmi magnaḥ tvām śoka sāgare |
tvām aham tārayiṣyāmi bāḍham prāpsyasi puṣkalam || 4-10-34

34. tvām shoka sāgare magnam = you; in sadness, sea of, deluged; ātma anumānāt = by self, inference -by my own experience; pashyāmi = I see; tvām aham tārayishyāmi = you, I will, make you to get across; bāDham = definitely; prāpsyasi = you will regain; pushkalam = abundantly.

"By my experience I see how you are deluged in a sea of sadness, but I will make you to get over that sea, and you will definitely regain abundantly whatever you lost." Rama assured Sugreeva thus. [4-10-34]

Sloka 35

tasya tat vacanam śrutvā harṣa pauruṣa vardhanam |
sugrīvaḥ parama prītaḥ su mahat vākyam abravīt || 4-10-35

35. tasya harṣa pauruṣa vardhanam = happiness, self-esteem, enhancing; tat vacanam shrutvā = his, that, sentence, having heard; ṣugreeva; parama prītaḥ = verily, gladdened; su mahat vākyam abravīt = very, great, sentence, [further] said.

Hearing that sentence of Rama, which is enhancing happiness and self-esteem, Sugreeva is very gladdened and further said this great sentence. [4-10-35]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe daśamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 10th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
