Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

[Sarga] 27
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Sept 09

Introduction

Sage Vishvamitra satisfied with the behaviour of Rama in obliging the orders of elders and performing the act that is assigned to him, gives many weapons to Rama, called shastra, astra-s. The weaponry is categorised mainly into two types. One is shastra - a handheld weapon like sword, lance or mace. The other is astra - a projectile missile invoked by reciting hymns. This chapter lists out those missiles that are given to Rama and in next chapter an account of some counter-attack missiles, called upasamhāra astra-s is given which can be used to nullify the enemy's missiles. In the endnote of this chapter some verses describing the nature of astra-s are incorporated.


Sloka 1

atha tām rajanīm uṣya viśvāmiro mahāyaśāḥ |
prahasya rāghavam vākyam uvāca madhura svaram || 1-27-1

1. atha = then; mahā yashāḥ = highly renowned; Vishvamitra; tām rajanīm uṣya = that, night, on staying; prahasya = smilingly; rāghavam = to Raghava; madhura aksharam vākyam uvāca = sweet worded, sentence, spoke.

Then, on staying that night there in Tataka forest, on the next day morning the greatly renowned sage Vishvamitra, smilingly and wording sweetly spoke to Rama. [1-27-1]

Sloka 2

parituṣṭo asmi bhadram te rājaputra mahāyaśaḥ |
prītyā paramayā yukto dadāmi astrāṇi sarvaśaḥ || 1-27-2

2. mahā yashaḥ rājaputra = great, glorious, prince; pari tuṣṭaḥ asmi = highly pleased, I am; te bhadram = to you, let safety be; paramayā prītyā yuktaḥ = utmost, affection, along with; astrāṇi sarvashaḥ dadāmi = divine missiles, all of them, I will give.

"I am highly pleased with you, oh, illustrious prince Rama, let safety be with you, I will now give you all of the divine missiles in utmost fondness of mine. [1-27-2]

Sloka 3

deva asura gaṇān vā api sa gandharva uragān bhuvi |
yaiḥ amitrān prasahya ājau vaśīkṛtya jayiṣyasi || 1-27-3
tāni divyāni bhadram te dadāmi astrāṇi sarvaśaḥ |

3, 4a. yaiḥ = by which - weapons; deva asura gaṇān vā api = gods, demons, scores of, or, even; gandharva uragān = gandharva-s, urugā-s; bhuvi = [or that are] on earth - earhling; a mitrān = un, friendly ones - enemies; ājau = in war; prasahya vashīkṛitya = by dominating, humbled down; jayiṣyasi = conquers; tāni divyāni = those, the divine [weaponry]; astrāṇni sarvashaḥ = missiles, all of them; dadāmi = I am going to give; bhadram te = safety, be with you.

"I am going to accord all of those divine missiles by which scores of gods, demons, or even from gandharva-s, uragā-s and the like, or even earthlings are dominated, humbled down and conquered in war. Let safety be with you. [1-27-3, 4a]

▼

aiṣīka missile is dried blade of grass, which by invoking with hymns will become a projectile weapon. Rama uses on Kakasura. In ṃahā Bharata Ashvadhāma also uses this at the final stage of war, which Krishna nullifies.

Sloka 7

gade dve caiva kākutstha modakī śikharī śubhe || 1-27-7
pradīpte naraśārdūla prayacchāmi nṛpāatmaja |

7b, 8a. kākutstha = oh, Rama; nara shārdūla = manly-tiger; nṛipa ātmaja = king's son - prince; pradīpte = radiant ones [the maces]; dve gade caiva = two, maces, also, thus; modakī shikharī subhe = ṃodaki - Beater, ṣhikhari - ṭower of Protection brilliant ones; pra yacchāmi = well, I accord.

"Oh manly tiger prince Rama, I am going to accord two maces that are brilliant by their radiance called, the ṃodaki, [the Beater,] and Shikhari, [the Tower of Protection.] [1-27-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8

dharma pāśam aham rāma kāla pāśam tathaiva ca || 1-27-8
vāruṇam pāśam astram ca dadāmi aham anuttamam |

8b, 9a. rāma = Rama; aham = I; dharma pāsham = dharma, noose [Virtue-noose]; tathaiva ca = like that, also; kāla pāsham = Kala, the noose [ṭime-noose]; vāruṇam pāsham = Rain god's, noose; anuttamam astram ca = unequalled, missile, also [of Rain-god]; dadāmi = I will give.

"Oh, Rama, like that I will be giving three nooses called Dharma pāsha and kāla-pāsha and Varuna pāsha, [Virtue noose and Time noose, Rain god's noose] as well as an unequalled missile called Varuna astra, Rain-god's missile. [1-27-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

aśanī dve prayacchāmi śuṣka ārdre raghunandana || 1-27-9
dadāmi ca astram painākam astram nārāyaṇam tathā |

9b, 10a. raghunandana = Raghu's scion; shuṣka = ṣhushka, [Drier]; ārdre = Aardra, [Drencher]; dve = two of them; ashanī = bolts; painākam astram = Pināka, missile; pra yacchāmi = well, I give; tathā = likewise; nārāyaṇam astram ca dadāmi = ṇarayana, missile, also, I will give.

"Oh, Raghu's scion, I will also give two projectile bolts, one called shushka, [The Drier] and the other ārdra, [The Drencher,] and even the missiles of Pināka and that of Narayana, [the missiles of god Shiva and Narayana,] I will give. [1-27-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

āgneyam astram dayitam śikharam nāma nāmataḥ || 1-27-10

10b, 11a. anagha = oh, impeccable one; nāmataḥ = one which is well-known one; shikharam nāma = the ṭower, known as; dayitam = [Fire-god's] fond; āgneyam astram = one belonging to Fire-god, missile; prathamam nāma = Prathama [Blower,] known as; vāyavyam [astram] = Air-god's, [missile]; tava = to you; dadāmi = I will give.

"Oh, impeccable Rama, thus a well-known missile shikhari [The Tower,] and another the fond of Fire-god known as prathama [The Blower,] and a missile of Air-god too, I will be giving. [1-27-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11

astram hayaśiraḥ nāma krauñcam astram tathaiva ca || 1-27-11
śakti dvayam ca kākutstha dadāmi tava rāghava |

11b, 12a. kākutstha = of Kakutstha-s; rāghava = Raghava; astram haya shiraḥ nāma = missile, ḥaya-shira [ḥorse-head,] named; tathaiva ca = like that, also; krauncam astram = Krouncha [Wrester,] missile; shakti dvayam ca = impellers, two, also; tava dadāmi = to you, I will be giving.

"Oh, Raghava of Kakutstha-s, I will give you two missiles named Haya-shira [The Horse-head] and Krouncha [The Wrester,] and two Impellers, one presided over by the power of Vishnu, and the other by the power of Rudra. [1-27-11b, 12a]

Sloka 12

kaṃkālam musalam ghoram kāpālam atha kinkiṇīm || 1-27-12
vadhārtham rākṣasām yāni dadāmi etāni sarvaśaḥ |

12b, 13a. ghoram kankālam musalam = deadly, Kankala named, Pounder; kāpālam = Kapālam named Rod; atha = next; kankaṇam = Kankanam named Rod; yāni = which are; [asurāḥ dhārayanti = by demons, wielded]; rākshasām vadha artham = demons, elimination, for purpose of; etāni = such of them; sarvashaḥ dadāmi = all of them, I will give.

"I will be giving all those weapons that are wielded by demons, namely the deadly Pounder named Kankālam, and Rods named as Kapālam and Kankanam, for the elimination of those very demons. [1-27-12b, 13a]

Sloka 13

13b, 14a. mahābāhuḥ = mighty armed one; nṛI vara ātmaja = best, king's, son, [the prince]; vaidyādharam = Vaidyadhara named as; mahā astram ca = great, missile, also; nāmataḥ nandanam = ṇandana; nāma = by name, ṇandana, named as; asi ratnam = sword, gemlike one; dadāmi = I am giving.

"Oh, mighty armed son of the best king, I am giving a great missile named Vaidyadhara by its name, and a gem of sword named Nandana. [1-27-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

14b, 15a. rāghava = Raghava; gāndharvam astram = Gandharva's, missiles; nāmataḥ = by name; mohanam nāma = mohana, named as; dayitam = liked very much by Gandharva-s; prasvāpana = Prasvapana [Inducer of ṣleep]; saumyam prashamanam ca = gentle, Prashamana, [Pacifier,] also.

"Thus, the missile which is very much liked by Gandharva-s, namely ṃohana, [The Stupefier,] the missile called Prasvapana [The Inducer of Sleep,] and the gentle Prashamana, [The Pacifier of enemy's anger.] [1-27-14b, 15a]

The nature of some astra-s - missiles

▼

There are some verses that depict the nature of these astra-s. A few of them are given under. Though we may not actually acquire a projectile power with these verses, they may at least detail what these missiles are. The source of these verses is untraceable, but they are said to be in purāṇā-s, and Shiva informs Sage Narada, about the missiles as below:

brahmāstram

vāntam vahni samāyuktam vyomahālā samanvitam |
meṣha dvayam danta yutam hālāhalam ataḥ param |
ghana ādyam vāyu pūrvam ca danta yuktam athānvitam |
saram ṛikṣha poaryāyam bhāntam bhṛigu mataḥ param ||
ambaram vāyu sa~Nyuktaḥ ari mardanam apyutaḥ |
pradīptam atha avaktavyam paramam ca padam tataḥ |
tat ete pade prayoktavye gāyatryā madhyamam tataḥ |
pada trayam prayoktavyam etat brahma astram īritam ||

▼

"It contains air, fire and cosmic poison, two goat-like fangs, full of poison, weighty, emits air, contains mercury, fiery, sparkling, sky is filled with air, enemy-killing, greatly radiant and it is projected with three hymns, Gayatri at its centre, it is known as brahma-astra

kāla cakram

karuṇam māyayā yuktam vāntam vāruṇa sa~Nyutam |
yūpāntam vahninā yuktam modhasāgni rathā triyuk |
dhāntam kāla ātmane iti etat cakrā eti param tataḥ |
phaDantam eṣha mantrastu kāla cakram prakītritam ||

"Renders a piteous state of the enemy, full with magical powers, ending in air, and water filled, ends in craters with fire, generates fire and and it is known as Time disc"

viṣhṇu cakram

tāra pūrvam ca hṛidayam bhāgyāt antau vana ādimaḥ |
caṇDīsha yukta āṣhāDha tīvra tejasa iti api |
paryāyam lakṣha sa~Nkhyāyāḥ āreti ca padam tataḥ |
bhīṣhayeti api vaktavyam viṣhṇu cakram idam viduḥ ||

"It first sparkles with cosmic holes, contains high degree radiance, revolves with one lakh revolutions per foot, very panicking is its projection"

Verse Locator

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye bāla kāṇḍe saptaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 27th chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
