Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 47
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The multitude that had caused Rama in his journey to the forest wake up to find Rama and his associates gone. They begin to rebuke themselves. Overwhelmed with grief, they hunt up to tracks of Rama's chariot. Being unable to find the tracks, they helplessly return to Ayodhya in sheer despair.

Sloka 1

prabhātāyām tu śarvaryām paurāḥ te rāghavo vinā |
śoka upahata niśceṣṭā babhūvur hata cetasaḥ || 2-47-1

1. sharvaryām = As the night; prabhatāyām = was beginning to become day light; te pourāḥ = those citizens; rāghavam vinā = relinquished by Rama; babhūvaḥ = became; hatachetasaḥ = perplexed; shokopahatachetasaḥ = their hearts stricken by grief; nishcheshhṭāḥ = and motionless.

As the night was beginning to become dawn, those citizens relinquished by Raghava were perplexed with grief and became motionless.

Sloka 2

śokaja aśru paridyūnā vīkṣamāṇāḥ tataḥ tataḥ |
ālokam api rāmasya na paśyanti sma duhkhitāḥ || 2-47-2

2. shokajāshru paridyūnāḥ = ṃade miserable with tears born of anguish; duḥkhitāḥ = and agony; vīkshhamāṇāḥ = seeing; tatastataḥ = in that and that place; na pashyantisma = they could not notice; ālokamapi = even a glimmer; rāmasya = of Rama.

ṃade miserable with tears born of anguish and agony, they could not notice even a glimmer of Rama, though casting their eyes everywhere.

Sloka 3

te viṣādārtavadanā rahitāstena dhimatā |
kṛpaṇāḥ karuṇā vāco vadanti sma manasvinaḥ || 2-47-3

3. te = ṭhey; manasvinaḥ = the magnanimous; rahitāḥ = bereft of ; tena = that Rama; dhīmatā = the intelligent man; vishhadārta vadanāḥ = had their faces afflicted with grief; kṛipaṇāḥ = and non-plused; vadantisma = spoke; vachāḥ = these words; karuṇāḥ = which were compassionate.

Their faces distorted with sorrow, deprived as they were of sagacious Rama and therefore non-pulsed, the citizens broke into piteous exclamations, saying:-

Sloka 4

dhigastu khalu nidrām tām yayāpahṛtacetasaḥ |
nādya paśyāmahe rāmam pṛthūraskam mahābhujam || 2-47-4

4. dhik astu = cursed be; tām nidrām = that slumber; yayā = by which; apahṛitachetasaḥ = consciousness was taken away; na pashyāmahe = and could not behold;adya = today; pṛithūraskam = broad chested; mahābhujam = and mighty armed; rāmam = Rama."

"Cursed be to that slumber, rendered unconscious, by which we could not behold today that mighty armed Rama with a broad chest."

Sloka 5

katham nāma mahābāhuḥ sa tathā.avitathakriyaḥ |
bhaktam janam parityajya pravāsam rāghavo gataḥ || 2-47-5

5. katham nāma = "how; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; mahābāhuḥ = the strong armed; avitatha kriyaḥ = whose actions are never in ineffectual; gataḥ = leave; pravāsam = for exile; parityajya = abandoning; bhaktam = his devoted; janam = people?"

"How that Rama the strong armed, whose actions are never ineffectual, leave for exile, abandoning his devoted citizens?"

Sloka 6

yo naḥ sadā pālayati pitā putrānivaurasān |
katham raghūṇām sa śreṣṭhastyaktvā no vipinam gataḥ || 2-47-6

6. "yaḥ = which Rama; shreshhṭaḥ = the chief; raghūṇām = of Raghu's; pālayati = who protected; naḥ = us; sadā = always; pita = like father; ourasān putrān iva = the children born of his lions; katham = how; saḥ = he; tyaktvā = could leave; naḥ = us; gataḥ = and proceed; vipinam = to the forest?"

"How did the chief of Raghus, who protected us ever, like a father the children born of his loins, could proceed to the forest, leaving us?"

Sloka 7

ihaiva nidhanam yāmo mahāprasthānameva vā |
rāmeṇa rahitānām hi kimartham jīvitam hi naḥ || 2-47-7

7. "yāmaḥ = (let us) invoke; nidhanam = death; ihaiva = here itself; mahā prasthānameva vā = or definitely set out for a grand journey( to the north with a resolve to die) kimartham = for what purpose; jīvatam = (can be) life; naḥ = for us; rahitānām = separated; rāmeṇa = from Rama."

"Let us have recourse to death here itself, or definitely set out for a grand journey ( to the north with a resolve to die). For what purpose can life be good for us, separated as we are from Rama?"

Sloka 8

santi śuṣkāṇi kāṣṭhāni prabhūtāni mahānti ca |
taiḥ prajvālya citām sarve praviśāmo.atha pāvakam || 2-47-8

8. "atha = or; santi = there are; prabhūtāni = a number of; mahānti = big; shushhkāṇi = dry; kāshhṭāni = logs of woods. prajvalya = Lighting; chitām = a funeral pile; taiḥ = by them; sarve = let us all; pravishāmaḥ = enter; pāvakam = the fire."

" Or there are a number of big dry logs of wood here. Lighting a funeral fire out of them, let us all enter the fire."

Sloka 9

kim vkhsyāmo mahābāhuranasūyaḥ priyamvada |
nītaḥ sa rāghavo.asmābhirti vaktum katham kṣamam || 2-47-9

9. kim = what; vakshhyāmaḥ = shall we say? Asmābhiḥ = By us; mahābāhuḥ = the great armed; saḥ rāghavaḥ = (that ) Rama; anasūyaḥ = who is free from egoism; priyamvadaḥ = and who speaks kindly (to all); nītaḥ = has been conveyed (to the forest); kshhamam = ḥow can we; vaktum = say; iti = so?"

"What shall we say to our fellow citizens? �The great armed Rama who is free from egoism and who speaks kindly ( to all ) has been conveyed to the forest by us!' How can we say so?"

Sloka 10

sā nūnam nagarī dīnā dṛṣṭvā.asmān rāghavam vinā |
bhaviṣyati nirānandā sastrībālavayodhikā || 2-47-10

10. "dṛishhṭvā = seeing; asmān = us; rāghavam vinā = bereft of Rama; sā nagarī = that city; nūnam = now; dīnā = desolate; sa strī bāla vayodhikā = together with women and children and the aged; bhavishhyati = will become; nirānandā = cheerless."

"Seeing us returning without Rama, that city of Ayodhya, already desolate, will with its women, children and the aged, become even deeply cheerless."

Sloka 11

niryātāstena vīreṇa saha nityam jitātmanā |
vihināstena ca punaḥ katham paśyāma tām purīm || 2-47-11

11. "niryātāḥ = having left the city; tena vīreṇa saha = thus with that hero; nityam = ever; jitātmanā = self-conquered man; katham = how; pashyāma = can we look on; tām purīm = that city; punaḥ = again; vihīnāḥ = without; tena cha = him?"

"Having left the city thus with that hero, the conqueror of one's own self, how can we look on that city again without him?"

Sloka 12

itīva bahudhā vāco bāhumudyamya te janāḥ |
vilapantisma duḥkhartā vivatsā iva dhenavaḥ || 2-47-12

12. udyamya = uplifting; bāhum = (their) arms; te janāḥ = those men; duhkhārtāḥ = stricken with anguish; dhenavaḥ = like cows; vivatsāḥ = bereft of their calves; vilapantisma = lamented; bahudhā = in various ways; itīva vāchaḥ = as aforesaid.

Uplifting their arms , those men stricken with anguish, like cows bereft of their calves, lamented in various ways as above.

Sloka 13

tataḥ mārga anusāreṇa gatvā kiṃcit kṣaṇam punaḥ
mārga nāśāt viṣādena mahatā samabhiplutaḥ || 2-47-13

13. tataḥ = then; gatvā = proceeding; kimchit = to some distance; mārgānusāreṇa = along the tracks; kshhaṇam = for a while; samabhiplutāḥ = they were overwhelmed with; mahatā = great; vishhadena = despair; punaḥ = again; mārganāshāt = due to disappearance of the tracks.

Then, proceeding to some distance along the tracks for a while, they were overwhelmed with great despair again due to sudden disappearance of tracks.

Sloka 14

rathasya mārga nāśena nyavartanta manasvinaḥ |
kim idam kim kariṣyāmaḥ daivena upahatāiti || 2-47-14

14. manasvinaḥ = the good natured citizens; mārga nāshena = due to disappearance of the tracks; rathasya = of the chariot; nyavartanta = returned (to Ayodhyas) iti = saying thus; kimidam = "how is it? kim karishhyāma = what shall we do? Upa hatāḥ = we are afflicted; daivena = by providence.

The good-natured citizens, due to disappearance of the tracks of the chariot, returned to Ayodhya, saying thus, " How is it? What shall we do? We are afflicted by providence"

Sloka 15

tataḥ yathā āgatena eva mārgeṇa klānta cetasaḥ |
ayodhyām agaman sarve purīm vyathita sajjanām || 2-47-15

15. tataḥ = thereafter; sarve = all of them; klānta chetanaḥ = with aggrieved hearts; āgaman = went; āyodhya purīm = to the city of Ayodhya; vyathita sajjanām = with distressed virtuous people; mārgeṇa = by the path; yathāgatenaiva = on which they had come.

Therafter, all of them with aggrieved hearts went to the city of Ayodhya, which was comprising of virtuous people with distress, by the same path on which they had come.

Sloka 16

ālokya nagarīm tām ca kṣayavyākulamānasāḥ |
āvartayanta t.aśrūṇi nayanaiḥ śokapīḍitaiḥ || 2-47-16

16. ālokya = beholding; tām nagarīm = that city; te = they; kshhayavyākula mānasāḥ = in their minds distracted through cheerlessness; āvartayanta = shed; ashrūṇi = tears; shoka pīditaiḥ = stricken as they were in grief.

Beholding the city, they shed tears through their eyes stricken as they were in grief , their minds distressed through cheerlessness.

Sloka 17

eṣā rāmeṇa nagarī rahitā nātiśobhate |
āpagā garuḍeneva hradāduddhṛtapannagā || 2-47-17

17. eshhā nagarī = this city; rāmeṇa rahitā = bereft of Rama; nātishobhate = was not enchanting even a little; āpagā iva = as a river; uddhhṛita pannagā = whose snakes were uplifted; hradāt = from its pool; garuDena = by Garuda.

Bereft of Rama, the aforesaid city of Ayodhya was not any more enchanting than a river whose snakes were uplifted from its pool by Garuda.

Sloka 18

candrahīnamivākāśam toyahīnamivārṇavam |
apaśyannihatānandam nagaram te vicetasaḥ || 2-47-18

18. ākāshamiva = as the sky; chandra hīnam = without the moon; ārṇavam iva = or the sea; toya hīnam = without the water; te vichetasaḥ = those disconcerted men; apashyan = beheld; nagaram = the city; nihatānandam = from which all joy had fled.

As the sky without the moon or the sea without the water, these disconcerted men beheld the city from which all joy had fled.

Sloka 19

te tāni veśmāni mahādhanāni |
duḥkhena duḥkhopahatā viśantaḥ |
naiva prajajñuḥ svajanam janam vā |
nirīkṣamāṇāḥ praviṇaṣṭaharṣāḥ || 2-47-19

19. te = they; praviṇashhṭa harshhāḥ = having lost their joy; vishantaḥ = entering; tāni = those; veshmāni = houses; mahādhanāni = with abundant riches ; duḥkhena = uneasily; duḥkhopahatāḥ = having stricken with grief; naiva prajaG^yuḥ = could not dishtinguish; svajanam = their own people; janam vā = or others; nirīkshhamānāḥ = (even if) being looked at.

Entering their houses full of abundant riches with uneasiness, the citizens could not distinguish between their own people and others, even though being looked at, stricken as they were with grief, their joy having altogether extinguished.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes the 47th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda in the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
