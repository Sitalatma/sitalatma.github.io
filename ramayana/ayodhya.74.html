Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 74
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Bharata censures Kaikeyi saying that because of her, Dasaratha is dead and Rama is sent to exile. He abuses his mother of her greed for the kingdom and the misfortune thus brought to Kausalya, Sumitra and other mothers because of his father's death and brother's exile to the forest. He compares the grief of the Kausalya with the grief of Kamadhenu the mythical cow who was aggrieved of a pair of bullocks (her sons) being harassed by a plough -man. Bharata then promises that he would bring Rama back to Ayodhya and make him as a king.

Sloka 1

tām tathā garhayitvā tu mātaram bharataḥ tadā |
roṣeṇa mahatā āviṣṭaḥ punar eva abravīd vacaḥ || 2-74-1

1. tadā = then; bharataḥ = Bharata; garhayitvā = reproaching; tām = her; tathā = in that manner; punareva = again; abravīt = spoke; vachaḥ = these words; āvishhṭaḥ = wrapped as he was; mahatāroshheṇa = in great anger.

Reproaching Kaikeyi in that manner, Bharata again spoke the following words, wrapped as he was in great anger.

Sloka 2

rājyāt bhraṃśasva kaikeyi nṛśaṃse duṣṭa cāriṇi |
parityaktā ca dharmeṇa mā mṛtam rudatī bhava || 2-74-2

2. kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi; nṛishamse; the cruel; dushhṭachāriṇi = and evil mannered woman! bhramshasva = Get lost; rājyāt = from the kingdom; parityaktā = you; having abandoned; dharmeṇa = righteousness; bhava = remain; rudatī = lamenting; mā = about me; mṛitam = who will be dead.

"O, Kaikeyi! The cruel and evil-mannered woman! Get lost from this kingdom. You having abandoned righteousness, remain lamenting about me, who will be dead soon."

Sloka 3

kim nu te adūṣayad rājā rāmaḥ vā bhṛśa dhārmikaḥ |
yayoḥ mṛtyur vivāsaḥ ca tvat kṛte tulyam āgatau || 2-74-3

3. kimnu = what; adūshhayat = discredit; te = to you; rājā = either the king; rāmovā = or Rama; bhṛishadhārmikaḥ = the highly righteous man (have done);yayoḥ = to whom; mṛityuḥ = death; vivāsashcha = and exile; āgatou = have come; tulyam = at once; tvatkṛite = because of you.

"What harm king Dasaratha or the highly righteous Rama have done to you so harshly that Dasaratha's death and Rama's exile have occurred at one and the same time because of you?"

Sloka 4

bhrūṇahatyām asi prāptā kulasya asya vināśanāt |
kaikeyi narakam gacca mā ca bhartuḥ salokatām || 2-74-4

4. kaikeyi = O;Kaikeyi! Prāpta asi = You got; bhrūṇa hatyām = the sin of killing an embryo. Vināshanāt = because of the destruction; asyakulasya = of this race; gachchha = go; narakam = to hell; Kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi! ṃā cha = Do not get; salokatām = residence in the same heaven; bhartuḥ = with your husband.

"O, Kaikeyi! You got the sin of killing an embryo because of the destruction of this race. O, Kaikeyi! Go to hell .Do not get the residence in the same heaven as your husband."

Sloka 5

yattvayā hīdṛśam pāpam kṛtam ghoreṇa karmaṇā |
sarvalokapriyam hitvā mamāpyāpāditam bhayam || 2-74-5

5. ghoreṇa karmaṇā = by which terrific act; yat = wherefore; īdṛisham = such; pāpam = a sin; kṛitam = was committed; tvayā = by you; hitvā = by forsaking; sarvalokapriyam = the persons beloved by all; bhayam = an awe; āpaditan = is created; mamāpi = in me also.

"You have done this terrific act and committed such a great sin. By forsaking the persons beloved by all, an alarm has been created in me also."

Sloka 6

tvat kṛte me pitā vṛttaḥ rāmaḥ ca araṇyam āśritaḥ |
ayaśo jīva loke ca tvayā aham pratipāditaḥ || 2-74-6

6. me pitā = my father; vṛittaḥ = died; tvatkṛite = because of you; rāmashcha = Rama; āshritaḥ = is dwelling; araṇyam = in a forest; aham = I; pratipāditaḥ = am caused to attain; ayashaḥcha = ill fame; jīvaloke = in this world of beings; tvayā = by you.

"ṃy father died and Rama is dwelling in a forest, because of you. You got me ill fame in this world of beings."

Sloka 7

mātṛ rūpe mama amitre nṛśaṃse rājya kāmuke |
na te aham abhibhāṣyo asmi durvṛtte pati ghātini || 2-74-7

7. amitre = O; belligerent woman; mama meatṛirūpe = in the form of my mother! ṇṛishamse = O; cruel woman; rājyakāmuke = O; woman greedy of kingdom; durvṛitte = of evil conduct; patimātini = and who killed the husband! Aham = I; nāsmi = am never; abhibhāshhyaḥ = to address; te = you.

"Though in appearance you are my mother, you are inimical to me. You are a cruel woman, greedy of kingdom. With evil conduct, you killed your husband. I should never speak to you."

Sloka 8

kausalyā ca sumitrā ca yāḥ ca anyā mama mātaraḥ |
duhkhena mahatā āviṣṭāḥ tvām prāpya kula dūṣiṇīm || 2-74-8

8. kausalyā = Kausalya; sumitrācha = ṣumitra; mama = and my; anyāḥ = other; mātaraḥ = mothers; āvishhṭāḥ = are engrossed; mahatā = in a great; duḥkhena = misfortune; yāḥ = wherefore; prāpya = by falling victim; tvām = to you; kuladūshhiṇīm = who brought disgrace to our family.

"Kausalya, Sumitra and my other mothers are engrossed in a great misfortune, by falling victim to you, who brought disgrace to our family."

Sloka 9

na tvam aśva pateḥ kanyā dharma rājasya dhīmataḥ |
rākṣasī tatra jātā asi kula pradhvaṃsinī pituḥ || 2-74-9
yat tvayā dhārmiko rāmaḥ nityam satya parāyaṇaḥ |
vanam prasthāpitaḥ duhkhāt pitā ca tridivam gataḥ || 2-74-10

9; 10. yat = by what reason; rāmah = Rama; dhārmikaḥ = the righteous man; nityam satyaparāyaṇaḥ = who is forever interested in truth; prasthāpitaḥ = was sent; vanam = to the forest; pitācha = and my father; gataḥ = went; tridivam = to heaven; duḥkhāt = because of grief; For that reason; tvām = you; na = are not; kanyā = the daughter; ashvapateḥ = of Asvapati; dharma rājasya = the pious king; dhīmataḥ = and a sensible man; asi = you were; jātā = born; tatra = there; rākshhasī = as a demo ness; kulapradhvamsinī = to destroy the house; pituḥ = of my father

"The righteous Rama, who is forever devoted to truth, was sent to the forest. Due to grief for his son, my father went to heaven. You do not seem to be the daughter of Asvapati, the pious and sensible king. You were born there as a demo ness, to destroy the house of my father."

Sloka 11

yat pradhānā asi tat pāpam mayi pitrā vinā kṛte |
bhrātṛbhyām ca parityakte sarva lokasya ca apriye || 2-74-11

11. yatpradhānāsi = by which sin you are mainly having; tat = that; pāpam = sin; kṛita = has made me; pitrāvinā = father less; parityakte = abandoned; bhrātṛibhyām = by my brothers; mayi apriyecha = and I was disliked; sarvalokasya = by all the people.

"The aforesaid sin you have committed has made me father less. Besides, I have been abandoned by my brothers and all the people dislike me now."

Sloka 12

kausalyām dharma samyuktām viyuktām pāpa niścaye |
kṛtvā kam prāpsyase tu adya lokam niraya gāminī || 2-74-12

12. pāpanishchaye = O; woman having evil desires! ṇirayagāmini = moving towards hell! Kam lokam = which world; prāpsyase = will you attain; adya = now; kṛityā = after making; kausalyām = Kausalya; dharma samyuktām = endowed with righteousness; viyuktām = deprived of her son?

"O, woman having evil desires, moving towards hell! Which world will you attain now, after making Kausalya endowed with righteousness, deprived of her son?"

Sloka 13

kim na avabudhyase krūre niyatam bandhu saṃśrayam |
jyeṣṭham pitṛ samam rāmam kausalyāya ātma sambhavam || 2-74-13

13. krūre = O; cruel woman! kim na avabuddhyase = don't you know ; rāmam = that Rama; ātmasambhavam = the son; kausalyāyāḥ = of Kausalya; niyatam = as self subdued; bandhu samshrayam = who is devoted to his relatives; jyeshhṭham = the eldest brother; pitrṛisamam = and equal to the father?

"O, cruel woman! Don't you know that Rama the son of Kausalya is a subdued man, who is devoted to his relatives and as an eldest brother, equal to a father?"

Sloka 14

anga pratyangajaḥ putraḥ hṛdayāc ca api jāyate |
tasmāt priyataraḥ mātuḥ priyatvān na tu bāndhavaḥ || 2-74-14

14. putraḥ = a son; aṇ^ga pratyaṇ^gajaḥ = is born from primary limbs and secondary members of the body ; jāyate = and born; hṛidayāchchāpi = from the heart too; tamāt = for that reason; (he is) priyatamaḥ = the most beloved; mātuḥ = to a mother; bāndhavāḥ = the other relatives; priyā evatu = are only like friends.

"A son is the most beloved to his mother as he is born from the primary and secondary limbs of her body and also from her heart. The other relatives are only like friends."

Sloka 15

anyadā kila dharmajñā surabhiḥ sura sammatā |
vahamānau dadarśa urvyām putrau vigata cetasau || 2-74-15

15. anyadā = once upon a time; surabhiḥ = Kamadhenu; the mythical cow of Vasista; dharmaj^ṇa = who knew righteousness; surasammatā = and worshipped by celestials; dadarshaka = is said to have seen; putrou = her sons; vahamānou = dreaming having burden; urvayām = on earth; vigatachetasou = and became unconscious.

"Once upon a time, Kamadhenu the mythical cow of Vasista, who knew righteousness and was worshipped by celestials, is said to have seen her sons, drawing a heavy burden on earth and became unconscious."

Sloka 16

tāv ardha divase śrāntau dṛṣṭvā putrau mahī tale |
ruroda putra śokena bāṣpa paryākula īkṣaṇā || 2-74-16

16. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; putrou = the sons; shrāntou = fatigued with labor; ardhadivasam = for a half part of their day; mahītale = on earth; ruroda = cried; bāshhpa paryākulekshhaṇā = with eyes full of tears; putra shokena = in grief for the fate of her sons.

"Seeing her sons (a pair of bullocks) fatigued, after toiling for half a part of their day on earth, Kamadhenu the mythical cow cried with her eyes full of tears in grief for the fate of her sons."

Sloka 17

adhastāt vrajataḥ tasyāḥ sura rājño mahātmanaḥ |
bindavaḥ patitā gātre sūkṣmāḥ surabhi gandhinaḥ || 2-74-17

17. tasyāḥ = that mythical cow's; sūkshhmāḥ = small; bindavaḥ = tear drops; surabhi gandhinaḥ = bearing good smell; patitaḥ = fell; gātre = on the limbs; mahātmanaḥ = of the high soled; surarājṇ^aḥ = Indra the god of celestials; vrajataḥ = who was traveling; adhastāt = in the lower region.

"Small and sweet smelling tear drops of that mythical cow fell on the limbs of the high soled Indra the lord of celestials, who was traveling below in a lower region."

Sloka 18

indro.apyaśrunipātam tam svagātre puṇyagandhinam |
surabhim manyate dṛṣṭvā bhūyasīm tām sureśvaraḥ || 2-74-18

18. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam = those; puṇ^yagandhinam = sweet scented; ashrupātam = tears falling; svagātre = on his limbs; indro api = Indra; sureshvarāḥ = the lord of celestials; manyate = thought; tām = her; bhūmyasīm = to be the great; surabhim = Kamadhenu the mythical cow.

"Seeing those sweet- scented tears falling on his limbs, Indra the Lord of celestials identified the tears to be those of the great Kamadhenu the mythical cow."

Sloka 19

nirīksamāṇaḥ śakrastām dadarśa surabhim sthitām |
ākāśe viṣṭhitām dīnām rudatīm bhṛśaduḥkhitām || 2-74-19

19. nirīkshhamāṇaḥ = looking; ākāshe = into the sky; shakraḥ = Indra; dadarsha = saw; tām surabhim = that Kamadhenu; sthitām = standing there; vishhṭhitām = with anguish; dīnām = and pitiably; rudatīm = crying; bhṛishaduḥkhitām = with great sorrow.

"Looking into the sky, Indra saw that Kamadhenu standing there with anguish and pitiably weeping with great grief."

Sloka 20

tām dṛṣṭvā śoka samtaptām vajra pāṇir yaśasvinīm |
indraḥ prānjalir udvignaḥ sura rājo abravīd vacaḥ || 2-74-20

20. surarājaḥ = the lord of celestials; indraḥ = Indra; vajraḥ pāṇiḥ = with a thunder bolt in his hand; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tām = that Kamadhenu the mythical cow; yashashvinīm = a beautiful one who was tormented with grief; abravīt = spoke; prāṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms; udvignaḥ = anxious as he was; vākyam = the following words:

"Indra the lord of celestials with a thunder bolt in his hand, seeing that beautiful Kamadhenu the mythical cow, tormented with grief, spoke eagerly with joined palms to her, as follows:

Sloka 21

bhayam kaccin na ca asmāsu kutaścit vidyate mahat |
kutaḥ nimittaḥ śokaḥ te brūhi sarva hita eṣiṇi || 2-74-21

21. sarvahitaishhiṇi = O; cow the well wisher of all ; na vidyate kachchit = Is there not indeed; kutashchit = from any where; mahat = a great; bhayam = panic; asmāsu = to us? Kutonimittam = for what occasion te = is your; shokaḥ sorrow?

"O, cow the well wisher of all! I hope there is no great panic from any quarter to us. For what occasion is your sorrow?"

Sloka 22

evam uktā tu surabhiḥ sura rājena dhīmatā |
patyuvāca tataḥ dhīrā vākyam vākya viśāradā || 2-74-22

22. evam = thus; uktā = spoken; dhīmatā = by the intelligent; surarājena = Indra the god of celestials; dhīrā = the prudent; surabhiḥ = Kamadhenu; vākya vishāradam = who was skilled in expressing words; tataḥ = then; pratyuvācha = replied; vākyam = the following words:

"Hearing the words of the intelligent Indra the god of celestials, the prudent Kamadhenu who was skilled in expressing words, replied as follows:

Sloka 23

śāntam pātam na vaḥ kiṃcit kutaścit amara adhipa |
aham tu magnau śocāmi sva putrau viṣame sthitau || 2-74-23
etau dṛṣṭvā kṛṣau dīnau sūrya raśmi pratāpinau |
ardhyamānau balī vardau karṣakeṇa sura adhipa || 2-74-24

23. pāpam shāntam = ḥeaven forefend the evil! Amarādhiapa = O; Indra the Lord of celestials! ṇa = there is no; kashchit = danger whatsoever; vaḥ = to you; kitashchit = from anywhere; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; etou = these; balivardou = pair of bullocks; svaputram = my sons; sthitou = who are; vishhame = in hardship; sūryarashmi pratāpitou = who are being scorched by sun's rays; kṛishou = who became weak; ardyamānou = being trobled; karshhakeṇa = by the one who ploughs; magnou = and immersed in grief ; aham = I; dīna = am pitiably; shochāmi = weeping ; surādhipa = O; Indra!

"Heaven forefend that evil! O, Indra the lord of celestials! There is no danger whatsoever to you from any quarter. Seeing these pair of bullocks, my sons, who are in hardship, being scorched by sun's rays, becoming weak, being troubled by the man who ploughs the land and being immersed in grief, I am pitiably weeping O, Indra!"

Sloka 25

mama kāyāt prasūtau hi duhkhitau bhāra pīḍitau |
yau dṛṣṭvā paritapye aham na asti putra samaḥ priyaḥ || 2-74-25

25. Dr^ishhṭvā = by seeing; you = whom; bhārapīDitou = that are afflicted with burden; duḥkhitou = and aggrieved; aham = I; paritapye = am anguished; (they) prasūtāḥ hi = are indeed born; mama = from my; kāyāt = body; nāsti = there is no; priyaḥ = dearest; putrā samah = equal to a son.

"By seeing them who are afflicted with the burden and aggrieved, I am greatly anguished. They are indeed born of my body. There is no dearest one equal to a son indeed!"

Sloka 26

yasyāḥ putra sahastraistu kṛtsnam vyāptamidam jagat |
tām dṛṣṭvā rudatīm śakro na sutānmanyate param || 2-74-26

26. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tām = such a sacred cow; rudatīm = weeping; yasyāḥ = whose; putrashataiḥ = hundreds of sons; vyāptam = pervaded; idam jagat = throughout this world; shakraḥ = indra ; na manyate = reckoned none; param = as more than; sutāt = a son(to a mother).

Seeing such a sacred cow weeping, whose hundreds of sons pervaded the entire world, Indra reckoned none whosoever as more than a son (to a mother) .

Sloka 27

sadā.apratimavṛttāyā lokadhāraṇakāmyayā |
śrīmatyā guṇanityāyāḥ svabhāvapariceṣṭayā || 2-74-27
yasyāḥ putrasahasrāṇi sāpi śocai kāmadhuk |
kim punar yā vinā rāmam kausalyā vartayiṣyati || 2-74-28

27. sā = such; kāmadhuk api = Kamadhenu even; loka dhāraṇa kāmyayā = who is desirous of maintaining the world; sadā = and always; apratimavṛttāyāḥ = having unequalled conduct; shrīmatyāḥ = the venerable one; svabhāvaparicheshhṭayā = by its nature functioning ; guṇa nityāyāḥ = forever with good qualities; yasyāḥ = to whom; putrasahasrāṇi = there are thousands of sons; shochati = is lamenting; kimpunaḥ = how much more; yā = indeed; kausalyā = Kausalya; vartayishhyati = will drag her existence; rāmam vinā = without Rama?

"Such a Kamadhenu the sacred cow, which is desirous of maintaining the world always having unequalled behavior, the venerable one, by its very nature functioning forever with good qualities and to whom there are thousands of sons, is lamenting, how much more indeed Kausalya will drag her existence, without Rama?"

Sloka 29

eka putrā ca sādhvī ca vivatsā iyam tvayā kṛtā |
tasmāt tvam satatam duhkham pretya ca iha ca lapsyase || 2-74-29

29. iyam = she; ekaputrācha = who has only one son; sādhvīcha = and who is a holy woman; kṛitā = has been made; vivitsā = without a child; tvayā = by you; tasmāt = therefore; tvam = you; lapsyase = will obtain; duḥkham = grief; satatam = forever; pretyacha = after death; ihacha = and even on earth."

"She, who has only one son and who is a holy woman, has been made without a child by you. Thereafter, you will obtain grief forever after your death or even here while living."

Sloka 30

aham hi apacitim bhrātuḥ pituḥ ca sakalām imām |
vardhanam yaśasaḥ ca api kariṣyāmi na saṃśayaḥ || 2-74-30

30. aham = I on my part; karishhyāmi = shall toil; imām = for this; sampūrṇām = complete; apachitam = compensation ; bhrātuḥ = for my brother; pitushcha = and my father; yashasaḥ = and for their glory; vardhanam = and prosperity ; na samshayaḥ = there is no doubt.

"I for my part, shall toil for the complete reinstatement of my brother and complete the obsequial rites due to my father as well as for their prosperity and glory. There is no doubt."

Sloka 31

ānāyayitvā tanayam kausalyāyā mahā dyutim |
svayam eva pravekṣyāmi vanam muni niṣevitam || 2-74-31

31. kausalyāh tanayam = Rama the son of Kausalya ; mahābalam = of great prowess; ānāyayitvā = will be brought back; svayameva = and I myself; pravekshhyāmi = will enter; vanam = the forest; muninishhevitam = frequented by the sages.

"Rama the son of Kausalya, of great prowess will be brought back to Ayodhya and I, myself will go to the forest inhabited by the sages."

Sloka 32

na hyaham pāpasamkalpe pāpe pāpam tvayā kṛtam |
śakto dhārayitum paurairaśrukaṇṭhai rnirīkṣitaḥ || 2-74-32

32. pāpe = O; wretched one! Pāpasamkalpe = O; the evil minded one! Pouraiḥ = the citizens; nirīkshhitaḥ = being seen; ashrukaṇ^ṭhaiḥ = with their throats filled with tears; nashaktaḥ hi = I am not indeed able; dhārayitum = to endure; pāpam = the inequity; kṛitam = done; tvayā = by you.

"O, wretched and evil minded woman! By seeing the sorrow stricken citizens, I am indeed not able to bear the inequity done by you."

Sloka 33

sā tvamagnim praviśa vā svayam vā daṇḍakānviśa |
rajjum badhāna vā kaṇṭhe na hi te.anyatparāyaṇam || 2-74-33

33. sā tvam = as for you; pravisha = enter; agnimvā = the fire or; visha = enter; svayam = yourself; daṇdakāranyā = into the forest of Dandaka; vā = or; badhāna = bind; rajjum = a rope; kaṇṭhe = around your neck; nahi = ṭhere is indeed no; anyat = other; parāyaṇam = way of recourse; te = to you.

"As for you, enter the fire or you yourself go to forest of Dandaka.or fasten a rope around your neck. There is no other recourse for you."

Sloka 34

ahamapyavanim prāpte rāme satyaparākrame |
kṛtakṛtyo bhaviṣyāmi vipravāsitakalmaṣaḥ || 2-74-34

34. (After) rāme = Rama; satyaparākrame = the truly mighty man; prāpte = obtains; avanim = his native land; ahamapi = I even; bhavishhyāmi = will become; kṛitakṛityaḥ = an accomplished man; vipravāsita kalmashhaḥ = with my sins duly washed away.

"After Rama the truly mighty man, obtains his native land, I will even become an accomplished man, with my sins duly washed away."

Sloka 35

iti nāgaiva araṇye tomara ankuśa coditaḥ |
papāta bhuvi samkruddho nihśvasann iva pannagaḥ || 2-74-35

35. nāgaḥ iva = as an elephant; araṇye = in a forest; choditaḥ = pricked with a javelin and a goad; pannaḥ iva = and as a serpent; niḥshvasan = hissing; (Bharata); samkṛiddhaḥ = was enraged; iti = after speaking in this manner; papāta = and fell; bhuvi = on the ground.

As an elephant in a forest pricked with a javelin and a goad and as a hissing serpent, Bharata was enraged and fell on the ground.

Sloka 36

samrakta netraḥ śithila ambaraḥ tadā |
vidhūta sarva ābharaṇaḥ paramtapaḥ |
babhūva bhūmau patitaḥ nṛpa ātmajaḥ |
śacī pateḥ ketur iva utsava kṣaye || 2-74-36

36. tadā = then; samraktanetraḥ = his eyes inflamed; shithilāmbaraḥ = his clothes in disarray; vidhūtasarvābharaṇaḥ = his all ornaments discarded; nṛipātmajaḥ = Bharata; the prince; paramtapaḥ = the tormentor of foes;patitaḥ = lay; bhūmou = on the earth; babhūva = was ; keturiva = like a banner; shachīpateḥ = of Indra; utsavakshhaye = raised at the end of a ceremony.

His eyes inflamed, his clothes in disarray, and his all ornaments discarded, Bharata the prince and the tormentor of foes, lay on the earth, like a banner of Indra raised at the end of a ceremony**

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe catuḥsaptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 74nd chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
