Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 27
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Seetha asks Rama to take her to the forest.

Sloka 1

evam uktā tu vaidehī priya arhā priya vādinī |
praṇayāt eva samkruddhā bhartāram idam abravīt || 2-27-1

1. vaidehi = ṣita; priya vādinī = who speaks kindly; priyārhā = deserves kind utterances; evam = thus; uktā = spoken; samkruddhā = became angry; praṇayādeva = only because of love; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; bhartāram = to husband.

Sita, who speaks kindly and deserves kind utterances, after hearing Rama's words, became angry out of love alone and spoke thus to her husband.

Sloka 2

kimidam bhāṣase rāma vākyam laghutayā dhruvam |
tvayā yadapahāsyam me śrutvā naravarātmaja || 2-27-2

2. rāma = Oh; Rama; naravarātmaja = the prince! kim vākyam = what words; idam = these; bhāshāse = you are speaking? yat = which words; laghutayā = as they are light; dhruvam = certainly; apahāsyam = are to be laughed at; tvayā = by you; me = and to me; shrutvā = after hearing.

Oh, Rama the prince! What words these are you speaking? These light words certainly are to be laughed at by you and to me after hearning.

Sloka 3

ārya putra pitā mātā bhrātā putraḥ tathā snuṣā |
svāni puṇyāni bhunjānāḥ svam svam bhāgyam upāsate || 2-27-3

3. ārya putra = oh; noble prince! pitā = father; mātā = mother; bhrātā = brother; putraḥ = son; tathā = and snushhā = daughter in law; upāsate = accomplish; svamsvam = their own; bhāgyam = good fortune; bhuJṇānāḥ = as benefitted; svāni = by their own; puṇyāni = pious deals.

"Oh, noble prince! Father, mother, brother, son and daughter-in-law accomplish their own good fortune, as benefitted by their own pious deeds."

Sloka 4

bhartur bhāgyam tu bhāryā ekā prāpnoti puruṣa ṛṣabha |
ataḥ caiva aham ādiṣṭā vane vastavyam iti api || 2-27-4

4. purushharshhabha = oh; best of men! bhāryā = wife; ekā = for one; prāpnoti = gets; bhartṛibhāgyamtu = fortune of the husband; atashcha = for that reason; ahamapi = I am also; ādishhṭā eva = ordered; vastavyamiti = to dwell; vane = in the forest.

"Oh, Rama the best of men! Wife for one gets fortune of the husband. For that reason, I am also destined to dwell in the forest."

Sloka 5

na pitā na ātmajo na ātmā na mātā na sakhī janaḥ |
iha pretya ca nārīṇām patir eko gatiḥ sadā || 2-27-5

5. pitā = father; na = not recomrese; mna = not; ātmajaḥ = son; na = not; ātmā = the self; ā = not; mātā = mother; na = not; sakhījanaḥ = female companions; nārīṇām = to women; patiḥ = husband; ekaḥ = for one; sadā = forever; gatiḥ = is recourse; iha = in this world; pretyacha = or after death.

" To a woman, father or son or self or mother or female companion are not the recourse. Husband alone is forever the best recourse either in this world or after her death".

Sloka 6

yadi tvam prasthitaḥ durgam vanam adya eva rāghava |
agrataḥ te gamiṣyāmi mṛdnantī kuśa kaṇṭakān || 2-27-6

6. rāghava = Oh; Rama! adyaiva = now itself; tvam prasthitaḥ yadi = if you set forth; vanam = to the forest; durgam = which is difficult to be travelled; gamishhyāmi = I shall proceed; te agrataḥ = before you; mṛidnantī = by trampling down; dusha kaṇdakān = grass with long pointed stalks and thorns.

"Oh, Rama! If you set forth now itself to the forest, which is difficult to be travelled, I shall come before you, by trampling down thorns and grass with long pointed stalks."

Sloka 7

īrṣyā roṣau bahiṣ kṛtya bhukta śeṣam iva udakam |
naya mām vīra viśrabdhaḥ pāpam mayi na vidyate || 2-27-7

7. vira = Oh; brave ma! bahishhkṛitya = by abandoning; īrshhyāroshhau = impatience and anger; udakam iva = as water; bhukta seshham = left over after driniking; visrabdhaḥ = having faith; naya = take; mām = me; navidyate = there is no; pāpam = sin; muyi = in me.

"Oh, brave man! By abandoning, as water left over after drinking, the impatience and anger and having faith, take me. There is no sin in me."

Sloka 8

prāsāda agraiḥ vimānaiḥ vā vaihāyasa gatena vā |
sarva avasthā gatā bhartuḥ pādac cāyā viśiṣyate || 2-27-8

8. pādachchhayā = protection under the feet; bhartuḥ = of husband; vishishhyate = is better than; prāsādāgraiḥ = top of a lofty building; vimānairvā = or aerial cars; vaihāyasagatena vā = or moving through the sky; sarvavasthagatā = or attaining all types of positions.

"Protection under the feet of the husband is better than being on top of a lofty building or in aerial cars or in moving through the sky or in attaining all types of positions."

Sloka 9

anuśiṣṭā asmi mātrā ca pitrā ca vividha āśrayam |
na asmi samprati vaktavyā vartitavyam yathā mayā || 2-27-9

9. anushishhṭā = I was taught; vividhāshrayam = of different subjects; mātrācha = by mother; pitrācha = and by father; nāsmi = I need not; vaktavyā = be told; samprati = now; yathā = how; vartitarvyam = to deal in any way with any one.

"ṃy mother and father taught me of different matters long ago. I need not be told now, how to deal in any way with anyone."

Sloka 10

aham durgam gamiṣyāmi vanam puruṣavarjitam |
nānāmṛgagaṇākīrṇam śārdūlavṛkasevitam || 2-27-10

10. aham = I; gamishhyāmi = can go; vanam = to forest; durgam = which is inaccessible; purushhavarjitam = which is devoid of people; nānā mṛigagaṇākīrṇam = filled with various types of animals; shārdūla vṛika sevitam = inhabited by tigers and jackals.

" I can come to forest which is inaccessible, which is devoid of people, filled with various types of animals and inhaited by tigers and jackals."

Sloka 11

sukham vane nivatsyāmi yathā eva bhavane pituḥ |
acintayantī trīml lokāmḥ cintayantī pati vratam || 2-27-11

11. achintayantī = ṇot thinking; trīn lokān = of there worlds; chintayantī = thinking pativratam = of loyalty to husband; nivatsyāmi = I shall dwell; vane = in the forest; sukham = happily; yathaiva = as though it is; pituḥ = father's; bhavane = house.

"Not thinking of three world, thinking of loyalty to husband, I shall dwell in the forest happily as though staying in our father's house".

Sloka 12

śuśrūṣamāṇā te nityam niyatā brahma cāriṇī |
saha raṃsye tvayā vīra vaneṣu madhu gandhiṣu || 2-27-12

12. vīva = Oh; brave man! shushrūshhamāṇā = doing service; te = to you; nityam = ragularly; niyatā = having discipline; brahmachāriṇī = observing chastity; ramsye = I would like to stay; tvayāsaha = with you; vaneshhu = in the forests; madhugandhishhu = which smell like honey.

"Oh, brave man! performing service to you regularly, having the necessary discipline, observing chastity, I would like to stay with you in the forests which smell like honey."

Sloka 13

tvam hi kartum vane śaktaḥ rāma samparipālanam |
anyasya pai janasya iha kim punar mama mānada || 2-27-13

13. rāma = Oh; Rama; mānada = the giver of honour! iha = here; vane = in the forest; tvam = you; shaktaḥ hi = are indeed capable; kartum = to undertake; samparipālanapi = protection also; anyasya janasya = of other people; kimpunaḥ = why to tell; mama = about me?

"Oh, Rama, the bestower of honour! Here in the forest, you are indeed capable of protecting other people also. Why to tell about my protection?"

Sloka 14

saha tvayā gamiśyāmi vasamadya na saṃśayaḥ |
nāham śakyā mahābhāga nivartayitu mudyatā || 2-2714

14. adya = today; gamishhyāmi = I will go; vanam = to forest; tvayāsaha = along with you; na = ṇo; samshayaḥ = doubt; mahābhāga = oh; the illustrious! aham = I; udyatā = ready(to go to forest) na shakyā = cannot; nivartayitum = be prevented.

"Today no doubt, I will come to forest along with you. Oh, the illustrious! I, with this intention, cannot be prevented to do so."

Sloka 15

phala mūla aśanā nityam bhaviṣyāmi na saṃśayaḥ |
na te duhkham kariṣyāmi nivasantī saha tvayā || 2-27-15

15. nityam = always; bhavishhyāmi = I will become; phalamulāshanā = the eater of fruits and roots; na = no; samshayaḥ = doubt; nivasantī = residing; tvayāsaha = along with you; na karishhyāmi = I will not create; duḥkham = unpleasantness; te = to you.

I shall live only on roots and fruits always, no doubt. Living along with you, I shall not create any unpleasantness to you.

Sloka 16

iccāmi saritaḥ śailān palvalāni vanāni ca |
draṣṭum sarvatra nirbhītā tvayā nāthena dhīmatā || 2-27-16

16. tvayā = with you; dhīmatā = the learned; nāthena = and the lord; nirbhītā = without fear; sarvatra = everywhere; ichchhāmi = I wish; drashhṭum = to see; saritaḥ = rivers; shailān = mountains; palvalāni = small lakes; vanānicha = and grooves.

" With you the learned and the lord, I shall not have fear anywhere. I wish to see rivers, mountains, small lakes and grooves."

Sloka 17

haṃsa kāraṇḍava ākīrṇāḥ padminīḥ sādhu puṣpitāḥ |
icceyam sukhinī draṣṭum tvayā vīreṇa samgatā || 2-27-17

17. sangatā = coming together; tvayā = with you; vīreṇa = the courageous man; sukhinī = being comfortable; ichchheyam = I desire; drashhṭum = to see; padminīḥ = lotus-ponds; hamsakāraṇdavākīrṇā = filled with swans; water-fowls; sādhu = excellent; pushhpitā = in bloom.

I shall be comfortable, by coming together with you, the courageous man. I desire to see lotus- ponds filled with swans, water-fowls and excellently flowered.

Sloka 18

abhiṣekam kariṣyāmi tāsu nityam yatavratā |
saha tvayā viśāla akṣa raṃsye parama nandinī || 2-27-18

18. vishālākshha = Oh; the large eyed lord! yatavratā = remaining dedicated; karishhyāmi = I shall take; abhishhekam = bath; nityam = regularly; tāsu = in those lakes; paramanandinī = with full of joy; ramsye = I shall sport; tvayāsaha = with you.

"Oh, the large eyed lord! Remaining dedicated to you, I shall take bath regularly in those lakes and with full of joy, I shall sport with you."

Sloka 19

evam varṣa sahasrāṇām śatam vā aham tvayā saha |
vyatikramam na vetsyāmi svargo.api hi na me mataḥ || 2-27-19

19. aham = I; tvayāsaha = along with you; evam = thus; shatamvāpi = spending also hundred; varshhasahasrāṇām = thousand years; ravetsyāmi = shall not find; vyatikramam = deviation me = to me; svaro.api = heaven also; namataḥ hi = indeed is not desired.

Thus spending even a hundred thousand years in your company I shall never find any deviation/ Heaven also will not be acceptable to me otherwise.

Sloka 20

svarge api ca vinā vāso bhavitā yadi rāghava |
tvayā mama nara vyāghra na aham tam api rocaye || 2-27-20

20. naravyāghra = Oh; lion among men! rāghava = oh; secion of Raghu! mama = to me; tvayā vinā = without you; svarge.api = even in heaven; bhavitāyadi = is to become; vāsaḥ = residence; aham = I; na rochaye = do not like tamapi = that too.

Oh, Lion among men and seion of Raghu! Even if in heaven there is residence for me without you, I do not like it.

Sloka 21

aham gamiṣyāmi vanam sudurgamam |
mṛga āyutam vānara vāraṇaiḥ yutam |
vane nivatsyāmi yathā pitur gṛhe |
tava eva pādāv upagṛhya sammatā || 2-27-21

21. aham = I; gamishhyāmi = can go; vanam = to forest; sundargamam = which is very much inaccessible; mṛigāyutam = mingled with animals; vānaravārṇaiḥ = occupied by monkeys and elephants; upagṛihya = clinging; tava pādāveva = your feet alone; samyatā = with restraint; nivatsyāmi = I shall dwell; vane = in the forest; gṛiheyathā = as thought in the house; pituḥ = of father.

I shall proceed to the forest, which is very much inaccessible and occupied by monkeys, elephants as well as other animals. Clining to your feet alone, I shall dwell in the forest as though in father's home.

Sloka 22

ananya bhāvām anurakta cetasam |
tvayā viyuktām maraṇāya niścitām |
nayasva mām sādhu kuruṣva yācanām |
na te mayā ataḥ gurutā bhaviṣyati || 2-27-22

22. ananya bhāvām = without anyother thought; anurakta chetanam = my mind attached to you; viyuktām = if separated; tvayā = from you; nischitām = having decided; maraṇāya = to die; nayasva = usher; mām = me; kurushhva = grant; sādhu = well; yāchanām = my prayer; ataḥ = due to this; na bhavishhyati = there will be no; gurutā = burden; te = to you; maya = by me.

"Therefore, grant well my prayer and usher me- me without any other thought, whose mind is fondly attached to you and who am determined to die if separated from you. Due to this, there will be no burden to you from me."

Sloka 23

tathā bruvāṇām api dharma vatsalo |
na ca sma sītām nṛ varaḥ ninīṣati |
uvāca ca enām bahu samnivartane |
vane nivāsasya ca duhkhitām prati || 2-27-23

23. nṛivaraḥ = Rama the best of men; dharmavatsalaḥ = who is fond of righteousness; na ninīshhitisma = was not inclined to take; sītām = ṣita; bruvāṇāmapi = who was even though speaking; tathā = thus; samnivartane = to hold her back; uvācha cha = he talked also; habu = highly; enām = to her; duḥkhitām prati = about afflictions; nivāsasya = fo dwelling; vane = in a forest.

sita along with him eventhought she was speaking as a foresaid. To hold her back from coming to forest, he also started talking highly of the various afflictions she might have to face in a forest.

- - -

|| iti śrimad rāmayane ayodhya kānde ṣatviṃsaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus complets the twenty sevent chapter of Ayodhya Kānda in Srimad Ramayana.
