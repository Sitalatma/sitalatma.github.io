chapter[Sarga] 23

Introduction

This chapter describes the valiant words of Lakshmana, his opposing depends on god and proposes to fight with Rama's enemies to endow Rama with Kingdom.

Sloka 1

iti bruvati rāme tu lakṣmaṇo adhaḥ śirā muhuḥ |
śrutvā madhyam jagāma iva manasā duhkha harṣayoh || 2-23-1

1. rāme = Rama; iti = thus; brutvati = speading; lakshhmanaḥ = Lakshmana; shrutvā = heard; adhaḥshshrirāḥ = having his head bowed down; jagameva = quite so got; madhyam = nto midst; duḥkha harshhayaḥ = of pain and pleasure; muhuḥ = again and again; manasā = effecting mind.

While Rama was thus speeding, Lakshmana heard, bowed down his head and got into the midst of pain and pleasure effecting his mind again and again.

Sloka 2

tadā tu baddhvā bhrukuṭīm bhruvor madhye nara ṛṣabha |
niśaśvāsa mahā sarpo bilasyaiva roṣitaḥ || 2-23-2

2. narashhabhaḥ = Lakshmana; the best of men; tadāta tu = then; baddhvā = firmed up; bhrukutīm = contraction of brows; madhye = in the middle; bhruvoḥ = of eyebrows; nishashvāsa = sighed; mahāsarpaḥ iva = like great snake; roshhitaḥ = in anger; bilasthaḥ = staying in hole.

Lakshmana then firmed up his eyebrows and sighed with frown, like an angry snake from its hole.

Sloka 3

tasya duṣprativīkṣyam tat bhrukuṭī sahitam tadā |
babhau kruddhasya simhasya mukhasya sadṛśam mukham || 2-23-3

3. tad = that; tasya = his; dushhprativīkshham = wickedly looking; mukham = face; bhrukutīsahitam = with contracted eyebrows; tadā = then; babhau = shined; sadrusham = like; kṛiddhasya = angry; simhasya = lion.

His wickedly looking face with eyebrows contracted, then appeared like the face of an angry lion.

Sloka 4

agrahaḥ tam vidhunvaṃs tu hastī hastam ivātmanaḥ |
tiryag ūrdhvam śarīre ca pātayitvā śiraḥ dharām || 2-23-4
agra akṣṇā vīkṣamāṇaḥ tu tiryag bhrātaram abravīt |

4. vīkshhamāṇaḥ = seeing; bhrātaram = brother Rama; agrākshhṇā = with tip of eyes; tiryak = obliquely; abravīt = (he) spoke; vidhūnvan = moving; agrahastam = forehand; ātmanaḥ hastamiva = like its trunk; hastī = the elephant; pātayitvā = causing to fall; sirodharāam = the neck; sharīre = in the body; tiryak = obliquely; ūrdhvamcha = and upwardly.

Seeing Rama with the tip of his eyes obliquely, Lakshmana spoke to him moving his forehand like an elephant moving its trunk and causing his head to fall in oblique and upward direction.

Sloka 5

asthāne sambhramaḥ yasya jātaḥ vai sumahān ayam || 2-23-5

katham hi etat asambhrāntaḥ tvad vidho vaktum arhati || 2-23-6

5;6. shauṇdīrakshhatriyarshhabha = oh; the best of proud military community! ayam = this; sumahān = very great; sambhramaḥ = confusion; asthāne = which is untimely; jātaḥvai = born; yasya = in you; dharma doshha prasangena = the subject of guilty righteousness; anatishaṇkayā = non suspicion; lokasya = of the world; katham = how; tvadvidhaḥ = such a person like you; arhati = is able; vaktum = to speak; etat = this; yathā = in which way; daivam = about destiny; ashauṇdīnam = which is powerless.

"Oh, the best of proud military community! This untimely great confusion is born in you due to your conjecture of quilty righteousness and of non suspicion of the world. Can such a person like you speak a person like you speak thus doubtlessly of the powerless destiny?"

Sloka 7

kim nāma kṛpaṇam daivam aśaktam abhiśaṃsati |
pāpayos tu katham nāma tayoh śankā na vidyate || 2-23-7

7. kimnām = why; abhishamsasi = are you praising; daivam = destiney; kṛipaṇam = which is pitiable; ashaktam = which is weak? katham nāma = how; navidyati = not; shaṇkā = doubtful; te = to you; tayoḥ = in both of them; pāpayoḥ = who are sinful?

"Why are you speaking about this weak and pitiable destiny? Why are you not doubting the sinful Kaikeyi and Dasaratha?"

Sloka 8

santi dharma upadhāḥ ślakṣṇā dharmātman kim na budhyase || 2-23-8

8. dharmātman = Oh; the righteous soul! tayoḥ = both of their; shlakshhṇāḥ = smooth; dharmopadhāḥ = deceit in the name of; santi = is there excisting; shāṭhyāt = by dishonest means; svārtham = with selfish motive; parijihīrshhitoḥ = to keep away; sucharitam = your good conduct; kim = why; na budhyase = you are not knowing?

"Oh, the righteous sould! Both of them are smoothly deceiving in the name of righteousness with an intent to keep away your good conduct, with selfish away your good conduct, with selfish motive and by dishonest means. You are not knowing."

Sloka 9

yadi naivam vyavasitam syāddhi prāgreva rāghava |
tayoḥ prāgeva dattaśca syādvaraḥ prakṛtaśca saḥ || 2-23-9

9. rāghava = Oh; Rama! na syādyadi = if it was not vyavasitam = decision; prageva = indeed long ago; evam = thus; tayoḥ = by both of them; prakṛitaḥ = naturally; saḥ = that; varaḥ = boon; syāt = might; dattaḥ = have been given; prāgeva = long ago indeed.

"Oh, Rama! If it was not indeed an earlier decision taken long ago by both of them, the boon must have been given naturally even long ago."

Sloka 10

loka vidviṣṭam ārabdham tvad anyasya abhiṣecanam |
notsahe sahitum vīra tatra me kṣantumarhasi || 2-23-10

10. vīra = Oh; brave man! abhishhechanam = inangurating; ārabdham = that is started; lokavidvishhṭam = is not to the liking of people; notsahe = whic I cannot; sahitum = tolerate; tatra = in that matter; arhasi = you are proper; kshhantum = to excense; me = me.

"Enthroning other than yourself is not to the liking of the people. I cannot tolerate this act that is started. Excuse me in this matter."

Sloka 11

yena iyam āgatā dvaidham tava buddhir mahī pate |
sa hi dharmaḥ mama dveṣyaḥ prasangāt yasya muhyasi || 2-23-11

11. mahāmate = oh; the great-minded! saḥ dharmaḥ = that righteousness; yena = bye which; tava = your; iyam = this; buddhiḥ = intellect; āgatā = got; dvaidham = disunited; yasya = by which; prasangāt = indulgence; muhyasi = you are stupefied; dveshhyaḥ = is disliking; mama = to me.

"Oh, Rama the great minded! This righteousness by which your intellect got disunited and by which indulgence, you are stupefied, is disliked by me."

Sloka 12

katham tvam karmaṇā śaktaḥ kaikeyīvaśavartinaḥ |
kariṣyasi piturvākyamadharmiṣṭham vigarhitam || 2-23-12

12. tvām = you; shaktaḥ = are capable; karmaṇā = of action; kathana = how; karishhyasi = can you act on; vākyam = the word; vigarhitam = which is reprehensible; adharmishhṭam = and which is unrighteous; pituḥ = of father; kaikeyīvashavartinaḥ = who is behaving; abediently to the will of Kaikeyi.

"you are capable of doing retaliatory action. How can you act on the word which is reprehensible and which is unjustified of your father who is surrendering obediently to the will of Kaikeyi?"

Sloka 13

yady api pratipattis te daivī ca api tayoḥ matam |
tathā api upekṣaṇīyam te na me tat api rocate || 2-23-13

13. kṛito.api = though ; being done; ayam = this; bhedaḥ = treachery; kilbishhāt = with sinful intent; duḥkam = regret; jāyate = has arisen; me = in me; tatra = in that; nagṛihyate = you are not grasping; evam = in this way; dharmasangashcha = such conduct; garhitaḥ = is to be censured.

"Though they are doing this treachery with a sinful intent, I regret that you are not grasping it. Such a conduct is to be censured."

Sloka 14

mansā.api katham kāmam kuryāstvam kāmavṛttayoḥ |
tayostvahitayornityam śatrvoḥ pitrabhidhānayoḥ || 2-23-14

14. katham = how; kuryāḥ tvam = can you do; manasā.api = even by mind; kāmam = the desire; shatrvaḥ = of enemies; pitrabhidhānayoḥ = in the name of parents; kāmavṛittayoḥ = who are action on their own free will; ahitayoḥ = who were not desirous of your welfare; nityam = forever.

"But of them, action on their own free will, were never desirous of your welfare. They are enemies in the name of parents. It is not proper even to think of fulfilling their desire."

Sloka 15

yadyapi pratipattiste daivī cāpi tayormatam |
tathā pyupekṣaṇīyam te na me tadapi rocate || 2-23-15

15. matam yadyapi = though opinion; te = of you; tayoḥ = their; pratipathiḥ = action; daivīcha = is indeed caused by divine power; tathāpi = even then; narochate = I do not desire; upekshhaṇīyam = to be overlooked; te = by you.

"It may be your opinion that their action is influenced by mere destiny. Even then, I do not like you to be indifferent to this also."

Sloka 16

viklavo vīrya hīno yaḥ sa daivam anuvartate |
vīrāḥ sambhāvita ātmāno na daivam paryupāsate || 2-23-16

16. yaḥ = who; vikalabaḥ = confused person; vīryahīnaḥ = cowardly person; saḥ = that person; anuvartate = follows; daivam = destiny; vīrāḥ = brave men; sambhāvitāt mānaḥ = with self respect naparyupāsate = do not honour; daivam = destiny.

"Only a confused and cowardly person depends upon destiny. Brave men with self-respect do not honour the destiny."

Sloka 17

daivam puruṣa kāreṇa yaḥ samarthaḥ prabādhitum |
na daivena vipanna arthaḥ puruṣaḥ so avasīdati || 2-23-17

17. yaḥ purushhaḥ = which person; samarthaḥ = is competant; prabādhitum = to keep off; daivam = destiny; purushhakāreṇa = by human effort; saḥ = that person; na avasīdati = does not regret; vipannārtha = in the sense of not being failed; daivam = by destiny.

"That person who is capable of keeping off destiny by human effort, does not have regret in the sense of not being failed by destiny."

Sloka 18

drakṣyanti tu adya daivasya pauruṣam puruṣasya ca |
daiva mānuṣayoḥ adya vyaktā vyaktir bhaviṣyati || 2-23-18

18. adya = today; drakshhyanti = can be seen; paurushham = the prowess; daivasya = of destiny; purushhasya = and of man; adya = this day; vyaktiḥ = distinction; daivamānnshhayoḥ = between man and destiny bhavishhyati = will become; vyaktā = clear.

"Today, all can see how much prowess is there in destiny and in man. This day, the distinction between man and the destiny will be clearly perceived."

Sloka 19

adya mat pauruṣa hatam daivam drakṣyanti vai janāḥ |
yad daivāt āhatam te adya dRṣṭam rājya abhiṣecanam || 2-23-19

19. adya = now; yaddaivāt = by which destiny; te = your; rājyābhishhechanam = coronation for; dṛishhṭam = is seen; āhatam = crushed; janāḥ = people; drakshhyantivai = will see; adya = today; daivam = (that)destiny; matpaurushhahatam = ebing crused by my prowess.

"By which destiny, your coronation is seen crushed, I shall crush that destiny by my prowess. Let all people see it!"

Sloka 20

atyankuśam iva uddāmam gajam mada bala uddhatam |
pradhāvitam aham daivam pauruṣeṇa nivartaye || 2-23-20

20. paurushheṇa = by prowess; aham = I; nivartate = shall turn back; daivam = destiny; gajam iva = as elephant; atyaṇkusham = which is not caring a hook; pradhāvitam = which is running; uddhāmam = fiercely; mada baloddhatam = with violent force.

"By my prowess, I shall turn back this destiny, as framing back an elephant which is not caring a hook and which is fiercely running with violent force."

Sloka 21

loka pālāḥ samastāḥ te na adya rāma abhiṣecanam |
na ca kRtsnāḥ trayo lokā vihanyuḥ kim punaḥ pitā || 2-23-21

21. adya = today; samastāḥ = all; te = those; lokapālāḥ = guardians of world; navihamyauḥ = cannot withhold; rāmābhishhechanam = Rama's coronation; na = nor; kṛitsnāḥ = all; trayaḥ lokāḥ cha = three worlds also; kimpunaḥ = why to talk of; pitaḥ = father.

"Neither all the guardians of the world nor the three worlds put together can withhold Rama's coronation today. Why to talk about father."

Sloka 22

yaiḥ vivāsaḥ tava araṇye mitho rājan samarthitaḥ |
araṇye tu vivatsyanti catur daśa samāḥ tathā || 2-23-22

22. rājan = Oh; king! yaiḥ = by whom; samarthitaḥ = being advocated; mithaḥ = secretly; tava = your; nivāsaḥ = residence; araṇye = in forest; te = they; tathā = in that way; nivatsyanti = will have to reside; araṇye = in forest; chaturdasha samāḥ = for fourteen years.

"Oh, King! Those who are secritlyadvocating you to live in a forest for fourteen years, will have to reside in forest for fourteen years in that manner."

Sloka 23

aham tadā āśām cetsyāmi pitus tasyāḥ ca yā tava |
abhiṣeka vighātena putra rājyāya vartate || 2-23-23

23. tat = that is why; aham = I; chhetsyāmi = shall break down; āshām = the hope; pituḥ = fo father; tasyāshcha = and is trying; putrarājyāya = for kingdom to her son; tava abhishhekavighātena = by creting obstacle to your coronation.

"That is why, I shall break down the hope of father and of Kaikeyi, who is trying for kingdom to her son by creating obstacle to your coronation."

Sloka 24

mad balena viruddhāya na syāt daiva balam tathā |
prabhaviṣyati duhkhāya yathā ugram pauruṣam mama || 2-23-24

24. yathā = how; mama paurushham = my prowess; ugram = which is terrific; prabhavishhyati = will originate; duḥkhāya = pain; viruddhāya = to those who are opposed to; madbalena = my strength; tathā = thus; daivabalam = the strength of destiny; nasyāt = will not do.

"The strength of destiny cannot create that much pain as that which my terrific prowess will create to those who are opposed to my strength."

Sloka 25

ūrdhvam varṣa sahasra ante prajā pālyam anantaram |
ārya putrāḥ kariṣyanti vana vāsam gate tvayi || 2-23-25

25. prajāpālyamanantaram = after having ruled the people; varshhāsahasrānte = at the end of thousand years; tvayi = you; vanavāsamgate = having gone to the forest; āryaputrāḥ = sons of you; the honourable; karishhyanti = will rule the people.

"At the end of a thousand years of your ruling the kingdom, you having gone to the forest, your sons will govern the kingdom."

Sloka 26

pūrva rāja Rṣi vRttyā hi vana vāso vidhīyate |
prajā nikṣipya putreṣu putravat paripālane || 2-23-26

26. nikshhipya = keeping; paripālane = protection; prajāḥ = of people; putravat = as sons; putreshhu = in sons; vidhīyate hi = it is indeed practice; pūrva rājarshhi vṛittyāḥ = as vocation of ancient kings; vanavāsaḥ = to reside in forest.

"It is indeed a practice for ancient kings to go to the forest after keeping care of people, to be nourished as children, into the hands of their sons."

Sloka 27

27;28. dharmātman = righteous; rāma = Rama; rājani = If the king Dasaratha; anekāgre = is not stable minded; rājyavibhrama shaṇkayā by a doubt of perturbation in kingdom; saḥ tvam = such of you; evam = thus; na ichchhasiyet = is not longing; rājyam = for kigdom; ātmani = in oneself; pratijānecha = I am promising; te = your; vīra = oh; hero! ma.abhūvam = I will not become; vīralokabhāk = the one who gets heroic world of heaven; aham = I; rakshheyam = shall protect; tava = your; rājyam = kingdom; sāgaram iva = as sea; velā = by sea court.

"If you think that you do not want kingdom because of your doubt that the kingdom will get disturbed due to unstable mind of king Dasaratha, you need not fear. I am promising you. I shall protect the kingdom as a sea coast protects the sea. If not, I will not obtain the heroic world of heaven."

Sloka 29

"You become engaged in getting coronated with auspicious things. I for one is competent to ward off the kings by force."

Sloka 30

Sloka 31

This earth will become impermeable, uninhabited with heads hands and thighs of elephants horses and men crushed, having been hit by my sword."

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

Sloka 40

yathā tava iyam vasudhā vaśe bhavet |
tathā eva mām śādhi tava asmi kimkaraḥ || 2-23-40

40. bravīhi = tell; kaḥ = who; tava = your; asuhṛit = enemy; viyujyatām = be made deprived of; prāṇayashassuhṭ^ijjanaiḥ = life; honour and friends; adyaiva = now itself; mayā = by me; shādhi = command; māma = me; tathaiva = so that; yathā = how; iyam = this; vasudhā = earth; bhavet = will become; tava = your; vashe = dominion; kinkaraḥ asmi = I am servant; tava = to you.

"Tell me now itself who your enemy be made deprived of life, honour and friendship, by me. Command me so that how this earth will become your dominion. I am your servant.

Sloka 41

vimRjya bāṣpam parisāntvya ca asakṛt |
sa lakṣmaṇam rāghava vaṃśa vardhanaḥ |
uvāca pitrye vacane vyavasthitam |
nibodha mām eṣa hi saumya sat pathaḥ || 2-23-41

41. saḥ = that; rāghavamsha vardhanaḥ = Rama; who is the cause for increasing Raghu dynasty; uvācha = spoke; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; parisāntvyacha = after consoling; asakṛit = time and again; vimṛijya = by wiping off; bhāshpam = tears; soumya = Oh; excellent man! nibodha = know; mām = me; vyavasthitam = who has stood; vachane = by word; pitryaiḥ = of father; eshhaḥ = this; satpathaḥ hi = indeed is good path!

Then, Rama spoke thus to Lakshmana after consoling him time and again by weeping off his tears; "Oh, Lakshmana! I stand by the words of our father. This indeed is a good path."

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe trayoviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes twenty third chapter of Ayodhya Kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
