Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 52
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dhumraksha along with army attacks the monkeys. A tumultuous battle ensued between the demons and the monkeys. The battle reached frightful proportions in that both the monkeys and demons were killed in large numbers. When Dhumraksha pounced on Hanuman, the latter shattered Dhumraksha's chariot to pieces. Lifting his mace, Dhumraksha fights with Hanuman, who in turn hits him on his head and kills him.

Sloka 1

dhūmra akṣam prekṣya niryāntam rākṣasam bhīma nisvanam |
vinedur vānarāh sarve prahṛṣṭā yuddha kānkṣiṇaḥ || 6-52-1

1. vīkṣya = beholding; rākṣasam = the demon; dhūmrākṣam = Dhumraksha; bhīma vikramam = of terrible prowess; niryāntam = coming forth; sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; vineduḥ = roared; prahṛiṣṭāḥ = with joy; yuddha kāṇkṣiṇaḥ = longing for war.

Beholding the demon, Dhumraksha of terrible prowess coming forth, all the monkeys waiting for the war, roared with joy.

Sloka 2

teṣām tu tumulam yuddham samjajne hari rakṣasām |
anyonyam pādapair ghorair nighnatam śūla mudgaraiḥ || 6-52-2

2. sutumulam = A highly tumultuous; yuddham = battle; samjajJṇe = was perceived; teṣām = (between) those; kapirakṣasām = monkeys and demons; nighnatām = who were slaying; anyonyam = each other; ghoraiḥ = with terrible; pādapaiḥ = trees; shūla mudgaraiḥ = lances and maces.

A highly tumultuous battle was seen between those monkeys and demons, who were slaying each other, with terrible trees, lances and maces.

Sloka 3

rākṣasair vānarā ghorā vinikṛttāh samantataḥ |
vānarai rākṣasāś ca api drumair bhūmau samī kṛtāḥ || 6-52-3

3. ghorāḥ = the terrible; vānarāḥ = monkeys; vinikṛittāḥ = were cut down; samantataḥ = on all sides; rākṣasaiḥ = by the demons; rākṣasāshchāpi = and the demons also; bhūmisamīkṛitāḥ = were levelled down to the earth; drumaiḥ = with trees; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys.

The terrible monkeys were moved down on all sides by the demons. The demon too were levelled down to the earth with trees by the monkeys.

Sloka 4

rākṣasāś ca api samkruddhā vānarān niśitaih śaraiḥ |
vivyadhur ghora samkāśaih kanka patrair ajihmagaiḥ || 6-52-4

4. rākṣasāstu = the demons on their part; abhisamkruddhāḥ = enraged with anger; vivyadhuḥ = paralyzed; vānarān = the monkeys; sharaiḥ = with arrows; kaṇkapatraiḥ = having wings; ajihmahaiḥ = going straight; nishitaiḥ = sharp; ghora samkāshaiḥ = and having frightful appearance.

Enraged with anger, the demons, on their part, paralyzed the monkeys with sharp and frightful arrows, going as straight as wings of eagle.

Sloka 5

te gadābhisḥ ca bhīmābhih paṭṭasaih kūṭa mudgaraiḥ |
ghoraisḥ ca parighaisḥ citrais triśūlaisḥ ca api saṃśitaiḥ || 6-53-5
vidāryamāṇā rakṣobhir vānarās te mahā balāḥ |
amarṣāj janita uddharṣāsḥ cakruḥ karmāṇy abhītavat || 6-52-6

5; 6. vidīryamāṇāḥ = (While) being torn asunder; rakṣobhiḥ = by the demons; bhīmābhiḥ = with terrible; gadābhiḥ = maces; paṭṭishaiḥ = spears; kūṭa mudgaraiḥ = hammers; ghoraiḥ = frightful; parighaishcha = iron bars; samshritaiḥ = and resorting to; chitraiḥ = variegated; trishūlaishcha = tridents; te vānarāḥ = those monkeys; mahābalāḥ = of exceeding prowess; abhītavat = fearlessly; chakruḥ = accomplished; karmāṇi = the tasks; amarṣajanitoddharṣāḥ = with an excitement born of anger.

While being torn asunder by the demons with terrible maces, spears, hammers, frightful iron bars and variegated tridents, the mighty monkeys fearlessly accomplished their tasks with an excitement born of anger.

Sloka 7

śara nirbhinna gātrās te śūla nirbhinna dehinaḥ |
jagṛhus te drumāṃs tatra śilāś ca hari yūthapāḥ || 6-52-7

7. shūla nirbhinna dehinaḥ = while their bodies were split up by the tridents; sharanirbhinna gātrāḥ = and their limbs broken by arrows; te = those; vānarayūthapāḥ = leaders of monkeys; jagruḥ = took up; drumān = trees; shilāshcha = and rocks; tatra = there.

While their bodies were split up by the tridents and their limbs broken by arrows, those leaders of monkeys took up trees and rocks there to fight.

Sloka 8

te bhīma vegā harayo nardamānās tatas tataḥ |
mamanthū rākṣasān bhīmān nāmāni ca babhāṣire || 6-52-8

8. te harayaḥ = those monkeys; bhīma vegāḥ = of terrible swiftness; nardamānāḥ = roaring aloud; mamanthuḥ = herassed; vīrān = the valiant; rākṣasān = demons; tatstataḥ = at all places; babāṣire = and called out; nāmānicha = their names.

Those terribly swift monkeys, roaring aloud harassed the valiant demons at all places, by calling out their names.

Sloka 9

tad babhūva adbhutam ghoram yuddham vānara rakṣasām |
śilābhir vividhābhiś ca bahu śākhaiś ca pādapaiḥ || 6-52-9

9. tat = that; ghoram = awfu; yuddham = battle; vividhābhiḥ = with various kinds of; shilābhiḥ = rocks; bahushākhaiḥ = and many branching; pādapaiḥ = trees; vānararakṣasām = between monkeys and demons; babhūva = became; adbhutam = wonderful.

That awful battle with all kinds of rocks and trees furnished with many branches between monkeys and demons appeared wonderful.

Sloka 10

rākṣasā mathitāh kecid vānarair jita kāśibhiḥ |
vavarṣū rudhiram kecin mukhai rudhira bhojanāḥ || 6-52-10

10. kechit = some; rākṣasāḥ = demons; mathitāḥ = were crushed; vānaraiḥ = by monkeys; jitakāshibhiḥ = who conquered fear; kechit = and some; rudhira bhojanāḥ = blood-sucking demons; pravemuḥ = vomitted; rudhiram = blood; mukhaiḥ = from their months.

Some demons were crushed by monkeys, who conquered fear and some blood-sucking demons vomited blood from their mouths.

Sloka 11

pārśveṣu dāritāh kecit kecid rāśī kṛtā drumaiḥ |
śilābhisḥ cūrṇitāḥ kecit kecid dantair vidāritāḥ || 6-52-11

11. kechit = some; dāritāḥ = were torn; pārshveṣu = at the sides; kechit = some; rāshikṛitāḥ = were formed into a heap; drumaiḥ = by trees; kechit = some; chūrṇitāh = were powdered; shilābhiḥ = by stones; kechit = some; vidāritāḥ = were torn; dantaiḥ = by teeth.

Some demons were slashed open at their sides. Some were formed into a heap by the trees. Some others were crushed by stones and yet others torn to pieces by the monkeys teeth.

Sloka 12

dhvajair vimathitair bhagnaiḥ kharaiś ca vinipātitaiḥ |
rathair vidhvaṃsitaisḥ ca api patitai rajanī caraiḥ || 6-52-12

12. dhvajaiḥ = their standards; vimathitaiḥ = crushed; bhagnaiḥ = and broken; khaDgaishcha = their swords; vimipātitaiḥ = snapped; rathaiḥ = their chariots; vidhamsitaiḥ = overturned; kechit = some; rajanīcharāḥ = demons; vyathitāḥ = were perturbed.

With their standards crushed and broken, their swords snapped and their chariots overturned, some demons were perturbed.

Sloka 13

gajendraiḥ parvatākāraiḥ parvatāgrairvanaukasām |
mathitairvājibhiḥ kīrṇam sārohairvasudhātalam || 6-52-13

13. mathitaiḥ = crushed; parvatāgraiḥ = by the great rocks; vanaukasām = of monkeys; vasudhātalam = the earth; kīrṇam = was scattered; gajendraiḥ = with elephants; parvatākāaiḥ = resembling hills; vājibhiḥ = and horses; sārohaiḥ = with their riders.

Crushed by the great rocks of monkeys, the earth was scattered with corpses of great elephants resembling hills and horses with their riders.

Sloka 14

vānarair bhīma vikrāntair āplutya āplutya vegitaiḥ |
rākṣasāḥ karajais tīkṣṇair mukheṣu vinikartitāḥ || 6-52-14

14. vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys; bhīmavikrāntaiḥ = of terrific prowess; vegitaiḥ = and swiftness; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; āplutya = jumped up horizontally; utputya = and vertically; vinidāritāḥ = and were torn; tīkṣṇaiḥ karajaiḥ = by their sharp nails; mukheṣu = in their faces.

The monkeys of terrific prowess rushed upon the demons, flinging themselves upon them with great bounds horizontically and vertically and scratching their faces with their sharp nails.

Sloka 15

vivarṇa vadanā bhūyo viprakīrṇa śiro ruhāḥ |
mūḍhāḥ śoṇita gandhena nipetur dharaṇī tale || 6-52-15

15. viṣaṇṇa vadanāḥ = with their faces dejected; bhūyaḥ = very much; viprakīrṇashiroruhāḥ = their hair torn out; mūDhaḥ = maddened; shoṇitagandhena = by the smell of blood; (those demons); nipetuḥ = fell; charaṇītale = on the ground.

With their faces dejected very much, their hair torn out and maddened by the smell of blood, those demons fell on the ground.

Sloka 16

naye tu parama kruddhā rākṣasā bhīma vikramāḥ |
talair eva abhidhāvanti vajra sparśa samair harīn || 6-52-16

16. anye = some other; rākṣasāḥ = demons; bhīma vikramāḥ = of exceeding valour; parama kruddhāḥ = very much enraged; abhidhāvanti = ran up towards; harīn = the monkeys; talaireva = with their palms; vajrasparsha samaiḥ = having a diamond-like blow.

Some other demons of exceeding valour, who were enraged, very much, ran up towards the monkeys to attack them with their palms having a diamond-like blow.

Sloka 17

vanarair āpatantas te vegitā vegavattaraiḥ |
muṣṭibhisḥ caraṇair dantaiḥ pādapaisḥ cāpa pothitāḥ || 6-52-17

17. te = those demons; āpatantaḥ = coming quickly; vegitāḥ = with a great speed; āvapothitāḥ = were crushed; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys; vegavattaraiḥ = of greater swiftness; muṣṭibhiḥ = with their fists; charaṇaiḥ = feet; dantaiḥ = teeth; pādapaishcha = and tress.

The monkeys, receiving that sharp shock, with even a greater ferocity, crushed the demons with blows of their fists, feet teeth and trees.

Sloka 18

sanyam tu vidrutam dṛṣṭvā dhūmra akṣo rākṣasa ṛṣabhaḥ |
roṣeṇa kadanam cakre vānarāṇām yuyutsatām || 6-52-18

18. dṛiṣṭvā = ṣeeing; sainyam = the army; vidrutam = routed; drūmrākṣaḥ = Drumraksha; rākṣasarṣabhaḥ = the lion among the demons; roṣeṇa = with anger; chakre = made; kadanam = a blood-shed; vānarāṇām = of the monkeys; yuyutsatām = wishing to fight.

Seeing his army routed, Dhumraksha that lion among the demons, in his anger began to create a blood-shed of the monkeys wishing to fight.

Sloka 19

prāsaiḥ pramathitāḥ kecid vānarāḥ śoṇita sravāḥ |
mudgarair āhatāḥ kecit patitā dharaṇī tale || 6-52-19

19. kechit = some; vānarāḥ = monkeys; pramathitāḥ = pierced; prāsaiḥ = with spears; shoṇita sravāḥ = lost rivers of blood; kechit = (while) others; āhatāḥ = struck down; mudgaraiḥ = by axes; pātitāḥ = fell; dharaṇītale = to the earth's surgface.

Some monkeys pierced with spears lost rivers of blood while others struck down by blows of axe, fell to the earth's surface.

Sloka 20

parighair mathitah kecid bhiṇḍi pālair vidāritāḥ |
paṭṭasair āhatāh kecid vihvalanto gata asavaḥ || 6-52-20

20. kechit = some; mathitāḥ = wee crushed; parighaiḥ = by iron bars; dāritāḥ = (others) torn; bhindipālaishcha = by harpoons; kechit = some; mathitāḥ = were pierced; paṭṭishaiḥ = by javelins; vihvalantaḥ = were exhausted; gatāsavaḥ = and lost their lives.

Some were crushed by iron bars, others torn by harpoons, some others pierced by javelins, all exhausted and lost their lives.

Sloka 21

kecid vinihatā bhūmau rudhira ārdrā vana okasaḥ |
kecid vidrāvitā naṣṭāh samkruddhai rākṣasair yudhi || 6-52-21

21. vinihatāḥ = slain; yudhi = in battle; samkṛiddhaiḥ = by infuriated; rākṣasaiḥ = demons; kechit = some; vanaukasaḥ = monkeys; rudhirādrāḥ = drenched with blood; bhūmau = fell on the ground; kechit = some others; naṣṭāḥ = disappeared; vidrāvitāḥ = having been driven away.

Slain in battle by infuriated demons, some monkeys, drenched with blood, fell on the ground and some others disappeared, having been driven away.

Sloka 22

vibhinna hṛdayāḥ kecid eka pārśvena śāyitāḥ |
vidārita astra śūlai ca kecid āntrair vinisrutāḥ || 6-52-22

22. vibhinna hṛidayāḥ = with pierced hearts; kechit = some (monkeys); shāyitāḥ = were made to lie down; ekapārshvena = on one side; kechit = some; vidāritāḥ = were torn asunder; tshūlaiḥ = by tridents; āntraiḥ = and their intestines; viniḥ sṛitāḥ = came out.

With pierced hearts, some monkeys were made to lie down on one side. Some were torn asunder by tridents that even their intestines came out.

Sloka 23

tat subhīmam mahad yuddham hari rākasa samkulam |
prababhau śastra bahulam śilā pādapa samkulam || 6-52-23

23. tat yuddham = that battle; mahat = which was mighty; prababhau = flashed; subhīmam = quite terribly; harirākṣasa samkulam = intense between monkeys and demons; shilāpādapa samkulam = crammed with rocks and trees; shastra bahulam = and multitude of weapons.

That mighty battle assumed most awful proportions in that monkeys and demons were crammed with rocks, trees and multitude of weapons.

Sloka 24

dhanur jyā tantri madhuram hikkā tāla samanvitam |
mandra stanita samgītam yuddha gāndharvam ābabhau || 6-52-24

24. tat = that; yuddha gāndharvam = combat in the form of a symphony ababhau = diffused; madhuram = sweet with (the sounds of) bow-strings; hikkātāla samanvitam = with neighing of the horses as rhythms; mandastanita gītam = and vocal music in the form of trumpeting of elephants.

With the bow-strings as the tuneful lute, the neighing of horses as a measure rhythm and the trumpeting of elephants as the vocal music, the whole battle resembled a symphony.

Sloka 25

dhūmra akṣas tu dhanuṣ pāṇir vānarān raṇa mūrdhani |
hasan vidrāvayām āsa diśas tān śara vṛṣṭibhiḥ || 6-52-25

25. dhūmrākṣastu = Dhumraksha on his part; dhanuṣpāṇiḥ = wielding a bow in his hand; hasan = laughing; raṇa mūrdhani = at the battle-front; vidrāyāmāsa tān vānarān = made those monkeys to run away; dishaḥ = to (all) quarters; sharavṛiṣṭhibhiḥ = by a shower of his arrows.

Dhumraksha on his part, wielding a bow in his hand and laughing at the battle-front, made those monkeys to run away to all the quarters by a shower of his arrows.

Sloka 26

dhūmra akṣeṇa arditam sainyam vyathitam dṛśya mārutiḥ |
abhyavartata samkruddhah pragṛhya vipulām śilām || 6-52-26

26. prekṣya = seeing; sainyam = the army; vyathitam = perturbed; arditam = and being tormented; dhūmrākṣeṇa = by Dhumraksha; mārutiḥ = ḥanuman; samkruddhaḥ = was enraged; abhyavartata = and turned towards him; pragṛihya = taking; vipulām = a gigantic; shilām = rock.

Seeing the army perturbed due to tormented by Dhumraksha, Hanuman was enraged and turned towards him, taking a gigantic rock in his hands.

Sloka 27

krodhād dviguṇa tāmra akṣah pitṛ tulya parākramaḥ |
śilām tām pātayām āsa dhūmra akṣasya ratham prati || 6-52-27

27. tulya parākramaḥ = ḥanuman; who was equal in strength; pituḥ = to his father; dviguṇa tāmrākṣah = with his eyes doubly red; krodhāt = due to anger; pātayāmāsa = threw down; tām shilān = that rock; ratham prati = towards the chariot; dhūmrākṣasya = of Dhumraksha.

Hanuman, who was equal in strength to his father, with his eyes inflamed with anger, flung the rock on the chariot of Dhumraksha.

Sloka 28

āpatantīm śilām dṛṣṭvā gadām udyamya sambhramāt |
rathād āplutya vegena vasudhāyām vyatiṣṭhata || 6-52-28

28. dṛiṣṭvā = behold; āpantantīm = the befalling; shilām = rock; (Dhumraksha) udyamya = lifting; gadām = his mace; sambhramāt = hurriedly; āplutya = jumped down; vegena = speedily; rathāt = from the chariot; vyatiṣṭhata = and stood; vasudhyām = on the earth.

Beholding the befalling rock, Dhumraksha lifting his mace hurriedly, jumped down speedily from the chariot and stood there on the earth.

Sloka 29

sā pramathya ratham tasya nipapāta śilā bhuvi |
sacakra kūbaram sāśvam sadhvajam saśara āsanam || 6-52-29

29. sā shilā = that rock; pramathya = shattered; tasya = his; ratham = chariot; sa chakra kubera mukham = along with banner; sasharāsanam = and bows; nipapāta = and rolled down; bhuvi = to the ground.

Shattering his chariot with its wheels, its pole, its crest along with banner and bows, that rock rolled down to the ground.

Sloka 30

sa bhanktvā tu ratham tasya hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
rakṣasām kadanam cakre saskandha viṭapair drumaiḥ || 6-52-30

30. saḥ hanumān = ṭhat ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of ṃaruta; bhaṇ^ktyā = breaking; tasya = his; ratham = chariot; chakre = caused; kadanam = the destruction; rakṣasām = of demons; drumaiḥ = by the use of trees; saskandha viṭapaiḥ = with their branches and shoots.

Thereafter, Hanuman the son of ṃaruta (the wind-god), after breaking the chariot, destroyed the demons with trunks of trees furnished with their branches.

Sloka 31

vibhinna śiraso bhūtvā rākṣasāh śoṇita ukṣitāḥ |
drumaiḥ pramathitāsḥ ca anye nipetur dharaṇī tale || 6-52-31

31. vibhinna shirasaḥ = with their crushed heads; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; bhūtvā = became; rudhirokṣitāḥ = drenched with blood; anye = some others; pramathitāḥ = crunched; drumaiḥ = by trees; nipetuḥ = fell; dharaṇītale = on the ground.

With their heads crushed, the demons were drenched with blood. Some others were crunched by the trees and fell down to the earth.

Sloka 32

vidrāvya rākṣasam sainyam hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
gireḥ śikharam ādāya dhūmra akṣam abhidudruve || 6-52-32

32. hanumān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = sainyam = the army; rākṣasam = of demons; ādāya = and taking shikharam = the peak; gireḥ = of a mountain; abhidudruve = ran towards; dhūmrākṣam = Dhumraksha.

Having driven away the army of demons, Hanuma born of ṃaruta, breaking off the peak of a mountain, ran towards Dhumraksha.

Sloka 33

tam āpatantam dhūmra akṣo gadām udyamya vīryavān |
vinardamānaḥ sahasā hanūmantam abhidravat || 6-52-33

33. vīryavān = the valiant; dhūmrākṣaḥ = Dhumraksha; udyamya = lifted; gadām = his mace; vinardamānaḥ = making a roaring sound; abhidravat = ran; tam hanumantam = towards that ḥanuma; āpatantam = attacking on him; sahasā = suddenly.

The valiant Dhumraksha lifted his mace and making a roaring sound, ran towards that Hanuman who was rushing on him suddenly.

Sloka 34

tataḥ kruddhas tu vegena gadām tām bahu kaṇṭakām |
pātayām āsa dhūmra akṣo mastake tu hanūmataḥ || 6-52-34

34. atha = thereafter; dhūmrākṣaḥ = Dhumraksha; roṣeṇa = with an outrage; pātayāmāsa = stroke down; tām gadām = that mace; bahukaṇṭakām = with spikes; mastake = on the head; tasya hanūmataḥ = of that ḥanuman.

Thereafter, Dhumraksha with an outrage, brought down that mace studded with countless spikes on the head of that Hanuma.

Sloka 35

tāḍitah sa tayā tatra gadayā bhīma rūpayā |
sa kapir māruta balas tam prahāram acintayan || 6-52-35
dhūmra akṣasya śiro madhye giri śṛngam apātayat |

35. saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuman; saḥ = the famous one; māruta balaḥ = with an energy similar to the wind; tāDitaḥ = struck; tatra = there; tayā gadayā = by that mace; bhīmavegayā = with a terrific velocity; achintayan = disregarding; tam prahāram = that blow; apātayat = threw down; girishṛiṇ^gam = his rocky peak; shiromadhye = on the middle of the skull; dhūmrākṣasya = of Dhumraksha.

That Hanuman, who was endowed with an energy similar to the wind, was in no way disturbed by that blow but struck Dhumraksha on the middle of his skull with his rocky peak.

Sloka 36

sa vihvalita sarva ango giri śṛngeṇa tāḍitaḥ || 6-52-36
papāta sahasā bhūmau vikīrṇa;iva parvataḥ |

36. saḥ = that Dhumraksha; tāDitaḥ = struck; girishṛiṇ^gena = by the rocky peak; visphārita sarvāṇgaḥ = having his shattered limbs; sahasā = soon; papāta = fell down; bhūmau = on the ground; parvataḥ iva = like a mountain; vikīrṇaḥ = crumbling.

That Dhumraksha, struck by the rocky peak, which shattered all his limbs, soon fell down on the ground like a mountain crumbling.

Sloka 37

dhūmra akṣam nihatam dṛṣṭvā hata śeṣā niśā carāḥ || 6-52-37
trastāḥ praviviśur lankām vadhyamānāḥ plavam gamaiḥ |

37. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; dhūmrakṣam = Dhumraksha; nihatam = having been killed; nishācharāḥ = the demons; hata sheṣāḥ = left surviving; trastāḥ = were frightened; vadhyamānāḥ = being killed; plavaṇ^gamaiḥ = by the monkeys; pravivishuḥ = entered; laṇ^kām = Lanka.

Seeing Dhumraksha having been killed, the demons left surviving were frightened of being killled by the monkeys and re entered Lanka.

Sloka 38

sa tu pavana suto nihatya śatrum |
kṣataja vahāh saritaś ca samvikīrya |
ripu vadha janita śramo mahātmā |
mudam agamat kapibhiś ca pūjyamānaḥ || 6-52-38

38. saḥ = that; mahātmā = illustrious; pavanasutaḥ = ḥanuma the son of Pavana; nihatya = having killed; shatrūn = his enemies; vahāḥ = causing; saritashcha = rivers; kṣataja = of blood; samvikīrya = to flow; ripu vadhajanita = shramaḥ = weary of slaughter of enemies; mudam = with delight; agamat = received; supūjyamānaḥ = the cordial felicitations; kapibhiḥ = by the monkeys.

That illustrious Hanuman the son of Pavana having destroyed his enemies, causing rivers of blood to flow, weary of slaughter of the enemies, with delight, received the cordial felicitations by the monkeys.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvipañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 52nd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
