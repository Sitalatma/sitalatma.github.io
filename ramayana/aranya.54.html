Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 54
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha drops her ornaments at Sugreeva and other monkeys available on a mountaintop during the course of her abduction. Ravana does not take notice of this as he is hurrying as though haunted by Rama. On crossing the southerly ocean, his jitteriness in stealing Seetha ceases and he hypocritically feels elated in gaining both Seetha and an animosity with Rama.


Sloka 1

hriyamāṇā tu vaidehī kaṃcit nātham apaśyatī |
dadarśa giri śṛṃgasthān paṃca vānara puṃgavān || 3-54-1

1. hriyamāṇā vaidehī = being abducted, Vaidehi; nātham = a rescuer; kamcit a pashyatī = none, not, seen; giri shṛingasthān = mountain, on top staying; panca vānara pungavān = five, Vanara, prominent ones [ṣugreeva, ḥanuma et al]; dadarsha = she saw.

Vaidehi has not seen any rescuer while she is abducted, but she has seen five prominent vanara-s staying on a mountaintop. [3-54-1]

Sloka 2

teṣām madhye viśālākṣī kauśeyam kanaka prabham |
uttarīyam varārohā śubhāni ābharaṇāni ca || 3-54-2
mumoca yadi rāmāya śaṃseyuḥ iti bhāminī |

2, 3a. vishālākṣī = broad-eyed one; varārohā = curvaceous lady; bhāminī = lady with resentment; kanaka prabham kausheyam uttarīyam = golden, in hue, silk sari, upper cloth; shubhāni ābharaṇāni ca = auspicious, ornaments, also; rāmāya = to Rama; shamseyuḥ yadi = they indicate, if [they may]; iti = thus [on thinking]; teṣām madhye mumoca = their, amid, released - dropped.

That broad-eyed and curvaceous lady with resentment Seetha, inwrapping her auspicious ornaments in the upper-fringe of her sari, dropped in the midmost of those five Vanara-s with a thinking that 'these creatures may perhaps indicate them to Rama.' [3-54-2, 3a]

▼

Some say that Seetha shore her upper cloth and bundled the ornaments while some others say that whole of the upper cloth was dropped. The Indian sari worn by those women of yore was in a different fashion, where they wore sari like the present day dhoti with pleated back stuffing like men, but they also used an upper cloth enough to cover their busts. A three-piece dress is a must, either to man or a woman in Indian style. It comprises of a loincloth dhoti or sari, and a shirt-like or a jacket-like bust cover, and an upper-cloth to hang on the shoulder to men or to muffle the bust for women. So some say the whole of upper cloth of Seetha is dropped bundled with ornaments.

Sloka 3

vastram utsṛjya tan madhye vinikṣiptam sa bhūṣaṇam || 3-54-3
saṃbhramāt tu daśagrīvaḥ tat karma na ca buddhvān |

3b, 4a. saha bhūṣaṇam vastram = along with, ornaments, upper cloth; utsṛijya = up, whipping; tan madhye = those [five vanara-s,] amongst; vi nikṣiptam = which is verily [carefully,] stowed away - that bundle; saḥ dashagrīvaḥ tu = he, Decahedron, on his part; sambhramāt = owing to his hurry; tat karma na buddhvān = that, deed, not, noticed [unbothered.]

On his part that Decahedron Ravana has not noticed her action in whipping up her silky upper-cloth and ornaments, their bundling and stowing away at five vanara-s, owing to his own hurry. [3-54-3]

▼

Ravana is in his own hurry because Rama may have heard the wailing of Seetha and thus may be chasing him, or will chase his shortly. He bothers like this until he crosses the ocean and enters his city-state Lanka. There he is absolutely secured. Until such time his 'mousy thievishness' haunts him. But Seetha is on his flank and under his arm, her trunk turned against his. Then she shortly saw the oddish Vanara-s, who look unlike ordinary monkeys, but with some presumably pro-human and non-demonic features, yet monkeyish with some regality, and then she quickly plucked off her ornaments and upcast her upper-cloth, and hastily but carefully, bundled her jewellery and downcast that bundle at them.

▼

These ornaments are said to be one nosering, one earring, one bracelet, one biceps-let, one anklet with tinkling bells - all from her right side - because showing ornaments/items worn on left-side to husband is held improper. The anklet that has fallen from her leg on earth, in earlier chapters, is from her left leg.

Sloka 4

piṃgākṣāḥ tām viśālākṣīm netraiḥ animiṣaiḥ iva || 3-54-4
vikrośantīm tadā sītām dadṛśuḥ vānara ṛṣabhāḥ |

4b, 5a. pinga akṣāḥ = ochreish, eyed ones; vānara ṛiṣabhāḥ = Vanara, bullish [best] ones; vishāla akṣīm = at broad, eyed [ṣeetha]; tadā = then; vi kroshantīm = verily, wailing; tām sītām = at her, at ṣeetha; a nimiṣaiḥ iva = not, winking, like; netraiḥ = with eyes; dadṛishuḥ = they observed.

Those ochreish-eyed best Vanara-s with their unwinking eyes have observed the broad-eyed Seetha, who at that time is verily wailing. [3-54-4b, 5a]

▼

The 'wink-less-ness' is the property of celestials, thus the vanara-s saw with wink-less eyes with their wide-eyed amazement. Here the Vanara-s are said to be bulishly impetuous beings and if so, could they not have averted or countered Ravana - will be the question. It is a regular scene for them to see this skywalker demon to carry off such goddess like ladies and they have seen many women thus carried off. But this particular one is appearing to be somewhat different, more over she is repeatedly calling for some 'Rama...' and she is wriggling in that demon's capture. They know this demon to be Ravana and they even know of the alliance of Ravana with Vali. If these handfuls of vanara-s dare Ravana, they die at the hands of Ravana instead at the hand of Vali. Hence, they remained silent spectators to save themselves. This is why Sugreeva did not indicate the way Seetha in which was abducted by 'some' demon, to Rama, in Kishkindha, when they firstly met.

Sloka 5

sa ca paṃpām atikramya laṃkām abhimukhaḥ purīm || 3-54-5
jagāma rudatīm gṛhya maithilīm rākṣasa īśvaraḥ |

5b, 6a. saḥ rākṣasa īshvaraḥ = he, demons, chief; pampām atikramya = Pampa [area,] on going across; lankām purīm abhimukhaḥ = to Lanka, city, facing towards; rudatīm maithilīm gṛihya = bewailing, ṃaithili, taking [captivating]; jagāma = advanced.

The chief of demons Ravana captivating ṃaithili, who is still wailing, has crossed the Pampa region down underneath and advanced facing the City of Lanka. [3-54-5b, 6a]

▼

So far Ravana's travel is proceeding with his looking backward, sideward, and frontward, expecting any attack from anywhere, as this Dandaka or Janasthāna are his frontiers for operation but not the areas of his stronghold. And when once these areas are crossed reaching Pampa, there is no more self-confessed fear.

Sloka 6

tām jahāra susaṃhṛṣṭo rāvaṇo mṛtyum ātmanaḥ || 3-54-6
utsaṃgena eva bhujagīm tīkṣṇa daṃṣṭrām mahāviṣām |

6b, 7a. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana is; su sam hṛiṣṭaḥ = very, highly, happily; ātmanaḥ mṛityum = for himself, death; tām = her; tīkṣṇa danṣṭrām mahā viṣām = of stinging, fanged, lethally, venomous; bhujagīm iva = she-snake, as with; utsangena eva = by lap, thus; jahāra = stole.

Ravana is jubilantly happy in stealing his own death alias Seetha, as one would carry a stingingly fanged and lethally venomous she-snake in his own lap. [3-54-6b, 7a]

Sloka 7

vanāni saritaḥ śailān sarāṃsi ca vihāyasā || 3-54-7
sa kṣipram samatīyāya śaraḥ cāpāt iva cyutaḥ |

7b, 8a. saḥ = he; cāpāt cyutaḥ sharaḥ iva = from bow, bolting, arrow, as with; kṣipram = at the double; vanāni saritaḥ shailān sarāmsi ca = woodlands, waterways, mounts, pools, also; vihāyasā = by skyway; sam atīyāya = well crossed over - bolted out.

He bolted out in skyway up above the woodlands, waterways, pools, and mountains at the double, as with an arrow bolting from a bow. [3-4-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8

timi nakra niketam tu varuṇa ālayam akṣayam || 3-54-8
saritām śaraṇam gatvā samatīyāya sāgaram |

8b, 9a. timi nakra niketam = sharks, alligators, housing of; varuṇa ālayam = Rain-god's, home; a kṣayam = never, decreasing - never-draining - ocean; saritām sharaṇam = for rivers, [ultimate] course; sāgaram = ocean; gatvā = on reaching; sam atīyāya = well [hastily] crossed over.

On reaching the ocean, a housing for sharks, alligators, a home of Rain-god, a never-draining deep and an ultimate course of the rivers, he hastily crossed it over. [3-54-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

saṃbhramāt parivṛtta ūrmī ruddha mīna mahoragaḥ || 3-54-9
vaidehyām hriyamāṇāyām babhūva varuṇa ālayaḥ |

9b, 10a. vaidehyām hriyamāṇāyām = of Vaidehi, being abducted; varuṇa ālayaḥ = Rain-god's, abode [ocean]; sambhramāt = by high flustering; parivṛitta ūrmī = topsy-turvy, waves; ruddha mina mahā uragaḥ = deterred [stopped hesitantly, shilly-shally] fishes, great, [water] snakes [marine reptiles]; babhūva = became.

That ocean being the abode of Rain-god is highly flustered when Vaidehi is being abducted over it, and its waves became topsy-turvy and its fishes and great marine-reptiles remained shilly-shally. [3-54-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

antarikṣa gatā vācaḥ sasṛjuḥ cāraṇāḥ tadā || 3-54-10
etat anto daśagrīva iti siddhāḥ tadā abruvan |

10b, 11a. tadā = then; antarikṣa gatā cāraṇāḥ = airspace, gone in [available,] cārana-s; dashagrīva etat antaḥ = Decahedron, this, as end - he ends with this; iti = thus; vācaḥ sasṛijuḥ = words, released [averred]; tadā = likewise; siddhāḥ abruvan = siddha-s, said.

Then the siddha-s and cārana-s available in airspace averred saying, "this alone is the end of Decahedron Ravana." [3-54-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11

sa tu sītām viceṣṭantīm aṃkena ādāya rāvaṇaḥ || 3-54-11
praviveśa purīm lankām rūpiṇīm mṛtyum ātmanaḥ |

11b, 12a. saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = he, Ravana; ātmanaḥ = for himself - Ravana's; rūpiṇīm mṛityum = by persona [in propria persona,] death - a personified death; vi ceṣṭantīm = who is verily, wriggling; such; sītām ankena ādāya = ṣeetha, on flank, taking; lankām purīm pravivesha = Lanka, city, he entered.

Ravana entered the city of Lanka transporting Seetha, while she wriggling in his flank, who appeared like the personified death of Ravana wriggling its way into his Lanka. [3-54-11b, 12a]

Sloka 12

saḥ abhigamya purīm laṃkām suvibhakta mahāpathām || 3-54-12
saṃrūḍha kakṣyā bahulam svam aṃtaḥ puram āviśat |

12b, 13a. saḥ = he; su vi bhakta = neatly, separately, divided, [well-symmetrized]; mahā pathām = wide, roadways - having; lankām purīm = to Lanka, city; abhi gamya = towards, on going; sam rūDha = well, thronged; kakṣyā bahulam = doors [houses / chambers,] many - having; svam antaḥ puram āvishat = his own, palace, chambers, entered.

On going towards the city Lanka that has well-symmetrized roadways, then he entered his own palace-chambers which is with very many doors and chambers thronging with crowds.

or

On going towards the city Lanka that has well-symmetrized roadways, he then entered his own palace chambers in which royal servants are thronging at many doorways as that palace has very many chambers. [3-54-12b, 13a]

Sloka 13

tatra tām asita apāṃgām śoka moha parāyaṇām || 3-54-13
nidadhe rāvaṇaḥ sītām mayo māyām iva āsurīm |

13b, 14a. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; asita apāngām = one with blackish, eyelashes; shoka moha parāyaṇām = by whimpering, bewildered, deep in; tām sītām = her, ṣeetha; mayaḥ = ṃaya, the demon; āsurīm māyām iva = demonic, maya, as with [guhām iti sheṣaḥ = black hole, thus ellipted]; [or, māyām iva striyam = ṃaya, as with, a lady-love]; tatra nidadhe = therein, he placed her down.

Ravana placed down Seetha whose eyelashes are blackish and who is deep in bewilderment caused by whimpering, therein his palace chambers, as demon ṃaya once kept his lady-love, a nymphet called Hema, in a demonic Black Hole.

or

Ravana placed down Seetha whose eyelashes are blackish and who is deep in bewilderment caused by whimpering, therein his palace chambers, as demon ṃaya kept his demonic power in a Black Hole.

or

Ravana placed down Seetha whose eyelashes are blackish and who is deep in bewilderment caused by whimpering, therein his palace chambers, as demon ṃaya had once kept al his demonic scriptures duly usurping them from his own mentor, namely Shukracārya, at an unknown illusory place. [3-54-13b, 14a]

▼

This verse has some divergences from the viewpoints of commentators. Govindaraja's text uses māyām iva striyam while Tilaka uses mayaḥ māyām iva āsurīm thereby the word 'āsurrīm...' suggests that Ravana brought a māyā sīta 'an illusory Seetha...' instead of original Seetha, which original Seetha is said to have been in safe keeping of Ritual-fire until the elimination of Ravana, and comes out of the same Ritual-fire after the elimination of Ravana. A very big controversy surrounds this statement and whole of the auxiliary Ramayana-s are brought in, along with purāṇa-s, which substantiate this riddler, māyā sīta.

▼

As it is, we do not find that much debatable aspect in Valmiki Ramayana, if the statement that 'Ravana placed Seetha in an unapproachable Black Hole like Lanka, as ṃaya once placed Hema in an unapproachable underground Black Hole...' is taken up. This episode of demon ṃaya-Hema occurs in Kishkindha, ch. 51, which may please be referred. Then it suffices to satisfy the verbiage of āsurīm, when the analogy of 1] Lanka of Ravana and ṛiksha bila, 'Black Hole' of ṃaya, both being the constructions of artifice, and again between 2] ṃaya, the demon and Ravana, the demon, [that too, the eliminable demons,] 3] nymphet Hema and nymphal Seetha, [the causative factors for elimination,] and the actual eliminators Indra and Rama, is taken into consideration.

▼

The eyes of Seetha are said to be blackish at their ends. This is not mascara or something like a makeup article. The eyelashes are longish like those of celestials, so as to give a blackish orb around her eyeball and because she is now ceaselessly shedding tears, these eyelashes are tear-wet and slanted edge-ward to give a blackish shade at the end of her eyes.

Sloka 14

abravīt ca daśagrīvaḥ piśācīḥ ghora darśanāḥ || 3-54-14
yathā na enām pumān strī vā sītām paśyati asammataḥ |

14b, 15a. dashagrīvaḥ = Decahedron; ghora darshanāḥ pishācīḥ = bogie, for sight, to bogeywomen; abravīt ca = spoke [instructed,] also; a sammataḥ = not, permitted; pumān strī vā = male, female, either; enām sītām = at her, ṣeetha; yathā na pashyati = as to how, not, sees; [tathā bhaviṣyatha = thus, you shall conduct - your watch and ward shall be.]

That Decahedron Ravana also instructed the bogey-sightly bogeywomen, "your watch and ward shall be in such a way that neither a female nor a male can see Seetha impermissibly." [3-54-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

muktā maṇi suvarṇāni vastrāṇi ābharaṇāni ca || 3-54-15
yat yat icchet tat eva asyā deyam mat cchaṃdato yathā |

15b, 16a. muktā maṇi suvarṇāni = pearls, jewels, gold; vastrāṇi ābharaṇāni ca = apparels, adornments, also; yat yat icchet = which, which, she wishes; tat eva = they all, thus; asyāḥ = to her; mat cchandataḥ yathā = to me, as per my whims, as with - as you give to me as per my wish; deyam = be given.

"Whatever she wishes to have, gold, jewels, pearls, apparels or adornments, they all be given to her as you would give me according to my whimsies. [3-54-15b, 16a]

Sloka 16

yā ca vakṣyati vaidehīm vacanam kiṃcit apriyam || 3-54-16
ajñānāt yadi vā jñānān na tasyā jīvitam priyam |

16b, 17a. yā ca = she who, also - whoever; a GYānāt = not, knowingly; yadi vā = else, or; GYānāt = knowingly; vaidehīm = to Vaidehi; kimcit = in the least; a priyam = not, pleasant; vacanam vakṣyati = words, if speaks; tasyāḥ jīvitam na priyam = her, life, not, to be pleasant.

"Whosoever speaks unpleasant words to Vaidehi in the least, either unknowingly or knowingly, her life becomes unpleasant for herself." Thus Ravana ordered the chamber-demonesses. [3-54-16b, 17a]

Sloka 17

tathā uktvā rākṣasīḥ tāḥ tu rākṣasendraḥ pratāpavān || 3-54-17
niṣkramya antaḥ purāt tasmāt kim kṛtyam iti ciṃtayan |
dadarśa aṣṭau mahāvīryān rākṣasān piśita aśanān || 3-54-18

17b, 18. pratāpavān = valorous one; rākṣasa indraḥ = demon's, chief; tāḥ rākṣasīḥ = to those. to demonesses; tathā uktvā = thus, on speaking; tu = on his part; tasmāt antaḥ purāt niṣkramya = from that, palace, chambers, on exiting; kim kṛityam = what, to do [next]; iti cintayan = thus, thinking; mahā vīryān = highly, vigorous ones; pishita ashanān = raw-flesh, feasters of; aṣṭau rākṣasān dadarsha = eight, demons, he saw - gave audience.

On speaking to those demonesses thus that chief of demons exited from that palace chamber, and giving a thought to what is to be done in sequel, then he gave audience to eight highly vigorous demons, the feasters on raw-flesh. [3-54-17b, 18]

Sloka 19

sa tān dṛṣṭvā mahāvīryo vara dānena mohitaḥ |
uvāca tān idam vākyam praśasya bala vīryataḥ || 3-54-19

19. mahā vīryaḥ = great-mighty [Ravana]; vara dānena mohitaḥ = boon, awarded, beguiled by [egocentric by the boon once accorded to him]; saḥ = he, Ravana; bala vīryataḥ = by [their] might, valour [of eight demons]; prashasya = praising them; tān = them; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing [on giving an audience]; tān idam vākyam uvāca = to them, this, sentence, spoke.

He who is egocentric by the boon awarded by Brahma for his invincibleness, that Ravana on giving an audience to those eight demons spoke this sentence to them praiseful of their might and valour. [3-54-19]

Sloka 20

nānā praharaṇāḥ kṣipram ito gacchata satvarāḥ |
janasthānam hata sthānam bhūta pūrvam khara ālayam || 3-54-20

20. nānā pra haraṇāḥ [gṛihītvā ] = multiple, highly, assaultive weaponry [on taking]; sa tvarāḥ = with, haste - speedily; itaḥ kṣipram = from here, immediately; hata sthānam = [demons] killed, place of [a deadpanned place of demons]; khara ālayam = Khara's, abode [placement]; bhūta pūrvam = which was, earlier; janasthānam = to Janasthāna; gacchata = begone.

"You immediately take up multiple assaultive weaponry and begone from here to Janasthāna speedily, a place now deadpanned for demons, which earlier was the placement of Khara. [3-54-20]

Sloka 21

tatra uṣyatām janasthāne śūnye nihata rākṣase |
pauruṣam balam āśritya trāsam utsṛjya dūrataḥ || 3-54-21

21. pauruṣam balam āshritya = [your own] self-assurance, self-confidence, depending on; trāsam dūrataḥ utsṛijya = fright, faraway, throwaway; nihata rākṣase = killed, demons - where demons are killed; shūnye = fell void; tatra janasthāne = there, in Janasthāna; uṣyatām = you stay.

"Throwaway your fright faraway and you stay in that Janasthāna which fell void as the demons are voided by someone called Rama, and there you shall stay self-assuredly and self-confidently. [3-54-21]

▼

Then those eight demons may as, 'when these many leading warriors and commanders are there why we eight are picked and chosen?' for this Ravana is saying 'you eight are self-assured and self-confident and can to mete out any assault on our race...' and again they may hesitate as to 'why we should depend on our own personal strengths...' for that Ravana is saying 'a still mightier Dushana, still mightiest Khara are eliminated... thus you perk up your sprites, personally...'

Sloka 22

bahu sainyam mahāvīryam janasthāne niveśitam |
sa dūṣaṇa kharam yuddhe nihatam rāma sāyakaiḥ || 3-54-22

22. mahāvīryam bahu sainyam = highly forceful, many, forces; janasthāne niveshitam = in Janasthāna, [though] deployed; rāma sāyakaiḥ = Rama, by [his] arrows; yuddhe nihatam = in combat, utterly destroyed; sa dūṣaṇa kharam = along with, Dūshana, Khara.

"Though highly forceful forces are deployed in Janasthāna, Rama's arrows have utterly destroyed them, together with Khara and Dūshana. [3-54-22]

▼

Expecting another enquiry from them as, 'when such mighty, mightier, and mightiest demons are dead at the hand of a lonely Rama, do you wish us also die in the same way?' and to them Ravana is saying 'you are the next best in line of order of military, hence it is your turn to assume command, as committed soldiers, as I am at loggerheads with Rama...' Ravana follows such 'order of priorities' in warring scrupulously and we chance to meet them in Yuddha Kanda, canto of Great War.

Sloka 23

tataḥ krodho mama apūrvo dhairyasya upari vardhate |
vairam ca sumahat jātam rāmam prati sudāruṇam || 3-54-23

23. tataḥ = thereby; mama = my; a pūrvaḥ = not, precedented; krodhaḥ = fury; dhairyasya upari vardhate = of [my] courage, [over and] above, escalating; rāmam prati = Rama, towards; su mahat = verily, heightened [unhindered]; su dāruṇam = utterly, unforgivable; vairam ca = animosity, also; jātam = is evoked;

"Thereby an unprecedented fury is escalating over and above my courage, and an unhindered and unforgivable animosity towards that Rama is evoked in me. [3-54-23]

▼

Then the eight commandant demons may say 'what if, let all of us wage a war and end him up...' for that Ravana is saying suggestively that 'my fury is hovering over my courage... thus fury alone is scaling up, while courage is scaling down... as I came to know that only one was warring against fourteen thousand demons and eliminated all... thus we cannot wage large scale war overtly when our courage is lessening... but we can covertly do some kind of harm... hence, you go there and undertake any kind of activity that will be harmful to that Rama... as I am uncourageous and uncomfortable, as of now...'

Sloka 24

niryātayitum icchāmi tat ca vairam aham ripoḥ |
na hi lapsyāmi aham nidrām ahatvā saṃyuge ripum || 3-54-24

24. aham = I; ripoḥ = enemy's [who bears a grudge against me]; tat vairam = that, animosity; nir yātayitum icchāmi = to out, cast, I wish; sanyuge ripum a hatvā = in war, enemy, without, eliminating; aham nidrām na lapsyāmi hi = I, sleep, not, getting, indeed.

"I wish to cast out my begrudging against my enemy who bears a grudge against me, and indeed, to me 'sleep' is not the word for it, until my enemy is undone in a war. [3-54-24]

▼

Sloka 25

tam tu idānīm aham hatvā khara dūṣaṇa ghātinam |
rāmam śarma upalapsyāmi dhanam labdhvā iva nirdhanaḥ || 3-54-25

25. aham idānīm = I, now - I for myself; khara dūṣaṇa ghātinam = Khara, Dūshana, killer of; tam rāmam hatvā = him, Rama, on eliminating; [yadi = if I can kill him]; nir dhanaḥ = not, a rich one - pauper; dhanam labdhvā iva = riches, on acquiring, as with; sharma upalapsyāmi = peace [comfortableness,] I acquire.

"I for myself will be comfortable only on eliminating him who has eliminated Khara and Dūshana as with a pauper acquiring riches. [3-54-25]

Sloka 26

janasthāne vasadbhiḥ tu bhavadbhiḥ rāmam āśritā |
pravṛttiḥ upanetavyā kim karoti iti tattvataḥ || 3-54-26

26. janasthāne vasadbhiḥ = in Janasthāna, while you remain; bhavadbhiḥ = by you; kim karoti iti = what, [he] does, thus; rāmam āshritā pravṛittiḥ = of on Rama, dependant, deportment - how he deports himself; tattvataḥ upanetavyā = accurately, be brought [to me.]

"While you remain in Janasthāna you have to accurately bring me the information about Rama's activities and deportment. [3-54-26]

Sloka 27

apramādāt ca gaṃtavyam sarvaiḥ eva niśācaraiḥ |
kartavyaḥ ca sadā yatno rāghavasya vadham prati || 3-54-27

27. nishācaraiḥ sarvaiḥ eva = by night-walkers, by all [of you,] thus; a pramādāt gantavyam = not, heedlessly, you have to go [there]; rāghavasya vadham prati = Raghava's, killing, apropos; sadā yatnaḥ kartavyaḥ ca = continually, [every] trial, is doable [conceivable,] also.

"Do not go heedlessly, you all night-walkers, for that Rama appears to be too dangerous, and on your going there heedfully, you have to continually try for the elimination of that Raghava. [3-54-27]

Sloka 28

yuṣmākam tu balam jñātam bahuśo raṇa mūrdhani |
ataḥ tu asmin janasthāne mayā yūyam niyojitāḥ || 3-54-28

28. raṇa mūrdhani = in battle, fronts; bahusha = many times; yuṣmākam balam GYātam = your, might, I know; ataḥ tu = by that reason, only; yūyam = you all; asmin janasthāne = in that, Janasthāna; mayā niyojitāḥ = by me, commissioned.

"I am aware of your might in battle fronts for a number of times, as such I have commissioned you all in that Janasthāna." Thus Ravana spoke to those eight demons. [3-54-28]

Sloka 29

tataḥ priyam vākyam upetya rākṣasā
mahāartham aṣṭau abhivādya rāvaṇam |
vihāya laṃkām sahitāḥ pratasthire
yato janasthānam alakṣya darśanāḥ || 3-54-29

29. tataḥ = then; aṣṭau rākṣasā = eight, demons; priyam = amiable; mahā artham = with important, purpose [or, words with allusions]; vākyam upetya = words [of Ravana,] on picking up; rāvaṇam abhivādya = Ravana, hailing; lankām vihāya = Lanka, departing from; sahitāḥ = collectively; a lakṣya darshanāḥ = not, perceivable, with appearances - unseen by anyone; janasthānam yataḥ = Janasthāna, at where it is - in that direction; pratasthire = journeyed to.

Then on picking up those words amiable to all intents and purposes of Ravana, those eight demons departed from Lanka on hailing Ravana, and they collectively journeyed in the direction of Janasthāna with their imperceivable appearances. [3-54-29]

Sloka 30

tataḥ tu sītām upalabhya rāvaṇaḥ
susaṃprahṛṣṭaḥ parigṛhya maithilīm |
prasajya rāmeṇa ca vairam uttamam
babhūva mohāt muditaḥ sa rākṣasaḥ || 3-54-30

30. tataḥ = then; rākṣasaḥ saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = demon, he, that Ravana; sītām upalabhya = ṣeetha, on gaining; maithilīm parigṛihya = ṃaithili, holding sway over; su sam pra hṛiṣṭaḥ = well, truly, highly, gladdened; rāmeṇa = with Rama; uttamam vairam = archly, animosity; pra sajya = well-wrought; mohāt = by hypocrisy; muditaḥ = amused; babhūva = he became.

Ravana is then highly gladdened well and truly on gaining over Seetha, and even for holding sway over that ṃaithili, and equally for the well-wrought and archly animosity with Rama, whereby that demon Ravana is hypocritically amused. [3-54-30]

▼

The wording in this verse mohāt mudiataḥ sa rākshasaḥ together with the word hṛiṣṭaḥ rāvaṇaḥ at verse 6, is discussed by commentators stating that he is self-conceitedly and hypocritically lost in his own hallucinations of possessing Seetha. Tilaka commentary says that bringing Seetha to Lanka is a villainous act but not with an iota of reverence to her, when elucidating verse 1 of next chapter where it contains: ātmānam buddhi vaiklbyāt kṛita kṛityam amanyata... 'he believed in his own self that he achieved a great feat, as his brain gone astray foolishly...' But the poet also says proximately that he is amused in gaining an archenemy's animosity, i.e. that of Rama. It is therefore said that he is happy as his release from his accursed demon-hood is set to happen soon, as he achieved the abduction of Seetha, thus paved way for Rama to come to Lanka to release him from his accursed being. This is supported by the statement in verse that Ravana's words have some mahā artham 'words with allusion, or secret message' to Rama perhaps, as at verse 29.

▼

If Lanka is a body, it embodies pleasure seeking traits seated in manas 'pleasuring heart...' along with an 'ever witnessing soul...' called sākshi būta ātma. Along with the release of captivated soul, called Seetha, this pleasure seeking manas 'heart' also gets released. And the releaser or the deliverer is Rama, and hence Ravana is happy.

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe catuḥ paṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 54th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
