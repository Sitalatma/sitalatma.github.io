Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

Introduction

Hanuma recounts to Bharata, broad details relating to the soujourn of Rama, Seetha and Lakshmana in the forest from the day Bharata takes wooden sandals of Rama, till the day Ravana is killed.

Sloka 1

bahūni nāma varṣāṇi gatasya sumahadvanam |
śṛṇomyahaṃ prītikaraṃ mama nāthasya kīrtanam || 6-126-1

1. aham = I; shṛiṇomi nāma = am indeed hearing; prītikaram = a delightful; kīrtanam = mention; mama nāthasya = of my lord; gatasya = who went; sumahat = to the vast; vanam = forest; bahūni varṣhāṇi = so many years ago.

"I am indeed hearing a delight mention of my lord, who proceeded to the vast forest so many years ago.

Sloka 2

kalyāṇī bata gātheyaṃ laukikī pratibhāti me |
eti jīvantamānando naraṃ varṣaśatādapi || 6-126-2

2. iyam = this; laukikī = common; gathā = saying; pratibhāti = sounds; mām = to me; kalyāṇī = good; ānandaḥ = that bliss; eti = comes; jīvantam naram = to a surviving man; varṣhashatādapi = even if it be after a hundred years!.

"This common saying sounds to me good, that bliss comes to a surviving man, even if it be after a hundred years!"

Sloka 3

rāghavasya harīṇāṃ ca kathamāsītsamāgamaḥ |
kasmindeśe kimāśritya tattvamākhyāhi pṛcchataḥ || 6-126-3

3. katham = how; kim āshritya = and being supported by what; samāgamaḥ = an alliance; āsīt = came to be concluded; rāghavasya acha kapīnāmcha = between Rama and he monkeys; kashmin deshe = and at what place?; ākhyāhi tattvam = tell the truth to me; pṛichchhataḥ = enquiring as I am.

"How and on what ground, an alliance has been concluded between Rama and the monkeys and at what place? Tell the truth to me, enquiring as I am."

Sloka 4

sa pṛṣṭo rājaputreṇa bṛsyāṃ samupaveśitaḥ |
ācacakṣe tataḥ sarvaṃ rāmasya caritaṃ vane || 6-126-4

4. pṛiṣhṭaḥ = when asked; rājaputreṇa = by Bharata; samupaveshitaḥ = after making ḥanuma to sit; bṛisyām = on a cushion; saḥ = ḥanuma; tataḥ = thereupon; āchachakṣhe = began to narrate; sarve = the entire; charitam = exploits; rāmasya = fo Rama; vane = in the forest.

Seated comfortably on a cushion and interrogated by Bharata, Hanuma thereupon began to narrate the entire exploits of Rama in the forest.

5-9. prabho = O lord; mahābāho = the long armed!; yathā = how; tava = your; mātuḥ = mother; dattau = was bestowed with; varau = two boons (by your father); rāmaḥ = how Rama; pravrājitaḥ = was sent to exile; yathā = how; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; rājā = the king; mṛitaḥ = died; yathā = how; rājyam = sovereignty; na cha īpsitam = was not coveted; tvayā = by you; praviṣhṭena = who entered; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; yathā = how; tvayā = by you; ācharatā dharmam = while following righteousness; gatvā = and going; chitrakūṭa girim = to ṃount chitrakuta bhrātā = Rama your elder brother; amitrakarshanaḥ = the tormentator of enemies; nimantritaḥ = was invited; rājyena = to take back the kingdom; yathā = how; rāJṇaḥ vachane sthitena = Rama who stood by the king's words; visarjitam = abandoned; rājyam = the sovereignty; yathā = how; punaḥ āgataḥ asi = you returned; gṛihya = taking; pāduke = the wooden sandals; āsyasya = of your brother; sarvam etat = all this; viditam = is known; tava = to you; yathāvat = exactly; me nibodha = you know from me; yat = what; vṛittam = occured; tvayi pratiprayāte = since you returned to Ayodhya.

"O Lord, the long-armed! How your mother was conferred with two boons by your father, how Rama was sent to exile, how Dasaratha the king died, with a shocking sorrow for his son's exile, how you were brought quickly from Rajagriha (the seat of government of Kekaya kings) by the envoys, how sovereignty was not coveted by you when you returned to Ayodhya, how on your going to mount chitrakuta, your, elder brother the tormentator of enemies was invited by you who followed righteousness to take back the kingdom, how Rama who stood by his father's words, renounced the kingdom and stood by his father's words, renounced the kingdom and how your returned to Ayodhya, taking with you the wooden sandals all this is known accurately to you. Hear from me now, that which occurred after you had returned to Ayodhya."

Sloka 10

apayāte tvayi tadā samudbhrāntamṛgadvijam |
praviveśātha vijanaṃ sumahaddaṇḍakāvanam || 6-126-10

10. tvayi apayāte = when you had gone away; tadvanam = that forest (of chitrakuta); samapadyata = assumed; tadā = then atyartham paridyūnam iva = a very miserable appearance; samudbhrārta mṛigadvijam = with the frightened animals and birds ( on seeing strange people in the forest).

"When you had gone away, that forest (of chitrakuta) then assumed a very miserable appearance, with the frightened animals and birds (on seeing strange people there)."

Sloka 11

11. saḥ = that Rama; atha = thereupon; pravivesha = entered; tatmahat = that large; ghoram = terrific; viJṇanam = and solitary; daṇ^Dakāranam = forest of Dandaka; taddhastimṛiditam = which had been trampled by elephants; simhavyāghra mṛigākulam = and extensive with lions; tigers and deers.

"Rama, thereupon, entered that large, terrific and solitary forest of Dandaka, which had been trampled by elephants and extensive with lions, tigers and deers."

Sloka 12

12. purastāt = in front; teṣhām = of them; gachchhatām = even as they were going; gahane vane = in that dense forest; pratyadṛishyata = there appeared; virādhaḥ = Viradha; a demon; vinadan = uttering forth; sumahānādan = a very loud roar.

"In front of them, even as they were going in that dense forest, there appeared Viradha, a demon, uttering forth a very loud roar.

Sloka 13

tamutkṣipya mahānādamūrdhvabāhumadhomukham |
nikhāte prakṣipanti sma nadantamiva kuñjaram || 6-126-13

13. tam prakṣhipantisma = (ṭhey) cast him; utkṣhpya ūrdhvabāhum = who rushed with uplifted arms towards them; nikhāte = into a pit; adho mukham = turning his head bent low; nadantam = and who was emitting a loud cry; kuṇ^jaramiva = like an elephant.

"They cast him, who rushed with uplifted arms towards them, into a pit, turning his head bent low, and who was emitting a loud cry, like an elephant."

Sloka 14

tatkṛtvā duṣkaraṃ karma bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |

14. kṛitvā = carrying out; tat = that; duṣhkarma karma = ardous task; rāmalakṣhmanau = Rama and Lakshmana; bhrātarau = both the brothers; īyatuḥ = went; ramyam = to the beautiful; āshramam = hermitage; shrabhaṇ^gasya = of ṣhrabhanga; sāyahne = in the evening.

"Carrying out that ardous task, Rama and Lakshmana, both the brothers, went to the beautiful hermitage of Sharbhanga, in that evening.

Sloka 15

śarabhaṅge divaṃ prāpte rāmaḥ satyaparākramaḥ |
abhivādya munīnsarvāñjanasthānamupāgamat || 6-126-15

15. abhivādya = offering salutation; sarvān = to all; munīn = the hermits; sharabhaṇ^ge = when ṣharabhanga; prāpate = ascended; divam = to heaven; rāmaḥ = Rama; satya parākramaḥ = who was truly mighty; upāgamat = reached; janasthānam = Janasthana = region.

"Offering salutation to all the hermits when Sharabhanga ascended to heaven, Rama who truly mighty, reached the region of Janasthana."

Sloka 16

16. pashchāt = thereafter; shūrpaṇakhā māma = a female-demon called ṣhurpanakha; upāgatā = obtained; rāmapārshvam = the presence of Rama; samdiṣhṭaḥ = as ordered; rāmeṇa = by Rama; mahābalah = the mighty; lakṣhmanaḥ = Lakshmana; utthitaḥ = rising; sahasā = quickly; pragṛihya = and seizing; khaDgam = a sword; chichchheda = chopped off; karṇanāse = her ears and nose.

"Thereafter, a female-demon called Surpanakha sought the presence of Rama. As ordered by Rama, the mighty Lakshmana thereupon rose quickly and seizing a sword, chopped off her ears and nose."

Sloka 17

17. mahātmanā rāghaveṇa = by the great-souled Rama; vasatā = who was living; tatra = there; chaturdasha sahasrāṇi = fourteen thousand; janasthāna vāsinām = demons inhabiting Janasthana; hatāni = were killed.

"Fourteen thousand demons, inhabiting Janasthana-region, were killed by the great-souled Rama, while sojourning that region."

Sloka 18

"The demons, who came together at the battle-front, were wholly finished by Rama alone, in a fraction of a quarter of a day."

Sloka 19

"The demons who were the mighty inhabitants of Dandaka-forest, who were causing obstacles to the austerity of the ascetics, were killed by Rama."

Sloka 20

20. rākṣhasāḥ cha = the demons; viniṣhpiṣhṭāḥ = were smashed; rane = in the battle; kharah cha = Khara also; nihataḥ = was killed; hatvā = after killing; dūṣhaṇam = Dushana; agrataḥ = first; trishiraḥ = ṭrishira (was killed); tadanantaram = thereafter.

"The demons were smashed in the battle. Khara (their leader) also was killed. After killing Dushana (his brother) first, Trishira (the other brother) was killed thereafter."

Sloka

Sloka 24

Sloka 25

Sloka 26

"Ravana, the demon, seized Seetha, even as a planet like ṃars would obscure the constellation, Rohini. Having killed a vulture named Jatayu, who sought to deliver her, Ravana then went quickly in haste, taking Seetha with him"

Sloka 28

Sloka 29

Sloka 30

Sloka 31

Sloka 32

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

Sloka 36

Sloka 38

"Having killed with the strength of his arms in combat, the mighty Vali with a colossal body, Rama caused the kingdom of Sugreeva restored.

Sloka 39

rāmāya pratijānīte rājaputryāstu mārgaṇam || 6-126-39

39. sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; sthāpitaḥ = who was established in his kingdom; sarva vānavaiḥ = with all his monkeys; prati jānīte = had given his pledge; rāmāya = to Rama; mārgaṇam = to commence a search; rājaputryāḥ = for ṣeetha the princess.

"Sugreeva, who was established in his kingdom with all, his monkeys, had given his pledge to Rama, to commence a search for Seetha the princess."

Sloka 40

ādiṣṭā vānarendreṇa sugrīveṇa mahātmanā |
daśakoṭyaḥ plavaṅgānāṃ sarvāḥ prasthāpitā diśaḥ || 6-126-40

40. ādiṣhṭāḥ = commanded; mahātmena sugrīveṇa = by the great-souled ṣugreeva; vānarendreṇa = the king of monkeys; dasha koṭya = ten crores; plavamgāanām- of monkeys; prasthāpitāḥ = were sent sarvāḥ dishaḥ = to all the four quarter.

"Ten crores of monkeys were accordingly commanded by the great-souled Sugreeva and sent to all the four quarters."

Sloka 41

teṣāṃ no vipranaṣṭānāṃ vindhye parvatasattame |
bhṛśaṃ śokābhitaptānāṃ mahānkāloatyavartata || 6-126-41

41. vipranaṣhṭānām = having lost our way; vindhye = in Vindhya; parvatasattame = the foremost of mountains; mahān kālaḥ = a long time; teṣhāmnaḥ atyavartata = slipped past us; bhṛisham shokābhitaptānām = and we felt sore stricken with sorrow.

"Having lost our way in Vindhya, the foremost of mountain-ranges, a long time slipped past us and we felt sore-stricken with sorrow."

Sloka 42

bhrātā tu gṛdhrarājasya sampātirnāma vīryavān |
samākhyāti sma vasatiṃ sītāyā rāvaṇālaye || 6-126-42

42. vīryavān = the valiant; sampātirnāma = ṣampati by name; bhrātā = the brother; gṛidhra rājasya = of Jatayu (the ling of vultures); samākhyāti sma = precisely communicated (to us); sītām = (that) ṣeetha; vasatīm = was dwelling; rāvaṇamandire = in the habitation of Ravana.

"ṃeanwhile, the brother of Jatayu (the king of vultures), Sampati by name, precisely communicated to us that Seetha was dwelling in the habitation of Ravana."

Sloka 43

soahaṃ duḥkhaparītānāṃ duḥkhaṃ tajjñātināṃ nudan |
ātmavīryaṃ samāsthāya yojanānāṃ śataṃ plutaḥ || 6-126-43

43. nudan = removing; tat = that; duḥkham = grief; Jṇātīnām = of my kinsfolk; duḥkhaparītānām = who were seized with sorrow; saḥ aham = I as such; plutaḥ = crossed; yojanānām shatām = one hundred yojanas (eight hundred miles); samāsthāya = resorting to; ātmavīryam = my own strength.

"Removing the grief of my kinsfolk, who were seized with sorrow, I, as such, crossed one hundred yojanas (eight hundred miles of the ocean), resorting to my own strength."

Sloka 44

tatrāhamekāmadrākṣamaśokavanikāṃ gatām |
kauśeyavastrāṃ malināṃ nirānandāṃ dṛḍhavratām || 6-126-44

44. aham = I; gatām = have gone; tatra = there; ashokavanikām = to Ashoka-grove; adrākṣham = and saw; ekām = a woman living alone; kausheyavastrām malinām = clad in a soiled silk-saree; dṛiDhavratām = unflinching as she was in her vow; nirānandām = and cheerless.

"There, I saw Seetha, living alone in a grove of Ashoka trees, clad in a soiled silk-saree, looking cheerless, but unflinching in her vow."

Sloka 45

tayā sametya vidhivatpṛṣṭvā sarvamaninditām |
abhijñānaṃ mayā dattaṃ rāmanāmāṅgulīyakam || 6-126-45

45. sametya = meeting; tayā = her; pṛiṣhṭvā = and duly enquiring; aninditām = that faultless ṣeetha; sarvam = of everything; rāmanāmaṇgulīyakam = a ring with the name; Rama engraved on it; dattam = was handover to her; mayā = by me; abhiJṇānam = as token.

"After meeting her and duly enquiring that faultless Seetha, everything of her, a ring with the name, Rama engraved on it, was handed-over to her by me as a token."

Sloka 46

abhijñānaṃ maṇiṃ labdhvā caritārthoahamāgataḥ |
mayā ca punarāgamya rāmasyākliṣṭakarmaṇaḥ || 6-126-46
abhijñānaṃ mayā dattamarciṣmānsa mahāmaṇiḥ |

46. labdhvā = receiving; abhiJṇānam = as a token (in return from her); maṇīm = a jewel (from her head); aham āgataḥ = I came (to the northern sea shore); charitārthaḥ = successful as I was in my undertaking; punaḥ āgamya = on returning; saḥ archiṣhmān mahāmaṇiḥ = that brilliant and valuable jewel; dattham = was given; mayā = by me; abhiJṇānam = as a token; rāmasya = to Rama; akliṣhṭakarmanaḥ = who was unweary in action.

"Receiving in return from her, a jewel (from her head), I came back to the northern shore, successful as I was in my undertaking. On my return, that brilliant and valuable jewel was given by me as a token to Rama who was unweary in action."

Sloka 47

śrutvā tāṃ maithilīṃ hṛṣṭastvāśaśaṃse sa jīvitam || 6-126-47
jīvitāntamanuprāptaḥ pītvāmṛtamivāturaḥ |

47. shrutvā = on hearing; tām maithilīm = the news of ṣeetha; rāmastu = Rama for his part; āshasham se = regained the hope; jivitam = to survivie; jīvitāntam anupāptah āturaḥ = as a dying patient; (would); ptivā = ond rinking; amṛitam = the ambrosia.

"On hearing the news of Seetha, Rama for his part regained the hope to survive, as a dying patient would, on drinking the ambrosia."

Sloka 48

udyojayiṣyannudyogaṃ dadhre laṅkāvadhe manaḥ || 6-126-48
jighāṃsuriva lokāṃste sarvāṃllokānvibhāvasuḥ |

48. udyojayiṣhyan = excited to put forth; udyogam = a war-effort; dadhre manaḥ = he set his mind; laṇ^kāvadhe = on the destruction of Lanka; vibhāvasuḥ iva = as the fire-god; jighāmsuḥ = intending to destroy; sarvān lokān = all the world; (would) lokānte = at the end of the universe.

"Excited as he was to put forth a war-effort, he set his mind on the destruction of Lanka, as the fire-god intending to destroy all the three worlds, would, at the end of the universe."

Sloka 49

tataḥ samudramāsādya nalaṃ setumakārayat || 6-126-49
ataratkapivīrāṇāṃ vāhinī tena setunā |

49. tataḥ = then; āsādya = on reaching; samudram = the ocean; setum akārayat = he got a bridge constructed; nalam = by ṇala; tena setunā = and through that bridge; vāhinī kapivīrāṇām = the army of the valiant monkeys; ātarat = crossed (the ocean).

"Then, on reaching the ocean, he got a bridge constructed by Nala and through that bridge, the army of the valiant monkeys crossed the ocean."

Sloka 50

prahastamavadhīnnīlaḥ kumbhakarṇaṃ tu rāghavaḥ || 6-126-50
lakṣmaṇo rāvaṇasutaṃ svayaṃ rāmastu rāvaṇam |

50. nīlaḥ = ṇeela; avadhīt = killed; prahastam = Prahasta; rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = the scion of Raghu dynasty; svayam = personally (killed); kumbhakarṇam = Kumbhakarna; rāvaṇam = and Ravana; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; (killed); rāvaṇasutam = Indrajit; the son of Ravana.

"Neela killed Prahasta. Rama, the scion of Raghu dynasty, personally killed Kumbhakarna and Ravana. Lakshmana killed Indrajit, the son of Ravana."

Sloka 51

sa śakreṇa samāgamya yamena varuṇena ca || 6-126-51
maheśvarasvayambhūbhyāṃ tathā daśarathena ca |
taiśca kākutthso varān lebhe paraṃtapaḥ || 6-126-52
surarṣibhiśca kākutstho varāṃllebhe parantapaḥ |

51-52. shrīmān = the illustrious; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; paramtapaḥ = the destroyer of enemies; samāgamya = meeting; shakreṇa = Indra the lord of dissolution; varunena cha = Varuna the lord of waters; maheshwara svayambhūbhyām = ṃaheshwara; the great lord (ṣhiva) and Brahma the lord of creation; tathā = and; dasharathena = Dasaratha (his deceased father); dattavaraḥ = and was bestowed boons; taiḥ = by them; lebhe varān = as also obtained boons; ṛishshibhiḥ = from sages; surarṣhibhishacha = and celestial sages; samāgataiḥ = who came there.

"The illustrious Rama, the destroyer of enemies, happened to meet Indra the lord of celestials, Yama the lord of dissolution, Varuna the lord of waters, ṃaheshvara, the great lord (Shiva), Brahma the lord of creation as also Dasaratha (his deceased father) and was bestowed boons by them. Sages and celestial masters who came there, too gave boons."

Sloka 53

sa tu dattavaraḥ prītyā vānaraiśca samāgataḥ || 6-126-53
puṣpakeṇa vimānena kiṣkindhāmabhyupāgamat |

53. saḥ tu = that Rama for his part; dattavaraḥ = having been granted boons; prītya = with affection (by them); abhyugāmat = reached; kiṣhkindhān = Kishkindha; samāgataḥ = accompanied; vānaraiḥ cha = with the monkeys; puṣhpakena vimānena = on Pushpaka; the aerial car.

"Having been granted boons, Rama for his part flew to Kishkindha in an aerial car called Pushpaka, along with the monkeys who gathered there."

Sloka 54

taṃ gaṅgāṃ punarāsādya vasantaṃ munisaṃnidhau || 6-126-54
avighnaṃ puṣyayogena śvo rāmaṃ draṣṭumarhasi |

54. punaḥ = again; āsādya = reaching; gaṇ^gām = the coast-line of River Ganga; tam rāmam = that Rama; vasantam = is staying; munisamnidhau = in the presence of the sage; Bharadwaja; shvaḥ = tomorrow; puṣhpayogena = when the moon will be in conjunction with asteroid; pushyārhasi = you will be able; draṣhṭum = to see (Rama); avighnam = without any obstacle.

"Reaching the coast-line of Ganga-river again, Rama is staying in the presence of the sage, Bharadwaja. Tomorrow, when the moon will be in conjunction with the asteroid, Pushya you will be able to see Rama, without any obstacle."

Sloka 55

tataḥ sa satyaṃ hanumadvaco mahan |
niśamya hṛṣṭo bharataḥ kṛtāñjaliḥ |
uvāca vāṇīṃ manasaḥ praharṣiṇī |
cirasya pūrṇaḥ khalu me manorathaḥ || 6-126-55

55. nishamya = on hearing (that narration); madhuraiḥ vākyaiḥ = with the sweet words; hanūmataḥ = of ḥanuma; bharataḥ = Bharata; tataḥ = then; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = was gladdened; kṛitāṇ^jaliḥ = and offering his salutation to him by joining his palms together; uvācha = spoke; vāṇīm = the following words; manasaḥ praharṣhiṇīm = which enraptured his mind; me manorathaḥ = "ṃy desire; pūrṇaḥ khalu = has been indeed fulfilled; chirasya = after a long time."

"Gladdened to hear Rama's impending return to Ayodhya in the sweet words of Hanuma, Bharata offered his salutation by joining his palms together and spoke the following words, which enraptured his mind: 'ṃy desire has been fulfilled indeed after a long time'."
