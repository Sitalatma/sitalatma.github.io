Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 23
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction


Tara's lamentation is narrated in here. Seeing at her departed husband Vali, Tara pitiably wails for his demise as any ordinary woman would.


Sloka 1

tataḥ samupajighraṃtī kapi rājasya tat mukham |
patim lokaśrutā tārā mṛtam vacanam abravīt || 3-23-1

1. tataḥ = then; loka shrutā = by world, well-heard - renowned; [lokāt cyutam = fell out from this world - Vali]; tārā = ṭara; kapi rājasya tat mukham = monkey, king's, that, face; samupajighrantī [sam upa jighrantī] = very, closely, on whiffing; mṛitam patim vacanam abravīt = to dead, husband, words, spoke.

Then that renowned Tara closely whiffed that face of monkey's king Vali and spoke these words to her dead husband. [3-23-1]

Sloka 2

śeṣe tvam viṣame duḥkham akṛtvā vacanam mama |
upala upacite vīra suduḥkhe vasudhā tale || 3-23-2

2. vīra = oh, brave one; mama vacanam a kṛitvā = my, word, not, doing - heedless of my word; tvam = you; viṣame = on an uneven; upala upacite = stones, spread over; su duḥkhe = very, rough; vasudhā tale = on earth's, surface; duḥkham sheṣe = miserably, you are reposing.

"Heedless of my word, oh, brave one, you are reposing miserably on a very rough and uneven ground spread with stones. [3-23-2]

Sloka 3

mattaḥ priyatarā nūnam vānarendra mahī tava |
śeṣe hi tām pariṣvajya mām ca na pratibhāṣase || 3-23-3

3. vānara indra = monkey's, the best; mahī = earth; tava to you; mat taḥ = than me; nūnam priya tarā = definitely, dear, more; hi = why because; tām pariṣvajya sheṣe = her - earth, on embracing, you repose; mām na prati bhāṣase = to me, not, in turn, speaking -replying.

"This earth must definitely be your beloved one, oh, best of monkeys, why because, you still embrace her while you recline on her, without replying me. [3-23-3]

Sloka 4

sugrīvasya vaśam prāpto vidhiḥ eṣa bhavatya aho |
sugrīva eva vikrāṃto vīra sāhasika priya || 3-23-4

4. vīra = oh, valiant one; sāhasika priya = oh, in adventures, enthusiast sugrīvasya vasham prāptaḥ = ṣugreeva's, control, you obtained - you have gone in; sugrīva eva vikrāntaḥ = ṣugreeva, alone, triumphed; eṣa vidhiḥ bhavati = this way, fate, is happening [in an interrogative]; aho = oho.

"You have gone into the control of Sugreeva and Sugreeva alone triumphed, oh, brave one, oh, enthusiast in adventures, oho, is it fated to happen this way. [3-23-4]

Sloka 5

ṛkṣa vānara mukhyāḥ tvām balinam paryupāsate |
teṣām vilapitam kṛcchram aṃgadasya ca śocataḥ || 3-23-5
mama ca imā giraḥ śrutvā kim tvam na pratibudhyase |

5, 6a. ṛikṣa vānara mukhyāḥ = bears, monkeys, chiefs; balinam = as worshipful one [not lexical mighty]; tvām pari upāsate = [you as the] mightiest one, you, wholly, are adoring; teṣām kṛicchram vilapitam = their, despairing, wailing; shocataḥ angadasya ca = lamentation, of Angada, also; mama imā giraḥ ca = mine, these, utterances [in keen]; shrutvā = on hearing; kim tvam na = why, you, not; prati budhyase = in turn, wake up, come to senses.

"These chiefs of bears and monkeys are adoring you in every respect as their most worshipful one, and on hearing their despairing wailing, and the lamentation of Angada, and even these utterance of mine in keen, why do not you comeback to senses. [3-23-5, 6a]

Sloka 6

idam tat vīra śayanam tatra śeṣe hato yudhi || 3-23-6
śāyitā nihatā yatra tvayā eva ripavaḥ purā |

6b, 7a. tvayyaiva [tvayā eva] = by you, only; purā nihatā ripavaḥ = once, eliminated, enemies; yatra shāyitā = where, made to recline [by you]; tat vīra shayanam = that, the brave, bed of; idam yudhi hataḥ = in this, fight, felled; tatra sheṣe? = there [on the very daybed,] do you wish to recline; [kim = or, what.]

"Do you wish to repose on the very daybed on which you have laid your enemies to rest when you have once felled them in fights, or what? [3-23-6b, 7a]

Sloka 7

viśuddha sattva abhijana priyayuddha mama priya || 3-23-7
mām anāthām vihāya ekām gataḥ tvam asi mānada |

7b, 8a. vishuddha sattva abhijana = pristine, calibre, successor of clan; priya yuddha = oh, one fond of - a devotee of, fighting; māna da = oh, dignity, accorder of; mama priya = oh, my, dear one - devout one to me; tvam = you; a nātham = without, husband - protector; mām ekām vihāya = me, lonely, on leaving; gataḥ asi = gone, you are.

"Oh, successor of a clan with pristine calibre, oh, accorder of dignity, oh, devotee of fighting, oh, my dear, have you gone on leaving me without a protector and lonely? [3-23-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8

śūrāya na pradātavyā kanyā khalu vipaścitā || 3-23-8
śūra bhāryām hatām paśya sadyo mām vidhavām kṛtām |

8b, 9a. vi pashcitā = by really, considerate ones [father of brides]; kanyā = brides; shūrāya na pradātavyā khalu = to adventurers, not, to be proposed, they say; sadyaḥ = immediately, in a trice; vi dhavām kṛitām = without, husband, made [widow, rendered as]; hatām = left in the lurch; shūra bhāryām = adventurer's, wife; mām pashya = me, see.

"Really considerate fathers shall never propose brides to adventurers, they say. Yes! See me the wife of an adventurer, in a trice rendered as a war-widow left in the lurch. [3-23-8b, 9a]

▼

For this Sage Kātyāna says : dūrasthānām avidyānām mokṣha mārga anusāriṇām | shūrāṇām nir dhanānām ca na deyā kanyakā budhaiḥ || 'to those bridegrooms who reside in distant places, uneducated, seekers of salvation, daring ones, un-wealthy brides shall not be given by wisemen...'

Sloka 9

avabhagnaḥ ca me māno bhagnā me śāśvatī gatiḥ || 3-23-9
agādhe ca nimagnā asmi vipule śoka sāgare |

9b, 10a. me mānaḥ = my, self-respect, honour as an empress; ava bhagnaḥ ca = down, broken - degenerated, also; me shāshvatī gatiḥ = my, eternal, course - happiness; bhagnā = broken - disintegrated; agādhe vipule shoka sāgare = in abyssal, boundless, anguish, [called] ocean; nimagnā asmi = deluged, I am.

"ṃy honour is degenerated and my eternal happiness is disintegrated, and I am deluged in an abyssal boundless ocean called anguish. [3-23-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

aśma sāramayam nūnam idam me hṛdayam dṛḍham || 3-23-10
bhartāram nihatam dṛṣṭvā yat na adya śatadhā gatam |

10b, 11a. nihatam bhartāram dṛiṣṭvā = killed, husband, on seeing; yat = which [heart]; adya shatadhā na gatam [kṛitam] = now, in hundredfold, not, gone into [splintered]; [tat = that heart]; me dṛiDham idam hṛidayam = mine, solid, this, heart; ashma sāra mayam = metallic, substance, dense with; nūnam = definitely.

"And dense with metallic substance is this heart of mine in its solidity, since it is not splintering into hundredfold even on seeing my husband killed, it is definite. [3-23-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11

suhṛt caiva hi bhartā ca prakṛtyā ca mama priyaḥ || 3-23-11
prahāre ca parākrāntaḥ śūraḥ paṃcatvam āgataḥ |

11b, 12a mama = to me prakṛityā priyaḥ = by nature, beloved one; su hṛit caiva = good, hearted one, also thus; bhartā ca = husband, also; prahāre para ākrāntaḥ ca = in assaults, others - enemies, overcoming [triumphant one]; shūraḥ = valiant one; such as you are, you have; pancatvam āgataḥ = fifth state, come over by.

"By your nature you are my beloved, besides, you are a good-hearted one, more so, you are my husband, moreover, you are a valiant triumphing over your enemies in assaults, such as you are you have attained the fifth state, the death. [3-23-11b, 12a]

Sloka 12

pati hīnā tu yā nārī kāmam bhavatu putriṇī || 3-23-12
dhana dhānya samṛddhā api vidhavā iti ucyate janaiḥ |

12b, 13a. yā nārī = which, woman; pati hīnā = husband, without; putriṇī bhavatu kāmam = mothered [many children,] she may be, perhaps; dhana dhānya sam vṛiddhā api = riches, food grains [yields,] well, prosperous with, even if; janaiḥ [budhiaḥ] = by people [by elders]; vi dhavā = departed, husband [a widow]; iti = thus as; ucyate = will be said, called.

"Perhaps a woman might have mothered many children, and perhaps affluent with riches and crops, still people will call her a widow, if she is without husband. [3-23-12b, 13a]

Sloka 13

sva gātra prabhave vīra śeṣe rudhira maṇḍale || 3-23-13
kṛmi rāga paristome svakīye śayane yathā |

13b, 14a. vīra = oh, brave one; sva gātra prabhave = you own, body parts, emerging; rudhira maṇDale = blood, sheet of; kṛimi rāga = insect, red [the red coloured insects, namely Indragopa insect, a bed sheet in that colour]; paristome = spread-sheet, bed sheet spread over a bed; svakīye shayane yathā = on your own, bed, as if; sheṣe = you repose now.

"How do you repose now on a sheet of blood flowing from your own body, oh, brave one, as if you are reclining on your own bed spread with a bed sheet of red blood colour? [3-23-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

reṇu śoṇita saṃvītam gātram tava samaṃtataḥ || 3-23-14
parirabdhum na śaknomi bhujābhyām plavagarṣabha |

14b, 15a. plavaga ṛiṣabha = oh, fly-jumper, the best; samantataḥ = all over; reṇu shoṇita samvītam = dust, blood, covered over; [when these blood of vengeance and dust of dishonour comes in between us]; tava gātram = your, body; bhujābhyām = with my two arms; pari rabdhum = to fully, embrace; na shaknomi = not, able to I have no strength.

"I have no strength to closely embrace you with both my arms, oh, best fly-jumper, when the blood and dust covering your body comes in between. [3-23-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

kṛta kṛtyo adya sugrīvo vaire asmin atidāruṇe || 3-23-15
yasya rāma vimuktena hṛtam eka iṣuṇā bhayam |

15b, 16a. ati dāruṇe asmin vaire = highly, deplorable, in this, enmity [between you and ṣugreeva]; adya sugrīvaḥ kṛita kṛityaḥ = now, ṣugreeva, is ambition is fulfilled - accomplished one; yasya bhayam = whose [which ṣugreeva's,] fear; rāma vimuktena = by Rama, released; eka iṣuṇā hṛitam = by single, arrow, evanished; [gist in vividness.]

"Single arrow released by Rama has doubly benefited Sugreeva in this highly deplorable enmity between you and Sugreeva, as his ambition to become the king is fulfilled and his fear from your persecution, too, is evanished. [3-23-15b, 16a]

Sloka 16

śareṇa hṛdi lagnena gātra saṃsparśane tava || 3-23-16
vāryāmi tvām nirīkṣantī tvayi paṃcatvam āgate |

16b, 17a. tvayi pancatvam āgate = for you, fifth state - death, when it came on - fatality has descended; tvām = you; [though]; nir īkṣantī = without wink, seeing - watching intently; tava hṛidi lagnena shareṇa = in your, chest, stuck, by arrow; gātra samsparshane = body, for touching - for embracing; vāryāmi = I am hindered.

"In vain, I have been watching you without a wink, on whom fatality has descended, as this arrow stuck in your chest is hindering me to embrace you..." Thus wailed Tara. [3-23-16b, 17a]

Sloka 17

udbabarha śaram nīlaḥ tasya gātra gatam tadā || 3-23-17
giri gahvara saṃlīnam dīptam āśī viṣam yathā |

17b, 18a. tataḥ = then; ṇila; tasya gātra gatam sharam = his [Valis's,] body - chest, gone in [stuck in,] arrow; giri gahvara = in mountain's, cavity; sam līnam = firmly, wedged in; dīptam = sparkling [arrow]; āshīviṣam yathā = serpent [like arrow,] as with; ut babarha = out, pulled - extricated.

"Then the monkey chief Nila extricated that arrow which is stuck in the chest of Vali, as with the extraction of a firmly wedged sparkling snake from the cavity of a mound. [3-23-17b, 18a]

Sloka 18

tasya niṣkṛṣyamāṇasya bāṇasya ca babhau dyutiḥ || 3-23-18
asta mastaka saṃruddho raśmiḥ dinakarāt iva |

18b, 19a. niṣkṛiṣyamāṇasya = while being extracted; tasya bāṇasya dyutiḥ = that, arrow's, sparkle; dinakarāt = from sun - that which is extricated from sun; asta mastaka = dusk-time [western mountain's,] summit; sam ruddhaḥ = fully, obstructed - as contrasted with [the hue of dusk]; rashmiḥ iva = sparkling rays, like; babhau = it became - shone forth.

The sparkle of Rama's arrow while it is being extracted from Vali's chest is much the same as the sparkles of more brilliant sunrays that are extracted by the end of daytime, when the sun is sinking beyond the summit of dusky western mountain, and as contrasted with the hue of dusk. [3-23-18b, 19a]

▼

ṃore brilliant days are indicated for the arrows of Rama as the dusky day of Vali has ended.

Sloka 19

petuḥ kṣataja dhārāḥ tu vraṇebhyaḥ tasya sarvaśaḥ || 3-23-19
tāmra gairika saṃpṛktā dhārā iva dharā dharāt |

19b, 20a. tasya vraṇebhyaḥ = from his, gashes; kṣataja dhārāḥ = blood, streams; dharā dharāt = from the one borne, by earth - from mountain; tāmra gairika sampṛiktā = coppery, mineral-ore, saturated with; dhārā iva = streams of water, as with; sarvashaḥ petuḥ = all over, fell out.

The blood streams flown out of the gashes of Vali fell everywhere like the streams of water flowing from a mountain saturated with coppery mineral-ores. [3-23-19b, 20a]

Sloka 20

avakīrṇam vimārjantī bhartāram raṇa reṇunā || 3-23-20
asraiḥ nayanajaiḥ śūram siṣeca astra samāhatam |

20b, 21a. raṇa reṇunā = of war, with dust; ava kīrṇam = all over, muffled up; bhartāram = her husband; vi mārjantī = out, rubbing - dusting, wiping off; astra samāhatam [sam ā hatam] = by missile, summarily hit; shūram = valorous one; nayana jaiḥ asraiḥ = eyes, born to, tears - eyes gushing tears; siṣeca = drenched.

On wiping off war dust with which her husband is muffled up, with tears gushing from her eyes she drenched that valiant one who is summarily hit by the missile of Rama. [3-23-20b, 21a]

Sloka 21

rudhirokṣita sarvāngam dṛṣṭvā vinihatam patim || 3-23-21
uvāca tārā piṃgākṣam putram aṃgadam aṃganā |

21b, 22a. anganā tārā = lady, ṭara; vinihatam = he who is killed; rudhira ukṣita sarva angam = by blood, wet, all, limbs; patim = at husband; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing - on giving attention; putram uvāca pinga akṣam = to son, to coppery red, eyed one; angadam uvāca = to Angada, said.

On giving attention to all of the blood wet limbs of her slain husband that lady Tara spoke to her son Angada whose eyes are coppery-red. [3-23-21b, 22a]

Sloka 22

avasthām paścimām paśya pituḥ putra sudāruṇām || 3-23-22
saṃprasaktasya vairasya gato antaḥ pāpa karmaṇā |

22b, 23a. putra = oh, son; pituḥ su dāruṇām = of father, oh, son, highly, harrowing; pashcimām = western [dusking, end]; avasthām pashya = plight, you see; pāpa karmaṇā = by sinful, deeds; samprasaktasya [sam pra saktasya = very, closely, attached] harnessed by; vairasya = enmity; antaḥ gataḥ = end, he reached.

"See the highly harrowing end time plight of your father, oh, son, he reached his end owing to the enmity harnessed by sinful deeds. [3-23-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

bāla sūryojjvala tanum prayātam yama sādanam || 3-23-23
abhivādaya rājānam pitaram putra mānadam |

23b, 24a. putra = oh, son; bāla sūrya ujjvala tanum = young [rising,] son, [as with in] dazzle, bodied - whose physical splendour is; yama sādanam prayātam = to ṭime-god's, abode, he journeyed [dead]; rājānam māna dam = to king, honour, accorder; pitaram = to father; abhivādaya = pay you [last] obeisance.

"To him, whose physical splendour vied with the dazzle of rising sun, to that father, king, and the accorder of honour you pay you last obeisance as he journeyed to the abode of Time-god." Thus Tara said to Angada. [3-23-23b, 24a]

Sloka 24

evam uktaḥ samutthāya jagrāha caraṇau pituḥ || 3-23-24
bhujābhyām pīna vṛtābhyām aṃgado aham iti bruvan |

24b, 25a. evam uktaḥ = thus, who is said to, Angada; sam utthāya = swiftly, on rising up; aham angadaḥ = I am, Angada; iti bruvan = thus, saying; pīna vṛitābhyām = with sinewy, spherical shoulders; bhujābhyām = with arms; pituḥ caraṇau jagrāha = father's, feet, he took.

When he is said so, Angada swiftly rose and touched the feet of his father with both of his rotund-shouldered sinewy arms saying, "father, I am Angada..." [3-23-24b, 25a]

Sloka 25

abhivādayamānam tvām aṃgadam tvam yathā purā || 3-23-25
dīrgha āyur bhava putra iti kim artham na abhibhāṣase |

25b, 26a. tvām = at you; abhivādayamānam = to him who is paying respects; angadam = at Angada; tvam = you; putra = oh, son; dīrgha āyuḥ bhava = long, live, you be; iti = thus as; kim artham = what, for - why don't you; yathā purā = as, earlier; abhibhāṣase = not, speaking [blessing.]

"Why do not you bless Angada saying, 'long live, son...' when he is paying resects to you, as earlier?" Thus Tara is addressing Vali in her anguish. [3-23-25b, 26a]

Sloka 26

aham putra sahāyā tvām upāse gata cetanam |
siṃhena pātitam sadyo gauḥ sa vatsā iva go vṛṣam || 3-23-26

26b, c. sadyaḥ = just now; simhena pātitam = lion, felled; go vṛiṣam = cow, male [when bull is felled]; sa gauḥ vatsā iva = with, calf, [a helpless] cow, as like; putra sahāyā = son, with the help of; aham = I; gata cetanam = [ you who] lost, vitality - lifeless; tvām = to you [at your near]; upāse = sitting close - living through.

"I am living through seeing the lifeless state of yours with the help of my son, no more than a helpless cow that sits near her bull with its calf, when that bull is slain by a lion, just now. [3-23-26b, c]

Sloka 27

iṣṭvā saṃgrāma yajñena rāma praharaṇa aṃbhasā |
asmin avabhṛthe snātaḥ katham patnyā mayā vinā || 3-23-27

27. sangrāma yajñena iṣṭvā = war, ritual, on performing; patnyā mayā vinā = wife, me, without; rāma praharaṇa ambhasā = Rama's, weapon [arrow,] [called] waters; asmin avabhṛithe = in there, avabridtha, [ritual bath]; katham snātaḥ = how, you take bath [singly.]

"How can you singly take the ultimate bath of a Vedic ritual after your conducting a ritual like combat, in the waters called bloodstreams caused by Rama's arrow, that too, when I, your wife, am available like a co-officiator of a Vedic ritual? [3-23-27]

▼

This avabṛitha is a religious bath taken after completion of the Vedic ritual by the conductor of that ritual along with his wife. Vedic ritual itself is forbidden to a wifeless person, and taking this final bath after pūrṇa āhuti, total oblation, is against the rules of ritual if taken without his wife. Here Vali, an adorer of combats as good as Vedic rituals is taking that bath for himself in the bloodstreams, called ritual waters, and Tara is questioning him about her propriety to participate in it.

Sloka 28

yā dattā deva rājena tava tuṣṭena saṃyuge |
śāta kaumbhīm priyām mālām tām te paśyāmi na iha kim || 3-23-28

28. samyuge = in combat [about your conduct in combats]; tuṣṭena deva rājena = by the satisfied, gods, king - Indra; yā tava dattā = which one, to you, given; tām = that one; shāta kaumbhīm = golden one; te priyām mālām = to you, a treasured, pendant; iha kim na pashyāmi = now, why, not, I see.

"Now where is that treasured golden pendant of yours which the king of gods gave to you when he is satisfied with your conduct in wars, I don't see it? [3-23-28]

Sloka 29

rājyaśrīḥ na jahāti tvām gata asum api mānada |
sūryasya āvartamānasya śaila rājam iva prabhā || 3-23-29

29. māna da = dignity, accorder; shaila rājam āvartamānasya = mountain, best [ṃt. ṃeru,] circumnavigating; sūryasya prabhā iva = sun's, shine, as with; rājya shrīḥ = kingly, pomp [majesty]; gata asum api = one with gone, lives, even if; tvām na jahāti = you, not, leaving.

"ṃajesty is not deserting you even if your lives have departed, oh, accorder of dignity, as with the sunshine that departs the sun while he circumnavigates ṃt. ṃeru, which mount is supposed to overshadow everything by its vastness. [3-23-29]

Sloka 30

na me vacaḥ pathyam idam tvayā kṛtam
na ca asmi śaktā hi nivāraṇe tava |
hatā saputrā asmi hatena saṃyuge
saha tvayā śrīḥ vijahāti mām api || 3-23-30

30. tvayā = by you; me = my; pathyam idam vacaḥ = expedient, this, words [my advise]; na kṛitam = not, done [heeded]; tava nivāraṇe = you, forestalling; shaktā na asmi ca hi = capable, not, I am, also, indeed; samyuge hatena = in fight, by you who is killed; sa putrā = along with, son; hatā asmi = doomed, I am; shrīḥ = Grace; tvayā saha = you, along with; mām api = me, even; vi jahāti = completely, deserting.

"Neither you heeded my word of expediency, nor I was indeed capable to forestall you, and I am doomed along with my son when you are exterminated in fight, whereby the Grace that forsook you, is deserting me too, in its entirety. [3-23-30]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe trayo viṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 23rd chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
