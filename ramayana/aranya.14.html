Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 14
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Rama and the other two on their way to Panchavati come into contact with Jatayu, the mighty eagle. When Rama questions about its identity, Jatayu narrates the creation of animal species along with humans, and informs Rama that he is a friend of King Dasharatha and would like to help Rama in exile.

Sloka 1

atha paṃcavaṭīm gaccann antarā raghunandanaḥ |
āsasāda mahākāyam gṛdhram bhīma parākramam || 3-14-1

1. atha = then; raghunandanaḥ pancavaṭīm gacchann = Rahu's descendent, to Panchavati, while proceeding; antarā = in midway; mahā kāyam bhīma parākramam = with mammoth, bodied, with marvellous, might; gṛidhram āsasāda = an eagle he reached, came across.

Then while proceeding to Panchavati that Rahu's descendent came across a mammoth eagle with marvellous might in midway. [3-14-1]

Sloka 2

tam dṛṣṭvā tau mahābhāgau vanastham rāma lakṣmaṇau |
menāte rākṣasam pakṣim bruvāṇau ko bhavān iti || 3-14-2

2. mahābhāgau tau rāma lakṣmaṇau = highly privileged, those two, Rama, Lakshmana; vanastham = in forest [vaṭastham = on tree]; tam pakṣim dṛiṣṭvā = him, that bird, on seeing; kau bhavān iti = who, you are, thus; bruvāṇau = saying; rākṣasam menāte = demon, they presumed.

On seeing such an eagle in the forest those two highly privileged brothers Rama and Lakshmana questioned it asking, "Who you are?" presuming it to be a demon. [3-14-2]

Sloka 3

sa tau madhurayā vācā saumyayā prīṇayann iva |
uvāca vatsa mām viddhi vayasyam pitur ātmanaḥ || 3-14-3

3. saḥ = he, that eagle; tau madhurayā saumyayā vācā = to them, with sweet, soft, with words; uvāca prīṇayann iva = spoke, pleasing them, as though; vatsa = oh boy Rama; mām ātmanaḥ pituḥ vayasyam viddhi = me, of your, father's, friend , thus you know.

But that eagle with sweet ad soft words spoke to them as though to please them, "oh boy Rama know me as your father's friend." [3-14-3]

Sloka 4

sa tam pitṛ sakham matvā pūjayāmāsa rāghavaḥ |
sa tasya kulam avyagram atha papracchha nāma ca || 3-14-4

4. saḥ = he that; rāghavaḥ = Raghava; tam = that bird; pitṛi sakham matvā pūjayāmāsa = him, as father's, friend, accepting, revered; atha saḥ avyagram = then, he [Rama,] without haste; tasya kulam nāma ca papraccha = its, lineage, name, also, asked for.

Accepting that bird as his father's friend Raghava revered it, and he that Rama then asked for that eagle's name and lineage. [3-14-4]

Sloka 5

rāmasya vacanam śrutvā kulam ātmānam eva ca |
ācacakṣe dvijaḥ tasmai sarvabhūta samudbhavam || 3-14-5

5. dvijaḥ = that bird; rāmasya vacanam shrutvā = Rama's, words, on hearing; sarva bhūta sam udbhavam = reg. all, beings, overall, genesis; kulam ātmānam eva ca = ancestry, himself, thus, also; ācacakṣe tasmai = said, to them.

On hearing Rama's words that bird said about his ancestry and himself, and in doing so, that eagle also narrated the overall genesis of all beings. [3-14-5]

Sloka 6

tān me nigadataḥ sarvān āditaḥ śṛṇu rāghava || 3-14-6

6. pūrva kāle mahābāhuḥ = once, upon a time, oh dextrous one; Rāghava; ye prajā patayaḥ abhavan = those, people's, lords, were there; tān sarvān = of them, all; me nigadataḥ = from me, while I narrate; āditaḥ = from beginning; shṛiṇu = you may listen.

"Once upon a time there were lords of people, oh dextrous Raghava, and you may listen all about them from the beginning, while I narrate [3-14-6]

Sloka 7

"Of them Kardama was there at first, and afterwards Sesha, and later Samshraya was there with many children and a vigorous one he was. Then Sthānu, ṃarīchi, Atri, the great mighty one Kratu, Pulasthya, Angira, Pracheta and Pulah were there. And oh, Raghava, Daksha, Vivaswan were while the other name of Vivasvan is Arishtanemi, and lastly the great resplendent Kashyapa, was there as Prajapati. [3-14-9] [3-14-7]

Sloka 12

Sloka 13

13b, 14a. aditiḥ tan manā = Aditi, that, agreed; rāma = Rama; mahābāhuḥ = oh, dextrous one; ditiḥ ca danuḥ eva ca = Diti, also, Danu, even, also kālakā ca[ manā] = Kālakā, also, [agreed]; sheṣāḥ tu a manasaḥ abhavan = others, but, not, heedful, they became;.

"Oh, dextrous Rama, Aditi, Diti, Kālakā and Danu have agreed for that, but others became unheedful of Kashyapa's words. [3-14-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

adityām jajñire devāḥ trayaḥ triṃśat ariṃdama || 3-14-14

14b, 15a. arindama = oh enemy-destroyer; adityām devāḥ ādityā vasavaḥ rudrā ashvinau ca = from Aditi, Aadityā-s, Vasu-s, two Ashvini-s; trayaḥ trimshat devāḥ jajñire = thirty three, gods, are born; parantapa = oh enemy-oppressor.

"Aditi gave birth to twelve Aditya-s, the Sun-gods, eight Vasu-s, the Terrestrials-gods, eleven Rudrā-s, the Fury-gods, and two Ashvinis, the medicine-gods, total thirty-three of them. [3-14-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

15b, 16a. tāta = oh, boy; ditiḥ tu yashasvinaḥ daityām putrān ajanayat = Diti, but, well-known ones, Daitya-s [so-called demons,] as sons, gave birth to; sa vana arṇavā = with, forests, oceans; iyam vasumatī teṣām purā āsīt = this, earth, theirs, earlier, was there.

Sloka 16

[3-14-18b, 19]

Sloka 32

32. kadrūḥ = [lady] Kadru; sahasram [sahasra āsyam] = thousand faced; dharaṇī dharan = earth, bearer -- one who can bear the earth; nāga [nāgam] vijajñe = to serpent, gave birth; vinatāyāḥ tu = of Vinata, but; garuDaḥ aruṇa eva ca = Garuda and Aruṇa, also, like that; dvau putrau = two, sons [took birth.]

"Kadru gave birth to a thousand-headed serpent who is the bearer of this earth, and Vinata gave birth to two sons namely Garuda and Aruna. [3-14-32]

▼

Garuda is the eagle-vehicle of Lord Vishnu, while Aruna, also called Anūru, for he is a thigh-less being, is the charioteer of Sun's seven-horse chariot. The seven horses of Sun's chariot are the seven colours in vibgyor as in a rainbow. Aruna and Garuda are of avian origination and attributed to be the speediest propellers, and Jatayu and Sampati belong to that lineage.

sītām ca tāta rakṣiṣye tvayi yāte salakṣmaṇe || 3-14-34

34. saḥ aham = such as I am; icchasi yadi = you wish, if; te vāsa sahāyaḥ bhaviṣyāmi = your, at residence, as helpmate, I can be; durgam idam kāntāram = impassable, this one, forest is; mṛiga rākshasa sevitam hi = by predators, demons, beloved to, isn't it; tāta = oh boy; sa lakṣmaṇe tvayi yāte = with Lakshmana, on your, going out; sītām rakṣiṣye = ṣeetha, I wish to protect.

"Such as I am, I can be your helpmate at your residence if only you wish me to... oh, boy, this impassable forest is a beloved one to predators and demons, isn't it... as such I can take care of Seetha if you and Lakshmana go out..." Thus Jatayu said to Rama. [3-14-34]

Sloka 35

jaṭāyuṣam tu pratipūjya rāghavo
mudā pariṣvajya ca sannato abhavat |
pitur hi śuśrāva sakhitvam ātmavān
jaṭāyuṣā saṃkathitam punaḥ punaḥ || 3-14-35

35. rāghavaḥ = Raghava; jaṭāyuṣam pratipūjya = at Jatayu, in turn, revering; mudā pariṣvajya ca = gladly, on hugging; sannataḥ abhavat = bowed down, he [Rama] became; ātmavān = kind-natured one [Rama]; jaṭāyuṣā punaḥ punaḥ sam kathitam = by Jatayu, again, again well, narrated; pituḥ sakhitvam shushrāva hi = father's, friendship, heard of [he is regardful,] indeed.

Raghava revered Jatayu in his turn by gladly hugging and stood by him with his head bent, and that kind-natured Rama is indeed regardful of the friendship of his father with Jatayu that is repeatedly said by Jatayu. [3-14-35]

Sloka 36

sa tatra sītām paridāya maithilīm
saha eva tena atibalena pakṣiṇā |
jagāma tām paṃcavaṭīm salakṣmaṇo
ripūn didhakṣan śalabhān iva analaḥ || 3-14-36

36. saḥ maithilīm sītām paridāya = he that Rama, ṃaithili's, ṣeetha, on taking; sa lakṣmaṇaḥ = with Lakshmana; ati balena tena pakṣiṇā saha eva = very, mighty one, with that, bird, along with, thus; analaḥ shalabhān iva = as fire, for grasshoppers, like; ripūn didhakṣan = enemies, to incinerate; tām pancavaṭīm = to that, Panchavati; jagāma = proceeded.

He that Rama took the princess from ṃithila Seetha, and proceeded to Panchavati along with that very mighty bird and Lakshmana, as though to incinerate enemies, like fire that burns down grasshoppers. [3-14-36]

----

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe caturdaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 14th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
