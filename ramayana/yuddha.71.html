Introduction

Seeing Atikaya, the colossal demon, coming towards him on a chariot, Rama asks Vibhishana who he is Vibhishana introduces Atikaya, as being a son of Ravana and Dhanyamali (another consort of Ravana). When some commanders of monkeys try to attack Atikaya, he chops off trees and crags hurled at him. Without fighting further with the monkeys, Atikaya, moves forward to Rama and challenges him for a fight. Then Lakshmana with his bow and arrows begins to fight with Atikaya. By a sharp arrow struck by Atikaya in Lakshmana's chest, Lakshmana is enraged and releases an arrow employed with a missile of fire and Atikaya attacks it with an arrow employed with a solar missile. On the advice the wind-god appeared before him, Lakshmana finally hurls an arrow employed with a missile of Brahma and chips off the head of Atikaya.

110. tadā = then; saḥ sharaḥ = that arrow; agnidīptaḥ = blazing like fire; moghāni kṛitvā tāni āyudhāni = made in vain; those weapons; adbhuta vigrahāni = of wonderful form; pragṛihya = taking; shiraḥ = his head; jahāra = carried it away.

Then, that arrow, blazing like fire, made in vain those weapons of wonderful form and taking Atikaya's head, carried it away.

Sloka 111

tacchiraḥ saśirastrāṇam lakṣmaṇeṣuprapīḍitam |
papāta sahasā bhūmau śṛṅgam himavato yathā || 6-71-111

111. tat = that; shiraḥ = head; sashirastrāṇam = with its diadem; lakṣhmaṇeṣhu pramaditam = destroyed by Lakshmana; papāta = fell down; sahasā = quickly; bhūmau = on the ground; shṛiṇgam yathā = like a peak; himavataḥ = of the ḥimalayan mountain.

That head with its diadem, chopped off by Lakshmana, quickly fell down on the ground, like a peak of the Himalayan ṃountain.

Sloka 112

taṃ bhūmau patitaṃ dṛṣṭvā vikṣiptāmbarabhūṣaṇam |
babhūvurvyathitāḥ sarve haraśeṣā niśācarāḥ|| 6-71-112

112. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam = that Atikaya; patitam = who fell; bhūmau = on the ground; vikṣhiptāmbara bhūṣhaṇam = with his clothes and ornaments scattered away; sarve = all; hataseṣhāḥ = the surviving; nishācharāḥ = demons; babhūvuḥ = became; vyathitāḥ = perturbed.

Seeing Atikaya falling on the ground, with his clothes and ornaments scattered away, all the surviving demons became perturbed.

Sloka 113

te viṣaṇṇamukhā dīnāḥ prahārajanitaśramāḥ |
vineduruccairbahavaḥ sahasā visvaraiḥ svaraiḥ|| 6-71-113

113. bahavaḥ = many; te dīnāḥ = of those depressed demons; viṣhaṇṇa mukhāḥ = dejected in countenance; prahārajanita shramāḥ = with a fatigue born of fighting; sahasā = suddenly; vineduḥ = began to shout; uchchaiḥ = loudly; svaraiḥ = with their voices; visvaraiḥ = in discordant notes.

ṃany of those depressed demons, dejected as they were, showing fatigue born of fighting, in their faces, suddenly began to shout loudly, with their voices in discordant notes.

Sloka 114

tatastatparito yātā nirapekṣā niśācarāḥ |
purīmabhimukhā bhītā dravanto nāyake hate || 6-71-114

114. nāyake hate = as their leader was dead; nishācharāḥ = the demons; tataḥ = then; bhītāḥ = were frightened; nirapekṣhāḥ = and being indifferent to fight; yātāḥ = went; dravantaḥ = running away; abhimukhāḥ = with their faces turned towards; tat purīm = that city; paritaḥ = all around.

As their leader was dead, the demons were then frightened and becoming indifferent to fight, went running away all around, with their faces turned towards that City of Lanka.

Sloka 115

praharṣayuktā bahavastu vānarā |
prabuddhapadmapratimānanāstadā |
apūjayamllakṣmaṇamiṣṭabhāginaṃ |
hate ripau bhīmabale durāsade || 6-71-115

115. bhīma bale durāsade ripau hate = after the dangerous demon with terrific strength was killed; bahavaḥ = the multitude was killed; bahavaḥ = the multitude; vānarāḥ = of monkeys; tadā = then; praharṣhayuktāḥ = were endowed with a great delight; praphullapadmapratimānanāḥ = with their faces; apūjayan = and worshipped; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; iṣhṭabhāginam = who was blessed with the fulfillment of his desire.

When the dangerous demon with the terrific strength was killed, the multitude of monkeys then were endowed with a great delight, with their faces looking like full blown lotuses and worshipped Lakshmana who got his desire fulfilled.

Sloka 116

atibala matikāyamabhrakalpaṃ |
yudhi vinipātya sa lakṣmaṇaḥ prahṛṣṭaḥ |
tvaritamatha tadā sa rāmapārśvaṃ
kapinivahaiśca supūjito jagāma || 6-71-116

116. vinipātya = throwing down; yudhi = in battle; atikāyam = that Atikaya; atibalam = who was exceedingly strong; abhrakalpam = and looking like a cloud; saḥ lakṣhmanaḥ = that Lakshmana; tadā = then; prahṛiṣhṭaḥ = was greatly delighted; supūjitaḥ = and while he was being worshipped; kapinivahaiḥ = by the multitude of monkeys; atha = thereupon; tvaritam = quickly; jagāma = hastened towards; rāma pārshvam = the vicinity of Rama.

Throwing down in battle, that Atikaya who was exceedingly strong and looking like a cloud, Lakshmana was greatly delighted and while he was being worshipped by the multitude of monkeys, thereupon quickly hastened towards the proximity of Rama.
