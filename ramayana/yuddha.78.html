Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 78
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana orders ṃakaraksha to proceed to the battle-field with an army and to kill Rama, Lakshmana and the monkeys. ṃakaraksha, surrounded by his army of demons, set out for the encounter. ṃakaraksha's whip and flag staff fall down all of a sudden, by the will of providence and a dust-storm ensued. Ignoring those portents, ṃakaraskha's army march forward in the battle-field, to reach Rama and Lakshmana.

Sloka 1

nikumbhaṃ nihataṃ śrutvā kumbham ca vinipātitam |
rāvaṇaḥ paramāmarṣī prajajvālānalo yathā || 6-78-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; nikumbham = ṇikumbha; nihatam = was killed; kumbham cha = and Kumbha also; vinipātitam = was lost; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; paramāmarṣhī = was very much enraged; prajajvāla = fiercely analoyathā = like fire.

On hearing of Nikumbha and Kumbha having been killed, Ravana was very much flared up like fire.

Sloka 2

nairṛtaḥ krodhaśokābhyāṃ dvābhyāṃ tu parimūrcitaḥ |
kharaputra viśālākṣaṃ makarākṣamacodayat || 6-78-2

2. parimūrchchhitaḥ = pervaded with; dvābhyām krodha shokābhyām = both anger and sorrow; nairṛitaḥ = Ravana; achodayat = impelled; vishālākṣham = the wide-eyed; makarākṣham = ṃakaraksha; kharaputram = the son of Khara (as follows):

Engulfed with both anger and sorrow, Ravana ordered the wide-eyed ṃakaraksha, the son of Khara (as follows):

Sloka 3

gaccha putra mayājñapto balenābhisamanvitaḥ |
rāghavaṃ lakṣmaṇam caiva jahi tau savanaukasau || 6-78-3

3. āJṇaptaḥ = commanded; mayā = by me; abhisamanvitaḥ = and accompanied; balena = by an army; gachchha = go; putra = my son; jahi = and kill; tau = those; rāghavam = Rama; lakṣhmaṇam chaiva = and even Lakshmana; savanaukasau = together with the monkeys.

"I am commanding you! Accompanied by an army, go my son! Kill Rama, Lakshmana and the monkeys."

Sloka 4

tāvaṇasya vacaḥ śrutvā śūramānī kharātmajaḥ |
bāḍhamityabravīddhṛṣṭo makarākṣo niśācāram || 6-78-4

4. shrutvā = hearing; rāvaṇasya vachah = Ravana's words; makarākṣhaḥ = ṃakaraksha; kharātmajaḥ = the son of Khara; shūramānī = thinking himself as a hero; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = was please; abravīt = and spoke; nishācharam = to Ravana; bāDham iti = that (he would do it) certainly.

Hearing Ravana's words, ṃakaraksha, the son of Khara, thinking himself as a hero, was rejoiced and assured Ravana that he would do it certainly as commanded.

Sloka 5

so.abhivādya daśagrīvaṃ kṛtvā cāpi pradakṣiṇam |
nirjagāma gṛhācchubhrādrāvaṇasyājñayā balī || 6-78-5

5. abhivādya = having respectfully saluted; pradakṣhiṇam kṛitvā chāpi = even by performing circumambulation; balī = the mighty; saḥ = ṃakaraksha; āJṇayā dashagrīvam = by the orders of Ravana; nirjagāma = came forth; shubhrāt gṛihāt = from the shining palace (of Ravana).

Having respectfully saluted, even by performing circumambulation, the mighty ṃakaraksha, by the orders of Ravana, came forth from these shining palace of Ravana.

Sloka 6

samīpasthaṃ balādhyakṣaṃ kharaputro.abravīdvacaḥ |
rathamānīyatāṃ tūrṇam sainyaṃ tvānīyatāṃ tvarāt || 6-78-6

6. kharaputraḥ = ṃakaraksha the son of Khara; abravīt = spoke; vachaḥ = the following words; balādhyakṣham = to the commander of an army; samīpastham = who was in the vicinity (as follows); ratham ānīyatām = let a chariot be brought; tūrṇam = quickly; sainyam = and the army; ānīyatām = be arranged; tvarāt = swiftly.

ṃakaraksha the son of Khara asked the commander of an army who was in the vicinity to bring a chariot and the army swiftly.

Sloka 7

tasya tadvacanam śrutvā balādhyakṣo niśācaraḥ |
syandanaṃ ca balaṃ caiva samīpaṃ pratyapādayat || 6-78-7

7. shrutvā = hearing; tasya tadvachanam = his words; nishācharaḥ = a demons; balādhyakṣhaḥ = being the commander of an army; pratyapādayat = brought; syandavamcha = the chariot; balamchaiva = and the army; samīpam = to his proximity.

Hearing his words, the army-commander of the demons brought the chariot and the army to his proximity.

Sloka 8

pradakṣiṇaṃ rathaṃ kṛtvā samāhūya niśācaraḥ |
sūtaṃ saṃcodayāmāsa śīgraṃ vai ratha māvaha || 6-78-8

8. pradakṣhiṇam kṛitvā = walking clockwise round; ratham = the chariot; samūhāya = and calling; sūtam = the charioteer; nishācharaḥ = ṃakaraksha; samchodayāmāsa = incited him; āvaha = to drive; ratham = the chariot; shīghram = quickly.

Walking clockwise round the chariot and ascending it, ṃakaraksha asked the charioteer to drive forward the chariot quickly to the battle-field.

Sloka 9

atha tān rākṣasān sarvān makarākṣo.abravīdidam |
yūyaṃ sarve prayudhyadhvaṃ purastānmama rākṣasāḥ || 6-78-9

.9. atha = then; makarakṣhaḥ = ṃakaraksha; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; sarvān = to all; rākṣhasān = demons; sarve = all; yūyam = of you; prayudhyadhvam = begin to fight; mama purastāt = in my presence; rākṣhasāḥ = O; demons!.

Then ṃakaraksha spoke the following words to those demons, "O demons! All of you begin your fight before my presence."

Sloka 10

ahaṃ rākṣasarājena rāvaṇena mahātmanā |
ājñptaḥ samare hantuṃ rāvubhau rāmalkṣmaṇau || 6-78-10

10. aham = I; aJṇaptaḥ = was commanded; mahātmanā rāvaṇena = by the mighty Ravana; rākṣhasa rājena = the king of demons; hantum = to kill; tau rāmalakṣhmaṇau = both Rama and Lakshmana; samara = in battle.

"The mighty Ravana, the king of demons commanded me to kill both Rama and Lakshmana in battle."

Sloka 11

adya rāmaṃ vadhiṣyāmi lakṣmaṇam ca niśācarāḥ |
śākhāmṛgaṃ ca sugrīvaṃ vānarāṃśca śarottamaiḥ || 6-78-11

11. adya = today; vadhiṣhyāmi = I will kill; rāmam = Rama; lakṣhmaṇam cha = Lakshmana; sugrīvam cha = ṣugreeva; shākhāmṛigam = the monkey; vānarān cha = and the (other) monkeys; sharottamaiḥ = with my excellent arrows; nishācharāḥ = O; demons!.

"Today, I will kill Rama, Lakshmana, Sugreeva the monkey and the other monkeys with my excellent arrows, O demons!"

Sloka 12

adya śūlanipātaiśca vānarāṇāṃ mahācamūm |
pradahiṣyāmi saṃprāptāṃ śuṣkendhanamivānalaḥ || 6-78-12

12. shūlanipātaiḥ = by the hurlings of spears; pradahiṣhyāmi = I will completely destroy; mahāchamūm = the huge army; vānarāṇām = of monkeys; samprāptām = who arrived ; adya = today; analaḥ iva = like when the fire; shuṣhkendhanam = destroys dry wood.

"By hurling the spears, I will destroy completely the huge army of monkeys who arrived today to the battle-field, as the fire destroys the dry wood."

Sloka 13

makarākṣasya tacchrutvā vacanaṃ te niśācarāḥ |
sarve nānāyudhopetā balavantaḥ samāhitāḥ || 6-78-13

13. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; makarākṣhasya = of ṃakaraksha; sarve = all; te = those; balavantaḥ = strong; nishācharāḥ = demons; nānāyudhopetāḥ = wielding various kinds of weapons; samāhitāḥ = were steadfast (to fight).

Hearing those words of ṃakaraksha, all those strong demons, wielding various kinds of weapons, were steadfast to fight.

Sloka 14

te kāmarūpiṇaḥ krūrā daṃṣṭriṇaḥ piṅgalekṣaṇāḥ |
mātaṅga iva nardanto dhvastakeśā bhayāvahāḥ || 6-78-14
parivārya mahākāyā mahākāyaṃ kharātmajam |
abhijaghnastato hṛṣṭāścalayanto nabhastalam || 6-78-15

14; 15. te krūrāḥ = the cruel demons; kāmarūpiṇah = who can change their forms at will; damṣhṭriṇaḥ = with their protruding tusks; piṇ^galekṣhaṇāḥ = with tawny coloured eyes; dhvantakeshāḥ = with their disheveled hair; bhayāvahāḥ = creating terror; mahākāyāḥ = with their colossal bodies; tataḥ = then; nardantaḥ = roaring; mātaṇgāḥ iva = like elephants; parivārya = having surrounded; mahākāyam kharātmajam = the huge-bodied ṃakaraksha; hṛiṣhṭāḥ = were overjoyed; abhijaghnuḥ = and marched forward; chālayantaḥ = shaking; nabhastalam = the firmament.

Those cruel demons, who can change their forms at will, who had protruding tusks, had tawny coloured eyes, had their disheveled hair, creating terror with their colossal bodies, then marched forward like roaring elephants, shaking the firmament and surrounded the huge-bodied ṃakaraksha.

Sloka 16

śaṅkhabherīsahasrāṇāmāhatānāṃ samantataḥ |
kṣvelitāsphoṭitānāṃ ca tatra mahānabhūt || 6-78-16

16. tatra abhūt = there arose; āhatānām = sounded; samantataḥ = on all sides; mahān = a great; shabdaḥ = tumult; shaṇ^kha bherī sahasrāṇām = of thousands of couches and kettle-drums; kṣhvelitāsphoṭitānām cha = leonine roars and clapping of arms.

There arose a great tumult sounded, on all sides, of thousands of couches and kettle-drums including leonine roars and clapping of arms.

Sloka 17

prabhraṣṭo.atha karāttasya pratodaḥ sārathestadā |
papāta sahasā daivāddhvajastasya tu rakṣasaḥ || 6-78-17

17. atha = thereupon; tasya sārathaḥ karāt = from that charioteers hand; pratodaḥ = the long whip; tadā = then; prabhraṣhṭaḥ = fell down; dhvajaḥ = and the flag-staff; tasya rakṣhasaḥ = of that demon; sahasā = suddenly; papāta = fell down; daivāt = by the will of the province.

The long whip then slipped down from the hands of ṃakaraksha's charioteer and the flag-staff of that demon also suddenly fell down by the will of the province.

Sloka 18

tasya te rathasamyuktā hayā vikramavarjitāḥ |
caraṇairākulairgatvā dīnāḥ sāsramukhā yayuḥ || 6-78-18

18. te hayāḥ = those horses; tasya ratha samyuktāḥ = yoked to his chariot; vikramavarjitāḥ = deprived of their diversified gait; gatvā = were walking; ākulaiḥ charaṇaiḥ = with flurried feet; yuyuḥ = and went; dināḥ = timidly; sasrumukhāḥ = with tears in their eyes.

Those horses, yoked to his chariot, deprived of their diversified gait, were walking with flurried feet and went timidly, with tears in their eyes.

Sloka 19

pravāti pavanastasmin sapāṃsuḥ kharadāruṇaḥ |
niryāṇe tasya raudrasya makarākṣasya durmateḥ || 6-78-19

19. tasmin niryāṇe = at the time of marching; tasya makarākṣhasya = of that ṃakaraksha; raudrasya = of dreadful appearance; durmateḥ = and of bad disposition of mind; pavanaḥ = the wind; sapāmsuḥ = with dust; khara dāruṇaḥ = which was dreadfully harsh; pravāti = blew forth.

At the time of marching of that dreadful and evil-minded ṃakaraksha, a harsh and fearful dust-storm blew forth.

Sloka 20

tāni dṛṣṭvā nimittāni rākṣasā vīryavattamāḥ |
acintya nirgatāḥ sarve yatra tau rāmalakṣmaṇau || 6-78-20

20. dṛiṣhṭvā = (ṭhough) seeing; tāni = all those; rākṣhasāḥ = demons; vīryavattamāḥ = of high prowess; achintya = ignoring (them); nirgatāḥ = set out; yatra = where; rāmalakṣhmaṇau = those Rama and Lakshmana (were there).

Though seeing those portents, all those highly valiant demons ignored them and set out to the place, where both Rama and Lakshmana were there.

Sloka 21

ghanagajamahiṣāṅgatulyavarṇāḥ |
samaramukheṣvasakṛdgadāsibhinnāḥ |
ahamahamiti yuddhakauśalāste |
rajanicarāḥ paribabhrāmurnadantaḥ || 6-78-21

21. nadantaḥ = saying thunderously; aham aham iti = "I shall take the lead; I shall take the lead"; te rajanīcharāḥ = those demons; ghanagajamahiṣhāṇ^ga tulya varṇāḥ = who resembled black clouds; elephants and buffaloes in hue; gadāsi bhinnāḥ asakṛit = who had been wounded with a pointed weapon more than once by maces and swords; samara mukheṣhu = in the battle-front; yuddha kaushalāḥ = and who were skilled in martial art; paribabhramuḥ = moved hither and thither (in the battle-field).

Saying thunderously, "I shall take the lead", those demons who resembled black clouds, elephants and buffaloes in hue, who had been wounded pointedly more than once by maces and swords in the battle-front and who were skilled in martial art, moved hither and thither in the battle-field.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe vālmīkīye ādikāvye yuddhakāṃḍe aṣṭasaptatitamaḥ sargaḥ
