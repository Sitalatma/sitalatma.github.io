Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 53
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

As ordered by Ravana, the demons wrap up old rags around Hanuma's tail, pour oil to it and burn it with fire. The demons tie Hanuma with ropes and conduct ihm to walk down the entire city, proclaining to the citizens of Lanka saying that Hanuma is a spy. The female-demons inform Seetha about the burning of Hanuma's tail and making him move around the city by demons. Then, Seetha starts praying the fire-god to do good to Hanuma. In response to her prayers, fire-god makes Hanuma feel cool with his burnign tail. Then, Hanuma approaches the city-gate of Lanka, takes an iron-rod from the arched door of the gate and kills all the security-guards there.


Sloka 1

tasya tat vacanam śrutvā daśagrīvo mahābalaḥ |
deśa kāla hitam vākyam bhrātuḥ uttamam abravīt || 5-53-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; desha kāla hitam = suitable for place and time; tasya mahāmanaḥ = spoken by that high-souled Vibhishana; bhrātuḥ = his brother; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the (following) words; uttaram = in reply.

ḥearing those words, which were aprropriate for that place and time, spoken by that hih-souled Vibhishana, his brother, Ravana spoke the following words in reply:

Sloka 2

samyag uktam hi bhavatā dūta vadhyā vigarhitā |
avaśyam tu vadhāt anyaḥ kriyatām asya nigrahaḥ || 5-53-2

2. uktamhi = It has been indeed told; samyak = well; bhavatā = by you; dūtavadhyā = killing of an envoy; vigrahitā = is forbidden; anyaḥ = another; nigrahaḥ = punishment; vadhāt = other than killing; avashyam kriyatām = needs be certainly meted out; asya = to him.

"You have spoken well indeed that killing of an envoy is forbidden. Another punishment other than killing needs be certainly meted out to him."

Sloka 3

kapīnām kila lāngūlam iṣṭam bhavati bhūṣaṇam |
tat asya dīpyatām śīghram tena dagdhena gacchatu || 5-53-3

3. kila = It is so said that; kapīnām = to monkeys; bhavati = it is possible; lāṇ^gūlam = that a tail; iṣṭam = is a beloved; bhūṣaṇam = ornament; tat lāṇ^gūlam = let that tail; asya = of ḥanuma; dīpyatām = be burnt; shīghram = immediately; gachchhatu = let him go; dagdhena tena = with a burnt tail.

"It is said that a tail is possibly a beloved ornament for the monkeys. Let his tail be burn immediately. Let him go with a burnt tail."

Sloka 4

tataḥ paśyantu imam dīnam anga vairūpya karśitam |
samitrā jñātayaḥ sarve bāndhavāḥ sasuhṛj janāḥ || 5-53-4

4. tataḥ = then; sarve = (Let) all; bāndhavāḥ = his relatives; samitrajJṇātāḥ = together with his friends and kinsmen; sasuhṛijjanāḥ = along with his amicable persons; pashyantu = see; imam = him; aṇ^gavairūpyakarshitano = injured iwth his deformed limbs; dīnam = miserable.

"Let all his relatives together with his friends, kinsmen and amicable persons then see him miserably injured with his deformed limbs."

Sloka 5

ājñāpayat rākṣasa indraḥ puram sarvam sacatvaram |
lāngūlena pradīptena rakṣobhiḥ pariṇīyatām || 5-53-5

5. rākṣasendraḥ = Ravana; ājJṇāpayat = ordered; pariṇīyatām = that let him be carried around; pradīptena; lāṇgūlena = with his burning tail; rakṣobhiḥ = by the demons; sarvam = in the entire; puram = city; sachatvaram = with its cross-roads.

Ravana ordered that let the monkey be carried around with his burning tail by the demons in the entire city around its cross-roads."

Sloka 6

tasya tat vacanam śrutvā rākṣasāḥ kopa karkaśāḥ |
veṣṭante tasya lāngūlam jīrṇaiḥ kārpāsikaiḥ paṭaiḥ || 5-53-6

6. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; tasya = of Ravana; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; kopakarkashāḥ = hard-tempered with their wrath; jīrṇaiḥ kārpāsakaiḥ paṭaiḥ = with old ragged garments.

ḥearing those words of Ravana, the demons, hard-tempered with their wrath, wrapped, up old ragged clothes around Hanuma's tail.

Sloka 7

samveṣṭyamāne lāngūle vyavardhata mahākapiḥ |
śuṣkam indhanam āsādya vaneṣv iva huta aśanaḥ || 5-53-7

7. lāṇ^gūle samveṣṭyamāne = while his tail was wrapped around with cottong tatters; mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; vyavardhata = increased his body; hutāshanaḥ iva = like a fire; āsādya = after catching; shuṣkam indhanam = dry wood; vaneṣu = in forests.

While his tail was being wrapped around with cotton tatters, ḥanuma proliferating, soon after catching dry wood in forests.

Sloka 8

tailena pariṣicya atha te agnim tatra avapātayan |
lāngūlena pradīptena rākṣasān tān apātayat || 5-53-8
roṣa amarṣa parīta ātmā bāla sūrya sama ānanaḥ |

8. atha = then; pariṣichya = sprinkling; tailena = oil; te = they; abhyapātayan = set out; agnim = fire; tatra = on that tail; bālasūryasamānanaḥ = ḥanuma; with his face resembling a rising sun; roṣāmarṣaparītātmā = and with his mind filled with rage and impatience; pradīptena lāṇgūlena = and with his burning tail; apātayat = threw down; tān rākṣasān = those demons.

ṭhe demons sprinkled oil and set out fire on that tail. ṭhen, ḥanuma, with his face resembling a rising sun with his mind filled with anger and impatience and with his burning tail, threw those demons down.

Sloka 9

lāṅgūlam sampradīptam tu draṣṭum tasya hanūmataḥ || 5-53-9
sahastrībālavṛddhāśca jagmuḥ prītā niśācarāḥ |

9. draṣṭum = to see; sampradītptam = the burning; lāṇ^gūlam = tail; tasya hanūmataḥ = of that ḥanuma; nishācharāḥ = the demons; sahastrī bāla vṛiddhāshcha = together with their women; children and elders; jagmuḥ = went; prītāḥ = delighted.

ṭo see the burning tail of that ḥanuma, the delighted demons together with their women, chldren and elders went there.

Sloka 10

sa bhūyaḥ samgataiḥ krūrai rākasaiḥ hari sattamaḥ || 5-53-10
nibaddhaḥ kṛtavān vīraḥ tat kāla sadṛśīm matim |

10. nibaddhaḥ = tied down; samgataiḥ = coming together; saḥ vīraḥ harisattamaḥ = that heroic ḥanuma; the foremost among the monkeys; kṛitavān = made up; matim = his mind; tatkāla sadṛishīm = appropriate for that occasion (as follows):

ṭied down again by the cruel demons, coming together, the heroic ḥanuma the foremost among monkeys made up his mind appropriate for that occasion (as follows):

Sloka 11

kāmam khalu na me śaktā nibadhasya api rākṣasāḥ || 5-53-11
cittvā pāśān samutpatya hanyām aham imān punaḥ |

11. nibaddhasyāpi = though I have been tied down; rākṣasāḥ = those demons; na shaktāḥ kāmam khalu = cannot do anything they wish; me = against me; chhitvā = breaking; pāshān = the nooses; punaḥ = again; aham = I; samutpatya = by jumping up; hanyām = can kill; imān = them.

"ṭhough I have been tied down, those demons cannot do whatever they wish to do against me. Breaking the ties, I can fly up and kill them."

Sloka 12

yadibharturhatārthāya carantam bhartṛśāsanāt || 5-53-12
badhnantyete durātmano na tu me niṣkṛtiḥ kṛtā |

12. ete durātmanaḥ yadi = If these evil minded demons; badhnanti = fasten; charantam = me; acting; hitārthāya = for the benefit; bhartuḥ = of Rama; my lord; bhartuḥ = of Rama; my lord; bhartṛishāsanāt = because of the command of their lord; na niṣkṛitiḥ = no rebuff; kṛitā = has been done; me = to me.

"If these evil-minded demons fasten me who is acting for the good of Rama my lord, because of the command by their lord, no rebuff has been done to me."

Sloka 13

sarveṣām eva paryāpto rākṣasānām aham yudhi || 5-53-13
kim tu rāmasya prīti artham viṣahiṣye aham īdṛśam |
lankā carayitavyā me punaḥ eva bhavet iti || 5-53-14

13; 14. ahami = I; paryāptaḥ = am adequate; sarveṣāmeva rākṣasānām = even for all the demons; yudhi = in battle; kimtu = but; prītyartham = for the delight; rāmasya = of Rama; īdṛisham = such; laṇ^kā = Lanka; punareva = again; bhavet = becomes; chārayitavyā = fit to be espionaged; iti = for this reason; viṣahiṣye = I am putting up with it.

"I am adequate enough to kill all these demons in battle. But, for the delight of Rama, such city of Lanka is fit to be espionaged again. For this reason, I am putting up with it."

Sloka 15

rātrau na hi sudṛṣṭā me durga karma vidhānataḥ |
avaśyam eva draṣṭavyā mayā lankā niśā kṣaye || 5-53-15

15. laṇ^kā = Lanka; na hi sudṛiṣṭā = was not indeed observed well; rātrau = during night-time; durgakarmavidhānataḥ = as to the particulars of specifications of the fort; meva = surely; draṣṭavyā = it is to be seen; mayā = by me; nishākṣaye = in the day-time.

"Lanka was not indeed observed well as to the particulars of specifications of the fort, when it was seen by me during the night. Surely, it is to be seen by me during the day-time."

Sloka 16

kāmam bandhaiḥ ca me bhūyaḥ pucchasya uddīpanena ca |
pīḍām kurvantu rakṣāṃsi na me asti manasaḥ śramaḥ || 5-53-16

16. me = to me; baddhasya = who has been tied down; bhūyaḥ = again; rakṣāmsi = let the demons; kurvantu pīDām = torment me; kāmam = as they wish; uddīpanenacha = by burning also; puchchhasya = my tail; me manasaḥ = to my mind; nāsti = there will be no; shramaḥ = travail.

"Let the demons tie me down of again and torment me as they wish, by burning my tail. There will be no travail to my mind."

Sloka 17

tataḥ te samvṛta ākāram sattvavantam mahākapim |
parigṛhya yayuḥ hṛṣṭā rākṣasāḥ kapi kunjaram || 5-53-17

17. tataḥ = then; te hṛiṣṭāḥ rākṣasāḥ = those delighted demons; yayuḥ = went; parigṛihya = taking; mahākapim = ḥanuma; savṛitākāram = who concealed all types of feelings; sattvavantam = who was strong; kapikuṇ^jaram = and the foremost among monkeys.

ṭhen, those delighted demons went, seizing ḥanuma, who concealed all types of his feelings, who was strong and foremost among monkeys.

Sloka 18

śankha bherī ninādaiḥ taiḥ ghoṣayantaḥ sva karmabhiḥ |
rākṣasāḥ krūra karmāṇaḥ cārayanti sma tām purīm || 5-53-18

18. shaṇkhabherī ninādaishcha = sounding couches and kettle-drums; ghoṣayantaḥ = proclaiming; svakarmabhiḥ = the acts of ḥanuma; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; krūrakarmāṇaḥ = performing terrible deeds; chārayantisma = dragged him; tām purim = throughout that city.

ṣounding couches and kettle-drums and proclaining the acts of ḥanuma like his damaging of the pleasure-garden, the demons performing terrible deeds, dragged Hanuma through out that city.

Sloka 19

anvīyamāno rakṣobhiryayau sukhamariṃdamaḥ |
hanumān cārayāmāsa rākṣasānām mahāpurīm || 5-53-19

19. anvīyamānaḥ = accompanied; rakṣobhiḥ = by demons; hanumān = ḥanuma; arimdamaḥ = the annihilator of enemies; yayau = went; sukham = happily; chārayāmāsa = and roamed about; mahāpurīm = in the great city; rākṣasānām = of the demons.

Accompanied by demons, ḥanuma the annihilator of enemies, went happily and roamed about in that great city of demons.

Sloka 20

atha apaśyat vimānāni vicitrāṇi mahākapiḥ |
samvṛtān bhūmi bhāgān ca suvibhaktān ca catvarān || 5-53-20

20. atha = then; mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; apashyat = saw; vichitrāṇi = wonderful; vimānāni = houses; samvṛitān = wrapped up; bhūmi bhāgāmshcha = plots of land; suvibhaktān = well-parted; chatvarān = cross-ways.

ṭhen, ḥanuma saw some wonderful house some wrapped up plots of land and well parted cross ways.

Sloka 21

vīthīśca gṛhasambādhāḥ kapiḥ śṛṅgaṭakāni ca |
tathā rathyoparathyāśca tathaiva gṛhakāntarān || 5-53-21
gṛhāṃśca meghasamkāśān dadarśa pavanātmajaḥ |

21. kapiḥ = ḥanuma; pavanātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; dadarsha = saw; vīthīḥ = streets; gṛihasambādhāḥ = congested with houses; shṛiṇgāṭakāni = places where several roads meet; tathā = and; rathyoparathyāshcha = high-ways as also approach-roads; gṛihakāntarān = small inner apartments; gṛihāmshcha = and palaces; meghasamkāshān = appearing in multitudes like clouds.

ḥanuma, the son of wind-god, saw streets congested with dwellings, places where several roads meet, high-ways as also approach-roads, small inner apartments and palaces appearing in multitudes, like clouds.

Sloka 22

catvareṣu catuṣkeṣu rāja mārge tathaiva ca || 5-53-22
ghoṣayanti kapim sarve cārīka iti rākṣasāḥ |

22. sarve = all; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; ghoṣayanti = proclaimed; chatvareṣu = in cross-roads; chatuṣkeṣu = four-pillared temples; tathaiva cha = and; rājamārge = royal high-ways; kapim chārikaḥ iti = saying that ḥanuma was a spy.

All the demons proclaimed in cross roads, four-pillared temples and in royal high-ways, saying that Hanuma was a spy.

Sloka 23

strībālavṛddhā nirjagmustatra tatra kutūhalāt || 5-53-23
tam pradīpitalāṅgūlam hanumantam didṛkṣavaḥ |

23. strībāla vṛiddhāḥ = women; children and old people; nirjagmuḥ = came out; tatra tatra = from their respective dwellings; kutūhalāt = with an eagerness; didṛikṣavaḥ = to see; tam hanumantam = that ḥanuma; pradīpila lāṇ^gmulam = with his burnign tail.

Women, children and old people came out from their respective dwellings, with an eagerness to see that Hanuma with his burning tail.

Sloka 24

dīpyamāne tataḥ tasya lāngūla agre hanūmataḥ || 5-53-24
rākṣasyaḥ tā virūpa akṣyaḥ śaṃsuḥ devyāḥ tat apriyam |

24. hanumataḥ lāṇgullāgre = (while) the tip of ḥanuma's tail; dīpyamāne = was being ignited; tatra = there; tāḥ rākṣasyaḥ = those female-demons; virūpākṣyaḥ = with monstrous eyes; tataḥ = then; shashamsuḥ = inforemed; tat apriyam = that unpleasant thing; devyāḥ = to ṣeetha the princess.

While the tip of ḥanuma's tail was being ignited by the demons there, those female-demons with monstrous eyes then informed that unpleasant matter to Seetha, the princess.

Sloka 25

yaḥ tvayā kṛta samvādaḥ sīte tāmra mukhaḥ kapiḥ || 5-53-25
lāngūlena pradīptena sa eṣa pariṇīyate |

25. sīte = O ṣeetha!; sa eṣāḥ kapiḥ = that monkey; yaḥ = who; tāmra mukhaḥ = was having a red face; kṛita samvādaḥ = and who had conversation; tvayā = with you; pariṇīyate = is being moved around; pradīptena lāṇgūlena = with his ignited tail; (in the city).

"O ṣeetha! ṭhat monkey with a red face, who had a conversation with you, is being moved around in the city, with his ignited tail."

Sloka 26

śrutvā tat vacanam krūram ātma apaharaṇa upamam || 5-53-26
vaidehī śoka samtaptā huta aśanam upāgamat |

26. shrutvā = hearing; tat krūram vachanam = that cruel news; ātmāpaharaṇopamam = which was equal to the news of her carrying away (by Ravana); vaidehī = ṣeetha; shoka samtaptā = was tormented with grief; upāgamat = and paid homage; hutāshanam = the fire-god.

ḥearing that cruel news, which created as much sorrow as when she was carried away by Ravana, Seetha was tormented with grief and started to pay homage to the fire-god.

Sloka 27

mangalā abhimukhī tasya sā tadā āsīn mahākapeḥ || 5-53-27
upatasthe viśāla akṣī prayatā havya vāhanam |

27. tadā = then; sā = she; āsīt = was maṇ^galābhimukhī = wishing for the welfare; mahākapeḥ = of ḥanuma; vishālākṣi = the large-eyed ṣeetha; prayatā = with her devoted self; upatasthe = worshipped; havya vāhanam = the fire-god.

ṭhen, the large-eyed ṣeetha, wishing for the welfare of ḥanuma, prayed the fire-god with her devoted self.

Sloka 28

yadi asti pati śuśrūṣā yadi asti caritam tapaḥ || 5-53-28
yadi ca asti eka patnītvam śīto bhava hanūmataḥ |

28. asti yadi = If I have done; patishushrūṣā = any service to my husband; astiyadi charitam = if I have done; tapaḥ = a penance; asti cha yadi = if I am; eka patnītvam = a faithful wife; tvam = you; bhava = be; shītaḥ = cool; hanumataḥ = to ḥanuma.

"If I have done any service to my husband, if I have performed a penance and if I am a faithful wife, please be cool to Hanuma."

Sloka 29

yadi kaścit anukrośaḥ tasya mayi asti dhīmataḥ || 5-53-29
yadi vā bhāgya śeṣam me śīto bhava hanūmataḥ |

29. dhīmataḥ tasya yadi = if the wise Rama; kimchit = has even a little; anukroshaḥ = of Kindness; mayi = towards me; yadi vā me = and if towards me; bhāgya sheṣaḥ = some good fortune is remaining; bhava = be; shītaḥ = cool; hanūmataḥ = to ḥanuma.

"If the wise Rama has even a little of kindness to me and if I have still some good fortune remaining to my credit, please be cool to Hanuma."

Sloka 30

yadi mām vṛtta sampannām tat samāgama lālasām || 5-53-30
sa vijānāti dharma ātmā śīto bhava hanūmataḥ |

30. saḥ dharmātmā yadi = If that virtuous Rama; vijānāti = understands; mām = me; vṛittasampannam = as endowed with a good moral conduct; tatsamāgama lālasām = ardently desirous of meeting him; bhava = be; shītaḥ = cool; hanumataḥ = to ḥanuma.

"If that virtuous Rama recognises me as one,, who is endowed with a good moral conduct and who is ardently desirous of meeting him, please be cool to Hanuma."

Sloka 31

yadi mām tārayati āryaḥ sugrīvaḥ satya samgaraḥ || 5-53-31
asmāt duhkhān mahābāhuḥ śīto bhava hanūmataḥ |

31. āryaḥ yadi = (If) the venerable; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; satya samgaraḥ = true to his promise; me tārayet = can make me traverse; asmāt duḥ khāmbu samrodhāt = from this ocean of sorrow; bhava = be; shītaḥ = cool; hanūmataḥ = to ḥanuma.

"If the venerable ṣugreeva, true to his promise, can make me traverse from this ocean of sorrow, please be cool to Hanuma."

Sloka 32

tataḥ tīkṣṇa arciḥ avyagraḥ pradakṣiṇa śikho analaḥ || 5-53-32
jajvāla mṛga śāva akṣyāḥ śaṃsann iva śivam kapeḥ |

32. tataḥ = then; analaḥ = the fire-god; shamsanniva = as though informing; mṛigashabākṣyāḥ = the fawn-eyed; ṣeetha; kapeḥ shivam = about the welfare of ḥanuma; jajvāla = sone brilliantly; tīkṣṇārchiḥ = with sharp flames; avyagraḥ = undisturbed; pradakṣiṇa shikhaḥ = shooting out pointed flames towards the right.

ṭhen, the fire-god, as though informing the fawn-eyed ṣeetha about the well-being of Hanuma, shone brightly with sharp flames, undisteurbed and shooting out pointed flames towards the right.

Sloka 33

hanumajjanakaścāpi pucchānalayuto.anilaḥ || 5-53-33
vavau svāsthyakaro devyāḥ prāleyānilaśītalaḥ |

33. anilaḥ apicha = even wind-god; hanumajjanakaḥ = father of ḥanuma; puchchhānala yutaḥ = combined with the fire on his tail; vavau = swept; prāleyānila shītalaḥ = coolly like a snow-breeze; svāsthyakaraḥ = creating a soloce; devyāḥ = to ṣeetha.

Even wind-god, the father of ḥanuma, combined with the fire on ḥanuma's tail, swept coolly like a snow-breeze, creating a solace to Seetha.

Sloka 34

dahyamāne ca lāngūle cintayāmāsa vānaraḥ || 5-53-34
pradīpto agniḥ ayam kasmān na mām dahati sarvataḥ |

34. laṇ^gūle = (While) his tail; dahyamāne = was burning; vānaraḥ = ḥanuma; chintayāmāsa = was reflecting; kasmāt = why; ayam agniḥ = this fire; pradīptaḥ = which was burning; sāvataḥ = on all sides; na dahati = was not burning; mām = him.

While his tail was in flames, ḥanuma was reflecting why the fire, which was blazing on all sides, was not burning him.

Sloka 35

dṛśyate ca mahājvālaḥ karoti ca na me rujam || 5-53-35
śiśirasya iva sampāto lāngūla agre pratiṣṭhitaḥ |

35. dṛishyate = It is conspicuous; mahājvālaḥ = with large flames; na karoti cha = not creating;rujam = paoin; me = to me; iva = as though; shishirasya samghātaḥ = a snow-ball; pratiṣṭhitaḥ = is kept; lāṇgūlāgre = at the tip of my tail.

"It is conspicuous with large flames. But it is not creating any paoin to me, as if a snow-ball is kept at the tip of my tail."

Sloka 36

athavā tat idam vyaktam yat dṛṣṭam plavatā mayā || 5-53-36
rāma prabhāvāt āścaryam parvataḥ saritām patau |

36. athava = Or; plavatā = while jumping over; mayā = by me; rāma prabhāvāt = due to the power of Rama; āshcharyam = a surprise; dṛiṣṭam = was seen; parvatodadhi samgame = in a friendly alliance with a mountain and the sea; yat tat = by which; idam = this; vyaktam = is clear.

"Or, while I was jumping over the ocean, a surprise-alliance was formed with ṃount ṃainaka and through the mountain, with the ocean, because of Rama's power. By this, the reason of the coolness is clear."

Sloka 37

yadi tāvat samudrasya mainākasya ca dhīmatha || 5-53-37
rāma artham sambhramaḥ tādṛk kim agniḥ na kariṣyati |

37. yadi = If; samudrasya = for the ocean; dhīmataḥ mynakasya = and the wise ṃount ṃainaka; tādṛik sambhramaḥ = such an eagerness was tehre; rāmārtham = for the sake of Rama; kim = why; agniḥ = the fire-god; nakariṣyati = will not do it?

"If such an eagerness was there in the mind of the sea and the wise ṃount ṃainaka for the cause of Rama; will not the fire-god show the same degree of eagerness in making its touch cold?"

Sloka 38

sītāyāḥ ca ānṛśaṃsyena tejasā rāghavasya ca || 5-53-38
pituḥ ca mama sakhyena na mām dahati pāvakaḥ |

38. pāvakaḥ = the fire; na dahati = does not burn; mām = me; ānṛishamsyena = due to kindness; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; tejasā = power; rāghavasya = of Rama; sakhyena = and friendship; mama pituḥ = of my father.

"ṭhe fire does not burn me, due to kindness of ṣeetha, powerfulness of Rama and friendlines of my father."

Sloka 39

bhūyaḥ sa cintayāmāsa muhūrtam kapi kunjaraḥ || 5-53-39
utpapāta atha vegena nanāda ca mahākapiḥ |

39. saḥ mahākapiḥ = that ḥanuma; kapikuṇ^jaraḥ = the foremost among mokeys; chintayāmāsa = reflected; bhūyaḥ = again; atha = and thereafter; utpapāta = jumped; vegena = with swiftness; nanāda cha = and roared also.

ṭhat ḥanuma, the foremost among the monkeys, reflected agian for a monment, jumped with swiftness and roared too.

Sloka 40

pura dvāram tataḥ śrīmān śaila śṛngam iva unnatam || 5-53-40
vibhakta rakṣaḥ sambādham āsasāda anila ātmajaḥ |

40. tataḥ = then; shrīmān = the glorious; anilātmajaḥ = ḥanuma; shaila shṛiṇ^gamiva = like a mountain-peak; āsasāda = approached; puradvāram = the city-gate; unnatam = which was looking high; vibhaktarakṣaḥ sambādham = and stood secluded from the crowds of demons.

ṭhen, the glorious ḥanuma like a mountain-peak, approached the city-gate, which was looking high and stood secluded from the crowds of demons.

Sloka 41

sa bhūtvā śaila samkāśaḥ kṣaṇena punaḥ ātmavān || 5-53-41
hrasvatām paramām prāpto bandhanāni avaśātayat |

41. saḥ ātmavān = that prudent ḥanuma; bhūtvā = became; shaila samkāshaḥ = similar to a mountain; kṣaṇena = and within a moment; prāptaḥ = reached; paramām hrasvatām = an exceedingly short form; avashātayat = and became free; bandhanāni = from tethers.

ṭhat prudent ḥanuma assumed the form of a mountain and immediately within a moment, assumed an exceedingly short form, thus becoming free from his tethers.

Sloka 42

vimuktaḥ ca abhavat śrīmān punaḥ parvata samnibhaḥ || 5-53-42
vīkṣamāṇaḥ ca dadṛśe parigham toraṇa āśritam |

42. shrīmān = the glorious ḥanuma; vimuktashcha = having been free from his tethers; punaḥ = again; abhavat = assumed; parvatasannibhaḥ = a form equal to a mountain; vīkṣamāṇashcha = and while looking around; dadṛishe = saw; parigham = an iron rod; toraṇāshritam = belonging to the arched door-way.

ṭhe glorious ḥanuma, having been free from his bonds, again assumed the form equal to a mountain and while looking around, found and iron rod belonging to the arched door-way.

Sloka 43

sa tam gṛhya mahābāhuḥ kāla āyasa pariṣkṛtam || 5-53-43
rakṣiṇaḥ tān punaḥ sarvān sūdayāmāsa mārutiḥ |

43. saḥ mahābāhuḥ = that long-armed; mārutiḥ = ḥanuma; punaḥ = again; gṛihya = taking; tam = that rod; kālāasapariṣkṛitam = made of iron; sūdayāmāsa = killed; sarvān = all; tān rakṣiṇaḥ = tose guards.

ṭhat long-armed ḥanuma, again taking taht rod made of iron, killed all those guards.

Sloka 44

sa tān nihatvā raṇa caṇḍa vikramaḥ |
samīkṣamāṇaḥ punaḥ eva lankām |
pradīpta lāngūla kṛta arci mālī |
prakāśata āditya iva aṃśu mālī || 5-53-44

44. saḥ = that ḥanuma; raṇa chaṇ^Da vikramaḥ = possessing impetuous valour in combat; nihattvā = destroying; tān = them; punareva = and again; samīkṣamāṇaḥ = looking over; laṇ^kām = Lanka; prakāshata = shone; āditya iva = like the sun; archimālī = encircled with rays; pradīpta lāṇgūlakṛitārchi mālī = with the wreath of flames; blazing on his tail.

ṭhat ḥanuma, having an impetuous valour in combat, destroying those demons and looking over Lanka, shone like the sun encircled with rays, with the wreath of flames blazing on his tail.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe tripañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 53rd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
