Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 24
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Kausalya requests Rama to take her to forest also. But Rama stops Kausalya saying that service of husband is the ultimate dharma of wife and he obtains her permission for him to go to forest.

Sloka 1

tam samīkṣya tu avahitam pitur nirdeśa pālane |
kausalyā bāṣpa samruddhā vaco dharmiṣṭham abravīt || 2-24-1

1. samīkshhya = seeing; tam = him; avahatam = firm minded; nirdeshapālane = in protecting the command; pituḥ = of father; bhāshhpa samruddā = in a choked voice; abravīt = spoke; vachaḥ = words; dharmishhṭham = which are righteous.

Seeing him firm minded in protecting the command of his father, Kausalya in a choked voice spoke these righteous words:

Sloka 2

adṛṣṭa duhkho dharmātmā sarva bhūta priyam vadaḥ |
mayi jātaḥ daśarathāt katham uncena vartayet || 2-24-2

2. jātaḥ = Rama who is born; mayi = in me; dasharathāt = and Dasharatha; dharmātmā = is a righteous person; sarvabhūtapriyamvada = who speaks lovingly to all beings; adṛishhṭa duḥkhah = who has not seen sorrow ; katham = how; vartayet = can he live; uṇchena = by gathering grains fallen on floor ?

�Rama who is born in me and Dasaratha, is a righteous person. He speaks lovingly to all beings. He has not faced grief at any time. How can such a person live in a forest, by gathering grains ?'

Sloka 3

yasya bhṛtyāḥ ca dāsāḥ ca mṛṣṭāni annāni bhunjate |
katham sa bhokṣyate nātho vane mūla phalāni ayam || 2-24-3

3. yasya = whose bhṛityāshcha = dependants and; dāsāshcha = servants bhuṇJjate = eat; mṛishṭāni = pleasant or tasty annāni = foods ; katham = how ayam = such Rama nātha = the lord ; bhokshhyate = can eat mūlaphalāni = roots and fruits vane = in forest ?

"While the dependants and the servants of Lord Rama eat pleasant foods all the while, how can he eat roots and fruits himself in the forest ?"

Sloka 4

ka etat śraddadhet śrutvā kasya vā na bhaved bhayam |
guṇavān dayitaḥ rājño rāghavo yad vivāsyate || 2-24-4

4. srutvā = hearing; etat = that; raghavaḥ = Rama; dayitaḥ = the beloved; guṇavān = the virtuous; vivāsyate itivat = is being sent to exile thus; rajJṇā = by king; kaḥ = who; shraddadhet = will believe ? kasyavā = to whom; bhayam = fear; nabhavet = is not created ?

"Hearing that Rama the beloved and virtuous is being thus sent to exile by the king , who will believe these words? Who will not develop fear ?"

Sloka 5

nūnam tu balavān loke kṛtāntaḥ sarvamādiśan |
loke rāmābhirāmastvam vanam yatra gamiṣyasi || 2-24-5

5. rāmaḥ = oh; Rama ! tvam = you; loke abhirāmaḥ = the charming in the world; yatra gamishhyati = that you are going ; vanam = to forest; sarvam = everything; ādishan = is ordered by; kṛitāntaḥ = destiny; nunām = certainly; balavān = having ;strength .

�Oh, Rama ! If you , the charming in the world are to go to exile , then it can be certainly said that everything is ordered by a strong destiny'

Sloka 6

ayam tu māmātmabhavastavādarśanamārutaḥ |
vilāpaduḥkhasamidho ruditāśruhutāhutiḥ || 2-24-6
cintābāṣpamahādhūstavāgamanacintajaḥ |
karśayitvā bhṛśam putra niśvāsāyāsasambhavaḥ || 2-24-7
tvayā vihīnām iha mām śoka agnir atulo mahān |
pradhakṣyati yathā kakṣam citra bhānur hima atyaye || 2-24-8

6;7;8. kumārā = Oh; ṣon ! ayam shokāgni = this fire of my sorrow; atulaḥ = which is unique; mahān = great ; ātmabhavāḥ = which is originating in the whole body; tava adarshana mārutaḥ = having wind of your not being seen; vilāpa duḥkha samidhaḥ = having fire - twigs of lamentation and grief; ruditāshru hutāhutiḥ = having offering of wailing tears as oblation to fire ; chinthā bhāshpa mahā dhumāḥ = having great smoke of choking caused by anxiety ; tavā āgamana chinthajaḥ = generated out of the thought of your return ; nishvāsāyasa sambhavaḥ = created out of sigh and fatigue; mām karshayitvā = makes me emaciated; bhṛisham = much; iha = here; vihīnām = isolated; tvayā = with you; pradhakshhyati = can burn; kakshham yathā = like dry wood ; chitra bhānuḥ = by fire ; himātyaye = in winter .'

"Oh, Son! Soon after your departure to a distant forest, a great and incomparable fire of sorrow will generate in my whole body. The wind which flares it up is your not being seen here. ṃy lamentation and grief are the fire-sticks. ṃy wailing tears will be the offerings as an oblation to fire. The choking and anxiety will be a great smoke .The thought that when you would return, will generate smoke again. Such a fire of grief will make me emaciated completely and will burn like dry wood by fire in winter.'

Sloka 9

katham hi dhenuḥ svam vatsam gaccantam na anugaccati |
aham tvā anugamiṣyāmi yatra putra gamiṣyasi || 2-24-9

9. putraḥ = Oh; ṣon; kathamhi = how indeed; dhenuḥ = a cow; nānugachhati = will not follow; svam vatsam = its calf; gachhantam = going ? aham = I; anugamishhyāmi = shall follow; yatra = where; tvā = you; gamishhyāsi = will go."

"Oh, Son! A cow follows its calf wherever it goes. So also, I shall come with you wherever you go."'

Sloka 10

tathā nigaditam mātrā tat vākyam puruṣa Rṣabhaḥ |
śrutvā rāmaḥ abravīd vākyam mātaram bhṛśa duhkhitām ||
2-24-10

10. rājā = king; vaṇch^itaḥ = deceived; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; mayi = I ; āshrite = having recourse to; araṇyam = forest; parityaktaḥ = abandoned; bhavatyāpi = by you also; na vartayishhyati = he will not be alive; nūnam = certain."

"Oh, son! A cow follows its calf wherever it goes. So also, I shall come with you wherever you go."

Sloka 11

kaikeyyā vancitaḥ rājā mayi ca araṇyam āśrite |
bhavatyā ca parityaktaḥ na nūnam vartayiṣyati || 2-24-11

11. rājā = king; vaṇch^itaḥ = deceived; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; mayi = I ; āshrite = having recourse to; araṇyam = forest; parityaktaḥ = abandoned; bhavatyāpi = by you also; na vartayishhyati = he will not be alive; nūnam = certain !'

"Kaikeyi already deceived the king .He will not be alive, when I leave for the forest and you also leave him .It is certain."

Sloka 12

bhartuḥ kila parityāgo nṛśaṃsaḥ kevalam striyāḥ |
sa bhavatyā na kartavyo manasā api vigarhitaḥ || 2-24-12

12. striyāḥ = to a woman; parityāgaḥ = abandonment; bhartuḥ = of husband; nṛishamsaḥ kila = is indeed cruel act; kevalam = entirely; saḥ = that; vigarhitaḥ = bad act; na Kartavyaḥ = is not to be done; bhavatyā = by you; manasāpi = even by mind.

"To a woman, abandonment of husband is entirely a cruel act. That bad act is not to be perceived even by mind."

Sloka 13

yāvaj jīvati kākutsthaḥ pitā me jagatī patiḥ |
śuśrūṣā kriyatām tāvat sa hi dharmaḥ sanātanaḥ || 2-24-13

13. yāvat = ṭill which time; kākutsaḥ = Dasaratha; me pitā = my father; jagatīpatiḥ = the lord of earth; jīvati = is alive; tavāt = till such time; kriyatām shushrūshā = he ;should be served; saḥ = that ; ṣanātanaḥ dharmaḥ hi = is indeed eternal justice .'

"Till Dasaratha, my father and the great king is alive, you should serve him well. It is eternal justice."

Sloka 14

evam uktā tu rāmeṇa kausalyā śubha darśanā |
tathā iti uvāca suprītā rāmam akliṣṭa kāriṇam || 2-24-14

14. evam = thus; uktā = spoken; rāmeva = by Rama; kausalyā = kausalya; shubhadarshanā = with auspicious appearance; suprītā = is very much pleased ; uvācha = spoke; rāma = to Rama; aklishhṭakāriṇam = who performs undefatigable actions; tatheti = showing concurrance.

After Rama spoke thus, Kausalya with auspicious appearance, was very much pleased and spoke to Rama, who performs indefatigable actions, in consent.

Sloka 15

evam uktaḥ tu vacanam rāmaḥ dharmabhRtām varaḥ |
bhūyaḥ tām abravīd vākyam mātaram bhṛśa duhkhitām || 2-24-15

15. rāmaḥ = Rama; dharmabhṛitām varaḥ = the best among upholders of righteousness; uktaḥ = having been spoken; evam = thus ; vachanam = the words; abravīt = spoke; bhūyaḥ = again; tā mātaram = to that mother; bhṛisha duḥKhitām = who was very much distressed .

That Rama, the best among upholders of righteousness, after hearing the words of his mother ,spoke again to her who was very much distressed.

Sloka 16

mayā caiva bhavatyā ca kartavyam vacanam pituḥ |
rājā bhartā guruḥ śreṣṭhaḥ sarveṣām īśvaraḥ prabhuḥ || 2-24-16

16. vachanam = words; pituḥ = of father; kartavyam = are fit to be done; mayāchaiva = by me; bhavatyācha = and by you; sarveshhām = for all; rājā = king; bhartā = nourisher; guruḥ = venerable man; shreshhṭaḥ = the best man; īshvaraḥ = lord; prabhuḥ = ruler.'

"The words of father are to be followed by you and me. For all, he is the king, the nourished, a venerable man, the best among men, the lord and the ruler."

Sloka 17

imāni tu mahā araṇye vihRtya nava panca ca |
varṣāṇi parama prītaḥ sthāsyāmi vacane tava || 2-24-17

17. "vihṛitya = after roaming; mahāraṇye = in great forest; imāni = these; nava paṇchacha = fourteen; varshhāṇi = years; sthāsyāmi = I shall stand by; tava = your; vachane = words; parama prītaḥ = very gladly".

"After roaming in the great forest for fourteen years, I shall come back and very gladly stand by your command."

Sloka 18

evam uktā priyam putram bāṣpa pūrṇa ānanā tadā |
uvāca parama ārtā tu kausalyā putra vatsalā || 2-24-18

18. tadā = then; kausalyā = Kausalya; putra vatsalā = who is affectionate to her son; uktā = having been spoken; ivam = thus; uvācha = spoke; priyam putram = to beloved son; bhāshhpapūrṇānanā = with her face ;filled with tears; paramārtā = in great sorrow .

After hearing the words of Rama, Kausalya who was affectionate to her son , was in great sorrow with her face filled with tears and spoke thus to her beloved son Rama .

Sloka 19

āsām rāma sapatnīnām vastum madhye na me kṣamam |
naya mām api kākutstha vanam vanyam mRgīm yathā || 2-24-19
yadi te gamane buddhiḥ kṛtā pitur apekṣayā |

19. "ramā = Oh; Rama! na khhamam = it is not capable; me = of me; vastum = to reside; madhye = in the middle of; āsām = these; sapathnīnām = step wives; kākutthsa = oh; Rama ! kṛitāyadi = If it is made; te buddhiḥ = of your mind; gamane = to go to forest; apekshhayā = by wish; pituḥ = of father ; naya = take; māmapi = also me; mṛigīm yathā = like female deer vanyām = belonging to forest; vanam = to forest"

"Oh, Rama! I can not reside in the middle of these step-wives. If you have made up your mind to go to forest as per your father's wish ,also take me, like taking a female deer of the forest ,to the woods ''

Sloka 20

tām tathā rudatīm rāmaḥ rudan vacanam abravīt || 2-24-20
jīvantyā hi striyā bhartā daivatam prabhur eva ca |

20. ramāḥ = Rama; abravīt = spoke ; tathā = thus; vachanam = these words; rudan = bemoaningly; tān = to her; rudatīm = who was weeping; stryāḥ = to woman; jīvantyāḥ = who is living; bhartā = husband; daivatam = is god; prabhurevacha = and master.

"Rama spoke thus these words bemoaningly to her, who was weeping= "as long as a woman is alive ,her husband is god and master to her".

Sloka 21

bhavatyā mama caiva adya rājā prabhavati prabhuḥ |
bharataḥ ca api dharmātmā sarva bhūta priyam vadaḥ || 2-24-21

21. "rājā = king Dasaratha; prabhuḥ = the supreme commander; adya = now; prabhavati = has authority; bhavatya = over you; mamacha = and me also ; vayam = we; nahi anāthāḥ = are not indeed without master; rājJṇā = by way of king ; lokanāthena = lord of the earth dhīmatā = the wise man''.

"King Dasaratha, supreme commander has authority over you and me also now. when king, the lord of the earth and the wise man is there , we are not indeed master-less.'

Sloka 22

bhavatīm anuvarteta sa hi dharma rataḥ sadā |
yathā mayi tu niṣkrānte putra śokena pārthivaḥ || 2-24-22

22. bharatashchāpi = Bharata also; dharmātmā = who is a righteous person; sarva bhūta priyamvadaḥ = who speaks lovingly to all beings ; anuvarteta = will be obliging ; bhavatīm = to you ; saḥ = ḥe ; sadā = always; dharmarataḥ hi = indeed is devoted to righteousness ."

"Bharata also, who is a righteous person and who speaks lovingly to all beings will be obliging to you. He is indeed always devoted to righteousness.'

Sloka 23

yathā mayi tu niṣkrānte putraśokena pārthivaḥ || 2-24-23
śramam na avāpnuyāt kiṃcit apramattā tathā kuru |

23. mayi nishhkrānte = while I take leave; yathā = how ; pārthivaḥ = the king; na avāpnuyāt = will not get; kinchit = even a little ; shrmam = fatigue; putra shokena = with grief over the son; tathā = in that manner; kuru = act; apramattā = attentively ."

"While I take leave, take care of the king attentively so that he will not get fatigued even a little with grief over his son."

Sloka 24

dāruṇaścāpyayam śoko yathainam na vināśayet || 2-24-24
rājño vṛddhasya satatam hitam cara samāhitā |

24. yathā = how; ayam shokaḥ = this grief; dāruṇaḥ = which is terrible; na vināshayet = will not destroy in that way; evam = him ; chara = act; satatam = always; samāhitā = soothingly; hitam = well being; rājJṇaḥ = of king vṛiddhasya = who is aged'

"Act always soothingly for a well-being of the aged king, seeing that his terrible grief will not destroy him ."

Sloka 25

vrata upavāsa niratā yā nārī parama uttamā || 2-24-25
bhartāram na anuvarteta sā ca pāpa gatir bhavet |

25. �yā nārī = which woman vratopavāsaniratā = who is interested in voes and fastings paramothamā = being the best of the excellent; nānuvarateta = will not attend to bhartāram = husband sā = she; bhavet = will become papagatiḥ = ill-fated.'

�Even if a woman is interested in religious vows and fastings in addition to being the best of the excellent ;if she does not obey her husband she will become ill-fated !''

Sloka 26

bhartuḥ śuśrūṣayā nārī labhate svargamu ttamam || 2-24-26
api yā nirnamaskārā nivṛttā devapūjanāt |

26. yā = which woman; nivṛittā = abstains from ; deva pūjanāt = worship of gods; nirnamaskārā = does not offer salutation such ; nārīapi = woman also; labhate = will attain ; uttamam = highest ; svargam = heaven ; shushrushhayā = by service; bhartuḥ = to husband ''.

�Even if a woman does not offer salutation to celestials and does not worship the gods, she will attain the highest heaven by service to her husband ."

Sloka 27

śuśrūṣam eva kurvīta bhartuḥ priya hite ratā || 2-24-27
eṣa dharmaḥ purā dRṣṭaḥ loke vede śrutaḥ smRtaḥ |

27. �ratā = devoted in priyahite = love and welfare bhartuḥ of husband kurvīta = she has to do sushrūshhameva = service truly eshhaḥ = ṭhis dṛishhṭaḥ = is seen loke = in the world pure = before ; shrutaḥ = was heard vede = in Vedas (ancient texts) smṛitaḥ = was told as dharmaḥ = righteousness.'

�She has to do service, truly devoted in love and welfare of her husband. This is seen in the world from the beginning, was heard from ancient texts and mentioned as righteousness.'

Sloka 28

agnikāryeṣu ca sadā sumanobhiśca devatāḥ || 2-24-28
pūjyāḥ te mat kRte devi brāhmaṇāḥ caiva suvratāḥ |

28. devī = Oh; ṃother ! matkṛite = for my sake; devatāḥ = gods; pūjyāḥ = are worthy of worship; te = to you ; agnikāryeshhu = by sacrificial fire; sumanobhishcha = and by flowers; sadā = always ; brāhmaṇāshchaiva = brahmans also; suvratāḥ = with right voes'.

Oh, ṃother! You always worship gods by sacrificial fire and by flowers for my sake . Brahmanas also with right voes are to be worshipped .'

Sloka 29

evam kālam pratīkṣasva mama āgamana kānkṣiṇī || 2-24-29
prāpsyase paramam kāmam mayi pratyāgate sati |

29. pratīkshhasva = look forward ; kālam = for time ; mama āgamana kāṇkshhiṇī = desiring my return; evam = in such a manner ; viyatā = with restraint; niyatāhāra = being temperate in food ; rate = devoted in ; bhartashushrūshhaṇe = service of husband.

"Look forward for the time of my return in such a manner with restraint, being temperate in food and being devoted in the service of your husband."

Sloka 30

prāpsyase paramam kāmam mayi pratyāgate sati || 2-24-30
yadi dharmabhRtām śreṣṭho dhārayiṣyati jīvitam |

30. mayi = I; pratyāgate sati = having come back; dhrmabhṛitām shreshhṭaḥ = our father; the best of protectors of righteousness; dhārāyishhyati yadi = if he sustains; jīvitam = life; prāpsyase = you will acquire; paramam = the best; kāmam = desire .

"After my return, your best desire will be fulfilled if our father the best among protectors of righteousness ,is still alive till then."

Sloka 31

evam uktā tu rāmeṇa bāṣpa paryākula īkṣaṇā || 2-24-31
kausalyā putra śoka ārtā rāmam vacanam abravīt |

31. kausalyā = kausalya; putra shokārtā = who is afflicted over her son ; uktā = having been spoken ; evam = thus; rāmeṇa = by Rama; bhāshhpaparyākulekshhaṇā = with eyes full of tears; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; rāmam = to Rama.

Kausalya who was afflicted with grief over her son, was with eyes full of tears after hearing Rama's utterances and spoke thus to Rama.

Sloka 32

gamane sukṛtām buddhim na te śaknomi putraka || 2-24-32
vinivartayitum vīra nūnam kālo duratyayaḥ |

32. vīra putraka = Oh; Brave son! na shaknomi = I am not able to; Vinivartayitum = avert ; te buddhim = your mind; sukrutām = which is well made; gamane = in the matter of your going; nūnanam = certainly; kālaḥ = time; duratyayaḥ = is inscrutable."

"Oh, Brave son! I am not able to avert your well-made resolution, in the matter of your going to forest. Time is inscrutable .It is certain."

Sloka 33

gacca putra tvam eka agraḥ bhadram te astu sadā vibho || 2-24-33
punastvayi nivṛtte tu bhaviṣyāmi gataklamā |

33. putraḥ = Oh ;son! tvam = You; gachchha = go; ekāgraḥ = un disturbed; vibho = Oh; the mighty; astu = let there be ; bhadram = auspiciousness; sadā = always; te = to you; bhavishhyāmi = I shall become; gata klamā = refreshed; tvayi nivṛittetu = when you return; punaḥ = again."

"Oh, son! You go undisturbed. Let there be auspiciousness always to you .I shall become refreshed, when you return again."

Sloka 34

pratyāgate mahābhāge kṛtārthe caritavrate || 2-24-34
piturānṛṇyatām prāptetvayi lapsye param sukham |

34. tvayi = You �mahābhāge = the illustrious kṛitārthe = who accomplished a purpose; charitavrate = who observed a vow ; prāpte = who attained anṛiṇyatām = freedom from debt pituḥ = to father ; pratyāgate = having come again lapsye = Ishall get varam = great sukham = happiness.

"You, the illustrious after accomplishing a purpose, having observed a vow and having attained freedom from debt to your father, after having come again, I shall get great happiness".

Sloka 35

kṛtāntasya gatiḥ putra durvibhāvyā sadā bhuvi || 2-24-35
yastvā saṃcodayati me vaca āccidya rāghava |

35. putra = Oh;son rāghava = Oh; Rama! Yaḥ = which destiny Aachchhidya = extingvishes me = my vachaḥ = word sanchodayati = impels tvā = you gatiḥ = the path kṛitāntasya = of such destiny sadā = always durvibhāvyā = is unimaginable bhuvī = on earth.

"The path of the destiny, which extinguishes my words and impels you to go to the forest ,is ever not imaginable on this earth"

Sloka 36

gacchedānīm mahābāho kṣemeṇa punarāgataḥ || 2-24-36
nandayiṣyasi mām putraḥ sāmnā vākyena cāruṇā |

36. mahābāho = Oh Rama; long armed ! gachchha = go; idānīm = just now; Putra = Oh; son! punaḥ āgataḥ = having come again; kshhemeṇa = safely; nandayishhyasi = you will rejoice; mām = me; sāmnā = with unanimity; vākyena = with words; chāruṇā = which are pleasing.

"Oh, Rama the valiant !Go just now and come again safely, when you will rejoice me with your pleasing and tranquillising words ."

Sloka 37

apīdānīm sa kālaḥ ssyādvanātpratyāgatam punaḥ || 2-24-37
yattvām putrakaḥ paśyeyam jaṭāvalkadhāriṇam |

37. putraka = Oh; son! Pratyāgatam = after returning; Punaḥ = again; vanāt = from forest; yat = when; pashyeyāt = shall I see; tvām = you; jatāvalkadhāriṇam = wearing twisted hair and garment made of bark? saḥ kālaḥ = ṭhat time; api syāt = may it be; idānīm = just now '

"How good it will be, if today itself is the day of your returning from the forest, wearing twisted hair and garment made of bark."

Sloka 38

tathā hi rāmam vana vāsa niścitam |
dadarśa devī parameṇa cetasā |
uvāca rāmam śubha lakṣaṇam vaco|
babhūva ca svastyayana abhikānkṣiṇī || 2-24-38

38. devī = ṭhe queen kausalya; dadarsha = saw; tathā = thus; rāma = Rama; vanavāsa mishchitam = who decided to stay in forest; uvācha = spoke; vachaḥ = words; chetasā = with mind; paramiṇa = most exellent; rāmam = to Rama; subhalakshhaṇam = having auspicious qualities; babhūva = become; svatyayanābhikāṇkshhiṇī = desirous of averting evil by recitation of auspicious words; ṭhus completes twenty fourth canto of Ayodhya Kanda.

"The queen kausalya thus saw Rama who decided to go to forest .She spoke these words with most excellent mind to Rama having auspicious qualities, being desirous of averting evil by recitation of auspicious words."

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe caturviśaḥsargaḥ ||

Thus completes twenty fourth chapter of Ayodhya kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
