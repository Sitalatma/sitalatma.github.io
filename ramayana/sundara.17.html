Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 17
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Hanuma sees Seetha surrounded by ogresses.

Sloka 1

tataḥ kumudaṣaṇḍābho nirmalam nirmalodayaḥ |
prajagāma nabhaścandro haṃso nīlamivodakam || 5-17- 1

1. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; chandraḥ = the moon; nirmala udayaḥ = with a peaceful rise; kumuda shhanDābhaḥ = with radiance equaling that of a group of lotuses; prajagāma = obtained;nirmalam nabhaḥ = clear sky; iva = like; hamsaḥ = a swan; nīlam udakam = blue water;

ṭhereafter the moon with a peaceful rise, with radiance equaling that of a group of lotuses, obtained the clear sky like a swan obtaining blue water.

Sloka 2

sācivyamiva kurvan sa prabhayā nirmalaprabhaḥ |
candramā raśmabhiḥ śītaiḥ siṣeve pavanātmajam || 5-17-2

2. saḥ chandramāḥ = that moon; nirmala prabhaḥ = with a peaceful glow; sishheve = served; pavanātmajam = ḥanuma; shītaiḥ rashmibhiḥ = with cool rays; kurvan iva = as though performing; sāchivyam = help; prabhāyā = with radiance.

ṭhat moon with a peaceful glow served ḥanuma with cool rays as though performing help with radiance.

Sloka 3

sa dadarśa tataḥ sītām pūrṇacandranibhānanām |
śokabhārairiva nyastāṃ bhārairnāvamivāmbhasi || 5-17-3

3. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; saḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; sītām = ṣeetha; pūrṇa chandra nibhhananām = with face like a full moon; nyastām = put down; shookabhāraiḥ = by the weight of grief; nāvamiva = like a ship; nyastām = sinking down; ambhasi = in water; bhāraiḥ = due to weight;

ṭhereafter ḥanuma saw ṣeetha with a face like a full moon, put down by the weight of grief, like a ship sinking down in water due to weight.

4;5;6;7;8. mārutātmajaḥ = ḥanuma; didṛikshamāṇaḥ = wanting to see; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; dadarsha = saw; vidūrasthā = nearby; rākshasīḥ = ogresses; ghora darshanāḥ = with horrible appearance; ekākshīm = (ogress) with one eye; ekakarṇām cha = and (another) with a single ear; tathā = and also; karṇaprāvaraṇām = one with ears as covering body; saṇ^ku karṇām = with conch shell like ears; mastakochchvāsa nāsikām = one with fattened nose up to head; tanu dīrgha shirodharām = one with a lean and long neck; dhvastakesīm = one with disheveled hair; tathā = and also; akeshīm = one without hair; keshakambala dhāriṇīm = one with hair like a blanket; lamba karṇa lalātām cha = one with hanging stomach and breasts; lamboshhṭhīm = one with hanging face; chubukoshhṭhīm = one with lips at chin; lambāsyām = one with hanging face; lambajānukām = with hanging knees; hrasvām = one who is short; dīrghām = one who is tall; tathā = and also; kubjām = hunch backed one; vikaṭām = one who is distorted; vāmanām = one is dwarf; tathā = and also; karālām = one with high teeth; bhugna vaktrām cha = and one with crooked mouth; piṇ^gākshīm = one with green eyes; vikṛitānanām = one with a horrible face.

ḥanuma wanting to see ṣeetha saw nearby ogresses with horrible appearance - one with one eye; one with a single ear and also one with ears as covering; one with conch shell like ears; one with fattened nose up to head; one with lean and long neck; one with disheveled hair and also one without hair; one with blanket like hair; one with hanging stomach and breasts; one with hanging face; one with lips at chin; one with hanging face; one with hanging knees; one who is short; one who is tall and also hunch backed one; one who is distorted; a dwarf one and also one with high teeth and one with crooked mouth, one with green eyes and one with a horrible face.

Sloka 18

tasyādhastācca tām devīm rājaputrīmaninditām || 5-17-18
lakṣayāmāsa lakṣmīvān hanumān jankātmajām |

18. hanumān = ḥanuma; lakshmīvān = the glorious one; lakshayāmāsa = saw; tām devīm = that ṣeetha; rājaputrīm = the princess;janakātmajām = daughter of Janaka; aninditām = unblemished one; tasya adhastāt = below that tree

ḥanuma the glorious one saw that ṣeetha, the princess, daughter of Janaka, unblemished one below that tree.

Sloka 19

niṣprabhām ṣokasantaptām malasamkulamūrdhajām || 5-17-19
kṣīṇapuṇyām cyutām bhūmau tārām nipatitāmiva |

19. nishhprabhām = lack lustre one; shokasamtaptām = being tormented by grief; malasamkulamūrdhajām = with hair covered by dirt; kshhiṇa puṇyām = with merits exhausted; tārāmiva = like a star; chyutām = fallen from heavens; bhūmau nipatitām = that fell on ground.

ṣhe was lack luster being tormented by grief, with hair covered by dirt, with merits exhausted, like star fallen from heavens onto ground.

Sloka 20

cāritravyapadeśāḍyāṃ bhartṛdarśanadurgatām || 5-17-20
bhūṣaṇairuttamorhīnām bhartṛvātsalyabhūṣaṇām |

20. chāritravyapadeshādyām = rich with fame of character; bhartṛidarshana durgatām = poor due to not seeing husband; hīnām = lacking; uttamaiḥ bhūshhanaiḥ = excellent ornaments; bhartṛivātsalya bhūshhaṇām = with husband's love as ornament.

ṣhe was rich with fame of lofty character, poor due to not seeing husband, lacking excellent ornaments, with husband's love as ornament.

Sloka 21

rākṣasādhipasamruddhām bandhubhiśca vinā kṛtām || 5-17-21
viyūthām simhasamruddhām baddhām gajavadhūmiva |

21. rākshādhipa saṃruddhām = Being held captive by Ravana; kṛitām = made; bandhubhiḥ vinā cha = without relatives; gajavadhūmiva = like a she-elephant; viyūdhām = without herd; siṃha saṃruddhām baddhām = being detained by a lion; .

Being held captive by Ravana, without any relatives, ṣhe was like a she-elephant without herd, being detained by a lion.

Sloka 22

candrarekhām payodānte śāradābrairivāvṛtām || 5-17-22
kliṣṭarūpāmasaṃsparśādayuktāmiva vallakīm |

22. chandrarekhāmiva = like moon; āvṛitām = being covered; shāradābhraiḥ = by clouds; payodānte = at the end of rainy season; klishhta rupām = With an appearance lacking lustre; asamsparshāt = due to lack of contact (with husband); valākīmiva = like Veena; āyuktām = which was unplucked.

ṣhe was like moon being covered by clouds at the end of rainy season with an appearance lacking lustre due to lack of contact with husband, was like an unplucked Veena.

Sloka 23

sa tām bhartavaśe yuktāmayuktām rākṣasīvaśe || 5-17-23
aśokavanikāmadhye śokasāgaramāplutām |

23. sītām = (ḥanuma saw)ṣeetha; bhartṛivashe yuktām = being suited to be with her husband; rākshasīvashe = in the custody of ogresses; ashokavanikā madhye = in the middle of Ashoka garden;āplutām = being immersed; shoka sāgarām = in an ocean of grief.

ḥanuma saw ṣeetha being suited to be with her husband, in the custody of ogresses, in the middle of Ashoka garden being immersed in an ocean of grief

Sloka 24

tābhiḥ parivṛtām tatra sagrahāmiva rohiṇīm || 5-17-24
dadarśa hanumān devīm latāmakusumāmiva |

24. hanumman = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; devīm = ṣeetha; tatra = there; parivṛitām = surrounded; tābhiḥ = by those ogresses; sagrahāmiva rohiṇīm = like Rohini with planets; lathāmiva = like a creeper; akusumām = without flowers.

ḥanuma saw ṣeetha there surrounded by those ogresses, like Rohini with planets, like a creeper without flowers.

Sloka 25

sā malena ca digdhāṅgīvapuṣā cāpyalaṃkṛtā || 5-17-25
mṛṇālī paṅkadigdheva vibhāti ca na bhāti ca |

25. igdhāṇ^gī = With body smeared; malena = with dirt; vapusshā chāpi alaṃkṛitā = being graced by her body; sā = that ṣeetha; vibhāti = shone; na vibhāti cha = and also not shining;mṛiṇālīva = like a lotus-fibre; paṇ^ka digdhāḥ = covered with dirt.

With body smeared with dirt, being graced by ḥer body, that ṣeetha shone and also did not shine, like a lotus-fibre covered with dirt.

Sloka 26

malinena tu vastreṇa parikliṣṭena bhāminīm || 5-17-26
saṃvṛtām mṛgaśābākṣīṃ dadarśa hanumān kapiḥ |
tām devīṃ dīnavadanāmadīnāṃ bhartṛtejasā || 5-17-27
rakṣitām svena śīlena sītāmasitalocanām |

26; 27. hanumān kapiḥ = the monkey ḥanuma;darasha = saw; tām devīm seethām = that ṣeetha; bhāminīm = with great beauty;asitalochanām = with black eye;samvṛitām = covered; vastreṇa = by a cloth; pariklishhṭena = which was creased; malinena = dirty; mṛiga shābākshīm = with eyes of a deer; dīnavadanām = with a pitiful face; adīnām = not depressed; bhartṛi tejasā = due to the brilliance of husband; rakshitām = protected; svena shīlena = by her own character.

ṭhe monkey ḥanuma saw that ṣeetha with great beauty with black eyes, covered by a cloth which was creased and dirty, with eyes of a deer, with a pitiful face, not depressed due to the brilliance of her husband, protected by her own character.

Sloka 28

tām dṛṣṭvā hanumān sītām mṛgaśābanibhekṣaṇām |
mṛgakanyāmiva trastām vīkṣamāṇām samantataḥ || 5-17-28
dahantīmiva niḥśvāsairvṛkṣān pallavadhāriṇaḥ |
samghātamiva śokānām duḥkhasyormimivothitām || 5-17-29

28; 29. hanumān = ḥanuma; sītām dṛishhṭvā = on seeing ṣeetha (became very happy); mṛiga shāba nibhekshaṇām = with eyes of a fawn; trastām mṛiga kanyāmiva = fearful like a she-deer; vīkshamaṇām = seeing; vṛikshān = trees; pallava dhāriṇaḥ = with young shoots; dahantīmiva = as though being burnt; niḥshvāsaiḥ = by sighs; shokānām samghātamiva = like a bundle of grief; ūrmimiva = like a wave; duḥkhasya = of sorrow; uthitām = risen up.

ḥanuma became happy on seeing ṣeetha with eyes of a fawn, fearful like a she-deer, seeing trees with young shoots, as though being burnt by sighs, like a bundle of grief, with a wave of sorrow risen up.

Sloka 30

tām kṣamāṃ suvibhaktāṅgīṃ vinābharaṇaśobhinīm |
praharṣamatulam lebhe mārutiḥ prekṣya maithilīm || 5-17-30

30. mārutiḥ = ṭhe son of wind-god ḥanuma;prekshya = on seeing; tām maithilīm = that ṣeetha; kshamām = like the goddess Earth; suvibhaktāṇ^gīm = with well divided body parts; vinābharaṇa shobhinīm = shining even without ornaments; lebhe = obtained; atulam praharshām = great joy.

ḥanuma the son of wind god on seeing ṣeetha that ṣeetha like the goddess earth, with well divided body parts shining eve without ornaments, obtained great joy.

Sloka 31

harṣajāni ca so.aśrūṇi tām dṛṣṭvā madirekṣaṇām |
mumuce hanumāṃstatra namaścakre ca rāghavam || 5-17-31

31. hanumān = ḥanuma; dṛishṭvā = on seeing; tām = her;tatra = there; madirekshaṇām = with intoxicating eyes ; mumuche = shed; harshajāni ashrūṇi = tears of joy;namashchakre cha = and also paid obeisance; rāghavam = to ṣri Rama.

ḥanuma on seeing ṣeetha there with intoxicating eyes shed tears of joy and also paid obeisance to Sri Rama.

Sloka 32

namaskṛtvā rāmāya lakṣmaṇāya ca vīryavān |
sītādarśanasamhṛṣṭo hanumān samvṛto.abhavat || 5-17-32

32. sīta darshana samḥṛisṭā = Being joyful on seeing ṣeetha; hanumān = ḥanuma; vīryavān = the mighty one; namaskṛitvā = paid obeisance; rāmāya = to ṣri Rama; lakshmaṇāya cha = and to Lakshmana;samvṛitaḥ abhavat = became covered (with leaves)

Being joyful on seeing ṣeetha, ḥanuma the mighty one paid obeisance to ṣri Rama and to Lakshmana and became covered with leaves.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe saptadaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 17th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
