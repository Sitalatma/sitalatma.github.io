Introduction

Rama, travelling in the aerial car towards Ayodhya, lands at the hermitage of Bharadwaja the sage. When Rama enquires of Bhardwaja about the welfare of Ayodhya-City, Bharata and his mothers. Bharadwaja informs Rama about their welfare. Further, the sage informs that by virtue of his asceticism, he could know everything about the exploits of Rama from the day he left Ayodhya, till he destroyed Ravana. Then, Rama seeks a boon from Bharadwaja, asking him to make the trees on the route to Ayodhya, to bear fruit in the off-season for which the sage grants the boon. Thereupon, the troops of monkeys feasted on those fruits at will.

Sloka 1

pūrṇe caturdaśe varṣe pañcamyāṃ lakṣmaṇāgrajaḥ |
bharadvājāśramaṃ prāpya vavande niyato munim || 6-124-1

1. chatrudashe varṣhe pūrṇe = after completion of fourteen years (of his exile); paṇ^chamyām = and on the fifth day (the bright half) of the lunar month of Ashvayuja (roughly corresponding to the month of september); lakṣhmaṇāgrajaḥ = Rama; niyataḥ = who was disciplined even now; rāpya = having reached; bharadvājāshramam = the hermitage of the sage; Bharadwaja; vavande = offered his salutation; (to the sage).

After completion of fourteen years of his exile and on the fifth day of the bright half of the lunar month of Ashvayuja (roughly corresponding to the month of September), Rama, who was disciplined even now, reaching the hermitage of the sage Bharadwaja, offered his salutation to the sage.

Sloka 2

so.apṛcchadabhivādyainaṃ bharadvājaṃ tapodhanam |
śṛṇoṣi kaccidbhagavan subhikṣānāmayaṃ pure || 6-124-2
kaccitsa yukto bharato jīvantyapi ca mātaraḥ |

2. abhivādya = having offered his obeisance; bharadvājam = to the sage; Bharadwaja; enam tapodhanam = who was rich in this asceticism; saḥ = Rama; apṛichchhat = enquired; bhagaran = O venerable one!; shṛiṇoṣhi kachchit = have you heard; pure = of Ayodhya city; subhikṣhānāmayam = having an abundance of food and free from disease?; saḥ bharataḥ kachchit = is that Bharata; yuktaḥ = attentive (in his rule)?; mātaraḥ mama jīvanti = I hope my mothers are (still) alive.

Having offered his obeisance to Bharadwaja, who was rich in asceticism, Rama enquired (of him): "Have you heard, O venerable one, of Ayodhya City having an abundance of food and free from disease? Is that Bharata attentive in his rule? I hope my mothers are still alive."

Sloka 3

evamuktastu rāmeṇa bharadvājo mahāmuniḥ || 6-124-3
pratyuvāca raghuśreṣṭhaṃ smitapūrvaṃ prahṛṣṭavat |

3. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmeṇa = by Rama; bharadvājaḥ = Bharadwaja; mahāmuniḥ = the great sage; prahṛiṣhṭavat = quite rejoiced as he was; pratyuvācha = replied; smitapūrvam = with a smile; raghushreṣhṭham = to Rama (as follows):

Hearing the words of Rama, Bharadwaja the great sage, quite rejoiced as he was, smilingly replied to Rama (as follows):

Sloka 4

paṅkadigdhastu bharato jaṭilastvāṃ pratīkṣate || 6-124-4
pāduke te puraskṛtya sarvaṃ ca kuśalaṃ gṛhe |

4. bharataḥ = Bharata; paṇ^kadigdhaḥ tu = mud-smeared as he is (throwing the burden and blame ie. mud on himself); jaṭilaḥ = with his hari twisted together; puraskṛitya = and keeping in front; te pāduke = your wooden sandals; pratīkṣhate = is looking forward; tvām = for you; sarvam cha = all are; kshalam = safe; gṛihe = at your home and in the city.

"Bharata, with his mud-smeared limbs 9throwing the burden and blame ie. mud and with matted locks, as also placing your wooden-sandals before him, is looking forward for your arrival. All are safe at your home and in the city."

Sloka 5

tvāṃ purā cīravasanaṃ praviśantaṃ mahāvanam || 6-124-5
strītṛtīyaṃ cyutaṃ rājyāddharmakāmaṃ ca kevalam |
padātiṃ tyaktasarvasvaṃ pitṛnirdeśakāriṇam || 6-124-6
sarvabhogaiḥ parityaktaṃ svargacyutamvāmaram |
dṛṣṭvā tu karuṇā pūrvaṃ mamāsītsamitiṃjaya || 6-124-7
kaikeyīvacane yuktaṃ vanyamūlaphalāśinam |

5-7. samitimjaya = O Rama; the victorious in battles!; purā = formerly; dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; tvām = you; pravishantam = penetrating deeply; mahāvanam = into the great forest; padātim = on foot; kevalam dharmakāmam cha = with the sole intention of practicing virtue; chīravasanam = clad in pieces of bark; chyutam = going away; rājyāt = from the sovereignty; sarvabhogaiḥ paritaktam = deprived of all enjoyments; amaram iva = like an immortal; svarga chyutam = fallen from heaven; tyakta sarvasvam = having renounced all your possession; strītṛitīyam = with your spouse (ṣeetha) as the third (of your party besides Lakshmana and yourself); pitṛinirdeshakāriṇam = resolved in carrying out the behest of your father; kaikeyī vachane yuktam = and intent on implementing the pledge given (by your father) to Kaikeyi; vanyamūla phalāshinam = and living; on wild roots and fruits; mama āshīt karuṇā = pity arose in me; pūrvam = earlier.

"O Rama, the victorious in battles! Formerly, on seeing you penetrating deeply into the great forest on foot, with the sole intention of practicing virtue, clad in pieces of bark, going away from sovereignty, deprived of all enjoyments, like an immortal fallen from heaven, having renounced all your possession, with your spouse (Seetha) as the third (of your party besides Lakshmana and yourself), resolved in carrying out the behest of your father and intent on implementing the pledge given (by your father) to Kaikeyi and living on wild roots and fruits, pity arose in me earlier."

Sloka 8

sāṃpratam tu samṛddhārthaṃ samitragaṇabāndhavam || 6-124-8
samīkṣya vijitāriṃ ca mamābhūtprītiruttamā |

8. sampratamtu = but now; uttamā prītiḥ = a great delight; abhūt = occurred; mama = to me; samīkṣhya = on seeing; samṛiddhārtham = you; having fulfilled your purpose; vijitārim = that you have conquered your enemies; samitragaṇa bāndhavam = and are accompanied by your host of friends and relatives.

"But now, a great delight occurred to me, on seeing you, having fulfilled your purpose, that you have conquered your enemies and are accompanied by your host of friends and relatives."

Sloka 9

sarvaṃ ca sukhakuḥkhaṃ te viditaṃ mamam rāghava |
yattvayā vipulaṃ prāptaṃ janasthānanivāsinā || 6-124-9

9. sarvam viditam = it is completely known; mama = to me; te = of you; janasthāna nivāsinā = having resided in Janasthana forest; prāptam = and having to; yat vipulam = that abundant; sukha duḥkham = joy and sorrow; tvayā = by you; rāghava = o Rama!

"That joy and sorrow experienced by you in abundance while residing in Janasthana-forest in completely known to me, O Rama!"

Sloka 10

brāhmaṇārthe niyuktasya rakṣataḥ sarvatāpasān |
rāvaṇena hṛtā bhāryā babhūveyamaninditā || 6-124-10

10. rakṣhataḥ = (While) you were protecting; sarvatāpasān = all the ascetics; niyuktasya = who prayed; brāhmaṇārthe = for safe guarding the interest of Brahmanas; aninditā iyam = this irreproachible wife of yours; babhūva hṛitā = was taken away; rāvaṇena = by Ravana.

"While you were busy in protecting all the ascetics who prayed you for safeguarding the interests of Brahmanas, this irreproachable wife of yours was taken away by Ravana."

11-16. dharmavatsala = O Rama; devoted to virtue!; mārīcha darshanamchaiva = even the apearance of ṃareecha; sītonmattanevacha = the abduction of ṣeetha (by Ravana); kabandhadarshanam chaiva = the sight of Kabandha; tathā = and pampābhigamanam = your arrival at the Pampa lake; te sakhyam = your alliance; sugrīveṇa = with ṣugreeva; yathā = and how; vāli = Vali; hataḥ = was killed; tvayā = by you; mārgaṇam = the search; vaidehyām = for ṣeetha; yathā = how; nala setuḥ = the bridge; ṇalasetu; kṛitaḥ = was constructed (over the sea); yathā = how; laṇ^kā = the city of Lanka; ādīpitā = was set fire; prahṛiṣhṭaiḥ hariyūthapaiḥ = by the rejoiced monkey-chiefs; yathā = how; tasmin rāvaṇe = that Ravana; devakaṇṭake = who was a thorn in the side of god; bala darpitaḥ = and who was arrogant of his might; nihataḥ = was killed; samkhye = in battle; saputra bāndhavāmātyaḥ = with his sons; kinsfolk and ministers; sabalaḥ = as well as his army; saharāhanaḥ = together with their mounts; yathā = how samāgamaḥ = yur meeting; tridashaiḥ = with the gods (occured); yathā = how; varaḥ cha = a boon; dattashcha = was conferred; te = on you; sarvam etat = all this; viditam = is known; mayā = to me; tapasā = by virtue of my asceticism; me shiṣhāḥ = my disciples; pravṛittākhyāḥ = who were accustomed to report to me the news; sampatnti = used to go; purīm = to Ayodhya City; itaḥ = from here.

"O Rama, devoted to virtue! Even the appearance of ṃareecha, the abduction of Seetha by Ravana, the sight of Kabandha, your arrival at the Pampa lake, your alliance with Sugreeva, how Vali was killed by you, the search-operation for Seetha, the exploit of Hanuma, the tracing of Seetha, how the bridge, Nalasetu was constructed (over the sea), how the City of Lanka was set fire by the rejoiced monkey-chiefs, how that Ravana who was the thorn in the side of gods and how was arrogant of his might, was killed in battle, with his sons kinsfolk and his ministers as the gods happened, how a boon was conferred by them on you- all this is known to me by virtue of my asceticism. ṃy disciples, who were accustomed to report to me the news, used to go to Ayodhya-City from here."

Sloka 17

mitrāṇi dhana dhānyāni prajānāṃ sammatāniva |
jananī nanma bhūmiśca svargādapi garīyasī || 6-124-17

17. mitrāṇi = the friends; dhana dhānyāni = the riches and the grains; sammatāni = are highly honoured; iha = in this world; jananī = mother; janmabhūmishcha = and mother-land; garīyasī = and far superior; svargādapi = to even the heaven.

"The friends, the riches and the grains are highly honoured in this world. ṃother and mother-land are far superior to even the heaven."

Sloka 18

tasya tacchirasā vākyaṃ pratigṛhya nṛpātmajaḥ |
bāḍhamityeva saṃhṛṣṭaḥ śrīmānvaramayācata || 6-124-18

18. shirasā tasya tat vākyam pratigṛiya = bowing to the aforesaid statement of the sage with his head bent low; samhṛiṣhṭaḥ = highly pleased as he was; bāDham ityeva = and saying by all means"; shrīmān = the glorious; nṛipātmajaḥ = prince (Rama); āyāchat = asked to him; varam = the following boon:

Bowing to the aforesaid statement of the sage with his head bent low, highly pleased as he was and saying, "By all means" the glorious prince (Rama) asked of him the following boon:

Sloka 19

akālaphalino vṛkṣāḥ sarve cāpi madhusravāḥ |
phalānyamṛtagandhīni bahūni vividhāni ca || 6-124-19
bhavantu mārge bhavannayodhyāṃ prati gacchataḥ |

19. nhagavan = O the venerable sir!; sarvechāpi vṛikṣhāḥ = let all the trees; mārge = on the way; gachchhataḥ = even as I fly; ayodhyām prati = ṭo Ayodhya; akālaphalinaḥ = bear fruit even in the off-season; madhusravāḥ = and flow with honey; bahūni vividhāni cha phalāni = let abundant fruits of various kinds; amṛitagandhīni = and emitting the fragrance of nectar; bhavantu = appear on them.

"O venerable sir! Let all the trees on the way, even as I fly to Ayodhya, bear fruit even in the off-season and flow with honey. Let abundant fruits of various kinds, emitting the fragrance of nectar, appear on them."

Sloka 20

tatheti ca pratijñāte vacanātsamanantaram || 6-124-20
abhavanpādapāstatra svargapādapasaṃnibhāḥ |

20. tatheti pratiJṇāte samanantaram vachanam = once the consent was given in the words "Be it so"; pādapāḥ = the trees; tatra = there; abhavan = grew; svarga pādapa samnibhāḥ = to be closely like the heavenly trees.

Once the consent of the sage was given in the words "be it so", the trees there grew to be closely like the heavenly trees.

Sloka 21

niṣphalāḥ phalinaścāsanvipuṣpāḥ puṣpaśālinaḥ || 6-124-21
śuṣkāḥ samagrapatrāste nagāścaiva madhusravāḥ |
sarvato yojanāstrisro gacchatāmabhavaṃstadā || 6-124-22

21-22. tadā = then; tistraḥ yojanāḥ = three Yojanas (twenty four miles); gachchhatām = of the direction of their travel; nagāḥ = the trees; niṣhphalāḥ = that no longer bore fruit; phalinashcha = were laden with fruit; vipuṣhpāḥ = and those which had ceased to blossom; puṣhpashālinaḥ = looked charming with blossom; shuṣhkāḥ = the withered trees; samagra patrāshcha = were fully clothed with foliage; abhavan madhusravāḥ = and further began to flow with honey.

Then, for an extent of three Yojanas (twenty four miles) on all sides, in the direction of their travel, the trees that no longer bore fruit, were laden with fruit and those which had ceased to blossom looked charming with blossom. The withered trees were fully clothed with foliage and further began to flow with honey.

Sloka 23

tataḥ prahṛṣṭāḥ plavagarṣabhāste |
bahūni divyāni phalāni caiva |
kāmādupāśnanti sahasraśaste |
mudānvitāḥ svargajito mudeva || 6-124-23

23. te vānararṣhabhāḥ = those monkey-chiefs; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = were highly pleased; anvitāḥ = and endowed; mudena = with delight; svargajitaḥ = as those who had conquered the heaven; tataḥ = thereupon upāshnanti consumed; kāmāt = at will; sahasrashaḥ = thousands; bahūni divyāni phalāni = of many of those wonderful fruits.

Highly rejoiced who had conquered the heaven, those monkey-chiefs thereupon consumed at will, thousands of many of those wonderful fruits.
