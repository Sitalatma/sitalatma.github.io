Translation

Purport

The yoga - โยค - โยคะ process explained by Lord Kapiladeva in this chapter is authorized and standard, and therefore these instructions should be followed very carefully. To begin, the Lord says that by yoga - โยค - โยคะ practice one can make progress towards understanding the Absolute Truth, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. In the previous chapter it has been clearly stated that the desired result of yoga - โยค - โยคะ is not to achieve some wonderful mystic power. One should not be at all attracted by such mystic power, but should attain progressive realization on the path of understanding the Supreme Personality of Godhead. This is also confirmed in Bhagavad-gītā, - ภควทฺ-คีตา, - ภะคะวัท-คีตา, which states in the last verse of the Sixth Chapter that the greatest yogī - โยคี - โยคี is he who constantly thinks of Kṛṣṇa within himself, or he who is Kṛṣṇa conscious.

It is stated here that by following the system of yoga - โยค - โยคะ one can become joyful. Lord Kapila, the Personality of Godhead, who is the highest authority on yoga - โยค - โยคะ, - โยค, - โยคะ, here explains the yoga - โยค - โยคะ system known as aṣṭāṅga-yoga - โยค - โยคะ, - อษฺฏางฺค-โยค, - อัษฏางคะ-โยคะ, which comprises eight different practices, namely yama, niyama, āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra, dhāraṇā, dhyāna - ยม, นิยม, อาสน, ปฺราณายาม, ปฺรตฺยาหาร, ธารณา, ธฺยาน - ยะมะ, นิยะมะ, อาสะนะ, ปราณายามะ, ปรัตยาหาระ, ธาระณา, ธยานะ and samādhi. - สมาธิฯ - สะมาธิฯ By all these stages of practice one must realize Lord Viṣṇu, who is the target of all yoga - โยค - โยคะ. - โยคฯ - โยคะฯ There are so-called yoga - โยค - โยคะ practices in which one concentrates the mind on voidness or on the impersonal, but this is not approved by the authorized yoga - โยค - โยคะ system as explained by Kapiladeva. Even Patañjali explains that the target of all yoga is Viṣṇu. Aṣṭāṅga-yoga - อษฺฏางฺค-โยค - อัษฏางคะ-โยคะ is therefore part of Vaiṣṇava practice because its ultimate goal is realization of Viṣṇu. The achievement of success in yoga is not acquisition of mystic power, which is condemned in the previous chapter, but, rather, freedom from all material designations and situation in one’s constitutional position. That is the ultimate achievement in yoga practice.

Translation

The blessed Lord Śiva becomes all the more blessed by bearing on his head the holy waters of the Ganges, which has its source in the water that washed the Lord’s lotus feet. The Lord’s feet act like thunderbolts hurled to shatter the mountain of sin stored in the mind of the meditating devotee. One should therefore meditate on the lotus feet of the Lord for a long time.

Purport

In this verse the position of Lord Śiva is specifically mentioned. The impersonalist suggests that the Absolute Truth has no form and that one can therefore equally imagine the form of Viṣṇu or Lord Śiva or the goddess Durgā or their son Gaṇeśa. But actually the Supreme Personality of Godhead is the supreme master of everyone. In the Caitanya-caritāmṛta - ไจตนฺย-จริตามฺฤต - ไจตันยะ-จะริตามฤตะ (Ādi - อาทิ - อาทิ 5.142) it is said, ekale īśvara kṛṣṇa, ara saba bhṛtya: - เอกเล อีศฺวร กฺฤษฺณ, อร สพ ภฺฤตฺย: - เอกะเล อีศวะระ กฤษณะ, อะระ สะพะ ภฤตยะ: the Supreme Lord is Kṛṣṇa, and everyone else, including Lord Śiva and Lord Brahmā — not to mention other demigods — is a servant of Kṛṣṇa. The same principle is described here. Lord Śiva is important because he is holding on his head the holy Ganges water, which has its origin in the foot-wash of Lord Viṣṇu. In the Hari-bhakti-vilāsa, - หริ-ภกฺติ-วิลาส, - หะริ-ภักติ-วิลาสะ, by Sanātana Gosvāmī, it is said that anyone who puts the Supreme Lord and the demigods, including Lord Śiva and Lord Brahmā, on the same level, at once becomes a pāṣaṇḍī, - ปาษณฺฑี, - ปาษัณฑี, or atheist. We should never consider that the Supreme Lord Viṣṇu and the demigods are on an equal footing.

Another significant point of this verse is that the mind of the conditioned soul, on account of its association with the material energy from time immemorial, contains heaps of dirt in the form of desires to lord it over material nature. This dirt is like a mountain, but a mountain can be shattered when hit by a thunderbolt. Meditating on the lotus feet of the Lord acts like a thunderbolt on the mountain of dirt in the mind of the yogī - โยคี - โยคี. - โยคีฯ - โยคีฯ If a yogī wants to shatter the mountain of dirt in his mind, he should concentrate on the lotus feet of the Lord and not imagine something void or impersonal. Because the dirt has accumulated like a solid mountain, one must meditate on the lotus feet of the Lord for quite a long time. For one who is accustomed to thinking of the lotus feet of the Lord constantly, however, it is a different matter. The devotees are so fixed on the lotus feet of the Lord that they do not think of anything else. Those who practice the yoga - โยค - โยคะ system must meditate on the lotus feet of the Lord for a long time after following the regulative principles and thereby controlling the senses.

It is specifically mentioned here, bhagavataś caraṇāravindam: - ภควตศฺ จรณารวินฺทมฺ: - ภะคะวะตัศ จะระณาระวินทัม: one has to think of the lotus feet of the Lord. The Māyāvādīs imagine that one can think of the lotus feet of Lord Śiva or Lord Brahmā or the goddess Durgā to achieve liberation, but this is not so. Bhagavataḥ - ภควตห์ - ภะคะวะตะห์ is specifically mentioned. Bhagavataḥ - ภควตห์ - ภะคะวะตะห์ means “of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Viṣṇu,” and no one else. Another significant phrase in this verse is śivaḥ śivo ’bhūt. - ศิวห์ ศิโว ’ภูตฺฯ - ศิวะห์ ศิโว ’ภูตฯ By his constitutional position, Lord Śiva is always great and auspicious, but since he has accepted on his head the Ganges water, which emanated from the lotus feet of the Lord, he has become even more auspicious and important. The stress is on the lotus feet of the Lord. A relationship with the lotus feet of the Lord can even enhance the importance of Lord Śiva, what to speak of other, ordinary living entities.

Purport

Synonyms

tasmāt — thus; imām — this; svām — own; prakṛtim — material energy; daivīm — divine; sat-asat-ātmikām — consisting of cause and effect; durvibhāvyām — difficult to understand; parābhāvya — after conquering; sva-rūpeṇa — in the self-realized position; avatiṣṭhate — he remains.

ตสฺมาตฺ - ตัสมาต — thus; อิมามฺ - อิมาม — this; สฺวามฺ - สวาม — own; ปฺรกฺฤติมฺ - ปรักฤติม — material energy; ไทวีมฺ - ไทวีม — divine; สตฺ - สัต-อสตฺ - อะสัต-อาตฺมิกามฺ - อาตมิกาม — consisting of cause and effect; ทุรฺวิภาวฺยามฺ - ทุรวิภาวยาม — difficult to understand; ปราภาวฺย - ปะราภาวยะ — after conquering; สฺว - สวะ-รูเปณ - รูเปณะ — in the self-realized position; อวติษฺฐเต - อะวะติษฐะเต — he remains.

Translation

Thus the yogī can be in the self-realized position after conquering the insurmountable spell of māyā, who presents herself as both the cause and effect of this material manifestation and is therefore very difficult to understand.

Purport

It is stated in Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา that the spell of māyā, - มายา, - มายา, which covers the knowledge of the living entity, is insurmountable. However, one who surrenders unto Kṛṣṇa, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, can conquer this seemingly insurmountable spell of māyā. - มายาฯ - มายาฯ Here also it is stated that the daivī prakṛti, - ไทวี ปฺรกฺฤติ, - ไทวี ปรักฤติ, or the external energy of the Supreme Lord, is durvibhāvyā, - ทุรฺวิภาวฺยา, - ทุรวิภาวยา, very difficult to understand and very difficult to conquer. One must, however, conquer this insurmountable spell of māyā, - มายา, - มายา, and this is possible, by the grace of the Lord, when God reveals Himself to the surrendered soul. It is also stated here, svarūpeṇāvatiṣṭhate. - สฺวรูเปณาวติษฺฐเตฯ - สวะรูเปณาวะติษฐะเตฯ Svarūpa - สฺวรูป - สวะรูปะ means that one has to know that he is not the Supreme Soul, but rather, part and parcel of the Supreme Soul; that is self-realization. To think falsely that one is the Supreme Soul and that one is all-pervading is not svarūpa. - สฺวรูปฯ - สวะรูปะฯ This is not realization of his actual position. The real position is that one is part and parcel. It is recommended here that one remain in that position of actual self-realization. In Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา this understanding is defined as Brahman realization.

After Brahman realization, one can engage in the activities of Brahman. As long as one is not self-realized, he engages in activities based on false identification with the body. When one is situated in his real self, then the activities of Brahman realization begin. The Māyāvādī philosophers say that after Brahman realization, all activities stop, but that is not actually so. If the soul is so active in its abnormal condition, existing under the covering of matter, how can one deny its activity when free? An example may be cited here. If a man in a diseased condition is very active, how can one imagine that when he is free from the disease he will be inactive? Naturally the conclusion is that when one is free from all disease his activities are pure. It may be said that the activities of Brahman realization are different from those of conditional life, but that does not stop activity. This is indicated in Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา (18.54): after one realizes oneself to be Brahman, devotional service begins. Mad-bhaktiṁ labhate parām: - มทฺ-ภกฺตึ ลภเต ปรามฺ: - มัท-ภักติง ละภะเต ปะราม: after Brahman realization, one can engage in the devotional service of the Lord. Therefore devotional service of the Lord is activity in Brahman realization.

For those who engage in devotional service there is no spell of māyā, - มายา, - มายา, and their situation is all-perfect. The duty of the living entity, as a part and parcel of the whole, is to render devotional service to the whole. That is the ultimate perfection of life.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Third Canto, Twenty-eighth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled “Lord Kapila’s Instructions on the Execution of Devotional Service.”
