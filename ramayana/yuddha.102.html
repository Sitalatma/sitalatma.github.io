Introduction

The battle between Rama and Ravana starts. Indra sends a chariot, an armour, some arrows and a powerful spear. A tumultuous and thrilling battle ensues between Rama and Ravana. A spear hurled by Ravana is thwarted by a powerful spear (sent by Indra) of Rama. Then, Rama strikes Ravana's horses with arrows. He also pierces the chest region and the forehead of Ravana with his fierce arrows. Ravana gets severely hurt.

Sloka 1

lakṣmaṇena tu tadvākyamuktan śrutvā sa rāghavaḥ |
saṃdadhe paravīraghno dhanurādāya vīryavān || 6-102-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; tat vākyam = those words; uktam = spoken; lakṣhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; rāghavaḥ = Rama; para vīraghnaḥ = the annihilator of his enemies; ādāya = taking hold; dhanuḥ = his bow; samdadhe = fixed the arrow on the bow-string.

Hearing those words of Lakshmana, that valiant Rama, the annihilator of his enemies, taking hold of his bow, fixed an arrow on the bow-string.

Sloka 2

rāvaṇāya śarānghorānvisasarja camūmukhe |
athānyaṃ rathamāsthāya rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ || 6-102-2
abhyadhāvā kākutsthaṃ svarbhānuriva bhāskaram |

2. chamūmukhe = in the forefront of battle; visasarja = (Rama) released; ghorān = terrific; sharān = arrows; rāvaṇāya = towards Ravana; atha = then; āsthāya = occupying; anyam = another; ratham = chariot; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣhasādhipa = the king of demons; abhyyadhāvata = rushed; kākutthsam = towards Rama; svarbhānuḥ iva = as Rahu the seizer-demon(rushes towards); bhāskaram = the sun.

In the forefront of battle, Rama released terrific arrows towards Ravana. Then, occupying another chariot, Ravana the king of demons rushed towards Rama, even as Rahu the seizer-demon rushes towards the sun.

Sloka 3

daśagrīvo rathasthastu rāman vajropamaiḥ śaraiḥ || 6-102-3
ājaghāna mahāghorairdhārābhiriva toyadaḥ |

3. dashagrīvaḥ tu = Ravana for his part; rathasthaḥ = standing in his chariot;ājaghāna = struck; rāmam = Rama; vajropamaiḥ sharaiḥ = with arrows equal to thunderbolts; toyadaḥ iva = as a cloud; (strikes); dharābhiḥ = gushes of rain; mahashailam = on a huge mountain.

Ravana for his part, standing in his chariot struck Rama with thunderbolt-like arrows, as a cloud strikes gushes of rain on a huge mountain.

Sloka 4

dīptapāvakasaṅkāśaiḥ śaraiḥ kāñcanabhūṣaṇaiḥ || 6-102-4
nirbibheda raṇe rāmo daśagrīvan samāhitaḥ |

4. samāhitaḥ = with an attentive mind; rāmaḥ = Rama; abhyavarṣhat = showered; kāṇchana bhūṣhaṇaiḥ sharaiḥ = golden decked arrows; dīpta pāvaka samkāshaiḥ = looking like blazing flames of fire; dashagrīvam = on Ravana; raṇe = in the combat.

With an attentive mind, Rama showered golden decked arrows, looking like blazing flames of fire, on Ravana in the battle-field.

Sloka 5

bhūmisthitasya rāmasya rathasthasya ca rakṣasaḥ || 6-102-5
na saman yuddhamityāhurdevagandharvadānavāḥ |

5. devagandharva kimnarāḥ = the gods; celestial musicians and kinnaras the mythical beings; āhuḥ = uttered; iti = that yuddham = the battle; na samam = was not equal; rāmasya = between Rama; sthitasya = standing; bhūmau = on the ground; rākṣhasaḥ = and Ravana; rathasthasya = occupying a chariot.

The gods, celestial musicians and kinnaras the mythical beings thought that the battle was not equal between Rama standing on the ground and Ravana fighting from a chariot.

Sloka 6

tato devavaraḥ śrīmān śrutvā teṣāṃ vaco.amṛtam || 6-102-6
āhūya mātaliṃ śakro vacanaṃ cedamabravīt |

6.shrutvā = hearing; teṣhām = their; vachomṛitam = nectar-like words; shrīmān = the illustrius; shakraḥ = Indra; deva varaḥ = chief of clestials; tataḥ = then; āhūya = calling; mātalim = ṃatali; bravīt = spoke; idam vachanamcha = te following words.

Hearing their nectar-like words, the illustrious Indra, the chief f celestials then called ṃatali and spoke as follows:

Sloka 7

rathena mama bhūmiṣṭhaṃ śīghraṃ yāhi raghūttamam || 6-102-7
āyūya bhūtalaṃ yātaḥ kuru devahitaṃ mahat |

7. yāhi = go; shīghram = quickly; mama rathena = with my chariot; raghottamam = to Rama; bhūmiṣhṭham = who is standing on the earth; yātaḥ = after reaching; bhūtalam = the earth; āhūya = calling (inviting Rama t take his seat in the chariot); kuru = and carry out; mahat = a great; devahitam = service to the gods.

"Go quickly with my chariot to Rama, who is standing on the earth. After reaching the earth, invite him to take his seat in the chariot and carry out a great service to the gods."

Sloka 8

ityukto devarājena mātalirdvasārathiḥ || 6-102-8
praṇamya śirasā devaṃ tato vacanamabravīt |

8. iti uktaḥ = thus spoken; devarājena = by Indra; mātaliḥ = ṃatali; deva sārathiḥ = the charioteer of Indra; praṇamya = offering salutation; shirasā = by bowing his head; devam = to his lord; abravīt = spoke; tataḥ = then; vachanam = the following words:

Hearing the words of Indra, ṃatali, the charioteer of Indra, offering salutation to him by bowing his head, spoke then the following words:

Sloka 9

śīghraṃ yāsyāmi devendra sārathyaṃ ca karomyaham || 6-102-9
tato hayaiśca saṃyojya haritaiḥ syandanottamam |

9. devendra = O Indra!; samyojya = providing with; haritaiḥ hayaiḥ = green horses; syandanottamam = the excellent chariot; aham = I; yāsyāmi = will proceed; tataḥ = then; shīghram = immediately; karomi = I will perform; sārathyam cha = the duty of a charioteer (to Rama).

"O Indra! Providing with green horses the excellent chariot, I will proceed then immediately and perform the duty of a charioteer to Rama."

Then came the glorious and excellent chariot of Indra, having variegated body decked in gold, which was fitted with hundreds of tiny bells, with its pole of cat's eye gems shone like the morning sun, yoked to excellent green horses, having golden chaplets on their heads, having white whisks, covered with nets of gold, shining like the sun and bearing a flag-staff raised on a golden bamboo. Ascending the chariot, as enjoined by Indra and descending from paradise, ṃatali approached Rama.

Sloka 14

abravīcca tadā rāman sapratodo rathe sthitaḥ |
prāñjalirmātalirvākyan sahasrākṣasya sārathiḥ || 6-102-14

14. tadā = then; mātaliḥ = ṃatali; sahasrākṣhasya sārathiḥ = the charioteer of Indra; sapratodaḥ = along with a whip in his hand; sthitaḥ = stayed; rathe = in the chariot itself; prāṇ^jaliḥ = and joining his palms in salutation; abravīt cha = spoke; vākyam = the following words; rāmam = to Rama.

Then ṃatali, the charioteer of Indra, along with a whip in his hand, stayed in the chariot itself and joining his palms in salutation, spoke the following words to Rama.

Sloka 15

sahasrākṣeṇa kākutstha rathoayan vijayāya te |
dattastava mahāsattva śrīmāñśatrunibarhaṇaḥ || 6-102-15

15. ayam = this; rathaḥ = chariot; dattaḥ = is given; sahasrākṣhaḥ = by Indra; tava = to you; te vijayāya = for your victory; mahāsattva = O the mighty; shrīmān = the glorious; kākutthsa = Rama; shatrunibarhaṇa = the destroyer of enemies!

"Indra has sent this chariot to you for your victory, O the mighty and the glorious Rama, the destroyer of adversaries!"

Sloka 16

idamaindraṃ mahaccāpan kavacaṃ cāgnisaṃnibham |
śarāścādityasaṅkāśāḥ śaktiśca vimalā śitāḥ || 6-102-16

16. idam = this; mahat chāpam = great bow; aindram = belonging to Indra; kavacham cha = an armour; agnisam nibham = shining as fire; sharāshcha = arrows; āditya samkāshāḥ = bright as the sun; vimalā = and stainless; shivā = auspicious; shaktishcha = spear; (were giving).

"Here is the great bow belonging to Indra, an armour shining as fire, some arrows as bright as sun and a stainless auspicious spear."

Sloka 17

āruhyeman rathaṃ vīra rākṣasan jahi rāvaṇam |
mayā sārathinā rāma mahendra iva dānavān || 6-102-17

17. vīra = O heroic; rājan = king!; āruhya = mounting; imam = this; ratham = chariot; mayā sārathinā = with me as a charioteer; jahi = kill; rāvaṇam = Ravana; rākṣhasam = the king; mahendraḥ iva = as Indra the lord of celestials; (killed); dānavān = the demons.

"O heroic king! ṃounting this chariot with me as a charioteer, kill Ravana the king, as earlier Indra the lord of celestials killed the demons."

Sloka 18

ityuktaḥ sa parikramya rathan tamabhivādya ca |
āruroha tadā rāmo lokāṇllakṣmyā virājayan || 6-102-18

18. iti = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; (by ṃatali); rāmaḥ = Rama; abhivādya cha = by offering his salutation; samparikramya = by circumambulating; tam = then; āruroha = ascended it; virājayan lokān = causing the three worlds; to shine forth; lakṣhmyā = with his splendour.

Duly circumambulating that chariot (as a mark of respect) and offering his salutation, when thus spoken by ṃatali, Rama then ascended the chariot, causing the three worlds to shine forth with his splendour.

Sloka 19

babhūva ca mahāyuddhaṃ tumulaṃ romaharṣaṇam |
rāmasya ca mahābāho rāvaṇasya ca rakṣasaḥ || 6-102-19

19. mahāyuddham = a great battle; tumulam = which was tumultuous; roma harṣhaṇam = causing the hair to stand erect; babhūva cha = ensued; mahābāhoḥ rāmasya cha = between the great armed Rama; rāvaṇasya cha = and Ravana; rakṣhasaḥ = the demon.

Then ensued a tumultuous and thrilling battle, between the great armed Rama and Ravana, the demon.

Sloka 20

sa gāndharveṇa gāndharvan daivaṃ daivena rāghavaḥ |
astran rākṣasarājasya jaghāna paramāstravit || 6-102-20

20. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; paramāstravit = who was skilled in the use of great missiles; jaghāna = struck; gāndharvam = the missile presided over by Gandharvas; daivam = and the missile presided over the gods; rākṣhasa rājasya = discharged by Ravana the king of demons; gāndharveṇa daivena = by means of Gandharva missile and Daiva missile.

That Rama, who was skilled in the use of great missiles, struck the missile presided over by Gandharvas and the missile presided over by gods, by means of missiles of the same type.

Sloka 21

astran tu paramaṃ ghoran rākṣasaṃ rākasādhipa |
sasarja paramakruddhaḥ punareva niśācaraḥ || 6-102-21

21. nishācharaḥ = Ravana; rākṣhasādhipaḥ = the king of demons; paramakruddhaḥ = was very much enraged; punareva = and again; sasarja = released; paramam ghoram = an extra ordinarily terrific; rākṣhasam astram = missile presided over by demons.

Ravana, the king of demons was very much enraged and again released another extra-ordinarily terrific missile presided over by demons.

Sloka 22

te rāvaṇadhanurmuktāḥ śarāḥ kāñcanabhūṣaṇāḥ |
abhyavartanta kākutsthan sarpā bhūtvā mahāviṣāḥ || 6-102-22

22. te sharāḥ = those arrows; kāṇ^chana bhūṣhaṇāḥ = decked with gold; rāvaṇadhanurmuktāḥ = discharged from Ravana's bow; bhūtvā = turning into; mahāviṣhāḥ sarpāḥ = highly poisonous snakes; abhyavartanta = rolled towards; kākutthsam = Rama.

Those arrows, decked with gold, discharged from Ravana's bow, turning as they were into highly poisonous snakes and rolled towards Rama.

Sloka 23

te dīptavadanā dīptan vamanto jvalanaṃ mukhaiḥ |
rāmamevābhyavartanta vyāditāsyā bhayānakāḥ || 6-102-23

23. te = those serpents; dīpta vadanāḥ = with flaming mouths; vyāditāsyāḥ = with mouths wide open; vamantaḥ = and vomiting; dīptam jvalanam = blazing fire; mukhaiḥ = by their mouths; bhayānakāḥ = those dreadful (arrows); abhyavartanta = dashed towards; rāmameva = Rama alone.

With flaming mouths wide open and vomiting blazing fire, those dreadful snake-like arrows dashed towards Rama alone.

Sloka 24

diśaśca santatāḥ sarvāḥ pradiśaśca samāvṛtāḥ || 6-102-24

24. sarvāḥ = all; dishshcha = the quarters; samtatāḥ = stood covered; taiḥ = by serpents; vāsukisamasparshaiḥ = whose impact was as hard as that of Vasuki (the king of serpents); dīpta bhogaiḥ = with flaming coils; mahāviṣhaiḥ = and having strong poison; vidishashcha = even the corners between the quarters; samāvṛitāḥ = stood enveloped (by them).

All the quarters stood covered by highly poisonous serpents, whose impact was as hard as that of Vasuki (the king of serpents), with the flaming coils. Even the corners between the quarters stood enveloped by them.

Sloka 25

astran gārutmataṃ ghoraṃ prāduścakre bhayāvaham || 6-102-25

25. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tān pannagān = those serpents; samāpatataḥ = rushing towards him; āhave = in the battle-field; rāmaḥ = Rama; prādushchakre = created; ghoram = the terrific; bhyāvaham = and dangerous; gārutmatam astram = missile presided over by Garuda the eagle (an enemy of serpents).

Seeing those serpents rushing towards him in the battle-field, Rama released the terrific and dangerous missile presided over by Garuda the eagle (an enemy of serpents).

Sloka 26

26. te = those arrows; rukmapuṇ^khāḥ = with golden shafts; shikhiprabhāḥ = and which shone like flames; rāghava dhanur-muktāḥ = discharged from Rama's bow; bhūtvā = turning into; kāṇ^chanāḥ suparṇāḥ = golden eagles; sarpashatravaḥ = the enemies of serpents; vicheruḥ = flew all around.

With golden shafts which shone like flames, discharged by Rama's bow turning into golden eagles (the enemies of serpents) those arrows flew all around.

Sloka 27

27. te vishikhāḥ = those arrows; rāmasya = of Rama; kāmarūpiṇaḥ = which can assume any form at will; suvarṇarūpaḥ = assumed the eagles; jaghnuḥ = and destroyed; tān sarvān sharān = all those arrows; mahājavān = which flew with a great speed; sarparūpān = in the form of snakes.

Those arrows of Rama, which can assume any form at will, appeared in the form of eagles and destroyed all those arrows, which flew with a great speed in the form of snakes.

Sloka 28

Enraged on his missile having been frustrated by Rama, Ravana streamed forth terrific showers of arrows on Rama.

Sloka 29

Tormenting Rama of unwearied action with thousand arrows, Ravana then pierced ṃatali with a multitude of arrows.

Sloka 30

Targeting with only one arrow, Ravana tore off the flag-staff. Striking down the golden flag-staff from the top of the chariot down to the seat of the chariot, Ravana struck even Indra's horses with a multitude of arrows.

Sloka 31

viṣedurdevagandharvā dānavāścāraṇaiḥ saha || 6-102-31

Seeing Rama afflicted, the gods, Gandharvas the celestial musicians, charanas the celestials bards along with the demons, as also Siddhas the demi-gods and the great sages felt dejected. Seeing the moon in the shape of Rama eclipsed by Rahu in the shape of Ravana, the monkey-chiefs too, along with Vibhishana, felt perturbed.

Sloka 33

prājāpatyan ca nakṣatran rohiṇīṃ śaśinaḥ priyām || 6-102-33
samākramya budhastasthau prajānāmaśubhāvahaḥ |

33. budham = the planet ṃercury; tasthau = stood; ākramya = assailing; rohiṇīm = the constellation Rohini; prājāpatyam = presided over by the god Prajapati (the lord of creation); priyām = the beloved; shasinaḥ = of the moon nakṣhatram = the heavenly body; ashubhāvahaḥ = and spelling disaster; prajānām = to the created beings.

Seeing thus the moon in the shape of Rama eclipsed by the planet Rahu in the shape of Ravana, the planet ṃercury stood, assailing the constellation Rohini presided over by the god Prajapati (the lord of creation), the beloved of the moon the heavenly body and spelling disaster to all created beings.

Sloka 34

sadhūmaparivṛttormiḥ prajvalanniva sāgaraḥ || 6-102-34

34. prajvalanniva = blazing as it were; kruddhaḥ = in fury; sāgaraḥ = the ocean; utpapāta = rose high; tadā = at that time; spṛishanniva = as though it was going to touch; divākaram = the sun; sadhūmaparivṛittormiḥ = its mist-wreathed waves moving to-and-fro.

Blazing as it were in fury, the ocean rose high at that time, as though it was going to touch the sun, its mist-wreathed waves moving to-and-fro.

Sloka 35

śastravarṇaḥ suparuṣo mandaraśmirdivākaraḥ || 6-102-35
adṛśyata kabandhāṅgaḥ sansakto dhūmaketunā |

35. divākaraḥ = the sun; shastravaraṇaḥ = looking ashy in colour; suparuṣhaḥ = and assuming a piercing aspect; mandarashmiḥ = its rays grown exhilarated; adṛishyata = appeared; kabandhāṇkaḥ = in a headless trunk in its lap; samsaktaḥ = and united; dhūmaketunā = with a comet.

The sun, looking ashy in colour assuming a piercing aspect, and its rays grown exhilarated, appeared in a headless trunk in its lap and united with a comet.

Sloka 36

kosalānān ca nakṣatran vyaktamindrāgnidaivatam || 6-102-36
ākramyāṅgārakastasthau viśākhāmapi cāmbare |

36. aṇ^gārakaḥ = the planet ṃars; tasthau = stood; ākramya = assailing; ambare = in the sky; nakṣhatram vishākhām = the coustellation Vishakha; aindrāgnidaivatam = presided over by the gods Indra and Agni (the god of fire) kosalānām vyaktam = which is adorned by the kings of Kosala.

The planet ṃars stood assailing in the sky, the constellation Vishakha, presided over by the gods Indra and Agni (the god of fire), which is adorned by the kings of Kosala.

Sloka 37

daśāsyo viṃśatibhujaḥ pragṛhītaśarāsanaḥ || 6-102-37
adṛśyata daśagrīvo maināka iva parvataḥ |

37. dashagrīvaḥ = (ṭhat) Ravana; dashāsyaḥ = having ten faces; vimshati bhujaḥ = and twenty arms; pragṛihīta sharāsanaḥ = holding a bow tightly in his hands; adṛishyata = looked; mainākaḥ parvataḥ iva = like ṃount ṃainaka.

That Ravana, having ten faces and twenty arms, holding a bow tightly in his hands, looked like ṃount ṃainaka.

Sloka 38

nirasyamāno rāmastu daśagrīveṇa rakṣasā || 6-102-38
nāśakadabhisandhātun sāyakānraṇamūrdhani |

38. nirasyamānaḥ = being overwhelmed; dashagrīveṇa = by Ravana; rakṣhasā = the demon; raṇamūrdhani = in the battle-front; rāmaḥ = Rama; na ashaknot = could not; sāyakān abhisamdhātum = fit his arrows to his bow.

Being overwhelmed by Ravana the demon in the battle-front, Rama could not fit his arrows to his bow.

Sloka 39

sa kṛtvā bhrukuṭīn kruddhaḥ kiṃ citsanraktalocanaḥ || 6-102-39
jagāma sumahākrodhaṃ nirdahanniva rakṣasān |

39. saḥ = that Rama; kruddhaḥ = enraged; bhṛikuṭim kṛitvā = knitting his eye-brows; kimchit samrakta lochanaḥ = his eyes turned slightly blood-red; jagāma = got; sumahākrodham = into a fierce anger; nirdahanniva rakṣhasān = as though he would burn out the demons.

The enraged Rama, knitting his eye-brows and with his eyes turned slightly blood-red, was provoked to fierce anger, as though he would burn out the demons.

Sloka 40

tasya kruddhasya vadanaṃ dṛṣṭvā rāmasya dhīmataḥ || 6-102-40
sarvabhūtāni vitresuḥ prākampata ca medinī |

40. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vadanam = the face; tasya = dhīmataḥ rāmasya = of that sagacious Rama; kruddhasya = getting provoked of anger; sarva bhūtāni = all the living bengs; vitreṣhu = got frightened; medinī cha = the earth too; prākampata = began to tremble.

Seeing the face of the sagacious Rama getting provoked of anger, all the living beings got frightened. The earth too began to tremble.

Sloka 41

siṃhaśārdūlavān śailaḥ saṃcacāla caladdrumaḥ |
babhūva cāpi kṣubhitaḥ samudraḥ saritāṃ patiḥ || 6-102-41

41. shailaḥ = the mountain; simha sārdūlān = with lions and tigers; chaladrumaḥ = as also with swaying trees; samchchāla = was trembled; samudraḥ chāpi = even the ocean; saritāmpatiḥ = the lord of rivers; babhūva = became; kṣhubhitaḥ = agitated.

The mountain, with lions, tigers and swaying trees, was trembled. Even the ocean, the lord of rivers, became agitated.

Sloka 42

kharāśca kharanirghoṣā gagane paruṣā ghanāḥ |
autpātikāśca nardantaḥ samantāt paricakramuḥ || 6-102-42

42. ghanāḥ = the clouds; kharāḥ = which were dense; khara nirghoṣhāḥ = with harsh sounds; paruṣhāḥ autpātikāḥ = and assuming a stern aspect; parichakramuḥ = roamed; samantāt = all over; gagane = in the sky; nardantaḥ = thundering.

Dense clouds, emitting harsh sounds and assuming a stern aspect, roamed all over in the sky, thundering.

Sloka 43

rāmaṃ dṛṣṭvā susaṃkruddhamutpātāṃśaiva dāruṇān |
vitresuḥ sarvabhūtāni rāvaṇasyābhavadbhayam || 6-102-43

43. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; susamkruddham = the very much enraged; rāmam = Rama; dāruṇān utpātān cha = and terrific portents; sarvabhūtāni = all the living beings; vitresuḥ = were frightened; bhayam = fear; abhavat = seized; rāvaṇasya = Ravana (too).

Seeing the very much enraged Rama and also the terrific portents, all the living beings were frightened. Fear seized Ravana too.

Sloka 44

vimānasthāsthadā devā gandharvāśca mahoragāḥ |
ṛṣidānavadaityāśca garutmantaśca khecarāḥ || 6-102-44
dadṛśuste tadā yuddhaṃ lokasaṃvartasaṃsthitam
nānāpraharaṇairbhīmaiḥ śūrayoḥ saṃprayudhyatoḥ || 6-102-45

44; 45. vimānasthāḥ = seated in their aerial cars; devāḥ = gods; gandharvāshcha = Gandharvas the celestial musicians; mahoragāḥ = great ṇagas (semi divine beings having the face of a man and the tail of serpent); ṛiṣhidānavadaityāshcha = the sages; demons and giants; khecharāḥ te garutmantashcha = and those eagles remaining in the air; dadṛishaḥ = saw; tadā = then; yuddham shūrayoḥ = the battle of two heroes; samprayudhyatoḥ = fighting steadily; bhīmaiḥ nānā praharaṇaiḥ = with various dreadful weapons; lokasamvarta samsthitam = looking like the final dissolution of the world.

Seated in their aerial cars, gods, Gandharvas the celestial musicians, great Nagas (semi divine beings having the face of a man and the tail of a serpent), the sages, demons, giants and those eagles remaining in the air, saw then the battle of two heroes, fighting steadily with various dreadful weapons and looking like the final dissolution of the world.

Sloka 46

ūcuḥ surāsurāḥ sarve tadā vigrahamāgatāḥ |
prekṣamāṇā mahadyuddhaṃ vākyaṃ bhaktyā prahṛṣṭavat || 6-102-46

46. prahṛiṣhṭavat = getting rejoiced; prekṣhamāṇāḥ = on observing; mahat yuddham = that great battle; sarve = all; surāsurāḥ = the gods and demons; vigraham āgatāḥ = who had come to witness the conflict; tadā = at that time; ūchuḥ = spoke; vākyam = the following words; bhaktyā = with devotion.

Getting rejoiced on observing that great battle, all the gods and demons who had come to witness the conflict at that time, responded:

Sloka 47

daśagrīvaṃ jayetyāhurasurāḥ samavasthitāḥ |
devā rāmamavocaṃste tvaṃ jayeti punaḥ punaḥ || 6-102-47

47. asurāḥ = the demons; samavasthitāḥ = who reached there; āhuḥ = cried out; dashagrīvam = to Ravana; jaya it = "Be Victorious!"; te = those; devāḥ = gods; āhuḥ = called; rāmam = to Rama; punaḥ punaḥ iti = saying again and again; jaya tvam = "Be you victorious!".

The demons who reached there cried out to Ravana, "Be victorious!". Those gods called to Rama saying again and again: "Be you victorious!".

etasminnantare krodhādrāghavasya sa rāvaṇaḥ |
prahartukāmo duṣṭātmā spṛśan praharaṇaṃ mahat || 6-102-48
vajrasāraṃ mahānādaṃ sarvaśatrunibarhaṇam |
śailaśṛṅganibhaiḥ kūṭaiścittadṛṣṭibhayāvaham || 6-102-49

48-51. etasmin antare = in the meanwhile; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; duṣhṭātmā = the evil-minded; rāvaṇaḥ = who caused enemies to cry; krodhāt = with anger; spṛihan = stroking; mahat praharaṇam = a great weapon; prahartukāmaḥ = wishing to strike; rāghavasya = Rama; jagrāha = took hold; roṣheṇa = with fury; shūlam = of a spike; vajrasāram = which was powerful as a thunderbolt; mahānādam = which made a loud noise (when hurled at its target); sarvashatrunibarhaṇam = capable of exterminating all enemies; shailashṛiṇ^ga nibhaiḥ = resembling mountain-peaks; kūṭaiḥ = which with its sharp points; chittadṛiṣṭibhayāvaham = dreadful to conceive and to behold; sadhūmamiva tīkṣhṇāgram yugāntāgnichayopamam = resembling a smoke-crested mass of fire blazing at the end of the world-cycle; atiraudram = exceedingly furious; anāsādyam = which was difficult to approach; kālenāpi = even for Death; durāsāsādyam = which was unassailable; trāsanam sarvabhūtānām = which was a terror for all living beings; dāruṇam tathā bhedanam = capable as it was of tearing and splitting them; pradīptam iva = and blazing as it was.

In the meantime, that evil-minded Ravana, who caused enemies to cry, stroking with anger a great weapon wishing to strike Rama, took hold off with fury, a spike, which was powerful as a thunderbolt, which made a loud noise (when hurled at a target), capable of exterminating all enemies, resembling the mountain-peaks, which with its sharp points dreadful to conceive and to behold, resembling a smoke-crested mass of fire blazing at the end of the world-cycle, exceedingly furious, which was difficult to approach even for Death, which was unassailable, which was a terror for all beings, capable as it was of tearing and splitting them and blazing as it was.

Sloka 52

tacchūlaṃ paramakruddho madhye jagrāha vīryavān |
anīkaiḥ samare śūrai rākṣasaiḥ parivāritaḥ || 6-102-52

52. vīryavān = that valiant Ravana; parivāritaḥ = surrounded; shūraiḥ anīkaiḥ = by warriors of demons; samare = in battle; parama kruddhaḥ = was very much enraged; jagrāha = and took hold of; tat = that; shūlam = spear; madhye = at its middle.

That valiant Ravana, surrounded by warriors of demons in battle, was very much enraged and took hold of that spear at its middle.

Sloka 53

samudyamya mahākāyo nanāda yudhi bhairavam |
saṃraktanayano roṣāt svasainyamabhiharṣayan || 6-102-53

53. samraktanayanaḥ = with blood-red eyes; roṣhāt = in anger; mahākāyaḥ = Ravana with his collossal body; samudyamya = duly lifting his spear and bringing rejoice to; svasainyam = his own army; nanāda = roared; bhairavam = terribly; yudhi = in battle.

With blood-red eyes in anger, Ravana with his collossal body, duly raising the spear and bringing rejoice to his own army, roared terribly in battle.

Sloka 54

pṛthivīṃ cāntarikṣaṃ ca diśaśca pradiśastathā |
prākampayattadā śabdo rākṣasendrasya dāruṇaḥ || 6-102-54

54. dāruṇaḥ = the terrific; shabdaḥ = roar; rākṣhasendrasya = of Ravana; tadā = then; prākampayat = trebled; pṛithivīmcha = the earth; antarikṣhamcha = the sky; dishashcha = the four quarters; tathā = and; pradishashcha = and the angles of the compass.

The terrific noise made by Ravana then trembled the earth, the sky, the four quarters and the angles of the compass

Sloka 55

atikāyasya nādena tena tasya durātmanaḥ |
sarvabhūtāni vitresuḥ sāgaraśca pracukṣubhe || 6-102-55

55. tena nādena = by that roar; tasya durāt manaḥ = of that evil-minded Ravana; atikāyasya = of a collossal body; sarvabhūtāni = all the living beings; vitresuḥ = were frightened; sāgarashcha = the ocean too; prachukṣhubhe = got agitated.

By that roar of that evil-minded Ravana of a collossal body, all the living beings were frightened. The ocean too got agitated.

Sloka 56

sa gṛhītvā mahāvīryaḥ śūlaṃ tadrāvaṇo mahat |
vinadya sumahānādaṃ rāmaṃ paruṣamabravīt || 6-102-56

56. gṛihītvā = taking hold of; tat mahat shūlam = that large spear; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; mahāvīrya = of great prowess; vinadya = emitting; sumahānādam = a very loud roar; abravīt = spoke; paruṣham = the following harsh words; rāmam = to Rama.

Taking hold of that large spear, that Ravana of great prowess, emitting a very loud roar, spoke the following harsh words to Rama:

Sloka 57

śūlo.ayaṃ vajrasāraste rāma roṣanmayodyataḥ |
tava bhrātṛsahāyasya samyak prāṇān hariṣyati || 6-102-57

57. udyataḥ = raised; roṣhāt = in fury; mayā = by me; rāma = O Rama!; ayam shūlaḥ = this spear; vajrasāraḥ = with the power of a thunder-bolt; samyak hariṣhyati = will surely take away; tava prāṇān = the lives of you; bhrātṛihāyasya = along with life of your brother; your help-mate.

"Raised in fury by me, O Rama! This spear with the power of a thunder-bolt, will take away surely your life along with the life of your bother, your help-mate."

Sloka 58

rakṣasāmadya śūrāṇāṃ nihatānāṃ camūmukhe |
tvāṃ nihatya raṇaślāghī karomi tarasā samam || 6-102-58

58. nihatya = killing; tvām = you; tavasā = with my strength; adya = now; raṇashlāghī = I; who always commend war-fare; karomi = will make you; samam = equal; shūrāṇām rakṣhasām = with the valiant demons; nihatānām = who have been killed; chamūmukhe = in the battle front.

"Killing you with my strength now, I, who always commend war-fare, will make you level with the valiant demons, who have been killed in the battle-front."

Sloka 59

tiṣṭhedānīṃ nihanmi tvāmeṣa śūlena rāghava |
evamuktvā sa cikṣepa tacchūlaṃ rākṣasādhipaḥ || 6-102-59

59. tiṣhṭha = wait; eṣhaḥ = this I; nihanmi = will kill; tvām = you; shūlena = with the spear; idānīm = now; rāghava = O Rama!; evam uktvā = thus speaking; saḥ rākṣhasādhipaḥ = that demon; chikṣhepa = hurled; tat shūlam = the spear.

"Wait, I will kill you with the spear now, O Rama!" Saying so, that demon hurled that spear.

Sloka 60

tadrāvaṇakarānmuktaṃ vidyunmālāsamāvṛtam |
aṣṭaghaṇṭaṃ mahānādaṃ viyadgatamaśobhata || 6-102-60

60. tat = that spear; vidyummālāsamavṛitam = wreathed as it was in a circle of lightning; aṣhṭaghaṇ^ṭam = provided as it was with eight bells; mahānādam = and making a loud noise; muktam = released; rāvaṇakarāt = from the hand of Ravana; ashobhata = splashed; viyadgatam = as it went into the sky.

That spear, wreathed as it was in a circle of lightning, provided as it was with eight bells and making a loud noise, released from Ravana's hand, splashed, as it went into tehs sky.

Sloka 61

tacchūlāṃ rāgavo dRiṣṭvā jvalantaṃ ghoradarśanam |
sasarja viśikhān rāmaścāpamāyamya vīryavān || 6-102-61

61. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tat = that; jvalantam = blazing; shūlam = spear; ghora darshanam = of terrible appearance; vīryavān = the valiant; rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = who was born in Raghu dynasty; āyamya = having stretched; chāpam = his bow; sasarja = released; vishikhān = the arrows.

Seeing that blazing spear of terrible appearance, the valiant Rama who was born in Raghu dynasty, having stretched his bow, released the arrows.

Sloka 62

āpatantaṃ śaraugheṇa vārayāmāsa rāghavaḥ |
utpatantaṃ yugāntāgniṃ jalaughairiva vāsavaḥ || 6-102-62

62. sharaugheṇa = with streams of arrows; rāghavaḥ = Rama; vārayāmāsa = warded off; āpatantam = that spear which was rushing upon him; jalaughaiḥ iva = as with streams of water; vāsavaḥ = Indra the lord of celestials (warded off); utpatantam yugāntāgnim = the fire shooting up at the time of dissolution of the world.

With streams of arrows, Rama warded off that spear, which was rushing upon him, as with streams of water, Indra the lord of celestials warded off the fire shooting up at the time of dissolution of the world.

Sloka 63

nirdadāha sa tān bāṇān rāmakārmukaniḥsṛtān |
rāvaṇasya mahān śūlaḥ pataṅgāniva pāvakaḥ || 6-102-63

63. saḥ = that; mahān = large; shūlaḥ = spear; rāvāsya = of Ravana; nirdadāha = consumed; tān bāṇān = those arrows; rāma kārmukaniḥsṛitān = coming forth from Rama's bow; pāvakaḥ iva = as fire; (would consumed); pataṇ^gān = the moths.

That large spear of Ravana consumed those arrows coming forth from Rama's bow, as fire would consume the moths.

Sloka 64

tān dṛṣṭvā bhasmasādbhūtān śūlasaṃsparśacūrṇatān |
sāyakānantarikṣasthān rāgavaḥ krodhamāharat || 6-102-64

64. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tān sāyakān = those arrows; shūlasamsparsha chūrṇitān = smashed; by coming into contact with the spear; bhasma sādbhūtān = and reduced to ashes; antarikṣhasthān = even while positioned in the sky; rāghavaḥ = Rama; āharat = was seized; krodham = with fury.

Seeing those arrows smashed by coming into contact with the spear and reduced to ashes even while positioned in the sky, Rama was seized with fury.

Sloka 65

sa tāṃ mātalinānītāṃ śaktiṃ vāsavasaṃmatām |
jagrāha paramakruddho rāghavo raghavanandanaḥ || 6-102-65

65. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; rāghunandanaḥ = te delight of the Raghus; parama kruddhaḥ = was quite enraged; jagrāha = and took hold; tām shaktim = of that spear; ānītām = brought; mātalinā = by ṃatali the charioteer; vāsāsammatām = as desired by Indra the lord of celestials.

That Rama, the delight of Raghus, was quite enraged and took hold of that spear brought by ṃatali the charioteer, as desired by Indra the lord of celestials.

Sloka 66

sā tolitā balavatā śaktirghaṇṭākṛtasvanā |
nabhaḥ prajvālayāmāsa yugāntolkeva saprabhā || 6-102-66

66. sā shaktiḥ = that spear; tolitā = lifted up; balavatā = by the powerful Rama; ghaṇṭākṛita svanā = which was rendered resonated by the bells; prajvālayāmāsa = began to blaze; saprabhā yugāntolkeva = like a blazing meteor at the time of dissolution of the world.

That spear, lifted up by the powerful Rama, which was rendered resonated by the bells, began to blaze like a blazing meteor at the time of the dissolution of the world.

Sloka 67

sā kṣiptā rākṣasendrasya tasmin śūle papāta ha |
bhinnaḥ śaktyā mahān śūlo nipapāta hatadyutiḥ || 6-102-67

67. sā = that spear; kṣhiptā = hurled (by Rama); nipapāta ha = fell; tasmin rākṣhasendrasya shūle = on that Ravana's spear; bhinnaḥ = torn down; shaktyā = by Rama's spear; mahān shūlaḥ = the large spear of Ravana; nipapāta = fell (on the ground); hata dyutiḥ = with its lustre extinguished.

That spear, hurled by Rama, fell on that Ravana's spear. Torn down by Rama's spear, the large spear of Ravana fell on the ground, with its lustre extinguished.

Sloka 68

nirbhabheda tato bāṇairhayānasya mahājavān |
rāmastrīkṣaṇairmahāvegairbāṇavadbhirajihmagaiḥ || 6-102-68

68. tīkṣhṇaiḥ = with terrific arrows; mahāvegaiḥ = of high speed; bāṇavadbhiḥ = with their shafts made of reeds; ajihmagaiḥ = going straight to their target; rāmaḥ = Rama; nirbibheda = pierced; mahājavān = the exceedingly fleet; hayān = horses; asya = of Ravana.

With terrific arrows of high speed, with their shafts made of reeds, going straight to their target, Rama pierced the exceedingly fleet horses of Ravana.

Sloka 69

nirbadhedorasi tadā rāvaṇaṃ niśitaiḥ śaraiḥ |
rāghavaḥ paramāyatto lalāṭe pattribhistribhiḥ || 6-102-69

69. rāghavaḥ = Rama; tadā = then; nirbibheda = pierced; nishitaiḥ sharaiḥ = with his sharp arrows; urasi = the chest-region; rāvaṇam = of Ravana; paramāyattaḥ = quite deliberately; (he struck); lalāṭe = the forehead of Ravana; tribhiḥ = with three; pattribhiḥ = arrows.

With his sharp arrows, Rama then pierced the chest-region of Ravana. He also struck, quite deliberately, the forehead of Ravana with three arrows.

Sloka 70

sa śarairbhinnasarvāṅgo gātraprasṛtaśoṇitaḥ |
rākṣasendraḥ samūhasthaḥ pullāśoka ivābabhau || 6-102-70

70. bhinna sarvāṇ^gaḥ = with all his limbs torn down; sharaiḥ = by arrows; gātra prashṛita shoṇitaḥ = with blood flowing from his limbs; saḥ rākṣhasendraḥ = that Ravana; ābabhau = shone; phullāshokaḥ iva = like a blooming Ashoka tree; samūhasthaḥ = in the midst of a multitude of trees.

With all his limbs torn down by arrows and with blood flowing from his limbs, that Ravana in the midst of demons, shone like a blooming Ashoka tree in the midst of a multitude of trees.

Sloka 71

sa rāmabāṇairatividdhagātro |
niśācarendraḥ kṣatajārdragātraḥ |
jagāma khedaṃ ca samājamadhye |
krodhaṃ ca cakre subhṛśaṃ tadānīm || 6-102-71

71. saḥ nishācharendraḥ = that Ravana; atividdhagātraḥ = with his limbs severely = injured; rāma bāṇaiḥ = by Rama's arrows; kṣhatajārdragātraḥ = and his body bathed in blood; jagāma khedam = felt exhausted; samājamadhye = in the midst of a multitude of demons; subhṛisham krodham cha chakre = gave went to a violent anger; tadānīm = at that time.

With his limbs severely injured by Rama's arrows and with his body bathed in blood, that Ravana felt exhausted in the middle of a multitude of demons and gave went to a violent anger at that time.

Thus, this is the 102nd chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
