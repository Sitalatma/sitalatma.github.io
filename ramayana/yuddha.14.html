Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 14
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana informs the assembly about the strength of the monkeys, and about the power of Rama`s arrow. He also informs that none on the demon's side can withstand Rama, in battle. Prahasta replies that they have no fear at any time to face Rama in battle. Then, Vibhishana reiterates the enormous strength of the fatal arrows discharged by Rama and requests the assembly to save Ravana by giving back Sita to Rama.

Sloka 1

niśācarendrasya niśamya vākyam |
sa kumbhakarṇasya ca garjitāni |
vibhīṣaṇo rākṣasarājamukhya |
muvāca vākyam hitamrthayuktam || 6-14-1

1. nishamya = hearing; vākyam = the words; nishācharendrasya = of the king of demons; garijitāni = and the growls; kumbhakarṇasya = of Kumbhakarna; saḥ vibhīshhaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; uvācha = spoke; hitam = friendly; arthayuktam = and meaningful; vākyam = words; rākshhasarājamukhyam = to that Ravana; the foremost king of demons.

Hearing the words of Ravana the king of demons and the growls of Kumbhakarna, Vibhishana spoke the following friendly and meaningful words to Ravana the foremost king of ogres:

Sloka 2

vṛto hi bāhvantarabhogarāśi |
ścintāviṣaḥ susmitatīkṣaṇadamṣṭraḥ |
pañcāṅgulīpañcaśiro.atikāyaḥ |
sītāmahāhistava kena rājan || 6-14-2

2. rājā = "O;king! Kena = by whom; vṛtaḥ = has been wrapped; tava = to you; sīta mahā hiḥ atikāyaḥ = this great serpent of gigantic body called ṣita; bāhvantarabhogarāshiḥ = with heap of expanded hoods as her bosom; chintāvishhaḥ = having the poison called anxiety; susmita tīkshhṇadamshhtraḥ = with sharp fangs as her sweet smile; paṇchāṇgulī paṇcha shiro atikāyaḥ = with five hoods as her five fingers."

"By whom, O, king, has been wrapped around your neck, this great serpent of gigantic body called Sita, with heap of expended hoods as her bosom, having poison as her anxiety and with sharp fangs as her sweet smile and with five hoods as her five fingers."

Sloka 3

yāvanna laṅkā samabhidravanti |
valīmukhāḥ parvatakūṭamātrāḥ |
damṣṭrayudhāścaiva nakhāyudhāśca |
pradīyatām dāśarathāya maithilī || 6-14-3

3. na yāvat = before; valumukhāḥ = the monkeys; parvata kūṭa mātrāḥ = with the size of mountain peaks; damshhṭrāyudhāḥ = with their teeth as weapons; nakhāyudhāshcha = and their nails too as weapons; samādhidravanti = rush full upon; laṇkām = the city of Lanka; maithilī = let ṣita; pradīyatām = be given back; dasharathāya = to Rama."

"Even before the monkeys with their body size as mountain peaks, having their teeth and nails as weapons, rush full upon the city of Lanka, give back Sita to Rama."

Sloka 4

yāvanna gṛhṇanti śirāṃsi bāṇā |
rāmeritā rākṣasapuṅgavānām |
vajropamā vāyusamānavegāḥ |
pradīyatām dāśarathāya maithilī || 6-14-4

4. na yāvat = before; bāṇāḥ = the arrows; rāmeritā = dispatched by Rama; vajropamā = resembling thunderbolts; vāyu samāna vegāḥ = with a speed equal to wind; gṛihṇanti = take away; shirāmsi = the heads; rākshhasapuṇgavān = of the chiefs of demons; maithilī = let ṣita; pradīyatām = be given back; dāsharathāya = to Rama."

"Even before arrows resembling thunder bolts and with a speed equal to wind, dispatched by Rama, take away the heads of chiefs of demons, give back Sita to Rama."

Sloka 5

na kumbhakarṇendrajitau ca rājam |
stathā mahāpārśvamahodarau vā |
nikumbhakumbhau ca tathātikāyaḥ |
sthātum samarthā yudhi rāghavasya || 6-14-5

5. rājan = O; king!; kumbhakarṇendrajitou = Either Kumbhakarna or Indrajit; tathā = and; mahaparshva mahodarou vā = either ṃahaparshvā or ṃahodara; nikumbhakumbhaou = or ṇikumbha or Kumbha; tathā = and; atikāyaḥ = Atikaya; na samarthāḥ = cannot; sthātum = withstand; rāghavasya = against Rama; yudhi = in battle."

"O, king! Either Kumbhakarna or Indrajit and either ṃahaparsva or ṃahodara and either Nikumbha or Kumbha or even Atikaya cannot withstand Rama in battle."

Sloka 6

jīvaṃstu rāmasya na moksyase tvam |
guptaḥ savitrāpyathavā marudbhiḥ |
na vāsavasyāṅkagato na mṛtyo |
rnabho na pātālamanupraviṣṭaḥ || 6-14-6

6. tvam = (Even if) you; guptaḥ = are guarded; savitrā = by the sun god; athavā = or marudbhiḥ = by ṃaruts; jīvan = alive; rāmasya = by Rama; na = nor; aṇgagataḥ = when gone in proximity; vāsavasya = of Indra; na = nor; mṛityoḥ = when gone nearer to the god of death; na = nor; anupravishhṭaḥ = when penetrated; nabhaḥ = the heaven; pātālam = or under the earth."

"Even if you are protected by the sun god, or by ṃaruts (storm gods) nor even seated in the lap of Indra or god of Death nor if you have penetrated into the regions of heaven or under the earth you will not be spared by the arrows of Rama."

Sloka 7

niśamya vākyam tu vibhīṣaṇasya |
tataḥ prahasto vacanam babhāṣe |
na no bhayam vidma na daivatebhyo |
na dānavebhyo.apyathavā kadācit || 6-14-7

7. nishamya = hearing; vākyam = the words; vibhīshaṇasya = of Vibhishana;prahastaḥ = Prahasta; tataḥ = then; babhāshhe = spoke;vachanam = these words; naḥ = "ṭo us; na vidma = is not known; bhayam = fear; na = neither; daivatebhyaḥ = for celestials; na athavā = nor; dānavebhyaḥ = for demons; kadāchit = at any time."

Hearing the words of Vibhishana, Prahasta spoke as follows: "We are not knowing of fear at all. We do not have fear of either celestials or demons at any time."

Sloka 8

na yakṣagandharvamahoragebhyo |
bhayam na samkhye patagoragebhyaḥ |
katham nu rāmādbhavitā bhayam no |
narendraputrātsamare kadācit || 6-14-8

8. na bhayam = there is no fear; (for us); yakshagandharva mahoragebhyaḥ = from Yakshas or Gandharvas or eminent ṇagas; na = nor from; patagoragebhyaḥ = birds and reptiles; samkhye = in battle; katham = how; bhavitā = can; kadāchit = at any time; bhayam = fear; naḥ = to us; samare = in battle; rāmāt = from Rama; narendraputrāt = the son of a human king?"

"We do not have fear from Yakshas (demi- gods) or Gandharvas (celestial musicians) or eminent Nagas (serpent demons) or from birds and reptiles on the field of battle. When such is the case, how can we have fear from Rama, the son of a human king at any time in battle?"

Sloka 9

prahastavākyam tvahitam niśamya |
vibhīṣaṇo rājahitānukāṅikṣī |
tato mahārtham vacanam babhāṣe |
dharmārthakāmeṣu niviṣṭabuddhiḥ || 6-14-9

9. tatah = then; vibhīshaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; rāja hitānukāṇkshhīi = who desired the welfare of the king; nivishhṭa buddhiḥ = who possessed a stable mind; dharmārthakāmashhu = in virtue wealth and pleasure; nishamya = having heard; Prahasta vākyam = the words of Prahasta; ahitam = which were inimical; babhāshhe = spoke; mahārtham = greatly meaningful; vachanam = words(as follows):

Vibhishana who desired the welfare of the king and who possessed a stable mind in virtue wealth and pleasure, after hearing the inimical words of Prahasta, spoke highly meaningful words as follows:

Sloka 10

prahasta rājā ca mahodaraśca |
tvam kumbhakarṇaśca yathārthajātam |
bravīta rāmam prati tanna śakyam |
yathā gatiḥ svargamadharmabuddheḥ || 6-14-10

10. prahasta = "O; Prahasta! Yathā = ḥow; rājācha = the king; mahodarashcha = and ṃahodara; tvam = you; kumbhakarṇashcha = and Kumbhakarna; bravīta = are telling; arthajātam = the collection of things; rāmamprati = about Rama; tat = that; nashakyam = is impossible; yathā = as ; adharmabuddheḥ = to the wicked minded; gatiḥ = going; svargam = to heaven(is impossible)"

"O, Prahasta! The series of actions king or ṃahodara as also yourself and Kumbhakarna propose to take against Rama are impossible of being accomplished, any more than going to heaven by wicked minded persons."

Sloka 11

vadhastu rāmasya mayā tvayā ca |
prahasta sarvairapi rākṣasairvā |
katham bhavedarthaviśāradasya |
mahārṇavam tartu mivāplavasya || 6-14-11

11. katham = how; bhavet = is it possible; mayā = by me; tvayācha = or by you; sarvaḥ = or by all; rākshhasairvā = demons; vadhaḥ = the killing; rāmasya = of Rama; arthavishāradasya = who is expert in any matter; tartum iva = like crossing; mahārṇavam = a great ocean; aplavasya = by a person without a bark"

"How is it possible by me or by you or by all the demons to kill Rama, who is expert in all matters, any more than a person without a bark to cross a great ocean."

Sloka 12

dharmapradhānasya mahārathasya |
iksvākuvaṃśaprabhavasya rājñaḥ |
puro.asya devāśca tathāvidhasya |
kṛtyeṣu śaktasya bhavanti mūḍhāḥ || 6-14-12

12. "devashcha = Even the celestials; bhavanti = become; mūDhāḥ = bewildered; puraḥ = before; rāG^yaḥ = the king; asya = Rama; tathāvidhasya = of such qualities as whose priority is righteousness; ;mahārathasya = who is great car warrior; ikshhvākuvamsha prabhavesya = who was born in Ikshvaku dynasty; shaktasya = who is capable of accomplishing; kṛiteshhu = his objects."

"Even the celestials would feel bewildered when placed in front of the king Rama who gives priority to righteousness, is a great car warrior, was born in the dynasty of Ikshvaku and is capable of accomplishing his objects."

Sloka 13

tīkṣaṇā na tāvattava kaṅkapatrā |
durāpadā rāghavavipramuktāḥ |
bhittvāśarīram praviśanti bāṇāḥ |
prahasta tenaiva vikatthase tvam || 6-14-13

13. prahasta = O;Prahasta ! bāṇāḥ = the arrows; rāghavavipramuktāḥ = released by Rama; tīkshhṇāḥ = which are sharp; kaṇ^kapatrāḥ = furnished with heron's feathers; durāsadāḥ = which are dangerous to be approached; na pravishanti tāvat = did not penetrate; bhittavā = piercing; tava = your; sharīram = body; tenaina = for that reason; tvam = you; vikatthase = are boasting"

"O, Prahasta! The sharp arrows discharged by Rama, which are furnished with heron's feathers and are dangerous to be approached, have not yet penetrated, duly piercing, your body. That is why, you indulge in a boastful talk."

Sloka 14

bhittvā na tāvatpraviśanti kāyam |
prāṇāntakāste.aśanitulyvegāḥ |
śitāḥ śarā rāghavavipramuktāḥ |
prahasta tenaiva vikatthase tvam || 6-14-14

14. prahaste = O;Prahasta! ṣharāḥ = ṭhe arrows; prāṇāhtakāḥ = which are fatal; ashanitulyavegāḥ = having the speed of a thunderbolt; shitāḥ = and are sharp; rāghave vipramuktāḥ = discharged by Rama; na tāvat pravishanti = have not yet penetrated; bhitvā = splitting; te = your; kāyam = body; tenaiva = that is why; tvam = you; vikatthase = are boasting."

"The sharp and fatal arrows, discharged by Rama with the speed of a thunderbolt, have not yet penetrated, duly splitting your body. That is why; you are indulged in a boastful talk."

Sloka 15

na rāvaṇo nāibalastrīśīrṣo |
na kumbhakarṇasya suto nikubhaḥ |
na cendrajiddāśarathim prasoḍhum |
tvam vā raṇe śakrasamam samarthaḥ || 6-14-15

15. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; nasamarthaḥ = is not capable; na = nor; trishīrshhaḥ = ṭriseersha; atibalaḥ = with great strength; na = nor; Indrajit = Indrajit; tvam vā = nor yourself; soDhum = to withstand; dāsharathim = Rama; shakrasamam = equal to Indra; raṇe = in battle."

"Ravana or Triseersha, with great strength or Nikumbha the son of Kumbhakarna or Indrajit or yourself are not capable to withstand Rama, who is equal in strength to Indra, in battle."

Sloka 16

devāntako vāpi narāntako vā |
tathātikāyo.atiratho mahātmā |
akampananaścādrisamānasāraḥ |
sthātum na śaktā yudhi rāghavasya || 6-14-16

16. devāntakova.api = or even Devantaka ; narātakovā:or ṇarantaka; tathā:and;atikāyaḥ:Atikaya;atirathaḥ:or Atiratha; mahātmā = of gigantic proportions; akampanashcha = or Akampana; adrisamāna sāraḥ = who is mighty as an ocean; na shaktāḥ = are not capable; sthātum = to withstand; yudhi = in battle; rāghavasya = against Rama."

"Even Devantaka or Narantaka or Atikaya or Atiratha of gigantic proportions, or Akampana who is mighty as an ocean are not capable to withstand against Rama in battle."

Sloka 17

ayam ca rājā nyasanābhibhūto |
mitrairamitrapratimairbhavadbhiḥ |
anvāsyate rākṣasanāśanārthe |
tīkṣaṇaḥ prakṛtyā hyasamīkṣyakārī || 6-14-17

17. ayam rājā = this king; vyasanābhibhūtaḥ = who is over come by evil addictions*; tīkshhṇaḥ = is rude; prakṛityā = in nature; asamīkshhyakārī = and acts inconsiderately; rākshhanāshanarthe = for the destruction of demons; anvāsyate = he is being seated round as a caucus; bhavadbhiḥ = by you; mitraiḥ = who are his friends; amitrapratimaiḥ = taking the form of enemies."

"This king who is overcome by evil addictions, * is rude in nature and acts inconsiderately. Such a king is being surrounded as a caucus by you who are his companions taking the form of his enemies for the ultimate destruction of demons."

*The following are the seven evil habits to which kings are generally found addicted: --Harshness of speech and severity of punishment, extravagance, drinking, love of women, hunting and gambling.

Sloka 18

anantabhogena sahasramūrdhnā |
vāgena bhīmena mahābalena |
balātparikṣiptamimam bhavanto |
rājānamutkṣipya vimocayantu || 6-14-18

18. bhavantaḥ = you; utekshhipya = lift up; vimochayantu = and release; imām rājānam = this king; parikshhiptam = who has been wrapped; balata = forcibly; bhīmana = by terrific; nāgena = snake; mahābalena = exceedingly strong; ananta bhogena = of boundless proportions; sahasra mūrdhnā = having thousand hoods."

"You lift up and release this king, who has been wrapped forcibly by a terrific and exceedingly strong snake of boundless proportions having thousand hoods and make him completely free."

Sloka 19

yāvaddhi keśagrahaṇātsuhṛdbhiḥ |
sametya sarvaiḥ paripūrṇakāmaiḥ |
nigṛhya rājā parirakṣitavyo |
bhūtairyathā bhīmabalairgṛhīaḥ || 6-14-19

19. sarvaiḥ = all; suhṛIdbhiḥ = the well wishers; paripūrṇakāmaiḥ = who are getting their desires fulfilled (by the king); sametya = should come together; rājā parirakshhitavyaḥhi = and the king is indeed to be protected; nigṛihya = by coersion; keshagrahaṇāt yāvat = even by pulling his hair; gṛihītaḥ yathā = as one who is taken possession of ; bhūtaiḥ = by evil spirits; bhīmabalaiaḥ = of terrific strength."

"All the well-wishers who are getting their desires fulfilled by the king, should come together and protect the king by coercion even by pulling his hair if necessary as one who is taken ossession of by evil spirits with terrific strength, is protected by one's kith and kin."

Sloka 20

suvāriṇā rāghavasāgareṇa |
praccādyamānastarasā bhavadbhiḥ |
praccādyamānastarasā bhavadbhiḥ |
yuktastvayam tārayitum sametya |
kākutthsapātālamukhe patansaḥ || 6-14-20

20. ayam = this Ravana; prachchādyamānaḥ = who is going to be enveloped; tarasā = forcibly; rāghavasāgareṇa = with full of excellent water; patan = and who is about to fall; kākutthsa pātāla mukhe = into the mouth of Patala (the nethermost subterranean region; ); in the shape of Rama; yuktaḥ = is fit; tārayitum = to be saved; sametya = united; bhavidbhiḥ = by you."

"This Ravana, for his part who is going to be enveloped forcibly by an ocean in the shape of Rama - which is full of excellent water (who possesses excellent moral character) and who is about to fall into the mouth of Patala (the nethermost subterranean region) in the shape of Rama, is fit to be rescued united by you."

Sloka 21

idam purasyāsya sarākṣasasya |
rājñaśca pathyam sasuhṛjjanasya |
samyagghi vākyam svamatam bravīmi |
narendraputrāya dadātu maithilīm || 6-14-21

21. bravīmihi = I am indeed telling; samyak = rightly; idam vākyam = these words; pathyam = which are wholesome; svamatam = as per my opinion; asya = to this; parasya = city; parākshhasasya = and the demons residing init; rāG^yashcha = to the king; sasuhṛijjanasya = along with his well wishers; maithilīm = Let ṣita; dadātu = be given back; narendraputrāya = to Rama".

"I am telling these right and wholesome words, as per my opinion, to this city and the demons inhabiting in it as well as to the king and his well wishers: Let Sita be given back to Rama."

Sloka 22

parasya vīryam svabalam ca buddhvā |
sthānam kṣayam caiva tathaiva vṛddhim |
tathā svapakṣe pyanumṛśya buddhyā |
vadt kṣamam svāmihitam sa mantrī || 6-14-22

22. buddhvā = knowing; vīryam = the strength; parasya = of the enemies; svabalamcha = and our own strength; anumṛIshya = grasping; buddhyā = by the intellect; tathā = like wise; sthānam = the status quo; kshhayamchaiva = the fall; tathaiva = and; vṛiddhim = rise; svapakshhe = in our army; vadet = (he who) speaks; svāmihitam = in the interest of the king; khhamam = in a befitting manner; saḥ = he; mantrī = is the councellor".

"A counselor is he, who after knowing the strength of the enemies and that of his own (master) and even so duly grasping with his intellect, the status quo, fall, or rise in military power like-wise on both sides, renders advice which is appropriate to the interest of his king."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe caturdaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 14th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
