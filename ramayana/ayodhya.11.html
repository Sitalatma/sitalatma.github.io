Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 11
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Kaikeyi asks King Dasaratha to fulfill the boons given to her in earlier times.

Sloka 1

taṃ manmathaśarairviddhaṃ kāmavegavaśānugam |
uvāca pṛthivīpālaṃ kaikeyī dāruṇaṃ vacaḥ || 2-11-1

1. kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; uvacha = spoke ; dāruṇam = harsh; vachaḥ = words; tam pṛithivīpālam = to that king; viddham = struck; manmadhasharaḥ = by cupid's arrows; kāmavega vashānugan = yielded to hasty lust.

Kaikeyi spoke harsh words to that king, who was struck by cupid's arrows and yielded to hasty lust.

Sloka 2

nāsmi viprakṛtā deva kena cinnāvamānitā |
abhiprāyastu me kaścittamicchāmi tvayā kṛtam || 2-11-2

2. deva = Oh; King!; nāsmi = I am not; viprakṛitā = insulted; kenachit = by anybody; na avamānitā = nor despired; tu = but; me = my; kashchit = certain; abhiprāyaḥ = wish ; ichchhāmi = I desire; tam = it; kṛitam = to be accomplished; tvayā = by you.

"Oh, King! no body has insulted me, nor despised me. But I have a certain, desire that needs to be fulfilled by you."

Sloka 3

pratijñāṃ pratijānīṣva yadi tvaṃ kartu micchasi |
atha tadvyāhariṣyāmi yadabhiprārthitaṃ mayā || 2-11-3

3. tvam ichchhasiyadi = If you wish; kartum = to do; pratijJṇām pratijānīshhva = you promise; atha = ṭhereafter; vyāharishhyāmi = I shall tell; at = that; yat = which; abhiprārthitam = desired.

"If you wish to fulfil my desire, you promise to fulfil it. After your promise I shall inform you that which is desired by me."

Sloka 4

tāmuvāca mahātejāḥ kaikeyīmīṣadutsmaitaḥ |
kāmī hastena saṃgṛhya mūrdhajeṣu śucismitām || 2-11-4

4. mahātejaḥ = ṭhe most majestic; kāmī = lustful Dasaratha; utsmitaḥ = out smiled; īshāt = a little; samgṛihya = seized; hastena = with hand; mūritaneshhu = into hair; tām kaikeyīm = that Kaikeyi; shuchismitām = having white smile; uvācha = and spoke

The most majestic and lustful Dasaratha smiled a little and caressingly kept his hand into her hair and spoke these words to Kaikeyi who was having white smile in her face.

Sloka 5

avalipte na jānāsi tvattaḥ priyataro mama |
manujo manujavyāghrādrāmādanyo na vidyate || 2-11-5

5. avalipte = Oh; proud woman!; najānāsi = don't you know; avidyate anyaḥ = there is no other; manujaḥ = man; priyataraḥ = more dearer; mama = to me; tvattaḥ = than you; rāmāt = except Rama; manuja vyāgrāt = the best among men.

"Oh, proud woman! Don't you know that there is nobody on this earth more dearer to me than you except Rama, the best among men."

Sloka 6

tenājayyena mukhyena rāghaveṇa mahātmanā |
śape te jīvanārheṇa brūhi yanmanasecchasi || 2-11-6

6. tena rāghaveṇa = By that Rama; ajayyena = who cannot be defeated; mukhyena = who is important person|; jeevanārheṇa = who is worthy of being lived upon; mahātmanā = who is highsouled; shape = I take oath; brūhi = ṭell; yat = what; ichhasi = is desired.

"Rama cannot be defeated by enemies. He is a very important person in our family. He is the supporter of life and a high souled man. I take oath by him. Tell me what is desired by you".

Sloka 7

yaṃ muhūrtamapaśyaṃstu na jīveyamahaṃ dhruvam |
tena rāmeṇa kaikeyi śape te vacanakriyām || 2-11-7

7. kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi!; aham = I; na jīveyam = cannot live; dhṛivam = certainly; muhūrtam = a moment; apashyan = with out seeing; yam = Rama; tena rāmeṇa = by that Rama; shape = I take oath; te vachana kriyām = to fulfill your word.

"Oh, Kaikeyi ! I can't live even for a moment without seeing Rama. This is certain. By that Rama, I am taking my oath and promising to fulfill your word".

Sloka 8

ātmanā vātmajaiścānyairvṛṇe yaṃ manujarṣabham |
tena rāmeṇa kaikeyi śape te vacanakriyām || 2-11-8

8. kaikeyi = Oh; Kaikeyi ! vṛiṇe = I desire; yam = which; manujarshhabham = best of men; anyaiḥ = by other; ātmajaishcha = sons; tena rāmeṇa = by that Rama ; manasa = by your mind

"I desire the well being of Rama, the best of men, even at the cost of my life or the lives of my other sons. I take oath by that Rama and tell you that I shall fulfil your word."

Sloka 9

bhadre hṛdayamapyetadannumṛśyoddharasva me |
etatsamīkṣya kaikeyi brūhi yatsādhu manyase || 2-11-9

9. bhadre = Oh; auspicious woman! uddharasva = Raise; tat = this; me = my; hṛidayam = heart; anum ṛishya = by touching; kaikeyi = Oh;Kaikeyi! samīkshhya = observe; etat = this; brūhi = tell; yat = which; manyase = you think; sādhu = as good.

"Oh, auspicious lady! ṃy heart is sinking. You raise it by your touch. Oh, Kaikeyi! you observe all this and tell me that which you think as good."

Sloka 10

balamātmani jānantī na māṃ śaṅkitumarhasi |
kariṣyāmi tava prītiṃ sukṛtenāpi te śape || 2-11-10

10. na arhasi shaṇk^itum = to doubt; mām = me; jānantī = you know; balam = your strength; ātmāni = over me; shape = I take oath; te = to you; sukṛitenāpi = by auspiciousness also; karishhye = I shall do; tava = your; prītim = desire.

"You need not doubt me as you know your authority over me. I am taking oath on my auspiciousness. I shall do whatever is desired by you."

Sloka 11

sā tadarthamanā devī tamabhiprāyamāgatam |
nirmādhyasthyācca harṣācca babhāṣe durvacaṃ vacaḥ || 2-11-11

11. devi = Kaikeyi; tadarthamanāḥ = keeping in mind her wish for coronation of Bharata and for Rama's exile; avedayanthi = informing; tam abhiprāyam = that wish; āgatam = coming to her mind; babhāshhe = spoke; durvacham = unspeakable; vachaḥ = words; nirmādhyasthuāt = with bias; harshhātcha = and with delight.

That Kaikeyi, who has utmost desire for coronation of Bharata and for sending Rama to exile, revealing that wish from her mind, uttered unspeakable wordswith bias and delight.

Sloka 12

tena vākyena saṃhṛṣṭā tamabhiprāyamāgatam |
vyājahāra mahāghoramabhyāgatamivāntakam || 2-11-12

12. sahṛishhṭā = Kaikeyi having delighted; tena vākyena = by those words; vyājahāra = spoke; tam abhiprāyam = that wish; āgatam = coming to her mind; mahāghoram = which is very terrific; antakamiva = like death; abhyāgatam = coming suddenly.

Kaikeyi, having been delighted with Dasaratha's words, conveyed him of the very horrible wish in her mind as conveying the news of a suddenly befallen death.

Sloka 13

yathā krameṇa śapasi varaṃ mama dadāsi ca |
tacchṛṇvantu trayastrīṃśaddevāḥ sāgnipurogamāḥ || 2-11-13

13. sāgnipurogamaḥ = with the god of Fire in the forefront; sriṇvantu trayastrimshaddevāḥ = thirty three celestials hear; tat = that; dadāsicha = offering of; varam = boon; mama = to me; thā = as; krameṇa = with series of; sapasi = your oaths.

"Let thirty three celestials with the god of Fire in the forefront hear your words giving boons to me, preceded by series of your oaths"

Sloka 14

candrādityau nabhaśaiva grahā rātryahanī diśaḥ |
jagacca pṛthivī ceyaṃ sagandharvā sarākṣasā || 2-11-14
niśācarāṇi bhūtāni gṛheṣu gṛhadevatāḥ |
yāni cānyāni bhūtāni jānīyurbhāṣitaṃ tava || 2-11-15

14;15. jānīyuḥ tava bhāshitam = Let your words be known to; chandrādityau = moon and sun; nabhaḥ evacha = sky; grahāḥ = planets; rātrayahāni = nights and days; dishaḥ = directions; jagachcha = universe ; sagandharvā = together with celestial musicians; sarākshhasā = with demons; iyam = this; pRi^thivī = earth; bhūtāni = spirits; nishācharāṇi = wandering in nights; gṛihadevatāḥ = house gods; gṛiheshhu = in houses; yāni = which; anyāni = other; bhūtāni = spirits.

"Let your words be heard by sun, moon, sky, planets, day, night, the directions, universe, earth, celestial musicians, demons, spirits wandering in nights , house-gods and other spirits."

Sloka 16

satysandho mahātejādharmajñḥ susamāhitaḥ |
varaṃ mama dadātyeṣa tanme śṛṇvantu devatāaḥ || 2-11-16

16. eshhaḥ = ṭhis king Dasaratha; satyasandhaḥ = who has true promise; mahātejaḥ = who has great valour; dharmagyaḥ = who knows righteousness; susamāhitaḥ = who has good equanimity ; dadāti = is giving; varam = boon; mama = to me; sṛiṇvantu devatāḥ = let celestials hear; tat = it; me = for my sake.

"King Dasaratha, who has true promise, who has great valour, who knows righteousness and who has good equanimity of mind, is giving boon to me. Let the celestials hear it for my sake."

Sloka 17

iti devī maheṣvāsaṃ parigṛhyabhiśasya ca |
tataḥ paramuvācedaṃ varadaṃ kāmamohitam || 2-11-17

17. devī = Kaikeyi; parigṛihya = overpowered; maheshhvāsam = Dasaratha; who has great bow ; abhishasyacha = promised too much and; tataḥaparam = thereafter; uvachā = spoke; idam = these words; shatruḥ = the enemy; āchya avayat = removed; tava jīvitam antarā = almost everything except your life.

Kaikeyi made Dasaratha to hear these words, praised him too much and thereafter spoke these words to him who was ready to give boons as he was infatuated by lust.

Sloka 18

smara rājn purā vṛttaṃ tasmin daivāsure raṇe |
tatra cācyāvayacchatrustava jīvatamantarā || 2-11-18

18. rājan = Oh;King! smara = remember; vṛitam = the occasion of; tasmin = that; daivāsure = between celestials and demons; purā = in the past; tatra = in the battle;varan = boons; me = to my self.

"Oh, King! Remember what happened in the battle between celestials and demons in the olden times. There the enemy destroyed almost every thing except your life".

Sloka 19

tatra cāpi mayā deva yattvaṃ samabhirakṣitaḥ |
jāgratyā yatamānāyāstato me prādadā varau || 2-11-19

19. deva = Oh; King! tatracha = ṭhere; yat = by which reason; tvam = you; samabhirakshitaḥ = were saved; mayā = by me; tataḥ = by that reason prādadāḥ = you give; kāma mohitam = infatuated by lust; varadam = who was ready to confer boons.

"Oh, King! There, I saved you. Hence you gave boon to myself who was attentive in trying to save you."

Sloka 20

tau tu dattau varau deva nikṣepau mṛgayāmyaham |
tathaiva pṛthivīpāla sakāśe satyasaṃgara || 2-11-20

20. deva = Oh;King! Pṛithivīpāla = ṭhe protector of the Earth; satyasangara = the one who has true promise; aham = I; mṛigayāmi = am hunting; tou varautu = for those boons; dattau = given; nikshhepau = kept for safe custody; tava sakāshe eva = at your vicinity only.

"Oh, King! The protection of the Earth, the one who has true promise! I am hunting for those boons, which were given by you and kept with you for safe custody."

Sloka 21

tatpratiśrutya dharmeṇa na ceddāsyasi me varam |
adyaiva hi prahāsyāmi jīvitaṃ tvadvimānitā || 2-11-21

21. tat = for that reason; pratishrutya = after promising; me = my; varam = boon; na dāshyasi = if you do not give it; dharmeṇa = as per right; tvadvimānitā = I being despised by you; prahāshyāmi hi = shall abandon; jīvitam = life; adyaiva = now itself.

"You have to give that boon, as per right, to me as promised by you. If you despise me by not giving it, I should abandon my life now itself".

Sloka 22

vāṅmātreṇa tadā rājā kaikeyyā svavaśe kṛtaḥ |
pracskanda vināśāya pāśaṃ vṛga ivātmanaḥ || 2-11-22

22. tadā = ṭhen; rājaḥ = Dasaratha; kṛitaḥ = who was made; svavashe = into her submission; vāṇ^mātreṇa = in full measure of words; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; prachaskanda = fell into; pāsham = trap; mṛigaḥiva = as a deer; ātmanaḥ = for his self; vināshāya = destruction.

Dasaratha, after having thus yielded in full measure to kaikeyi's words, fell into her trap as a deer, for his self destruction.

Sloka 23

tataḥ paramuvācedaṃ varadaṃ kāmamohitam |
varau yau me tvayā deva tadā dattau mahīpate || 2-11-23
tau tāvadahaṃdyaiva vakṣyāmi śṛṇu me vacaḥ |

23. tataḥparam = ṭhere afterwards; uvācha = spoke; idam = these words; varadam = to Dasaratha who is ready to give boons; kāma mohitam = and who was infatuated by lust; deva = Oh; King! mahīpate = the lord of the Earth! adyaivatāvat = ṇow only; vakshhyāmi = Iam asking; tau = those; varau = boons; yau = which were; datton = given; tvayā = by you; me = to me; tadā = then; shṛiṇu = Listen to; me vachaḥ = my words.

There-afterwards, Kaikeyi spoke these words to Dasaratha who was ready to give boons and who was infatuated by lust: "Oh, King! the lord of Earth, now only I am asking for those boons which were given by you then. Listen to my words".

Sloka 24

abhiṣekasamārambhao rāghavasyopakalpitaḥ || 2-11-24
anenaivābhiṣe keṇa bharato me.abhiṣicyatām |

24. upakalpitaḥ = Arrangements have been made; rāghavasya abhishheka samārambhaḥ = to undertake Rama's coronation; me bharataḥ abhi- shhichyatām = Let my Bharata coronated; anena = in this; abhishhekeṇaiva = anointment ceremony itself.

"All arrangements have been made to undertake Rama's coronation. Let my Bharata instead be coronated on this occasion itself".

Sloka 25

yo dvitīyo varo deva dattaḥ prītena me tvayā || 2-11-25
tadā daivāsure yuddhe tasya kālo.aya māgataḥ |

25. tasya = for it; ayam = this; kālaḥ = time; āgataḥ = has come; yaḥ = for which; dvitīya varaḥ = second boon; dattaḥ = is given; me = to me; prītenā = affectionately; tvayā = by you; tāda = then; daivāsurayuddhe = in the battle between celestials and demons.

"Now, the time has arrived for the second boon given by you affectionately then in the battle between celestials and demons."

Sloka 26

nava pañca ca varṣāṇi daṇḍakāraṇyamāśritaḥ || 2-11-26
cīrājinajaṭādhārī rāmo bhavatu tāpasaḥ |

26. rāmaḥ = Rama; āshritaḥ = has to take refuge; daṇdakāranyam = in the forest of Dandaka; nava paJṇcha cha varshhaṇi = for fourteen years; bhavatu tāpasaḥ = let him become ascetic; chīrājīna jatādhārī = wearing rags; deer skin and matted hair.

"Rama has to take refuge in the forest of Dandaka for fourteen years and let him become an ascetic wearing rags, deer skin and matted hair".

Sloka 27

bharato bhajatāmadya yauvarājyamakaṇṭakam || 2-11-27
eṣa me paramaḥ kāmo dattameva varaṃ vṛṇe |
adyacaiva hi paśyeyaṃ prayāntaṃ rāghavaṃ vanm || 2-11-28

27;28. adya = ṇow; bharataḥ = Bharata bhajatām = should get; yauvarājyam = rights of succession to kingdom; ; akaṇṭakam = which is free of enemies.; eshhaḥ = ṭhis is; me = my ; paramaḥ = greatest; kāmaḥ = desire; vṛiṇe = I am asking; dattam parameva = for boon given earlier; adyamaiva = ṇow itself; pashyeyam = let me see; rāghavam = Rama; prayāntam = going; vanam = to forest .

"Now itself, Bharata has to get rights of succession to kingdom, which is free of enemies. This is my greatest desire. Now I am asking for the boon given by you in earlier. "

Sloka 29

sa rājarājo bhava styasaṃgaraḥ |
kulaṃ ca śīlaṃ ca hi rakṣa janma ca |
paratra vāse hi vadntyanuttamaṃ |
tapodhanāḥ satyavaco hitaṃ nṛṇām || 2-11-29

29. saḥ = you as that; rājarājaḥ = king of kings; bhava = become; satya sangaraḥ = true to your promise; rakshhahi = protect; kulamcha = caste; shīlamcha = character; janmacha = and birth; tapodhanāḥ = Ascetics; vadantihi = even told that; nṛipāṇām = for human beings; satyavachaḥ = speaking only truth; anuttamam hitam = is greatly benificial; vāse = while dwelling; paratra = in another world.

"Oh King of the Kings, Dasaratha ! Be true to your promise and protect your caste, character and birth. Are not the ascetics tell that for human beings to get happiness in another world, they have to speak only the truth."

- - -

|| iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe 11th sargaḥ ||

Thus completes eleventh sarga of Ayodhya Kanda in Srimad Ramayana .
