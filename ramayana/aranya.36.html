Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 36
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana seeks ṃāreecha's help to abduct Seetha. He asks ṃāreecha to assume the shape of a golden-deer and lure Seetha, and then Seetha asks Rama and Lakshmana to fetch that golden-deer. If Rama and Lakshmana are distracted from the hermitage, Ravana can safely abduct Seetha. Listening to this ṃāreecha is struck dead, because he is already struck with the arrow of Rama when he was in Tataka forest. As such, ṃāreecha tries to explain Ravana about Rama's quintessence.


Sloka 1

mārīca śrūyatām tāta vacanam mama bhāṣataḥ |
ārto asmi mama ca ārtasya bhavān hi paramā gatiḥ || 3-36-1

1. tāta mārīca = oh, sire, ṃāreecha; bhāṣataḥ mama vacanam shrūyatām = as I speak, my, sentence, listen - give attention; ārtaḥ asmi anguished one, I am; ārtasya mama = anguished one, for me; bhavān paramā gatiḥ hi = you are, ultimate, course, isn't it.

"Give attention to my words as I speak, oh, sire, ṃāreecha, I am an anguished one, and when I am in such an anguish you are the ultimate course to me, isn't so." Thus Ravana started addressing ṃāreecha. [3-36-1]

Sloka 2

jānīṣe tvam janasthāne bhrātā yatra kharo mama |
dūṣaṇaḥ ca mahābāhuḥ svasā śūrpaṇakhā ca me || 3-36-2
triśirāḥ ca mahātejā rākṣasaḥ piśita aśanaḥ |
anye ca bahavaḥ śūrā labdha lakṣā niśācarāḥ || 3-36-3
vasanti mat niyogena adhivāsam ca rākṣasaḥ |
bādhamānā mahāraṇye munīn ye dharma cāriṇaḥ || 3-36-4

2, 3, 4. mama bhrātā kharaḥ = my, brother, Khara; mahābāhuḥ dūṣaṇaḥ ca = might armed, Dūshana, even; svasā shūrpaṇakhā ca = sister, ṣhūrpanakha, even; mahātejā pishita ashanaḥ rākṣasaḥ trishirāḥ ca = highly fiery one, raw-flesh, devourer, demon, ṭrishira, also; and; shūrāḥ = braves ones; labdha lakṣāḥ = those who obtained, target - those that can hit target without missing; anye bahavaḥ nishācarāḥ rākṣasaḥ = other, numerous, night-walkers, demons; mat niyogena = by me, assigned; mahā araṇye = in great-forest; dharma cāriṇaḥ munīn bādhamānā = righteousness, treaders in, sages, while torturing; janasthāne = in Janasthāna; [yathā = as to how]; adhivāsam vasanti = in residency, are residing; tvam jānīṣe = you are, aware of.

"You are aware as to how my brother Khara, and the mighty armed Dūshana, also my sister Shūrpanakha, and even the highly fiery demon and devourer of raw-flesh Trishira, and even numerous other night-walking demons who are cocksure of hitting their targets have made Janasthāna as their residency, and while residing there they put the sages of that great forest that tread a righteous path to torture, of course, all this as assigned by me. [3-36-2, 3, 4]

Sloka 5

caturdaśa sahasrāṇi rakṣasām bhīma karmaṇām |
śūrāṇām labdha lakṣāṇām khara citta anuvartinām || 3-36-5
te tu idānīm janasthāne vasamānā mahābalāḥ |

5. bhīma karmaṇām = of fiendish, exploits; shūrāṇām = braving ones; labdha lakṣāṇām = attained, targets - marks-demons - or, the possessors, of qualities to pick up quarrels, troublemakers to sages; khara citta anuvartinām = Khara's, wishes, followers of; rakṣasām = of demons; caturdasha sahasrāṇi = fourteen, thousand; [tvam jānīṣe = you are, aware of; you are equally aware of.]

"You are equally aware of the stationing fourteen thousand braving demons in Janasthāna, who are the followers of the wish of Khara, who are with fiendish exploits, and who are the troublemakers to the sages of Dandaka forest or to the intruders therein, aren't you. [3-36-5]

Sloka 6

saṃgatāḥ parama āyattā rāmeṇa saha saṃyuge || 3-36-6
nānā śastra praharaṇāḥ khara pramukha rākṣasaḥ |

6, 7a. janasthāne vasamānā = in Janasthāna, who are residing; mahābalāḥ = great-mighty ones; te tu = they, on their part; khara = Khara; pra mukha = verily, in front - that are lead by Khara - others; rākshasaḥ = demons; idānīm = now - recently; parama āyattāḥ = inordinately, preparing - themselve; nānā shastra praharaṇāḥ = with diverse, weapons, those weapons that assault - assault and battery; sanyuge = in combat; rāmeṇa saha = Rama, with; sangatāḥ = met - had an encounter with Rama.

"But recently those great mighty demons that are the residents of Janasthāna, namely Khara and others, preparing themselves inordinately and wielding diverse weapons and assaulters, for their part had an encounter with Rama in a combat. [3-36-6, 7a]

▼

This episode is narrated in chapter 33, i.e., the episode of Akampana, but there is a difference in the way of Ravana's narration at this place than the previous one. This is another point to discuss whether Akampana's episode is an original work or an interpolation.

Sloka 7

tena saṃjāta roṣeṇa rāmeṇa raṇa mūrdhani || 3-36-7
anuktvā paruṣam kiṃcit śarair vyāpāritam dhanuḥ |

7b, 8a. sam jāta roṣeṇa = well emerged, rancour - rancour tiding in him; tena rāmeṇa = by him, by Rama; raṇa mūrdhani = in combat, vanguard of; paruṣam kimcit an uktvā = scathing remark, at the least, without, saying; dhanuḥ sharaiḥ vyāpāritam = bow, with arrows, brought into play.

"With rancour tiding in him, and without making any scathing remark in the least, that Rama brought his bow into play with arrows in the van of the combat. [3-36-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8

caturdaśa sahasrāṇi rakṣasām ugra tejasām || 3-36-8
nihatāni śaraiḥ dīptaiḥ mānuṣeṇa padātinā |

8b, 9a. padātinā = by a foot-soldier; mānuṣeṇa = by a human; ugra tejasām rakṣasām = flaring, fieriness, demons of; caturdasha sahasrāṇi = fourteen, thousand; dīptaiḥ sharaiḥ ni hatāni = with fiery, arrows, completely wiped out.

"But that foot-soldier, that too, a human, has completely wiped out those fourteen-thousand demons with flaring fieriness with his fierce arrows. [3-36-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

kharaḥ ca nihataḥ saṃkhye dūṣaṇaḥ ca nipātitaḥ || 3-36-9
hatvā triśirasam ca api nirbhayā daṇḍakāḥ kṛtāḥ |

9b, 10a. sankhye = in war; kharaḥ ca nihataḥ = Khara, also, is hewed down; dūṣaṇaḥ ca nipātitaḥ = Dūshana, also, is mowed down; trishirasam ca api = ṭrishira, also, even; hatvā = on hacking down; daṇDakāḥ = Dandaka forest; nir bhayā = free, from fear - of demons; kṛitāḥ = is made.

"Khara is hewed down, Dūshana is mowed down, and even Trishira is hacked down, thereof that Dandaka forest is rendered free from the fear of demons. [3-36-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

pitrā nirastaḥ kruddhena sa bhāryaḥ kṣīṇa jīvitaḥ || 3-36-10
sa hantā tasya sainyasya rāmaḥ kṣatriya pāṃsanaḥ |

10b, 11a. kruddhena pitrā = by infuriated, father; sa bhāryaḥ = with, wife; nir astaḥ = without, foothold - put to flight - exiled; kṣīṇa jīvitaḥ = diminished, lifespan - of that Rama; kṣatriya pāmsanaḥ = among Kshatriya-s, an ignoble one; tasya sainyasya hantā = of that, [demonic-] army, he is the slaughterer of my demonic army.

"He who is put to flight by his infuriated father, and who came to Dandaka forest along with his wife, that ignoble Kshatriya is the slaughterer of that demonic army of mine, as his lifespan is diminished. [3-36-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11

aśīlaḥ karkaśaḥ tīkṣṇo mūrkho lubdho ajita indriyaḥ || 3-36-11
tyakta dharmaḥ tu adharma ātmā bhūtānām ahite rataḥ |

11b, 12a. a shīlaḥ = without, character - out of character; karkashaḥ = brutal; tīkṣṇaḥ = firestorm; mūrkhaḥ = senseless; lubdhaḥ = selfish; a jita indriyaḥ = un, conquered, senses; tyakta dharmaḥ = who discarded, righteousness; a dharma ātmā = un, righteous, souled one; bhūtānām = of all beings; a hite rataḥ = in dis, service, delights in.

"He is out of character, brutal, firestorm, senseless, selfish, one with his senses unconquered and righteousness discarded, and he is an unrighteous soul just delighting in the disservice of all beings. [3-36-11b, 12a]

▼

kruddhā pitrā nirastaḥ kim? 'is he necked out by his angry father?' No. To please Kaikeyi and the boon given to her by his father, Rama is unhoused. sa bhārya= sabhā āryaḥ 'in assemblages, venerable one.' Rama is one with high esteem. kshīṇa jīvitaḥ kim? 'is his lifespan diminishing? No. He is eternal. kshatriya pāmsana= kshatriyān pāti iti kshatriyaḥ paḥ 'because he protects Kshatriya-s he is a noble Kshatriya, and sa ca asau a.msanaḥ ca, amsana āghāte 'enemy destroyer,' destroyer of enemies of Kshatriya-s. karkashaḥ 'brutal' to enemies, not to adherents, therefore he is: amūrkhaḥ, alubdhaḥ: he s not senseless, not selfish. adharma ātmā kim? No, he is not an unrighteous person. And bhūtānām a hite a rataḥ, 'for beings, in disservice, not, delights': he does not delight in the disservice of all created beings.' Thus, he is Supreme Person.

▼

But Rama Tilaka, on the other hand refutes this derivation stating that Ravana is not an out and out devotee of Rama, and these many adhyādhāra-s 'ellipses' need not be brought in to justify mythological import to Ramayana. There is a section of pundits that argue Ramayana precedes 18 purāṇā-s the 18 mythological treatises, and they refuse to believe that Ravana is NOṭ a devotee of Rama.

▼

In anyway, the innuendo incorporated in certain verses has given rise to diverse commentaries, but the unity of Ramayana is kept up, either by Shaivaites or Vaishnavaite or other sects of Hindu thinking.  

Sloka 12

yena vairam vinā araṇye sattvam āśritya kevalam || 3-36-12
karṇa nāsa apahāreṇa bhaginī me virūpitā |
tasya bhāryām janasthānāt sītām sura suta upamām || 3-36-13
ānayiṣyāmi vikramya sahāyaḥ tatra me bhava |

12b, 13, 14a. yena = by whom; vairam vinā = enmity, without; kevalam sattvam āshritya = just, strength, depending upon; karṇa nāsa apahāreṇa = ears, nose, due to stripping off; me bhaginī virūpitā = my, sister, is disfigured; tasya = such a Rama's; bhāryām = wife is to be; sura suta upamām = celestials, daughter, similar to - nymph like - nymphean ṣeetha; sītām = ṣeetha; vikramya = holding sway; janasthānāt = from Janasthāna; ānayiṣyāmi = lead her in - inveigle her; tatra = in that matter; araṇye = in forest; me sahāyaḥ bhava = to me, as aide, you shall be.

"By whom my sister is disfigured stripping off her nose and ears, that too, without any enmity but just depending upon his own brawn, I would like to inveigle such a man's nymphean wife Seetha holding sway on her in Janasthāna, and in that matter I want you to be my aide in Dandaka forest. [3-36-12b, 13, 14a]

Sloka 14

tvayā hi aham sahāyena pārśvasthena mahābala || 3-36-14
bhrātṛbhiḥ ca surān yuddhe samagrān na abhiciṃtaye |
tat sahāyo bhava tvam me samartho hi asi rākṣasa || 3-36-15

14b. mahābala = oh, highly powerful one - ṃāreecha; sahāyena = as associate; pārshvasthena = having at my side; tvayā = with you; and; bhrātṛibhiḥ ca = with my brothers, also; yuddhe = in war; samagrān surān = all of the, gods; aham na = I, do not; abhi cintaye = towards [them,] I think - I don't care them; hi = indeed; rākṣasa = oh, demon ṃāreecha; [tat = therefore]; tvam me sahāyaḥ bhava = you, to me, aide, you become; samarthaḥ asi hi = capable [to render aid,] you are, indeed - in this venture of abducting ṣeetha.

"Oh, highly powerful ṃāreecha, with you and with my brothers standing by my side as my associates, I indeed care a damn for all of the gods if they are going to wage a war against me, therefore, oh, demon ṃāreecha, as you are capable of rendering aid you should indeed become my aide in this venture. [3-36-14b, 15]

Sloka 16

vīrye yuddhe ca darpe ca na hi asti sadṛśaḥ tava |
upāyato mahān śūro mahā māya viśāradaḥ || 3-36-16

16. vīrye = in valour; yuddhe ca = in war, even; darpe ca = in verve, also; tava sadṛishaḥ na asti hi = to you, similar one, not, is there, indeed; you are; upāyataḥ [upāyajñaḥ] = = by trickery [by ideation]; mahān shūraḥ = tomost, stalwart; mahā māya vishāradaḥ = matchless, illusive-tricks, expert.

"In valour, war and verve there is none similar to you, you are a topmost stalwart in trickery, and a matchless expert in illusive-tricks. [3-36-16]

Sloka 17

etat artham aham prāptaḥ tvat samīpam niśācara |
śṛṇu tat karma sāhāyye yat kāryam vacanāt mama || 3-36-17

17. nishācara = oh, nightwalker; etat artham = for that, reason; aham tvat samīpam prāptaḥ = I have, to your, near - nigh, come; sāhāyye = in the course of helping; yat kāryam = which, is to be done; tat karma = that, deed; mama vacanāt = by my, word - as I tell you, detail you; shṛiṇu = you listen.

"I have come nigh of you only for that reason, oh, nightwalker, and listen to that deed which you have to do in the course of helping me as I detail you. [3-36-17]

Sloka 18

sauvarṇaḥ tvam mṛgo bhūtvā citro rajata bindubhiḥ |
āśrame tasya rāmasya sītāyāḥ pramukhe cara || 3-36-18

18. tvam = you; rajata bindubhiḥ = with silver, dots; citraḥ = astounding; sauvarṇaḥ mṛigaḥ bhūtvā = golden, you, deer, on becoming; tasya rāmasya āshrame = his, of Rama, in hermitage; sītāyāḥ pramukhe cara = ṣeetha's, before, - in front of, you move.

"On becoming an astounding golden deer with silver dots, you move in front of Seetha in the hermitage of that Rama. [3-36-18]

Sloka 19

tvām tu niḥsaṃśayam sītā dṛṣṭvā tu mṛga rūpiṇam |
gṛhyatām iti bhartāram lakṣmaṇam ca abhidhāsyati || | 3-36-19

19. sītā = ṣeetha; mṛiga rūpiṇam = in deer, shape; tvām dṛiṣṭvā = you, on seeing; gṛihyatām = lay hold of; iti bhartāram lakṣmaṇam ca = thus, to husband, to Lakshmana, also; niḥ samshayam = without, doubt; abhi dhāsyati = forthwith bids.

"On seeing you in the shape of a deer, Seetha undoubtedly bids her husband forthwith, and even Lakshmana, saying, 'lay hold of it.' [3-36-19]

Sloka 20

tataḥ tayoḥ apāye tu śūnye sītām yathā sukham |
nirābādho hariṣyāmi rāhuḥ candra prabhām iva || | 3-36-20

20. tataḥ = then; tayoḥ = of those two - Rama, Lakshmana; apa āye = side, tracked - diverted; when it happened, and when; shūnye [pradeshe] = in lonely [place]; yathā sukham = as, comfortably; nir ābādhaḥ = without, being impeded; sītām = ṣeetha will be; rāhuḥ candra prabhām iva = Rahu the planet, ṃoon's, shine, as with; hariṣyāmi = I wish to abduct;.

"Then on the diversion of those two, Rama and Lakshmana, I will comfortably and unimpededly abduct Seetha in that lonely place, as planet Rahu abducts the shine of moon. [3-36-20]

Sloka 21

tataḥ paścāt sukham rāme bhāryā āharaṇa karśite |
visrabdham prahariṣyāmi kṛta arthena antar ātmanā || 3-36-21

21. tataḥ pashcāt = then, afterwards; bhāryā āharaṇa karshite = wife; by abduction, caught hold of - enervated; rāme = in Rama - Rama will be; kṛita arthena antar ātmanā = fulfilled [gratified in the first instance,] volition, with an inner, soul; sukham visrabdham prahari ṣyāmi = conveniently, surely, I wish to retaliate - Rama.

"Thereafter Rama will be enervated by the abduction of his wife, and then surely and conveniently I wish to retaliate him if he is going to come up against me, for my inner-soul will be firstly gratified with its volition, namely possessing Seetha." Thus Ravana requested ṃāreecha. [3-36-21]

▼

ṃaheshvara Tīrtha expresses this in terms of Ravana's devotion to Rama. rāme bhāryā haraṇa karṣite: rāma ibha 'Rama, the elephant...' āryā 'a venerable one, Seetha...' haraṇa karṣite 'by abduction, harmed by...' adhyadhāra: mat sharīram elliptical: 'my body...' tataḥ 'thereby, by virtue of my act of abducting...' kṛita arthena antarātmanā upalakshitaḥ san 'on my soul-cherished desire becoming fulfilled to get salvation...' paschāt 'thereafter...' visrabdham 'impeccably...' sukham = moksha, salvation, highest bliss...' prahariṣyāmi: prakarṣeṇa hariṣyāmi 'evidently I snatch off that bliss...'

▼

"When I abduct that venerable Seetha, the wife of Elephant like Rama, my body gets harmed like an eclipsed moon, but my soul gets fulfilled as I desire eternal salvation, and I can get it evidently and impeccably at the hand of Rama, owing to the ill-virtue of my abduction of Seetha.

▼

The eclipsing planet Rahu may hide moon temporarily by which the world looses moonshine for a while. If Ravana temporarily hides Seetha, as Rahu hides the moon, Rama, who metaphors with the three worlds, may be in gloom temporarily. But neither Rahu nor Ravana are illuminated or enlightened with the presence of the moonbeams of Seetha, alias Goddess Lakshmi, for they tried to seize that all-illuminating ṃoon/Seetha by force.

Sloka 22

tasya rāma kathām śrutvā mārīcasya mahātmanaḥ |
śuṣkam samabhavat vaktram paritrasto babhūva ca || 3-36-22

22. rāma kathām = Rama's, story - very word, mention of Rama; shrutvā = on listening; mahātmanaḥ tasya mārīcasya = honest-souled one, his, of ṃāreecha; vaktram = mouth; shuṣkam = dried up; sam abhavat = completely, became; pari trastaḥ babhūva ca = fully, frightened, he became, even.

On listening the very word of Rama that honest-souled ṃāreecha's mouth is completely dried up, and he is fully frightened. [3-36-22]

Sloka 23

oṣṭau parilihan śuṣkau netraiḥ animiṣaiḥ iva |
mṛta bhūta iva ārtaḥ tu rāvaṇam samut īkṣataḥ || 3-36-23

23. shuṣkau oṣṭau = dried up, lips; pari lihan = over, licking -ṃāreecha wetting with tongue; a nimiṣaiḥ iva = without, wink, as if - he started goggling; netraiḥ = with eyes - looks; mṛita bhūta iva = lifeless, being, as if - wide-eyed like an about-to-die-animal; ārtaḥ tu = agonised, on his part; rāvaṇam = at Ravana; samudaikshata [sam ut īkshataḥ = well, up, looked] = looked up, with raised and searching eyes.

Wetting dried up lips with his tongue he has become goggle-eyed almost like an about-to-die animal, and he agonisingly looked up at Ravana with winkless eyes. [3-36-23]

▼

At the very word 'Rama' ṃāreecha is shuddered. dashānana samudīrita vacanam ākarṇya rāma shabda shravaṇa mātreṇa sa.mtrasta hṛidayo mārīcaḥ kṛitānjaliḥ evam uvāca - dk From the time when he was firstly struck by Rama's arrow he retired to hermitage and no demonic activity is undertaken by ṃāreecha. But now Ravana is instigating, which leads ṃāreecha to his own end, of which he is perfectly aware.

Sloka 24

sa rāvaṇam trasta viṣaṇṇa cetā
mahāvane rāma parākramajñaḥ |
kṛta aṃjaliḥ tattvam uvāca vākyam
hitam ca tasmai hitam ātmanaḥ ca || 3-36-24

24. mahā vane = in great [ṭataka,] forest; rāma parākrama jñaḥ = Rama's, valour, knower [aware] of; saḥ = he that ṃāreecha; trasta viṣaṇṇa cetāat = by scare, sank, heart; kṛita anjaliḥ = who made palm-fold; tasmai hitam = to him - to Ravana, beneficial; ātmanaḥ ca hitam ca = for himself, also, beneficial, also; tattvam vākyam = factual, words; rāvaṇam = to Ravana; uvāca = [started to] speak - to Ravana.

ṃāreecha's heart sank with scare as he is aware of Rama's valour when he was in the great forest of Tataka, thus suppliantly making palm-fold he started telling about factuality of Rama to Ravana, which is beneficial both to Ravana and to himself, as well, if only Ravana is heedful of it. [3-36-24]

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe ṣaṭ triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 36th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
