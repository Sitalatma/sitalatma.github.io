Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda


Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 36
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dasharatha orders to send army and treasury with Rama to forests. Kaikeyi objects to such a lavish exile and she retorts the same legend of Sagara who exiled his son, Asamanja, without riches. ṃinister Siddhartha still ties to persuade her. Then vexed King Dasharatha also tries to embark on the exile along with Rama.


Sloka 1

tataḥ sumantram aikṣvākaḥ pīḍitaḥ atra pratijñayā |
sabāṣpam atinihśvasya jagāda idam punaḥ punaḥ || 2-36-1

1. tataḥ = then; aikshhvākaḥ = Dasaratha; the scion of Ikshvaku dynasty; pīditaḥ = squeezed; pratiJṇayā = by his promise; atra = in this matter; atiniḥshvasya = sighed heavly; sabhāshhpam = with tears; jagāda = spoke; idam = the following words; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; sumantra = to ṣumantra.

Then, Dasaratha the scion of Ikshvaku dynasty, squeezed by his own promise in the matter of sending Rama to exile, sighed heavily with tears and spoke the following words again and agian to Sumantra.

Sloka 2

sūta ratna susampūrṇā catur vidha balā camūḥ |
rāgavasya anuyātrā artham kṣipram pratividhīyatām || 2-36-2

2. sūtaḥ = Oh; ṣumantra! chamūḥ = Let a military body; chaturvidha balāḥ = consisting of (all the four limbs of the army; ratna sampūrṇā = filled with the best of its kind; pratividhīyatām = be arranged; kshhipram = immediately.

"Oh, Sumantra! Let a military body consisting of (all) the four limbs of the arms, filled with the best of its kind, be arranged immediately"

Sloka 3

rūpa ājīvā ca śālinyo vaṇijaḥ ca mahā dhanāḥ |
śobhayantu kumārasya vāhinīm suprasāritāḥ || 2-36-3

3. rūpājīvāshcha = let courtesans; vādinyaḥ = well versed in speaking; vaṇijashcha = merchants; mahādhanāḥ = with great riches; suprasāritāḥ = having exposure for sales; shobhayantu = bestow elegance; vāhinīm = to the forces; kumārasya = of the prince.

"Let courtesans well versed in speaking and merchants with great riches having exposure for extension of sales bestow elegance to the forces of the Prince"

Sloka 4

ye ca enam upajīvanti ramate yaiḥ ca vīryataḥ |
teṣām bahu vidham dattvā tān api atra niyojaya || 2-36-4

4. niyojaya = appoint; atra = in this contingent; tānapi = those too; dattvā = by giving; bahudhanam = abundant money; teshhām = to them; yecha = who; upajīvanti = were dependent; enam = on him(Rama) yaishcha = with whom; ramate = he took delight; vīryataḥ = in gallantry.

Appoint in this contingent those too, by giving abundant money to them who were dependent on Rama and on whom he took delight in gallantry"

Sloka 5

āyudhāni ca mukhyāni nāgarāḥ śakaṭāni ca |
anugacchantu kākutthsam vyādhāścāraṇyagocarāḥ || 2-36-5

5. mukhyāni = Let important; āyadhānicha = weapons; nāgarāḥ = chiefs of police; shakatānicha = and vehicles; vyādhāshcha = hunters; āraṇya gocharāḥ = knowing the secret of forest; anugachchhantu = accompany; kākutthsam = Rama(scion of Kakutsa)

"Let important weapons, chiefs of police and vehicles, hunters knowing the secrets of forest accompany Rama(scion of Kakutsa)"

Sloka 6

nighnan mṛgān kunjarāmḥ ca pibamḥ ca āraṇyakam madhu |
nadīḥ ca vividhāḥ paśyan na rājyam saṃsmariṣyati || 2-36-6

6. nighnan = Killing; mṛigān = deer; kuṇjarāshcha = and elephants; pibamshcha = drinking; madhu = honey; araṇyakam = of forest variety; pashyan = seeing; vividhāḥ = various; nadīshcha = rivers; na smarishhyati = he would not remember; rājyasya = of sovereignity.

"Killing deer and elephants, drinking forest-honey, seeing various rivers, he would not remember of sovereignty"

Sloka 7

dhānya kośaḥ ca yaḥ kaścit dhana kośaḥ ca māmakaḥ |
tau rāmam anugaccetām vasantam nirjane vane || 2-36-7

7. yaḥ = that which; mānaḥ = belonging to me; dhānyakoshaḥ cha = the gravnary; dhanakoshashcha = and the treasury; tau = (let) them; anugachchhetām = accompany; rāmam = Rama; vasantam = residing; vane = in the forest; nirjane = which is uninhabited.

"Let the granary and the treasury belonging to me be sent to Rama residing in the uninhabited forest."

Sloka 8

yajan puṇyeṣu deśeṣu visṛjamḥ ca āpta dakṣiṇāḥ |
ṛṣibhiḥ ca samāgamya pravatsyati sukham vane || 2-36-8

8. pravatsyati = he can live away; sukham = happily; yajan = by performing sacrificial rite; desheshhu = in places; puṇyeshhu = which are holy; visṛijamshcha = and disbursing; āptu dakshhiṇāḥ = appropriate gifts; samāgamya = along with; ṛishhibhiḥ = sages; vane = in the forest.

"Rama can spend his exile happily, by performing sacrificial rite in holy places and disbursing appropriate gifts, along with sages living in the forest "

Sloka 9

bharataḥ ca mahā bāhur ayodhyām pālayiṣyati |
sarva kāmaiḥ punaḥ śrīmān rāmaḥ saṃsādhyatām iti || 2-36-9

9. mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; bharatashcha = Bharata; pālayishhyati = will rule; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; shrīmān = (let) the glorious; rāmaḥ = Rama; samsādhyatām = be sent; sarvakāmaiḥ saha = along with all objects of enloyments"; iti = thus spoke.

"The mighty armed Bharata will rule Ayodhya. Let the glorious Rama be sent alog with all objects of enjoyment"

Sloka 10

evam bruvati kākutsthe kaikeyyā bhayam āgatam |
mukham ca api agamāt śeṣam svaraḥ ca api nyarudhyata || 2-36-10

10. kakutthse = while Dasaratha; bruvati = was speaking; evam = thus; bhayam = fear; āgatam = drew near; kaikeyyāḥ = Kaikeyi; mukhamchāpi = here mouth too; āgamat = got; shoshham = parched; svarashchāpi = her voice also; nyarndhyata = got choked.

While Dasartha was speaking thus, fear drew near Kaikeyi. Her mouth too also got parched. her voice also got choked.

Sloka 11

sā viṣaṇṇā ca samtrastā kaikeyī vākyam abravīt |
rājānamevābhimukhī kaikeyī vākyamabravīt || 2-36-11

11. vishhaṇṇācha = feeling depressed; samtrastā = and apprehensive; mukhena = with her mouth; parishushhyatā = fetting parched; abhimukhī = with her face turned; rāmameva = towards the king; sā kaikeyi = that Kaikeyi; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = these words.

Feeling depressed and apprehensive, with her mouth getthing parched and her face turned towards the king, Kaikeyi spoke as follows:

Sloka 12

rājyam gata janam sādho pīta maṇḍām surām iva |
nirāsvādyatamam śūnyam bharataḥ na abhipatsyate || 2-36-12

12. bharataḥ = Bharata; nābhipatsyate = will not accept; rājyam = the kingdom; gatajanam = uninhabited; shūnyam = hollow; niāsvādyatamam = which is absolutely unenjoyable; surām iva = like wine; pītamaṇdām = quaffed of spirituous part; sādho = Oh; noble sould!

"Bharata will not accept the kingdom, which will become uninhabited, hollow and which will be absolutely unenjoyable like wine, quaffed of its spiriteous part, Oh noble soul!"

Sloka 13

kaikeyyām mukta lajjāyām vadantyām atidāruṇam |
rājā daśaratho vākyam uvāca āyata locanām || 2-36-13

13. kaikeyyām = (when) Kaikeyi; muktalajjāyām = who has cast off her shame; vadantyām = was uttering; atidāruṇam = the most cruel words; rājā = king; dasharatha = Dasaratha; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = these words; āyatalochanām = to that lady who has large eyes.

While Kaikeyi who has cast off her shame was thus uttering th emost cruel words, King Dasaratha spoke these words to that lady with large eyes:

Sloka 14

vahantam kim tudasi mām niyujya dhuri mā āhite |
anārye kṛtyamārbdham kim na pūrvamupārudhaḥ || 2-36-14

14. ahite = Oh; hostile woman! kim = why; tudasi = do you prick; mām = me; vahantam = who is carrying forward; niyujya = while charged;mā = me; dhuri = with a burden? kim = why; na upārudhaḥ = did you not check; pūrvam = before; kṛityam = the deed; ārabdham = in the begining(itself); anārye = Oh the vulgar woman!

Having charged me with a burden, Oh hostile woman, why do you prick me while I am carrying it? Why did you not check before(while asking the boons by putting a condition that Rama should not be allowed to take anything with him) the deed(of sending troups etc with Rama) in the begining itself, Oh the vulgar woman!"

Sloka 15

tasyaitatkrodhasamyuktamuktam śrutvā varāṅganā |
kaikeyī dvi guṇam kruddhā rājānam idam abravīt || 2-36-15

15. kaikeyyī = Kaikeyi; varāṇganā = the beautiful woman; dviguṇam = doubly; kṛiddhā = enraged; shrutvā = in hearing; etat = this word; uktam = spoken; tasya = of Dasaratha; krodhasamyuktam = possessed of anger; abravīt = uttered; idam = these words; rājānām = to the king.

Doubly enraged in hearing this angry question of Dasaratha, Kaikeyi the beautiful woman replied to the king as follows:

Sloka 16

tava eva vaṃśe sagaraḥ jyeṣṭham putram upārudhat |
asamanjaiti khyātam tathā ayam gantum arhati || 2-36-16

16. tava vashe eva = In your dynasty itself; sagaraḥ = ṣagara; upārudhat = cast away; jyeshhṭa putram = his eldest son; dhyātam = called; asamaṇjaḥ iti = as Asamanja; āyam = this Rama(too); arhati gantum = to go; tathā = like that.

ṣloka 17

evam uktaḥ dhig iti eva rājā daśaratho abravīt |
vrīḍitaḥ ca janaḥ sarvaḥ sā ca tan na avabudhyata || 2-36-17

17. uktaḥ = spoken to ; evam = as above; rajā = king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; abravīt = uttered; thik ityeva = as only"shame!"; sarvaḥ = the entire; janaḥ = people; vrīditaḥ = were ashamed; sācha = that Kaikeyi; nāvabudhyata = did not perecive; tam = it.

Spoken to as above, king Dasaratha uttered only a word "Shame!". The entire people there were ashamed. But Kaikeyi did not perceive it"

Sloka 18

tatra vṛddho mahā mātraḥ siddha artho nāma nāmataḥ |
śucir bahu mataḥ rājñaḥ kaikeyīm idam abravīt || 2-36-18

18.tatra = there; vṛiddhaḥ = the aged; shuchiḥ = upright; mahāmātraḥ = cheif minister; nāmataḥ = named; siddhārtho nāme = well as ṣiddhartha; bahumataḥ = who wa shighly esteemed; rāJṇaḥ = by the king; abravīt = spoke; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi; idam = these words.

There, the aged and the upright chief minister siddhartha by name who was highly esteemed by the king, spoke to Kaikeyi these words:

Sloka 19

asamanjo gṛhītvā tu krīḍitaḥ pathi dārakān |
sarayvāḥ prakṣipann apsu ramate tena durmatiḥ || 2-36-19

19. asamṇjaḥtu = but Asamanja; durmatiḥ = the evil-minded; gṛihītvā = catching hold; bālakān = of children; krīdataḥ = playing; pathi = in the street; prakshhipan = throwing; apsu = in waters; sarayvāḥ = of ṣarayu river; ramate = took delight; tena = by it.

"Asamanja the evil-minded, catching hold of children playing in the street and throwing them in the waters of saryu river, took delight in it"

Sloka 20

tam dṛṣṭvā nāgaraḥ sarve kruddhā rājānam abruvan |
asamanjam vṛṣīṇva ekam asmān vā rāṣṭra vardhana || 2-36-20

20. dṛishhṭvā = by watching; tam = him; sarve = all the; nāgarāḥ = citizens; kṛiddhāḥ = were angry; abruvan = and spoke; rājānam = to the king; rāshhṭra vardhana = "Oh promoter of the state! vṛiṇīshhva = choose; asamṇjam = either Asamanja; ekam = alone; asmānvā = or us.

By watching him, all the citizens were angry and spoke to the king, "choose either to keep yourself Asamanja alone or to preserve us, Oh the promoter of the state!"

Sloka 21

tān uvāca tataḥ rājā kim nimittam idam bhayam |
tāḥ ca api rājñā sampṛṣṭā vākyam prakṛtayo abruvan || 2-36-21

21.tataḥ = thereupon; rājā = the king; uvācha = said; tān = to them; kimnimittam = "for what reason; idam bhayam = is this fear? sampṛishhṭāḥ = having been asked; rāJṇā = by the king; tāḥ = those; prakṛitayashchāpi = people also; abruvan = spoke; vākyam = (these) words.

Thereupon the king said to them, "For what reason is this fear? Duly asked by the king, those people also replied these words:

Sloka 22

krīḍitaḥ tu eṣa naḥ putrān bālān udbhrānta cetanaḥ |
sarayvām prakṣipan maurkhyāt atulām prītim aśnute || 2-36-22

22.eshhaḥ = he; ashnute = obtains; atulām = incomparable; prītim = delight; prakshhipan = by throwing; maurkhyāt = through studpidity; naḥ = our; krīdataḥ = playing; bālān = children; sarayvām = in ṣarayn river; udbhrānta chetasaḥ = in a whirled state of mind.

"He obtains incomparable delight by throwing, through stupidity, our playing children in sarayu river in a whirled state of mind."

Sloka 23

sa tāsām vacanam śrutvā prakṛtīnām nara adhipa |
tam tatyāja ahitam putram tāsām priya cikīrṣayā || 2-36-23

23. shrutvā = hearing; vachanam = the words; tāsām prakṛitīnām = of those people; saḥ narādhipaḥ = that king; tatyāja = abandoned; tam putram = that son; ahitam = repugnant(to his subjects) priyachikīrshhayā = in a bid to do good; tāsām = to them.

Hearing the words of those poeple, that king abandoned the son repugnant to his subjects, in a bid to do good to them.

Sloka 24

tam yānam śrīghramāropya sabhāryam saparicchadam |
yāvajjīvam vivāsyo.ayamiti svānanvaśāt pitā || 2-36-24

24. pitāḥ = ṣagara the father; shīghram = immediately; āropya = arranging to place; tam = him; sabhāryam = along with his wife; saparichchhadam = together with their belongings(utensils; implements etc); yānam = in a vehicle; anvashāt = instructed; sān = his persons; iti = thus; ayam = "ḥe; vivāsyaḥ = should be exiled; yāvajjīvam = for life."

Arranging immediately to place him along with his wife together with the paraphrenalia (like utensils, implements etc) in a vehicle, the king instructed his persons thus: "He should be exiled for life."

Sloka 25

sa phālapiṭakam gṛhya giridurgānyaloḍayat |
diśaḥ sarvāstvanucaran sa yathā pāpakarmakṛt || 2-36-25

25. saḥ = ḥe; aloDayat = ransacked; giridrgrāṇi = mountain passes; gṛihya = by taking phāla pitakam = a basket and a shovel; anucharan = (and) roaming; sarvāḥ = in all; dishaḥ = directions; saḥ = he; pāpakarmakṛityathā = as having done sinful deeds.

Roaming in all directions as though he has done sinful deeds, he ransacked mountain passes, by taking a basket and a shovel.

Sloka 26

iti evam atyajad rājā sagaraḥ vai sudhārmikaḥ |
rāmaḥ kim akarot pāpam yena evam uparudhyate || 2-36-26

26. agaraḥ rājā = king ṣagara; sudhārmikaḥ = the highly righteous man; atyajat = abandoned; enam = him; iti = in this manner; kim pāpam = what sin; rāmaḥ = Rama; akarot = has done; yena = for which; uparudhyate = he was debarred; evam = in this way?

"The highly righteous King Sagara abandoned Asamanja for perpetration sinful deeds as above. But, what sin Rama has done, for which he was debarred in this way?"

Sloka 27

na hi kaṃcana paśyāmo rāghavasyāguṇam vayam |
durlabho yasya nirayaḥ śśāṅkasyeva kalmaṣam || 2-36-27

27. vayam = we; napashyāmaḥ hi = do not indeed perceive; kamāhana = any; avaguṇam = fault; rāghavasya = of Rama; durlabhaḥ = (It is as) difficult; nirayaḥ = (to find) taint; asya = in him; shashāṇkasya = as of a (new) moon.

We do not indeed perceive any fault in Rama. It is as difficult to find a taint in him as in a new moon.

Sloka 28

athavā devi doṣam tvaṃ kaṃcitpaśyasi rāghave |
tamadya brūhi tatvaina tadā romo vivāsyatām || 2-36-28

28. athavā = Or; pashyasi = if you see; kamchit doshham = any fault; rāghave = in Rama; devi = Oh; Kaikeyi; tvam = you; brūhi = tell; tam = about it; adya = now; tattvena = correctly; tadā = then; rāmaḥ = rama; vivāsyatām = will be exiled.

"Or if you see any fault in Rama, Oh Kaikeyi you tell about it now correctly. Then, Rama will be exiled."

Sloka 29

aduṣṭasya hi samtyāgaḥ satpathe niratasya ca |
nirdahe dapi śakrasya dyutim dharmanirodhanāt || 2-36-29

29. dharmanirodhanāt = It is hindering righteousness; samtyāgaḥ = to abandon; adushhṭasya = one who is not guilty; niratasyacha = and who is devoted to in; satpathe = a god path; nirdahet = It would consume; dyutim = the splendour; shakrasya api = of even Indra (the ruler of gods).

As it is contradictory to righteousness to abandon one who is guilty and who is devoted to a good path, it would consume the splendour of even Indra (the ruler of gods)

Sloka 30

tadalam devi rāmasya śriyā vihatayā tvayā |
lokato.ap hi te rakṣyaḥ parivādaḥ śubhānane || 2-36-30

30. devi = Oh Kaikeyi! tat = hence; alam = no need of; vihatayā = impeding; shriyā = the royal fortune; rāmasya = of Rama; shubhānane = Oh lady with charming face! parivādaḥ api = even the public; rakshhyah hi = should be taken care of; te = by you.

"Oh, Kaikeyi! Hence, there is no need of impeding the royal fortune of Rama. Oh, lady with a charming face! Even blame from the public should be taken care of by you."

Sloka 31

śrutvā tu siddha artha vaco rājā śrāntatara svanaḥ |
śoka upahatayā vācā kaikeyīm idam abravīt || 2-36-31

31. shrutvā = hearing; siddhārthavachaḥ = the words of ṣiddhartha; rāja = king Dasaratha; abravīt = spoke; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi; shrāntatara svanaḥ = in the most tiresome tone; vāchā = in a voice; shokapahatā = choked with deep angnish.

Hearing the words of Siddhartha, king Dasaratha spoke to Kaikeyi in the most tiresome tone, with a voice choked with deep anguish, as follows.

Sloka 32

etadvaco necchsi pāpavṛtte |
hitam na jānāsi mamātmano vā |
āsthāya mārgam kṛpaṇam kuceṣṭā |
ceṣṭā hi te sādhupadādapetā || 2-36-32

32. pāpavṛitte = Oh; the sinful being! nechchhasi = do you not agree; etat = with these; vachaḥ = words? kucheshhṭā = you; with a bad behaviour; āsthāya = by resortin gto; mārgam = the path; kṛipaṇam = of the deplorable; na jānāsi = are you not alive; hitam = to the interest; mama = of myself; ātmanovā = or of your own; te = you; cheshhṭā = action; apetā = is deprived of; sādhupathāt = the path of righteousness.

"Oh, the sinful being! Do you not agree with these words of Siddhartha? By resorting to the path of the deplorable, you with a bad behaviour, are not alive to my interests or to your own. Your action is deprived of a righteous path."

Sloka 33

anuvrajiṣyāmy aham adya rāmam |
rājyam parityajya sukham dhanam ca |
saha eva rājñā bharatena ca tvam |
yathā sukham bhunkṣva cirāya rājyam || 2-36-33

33. aham = I; anuvrajishhyāmi = am accompanying; rāmam = Rama; adya = now; parityajya = by abandoning; rājyam = kingdom; sukham = happiness; dhanamcha = and wealth; tvam = you; bhuṇkshhva = enjoy; rājyam = the kingdom; chirāya = for a long time; yathāsukham = happily; bharatena sahaiva = together with Bharatha; rāJṇā = the king.

"I am accompanying Rama now, by abandoning my kingdom, happiness and wealth. You enjoy the kingdom for a long time, happily together with Bharata the King."

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ṣat triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 36th chapter in the Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
