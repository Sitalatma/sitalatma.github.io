Introduction

Ravana gives Seetha two months to marry hm. He orders ogres to frighten Seetha and bring Her to his control. Ravana then goes back to his own house with his consorts.


Sloka 1

sītāya vacanaṃ śrutvā paruṣaṃ rākṣaseśvaraḥ |
pratyuvāca tataḥ sītāṃ vipriyaṃ priyadarśanam || 5-22-1

1. rākshasesvaraḥ = King of ogres; shrutvā = listening; sitāyāḥ vachanam = to ṣeetha's words; parushham = which were harsh; tataḥ = thereafter; sītām pratyuvācha = replied to ṣeetha; vipriyam = unpleasant (words); priyadarshanam = which are seen to be pleasant.

Ravana listening to ṣeetha's words which were harsh thereafter replied to ṣeetha unpleasant words which are seen to be pleasant.

Sloka 2

yathā yathā sāntvayitā vaśyaḥ strīṇāṃ tathā tathā |
yathā yathā priyam vaktā pariBhūtastathā tahā || 5-22-2

2. yathā yathā = in whatever way; sāntvayitā = gentle language (is used); strīṇām = to women; tathā tathā = in that way; vashyaḥ = (they) will be under control; yathā yathā = in whatever way; vaktā = (men) speak; priyam = dear words; tathā tathā = in that way; paribhūtaḥ = they will be rejected.

"In whatever way gentle language is used to women in that way they will be under control. In whatever way men speak dear words, in that way they will be rejected."

Sloka 3

saṃniyaccati me krodhaṃ tvayi kāmaḥ samutthitaḥ |
dravato.amārgamāsādya hayāniva susārathiḥ || 5-22-3

3. tvayi = in your matter; kāmaḥ = desire;samutthitaḥ = which has risen up; saṃniyachchhati = is subdueing; krodham = anger;hayaniva = like horses; dravataḥ = running;amārgam āsādya = obtaining a bad path; susārathiḥ = (being subdued by) a good charioteer.

"In your matter desire which has risen up is subdueing my anger like horses running obtaining a bad path being subdued by a good charioteer."

Sloka 4

vāmaḥ kāmo manuṣyāṇām yasmin kila nibadhyate |
jane tasmiṃstvanukrośaḥ snehaśca kila jāyate || 5-22-4

4. kāmaḥ = deisre; manushhyāṇām = of men; vāmaḥ = is unfavourable; yasmin = in which; jane = man;nibadhyate = (desire) is tied up; tasmin = in that man; jāyate kila = there is; anukroshaḥ = compassion;snehashcha = and fondness.

"Desire of men is unfavourable. In which man desire is tied up, in that man there is compassion and fondness."

Sloka 5

etasmātkāraṇānna tvāṃ ghātayāmi varānane |
vadhārhamavamānārhāṃ mithyāpravrajite ratām || 5-22-5

5. varānane = O one with a beautiful face!; etasmāt = for this reason; tvām = (although) you (are); vadhārhām = suitable to be killed; avamānārhām = (and) suitable to be humiliated; ratām = interested; mithyāpravrajite = ( in Rama who is in) sham exile; na ghātayāmi = I am not killing you.

"O one with a beautiful face! For this reason although you are suitable to be killed and suitable to be humilaited, interested in Rama who is in sham exile, I am not killing you."

Sloka 6

paruṣāṇīha vākyāni yāni yāni bravīṣi mām |
teṣu teṣu vadho yuktastava maithili dāruṇaḥ || 5-22-6

6. maithilī = O ṣeetha!; iha = here; yāni yāni = whatever; parushhaṇi = harsh; vākyāni = words; bravīshhi = you are speaking; mām = about me; teshhu teshhu = (because of) those (words); tava dāruṇaḥ vadha = your horrible slaying; yuktaḥ = is suitable;

"O ṣeetha! ḥere whatever harsh words you are speaking about me, because of those words, you are suitable to be horribly slayed."

Sloka 7

ekamuktvā tu viadeśīṃ rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ |
krodhasaṃrambhasamyuktaḥ sītāmuttaramabravīt || 5-22-7

7. rākshasādhipaḥ = king of ogres; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; evam uktvā = speaking thus; vaidehīm = to ṣeetha; krodhasaṃrambhasaṃyuktaḥ = full of anger and fury; abravīt = spoke; uttaram = subsequent word; sītām = to ṣeetha.

ṭhe king of ogres Ravana speaking thus to ṣeetha, full of anger and fury spoke subsequent word to Seetha.

Sloka 8

dvau māsau rakṣitavyau me yo.avadhiste mayā kṛtaḥ |
tataḥ śayanamāroha mama tvaṃ varavarṇini || 5-22-8

8. varavarṇini = O ṣeetha with best complexion!; yaḥ = whatever; avadhiḥ = limit; kṛita = has been made; mayā = by me; te = to you; dvau = (those) two; māsau = months; rakshitavyau = are protectable; me = to me; tataḥ = thereafter; āroha = ascend; mama shayanam = my bed.

"O ṣeetha with best complexion! Whatever limit has been made by me to you, those two months are protectable to me. Thereafter ascend my bed."

Sloka 9

ūrdhvaṃ dvābhyāṃ tu māsābhyāṃ bhartāram māmaniccatīm |
mama tvāṃ prātarāśārthamālabhante mahānase || 5-22-9

9. ūrdhvam = above; dvābhyām māsābhyāṃ = two months; tvām = you; anichchhatīm = not desiring; mama = me; bhartāram = as husband; ālabhante = will be killed; mahānase = in my kitchen; mama prātarāshārtham = for my breakfast.

"Above two months you not desiring me as husband will be killed in my kitched for my breakfast."

Sloka 10

tāṃ tarjyamānāṃ samprekṣya rākṣasendreṇa jānakīm |
devagandharvakanyāstā viṣedurvikṛtekṣaṇāḥ || 5-22-10

10. saṃprekshya = ṣeeing; jānakīm = ṣeetha; tarjyamānām = being frightened; rākshasendreṇa = by Ravana; tāḥ = those; devagandharva kanyā = Deva and Gandharva maidens; vishheduḥ = grieved; vikṛitekshaṇāḥ = with troubled eyes.

ṣeeing ṣeetha being frightened by Ravana those Deva and Gandharva maidens grieved with troubled eyes.

Sloka 11

auṣṭhaprakārairaparā vaktranetraistathāparāḥ |
sītāmāśvāsayāmāsustarjitāṃ tena rakṣasā || 5-22-11

11. taparāḥ = some;oshhṭhaprakaraiḥ = in the way of lips; tathā = and in the like manner; aparāḥ = some (others); vaktranetraiḥ = with faces and eyes; āshvāsayāmāsuḥ = consoled;tām sītām = that ṣeetha; tarjitām = frightened; tena rakshsā = by that ogre;

ṣome in the way of lips and in like manner some others with faces and eyes consoled that ṣeetha frightened by that ogre.

Sloka 12

tābhirāśvāsitā sītā rāvaṇam rākṣasādhipam |
uvācātmahitaṃ vākyaṃ vṛttaśauṇḍīryagarvitam || 5-22-12

12. āshvāsitā = consoled; tābhiḥ = by them; sītā = ṣeetha; vṛitta shaunDīrya garvitam = proud of virtuous conduct; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = words; ātmahitam = beneficial to self;rākshsādhipam rāvaṇam = to the king of ogres Ravana.

Consoled by them ṣeetha proud of virtuous conduct spoke words beneficial to self to the king of ogres Ravana.

Sloka 13

nūnam na te janaḥ kaścidasti niḥśreyase sthitaḥ |
nivārayati yo na tvāṃ karmaṇo.asmādvigarhitāt || 5-22-13

13. yaḥ = Whoever; nivārayati = prevents; tvām = you; asmāt karmaṇaḥ = from these actions; nigarhitāt = (which are) blameable; janaḥ = (such) a person; rataḥ = interested; te niḥshreyase = in your welfare; kashchit nāsti = there is none. nūnam = this is definite.

"Whoever prevents you from these actions which are blameable, such a person interested in your welfare there is none. This is definite."

Sloka 14

māṃ hi dharmātmanaḥ patnīṃ śacīmiva śacīpateḥ |
tvadanyastriṣu lokeṣu prārthayenmanasāpi kaḥ || 5-22-14

14. trishhu lokeshhu = in all the three worlds; tvadanyaḥ = except you; kaḥ = who; manasāpi = even by heart; prārthayet = will desire;mām = me; patnīm = (who am the) wife; dharmātmanaḥ = of virtuous Rama;shachīmiva = like ṣachi; shachīpateḥ = the wife of Indra.

"In all the three worlds except you who even by heart will desire me who am the wife of virtuous Rama like Sachi the wife of Indra."

Sloka 15

rākṣasādhama rāmasya bhāryāmamitatejasaḥ |
uktavānasi yatpāpaṃ kva gatastasya mokṣyase || 5-22-15

15. rākshasādhama = O vile ogre!;yat pāpam = whatever sinful word; uktavān asi = you spoke; bharyām = to me who is the wife; amitatejasaḥ rāmasya = of the great respendent Rama; tasya = from that word; kva = where; gataḥ = will you go; mokshyase = to be released?

"O vile ogre! Whatever sinful word you spoke to me who is the wife of the great resplendent Rama, from that word where will you go to be released?"

Sloka 16

yathā dṛptaśca mātaṅgaḥ śaśaśca sahitau vane |
tathā dviradavadrāmastvaṃ nīca śaśavat smṛtaḥ || 5-22-16

16. yathā = like;dṛiptaḥ mātaJṇgāshcha = an elephant in rut; shashashcha = and a hare;sahitau = at war; vane = in the forest;tathā = in the same way; nīcha = O vile one! rāmaḥ = Rama; dviradavat = is like an elephant; tvam = you; smṛitaḥ = are said; shshavat = to be like a hare.

"Like an elephant in rut and a hare at war in the forest, in the same way O vile one! Rama is like an elephant and you are said to be like a hare."

Sloka 17

sa tvamikṣvākunātham vai kṣipanniha na lajjase |
cakṣuṣorviṣayam tasya na tāvadupagacchasi || 5-22-17

17. saḥ = such; tvam = you; na lajjase = are not shameful; kshipan = abusing; ikshvākunātham = the lord of Ikshvaku dynasty Rama; na upagachchhasi = you are not getting; tasya = Rama's; vishayam tāvat = matter so much.

"ṣuch you are not shameful abusing the lord of Ikshvaku dynasty Rama. You are not getting Rama's matter so much."

Sloka 18

ive te nayane krūte virūpe kṛṣṇapiṅgale |
kṣitau na patite kasmānmāmanārya nirīkṣataḥ || 5-22-18

18. anārya = O ignoble one!; te = your; ime nayane = these eyes; krūre = (which are) cruel; virūpe = crooked; kṛishhṇa piJṇgaLe = which are black and reddish brown; mām nirīkshitaḥ = which are seeing me; kasmāt = for what reason; na patite = are (they) not falling down.

"O ignoble one! your these eyes which are cruel, crooked, which are black and reddish brown, which are seeing me, for what reason are they not falling down."

Sloka 19

tasya dharmātmanaḥ patnīṃ snuṣāṃ daśarathasya ca |
kathaṃ vyāharato māṃ te na jihvā vyavaśīryate || 5-22-19

19. vyāharataḥ = thus speaking; mām = a bout me;patnīm = (who am) the wife; dharmātmanaḥ tasya = of virtuous Rama; snushhām cha = and the daughter-in-law; dasharathasya = of Dasaratha; katham = how; te jihvā = your tongue;vyavashīryate = did not fall off.

"ṭhus speaking about me who am the wife of virtuous Rama and the daughter-in-law of Dasaratha how your tongue did not fall off."

Sloka 20

asaṃdeśāttu rāmasya tapasaścāmapālanāt |
na tvāṃ kurmi daśagrīva bhasma bharmārha tejasā || 5-22-20

20. dashagrīva = O Ravana!; bhasmārha = (although you are) suited to be burnt into ashes; asaṃdeshāt = not having mandate; rāmasya = of Rama; anupālanāt = (and) preserving; tapasaḥ = austerity; na kurmi = I am not making; tvām bhasmam = you into ashes; tejasā = (with my) glory.

"O Ravana! Although you are suited to be burnt into ashes, not having the mandate of Rama and preserving austerity, I am not reducing you into ashes with my glory."

Sloka 21

nāpahartumahaṃ śakyā tvayā rāmasya dhīmataḥ |
vidhistava vadhārthāya vihito nātra saṃśayaḥ || 5-22-21

21. dhīmataḥ = wise; rāmasya = Rama's; aham = I; na shakyā = am not capable of being; apahartum = abducted; tvayā = by you; vidhiḥ = (this) act; vihitaḥ = has been made; tava = for your; vadhārthāya = slaying; atra = in this; na saṃshayaḥ = there is no doubt.

"Wise Rama's I am not capable of being abducted by you. ṭhis act has been made for your slaying. In this there is no doubt."

Sloka 22

śūreṇa dhanadabhrātrā balaiḥ samuditena ca |
apihya rāmaṃ kasmāddhi dāracauryaṃ tvayā kṛtam || 5-22-22

22. tvayā = by you; shūreṇa = (who is) strong; dhanadabhrātrā = brother of Kubera; samuditena cha = possessed of; balaiḥ = army; apohya = removing; rāmam = Rama; kasmāt = for what reason; dārachauryam = abduction of wife; kṛitam = has been done.

"By you who is strong, brother of Kubera possessed of army, removing Rama for what reason abduction of wife has been done."

Sloka 23

sītāyā vacanaṃ śrutvā rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ |
vinṛtya nayane krūre jānakīmanvavaikṣata || 5-22-23

23. rākshasādhipaḥ = king of rakshasas; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; shrutvā = listening; sītāyāḥ vachanam = to ṣeetha's words; vivṛitya = turned around; krūre = (his) cruel; nayane = eyes; anvavaikshata = and saw; jānakīm = ṣeetha.

King of rakshasas Ravana listening to ṣeetha's words turned around his cruel eyes and saw ṣeetha.

Sloka 24

nīlajīmūtasamkāśo mahābhujaśirodharaḥ |
siṃhasattvagatiḥ śrīmān dīpta jihvagralocanaḥ || 5-22-24

24. nīlajīmūta saṃkāsho = (Ravana was) equal to a black cloud; mahābhujashirodharaḥ = with big shoulders and neck; siṃhasattvagatiḥ = with the strength and gait of a lion; shrīmān = glorious; dīpta jihvāgra lochanaḥ = with radiant tip of tongue and eyes.

Ravana was qual to a black cloud, with big shoulders and neck with the strength and gait of a lion, glorious with radiant tip of tongue and eyes.

Sloka 25

calāgramakuṭaprāṃśuścitramālyānulepanaḥ |

25. chalāgramakutaprāṃshuḥ = (ḥe was) tall with moving front portion of crown; chitramālyānulepananaḥ = with excellent garlands and anointments; raktamālyāmbara dharaḥ = wearing red garlands and clothes; taptāṇ^gada vibhūshhanaḥ = with ornaments made of refined gold;

ḥe was tall with moving front portion of crown, with excellent garlands and anointments, wearing red garlands and clothes, with ornaments made of refined gold.

Sloka 26

śroṇisūtreṇa mahatā mecakena susamvṛtaḥ |
amṛtotpādanaddhena bhujageneva mandaraḥ || 5-22-26

26. mandaraḥ iva = Like ṃount ṃandara; bhujagena = by Vasuki; amṛitotpādaneddhena = tied for producing nectar;susaṃvṛitaḥ = (Ravana was) well tied; mahatā mechakena shroṇi sūtreṇa = by a black big thread

Like ṃount ṃandara tied by Vasuki for producing nectar, Ravana was well tied by a black big thread.

Sloka 27

śuśubhe.acalasaṃkāśaḥ śṛṅgābhyāmiva mandaraḥ || 5-22-27

27. achala saṃkāshaḥ = Equalling a mountain; rākshasesvaraḥ = the king of ogres; shushubhe = shone; tābhyām paripūrṇābhyām bhujābhyām = (with) those perfect shoulders; mandaraḥ i va = like ṃount ṃandara; shṛiṇ^gābhyām = with its peaks.

Equalling a mountain the king of ogres shone with those perfect shoulders like ṃount ṃandara with its peaks.

Sloka 28

28. taruṇāditya varṇābhyām = With the color of just risen sun; vibhūshhitaḥ = decorated; kuṇDalābhyām = by earrings; achalaḥ iva = (he) was like a mountain;ashokābhyām = with Ashoka trees; raktapallava pushhpābhyām = (with) red flowers and shoots.

With the color of just risen sun, decorated by earrings, he was like a mountain with Ashoka trees with red flowers and shoots.

Sloka 29

29. kalpavṛiksha pratima = Equalling a Kalpavriksha; saḥ = he; vasantaḥ mūrtimān iva = was like the embodiment of spring; shmashāna chaitya pratimaḥ = like a funeral mound in a grave-yard; bhayankaraḥ = was horrible; bhūshhito api = even though decorated.

Equalling a Kalpavriksha he was like the embodiment of spring season. Like a funeral mound in a grave-yard was horrible even though decorated.

Sloka 30

With red eyes due to anger Ravana was looking at ṣeetha sighing like a serpant and spoke to ṣeetha.

Sloka 31

Sloka 36

Sloka 39

upagamya tataḥ śīghraṃ rākṣasī dhānyamālinī || 5-22-39
pariṣvajya daśagrīvamidaṃ vacanamabravīt |

39. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasī = an ogre; dhānyamālinī = (called) Dhanyamalini; shīghram = quickly; upagamya = neared; dashagrīvam = Ravana; parishhvajya = embraced (him); abravīt = (and) spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words.

ṭhereafter an ogre named Dhanyamalini quickly neared Ravana, embraced him and spoke these words.

Sloka 40

mayā krīḍa mahārāja sītayā kiṃ tavānayā || 5-22-40
vivarṇayā kṛpaṇayā mānuṣyā rākṣaseśvara |

40. mahārājā = O king!; krīDa = sport; mayā = with me; rākshasesshvara = O lord of ogres!; kim prayojanam = of what use; tava = to you; anayā = (is) this; sītayā = ṣeetha; vivarṇayā = (who is) colorless; kṛipaṇayā mānushhyā = and a wreched human;

"O king! ṣport with me. O lord of ogres! Of what use to you is this ṣeetha who is colorless and a wreched human."

Sloka 41

nūnamasyā mahārāja na divyān bhogasattamān || 5-22-41
vidadhātyamaraśreṣṭhastava bāhubalārjitān |

41. maharaja = O king!; amarashreshhṭhaḥ = Lord Brahma; na vidadhāta = did not assign; asyāḥ = to ḥer; divyān = wonderful; bhogasattamān = best luxuries; tava bāhubalārjitān = earned by the might of your arms; ;nūnam = this is definite.

"O king! Lord Barhma did not assign to ḥer wonderful best luxuries earned by the might of your arms. This is definite."

Sloka 42

akāmāṃ kāmayānasya śarīramupatapyate || 5-22-42
icchantīṃ kāmayānasya prītirbhavati śobhanā |

42. sharīram = the body; kāmayānasya = of one who desires; akāmām = (a woman) without desire; upatapyate = suffers pain; shobhanā = good; prītiḥ = happiness; bhavati = will occur; kāmayānasya = to one who loves;icchantīm = (a woman) who desires (him).

"ṭhe body of one who desires a woman without desire suffers pain. Good happiness will occur to one who loves a woman who desires him."

Sloka 43

evamuktastu rākṣasyā samutkṣiptastato balī || 5-22-43
prahasanmeghasamkāśo rākṣasaḥ sa nyavartata |

43. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken to; rākshasyāḥ = by that ogre; saḥ = that; rākshasaḥ = Ravana; balī = who was strong; meghasaṃkāshaḥ = equalling a cloud; tataḥ = thereafter; samutkkshipataḥ = being taken away; nyavartata = turned back; prahasan = laughing.

ṭhus spoken to by that ogre that Ravana who was strong equalling a cloud, thereafter being taken away turned back laughing."

Sloka 44

prasthitaḥ sa daśagrīvaḥ kampayanniva medinīm || 5-22-44
jvaladbhāskaravarṇābham praviveśa niveśanam |

44. saḥ = that; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; prasthitaḥ = departing; kampayanniva = was as though shaking; medinīm = the earth; pravivesha = entered; niveshanam = (his) house jvaladbhāskaravarṇābham = with the lustre of blazing sun shine;

ṭhat Ravana departing was as though shaking the earth and entered his house with the lustre of blazing sun shine.

Sloka 45

devagandharvakanyāśca nāgakanyāśca sarvataḥ || 5-22-45
parivārya daśagrīvaṃ viviśustaṃ gṛhottamam |

45. deva gandharva kanyāshcha = Deva and Gandharva maidens; nāgakanyāshcha = and ṇaga maidens; parivārya = surrounding; rāvaṇam = Ravana; sarvataḥ = in all directions; vivishuḥ = entered; tam = that; gṛihottamam = best among houses.

Deva and Gandharva maidens and ṇaga maidens surrounding Ravana in all directions entered that best among houses.

Sloka 46

sa maithilīṃ dhrama parāmavasthitāṃ |
pravepamānāṃ paribharsya rāvaṇaḥ |
vihāya sītāṃ madanena mohitaḥ |
svameva veśma praviveśa bhāsvaram || 5-22-46

46. saḥ = that; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; paribhartsya = frightening; maithilīm = ṣeetha; avasthitām = (who was) steadfastly; dharmaparām = intent on virtue; pravepamānām = (and who was) shaking; vihāya = leaving; sītām = ṣeetha; mohitaḥ = infatuated; madanena = by love;pravivesha = entered; svam = his own; veshmaiva = house; bhāsvaram = which was shining.

ṭhat Ravana frightening ṣeetha who was steadfastly intent on virtue and who was shaking, leaving Seetha, infatuated by love, entered his own house which was shining.

- - -

ityārṣe śīrmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe dvāviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 22nd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
