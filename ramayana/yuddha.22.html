Introduction

When Rama is about to release a missile presided over by Brahma from his bow, the sea-god appears in person before him with joined palms and advises him to get a bridge constructed by Nala across the ocean. The ocean god disappears after giving this advice to Rama. Nala accordingly constructs a bridge across the sea with the help of other monkeys.

Sloka 1

athovāca raghuśreṣṭhaḥ sāgaram dāruṇam vacaḥ |
adya tvām śoṣayiṣyāmi sapātālam mahārṇava || 2-22-1

1. atha = then; raghushreshhṭhaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; dāruṇam = (these) harsh; vachanam = words; sāgaram = to the ocean; mahārṇava = "O; Ocean! shoshhayishhyāmi = I will make you dry up; adya = now; sapātālam = along with your nethermost subterranean region."

Then, Rama spoke these harsh words to the ocean: "O, ocean! I will make you dry up now along with your nethermost subterranean region."

Sloka 2

śaranirdagdhatoyasya pariśuṣkasya sāgara |
mayā nihatasattvasya pāṃsurutpadyate mahān || 2-22-2

2. sāgara = O; ocean! ṃahān = a vast; pāmsuḥ = sand; utpadyate = will appear; shara nirdagdha toyasya = (when) your water gets consumed by my arrows; parishushhkasya = you get dried up; nihata sattvastya = and the creations inhabiting you get destroyed; mayā = by me.

"O, Ocean! A vast stretch of sand will appear, when your water gets consumed by my arrows; when you get dried up and the creatures inhabiting you get destroyed by me."

Sloka 3

matkārmukanisṛṣṭena śaravarṣeṇa sāgara |
param tīram gamiṣyanti padbhireva plavaṅgamāḥ || 2-22-3

3. sāgara = O; ocean! ṣharavarshheṇa = by a gush of arrows; matkārmuka nisṛishhṭena = released by my bow; plavaṇ^gamāḥ = our monkeys; gamishhyanti = can proceed; param tīram = to the other shore; padbhireva = with even their feet.

"By a gush of arrows released by my bow, our monkeys can proceed to the other shore even by foot; O, ocean!"

Sloka 4

vicinvannābhijānāsi pauruṣam vāpi vikramam |
dāvnavālaya samtāpam matto nāma gamiṣyasi || 2-22-4

4. dānavālaya = O; ṣea the abode of demons! na abhijānāsi = you are not able to recognize; pourushham = my valor ; na vikramapi = nor prowess;vichinvan = by your discernment; gamishhyapi nāma = you will indeed get ; santāpam = repentance; mattaḥ = at my hands.

"O, Sea the abode of demons! You are not able to recognize my valor or prowess through your intelligence .You will indeed get repentance at my hands."

Sloka 5

brāhmeṇāstreṇa samyojya brahmadaṇḍanibham śaram |
samyojya dhanuṣi śre ṣṭhe vicakarṣa mahābalaḥ || 2-22-5

5. mahābalaḥ = the exceedingly powerful Rama; samyojya = fixing; sharam = an arrow; brahmadaṇda nibham = resembling the Rod of Brahma(creator); samyojya = and charged; brahmeṇāstreṇa = with a missile presided over by Brahma; shreshhṭhe dhanushhi = to his excellent bow; vichakarshha = stretched it.

"Fixing an arrow resembling the Rod of Brahma (the creator) charged with a missile (presided over by Brahama) to his excellent bow, the exceedingly powerful Rama stretched it towards the sea."

Sloka 6

tasminvikṛṣṭe sahasā rāghaveṇa śarāsane |
rodasī sampaphāleva parvatāśca cakampire || 2-22-6

6. tasmin = (while)that; sharāsane = bow; vikṛishhṭe = was being stretched; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; rodasī = both the heaven and earth; sahasā = suddenly; sampaphāleva = seemed to be split asunder; parvatāshcha = ṃountains also; chakampire = were shaken.

While Rama was stretching that bow, both the heaven and earth suddenly seemed to be split asunder. ṃountains also were shaken.

Sloka 7

tamaśca lokamāvavre diśaśca na cakāśire |
praticukṣubhire cāśu sarāṃsi saritastadā || 2-22-7

7. tadā = then; tamashcha = darkness; āvavre = enveloped; lokam = the world; dishashcha = the quarters; na chakāshire = did not shine; sarāmsi = lakes; saritaḥ = and rivers; āshu = soon; pratichukshhbhire = were agitated.

Darkness enveloped the world. All the quarters were obscured. Lakes and rivers were soon agitated.

Sloka 8

tiryak ca saha nakṣatraiḥ samgatau candrabhāskarau |
bhāskarāṃśubhirādīptam tamasā ca samāvṛtam || 2-22-8
pracakāśe tadākāśamulkāśatavidīpitam |
antarikṣācca nirghātā nirjagmuratulasvanāḥ || 2-22-9

8; 9. chandra bhāskarou = moon; sun; nakshhatraischa = along with stars; sangatou = moved; tiryak = obliquely; (and though); ākāsham = the sky; ādīptam = was lit; bhāskarāmshubhiḥ = by the sun's rays; samāvṛitam = it was enveloped; tamasācha = by darkness; prachakāshe = and shined; ulkāshata vidīpitam = with a blaze of hundreds of meteors; (while) nirghātāḥ = thunders; nirjagmuḥ = reverberated; atulasvanāḥ = with an unparallel sound; antarikshhāt = in sky.

The moon sun and the stars moved obliquely and though the sun's rays lighted the sky, it was enveloped by darkness and shined with a blaze of hundreds of meteors while thunders reverberated with an unparalleled sound in the sky.

Sloka 10

vapuḥprakarṣeṇa vavurdivyamārutapaṅktyaḥ |
babhañja ca tadā vṛkṣān jaladānudvahan muhuḥ || 2-22-10

10. divyamāruta paṇ^ktayaḥ = series of celestial winds; vavuḥ = blew; vapuḥ prakarshheṇa = in their colossal forms; udvahan = drawing out; jaladān = clouds; muhuḥ = again and again; tadā = then; babhaṇ^jacha = tore up; vṛikshhān = the trees.

Series of celestial winds blew in their colossal form and the winds, then sweeping away the clouds, tore up the trees again and again.

Sloka 11

ārujaṃścaiva śailāgrān śikharāṇi babhañja ca |
divi ca sma mahāvegāḥ samhatāḥ samahāsvanāḥ || 2-22-11
mumucurvaidyutānagnīṃste mahāśanayastadā |

11. (the wind) ārujan cha = shattering; shailāgrān = the mountain peaks; babhaṇ^jacha = broke off; shikharāṇi = the points of the rocks; mahāvegāḥ = winds of great velocity; samhatāḥ = struck together; divi = in the sky; mumuchuḥ = emitted; agnīn = flashes of radiance; vaidyutān = proceeding from lightning; samahā svanāḥ = with a great sound; tadā = and then; te = they (became); mahāshanayaḥ = great thunders.

The wind, shattering the mountain peaks, broke off the points of the rocks. Winds of great velocity struck together in the sky and emitted flashes of radiance proceeding from lightning with a great sound and then there were great thunders.

Sloka 12

yāni bhūtāni dṛśyāni cukruśuścāśaneḥ samam || 2-22-12
adṛśyāni ca bhūtāni mumucurbhairavasvanam |
śiśyare cābhibhūtāni samtrastāmyadvijanti ca || 2-22-13
sampravivyathire cāpi na ca paspandire bhayāt |

12; 13. bhūtāni = the living beings; yāni = which were; dṛishhyāni = visible; chukushruḥ = cried out; ashaneḥ samam = along with the thunders; adṛishyāni = the visible; bhūtānicha = beings too; mumuchuḥ = gave off; bhairava svanam = terrific noise; (the beings) abhibhūtāni = were overpowered; sampravivyathirechāpi = and also very much anguished; na cha paspandiri = they did not move; bhayāt = due to fear.

The visible creatures cried out along with the thunders. The invisible beings too gave off a terrific noise. The creatures were overpowered, frightened, agitated, lied down and also very much anguished. They did not move due to fear.

Sloka 14

sahabhūtaiḥ satoyormiḥ sanāgaḥ saharākṣasaḥ || 2-22-14
sahasābhūttato vegādbhīmavego mahodadhiḥ |
yojanam vyaticakrāma velāmanyatra samplavāt || 2-22-15

14; 15. tataḥ = thereafter; mahodadhiḥ = the great ocean; satoyormiḥ = with its waves and water; sahabhūteḥ = along with creatures; sanāgah = including snakes; saha rākshhasaḥ = and demons; abhūt = became; sahasā = soon; bhīmavegaḥ = possessed with terrific velocity; vegāt = due to speed; samplavāt = and swelling of waters; vyatichakrāma = it crossed beyond; anyatra = the other; velām = shore; yojanam = for a Yojana (eight miles)

The great ocean with its waves and water, along with its living creatures including snakes and demons soon became possessed of a terrific velocity. Due to that speed and swelling of waters, the ocean crossed beyond the other shore to the extent of a Yojana (eight miles).

Sloka 16

tam tathā samatikrāntam nāticakrāma rāghavaḥ |
samuddhatamamitraghno rāmo nadanadīpatim || 2-22-16

16. rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = born in Raghu dynasty; amitraghnaḥ = and the annihilator of enemies; nātichakrāma = did not retreat; (before) tam = that; nadanadīpatim = ocean; samuddhatam = and crossed its limits.

Rama born in Raghu dynasty and the annihilator of enemies, did not retreat before that ocean, which swelled and crossed its limits.

Sloka 17

tato madhyāt samudrasya sāgaraḥ svayam utthitaḥ |
udayan hi mahāśailān meror iva divā karaḥ || 2-22-17

17. tataḥ = then; sāgaraḥ = ṣagara the god of ocean; svayam = himself; utthitah = raised;madhyāt = from the middle; samudrasya = of the ocean; divākaraḥ iva = as the sun;( raising) mahā shailān meroḥ = from the huge mountain of ṃeru; udayam = at dawn.

Then, Sagara (the ocean god) himself rose from the middle of the ocean as the sun rises at dawn from the huge mountain of ṃeru.

Sloka 18

pannagaiḥ saha dīpta āsyaiḥ samudraḥ pratyadṛśyata |
snigdha vaidūrya samkāśo jāmbū nada vibhūṣitaḥ || 2-22-18

18. samudraḥ = the ocean; pannagaiḥ saha = along with snakes; dīptāsyaiḥ = of flaming jaws; pratyadṛishyata = appeared; snigdha vaidūryasamkāshaḥ = with a hue of glossy emerald; jāmbūnada vibhūshhaṇaḥ = adorned with gold.

That ocean along with snakes of flaming jaws appeared with a hue of glossy emerald adorned with gold.

Sloka 25

pṛthivī vāyur ākāśam āpo jyotiḥ ca rāghavaḥ |
svabhāve saumya tiṣṭhanti śāśvatam mārgam āśritāḥ || 2-22-25

25. soumya = O; beloved; rāghava = Rama! Prithivī = earth; vāyuḥ = wind; ākāsham = ether; āpaḥ = water; jyothishcha = and light; tishhṭhanti = remain fixed; svabhāve = in their own nature; āshhritāḥ = taking refuge; shāshvatam = in an eternal; mārgam = path.

"O, beloved Rama! Earth, wind ether, water and light remain fixed in their own nature, resorting to their eternal path."

Sloka 26

26. tat = hence; aham = I; agādhaḥ = am fathomless; mamāpi = and my; svābhaḥ = nature; eshhaḥ = is this; aplavaḥ iti yat = being impossible of being swum across; bhavet = It becomes; vikāraḥ = unnatural; gādhahtu = If I am shallow; pravadām = I am telling; te = you; etat = this (the following device to cross me).

"Therefore, I am fathomless and my nature is that it is impossible of being swum across. It becomes unnatural if I am shallow. I am telling you the following device to cross me."

Sloka 27

"O, prince! Neither from desire nor ambition nor fear nor from affection, I am able to solidify my waters inhabited by alligators."

Sloka 41

vaihāyasau yuvāmetau vānarau dhārayiṣyataḥ || 2-22-81

80; 81. tataḥ = thereafter; sugrivantu = ṣugriva on his part; prāha = spoke; rāmam = to Rama; satyaparākramam = the truly brave man; "Vīra = O;valiant man! ayam = ṭhis; sāgaraḥ = ocean; makarālayaḥ = the abode of alligators; vipulaḥ hi = is indeed vast; tvam = you; āroha = ascend; hanumantam = on ḥanuman; atha = and; lakshhmaṇaḥ = let Lakshmana; (ascend); aṇgadam = Angada; etou = these; vānarou = monkeys; dhārayishhyataḥ = can hold; yuvām = both of you; vaihāyason = while flying in the sky.

Thereafter, Sugriva on his part spoke to Rama, the truly brave man as follows: "O, valiant man! This ocean, the abode of alligators, is indeed vast. You ascend the shoulder of Hanuman and let Lakshmana ascend the shoulder of Angada. These monkeys can hold both of you while flying in the sky."

Sloka 82

agratas tasya sainyasya śrīmān rāmaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |
jagāma dhanvī dharma ātmā sugrīveṇa samanvitaḥ || 2-22-82

82. rāmaḥ = Rama; shrīmān = the glorious; dharmātmā = and the righteous man; dhanvī = wielding a bow; sa lakshhmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana; samanvitaḥ = together; sugrīveṇa = with ṣugriva; jagāma = went; agrataḥ = in front; tasya sainyasya = of that army.

Rama the glorious and righteous man, wielding a bow along with Lakshmana together with Sugriva, went in front of that army.

Sloka 83

anye madhyena gaccanti pārśvato anye plavam gamāḥ |
salile prapatanti anye mārgam anye na lebhire || 2-22-83
kecid vaihāyasa gatāḥ suparṇā iva pupluvuḥ |

83. anye = some; plavaṇgamāḥ = monkeys; gachchhanti = went; madhyena = through the middle(of the bridge); anye = some others; pārshvatah = went through the sides of the bridge. Anye = some others; prapatanti = were jumping into; salilam = water; anye = some others; prapedire = went forward; mārgam = on the path; kechit = some others; vaihāyasagatāḥ = entered the sky; pupluvuḥ = and aviated; suparṇāḥ iva = like Garuda; the eagle.

Some monkeys went along the middle of the bridge. Some others went along the sides. Some others were jumping into water. Some others marched forward on the path. Some monkeys entered the sky and aviated like Garuda the eagle.

Sloka 84

ghoṣeṇa mahatā ghoṣam sāgarasya samuccritam || 2-22-84
bhīmam antar dadhe bhīmā tarantī hari vāhinī |

84. mahatā ghoshheṇa = by the great sound; bhīmā = of the terrific; harivāhinī = army of monkeys; tarantī = who were crossing (the ocean); ghoshham = the sound; sāgarasya = of the ocean; samuchchhritam = which was very high; bhīmam = and terrific; antardadhe = was covered up.

The highly terrific sound of the ocean was covered up by the great sounds of the terrific monkeys who were crossing the sea.

Sloka 85

vānarāṇām hi sā tīrṇā vāhinī nala setunā || 2-22-85
tīre niviviśe rājñā bahu mūla phala udake |

85. sā = that; vāhinī = army; vānarāṇām = of monkeys; tīrṇā = which crossed; nala setunā = the bridge constructed by ṇala; nivivishe = was encamped; tire = at a shore; bahu mūla phaodake = having many fruits roots and water; rāj^ṇā = by ṣugriva.

That army of monkeys, which crossed the ocean by the bridge constructed by Nala, was encamped by Sugriva at a shore having many fruits tubers and water.

Sloka 86

tad adbhutam rāghava karma duṣkaram |
samīkṣya devāḥ saha siddha cāraṇaiḥ |
upetya rāmam sahitā maharṣibhiḥ |
samabhyaṣincan suśubhiar jalaiḥ pṛthak || 2-22-86

86. samīkshhye = ṣeeing; tat = that; rāghava karm = Rama's accomplishment; adbhutam = which was amazing; dushhkaram = and arduous; devāḥ = celestials; ṣiddha chāriṇaiḥ = ṣiddhas(semi-divine beings possessing supernatural faculties) and charanas(celestial bards); maharshhibhiḥ saha = along with great sages; sahasā = forthwith; upetya = approached; rāmaḥ = Rama; abhishhinchan = consecrated;sushubhaiḥ = with very sacred; jalaiḥ = water; pṛithak = separately.

Seeing that Rama's accomplishment, which was amazing and arduous; celestials, Siddhas (semi-divine beings possessing supernatural faculties) and charanas (celestial bards) along with great sages, forthwith approached Rama and consecrated him with very splendid waters separately.

Sloka 87

jayasva śatrūn nara deva medinīm |
sasāgarām pālaya śāśvatīḥ samāḥ |
iti iva rāmam nara deva satkṛtam |
śubhair vacobhir vividhair apūjayan || 2-22-87

87. (ṭhe celestials ṣiddhas and others); āpūjayan = exalted; rāmam = Rama; naradeva satkṛitam = who was respected by kings; vividhaiḥ = with various; shubhaiḥ = auspicious; vachobhiḥ = words; itīva = thus; naradeva = O king! Jayasva = defeat; shatrūn = the enemies; pālaya = rule; medinīm = the earth; sa sāgarān = along with the sea; shāsvatīḥ = eternally; samāḥ = for years.

The celestials, Siddhas and others exalted Rama, who was duly respected by kings with their auspicious -words as follows: " O, king! Defeat the enemies. Rule the earth and ocean eternally for years."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvāviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 22nd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
