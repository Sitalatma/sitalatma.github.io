Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

chapter/sarga 47
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Sept 09

Introduction

The legend of Seven-ṃaruts, the Seven-Wind gods, and the legend of the kings of Vishāla are narrated to Rama and Lakshmana, while Vishvamitra is narrating the legend of Vishāla. At the request of Diti, Indra blesses the seven segments of her foetus to become sapta marut gaṇa-s, Seven Wind gods, and the place where the legend of Indra-Diti has happened, that province later became the City of Vishāla.


Sloka 1

saptadhā tu kṛte garbhe ditiḥ parama duḥkhitā |
sahasrākṣam durādharṣam vākyam sa anunayā abravīt || 1-47-1

1. garbhe saptadhā tu kṛite = foetus, into seven ways, but, on rendering; ditiḥ parama duḥkhitā = Diti, highly, anguished; durādharṣam sahasrākṣam = to indomitable one, ṭhousand-eyed Indra; sa anunayā vākyam abravīt = with, appeasement, words, spoke.

"Diti was highly anguished when her foetus is rendered into sevenfold, and spoke these appeasing words to the indomitable Thousand-eyed Indra." Thus Vishvamitra continued his narration about City Vishala. [1-47-1]

Sloka 2

mama aparādhāt garbho ayam saptadhā śakalī kṛtaḥ |
na aparādho hi deva īśa tava atra balasūdana || 1-47-2

2. bala sūdana = demon Bala, killer of - oh, Indra; deva īsha = oh, gods, ruler of - Indra; mama aparādhāt = my, by misdeed [alone]; ayam garbhaḥ = this, foetus; saptadhā shakalī [viphalī ] kṛitaḥ = in seven, segments [foiled,] made into [sliced]; atra = in that matter; tava aparādhaḥ = your, iniquity; na hi = not, indeed.

" 'Oh, Indra, the eliminator of demon Bala, this foetus of mine is sliced into seven segments owing to my own misdeed, oh, ruler of gods, Indra, indeed there is no iniquity of yours in this matter.' Thus Diti started speaking to Indra. [1-47-2]

Sloka 3

priyam tvat kṛtam icchāmi mama garbha viparyaye |
marutām sapta saptānām sthānapālā bhavantu te || 1-47-3

3. garbha viparyaye [mama garbha viṣaye viparyaye sambhave san] = pregnancy, reversal, [in my, pregnancy, matter of, reversal, happened, though]; = from you; mama = to me; tvat kṛitam = by you, done; priyam icchāmi = a courtesy, I wish to have; [ime = these]; sapta = seven [segments of foetus]; saptānām = as seven; marutām = of Wind-gods; te = in your [ruler-ship]; sthāna pālā = [Airy, Ethereal] places, as rulers; bhavantu = let them become.

" 'Though a reversal has happened against my thinking in the matter of my pregnancy, let these seven segments become your seven Wind-gods, the rulers of seven ethereal-places, under your ruler-ship. [1-47-3]

▼

According to mythology there are seven ethereal places in cosmos on which the galaxies and planets are dependent. According to Vishnu Purāna: āvaha pravahava caiva samvahaḥ ca udvaḥ ca tathā | vihā ākhyaḥ praivāhaḥ parāvaha iti kramāt || gaganaḥ sparshanaḥ vāyu anilaḥ ca tathā aparaḥ | prāṇaḥ prāṇeshvaraḥ jīva iti ete sapta mārutāḥ || They are: āvaha the air called by this name will be pervading in clouds, thunderbolts, rain, meteors; pravaha air in solar orbit; samhava in lunar orbit; udvaha in galaxies; vivaha in planetary spheres; parivaha in the Seven-Sages sphere; varāvaha in north polar regions. These are otherwise called by names gagana, sparshana, vāyu, anila, prāṇa, prāṇeshvara, jīva. Each of the ṃarut god has a batch of seven ṃarut-s, thus they are forty-nine entities, in total.

Sloka 4

vāta skaṃdhā ime sapta carantu divi putraka |
mārutā iti vikhyātā divyarūpā mama ātmajāḥ ||1-47-4

4. putrakāḥ = oh, son, Indra; sapta ime mama ātmajāḥ = seven, of these, my, sons; divya rūpā = with heavenly, forms; vāta skandhā = to air divisions, presiding deities - let them become presiding deities in cosmos; mārutā iti vikhyātā = ṃāruta-s, thus, renowned; carantu divi = let them move, in heaven.

" 'Oh, son Indra, let these seven sons of mine become the seven presiding deities of Cosmic Air Divisions and let them move in heaven with heavenly forms. [1-47-4]

Sloka 5

brahma lokam caratu eka indra lokam tathā aparaḥ |
divya vāyuḥ iti khyātaḥ tṛtīyo api mahāyaśāḥ ||1-47-5

5. ekaḥ = one - of the seven; brahma lokam caratu = Brahma's, abode, move in - permeate; tathā aparaḥ = likewise, another one; indra lokam = in Indra's, heaven; mahā yashāḥ tṛitīyaḥ api = greatly, celebrated, third one, even; vāyuḥ iti khyātaḥ = Wind, thus, reputed as; divya [divi caratu] = Divine, - in Universe, let him circulate.

" 'One from the seven may permeate Brahma's abode, likewise another may permeate the heaven of Indra, and even the third one, let him become a greatly celebrated and reputed as Divine Wind, and he may circulate in entire universe. [1-47-5]

Sloka 6

catvāraḥ tu suraśreṣṭha diśo vai tava śāsanāt |
saṃcariṣyanti bhadram te kalena hi mama ātmajāḥ ||1-47-6
tvat kṛtena eva nāmnā vai mārutā iti viśrutāḥ |

6, 7a. sura shreṣṭha = oh, god's, chief; catvāraḥ mama ātma jāḥ = four, of my, soul, born ones - sons; tava shāsanāt vai = by your, command, indeed; tvat kṛitena eva nāmnā vai = by you, given, thus, by name, indeed; mārutā iti vishrutāḥ = ṃāruta, thus, be renowned; kālena dishaḥ sam cariṣyanti hi = in time, in directions, verily, let them permeate, indeed; te bhadram = let safety betide you.

" 'Oh, chief of gods, Indra, let four of my sons permeate four directions according to time and at your command, let safety betide you, and let them be renowned verily by the name given by you as 'ṃarut,' because you have coaxed the crying fragments of foetus saying, 'mā ruda, mā ruda, 'do not cry, do not cry...' Thus Diti said to Indra. [1-47-6, 7a]

Sloka 7

tasyāḥ tat vacanam śrutvā sahasrākṣaḥ puraṃdaraḥ ||1-47-7
uvāca prāṃjaliḥ vākyam ditim balasūdanaḥ |

7b, 8a. sahasra akṣaḥ = ṭhousand-eyed one; puram daraḥ = enemy-citadels, destroyer; bala sūdanaḥ = demon Bala, destroyer of - Indra; tasyāḥ tat vacanam shrutvā = of her, that, words, on hearing; pra anjaliḥ ditim vākyam uvāca = with adjoined- palms, to Diti, words, said.

"On hearing her words, the Thousand-eyed destroyer of enemy's citadels and a demon named Bala, namely Indra, spoke this word to Diti, humbly adjoining his palms. [1-47-7b, 8a]

Sloka 8Verse Locator

sarvam etat yathā uktam te bhaviṣyati na saṃśayaḥ ||1-47-8
vicariṣyanti bhadram te devarūpāḥ tava ātmajāḥ |
evam tau niścayam kṛtvā mātā putrau tapovane ||1-47-9
jagmatuḥ tridivam rāma kṛtārthau iti naḥ śrutam |

8b, 9, 10a. te = to you [by you]; yathā uktam = as, said; etat sarvam bhaviṣyati = this, all, will happen; samshayaḥ na = doubt, without; tava ātmajāḥ = you, own offspring; deva rūpāḥ = gods, in the form of; vicariṣyanti = will be moving [pervading]; te bhadram = you be safe; so said Indra to Diti; rāma = oh, Rama; tau = those two; mātā putrau = [Diti, the step] mother, [Indra the] son; tapaḥ vane = in ascetic, forest; evam nishcayam kṛitvā = in this way, decision, on taking; kṛita arthāu = fulfilling, their purposes; tridivam jagmatuḥ = to heaven, they departed; iti naḥ shrutam = thus, for us [by us, the legend is] heard.

" 'All this will happen as you have said, undoubtedly, and your offspring, the ṃāruta-s, would be pervading in the forms of gods, you be safe.' Thus Indra assured Diti. Those two, the stepmother Diti and the stepson Indra, on arriving at a decision in that ascetic forest, departed to heaven fulfilling their purposes. Thus we heard the legend." Thus Vishvamitra continued his narration. [1-47-8b, 9, 10a]

Sloka 10

eṣa deśaḥ sa kākutstha mahendrāt adyuṣitaḥ purā || 1-47-10
ditim yatra tapaḥ siddhām evam paricacāra saḥ |

10b, 11. kākutstha = oh, Rama, of Kakutstha; eṣa = this is; purā = once; mahendrā adhyuṣitaḥ = by Indra, presided over; saḥ deshaḥ = that, province; yatra = where; saḥ = he - Indra; tapaḥ siddhām = in her asceticism, an accomplished one; ditim = to Diti; evam paricacāra = in this way, rendered services.

"Oh, Rama of Kakutstha, this province is that one which was once presided over by Indra, and where he rendered services to that accomplished one in her asceticism, namely lady Diti. [1-47-10b, 11a]

Sloka 11 Verse Locator

ikṣvākostu naravyāghra putraḥ parama dhārmikaḥ || 1-47-11
alaṃbuṣāyām utpanno viśāla iti viśrutaḥ |
tena ca āsīt iha sthāne viśāle iti purī kṛtā || 1-47-12

11b, 12. naravyāghra = oh, tigerly-man, Rama; ikṣvākoḥ tu = to King Ikshvaku, on his part; alambuṣāyām = throguh queen Alambusa; parama dhārmikaḥ = most, righteous one [is born]; vishāla iti vishrutaḥ = Vishāla, as, renowned; putraḥ utpannaḥ = a son, is born; tena = by him - owing to him; iha sthāne = at this, place; vishāla iti purī kṛitā = Vishāla, as known as,] city, constructed; āsīt = is there.

"To king Ikshvaku of Vishāla, oh, tigerly-man Rama, a highly righteous son was born through queen Alambusa, and he was renowned as Vishāla. There is a city in this place constructed by him and known by his own name, Vishāla. [1-47-11b, 12]

▼

These Ikshvaku-s of Vishāla are not the Ikshvaku-s of Ayodhya, i.e., of Solar Dynasty. The Bhāgavata Purāna says separately in its seventh canto about this dynasty of Ikshvaku-s of Vishāla.

Sloka 13

viśālasya suto rāma hemacandro mahābalaḥ |
sucandra iti vikhyāto hemacandrāt anaṃtaraḥ || 1-47-13

13. rāma = oh, Rama; mahābalaḥ hemacandraḥ = great-mighty one, ḥemachandra; vishālasya sutaḥ = Vishala's, son; hemacandrāt anantaraḥ = from ḥemachandra, latter one - son of ḥemacandra; sucandra iti vikhyātaḥ = ṣuchandra, as, highly renowned one.

"Oh, Rama, Vishāla's son is the great-mighty Hemachandra, and Hemachandra's son is highly renowned as Suchandra.. [1-47-13]

Sloka 14

sucandra tanayo rāma dhūmra aśva iti viśrutaḥ |
dhūmrāśva tanayaḥ ca api sṛṃjayaḥ samapadyata || 1-47-14

14. rāma = oh, Rama; sucandra tanayaḥ = ṣuchandra's, son; dhūmrāshva iti vishrutaḥ = Dhūmra ashva, as, renowned; dhūmrāshva tanayaḥ ca api = Dhūmrāshva, son, also, even; sṛinjayaḥ samapadyata = ṣrinjaya, derived.

"The son of Suchandra became famous as Dhūmrāshva, and oh, Rama, Dhūmrāshva derived his son named Srinjaya. [1-47-14]

Sloka 15

sṛṃjayasya sutaḥ śrīmān sahadevaḥ pratāpavān |
kuśāśvaḥ sahadevasya putraḥ parama dhārmikaḥ || 1-47-15

15. shrīmān pratāpavān = fortunate one, valorous one; sahadevaḥ = ṣahadeva; sṛinjayasya sutaḥ = is ṣrinjaya's, son; parama dhārmikaḥ kushāshvaḥ sahadevasya putraḥ = most righteous, Kushāshva is, ṣahadeva's, son.

"The fortunate and valorous son of Srinjaya is Sahadeva, and the son of Sahadeva is the most righteous Kushāshva. [1-47-15]

Sloka 16

kuśāśvasya mahātejāḥ somadattaḥ pratāpavān |
somadattasya putraḥ tu kākutstha iti viśrutaḥ ||1-47-16

16. mahātejāḥ pratāpavān somadattaḥ = highly resplendent, dauntless, ṣomadatta is; kushāshvasya [putraḥ] = Kushāshva's, [son]; somadattasya putraḥ tu = ṣomadatta's, son, on his part; kākutstha iti vishrutaḥ = Kakutstha, thus, highly reputed one.

"The highly resplendent and dauntless Somadatta is the son of Kushāshva and Somadatta's son is highly reputed as Kakutstha. [1-47-16]

Sloka 17

tasya putro mahātejāḥ saṃprati eṣa purīm imām |
āvasat parama prakhyaḥ sumatiḥ nāma durjayaḥ || 1-47-17

17. samprati = presently; eṣa imām purīm = in this, city; tasya = his [Kakutstha's]; putraḥ mahā tejāḥ = son [of Kakutstha,] great-resplendent one; parama [amara] prakhyaḥ = highly [godlike,] celebrated one; dur jayaḥ = un, conquerable one; sumatiḥ nāma = ṣumati named one; āvasat = is dwelling.

"Presently that Kakutstha's son named Sumati, who is a great-resplendent one, an unconquerable one, and a highly celebrated one is dwelling in this city. [1-47-17]

▼

These two Ikshvaku and Kakutstha are of Vishāla dynasty. They are not to be confounded with the grandparents of Rama.

Sloka 18

ikṣvākostu prasādena sarve vaiśālikā nṛpāḥ |
dīrgha āyuṣo mahātmāno vīryavantaḥ sudhārmikāḥ || 1-47-18

18. ikṣvākaḥ tu prasādena = by Ikshvaku, but, by the grace of; vaishālikāḥ nṛipāḥ = relating to Vishāla, [successive] kings; sarve = all of them; dīrgha āyuṣaḥ = had long, life [had longevity]; mahātmānaḥ = great souled ones; vīrya vantaḥ = intrepid ones; su dhārmikāḥ = highly, generous ones [well-off, in generosity.]

"By the grace of Ikshvaku, the successive kings of Vishāla have longevity, intrepidity, well-off in generosity, and they were great-souled ones. [1-47-18]

Sloka 19

iha adya rajanīm ekām sukham svapsyāmahe vayam |
śvaḥ prabhāte naraśreṣṭha janakam draṣṭum arhasi || 1-47-19

19. nara shreṣṭha = oh, best among men - Rama; adya ekām rajanīm = today, one, night; iha = here alone; vayam sukham svapsyāmahe = we, gladly, go to sleep; shvaḥ prabhāte = tomorrow, morning; janakam draṣṭum arhasi = Janaka [King of ṃithila,] to see, will be apt of you.

"Today we may gladly go to sleep here for a night, and oh, best one among men, and it will be apt of you to see Janaka, the King of ṃithila tomorrow morning." Thus Sage Vishvamitra spoke to Rama and Lakshmana. [1-47-19]

Sloka 20

sumatiḥ tu mahātejā viśvāmitram upāgatam |
śrutvā nara vara śreṣṭhaḥ pratyāgacchan mahāyaśāḥ || 1-47-20

20. mahātejāḥ mahā yashāḥ = highly resplendent one, widely, reputed one; nara vara shreṣṭhaḥ = among men, best, best of [best among best men]; sumatiḥ tu = ṣumati [King of Vishāla,] on his part; vishvāmitram upa agatam = Vishvamitra, to nearby, came - arrived at the outskirts of city; shrutvā = on hearing; prati āgacchat = towards, came - for warm welcome.

The best one among best men, a widely reputed and the highly resplendent king of Vishāla, namely Sumati, having heard that Vishvamitra has arrived at the precincts of the city, came to him for a warm welcome. [1-47-20]

Sloka 21

pūjām ca paramām kṛtvā sa upādhyāyaḥ sabāndhavaḥ |
prāṃjaliḥ kuśalam pṛṣṭvā viśvāmitram atha abravīt || 1-47-21

21. sa upādhyāyaḥ sa bāndhavaḥ = with, mentors, with family members - ṣumati on arriving with; paramām pūjām kṛitvā = excellent, veneration, on performing; pra anjaliḥ = with adjoined palms; kushalam pṛiṣṭvā = well being, on enquiring about; atha = then; vishvāmitram abravīt = to Vishvamitra, spoke.

King Sumati arrived along with his mentors and family members, offered an excellent veneration to Vishvamitra, and then spoke to the sage with adjoined palms enquiring about his well being. [1-47-21]

Sloka 22

dhanyo asmi anugṛhīto asmi yasya me viṣayam mune |
saṃprāpto darśanam caiva na asti dhanyataro mama || 1-47-22

22. yasya me viṣayam = to which, mine, province - to which province, that of mine; mune = oh, saint; sam prāptaḥ darshanam = well, chanced, your audience; such as I am I am; dhanyaḥ asmi = fortunate, I am; anugṛihītaḥ asmi = much obliged, I am; mama dhanya taraḥ = me, fortunate one, than, higher in degree; na asti = not, is there.

"To which province you have visited that happens to be mine, thereby I could get an audience of yours without stirring myself from my country, hence none will be more fortunate than me." So said king Sumati to Vishvamitra. [1-47-22]

Verse Locator

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye bāla kāṇḍe sapta catvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 47th chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
