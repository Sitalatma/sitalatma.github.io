Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 3
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

On hearing the words of Sugreeva, Rama requests Hanuma to describe Lanka in detail. Hanuma gives a detailed description of Lanka as he saw it.

Sloka 1

sugrīvasya vacaḥ śrutvā hetumat parama arthavit |
pratijagrāha kākutstho hanūmantam atha abravīt || 6-3-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; hetumat = the well-founded; paramārthavat = and highly meaningful; vachaḥ = words; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva; kākutsthoḥ = Rama; pratijagrāha = concurred with him; atha = (and) then; abravīt = spoke; hanūmantam = to ḥanuman(as follows)

Hearing the well-founded and highly reasonable words of Sugreeva, Rama concurred with him and spoke then to Hanuma as follows:

Sloka 2

tarasā setu bandhena sāgara uccoṣaṇena vā |
sarvathā susamartho asmi sāgarasya asya langhane || 6-3-2

2. asmi = I am; samarthoḥ = competent of; laṇ^ghane = crossing; asya sāgarasya = this ocean; sarvathāpi = by all means; tapasā = either by austerity; setubandhena = or by forming a bridge; sāgarochchhoshhaṇena = or by drying up the ocean.

"I am competent of crossing this ocean by all means, either by way of austerity or by forming a bridge or by drying up the ocean."

Sloka 3

kati durgāṇi durgāyā laṃkāyās tad bravīhi me |
jñātum iccāmi tat sarvam darśanād iva vānara || 6-3-3

3. bravīshhva = tell; me = me; durgāṇi = citadels; laṇ^kāyāḥ = in Lanka; durgāyāḥ = are difficult of access; vānara = Oh ḥanuman! ichchhāmi = I desire; jJṇātum = to know; tat = that; darshanādiva = as though perceived with an eye.

"Tell me how many citadels in Lanka are difficult of access, Oh, Hanuma! I desire to know all that, as though viewed with an eye."

Sloka 4

balasya parimāṇam ca dvāra durga kriyām api |
gupti karma ca laṃkāyā rakṣasām sadanāni ca || 6-3-4
yathā sukham yathāvac ca laṃkāyām asi dṛṣṭavān |
saram ācakṣva tattvena sarvathā kuśalo hi asi || 6-3-5

4;5. asi = you were; dṛishhṭavān = the on looker; yathā sukham = as per your convenience; yathāvachcha = exactly; parimāṇam cha = the size; balasya = of the army; dvāradurga kriyām api = fortification of the gates and citadels; laṇ^kāyāḥ guptikarmacha = the way in which Lanka was guarded; sadanāni cha = and the mansions; rakshasām = of ogres; sarvema = every thing; āchakshhva = tell; tattvena = in accordance with facts; asi hi = you are indeed; kushalaḥ = skilled; sarvathā = in all ways.

"You had the opportunity to catch a glimpse of the size of the army, the fortification-details of the gates and citadels, the way in which Lanka was guarded and the various mansions of ogres. Describe everything in accordance with facts, for you are skilled in all ways."

Sloka 6

śrutvā rāmasya vacanam hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
vākyam vākyavidām śreṣṭho rāmam punar atha abravīt || 6-3-6

6. shrutvā = hearing; rāmasya = Rama's; vachanam = words; hanumān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; shreshhṭhah = and who was excellent; vākyavidām = in the art of expression; atha = forthwith; abravīt = spoke; punaḥ = once more; rāmam = to Rama (as follows):

Hearing the words of Rama, Hanuma the son of wind-god, who was excellent in the art of expression, forthwith spoke once more to Rama as follows:

Sloka 7

śrūyatām sarvam ākhyāsye durga karma vidhānataḥ |
guptā purī yathā laṃkā rakṣitā ca yathā balaiḥ || 6-3-7

7. shrūyatām = let it be listened! ākhyāsye = I shall tell (you); sarvam = everything; yathā = how; laṇ^kā purī = the city of Lanka; guptā = has been defended; durgakarma vidhānataḥ = by the various methods of fortifications; yathā = (and) how; rakshhitācha = (it) has been guarded; balaiḥ = by the troops.

"Listen to me! I shall tell you everything-how the city of Lanka has been defended by the various methods of fortification and how it has been guarded by the troops."

Sloka 8

rākṣasāśca yathā snigdhā rāvaṇasya ca tejasā |
parām samṛddhim laṃkāyāḥ sāgarasya ca bhīmatām || 6-3-8
vibhāgam ca bala oghasya nirdeśam vāhanasya ca |
evamuktvā kapiśreṣṭhaḥ kathayāmāsa tatvavit || 6-3-9

8;9. nirdeshamcha = (I shall tell You) the details; yathā = how; rākshhāsāḥ = ogres; snigdhāḥ = are attached (to their king); parām = the excellent; samṛiddhim = prosperity; laṇ^kāyāḥ = of Lanka; tejasā = (generated) by the glory; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; bhīmatām = the awfulness; sāgarasya = of the ocean; vibhāgam = the division; balaughasya = of the body of his force; vāhanasya = (and) of animals like horses and elephants (carrying his forces other than infantry); uktvā = saying so; kapishreshhṭhaḥ- ḥanuma the foremost among monkeys; tattvavit = who knew the truth; kathayāmāsa = told; evam = thus.

"I shall tell you the details of how the ogres are attached to their king, the excellent prosperity of Lanka generated by the glory of Ravana, the awfulness of the ocean, the division of the body of his forces and of animals like horses and elephants carrying his forces other than the infantry." Saying so, Hanuma the foremost among monkeys, who knew the truth narrated as follows.

Sloka 10

prahṛṣṭā muditā laṃkā matta dvipa samākulā |
mahatī ratha sampūrṇā rakṣo gaṇa samākulā || 6-3-10

10. mahatī = the great; laṇ^kā = Lanka; hṛishhṭa pramuditā = was rejoiced and gayful; matta dvipasamākulā = full of elephants in rut; rathā sampūrṇā = abounding in chariots; rakshhogaṇa nishhevitā = and inhabited by gangs of ogres.

"The great Lanka was rejoiced and gayful, full of elephants in rut, abounding in chariots and inhabited by gangs of ogres."

Sloka 11

dṛḍha baddha kavāṭāni mahāparighavanti ca |
catvāri vipulānyasyā dvārāṇi sumahānti || 6-3-11

11. chatvāri = four; sumahānti = fairly big; vipulāni = and extensive; dvārāṇi = gates; asyāḥ = of the this Lanka; dṛiDhabaddhakapāṭāni = are fitted with strong doors; mahāparighavanti = with huge beans for locking the gates.

"Four fairly big and extensive gates are fitted with strong doors along with huge beams for locking those gates."

Sloka 12

tatreṣūpayantrāṇi balavanti mahānti ca |
āgatam para sainyam tais tatra pratinivāryate || 6-3-12

12. tatra = there; balavanti = strong; mahānti cha = and mighty; isshūpalayantrāṇi = ballista capable of hurling darts and stones; pratisainyam = the enemy troops; āgatam = which come; tatra = there; pratinivāryate = are warded off; taiḥ = by those catapults.

"Strong and mighty ballista capable of hurling darts and stones have been attached to them. The enemy troops which arrive there are warded off by those catapults at the very gates."

Sloka 13

dvāreṣu saṃskṛtā bhīmāḥ kāla āyasa mayāḥ śitāḥ |
śataśo rocitā vīraiḥ śataghnyo rakṣasām gaṇaiḥ || 6-3-13

13. bhīmāḥ = Dangerous; shitāḥ = sharp edged; shataghnyaḥ = ṣataghnis; kālāyasa mayāḥ = made of iron; rachitāḥ = forged; gaṇaiḥ = by hordes; rakshasām = of ogres; samskṛitaḥ = have been kept in readiness; shatashaḥ = in hundreds; dvāreshhu = at the gates.

"Dangerous and sharp edged Sataghnis made of iron, forged by hordes of ogres, have been kept in readiness in hundreds at the gates.

▼

Comment: Sataghnis are four forearms in length bristled with iron spikes and are so called because they are supposed to kill hundreds at a time.

Sloka 14

sauvarṇaḥ ca mahāṃs tasyāḥ prākāro duṣpradharṣaṇaḥ |
maṇi vidruma vaidūrya muktā vicarita antaraḥ || 6-3-14

14. mahā prākāraḥ = A great rampart; sauvarṇaḥ = of gold; tasyāḥ = (encircle) that Lanka; dushhpadharshhaṇaḥ = which is difficult to assail with violence; maṇividrumavaidūryamuktāvirachitāntaraḥ = and is inlaid at intervals with gems; corals; cat's eyes and pearls.

"A great rampart made of gold, which is difficult to assail with violence and is inlaid at intervals with gems, corals, cat's eyes and pearls, encircle that Lanka."

Sloka 15

sarvataḥ ca mahābhīmāḥ śīta toyā mahāśubhāḥ |
agādhā grāhavatyaḥ ca parikhā mīna sevitāḥ || 6-3-15

15. mahābhīmāḥ = most fearful; agādhāḥ = and fathomless; parikhāḥ = deep trenches; mahā shubhaiḥ = of great splendor; shītatoyāḥ = filled with cold water; grāhavatyashcha = infested with alligators; mina sevitāḥ = and inhabited by fishes; sarvataḥ = (are there) all round (the city).

"ṃost awe-inspiring and fathomless moats (deep trenches) of great splendor, filled with cold water, infested with alligators and inhabited by fishes are there all round the city."

Sloka 16

dvāreṣu tāsām catvāraḥ samkramāḥ parama āyatāḥ |
yantrair upetā bahubhir mahadbhir dṛḍha saṃdhibhiḥ || 6-3-16

16. dvāreshhu = in front of the gateways; chatvāraḥ = (there are) four; paramāyatāḥ = very extensive; tāsām samkramāḥ = draw-bridges; bahubhiḥ = equipped with numerous; yantraiḥ = engines; upetāḥ = and furnished with; mahadbhiḥ = considerable; buildings (built on the rampart and meant for those entrusted with the duty of guarding the entrances).

"Anterior to the gateways, there are four very extensive draw-bridges, equipped with numerous engines and furnished with many rows of buildings (built on the rampart and meant for those entrusted with the duty of guarding the entrances)."

Sloka 17

trāyante samkramās tatra para sainya āgame sati |
yantrais tair avakīryante parikhāsu samantataḥ || 6-3-17

17. parasainyāgatesati = on the arrival of hostile force; tatra = there; samkramāḥ = are protected; taiḥ = by the aforesaid; yantraiḥ = engines; avakīryante = (and the battalions) are flung; parikhāsu = into the moats; samantataḥ = on every side.

"Against the approach of the enemy forces there, the draw-bridges are protected by the aforesaid engines and the enemy-battalions are flung into the moats on every side."

Sloka 18

ekas tv akampyo balavān samkramaḥ sumahādṛḍhaḥ |
kāñcanair bahubhiḥ stambhair vedikābhiḥ ca śobhitaḥ || 6-3-18

18. ekaḥ = the principal; samkramastu = draw-bridge; akampyaḥ = which is unshakable; sumahādṛiDhaḥ = firmly fastened very well; balavān = and strong; shobhitaḥ = is embellished with bahubhiḥ = numerous; kāJṇchanaiḥ = gold; stambhaiḥ = pillars vedikābhishcha = and pedestals.

"The most important draw-bridge which is unshakable, firmly fastened very well and strong, is dazzling with numerous gold pillars and pedestals."

Sloka 19

svayam prakṛti sampanno yuyutsū rāma rāvaṇaḥ |
utthitaḥ ca apramattaḥ ca balānām anudarśane || 6-3-19

19. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; yuyutsuḥ = inclined to war; āpannaḥ = endowed with; prakṛitam = powers of the state; svayam = himself; apramattashcha = remains alert; utthitashcha = and in readiness; anudarshane = to review; balānām = the forces; rāma = Oh; Rama!

"Ravana, inclined to war and endowed with powers of the state, himself remains alert and in readiness to review the forces, Oh, Rama!"

▼

Seven powers of the state are usually enumerated, viz. king, minister, allies, treasure, army, territory and fortresses.

Sloka 20

laṃkā purī nirālambā deva durgā bhaya āvahā |
na adeyam pārvatam vanyam kṛtrimam ca catur vidham || 6-3-20

20. laṇ^kā punaḥ = "Lanka again; nirālamba = is impregnable; devadurgā = a celestial citadel; bhayāvahā = that inspires terror; nādeyam = surrounded by flowing water; pārvatam = built on a mountain; chaturvidham = with its four fold defenses; vānyam = consisting of forest; kṛittimamcha = and by artificial fortification.

"Lanka therefore is impregnable, it is a celestial citadel, that inspires terror. Surrounded by water and built on a mountain, it has four fold defenses including forest and artificial fortification."

▼

Comment: Fourfold defences, defence by water, mountain, forest or by artificial means.

Sloka 21

sthitā pāre samudrasya dūra pārasya rāghava |
nau pathaḥ ca api na asti atra nirādeśaḥ ca sarvataḥ || 6-3-21

21. sthitā = (Lanka) is situated; pare = on the other side; samudrasya = of the ocean; dūrapārasya = which is difficult of access; rāghava = Oh Rama! nāsti = there is no; naupathashcha = reach for vessels; atra = there; sarvashaḥ = on all sides; nirādeshashcha = there is no communication."

"Lanka is situated on the other side of the ocean, which is difficult of access, Oh Rama! It offers no passage for vehicles either and there is no proper communication from all sides."

Sloka 22

śaila agre racitā durgā sā pūr deva pura upamā |
vāji vāraṇa sampūrṇā laṃkā parama durjayā || 6-3-22

22. sā = that; pūḥ = city (of Lanka); deva puropamā = resembling the City of Gods; rachitā = is built; shailāgre = on a mountain peak; durgāḥ = (and is inaccessible; sā = that; laṇ^kā = Lanka; vājivāraṇa sampūrṇā = is abound with horses and elephants; paramadurjayā = and is extremely difficult to conquer.

"That city of Lanka, resembling the city of Gods, is built on a mountain peak and is inaccessible. That Lanka is abound with horses and elephants and is extremely difficult to conquer."

Sloka 23

parighāḥ ca śataghnyaḥ ca yantrāṇi vividhāni ca |
śobhayanti purīm laṃkām rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 6-3-23

23. parikhāshcha = deep trenches; shataghnyashcha = and ṣataghnis too; yantrāṇi cha = as well as engines; vividhāni = of various kinds; shobhayanti = adorn; laṇ^kām = Lanka; purīm = the city; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; durātmanaḥ = the evil-minded.

Deep trenches and Sataghnis too as well as engines of war of every kind adorn Lanka the city of wicked Ravana."

Sloka 24

ayutam rakṣasām atra paścima dvāram āśritam |
śūla hastā durādharṣāḥ sarve khaḍga agra yodhinaḥ || 6-3-24

24. ayutam = ten thousand; rakshasām = ogres; sarve = all; shūlahastāḥ = carrying darts in their hands; khadgāgrayodhinaḥ = warriors contending with swords; durādharshhāḥ = who are difficult to assail; samāshritam = are positioned; pūrvam dvāram = at the eastern gate.

"Ten thousand ogres all carrying darts in their hands and warriors contending with swords, who are difficult to assail, are positioned at the eastern gate."

Sloka 25

niyutam rakṣasām atra dakṣiṇa dvāram āśritam |
catur angeṇa sainyena yodhās tatra api anuttamāḥ || 6-3-25

25. niyutam = one hundred thousand; rakshasām = of ogres; āshritam = are positioned; atra = there; dakshhiṇa dvāram = at the southern gate; tatrāpi = there also; yodhāḥ = warriors; anuttamāḥ = unsurpassed by others; sainyena = constitute the army; chaturaṇ^geṇa = with its four limbs(viz. horses; foot soldiers; elephants and chariots).

"One hundred thousand of ogres, with an army of four limbs (viz. horses, foot soldiers, elephants and chariots) are positioned at the southern gate of the city. Warriors unsurpassed by others constitute that army."

Sloka 26

prayutam rakṣasām atra pūrva dvāram samāśritam |
carma khaḍga dharāḥ sarve tathā sarva astra kovidāḥ || 6-3-26

26. prayutam = one million; rakshasām = ogres; atra = there; āshritam = are positioned; pashchima dvāram = at the western gate; sarve = all of them; charmakhaDgadharāḥ = carry shields and swords; tathā = and; sarvāstrakovidaḥ = proficient in the use of all mystic missiles.

One million troops arrived with shields and swords as well as proficient in the use of all mystic missiles, are positioned at the western gate."

Sloka 27

nyarbudam rakṣasām atra uttara dvāram āśritam |
rathinaḥ ca aśva vāhāḥ ca kula putrāḥ supūjitāḥ || 6-3-27

27. nyarbudam = a hundred millions; rakshasām = of ogres; atra = there; āshritam = are positioned; uttaradvāram = at the northern gate; rathinaḥ = (they are) car-warriors; ashvavāhāshcha = horse-soldiers; kulaputrāḥ = sons of noble families; supūjitāḥ = and greatly honored."

"A hundred millions of ogres, who are mounted in chariots or who ride on hoses, sons of distinguished families and greatly honored, are positioned there at the northern gate."

Sloka 28

śatam śata sahasrāṇām madhyamam gulmam āśritam |
yātu dhānā durādharṣāḥ sāgra koṭiḥ ca rakṣasām || 6-3-28

28. atha = moreover; yātudhānāḥ = ogres; shatashaḥ sahasrāṇi = hundreds of thousands in number; durādarshhāḥ = difficult to assail; sāgrakoṭishcha = aggregating to one and quarter of a crore; rakshasām = of ogres; āshritāḥ = are positioned; madhyamaskandham = at the central division."

"ṃoreover, ogres hundreds of thousands in number, difficult to attack, aggregating to one and quarter of a crore of ogres are positioned at the central division."

Sloka 29

te mayā samkramā bhagnāḥ parikhāḥ ca avapūritāḥ |
dagdhā ca nagarī laṃkā prākārāḥ ca avasāditāḥ || 6-3-29
balaikadeśaḥ kṣapito rākṣasānām mahātmanām |

29. te = those; samkramāḥ = bridges; bhagnāḥ = were broken; mayā = by me; parikhāshcha = moats also; avapūritāḥ = filled up (with the wreckage); laṇ^kā nagarīm = the city of Lanka; daghācha = was burnt; prākārāshcha = defensive walls; avasāditāḥ = pulled down; balaikadeshaḥ = a part of the army; mahātmanām = of the gigantic; rākshasānām = ogres; kshapitaḥ = were destroyed.

"Those bridges were broken by me and the moats were filled up (with the wreckage). The city of Lanka was burnt by me and the defensive walls were pulled down. A part of the army of the gigantic ogres was destroyed."

Sloka 30

yena kena tu mārgeṇa tarāma varuṇa ālayam || 6-3-30
hatā iti nagarī laṃkām vānarair avadhāryatām |

30. yena kena tu mārgeṇa = by some way or other; tarāma = we shall cross; varuṇālayam = the ocean; laṇ^kānagarī = the city of Lanka; upadhāryatām = should be assumed; hatā iti = as destroyed; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys."By some way or other; let us cross the ocean. ṭhe city of Lanka should be assumed then as destroyed by the monkeys."

"By some way or other, let us cross the ocean. The city of Lanka should be assumed then as destroyed by the monkeys."

Sloka 31

aṅgado dvivido maindo jāmbavān panaso nalaḥ || 6-3-31
nīlaḥ senā patiḥ caiva bala śeṣeṇa kim tava |
plavamānā hi gatvā tām rāvaṇasya mahāpurīm || 6-3-32
saprakārām sabhavanām ānayiṣyanti maithilīm |
saprākārām sabhavanāmānayuṣyanti rāghava || 6-3-33

31;32;33. rāghava = Oh; Rama! kim = what is the use; balasheshhaṇa = for the rest of the forces; tava = to you? aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; dividaḥ = Divida; maindaḥ = ṃainda; analaḥ = Anala; nīlashchaiva = and ṇila alone; senāpatiḥ = the commander-in-chief; gatvā = by reaching; tām = that; rāvaṇasya mahāpurīm = great city of Ravana; plavamānāḥ = leaping; bhitvā = and storming (it) saparvatavanām = with its mountains and woods; sakhātām = with moats; satoraṇām = with arch-ways; saprākārām = with protective walls; sabhavanām = with buildings; ānayishhyanti = and recover (ṣeetha).

"Oh, Rama! What is the use for the rest of the forces to you? Angada, Divida, ṃainda, Jambavan, Panasa, Anala and Nila the commander-in-chief alone, by reaching that great city of Ravana leaping and storming Lanka with its mountains and woods, moats and archways, protective walls and buildings, will recover Seetha."

Sloka 34

evam ājñāpaya kṣipram balānām sarva samgraham |
muhūrtena tu yuktena prasthānamabhirocaya || 6-3-34

34. āJṇāpaya = order; kshhipram = quickly; balānām sarvasangraham = to get all the forces; evam = accordingly; abhirochaya = be inclined; prasthānam = for the march; yuktena = at the appropriate moment.

"Order quickly to get all the forces accumulated accordingly. Let us set out at the appropriate moment."

- - -

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe tṛtīyaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 3rd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
