Valmiki Ramayana

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 64

Introduction

Coming back to ṃadhuvana as instructed by Sugreeva, and craving forgiveness of Angada, Dadhimukha submits to him that he along with the other monkeys is urgently required by Sugreeva. Angada and other monkeys including Hanuma spring forward into the air towards Kishkindha. Even before the arrival of those monkeys, Sugreeva explains to Rama the valid reasons for concluding that Seetha has since been found. Angada, Hanuma and other, monkeys approach Sugreeva. Then, Hanuma apprises Rama of the discovery of Seetha. Hearing the news, Rama and Lakshmana were thrilled with joy and looked on Hanuma with a high esteem.


Sloka 1

sugrīveṇa evam uktaḥ tu hṛṣṭo dadhi mukhaḥ kapiḥ |
rāghavam lakṣmaṇam caiva sugrīvam ca abhyavādayat || 5-64-1

1. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; dadhi mukhaḥ = Dadhimukha; kapiḥ = the monkey; hṛiṣṭaḥ = was rejoiced; abhyavādayat = and offered his salutation with reverence; rāghavam = to Rama; lakṣmaṇam chaiva = Lakshmana; sugrīvam cha = and ṣugreeva.

ḥearing the words of ṣugreeva, Dadhimukha the monkey was pleased and offered his salutation with reverence to Rama, Lakshmana

Sloka 2

sa praṇamya ca sugrīvam rāghavau ca mahābalau |
vānaraiḥ sahitaiḥ śūraiḥ divam eva utpapāta ha || 5-64-2

2. praṇamye = after offering salutation; sugrīvam = to ṣugreeva; mahābalau rāghavau cha = and to the mighty Rama and Lakshmana; saḥ = that Dadhimukha; sahitaḥ = together; shūraiḥ vānaraiḥ = with the valiant monkeys; utpapāta = sprang; divam eva = into the air.

After offering salutation to ṣugreeva and to the mighty Rama and Lakshmana, that Dadhimukha accompanied by his valiant monkeys, sprang into the air.

Sloka 3

sa praviṣṭo madhu vanam dadarśa hari yūthapān |
vimadān uddhatān sarvān mehamānān madhu udakam || 5-64-3

3. saḥ = that Dadhimukha; praviṣṭaḥ = who entered; madhu vanam = ṃadhuvana; dadarsha = saw; sarvān = all; hari yūthapān = the chiefs of monkeys; vimadān = who were free from intoxication; madātyaye = having passed off the honey; vepamānān = and who were inwardly excited.

ṭhat Dadhimukha, after entering ṃadhuvana, saw all the chiefs of monkeys, who were free from their intoxication, having passed off the honey and who were inwardly excited.

Sloka 4

sa tān upāgamat vīro baddhvā kara puṭa añjalim |
uvāca vacanam ślakṣṇam idam hṛṣṭavat angadam || 5-64-4

4. baddhvā kara puṭa aṇ^jalim = with his hands joined for salutation; saḥ vīraḥ = that valiant Dadhimukha; upāgamat = approached; tān = them; hṛiṣhṭavat = and cheerfully; uvācha = spoke; idam shlakṣṇam vachanam = these gentle words; aṇ^gadam = to Angada.

With his hands joined together for salutation, that valiant Dadhimukha approached them and cheerfully spoke the following gentle words to Angada.

Sloka 5

saumya roṣo na kartavyo yat ebhiḥ abhivāritaḥ |
ajñānāt rakṣibhiḥ krodhāt bhavantaḥ pratiṣedhitāḥ || 5-64-5

5. saumya = O gentle sir!; na roṣaḥ = no anger; na kartavyaḥ = should be felt (by you); yat parivāritam = about the obstruction; etat = (made) in this way; bhavantaḥ = you; pratiṣheditāḥ = were prevented; rakṣibhiḥ = by the guards; ajJṇānāt = due to their ignorance; krodhāt = and anger.

"O gentle sir! Due to their ignorance, these guards in anger obstructed you to enter the grove. No resentment should be harboured by you about this obstruction."

Sloka 6

yuva rājaḥ tvam īśaḥ ca vanasya asya mahābala |
maurkhyāt pūrvam kṛto doṣaḥ tat bhavān kṣantum arhati || 5-64-6

6. mahābala = O exceedingly strong Angada!; tvam = you; yuva rājaḥ = are the prince; īshasḥ cha = and also the master; asya vanasya = of this grove; doṣhaḥ = a mistake; kṛitaḥ = was done; pūrvam = formerly; maurkhāt = due to our foolishness; bhavān = you; arhati = mighty; kṣantum = to forgive; tam = it.

"O exceedingly strong Angada! You are the prince and also the master of this grove. We have done a mistake

Sloka 7

ākhyātam hi mayā gatvā pitṛvyasya tava anagha |
iha upayānam sarveṣām eteṣām vana cāriṇām || 5-64-7

7. anagha = O faultless Angada!; ākhyātam hi = it was indeed informed; tava pitṛivyasya = to your uncle; gatvā = after going there; upayātam = of the coming; sarveṣhām = of all; eteṣhām = these; vana chāriṇām = monkeys; iha = here.

"O faultless Angada! After going there, I indeed informed your uncle about the arrival of all these monkeys here."

Sloka 8

sa tvat āgamanam śrutvā saha ebhiḥ hari yūthapaiḥ |
prahṛṣṭo na tu ruṣṭo asau vanam śrutvā pradharṣitam || 5-64-8

8. shrutvā = hearing; tvat āgamanam = your arrival; ebhiḥ hari yūthapaiḥ saha = along with these leaders of monkeys; saḥ = your uncle; prahṛiṣṭaḥ = was exceedingly pleased; tu = but; shrutvā = on hearing; vanam = that the grove; pradharṣhitam = was attacked; asau = he; na ruṣhṭaḥ = was not enraged.

"ḥearing your arrival along with these leaders of monkeys, your uncle was exceedingly pleased. Even after hearing of ṃadhuvana having been attacked, he did not develop any anger."

Sloka 9

prahṛṣṭo mām pitṛvyaḥ te sugrīvo vānara īśvaraḥ |
śīghram preṣaya sarvān tān iti ha uvāca pārthivaḥ || 5-64-9

9. sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; te pitṛivyaḥ = your uncle; vānara īshvaraḥ = the lord of monkeys; pārthivaḥ = and the king; prahṛiṣṭaḥ = was pleased; uvācha ha mām = and told me; iti = saying; preṣhaya = send; tān sarvān = all those monkeys; shīghram = immediately.

"ṣugreeva, your uncle, the lord of monkeys and the king was pleased and told me, 'send all the monkeys here'."

Sloka 10

śrutvā dadhi mukhasya etat vacanam ślakṣṇam angadaḥ |
abravīt tān hari śreṣṭho vākyam vākya viśāradaḥ || 5-64-10

10. shrutvā = hearing; idam shlakṣhṇam = these smooth; vachanam = words; dadhimukhasya = of Dadhimukha; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; hari shreṣṭhaḥ = the foremost among the monkeys; vākya vishāradaḥ = who was skilled in speech; abravīt = spoke; tān = to those monkeys; vākyam = (the following) words:

ḥearing these smooth words of Dadhimukha, the eloquent Angada, the foremost among the monkeys, spoke to those monkeys as follows:

Sloka 11

śanke śruto ayam vṛtta anto rāmeṇa hari yūthapāḥ |
tat kṣamam na iha naḥ sthātum kṛte kārye param tapāḥ || 5-64-11

11. hari yūthapāḥ = O the foremost of monkeys; param tapāḥ = the tormentators of enemies!; shaṇ^ke = I assume; ayam vṛitta antam = (that) this occurrence; shrutaḥ = was heard; rāmeṇa = by Rama; tat = that is why; na kṣhamam = it is not proper; naḥ = for us; sthātum = to remain; iha = here; kṛite kārye = when the act has been accomplished.

"O the foremost of monkeys, the tormentators of enemies! I assume that Rama might have heard the news (of our arrival). Hence, it is not proper for us to remain here any longer, now that our work has been accomplished."

Sloka 12

pītvā madhu yathā kāmam viśrāntā vana cāriṇaḥ |
kim śeṣam gamanam tatra sugrīvo yatra me guruḥ || 5-64-12

12. vana chāriṇaḥ = the monkeys; vishrāntāḥ = rested; pītvā = after drinking; madhu = the honey; yathā kāmam = as much as they liked; kim sheṣam = what is there remaining; gamanam = (except) going; yatra = to the place where; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; me guruḥ = my uncle; tatra = is there.

"ṭhe monkeys are taking, rest, after drinking the honey to their heart's content. What is there remaining, except to go to the place where Sugreeva my uncle is there?"

Sloka 13

sarve yathā mām vakṣyanti sametya hari yūthapāḥ |
tathā asmi kartā kartavye bhavadbhiḥ paravān aham || 5-64-13

13. yathā = whatever; sarve = all; hari yūthapāḥ = the leaders of monkeys; sametya = together; vakṣhyanti = can tell; mām = me; kartā asmi = I am the doer; aham = I; paravān = am obedient; bhavadbhiḥ = to you; kartavye = in what ought to be done.

"I shall do whatever all the leaders of monkeys together tell me. I am obedient to you, in the matter of what ought to be done."

Sloka 14

na ājñāpayitum īśo aham yuva rājo asmi yadi api |
ayuktam kṛta karmāṇo yūyam dharṣayitum mayā || 5-64-14

14. aham = I; na īshaḥ = am not the master; ājJṇāpayitum = to command (you); asmi yadi api yuva rājaḥ = even though I am a prince; ayuktam = It is not befitting; mayā = for me; dharṣayitum = to violate; yūyam = you; kṛita karmāṇaḥ = who have accomplished the act.

"I am not the master to command you, eventhough I am the crown prince. It is not befitting for me to play the lord over you, who have accomplished the task."

Sloka 15

bruvataḥ ca angadaḥ ca evam śrutvā vacanam avyayam |
prahṛṣṭa manaso vākyam idam ūcuḥ vana okasaḥ || 5-64-15

15. shrutvā = hearing; avyayam vachanam = the indeclinable words; bruvataḥ = spoken; evam = thus; aṇ^gadasya = of Angada; vana okasaḥ = the monkeys; prahṛiṣṭa manasaḥ = with their pleased minds; ūchuḥ = spoke; idam = these words:

ḥearing the indeclinable words spoken thus by Angada, the monkeys with their pleased minds, spoke to him (as follows:

Sloka 16

evam vakṣyati ko rājan prabhuḥ san vānara ṛṣabha |
aiśvarya mada matto hi sarvo aham iti manyate || 5-64-16

16. rājan = O king; vānara ṛiṣhabha = the foremost of monkeys!; kaḥ = who; vadati = speaks; evam = like this; prabhuḥ san = while he is the lord?; sarvaḥ = every one; manyate hi = indeed thinks; aishvarya mada mattaḥ = of one's supremacy; with excited arrogance; aham iti = and says "I; I."

"O prince, the foremost of monkeys! Who would speak like this, while he is the lord? Every one indeed thinks one's supremacy with an excited arrogance and says 'I, I'."

Sloka 17

tava ca idam susadṛśam vākyam na anyasya kasyacit |
samnatiḥ hi tava ākhyāti bhaviṣyat śubha bhāgyatām || 5-64-17

17. susadṛisham = It is so worthy; tava cha = of you alone; idam vākyam = (that you have spoken) these words; na kasya chit = none else (can speak like this); tava = your; samnatiḥ = humility; ākhyāti = is telling; bhaviṣhyat shubha yogyatām = of your future suitableness for prosperity.

"It is so worthy of you alone that you have spoken such words. ṇone else can utter like this. Your humility itself is telling of your future suitableness for prosperity."

Sloka 18

sarve vayam api prāptāḥ tatra gantum kṛta kṣaṇāḥ |
sa yatra hari vīrāṇām sugrīvaḥ patiḥ avyayaḥ || 5-64-18

18. sarve.api vayam prāptāḥ = all of us; who reached here; kṛita kṣhaṇāḥ = are waiting for the right moment; gantum = to go; tatra = to that place; yatra = where; saḥ sugrīvaḥ = that ṣugreeva; avyayaḥ patiḥ hari vīrānām = the imperishable king of the heroic monkeys; (is).

"All of us, who reached here, are waiting for the right moment to go to that place where Sugreeva, the imperishable king of the heroic monkeys, is."

Sloka 19

tvayā hi anuktaiḥ haribhiḥ na eva śakyam padāt padam |
kvacit gantum hari śreṣṭha brūmaḥ satyam idam tu te || 5-64-19

19. hari shreṣhṭha = O the excellent of monkeys!; haribhiḥ = for the monkeys; anuktaiḥ = who are not ordered; tvayā = by you; na shakyam = it is not possible; padāt padam gantum = to go even a step forward; kvachit = anywhere; brūmaḥ = we are speaking; idam satyam = this truth; te = to you.

"O the excellent of monkeys! Without your command, it is not possible for the monkeys anywhere, even a step forward. We are speaking this truth to you."

Sloka 20

evam tu vadatām teṣām angadaḥ pratyabhāṣata |
bāḍham gacchāma iti uktvā utpapāta mahī talāt || 5-64-20

20. teṣhām padatām = while they were severe speaking; evam = thus; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; pratyuvācha ha = replied; bāDham gacchāma = let us surely go; iti = thus; uktvā = speaking; mahābalāḥ = those mighty monkeys; utpetuḥ = sprang; kham = into the air.

While they were thus speaking; Angada replied, "Let us go." ṭhe mighty monkeys then sprang into the air.

Sloka 21

utpatantam anūtpetuḥ sarve te hari yūthapāḥ |
kṛtvā ākāśam nirākāśam yajña utkṣiptā iva analāḥ || 5-64-21

21. sarve = all; te hari yūthapāḥ = those leaders of monkey-troops; achalāḥ iva = like stones; yantrotkṣhiptāḥ = shot from a catapult; kṛitvā = making; ākāsham = the space; nirākāsham = space-less; anūtpetuḥ utpatantam = followed Angada; who was springing forward.

All those leaders of monkey-troops, like stones shot from a catapult, making the space space-less, followed Angada, who was springing forward.

Sloka 22

te ambaram sahasā utpatya vegavantaḥ plavamgamāḥ |
vinadanto mahānādam ghanā vāta īritā yathā || 5-64-22

22. te plavaṇ^gamāḥ = those monkeys; vegavataḥ = with swiftness; vinadantāḥ = sounding forth; mahānādam = a loud noise; sahasā = quickly; utpatya = sprang; ambaram = into the sky; ghanāḥ yathā = like clouds; vāteritāḥ = driven by the wind.

ṭhose monkeys with speed, sounding forth a loud noise, sprang into the sky, like clouds driven by the wind.

Sloka 23

angade hi ananuprāpte sugrīvo vānara adhipaḥ |
uvāca śoka upahatam rāmam kamala locanam || 5-64-23

23. ananuprāpte = without the landing; aṇ^gada = Angada; sugrīsaḥ = ṣugreeva; vānarādhipaḥ = the king of monkeys; uvācha = spoke; kamala lochanam = to the lotus-eyed; rāmam = Rama; shoka upahatam = afflicted with grief.

Even before Angada landed there, ṣugreeva, the king of monkeys spoke to the louts-eyed Rama, who was afflicted with grief (as follows):

Sloka 24

samāśvasihi bhadram te dṛṣṭā devī na saṃśayaḥ |
na āgantum iha śakyam taiḥ atīte samaye hi naḥ || 5-64-24

24. samāshvasihi = be pacified!; te bhadram = happiness to you!; devi = the princess; dṛiṣhṭā = has been scan; na samshayaḥ = there is no doubt; naḥ samaye atīte = when the time fixed by us goes beyond limit; na shakyam = it is not possible; taiḥ = for them; āgantum = to come; iha = here.

"Be pacified! ḥappiness to you! ṭhe princess has been seen. ṭhere is not doubt. When the time earlier fixed by us exceeds beyond limit, it is not possible for them to come here."

Sloka 25

na mat sakāśam āgacchet kṛtye hi vinipātite |
yuva rājo mahābāhuḥ plavatām pravaro angadaḥ || 5-64-25

25. aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; mahābāhuḥ = the long-armed; yuva rājaḥ = prince; pravaraḥ = and the most excellent; plavatām- of monkeys; na āgachchhet = does not make his appearance; matsakāsham = before my vicinity; kṛitye vinipātite = if the work is ruined.

"Angada, the long-armed prince and the most excellent of monkeys does not make his appearance before my vicinity, if the work is ruined."

Sloka 26

yadi api akṛta kṛtyānām īdṛśaḥ syāt upakramaḥ |
bhavet tu dīna vadano bhrānta vipluta mānasaḥ || 5-64-26

26. yadi api syāt = even if; īdṛishaḥ = such; upakramaḥ = an exploit; (may be possible); akṛita kṛityānām = by these monkeys; who have not accomplished their work; saḥ = that Angada; bhavet dīna vadanaḥ = would have worn a sad face; bhrānta vipluta mānasaḥ = and felt perplexed and scattered in mind.

"Even if such an exploit may be possible by these monkeys who have not been able to accomplish their work, Angada would have worn a sad face and felt perplexed and scattered in mind."

Sloka 27

pitṛ paitāmaham ca etat pūrvakaiḥ abhirakṣitam |
na me madhu vanam hanyāt ahṛṣṭaḥ plavaga īśvaraḥ || 5-64-27
kausalyā suprajā rāma samāśvasihi suvrata |

27. ahṛiṣṭaḥ plavaga īshvaraḥ = if Angada the lord of monkeys is not rejoiced; nahanyāt = he would not have damaged; me madhuvanam = my ṃadhuvana; pitṛi paitāmaham = which belongs to my father and grandfather; abhirakṣhitam = and protected; pūrvakaiḥ = by my forebears; samāshvasihi = get consoled well!; rāma = O Rama; kausalyā suprajāḥ = the splendid son of Kausalya; suvrata = the virtuous.

"If Angada the lord f the monkeys is not rejoiced, he would not have damaged my madhuvana, which belongs to my father and grandfather as well as protected by my forbears. Get consoled well, O virtuous Rama, the splendid son of Kausalya!"

Sloka 28

dṛṣṭā devī na saṃdeho na ca anyena hanūmatā || 5-64-28
na hi anyaḥ karmaṇaḥ hetuḥ sādhane tat vidho bhavet |

28. devī = ṣeetha; dṛiṣhṭā = was seen; na samdehaḥ = there is no doubt; na = none; anyena = other; hanūmatā = than ḥanuma; na hi anyaḥ = there is indeed none other; hanūmataḥ = than ḥanuma; hetuḥ = who is the executor; sādhane = in accomplishing; asya karmaṇaḥ = this work.

"ṣeetha was seen. ṭhere is no doubt. ṇone other than ḥanuma must have seen ṣeetha. ṭhere is indeed none other than Hanuma, who is the executor."

Sloka 29

hanūmati hi siddhiḥ ca matiḥ ca mati sattama || 5-64-29
vyavasāyaḥ ca vīryam ca sūrye teja iva dhruvam |

29. mati sattama = O Rama; having a very good understanding!; hanūmati = in ḥanuma; siddhiḥ cha = capacity to accomplish any object; matiḥ cha = intelligence; vyavasāyaḥ cha = strenuous effort; vīryam cha = and vigour; dhruvam = are unchangeable; tejaḥ iva = like a splendour; sūrye = in the sun.

"O Rama, having a very good understanding! In ḥanuma, qualities like capacity to accomplish any object, intelligence, strenuous effort and vigour are unchangeable, like a splendour in the sun."

Sloka 30

jāmbavān yatra netā syāt angadaḥ ca bala īśvaraḥ || 5-64-30
hanūmān ca api adhiṣṭhātā na tasya gatiḥ anyathā |

30. yatra = where; jāmbavān = Jambavan; netāsyāt = is a leader; aṇ^gadashcha = Angada; bala īshvaraḥ = the commander of the troops; hanūmān cha = and hanuma; adhiṣhṭhātā = the administrator; gatiḥ = the progress; tasya = of that work; na anyathā = cannot be otherwise.

"Where Jambavan is a leader; Angada as the commander of troops and moreover ḥanuma as the administer, the progress of that work cannot be as otherwise."

Sloka 31

mā bhūḥ cintā samāyuktaḥ samprati amita vikrama || 5-64-31
tataḥ kila kilā śabdam śuśrāva āsannam ambare |
hanūmat karma dṛptānām nardatām kānana okasām || 5-64-32
kiṣkindhām upayātānām siddhim kathayatām iva |

31; 32. amita vikramaḥ = O Rama of unlimited prowess! mābhūḥ chintā samāyuktaḥ = do not encounter troubled thoughts; samprati = now!; tataḥ = thereupon; kānana okasām = (while) the monkeys; hanumat karma dṛiptānām = who are proud of ḥanuma's performance; nardatām = who are making noise; kathayatām iva = as though telling about; siddhim = the accomplishment of their act; upayātānām = and approaching towards; kiṣhkindhām = Kishkindha; shushrāva = was heard; kila kilā shabdam = certain sounds expressing joy; āsannam = in the proximity; ambare = of the sky.

"O Rama, of unlimited prowess! Do not encounter troubled thoughts now!" ṃeanwhile, when the monkeys, who are proud of Hanuma's performance and who are making noise as though they are proclaiming about the accomplishment of their act are approaching Kishkindha Sugreeva heard certain sounds expressing joy, in the vicinity of the sky."

Sloka 33

tataḥ śrutvā ninādam tam kapīnām kapi sattamaḥ || 5-64-33
āyata ancita lāngūlaḥ so abhavadt hṛṣṭa mānasaḥ |

33. tataḥ = then; shrutvā = hearing; tam ninādam = that clamour; kapīnām = of the monkeys; saḥ kapi sattamaḥ = that ṣugreeva; the chief of monkeys; abhavat = became; hṛiṣṭa mānasaḥ = rejoiced in mind; āyata aṇchita lāṇgūlaḥ = duly stretching and curling up his tail.

ṭhen, hearing that clamour of the monkeys, ṣugreeva the chief of the monkeys became rejoiced at heart, duly stretching and curling up his tail (in joy).

Sloka 34

ājagmuḥ te api harayo rāma darśana kānkṣiṇaḥ || 5-64-34
angadam purataḥ kṛtvā hanūmantam ca vānaram |

34. kṛitvā = keeping; aṇ^gadam = Angada; hanūmantam = and ḥanuma; vānram = the monkey; purataḥ = in front of them; te harayaḥ api = those monkeys too; ājagmuḥ = came; rāma darshana kāṇ^kṣiṇaḥ = with their eagerness or see Rama.

Keeping Angada and ḥanuma in front of them, those monkeys too arrived with their eagerness to see Rama.

Sloka 35

te angada pramukhā vīrāḥ prahṛṣṭāḥ ca mudā anvitāḥ || 5-64-35

35. te vīrāḥ = those heroes; aṇ^gada pramukhāḥ = having Angada as their chief; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ cha = exceedingly pleased; mudā anvitāḥ = and filled with joy; nipetuḥ = landed; samīpe = at the vicinity; hari rājasya = of ṣugreeva; rāghavasya cha = and Rama.

ṭhose heroes, having Angada as their chief, exceedingly pleased as they were and filled with joy, landed at the vicinity of Sugreeva and Rama.

Sloka 36

hanūmān ca mahābahuḥ praṇamya śirasā tataḥ || 5-64-36
niyatām akṣatām devīm rāghavāya nyavedayat |

36. tataḥ = thereafter; mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; hanumān = ḥanuma; praṇamya = offering salutation; shirasā = by bending his head low; nyavedayat = reported; devīm = that ṣeetha the princess; niyatām = is constant in devotion to her husband; akṣhatām = and sound in body.

ṭhereafter, the mighty armed ḥanuma, offering his salutation in bending his head low, informed Rama that Seetha the princess is constant in devotion to Rama and sound in body.

Sloka 37

dṛṣṭā devīti hanumadvadanādamṛtopamam || 5-64-37
ākarṇya vacanam rāmo harṣamāpa salakṣmaṇaḥ |

37. ākarṇya = hearing; vachanam = the words; amṛitopamam = which were nectar-like; devī dṛiṣhṭā iti = saying ṣeetha was found"; hanumadvānāt = from the month of ḥanuma; rāmaḥ = Rama; salakṣmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana; āpa = obtained; harṣham = joy.

ḥearing the nectar-like words, saying "ṣeetha was found" from the mouth of ḥanuma, Rama and Lakshmana felt happy.

Sloka 38

niścita artham tataḥ tasmin sugrīvam pavana ātmaje || 5-64-38
lakṣmaṇaḥ prītimān prītam bahu mānāt avaikṣata |

38. tataḥ = thereupon; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; prītimān = with love; avaikṣhata = looked at; prītam = the joyful; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; bahu mānāt = with reverence; nishchita artham = who decidedly apprised; tasmin pavana ātmaje = about the aforesaid ḥanuma; (having accomplished his work).

ṭhereupon, Lakshmana with love looked with reverence on the joyful ṣugreeva, who earlier decidedly apprised about the aforesaid Hanuma, having accomplished the task.

Sloka 39

prītyā ca ramamāṇo atha rāghavaḥ para vīrahā || 5-64-39
bahu mānena mahatā hanūmantam avaikṣata |

39. rāghavaḥ = Rama; para vīrahā = the annihilator of hostile heroes; upetaḥ = who obtained; parayā = a great; prītyā = joy; avaikṣhata = looked at; hanumantam = ḥanuma; mahatā bahumānena = with a high esteem.

Rama, the annihilator of hostile heroes, who obtained a great joy, looked on ḥanuma with a high esteem.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe catuḥṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 64th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
