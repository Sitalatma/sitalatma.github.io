Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 106
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seeing Ravana's chariot coming, Rama asks ṃatali the charioteer to be on his guard. Bad omens prognosticating the fall of Ravana and good omens predicting the victory of Rama appeared in the scene of battle-field.

Sloka 1

sārathiḥ sa ratham hṛṣṭaṃ parasainyapradharṣaṇam |
gandharvanagarākāraṃ samucchritapātākinam || 6-106-1
yuktaṃ paramasaṃpannairvāibhirhemamālibhiḥ |
yuddhopakaraṇaiḥ pūrṇaṃ patākādhvajamālinam || 6-106-2
grasantamiva cākāśaṃ nādayantaṃ vasuṃdharām |
praṇāśaṃ paranainyānāṃ svanainyasya praharṣaṇam || 6-106-3
rāvaṇasya rathaṃ kṣipraṃ codayāmāsa sārathiḥ |

1; 2. saḥ sārathiḥ = that charioteer; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = thrilling with rapture; kṣhipram = quickly; chodayāmāsa = drove forward; rāvaṇasya ratham = Ravana's chariot; para sainya pradharṣhaṇam = which attacked the army of enemies; gandharva nagarākāram = which was in form of Gandharva-city; an imaginary city in the sky; samuchchhrita patākinam = mounted with flags; yuktam = yoked; vājibhiḥ = with horses; parama sampannaiḥ = of excellent quality; hema mālibhiḥ = having golden necklaces; pūrṇam = filled; yuddhopakaraṇaiḥ = with war-implements; patakādhvajamālinam = garlanded with flags and banners; grasantamiva = as though devouring the sky; nādayantam vasundharām = making the earth; resound; praṇāsham = which destroyed; parasainyānām = the army of adversaries; para sainyānām = the army of adversaries; pracharṣhaṇam = and caused delight; sva sainyasya = to its own army.

Thrilling with rapture, that charioteer, the charioteer of Ravana, drove forward quickly, his chariot, a chariot, which was capable of attacking the army of enemies, a wonderful piece of art like Gandharva-city, an imaginary city in the sky, mounted with flags, yoked with horses of excellent quality adorned with golden necklaces, filled with war-implements, adorned with rows of flags and banners, which was devouring the sky as it were, making the earth resound, which was the destroyer of the army of adversaries and caused delight to its own.

Sloka 4

tamāpatantan sahasā svanavantaṃ mahādhvajam || 6-106-4
rathan rākṣasarājasya nararājo dadarśa ha |
kṛṣṇavājisamāyuktan yuktaṃ raudreṇa varcasā || 6-106-5
dīpyamānamivākāśe vimānaṃ sūryavarcasaṃ |
taḍitpatākāgahanan darśitendrāyudhāyudham || 6-106-6

3; 4; 5; 6. nararājaḥ = Rama; dadarshaha = saw; tam = that; rākṣhasa rājasya ratham = Ravana's chariot; āpatantam = which was coming; sahasā = speedily; svanavantam = with a noise; mahā dhvajam = with a large flag-staff; kṛiṣhṇa rājisamāyuktam = yoked with black horses; yuktam = endowed; raudreṇa varchasā = with a terrific luster; vimānamiva = like an aerial car; dīpyamānam = blazing; ākāshe = in the sky; sūryavarchasam = with a luster of the sun; taDitpatākāgahanam = filled with lightning-like flags; darshitendrāyudhaprabham = and with a beautiful appearance of a rain-bow.

Rama saw that Ravana's chariot, which was coming speedily with a noise, bearing a large flag-staff, yoked with black horses, endowed with a terrific luster, blazing like an aerial car in the sky, with a luster of the sun, filled with lightning-like flags and with a beautiful appearance of a rain-bow.

Sloka 7

śaradhārā vimuñcantan dhārāsāramivānbudam |
tan dṛṣṭvā meghasaṅkāśamāpatantan rathaṃ ripoḥ || 6-106-7
girervajrābhimṛṣṭasya dīryataḥ sadṛśasvanam |
vispārayanvai vegena bālacandrānataṃ dhanuḥ || 6-106-8
uvāca mātalin rāmaḥ sahasrākṣasya mātalim |

7; 8. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; vimuṇchantam = (that chariot) releasing; sharadharāḥ = streams of arrows; ambudamiva = like a cloud; (releasing) dharāsāram = streams of rain; sadṛisha svanam = with a noise equal to that; dīryataḥ gireḥ = of a bursting mountain; vajrābhimṛiṣhṭasya = struck with a diamond; rāmaḥ = Rama; vegena = quickly; visphārayan = after stretching; dhanuḥ = his bow; bālachandrānatam = in a crescent shape; uvācha = spoke; mātalim = to ṃatali; sahasrākṣhasya sārathin = Indra's charioteer (as follows):

Seeing that chariot, releasing streams of arrows, like a cloud releasing streams of rain, with a noise equal to that of a bursting mountain, struck with a diamond, Rama after stretching his bow in a crescent shape, spoke to ṃatali, Indra's charioteer as follows:

Sloka 9

mātale paśya sanrabdhamāpatantan rathaṃ ripoḥ || 6-106-9
yathāpasavyaṃ patatā vegena mahatā punaḥ |
samare hantumātmānan tathānena kṛtā matiḥ || 6-106-10

9; 10. ṃātale = O ṃatali!; pashya = look at; ripoh ratham = this chariot of the enemy; āpatantam = coming forward; samrabdham = furiously; yathā = as; anena = by him; punaḥ = once more; patatā = comng; mahatā vegena = with a great speed; apasavyam = from the right to the left; tathā = therefore; matikṛitā = heart has been set; hantum = upon destroying; ātmānam = himself samara = in the battle.

"O ṃatali! From the way in which the enemy is marching forward from left to right with a great speed in his chariot more, it appears that heart has been set by him upon destroying himself in the battle."

Sloka 11

tadapramādamātiṣṭha pratyudgaccha rathan ripoḥ|
vidhvansayitumicchāmi vāyurmeghamivotthitam || 6-106-11

11. tat = therefore; ātiṣhṭha apramādam = take care; pratyudgachchha = and go opposite to; ripoḥ ratham = the chariot of the enemy; ichchhāmi = I wish; vidhvamsayitum = to destroy (it); vāyuḥ iva = even as the wind; utthitam = would blow; megham = a cloud.

"Therefore, take care and march forward, facing the chariot of the enemy I wish to destroy it, even as the wind would blow a cloud."

Sloka 12

aviklavamasambhrāntamavyagrahṛdayekṣaṇam |
raśmisañcāraniyataṃ pracodaya rathan drutam || 6-106-12

12. aviklabam = without fear; asambhrāntam = and without flurry; avyagrahṛidayekṣhaṇam and with a steady heart and vision; rashmi samchāra niyatam = and the move of the reins fully controlled; prachodaya = drive; ratham = the chariot; drutam = swiftly.

"Without fear and flurry and with a steady heart and vision, and the movement of the reins fully controlled, drive the chariot swiftly."

Sloka 13

kāmaṃ na tvan samādheyaḥ purandararathocitaḥ |
yuyutsurahamekāgraḥ smāraye tvāṃ na śikṣaye || 6-106-13

13. tvam = you; na samādheya = need not be instructed (by me); purandara rathochitaḥ = accustomed as you are to drive the chariot of Indra the lord of celestials; aham yuyutsuḥ = keen as I am to fight; ekāgraḥ = with a close attention smāraye = I am just reminding; tvām = you; na shikṣhaye = and not teaching you.

"You need not be instructed by me, accustomed as you are to drive the chariot of Indra the lord of celestials. Keen as I am to fight with a close attention, I am just reminding you and not teaching you."

Sloka 14

parituṣṭaḥ sa rāmasya tena vākyena mātaliḥ |
pracodayāmāsa rathan surasārathisattamaḥ || 6-106-14

14. parituṣhṭaḥ = extremely gratified; tena vākyena = with those words; rāmasya = of Rama; saḥ mātaliḥ = that ṃatali; uttamaḥ = the excellent; sura sārathiḥ = charioteer of gods; prachodayāmāse = drove on; ratham = the chariot.

Extremely gratified with those words of Rama, that ṃatali the excellent charioteer of gods drove on the chariot.

Sloka 15

apasavyan tataḥ kurvanrāvaṇasya mahāratham |
cakrotkṣiptena rajasā rāvaṇan vyavadhūnayat || 6-106-15

15. kurvan = passing; mahā ratham = the huge chariot; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; apasavyam = on the right; tataḥ = (the charioteer) then; rāvaṇam = (set) Ravana; vyavadhūnayam = shaking; chakra sambhūta rajasā = by the dust risen from the wheels (of his own chariot).

Passing the huge chariot of Ravana on the right, the charioteer then set Ravana shaking, by the dust risen from the wheels of his own chariot.

Sloka 16

tataḥ kruddho daśagrīvastāmravisphāritekṣaṇaḥ |
rathapratimukhan rāmaṃ sāyakairavadhūnayat || 6-106-16

16. kruddhaḥ = the enraged; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; tataḥ = then; tāmra visphāritekṣhaṇaḥ = with his coppery eyes wide open; avadhūnayat = trembled; rāmam = Rama; rathapramukhan = who stood facing his chariot; sāyakaiḥ = with arrows.

The enraged Ravana then, with his coppery eyes wide open, trembled Rama, who stood facing his chariot, with arrows.

Sloka 17

dharṣaṇāmarṣito rāmo dhairyan roṣeṇa laṅghayan |
jagrāha sumahāvegamaindran yudhi śarāsanam || 6-106-17

17. lambhayan dhairyam = meeting fortitude; roṣheṇa = with anger; dharṣhaṇāmarṣhitaḥ = though provoked with assault; rāmaḥ = Rama; jagrāha = took hold; aindram sharāsanam = of Indra's bow; sumahāvegam = which was possessed of extra ordinary impulse; yudhi = in the battle-field.

ṃeeting fortitude with anger, though provoked with assault, Rama took hold of Indra's bow, which was possessed of extra ordinary impulse in the battle-field.

Sloka 18

śarāṃśca sumahātejāḥ sūryaraśmisamaprabhān |
tadupoḍhaṃ mahadyuddhamanyonyavadhakāṅkṣiṇoḥ || 6-106-18
parasparābhimukhayordṛptayoriva sinhayoḥ |

18. sharān cha = he also seized hold of arrows; sumahāvegān = of exceeding swiftness; sūrya rashmi samaprabhān = which were shining like sun-beams; upoDham = (ṭhen) began; tat mahat = that great; yuddham = battle; anyonya vadrakāṇkṣhiṇau = between the two warriors (Rama and Ravana) who wee desirous of killing each other; parasparābhimukhayoḥ = and who were facing each other; dṛiptayoḥ simhayoriva = like two proud lions.

He also seized hold of arrows of exceeding swiftness, which were shining like sun-beams. Then began that great battle between the two warriors (Rama and Ravana) who were desirous of killing each other, while facing each other like two proud lions.

Sloka 19

tato devāḥ sagandharvāḥ siddhāśca paramarṣayaḥ || 6-106-19
samīyurdvairathan draṣṭun rāvaṇakṣayakāṅkṣiṇaḥ |

19. tataḥ = then; devāh = the gods; sagandharvāḥ = along with Gandharvas the celestial musicians; siddhāshcha = ṣiddhas; the demigods; paramarṣhayaḥ = and great sages; rāvaṇa kṣhayakāṇkṣhiṇaiḥ = desirous of Ravana's ruin; samīyuḥ = arrived; draṣhṭum = to see; dvairatham = the battle of both the chariot-warriors.

Then, the gods along with the Gandharvas the celestial musicians, Siddhas the demigods and great sages, desirous of Ravana's ruin, arrived to see the battle of both the chariot-warrriors.

Sloka 20

samutpeturathotpātā dāruṇā lomaharṣaṇāḥ || 6-106-20
rāvaṇasya vināśāya rāghavasya jayāya ca |

20. atha = thereupon; dāruṇāḥ = terrific; utpātāḥ = portents; romaharṣhaṇāḥ = that caused one's hair to stand on end; samutpetuḥ = appeared; rāvaṇasya vināshāya = giving an augury of doom to Ravana; rāghavasya udayāaya cha = and prosperity to Rama.

Thereupon, terrific portents that caused one's hair to stand on end, appeared giving an augury of doom to Ravana and prosperity to Rama.

Sloka 21

vavarṣa rudhiran devo rāvaṇasya rathopari || 6-106-21
vātā maṇḍalinastīvrā apasavyaṃ pracakramuḥ |

21. devaḥ = the god of rains; vavarṣha = poured; rudhiram = blood; rāvaṇasya rathopari = on Ravana's chariot; tīvrāḥ- horrible; vātāḥ = winds; rachakramuḥ = blew; apasavyam = from right to the left; maṇ^Dalinaḥ = forming circles.

The god of rains poured blood on Ravana's chariot. Horrible winds blew from right to left, forming circles.

Sloka 22

mahad gṛdhrakulan cāsya bhramamāṇaṃ nabhastale || 6-106-22
yena yena ratho yāti tena tena pradhāvati |

22. mahat = a large; gṛidhrakulam = flock of vultures; bhramamāṇam = roaming about; nabhastale = in the sky; pradhāvati = was flying forward; tena tena = in the same way; yena yena = in which; asya rathaḥ = Ravana's chariot; yāti = was going.

A large flock of vultures, roaming about in the sky, was flying forward in the same direction in which Ravana's chariot was going.

Sloka 23

sandhyayā cāvṛtā laṅkā japāpuṣpanikāśayā || 6-106-23
dṛśyate sampradīteva divaseapi vasundharā |

23. laṇ^kā = Lanka; āvṛitā = was overcast; samdhyayā = with dusk; japāpuṣhpanikāshayā = resembling a (red) Japa flower; vasumdharā = the land (in Lanka); divase.api = as also the day; dṛishyate = appeared; sampradīpteva = as if; they were blazing.

Lanka was overcast with dusk, resembling a (red) Japa flower. The land in Lanka, as also the day appeared as if they were blazing.

Sloka 24

sanirghātā maholkāśca sampraceturmahāsvanāḥ || 6-106-24
viṣādayantyo rakṣānsi rāvaṇasya tadāhitāḥ |

24. maholkāshcha = Large meteors; sanirghātāḥ = along with lightnings; samprapetuḥ = fell; mahāsvanāḥ = with great noise; te = those entities ahitāḥ = inimical; rāvaṇasya = to Ravana; rakṣhāmsi viṣhādayan = made demons to lament.

Large meteors, along with lightnings fell with great noise. Those entities, inimical to Ravana, made demons to lament.

Sloka 25

rāvaṇaśca yatastatra pracacāla vasundharā || 6-106-25
rakṣasān ca praharatāṃ gṛhītā iva bāhavaḥ |

25. vasumdharā = the earth; yataḥ = on which; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; tatra = was there; prachachāla = trembled; bāhavaḥ = the arms; rakṣhasāmcha = of demons; praharatām = who were fighting; gṛihītā iva = were like as if they were clasped.

The earth on which Ravana was there, trembled. The arms of fighting demons looked as if they were clasped.

Sloka 26

tāmrāḥ pītāḥ sitāḥ śvetāḥ patitāḥ sūryaraśmayaḥ || 6-106-26
dṛśyante rāvaṇasyāṅge parvatasyeva dhātavaḥ |

26. patitāḥ = fallen; agre = before; rāvaṇasya = Ravana; sūrya rashmayaḥ = the rays of the sun; dṛishyante tāmrāḥ = appeared coppery; pītāḥ = yellow; sitāshvetāḥ = white and dark; dhātavaḥ iva = like mineral-ores on a mountain.

Fallen before Ravana, the rays of the sun appeared coppery, yellow, white and dark, like mineral ores on a mountain.

Sloka 27

gṛdhrairanugatāścāsya vamantyo jvalanaṃ mukhaiḥ || 6-106-27
praṇedurmukhamīkṣantyaḥ sanrabdhamaśivan śivāḥ |

27. shirāḥ = the she-jackals; anugatāḥ = followed; gṛidhraiḥ = by vultures; samrabanam = hastily; praṇeduḥ = uttered; ashivam = inauspicious howls; īkṣhyantaḥ = on beholding; asya mukham = Ravana's face; vamantyaḥ = (as also) vomiting; jvalanam = fire; mukhaiḥ = from their mouths.

The she-jackals followed by vultures hastily uttered inauspicious howls, on beholding Ravana's face as also vomiting fire from their mouths.

Sloka 28

pratikūlan vavau vāyū raṇe pānsūn samutkiran || 6-106-28
tasya rākṣasarājasya kurvandṛṣṭivilopanam |

28. kurvan dṛiṣhṭivilopanam = clouding the vision; tasya rāvaṇasya = fo that Ravana; vāyuḥ = the wind; samutkiran pāmsūn = scattering dust upwards; vavau = blew; pratikūlam = in an inverted order.

Scattering the dust upwards over the battle-field and clouding the vision of that Ravana, the wind blew in a direction inimicable to him.

Sloka 29

nipeturindrāśanayaḥ sainye cāsya samantataḥ || 6-106-29
durviṣahya svanā ghorā vinā jaladharasvanam |

29. jala dharodayam vinā = even without the appearance of clouds; ghoram = terrific; indrāshanayaḥ = thunderbolts; nipetuḥ = fell; asya sainye = on his army; samantataḥ = on all sides; durviṣhahya svanāḥ = with a noise; which was hard to endure.

Even without the appearance of clouds, thunderbolts fell on his army on all sides, with a noise which was hard to endure.

Sloka 30

diśaśca pradiśaḥ sarvā babhūvustimirāvṛtāḥ || 6-106-30
pānsuvarṣeṇa mahatā durdarśan ca nabhoabhavat |

30. sarvāh = all; dishaḥ cha = the quarters; pradishashcha = and intermediate points of the compass; babhūvaḥ = became; timirāvṛitāḥ = covered with darkness; mahatā pāmsu varṣheṇa = and due to outpourings of dust; nabhaḥ = the sky; abhavat = became; durdarsham = difficult to be seen.

All the quarters and intermediate points of the compass became covered with darkness and due to outpourings of dust, the sky became difficult to be seen.

Sloka 31

kurvantyaḥ kalahan ghoran sārikāstadrathaṃ prati || 6-106-31
nipetuḥ śataśastatra dāruṇā dāruṇasvanāḥ |

31. shatashaḥ = hundreds of; dāruṇāh = dreadful; sārikāḥ* = ṣarika birds; dāruṇārutāḥ = with awful howls; kurvantyaḥ kalaham = fighting; ghoram = terribly; tatra = there; nipetuḥ = fell; tadratham prati = down upon Ravana's chariot.

Hundreds of dreadful Sarika* birds, with their awful howls, terribly fighting there, fell down upon Ravana's chariot.

*Sarika bird: zoological name: Turdus salica.

Sloka 32

jaghanebhyaḥ sphuliṅgāṃśca netrebhyoaśrūṇi santatam || 6-106-32
mumucustasya turagāstulyamagnin ca vāri ca |

32. tasya = his; turagāḥ = horses; mumuchuḥ = discharged; sphuliṇ^gān = sparks of fire; jaghanebhyaḥ = from their hips and loins; ashrūṇicha = tears; netrebhyaḥ = from their eyes; vāricha = and water; agnimcha = as also fire; tulyam = equally; samtatam = and continuously.

His horses discharged sparks of fire from their hips and loins as also tears from their eyes, releasing out fire and water at the same time continuously.

Sloka 33

evaṃ prakārā bahavaḥ samutpātā bhayāvahāḥ || 6-106-33
rāvaṇasya vināśāya dāruṇāḥ samprajajiñire |

33. dāruṇāḥ = frightful; samutpātāḥ = portents; baharaḥ = in multitude; samprajaJṇire = sprang up; evamprakārāḥ = in the way; bhayāvahāḥ = bringing danger; vināshāya = and destruction; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana.

Frightful portents in multitude thus sprang up, bringing danger and destruction to Ravana.

Sloka 34

rāmasyāpi nimittāni saumyāni ca śivāni ca || 6-106-34
babhūvurjayaśansīni prādurbhūtāni sarvaśaḥ |

34. saumyāni shivāni cha = pleasant and auspicious; jaya shamsīni = depicting victory; rāmasya = of Rama; babhūvuḥ = arose; prādurbhūtāni = and appeared; sarvashaḥ = on all sides.

Pleasant and auspicious omens, depicting victory of Rama appeared on all sides, before Rama.

Sloka 35

nimittāni ca saumyāni rāghavaḥ svajayāya vai || 6-106-35
dṛṣṭvā paramasaṃhṛṣṭo hataṃ mene ca rāvaṇam |

35. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; saumyāni nimittāni = the pleasant omens; svajayāya = depicting his victory; rāghavaḥ = Rama; param asamhṛiṣhṭaḥ = was very much pleased; mane = and considered; rāvaṇam = Ravana; hatam = as (already) dead.

Seeing the pleasant omens depicting his victory, Rama was very much pleased and considered Ravana as (already) dead.

Sloka 36

tato nirīkṣyātmagatāni rāghavo |
raṇe nimittāni nimittakovidaḥ |
jagāma harṣan ca parāṃ ca nirvṛtiṃ |
cakāra yuddheabhyadhikan ca vikramam || 6-106-36

36. tataḥ = then; rāghavaḥ = Rama; nimitta kovidaḥ = having a knowledge of the omens; nirīkṣhya = having seen; nimittāni = the portents; ātmagatani = in his own way; raṇe = in the battle-field; jagāma = got; harṣhamcha = rejoice; parām nirvṛitim = and extreme happiness; chakāra adhikam vikramamcha = as also shown enhanced prowess; yuddhe = in combat.

Then Rama, with the knowledge of all omens, having seen those portents in his own way in the battle-field, obtained rejoice as well as extreme happiness and shown enhanced prowess in combat.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ṣaḍuttaraśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 106th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
