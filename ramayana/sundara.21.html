Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 21
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Seetha replies to Ravana saying that He should seek friendship with Rama and return Her back to Rama.


Sloka 1

tasya tadvacanam śrutvā sītā raudrasya rakṣasaḥ |
ārtā dīnasvarā dīnam pratyuvāca tataḥ śanaiḥ || 5-21-1

1. sītā = ṣeetha; shrutvā = listening; tasya raudrasya rakshasaḥ = to that horrible ogre's; tat = that; vachanam = word; ārtā = being tormented; pratyuvacha = spoke in reply; dīna svarā = with a pitiable voice; dīnam = pitifully; shanaiḥ = slowly.

ṣeetha listening to that horrible ogre's word, being tormented spoke in reply with a pitiable voice, pitifully and slowly.

Sloka 2

duḥkhārtā rudatī sītā vepamānā tapasvinī |
cintayantī varāroha patimeva pativratā || 5-21-2
tṛṇamantarataḥ kṛtvā pratyuvāca śucismitā |

2. duhkhārtā = Being tormented by grief; tapasvinī = in a pitiful state; varārohā = with excellent limbs; pativratā = devoted to husband; chintayantī = thinking; patimeva = about husband alone; shuchi smitā = with pleasant smiles; sītā = ṣeetha; rudati = was sobbing;vepamānā = shivering; kṛitvā = making; tṛiṇam = a grass straw;antarataḥ = in between (herself and Ravana);pratyuvācha = spoke in reply.

Being tormented by grief, in a pitiful state, with excellent limbs, devoted to husband, thinking about husband alone, with pleasant smiles Seetha was sobbing, shivering and placing a straw in between herself and Ravana spoke thus in reply.

Sloka 3

nivartaya mano mattaḥ svajane kriyatāṃ manaḥ || 5-21-3
na māṃ prārthayituṃ yuktaṃ susiddhimiva pāpakṛt |

3. nivartaya = turn back; manaḥ = mind; mattaḥ = from me; manaḥ kriyatām = let your mind be made; svajaḥ = on your own wives; susiddhimiva = like best emancipation;pāpakṛit = (for) a sinner; na yuktam = it is not appropriate; prārthayitum = to pray;mām = me.

"ṭurn your mind back from me. Let your mind be made on your own wives. Like best emancipation for a sinner, it is not appropriate to pray me."

Sloka 4

akāryam na mayā kāryamekapatnyā vigarhitam || 5-21-4
kulam samprāptayā puṇyam kule mahati jātayā |

4. jātayā = I was born; mahati = in a great; kule = family; saṃprāptayā = getting;kulam = family;ekapatnyā = having one husband; akāryam = a bad deed; nigarhitam = which is blameable;na kāryam = is not to be made; mayā = by me.

"I was born in a great family. Getting such family, having one husband, a bad deed which is blameable is not to be made by me."

Sloka 5

evamuktvā tu vaidehī rāvaṇam tam yaśasvinī || 5-21-5
rāvaṇam pṛṣṭhataḥ kṛtvā bhūyo vacanambravīt |

5. vaidehī = ṣeetha;yashasvinī = with renown; evam uktvā = thus speaking; tam ravaṇam = to that Ravana; kṛitvā = making; pṛishhṭhataḥ = the back; rākshasam = to (that) ogre; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = words; būyaḥ = again.

ṣeetha with renown thus speaking to that Ravana, turned back and spoke words again.

Sloka 6

vāhamaupayikī bhāryā parabhāryā satī tava || 5-21-6
sādhu dharmamavekṣasva sādhu sādhuvratam cara |

6. aham = I; parabharyā = who am wife of another;satī = and devoted to husband; na = am not; aupaikī = suitable; bhāryā = wife; tava = to you; anvekshasva = observe; dharmam = righteousness; sādhu = well; chara = follow; sādhuvratam = course of conduct of virtuous; sādhu = well.

"I who am wife of another and devoted to husband am not suitable wife to you. Observe righteousness well. Follow well course of conduct of virtuous."

Sloka 7

yathā tava tathānyeṣāṃ dārā rakṣyā viśācara || 5-21-7
ātmānamupamāṃ kṛtvā sveṣu dāreṣu ramyatām |

7. nishāchara = O ogre!; yathā = how; tava = your (wives) rakshyāḥ = are to be protected; tathā = in the same way; anyeshhām = others'; dārāḥ = wives (are to be protected); ātmānam upamām kṛitvā = making yourself as an example; ramyatām = enjoy; sveshhu dārseshhu = .your own wives.

"O Ogre! ḥow your wives are to be protected, in the same way others' wives are to be protected. ṃaking yourself as an example enjoy your own wives."

Sloka 8

atuṣṭaṃ sveṣu dāreṣu capalam calitendriyam || 5-21-8
nayanti nikṛtiprajñaṃ paradārāḥ parābhavam |

8. atushhṭam = one who is not satisfied; sveshhu dāreshhu = in own wives; chapalam = fickle-minded one; chalitendriyam = with disturbed senses; nikṛiti prajJṇām = mean minded one; (to such a man)parabhāryāḥ = others' wives; nayanti = will lead; parābhavam = to humiliation.

"One who is not satisfied in own wives, a fickle-minded one with disturbed senses, mean minded one, to such a man, others' wives will lead to humilation."

Sloka 9

iha santo na vā santi sato vā nānuvartase || 5-21-9
tathāhi viparītā te buddhirācāravarjitā |

9. iha = here; na vā santi = are there not; santaḥ = good people?; nānuvartase vā = or are you not following; sataḥ = those (who are good)?; tathhahi = it must be like that; te buddhiḥ = (beacuse) your mind; āchāravarjitā = devoid of traditions; viparītā = is perverse.

"ḥere are there not good people? Or are you not following those who are good? It must be like that because your mind, devoid of traditions is perverse."

Sloka 10

vaco mithyāpraṇītātmā pathyamuktaṃ vicakṣaṇaiḥ || 5-21-10
rākṣasānāmabhāvāya tvam vā na vratipadyase |

10. tvam = you; mithyā praṇītātmā = who is being led by mind towards unreal; na pratipadya se = is not taking; pathyam = wholesome; vachaḥ = words; uptam = being said; vichakshaṇaiḥ = by righteous ones; abhāvāya = for the destruction; rākshasānām = of ogres.

"You who is being led by mind towards unreal is not taking wholesome words being said by righteous onesfor the destruction of ogres."

Sloka 11

ākṛtātmānamāsādya rājānamanaye ratam || 5-21-11
samṛddhāni vinaśyanti rāṣṭrāṇi nagarāṇi ca |
tatheyam tvām samāsādya laṅkā ratnaughasamkulā || 5-21-12
aparādhāttavaikasya vacirādvinaśiṣyati |

11; 12. āsādya = (after) getting; rājānam = a king; akṛitātmānam = who has uncontrolled mind; ratam = interested; anaye = in bad path; samṛiddani = wealthy; rāshhṭrāṇi = states; nagarāṇi cha = and cities also; vinashyanti = will be destroyed; samāsādya = (after)getting; tvām = you; tathā = like that; iyam laṇ^kā = this Lanka;ratnaugha saṃkulā = filled with best things; nachirāt = in a short while; vinashishhyati = will be destroyed; tava = due to your; ekasya = one; aparādhāt = sin.

"After getting a king who has uncontrolled mind, interested in a bad path, wealthy states and cities also will be destroyed. After getting you like that this Lanka filled with best things in a short while will be destroyed due to your one sin."

Sloka 13

svakṛtairhanyamānasya āvaṇādīrghadarśinaḥ || 5-21-13
abhinandanti bhūtāni vināśe pāpakarmaṇaḥ |

13. rāvana = O Ravana!; vināshe = (when there is) destruction; adīrgha darshinaḥ = (of) a short sighted one; hanyamānasya svakṛitaiḥ = being hit by his own deeds; pāpakamaṇaḥ = a sinner; bhūtāni = living beings; abhinandanti = will be happy;

"O Ravana! When there is destruction of a short sighted one being hit by his own deeds, a sinner, living beings will be happy."

Sloka 14

evaṃ tvāṃ pāpakarmāṇam vakṣyanti nikṛtā janāḥ || 5-21-14
diṣṭyaitad vyasanaṃ prāpto raudra ityeva harṣitāḥ |

14. tvām = about you;pāpakarmāṇam = who is a sinner; evam = thus being destroyed; janāḥ = people; nikṛitāḥ = who have been humiliated by you; harshhitāḥ = being happy; ityeva vakshyanti = will speak thus; raudraḥ = the cruel Ravana; dishhṭyā = by God's grace; prāptaḥ = has got; etat vyasanam = this danger.

"About you who is a sinner thus being destroyed, people who have been humiliated by you being happy will speak thus: 'The cruel Ravana by God's grace has got this danger' ".

Sloka 15

śakyā lobhayituṃ nāhamaiśvaryeṇa dhanena vā || 5-21-15
ananyā rāghaveṇāhaṃ bhāskareṇa prabhā yathā |

15. aham = I; na shakyā = am not capable; lobhayitum = to be tempted; aisvaryeṇa = by wealth; dhanena vā = or by money;aham = I; ananyā = am not separate; rāghaveṇa = with Raghava; prabhā yathā = like the sun-shine; bhāskareṇa = with the sun.

"I am not capable to be tempted by wealth or by money. I am not separate with Raghava like the sun-shine with the sun."

Sloka 16

upadhāya bhujam tasya lokanāthasya satkṛtam || 5-21-16
katham nāmopadhāsyāmi bhujamanyasya kasya cit |

16. upadhāya = (after making) as a pillow; satkṛitam bhujam = that worshipped shoulder; lokanathasya = of that lord of the world; tasya = of that Rama;katham nāma = how; upadhāsyāmi = can I make as pillow; .anyasya kasya chit = some other one's; bhujam = shoulder.

"After making as a pillow that worshipped shoulder of that lord of the world, of that Rama, how can I make as pillow some other one's shoulder?"

Sloka 17

ahamaupayīkī bhāryā tasyaiva vasudhāpateḥ || 5-21-17
vratasnātasya viprasya vidyeva viditātmanaḥ |

17. vidyā iva = like the education;viprasya = of a brahmin; viditātmanaḥ = with a famous mind; vrata snātasya = initiated in religious vow; aham = I;aupayikī bharyā = am a suitable wife; tasyaiva = to that Rama alone; vasudhāpateḥ = the lord of earth.

"Like the education of a brahmin with a famous mind, initiated in religious vow, I am suitable wife to that Rama alone the lord of earth."

Sloka 18

sādhu rāvaṇa rāmeṇa māṃ samānaya duḥkhitām || 5-21-18
vane vāśitayā sārtham kareṇveva gajādhipam |

18. rāvaṇa = O Ravana!; mām samānaya = let me meet;sādhu = well; rāmeṇa sārdham = together with Rama; vāshitayā kareṇveva = like a female elephant; gajādhipam = with a (male) elephant; vane = in a forest.

"O Ravana! Let me meet together with Rama like a female elephant with a male elephant in a forest."

Sloka 19

mitramaupayikam kartum rāmaḥ sthānam parīpsatā || 5-21-19
vadham cānicchatā ghoraṃ tvayāsau puruṣarṣabhaḥ |

19. aupayikam = it is suited; asau = this; rāmaḥ = Rama; purushharshhabhaḥ = best among men; mitram kartum = is made to be a friend; tvayā = by you; parīpsatā = who is desiring; sthānam = continued existence; anichchhatā cha = and not desiring; ghoram vadham = a horrible slaying.

"It is suited that this Rama, best among men, is made to be a friend by you who is desiring continued existence and not desiring a horrible slaying."

Sloka 20

viditaḥ sa hi dharmajñaḥ śaraṇāgatavatsalaḥ || 5-21-20
tena maitrī bhavatu te yadi jīvitumiccasi |

20. saḥ = ḥe (Rama); dharmajJṇaḥ = the righteous one; viditaḥ hi = is indeed known;sharaṇāgatavatsalaḥ = as one who loves those who seek protection; ichchhasi yadi = if you desire; jīvitam = to live; maitrī bhavatu = let there be friendhip; te = to you; tena = with Rama.

"Rama the righteous one is indeed known as one who loves those who seek protection. If you desire to live. let there be friendship between you and Rama."

Sloka 21

prasādayasva tvaṃ canaṃ śaraṇāgatavatsalam || 5-21-21
māṃ cāsmai niyato bhūtvā niryātayitumarhasi |

21. tvam = you; prasādayasva = seek graciousness;enam = of this Rama; sharaṇāgatavatsalam = who loves those who seek protection;prayataḥ bhūtvā = becoming piously disposed; arhasi = you are suited; niryātayitum = to return; mām = me; asmai = to this Rama.

"You seek graciousness of this Rama who loves those who seek protection. Becoming piously disposed, you are suited to return me to this Rama."

Sloka 22

evam hi te bhavetsvasti sampradāya raghūttame || 5-21-22
anyathā tvam hi kurvaṇo vadham prāpsyasi rāvaṇa |

22. evam = in this way; sampradāya = giving (me); raghūttame = to Rama;bhavet = will become; svasti = fortunate; te = to you; ravaṇa = O Ravana!; tvam = you; anyathā kurvāṇa = doing in a different way; vadham prāpsyasi = will get slaying.

"In this way giving me to Rama will become fortunate to you. O Ravana! If you do in a different way, you will get slayed."

Sloka 23

varjayedvajramutsṛṣṭaṃ varjayedantakaściram || 5-21-23
tvadvidham tu na sa kruddho lokanāthaḥ sa rāghavaḥ |

23. utKRishhṭam = drawn out; vajram = Vajrayudha; the weapon of Indra; varjayet = may spare;tvadvidham = some one like you; antakaḥ = Lord of death; varjayet = may spare you; chiram = for a long time; saḥ Rāghavaḥ = that Rama; lokanāthaḥ = the lord of worlds; saṃkruddhaḥ = greatly enraged; na = will not (spare you).

"Drawn out Vajrayudha, the weapon of Indra may spare some one like you. Lord of death may spare you for a long time. That Rama the lord of worlds greatly enraged will not spare you."

Sloka 24

rāmasya dhanuṣaḥ śabdaṃ śroṣyasi tvaṃ mahāsvanam || 5-21-24
śatakratuvisṛṣṭasya nirghoṣamaśaneriva |

24. nirghoshhamiva = like the great sound; ashaneḥ = of Vajrayudha; shatakratuvisṛiṣṭaya = hurled by Indra; tvam shroshhyasi = you will hear; mahāsvanam = a loud sound; shabdam = which is sonorous; rāmasya dhanushhaḥ = of Ram's bow.

"Like the great sound of Vajrayudha hurled by Indra, you will hear a loud sound which is sonorous of Rama's bow."

Sloka 25

iha śīgraṃ suparvāṇo jvalitāsya ivoragāḥ || 5-21-25
iṣavo nipatiṣyanti rāmalakṣmaṇalakṣaṇāḥ |

25. ishhavaḥ = arrows; suparvāṇaḥ = with good nodes;prajvalitāsyāḥ = with blazing tips; uragāḥ iva = like snakes; rāmalakshmaṇa lakshaṇāḥ = with characteristic signs of Rama and Lakshmana nipatishhyanti = will fall down; iha = here (in Lanka); shīghram = soon.

"Arrows with good nodes, with blazing tips, like snakes, with characteristic signs of Rama and Lakshmana will fall down soon here in Lanka."

Sloka 26

rakṣāṃsi parinighnastaḥ puryāmasyāṃ samantataḥ || 5-21-26
asampātaṃ kariṣyanti patantaḥ kaṅkavāsasaḥ |

26. kaṇ^ka vāsasaḥ = arrows tied with the plumes of an eagle; parivighnantaḥ = will hit; rakshāṃsi = ogres; samantataḥ = everywhere; asyām puryām = in this city; karishhyanti = (and) will make; asaṃpātam = without space.

"Arrows tied with the plumes of an eagle will hit ogres everywhere in this city and will cover the city without space."

Sloka 27

rākṣasendramahāsarpān sa rāmagaruḍo mahān || 5-21-27
uddhariṣyati vegena vainateya ivoragān |

27. saḥ = ṭhat; rāmagarudaḥ = Garuda called Rama; vegena = speedily; uddharishhyati = will pluck; rākshasendramahāsarpān = the great serpents called ogres; vainateyaḥ uragāniva = like Vainateya (plucking) the serpents.

"ṭhat Garuda called Rama speedily will pluck the great serpants called ogres like Vainateya plucking the serpants."

Sloka 28

apaneṣyati māṃ bhartā tvattaḥ śīghramarindamaḥ || 5-21-28
asurebhyaḥ śriyaṃ dīptāṃ viṣṇustribhiriva kramaiḥ |

28. bhartā = my husband;ariṃdamaḥ = the destoyer of foes; mām apaneshhyati = will take me away; tvattaḥ = from you; vishhṇuḥ iva = like Lord Vishnu; tribhiḥ kramaiḥ = with three steps; dīptām shriyam = (taking away) brilliant prosperity; asurebhyaḥ = from Asuras.

"ṃy husband who is a destroyer of foes will take me away from you like Lord Vishnu with three steps taking away the brilliant prosperity from Asuras."

Sloka 29

janasthāne hatasthāne nihate rakṣasāṃ bale || 5-21-29
aśaktena tvayā rakṣaḥ kṛtametadasādhu vai |

29. rakshaḥ = O ogre!;rakshasām bale = (while the) army of ogres; nihate = was being killed; janasthāne = at Janasthana; hatasthāne = (which became) land of the dead; etat = this; asādhu = bad (deed); kṛitam vai = has been done; tvayā = by you; ashaktena = being powerless;.

"O ogre! While the army of ogres was being killed at Janasthana which became the land of dead, this bad deed has been done by you being powerless."

Sloka 30

aśramam tu tayoḥ śūnyaṃ praviśya narasimhayoḥ || 5-21-30
gocaraṃ gatayorrbhātrorapanītā tvayādhama |

30. adhama = O lowest one!; tayoḥ bhrātroḥ = (when) those brothers; gatayoḥ = were gone; gocharam = out; pravishya = entering; āshramam = hermitage; shūnyam = without them; apanitā = I have been abducted; tvayā = by you.

"O lowest one! When those brothers were gone out, entering hermitage without them, I have been abducted by you."

Sloka 31

na hi gandhamupāghrāya rāmalakṣmaṇayostvayā || 5-21-31
śakyaṃ saṃdarśane sthātuṃ śunā śārdūlayoriva |

31. shārddulayoriva = like tiger; shunā = by dog; upāghrāya = smelling; gandham = scent;rāmalakshmaṇayoḥ = of Rama and Lakshmana; na shakyam hi = it is indeed not possible; tvayā = by you; sthātum = to stand; saṃdarshane = (in their) view.

"Like tiger by dog, smelling the scent of Rama and Lakshmana it is indeed not possible by you to stand in their view."

Sloka 32

tasya te vigrahe tābhyāṃ yugagrahaṇamasthiram || 5-21-32
vṛtrasyevendrabāhubhyāṃ bāhorekasya vigrahe |

32. te = ṭo you; tasya = like that; vigrahe = enmity; tabhyām = with those two; vigrahe iha = is like the enmity; ekasya bahoḥ = of one hand; vṛitrasya = of Vruttasura; indra bāhubhyām = with two hands of Indra; asthiram = (and) it is not persevering; yugagrahanam = (for you) to be an opponent (with them).

"ṭo you like that, enmity with those two is like the enmity of one hand of Vruttasura with two hands of Indra and it is not persevering for you to be an opponent with them."

Sloka 33

kṣipraṃ tava sa nātho me rāmaḥ saumitriṇā saha || 5-21-33
toyamalpamivādityaḥ prānānādāsyate śaraiḥ |

33. me nāthaḥ = my husband; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; saumitriṇā saha = together with Lakshmana; adāsyate = will take away; sharaiḥ = with ḥis arrows; tava prāṇān = your life; kshipram = quickly;ādityaḥ iva = like the sun;alpam toyam = (drying) shallow water.

"ṃy husband that Rama together with Lakshmana will take away with ḥis arrows your life quickly like the sun drying up shallow water."

Sloka 34

giriṃ kuberasya gato.athavālayam |
sabhāṃ gato vā varuṇasya rājñaḥ |
asaṃśayaṃ dāśaratherna mokṣyase |
mahādrumaḥ kālahato.aśaneriva || 5-21-34

34. kāla hataḥ = Killed by time; kuberasya girim gatam = (even if you) go to the mountain of Kubera; athavā = or; ālyam = to his house; gato vā = or if you go; sabhām = to the assembly; rājJṇaḥ varunasya = of King Varuna; asaṃshayaṃ = without doubt; na mokshyase = you will not be released; dāsharatheḥ = from Rama; mahādrumaḥ = like a great tree; ashani = (from) thunderbolt.

"Killed by time even if you go to the mountain of Kubera or to his house or if you go to the assembly of King Varuna, without doubt you will not be relased from Rama like a great tree from thunderbolt."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ekaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 21st chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
