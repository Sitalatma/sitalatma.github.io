Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 30
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The persistence of Seetha continues to go along with Rama to forest. But Rama tried to pacify her telling that rendering service to elders staying back at home is advisable for ladies like Seetha, but in vain. Finally Rama had to yield to her prevail and accepts her to come along and asks her to give away her personal belongings to Brahmins in charity before they proceed.

Sloka 1

sāntvyamānā tu rāmeṇa maithilī janaka ātmajā |
vana vāsa nimittāya bhartāram idam abravīt || 2-30-1

1. maithili = ṣeetha; janakātmajā = daughter of Janaka; sāntvamānā = being pacified; rāmeṇa = by Rama; vanavāsanimittāya = in the matter of living in the forest; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; bhartāram = to her husband.

Seetha daughter of Janaka being pacified by Rama in the matter of living in the forest, spoke the following words to her husband.

Sloka 2

sā tam uttama samvignā sītā vipula vakṣasam |
praṇayāc ca abhimānāc ca paricikṣepa rāghavam || 2-30-2

2. sāsīta = that ṣeetha; tam = distressed; uttamasamvignā = highly agitated; praṇayāt = from affection abhimānāchcha = and pride; parichikshhepa = reproached; rāghavam = Rama; vipula vakshhasam = having a broad chest.

Distressed and highly agitated, the said Seetha reproached Rama having a broad chest, from affection and pride in the following words:-

Sloka 3

kim tvā amanyata vaidehaḥ pitā me mithilā adhipaḥ |
rāma jāmātaram prāpya striyam puruṣa vigraham || 2-30-3

3. rāma = Oh; Rama! kim = what; mepitā = my father; mithilādhipaḥ = the king of ṃithila; vaidehaḥ = belonging to the country of Videha; amanyata = think of himself; prāpya = having got; jāmātaram = as son-in-law; tvam = you; striyam = a woman; purushhavigraham = having form of a man.

"What my father, the king of ṃithila belonging to the country of Videha, think of himself having got as so-in-law you, a woman having the form of a man?"

Sloka 4

anṛtam bala loko ayam ajñānāt yadd hi vakṣyati |
tejo na asti param rāme tapati iva divā kare || 2-30-4

4. bata = what a pity! ayam lokaḥ = these people; vakshhyati yadi = if they tell; ajJṇānāt = by ignorance; anṛitam = the falsehood; param = excellent; tejaḥ = valour; nāsti = is lacking; rāme = in Rama; divākare iva = as in a sun; tapati = which is blazing.

"It is a pity if these people of Ayodhya through ignorance tell the falsehood that excellent valour is lacking in Rama as in a blazing sun."

Sloka 5

kim hi kṛtvā viṣaṇṇaḥ tvam kutaḥ vā bhayam asti te |
yat parityaktu kāmaḥ tvam mām ananya parāyaṇām || 2-30-5

5. kihikṛitvā = for what reason; tvam = you; vishhaṇṇaḥ = are cast down? kutaḥvā = whence; bhayam = fear; asti = is there; te = to you; parityaktukāmaḥ itityat = for which you are willing to desert; mām = me; ananya parāyaṇām = for whom there is no other recourse.

"For what reason are you cast down or whence fear in you, for which you are willing to desert me, for whom there is no other recourse."

Sloka 6

dyumatsena sutam vīra satyavantam anuvratām |
sāvitrīm iva mām viddhi tvam ātma vaśa vartinīm || 2-30-6

6. vīra = Oh; brave man! viddhi = know; mām = me; ātmavashavartinīm = to be as dependent on your will; savitrīmiva = as ṣavitri; anuvratām = who was devoted to; satyavantam = ṣatyavan; dyumatsenasutan = son of Dyunmatsena.

"Oh, Brave man! Know me to be as dependent on your will as Savitri who was devoted to Satyavan the son of Dyumatsena."

Sloka 7

na tu aham manasā api anyam draṣṭā asmi tvad ṛte anagha |
tvayā rāghava gacceyam yathā anyā kula pāṃsanī || 2-30-7

7. rāghava = Rama; anagha = the faultless man! aham = I; na drashhṭāsmi = will not see; manasāpi = even in thought; anyam = anyone; tvadṛite = except you; anyāyathā = as any other woman; kulapāmsanī = bringing disrepute to her family would! gachchheyam = I shall go; tvayā = along with you.

"Rama, the faultless man! I will not see, even in thought, anyone except you as any other woman bringing disrepute to her family would! I shall accompany you to the forest."

Sloka 8

svayam tu bhāryām kaumārīm ciram adhyuṣitām satīm |
śailūṣaiva mām rāma parebhyo dātum iccasi || 2-30-8

8. rāma = Oh; Rama! kaumārīm = I am a young girl; adhyushhitām = lived; chiram = for a long time; satīm = a chaste woman; bhāryām = and a wife; ichchhasi = you intend; dātum = to deliver; parebhyaḥ = to others; mām = me; shailūshhaḥ iva = like an actor; svayam = by your own will.

" Oh, Rama! I am a young girl, lived for a long time with you, a chaste woman and your wife. How would you like an actor intend to deliver me to oehrs by your own will?"

Sloka 9

yasya pathyam ca rāmāttha yasya cārthe.avarudhyase |
tvam tasya bhava vaśyaśca vidheyaścha sadānagha|| 2-30-9

9. rāma = Oh; Rama; anagha = the sinless one! bhava = be; tvam = you; sadā = always; vashyaḥ cha = dutiful and; vidhayaḥ cha = obedient; tasya = to him; yasya = whom; ātha = you speak; pathyam = of welfare; yasyachārthe = for whose sake; avarudhyase = I am beikept back.

"Oh, Rama the sinless one! Be you always dutiful and obedient to him, whom you speak of welfare, for whose sake I am being kept back.(But it is improper to ask me to stay obedient to him)."

Sloka 10

sa mām anādāya vanam na tvam prasthātum arhasi |
tapo vā yadi vā araṇyam svargo vā syāt saha tvayā || 2-30-10

10. nārhasi = It is not proper; saḥ tvam = for you as such; prasthātum = to set out; vanam = to the forest; anādāya = without taking; mām = me; tapovā = even austerities; yadivā = or; araṇyam = forest; svargovā = or even heaven; me = to me; tvayā saha = along with you only.

"It is not proper for you as such to set out to the forest without taking me. Any term of austerities or forest or even heaven, let it be to me with you only."

Sloka 11

na ca me bhavitā tatra kaścit pathi pariśramaḥ |
pṛṣṭhataḥ tava gaccantyā vihāra śayaneṣv api || 2-30-11

11. me = to me; gachchhantyāḥ = who is going; pṛishhṭhataḥ = behind; tava = you; tatra = there; na bhavitā = will be no; parishramaḥ = fatigue; kashchit = whatsoever; pathi = in the path; vitārashayaneshhviva = as in a place of recreation or sleep.

"To me, who follow you behind, there will be no tiresomeness. I shall remain in the path without any fatigue, as remaining in a place of recreation or as in a sleep.

Sloka 12

kuśa kāśa śara iṣīkā ye ca kaṇṭakino drumāḥ |
tūla ajina sama sparśā mārge mama saha tvayā || 2-30-12

12. mārge = while in the path; tvayāsaha = with you; kushakāsha shareshhīkāḥ = blades of kusha grass; shrubs by the name of Kasa; reeds and rushes; yedrumāḥ cha = which plants; kaṇtakina = with prickles; tulājina sama sparshāḥ = will touch like heap of cotton or soft deerskin.

"While walking with you, blades of kusha grass, shrubs by the name of kāsa, reeds and rushes and plants with prickles which fall in the path will touch my soles like a heap of cotton or soft deerskin."

Sloka 13

mahā vāta samuddhūtam yan mām avakariṣyati |
rajo ramaṇa tan manye para ardhyam iva candanam || 2-30-13

13. ramaṇa = Oh; beloved! yat = which; rajaḥ = dust; mahāvāta samuddhūtam = hurled by a strong wind; avakarishhyati = will cover; mām = me; manye = I reckon; tat = it; chandanam iva = as sandal dust; parārthyam = of highest blessing.

"Oh, beloved! I shall reckon the dust raised by the strongest wind that will cover my body as sandal dust of highest advantage."

Sloka 14

śādvaleṣu yad āsiṣye vana ante vana goracā |
kuthā āstaraṇa talpeṣu kim syāt sukhataram tataḥ || 2-30-14

14. syātkim = Is it; sukhataram = more comfortable; kuthāstaraṇa talpeshhu = on beds covered with carpets; tataḥ = than; yathā = as like; shishye = lying; shādvaleshhu = on gree grass; vanānte = in the midst of a forest; vanagocharā = while dwelling in the forest.

"While dwelling in the forest, in its midst, I shall lie down on green grass. Will lying in beds with carpets be more comfortable than that?"

Sloka 15

patram mūlam phalam yat tvam alpam vā yadi vā bahu |
dāsyasi svayam āhṛtya tan me amṛta rasa upamam || 2-30-15

15. yat = which; patram = leaf; mūlam = tuber; phalam = fruit; alpam vā = either a little; yadivā = or; bahu = abundant; āhṛitya = brought; dāsyasi = given by; tvam = you; svayam = yourself; tat = that; bhavet = will be; amṛitarasopamam = like nectar; me = to me.

"Leaves, tubers and fruits either a little or abundant in quantity brought and given by you yourself will be like nectar to me."

Sloka 16

na mātur na pitus tatra smariṣyāmi na veśmanaḥ |
ārtavāni upabhunjānā puṣpāṇi ca phalāni ca || 2-30-16

16. upabhuṇjānā = enjoying; pushhpāṇi = the flowers; phalānicha = and fruits; ārtavāni = belonging to various seasons; tatra = there; na smarishhyāni = I shall neither recollect; mātuḥ = my mother; pituḥna = nor my father; na = nor; veshmanaḥ = my home.

"Enjoying the flowers and fruits belonging to various seasons there, I shall neither recollect my mother nor father nor my home."

Sloka 17

na ca tatra gataḥ kiṃcit draṣṭum arhasi vipriyam |
mat kṛte na ca te śoko na bhaviṣyāmi durbharā || 2-30-17

17. tataḥ = hence; nārhasi = you are not justified drashhṭum = to see; kimchit = anything; vipriyam = unpleasant; tatra = there; natkṛite = because of me; na = no; shokaḥ = annoyance; te = to you; na bhavishhyāmi = I will not be; durbharā = difficult to be maintained.

"Hence, you are not justified to see anything unpleasant there. There will be no annoyance to you because of me. I will not be difficult to be maintained."

Sloka 18

yaḥ tvayā saha sa svargo nirayo yaḥ tvayā vinā |
iti jānan parām prītim gacca rāma mayā saha || 2-30-18

18. yaḥ = which companionship; tvayāsaha = with you; saḥ = that(itself is) svargaḥ = heaven; tvayā vinā = without you; nirayaḥ = hell; jānan = by knowing; iti = thus; rama = Oh; Rama; gachchha = obtain; parām- great; prītim = joy; mayāsaha = with me.

"Your companionship will be a heaven to me. Without you, it will be a hell. Oh, Rama! By knowing thus my great love, obtain supreme joy with me."

Sloka 19

atha mām evam avyagrām vanam na eva nayiṣyasi |
viṣam adya eva pāsyāmi mā viśam dviṣatām vaśam || 2-30-19

19. atha = on the contrary; naiva nayishhyasiyadi = if you do not take; mām = me; avyagrām = who is not alarmed; evam = as such; vanam = of the forest; pāsyāmi = I shall drink; vishham = poison; adyaiva = now itself; māgamam = I shall not bow to the will; dvishhatām = of the enemies.

"On the contrary, if you do not take me, who is not alarmed of the forest as such, I shall drink poison now itself. But on no account would I bow to the enemies."

Sloka 20

paścāt api hi duhkhena mama na eva asti jīvitam |
ujjhitāyāḥ tvayā nātha tadā eva maraṇam varam || 2-30-20

20. nātha = Oh; Lord! mam = to me; ujjhitāyāḥ = abandored; tvayā = by you; duḥkhana = because of grief; jīvitam = life; naivāsti = will not be there; pashchādapi = even afterwards; maraṇam = death; varam = is better; tadaiva = at the time of your relinquishment itself.

"Oh, Lord! As a result of grief I will not going to live even afterwards when abandoned by you. Death is therefore better at the time of your relinquishment itself."

Sloka 21

idam hi sahitum śokam muhūrtam api na utsahe |
kim punar daśa varṣāṇi trīṇi ca ekam ca duhkhitā || 2-30-21

21. notsahe = I do not agree; sahitum = to bear; imam = this; shokam = grief; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment; kimpunaḥ = why again; dashatrīṇiekamcha = fourteen(ten; three and one) varshhāṇi = years; duḥkhitā = in misery.

"I cannot bear this grief even for a moment, why again fourteen years in misery."

Sloka 22

iti sā śoka samtaptā vilapya karuṇam bahu |
cukrośa patim āyastā bhṛśam ālingya sasvaram || 2-30-22

22. sā = that ṣeetha; shokasantaptā = burnt by grief; āyastā = having exerted; vilapya = lamented; bahu = much; karuṇam = in pity; āliṇgya = embraced; patim = the husband; chukrosha = cried; bhṛisham = a lot; sasvaram = with loud voice.

Seetha, burnt by grief, having got exerted, lamented much piteously, embraced her husband and cried a lot with loud voice.

Sloka 23

sā viddhā bahubhir vākyaiḥ digdhaiḥ iva gaja anganā |
cira samniyatam bāṣpam mumoca agnim iva araṇiḥ || 2-30-23

23. viddhā = pierced; bahubhiḥ = by many; vākyaiḥ = words; sā = she; gajāṇganā iva = like a female elephant; dighaiḥ = by poisoned arrows; bhāshhpam = tears; chirasanniyatam = which were held back for long; mumocha = were shed; agrimiva = like fire; araṇiḥ = by piece of wood(through attrition with another)

persecuted with many homilies like a female elephant pierced with poisoned arrows, she shed tears that had long been held back even as a piece of wood would emit fire(throught attrition with another)

Sloka 24

tasyāḥ sphaṭika samkāśam vāri samtāpa sambhavam |
netrābhyām parisusrāva pankajābhyām iva udakam || 2-30-24

24. spatika sankāsham = crystal-like; vāri = water; parisusrāva = oozed; netrābhyām = from eyes; tasyāḥ = of her; santāpa sambharam = by meeting sorrow; udakamiva = like water; paṇkajābhyām = from two lotus flowers.

Crystal-like tears woozed from her eyes because of grief, like water-drops from two lotus flowers.

Sloka 25

taccaivāmalacandhrabham mukhamāyatalocanam |
paryaśuṣyata bāṣpeṇa jaloddhṛtamivāmubujam || 2-30-25

25. tat = that; mukhamchaiva = face too; amalachandrābham = with shining brilliance of the moon; āyatalochanam = having longish eyes; paryashushhyata = withered; bhāshhpeṇa = with tears; ambujamiva = like lotus; jaloddhṛitam = pulled out from water.

Her face with shining brilliance of the moon and having longish eyes, withered with tears as a lotus flower pulled out from water.

Sloka 26

tām pariṣvajya bāhubhyām visamjñām iva duhkhitām |
uvāca vacanam rāmaḥ pariviśvāsayaṃs tadā || 2-30-26

26. tadā = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; parishhvajya = embraced; tām = her; bāhubhyām = with arms; duḥkhitām = who was depressed; visaṃjJṇāmi = and had fainted; uvācha = spoke; vachanam = the words; parivishvāsayan = fully reassuring.

Then Rama embraced her, with arms, who was depressed and had fainted as it were, spoke the following words, fully reassuring her.

Sloka 27

na devi tava duhkhena svargam api abhirocaye |
na hi me asti bhayam kiṃcit svayambhor iva sarvataḥ || 2-30-27

27. devi = Oh; queen! na abhirochaye = I do not like; svargamapi = even heaven; tava duḥkhena = by your sorrow; svayambhoriva = like to the god of Brahma; me = to me; nāsti hi = there is indeed no; bhayam = fear; kimchit = whatsoever.

"Oh, queen! I do not relish even heaven while you are in grief. There is indeed no fear whatsoever to me, like the god of Brahma.'

Sloka 28

tava sarvam abhiprāyam avijñāya śubha ānane |
vāsam na rocaye araṇye śaktimān api rakṣaṇe || 2-30-28

28. shubhānane = Oh; ṣeetha with the auspicious face! shaktimānapi = eventhough I am able; rakshhaṇe = to protect; avijJṇāya = without knowing; tava = your; abhiprāyam = opinion; sarvam = in entirety; na rochaye = I do not like; vāsam = your dwelling; araṇye = in the forest.

"Oh, Seetha with the auspicious face! Eventhough I am able to protect you, without knowing your entire opinion, I do not like to take you to the forest."

Sloka 29

yat sṛṣṭā asi mayā sārdham vana vāsāya maithili |
na vihātum mayā śakyā kīrtir ātmavatā yathā || 2-30-29

29. maithili = Oh; ṣeeta! yat = for what reason; sṛishhṭāsi = you are created; vanavāsāya = for dwelling in the forest; mayā sārtham = along with me;(for that reason) na shakyā = you cannot be; vihātum = left behind; mayā = by me; kīrtiryathā = like honour; ātmavatā = by a man of self-regard.

"Oh, Seetha! it appears that you are created indeed for dwelling in the forest with me. Hence, you cannot be left behind by me, like the honour by a man of self-regard."

Sloka 30

dharmaḥ tu gaja nāsa ūru sadbhir ācaritaḥ purā |
tam ca aham anuvarte adya yathā sūryam suvarcalā || 2-30-30

30. gajanāsoru = Oh; ṣeetha; with thighs like posteriors of elephant! purā = earlier; dharmaḥ = righteousness(like fulfilling father's command); ācharitaḥ = was practised; sadbhiḥ = by good men; adya = now; aham = I; anuvarte = shall follow; tam = that righteousness; sūryam yathā = like the sun; suvarchalā = by suvarchala(wife of the sun).

"Oh Seetha, with thighs like the posteriors of an elephant! Earlier, righeousness (like fulfilling the father's command) was practised by good men. Now, I shall follow that righteousness, like Suvarchala(wife of the sun) following the sun."

Sloka 31

na khalvaham na gaccheyam vanam janakanandini |
vacanam tnnayati mām pituḥ satyopabṛṃhitam || 2-30-31

31. janakanandini = Oh; ṣeetha! aham = I; nakhalunagachchheyam = cannot desist from not going; vanam = to forest; tatvachaḥ = that word; pituḥ = of my father; satyopabṛimhitam = made strong by his truthfulness; nayati = is taking; mām = me.

"Oh, Seetha! I cannot desist from not going to forest. That word, of my father, made strong by his truthfulnes is taking me to the forest."

Sloka 32

eṣa dharmaḥ tu suśroṇi pitur mātuḥ ca vaśyatā |
ataḥ ca ājñām vyatikramya na aham jīvitum utsahe || 2-30-32

32. sushroṇi = Oh; comely lady! vashyatā = being obedient; putuḥ = to father; mātushcha = and mother; eshhaḥ = this is ; dharmastu = sacred duty; atashcha = that is why; aham = I na utsahe = do not wish; jīvitum = to survive; vyatikramya = in violation of tam = that duty.

"Oh, comely lady! Being obedient to father and mother is one's sacred duty. That is why, I do not wish to survive, in violation of that duty."

Sloka 33

asvādhīnam katham daivam prakārairabhirādhyate |
svādhīnam samatikramya mātaram pitaram gurum || 2-30-33

33. samatikramya = by neglecting; mātaram = mother; pitaram = father; gurum = teacher; svādhīnam = who are at one's disposal; katham = how; daivam = god; asvādhīnam = who is not one's disposal; abhirādhyate = can be worshipped; prakāraiḥ = by various modes?

"ṃother, father and teacher are at our own disposal. By negelecting them as such, how can we worship god, who is not at our disposal, by various modes?"

Sloka 34

yattrayam tattrayo lokāḥ pavitram tatsamam bhuvi |
nānyadasti śubhāpāṅge tenedamabhirādhyate || 2-30-34

34. shubhāpāṇge = Oh; lady with enchanting glances! yat = which trayam = tinity (of mother; father and teacher) is there; tat = that is ; trayaḥ lokāḥ = the world-triad; nāsti = there is none; anyat = other; loke = in the world; tatsamam = equal to it; tena = hence; idam = this; abhirādhyate = is being worshipped.

"Oh, lady with enchanting glances! The triad of mother, father and teacher is the world-triad. There is none other in the world equal to it. Hence, this triad is worthy of worship."

Sloka 35

na satyam dānamānau vā na yajñāścāptadakṣiṇāḥ |
tathā balakarāḥ sīte yathā sevā piturmatā || 2-30-35

35. sīte = Oh; ṣeetha! yathā = In which manner; sevā = service; pituḥ = to father; matā = is regarded; tathā = in that manner; satyam = truth; na = is not; yajJṇāḥcha = even sacrificial rites; āptadakshhiṇāḥ = where presents are received by officiating priest; balakarāḥ na = are not strengthening.

"Oh, Seetha! In which manner service to father is regarded as strengthening, in the same manner truth or sacrificial rites where presents are received by officiating priest are not strengthening."

Sloka 36

svargo dhanam vā dhānyam vā vidyāḥ putrāḥ sukhāni ca |
guruvṛttyanurodhena na kiṃcit\dapi durlabham || 2-30-36

36. guruvṛittyanurodhena = by compliance with the wishes of one's elders; svargaḥ = heavenly bliss; dhanamvā = or riches; dhānyamvā = foodgrains; vidyāḥ = or learning; putrāḥ- sons; sukhānicha = and amenities of life; na = nothing; kimchidapi = whatsoever; durlabham = is hard to obtain.

"By compliance with the wishes of one's elders, heavenly bliss or riches, foodgrains or leaving, sons or amenities of life - nothing whatsoever is hard to obtain."

Sloka 37

devagandharvagolokān brahmalokam tathāparān |
prāpnuvanti mahātmāno mātāpitṛparāyaṇāḥ || 2-30-37

37. mahātmanaḥ = high souled men; mātāpitṛiparāyaṇāḥ = wholly devoted to their mother and father; prāpnuvanti = obtain; deva gandharva golokān = the regions of gods and the gandharvas; and goloka (highest heaven abiding in cows and presided over by ṣri Radha and ṣri Krishna the first couple) brahma loka; = the seventh heaven presided by Brahma ( creator) tathā = and; aparan = other regions.

"High souled men, wholly devoted to their parents obtain the regions of gods and the gandharvas, the seventh heaven presided the gandharvas, the seventh heaven presided by Brahma (the creator) and Goloka (the highest heaven abiding in cows and presided over by Sri Radha and Sri Krishna the first couple).

Sloka 38

sa mām pitā yathā śāsti satya dharma pathe sthitaḥ |
tathā vartitum iccāmi sa hi dharmaḥ sanātanaḥ || 2-30-38

38. ichchhāmi = i desire; vartitum = to obey; tathā = in the same manner; yathā = as to how; saḥ pitā = such a father; sthitaḥ = abiding; satyadharma pathe = in truthful and righful way; shāsti = commands; mām = me; saḥ = that itself; dharmaḥ hi = is indeed righteousness; sanātanaḥ = which is eternal.

"I desire to obey in the same manner as to how my father, abiding in truthfulness and virtue, commands me. That itself is indeed the law of morality, which is eternal."

Sloka 39

mama sannā matiḥ sīte tvām netum daṇḍakāvanam |
vasiṣyāmīti sātvam māmanuyātum suniścitā || 2-30-39

39. sīte Oh; ṣeetha! mama = my; matiḥ = mind; sannā = was depressed; netum = to take; tvām- you; daṇdakā vanam = to the forest of Dandaka; sā tvam = you as such; iti = stating that; vasishhyāmi = you would reside in the forest; sunishchitā = well-determined; amyātum = to follow; mām = me.

"Oh, Seetha! ṃy mind was depressed to take you to the forest of Dandaka. But you are saying that you will reside in the forst, duly determined to follow me."

Sloka 40

sā hi diṣṭā.anavadyāṅgī vanāya vadirekṣaṇe |
anugaccasva mām bhīru saha dharma carī bhava || 2-30-40

40. madirekshhaṇe = Oh; mistress with fascinating eyes; anavadyāṇgī = and flawless limbs! sā = since you; dishhṭā = were allowed; vanāya = to come to forest; anugachchhasva = follow; mām = me; bhīru = Oh ṣeetha; the timid lady! bhava = become; shadharmachārī = a help-mate.

"Oh, mistress with fascinating eyes and flawless limbs! Since you were allowed to come to forest, Oh Seetha the timid lady, follow me and become my help-mate."

Sloka 41

sarvathā sadṛśam sīte mama svasya kulasya ca |
vyavasāyamanukrāntā kānte tvamatiśobhanam || 2-30-41

41. sīte = Oh; ṣeetha; kānte = the beautiful! tvam = you; anukrāntā = followed; atishobhanam = very auspicious; vyavasayam = resolve; sadṛisham = suitable; sarvathā = in all ways; mama = to me; svasya kulasyacha = and to your race.

"Oh, Seetha the beautiful! You adopted very auspicious resolve, suitable in all ways to me and to your race."

Sloka 42

ārabhasva śubhaśroṇi vanavāsakṣamāḥ kriyāḥ |
nedānīm tvadṛte sīte svargo.api mama rocate || 2-30-42

42. shubhashroṇi = Oh lady; with charming hip and loins! idānīm = now itself; ārbhasva = begin with; driyāḥ = the duties; vanavāsakshhamāḥ = appropriate for living in exile; sīte = Oh; ṣeetha! svargo.api = even heaven; na rochate = is not a liking; mama = to me; tvadṛite = without you.

"Oh, lady with charming hip and loins! Now itself, begin with duties appropriate for living in exile. Oh, Seetha! Even a heaven is not a liking to me without your presence."

Sloka 43

brāhmaṇebhyaḥ ca ratnāni bhikṣukebhyaḥ ca bhojanam |
dehi ca āśaṃsamānebhyaḥ samtvarasva ca māciram || 2-30-43

43. dehi = give; ratnānicha = valnable gifts; brāhmaṇebhyaḥ = to Brahmanas; bhojanamcha = and good; bhikshhukebhyaḥ = to mendicants; santvarasvacha = be very quick; mā = do not; chiram = delay.

"Give valuable gifts to Brahmanas and food to mendicants. Be very quick. Do not delay."

Sloka 44

bhūṣaṇāni mahārhāṇi varavastrāṇi yāni ca |
ramaṇīyāśca ye kecitkrīḍārthāścāpuyupaskarāḥ || 2-30-44
śayanīyāni yānāni mama cānyāni yāni ca |
dehi svabhṛtyavargasya brāhmaṇānāmanantaram || 2-30-45

44;45. dehi = give; svabhṛityavargasya = to the various classes of your dependents; yāni = whatever; mahārhāṇi = fine articles of wearing apparel; upaskarāḥ = gadgets; krīdārthāḥ = used for sports; mama = my; shayanāni = couches; yānāni = conveyances; anyāni = other; yānicha = articles left; anantaram = after; brahmaṇānām = satisfying brahmanas.

"Give to the various classes of your dependents whatever costly ornaments, beautiful and fine articles of wearing apparel, gadgets used for sports, my couches, conveyances and other articles left after satisfying brahmanas."

Sloka 46

anukūlam tu sā bhartur jñātvā gamanam ātmanaḥ |
kṣipram pramuditā devī dātum eva upacakrame || 2-30-46

46.sā devī = ṣeetha; the divine lady; pramuditā = elated; jJṇātvā = to know; ātmanaḥ = her; gamanam = departure; anukūlam = acceptable; bhartuḥ = to her husband; kshhipram = quickly; upachakrame = se about; dātumeva = making gifts.

Seetha the divine lady, elated to know her departure acceptable to her husband, quickly set about, making gifts.

Sloka 47

tataḥ prahṛṣṭā paripūrṇa mānasā |
yaśasvinī bhartur avekṣya bhāṣitam |
dhanāni ratnāni ca dātum anganā |
pracakrame dharmabhṛtām manasvinī || 2-30-47

47. tataḥ = thereafter; yashashivinī = the illustrious; manssvinī = and the pure minded; aṇganā = lady; avekshhya = hearing; bhāshhitam = the speech; bhartuḥ = of her husband; prahṛishhṭā = and delighted; pratipūrṇamānasā = feeling re-assured; prachakrame = started in mind; dātum = giving; dhanāni = riches; ratnānicha = and valuable gifts; dharma bhṛitām = to virtuous souls.

Thereafter, the illustrious and pure-minded lady, becoming delighted on hearing the speech of her husband, started giving riches and valuable gifts to virtuous souls.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 30th chapter in the Ayodhya kanda of the glorious Ramayayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
