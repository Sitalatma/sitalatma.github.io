Book VI : Kishkindha Kanda - Book Of War

Introduction

Rama sees Kumbhakarna with a colossal body and enquires Vibhishana about a report of him. Then Vibhishana apprises Rama about Kumbhakarna and narrates his story of his getting curse from Brahma, imprecating him to sleep apparently Vibhishana adds that on an appeal from Ravana Brahma relaxed the curse saying that Kumbhakarna would keep sleeping for every six months and then waking up for a day in a year Vibhishana informs Rama that Ravana got Kumbhakarna awakened and is sending now to the battle. Rama then orders Neela the commander-in-cheif to marshal his entire army of monkeys in their appropriate positions in Lanka. On hearing Rama's orders the army of monkeys start their fighting operation.

Sloka 1

kirīṭinam mahākāyam kumbhakarṇam dadarśa ha || 6-61-1

1. ādāya = having taken; dhanuḥ = his bow; rāmaḥ = Rama; mahātejāḥ = of great brilliance; vīryavān = and prowess; tataḥ = then; dadarsha = saw; mahākāyam = the bulky; kumbhakarṇam = Kumbhakarna; kirriṭinam = decorated with a diadem.

Having taken his bow, Rama of great brilliance and prowess, then beheld the bulky Kumbhakarna, adorned with a diadem.

Sloka 2

2. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam rākṣasashreṣṭham = that paramount demon; parvatākāra darshanam = whose appearance was in the form of a mountain; prabhum nārāyaṇam yathā = looking like Lord ṇarayana (the son of the original ṃan); kramamāṇam = taking strides across; ākāsham = space; purā = long ago (manifested in the form of ṭrivikrama; who measured the whole universe in a couple of strides) Rama became vigilant).

Seeing that paramount demon, whose appearance was in the form of a mountain and looking like Lord Narayana (the son of the original ṃan); taking strides across space, (manifested in the form of Trivikrama, who measured the whole universe in a couple of strides) Rama became vigilant.

Beholding him again, who was appear like a rainy black cloud and wearing a golden bracelet as an ornament on his upper arm, that huge army of monkeys took to their heels in full speed.

Sloka 5

"This eminent demon, appearing as a mountain, with a diadem and tawmy eyes and looking like a cloud with a flash of lightning, is seen in Lanka. Who is he?"

Sloka 6

Thus asked by Rama the prince, who was unweary in action, the highly intelligent Vibhishana replied to Rama as follows:

Sloka 9

saiṣa viśravasaḥ putraḥ kumbhakarṇaḥ pratāpavān || 6-61-9

"He is the powerful Kumbhakarna, the son of Visravasa. He conquered Yama and Indra in battle. Tehre is no other demon, corresponding to the size of his body."

Sloka 10

yakṣābhujamgāḥ piśitāśanāśca |

"O Rama! He conquered thousands of celestials, demons, a class of demi-gods, serpent demons, ogres, Gandharvas teh celestial musicians, Vidyadharas the celestials artistes and Kinnaras (the semi-divine beings with the human form and the head of a horse) in battle."

Sloka 11

11. kumbhakarṇam mahābalam = (ṣeeing) the mighty Kumbhakarna; virūpakṣam = having ghastly eyes; shūlapāṇim = with a spear in hand; tridashāḥ = the celestials; na shekuḥ = were not able; hantum = to kill (him); mohitāḥ = confused to think ayam kālaḥ iti = that he was Yama the god of Death.

"Seeing the mighty Kumbharana with ghastly eyes and a spear in hand, the celestials were not able to kill him, having confused to think that he was yama the god of Death."

Sloka 12

anyeṣām rākṣaseṃdrāṇām varadānakṛtam balam || 6-61-12

12. eṣaḥ = this; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbharna; prakṛitā = by his very nature; tejasvī = is energetic; mahābalaḥ = and mighty; balam = the strength; anyeṣām rākṣasendrāṇām = of the other demons-chiefs; varadānakṛitam = is attributable to boons; (granted on request by gods).

"This Kumbhakarna, by his very nature, is energetic and mighty. The strength of the other demon-chiefs is attributable to boons (granted on request by gods)."

Sloka 13

bhakṣitāni sahasrāṇi sattvānām subahūnyapi || 6-61-13

13. jātamātreṇa = soon after his birth; sabahūni api = several; sahasrāṇi = thousands; sattvānām = were eaten; etena mahātmanā = by this great souled demon; kṣudhārtena = afflicted as he was by hunger.

"Soon after his birth, this great-souled demon, afflicted as he was by hunger, ate thousands of living creatures."

Sloka 14

yānti sma śaraṇam śakram tamapyartham nyavedayan || 6-61-14

Sloka 15

sa śakravajrabhihato mahātmā |

That ṃahendra wielding a thunder-bolt was enraged and struck Kumbhakarna with his weapon of the white thunderbolt. The great souled Kumbhakarna struck by Indra's thunderbolt was quite agitatated at it and roared loudly with anger."

Sloka 16

16. shrutvā = hearing; ninādam = the roar; tasya kumbhakarṇasya = of that Kumbhakarna; rakṣasaḥ = the demon; nānadyamānasya = who was howling loudly; vitrastāḥ = the frightened; prajāḥ = people; bhūyaḥ viratrasuḥ = were further frightened.

Sloka 17

"Enraged as he was on ṃahendra, the mighty Kumbhakarna then pulled out a tusk of Airavata, Indra's elephant and struck it into Indra's chest."

Sloka 18

"That Devendra, tormented by the hit of Kumbhakarna, was burning with rage. The celestials, Brahmanical sages and demons were suddenly aggrieved. Even Indra with his people went to the abode of Brahma the Lord of creation."

Sloka 19

āśramadhvaṃsanam cāpi parastrīharaṇam tathā || 6-61-20

19; 20. te = they; shashamsuḥ = informed; daurātmyam = about the evil-mindedness; kumbhakarṇasya = of Kumbhakarna; prajāpateḥ = to brahama; (saying about); bhakṣaṇamchāpi = the eating away; prajānām = of the living creatures; dharṣaṇam cha = the assault; divaukasām = on celestials; āshrama dhvamsanamcha api = the destroyal of hermitages; tathā = and; parastrīharaṇam = the taking away of others' wives.

"They informed about the evil-mind of Kumbhakarna to Brahma, how he devoured the living creatures, his assault on celestials, the destroyal of hermitages and his taking away of others' wives."

Sloka 24

Sloka 26

Sloka 28

Sloka 29

Sloka 30

Sloka 31

Sloka 35

Sloka 36

śailaśṛṅgāṇi vṛkṣāṃśca śilāścāpyupasamhara |
tiṣṭhantu sāyudhāḥ sarve vānarāḥ śailapāṇayaḥ || 6-61-36

36. upasamhara = draw together; shaila shṛiṇ^gāṇi = the mountain-tops; vṛikṣāmshcha = trees; shilāshchāpi = and even the rocks; sarve vānarāḥ = (Let) all the monkeys; sāyudhāḥ = with their weapons; shaila pāṇayaḥ = and rocks in hand; tiṣṭhantu = stand ready.

"Draw together the mountain-tops, trees and even the rocks. Let all the monkeys with their weapons and rocks in hand, stand ready."

Sloka 37

rāghaveṇa samādiṣṭo nīlo haricamūpatiḥ |
śaśāsa vānarānīkam yathāvatkapikuñjaraḥ || 6-61-37

37. samādiṣṭah = as directed; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; nīlaḥ = ṇeela; harichamūpatiḥ = the commander-in-cheif; kapikuṇ^jaraḥ = and the foremost among the monkeys; shashāsa = ordered; vānarānīkam = the monkey-troops; yathāvat = suitably.

As directed by Raghavana, Neela the commander-in-chief and the foremost of the monkeys, ordered the monkey-troops suitably.

Sloka 38

tatto gavākṣaḥ śarabho hanūmānaṅgadastathā |
śailaśṛṅgāṇi śailābhā gṛhītvā dvāramabhyayuḥ || 6-61-38

38. tataḥ = then; gavākṣaḥ = Gavaksha; sharabhaḥ = ṣharabha; hanūmān = ḥanuma; tathā = and; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; shailābhāḥ = looking like mountains; abhyayuḥ = reached; dvāram = the gate; gṛihītvā = taking; shailashṛiṇgāṇi = the mountain-tops.

Then, Gavaksha, Sharabha, Hanuma and Angada looking like mountains, reached the gate, taking the mountian-tops.

Sloka 39

rāmavākyamupaśrutya harayo jītakāśinaḥ |
pādapairardayanvīrā vānarāḥ paravāhinīm || 6-61-39

39. upashrutya = on hearing; rāmavākyam = Rama's words; jitāshinnaḥ = the fearless; harayaḥ = monkeys; (started their operation); vīrāḥ = the heroic; vānarāḥ = monkeys; ardayan = tormented; paravāhinīm = the enemy-troops; pādapaiḥ = with trees.

On hearing Rama's words, the fearless monkeys started their operation. The heroic monkeys began to torment the enemy-troops with uplifted trees.

Sloka 40

tato harīṇām tadanīkamugram |
rarāja śailodyatavṛkṣahastam |
gireḥ samīpānugatam yathaiva |
mahanmahāmbhodharajālamugram || 6-61-40

40. tataḥ = then; tat = that; ugram = terrrific; harīṇām = (army) of monkeys; shailodayata vṛikṣahastam = with uplifted rocks and trees in their hands; rarāja = shne; mahat mahāmbhodharajālamyathaiva = like a big collection of giagantic clouds; samīpānugate = hanging close; gireḥ = to a mountain.

That terrific army of monkeys, with uplifted rocks and tress in their hands, shone like a big collection of gigantic clouds, hanging close to a mountain.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekaṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 61st chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
