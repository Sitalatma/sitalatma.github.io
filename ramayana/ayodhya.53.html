Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 53
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama tries to send Lakshmana back to Ayodhya, apprehending trouble for Kousalya and others in the hands of Kaikeyi. Lakshmana however refuses to return to Ayodhya, pleasing his inability to survive in the absence of Rama. Rama thereafter agrees again for Lakshmana's stay in the forest with him.

Sloka 1

sa tam vṛkṣam samāsādya saṃdhyām anvāsya paścimām |
rāmaḥ ramayatām śreṣṭhaiti ha uvāca lakṣmaṇam || 2-53-1

1. rāmaḥ = Rama; shreshhṭaḥ = the foremost; ramayatām = of these affording happiness to others; samasādya = reached; tam vṛikshham = the aforesaid tree; anvāsya = worshipped; pashchimām = the western; sandhyām = twilight; uvācha ha = and spoke; iti = thus; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana:

Rama, the foremost of those affording happiness to others, reached the aforesaid tree, worshipped the western twilight and spoke to Lakshmana as follows:

Sloka 2

adya iyam prathamā rātrir yātā jana padāt bahiḥ |
yā sumantreṇa rahitā tām na utkaṇṭhitum arhasi || 2-53-2

2. iyam = "ṭhis; rātriḥ = night; yā = which; yātrā = has got past (us); apya = today; bahiḥ = outside; janapadāt = the inhabited territory; prathamā = is the first one; rahitā = without; sumantreṇa = ṣumantra. nārahasi = You ought not; utkaṇ^ṭhitum = repent; tām = over it."

"This might which has got past us today outside the inhabited territory is the first night which has passed without Sumantra. You ought not repent over it."

Sloka 3

jāgartavyam atandribhyām adya prabhṛti rātriṣu |
yoga kṣemaḥ hi sītāyā vartate lakṣmaṇa āvayoh || 2-53-3

3. lakshhmaṇaḥ = "Oh; Lakshmana! atandribhyām = Remaining free from lassitude; jāgartavyam = (we must both) keep awake; rātrishhu = during nights; adya prabhṛiti = from today. yoga kshhemou = ṭhe gain and security; sītāyāḥ = of ṣita; vartate hi = indeed depend; āvayoḥ = on us two."

"Remaining free from lassitude, we must both keep awake during nights, from today Lakshmana! The safety and welfare of Sita indeed depend on us two."

Sloka 4

rātrim kathaṃcit eva imām saumitre vartayāmahe |
upāvartāmahe bhūmāv āstīrya svayam ārjitaiḥ || 2-53-4

4. vartayāmahe = "Let us pass; imām rātrim = this night; kathamchideva = any how; soumitre = Lakshmana! upāvartāmahe = Let us lie down; bhūmou = on the ground;āstīrya = covering it; ārjitaiḥ = (by things ) procured; svayam = by our own(hands)."

"Let us pass this night anyhow, Lakshmana! Let us lie down on the ground, covering it by straw, leaves and so on, procured by our own hands."

Sloka 5

sa tu samviśya medinyām mahā arha śayana ucitaḥ |
imāḥ saumitraye rāmaḥ vyājahāra kathāḥ śubhāḥ || 2-53-5

5. samvishya = ṣtreching himself; madinyām = on the ground; saḥ = Rama; mahārha shayanochitaḥ = who was accustomed to a luxurious coach; vyājahāra = uttered; imāḥ = these; shubhāḥ = suitable; kathāḥ = words; soumitraye = to Lakshmana:

Streching himself on the bare ground, Rama, who was accustomed to a luxurious coach, uttered the following suitable words to Lakshmana:

Sloka 6

dhruvam adya mahā rājo duhkham svapiti lakṣmaṇa |
kṛta kāmā tu kaikeyī tuṣṭā bhavitum arhati || 2-53-6

6. dhruvam = "assuredly; mahārājaḥ = the emperor; svapiti = is sleeping; duḥkham = badly; adya = to-night; kaikeyītu = while Kaikeyi; kṛtakāmā = having realized her ambitions; arhati = ought; bhavitum = to feel; tushhṭā = satisfied."

"Assuredly the emperor is sleeping badly tonight, while Kaikeyi, having realized her ambitions, ought to feel satisfied."

Sloka 7

sā hi devī mahā rājam kaikeyī rājya kāraṇāt |
api na cyāvayet prāṇān dṛṣṭvā bharatam āgatam || 2-53-7

7. dṛishhṭvā = "seeing; bharatam = Bharata; āgatam = returned; sā = that; devī = queen; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; na chyāvayedapi = (I am afraid) may not expel; prāṇān = the lives; mahārājam = of the emperor; rājya kāraṇāt = hi indeed for the sake of kingdom."

"Seeing Bharata returned, queen Kaikeyi, I am afraid, may not expel the lives of the emperor for the sake of kingdom."

Sloka 8

anāthaḥ caiva vṛddhaḥ ca mayā caiva vinākṛtaḥ |
kim kariṣyati kāma ātmā kaikeyyā vaśam āgataḥ || 2-53-8

8. vṛiddhascha = "aged; anāthashcha = (and therefore) helpless; mayārinācha = deprived of my presence; kim karishhyati = what will he do; kṛitaḥ = dominated as he is; kāmātmā = by his passion (for Kaikeyi); āgataḥ = and who has fallen; kaikeyīvasham = into clutches of Kaikeyi."

"Aged and (therefore) helpless, deprived of my presence, what will he do, dominated as he is by his passion for Kaikeyi and who has fallen into the clutches of Kaikeyi."

Sloka 9

idam vyasanam ālokya rājñaḥ ca mati vibhramam |
kāmaeva ardha dharmābhyām garīyān iti me matiḥ || 2-53-9

9. ālokya = "reflecting on; idam = this; vyasanam = misfortune; rāG^yaḥ = of the king; mativibhramamcha = and his mental derangement; me = my; matiḥ = belief; iti = (is) that; kāma eva = passion alone; garīyān = is stronger; artha dharmābhyām = than earthly gain and religious merit."

"Reflecting on this misfortune of the king and his mental derangement, I deem that passion alone is greater than early gain and religious merit."

Sloka 10

ko hi avidvān api pumān pramadāyāḥ kṛte tyajet |
canda anuvartinam putram tātaḥ mām iva lakṣmaṇa || 2-53-10

10. kaḥ pumān = "what man; avidyānapi = however deluded; tataḥ = (what) father; pramadāyāḥ kṛite = on account of a woman; chandāmvartinam = at his own will and pleasure; tyajet = abandon; putram = a son; māmiva = like myself?"

"what man however deluded, what father on account of a woman, at his own will and pleasure, abandon a son like myself?

Sloka 11

sukhī bata sabhāryaḥ ca bharataḥ kekayī sutaḥ |
muditān kosalān eko yo bhokṣyati adhirājavat || 2-53-11

11. bataḥ = "Alas; kaikeyīsutaḥ = Kaikeyi's son; bharataḥ = Bharata; ekaḥ = alone; sukhī = is happy; sa bhāryaḥ = along with his wife; yaḥ = who; adhirājavat = like an overlord; bhokshhyati = will enjoy; muditān = the prosperous; kosalān = kingdom of Kosala."

"Alas, Kaikeyi's son Bharata (alone) is happy along with his wife. Like an overlord, he is going to enjoy the prosperous kingdom of Kosala."

Sloka 12

sa hi sarvasya rājyasya mukham ekam bhaviṣyati |
tāte ca vayasā atīte mayi ca araṇyam āśrite || 2-53-12

12. tāte = "father; vayasā atīte = is superannuated. ṃayicha = I; āsthite = am staying; areṇyam = in the forest. saḥ = Bharata; bhavishhyanti = will become; ekam = the prime; mukham = head; sarvasya = for the entire; rājyasya = kingdom."

"Father is superannuated. I am staying in the forest. Bharata will become the prime head for the entire kingdom."

Sloka 13

artha dharmau parityajya yaḥ kāmam anuvartate |
evam āpadyate kṣipram rājā daśaratho yathā || 2-53-13

13. yaḥ = "he who; anuvartate = persues; kāmam = sensuous pleasures; parityajya = neglecting; artha dharmou = his real interests and discipline; kshhipram = soon; āpadyate = comes to distress; evam = in the same way; rāja dasharatho yathā = as king Dasaratha has."

"He who pursues sensuous pleasures neglecting his real interests and discipline soon comes to distress; in the same way as king Dasaratha has."

Sloka 14

manye daśaratha antāya mama pravrājanāya ca |
kaikeyī saumya samprāptā rājyāya bharatasya ca || 2-53-14

14. soumya = "Oh;good brother; manye = I think; kaikeyī- = that Kaikeyi; samprāptā = came (into our house); dasharathāntāya = to bring about an end to Dasaratha; mama pravrājanāyacha = to send me into exile; rājyāyacha = (and to secure) kingship; bharatasya = for Bharata."

"It seems that Kaikeyi came into our house, oh good brother, to bring about an end to Dasaratha, to send me into exile and to secure kingship for Bharata."

Sloka 15

api idānīm na kaikeyī saubhāgya mada mohitā |
kausalyām ca sumitrām ca samprabādheta mat kṛte || 2-53-15

15. soubhāgya mada mohitā = "blinded by pride of good fortune; kaikeyī = will Kaikeyi; api idānīm = even now; na samprabādheta = not persecute; kausalyāmcha = and ṣumitra; matkṛite = because of their relationship with me."

"Blinded by pride of good fortune, Kaikeyi may even now persecute Kausalya and Sumitra because of their relationship with me."

Sloka 16

mā sma mat kāraṇāt devī sumitrā duhkham āvaset |
ayodhyām itaeva tvam kāle praviśa lakṣmaṇa || 2-53-16

16. devī = "queen; sumitrā = ṣumitra; mā āvaset sma = will she not dwell; duḥkham = with hardship; matkāraṇāt = because of her affinity to me? tvam = You; pravisha = enter; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; ita eva = from this very place; kālye = next morning; lakshhmaṇa = oh; Lakshmana!"

"Queen Sumitra is likely to suffer hardship because of her affinity to us. From this very place, you proceed to Ayodhya next morning, oh, Lakshmana!"

Sloka 17

aham eko gamiṣyāmi sītayā saha daṇḍakān |
anāthāyā hi nāthaḥ tvam kausalyāyā bhaviṣyasi || 2-53-17

17. aham = "I; ekaḥ = alone; gamishhyāmi = will go; sītayā saha = along with ṣita; daṇdakān = to Dandaka forest. tvam = You; bhavishhyasi = become; nāthaḥ = protector; kausalyā = to Kausalya; anāthāyāḥ = the supportless."

"I shall proceed to Dandaka forest alone with Sita, while you will be the protector for Kausalya, who has no defender."

Sloka 18

kṣudra karmā hi kaikeyī dveṣāt anyāyyam ācaret |
paridadyā hi dharmajñe bharate mama mātaram || 2-53-18

18. kaikeyī = "Kaikeyi; kshhudra karmā = of base deeds; ācharet = may resort to; anyāyyam = unjustified means; dveshhyam = to be disliked. paridadyaḥhi = Give(for protection); mama = my; mātaram = mother; bharate = to Bharata; dharmaG^ye = oh; virtuous prince!"

"Kaikeyi of base deeds may resort to unjustified means to be disliked. Give (for protection) my mother to Bharata, oh virtuous Prince!"

Sloka 19

nūnam jāti antare kasmiṃs striyaḥ putraiḥ viyojitāḥ |
jananyā mama saumitre tat api etat upasthitam || 2-53-19

19. kasmin jātyantare = "In some other birth; soumitre = Oh; Lakshmana; striyaḥ = women; viyojitāḥ = must have been deprived; putraiḥ = of their sons; mama jananyā = by my mother. ṭasmāt = For that reason; etat = this upasthitam = has arisen; nūnam = certainly."

"In some other (past) birth, women must have been deprived of their sons by my mother (Kausalya), oh, Lakshmana! For that reason this has arisen certainly."

Sloka 20

mayā hi cira puṣṭena duhkha samvardhitena ca |
viprāyujyata kausalyā phala kāle dhig astu mām || 2-53-20

20. phala kale = "At a time when benefit is to be obtained; mayā = by me; kausalyā = Kausalya; viprāyujyata = has been deprived of my company by me; chirapushhṭena = who was nurtured up with great pains; astu = Let it be; dhik = woe; mām = to me."

"At a time when Kausalya should have obtained benefits for her labors repaid by me, she has been deprived of my company by me, who was nurtured by her for a long time and brought up with great pains. Woe to me."

Sloka 21

mā sma sīmantinī kācij janayet putram īdṛśam |
saumitre yo aham ambāyā dadmi śokam anantakam || 2-53-21

21. māsma kāchit sīmantinī = "Let no woman ever; janayet = give birth; īdṛisham = to such; putram = a son; yaḥ = as; aham = myself; dadmī = who has given; ananthakam = endless; shokam = sorrow; ambāyāḥ = to my mother; soumitre = oh; Lakshmana!"

"Let no woman ever give birth to such a son as myself, who have caused perpetual grief to my mother, oh, Lakshmana!"

Sloka 22

manye prīti viśiṣṭā sā mattaḥ lakṣmaṇa sārikā |
yasyāḥ tat śrūyate vākyam śuka pādam arer daśa || 2-53-22

22. manye = "I think; sā shārikā = that myna (which is kept as a pet by mother Kausalya); prītivishishhṭā = is more affectionate; mattaḥ = than I; yasyāḥ = (since)her; shrūyate = are heard; tat vākyam = the words; dasha = "Bite ; shuka = oh; parrot; pādam = the foot; areḥ = of the enemy'; lakshhmaṇa = oh; Lakshmana!"

"Oh, Lakshmana! I think that myna (which is kept as a pet by mother Kausalya) is more affectionate than I, since her are heard the words, "Bite, oh parrot, the foot of the enemy."

Sloka 23

śocantyāḥ ca alpa bhāgyāyā na kiṃcit upakurvatā |
purtreṇa kim aputrāyā mayā kāryam arim dama || 2-53-23

23. kim = "what; kāryam = can be done; mayā = by me; putreṇa = her son; nopakurvatā = who cannot go to her aid; kamchit = even a little(to her); shochantyāḥ = who is weeping; alpa bhāgyāyāḥ = who is unfortunate; aputrāyāḥ = and who has no son; arindama = oh; conqueror of foes!"

"What can be done by me, her son, who cannot go to her aid even a little to her, my mother, who is weeping, who is unfortunate and who has no son, oh conqueror of foes?"

Sloka 24

alpa bhāgyā hi me mātā kausalyā rahitā mayā |
śete parama duhkha ārtā patitā śoka sāgare || 2-53-24

24. kausalyā = "Kausalya; me mātā = my mother; alpabhāgyā hi = of poor luck indeed; rahitā = bereft; mayā = of me; parama duhkhārtāḥ = is stricken with great melancholy; shate = and lies; patitā = fallen; shoka sāgare = in a sea of grief."

"Kausalya my mother of poor luck indeed, bereft of me, is stricken with great melancholy and lies plunged in a sea of grief"

Sloka 25

eko hi aham ayodhyām ca pṛthivīm ca api lakṣmaṇa |
tareyam iṣubhiḥ kruddho nanu vīryam akāraṇam || 2-53-25

25. kruddhaḥ = "Enraged; aham = I; tareyam = can subdue; ishhubhiḥ = with my arrows; ekaḥ = single-handed; ayodhyāmcha = not only Ayodhya; pṛithivīmchāpi = but also earth; lakshhmaṇa = oh Lakshmana! vīryam = Valor; akāraṇam nam = is not the cause; not at all"

"Enraged, I can subdue with my arrows single handed not only Ayodhya but also the earth. But it is not a question of valor here."

Sloka 26

adharma bhaya bhītaḥ ca para lokasya ca anagha |
tena lakṣmaṇa na adya aham ātmānam abhiṣecaye || 2-53-26

26. anagha = "Oh; the sinless Lakshmana! aham = I; adharma bhaya bhītashcha = am terribly concerned of doing wrong; paralokasyacha = and(of ruining my prospects) in the other world. tena = For that reason; ātmānam = I do not allow myself; nābhishhechaye = to be crowned"

"Oh, the sinless Lakshmana! I am terribly concerned of doing wrong and of ruining my prospects in the other world. Hence, I do not allow myself to be crowned."

Sloka 27

etat anyac ca karuṇam vilapya vijane bahu |
aśru pūrṇa mukho rāmaḥ niśi tūṣṇīm upāviśat || 2-53-27

27. rāmaḥ = Rama; nishi = during the night; vijane = in the lonely forest; vilapya = wailed; karuṇam = piteously; etat = thus; anyashcha = and in other ways; upāvishat = sat; tushhṇ^īm = quite; ashrupūrṇa mukhaḥ = his face full of tears.

Rama during the night in that lonely forest, wailed piteously thus and in so many other ways and sat quite, his face full of tears.

Sloka 28

vilapya uparatam rāmam gata arciṣam iva analam |
samudram iva nirvegam āśvāsayata lakṣmaṇaḥ || 2-53-28

28. kajṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; āshvāsayata = consoled; rāmam = Rama (as follows); uparatam = when the latter had ceased lamenting; analam iva = like a fire; gatārchiṣam = whose flames had gone out; samudramiva = and as a sea; nirvegam = without a violent motion.

Lakshmana consoled Rama as follows when the later had ceased lamenting like a fire whose flames had gone out and as a sea without a violent motion.

Sloka 29

dhruvam adya purī rāmāyodhyā yudhinām vara |
niṣprabhā tvayi niṣkrānte gata candrā iva śarvarī || 2-53-29

29. ayodhyā purī = "the city of Ayodhya; adya = now; tvayi = you; nishhkrānte = have come away from it; dhruvam = has certainly; nishhprabhā = been divested of its splendor; sharvarīva = and resembles a night; gatachandrā = without the moon; rājan = Oh; king; vara = the jewel; āyudhinām = among armed warriors!"

"The city of Ayodhya, now that you have come away from it, has certainly been divested of its splendor and resembles a night without the moon, Oh, Rama the jewel among armed warriors!"

Sloka 30

na etat aupayikam rāma yad idam paritapyase |
viṣādayasi sītām ca mām caiva puruṣa ṛṣabha || 2-53-30

30. etat = "this; na = is not; oupayikam = proper; rāma = oh Rama; yat paritapyate = that you should grieve; idam = in the manner. vishhādayasi = You cause distress; sītamcha = to ṣita; māmchaiva = and me too; purushharshhabha = oh jewel among men!"

"It is not proper, oh Rama, that you should grieve in this way. You cause distress to Sita and me too, oh jewel among men!"

Sloka 31

na ca sītā tvayā hīnā na ca aham api rāghava |
muhūrtam api jīvāvo jalān matsyāv iva uddhṛtau || 2-53-31

31. rāghava = "Oh; Rama! hīnā = Bereft; tvayā = of you; sītā = ṣita; na = will not survive. ahamapi = I also; na jīvāvaḥ = We shall not survive; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment; matsyāviva = like fishes; uddhṛiton = pulled out of water."

"Oh, Rama! Bereft of you , neither Sita nor I will not survive even for a moment, like fish pulled out of water."

Sloka 32

na hi tātam na śatrughnam na sumitrām param tapa |
draṣṭum icceyam adya aham svargam vā api tvayā vinā || 2-53-32

32. paramtapa = "Oh; Rama causing pain to the foes! ṭvayā vinā = without you; aham = I; na hi ichchheyam = do not wish; drashhṭum = to see either; tātam = our father; na = nor; shatrughnam = ṣatrughna; sumitrām = ṣumitra; svargamvāpi = nor even heaven."

"Oh, Rama causing pain to the foes! Without you, I do not wish to see either our father or Satrughna or Sumitra or even the heaven."

Sloka 33

tatastatra sukhāsīne nātidūre nirīkṣya tām |
nyagrodhe sukṛtām śayyām bhejāte dharmavatsalau || 2-53-33

33. tataḥ = then; dharmavatsalou = Rama and Lakshmana; the lovers of piety; sukhasīnou = sitting comfortably; tatra = there; nirīkshhya = (and) seeing; tām shayyām = that bed; sukṛitām = well arranged; nyagrodha = under a banyan tree; bhejate = sought (for the bed)."

Then Rama and Lakshmana the lovers of piety, sitting comfortably there and on seeing a bed well- prepared under a banyan tree, sought for the bed.

Sloka 34

sa lakṣmaṇasya uttama puṣkalam vaco |
niśamya ca evam vana vāsam ādarāt |
samāḥ samastā vidadhe param tapaḥ |
prapadya dharmam sucirāya rāghavaḥ || 2-53-34

34. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; paramtapaḥ = the terminator of foes; ādarāt = attentively; nishamya = hearing; lakshhmaṇasya = Lakshmana's; vachaḥ = words; uttama pushhkalam = which were excellent and holistic; prapadya = and adopting; suchirāya = for a fairly long period; dharmam = the course of conduct prescribed for hermits; vidadhe = resolved; samastāḥ = (to stand) all; samāḥ = the years; vanavāsam = in exile(with Lakshmana).

Attentively hearing Lakshmana's words which were excellent and wholistic and adopting for a fairly long period the course of conduct prescribed for hermits, Rama resolved to spend all the fourteen years in exile with Lakshmana.

Sloka 35

tatastu tasmin vijane vane tadā |
mahābalau rāghavavaṃśavardhanau |
na tau bhayam sambhramamabhyupeyatu |
ryathaiva simhau girisānugocarau || 2-53-35

35. tataḥ tadā = thence forward; tasmin vane = in that forest; vijane = which was lonely; tou = those; mahābalou rāghava vamsha vardhanon = (two) powerful offspring of the Raghu race; na abhyupeyatuḥ = did not admit; bhayam = fear; sambhramam = or agitation; simhou yathaiva = like unto two lions; girisānu gocharon = dwelling on the slopes of a mountain.

Thence forward, those two powerful offspring of the Raghu race (Rama and Lakshmana) never admitted fear or agitation (while dwelling) in that vast and lonely forest any more than a couple of lions on the slopes of a mountain.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe tripañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes canto fifty-three of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
