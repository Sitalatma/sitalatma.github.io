Introduction

Agitated to learn that Rama had arrived to Lanka and is stationed at Suvela mountain along with his army, Ravana confers with his ministers and after dismissing them, approaches Seetha with Vidyujjuha, knower of conjuring tricks. Informing Seetha that her husband along with his army had been killed in battle, he produces before her an illusive Rama's head along with bow and arrows created with Vidyujjuha's conjuring trick. Thus, Ravana deceives Seetha about the death of Rama.

Sloka 1

1. tataḥ = then; nṛipateḥ = the king's; charāḥ = spies; pratyavedan = informed (Ravana); tam rāghavam = that Rama; akṣobhya balam = with his imperturbable army; niviṣṭam = was stationed; suvele shaile = at ṃount ṣuvela; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka.

ṭhe spies of king Ravana informed him that Rama with his imperturbable army was encamped at ṃount Suvela in Lanka.

ḥearing through his spies that Rama with his mighty army had arrived to Lanka, Ravana was a little agitated and spoke to his counsellors as follows:

yena tadraghanaśiraḥ saṅgramātsvayamāhṛtam || 6-31-39

39. samānaya = bring; vidyujjihvam = Vidyujjihva; rākshhasam = the demons; krūrakarmāṇam = of the cruel deed; yena = by whom; tat = that; rāghava shiraḥ = Raghava's head; āhṛitam = was brought; svayam = by himself; saṇ^grāmāt = from the battle-field.

"Bring Vidyujjihva, the demon of the cruel deed, by whom himself Raghava's head was brought back from the battle-field."

Sloka 40

vidyujjihva stadā gṛhya śirastatsaśarāsanam |
praṇāmam śirasā kṛtvā rāvaṇasyāgrataḥ sthitaḥ || 6-31-40

40. tadā = then; gṛiya; holding; tat shiraḥ = that head; sa sharāsanam = along with a bow; vidyujjihvaḥ = Vidyujjihva; kṛitva = making a respectful salute; shirasā = with his head bent low; sthitaḥ = stood; agrataḥ = in front; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana.

ḥolding that head along with a bow, Vidyujjihva making a respectful salute with his head bent low, stood in front of Ravana.

Sloka 41

tamabravīttato rājā rāvaṇo rākṣasam sthitam |
vidyujjihvam mahājihvam samīpaparivartinam || 6-31-41

41. tataḥ = thereafter; rājā = the king; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; abravīt = spoke; tam vidyujjihvam = to that Vidyujjihva; rākshhasam = the demon; mahājihvam = who had a huge tongue; sthitam = and who was standing; samīpa parivartinam = and moving closely.

ṭhereafter, the king Ravana spoke the following words to Vidyujjihva, the demon who had a large tongue and who was standing and moving closely.

Sloka 42

agrataḥ kuru sītāyāḥ śrīghraṃ dāśaratheḥ śiraḥ |
avasthāṃ paśicamāṃ bhartuḥ kṛpaṇā sādhu paśyatu || 6-31-42

42. kuru = keep; dāsharatheḥ = Rama's; shiraḥ = head; shīghram = immediately; agrataḥ = in front; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; kṛipaṇā = (let) the miserable ṣeetha; pashyatu = see; sādhu = well; pashchimām = the find; avasthām = condition; bhartaḥ = of her husband.

"Keep Rama's head immediately in front of ṣeetha. Let the miserable ṣeetha see well the final condition of her husband.

Sloka 43

evamuktaṃ tu tadrakṣaḥ śirastatpriyadarśanam |
upanikṣipya sītāyāḥ kṣipramantaradhīyata || 6-31-43

43. tat rakshhaḥ = that demon; evam = thus; uktam = spoken; upamikshhipya = desposited in the vicinity; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; tat = that; shiraḥ = head; priyadarshanam = whih was pleasant to look at; kshhipram = and soon; antaradhīyata = disappeared from the site.

ḥearing Ravana's words, that demon deposited the cherished head in the vicinity of Seetha and soon disappeared from the site.

Sloka 44

rāvaṇaścāpi cikṣepa bhāsvaram kārmukam mahat |
triṣu lokeṣu vikhyātam rāmasyaitaditi bruvan || 6-31-44

44. etat = this is; rāmasya = Rama's; vikhyātam = (bow) famous; tishhu lokeshhu = in the three worlds; rāvaṇashhāi = Ravana; bruvan = speaking; iti = thus; hikshhepa = threw ( in front of ṣeetha); bhāsvaram = the shining; mahat = great; kārmukam = bow.

"ḥere is Rama's bow famous in the three worlds!" - saying thus, Ravana threw a great shining bow in front of Seetha.

Sloka 45

idam tattava rāmasya kārmukaṃ jyāsamāvR6itam |
iha prahastevānītam tam hatvā niśi mānuṣam || 6-31-45

45. idam = this is; tat = that; kārmukam = bow; jyā samāvṛitam = with its cord stretched; tava rāmasya = belonging to Rama; ānītam = that was brought back; prahastena = by Prahasta; iha = here; hatvā = after killing; tam = that; mānushham = man; nishi = in the night.

"ṭhis is the bow with its cord stretched, belonging to Rama, that was brought back by Prahasta here, after killing that hero in the night."

Sloka 46

sa vidyujihvena sahaiva tacchiro |
dhamaśca bhūmau vinikīrya rāvaṇaḥ |
videharājasya sutām yaśasvinīṃ |
tato.abravīttām bhava me vaśāmugā || 6-31-46

46. saḥ āvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; vidyujihvena sahaiva = along with Vidyujjihva; vinikīrya = threw; tat = that; shiraḥ = head; dhanushha = and the bow; bhūmau = on the ground; tām = towards ṣeetha; yashasvinīm = the illustrious; sutām = daughter; videharājasya = of Janaka; abravīt = and said; bhava = you become; rashāmgā = but missive; me = to me.

ṭhat Rama's head and bow on the ground in front of ṣeetha the illustrious daughter of Janaka, saying: "You now become submissive to my desire."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekatriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 31st chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
