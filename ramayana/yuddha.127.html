Introduction

Bharata, after hearing the good news from Hanuma about the arrival of Rama, calls Shatrughna and commands him to make appropriate arrangements for the reception of Rama in the City of Ayodhya. Bharata departs with all others, for Nandigrama to receive Rama. The aerial car lands at Nandigrama. Bharata welcomes Rama and others, by embracing them and by greeting them joyously. Rama too offers his salutations to all his mothers, who come to receive him. Then, Bharata brings Rama's wooden sandals and places them below the feet of Rama. Rama commands Pushpaka, the aerial car to return to Kubera, the lord of riches to whom it originally belonged.

Sloka 37

tato rāmābhyanujñātaṃ tadvimānamanuttamam || 6-127-37
haṃsayuktaṃ mahāvegam nipapāta mahītale |

37. rāmābhyanujJṇātam = as authorized by Rama; tat anuttamam vimānam = that excellent aerial car; mahāvegam = having a great speed; hamsayuktam = and endowed with the images of swans; nipapāta = landed; mahītale = on the ground.

As authorized by Rama, that excellent aerial car, having a great speed and endowed with the images of swans, landed on the ground.

Sloka 38

āropito vimānaṃ tadbharataḥ satyavikramaḥ || 6-127-38
rāmamāsādya muditaḥ punarevābhyavādayat |

38. muditaḥ = feeling glad; āropitaḥ = when lifted on; tat vimānam = to that aerial ear; bharataḥ = Bharata; satyavikramaḥ = of true valour; āsādya = approaching; rāmam = Rama; abhyavādayat = greeted him; punareva = yet again.

Feeling glad, when lifted on that aerial car and approaching Rama, Bharata of true valour, greeted him yet again.

Sloka 39

taṃ samutthāpya kākutsthaścirasyākṣipathaṃ gatam || 6-127-39
aṅke bharatamāropya muditaḥ pariṣaṣvaje |

39. samutthāpya tat = fully rising from his seat; āropya tam bharatam = and placing that Bharata; akṣhipatham gatam chirāya = who was seen after a long time; aṇ^kam = on his lap; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; muditaḥ = delightfully; pariṣhvaje = embraced him.

Fully rising from his seat and placing that Bharata, who was seen after a long time, on his lap, Rama delightfully embraced him.

Sloka 40

tato lakṣmaṇamāsādya vaidehīṃ ca parantapaḥ || 6-127-40
abhyavādayata prīto bharato nāma cābravīt |

40. tataḥ = thereafter; prītaḥ = the delighted; bharataḥ = Bharata; paramtapaḥ = the destroyer of adversaries; āsādya = approaching; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; vaidehīm cha = and ṣeetha; atha = then; abhyavādayat = saluted in reverence; abravīt = (and also) announced; nāma = his own name.

Thereafter, the jubilant Bharata, the destroyer of adversaries, approaching Lakshmana and Seetha, then saluted them in reverence and also announced his name.

Sloka 41

sugrīvaṃ kaikayī putro jāmbavantaṃ tathāṅgadam || 6-127-41
maindaṃ ca dvividaṃ nīlamṛṣabhaṃ caiva sasvaje |

41. atha = then; kaikeyī putraḥ = Bharata; pariṣhasvaje = embraced; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; jāmbavantam = Jambavan; aṇ^gadam = Angada; maindam = ṃainda; dvividam chaiva = Divivida; nīlam = ṇeela; ṛiṣhabham = and Rishabha.

Then, Bharata embraced Sugreeva, Jambavan, Angada, ṃainda, Dvivida, Neela and Rishabha.

Sloka 42

suṣeṇaṃ ca nalaṃ caiva gavākṣam gandhamādanam || 6-127-42
śarabhaṃ panasaṃ caiva paritah pariṣsvaje |

42. pariṣhasvaje = he also embraced; suṣheṇamcha = ṣushena; nalamchaiva = ṇala; gavākṣham = GAvaksha; gandhamādanam = Gandhamadana; sharabham = ṣharabha; panasamchaiva = Panasa; paritaḥ = and the surrounding monkeys.

He also embraced Sushena, Nala, Gavaksha, Gandhamadana, Sharabha, Panasa and the surrounding monkeys.

Sloka 43

te kṛtvā mānuṣaṃ rūpaṃ vānarāḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ || 6-127-43
kuśalaṃ paryapṛṣanta prahṛṣṭā bharataṃ tadā |

43. tadā = then; te vānarāḥ = those monkeys; kāma rūpiṇaḥ = who can change their format will; kṛitvā mānuṣham rūpam = assumed the form of humans; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = and rejoicingly; paryapṛichchhan = asked; kushalam = about the welfare; bharatam = of Bharata.

Then, those monkeys, who can change their form at will, assumed the form of humans and rejoicingly asked about the welfare of Bharata.

Sloka 44

athābravīdrājaputraḥ sugrīvaṃ vānararṣabham || 6-127-44
pariṣvajya mahātejā bharato dharmiṇāṃ varaḥ |

44. atha = thereupon; mahātejāḥ = the immensely brilliant; bharataḥ = Bharata; rājaputraḥ = the son of Dasaratha; dharmīṇām varaḥ = and the foremost among the virtuous; pariṣhvajya = after embracing; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; vānarṣhabham = the excellent monkey; abravīt = spoke to him (as follows):

Thereupon, the immensely brilliant Bharata, the son of Dasaratha and the foremost among the virtuous, after embracing Sugreeva the excellent monkey, spoke to him (as follows):

Sloka 45

tvamasmākaṃ caturṇāṃ vaibhrātā sugrīva pañcamaḥ || 6-127-45
sauhārdājjāyate mitramapakāro.arilakṣaṇam |

45. sugrīva = O ṣugreeva!; tvam = you; paṇ^chamaḥ = are a fifth; bhrātā = brother; asmākam chaturṇām = for all the four of us; mitram = a friend; jāyate = is born; sauhārdāt = of affection; apakāraḥ = malifience; arilakṣhaṇam = is the attribute of an adversary.

"You are a fifth brother, for all the four of us, O Sugreeva! A friend is born of affection, while malifience is the attribute of an adversary."

Sloka 46

vibhīṣaṇaṃ ca bharataḥ sāntvayanvākyamabravīt || 6-127-46
diṣṭyā tvayā sahāyena kṛtaṃ karma suduṣkaram |

46. atha = thereafter; bharataḥ = bharata; abravīt = spoke; sāntvavākyam = (the following) kind words; vibhīṣhaṇamcha = to Vibhishana; diṣhṭyā = thank heaven!; suduṣhkarma = a very difficult; karma = task; kṛitam = was accomplished; tvayā = by you; shāyena = as a companion (of Rama).

Thereafter, Bharata spoke the following kind words to Vibhishan: "Thank heaven! A very difficult task was accomplished by you, as a companion of Rama."

Sloka 47

śatrughnaśca tadā rāmamabhivādya salakṣmaṇam || 6-127-47
sītāyāścaraṇau paścādvavande vinayānvitaḥ |

47. tadā = then; vīraḥ = the valiant; shatrughnashcha = ṣhatrughna; abhivādya = offered his salutation; rāmam = to Rama; sa lakṣhmaṇam = along with Lakshmana; abhyavādayat = and offered his reverential salutation; sītāyāḥ charaṇau = to ṣeetha's feet; vinayāt = by bowing in humility.

Then, the valiant Shatrughna offered his salutation to Rama, along with Lakshmana and offered his reverential salutation to Seetha's feet, by bowing in humility.

Sloka 48

rāmo mātaramāsādya viṣaṇṇaṃ śokakarśitām || 6-127-48
jagrāha praṇataḥ pādau mano mātuḥ prasādayan |

48. āsādya = by going nearer; mātaram = to his mother; vivarṇām = who became pale; shoka karshitām = and emaciated due to her grief; rāmaḥ = Rama; jagrāha = seized; pādau = her feet; praṇataḥ = having bowed; mātuḥ manaḥ = making his mother's heart; praharṣhayan = delighted.

By going to his mother, who became pale and emaciated due to her grief, Rama, by bowing, seized her feet in salutation, making his mother's heart delighted.

Sloka 49

abhivādya sumitrāṃ ca kaikeyīṃ ca yaśasvinīm || 6-127-49
sa mātṝśca tadā sarvāḥ purohitamupāgamat |

49. abhivādya = offering salutation; sumitrāmcha = to ṣumitra; yashasvinīm = the illustrious; kaikeyīmcha = Kaikeyi; sarvāḥ mātṛishcha = and all his mothers; saḥ = Rama; tataḥ = then; upāgamat = approached; purohitam = Vasishta; the priest (and offered his salutation).

Offering salutation to Sumatra, the illustrious Kaikeyi and all his mothers, Rama then went to Vasishta the priest and offered his salutation in reverence.

Sloka 50

svāgataṃ te mahābāho kausalyānandavardhana || 6-127-50
iti prāñjalayaḥ sarve nāgarā rāmamabruvan |

50. mahābāho = O the great armed!; kausalyānandavardhana = O the enhancer of delight to Kausalya!; svāgatam = welcome; te = to you!; abruvan iti = thus spoke; sarve = all; nāgarāḥ = the citizens; rāmam = to Rama; prāṇ^jalayaḥ = with their joined palms.

With joined palms, all the citizens of Ayodhya said to Rama: "Welcome to you, O the great-armed! O the enhancer of Kausala's delight!"

Sloka 51

tanyañjalisahasrāṇi pragṛhītāni nāgaraiḥ || 6-127-51
vyākośānīva padmāni dadarśa bharatāgrajaḥ |

51. bharatāgrajaḥ = Rama; dadarsha = saw; tāni = those; aṇ^jali sahasrāṇi = thousands of joined palms; pragṛihītāni = held; nāgaraiḥ = by the citizens; padmanīva = as lotus-flowers; vyākochāni = in bloom.

Rama saw those thousands of joined palms held by the citizens, appearing as lotus-flowers in bloom.

Sloka 52

pāduke te tu rāmasya gṛhītvā bharataḥ svayam || 6-127-52
caraṇābhyāṃ narendrasya yojayāmāsa dharmavit |

52. gṛihītvā = taking; te = those; pāduke = wooden sandals; rāmasya = of Rama; bharataḥ = Bharata; dharmavit = the knower of virtue; svayam = personally; yojayāmāsa charaṇābhyām = placed them below the feet; narendrasya = of Rama; the lord of men.

Taking those wooden sandals of Rama, Bharata, the knower of virtue, personally placed them below the feet of Rama, the lord of men.

Sloka 53

abravīcca tadā rāmaṃ bharataḥ sa kṛtāñjaliḥ || 6-127-53
etatte rakṣitaṃ rājanrājyaṃ niryātitaṃ mayā |

53. kṛitāṇ^jaliḥ = having offered his salutation with joined palms; saḥ bharataḥ = that Bharata; tadā = then; abravīchcha = spoke; rāmam = to Rama (as follows); etat = this; sakalam = entire; rājyam = sovereignty; te = of yours; nyāsam = (kept with me) as a deposit; niryātitam = is being returned (to you) mayā = by me.

Having offered his salutation with joined palms, Bharata spoke to Rama as follows: "This entire sovereignty of yours, kept with me as a deposit, is being returned to you, by me."

Sloka 54

adya janma kṛtārthaṃ me saṃvṛttaśca manorathaḥ || 6-127-54
yastvāṃ paśyāmi rājānamayodhyāṃ punarāgatam |

54. pashyāmi = I am seeing; tvām = you; rājānam = as a king; punaḥ āgatam = after having come back; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; yat = for which; me janma = my life; kṛitārtham = has accomplished its purpose; manorathaḥ cha = my wish too; samvṛittaḥ = is fulfilled; adya = today.

"ṃy life has accomplished its purpose today and my wish too stands fulfilled, in that I see you, its king, come back to Ayodhya."

Sloka 55

avekṣatāṃ bhavānkośaṃ koṣṭhāgāraṃ puraṃ balam || 6-127-55
bhavatastejasā sarvaṃ kṛtaṃ daśaguṇaṃ mayā |

55. bhavān = you; avekṣhatām = review; kosham = your treasury; koṣhṭhāgāram = granary; gṛiham = palace; balam = and army; bhavataḥ tejasā = by virtue of the power of your spirit; sarvam = everything; kṛitam dashaguṇam = has been enhanced tenfold; mayā = by me.

"You review your treasury, granary, palace of your spirit, everything has been enhanced tenfold by me."

Sloka 56

tathā bruvāṇaṃ bharataṃ dṛṣṭvā taṃ bhrātṛvatsalam || 6-127-56
mumucurvānarā bāṣpaṃ rākṣasaśca vibhīṣaṇaḥ |

56. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam bharatam = that Bharata; tathā = thus; bruvāṇam = speaking; bhrātṛivatsalam = with affection towards his brother; vibhīṣhaṇah cha = Vibhishana; rākṣhasaḥ = the demon; vānarāḥ = and the monkeys; mumuchuḥ = shed; bāṣhpam = their tears.

Seeing that Bharata, speaking thus with affection with his brother, Vibhishana the demon and the monkeys shed their tears.

Sloka 57

tataḥ praharṣādbharatamaṅkamāropya rāghavaḥ || 6-127-57
yayau tena vimānena sasainyo bharatāśramam |

57. āropya = placing; bharatam = Bharata; aṇ^kam = on his lap; rāghavaḥ = Rama; praharṣhāt = with delight; tataḥ = thereupon; yayau = went; tena vimānena = in that aerial car; bharatāshramam = to the hermitage of Bharata; sasainyaḥ = along with the army.

Placing Bharata on his lap with delight, Rama thereupon flew with his army (of monkeys and bears) in that aerial car to the hermitage of Bharata.

Sloka 58

bharatāśramamāsādya sasainyo rāghavastadā || 6-127-58
avatīrya vimānāgrādavatasthe mahītale |

58. āsādya = reaching; bharatāshramam = the hermitage of Bharata; sasainyaḥ = along with his army; rāghavaḥ = Rama; tadā = then; avatīrya = descended; vimanāgrāt = from the anterior of the aerial car; avastasthe = and stood for the time being; mahītale = on the ground.

Reaching the hermitage of Bharata along with his army Rama then descended from the anterior of the aerial car and stood, for the time-being, on the ground.

Sloka 59

abravīcca tadā rāmastadvimānamanuttamam || 6-127-59
vaha vaiśravaṇaṃ devamanujānāmi gamyatām |

59. tadā = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; abravīt = spoke; tat anuttamam vimānam = to that excellent aerial car (as follows); vaha = carry; devam = the lord; vaishravaṇam = Kubera; anujānānmi = I permit you; gamyatām = to leave.

Then, Rama, for his part, spoke to that excellent aerial car, "Serve as a transport to Kubera, the lord. I permit you to leave."

Sloka 60

tato rāmābhyanujñātaṃ tadvimānamanuttamam || 6-127-60
uttarāṃ diśamuddiśya jagāma dhanadālayam |

60. ramānubhyanujJṇātam = thus permitted by Rama; tat anuttamam vimānam = that excellent aerial car; tataḥ = thereupon; jagāma = proceeded; uttarām disham = towards northern directions; dhanadālayam uddishya = to reach the abode of Kubera the lord of riches.

Thus permitted by Rama, that excellent aerial car, thereupon proceeded towards the northern direction, so as t reach the abode of Kubera the lord of riches.

Sloka 61

vimānaṃ puṣpakaṃ divyaṃ samgṛhītaṃ tu rakṣasā || 6-127-61
agamaddhanadaṃ vegādrāmavākyapracoditam |

61. diryam puṣhpakam vimānam = that wonderful aerial car; Pushpaka; samgṛihītam = which was seized (once); rakṣhasā = by Ravana; agamat = went; vegāt = in speed; dhanadam = to Kubera; rāma vākya prachoditam = as impelled by the advice of Rama.

That wonderful aerial car, Pushpaka which was seized by Ravana once, went in speed to Kubera, as impelled by Rama's advice.

Sloka 62

purohitasyātmasamasya rāghavo |
bṛhaspateḥ śakra ivāmarādhīapḥ |
nipīḍya pādau pṛthagāsane śubhe |
sahaiva tenopaviveśa vīryavān || 6-127-62

62. nipīDya = affectionately pressing pādau = the feet; ātmasamasya purohitasya = of his family-priest (Vasishta the sage); who was his well-wisher; shakraḥ iva = even as Indra; amarādhipaḥ = the lord of celestials; (would press the feet); bṛihaspataḥ = of Brihaspati (the preceptor of gods); vīryavān rāghavaḥ = the valiant Rama; upavivesha = sat; tena sahsha = by his side; shubhe pṛithagāsane = on a separate seat.

Affectionately pressing the feet of Vasishta, his well-wisher and family-priest, even as Indra the lord of celestials would press the feet of Brihaspati (the preceptor of gods), the valiant Rama sat by his side, on a separate seat.
