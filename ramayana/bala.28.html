Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

[Sarga] 28
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Sept 09

Introduction

Rama requests sage Vishvamitra for endowing annulment missiles, besides projectile ones that have been accorded in the previous chapter. Sage Vishvamitra accords them, and while they are journeying further, they chance to see a picturesque forest on their way and Rama enquires about it.


Sloka 1

pratigṛhya tataḥ astrāṇi prahṛṣṭa vadanaḥ śuciḥ |
gacchan eva ca kākutstho viśvāmitram atha abravīt || 1-28-1

1. kākutsthaḥ = Kakustha = descendent, Rama; shuciḥ = decent one, Rama; astrāṇi pratigrṛhya = missiles, on receiving; tataḥ prahṛiṣṭa vadanaḥ = then, with a pleasant, face; gacchann eva = while on the way, thus; atha = then - now; vishvāmitram abravīt = , to Vishvamitra, he is speaking.

On becoming personally purified Rama, the descendent of Kakutstha-s, received the missiles, and then on their way he is now speaking to Sage Vishvamitra with a pleasant face. [1-28-1]

Sloka 2

gṛhīta astro asmi bhagavan durādharṣaḥ suraiḥ api |
astrāṇām tu aham icchāmi saṃhāram munipuṃgava || 1-28-2

2. bhagavan = oh god; gṛihīta astraḥ asmi = taken, missiles, I have; suraiḥ api durādharṣaḥ [asmi] = to gods, even, unassailable, I have become; munipungava = oh, sage, the eminent; aham tu = I, but; astrāṇām samhāram icchāmi = for the missiles, annulment, I wish [to know.]

"Oh, godly sage, I have taken the missiles and became unassailable even for gods, but oh, eminent sage, I wish to know the annulment of missiles too." So said Rama to the sage. [1-28-2]

Sloka 3

evam bruvati kākutsthe viśvāmitro mahā tapāḥ |
saṃhārān vyājahāra atha dhṛtimān suvrataḥ śuciḥ || 1-28-3

3. kākutsthe evam bruvati = by Rama, thus, said; atha = next; dhṛitimān su vrataḥ shuciḥ = resolute, well-averred one, purified one; vishvāmitraḥ mahāmuniḥ = Vishvamitra, great saint; samhāram vyājahāra = annulment, -started to say - imparted the knowledge of.

When Rama of Kakutstha said thus, next that resolute, well-averred and purified sage of eminence Vishvamitra imparted the knowledge of annulment missiles. [1-28-3]

jyotiṣam śakunam caiva nairāśya vimalau ubhau || 1-28-6
yaugaṃdhara vinidrau ca daitya pramadhanau tathā |
śuci bāhur mahābāhur niṣkali virucar tathā
sārcirmālī dhṛtimālī vṛttimān ruciraḥ tathā || 1-28-7
pitryaḥ saumanasaḥ caiva vidhūta makarau ubhau |

4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10. Rāghava = oh, Raghava; Rama; satyavantam = ṣatyavanta; satyakīrtim = ṣatyakirti; dhṛiṣṭam = Dhristha; rabhasam = Rabhasa; eva ca = like that, also; pratihārataram = Pratihāratara; nāma = named; parānmukham = Parānmukam; avānmukham = Avānmukha; lakshya = Lakshya; alakṣya = Alakshya; imau ca eva = these, also, thus; dṛiDhanābha = Dhridhanābha; sunābhakau = ṣunābha; dashāksha = Dashāksha; shatavaktrau = ṣhatavaktra; ca = also; dashashīrsha = dashashīrsha; shataudarau = ṣhatodara; padmanābha = Padmanābha; mahānābhau = ṃahānābha; dundunābha = Dundunābha; svanābhakau = ṣvanābha; jyotisham = Jyotisha; shakunam = ṣhakuna; ubhau ca eva = the two also, thus; nairāshya = ṇairāshya; vimalā = Vimala; yaugandhara = Yungandhara; vinidrau = Vinidra; ca = also; daitya = Daitya; pramadhanau = Prāmadhana; tathā = then; shucibāhuḥ = ṣuchibāhu; mahābāhur = ṃahābāhu; niṣkali = ṇishkali; virucar = Virucha; tathā = like that; sārchirmālī = ṣārrchirmāli; dhṛitimālī = Dhritimāli; vrittimān = Vrittiman; ruchiraḥ = Ruchira; tathā = then; pitryaḥ = Pitrya; saumanasam = ṣumana; ca eva = also, thus; vidhūta = Vidhoota; ṃakarau = ṃakara, ubhau = two of them; karavīrakaram = Karaveerakaram; ca eva = also; dhana = Dhana; dhānyau = Dhānya; kāmarūpam = Kāmaroopa; kāmarucim = Kāmaruchira; moham = ṃoha; tathā = thus; āvaranam = Aavarana; jṛimbhakam = Jrimbhaka; ṣarpa nābham = ṣarpa nābha; ca = also; panthāna = Prathāna; varanau = Varana; bhāsvarān = radiant ones; tathā = thus; kāma rūpinaḥ = disguise-wizards; kṛishāshva tanayān = Krishāshva, children - the missiles; mama pratīccha = from me, you take; te bhadram = to you, safety; pātra bhūtaḥ asi = eligible one, you are.

"Oh, Raghava, take these radiant missile-children of Krishāsva from me, namely Satyavanta, Satyakeerti, Dhristha, Rabhasa, Pratihāratara, Parānmuka, Avānmukha, Lakshya, Alakshya, Dhridhanābha, Sunābha, Dashāksha, Shatavaktra, Dashasheersha, Shatodara, Padmanābha, ṃahānābha, Dundunābha, Svanābha, Jyotisha, Shakuna, Nairāshya, Vimala, Yungandhara, Vinidra, Daitya, Pramadhana, Suchibāhu, ṃahābāhu, Nishkali, Virucha, Sārchirmāli, Dhritimāli, Vrittiman, Ruchira, Pitrya, Ssaumansa, and also thus Vidhoota, ṃakara, are two of them...Karaveerakara, Dhana, Dhānya, Kāmaroopa, Kāmaruchira, ṃoha, Aavarana, and thus Jrimbhaka, Sarvanābha, Varana... and these are the guise changing wizards ad you are the eligible one to handle these missiles, let safety be with you." Thus Vishvamitra gave missiles to Rama. [1-28-4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10]

Sloka 11 Verse Locator

bāḍham iti eva kākutstha prahṛṣṭena aṃtarātmanā |
divya bhāsvara dehāḥ ca mūrtimantaḥ sukhapradāḥ || 1-28-11
kecid aṃgāra sadṛśāḥ kecid dhūma upamāḥ tathā |
candra arka sadṛśāḥ kecit prahva aṃjali puṭāḥ tathā || 1-28-12
rāmam prāṃjalayo bhūtvā abruvan madhura bhāṣiṇaḥ |
ime sma naraśārdūla śādhi kim karavāma te || 1-28-13

11, 12, 13. kākutstha = Kakustha Rama; prahṛiṣṭtena = gladdened; antar ātmanā = in inner soul; bāDham iti eva = 'thus only', saying thus; received those missiles; divya bhāsvara dehāḥ ca = divine, radiant, bodied, also; mūrtimantaḥ = appealing ones; sukhapradāḥ = bliss-endowers; kechit angāra sadṛishāḥ = some, fire-like; kechit dhūma upamaḥ = some, smoke-like, thus; tathā = like that; chandra arka sadṛishāḥ = moon, sun similar to; kechit = some; prahva anjali puṭāḥ = concave, palms, holding-out - adjoined hollow palms in reverence - as in begging; tathā = then; prānjalayaḥ bhūtvā = adjoined palms, on becoming; madhura bhāshiṇaḥ = melodically, speaking; rāmam abruvan = to Rama, said; nara shārdūla = oh, manly tiger; ime sma = here, we are; shādhi = order us; te kim karavāma = what, for you, can we do.

Gladdening in his heart of hearts Rama of Kakutstha received the annulment missiles saying, "Agreed! Sire!" Those missiles are with radiantly divine bodies, appealing and endowing bliss. Some are fire-like, some smoke-like, and some moon-like and some sun-like, and such as they are, some of them holding out their hollowed palms, and some with palms adjoined melodiously spoke to Rama saying, "here we are, oh, manly-tiger, what can we do for you." [1-28-11, 12, 13]

Sloka 14

gaṃyatām iti tān āha yathā iṣṭam raghunandanaḥ |
mānasāḥ kārya kāleṣu sāhāyyam me kariṣyatha || 1-28-14

14. mānasāḥ = [while remaining in] memory; kārya kāleshu = , in work's, time, [need of the hour]; me sāhāyyam kariṣyatha = to me, assistance, you render; till then; yathā iṣṭam gamyatām = as you, wish, you can go; iti raghu nandanaḥ tān āha = thus, Ragu's legatee, to them, said.

"While remaining in my memory you assist me as and when needed, and till then you may go away as you wish," thus that legatee of Raghu-s said to those deities of annulment missiles. [1-28-14]

Sloka 15

atha te rāmam āmantrya kṛtvā ca api pradakṣiṇam |
evam astu iti kākutstham uktvā jagmuḥ yathāāgatam || 1-28-15

15. atha te = then, they; evam astu iti = thus, it will be, thus; kākutstham uktvā = to Kakutstha's, legatee, on saying; pradakshinam ca api kṛitvā = circumambulation, also, even, on performing; rāmam āmantrya = by Rama, consented; jagmuḥ yathāāgatam = went away, as they have come.

Saying "it will be done that way" to Rama, the legatee of Kakutstha-s, also on performing circumambulation to him and then on taking his consent those deities have gone away as they have come. [1-28-15]

Sloka 16

sa ca tān rāghavo jñātvā viśvāmitram mahāmunim |
gacchan eva atha madhuram ślakṣṇam vacanam abravīt || 1-28-16

16. saḥ rāghavaḥ ca = he that Raghava, also; tān jñātvā = them - annulment missiles, on knowing - learning; atha gacchan eva = then, while going - on path, only; vishvāmitram mahāmunim = to Vishvamitra, great sage; madhuram shlakshṇam vacanam abravīt = sweet, soft, words, spoke - asked.

After learning the nullifying missiles and then while proceeding on the walkway Raghava asked the great sage Vishvamitra with sweet and soft words. [1-28-16]

darśanīyam mṛgāakīrṇam manoharam atīva ca |
nānā prakāraiḥ śakunaiḥ valgubhāṣaiḥ alaṃkṛtam || 1-28-18

17, 18. itaḥ parvatasya = on this side, of mountain; a vi dūrataḥ = not, very, far; megha sankāsham = cloud-like formation; darshanīyam = picturesque; mṛiga ākīrṇam = with animals, spread-over; atīva manaḥ ramam = highly, heart, pleasing one; valgu bhāṣaiḥ = with pleasant; callings [of birds]; nānā prakāraiḥ shakunaiḥ alankṛitam = with numerous, kinds of, birds, embellished; vṛiksha khaṇDam = trees, a group of - a thicket; bhāti = = is shining forth; etat kim nu = this, what, really; me paramam kautūhalam hi = to me, very much, inquisitiveness, indeed.

"What is this cloudlike picturesque thicket shining forth not very far away on this side of mountain, with animals spreading over it, and embellished with numerous kinds of birds that have pleasant callings it is highly soul-delighting, thus, my inquisitiveness is growing very much to know it, what is this, really! [1-28-17, 18]

Sloka 19

niḥsṛtāḥ sma muniśreṣṭha kāntārāt romaharṣaṇāt |
anayā tu avagacchāmi deśasya sukhavattayā || 1-28-19
sarvam me śaṃsa bhagavan kasya āśrama padam tu idam |

19, 20a. muni shreṣṭha = oh, sage, the eminent; anayā deshasya sukhavattayā = by this, place's, happy environs; roma harṣaṇāt kāntārāt = from hair-raising, forest [extremely alarming forest of ṭataka]; niḥsṛitāḥ sma = came out, we have; thus; avagacchāmi = I understand; idam kasya āshrama padam = this is, whose, hermitage's, threshold; bhagavan = oh, god; sarvam me shamsa = all, to me, you tell.

"By the nature of happy environs at this place, oh, eminent sage, I understand that we have come out of the extremely alarming forest of Tataka, but oh, god, whose hermitage's threshold is this, kindly tell me all. [1-28-19]

Sloka 20 Verse Locator

20b, 21, 22. mahāmuneḥ = oh, great saint; bhagavan = oh, venerable one; brahman = oh, Brahman; yatra = where; brahma ghnāḥ = Brahman, murderers; duṣṭta cāriṇaḥ = evildoers; dur ātmanaḥ = evil-minded ones; te pāpāḥ = those, sinners; tava yajñasya vighnāya = your, ritual, for hindrance; sam prāptāḥ = they have come - are coming; tava yājñakī kriyā = your, pertaining to ritual, ceremonies; sā yatra = those [ritual works,] where; mayā rakshitavyā = by me, to be safeguarded; rākshasāḥ ca vadhyāḥ = demons, also, to be eliminated; tasya deshaḥ kaḥ = that, place, is what; munishreṣṭaḥ = oh, sage, the eminent; prabho = oh lord; aham etat sarvam = I, all, this; shrotum icchāmi = to listen, wish to.

"Oh, great saint, where are those murderers of Brahmans? Oh, venerable one, at which place those evil minded sinners and evildoers will be coming to hinder your ritual? And where, oh, Brahman, I have to safeguard your ritual ceremonies, and demons too are to be eliminated? Oh, eminent sage, what is that place? I would like to hear all of it." Rama asked Vishvamitra in that way. [1-28-20b, 21, 22]

Thus, this is the 28th chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
