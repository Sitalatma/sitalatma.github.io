Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 48
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Informed by the citizens of Rama's departure for the forest, when they returned to Ayodhya after being unable to find out the tracks of his chariot, the wives of the citizens reproach Kaikeyi and break into lamentation.

Sloka 1

teṣāmevam viṣṇṇānāmpīḍitānāmatīva ca |
bāṣpaviplutanetrāṇām saśokānām mumūrṣayā || 2-48-1
anugamya nivṛttānām rāmam nagara vāsinām |
udgatāni iva sattvāni babhūvur amanasvinām || 2-48-2

1;2. sattvāni = the lives of; teshhām = those; nagaram vāsinām = citizens; evam = thus; vishha ṇṇanām = dejected; atīva = greatly; pīditānām = hurt; bāshhpa vipluta netrāṇām = having eyes filled with tears; sa shokāmām = affected by grief; mumūrshhayā = impatient of life; anugamya = accompanied; rāmam = Rama; nivṛittānām = and returned; amanasvinām = cheerless; udgatānīva = as though their lives are gone.

The lives of those citizens, who had returned dejected and cheerless in this way after accompanying Rama were greatly hurt, having their eyes filled with tears afflicted with grief, longing to give up their lives and appeared as though they were dead.

Sloka 3

svam svam nilayam āgamya putra dāraiḥ samāvṛtāḥ |
aśrūṇi mumucuḥ sarve bāṣpeṇa pihita ānanāḥ || 2-48-3

3. āgamya = reaching; svam svam = each his own; nilayam = house; sarve = all of them; samāvṛitāḥ = having been surrounded; putra dāraiaḥ = by their sons and wives; mumuchuḥ = shed; ashrooṇi = tears; pihitānanāḥ = their face being covered; bashhpeṇa = by tears.

Reaching each his own house, all of them surrounded by their sons and wife, shed tears, their faces being covered by them.

Sloka 4

na ca āhṛṣyan na ca amodan vaṇijo na prasārayan |
na ca aśobhanta paṇyāni na apacan gṛha medhinaḥ || 2-48-4

4. na chāhṛishhyan = (they) did not make rejoice; na amodancha = did not make merry; vaṇijaḥ = merchants; na prasārayan = did not exhibit their wares; paṇyānicha = even their merchandise; na ashobhante = did not look charming. gṛiha methinaḥ = ṭhose in charge of home; na apachan = did not cook.

None was either delightful or merry. ṃerchants no longer exhibited their wares, nor their merchandise looked charming. Those in charge of home did not attend to cooking.

Sloka 5

naṣṭam dṛṣṭvā na abhyanandan vipulam vā dhana āgamam |
putram prathamajam labdhvā jananī na abhyanandata || 2-48-5

5. na abhyanandan = none was delightful;dṛshhṭam = a lost fortune; dhanāgamam vā = or on getting wealth; vipulam = in abundance. Jananī = ṃother;nābhyanandata = did not rejoice; labdhvā = on obtaing; putram = a son; prathamajam = born for the first time.

None was delightful for instance on finding out a lost fortune, or on getting riches in abundance. No mother did rejoice even on obtaining a son born for the first time.

Sloka 6

gṛhe gṛhe rudantyaḥ ca bhartāram gṛham āgatam |
vyagarhayantaḥ duhkha ārtā vāgbhis totraiḥ iva dvipān || 2-48-6

6. duhkhārtāḥ = oppressed with sorrow; rudantyaḥ = and lamenting; gṛihe gṛihe = (women) in every house; vyagarhayanta = heaped reproaches; bhartārām = on husbands; āgatam = who came; gṛiham = home; vāgbhiḥ = with words; dvipān iva = like elephants; totraiḥ = by goads.

Oppressed with sorrow and weeping, women in every house heaped reproaches on their husbands who came home, with words as sharp as pricks of the goad which attack an elephant.

Sloka 7

kim nu teṣām gṛhaiḥ kāryam kim dāraiḥ kim dhanena vā |
putraiḥ vā kim sukhaiḥ vā api ye na paśyanti rāghavam || 2-48-7

7. kim = "What purpose; teshhām = of theirs( will be served); ye = who; na pashyanti = do not see; rāghavam = Rama; gṛIhaiḥ = by their dwellings? Kim kāryam = For what purpose; dāraih = their wives?kim = what purpose dhananivā = by their wealth even? Kim = What purpose? Putraiḥ vā = by their sons or; sukhairvāpi = even pleasures even?"

"What purpose of theirs who do not see Rama, will be served by their dwellings, wife or wealth or sons or pleasures even?"

Sloka 8

ekaḥ sat puruṣo loke lakṣmaṇaḥ saha sītayā |
yo anugaccati kākutstham rāmam paricaran vane || 2-48-8

8. lakshhma ṇaḥ = Lakshmana; ekaḥ = alone; satpurushhaḥ = is a good man; loke = in this world; yaḥ = who; anugachchati = was following; rāmam = rama; kākutstham = belonging to Kakutstha dynasty; sītayā saha = along with ṣita; paricharam rending service; vane = in the forest."

Lakshmana alone is a good man in this world, who was accompanying Rama belonging to Kakutstha dynasty, along with Sita ,duly rendering service to them in the forest."

Sloka 9

āpagāḥ kṛta puṇyāḥ tāḥ padminyaḥ ca sarāṃsi ca |
yeṣu snāsyati kākutstho vigāhya salilam śuci || 2-48-9

9. kṛita puṇyāḥ = fortunate are; taḥ = such; āpagāḥ = rivers; padminyaḥ = lotus-ponds; sarāmsicha = and lakes; snāsyati = bathing; yeshhu = in whose; shuchi = sacred; salilam = waters; kākutstha = Rama; vigāhya = plunged into;

"Fortunate too are the rivers; lotus ponds and lakes for bathing in whose sacred waters Rama entered into."

Sloka 10

śobhayiṣyanti kākutstham aṭavyo ramya kānanāḥ |
āpagāḥ ca mahā anūpāḥ sānumantaḥ ca parvatāḥ || 2-48-10

10. āṭavyaḥ = forests; ramya kānanāḥ = with beautiful row of trees; mahānūpāḥ = tracks of land abounding in water; āpagāḥ cha = rivers; parvatāḥ = and mountains; sānumantaḥ = with alluring peaks; shobhayishhyanti = will bring splendor to; kākutstham = Rama.

"Forests with beautiful row of trees, tracks of land abounding in water, rivers and mountains with alluring peaks will bring splendor to Rama."

Sloka 11

kānanam vā api śailam vā yam rāmaḥ abhigamiṣyati |
priya atithim iva prāptam na enam śakṣyanti anarcitum || 2-48-11

11. "yam = Which; shailam vā = mountain; kānanamvāpi = or forest; rāmaḥ = Rama; adhigamishhyati = will visit; na shakshhyanti = they can not; anarchitum = remain without respecting; enam = him; priyātithim iva = like a beloved guest; prāptam = who has arrived."

"Any mountain or forest which Rama will visit, will not fail to respect him like a beloved guest who has arrived."

Sloka 12

vicitra kusuma āpīḍā bahu manjali dhāriṇaḥ |
akāle ca api mukhyāni puṣpāṇi ca phalāni ca || 2-48-12

12. nagāḥ = Plants; vichitra kusuma pīdāḥ = with many - colored flowers as their chaplets; bahumaṇjari dhāriṇaḥ = bearing copious cluster of blossoms; bhramara shālinaḥ = full of bees; darshayishhyanti = exhibit themselves; rāghavam = at Rama."

"Plants with many -colored flowers as their chaplets, bearing copious clusters of blossoms full of bees exhibit themselves at Rama."

Sloka 13

akāle cāpi mukhyāni puṣpāṇi ca phalāni ca |
darśayiṣyanti anukrośāt girayo rāmam āgatam || 2-48-13

13. giryaḥ = mountains; darshayishhyanti = will display; mukhyāni = principal; pushhpāṇi = flowers; phalānicha = and fruits; rāmam = to Rama; āgatam = (when) arrived; akālechāpi = even in unseasonable ness anukroshāt = with compassion.

"Even in unseasonable ness, mountains in compassion will present principal flowers and fruits to Rama, on his arrival."

Sloka 14

prasraviṣyanti toyāni vimalāni mahīdharāḥ |
vidarśayantaḥ vividhān bhūyaḥ citrāmḥ ca nirjharān || 2-48-14

14. "mahīdharāḥ = mountains; vidarshayantaḥ = will show; vividhān = various; chitrāmshcha = wonderful; nirjharān = waterfalls; bhūyaḥ = again and again; prasravishhyanti = streaming forth; vimalāni = uncontaminated; toyāni = waters. "

"ṃountains will show various wonderful waterfalls again and again, duly streaming forth uncontaminated waters."

Sloka 15

pādapāḥ parvata agreṣu ramayiṣyanti rāghavam |
yatra rāmaḥ bhayam na atra na asti tatra parābhavaḥ || 2-48-15

15. "pādapāḥ = trees; parvatāgrashhu = on mountain -peaks; ramayishhyanti = will enrapture; rāghavam = Rama; yatra = where; rāmaḥ = there is Rama; atra = there is ; na = neither; bhayam = fear; nāsti = nor; tatra = there is ; parābhavaḥ = overthrow."

"Trees on apex of mountains will enrapture Rama. Where there is Rama, there is neither fear nor humiliation".

Sloka 16

sa hi śūraḥ mahā bāhuḥ putraḥ daśarathasya ca |
purā bhavati no dūrāt anugaccāma rāghavam || 2-48-16

16. "saḥ = that Rama; putraḥ = the son; dasarathasya = of Dasaratha; shūraḥ = the hero and; mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; purā bhavati = will become; dūrat = distant; naḥ = from us. Anugachhāma = Let us run after; rāghavam = Rama."

"That Rama the son of Dasaratha, the hero and the mighty armed will come to our view not far from us. Let us run after him."

Sloka 17

pādac cāyā sukhā bhartus tādṛśasya mahātmanaḥ |
sa hi nātho janasya asya sa gatiḥ sa parāyaṇam || 2-48-17

17. "pāda chchhāyā = ṭhe shelter of feet; bhartuḥ = of the Lord; mahātmanaḥ = and the high-souled; tādṛishsya = is in such a manner; sukhā = a joy; saḥ = ḥe; nātha hi = indeed is protector; asyajanasya = of these people. saḥ = ḥe; gatiḥ = the refuge; saḥ = he; parāyaṇam = the supreme asylum."

"The shelter of the feet of the lord and the high-souled Rama is in itself a joy. Rama indeed is the protector of all of us, he the refuge and our supreme asylum "

Sloka 18

vayam paricariṣyāmaḥ sītām yūyam tu rāghavam |
iti paura striyo bhartṛṛn duhkha ārtāḥ tat tat abruvan || 2-48-18

18. "vayam = we; paricharishhyāmaḥ = shall serve; sītām = ṣita; yāyamtu = while you (serve); rāghavam = Rama." iti = ṭhus; poura striyaḥ = the citizen's wives; duhkhārtāḥ = afflicted with agony; abruvam = spoke; tattat = this or that word; bhartṛīn = to their husbands.

"We shall serve Sita; while you attend on Rama." Thus, the citizen's wives, afflicted with agony, spoke in so many words to their husbands.

Sloka 19

yuṣmākam rāghavo araṇye yoga kṣemam vidhāsyati |
sītā nārī janasya asya yoga kṣemam kariṣyati || 2-48-19

19. rāghavaḥ = "Rama; vidhāsyati = will secure; yoga kshhemam = the needs and interests; yushhmākam = of yours. ṣītā = ṣita; karishhyati = will secure; yogakshhemam = the needs and interests; asya janasya nārī = of these people; the women folk.

"Rama will secure the needs and interests of yours in the forest, while Sita will do the same thing with regard to us womenfolk."

Sloka 20

ko nv anena apratītena sa utkaṇṭhita janena ca |
samprīyeta amanojñena vāsena hṛta cetasā || 2-48-20

20. "kaḥ nu = who will be; sampriyeta = pleased; anena vāsena = with this residence;(in this city); apratitena = which is apprehensible; sotkaṇṭhitajanenacha = with anxious people in it; amanoG^yena = not a pleasant spot; hṛita chetasā = with unsettled minds?"

"Who will be highly pleased with this residence in the city, which is apprehensible, with anxious people in it and not being a pleasant spot with unsettled minds?"

Sloka 21

kaikeyyā yadi ced rājyam syāt adharmyam anāthavat |
na hi no jīvitena arthaḥ kutaḥ putraiḥ kutaḥ dhanaiḥ || 2-48-21

21. syādyadi = If it were; rājyam = rule; kaikeyyāḥ = by Kaikeyi; adharmyam = it will not be in consonance with righteousness; anāthavat = with no protector; na hi = indeed; no; arthaḥ = use; jīvitena = by life; naḥ = to us. Kutaḥ = much less; putraiḥ = by sons;kutaḥ = much less;dhanaiḥ = by riches.

"If it were to be the rule of Kaikeyi, it will not be in consonance with righteousness, with no protector and indeed with no use for our lives, mush less for our sons and riches."

Sloka 22

yayā putraḥ ca bhartā ca tyaktāv aiśvarya kāraṇāt |
kam sā parihared anyam kaikeyī kula pāṃsanī || 2-48-22

22. sā Kaikeyi = "ṭhat Kaikeyi; yayā = by whom; putrascha = her son; bharatācha = and her husband; tyaktā = were forsaken; aishvarya kāraṇāt = for the sake of power;kula pāmsanī = (and)who brought disagrace to her family; kam anyam = whom else; pari haret = she will not abandon?"

"Whom else Kaikeyi will not abandon? --that Kaikeyi, by whom her son and her husband were forsaken for the sake of power and who brought disgrace to her family."

Sloka 23

kaikeyyā na vayam rājye bhṛtakā nivasemahi |
jīvantyā jātu jīvantyaḥ putraiḥ api śapāmahe || 2-48-23

23. kaikeyyāḥ = (while) Kaikeyi; jīvantyāḥ = is surviving ; vayam = we ;jātu = ever; na nivasemahi = will not inhabit; rājye = this kingdom; bhṛitakāḥ = as (Kaikeyi's) servants; jīvityāḥ = (as long as we are) living; shapāmahe = we swear even; putrairapi = even by our sons."

"We swear even by our sons that while Kaikeyi is surviving and as long as we live, we will never inhabit this kingdom as Kaikeyi's servants!"

Sloka 24

yā putram pārthiva indrasya pravāsayati nirghṛṇā |
kaḥ tām prāpya sukham jīved adharmyām duṣṭa cāriṇīm || 2-48-24

24. "kaḥ = who; jīvet = can live; sukham = happily; prāpya = on having obtained; tām = her; yā = who; nirjhṛI ṇā = without pity; pravāsayati = banished; putram = the son; pārthivendrasya = of king; adharmyām = the impious woman; dushhṭachāriṇīm = of wicked conduct?"

"Who can live happily on having obtained (as one's ruler) that impious woman of wicked conduct, who banished the son of the king without any pity?"

Sloka 25

upadrutamidam sarvamanālambamanāyakam |
kaikeyyā hi kṛte sarvam vināśamupayāsyati || 2-48-25

25. idam sarvam = " the whole of this kingdom; upadrutam = visited by calamities; anālambam = having no support;anāyakam = without any leader; upayāsyati = will meet with;vināsham = ruin;kaikeyyāḥ kṛite = through Kaikeyi's fault."

"The whole of this kingdom, without any leader, having no support and visited by calamities, will meet with ruin because of Kaikeyi's fault."

Sloka 26

na hi pravrajite rāme jīviṣyati mahī patiḥ |
mṛte daśarathe vyaktam vilopaḥ tat anantaram || 2-48-26

26. rāme = Rama; pravrajite = having gone to exile; mahīpatiḥ = the monarch; na jīvishhyati hi = will indeed not survive! mṛite = After the death; dasharathe = of Dasaratha; vilāpaḥ = utter moaning sounds; tadanantaram = thereafter; vyaktam = It is certain.

"For, Rama having gone into exile, the monarch will not survive and when Dasaratha is dead, utter regrets will remain thereafter. It is certain!"

Sloka 27

te viṣam pibata āloḍya kṣīṇa puṇyāḥ sudurgatāḥ |
rāghavam vā anugaccadhvam aśrutim vā api gaccata || 2-48-27

27. te = you; as such; pibata = drink; vishham = poison; āloDya = duly stirred up; kshhīṇa pu ṇyāḥ = since your merits are exhausted; sudurgatāḥ = and you are marked out by ill fortune; anugachchhadhvam vā = or follow; rāmam = Rama (to the forest); gachchhati = or reach; ashrutim vā = (the land where the name of Kaikeyi) may not reach your ears."

"So, drink poison duly stirred up, since your merits are exhausted and you are marked out by ill fortune. Otherwise, follow Rama to forest or reach a place where even the name of Kaikeyi may not reach your ears."

Sloka 28

mithyā pravrājitaḥ rāmaḥ sabhāryaḥ saha lakṣmaṇaḥ |
bharate samniṣṛṣṭāḥ smaḥ saunike paśavo yathā || 2-48-28

28. rāmaḥ = "Rama; pravrājitaḥ = has been sent to exile; sa sītaḥ = with ṣita; saha lakshhmaṇaḥ = and with Lakshmana; mithyā = deceit fully; smaḥ = we have been;sannisṛishhṭāḥ = handed over; bharata = to Bharata; sounike yathā = like to a slaughterer; pashavaḥ = the beasts."

"Rama has been sent to exile along with Sita and Lakshmana deceitfully. We have been handed over now to Bharata, like the beasts in the hands of a slaughterer."

Sloka 29

pūrṇacandrānanaḥ śyāmo gūḍhajatrurariṃdamaḥ |
ājānubāhuḥ padmākṣo rāmo lakṣmanapūrvajaḥ || 2-48-29
pūrvābhibhāṣī madhuraḥ satyavādī mahābalaḥ |
saumyaḥ sarvasya lokasya candravatpriyadarśanaḥ || 2-48-30
nūnam puruṣaśārdūlo mattamātaṅgavikramaḥ |
śobhayuśyatyaraṇyāni vicaran sa mahārathaḥ || 2-48-31

29;30;31. rāmo = Rama; pūrṇa chandrānanaḥ = whose face is like the full moon; shyāmo = dark brown of complexion; gūDha jatraḥ = whose collar bone is invisible (because it is covered with flesh) arindamaḥ = conquerer of his foes; ājānubāhuḥ = whose arms descend to the knees; padmākshho = whose eyes resemble lotuses; lakshhmaṇa pūrvajaḥ = the elder brother of Lakshmana; pūrvābhibhāshhī = who takes initiative in speaking; satyavādī = speaking with candor; madhuvaḥ = and sweetness; mahābalaḥ = very strong; soumyaḥ = benevolent; sarvasya lokasya = to all people; chandravat priyadarshanaḥ = charming sight as the moon; purusha shārdūlo = tiger among men; matta mātaṇ^ga vikramaḥ = as mighty as an elephant in rut; sa mahārathaḥ = that great car- warrior; nūnam = surely; shobhayishjyati = will adorn; araṇyāni = the woods; vicharam = while roaming (through them)."

"Rama, whose face is like the full moon, of dark brown complexion, whose collar-bone is invisible (because of its being covered with flesh), a conqueror of foes, whose arms descend to his knees, whose eyes resemble lotuses, the elder brother of Lakshmana, who takes initiative in speaking and expresses with sweetness, truthful of speech and possessed of extra ordinary strength, is benevolent to all, delightfully charming as the moon, that tiger among men, as mighty as an elephant in rut, that great car-warrior, will surely adorn the woods, while roaming through them.."

Sloka 32

tāstathā vilapantyastu nagare nāgarastriyaḥ |
cukruśurduḥkhasamtaptāmṛtyoriva bhayāgame || 2-48-32

32. tāḥ = those; nāgara strīyaḥ = wives of citizens; nagare = in the city (of Ayodhya); vilapantyaḥ = lamenting; tathā = in that manner; chukrushuḥ = cried; bhayāgame iva = as though fear has cropped up; mṛityoḥ = for death.

Those wives of citizens in the city of Ayodhya, lamenting as aforesaid, began weeping, as though fear has cropped up for a forth-coming death.

Sloka 33

ityeva vilapantīnām strīṇām veśmasu rāghavam |
jagāmāstam dinakaro rajanī cābhyavartata || 2-48-33

33. dinakaraḥ = the sun; jagāma = sank; astam = below the horizon; rajanīcha = and the night; abhyavartata = fell; strīṇām = ( while) the women; veshmasu = in the houses; vilapantīnām = were weeping; ityevam = in the way; rāghavam = about Rama.

The sun sank below the horizon and the night fell, while the women in the houses were weeping in that manner about Rama.

Sloka 34

naṣṭajvalanasampātā praśāntādhyāyasatkathā |
timireṇābhilipteva tadā sā nagarī babhau || 2-48-34

34. sā nagarī = ṭhat city of Ayodhya; nashhṭa jvalana sampatā = in which kindling of fires had ceased; prashantādhyāya satkathā = chanting of Vedas and narration of sacred stories died out; babhou = looked; abhilipteva = as though coated; timireṇa = with darkness; tadā = at this time.

The city of Ayodhya, in which the kindling of fires had ceased and the chanting of Vedas and narration of sacred stories died out, looked as though it was coated with darkness at that time.

Sloka 35

upaśāntavaṇikpaṇyā naṣṭaharṣā nirāśrayā |
ayodhyā nagarī cāsīnnaṣṭatāramivāmbaram || 2-48-35

35. ayodhyā nagarī = ṭhe city of Ayodhya; upashānta vaṇikpaṇyā = in which the business of the trading class had come to a stand-still; nashhṭa harshhā = in which joy had faded out; nirāshrayā = which had become support less; āsīt = became; ambaramiva = like a sky; nashhṭa tāram = in which the stars had disappeared.

The city of Ayodhya, in which the business of the trading class had come to a stand-still, in which joy had been faded out, which had become (now) support less, looked dim as though stars had disappeared in the sky.

Sloka 36

tathā striyo rāma nimittam āturā |
yathā sute bhrātari vā vivāsite |
vilapya dīnā rurudur vicetasaḥ |
sutaiḥ hi tāsām adhiko hi so abhavat || 2-48-36

36. striyaḥ = the women; vichetasaḥ = whose minds became sick; yathā = as one would feel on; sute = (one's own) son; bhrātarivā = or brother; vivāsite = having been sent into exile; āturāḥ = cried; rāma nimittam = on account of Rama; dīnaḥ = miserably; vilapya = lamented; ruruduḥ = (and) wept. tāsām = ṭo them; saḥ = that Rama; abhavat hi = indeed became; adhikaḥ = more than; sutāḥ = their sons.

The women whose minds became sick on account of Rama, as one would feel on one's own son or brother having been sent into exile, cried miserably expressing their grief in various ways. To them, Rama was dearer than their very sons!

Sloka 37

praśāntagītotsava nṛttavādanā |
vyapāstaharṣā pihitāpaṇodayā |
tadā hyayodhyā nagarī babhūva sā |
mahārṇavaḥ samkṣapitodako yathā ||2-48-37

37. sā = that;ayodhyā nagari = city of Ayodhya; prasānta gītotsava nṛtta vādanā = in which singing;rejoicing and instrumental music had been completely set at rest;ivyapāsta harshhā = whose joy had departed; pihitā pa ṇodayā = whose shops had been closed; tadā = then; babhūva = became; mahārṇavaḥ yathā = like a great ocean; samkshhapitodakaḥ = whose waters had dried up.

That city of Ayodhya, in which singing, rejoicing, dancing and instrumental music had been completely set at rest, when jpy had departed forever and whose shops had been closed, looked at that time like a grat ocean whose waters had dried up.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe aṣṭacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 48th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
