Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 86
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

On the advice of Vibhishana, Lakshmana releases a multiple of arrows towards Indrajit. A fight ensues between bears monkeys and demons. Hearing the terrible noise of the battle, Indrajit discontinues his sacrificial rite and enters the battle-field. Hanuma with trees as weapons, plays havoc among the demons and challenges Indrajit to a duel. Lakshmana catches sight of Indrajit, coming in his chariot.

Sloka 1

atha tasyāmavasthāyāṃ lakṣmaṇaṃ rāvaṇānujaḥ |
pareṣāmahitaṃ vākyamarthasādhakamabravīt || 6-86-1

1. tasyām avasthāyām = In that situation; rāvaṇānujaḥ = Vibhishana; atha = then; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; ahitam = which were hostile; pareṣhām = to the enemies; artha sādhakam = and which promoted an advantage (to Lakshmana).

In that situation, Vibhishana then spoke the following words, which were hostile to the enemies and advantageous to Lakshmana.

Sloka 2

yadetadrākṣasānīkaṃ meghaśyāmaṃ vilokyate |
etadāyodhyatāṃ śīgramṃ kapibhiśca śilāyudhaiḥ || 6-86-2

2. yat yetat rākṣhasānīkam = = Let this army of demons; vilokyate = which is seen here; meghashyāmam = dark as a cloud; shīghram etat āyodhyatām = be quickly engaged in this battle; kapibhiḥ = by the monkeys; shilāyudhaiḥ = using rocks as their weapons.

"Let this army of demons which is seen here, dark as a cloud, be quickly engaged in battle, by the monkeys using rocks as thier weapons."

Sloka 3

asyānīkasya mahato bhedane yatalakṣmaṇa |
rākṣasendrasuto.apyatra bhinne dṛśyo bhaviṣyati || 6-86-3

3. lakṣhmaṇa = O Lakshmana!; yata = make an effort; bhedane = to break; asya mahataḥ anīkasya = this mighty army; atra bhinne = while this army is borken; rākṣhasendra sutaḥ.api = Indrajit also; dṛishyaḥ bhaviṣhyati = will become visible.

"O Lakshmana! ṃake an effort to break this mighty army. When this army is destroyed, Indrajit also will become visible."

Sloka 4

sa tvamindrāśaniprakhyaiḥ śarairavakiranparān |
abhidravāśu yāvadvai naitatkarma samāpyate || 6-86-4

4. saḥ tvam = you as such; abhidrava vai = rush forward; āshu = quickly; avakiran = gushing out; sharaiḥ = arrows; indrāshaniprakhyaiḥ = equal to Indra's thunderbolt; parān = on the enemies; etat = so long as; etat = this; karma = sacrificial act; na samāpyate = is not carried through.

"You as such, rush forward quickly, gushing out arrows, equal to Indra's thunderbolt, on the enemies, so long as this sacrificial act of Indrajit is not actually carried through."

Sloka 5

jahi vīradurātmānaṃ māyāparamadhārmikam |
rāvaṇiṃ krūrakarmāṇaṃ sarvalokabhayāvaham || 6-86-5

5. vīra = O hero!; jahi = kill; rāvaṇim = Indrajit; durātmanam = the evil-minded; māyāparam = who is interested in doing conjuring tricks; adharmikam = who is unjust; krūra karmāṇam = engaged in cruel acts; sarvaloka bhayāvaham = and who brings fear to all the worlds.

"O hero! ṃake a short work of Indrajit, the evil minded, who is interested in performing conjuring tricks, who is unjust, engaged in cruel acts and who brings fear to all the worlds."

Sloka 6

vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā lakṣmaṇaḥ śubhalakṣaṇaḥ |
vavarṣa śaravarṣāṇi rākṣasendrasutaṃ prati || 6-86-6

6. shrutvā = hearing; vibhīṣhaṇa vachaḥ = the words of Vibhishana; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; shubhalakṣhaṇaḥ = having auspicious marks; vavarṣha = showered frth; sharavarṣhāṇi = streams of arrows; rākṣhasendrasutam prati = towards Indrajit.

Hearing the words of Vibhishana, Lakshmana, having auspicious marks, showered forth streams of arrows in the direction of Indrajit.

Sloka 7

ṛkṣāḥ śākhāmṛgāścaiva drumādrivarayodhinaḥ |
abhyadhāvanta sahitāstadanīkamavasthitam || 6-86-7

7. ṛikṣhāḥ = bears; shākhāmṛigaishchaiva = and monkeys; druma pravara yodhinaḥ = who fight with gigantic trees; abhyadhāvanta = rushed towards; tat ānīkam = that army (of demons); avasthitam = who were drawn up in battle-array; sahitāḥ = together.

Bears and monkeys, who fight was gigantic trees, rushed towards that army of demons, drawn up in battle-array.

Sloka 8

rākṣasāśca śitairbāṇairasibhiḥ śaktitomaraiḥ |
abhyavartanta samavare kapisainyajighāṃsavaḥ || 6-86-8

8. rākṣhasāḥ cha = Demons also; kapisainya jighāmsavaḥ = with an intent to kill the monkeys; samare = in battle; abhyavartanta = attacked; shitaiḥ bāṇaiḥ = with sharp arrows; asibhiḥ = swords; shaktitomaraiḥ = spears and javelins.

The demons too, with an intent to kill the monkeys in battle, attacked them with sharp arrows, sowrds, spears and javelins.

Sloka 9

sa samprahārastumulaḥ sañjajñe kapirakṣasām |
śabdena mahatā laṅkāṃ nādayanvai samantataḥ || 6-86-9

9. mahatā shabdena = with a great uproar; saḥ = that; tumulaḥ = tumultuous; samprahāraḥ = battle; samjaJṇe = occured; kapirākṣhasām = between the monkeys and the demons; nādayantvai = reverberating; laṇ^kām = Lanka; samantataḥ = on all sides.

That tumultuous battle between the monkeys and the demons rendered Lanka noisy on all sides with its great uproar.

Sloka 10

śastrairbahuvidhākāraiḥ śitairbāṇaiśca pādapaiḥ |
udyatairgiriśṛṅgaiśca ghorairākāśamāvṛtam || 6-86-10

10. ākāsham = the sky; āvṛitam = was covered; shastraiḥ = with weapons; vividhākāraiḥ = of various shapes; shitaiḥ bāṇaishcha = with sharp arrows; pādapaiḥ = with trees; ghoraiḥ udyataiḥ girishṛingaishcha = and with tossed mountain-tops.

The sky was covered with weapons of various shapes, sharp arrows, trees and tossed mountain tops.

Sloka 11

te rākṣasā vānareṣu vikṛtānanabāhavaḥ |
niveśayantaḥ śastrāṇi cakruste sumahadbhayam || 6-86-11

11. te rākṣhasāḥ = those demons; vikṛitānana bāhavaḥ = of ugly faces and arms; niveshayantaḥ = hurling; shastrāṇi = weapons; vānareṣhu = on monkeys; chakruḥ sumahat bhayam = created a great fear.

Those demons of ugly faces and arms, hurling weapons on the monkeys, created a great fear.

Sloka 12

tathaiva sakalairvṛkṣairgiriśṛṅgaiśca vānarāḥ |
abhijaghnurnijaghnuśca samare rākṣasarṣabhān || 6-86-12

12. vānarāshcha tathaiva = even monkeys too; abhijaghnuḥ = struck; jaghnuḥ = and killed; sarva rākṣhasān = all the demons; samare = in the battle; sakalaiḥ = with all types; vṛikṣhaiḥ = trees; girishṛiṇgaishcha = and mountain-peaks.

Even the monkeys too struck and killed all the demons in the battle, with various types of trees and mountain-peaks.

Sloka 13

ṛkṣavānaramukhyaiśca mahākāyairmahābalaiḥ |
rakṣasāṃ vadhyamānānāṃ mahadbhayamajāyata || 6-86-13

13. mahat = a great; bhayam = fear; ajāyata = developed; rakṣhasām = among the demons; yudhyamānānām = who were fighting; mahābalaiḥ = with mighty; ṛikṣha vānara mukhyaiḥ = leaders of bears and monkeys; mahākāyaiḥ = possessing collosal bodies.

A great fear developed among the demons who were fighting with mighty leaders of bears and monkeys endowed with colossal bodies.

Sloka 14

svamanīkaṃ viṣaṇṇaṃ tu śrutvā śatrubhirarditam |
udatiṣṭhata durdharṣastatkarmaṇyananuṣṭhite || 6-86-14

14. shrutvā = hearing; svam anīkam = that his own army; arditam = tormented; shatrubhiḥ = by the enemies; viṣhaṇṇam = was dejected; saḥ durdharṣhaḥ = that unconquerable Indrajit; udatiṣhṭhata = left off; ananuṣhṭhite = without performing; karmaṇi = the sacrificial rite.

Hearing that his own army being tormented by the enemies, was dejected; that unconquerable Indrajit left off, without performing the sacrificial rite.

Sloka 15

vṛkṣāndhakārānniṣkramya jātakrodhaḥ sa rāvaṇiḥ |
āruroha rathaṃ sajjaṃ pūrvayuktaṃ sa rākṣasaḥ || 6-86-15

15. saḥ = that; jātakrodhaḥ = enraged; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit; nirgamya = coming out; vṛikṣhāndhakarāt = from the gloominess of the trees; āruroha = ascended; ratham = his chariot; sajjam = kept ready; susamyatam = and well-fastened; pūrvayuktam = with horss; before hand.

That enraged Indrajit, coming out from the gloominess of the trees, ascended his firmly yoked chariot which had already been united

Sloka 16

sa bhīmakārmukaśaraḥ kṛṣṇāñjanacayopamaḥ |
raktāsyanayanaḥ krūro babhau mṛtyurivāntakaḥ || 6-86-16

16. saḥ bhīmaḥ = that awful Indrajit; bhīma kārmuka sharaḥ = armed with terrific bow and arrows; kṛiṣhṇāṇ^jana chayopamaḥ = equal to a mass of black collyrium; raktāsyanayanaḥ = with his blood-red eyes; babhau = shone; antakaḥ mṛityuriva = like the destructive Death.

That awful Indrajit, armed with terrific bow and arrows, looking like a mass of black collyrium with his blood-red eyes, appeared like the destructive Death.

Sloka 17

dṛṣṭvaiva tu rathasthaṃ taṃ paryavartata tadbalam |
rakṣasāṃ bhīmavegānāṃ lakṣmaṇena yuyutsatām || 6-86-17

17. dṛiṣhṭvaiva = just on seeing; tam = him; rathastham = being on a chariot; tat balam = that army; rakṣhasām = of demons; bhīma vegānām = with a terrific speed; paryavartata = turned back; lakṣhmaṇena yuyutsatām = wishing to fight with Lakshmana.

Just on seeing Indrajit being on a chariot, that army of demons with a terrific speed, turned back, wishing to fight with Lakshmana.

Sloka 18

tasmin kāle tu hanumānudyamya sudurāsadam |
dharaṇīdharasaṅkāśī mahāvṛkṣamarindamaḥ || 6-86-18
sa rākṣasānāṃ tatsainyaṃ kālāgniriva nirdahan |
cakāra bahubhirvṛkṣairniḥsaṃjñaṃ yudhi vānaraḥ || 6-86-19

18; 19. tasmin kāle = At that time; hanumān = ḥanuma; arimdamaḥ = the destroyer of enemies = dharaṇīdhara samkāshaḥ = looking like a mountain; udyamya = uplifting; sudurāsadam = an absolutely unparalleled; mahāvṛikṣham = large tree; nirdahan = consuming; tat = taht; sainyam = army; rākṣhasānām = of demon; kālāgniriva = like the fire at the time of dissolution of the world; saḥ vānaraḥ = and that aforesaid monkey (ḥanuma); chakāra niḥsamJṇam = made unconscious (the army); bahubhiḥ vṛikṣhaiḥ = with many trees; yudhi = in the battle.

At that time, Hanuma the destroyer of enemies looking like a mountain, uplifted an absolutely unpālleled large tree. Consuming that army of demons, as a fire at the time of universal dissolution, the aforesaid monkey made that army unconscious on the battle field, with many trees.

Sloka 20

vidhvaṃsayantaṃ tarasā dṛṣṭvaiva pavanātmajam |
rākṣasānāṃ sahasrāṇi hanūmantamavākiran || 6-86-20

20. dṛiṣhṭvaiva = immediately on seeing; pavanātmajam = ḥanuma; vidhvamsayantam = destroying; tavasā = (their army) rapidly; sahasrāṇi = thousands; rākṣhasānām = of demons; avākiran = hurled weapons.

Immediately on seeing Hanuma destroying thier army rapidly, thousands of demons hurled weapons on him.

21; 22; 23; 24. samāsādya = approaching; parvatopamam = ḥanuma looking like a mountain; rākṣhasāḥ shta shūladharāḥ = demons bearing pointed pikes; abhiaghnuḥ = struck him; samantatāt = from all sides; shūlāḥ = with pikes; asi pāṇayaḥ = those = carrying swords in hands; asibhiḥ cha = with swords; shaktihastāḥ = those carrying javelins in their hands; shaktībhiḥ cha = with javelin; parighaishcha gadābhishcha = with iron rods and maces; paṭṭishāyudhāḥ = those armed with sharp-edged spears; paṭṭishaiḥ = with spears; shubhadarshanaiḥ = with bright-looking; kuntaiḥ cha = spears; shatashaḥ = with hundreds; shataghnībhiḥ = of cylindrical pieces of wood studded with iron spikes; āyasaiḥ mudgarairapi = with steel hammers; gharaiḥ parashubhishchaiva = with awful axes; bhindi pālaishcha = with slings for throwing stones; vajrakalpaiḥ muṣhṭibhiḥ = with their thunderbolt-like fists; talaiḥ = and slaps; ashani samnibhaiḥ = which fell like flashes of lightning; samkruddhaḥ = the enraged ḥanuma; chakāra = made; mahat kadanam = a colossal destruction; teṣhām = of those demons.

Approaching Hanuma looking like a mountain, the demons bearing pointed pikes struck him from all sides with pkes, those carrying swords in their hands with swords, those carring javelins in their hands with jalins, with iron rods with maces; those armed with sharp-edged spears with spears, as also with bright-looking spears, with hundreds of cylindrical wooden pieces studded with iron spikes, with steel hammers, ,with awful axes, with slings for the rwing stones, with their thunderbolt-like fists and slaps which fell like flashes of lighning. Then, the enraged Hanuma made a colossal destruction of those demons.

Sloka 25

sa dadarśa kapiśreṣṭhamacalopamamindrajit |
sūdayānamamitraghnamamitrānpavanātmajam || 6-86-25

25. saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; dadarsha = saw; pavanātmajam = that ḥanuma; kapishreṣhṭham = the foremost of monkeys; achalopamam = looking like a mountain; asantrastam = not at all fearing; sūdamānam = and killing; amitrān = the enemies.

That Indrajit saw Hanuma, the foremost of monkeys, looking like a mountain, fearlessly killing his enemies.

Sloka 26

sa sārathimuvācedaṃ yāhi yatraiṣa vānaraḥ |
kṣayameva hi naḥ kuryādrākṣasānāmupekṣitaḥ || 6-86-26

26. saḥ = ḥe; uvācha = spoke; idam = the following words; sārathim = to his charioterr; yāhi = proceed; yatra = where; eṣhaḥ = this; vānaraḥ = monkey (is); upekṣhataḥ = if he is neglected; kuryādeva hi = he will indeed do; kṣhayam = the destruction; naḥ rākṣhasām = of our demons.

He spoke the following words tohis charioteer: "Take the chariot to the place where that monkey is standing. If we neglect him, he will surely destroy our demons."

Sloka 27

ityuktaḥ sārathistena yayau yatra sa mārutiḥ |
vahan paramadurdharṣaṃ sthitamindrajitaṃ rathe || 6-86-27

27. sārathiḥ = the charioteer; iti = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; tena = by him; yayau = went; vahan = carrying; parama durdharṣham = that unusually unconquerable; indrajitam = Indrajit; sthitam rathe = sitting in the chariot; yatra = to the place where; mārutiḥ = ḥanuma (was there).

Hearing the words of that Indrajit, the charioteer went, carrying that very unconquerable Indrajit sitting in the chariot, to the place where Hanuma was there.

Sloka 28

so.abhyupetya śarānkhaḍgānpaṭṭasāsiparaśvadhān |
abhyavarṣata durdharṣaḥ kapimūrdhni sa rākṣasaḥ || 6-86-28

28. abhyupetya = coming close (to ḥanuma); saḥ rākṣhasaḥ = that demon; durdharṣhaḥ = who was unconquerable; abhyavarṣhata = hurled; sharān = arrows; khaDgān = swords; pathishāsi parashvadhān = sharp-edged spears and axes; kapimūrdhāni = on the head of ḥanuma.

Coming close to Hanuma, that unconquerable Indrajit hurled swords, sharp-edged spears and axes on the head of Hanuma.

Sloka 29

tāni śastrāṇi ghorāṇi pratigṛhya sa mārutiḥ |
roṣeṇa mahatāviṣo vākyaṃ cedamuvāca ha || 6-86-29

29. pratigṛihya = receiving; tāni ghorāṇi = shastrāni = those terrible weapons; saḥ mārutiḥ = that ḥanuma; uvācha = spoke; idam vākyam = the following words; mahatā roṣheṇa āviṣhṭo = wrapped with a great rage.

Receiving those terrible weapons, that Hanuma spoke, seized as he was, with great rage, the following words:

Sloka 30

yudhyasva yadi śūro.asi rāvaṇātmaja durmate |
vāyuputraṃ samāsādya na jīvanpratiyāsyasi || 6-86-30

30. durmate rāvaṇātmaja = O evil-minded Indrajit!; asi yadi shūraḥ = if you are valiant; yudhyasva = you fight (with me); samāsādya = to ḥanuma; na pratiyāsyasi = you will not return; jīvan = alive.

"O evil-minded Indrajit! If you are valiant, fight with me. Coming nearer to Hanuma, you will not return alive."

Sloka 31

bāhubhyāṃ samprayudhyasva yadi me dvandvamāhave |
vegaṃ sahasva durbuddhe tatastvaṃ rakṣasāṃ varaḥ || 6-86-31

31. durbuddhe = O foolish fellow!; dvandvam yadi = If you enter into a duel; me = with me; samprayudhyasva = fight; bāhubhyām = with arms; sahasva = withstand; vegam = my force; āhave = in the battle-field; tataḥ = then; tvam = you; varaḥ = will be considered as the best; rakṣhasām = among the demons.

"O foolish fellow! If you enter into a duel with me, fight with your arms. Withstand my force in the battle-field. Then you will be considered as the best among the demons."

Sloka 32

hanūmantaṃ jighāṃsantaṃ samudyataśarāsanam |
rāvaṇātmajamācaṣṭe lakṣmaṇāya vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-86-32

32. vibhiṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; āchaṣhṭe = told; lakṣhmaṇāya = lakshmana (in the following words); samudyata sharāsanam = while with bow upraised; rāvaṇātmajam hanūmantam jighāmsantam = Indrajit was seeking to kill ḥanuma.

Vibhishana told Lakshmana in the following words that while with bow upraised, Indrajit sought to kill Hanuma.

Sloka 33

yaḥ sa vāsavanirjetā rāvaṇasyātmasambhavaḥ |
sa eṣa rathamāsthāya hanūmantaṃ jighāṃsati || 6-86-33

33. āsthāya = having seated; ratham = in a chariot; saḥ = he; yaḥ = who; rāvasya sambhavah = was born to Ravana (Indrajit); vāsavanirjetā = who conquered Indra; jighāmsati = is seeking to kill; hanūmantam = ḥanuma.

"Having seated in a chariot, Indrajit, who conquered Indra, is seeking to kill Hanuma."

Sloka 34

tamapratimasaṃsthānaiḥ śaraiḥ śatruvidāraṇaiḥ |
jīvitāntakarairghoraiḥ saumitre rāvaṇiṃ jahi || 6-86-34

34. saumitra = O Lakshmana!; jahi = kill; tam = that; rāvaṇim = Indrajit; ghoraiḥ sharaiḥ = with terrific arrows; apratima samsthānaiḥ = in incomparable workmanship; shatrunivāraṇaiḥ = which ward off enemies; jīvitānta karaiḥ = and putting an end to their lives.

"O Lakshmana! Kill that Indrajit with your terrific arrows of incomparable workmanship, which ward off the enemies and putting an end to their lives."

Sloka 35

ityevamuktastu tadā mahātmā |
vibhīṣaṇenārivibhīṣaṇena |
dadarśa taṃ parvatasaṃnikāśaṃ |
rathasthitaṃ bhīmabalaṃ durāsadam || 6-86-35

35. ityevam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken to; tadā = then; vibhīṣhaṇena = by Vibhishana; arivibhīṣhaṇena = who was fearful to the adversaries; mahātmā = the great souled Lakshmana; dadarsha = saw; tam = Indrajit; parvata samnikāsham = who closely resembled a mountain; rathasthitam = seated in a chariot; bhīma balam = of terrific strength; durāsadam = and who was difficult to approach.

Hearing the words of Vibhishana, who was fearful to his adversaries, the great-souled Lakshmana saw Indrajit, who closely resembled a mountain, seated in a chariot, of terrific strength and who was difficult to approach.
