Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 9
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter further describes the inner buildings of Ravana's house. Hanuma passess through a hall filled with Ravana's wives.


Sloka 1

tasya ālaya variṣṭhasya madhye vipulam āyatam |
dadarśa bhavana śreṣṭham hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ || 5-9-1

1. hanumān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of Vayu; dadarsha = saw; bhavanashreshhṭam = a best building; āyatam = which was a tall one; vipulam = and a wide one; madhye = in the middle; tasyālayavarishhṭasya = of that best group of houses.

Hanuma, the son of Vayu, saw a best tall building and a wide one in the middle of that best group of houses.

Sloka 2

ardha yojana vistīrṇam āyatam yojanam hi tat |
bhavanam rākṣasa indrasya bahu prāsāda samkulam || 5-9-2

2. tat = that; bhavanam = building; rākshasendrasya = of Ravana; bahuprāsādashamkulam = filled with many edifices; ardhayojanavisthīrṇam = (was) half a Yojana long; yojanam āyatam = and one Yojana tall.

That building of Ravana filled with many edifices was half a Yojana long and one Yojana tall.

Sloka 3

mārgamāṇaḥ tu vaidehīm sītām āyata locanām |
sarvataḥ paricakrāma hanūmān ari sūdanaḥ || 5-9-3

3. hanumān = ḥanuma; arisūdanaḥ = the destructor of enemies; parichakrāma = moved around; sarvataḥ = in all directions; mārgamāṇaḥ tu = searching; sītām = ṣeetha; āyatalochanām = the wide eyed; viadehīm = the daughter of King of Videha.

Hanuma, the destructor of enemies, moved around in all directions searching for Seetha the wide eyed daughter of King of Videha

Sloka 4

uttamam rākṣasāvāsam hanumānavalokayan |
āsasādātha lakṣmīvān rākṣasendraniveśanam || 5-9-4
catur viṣāṇair dviradaiḥ triviṣāṇaiḥ tathaiva ca |
parikṣiptam asambādham rakṣyamāṇam udāyudhaiḥ || 5-9-5

4;5. atha = thereafter; hanumān = ḥanuma; lakshmīvān = the glorious one; āsasāda = neared; uttamam rākshasāvāsam = the best residence of Rakshas; rākshasendraniveshanam = (and) the house of Ravana; parikshiptam = containing; chaturvishhāṇaiḥ = ( elephants) with four tusks; tathaivacha = and also; trivishhāṇaiḥ = those with three tusks; dviradaiḥ = two tusks; asambādham = not crowded; rakshyamāṇam = protected; udāyudhaiḥ = by those bearing raised weapons; avalokayan = observing (it).

Thereafter, Hanuma the glorious one neared and observed the best residence of Rakshasas and the house of Ravana, containing elephants with four tusks and also those with three tusks, those with two tusks and still not crowded. It was protected by soldiers bearing raised weapons.

Sloka 6

rākṣasībhiḥ ca patnībhī rāvaṇasya niveśanam |
āhRtābhiḥ ca vikramya rāja kanyābhir āvRtam || 5-9-6
tan nakra makara ākīrṇam timimgila jhaṣa ākulam |
vāyu vega samādhūtam pannagair iva sāgaram || 5-9-7

6;7. tat niveshanam = that building; āvṛitam = surrounded; rāvaṇasyapatnībhiḥ = by the wives of Ravana; rākshasībhiḥ = by demonesses; rājakanyābhiḥ = princesses; āhṛitābhiḥ = brought; vikramya = by strength; sāgaram iva = (was) like an ocean; nakramakarākīrṇam = by crocodiles; big fishes; timiṇ^gilajhashhākulam = filled with sharks and other fishes; vāyuveghasamādhūtam = moved by the speed of wind; pannagaiḥ = and together with serpants.

That building surrounded by the wives of Ravana, by demonesses, and princesses brought by strength, was like an ocean filled with crocodiles, big fishes, sharks and other fishes, moved by the speed of wind, and together with serpants.

Sloka 8

yā hi vaiśvaraṇe lakṣmīr yā ca indre hari vāhane |
sā rāvaṇa gRhe sarvā nityam eva anapāyinī || 5-9-8

8. yā lakshmīḥ = whatever wealth; vaishravaṇe = is at Kubera; yā lakshmī = whatever riches; indre cha = at Indra; harivāhane = with green horses; sā sarvā = all that affluence; rāvaṇagṛihe = was at Ravana's house; nityameva = always; ānapāyinī = without reduction.

Whatever wealth is at Kubera, whatever riches at Indra with green horses, all that affluence was at Ravana's house. That wealth was always without reduction.

Sloka 9

yā ca rājñaḥ kuberasya yamasya varuṇasya ca |
tādRśī tad viśiṣṭā vā Rddhī rakṣo gRheṣv iha || 5-9-9

9. yā = whatever fortune; kuberasya = of Kubera; rājJṇaḥ = the King of Yakshās; yamasya = of Yama; varuṇasya cha = and of Varuna; tādrushī = the same fortune; vā tadvishishhṭā = or even greater; buddiḥ = treasure; iha rākshogṛiheshhu = was at this house of Ravana.

Whatever was the fortune of Kubera the King of Yakshas, of Yama and of Varuna, the same fortune or even greater treasure was at this house of Ravana.

Sloka 10

tasya harmyasya madhyastham veśma ca anyat sunirmitam |
bahuniryūha samkīrṇam dadarśa pavana ātmajaḥ || 5-9-10

10. pavanātmajaḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; anyatveshma = another house; madhyastham = in the middle; tasya harmyasya = of that house; sunirmitam = a well build one; bahuniryūhssamkīrṇam = consisting of many elephants in rut.

Hanuma saw another house in the middle of that house, a well built one consisting of many elephants in rut.

Sloka 11

brahmaṇo arthe kRtam divyam divi yad viśva karmaṇā |
vimānam puṣpakam nāma sarva ratna vibhūṣitam ||5-9-11
pareṇa tapasā lebhe yat kuberaḥ pitāmahāt |
kuberam ojasā jitvā lebhe tad rākṣasa īśvaraḥ || 5-9-12

11. yat = whatever; divyam vimānam = wonderful aeroplane; pushhpakam nāma = by the name of Pushpaka; sarvaratnavibhūshhitam = decorated with all kinds of precious stones; kṛitam = made; visvakarmaṇa = by Visvakarma; divi = in heaven; brahmaṇaḥ arthe = for lord Brahma; yat = whatever plane; kuberaḥ = Kubera; lebhe = obtained; pareṇa tapasā = by great austerity; pitāmahāt = from Lord Brahma; rākshasheshvaraḥ = Ravana; lebhe = obtained; tat = that plane; ojasā = by prowess; jitvā = defeating; kubera = Kubera.

Whatever wonderful aeroplane by the name of Pushpaka, decorated with all kinds of precious stones, made by Visvakarma in heaven for lord Brahma, whatever plane Kubera obtained by great austerity from Lord Brahma, Ravana obtained that plane by prowess defeating Kubera.

Sloka 13

īhā mRga samāyuktaiḥ kārya svara hiraṇmayaiḥ |
sukRtair ācitam stambhaiḥ pradīptam iva ca śriyā || 5-9-13
meru mandara samkāśair ullikhadbhir iva ambaram |
kūṭa agāraiḥ śubha ākāraiḥ sarvataḥ samalamkRtam || 5-9-14

13;14. āchitam = supported; stambhiaḥ = by pillars; īhāmṛigasamāyuktaiḥ = decorated with images of wolves; kārtasvarahiraṇmayaiḥ = with silver and golden hue; sukṛitaiḥ = (and that were) well made; pradīptamiva = radiant; shriyā = with splendour; sarvataḥ samalankṛitam = decorated all around; kūṭāgāraiḥ = with rooms; merumandarasamkāshaiḥ = equalling ṃount ṃeru and ṃount ṃandara; ambaram vullikhadbhiriva = as though touching the sky; subhākāraiḥ = with an auspicious shape.

Supported by pillars decorated with images of wolves, with silver and golden hue and which were well made, radiant with splendour, decorated all around with rooms equalling ṃount ṃeru and ṃount ṃandara, as though touching the sky with an auspicious shape.

Sloka 15

jvalana arka pratīkāśam sukRtam viśva karmaṇā |
hema sopāna samyuktam cāru pravara vedikam || 5-9-15

15. sukṛitam = well made; vishvakarmaṇa = by Visvakarma; jvalanārkapratīkasham = resembling Fire and ṣun(in splendour); hemasopānasamyuktam = with stairways made of gold; chārupravaravedikam = with beautiful and best platforms.

Well made by Visvakarma and resembling Fire and Sun in splendour, with stairways made of gold and with beautiful and best platforms;

Sloka 16

jāla vāta ayanair yuktam kāncanaiḥ sthāṭikair api |
indra nīla mahā nīla maṇi pravaravedikam || 5-9-16

16. jālavātānaiḥ yuktam = with windows and ventilation holes; kāJṇchanaiḥ = made of gold; spaṭikairapi = and also made of crystal; indranīlamahānīlamaṇipravaravedikam = with platforms made of sapphires and emeralds.

With windows and ventilation holes made of gold and also made of crystal, with platforms made of sapphires and emeralds;

Sloka 17

vidrumeṇa vicitreṇa maṇibhiśca mahādhanaiḥ |
vistulābhiśca muktābhistalenābhivirājitam || 5-9-17

17. abhivirājitam = shone; talena = by floors; vichitreṇa vidrumeṇa = embellished with corals of strange hue; maṇibhishcha = and with precious stones; mahādhanaiḥ = of great value; muktābhiḥ = with pearls; nistulābhiḥ = without comparison.

Shone by floors embellished with corals of strange hue and with precious stones of great value, with pearls without comparison;

Sloka 18

candanena ca raktena tapanīyanibhena ca |
supuṇyagandhinā yuktamādityataruṇopamam || 5-9-18

18. raktena = reddish; tapanīyanibhenacha = and resembling gold; yuktam = together; chandanena = with sandal wood; supuṇyagandhinā = having a good smell; āditya taruṇopamam = (brilliant) like the ṣun at noon.

Reddish and resembling gold, together with sandal wood having a good smell and brilliant like the Sun at noon;

Sloka 19

kūṭāgārairvarākārairvividhaiḥ samalamkṛtam |
vimānam puṣpakam divyam āruroha mahā kapiḥ || 5-9-19

19. mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; āruroha = alighted; divyam pushhpakam vimānam = the best aeroplane called Pushpaka; varākāraiḥ = with an excellent form; samalankṛitam = decorated; kūtāgāraiḥ = by rows of upper floors

The great Hanuma alighted the best aeroplane called Pushpaka with an excellent form decorated by rows of upper floors.

Sloka 20

tatrasthaḥ sa tadā gandham pāna bhakṣya anna sambhavam |
divyam sammūrcitam jighran rūpavantam iva anilam || 5-9-20

20. saḥ = that ḥanuma; tadā = then; tatrasthaḥ = being there; jighram = smelled; divyamgandham = a wonderful sweet fragrance; anilamiva = like wind; rūpavantam = with an appearance; pānabhakshyānnsambhavam = created from drinks and foods including cooked rice; sammūrchhitam = diffused on all sides.

That Hanuma then being there, smelled a wonderful sweet fragrance like wind with an appearance, created from drinks and foods, including cooked rice diffused on all sides.

Sloka 21

sa gandhaḥ tam mahā sattvam bandhur bandhum iva uttamam |
ita ehi iti uvāca iva tatra yatra sa rāvaṇaḥ || 5-9-21

21. saḥ gandhaḥ = that sweet smell; uvācheva iti = was as though saying thus; tam = to that ḥanuma; itaḥ ehi = come here; bandhuriva = like a relative; uttamam bhandhum = to a best relative; tatra = there; yatra = wherever; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana(was).

That sweet smell was as though saying thus to that Hanuma : "Come here!", like a relative to a best relative pointing to there wherever that Ravana was.

Sloka 22

tataḥ tām prasthitaḥ śālām dadarśa mahatīm śubhām |
rāvaṇasya manaḥ kāntām kāntām iva vara striyam || 5-9-22

22. prasthitaḥ = ḥanuma started; tataḥ = thereafter; tataḥ = there(in that direction); dadarsha = and saw; shubhām = in auspicious; mahatīmshālām = great hall; manaḥ kāntām = close to heart; rāvanasya = of Ravana; kāntām varastriyamiva = like a delightfull best woman.

Hanuma started thereafter there (in that direction) and saw an auspicious great hall, close to heart of Ravana, like a delightful best woman.

Sloka 23

maṇi sopāna vikRtām hema jāla virājitām |
sphāṭikair āvRta talām danta antarita rūpikām || 5-9-23

23. (ṭhat hall was)maṇisopānavkṛitām = made of stairways embedded with diamonds; hemajālavibhūshhitām = decorated with golden windows; spatikaiḥ āvṛitatalām = with a floor embellished with crystal; dantāntaritarūpikām = with shapes made ivory in the middle.

That hall was made of stairways embedded with diamonds, decorated with golden windows, with a floor embellished with crystal, with shapes made from ivory in the middle.

Sloka 24

muktābhiḥ ca pravālaiḥ ca rūpya cāmī karair api |
vibhūṣitām maṇi stambhaiḥ subahu stambha bhūṣitām || 5-9-24

24. vibhūshhitām = well decorated; muktābhishcha = with pearls; pravāLaishca = with corals; rūpyachāmīkarairapi = and also with silver and gold; maṇistambhaiḥ = and with diamond studded pillars; shubhahusthambhabhūshhitām = and also decorated with many pillars.

Well decorated with pearls, corals and also with silver and gold and with diamond studded pillars and also decorated with many pillars.

Sloka 25

namrairṛjubhiratyuccaiḥ samantātsuvibhūṣitaiḥ |
stambhaiḥ pakṣair iva atyuccair divam samprasthitām iva || 5-9-25

25. sthambhaiḥ = by pillars; namraiḥ = slightly bent; ṛijubhiḥ = with no distortion; atyuchchaiḥ = which were very tall; suvibhūshhitaiḥ = and well decorated; samantāt = all over; samprasthitāmiva = (that hall) was like starting; divam = for sky; atyuchchaiḥ pakshaiḥ = with very high wings.

By pillars slightly bent with no distortion, which were very tall and well decorated all over, that hall was as though starting for sky with very high wings.

Sloka 26

mahatyā kuthaya āstrīṇam pRthivī lakṣaṇa ankayā |
pṛthivīm iva vistīrṇām sarāṣṭra gRha mālinīm || 5-9-26

26. āsthīrṇām = covered; mahatyā kuthayā = by a wonderful large carpet; pṛithivīlakshaṇāṇ^kayā = with all earth's characteristics (like rivers mountains; oceans; forests; painted over); vistīrṇām pṛithivīmiva = (that hall was)like the vast earth; sarāshhṭragṛihamālinīm = together with states and rows of houses.

Covered by a wonderful large carpet with all earth's characteristics like rivers, mountains, oceans, forests, painted over, that hall was like the vast earth together with states and rows of houses.

Sloka 27

nāditām matta vihagair divya gandha adhivāsitām |
para ardhya āstaraṇa upetām rakṣo adhipa niṣevitām || 5-9-27

27. nāditām = resounded; mattavihagaiḥ = by birds in heat; divyagandhādhivāsitām = made fragrant by excellent perfumes; parārthyāstaraṇopetām = hung with best tapestries; rakshodhipanishhevitām = inhabited by the King of rakshasas.

It was resounded by birds in heat, made fragrant by excellent perfumes, hung with best tapestries and inhabited by the king of rakshasas

Sloka 28

dhūmrām agaru dhūpena vimalām haṃsa pāṇḍurām |
citrām puṣpa upahāreṇa kalmāṣīm iva suprabhām || 5-9-28

28. dhūmrām = smokey; agarudhūpena = by the fumes of agallocum; vimalām = a pure one; hamsapāṇDurām = white like a swan; chitrām = wonderful; pushhpopahāreṇa = by the decoration of flowers; kalmāshhīmiva = like divine cow; suprabhām = with a great radiance.

It was made smokey by the fumes of agallocum. It was a pure one white like a swan, wonderful by the decoration of flowers, like divine cow with a great radiance.

Sloka 29

manaḥ samhlāda jananīm varṇasya api prasādinīm |
tām śoka nāśinīm divyām śriyaḥ samjananīm iva || 5-9-29

29. tām divyām = that excellent hall; manaḥsamhlādajananīm = causing happiness to heart; prasādinīm = causing; varnasyāpi = a fair complexion; sokanāshanīm = destroying sorrow; samjananīm iva = and was as though producing; sriyaḥ = prosperity.

That excellent hall was causing happiness to heart, causing a fair complexion, destroying sorrow and was as though producing prosperity.

Sloka 30

indriyāṇi indriya arthaiḥ tu panca pancabhir uttamaiḥ |
tarpayām āsa mātā iva tadā rāvaṇa pālitā || 5-9-30

30. māteva = like a mother; tadā = then; rāvaṇapālitā = that hall governed by Ravana; tarpayāmāsa = caused satisfaction; paJṇchadriyāṇī = to all five sense organs; uttamaiḥ paJṇchabhiḥ indriyārthaiḥ = with the excellent five objects of the senses.

Like a mother, then that hall governed by Ravana caused satisfaction to all five sensory organs with all the excellent five objects of the senses.

Sloka 31

svargo ayam deva loko ayam indrasya iyam purī bhavet |
siddhir vā iyam parā hi syād iti amanyata mārutiḥ || 5-9-31

31. mārutiḥ = ḥanuma; amanyatā = thought; iti = thus; iyam svargaḥ = this is heaven; iyam devalokaḥ = this is the aboard of Devas; iyam = this; bhavet = is; indrasya purī = a city of Indra; iyam = this; syādvā = might be; parāsiddhiḥ = the result of great austerity.

Hanuma thought thus : "This is heaven! This is indeed the abode of Devas! This is a city of Indra. This might be the result of a great austerity."

Sloka 32

pradhyāyata iva apaśyat pradīpāmḥ tatra kāncanān (ḥiatus!)|
dhūrtān iva mahā dhūrtair devanena parājitān || 5-9-32

32. dhūrtāniva = like gamblers; parājitān = defeated; mahādhūraiḥ = by greater gamblers; devanena = in gambling; apashyat = ḥanuma saw; kāJṇchanān pradīpān = golden hued lamps; pradhyāta iva = being still as though in thought.

Like gamblers defeated by greater gamblers in gambling, Hanuma saw golden hued lamps being still, as though in thought.

Sloka 33

dīpānām ca prakāśena tejasā rāvaṇasya ca |
arcirbhir bhūṣaṇānām ca pradīptā iti abhyamanyata || 5-9-33

33. amanyata = ḥanuma thought; iti = thus; pradīpta = (this building) shines; dīpānāmprakāshena = by the light of lamps; rāvaṇasya tejasā cha = and the brilliance of Ravana; bhushhṇānām archirbhiḥ cha = and also due to the radiance of jewellery.

Hanuma thought thus : "This building shines by the light of lamps and the brilliance of Ravana and also due to the radiance of jewellery."

Sloka 34

tato apaśyat kuthā āsīnam nānā varṇa ambara srajam |
sahasram vara nārīṇām nānā veṣa vibhūṣitam || 5-9-34

34. tataḥ = thereafter; apasyat = ḥanuma saw; sahasram = a thousand; varanārīṇām = of best women; kuthāsīnam = sitting on the rug; nānāvarṇāmbarasrajam = with clothing of various colours; nānāveshavibhūshhitam = and decorated in a variety of ways.

Thereafter Hanuma saw a thousand of best women sitting on the rug with clothing of various colours and decorated in a variety of ways.

Sloka 35

parivRtte ardha rātre tu pāna nidrā vaśam gatam |
krīḍitvā uparatam rātrau suṣvāpa balavat tadā || 5-9-35

35. tadā = then; ardharātreparivṛitte = at the turn of midnight; shushhvāpa = (that women folk)slept; balavat = deeply; uparatam = resting; rātraukrīDitvā = after playing during night; pānanidrāvasham gatam = getting the sleep caused by liquor.

Then at the turn of midnight, those women slept deeply resting after playing during night, with a deep sleep caused by liquor.

Sloka 36

tat prasuptam viruruce nihśabda antara bhūṣaṇam |
nihśabda haṃsa bhramaram yathā padma vanam mahat || 5-9-36

36. tat prasuptam = that sleeping group of women; nishabdāntarabhūshhaṇam = adorned with jewels which were not making sound; viruruche = shone; mahat padmavanam yathā = like a great park of lotuses; niḥshebdahamsabramaram = with calm swans and bees.

That sleeping group of women, adorned with jewels which were not making sound, shone like a great park of lotuses with calm swans and bees.

Sloka 37

tāsām samvRta dantāni mīlita akṣāṇi mārutiḥ |
apaśyat padma gandhīni vadanāni suyoṣitām || 5-9-37

37. mārutiḥ = ḥanuma; apasyat = saw; vadanāni = faces; tāsām shuyoshhitām = of those best women; samvṛitadantāni = with teeth covered (by lips); mīlitākshīṇi = with closed eyes; padmaghandhīni = and with lotus fragrance.

Hanuma saw faces of those best women, with teeth covered by lips with closed eyes and with lotus fragrance.

Sloka 38

prabuddhāni iva padmāni tāsām bhūtvā kṣapā kṣaye |
punaḥ samvRta patrāṇi rātrāv iva babhuḥ tadā || 5-9-38

38. tadā = at that time; tāsām = those faces; babhuḥ = shone; prabudhāniva padmāniva bhūtvā = became like lotuses in blossom; kshāpakshyaye = during day time; punaḥ = and again; samvṛitapatrāṇīva = were like lotuses with closed petals; rātrau = at night.

At that time, those faces shone which became like lotuses in blossom during day time and again were like lotuses with closed petals at night.

Sloka 39

imāni mukha padmāni niyatam matta ṣaṭpadāḥ |
ambujāni iva phullāni prārthayanti punaḥ punaḥ || 5-9-39

39. (ḥanuma thought that) mattashhaṭpadāḥ = drunk bees; niyatam = definitely; prātrhayantī = are desiring; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; imānī = these; mukhapadmāni = lotuses faces; pullāni ambujānīva = like lotuses in blossom.

Hanuma thought that drunk bees definitely are desiring again and again these lotus faces like lotuses in blossom.

Sloka 40

iti vā amanyata śrīmān upapattyā mahā kapiḥ |
mene hi guṇataḥ tāni samāni salila udbhavaiḥ || 5-9-40

40. shrīmān = the glorious; mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; amanyata = thought; itīva = thus; upapatya = by reason; mene hi = thought also that; tāni = those faces; samāni = equal salilodbhavaiḥ = lotuses; guṇataḥ = by their qualities.

The glorious great Hanuma thought as above by recourse to reason. He also thought thus "Those faces equal lotuses by their qualities such as beauty, fragrance, and others".

Sloka 41

sā tasya śuśubhe śālā tābhiḥ strībhir virājitā |
śāradī iva prasannā dyauḥ tārābhir abhiśobhitā || 5-9-41

41. sā shālā = that hall; tasya = of Ravana's; virājitā = being shone; tabhiḥstrībhi = by those women; shushubhe = glittered; prasannā dyauḥ iva = like a calm cloudless sky; shāradīva = in autumn; abhishobhitā = shone; tārābhiḥ = by stars.

That hall of Ravana's being shone by those women, glittered like a calm cloudless sky in autumn shone by stars.

Sloka 42

sa ca tābhiḥ parivRtaḥ śuśubhe rākṣasa adhipaḥ |
yathā hi uḍu patiḥ śrīmāmḥ tārābhir abhisamvRtaḥ || 5-9-42

42. saḥ rākshasādhipaḥ = that lord of Rakshasas; parivṛitaḥ = surrounded; tābhḥ = by those women; shushubhe = shone; hyuDupathiḥ yathā = like the moon; srīmān = with radiance; abhisamvṛitaḥ = surrounded; tārābhiḥ = by stars.

That lord of Rakshasas surrounded by those women shone like the radiant moon surrounded by stars.

Sloka 43

yāḥ cyavante ambarāt tārāḥ puṇya śeṣa samāvRtāḥ |
imāḥ tāḥ samgatāḥ kRtsnā iti mene hariḥ tadā || 5-9-43

43. tadā = then; hariḥ = ḥanuma; mene = thought; iti = thus; yāḥ = whatever; tārāḥ = meteors; puṇyasheshhasamāvṛitāḥ = together with residue of piety; chyavante = fall down; ambarāt = from the sky; tāḥ kṛistnāḥ = all those meteors; imāḥ sangatāḥ = had been together as these women.

Then Hanuma thought thus : "Whatever meteors together with residue of piety fall down from the sky, all those meteors had been together as these women".

Sloka 44

tārāṇām iva suvyaktam mahatīnām śubha arciṣām |
prabhā varṇa prasādāḥ ca virejuḥ tatra yoṣitām || 5-9-44

44. tatra = there; prabhāvarṇaprasādāshcha = the luster; complexion and grace; yoshhitām = of those women; suvyaktam = clearly; mahatīnām tārāṇām iva = resembled great stars; virejuhu = which emitted; shubhārchishhām = an auspicious radiance.

There the luster, complexion and grace of those women clearly resembled great stars which emitted an auspicious radience.

Sloka 45

vyāvRtta guru pīna srak prakīrṇa vara bhūṣaṇāḥ |
pāna vyāyāma kāleṣu nidrā apahRta cetasaḥ || 5-9-45

45. vyāvṛittagurupīnasrakprakīrṇavarabhūshhaṇāḥ = those women with dishevelled large thick garlands and scattered excellent jewels; pānavyāyamakāleshhu = during the time of drinking dancing etc.; nidrāpahṛitachetasaḥ = were with a consciousness stolen by sleep.

Those women with dishevelled large thick garlands and scattered excellent jewels during the time of drinking, dancing etc., were with consciousness stolen by sleep.

Sloka 46

vyāvRtta tilakāḥ kāścit kāścid udbhrānta nūpurāḥ |
pārśve galita hārāḥ ca kāścit parama yoṣitaḥ || 5-9-46

46. kāshchitparamayoshhitaḥ = best women; vyāvṛittatilakāḥ = became with disturbed marks on forehead; kāshchitkāshchidudbhrāntanūpurāḥ = became with displaced anklets; kāshchitkāgaLitahārāshcha = became with necklaces fallen off; pārshve = to side.

Best women with disturbed sacred marks (tilaka) on forehead became with displaced anklets, became with necklaces fallen off to side.

Sloka 47

mukhā hāra vRtāḥ ca anyāḥ kāścit prasrasta vāsasaḥ |
vyāviddha raśanā dāmāḥ kiśorya iva vāhitāḥ || 5-9-47

47. anyāḥ = some other women; muktāhārāvṛitāḥ = became tied with pearl necklaces; kāshchit = some others; visrastavāsasaḥ = became with tropped raiment; vyāviddharashanādāmāḥ = (some others) became with tied-up waist ornaments; kishoryaḥ iva = (and) resembled young female horses; vāhitāḥ = who had walked long.

Some other women became tied with pearl necklaces, some others became with tied-up waist ornaments (and) resembled young female horses who had walked long.

Sloka 48

sukuṇḍala dharāḥ ca anyā viccinna mRdita srajaḥ |
gaja indra mRditāḥ phullā latā iva mahā vane || 5-9-48

48. anyāḥ = some others; sukuṇDala dharāḥ = wearing nice earrings; vicchinna mṛidita srajaḥ = with flower garlands that were torn and disturbed; latāḥ iva = resembled creepers; gajendra mṛiditāḥ iva = tread upon by an elephant; mahāvane = in a big forest.

Some others wearing nice earrings with flower garlands that were torn and disturbed, resembled creepers, tread upon by an elephant in a big forest.

Sloka 49

candra aṃśu kiraṇa ābhāḥ ca hārāḥ kāsāṃcid utkaṭāḥ |
haṃsā iva babhuḥ suptāḥ stana madhyeṣu yoṣitām || 5-9-49

49. utkaṭāḥ = big; hārāḥ = (pearl) necklaces; chandrāṃshhu kiraṇābhāshcha = with the radiance of moon rays; stana madhyeshhu = in the middle of breasts; kāsāṃchit yoshhitām = of some other women; babhuḥ = shone; suptāḥ haṃsāḥ iva = like sleeping swans.

Big pearl necklaces with the radiance of moon-rays in the middle of breasts of some other women shone like sleeping swans.

Sloka 50

aparāsām ca vaidūryāḥ kādambā iva pakṣiṇaḥ |
hema sūtrāṇi ca anyāsām cakra vākā iva abhavan || 5-9-50

50. aparāsāṃ cha = And for some other women; vaiDūryāḥ = necklaces made with cat's eye gems; kādaṃbāḥ pakshiṇaḥ iva = resembled birds called Kadambas; anyāsām = and for some others; hemasūtrāṇi = golden chains; abhavan chakravākāḥ iva = were like chakravaka birds.

And of some other women, necklaces made with cat's eye gems resembled birds called Kadambas and for some others golden chains were like chakravaka birds.

Sloka 51

haṃsa kāraṇḍava ākīrṇāḥ cakra vāka upaśobhitāḥ |
āpagā iva tā rejur jaghanaiḥ pulinair iva || 5-9-51

51. tāḥ = those women; jaghanaiḥ = with buttocks; pulinairiva = resembling sand dunes; rejuḥ = shone; āpagā iva = like rivers; hamsa kāranDavapakshrṇāḥ = filled with swans and bird called Karandas; chakravākopishobhitāḥ = and decorated by chakravaka birds.

Those women with buttocks resembling sand dunes, shone like rivers filled with swans and birds called Karandas and decorated by chakravaka birds.

Sloka 52

kinkiṇī jāla samkāśāḥ tā hema vipula ambujāḥ |
bhāva grāhā yaśaḥ tīrāḥ suptā nadya iva ābabhuḥ || 5-9-52

52. suptāḥ = those sleeping women; ābabhuḥ = shone; nadyaḥ iva = like rivers; kiṇ^kiṇījālashaṃkoshāḥ = with their smiles as flower buds; haimavipulāṃbujāḥ = with golden jewellery as big lotuses; bhāva grāhāḥ = with their behaviours as crocodiles; yashastīrāḥ = with fame as banks.

Those sleeping women shone like rivers with their smiles as flower buds, with golden jewellery as big lotuses, with their behaviours as crocodiles, with fame as banks.

Sloka 53

mṛduṣv angeṣu kāsāṃcit kuca agreṣu ca saṃsthitāḥ |
babhūvur bhūṣaṇāni iva śubhā bhūṣaṇa rājayaḥ || 5-9-53

53. shubhāḥ = auspicious; bhūshhaṇa rājayaḥ = lines of decoration; saṃstitāḥ = which were; mṛidushhu aṇgeshhu = on smooth parts; kāsāṃchit = of some other women; kuchāgreshhu cha = and on nipples; babhūvuḥ = were; bhūshhaṇānīva = were like jewellery.

Auspicious lines of decoration which were on smooth parts of some other women and on nipples, were like jewellery.

Sloka 54

aṃśu kāntāḥ ca kāsāṃcin mukha māruta kampitāḥ |
upari upari vaktrāṇām vyādhūyante punaḥ punaḥ || 5-9-54

54. amshukantāshcha = Edges of clothing (worn by them); mukha māruta kampitāḥ = disturbed by air from breathing; kāsāmchit = of some other women; vyādhūyante = were being fluttered; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; vaktrāṇām uparyupari = on their faces.

Edges of clothing (worn by them) disturbed by air from breathing of some other women, were being fluttered again and again on their faces.

Sloka 55

tāḥ pātākā iva uddhūtāḥ patnīnām rucira prabhāḥ |
nānā varṇa suvarṇānām vaktra mūleṣu rejire || 5-9-55

55. tāḥ = ṭhose edges of clothes; nānā varṇa suvarṇānām = with various beautiful colours; rejire = shone; patnīnām vaktra mūleshhu = at the base of Ravana's wives' throats; ruchira prabhāḥ patākāḥ = like flags of beautiful shine; uddhūtāḥ = hoisted.

Those edges of clothes with various beautiful colours, shone at the base of Ravana's wives' throats like hoisted flags of beautiful shine.

Sloka 56

vavalguḥ ca atra kāsāṃcit kuṇḍalāni śubha arciṣām |
mukha māruta saṃsargān mandam mandam suyoṣitām || 5-9-56

56. atra = ḥere; kuṇDalāni = ear-rings; kāsāmchit suyoshhitām = of some beautiful women; shubhārchishhām = with a good glory; vavalgushcha = also moved; mandam mandam = lightly; mukha māruta saṃsargāt = due to the air from breathing (of those women).

Here ear-rings of some beautiful women with a good glory, also moved lightly due to the air from breathing of those women.

Sloka 57

śarkara āsava gandhaḥ sa prakRtyā surabhiḥ sukhaḥ |
tāsām vadana nihśvāsaḥ siṣeve rāvaṇam tadā || 5-9-57

57. tadā = then; sukhaḥ = comforting; vadananiḥshvāsaḥ = breath from the faces; tāsām = of those women; prakṛityā = by nature; surabhiḥ = fragrant; sharkarāsava gandhaishcha = with the smell of liquor made of ṣarkara; sishheve = served; rāvaṇam = Ravana.

Then comforting breath from the faces of those women, by nature fragrant with the smell of liquor made of Sarkara, served Ravana.

Sloka 58

rāvaṇa ānana śankāḥ ca kāścid rāvaṇa yoṣitaḥ |
mukhāni sma sapatnīnām upājighran punaḥ punaḥ || 5-9-58

58. kāshchit = ṣome; rāvaṇa yoshhitaḥ = women of Ravana; upājighran = smelled; sapatnīnām = co-wives'; mukhāni = faces; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; rāvaṇānana shaṇkāshcha = imagining them to be face of Ravana.

Some women of Ravana smelled co-wives' faces again and again imagining them to be face of Ravana.

Sloka 59

atyartham sakta manaso rāvaṇe tā vara striyaḥ |
asvatantrāḥ sapatnīnām priyam eva ācaramḥ tadā || 5-9-59

59. tāḥ = those; varastriyaḥ = best women; atyartham saktamanasaḥ = with greatly devoted mind; rāvaṇe = in Ravana; tadā = then; asvatantrāḥ = not being on own will; ācharan = brought; priyameva = delight only; sapatnīnām = to their co-wives.

Those best women with greatly devoted mind in Ravana, then not being on own will, brought delight only to their co-wives.

Sloka 60

bāhūn upanidhāya anyāḥ pārihārya vibhūṣitāḥ |
aṃśukāni ca ramyāṇi pramadāḥ tatra śiśyire || 5-9-60

60. anyāḥ = some other; pramadāḥ = women; shishyire = slept; tatra = there; upanidhāyā = making as pillows; bāhūn = their arms; pārihārya vibhūshhitān = decorated with bracelets; ramyāṇi amshukāṇi = and beautiful clothes.

Some other women slept there, making as pillows their arms decorated with bracelets and beautiful clothes.

Sloka 61

anyā vakṣasi ca anyasyāḥ tasyāḥ kācit punar bhujam |
aparā tv ankam anyasyāḥ tasyāḥ ca api aparā bhujau || 5-9-61

61. anyā = another woman; anyasyāḥ vakshasī = (slept) on another's bosom; kāchitpunaḥ = again another women; tasyāḥ bhujam = (slept) on her shoulder; aparātu = another woman; anyasyāḥ aṇ^kam = (slept) on another woman's thigh; aparā = another; tasyāḥ kuchau = (slept) on her breasts.

Another woman slept on another's bosom, again another woman slept on her shoulder, another woman slept on another woman's thigh and another slept on her breasts.

Sloka 62

ūru pārśva kaṭī pRṣṭham anyonyasya samāśritāḥ |
paraspara niviṣṭa angyo mada sneha vaśa anugāḥ || 5-9-62

62. madasneḥ = overcome with heat and friendship; samāshritāḥ = resorting; ūrupārshva kaṭīpṛishhṭam = to thighs; sides; waist and back; anyonyasya = of one another; parasparanivishhṭāṇ^gyaḥ = with mutual limbs placed on one another.

Overcome with heat and friendship, they resorted to thighs, sides, waist and back of one another, with mutual limbs placed on one another.

Sloka 63

anyonyasya anga saṃsparśāt prīyamāṇāḥ sumadhyamāḥ |
ekī kRta bhujāḥ sarvāḥ suṣupuḥ tatra yoṣitaḥ
anyonya bhuja sūtreṇa strī mālā grathitā hi sā |
mālā iva grathitā sūtre śuśubhe matta ṣaṭpadā || 5-9-63

63. sā = that; strīmālā = garland of women; grathitā = tied; anyonyabhujasūtreṇa = with the rope of each other's shoulders; shushubhe = shone; māleva = like a flower garland; mattashhṭpatā = with dragon flies on it; ghrathitā = tied; sūtre = in thread.

That garland of women tied with the rope of each other's shoulders shone like a flower garland with dragon flies on it tied in thread.

Sloka 64

latānām mādhave māsi phullānām vāyu sevanāt |
anyonya mālā grathitam saṃsakta kusuma uccayam || 5-9-64
vyativeṣṭita suskantham anyonya bhramara ākulam |
āsīd vanam iva uddhūtam strī vanam rāvaṇasya tat || 5-9-65

64;65. tat strīvanam = that group of women; rāvanasya = of Ravana; anyonyamālāgrathitam = tied by each other as garland; samsaktkusumochchhayam = with mixed group of flowers; vyativeshhṭitasuskhandham = with beautiful shoulders entwined; anyonyabhramalākulam = promiscuously hair; āsīt = was; uddhutam vanamiva = like an exalted garden; anyonyamālāgrathitam = with a flower garland tied together; latānām = of creepers; pullānām = in bloom; vāyusamsevanāt = due to the caress of the lord of wind; madhavemāsi = in the vernal in the month of Vaisākha; vyativeshhṭitasuskhandam = with entwined beautiful trees

That group of women of Ravana, tied by each other as garland with mixed group of flowers, with beautiful shoulders entwined promiscuously, hair like an exalted garden with a flower garland tied together of creepers in bloom due to the caress of the lord of wind in the vernal in the month of Vaisakha, with entwined beautiful trees.

Sloka 66

uciteṣv api suvyaktam na tāsām yoṣitām tadā |
vivekaḥ śakya ādhātum bhūṣaṇa anga ambara srajām || 5-9-66

66. tadā = then; na shekyaḥ = it was not possible; ādhātum = to make; suvyaktam = clear; vivekaḥ = the knowledge; bhūshhṇ^gāmbarasrajam = of jewellery body parts and clothes; tāsāmyoshhitām = of those women; vuchiteshhvapi = even for those used (to them).

Then it was not possible to make clear the knowledge of jewellery, body parts and clothes of those women even for those used to them.

Sloka 67

rāvaṇe sukha samviṣṭe tāḥ striyo vividha prabhāḥ |
jvalantaḥ kāncanā dīpāḥ prekṣanta animiṣā iva || 5-9-67

67. rāvaṇe shukhasamvishhṭe = while Ravana slept comfortably; kāJṇchanāḥ dīpāḥ = golden lamps; jvalantaḥ = shining brilliant; praikshanta iva = were as though seeing; animishhāḥ = without closing eyes; tāḥ striyaḥ = those women; vividhaprabhāḥ = of various glows.

While Ravana slept comfortably, golden lamps shining brilliant, were as though seeing without closing eyes, those women of various glows.

Sloka 68

rāja Rṣi pitR daityānām gandharvāṇām ca yoṣitaḥ |
rakṣasām ca abhavan kanyāḥ tasya kāma vaśam gatāḥ || 5-9-68

68. yoshhitaḥ = women; rājarshhipitrudaityānām = of royal sages; brahmanas and demons; gandharvaṇām = and of Gandharvas; rākshasānām = of Rakshasas; yāḥ kanyāḥ = all those unmarried girls; kāmavasham gatāḥ = surrendered from lust; tasya = to Ravana.

Women of royal sages, brahmanas and demons and of Gandharvas, of Rakshasas - all those unmarried girls surrendered from lust to Ravana.

Sloka 69

yuddhakāmena tāḥ sarvā rāvaṇena hṛtāḥ striyaḥ |
samadā madanenaiva mohitāḥ kāścidāgatāḥ || 5-9-69

69. sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ striyaḥ = those women; hṛitāḥ = have been stolen; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; yuddhakāmena = with a desire for war; kāshchit = some; samadā = together with heat (of youth); āgatāḥ = obtained (Ravana); madanenaivamohitāḥ iva = being desired by god of love.

All those women had been stolen by Ravana with a desire for war, some together with heat of youth obtained Ravana being desired by god of love.

Sloka 70

na tatra kācit pramadā prasahya |
vīrya upapannena guṇena labdhā |
na ca anya kāmā api na ca anya pūrvā |
vinā vara arhām janaka ātmajām tu || 5-9-70

70. tatra = there; kāchitpramada = even one woman; na labhdhā = has not been obtained; prasahya = forcefully; vīryopapannena = by the strong Ravana; tām janakātmajām vinā = except for that daughter of Janaka ṣeetha; varārhām = the best among women; guṇena = (every other woman has been obtained) by character; na cha = and there is no(woman); anyakāmāpi = who had desire in another(man); na anyapūrvācha = and also there was no one with another (lover).

There, even one woman had not been obtained forcefully by the strong Ravana, except for that daughter of Janaka, Seetha. Every other woman had been obtained by her character alone and there was no woman who had desire in another man, and there also was none with another lover.

Sloka 71

na ca akulīnā na ca hīna rūpā |
na adakṣiṇā na anupacāra yuktā |
bhāryā abhavat tasya na hīna sattvā |
na ca api kāntasya na kāmanīyā || 5-9-71

71. na cha abhavat = and there was no; tasya bhārya = wife of his; akulīnā = without a good lineage; nahīna rūpācha = and there was no one with less beauty; na adakshiṇā = no one without skill; na anupachāra yuktā = no one without service; na hīnastvā = none with low intellect; na = no one; kāntasya na kāmanīyā cha = without causing desire to lover.

And there was no wife of his without a good lineage, there was no one with less beauty, no one without skill, no one without service, none with low intellect, no one without causing desire to lover.

Sloka 72

babhūva buddhiḥ tu hari īśvarasya |
yadi īdRśī rāghava dharma patnī |
imā yathā rākṣasa rāja bhāryāḥ |
sujātam asya iti hi sādhu buddheḥ || 5-9-72

72. iti budhistu = following thought; babhūva = occurred; harīshvarasya = to ḥanuma; sādhubudhhe = with a pious mind; sujātam = it is good; asya = for this Ravana; rāghava dharmapatnīm = (if) the virtuous wife of Rama(had been left happy with her husband) idṛishī yatha = in the same such manner; imāḥ rakshasarāja bhāryāḥ = as these wives of the king of rakshasas.

Following thought occurred to Hanuma with a pious mind : "It would have been good for this Ravana if the virtuous wife of Rama had been left happy with her husband in the same such manner as these wives of the king of rakshasas."

Sloka 73

punaḥ ca so acintayad ārta rūpo |
dhruvam viśiṣṭā guṇato hi sītā |
atha ayam asyām kRtavān mahātmā |
lankā īśvaraḥ kaṣṭam anārya karma || 5-9-73

73. saḥ = ḥanuma; ārtharūpaḥ = became gloomy; punashcha so.achintayat = and thought (thus;); sītā = ṣeetha; dhruvamvishishhṭāḥ = is definitely the best guṇataḥ = by virtues; atha = then; ayam laṇ^keshvaraḥ = this lord of Lanka; mahātma = even though being great otherwise; kṛitavān = did; kashhṭam = evil; anāryam = (and)an un-gentlemanly deed) asyām = with her.

Hanuma became gloomy and thought thus: "Seetha is definitely the best by virtues; then this lord of Lanka even though being great otherwise, did an evil and an un-gentlemanly deed with her.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye sundara kāṇḍe navamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 9th chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
