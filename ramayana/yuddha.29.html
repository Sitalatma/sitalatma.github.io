Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 29
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana reprimands Shuka and Sarana, asking them to leave the assembly. He again sends some spies to the place of Rama and Lakshmana. Those spies were got caught by Vibhishana and the monkeys start harassing them. But the compassionate Rama gets them released and the spies reach back Lanka.

Sloka 1Verse Locator

śukena tu samākhyātāṃs tān dṛṣṭvā hari yūthapān |
lakṣmaṇam ca mahāvīryam bhujam rāmasya dakṣiṇam || 2-29-1
samīpastham ca rāmasya bhrātaram svam vibhīṣaṇam |
sarva vānara rājam ca sugrīvam bhīma vikramam || 2-29-2
aṅgadam cāpi balinam vajrahastātmajātmajam |
hanūmantam ca vikrāntam jāmbavantam ca durjayam || 2-29-3
suṣeṇam kumudam nīlam nalam ca plavagarṣabham |
gajam gavākṣam śarabham vaindam ca dvividam tathā || 2-29-4
kiṃcid āvigna hṛdayo jāta krodhaḥ ca rāvaṇaḥ |
bhartsayām āsa tau vīrau kathā ante śuka sāraṇau || 2-29-5

1; 2; 3; 4; 5. dṛiṣṭvā = beholding; hariyūthapān = those foremost of monkey-leaders; samādiṣṭān = shown; shukena = ṣhuka; mahāvīryam = the most valiant; lakshmaṇamcha = Lakshmana; rāmasya = Rama's; dakṣiṇambhujam = right arm; bhrātaram = his own brother; vibhīṣaṇam = Vibhishana; samīpastham = standing close; rāmasya = to Rama; bhīmavikramam = the terribly powerful; sugrīvamcha = ṣugreeva; sarvavānararājam = the king of all monkeys; balinam = the strong; aṇ^gadam chāpi = Angada; vajra hastātmajātmajam = grand son of Indra; the Bearer of the ṭhunder bolt; vikrāntam = the powerful; hanūmantam = ḥanuman; durjayam = the invincible; jāmbavantam = Jambavan; suṣeṇam = ṣushena; kumudam = Kumuda; nīlam = ṇila; nalamcha = ṇala; plavagarṣabham = the excellent of monkeys; gajam = Gaja; gavākṣam = Gavaksha; sharabham = ṣharabha; maindam = ṃainda; tathā = and; dvividam = Divivida; saḥrāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; āvigna hṛidayaḥ = his heart agitated; kimchit = a little; jātakrodhashcha = became enraged; bhartsayāmāsa = abused; tau = those two; vīrau = heroes; shuka sāraṇau = ṣhika and ṣarana; kathānte = who had completed their report.

Beholding those foremost of monkey leaders pointed out by Shuka- the most valiant Lakshmana; Rama's right arm, his own brother Vibhishana standing close to Rama, the terribly powerful Sugreeva the king of all monkeys, the strong Angada grandson of Indra the wielder of thunderbolt, the powerful Hanuman, the imincible Jambavan, Sushena, Kumuda, Nila, Nala the excellent of monkeys, Gaja, Gavaksha, Sharabha, ṃainda and Dvivida- that Ravana - his heart became agitated a little, was enraged and then abused those two heroes Shuka and Sarana who had completed their report.

Sloka 6

adho mukhau tau praṇatāv abravīt śuka sāraṇau |
roṣa gadgadayā vācā samrabdhaḥ paruṣam vacaḥ || 2-29-6

6. (Ravana) abravīt = spoke; tau = to those; shuka sāraṇau = ṣhuka and ṣarana; praṇatau = who saluted; adhomukhau = with their faces bent donw; vāchā = in a voice; roṣagadgadayā = choked in anger; samrabdham = excited; tathā = and paruṣam = and harsh.

Ravana spoke (the following) excited and harsh words, in a voice choked in anger to Shuka and Sarana who stood saluting with their faces bent down.

Sloka 7

na tāvat sadṛśam nāma sacivair upajīvibhiḥ |
vipriyam nṛpater vaktum nigraha pragrahe vibhoḥ || 2-29-7

7. na tāvatnāma = It is not; sadṛisham = befitting; vaktum = to utter; vipriyam = unpleasant words; upajivibhiḥ = by dependent; sachivaiḥ = ministers; nṛipate = to their king; prabhoḥ = who has the power; nigrahe = to mete out punishment; pragrahe = or reward.

"It is not befitting to utter unpleasant words by dependent ministers to their king who has the power to mete out any punishment or reward."

Sloka 8

ripūṇām pratikūlānām yuddha artham abhivartatām |
ubhābhyām sadṛśam nāma vaktum aprastave stavam || 2-29-8

8. sadṛisham nāma = Is it proper; vaktum = to utter; stavam = praise; vipūṇām = of the enemies; pratikūlānām = who are adverse to us; abhivartatām = and who are coming; yuddhartham = for war; ubhābhyām = by both of you; aprastave = irrelevantly?

"Is it proper for both of you to shower irrelevant praise on our enemies who are adverse to us and are approaching for a war?"

Sloka 9

ācāryā guravo vṛddhā vṛthā vām paryupāsitāḥ |
sāram yad rāja śāstrāṇām anujīvyam na gṛhyate || 2-29-9

9. vṛithā = in vain; paryupāsitāḥ = have you sat at the feet; āchāryāḥ = of your elders; guravaḥ = and your preceptors; vṛiddhāḥ = and the aged; yat = since; sāram = the essence; anujīvyam = to be followed; rāja shastrāṇām = from political sciences; nagṛihyate = has not been grasped; vām = by both of you.

"In vain have you sat at the feet of your elders, your preceptors and the aged, since the essence to be followed from political sciences has not been grasped by both of you."

Sloka 10

gṛhīto vā na vijñāto bhāro jñānasya vā ucyate |
īdṛśaiḥ sacivair yukto mūrkhair diṣṭyā dharāmi aham || 2-29-10

10. grahitaḥ = or if you have imbibed them; na vijJṇāto rā = you have not remembered them; vāhyate vā bhāraḥ = you are over-burdened; ajJṇānasya = with ignorance; yuktaḥ = being associated; īdṛishaiḥ = with such; sachiraiḥ = ministers; murkhaiḥ = who are fools; diṣṭyā = it is a miracle; aham (that) I; dharāmi = I am able to retain my sovereignity.

"Or if you have imbibed them, you have not remembered them. You are over-burdened with ignorance! Being associated with such foolish ministers, it is a miracle that I am still able to retain my sovereignty."

Sloka 11

kim nu mṛtyor bhayam na asti mām vaktum paruṣam vacaḥ |
yasya me śāsato jihvā prayaccati śubha aśubham || 2-29-11

11. nāsti kimnu = have you no; bhayam = fear; mṛityoḥ = of death; vaktum = to speak; paruṣam = (such) rude; vachaḥ = words; mām = to me; prayachchhati = who dispenses; shubhāshubham = good and evil; yasyame = by my; jihvā = tongue; shāsataḥ = which commands.

"Have you no fear of death that you dare address me thus rudely, I whose tongue that you dare tongue dispenses good and evil?"

Sloka 12

api eva dahanam spṛṣṭvā vane tiṣṭhanti pādapāḥ |
rāja doṣa parāmṛṣṭās tiṣṭhante na aparādhinaḥ || 2-29-12

12. pādapāḥ = trees; tiṣṭhanti = survive; vane = in the forest; spṛiṣṭvā apyaiva = even if disturbed; dahanam = by fire; (But); aparādhinaḥ = the guilty; na tiṣṭhante = cannot survive; raja daṇ^Daparāmṛiṣṭāḥ = if touched by the royal scepter.

"Trees may survive in the forest, even if disturbed by fire. But the guilty cannot survive, if touched by the royal scepter."

Sloka 13

hanyām aham imau pāpau śatru pakṣa praśaṃsakau |
yadi pūrva upakārair me na krodho mṛdutām vrajet || 2-29-13

13. yadi = If; me = my; krodhaḥ = anger; na vrajet mṛidutām = is not softened; pūrvopakāraiḥ = by the services they rendered earlier; aham = I; hanyām = would have killed; imau pāpau = these two miscreants; satrupakṣaprashamsinau = who are praising the band of enemies.

"If my anger is not softened by the services they rendered earlier, I would have killed thse two miscreants who are praising the band of enemies."

Sloka 14

apadhvaṃsata gaccadhvam samnikarṣād ito mama |
na hi vām hantum iccāmi smarann upakṛtāni vām || 2-29-14
hatāv eva kṛtaghnau tau mayi sneha parān mukhau |

14. apadhvamsata = keep away; mama = from my; itaḥ = this; samnikarṣāt = neighbourhood; na shyadhvam = do not be seen anywhere here; smarāni = I am recollectin; vām = your; upakṛitāni = services; na ichchhāmi = I do not wish; hantum = to kill; vām = you; dvau = both of you; kṛitaghnau = who are ungrateful; snehaparāṇ^mukhau = and affection-less; mayi = towards me; hatāveva = are just as dead.

"Keep away from my neighbourhood. Do not be seen anywhere here. I am recollecting your past services and hence do not wish to kill you. Both of you, who are ungrateful and unfaithful towards me, are just as dead to me."

Sloka 15

evam uktau tu savrīḍau tāv ubhau śuka sāraṇau || 2-29-15
rāvaṇam jaya śabdena pratinandya abhinihsṛtau |

15. tau shukasāriṇau = those ṣhuka and ṣarana; evam = thus; uktau = told; savrīdau = they were ashamed; dṛiṣṭvā = having seen; rāvaṇam = Ravana; niḥsṛitau = and went away; pratinandya = having enlogised; jayashabdena = with words of victory.

Hearing Ravana's words, Shuka and Sarana felt ashamed to see Ravana paid obeisance to him saying, "Be thou victorious!" and went away.

Sloka 16

abravīt sa daśagrīvaḥ samīpastham mahāudaram || 2-29-16
upasthāpaya śīghram me cārān nīti viśāradān |

16. nishācharaḥ = the deomon; dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; abravīt = spoke; mahodaram = to ṃahodara; samipastham = who was standing nearby (as follows):- upasthāya = bring; me = me; chārān = spies; shīghram = quickly.

The demon Ravana spoke to ṃahodara who was standing nearby as follows: "Bring me the spies here quickly."

Sloka 17

mahodarastathoktastu śīghramājñāpayaccarān || 2-29-17
tataścārāḥ samtvaritāḥ prāptāḥ pārthivaśāsanāt |
upasthithāḥ prāñjalayo vardhayitvā jayāśiṣaḥ || 2-29-18

17; 18. mehodaraḥ = ṃahodara; tathā = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; shīghram = quickly; ājJṇāpayat = ordered; charān = for the spies; tataḥ = then; chārāḥ = the spies; prāptāḥ = who came; samtvaritāḥ = hurriedly; pārthiva shāsanat = as per the order of the king; prāJṇjalayaḥ = paid obeisance by joining their palms; upasthitāḥ = and approached; vadhayitvā = having felicitated (Ravana); jayāshiṣaḥ = expressing their desire to see him victorious.

Hearing those words, ṃahodara immediately ordered for the spies. The spies came hurriedly as per the orders of the king, paid obeisance to him by joining their palms and approached him, having made a complement expressing their desire to see him victorious.

Sloka 19

tān abravīt tato vākyam rāvaṇo rākṣasa adhipaḥ || 2-29-19
cārān pratyayikān śūrān bhaktān vigata sādhvasān |

19. tataḥ = then; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākshasādhipaḥ = the king of demons; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; tān charān = to those spies; prātyayikam = who were faithful; shūrān = brave; dhīrān = energetic; vigata sādhvasān = and free from fear.

Then, Ravana the king of demons spoke the following words to those spies, who were faithful, brave, energetic and free from fear:

Sloka 20

ito gaccata rāmasya vyavasāyam parīkṣatha || 2-29-20
mantreṣv abhyantarā ye asya prītyā tena samāgatāḥ |

20. gachchhata = you go; itaḥ = from here; parīkshitum = to investigate; vyavasāyam = about the first impression; rāmasya = about Rama; ye = (as regards) who; abhyantarāḥ = are the intimate friends; asya = to him; samāgatāḥ = and those who joined; tena = with him; prītyā = in affection.

"You go from here to investigate about the first impression concerning Rama, as regards who are his intimate friends and in respect of those who joined on his side with a liking towards him."

Sloka 21

katham svapiti jāgarti kim anyac ca kariṣyati || 2-29-21
vijñāya nipuṇam sarvam āgantavyam aśeṣataḥ |

21. katham = how; (is he); svapiti = sleeping? Jāgarti = and waking up? Kim = what; kariṣyati = will he do; adya = now? Aagantavyam = you ought to come; vijJṇāya = after acquainting with; sarvam = all this; asheṣāḥ = completely; nipuṇam = and skillfully.

"Find out what are his hours of sleeping and waking and what he intends to do next. You ought to come here, after acquainting with all this information completely and skillfully."

Sloka 22

cāreṇa viditaḥ śatruḥ paṇḍitair vasudhā adhipaiḥ || 2-29-22
yuddhe svalpena yatnena samāsādya nirasyate |

22. vasudhādhipaiḥ = by kings; paṇDitaiḥ = who are shrewd; shatruḥ = an enemy; yuddhe = in battle; samāsādhya = who is got; chāreṇa = through spies; nirasyate = will be defeated; svalpena = with a little; yatnena = of effort.

"An enemy in battle, got known through spies by kings, will be defeated with only a little of effort."

Sloka 23

cārās tu te tathā iti uktvā prahṛṣṭā rākṣasa īśvaram || 2-29-23
śārdūlamagrataḥ kṛtvā tataścakruḥ pradakṣiṇam |

23. te = those; chārāstu = spies on their part; prahṛiṣṭāḥ = were delighted; uktvā = spoke; tatheti = "ṃay it be so"; kṛitvā = kept; shārdūlam = ṣhardula; agrataḥ = in front; chakruḥ = and made; pradakshiṇam = clockwise circumambulation; rākshasesvaram = around Ravana.

Those spies on their part delightfully replied, "ṃay it be so", kept Shardula in their front and made their circumambulation clockwise around Ravana.

Sloka 24

tatastam tu mahātmānam cārā rākṣasasattamam || 2-29-24
kṛtvā pradakṣiṇam jagmur yatra rāmaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |

24. tataḥ = then; chārāḥ = the spies; pradakṣiṇam kṛitvā = having made the circumambulation; (around); tam = that; rākṣasa sattamam = excellent demon; mahātmānam = the distinguished; jagmuḥ = and went; yatra = to the place where; rāmaḥ = Rama; salakṣmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana (were there).

Having thus made circumambulation to Ravana the excellent and the distinguished demon, the spies went to the place where Rama along with Lakshmana were there.

Sloka 25

te suvelasya śailasya samīpe rāma lakṣmaṇau || 2-29-25
praccannā dadṛśur gatvā sasugrīva vibhīṣaṇau |

25. gatvā = having gone; prachchhannāḥ = in a disguised manner; te = they; dadṛishuḥ = saw; rāmalakshmaṇau = Rama and Lakshman; sa sugrīva vibhīṣaṇau = together with ṣugreeva and Vibhishana; sanīpe = in the vicinity; suvelasya shailasya = of mountain ṣuvela.

Having gone in a disguised manner, those spies saw Rama and Lakshman together with Sugreeva and Vibhishana in the neighbourhood of ṃountain Suvela.

Sloka 26

prekṣamāṇāścamūm tām ca babhūvurbhayavihvalāḥ || 2-29-26
te tu dharma ātmanā dṛṣṭā rākṣasa indreṇa rākṣasāḥ |

26. prekshamāṇāḥ = seeing; tām chamūm = that army; te rākshasāḥ = those demons; babhūvuḥ = became; bhayavihvalāḥ = overwhelmed with fear; dṛiṣṭāḥ = and were observed; rākshasendreṇa = by Vibhishana the Lord of demons; dharmātmanā = the high-souled.

Seeing that army, those demons became overwhelmed with fear. However, they were observed by the high-souled Vibhishana the Lord of demons.

Sloka 27

vibhīṣaṇena tatrasthā nigṛhītā yadṛccayā || 2-29-27
śārdūlo grāhitastvekaḥ pāpo.ayamiti rākṣasaḥ |

27. yard^ichchhayā = accidentally; tatrasthāḥ = the demons there; nigṛihītāḥ = were caught; vibhīṣaṇena = by Vibhishana; iti = saying that; ayam = this; rākṣasaḥ = demon; pāpaḥ = was a wicked person; shārdūlaḥ tu = ṣhardula; ekaḥ = alone; grāhitaḥ = was got seized.

Accidentally, the deomons there were caught by Vibhishana, who said that the demon Shardula among them was wicked and got him alone seized.

Sloka 28

mokṣitaḥ so.api rāmeṇa vadhyamānaḥ plavaṅgamaiḥ || 2-29-28
anṛśaṃsena rāmeṇa mokṣitā rākṣasāḥ sare |

28. so.api = that ṣhardula also; vadhyamānaḥ = who was about to be killed; plavaṇ^gamaiḥ = by the monkeys; mochitaḥ = was got released; rāmeṇa = by Rama; pare = the other; rākṣasāḥ = demons; mokṣitāḥ = wee got released; rāmeṇa = by Rama; anṛishamsena = the kind man.

That Shardula also, who was going to be killed by the monkeys, was got released by Rama. The other demons also were got released by Rama, the kind man.

Sloka 29

vānarair arditās te tu vikrāntair laghu vikramaiḥ || 2-29-29
punar laṃkām anuprāptāḥ śvasanto naṣṭa cetasaḥ |

29. te = those demons; arditāḥ = harassed; vānaraiḥ = by the monkeys; vikrāntaiḥ = the courageous; laghuvikramaiḥ = and the quick-footed; naṣṭachetasaḥ = became insensible; shvasantaḥ = heaved a sigh; anuprāptāḥ = and reached; laṇ^kām = Lanka; punaḥ = again.

Those demons, harassed by the courageous and the quick-paced monkeys, became insensible, heaved a sigh and reached back Lanka.

Sloka 30

tato daśagrīvam upasthitās te |
cārā bahir nitya carā niśā carāḥ |
gireḥ suvelasya samīpa vāsinam |
nyavedayan bhīma balam mahābalāḥ || 2-29-30

30. chārāḥ = (those) spies; nityacharāḥ = mahābalāḥ = valiant; nishācharāḥ = rangers of the night; tataḥ = thereafter; upasthitāḥ = approached; dashagrīvam = Ravana; nyavedayan = and informed him; rāma balam = that Rama's army; samīpavāsinam = was camping in the vicinity; suvelasya = of the ṣuvela mountain.

Those spies, who always wander outside and who were valiant rangers of the night, thereafter approached Ravana and informed him that Rama's army was camping in the vicinity of the Suvela mountain.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekonatriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 29th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
