Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

chapter [Sarga] 66
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Legend of Shiva's bow is narrated as Vishvamitra asks Janaka to show that bow to Rama and Lakshmana, as they have come this far to have a glimpse of that great bow. Janaka, while narrating its history, makes an offer saying that his daughter Seetha will be given in marriage, if only Rama can string the bowstring of Shiva's bow. In the very same sixty-sixth chapter of Kishkindha Kanda, Valmiki narrates the birth of Hanuma through Jambavanta, elaborately. But here Valmiki informs through Janaka about the nature of birth of Seetha, very concisely, which gave raise to too much of debate about this enigmatic Seetha and her birth. Some points are given in the endnote.

Sloka 1

tataḥ prabhāte vimale kṛta karmā narādhipaḥ |
viśhvāmitram mahātmānam ājuhāva sa rāghavam || 1-66-1

1. tataḥ prabhāte vimale = then, in morning, aurora; kṛita karmā narādhipaḥ = on performing, duties [morning rites,] king [Janaka]; vishvāmitram mahātmānam = Vishvamitra, great souled one; ājuhāva sa rāghavam = invited, with, Raghava-s.

Then on performing morning time rites in the aurora of next morning, king Janaka invited great souled Vishvamitra, along with Raghava-s. [1-66-1]

Sloka 2

tam archayitvā dharmāatmā śhāstra dṛṣhṭena karmaṇā |
rāghavau cha mahātmānau tadā vākyam uvācha ha || 1-66-2

2. tam arcayitvā dharmāatmā = him [Vishvamitra,] on reverencing, virtue souled [Janaka]; shāstra dṛiṣṭena karmaṇā = by scriptures, envisaged, formalities; rāghavau ca = Raghava-s, also; mahā ātmānau = noble souled ones [Raghava-s]; tadā vākyam uvāca ha = then words, spoke, indeed.

On reverencing Vishvamitra and the noble souled Raghava-s according to the formalities envisaged by scriptures, then the virtue souled Janaka spoke these words, indeed. [1-66-2]

Sloka 3

bhagavan svāgatam te astu kim karomi tava anagha |
bhavān ājñāpayatu mām ājñāpyo bhavatā hi aham || 1-66-3

3. bhagavan svāgatam te astu = oh, god, welcome, to you, be there; kim karomi tava anagha = what, I should do, for you, oh, holy sage; bhavān ājñāpayatu mām = you, bid, me; ājñāpyaḥ bhavatā hi aham = biddable, by you, indeed, I am.

"Oh, god, you are welcome, oh, holy sage, bid me as to what I should for you, for I am biddable by you, indeed... [1-66-3]

Sloka 4

evam uktaḥ sa dharmātmā janakena mahātmanā |
pratyuvācha munir vīram vākyam vākya viśhāradaḥ || 1-66-4

4. anvaya/word-order: mahātmanā janakena = noble souled [foreseer,] by Janaka; evam uktaḥ = thus, said; dharmātmā = virtue souled [presager]; vākya vishāradaḥ = sentence, expert [wordsmith]; saḥ muniḥ = that sage [Vishvamitra]; vīram vākyam pratyuvāca = to valiant Janaka, words, in reply said.

When said thus by the foreseer Janaka, [who can foresee as to why Vishvamitra came with Raghava-s this far, and thus asked him as to what next is to be done,] that sage Vishvamitra, for he is presager and wordsmith, [and who knows what is to be done next,] said these words in reply to that valiant king Janaka. [1-66-4]

Sloka 5

putrau daśharathasya imau kṣhatriyau loka viśhrutau |
draṣhṭu kāmau dhanuḥ śhreṣhṭham yat etat tvayi tiṣhṭhati || 1-66-5

5. putrau dasharathasya imau = sons, of Dasharatha, these two; kṣatriyau loka vishrutau = Kshatriya-s, in world, renowned; draṣṭu kāmau dhanuḥ shreṣṭham = to see, desirous, bow, marvellous; yat etat tvayi tiṣṭhati = which [bow,] that one, in you [with you,] have a place.

"These two are the sons of Dasharatha, well-renowned Kshatriya-s in world, and they are desirous to see that marvellous bow which has a place with you... [1-66-5]

Sloka 6

etat darśhaya bhadram te kṛta kāmau nṛpa ātmajau |
darśhanāt asya dhanuṣho yathā iṣhṭam pratiyāsyataḥ || 1-66-6

6. etat darshaya = that [bow,] show; bhadram te = bodes well, to you; kṛita kāmau nṛipa ātmajau = done [come true,] desires, king's, sons [two princes]; darshanāt asya dhanuṣaḥ = by beholding, that, bow; yathā iṣṭam prati yāsyataḥ = as, they like, return [back,] they go.

"You may show that bow, well bodes you, and on beholding that bow the desire of these two princes will come true, and they will go back as they like... [1-66-6]

Sloka 7

evam uktaḥ tu janakaḥ pratyuvācha mahāmunim |
śhrūyatām asya dhanuṣho yat artham iha tiṣhṭhati || 1-66-7

7. evam uktaḥ tu janakaḥ = thus, addressed, but, Janaka; pratyuvāca mahāmunim = replied, great sage; shrūyatām asya dhanuṣaḥ = I let you hear, about that, bow; yat artham iha tiṣṭhati = by which, reason, here, it has a place.

But Janaka replied the great sage Vishvamitra when he was addressed thus, "I shall [firstly] tell by which reason that bow has its place here... [1-66-7]

Sloka 8

devarāta iti khyāto nimeḥ jyeṣhṭho mahī patiḥ |
nyāso ayam tasya bhagavan haste datto mahātmanā || 1-66-8

8. anvaya/word-order: bhagavan = oh, godly sage; nimeḥ = from ṇimi; ṣaṣṭaḥ [jyeṣṭaḥ] = sixth [eldest]; devarāta iti khyātaḥ = Devarāta, thus, renowned; mahīpatiḥ = king; ayam = this [bow]; tasya haste nyāsaḥ = in his, hand, as custodial care; mahātmanā = by ṣublime ṣoul [ṣhiva]; dattaḥ = given.

"Oh, godly sage, there was a king renowned as Devarāta, sixth one from Nimi, [the originator of our lineage,] and this bow was handed down to him for custodial care by the Sublime Soul, Shiva... [1-66-8]

▼

Comment: Some mms use the word jyeṣṭaḥ instead of Saṣṭa for Nimi and then the meaning is that Nimi's eldest son is Devarāta. Basing on the account that is given by Janaka at the time of Rama's mariage with Seetha, Devarāta is the sixth one from Nimi, the originator of Janaka's lineage.

Sloka 9

dakṣha yajña vadhe pūrvam dhanuḥ āyaṃya vīryavān |
rudraḥ tu tridaśhān roṣhāt sa līlam idam abravīt || 1-66-9

9. dakṣa yajña vadhe = Daksha Prajapati's, Vedic-ritual, devastation; pūrvam = once; dhanuḥ āyamya vīryavān = bow, outstretching [bowstring,] mettlesome [god]; rudraḥ tu tridashān = Rudra, but, to all gods; roṣāt sa līlam idam abravīt = rancorously, with, playing with [superciliously,] this, said.

"Once, during the devastation of the Vedic-ritual of Daksha Prajapati, the mettlesome god Rudra, rancorously outstretching the bowstring of this bow, said this to all gods, superciliously... [1-66-9]

Sloka 10

yasmāt bhāga arthino bhāgān na akalpayata me surāḥ |
vara aṃgāni mahāarhāṇi dhanuṣhā śhātayāmi vaḥ || 1-66-10

10. yasmāt = whereby; bhāga arthinaḥ = portion [of oblations in Vedic-ritual,] desirer; bhāgān na a kalpayata = apportioned, not, made; me = to me; surāḥ = oh, gods; vara angāni = best, limbs [heads]; mahā arhāṇi = highly, revered [heads]; dhanuṣā shātayāmi = with bow, I will shred; vaḥ = yours.

" 'Oh, gods, whereby you have not apportioned my portion [of oblations in Vedic-ritual s, as I am also the] desirer of such portion, [thereby] I will shred the highly revered heads of yours with this bow...' [So said Shiva to gods.] [1-66-10]

Sloka 11

tato vimanasaḥ sarve devā vai munipuṃgava |
prasādayanti deveśham teṣhām prīto abhavat bhavaḥ || 1-66-11

11. tataḥ vimanasaḥ sarve devā vai = then, dismayed, all, gods truly; munipungava = oh, best saint; prasādayanti devesham = on supplicating, God of Gods [ṣhiva]; teṣām prītaḥ abhavat bhavaḥ = of them, glad, ḥe became, Bhava [another name of ṣhiva.]

"Then, oh, best saint Vishvamitra, all gods are truly dismayed, and on their supplicating, Bhava, namely Shiva, the God of Gods is gladdened... [1-66-11]

Sloka 12

prīti yuktaḥ tu sarveṣhām dadau teṣhām mahātmanām |
tat etat devadevasya dhanū ratnam mahātmanaḥ || 1-66-12
nyāsabhūtam tadā nyastam asmākam pūrvaje vibho |

12, 13a. prīti yuktaḥ tu = glad, having [gladly,] but; sarveṣām dadau = to all, gave; teṣām mahā ātmanām = to them, great souled gods; tat etat devadevasya = that, this, God of God's; dhanū ratnam = bow, gem of a; mahātmanaḥ = by ṣublime ṣoul ṣhiva; nyāsabhūtam = for custodial care; tadā nyastam = then, given; asmākam pūrvaje = to our, ancestor vibho = oh, godly saint.

"And that Sublime Soul Shiva gladly gave that bow to all of the great souled gods, and oh, godly saint, then those great souled gods gave this gem of a bow of Shiva, the God of Gods, to our ancestor [Devarāta,] for custodial care... [1-66-12, 13a]

Sloka 13

atha me kṛṣhataḥ kṣhetram lāṃgalāt utthitā mama || 1-66-13
kṣhetram śhodhayatā labdhvā nāmnā sītā iti viśhrutā |

13b, 14a. atha = later; me kṛiṣataḥ kṣetram = by me, when ploughing, ritual-field; lāngalāt utthitā tataḥ = from plough [by plough from furrow,] raised, then; kṣetram shodhayatā = ritual-filed, while purifying [consecrating]; labdhvā = gained; nāmnā sītā iti vishrutā = by name [named as,] ṣeetha, thus, renowned.

"Later, when I was ploughing the ritual field then raised by the plough [from the furrow is a baby girl... since she is] gained while consecrating the ritual-field, she is named as Seetha, and thus she is renowned... [1-66-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

bhū talāt utthitā sā tu vyavardhata mama ātmajā || 1-66-14
vīrya śhulkā iti me kanyā sthāpitā iyam ayonijā |

14b, 15a bhū talāt utthitā sā tu = earth, from surface, arose [surfaced,] she, but; vyavardhata mama ātmajā = fostered, my [own,] soul-born girl; vīrya shulkā iti = boldness, bounty, thus; me = by me; kanyā = girl; sthāpitā = determined; iyam = she is; a yoni jā = not, uterine, birthed.

"Hers is a non-uterine birth as she surfaced from the surface of the earth, but fostered as my own soul-born girl and I determined [to giver her in marriage to a bridegroom where his] boldness is the only bounty, [I receive in that marriage...] [1-66-14b, 15a]

▼

Comment: 'Dowry is property or money brought by a bride to her husband' and this is familiar throughout the world. In ancient India, there was a counterpart custom to this, called kanyā shulkam meaning 'some bounty, property or money offered by a bridegroom's family to the bride's family' since they are getting a worthy bride, coming into their family, not just as a mere wife of the bridegroom, but to upkeep and promote that family and its progeny. And this dowry or its counterpart is not compulsorily be paid in hard cash, but it may be any kind of gifts mutually exchanged, which has slowly developed into a mega havoc these days. Here Janaka wants the 'valour' of his prospective son-in-law as bounty due to him in the marriage of Seetha.

Sloka 15

bhūtalāt utthitām tām tu vardhamānām mama ātmajām || 1-66-15
varayāmāsuḥ āgamya rājāno munipuṃgava |

15b, 16a. bhūtalāt utthitām tām tu = from earth's, surface, surfaced from, her, but; vardha mānām mama ātmajām = come of age, my, daughter is; [vīra sulkatayā sthāpitam upshrutya = boldness, bounty of, ascribed to be, on my informing; having heard my declaration that the bounty for ṣeetha is boldness alone;] varayāmāsuḥ āgamya rājānaḥ = besought, on coming, kings; munipungava = oh, eminent sage.

"Oh, eminent sage, as my daughter has surfaced from the surface of earth and has come of age, the kings, [having heard my declaration that the bounty for Seetha is boldness alone,] have come and besought for her... [1-66-15b, 16a]

Sloka 16

teṣhām varayatām kanyām sarveṣhām pṛthivīkṣhitām || 1-66-16
vīrya śhulkā iti bhagavan na dadāmi sutām aham |

16b, 17a. teṣām varayatām kanyām = to them, who are beseeching, for girl; sarveṣām = to all; pṛithivīkṣitām = kings; vīrya shulkā iti = boldness, bounty, thus [saying]; bhagavan = oh, godly sage; na dadāmi sutām aham = not, I gave, daughter, I have.

"To all of those kings who are beseeching for the girl, I have not given my daughter, saying that she will be given for a bounty of boldness... [1-6-16b, 17a]

Sloka 17

tataḥ sarve nṛpatayaḥ sametya munipuṃgava || 1-66-17
mithilām abhyupāgaṃya vīryam jijñāsavaḥ tadā |

17b, 18a. tataḥ sarve nṛipatayaḥ sametya = then, all, kings, convoked; munipungava = oh, eminent sage; mithilām abhyupāgamya = at ṃithila, on arriving; vīryam jijñāsavaḥ tadā = calibre [of bow,] they wanted to ascertain, then.

"Then all the kings convoked and on arriving at ṃithila, then they wanted to ascertain the calibre [of the bow, vis-a-vis their own...] [1-66-17b, 18a]

Sloka 18

teṣhām jijñāsamānānām śhaivam dhanuḥ upāhṛtam || 1-66-18
na śhekuḥ grahaṇe tasya dhanuṣhaḥ tolane api vā |

18b, 19a. teṣām jijñāsamānānām = for them, those who want to ascertain [the calibre of bow]; shaivam dhanuḥ upa āhṛitam = ṣhiva's, bow, to [their] proximity, fetched; na shekuḥ = not, capable; grahaṇe tasya dhanuṣaḥ = in catching hold of, its, bow; tolane api vā = to balance it [joggle,] even, or.

"For them, those who wanted to ascertain the calibre of the bow, that bow of Shiva is fetched to their proximity, but they are incapable to joggle it, or even to catch hold of it... [1-66-18b, 19a]

▼

Comment: This bow of Shiva will be transported on a wheeled casket-cart with eight wheels and drawn by five thousand robust persons. This is narrated in next chapter. 'It is drawn by drawn by five hundred bulls...' ānanda rāmayana says so. In other versions of Ramayana, it is said that many people will pull that casket-cart, as one or two persons cannot haul it. Once, when Seetha was playing with other girls, their flowery ball of girl's rugby goes under this cart. None of her girlfriends is dare enough to near this bow-casket-cart, since it is a reverential casket-cart. But Seetha goes there and pushes that casket-cart aside with her left hand, as though it is a garland, and retrieves that flower ball. This capability of Seetha in easy handling of Shiva's bow, becomes a bane to her, when one among the wives of sapta ṛiṣi 'Seven Sages...' issues a curse to Seetha, saying that 'Seetha will be separated from her husband for some time, of course, for the good of people...' So says the tradition.

Sloka 19

teṣhām vīryavatām vīryam alpam jñātvā mahāmune || 1-66-19
pratyākhyātā nṛpatayaḥ tan nibodha tapodhana |

19b, 20a. teṣām vīryavatām = their, valorous ones'; vīryam alpam jñātvā = valour, trivial [valueless,] on knowing; mahāmune = oh, great saint; prati ākhyātā = counter, said [countermanded by me]; nṛipatayaḥ = kings; tat nibodha tapodhana = [by] that, you may know, oh, ascetically wealthy Vishvamitra.

"Oh, great saint, on knowing the valour of those valorous ones as valueless, I countermanded them... oh, ascetically wealthy Vishvamitra, by that you may know [the sequel of it...] [1-66-19b, 20a]

"Then, oh, eminent sage, those kings beleaguered ṃithila in a blind fury, since a self-mistrust bechanced among them all, about their own valour... [1-66-20b, 21a]

Sloka 21

ātmānam avadhūtam te vijñāya munipuṃgava || 1-66-21

"They surmised for themselves that they are brushed off by me, and they possessed by a high rancour, they strangled the City of ṃithila... [1-66-21b, 22a]

Sloka 22

tataḥ saṃvatsare pūrṇe kṣhayam yātāni sarvaśhaḥ || 1-66-22
sādhanāni muniśhreṣhṭha tato aham bhṛśha duḥkhitaḥ |

22b, 23a. tataḥ samvatsare pūrṇe = then, a year, completed [elapsed]; kṣayam yātāni sarvashaḥ = decline, went into, in anyway; sādhanāni = possessions [for livelihood]; munishreṣṭha = oh, eminent sage; tataḥ aham bhṛisha duḥkhitaḥ = thereby, I was, highly, anguished.

"Then elapsed is an year and in anyway the possessions for livelihood went into a decline, oh, eminent sage, thereby I am highly anguished [1-66-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

23b, 24a. tataḥ deva gaṇān sarvān = then, of gods, assemblages, all; tapasā aham prasādayam = by ascesis, I have, assuaged; daduḥ ca parama prītāḥ = gave, also, highly, gladdened; caturanga balam surāḥ = fourfold, forces, gods

"Then I have assuaged the assemblages of gods by my ascesis and gods are also highly gladdened and gave me fourfold forces... [1-66-23b, 24a]

▼

Comment: The four components of army are foot soldiers, cavalry, elephant-squadrons, and chariot-warriors.

Sloka 24

tato bhagnā nṛpatayo hanyamānā diśho yayuḥ || 1-66-24
avīryā vīrya saṃdigdhā sa amātyāḥ pāpa kāriṇaḥ |

24b, 25a. tataḥ = then; a vīryā = without, vigour; vīrya sandigdhā = of valour, self-mistrustful [of their own valour]; pāpa kāriṇaḥ = evildoers; nṛipatayaḥ = kings; hanyamānā = being drubbed; bhagnā = broken [defeated]; sa amātyāḥ = with, ministers [retinue]; dishaḥ yayuḥ = to directions, went [beat a hasty retreat.]

"Then those evildoers and self-mistrustful kings while being drubbed [by the heaven-sent army, they have become] vigourless and broken, and they beat a hasty retreat... [1-66-24b, 25a]

Sloka 25

tat etat muniśhārdūla dhanuḥ parama bhāsvaram || 1-66-25
rāma lakṣhmaṇayoḥ cha api darśhayiṣhyāmi suvrata |

25b, 26a tat etat = that, this; munishārdūla = oh, tigerly sage; dhanuḥ parama bhāsvaram = bow, supremely, radiant; rāma lakṣmaṇayoḥ ca api = to Rama, to Lakshmana, also, even; darshayiṣyāmi suvrata = I will show, oh, saint of sacred vow.

"Oh, tigerly sage this is that supremely radiant bow, and oh, saint of sacred vows, I will show it, even to Rama and Lakshmana... [1-66-25b, 26a]

▼

Comment: Here by the use of word 'even' 'also' Janaka is reckoning Rama or Lakshmana on par with other kings who have tried their hand in lifting it. As of now, Janaka is not admitting any supremeness or super-humanness to them. In Janaka's asking at verse 4 'what can I do next...' etc., Janaka has sensed as to why this Vishvamitra brought some boys on this long a route, that too by foot. But he is not yet self-assured of Rama's capability or otherwise. Hence, the next verse starts with the clause 'if'.

Sloka 26

"If Rama strings the bowstring of that bow, oh, sage, I will offer my daughter, whose birth is non-uterine, to Dasharatha's Rama..." [So said Janaka to Vishvamitra.] [1-66-26b, c]

Enigmatic Seetha and her enigmatic birth


▼

In the endnote of ch. 56 of Aranya Kanda, some details are given about the enigmatic Seetha. Here also some points about her enigmatic birth are presented.

▼

Seetha is considered to be the nature's nature, Primordeal Nature, mahā prakriti, and when the five elements of nature intermingle into one, that is the ṃanifest Nature, where the five elements are pridhvi, āpa, teja, vāyu, ākāsha 'earth, waters, fire, air and space...' and there are many theories of their commingling like, pachī karaṇa, saptī karaṇa quintuplication, and sevenfold commingling etc. The process of creation gives rise to 24 items: 1] empirical or fundamental matter, 2] mahat, 3] ahamkāra, 4-8] five subtle elements, 9-13] five gross elements, 14] mind, 15-19] five sense organs, 20-24] five motor organs, 25] jīva ātma, individual's soul, 26] Brahman, The Absolute. With these, a created living being is created. This is in the case of mortals and their births. But when Seetha is found in the furrow all these things have already happened and there is no question of human pregnancy to be attached to her. Hence Seetha is to be taken as Goddess Lakshmi, who is parā shakti, Vishnu ṃaya and when she has to take an incarnation to become the prime cause to the effect of eliminating Ravana, no human activity of reproduction can be attached to such beings. If she were to be a human, perhaps she would not have exited from the pyre of fire when she self-immolated, after Rama and Ravana war. Some more information, though unrelated to Valmiki Ramayana, is given hereunder.

lakṣhmīm kṣhīra samudra rāja tanayām srīranga dhāmeshvarīm |
dasī bhūta samasta deva vanitām lokaika dīpa ankurām ||

▼

Lakshmi evolved from the ṃilky Ocean, when gods and demons churned it for amrita, the Divine Elixir, along with it a host of other items like, hālāhala, [cosmic poison,] candra ṃoon, kalpa vriksha, [Benevolent Divine Tree,] and the medicinal sage-god Dhanvantari holding the golden pot of amrita, have come out as has been explained by Vishvamitra to Rama in this Kanda.

▼

Vishnu is the all-inclusive deity, known as purusha or mahā puruṣa... parama ātma Supreme Person, narāyaṇa one who peregrinates mortals in and out this mortal world, antaryAmi The In-dweller of all beings, and He is the sheshin the Totality, in whom all souls are contained. He is bhagavat where bhaga, derives from the root bhaj meaning Gracious Lord, and vat one who has it. Vishnu possesses six such divine glories SaD guna vibhūti, namely, 1] jñāna, Omniscience 2] aishwarya, Providence, 3] shakti, Omnicompetence, 4] bala, Omnipotence, 5] vīrya, Immutability. 6] tejas, Resplendence, shakti is the samvit, the Primary Intelligence of God, while the other five attributes emerge from this samvid and hence shakti is the god's ahamata Personality and Activity. Thus this Shakti of god is personified in mythological lore and is called Shri or Lakshmi, and She is said to manifest herself in, 1] kriyā shakti, Creative Activity and 2] bhūti shakti, Creation of God. Hence Vishnu cannot part with His own personality or creativity i.e., ahamta, which in its feminine form is called shrī or Lakshmi. He therefore needs His consort Goddess Lakshmi to be with Him always, untouched by any. Thus, Goddess Lakshmi has to accompany Vishnu in all His incarnations, along with other paraphernalia like his conch-shell, his quoit and mace, and the thousand headed serpent ādi sheṣa etc. Such a delightful deity Goddess Lakshmi is a treasure house that can be abducted by the greedy, but she is fickle like ripples lakṣhmī hi toya taranga capalā Lakshmi= Goddess Lakshmi; hi= indeed; toya= water; taranga= wave like; capalā= wavering. 'Goddess Lakshmi is wavering like the waves of water...' chapala, chanchala, asthira, wavering, faltering, unstable, and māyā, mā + yā 'whose illusory play she is, or līla play of god. She comes in unnoticeably like the water in a coconut and evaporates innocently into thin air, at Her will. Such as she is she is easily grabbed by the greedy. Hence none can give birth to her except the ṃother Nature, and hence she is called mahilā ; mahi + lā where mahi is earth and lā ; laḥ khaṇDane triṣu ādāne stri because ṃother Earth gave birth to Seetha, Seetha is mahila Hence it is better to avoid assuming her to be an ordinary lady of an ordinary birth, and she may be viewed either legendarily or mythologically, for Valmiki as an epical poet, himself has not chronicled the details of her birth.

▼

Even Vishnu is deprived of Her in His incarnations, not only in Ramayana but also in His nṛi simha ṃan-Lion incarnation. There, ṃaya Lakshmi becomes a tribal woman to bring back the ferocious Lion-ṃan to his original form. And in other versions of Ramayana, it is said that Ravana abducted ṃaya Seetha, but not real Seetha. 'When the real Seetha herself is an enigma, what if Ravana abducts real or duplicate copy of that Seetha...' is the brushed aside adjustment. In either case, it is maya, līla illusory power, sport of Vishnu together with that of Goddess Lakshmi, cause this enigma. Hence, whenever She is thus absconding from Him, Vishnu searches and retrieves Her, for no one in Universe can handle her, nor amass all the wealth. The predestined wealth alone is enjoyable but nothing more can be amassed. So also are the riches in the present day world, come too suddenly and vanish that suddenly. No one is rich ever and anon, and on someday every one is likely to see the ups and downs in his graph of riches. It is Vishnu that retrives the real wealth back to His abode Vaikuntha, and even punishes anyone for his accumulation of beyond-the-means-wealth. The burning of Lanka by Hanuma in Sundara Kanda is an example to this.

▼

In Lakshmi tantra , a Vaishnavaite āgama text, She that unrestrainable Goddess Lakshmi declares Herself, as nārāyaṇī, vaiṣṇavī i.e., a coequal of Vishnu, in the play of the universe i.e., in God's līla, saying that: aham nārāyanī nāma sā sattā vaiṣhṇavī parā 'I am indeed nārāyaṇī i.e. Lakshmi, the Supreme Essence of Vishnu... [Lakshmi Tantra 3.1.]

▼

The Hindu mythology bases more on its eighteen Purāna-s, each of the six is rendered to each of the Hindu Trinity, Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. A] Vishnu Purāna-s are: - 1] Vishnu, 2] bhāgavata, 3] nāradīya, 4] garuda, 5] padma. 6] varāha. B] Brahma Purāna-s : -1] Brahma, 2] brahmānda, 3] brahma vaivarta, 4] mārkandeya, 5] bhaviṣya,6] vāmana. C] Shiva Purāna-s : - 1] vāyu, 2] linga, 3] skanda, 4] agni, 5] matsya, 6] kūrma. In all these Purāna-s Goddess Lakshmi is given a laudable place without any sectarian dispute. In the Vaishnavaite Purāna-s, Shiva starts telling the efficacy of Vishnu to Goddess Parvati. While Shaiva mythology places Goddess Parvati, the consort of Shiva, in one half of Shiva's body artha nārīshvara tattva, and Vaishnavaite tradition places Goddess Lakshmi in the heart of Vishnu itself, as if it is a Lotus hridaya kamala. This is to depict the inseparable unison of Universal purusha, ṃale and prakriti , Female, the seed and field.

▼

While Valmiki Ramayana names Rama as Rama, the Padma Purāna etc., mythological texts bring in the Vedic texts and prefix shrī of shrī sūkta of Rig Veda khila 5-87, which is a feminine counterpart of puruṣa sūkta of Rig Veda 10-90, for parama purusha i.e., Vishnu or Narayana, and thus call Rama of Valmiki as shrī rāma. Vaishnavaite mythologies take hold of these and other Vedic references to Vishnu, and treat Vishnu / Narayana and Lakshmi as the inseparable divine couple, who maintain this Universe. The prefix shrī means as 'one who takes delight in shrī i.e., Lakshmi, meaning wealth, wealth of any kind. shrī riti prathamam nāma lakshmyā and ' if a is the first name of Vishnu, shrī is the first name of Lakshmi. Thus, primarily eight kinds of wealth are established, to be associated with Goddess Lakshmi. They are 1] ādi Lakshmi Wealth a priori 2] dhānya Lakshmi Cereal Wealth 3] Dhairya Lakshmi Wealth of Courage 4] gaja Laksmi Elephant Wealth, i.e., Elephantine Wealth of all animals and livestock, santāna Lakshmi Wealth of Progeny, 6] vijaya Lakshmi, Wealth of Victory, 7] vidyā Lakshmi Wealth of Education, 8] dhana Lakshmi ṃonetary Wealth. And any thing that need be affluent gets the auspicious prefix shrī or suffix lakshmi, and called rājya Lakshmi , Wealth of Empire, bhāgya lakshmi Wealth of Fortune, and the like...

▼

The play of Goddess Lakshmi in the form of Seetha is Ramayana. As such, her absence from Rama caused Rama to travel up to Lanka, annihilate the evil, and retrieve her. Seetha herself expresses her anguish about Rama's indulgence in a Supreme welfare-state, than in herself, as ṃahākavi Kalidasa, in his epical poem Raghu Vamsha, and depicts what this bhāgya lakshmi has to say, when Rama, basing on some satirical remarks of his lowly subject, deserts Seetha in favour of rājya lakshmi when Seetha said as below:

upasthitām pūrvam apāsya lakṣhmīm vanam mayā sārthamasi prapannaḥ |
tad āspadam prāpya tayāti roṣhāt soDhā asmi na tad bhavane vasantī || raghuvamsha

▼

"Earlier you refused the kingdom-wealth, rājya lakshmi and came to forests, where I was with you. Now that, that kingdom-wealth, rājya lakshmi on winning your affection is jealous of me, who am your bhāgya lakshmi 'fortune-wealth...' and she does not let me live in your palace." And thus, Seetha departs Rama to forests, at the fag end of Ramayana.

▼

Valmiki declares Ramayana is sīthāyāḥ charitam mahān... 'Seetha's impeccable conduct...' by giving her an unusual birth from the furrow of the plough, and in the end she exits from this world into the same soil, where the earth is called vasundhara, which cleaves under Seetha's feet, and takes Seetha into her womb again. As such, the reason for Seetha to be there in that furrow as baby is said to be a vow of Goddess Lakshmi, in her earlier incarnation as Vedavati. The mythologies go on to say that Vedavati is the brainchild of a sage called Kushadhwaja, and he decides to give Vedavati in marriage none less than Vishnu. But in his lifetime, it is an unfulfilled desire. Then Vedavati starts a rigorous penance to achieve her father's wish. When she is at her culmination point of her penance, Ravana, passing that way in sky sees this beautiful lady, nears, and disturbs her penance. Vedavati coming out of her meditation, curses Ravana saying that she will reincarnate herself, to destroy Ravana and his entire dynasty. Then she causes a yogic fire and immolates herself in it. And Vedavati is reborn as Seetha of Ramayana, in an unusual way.

▼

Here ānanda rāmāyaṇa has an interesting parable. Once there was a king named Padmāksha who wanted Lakshmi as his daughter. On practising ascesis Vishnu appears and gives a fruit called mātulunga phala, and girl emerges from out of that fruit, and she is named as Padma. But greedy to possess her, all the wooing kings war with Padmāksha and his entire family is ruined and Padma jumps into fire and self immolates herself. Later when Vishnu's māya comes out of an altar of fire, sits in her meditation, Ravana sees her and wants to abduct her. Of course, she is otherwise said to be Vedavati in other texts. But again, she enters the fire altar and reduces herself to ashes. Even then, Ravana searches in those ashes for her. In there, he gets five diamonds of high quality. He comes to Lanka and paces those diamonds in a casket and jovially presents them to his wife ṃandodari. When ṃandodari could not lift the casket Ravana lifts it and opens its lid, as he lifted ṃt. Kailash. When the casket is opened, ṃandodari finds a baby girl in it and recognises her to be Goddess Lakshmi. Then they consult their teachers about that baby's arrival. Those teaches wishing good for Ravana, advise to get rid off this girl immediately, for he is Goddess Lakshmi, arrived here only to end Ravana and his dynasty. Then ṃandodari orders his servants to carry away this baby in a casket by an aircraft and get rid off it.

▼

But Ravana rushes after the girl with a sword, to put that girl to sword. Empress ṃandodari pacifies Ravana and says "Why purchase a later time death now itself at the hand of this baby... let that the casket be buried..." Ravana agrees. ṃandodari also curses this girl saying, "this faithless girl, [for wealth is unfaithful,] will thrive only in a house, where the householder has his senses conquered, and who being an emperor lives like a perfect hermit, and who though wealthy and supreme by himself, will care nothing for the riches but view whole of the world and people as his own soul, with an impartial attitude..." Thus this casket is buried in the fields of King Janaka's empire by demons, clandestinely. ṃandodari thought that such a person is an impossibility to take birth in this mortal world, to foster this buried girl, and thus presumed her curse to be twisty. But there is King Janaka with all the above attributes. A king without ego, wealthy but living simple, childless, yet does not crave for one, like King Dasharatha. Hence, he is called rājarṣi a saintly king. Seetha's birth is to be limitedly understood, as said by Janaka in this chapter. Otherwise, the nuances about the birth of Seetha are to be viewed through the viewfinders of mythologies, legends, and above all, through the viewfinders of tradition... but not in the vast of epical poetry, as Valmiki himself has undocumented it...

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye bāla kāṇḍe ṣhaṭ ṣhaṣhṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 66th chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
