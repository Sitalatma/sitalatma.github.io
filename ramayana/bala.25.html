Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

chapter [Sarga] 25
Verses converted to UṭF-8 - Sep 2009

Introduction

Sage Vishvamitra narrates the birth, marriage and curse of demoness Tataka, and bids Rama to eliminate her menace without any hesitation of killing a female, for she is atrocious towards the society.


Sloka 1

atha tasya aprameyasya muner vacanam uttamam |
śrutvā puruṣa śārdūlaḥ pratyuvāca śubhām giram || 1-25-1

1. a prameyasya = sage with inestimable power; tasya muneḥ uttamam vachanam shrutvā = worthy, words, on hearing; puruṣa shārdūla = manly-tiger - Rama; tataḥ = then; shubām giram = with estimable, words; prati uvācha = in turn, said - replied.

On hearing the worthy words of that sage with inestimable power, then the manly-tiger Rama replied him with estimable words. [1-25-1]

Sloka 2

alpa vīryā yadā yakṣī śrūyate munipuṅgava |
katham nāga sahasrasya dhārayati abalā balam || 1-25-2

2. muni pungava = oh, sage, the eminent; yakshī [yakshāḥ] alpa vīryā = female-yakshī [all yaksha-s,] trivial, in strength; yadā shrūyate [shrūyante] = while, we hear so  it is said; a balā = without, strength - woman frail by nature; nāga sahasrasya balam = elephants, of a thousand, strength; katham dhārayatī = how, she exerts.

"It is said that yaksha's strength is trivial, and this is an yakshī, more so a female, how then this yakshī frail by her femineity can exert the strength of a thousand elephants? [1-25-2]

Sloka 3

iti ukta,m vacanam śrutvā rāghavasya amita ojasā |
harṣayan ślakṣṇayā vacā sa lakṣmaṇam arindamam || 1-25-3
viśvāmitro.abravīt vākyam śṛṇu yena balotkaṭā |
vara dāna kṛtam vīryam dhārayati abalā balam || 1-25-4

3, 4. iti uktvā = thus, one who is spoken - Vishvamitra; amita ojasā rāghavasya = one with illimitable, dynamism, of Raghava; vacanam shrutvā = sentence, on hearing; Vishvamitra; harṣayan = cheering them up; abravīt vākyam = said, sentence; arindamam sa lakshmaṇam = to enemy-subduer [Rama,] along with, Lakshmana; shlakshṇayā vacā = with pleasant, words; a balā = female; yena bala utkaṭā = by which, [as to how] by strength, outstanding; shruṇu = you listen; vara dāna kṛitam vīryam = boon, bestowal, made - obtained; balam dhāryati = strength, she assumes.

On hearing the sentence of Raghava with illimitable dynamism, Vishvamitra said this sentence to the enemy subduer Rama along with Lakshmana, cheering them up with his pleasant words, "Listen by which a female became outstanding by her strength... she assumes strength by the bestowal of a boon. [1-25-3, 4]

Sloka 5

pūrvam āsīt mahā yakṣaḥ suketur nāma vīryavān |
anapatyaḥ śubhācāraḥ sa ca tepe mahat tapaḥ || 1-25-5

5. pūrvam = once upon a time; sukethuḥ nāma vīryavān = ṣuketu, named, formidable one; mahā yakshaḥ āsīt = great, yaksha, was there; anapatyaḥ saḥ childless, he; subha āchāraḥ = with devout, conduct; mahat tapaḥ tepe = great, ascesis, he performed.

"Once there was a formidable yaksha named Suketu, but he was childless, and he who had devout conduct performed a great ascesis. [1-25-5]

Sloka 6

pitāmahaḥ tu suprītaḥ tasya yakṣapateḥ tadā |
kanyā ratnam dadau rāma tāṭakām nāma nāmataḥ || 1-25-6

6. Rama; tadā = then; pitā maha suprītaḥ = Forefather Brahma, is gladdened; tasya yaksha pateḥ = to him, yaksha's, lord; namataḥ tāṭakām nama = by name, ṭataka, renowned as; kanya ratnam dadau = girl, gemlike, gave.

"Gladdening at his ascesis, Rama, then the Forefather Brahma gave him a gemlike daughter who is renowned by the name of Tataka. [1-25-6]

Sloka 7

dadau nāga sahasrasya balam ca asyāḥ pitāmahaḥ |
na tu eva putram yakṣāya dadau ca asau mahāyaśāḥ || 1-25-7

7. pitāmahaḥ = Forefather Brahma; asyāḥ = to her; nāga sahasrasya balam cha = elephants, thousand, strength, also; dadau = gave; mahā yashāḥ = highly, remarkable Brahma; asau yakshāya putram tu = to that, yaksha, son, but; na eva dadau ca = not, that way, given, also; [as male child will be more hazardous.]

"Forefather Brahma also gave the strength of a thousand elephants to her, but he that highly remarkable Brahma did not give a son to that yaksha, anticipating male to be more hazardous. [1-25-7]

Sloka 8

tām tu bālām vivardhantīm rūpa yauvana śālinīm |
jaṃbha putrāya sundāya dadau bhāryām yaśasvinīm || 1-25-8

8. bālam vi vardhantīm = girl, well, growing up; rūpa yauvana shalinīm = beauty, youth, one having them; yashashvinīm = bright one; tām = her; jambha putrāya = to Jambha's, son; sundāya = to ṣunda; bhāryām dadau = as wife, [her father ṣuketu] gave.

"When that bright girl is growing up into a youthful beauty her father Suketu gave her to Jambha's son Sunda as wife. [1-25-8]

Sloka 9

kasyacit tu atha kālasya yakṣī putram vyajāyata |
mārīcam nāma durdharṣam yaḥ śāpāt rākṣaso.abhavat ||1-25-9

9. atha kasyachit kālasya = then, some, after time; yakshī = that yakshī, ṭataka; mārīcam nāma putram = ṃareecha, named, son; dur dharṣam = indomitable one; vyajāyata = delivered; yaḥ shāpāt rākshasaḥ abhavat = who, by curse, demon, he became.

"Then after sometime that yakshī Tataka delivered an indomitable son named ṃareecha, who by curse became a demon. [1-25-9]

Sloka 10

sunde tu nihate rāma sā agastyam ṛṣi sattamam |
tāṭakā saha putreṇa pradharṣayitum icchati || 1-25-10

3. iti uktvā = thus, one who is spoken - Vishvamitra; amita ojasā rāghavasya = one with illimitable, dynamism, of Raghava; vacanam shrutvā = sentence, on hearing; Vishvamitra; harṣayan = cheering them up; abravīt vākyam = said, sentence; arindamam sa lakshmaṇam = to enemy-subduer [Rama,] along with, Lakshmana; shlakshṇayā vacā = with pleasant, words; a balā = female; yena bala utkaṭā = by which, [as to how] by strength, outstanding; shruṇu = you listen; vara dāna kṛitam vīryam = boon, bestowal, made - obtained; balam dhāryati = strength, she assumes.

"But when Sunda is eliminated by sage Agastya's curse, she that Tataka wished to retaliate that eminent sage along with her son ṃareecha. [1-25-10]

Sloka 11

bhakṣārtham jāta saṃrambhā garjantī sā abhyadhāvata |
āpatantīm tu tām dṛṣṭvā agastyo bhagavān ṛṣiḥ || 1-25-11
rākṣasatvam bhajasva iti mārīcam vyājahāra saḥ |

11, 12a. sā garjantīi = she, roaring; jāta samrambhā = caused, hastiness - with brewing desperation; bhaksha artham abhya dhātava = [as though] to eat away, aiming to, towards [sage,] she rushed; bhagavān ṛiṣiḥ agastyaḥ = godly, saint, Agastya; ā patantīm = coming and falling upon - rushing to attack; tām dṛiṣṭtvā = her, on seeing; rākshsatvam bhajasva = demon-hood, you attain; iti saḥ marīcham vyajahāra = thus, he, to ṃareecha, said cursed.

"With desperation brewing in her she roaringly rushed towards the sage as though to eat him away, and on seeing her rushing along with her son ṃareecha towards him, the godly saint Agastya firstly cursed ṃareecha saying, 'you will attain demon-hood.' [1-25-11, 12a]

Sloka 12

agastyaḥ parama amarṣaḥ tāṭakām api śaptavān || 1-25-12
puruṣādī mahāyakṣī virūpā vikṛta ānanā |
idam rūpam vihāyāśu dāruṇam rūpam astu te || 1-25-13

12b, 13. parama amarṣaḥ agastya = highly, infuriated, Agastya; tāṭakām api shaptavān = ṭataka, even, cursed; āshu idam rūpam vihāya = forthwith, this, form, divested of; vi rūpā = with distorted form; vikṛita ānanā = with contorted, faced; puruṣa adī = man, one who eats; mahā yakshī = oh, great yakshī; te dāruṇam rūpam astu = to you, monstrous, form, shall be.

"Highly infuriated sage Agastya even cursed Tataka saying, 'forthwith divested of this form of a beautiful female, oh, great yakshī, you shall become a man eater with your form distorted, face contorted, and shape monstrous.' [1-25-12b, 13]

Sloka 14

saiṣā śāpa kṛtām arṣā tāṭakā krodha mūrchitā |
deśam utsādayati enam agastyā caritam śubham || 1-25-14

14. shāpa kṛitām amarṣā = by curse, caused, frenzy; sā eṣā = she, thus; that ṭataka; krodha mūrcchitā = in fury, convulsed; agastya charitam = sage Agastya, sauntered; subham enam desham = auspicious one, this, province; utsādayati = is vandalizing.

"Frenzied by the curse and convulsed in fury she that Tataka is thus vandalising this auspicious province, in which sage Agastya once sauntered. [1-25-14]

Sloka 15

enām rāghava durvṛttām yakṣīm parama dāruṇām |
go brāhmaṇa hitārthāya jahi duṣṭa parākramām || 1-25-15

15. Raghava; dur vṛittām = one with horrific, behaviour; panama dāruṇam = highly, atrocious one; duṣṭa parākramām = one with malefic, valour; enām yakshīm = this, yakshī be; go brāhmaṇa hita arthāya = cows, Brahmins, for welfare of; jahi = you eliminate [her.]

"She that highly atrocious one is with horrific behaviour and malefic valour, hence you shall eliminate this yakshī for the welfare of Brahmans and cows. [1-25-15]

Sloka 16

na hi enām śāpa saṃsṛṣṭām kaścit utsahate pumān |
nihantum triṣu lokeṣu tvām ṛte raghu nandana || 1-25-16

16. raghu nandana = oh, Raghu's, legatee; shāpa samsṛiṣṭām = in curse, sheathed - indomitable; enām ni hantum = her, to completely, eliminate; tvām ṛite = you, excepting; kashchit pumān = some man none; triṣu lokeṣu = in three, worlds; na utsahate hi = not, braves her, indeed - possibly.

"Excepting you there is none to eliminate her who is indomitably sheathed in a curse, oh, Raghu's legatee, and none in the three worlds can possibly brave her. [1-25-16]

Sloka 17

na hi te strī vadha kṛte ghṛṇā kāryā narottama |
cātur varṇya hitārthām hi kartavyam rāja sūnunā || 1-25-17

17. nara uttama = among men, the best, Rama; strī vadha = female, killing; kṛite ghṛiṇā = regarding, compassion; na kāryā = no, to be done not, germane; rāja sūnuna = by king's, son; chātur varṇya hita arartham = four, caste-system, welfare, intending; kartavyam = is to be done effectuated.

"Compassion regarding the elimination of a female is ungermane, oh, best one among men, since a prince has to effectuate it intending the welfare of four categories of society. [1-25-17]

Sloka 18

nṛśaṃsam anṛśaṃsam vā prajā rakṣaṇa kāraṇāt |
pātakam vā sadoṣam vā kartavyam rakṣatā sadā || 1-25-18

18. rakshata = [to a king] who is protector [of subjects]; prajā rakshaṇa kāraṇāt = people, guarding, for the reason of; nṛishamsam vā = ruthless, either; a nṛishamsham vā = humane, or; pātakam vā = sinful, or; sa doṣam vā = with, vilification, or; that deed; sadā kartavyam = always, is to be done.

"For the reason of protecting people, a protector always has to execute such deed, either it is ruthless or humane, sinful or vilifying. [1-25-18]

Sloka 19

rājya bhāra niyuktānām eṣa dharmaḥ sanātanaḥ |
adharṃyām jahi kākutstha dharmo hi asyām na vidyate || 1-25-19

19. rājya bhāra niyuktānām = kingdom's, burde, to those that are nominated; eṣa sanātanaḥ dharma = this is, age-old, duty; kakutstha = Rama; a dharmyām jahi = one with infamy, eliminate; asyām dharmaḥ na vidyate hi = in her, goodness, not, evident, isn't it.

"To the nominee who bears the burden of kingdom this is the age-old duty, and hence oh, Rama, the legatee of Kakutstha, eliminate this infamy, as goodness is inevident in her, isn't it. [1-25-19]

Sloka 20

śrūyate hi purā śakro virocana sutām nṛpa |
pṛthivīm hantum icchantīm mantharām abhyasūdayat || 1-25-20

20. nṛi pa = oh, people, protector; purā pṛithivīm hantum icchantīm = once, Earth, to annihilate, she who wished; virochana sutām mantharām = Virochana's, daughter, ṃanthara [namesake of this epic's ṃanthara]; shakraḥ abhyasūdayat = Indra, eliminated; shrūyate hi = thus heard, isn't it.

"Oh, Rama, the protector of people, we have heard that Indra once eliminated ṃanthara, the daughter of Virochana, when she wished to annihilate earth, haven't we. [1-25-20]

Sloka 21

viṣṇunā ca purā rāma bhṛgu patnī pativratā |
anindram lokam icchantī kāvyamātā niṣūditā || 1-25-21

21. Rama; purā = once; lokam = world; an indram = without, Indra; icchantī = she who wished; pativratā bṛrigu patnī = husband-devote, sage Bhrigu's, wife; kāvya mātā api = sage ṣhukra's, mother, even; viṣṇuna nishūditā = by Vishnu, she is wiped out.

"And Rama, once Vishnu wiped out even the wife of sage Bhrigu and sage Shukracarya's mother when she wished the world to become one without a governing factor, namely Indra. [1-25-21]

Sloka 22

etaiḥ ca anyaiḥ ca bahubhī rājaputraiḥ mahātmabhiḥ |
adharma sahitā nāryo hatāḥ puruṣasattamaiḥ |
tasmād enām ghṛṇām tyaktvā jahi mat śāsanān nṛpa || 1-25-22

22. raja putraiḥ = oh, prince; etaiḥ mahātmabhiḥ = by these, great souls; anyaiḥ cha = by others, also; puruṣa sattamaiḥ = by personalities, celebrated ones; bahubhī a dharma sahitā nāryaḥ = many of the, un, righteousness, who have, females; hatāḥ = were eliminated; tasmāt = therefore; nṛi pa = oh, people protector; mat shāsanāt = by my, decree; ghṛiṇām tyaktvā = compassion, leaving off; enām jahi = her, you eliminate.

"Oh, prince, these great souls and many other celebrated personalities have eliminated females who deported themselves with unrighteousness, and hence, oh, Rama, the protector of people, by my decree you leave off compassion and eliminate her." Thus said sage Vishvamitra to Rama regarding Tataka's elimination. [1-25-22]

.

Elimination of females - right or wrong

▼

This is a much discussed topic in Ramayana. Rama's adventures start with the killing of a female. While scriptures say that killing a female is sinful, is this killing of a female righteous or wrongful is the topic for discussion. It is said in answer that there is no harm to dharma since unrighteousness is eliminable though existing in the form of a female. duṣhṭa strī vadho doṣho n astīti shāstreṇa na anyena - dharmakūtam ||

▼

And Vishnu Purana says that eliminating one evildoer for the sake of many innocent is neither sin nor a crime of course, to the kings and rulers: easmin atra nidhanam prāpite duṣhṭakāriṇi | bahūnām bhavati kṣhemaḥ tasya puṇyaprado vadhaḥ || - vishṇu purāna 1-13-73-74

▼

For the sake many, that too defenceless rural people, it is the duty of the king to eliminate whether it is a male or a female if involved in atrocities: dharmārtha pūrvakam tāDakā vadhe doṣho na asti - -- puṇyam eva bhaviṣhyati iti tātparyeṇa maharṣhi vishvāmitreṇa uktam | idānīmtanairapi bahūnām kṣhemārtham ekasya hanane doṣho na asti iti jñeyam | visheṣhitaḥ rājñā janapada rakṣhaṇa dīkṣhitena avadhya vadho api kartavya ityapi sūcitam || --- dharmākūtam
