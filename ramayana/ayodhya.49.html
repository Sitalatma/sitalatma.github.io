Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 49
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Having traveled for a pretty long distance, Rama crossed Vedasruti, Gomati and Syandika rivers. He presses forward in the chariot talking with Sumantra.

Sloka 1

rāmaḥ api rātri śeṣeṇa tena eva mahad antaram |
jagāma puruṣa vyāghraḥ pitur ājñām anusmaran || 2-49-1

1. rāmo.api = Also Rama; purushhavyāghraḥ = the tiger among men; anusmaran = remembering; āG^yām = the command; pituḥ = of his father; jagāma = obtained; mahat = a long; antaram = distance; tena = (during) that; rātrisesheṇa = rest of the night.

Rama the tiger among men, revolving in his mind the command of his father, covered a long distance during the rest of the night.

Sloka 2

tathaiva gaccataḥ tasya vyapāyāt rajanī śivā |
upāsya sa śivām saṃdhyām viṣaya antam vyagāhata || 2-49-2

2. shivā = delightful; rajanī = night; vyapāyat = passed away; tasya = (while) Rama; gachchhataḥ eva = was traveling; tathā = in that way. saḥ = Rama; upāsya = having worshipped; shivam = the blissful; sandhyām = (morning) twilight; vyagāhata = passed beyond; vishhayāntam = the boundary of that country.

While Rama was traveling with the same alacrity, that delightful night passed away. Having worshipped the blissful morning twilight, he passed beyond the boundary of that country.

Sloka 3

grāmān vikṛṣṭa sīmān tān puṣpitāni vanāni ca |
paśyann atiyayau śīghram śaraiḥ iva haya uttamaiḥ || 2-49-3
śṛṇvan vāco manuṣyāṇām grāma samvāsa vāsinām |

3. pashyan = seeing; grāmān = villages; vikṛishhṭa sīmānān = whose outskirts have been filled; vanāmicha = and woodlands; pushhpitāni = laden with blossoms; shṛiṇvan = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; manushhyāṇām = of men; grāma samvāsa vāsinām = dwelling together in mist of the village; shīghram = proceeded apace; shanairiva = as though slowly; atiyayon = and passed over those villages.

Seeing villages, whose outskirts have been tilled and the woodlands laden with blossoms and hearing as follows the words of men dwelling together in the midst of the village, Rama proceeded apace in those excellent horses as though slowly (engrossed as he was in enjoying the sights).

Sloka 4

rājānam dhig daśaratham kāmasya vaśam āgatam || 2-49-4
hā nṛśaṃsa adya kaikeyī pāpā pāpa anubandhinī |
tīkṣṇā sambhinna maryādā tīkṣṇe karmaṇi vartate || 2-49-5
yā putram īdṛśam rājñaḥ pravāsayati dhārmikam |
vana vāse mahā prājñam sānukrośam atandritam || 2-49-6

4;5;6. "dhik = woe unto; rājānām = the king Dasaratha; vashamāgatam = who fell into the clutches; kāmasya = of concupiscence. hā = Alas! Kaikeyi = Kaikeyi; nṛishamasā = the cruel; pāpā = and the sinful; adya = now; pāpānubandhinī = is following the sin. Yā = which Kaikeyi; pravāsayati = is sending to exile; īdṛisham = such; rāG^yaḥputram = prince Rama; dhārmikam = the pious man; mahāprāG^yam = a great intellectual; sānukrosham = the compassionate man; jitendriyam = he who has subdued senses; tīkshhṇā = that hot tempered Kaikeyi; vartate = is abiding; tīkshhṇa karmaṇi = in a rude action; sambhinna maryādā = transgressing the bounds of propriety.

"Woe unto the king Dasaratha who fell into the clutches of concupiscence. Alas! Kaikeyi the cruel and the sinful one now is still engaged in a cruel game. She is sending to exile the prince Rama, the pious man, the great intellectual, the compassionate man and he who subdued the senses. That hot-tempered Kaikeyi is behaving in a rude manner, transgressing the bounds of propriety"

Sloka 7

katham nāma mahābhāgā sītā janakanandinī |
sadā sukheṣvabhiratā duḥkhānyanubhaviṣyati || 2-49-7

7. "katham nāma = how; sīta = ṣita;mahābhāgā = the venerable woman; janaka nandinī = the daughter of king Janaka; abhirathā = who was delighted; sadā = always; sukheshhu = in(homely)comforts; anubhavishhyati = can experience; duḥkhāni = difficulties (in the forest)?"

"How Sita the venerable woman, the daughter of Janaka, who was delighted always in homely comforts can now experience hardships in the forest?"

Sloka 8

aho daśaratho rājā nisnehaḥ svasuta priyam |
prajānāmanagham rāmam parityaktumihecchati || 2-49-8

8. "rajā = the king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; nisnehaḥ = having no love; svasutam = for his son; iha = now; ichchhati = wants; parityaktum = to abandon; rāmam = Rama; priyam = who is beloved; prajānam = to his people; anagham = and faultless; aho = what a surprise!"

"What a surprise! The king Dasaratha, having no love for his son, now wants to abandon Rama who is so beloved to the people and is even faultless."

Sloka 9

etā vāco manuṣyāṇām grāma samvāsa vāsinām |
śṛṇvann ati yayau vīraḥ kosalān kosala īśvaraḥ || 2-49-9

9. shruṇvā = hearing; etāḥ = these; vāchaḥ = words; manushhyāṇām = of people; grāma samvāsa vāsinām = residing in villages and hamlets; vīrah = the heroic; kosaleshvaraḥ = prince of Kosala; atiyayon = crossed the boundaries; kosalān = of Kosala state.

Hearing these words of people residing in villages and hamlets, Rama the heroic prince of Kosala crossed the boundaries of Kosala state.

Sloka 10

tataḥ veda śrutim nāma śiva vāri vahām nadīm |
uttīrya abhimukhaḥ prāyāt agastya adhyuṣitām diśam || 2-49-10

10. uthīrya = having crossed; nadīm = the river; shiva vārivahām = of auspicious waters; vedashrutim nāma = named Vedashruti; tataḥ prāyāt = he then proceeded forth; abhimukhaḥ = facing; disham = the quarter; agastyādhyushhitām = occupied by the ṣage Agastya.

Having crossed the river of auspicious waters called Vedashruti, Rama then stretched forth, facing the quarter occupied by Sage Agastya.

Sloka 11

gatvā tu suciram kālam tataḥ śīta jalām nadīm |
gomatīm goyuta anūpām atarat sāgaram gamām || 2-49-11

11. gatvā = after traveling; suchiram kālam = for a pretty time; tataḥ = from there; atarat = (Rama) crossed; nadīm = the river; gomatīm = called Gomati; shivajalām = having beautiful waters; goyutā nūpam = whose banks were adorned with cows; sāgarangamām = and headed towards the sea.

After traveling a pretty long time from there, Rama crossed the river Gomati having beautiful waters, whose banks were adorned with cows and which headed towards the sea.

Sloka 12

gomatīm ca api atikramya rāghavaḥ śīghragaiḥ hayaiḥ |
mayūra haṃsa abhirutām tatāra syandikām nadīm || 2-49-12

12. rāghavaḥ = Rama;atikramya = crossing; gomatīmcha api = Gomati river too; hayaiḥ = by horses; shīghragaiḥ = which are swift-moving; tatāra = crossed; nadīm = the river; syandikām = called ṣyandika; mayūra hamsābhirutām = resounding with howls of peacocks and swans.

Reaching the other bank of Gomati river with the help of the swift moving horses, Rama crossed the river called Syandika which had resounded with howls of peacocks and swans.

Sloka 13

sa mahīm manunā rājñā dattām ikṣvākave purā |
sphītām rāṣṭra āvṛtām rāmaḥ vaidehīm anvadarśayat || 2-49-13

13. rāmaḥ = that Rama; anvadarshayat = showed; vaidehīm = to ṣita; mahīm = (that) land; dattām = given; rāG^yā = by the king; manunā = ṃanu; ikshvākave = to Ikshvaku; purā = long ago; rāshhṭrāvṛtām = filled with territories; sphītām = many in number.

The said Rama showed to Sita the land (of Kosala, the southern boundary of which was defined by Syandika river)given long ago by the king ṃanu to Ikshvaku and which was bounded by many territories.

Sloka 14

sūtaiti eva ca ābhāṣya sārathim tam abhīkṣṇaśaḥ |
haṃsa matta svaraḥ śrīmān uvāca puruṣa ṛṣabhaḥ || 2-49-14

14. shrīmān = the glorious Rama; purushharshhabhaḥ = the foremost among men; hamsa matta svaraḥ = whose voice resembled the cackling of a swan in rut; ābhāshhya = addressed; tam sārathim = that charioteer; abhīkshhṇashaḥ = with great affection; sūta ityeva = in the words "Oh; charioteer!" uvācha = (and) spoke( as follows) :

The glorious Rama, the foremost among men, whose voice resembled the cackling of a swan in rut, addressed the charioteer with great affection, in the words "Oh, charioteer!" and spoke as follows :

Sloka 15

kadā aham punar āgamya sarayvāḥ puṣpite vane |
mṛgayām paryāṭaṣyāmi mātrā pitrā ca samgataḥ || 2-49-15

15. " kadā = when; punarāgamya = coming back; sangataḥ = and united; mātrā = with mother; pitrācha = and father; paryaṭishhyāmi = shall I roam; mṛigayām = hunting; vane = in the forest; sarayvāḥ = bordering on ṣarayu river; pushhpite = and laden with blossoms?"

"When, coming back and united with my mother and father, shall I roam hunting in the forest, bordering on Sarayu river and laden with blossoms?"

Sloka 16

na atyartham abhikānkṣāmi mṛgayām sarayū vane |
ratir hi eṣā atulā loke rāja ṛṣi gaṇa sammatā || 2-49-16
rājarṣīṇām hi loke.asmin ratyartham mṛgayā vane |
kāle kṛtām tām manujairdhanvināmabhikāṅkṣitām || 2-49-17

16;17. eshhā = this hunting; ratirhi = is a delightful sport; atulā = made much of; loke = in the world; rājarshhi gaṇasammatā = approved by a host of royal sages; nābhikāṇkshhāmi = I do not hanker; atyartham = much; mṛigayām = of hunting; sarayūvane = in the woodlands bordering in ṣarayu . mṛigayā = hunting; vane = in the forest; ratyarthamhi = is for gratification indeed; rājarshhīṇām = of royal sages; asmin loke = in this world ; .kṛitām = (It was) adopted; kāle = at times; manujaiḥ = by the sons of ṃanu; abhikāṇkshhitām = and sought after by; dhanvinām = bowmen. tām = ṭhat hunting;(I do not long for it excessively).

"I do not hanker much after hunting in the woodlands bordering on Sarayu river. In fact it is a delightful spot, made much of in the world by hosts of royal sages. Hunting in the forest is indeed for gratification of royal sages in this world. At times; the bow- men adopted it. But I do not long for it excessively."

Sloka 18

sa tam adhvānam aikṣvākaḥ sūtam madhurayā girā
tam tam artham abhipretya yayauvākyam udīrayan || 2-49-18

18. udīrayan = uttering; madhurayā = in sweet; girā = voice; sūtam = to the charioteer; tam tam = on various; artham = topics; abhipretya = dearer; (to him) saḥ ikshhvākaḥ = that Rama; yayou = went; adhvānam = along that route.

Uttering in sweet voice to the charioteer on various topics dearer to him, Rama advanced thus along that route.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekonapañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 49th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
