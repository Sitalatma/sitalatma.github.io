Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of
chapter [Sarga] 98
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

When ṃahaparshva enters the battle-field and torments the monkeys, Angada comes to their rescue and hits him. Jambavan also enters the field and breaks the chariot and the horses of ṃahaparshva. Then, Angada knocks the bow and the helmet of ṃahaparshva, as they fall down. Finally, ṃahaparshva falls dead, succumbing to a forcible blow of Angada's fist.

1. samīkṣhya = seeing; mahodara = ṃahodara; nihate = killed; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; mahāpārshvaḥ = ṃahaparshva; krodha samrakta lochanaḥ = with his red-blood eyes in anger; atha = then; kṣhobhayāmāsa = stirred up; bhīmām = the terrific; chamūm = army; aṇ^gadasya = of Angada; mārgaṇaiḥ = with his arrows.

Seeing ṃahodara killed by Sugreeva, the mighty ṃahaparsva for his part with his red-blood eyes through anger, forthwith stirred up the terrific army of Angada with his arrows.
