Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 22
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Rama further tries to pacify Lakshmana and tells him that his stay in forest is the will of gods. He also wants all the celebration for his coronation to be stopped.

Sloka 1

atha tam vyathayā dīnam saviśeṣam amarṣitam |
śvasantam iva nāga indram roṣa visphārita īkṣaṇam || 2-22-1
āsadya rāmaḥ saumitrim suhRdam bhrātaram priyam |
uvāca idam sa dhairyeṇa dhārayan sattvam ātmavān || 2-22-2

1;2. atha = afterwards; ātmavān = the self composed; saḥ ramaḥ = that Rama; dhārayan = by controlling; sattvam = mind dhairyeṇa = with courage; āsādya = approached; suhṛidam = the kind hearted; priyam bhrātaram = and affectionate brother; soumitrim = Lakshmana; dīnam = who was distressed with agony; savisheshham = very much; amarishhitam = angry; nāgendramiva = like king cobra; shvasantam = doing hissing; roshha visphāritatekshhaṇam = having eyes dilated with wrath; uvācha = spoke; idayam = these words.

Afterwards ,the self composed Rama by controlling his thoughts with courage ,approached the kind -hearted and affectionate brother Lakshmana who was distressed with agony who was very much angry like a hissing king cobra ,with his eyes dilated with wrath and spoke the following words.

Sloka 3

nigṛhya roṣaṃ śokaṃ ca khairyamāśritya kevalam |
avamānam nirasyemam gṛhītvā harṣamuttamam || 2-22-3
upakluptam hi yatkiṃcidabhiṣekārthamadya me |
srvam visarjaya kṣipram kuru kāryam niratyayam || 2-22-4

3;4. nigṛiyya = ḥaving held back; rosham = anger; shokamcha = and grief; āshritya = take refuge; dhairyam = in courage; kavalam = alone; nirasya = ḥaving expelled; imani = this; avamānam = insult; gṛihītvā = receive; uttamam = great; harshham = joy ! visarjaya = Abandon; sarvam = all; yatkinchit = that is; upakluptam = arranged; adya = today; me abhishhekārtham = for my coronation; kuru = Do; kshhipram = immediately; kāryam = action; nirtyayam = that is faultless."

" ḥold back grief and anger. Forget this insult, by taking refuge in courage alone. Obtain great joy! Abandon all these arrangements made today for my coronation and immediately take up action that is faultless ."

Sloka 5

saumitre yo abhiṣeka arthe mama sambhāra sambhramaḥ |
abhiṣeka nivṛtti arthe so astu sambhāra sambhramaḥ || 2-22-5

5. soumitre = Oh;Lakshmana; yaḥ = which; sambhāra sambhramaḥ = zeal for preparations; mama abhishhekārthe = for my coronation; saḥ = that; sambhāra sambhramaḥ = zeal for preparations; astu = be ; abhishheka nivṛittyarthe = for terminating coronation."

"Oh, Lakshmana! ṣhow the same zeal now in terminating my coronation as the zeal shown earlier in preparations for my coronation."

Sloka 6

yasyā mad abhiṣeka artham mānasam paritapyate |
mātā naḥ sā yathā na syāt saviśankā tathā kuru || 2-22-6

6. yasyāḥ = which mother's; mānasam = mind ; paritapyate = is angvished; madabhishhekārthe = about my coronation; sā = such; me matā = my mother (Kaikeyi); savishaṇkā = having apprehension; yathā = in which manner; nasyāt = it should not occur; tathā = in that matter ; kuru = do it ."

"ṃy mother Kaikeyi is still agonized about my coronation. Behave in such a way that no apprehension comes to her mind on this matter."

Sloka 7

tasyāḥ śankāmayam duhkham muhūrtam api na utsahe |
manasi pratisamjātam saumitre aham upekṣitum || 2-22-7

7. "soumitre = Oh; Lakshmana! aham = I ; notsahe = do not wish; upekshhitum = to disregard ; duḥkham = pain; shaṇkāmayam = in the form of suspision; pratisanjātamapi = created; manasi = in mind ; tasyāḥ = of her; muhūrtamapi = even for a moment."

"Oh, Lakshmana! I do not wish to disregard the painful doubt in her mind even for a moment".

Sloka 8

na buddhi pūrvam na abuddham smarāmi iha kadācana |
mātṛṇām vā pitur vāham kṛtam alpam ca vipriyam || 2-22-8

8. "aham = I; na smarāmi = do not remember; kṛitam = to have done; alpam = small; vipriyam cha = offensive thing indeed; iha = here ; kadāchana = at any time; buddhipūrvam = intentionally; mātṛīṇāmvā = either to mothers; piturvā = or to fathers; na = nor do I remember; abuddham = even un intentional action.

"I do not remember to have done even a small disagreeable thing here at any time to my mothers or to my father, either intentionally or unintentionally."

Sloka 9

satyaḥ satya abhisaṃdhaḥ ca nityam satya parākramaḥ |
para loka bhayāt bhītaḥ nirbhayo astu pitā mama || 2-22-9

9. astu = Let it be ; mama pitā = my father; satyaḥ = who is truthfull; satya parākramaḥ = who is truly mighty; bhītaḥ = who is afraid of; paralokabhayāt = the fear of the other world; nityam = always; satyābhisandhaḥ = true to his promise; nirbhayaḥ = fearless".

"Let the promise made by my father ,who is truthful, who is truly mighty and who is afraid of the fear of the other world, become true .Let him be fearless."

Sloka 10

tasya api hi bhaved asmin karmaṇi apratisamhRte |
satyam na iti manaḥ tāpaḥ tasya tāpaḥ tapec ca mām || 2-22-10

10. tasyāpi = ṭo him also; manastāpaḥ = anguish; bhavet = will arise; iti = that; satyam na = his word has not come true; asmin karmaṇi apasamhṛite = if this coronation is not with drawn; tasya = his; tapāḥ = anguish; tapechcha = will pain; mām = me."

"Our father will feel anguished that his word has not come true ,if this coronation is not withdrawn. His distress will cause pain to me."

Sloka 11

abhiṣeka vidhānam tu tasmāt samhṛtya lakṣmaṇa |
anvag eva aham iccāmi vanam gantum itaḥ punaḥ || 2-22-11

11. lakshhmana = "Oh; Lakshmana; tasmāt = for that reason; aham = I; ichchāmi = wish; gantum = to go ; vanam = to forest; anvageva = immediately; itaḥ = from here; samhṛitya = by withdrawing; abhishheka vidhānam = coronation arrangement."

"Oh, Lakshmana for that reason, I wish to go to forest immediately from her, by withdrawing, coronation arrangement."

Sloka 12

mama pravrājanāt adya kṛta kṛtyā nṛpātmajā |
sutam bharatam avyagram abhiṣecayitā tataḥ || 2-22-12

12. adya = ṇow; mama pravrajanāt = due to my exile to forest; nṛitakrityā = having; accomplished her purpose; sutam bharatam abhishhechayitā = will get son Bharata coronated; avyagram = coolly; tataḥ = afterwards ."

"ṣoon after my departure to the forest now, Kaikeyi having accomplished her purpose, will coolly get her son coronated."

Sloka 13

mayi cīra ajina dhare jaṭā maṇḍala dhāriṇi |
gate araṇyam ca kaikeyyā bhaviṣyati manaḥ sukham || 2-22-13

13. mayi gate = If I go; araṇyam = to forest; chīrājina dhare = wearing deer skin rags; jāṭāmaṇdale dhāriṇi = wearing twisted locks of hair; kakeyyā = Kaikeyi; bhavishhyato = will become ; manassukham = mentally pleasant."

"If go to forest , wearing deer -skin rags and with my hair twisted together Kaikeyi will have mental peace."

Sloka 14

buddhiḥ praṇītā yena iyam manaḥ ca susamāhitam |
tat tu na arhāmi samkleṣṭum pravrajiṣyāmi māciram || 2-22-14

14. na arhāmi = to cause trouble tam = to such father yena = by whom iyam = this buddhiḥ = decision praṇītam = was taken susamāhitam = with well composed manascha = mind mā = without chiram = delay."

"I do not want to create trouble to my father, who has taken this hard decision in his mind . Hence, I will go to the forest at once

Sloka 15

kṛta antaḥ tu eva saumitre draṣṭavyo mat pravāsane |
rājyasya ca vitīrṇasya punar eva nivartane || 2-22-15

15. soumitre = Oh;Lakshmana! drashhṭavyaḥ = It is perceivable kṛitāntastyaiva = as destiny mivartanecha = in taking back punaraiva = again rājyasya = of kingdom vitīrṇasya = given. ṃatpravāsane = and in sending me to exile".

"Oh, Lakshmana! Look at it as destiny which has taken back kingdom given to me and which is sending me to exile"

Sloka 16

kaikeyyāḥ pratipattir hi katham syān mama pīḍane |
yadi bhāvo na daivo ayam kṛta anta vihitaḥ bhavet || 2-22-16

16. na bhavet yati = If not for kṛitānta vihitaḥ = making of destiny; ayam = this bhāvaḥ = thought tasyāḥ = in her; katham = how syāt = perhaps pratipattiḥ = resolution kaikeyyāḥ = to Kaikeyi pīdane = to herass mama = me

-"If not making of destiny to create such a thought in Kaikeyi, how come she resolved to harass?"

Sloka 17

jānāsi hi yathā saumya na mātṛṣu mama antaram |
bhūta pūrvam viśeṣo vā tasyā mayi sute api vā || 2-22-17

17. soumya = Oh; gentleman jānāsi = you know ; yathā = how na bhūtapūrvam = that in the past; there was no antaram = discrimination mama = to me mātṛishu = among mothers. tasyāḥ = to her; visheshhaḥ = particularly mayi = either in me sute api vā = or in her son Bharata."

"You know that I had no discrimination among mothers and Kaikeyi also in the past had no discrimination between me and her son Bharata"

Sloka 18

so abhiṣeka nivṛtti arthaiḥ pravāsa arthaiḥ ca durvacaiḥ |
ugraiḥ vākyaiḥ aham tasyā na anyad daivāt samarthaye || 2-22-18

18. aham = I na samarthaye = do not perceive anyat = any other thing daivāt = than an accident tasyāḥ vākyaiḥ = for her words; abhishheka nivṛittyarthaiḥ = intended for abolishion of my coronation ; pravāsārthaishena = desirous of sending me to exile; ugraiḥ = which are ferocious; durvachaiḥ = and are not to be talked about."

"I do not perceive any other reason than an accident that made Kaikeyi to abolish my coronation , to send me to exile ,and to speak ferocious words ,not to be talked about."

Sloka 19

katham prakṛti sampannā rāja putrī tathā aguṇā |
brūyāt sā prākṛtā iva strī mat pīḍām bhartṛ samnidhau || 2-22-19

19. katham = ḥow sā = she prakṛitisampannā = who is of a perfect nature tathāguṇā = and such good qualities; rājaputrī = who was princess brūyāt = can tell prākṛitā atrīva = like common woman; bhartṛI sannidhou = before husband matpīdām = for my trouble?"

"If it is for not an accidental reason, how Kaikeyi who is of a perfect temperament and who is born in a royal family can tell like a common woman to her husband to create trouble for me ?"

Sloka 20

yad acintyam tu tat daivam bhūteṣv api na hanyate |
vyaktam mayi ca tasyām ca patitaḥ hi viparyayaḥ || 2-22-20

20. "yat = which daivam = destiny achintyam = is unimaginable tat = that na hanyete = can not be counteracted bhūteshhvapi = by any living beings viparyayaḥ = adverse fate patitaḥ = befall; mayicha = in me tasyāmcha = and in her vyaktam = It is clear."

"ṭhe influence of destiny is un imaginable. ṇo living being can counteract its influence. An adverse fate has befallen on me and on her .It is clear now ."

Sloka 21

kaścit daivena saumitre yoddhum utsahate pumān |
yasya na grahaṇam kiṃcit karmaṇo anyatra dṛśyate || 2-22-21

21. "soumitre = Oh ;Lakshmana! Pumān kaḥ = which man utsahate = will be able yoddhum = to fight against daivena = destiny? kanchit = whatever anyatra = other thing na dṛishyate = is not being seen karmaṇaḥ = that to follow its course grahaṇam = accepting yasya = of which destiny."

"Oh, Lakshmana! ṭhere is no means whatsoever to abstain from it otherwise than to follow the course of destiny. Who will be able to fight against the destiny. ?"

Sloka 22

sukha duhkhe bhaya krodhau lābha alābhau bhava abhavau |
yasya kiṃcit tathā bhūtam nanu daivasya karma tat || 2-22-22

22. sukha duḥkhe = pleasure and pain. bhaya krodhou = fear and anger labhālabhou = gain and loss; bhābhāvon = birth and death; yachcha kimchit = what ever other thing tathābhūtam = of such nature tat = that karmanamu = indeed is action daivasya = of destiny

"Pleasure and pain, fear and anger, gain and loss, birth and death and such other things are all the acts of destiny"

Sloka 23

ṛṣayo pyugratapaso daivenābhiprapīḍitāḥ |
utsṛjya niyamām stīvrān bhraśyante kāmamanyubhiḥ || 2-22-23

23. "ṛishhayo api = Even sages; ugra tasaḥ = with severe penance; abhiprapīditā = having harassed daivena = by destiny; utsṛijya = leave aside niyamān = restraint; bhrashyante = get ruined kāmamanyubhiḥ = by lust and anger."

"Even sages, who performed sever penance, having been harassed by destiny leave aside restraint and get ruined by lust and anger."

Sloka 24

asamklpitameveha yadakasmāt pravartate |
nivartyārambhamārabdham nanu daivasya karma tat || 2-22-24

24. "tat = It daivasya karmananu = is indeed act of destiny; nivartya = which prevents ārambham = effort ārabdham = undertaken iha = in this world yat = which pravartate ;is going on; akasmāt = suddenly asamkalpitam iva = and unimaginably."

"It is indeed an act of destiny which suddenly and unimaginably obstructs an action, undertaken in the world ,at the starting point itself."

Sloka 25

etayā tattvayā buddhyā saṃstabhyātmānamātmanā |
vyāhate api abhiṣeke me paritāpo na vidyate || 2-22-25

25. vyāhate api abhishheke = Even if coronation is obstructed; navidyate = ṭhere is no paritāpaḥ = grief me = to me samtabhyātmānamāt manā = since I have restrained the self by the self; etayā buddyā = with this intellect tattvayā = which is real."

"Although my coronation is obstructed, I have no grief since I have restrained the self by myself with real consciousness."

Sloka 26

tasmāt aparitāpaḥ saṃs tvam api anuvidhāya mām |
pratisamhāraya kṣipram ābhiṣecanikīm kriyām || 2-22-26

26. "tasmāt = ḥence tvamapi = you also; anuvidhāyi = in obedience mām = to me; pratisamhārāya = get withdrawn kriyām = the execution abhishhechanikīm = relating to coronation kshhipram = immediately aparitāpaḥ = without agony."

"ḥence ,you also ,like me, get the arrangements for coronation withdrawn immediately without any anguish"

Sloka 27

ebhireva ghaṭaiḥ sarvairabhiṣecanasambhṛtaiḥ |
mama laksmaṇa tāpasye vratasnānam bhaviṣyati || 2-22-27

27. lakshhmana = Oh; Lakshmana! sarvaiḥ = All eohiḥ = these ghataireva = very pots abhishhechana sambṛitaiḥ = arranged for coronation bhavishhyati = become vratasnānam = bath after completing religious vow tāpasye = of asceticism mama = to me."

Oh, Lakshmana! Let all these very pots of water arranged for coronation be used by me for my bath after completing the religious vow of asceticism."

Sloka 28

athavā kim mamaitena rājadravyamayena tu |
uddhṛtam me svayam to yam vratādeśam kariṣyati || 2-22-28

28. "athavā = Otherwise; kim = what use mama = to me etena = this water in pots rājadravyena = which is royal property? toyam = the water uddhṛitam = drawn out svayam = by myself karishhyati = will do vratādesham = imposition of the vow me = to me."

"Otherwise ,why to me this water in these pots ,which is royal property? ṭhe water drawn out by myself ,will be used for my bath after completing the religious vow."

Sloka 29

mā ca lakṣmaṇa samtāpam kārṣīrlakṣmyā viparyaye |
rājyam vā vanavāso vā vanavāso mahodayaḥ || 2-22-29

29. lakshhmaṇa = Oh;Lakshmana! mākārshhīḥ = do not cultivate santāpam = repentence viparyaye = about perverseness lakshhmajāḥ = of Lakshmi;the goddess of wealth rājyam vā = kingdom or vanavāsovā = dwelling in forest; vanavāsaḥ = forest dwelling is indeed mahodayaḥ = final beautitude."

"Oh, Lakshmana! Do not repent about this perverseness of Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth. When you talk of kingdom or dwelling in the forest, this is the final beautitude."

Sloka 30

na lakṣmaṇa asmin mama rājya vighne |
mātā yavīyasy atiśankanīyā |
daiva abhipannā hi vadanti aniṣṭam |
jānāsi daivam ca tathā prabhāvam || 2-22-30

30. "lakshmaṇa = Oh; Lakshmana! yavīyasī mātā = our younger mother na atisaṇkanīyā = is not worthy of suspicion asmin karmavighne = in this obstruction of ceremony. vadatiḥi = she is indeed uttering amishhṭam = the evil daivābhipaunā = being over powered by destiny. jānāsi = You know daivamcha = also the destiny; tathā prabhāvam = which has such influence."

"Oh, Lakshmana! Do not suspect our mother for obstruction of coronation ceremony. ṣhe is uttering evil words, being overpowered by destiny. You are aware of this destiny, which has such influence."

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrrimadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe dvāviṃśaḥ saragaḥ ||

Thus the end of twenty second chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of Srimad Ramayana.
