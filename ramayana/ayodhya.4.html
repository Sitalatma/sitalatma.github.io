Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 4
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dasaratha decides to coronate Rama as the prince the next day. He asks Sumantra to bring Rama to his presence once again. After Rama arrives, Dasaratha expresses to Rama his desire to anoint Rama as a prince the very next day. He cites various bad dreams and inauspicious signs as the reason for his haste. Rama thus informed by his father, goes to his mother's house and informs her of the good news. Kausalya becomes delighted at the news and gives Rama her blessings.

Sloka 1

gateṣvatha nṛpo bhūyaḥ paureṣu saha mantribhiḥ |
mantrayutvā tataścakre niścayajñaḥ sa niścayam || 2-4-1
śva eva puṣyo bhavitā śvo.abhiṣecyastu me sutaḥ |
rāmo rājīvatāmrākṣo yauvarājya iti prabhuḥ || 2-4-2

1;2. atha = after; paureshhu = citizens; gateshhu satsu = had been leaving; nṛipaḥ = king Dasaratha; nishchayaGYaḥ = (who is an excellent) decision maker; prabhuḥ = (and who is) efficient; bhūyaḥ = again; mantrayitvā = conferred; mantribhiḥ saha = with ministers; chakre = made; nishchayam = decision; iti = thus; shvaḥ eva = tomorrow only; bhavitā = will be; pushhyaḥ = Pushya constellation; me sutaḥ = my son; rāmaḥ = Rama; rājīvatāmrākshaḥ = who has eyes like red lotus; abhishhechyaḥ = can be anointed; yauvarājye = for princely hood; shvaḥ = tomorrow.

After the citizens left, Dasaratha who was efficient in taking decisions pertaining to place and time of ceremonies, again conferred with the ministers and decided thus: "Tomorrow will be a day when the constellation of Pushya would be in the ascendant. ṃy son Rama, who has eyes like red lotus, can be crowned as a prince tomorrow".

Sloka 3

athāntargṛhamāsādya rājā daśarathastadā |
sūtamāmantrayāmāsa rāmaṃ punarihānaya || 2-4-3

3. atha = Afterwards; rājā dasarathaḥ = king Dasaratha; āsādya = got (into); antargṛiham = inner palace; tadā = (and) then; āmantrayāmāsa = ordered; sūtam = the charioteer ṣumantra; ānaya = bring; rāmam = Rama; punaḥ = again; iha = here.

Thereafter, king Dasaratha entered the inner palace and then ordered Sumantra thus, "Bring Rama again here".

Sloka 4

pratigṛhya sa tadvākyaṃ sūtaḥ punarupāyayau |
rāmasya bhavanaṃ śīghraṃ rāmamānayituṃ punaḥ || 2-4-4

4. pratigṛihya = Receiving; tat = those; vākyam = words; saḥ = that; sūtaḥ = charioteer ṣumantra; punaḥ = again; upāyayau = got (to); rāmasya = Rama's; bhavanam = house; sheeghram = immediately; ānayitum = to bring; rāmam = Rama; punaḥ = once more.

Obeying the king's words, Sumantra went to Rama's house immediately to bring Rama once again.

Sloka 5

dvāḥsthairāveditaṃ tasya rāmāyāgamanaṃ punaḥ |
śrutvaiva cāpi rāmastaṃ prāptaṃ śaṅkānvito.abhavat || 2-4-5

5. tasya = ḥis; āgamanam = arrival; punaḥ = again; āveditam = was informed; rāmāya = to Rama; dvāḥ sthaiḥ = by doorkeepers; shrutvā cha api eva = and as soon as Rama heard; tam = his (ṣumantra's); prāptam = arrival; rāmaḥ = Rama; shaṇ^kānvitaḥ abhavat = became doubtful (about the reason for his arrival again.)

The doorkeepers informed Rama that Sumantra had come again. Hearing that, Rama was uncertain as to the reason for Sumantra's arrival again.

Sloka 6

praveśya cainaṃ tvaritaṃ rāmo vacana mabravīt |
yadāgamanakṛtyaṃ te bhūyastadbhruhyaśeṣataḥ || 2-4-6

6. rāmaḥ = Rama; tvaritam = soon; praveshya enam cha = allowed him to enter and; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = (these) words; brūhi = tell; asheshhataḥ = completely; tat = that; yat = for what; te āgamana kṛityam = purpose is your arrival.

Rama immediately allowed Sumantra inside and spoke these words to him: "Tell me completely the purpose of your arrival again."

Sloka 7

tamuvāca tataḥ sūto rājā tvāṃ draṣṭu micchati |
śrutvā pramāṇamatra tvaṃ gamanāyetarāya vā || 2-4-7

7. tataḥ shrutvā = After hearing that; sūtaḥ = the charioteer ṣumantra; uvācha = spoke; tam = to him; rājā = king Dasaratha; ichchhati = wants to; drashhṭum = see; tvām = you; tvam = you; pramāṇam = decide (in your mind); atra = in this; gamanāya = to go; vā itarāya = or otherwise.

Hearing those words, Sumantra said: "King Dasaratha wants to see you. Decide yourself in this matter whether to proceed to your father or otherwise".

Sloka 8

iti sūtavacaḥ śrutvā rāmo.atha tvarayānvitaḥ |
prayayau rājabhavanaṃ punardraṣṭuṃ nareśvaram || 2-4-8

8. rāmaḥ = Rama; shrutvā = hearing; iti = thus; sūtavachaḥ = the word of charioteer ṣumantra; tvarayā anvitaḥ = with swiftness; atha = thereafter; prayayau = went; rājabhavanam = to royal palace; drashhṭum = to see; nareshvaram = Lord of people Dasaratha; punaḥ = again.

Rama, after hearing Sumantra's words, started immediately and went to the royal palace to see king Dasaratha, the Lord of people, again.

Sloka 9

taṃ śrutvā samanuprāptaṃ rāmaṃ daśaratho nṛpaḥ |
praveśyāmāsa gṛhaṃ vivakṣuḥ priyamuttamam || 2-4-9

9. shrutvā = hearing; tam = that; rāmam = Rama; samanuprāptam = has arrived; dasaratha nṛipaḥ = king Dasaratha; praveshayāmāsa = allowed him to enter; gṛiham = the house; vivakshuḥ = to tell; uttamam = excellent; priyam = affectionate (word).

Hearing Rama to have arrived, king Dasaratha allowed him to come into the house, to tell him an excellent and affectionate word.

Sloka 10

praviśnnepa ca śrīmān rāghavo bhavanaṃ pituḥ |
dadarśa pitaraṃ dūrāt praṇipatya kṛtāñjliḥ || 2-4-10

10. shrīmān = the glorious; rāghavaḥ = Rama (lit. scion of Raghu); pravishanneva = soon after entering; pituḥ = father's; bhavanam = house; dadarsha = saw; pitaram = (his) father; dūrāt = from a distance; praṇipatya = (and) fell prostrate before him; kṛitānJṇjaliḥ = with joined palms.

The glorious Rama, soon after entering his father's house, saw his father from a distance and fell prostrate before him in an act of submission, with his palms joined together.

Sloka 11

praṇamantaṃ samutthāpya taṃ pariṣvajya bhūmipaḥ |
pradiśya cāsmai ruciramāsanaṃ punarabravīt || 2-4-11

11. bhūmipaḥ = king Dasaratha (lit. lord of earth); samutthāpya = lifted; tam = him; praṇamantam = who was lying prostrate; parishhvajya = embraced; pradishya cha = and offered; ruchiram = (a) beautiful; āsanam = seat; asmai = to him; abravīt = (and) spoke; punaḥ = again.

Dasaratha lifted Rama up and took him into his embrace. Then, he offered a beautiful seat to Rama and spoke to him as follows:

Sloka 12

rāma vṛddho.asmi dīrghāyurbhuktā bhogā mayepsitāḥ |
annvadbhiḥ kratuśtaistatheṣṭaṃ bhūridakṣiṇaiḥ || 2-4-12

12. rāma = Oh Rama; dīrghāyuḥ = (having) lived long; vṛiddhaḥ asmi = I have become aged; bhogāḥ = luxuries; īpsitaḥ = desired; mayā = by me; bhuktāḥ = have been enjoyed; ishhṭaṃ = holy ceremonies (have been performed); kratushataiḥ = (through) hundreds of sacrifices; annavadbhiḥ = (comprising) of food; bhūridakshiṇaiḥ = (and) of lots of fees (given away to priests performing the sacrifices).

"Oh Rama, I have now become aged after living for a long period. I enjoyed all the luxuries in life, as I desired. I propitiated Gods by performing hundreds of sacrifices, in which food and lots of fees were given away to the officiating priests. "

Sloka 13

jātamiṣṭamapatyaṃ me tvamadyānupamaṃ bhuvi |
dattamiṣṭamadhītaṃ ca mayā puruṣasattama || 2-4-13

13. purushhasattama = ṭhe best of men; Rama!; adya = now; tvam = you; jātam = are born; me = to me; anupamam = as an incomparable; ishhṭam = (and) beloved; apatyam = child; bhuvi = in the world; dattam = (donations) have been given; ishhṭam = holy sacrifices (have been performed); adhītam cha = and (Vedas and other holy scriptures have been) studied; mayā = by me.

"O Rama, the best of men! You are now born to me as without an equal on earth and as my beloved child. I had given away lots of donations, I had performed holy rites and I have also studied Vedas and other Holy Scriptures during my life time."

Sloka 14

anubhūtāni ceṣṭāni mayā vīra sukhānyapi |
devarṣipitṛviprāṇāmanṛṇo.asmi tathātmanaḥ || 2-4-14

14. vīra = Oh; gallant one!; sukhānyapi = comforts also; anubhūtām = have been enjoyed; mayā = by me; ishhṭāmi = as desired by me; anṛiṇaḥ asmi = (thus) I am without any debts; devarshhi pitṛi viprāṇām = to celestials; sages; ancestors; brahmanas; tatha = and; ātmanaḥ = to myself.

"Oh gallant Rama! I also enjoyed all the comforts, as I desired. Thus I am relieved of all debts to the celestials (by performing holy sacrifices), to the sages (by studying Vedas etc.), to my ancestors (by begetting you), to the Brahmans (by giving away donations and food) and to myself (by enjoying all comforts as I desired)."

Sloka 15

na kiñcinma kartavyaṃ tavānyatrābhiṣecanāt |
ato yuttvāmahaṃ brūyāṃ tanme tvaṃ kartumarhasi ||2-4-15

15. mama = to me; na kiJṇchit = no other; kartavyam = duty; tava abhishhechanāt avyatra = other than anointing you for the crown; ataḥ = ḥence; tvam = you; arhasi = are fit; kartum = to do; tat = that; me = to me; yat = what; aham = I; brūyām = tell; tvāṃ = you.

"I have no duty other than to anoint you for the crown. Hence, do what I tell you."

Sloka 16

adya prakṛtayaḥ sarvāstvāmicchanti narādhipam |
atastvāṃ yuvarājānamabhiṣekṣyāmi putraka || 2-4-16

16. adya = ṇow; sarvāḥ = all; prakṛitayaḥ = the people; ichchhanti = want; tvām = you; narādhipam = as king; putraka = oh; son!; ataḥ = hence; abhishhekshyāmi = can install; tvām = you; yuvarājānam = as prince.

"Now all the people want you to be the king. Hence, I can install you as prince."

Sloka 17

api cādyāśubhān rāma svapne pśyāmi dāruṇān |
sanirghātā divolkā ca paratīha mahāsvanā || 2-4-17

17. rāma = Oh! Rama; api cha = and; adya = now; pashyāmi = I am seeing; dāruṇān = fearful; ashubhān = (and) inauspicious; svapne = dreams; iha = here; sanirghātā = thunderous; ulkā = comet; patati = is falling; mahāsvanā = with great sound; divā = during day time.

"Oh, Rama! It is not only the people's desire, but also these days I am getting fearful and inauspicious dreams. Here, thunderous comets are falling with great sound, during day time."

Sloka 18

avaṣṭabdhaṃ ca me rāma nakṣatraṃ dāruṇairgrahaiḥ |
āvedayanti daivajñāvḥ sūryāṅgārakarāhubhiḥ || 2-4-18

18. rāma = oh! Rama; daivajnāḥ = astrologers; āvedayanti = are informing (that); dāruṇaiḥ = fearful; grahaiḥ = planets; sūryāṇ^gārakarāhubhiḥ = like sun; ṃars and Rahu; avashhṭabdham = are encroaching; me = my; nakshatram = natal star.

"Oh, Rama! Astrologers are informing me that fearful planets like Sun, ṃars and Rahu are encroaching my birth star."

Sloka 19

prāyeṇa hi nimittānāmīdṛśānāṃ samudbhave |
rājā hi mṛtyumāpnoti ghoraṃ vāpadamṛcchati || 2-4-19

19. īdṛishānām = such; nimittānām = signs; samudbhave = whenever produced (then); rājā = king; prāyeṇa hi = generally; āpnoti = will get; mṛityum = death; vā = or; ṛichchhati = will get; ghoram = fearful; āpadam = accident.

"Whenever such inauspicious signs are produced, the king generally will get either death or a fearful accident."

Sloka 20

tadyāvadeva me ceto na vimuñcati rāghava |
tāvadevābhiṣiñcasva calā hi prāṇināṃ matiḥ || 2-4-20

20. rāghava = O Rama!; tat = that is why; yāvadeva = while; me = my; chetaḥ = mind; na vimuJṇchati = does not leave (or change); tāvadeva = before then; abhishhiJṇchasva = get anointed to crown; chalāhi = Is it not unstable; matiḥ = the mind; prāniṇām = of human beings?

"Oh, Rama! Hence, before my mind gets changed, get you anointed to the crown. Are not the minds of men unstable?"

Sloka 21

adya candrobhyupagataḥ puṣyātpūrvaṃ punarvasū |
śvaḥ puṣyayogaṃ niyataṃ vakṣyante daivacintakāḥ || 2-4-21

21. adya = today; chandraḥ = the moon; abhyupagataḥ = is getting; punarvasū = the star of punarvasu; pūrvam = earlier to; pushhyāt = the star of pushyami; daivachintakāḥ = astrologers; vakshyante = inform that; svaḥ = tomorrow; pushhyayogam = when the moon joins pushyami star; niyatam = can be fixed (for coronation ceremony).

"Today, the moon is entering the constellation known as Punarvasu which comes before Pushyami star. The astrologers inform that the coronation ceremony can be fixed for tomorrow, when the moon joins Pushyami star."

Sloka 22

tataḥ puṣye.abhiṣiñcasva manastvarayatīva mām |
śvastvāhamabhiṣekṣyāmi yauvarājye paraṃtapa || 2-4-22

22. tataḥ = hence; abhishhiJṇchasva = get anointed for the crown; pushhye = on pushyami star; manaḥ = my mind; tvarayatīva = is hastening; mām = me; paraṃtapa = oh! Annihilator of enemies; aham = I; abhishhekshyāmi = shall anoint; tvā = you; yauva rājye = for princely kingdom; svaḥ = tomorrow.

"Hence, get anointed for the crown on the day of Pushyami star. ṃy mind is urging me as it were to expedite things. O, annihilator of the enemies! I shall anoint you for the princely kingdom tomorrow."

Sloka 23

tasmāttvayādaprabhṛti niśeyaṃ niyatātmanā |
saha vadhvopavastavyā darbhaprastaraśāyinā || 2-4-23

23. tasmāt = ṭhat is why; niyatātmanā = with self control; tvayā = by you; vadhvā saha = along with ṣeetha; the daughter in law; iyam = this; nishā = night; upavastavyā = can be fasted; adyaprabhṛiti = from now onwards; darbhaprastara shāyinā = slept on a mat of Kusha grass.

"That is why, with self control, you along with your wife should observe fast for this night from now onwards, and sleep on a couch made of Kusha grass."

Sloka 24

suhṛdaścāpramattāstvāṃ rakṣantvadya samantataḥ |
bhavanti bahuvighnāni kāryāṇyevaṃvidhāni hi || 2-4-24

24. evam vidhāni = such type of; kāryāṇi = functions; bhavanti hi = are having; bahu vighnāni = many obstacles; adya = today; suhṛidashcha = your friends also; rakshantu = let guard; tvām = you; apramathāḥ = vigilantly; samantataḥ = from all directions.

"Generally, there are many obstacles for such type of functions. Hence, your friends should guard you vigilantly from all directions."

Sloka 25

viproṣitaśca bharato yāvadeva purāditaḥ |
tāvadevābhiṣekaste prāptakālo mato mama || 2-4-25

25. tāvadeva = within such time; te = your; abhishhekaḥ = coronation ceremony; prāptakālaḥ = should occur; yāvadeva = before which time; bharataḥ = Bharata; viproshhitaḥ = is distant from ; itaḥ = this; purāt = city; mataḥ mama = this is my opinion.

"It is my opinion that your coronation function should occur, while Bharata is away from the city."

Sloka 26

kāmaṃ khalu satāṃ vṛtte bhrātā te bharataḥ sthitaḥ |
jyeṣṭhanuvartī dharmātmā sānukrośo jitendriyaḥ || 2-4-26

26. te = your; bhrātā = brother; bharataḥ = Bharata; jyeshhṭhānuvartī = goes according to the eldest; dharmātmā = (being a ) righteous man; sānukroshaḥ = compassionate person; jitendriyaḥ = one who controlled senses; sthitaḥ = stays; kāmam = verily; satāṃ vṛithe = in the path of good people.

" Bharata, your brother, goes according to his eldest brother. He is righteous, compassionate and has the senses under control. He verily follows the path of good people."

Sloka 27

kintu cittaṃ manuṣyāṇāmanityamiti me matiḥ |
satāṃ ca dharmanityānāṃ kṛtaśobhi ca rāghava || 2-4-27

27. rāghava = Oh! Rama; me = my; matiḥ = opinion (is that); chittam = mind; manushhyāṇām = of men; anityam iti = is inconstant; kintu = but; dharmanityānām = the ever righteous; satām cha = endowed with goodness; kṛita shobhi = may act unexpectedly on impulse.

"Oh, Rama! It is my opinion that minds of men are inconstant. But the ever righteous, endowed with goodness, sometimes may act unexpectedly on impulse."

Sloka 28

ityuktaḥ soo.abhyanujñātaḥ śvobhāvinyabhiṣecane |
vrajeti rāmaḥ pitaramabhivādyābhyayādgṛham || 2-4-28

28. iti = thus; uktaḥ = told; abhishhechane = about the coronation ceremony; bhāvini = to happen; svaḥ = tomorrow; abhyānujJṇātaḥ = and having been permitted; vraja iti = to go; saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; abhivādya = offered salutations; pitaram = to father; abhyayāt = went; gṛiham = to house.

Dasaratha, after telling in this way about the ensuing coronation ceremony scheduled for the next day, permitted Rama to depart. Rama offered his salutations to his father and went to his house.

Sloka 29

praviśya cātmano veśma rājñoddhiṣṭe.abhiṣecane |
tatkṣaṇena ca nirgamya māturntaḥpuraṃ yayau || 2-4-29

29. uddishhṭe = After having been decided; abhishhechane = about coronation cermony; rājJṇā = by the king Dasaratha; pravishya = (Rama) entered; ātmanaḥ = his; veshma = house; tatkshaṇena = and immediately; nirgamya = started (and); yayau = went; mātuḥ antaḥ puram = (his) mother's queenly palace.

After hearing the decision of Dasaratha regarding coronation ceremony, Rama entered his house and by starting immediately, went to his mother's queenly house.

Sloka 30

tatra tāṃ pravaṇāmeva mātaraṃ kṣaumavāsinīm |
vāgyatāṃ devatāgāre dadarśāyācatīṃ śriyam || 2-4-30

30. tatra = there; devatāgāre = in the worshipping place; dadarshā = saw; tām = that; mātaram = mother; pravaṇāmeva = who is in meditation; kshaumavāsinī = who is wearing silk clothes; vāgyatām = in a silent way; ayāchatīm = who is praying; shriyam = goddess Lakshmi.

There, in the queen's worshipping place, Rama saw Kausalya silently in meditation, wearing silk clothes and praying to goddess Lakshmi.

Sloka 31

prāgeva cāgatā tatra sumitrā lakṣmaṇa stadā |
sītā cānāyitā śrutvā priyaṃ rāmābhiṣecanam || 2-4-31

31. shrutvā = hearing; priyam = the good news; rāmābhishhechanam = of Rama's coronation ceremony; sumitrā = ṣumitra; lakshmanaḥ = (and) Lakshmana; āgathā = came; prāgeva = even before; sītā cha = ṣeetha also; ānāyita = was brought.

Hearing the good news of the coronation ceremony of Rama, Sumitra and Lakshmana came there even before Seetha was brought.

Sloka 32

tasmin kāle hi kausalyā tasthāvāmīlitekṣaṇā |
sumitrayānvāsyamānā sītayā lakṣmaṇena ca ||2-4-32

32. tasmin kāle = at that time; kausalya = Kausalya; tasthau = was having; amīlitekshaṇa = closed eyes; anvāsyamānā = being attended closely ; sumitrayā = by ṣumitra; sītayā = by ṣeetha; lakshmaṇena cha = and by Lakshmana.

At that time, Kausalya closed her eyes and was in meditation. Along with her, Sumitra, Lakshmana and Seetha were sitting nearby.

Sloka 33

śrutvā puṣyeṇa putrasya yauvarājyābhiṣecanam |
prāṇāyāmena puruṣaṃ dhyāyamānā janārdanam || 2-4-33

33. shrutvā = ḥearing; yauvarājyābhishhechanam = anointment ceremony for the princely kingdom; putrasya = of (her) son; pushhyeṇa = on the day of pushyami star; prāṇāyāmena = with controlled breath; dhyāyamāna = was meditating; purushham = lord; janārdanam = Vishnu.

Hearing that her son will be anointed for the princely kingdom on the day of Pushyami star, Kausalya with controlled breath, was meditating on lord Vishnu.

Sloka 34

tathā saniyamāmeva so.abhigamyābhivādya ca |
uvāca vacanaṃ rāmo harṣyaṃstāmidaṃ tadā || 2-4-34

34. saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; abhigamya = approached; tathā = such; saniyamāmeva = pious observing mother; abhivādyacha = and offered salutation; tadā = then; uvācha = spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words; tām harshhayan = making her delighted.

Rama approached his mother, even while she was engaged in pious observance, offered salutation and spoke the following words making her delighted.

Sloka 35

amba pitrā niyukto.asmi prajāpālanakarmaṇi |
bhavitā śvo.abhiṣeko me yathā mi śāsanaṃ pituḥ || 2-4-35

35. amba = oh! ṃother!; niyuktaḥ asmi = I am appointed; pitra = by father; prajāpālana karmaṇi = for the act of ruling the people; yathā = as per; sāsanam = order; me pituḥ = of my father; bhavitā = there will be; me = my; abhishekaḥ = anointment ceremony; swaḥ = tomorrow.

"Oh, mother! ṃy father ordered that I should rule the people. As per his orders, there will be anointing ceremony to me tomorrow."

Sloka 36

sītayā pyupavastavyā rajanīyaṃ mayā saha |
evamṛtvigupādhyāyaissaha māmuktavān pitā || 2-4-36

36. sītayāpi = by ṣeetha too; mayā saha = along with me; iyam = this; rajanī = night; upavastavyā = can be observed fast; pitā = father; ṛitvigupādhyāyaiḥ saha = together with preceptors and teachers; uktavān = spoke; evam = this; mām = to me.

"Seetha too, along with me should observe fast this whole night. The father together with preceptors and teachers said this to me."

Sloka 37

yāni yānyatra yogyāni śvo bhāvinyabhiṣecane |
tāni me maṅgaldānyadya vaidehyāścaiva kāraya || 2-4-37

37. yāni yāni = whatever; maṇ^gaLāni = auspicious rites; yogāni = suitable; abhishhechane = in anointment ceremony; bhāvini = to occur; svaḥ = tomorrow; tānikāraya = get those things done; me = to me; vaidehyāḥ chaiva = and to ṣeetha; adya = today.

"Have all the auspicious rites that are required for the ensuing tomorrow's anointment ceremony, performed to me and to Seetha today."

Sloka 38

etacchrutvā tu kausalyā cirakālābhikāṅkṣitam |
harṣbāṣpakalaṃ vākyamidaṃ rāma mabhāṣata || 2-4-38

38. shrutvā = ḥearing; etat = this news of anointment ceremony; chirakālābhikāṇ^kshitam = which was being desired for a long time; kausalyā tu = Kausalya; abhāshhata = spoke; rāmam = to Rama; idam = these; kalam = sweet sounding; vākyam = words; harshhabāshhpa = with tears of joy.

Hearing the news of anointment ceremony, which was being desired by her for a long time, Kausalya spoke to Rama the following sweet words with tears of joy in her eyes.

Sloka 39

vatsa rāma ciraṃ jīva hatāste paripanthinaḥ |
jñātīnme tvaṃ śriyāyuktaḥ sumitrāyāśca nandaya || 2-4-39

39. vatsa = oh; child!; rāma = Rama; jīva = live; chiraṃ = long; te = your; paripanthinaḥ = enemies; hatāḥ = are destroyed; tvam = you; shriyā = by your glory; nandaya = make happy; jJṇātīn = cousins; me = of mine; sumitrāyāḥ cha = and of ṣumitra.

"Oh, child Rama! You live a long life! Let your enemies be destroyed! Let the cousins of mine and of Sumitra be made happy by your glory."

Sloka 40

kalyāṇe bata nkṣatre mayi jāto.asi putraka |
yena tvayā daśaratho guṇairārādhitaḥ pitā || 2-4-40

40. putraka = o; little son! bata = how much joy! jātaḥ asi = you were born; mayi = in me; kalyāṇe nakshatre = on a day of an auspicious star; yena = by which; pitā = father; dasarathaḥ = Dasaratha; ārādhitaḥ = was made delightful; tvayā = by your; guṇaiḥ = virtues.

"O, my little son! You were born to me on a day of an auspicious star. That is why, your father king Dasaratha was propitiated by your virtues."

Sloka 41

amoghaṃ bata me kṣāntaṃ puruṣe puṣkarekṣaṇe |
yeyamikṣvākurājyaśrīḥ putra tvāṃ saṃśrayiṣyati || 2-4-41

41. putra = O; son!; me = my; kshāntam = austerity with endurance; pushhkarekshaṇe = to lotus eyed; purushhe = lord Vishnu; amogham = has not gone waste; bata = how much joy! yā = which; iyam = this one; ikshvāku rājyashrīḥ = the glorious kingdom of Ikshvaku dynasty; saṃshrayishhyati = is seeking refuge; tvām = in you.

"O, son! ṃy worship to the lotus eyed Lord Vishnu with endurance has become fruitful. Hence, this glorious kingdom of Ikshvaku dynasty is going to embrace you."

Sloka 42

ityevamukto mātredaṃ rāmo bhrātaramabravīt |
prāñjaliṃ prahvamāsīnamabhivīkhsya smayanniva || 2-4-42

42. rāmaḥ = Rama; uktaḥ = who was spoken to; ityevam = thus; mātrā = by mother; abhivīkshya = saw; bhrātaram = brother Lakshmana; āsīnam = sītting; prahvam = humbly; prāJṇjalim = with folded palms; abravīt = spoke; smayanniva = smilingly; idam = these words.

Rama, after hearing the words spoken by his mother, saw his brother Lakshmana who was sitting humbly nearby with joined palms and spoke to him smilingly as follows:

Sloka 43

lakṣmaṇemāṃ māyā sārdhaṃ praśādhi tvaṃ vasundharām |
dvitīyaṃ me.antarātmānaṃ tvāmiyaṃ śrīrupasthitā || 2-4-43

43. lakshmaṇa = O; Lakshmana! tvam = you; prashādhi = rule; imām = this; vasundharām = earth;mayā sārdham = together with me; iyam = this; shrīḥ = glorious kingdom; upasthitā = reached; tvām = you; me = my; dvitīyam = second; antarātmānam = conscience.

"O, Lakshmana! You rule this earth together with me. This glorious kingdom has reached you, who are my second conscience."

Sloka 44

saumitre bhuṅkṣva bhogāṃ stvamiṣṭān rājyaphalāni ca |
jīvitaṃ ca hi rājyaṃ ca tvadarthamabhikāmaye || 2-4-44

44. saumitre = O; Laksmana! tvam = you; bhuṇ^kshva = enjoy; bhogān = the benefits; rājyaphalānicha = and fruits of kingdom; ishhṭān = as desired; abhikāmaye hi = I desire; jīvitaṃ cha = life; rājyaṃ cha = and kingdom; tvadartham = for you only.

"O, Lakshmana! You enjoy the benefits as desired by you and the fruits of kingdom. I desire to live and even to rule the kingdom but for you."

Sloka 45

ityuktvā lakṣmaṇaṃ rāmo mātarāvabhivādya ca |
abhyanujñāpya sītāṃ ca jagāma svaṃ niveśnam || 2-4-45

45. rāmaḥ = Rama; uktvā = spoke; iti = thus; lakshmaṇam = to Lakshmana; abhivādya cha = offered salutations; mātarau = to both the mothers; abhyanujJṇāpya sītāṃ cha = got ṣeetha to obtain permission and; jagāma = went; svam nivesanam = to his house.

Rama, after speaking thus to Lakshmana, offered salutations to both the mothers, got Seetha to obtain permission from them and went to his house along with Seetha.

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmad rāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhya kānde caturthaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes 4th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
