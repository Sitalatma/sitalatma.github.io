Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 18
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Rama arrives at the king's presence. Seeing King disturbed, Rama becomes grieved and wants to know the cause of king's perturbance. Queen Kaikeyi describes to Rama about the boons that Dasaratha had given earlier and also her wishes of coronating Bharatha instead of Rama and sending Rama to Dandaka forest. On listening to these harsh words of Kaikeyi, Rama did not become sorrowful, but Dasaratha was very much disturbed.

Sloka 1

sa dadarśa āsane rāmaḥ niṣaṇṇam pitaram śubhe |
kaikeyī sahitam dīnam mukhena pariśuṣyatā || 2-18-1

1. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; dadarsha = saw; pitaram = father; nishhaṇṇam = sitting; shubhe āsane = on beautiful seat; mukhena = with face; parishushhyatā = parched up; dīnam = miserable; kaikeyyā sahitam = together with Kaikeyi.

Rama saw his father sitting on a beautiful seat, but with his face looking completely parched up and miserable. Kaikeyi was at his side.

Sloka 2

sa pituḥ caraṇau pūrvam abhivādya vinītavat |
tataḥ vavande caraṇau kaikeyyāḥ susamāhitaḥ || 2-18-2

2. saḥ = Rama; vinītavat = being humble; susamāhitaḥ = well compsed; pūrvam = first; abhivādya = reverentially saluted; pituḥ = father's charaṇau = feet; tataḥ = then; vavande = saluted; kaikeyyāḥ = Kaikeyi's; charaṇau = feet.

Rama being humble and well composed, first reverentially saluted his father's feet and then saluted Kaikeyi's feet.

Sloka 3

rāma iti uktvā ca vacanam vāṣpa paryākula īkṣaṇaḥ |
śaśāka nRpatir dīno na īkṣitum na abhibhāṣitum || 2-18-3

3. nṛipatiḥ = the king Dasaratha; uktvā = spoke; vachanam = the word; rāmaiti = "Oh; Rama!" bhāshhpa paryākulekshhaṇaḥ = with his eyes filled with tears; dīnaḥ = dejected; nashashāka = being not able; īkshhitum = to see; na = nor able; abhibhāshhitum = to talk.

Dasaratha spoke only one word "Rama!" with his eyes filled with tears and dejected, being not able to see or to talk any further words.

Sloka 4

tat apūrvam nara pater dRṣṭvā rūpam bhaya āvaham |
rāmaḥ api bhayam āpannaḥ padā spRṣṭvā iva pannagam || 2-18-4

4. dṛishhṭvā = having seen; apūrvam = not earlier; tat rūpam = that form; narapateḥ = of king; bhayāvaham = producing fear; rāmo.api = even Rama; bhayam āpannaḥ = was struck with fear; spṛishhṭeva = as seizure; pannagam = of snake; padā = with foot.

Having not seen earlier such a fearful form of his father, even Rama was frightened as though he seized a snake by his foot.

Sloka 5

indriyaiḥ aprahRṣṭaiaḥ tam śoka samtāpa karśitam |
nihśvasantam mahā rājam vyathita ākula cetasam || 2-18-5
ūrmi mālinam akṣobhyam kṣubhyantam iva sāgaram |
upaplutam iva ādityam ukta anRtam Rṣim yathā || 2-18-6

5;6. tam mahārājam = that king Dasaratha; niḥ shvasantam = was sighing; indriyaiḥ = with senses; aprahṛishhṭaiḥ = sans pleasure; shoka santāpa karishitam = emaciated with grief and anguish; vyathitākulachetasam = with distressed and agitated mind; sāgaram iva = like ocean; akshhobhyantam = becoming perturbed; ūrmimālinam = with now of waves; ādityam iva = like sun; upaplutam = which is eclipsed; ṛishhimyathā = as a sage; uktānṛitam = who spoke untruth.

That king Dasaratha was sighing heavily with senses sans pleasure, emaciated with grief and anguish, with distressed and agitated mind, like ocean which cannot be perturbed, becoming perturbed with row of waves, like sun which is eclipsed and who lost lustre like by a sage who spoke untruth.

Sloka 7

acintya kalpam hi pitus tam śokam upadhārayan |
babhūva samrabdhataraḥ samudraiva parvaṇi || 2-18-7

7. upadhārayan = contemplating over; tam = that; achintyakalpam = unimaginable; shokam = grief; nṛipate = of the king; babhūva = became; samrabdhataraḥ = very much perturbed; samudra iva = like ocean; parvaṇi on full moon day.

Contemplating over the unimaginable sorrow of the king, he was very much perturbed as an ocean does on a full moon day.

Sloka 8

cintayām āsa ca tadā rāmaḥ pitR hite rataḥ |
kiṃsvid adya eva nRpatir na mām pratyabhinandati || 2-18-8

8. chaturaḥ = the ingenious; rāmaḥ = Rama; rataḥ = who is fond of; pitṛihite = father's welfare; chintayāmāsa = anxiously thought; kimsvid = "why indeed; nṛipatiḥ = king; adyaiva = just today; napratyabhinandati = is not greeting in return; mām = to me?"

The ingenious Rama, who is fond of his father's welfare, anxiously thought : " why indeed the king is not greeting me in return just today?"

Sloka 9

anyadā mām pitā dRṣṭvā kupitaḥ api prasīdati |
tasya mām adya samprekṣya kim āyāsaḥ pravartate || 2-18-9

9. anyadā = at other times; pitā = father; kupito.api = eventhough angry; prasīdati = used to be gracious; dṛishhṭvā = after seeing; mām = me;kim = why; tasya = such father; adya = today; pravartate = has become; āyāsaḥ = weary; samprekshhya = after seeing; mām = me;

"At other times, the father, even though angry used to be gracious after seeing me why today such a father has become weary after seeing me?"

Sloka 10

sa dīnaiva śoka ārtaḥ viṣaṇṇa vadana dyutiḥ |
kaikeyīm abhivādya eva rāmaḥ vacanam abravīt || 2-18-10

10. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; dīna eva = indeed distressed; shokārtaḥ = afflicted with grief; vishhaṇṇavadanadyntiḥ = with lustre reduced from his face; abhivādyaiva = salutted; kaikeyām = to Kaikeyi; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words.

Rama indeed distressed and afflicted with grief, with lustre faded from his face, offered his salutation to Kaikeyi and spoke these words.

Sloka 11

kaccin mayā na aparādham ajñānāt yena me pitā |
kupitaḥ tan mama ācakṣva tvam caiva enam prasādaya || 2-18-11

11. nāparaddham kachchit = I hope that no wrong was done; mayā = by me; aJṇyānāt = through ignorance; āchakshhva = tell; mama = me; yena = why; pitā = father; kupitaḥ = is angry; me = with me; tvameva = you alone; prasādaya = propitiate; enam = him.

"I hope that I have not done anything wrong through ignorance. Tell me why father is angry with me. You alone propitiate him in my favour."

Sloka 12

aprasannamanāḥ kim nu sadā māṃ prati vatsalaḥ |
vivarṇa vadano dīno na hi mām abhibhāṣate || 2-18-12

12. aprasannā manāḥ kimnu = how mind has become displeased; mām prati = towards me; vatsalaḥ = who was affectionate towards offspring; sadā = always; vivarṇa vadanaḥ = with face becoming pale; dīnaḥ = dejected; nābhibhashhate hi = not talking; mām = to me.

"Why my father, who was always affectionate to me, has become displeased? With his face becoming pale and dejected, he is not talking to me."

Sloka 13

śārīraḥ mānaso vā api kaccit enam na bādhate |
samtāpo vā abhitāpo vā durlabham hi sadā sukham || 2-18-13

13. kachchit = I hope that; santāpova.api = either affliction; shārīraḥ = of body; abhitāpovā = or distress; mānasaḥ = of mind; na bādhate = is not troubling; evam = him; sukham = happiness; durlabham hi = is indeed difficult to be attained; sadā = always.

"I hope that either bodily affliction or mental distress is not troubling him. The object of happiness is indeed difficult to be attained always!"

Sloka 14

kaccin na kiṃcit bharate kumāre priya darśane |
śatrughne vā mahā sattve mātRRṇām vā mama aśubham || 2-18-14

14. nakachchit ashubham = I hope no bad thing occured; kimchit = a little; bharate = to Bharata; priyadarshane = the delightful man to look at; shatrughne = or to great strength; matrūṇāmva = or to mothers; mama = of mine.

"I hope that no bad thing occurred to Bharata, the delightful man to look at or to Shatrughna, the man of great strength or to my mother. "

Sloka 15

atoṣayan mahā rājam akurvan vā pitur vacaḥ |
muhūrtam api na icceyam jīvitum kupite nRpe || 2-18-15

15. nṛipe kupite = if our father is angry; nechchheyam = i do not desire; jīvitum = to live; muhūrtampai = even for a moment; atoshhayan = without satisfying; mahārajan = the king; akurvan = without doing; vachaḥ = word; pituḥ = of father.

"If our father becomes angry, I do not desire to live even for a moment without satisfying him or without fulfilling his command."

Sloka 16

yataḥ mūlam naraḥ paśyet prādurbhāvam iha ātmanaḥ |
katham tasmin na varteta pratyakṣe sati daivate || 2-18-16

16. naraḥ = ṃṇ; iha = in this world; pashyet = sees; yatomūlam = from what root; ātmanaḥ one's own; pradurbhāvam = coming into existence; tasmin = that father; sati = being; pratyakshhe = the visible; daivate = god; katham = how; navarteta = behaves not.

"ṃan sees father as the visible god who is the root cause of one's own coming into existence and how else can he behave towards him other than like that?"

Sloka 17

kaccit te paruṣam kiṃcit abhimānāt pitā mama |
uktaḥ bhavatyā kopena yatra asya lulitam manaḥ || 2-18-17

17. mama pitā kachchituktaḥ = i hope my father has been spoken; kinchit = any words; yena = by which; lulitam = (they) disturbed; asya = his; manaḥ = conscience; te abhimānāt = by your pride; bhavityā roshheṇa = by your angry; parushham = which was harsh.

"I hope you have not spoken anything which hurt my father's conscience with your pride and anger."

Sloka 18

etat ācakṣva me devi tattvena paripRccataḥ |
kim nimittam apūrvo ayam vikāraḥ manuja adhipe || 2-18-18

18. devi = oh; queen ! kimninunīḥam = for what vacason; ayam = this; vikāraḥ = perturbation;apūrvaḥ = which was not there earlier; manujādhipe = in the king; achakshhva = tell; me = me; paripṛichchhataḥ = who is questioning; etat = this matter; tattvaina = in reality.

"I am asking you why my father got this perturbation, which was not there earlier. Tell me the true position."

Sloka 19

evamuktā tu kaikeyī rāghaveṇa mahātmanā |
uvācedaṃ sunirlajjā dhṛṣṭamātmahitaṃ vacaḥ || 2-18-19

19. evam = thus; uktā = spoken; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; mahātmanā = the great-souled; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; dhṛishhṭam = fearlessly; uvācha = spoke; idam vachaḥ = these words; ātmahitam = for her own benefit; sunirlajjā = without any shame.

Thus questioned by the great souled Rama, Kaikeyi fearlessly spoke these words for her own benefit, without any shame on her part.

Sloka 20

na rājā kupito rāma vyasanam nāsya kiṃcana |
kiṃcinmanogataṃ tvasya tvadbhayānnābhibhāṣate || 2-18-20

20. rāma = Oh; Rama! rājā = the king; nakupitaḥ = is not angry; nakinchana = there is nothing of vyasanam = distress; asya = for him; tu = but; asya = to him; knchit = there is something manogatham = in mind; nābhibhāshhate = which he is not telling; tvadbhayāt = from fear of you.

"Oh, Rama! The king is not angry. There is nothing of distress for him. But he has something in mind which he is not telling from fear."

Sloka 21

priyam tvāmapriyam vaktum vāṇī nāsyopapartate |
tadavaśyam tvayā kāryam yadanenāśrutam mama || 2-18-21

21. asya = his; vāṇī = words; nopavartate = are not coming off; vaktum = to tell; apriyam = the disagreeable; tvām = to you; priyam = the loveful one; yat = which; anena = by him; ashrutam = is promised; mama = to me; tat = that; kāryam = is to be done; avashyam = certainly; tvayā = by you.

"To him, words are not coming off to tell disagreeable utterance to you, the dear one. He promised me a word. It is to be certainly done by you."

Sloka 22

eṣa mahyam varam dattvā purā māmabhipūjya ca |
sa paścāttapyate rājā yathānyaḥ prākṛtastathā || 2-18-22

22. purā = long ago; saḥ eshaḥ rājā = such of this king; mām abhipūjyacha = honoured me and; dattvā = gave; varam = the boon; yatha = as; anyaḥ = other; prākṛitaḥ = common man; tathā = so; pashchāttapyate = he is repenting.

"Long ago, the king honoured me and gave me a boon. Like a common man, he is repenting now for having given the boon."

Sloka 23

atisṛjya dadānīti varam mama viśāmpatiḥ |
sa nirarthaṃ gatajale setum bandhitumicchati || 2-18-23

23. saḥ vishāmpatiḥ = that king; atisṛijya = promised; dadāmi iti = to give thus; varam = the boon; mama = to me; ichchhati = desires; bandhitum = to build; nivartham = wastefully; setum = dam; gatajale = on a place where water has gone away.

"This king promised me to give the boon and now he desires to build a dam wastefully on a place where water has gone away."

Sloka 24

dharmūlamidam rāma viditam ca satāmapi |
tatsatyam na tyajedrājā kupitastvatkṛte yathā || 2-18-24

24. rāma = oh; Rama! viditam = it is known; satāmapi = even by wise men; idam = that truth; dharmamūlam = is the root of righteousness; kupitaḥ = the offeneded; rajā = king; natyajet = let him not abandon; tat satyam = such truth; yathā = as; tvatkṛite = for your sake.

"Oh, Rama! It is known by even wise men, that truth is the root of righteousness. See that the offended king does not abandon such a truth for your sake."

Sloka 25

yadi tadvakṣyate rājā śubham vā yadi vā.aśubham |
kariṣyasi tataḥ sarvamākhyāmi punastvaham || 2-18-25

25. rājā = the king; vakshhyate = is about to mention(to you); tat = that truth; shubhamvā = either auspicious; ashubhamyadivā = or inauspicious; aham punastu = still; I; ākhyāsyāmi = shall tell; sarvam = all that; karishhyasi yadi = if you shalldo it; tataḥ = afterwards.

"The king is about to mention that truth which may look auspicious or inauspicious to you. Still, I shall tell you all that, if you shall do it afterwards."

Sloka 26

yadi tvabhihitaṃ rājñā tvayi tanna vipatsyate |
tato.ahamabhidhāsyāmi na hyeṣa tvayi vakṣyati || 2-18-26

26. tat = what; abhihitam = is spoken; rājJṇyā = by the king; na vipatsyate = does not go wrong; tvayi = in you; aham = I; abhidhāsyāmi = shall tell; tatḥ = then; eshhaḥ = this king; navakshhyatihi = whill not indeed tell; tvayi = unto you.

"If what is spoken by the king does not create unfavourableness in you, I shall then tell you. This king will not indeed tell you."

Sloka 27

etāttu vacanaṃ śrutvā kaikeyyā samudāhṛtam |
uvāca vyathito rāmastām devīm nṛpasannidhau || 2-18-27

27. shrutvā = after hearing; etat = these; vachanam = words; samudāhṛitam = spoken; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; rāmaḥ = Rama; vyathitaḥ = was pained; uvācha = and spoke; tām devīm = to that queen; nṛipasannidhau = in the presence of the king.

After hearing the words of Kaikeyi, Rama felt painful and spoke thus to her in the presence of the king.

Sloka 28

aho dhiṅnārhase devi paktuṃ māmīdṛśaṃ vacaḥ |
aham hi vacanāt rājñaḥ pateyam api pāvake || 2-18-28
bhakṣayeyam viṣam tīkṣṇam majjeyam api ca arṇave |
niyuktaḥ guruṇā pitrā nRpeṇa ca hitena ca || 2-18-29

28; 29. aho devī = Oh; queen! dhik = what a pity! nārhasi = you are not worthy; vaktum = to talk; īdṛisham = theses type of ; vachaḥ = words; mām = about me; hi = because; vachanāt = by word; rāJṇyaḥ = of king; aham = I; pateyam = shall jump; pāvake.api = even in fire; niyuktaḥ = commanded; pitrā = by father; nṛipeṇa = who is the king; hitenacha = who wishes my welfare; guruṇā = who is venerable; bhakshhayeyam = I shall eat; tīkshhṇam = fiery; vishham = poison; apcha = and majjeyam = sink; ārṇave = into ocean.

"Oh, queen! what a pity! Do not talk like that to me. If father tells me, I shall jump even in fire. If father, who is a king, who wishes my welfare and who is venerable, commands me, I shall eat fiery poison and shall sink into an ocean."

Sloka 30

tat brūhi vacanam devi rājño yad abhikānkṣitam |
kariṣye pratijāne ca rāmaḥ dvir na abhibhāṣate || 2-18-30

30. devī = oh; queen! tat = hence; brūhi = tell; vachanam = the words; yat = which; abhikāṇkshhitam = are desired; rāJṇyaḥ = by the king; pratijānecha = I even promise; karishhye = to do; rāmaḥ = Rama; nābhibhāshhate = does not speak; dviḥ = two things.

"Oh queen! Tell me the words of the king as desired by him. I even promise to do it. Rama does not speak two things."

Sloka 31

tam ārjava samāyuktam anāryā satya vādinam |
uvāca rāmam kaikeyī vacanam bhRśa dāruṇam || 2-18-31

31. anāryā = the vulgar; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; uvācha = uttered; bhṛisha dārunam = very cruel; vachanam = words; tam rāmam = to that Rama; ārjavasamāyuktam = who was committed to sincerity; satyavādinam = and speaking of truth and a great war.

That vulgar Kaikeyi uttered very cruel words to that Rama, who was committed to sincerity and speaking of truth.

Sloka 32

purā deva asure yuddhe pitrā te mama rāghava |
rakṣitena varau dattau saśalyena mahā raṇe || 2-18-32

32. rāghava = oh; Rama! purā = long ago; daivāsurayuddhe = during battle between celestials; mahāraṇe = during the great war; te pitrā = by your father; sa shalyena = pierced by darts; rakshhitena = protected(by me) dattau = were given; mama = to me; varau = two boons.

"Oh, Rama! During a battle and a great war between celestials and demons long ago, I protected your father who was pierced by darts. Then, he gave me two boons."

Sloka 33

tatra me yācitaḥ rājā bharatasya abhiṣecanam |
gamanam daṇḍaka araṇye tava ca adya eva rāghava || 2-18-33

33. rāghava = oh; Rama! tatra = under those circumstances; rājā = the king; yāchitaḥ = was asked; abhishhechanam = for coronation; bharatasya = of Bharata; tava = your; gamanam = going; daṇdakāraṇye = to the forest of Dandaka; adyaiva = now itself.

"Oh, Rama! According to those boons, I asked the king for coronation of Bharata and for your going to Dandaka forest today itself."

Sloka 34

yadi satya pratijñam tvam pitaram kartum iccasi |
ātmānam ca nara reṣṭha mama vākyam idam śRṇu || 2-18-34

34. narashreshhṭa = Oh Rama; the best of men! tvam ichchhasi yadi = If you want; kartum = to make; pitaram = father; ātmānamcha = and yourself; satya pratiJṇyam = faithful to a promise; shruṇu = listen to idam vākyam = these words; mama = of mine.

"Oh, Rama! If you want to make yourself and your father faithful to a promise, listen to these words of mine.

Sloka 35

sa nideśe pitus tiṣṭha yathā tena pratiśrutam |
tvayā araṇyam praveṣṭavyam nava varṣāṇi panca ca || 2-18-35

35. tishhṭa = abide; pituḥ sannideshe = in father's command; yathā = as; pratishrutam = promised; tena = by him; araṇyam = forest; praveshhṭavyam = is to be occupied for navapanchacha = faourteen; varshhāṇi = years; tvayā = by you.

"Abide by your father's command. As per his assurance, you have to stay in the forest for fourteen years."

Sloka 36

bharatastvabhiṣicyeta yadetadabhiṣecanm |
tvadarthe vihitam rājñā tena sarveṇa rāghava || 2-18-36

36. rāghava = oh; Rama! bharataḥ = Bharata; abhishhechyeta = should be enthroned; tena sarveṇa = completely in that manner; yat = in line with; etat = this; abhishhechanam = initiation; vihitam = arranged; tvadarthe = for your sake; rāJṇyā = by the king.

"Bharata is to be enthroned with the use of complete collection of materials already arranged by the king for your sake."

Sloka 37

sapta sapta ca varṣāṇi daṇḍaka araṇyam āśritaḥ |
abhiṣekam imam tyaktvā jaṭā cīra dharaḥ vasa || 2-18-37

37. tyaktvā = leaving; imam abhishhekam = this coronation function; vasa = dwell; daṇdakāraṇyam āshritaḥ = taking refuge in the forest of Dandaka; sapta saptacha varshhāṇi = for fourteen years; jaṭājinadharaḥ = wearing braided hari and covered with a hide.

"You have to leave this coronation function and dwell in the forest of Dandaka for fourteen years, wearing braided hair and covered with a hide."

Sloka 38

bharataḥ kosala pure praśāstu vasudhām imām |
nānā ratna samākīrṇam savāji ratha kunjarām || 2-18-38

38. bharataḥ prashāstu = let Bharata rule; imām vasudhām = this earth; kosalapure = Ayodhya; the capital of kosala; nānāratna samākīrṇam = completely covered with various types of precious things; savājirathakuṇ^jarām = filled with horses; chariots and elephants.

"Let Bharata rule this earth, by residing in Ayodhya filled with various types of precious thing together with horses, chariots and elephants."

Sloka 39

etena tvāṃ narendroyam kāruṇyena samāplutaḥ |
śokasaṃkliṣṭavadano na śaknoti nirīkṣitum || 2-18-39

39. etena = for this reason; ayam narendraḥ = this king Dasaratha; samāplutaḥ = overwhelmed; kāruṇyena = with sympathy; na shaknoti = is not able; nirīkshhitum = to see; tvām = you; shoka samklishhṭavadanaḥ = with face afflicted with grief.

"For this reason, king Dasaratha who is overwhelmed with sympathy towards you is not able to see you with his face, afflicted with grief."

Sloka 40

etatkuru narendhrasya vacanaṃ raghunandana |
satyana mahatā rāma tārayasva nareśvaram || 2-18-40

40. raghunandana = oh; Rama! kuru = do; etat vachanam = these words; narendrasya = of the king; tārayasya = liberate; narenshvaram = the king.

"Oh, Rama! Do as per the words of the king and liberate him by giving your father an opportunity to maintain the truth."

Sloka 41

itīva tasyāṃ paruṣam vadantyām |
nacaiva rāmaḥ praviveśa śokam |
pravivyadhe cāpi mahānubhāvo |
rājā tu putravyasanābhitaptaḥ || 2-18-41

41. tasyām vadantyām = while she was speaking; parushham = harsh words; itīva = in this way; rāmaḥ = Rama; nachaiva pravivesha = did not even enter; shokam = the sorrowfulness; rājātu = but the king; mahānubhāvaḥ = the mighty; pravivyathe = become much disturbed putravyasanābhitaptaḥ = having been afflicted by calamity occured to hi son.

Though she was speaking such harsh words, Rama was not affected with sorrow. But the mighty Dasaratha became highly disturbed, having been afflicted by the calamity occurred to his son.

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe aṣṭādaśaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes the eighteenth sarga of Ayodhya kanda of Srimad Ramayana.
