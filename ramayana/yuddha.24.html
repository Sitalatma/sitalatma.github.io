Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 24
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seeing Lanka and describing it, Rama instructs Lakshmana to draw up his army in battle array. Rama orders for release of Shuka who was captured by the army earlier. Shuka approaches Ravana and describes the strength of Rama's side of the army. Ravana boasts himself of his own army's strength.

Sloka 1

sā vīrasamitī rājñā virarāja vyavasthitā |
śaśinā śubhinakṣatrā paurṇamāsīva śāradī || 6-24-1

1. sā = that; vīra samitīḥ = assemblage of valiant monkeys; rājṇ^ā = along with the king ṣugriva; virarāja = looked charmingly; pournamāpi iva = like a full moon night; sharadī = in autumn; shashivā = along with moon; shubha nakshhatrā = and bright stars.

That army of valiant monkeys along with the king Sugriva looked charming like a full moon night in autumn presided over by the moon and illuminated by bright stars.

Sloka 2

pracacāla ca vegena trastā caiva vasundharā |
pīḍyamānā balau ghena tena sāgaravarcasā || 6-24-2

2. vasundharā = the earth; pīDyamānā = pressed under foot; tena = by that; baloughena = multitude of army; sāgara varchasā = which was energetic like a sea; prachachāla = was trembled; trastā = with fear; vegena = by the rapid movement of the army.

The earth pressed under foot by that multitude of army which was energetic like a sea, was trembled with fear by the rapid movement of that army.

Sloka 3

tataḥ śuśrupurākruṣṭam laṅkāyāḥ kānanaukasaḥ |
bherīmṛdaṅgasamghuṣṭam tumulam romaharṣaṇam || 6-24-3

3. tataḥ = then; kānanoukasaḥ = the monkeys; shushruvaḥ = heard; ākrushhṭam = a great tumult; laṇ^kāyāḥ = arising in Lanka; tumulam = and noisy; bherīmṛidaṇga samghushhṭam = sounds of kettledrums and tabours; romaharshhaṇam = which caused their hair to stand on end.

Then the monkeys heard a great tumult arising in Lanka and noisy sounds of kettledrums and tabours, which caused their hair to stand on end.

Sloka 4

babhūvustena ghoṣeṇa samhṛṣṭā hariyūthapāḥ |
amṛṣyamāṇāstam ghoṣam vinedurghoṣavattaram || 6-24-4

4. hariyutāpaḥ = the leaders of monkeys; samhṛishhṭāḥ = were rejoiced; tena ghosheṇa = by that sound; amṛishhyamāṇāḥ = not tolerating; tam = that; ghoshham = sound; vineduḥ = they emitted shouts; ghoshhavattaram = which surpassed that uproar.

The leaders of monkeys were rejoiced by that sound. But by not tolerating that sound, they emitted shouts, which surpassed that uproar.

Sloka 5

rākṣasāstam plavaṅgānām śuśruvuste.api garjitam |
vardatāmiva dṛptānām meghānāmambare svanam || 6-24-5

5. te = those; rākshhasāḥ = demons too;shushruvuḥ = heard; garjitam = the roaring; dṛiptānām = of the wildly delighted; plavaṇ^gānām = monkeys; svanam iva = which was like the sound; nardatām meghānām = of thundering clouds; ambare = in the sky.

Those demons too heard the roaring of the wildly delighted monkeys, which roar sounded like thundering of clouds in the sky.

Sloka 6

dṛṣṭvā dāśarathirlaṅkām citradhvajapatākinimm |
jagāma manasā sītām dūyamānena cetasā || 6-24-6

6. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; laṇ^kām = Lanka; chitradhvaja patākinīm = dressed with multicolored flags and butings; dāsharathiḥ = Rama; manasā = by his mind; jagāma = went; sītām = to ṣita; chetasā = and his heart; dūyamānena = was filled with grief.

Seeing Lanka dressed with multi colored flags and buntings, Rama bethought himself of Sita and his heart was filled with grief.

Sloka 7

atra sā mṛgaśābākṣī rāvaṇenoparudhyate |
abhibhūtā graheṇeva lohitāṅgena rohiṇī || 6-24-7

7. sā = that ṣita; mṛigashabākshhī = whose eyes resemble like a young deer; uparudhyate = was detained; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; atra = there; rohiṇīva = as Rohini star; abhibhūtā = is overshadowed; lohitāṇgena = by red bodied; graheṇa = planet(ṃars).

"Sita whose eyes resemble those of a young deer, was detained by Ravana there, as Rohini star is overshadowed by the red bodied planet (mars)."

Sloka 8

dīrghamuṣṇam ca niḥśvasya samudvīkṣya ca lakṣmaṇam |
uvāca vacanam vīrastatkālahitamātmanaḥ || 6-24-8

8. niḥshvasya = sighing; ushhṇamcha = warmly; dīrgham = and deeply; samudvīkshhyacha = and seeing; lakshhmaṇam = Lakshmana; vīraḥ = the heroic Rama; vuvācha = spoke; vachanam = the following words; tatkālahitam = beneficial at that time; ātmānaḥ = for him.

Seeing Lakshmana after a warm and deep sigh, the heroic Rama spoke the following words, which were beneficial for him at that time :-

Sloka 9

ālikhantīmivākāśamutthitām paśya lakṣmaṇa |
manseva kṛtām laṅkām nagāgre viśvakarmaṇā || 6-24-9

9. lakshhmaṇa = O; Lakshmana! Pashya = look; laṇ^kām = at Lanka; kṛitām iva = which appears as though constructed; nagāgre = on a hill top; vishvakarmaṇā = by Viswakarma; manasā = with his imagination; utthitām = so elevated; ālikhantim = scraping; ākāsham = the sky.

"O, Lakshmana! Look at this Lanka, which appears as though constructed on a hilltop by Visvakarma with his imagination and so elevated scraping the sky."

Sloka 10

vimānairbahubhirlaṅka samkīrṇā racitā purā |
viṣṇoḥ padamivākāśam cāditam pāṇḍubhirghanaiḥ || 6-24-10

10. laṇkā = the city of Lanka; purā = was formerly; rachitā = constructed; kīrṇā = and filled with; bahubhiḥ = many; vimānaiḥ = seven storied buildings; chhāditam iva = as though covered; pāṇ^Dubhiḥ = with white; ghanaiḥ = clouds; ākāsham = the sky; padam = the abode; vishhṇoḥ = of Vishnu; the all pervaded.

The city of Lanka was constructed densely with many seven storied buildings and appears like a sky the abode of Vishnu (the all pervaded), covered with white clouds."

Sloka 11

puṣpitaiḥ śobhitā laṅkā vanaiścatrarathopamaiḥ |
nānāpatagasamghuṣṭaphalapuṣpopagaiḥ śubhaiḥ || 6-24-11

11. lankā = Lanka; shobhitā = is made beautiful; vanaiḥ = by the gardens; chaitrarathaiḥ = vying with chaitraratha; nānāpatagasamghushhṭaphala pushhpopagaiḥ = with various sounds of birds; fruit bearing flowers; shubhaiḥ = and charms; pushhpitaiḥ = in bloom.

"Lanka is made beautiful by the garden vying with chaitraratha (the garden of Kubera the god of riches) with songs of birds of various species, with fruit bearing flowers and charms in bloom.

Sloka 12

paśya mattavihaṅgani pralīnabhramarāṇi ca |
kokilākulakhaṇḍāni dodhavīti śivo.avilaḥ || 6-24-12

12. pashya = see; (how); shivaḥ = a gentle; anilaḥ = breeze; dodhavīti = sways; kokilākula khan^Dāni = the branches where the cuckoos abound; pralīnabhramarānicha = where bees swarm; mattavihaṇ^gāni = and where the birds are exited with joy.

"See how a gentle breeze sways the branches where the cuckoos abound where bees swarm and where the birds are excited with joy."

Sloka 13

iti dāśarathīramo lakṣmaṇam samabhāṣata |
balam ca tatra vibhajaccāstradṛṣṭena karmaṇā || 6-24-13

13. rāmaḥ = Rama; dāsharathiḥ = son of Dasaratha; samabhāshhata = spoke; iti = thus; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana;tatra = there; balam = the army; vibhajachcha = was divided into battalions; karmaṇā = according to method; shāstradṛishhṭena = found in the scriptures.

Rama the son of Dasaratha spoke as aforesaid to Lakshmana. The army there was divided into battalions according to the procedure found in scriptures.

Sloka 14

śaśāsa kapisemām tām balādādāya vīryavān |
aṅgadaḥ saha nīlena tiṣṭhe durapi durjayaḥ || 6-24-14

14. shashāsa = (ḥe issued the following) commands; ādāya = taking; tām = these; kapisenām = troops of monkeys; balata = from the army; (let) vīryavān = the valiant; durjayaḥ = and the invincible; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; tishhṭhāt = take up his position; nīlena saha = with ṇila; urapi = at the center of the formation.

He issued the following commands: --"Taking these troops of monkeys from the army, let the valiant and the invincible Angada take up his position with Nila at the center of the formation.

Sloka 15

tiṣṭhedvānaravāhinyā vānaraughasamāvṛtaḥ |
āśirto dakṣiṇam pārśvamṛṣabho nāma vānaraḥ || 6-24-15

15. vānaraḥ = (let)the monkeys; ṛishhabhonāma = named Rishabha; vānarougha samavṛitaḥ = along with the multitude of monkeys; tishhṭhet = take up his position; āshritaḥ = having recourse to ; dakshhiṇam = to the right; pārshvam = side; vānara vāhinyāḥ = of the monkey-troops.

Let Rishabha along with the multitude of monkeys take up his position, having recourse to the right side of the army."

Sloka 16

gandhahastīva durdharṣastarasvī gandhamādanaḥ |
tiṣṭhedvānaravāhinyāḥ savyam pakṣamadhiṣṭhataḥ || 6-24-16

16. gandhamādanaḥ = (let)Gandhamadana; tarasvī = the strong; durdarshhaḥ = and the unconquerable; gandhahastīva = like an elephant in rut; tishhṭhet = take his position; adhishhṭhitaḥ = superintending; savyampakshham = the left side; vānara vāhinyāḥ = of the monkey troops.

"Let Gandhamadana, the strong and unconquerable, resembling an elephant in rut, take his position superintending the left side of the monkey troops."

Sloka 17

mūrdhni sthāsyāmyaham yatto lakṣmaṇena samanvitaḥ |
jāmbavāṃśca suṣeṇaśca vegadarśī ca vānaraḥ || 6-24-17
ṛkṣamukhyā mahātmānaḥ kukṣim rakṣantu te trayaḥ |

17. aham = I; sthāsyāmi = shall stand; yattaḥ = alert; mūrdhni = in the forefront of the army; samanvitaḥ = along with; lakshhmaṇena = Lakshmana; jambavāshcha = and Jambavan; sushheṇashcha = ṣushena; vegadarshhīcha = and Vegadarshi; vānarāḥ = the monkey; te = those; trayah = three; mahātmanāḥ = exceedingly wise ones; ṛikshhamukhyāḥ = the leaders of the bears; rakshhanti = protect; kukshhim = the belly.

"I shall stand alert in the forefront of the army along with Lakshmana. Let Jambavan, Sushena and Vegadarshi the monkey - those three exceeding wise leaders of bears and monkeys- protest the belly portion of the army."

Sloka 18

jaghanam kapisenāyāḥ kapirājo.abhirakṣatu || 6-24-18
paścārdhamiva lokasya pracetāstejapā vṛtaḥ |

18. kapirājaḥ = ṣugriva; the king of monkeys; abhirakshhatu = protect; jaghanam = the hips and loins; kapisenāyāḥ = of the monkey troops; pravetāḥ = as Varuna (the god of water); vṛitaḥ = who remains enveloped; tejasā = with splendor; (protects); pashchārdhamiva = the western quarter; lokasya = of the earth.

"Let Sugriva the king of monkeys protect the rear guard of the army, resembling Varuna (the god of water) who remains enveloped with splendor protects the western quarter of the earth."

Sloka 19

suvibhaktamahāvyūhā mahāvānararakṣitā || 6-24-19
anīkinī sā vibabhau yathādyauḥ sābhrasamplavā |

19. sā amīkinī = that army; suvibhakta mahāvyūhā = with innumerable divisions being skillfully distributed; mahā vānararakshhitā = led by the foremost of monkeys; vibabhou = shone; yathā dyouḥ = like heavens; bābhrasamplavā = with mass of clouds.

That army with innumerable divisions, being skillfully distributed led by the foremost of monkeys, resembled heavens with their mass of clouds.

Sloka 20

praGṛhya giriśṛṅgāṇi mahataśca mahīruhān || 6-24-20
āsedurvānarā laṅkām mimardayuṣavo raṇe |

20. mimardayishhavah = with a desire to crush(the demons); raṇe = in battle; vānarāh = the monkeys; pragṛihya = seizing; girishṛiṇgāṇi = peaks of mountains; mahataḥ = and gigantic; mahīruhān = trees; āseduḥ = reached; laṇ^kām = Lanka.

With a desire to crush the demons in battle, the monkeys, seizing peaks of mountains and gigantic trees, reached Lanka.

Sloka 21

śikharairvikirāmainām laṅkām muṣṭibhireva vā || 6-24-21
iti sma dadhire sarve mānāṃsi haripuṅgavāḥ |

21. sarve = all; haripuṇ^gavāḥ = the heroic monkeys; dadhire = held; manāmsi = in their minds; iti = the following resolve; "shikharaiḥ = with the peaks of mountains; mushhṭibhireva vā = or with our bare fists; vikirāma = we shall shatter into pieces; enām = this; laṇkām = Lanka.

All the heroic monkeys held the following resolve in their minds, "It is with peak of mountains or with our bare fists even, we shall shatter this Lanka into pieces."

Sloka 22

tato rāmo mahātejāḥ sugrīva midamabravīt || 6-24-22
suvibhaktāni sainyāni śuka eṣa vimucyatām |

22. tataḥ = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; mahātejāḥ = with great splendor; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; sugrivam = to ṣugriva; sainyāni = our forces; suvibhaktām = are properly marshaled; eshhaḥ shukaḥ = let this ṣhuka; vimuchyatām = be released.

Then, Rama with great splendor spoke the following words to Sugriva, "Our forces are properly marshaled. Let this Shuka be released."

Sloka 23

rāmasya tu vacaḥ śrutvā vānarendro mahābalaḥ || 6-24-23
mocayāmāsa tam dūtam śukam rāmasya śāsanāt |

23. ṣhrutvā = hearing; vachanam = the words; rāmasya = of Rama; vānarendraḥ = ṣugriva the lord of monkeys; mochayāmāsa = released; shukam = ṣhuka; tam dūtam = that messenger; shāsanāt = by the order; rāmasya = of Rama.

Hearing the words of Rama, Sugriva the lord of monkeys released Shuka the messenger, as per the order of Rama.

Sloka 24

mocito rāmavākyena vānaraiśca nipīḍitaḥ || 6-24-24
śukaḥ paramasamtrasto rakṣodhipamupāgamat |

24. shukaḥ = ṣhuka; nipīDitaḥ = who was harassed; vānaraiḥ = by monkeys; mochitaḥ = and released; rāmavākyaena = through the words of Rama; paramasamtrastaḥ = was trembling with great fear; upāgamat = and reached; rakshhodhipam = Ravana.

Shuka who was harassed by the monkeys and released then as per the words of Rama, was trembling with great fear and reached Ravana.

Sloka 25

rāvaṇaḥ prahasanneva śukam vākyamuvāca ha || 6-24-25
kimimau te sitau pakṣau lūnapakṣśca dṛśyase |
kaccinnānekacittānām teṣām tvam vaśamāgataḥ || 6-24-26

25; 26. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; prahasanneva = even while laughing; uvāchaha = enquired; shukam = ṣhuka; vākyam = with the following words: kim = why; imou = these; te = your; pakshhou = wings; sitou = were tied up? dṛishyase = you are appearing; lūnapakshhashcha = with your wings plucked up; tvam na āgataḥ kachchit = ḥave you not fallen; teshhām = into their; anekachittānām = various(fickle)minds?

Ravana, even while laughing, enquired Shuka with the following words: " why were your wings tied up? You appear with your wings plucked up. Have you not fallen as a victim to their various fickle minds?"

Sloka 27

tataspa bhayasamvigna stadā rājñābhicoditaḥ |
vacanam pratyuvācedam rākṣasādhipamuttamam || 6-24-27
sāgarasyottare tīre.abravam te vacanam tathā |
yathāsaṃdeśamakliṣṭam sāntvayan ślakṣṇayā girā || 6-24-28

27; 28. tataḥ = then; saḥ = that ṣhuka; bhayasamvignaḥ = agitated with fear; tadā = then; abhichoditaḥ = prompted; rāj^ṇā = by the king Ravana; pratyuvācha = replied; idam = with these; uttamam = exellent; vachanam = words; rākshhasādhipam = to Ravana: uttare tīre = on the northern shore; sāgarasya = of the ocean; abravam = I spoke; tathā = as; te = your; vachanam = words; shlakshhṇayā = conciliating; yathāsandesham = as per your instructions; aklishhṭam = without any ambiguity.

Asked as aforesaid by the king Ravana, Shuka agitated as he was with fear, gave him the following excellent reply: "On the northern shore of the ocean, I conveyed your message in a smooth tone, conciliating and without any ambiguity exactly as per your instructions."

Sloka 29

kruddhaisterahamutplutya dṛṣṭamātraḥ plavaṅgamaiḥ |
gṛhito.asmyapi cārabdho hantum loptum ca muṣṭibhiḥ || 6-24-29

29. aham = I; dṛishhṭamātraḥ = on just being seen; gṛihītaḥ = by those monkeys; kruddhaiḥ = in rage; apicha = and; ārābddhaiḥ = started; hantum = beating; loptumcha = and plucking; mushhṭibhiḥ = with their fists.

"Just on seeing me, the enraged monkeys jumped on me and seized me. They soon started beating and plucking me with their fists."

Sloka 30

na te sambhāṣitum śakyāḥ sampraśno.atra na vidyate |
prakṛtyā kopanāstīkṣiṇā vānarā rākṣasādhipa || 6-24-30

30. rākshhasādhipa = O; lord of demons! ṭe = those monkeys; nashakyāḥ = are not capable of being; sambhāshhitum = spoken to; navidyate = nor possible; samprashnaḥ = of being questioned; atra = in this matter; vānarāḥ = monkeys; prakṛityāḥ = by their nature; kopanāḥ = are angry; tīkshhṇāḥ = and ferocious.

"O, Lord of demons! Those monkeys are not capable of being spoken to, or possible of being questioned in this matter. ṃonkeys, by their very nature, are angry and ferocious."

Sloka 31

sa ca hantā virādhasya kabandhasya kharasya ca |
sugrīvasahito rāmaḥ sītāyāḥ padamāgataḥ || 6-24-31

31. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; hantā = the killer; virādhasya = of Viradha; kabandhasya = Kabandha; kharasyacha = and Khara; āgataḥ = came; sugrīva sahitaḥ = along with ṣugriva; padam = to the place; sītāyāḥ = of ṣita.

"That Rama the killer of Virādha, Kabandha and Khara came along with Sugriva to the place of Sita."

Sloka 32

sa kṛtvā sāgare setum tīrtvā ca lavaṇodadhim |
eṣa rakṣāsi nirdhūya dhanvī tiṣṭhati rāghavaḥ || 6-24-32

32. kṛitvā = having constructed; setum = a bridge; sāgare = across the sea; tīrtvā = and having crossed; lavaṇodadhim = the salty ocean; nirdhūya = and expelling; rakshāmsi = the demons; rāghavaḥ = Rama; eshhaḥ = as such; tishhṭhati = stands; dhanvī = wielding a bow.

"Having constructed a bridge across the sea and crossed the salty ocean and expelling the demons, here stands Rama wielding a bow."

Sloka 33

ṛkṣavānarasamghānāmanīkāni sahasraśaḥ |
girimeghanikāśānām cādayanti vasundharām || 6-24-33

33. sahasrashaḥ = thousands; anīkāni = of divisions; ṛikshhavānarasamghavām = of crowds of bears and monkeys; girimegha nikashānām = resembling mountains and clouds; chhādayanti = cover; vasundharām = the earth.

"Thousands of divisions of hordes of bears and monkeys resembling mountains and clouds, cover the earth."

Sloka 34

rākṣasānām balaughasya vānarendrabalasya ca |
naitayorvidyate saṃdhirdevadānavayoriva || 6-24-34

34. navidyate = there is no more possibility; samdhiḥ = of an alliance; etayoḥ = between these two armies; baloughasya = the army; rākshhasānām = of demons; vānarendra balasya = and the army of ṣugriva; the lord of monkeys; devadānavayoriva = than between a god and a demon.

"There is no more possibility of an alliance between these two armies - the army of demons and the army of monkeys- than between a God and a demon."

Sloka 35

purā prākāramāyānti kṣipramekataram kuru |
sītām vāsmai prayaccāśu yuddham vāpi pradīyatām || 6-24-35

35. purā āyanti = very soon; (they) can come; prākāram = to the rampart; kuru = do; ekataram = any one of the two acts; kshhipram = immediately; prayachchha vā = either to restore; sītām = ṣita; yuddhamvāpi = or combat ; pradīyatām = may be offered; asmai = to him; āshu = soon.

"Very soon they will come to our rampart. Immediately, do any one of these two acts- either to restore Sita or to offer a combat to him."

Sloka 36

śukasya vacanam śrutvā rāvaṇo vākya mabravīt |
roṣasamraktanayano nirdahanniva cakṣuṣā || 6-24-36

36. shrutvā = hearing; vachanam = the words; shukasya = of ṣhuka; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; roshhasamraktanayanaḥ = with his eyes becoming blood red in anger; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = these words; nirdahanniva = as if he would consume him; chakshhusā = with his glance.

Hearing the words of Shuka, Ravana with his eyes becoming blood red in anger, as if he would consume him with his glance, said as follows:

Sloka 37

yadi mām pratiyudhyeran devagandharvadānavāḥ |
naiva sītām pradāsyāmi sarvalokabhayādapi || 6-24-37

37. sarva loka bhayādapi = even if I were frightened by all worlds; mām pratiyuddhyerannapi = or even if I were to face a battle; devagandharva dānavāḥ = with celestials; Gandharvas(celestial singers) or demons; naiva pradāsyāmi = I would not give back; sītām = ṣita.

"Even if I were frightened by all the worlds or even if I were to face a battle with celestials or Gandharvas (celestial singers) or demons, I would not give back Sita."

Sloka 38

kadā samabhidhānanti māmakā rāghavam śarāḥ |
vasante puṣpitam mattā bhramarā iva pādapam || 6-24-38

38. kadā = when; māmakāḥ = will my; shavāḥ = arrows; samabhidhāvanti = soon fall; rāghavam = upon Rama; bhramarāḥ iva = as large black bees; mattāḥ = exited with joy; (fall upon) pādapam = a tree; pushhpitam = in bloom; vasante = at spring time?

"When will my arrows soon fall upon Rama, as large black bees excited with joy fall upon a tree in bloom at spring time?"

Sloka 39

kadā śoṇitadigdhāṅgam dīpaiḥ kārmukavicyutaiḥ |
śarairādīpayiṣyāmi ulkābhiriva kuñjaram || 6-24-39

39. kadā = when; ādī payishhyāmi = shall I consume; dīptaiḥ = by the blazing; sharaiḥ = arrows; kārmuka vichyutaiḥ = released from my bow; shoṇita digdhāṇgam = his body flowing with blood; ulkābhiriva = as flaming torches; (destroy) kuṇ^jaram = an elephant?

"When shall I consume his body flowing with blood, by the blazing arrows released from my bow, as flaming torches destroy an elephant?"

Sloka 40

taccāsya balamādāsye balena mahatā vṛtaḥ |
jyotiṣāmiva sarveṣām prabhāmudyandivākaraḥ || 6-24-40

40. vṛitaḥ = endowed with; mahatā = a huge; balena = army; ādāsye = I shall eclipse ; tat = the aforesaid; balam = army; asya = of Rama; prabhāmiva = as the brilliance; sarveshhām jyotishhām = of all stars; udyan = at the rising; divākaraḥ = of the sun.

"Endowed with a huge army I shall eclipse the aforesaid army of Rama, as the brilliance of all stars is obscured at the rising of the sun."

Sloka 41

sāgarasyeva me vego mārutasyeva me balam |
na ca dāśarathirveda tena mām yoddhumiccati || 6-24-41

41. me = my; vegaḥ- = rashness; sagarasyeva = is like that of the sea. ṃe = my; balam = strength; marutasyeva = is like that of the wind; dāsharathiḥ = Rama; nachaveda = is not aware of it; tena = that is why; ichchati = he desires; mām yoddhum = to meet me in combat.

"ṃy rashness is like that of the sea and my strength is like that of the wind. Rama is not aware of it. That is why, he is desirous of meeting me in a combat."

Sloka 42

na me tūṇīśayān bāṇān saniṣāniva pannagān |
rāmaḥ paśyati samgrāme tena mām yoddhumiccati || 6-24-42

42. rāmaḥ = Rama; na pashyati = has not seen; me bānān = my arrows; savishhān pannagāniva = resembling venomous serpents; tūṇīshayān = in my quiver; tena = that is why; ichchhati = he desires; yoddhum = to fight; mām = with me.

"Rama has not seen my arrows resembling venomous serpents lying in my quiver. That is why, he desires to fight with me."

Sloka 43

na jānāti purā vīryam mama yuddhe sa rāghavaḥ |
mama cāpamayīm vīṇām śarakoṇaiḥ pravāditām || 6-24-43
jyāśabadatumulām ghorāmārtagītamahāsvanām |
nārācatalasamnādām tām mamāhitavāhinīm || 6-24-44
avagāhya maharaṅgam vādayiṣyāntagan raṇe |

43; 44. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; purā = formerly; najānāti = was not aware; mama = of my; vīryam = prowess; Yuddhe = in battle; vādayishhyāmi = I shall cause to resound; vīṇām = Vina; mama chāpa mayīm = in the form of my bow; nārāchatala samvādām = which is played on; with the heads of my arrows; jyāshabda tumulām = the bow string producing a tumultuous sound; ārtagīta mahāsvanām = the huge cries of the wounded ; ghorām = its terrible accompaniment; shara koṇaiḥ = the darts; pravāditām = sounding its innumerable notes; (when) aham = I; avagāhya = enter; mahāraṇ^gam = a vast stage; mām ahitavāhinīm = in the form of my enemy ranks; tām = in that; raṇe = battle.

Rama was not formerly aware of my prowess in battle. I shall cause to resound Vina (a musical instrument) in the form of my bow, which is played on with the heads of my arrows, the bow string producing a tumultuous sound, the huge cries of the wounded its terrible accompaniment, the darts sounding its innumerable notes, when I enter a vast stage in the form of my enemy ranks in that battle."

Sloka 45

na vāsavenāpi sa hasracakṣuṣā |
yuddhe.asmi śakyo varuṇena vāsvayam |
yameva vā dharṣayitum śarāgninā |
mahāhave vaiśravaṇena vā svayam || 6-24-45

45. vāasavenāpi = neither by Devendra; the god of celestials; sahasrachakshhushhā = the thousand eyed; varuṇenāpi = nor by Varuna; the god of waters; svayam = in person; yuddhe = in combat; yamanaivā = nor by Yama the god of death; sharāgninā = with the fire of his arrows; vaishravaṇenavā = nor by Kubera the lord of riches; svayam = in person; shakyaḥ = can I be able; dharshhayitum = to be attacked; mahāhave = in a great battle.

"Neither by the thousand eyed Indra the god of celestials nor by Varuna the God of waters in person in a combat, nor by Yama the god of death with the fire of his arrows, nor by Kubera the lord of riches in person, can I be able to be attacked in a great battle."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe caturviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 24th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
