Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties
Verses converted to UṭF_8 Sept, 09

chapter [Sarga] 7

Introduction

The truthful and upright characters of the ministers of Dasharatha are portrayed, who make the rulership meaningful with their virtuous, skilful and efficient administration. Along with the political ministers, the religious ministry is also portrayed.


Sloka 1

tasya amātyā guṇair āsan ikṣhvakostu mahātmanaḥ |
maṃtrajñāḥ ca iṅgitajñāḥ ca nityam priya hite ratāḥ || 1-7-1

1. mahāatmanaḥ tasya ikshwakuḥ tu = for great soul, to him, one born in Ikshwaku-s, Dasharatha; guṇaiḥ = those with epitomised attribute; mantrajñaḥ cha = tactful, also; iṇ^gitajñaḥ cha = adroit ones, also; nityam priya hite rataḥ = always, in welfare alone, obliged to; amātyā = ministers; āsan = are there.

The misters for the great soul from Ikshvaku kings of Emperor Dasharatha, are epitomised ones of their tactfulness, adroitness and are always obliged to undertake welfare activities of their king and the kingdom. [1-7-1]

Sloka 2

aṣṭau babhūvuḥ vīrasya tasya amātyā yaśasvinaḥ |
śucayaḥ ca anuraktāḥ ca rājakṛtyeṣu nityaśaḥ || 1-7-2

2. vīrasya = of the valiant one; yashasvinaḥ = glorious king; tasya = his; shuchayaḥ = clean at heart; nityasaḥ = all time; rāja kṛityeṣu = in king' s, works; anuraktāḥ cha = involved in, also; amātyāḥ = ministers; aṣṭaḥ bhabhuvuḥ = eight, are there.

Eight ministers are there for that valiant and glorious King Dasharatha, who are clean at heart and are involved in the works of the king and kingdom at all time. [1-7-2]

Sloka 3

dhṛṣṭir jayanto vijayo surāṣṭro rāṣṭra vardhanaḥ |
akopo dharmapālaḥ ca sumaṃtraḥ ca aṣṭamo arthavit || 1-7-3

3. dhṛiṣṭiḥ = Dhristi; jayantaḥ = Jayantha; vijayaḥ = Vijaya; surāṣṭraḥ = ṣurāshtra; rāṣṭra vardhanaḥ = Rāshtravardhana; akopaḥ = Akopa; dharmapālaḥcha = Dharmapāla, also; ṣumantraḥ = ṣumantra; aṣṭamaḥ = eighth; abhavat = will be.

Dhristi, Jayantha, Vijaya, Surāshtra, Rāshtravardhana, Akopa, Dharmapāla, are seven, and Sumantra is the eighth one. [1-7-3]

Sloka 4

ṛtvijau dvau abhimatau tasyāḥ tām ṛṣi sattamau |
vaśiṣṭho vāmadevaḥ ca maṃtriṇaḥ ca tathā apare || 1-7-4

4. tasyāḥ = to him, Dasharatha; vashiṣṭaḥ = Vashishta; vāmadevaḥ cha = Vamadeva, also; dvau = two; tām ṛiṣisattamau = those, saints, of eminence; abhimatau = acquiescent; ṛitwijau = Vedic ritual-authorities; tathā apare = like that, furthermore; mantriṇaḥ cha = ministers, also [are there.]

Two venerable saints of eminence are religious ministers for they are authorities in Vedic rituals, namely Vashishta and Vamadeva, who are the acquiescent with religious matters, and apart from these two some more religious ministers are also there to King Dasharatha. [1-7-4]

Sloka 5

suyajño api atha jābāliḥ kāśaypo api atha gautamaḥ |
mārkaṇḍeyaḥ tu dīrghāyuḥ tathā kātyāyano dvijaḥ || 1-7-5
etaiḥ brahmarṣibhir nityam ṛtvijaḥ tasya paurvakāḥ |

5, 6a. suyajño api = ṣuyajna, also; atha jābāliḥ = then, Jābāli; kāshaypaḥ api = Kashyapa, also; atha gautamaḥ = then, Gautama; mārkaṇDeyaḥ tu = ṃārkandeya, also; dīrghāyuḥ tathā = Deerghāyu, like that; kātyāyanaḥ = Kātyayana; dvijaḥ = Brahman; etaiḥ = along with them; brahmarṣibhiḥ = Brahma-sages; nityam = always; ṛitvijaḥ = ritual scholars; tasya paurvakāḥ = his ancestral pundits.

Suyajna, Jabāli, Kāshyapa, Gautama, ṃārkandeya, Deerghāyu, and then Kātyayana are the scholarly Brahmans acting as religious ministers, and along with them there are also Brahma-sages who are always the ancestral ritual scholars for Dasharatha's family. [1-7-5, 6a]

Sloka 6

vidyā vinītā hrīmaṃtaḥ kuśalā niyatendriyāḥ || 1-7-6
śrīmantaḥ ca mahātmanaḥ śāstrajñā dhṛḍha vikramāḥ |
kīrtimantaḥ praṇihitā yathā vacana kāriṇaḥ || 1-7-7
tejaḥ kṣamā yaśaḥ prāptāḥ smita pūrva abhibhāṣiṇaḥ |

6b, 8a vidyā vinīta = in scriptures, well-versed; hrīmantaḥ = shun bad deeds; kushalāḥ = skilful; niyataḥ indriyaḥ = with regulated, senses; srīmantaḥ cha = affluent ones; mahāatmanaḥ = great, souls; shastra jñā = sciences, knowers of; dhṛiDha vikramaḥ = firmly, courageous; kītrimantaḥ = distinguished, ones; praṇihitāḥ = quiet souls; yathā vachana kārinaḥ = as per, their word, doers of [true to their word]; tejaḥ kshamā yashaḥ = they have magnificence, patience, fame; prāptāḥ = valiant ones; smitaḥ pūrva abhibhāshana = smile, afore, while, they converse.

All the ministers are well versed in scriptures, they shun bad deeds, skilful ones in their duties with their senses regulated. Those great souls are affluent, knowers of all sciences, firmly courageous, and they are distinguished and quiet-souls, and those ministers are true to their word. They are magnificent, patient and famed ones and they smile afore they converse. [1-7-6b, 8a]

Sloka 8

krodhāt kāma artha hetor vā na brūyur anṛtam vacaḥ || 1-7-8
teṣām aviditam kiṃcat śveṣu nāsti pareṣu vā |
kriyamāṇam kṛtam vā api cāreṇa api cikīrṣitam || 1-7-9

8b, 9. krodhāt kāma artha hetoḥ vā = in anger, in greed, for monetary, reason of, either; anṛitam = untruthful; vachaḥ = words; na brūyuḥ = never, they speak; teshām = to them; a viditam = unknown; kimchat = a little; sveṣu = in their own [country]; nāsti = not there; pareṣu vā = in other [countries,] either; kriyamāṇam = that is happening; kṛritam = already happened; vā api = either, that too; chāreṇa = through agents; chikīrṣitam = that is going to be undertaken [by others, is not there.

They never speak untruthful words in anger or in greed or for monetary reasons either. There is nothing unknown to them, even a little, in their own country or in the others either, or about everything that is happening or has happened, or that is going to happen, for they know them through agents. [1-7-8b, 9]

Sloka 10

kuśalā vyvahāreṣu sauhṛdeṣu parīkṣitāḥ |
prāpta kālam yathā daṇḍam dhārayeyuḥ suteṣu api || 1-7-10

10. vyavahareṣu = in administration; kushalāḥ = efficient; sauhṛideṣu = in their friendships; parīkshitāḥ = well examined [by the king]; sutaḥ api = to their sons, even; prāpta kālam yathā = comes, time, according to [if situation demands]; daṇDam dhārayeyu = punishment, they impose.

They are efficient in administration and their friendships are well examined by the king, and those ministers impose punishment even on their own sons, if situation demands it. [1-7-10]

Sloka 11

kośa saṃgrahaṇe yuktā balasya ca parigrahe |
ahitam ca api puruṣam na hiṃsyur avidūṣakam || 1-7-11

11. kosha samgrahaṇe yukatā = treasury, in collections, dutiful; balasya cha parigrahe = of armies, also, militarising; a hitam cha api puruṣam = unfriendly, also, even, a person; a vi dūshakam = not, really, blameworthy; na himsyuḥ = do not, torture.

In collections to their treasury and to militarise their armies they are dutiful, even an unfriendly person will not be tortured, if he were not really blameworthy. [1-7-11]

Sloka 12

vīrāḥ ca niyatotsāhā rāja śāstram anuṣṭhitāḥ |
śucīnām rakṣitāraḥ ca nityam viṣaya vāsinām || 1-7-12

12. vīraḥ = valiant; niyata utsāha = engineered enthusiasm; rāja shāstram = political science; anuṣṭitāḥ = administrators of; viṣaya vāsinām = in kingdom, dwelling ones [subjects of kingdom.]; suchīnām = clean persons; nityam = at all times; rakshitāraḥ = protectors.

They are valiant ones with engineered enthusiasm, administrators of political science, clean persons and protectors of subjects of their kingdom at all times. [1-7-12]

Sloka 13

brahma kṣatram ahiṃsantaḥ te kośam samapūrayan |
sutīkṣṇa daṇḍāḥ saṃprekṣya puruṣasya balābalam || 1-7-13

13. brahma kshatram = Brahmans, Kshatriya-s; te a himsantaḥ = they, do not, persecute; kosham samapūrayan = treasury, to fill up; puruṣasya = person's; bala a balam = strength, weakness; samprekshya = after assessing; sutīkshṇa danDāḥ = high, degree, punishment givers.

They do not persecute Brahman-s and Kshatriya-s to fill-up the treasury, and high degree punishments will be given on assessing that person's, or the offender's strength and weakness. [1-7-13]

Sloka 14

śucīnām eka buddhīnām sarveṣām saṃprajānatām |
na āsīt pure vā rāṣṭre vā mṛṣā vādī naraḥ kvacit || 1-7-14

14. suchīnām = decent ones; ekabudhinām = one, minded, [in league with]; sarveṣām = all of them; samprajānatām = administering; kwachit = anywhere; pure vā rāṣṭre vā = in capital, or, in kingdom, either; mṛiṣa vādī naraḥ = lies, speaking, person [liar]; na āsīt = is not, there.

All of those ministers are clean administrators of kingdom, and are in league with each other, as such there is none anywhere, either in capital or in country, a liar. [1-7-14]

Sloka 15

kaścin na duṣṭaḥ tatra āsīt para dāra ratir naraḥ |
praśāṃtam sarvam eva asīt rāṣṭram puravaram ca tat || 1-7-15

15. tatra = there; duṣṭaḥ = evil-minded; para dāra ratoḥ naraḥ = in other's, wife, interested, man; kaschit na āsīt = anyone, none, is there; sarvam rāṣṭram = all, kingdom; tat pura varam cha = that, capital, also; prashāntam eva āsīt = undisturbed, only, it is there.

None with an evil-mind or with an interest in other man's wife is there, and thus on whole in the kingdom and also like that in the capital, there is an undisturbed society. [1-7-15]

Sloka 16

su vāsasa su veṣāḥ ca te ca sarve śucivratāḥ |
hitārthaḥ ca narendrasya jāgrato naya cakṣuṣā || 1-7-16

16. te cha sarve = they, also, all; su vāsasaḥ = well dressed; su veshāḥ cha = well decorated, also; suchi vratāḥ = decency, observing; narendrasya hitārthaḥ cha = of king, in the interest of, also,; naya chakshuṣā = truthful-eyed; jāgrataḥ = are diligent.

Well-dressed and well-decorated and they the ministers of King Dasharatha, observe decency in the interest of the king and also of the kingdom, with diligence and with a truthful-eye. [1-7-16]

Sloka 17

guror guṇa gṛhītāḥ ca prakhyātāḥ ca parākrame |
videśeṣu api vijñātā sarvato buddhi niścayāḥ || 1-7-17

17. guroḥ = from their mentors, mother, father and teachers; guṇa gṛihītaḥ = good qualities, acquired; parākrame = expertise; prakyātaḥ = renowned; sarvataḥ = in all affairs; buddhi nischayaḥ = intellectual, determinations; videsheṣu api = in foreign countries, also; vijñātāḥ = famous.

They acquired good qualities from their mentors and they are renowned by their expertise, and even in foreign countries they are famous for their intellectual determinations in all affairs. [1-7-17]

Sloka 18

abhito guṇavantaḥ ca na ca āsan guṇa varjitāḥ |
sandhi vigraha tatvajñāḥ prakṛtyā saṃpadānvitāḥ | 1-7-18

18. abhitaḥ = versatile; guṇavantaḥ cha = virtuous ones, also; na cha āsan = not, also, there is; guṇa varjitāḥ = virtue, discarded ones; sandhi = truce; vigraha = war; tatvajñaḥ = determiners; prakṛityā = by nature; sampada anvitāḥ = opulence, possessing.

Versatile and virtuous are they the ministers and there is none who discarded his virtuosity, and they are the determiners of truce or war, and by their nature they possess opulence. [1-7-18]

Sloka 19

maṃtra saṃvaraṇe śaktāḥ śaktāḥ sūkṣmāsu buddhiṣu |
nīti śāstra viśeṣajñāḥ satatam priya vādinaḥ || 1-7-19

19. mantra samvaraṇe shaktāḥ = strategies, their confidentiality, capable of; shaktāḥ = capable of; sūkshmāsu = micro-affairs; buddhiṣu = applying mind; nīti shāstra vishesha jñaḥ = moral, science, comprehensively, known; satatam priya vādinaḥ = always, gentle, articulators.

They are capable to keep up the confidentialities of strategies, and also capable to apply their mind even in micro-affairs, and they know moral science comprehensively, and above all, they are gentle articulators. [1-7-19]

Sloka 20

īdṛśaiḥ taiḥ amātyaiḥ ca rājā daśaratho.anaghaḥ |
upapanno guṇopetaiḥ anvaśāsad vasuṃdharām || 1-7-20

20. anaghaḥ = exalted - [here, not sinless, but exalted king]; dasarathaḥ = Dasharatha; guṇa upetaiḥ = good-natured; īdṛishaiḥ = suchlike [that efficacious]; taiḥ amātyaiḥ cha = with those, ministers, also; upapannaḥ = accompanied with; vasundharām = the earth; anvashāsat = ruled.

Accompanied with such of those effectual and good-natured ministers the exalted king Dasharatha ruled the earth. [1-7-20]

Sloka 21

avekṣamāṇaḥ cāreṇa prajā dharmeṇa rakṣayan |
prajānām pālanam kurvan adharmam parivarjayan || 1-7-21
viśrutaḥ triṣu lokeṣu vadānyaḥ satya saṃgaraḥ |
sa tatra puruṣavyāghraḥ śaśāsa pṛthvīm imām || 1-7-22

20-21. puruṣa vyāghraḥ = manly, tiger [most generous among people]; chāreṇa avekshyamā = by spies, observing; prajāḥ = people; rakshyan = to protect; dharma = virtuously; prajānām pālanam kurvan = to people, good rule, to give; a dharmān pari varjayan = unrighteousness, entirely, giving up; vadānyaḥ = generous; satya sanagaraḥ = truthful, avowedly; triṣu lokeṣu vishrutaḥ = in three, worlds, renowned; pṛithvīm imām = the earth, this one; saḥ = he, Dasharatha; tatra = from there; shashāsa = ruled.

He that most generous one among men, Dasharatha, while observing through spies, and to protect people righteously, and to give a good governance to them, he forsook unrighteousness and became a generous king avowed to truthfulness alone, and thus he that Dasharatha ruled the earth, which rulership is renowned in all the three worlds. [1-7-21,22]

Sloka 23

na adhyagacchat viśiṣṭam vā tulyam vā śatrum ātmanaḥ |
mitravān nata sāmantaḥ pratāpa hata kaṇṭakaḥ |
sa śaśāsa jagat rājā divi deva patir yathā || 1-7-23

23. mitravān = one who has many friends; nata sāmanta = subdued, provincial kings; pratāpa hata kanṭakaḥ = by valour, eliminated, thorniness; ātmanaḥ = to himsef; vishiṣṭam vā = a superior one, either; tulyam vā = equal one, or; shatrum = an enemy; na adhyagacchat = not encountered; divi devapatiḥ yathā = in ḥeaven, Indra, like; saḥ shashāsa jagat = he, ruled, the world.

Emperor Dasharatha has not encountered either a superior or an equal in his kingship, and to him there are many friends, subdued are his provincial kings and eliminated is thorniness by his own valour. He thus ruled the world like Indra would in Heaven. [1-7-23]

Sloka 24

taiḥ maṃtribhiḥ maṃtra hiteḥ niviṣṭaiḥ
vṛto.anuraktaiḥ kuśalaiḥ samarthaiḥ |
sa pārthivo dīptim avāpa yuktaḥ
tejomayaiḥ gobhiḥ iva uditaḥ arkaḥ || 1-7-24

24. mantra hite niviṣṭaiḥ = in stratejñ, conducive, concerned; anuraktaiḥ = interested in; kushalaiḥ = well skilled; samarthaiḥ = efficient; taiḥ mantribhiḥ = with those, ministers; vṛitaḥ = surrounded by, in the company of; saḥ = he; pārthiva = king; uditaḥ arkaḥ [yathā] = rising, ṣun [as with]; yuktaḥ tejomayaiḥ gobhiḥ yuktaḥ = along with, resplendent, sunrays, having; dīptim avāpa = brilliance, obtained.

In the company of those ministers, who are conducive to the strategies, interested in the king and subjects as well, skilful and efficient ones, he that King Dasharatha obtained brilliance, like the rising Sun along with resplendent sunrays. [1-7-24]
