Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 32
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha is shocked to see Hanuma, perched on the branches of a shimshupa tree She is confused whether it is a dream or a reality. Finally, she thinks that as the monkey is talking to her in a manifested form, it is quite a reality. She prays Lord Brihaspati and Brahma to make he words spoken by Hanuma as true.


Sloka 1

tataḥ śākhā antare līnam dṛṣṭvā calita mānasā |
veṣṭitārjunavastram taṃ vidyutsamghātapiṅgalam || || 5-32-1

1. tataḥ = then; (ṣeetha); chalita mānasā = had her mind shaken; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = that ḥanuma; vidyutsanghāta piV^galam = who was tawny coloured; vidyutsanghāta = like a series of flashing thunder bolts; veṣṭitārjuna vastram = and wrapped up in a white cloth; līnam = hiding; shākhāntare = behind branches of ṣeetha beheld there a monkey.

ṭhen, seeing ḥanuma, who was tawny coloured like a series of flashing thunderbolts wrapped up in a white cloth and hiding behind the branches of a tree, Seetha had her mind shaken.

Sloka 2

sā dadarśa kapim tatra praśritam priya vādinam |
pullāśokotkarābhāsam taptacāmikarekṣaṇam || || 5-32-2

2. nā = that ṣeetha; dadarsha = beheld; tatra = there; kapim = a monkey; phulla shokotkarābhāsam = beaming like a cluster of Ashoka flowers wide opened; tapta chāmīkarekṣaṇam = with his eyes resembling refined gold; prashritam = humble; priya vādinam = and speaking agreeably.

ṣeetha beheld there a monkey, beaming like a cluster of Ashoka flowers wide opened, with his eyes resembling regined gold, humble and speaking agreeable words.

Sloka 3

maithilī cintayāmāsa svapno ayam iti bhāminī |
aho bhīmamidaṃ rūpaṃ vānarasya durāsadam || 5-32-3
durnirīkṣamiti jñātvā punareva mumoha sā |

3. maithilī = ṣeetha; gatā = got; paramam = a great; vismayam = surise; chintayāmāsa = and thought (as follows): aho = Alas!; idam = this; rūpam = appearance; vānarasya = of the monkey; durāsadam = is hard to be approached; bhīmama = fearful; durnirīkṣam = and difficult to be looked at; iti = thus; jJṇātvā = thinking; sā = she; mumoha = was bewildered; punareva = again.

ṣeetha got a great surprise and thought (as follows): "Alas! ṭhis monkey is hard to be approached, fearful and difficult to be looked at." Thus thinking, she was bewildered again.

Sloka 4

vilalāpa bhṛśaṃ sītā karuṇam bhayamohitā || 5-32-4
rāmarāmeti duḥkhārtā lakṣmaṇeti ca bhāminī |
ruroda bahudhā sītā mandam mandasvarā satī || 5-32-5

4; 5. sītā = ṣeetha; bhāminī = the splendid woman; bhaya mohitā = bewildered by fear; duḥkhārtā = afflicted with grief; vilalāpa = lamented; bhṛisham = most; karuṇam = piteously; rāma rāmeti = saying O; Rama! O; Rama!; lakṣmaṇeti cha = and O Lakshmana; sītā = ṣeetha; rurode = wept; mandam = faintly; mandasvarā = satī = in a low voice; bahudhā = in many ways.

ṭhe splendid woman ṣeetha, bewildered by fear and afflicted with grief, lamented most piteously saying "O, Rama! O, Rama! and "O Lakshmana!". Seetha wept faintly in a low voice in many ways.

Sloka 6

sā tam dṛṣṭvā hariśreṣṭhaṃ vinītavadupasthitam |
maithilī cintayāmāsa svapno.ayamiti bhāminī || 5-32-6

6. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = that; harishreṣṭham = excellent monkey; upasthitam = appraoching; vinītavat = humbly; sā = that; bhāminī = splendid woman; maithilī = ṣeetha; chintayāmāsa = thought; ayam = it; iti = as; svapnaḥ = a dream.

ṣeeing that excellent monkey appraching humbly, that splendid woman ṣeetha thought it as a dream.

Sloka 7

sā vīkṣamāṇā pṛthubhugnavaktraṃ śākhāmṛgendrasya yathoktakāram |
dadarśa piṅgapravaraṃ mahārhaṃ vātātmajaṃ buddhimatāṃ variṣṭham || 5-32-7

7. sā = that ṣeetha; vīkṣamāṇā = looking hither and thither; dadarsha = saw; vātātmajam = ḥanuma; pṛithubhngnavaktram = who had wide and curved mouth; yathokta kāram = doing as told; shākhāmṛigendrasya = by ṣugreeva the king of monkeys; piṇ^ga pravaram = the foremost of monkeys; mahārham = greatly respectable; variṣṭham = and excellent; buddhimatām = among the intelligent.

ṣeetha, looking hither and thither, saw ḥanuman, who was having a wide and curved mouth, doing acts as told by sugreeva the king of monkeys, the foremost of monkeys, greatly adored and the best among the inelligent.

Sloka 8

sā tam samīkṣya eva bhṛśam visamjñā gata asu kalpā iva babhūva sītā |
cireṇa samjñām pratilabhya caiva caiva vicintayāmāsa viśāla netrā || 5-32-8

8. samīkṣyaiva = as soon as seeing; tam = that ḥanuma; sā sītā = that ṣeetha; visamjṇāa = lost her consciousness; bhṛisham = very much; babhūva = and became; gatāsmkalpena = as though lifeless; pratilabhya = regaining; samjJṇām = her cnciousness; chireṇa = after a long time; vishāla netrā = the wide-eyed ṣeetha; bhūyaḥ = again; vichintayāmāsa = thought (as follows).

As soon as seeing ḥanuma ṣeetha lost her consciousness very much and became seemingly lifeless. Regaining her consciousness after a long time the wide eyed Seetha moreover thought (as follows)

Sloka 9

svapno mayā ayam vikṛto adya dṛṣṭaḥ śākhā mṛgaḥ śāstra gaṇaiḥ niṣiddhaḥ |
svasti astu rāmāya salakṣmaṇāya tathā pituḥ me janakasya rājñaḥ || 5-32-9

9. adya = today; shākhāmṛigaḥ = a monkey; vikṛitaḥ = in an ugly form; dṛiṣṭaḥ = was seen; mayā = by me; svapne = in a dream; niṣiddhaḥ = which is forbidden; shāstragaṇaiḥ = according to a series of scriptures; svasti astu = ṃay it be well; rāmāya = with Rama; salakṣmaṇāya = together with Lakshmana; tathā = and; together with Lakshmana; tathā = and; me pituḥ = with my father; janakasya rājJṇaḥ = king Janaka.

"ṭoday, I saw an ugly monkey in my dream, which is forbidden according to a body of scriptures. ṃay it be well with Rama together with Lakshmana and with my father, King Janaka.

Sloka 10

svapno api na ayam na hi me asti nidrā |
śokena duhkhena ca pīḍitāyāḥ |
sukham hi me na asti yato asmi hīnā |
tena indu pūrṇa pratima ānanena || 5-32-10

10. ayam = this; na asti = is not; svapno.api = a dream too; me = to me; yataḥ = since; pīDitāyāḥ = tormented; shokena = by sorrow; duḥkhena cha = and grief; nāsti hi nidrā = there is indeed no sleep; me = to me; asmi = who is; hīnā = bereft; tena = of Rama; indu pūrṇa pratimāna nena = whose countenance resembles a full moon; nāsti hi = there is indeed no; sukham = happiness.

"ṭhis is not a dream. ṭo me, tormented by sorrow and grief, there is indeed no sleep. ṭo me, separated from Rama whose countenance resembles a full moon, there is no happiness indeed!"

Sloka 11

rāmeti rāmeti sadaiva buddhyā vicintyā vācā bruvatī tameva |
tasyānurūpaṃ ca kathāṃ tadarthā mevaṃ prapaśyāmi tathā śṛṇomi || 5-32-11

11. vichintya = (since) I am thinking; buddhyā = with intellect; tameva = (about) that Rama only; bruvatī = and saying; vāchā = in my voice; rāmeti rāmeti = thus 'Rama! Rama!'; sadaiva = always; anurūpam = conformably; prapashyāmi = I am seeing; tathā = and; shṛiṇomi = hearing; kathām = story; evam = in this way; tadarthām = with the same object.

"ṣince I am pondering with my mind about that Rama only, and always saying 'Rama! Rama!' thus, confomably I am seeing and hearing a story thus corresponding to he same thought."

Sloka 12

aham hi tasya adya mano bhavena sampīḍitā tat gata sarva bhāvā |
vicintayantī satatam tam eva tathaiva paśyāmi tathā śṛṇomi || 5-32-12

12. sampīDitā = tormented; adya = today; manobhavena = by ṃanmatha; the god of love; tasya = about that Rama; tadgata sarva bhāvā = having all my feelings directed towards him; vichintayantī = and thinking; satatam = always; tameva = about him; tathaiva = in the like manner; aham pashyāmi = I am seeing; tathaiva = and in the like manner; shṛiṇomi = I am hearing (the same matters).

"ṭormented as I am today by manmatha god of love longing for Rama, my whole feelings directed towards him and constantly thinking of him alone, in the like manner I am seeing him and in the like manner i am hearing of him alone."

Sloka 13

mano rathaḥ syāt iti cintayāmi |
tathā api buddhyā ca vitarkayāmi |
kim kāraṇam tasya hi na asti rūpam |
suvyakta rūpaḥ ca vadati ayam mām || 5-32-13

13. chintayāmi = I am imagining; iti = that; manorathaḥ = my wish; syāt = will be fulfilled; tathā = likewise; vitarkayāmi = I am reasoning (it); buddhyāpi cha = by my intellect also; nāsti hi = there is indeed no; rūpam = form; tasya = for that wish; ayam = ḥe; suvyakta rūpaḥ = with a well-manifested form; vadati = is speaking; mām = about me; kim = what; kāraṇām = is the reason?

"I am imagining that my wish will be fulfilled. Likewise, I am reasoning my wish by my intellect also. There is no concrete form indeed for my wish. He, with a well manifested form is speaking to me. for what reason?"

Sloka 14

namo astu vācaḥ pataye savajriṇe svayambhuve caiva huta aśanāya |
anena ca uktam yat idam mama agrato vana okasā tac ca tathā astu na anyathā || 5-32-14

14. namaḥ = my salutation; vāchaspataye = to Brihaspati the lord of eloquence; savajriṇe = together with Indra; wielding a thunderbolt; svayambhuve chaiva = to Brahma; the self-existent Lord; hutāshanāya cha = and to the Lord of fire; eating the oblations; yat = whatever; idam = these words; uktam = are spoken; agrataḥ = before me; anena vanaukasā = by this monkey; astu = let them be so; tat astu = let it be so; tathā = in the like manner; na anyathā = let it not be otherwise!.

"ṃy salutation to Brihaspati the Lord of eloquence, together with Indra the wielder of a thunderbolt, to Brahma the self-existent Lord and to the Lord of Fire eating the oblations! Let whatever words spoken by this monkey be true! Let them not be untrue!"

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe dvātriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 32nd chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic
