Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 42
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

The demons break the news to Ravana about Lanka. Ravana having been laid siege to by the monkeys. Ravana then ascends his mansion and surveys the innumerable troops of monkeys, occupying the entire city of Lanka. ṃeanwhile, Rama issues a command to the monkeys to destroy the enemies forthwith. The monkeys start demolishing various important defensive structures of the city of Lanka and besiege all the city-gates. Ravana also commands his army to commence combat. Sounds of couches blown by the army and terrible roars on both sides re-echoes the air, earth and sea. Demons begin to strike the monkeys with their weapons and the monkeys respond the strike with trees, mountain-tops, nails and teeth.

Sloka 1

tatas te rākṣasās tatra gatvā rāvaṇa mandiram |
nyavedayan purīm ruddhām rāmeṇa saha vānaraiḥ || 6-42-1

1. tataḥ = then; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; tatra = there; gatvā = having gone; rāvaṇamandiram = to the house of Ravana; nyavedayan = informed (that); purīm = the city of Lanka; ruddhām = has been besieged; rāmeṇa = by Rama; vānaraiḥ saha = along with his monkeys.

Then, the demons there, approached Ravana and informed him that the city had been besieged by Rama along with his monkeys.

Sloka 2

ruddhām tu nagarīm zrutvā jāta krodho nizā caraḥ |
vidhānam dviguṇam zrutvā prāsādam so adhyarohata || 6-42-2

2. shrutvā = hearing; nagarīm = that the city; ruddhām = has been attacked; nishācharaḥ = Ravana; jātakrodhaḥ = exhibiting his anger; dviguṇam = doubly; kṛitvā = made; vidhānam = his arrangements; ārohatacha = and ascended; prāsādam = his mansion.

Hearing that the city has been attacked, Ravana exhibiting his anger, doubly made the necessary arrangements for the war and ascended his mansion.

Sloka 3

sa dadarza āvṛtām lankām sazaila vana kānanām |
asamkhyeyair hari gaṇaiḥ sarvato yuddha kānkṣibhiḥ || 6-42-3

3. saḥ = that Ravana; dadarsha = saw; laṇ^kām = the city of Lanka; sashailavanakānanām = with its mountains; grooves and forests; vṛitām = being covered; sarvataḥ = on all sides; asamkhyeyaiḥ = by innumerable; harigaṇaiḥ = troops of monkeys; yuddha kāṇkṣibhiḥ = looking for war.

That Ravana caught a glimpse of the city of Lanka, with its mountains, groves and forests all being covered by innumerable troops of monkeys, waiting for war.

Sloka 4

sa dṛṣṭvā vānaraih sarvām vasudhām kavalī kṛtām |
katham kṣapayitavyāh syur iti cintā paro abhavat || 6-42-4

4. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; vasudhām = the earth; kapilīkṛitām = made brown in colour; sarvaiḥ = with all; vanarāḥ = monkeys; saḥ = Ravana; abhavat = became; chintāparaḥ = lost in thought; iti = as to; katham = how; syuḥ = (they) can be; kṣapayitavyā = exterminated.

Seeing the earth made all brown with innumerable monkeys, Ravana in great perplexity reflected: "How can they be exterminated?"

Sloka 5

sa cintayitvā suciram dhairyam ālambya rāvaṇaḥ |
rāghavam hari yūthāmz ca dadarza āyata locanaḥ || 6-42-5

5. chintayitvā = having pondered; suchiram = for long; ālambya = regaining; dhairyam = his confidence; āyata lochanaḥ = and with his eyes widened by surprise; saḥ = that Ravana; dadarsha = saw; rāghavam = Rama; hariyūthāmshcha = and his troops of monkeys.

Having pondered for long, regaining his confidence and with his eyes widened by surprise, Ravana gazed on Rama and his troops of monkeys.

Sloka 6

rāghavaḥ sahasainyena mudito nāma pupluve |
laṅkām dadarśa guptām vai sarvato rākṣasairvṛtām || 6-42-6

6. rāghavaḥ = Rama; muditaḥ = delightful; saha sainyena = along with the army; pupluve nāma = pressed forward (on the back of a monkey); dadarsha = and saw; laṇ^Kām = Lanka; guptām = being guarded; sarvataḥ = on all sides; vṛitām = and thronged; rākṣasaiḥ = with demons.

Full of delight, Rama passed forward (on the back of a monkey) along with his army and saw Lanka being guarded on all sides and thronged with demons.

Sloka 7

dṛṣṭvā dāśarathirlaṅkām citradhvajapatākinīm |
jagāma manasā sītām dūyamānena cetasā || 6-42-7

7. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; laṇ^kām = Lanka; chitra dhvaja patākinīm = decorated with banners and flags; dāsharathiḥ = Rama; jagāma = recollected; manasā = in his mind; sītām = about ṣeetha; dūyamānena = with a distressed chetasā = heart.

Seeing that city of Lanka duly decorated with banners and flags, Rama remembered Seetha with a distressed heart.

Sloka 8

atra sā mṛgaśābākṣī matkṛte janakātmajā |
pīḍyate śokasaṃtaptā kṛśā sthaṇḍilaśāyinī || 6-42-8

8. (ḥe said to himself); sā jankātmajā = here is that ṣeetha; mṛiga shābākṣī = whose eyes resemble those of a fawn; shoka samtaptā = tormented with grief; kṛishā = emaciated; sthaṇDila shāyinī = with a bare ground as her bed; pīDyate = and suffereing matkṛite = on my account; atra = here.

He said to himself, "Here is that daughter of Janaka, whose eyes resemble those of a fawn, tormented with grief, emacitated, with a bare ground as her bed and suffering here on my account."

Sloka 9

nipīḍyamānāṃ dharmātmā vaidehīmanucintayan |
kṣipramājñāpayadrāmo vānarān dviṣatām vadhe || 6-42-9

9. anuchintayan = reflecting on; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; nipīDyamānām = being troubled; dharmātmā = the virtuous; rāmaḥ = Rama; kṣipram = speedily; ājJṇāpayat = issued a command; vānarām = to the monkeys; vadhe = to destroy; dviṣatām = the enemies.

Reflecting on Seetha being troubled, the virtuous Rama speedily issued a command to the monkeys to destroy the enemies forthwith.

Sloka 10

evamukte tu vacasi rāmeṇākliṣṭakarmaṇā |
saṃgharṣamāṇāḥ plavagāḥ simhanādai rapūryan || 6-42-10

10. vachasi = (While) the aforesaid words; uktesati = were spoken; evam = thus; rameṇa = by Rama; akliṣṭakarmaṇā = who was unwearied in action; plavagāḥ = the monkeys; samgharṣamāṇāḥ = vying with one another; āpūrayan = filled the air; simhanādaiḥ = with their roaring; resembling those of a lion.

Hearing those words of Rama, who was unwearied in action, the monkeys vying with one another filled the air with their roaring resembling those of lion.

Sloka 11

śikharairvikirāmaitāṃ laṅkām muṣṭibhireva vā |
iti sma dadhire sarve manāṃsi hariyūthapāḥ || 6-42-11

11. vikirāma = we shall tear asunder; etām laṇkām = this Lanka; shikharaiḥ = with mountain = peaks; muṣṭibhireva vā = or with fists alone; iti = thus; dadhire manāmsi = resolved ; sarve = all; hariyūthapāḥ = the monkey- generals.

"We shall tear asunder this Lanka with mountain-peaks or with fists alone." - thus resolved all the monkey- generals.

Sloka 12

udyamya giriśṛṅgāṇi mahānti śikharāṇi ca |
tarūṃścotpāṭya vividhāṃstiṣṭhanti hariyūthapāḥ || 6-42-12

12. hariyūthapāḥ = the monkey- generals; udyamya = having lifted; girishṛiṇgāṇi = the mountain-peaks; mahānti = and huge; shikharāṇi = rocks; utpāṭya = and having plucked up; vividhān = varius kinds; tarūmshcha = of trees; tiṣṭhanti = stood prepared (for the attack).

Lifting up mountain peaks and huge rocks and plucking up various kinds of trees, the monkey-generals stood prepared (for the attack).

Sloka 13

prekṣato rākṣasa indrasya tāny anīkāni bhāgazaḥ |
rāghava priya kāma artham lankām āruruhus tadā || 6-43-13

13. tadā = then; tāni anīkāi = those troops; bhāgashaḥ = forming into columns āruruhuḥ = began to scale the heights; laṇ^kām = of Lanka; rākṣasendrasya = (while) Ravana; prekṣataḥ = stood gazing; rāghava priya kāmārtham = in order to fulfill Rama's cherished desire.

In order to fulfill Rama's cherished desire, those troops, forming themselves into columns, began to scale the heights of Lanka, while Ravana stood gazing.

Sloka 14

te tāmra vaktrā hema ābhā rāma arthe tyakta jīvitāḥ |
lankām eva ahyavartanta sāla tāla zilā āyudhāḥ || 6-42-14

14. te = those monkeys; hemābhāḥ = of golden hue; tāmra vaktrāḥ = with coppery countenance; sāla bhūdhara yodhinaḥ = fighting with sala trees and mountain- peaks; abhyavartanta = marched ahead; laṇ^kam = towards Lanka; tyakta jīvitāḥ = ready to lay down their lives; rāmārthe = in Rama's sevice.

Those monkeys, of golden hue with coppery countenance, fighting with Sala trees and mountain-peaks, marched ahead towards the city of Lanka, ready as they were to lay down their lives in Rama's service.

Sloka 15

te drumaiḥ parvata agraiz ca muṣṭibhiz ca plavam gamāḥ |
prāsāda agrāṇi ca uccāni mamantus toraṇāni ca || 6-42-15

15. te plavaṇ^gamāḥ = those monkeys; mamanthuḥ = demolished; asamkhyāni = innumerable; prākārāṇi = defensive walls; toraṇānicha = and arches; drumaiḥ = (with blows) from trees; parvatāgraiḥ = mountain-tops; muṣṭibhishcha = an fists.

Those monkeys demolished innumerable defensive walls and arches with blows from trees, mountain-tops and fists.

Sloka 16

pārikhāḥ pūrayanti sma prasanna salila āyutāḥ |
pāṃsubhih parvata agraiz ca tṛṇaiḥ kāṣṭhaiz ca vānarāḥ || 6-42-16

16. vānarāḥ = the monkeys; pūrayantisma = filled; parikhaiḥ = the moats; prasanna salilāyutāḥ = containing clear water; pāmsubhiḥ = with sands; parvatāgraiḥ = mountain-tops; tṛiṇaiḥ = grasses; kāṣṭhaishcha = and logs of woods.

The monkeys filled the moats containing clear water with sands, mountain-tops, grasses and logs of wood.

Sloka 17

tataḥ sahasra yūthāz ca koṭi yūthāz ca yūthapāḥ |
koṭī zata yutāz ca anye lankām āruruhus tadā || 6-42-17

17. tataḥ = thereafter; yūthapāḥ = the commanders; āruruhuḥ = scaled (the walls); laṇ^kām = of Lanka; (taking with them); sahasrayūthāshcha = battalions of monkeys in thousands; koṭiyūthāshcha = in crores; koṭiyūtha shatāshcha = (and even) hundreds of crores; anye cha = along with other.

The commanders scaled the walls of Lanks, taking with them battalions of monkeys in thousands, in crores and even hundreds of crores.

Sloka 18

kāncanāni pramRdnantas toraṇāni plavam gamāḥ |
kailāsa zikhara ābhāni gopurāṇi pramathya ca || 6-42-18

18. pramardantaḥ = tearing up; kāṇchanāni = the golden; toraṇāni = arches; pramathyacha = and breaking down; gopurāṇi = the gates; kailāsa shikharābhāṇi = that equaled the peak of Kailasa the mountainous abode of ṣhiva the god of destruction; plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys (attacked Lanka).

The monkeys stood tearing up the golden arches and breaking down the gates that equaled the peak of Kailasa the mountainous abode of Shiva the Lord of Destruction.

Sloka 19

āplavantaḥ plavantaz ca garjantaz ca plavam gamāḥ |
lankām tām abhyavartanta mahā vāraṇa samnibhāḥ || 6-42-19

19. plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys; mahā vāraṇa samnibhāḥ = who resembled great elephants; abhidhāvanti = rushed; tām laṇ^kām = towards that Lanka; āplavanta = sprining; plavantashcha = leaping; garjantashcha = and roaring.

The monkeys who resembled great elephants hurled themselves towards that Lanka, springing, leaping and roaring.

Sloka 20

jayaty atibalo rāmo lakṣmaṇaz ca mahā balaḥ |
rājā jayati sugrīvo rāghaveṇa abhipālitaḥ || 6-42-20
ity evam ghoṣayantaz ca garjantaz ca plavam gamāḥ |
abhyadhāvanta lankāyāh prākāram kāma rūpiṇaḥ || 6-42-21

20; 21. plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys; kāma rūpiṇaḥ = who were able to change their form at will; ghoṣayantascha = shouting thus; jayati = victory; urubalaḥ = to the mighty; rāmaḥ = Rma; mahābalaḥ = and the valiant; lakṣmaṇashca = Lakshmana; jayati = victory; rājā = to the king; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; abhipālitaḥ = protected; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; garjantashcha = and roaring; abhyadhāvanta = rushed; prākāram = towards the defensive walls; laṇ^kāyāḥ = of Lanka.

The monkeys, who were able to change their form at will, shouting - "Victory to the mighty Rama and the valiant Lakshmana!" "Victory of Sugreeva protected by Raghava!" and roaring, rushed towards the defensive walls of Lanka.

Sloka 22

vīra bāhuḥ subāhuz ca nalaz ca vana gocaraḥ |
nipīḍya upaniviṣṭās te prākāram hari yūthapāḥ || 6-42-22
etasminn antare cakruh skandha āvāra nivezanam |

22. te = those; hariyūthapāḥ = monkey-generals; vīrabāhuḥ = Virabhabu; subāshcha = ṣubahu; nalashcha = ṇala; tathā = and panasaḥ = Panasa; nipīDya = having stormed; prākāram = the defensive walls; upaniviṣṭāḥ = took up position on them; etasmin antare = meanwhile; chakre = (they); formed; skandhāvāraniveshanam = an encampment of a multitude of military divisions.

Those monkey-generals Virabahu, Subahu, Nala and Panasa, having position on them. ṃeanwhile, they formed an encampment of a multitude of military divisions there.

Sloka 23

pūrva dvāram tu kumudah koṭibhir dazabhir vṛtaḥ || 6-42-23

23. balavān = the mighty; kumudaḥ = Kumuda; vṛitaḥ = surrounded; koṭibhiḥ = by a crore; haribhiḥ = of monkeys; jita kāshibhiḥ = who behaved like conquerors; tasthau = stood; āvṛitya = beseizing; pūrva dvāram = the eastern gate (while remaining stationed in north-east).

The mighty Kumuda, surrounded by a crore of monkeys, who behaved like conquerors, stood besieging the eastern gate (while remaining stationed in north-east).

Sloka 24

sahāyārthe tu tasyeva niviṣṭaḥ prasabho hariḥ || 6-42-24

24. prasabhaḥ hariḥ = A monkey called Prasabha; mahābāhuḥ = and the mighty armed; panasashcha = panasa; niviṣṭaḥ- took up their position; abhisamvṛitaḥ = surrounded; vānaraiḥ = by (other) monkeys; sahāyārthe = in order toa ssist; tasyaiva = Kumuda himself.

Surrounded by other monkeys, a monkey called Prasabha and the mighty armed Panasa too up their position, in order to assist Kumuda himself.

Sloka 25

āvṛtya balavāṃs tasthau vimzatyā koṭibhir vṛtaḥ |

25. balavān = the strong; vīraḥ = and valiant; shatabaliḥ kapiḥ = monkey; ṣhatabali; vṛitaḥ = surrounded; vimshatyā koṭibhiḥ = by twenty crores (of monkeys); āsādya = approached; dakṣiṇa dvāram = the southern gate (while remaining stationed in south-east); tasthau = and stood; āvṛitya = surrounding it.

The strong and valiant monkey, Shatabali accompanied by twenty crores of monkeys, approached the southern gate (while remaining stationed in south-east) and stood there, to obstruct the exit.

Sloka 26

suṣeṇah pazcima dvāram gatas tārā pitā hariḥ || 6-42-26

The mighty monkey named Sushena, the father of Tara (Vali's wife), going to the western gate (while remaining stationed in the south-west) surrounded by crores and croes of monkeys, stood besieging that gate.

Sloka 27

Rama along with Lakshmana and Sugreeva the mighty lord of the monkeys, seeking the northern gate (while remaining stationed in the north west) stood besieging that gate.

Sloka 28

ṛṣkāṇām bhīma vegānām dhūmrah zatru nibarhaṇaḥ || 6-42-28

28. dhūmraḥ = Dhumra; mahāvīryaḥ = of great prowess; shatrunibarhaṇaḥ = and annihilator of enemies; vṛitaḥ = accompanied; koṭyā = ṛikṣāṇām = by a crore of bears; bhīma kopānām = having terrific rage; tasthau = stood; pārshvataḥ = by the side; rāmsya = of Rama.

Accompanied by a crore of bears having terrific rage, Dhumra (brother of Jambavan, the king of bears) of great prowess and the annihilator of enemies, took up his position by the side of Rama.

Sloka 29

samnaddhas tu mahā vīryo gadā pāṇir vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-42-29

Vibhishana of great energy with mace in hand, clad in defensive armour and accompanied by his watchful ministers, took his position where the mighty Rama is stationed.

Sloka 30

gajo gava akṣo gavayah zarabho gandha mādanaḥ || 6-42-30

30. gajaḥ = Gaja; gavākṣaḥ = Gavaksha; gavayaḥ = Gavayaḥ = Gavaya; sharabhaḥ = sharabha; gandhamādanaḥ = and Gadhamdana; paridhāvantaḥ = galloping; samantāt = on every side; rarakṣuḥ = defended; hari vāhinīm = the army of monkeys.

Gavaksha, Gavaya, Sharabha and Gandhamadana, galloping on all sides, defended the army of monkeys.

Sloka 31

tataḥ kopa parīta ātmā rāvaṇo rākṣasa īzvaraḥ || 6-42-31

31. tataḥ = then; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣashvaraḥ = the lord of demons; kopa parītātmā = with his mind filled in anger; tadā = then; ājJṇāpayat = commanded; niryāṇam- for the decamping; sarva seinyānām = of all the army; drutam = immediately.

Ravana the lord of demons with his mind filled in anger, then commanded for the decamping of the entire army immediately.

Sloka 32

etaccrutvā tadā vākyaṃ rāvaṇasya mukheritam || 6-42-32

At this command coming from Ravana lips, a tremendous clamour arose among the demons.

Sloka 33

By beating with sticks of gold, the demons stirred up on every side, kettle drums whose discs were white as the moon.

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

With their handsome limbs equipped with couches, those rangers of night shone like clouds bordered with lightning accompanied by rows of cranes.

Sloka 36

The battalions rushed forth under Ravana's instructions like the rushing forth of the ocean, which is swollen by the clouds, at the time of universal dissolution.

Sloka 37

Then, from every side a clamour, arose from the army of monkeys, which filled ṃalaya mountain with its ridges, plains and caves.

Sloka 38

śaṅkhadundubhinirghoṣaḥ simhanādastarasvinām || 6-42-38
pṛthivīṃ cāntarikṣam ca sāgaraṃ cābhyanādayat |

38. shaṇkha du ndubhi nirghoṣaḥ = the sound of couches and drums; simhanādaḥ = and the lionine roars; tarasvinām = of those warriors; abhyanādayat = reechoed; pṛithivīm = over the earth; antarikṣamcha = sky; sāgaramcha = and sea.

The sound of couches and drums made by the demons and the leouine roars of those monkey- warriors re-echoed over the earth, sky and sea.

Sloka 39

gajānāṃ bṛṃhitaiḥ sārdhaṃ hayānāṃ heṣitairapi || 6-42-39
rathānāṃ neminirghoṣai rakṣasām padaniḥsvanaiḥ |

39. bṛihitaiḥ sārtham = with the trumpeting; gajānām = of elephants; heṣitairapi = the neighing; hayānām = of the horses; nemi nirghoṣaiḥ = the clatters of wheels; rathānām = of the chariots; pada niḥsvanaiḥ = and the sounds of the couches re-echoed over the earth; sky and sea).

With the trumpeting of elephants, the neighing of the horses, the clattering of the chariot-wheels and the sounds of the foot-steps, the sound of the couches reechoed over the earth, sky and sea.

Sloka 40

etasminn antare ghorah samgrāmah samapadyata || 6-42-40
rakṣasām vānarāṇām ca yathā deva asure purā |

40. etasmin antare = in the meantime; ghoraḥ = a terrible; samgrāmaḥ = struggle; samapadyata = ensued; rakṣasām = between the demons; vānarāṇām = and the monkeys; purā yathā = as; in former times; devāsure = between celestials and demons.

In the meantime, a terrible struggle ensued between the demons and the monkeys, as in the former times between celestials and demons.

Sloka 41

te gadābhih pradīptābhih zakti zūla parazvadhaiḥ || 6-42-41
nijaghnur vānarān ghorāh kathayantah sva vikramān |

41. kathayantaḥ = exhibiting; svavikramān = their native prowess; te = those demons; nijaghnuḥ = struck; sarvān = all; vānarān = the monkeys; gadābhiḥ = with their maces; shaktishūla parasvadhaiḥ = spears; harpoons and axes; pradīptābhiḥ = which were flaming.

Exhibiting their native prowess, the demons began to strike all the monkeys with their flaming maces, spears, harpoons and axes.

Sloka 42

tatho vṛkṣair mahā kāyāḥ parvata agraiz ca vānarāḥ || 6-42-42
nijaghnastāni rakṣāṃsi nagairdanaiśca veginaḥ |
rājā jayati sugrīva iti śabdo mahānabhūt || 6-42-43

42; 43. tataḥ = then; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; mahākāyāḥ = with huge bodies; veginaḥ = swiftly; nijaghnuḥ = struck; tāni rakṣāmsi = those demons; vṛikṣaiḥ = with trees; parvatāgraishcha = mountain-tops; nakhaiḥ = nails; dantaishcha = and teeth; jayati = victorious; rājā = is king; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; iti = thus; abhūt = arose; mahān = a loud; shabdaḥ = war-cry.

Then, the gigantic monkeys swiftly struck those demons with trees, mountain-tops, nails and teeth "Victorious is king Sugreeva" - thus arose a loud war-cry.

Sloka 44

rājan jaya jayetyuktvā svasvanāmakathāṃ tataḥ |
rākṣasāstvapare bhīmāḥ prākārasthāmahīṃ gatān || 6-42-44
vānarān bhindipālaiśca śūlai ścaiva vyadārayan |

44. tataḥ = then; apre = some other; bhīmāḥ = terrifying; rākṣasāstu = demons on their part; uktvā = uttering; iti = thus; jaya jaya = "Be winsome! Be victorious" sva sva nāma kathām = proclaimed their own respective names; prākārasthāḥ = (while some) standing on walls; vyadārayan = hacked; vānarān = at the monkeys; gatān mahīm = standing on the ground (below); bhindi palāishcha = with hooks and harpoons.

Then, some other terrifying demons on their part, shouting "Be winsome! Be victorious!", proclaimed their own respective names. While some demons standing on walls hacked at the monkeys standing on the ground (below) with hooks and harpoons.

Sloka 45

vānarācāpi samkruddhāh prākārasthān mahī gatāḥ || 6-42-45
rākṣasān pātayām āsuh samāplutya plavam gamāḥ |

45. samkruddhāḥ = the enraged; vānarāshchāpi = monkeys too; mahīm gatāḥ = who were standing on the ground; āplutya = leapt; kham = into the air; pātayāmāsuḥ = and dragged down; rākṣasān = those demons; prākārastāḥ = stationed on the walls; svabāhubhiḥ = (by seizing them) with their arms.

The enraged monkeys, who wee standing on the ground, leapt into the air and dragged down the demons stationed on the walls by seizing them with their arms.

Sloka 46

sa samprahāras tumulo māṃsa zoṇita kardamaḥ || 6-42-46
rakṣasām vānarāṇām ca sambabhūva adbhuta upamāḥ |

46. saḥ = that; tumulaḥ = tumultuous; samprahāraḥ = combat; rakṣasām = between demons; vānarāṇām cha = and monkeys; sambadhūvaḥ = became; adbhutopamaḥ = resembling a wonder; māmsa shoṇita kardamaḥ = leaving a mire of flesh and blood.

That tumultuous combat between demons and monkeys transformed into a wonder, leaving a mire of flesh and blood.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvyeyuddhakāṇḍe dvicatvāriṃśaḥ

Thus completes 42nd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
