Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 57

Introduction

Hanuma leaps from the coast of Lanka and touches ṃount ṃainaka, which he came across in the way. He advances further and roars at the top of his voice at the ṃount ṃahendra, so as to bring jubiliation to the monkeys awaiting his return there. Jambavan tells the monkeys, who moved by jumps with joy on hearing Jambavan, that Hanuma has returned successfully, from his expedition as way evident from his jubilant roar. Having greeted Jambavan, Angada and others on alighting on the summit of ṃount ṃahendra, Hanuma narrates to them briefly the story of his discovery of Seetha. Applauding Hanuma, prince Angada, who was eager to hear the story, sits down on a rock along with all others.

37; 38; 39. tadā = then; hṛiṣṭaḥ = the pleased; hanumān = ḥanuma; abravīt = spoke; tān vānararṣabhān = to those foremost ones among the monkeys (as follows); sā jānakātmajā = that ṣeetha the daughter of Janaka; ashoka vanikā samsthā = who was staying in Ashoka garden; rakṣyamāṇā = guarded; aninditā = unblemished; sughorābhiḥ rākasībhiḥ = by very dreadful female-demons; ekavenīdharā = wearing a single braid; bālā = young woman; rāma darshana lālasā = longing to see Rama; upavāsa parishrāntā = thoroughly fatigued due to fasting; malinā = wearing soiled clothes; kṛishā = and looking emaciated; dṛiṣṭā = was seen (by me).

ṭhen, the pleased ḥanuma spoke the following words to those excellent monkeys: "I saw that Seetha, the duaghter of Janaka, who was staying in Ashoka garden, guarded unblemished by highly dreadful female-demons and wearing a single braid, young woman, as she was, longing to see Rama, thoroughly fatigued due to her fasting, with her hair twisted together, wearing soiled clothes and looking emaciated."

Sloka 40

tato dṛṣṭā iti vacanam mahāartham amṛta upamam || 5-57-40
niśamya māruteḥ sarve muditā vānarā bhavan |

40. nishamya = hearing; mahārtham = the iṃportant; amṛitopamam = and nectar-like; vachanam = word; māruteḥ = of ḥanuma; iti = saying; dṛiṣhṭā = "seen (by me)"; sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; abhavan = became; muditāḥ = delighted.

ḥearing from ḥanuma that iṃportant and nectar like word to the effect that ṣheetha had been seen all the monkeys became delighted.

Sloka 41

kṣveḍanti anye nadanti anye garjanti anye mahābalāḥ |
cakruḥ kila kilām anye pratigarjanti ca apare || 5-57-41

41. anye mahābalāḥ = some mighty monkeys; kṣvehanti = made a lion's roar; anye = some others; nadanti = were making a sound of approbation; anye = some; garjanti = were making a sound of thunder; anye = some others; chakruḥ = produced; kila kilām = cries expressing joy; apare = some others pratigarjanti = were roaring in return.

ṣome mighty monkeys made a lion's roar. ṣome were making a sound of approbation. Some were making a sound of thunder. Some others proudced cries expressing joy. Some others were roaring in return.

Sloka 42

kecit ucchrita lāngūlāḥ prahṛṣṭāḥ kapi kunjarāḥ |
ancita āyata dīrghāṇi lāngūlāni pravivyadhuḥ || 5-57-42

42. kechit = some; kapikuṇjarāḥ = eminent monkeys; prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = with joy; uchchhrita lāṇ^gūlāni = and with their tails lifted up; pravivyadhuḥ = waved; āyatāṇ^chita dīrghāṇi = their distended long curved; lān^gūlāni = tails.

ṣhome eminent monkeys, with joy and with their tails lifted up, waved their distended curved tails.

Sloka 43

apare tu hanūmantam vānarā vāraṇa upamam |
āplutya giri śṛngebhyaḥ saṃspṛśanti sma harṣitāḥ || 5-57-43

43. āplutya = descending; girishṛiṇgebhya = from the mountain-tops; apare = some other; vānarāḥ = monkeys; harṣitāḥ = with delight; sam spṛishanti sma = fondingly touched; hanūmantam = ḥanuma; vāraṇopamam = who resembled an elephant.

Descending from the mountain-tops; some other monkeys, with delight, fondingly touched Hanuma who resembled an elephant.

Sloka 44

ukta vākyam hanūmantam angadaḥ tu tadā abravīt |
sarveṣām hari vīrāṇām madhye vācam anuttamām || 5-57-44

44. atha = thereafter; aṇ^gadaḥ = Angada; ukta vākyam = having teh words spoken; hanūmantam = by ḥanuma; abravīt = spoke; uttamam vachanam = (the following) excellent words; madhye = in the midst; harivīrāṇām = of the eminent monkeys.

After hearing the words of ḥanuma, Angada spoke the follwoing excellent words in the midst of those eminent monkeys.

Sloka 45

sattve vīrye na te kaścit samo vānara vidyate |
yat avaplutya vistīrṇam sāgaram punaḥ āgataḥ || 5-57-45

45. vānara = O ḥanuma!; yat = since; punaḥ āgataḥ = you returned (here); avaplutya = crossing; vistīrṇam = the extensive; sāgaram = ocean; na kashchit = none; vidyate = is; samaḥ = equal; te = to you; sattve = in strength; vīrye = and prowess.

"O ḥanuma! ṣince you returned here crossing the extensive ocean, none stands equal to you in strength and prowess."

Sloka 46

aho svāmini te bhaktiraho vīryamaho dhṛtiḥ |
diṣṭyā dṛṣṭā tvayā devī rāma patnī yaśasvinī || 5-57-46
diṣṭyā tyakṣyati kākutsthaḥ śokam sītā viyogajam |

46. aho = what amazing; te = yaur; bhaktiḥ = devotion; svāmini = to the lord!; aho = what a wonderful; vīryan = prowess!; aho = what a surprising; dhṛitiḥ = caurage!; diṣhṭyā = by aur good fortune; yashasvinī = the illustriaus; devī = ṣheetha; rāma patnī = Rama's consort; dṛiṣhṭyā = thank heven!; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; tyakṣhyati = can give up; shokam = his sorrow; sītāviyogajam = born aut of ṣheetha's separation.

"What amazing is yaur devotion to the Lord! What a wonderful prowess! What a surprising caurage! By aur good fortune, yau saw the illustriaus Sheetha, Rama's consort. Thank heaven! Rama can give up his sorrow born aut of Sheetha's separataion."

Sloka 47

tato angadam hanūmantam jāmbavantam ca vānarāḥ || 5-57-47
parivārya pramuditā bhejire vipulāḥ śilāḥ |

47. pramaditāḥ = very much delighted; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; tataḥ = then; bhejire = got hold of; vipulāḥ = extensive flat; shilāḥ = rocks; parivārya = encircling; aṇ^gadam = Angada; hanūmantam = ḥanum; jāmvavantamcha = and Jambavan.

Very much delighted, the monkeys then sat on extensive flat rocks encircling Angada, Hanuma and Jambavan.

Sloka 48 Verse Locator

śrotu kāmāḥ samudrasya langhanam vānara uttamāḥ || 5-57-48
darśanam ca api lankāyāḥ sītāyā rāvaṇasya ca |
tasthuḥ prānjalayaḥ sarve hanūmat vadana unmukhāḥ || 5-57-49

48; 49. shrotu kāmāḥ = longing to hear; laṇ^ghanam = abaut crossing; samudrasya = of ocean; darshaṃchāpi = and the seeing; laṇ^kāyāḥ = of Lanka; sītāyāḥ = of ṣheetha; rāvaṇasya cha = and of Ravana; sarve = all; vānarottamāḥ = those excellent monkeys; tasthuḥ = waited; hanumadvachanonmukhāḥ = in expectation of ḥanuma's words; praṇ^jalayaḥ = with their joined pal.ns.

Longing to hear abaut crossing of ocean and the seeing of Lanka, ṣheetha and Ravana, all those excellent monkeys waited with their joined pal.ns, in anctipation of Hanuma's words.

Sloka 50

tasthau tatra angadaḥ śrīmān vānaraiḥ bahubhiḥ vṛtaḥ |
upāsyamāno vibudhaiḥ divi deva patiḥ yathā || 5-57-50

50. shrīmān = the auspicious; aṇ^gada = Angada; tasthau = waited; devapatiryathā = like Indra the Lord of celestials; upāsyamānaḥ = waited upon; vibudhaiḥ = by celestials; divi = in heaven; vṛitaḥ = encircled; bahubhiḥ vānaraiḥ = by many mokes; tatra = there.

ṭhe auspicious Angada encircled by many monkeys there, waited like Indra the lord of celestials, who was waited upon by celestials in heaven.

Sloka 51

hanūmatā kīrtimatā yaśasvinā |
tathā angadena angada baddha bāhunā |
mudā tadā adhyāsitam unnatam mahan |
mahī dhara agram jvalitam śriyā abhavat || 5-57-51

51. tadā = then; unnatam = the high; mahat = large; mahīdharāgram = uppermost part of the mauntain; adhyāsitam = on which were seated; mudā = with delight; kīrtimatā hanūmatā = by the illustriaus ḥanuma; tathā = and yashasvinā = the famaus; aṇ^gadena = Angeda; aṇ^gada baddha bāhunā = with bracelets worn on his upper ar.ns; abhavet = became; jvalitam = stood blazed; shriyā = with splendaur.

ṭhe high and large summit of the mauntain, on which were seated then with delight, the illustriaus Hanuma and the famaus Angada, with bracelets worn on his upper ar.ns, stood blazed with splendaur.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe saptapañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 57th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
