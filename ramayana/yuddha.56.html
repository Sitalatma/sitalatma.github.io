Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 56
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Akampana enters the battle-field and assails the monkeys with his bow and arrows. Then, Hanuman comes to the rescue of the monkeys. A great fight ensued between Akampana and Hanuman. Hanuman strikes the head of Akampana with a huge tree and Akampana dies. All the demons take to their heels and return to Lanka.

Sloka 1

tad dṛṣṭvā sumahat karma kṛtam vānara sattamaiḥ |
krodham āhārayām āsa yudhi tīvram akampanaḥ || 6-56-1

1. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tat = that; sumahat = very great; karma = exploit; kṛitam = done; vānara sattamaiḥ = by the chiefs of monkeys; yudhi = in the battle; akampanaḥ = Akampana; āhārayāmāsa = was seized; tīvram = a fierce; krodham = anger.

Seeing that very great exploit executed by the chiefs of monkeys in the battle, Akampana was seized with a fierce anger.

Sloka 2

krodha mūrcita rūpas tu dhnuvan parama kārmukam |
dṛṣṭvā tu karma śatrūṇām sārathim vākyam abravīt || 6-56-2

2. dṛiṣṭvā = witnessing; karma = the work; shatrūṇām = of the enemies; krodha mūrchhitarūpaḥ = appearing fully of fierce anger; dhunvan = and moving; parama kārmukam = his great bow; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the following words; sārathim = to his charioteer.

Witnessing that work of the enemies, his features were distorted by anger and brandishing his powerful bow, Akampana addressed his charioteer in these words.

Sloka 3

tatra eva tāvat tvaritam ratham prāpaya sārathe |
ete atra bahavo ghnanti subahūn rākṣasān raṇe || 6-56-3

3. sārathi = O; charioteer!; ete balinaḥ = these strong monkeys; ghnanti = are killing; subahūn = innumerable; rākṣasān = demons; raṇe = in the battle; prāpaya = get; ratham = the chariot; tatraiva = just there; tvaritaḥ = soon.

"Drive the chariot quickly to that place, for, those monkeys are killing innumerable demons on the battle field."

Sloka 4

ete atra balavanto hi bhīma kāyāś ca vānarāḥ |
druma śaila praharaṇās tiṣṭhanti pramukhe mama || 6-56-4

4. ete = these; vānarāḥ = monkeys; balavantaḥ = who are strong; bhīmakopāshcha = with terrific anger; druma shaila praharaṇāḥ = with trees and rocks as their weapons; tiṣṭhanti = are standing; mama = in my; pramukhe = front; atra = here.

"Those arrogant monkeys of terrific ferocity, armed with trees and rocks, dare to affront me here."

Sloka 5

etān nihantum iccāmi samara ślāghino hy aham |
etaiḥ pramathitam sarvam dṛśyate rākṣasam balam || 6-56-5

5.aham ichchhami = I wish; nihantum = to kill; etān = these monkeys; samarashlāghinaḥ = who are praising themselves in battle; sarvam = all; balam = the army; rakṣasāam = of demons; dṛishyate = who are seen; pramathitam = to be crushed; etaiḥ = by them.

"I wish to kill those andicious monkeys, who are seen herassing all the army of demons."

Sloka 6

tataḥ prajavita aśvena rathena rathinām varaḥ |
harīn abhyahanat krodhāt śara jālair akampanaḥ || 6-56-6

6. tataḥ = thereafter; rathena = in his chariot; prachalitāshvena = drawn by fast-moving horses; akampanaḥ = Akampana; varaḥ = the most skillful; rathinām = of chariot-warriors; sharajālaiḥ = with a hail of darts; abhyapatat = assailed harīn = the monkeys; dūrāt = from a distance.

Thereafter, in his chariot, drawn by fast-moving horses, Akampana the most skillful of chariot-warriors, with a hail of darts, assailed the monkeys from a distance.

Sloka 7

na sthātum vānarāh śekuh kim punar yoddhum āhave |
akampana śarair bhagnāh sarva;eva pradudruvuḥ || 6-56-7

7. vānarāḥ = the monkeys; na shekuḥ = were no longer able; sthātum = to maintain their formation; āhave = in the battle; kim punaḥ = how much less; yuddhum = to fight; sarve eva = all of them; bhagnāḥ = were crushed; akampana sharaiḥ = under the shafts of Akampana; abhidudruvuḥ = and took to their heels.

The monkeys were no longer able to maintain their formation, much less fight in the battle. all of them were crushed under the shifts of Akampana and took to their heels.

Sloka 8

tān mṛtyu vaśam āpannān akampana vaśam gatān |
samīkṣya hanumān jnātīn upatasthe mahā balaḥ || 6-56-8

8. samīkṣya = seeing; tān = those; jJṇātīn = companions; āpannān = getting; mR6ityu vasham = under the sway of death; akampana sharānugān = pursued by Akampana's darts; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; hanumān = ḥanuman; upatasthe = went to their rescue.

Seeing those companions, getting under the sway of death pursued by Akampana's darts, the mighty Hanuman went to their rescue.

Sloka 9

tam mahā plavagam dṛṣṭvā sarve plavaga yūthapāḥ |
sametya samare vīrāh sahitāh paryavārayan || 6-56-9

9. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = him; mahā plavagam = the giant monkey; sarve = all; te = those; vīrāḥ = valiant; plavagarṣabhāḥ = and best of the monkeys; hahitāḥ = together; sametya = approached; paryavārayan = and surrounded him.

Seeing Hanuman their great leader, all those valiant and the best of the monkeys together rallied and grouped themselves boldly round him.

Sloka 10

vyavasthitam hanūmantam te dṛṣṭvā hari yūthapāḥ |
babhūvur balavanto hi balavantam upāśritāḥ || 6-56-10

10. dṛiṣṭvā = observing; hanūmantam = ḥanuman; vyavasthitam = standing comageously; te = those; plavagarṣabhaḥ = excellent monkeys; upāshritāḥ = took shelter; balavantam = in his powerfulness; babhūvuḥ = and became; balavantaḥ hi = indeed powerful.

Observing Hanuman's courage, those excellent monkeys took shelter in his powerfulness and became powerfulness and became powerful indeed.

Sloka 11

akampanas tu śaila ābham hanūmantam avasthitam |
mahā indra;iva dhārābhih śarair abhivavarṣa ha || 6-56-11

11. akampastu = Akampana; mahendraḥ iva = like ṃahendra; the lord of celestials; abhivavarṣaha = assailed; dharābhiḥ = a hail; sharaiaḥ = of arrows; hanūmantam = on ḥanuman; avasthitam = who remained as firm; shailābham = as a rock.

Akampana, like ṃahendra the Lord of celestials, assailed a hail of arrows on Hanuman, who remained as firm as a rock.

Sloka 12

acintayitvā bāṇa oghān śarīre patitān śitān |
akampana vadha arthāya mano dadhre mahā balaḥ || 6-56-12

12. achintayitvā = heedless; bāṇaughān = of the flood of weapons; patitān = that fell; sharīre = upon his body; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; kapiḥ = ḥanuman; manaḥ dadhre = resolved in his mind; akampanavadhārthāya = to slay Akampana.

Heedless of the flood of weapons that fell upon his body, the mighty Hanuman resolved in his mind to slay Akampana.

Sloka 13

sa prahasya mahā tejā hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
abhidudrāva tad rakṣah kampayann iva medinīm || 6-56-13

13. prahasya = laughing heartily; saḥ = that; hanumān = ḥanuma; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; mārutātmajaḥ = and thes on of ṃaruta the wind-god; abhirudrāva = leapt; tat rākṣaḥ = on that demon; medinīm = causing the earth; kampayanniva = to shake as it were.

Laughing heartily , that Hanuman, the son of ṃaruta the wind-god and possessing a great splendour, leapt on the demon, causing the earth to shake as it were.

Sloka 14

tasya abhinardamānasya dīpyamānasya tejasā |
babhūva rūpam durdharṣam dīptasya iva vibhāvasoḥ || 6-56-14

14. dīpta mānasya = while burning; tejasā = with energy; nardamānasya = and emitting yells; tasya = his; rūpam = form; babhūva = became; durdharṣam = difficult to overpower; dīptasya vibhāvasoḥ iva = like a blazing fire; atha = thereafter.

As he roared burning with energy, the form of Hanuman became difficult to overpower, like a blazing fire.

Sloka 15

ātmānam tv apraharaṇam jnātvā krodha samanvitaḥ |
śailam utpāṭayām āsa vegena hari pumgavaḥ || 6-56-15

15. jJṇātvā = knowing; ātmānam = himself; a praharaṇam = to be bereft of any weapon; haripuṇ^gavaḥ = ḥanuman the excellent monkey; krodha saman votaḥ = with anger; utpāṭayāmāsa = uplifted; shailam = a mountian; vegena = quickly.

Knowing himself to be bereft of any weapon, Hanuman the excellent monkey, with a rage, uplifted a mountain quickly.

Sloka 16

tam gṛhītvā mahā śailam pāṇinā ekena mārutiḥ |
vinadya sumahā nādam bhrāmayām āsa vīryavān || 6-56-16

16. saḥ = that; vīryavān = valiant; mārutiḥ = ḥanuman; gṛihītvā = seizing; sumahāshailam = very great mountain; ekena pāṇinā = with one hand; vinadye mahānādam = letting up a roar; bhrāmayāmāsa = began to spin it rapidly.

That valiant Hanuman, seizing a very great mountain with one hand and letting up a roar, began to spin it rapidly.

Sloka 17

tatas tam abhidudrāva rākṣasa indram akampanam |
yathā hi namucim samkhye vajreṇa iva puram daraḥ || 6-56-17

17. tataḥ = thereafter; purā iva = as formerly; samkhye = in an encounter; purandaraḥ = Indra the Lord of celestials; vajreṇa = (hurled) his thunderbolt; namuchimiva = at ṇamuchi; (ḥanuman); abhirudrāva = rushed; tam akampanam = towards that Akampana.

Thereafter, as formerly in an encounter Indra the Lord of celestials hurled his thunderbolt at Namuchi, Hanuman rushed towards Akampana.

Sloka 18

akampanas tu tad dṛṣṭvā giri śṛngam samudyatam |
dūrād eva mahā bāṇair ardha candrair vyadārayat || 6-56-18

18. dṛiṣṭvā = beholding; samudyatam = uplifted; girishṛiṇ^gam = mountain-peak; akampanstu = Akampana on his part; adārayat = shattered it;dūrādeva = even from a distance; mahā bāṇaiḥ = by mighty arrows; ardhachandraiḥ = each having the shape of a crescent.

Beholding that crag flying towards him, Akampana shattered it even from a distance by means of his great crescent-shaped arrows.

Sloka 19

tat parvata agram ākāśe rakṣo bāṇa vidāritam |
vikīrṇam patitam dṛṣṭvā hanūmān krodha mūrcitaḥ || 6-56-19

19. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = that; parvatāgram = rocky peak; rakṣobāṇa vidāritam = shattered by the demon's arrows; patitam = and falling; vikīrṇam = in pieces; ākāshee = in the sky; hanumān = ḥanuman; krodha mūrchhitaḥ = became mad with anger.

Seeing that rocky peak shatterd in the air by the demon's arrows and falling in pieces, Hanuman became mad with anger.

Sloka 20

so aśva karṇam samāsādya roṣa darpa anvito hariḥ |
tūrṇam utpāṭayām āsa mahā girim iva uccritam || 6-56-20

20. samāsādya = approaching; ashvakarṇam = an Ashvakarna tree; mahāgirimiva = as large as a mountain; roṣadarpānvitaḥ = in the transport of rage that possessed him; saḥ = that; hariḥ = ḥanuman; utpāṭayāmāsa = uprooted it; tūrṇam = speedily.

Approaching an Ashwakarna tree as large as a mountain, in the transport of rage that possessed him, that Hanuman uprooted it speedily.

Sloka 21

tam gṛhītvā mahā skandham so aśva karṇam mahā dyutiḥ |
pragṛhya parayā prītyā bhrāmayāmāsa bhūtale || 6-56-21

21. saḥ = that ḥanuman; mahādutiḥ = of great strength; gṛihītvā = taking hold; tam ashvakarṇam = of that Ashwakarna tree; mahā skandam = with large branches; pragṛihya = and tightly holding it; parayā prītyā = with grea delight; bhrāmayāmāsa = spinned it; bhūtale = on the ground.

Taking hold of that Ashvakarna tree with large branches, in his great strength, Hanuman with immense delight spinned it, while standing on the ground.

Sloka 22

pradhāvann uru vegena prabhanjaṃs tarasā drumān |
hanūmān parama kruddhaś caraṇair dārayat kṣitim || 6-56-22

22. paramakṛiddhaḥ = the highly enraged; hanumān = hanuman; pradhāvan = began to run; uruvegena = with great strides; babhaṇ^jaḥ = breaking down; drumān = the trees; tarasā = by his strength; dārayan = and tearing up; mahīm = the earth; charaṇaiḥ = with his feet.

Then, the highly enraged Hanuman began to run with great strides, breaking down the trees by his strength and tearing up the earth with his feet.

Sloka 23

gajāṃsḥ ca sagaja ārohān sarathān rathinas tathā |
jaghāna hanumān dhīmān rākṣasāṃś ca padātikān || 6-56-23

23. hanumān = ḥanuman; jaghāna = struck down; gajāmshcha = elephants; sagajārohān = as also who rode upon them; rathinaḥ = and charioteers; sarathān = with their chariots; bhīmān = and the terrific; padātigān = infantry; rākṣasān = of demons.

Hanuman struck down elephants as also who rode upon them and charioteers with their chariots and the terrific infantry of demons.

Sloka 24

tam antakam iva kruddham samare prāṇa hāriṇam |
hanūmantam abhiprekṣya rākṣasā vipradudruvuḥ || 6-56-24

24. abhiprekṣya = seeing; tam hanūmantam = that ḥanuman; antakmiva = like unto Yama the Destroyer of Vital breaths; kruddham = full of wrath; sadrumam = aimed with a tree; praṇahāriṇam = and taking off lives; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; vipradudruvuḥ = took to flight.

Seeing that Hanuman, like unto Yama the Destroyer of vital Breaths, full of wrath armed with a tree and taking off lives, the demons took of flight.

Sloka 25

tam āpatantam samkruddham rākṣasānām bhaya āvaham |
dadarśa akampano vīraś cukrodha ca nanāda ca || 6-56-25

25. vīraḥ = the valiant; akampanaḥ = Akampana; dadarsha = beheld; tam = that ḥanuman; samkruddham = who was enraged; āpatantam = and rushing on; bhayāvaham = sowing terror; rākṣasānām = among his demons; chukṣobha cha = was greatly perturbed; nanāda cha = and set up a mighty shout.

The valiant Akampana, beholding that Hanuman who was enraged and rushing on, sowing terror among his soldiers, was greatly perturbed and set up a mighty shout.

Sloka 26

sa caturdaśabhir bāṇaih śitair deha vidāraṇaiḥ |
nirbibheda hanūmantam mahā vīryam akampanaḥ || 6-56-26

26. saḥ akampanaḥ = that Akampana; dasha dashabhiḥ = with his fourteen; nishitaiḥ = sharp; bāṇaiḥ = arrows; deha dāraṇaiḥ nirbibhedha = pierced that tore the body; mahā vīryam = the highly valiant; hanumantam = ḥanuman.

With fourteen sharp arrows that tore the flesh, that Akampana pierced the highly valiant Hanuman.

Sloka 27

sa tathā pratividdhas tu bahvībhih śara vṛṣṭibhiḥ |
hanūmān dadṛśe vīrah prarūḍha;iva sānumān || 6-56-27

27. viprakīrṇaḥ = Riddled; shita shaktibhiḥ = with sharp-pointed; nārāchaiḥ = iron shafts; saḥ vīraḥ = that warrior; hanumān = ḥanuman; dadṛishe = appeared; sānumāniva = like a mountain; pravūDhaḥ = on which number of plants sprang up.

Riddled with sharp-pointed shafts, that warrior Hanuman appeared like a mountain on which a number of plants shooted up.

Sloka 28

virarāja mahāvīryo mahākāyo mahābalaḥ |
puṣpitāśokasamkāśo vidhūma iva pāvakaḥ || 6-56-28

28. mahābalaḥ = the mighy ḥanuman; mahāvīryaḥ = of great strength; mahā kāyaḥ = with a large body; virarāja = shone; pāvakaḥ iva = like a fire; vidhūmaḥ = without smoke; puṣpitāshoka samkāshaḥ = and resembling an Ashoka tree in flowering.

That mighty Hanuman of great strength with a large body shone like a fire without smoke and resembled a blooming Ashoka tree.

Sloka 29

tato anyam vṛkṣam utpāṭya kṛtvā vegam anuttamam |
śirasy abhijaghāna āśu rākṣasa indram akampanam || 6-56-29

29. tatḥ = thereafter; kṛitvā = showing; anuttamam = a great; vegam = haste; utpāṭya = in uprooting; anyam = another; vṛikṣam = tree; (ḥanuman); jaghāna = struck; āshu = quickly; shirasi = on the head; akampanam = of Akampana; rākṣasendram = the General of demons.

Thereafter, showing a great haste in uprooting another tree, Hanuman struck the head of Akampana the General of demons.

Sloka 30

sa vṛkṣeṇa hatas tena sakrodhena mahātmanā |
rākṣaso vānara indreṇa papāta sa mamāra ca || 6-56-30

30. hataḥ = struck; vṛikṣeṇa = with a tree; tena vānarendraṇa = by that ḥanuman; sakrodhena = the enraged; mahātmanā = and the high-souled; saḥ = that; rākṣasaḥ = demon; papāta = fell down; mamāracha = and died.

Struck with a tree by that high-souled and enraged Hanuman, that demon fell down and died.

Sloka 31

tam dṛṣṭvā nihatam bhūmau rākṣasa indram akampanam |
vyathitā rākṣasāh sarve kṣiti kampa;iva drumāḥ || 6-56-31

31. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam akampanam = that Akampana; rākṣasendram = the leader of demons; bhummau = on the ground; nihatam = killed; sarve = all; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; vyathitāḥ = were perturbed; drumāḥ iva = as trees; kṣitikampe = during an earth-quake.

Seeing their leader Akampana lying lifeless on earth, all the demons trembled as the trees do during an earthquake.

Sloka 32

tyakta praharaṇāh sarve rākṣasās te parājitāḥ |
laṅkām abhiyayus trastā vānarais tair abhidrutāḥ || 6-56-32

32. sarve = all; te = those; parājitāḥ = defeated; rākṣasāḥ = demons; abhiyayuḥ = were going; laṇ^kām = towards Lanka; trāsā = due to fear; tyakta praharaṇāḥ = abandoning their weapons; abhidrutāḥ = and having been attacked; taiḥ vānaraiḥ = by those monkeys.

All those defeated warriors, throwing their arms, escaped in the direction of Lanka, terrified, pursued by those monkeys.

Sloka 33

te mukta keśāḥ sambhrāntā bhagna mānāh parājitāḥ |
sravat śrama jalair angaih śvasanto vipradudruvuḥ || 6-56-33

33. mukta keshāḥ = their hair loosened; sambhrāntāḥ = bewildered; bhagnamānāḥ = their pride broken; parājitāḥ = by the defeated; aṇ^gaiḥ = their limbs; sravachchhramajalaiḥ = dripping with sweat; te = those demons; vipradudruvuḥ = fled; shvasantaḥ = blowing out their breaths.

Their hair loosened, bewildered, their pride broken by defeat, their limbs dripping with sweat, those demons fled, blowing out their breaths.

Sloka 34

anyonyam pramamantus te viviśur nagaram bhayāt |
pṛṣṭhatas te susammūḍhāh prekṣamāṇā muhur muhuḥ || 6-56-34

34. sammūDhāḥ = mad; bhayāt = with fear; prekṣyamāṇāḥ = looking; pṛiṣṭhataḥ = back; muhurmuhuḥ = again and again; pramathnantaḥ = crushing; anyonyam = each other; te = they; vivishuḥ = entered; nagaram = the city.

ṃad with fear, looking back again and again and crushing each other in haste, they entered the city.

Sloka 35

teṣu lankām praviṣṭeṣu rākṣaseṣu mahā balāḥ |
sametya harayah sarve hanūmantam apūjayan || 6-56-35

35. teṣu rākṣaseṣu = (when) those demons; praviṣṭeṣu = had entered; laṇkām = Lanka; sarve = all; mahābalāḥ = the mighty; harayaḥ = monkeys; sametya = approached; hanūmantam = ḥanuman; apūjayan = and paid homage (to him).

When those demons had entered Lanka, all the mighty monkeys approached Hanuman and paid homage to him.

Sloka 36

so api prahṛṣṭas tān sarvān harīn sampratyapūjayat |
hanūmān sattva sampanno yathā arham anukūlataḥ || 6-56-36

36. saḥ = that; hanumānapi = ḥanuman; sattva sampannaḥ = of noble nature; sampratya pūjayat = honoured; sarvān = all; tān = those; prahṛiṣṭaḥ = delighted; harīn = monkeys; yathārham = in accord with their rank; anukūlataḥ = as per the occasion.

That Hanuman, of noble nature honoured all those delighted monkeys, in accord with their rank and the occasion.

Sloka 37

vinedusḥ ca yathā prāṇam harayo jita kāśinaḥ |
cakarṣusḥ ca punas tatra saprāṇān eva rākṣasān || 6-56-37

37. jitakāshinaḥ = the triumphant; harayaḥ = monkeys; vineduḥ = shouted; yathā prāṇam = in accord with their might; punaḥ = and once again; chakṛiṣushcha = dragged; rākṣesān = the demons; saprāṇāneva = who were still alive; tatra = there on the battle field.

The triumphant monkeys shouted in accord with their nighty and once again dragged the demons who were still alive there on the battle-field.

Sloka 38

sa vīra śobhām abhajan mahā kapiḥ |
sametya rakṣāṃsi nihatya mārutiḥ |
mahā asuram bhīmam amitra nāśanam |
yathaiva viṣṇur balinam camū mukhe || 6-56-38

38. saḥ mahākapiḥ = that great monkey; mārutiḥ = born of ṃaruta; sametya = having encountered; nihatya = and killed; rakṣāmsi = the demons; abhajat = enjoyed; vīra shobham = the heroic lustre; viṣṇuryathā = as Vishnu; (when he overcame); mahāsuram = the mighty demons; bhīmam = of terror; urubalam = of immense power; amitranāshanam = destroying his enemies; chamūmukhe = in the forefront of the battle.

That great monkey, born of ṃaruta having encountered and killed the demons, enjoyed the same renown as Vishnu when he overcame the mighty and terrific demon of immense power destroying his enemies in the forefront of the battle.

Sloka 39

apūjayan deva gaṇās tadā kapim |
svayam ca rāmo atibalaś ca lakṣmaṇah |
tathaiva sugrīva mukhāh plavam gamā |
vibhīṣaṇaś caiva mahā balas tadā || 6-56-39

39. tadā = then; devagaṇāḥ = the troops of deities; rāmaḥ = along with Rama; svayam = himself; atibalaḥ = the exceedingly strong; lakṣmaṇaḥ cha = Lakshmana; tathaiva = and; sugrīva mukhāḥ = ṣugreeva etal; plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys; tathā = and; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; vibhīṣaṇashchaiva = Vibhishana; apūjayam = paid homage; kapim = to ḥanuman.

Then, the troops of deities along with Rama himself, the exceedingly strong Lakshmana, Sugreeva and other monkeys and the mighty Vibhishana paid homage to Hanuman.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ṣaṭpañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 56th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
