Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 21
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

This chapter describes Lakshmana's anger and Rama consoling Lakshmana.

Sloka 1

tathā tu vilapantīm tām kausalyām rāma mātaram |
uvāca lakṣmaṇo dīnaḥ tat kāla sadṛśam vacaḥ || 2-21-1

1. lakshhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; dīnaḥ = became dejected; uvācha = spoke; vachaḥ = the words; tatkāla sadṛisham = suitable for that time; tām kausalyyām = to that kausalya; rama mātaram = Rama's mother; tathā = thus; vilapantīm = weaping.

Lakshmana became dejected and spoke these words, properly befitting for that time, to that Rama's mother Kausalya who was thus weeping.

Sloka 2

na rocate mama api etat ārye yad rāghavo vanam |
tyaktvā rājya śriyam gaccet striyā vākya vaśam gataḥ || 2-21-2

2. "ārye = Oh; the venerable lady! atat = this; na rochate = is not liking; mamāpi = to me also; itiyat = as in the manner this; rāghavaḥ = Rama; vākyārasham gataḥ = being influenced by the words; striyāḥ = of a woman; gachchhet = go; vanam = to forest; tyaktvā = leaving; rājya srīyam = prosperous kingdom."

"Oh, the honoured lady ! I also do not like Rama to be influenced by the words of a woman and go to the forest, leaving the prosperous kingdom."

Sloka 3

viparītaḥ ca vṛddhaḥ ca viṣayaiḥ ca pradharṣitaḥ |
nṛpaḥ kim iva na brūyāc codyamānaḥ samanmathaḥ || 2-21-3

3. "nṛipaḥ = king; viparītasheha = with perverted mind; vṛidhdhhashcha = old aged; pradharshhitaḥ = who is outraged; vishhayaiḥ = by sensual enjoyments; samanimadhaḥ = who is possessed of passion; na brūyāt = can he not talk; kimiva = of anything; chodyamānaḥ = prompted (by Kaikeyi).

"The king with perverted mind, of old age, one who is outraged by sensual enjoyments and possessed of passion can talk any thing, prompted by Kaikeyi."

Sloka 4

na asya aparādham paśyāmi na api doṣam tathā vidham |
yena nirvāsyate rāṣṭrāt vana vāsāya rāghavaḥ || 2-21-4

4. na pashyāmi = I can neither see; aparādham = offence; na pashyāmi = nor can I see; doshshamapi = even fault; asya = in him; tathā vidham = that can; yena = by any means; nirvāsyate = expel; rāghavaḥ = Rama; rāshhṭrāt = from state ; vanavāsāya = to forest."

"I can not see any offence or cause for blame in Rama that can expel him from the state to the forest."

Sloka 5

na tam paśyāmy aham loke parokṣam api yo naraḥ |
svamitro.api nirasto.api yo.asyadoṣamudāharet || 2-21-5

5. aham = I; na pashyāmi = have not seen; loke = in this world; tam = such a person; udāharet = who talks; dosham = ill; asya = of Rama; parokshhamapi = even indirectly; yaḥ naraḥ = that person who is; svamitro api = even an enemy; nirasto api = or an expellee."

I have not seen in this world any person, whether an enemy or an expellee, speaking ill of Rama indirectly."

Sloka 6

deva kalpam ṛjum dāntam ripūṇām api vatsalam |
avekṣamāṇaḥ ko dharmam tyajet putram akāraṇāt || 2-21-6

6. "kaḥ = who; avekshhamāṇaḥ = observing; dharmam = ethicalness; akāraṇāt = causelessly; tyajet = get rid of; putram = son; devakalpam = who is equal to god; ṛijum = who is honest; dāntam = who is self-restrained; vatsalam = who is affectionate; ṛipūṇāmapi = even towards enemies?"

"Can anybody observing ethicalness, causelessly get rid of a son who is equal to god who is honest, who is self-restrained and who is affectionate even towards adversaries?"

Sloka 7

tat idam vacanam rājñaḥ punar bālyam upeyuṣaḥ |
putraḥ ko hṛdaye kuryāt rāja vRttam anusmaran || 2-21-7

7. "kaḥ = which son; anusmaran = who remembers; raja vṛittam = royal usage; kuryāt = act; hṛidaye = with heart; tat idam vachanam = such of these words; rājJṇaḥ = of king; upeyushhaḥ = who got; bālyam = childhood punaḥ = again?"

"Which son, knowing royal usage, can agree to the words of this king who is behaving as though he got childhood again?"

Sloka 8

yāvad eva na jānāti kaścit artham imam naraḥ |
tāvad eva mayā sādham ātmastham kuru śāsanam ||2-21-8

8. "yāvadeva = even till; kashchit = any; naraḥ = person; na jānāti = does not know; ivam artham = this matter; tāva deva = in the mean time; kuru = make; shāsanam = dominion; ātmastham = your own; mayā sa ardham = together with me."

"Even before others get to know about this matter, make this dominion your own, with my help."

Sloka 9

mayā pārśve sadhanuṣā tava guptasya rāghava |
kaḥ samartho adhikam kartum kṛta antasya iva tiṣṭhataḥ || 2-21-9

9. rāghava = Oh; Rama! guptasya = protected; mayā = by me ; parshve = on your side; sadhamshhā = along with a bow; tishhṭhataḥ = you standing; kṛitāntasyeva = like Yama; the god of death; kaḥ = who; samarthaḥ = the competent one; kartum = to do; adhikam = too much?"

"Oh, Rama! While I am standing by your side along with a bow, protecting you who are standing as God of Death, who is capable of doing too much ?"

Sloka 10

nirmanuṣyām imām sarvām ayodhyām manuja ṛṣabha |
kariṣyāmi śaraiaḥ tīkṣṇaiḥ yadi sthāsyati vipriye || 2-21-10

10. manushharshhabha = Oh the best of men! sthāsyati yadi = If it stands; vipriye = disagreeable to you; karishhyāmi = I shall make; imām = this; kṛitsnām = entire; ayodhyam = Ayodhya; nirmanushhyām = deserted of people; sharaḥ tīkshhṇaiḥ = with sharp arrows."

"Oh, Rama, the best of men! If the city of Ayodhya turns against you, I shall make it desolate of men with sharp arrows"

Sloka 11

bharatasya atha pakṣyo vā yo vā asya hitam iccati |
sarvān etān vadhiṣyāmi mṛdur hi paribhūyate || 2-21-11

11. "atha = and vadhishhyāmi = I shall kill; etām sarvān = all those; pakshhyovā = who are siding; bharatasya = Bharata; yovā = and who; ichchhati = desire; asya = his; hitam = benefit; mṛiduḥ = soft person; paribhūyate hi = indeed gets disgraced."

"I shall kill all those who are siding Bharata ,and are favourable to him. Soft person indeed gets disgraced!"

Sloka 12

protsāhito.ayam kaikeyyā sa duṣṭo yadiḥ pitā |
amitrabhūto nissaṅgam vadhyatām badhyatāmapi || 2-21-12

12. "protsāhitaḥ = Instigated; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi saḥ = such of; naḥ putrā = our father; dushhṭaḥ = as bad person; amitra bhūtaḥ = becomes enemy; ayam badhyatām = let him be imprisoned; mi ssaṇgam = without personal attachment; vādhyatāmapi = and be killed!"

"If our father with an evil mind behaves like our enemy with instigation by Kaikeyi. I shall keep him imprisoned with out personal attachment or if necessary, kill him."

Sloka 13

gurorapyavaliptasya kāryākāryamajānataḥ |
utphatham pratipannasya kāryam bhavati śāsanm || 2-21-13

13. gurorapi = Even for a venerable person; avaliptasya = who is arrogant; ajānataḥ = who does not know; kāryākāryam = good and evil actions; pratipunnasya = who resorts to; utpatham = wrong route; shāsanam = punishment; bhavati = becomes; kāryam = feasible."

"Even a venerable person is to be punished, if he becomes arrogant, if he does not know good and evil actions and if he takes to a wrong route."

Sloka 14

balameṣa kimāśritya hetum vā puruṣarṣabha |
dātumicchati kaikeyya rājyam sthitamidam tava || 2-21-14

14. purushharshhabha = Oh the best of men! kim = what; balam = strength; hetum vā = or reason; eshhaḥ = he; āstritya = has taken shelter; ichchhati = desiring ; dātum = to give; idam = this ; rājyam = kingdom; tava sthitam = belonging to you; kaikeyyai = for Kaikeyi.

"Oh, Rama, the best of men! On what strength or season has he taken shelter to give away this kingdom belonging to you to Kaikeyi?"

Sloka 15

tvayā caiva mayā caiva kṛtvā vairam anuttamam |
kasya śaktiḥ śriyam dātum bharatāya ari śāsana || 2-21-15

15. arishāsana = Oh; the chastiser of enemies ! kā shaktiḥ = what ability is there; asya = to him; dātum = to give; shriyam = high rank; bharatāya = to Bharata; kṛitvā = by making; anuttamam = great; vairam = enmity; tvayā chaiva = with you; mayā chaiva = and with me."

"Oh, Rama the chestiser of enemies! Where is the ability for him to give kingdom to Bharata, by making great enmity against you and me "

Sloka 16

anuraktaḥ asmi bhāvena bhrātaram devi tattvataḥ |
satyena dhanuṣā caiva dattena iṣṭena te śape || 2-21-16

16. devi = Oh; queen! tattvataḥ = really; anuraktaḥ asmi = I am attached; bhāvena = devotedly; bhrātaram = my brother Rama; shape = I am swearing an oath; te = to you; satyena = by truth; dhanushhāchaiva = by bow; daltena = by the act of giving; ishhṭena = by the act of sacrifice."

"Oh, queen! Really, I am devotedly attached to my brother Rama. I am swearing an oath to you by truth by bow, by the act of giving and by the act of sacrifice."

Sloka 17

dīptam agnim araṇyam vā yadi rāmaḥ pravekṣyate |
praviṣṭam tatra mām devi tvam pūrvam avadhāraya || 2-21-17

17. devi = oh;queen! ramaḥ pravekshhyati yadi = If Rama can enter; dīptam = blazing; agnim = fire; araṇyam vā = or forest; tvam = you avadhāraya = make certain; pūrvam = even before; mām = mine; pravishhṭam = who can enter; tatra = there.

"Oh, queen! If Rama can enter blazing fire or forest, you make certain that even before, I can enter there"

Sloka 18

harāmi vīryāt duhkham te tamaḥ sūryaiva uditaḥ |
devī paśyatu me vīryam rāghavaḥ caiva paśyatu || 2-21-18

18. harāmi = I shall alleviate; te duḥkham = your grief; vīryāt = by my valour; tama iva = as darkness; uditaḥ = by rising; sūryaḥ = sun; pashyatu devī = Let the queen see; me vīryam = my valour; pashyatu rāghavashchaiva = Let Rama also see."

"I shall alleviate your grief, by showing my valour like the rising sun alleviating darkness .Let Rama and yourself see my valour"

Sloka 19

etat tu vacanam śrutvā lakṣmaṇasya mahātmanaḥ |
uvāca rāmam kausalyā rudantī śoka lālasā || 2-21-19

19. shrutva = ḥearing; etat vachanam = this word; lakshmaṇasya = of Lakshmana; mahāt manaḥ = the magnanimous; kausalya = Kausalya; shokalālasā = entirely; given up to anguish; rudāti = weaping; uvācha = spoke; rāmam = to Rama.

Hearing these words of Lakshmana the magnanimous, Kausalya entirely given up to anguish and weaping, spoke thus to Rama.

Sloka 20

bhrātus te vadataḥ putra lakṣmaṇasya śrutam tvayā |
yad atra anantaram tat tvam kuruṣva yadi rocate || 2-21-20

20. putra = Oh;son shrutam = It is heard tvayā = by you; te = your; bhrātuaḥ = brother lakshhmanasya = Lakshmana; vadataḥ = speaking; rochate yadi = If you agree; kurushhva = you do; yat = what; kāryam = action to be done; anantaram = immediately after."

"Oh, son !You have heard the words of Lakshmana .If you agree, you do what is to be done, immediately."

Sloka 21

na ca adharmyam vacaḥ śrutvā sapatnyā mama bhāṣitam |
vihāya śoka samtaptām gantum arhasi mām itaḥ || 2-21-21

21. na arhasi = you are not suited; gantum = to go; itaha = from here; vihāya = leaving; mām = me; sokasamtaptām = in deep grief; shrutvā = hearing; adharmyam vachaḥ = the un-just words; bhāshitam = said; mama sapatnyā = by my co-wife.

"It is not proper for you to go away from here, leaving me afflicted with grief, after hearing the un-just words spoken by my co-wife."

Sloka 22

dharmajña yadi dharmiṣṭho dharmam caritum iccasi |
śuśrūṣa mām ihasthaḥ tvam cara dharmam anuttamam || 2-21-22

22. dharmajJṇa = Oh; the knower of goodness! tvam ichchhasi yadi = If you desire; charitum = to practice dharmam = virtue; dharnushhṭaḥ = be a virtuous man; ihasthaḥ = stay here; shushrūshha = serve mām = me chara = practice anuttamam = best dharmam = goodness"

"Oh, Rama! You are righteous man. If you desire to practice virtue, be a virtuous man and stay here serving me thereby practicing the best morality in the form of serving your mother.

Sloka 23

śuśrūṣur jananīm putra sva gṛhe niyataḥ vasan |
pareṇa tapasā yuktaḥ kāśyapaḥ tridivam gataḥ || 2-21-23

23. kāsyapaḥ = sage; kasyapa niyataḥ = with self restraint; vasan = was residing; svagṛihe = in own house; yuktaḥ = accompanied; vareṇa tapasā = with great austerity; sushrūshhuḥ = of serving; jananīm = the mother; gataḥ = went; tridivam = to heaven"

"Oh, son! Formerly Kasyapa was staying in his own house itself with self restraint, performed great austerity by serving his mother and went to heaven"

Sloka 24

yathā eva rājā pūjyaḥ te gauraveṇa tathā hi aham |
tvām na aham anujānāmi na gantavyam itaḥ vanam || 2-21-24

24. yathaiva = Just as rājā = king; gouraveṇa = by respectable feeling; te = to you; puUjyaḥ = is worthy of worship; aham = I am also; tathā hi = indeed so; nānujānāmi = I am not permitting ; tvām = you; nagantavyam = it is proper to go; itaḥ = from here; vanam = to forest"

"Just as the king, by respectable feeling to you is worthy of worship, so also being your mother, I am worthy of worship to you. Hence, do not go to the forest"

Sloka 25

tvad viyogān na me kāryam jīvitena sukhena vā |
tvayā saha mama śreyaḥ tṛṇānām api bhakṣaṇam || 2-21-25

25. tvadviyogāt = Because of your separation; na kāryam = there is no purpose; jīvitena = for life; su khena vā = or comforts; mama = for me; shreyaḥ = it is good; bhakshhaṇam api = even to eat; tṛiṇānām = grass; tvayā saha = while staying with you"

"After your separation, I shall have no use of this life or comforts for me, it is good even to eat grass while staying with you"

Sloka 26

yadi tvam yāsyasi vanam tyaktvā mām śoka lālasām |
aham prāyam iha āsiṣye na hi śakṣyāmi jīvitum || 2-21-26

26. tvām yāsyasiyadi = If you go; vanam = to forest; tyaktvā = leaving; mām = me; shokalālasām = who is afflicted with grief; aham = I prāyam asishhye = shall seek death through starvation; iha = here; na shakshhyāmi hi = Indeed; I shall not be able; jīvutum = to live

"I shall not live if you leave for the forest, leaving me who is afflicted with grief. I shall seek death through starvation"

Sloka 27

tataḥ stvam prāpsyase putra nirayam loka viśrutam |
brahma hatyām iva adharmāt samudraḥ saritām patiḥ || 2-21-27

27. putra = Oh;son! tataḥ = ṭhereafter; tvam = you; prāpsyase = will attain; lokavishrutam = the universally famous; nirayam = hell; brahmahatyāmiva = like obtaining the sin of killing a Brahmana by; samudraḥ = the god of ocean; saritāmpatiḥ = lord of rivers adharmāt = due to un justice behaviour"

"Oh, son! If I fast myself to death for your sake, you will attain the universally famous hell, like the god of the ocean obtaining trouble because of his un justice behaviour, towards the sage called Pippalada, producing the latter's wrath."

Sloka 28

vilapantīm tathā dīnām kausalyām jananīm tataḥ |
uvāca rāmaḥ dharma atmā vacanam dharma samhitam || 2-21-28

28. tataḥ = thereafter; dharmātma = righteous words ; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; vachanam = the words; dharmasamhitam = attended with virtuous; kausalyām = to Kausalya; jananīm = the mother; tathā = thus; vilapantīm = weaping; dīnām = miserably.

The honest Rama spoke these virtuous words to his mother Kausalya, who was thus weeping miserably.

Sloka 29

na asti śaktiḥ pitur vākyam samatikramitum mama |
prasādaye tvām śirasā gantum iccāmy aham vanam || 2-21-29

29. nāsti = there is no; shaktiḥ = ability; mama = for me ; samatikramitum = to violet; pituḥ = father's; vākyam = words; aham = I; prasādaye = am asking for favour; tvām = to you; shirasā = with head; ichchāmi = I wish gantum = to go; vanam = to forest."

"I am not able to violate my father's words. I am bowing my head and asking your favour. I shall have to go to the forest"

Sloka 30

ṛṣiṇā ca pitur vākyam kurvatā vrata cāriṇā |
gaur hatā jānatā dharmam kaṇḍunā api vipaścitā || 2-21-30

30. gouḥ api = Even a cow; hatā = was killed; kurvatā = acting as per; pituḥ = father's; vākyam = words; kandunā = by Kandu; ṛishhiṇā = the sage; vratachāriṇā = who performed religious vows; jānatā = who knew; dharmam = righteousness; vipashchita = who was learned."

"A sage called Kandu, who knew righteousness, who performed religious vows and who was a learned man, killed even a cow, acting as per his father's words"

Sloka 31

asmākam ca kule pūrvam sagarasya ājñayā pituḥ |
khanadbhiḥ sāgaraiḥ bhūtim avāptaḥ sumahān vadhaḥ || 2-21-31

31. pūrvam = previously; sāgaraiḥ = by the sons of ṣagara; asmākam = of our; kulecha = race also; sumahān = a great vadhaḥ = killing; avāptaḥ = was obtained; kharadbhiḥ = while digging up; bhūmim = the earth; ājJṇayā = by the order of ; sagarasya = of ṣagara pituḥ = their father."

"Previously, the sons of Sagara belonging to our race, were got bitterly digging up the earth, as commanded by their father."

Sloka 32

jāmadagnyena rāmeṇa reṇukā jananī svayam |
kṛttā paraśunā araṇye pitur vacana kāriṇā || 2-21-32

32. rāmeṇa = by Parasurama; jāmadagniyaina = the son of Jamadagni; renuka = Renuka; janamī = mother kṛittā = was cut off; svayam = spontaneously ; parashunā = by axe; araṇye = in the forest; vachana kāriṇā = as per words; pituḥ = of father;

"Parasu Rama, the son of Jamadagni personally slayed his mother Renuka in the forest with an axe as per his father's words."

Sloka 33

etairanyaiśca bahubhirdevi devasamaiḥ kṛtam |
piturvacanamaklībam kariṣyāmi piturśitam || 2-21-33

33. devi = Oh;queen! etaiḥ = by these; bahubhiḥ anyaisheha = by many others; devasamānaiḥ = equivelant to gods -pituḥ = father's; vachanam = words kṛitam = was made aklībam = no waste; karishhyāmi = I shall do; pituḥ = father's hitam = assignment"

"Oh, mother! These and many others who were equivalent to gods, did not make their father' s words to go waste. I shall also do what is desired by my father"

Sloka 34

na khalv etan mayā ekena kriyate pitṛ śāsanam |
etairapi kṛtam devi ye mayā tava kīrtitāḥ || 2-21-34

34. devi = Oh;queen! etat = ṭhis; pitṛishasanam = father's command; na kriyate = was not done mayā = by me; ekena = only; etairapi = Also by these; ye = who kīrtitā = were; mentioned tava = to you; mayā = by me; kṛitam = it was done."

"Oh, mother! I am not the only person to act according to father's command. Those I have mentioned to you hitherto also complied with their father's command."

Sloka 35

nāham dharmamapūrvam te pratikūlam pravartaye |
pūrvaiḥ ayam abhipretaḥ gataḥ mārgo anugamyate || 2-21-35

35. "aham = I; na pravartaye = am not setting out; apūrvam = new; pratikūlam = contradicting; dharmam = customary conduct; te = for you; anugamyate = I am following; ayam = this; mārgam = way; abhipretaḥ = agreed; gataḥ = followed by; pūrvaiḥ = the ancient."

"I am not setting any new contradicting customary conduct for your sake. I am adhering to the way agreed and followed by the ancient."

Sloka 36

tat etat tu mayā kāryam kriyate bhuvi na anyathā |
pitur hi vacanam kurvan na kaścin nāma hīyate || 2-21-36

36. na kriyate = It cannot be done; anyathā = otherwise; mayā = by me; tat etat = than such of this; kāryam = practice; bhuvi = on earth; na hīyate nāma = ṇo deprivation indeed; kashchit = for anyone kurvam = doing; pituḥ = father's; vachanam = words."

"I cannot do otherwise than acting in accordance with father's words, the prevalent practice on earth. There is no deprivation indeed for anyone who complies with father's commands."

Sloka 37

tām evam uktvā jananīm lakṣmaṇam punar abravīt |
tava lakṣmaṇa jānāmi mayi sneham anuttamam || 2-21-37

37. shreshhṭhaḥ = the best; vākyavidām = of those knowing the speech; shreshhṭaḥ = the best; sarva dhanushhṭatām = of all those wearing the bow; evam = thus; uktāva = spoke; tām jananīm = to that mother; punaḥ = again; abravīt = spoke; lakshmanam = to Lakshmana.

Rama, the best of those who speak skilfully and the best of all those wearing the bow, spoke thus to his mother and turned round to Lakshmana to speak.

Sloka 38

tava lakṣmaṇa jānāmi mayi snehamanuttamam |
vikramam caiva satyam ca tejaśca sudurāsadam || 2-21-38

38. lakshmana = Oh; Lakshmana! jānāmi = I know tava = your anuttamam = highest sneham = affection mayi = in me vikramam chaiva = heroic valour sattvam cha = strength; sudurāsadam = un assailable tejashcha = splendour."

"Oh, Lakshmana ! I know your highest affection towards me, your heroic valour ,your strength and your unassailable splendour ."

Sloka 39

mama māturmahadduḥkhamatulam śubhalakṣamṇa |
abhiprāyam avijñāya satyasya ca śamasya ca || 2-21-39

39. subhakshhaṇa = Oh; Lakshmana; with good attributes! atulam mahat duḥkham = there is an unqualled and great grief; mama mātuḥ = to my mother; aviṇ^yāya = by not knowing; abhiprāyam = the meaning; satyasya cha = of truth and shamasyacha = tranquility.

" Oh, Lakshmana, with good attributes! ṃy mother is feeling a great and unequalled sorrow, by not knowing the secret of truth and tranquillity."

Sloka 40

dharmaḥ hi paramaḥ loke dharme satyam pratiṣṭhitam |
dharma saṃśritam etac ca pitur vacanam uttamam || 2-21-40

40. dharmaḥ = righteousness; paramohi = is indeed the best; loke = in the world; satyam = truth; pratishhṭhiam = is established; dharma = in righteousness; etat = these; uttamam = best; vachanamcha = words also; pituḥ = of father; dharmasamshnitam = are enjoined in righteousness.

"Righteousness is the best of all qualities in the world. Truth is established in righteousness. Even these best words of father are enjoined with righteousness."

Sloka 41

saṃśrutya ca pitur vākyam mātur vā brāhmaṇasya vā |
na kartavyam vṛthā vīra dharmam āśritya tiṣṭhatā || 2-21-41

41. vīraḥ = oh; Lakshmana the valiant! tishhṭhatā = one who is āshritya = following; dharmam = righteousness; nakartavyam = is not obliged; vṛithā = to waste; vākyam = the word; samshrutya = promised; pituḥ = to father or; māturvā = mother or; brahmanasya vā = Brahmana.

"One who follows righteousness, does not waste his promise given to one's father or mother or Brahmana."

Sloka 42

so aham na śakṣyāmi pitur niyogam ativartitum |
pitur hi vacanāt vīra kaikeyyā aham pracoditaḥ || 2-21-42

42. vīra = oh; the valiant! saḥ aham = I; being such person; nashakshhyāmi ativartitum = cannot trasgrass; niyogam = the command; pituḥ = of father; vachanāt = for the word; pituḥ = of father; aham = I; prachoditohi = was indeed instigated; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi.

" I cannot transgress my father's command. On the word given by my father only, Kaikeyi instigated me to go to the forest."

Sloka 43

tat enām visṛja anāryām kṣatra dharma āśritām matim |
dharmam āśraya mā taikṣṇyam mad buddhir anugamyatām || 2-21-43

43. tat = hence; visṛija = leave; anāryām = the mean; matim = mentality; kshhatra dharmāshritām = of followig military heroism; āshraya = take refuge; dharamam = in righteousness; mā = not; taikhhṇyam = in rudeness; anugamya = follow; madbuadhiḥ = my perception.

"Hence, leave this mean mentality of military heroism. Observe righteousness and not rudeness. follow my perception."

Sloka 44

tam evam uktvā sauhārdāt bhrātaram lakṣmaṇa agrajaḥ |
uvāca bhūyaḥ kausalyām prānjaliḥ śirasā ānataḥ || 2-21-44

44. lakshhmaṇāgrajaḥ = Rama; the elder brother of Lakshmana; evam = thus; uktvā = spoke; sauhārdāt = affectionately; bhrātaram = to brother; nataḥ = bowed; shirasā = with head; prāṇjaliḥ = with folded hands; uvācha = spoke; bhūyaḥ = again; kausalyām = to Kausalya.

Rama spoke thus to his brother affectionately, bowed to his mother and with folded hands, spoke again to Kausalya as follows.

Sloka 45

anumanyasva mām devi gamiṣyantam itaḥ vanam |
śāpitā asi mama prāṇaiḥ kuru svastyayanāni me || 2-21-45

45. devī = oh; mother! anumanyasva = permit; mām = me; gamishhyantam = who is about to go; vanam = to forest; shāpitā asi = on my life; kuru = invode; svastyanāni = blessings; me = on me.

"Oḥ, mother! I have decided to go to forest. Please give me permission. I am swearing to you on my life. Please invoke blessings on me."

Sloka 46

tīrṇa pratijñaḥ ca vanāt punar eṣyāmy aham purīm |
yayātiriva rājarṣiḥ purā hitvā punardhivam || 2-21-46

46. purā = in olden times; iva = as; rājarshhiḥ = the sage king; yayātiḥ = named yayāti; hitvā = left; divam = heaven;divam = reached heaven; punaḥ = again; aham eshhyāmi = I can come; punaḥ = back; purīm = to city; vanāt = from forest; tirṇa pratijJṇyaḥ = after fulfuilling the promise.

"As sage king named Yayati in olden times left the heaven and reached back heaven again, I shall be back to the city from the forest, after fulfilling my promise."

Sloka 47

śokassaṃdhāryatām mātarhṛdaye sādhu mā śucaḥ |
vanavāsādihaiṣyāmi punaḥ kṛtvā piturvacaḥ || 2-21-47

47. mātaḥ = oh; mother! māshuchaḥ = do not grieve; samdhāryām = bear; sādhu = well; hṛidaye = in heart; eshhyāmi = I shall come; iha = here; punaḥ = again; vanavāsāt = from forest; kṛitvā = after fulfilling; pituḥ = father's; vachaḥ = command.

"Oh, mother! Please do not grieve, Bear it well in your heart. I shall come back here again from the forest after fulfilling the father's command."

Sloka 48

tvayā mayā ca vaidehyā lakṣmaṇena sumitrayā |
piturniyoge sthātavyameṣa dharmaḥ sanāanaḥ || 2-21-48

48. pituḥ niyoge = In father's command; sthhātavyam = should be remainded; tvayā = by you; mayā cha = by me; vaidehyā = by ṣita; lakshhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; sumitrayā = by ṣumitra; eshhaḥ = this is; sanātanaḥ = eternal; dharmaḥ = custom.

"You and I, Sita, Lakshmana, Sumintra and all of us should follow father's directions. This is an eternal custom."

Sloka 49

amba samhṛtya sambhārān duḥkham hṛdi nigṛhya ca |
vanavāsakṛtā buddhirmama dharmyānuvartyatām || 2-21-49

49. amba = oh; mother! mama buddhiḥ = my intention; vanavāsa kṛitā = of proceeding to forest; dharmyā = which is righteous; anuvartyatām = be approved; samhṛitya = duly with drawing; sambhārān = preparations for coronation; nigṛihyacha = keeping in check; duḥkham = sorrow; hṛidi = in heart.

"Oh mother! Please approve my righteous intention of going to the forest, duly withdrawing preparations for my coronation and keeping in check the sorrow in your heart."

Sloka 50

etadvaca stasya niśamya mātā |
sudharmyamavyagramaviklabam ca |
mṛteva samjñām pratilabhya devī |
samīkṣya rāmam punarityuvāca || 2-21-50

50. mātā = the mother; devī = kausalya; nishamya = heard; tasya = that Rama's; vachaḥ = words; etat = here; sudharmyam = which were the most virtuous; avyagram = which wer cool; aviklabam cha = and which were not unsteady; pratilabhya = regained; saṇjJṇyām = consciousness; mṛiteva = looked at; rāmam = Rama; uvācha = spoke; punaḥ = again; iti = thus .

The mother Kausalya heard the most virtuous, cool and steady words of Rama and rose, as though regained consciousness once more after death, looked at Rama and spoke thus again.

Sloka 51

yathaiva te putra pitā tathāham |
guruḥ svadharmeṇa suhṛttayā ca |
na tvānujānāmi na māṃvihāya |
suduḥkhitāmarhasi gantumevam || 2-21-51

51. putra = oh; son! aham = I am also; guruḥ = respectable person; te = to you; pitā yathaiva = as indeed your father; svadharmeṇa = in my own right; suhṛittayācha = and due to great affection; nānujānāmi = I do not permit; tvā = you; nārhasi gantum = to go; evam = thus; vihāya = leaving; mām = me ; suduḥkhitām = in great sorrow.

"Oh, son! I am also as respectable a person to you as your father, in my own right and due to great affection. I do not give permission to you. You cannot thus go away, leaving me in great sorrow."

Sloka 52

kim jīviteneha vinā tvayā me |
lokena vā kim svadhayā.amṛtena |
śreyo muhūrtam tava sannidhānam |
mameha kṛtsnādapi jīvalokāt || 2-21-52

52. kim = what is the use; jīvitena = of living; iha = here; me = to me; tvayāvinā = without you? kim = what is the use; lokena = of the other world; svadhayā = or the oblation of food offered to the deceased ancestors; amṛitenavā = or the nectar of immortality? tava = your; samnidhānam = proximity; muhūrtam = even for a moment; shreyaḥ = is better; kṛitsnāt = than entire; jīvalokāt api = world of living beings too.

"What is the use of my living in this world without you? What is the use of the other world or the oblation of food offered to the deceased ancestors or the nectar of immortality? Your proximity even for a moment is better than that of the entire world of living beings."

Sloka 53

narairivolkābhirapohyamāno |
mahāgajo.adhvānamanupraviṣṭaḥ |
bhūyaḥ prajajvāla vilāpamevam |
niśamya rāmaḥ karuṇam jananyā || 2-21-53

53. nishamya = hearing; karuṇam = pathetic; vilāpam = lamentation; jananyā = of mother; prajajvāla = he was highly agonised; bhūyaḥ = again; mahāgaja iva = as a great elephant; ampravishhṭaḥ = having entered; adhvānam = way; apohyamānaḥ = being taken away; naraiḥ = ny men; ulkābhiḥ = with flames of fire.

After hearing the pathetic lamentation of his mother, he was highly anguished, as when an elephant is removed highly from its way by men with flames of fire.

Sloka 54

sa mātaram caiva visamjñakalpā |
mārtam ca saumitri mabhiprataptam |
dharme sthito dharmyamuvāca vākyam |
yathā sa evārhati tatra vaktum || 2-21-54

54. saḥ = that Rama; sthitaḥ = who was established; dharme = in righteousness; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = the words; dharmyam = endued with virtue; yathā = in which manner; tatra = at that time; sa eva = he only; arhati = was fit; vaktum = to speak; mātaramchaiva = to mother; visaṃjJṇakalpām = appearing unconscious; saumitrim = to Lakshmana; ārtam = who was disturbed; abhiprataptam = and exhausted with distress.

Rama, who was established in righteousness, spoke these words endowed with virtue, to his mother who was looking unconscious and to Lakshmana who was disturbed and exhausted with distress. He was the only person worthy of speaking thus at that time.

Sloka 55

aham hi te lakṣmaṇa nityameva |
jānāmi bhaktim ca parākramam ca |
mama tvabhiprāya masannirīkṣya |
mātrā sahābhyardasi mā suduḥkham || 2-21-55

55. lakshmana = Oh; Lakshmana! aham = I; nityameva = always; jānāmi = know; te = your; bhakitmacha = devotion and; parakramamcha = strength; tu = but; mātrā saha = along with mother; abhyardamasi = you are afflicting; suduḥkham = much pain; mama = to me; asannirirīkshhya = without looking at; mama = my; abhiprāyam = opionon.

"Oh, Lakshmana! I always know your devotion towards me as well as your strength. But now, you are joining with mother in afflicting much trouble to me without looking at my opinion."

Sloka 56

dharmārthakāmāḥ khalu tāta loke |
samīkṣitā dharmaphalodayeṣu |
te tatra sarve syurasaṃśayam me |
bhāryeva vaśyābhimatā suputrā || 2-21-56

56. tāta = Oh; father a ter (of affection addressed to a junior or senior); loke = in the world; dharmaphalodayeshhu = in obtaining the fruit of good works; dharmārthakāmāḥ = righteousness; utility and free will; samīkshhitāḥ = are looked at; bhāryeva = as a wife; vashyā = who is obedient; abhimatā = who is beloved; suputrā = who is having good sons; tatra = in those good words; te sarve = all those; syuḥ = are there; asamshayam = no doubt; me = for me.

"Oh, Lakshmana! In obtaining the fruit of good works in the world, righteousness utility and free will are being considered. As a wife who is obedient, who is beloved and who is having good sons, good work yields all these three things."

Sloka 57

yasmiṃstu sarve syurasanniviṣṭā |
dharmo yataḥ syāt tadupakrameta |
dveṣyo bhavatyarthaparo hi loke |
kāmātmatā khalvapi na praśastā || 2-21-57

57. tat = that; yasmin = in which; sarve = all these; asannivishhṭāḥ syuḥ = do not come together; yataḥ by which; dharmaḥ = righteousness; syāt = is created; upakrameta = is to be initiated; loke = in the world; arthaparaḥ = one who is intested in wealth alone; bhavatihi = becomes indeed; devshhyaḥ = fit to be hated; kāmātmatāpi = so also; the ne whose very essence is desire; na prashastā khalu = cannot indeed be considered good.

"Only such an action, which is righteous, is to be initiated, leaving that in which wealth, desire and righteousness do not come together. One who is intested in wealth alone becomes indeed fit to be hated in the world. So also the one whose very essence is desire, cannot indeed be considered good.

Sloka 58

guruśca rājā ca pitā ca vṛddhaḥ |
krodhātpraharṣa dyadi vāpi kāmāt |
yadvyādiśet kāryamavekṣya dharmam |
kastanna kuryādanṛdanṛśaṃsavṛttiḥ || 2-21-58

58. pitā = father; gurshcha = who is venerable; rajācha = a king; vṛiddhaḥ cha = and old aged; vyādishet = commends; yat = for which reason; kāryam = an action; krodhāt = either by anger; prahrshhāt = or by extreme joy; yadivā kamāt api = or by even desire; kaḥ = who; apekshhya = with reference to dharmam = righteousness; anṛishamsa vṛittīḥ = and not choosing cruelty; nakuryāt = will not do?

"When father, who is venerable, a king and old-aged, commands an action either by anger or by extreme joy or by even desire, which right person will not perform it? Only a person who chooses to be cruel will not do it."

Sloka 59

savai na śaknomi pituḥ pratijñā |
mimāmakartum sakalam yathāvat |
sa hyavayostata gururniyoge |
devāśca bhartā sa gatissa dharmaḥ || 2-21-59

59. saḥ = such of myself; na shaknomivgi = cannot; akartum = avoid; pituḥ = father's; imām pratijJṇyām = this propostion; sakalām = in entirety; tāta = oh; father! āvoyaḥ = for both of us; saḥ = he; furuḥ hi = is indeed important person; niyoge = to command; devyāshcha = for mother also; saḥ = he; bhartā = the husband; saḥ = he; gatiḥ = the resource person; dharmaḥ = the law; personified.

"I cannot avoid fulfilling the father's command completely. For both of us, he is an important person indeed to command us. To mother Kausalya also, he is the husband, the resource person and the personified law."

Sloka 60

tasmin punarjīvati dharmarāje |
viśeṣataḥ sve pathi vartamāne |
devī mayā sārthamito.apagacchet |
katham svidanyā vidhaveva nārī || 2-21-60

60. dharmarāje = the righteous king; tasmin = himself; jīvati = alive; visheshhatatḥ = espacially; vartamāne = being; sve pathhi = in his own path; katham svit = how; devī = mother kausalya; avagachhet = can go out; mayā sārdham = with me; itaḥ = from here; anyā nārīva = like other woman; vidhavā = without husband?

"while the righteous king Dasaratha is alive and especially when he is following his own righteous path, how the mother Kausalya can go out with me from here leaving the city like other widowed woman?"

Sloka 61

sā mānumanyasva vanam vrajantam |
kuruṣva naḥ svastyayanāni devi |
yathā samāpte punarāvrajeyam |
yathā hi styena punaryayātiḥ || 2-21-61

61. devī = Oh; queen! anumanyasva = permit; mā = me; vrajantam = going; vanam = to forest; yathā hi = as indeed; yayātiḥ = yayati; punaḥ = returned; satyena = by power of stregth; yathā = so; avrajeyam = I can come; punaḥ = again; samāpte = after completion of excile; kurushhva = perform; svastyayanāni = recitation of ṃantra for well-being.

"Oh, mother the queen! Permit me to go to forest. As Yayati returned to heaven by the power of truth, give me your power of blessings so that I can return here after completion of exile."

Sloka 62

yaśo hyaham kevalarājyakāraṇāt |
na pṛṣṭhataḥ kartumalam mahodayam |
adīrghakāle na tu devi jīvite |
vṛṇe.avarāmadya mahīmadharmataḥ || 2-21-62

62. aham = I; na alam = cannot; vṛishhṭataḥ kartum = keep back; yashaḥ = reputation; mahodayam = and glory ; kevalarājyakāraṇāt = for the sake of kingdom alone; devī = oh; queen! jīvite = In this life; adīrghakāle = which is not long; navṛiṇe = I cannot long for; adya = now; avarām = inferior; mahīm = earth; adharmataḥ = unrighteously.

"I cannot keep back reputation and glory for the sake of kingdom alone. This cannot unrighteously long for this inferior kingdom."

Sloka 63

prasādayan nara vṛṣabhaḥ sa mātaram |
parākramājjigamiṣureva domḍakān |
atha anujam bhRśam anuśāsya darśanam |
cakāra tām hRdi jananīm pradakṣiṇam || 2-21-63

63. saḥ = that Rama; navṛishhabhaḥ = the best of men; jigimishhureva = desired to go; daṇdakān = to Dandaka forest ; parākramāt = with prowess; prasādayan = consoling; mātaram = mother; atha = and afterwards; anushāsya = advised; amjam = brother; bhṛisham = intensely; darshanam = with good thoughts; pradakshhiṇam chakāra = made circumbulatory salutation; tāmjanamīm = to his mother; hṛidi = heartily.

Rama, the best of men, decided to go to Dandaka forest fearlessly with his prowess, consoled his mother, advised his brother by good thoughts and made hearty circumbulatory salutation to his mother.

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ekaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus this is the twenty first chapter in Ayodhya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana the first epic of Inida.
