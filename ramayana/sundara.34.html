Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 34
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hearing the words of the grief-stricken Seetha, Hanuma reveals himself to be a messenger of Rama and repeats the enquiries made by Rama, Lakshmana and Sugreeva. Seetha, however, suspects Hanuma to be Ravana in disguise and chides him when Hanuma approaches nearer to her. Seetha then asks Hanuma to describe the good qualities of Rama. Hanuma on his part discloses to her his actuality as a minister of Sugreeva and tells the important qualities of Rama. He requests her to have faith in his words.


Sloka 1

tasyāḥ tat vacanam śrutvā hanūmān hari yūthapaḥ |
duhkhāt duhkha abhibhūtāyāḥ sāntam uttaram abravīt || 5-34-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; tat = that; vachanam = speech; tasyāḥ = of that ṣeetha; duḥkhāt duḥkhābhibhūtayāḥ = who was overthrown from one grief to another; hanumān = ḥanumān; haripuṇ^gavaḥ = the best of monkeys; abravīt = spoke; sāntvam = in a kindly and affectionate; uttaram = reply.

ḥearing those words of ṣeetha who was overthrown from one grief to another, ḥanuma the best of monkeys gave the following reply in a kindly and affectionate manner.

Sloka 2

aham rāmasya saṃdeśāt devi dūtaḥ tava āgataḥ |
vaidehi kuśalī rāmaḥ tvām ca kauśalam abravīt || 5-34-2

2. devī = O princess; vaidehi = ṣeetha; the daughter of the king of Videha Kingdom!; aham = I; āgataḥ = have come; tava dūtaḥ = as your messenger; rāmasya sandeshāt = on the direction of Rama; kusalī = the virtuous; rāmaḥ = Rama; abravīt = enquired; tvāmcha kushalam = about your welfare too.

"O ṣeetha the daughter of the king of Videha Kingdom! I have come here as your messenger on Rama's directions. The virtuous Rama enquired about your welfare too."

Sloka 3

yo brāhmam astram vedān ca veda vedavidām varaḥ |
sa tvām dāśarathī rāmo devi kauśalam abravīt || 5-34-3

3. devī = O divine lady! saḥ = such; rāmaḥ = Rama; dāsharathiḥ = the son of Dasaratha; yaḥ = who; varaḥ = is excellent; veda vidām = among the knower of Veda; veda = and who knows; brahmam astram = Brahma's missile (a mythical weapon which deals infallible destruction); vedāmshcha = and Vedas the sacred knowledge; abravīt = enquired; tvām = you; kaushalam = about your welfare.

"O Divine Lady! Rama the son of Dasaratha, who is excellent among the knowers of Veda and who knows Brahma's missile (a mythical weapon which deals infallible destruction) and Vedas the sacred knowledge, enquired about your welfare."

Sloka 4

lakṣmaṇaḥ ca mahātejā bhartuḥ te anucaraḥ priyaḥ |
kṛtavān śoka samtaptaḥ śirasā te abhivādanam || 5-34-4

4. lakṣmaṇashcha = also Lakshmana; mahātejāḥ = having a great splendour; priyaḥ = the favourite brother; anucharaḥ = and the follower; te bhartuḥ = of your husband; shoka santaptaḥ = tormented with grief; kṛitavān = performed; te abhivādanam = salutation to you; shirasā = with his head.

"Also the greatly splendourous Lakshmana, the favourite brother and the follower of your husband, himself tormented with grief, performed salutation to you by bowing his head."

Sloka 5

sā tayoḥ kuśalam devī niśamya nara simhayoḥ |
prīti samhṛṣṭa sarva angī hanūmāntam atha abravīt || 5-34-5

5. nishamya = hearing; tayoḥ = that; kushalam = (news of) welfare; narasimhayoḥ = of Rama and Lakshmana the excellent among men; sā devī = that divine lady; ṣeetha; prīti samhṛiṣṭa sarvāṇgī = with all her limbs thrilled with joy; atha = then; abravīt = spoke; hanumantam = to ḥanuma (as follows).

ḥearing that news of welfare of Rama and Lakshmana the excellent of men, the divine lady, with all her limbs thrilled with joy, spoke to Hanuma (as follows):

Sloka 6

kalyāṇī bata gathā iyam laukikī pratibhāti me |
ehi jīvantam ānado naram varṣa śatāt api || 5-34-6

6. ānandaḥ = joy; eti = rushes; jīvantam- to surviving; naram = man; varṣashatādapi = even though (it be) at the end of a hundred years; iyam = this; laukikīr = wordly; gāthā = adage; pratibhāti = appears; kalyāṇi = auspicious; mā = to me.

" 'Joy rushes to surviving man even though (it be) as the end of a hundred years' - this popular adage appears true and and auspicious for me."

Sloka 7

tayoḥ samāgame tasmin prītiḥ utpāditā adbhutā |
paraspareṇa ca ālāpam viśvastau tau pracakratuḥ || 5-34-7

7. tasmin = In that ḥanuma; samāgate = who approached (her); adbhutā = a wonderful; prītiḥ = friendly disposition; utpāditā = was brought forth; tayā = by her; tau = the two of them; vishvastau = with full of confidence; chakratuḥ = made; ālāpamcha = conversation; paraspareṇa = with each other.

ṣeetha showed wonderful friendly disposition towards ḥanuma who approached her. Both of them also conversed with each other in full confidence.

Sloka 8

tasyāḥ tat vacanam śrutvā hanūmān hari yūthapaḥ |
sītāyāḥ śoka dīnāyāḥ samīpam upacakrame || 5-34-8

8. shrutvā = hearing; tat = that; vachanam = reply; tasyāḥ = of that; sītāyāḥ = ṣeetha; shoka dīnayāḥ = who was afflicted with; hanumān = ḥanuma; hari yūthapaḥ = the army-chief of monkeys; upachakrame = endeavoured to draw; samīpam = nearer (to her).

ḥearing that reply of ṣeetha who was afflicted with grief, ḥanuma the army-chief of monkeys endeavoured to draw nearer to her.

Sloka 9

yathā yathā samīpam sa hanūmān upasarpati |
tathā tathā rāvaṇam sā tam sītā pariśankate || 5-34-9

9. yathā yathā = in as much as; saḥ hanumān = that ḥanuma; upasarpati = drew; samīpam = nearer to her; tathā tathā = in so much way; sā sītā = that ṣeetha; parishaṇ^kate = suspected; tam = him; rāvaṇam = as Ravana.

In as much as that ḥanuma drew nearer to her, in the same way, that ṣeetha suspected him as Ravana.

Sloka 10

aho dhig dhig kṛtam idam kathitam hi yat asya me |
rūpa antaram upāgamya sa eva ayam hi rāvaṇaḥ || 5-34-10

10. aho dhik = O shame!; me duṣkṛitam = It was my guilt; kathitam = to tell; asya = him; idam = (all) this; ayam = he; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ hi = is indeed that Ravana; upāgamya = who obtained; rūpāntaram = another guise.

"O shame! It was my guilt to tell him all this to him. ḥe is indeed that Ravana, who obtained another guise."

Sloka 11

tām aśokasya śākhām sā vimuktvā śoka karśitā |
tasyām eva anavadya angī dharaṇyām samupāviśat || 5-34-11

11. sā = that ṣeetha; anavadyāṇ^gī = having faultless limbs; vimuktyā = leaving; tām = that; shākhām = branch; ashokasya = of Ashoka tree; shoka karshitā = and emaciated with grief; samupāvishat = sat down; tasyām dharaṇyāmeva = on that floor itself.

ṭhat ṣeetha, having faultless limbs, leaving that branch of Ashoka tree and as emaciated with grief, sat down on that ground itself.

Sloka 12

hanumānapi duḥkhārtāṃ tāṃ dṛṣṭvā bhayamohitām |
avandata mahābāhuḥ tataḥ tām janaka ātmajām || 5-34-12
sā ca enam bhaya vitrastā bhūyo na eva abhyudaikṣata |

12. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tām = her; duḥkhārtam = afflicted with grief; bhaya mohitam = and deluded with fear; hanumānapi = also ḥanuma; mahābāhuḥ = the grea armed; tataḥ = then; avandata = saluted; tām janakātmajam = that ṣeetha; sā cha = she too; bhaya vitrastā = trembling with fear; nā abhyudaikṣata = did not look towards; enam = him; bhūyaḥ = again.

ṣeeing ṣeetha afflicted with grief and deluded with fear, the great armed ḥanuma also then simply saluted her. She too, trembling with fear, did not look towards him again.

Sloka 13

tam dṛṣṭvā vandamānam tu sītā śaśi nibha ānanā || 5-34-13
abravīt dīrgham ucchvasya vānaram madhura svarā |

13. sītā = ṣeetha; shashinibhānanā = with her countenance resmbling the moon; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tam = him; vandamānam = saluting her; uchchhvasya = sighed; dīrgham = deeply; abravīt = and spoke; vānaram = to ḥanuma; madhura svarā = in a sweet-sounding voice (as follows):

ṣeetha, with her countenance resembling the moon, seeing ḥanuma saluting her, had a deep sigh and spoke to Hanuma in a sweet-sounding voice (as follows)

Sloka 14

māyām praviṣṭo māyāvī yadi tvam rāvaṇaḥ svayam || 5-34-14
utpādayasi me bhūyaḥ samtāpam tan na śobhanam |

14. tvam rāvaṇaḥ yadi = If you are Ravana; praviṣṭaḥ = who made use of; māyām = an illusive guise; svayam = and yourself; māyāvī = a conjurer; utpādayasi = you are causing; bhūyaḥ = a further; samtāpam = agony; me = to me; tat = that; na shobhanam = is not good.

"If you are Ravana, who made use of an illusive guise and yourself a conjurer, you are causing me a further agony. It is no good."

Sloka 15

svam parityajya rūpam yaḥ parivrājaka rūpadhṛt || 5-34-15
jana sthāne mayā dṛṣṭaḥ tvam sa eva asi rāvaṇaḥ |

15. yaḥ rāvaṇaḥ = which Ravana; parityajya = leaving; svam = rūpam = his real form; dṛiṣṭaḥ = was seen; mayā = by me; parivrājakarūpavān = in the guise of a vagrant religious mendicant; janasthāne = in Janasthana; sa eva = that Ravana indeed; tvam asi = you are.

'You are indeed the same Ravana, who, abandoning your real form, appeared to me in the guise of a vagrant religious mendicant in Janasthana."

Sloka 16

upavāsa kṛśām dīnām kāma rūpa niśā cara || 5-34-16
samtāpayasi mām bhūyaḥ samtāpam tan na śobhanam |

16. nishāchara = O demon; kāmarūpa = who can assume any form at will!; tat = It; shobhanam na = is not good; santāpayasi samtāpam = that you are causing any; bhūyaḥ = again; mām = to me; upavāsakṛishām = who is emaciated by fasting; dīnām = and very miserable.

"O demon who can assume any form at your will! It is no good that again you are causing agony to me, emaciated because of fasting and feeling very miserable."

Sloka 17

athavā naitadevaṃ hi yanmayā pariśaṅkitam || 5-34-17
manaso hi mama prītirutpannā tava darśanāt |

17. athavā = otherwise; yat = whatever; parishaṇ^kitam = is suspected; mayā = by me; etat = that; na hi = is not indeed; evam = so; mama manasaḥ = in my mind; prītiḥ = a pleasurable sensation; utpannā hi = occurred indeed; tava darshanāt = by reason of your audience.

"Otherwise, whatever is suspected by me is not indeed true because in my mind, a pleasurable sensation indeed occurred by reason of your audience."

Sloka 18

yadi rāmasya dūtaḥ tvam āgato bhadram astu te || 5-34-18
pṛcchāmi tvām hari śreṣṭha priyā rāma kathā hi me |

18. tvam āgataḥ yadi = if you have come; rāmasya dūtaḥ = as Rama's messenger; astu te = may you be; bhadram = blessed; harishreṣṭha = O; the best of monkeys!; me = to me; rāma kathā = Rama's anecdote; piyā hi = is indeed pleasurable; pṛichchhāmi = (hence) I am asking; tvām = you (to narrate it).

"If you have come as Rama's messenger, may you be blessed! O the best of monkeys! Rama's anecdote is very pleasurable for me. Hence, I am requesting you to narrate it."

Sloka 19

guṇān rāmasya kathaya priyasya mama vānara || 5-34-19
cittam harasi me saumya nadī kūlam yathā rayaḥ |

19. vānara = O monkeys!; kathaya = ṇarrate; guṇān = the qualities; prīyasya rāmasya = of my beloved Rama; saumya = O gentle one!; harasi = you are eroding; me chittam = my mind; rayaḥ yathā = as a rapid course of steram nadī kūlam = (erodes) a river-bank.

"O monkey! ṇarrate the qualities of my beloved Rama. O gentle one! You are eroding my mind as a rapid course of stream erodes a river bank."

Sloka 20

aho svapnasya sukhatā yā aham evam cira āhṛtā || 5-34-20
preṣitam nāma paśyāmi rāghaveṇa vana okasam |

20. yā ham = I; who; chirāhṛitā = was taken away long ago; pashyāmi = is seeing; vanaukasam = a monkey; preṣitam nām = sent; rāghavaṇa = by Rama; evam = in this way; aho = Alas!; sukhatā = how delightful; sapnasya = of this dream!.

"I, who was taken away long ago, is seeing a monkey sent by Rama in this way. Alas! ḥow delightful this dream is!'

Sloka 21

svapne.api yadyaham vīraṃ rāghavam sahalakṣmaṇam || 5-34-21
paśyeyam nāvasīdeyam svapno.api mama matsarī |

21. aham pashyeyam yadi = If I have seen; rāghavam = Rama; vīram = the eminent man; saha lakṣmaṇam = along with Lakshmana; na avasīdeyam = I would not have disheartened; mama = in my case; svapno.api = even the dream; matsarī = is wicked.

"If I have seen the eminent Rama together with Lakshmana, I would not have disheartened. In my case, even my dream is wicked!"

Sloka 22

na aham svapnam imam manye svapne dṛṣṭvā hi vānaram || 5-34-22
na śakyo abhyudayaḥ prāptum prāptaḥ ca abhyudayo mama |

22. aham = I; na manye = do not deem; imam = it; svapnam = as a dream; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; vānaram = a monkey; svapne = in a dream; na shakyaḥ = is not fit; prāptum = to get; abhyudayam = prosperity; abhyudayaḥ = (But) prosperity; prāptashcha = has come; mama = to me.

"I do not deem it as a dream. ṣeeing a monkey in a dream does not bring about prosperity But prosperity has come to me!"

Sloka 23

kim nu syāc citta moho ayam bhavet vāta gatiḥ tu iyam || 5-34-23
unmādajo vikāro vā syāt iyam mṛga tṛṣṇikā |

23. syāt kim nu = how possibly; ayam = is it; chitta mohaḥ = a delusion of my mind? iyan bhavet = is it the impending; vātagatiḥ = course of a flatulence? vikārovā = or change of mental condition; unmādaja = born out of madness?; syātiyam = is it a looming; mṛigatṛiṣṇikā = mirage?

"ḥow possibly is it a delusion of my mind? Is it an impending course of flatulence or changing mental condition of madness? Is it a looming mirage?"

Sloka 24

athavā na ayam unmādo moho api unmāda lakṣmaṇaḥ || 5-34-24
sambudhye ca aham ātmānam imam ca api vana okasam |

24. athavā = Otherwise; ayam = It; na unmādaḥ = is neither a madness; moho.api = nor even a delusion; unmāda lakṣaṇaḥ = which is a symptom of madness; aham = I; sambudhye = am knowing; ātmānam = about myself; imam vanonkasam = and this monkey.

"Otherwise, it is neither madness nor even a delusion, which is a symptom of madness. Because I am knowing about myself as also about this monkey."

Sloka 25

iti evam bahudhā sītā sampradhārya bala abalam || 5-34-25
rakṣasām kāma rūpatvān mene tam rākṣasa adhipam |

25. ityevam = thus; sampradhārya = deliberating upon; balābalam = the strengths and weaknesses; bahudhā = in several ways; sīta = ṣeetha; mane = thought; tam = him; rākṣasādhipam = as Ravana; kāmarūpatvāt = as they can change their forms at will.

ṭhus deliberating upon the strengths and weaknesses in several ways, ṣeetha finally imagined him to be Ravana, as demons can change their forms at will.

Sloka 26

etām buddhim tadā kṛtvā sītā sā tanu madhyamā || 5-34-26
na prativyājahāra atha vānaram janaka ātmajā |

26. tadā = then; sā = that; tanumadhyamā = slender waisted; sītā = ṣeetha; janakātmajā = the daughter of Janaka; etām buddhim kṛitvā = thus having made up her mind; na prativyājahāra = did not reply in return; vānaram = to ḥanuma; atha = thereafter.

ṭhen, that slender waisted ṣeetha, the daughter of Janaka, thus having made up her mind, did not reply in return anything further to Hanuman.

Sloka 27

sītāyāḥ cintitam buddhvā hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ || 5-34-27
śrotra anukūlaiḥ vacanaiḥ tadā tām sampraharṣayat |

27. buddhvā = understanding; chintitam = the contemplation; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; hanumān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; tadā = then; sampraharṣayat = brought about a great joy; tām = in her; vachanaiḥ = with his words; shrotrānukūlaiḥ = favourable to her ears.

Understanding the contemplation of ṣeetha, ḥanuma the son of wind-god then brought about a great joy in her with his words most favourable to her ears (as follows):

Sloka 28

āditya iva tejasvī loka kāntaḥ śaśī yathā || 5-34-28
rājā sarvasya lokasya devo vaiśravaṇo yathā |
vikrameṇa upapannaḥ ca yathā viṣṇuḥ mahāyaśāḥ || 5-34-29

28; 29. tejasvī = (Rama) has a great splendour; āditya iva = like that of the sun; loka kāntaḥ = pleasing to all; shasīyathā = like the moon; rājā = lord; sarvasya = to the entire; lokasya = world; devo vaishravaṇo yathā = like Kubera the lord of wealth; mahāyashāḥ = greatly renowned; viṣṇuḥ yathā = like Vishnu; upapannashcha = and endowed with; vikrameṇa = valour.

"Rama has a great splendour like that of the sun. ḥe is pleasing to all like the moon. As Kubera the Lord of wealth, he is a lord to the entire world. Like Vishnu, he is greatly renowned and endowed with valour."

Sloka 30

satya vādī madhura vāg devo vācaspatiḥ yathā |
rūpavān subhagaḥ śrīmān kandarpa iva mūrtimān || 5-34-30

30. satyavādī = (Rama) is a speaker of truth; devaḥ vāchaspatiryathā = like lord Brihaspati; madhuravāk = he has a sweet voice; rūpavān = a handsome man; subhagaḥ = possessing good fortune; shrīmān = and royal dignity; kandaraḥ iva = ḥe is like ṃanmadha; the Lord of love; mūrtimān = personified himself.

"Rama is a speaker of truth. Like Lord Brihaspati, he has a sweet voice. ḥe is a handsome man, possessing good fortune and a royal dignity. He is a personified ṃanmadha, the lord of love."

Sloka 31

sthāna krodha prahartā ca śreṣṭho loke mahārathaḥ |
bāhuc cāyām avaṣṭabdho yasya loko mahātmanaḥ || 5-34-31

31. sthāna krodhaḥ = (Rama) gets anger at the right time; prahartācha = ḥe strikes a befitting person; shreṣṭhaḥ = he is an excellent; mahārathaḥ = chariot-warrior; yasya = in which; bāhu chchhāyām = shelter of arms; mahātmanaḥ = of the high-souled man; lokaḥ = the world; avasṭabdhaḥ = is resting on.

"Rama gets anger only at right time. ḥe strikes a befitting person at a right time. ḥe is an excellent chariot-warrior. The world is resting under the shelter of arms of this high souled man."

Sloka 32

apakṛśṣya āśrama padān mṛga rūpeṇa rāghavam |
śūnye yena apanītā asi tasya drakṣyasi yat phalam || 5-34-32

32. yena = by which Ravana; mṛigarūpeṇa = by means of an illusory antelope; rāghavam = Rama; apakṛiṣya = was taken far away; apranītā asi = you were stolen; shūnye āshram padāt = from a deserted hermitage; yat = which; phalam = consequence; tasya = to such Ravana; drakṣyasi = you will see (later).

"ṣhowing an illusory antelope, Ravana detracted Rama and took you away from a deserted hermitage. You will see later, the consequence Ravana will get for this act."

Sloka 33

nacirāt rāvaṇam samkhye yo vadhiṣyati vīryavān |
roṣa pramuktaiḥ iṣubhiḥ jvaladbhiḥ iva pāvakaiḥ || 5-34-33
tena aham preṣito dūtaḥ tvat sakāśam iha āgataḥ |
tvat viyogena duhkha ārtaḥ sa tvām kauśalam abravīt || 5-34-34

33; 34. yaḥ vīryavān = which valiant Rama; achirāt = soon; vadhiṣyati = is going to kill; rāvaṇam = Ravana; samkhye = in battle; iṣubhiḥ = with arrows; jvaladbhiḥ = strongly shining; pāvakaiḥ iva = like sparkles; roṣa pramuktaiḥ = released with anger; tena = by the same Rama; preṣitaḥ = I was sent; iha = here; dūtaḥ = as a messenger; āgataḥ = and I came; tvatsakāsham = to your presence; saḥ = that Rama; duḥkhārtaḥ = pained with grief; tvadviyogena = by your separation; abravīt = enquired; tvām kaushalam = about your welfare.

"Which valiant Rama with arrows strongly shining like sparkles released with anger, by the same Rama I was sent here as a messenger and I arrived at your presence. That Rama, agonised by your separation wishes to enquire of your welfare."

Sloka 35

lakṣmaṇaḥ ca mahātejāḥ sumitra ānanda vardhanaḥ |
abhivādya mahābāhuḥ so api kauśalam abravīt || 5-34-35

35. saḥ lakṣmaṇashcha = that Lakshmana too; mahā tejāḥ = having a great splendour; sumitrā nanda vardhanaḥ = who augments the happiness of ṣumitra his mother; mahā bāhuḥ = and having long arms; abhivādya = saluted; tvām = you; abravīt = and enquired; kaushalam = about your welfare.

"ṭhat long-armed Lakshmana too, having a great splendour and who augments the happiness of ṣumitra his mother, saluted you and enquired about your welfare."

Sloka 36

rāmasya ca sakhā devi sugrīvo nāma vānaraḥ |
rājā vānara mukhyānām sa tvām kauśalam abravīt || 5-34-36

36. devī = O Princess! saḥ vānaraḥ = that monkey; sugrīvonāma = named ṣugreeva; sakhā = a companion; rāmasya = of Rama; rājā = and the king; vānara mukhyānām = of the excellent monkeys; abravīt = asked; tvām kaushalam = about your welfare.

"O princess! ṭhat monkey called ṣugreeva, a companion of Rama and the king of the excellent monkeys, asked about your welfare."

Sloka 37

nityam smarati rāmaḥ tvām sasugrīvaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |
diṣṭyā jīvasi vaidehi rākṣasī vaśam āgatā || 5-34-37

37. rāmaḥ = Rama; sa sugrīvaḥ = together with ṣugreeva; sa lakṣmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana; nityam = is always; smarati = recollecting; tvām = you; vaidehi = O ṣeetha!; āgatā = (eventhough) coming into; rākṣasīvasham = the power of female-demons; jīvasi = you are surviving; diṣṭyā = by your good luck.

"Rama, together with ṣugreeva and Lakshmana, is always recollecting you. O ṣeetha! Eventhough coming into the control female-demons, you are somehow surviving by your good luck!"

Sloka 38

nacirāt drakṣyase rāmam lakṣmaṇam ca mahāratham |
madhye vānara koṭīnām sugrīvam ca amita ojasam || 5-34-38

38. na chirāt = within a few days; drakṣyase = you will see; rāmam = Rama; mahābalam = the mighty; lakṣmaṇam = lakshmana; amitaujasam = and the highly splendorous; sugrīvam cha = ṣugreeva; madhye = in the presence of; vānarakoṭīnām = crores of monkeys.

"Within a few days, you will see Rama, the mighty Lakshmana and the highly splendorous ṣugreeva, in the presence of crores of monkeys."

Sloka 39

aham sugrīva sacivo hanūmān nāma vānaraḥ |
praviṣṭo nagarīm lankām langhayitvā mahāudadhim || 5-34-39

39. aham = I; vānaraḥ = am a monkey; sugrīva sachivaḥ = the minister of ṣugreeva; humān nāmaḥ = named ḥanuma; praviṣṭaḥ = I entered; laṇ^kām nagarīm = the City of Lanka; laṇ^ghayitva = by traversing; mahodadhim = the great ocean.

"I am a monkey, the minister of ṣugreeva, called ḥanuma. I entered the city of Lanka, by traversing the great ocean."

Sloka 40

kṛtvā mūrdhni pada nyāsam rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ |
tvām draṣṭum upayāto aham samāśritya parākramam || 5-34-40

40. samāshritya = asserting; parākramam = my strength; kṛitvā padnyāsam = and putting down my feet; mūrdhni = on the head; durātmanaḥ rāvaṇaḥ = of the evil-minded Ravana; aham = I; upayātaḥ = have come; draṣṭum = to see; tvām = you.

"Asserting my strength and putting down my feet on the head of the evil minded Ravana, I have come to see you."

Sloka 41

na aham asmi tathā devi yathā mām avagacchasi |
viśankā tyajyatām eṣā śraddhatsva vadato mama || 5-34-41

41. devi = O princess!; yathā = As; avagachchhasi = you comprehend; mām = about me; aham = I; na asmi = am not; tathā = so; tyajyatām eṣā vishaṇkā = let this suspicion be given up; shraddhatsva = believe; mama = me; vadataḥ = who is saying.

"O Princess! I am not so, as you comprehend me (as Ravana). Let your suspicion be given up. Believe me as I say it."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe catustriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 34th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
