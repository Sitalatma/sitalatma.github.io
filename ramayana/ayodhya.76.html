Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 76
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vasistha advises Bharata to stop lamenting and to perform the last rites of the king. Bharata then places the body of Dasaratha on a couch, taking it out from the vessel where it had been immersed in oil and proceeds with the funeral rites. The appointed priests, attendants, people of the city and the weeping royal women followed the funeral pile of the king to the banks of Sarayu River where the body was cremated. Bharata along with others return to the city after the completion of the cremation-ceremony.

Sloka 1

tam evam śoka samtaptam bharatam kekayī sutam |
uvāca vadatām śreṣṭho vasiṣṭhaḥ śreṣṭha vāg ṛṣiḥ || 2-76-1

1. vasishhṭhaḥ ṛishhiḥ = the sage Vasishta; shreshhṭhaḥ = the excellent; vadatām = among the speakers; shreshhṭhavāk = having eloquence; uvācha = spoke; tam bharatam = to that Bharata; Kaikeyi sutam = the son of Kaikeyi; evam = who was thus; shokatamtaptam = tormented with grief.

The sage Vasishta, the excellent one among the speakers having eloquence, spoke to that Bharata, Kaikeyi's son who was thus tormented with grief.

Sloka 2

alam śokena bhadram te rāja putra mahā yaśaḥ |
prāpta kālam nara pateḥ kuru samyānam uttaram || 2-76-2

2. rājaputra = O; the prince; mahāyashaḥ = the greatly illustrious one! bhadram = blessing; te = to you! ālam = enough; shokena = of your sorrow; kuru = perform; uttamam = in an excellent; samyānam = way; prāptakālam = the last rites; narapateḥ = of the king.

"O, the greatly illustrious prince! ṃy blessing to you. You have lamented long enough. Perform in an excellent way, the last rites of the king."

Sloka 3

vasiṣṭhasya vacaḥ śrutvā bharataḥ dhāraṇām gataḥ |
preta kāryāṇi sarvāṇi kārayām āsa dharmavit || 2-76-3

3. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; vasishhṭhasya = of Vasishta; bharataḥ = Bharata; dharmavit = conversant with his duty; gataḥ = fell; dharaṇīm = prostate; kārayāmāsa = and proceeded; sarvāṇi = with all; pretakāryāṇi = the arrangements for obsequies.

Hearing the words of Vasishta, Bharata conversant with his duty fell prostate and proceeded with all the arrangements for the obsequies.

Sloka 4

uddhṛtam taila samkledāt sa tu bhūmau niveśitam |
āpīta varṇa vadanam prasuptam iva bhūmipam || 2-76-4
samveśya śayane ca agrye nānā ratna pariṣkṛte |
tataḥ daśaratham putraḥ vilalāpa suduhkhitaḥ || 2-76-5

4; 5. uddharitam = raising; bhūmipam = the body of the king; dasharatham = Dasaratha; bhūmou = from the place; taila samrodhāt niveshitam = where it had been immersed in oil; prasuptam iva = seeming as it were asleep; āpīta vadanam = with face in the color of gold; saḥ putraḥ = that son; (Bharata);samveshya = placed it; agrye = in a magnificent; shayane = couch; nānāratnaparishhkṛite = adorned with energy kind of precious stone; tatah = and thereafter; suduḥkhitaḥ = in a great grief; vilalāpa = lamented (as follows):

Raising the body of king Dasaratha, from the vessel where it had been immersed in oil, seeming as it were asleep with face in the color of gold, that son Bharata placed it in a magnificent couch, adorned with every kind of precious stone and in a great grief, lamented.(as follows):

Sloka 6

kim te vyavasitam rājan proṣite mayy anāgate |
vivāsya rāmam dharmajñam lakṣmaṇam ca mahā balam || 2-76-6

6. rājan = O; king! Kim = what; vyavasitam = was intended; te = by you; mayi = while I; proshhite = who was absent from home; anāgate = did not arrive; vivāsya = having sent into exile; dharmajṇ^am = the virtuous; rāmam = Rama; lakshhmaṇamcha = and Lakshmana; mahābalam = possessed of great strength?

"O, king! Having sent into exile the virtuous Rama and Lakshmana who is possessed of great strength, while I was away from home and had not yet returned, what did you intend to do?"

Sloka 7

kva yāsyasi mahā rāja hitvā imam duhkhitam janam |
hīnam puruṣa simhena rāmeṇa akliṣṭa karmaṇā || 2-76-7

7. kva = where; yāsyasi = will you go; hitvā = leaving; imam janam = this person; duḥkhitam = who is distressed; hīnam = and bereft; rāmeṇa = of Rama; aklishhṭa karmaṇā = who is unwearied in action; purushhasimhena = and excellent among men?

"Where will you go, leaving this person (me) who was distressed and bereft of Rama the unwearied one in action and the excellent one among men?"

Sloka 8

yoga kṣemam tu te rājan ko asmin kalpayitā pure |
tvayi prayāte svaḥ tāta rāme ca vanam āśrite || 2-76-8

8. tāta = O; father! Rājan = O; king! Kaḥ = who; kalpayitā = is supposed to see; yogakshhemam = the welfare and security; te = of your; asmin = this; pure = city of Ayodhya; tvayi = while you; prayāte = have gone;svaḥ = to heaven; rāmecha = and Rama; āshrite = taking; refuge; vanam = in a forest?

"O, king! O, father! Who is supposed to see the welfare and security of your city of Ayodhya, while you have departed for heaven and while Rama has gone, taking refuge in a forest?"

Sloka 9

vidhavā pṛthivī rājaṃs tvayā hīnā na rājate |
hīna candrā iva rajanī nagarī pratibhāti mām || 2-76-9

9. rājan = O; king; hīnā = bereft; tvayā = of you; pṛithivī = this earth;; vidhavā = and deprived of its lord; na rājate = does not look charming ;mā = to me; nagarī = this city; pratibhāti = is looking; rajanīva = like a night; hinachandrā = with out a moon.

"O, king! Bereft of you and deprived of its lord, this earth does not look charming. To me, this city is looking like a night without a moon."

Sloka 10

evam vilapamānam tam bharatam dīna mānasam |
abravīd vacanam bhūyo vasiṣṭhaḥ tu mahān ṛṣiḥ || 2-76-10

10. mahāmuniḥ = the great sage; vasishhṭhaḥ = Vasishta; bhūyaḥ = again; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; tam bharatam = to that bharata; dīnamānasam = who was distressed in mind; evam = thus; vilapamānam = lamenting.

The great sage Vasishta again spoke the following words to that Bharata who was distressed in mind and thus lamenting.

Sloka 11

preta kāryāṇi yāni asya kartavyāni viśāmpateḥ |
tāni avyagram mahā bāho kriyatām avicāritam || 2-76-11

11. mahābāho = O; mighty armed! Avyagram = without hesitation; avichāritam = or repining; kriyantām = carry out ; tāmi = those; pretakāryāṇi = funeral rites; asya vishāmpate = to this king; yāni = which; kartavyāni = should be done.

"O, mighty armed Bharata! Without hesitation or repining, carry out the funeral rites of the king that should be done."

Sloka 12

tathā iti bharataḥ vākyam vasiṣṭhasya abhipūjya tat |
ṛtvik purohita ācāryāṃs tvarayām āsa sarvaśaḥ || 2-76-12

12. tatheti = Be it so; bharataḥ = Bharata answered; abhipūjya = and obedient; tat vasishhṭasya = to that Vasishta's; vākyam = command; tvarayāmāsa = he summoned speedily; ṛitvik purohitāchāryān = the appointed priests; knowing the rules of the funeral rites.

"Be it so" answered Bharata and obedient to Vasishta's command, he summoned speedily the appointed priests who were knowing the rules of the funeral rites."

Sloka 13

ye tu agrataḥ nara indrasyāgni agārāt bahiṣ kṛtāḥ |
ṛtvigbhir yājakaiḥ caiva te hriyante yathā vidhi || 2-76-13

13. ye = which; agnayaḥ = fires; narendrasya = of that king; bahishhkrutāḥ = were prepared outside; agnayagārāt = the fire chamber; te = those fires; ahriyanta = were kindled; ṛitvigbhiḥ = by the appointed priests; yājakāshchaiva = and sacrificial attendants; yathāvidhi = in accordance with the rituals.

The fires of that king were prepared outside the chamber and kindled in accordance with the rituals by the priests and the sacrificial attendants.

Sloka 14

śibilāyām atha āropya rājānam gata cetanam |
bāṣpa kaṇṭhā vimanasaḥ tam ūhuḥ paricārakāḥ || 2-76-14

14. atha = thereafter; parichārakāḥ = the attendants; bāshhpakaṇṭhāḥ = with tears in their throats; vimanasaḥ = and dejected in mind; āropya = made to mount; gatachetasam = the dead; rājānam = king; shibikāyām = on a small palanquin; ūhuḥ = and carried; tam = it(away).

The attendants were choked with tears in their throats and disconsolate in their mind, made the dead king to mount on a small palanquin and carried it away.

Sloka 15

hiraṇyam ca suvarṇam ca vāsāṃsi vividhāni ca |
prakirantaḥ janā mārgam nṛpater agrataḥ yayuḥ || 2-76-15

15. janaḥ = the people; yayuḥ = went along; mārgam = the path; prakirantaḥ = scattering; hiraṇyam = silver; suvarṇamcha = gold; vividhāni = and many kinds; vāsāmsicha = of clothes; agrataḥ = in front; nṛipate = of the king.

The people went along the path, scattering silver, gold and various kinds of clothes in front of the king.

Sloka 16

candana aguru niryāsān saralam padmakam tathā |
deva dārūṇi ca āhṛtya citām cakrus tathā apare || 2-76-16
gandhān ucca avacāmḥ ca anyāṃs tatra dattvā atha bhūmipam |
tataḥ samveśayām āsuḥ citā madhye tam ṛtvijaḥ || 2-76-17

16. tathā = in the same manner; apare = some others; āhṛitya = brought; chandanāguru niryāsān = sandalwood; sweet aloes; different fragrant essences; saraLam = with heaps of ṣarala.Padmaka; devadārūṇicha = devadāru wood; anyān = and other; uchchavāchān = many kinds; gandhān = of fragrant substances; gatvā = went; tatra = there; kshhepayanti = and threw them; atha = thereafter; ṛitvijaḥ = the appointed priests; samveshayāmāsuḥ = caused to lie down; bhūmipam = the king; tatra = there; chitāmadhye = in the midst of the funeral pile.

In the same manner, some others brought sandal wood, sweet aloes, different fragrant essences, with leaps of Sarala Padmaka and Devadaru woods and many other kinds of fragrant substances, went there and threw them into the funeral pile. Thereafter the appointed priests caused the king's body to lie down there in the midst of the funeral pyre.

Sloka 18

tathā huta aśanam hutvā jepus tasya tadā ṛtvijaḥ |
jaguḥ ca te yathā śāstram tatra sāmāni sāmagāḥ || 2-76-18

18. tadā = then; tadṛitvijaḥ = those appointed priests; tasya = engaged for the bereft of the king; hutvā = poured oblations; hutāshanam = into the fire; jepuḥ = and recited sacred texts;(relevant to the funeral rites); te sāmagāḥ = those among the priests who could recite the hymns of ṣamaveda; jagushcha = chanted; sāmāni = the hymns of ṣamaveda; yathā shāstram = according to the rules.

Then, those priests engaged for the benefit of the king poured oblations into the fire and recited sacred texts (relevant to the funeral rites). Those among the priests, who could recite the hymns of Samaveda, chanted them according to the rules.

Sloka 19

śibikābhiḥ ca yānaiḥ ca yathā arham tasya yoṣitaḥ |
nagarān niryayus tatra vṛddhaiḥ parivṛtāḥ tadā || 2-76-19

19. tadā = then; tasya = his; yoshhitaḥ = ladies; parivṛitāḥ = encircled; vṛiddaiḥ = by elders; shibikābhishcha = (mounted) palanquins; yānaishcha = or other vehicles; yathārham = as deserving; niryayuḥ = departed; nāgarāt = from the city; tatra = to that place.

Then, his ladies, encircled by elders mounted palanquins or other vehicles, as deserving and departed from the city to that place.

Sloka 20

prasavyam ca api tam cakrur ṛtvijo agni citam nṛpam |
striyaḥ ca śoka samtaptāḥ kausalyā pramukhāḥ tadā || 2-76-20

20. tadā = then; ṛitvijaḥ = the appointed priests; kausalyāpramukhāḥ = Kausalya and other; striyashcha = women; shokasamtaptāḥ = tormented with grief; prasavyamchāpi chakruḥ = circumambulated in anti-clockwise direction; tam nṛipam = that king; agrichitam = who was lying on a funeral pile.

Then, the appointed priests as well as Kausalya and other women who were tormented with grief, circumambulated in anti clockwise direction that king who was lying on a funeral pile.

Sloka 21

krauncīnām iva nārīṇām ninādaḥ tatra śuśruve |
ārtānām karuṇam kāle krośantīnām sahasraśaḥ || 2-76-21

21. tatra kale = at that time; ninādaḥ = the crying sounds; sahasrashaḥ = of thousands; nārīṇām = of women; karuṇam = pitiably; kroshantīnām = weeping; ārtānām = with agony; shushruve = were heard; krouṇ^chīnamiva = like the sounds of female curlew birds.

At that time the crying sounds of thousands of women, pitiably weeping with agony, were heard like the sounds of female curlew birds.

Sloka 22

tataḥ rudantyo vivaśā vilapya ca punaḥ punaḥ |
yānebhyaḥ sarayū tīram avaterur vara anganāḥ || 2-76-22

22. tataḥ = thereafter; rudantyaḥ = the lamenting; varāṇganāḥ = ladies; vivashāḥ = deprived of their grit; vilapya = wept; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; avateruḥ = and descended; yānebhyaḥ = from their vehicles; sarayū tīram = at the bank of ṣarayu River.

Thereafter, the lamenting ladies deprived of their grit wept again and again and descended from their vehicles at the bank of Sarayu River.

Sloka 23

kṛta udakam te bharatena sārdham |
nṛpa anganā mantri purohitāḥ ca |
puram praviśya aśru parīta netrā |
bhūmau daśa aham vyanayanta duhkham || 2-76-23

23. bharatena sārdham = along with Bharata; nṛipāṇganāḥ = the royal women; te = and those; mantri purohitāshcha = ministers and family priests; kṛtvā = offered; udakam = (oblations with) water; pravishya = entered; puram = the city; ashruparītanetrāḥ = with their eyes filled in tears; vyanayanta = and spent; duḥkham = with great difficulty; dashāham = the ten days; bhūmou = sleeping on the floor.

Along with Bharata, the royal women, the ministers and family priests offered their oblations with water and entered the city with their eyes filled in tears and spent ten days with great difficulty by sleeping on bare floor.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ṣaṭsaptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 76th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
