Purport

Lord Kṛṣṇa is also known as avatārī, - อวตารี, - อะวะตารี, which means “one from whom all the incarnations emanate.” In Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา (10.8) Lord Kṛṣṇa says, ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate: - อหํ สรฺวสฺย ปฺรภโว มตฺตห์ สรฺวํ ปฺรวรฺตเต: - อะหัง สัรวัสยะ ประภะโว มัตตะห์ สัรวัง ประวัรตะเต: “I am the source of all spiritual and material worlds. Everything emanates from Me.” Thus Lord Kṛṣṇa is the origin of everyone’s appearance. As far as this material world is concerned, Lord Brahmā, Lord Viṣṇu and Lord Śiva are all emanations from Kṛṣṇa. These three incarnations of Kṛṣṇa are called guṇa-avatāras. - คุณ-อวตารสฺฯ - คุณะ-อะวะตารัสฯ The material world is governed by three material modes of nature, and Lord Viṣṇu, Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva respectively take charge of the modes of goodness, passion and ignorance. Mahārāja Pṛthu is also an incarnation of those qualities of Lord Kṛṣṇa by which one rules over conditioned souls.

In this verse the word adhokṣaja, - อโธกฺษช, - อะโธกษะชะ, meaning “beyond the perception of the material senses,” is very significant.
