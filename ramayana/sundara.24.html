Valmiki Ramayana

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 24
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha cites the examples of Sachi, Arundhati, Rohini et.al., on their devotion towards their husbands. Ogre women threaten to kill Seetha and eat her.


Sloka 1

tataḥ sītāmupāgamya rākṣasyo vikṛtānanāḥ |
paruṣaṃ paruṣā nārya ūcustāṃ vākyamapriyam || 5-24-1

1. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasyaḥ nāryaḥ = ogre women; parushhāḥ = with a cruel nature; vikṛitānanaḥ = with crooked faces; upāgamya = neared; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; ūchuḥ = (and) spoke; apriyam = unpleasant; vākyam = words; parushham = harshly.

ṭhereafter ogre women with a cruel nature, with crooked faces neared that ṣeetha and spoke unpleasant words harshly.

Sloka 2

kiṃ tvamantaḥpure sīte sarvabhūtamanohare |
mahārhaśayanopete na vāsamanumanyase || 5-24-2

2. sīte = O ṣeetha; kim = why; tvam = (are) you; nānuamanyase = not agreeing; vāsam = to stay; antaḥpure = in the gynaeceum; sarvabhūta manohare = pleasing to all beings; mahārhashayanopete = together with the very best beds.

"O ṣeetha! Why are you not agreeing to stay in the gynaeceum pleasing to all beings together with very best beds."

Sloka 3

mānuṣī mānuṣasyaiva bhāryātvaṃ bahumanyase |
pratyāhara mano rāmānna tvaṃ jātu bhaviṣyasi || 5-24-3

3. mānyshhī = you who are a human; bahumanyase = are thinking highly; bhāryātvam = (about) the wifehood; mānushhasyaiva = of a human being; pratyahāra = withdraw; manaḥ = mind; rāmāt = from Rama; tvam = (otherwise) you; na bhavishhyasi = will not exist; jātu = at all;

"You who are a human are thinking highly about the wifehood of a human being. Withdraw your mind from Rama. Otherwise you will not exist at all."

Sloka 4

trailokyavasubhoktāraṃ rāvaṇaṃ rākṣaseśvaram |
bhartāramupasaṃgamya viharasva yathāsukham || 5-24-4

4. viharasva = sport; yathāsukham = according to (your) comfort; saṃgamya = coming together; rākshaseshvaram = (with) the king of ogres; rāvaṇam = Ravana; trailokyavasubhoktāram = enjoying luxuries of the three worlds; bhartāram = (and taking him) as husband.

"ṣport according to your comfort coming together with the king of ogres Ravana enjoying luxuries of the three worlds and taking him as husband.

Sloka 5

mānuṣī mānuṣaṃ taṃ tu rāmamicchasi śobhane |
rājyādbhrāṣṭamasiddhārthaṃ viklabaṃ tvamanindite || 5-24-5

5. shobhane = O beautiful one!; anindite = O irreproachable one!; mānushhī tu = but being a human; tvam = you; iccchhasi = are desiring; tam rāmam = that Rama; mānushham = who is a human; bhrashhṭam = banished; rājyāt = from kingdom; asiddhārtham = unsuccessful; viklabam = (and) gloomy.

"O beautiful one! O irrreproachable one! But being a human you are desiring that Rama who is a human, banished from kingdom, unsuccessful and gloomy."

Sloka 6

rākṣasīnāṃ vacaḥ śrutvā sītā padmanibhekṣaṇā |
netrābhyāmaśrupūrṇābhyāmidaṃ vacanamabravīt || 5-24-6

6. sītā = ṣeetha; padma nibhekshaṇā = with eyes like lotuses; shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = words; rākshasīnām = of ogre women; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; netrābhyām = with eyes; ashrupūrṇābhyām = filled with tears.

ṣeetha with eyes like lotuses hearing the words of ogre women spoke these words with eyes filled with tears.

Sloka 7

yadidam lokavidviṣṭamudāharatha saṃgatāḥ |
naitanmanasi vākyaṃ me kilbiṣaṃ pratibhāti vaḥ || 5-24-7

7. saṃgatā = (all of you) coming together; yat vākyam = whatever words; lokavidviṣṭam = which are hated in the world; udāharatha = you spoke; me = in my matter; etat = this; na pratibhāti = does it not seem; kilbishham = sinful; vaḥ = in your; manasi = mind;

"All of you coming together whatever words which are hated in the world you spoke in my matter, this does it not seem sinful in your mind?"

Sloka 8

na mānuṣī rākṣasasya bhāryā bhavitumarhati |
kāmaṃ khādata māṃ sarvā na kariṣyāmi vo vacaḥ || 5-24-8

8. mānushhī = ḥuman woman; na arhati = is not suited; bhavitum = to become; bhāryā = wife; rākshasasya = of an ogre; sarvāḥ = all of you; kāmam = freely; khādata = eat; mām = me; na karishhyāmi = I will not honour; vaḥ vachanaḥ = your words.

"ḥuman woman is not waited to become wife of an ogre. All of you freely eat me. I will not honour your words."

Sloka 9

dīno vā rājyahīno vā yo me bhartā sa me guruḥ |
taṃ nityamanuraktāsmi yathā sūryaṃ suvarcalā || 5-24-9

9. dīnaḥ vā = (although) wretched; rājya hīnaḥ vā = or without kingdom; yaḥ = whoever; me bhartā = is my husband; saḥ = ḥe (alone); me = is my; guruḥ = master; suvarchalā yathā = like ṣuvarchala; sūryam = with the ṣun God; anuraktā asmi tam = I am fond of ḥim; nityam = always;

"Although wretched or without kingdom, whoever is my husband, ḥe alone is my master. Like ṣuvarchala with the Sun God, I am fond of Him always."

Sloka 13

sītāyā vacanaṃ śrutvā rākṣasyaḥ krodhamūrcitāḥ || 5-24-13
bhartsayanti sma paruṣairvākyai rāvaṇacoditāḥ |

13. rākshasyaḥ = ogre women; rāvaṇa choditāḥ = incited by Ravana; shrutvā = listening; vachanam = to the words; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; krodhamūrchitāḥ = swooned with anger; bhartsayanti = frightened (ḥer); parushhaiḥ vākyaiḥ = with harsh words.

Ogre women incited by Ravana listening to the words of ṣeetha, swooned with anger and frightened Her with harsh words.

Sloka 14

avalīnaḥ sa nirvākyo hanumān śiṃśupādrume || 5-24-14
sītāṃ saṃtarjayantīstā rāksasīraśṛṇot kapiḥ |

14. saḥ hanumān = that ḥanuma; kapiḥ = a monkey; nirvākyaḥ = not talking; avalīnaḥ = (and) hiding; shiṃshupā drume = in the ṣimsupa tree; ashṛiṇot = listened; tāḥ rākshasīḥ = to those ogre women; saṃtarjayantīḥ = frightening; sītām = ṣeetha.

ṭhat monkey ḥanuma not talking and hiding in the ṣimsupa tree listened to those ogre women frightening Seetha.

Sloka 15

tāmabhikramya saṃkruddā vepamānāṃ samantataḥ || 5-24-15
bhṛśaṃ saṃlilihurdīptān pralabman daśanacchadān |

15. abhikramya = nearing; tām = that ṣeetha; vepamānām = shaking (with fear); samantataḥ = in all directions; saṃkṛiddhaḥ = (those ogres) being angry; saṃlilihuḥ = licked; bhṛisham = a lot; dīptān = shining; pralambān dashanachchhadān = hanging lips.

ṇearing that ṣeetha shaking with fear in all directions, those ogres being angry licked a lot shining and hanging lips.

Sloka 16

ūcuśca paramakruddhāḥ pragṛhyāśu paraśvadhān || 5-24-16
neyamarhati bhartāraṃ rāvaṇaṃ rākṣasādhipam |

16. paramkruddhāḥ = being angry a lot; pragṛihya = grasping; āshu = quickly; parashvadhān = axes; ūchushcha = and spoke; iyam = this woman; nārhati = is not suited; rākshasādhipam = (to have) the king of ogres; rāvaṇAm = Ravana; bhartāram = as husband.

Being angry a lot and grasping axes quickly spoke as follows: "ṭhis woman is not suited to have the king of ogres Ravana as husband."

Sloka 17

17.bhatsyamānā = being frightened; rākshasībhiḥ = by ogre women; bhīmābhiḥ = who were horrible; sā varānanā = that ṣeetha with best face; sabāshhpam = with tears in eyes; apasarpantī = moving from there; uppagamat = reached; tām shiṃshupām = that ṣimsupa tree.

Being frightened by ogre women who were horrible that ṣeetha with best face with tears in eyes and moving from there reached that Simsupa tree.

Sloka 18

tatastāṃ śiṃśupāṃ sītā rākṣasībhiḥ samāvṛtā || 5-24-18

ṭhereafter the wide eyed ṣeetha nearing that ṣimsupa tree being surrounded by ogre women was situated there with gloom.

Sloka 19

tāṃ kṛśāṃ dīnavadanāṃ malināmbaradhāriṇīm || 5-24-19

19. tāḥ = those; rākshasyaḥ = ogres; bhartsayāṃchakrire = frightened; samantataḥ = from all directions; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; kṛishām = (who was) emaciated; dīnavadanām = with a pitiful face; malināmbaradhāriṇīm = wearing dirty garments;

ṭhose ogres frightened from all directions that ṣeetha who was emaciated with a pitiful face and wearing dirty garments.

Sloka 20

20. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; vinatā nāma = named Vinata; bhīmadarshnā = with a horrible appearance; kupitākārā = with an angry form; karāLā = crooked; nirṇatodarī = having a prominent belly; tām abravīt = spoke to ḥer.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named Vinata with a horrible appearance with an angry form, crooked, having a prominent belly spoke to Her.

Sloka 21

sarvātrātikṛtaṃ bhadre vyasanāyopakalpate |

21. sīte = O ṣeetha!; snehaḥ = love; bhartuḥ = of husband; nidarshitaḥ = has been shown (by you); etāvat = this much; paryāptam = is enough; bhadre = O auspicious one!; sarvatra = at all times; atikṛitam = doing (something) excessively; upakalpate = serves as; vyasanāya = vice ;

"O ṣeetha! You have showed love of husband. ṭhis much is enough. O auspicious one! At all times doing something excessively serves as vice."

Sloka 22

22. maithili = O ṣeetha! paritushhṭā asmi = I am happy; mānushhaḥ vidhiḥ = human duty; kṛitaḥ = has been done; te = by you; bhadram = (let there be) fortune; te = to you; kuru = do (according to); mama = my; pathyam = wholesome; vachaḥ api = words also;bruvantyāḥ = (which are) being told;

"O ṣeetha! I am happy. ḥuman duty has been done by you. Let there be fortune to you. Do also according to my wholesome words being told."

Sloka 23

"Obtain as husband Ravana who is the lord of al l ogres, bold and handsome, like Indra the lord of Devas, able one and liberal with a pleasing appearance to all."

Sloka 25

"Leaving Rama who is a human and miserable, seek refuge in Ravana. O ṣeetha! With best unguents to the body and decorated with best ornaments, become mistress of all the worlds from today."

Sloka 27

Sloka 30

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

Sloka 35

"O one with intoxicating eyes! You along with king of ogres roam beautiful groves, mountains and nearby forests.

Sloka 37

Sloka 39

Sloka 40

imāṃ hariṇalolākṣīṃ trāsotkampipayodharāṃ |
rāvaṇena hṛtāṃ dṛṣṭvā dauhṛdo me mahānabhūt || 5-24-40

40. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; imām = this woman; hariṇalolākshīm = with eyes like that of a deer; trāsotkampipayodharām = with moving breasts due to fear; hṛitām = abducted; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; abhūt = (there) occurred; me = to me; mahān = a great; dauhṛidaḥ = desire.

"ṣeeing this woman with eyes like that of a deer, with moving breasts due to fear, abducted by Ravana, there occured to me a great desire."

Sloka 41

yakṛtlpīhamathotpīḍaṃ hṛdayaṃ ca sabandhanam |
antrāṇyapi tathā śīrṣaṃ khādeyamiti me matiḥ || 5-24-41

41. me matiḥ = it is my mind ;khadeyam iti = to eat; yakṛit = the liver; plīham = the spleen; utpīDam = flesh above heart; hṛidayaṃ cha = and heart; sabandhanam = together with muscle; antrāṇyapi cha = and entrails; tathā = and; shīrshham = the head.

"It is my mind to eat the liver, the spleen, flesh above heart and heart together with muscle and entrails and the head."

Sloka 42

tatastu praghasā nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt |
kaṇṭhamasyā nṛśaṃsāyāḥ pīḍayāma kimāsyate || 5-24-42

42. rākshasī = ogre woman; praghasā nāma = named Pragasa; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; pIDayāma = squeeze; kaṇṭham = the neck; asyāḥ = of this; nṛishaṃsāyāḥ = cruel woman; kim = why; āsyate = do we delay?

An ogre woman named Pragasa spoke these words : "ṣqueeze the neck of this cruel woman. Why do we delay?"

Sloka 43

nivedyatāṃ tato rājñe mānuṣī sā mṛteti ha |
nātra kaścana saṃdehaḥ khādateti sa vakṣyati || 5-24-43

43. tataḥ = thereafter; nivedyatām = let it be known; rājJṇe = to the king (that) ;sā = that; mānushhī = human woman; mṛiteti = has died; saḥ = he; vakshyati = will say; iti = thus;khādata = eat; na sandehaḥ = there is no doubt; atra = in this matter.

"ṭhereafter let it be known to the king that that human woman has died. ḥe will say thus : "Eat.". There is no doubt in this matter."

Sloka 44

tatastvajāmukhī nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt |
viśasyemāṃ tataḥ sarvāḥ samān kuruta pīlukān || 5-24-44

44. tataḥ = thereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; ajāmukhī nāma = named Ajamukhi; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; sarvāḥ = all of you; vishasya = killing; imām = this woman; tataḥ = thereafter; kuruta = do; samān = equal; pīlukān = pieces.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named Ajamukhi spoke these words : "All of you killing this woman thereafter do equal pieces."

Sloka 45

vibhajāma tataḥ sarvā vivādo me na rocate |
peyamānīyatāṃ kṣipraṃ lehyamuccāvacaṃ bahu || 5-24-45

tataḥ = thereafter; sarvāḥ = all of us; vibhajāma = will divide; vivādaḥ = quarrel; na rochate = is not desirable; me = to me; peyam = liquor; uchchāvacham = many kinds; bahu = a lot of; lehyam = lickables; anīyatām = be brought; kshipram = quickly.

"ṭhereafter all of us will divide. Quarrel is not desirable to me. Liquor, many kinds and a lot of lickables be brought quickly."

Sloka 46

tataḥ śūrpaṇakhā nāma rākṣasī vākyamabravīt |
ajāmukhyā yaduktaṃ hi tadeva mama rocate || 5-24-46

46. tataḥ = thereafter; rākshasī = an ogre woman; shūrpaṇakhā nāma = named ṣurpanakha; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; yat = that; uktām = (which was) spoken; ajāmukhyā = by Ajamukhi; tadeva = that; rochate = is desirable; mama = to me.

ṭhereafter an ogre woman named ṣurpanakha spoke these words: "ṭhat which was spoken by Ajamukhi, is desirable to me."

Sloka 47

surā cānīyatāṃ kṣipraṃ sarvaśokavināśinī |
mānuṣaṃ māṃ samāsvādya nṛtyāmotha nikumbhilām || 5-24-47

47. surā cha = liquor; sarvashokavināshinī = (which is the) destroyer of all sorrow; anīyatām = be brought; kshipram = quickly;āsvādya = eating; mānushham māṃsam = human flesh; atha = afterwards; nṛityāmaḥ = we will dance; nikumbhilām = for (propitiating) ṇikumbhila;

"Liquor which is the destroyer of all sorrow be brought quickly. Eating human flesh afterwards, we will dance for propitiating Nikumbila."

Sloka 48

evaṃ saṃbhartsyamānā sā sītā surasutopamā |
rākṣasībhiḥ sughorābhirdhairyamutsṛjya roditi || 5-24-48

48. evam = thus; saṃbhartsyamānā = being frightened; sughorābhiḥ = by horrible; rākshasībhiḥ = ogres; sā sītā = that ṣeetha; surasutopamā = equalling a daughter of gods; utsṛijya = abandoning; dhairyam = fortitude; rodati = is crying.

ṭhus being frightened by horrible ogres, that ṣeetha equalling a daughter of gods, abandoning fortitude, was crying.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe caturviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 24th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
