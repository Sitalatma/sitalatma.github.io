Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 45
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama requests the citizens to return to Ayodhya and to show respect to Bharata the prince Regent and Dasaratha the king. The citizens try to persuade Rama to return to Ayodhya, but in vain. Rama along with all of them reach the bank of Tamasa.

Sloka 1

anuraktā mahātmānam rāmam satya parakramam |
anujagmuḥ prayāntam tam vana vāsāya mānavāḥ || 2-45-1

1. mānavāḥ = ṭhe humans; anuraktāḥ = who are fond of Rama; anujagnuḥ = accompanied; tam rāmam = that Rama; mahātmānam = the high soled; satya parākramam = and the truly brave; prayāntam = who is going; vanavāsāsya = for dwelling in the forest.

The people devoted to the high- soled Rama of unfailing bravery accompanied him on his way to the forest for exile.

Sloka 2

nivartite api ca balāt suhṛd varge ca rājini |
na eva te samnyavartanta rāmasya anugatā ratham || 2-45-2

2. rājani cha = when the king; balata = by force; nivartite apicha = was turned back; te = those; suhṛidvarge = group of friends; anugatāḥ = who were following; rāmasya = Rama's; ratham = chariot; naiva samsyavartanta = did not return.

When the king was forced (in the interests of his son) to turn back, the group of friends who were following Rama's chariot did not return.

Sloka 3

ayodhyā nilayānām hi puruṣāṇām mahā yaśāḥ |
babhūva guṇa sampannaḥ pūrṇa candraiva priyaḥ || 2-45-3

3. mahāyashāḥ = (Rama) who enjoyed great celebrity; guṇasampannaḥ = and richly endowed with every virtue; pūrṇachandra iva = resembling the full moon; babhūvahi = had indeed become; priyaḥ = the favorite; purushhāṇam = for the people; ayodhyānilayānam = living in Ayodhya .

Rama who enjoyed great celebrity and who was richly endowed with every virtue, resembling the full moon, had indeed become the favorite for the people living in Ayodhya.

Sloka 4

sa yācyamānaḥ kākutsthaḥ svābhiḥ prakṛtibhis tadā |
kurvāṇaḥ pitaram satyam vanam eva anvapadyata || 2-45-4

4. yāchyamānaḥ = Despite the entreaties ; tadā = then; prakṛitibhiḥ = of the people; svabhiḥ = of his own; saḥ = that; kākutstha = Rama( the scion of Kakustha); kurvāṇaḥ = proving; pitaram = his father; satyam = to be true; anvapa dyata = proceeded; vanameva = to the forest only.

Despite the entreaties of the people of his own, Rama(the scion of the Kakutstha), in order to honor his father's vow; proceeded towards the forest.

Sloka 5

avekṣamāṇaḥ sasneham cakṣuṣā prapibann iva |
uvāca rāmaḥ snehena tāḥ prajāḥ svāḥ prajāiva || 2-45-5

5. avekshhamāṇaḥ = fondly gazing on; tāḥ = those; prajāḥ = people; prapibanniva = as though drinking; chakshhushhā = with his eyes; rāmaḥ = Rama; sasneham = lovingly; uvācha = spoke; (as follows) prajā iva = as though children; svāḥ = of his own.

Fondly gazing on the people as though drinking with his eyes, Rama lovingly spoke (as follows) as though they were his own children:

Sloka 6

yā prītir bahumānaḥ ca mayy ayodhyā nivāsinām |
mat priya artham viśeṣeṇa bharate sā niveśyatām || 2-45-6

6. "yā = which; prītiḥ = affection; bahumānashcha = and respect; ayodhya nivāsinām = you the people living in Ayodhya; mayi = (have) in me; sā = that ; niveshyatām = be bestowed; visheshheṇa = in a special measure; bharate = on Bharata; matpriyārtham = for my satisfaction.

"The respect and affection that has been bestowed upon me by you (the residents of Ayodhya) may for my satisfaction be bestowed in a special measure on Bharata.

Sloka 7

sa hi kalyāṇa cāritraḥ kaikeyyā ānanda vardhanaḥ |
kariṣyati yathāvad vaḥ priyāṇi ca hitāni ca || 2-45-7

7. saḥ = Bharata; kaikeyyā nandavardhanaḥ = who enhances the delight of Kaikeyi; kalyāṇacharitraḥ = and who possessed excellent conduct; karishhyati hi = will indeed do; priyāṇi = pleasing things; hitānicha = and beneficial things; vaḥ = to you; yathāvat = properly."

"Bharata who enhances the delight of Kaikeyi and who possesses excellent conduct, will indeed do pleasing and beneficial things to you appropriately."

Sloka 8

jñāna vṛddho vayo bālo mṛdur vīrya guṇa anvitaḥ |
anurūpaḥ sa vo bhartā bhaviṣyati bhaya apahaḥ || 2-45-8

8. saḥ = ṭhat Bharata; ṇ^gyāna vṛiddhaḥ = who is matured in knowledge; vayobālaḥ = and young in age; mṛiduḥ = who is gentle; vīrya guṇānvitaḥ = and endowed with virility and virtue; bhavishhyati = will become; anurūpaḥ = worthy; bhartā = master; bhayāpahaḥ = who can dispel your fears."

"Bharata who is matured in knowledge but young in age, who is gentle but endowed with virility and virtue, will become your worthy master who can dispel your fears"

Sloka 9

sa hi rāja guṇaiḥ yuktaḥ yuva rājaḥ samīkṣitaḥ |
api ca api mayā śiṣṭaiḥ kāryam vo bhartṛ śāsanam || 2-45-9

9. saḥ = ṭhat Bharata; yuktaḥ = who is endowed; rājaguṇaiḥ = with royal virtues; samīkshhitaḥ = is being marked; yuvarājaḥ = as the prince . apicha = And further; bhartṛishāsanam = king's command; kāryam = is to be carried out; mayā = by me; shishhṭaiḥ = and by the rest; vaḥ = of you.

"Bharata who is endowed with royal virtues is being marked as the prince. Further, king's command is to be carried out by me and the rest of you.

Sloka 10

na ca tapyed yathā ca asau vana vāsam gate mayi |
mahā rājaḥ tathā kāryo mama priya cikīrṣayā || 2-45-10

10. mayi = (when) I; gate = have gone; vanavāsam = to the forest for exile; yathā = how; asou = this; mahārajah = monarch; na santapyet = does not suffer agony; tathā = in that manner; kāryaḥ = to be done; priya chikīrshhayā = with an intent to please; mama = me."

"If you desire to please me, see that the king does not suffer agony when I have gone to forest for exile"

Sloka 11

yathā yathā dāśarathir dharmam eva āsthitaḥ abhavat |
tathā tathā prakṛtayo rāmam patim akāmayan || 2-45-11

11. yathā yathā = more and more; dāsharathiḥ = Rama; the son of Dasaratha; abhavat = became; āsthitaḥ = established; dharma eva = in his duty alone; tathā tathā = the more and more; prakṛitayaḥ = the people; akāmayan = wished; rāmam = Rama; patim = (as their) master."

The more Rama the son of Dasaratha, showed himself to be faithful to his duty, the more the people wished to have him as their master "

Sloka 12

bāṣpeṇa pihitam dīnam rāmaḥ saumitriṇā saha |
cakarṣa iva guṇaiḥ baddhvā janam punar iva āsanam || 2-45-12

12. rāmaḥ = Rama; soumitriṇā saha = accompanied by the son of ṣumitra; chakarshha = drew; janam = the people (of the city); pihitam = filled with; bāshhpeṇa = tears; dīnam = piteously; baddhvā iva = as though tied; guṇaiḥ = with cords.

Rama accompanied by son of Sumitra, drew the people of the city, who were weeping piteously, after him as if tied and pulled with cords.

Sloka 13

te dvijāḥ trividham vṛddhā jñānena vayasā ojasā |
vayaḥ prakampa śiraso dūrāt ūcur idam vacaḥ || 2-45-13

13. te dvijāḥ = the brahmanas; vṛiddhāḥ = who were elderly; trividham = in three ways; G^yānena = by knowledge; vayasā = by age; ojasā = and by luster; vayaḥ prakampa shirasaḥ = with their heads shaking with advanced years; ūchuḥ = spoke; idam = these; vachaḥ = words; dūrāt = from distance."

Those brahmanas, who were triply revered for their learning, their age and their spirituality, their heads shaking with advanced years, cried out :

Sloka 14

vahantaḥ javanā rāmam bho bho jātyāḥ turam gamāḥ |
nivartadhvam na gantavyam hitā bhavata bhartari || 2-45-14

14. "bho bho turaṇgamāḥ = Oh ṇoble ṣteeds! jātyāḥ = belonging to a breed; javanāḥ = of speed; vahantaḥ = who are carrying;rāmam = Rama! nivartadhvam = You come off. na gantaryam = Do not proceed. bhavata = Become; hitāḥ = friendly; bhartari = to your master.

"Oh Noble Steeds, of speedy breed! You who are carrying Rama, do come back! Do not proceed! Be friendly to your master (since by taking Rama against our wishes, you will be doing a disservice to him)"

Sloka 15

karṇavanti hi bhūtāni viśeṣaṇa turamgamāḥ |
yūyam tasmānnivartadhvam yācanām prativeditāḥ || 2-45-15

15. bhutānihi = Indeed all beings; karṇavanti = which are endowed with ears; visheshhe ṇa = more so; turaṇ^gamāḥ = horses; prativeditāḥ = stand appraised of; yāchanam = (our) entreaty. tasmāt = ṭherefore; yūyam = you; nivartadhvam = return.

"Indeed all things, which are endowed with ears, more so horses, stand appraised of our entreaty. Therefore, you return."

Sloka 16

dharmataḥ sa viśuddhātmā vīraḥ śubhadṛḍhapratah |
upavāhyaḥ tu vo bhartā na apavāhyaḥ purāt vanam || 2-45-16

16. vīraḥ = ṭhe hero; vishuddhātmā = of the pure soul; dharmataḥ = of virtuous; subha dṛiDha vrataḥ = and auspicious firm resolve; saḥ = that; bhartā = master; upa vāhyaḥ = and not to be conveyed away; purāt = from the city; vanam = to the forest."

"That hero of the pure soul, of virtuous and auspicious firm resolve, that master, deserves to be brought back to the city and not to be taken to the forest."

Sloka 17

evam ārta pralāpāṃs tān vṛddhān pralapataḥ dvijān |
avekṣya sahasā rāmaḥ rathāt avatatāra ha || 2-45-17

17. avekshhya = ṣeeing; tān dvijān = those brahmanas; vṛiddhān = who are aged; pralapataḥ = uttering; evam = those; ārtapralāpan = painful words; rāmaḥ = Rama; sahasā = quickly; avatatāraha = got down; rathāt = from the chariot.

Seeing those aged brahmanas uttering those painful words, Rama quickly got down from the chariot.

Sloka 18

padbhyām eva jagāma atha sasītaḥ saha lakṣmaṇaḥ |
samnikṛṣṭa pada nyāso rāmaḥ vana parāyaṇaḥ || 2-45-18

18. atha = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; sa sītaḥ = with ṣita; sa lakshhmaṇāḥ = and with Lakshmana; sannikṛishhṭa padanyāsaḥ = taking close strides ; jagāma iva = on foot only; vana parāyaṇaḥ = towards the forest.

Rama with Sita and Lakshmana, taking close strides, proceeded on foot towards the forest.

Sloka 19

dvijātīṃs tu padātīṃs tān rāmaḥ cāritra vatsalaḥ |
na śaśāka ghṛṇā cakṣuḥ parimoktum rathena saḥ || 2-45-19

19. saḥ rāmaḥ = ṭhat Rama; chāritra vatsalaḥ = who was affectionate in his disposition; ghṛiṇāchakshhuḥ = and had compassion in his eyes; na shashāka = could not; parimoktum = abandon; tān dvijātīn = those brahmanas; padātīn = walking on foot; rathena = behind the chariot.

That Rama who was affectionate in his disposition and had compassion in his eyes, could not abandon those brahmanas walking on foot, far behind the chariot.

Sloka 20

gaccantam eva tam dṛṣṭvā rāmam sambhrānta mānasāḥ |
ūcuḥ parama samtaptā rāmam vākyam idam dvijāḥ || 2-45-20

20. dṛIshhṭvā = perceiving; tam rāmam = that Rama; gachchhantameva = still going; (towards the forest) ; dvijāḥ = those brahmanas; sambhrāanta chetasaḥ = perplexed in mind; parama samtaptāḥ = and very much distressed; ūchuḥ = spoke; idam vākyam = these words :

Perceiving Rama still going towards the forest, those brahmanas perplexed in mind and greatly distressed, spoke the following words :

Sloka 21

brāhmaṇyam kṛtsnam etat tvām brahmaṇyam anugaccati |
dvija skandha adhirūḍhāḥ tvām agnayo api anuyānti amī || 2-45-21

21. sarvam = the whole of; etat = this; brāhmaṇyam = brahmana community anugachchhati = is following; tvām = you; brahmaṇyam = devoted to the brahmanas. Dvija skandādhirūdhāḥ = Bearing on the shoulders of brahmanas; amī = these; agnayo api = sacred fires also; anuyānti = are following; tvām = you.

"The whole of this brahmana community is following you, devoted (as you are) to the brahmanas. See, they are bearing the sacred fires on their shoulders"

Sloka 22

vājapeya samutthāni catrāṇi etāni paśya naḥ |
pṛṣṭhataḥ anuprayātāni haṃsān iva jala atyaye || 2-45-22

22. pashya = look at; etāni = these; chhatrāṇi = canopies; vājapeya samutthāni = (obtained by us when observing Vajpeya sacrifice); anuprayātāni = accompanying; naḥ = our; pṛishhṭataḥ = backs; meghāniva = like clouds; jalātyaye = at end of the rainy season."

"Look at these canopies (obtained by us while observing Vajpeya sacrifice*) accompanying our backs like clouds at the end of the rainy season"

*-It is laid down in the Vedas that he who performs a Vajpeya sacrifice must be supplied with a white canopy.

Sloka 23

anavāpta ātapatrasya raśmi samtāpitasya te |
ebhiḥ cāyām kariṣyāmaḥ svaiḥ catraiḥ vājapeyikaiḥ || 2-45-23

23. te = to you; anavāptātapatrasya = who have not got a canopy; rashmisamtāpitasya = and are being scorched with the rays (of the sun); karishhyāmaḥ = we shall give; chhayām = shade; chhatraiḥ = by canopies; svaiḥ = of ours."

"With these canopies of ours, we shall give shade to you, who have got no canopy and are being scored with rays (of the sun.)"

Sloka 24

yā hi naḥ satatam buddhir veda mantra anusāriṇī |
tvat kṛte sā kṛtā vatsa vana vāsa anusāriṇī || 2-45-24

24. vatsa = oh; dear child! Yā buddhiḥ = Which intellect; naḥ = of ours; satatam = forever; vedamantrānusāriṇī = engaged in perusing the study of Vedic ṭext; sā = that intellect; kṛitā = has been made; vanavāsānusāriṇī = to follow the course of exile to the forest."

"Oh, Dear child! The intellect of ours, which was forever engaged in perusing the study of Vedic texts has been now made to follow the course of exile to the forest"

Sloka 25

hṛdayeṣv avatiṣṭhante vedā ye naḥ param dhanam |
vatsyanti api gṛheṣv eva dārāḥ cāritra rakṣitāḥ || 2-45-25

25. ye = which; vedāḥ = Vedas; param dharma = are the excellent riches; naḥ = of ours; trishhṭanti = (they are) established; hṛidayeshhvena = in our hearts alone. chāritrarakshhitāḥ = Protected by their character; darāḥ api = our wives too; vatsyanti = remain; gṛiheshhveva = at home.

"We will carry the Vedas in our hearts as our excellent treasure and our wives too will remain at home, protected by their character"

Sloka 26

na punar niścayaḥ kāryaḥ tvad gatau sukṛtā matiḥ |
tvayi dharma vyapekṣe tu kim syāt dharmam avekṣitum || 2-45-26

26. nishchayaḥ = " A decision; punaḥ = again( another one);na kāryaḥ = is not to be done. tvadgatou = in the matter our going along with you (to the forest); matiḥ = determination; sukṛitā- = has been well done. tvayi dharmavyapakshhe = If you do not pay attention to piety; kim = which; sthitamsyāt = will remain; dharma pathe = in the path of virtue?"

"We shall not revoke our decision. Our mind is fully determined to go along with you (to the forest). If you do not pay attention to piety, what being will remain devoted to the path of virtue?"

Sloka 27

yācitaḥ no nivartasva haṃsa śukla śiraḥ ruhaiḥ |
śirobhir nibhṛta ācāra mahī patana pāṃśulaiḥ || 2-45-27

27. "nibhṛitāchāra = oh; prince of resolute conduct! yāchitaḥ = We entreat you; shirobhiḥ = by the heads; naḥ = of ours; hamsa shukla shiroruhaiḥ = having gray hair like white plumes of swans; mahīpatana pānsulaiḥ = that are solid with dust as a result of their falling on the ground(in the cause of our prostration to you). nivartasva = ṭurn back."

" Oh, prince of resolute conduct! We entreat you by our heads having gray hair like the white color of the swans, that are soiled with dust as a result of their falling on the ground (in the course of our prostration to you) (pray) turn back."

Sloka 28

bahūnām vitatā yajñā dvijānām yaiha āgatāḥ |
teṣām samāptir āyattā tava vatsa nivartane || 2-45-28

28. "ye = whoever; āgatāḥ = came; iha = here; (such); bahūnām = many; dvijānām = brahmanas; vitatāḥ = started; ajaṇ^yās = sacrificial rites. vatsa = Oh;dear child! teshhām = their; samāptiḥ = completion; āyattā = depends; tava = on your; nivartane = return."

"Sacrifices have been commenced by many of those Brahmanas that have come here. Their completion, oh dear child, depends on your return"

Sloka 29

bhaktimanti hi bhūtāni jamgama ajamgamāni ca |
yācamāneṣu teṣu tvam bhaktim bhakteṣu darśaya || 2-45-29

29. "rāma = Oh;Rama! Bhūtāni = Beings; jaṇ^gamāja^ṇgamāni = both animate and inanimate; bhaktimantihi = are indeed devoted to you. darshaya = ṣhow; bhaktim = affection; bhakteshhu = to those devotees; yāchamāneshhu = who entreat you to return."

"Beings both animate and inanimate, Oh, Rama, are indeed devoted to you. Show affection to those devotees, who entreat you to return."

Sloka 30

anugamtum aśaktāḥ tvām mūlaiḥ uddhiṛta vegibhiḥ |
unnatā vāyu vegena vikrośanti iva pādapāḥ || 2-45-30

30. "pādapāḥ = trees; uddhitaha veginaḥ = which are raised swiftly; mūlaiḥ:by their roots; ashaktāḥ = being unable; anugantum = to accompany; tvām = you; vikroshantīva = appear to be weeping; unnatāḥ = hump-backed; vāyuvegena = by the force of mind."

"The trees held by their roots, unable to accompany you, seen to be weeping, hump-backed by the force of wind"

Sloka 31

niśceṣṭa āhāra saṃcārā vṛkṣa eka sthāna viṣṭhitāḥ |
pakṣiṇo api prayācante sarva bhūta anukampinam || 2-45-31

31. pakshhiṇo api = birds too; nishcheshhṭāhāra samchārāḥ = which sit motionless and are unable to go out in search of food; vṛikshhaika sthāna vishhṭhitāḥ = which remain fixed at one spot on trees; prayāchante = are supplicating you (to return); sarva bhūtānukampinam = compassionate as you are to all created beings."

"Birds too, which sit motionless and are unable to go out in search of food and which remain fixed at one spot on trees, are supplicating you to return, compassionate as you are to all created beings"

Sloka 32

evam vikrośatām teṣām dvijātīnām nivartane |
dadṛśe tamasā tatra vārayanti iva rāghavam || 2-45-32

32. teshhām dvijātīnām = while those brahmanas; vikroshatām = were crying; evam = thus; nivartane = for the return; (of Rama); tamasā = the river ṭamasa; tatra = there; dadṛishe = appeared; varayantīva = as though preventing; rāghavam = Rama."

While those brahmanas were crying thus with a view to persuading Rama to return, the river Tamasa appeared there, as though retarding the progress of Rama "

Sloka 33

tataḥ sumantro.api rathādvimucya |
śrāntānhayānsamparivartya śrīghrām |
pītodakāṃstoyapariplutāṅgā |
nacārayadvai tamasāvidūre || 2-45-33

33. tataḥ = thereafter; sumantro api = ṣumantra the charioteer also; vimuchya = releasing; shrāntān = the tired; hayān = horses; rathāt = from the chariot; shīghram = quickly;samparivartya = making them roll; pītodakān = having drunk water; toya pariplutāṇ^gān = with their bodies drenched in water; achārayat = allowed them to graze; tamasā vidūre = not very far from the ṭamasa.

Thereafter Sumantra the charioteer too released the horses, tired as they were, from the chariot and quickly making them roll, allowed them to graze not very far from the (bank of) Tamasa once they had drunk water and had their body washed in the river."

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe ekacatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 45th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
