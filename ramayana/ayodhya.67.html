Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 67
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

That night in Ayodhya passed over with agony. At sunrise the Brahmins who enthrone a person as a king, assembled and requested Vasista the family priest of Ikshvakus to select a youth to be crowned as a king of Ayodhya. They enunciated the various evil consequences of allowing the country without a king and leaving it in anarchy.

Sloka 1

ākranditanirānandā sāsrakamṭhajanāvilā |
āyodhyāyāmatitatā sā vyatīyāya śarvarī || 2-67-1

1. sā sarvarī = that night; ayodhyāyām = in Ayodhya; ākrandita nirānandā = which was joyless with a weeping tone; sāsrakaṇṭhajanāvilā = thronged with people with tears in their throats; vyatītāya = passed away; atitāyā = very much dragging.

That night in Ayodhya, which was joyless in a weeping tone and thronged with people with tears in their throats, slowly passed over.

Sloka 2

vyatītāyām tu śarvaryām ādityasya udaye tataḥ |
sametya rāja kartāraḥ sabhām īyur dvijātayaḥ || 2-67-2

2. tataḥ = afterwards; sharvaryām = when the night. Vyatītāyām = was passing away; udaye = and with rising; ādityasa = of the sun; dvijātayaḥ = the brahmanas; rājakartāraḥ = who place a king on the throne; sametya = together; īyuḥ = went; sabhām = to the assembly.

At sunrise, when the night had passed over, the Brahmins who place a king on the throne, together gathered at the assembly.

Sloka 3

mārkaṇḍeyo atha maudgalyo vāmadevaḥ ca kāśyapaḥ |
kātyayano gautamaḥ ca jābāliḥ ca mahā yaśāḥ || 2-67-3
ete dvijāḥ saha amātyaiḥ pṛthag vācam udīrayan |
vasiṣṭham eva abhimukhāḥ śreṣṭhaḥ rāja purohitam || 2-67-4

3;4. mārkaṇdeyaḥ = ṃarkandeya; atha = and; moudgalyaḥ = ṃoudgalya; vāmadevaḥ = Vamadeva; kāsyapaḥ = Kasyapa; kātyāyanaḥ = Katyayana; goutamāshcha = Goutama; mahāyashāḥ = the greatly famous; jābālishcha = Jabali; etc;;these; dvijāḥ = Brahmans; amātyaiḥscha = along with ministers; abhimukhāḥ = turned their face; vashishhṭhameva = towards Vasista; shreshhṭaham = the excellent; rājapurohitam = royal priest; pṛithak = and one by one; udīrayan = spoke; vācham = words:

ṃarkandeya, ṃoudgalya, Vamadeva, Kasyapa,Katyayana, Goutama and the greatly famous Jabali-all these Brahmans together with the ministers turned towards the excellent royal priest Vasishta and one by one spoke as follows:

Sloka 5

atītā śarvarī duhkham yā no varṣa śata upamā |
asmin pancatvam āpanne putra śokena pārthive || 2-67-5

5. asmin = "ṭhis; pārthiva = king; āpanne = having obtained; paṇchatvam = death; putrashokena = due to grief for his sons; yā = which night; varshhashatopamā = which was equal to a hundred years; naḥ = to us; sharvarī = that night; atītā = has passed away; duḥkham = with great difficulty."

"King Dasaratha having obtained death due to grief born of separation from his sons (Rama and Lakshmana), the night which burdened like a hundred years, has passed over with great difficulty."

Sloka 6

svar gataḥ ca mahā rājo rāmaḥ ca araṇyam āśritaḥ |
lakṣmaṇaḥ ca api tejasvī rāmeṇa eva gataḥ saha || 2-67-6

6. mahārājaḥ = the monarch; svargatashcha = has gone to heaven; rāmashcha = Rama; āshritaḥ = took retreat; araṇyam = in a forest. tejasvī = ṭhe brilliant; lakshhmaṇashcha = Lakshmana; gataḥ = went; rāmeṇaiva saha = along with Rama in the same manner."

"The monarch has gone to heaven. Rama took retreat in a forest. The brilliant Lakshmana followed suit and went along with Rama."

Sloka 7

ubhau bharata śatrughnau kkekayeṣu param tapau |
pure rāja gṛhe ramye mātāmaha niveśane || 2-67-7

7. ubhou = both; bharata shatrughnou = Bharata and ṣatrughna; paramtapou = the annihilators of enemies; ramye mātāmaha ni veshane = are encamped in the beautiful house of their maternal uncle; pure = in the city; rājagṛīhe = of Rajagriha; kaikeyeshhu = in the kingdom of Kekaya."

"Both Bharata and Satrughna, the annihilator of enemies, are encamped in the beautiful house of their maternal uncle in the city of Rajagriha in the kingdom of Kekaya."

Sloka 8

ikṣvākūṇām iha adya eva kaścit rājā vidhīyatām |
arājakam hi no rāṣṭram na vināśam avāpnuyāt || 2-67-8

8. kashchit = anyone; ikshhvākūnām = in Ikshvaku dynasty; iha = here; vidhīyatām = should be made; rājā = a king; adyaiva = today itself; naḥ = our; rāshhṭram = nation; arājakam = without a king; samavāpnuyāt = will obtain; hi = indeed; vināsham = a collapse."

"Hence, let anyone here in Ikshvaku dynasty be made a king today itself lest our nation without a king should result in a collapse indeed."

Sloka 9

na arājale jana pade vidyun mālī mahā svanaḥ |
abhivarṣati parjanyo mahīm divyena vāriṇā || 2-67-9

9. parjanyaḥ = clouds; vidyunmālī = with a garland of lightnings; mahāsvanaḥ = and with a great noise; nābhivarshhati = will not be showering; vāriṇā = with water; divyena = from sky; mahīm = on earth; janapade = in a country; arājake = without a king."

"Lightning clouds in the sky with a great noise of thunder, will not yield rain on the land in a country without a king."

Sloka 10

na arājake jana pade bīja muṣṭiḥ prakīryate |
na arākake pituḥ putraḥ bhāryā vā vartate vaśe || 2-67-10

10. janapade = in a country; arājake = without a king; bījamushhṭih = even a fist of seeds; naprakīnyate = are not dispersed; putraḥ = a son; bhāryāvā = or a wife; navartate = does not abide; vashe = in the authority; pituḥ = of a father; arājake = in an anarchy."

"Even a fist of seeds are not disbursed in a ruler- less land. In anarchy, even a son is not amenable to the control of a father nor a wife in the authority of her husband."

Sloka 11

arājake dhanam na asti na asti bhāryā api arājake |
idam atyāhitam ca anyat kutaḥ satyam arājake || 2-67-11

11. arājake = in a rulerless land; nāsti = there is no; dhanam = wealth; arājake = where there is no king; nāsti = there is no; bhāryāpi = (faithful) wife. anyat = Another; atyāhitam = very dangerous thing; idam cha = is there; arājake = In a rulerless land; kutaḥ = how can there be; satyam = truth?"

"In a rulerless land, there is no wealth. Where there is no king, there is no (faithful) wife. Another very dangerous thing is there. In a ruler less territory, how can there be truth? "

Sloka 12

na arājake jana pade kārayanti sabhām narāḥ |
udyānāni ca ramyāṇi hṛṣṭāḥ puṇya gṛhāṇi ca || 2-67-12

12. arājake = In a ruler less; janapade = country; narāḥ = people; nakārayante = do not arrange; sabhām = assembly; hṛishhṭāḥ = with delight; ramyāṇi = beautiful; udyānāni = parks; puṇyagṛihāṇi = or temples."

"In a ruler- less country, people do not cause to make a delightful assembly nor erect beautiful parks or temples."

Sloka 13

na arājake jana pade yajña śīlā dvijātayaḥ |
satrāṇi anvāsate dāntā brāhmaṇāḥ saṃśita vratāḥ || 2-67-13

13. arājake = In a rulerless; janapade = territory; yaṇ^gashīlaḥ = ṭhose who frequently perform sacrificial rites; dāntāḥ = those who are self restraint; brahmaṇāḥ = those who have divine knowledge; samshitavratāḥ = those who firmly adhere to a vow; dvijātayaḥ = and brahmanas; na anvāsate = do not carry out; sattrāṇi = ṣoma sacrifices."

"In a ruler- less territory, those who frequently perform sacrificial rites, those who have self-restraint, those who firmly adhere to a vow as well as Brahmins do not carry out Soma sacrifices."

Sloka 14

na arājake janapade mahāyajñeṣu yajvanaḥ |
brāhmaṇā vasusampannā visṛjantyāptadakṣiṇāḥ || 2-67-14

14. arājake = in a ruler less; janapade = land; vasusampannāḥ = who accomplished wealth; yajvanaḥ = and sacrificers; mahā yaṇ^geshhu = in great sacrificial rites; na visṛijanti = do not bestow; āpta dakshhi ṇāḥ = proper gifts."

"In a rulerless land, Brahmans who obtained wealth and sacrificers in great sacrificial rites do not bestow proper gifts"

Sloka 15

na arājake jana pade prabhūta naṭa nartakāḥ |
utsavāḥ ca samājāḥ ca vardhante rāṣṭra vardhanāḥ || 2-67-15

15. arājake = In a ruler less; janapade = territory; utsavāshcha = festivals; samājāshcha = and gatherings; rāshhṭra vardhanāḥ = exalting the kingdom; prabhūta naṭa nartakāḥ = having actors and dancers; na vardhante = are not intensified."

"In a ruler- less territory, festivals and gatherings exalting the kingdom in which actors, and dancers exhibit their talents are not frequently arranged"

Sloka 16

na arajake jana pade siddha arthā vyavahāriṇaḥ |
kathābhir anurajyante kathā śīlāḥ kathā priyaiḥ || 2-67-16

16. arājake = in a ruler less; janapade = land; vyavahāriṇaḥ = litigants; na = do not; siddhārthāḥ = do not get their disputes settled; kathāshīlāḥ = and those who frequently narrate stories; na anurajyante = do not gratify; kathāpriyaiḥ = story-lovers; kathābhiḥ = with their stories."

"In a ruler less land, litigants do not get their disputes settled and persons who have a knack for narrating stories do not gratify story- lovers with their tales."

Sloka 17

na arājake janapade udyānāni samāgatāḥ |
sāyāhne krīḍitum yānti kumāryo hemabhūṣitāḥ || 2-67-17

17. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = territory; kumāryāḥ = girls; samāgatāḥ = together; hemabhūshhitāḥ = adorned in gold; na yānti = do not go; udyānāni = to parks; krīDitum = to play; sāyāhne = in the evening."

"In a ruler less territory, girls adorned in gold do not go together to parks to play there in the evening."

Sloka 18

na arājake jana pade vāhanaiḥ śīghra gāmibhiḥ |
narā niryānti araṇyāni nārībhiḥ saha kāminaḥ || 2-67-18

18. arājake = in a lord less; janapade = land; kāminaḥ = lustful; narāḥ = men; na niryānti = do not go out; nāribhiḥ = with ladies; araṇyāni = to forests; shīghragāmibhiḥ = in speedily moving. vāhanaiḥ = vehicles."

"In a lord less land, lustful men do not go out to woods with ladies in speedily moving vehicles"

Sloka 19

na arākaje jana pade dhanavantaḥ surakṣitāḥ |
śerate vivṛta dvārāḥ kṛṣi go rakṣa jīvinaḥ || 2-67-19

19. arājake = in a lord less; janapade = land; dhanavantaḥ = living on agriculture and cattle rearing; na sherate = do not sleep; surakshhitāḥ = well protected; vivṛitadvārāḥ = with their doors open."

"In a lord less land, rich people living on agriculture and cattle rearing do not sleep in their abodes well protected, with their doors open"

Sloka 20

na arājake janapade baddaghaṇṭā viṣāṇīnaḥ |
āṭanti rājamārgeṣu kuñjarāḥ ṣaṣṭihāyanāḥ || 2-67-20

20. janapade = in a land; arājake = without an emperor; kuṇjarāḥ = elephants; shhashṭi hāyanāḥ = with sixty years of age; na āṭanti = do not roam; baddhaghaṇṭā vishhāṇinaḥ = with bells tied around their tusks ; rājamārgeshhu = on royal highways."

"In a land without emperor, sixty year old elephants with bells tied around their tusks, do not roam around on royal high ways

Sloka 21

na arājake janapade śarān samtatamasyatām |
śrūyate talanirghoṣa iṣvastrāṇāmupāsane || 2-67-21

21. janapade = in a land; arājake = without a king; upāsane = while practicing; ishhvastrāṇām = the use of bows; talanirghoshhaḥ = the noise of plucking the bow string with the palm; asyatām = of archers who discharge; sharān = arrows; santatam = continuously; na shrūyati = is not heard."

"In a ruler less land, the noise of plucking the bow string with the palm, produced by archers continuously discharging arrows while practicing the use of their bows, is not heard."

Sloka 22

na arājake jana pade vaṇijo dūra gāminaḥ |
gaccanti kṣemam adhvānam bahu puṇya samācitāḥ || 2-67-22

22. arājake = in a ruler less; janapade = land; vaṇijaḥ = traders; dūra gāminaḥ = who go to distant places; bahuvaṇya samāchitā = along with several merchandises; na gachchhanti = can not travel; kshhemam = safely; adhvānam = on the path."

"In a rulerless land, traders who go to distant places, carrying with them several merchandises, cannot travel safely in their voyages."

Sloka 23

na arājake jana pade carati eka caraḥ vaśī |
bhāvayann ātmanā ātmānam yatra sāyam gṛho muniḥ || 2-67-23

23. arājake = in rulerless; janapade = land; muniḥ = a monk; ekacharaḥ = who wanders alone; vashī = who has subdued his senses; bhāvayan = who meditates; ātmānā = himself; ātmānam = on the soul; yatrasāyamgṛihaḥ = and who takes up an abode wherever evening overtakes him; nacharati = cannot roam around."

"In a rulerless land, a monk who wanders alone, who has subdued his senses, who mediates himself on the soul and who takes up an abode wherever evening overtakes him, cannot roam around the city."

Sloka 24

na arājake jana pade yoga kṣemam pravartate |
na ca api arājake senā śatrūn viṣahate yudhi || 2-67-24

24. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = land; yogakshhemam = gain and security; na pravartate = are not established; arājake = In anarchy ;senā = army; na vishhahate = can not conquer; shatrūn = enemies; yudhi = in battle."

"In a rulerless land; gain and security are not duly established. In anarchy, army cannot conquer enemies in battle."

Sloka 25

na arājake janapade hṛṣṭaiḥ paramavājibhiḥ |
narāḥ samyānti sahasā rathaiśca parimaṇḍitāḥ || 2-67-25

25. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = land; narāḥ = people; parimanDitāḥ = beautifully adorned; na samyānti = cannot go; sahasā = fast; paramavājibhiḥ = in excellent horses; rathaishcha = and chariots; hṛishhṭaiḥ = cheerfully."

"In a rulerless land, people beautifully adorned, cannot go fast cheerfully in excellent horses and chariots."

Sloka 26

na arājake janapade narāḥ śāstraviśāradāḥ |
sampadanto.avatiṣṭhante vaneṣūpavaneṣu ca || 2-67-26

26. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = land; narāḥ = persons; shāstra vishāradāḥ = skilled in sacred scripts; na avatishhṭhante = can not sit; vaneshhu = in forests; upavaneshhu = and groves; samvadantaḥ = speaking together."

"In a rulerless land, persons skilled in sacred treatises cannot sit speaking together in forests and groves."

Sloka 27

na arājake janapade mālyamodakadakṣiṇāḥ |
devatābhyarcanārthaya kalpyante niyatairjanaiḥ || 2-67-27

27. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = territory; mālyamodakadakshhiṇāḥ = garlands; sweetmeats and gifts; devatābhyarchanārthāya = for the purpose of worshipping deities; nakalpayante = are not arranged; niyataiḥ = by the appointed; janaiḥ = persons."

"In a rulerless land, floral garlands, sweet-meats and gifts for the sake of offering to the deities, are not arranged by the appointed persons."

Sloka 28

na arājake janapade candanāgururūṣitāḥ |
rājaputrā virājante vasanta iva śākhinaḥ || 2-67-28

28. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = land; rājaputrāḥ = royalprinces; chanda nāguru rūshhitāh = smeared with paste of sandal and aloe wood; na rājante = do not look brilliantly; shakhiraḥ iva = like trees; vasante = in springtime."

"In a rulerless land, royal princes smeared with paste of sandal and aloe-wood do not figure brilliantly like trees in a spring time."

Sloka 29

yathā hi anudakā nadyo yathā vā api atṛṇam vanam |
agopālā yathā gāvaḥ tathā rāṣṭram arājakam || 2-67-29

29. anūdāḥ = waterless; yathā = like; nadyaḥ = the rivers; yathā = like; atṛiṇam = a lawnless; vanam = garden; yathā vā = and like ; agopālāḥ = cowherd less; yāvah = cows; yathātathā = in the same manner; arājakam = is a rulerless; rāshhṭram = country."

"A rulerless country is like the water less rivers, like the lawn less garden and like the cowherd less cows."

Sloka 30

dhvajo rathasya prajñānam dhūmo jñānam vibhāvasoḥ |
teṣām yo no dhvajo rāja sa devatvamito gataḥ || 2-67-30

30. dhvajaḥ = A flag; praG^yānam = is an identity; rathasya = for a chariot; dhūmaḥ = smoke; jJṇānam = is an identity; vibhāvasoḥ = for fire; teshhām = for such; naḥ = of us; yaḥ = who; dhvajaḥ = like a flag; saḥ rājā = that king; gatah = obtained; devatvam = godhead; itaḥ = from here."

"A flag is an identity for a chariot. A smoke is an identity for a fire. King Dasaratha, like a flag, was an identity for us and he has left the world for a heavenly abode."

Sloka 31

na arājake jana pade svakam bhavati kasyacit |
matsyāiva narā nityam bhakṣayanti parasparam || 2-67-31

31. arājake = in a rulerless; janapade = land; na bhavati = there is no; svakam = one's own property; kasyachit = for any one; narāḥ = persons ; nityam = always; bhakshhyanti = eat away; parasparam = each other; matsyāh iva = like eating fishes .

"In a rulerless land, there is no one's own property for any one. Like fishes, persons always eat away each other."

Sloka 32

yehi sambhinna maryādā nāstikāḥ cinna saṃśayāḥ |
te api bhāvāya kalpante rāja daṇḍa nipīḍitāḥ || 2-67-32

32. yehi = which; nāstikāḥ = atheists; sambhinnamaryādāh = who were separated from the right path; te api = they also; rājadaṇDitapīDitāḥ = have been tormented; with punishment by the king; chhinna samshayaḥ = and now their fear being dispelled; kalpante = are able to excersise; bhāvāya = their temperament."

"The atheists, who were separated from their right path and who had been tormented; with punishment earlier by the king, are now without fear and are able to exercise their old temperament (due to anarchy)"

Sloka 33

yathā dṛṣṭiḥ śarīrasya nityamevapravartate |
tathā narendro rāṣṭrasya prabhavaḥ satyadharmayoḥ || 2-67-33

33. yathā = how; dṛIshhṭiḥ = the eye-sight; (is helpful); nityameva = regularly; pravartate = for the proper setting in motion; sharīrasya = of the body; tathā = in the same manner; narendraḥ = a king; prabhavaḥ = is the cause of existence; satya dharmayoḥ = for truth and righteousness; rāshhṭrasya = of the country."

"As the eye sight is helpful regularly for a proper setting -in-motion of the body, so also a king is the cause of existence for truth and righteousness of a country."

Sloka 34

rājā satyam ca dharmaśca rājā kulavatām kulam |
rājā mātā pitā caiva rājā hitakaro nṛṇām || 2-67-34

34. rājā = the king; satyamcha = is truthfulness; dharmashcha = and righteousness; kulavatām = belonging to a noble family; kulam = in a race; rājā = the king; māta = is a mother; pitāchaiva = and even a father; raja = the king; hitakaraḥ = is a benefactor; nṛiṇām = to people."

"The king is truthfulness and righteousness personified in him. In a race, he belongs to a noble family. The king is a mother and a father. The king is a benefactor to people."

Sloka 35

yamo vaiśravaṇaḥ śakro varuṇaśca mahābalaḥ |
viśeṣyante narendreṇa vṛttena mahātā tataḥ || 2-67-35

35. tataḥ = hence; (the god of death); Yama = (the god of riches);shakraḥ = Indra(the ruler of celestials); varuṇashcha = Varuna(the ruler of waters); mahābalaḥ = of great strength; viseshhyante = are out stretched; narendreṇa = by the king; mahatā = of the high; vṛittena = conduct."

"Hence, Yama (the god of Death); Kubera (the god of riches), Indra (the ruler of celestials) and the very mighty Varuna (the Lord of Waters) are outstretched by the a high and eminent conduct."

Sloka 36

aho tamaiva idam syān na prajñāyeta kiṃcana |
rājā cen na bhaven loke vibhajan sādhv asādhunī || 2-67-36

36. "aho = Alas! rājāna bhavedyati = If a king is not there; loke = in the world; vibhajan = discriminating; sādhvasādhunī = between good and evil; idam = this world; syāt = will be; tamaḥ = dark; iva = as it were; na kimchana = ṇothing; na prajāyata = will be clearly perceived."

"Alas! If a king is not there in the world, with his power of discrimination between good and evil, this world will be dark and nothing will be clearly perceived."

Sloka 37

jīvati api mahā rāje tava eva vacanam vayam |
na atikramāmahe sarve velām prāpya iva sāgaraḥ || 2-67-37

37. mahārājye jīvatyapi = even when the emperor was alive; vayam = we; sarve = all of us; nātikramāmahe = did not transgress;; tava = your; vachanameva = words; prāpyeva = as with obtaining; velām = the coast; sāgaraḥ = by an ocean."

"Even when the emperor was alive, we all of us did not transgress your words, only any more than an ocean transgresses its coast."

Sloka 38

sa naḥ samīkṣya dvija varya vṛttam |
nṛpam vinā rājyam araṇya bhūtam |
kumāram ikṣvāku sutam vadānyam |
tvam eva rājānam iha abhiṣincaya || 2-67-38

38. dvijararya = O; the excellent Brahmana! ṣaḥ = such of you; samikshhya = after reviewing; vṛIttam = the circumstance; rājyam = the kingdom; nṛipam vinā = without a king; araṇyabhūtam = becoming a jungle; tvamena = you yourself; abhishhiṇcha = anoint; kumāram = a youth; ikshhvākusutam = born in Ikshvaku dynasty; tathānyam = or any one else; iha = here; rājānam = as king."

"O, the excellent Brahmana! After renewing the circumstances here and seeing that the kingdom is becoming a jungle without a king, you yourself anoint a youth born in Ikshvaku race or anyone here as a king."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe saptaṣaṣṭitamḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 67th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
