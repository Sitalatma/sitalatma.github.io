chapter [Sarga] 3

Introduction

In this chapter Hanuma faces Lanka, the city of Lanka herself in the form of a demoness.


Sloka 1

sa lamba śikhare lambe lamba toyada samnibhe |
sattvam āsthāya medhāvī hanumān māruta ātmajaḥ || 5-3-1
niśi lankām mahā sattvaḥ viveśa kapi kunjaraḥ |
ramya kānana toya āḍhyām purīm rāvaṇa pālitām || 5-3-2

1;2. saḥ = ṭhat; hanumān = ḥanuma; medhāvī = the intellectual; mārutātmajaḥ = son of God of Wind; mahāsattvaḥ = one with great might; kapikunjaraḥ = best among monkeys; lambe = on the mountain called Lamba; lamba shikhare = with high peaks; lambatoyada sannibhe = equalling a high cloud; āsthāya = gathered; sattvam = courage; vivesha = and entered; nishi = at night; laṇ^kām purīm = the city of Lanka; rāvaṇa pālitām = ruled by Ravana; ramya kānana toyāDhyām = and filled with beautiful forests and (places of) water.

ṭhat ḥanuma, best among monkeys, the intellectual son of Vayu, one with great might, stood on the moutain called Lamba with its high peaks equalling a high cloud, gathered courage and entered at night the city of Lanka ruled by Ravana and filled with beautiful forests and places of water such as lakes.

Sloka 3

śārada ambu dhara prakhyaiḥ bhavanaiḥ upaśobhitām |
sāgara upama nirghoṣām sāgara anila sevitām || 5-3-3
supuṣṭa bala samguptām yathaiva viṭapāvatīm |
cāru toraṇa niryūhām pāṇḍura dvāra toraṇām|| 5-3-4

3;4. upashobhitām = (city of Lanka was) shone; bhavanaiḥ = by buildings; shāradāmbhudhara prakhyaiḥ = equalling autumnal clouds; sāgaropama nirghoshhām = with a sound resembling that of an ocean; sāgara anila sevitām = served by ocean breeze; supushhṭa bala saṃpushhṭām = filled by mighty armies; yathaiva = like; viṭapāvatīm = Vitapavati; chharu toraṇa niryūhām = with elephants standing at archways; pāṇDuradvāra toraṇām = with white gates and archways.

ṭhe city of Lanka was shone by buildings equalling autumnal clouds. With a sound resembling that of an ocean, it was served by ocean breeze. It was filled with well fed and mighty armies like Vitapati, the capital of Kubera. It had elephants positioned at archways and had white gates and archways.

He became happy and leapt up the wall.

Sloka 13

ṣeeing that city of Ravana, which was best among cities, a wealthy city, a beautiful and auspicious city, that powerful Hanuma thought thus.

Sloka 14

"It is not possible for anyone else to oppose this city, by strength alone, which is being guarded by armies of Ravana with raised weapons."

Sloka 15

sā tu tena prahāreṇa vihvalāṅgī nīśācarī || 5-3-41
papāta sahasā bhūmau vikṛtānanadarśanā |

sā nishācharī tu = that demon; papāta = fell; bhūmau = on land; sahasā = immediately;tena prahāreṇa = by that hit; vihvlāṇ^gī = with a distressed body; vikṛitānana darshanā = and became with a horrific face in appearance.

ṭhat demon fell on land immediately by that hit with a distressed body and appeared with a horrific face.

Sloka 42

tatastu hanumān prājñastām dṛṣṭvā vinipātitām || 5-3-42
kṛpām cakāra tejasvī manyamānaḥ striyam tu tām |

tataḥ = thereafter; praJṇaḥ = the intellectual; tesjasvī = the glorious one; hanumān = ḥanuma; dṛishhṭvā = saw; tām = her; vinipātitām = fallen down; manya māānaḥ = thinking; tām = her; striyam = to be woman; chakāra = made; kṛipām = kindness.

ṭhereafter the intellectual and glorious ḥanuma saw her fallen down and thinking her to be woman, showed kindness.

Sloka 43

tato vai bhṛśasamvignā laṅkā gadgadākṣaram || 5-3-43
uvācāgarvitam vākyam hanūmantam plavaṅgamam |

tataḥ = thereafter; sā lan^kā = that Lanka; bhṛisha saṃvignā = being very gloomy; uvācha = spoke; gadgadāksharam = with faltering tone; vākyam = these words; agarvitam = without being proud; hanūmantam = to ḥanuma; plavaṇ^gam = the Vanara.

ṭhereafter that Lanka being very gloomy spoke with a falterting tone without pride these words to Hanuma the Vanara.

Sloka 44

prasīda sumahābāho trāyasva harisattama || 5-3-44
samaye saumya tiṣṭhanti sttvavanto mahābalāḥ |

su mahābāho = O long armed one!; hari sattama = O best among Vanaras!; prasīda = get propitiated; trāyasva = protect me; saumya = O gentle one!; mahābalāḥ = those with great strength; sattvavantaḥ = with courage; tishhṭhanti = stand; samaye = on tradition.

"O long armed one! O best among Vanaras! Get propitiated and protect me. O gentle one! ṭhose with great strength and courage always stand firm on tradition."

Sloka 45

ahaṃ tu nagarī laṅkā svayameva plavaṅgama || 5-3-45
nirjitāham tvayā vīra vikrameṇa mahābala |

plavangama = O Vanara! aham tu = I am; lankānagaryeva = city of Lanka itself; svayameva = myself; mahā bala = O strong one! vīa = O courageous one! aham = I; nirjitā = have been defeated; tvayā vikrameṇa = by your strength.

"O Vanara! I am myself the city of Lanka. O strong one! O courageous one! I am defeated by your strength."

Sloka 46

idam tu tathyam śṛṇu vai bruvantya me harīśvara || 5-3-46
svayambhuvā purā dattam varadānam yathā mama |

harrishvarā = O lord of Vanaras! purā = in earlier times; yathā = how; varadānam = a gift of boon; dattam = had been given; mama = to me; svayambhuvā = by Brahma; shṛiṇu vai = hear; me = by me; bruvanyāḥ = being spoken; idam tathyam tu = this truth.

"O lord of Vanaras! In earlier times I had been given a boon by Lord Brahma. ḥear from me about this matter."

Sloka 47

yadā tvām vānaraḥ kaścidvikramādvaśamānayet || 5-3-47
tadā tvayā hi vijñeyam rakṣasām bhayāmāgatam |

yadā = whenever; kashchit vānaraḥ = one Vanara; vikramāt = by his prowess; tvām vasham ānayet = conquers you; tadā = then; bhayam = fear; āgatam = will come; rakshasām = for Rakshasas; tvayā vigneyam = let it be known by you.

"Let this be known by you - whenever a Vanara conquers you with his might, then a fear will come for Rakshasas."

Sloka 48

sa hi m samayaḥ saumya prāpto.aya tava drśanāt || 5-3-48
svayambhūvihitaḥ satyo na tasyāsti vyatikramaḥ |

saumya = O gentle one! tava darshanāt = with your apparance; adya = now; saḥ samayaḥ prāptaḥ = that time has arrived; me = for me; svayambhūvihitaḥ = the ordinance of Brahma; satyaḥ = is true; nāsti = there is no; vyatikramaḥ = change; tasya = to that.

"O gentle one! With your appearance now, that time has come for me. Ordinance of Brahma is the truth and is immutable."

Sloka 49

sītānimittaṃm rājñastu rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 5-3-49
vidhatsva sarvakāryāṇi yāni yāniha vāñcasi |

vināshaḥ = destruction; samupāgataḥ = has come; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; durrātmanaḥ = the evil minded; rāJṇAḥ = king; sarveshhām rakshasām cha = and to all Rakshasas; sītā nimittam = due to ṣeetha.

"ṭhe destruction has come for Ravana the evil minded king and to all Rakshasas due to ṣeetha."

Sloka 50

tatpraviśya hariśreṣṭham purīm rāvaṇapālitām || 5-3-50
vidhatsva sarvakāryāṇ yāni yānīha vāñcasi |

hari shreshhṭha = O best among Vanaras! tat = for that reason; pravishya = enter; purīm = the city; rāvaṇa pālitām = ruled by Ravana; i vidhtva = do; yāni yāni = whatever; vānchasi = you desire; sarva kāryāṇi = all those tasks; iha = here.

"O best aming Vanaras! For that reason, enter the city of Lanka ruled by Ravana and do here whatever tasks you want to."

Sloka 51

praviśya śāpopahatām harīśvara |
śubhām purīm rākṣasarājapālitām |
yadṛcchayā tvam janakātmajām satīm |
vimārga sarvatra gato yathāsukham || 5-3-51

harīshvarā = O lord of Vanaras! pravishya = enter; purīm = the city; yadṛichhayā = freely; shāpopahatām = stuck by curse; rākshasa rāja pālitām = ruled by the king of Rakshasas; shubhām = auspicious one; tvam = you; sarvatra gataḥ = being gone everywhere; vimārgasva = search; satīm janakātmajām = the chaste wife ṣeetha; yathā sukham = according to comfort.

"O lord of Vanaras! Enter the auspicious city of Lanka stuck by the curse, ruled by the king of Rakshasas, roam everywhere and search for the chaste wife Seetha according to your comfort."

- - -

|| iti śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakānṇḍe tṛtayaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes the third chapter of Sundara Kanda in Valmiki Ramayana, the first poem.
