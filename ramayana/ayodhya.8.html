Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 8
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

ṃanthara with her wicked thoughts tries to convince Kaikeyi of the possible misfortune that could occur to Kaikeyi and her family if Sri Rama becomes the King. Although Kaikeyi's initial reaction is to praise Rama's qualities; her mind is slowly but surely poisoned by ṃanathara's sinful words.

Sloka 1

mantharā tvabhyasūyaināmutsṛjyābharaṇaṃ ca tat|
uvācedaṃ tato vākyaṃ kopaduḥkhasamanvitā || 2-8-1

1. mantharātu = but ṃanthara; abhyasūya = became indignant; enām = with her; utsṛijya = cast down; tat = that; ābharanam = ornament; tataḥ = afterwards; kopaduḥkhasamanvitā = with anger and pain; uvācha = spoke; idam = these words.

ṃanthara, becoming indignant with Kaikeyi, threw down the ornament given to her and with anger and distress spoke the following words:

Sloka 2

harṣaṃ kimidamasthāne kṛtavatyasi bāliśe |
śokasāgaramadhyasthamātmānaṃ nāvabudhyase || 2-8-2

2. bālishe = O; foolish one! nāvabudhyase = not knowing; ātmānam = about you; shokasāgara madhyastam = who are in the midst of an ocean of trouble; kim idam = what is it; kṛitavatī asi = you are doing; harshham = (in making) delight; asthāne = of an ill suited matter.

"O, foolish one! Not knowing that you are in the midst of an ocean of trouble, what is it that you are so delighted about, in a matter to be grieved?"

Sloka 3

manasā prahasāmi tvāṃ devi duḥkhārdhitā satī |
yacchocitavye hṛṣṭāsi prāpyedaṃ vyasanaṃ mahat || 2-8-3

3. devi = O; queen! duḥ.khārditāsatī = stricken with grief; prahasāmi = I laugh; manasā = mentally; tvām = at you; yat = for; hrishhṭā asi = you are rejoicing; prāpya = after getting; idam = this; mahat = great; vyasanam = calamity; shochitavye = which is to be grieved.

"Oh, queen! Though stricken with grief, I mentally laugh at you in that you are rejoicing at the time when a great calamity is befalling you."

Sloka 4

śocāmi durmatitvaṃ te kā hi prājñā praharṣayet |
areḥ sapatnīputrasya vṛddhiṃ mṛtyumivāgatām || 2-8-4

4. shochāmi = I lament over; te = your; durmatitvam = ill-considered mind; kā prāGyā = which intelligent woman; praharshhayet = gets delighted; vṛiddhim = over prosperity; sapatnīputrasya = of stepson; areḥ = who is an enemy; mṛityumiva = (just as rejoicing) over death; āgatām = which has befallen.

"I am lamenting over your foolish mind. Does any intelligent woman feel happy over the prosperity of a stepson who is considered an enemy? Does it not amount to praising a befalling death?"

Sloka 5

bharatādeva rāmasya rājyasādhāraṇādbhayam |
tadvicintya viṣaṇṇāsmi bhaya bhītāddhi jāyate || 2-8-5

5. rāmasya = ṭo Rama; bhayam = (there is) fear; bharatādeva = from Bharata; rājyasādhāraṇāt = who has equal right to kingdom; vichintya = In thinking over; tat = that (matter); vishhaṇṇa asmi = I am sorrowful; bhayam = Fear; jāyate hi = begets from; bhītāt = those who fear (us).

"Rama has a fear about Bharata because Bharata has equal rights over the kingdom. In thinking about this matter, I am getting anguished. Do we not get disasters from those who are afraid of us?"

Sloka 6

lakṣmaṇo hi maheṣvāso rāmaṃ sarvātmanā gataḥ |
śatrughnaścāpi bharataṃ kākutthsaṃ lakṣmaṇo yathā || 2-8-6

6. lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; maheshhvāsaḥ = having mighty bow; gataḥ = joined; rāmam = Rama; sarvātmanā = with all his heart; shatrughnaḥ cha api = ṣatrughna also; bharatam = (is faithful) to Bharata; kākutsam yathā = as to Rama; lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana.

"Lakshmana, wielding a great bow, joined Rama with all his heart. Shatrughna is as faithful to Bharata as Lakshmana to Rama".

Sloka 7

pratyāsannakrameṇāpi bharatastaiva bhāmini |
rājyakramo viprakṛṣṭastayostāvatkanīyasoḥ || 2-8-7

7. bhāmini = Kaikeyi!; pratyāsanna krameṇāpi = In line with proximity of birth; rājyakramaḥ = the lineage of kingdom; bharatasyaiva = is to Bharata only; tayoḥ tāvat = to the extent of other two(Lakshmana and ṣatrughna); kanīyasoḥ = who are younger; viprakṛishhṭaḥ = it is far away.

"Oh Kaikeyi! In line with even the proximity of birth; the claim to the throne of Bharata alone can be pressed, that of Lakshmana and Satrughna who are younger is out of question."

Sloka 8

viduṣaḥ kṣatracāritre prājñasya prāptakāriṇaḥ |
bhayātpravepe rāmasya cintayantī tavātmajam || 2-8-8

8. vidushhaḥ = (Rama is a )learned man; prāGyasya = efficient; kshhātrachāritre = in politics; prāpta kāriṇaḥ = he who acts appropriately and in time; bhayāt = fearing (such Rama); pravepe = I am shaken; chintayantī = to be thinking; ātmajam = of your son.

"Rama is a learned man and a political statesman. His actions are timely and appropriate. When thinking of your son's calamity to be resulted from Rama, I get shaken with fear".

Sloka 9

subhagā khalu kausalyā yasyāḥ putro.abhiṣekṣyate |
yauvarājyena mahatā śvaḥ puṣyeṇa dvijottamaiḥ || 2-8-9

9. kausalyā = Kausalya; subhagākhalu = is having good fortune; yasyāḥ = that Kausalya's; putraḥ = son; svaḥ = tomorrow; pushyeṇa = on the day of Pushyami star; abhishhekshhyate = is going to be anointed; mahatā yauvarājyena = for great princely kingdom; dvijottamai = by the best of Brahmans.

"Kausalya is very fortunate. Brahmans are going to anoint her son for the great princely kingdom tomorrow on the day of Pushyami star".

Sloka 10

prāptāṃ sumahatīṃ prītiṃ pratītāṃ tāṃ hatadviṣam |
upasthāsyasi kausalyāṃ dāsīvattvaṃ kṛtāñjaliḥ || 2-8-10

10. kṛitānjaliḥ = with folded arms; dāsīvat = as a maid servant; tvam = you; upasthāsyasi = will serve; tām kausalyām = that Kausalya; prāptām = who gets; sumahatīm = very great; prītim = happiness; pratītām = famous; hatadvishham = annihilator of enemies.

"With folded arms, as a maid-servant, you have to serve that Kausalya who having reached great prosperity, in the height of joy, will dispose of her adversaries (in the person of Bharata and yourself)".

Sloka 11

evam cettvaṃ sahāsmābhistasyāḥ preṣya bhaviṣyasi |
putraśca tava rāmasya preṣyabhāvaṃ gamiṣyati || 2-8-11

11. evam = thus; tvam bhavishhyasi yadi = if you become; preshhyā = servant maid; tasyāḥ = to her; asmābhiḥ saha = along with us; tava = your; putrashcha = son also; gamishhyati = will get; preshhyabhāvam = servitude; rāmasya = of Rama.

"Thus, if you become Kausalya's servant-maid along with us, your son Bharata will be Rama's attendant."

Sloka 12

hṛṣṭāḥ khalu bhaviṣyanti rāmasya paramāḥ striyaḥ |
aprahṛṣṭā bhaviṣyanti snuṣāste bharatakṣaye || 2-8-12

12. rāmasya = Rama's; paramāstriyaḥ = great wives; bhavantikhalu = will become; hṛishṭāḥ = delighted; te = your; snushhāḥ = daughters-in-law; bhavishhyanti = will become; aprahṛisṭāḥ = unhappy; bharata kshhaye = of Bharata's weak position.

"Rama's wives will get delighted. Your daughters-in-law will be unhappy because of Bharata's waning position."

Comment: The words 'Rama's wives' here do not indicate that Rama had multiple wives. ṃanathara refers to a possible future where Rama being a King would marry other women. It was a norm then for a king to have more than one wife.

Sloka 13

tāṃ dṛṣṭvā paramaprītāṃ bruvantīṃ mantharāṃ tataḥ |
rāmasyaiva guṇān devī kaikeyi praśaśaṃsa ha || 2-8-13

13. tataḥ = afterwards; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tām = that; mantharāam = manthara; bruvantīm = thus talking; param = much; aprītām = distasteful word; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; prashashamsaḥ = praised; rāmasya guṇena = Rama's virtues.

Hearing ṃanthara speak thus in many distasteful words, Kaikeyi began to extol Rama's virtues, saying: -

Sloka 14

dharmajño gurubhirdāntaḥ kṛtajña satyavākcuci |
rāmo rājñḥ suto jyeṣṭho yauvarājyamato.arhati || 2-8-14

14. rāmaḥ = Rama; dharmaGyaḥ = knows righteousness; dāntaḥ = trained by; gurubhiḥ = elders; kṛitajJṇyaḥ = one who has gratitude; satyavāk = speaker of truth; suchiḥ = clean in conduct; jyeshhṭaḥ = elder; sutaḥ = son; rājJṇaḥ = of king Dasaratha; ataḥ = hence; arhati = eligible; youvarājyam = for princely kingdom.

"Rama knows all righteousness. Elders trained him. He has a proper gratitude. He speaks truth. He has a clean conduct. He is the eldest son of king Dasaratha and hence eligible for the kingdom."

Sloka 15

bhrātṛūnbhṛtyāṃśca dīrghāyuḥ pitṛvatpālayiṣyati |
saṃtapyase kathaṃ kubje śrutvā rāmābhiṣecanam || 2-8-15

15. dīrghāyuḥ = the long living Rama; pālayishhyati = will protect; bhrātroom = brothers; bhṛityāmshcha = and servants; pitṛivat = like father; kubje = O; the hunch-backed one! katham = how; santapyase = are you pained; shrutvā = after hearing; rāmābhishhechanam = Rama's anointment.

"The long living Rama will protect his brothers and servants like a father. O, the hunch backed one! Why are you so pained on hearing about Rama's coronation?"

Sloka 16

bharataścāpi rāmasya dhruvaṃ varṣaśatātparam |
pitṛpaitāmahaṃ rājyamavāptā puruṣarṣabhaḥ || 2-8-16

16. param = after; varshhashatāt = one hundred years; purushharshhabhaḥ = the best among men; bharatashchāpi = Bharata also; avāptā = will get; rāmasya = Rama's; rājyam = kingdom; pitṛipaitāmaham = which belongs to (his) father and forefathers.

"After Rama's rule for one hundred years, Bharata the best among men will certainly replace Rama on his father's throne, which is that of his ancestors."

Sloka 17

sā tvamabhyudaye prāpte vartamāne ca manthare |
bhaviṣyati ca kalyāṇe kimarthaṃ paritapyase || 2-8-17

17. manthare = O; ṃanthara! prāpte = (When we) got; abhyudaye = an occasion for rejoicing; vartamānecha = as at present ; kalyāṇe = and when a festive occasion; bhavishhyati = is to come off in future (too in the form of Bharata's installation; no matter even if it comes after a hundred years); kimartham = why; sā tvam = that you; paritapyase = feel agonized like this as though burning (with jealousy)?

"Oh, ṃanthara! When we got an occasion for rejoicing as at present and when a festive occasion is to come off in future (too in the form of Bharata's installation, no matter even if it comes after a hundred years), why do you feel agonized like this as though burning (with jealousy)?"

Sloka 18

yathā ne bharato mānyastathā bhūyo.api rāghāvaḥ |
kausalyāto.ariktaṃ ca so hi śuśrūṣate hi mām || 2-8-18

18. me = to me; bharataḥ = Bharata; yathā = how; mānyaḥ = fit to be loved; tathā = like that; rāghavaḥ = Rama also; bhūyaḥ api = and even more; saḥ = he; anushushrūshhate hi = doing service; atiriktam = more; mām = to me; kausalyātaḥ = than Kausalya.

"For me, Rama is as lovable as Bharata and even more. Is he not doing more service to me than to Kausalya?"

Sloka 19

rājyaṃ yadi hi rāmasya bharatasyāpi tattadā |
manyate hi yathātmānaṃ tathā bhrātṛūṃśca rāghavaḥ || 2-8-19

19. rājyam = kingdom; yadi rāmasya = if it is of Rama; tadā = then; tat = that; bharatasyāpi hi = is of Bharata also; rāghavaḥ = Rama; manyate = thinks about; bhrātṛīṃshcha = his brothers; yathā tathā = on par with; ātmānam = his own self.

"If Rama has kingdom then Bharata has it as well. Rama esteems his brothers just as his own self."

Sloka 20

kaikeyīvacanaṃ śrutvā mantharā bhṛśaduḥkhitā |
dīrghamuṣṇaṃ niḥśvasya kaikeyīmidamabravīt || 2-8-20

20. mantharā = ṃanthara; shrutvā = after hearing; kaikeyī vachanam = Kaikeyi's words; bhrisha duḥ.khitā = felt very sad; niḥshvasya = and sighing; dīrgham = long; ushhṇam cha = and hot; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; Kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi.

ṃanthara, after hearing Kaikeyi's words, felt very sad and after a long and hot sigh, spoke to her the following words:

Sloka 21

anarthadarśinī maurkhyānnātmānamavabudhyase |
śokavyasanavistīrṇe majjantī duḥkhasāgare || 2-8-21

21. majjantī = (You are getting) immersed; duḥkhasāgare = in an ocean of sorrow; shoka vyasana vistīrṇe = filled with misery and danger; mourkhyāt = Due to stupidity; anarthadarshinī = (you are) not seeing the truth; na avabudhyase = not knowing; ātmānam = yourself.

"On one side, you are getting immersed in an ocean of sorrow filled with misery and danger. But due to stupidity, you are not able to perceive the truth and you fail to assess your real position."

Sloka 22

bhavitā rāghavo rājā rāghavasyānu yaḥ sutaḥ |
rājavaṃśāttu kaikeyi bharataḥ parihāsyate || 2-8-22

22. kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi! rāghavaḥ = Rama; bhavitā = will become; rājā = king; rāghavasya anu = after Rama; yaḥ = whoever; sutaḥ = the son is (he becomes the king);bharataḥ tu = but Bharata; parihāsyate = will be removed; rājavaṃshāt = from the royal clan.

"O,Kaikeyi! If Rama becomes the king, his son will become the king after him in succession. Thus, Bharata's name itself will be removed from the royal clan."

Sloka 23

na hi rājñaḥ sutāḥ sarve rājye tiṣṭhanti bhāmini |
sthāpyamāneṣu sarveṣu sumahānanayo bhavet || 2-8-23

23. bhāmini = O; Kaikeyi! sarve = all; sutāḥ = sons; rājJṇyaḥ = of a king; natishhṭantiḥ = will not be installed; rājye = for kingdom; sarveshhu sthāpyamāneshhu = If all are installed; bhavet = there will be; sumahān = a great; anayaḥ = lawlessness.

"O, Kaikeyi! All the sons of a king will not be crowned for a kingdom. If all are installed, there will be a great lawlessness."

Sloka 24

tasmājjyeṣṭhe hi kaikeyi rājyatantrāṇi pārthivāḥ |
sthāpayantyanavadyāṅgi guṇavatsvatareṣvapi || 2-8-24

24. kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi! anavadyāṇ^gi = who has deformless body-parts; tasmāt = that is why; pārthivāḥ = kings; sthāpayanti = install; jyeshhṭe = eldest son; itareshhvapi = eventhough others; guṇavatsu = may be full of virtues; rājyatantrāṇi = to kingly affairs.

"Therefore, kings, O, Kaikeyi of faultless limbs, install their eldest son, eventhough others may be full of virtues, as their successor to the throne."

Sloka 25

asāvatyantanirbhagna stavaputro bhaviṣyati |
anāthavatsukhebhyaśca rājavaṃśācca vatsale || 2-8-25

25. vatsale = O; affectionate one! tava = your; asou = this; putraḥ = son; bhavishhayati = will become; atyanta nirbhagnaḥ = a complete loser; sukhebhyaḥ cha = from comforts; rājavamshāchcha = and even from royal clan; anāthavat = like an orphan.

"Oh, Kaikeyi the affectionate one! Your son will be completely distant from comforts and even from the royal clan; like an orphan."

Sloka 26

sāhaṃ tvadarthe saṃprāptā tvaṃ tu māṃ nāvabudhyase |
sapatnivṛddau yā me tvaṃ pradeyaṃ dātumiccisi || 2-8-26

26. sā aham = ṭhat I; samprāpta = came; tvadarthe = for your sake; tvam tu = but you; nāvabudhyase = are not understanding; mām = me; yā = that; tvam = you; ichchhasi = wish; dātum = to give; me = me; pradeyam = a present; sapatni vriddhou = when there is prosperity for step-wife.

"I came here in your interests. But you have not understood me. You think it fit to give me a present when your step-wife is getting prospered."

Sloka 27

dhruvaṃ tu bharataṃ rāmaḥ prāpya rājyamakaṇṭakam |
deśāntaraṃ vāsayitā lokāntaramathāpi va || 2-8-27

27. rāmaḥ = Rama; prāpya = after getting; rājyam = kingdom; akaṇṭakam = without hindrance; nayitā = will send; bharatam = Bharata; deshāntaram vāsayitā vā = either to some other country; athāpivā = or otherwise; lokāntaram = to some other world; dhruvam = this is certain.

"Rama, ascending the throne without hindrance, will either send away Bharata to some other country or have him put to death. This is certain."

Sloka 28

bāla eva hi mātulyaṃ bharato nāyitastvayā |
sannikarṣācca sauhārdaṃ jāyate sthāvareṣvapi || 2-8-28

28. bharataḥ = Bharata; nāyitaḥ hi = was sent; bālaḥ eva = in his childhood itself; mātulyam = to his maternal uncle's house; tvayā = by you; sannikarshhāt = by being nearer; souhārdam = friedship; jāyate = is created; sthāvareshhvapi = even on inanimate things.

"You sent Bharata to his maternal uncle's house even in his childhood. By being nearer, love is born even on inanimate objects. By sending Bharata to a distant place, you made Dasaratha to have no affection towards him."

Sloka 29

bharatasyānuvaśagaḥ śatrughno.api samaṃ gataḥ |
lakṣmaṇo hi yathā rāmaṃ tathāsau bharataṃ gataḥ || 2-8-29

29. shatrughno.api = ṣhatrughna also; anuvashagaḥ = was drawn; bharatsya = towards Bharata; gataḥ = and went; samam = along with him; yathā = how; lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; gataḥ = joined with; rāmam = Rama; tathā = like that; asou = this (ṣhatrughna); bharatam = with Bharata.

"Shatrughna was drawn towards Bharata and went along with him. Just as Lakshmana joined Rama, Shatrughna joined Bharata."

Comment: Sage Valmiki might be implying here that if Shatrughna were to be present then at Ayodhya, he would have tried for the prosperity of Bharata.

Sloka 30

śrūyate hi drumaḥ kaściccettavyo vanajīvibhiḥ |
sannikarṣādiṣīkābhirmo citaḥ paramādbhayāt || 2-8-30

30. shrūyate hi = it is heard that; kashchit = certain; drumaḥ = tree; chhettavyaḥ = which is to be cut; vanajīvibhiḥ = by people living in forest; mochitaḥ = was relieved; paramāt bhayāt = of (this) great danger; ishhīkābhiḥ sannikarshhāt = by proximity to Ishhīka grass.

"We hear that a tree marked down for felling by foresters, when covered by thorny Ishhīka grass, is saved from this great danger of cutting. So also, Dasaratha might have supported Bharata if he was staying near to him in Ayodhya."

Sloka 31

goptā hi rāmaṃ saumitrirlakṣmaṇaṃ cāpi rāghavaḥ |
aśvinoriva saubhrātraṃ tayorlokeṣu viśrutam || 2-8-31

31. soumitriḥ = Lakshmana; goptāhi = will protect; rāmam = Rama; rāghavaḥ = Rama; lakshmaṇaṃ chāpi = (will protect) Lakshmana also; tayoḥ = their; soubhrātram eva = brotherly love; vishrutam = is famous; lokeshhu = in the world; asvinoḥ = as that of Aswinis.

"Lakshmana protects Rama. Rama protects Lakshmana. Their brotherly love is as famous as that of Aswini celestials."

Sloka 32

tasmānna lakṣmaṇe rāmaḥ pāpaṃ kiñcitkariṣyati |
rāmastu bharate pāpaṃ kuryāditi na saṃśayaḥ || 2-8-32

32. tasmāt = ḥence; rāmaḥ = Rama; nakarishyati = will not do; kiJṇchit = any little; pāpam = sin; lakshmaṇe = to Lakshmana; bharate tu = but to Bharata; rāmaḥ = Rama; kuryāt = will do; pāpam = a sinful act; iti = in this; nasamshayaḥ = there is no doubt.

"Hence, Rama will not do a sinful act of killing Lakshmana. However there is no doubt that he will do so in the case of Bharata."

Sloka 33

tasmādrājagṛhādeva vanaṃ gacchatu te sutaḥ |
etaddhi rocate mahyaṃ bhṛśṃ cāpi hitaṃ tava || 2-8-33

33. tasmāt = ḥence; etat = this; rochate hi = is wished; mahyam = by me; te sutaḥ = (that) your son; gachhatu = goes; vanam = to forest; rājagṛihādeva = from royal palace (of his uncle) itself; bhṛisham = (ṭhis is) very much; hitam = beneficial; tavāpicha = to you also.

"Hence, I feel that it is better for your son to go to forest directly from his uncle's house. This is good for you too."

Sloka 34

evaṃ te jñātipakṣasya śreyaścaiva bhaviṣyati |
yadi cedbharato dharmātpitryaṃ rājyamavāpsyati || 2-8-34

34. bharataḥ avāpsyati yadi = If Bharata gets; rājyam = kingdom; pitryam = of his father; dharmāt = as per law; bhavishhyati = it will become; shreyaḥ = beneficial; te = to you; jṇyātipakshhasya chaiva = and to your side of relatives; evam chet = if it happens thus.

"If Bharata gets his father's kingdom as per law, it will be beneficial to you and your side of relatives."

Sloka 35

sa te sukhocito bālo rāmasya sahajo ripuḥ |
samṛddhārthasya naṣṭārtho jīviṣyati kathaṃ vaśe || 2-8-35

35. te = your; saḥ = that; bālaḥ = child; sukhochitaḥ = who is habituated to comforts; rāmasya sahajaḥ ripuḥ = is Rama's natural enemy ; katham = how; nasṭārthaḥ = Bharatha; who failed to achieve purpose; jīvishhyati = will live; vashe = under control; samṛiddhārthasya = of Rama whose flourishing object has been realized?

"Your youthful son, habituated to comforts, is a natural enemy to Rama. How can Bharata who failed to achieve his purpose live under control of Rama; whose flourishing object has been realised?"

Sloka 36

abhidrutamivāraṇye siṃhena gajayūthapam |
pracchādyamānaṃ rāmeṇa bharataṃ trātumarhasi || 2-8-36

36. trātum arhasi = you ought to protect; bharatam = Bharata; prachchhādyamānam = who is being wrapped up; rāmeṇa = by Rama; gajayūdhapam iva = as a leading elephant; abhidrutam = being chased; siṃhena = by a lion; araṇye = in forest.

"Rama is chasing and bringing down Bharata as a lion chases an elephant-king in forest. You ought to protect Bharata."

Sloka 37

darpānnirākṛtā pūrvaṃ tvayā saubhāgyavattayā |
rāmamātā sapatnī te kathaṃ vairaṃ na śātayet || 2-8-37

37. pūrvam = previously; nirākṛitā = being disrepected; darpāt = with arrogance; tvayā = by you; soubhāgyavathayā = as you were fortunate; katham = how; rāmamātā = Rama's mother (Kausalya); te = your; sapatnī = rival wife; na shātayet = will not revenge; vairam = for that animosity?

"Previously, you treated Kausalya with disrespect due to arrogance that you were fortunate. Will not such Kausalya, your rival wife, revenge for that animosity?"

Sloka 38

yadā hi rāmaḥ pṛthivīmavāpsyati |
prabhūtaratnākaraśailapattanām |
tadā gamiṣyasyaśubhaṃ parābhavaṃ |
sahaiva dīnā bharatena bhāmini || 2-8-38

38. bhāmini = O; Kaikeyi! yadā = when; rāmaḥ = Rama; avāpsyati = gets; pṛithivīm = this earth; prabhūta ratnākara shaila pāttanām = holding great ocean; mountains and towns; tadā = then; bharatena sahaiva = along with Bharata; gamishhyasi = you will get; ashubham = bad; dīnā = pitiable; parābhavam = ignominy.

"O, Kaikeyi! The day Rama becomes lord of this earth with its great oceans, mountains and towns; that day you and your Bharata will get a bad and pitiable position of ignominy."

Sloka 39

yadā hi rāmaḥ pṛthivīmavāpsyati |
dhruvaṃ praṇaṣṭo bharato bhaviṣyati |
ato hi saṃcintaya rājyamātmaje |
parsya caivādya vivāsakāraṇam || 2-8-39

39. yadāhi = when; rāmaḥ = Rama; avāpsyasi = gets; pr^ithivīm = the earth; bharataḥ = Bharata; bhavishhyati = will become; dhruvam = certainly; praṇashhṭaḥ = ruined; ataḥ = hence; saṃchintaya = think of; rājyam = kingdom; ātmaje = to your son; vivāsakāraṇam = and causing exile; parasya = to your enemy; Rama.

"When Rama gets power of the kingdom Bharata will certainly get ruined. Hence, think of a solution to get your son Bharata the kingdom and to send Rama, your enemy, to exile."

- - -

|| ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyakāṇḍe aṣṭhamaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes 8th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
