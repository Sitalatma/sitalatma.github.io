Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 13
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Hanuma starts to search for Seetha overcoming the depression. He begins to go towards Ashoka garden to continue his search.


Sloka 1

vimānāt tu susamkramya prākāram hari yūthapaḥ |
hanūmān vegavān āsīd yathā vidyud ghana antare || 5-13-1

1. hanuman = ḥanuma; hariyūdhapaḥ = the leader of vanaras; susamkramya = crossed; vimānāt = from pushpaka; prākāram = toward the fort wall; āsīt = and became; vegavān = with speed; vidhyut yathā = like a lighting; ghanantare = in the middle of cloud.

ḥanuma the leader of Vanaras crossed from pushpaka toward the fort wall and became with speed like lighting in the middle of a cloud.

Sloka 2

samparikramya hanumān rāvaṇasya niveśanān |
adṛṣṭvā jānakīm sītām abravīd vacanam kapiḥ || 5-13-2

2. kapiḥ hanuman = the simian ḥanuma; samparikramya = went far; rāvaṇasya niveshanāt = from the house of Ravana; adṛishhṭvā = not seeing; sītām = ṣeetha; jānakīm = the daughter of king Janaka; abravīt = said; vachanam = (these)words.

ṭhe simian ḥanuma went far from the house of Ravana and not seeing ṣeetha, the daughter of King Janaka, said these words:

Sloka 3

bhūyiṣṭham loḍitā lankā rāmasya caratā priyam |
na hi paśyāmi vaidehīm sītām sarva anga śobhanām || 5-13-3

3. laṇ^kā = the city of Lanka; bhūyishhṭham lolitā = had been mostly explored; charatā = (by me)attempting; rāmasya priyam = the pleasure of ṣri Rama; na hi pashyāmi = but I am unable to see; sītām = ṣeetha; sarvāṇ^ga shobhanām = beautiful in all limbs; vaidehīm = and the daughter of Videha;

"ṭhe city of Lanka has been mostly explored by me attempting for the pleasure of Rama. But I am unable to see Seetha, beautiful in all limbs, and the daughter of Videha."

Sloka 4

palvalāni taṭākāni sarāṃsi saritaḥ tathā |
nadyo anūpavana antāḥ ca durgāḥ ca dharaṇī dharāḥ || 5-13-4
loḍitā vasudhā sarvā na ca paśyāmi jānakīm |

4. sarvāḥ vasudā = all earth; palvālāni = (consisting of) pools; taṭākāni = ponds; sarāmsi = lakes; tathā = and; saritaḥ = streams; nadyaḥ = rivers; anupavanāntashcha = and forest areas; filled with water; dharaṇīdharāḥ = mountains; durgāḥ = difficult of access; Lolitā = have been explored; na tu pashyāmi = but I did not see; jānakīm = ṣeetha.

"All earth consisting of pools, ponds, lakes and streams, rivers and forest areas filled with water, mountains difficult of access have been explored but I did not see Seetha."

Sloka 5

iha sampātinā sītā rāvaṇasya niveśane || 5-13-5
ākhyātā gṛdhra rājena na ca paśyāmi tām aham |

5. akhyātā = it has been said; gṛidhrarājena sampātinā = by the eagle king ṣampāti; sītā = ṣeetha; iha = (to be) here; rāvaṇasya niveshane = in the house of Ravana; aham = I; na cha pashyāmi = I am unable to see; tām = her.

"It has been said by the eagle King ṣampati that ṣeetha is here in the house of Ravana. I am unable to see her."

Sloka 6

kim nu sītā atha vaidehī maithilī janaka ātmajā || 5-13-6
upatiṣṭheta vivaśā rāvaṇam duṣṭa cāriṇam |

6. atha = otherwise; sītā = ṣeetha; vaidehī = belonging to Videha; maithilī = born in ṃithila; janakātmaja = and daughter of king Janaka; kim nu upatishhṭheta = can she be; vivashā = helplessly; dushhṭachāriṇam rāvaṇam = (together with) the evil minded Ravana.

"Otherwise ṣeetha belonging to Videha born in ṃithila and daughter of king Janaka - Can she be helplessly together with the evil minded Ravana."

Sloka 7

kṣipram utpatato manye sītām ādāya rakṣasaḥ || 5-13-7
bibhyato rāma bāṇānām antarā patitā bhavet |

7. manye = I think; rakshasaḥ = while Ravana; bibhyataḥ = fearing; rāmabāṇānām = Rama's arrows; kshipramutpatataḥ = flew quickly; sītām ādāya = carrying ṣeetha; patitā bhavet = (ṣhe) might have fallen down; antarā = in the middle(of the journey).

"I think while Ravana fearing Rama's arrows flew quickly carrying ṣeetha, she might have fallen down in the middle of the journey."

Sloka 8

athavā hriyamāṇāyāḥ pathi siddha niṣevite || 5-13-8
manye patitam āryāyā hṛdayam prekṣya sāgaram |

8. athavā = or; manye = I think; hṛidayam = the heart; āyrāyāḥ = of the noble ṣeetha; hṛiyamāryāyāḥ = being stolen; patī = in the path; sidhhanishhevite = served by ṣiddhās; patitam = must have sunk; sāgaram prekshya = at the site of the ocean.

"Or I think the heart of the noble ṣeetha being stolen in the path served by ṣiddhas must have sunk at the site of the ocean."

Sloka 9

rāvaṇasya ūru vegena bhujābhyām pīḍitena ca || 5-13-9
tayā manye viśāla akṣyā tyaktam jīvitam āryayā |

9. manye = I think; rāvanasya uruvegeṇa = due to great speed of Ravana; bhujābhyām pīDitenacha = and due to the torture of Ravana's shoulders; jīvitam chaktam = the life has been given up; tayā āryayā = by that noble one; vishālākshyā = with wide eyes.

"I think due to great speed of Ravana and due to the torture of Ravana's shoulders the life has been given up by that noble one with wide eyes."

Sloka 10

upari upari vā nūnam sāgaram kramataḥ tadā || 5-13-10
viveṣṭamānā patitā samudre janaka ātmajā |

10. tadā = then; kramataḥ = while being flown; sāgaram upari upari = upon the surface of the ocean; janakātmaja = ṣeetha; nunam = definitely; patitā = fell down; sāgare = in the ocean; viveshhṭhamāna = while wriggling.

"ṭhen while being flown upon the surface of the ocean, ṣeetha definitely fell down in the ocean while wriggling."

Sloka 11

āho kṣudreṇa ca anena rakṣantī śīlam ātmanaḥ || 5-13-11
abandhur bhakṣitā sītā rāvaṇena tapasvinī |

11. aho = or; tapasvinī sītā bhakishtā = has the austere ṣeetha been eaten; anena rāvaṇena = by this Ravana; kshudreṇa = the evil minded one; atmanaḥ shīlam rakshantī = while she was protecting her chastity; abandhuḥ = without any relatives(near her).

"Or has the austere ṣeetha been eaten by this Ravana, the evil minded one while she was protecting her chastity without any relatives near her."

Sloka 12

athavā rākṣasa indrasya patnībhir asita īkṣaṇā || 5-13-12
aduṣṭā duṣṭa bhāvābhir bhakṣitā sā bhaviṣyati |

12. athavā = otherwise; adushhṭā = not being evil; asitekshaṇā = with black eyes sā = she; bhakshitā bhavishhyati = may have been eaten; rākshasendrasya patnībhi = by the wives of Ravana; dushhṭabhāvābhi = with evil thoughts.

"Otherwise not being evil with black eyes, ṣhe may have been eaten by the wives of Ravana with evil thoughts."

Sloka 13

sampūrṇa candra pratimam padma patra nibha īkṣaṇam || 5-13-13
rāmasya dhyāyatī vaktram pancatvam kṛpaṇā gatā |

13. kṛipaṇā = the pitiable ṣeetha; sampūrṇachandra pratimam = equaling full moon; padmapatra nibhyekshaṇam = with eyes equaling lotus petals; paJṇchantvam gatā = must have obtained death; dhyāyatī = while meditating; rāmasya vaktram = upon Rama's face.

"ṭhe pitiable ṣeetha equaling full moon, with eyes equaling lotus petals must have obtained death while meditating upon Rama's face."

Sloka 14

hā rāma lakṣmaṇa iti eva hā ayodhyeti ca maithilī || 5-13-14
vilapya bahu vaidehī nyasta dehā bhaviṣyati |

14. vaidehī = the daughter of Videha; maithilī = ṣeetha; ha rāmā = O Rama; ha lakshmaṇa = O Lakshmana; ha ayodhye = O Ayodhya; iti = thus; bahuvilapya = weeping greatly; nyasta dehā bhavishhyati = may have given up her body.

"ṭhe daughter of Videha, ṣeetha. �O Rama!' �O Lakshmana!' �O Ayodhya!', thus weeping greatly may have given up her body."

Sloka 15

athavā nihitā manye rāvaṇasya niveśane || 5-13-15
nūnam lālapyate mandam panjarasthā iva śārikā |

15. manye = I think; athavā = otherwise; nihitā = being put; rāvaṇasya niveshane = in the house of Ravana; sītā = ṣeetha; nunam lālpyate = is definitely crying; paJṇjarastā shārikā iva = like a caged myna.

"I think otherwise being put in the house of Ravana ṣeetha is definitely crying like a caged myna."

Sloka 16

janakasya kule jātā rāma patnī sumadhyamā || 5-13-16
katham utpala patra akṣī rāvaṇasya vaśam vrajet |

16. katham = how(can); janakasya sutā = the daughter of King Janaka; ramapatnīm = Rama's wife; sīta = ṣeetha; su madhyamā = with a beautiful waist; utpala patrākshi = with eyes like black lotus petals; vrajet = obtain; rāvanasya vasam = Ravana's capture.

"ḥow can the daughter of King Janaka, Rama's wife ṣeetha with a beautiful waist, with eyes like black lotus petals obtain Ravana's capture."

Sloka 17

vinaṣṭā vā pranaṣṭā vā mṛtā vā janaka ātmajā || 5-13-17
rāmasya priya bhāryasya na nivedayitum kṣamam |

17. na kshamam = it is not fair; nivedayutum = to let know; rāmasya priyabhāryasya = to Rama who has a dear wife; janakātmaja = (if) ṣeetha; vinashhṭā vā = is lost; praṇashhṭavā = or is not to be seen; mṛitāvā = or has died.

"It is not fair to let know Rama who has a dear wife if ṣeetha is lost or is not to be seen or has died."

Sloka 18

nivedyamāne doṣaḥ syād doṣaḥ syād anivedane || 5-13-18
katham nu khalu kartavyam viṣamam pratibhāti me |

18. nivedyamāne = to be let known; syāt doshhaḥ = becomes an error; anivedane = not to let know; syāt = becomes; doshhaḥ = an error; khatham = how; kartavyam nu khalu = to perform the duty; me = to me; pratibhāti = (this) seems; vishhamam = dire (situation).

"ṭo be let known becomes an error; not to let know becomes an error how to perform the duty? ṭo me this seems dire situation."

Sloka 19

asminn evam gate karye prāpta kālam kṣamam ca kim || 5-13-19
bhaved iti matim bhūyo hanumān pravicārayan |

19. asmin kārye = this task; evam gate = while it goes this way; prāptakālam = the time is approaching; kim bhavet = what becomes; kshamam = appropriate; iti = thus; hanuman = ḥanuma; bhūyaḥ pravichārayat = thought again; matam = (this) view point.

"ṭhis task while it goes this way the time is approaching. What becomes appropriate" thus ḥanuma thought again this view point.

Sloka 20

yadi sītām adṛṣṭvā aham vānara indra purīm itaḥ || 5-13-20
gamiṣyāmi tataḥ ko me puruṣa artho bhaviṣyati |

20. yadi aham gamishhyāmi = if I go; vānarendra purīm = towards ṣugreeva's city; ita ha = from here; adṛishhṭvā = without seeing; sītām = ṣeetha; tataḥ = from that; kaḥ = (to) what; purushhārthaḥ bhavishhyati = avail will this effort be.

"If I go towards ṣugreeva's city from here without seeing ṣeetha from that to what avail will this effort be?"

Sloka 21

mama idam langhanam vyartham sāgarasya bhaviṣyati || 5-13-21
praveśaḥ civa lankāyā rākṣasānām ca darśanam |

21. idam = this; mama = my; sāgarasya laṇ^ghanam = crossing of the ocean; laṇ^kāyāḥ praveshascha = entering the city of Lanka; rākshasānām darshanam = the site of Rakshasas; vyartham bhavishhyati = (all this) will become waste.

"ṃy crossing of the ocean entering the city of Lanka the site of rakshasas - all this will become waste."

Sloka 22

kim vā vakṣyati sugrīvo harayo va samāgatāḥ || 5-13-22
kiṣkindhām samanuprāptau tau vā daśaratha ātmajau |

22. mām = to me; samanuprāptam = reaching; kishkindhām = Kishkindha; kim vaksyati sugrīvaḥ = what would ṣugreeva say; samāgatāḥ harayaḥ vā = or vanaras there; tau dasharāthmajau vā = or those sons of Dasharatha (what would they say).

"ṭo me reaching Kishkindha what would ṣugreeva say or Vanaras there or those sons of Dasharatha what would they say?"

Sloka 23

gatvā tu yadi kākutstham vakṣyāmi param apriyam || 5-13-23
na dṛṣṭā iti mayā sītā tataḥ tyakṣyanti jīvitam |

23. gatvā = going(there); mayā = by me; kākustham = for Rama; sītā na dṛishhṭeti = ṣeetha has not been seen; iti = thus; vakshāmi yadi = if I say; param apriyam = a very unpleasant (word); tataḥ = thereafter; jīvitam tyakshati = (Rama) will give up life.

"Going there by me for Rama, �ṣeetha has not been seen' - thus if I say a very unpleasant word thereafter Rama will give up life."

Sloka 24

paruṣam dāruṇam krūram tīkṣṇam indriya tāpanam || 5-13-24
sītā nimittam durvākyam śrutvā sa na bhaviṣyati |

24. shrutvā = hearing; durvākhyam = bad words; sītā nimittam = belonging to ṣeetha; parushham = (that is) harsh; dāruṇam = horrible; kruram = cruel; tīkshaṇam = sharp; indriyatāpanam = sense agonizing; saḥ na bhavishhyati = ḥe will not exist.

"ḥearing bad word belonging to ṣeetha that is harsh, horrible, cruel, sharp, sense agonizing, ḥe will not exist."

Sloka 25

tam tu kṛccra gatam dṛṣṭvā pancatva gata mānasam || 5-13-25
bhṛśa anurakto medhāvī na bhaviṣyati lakṣmaṇaḥ |

25. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam = that Rama; kṛichchhra gatam = being with troubles; paJṇchatva gatamānasam = being with a heart towards death; lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; bhṛishānuraktaḥ = one with great love; medhāvi = and an intellectual; na bhavishhyati = will not exist.

"ṣeeing that Rama being with troubles, being with a heart towards death, Lakshmana, one with great love and an intellectual will not exist."

Sloka 26

vinaṣṭau bhrātarau śrutvā bharato api mariṣyati || 5-13-26
bharatam ca mṛtam dṛṣṭvā śatrughno na bhaviṣyati |

26. shṛitvā = hearing; brātarau = brothers; vinashhṭau = to be dead; bharataḥ api = Bharata also; marishhyati = will die; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; mṛitam = the dead; bharatam = Bharata; shatrughnashcha = ṣhatrughana also; na bhavishhyati = will not exist.

"ḥearing brothers to be dead, Bharata also will die. ṣeeing the dead Bharata, ṣhatrughana also will not exist."

Sloka 27

putrān mṛtān samīkṣya atha na bhaviṣyanti mātaraḥ || 5-13-27
kausalyā ca sumitrā ca kaikeyī ca na saṃśayaḥ |

27. atha = thereafter; sa mīkshya = seeing; putrān = sons; mṛitān = to be dead; mātaraḥ = the mothers; kausalyā cha = Kausalya sumitrā; cha = and ṣumitra; kaikeyī cha = and Kaikeyi; na bhavishhyanti = will not exist; samsayaḥ na = there is no doubt.

"ṭhereafter seeing sons to be dead the mothers Kausalya, ṣumitra and Kaikeyi will not exist - there is no doubt."

Sloka 28

kṛtajñaḥ satya saṃdhaḥ ca sugrīvaḥ plavaga adhipaḥ || 5-13-28
rāmam tathā gatam dṛṣṭvā tataḥ tyakṣyanti jīvitam |

28. kṛitajJṇaḥ = grateful; satyasandhashcha = true to pledge; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; plavagādhi paḥ = the leader of Vanaras; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; rāmam = Rama; tathā gatam = die in that way; jīvitam tyakshyati = will give up life; tataḥ = thereafter.

"Grateful, true to pledge ṣugreeva, the leader of Vanaras, seeing Rama the leader of Vanaras seeing Rama die in that way will give up life thereafter."

Sloka 29

durmanā vyathitā dīnā nirānandā tapasvinī || 5-13-29
pīḍitā bhartṛ śokena rumā tyakṣyati jīvitam |

29. pīDitā = being tortured; bhartrushokena = by the sorrow of husband; vyathitā = made sad; durmanāḥ = by a gloomy heart; dīnā = disconsolate; nirānandā = without happiness; tapasvinī = to be pitied; rumā = Ruma; tyakshati = will give up; jīvitam = life.

"Being tortured by the sorrow of husband, made sad by a gloomy heart, disconsolate without happiness, to be pitied, Ruma will give up life."

Sloka 30

vālijena tu duhkhena pīḍitā śoka karśitā || 5-13-30
pancatva gamane rājñaḥ tārā api na bhaviṣyati |

30. pīDitā = being distressed; vālijenadukhena = from the sorrow of Vali; shokakarshitā = being emaciated from grief; tārāpi = ṭara also; na bhavishhyati = will not exist; rājJṇi = (while) the king ṣugreeva; paJṇchatvam gate = is obtaining death.

"Being distressed from the sorrow of Vali, being emaciated from grief, ṭara also will not exist while the king Sugreeva is obtaining death."

Sloka 31

mātā pitror vināśena sugrīva vyasanena ca || 5-13-31
kumāro api angadaḥ kasmād dhārayiṣyati jīvitam |

31. kumāraḥ = the young; angada api = Angada also; vināshenā = from the death; mātā pitro = of mother and father; vyasanena = from the grief; surīvasya = of ṣugreeva; kasmāt = why will (he); dhārayishhyati jīvitam = retain life.

"ṭhe young Angada also from the death of mother and father, from the grief of ṣugreeva - why will he retain life?"

Sloka 32

bhartṛjena tu śokena abhibhūtā vana okasaḥ || 5-13-32
śirāṃsi abhihaniṣyanti talair muṣṭibhir eva ca |

32. vanaukasaḥ = Vanaras; hyabhibhūtāḥ = being rejected; bhatrujena duḥkhena = by the sorrow due to their lord; abhihanishhyanti = will strike; shirāmsi = their heads; talaiḥ = with palms; mushhṭibhirevacha = and fists.

"Vanaras being rejected by the sorrow due to their lord will strike their heads with palms and fists."

Sloka 33

sāntvena anupradānena mānena ca yaśasvinā || 5-13-33
lālitāḥ kapi rājena prāṇāmḥ tyakṣyanti vānarāḥ |

33. vānārāḥ = Vanaras; lālitāḥ = loved; yashasvinā kapirājeḥ = by the famous ṣugreeva; sāntvenā = through good words; anupradānena = through small gifts; mānena = through respect; chakshyanti = will give up praṇān = lives.

"Vanaras loved by the famous king ṣugreeva through good words, through small gifts, through respect will give up lives."

Sloka 34

na vaneṣu na śaileṣu na nirodheṣu vā punaḥ || 5-13-34
krīḍām anubhaviṣyanti sametya kapi kunjarāḥ |

34. kapikuJṇjarāḥ = best among Vanaras; na anubhavishhyanti = will not enjoy; sametya = together; krīDām = sport; vaneshhu = in forest; na shaileshhu = not on mountains; nirodheshhu vā punaḥ = not again in covered places.

"Best among Vanaras will not enjoy together sport in forest, not on mountains, not again in covered places."

Sloka 35

saputra dārāḥ sāmātyā bhartṛ vyasana pīḍitāḥ || 5-13-35
śaila agrebhyaḥ patiṣyanti sametya viṣameṣu ca |

35. sa putra dhārāḥ = together with wife and children; sāmātyāḥ = and together with ministers; bhartuvyasana pīDitāḥ = being tortured by grief of their lord; patishhyanti = they will fall down; shailāgrebhyaḥ = from top of mountains; sameshhu = on to flat lands; vishhameshhu cha = and on to rugged lands.

"ṭogether with wife and children and together with ministers being tortured by grief of their lord, they will fall down from top of mountains on to flat lands and on to rugged lands."

Sloka 36

viṣam udbandhanam vā api praveśam jvalanasya vā || 5-13-36
upavāsam atho śastram pracariṣyanti vānarāḥ |

36. vānarāḥ = Vanaras; pracharishhyanti = will perform(death); vishham = (taking) poison; utbandhanam vāpi = or get hanged; jvalanasya pravesham vā = or entering fire; upavāsam = or fasting; atho = or; shastram = a weapon.

"Vanaras will perform death by taking poison or getting hanged or entering fire or fasting or a weapon."

Sloka 37

ghoram ārodanam manye gate mayi bhaviṣyati || 5-13-37
ikṣvāku kula nāśaḥ ca nāśaḥ caiva vana okasām |

37 mayi gate = while I am going; manye = I think; ikshvāku kula nāshashcha = (about) the destruction of Ikshvāku dynasty; nāshashcaiva = and destruction; vanaukasām = of Vanaras; bhavishhyati = and there will be; ghoram ārodhanam = a terrible wail.

"While I am going I think about the destruction of Ikshvāku dynasty and destruction of Vanaras and there will be a terrible wail."

Sloka 38

so aham na eva gamiṣyāmi kiṣkindhām nagarīm itaḥ || 5-13-38
na hi śakṣyāmi aham draṣṭum sugrīvam maithilīm vinā |

38. aham = I; naiva gamishhyāmi = will not go; itaḥ = from here; kishkindhām nagarīm = to the kishkindha city; aham = I; na cha shakshyāmyaham = (am)not capable; drashhṭum = to see; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; maithilīm vinā = without ṣeetha.

"I will not go from here to the Kishkindha city. I am not capable to see ṣugreeva without ṣeetha."

Sloka 39

mayi agaccati ca ihasthe dharma ātmānau mahā rathau || 5-13-39
āśayā tau dhariṣyete vanarāḥ ca manasvinaḥ |

39. mayī = I; agachchhati = while not going; ihasthe = being here; dharmātmānau = those virtuous ones; mahā rathau = those great warriors; tau = those two Rama and Lakshmana; dharishhyate = will live; āshayā = by hope; vānarāshcha = Vanaras also; manasvinaḥ = who are agile.

"I while not going, being here those virtuous ones, those great warriors those two Rama and Lakshmana will live by hope. Vanaras also who are agile will live."

Sloka 40

hasta ādāno mukha ādāno niyato vṛkṣa mūlikaḥ || 5-13-40
vānaprastho bhaviṣyāmi adṛṣṭvā janaka ātmajām |
sāgara anūpaje deśe bahu mūla phala udake || 5-13-41

40; 41. adṛishhtvā = without seeing; janakātmajā = ṣeetha; hastā danaḥ = (subsisting on) whatever falls on hand; mukhādānaḥ = whatever comes to mouth; niyataḥ = being self restrained; bhavishhyāmi = will become; vāna prastaḥ = a hermit; vṛikshamūlikaḥ = living on trees and herbs; sāgarānupaje dese = at a region near ocean; bahumūlaphalodake = which has many roots; fruits and much water.

"Without seeing ṣeetha subsisting on whatever falls on hand, whatever comes to mouth, being self restrained I will become a hermit living on trees and herbs at a region near ocean which has many roots, fruits and much water."

Sloka 42

citām kṛtvā pravekṣyāmi samiddham araṇī sutam |
upaviṣṭasya vā samyag linginam sādhayiṣyataḥ || 5-13-42
śarīram bhakṣayiṣyanti vāyasāḥ śvāpadāni ca |

42. pravekshyāmi = or I will enter; araṇīsutam = fire; samidham = which is glowing; chitām kṛitvā = after making a funeral pile; vā = or; upavishhṭasya = while sitting; sādhayishhyataḥ = performing; liṇ^gnam = fasting onto death; sariram bhakshayishhyanti = my body will be eaten; vāyasāḥ = by crows; shvapadāni cha = and by beasts of prey.

"Or I will enter fire which is glowing after making a funeral pile or while sitting performing fasting onto death my body will be eaten by crows and by beasts of prey."

Sloka 43

idam api ṛṣibhir dṛṣṭam niryāṇam iti me matiḥ || 5-13-43
samyag āpaḥ pravekṣyāmi na cet paśyāmi jānakīm |

43. idam = this; niryāṇam = way to giving up body; dṛishhṭam = has been seen; maharshhibhi = by sages; iti = this; me matiḥ = is my; opinion; jānakīm na chetpashyāmi = if I do not see ṣeetha; pravekshyāmi = I will enter; samyak āpaḥ = lot of water.

"ṭhis way to giving up body has been seen by sages. ṭhis is my opinion if I do not see ṣeetha, I will enter lot of water."

Sloka 44

sujāta mūlā subhagā kīrti mālā yaśasvinī || 5-13-44
prabhagnā cira rātrī iyam mama sītām apaśyataḥ |

44. sītām apashyataḥ = not seeing ṣeetha; mama kīrtimālā = my garland of glory; chirarātrāya = of long time; sujātamūla = with a strong foundation; subhagā = beautiful one; yashasvinī = together with fame; prabhagnā = has been destroyed.

"ṇot seeing ṣeetha, my garland of glory of long time with a strong foundation, beautiful one together with fame has been destroyed."

Sloka 45

tāpaso vā bhaviṣyāmi niyato vṛkṣa mūlikaḥ || 5-13-45
na itaḥ pratigamiṣyāmi tām adṛṣṭvā asita īkṣaṇām |

45. bhavishhyāmi = I will become; tāpasovā = as a hermit; niyataḥ = who is self restrained; vṛikshamūlikaḥ = resorting to trees and herbs; na pratigamishhyāmi = I will not go; itaḥ = from here; adṛishhṭvā = not seeing; tām = ṣeetha; asitekshanām = with black eyes.

"I will become a hermit who is self restrained resorting to trees and herbs, I will not go from here not seeing Seetha with black eyes."

Sloka 46

yadi itaḥ pratigaccāmi sītām anadhigamya tām || 5-13-46
angadaḥ sahitaiḥ sarvair vānarair na bhaviṣyati |

46. yadi pratigachchhāmi = if I go back; itaḥ = from here; anadhigamyā = without knowing; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; na bhavishhyati = there will not be; angadhaḥ = Angada; sarvaiḥ tai vānaraiḥ saḥ = together with all those Vanaras.

"If I go back from here without knowing that ṣeetha, there will not be Angada together with all those Vanaras."

Sloka 47

vināśe bahavo doṣā jīvan prāpnoti bhadrakam || 5-13-47
tasmāt prāṇān dhariṣyāmi dhruvo jīvati samgamaḥ |

47. bahavaḥ doshhāḥ = there are lot of blemishes; vināshe = in dieing; jīvan = one who is alive; pashyati = sees; bhadrāṇi = auspicious things; tasmāt = for that reason; dharishhyāmi = I will keep; prāṇān = my life; druvaḥ = it is definite; jīvitasamgāḥ = for the meeting of people who are alive.

"ṭhere are lots of blemishes in dying; one who is alive sees auspicious things. For that reason I will keep my life. It is definite for the meeting of people who are alive."

Sloka 48

evam bahu vidham duhkham manasā dhārayan muhuḥ || 5-13-48
na adhyagaccat tadā pāram śokasya kapi kunjaraḥ |

48. kapikuJṇjaraḥ = ḥanuma; dhārayan = carrying; manasā = in mind; bahuvidham duḥkham = sorrow of various kinds; muhuḥ = again and again; nādhyagachchhat = did not get; tadā = then; shokhasya pāram = the crossing of sorrow.

ḥanuma carrying in mind sorrow of various kinds again and again did not get then the crossing of sorrow.

Sloka 49

rāvaṇam vā vadhiṣyāmi daśagrīvam mahā balam || 5-13-49
kāmam astu hṛtā sītā pratyācīrṇam bhaviṣyati |

49. vā = otherwise; vadhishhyāmi = I will kill; rāvaṇam = Ravana; dashagrīvam = with ten heads; mahābalam = with great prowess; hṛitā = the stolen; sītā = ṣeetha; kāmam astu = whatever happened to her; pratyāchīrṇam bhavishhyati = this will be a revenge to that.

"Otherwise I will kill Ravana with ten heads, with great prowess. Whatever happened to the stolen Seetha, this will be revenge to that."

Sloka 50

athavā enam samutkṣipya upari upari sāgaram || 5-13-50
rāmāya upahariṣyāmi paśum paśu pater iva |

50. athavā = otherwise; samutkshipyā = carrying; evam = this Ravana; sāgaram uparyupari = over the surface of ocean; upaharishhyāmi = I will take him; rāmāya = to Rama; pashum iva = like an animal; pashupate = to the lord of animals.

"Otherwise carrying this Ravana over the surface of ocean I will take him to Rama like an animal to the lord of animals."

Sloka 51

iti cintā samāpannaḥ sītām anadhigamya tām || 5-13-51
dhyāna śokā parīta ātmā cintayām āsa vānaraḥ |

51. vānaraḥ = ḥanuma; anadhigamya = not seeing; tām sītām = that ṣeetha; iti = thus; samāpannaḥ = begetting; chintām = sorrow; chintayāmāsa = thought; dhyānashokaparītātma = with a mind cluttered with thoughts and sorrow.

ḥanuma not seeing that ṣeetha thus begetting sorrow, thought with a mind cluttered with thoughts and sorrow.

Sloka 52

yāvat sītām na paśyāmi rāma patnīm yaśasvinīm || 5-13-52
tāvad etām purīm lankām vicinomi punaḥ punaḥ |

52. yāvat = to wherever; pashyāmi = I see; sītām = ṣeetha; rāmapatnīm = the wife of ṣri Rama; yashasvinīm = and one with fame; tavat = till there; vichinomi = I will search; etām laṇ^kām purīm = the city of Lanka; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again.

"ṭo wherever I see ṣeetha, the wife of ṣri Rama and one with fame, till there I will search the city of Lanka again and again."

Sloka 53

sampāti vacanāc ca api rāmam yadi ānayāmi aham || 5-13-53
apaśyan rāghavo bhāryām nirdahet sarva vānarān |

53. sampāti vachanāt = based on words of ṣampati; aham ānayāmi yadi = if I bring; rāmam = ṣri Rama(then); raghavaḥ = ṣri Rama; apasyan = not seeing; bhāryām = wife; nirdahet = will burn; sarva vānarān = all Vanaras.

"Based on words of ṣampati if I bring ṣri Rama then ṣri Rama not seeing wife will burn all Vanaras."

Sloka 54

iha eva niyata āhāro vatsyāmi niyata indriyaḥ || 5-13-54
na mat kṛte vinaśyeyuḥ sarve te nara vānarāḥ |

54. niyatāhāraḥ = with limited food; yatendriyaḥ = and with restrained senses; vatsyāmi = and I live; ihaiva = here itself; matkṛite = by my deed; te naravānarāḥ = those men and vanaras; na vinasheyuḥ = let they not be destroyed.

"With limited food and with restrained senses, I live here itself. By my deed those men and vanaras - let they not be destroyed."

Sloka 55

aśoka vanikā ca api mahatī iyam mahā drumā || 5-13-55
imām abhigamiṣyāmi na hi iyam vicitā mayā |

55. yā = whatever; iyam = this; ashokavanikā = Ashoka garden; mahādṛimā = with great trees; dṛishyate = is being seen; imam = this; adhigamishhyāmi = I will obtain; iyam = this; na vichitāḥ = has not been searched; mayā = by me.

"Whatever this Ashoka garden with great trees is being seen this I will obtain. ṭhis has not been searched by me."

Sloka 56

vasūn rudrāmḥ tathā ādityān aśvinau maruto api ca || 5-13-56
namaḥ kṛtvā gamiṣyāmi rakṣasām śoka vardhanaḥ |

56. namaskṛitvā = saluting; vasūn = the eight Vasus; rudrān = Rudras; tathā = and; ādityān = Adityas; aswinau = the two Aswinis; maruto api cha = seven maruts; gamishhyāmi = I will go; shokavardhanaḥ = to increase the grief; rakshasām = of Rakshasas.

"ṣaluting the eight Vasus, Rudras and Adityas, the two Aswinis, seven ṃaruts, I will go to increase the grief of Rakshasas."

Sloka 57

jitvā tu rākṣasān devīm ikṣvāku kula nandinīm || 5-13-57
sampradāsyāmi rāmāyā yathā siddhim tapasvine |

57. jitvātu = defeating; sarvān rakshasān = all Rakshasas; sampradāsyāmi = I will give; ikshvāku kulanandinī = ṣeetha who gives happiness to Ikshvāku dynasty; rāmaya = to ṣri Rama; sidhhim yathā = as the fruit of austerity; tapasvine = to an ascetic.

"Defeating all rakshasas, I will give ṣeetha, who gives happiness to Ikshvāku dynasty to ṣri Rama as the fruit of austerity to an ascetic."

Sloka 58

sa muhūrtam iva dhyātvā cintā vigrathita indriyaḥ || 5-13-58
udatiṣṭhan mahā bāhur hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |

58. mahā tejāḥ = the great gloried; mārutātmajaḥ = son of Vayu; saḥ hanuman = that ḥanuma; vudatishhṭat = got up; chintāḥ grathitedriyaḥ = with his senses tied by grief; dhyātvā = meditating; muhurtam iva = for a moment.

ṭhe gloried son of Vayu that ḥanuma got up with his senses tied by grief.

Sloka 59

namo astu rāmāya salakṣmaṇāya |
devyai ca tasyai janaka ātmajāyai |
namo astu rudra indra yama anilebhyo |
namo astu candra arka marud gaṇebhyaḥ || 5-13-59

59. namaḥ astu = let there be salutations; rāmāya = to ṣri Rama; sa lakshmaṇāya = together with Lakshmana; tasyai janakātmajāyai cha = also to that; daughter of Janaka; devyai = who is divine namaḥ astu = let there be salutations; rudrendra yamānilebhyaḥ = to Rudra; Indra; Yama and Vāyu; namaḥ astu = let there be salutations; chandrarkamarudgaṇebhyaḥ = to chandra; ṣun and ṃarut ganas.

"Let there be salutations to ṣri Rama together with Lakshmana, also to that, who is divine let there be salutations. To Rudra, Indra, Yama and Vāyu let there be salutations."

Sloka 60

sa tebhyaḥ tu namaḥ kṛtvā sugrīvāya ca mārutiḥ |
diśaḥ sarvāḥ samālokya aśoka vanikām prati || 5-13-60

60. saḥ marutiḥ = that ḥanuma; namaskṛitvā = saluting; tebhyaḥ = to all those; sugrīvāya cha = and also to ṣugreeva; samālokya = observing; sarvāḥ dishaḥ = all directions; gataḥ = went; ashokavanikām = towards Ashoka garden.

ṭhat ḥanuma saluting to all those and also to ṣugreeva, observing all directions went towards Ashoka garden.

Sloka 61

sa gatvā manasā pūrvam aśoka vanikām śubhām |
uttaram cintayām āsa vānaro māruta ātmajaḥ || 5-13-61

61. mārutātmajaḥ = the son of Vayu; saḥ vānara = that Vanara; gatvā = went; manasā = by mind; pūrvam = before itself; ashoka vanikām = toward that Ashoka garden; shubham = the auspicious one; chintayāmāsa = pondered; uttaram = the task to be done later.

ṭhe son of Vayu, that Vanara went by mind before itself toward that Ashoka garden. ṭhe auspicious one pondered the task to be done later.

Sloka 62

dhruvam tu rakṣo bahulā bhaviṣyati vana ākulā |
aśoka vanikā cintyā sarva saṃskāra saṃskṛtā || 5-13-62

62. ashokavanikā = the Ashoka garden; dṛivam = definitely; bhavishhyati = will be; rakshobahulā = with many rakshasas; vanākulā = filled with trees; sarvasamsāra samskṛitā = adorned with various cultures; puṇyā = and sacred.

"ṭhe Ashoka garden definitely will be with many rakshasas, filled with trees, adorned with various cultures and sacred."

Sloka 63

rakṣiṇaḥ ca atra vihitā nūnam rakṣanti pādapān |
bhagavān api sarva ātmā na atikṣobham pravāyati || 5-13-63
samkṣipto ayam mayā ātmā ca rāma arthe rāvaṇasya ca |

63. tatra = there; mūlam = definitely; rakshinashcha = guards; rakshyanti = will be protecting; vihitāha = assigned; pādapān = trees; sarvātma = omni present; bhagavān api = lord Vayu also; pravāti vai = will be blowing by; nātikshobham = without force; mayā = by me; iyam atmā = this body; samkshiptaḥ = has been made small; rāmādhe = for the sake of ṣri Rama; ravaṇasya cha = and (to avoid) Ravana.

"ṭhere definitely guards will be protecting assigned, trees, omnipresent Lord Vayu also will be blowing by without force, and by me this body has been made small for the sake of Sri Rama and to avoid Ravana."

Sloka 64 Verse Locator

siddhim me samvidhāsyanti devāḥ sarṣi gaṇāḥ tv iha || 5-13-64
brahmā svayambhūr bhagavān devāḥ caiva diśantu me |
siddhim agniḥ ca vāyuḥ ca puru hūtaḥ ca vajradhṛt || 5-13-65
varuṇaḥ pāśa hastaḥ ca soma ādityai tathaiva ca |
aśvinau ca mahātmānau marutaḥ sarva eva ca || 5-13-66
siddhim sarvāṇi bhūtāni bhūtānām caiva yaḥ prabhuḥ |
dāsyanti mama ye ca anye adṛṣṭāḥ pathi gocarāḥ || 5-13-67

64;65;66;67. devāḥ = Devas; sarshhigaṇāḥ = together with ascetics; samvidhāsyanti = will make; siddhim = success; me = to me; iha = here; svayambhūḥ = self born; brahmā = Brahma; bhagvān = the divine one; devāshchaivā = and devas; agnishcha = and Agni; vāyushcha = and Vāyu; puruhūtashcha = devendra; vajrabhṛit = wearing the weapon of Vajra; varuṇashcha = and Varuna ; pāshahastaḥ = with Pasha in hand; tathaiva cha = and also; somādityau = the ṣun and the moon; aswinau cha = and the aswinis; mahātmānau = the great ones; marutaḥ = maruts; sharvaḥ eva cha = and Eshwara; dishantu = may give; me siddhim = my success; yaḥ = whoever; prabhūḥ = is the lord; bhūtānām = of all living creatures; sarvāṇi bhūtāni = among all living beings; anye = others; ye = who; adṛishhṭāḥ = being unseen; ghocharāḥ = exist; pathi = in the path; dāsyanti = may give; sidhhim = success; mama = to me.

"Devas together with ascetics will make success to me here, self born Brahma the divine one and devas and Agni and Vāyu, Devendra wearing the weapon of Vajra and Varuna with Pasha in hand and also the Sun and the moon and the aswinis. The great ones ṃaruts and Eshwara may give my success. Whoever is the lord of all living creatures among all living beings others who being unseen exist in the path may give success to me."

Sloka 68

tad unnasam pāṇḍura dantam avraṇam |
śuci smitam padma palāśa locanam |
drakṣye tad āryā vadanam kadā nv aham |
prasanna tārā adhipa tulya darśanam || 5-13-68

68. kadā = when; aham drakshye nu = will I see; tat ārya vadanam = that noble face; unnasam = with a high nose; pāṇDuram dantam = with white teeth; avraṇam = without any injuries; shuchismitam = with a bright smile; padmaphalāsha lochanam = with eyes resembling lotus petals; prasanna tāradhipa tulya darshanam = with sight equaling the splendor of clear full moon.

"When will I see that noble face with a high nose, with white teeth without any injuries with a bright smile, with eyes resembling lotus petals with sight equaling the splendor of clear full moon?"

Sloka 69

kṣudreṇa pāpena nṛśaṃsa karmaṇā |
sudāruṇa alāmkṛta veṣa dhāriṇā|
bala abhibhūtā abalā tapasvinī |
katham nu me dṛṣṭa pathe adya sā bhavet || 5-13-69

69. kadam = how; sā abalā = that powerless ṣeetha; tapasvinī = helpless one; balābhibhūtā = forcefully taken away; kshudreṇa = (by the) mean ; pāpena = vile (Ravana); nṛishamsa karmaṇa = with a cruel action; sudāruṇa alankṛita veshha dhāriṇā = with a horribly decorated form; bhavet nu = will fall; me dṛishhṭipathe = in the range of my sight; adya = today.

"ḥow that powerless ṣeetha, a helpless one, forcefully taken away by the mean vile Ravana with a cruel action, with a horribly decorated form will fall in the range of my sight today."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe trayodaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 13th chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
