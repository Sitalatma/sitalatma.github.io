Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 123
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In the course of their flight to Ayodhya, Rama shows to Seetha, the whole City of Lanka, the battle-field where Ravana and other demons were killed, Nala-Setu the bridge which was built across the ocean, ṃount ṃainka, Setu-bandha (where the construction of the bridge was started), ṃount Rishyamuka, Pampa River, Janasthana-forest, Panchavati, Godavari river, the hermitage of sage-Agastya, the hermitage of Atri and Anasuya, ṃount chitrakuta, Yamuna-river, the hermitage of Bharadwaja, Ganga-river, Sarayu-river and finally the City of Ayodhya.

Sloka 1

anujñātan tu rāmeṇa tadvimānamanuttamam |
utpapāta mahāmeghaḥ śvasanenoddhato yathā || 6-123-1

1. anujJṇātam = as approved; rāmeṇa = by Rama; tat anuttamam = that excellent; vimānam = aerial car; hamsayukam = with images of swans painted on it; utpapātam = flew; vihāyasam = into the sky; mahānādam = with a great sound.

As approved by Rama, that excellent aerial car, with images of swan painted on it, flew into the sky, with a great sound.

Sloka 2

pātayitvā tataścakṣuḥ sarvato raghunandanaḥ |
abravīnmaithilīn sītāṃ rāmaḥ śaśinibhānanām || 6-123-2

2. tataḥ = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; raghunanadanaḥ = the delight of Raghu dynasty; pātayitvā = casting; chakṣhuḥ = his look; sarvataḥ = on all sides; abravīt = spoke; sītām = to ṣeetha; maithilīm = the princes; of ṃithila kingdom; shashinibhānanām = having her face looking like the moon (as follow):

Casting his look on all sides, Rama, the delight of Raghu dynasty, then spoke to Seetha, who had a moon-like face (as follows):

Sloka 3

kailāsaśikharākāre trikūṭaśikhare sthitām |
laṅkāmīkṣasva vaidehi nirmitān viśvakarmaṇā || 6-123-3

3. vaidehi = O ṣeetha; īkṣhasva = look at; laṇ^kām = the City of Lanka; nirmitām = built; vishvakarmaṇā = by Vishvakarma; the universal architect; sthitām = and resting firmly; trikūṭa shikharākāre = like a peak of ṃount Kailasa in appearance.

"O Seetha! Look at the City of Lanka, resting firmly on the mount of Trikuta, looking like a peak of ṃount Kailasa and built by Viswarakarma, the universal architect."

Sloka 4

etadāyodhanaṃ paśya mānsaśoṇitakardamam |
harīṇān rākṣasānān ca sīte viśasanaṃ mahat || 6-123-4

4. sīte = O ṣeetha!; pashya = look at; etat mahatāyodhanam = this great battle-field; māmsashoṇita kardamam = covered with a mud a flesh and blood; vishasanam = and a cause for the death; harīṇām rākṣhasānāmcha = of monkeys and demons.

"O Seetha! Look at this great battle-field, covered with a mud of flesh and blood as also a cause for the death of monkeys and demons."

Sloka 5

atra dattavaraḥ śete pramāthī rākṣaseśvaraḥ |
tava hetorviśālākṣi rāvaṇo nihato mayā || 6-123-5

5. vishālākṣhi = O the large-eyed ṣeetha!; atra shete = here lies; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣhaseshvaraḥ = the king of demons; pramāthī = the tormentator of people; dattavaraḥ = on whom boons had been conferred (by Brahma); nihataḥ = was killed; mayā = by me; tava hetoḥ = for your sake.

"O the large-eyed Seetha! Here lies Ravana, the king of demons, the tormentator of people, on whom boons were conferred by Brahma and who was killed by me for your sake."

Sloka 6

kumbhakarṇoatra nihataḥ prahastaśca niśācaraḥ |
dhūmrākṣaścātra nihato vānareṇa hanūmatā || 6-123-6

6. atra = here; kumbhakarṇa = Kumbhakarna; nihataḥ = was killed; prahastashcha = and Prahasta; nishācharaḥ = the demon; atra = here; dhūmrakṣhaḥ = Dhumraksha; nihataḥ = was killed; hanūmatā = by ḥanuma; vānareṇa = the monkey.

"Here, Kumbhakarna and Prahasta the demon were killed. Here, Dhumraksha was killed by Hanuma, the monkey."

Sloka 7

vidyunmālī hataścātra suṣeṇena mahātmanā |
lakṣmaṇenendrajiccātra rāvaṇirnihato raṇe || 6-123-7

7. atra = here; mahātmanā suṣheṇena = by the great-souled ṣushena; vidyunmālī = Vidyunmali; hata = was killed; atra = here; lakṣhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; indrajit = Indrajit; rāvaṇiḥ = the son of Ravana; nihataḥ = was killed; raṇe = in a combat.

"Here was Vidyunmali killed by the great-souled Sushena and here was Indrajit, the son of Ravana, killed by Lakshmana in a combat."

Sloka 8

aṅgadenātra nihato vikaṭo nāma rākṣasaḥ |
virūpākṣaśca duṣprekṣyo mahāpārśvamahodarau || 6-123-8
akampanaśca nihato balinoanye ca rākṣasāḥ |
triśirāścātikāyaśca devāntakanarāntakau || 6-123-9

8-9. atra = here; nihataḥ was killed; vikaṭo nāma rākṣhasaḥ = a demon called Vikata; aṇ^gadena = by Angada; virūpākṣhaḥ = Virupaksha; duṣhprekṣhaḥ = who was disagreeable to the sight; mahāpārshvo mahodarau = ṃahaparshva; ṃahodara; akampanashcha = and Akampana too; nihataḥ = were killed; anye = other; balinaḥ = mighty; rākṣhasāḥ cha = demons; trishirāḥ = ṭrishiras; atikāyashcha = Atikaya; devāntakanarāntakau = Devantaka and ṇarantaka (were killed).

"Here was killed a demon called Vikata by Angada. Virupaksha, who was disagreeable to the sight, ṃahaparsva, ṃahodara, Akampana, Trishiras, Atikaya Devantaka, Narantaka and other mighty demons were also killed here."

Sloka 10

yuddhonmattaśca mattaśca rākṣasapravarāvubhau |
nikumbhaścaiva kumbhaśca kumbhakarṇātmajau balī || 6-123-10
vajradaṃṣṭraśca daṃṣṭraśca bahavo rākṣasā hatāḥ |
makarākṣca durdharṣo mayā yudhi nipātitaḥ || 6-123-11

10-11. ubhau = both; yuddhanmattashcha = yuddhonmatta; mattashcha = and ṃatta; rākṣhasa pravarau = the foremost of demons; nikumbhashchaiva = ṇikumbha; kumbhashcha = and Kumbha; kumbhakarṇātmajau = the sons of Kumbhakarna; balī = the strong; vajradamṣhṭrashcha = Vajradamshtra; damṣhṭrashcha = Damstra; bahavaḥ = and many; rākṣhasāḥ = demons; hatāḥ = were killed; makarākṣhasya = ṃakaraksha; durdharṣhaḥ = the most difficult demon to be attacked; nipātitaḥ = was struck down; mayā = by me; yudhi = in this battle-field.

"Both Yuddonmatta and ṃatta, the foremost of demons, as also Nikumbha and Kumbha the sons of Kumbhakarna, as also the strong Vajradamshtra, Damshtra and many demons were killed. ṃakaraksha, the most difficult demon to be attacked, was struck down by me in this battle-field."

Sloka 12

akampanaśca nihataḥ śoṇitākṣaśca vīryavān |
yūpākṣaśca prajaṅghaśca nihatau tu mhāhave || 6-123-12

12. akampanashcha = Akampana; nihataḥ = was killed; mahāhave = in the great battle; vīryavān = the valiant; shoṇitākṣhasheha = ṣhonitaksha;(was killed); yūpākṣhashcha = Yupakasha; prajaṇ^ghashcha = and Prajangha; nihatau = were killed.

"Akampana was killed in the great battle. The valiant Shonitaksha was also killed. Yupaksa and Prajangha were killed."

Sloka 13

vidyujjihvo.atra nihato rākṣaso bhīmadarśanaḥ |
yajñaśatruśca nihataḥ suptaghnaśca mahābalaḥ || 6-123-13
sūryaśatruśca nihato brahmaśatrustathāparaḥ |

13. atra = here; vidyujjihvaḥ = Vidyujjihva; rākṣhasah = the demon; bhīma darshanaḥ = with a terrific appearance; nihataḥ = was killed; yajJṇashatrushcha = Yajnashatru also; nihataḥ = was killed; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; suptaghnashcha = ṣuptaghna; nihataḥ = was killed.

"Here, Vidyujjihva, the demon was a terrific appearance, was killed. Yajnashatru also was killed. The mighty Suptaghna was killed. Surya shatru was killed and another demon called Brahma shatru was too killed."

Sloka 14

atra mandodarī nāma bhāryā taṃ paryadevayat || 6-123-14
sapatnīnān sahasreṇa sāsreṇa parivāritā |

14. atra = here; parivāritā = surrounded; sahasreṇa sāgreṇa = by more than a thousand; sapatnīnām = fellow-wives; bhāryā = a wife; mandodarīnāma = named ṃandodari; paryadevayat = lamented; tam = about that Ravana.

"Here, surrounded by more than a thousand fellow-wives, Ravana's wife named ṃandodari lamented for Ravana."

Sloka 15

etattu dṛśyate tīrthan samudrasya varānane || 6-123-15
yatra sāgaramuttīrya tān rātrimuṣitā vayam |

15. varānane = O ṣeetha; the lovely-faced one!; etat dṛishyate = here is seen; tīrtham = a water-descent; samudrasya = of the ocean (on the sea-shore) yatra uttīrya = where having crossed; sāgaram = the ocean; vayam = we; uṣhitāḥ = spent; tām rātrim = that night.

"O Seetha, the lovely-faced one! Here is seen a water-descent of the ocean on the sea-shore, where having crossed the ocean, we spent that night."

Sloka 16

eṣa seturmayā baddhaḥ sāgare salilārṇave || 6-123-16
tavahetorviśālākṣi nalasetuḥ suduṣkaraḥ |

16. vishālākṣhi = O the large-eyed ṣeetha!; eṣhaḥ = here is; nalasetuḥ = the bridge called ṇala ṣetu; suduṣhkaraḥ = which was so difficult (to execute for others); baddhaḥ = got built; mayā = by me; lavaṇārṇave = over the salt-sea; tava hetoḥ = for your sake.

"O the large-eyed Seetha! Here is the bridge called Nala Setu, which was so difficult to execute for others, got built by me over the salt-sea for your sake."

Sloka 17

paśya sāgaramakṣobhyan vaidehi varuṇālayam || 6-123-17
apāramabhigarjantan śaṅkhaśuktiniṣevitam |

17. vaidehi = O ṣeetha!; pashya = see; sāgaram = this ocean; akṣhobhyam = the imperturbable; varuṇālayam = the habitation of Varuna; garjantam = and which is roaring; apāramiva = as though it is boundless; shaṇ^kha shukti samākulam = and teeming well with couches and oyster-shells.

"O Seetha! See this roaring imperturbable and seemingly boundless ocean, the habitation of Varuna (the god of waters), which is teeming well with couches and oyster-shells."

Sloka 18

hiraṇyanābhan śailendran kāñcanaṃ paśya maithili || 6-123-18
viśramārthan hanumato bhittvā sāgaramutthitam |

18. maithili = O ṣeetha!; parhya = see; kāṇ^chanam hiraṇya nābham = this golden mountain; ṃainka; shailendram = the ruler of mountains; utthitam = and which rose; bhittvā = by passing through; sāgaram = the ocean; vishramārtham = in order to provide rest and relaxation; hanumataḥ = to ḥanuma.

"O Seetha! See the golden mountain, ṃainaka (the ruler of mountains) which rose passing through the ocean, in order to provide rest and relaxation to Hanuma."

Sloka 19

etat kukṣau samudrasya skandhāvāraniveśanam || 6-123-19
atra pūrvaṃ mahādevaḥ prasādamakarotprabhuḥ |

19. etat = this; (is the island); kukṣhau = located in the middle; samudrasya = of the ocean; skandhāvāra niveshanam = where my troops were stationed; atra = at this place; pūrvam = formerly; prabhuḥ = the Lord; mahādevaḥ = ṣhiva (the supreme deity); akarot = bestowed; prasādam = his grace; (on me).

"See this island, located in the middle of the ocean, where my troops were stationed. At this place, the lord Shiva (the supreme deity) formerly bestowed his grace on me."

Sloka 20

etattu dṛśyate tīrthaṃ sāgarasya mahātmanaḥ || 6-123-20
setubandha iti khyātaṃ trailokyena ca pūjitam |
etatpavitraṃ paramaṃ mahāpātakanāśanam || 6-123-21
atra rākṣasarājo.ayamājagāma vibhīṣaṇaḥ |

20-21. etat tu dṛishyate = here is seen; tīrtham mahātmanaḥ sāgarasya = the water-descent of the great-souled ocean; pūjitam = worshipped; trailokyena = by the three worlds; khyātam = called; setubandhaḥ iti = as ṣetu bandha; etat = this; paramam pavitram = is very much a sacred spot; mahāpātakanāshanam = which destroys major sins; ayam = this; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; rākṣhasarājaḥ = the king of demons; ājagāma = (first) came; atra = here.

"Here is seen the water-descent of the gigantic ocean, called a Setubandha, adored in all the three worlds. This is very much a sacred spot, capable of washing away major sins. At this very place, Vibhishana the king of demons first came."

Sloka 22

eṣā sā dṛśyate sīte kiṣkindhā citrakānanā || 6-123-22
sugrīvasya purī ramyā yatra vālī mayā hataḥ |

22. sīte = O ṣeetha!; eṣhā = here is; dṛishyate = see; sā kiṣhkindhā = that Kishkindha; ramyā purī = a beautiful City; chitrakānanā = with colourful groves; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva; yatra = where; vālī = Vali; hataḥ = was killed; mayā = by me.

"O Seetha! Here is seen the beautiful City of Sugreeva, Kishkindha, which is full of colourful groves, where Vali was killed by me."

Sloka 23

atha dṛṣṭvā purīṃ sītā kiṣkindhāṃ vālipālitām || 6-123-23
abravītpraśritaṃ vākyaṃ rāmaṃ praṇayasādhvasā |

23.dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; kiṣhkindhām purīm = the City of Kishkindha; vālipālitām = which was ruled by Vali (earlier); sītā = ṣeetha; atha = then; praṇaya sādhvasā = who was feeling timed through love; abravīt = spoke; prashritam vākyam = the following courteous words; rāmam = to Rama.

Seeing the City of Kishkindha, which was earlier ruled by Vali, Seetha, who was feeling timid through love, then spoke the following courteous words to Rama.

Sloka 24

sugrīvapriyabhāryābhistārāpramukhāto nṛpa || 6-123-24
anyeṣāṃ vānarendrāṇāṃ strībhiḥ parivṛtā hyaham |
gantumicche sahāyodhyāṃ rājadhānīṃ tvayā saha || 6-123-25

24-25. nṛipa = O king!; aham = I; ichchhe = wish; gantum = to go; rājadhānīm ayodhyām = to the capital City of Ayodhya; tvayā saha = along with you; parivṛitā = accompanied by; strībhiḥ = the wives; anyeṣhām vānarendrāṇām of other monkey-chiefs; tārā pramukhataḥ = with ṭara and other; sugrīva priyabhāryābhiḥ = beloved wives of ṣugreeva.

"O king! I wish to reach the capital City of Ayodhya along with you, accompanied by the wives of other monkey-chiefs with Tara and other beloved wives of Sugreeva."

Sloka 26

evamukto.atha vaidehyā rāghavaḥ pratyuvāca tām |
evamastviti kiṣkindhāṃ prāpya saṃsthāpya rāghavaḥ || 6-123-26
vimānaṃ prekṣya sugrīvaṃ kākyametaduvāca ha |

26. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; vaidehyāḥ = by ṣeetha; rāghavaḥ = Rama; atha = then; pratyuvācha = replied; tām = to her; evam astu iti = "Let it be"; prāpya = reaching; kiṣhkindhām = Kishkindha; samsthāpya = and halting; vimānam = the aerial car; preṣhya = and seeing; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; uvācha ha = spoke (to him); etat vākyam = the following words:

Hearing the words of Seetha, Rama then said to her in reply "let it be". Reaching Kishkindha, causing the aerial car to halt, and looking at Sugreeva, Rama spoke to him the following words:

Sloka 27

brūhi vānaraśārdūla sarān vānarapuṅgavān || 6-123-27
strībhiḥ parivṛtāh sarve hyayodhyāṃ yāntu sītayā |

27. vānara shārdūla = O the foremost of the monkeys!; brūhi = tell; sarvān = all; vānarapungavān = the monkey-chiefs; sarve = "All of you"; yantu = come; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; sītayā = with ṣeetha; parivṛitāḥ strībhiḥ = accompanied by your wives.

"O the king of monkeys! Instruct all the monkey-chiefs to come to Ayodhya in the company of their wives."

Sloka 28

tathā tvamapi sarvābhiḥ strībhiḥ saha mahābala || 6-123-28
abhitvarasva sugrīva gacchāmaḥ plavagādhipa |

28. mahābala = O the mighty; sugreeva = ṣugreeva; plavagādhipa = the king of monkeys; tathā = and; tvam api abhitvarasva = you too hasten to depart; sarvābhiḥ strībhiḥ saha = taking the wives of all the monkeys; gachchhāmaḥ = we shall proceed.

"O the mighty Sugreeva, the King of monkeys! You too hasten to depart, taking the wives of all the monkeys "we shall proceed."

Sloka 29

evamuktastu sugrīvo rāmeṇāmitatejasā || 6-123-29
vānarādhipatiḥ śrīmāṃstaiśca sarvaiḥ samāvṛtaḥ |
praviśyāntaḥpuraṃ śīghram tārāmudvīkṣya so.abravīt || 6-123-30

29-30. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmeṇa = by Rama; amita tejasā = of great splendour; saḥ shrīmān sugrīvaḥ = that illustrious ṣugreeva; vānarādhipatiḥ = the lord of monkeys; taiḥ sarnesamāvṛitah accompanied by all those monkeys; shīghram = quickly; pravishya = entering; antaḥpuram = his gynaecium; udvīkṣhya = and looking; tārām = at ṭara; abravīt = spoke; (as follows):

Hearing the words of Rama of great splendour, that illustrious Sugreeva, the lord of monkeys accompanied by all those monkeys, quickly entering his gynaecium, spoke to Tara as follows:

Sloka 31

priye tvaṃ sahanārībhirvānarāṇāṃ mahātmanām |
rāghaveṇābhyanujñātā maithilīpriyakāmyayā || 6-123-31

31. priya = O the beloved lady!; tvam = you; abhyanujJṇātā = have been permitted; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; 9to proceed to Ayodhya); nārībhiḥ saha = in the company of the wives; mahātmānam vānarāṇān = of the great-souled monkeys; maithilīpriya kāmyayā = with an intent to gratify ṣeetha.

"O the beloved lady! You have been permitted by Rama to proceed to Ayodhya, in the company of the wives of the great-souled monkeys, with an intent to gratify Seetha."

Sloka 32

tvara tvamabhigacchāmo gṛhya vānarayoṣitaḥ |
ayodhyāṃ darśayiṣyāmaḥ sarvā daśarathastriyaḥ || 6-123-32

32. tvam = you; tvara = hurry up; gachchhāma = we shall proceed; gṛihya = by taking; vānarayoṣhitaḥ = the wives of the monkeys; darshayiṣhyāmaḥ = we shall show; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; sarāḥ = and all; dasharathastriyaḥ = the widows of Dasaratha.

"You hurry up, to proceed to Ayodhya, with the wives of the monkeys. We shall show Ayodhya and all the widows of Dasaratha to them."

Sloka 33

sugrīvasya vacaḥ śrutvā tārā sarvāṅgaśobhanā |
āhūya cābravītsarvā vānarāṇāṃ tu yoṣitaḥ || 6-123-33

33. shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva; tārā = ṭara; sarvāṇ^ga shobhanā = who was charming of every limb; āhūya cha = called; sarvāḥ striyaḥ = all the spouses; vānarānām = of the monkeys abravīt = and spoke; (as follows):

Hearing the words of Sugreeva, Tara, who was charming of every limb, called all the spouses of the monkeys and spoke to them as follows:

Sloka 34

sugrīveṇābhyanujñātā gantuṃ sarvaiśca vānaraiḥ |
mama cāpi priyaṃ kāryamayodhyādarśanena ca || 6-123-34

34. anujJṇātāḥ = duly permitted; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; sarvaiḥ vānaraishcha = and all the monkeys; gantum = hurry up to proceed (to Ayodhya); priyam kāryam = a kindly act; mama chāpi = will be done to me too; ayodhyādarshanena = through the sight of Ayodhya (which is so dear to me)."

"Duly permitted by Sugreeva and all the monkeys, hurry up to proceed to Ayodhya. A kindly act will be done to me too, through the sight of Ayodhya (which is so dear to me)."

Sloka 35

praveśam caiva rāmasya paurajānapadaiḥ saha |
vibhūtiṃ caiva sarvāsāṃ strīṇāṃ daśarathasya ca || 6-123-35

35. (we shall also see) praveshamchaiva = the entry; rāmasya = of Rama; (into Ayodhya); paurajānapadaiḥ saha = along with the people of that City as well as the inhabitants of the country-side; vibhūti chaiva = as also the affluence; dasharathasya strīṇām = of the widows of Dasaratha.

"We shall also see the entry of Rama into Ayodhya, along with the people of that city as well as the inhabitants of the country-side, as also the affluence of the widows of Dasaratha."

Sloka 36

tārayā cābhyanujñātāḥ sarvā vānarayoṣitaḥ |
nepathyavidhipūrvaṃ tu kṛtvā cāpi pradakṣiṇam || 6-123-36
adhyārohanvimānaṃ tatsītādarśanakāṅkṣayā |

36. abhyanujJṇātāḥ = duly permitted; tārayācha = by ṭara; sarvāḥ = all; vānarayoṣhitaḥ = the wives of the monkeys; nepathya vidbhipūrvakam = wearing their costumes as per rule; kṛitvā pradakṣhiṇam = and going round clockwise; tat = that; vimānam aerial car; adhyāroham = ascended (it); sītādarshana kāṇ^kṣhayā = with an intent to see ṣeetha.

Duly permitted by Tara, all the wives of the monkeys, wearing their costumes as per rule and going round the aerial car clockwise, ascended it with an intent to see Seetha.

Sloka 37

tābhiḥ sahotthitaṃ śīghraṃ vimānaṃ prekṣya rāghavaḥ || 6-123-37
ṛṣyamūkasamīpe tu vaidehīṃ punarabravīt |

37. prekṣhya = seeing; vimānam = the aerial car; utthitam = having risen; shīghram = quickly; tābhiḥ saha = together with the wives of monkeys; rāghavaḥ = Rama; punaḥ = again; abravīt = spoke; vaidehīm = to ṣeetha; Riṣhyamūka sapīpe = at the proximity of ṃount Rishyamuka.

Seeing the aerial car having risen quickly, after having taken the wives of monkeys too, Rama again spoke to Seetha at the vicinity of ṃount Rishyamuka (as follows):

Sloka 38

dṛśyateasau mahānsīte savidyudiva toyadaḥ || 6-123-38
ṛśyamūko giriśreṣṭhaḥ kāñcanairdhātubhirvṛtaḥ |

38. sīte = O ṣeetha! asau dṛishyate = here is seen; ṛiṣhyamūkaḥ = Rishyamuka; girivaraḥ = the excellent mountain; vṛitaḥ = endowed; kāṇchanaiḥ dhātubhiḥ = with layers of gold savidyuttoadaḥ iva = and looking like a cloud with lightning.

"O Seetha! Here is seen Rishyamuka, the excellent mountain, endowed with layers of gold and looking like a cloud with lightning."

Sloka 39

atrāhan vānarendreṇa sugrīveṇa samāgataḥ || 6-123-39
samayaśca kṛtaḥ sīte vadhārthan vālino mayā |

39. sīte = O ṣeetha!; aham = I; samāgataḥ = met; sugrīveṇa = with ṣugreeva; vānarendreṇa = the king of monkeys; atra = here; samayashcha = an agreement too; vadhārtham = for the killing; vālinaḥ = of Vali; kṛitaḥ = was made; mayā = by me.

"O Seetha! Here did I meet Sugreeva, the king of monkeys and an agreement too was made by me for the killing of Vali."

Sloka 40

eṣā sā dṛśyate pampā nalinī citrakānanā || 6-123-40
tvayā vihīno yatrāhan vilalāpa suduḥkhitaḥ |

40. eṣhā dṛishyate = here is seen; pampā = Pampa-river; nalinī = abounding in lotuses; chitrakānanā = and edged with colourful groves; yatra = where; aham = I; vilalāpa = lamented; suduḥkhitaḥ = with great pain; tvayā vihīnaḥ = having been separated from you.

"Here is seen Pampa-river, abounding in lotuses and edged with colourful groves, where I lamented with great pain, having been separated from you."

Sloka 41

asyāstīre mayā dṛṣṭā śabarī dharmacāriṇī || 6-123-41
atra yojanabāhuśca kabandho nihato mayā |

41. dharmachāriṇī = the virtuous; shabarī = ṣhabari; dṛiṣhṭā = was seen; mayā = by me; asyāḥ tīre = at the bank of this river; kabandhaḥ = Kabandha; yojana bāhuḥ = having arms; a Yojana (eight miles) long; nihataḥ = was killed; mayā = by me; atra = here.

"The virtuous Shabari was seen by me at the bank of this river. Here was killed, Kabandha, having arms, a Yojana (eight miles) long."

Sloka 42

dṛśyateasau janasthāne sīte śrīmānvanaspatiḥ || 6-123-42
yatra yuddhaṃ mahadvṛttan tavahetorvilāsini |
rāvaṇena hato yatra pakṣiṇāṃ pravaro balī || 6-123-43

42-43. sīte = O ṣeetha!; asau dṛishyate = here is seen; shrīmān vanaspatiḥ = the illustrious tree; janasthāne = in the forest of Janasthana; yatra = here itself; jaṭāyushcha = Jatayu; mahātejāḥ = the highly brilliant; balī = mighty; pravarah pakṣhīṇām = and the foremost among the birds; nihataḥ = was killed; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; tava hetoḥ = for your sake; vilāsini = O sporting lady!

"And over there is seen in the forest of Janasthana, O Seetha, that illustrious tree under which the highly brilliant and mighty Jatayu, the foremost among the birds, was killed by Ravana, for your sake, O sporting lady!."

Sloka 44

kharaśca nihataśsaṅkhye dūṣaṇaśca nipātitaḥ |
triśirāśca mahāvīryo mayā bāṇairajihmagaiḥ || 6-123-44
parṇaśālā tathā citrā dṛśyate śubhadarśanā |
yatra tvaṃ rākṣasendreṇa rāvaṇena hṛtā balāt || 6-123-45

44-45. varavarṇini = O lady of beautiful complexion!; etat yatra = here is where; kharashcha = Khara; nihataḥ = was killed mayā = by me; ajihmagaiḥ sharaiḥ = with straight going arrows; duṣhaṇashcha = Dushana; npātitaḥ = was struck down; mahāvīryaḥ = and the mighty; trishirashcha = ṭrishiras (was also killed); tat = over there; asmākam = is our; āshramapadam = place of hermitage; tathā = and shubhadarshane = O charming lady!; dṛishyate = here is seen; parṇashālā = charming lady!; dṛishyate = here is seen; parashālā = the leaf-hut; yatra = where; tvam = you; hṛitā = were taken away; rākṣhasendreṇa = the king of demons.

"O lady of beautiful complexion! Here is the region, (known as Panchavati, so called because it consists of five banyan trees) where Khara the demon was killed by me with straight-going arrows, as also his brothers, Dushana and the mighty Trishiras. And over there is seen our enchanting leaf-hut, where you were forcibly taken away by Ravana the king of demons, O charming lady!"

Sloka 46

eṣā godāvarī ramyā prasannasalilā śivā || 6-123-46
agastyasyāśramo hyeṣa dṛśyate kadalīvṛtaḥ |

46. eṣha = here is seen; shubhā = the auspicious; ramyā = and charming; godāvarī = (river named) Godavari; prasanna salilā = containing clear waters; dṛishyate = here is also seen; āshramashchaiva = the hermitage of Agastya; kadalī vṛitah = surrounded with banana-groves."

"Here is the shining hermitage of Sutikshna, the great-souled sage. And over there is seen the great hermitage of Sharabhanga, where the thousand-eyed Indra, the destroyer of Citadels, came."

Sloka 47

dīptaścaivāśramo hyeṣa sutīkṣṇasya mahātmanaḥ || 6-123-47
dṛśyate caiva śarabhaṅgāśramo mahān |
upayātaḥ sahasrākṣo yatra śakraḥ purandaraḥ || 6-123-48

47-48. eṣhaḥ dīptaḥ āshramaḥ = here is the shining hermitage; sutīkṣhṇasya = of ṣutikshana; mahātmanaḥ = the great-souled sage; dṛishyate = (and over there) is seen; mahān = the great; sharabhaṇ^gashramaḥ = hermitage of ṣharabhanga; yatra = where shakra = Indra; sahasrākṣhaḥ = the thousand-eyed; puramdaraḥ = the destroyer of citadels; upāyātaḥ = came.

"Here is the shining hermitage of Sutikshna, the great-souled sage. And over there is seen the great hermitage of Sharabhanga, where the thousand-eed Indra, the destroyer of Citadels, came."

Sloka 49

ete te tāpasāvāsā dṛśyante tanumadhyame |
atriḥ kulapatiryatra sūryavaiśvānaraprabhaḥ || 6-123-49

49. devi tanumadhyame = O slender-waisted lady!; ete dṛishyante = here are seen; te = those; tāpasāḥ = sages; yatra = where; atriḥ = Atri; kulapatiḥ = the head of the hermitage; sūryavaishvānaropamaḥ = was equal in splendour to the sun and the fire.

"O slender-waisted lady! Here are seen those sages, where, Atri the head of the hermitage, was equal in splendour to the sun and the fire."

Sloka 50

asmindeśe mahākāyo virādho nihato mayā |
atra sīte tvayā dṛṣṭā tāpasī dharmacāriṇī || 6-123-50

50. asmin deshe = at this place; mahākāyaḥ = the gigantic; virādhaḥ = Viradha; nihataḥ = was killed; mayā = by me; sīte = O ṣeetha!; atra = here; dṛiṣhṭā = was seen; dharma chariṇī = the virtuous; tāpasī = Anasuya; the ascetic lady (the wife of sage Atri).

"At this place, the gigantic Viradha was killed by me. O Seetha! Here was seen by you, the virtuous Anasuya, the ascetic lady (the wife of sage, Atri)."

Sloka 51

asau sutanuśailendraścitrakūṭaḥ prakāśate |
yatra mān kaikayīputraḥ prasādayitumāgataḥ || 6-123-51

51. sutanu = O ṣeetha; having a beautiful body! asau chitrakūṭaḥ = here is chitrakuta; shailendraḥ = the excellent mountain; prakāshate = shining; kaikeyīputraḥ = Bhrarata; āgataḥ = came; atra = here; mām prasādayitum = to seek my favour (in the form of my consent to return to Ayodhya).

"O Seetha, having a beautiful body! Here is chitrakuta, the excellent mountain, shining. Bharata came here to seek my favour (in the form of my consent to return to Ayodhya)."

Sloka 52

eṣā sā yamunā dūrāddṛśyate citrakānanā |
bharadvājāśramo śrīmāneṣa dṛśyate caiṣa maithili || 6-123-52

52. maithilī = O ṣeetha!; eṣhā = here is dṛishyate = seen; sā ramyā yamunā = the beautiful river of Yamuna; chitra kānanā = surrounded by colourful groves; eṣhah dṛishyate = here is seen; shrīmān = the illustrious; bharadvājāshramaḥ = hermitage of sage Bharadwaja.

"O Seetha! Here is seen the beautiful river of Yamuna, surrounded by colourful groves. Here is seen the illustrious hermitage of Sage Bharadwaja."

Sloka 53

iyaṃ ca dṛśyate gaṅgā puṇyā tripathagā nadī |
nānādvijagaṇākīrṇā saṃprapuṣpitakānanā || 6-123-53

53. iyam dṛishyate = here is seen; puṇyā = the holy; gaṇ^gānadī = river Ganga; tripathagā = which wends its way through the three worlds (viz. heaven; the earth and the subterranean world); nānādvijayaṇākīrṇā = whose banks are crowded with flocks of birds; samprapuṣhpita pādapā = and which is line diwth trees in full flowering.

"Here is seen the holy River Ganga, which wends its way through the three worlds (viz. heaven, the earth and the subterranean world), whose banks are crowded with flocks of birds and which is lined with trees in full-flowering."

Sloka 54

śṛṅgaverapuran caitadguho yatra samāgataḥ |
eṣā tripathagā gaṅgā dṛśyate varavarṇini || 6-123-54
nānātaruśatākīrṇā saṃprapuṣpitakānanā |

54. etat = here is; shṛiṇ^ga bherapura cha = the town of ṣhringabheripura; yatra = where; guha = Guha; mamasakhā = my friend; (stays); eṣhā = here is; dṛishyate = seen; sā = that; sarayūḥ = rows of sacrificial posts (the relies of sacrifices performed from time to time by Ikshvaku dynasty); nānātarushatākīrṇā = bordered with hundreds of trees; samprapuṣhpita kānanā = and with groves in full-blossom.

"Here is the town of Shringaberapura, where Guha my friend stays. Here is seen that river, Sarayu, lined with rows of sacrificial posts (the relics of sacrifices performed from time to time by Ikshvaku dynasty), bordered with hundreds of trees and with groves in full blossom."

Sloka 55

eṣā sā dṛśyateayodhyā rājadhānī piturmama || 6-123-55
ayodhyān kuru vaidehi praṇāmaṃ punarāgatā |

55. sīte = O ṣeetha!; eṣhā dṛishyate = here is seen; sā rājadhānī = that Capital-City'; mama pituḥ = of my father; vaidehi = O the princess of Videha territory!; punaḥ āgatāḥ = having duly returned; kuru praṇāmam = offer your salutation; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya.

"O Seetha! Here is seen that Capital City of my father. O the princess of Videha territory! Having duly returned, offer your salutation to Ayodhya."

Sloka 56

tataste vānarāḥ sarve rākṣasaśca vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-123-56
utpatyotpatya dadṛśustāṃ purīn śubhadarśanām |

56. tataḥ = thereupon; sarve te vānarāḥ = all those monkeys; samhṛiṣhṭāḥ rākṣhasāḥ = and the rejoiced demons; savibhīṣhaṇāḥ = along with Vibhishana; tadā = at that time; utpatyotpatya = bounding again and again (to have a glimpse of it); dadṛishuḥ = saw; tām purīm = that City.

Thereupon, all those monkeys and the rejoiced demons along with Vibhishana, at that time, bounding again and again (to have a glimpse of it) saw that City.

Sloka 57

tatastu tāṃ pāṇḍuraharmyamālinīṃ |
viśālakakṣyān gajavājisaṅkulām |
purīmayodhyān dadṛśuḥ plavaṅgamāḥ |
purīṃ mahendrasya yathāmarāvatīm || 6-123-57

57. tataḥ = thereupon; plavaṇ^gamāḥ = the monkeys; sarākṣhasāḥ = along with the demons; apashyan = beheld; tām purīm = that Ayodhya; pāṇ^Du harmya mālinīm = having rows of white palces; vishāla kakṣhyām = intersected with wide roads; vṛitām gajavājabhiḥ = crowded with elephants and horses; amarāvatīm yathā = looking like Amaravathi; purīm = the City; mahendrasya = of Indra the lord of celestials.

Thereupon, the monkeys along with the demons beheld that Ayodhya, having rows of white palaces, intersected with wide roads, and crowded with elephants and horses, looking like Amaravathi, the City of Indra the lord of Celestials.
