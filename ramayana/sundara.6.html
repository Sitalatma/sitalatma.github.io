Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 6
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hanuma moves about the city of Lanka, going from one house to another in search of mother Seetha. He finds amazing wealth and riches abounding in that city of Ravana. He finally enters the huge house of Ravana.


Sloka 1

sa nikāmam vimāneṣu viṣaṇṇaḥ kāma rūpadhṛt |
vicacāra punarlaṅkām lāghavena samanvitaḥ || 5-6-1

1. saḥ = that ḥanuma; kāmarūpadṛit = who could assume any desire form; vimāneshhu = (searching) among houses; nikāmamvishhaṇṇaḥ = had a lot of grief; lāghavenasamanvitaḥ = endowed with great speed; vichachāra = roamed; punaḥ = again; laṇ^kām = the city of Lanka.

That Hanuma who could assume any desire form, searching among houses had a lot of grief. Endowed with great speed, He roamed again the city of Lanka.

Sloka 2

āsasāda atha lakṣmīvān rākṣasa indra niveśanam |
prākāreṇa arka varṇena bhāsvareṇa abhisamvRtam || 5-6-2

2. atha = after that; lakshmivān = the glorious ḥanuma; asasāda = neared; rākshasendranīveshanam = the house of Ravana; bhāsvareṇa = shining; arkavarṇena = with the hue of ṣun; abhisamvṛitam = surrounded; prākāreṇa = by a compound wall.

After that, the glorious Hanuma neared the house of Ravana shining with the golden hue of Sun, surrounded by a compound wall.

Sloka 3

rakṣitam rākṣasair bhīmaiḥ simhair iva mahad vanam |
samīkṣamāṇo bhavanam cakāśe kapi kunjaraḥ || 5-6-3

3. kapikunJṇjaraḥ = ḥanuma; chakāshe = shined; samīkshamāṇaḥ = purveying; bhavanam = the building; rakshitām = protected; bhīmaiḥ rākshasaihi = by horrible rakshasas; mahāvanamiva = like a forest; simhaiḥ = by lions.

Hanuma shined purveying the building protected by horrible rakshasas, like a forest by lions.

Sloka 4

rūpya kopa hitaiḥ citraiḥ toraṇair hema bhūṣitaiḥ |
vicitrābhiḥ ca kakṣyābhir dvāraiḥ ca rucirair vRtam || 5-6-4

4. vṛitam = (Ravana's house)consisted of; toraṇaiḥ = archways; rūpyakopahitaiḥ = decorated with sliver; chitraiḥ = surprising one's; hemabhhūshhitaiḥ = decorated with gold; vichitrābhi = strange; kakshyābhiḥ = entrances; ruchiraiḥ dvāraishcha = and beautiful doors.

Ravana's house consisted of surprising archways decorated with sliver and gold, strange entrances and beautiful doors.

Sloka 5

gaja āsthitair mahā mātraiḥ śūraiḥ ca vigata śramaiḥ |
upasthitam asamhāryair hayaiḥ syandana yāyibhiḥ || 5-6-5

5. upasthitam = (Ravana's house)waited upon; gajāsthitaiḥ = by men on elephants; shūraiḥ = warriors; vigatasramaiḥ = people with removed tiredness; mahāmātraiḥ = drivers of elephants; asamhāraiḥ hayaiḥ = by undefeatable horses; syandanayāyibhiḥ = by charioteers.

Ravana's house waited upon by men on elephants, warriors, people with removed tiredness, drivers of elephants, by undefeatable horses, and by charioteers.

Sloka 6

simha vyāghra tanu trāṇair dānta kāncana rājataiḥ |
ghoṣavadbhir vicitraiḥ ca sadā vicaritam rathaiḥ || 5-6-6

6. (ḥanuma saw Ravana's house) simhavyāghrātanutrāṇaiḥ = with protective shields made of lion and tiger skins; dāntakāJṇchanarājataiḥ = decorated with ivory; gold and silver; vicharitam = being roamed; sadā = always; vichiraiḥradhaiḥ = by strange chariots; ghoshhavadbhiḥ = with great sound.

Hanuma saw Ravana's house with protective shields made of lion and tiger skins, decorated with ivory, gold and silver, being roamed always by strange chariots with great sound.

Sloka 7

bahu ratna samākīrṇam para ardhya āsana bhājanam |
mahā ratha samāvāsam mahā ratha mahā āsanam || 5-6-7

7. (ḥanuma saw Ravana's house) bahuratnasamākīrṇam = filled with a lot of diamonds; parārdhyāsanabhājanam = having valuable seats and utensils; mahārathasamāvāsam = abode of great warriors; mahārathamahāsanam = with great chariots and great utensils.

Hanuma saw Ravana's house filled with a lot of diamonds, having valuable seats and utensils, abode of great warriors, with great chariots and great utensils.

Sloka 8

dṛśyaiḥ ca parama udāraiḥ taiḥ taiḥ ca mṛga pakṣibhiḥ |
vividhair bahu sāhasraiḥ paripūrṇam samantataḥ || 5-6-8

8. paripūrṇam = filled all around with; taiḥ taiḥ = those and those; vividhaiḥ = various kinds of; mṛigapakshibhiḥ = animals and birds; bahusāhasraiḥ = in those thousands; dṛishyaiḥ = lovely to watch; paramodāraiḥ = very beautiful ones.

Filled all around with those and those various kinds of animals and birds in thousands, lovely to watch - very beautiful ones.

Sloka 9

vinītair anta pālaiḥ ca rakṣobhiḥ ca surakṣitam |
mukhyābhiḥ ca vara strībhiḥ paripūrṇam samantataḥ || 5-6-9

9. surakshitam = well protected; rakshobhi = by rakshasas; vinītaiḥ = well trained one's; antapālaiḥ = protecting the inner area; paripūrṇam = filled; samantataḥ = all over; varastrībhiḥ = by best women; mukhyābhiḥ = who were important.

Well protected by well trained rakshasas, protecting the inner area, filled all over by best women who were important.

Sloka 10

mudita pramadā ratnam rākṣasa indra niveśanam |
vara ābharaṇa nirhrādaiḥ samudra svana nihsvanam || 5-6-10

10. muditapramādāratnam = having happy women; rākshasendraniveshanam = the abode of Ravana; varābharaṇasamhrādaiḥ = with the tinkling of best jewellery; samudrasvananisvanam = had a sound like that of an ocean.

Having happy women, the abode of Ravana with the tinkling of best jewellery had a sound like that of an ocean.

Sloka 11

tad rāja guṇa sampannam mukhyaiḥ ca vara candanaiḥ |
bherī mRdanga abhirutam śankha ghoṣa vināditam || 5-6-11

11. rājaguṇasampannam = together with royal insignia; mukhaiḥ agaruchandanaiḥ = with best scents and sandal woods; samākīrṇam = spread with; mahājanaiḥ = great number of people; mahatvanamiva = like a great forest; simhaiḥ = with lions.

Together with royal insignia, with best scents and sandal wood, spread with great number of people like a great forest with lions.

Sloka 12

bherīmṛdaṅgābhirutam śaṅkhaghoṣanināditam |
nitya arcitam parva hutam pūjitam rākṣasaiḥ sadā || 5-6-12

12. bherīmṛidaṇ^gabhirutam = resounded by bheris and mrindagas; shankhaghoshhitanināditam = resounded by the sound of conch-shells; nityārchitam = prayed upon daily; parvahutam = with rituals on festivals; sadā = always; pūjitam = worshipped; rakshaiḥ = by rakshasas.

Resounded by bheris and mridagas, resounded by the sound of conch-shells, prayed upon daily by Rakshsas, with rituals on festivals, always worshipped by rakshasas.

Sloka 13

samudram iva gambhīram samudram iva nihsvanam |
mahātmāno mahad veśma mahā ratna pariccadam || 5-6-13

13. samahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; mahātmanaḥ = the wealthy Ravana's; mahatveshma = big house; ghambhīram = inscrutable; samudramiva = like an ocean; nissvanam samudramiva = like a noiseless ocean; mahāratna parichchhadam = with a roof embedded with great diamonds; mahāratnasamākīrṇam = filled with great diamonds.

The great Hanuma saw the wealthy Ravana's huge house, inscrutable like an ocean, like a noiseless ocean, with a roof embedded with great diamondsand filled with great diamonds.

Sloka 14

virājamānam vapuṣā gaja aśva ratha samkulam || 5-6-14
lankā ābharaṇam iti eva so amanyata mahā kapiḥ |
cacāra hanumāṃstatra rāvaṇasya samīpataḥ || 5-6-15

14;15. saḥ = that great ḥanuma; amanyata = thought; virājamānam = (the house which was) shining; vipushhā = with its form; gajāshvarathasankulam = filled with elephants; horses; chariots; laṇ^kābharaṇam ityeva = to be the jewel of Lanka; hanumān = ḥanuma; chachāra = walked about; tatra = there; samīpataḥ = near hood; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana;

That great Hanuma thought the house, which was shining with its form filled with elephants, horses, chariots, to be the jewel of Lanka. Hanuma walked about there in the near-hood of Ravana.

Sloka 16

vīkṣamāṇo hi asamtrastaḥ prāsādāmḥ ca cacāra saḥ || 5-6-16

16. saḥ = ṭhat ḥanuma; chchāra = moved about; gṛihāt gṛiham = from one house to another; rakshsānām = of rakshasas; vudyānānicha = and also parks; vīkshamāṇaḥ api = and observing; sarvashaḥ = in all directions; asantrastaḥ = without fear; prāsādāmshcha = and also courtyards.

That Hanuma moved about from one house to another of Rakshasas and also parks and observing in all directions without fear and also courtyards.

Sloka 17

avaplutya mahā vegaḥ prahastasya niveśanam |

17. mahāvīryaḥ = ḥanuma with great prowess; mahāvegaḥ = and one with great speed; avaplutya = jumping; niveshanam = for the house; prahastasya = of Prahasta; tataḥ = and from there; plupluve = leapt; anyatveshma = for another house; mahāpārshvasya = that of ṃahaparshva.

Hanuma with great prowess, and one with great speed, jumped for the house of Prahasta and from there leapt for another house that of ṃahaparshva.

Sloka 18

atha megha pratīkāśam kumbha karṇa niveśanam |

18. atha = thereafter; mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; plupluve = leapt; kumbhakarṇaniveshanam = for the house of Kumbhakarna; meghapratīkāsham = which resembled a cloud; tathā = and in the same way; vibhīshhanasya = for the house of Vibhishana.

Thereafter the great Hanuma leapt for the house of Kumbhakarna which resembled a cloud and in the same way for the house of Vibhishana.

Sloka 19

mahā udarasya ca tathā virūpa akṣasya caiva hi |

19. saḥ mahākapiḥ = ṭhat great ḥanuma; tathā = in the same way; plupluve = leapt; bhavanam = for the house; mahodarashya = of ṃahodara; virūpākshasya caivaḥ = and also that of Virupāksha; viddyujjihvasya = that of Viddutjihva; tathaivacha = and in the same fashion; vidyunmāleḥ = that of Vidhunmāli; tathaiva = and in the same way; vjradamshhṭrasya = that of Vajradamshhtra.

That great Hanuma in the same way leapt for the house of ṃahodara and also that of Virupāksha, that of Viddutjihva.

Sloka 20

20. hariyūdhapaḥ = ḥanuma the commander of Vanara army; mahātejāḥ = with great radiance; jagāma = went; shukasya = for ṣuka's house; dhīmataḥ shukasya = for the intellectual ṣuka's house; sāraṇasya = for ṣarana's house; tathā = in the same way; gṛiham = for the house; indrajitaḥ = of Indrajit.

Hanuma the commander of Vanara army with great radiance went for the intellectual Suka's house, for Sarana's house, and in the same way for the house of Indrajit.

Sloka 21

jambu māleḥ sumāleḥ ca jagāma hari yūthapaḥ || 5-6-21

21;22. harisattamaḥ = the best among Vanaras ḥanuma; jagāma = went; jambumāleḥ = for the house of Jambumali; sumāleshcha = and for the house of ṣumali; saḥ mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; pupluve = jumped; rashmiketoḥ = for the house of Rasmiketu; tathaivacha = and in the same way; suryaketoḥ = for the house of ṣuryaketu; tathā = and in that way; bhavanam = for the building; vajrakāyasya = of Vajrakāya.

The best among Vanaras Hanuma went for the house of Jambumali and for the house of Sumali. The great Hanuma jumped for the house Rasmiketu and in the same way for the house of Suryaketu and in that way for the building of Vajrakāya.

Sloka 23

Hanuma jumped for Dhumrah's house, for Sampati's house, for Vidhudrupa's, Bhīma's house, for Ghana's house and Vighana's house, for Sukhana's house, for Vakra's, for Satha's house and also for Vikata's house, for Brahmakarna's house, for Damshra's house, for Roma's house, for Raksha's house, for Ydhonmata's and Indrajihva's house, in the same way for Hastimukas house, for Karala's house, for Pisacha's house, and also for the building of Shonita.

Sloka 26

The son of Vayu, one with great fame, great one among Vanaras , moving about in a sequence in those and those best buildings, saw the wealth of those rakshasas, who were rich.

Sloka 28

The glorious Hanuma passing everyone's houses all round, there-after neared the house of Ravana.

Sloka 29

Hanuma best among Vanaras, tiger among Vanaras, moving about saw Rakshas women, those who were sleeping near to Ravana, those who had horrific eyes, those who had Sulās, ṃudgaras in their hands, those who had Shaktis and Tomaras.

Sloka 30

Hanuma saw in that house of Ravana a variety of army divisions and also Rakshasas those who had huge bodies having different weapons raise up.

Sloka 31

Sloka 34

34;35. tatra = there; dadarsha = (ḥanuma) saw; niveshane = in the house; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rakshasendrasya = the king of rakshasas; vāhinīḥ = army divisions; sahasram = in thousands; jābūnadaparishhkṛitāḥ = decorated with gold; hemajālaparichchhannaḥ = covered with heaps of gold; taruṇādityasannibhāḥ = equalling the sun who has fully come up.

There Hanuma saw in the house of Ravana, the king of rakshasas, army divisions in thousands decorated with gold, covered with heaps of gold, equalling the Sun who has fully come up.

Sloka 36

36;37. saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of Vayu; dadarsha = saw; niveshane = in the house; ravanasya = of Ravana; rakshasendrasya = the king of Rakshasas; shibikāḥ = palanquins; vividhākārāḥ = of various shapes; chitrāṇilathāgṛihāṇi = wonderful bowers; chitrāshālāgṛihāṇi = art galleries; anyāni = and other; krīDāgṛihāṇi = pleasure houses; dāruparvatakānapi = (which were constructed) with wooden mountains; kāmasyagṛihakam = house for sexual delights; ramyam divāgṛikamevacha = and a beautiful diurnal house.

That Hanuma the son of Vayu saw in the house of Ravana the king of rakshasas, palanquins of various shapes, wonderful bowers, art galleries and other pleasure houses which were constructed with wooden mountains, house for sexual delight and a beautiful diurnal house.

Sloka 38

38;39. saḥ = ṭhat ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; bhānuvantam = the best among building; mandaragiriprakhyam = equalling mount ṃandara; mayūrasthānasamkulam = filled with pens for peacocks; ākīrṇam = spread; dhvajayashhṭibhiḥ = by flag staffs; dhīranishhṭhatakarmāntam = managed by courageous one's; bhūtapateḥgṛihamiva = like the house of Kubera; anekaratnasamkīrṇam = filled with many diamonds; nidhijālam = and also heaps of riches.

That Hanuma saw the best among buildings equalling mount ṃandara filled with pens for peacocks, spread by flag staffs managed by courageous ones like the house of Kubera filled with many diamonds and also heaps of riches.

Sloka 40

40. tat = ṭhat; veshma = building; virarāja = shone; archirbhishchāpi = by the rays; ratnānām = of diamonds; tejasācha = by the splendour; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rashmimāniva = like the ṣun; rashmibhiḥ = by rays.

That building shone by the rays of diamonds, by the splendour of Ravana like the Sun by rays.

Sloka 41

jāmbū nadamayāni eva śayanāni āsanāni ca || 5-6-41
bhājanāni ca śubhrāṇi dadarśa hari yūthapaḥ |

41. hariyūdhapaḥ = leader of Vanaras; ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; shayanāni = couches; āsanānicha = and seats;mukhyāni = and chief vessels; jāmbūnadamayānyeva = all made with gold.

Hanuma leader of Vanaras saw couches and seats and chief vessels all made with gold.

Sloka 42

madhv āsava kṛta kledam maṇi bhājana samkulam || 5-6-42
mano ramam asambādham kubera bhavanam yathā |
nūpurāṇām ca ghoṣeṇa kāncīnām ninadena ca || 5-6-43
mṛdanga tala ghoṣaiḥ ca ghoṣavadbhir vināditam |
prāsāda samghāta yutam strī ratna śata samkulam || 5-6-42
suvyūḍha kakṣyam hanumān praviveśa mahā gṛham |

42;43;44. hanumān = ḥanuma; pravivesha = entered; mahāgṛiham = the big house; madhvāsavakṛitakledam = moistened by liquor made of honey; maṇibhājanashankulam = filled with vessels made of gems; manoramam = delightful one; asambādham = un-congested one; kuberabhavanamyathā = like the building of Kubera; nināditam = resounded; nūpurāṇāmghoshheṇa = by the sound of tinkles; kāJṇchīnām ninadena = by the sound waist ornaments; mṛidaṇ^gatalaghoshhaīshcha = by the sound of percussion on ṃrudangas; ghoshhvidbhiḥ = with deep sound; prāsādasamgātayutam = which consisted of many mansions; strīratnashetasamkulam = filled with hundreds of best women; suvyūDhakakshyam = encircled by many spacious enclosures.

Hanuma entered the big house moistened by liquor made of honey, filled with vessels made of gems delightful one, un-congested one like the building of Kubera resounded by the sound of tinkles by the sound of waist ornaments, by the sound of percussion on ṃrudangas with deep sound, which consisted of many mansions filled with hundreds of best women, encircled by many spacious enclosures.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye sundara kāṇḍe ṣaṣṭhaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 6th chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
