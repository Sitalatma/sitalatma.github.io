Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 5
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama recollects the lotus-eyed Seetha and laments much about his separation from her Lakshmana consoles him and there approaches the sun-set.

Sloka 1

sā tu nīlena vidhivat svārakṣā susamāhitā |
sāgarasya uttare tīre sādhu senā vinieśitā || 6-5-1

1. sā = that army; sā = he famous one; vidhivat = duly; svārakṣā = well-protected; nīlena = by ṇeela; viniveshitā = was kept stationed; sādhu = well; uttare tīre = at the northern shore; sāgarasya = of the ocean.

That famous army, well-protected by Neela, was kept stationed nicely at the northern shore of the ocean.

Sloka 2

maindaḥ ca dvividhaḥ ca ubhau tatra vānara pumgavau |
viceratuḥ ca tām senām rakṣā artham sarvato diśam || 6-5-2

2. ubhau = both; maindaḥ ca = ṃaina; dvividashcaiva = and Divida; vānara pumgavau = the distinguished; tatra = there monkeys; viceratuḥ = moved; sarvato disham = in all directions; tām senām = in that army; rakṣā artham = for the purpose of vigilance.

Both ṃaina and Dvivida the distinguished monkeys there moved in all directions through that army, for the purpose of vigilance.

Sloka 3

niviṣṭāyām tu senāyām tīre nada nadī pateḥ |
pārśvastham lakṣmaṇam dṛṣṭvā rāmo vacanam abravīt || 6-5-3

3. senāyām = (after) the army; niviṣṭāyām = was settled; tīre = at the shore; nada nadī pate = of the sea; rāmaḥ = Rama; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = these words; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; lakshmaṇam = Lakshmana; pārshvastham = at his side.

After the army was settled at the sea-shore, Rama spoke to Lakshmana who was by his side (as follows):

Sloka 4

śokaḥ ca kila kālena gaccatā hi apagaccati |
mama ca apaśyataḥ kāntām ahani ahani vardhate || 6-5-4

4. kila = It is so said; shokaḥ = (that) sorrow; apagachchhati = gets vanished; gachchhatāa = at the passing; kālena = of time; mama cha = but my agony; apashyataḥ = of not seeing kāntām = my beloved; vardhate = is getting increased; ahanyahani = day n and day out.

"It is so said that sorrow gets vanished at the passing of time. But my agony of not seeing my beloved is getting increased every day."

Sloka 5

na me duhkham priyā dūre na me duhkham hṛtā iti ca |
tad eva anuśocāmi vayo asyā hi ativartate || 6-5-5

5. na = no; duḥkham = anguish; me = to me;; priyā = (that) my beloved; dūre = is at a distance; na = ṇo; duḥkham = anguish; me = to me; hṛiteti cha = tat she was taken away; asyāḥ = ḥer; vayaḥ = age; ativartate hi = is indeed passing away; amshochāmi = I am repenting; eetadeva = only about this.

"There is no anguish for me that my beloved is at a distance, nor that she was taken away. Her age is indeed passing away. Only about this, I am repenting."

Sloka 6

vāhi vāta yataḥ kanyā tām spṛṣṭvā mām api spṛśa |
tvayi me gātra saṃsparśaḥ candre dṛṣṭi samāgamaḥ || 6-5-6

6. vāta = Oh; wind!; vāhi = flow; yataḥ = from which side; kāntā = is my beloved; spṛiṣṭvā = touch; tām = her; spṛisha = (and) touch; mām api = me too; gātra sparshaḥ = contact of her limb; me = to me; tvayi = is obtained through you; dṛiṣṭisamāgamaḥ = contact of our eyes; chandre = is through moon."

"Oh, wind! Flow from the side of my beloved. Touch her and touch me too. It is through you that I get a contact of her limbs. It is through moon that I get a contact of her eyes."

Sloka 7

tan me dahati gātrāṇi viṣam pītam iva āśaye |
hā nātha iti priyā sā mām hriyamāṇā yad abravīt || 6-5-7

7. sā priyā = that darling; hriyamāṇā = being carried away; abravīt iti yat = might have cried; hā nāthā iti = Oh; Lord (seeking for help); tat = It; viṣam iva = is like poison; pītam = gulped (by me); āshaye = and hovering in my stomach; dahati = scorching; me = my; gātrāṇi = limbs.

"That darling being carried away; might have cried "Oh, Lord!" Seeking for help. That thought is like poison gulped by me, hovering in my stomach and scorching my limbs."

Sloka 8

tad viyoga indhanavatā tac cintā vipula arciṣā |
rātrim divam śarīram me dahyate madana agninā || 6-5-8

8. me = ṃy; sharīram = body; dahyate = is scorching; rātrim divam = night and day; madanāgninā = by the fire of passion; tadviyogendhana vatā = holding fuel of my separation from her; tacchintāvimalarchiṣā = and my thoughts of her flaring up into shimmering flames.

"ṃy body is scorching night and day, by the fire of passion, holding fuel of my separation from her and my thoughts of her flaring into shimmering flames."

Sloka 9

avagāhya arṇavam svapsye saumitre bhavatā vinā |
kathaṃcit prajvalan kāmaḥ samāsuptam jale dahet || 6-5-9

9. saumitre = Oḥ; Lakshmana!; avagāhya = going deep into; ārṇavam = the sea; bhavatā vina = without you; svapasye = I shall fall asleep; prajvalan = flaming; kāmaḥ = passion; na dahet = will not scorch; mām = me; evam cha = thus; suptam = sleeping; jale = in water.

"Oh, Lakshmana! Diving deep into the sea without you, I shall fall asleep. This flaming passion will not thus scorch me, lying down in water.

Sloka 10

bahv etat kāmayānasya śakyam etena jīvitum |
yad aham sā ca vāma ūrur ekām dharaṇim āśritau || 6-5-10

10. etat = this; bahu = is great thing; kāmayānasya = for me who is passionate; iti yat = that; aham = I; sā vāmorūḥ = and that ṣeetha; with charming thighs āshritau = are resting; ekām = on one and the same; dharaṇim = earth; shakyam = I am able; jīvitum = to survive; etena = on this fact.

"It is enough for me, who is passionate, that Seetha with charming thighs and myself are resting on one the same earth and on this fact I am able to survive."

Sloka 11

kedārasya iva kedāraḥ sa udakasya nirūdakaḥ |
upasnehena jīvāmi jīvantīm yat śṛṇomi tām || 6-5-11

11. yat = that; shruṇomi = I am hearing; tām = her; jīvantīm = as surviving; jīvāmi = I also survive; kedāraḥ iva = as a paddy-field; nirūdakaḥ = without water (survives); upasnehena = getting wet; kedārasya = by another paddy-field; sodakasya = under water.

"Even as a paddy-field without water survives, by getting wet from a neighboring paddy-field under water, I also survive since I am hearing her as surviving.

Sloka 12

kadā tu khalu susśoṇīm śata patra āyata īkṣaṇām |
vijitya śatrūn drakṣyāmi sītām sphītām iva śriyam || 6-5-12

12. kadā khalu = when; drakshhyāmi = shall I see; sītām = ṣeetha; shushroṇīm = with charming hips; shata patrāyatekshhaṇām = having long lotus-like eyes; sphītām = flourishing; shriyamiva = as prosperity; vijitya = by conquering; shatrūn = the enemies.

"When shall I behold Seetha with charming hips, having long lotus-like eyes flourishing as prosperity, by conquering the enemies?"

Sloka 13

kadā nu cāru bimba oṣṭham tasyāḥ padmam iva ānanam |
īṣad unnamya pāsyāmi rasāyanam iva āturaḥ || 6-5-13

13. kadā = when; unnamya = raising; īshhat = a little; tasyāḥ = her; ānanam = face; padmamiva = looking like louts; suchārudantoshhṭham = with its beautiful teeth and lips; pāsyāmi = shall I drink; rasāyanamiva = as a sovereign drink of remedy; āturaḥ = by a sick man?"

"When, gently raising her face looking like louts, with its beautiful teeth and lips, shall I drink as a sick man the sovereign drink of remedy?"

Sloka 14

tau tasyāḥ samhatau pīnau stanau tāla phala upamau |
kadā nu khalu sa utkampau hasantyā mām bhajiṣyataḥ || 6-5-14

14. khalu = when will; tau = those; stanau = breasts; hasantyāḥ = which are delightful; sahitau = close; pīnau = bulging; sotkampau = and quivering; tālaphalopamau = looking like Palmyra fruits; bhajishhyathaḥ nu = indeed press; mām = me?

"When will those breasts which are delightful close, bulging and quivering, looking like palmary fruits, indeed press me?"

Sloka 15

sā nūnam asita apāngī rakṣo madhya gatā satī |
man nāthā nātha hīnā iva trātāram na adhigaccati || 6-5-15

15. sā = she; asitāpāṇ^gī = with dark-cornered eyes; rakshhomadhyagatā satī = who has fallen in the middle of ogres; mannāthā = though I am her support; nūnam = is certainly; nādhigachchhati = not getting; trātāram = any defender; nātha hīnena = resembling an orphan.

"She, with dark-cornered eyes, who has fallen into the midst of demons, is surely not getting any defender, she resembling an orphan, though I am her support."

Sloka 16

kadā vikṣobhya rakṣāṃsi sā vidhūya utpatiṣyati |
rākṣasīmadhyagā śete snuṣā daśarathasya ca || 6-5-16

16. katham = how; janakarājasya duhitā = ṣeetha; the daughter of king Janaka; mama cha = my; priyā = darling; dasharathasya cha = and Dasaratha's; snushhā = daughter-in-law; shete = is sleeping; rākshhasīmadhyagā = in the midst of ogresses?

"How Seetha the daughter of King Janaka, my darling and Dasaratha's daughter-in-law is sleeping among ogresses?"

Sloka 17

avikṣobhyāṇi rakṣāṃsi sā vidhūyotpatiṣyati |
vidhūya jaladān nīlān śaśi lekhā śaratsv iva || 6-5-17

17. sā = ṣeetha; utpatishhyati = will come out; vidhūya = driving away (through my force) avikshhobhyāṇi = the unassailable; rakshhāmsi = demons; vidhūya = as driving away; nīlān = black; jaladān = clouds; shasilekhā = by a digit of the moon; sharat su = in autumn.

"Seetha will come out, driving away (through my force) the unassailable demons, as driving away black clouds by a digit of the moon, in autumn."

Sloka 18

svabhāva tanukā nūnam śokena anaśanena ca |
bhūyas tanutarā sītā deśa kāla viparyayāt || 6-5-18

18. sītā = ṣeetha; svabhāvatanukā = who; by nature is slender; nūnam = will undoubtedly; bhūyaḥ = further; tanutarā = become more lean; shokena = because of anguish; anashanena = abstinence from food; desha kālaviparyayāt = and adverse position of time and place.

"Seetha, who by nature is slender, will undoubtedly become leaner further because of her anguish, her abstinence from food and her adverse position of time and place."

Sloka 19

kadā nu rākṣasa indrasya nidhāya urasi sāyakān |
sītām pratyāhariṣyāmi śokam utsṛjya mānasam || 6-5-19

19. kadām = when; pratyaharishhyāmi = shall I bring back; sītām = ṣeetha; nidhāya = having lodged; sāyakān = arrows; rākshasendrasya = in Ravana's urasi = chest; utsṛijya = and having abandoned; mānasam = (this) mental; shokam = agony?

"When shall I bring back Seetha, having lodged arrows in Ravana's chest and having abandoned this mental agony?"

Sloka 20

kadā nu khalu mām sādhvī sītā amara sutā upamā |
sa utkaṇṭhā kaṇṭham ālambya mokṣyati ānandajam jalam || 6-5-20

20. kadā khalu = when indeed shall; sītā = ṣeetha; sādhvī = the virtuous woman; surasutopamā = similar to the child of a celestial; sotkaṇṭhā = with an excited longing; ālambya = embrace; me = my; kaṇṭham = neck; mokshhyati nu = (and) will release; jalam = tears; ānandajam = begotten of joy?

"When indeed Seetha the virtuous lady similar to the child of a celestial, with an excited longing, will embrace my neck and release tears of joy?"

Sloka 21

kadā śokam imam ghoram maithilī viprayogajam |
sahasā vipramokṣyāmi vāsaḥ śukla itaram yathā || 6-5-21

21. kadā = when; vipramokshhyāmi nu = shall I thrillingly abandon; imam = this; ghoram = terrible; shokam = anguish; maithilīviprayogajam = born out of my separation from; sahasā = so ṣeetha soon; shukletaram vāsam yathā = as (abandoning) a soiled clothing?

"When shall I thrillingly abandon this terrible anguish, born out of my separation from Seetha so soon, as we abandon a soiled clothing?"

Sloka 22

evam vilapatas tasya tatra rāmasya dhīmataḥ |
dina kṣayān manda vapur bhāskaro astam upāgamat || 6-5-22

22. tasya = (while) that; dhīmataḥ = sagacious; rāmasya = Rama; vilapataḥ = was lamenting; tatra = there; evam- = thus; bhāskaraḥ = sun; mandavapuḥ = sun; dinakshhayāt = due to day-decline; astam upāgataḥ = approached dusk.

While that sagacious Rama was lamenting thus there, the sun, the weak-rayed due to day-decline, approached the dusk.

Sloka 23

āśvāsito lakṣmaṇena rāmaḥ saṃdhyām upāsata |
smaran kamala patra akṣīm sītām śoka ākulī kṛtaḥ || 6-5-23

23. āshvāsitaḥ = consoled; lakshmaṇena = by Lakshmana; rāmaḥ = Rama; shokākulīkṛitaḥ = made overcome by anguish; smarau = recollecting; sītām = ṣeetha; kamelapatrākshhīm = the lotus-eyes; upāsataḥ = worshipped; sandhyām = the evening-twilight.

Lakshmana consoled Rama who was overwhelmed with anguish, duly recollecting the lotus-eyed Seetha. Thereafter, Rama worshipped the evening-twilight.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmayaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍepañcamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 5th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
