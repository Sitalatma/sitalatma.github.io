Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 7
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

ṃanthara feels very sad after hearing about anointment of Rama for the princely kingdom. She tells Kaikeyi about the coronation of Rama. Kaikeyi becomes delighted and gives ṃanthara one of her jewels.

Sloka 1

jñātidāsī yato jātā kaikeyyā tu sahoṣitā |
prāsādaṃ candrasaṅkāśamāruroha yadṛcchayā || 2-7-1

1. GYātidāsi = housemaid of relatives (ṃanthara); yataḥ jātā = since her birth; ushhitā = residing; kaikeyyā saha = with Kaikeyi; yadṛichchhayā = by chance; āruroha = ascended; prāsādam = the balcony of palace; chandrasa~ṇkāsham = which resembled the full moon.

ṃanthara, a housemaid who was residing with Kaikeyi since her birth, accidentally ascended the balcony of Kaikeyi's white palace, which resembled the full moon.

Sloka 2

siktarājapathāṃ kṛtsnāṃ prakīrṇakusumotkarām |
ayodhyāṃ mantharā tasmātprāsādādanvavaikṣata || 2-7-2

2. mantharā = ṃanthara; prāsādāt = from that balcony; anvavaikshata = saw; tasmāt = that; kṛitsnām = entire; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; siktarājapathām = the principal roads being sprinkled with water; prakīrṇakusumotkarām = and stewn with heaps of flowers.

ṃanthara, from that balcony, saw the entire city of Ayodhya, with all its principal roads being sprinkled with water and strewn with heaps of flowers.

Sloka 3

patākābhirvarārhābhirdhvajaiśca samalaṅkṛtām |
vṛtāṃ caṃdapathaiścāpi śiraḥsnātajanairvṛtām || 2-7-3
mālyamodakahastaiśca dvijendrairabhināditām |
śukladevagṛhadvārāṃ sarvavāditranisvanām|| 2-7-4
saṃprahṛṣṭajanākīrṇāṃ brahmaghoṣābhināditām |
prahṛṣṭavarahastyaśvāṃ saṃpraṇardhitagovṛśām ||2-7-5
prahṛuṣṭamuditaiḥ paurairuccri tadvajamālinīm |
ayodhyāṃ vantharā tasmātprāsādādanvavaikṣata|| 2-7-6

3;4;5;6ṃantharā = ṃanthara; anvavaikshatā = saw; tasmāt prāsādāt = from that balcony; ayodhyām = the city of Ayodhya; varārḥābhiḥ = which was an excellent one; samalaṇ^kṛitām = decorated by; patākābhiḥ = flags; dhvajaishcha = and by buntings; vṛitām = consisting of; chaṃda pathaiḥ cha api = well laid out pathways; vṛitām = consisting of; shiraḥ snāta janaiḥ = people who had head bath; abhināditām = and resounding with; dvijendraiḥ = brahmins; mālya modaka hastaiḥ = with flowers and sweets in their hands; shukla deva gṛiha dvārām = (consisting of) temples with white doorways; sarva vāditra nisvanām = resonating with sounds from various musical instruments; saṃprahṛishhṭa janākīrṇām = filled with happy people; brahmaghoshha abhināditām = resonating with Vedic chants; prahṛishhṭa vara hastyashvām = with overjoyed elephants and horses; saṃpraṇardita govṛishhām = with roaring bulls and cows; prahṛishhṭamuditaiḥ = with greatly overjoyed; pauraiḥ = citizens; uchchhrita dhvaja mālinīm = and decked with rows of tall flags .

ṃanthara further saw from that balcony the city decorated with the best of flags and banners. The roads were cleared for uninterrupted traffic. All the people took head-bath. Brahmans with flowers and sweets in their hands were chanting ṃantras. The gateways of temples were white washed. There were sounds of all musical instruments. The city was filled with rejoicing people and resounding chant of Vedas. The elephants and horses were also merry. The bulls were making roaring noise. The highly delighted citizens hoisted rows of flags on their housetops.

Sloka 7

praharṣotphullanayanāṃ pāṇḍurakṣaumavāsinīm |
avidūre sthitāṃ dṛṣṭvā dhātrīṃ papraccha mantharā || 2-7-7

7. mantharā = ṃanthara; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; dhātrīm = a house maid; praharshhotphullanayanām = with bright delighted eyes; pāṇDurakshaumavāsinīm = wearing white silk clothes; sthitām = staying; avidūre = nearby; paprachchha = and asked(as follows):

ṃanthara asked a house maid with bright pleasing eyes and wearing white silk clothes standing nearby, as follows: -

Sloka 8

uttamenābhisaṃyuktā harṣeṇārthaparā satī |
rāmamātā dhanaṃ kiṃ nu janebhyaḥ saṃprayacchati | 2-7-8

8. kim nu saṃprayachchhati = why Kausalya is giving away; dhanam = money; janebhyaḥ = to people; uttamena = most; harsheṇa abhisamyukta = delightfully; arthaparāsatī = (though) she is badly lured of money.

"Why is Kausalya giving away money to people so delightfully today in charity, eventhough she is badly lured of money?"

Sloka 9

atimātrapraharṣo.ayaṃ kiṃ janasya ca śaṃsa me |
kārayiṣyati kiṃ vāpi saṃprahṛṣṭo mahīpatiḥ || 2-7-9

9. kim = why; ayam = this; atimātra praharshaḥ = great rejoicing; janasya = of people ?; mahīpatiḥ = (Is) king Dasaratha; samprahṛishhṭa = duly pleased; kimvāpi kārayishyati vā = getting done any act? shamsa = tell; me = me.

"Why are people so happy today? Is the king duly pleased with something, doing any great act? Tell me."

Sloka 10

vidīryamāṇā harṣeṇa dhātrī tu parayā mudā |
ācacakṣe.atha kubjāyai bhūyasīṃ rāghavaśriyam || 2-7-10

10. dhātrī tu = that house maid; vidīryamāṇā = bursting; harsheṇa = with joy; atha = thereafter; āchachakshe = told; kubjāyai = the hunch backed ṃanthara; paramayā mudā = with great gladness; bhūyasīm raghavashriyam = about the great honor to be conferred on Rama.

That housemaid, bursting with joy, told ṃanthara in a great gladdening tone about the great honor to be conferred upon Rama.

Sloka 11

śvaḥ puṣyeṇa jitakrodhaṃ yauvarājyena rāghavam |
rājā daśaratho rāmamabhiṣecayitānagham || 2-7-11

11. svaḥ = tommorow; pushyeṇa = on the day of Pushyami star; rājā dasarathaḥ = king Dasaratha; abhishechayitā = is going to anoint; anagham = faultless; rāmam = Rama; rāghavam = who was born in Raghu clan; yauvarājyena = for the princely kingdom.

"Tomorrow on the day of Pushyami star, king Dasaratha is going to anoint the fault-less Rama for the princely kingdom."

Sloka 12

dhātryāstu vacanaṃ śrutvā kubjā kṣipramamarṣitā |
kailāsaśikharākārātprāsādādavarohata || 2-7-12

12. shrutvā = after hearing; vachanam = words; dhātryā = of the maid; kubjā = the hunch backed ṃanthara; kshipram = quickly; amarshhitā = with anger; avarohata = stepped down; prāsādāt = from roof of the palace.

After hearing the words of that maid, ṃanthara quickly with wrath walked down from the roof of the palace, which was in the shape of mount Kailasa.

Sloka 13

sā dahyamānā kopena manatharā pāpadarśinī |
śayanāmetya kaikeyīmidaṃ vacana mabravīt || 2-7-13

13. sā = that; mantharā = ṃanthara; pāpa darshinī = who has sinful thought; dahyamānā = burning; kopena = with anger; etya = approached; kaikeyīm = Kaikeyi; shayānām = who was lying on bed; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words.

ṃanthara of sinful thought; burning with anger, approached Kaikeyi who was lying on a bed and spoke these words:

Sloka 14

uttiṣṭha mūḍhe kiṃ śeṣe bhayaṃ tvāmabhivartate |
upaplutamaghaughena kimātmānaṃ na budhyase || 2-7-14

14. mūDhe = O; fool! uttishṭha = get up; kim = why; sheshhe = sleeping? bhayam = dreadful situation; abhivartate = is befalling; tvā = you; upaplutam = are submerged by; aghaughena = flood of troubles; kim = why (are you); na budhyase = not knowing; ātmānam = yourself?

"O, fool! Get up. Why are you sleeping? A dreadful situation is befalling you. A flood of troubles submerges you. Why are you not knowing this yourself?"

Sloka 15

aniṣṭe subhagākāre saubhagyena vikatthase |
calaṃ hi tava saubhāgyaṃ nadyāḥ srota ivoṣṇage ||2-7-15

15. subhakāgāre = you; who appear to be beloved to your husband! anishhṭe = (but) who is not liked by your husband! vikatthase = (you) boast; saubhāgyena = of fortune; tava = your; saubhāgyam = fortune; chalam hi = is unsteady; srotaḥ iva = like a stream; nadeyāḥ = of river; ushhṇage = in summer.

"Oh, Kaikeyi ! You appear to be a beloved wife for your husband, but in reality he does not like you. You boast of your fortune. Like the flow of a stream in summer; your fortune is unsteady".

Sloka 16

evamuktā tu kaikeyī ruṣṭayā paruṣaṃ vacaḥ |
kubjayā pāpadarśinyā viṣādamagamatparam || 2-7-16

16. kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; agamat = got; param = too much; vishhādam = sadness; uktā = after being spoken; parushham = of hard; vachaḥ = words; evam = thus; rushhṭayā = with anger; kubjayā = by ṃanthara; pāpadarshinayā = of sinful thoughts

Kaikeyi was very much worried after hearing hard words spoken with anger by ṃanthara of sinful thoughts.

Sloka 17

kaikeyi tvabravītkubhāṃ kaccitkṣemaṃ na manathare |
viṣaṇṇavadanāṃ hi tvāṃ lakṣaye bhṛ śaduḥkhitām || 2-7-17

17. kaikeyī tu = Kaikeyi; abravīt = spoke; kubjām = to the hunch backed; manthare = O; ṃanthara ! kshhemam na kachchit = (you are feeling) not safe or what ? lakshaye hi = I am seeing; tvām = you; bhṛishaduḥkhitām = being very sorrowful; vishhaṇṇavadanām = with a worried face.

Kaikeyi asked the hunch-backed ṃanthara as follows; "O, ṃanthara! Are you not safe? You appear to be very sorrowful with your worried face".

Sloka 18

mantharā tu vacaḥ śrutvā kaikeyyā madhurākṣaram |
uvāca krodhasaṃyuktā vākyaṃ vākyaviśāradā || 2-7-18

18. srutvā = after hearing; kaikeyyāḥ vachaḥ = Kaikeyi's words; madhurāksharam = with sweet words; mantharā tu = ṃanthara; vākyavishāradā = who was an expert in talking; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = these words; krodhasamyuktāḥ = with anger.

After hearing Kaikeyi's sweet words, ṃanthara who was an expert in talking, spoke with anger.

Sloka 19

sā viṣaṇṇatarā bhūtvā kubjā tasyā hitaiṣiṇī |
viṣadayantī provāca bhedayantī ca rāghavam || 2-7-19

19. sā = that; kubjā = ṃanthara; tasyāḥ hitaishhiṇī = wishing Kaikeyi's welfare; vishhādayantī = making her sorrowful; bheda yantī cha = and to create hostility; rāghavam = with Rama; provācha = said (as follows).

That ṃanthara, who was full of cunning, feigning to be seeking Kaikeyi's welfare, appeared more worried, and making Kaikeyi sorrowful, spoke the following, to create hostility between Rama and Kaikeyi.

Sloka 20

akṣayyaṃ sumahaddevi pravṛttaṃ dvadvināśanam |
rāmaṃ daśaratho rājā yauvarājye.abhiṣekṣyati || 2-7-20

20. devi = Oh; Queen! akshhayyam = an unending; sumahat = great; tvadvināshanam = ruin to you; pravṛittam = has encircled; rājā = king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; abhishhekshyati = is anointing; rāmam = Rama; yauvarājye = for princely kingdom.

"Oh, queen! An unending great ruin has encircled you. King Dasaratha is going to anoint Rama for the princely kingdom".

Sloka 21

sāsmyagādhe bhaye magnā duḥkhaśokasamanvitā |
dahyamānā.a naleneva tvaddhitārthamihāgatā || 2-7-21

21. sā asmi = that myself; magnā = is immersed; agādhe = in bottomless pit; bhaye = of fear; duḥkha shoka samanvitā = with pain and sorrow; dahyamānena = (I am) burning; analena = as if by fire; āgatā = came; iha = here; tvaddhitārtham = for your sake.

"I am immersed in a bottomless pit of fear. I am in distress and my mind is burning as if by fire. I hastened here to seek you out."

Sloka 22

tava duḥkhena kaikeyi mama duḥkhaṃ mahadbhavet |
tvadvṛddhau mama vṛddhiśca bhavedatra na saṃśayaḥ || 2-7-22

22. kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi; tava duḥkena = by your grief; mahat = great; duḥkham = grief; bhavena = happens; mama = to me; tvadvṛiddhau = your prosperity; bhavet = becomes; vṛiddhishcha = prosperity; mama = to me; na = no ; saṃshayaḥ = doubt; atra = about this.

"Kaikeyi! If Rama becomes king, his son will become king after him. Thus, Bharata's name itself will be removed from the royal clan."

Sloka 23

narādhipakule jātā mahiṣī tvaṃ mahīpateḥ |
ugratvaṃ rājadharmāṇāṃ kathaṃ devi na budhyase || 2-7-23

23. devi = Oh; queen! jātā = having born; narādhipakule = in royal family; mahishhī = being wife; mahīpate = of a king; katham = why; na buddhyase = not knowing; ugratvam = cruelty; rājadharmāṇām = in kingly duties.

"Oh, Kaikeyi! Having born in a royal family and being an emperor's wife, how is it that you are not knowing about the cruelty in kingly duties?"

Sloka 24

dharmavādī śaṭho bhartā ślakṣṇavādī ca dāruṇaḥ |
śuddhabhāve na jānīṣe tenaivamatisandhitā || 2-7-24

24. bhartā = your husband; dharma vādī = talks of righteousness; shaṭhaḥ = but does harm secretly; shlakshhṇa vādī = talks sweetly; dāruṇaḥ cha = but is a cruel man; shuddhabhāve = O; the clean minded! najānīshhe = you are not able to know; atisandhitā = that you are cheated; tena = by him; evam = thus.

"Your husband talks about righteousness, but does harm secretly. He says sweet words but behaves cruelly. Because of your clean thinking; you are not able to know that he is cheating you thus."

Sloka 25

upasthitaṃ payuñjānastvayi sāntvamanarthakam |
arthenaivādya te bhartā kausalyāṃ yojayiṣyati || 2-7-25

25. te bhartā = your husband; upasthitaḥ = approaches you; prayuJṇjānaḥ = employs; anarthakam = useless; sāntvam = good words; tvayi = in your instance; adya = today; yojayishhyati = he bestows; arthena = benefit to; kausalyameva = only Kausalya.

"Your husband approaches you and talks good words, which are useless. But he passes on benefit today to Kausalya only."

Sloka 26

apavāhya sa duṣṭātmā bharataṃ tava bandhuṣu |
kālyaṃ sthāpayitā rāmaṃ rājye nihatakaṇṭake || 2-7-26

26. dushhṭātmā = the evil minded; saḥ = he; apavāhya = sent; bharatam = Bharata; tava bandhushhu = to his relatives; kalye = at dawn; sthāpayitā = is going to install; ramam = Rama; rājye = in kingdom; nihatakaṇṭake = without hindrance.

"The evil minded Dasaratha sent Bharata to the house of his relatives and at dawn, is going to install Rama on the throne without hindrance."

Sloka 27

śatruḥ patipravādena mātreva hitakāmyayā |
āśīviṣa ivāṅkena bāle paridhṛtastvayā || 2-7-27

27. bāle = O; childish one! mātreva = like mother; hitakāmyayā = being wished well being; tvayā = by you; āshīvishhaḥ ankeneva = like a poisonous serpent in your bosom; shatruḥ = enemy; paridhṛitaḥ = being worn; patipravādena = with husband's name.

"O, childish one! Like a mother, you wish for his well being. But he is an enemy, and is called husband for a namesake. He is like a poisonous serpent in your bosom."

Sloka 28

yathā hi kuryātsarpo vā śatrurvā pratyupekṣitaḥ |
rājñā daśarathenādya saputrā tvaṃ tathā kṛtā || 2-7-28

28. tvam = you; saputrā = along with your son; kṛitā = have been done; rāJṇā dasarathena = by king Dasaratha; adya = today;yathā tathā kurvāt = as does; pratyupekshitaḥ = neglected; sarpo vā = serpent or; shatruruvā = an enemy

"King Dasaratha has acted towards you and your son today in the same way as an enemy or a serpent would behave if ignored."

Sloka 29

pāpenānṛtasāntvena bāle nityaṃ sukhocite |
rāmaṃ sthāpayatā rājye sānubandhā hatā hyasi || 2-7-29

29. bāle = O; childish one! nityasukhochite = you; who are ever deserving of ease; anṛita sāntvena = with his untruthful sweet words; sthāpayitā = by installing; rāmam = Rama; rājye = in kingdom; sānubandhā = you along with your child; asihi hatā = are being hit; pāpena = by the sinful king.

"O, stupid one! With his untruthful sweet words to you, who are ever deserving of ease, the evil minded king is installing Rama on the throne and is thus ruining you along with your son."

Sloka 30

sā prāptakālaṃ kaikeyi kṣipraṃ kuru hitaṃ tava |
trāyasva putramātmānaṃ māṃ ca vismayadarśane || 2-7-30

30. kaikeyi = O; Kaikeyi! vismaya darshane = you; with amazing beauty! sā = that you; kuru = do; hitam = required action; prāptakālam = appropriate to the time; kshipram = immediately; trāyasva = save; ātmānam = yourself; putram = your son; mām cha = and me.

"O, Kaikeyi, of amazing beauty! You have to take timely action immediately, so as to save yourself, your son and me too."

Sloka 31

mantharāyā vacaḥ śrutvā śayanātsa śubhānanā |
uttasthau harṣasaṃpūrṇā candralekheva śāradī || 2-7-31

31. shayanā = lying in bed; sā = that; shubhānanā = beautiful faced Kaikeyi; shrutvā = hearing; mantharāyāḥ = ṃanthara's; vachaḥ = words; harshhasampūrṇā = full of delight; uttasthau = rose(from the coach); chandralekheva = as crescent moon; shāradī = in autumn.

Hearing ṃanthara's words, that Kaikeyi of charming countenance who was lying in bed, rose from her coach full of delight like unto the crescent moon in autumn.

Sloka 32

atīva sā tu saṃhṛṣṭāa kaikeyī vismayānvitā |
ekamābharaṇaṃ tasyai kubjāyai pradadau śubham || 2-7-32

32. sā kaikeyī = that Kaikeyi; atīva = much; santushhṭā = delighted; vismayānvitā = with surprise; dadau = gave; ekam = one; shubham = graceful; ābharaṇam = jewel; tasyai = to that; kubjāyai = hunch backed ṃanthara.

That Kaikeyi in the midst of her astonishment and joy gave a graceful jewel to the hunch-backed ṃanthara.

Sloka 33

dattvā tvābharaṇaṃ tasyai kubjāyai pramadottamā |
kaikeyī mantharāṃ hṛṣṭā punarevābravīdidam || 2-7-33

33. kaikeyi = Kaikeyi; pramadottamā = the best among women; datvā = gave; ābharaṇam = a jewel; tasyai kubjāyai = to that hunch backed one; dṛistvā = saw; mantharām = ṃanthara; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; punaḥ eva = again.

Kaikeyi, the best among women, gave a jewel to ṃanthara and spoke to her as follows:

Sloka 34

idaṃ tu manthare mahyamākhyāsi paramaṃ priyam |
etanme priyamākhyātuḥ kiṃ vā bhūyaḥ karomi te || 2-7-34

34. manthare = O; ṃanthara! ākhyāsi = you informed; idam = this; paramam = much; priyam = delightful news; mahyam = to me; kim vā = what; bhūyaḥ = additional (favour); karomi = can I do; te = for you; ākhyātuḥ = who informed; etat = such; priyam = good (news); me = to me.

"Oh, ṃanthara! You informed much gladdening news to me. What else can I do for you, who informed such a good news?"

Sloka 35

rāme vā bharate vāhaṃ viśeṣaṃ nopalakṣaye |
tasmāttuṣṭāsmi yadrājā rāmaṃ rājye.abhiṣekṣyati || 2-7-35

35. aham = I; na upalakshaye = do not see; visheshham = difference; rāmevā = in either Rama; bharatevā = or Bharata; tasmāt = that is why; tushhṭāsmi = I am happy; yat = for; rājā = king; abhishekshyati = anointing; ramam = Rama; rājye = in kingdom.

"I do not see any difference between Rama and Bharata. That is why, I am happy that Rama is being anointed for crown by the king."

Sloka 36

na me param kiñci ditastvayāpi na |
priyaṃ priyārhe suvacam vaco param |
tathā hyavocastvamataḥ priyottaraṃ |
varaṃ varaṃ te pradadāmi taṃ vṛṇu || 2-7-36

36. priyārhe = O; ṃanthara; who are eligible to be delighted! na kinchit = ṇo other; param = greater; itaḥ = than this; me = to me; na suvacham = cannot be told easily; param = greater; priyam = lovely; vachaḥ = words; tvayāpi = by you also; tvam = you; avochaḥ hi = informed; tathā = thus; priyottaram = the best of loving news; ataḥ = for this reason; pradadāmi = I shall give; param = good; varam = boon; te = to you; vṛiṇu = ask for; tam = it.

"O, ṃanthara! You do merit every favor. I have no other pleasant news than this. Hence, you cannot ever again inform a better loving news than this. I shall give you a good boon. Ask for it!"

- - -

|| ityārṣe srīmadrāmāyane ādikāvye ayodhyakānde śastaḥ sargaḥ ||

Thus completes 7th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
