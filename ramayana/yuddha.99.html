Introduction

Ravana enters the battle-field and with his mystic missile, frightens several monkeys and destroys them. Rama and Lakshmana come and attack Ravana. Then, Rama and Ravana fight each other with various kinds of arrows. When the missile employed by Ravana is struck down by Rama, all the monkeys shout loudly with a rejoice.

śvasanto viviśurbhūmin rāvaṇapratikūlatāḥ |

39. bhitvā = having pierced; bāṇarūpāṇi = the frames of arrows (of Ravana); te = the arrows of Rama; vivishuḥ = penetrated; paṇ^cha shīrṣhā shvasantaḥ uragāḥ iva = like five-headed hissing serpents; bhūmim = into the earth; rāvaṇa pratikūlitāḥ = when rebuffed by Ravana.

Having pierced the frames of arrows of Ravana, Rama's arrows penetrated like five-headed hissing serpents into the earth, when rebuffed by Ravana.

Verse Locator

nihatya rāghavasyāstran rāvaṇaḥ krodhamūrchitaḥ || 6-99-40
āsuran sumahāghoramanyadastraṃ samādade |

40. nihatya rāghavasya astram = striking down Rama's missile; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; krodhamūrchhitaḥ = who was stupefied with anger; chakāra = employed; anyat sumahāghoram = another exceedingly terrific; āsuram astram = missile presided over by demoniac forces.

Striking down Rama's missile, that Ravana, who was stupefied with anger, employed another exceedingly terrific missile presided over by demoniac forces.

Verse Locator

sinhavyāghramukhāṃścānyānkaṅkakāka mukhānapi || 6-99-41

īhāmṛgamuhāṃścānyānvyāditāsyānbhayāvahān || 6-99-42
pañcāsyāṇllelihānāṃśca sasarja niśitāñśarān |

41; 42. sasarja = (ḥe) released; nishitān = sharp; sharān = arrows; simhavyāghra mukhān cha api = having the heads of lions and tigers; kaṇ^kakākamukhānapi = those of buzzards and crows; gṛidhrashyena mukhāmshchāpi = even of vultures and faloons; tathā = and; shṛigāla vadanān = those of jackal; īhāmṛigamukhāshchāpi = and wolves; vyāditāsyān = arrows with their months wide open; lehānāmshcha = having the heads of venomous snakes; pāṇ^chāsyān = with five heads; bhayāvahān = bringing fear.

He released sharp arrows, having the heads of lions and tigers, those of buzzards and crows, even of vultures and faloons, as well as those of jackals and wolves, arrows with their mouths wide open, having the heads of venomous snakes with five heads, bringing fear.

śarānkharamukhāṃścānyānvarāhamukhasansthitān || 6-99-43

43; 44. shvasan = hissing; sarpaḥ iva = like a serpent; kruddhaḥ = provoked to anger; (Ravana); mahātejāḥ = who was endowed with extraordinary energy; māyāvī = and conjuring tricks; sasarja = released; rāmam prati = towards Rama; etān = these; anyān sharān = and other arrows; kharamukhān = having the heads of donkeys; varāhamukha samshritān = those of boars; shvāna kukkuṭa vaktrāmshcha = dogs and cocks; makarāshīviṣhānanāna = alligators and venomous snakes.

Hissing like a serpent provoked to anger Ravana who was endowed with extraordinary energy and conjuring tricks, released towards Rama these and other arrows, having the heads of donkeys, those of boars, dogs and cocks as also of alligators and venomous snakes.

Verse Locator

āsureṇa samāviṣṭaḥ soastreṇa raghunandanaḥ || 6-99-45

45. saḥ raghupuṇ^gavaḥ = that Rama; mahotsāhaḥ = with a great strength; pāvakopamaḥ = and equal to god of fire; samāviṣhṭaḥ = but possessed by; āsureṇa astreṇa = a missile presided over by demoniac forces; sasarja = released; pāvakam astram = a missile presided over by the god of fire.

Possessed by a missile presided over by demoniac forces, that Rama endowed with a great strength and resembling the god of fire, released a missile presided over by the god of fire.

(Rama) sasarja = employed; tatra = there; vividhān sharān = various kinds of arrows; agnidīpta mukhān bāṇān = some with heads bright as fire; sūrya mukhānapi = others with heads shining like the sun; chandrārdhachandra vaktrān = the moon; the crescent; dhūmaketu mukhānapi = a comet; grahanakṣhatra varṇāmshcha = others shining like planets; lunar mansions; maholkāmukhasamsthitān = and a huge meteor; vidyujjihvopamān = and some resembling flashes of lightning.

Rama employed there, various kinds of arrows, with heads bright as fire, others with heads shining like the sun, the moon, the crescent, a comet, others shining like planets, lunar mansions and a huge meteor and some arrows resembling flashes of lightning.

48. rāghavāstra samāhatāḥ = struck by the missile of Rama; te = those; ghorāḥ = terrific; rāvaṇasharāḥ = arrows of Ravana; jagmuḥ = got; vilayam = destroyed; ākāshe = in the sky; jagmuḥ = and fell; sahasrashaḥ = in thousands of pieces.

Struck by Rama's missile, the terrific arrows of Ravana got destroyed in the sky and fell down in thousands of pieces.

Rejoiced to see that missile struck down by Rama, who was unweary in action, all the valiant monkeys, who were capable of changing their form at will, then encompassing Rama, raised a clamour, facing Sugreeva.

51. vinihatya = striking down; prasahya = with force; tat = that; astram = missile; tadrāvaṇabāhuniḥsṛitam = which came forth from the hands of that Ravana; mahātmā = the great souled; dāsharathiḥ = Rama; tataḥ = then; mudā anvitaḥ = was filled with joy; muditaiḥ = full of joy; vineduḥ uchchaiḥ = shouted loudly.

Striking down with force that missile which came forth from Ravana's arms, the great-souled Rama, then was filled with rejoice while the chiefs of monkeys, full f joy, shouted loudly.
