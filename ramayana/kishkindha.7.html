Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 7
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sugreeva consoles Rama soothingly and advises him to come out of his frenzy emotion at the loss of Seetha, which is not befitting to a person like Rama. Rama recovering from his emotional state, promises Sugreeva to be truthful to his word in alleviating the fear of Vali for Sugreeva. And Sugreeva also promises to make all his efforts to search for Seetha.


Sloka 1

evam uktaḥ tu sugrīvaḥ rāmeṇa ārtena vānaraḥ |
abravīt prāñjaliḥ vākyam sabāṣpam bāṣpa gadgadaḥ || 4-7-1

1. ārtena = one in agony; rāmeṇa = by Rama; evam uktaḥ tu = thus, said; ṣugreeva; vānaraḥ = the monkey; bāṣpa gadgadaḥ = with tears, trembling voice; sa bāṣpam = with tears;. pra anjaliḥ = with adjoined palms; vākyam = sentence; abravīt = said.

Thus said by Rama in agony that monkey Sugreeva with his palms adjoined, and with tears rolling in eyes and also voice trembling with tears said this to Rama. [4-7-1]

Sloka 2

na jāne nilayam tasya sarvathā pāpa rakṣasaḥ |
sāmarthyam vikramam vā api dauṣkuleyasya vā kulam || 4-7-2

2. daushkuleyasya = of that sinister dynasty; tasya pāpa rakshasaḥ = of his, of that sinning, demon's; nilayam = residence; sāmarthyam = capability; vikramam = valour; vā = either; api = even; kulam vā = his lineage, even; sarvathā = at all; na jāne = not, familiar.

"Unfamiliar is the residence, or capability, or valour, or lineage of that sinning demon from a sinister dynasty, in any wise... [4-7-2]

Sloka 3

satyam tu pratijānāmi tyaja śokam arindama |
kariṣyāmi tathā yatnam yathā prāpsyasi maithilīm || 4-7-3

3. arindama = oh, enemy destroyer; te satyam pratijānāmi = to you, truthfully, I am promising; maithilīm yathā prāpsyasi = ṃaithili, as to how, you get back; tathā yatnam karishyāmi = therefor, make effort, I strive to; tyaja shokam = discard, anguish.

"Truthfully I am promising you, oh, enemy destroyer, as to how you will get back ṃaithili therefor I strive to make effort, let anguish be discarded... [4-7-3]

Sloka 4

rāvaṇam sagaṇam hatvā paritoṣya ātma pauruṣam |
tathā asmi kartā nacirād yathā prīto bhaviṣyasi || 4-7-4

4. rāvanam sa gaṇam hatvā = Ravana, with, henchmen, on killing; paritoṣya = satisfying your; ātma pauruṣam = self, pride; yathā prītaḥ bhaviṣyasi = as to how, you will be gladdened; tathā na cirāt kartā asmi = that way, not, before long, done, by me.

"As to how you will satisfy yourself on killing Ravana along with his henchmen, and as to how to you pride yourself thereby, I will make it happen in that way in no longer a time... [4-7-4]

Sloka 5

alam vaiklavyam ālambya dhairyam ātmagatam smara |
tvat vidhānām na sadṛśam īdṛśam buddhi lāghavam || 4-7-5

5. alam vaiklavyam ālambya = enough, despair, clinging on to; ātma gatam dhairyam smara = soul gone in [inherent,] courage, you recollect; tvat vidhānām = your like persons; īdṛisham buddhi lāghavam = this sort of, mental, ineptness; na sadṛisham = not, seemly.

"Enough is this clinging on to despair, recollect your inherent courage, and it is unseemly for persons of your sort to have this kind of mental ineptness... [4-7-5]

Sloka 6

mayā api vyasanam prāptam bhāryā virahajam mahat |
na aham evam hi śocāmi dhairyam na ca parityaje || 4-7-6

6. mayā api = by me, too; bhāryā = wife's; viraha jam = separation, caused by; mahat = greatly; vyasanam = yearning; prāptam = came upon; na ca aham = not, also, I; evam = this way; shocāmi = despaired; na ca dhairyam = not, also, courage; parityaje = forsook.

"Upon me too this yearning caused by the separation of wife has chanced, but I did not despair this way, nor I have forsaken my courage... [4-7-6]

Sloka 7

na aham tām anuśocāmi prākṛto vānaro api san |
mahātmā ca vinītaḥ ca kim punar dhṛtimān mahān || 4-7-7

7. aham prākṛitaḥ vānaraḥ api san = I am, being primitive, monkey, even though; tām anu shochāmi = of her [for distancing wife,] not, [totally] disheartening; mahātmā ca = great soul, also; vinītaḥ ca = well-read one, also; dhṛītimān = courageous one; bhavān kim punaḥ = you are, what again [why tell about you.]

"Though I am a primitive monkey I am not totally disheartened for the distancing of wife, why tell about a great soul like you who are well read and courageous... [4-7-7]

Sloka 8

bāṣpam āpatitam dhairyāt nigrahītum tvam arhasi |
maryādām sattva yuktānām dhṛtim na utsraṣṭum arhasi || 4-7-8

8. tvam ā patitam bāshpam dhairyāt nigrahītum arhasi = you, trickling, tears, with courage, to control, apt of you; sattva yuktānām = that which belonging to self-controlled ones; maryādām = propriety; dhṛitim = courage; na utsraṣṭum arhasi = not, to discard, apt of you.

"It is apt of you to control trickling tears with courage, and equally it is inapt of you to discard the courage and propriety of self-controlled people... [4-7-8]

Sloka 9

vyasane vā artha kṛcchre vā bhaye vā jīvitāntage |
vimṛśan vai svayā buddhyā dhṛtimān na avasīdati || 4-7-9

9. vyasane vā = in a riddle, or; artha kṛicchre vā = financial loss, or; jīvita antage = life, at end of; bhaye vā = in fear, either; dhṛitimān = courageous one; svayā buddhyā = in ones own, mind; vimṛishan = by introspection; = verily; na avasīdati vai = not, sinks down, indeed.

"Either in a riddle, or in financial loss, or at the end of life, or in fear, a courageous one does not sink down, but indeed introspects in his own mind... [4-7-9]

Sloka 10

bāliśas tu naro nityam vaiklabyam yo.anuvartate |
sa majjati avaśaḥ śoke bhāra ākrāntā iva nauḥ jale || 4-7-10

10. yaḥ naraḥ = which, man; bālisaḥ nityam vaiklabyam anuvartate = like stupid, always, self-pity, he who follows; saḥ = he; a vasaḥ = not, self-controlled; bhāra ākrāntā = by weight, overpowered [overweighing]; nauḥ jale iva = ship, in waters, like; shoke majjati = in sadness, drowns.

"Stupid is that man who always follows self-piteous sadness unable to control himself, and he drowns down in that melancholy like a overweighing ship in waters... [4-7-10]

Sloka 11

eṣo anjaliḥ mayā baddhaḥ praṇayāt tvām prasādaye |
pauruṣam śraya śokasya na antaram dātum arhasi || 4-7-11

11. mayā eṣaḥ anjaliḥ baddhaḥ = by me, this, palms, adjoined; praṇayāt tvām prasādaye = in friendship, you, I beseech; pauruṣam shraya = self-esteem, bear up; shokasya antaram dātum na arhasi = for sadness, leeway, to give, not, apt of you.

"Adjoined are my palms in this way beseeching you in friendship, kindly bear up your self-esteem and you should not give any leeway to sadness... [4-7-11]

Sloka 12

ye śokam anuvartante na teṣām vidyate sukham |
tejaḥ ca kṣīyate teṣām na tvam śocitum arhasi || 4-7-12

12. ye = those; shokam anuvartante = moroseness, delve into; na teṣām = not, to them; vidyate sukham = enjoy, happiness; tejaḥ cha = brilliance, too; kshīyate = dwindle; teṣām = their; na tvam shocitum arhasi = not, your, saddening, is apt.

"Those that delve deep into moroseness, to them there is no happiness, and their brilliance too dwindles, thus saddening is inapt on your part... [4-7-12]

Sloka 13

śokena abhiprapannasya jīvite ca api saṃśayaḥ |
sa śokam tyaja rājendra dhairyam āśraya kevalam || 4-7-13

13. rājendra = oh, king of kings; shokena = by sadness; abhi prapannasya = well, engulfed; jīvite cha api = of life, also, even; samshayaḥ = doubts; saḥ shokam = such as you are, sadness; tyaja = leave off; kevalam dhairyam āshraya = just, courage, hold on to.

"One well-engulfed in sadness doubts even his life, oh, king of kings, leave off that sadness and just hold on to courage...[4-7-13]

Sloka 14

hitam vayasya bhāvena brūmi na upadiśāmi te |
vayasyatām pūjayan me na tvam śocitum arhasi || 4-7-14

14. hitam = benefiting; vayasya bhāvena = friendly, thought; brūmi = I tell; na upadishāmi te = not, instructing, you; vayasyatām = friendship; pūjayan = regarding; me = of mine; na tvam shocitum arhasi = not, your, saddening, is apt.

"I am telling a benefiting and friendly thought, but not instructing you, should you regard my friendship then your saddening is inapt..." [Thus said Sugreeva to Rama.] [4-7-14]

Sloka 15

madhuram sāntvitaḥ tena sugrīveṇa sa rāghavaḥ |
mukham aśru pari klinnam vastra antena pramārjayat || 4-7-15

15. tena sugrīvena = by that, ṣugreeva; madhuram = sweetly; sāntvitaḥ = solaced; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; ashru pari klinnam = by tears, fully covered; mukham = face; vastra antena = with cloth's, edge; pramārjayat = dabbed.

Sweetly solaced thus by that Sugreeva Rama dabbed his face with the edge of cloth, which is covered fully with tears. [4-7-15]

Sloka 16

prakṛtiḥ sthaḥ tu kākutsthaḥ sugrīva vacanāt prabhuḥ |
saṃpariṣvajya sugrīvam idam vacanam abravīt || 4-7-16

16. prabhuḥ = the lord; kākutsthaḥ = Rama; ṣugreeva; vacanāt = by words of; prakṛitiḥ sthaḥ tu = in his own nature, steadied himself; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva is; sam pariṣvajya = on embracing; idam vacanam abravīt = this, sentence, said.

Lord Rama steadied himself in his own nature by the words of Sugreeva, and on embracing him Rama said this sentence to him. [4-7-16]

Sloka 17

kartavyam yat vayasyena snigdhena ca hitena ca |
anurūpam ca yuktam ca kṛtam sugrīva tat tvayā || 4-7-17

17. ṣugreeva; snigdhena hitena ca = by a friend, friendly one, also; vayasyena yat kartavyam = by friend, that which, is to be done; anurūpam ca = timely, also; yuktam ca = befitting, also; tat tvayā = that which, by you; kṛitam = is done.

"Sugreeva, a friendly obligation that which is to be done, a benefiting, befitting and a timely one too, is what you have performed with your friendly advise... [4-7-17]

Sloka 18

eṣa ca prakṛtiḥ sthaḥ aham anunītaḥ tvayā sakhe |
durlabho hi īdṛśo bandhuḥ asmin kāle viśeṣataḥ || 4-7-18

18. sakhe = oh, friend; tvayā = by you; anunītaḥ = implored; eṣa aham = this, I am; prakṛitiḥ sthaḥ = in my nature, I steadied myself; īdṛishaḥ bandhuḥ durlabhaḥ hi = this kind of, friend, impossible to get, indeed; asmin kāle = in these times; visheshataḥ = especially.

"Oh, friend, I steadied myself in my own nature when implored by you... it is impossible to get this kind of friend, especially at these times... [4-7-18]

Sloka 19

kim tu yatnaḥ tvayā kāryo maithilyāḥ parimārgaṇe |
rākṣasasya ca raudrasya rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 4-7-19

19. kim tu = but; tvayā maithilyāḥ = by you, ṃaithili's; durātmanaḥ rākshasasya raudrasya rāvaṇasya = evil-minded one, of demon's, furious, Ravana's; parimārgaṇe yatnaḥ kāryaḥ = search for, endeavour, is to be done.

"But, trying to search for ṃaithili and that furious, evil-minded demon Ravana, is your endeavour... [4-7-19]

Sloka 20

mayā ca yad anuṣṭheyam visrabdhena tat ucyatām |
varṣāsu iva ca sukṣetre sarvam saṃpadyate tava || 4-7-20

20. mayā ca yat anuṣṭeyam = by me, also, what, that is to be done; visrabdhena = in believability; tat ucyatām = that, be said; tava sarvam = your, all endeavour; varṣāsu su kshetre iva = in rainy season, in good lands, as though [seeded crop]; sampadyate = be reaped.

"What is to done by me that be said in all believability, and all your endeavour will reap like the crops planted during rainy season in good lands... [4-7-20]

Sloka 21

mayā ca yadidam vākyam abhimānāt samīritam |
tat tvayā hariśārdūla tat tvam iti upadhāryatām || 4-7-21

21. hari shārdūla = oh, monkey, the tiger; mayā = by me; abhimānāt = in self-confidence; yat idam vākyam samīritam = which, this, word, is spoken; tat tvayā = that, by you; tattvam iti = factually, thus; upadhāryatām = they may be deemed.

"What that is spoken by me is in my self-confidence but not in any self-conceit, oh, tigerly-monkey, deem them as doubtless facts... [4-7-21]

Sloka 22

anṛtam na ukta pūrvam me na ca vakṣye kadācana |
etat te pratijānāmi satyena eva śapāmi aham || 4-7-22

22. me = by me; anṛitam na ukta pūrvam = untruth, not, said, earlier; kadācana na ca vakshye = anytimec[from now,] not, also, will speak; etat te pratijānāmi = this, to you, I promise; aham satyena eva shapāmi = I am, by truthfulness, alone taking oath.

"Untruth is not uttered by me earlier nor will be at anytime from now, and all this I am promising you taking oath on that truthfulness alone.. [4-7-22]

Sloka 23

tataḥ prahṛṣṭaḥ sugrīvaḥ vānaraiḥ sacivaiḥ saha |
rāghavasya vacaḥ śrutvā pratijñātam viśeṣataḥ || 4-7-23

23. tataḥ = then; sacivaiḥ = vānaraiḥ saha = with ministers, monkeys, along with; ṣugreeva; rāghavasya vacaḥ = Rama's word; shrutvā = on hearing; visheshataḥ = especially; pratijnātam = of promise; prahṛiṣṭaḥ = is gladdened.

Then on hearing the words of Rama Sugreeva along with his monkey ministers is gladdened, especially of Rama promise. [4-7-23]

Sloka 24

evam ekānta saṃpṛktau tataḥ tau nara vānarau |
ubhau anyonya sadṛśam sukha duḥkham abhāṣtām || 4-7-24

24. tataḥ = then; evam = thus; ekānta = in privacy; sampṛiktau = met; tau = those two; nara = man; vānarau = monkey; ubhau = both; anyonya sadṛisham = mutually befitting; sukha duḥkham = pleasure and pain; abhāshtām = discussed.

Then those two, the man and the monkey, met in privacy and both discussed in a mutually befitting manner about their pleasures and pains. [4-7-24]

Sloka 25

mahānubhāvasya vaco niśamya
harir nṛpāṇām adhipasya tasya |
kṛtam sa mene harivīra mukhyaḥ
tadā ca kāryam hṛdayena vidvān || 4-7-25

25. vidvān = the intellectual; saḥ hari vīra mukhyaḥ = he that one, among monkey, heroes, chief; hariḥ = monkey - ṣugreeva; mahānubhāvasya = highly capable one's [Rama's]; tasya = of his; nṛipāṇām adhipasya = among kings, the best king [Rama]; vacaḥ = word; nishamya = on hearing; tadā ca = then; kṛitam = accomplished; kāryam = manoeuvre; hṛidayena = at heart; mene = he [ṣugreeva], thought so.

On hearing the word of that intellectual, highly capable one and the best king among kings, namely Rama, he that Sugreeva, the chief among the monkey heroes, thought at heart that his manoeuvre to surpass his dominant brother Vali is accomplished. [4-7-25]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe saptamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 7th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
