Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 25
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Kausalya bids farewell to Rama preparing to go to forest. Rama leaves her residence and goes to Seetha.

Sloka 1

sā apanīya tam āyāsam upaspṛśya jalam śuci |
cakāra mātā rāmasya mangalāni manasvinī || 2-25-1

1. sā = that Kausalya; manasvinī = themagnanimous; mātā = mother; apanīya = putting away; tam āyāsam = = that grief; upaspṛishya jalam = sipped water from the palm of the hand; shuchiḥ = became purified; chakāra = performed; maṇgaLāni = rites ; conduciveto welfare; rāmasya = of Rama.

Kausalya, the magnanimous mother putting away her grief, sipped water from the palm of the hand, became purified and performed rites conducive to the welfare of Rama.

Sloka 2

na śakyase vārayauitum gacchedānīm raghuttama |
śrīghram ca vinivartasva vartasva ca satām krame || 2-25-2

2. raghottama = Oh; Rama!; na shakyase = you cannotbe; vārayitum = restrained; gachcha = Depat; idāmīm = now; vinivartasva = Return; shīghram = soon. > vartasva = Abide; krame = in the way; satām = of the righteous.

"Oh, Rama! Your departure cannot be restrained, depart now, return soon. Abide in the footsteps of the virtuous."

Sloka 3

yam pālayasi dharmam tvam dhṛtyā ca niyamena ca |
savai rāghavaśārdula! dharmastvāmabhirakṣatu || 2-25-3

3. rāghava shārdūla = Oh; Rama; the bestof born in Raghu dynasty! yam dharmam = which righteousness; tvam = you; pālayasi = are fostering; dhṛitvā = with courage ; niyamenacha = and discipline; saḥ dharmaḥ vai = thatrighteousness; abhirakshhatu = will protect; tvām = you.

"Oh, Rama! Let that righteousness, which you are fostering with courage and discipline , protect you".

Sloka 4

yebhyaḥ praṇamase putra caityeṣvāyataneṣu ca |
te ca tvāmabhirakṣantu vane saha maharṣibhiḥ || 2-25-4

4. putra = Oh; son!; yebhyaḥ = ṭo whom; praṇamase = you will be bowing in salutation; chaityeshu = in crossroads; āyataneshhucha = and in temples; te = those; maharshhibhisaha = along with greatsaints; abhirakshhatu = will protect; tvām = you; vane = in the forest.

"Oh, son! To whom you will be bowing in salutation at cross-roads and in temples, those along with great saints will protect you in the forest.

Sloka 5

yāni dattāni te.a strāṇi viśvāmitreṇa dhīmatā |
tāni tvāmabhirakṣantu guṇaissamuditam sadā || 2-25-5

5. yāmi astrāṇi = which arms; dattāni = were given; te = to you visvāmitreṇa = by Viswamitra ; dhīmatā = the wise; tāni = those; sadā = always ; abhirakshhatu = will protect; tvām = you ; samuditam = who are filled; guṇaiḥ = with good qualities.

"All the arms given by the wise sage Viswamitra to you, will ever protect you who are rich in good qualities"

Sloka 6

pitṛśuśruṣayā putra mātṛśu śrūṣayā tathā |
satyena ca mahābāho ciram jīvābhirakṣitaḥ || 2-25-6

6. putra = Oh; son; mahā bāho = with magnificientarms!; abhirakshhitaḥ = Protected; pitṛisushrūshhayā = by service to father; tathā = and; mātRi sushrūshhayā = service to mother; satyena = by ṭruth; chiramjīva = you will be long lived!

"Oh, son with magnificent arms! Protected by your service to father and service to mother as well as truthfulness, you will belong-lived"

Sloka 7

samitkuśapavitrāṇi vedyaścāyatanāni ca |
sthaṇḍhilāni vicitrāṇi śailā vṛkṣāḥ kuśuphā hradāḥ || 2-25-7
pataṅgāḥ pannagāḥ simhāstvām rakṣantu narottama |

7. narottama = Oh; Rama; among men!; samitkusha pavitrāṇi = woodensticks used for feeding the sacred fire ; blades of the sacred kusa grass andrings of kusa grass.vedaishcha = sacrificialaltars; āyatanānicha = temples; vichitrāṇi = various; sthaṇdilāni = sitesselected by Brahmanas for worship ofgods; shailāḥ = mountains; vṛikshhāḥ = trees; kshhupāḥ = bushes; hradāḥ = pools of water; pataṇgāḥ = birds; ; pannagāḥ = snakes; simhāḥ = lions; rakshhantu = may protect; tvām = you.

"Oh, Rama, the best among men! ṃay the wooden sticks used for feeding the sacred fire, blades of the sacred Kusa grass and rings of Kusa grass(worn on the forth finger on sacrificial occasions)sacrificial altars , temples and various sites selected by Brahmanas for worship of gods, mountains, trees, bushes, large and deep pools of water, birds, snakes and lions protect you."

Sloka 8

svasti sādhyāḥ ca viśve ca marutaḥ ca maharṣayaḥ || 2-25-8
svasti dhātā vidhātā ca svasti pūṣā bhago aryamā |
ṛtavaḥ caiva pakṣāḥ ca māsāḥ samvatsarāḥ kṣapāḥ || 2-25-9

8;9. sādhyāshcha = ṣādhyas; vishvecha = viswadevas ; marutashcha = wind-gods; maharshhayaḥ = eminentsages; svasti = may ensure your happiness; dhātā = ṭhe CosmicPerson; vidhātācha = the creator; svasthi = may bring happiness toyou; pūshhā = Pusha; bhagaḥ = Bhaga; āryamā = Aryama; svasthi = may do good to you; tathā = and; sarve = all; te lokapālashcha = those guardians of spheres; vāsavapramukhāḥ = with head as Indra.

"ṃay the sādhyas and viswadevas (two different kinds of gods) and wind-gods along with eminent sages ensure your happiness. ṃay the cosmic person and the creator bring happiness to you. ṃay the Pusha, Bhaga and Aryama (three of the twelve sons of Aditi)and the guardians of the spheres with Indra as their Head do good to you."

Sloka 10

ṛtavaścaiva pakṣāśca māsāssamvatsarāḥ kṣapāḥ |
dināni ca muhūrtāḥ ca svasti kurvantu te sadā || 2-25-10

10. Ritavashchaiva = ṭheseasons; pakshhāshcha = the fortnights ; māsaḥ = the months; samvatsarāḥ = years; kshhapāḥ = nights; divānicha = days; muhūrtāshcha = hours ; (each muhurtha containing 48 minutes being 30 th part of aday); sadā = always; kurvanta = may do svasti = good; te = to you

"ṃay the six seasons, all the fortnights and months, years, nights, days and hours always do good to you.

Sloka 11

smṛtir dhṛtiḥ ca dharmaḥ ca pāntu tvām putra sarvataḥ |
skandaḥ ca bhagavān devaḥ somaḥ ca sabṛhaspatiḥ || 2-25-11
sapta ṛṣayo nāradaḥ ca te tvām rakṣantu sarvataḥ |

11. putra = Oh; son! ; smṛitiḥ = veda; the smrititexts; dhṛitishcha = Resolution and satisfaction personified as a daughter of Daksha and wifeof Dharma ; dharmashcha = piety personified; pātu = may protect ; tvām = you; sarvataḥ = from all sides. Bhagavān = ṭhe gracious Lord; devaḥ = thediety; skandashcha = ṣkanda; somashcha = the moon-god; saḥ = that; bṛihaspatiḥ = Brihaspati; preceptor of gods; saptarshhayoḥ = the sevensages ; nāradashcha = ṣage ṇarada; rakshhantu = may guard; tvām = you; sarvataḥ = on all sides.

"ṃay Veda, the Smriti texts taken as one body, the resolution and the piety protect you, my son! ṃay lord Skanda (the younger son of Lord Siva)and the moon god along with the sage Brihaspati(the preceptor of gods), the well known seven sages as well as Sage Narada guard you on all sides."

Sloka 12

yāścāpi sarvataḥ siddā diśśca sadigīśvarāḥ || 2-25-12
stutā mayā vane tasmin pāntutvām putra nityaśaḥ |

12. putra = Oh; son! yāḥ = Which ; dishashcha = four quarters; sadigīshvarāḥ = along with guardiansof quarters; siddhāḥ = who are glorious; stutāḥ = are praised; mayā = byme; (they); pāntu = may protect; nityashaḥ = always; sarvataḥ = on all sides; tasmin vane = in that forest.

"ṃay the four quarters along with the glorious guardians of the four quarters which are enlogised by me may protect you always on all sides in that forest."

Sloka 13

śailāḥ sarve samudrāśca rājā varuṇa eva ca || 2-25-13
dyaurantarikṣam pṛthivī nadyassarvāstathaiva ca |
nakṣatrāṇi ca sarvāṇi grahāḥ ca sahadevatāḥ || 2-25-14
ahorātre tathā sandhye pāntu tvām vanamāśritam |

13;14. sarve = All; shailāḥ = mountains; samudrāshcha = oceans; rājā = king(of oceans)varuṇa eva> cha = Varuna; dyou = heaven ; pṛithivīcha = and the earth ; antharikshham = theintermediate space betweenthem; tathaivacha = and; sarvāḥ = all; nadyaḥ = rivers; sarvāṇi = all; nakshhatrāṇicha = stars; gṛihāshcha = astrological mansions ; sahadevatāḥ = including gods; ahorātre = day and night; tathā = and; sandhye = the morning and evening twilights; pāntu = may protect; tvām = you; vanam āshritam = residing in forest.

" Let all mountains , oceans, king(of oceans) Varuna, heaven and earth, the intermediate space between them , rivers , astrological mansions including gods presiding over them, day and night, the morning and evening twilights may protect you while you reside in the forest ."

Sloka 15

ṛtavaścaiva ṣṭpuṇyā māsāḥ samvatsarāstathā || 2-25-15
kalāśca kāṣṭhāśca tathā tava śarma diśantu te |

15. puṇyāḥ = ṭhe holy; shhaṭ = six; ṛitavashchaiva = seasons; te = those; māsāḥ = months; tathā = and; samvatsarāḥ = years; kalāshcha = the measure of time; Kala ; kāshhṭāshcha = andKasta; dishastu = bestow; tava = you; sharma = happiness.

"The six holy seasons, months, years and the measures of time known as Kala (equilent to one minute) and Kastha(1/30th of Kala or two seconds)bestow you happiness"

Sloka 16

mahā vanāni carataḥ muni veṣasya dhīmataḥ || 2-25-16
tavādityāśca daityāśca bhavantu sukhadāḥ sadā |

16. ādityāshcha = ḥeavenly deities; the sons of Aditi; daityāshcha = demons ; the sons of Diti; sadā = ever; bhavantu = become; sukhadāḥ = bestowers of happiness; tava = to you; vicharataḥ = while you traverse; mahāvane = in the large forest; muniveshhasya = in the guise of a hermit; dhīmataḥ = full of wisdom.

"Let the heavenly deities and the demons too ever grant happiness on you while you traverse through the large forest, in the guise of a hermit and full of wisdom "

Sloka 17

17. putraka = Oh; son!; mābhūt = Let there notbe; bhayam = fear; te = for you; rākshhasānām = of demons; raudrāṇām = the terrible ones; krūra karmāṇām = performing crueldeeds; pishāchānām = of devilish beings called Pisāchas (possibly so called either from their fondness of flesh or from their yellowish appearance)sarveshhām = of all; kravyādānāmcha = of cornivorousanimals

"Oh, son! Let there not be fear for you of demons the terrible ones performing cruel deeds, of devilish beings called Pisachas and of all carnivorous animals."

Sloka 18

plavagā vṛścikā daṃśā maśakāḥ caiva kānane || 2-25-18
sarī sṛpāḥ ca kīṭāḥ ca mā bhūvan gahane tava |

18. mā bhūvan = Let there notbe; plavagāḥ = monkeys; vṛihchikāḥ = scorpions; damshāḥ = gad-flies; mashakāshchaivaḥ = mosquitoes; sarīsṛipāshcha = snakes; kītāshcha = and other insects; tava = foryou; kānane = in the forest; gahane = which is impenetrable.

"In that impenetrable forest occupied by you, let there be no monkeys, scorpions, gad fies, mosquitoes, snakes or other insects ."

Sloka 19

mahā dvipāḥ ca simhāḥ ca vyāghrāṛkṣāḥ ca damṣṭriṇaḥ || 2-25-19

19. putraka = Oh; son!; mahādvipāshcha = Great ellphants; damshhṭriṇaḥ = with tusks; vyāghrāḥ = tigers; ṛikshhashcha = bears; raudraḥ = terrible mahishhāḥ = he-baffaloes; sṛiṇgiṇah = with horns; na druhyantu = be nothostile; te = to you.

"Oh, son! Let the great elephants , lions with tusks, tigers, bears, terrible he-buffaloes with horns not to be hostile to you.

Sloka 20

nṛ māṃsa bhojanā raudrā ye ca anye sattva jātayaḥ || 2-25-20

20. putra = Oh; son!; ye = which; anye = other; roudrāḥ = ferocious; nṛimāmsa bhojinaḥ = man-eaters; sattva jātayaḥ = belonging to animal species; sampūjitāḥ = worshipped; mayā = by me; iha = here; mācha himsishhuḥ = let them nothurt; tvām = you.

"Oh, son! Let the other ferocious man-eaters belonging to animal-species, being worshipped by me here, not hurt you."

Sloka 21

āgamāḥ te śivāḥ santu sidhyantu ca parākramāḥ || 2-25-21
sarva sampattayo rāma svastimān gacca putraka |

21. rāma = Oh; Rama; putraka = my son! te = your ; āgamāḥ = path-ways; santu = be; shivāh = propitious!; parākramāshcha = valour; siddhyantu = powerful ! gachchha = go; svastimān = having felicitousness; sarvasampattaye = with all accomplishments !

"Oh, Rama, my son! ṃay your path-ways be propitious ! Let your valour be powerful! Go well with all accomplishments!.

Sloka 22

svasti te astu āntarikṣebhyaḥ pārthivebhyaḥ punaḥ punaḥ || 2-25-22

22. astu = Let there be; svasti = security; te = to you ; antarikshhebhyaḥ = against those dwelling in theatmosphere ; punaḥ = and; sarvebhyaḥ = all; devebhyashchaiva = celestial beings; ye = who; paripanthinaḥ = the enemies; te = to you .

"Let there be security to you against those dwelling in the atmosphere and on the earth as well as against all the celestials as also your enemies."

Sloka 23

23. rāma = Oh; Rama! ; guruḥ = Venus; somashcha = moon; sūryashcha = sun; atha = and ; dhanadaḥ = Kubera; the god of riches; tathā = and; yamaḥ = Yama; the god of death ; architaḥ = = being worshipped; pāntu = may protect ; tvām = you; daṇdakāraṇya vāsinam = dwelling in the forest of Dandaka.

"Oh, Rama! ṃay Venus and the moon as also the sun and Kubera (the god of wealth) and Yama (the god of death) who are being worshipped by me, protect you, while you reside in the forest of Dandaka"

Sloka 24

agnirvāyustathā dhūmomantrāścarṣimukhāccyutāḥ || 2-25-24

24. raghunandana = Oh; Rama! mantrāshcha = ṭhe sacred hymns; chyutāḥ = coming forth from ; agniḥ = fire; vāyuḥ = air; tathā = and; dhūmaḥ = vapour; ṛishhi mukhāt = from the mouth of the singer of sacred hymns; pāntu = may protect; tvām = you; upasparshana kāletu = at the time of taking bath.

"Oh, Rama! ṃay sacred hymns coming forth from fire, air , vapour, and from the mouth of singer of sacred hymns protect you at the time of taking bath."

Sloka 25

sarva loka prabhur brahmā bhūta bhartā tathā ṛṣayaḥ || 2-25-25

25. sarvaloka prabhuḥ = ṣiva; the lord of all spheres; brahmā = brahma; the lord of creation; bhūta bhartā = Vishnu; the supporter of beings; tathā = and; Rishhayaḥ = the sages; sheshhāh = rest of; ye = which ; surāḥ = celestials; te = they too; rakshhantu = may protect; tvām = you; vanavāsinam = dwelling in the forest.

"Let Siva, the lord of all spheres, Brahma, the lord of creation, Vishnu, the nourisher of beings, the sages and the rest of the celestials may protect you dwelling in the forest.

Sloka 26

iti mālyaiḥ sura gaṇān gandhaiḥ ca api yaśasvinī || 2-25-26

Uttering thus, the illustrious Kausalya having large eyes, worshipped troops of divinities with garlands , sandal paste and befitting hymns of praise.

Sloka 27

Duly procuring fire, she caused oblation to be poured into it, as per the direction of a high souled brahmana for the purpose of Rama's well- being .

Sloka 28

ghṛtam śvetāni mālyāni samidhaḥ śvetasarṣapān || 2-25-28

28. kausalyā = Kausalya; paramāṇganā = the excellent woman; upasampādayāmāsa = procured together; ghṛitam = clarified butter; shvetāni = white; mālyāni = garlands; samidhaḥ = ceremonial sticks; svetha sarshhapān = white mustard seeds.

Kausalya, the excellent woman procured clarified butter , white garlands, ceremonial sticks and white mustard seeds.

Sloka 29

29. saḥ = ṭhat; upādhyāya = preceptor; shāntim hutvā = performed ceremony for peace; vidhinā = as per statute; anāmayam = without error; akalpayat = carried out; balim = propitiatory oblation ; bāhyam = externally; huta havyāvasheshheṇa = with the remaining sacrificial food ; left after the main offering.

That preceptor performed ceremony for peace as per the statute as well as without error and carried out propitiatory oblation externally with the remaining sacrificial food left after the main offering .

Sloka 30

She then got brahmanas to pronounce blessings with honey, curd , unbroken grains of rice and clarified butter by the repetition of certain hymns and to utter prayers for the well - being of Rama in the forest.

The illustrious Kausalya gave the desired fee to that chief of brahmanas and spoke as follows to Rama:-

Sloka 32

"Which felicity occurred in Devendra the thousand- eyed, adored by all celestials when a demon called Vritta was destroyed, let that felicity occur to you"

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

"While blessing was bestowed on Devendra, the wielder of the thunderbolt , killing the demons at the time of producing nectar, let that blessing come to you "

Sloka 35

"Oh, Rama! Which good fortune occurred to Vishnu who was immensely glorious while making his three strides (in order to measure the three worlds), let that good fortune come to you

Sloka 36

36. mahābāho = Oh; Rama the mighty armed!; ṛitavaḥ = ṭhe seasons; sāgarāḥ = the oceans; dvipāḥ = Dvipas; the principal divisions of the earth ; girdled by oceans; vedāḥ = vedas; lokāḥ = the spheres; dishashcha = the quarters; dishantu = may confer ; maṇgalām = blessings ; shubhamangalāḥ = with good luck; te = to you.

"Oh, Rama, the mighty armed! ṃay the seasons , oceans , Dvipas (principal division of earth girdled by oceans ), vedas, various spheres and quarters confer blessings with good luck to you."

Sloka 37

iti putrasya śeṣāśca kṛtvā śirasi bhāminī || 2-25-37
gandāṃścāpi samālabhya rāmamāyatalo canā |
oṣadhīm ca api siddha arthām viśalya karaṇīm śubhām || 2-25-38
cakāra rakṣām kausalyā mantraiḥ abhijajāpa ca |

37;38. iti = saying so; kausalya = Kausalya; āyatalochanāḥ = the large eyed; bhāminī = proud woman; kṛitvā = placed; sheshāshcha = unbroken rice grains; shirasi = on the head; putrasya = of her son; sammalabhya = smeared; gandhāmshchāpi = varieties of sandal pastes; rāmam = over Rama; cakāra = made; rakshhām = an amulet; oshhadhīm = with a herb; vishalyakaraṇi = named Vishalyakarani; siddharthām = which is efficacious; shubhām = auspicious; abhijajāphacha = and muttered; mantraiḥ = ṣacred hymns.

saying so, Kausalya, the large eyed proud woman placed some unbroken rice grains on her son's head, smeared varieties of sandal pastes over his body, fastened about his wrist by way of an amulet, a herb called Visalyakarani(so called because it helps in painlessly extracting an arrow stuck into one's body )which is efficacious and auspicious and muttered sacred hymns in order to enhance its virtue.

Sloka 39

uvācātiprahṛṣṭeva sā duḥkhavaśartinī || 2-25-39
vāṅmātreṇa na bhāvena vācā saṃsajjamānayā |

39. sā = ṣhe; dukhavashvartinī = though filled with grief; atiprahṛishhyeva = appeared like over -joyed; samsajjamānayāḥ = with faultering ; vachā = words; uvācha = spoke; vāṇmātreṇa = with only mouth; na = not; bhāvena = with heart.

Though filled with grief inside, she appeared to be over-joyed outside and with faltering words , spoke thus with only mouth and not with her heart.

Sloka 40

ānamya mūrdhni ca āghrāya pariṣvajya yaśasvinī || 2-25-40
avadat putra siddha artho gacca rāma yathā sukham |

40. yashashvinī = ṭhe illustrious Kausalya; ānamya = bent Rama lower; āghrāya = smelling mūrdhni = his head; parishhrajva = embraced; avadat = spoke thus; rāma = Rama; putra = my son! gachchha = = go; yathāsukham = with ease; siddhārtha = with accomplishment of purpose as you are.

The illusrious Kausalya embraced Rama, smelling his head by bending it and spoke these words "Rama, my son! Go with ease with accomplishment of purpose, as you are."

Sloka 41

arogam sarva siddha artham ayodhyām punar āgatam || 2-25-41
paśyāmi tvām sukham vatsa susthitam rāja veśmani |

41. vatsa = Oh; son; arogam = with health(without disease) sarva siddhhartham = with all your purposes accomplished; pushyāmi = I shall see; tvām = you; punaḥ = again; āgatam = coming; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; sukham = happily ; susthitam = well-established; rāja vartmani = in the path of a king

Oh, son! With all your purposes fulfilled and with good health, I shall see you returning to Ayodhya happily and taking over the reins of the kingdom

Sloka 42

praṇaṣṭaduḥkhasaṃkalpā harṣavidyotitānanā || 2-25-42
drakṣyāmi tvām vanātrpāptam pūrṇacandramivoditam |

42. tvām = You; prāptam = having come; vanāt = from forest; pūrṇachandramiva = like a full moon; uditam = rising ; praṇashhṭa duḥkha samkalpā = my thoughts of grief having been expulsed ; drakshhyāmi = I shall behold ; harshha vidyotitānanā = with my face shining with joy.

"All my thoughts of grief having been expulsed and my face shining with joy, I shall behold you returning from the forest like the full moon rising from the horizon"

Sloka 43

bhadrāsanagatam rāma vanavāsādihāgatam || 2-25-43
drakṣāmi ca punastvām tu tīrṇavantam piturvacaḥ |

43. rāma = Oḥ; Rama! tīrṇavantam = ḥaving fulfilled; pituḥ = the father's; vachaḥ = word; drakshhyāmi = I shall behold ; tvām = you; punaḥ āgatam = returning; iha = here; vanavāsāt = from so journ in the forest; bhadrāsanagatam = and sitting on the splendid throne.

"Oh, Rama! I shall behold you returning here from your sojourn in the forest, having fulfilled your father's word and installed on the splendid throne."

Sloka 44

maṅgaśairupasampanno vanavāsādihāgataḥ || 2-25-44
padhvā mama ca nityam tvam kāmān samvardha yāhi bhoḥ |

44. āgataḥ = ḥaving come; iha = here; vanavāsāt = from sojourn in the forest ; tvām = you; upasampannaḥ = will be familiar; maṇgalaiḥ = with auspicious things; samvardha = will be augmenting; nityam = ever; kāmān = the desires; vadhvāḥ = of ṣita the daughter -in-law; mama cha = and of me.yāhi = Depart; bhoḥ = Oh; Rama!

"Having come here from your sojourn in the forest, you will be familiar with auspicious things and will ever be augmenting the desires of my daughter-in-law and of me. Depart now, Oh, Rama !"

Sloka 45

mayā arcitā deva gaṇāḥ śiva ādayo |
maharṣayo bhūta mahā asura uragāḥ |
abhiprayātasya vanam cirāya te|
hitāni kānkṣantu diśaḥ ca rāghava || 2-25-45

45. rāghava = Oh; Rama! architā = Worshipped ; mayā = by me; devagaṇāḥ = let the hosts of gods ; shivādayaḥ = headed by Lord ṣiva ; the ṣupreme Being ; maharshhayaḥ = the great sages; bhūta mahāsuroragāḥ = spirits; the chief of evil spirits and the celestial serpants; dishashcha = the four quarters; kāṇkshhantu = wish; hitāni = welfare; te = of you; abhiprayātasya = who is setting forth; vanam = to forest.

"Oh, Rama! Worshipped by me, let the hosts of gods headed by Siva, the Supreme Being, the great sages, spirits, the chief of evil spirits, the celestial serpents and the (four)quarters wish well of you, who is setting forth to forest, "

Sloka 46

iti iva ca aśru pratipūrṇa locanā|
samāpya ca svastyayanam yathā vidhi |
pradakṣiṇam caiva cakāra rāghavam |
punaḥ punaḥ ca api nipīḍya sasvaje || 2-25-46

46. itīva = ḥaving so said; samāpyacha = and having completed ; svastyayanam = ṭhe rite of invoking blessings; yathāvidhi = befitting; ashruprati pūrṇa lochanā = Kausalya whose eyes were overflowingwith tears; pradakshhiṇam chakāra = further went clockwise ; rāghavam = around Rama; nipīdya = having pressed close to him ; shhasvaje = embraced ; punaḥ punaḥcha api = again and again.

Having so said and having completed the rite of invoking blessings befittingly, Kausalya whose eyes were overflowing with tears, further went clockwise around Rama and having pressed close to him, embraced again and again.

Sloka 47

tathā tu devyā sa kṛta pradakṣiṇo |
nipīḍya mātuḥ caraṇau punaḥ punaḥ |
jagāma sītā nilayam mahā yaśāḥ |
sa rāghavaḥ prajvalitaḥ svayā śriyā || 2-25-47

47. kṛita pradakshhiṇaḥ = ḥaving been done circumbulation(as a protection); tathā = thus; devyā = by the pious woman Kausalya; nipīda = pressing ; mātuḥ = mother's; charaṇau = feet; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; mahāshayā = the highly illustious; prajvalitaḥ = shining ; svayā = by his own ; shriyā = splendour; jagāma = went; sitānilayam = to ṣita's house .

Having been done circumbulation (as a mark of protection) thus by the pious woman Kausalya, that highly illustrious Rama pressing his mother's feet again and again , shining by his own splendour, went to Sita's house

- - -

|| iti rāmayane ayodhya kānda pancaviṃsaḥ sarga ||

Thus completes twenty fifth chapter of Ayodhya kanda in Srimad Ramayana.
