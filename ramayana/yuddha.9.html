Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 9
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sumantra was unable to console Kausalya, who lied on the floor due to her extreme grief over her separation from Rama, even though he tries to avert her grief by telling her that Rama can reside in the forest delightfully, by warding off his agony.

tato nikumbho rabhasaḥ sūrya śatrur mahābalaḥ |

agni ketuḥ ca durdharṣo raśmi ketuḥ ca rākṣasaḥ |

dhūmra akṣaḥ ca atikāyaḥ ca durmukhaḥ caiva rākṣasaḥ || 6-9-3

Then, Nikumbha, Rabhasa, the exceedingly strong Surya satru, Suptaghna, Yaj~akopa, ṃahaparsva and ṃahodara, the unconquerable demons Agniketu and Rasmiketu, then the powerful son of Ravana named Indrajit endowed with great energy, Prahasta and Virupaksha, Vajradamstra who was extraordinarily strong, Dhumarksha and Atikaya and the demon by name Durmukha, carrying iron clubs, sharp-edged spears, spikes, darts, javelins and axes, bows with excellent arrows and swords shining like a vast expanse of water, and who were all extremely enraged, and flaming as it were with glory, those demons rose up and spoke to Ravana (as follows).

Sloka 6

adya rāmam vadhiṣyāmaḥ sugrīvam ca salakṣmaṇam |
kṛpaṇam ca hanūmantam laṃkā yena pradharṣitā || 6-9-6

6. adya = now itself; vadhiṣyāmaḥ = we shall kill; rāmam = Rama; sahalakṣmaṇam = along with Lakshmana; sugrīvam ca = and ṣugreeva; kṛipaṇam = and the pitiable; hanūmantam = ḥanuma; yena = by whom; la.nkā = Lanka; pradharṣitā = was attacked.

"Now itself, we shall kill Rama along with Lakshmana and Sugreeva as well as the pitiable Hanuma by whom Lanka was attacked."

Sloka 7

tān gṛhīta āyudhān sarvān vārayitvā vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
abravīt prānjalir vākyam punaḥ pratyupaveśya tān || 6-9-7

7. vibhīṣaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; vārayitvā = restraining; tān sarvān = all those; gṛihīta āyudhān = who were made to seize weapons; pratyupaveshya = were made to sit again; abravīt = and spoke; vākyam = (these) words; prānjaliḥ = with joined palms.

Vibhishana (Ravana's youngest half-brother) restraining all those who were made to seize weapons, were made to sit again by saluting them with joined palms and spoke as follows:

Sloka 8

api upāyais tribhis tāta yo arthaḥ prāptum na śakyate |
tasya vikrama kālāṃs tān yuktān āhur manīṣiṇaḥ || 6-9-8

8. tāta = dear brother; yaḥ = which; arthaḥ = act; na shakyate = cannot; prāptum = be accomplished; tribhiḥ = by three; upāyais api = strategies too; tasya = that act; āhuḥ = is said; yuktān = to be suitable; nikramakālān = occasion for displaying prowess manīṣiṇaḥ = by wise men.

"Dear brother! That act which cannot be accomplished by three well-known strategies (viz . conciliation, gift and sowing dissention) , is only then to be accomplished by exhibiting prowess, as stated by wise men."

Sloka 9

pramatteṣv abhiyukteṣu daivena prahateṣu ca |
vikramās tāta sidhyanti parīkṣya vidhinā kṛtāḥ || 6-9-9

9. tāta = Oh ; brother; pramatteṣu = in respect of indifferent people; abhiyukteṣhu = in those who have been attacked by an enemy; prahateṣu ca = in those who were struck down; daivena = by fate; vikramāḥ = the gallantries; kṛitaḥ = performed; vidhinā = according to precept; parīkṣya = after being examined carefully; sidhyanti = succeed.

"The gallantries performed according to the prescribed precept, after being tested, O dear brother, only succeed against those who are indifferent, who have been already attacked by an enemy or who stand struck down by fate."

Sloka 10

apramattam katham tam tu vijigīṣum bale sthitam |
jita roṣam durādharṣam pradharṣayitum iccatha || 6-9-10

10. katham = how; icchatha = do you wish; dharṣayitum = to attack; tam = that famous; tam = Rama; apramattam = who is attentive; vijigīṣum = who has subdued his anger; durādharṣam = and who is difficult to be conquered.

"How do you wish to attack that famous Rama, who is always attentive, who has a will to conquer, who is established in strength, who has subdued his anger and who is difficult to be conquered."

Sloka 11

samudram langhayitvā tu ghoram nada nadī patim |
kṛtam hanumatā karma duṣkaram tarkayeta kaḥ || 6-9-11

11. kaḥ = who; vidyāt = can know; tarkayeta vā = or even imagine; loke = in the world; hanumataḥ = ḥanuma's; gatim = speed; langhayitvā = in crossing; ghoram = the formidable; samudram = ocean; nada nadī patim = the lord of streams and rivers?

"Who can conceive or even imagine in this world, that Hanuma's speed in crossing the formidable ocean, which is the lord of streams and rivers?"

Sloka 12

balāni aparimeyāni vīryāṇi ca niśā carāḥ |
pareṣām sahasā avajñā na kartavyā kathaṃcana || 6-9-12

12. nishācarāḥ = O; demons; balāni = the strength; vīryāṇi ca = and the abilities; pareṣām = of the enemies; aparimeyāni = are difficult to be measured; na kartavyā = they cannot be treated; avajJṇyā = with contempt; sahasā = hastily; kathamcana = by any means.

"O, demons! The strength and the abilities of the enemies are difficult to be measured. They cannot be treated with contempt hastily by any means."

Sloka 13

kim ca rākṣasa rājasya rāmeṇa apakṛtam purā |
ājahāra jana sthānād yasya bhāryām yaśasvinaḥ || 6-9-13

13. kim = what; apakṛitam = offence (was done); purā = earlier; rākṣasarājasya = to the king of demons; yashashvinaḥ = by the illustrious; rāmeṇa = Rama; yasya = whose; bhāryām = wife; ājahāra = (he) bore away; janasthānāt = firm janasthana?"

"What offence was done earlier to the king of demons by the illustrious Rama, whose wife that he bore away from Janasthana?"

Sloka 14

kharo yadi ativṛttas tu rāmeṇa nihato raṇe |
avaśyam prāṇinām prāṇā rakṣitavyā yathā balam || 6-9-14

14. kharaḥ = Khara; ativṛittaḥ = who has gone beyond his limits; nihataḥ yadi = was of course killed; rāmeṇa = by Rama; raṇe = in an encounter; prāṇāḥ = lives; avashyam = certainly; rakṣitavyāḥ = are to be protected; yathā balam = according to one's own strength; prāṇinām = by living beings."

"Khara, who has gone beyond limits, was of course killed by Rama in an encounter. Lives are certainly to be protected, according to one's own strength, by living beings."

Sloka 15

etan nimittam vaidehī bhayam naḥ sumahad bhavet |
āhṛtā sā parityājyā kalaha arthe kRte na kim || 6-9-15

15. etan nimittam = for this reason; sumahat = a very great; vaidehī bhayam = danger because of ṣeetha; bhavet = will occur; naḥ = to us; sā = she; āhṛitā = who was brought; parityājyā = is to be given up; kim = what is the use; kṛite na = in doing anything; kalaha arthe = with an animosity as an end?.

"For this reason, a very great danger because of Seetha will occur to us. She, who was brought here, is to be given away. What is the use in doing anything, with an animosity as an end?"

Sloka 16

na naḥ kṣamam vīryavatā tena dharma anuvartinā |
vairam nirarthakam kartum dīyatām asya maithilī || 6-9-16

16. na naḥ kṣamam = It is not appropriate; kartum = to make; vairam = enmity; nirarthakam = uselessly; tena = with Rama; vīryavatā = who is valiant; dharma anuvartinā = and who follows; righteousness = maithilī = (let) ṣeetha; dīyatām = be given away; asya = to him.

"It is not appropriate to make enmity uselessly with Rama, who is valiant and who follows righteousness. Let Seetha be given away to him."

Sloka 17

yāvan sagajām sāśvām bahu ratna samākulām |
purīm dārayate bāṇair dīyatām asya maithilī || 6-9-17

17. yāvan = before; purīm = the city of Lanka; sagajām = along with elephants; sāshvām = with horses; bahu ratna samākulām = and filled with many riches; dārayate = are shattered; maithilī = (let) ṣeetha; dīyatām = be given away; asya = to him.

Before the City of Lanka with its elephants, horses and many riches are shattered by arrows, let Seetha be given away to him."

Sloka 18

yāvat sughorā mahatī durdharṣā hari vāhinī |
na avaskandati no laṃkām tāvat sītā pradīyatām || 6-9-18

18. na yāvat tāvat = not so long as; mahatī = the huge; harivāhinī = army of monkeys; sughorā = which is very dangerous; durdharṣā = and unconquerable; nāvaskandati = attacks; naḥ = our; la.nkām = Lanka; sītā = (let) ṣeetha; pradīyatām = be given away.

"Not so long as the huge army of monkeys which is very dangerous and unconquerable attacks our Lanka, let Seetha be given away."

Sloka 19

vinaśyedd hi purī laṃkā śūrāḥ sarve ca ākṣasāḥ |
rāmasya dayitā patnī na svayam yadi dīyate || 6-9-19

19. dayitā = (If) the beloved; patnī = wife; rāmasya = of Rama; na dīyate yadi = is not given away; svayam = of your own accord; la.nkā purī = the City of Lanka; vinshyet hi = will indeed; perish; sarve = all; shūrāḥ = the valiant; rākṣasāshca = demons too(will perish).

"If the beloved wife of Rama is not given away of your own accord, the city of Lanka will indeed perish. All our valiant demons too will Perish."

Sloka 20

prasādaye tvām bandhutvāt kuruṣva vacanam mama |
hitam pathyam tv aham brūmi dīyatām asya maithilī || 6-9-20

20. prasādaye = I am propitiating; tvām = you; bandhutvāt = because of my relationship; kuruṣva = act on; mama = my; vachanam = words; aham = I; brūmi = am telling; hitam = the beneficial; tvaham tu = and the truthful (word); maithilī = (let) ṣeetha; dīyatām = be given away; asya = to him.

"I am propitiating you because of my relationship. Act on my words. I am telling you beneficial as well as truthful word. Let Seetha be given away to him."

Sloka 21

purā śarat sūrya marīcci samnibhān |
nava agra punkhān sudṛḍhān nṛpa ātmajaḥ |
sṛjati amoghān viśikhān vadhāya te |
pradīyatām dāśarathāya maithilī || 6-9-21

21. purā = before; nṛipa ātmajaḥ = Rama the prince; sṛijati = discharges; te vadhāya te = for your destruction; sudṛiDhān = very strong; vishikhān = arrows; sharat sūrya marīcci samnibhān = equal to the rays of autumnal sun; nava agra punkhān- provided with new heads and shafts; amoghān = which are unfailing; maithilī = (let) ṣeetha; pradīyatām = be given away; dāsharathāya = to Rama.

"Before Rama the prince discharges for your destruction, very strong and unfailing arrows equal to the rays of autumnal sun and provided with new heads and shafts, let Seetha be given away to Rama."

Sloka 22

tyajasva kopam sukha dharma nāśanam |
bhajasva dharmam rati kīrti vardhanam |
prasīda jīvema saputra bāndhavāḥ |
pradīyatām dāśarathāya maithilī || 6-9-22

22. tyaja = abandon; āshu = soon; kopam = anger; sukha dharma nāshanam = which destroys; happiness and piety; bhajasva = resort to; dharmam = righteousness; rati kīrti vardhanam = which augments joy and fame; prasīda = become placid; jīvema = we shall live; saputra bāndhavāḥ = with our sons and relatives; maithilī = (let) ṣeetha; pradīyatām = be given away; dāsharathāya = to Rama.

"Abandon soon your anger, which destroys happiness and piety. Resort to righteousness, which augments joy and fame. Become placid so that we may live with our sons and relatives. Let Seetha be given away to Rama."

Sloka 23

vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā rāvaṇo rākṣaseśvaraḥ |
visarjayitvā tān sarvān praiveśa svakam gṛham || 6-9-23

23. shrutvā = having; vibhīṣaṇaḥ vachaḥ = Vibhishana's words; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣaseshvaraḥ = the king of demons; visarjayitvā = left; sarvān = all; tān = of them; pravivesha = and entered; svakam = his own; gṛiham = house.

After hearing Vibhishana's words, Ravana the king of demons left all of them and entered his own palace.

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmayaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe navamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 9th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
