Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 109
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana laments a lot, after seeing Ravana lying dead on the battle-field. Rama comforts him, saying that a warrior killed in battle, need not be mourned. Vibhishana describes the personality of Ravana and his qualities to Rama and seeks permission of Rama to perform funeral rites to Ravana. Rama directs Vibhishana to perform the obsequies to Ravana, his deceased brother.

Sloka 1

bhrātaraṃ nihataṃ dṛṣṭvā śayānaṃ nirjitaṃ raṇe |
śokavegaparītātmā vilalāpa vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-109-1

1. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; bhrātaram = his brother; shayānam = lying down; nihatam = dead; nirjitam = after defeat; raṇe = in battle; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; vilalāpa = lamented; shokavega parītātmā = with his mind filled with an outburst of sorrow.

Seeing his brother lying down dead after defeat in battle, Vibhishana lamented, with his mind filled with an outburst of sorrow:

Sloka 2

vīra vikrānta vikhyāta pravīṇa nayakovida |
mahārhaśayanopeta kiṃ śeṣe nihato bhuvi || 6-109-2

2. kim = why; sheṣhe = are you lying; nihataḥ = killed; bhuvi = on the ground?; vīra = O hero!; vikrānta = the valiant one!; vikhyāta = the celebrated one!; pravīṇa = O the skillful one; nayakovida = prudent in polity!; mahārhashayanopeta = though the highly worthy of turning towards lofty beds.

"Why are you lying killed on the ground, though you are highly worthy of turning towards lofty beds, O hero! The valiant one, the celebrated one, prudent in polity?"

Sloka 3

nikṣipya dīrghau niśceṣṭau bhujāvaṅgadabhūṣitau |
mukuṭenāpavṛttena bhāskarākāravarcasā || 6-109-3

3. dirghau bhuhau nikṣhipya = having thrown about your two long arms; aṇ^gada bhūṣhitau = which though decked with armlets; nishcheṣhṭau = are (now) motionless; mukuṭena = and with your diadem; bhāskākāra varchasā = brilliant as the sun; apavṛittena = knocked down; (why are you lying killed on the ground?)

"Having thrown about your two long arms which though decked wit armlets, are now motionless and with your diadem brilliant as the sun, knocked down; why are you lying killed on the ground/"

Sloka 4

tadidaṃ vīra saṃprāptaṃ yanmayā pūrvamīritam |
kāmamohaparītasya yattnna rucitaṃ tava || 6-109-4

4. vīra = O the valiat brother!; yat = that which; īritam = was told; mayā = by me; pūrvam = earlier; yat = and that; na ruchitan = which was not liked; tava = by you; kāma moha parītasya = overcome as you were with sensuality and infatuation; tat idam = that very fate; prāptam = has been obtained (by you).

"O the valiant brother! That which was told by me earlier and that which was not liked by you, as you were overcome with sensuality and infatuation; that very fate has been obtained by you."

Sloka 5

yanna darpāt prahasto vā nendrajinnāpare janāḥ |
na kumbhakarṇo.atiratho nātikāyo narāntakaḥ || 6-109-5
na svayaṃ tvamamanyethāstasyodarko.ayamāgataḥ |

5. yat = for that which; darpāt = because of arrogance; na prahato vā = netither prahasta; indrajit na apare janāḥ = nor Indrajit and others; na kumbhakarṇaḥ = nor kumbhakarna; atirathaḥ = nor Atiratha; atikāyaḥ = nr Atikaya; na narāntakaḥ = nor ṇarantaka; na tvam svayam = nor you yourself; amanyethāḥ = has agreed; tasya ayan udarkaḥ = this consequence of it; āgataḥ = has come.

"For that which, because of arrogance, neither Prahasta nor Indrajit and others, nor Kumbhakarna nor Atiratha nor Narantaka nor you yourself has agreed to my counsel, the consequence of it has come now."

Sloka 6

gataḥ setuḥ sunītīnāṃ gato dharmasya vigrahaḥ || 6-109-6
gataḥ sattvasya saṃkṣepah prastāvānāṃ gatirgatā |
ādityaḥ patito bhūmau magnastamasi candramāḥ || 6-109-7

6-7. asmin vīre = as this hero; vare = the foremost; shastra bhṛitām = among those who wield weapons; nipatite = has fallen; bhūmau = on the ground setuḥ = the established rule; sunītīnām = of well-conducted persons; gataḥ = has gone; vigrahaḥ = the incarnation; dharmasya = of virtue; gataḥ = has departed; samkṣhepaḥ = the epitome; sattvasya = of strength; gataḥ = has gone; gatiḥ = the refuge; prastāvānām = enlogies; gatā = has gone out of sight; ādityaḥ = the sun; patitaḥ = has fallen; bhūmau = to the earth; chandramāḥ = the moon; magnaḥ = has merged; tamasi = in darkness; chitrabhānuḥ = fire; prashāntārchiḥ = has extinguished its flames; vyavasāyaḥ = and a strennous; nirudyamaḥ = has become inactive.

"As this hero, the foremost of those who wield the weapons, has fallen on the ground, the established rule of well-conducted persons has gone. The incarnation of virtue has departed. The epitome of strength has gone. The refuge of enlogies has gone out of sight. The sun has fallen to the earth. The moon has merged in darkness. Fire has extinguished its flames and a strennous effort has become inactive."

Sloka 8

citrabhānuḥ praśāntārcirvyavasāyo nirudyamaḥ |
asminnipatite vīre bhūmau śastrabhṛtāṃ vare || 6-109-8

8. kim = what; sheṣham = is remaining; lokasya = in this world; iha = now; rākṣhasashārdūle = while Ravana the foremost among demons; samprati = is now; prasupte iva = lying fast asleep; pāmsuṣhu = in the dust?

"What is remaining in this world now, while Ravana the foremost of demons, is at present lying fast asleep in the dust?"

Sloka 9

kiṃ śeṣamiha lokasya gatasttvasya saṃprati |
raṇe rākṣasaśārdūle prasupta iva pāṃsuṣu || 6-109-9

9. dṛitipravālaḥ = with firmness for its shoot; prasahāgryapuṣpaḥ = endurance for its excellent bloossom; tapabalaḥ = asceticism for its strength; shauryanibaddha mūlaḥ = and valour for its firm root; mahān rākṣhasarāja vṛikṣhaḥ = the large tree in the shape of Ravana; sammarditaḥ = has been crsuhed; raṇe = in the battle-field; rāghava mārutena = by the tempest in the shape of Rama.

"With firmness for its shoot, endurance for its excellent blossom, asceticism for its strength, and valour for its firm root, the large tree in the shape of Ravana has been crushed in the battle-field, by the tempest in the shape of Rama."

Sloka 10

dhṛtipravālaḥ prasahāgryapuṣpa |
stapobalaḥ śauryanibaddhamūlaḥ |
raṇe mahān rākṣasarājavṛkṣaḥ |
saṃmardito rāghavamārutena || 6-109-10

10. tejoviṣhaṇaḥ = with sharpness for its tusks; kula vamshavamshaḥ = the line of ancesters for its back-bone; kopa prasādāpara gātrahastaḥ = anger for its lower parts and graciousness for its proboscis; rāvaṇagandhahastī = the elephant in rut in the shape of suptaḥ = is lying asleep; kṣhitau = on the ground; ikṣhvāku simhāvagṛihīta dehaḥ = its body having been overthrown by a lion in the shape of Rama.

"With sharpness for its tusks, the line of ancestors for its back-bone, anger for its lower parts and graciousness for its proboscis, the elephant in rut in the shape of Ravana is lying asleep on the ground, its body having been overthrown by a lion in the shape of Rama."

Sloka 11

tejoviṣāṇaḥ kulavaṃśavaṃśaḥ |
kopaprasādāparagātrahastaḥ |
ikṣvākusiṃhāvagṛhītadehaḥ |
suptaḥ kṣitau rāvaṇagandhahastī || 6-109-11

11. parākramotsāhavijṛibhitārchiḥ = with prowess and power for its expanded flames; niḥshvāsadhūmaḥ = sighs for its smoke; svabalapratāpaḥ = and his native strength for its glowing heat; agniḥ pratāpavān = the blazing fire; rākṣhasah = in the shape of Ravana; the demon; nirvāpitaḥ = has been extinguished; samyati = in the battle0field; rāmapayodhareṇa = by the rainy cloud in the shape of Rama.

"With prowess and power for its expanded flames, sighs for its smoke and his native strength for its glowing heat, the blazing fire in the shape of Ravana the demon has been extingushed in the battle-field by the rainy cloud in the shape of Rama."

Sloka 12

parākramotsāhavijṛmbhitārci |
rniḥśvāsadhūmaḥ svabalapratāpaḥ |
pratāpavān saṃyati rākṣasāgni |
rnirvāpito rāmapayodhareṇa || 6-109-12

12. simharkṣha lāṇ^gūlaka kudviṣhaṇaḥ = with the demons for its tail; hump and horns; chāpalakarṇa chakṣhuḥ = and fickleness for its ears and eyes; rakṣho vṛiṣhabhaḥ = the bull in the shape of Ravana; the demon; parābhijit = the conqueror of its enemies; gandhanagandha vāha = which vied with the wind in energy; avasannaḥ = is lying dead; kṣhitīshvaravyāghra hataḥ = struck down by a tiger in the shape of Rama; the ruler of the earth.

"With the demons for its tail; hump and horn and fickleness for its ears and eyes, the bull in the shape of Ravana the demon, the conqueror of its enemies, which vied with the wind in energy, is lying dead, struck down by a tiger in the shape of Rama, the ruler of the earth."

Sloka 13

siṃharkṣalāṅgūlakakudviṣāṇaḥ |
parābhijidgandhanagandhahastī |
rakṣovṛṣaścāpalakarṇacakṣuḥ |
kṣitīśvaravyāgrahato.avasannaḥ || 6-109-13

13. vibhīṣhaṇam = to Vibhishana; vadantam = who was speaking thus; shoka samāviṣhṭam = enveloped in sorrow; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; iti vākyam = the following words; hetumat = full of reason; paridṛiṣhṭārthanishchayam = and which revealed his determined view of the matter.

To Vibhishana, who was thus speaking; enveloped in sorrow, Rama spoke the following words, full of reason and which revealed his determined view of the matter.

Sloka 14

vadantaṃ hetumadvākyaṃ paridṛṣṭarthaniścayam |
rāmaḥ śokasamāviṣṭamityuvāca vibhīṣaṇam || 6-109-14

14. ayam = ḥe (Ravana); na vinaṣhṭaḥ = did not die; samare = in battle; nishcheṣhṭaḥ = without making an effort; ayam patitaḥ = ḥe has fallen (in combat); chaṇ^Da vikramaḥ = eventhough he was endowed with terrible prowess; atyunnatamahotsāhaḥ = and exhibited extra ordinary enthusiasm of a very exalted type; ashaṇkitaḥ = and remained confident (throughout).

"Ravana did not die in battle, without making an effort. He has fallen in combat, eventhough he was endowed with terrible prowess and exhibited extra ordinary enthusiasm of a very exalted type and remained confident throughout."

Sloka 15

nāyaṃ vinaṣṭo niśceṣṭaḥ samare caṇḍavikramaḥ |
atyunnatamahotsāhaḥ patito.ayamaśaṅkitaḥ || 6-109-15

15. evam na shochyante = there is no occasion to grieve; nipatanti vinaṣhṭāḥ = raṇājire = for him having fallen into death in the battle-field; ye vṛiddhim āshamsamānāḥ = by whom the development was wished for; kṣhatra dharma vyavasthitāḥ = while remaining steadfast in the duty of kshatriya; the warrior.

"There is no occasion to grieve for him having fallen into death in the battle-field and by whom the development of his country was wished for, while remaining steadfast in the duty of Kshatriya the warrior."

Sloka 16

naivaṃ vinaṣṭāḥ śocyante kṣatradharmavyavasthitāḥ |
vṛddhimāśaṃsamānā ye nipatanti raṇājire || 6-109-16

16. na kālaḥ = there is no occasion; parishochitum = to grieve; tasmin kāla samāyukta = for his having been brought under the sway of death; yudhi = in battle; yena = by whom; dhīmatā = intelligent as he was; trayaḥ lokāḥ = all the three worlds; sendrāḥ = including Indra; trāsitāḥ = were frightened.

"There is no occasion to grieve for his having been brought under the sway of death by whom intelligent as he was, all the three worlds including indra were frightened."

Sloka 17

yena sendrāstrayo lokāstrāsitā yudhi dhīmatā |
tasmin kālasamāyukte na kālaḥ pariśocitum || 6-109-17

17. bhūta pūrvaḥ = in the past; na = none; kadāchana ekānta vijayaḥ = has ever been exclusively victorius; yuddhe = in a combat; vīraḥ = A hero; hanyateṇā = has either been killed; paraiḥ = by his adversaries; hanti vā = or has killed; parān = the enemies; samyuge = in battle.

"In the past, none has ever been exclusively victorious in a combat. A hero either has been killed by his adversaries or had killed the enemies in battle."

Sloka 18

naikāntavijayo yuddhe bhūtapūrvaḥ kadācana |
parairvā hanyate vīraḥ parānvā hanti saṃyuge || 6-109-18

18. iyam gatiḥ = such is the destiny; samdiṣhṭā = proclaimed; pūrvaiḥ = by the ancients; kṣhatriyasammatā = as highly esteemed for a warrior; kṣhatriyaḥ = a warrior; nihataḥ = killed; samkhye = in battle; na shochyaḥ = does not deserve to be mourned; iti nishchayaḥ = such is the ascertainment of the sacred scriptures.

"Such is the destiny proclaimed by the ancients, as highly esteemed for a warrior. A warrior killed in battle, does not deserve to be mourned. Such is the ascertainment of the sacred scriptures."

Sloka 19

iyaṃ hi pūrvaiḥ saṃdiṣṭā gatiḥ kṣatriyasaṃmatā |
kṣatriyo nihataḥ saṃkhye na śocya iti niścayaḥ || 6-109-19

19. tat = therefore; dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; evam nishchayam = this ascertainment; āsthāya tattvam = understanding the true principle; vijvaraḥ = and free from grief; iha anuchintaya = think here of; yat = what; kāryam = duty; tat kalpyam = that deserves to be performed; anantaram = thereafter.

"Therefore, seeing this ascertainment, understanding the true principle, and free from grief, think here of what duty that deserves to be performed."

Sloka 20

tadevaṃ niścayaṃ dṛṣṭvā tattvamāsthāya vijvaraḥ |
yadihānantaraṃ kāryaṃ kalpyaṃ tadanucintaya || 6-109-20

20. vikrāntam tam rājaputram = to that valiant prince; Rama; uktavākyam = by whom the aforesaid words were spoken; vibhīṣhaṇah = Vibhishana; shoka samtaptaḥ = who was tormented with grief; uvācha = spoke; hitam = about the suitable action; anantaram = to be done next; bhrātuḥ = in relation to his brother.

To that valiant prince (Rama) by whom the aforesaid words were spoken, Vibhishana, who was tormented with grief, spoke (as follows) about the suitable action to be done next in relation to his brother.

Sloka 21

yo.ayaṃ vimardeṣvavibhagnapūrvaḥ |
suraiḥ samastairapi vāsavena |
bhavantamāsādya raṇe vibhagno |
velāmivāsādya yathā samudraḥ || 6-109-21

21. yaḥ ayam = this demon who; avibhagna pūrvaḥ = had never been conquered before; vimardeṣhu = in battle; samastaiḥ surairapi = by even all the gods combined; vāsavena = or by Indra himself; vibhagnaḥ = has been conquered; āsādya = on confronting; bhavantam = you; raṇe = in the battle-field; samudraḥ yathā = as the sea; (breaks up); āsādya = on reaching; velām = the shore.

"The demon, who had never been conquered before in battles, by even all the gods combined or by Indra himself, has been conquered, on confronting you in the battle-field, as the sea breaks up, on reaching the shore."

Sloka 22

anena dattāni vanīpakeṣu |
bhuktāśca bhogā nibhṛtāśca bhṛtyāḥ |
dhanāni mitreṣu samarpitāni |
vairāṇyamitreṣu nipātitāni || 6-109-22

22. anena = by him; dattāni = gifts were endowed; vanīpakeṣhu = to mendicants; bhogāshcha = pleasures too; bhuktāḥ = were enjoyed; bhṛityāḥ = the kings servents; nibhṛitāḥ = were fully maintained; dhānāni = riches; samarpitāni = were made over; mitreṣhu = to friends; vairāṇi = grudges; amitreṣhu = against enemies; nipātitāni = were revenged.

"By him, gifts were endowed to to mendicents. Pleasures too were enjoyed. The king's servents were fully maintained. Rches were made over to friends. Grudges against enemīes were revenged."

Sloka 23

eṣohitāgniśca mahātapāśca |
vedantagaḥ karmasu cāgryaśūraḥ |
etasya yatpretagatasya kṛtyaṃ |
tatkartumicchāmi tava prasādāt || 6-109-23

23. eṣhaḥ = he; hitāgniḥ = (maintained) perpetually sacred fire; mahātapāshcha = ḥe practised great religious austerities; vedāntagaḥ = ḥe completely mastered the Vedas; the sacred scriptures; agrashūraḥ = he was highly proficient; karmasucha = in even the ritual acts; ichchhāmi = i desire; kartum = to do; yat = that; tava prasādāt = with your graciousness which; kṛitya = is to be done; pretagatasya etasya = to him; who has departed to the other world.

"He maintained a perpetually sacred fire. he practised great religious austerities. He completely mastered Vedas, the sacred scriptures. He was highly proficient even in the ritual acts. I desire to do, with you graciousness, that which is to be performed to him, who has departed to the other world."

Sloka 24

sa tasya vākyaiḥ karuṇairmahātmā |
saṃbodhitaḥ sādhu vibhīṣaṇena |
ājñāpayāmāsa narendrasūnuḥ |
svargīyamādhānamadīnasattvaḥ || 6-109-24

24. sādhu sambodhitaḥ = thus getting the personality of Ravana well acquainted; vibhiṣhaṇena = by Vibhishana; tasya karuṇaiḥ vakyaiḥ = by his compassionate words; saḥ = that; narendrasūḥ = Rama; adīna sattvaḥ = possessing unimpaired goodness; āJṇāpayāmāsa = directed; ādhānam = to perform funeral rites; svargīyam = which were intended to lead the departed soul to heaven.

Thus getting the personality of Ravana well-acquainted by vibhishana by his compassionate words, Rama possessing unimpaired goodness, directed Vibhishana to perfom funeral rites, which were intended to lead the departed soul to heaven:

Sloka 25

maraṇāntāni vairāṇi nirvṛttaṃ naḥ prayojanam |
kriyatāmasya saṃskāro mamāpyeṣa yathā tava || 6-109-25

25. vairāni = hostilities; maraṇāntāni = end with death; naḥ = our; prayojanam = purpose; nirvṛittam = has been accomplished; asya samskāraḥ = let his funeral rites; kriyatām = be performed; eṣhaḥ = he; mamāpi = is as good mine even; yathā tava = as yours.

"Hostilities end with death. Our purpose has been accomplished. Let his funeral rites be performed. He is even as good mine, as yours."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe navādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 109th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
