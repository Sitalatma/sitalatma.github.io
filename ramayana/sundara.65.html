Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 65
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Angada, Hanuma and other monkeys approach Rama, Lakshmana and Sugreeva at ṃount Prasravana. When monkeys start narrating the news of Seetha, Rama asks further details of Seetha. Then, the monkeys request Hanuma to explain the occurrences to Rama in detail. Then, Hanuma appraises Rama of Seetha's presence at the foot of a Simsupa tree in the midst of female-demons. After narrating the incidents related by her and delivering to Rama the jewel for the head, earlier handed-over by Seetha as a token, Hanuma conveys her message to Rama.


Sloka 1

tataḥ prasravaṇam śailam te gatvā citra kānanam |
praṇamya śirasā rāmam lakṣmaṇam ca mahābalam || 5-65-1
yuva rājam puraḥ kṛtya sugrīvam abhivādya ca |
pravṛttam atha sītāyāḥ pravaktum upacakramuḥ || 5-65-2

1; 2. puraḥ kṛitya yuva rājan = keeping Angada the prince; in their front; te = those monkeys; tataḥ = then; gatvā = going; prasravaṇam shailam = to ṃount Prasravana; chitra kānanam- with forests of variegated appearance; praṇamya = offering their salutation; shirasā = by bowing their heads; rāmam = to Rama; mahābalam lakṣmaṇamcha = and the mighty Lakshmana; abhivādya cha = and respectfully saluting; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; atha = and then; upachakramaḥ = started; pravaktum = to narrate; pravṛittim = the news; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha.

Keeping Angada the prince in their forefront those monkeys, on reaching ṃount Prasravana having forests of variegated appearance, offered their salutation by bowing their heads in reverence to Rama and the mighty Lakshmana, greeting Sugreeva respectfully. Then, they started narrating the news of Seetha.

Sloka 3

rāvaṇa antaḥ pure rodham rākṣasībhiḥ ca tarjanam |
rāme samanurāgam ca yaḥ ca api samayaḥ kṛtaḥ || 5-65-3
etat ākhyānti te sarve harayo rāma samnidhau |

3. sarve = all; te harayaḥ = those monkeys; ākhyānti = were narrating; rāme samnidhau = in the presence of Rama; etat = all this; rodham = about restraining (ṣeetha); rāvaṇa antaḥ pure = in Ravana's gynaecium; rākṣhasībhiḥ = by the female-demons; tarjanam cha = frightening her; samanurāgamcha = and her complete affection; rāme = towards Rama; yaḥ ayam samayaḥ kṛitaḥ = and the time-limit (two months) given by Ravana.

ṭhose monkeys started to narrate, in the presence of Rama, about, the female-demons, and frightening Seetha in Ravana's gynaecium, her complete affection towards Rama and the time-limit of two months given by Ravana.

Sloka 4

vaidehīm akṣatām śrutvā rāmaḥ tu uttaram abravīt || 5-65-4
kva sītā vartate devī katham ca mayi vartate |
etan me sarvam ākhyāta vaidehīm prati vānarāḥ || 5-65-5

4; 5. shrutvā = hearing vaidehīm = of ṣeetha; akṣhatām = being alive; rāmatu = Rama on his part; uttaram abravīt = replied; kva = where; vartate = is; devī sītā = ṣeetha; the princess?; katham = how; vartate = is she disposed; mayi = towards me? vānarāḥ = O monkeys!; ākhyāta = tell; me = me; sarvam = all; etat = this; vaidehīm prati = about ṣeetha.

ḥearing of ṣeetha being alive, Rama on his part replied, "Where is ṣeetha, the princess? How is she disposed towards me? O monkeys! Tell me all that about Seetha."

Sloka 6

rāmasya gaditam śrutva harayo rāma samnidhau |
codayanti hanūmantam sītā vṛtta anta kovidam || 5-65-6

6. shrutvā = hearing; gaditam = the words; rāmasya = of Rama; harayaḥ = the monkeys; chodayanti hanumantam = urged ḥanuma to tell; rāma sannidhau = in the presence of Rama; sītā vṛitta anta kovidam = (since) ḥanuma knows all the incidents about ṣeetha.

ḥearing the words of Rama, the monkeys urged ḥanuma to tell, in the presence of Rama, all the incidents because he knew them better, about Seetha.

Sloka 7

śrutvā tu vacanam teṣām hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
praṇamya śirasā devyai sītāyai tām diśam prati || 5-65-7
uvāca vākyam vākyajñaḥ sītāyā darśanam yathā |

7. shrutvā = hearing; teṣhām vachanam = their words; vākyajJṇaḥ = eloquent; hanumān = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; praṇamya = saluting; shirasā = with his head; tām disham prati = towards that direction; devyai sītāyai = of ṣeetha; the princess; uvācha = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; yathā sītāyāḥ darshanam = how ṣeetha's seeing occurred.

ḥearing the words of those monkeys, the eloquent ḥanuma, the son of wind-god saluting towards the direction of the Seetha the princess by bowing his head, spoke the following words, explaining how he saw Seetha.

Sloka 8

samudram langhayitvā aham śata yojanam āyatam || 5-65-8
agaccham jānakīm sītām mārgamāṇo didṛkṣayā |

8. didṛikṣayā = with a wish to see ṣeetha; aham = I; āgachchham = went; laṇ^ghayitvā = crossing; samudram = the ocean; shata yojanam āyatam = of a width of one hundred Yojanas; mārgamāṇaḥ = searching; sītām = ṣeetha; jānakīm = the daughter of Janaka.

"ḥaving gone across the ocean, a hundred Yojanas wide, I reached the southern shore looking in search of Seetha, Janaka's daughter, with a wish to see her."

Sloka 9

tatra lankā iti nagarī rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 5-65-9
dakṣiṇasya samudrasya tīre vasati dakṣiṇe |

9. tatra = there; dakṣiṇa tīre = at the southern shore; dakṣiṇasya samudrasya = of the southern ocean; vasati = there is; nagarī = a city; laṇketi = called Lanka; durātmanaḥ rāvaṇasya = of the evil-minded Ravana.

"ṭhere at the southern shore of the southern ocean, there is city called Lanka of the evil-minded Ravana."

Sloka 10

tatra dṛṣṭā mayā sītā rāvaṇa antaḥ pure satī || 5-65-10
samnyasya tvayi jīvantī rāmā rāma mano ratham |

10. rāma = O; Rama!; tatra = there; dṛiṣṭā = it was seen; mayā = by me; rāvaṇāntaḥ pure = in Ravana's gynaecium; sītā = ṣeetha; rāmā = the lady; satī = and the virtuous wife; jīvantī = living; samnyasya = placing; mano ratham = heart's desire; tvayi = in you.

"O Rama! ṭhere, living in Ravana's gynaecium in the city, I saw the lady ṣeetha, your virtuous wife, centering here heart's desire in you."

Sloka 11

dṛṣṭā me rākṣasī madhye tarjyamānā muhuḥ muhuḥ || 5-65-11
rākṣasībhiḥ virūpābhī rakṣitā pramadā vane |

11. pramadāvane = In the pleasure-garden; (that ṣeetha); dṛiṣṭā = was seen; me = by me; rākṣasīmadhye = in the midst of female demons; rakṣitā = guarded; virūpābhiḥ = by ugly-faced; rākṣabhiḥ = female-demon; tarjyamānā = and threatened; muhuḥ muhuḥ = again and again (by them).

"In that pleasure garden, I saw ṣeetha sitting in the midst of female demons, guarded by those ugly-faced female-demons as also time and again being threatened by them."

Sloka 12 Verse Locator

duhkham āpadyate devī tava aduhkha ucitā satī || 5-65-12
rāvaṇa antaḥ pure ruddhvā rākṣasībhiḥ surakṣitā |
eka veṇī dharā dīnā tvayi cintā parāyaṇā || 5-65-13
adhaḥ śayyā vivarṇa angī padminī iva hima āgame |
rāvaṇāt vinivṛtta arthā martavya kṛta niścayā || 5-65-14
devī kathaṃcit kākutstha tvan manā mārgitā mayā |

12; 13; 14. vīra = O hero!; tvayā sukhocitā devī = ṣeetha; who deserves to live happily with you; āpadyate = is obtaining; duḥkham = grief; kākutthsa = O Rama!; kathamchit = somehow; mārgitā = searched through; mayā = by me; devī = that princess; ekveṇīdharā = wearing a single plait (as a mark of desolation); dīnā = miserable; tvayi chintāparāyaṇā = remaining absorbed in your thought; adaḥ shayyā = reposing n the floor; vivarṇāṇgī = having discoloured limbs; padminīva = like a louts; himāgame = in the winter-season; vinivṛittārthā = turning back the wish; rāvaṇāt = from Ravana; martavyakṛita nishchayā = having determined to die; tvanmanāḥ = and thinking of you.

"O hero! ṣeetha, who deserves to live happily with you, is obtaining grief. O Rama! I somehow searched that princess, Seetha wearing a single plait (as a as a mark of desolation), looking miserable, remaining absorbed in your thought, reposing on the floor, having discoloured limbs like a lotus in winter, turning back the wish from Ravana, having determined to die, but thinking of you alone."

Sloka 15

ikṣvāku vaṃśa vikhyātim śanaiḥ kīrtayata anagha || 5-65-15
sa mayā nara śārdūla viśvāsam upapāditā |

15. anagha = O faultless; narashārdūla = tiger among men!; sā = that ṣeetha; upapāditā = was established; vishvāsam = confidence; mayā = by me; shanaiḥ kīrtayatā = in slowly mentioning; ikṣvāku kula vikhyātim = the fame of Ikshvaku dynasty.

"O faultless tiger among men! ṣeetha established confidence in me, after I slowly described to her the fame of Ikshvaku dynasty."

Sloka 16

tataḥ sambhāṣitā devī sarvam artham ca darśitā || 5-65-16
rāma sugrīva sakhyam ca śrutvā prītim upāgatā |
niyataḥ samudācāro bhaktiḥ ca asyāḥ tathā tvayi || 5-65-17

16; 17. tataḥ = thereafter; devī = ṣeetha the princess; sambhāṣitā = was spoken; darshitā = and explained; sarvam artham = about all the significance (of my arrival); shrutvā = on hearing; rāma sugrīvasakhyam = about the friendship between Rama and ṣugreeva; upāgatā = she obtained; prītim = satisfaction; asyāḥ = her; samudāchāraḥ = right practice of conduct; niyataḥ = is constant; tathā = and; bhaktishcha = her devotion; tvayi = in you; is also constant.

"ṭhereafter, I spoke and explained to ṣeetha, about the entire significance of my arrival there. On hearing about the alliance between Rama and Sugreeva, she was quite pleased. Her morality as well as her devotion in you are steadfast."

Sloka 18

evam mayā mahābhāgā dṛṣṭā janaka nandinī |
ugreṇa tapasā yuktā tvat bhaktyā puruṣa ṛṣabha || 5-65-18

18. puruṣarṣabha = O foremost of men!; mahābhāgā = the illustrious; janakanandinī = ṣeetha; dṛṣhiṭā = was seen; evam = thus; mayā = by me; (ṣeetha) yuktā ugreṇa tapasā = was endowed with severe penance; tvadbhaktyā = and devotion towards you.

"O, the foremost of men! I thus saw that illustrious ṣeetha, endowed with severe penance and devotion towards you."

Sloka 19

abhijñānam ca me dattam yathā vṛttam tava antike |
citra kūṭe mahāprājña vāyasam prati rāghava || 5-65-19

19. rāghava = O Rama; mahāprājJṇa = of great intelligence!; abhijJṇānam = a token of remembrance; dattam = was given; me = to me; yathā vṛittam = a previous event; vāyasam prati = about a crow; tava antike = at your vicinity; chitrakūṭe = in ṃount chitrakuta.

"O Rama, of great intelligence! ṭo me, she gave as a token of remembrance, a previous event of a crow occurred at your vicinity in ṃount chitrakuta."

Sloka 20

vijñāpyaḥ ca nara vyāghro rāmo vāyu suta tvayā |
akhilena iha yat dṛṣṭam iti mām āha jānakī || 5-65-20

20. jānakī = ṣeetha; saha = spoke; mām = to me; iti = saying; vāyusut = "O ḥanuma!; rāmaḥ = Rama; nara vyāghraḥ = the foremost of men; vijJṇāpya = is to be informed; akhilena = all; yat = of which; dṛiṣhṭam = seen; tvayā = by you; iha = here."

"ṣeetha spoke to me saying, 'O ḥanuma! Inform Rama, the foremost among men, all that you have seen here."

Sloka 21

ayam ca asmai pradātavyam yatnāt suparirakṣitam |
bruvatā vacanāni evam sugrīvasya upaśṛṇvataḥ || 5-65-21

21. sugrīvasya = (while) ṣugreeva; upashṛiṇvataḥ = is hearing; bruvatā = and while you are telling; vachanāni = the words; evam = in this way; ayam cha = (let) this jewel; suparikṣhitaḥ yatnāt = well-guarded deliberately; pradātavyaḥ ayam cha = be given; asmai = to this Rama.

"While ṣugreeva is hearing nearby and while you are telling the words in this way, let this jewel, which is deliberately well-guarded, be given to Rama."

Sloka 22

eṣa cūḍā maṇiḥ śrīmān mayā te yatna rakṣitaḥ |
manaḥ śilāyāḥ tikalaḥ tam smarasva iti ca abravīt || 5-65-22
tvayā prāṣṭe timke tam kila smartumarhasi |

22. eṣaḥ = this; shrīmān = beautiful; chūDā maṇiḥ = jewel for the head; suparirakṣitaḥ = has been well-guarded; mayā = by me; arhasi = you ought; smartum = to recall; niveshitaḥ kila tilakaḥ = a mark indeed painted; tvayā = by you (by a slip); gaṇ^Dapārshve = on the curve of my cheek; orange-red; tilake = when my former mark on my forehead; praṇaṣhṭe = got effaced.

"ṭhis beautiful jewel for the head has been well-guarded by me. You also ought to recall a mark indeed painted by you (by slip) on the curve of my cheek with powder of an orange-red rock, when my former mark on my forehead got effaced."

Sloka 23

eṣa niryātitaḥ śrīmān mayā te vāri sambhavaḥ || 5-65-23
etam dṛṣṭvā pramodiṣye vyasane tvām iva anagha |

23. eṣaḥ shrīmān = this beautiful jewel; vāri sambhavaḥ = which has its origin in sea-water; niryātitaḥ = has been sent; te = to you; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; etam = this vyasahe = in my grief; pramodiṣhye = I am feeling happy; tvānīva = as though I am seeing you.

"ṭhis beautiful jewel, which has its origin in sea-water, has been sent to you. ṣeeing this in my grief, I am feeling always happy as though I am seeing you."

Sloka 24

jīvitam dhārayiṣyāmi māsam daśaratha ātmaja || 5-65-24
ūrdhvam māsān na jīveyam rakṣasām vaśam āgatā |

24. dasharātmaja = O Rama!; dhārayiṣhyāmi = I shall be sustaining; jīvitam = my life; māsam = for a mouth; āgatā = having come into; vasham = the control; rakṣhasām = of demons; na jīvayam = I cannot survive; ūrdhvam = above; māsāt = one month.

"O Rama! I shall be sustaining my life for a month. ḥaving come into the control of demons, I cannot survive beyond one month."

Sloka 25

iti mām abravīt sītā kṛśa angī dharma cāriṇī || 5-65-25
rāvaṇa antaḥ pure ruddhā mṛgī iva utphulla locanā |

25. sītā = ṣeetha; kṛisha aṇgī = with her emaciated body; dharma chāriṇī = practising virtue; ruddhā = confined; rāvaṇāntaḥ pure = in Ravana's gynaceium; utphulla lochanā = with her wide-open eyes; mṛigīva = like that of a doe; abravīt = spoke; iti = as following; mām = to me.

"ṣeetha, with her emaciated body, practicing virtue, confined in Ravana's gynaecium and with her wide-open eyes like that of a doe, spoke to me the following words:

Sloka 26

etat eva mayā ākhyātam sarvam rāghava yat yathā || 5-65-26
sarvathā sāgara jale samtāraḥ pravidhīyatām |

26. rāghava = O Rama!; etat = this; sarvam eva = exactly in its entirety; khyātam = has been narrated; mayā = by me; yat yathā = as it occurred; samtāraḥ pravidhīyatām = let a bridge by constructed; sarvathā = by all means; sāgara jale = in the waters of the ocean.

"O Rama! I have narrated to you whatever has occurred, in its entirety. Let a bridge be constructed, by all means, across the waters of the ocean."

Sloka 27

tau jāta āśvāsau rāja putrau viditvā |
tac ca abhijñānam rāghavāya pradāya |
devyā ca ākhyātam sarvam eva ānupūrvyāt |
vācā sampūrṇam vāyu putraḥ śaśaṃsa || 5-65-27

27. viditvā = coming to know; tau rāja putrau = that the two princes; Rama and Lakshmana; jāta āshvāsau = have become confident; pradāya = and having given; tat abhijJṇānam = that token of remembrance; rāghavāya = to Rama; vāyu putraḥ = ḥanuma; shashamsa = apprised; vāchā = in words; sampūrṇam = completely; anupūrvyāt = in regular succession; sarvameva = all that; ākhyātam = was conveyed; devyā = by ṣeetha the princess.

Coming to know that the two princes, Rama and Lakshmana have become confident and having given that token of remembrance to Rama, Hanuma apprised completely in so many words, in regular succession, all that was conveyed by Seetha, the princess.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe pañcaṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 65th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
