Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 100
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama and Ravana confront each other with various arrows and missiles. ṃeanwhile, Lakshmana joins the fight and chops off Ravana's bow with his arrows. Vibhishana also fights and kills Ravana's horses. Lakshmana rescues Vibhishana, when a spear is hurled on him by Ravana. Then, Ravana throws a spear on Lakshmana. As it penetrates Lakshmana's bossom, Lakshmana falls down unconscious. Rama hurls hails of arrows on Ravana from his flaming bow. Ravana takes to his heels out of fear.

Sloka 1

tasminpratihateastre tu rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ |
krodhan ca dviguṇaṃ cakre krodhāccāstramanantaram || 6-100-1

1. tasmin astram = when that missile; pratihate = was struck down; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣhasādhipaḥ = the king of demons; chakre krodham divguṇam = (for his part) doubled his fury; krodhāt cha = and in his anger; astram = (employed another) missile; anantaram = immediately.

When that missile was made defunct, Ravana the king of demons, for his part, doubled his fury and in his anger, began to employ another missile immediately.

Sloka 2

mayena vihitan raudramanyadastraṃ mahādyutiḥ |
utsraṣṭun rāvaṇo ghoraṃ rāghavāya pracakrame || 6-100-2

2. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; mahādyutiḥ = who was endowed with extra ordinary splendour; prachakrame = started; utsṛiṣhṭum = to release; anyat = another; astram = missile; raudram = which was cruel; bhīmam = and terrific; vihitam = built; mayena = by ṃaya; the demon; rāghavāya = on Rama.

Ravana, who was endowed with extraordinary splendour, started to release another cruel and terrific missile built by ṃaya the demon, on Rama.

Sloka 3

tataḥ śūlāni niścerurgadāśca musalāni ca |
kārmukāddīpyamānāni vajrasārāṇi sarvaśaḥ || 6-100-3

3. tataḥ = then; kārmukāt = from that bow; nishcheruḥ = came forth; dīpyamānāni = blazing; shūlām = pikes; gadāshcha = maces; musalāni = and clubs; vajrasārāṇi = which were hard as adamant; sarvashaḥ = on all sides.

Then, from that bow, came forth, blazing pikes, maces and clubs, which were hard as adamant, on all sides.

Sloka 4

mudgaraḥ kūṭapāśāśca dīptāścāśanayastathā |
niṣpeturvividhāstīkṣṇā vātā iva yugakṣaye || 6-100-4

4. vividhāḥ = various kinds; tīkṣhaṇāḥ mudgarāḥ = of furious mallets; kūṭapāshāshcha = deceptive nooses; tathā = and; dīptāḥ = blazing; ashanayashcha = thunderbolts; niṣhpetuḥ = came forth; vātāḥ iva = like piercing gales; yugakṣhaye = at the time of the destruction of the world.

Various kinds of furious mallets, deceptive nooses and blazing thunderbolts came forth, like piercing gales at the time of the destruction of the world.

Sloka 5

tadastran rāghavaḥ śrīmānuttamāstravidāṃ varaḥ |
jaghāna paramāstreṇa gandharveṇa mahādyutiḥ || 6-100-5

5. rāghavaḥ = Rama; shrīmān = the glorious; varaḥ uttamāstra vidām = the foremost among the knower of excellent missiles; mahādyutiḥ = who was endowed with extraordinary splendour; jaghāna = struck down; tat = that; astram = missile; paramāstreṇa = with an excellent missiles; gāndharveṇa = presided over by Gandharavas the celestials musicians.

The glorious Rama, the foremost among the knower in the use of excellent missiles and who was endowed with extra ordinary splendour, struck down that missile with an excellent missile presided over by Gandharvas the celestial musicians.

Sloka 6

tasminpratihateastre tu rāghaveṇa mahātmanā |
rāvaṇaḥ krodhatāmrākṣaḥ sauramastramudīrayat || 6-100-6

6. tasmin astre = when that missile; pratihate = was struck down; mahātmanā rāghaveṇa = by the great souled Rama; rāvanaḥ = Ravana; krodha tāmrākṣhaḥ = with his coppery eyes in anger; udairayat = employed; sauram astram = a missile presided over by the sun-god.

When that missile was made defunct by the great souled Rama, Ravana with his coppery eyes in anger, employed a missile presided over by the sun-god.

Sloka 7

tataścakrāṇi niṣpeturbhāsvarāṇi mahānti ca |
kārmukādbhīmavegasya daśagrīvasya dhīmataḥ || 6-100-7

7. tataḥ = then; niṣhpetuḥ = came forth; bhāsvarāṇi = resplendent; mahānti = and large; chakrāṇi = discuses; kārmukāt = from the bow; dhīmataḥ dashagrīvashcha = of the intelligent Ravana; bhīmavegasya = of terrific impetuosity.

Then came forth resplendent and large discuses, from the bow of that intelligent Ravana, of terrific impetuosity.

Sloka 8

tairāsīdgaganan dīptan sampatadbhiritastataḥ |
patadbhiśca diśo dīptaiścandrasūryagrahairiva || 6-100-8

8. sampatadbhiḥ = even as they came forth; patadbhishcha = and falling; samantataḥ = on all sides; taiḥ = by them; gaganam = the sky; āsīt = dīptam = was blazing; dishashcha = and the quarters (illuminated); chandra sūrya grahairiva = as by the moon; the sun and other planets.

Even as they came forth and falling on all sides, the sky was blazing and the quarters illuminated, as by the moon, the sun and other planets.

Sloka 9

tāni ciccheda bāṇaughaiścakrāṇi tu sa rāghavaḥ |
āyudhāni vicitrāṇi rāvaṇasya camūmukhe || 6-100-9

9. chamunmukhe = in the forefront; rāvanasya = of Ravana's army; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; chichchheda = split; tāni = those; chakrāṇi = discuses; āyudhāni cha chitrāṇi = and (other) strange weapons; bāṇaughe = with a multitude of arrows.

In the forefront of Ravana's army, that Rama split those discuses and other strange weapons, with a multitude of arrows.

Sloka 10

tadastran tu hataṃ dṛṣṭvā rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ |
vivyādha daśabhirbāṇai rāman sarveṣu marmasu || 6-100-10

10. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tat = that; astram = missile; hatam = struck down; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rākṣhasa adhipaḥ = the king of demons; vivyādha = hit; rāmam = Rama; sarveṣhu marmasu = in all his vital organs; dashabhiḥ bāṇaiḥ = with ten arrows.

Seeing that missile struck down, Ravana the king of demons, for his part, pierced Rama with ten arrows in all his vital parts.

Sloka 11

sa viddho daśabhirbāṇairmahākārmukaniḥsṛtaiḥ |
rāvaṇena mahātejā na prākampata rāghavaḥ || 6-100-11

11. viddhaḥ = (eventhough) struck; dashabhiḥ bāṇaiḥ = by ten arrows; mahākārmukaniḥsṛitaiḥ = released from the great bow; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; mahātejāḥ = of extraordinary energy; na prākampata = was not shaken.

Eventhough that Ravana struck Rama with those ten arrows released from his great bow, Rama of extraordinary energy was not shaken.

Sloka 12

tato vivyādha gātreṣu sarveṣu samitiñjayaḥ |
rāghavastu susaṅkruddho rāvaṇaṃ bahubhiḥ śaraiḥ || 6-100-12

12. tataḥ = then; rāghavastu = Rama; samitimjayaḥ = the victorious in battle; susamkruddhaḥ = was quite enraged; vivyādha = and struck; rāvaṇam = Ravana; sarveṣhu gātreṣhu = in all his organs; babhubhiḥ saraiḥ = with a multitude of arrows.

Then, Rama the victorious in battle, was quite enraged and struck Ravana in all his organs with a multitude of arrows.

Sloka 13

etasminnantare kruddho rāghavasyānujo balī |
lakṣmaṇaḥ sāyakānsapta jagrāha paravīrahā || 6-100-13

13. etasmin antare = in the meantime; kruddhaḥ = the enraged lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; rāghvasya anujaḥ = Rama's younger brother; bahī = whom as strong; paravīrahā = and the destroyer of valiant enemies; jagrāha = seized hold; sapta sāyakān = of seven arrows.

In the meantime, the mighty and enraged Lakshmana, Rama's younger brother, the destroyer of valiant enemies, seized hold of seven arrows.

Sloka 14

taiḥ sāyakairmahāvegai rāvaṇasya mahādyutiḥ |
dhvajaṃ manuṣyaśīrṣan tu tasya ciccheda naikadhā || 6-100-14

14. mahādyutiḥ = Lakshmana of great brilliance; taiḥ sāyakaiḥ = with those arrows; mahāvegaiḥ = of high velocity; chichchheda = tore off; naikadhā = into many pieces; dhvajam = the flag-staff tasya rāvaṇasya = of that Ravana; manuṣhya shīrṣham = which bore the emblem of a man's head.

Lakshmana of great brilliance, with those arrows of high velocity, tore off into many pieces, the flag-staff of Ravana, which bore the emblem of a man's head.

Sloka 15

sāratheścāpi bāṇena śiro jvalitakuṇḍalam |
jahāra lakṣmaṇaḥ śrīmānnairṛtasya mahābalaḥ || 6-100-15

15. shrīmān = the glorious; mahābalaḥ = and mighty; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; bāṇena = with a single arrows; jahāra = tore; nairṛitasya sāratheḥ shiraḥ = the head of Ravana's charioteer; jvalita kuṇ^Dlam = which was adorned with blazing ear-rings.

The glorious and mighty Lakshmana, with a single arrow, tore the head of Ravana's charioteer, which was adorned with blazing ear-rings.

Sloka 16

tasya bāṇaiś ca ciccheda dhanurgajakaropamam |
lakṣmaṇo rākṣasendrasya pañcabhirniśitaiḥ śaraiḥ || 6-100-16

16. tadā = then; paṇ^chabhiḥ = with five; nishitaiḥ = sharp; bāṇaiḥ = arrows; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; chichchheda = chopped off; rākṣhasendrasya dhanuḥ = Ravana's bow; gajakaropamam = looking like an elephant's trunk.

With five sharp arrows, Lakshmana then chopped off Ravana's bow, which looked like an elephant's trunk.

Sloka 17

nīlameghanibhāṃścāsya sadaśvānparvatopamān |
jaghānāplutya gadayā rāvaṇasya vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-100-17

17. āplytya = bouncing forward; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; jaghāna = killed; gadayā = with his mace; asya rāvaṇasya sadashvān = the excellent horses of this Ravana; nīlamegha nibhān = which looked like black clouds; parvatopamān = and were tall as mountains.

Bouncing forward, Vibhishana killed, with his mace, the excellent horses of Ravana, which looked like black clouds and were tall as mountains.

Sloka 18

hatāśvādvegavānvegādavaplutya mahārathāt |
krodhamāhārayattīvraṃ bhrātaraṃ prati rāvaṇaḥ || 6-100-18

18. avaplutya = leaping down; vegāt = with speed; mahārathāt = from his large chariot; hatāshvāt = whose horses had been killed; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; tāa = then; āhārayat = exhibited; tīvram = a violent; kopam = anger; bhrātaram prati = towards the younger brother (of Rama).

Leaping down with speed from his large chariot, whose horses had been killed, Ravana then exhibited a violent anger towards the younger brother of Rama.

Sloka 19

tataḥ śaktiṃ mahāśaktirdīptān dīptāśanīm iva |
vibhīṣaṇāya cikṣepa rākṣasendraḥ pratāpavān || 6-100-19

19. tataḥ = then; rākṣhasendraḥ = Ravana; mahāshaktiḥ = of great energy; pratāpavān = and prowess; chikṣhepa = hurled; shaktim = a spear; pradīptām ashanīmiva = which was like a blazing thunderbolt; vibhīṣhaṇasya = on Vibhishana.

Then, Ravana, of great energy and prowess, hurled a spear looking like a blazing thunderbolt, on Vibhishana.

Sloka 20

aprāptāmeva tāṃ bāṇaistribhiściccheda lakṣmaṇaḥ |
athodatiṣṭhatsaṃnādo vānarāṇān tadā raṇe || 6-100-20

20. aprāptāmeva = even before(the spear) reached Vibhishana; chichchheda = tore; tām = it; tribhiḥ bāṇaiḥ = with three of his arrows; atha = thereupon; vānarāṇām samnādaḥ = a loud cheer of the monkeys; udatiṣhṭhat = arose; mahāraṇi = in that great battle.

Even before the spear reached Vibhishana, Lakshmana tore it with three of his arrows and a loud cheer then arose from the monkeys in the battle-field.

Sloka 21

saṃpapāta tridhā chinnā śaktiḥ kāñcanamālinī |
savisphuliṅgā jvalitā maholkeva divaścyutā || 6-100-21

21. shaktiḥ = that spear; kāṇchana mālinī = crowned with gold; chhinnā = torn; tridhā = into three pieces; sampapāta = fell; maholkeva = like a large meteor; savishphuliṇ^gā = with sparks of fire; chyutā = fallen; divaḥ = from the sky.

That gold-crowned spear, torn into three pieces, fell like a large meteor with sparks of fire falling from the sky.

Sloka 22

tataḥ sambhāvitatarān kālenāpi durāsadām |
jagrāha vipulān śaktin dīpyamānāṃ svatejasā || 6-100-22

22. tataḥ = thereupon; (Ravana) jagrāha = took hold; vipulām shaktim = of another large spear; sambhāvitatarām = which was the most competent one; durāsadām = which was difficult to be approached; kālenāpi = even by Yama; dīpyamānām = and blazing; svatejasā = with its own splendour.

Thereupon, Ravana took hold of another large most competent spear, which was difficult to be approached even by Yama the lord of Death and blazing as it was, with its own splendour.

Sloka 23

sā veginā balavatā rāvaṇena durātmanā |
jajvāla sumahāghorā śakrāśanisamaprabhā || 6-100-23

23. sā = that spear; sumahātejāḥ = with very great splendour; jajvāla = and burning; dīptāshani samaprabhā = with a radiance equal to a blazing thunderbolt; vegitā = was hurled swiftly; balavatā = by the mighty; durātmanā = and the evil-minded; rāvaṇena = Ravana.

The mighty and the evil-minded Ravana swiftly hurled that very great spear, burning with a radiance similar to that of a blazing thunder-bolt.

Sloka 24

etasminnantare vīro lakṣmaṇastan vibhīṣaṇam |
prāṇasaṃśayamāpannan tūrṇamevābhyapadyata || 6-100-24

24. etasmin antare = In the meanwhile; vīraḥ = the valiant; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; tūrṇam = quickly; abhyavapadyata = hastened = towards; tam vibhīṣhaṇam = that Vibhishana; āpannam = who got; prāṇa samshayam = a danger to his life.

In the meanwhile, the valiant Lakshmana quickly rushed towards that Vibhishana, who got a danger to his life.

Sloka 25

taṃ vimokṣayituṃ vīraścāpamāyamya lakṣmaṇaḥ |
rāvaṇan śaktihastan taṃ śaravarṣairavākirat || 6-100-25

25. vimochayitum = to rescue; tam = that Vibhishana; vīraḥ = the heroic; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; āyamya = having stretched; chāpam = his bow; avākirat = threw out; sharavarṣhaiḥ = streams of arrows; rāvaṇam = on Ravana; shaktihastam = having the spear in his hand.

To rescue that Vibhishana, the heroic Lakshmana, having stretched his bow, threw out streams of arrows on Ravana who was holding a spear in his hand.

Sloka 26

kīryamāṇaḥ śaraugheṇa visṛṣṭtena mahātmanā |
na prahartuṃ manaścakre vimukhīkṛtavikramaḥ || 6-100-26

26. kīryamāṇaḥ = poured in; sharaugheṇa = with a multitude of arrows; visṛiṣhṭena = discharged; mahātmanā = by the great-souled Lakshmana; (Ravana); vimukhīkṛitavikramaḥ = with his attack frustrated; na chakre manaḥ = did not make up his mind; prahartum = to attack Vibhishana (further).

Poured in with a multitude of arrows discharged by the great-souled Lakshmana, Ravana with his attach frustrated, did not make up his mind to attack Vibhishana further.

Sloka 27

mokṣitaṃ bhrātaran dṛṣṭvā lakṣmaṇena sa rāvaṇaḥ |
lakṣmaṇābhimukhastiṣṭhannidan vacanamabravīt || 6-100-27

27. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; bhrātaram = his brother; mokṣhitam = rescued; lakṣhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = the following words; tiṣhṭhan = standing; lakṣhmaṇa abhimukhaḥ = for his part; facing Lakshmana.

Seeing Vibhishana his brother rescued by Lakshmana, that Ravana, turning his face towards Lakshmana spoke the following words:

Sloka 28

mokṣitaste balaślāghinyasmādevan vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
vimucya rākṣasan śaktistvayīyaṃ vinipātyate || 6-100-28

28. balashlāghin = O Lakshmana; exalter your own strength!; yasmāt = as; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; mokṣhitaḥ = was rescued; te = by you; evam = in this way; iyam shaktiḥ = this spear; vimchya = leaving; rākṣhasam = the demon; vinipātyate = is being hurled; tvayi = on you.

"O Lakshmana, exalter of your own strength! As Vibhishana was rescued by you in this way, this spear, on letting off Vibhishana the demon, is being hurled on you now."

Sloka 29

eṣā te hṛdayaṃ bhittvā śaktirlohitalakṣaṇā |
madbāhuparighotsṛṣṭā prāṇānādāya yāsyati || 6-100-29

29. eṣhā shaktiḥ = this spear; lohita lakṣhaṇā = having blood-marks; madbāhu parighotsṛiṣhṭā = and hurled by my steel rod-like arm; bhitvā = piercing; hṛidayam = your heart; yāsyati = will depart; ādāya = (only after) taking; prāṇān = your life.

"This spear, having blood-marks and hurled by bludgeon-like arm, having pierced your heart, will depart only after taking your life."

Sloka 30

ityevamuktvā tān śaktimaṣṭaghaṇṭāṃ mahāsvanām |
mayena māyāvihitāmamoghān śatrughātinīm || 6-100-30
lakṣmaṇāya samuddiśya jvalantīmiva tejasā |
rāvaṇaḥ paramakruddhaścikṣepa ca nanāda ca || 6-100-31

30; 31. parama kruddhaḥ = the greatly enraged; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; ityevam uktvā = thus speaking; chikṣhepa = hurled; samuddishya = towards; lakṣhmaṇāya = Lakshmana; tām shaktim = that spear; aṣhṭa ghaṇṭām = with eight bells; mahāsvanām = making huge sounds; māyāvihitām = designed with a conjuring trick; mayena = by maya; amoghām = unfailing; shatru vighātinīm = which destroys adversaries; jvalantīmiva = and as with a blazing; tejasā = splendour; nanāda cha = (ḥe) also roared.

Thus saying, the greatly enraged Ravana roared and hurled towards Lakshmana, that unfailing spear, which was adorned with eight loudly clamouring bells, which had been designed by ṃaya the demon by dint of his conjuring trick, capable of destroying adversaries and blazing as it was with splendour.

Sloka 32

sā kṣiptā bhīmavegena śakrāśanisamasvanā |
śaktirabhyapatadvegāllakṣmaṇan raṇamūrdhani || 6-100-32

32. sā shaktiḥ = that spear; vajrāshani samasvanā = with a sound equal to Indra's thunder-bolt; kṣhiptā = hurled; bhīma vegena = by Ravana having terrible swiftness; abhyapatat = soon fell; vegāt = with speed; lakṣhmaṇam- on Lakshmana; raṇamūrdhani = into the battle-front.

That spear, with a sound equal to Indra's thunder-bolt, hurled by the terribly swift Ravana, soon fell with speed on Lakshmana in the battle-front.

Sloka 33

tāmanuvyāharacchaktimāpatantīn sa rāghavaḥ |
svastyastu lakṣmaṇāyeti moghā bhava hatodyamā || 6-100-33

33. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; anuvyāharat = repeatedly uttered; tām shaktim = to that spear; āpatantīm = which was rushing forth; iti = as follows: svasti astu = may it be well; lakṣhmaṇāya = with Lakshmana! bhava = become; moghā = vain; hatodyamā = may your effort be frustrated!

Rama repeatedly uttered to that spear even as it was rushing forth (on Lakshmana) as follows: "ṃay it be well with Lakshmana! ṃay you prove useless! ṃay your effort be frustrated!"

Sloka 34

tāvaṇena raṇe śaktiḥ kruddhenāśīviṣopamā |
muktāśūrasyabhītasya lakṣmaṇasya mamajja sā || 6-100-34

34. sā shaktiḥ āshīviṣhopamā = that spear; looking like a venomous serpant; muktā = released; kruddhena = by the enraged; rāvaṇena = Ravana; raṇe = in the battle; āshu = quickly; mamajja = penetrated; urasi = the chest; abhītasya lakṣhmaṇasya = of the fearless Lakshmana.

That spear, which was looking like a venomous serpent, released by the enraged Ravana in the battle, quickly penetrated the bosom of Lakshmana, who stood fearless.

Sloka 35

nyapatatsā mahāvegā lakṣmaṇasya mahorasi |
jihvevoragarājasya dīpyamānā mahādyutiḥ || 6-100-35

35. sā = that spear; uragarājasya jihveva = like the tongue of Vasuki (the lord of serpents); dīpyamānā = blazing; mahādyutiḥ = with a great dazzle; nyapatat = fell; mahorasi = on the broad chest; lakṣhmaṇasya = of Lakshmana; mahāvegā = with enormous speed.

That spear, like the tongue of Vasuki (the lord of serpents), blazing with a great dazzle, fell on the broad chest of Lakshmana, with enormous speed.

Sloka 36

tato rāvaṇavegena sudūramavagāḍhayā |
śaktyā nirbhinnahṛdayaḥ papāta bhuvi lakṣmaṇaḥ || 6-100-36

36. avagāDhayā = penetrated; sudūram = very deeply; shaktyā = by the spear; vibhinna hṛidayaḥ = and pierced through the heart; rāvaṇavegena = due to the force exerted by Ravana; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; papāta = fell; bhuvi = to the ground; vibhinnahṛidayaḥ = with his heart broken.

Penetrated very deeply and pierced through his heart by the spear due to the force exerted by Ravana, Lakshmana fell to the ground.

Sloka 37

tadavasthan samīpastho lakṣmaṇaṃ prekṣya rāghavaḥ |
bhrātṛsnehānmahātejā viṣaṇṇahṛdayoabhavat || 6-100-37

37. prekṣhya = seeing; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; tadavastham = in that state; rāghavaḥ = Rama; mahātejāḥ = of extra ordinary splendour; samīpasthaḥ = who was in the vicinity; abhavat = became; viṣhaṇṇahṛdayaḥ = sorrowful at heart; bhrātṛisnehāt = because of his fraternal love.

Seeing Lakshmana in that state, Rama, of extraordinary splendour, who was stationed in the vicinity, became sorrowful at heart because of the love towards his brother.

Sloka 38

sa muhūrtamanudhyāya bāṣpavyākulalocanaḥ |
babhūva sanrabdhataro yugānta iva pāvakaḥ || 6-100-38

38. saḥ = Rama; bāṣhpa paryākulekṣhaṇaḥ = whose eyes were filled with tears; dhyātvā = reflected; muhūtamiva = just for a while; babhūva = and was; samrabdhataraḥ = very much enraged; yugānte pārakaḥ iva = like fire at the time of dissolution of the world.

Rama, whose eyes were filled with tears, reflected just for a while and then was very much enraged, like fire at the time of dissolution of the world.

Sloka 39

na viṣādasya kāloayamiti sañcintya rāghavaḥ |
cakre sutumulan yuddhaṃ rāvaṇasya vadhe dhṛtaḥ || 6-100-39
sarvayatnena mahatā lakṣmaṇaṃ parivīkṣya ca |

39. samchitya = becoming aware; iti = that; ayam = this one; na kālaḥ = is not the time; vihhādasya = for despodency; rāghavaḥ = Rama; parivīkṣhya = looking at; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; dhṛitaḥ = was determined; vadhe = upon killing; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; chakre = and performed; sutumulam = the highly tumultuous; yuddham = battle; mahatā sarvayatnena = with a great and whole-hearted endeavour.

Becoming aware that it was not the time for feeling despondent, and looking at Lakshmana, Rama resumed that highly tumultuous battle with a great and whole-hearted endeavour, determined as he was to destroy Ravana.

Sloka 40

sa dadarśa tato rāmaḥ śaktyā bhinnaṃ mahāhave || 6-100-40
lakṣmaṇan rudhirādigdhaṃ sapannagamivācalam |

40. tataḥ = then; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; dadarsha = saw; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; bhinnam = who was struck; shaktyā = by the spear; mahāvahe = in that great war; rudhirādigdham = bathed as he was in blood; achalam iva = and looking like a mountain; sapannagam = with a snake (entering its centre).

Then, Rama saw Lakshmana, who was struck by the spear in that Great War, bathed as he was in blood and looking like a mountain with a snake (entering its centre).

Sloka 41

tāmapi prahitān śaktiṃ rāvaṇena balīyasā || 6-100-41
yatnataste hariśreṣṭhā na śekuravamarditum |
arditāścaiva bāṇaughaiḥ kṣiprahastena rakṣasā || 6-100-42

41; 42. yatnataḥ api = even though exerting; ava marditum = to pull out; tām = that; shaktim = spear; prahitām = hurled; balīyasā rāvaṇena = by the mighty Ravana; te harishreṣhṭhāḥ = those foremost among monkeys; na shekuḥ = could not do it; arditāḥ chaiva = as they were being tormented; pravakeṇa rakṣhasām = by Ravana the foremost among the demons; bāṇaughaiḥ = with a multitude of his arrows.

Even with an exertion to pull out that spear hurled by the mighty Ravana, those foremost of the monkeys could not those foremost of the monkeys could not do it because they were being tormented by Ravana the foremost of demons with a stream of his arrows.

Sloka 43

saumitrin sā vinirbhidya praviṣṭā dharaṇītalam |
tān karābhyāṃ parāmṛśya rāmaḥ śaktiṃ bhayāvahām || 6-100-43
babhañja samare kruddho balavadvicakarṣa ca |

43. vinirbhidya = penetrating; saumitreḥ = through Lakshmana's bosom; sā = that spear; praviṣhṭā = touched; dharaṇītalam = the earth's surface; parāmarshya karābhyām = seizing hold with his hands; tām shaktim bhayārchām = that awful spear; balavān = the mighty; rāmaḥ = Rama; vichakarṣha = drew it out; kruddhaḥ = and getting enraged; babhaṇ^jacha = broke it; samare = during the course of the battle.

Penetrating through Lakshmana's bosom, that spear touched the earth. Seizing hold of awful spear with his hands, the mighty Rama drew it out and getting enraged, broke it during the course of the battle.

Sloka 44

tasya niṣkarṣataḥ śaktin rāvaṇena balīyasā || 6-100-44
śarāḥ sarveṣu gātreṣu pātitā marmabhedinaḥ |

44. balīyasā rāvaṇena = by the mighty Ravana; pāittāḥ = were sunk; sharāḥ = arrows; marma bhedinaḥ = which pierced his vital organs; sarveṣhu gātreṣhu = and all the limbs; tasya = of Rama; niṣhkarṣhataḥ = who was drawing; shaktim = that spear.

While Rama was drawing the spear out; arrow, which pierced his vital organs, were sunk by the mighty Ravana on all his limbs.

Sloka 45

acintayitvā tānbāṇānsamāśliṣyā ca lakṣmaṇam || 6-100-45
abravīcca hanūmantan sugrīvan caiva rāghavaḥ |

45. achintayitvā = ignoring; tān = those; bāṇān = arrows; samāshliṣhya = and having embraced; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; abravīchcha = (Rama) spoke; hanūmantam = to ḥanuma; sugrīvamcha = and ṣugreeva; mahākapim = the king of the monkeys (as follows):

Ignoring those arrows and having embraced Lakshmana, Rama spoke to Hanuma and Sugreeva, the king of monkeys, as follows:

Sloka 46

lakṣmaṇaṃ parivāryeha tiṣṭhadhvan vānarottamāḥ || 6-100-46
parākramasya kāloayan samprāpto me cirepsitaḥ |
pāpātmāyan daśagrīvo vadhyatāṃ pāpaniścayaḥ || 6-100-47
kāṅkṣitaḥ stokakasyeva gharmānte meghadarśanam |

46; 47. vānarottamāḥ = O the foremost among the monkeys!; tiṣhṭhadhvam = remain; parivāryeva = just encompassing; lakṣhmaṇam = Lakshmana; ayam pāpātmāyam dashagrīvaḥ = let this malicious Ravana; pāpa nishchayaḥ = having evil designs; vadhyatām = be killed; ayam = this; parākramasya kālaḥ = time to show my prowess; me chirepsitaḥ = which I have been wishing for long; samprāptaḥ = has come; chātakasya iva = as for a chataka bird; meghadarshanam = the site of a cloud (would come) kāṇ^kṣhitam = as desired by it; gharmānte = at the end of the hot season.

"O the chiefs of monkeys! Remain just encompassing Lakshmana. Let this malicious Ravana of evil designs be killed. The time to manifest my prowess, which I have been wishing for long, has come, as for a chataka bird, the site of a cloud would come, as sought by it, at the end of a hot season."

Sloka 48

asminmuhūrte nacirātsatyaṃ pratiśṛṇomi vaḥ || 6-100-48
arāvaṇamarāman vā jagaddrakṣyatha vānarāḥ |

48. pratishṛiṇomi = I take this owe; satyam = in truth; vaḥ = before you; asmin muhūrte = at this moment; vānarāḥ = O monkeys!; drakṣhyatha = you will see; nachirāt = without delay; jagat = (either) the world; arāvaṇam = devoid of Ravana; arāmam vā = or devoid of Rama (myself).

"I take this vow in truth before you, at this moment, O monkeys, that without delay, you will see the world devoid of Ravana or Rama (myself)."

Sloka 49

rājyanāśan vane vāsan daṇḍake paridhāvanam || 6-100-49
vaidehyāśca parāmarśan rakṣobhiśca samāgamam |

49. samāgamam = I encountered; rājyanāsham = with the loss of soverighnity; vane vāsam = the dwelling in the forest; paridhāvanam = the wandering around; daṇ^Dake = in the forest of Dandaka; parāmarsham = and the insult offered; vaideyehyāḥ = to ṣeetha; rakṣhobhiḥ = by the demons.

"I encountered with the loss of sovereignity, the dwelling in the forest, the wandering around in the woods of Dandaka and the rough treatment of Seetha by the demons."

Sloka 50

prāptan duḥkhaṃ mahadghoraṃ kleśaṃ ca nirayopamam || 6-100-50
adya sarvamahan tyakṣye hatvā tan rāvaṇaṃ raṇe |

50. mahat = A great; ghoram = and terrible; duḥkham = suffering; prāptam = was obtained; kleshashcha = a trouble too; nirayopamaḥ = tantamounting to hell; adya = now; aham = I; nihatvā = by killing; rāvaṇam = Ravana; raṇe = in battle; tyakṣhye = will relieve myself; sarvam = of all that trouble.

"A great and terrible suffering has happened to me and an agony equivalent to hell. Now, by killing Ravana in battle, I will relieve myself of all that agony."

Sloka 51

yadarthan vānaraṃ sainyaṃ samānītamidaṃ mayā || 6-100-51
sugrīvaśca kṛto rājye nihatvā vālinan raṇe |
soayamadya raṇe pāpaścakṣurviṣayamāgataḥ || 6-100-52

51; 52. yadartham = for whose sake; idam = this; vānaram sainyam = army of monkeys; samānītam = has been brought; mayā = by me; rājyekṛite sugrīvaḥ cha = and ṣugreeva has been anointed to his kingdom; nihatvā = by killing; vālinam = Vali; yadartham = and for whose sake; sāgaraḥ = the ocean; krāntaḥ = has been crossed; setuḥ = and a bridge; baddhaḥ = built; saḥ ayam pāpaḥ = that sinful demon; chakṣhurviṣhayam āgataḥ = has come within the range of my sight; raṇe = in the battle-field; adya = now.

"That sinful demon, for whose sake, this army of monkeys has been brought by me, Sugreeva has been anointed on the throne after killing Vali and for whose sake the ocean has been crossed and a bridge built on it, has come within the range of my sight in the battle-field now."

Sloka 53

cakṣurviṣayamāgamya nāyan jīvitumarhati || 6-100-53
dṛṣṭin dṛṣṭiviṣasyeva sarpasya mama rāvaṇaḥ |
yathā vā vainateyasya dṛṣṭiṃ prāpto bhujaṃgamaḥ || 6-100-54

53; 54. ayam rāvaṇaḥ = this Ravana; āgatya = having come; mama chakṣhurviṣhayam = within the range of my vision; nārhati jīvitam = cannot survive; dṛiṣhṭim iva = any more than the one who has come within the range of vision; sarpasya = of snake; dṛiṣhṭiviṣhasya = injecting deadly poison with its very glance; bhujangamaḥ = or than a serpent; dṛiṣhṭim prāptam = who has come within the range of vision; vainateyasya = of Garuda; the King of birds and the enemy of serpents.

"This Ravana, having come within the range of my vision, cannot survive, any more than the one who has come within the range of vision of a snake injecting deadly poison with its very glance or than a serpent who has come within the range of vision of Garuda, the king of birds and the enemy of serpents."

Sloka 55

svasthāḥ paśyata durdharṣā yuddhan vānarapuṅgavāḥ |
āsīnāḥ parvatāgreṣu mamedan rāvaṇasya ca || 6-100-55

55. vānara pungavāḥ durdharṣhāḥ = O the foremost among the monkeys; who are so difficult to conquer!; āsīnāḥ = seated; parvatāgreṣhu = on the mountain-peaks; pashyata = you witness; svasthāḥ = at ease; idam = this; yuddham = battle; mama = between myself; rāvaṇasya cha = and Ravana.

"O the foremost of the monkeys who are so difficult to conquer! Seated on the mountain-peaks, you witness at ease, this battle between myself and Ravana."

Sloka 56

adya rāmasya rāmatvaṃ paśyantu mama sanyuge |
trayo lokāḥ sagandharvāḥ sadevāḥ sarṣicāraṇāḥ || 6-100-56

56. trayaḥ lokāḥ = Let the three worlds; sagandharvāḥ = including Gandharvas the celestial musicians; sadevāḥ = gods; sarṣhiḥ = the sages; chāraṇāḥ = the celestial bards; pashyantu = see; adya = today; rāmatvam = the Ramahood; rāmasya = of Rama; mama samyuge = during the course of my battle.

"Let the three worlds (viz. the earth, heaven and the intermediate region) including Gandharvas the celestial musicians, the gods, the sages and the celestial bards see today the Ramahood of Rama during the course of my battle."

Sloka 57

adya karma kariṣyāmi yallokāḥ sacarācarāḥ |
sadevāḥ kathayiṣyanti yāvadbhūmirdhariṣyati || 6-100-57
samāgamya sadā loke yathā yuddhaṃ pravartitam |

57. kariṣhyāmi = I shall perform; adya = today; karma = a feat; yat = which; lokāḥ = people; loke = in the world; sacharācharāḥ = including all living beings; both mobile and immobile; sadevāḥ = as also gods; sadā kathayiṣhyanti = will ever narrate; samāgamya = together; yathā = describing how; yuddham = the battle; pravartitam = proceeded; yāvat = as long as; bhūmiḥ = the earth; dhariṣhyati = is able to support; yat = those who (occupy it).

"I shall perform today a feat, which people in the world including all living beings both mobile and immobile, as also gods, will ever narrate together, describing how the battle proceeded, as long as the earth is able to support those who occupy it."

Sloka 58

evamuktvā śitairbāṇaistaptakāñcanabhūṣaṇaiḥ |
ājaghāna daśagrīvan raṇe rāmaḥ samāhitaḥ || 6-100-58

58. evam = thus; uktvā = speaking; rāmaḥ = Rama; samāhitaḥ = with an attentive mind; raṇe = in the battle-field; ājaghāna = struck; dashagrīvam = Rama; shitaiḥ bāṇaiḥ = with sharp arrows; tapta kāṇ^chana bhūṣhaṇaiḥ = decked with pure gold.

Thus speaking, Rama with an attentive mind in the battle-field, struck Rama with sharp arrows, decked in pure gold.

Sloka 59

atha pradīptairnārācairmusalaiścāpi rāvaṇaḥ || 6-100-59
abhyavarṣattadā rāman dhārābhiriva toyadaḥ |

59. atha = thereupon; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; abhyavarṣhat = streamed forth; pradīptaiḥ = blazing; nārāchaiḥ = steel arrows; musalaishchapi = and clubs; tadā = then; toyadaḥ iva = as cloud; (would shower) dhārābhiḥ = the rains.

Thereupon, Ravana streamed forth blazing steel arrows and then clubs, as clouds would stream forth the rains.

Sloka 60

rāmarāvaṇamuktānāmanyonyamabhinighnatām || 6-100-60
śarāṇān ca śarāṇāṃ ca babhūva tumulaḥ svanaḥ |

60. tumulaḥ = A tumultuous; svanaḥ = sound; babhūva = arose; varāṇāmcha sharānām cha = from the excellent arrows; rāma rāvaṇa muktānām = released by Rama and Ravana; abhinighnatām = as they struck; anyonyam = each other.

A tumultuous sound arose from the excellent arrows, released by Rama and Ravana, as they struck each other.

Sloka 61

te bhinnāśca vikīrṇāśca rāmarāvaṇayoḥ śarāḥ || 6-100-61
antarikṣātpradīptāgrā nipeturdharaṇītale |

61. sharāḥ = the arrows; rāma rāvaṇayoḥ = of Rama and Ravana; vibhinnāshcha = split asunder; vikīrṇāshcha = and seattered off; pradīptāgrāḥ = with their blazing points; nipetuḥ = fell; dharaṇītale = on to the floor; antarikṣhāt = from the space.

The arrows of Rama and Ravana, split asunder and scattered off, with their blazing points, fell from the space on to the surface of the earth.

Sloka 62

tayorjyātalanirghoṣo rāmarāvaṇayormahān || 6-100-62
trāsanaḥ sarvabūtānān sa babhūvādbhutopamaḥ |

62. mahān = the loud; nirghoṣhaḥ = sound; jyātala = produced by the impact of the bow string on the palms; tayoḥ rāma rāvaṇayoḥ = of those heroes; Rama and Ravana; trāsanaḥ = which struck terror; sarva bhūtānām = in all the living beings; adbhuta darshanaḥ = was fell astonished by the audience.

The loud sound, produced by the impact of the bow-string on the palms of those two heroes Rama and Ravana, which struck terror in all the living beings, was felt astonished by the audience.

Sloka 63

vikīryamāṇaḥ śarajālavṛṣṭibhir |
rmahātmanā dīptadhanuṣmatārditaḥ |
bhayātpradudrāva sametya rāvaṇo |
yathānilenābhihato balāhakaḥ || 6-100-63

63. vikīryamāṇaḥ = torn asunder; sharajāla vṛiṣhṭibhiḥ = by the streams of a multitude of arrows; mahātmanaḥ = by the great souled Rama; dīptadhanuṣhmatā = who was armed with a blazing bow; sametya = while coming in collusion with him; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; pradudrāva = ran away; bhayāt = out of fear; valāhakaḥ yathā = like a cloud (dispersing); abhihataḥ = when pushed; anilena = by a tempest.

Torn asunder by the streams of a multitude of arrows by the great-souled Rama, who was armed with a blazing bow, while coming in collusion with him, Ravana took to his heels out of fear, like a cloud when pushed by a tempest.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe śatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 100th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
