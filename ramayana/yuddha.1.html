Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 1
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama appreciates Hanuma and embraces him after hearing his report.Rama was gladdend that Hanuma did something worthy of a noble servent. Then, Rama reflected upon the problem of how to cross the ocean.

Sloka 1

śrutvā hanumato vākyam yathāvadbhibhāṣitam |
rāmaḥ prītisamāyukto vākyamuttaramabravīt || 6-1-1

1. prītisamāyuktaḥ = touched with joy; shrutvā = to hear; vākyam = the words; yathāvat = rightly; humanumataḥ = ḥanuman; rāmaḥ = Rama; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words; uttaram = in reply.

Touched with joy to hear the words rightly spoken by Hanuman, Rama replied as follows: -

Sloka 2

kṛtam hanumatā kāryam sumahadbhuvi durlabham |
manasāpi yadanyena na śakyam dharaṇītale || 6-1-2

2. sumahat = "a very outstanding; kāryam = work; durlabham = the most arduous; bhuvi = in the world; kṛitam = has been done; hanumatā = by ḥanuman; yat = which; na shakyam = could not be achieved; manasāpi = even in thought; anyena = by another; dharaṇītale = on the surface of this earth"

"A very outstanding work, the most arduous in the world has been done by Hanuman, which could not be carried out even in thought by any other on the surface of this earth."

Sloka 3

na hi tam paripaśyāmi yastareta mahodadhim |
anyatra guruḍādvāyoranyatra ca hanūmataḥ || 6-1-3

3. na paripashyāmihi = Indeed; I cannot perceive; tam = as such; anyatra = anyone other than garuDāt = Garuda (chief of the feathered race and vehicle of Lord Vishnu); vāyoḥ = wind-god; anyatracha = and anyone other than; hanūmataḥ = ḥanuman; yaḥ = who; tareta = can cross; mahodadhim = the great sea."

"Indeed, I cannot perceive as such anyone other than Garuda (chief of the feathered race and vehicle of Lord Vishnu), wind- god and Hanuman, who can cross the mighty ocean."

Sloka 4

devadānavayakṣāṇām gandharvoragarakṣasām |
apradhṛṣyām purīm laṅkām rāvaṇena surakṣitām || 6-1-4
praviṣṭaḥ sattvamāśritya jīvanko nāma niṣkramet |

4. konāma = "who; nishhkramet = can return; jīvan = alive; pravīshhṭaḥ = having entered (once); āshritya = taking refuge; sattvam = in self-command; laṇ^kām purīm = the city of Lanka; apradhṛshhyām = which is unconquerable; devadānava yakshhāṇām = by gods;demons; and yakshas;gandharvoraga rakshhasām = and even by Gandharvas; ṇagas and ogres; surakshhitām = and well under the custody; rāvaṇena = of Ravana?"

"Who can return alive, having entered (once) taking refuge only in self- command, the city of Lanka, which is unconquerable by gods demons and Yakshas and even by Gandharvas, Nagas and ogres and well under the custody of Ravana?"

Sloka 5

ko viśetsudurādharṣām rākṣasaiśca surakṣitām || 6-1-5
yo vīryabalasampanno na samaḥ syāddhanūmataḥ |

5. kaḥ = "who; vishat = can enter; (that citadel); sudūradharshhām = which is exceedingly dangerous to be attacked; surakshhitām = and which is strongly protected; rākshhasaḥ = by ogres; na syāt = unless; yaḥ = whoever (he); vīrya bala sampannaḥ = is richly endowed with vigour and strength; samaḥ = like; hanūmataḥ = ḥanuman?"

"Who is able to capture that citadel, by assault, that is exceedingly dangerous to be attacked and which is powerfully guarded by ogres, but one whose courage and valour are equal to Hanuman's?"

Sloka 6

bhṛtyakāryam hanumatā sugrīvasya kṛtam mahat || 6-1-6
evam vidhāya svabalam sadṛśam vikramasya ca |

6. mahat = "a great; bṛitya kāryam = act of service; kṛityam = has been done; hanumatā = by ḥanuman; sugrīvasya = to ṣugriva; evam = thus; vidhāya = by exhibiting; svabalam = his strength; sadṛisham = worthy; vikramasya = of his pace."

"A great act of service has been done by Hanuman to Sugriva thus by exhibiting his strength corresponding to his pace."

Sloka 7

yo hi bhṛtyo niyuktaḥ san bhartrā karmaṇi duṣkare || 6-1-7
kuryāttadunurāgeṇa tamahuḥ puruṣottamam |

7. āhuḥ = (they) speak; tam = of him; purushhottamam = as the best of servants; yaḥ = who; niyuktaḥ san = when entrusted; bhartrā = by his master; karmaṇi = with a duty; dushhkare = difficult to perform; bhrutyaḥ = the servant; kuryāt = does; tat = it; anurāgeṇa = with a passion."

"That servant to whom his master entrusts a difficult task and who performs it with zeal is said to be a superior person."

Sloka 8

yo niyuktaḥ param kāryam na kuryānnaṛpateḥ priyam || 6-1-8
bhṛtyo yuktaḥ samarthaśca tamāhurmadhyam naram |

8. āhuḥ = (they) speak; tam = of him; madhyamam naram = as a mediocre man; yaḥ = which; bhṛityaḥ = though qualified; samarthaḥcha = and capable; na kuryāt = does not perform; param = superior; kāryam = work; priyam = liked; nṛipate = by the king."

"The one who is ready and capable but who yet does no more than his master extracts from him is called a mediocre person."

Sloka 9

niyukto nṛpateḥ kāryam na kuryādyaḥ samāhitaḥ || 6-1-9
bhṛtyo yuktaḥ samarthaśca tamāhuḥ puruṣdhamam |

9. āhuḥ = (they) speak; tam = of him; purushhādhamam = as the lowest of men; yaḥ = which; bhṛityuḥ = servant; niyuktaḥ = when entrusted; yuktaḥ = though qualified; samarthaḥcha = and capable; na karyāt = does not accomplish; kāryam = the work; nṛipateḥ = of the king; samāhitaḥ = as directed."

"The one who is well and able and yet does not carry out the instructions of his master as directed is said to be the least of men ."

Sloka 10

tanniyoge niyuktena kṛtam hanūmatā || 6-1-10
na cātmā laghatām nītaḥ sugrīvaścāpi toṣitaḥ |

10. tat = "for that reason; kṛityam = the work; kṛitam = was done; hanūmatā = by ḥanumanta; niyuktām = when entrusted; niyoge = with duty (of search for ṣita). Laghutām = diminution; na nītaḥ = was not brought; ātmā = to his own self; sugrīvashchāpi = and ṣugriva too; toshhitaḥ = was delighted."

"Through the discovery of Sita's retreat by this faithful messenger, Hanuman has fulfilled the task entrusted to him unfalteringly to the satisfaction of Sugriva and hence there was no diminution to his self."

Sloka 11

aham ca raghavaṃśaśca lakṣmaṇaśca mahābalaḥ || 6-1-11
vaidehyā darśanenādya dharmataḥ parirakṣitāḥ |

11. darshanena = "by the finding; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣita; raghuvamshashcha = the Raghu dynasty; lakshmaṇashcha = and Lakshmana; mahābalaḥ = who is endowed with great strength; parirakshhitāḥ = have been saved; adya = today; dharmataḥ = rightly."

"By finding out Sita, the Raghu dynasty as well as myself and the valiant Lakshmana too, have been rightly saved today."

Sloka 12

idam tu mama dīnasya mano bhūyaḥ prakarṣati || 6-1-12
yadihāsya priyākṣyāturna kurmi sadṛśam priyam |

12. tu = "but; prakarshhati = it squeezes; mama = my; manaḥ = conscience; bhūyaḥ = further; dīnasya = hopeless as I am; idam = (to think) that; yat na kurmi = I can not do; priyam = a pleasant act; sadṛisham = worthy; asyam = of ḥanuman; priyāakhyātuḥ = who has informed me these good tidings."

"But it squeezes my conscience further, hopeless as I am, to think that I am not able to do a pleasant act befittingly to the bearer of these good tidings."

Sloka 13

eṣa sarvasvabhūtastu pariṣvaṅgo hanumataḥ || 6-1-13
mayā kālamimam prāpya dattastasya mahātmanaḥ |

13. eshhaḥ = ālingaḥ = "this embrace; sarvasya bhūtaḥ = which is all that I can call really my possession; prāpya = obtained; imam kālam = in these present circumstances; dattaḥ = is offered; mayā = by me; tasya mahātmanaḥ = to such high soled; hanūmataḥ = ḥanuman."

"Let me at least embrace this magnanimous Hanuman since in the present circumstances, this is all that is easily obtained from me."

Sloka 14

ityuktvā prītihṛṣṭāṅgo rāmastam pariṣsvaje || 6-1-14
hanumantam kṛtātmānam kR6itavākyamupāgatam |

14. uktvā = uttering; iti = thus; rāmaḥ = Rama; prīti hṛishhṭāngaḥ = whose limbs were thrilled with joy; parishhasvaje = embraced; tam hanūmantam = that ḥanuman; kṛitātmānam = who had mastered his mind; upāgatam = (and) who had come; kṛita vākyam = having carried out his word.

Thus saying, Rama vibrating with joy, clasped Hanuman in his arms who, master of himself, his mission fulfilled, had returned.

Sloka 15

dhyātvā punaruvācedam vacanam raghusattamaḥ || 6-1-15
harīnāmīśvarasyāpi sugrīvasyopaśṛṇvataḥ |

15. dhyātvā = Reflecting (for a while); raghusattamaḥ = Rama (the great scion of Raghus); punaḥ = again; uvācha = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; sugrīvasya = ṣugriva; īshvarasya = the ruler; harīṇām = of monkeys; upashṛiṇvataḥ = listening arttentively.

After reflecting a while, Rama the great scion of Raghus, again spoke as follows, Sugriva the ruler of monkeys too listening attentively.

Sloka 16

sarvathā sukṛtam tāvatsītāyāḥ parimārgaṇam || 6-1-16
sāgaram tu samāsādya punarnaṣṭam mano mama |

16. parimārgeṇam = "the search; sītāyāḥ = for ṣita; tāvat = is so much; sukṛitam = well done; sarvathā = in all ways. tu = But; mama manaḥ = my mind; punaḥ = is once more; nashhṭam = lost; samasādya = on duly reaching; sāgaram = the ocean."

"The search for Sita has been performed so much well in all ways. But my mind gets dejected once more, when I behold this vast ocean."

Sloka 17

katham nāma samudrasya duṣpārasya mahāmbhasaḥ || 6-1-17
harayo dakṣiṇam pāram gamiṣyanti samāgatāh |

17. katham = "how; harayaḥ = (these) monkeys; samāgataḥ = put together; gamishhyanti = can obtain; dakshhiṇam = the southern; pāram = bank; samudrasya = of the ocean; dushhpārasya = which is difficult to cross; mahāmbhasaḥ = and which contains voluminous water?"

How can these monkeys put together will reach the southern bank of the ocean, which is so difficult to cross and which contains voluminous water?"

Sloka 18

yadyapyeṣa tu vṛttānto vaidehyā gadito mama || 6-1-18
samudrapāragamane harīṇām kimihottaram |

18. gaditoyadyapi = "even though informed; mama = to me; eshhaḥ = this; vṛittāntaḥ = result; vaidehyāḥ = by ṣita; kim = what is; uttaram = the answer; samudrapāragamane = to the crossing of ocean; harīṇām = by the monkeys?"

"Having received the tidings of Sita, what can now be done to take the monkeys to the farther side the sea?"

Sloka 19

ityuktvā śokasambhrānto rāmah śatrunibarhaṇaḥ || 6-1-19
hanūmantam mahābāhustato dhyānamupāgamat |

19. iti = thus; uktvā = speaking; hanunmantam = to ḥanumanta; rāmaḥ = Rama; shatrunibarhaṇaḥ = the destroyer of enemies; mahābāhuḥ = and the mighty armed; shoka sambhrāntaḥ = was perplexed with anguish; upāgamat = (then) became; dhyānam = thoughtful."

Thus speaking to Hanuman, Rama the destroyer of enemies and the mighty armed, was filled with apprehension and then became absorbed in thought.

- - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye yuddha kāṇḍe prathamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 1st chapter in the YuddhaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
