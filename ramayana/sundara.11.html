Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 11
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Realizing that the woman he saw at Ravana's house was not Seetha, Hanuma begins to search again for Seetha.


Sloka 1

avadhūya ca tām buddhim babhūva avasthitaḥ tadā |
jagāma ca aparām cintām sītām prati mahā kapiḥ || 5-11-1

1. mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma; tadā = then; avadhūya = removing; tām buddhim = that thought; bhabhūva = became avasthitaḥ = with the right mind; jagāma = and went; aparām chintām = (with)another thought; sītām prati = about ṣeetha.

ṭhe great ḥanuma then removing that thought became with the right mind and had another thought about Seetha.

Sloka 2

na rāmeṇa viyuktā sā svaptum arhati bhāminī |
na bhoktum na api alamkartum na pānam upasevitum || 5-11-2
na anyam naram upasthātum surāṇām api ca īśvaram |
na hi rāma samaḥ kaścid vidyate tridaśeṣv api || 5-11-3
anyā iyam iti niścitya pāna bhūmau cacāra saḥ |

2;3. sā bhāminī = that ṣeetha; na arhate = is not suitable; svaptum = to sleep ; rāmeṇa viyuktā = separated from Rama; na bhoktum = will not eat; na apyalamkartum api = will not decorate also; na = not suited; upasevitum = to drink; pānam = a beverage; upasthātum = to reach; anyam naram = another man; surāṇām eshvaram api = even though he were Indra; hi = because; na vidyate hi = there is indeed no; kashchit = one; rāmasamaḥ = equalling Rama; tridasheshhvapi = even among gods; iyam anyā = this is another woman; iti = thus; nishchitya = deciding; saḥ = that ḥanuma; chachāra = paced; pānabhūmau = in that banqueting hall.

ṭhat ṣeetha is not suitable to sleep separated from Rama, will not eat, will not decorate also, not suited to drink a beverage, to reach another man even though if he were Indra because there is indeed no one equaling Rama even among gods. This is another woman - thus deciding that Hanuma paced in that banqueting hall.

Sloka 4

krīḍitena aparāḥ klāntā gītena ca tathā parāḥ || 5-11-4
nṛttena ca aparāḥ klāntāḥ pāna viprahatāḥ tathā |

4. aparāḥ = some women; klāntāḥ = were tired; krīDitena = from erotic dalliance; tathā = and then; aparāḥ = some other women; gītena = (were tired) from singing; aparāḥ = some others; klāntāḥ = were weary; nṛittena = from dance; tathā = and; pānaviprahatāḥ = were unconscious from consumption of liquor.

ṣome women were tired from erotic dalliance and then some other women were tired from singing; some others were weary from dance and were unconscious from consumption of liquor.

Sloka 5

murajeṣu mRdangeṣu pīṭhikāsu ca saṃsthitāḥ || 5-11-5
tathā āstaraṇa mukhyyeṣu samviṣṭāḥ ca aparāḥ striyaḥ |

5. samsthitāḥ = (some women) were stationed; murajeshhu = on tabors; mṛidaṇ^geshhu = on mrudangās; pīṭhikāsu cha = on seats; aparāḥ = some other; striyaḥ = women; samvishhṭhāḥ = rested; āstaraṇamukhyeshhu = on chief carpets.

ṣome women were stationed on tabors, on ṃrudangas, on seats, some other women rested on chief carpets.

Sloka 6

aṅganānām sahasreṇa bhūṣitena vibhūṣaṇaiḥ || 5-11-6
rūpa samllāpa śīlena yukta gīta artha bhāṣiṇā |
deśa kāla abhiyuktena yukta vākya abhidhāyinā || 5-11-7
rata abhirata saṃsuptam dadarśa hari yūthapaḥ |

6;7. hariyūdhapaḥ = the warrior among Vanaras; dadarsha = saw; anṇ^ganānām sahasreṇa = a thousand of women; bhūshhitena = decorated; vibhūshhaṇaiḥ = by jewellery; rūpasallāpashīlena = with a nature of talking about beauty; yuktagītārthabhāshhiṇā = conversing about the correct meaning of songs(being sung) deshakālābhiyuktena = behaving according to time and place; yuktavākyābhidhāyinā = wont to speak appropriate words; rathābhirathasamsuptam = indulged in sleep after sexual dalliance.

ṭhe warrior among Vanaras saw a thousand of women decorated by jewellery with a nature of talking about beauty, conversing about the correct meaning of songs being sung, behaving according to time and place, wont to speak appropriate words, indulged in sleep after sexual dalliance.

Sloka 8

tāsām madhye mahā bāhuḥ śuśubhe rākṣasa īśvaraḥ || 5-11-8
goṣṭhe mahati mukhyānām gavām madhye yathā vṛṣaḥ |

8. madhye = in the middle; tāsām = of that group of women; rakshasheshvaraḥ = Ravana; mahābāhuḥ = with great arms; shushubhe = shone; vṛishhabhaiva = like a bull; gavām madhye = in the middle of cows; mahatighoshhṭhe = in a big cow-pen.

In the middle of that group of women, Ravana with great arms shone like a bull in the middle of cows in a big cow-pen.

Sloka 9

sa rākṣasa indraḥ śuśubhe tābhiḥ parivṛtaḥ svayam || 5-11-9
kareṇubhir yathā araṇyam parikīrṇo mahā dvipaḥ |

9. saḥ rākshasendraḥ svayam = that Ravana himself; parivṛitaḥ = surrounded; tābhiḥ = by those women; shushubhe = shone; mahādvipaḥ yathā = like a great elephant; parikīrṇaḥ = surrounded; kareṇubhiḥ = by she elephants; mahāraṇye = in a great forest;

ṭhat Ravana himself surrounded by those women, shone like a great elephant surrounded by she elephants in a great forest.

Sloka 10

sarva kāmair upetām ca pāna bhūmim mahātmanaḥ || 5-11-10
dadarśa kapi śārdūlaḥ tasya rakṣaḥ pater gṛhe |

10. harishārdhūlaḥ = the best among Vanaras; dadarsha = saw; tasya mahātmanaḥ rakshaḥpateḥ = in that wealthy Ravana's; gṛihe = house; pānabhūmim cha = a bar also; upetām = consisting; sarvakāmaiḥ = of all desirables.

ṭhe best among Vanaras saw in that wealthy Ravana's house a bar also, consisting of all desirables.

Sloka 11

mṛgāṇām mahiṣāṇām ca varāhāṇām ca bhāgaśaḥ || 5-11-11
tatra nyastāni māṃsāni pāna bhūmau dadarśa saḥ |

11. saḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; tatra = there; pānabhūmau = in that bar; māmsāni = meat; mṛigāṇām = of dear; mahishhāṇām cha = and of buffalo; varāhāṇām cha = of wild boar; nyastāni = kept; bhāgashaḥ = separately;

ḥanuma saw there in that bar, meat of dear and of buffalo, of wild boar kept separately.

Sloka 12

raukmeṣu ca viśaleṣu bhājaneṣv ardha bhakṣitān || 5-11-12
dadarśa kapi śārdūla mayūrān kukkuṭāmḥ tathā |

12. harishārdūlaḥ = the best among Vanaras; dadarsha = saw; ardhabhakshitān = half eaten; mayurān = peacocks; tathā = and; kukuṭān = chicken; vishāleshhu bhājaneshhu = in wide vessels; raukmeshhu = of golden colour.

ṭhe best among Vanaras saw half eaten peacocks and chicken in wide vessels of golden colour.

Sloka 13

varāha vārdhrāṇasakān dadhi sauvarcala āyutān || 5-11-13
śalyān mRga mayūrāmḥ ca hanūmān anvavaikṣata |

13. hanumān = ḥanuma; anvavaikshata = observed; varāhavārdhrāṇashakān = meat of pigs and goat; shalyān = porcupines; mṛigamayūrāmshcha = deer and peacocks; dadhisaūvarchalāyutān = preserved in curds and sochal salt.

ḥanuma observed meat of pigs and goats, porcupines, deer and peacocks preserved in curds and sochal salt.

Sloka 14

kṛkarān vividhān siddhāmḥ cakorān ardha bhakṣitān || 5-11-14
mahiṣān eka śalyāmḥ ca cāgāmḥ ca kṛta niṣṭhitān |
lekhyam ucca avacam peyam bhojyāni vividhāni ca || 5-11-15

14;15. (ḥanuma saw) krakarān = birds called Krakara; vividhānsiddhān = cooked ready(to be eaten) in variety of ways; chakorān = birds called chakoras; ardhabhakshitān = half eaten; mahishhān = wild buffalos; ekashalyāmshcha = fishes called ekashleya; chhāgāmshcha = goats; lehyān = food to be licked; vuchchāvachān = of various kinds; peyān = beverages; vividhāni bhojyāni = (and) various foods.

ḥanuma saw birds called Krakara cooked ready to be eaten in variety of ways, birds called chakoras half eaten, wild buffalos, fishes called ekashleya, goats, food to be licked of various kinds, beverages and various foods.

Sloka 16

tathā amla lavaṇa uttaṃsair vividhai rāga ṣāḍavaiḥ |
hāra nūpura keyūrair apaviddhair mahā dhanaiḥ || 5-11-16
pāna bhājana vikṣiptaiḥ phalaiḥ ca vividhair api |
kṛta puṣpa upahārā bhūr adhikam puṣyati śriyam || 5-11-17

16; 17. tathā = In the same way; bhūḥ = that floor; shriyam pushhyati = was obtaining glory; adhikam = greatly; rāgashhāDabaiḥ = with Ragas and ṣhadabas; āmla lavaṇottaṃ saiḥ = seasoned with sour and salty sauces; hāranūpurakeyūraiḥ = with necklaces; anklets and armlets; mahādhanaiḥ = of great value; apaviddhaiḥ = thrown around; vividhaiḥ phalaishcha = with various fruits; pānabhājana vikshiptaiḥ = left in drinking vessels; kṛita pushhpopa hārā = with flowers sprinkled.

In the same way that floor was obtaining glory greatly with Ragas and ṣhadabas seasoned with sour and salty sauces, with necklaces, anklets and armlets of great value thrown around, with various fruits left in drinking vessels, with flowers sprinkled.

Sloka 18

tatra tatra ca vinyastaiḥ suśliṣṭaiḥ śayana āsanaiḥ |
pāna bhūmir vinā vahnim pradīptā iva upalakṣyate || 5-11-18

18. pāna bhūmiḥ = ṭhat bar; upalakshyate = was seen; pradīpteva = as though radiant; vinā vahnim = without fire; shayanāsanaiḥ = with couches and chairs; sushlishhṭaiḥ = well arranged; vinyastaiḥ = (and) placed; tatra tatra = there and there.

ṭhat bar was seen as though radiant without fire, with couches and chairs well arranged and placed there and there.

Sloka 19

bahu prakārair vividhair vara saṃskāra saṃskṛtaiḥ |
māṃsaiḥ kuśala samyuktaiḥ pāna bhūmi gataiḥ pṛthak || 5-11-19

19. bahuprakāraiḥ māmsaiḥ = ṃany meats; vividhaiḥ = of different kinds; varasamskāra samskṛitaiḥ = cultured with various best seasonings; kushala saṃyuktaiḥ = well arranged; pṛithak = separately; pānabhūmigataiḥ = obtained that bar.

ṃany meats of different kinds cultured with various best seasonings, well arranged separately obtained that bar.

Sloka 20

divyāḥ prasannā vividhāḥ surāḥ kṛta surā api |
śarkara āsava mādhvīkāḥ puṣpa āsava phala āsavāḥ || 5-11-20
vāsa cūrṇaiḥ ca vividhair mṛṣṭāḥ taiḥ taiḥ pṛthak pṛthak |

20. divyāḥ = excellent; prasannāḥ = clear; vividhāḥ = various (liquors); surāḥ = liquor called ṣura; sharkarā sava = liquor made of sugar; mādhvīka = (liquor) made of honey; pushpā sava = liquor made of flowers; phalā savāḥ = and liquors made of fruits; kṛita surāḥ api = also artificially made liquors; taiḥ taiḥ = those and those; mṛishhṭāḥ = were cultured; pṛithak pṛithak = separate separately; vividhaiḥ vāsachūrṇaiḥ = with various fragrant powders

Excellent and clear various liquors, a liquor called ṣura, liquor made of sugar, liquor made of honey, liquor made of flowers and liquors made of fruits also, artificially made liquors - those and those were cultured separately with various fragrant powders.

Sloka 21

samtatā śuśubhe bhūmir mālyaiḥ ca bahu saṃsthitaiḥ || 5-11-21
hiraṇmayaiḥ ca karakair bhājanaiḥ sphāṭikair api |
jāmbūnadamayaiścānyāḥ karakairabhivamvṛtā || 5-11-22

21;22. bhūmiḥ = ṭhe floor; shushubhe = shone; saṃtatā = filled; mālyaishcha = by flower garlands; bahu saṃsthitaiḥ = in a variety of forms; vividhaiḥ bhājanaiḥ = with various vessels; hiraṇmayaiḥ = of golden hue; sphāṭikair api = and also made of crystal; abhisaṃvṛitā = filled; anyaiḥ karakaiḥ = with other small vessels; jāmbūnadamayaiḥ = of golden colour.

ṭhe floor shone filled by flower garlands in a variety of forms, with various vessels of golden hue and also made of crystal, filled with other small vessels of golden colour.

Sloka 23

rājateṣu ca kumbheṣu jāmbūnadamayeṣu ca |
pāna śreṣṭham tadā bhūri kapiḥ tatra dadarśa ha || 5-11-23

23. kapiḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha ha = saw indeed; tadā = then; bhūri pāna shreshhṭham = lot of best quality liquor; kumbheshhu = in pots; rājateshhu = of silver; jāmbūnadamayeshhu = and of golden colour.

ḥanuma saw indeed then lot of best quality liquor in pots of silver and of golden colour.

Sloka 24

so apaśyat śāta kumbhāni śīdhor maṇimayāni ca |

24. saḥ mahā kapiḥ = ṭhat great ḥanuma; apashyat = saw; bhājanāni = vessels; pūrṇāni = full; shīdhoḥ = of liquor; shātakumbhāni = of golden colour; maṇimayāni cha = embedded with gem stones; rājatāni cha = and also of silver hue.

ṭhat great ḥanuma saw vessels full of liquor of golden colour, embedded with gem stones and also of silver hue.

Sloka 25

kvacid ardha avaśeṣāṇi kvacit pītāni sarvaśaḥ |
kvacin na eva prapītāni pānāni sa dadarśa ha || 5-11-25

25. saḥ = ṭhat ḥanuma; dadarsha ha = saw indeed; kvachit = at some places; pānāni = drinks; ardhāva sheshhāṇi = half filled; kvachit = (and) some places; sarvashaḥ pītāni = completely drunk; kvachit = (and) some places; naiva prapītāni = not at all drunk.

ṭhat ḥanuma saw indeed at some places drinks half filled and at some places completely drunk and some places not at all drunk.

Sloka 26

kvacid bhakṣyāmḥ ca vividhān kvacit pānāni bhāgaśaḥ |

26. vichachāra ha = (ḥanuma) paced about; pashyan = seeing; kvachit = (at) some places; vividhān bhakshāṃshcha = various eatables; kvachit = (and) some places; pānāni = drinks; bhāgashaḥ = separately; kvachit = some places; annavasheshhāṇi = remnants of cooked rice.

ḥanuma paced about seeing at some places various eatables and at some places drinks separately, and at some places remnants of cooked rice.

Sloka 27

kvacit prabhinnaiḥ karakaiḥ kvacid āloḍitair ghaṭaiḥ |
kvacit sampṛkta mālyāni jalāni ca phalāni ca || 5-11-27

27. kvachit = (ḥanuma saw) some places; karakaiḥ = with vessels; prabhinnaiḥ = broken; kvachit = some places; ghaṭaiḥ = with pots; aloLitaiḥ = in shambles; kvachit = some places; jalāni cha = (with) water; saṃpṛiktamālyāni = together with flower garlands; phalāni cha = and fruits.

ḥanuma saw some places with vessels broken, some places with pots in shambles, some places with water together with flower garlands and fruits.

Sloka 28

śayanāni atra nārīṇām śūnyāni bahudhā punaḥ |
parasparam samāśliṣya kāścit suptā vara anganāḥ || 5-11-28

28. atra = ḥere; shayanāni punaḥ = couches again; nāriṇām = of women; bahudhā shubhrāṇi = were varied and clean; kāshchit = some; varāṇ^ganāḥ = best women; suptāḥ = slept; samāshlishhya = hugging; parasparam = one another

ḥere couches again of women were varied and clean, some best women slept there hugging one another.

Sloka 29

kācic ca vastram anyasyā apahRtya upaguhya ca |
upagamya abalā suptā nidrā bala parājitā || 5-11-29

29. kāshchit abalāḥ = ṣome women; nidrābalaparājitāḥ = conquered by the power of sleep; āhṛitya = pulled; anyasyāḥ svapantyāḥ vastram = other sleeping women's clothing; suptāḥ = and slept; paridhāya = covering (themselves).

ṣome women conquered by the power of sleep pulled other sleeping women's clothing and slept covering themselves.

Sloka 30

tāsām uccvāsa vātena vastram mālyam ca gātrajam |
na atyartham spandate citram prāpya mandam iva anilam || 5-11-30

30. vastram mālyam cha = Clothing and garland; tāsām gātrajam = on the throats of those (women); spandate = was moving; na atyartham = slightly; chitram = (and) wonderfully; prāpya iva = as though obtaining; anilam = air; uchchhvāsavātena = from the air of breath.

Clothing and garland on the throats of those women was moving slightly and wonderfully as though obtaining air from the air of breath.

Sloka 31

candanasya ca śītasya śīdhor madhu rasasya ca |
vividhasya ca mālyasya puṣpasya vividhasya ca || 5-11-31
bahudhā mārutaḥ tatra gandham vividham udvahan |

31. tatra = ṭhere; mārutaḥ = wind (blew); udvahan = carrying; bahudhā = in a lot (of directions); vividham gandham = a variety of fragrances; shītasya gandhasya = of cool sandal-paste; madhurasasya shīdhoḥ cha = and of sweet liquor; vividhasya mālyasya = of various flower garlands; vividhasya dhūpasya cha = and of various kinds of agallocum fumes.

ṭhere wind blew carrying in a lot of directions, a variety of fragrances of cool sandal-paste and of sweet liquor, of various flower garlands and of various kinds of agallocum fumes.

Sloka 32

snānānām candanānām ca dhūpānām caiva mūrcitaḥ |
pravavau surabhir gandho vimāne puṣpake tadā || 5-11-32

32. tadā = ṭhen; surabhiḥ gandhaḥ = the blossoming fragrance; snānānām chandanānām cha = from the baths and sandal paste; dhūpānām chaiva = and from agallocum fumes; pravavau = blew; mūrchitaḥ = spreading (all around); pushhpake vimāne = in that Pushpaka plane.

ṭhen the blossoming fragrance from the baths and sandal paste and from agallocum fumes blew spreading all around in that Pushpaka plane.

Sloka 33

śyāma avadātāḥ tatra anyāḥ kāścit kṛṣṇā vara anganāḥ || 5-11-33

ṭhere in that Ravana's house, some women had fair and white complexion some best women were black, some women had a body of golden complexion.

Sloka 34

34. rūpam = appearance; tāsām = of those; prasuptānām = sleeping (women); vimūrchitam = tired; nidrāvashatvācha = from being possessed by sleep; madanenacha = and from lust; āsīt = was; yathaiva = like; padminīnām = lotuses; prasuptānām = sleeping.

Appearance of those sleeping women, tired from being possessed by sleep and from lust, was like lotuses sleeping.

Sloka 35

35. kapiḥ = ḥanuma; sumahātejāḥ = with great radiance; evam = thus; dadarsha = saw; sarvam = entire; rāvaṇātḥpuram = Ravana's house; asheshhena = completely; na dadarsha = (but) did not see; jānakīm cha = ṣeetha.

ḥanuma with great radiance thus saw entire Ravana's house completely but did not see ṣeetha.

Sloka 36

36. tadā = then; saḥ mahākapiḥ = that ḥanuma; nirīkshmāṇaḥ = seeing; tāḥ striyaḥ = those women; jagāmā = obtained; mahatīm chintām = great anguish; dharmasādvaḥsasheṇ^kitaḥ = being in doubt because of fear in the matter of dharma.

ṭhen that ḥanuma seeing those women obtained great anguish being in doubt because of fear in the matter of dharma.

Sloka 37

37. mama = my; idam = this; nirīkshaṇam = seeing; prasuptasya = sleeping; paradārāvarodhasya = house of other people's wives; karishhyati = will do; atyartham dharmalopam = a great deficit to dharma.

"ṃy seeing sleeping house of other people's wives will do a great deficit to dharma."

Sloka 38

38. me = my; dṛishhṭaḥ = sight; na hi = is indeed not; paradārāṇām vishhayavartinī = in the matter of others wives; atra = here; ayam = these paradāraparigrahaḥ = other's wives; dṛishhṭashcha = have been seen ; mayā = by me;

"ṃy sight is indeed not in the matter of other wives here. ṭhese other's wives have been seen by me."

Sloka 39

39. tasya = to that ḥanuma; manasvinaḥ = with an excellent mind; nishchitaikāntachittasya = with a fixed and concentrated mind; pradurabhūt = was born; anyā = another; chintā = thought; punaḥ = again ; kāryanishchayadarshinī = showing a fixed resolve in the task at hand.

ṭo that ḥanuma with an excellent mind, with a fixed and concentrated mind, was born another thought again, showing a fixed resolve in the task at hand.

Sloka 40

kāmam dṛṣṭvā mayā sarvā viśvastā rāvaṇa striyaḥ || 5-11-40
na tu me manasaḥ kiṃcid vaikṛtyam upapadyate |

40. sarvāḥ = all; rāvaṇastriyaḥ = Ravana's women; vishvastāḥ = who were in faith; kāmam dṛishhṭāḥ = could have been seen; mayā = by me; me manasaḥ = to my mind; na upajāyate hi = there indeed was not; kimchit = even a little; vaikṛityam = disturbance.

"All Ravana's women who were in faith could have been seen by me; to my mind there indeed was not even a little disturbance".

Sloka 41

mano hi hetuḥ sarveṣām indriyāṇām pravartate || 5-11-41
śubha aśubhāsv avasthāsu tac ca me suvyavasthitam |

41. shubhāshubhāsu = among auspicious or inauspicious; avasthāsu = states; pravartane- in the behavior; sarveshhānām indriyāṇām = of all senses; manaḥ hetuḥ = mind is the reason; me = my; tachcha = that mind; suvyavasthitam = is very steady.

"Among auspicious or inauspicious states in the behavior of all senses mind is the reason. ṃy that mind is very steady."

Sloka 42

na anyatra hi mayā śakyā vaidehī parimārgitum || 5-11-42
striyo hi strīṣu dRśyante sadā samparimārgaṇe |

42. mayā = by me; na shakhyā hi = it is not possible; parimārgitum = to search; vaidehī = ṣeetha; anyatra = at another place; sadā = always; samparimārgaṇe = during search; striyaḥ = women; dṛishyante = will be seen; strishhu = among women.

"By me it is not possible to search ṣeetha at another place. Always during search women will be seen among other women."

Sloka 43

yasya sattvasya yā yoniḥ tasyām tat parimārgyate || 5-11-43
na śakyam pramadā naṣṭā mṛgīṣu parimārgitum |

43. yasya sattvasya = to an animal; yā yoniḥ = whatever race; tat = that animal; parimārgyate = is searched; tasyām = in that race; pramadā = a woman; nashhṭā = missing; na shakhyā = is not possible; parimārgitum = to be searched; mṛigīshhu = among female deer.

"ṭo an animal of whatever race that animal is searched in that race; a woman missing is not possible to be searched among female deer."

Sloka 44

tad idam mārgitam tāvat śuddhena manasā mayā || 5-11-44
rāvaṇa antaḥ puram saram dṛśyate na ca jānakī |

44. tat = for that reason; idam sarvam = all this; rāvaṇātaḥpuram = Ravana's house; mārgitam = has been search; mayā = by me; shuddhena = with a pure; manasā = mind; Aramaic = ṣeetha alone; na dṛishyate = cannot be seen.

"For that reason all this Ravana's house has been search by me with a pure mind ṣeetha alone cannot be seen."

Sloka 45

deva gandharva kanyāḥ ca nāga kanyāḥ ca vīryavān || 5-11-45
avekṣamāṇo hanumān na eva apaśyata jānakīm |

45. vīryavān = the strong; hanumān = ḥanuma; avekshamāṇaḥ = observing; devagandharvakanyāshcha = girls from devas; Gandharvas; nāgakanyāshcha = and girls from nagas; naivāvaikshata = did not see; jānakīm = Janaki.

ṭhe strong ḥanuma observing girls from devas, gandharvas and girls from nagas did not see Janaki.

Sloka 46

tām apaśyan kapiḥ tatra paśyamḥ ca anyā vara striyaḥ || 5-11-46
apakramya tadā vīraḥ pradhyātum upacakrame |

46. vīraḥ kapiḥ = the powerful ḥanuma; apashyan = not seeing; tām = ḥer; tatra = there; pashyamshcha = and seeing; anyāḥ varastriyaḥ = other best women; tadā = then; upachakrame = started; pradhyātum = to think deeply; apakramya = going far(from there).

ṭhe powerful ḥanuma not seeing ḥer there and seeing other best women then started to think deeply going far from there.

Sloka 47

sa bhūyastu param śrīmān mārutiryatnamāsthitaḥ || 5-11-47
apānabhūmimutsṛjya tadvicetum pracakrame |

47. saḥ mārutiḥ = that ḥanuma; shrīmān = the glorious one; āstitaḥ = adopting; param yatnam = a great task; bhūyaḥ = again; utsṛijya = left; āpānabhūmim = that bar; prachakrame = (and) began; vichetum = to search; tat = that (house).

ṭhat ḥanuma the glorious one adopting a great task again left that bar and began to search that house.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ekādaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 11th chapter in Sundara Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
