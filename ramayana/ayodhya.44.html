Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda


Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 44
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Establishing the greatness of Rama, Sumitra of gracious speech, assuages Kausalya's grief.


Sloka 1

vilapantīm tathā tām tu kausalyām pramada uttamām |
idam dharme sthitā dharmyam sumitrā vākyam abravīt || 2-44-1

1. sumitrā = ṣumitra; sthitā = devoted; dharma = to righteousness; abravīt = spoke; idam vākyam = these words; dharmam = which were virtuous; tām kausalyām = to that Kausalya; vilapantīm = who was wailing; tathā = thus; pramadottamām = and who was best among women.

Sumitra, who was devoted to righteousness spoke the following words, which were virtuous, to the celebrated Kausalya, the foremost among women, who had been wailing as aforesaid

Sloka 2

tava ārye sad guṇaiḥ yuktaḥ putraḥ sa puruṣa uttamaḥ |
kim te vilapitena evam kṛpaṇam ruditena vā || 2-44-2

ārye = "Oh; the venerable one! tava = your; saḥ putraḥ = that son Rama; yuktaḥ = is endowed with; sadguṇaiaḥ = excellent virtues; purushhottamaḥ = and foremost among men; kim = what is the use in; te = your; vilapitena = lamenting; evam = thus; ruditenavā = or weeping ; kṛipaṇam = piteously?"

"That son of yours, oh the venerable one, is endowed with excellent virtues and is the foremost among men. What is the use in your lamenting in this way or in weeping piteously?"

Sloka 3

yaḥ tava ārye gataḥ putraḥ tyaktvā rājyam mahā balaḥ |
sādhu kurvan mahātmānam pitaram satya vādinām || 2-44-3
śiṣṭaiḥ ācarite samyak śaśvat pretya phala udaye |
rāmaḥ dharme sthitaḥ śreṣṭho na sa śocyaḥ kadācana || 2-44-4

3;4. ārye = Oh; noble lady! yaḥ = Which; tava putraḥ = your son; mahābalaḥ = having mighty strength; kurvan = proving;apitaram = his father; mahātmānam = the high-soled; sādhu = perfectly;satyavādinam = true-speaking; tyaktvā = by renouncing; rājyam = the throne; sthitaḥ = standing firmly; dharme = in righteousness; ācharite = which is practiced; shashvat = always; samyak = perfectly;sishhyaiḥ = by disciples; phalodaye = which gives fruits; pretya = even after death; saḥ = that; rāmaḥ = Rama; shreshhṭaḥ = the most excellent; na kadāchana = never; shochya = deserves to be lamented. "

"Oh, noble lady! Your son is having mighty strength. He proved his high- soled father perfectly truthful by renouncing his throne. He stood firmly in righteousness, which is always practiced perfectly by disciples and which gives fruits even after death. That Rama, the most excellent man, never deserves to be lamented."

Sloka 5

vartate ca uttamām vṛttim lakṣmaṇo asmin sadā anaghaḥ |
dayāvān sarva bhūteṣu lābhaḥ tasya mahātmanaḥ || 2-44-5

5. lakshhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; anaghaḥ = the faultless man; dayavān = and the compassionate man; sarva bhūteshhu = towards all beings; sadā = always; vartate = conducts; vṛittim = his behaviour; uttamām = excellently;asmin = in the case of Rama. Lābhaḥ = It is beneficial; tasya = to him(Rama); mahātmanaḥ = the high-soled man."

"Lakshmana, the faultless man and the compassionate man towards all beings, always renders the best form of service to Rama. His conduct is beneficial to the high-soled Rama."

Sloka 6

araṇya vāse yad duhkham jānatī vai sukha ucitā |
anugaccati vaidehī dharma ātmānam tava ātmajam || 2-44-6

6. "vaidehi = ṣita; sukhochitā = who was habituated to comforts; jānatīvai = even after knowing; duḥkham = the pain; yat = whatever; anugachchhati = is accompanying; tava = your; ātmajam = son; dharmātmānam = the virtuous soled."

Sita, who was habituated to comforts, even after knowing the pain whatsoever, is accompanying your virtuous- soled son."

Sloka 7

kīrti bhūtām patākām yo loke bhrāmayati prabhuḥ |
dama satya vrata paraḥ kim na prāptaḥ tava ātmajaḥ || 2-44-7

7. yaḥ = Who; prabhuḥ = the capable man; bhramayati = fluttering; patākam = the banner; kīrti bhūtām = of fame; loke = in the world; tava ātmajaḥ = that son of yours; dharmaḥsatyavrata paraḥ = who is engrossed in virtue and is pledged to the vow of truthfulness; kim = what; na praptaḥ = has not been secured(by him)?"

"What has not been secured by your capable son, who is rich in virtue and is engrossed in the vow of truthfulness, and the banner of whose fame flutters though out the world?"

Sloka 8

vyaktam rāmasya vijñāya śaucam māhātmyam uttamam |
na gātram aṃśubhiḥ sūryaḥ samtāpayitum arhati || 2-44-8

8. viG^yāya = ḥaving known; shoucham = purity; uttamam = and the mighty; māhātmayam = magnanimity; rāmasya = of Rama; sūryaḥ = the sun; vyaktam = will assuredly; na arhati = not be able; santāpayitum = to torment; gātram = his body; amshubhiḥ = with his rays."

"Having known the purity and magnanimity of Rama , the sun assuredly will not be able to torment Rama's body with his rays."

Sloka 9

śivaḥ sarveṣu kāleṣu kānanebhyo vinihsṛtaḥ |
rāghavam yukta śīta uṣṇaḥ seviṣyati sukho anilaḥ || 2-44-9

9. shivaḥ = Auspicious; sukhaḥ = and delightful; anilaḥ = breeze; yuktashītoshhṇaḥ = of moderate heat and cold; viniḥsṛitaḥ = blowing; kānanebhyaḥ = from woods; sarveshhu kāleshhu = at all times; sevishhyati = will render service; rāghavam = to Rama."

"Auspicious and delightful breeze of moderate heat and cold, blowing from woods at all times , will render service to Rama"

Sloka 10

śayānam anagham rātrau pitā iva abhipariṣvajan |
raśmibhiḥ saṃspṛśan śītaiḥ candramā hlādayiṣyati || 2-44-10

10. samspṛishan = gently touching;rashmibhiḥ = with its rays; shītāiḥ = which are cool; shayānam = when he is reposing; rātrou = at night; abhiparishhvajan = and embracing him ; piteva = like his father; chandraḥ = the moon; ahlādayishhyati = will refresh him."

"Gently touching with its cool rays when he is reposing at night and embracing him like a father, the moon will refresh Rama"

Sloka 11

dadau ca astrāṇi divyāni yasmai brahmā mahā ojase |
dānava indram hatam dṛṣṭvā timi dhvaja sutam raṇe || 2-44-11
sa śūraḥ puruṣavyāghraḥ svabāhubalamāśritaḥ |
asantrasto.a pyaraṇyastho veśmanīva nivatsyati || 2-44-12

11;12. dṛishhṭvā = ṣeeing; timidhvajasutam = ṣubahu; the son of ṣhambarasura; dānavendram = the chief of demons ; hatam = being killed; raṇe = in battle; brahmā = Brahma ;the god of creation; dadan = gave; yasmai = to which Rama; mahanjase = the man with great splendor; divyāni = the celestial ; astrāṇi = weapons; saḥ = that Rama; shūraḥ = the hero; purushha vyāghraḥ = and the tiger among men; āshritaḥ = relying; svabāhubalam = on the strength of his own arms; nivatsyasi = will dwell; asantrastaḥ = fearlessly; araṇyastho. api = even when living in the forest; veshmamīva = as his own palace."

"Formerly, when Rama killed Subahu the son of Sambarasura the chief of demons, Brahma being delighted , gave celestial weapons to Rama, the man with great splendor. Rama the hero and the tiger among men, relying on the strength of his own arms, will dwell fearlessly in the forest as if in his own palace"

Sloka 13

yasyeṣupadamāsādya vināśam yānti śatravaḥ |
katham na pṛthivī tasya śāsane sthātumarhati || 2-44-13

13. yasya = Whose; ishhupadam = aim of arrows; āsādya = obtains;yānti = getting into; vināsham = destruction; shatrvaḥ = of enemies; tasya = in such Rama's; shāsane = rule; katham = why; pṛithivī = earth; nārhati = cannot; sthātum = be stable?"

"How can earth cannot be under the command of Rama, to whose aim of arrows, the enemies fall an easy prey?"

Sloka 14

yā śrīḥśauryam ca rāmasya yā ca kalyāṇasatvatā |
nivṛttāraṇyavāsaḥ svaṃ kṣipram rājyamavāpsyati || 2-44-14

14. yā = which; shrīḥ = splendor; rāmasya = of Rama; yā = which; shouryamcha = velour; kalyāna sattvatā = the beneficent strength that resides in him; avāpsyati = will procure; saḥ = that Rama; svam rājyam = his own kingdom; kshhipram = soon; nivṛittāraṇya vāsaḥ = after completion of his exile in the forest."

"The splendor endowed with Rama, the valor that is in him and the beneficent strength that resides in him lead one to believe that when he completes the term of his exile in the forest, he will speedily regain his kingdom."

Sloka 15

sūryasyāpi bhavetsūryohyagneragniḥ prabhoḥ prabhoḥ |
śriyaśca śrīrbhavedagryā kīrtyāḥ kṣamākṣamā || 2-44-15
daivatam daivatānām ca bhūtānām bhūtasattamaḥ |
tasya kehyaguṇā devi vane vā pyathavā pure || 2-44-16

15;16. devi = Oh; Kausalya!; bhavet = ḥe becomes; sūryaḥ = illuminator; sūryasyāpi = of even the sun; agniḥ = fire; (revealer) ; agneḥ = of fire; prabhuḥ = the ruler; prabhoḥ = of rules; shrīḥ = the splendor; shriyashcha = of splendor; agryā = the foremost; kīrtiḥ = glory(essence); kīrtyāḥ = of glory; kshhamā kshhamā = the forbearance of forbearance; daivatam = the god; daivatānām = of gods; bhūtasattamaḥ = the foremost being; bhūtānām = of beings. ke hi = Indeed what; aguṇāḥ = handicaps(for him); vanevāpi = in the forest; athavā = or; pure = in Ayodhya?"

"Oh, Kausalya! Rama is the illuminator of even the sun, fire of fire, the ruler of rules, the splendor of splendor, the foremost glory of glory, the forbearance of forbearance, the god of gods and the foremost being of all the beings. Indeed, what handicaps are there for him in the forest or in Ayodhya?"

Sloka 17

pṛthivyā saha vaidehyā śriyā ca puruṣa ṛṣabhaḥ |
kṣipram tisṛbhir etābhiḥ saha rāmaḥ abhiṣekṣyate || 2-44-17

17. rāmaḥ = Rama; purushharrshhabhaḥ = the best among men; abhishhekshyate = will be installed on the throne; kshhipram = soon; pṛithivyāsaha = along with the ṃother Earth(the consort of Lord Vishnu); vaidehyā = ṣita(princess of Vaideha kingdom)shriyācha = and Lakshmi (the goddess of Fortune another consort oh Vishnu)"

"Rama the best among men will be installed on the throne soon along with the ṃother Earth (the consort of lord Vishnu), Sita the princess of Videha kingdom and Lakshmi ( the goddess of fortune another consort of Vishnu)"

Sloka 18

duhkhajam visṛjanti asram niṣkrāmantam udīkṣya yam |
samutsrakṣyasi netrābhyām kṣipram ānandajam payaḥ || 2-44-18
kuśacīradharam devam gacchantamaparājitam |
sītevānugatā lakṣmīstasya kim nāma durlabham || 2-44-19

18;19. udikshhya = ṣeeing; yam = which Rama; nishhkrāmantam = departing; sarve = all; janāḥ = the people; ayodhyām = in Ayodhya; shoka vega samāhatāḥ = smitten as they were with upsurge of grief; visṛijanti = shedding; ashruḥ = tears; duḥkhajam = born of agony; devam = which Rama; gachchhantam = is departing; kushachīra dharam = clad in a garment of the sacred Kusa grass; anugatā = followed by; lakshmiḥ = the goddess of fortune; sīteva = as ṣita; tasya = for such Rama; kimnāma = which; durlabham = is difficult to obtain?"

"Seeing Rama departing, all the people in Ayodhya smitten as they were with upsurge of grief shed tears born of agony. The goddess of fortune as Sita followed the invincible hero even as he departed for the forest, clad in a garment of the sacred Kusa grass. Indeed, what can be difficult for him to obtain?"

Sloka 20

dhanurgrahavaro yasya bāṇakhaḍgāstrabhṛtsvayam |
lakṣmaṇovrajati hyagre tasya kim nāma durlabham || 2-44-20

20. lakshmanaḥ = Lakshmana; dhanurgraha varaḥ = the best among the holders of bow; svayam = himself; bāṇa khadgāstrabhṛit = wearing arrows; sword and missiles; vrajati = walking ; agre = in front; yasya = of whom; tasya = to that Rama; kimnāma = what is ;durlabham = difficult to obtain?"

"What can be difficult to obtain for, to whose front indeed walks Lakshmana himself the best among the holders of bow wielding arrows, sword and missiles?"

Sloka 21

nivṛttavanavāsam tam draṣṭāsi punarāgatam |
jahiśokam ca moham ca devi satyam bravīmi te || 2-44-21

21. devi = oh; Kausalya! ; drashhṭāsi = you can see; tam = that Rama; āgatam = when he comes; punaḥ = again; nivRitta vanavāsam = having completed the period of dwelling in the forest. jahi = abandon; shokamcha = grief ; mohamcha = and infatuation; bravīmi = I tell; te = you; satyam = the truth "

"Oh, Kausalya! I tell you the truth : you can see Rama coming back, on having completed the term of his exile in the forest . Abandon grief and infatuation."

Sloka 22

śirasā caraṇāvetau vandamānamanindite |
punardrakṣyasi kalyāṇi! putraṃ candramivoditam || 2-44-22

22. kalyāṇi = Oh; auspicious Kausalya! anindite = the irreproachable one! Drakshhyasi = you will be able to see; putram = your son; chandramiva = like the moon; uditam = who has risen; vandamānam = saluting respectfully; etou = these; charaṇou = feet; sirasā = with the head."

"Oh, auspicious Kausalya, the irreproachable one! You will be able to see your son, like the rising moon, saluting respectfully your feet, by bowing his head."

Sloka 23

punaḥ praviṣṭam dṛṣṭvā tamabhiṣiktam mahāśriyam |
samutsrakṣyasi netrābhyām kṣipramānndajam payaḥ || 2-44-23

23. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam = that Rama; mahāshhriyam = with great splendor pravishhṭam = appearing ; punaḥ = again; abhishhiktam = having been coroneted; samutsryakshhasi = you will be shedding ; kshhipram = soon; payaḥ = tears; ānandajam = born of joy; netrābhyām = from your eyes."

"Seeing Rama shining with great splendor appearing before you again, having been coroneted, you will be shedding soon tears of joy from your eyes "

Sloka 24

mā śoko devi duḥkham vā na rāame dṛahyRW.aśivam |
kṣipram drakṣyasi putram tvam sasītam saha lakṣmaṇam || 2-44-24

24. devi = Oh; Kausalya! mā = Do not have; shokaḥ = grief; duḥkhamvā = or uneasiness. ashivam = Ill luck; na dṛishyate = is not being seen; rāme = in Rama; tvam = you; drakshhyasi = will be able to see; kshhipram = soon; putram = your son; sa sītam = along with ṣita; saha lakshhmaṇam = together with Lakshmana."

"Oh, Kausalya! Do not have uneasiness or grief. Rama has no ill luck. You will be able to see soon your son along with Sita, accompanied by Lakshmana".

Sloka 25

tvayā śeṣoo janaścaiva samāśvāsyo yadā.anaghe |
kimidānīmidam devi karoṣi hṛdi viklabam || 2-44-25

25. devi = "Oh; queen; anaghe = the faultless! Yadā = Whereas; janaḥ = people; samāshvāsaḥ = are to be consoled; tvayā = by you; idānīm = now; karoshhi = you are promoting; viklabam = grief; hṛidi = in your heart ; kim = what ; idam = is this ?"

"Oh, the faultless queen! While people are to be consoled by you at this moment, why are you fostering grief in your heart?"

Sloka 26

nārhā tvam śocitum devi yasyāste rāghavassutaḥ |
na hi rāmātparo loke vidyate satpathe sthitaḥ || 2-44-26

26. devi = oh; queen! Yasyāḥ = which; te = to you; rāghavaḥ = Rama; sutāḥ = is your son; tvam = you as such; nārhā = are not deserving; shochitum = to aggrieve. navidyate = ṇone is there; loke = in the world; paraḥ = other; rāmāt = than Rama; sthitaḥ = who is established; satpathe = in right path."

"Oh,queen! You, whose son is Rama, ought not to grieve, for none is established in right path in the world than Rama."

Sloka 27

abhivādayamānam tam dṛṣṭvā sasuhṛdam sutam |
mudā aśru mokṣyase kṣipram megha lekā iva vārṣikī || 2-44-27

27. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam sutam = that son; sa suhṛidam = along with friends ; abhivādamānau = saluting; mokshhyase = you will be shedding; ashru = tears; mudā = with joy; kshhipram = soon; megharekheva = like a line of clouds; vārshhikī = belonging to rainy season "

"Seeing your son saluting with his friends, you will at once begin to shed tears, like a line of clouds in a rainy season"

Sloka 28

putraḥ te varadaḥ kṣipram ayodhyām punar āgataḥ |
pāṇibhyām mṛdupīnābhyām caraṇau pīḍayiṣyati || 2-44-28

28. te putraḥ = Your son; varadaḥ = who is the bestower of boons; kshhipram = soon; punarāgataḥ = returning; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; pīdayishhyati = will press; charaṇou = your feet; pāṇibhyām = with his hands; mṛidu pīnābhyām = which are gentle but muscular."

"Your son the bestower of boons, will soon return to Ayodhya, and touch your feet with his gentle but muscular hands "

Sloka 29

abhivādya namasyantam śūram sasuhṛdam sutam |
mudāsraiḥ prokṣyasi punarmegharāji rivācalam || 2-44-29

29. sutam = your son; shūram = the hero; sa suhṛidam = along with his friends; abhivādya = saluting respectfully; namasyantam = in adoration; prokshhyasi = you will sprinkle; astraiḥ = tears; mudā = with joy; achalamiva = like on a mountain; megharājiḥ = by a line of clouds."

"When your son the hero along with his friends salute you respectfully in adoration, you will sprinkle tears of joy in the same way as a line of clouds pour rain on the mountain."

Sloka 30

āśvāsayantī vividhaiśca vākyai |
rvākyopacāre kuśalānādyā |
rāmasya tām mātaramevamuktvā |
devī sumitrāvirarāma rāmā || 2-44-30

30. sumitra = ṣumitra; devī = the queen; kushalā = skilled; vākyopachāre = in employment of words; anavadyā = who is faultless; rāmā = and charming; evam = thus; āshvāsayantī = consoled; rāmasya = Rama's; tām mātaram = that mother; uktvā = by speaking; vividhaiḥ = with various; vākyaiḥ = words; virarāma = and stopped.

Sumitra the queen, skilled in her employment of words, who is faultless and charming, thus consoled Rama's mother with various words and finally stopped speaking.

Sloka 31

niśamya tal lakṣmaṇa mātṛ vākyam |
rāmasya mātur nara deva patnyāḥ |
sadyaḥ śarīre vinanāśa śokaḥ |
śarad gataḥ meghaiva alpa toyaḥ || 2-44-31

31. nishamya = after hearing; tat = those; lakshmanamātru vākyam = words of Lakshmana's mother; shokaḥ = the agony; rāmasya mātuḥ = of Rama's mother; naradeva patnyāḥ = and the wife of Dasaratha; sadyaḥ = quickly; vinanāsha = disappeared; sharīre = in her body; megha iva = like a cloud; alpatoyaḥ = with a little water ; sharadgataḥ = disappears in autumn .

Hearing those words of Sumitra the mother of Lakshmana, the agony of Kausalya Rama's mother and the wife of Dasaratha quickly disappeared in the same way as an autumnal cloud with a little water in it disappears quickly.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe catuścatvāriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 44th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
