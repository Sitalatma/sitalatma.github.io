Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 1
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Valmiki describes the innumerable virtues of Sree Rama in this sarga. Dasaratha wants to coronate Rama as the crown prince with the approval of all his people. With this end in mind, he invites various citizens, important people from all cities and villages in his kingdom.

Sloka 1

gacchatā mātulakulaṃ bharatena tadā.anaghaḥ |
śatrughno nityaśatrughno nītaḥ prītipuraskṛtaḥ || 2-1-1

1. shatrughnaḥ = ṣatrughna; anaghaḥ = who has no sins; nitya shatrughnaḥ = he who always annihilated his enemies; neetaḥ = was taken; prīti puraskṛithaḥ = with love; bharathena = by Bharatha; gachchhathā = while going; tadā = then; mātulakulam = to the maternal uncle's house.

Bharatha, while going to his maternal uncle's house, has taken his brother Satrughna (he who has no sins and who annihilates his enemies) along with him with love.

Sloka 2

sa tatra nyavasadbhrātrā saha satkārasatkṛtaḥ |
mātulenāśvapatinā putrasnehena lālitaḥ || 2-1-2

2. satkāra satkṛithaḥ = treated with good hospitality; putra snehena = with paternal love; asvapathinā = lord of cavalry; māthulena = his maternal uncle; saḥ = that Bharatha; bhratrā saḥ = with his brother; nyavasat = stayed; tatra = there.

Treated with good hospitality and paternal love by his maternal uncle, Yudhajit, who was a lord of cavalry, Bharatha stayed with his brother there.

Sloka 3

tatrāpi nivasantau tau tarpyamāṇau ca kāmataḥ |
bhrātarau smaratāṃ vīrau vṛddhaṃ dasarathaṃ nṛpam || 2-1-3

3. vīrau = those heroes; bhrātarau = both the brothers; nivasanthau = staying; tatra = there; tarpyamāṇauchāpi = though satisfied by; kāmathaḥ = all the wants; smarathāṃ = were remebering; dasaratham nṛipam = the king Dasaratha.

Those heroes of valour Bharatha and Satrughna, though staying there enjoying all the comforts, were remembering their age old father.

Sloka 4

rājāpi tau mahātejāḥ sasmāra proṣitau sutau |
ubhau bharataśatrughnau mahendravaruṇopamau || 2-1-4

4. mahā tejāḥ = ṃighty; rājāpi = king (Dasaratha); also; sasmāra = was remembering; sutau = his sons; bharata shatrughnau ubhau = both Bharatha and ṣatrughna; proshhitau = who were out of state; mahendra varunopamau = (and who were) equivalent to Indra and Varuna.

The mighty Dasaratha was also often remembering his sons Bharatha and Satrughna who were out of his state and who were equivalent to Indra and Varuna.

Sloka 5

sarva eva tu tasyeṣṭa ścatvāraḥ puruṣarṣabhāḥ |
svaśarīrādvinirvṛttāścatvāra iva bāhavaḥ || 2-1-5

5. tasya = that Dasaratha; isṭāḥ = was loving; chatvāraḥ = his four sons; purushharshhabhāḥ = best among men; chatvāraḥ bāhavaḥ = like four hands; sarve evatu = all of them; nirvṛittāḥ = emerging; svasharīrāt = out of his own body.

Dasaratha was bestowing his equal love to all his four sons who were best among men, as though they were his four hands emerging out of his own body.

Sloka 6

teṣāmapi mahātejā rāmo ratikaraḥ pituḥ |
svayambhūriva bhūtānāṃ babhūva guṇavattaraḥ || 2-1-6

6. rāmaḥ = Rama; svayambhūriva = like the Brahma; bhūtānāṃ = among all the living beings; guṇavattaraḥ = the most virtuous; teshhāmapi = among those brothers; mahātejaḥ = the mightiest; ratikaraḥ babhūva = was a source of joy; pituḥ = for his father.

Rama, like the Brahma among all the living beings, the most virtuous among those brothers and the mightiest was a great source of joy for his father.

Sloka 7

sa hi devai rudīrṇasya rāvaṇasya vadhārthibhiḥ |
arthito mānuṣe loke jajñe viṣṇuḥ sanātanaḥ || 2-1-7

7. saḥ = ṭhat Rama; sanātanaḥ = the eternal; vishhnuḥ = Vishnu; jajJṇehi = was born; mānushhe loke = on the earth; arthita = as urged by; devaihi = gods; vadhārthibhihi = to kill; udīrṇasya rāvaṇasya = the egoistic Ravana.

That Rama - was He not the eternal Vishnu who was born on earth as prayed by celestials to kill the egoistic Ravana?

Sloka 8

kausalyā śuśubhe putreṇāmitatejasā |
yathā vareṇa devānāmaditirvajrapāṇinā || 2-1-8

8. aditiryathā = like Adithi; vajrapāṇinā = by Indra; devānāṃ vareṇa = the best among celestials; kausalyā = Kausalya; shushubhe = was shone by; putreṇa = her son; amitatejasā = the mighty.

Like Adithi by Indra, best among the celestials, Kausalya shone by her son Rama, the mighty.

Sloka 9

sa hi rūpopapannaśca vīryavānanasūyakaḥ |
bhūmāvanupamaḥ sūnurgaṇairdhaśarathopamaḥ || 2-1-9

9. saḥ = Rama; rūpopapannaḥ cha = having beautiful form; vīryavān = a hero of valor; anasūyakaḥ = without envy; gunaiḥ = by virtues; dasarathopamaḥ = like Dasaratha; anupamaḥ = an incomparable; sūnuhu hi = son indeed; bhūmau = on earth.

Rama was beautiful in form, a hero of valor and without envy. By virtues, he was like Dasaratha. In this way, he was an incomparable son on earth.

Sloka 10

sa ca nityaṃ praśāntātmā mṛdupūrvaṃ tu bhāṣate |
ucyamāno.api paruṣaṃ nottaraṃ pratipadyate || 2-1-10

10. saḥ = that Rama; nityam = always; prashāntātmā = with a peaceful mind; bhāshhate = talked; mṛidupūrvakaṃ = softly; uttaram na prapadyate = he did not respond to; parushhaṃ = hard words; uchyamānaha api = spoken by others.

That Rama was always peaceful in mind and spoke softly. He did not react to the hard words spoken by others.

Sloka 11

kathaṃcidupakāreṇa kṛtenai kena tuṣyati |
na smaratyapakārāṇāṃ śatamapyātmavattayā || 2-1-11

11. saḥ = ṭhat Rama; ātmavattayā = because of his good bent of mind; tushhyati = feels glad; kathaṃchit = even by ; aikena = one; upakāreṇa = good thing; kṛitena = done; na smarati = (but) does not mind; satamapi = even a hundred; apakārāṇām = bad things.

That Rama, because of his good bent of mind, feels glad even by whatever way a good thing is done to him. He does not remember any number of bad things done to him.

Sloka 12

śīlavṛddhai rjñānavṛddhairvayovṛddhaiśca sajjanaiḥ |
kathayannāsta vainitya mastrayogyāntareṣvapi || 2-1-12

12. astrayoga antareshhvapi = Even during intervals while practising archery; nityam = always; kathayannāsta = used to converse with; shīlavṛiddhai = elders by conduct; jJṇānavṛiddhai = elders by wisdom; vayovṛiddhai = elders by age (or); sajjanaiḥ = with good people.

Whenever he finds some time even while practising archery, Rama used to converse with elderly people, elder by way of conduct or wisdom or age or with good- natured people.

Sloka 13

buddhimān madhurābhāṣī pūrvabhāṣī priyaṃvadaḥ |
vīryavānna ca vīryeṇa mahatā svena vismitaḥ || 2-1-13

13. buddhimān = wise man; madhurabhāshhī = sweet conversationalist; pūrvabhāshhī = one who initiated a talk; priyaṃvadaḥ = whose speech was compassionate; vīryavān = person with valor; na cha vismitaḥ = not arrogant of; svena = his own; mahatā = great; vīryeṇa = valor.

Rama was a wise man. He used to speak sweetly. He was the first man to initiate a talk. His speech was compassionate. He was valorous. But he was not arrogant of his mighty valor.

Sloka 14

na cānṛtakatho vidvān vṛddhānāṃ pratipūjakaḥ |
anuraktaḥ prajābhiśca prajāścāpyanurajyate || 2-1-14

14. na cha = not; anṛitakathaḥ = speaking untruth; vidvān = all knowing person; pratipūjakaḥ = Receptive and worshipful to; vṛidhānām = elders; anuraktaḥ = being loved; prajābhi = by people; anurajyate = loving; prajāshchapi = also the people.

He did not speak untruth. He was all knowing. He used to be receptive and worshipful to the elders. People used to love him and he used to love the people.

Sloka 15

sānukrośo jitakrodho brāhmaṇapratipūjakaḥ |
dīnānukampī dharmajzno nityaṃ pragrahavān śuciḥ || 2-1-15

15. sānukroshaḥ = had compassion; jitakrodhaḥ = conquered anger; brāhmana pratipūjakaḥ = receptive and worshipful to the wise; dīnānukampī = had mercy towards the meek; dharmajṇaḥ = knew what was to be done; nityam pragrahavān = ḥad always self control; suchiḥ = was clean (in conduct).

He had compassion. He conquered anger. He used to be receptive and worshipful to the wise. He had mercy towards the meek. He knew what was to be done. He had always self-control. He was clean (in conduct).

Sloka 16

kulocitamatiḥ kṣātraṃ dharmaṃ svaṃ bahumanyate |
manyate parayā kīrtya mahatsvargaphalaṃ tataḥ || 2-1-16

16. kulochitamatiḥ = attitude suitable for his social rank; bahumanyate = giving due respect to; kshātraṃ dharmam = righteousness of warrior-class; manyate = thought; tataha = by following that righteousness; parayā kīrtya = by great fame; mahat = great; swargaphalam = fruit of heaven.

That Rama, having an attitude suitable for his social rank, giving due respect to righteousness of warrior-class, believed that by following the righteousness he would attain great fame and through it the fruit of heaven.

Sloka 17

nāśreyasi rato vidvānna viruddhakathāruciḥ |
uttarottarayuktau ca vaktā vācaspati ryathā || 2-1-17

17. na rataḥ = not interested(in); ashreyasi = actions not beneficial; vidvān = scholar; ṇa viruddhakathāruchiḥ = no taste in tales opposing righteousness; vāchaspatiryathā = like brihaspathi; uttarottarayuktau = in showing series of strategies; vaktā = a fluent speaker.

Rama was not interested in actions, which were not beneficial. He was a scholar. He had no taste in tales opposing righteousness. Like v�chaspathi, his eloquent speech contained a series of strategies for action.

Sloka 18

arogastaruṇo vāgmī vapuṣmān deśakālavit |
loke puruṣasārajñassādhureko vinirmitaḥ || 2-1-18

18. arogaḥ = without disease; taruṇaḥ = young man; vāgmī = speaker; vapushhmān = person with a good body; deshakālavit = knew time and place; purushhasārajJṇaḥ = could grasp the essence of men; ekaḥ = the one; sādhuḥ = gentleman; vinirmitaḥ = created; loke = on earth.

Rama was a young man without any disease. He was a good speaker. He had a good body. He knew both time and place. He could grasp the essence of men. He was the one gentleman born on earth.

Sloka 19

sa tu sreṣṭhairguṇairyuktaḥ prajānāṃ pārthivātmajaḥ |
bahiścara iva prāṇo babhūva guṇataḥ priyaḥ || 2-1-19

19. prajānām = ṭo people; priyaḥ = loving; saḥ = that; pārthivātmajaḥ = prince; shreshhṭhaiḥ = with good; guṇaiḥ = virtues; yuktaḥ = contained; babhūva = existed; prānaḥ aiva = like spirit; bahishcharaḥ = moving outside; guṇataḥ = by virtues.

People loved the virtuous prince Rama and treated him as their spirit moving outside.

Sloka 20

samyagvidyāvratasnāto yathāvatsāṅgavedavit |
iṣvastre ca pituḥ śreṣṭho babhūva bharatāgrajaḥ || 2-1-20

20. vidyāvratasnātaḥ = after bathing in the discipline of education; samyak = properly; sāṇ^gavedavit = After knowing archery; yathāvat = as prescribed; bharatāgrajaḥ = elder brother of Bharatha (Rama); babhūva = was; shreshhṭhaḥ = better than; pituḥ = father; ishhvastre = in archery.

After completing his education properly, Rama, after knowing the science of archery as prescribed, was better than his father in the use of bow and arrows.

Sloka 21

kalyāṇābhijanaḥ sādhuradīnaḥ satyavāgṛjuḥ |
vṛddhairabhivinītaśca dvijairdharmārthadarśibhiḥ || 2-1-21

21. kalyānābhijanaḥ = having born in a good clan; sādhuḥ = gentleman; adīnaḥ = not a feeble man; satyavāk = speaker of truth; ṛijuḥ = straightforward man; abhivinītaḥ = properly trained by; vṛiddhaiḥ = elderly; dvijaiḥ = wisemen; dharmartha darshibhiḥ = who realized righteousness.

Rama, having born in a good clan, was gentle minded. He was not feeble. He spoke truth. He was straightforward. He was properly trained by elderly wise men that knew righteousness.

Sloka 22

dharmakāmārthatattvajñaḥ smṛtimān pratibhānavān |
laukike samayācāre kṛtakalpo viśāradaḥ || 2-1-22

22. dharma kāmārtha tattvajnaḥ = one who knew the real form of desire; wealth and righteousness; smṛitimān = one who had a good power of memory; pratibhānavān = one who had a spontaneous wisdom to respond; vishāradaḥ = one who had skill; kṛitakalpaḥ = one who had created arrangement of; samayāchāre = customs useful at that time; laukike = for the society.

Rama knew the real form of desire, wealth and righteousness. He had a good memory power. He had a spontaneous wisdom. He had skills in arranging customs useful to society prevalent at that time.

Sloka 23

nibhṛtaḥ saṃvṛtākāro guptamantraḥ sahāyavān |
amoghakrodhaharṣaśca tyāgasaṃyamakālavit || 2-1-23

23. nibhṛitaḥ = ḥumble man; saṃvṛitākāraḥ = ḥad an enclosed form; gupta mantraḥ = kept thoughts to himself; sahāyavān = helped others; amogha krodha harshhashcha = his anger and pleasure not wasteful; tyāgasaṃyama kālavit = knew the timing of giving and non-giving.

Rama was humble. He did not let his feelings appear outwardly. He kept his thoughts to himself. He helped others. His anger and pleasure were not wasteful. He knew when to give and when not to give.

Sloka 24

dṛḍhabhaktiḥ sthiraprajño nāsadgrāhī na durvacāḥ |
nistandrirapramattaśca svadoṣaparadoṣavit || 2-1-24

24.dṛiDha bhaktiḥ = had a firm devotion; sthira prajJṇaḥ = had steadfast mind; āsadgrāhī = was not stubborn; na durvachāḥ = not speaking evil words; nistandriḥ = had no idleness; apramattaḥ = was alert; svadoshhaparadoshhavit = and recognized his own errors and those of others.

Rama had a firm devotion and steadfast mind. He was not stubborn nor did he speak evil words. He was free from idleness and was ever alert. He recognized his own errors and those of others.

Sloka 25

śāstrajñaśca kṛtajñaśca puruṣāntarakovidaḥ |
yaḥ pragrahānugrahayoryathānyāyaṃ vicakṣaṇaḥ || 2-1-25

25. shāstrajJṇaḥ cha = ḥe knew sciences; kṛitajJṇaḥ cha = knew their practice; purushhāntarakovidaḥ = understood differences among men; yathānyāyam = as per justice; vichakshaṇaḥ = discriminator of; pragrahānugrahayoḥ = punishment and protection.

Rama knew the theory and practice of sciences. He understood the differences among men. He could judiciously discriminate whom to protect and whom to punish.

Sloka 26

satsaṃgrahapragrahaṇe sthānavinnigrahasya ca |
āyakarmaṇyupāyajñaḥ saṃdṛṣṭavyayakarmavit || 2-1-26

26. satsaṃgrahapragrahaṇe = identifying the good and protecting them; sthānavit = knew the people; nigrahasya cha = to be reprimanded; upāyajJṇaḥ = knew the ways and means of; āya karmaṇi = getting income; sandṛishhṭa vyaya karmavit = knew the system of spending as mentioned in sastra.

He identified good men and protected them. He knew the people worthy of reprimand. He knew the ways and means of getting income as well as the system of spending, as perceived by economic sciences.

Sloka 27

śraiṣṭhyaṃ śāstrasamūheṣu prāpto vyāmiśrakeṣu ca |
arthadharmau ca saṃgṛhya sukhatantro na cālasaḥ || 2-1-27

27. prāptaḥ = obtained; shraishhṭhyam = great skill; shāstrasamūheshhu = in groups of sciences; vyāmishrakeshhu cha = and in their subsidiaries; sukhatantraḥ = Interested in enjoying comforts; saṃgṛihya = after understanding; artha dharmau = economic realities; na alasaḥ = and never remained inactive.

Rama could obtain great skill in the groups of sciences along with their subsidiaries. He was interested in enjoying comforts only after understanding the economic realities. He never remained inactive.

Sloka 28

vaihārikāṇāṃ śilpānāṃ vijñātārthavibhāgavit |
ārohe vinaye caiva yuktovāraṇavājinām || 2-1-28

28. vijJṇātā = acquainted with; shilpānām = fine arts; vaihārikāṇām = useful in entertainment; arthavibhāgavit = knew how to distribute wealth; yuktaḥ = efficient in; ārohe = in riding; vinaye = in taming; vāraṇavājinām = elephants and horses.

Rama was acquainted with the fine arts useful for entertainment. He knew how to distribute the wealth. He was efficient in riding and taming of elephants and horses.

Sloka 29

dhanurvedavidāṃ sreṣṭho loke.atirathasaṃmataḥ |
abhiyātā prahartā ca senānayaviśāradaḥ || 2-1-29

29. shreshhṭhaḥ = best of persons; dhanur veda vidām = knowing the science of archery; athirathasaṃmataḥ = appreciated by the champions of archery; senā nayavishāradaḥ = Attained skills in moving the army properly; abhiyātā = facing the enemies; prahartā cha = (ḥe) used to kill them.

Rama was the best of persons knowing the science of archery in the world; and was well appreciated by the champions of archery. He attained skills in marshalling the army. He faced and killed the enemies in battle.

Sloka 30

apradhṛṣyaśca saṃgrāme kruddhairapi surāsuraiḥ |
anasūyo jitakrodho na dṛpto na ca matsarī |
na cāvamantā bhūtānāṃ na ca kālavaśānugaḥ || 2-1-30

30. kruddhaiḥ = enraged; surāsurair api = even by suras and asuras; apradhṛishhyaḥ = (ḥe) could not be defeated; saṃgrāme = in battle; anasūyaḥ = had no jealousy; jitakrodhaḥ = conquered anger; na dṛiptaḥ = ḥad no arrogance; na matsarī cha = ḥad no envy; na cha avamantā = not humiliated; bhūtānām = living beings; na kāla vashānugaḥ cha = had not surrendered to time.

Even enraged celestials and demons could not defeat Rama in battle. He had no jealousy. He conquered anger. He had no arrogance and envy. He had not humiliated any living being. He had not surrendered to time.

Sloka 31

evaṃ śreṣṭhaguṇairyuktaḥ prajānāṃ pārthivātmajaḥ |
saṃmatastriṣu lokeṣuvasudhāyāḥ kṣamāguṇaiḥ || 2-1-31
buddyā bṛhaspatestulyo vīryeṇāpi śacīpateḥ |

31. pārthivātmajaḥ = ṭhat prince Rama; evam shreshhṭhagunaiḥ = with these good virtues; yuktaḥ = fair; prajānām = to the people; saṃmataḥ = was agreeable; trishhu lokeshhu = to the three worlds; kshamā guṇaiḥ = virtue of forgiveness; vasudhāyāḥ = (comparable) to the earth; budhyā = By wisdom; bṛihaspate = to brihaspathi; vīryeṇa = by valour; shachīpate = to devendra; tulyaḥ = (ḥe was) equal.

That Prince Rama, with these good virtues, was fair to the people. He was agreeable to the three worlds. By patience and the related virtues, he was equal to earth, by wisdom to Brihaspathi and by valor to Devendra.

Sloka 32

tathā sarvaprajākāntaiḥ prītisaṃjananaiḥ pituḥ || 2-1-32
guṇairviruruce rāmo dīptaḥ sūrya ivāṃśubhiḥ |

32. rāmaḥ = Rama; guṇaiḥ = by virtues; sarva prajākāntaiḥ = which are enlightening to all the people; prītisamjananaiḥ = which are source of liking; pituḥ = to his father; viruruche = was shining; sūrya eva = like the sun; dīptaḥ = shining; aṃshubhiḥ = by the rays.

Rama, by his virtues, was a source of happiness to all the people and a spring of joy to his father. As the sun shines with his rays, Rama was shining, thus, with his virtues.

Sloka 33

tamevaṃvratasaṃpannamapradhṛṣyaparākramam || 2-1-33
lokapālopamaṃ nāthamakāmayata medinī |

33. medinī = the Earth; akāmayata = wished; tam = Rama; nātham = (to be) the lord; evaṃ vrathasaṃpannam = as he was adorned with disciplined life; apradhṛishhya parākramam = having undefeatable valor; lokapālopamam = equal to the universal lords like Indra.

The earth wished Rama to be her Lord as he was adorned with self -control and norms of behavior bearing undefeatable valor equal to that of universal lords like Indra.

Sloka 34

etaistu bahubhiryuktaṃ guṇairanupamaiḥ sutam || 2-1-34
dṛṣṭvā daśaratho rājā cakre cintāṃ paraṃtapaḥ |

34. rājā = King dasaratha; paraṃtapaḥ = who annihilates enemies; chakre = constructed; chintāṃ = thoughts; dṛishhṭvā = after observing; sutam = the son; bahubhiḥ = (having) many; anupamaiḥ = incomparable; etaiḥ = these; guṇaiḥ = virtues.

Dasaratha, who annihilates enemies, started thinking as follows after observing his son with his many incomparable virtues.

Sloka 35

atha rājño babhūvaivaṃ vṛddhasya cirajīvinaḥ || 2-1-35
prītireṣā kathaṃ rāmo rājā syānmayi jīvati |

35. atha = thereafter; chiranjīvinaḥ = long living; vṛidhasya = aged; rājJṇaḥ = Dasaratha; evam babhūva = thus thought; katham syāt = how will; rāmaḥ = Rama (become); rājā = king; maya jīvati = while I am alive; eshhā prītiḥ = (shall I enjoy) this happiness.

The long living and aged Dasaratha thought: "Will Rama become king while I am still alive? Shall I enjoy that happiness?"

Sloka 36

eṣā hyasya parā prītirhṛdi saṃparivartate || 2-1-36
kadā nāma sutaṃ drakṣyāmyabhiṣiktamahaṃ priyam |

36. eshhā parā = this great; prīthiḥ = loving thought; saṃparivartatehi = was ringing; hṛidi = in his heart; nāma = when; drakshyāmi = shall I see; priyam sutam = beloved son Rama; abhishhiktam = crowned as king.

A great loving thought was ringing in his mind that when he would be able to see his beloved son Rama crowned as a king.

Sloka 37

vṛddhikāmo hi lokasya sarvabhūtānukampanaḥ || 2-1-37
mattaḥ priyataro loke parṅanya iva vṛṣṭimān |

37. loke = In the country; mattaḥ = Better than me; priyataraḥ hi = (he is) better liked; vṛishhṭimān = raining; parjanyaḥ iva = like cloud; vṛiddhi kāmaḥ = desires development of; lokasya = world; sarvabhūta anukampanaḥ = has equal compassion towards all living creatures.

"Is not Rama, as a raining cloud to the earth, better liked by people than me, as he desires the development of the world and has equal compassion towards all living beings."

Sloka 38

yamaśakrasamo vīrye bṛhaspatisamo matau || 2-1-38
mahīdharasamo dhṛtyāṃ mattaśca guṇavattaraḥ |

38. vīrye = In valour; yama sakra samaḥ = equal to Yama and Indra; matau = in wisdom; bṛihaspati samaḥ = equal to Bruhaspati; dhṛityām = in courage; mahīdhara samaḥ = equal to mountain; guṇavattaraḥ = better virtues; mattaḥ cha = than me.

"Rama is equal to Yama and Devendra in valor, to Brihaspati in wisdom and to a mountain in courage. He is more virtuous than me."

Sloka 39

mahīmahamimāṃ kṛtsnāmadhitiṣṭhantamātmajam || 2-1-39
anena vayasā dṛṣṭvā yathā svargamavāpnuyām |

39. yathā = ḥow; aham = I; avāpnuyām = attain; svargam = heaven; dṛishhṭvā = after seeing; anena vayasā = in this age; ātmajām = my son; adhitishhṭhantam = ruling; kṛitsnām = the entire; imām mahīm = this Earth.

"Shall I attain heaven, after seeing in this age, my son ruling the entire earth?"

Sloka 40

ityetai rvividhai staistai ranyapārthivadurlabhaiḥ || 2-1-40
śiṣṭairaparimeyaiścha loke lokottarairguṇaiḥ |
taṃ samīkṣya mahārājo yuktaṃ samuditaiḥ śubhaiḥ || 2-1-41
niścitya sacivaiḥ sārdhaṃ yuvarājamamanyata |

40;41. iti = this way; vividhaiḥ = various; anya pārthiva durlabhaiḥ = ṇot at all seen in other kings; taistaiḥ = those and those; ethaiḥ = these virtues; loke = in the world; aparimeyaiḥ = which can not be counted; lokottaraiḥ = Best in the world; samuditaiḥ = gathered at one place; shubhaiḥ = auspicious; yuktaṃ = containing; shishhṭaih = the remaining; guṇaiḥ = virtues; samīkshya = seeing; tam = that Rama; mahārājaḥ = king of Dasaratha; sachivaiḥ sārdhaṃ = along with ministers; nishchitya = decided; yuvarājam = as prince; amanyata = thought.

Rama had many other virtues beyond hitherto stated virtues not to be seen in other kings. His virtues cannot be counted and they are the best in the world. Seeing that type of virtuous Rama, Dasaratha along with his ministers, decided to make Rama the prince.

Sloka 42

divyantarikṣe bhūmau ca ghoramutpātajaṃ bhayam || 2-1-42
saṃcacakṣe.atha medhāvī śarīre cātmano jarām |

42. atha = thereafter; medhāvī = the wise Dasaratha; āchachakshe = said; ghoram = great; bhayam = alarm; divi = in heaven; antarikshe = in space; bhūmau cha = in earth; utpādajam = comet like things; ātmanaḥ = his; sharīre = body; jarām cha = getting aged.

The wise Dasaratha said that there was sign of a great alarm being forecast because of comet like things found in earth heaven and the sky. He also told the ministers that his body was getting aged.

Sloka 43

pūrṇacandrānanasyātha śokāpanudamātmanaḥ || 2-1-43
loke rāmasya bubudhe saṃpriyatvaṃ mahātmanaḥ |

43. bubudhe = (ḥe) recognised; rāmasya = Rama; pūrṇachandrānanasya = one who has a face like a full moon; mahātmanaḥ = great wise man; loke saṃpriyatvaṃ = liked by the people; athaḥ = and; ātmanaḥ shokāpanudam = will remove his worry.

He recognized that if Rama were crowned as king, he would not have worries as Rama had beautiful face as a full moon; was a great wise man; and was liked by the people.

Sloka 44

ātmanaśca prajānāṃ ca śreyase ca priyeṇa ca || 2-1-44
prāptakālena dharmātmā bhaktyā tvaritavān nṛpaḥ |

44. dharmātma = the righteous; nṛipaḥ = King Dasaratha; priyeṇa cha = to his liking; tvaritavān = was hurried; bhaktyā = with interest; sreyase cha = beneficial to; ātmanashcha = his own self (and); prajānām cha = people; prāptakālena = time has come.

The righteous Dasaratha was hurried with concern in the coronation of Rama as it is for his own benefit and for the benefit of people. Because it is as per his liking and also as the appropriate time has come.

Sloka 45

nānānagaravāstavyān pṛthagjānapadānapi || 2-1-45
samānināya medinyāḥ pradhānān pṛthivīpatīn |

45.nānā nagaravāstavyān = those residing in various cities; jānapadānapi = those residing in villages; pradhānān = principal officers; medinyāḥ = of lands; pṛithivīpatīn = kings; pṛithak = separately; samānināya = called for.

Dasaratha called for other kings and officers staying in various cities and villages in his kingdom separately.

Sloka 46

na tu kekayarājānaṃ janakaṃ vā narādhipaḥ || 2-1-46
tvarayā cānayāmāsa paścāttau śroṣyataḥ priyam |

46. tvarayā = By hurry; narādhipaḥ = the king; anayāme = did not call for; kekayarājānaṃ = the king of kekaya; janakaṃ vā = or Janaka; tau = both of them; shroshhyataḥ = could hear; priyam = the good news; pashchāt = afterwards.

The hurried Dasaratha did not call for the king Kekaya, the maternal uncle of Bharatha or the King Janaka as he thought they both could hear the good news even afterwards.

Sloka 47

tānveśmanānābharaṇairyathārhaṃ pratipūjitān || 2-1-47
dadarśālaṃkṛto rājā prajāpatiriva prajāḥ |

47. rājā = ṭhe king; prathipūjitān = presented; tān = all of them; veshmanānābharaṇaiḥ = houses and various jewelry; yathārhaṃ = suitably; alaṃkṛitaḥ = adorned himself; dadarsha = looked them; prajāpatiḥ = (like) lord Brahma; prajāḥ iva = as children.

The king Dasaratha presented them suitably with houses and jewelry. Duly adorned himself, he looked after them in the manner Lord Brahma looks after his children.

Sloka 48

athopaviṣṭe nṛpatau tasmin parabalārdane || 2-1-48
tataḥ praviviśuḥ śeṣa rājāno lokasammatāḥ |

48. atha = ṭhere afterwards; sheshhāḥ = the remaining; rajānaḥ = kings; lokasammatāḥ = loved by the people; pravivishuḥ = entered; tataḥ = after; nṛipatau = the king; parabalārdhane = who annihilates the opponent's army; upavishhṭe = occupied his seat.

All the invited kings, duly liked by their people, entered the assembly after the king Dasaratha , who annihilates the opponent's army, occupied his seat.

Sloka 49

atha rājavitīrṇeṣu vividheṣvāsaneṣu ca || 2-1-49
rājānamevābhimukhā niṣedurniyatā nṛpāḥ |

49. atha = ṭhere afterwards; nṛipāḥ = those kings; nishheduḥ = sat; vividheshhu = in various; āsaneshhu = seats; rājavitīrneshhu = allotted by king; abhimukheḥ = facing towards; rājānameva = the said king; niyataḥ = as per rules.

The kings thus entered, occupied their various seats allotted to them by the king, by facing toward the king as per the prescribed rules.

Sloka 50

sa labdhamānairvinayānvitairnṛpaiḥ |
purālayai rjānapadaiśca mānavaiḥ |
upopaviṣṭairnṛto babhau |
sahasracakṣurbhagavānivāmaraiḥ || 2-1-50

50. vṛitaḥ = surrounded by; labdhamānaiḥ = Respected; vinayānvitaiḥ = humble; upopavishhṭaiḥ = closely sat; nṛipaiḥ = kings; mānvaiḥ = the officers of; purālayaiḥ = cities; jānapadaiḥ = villagers; saḥ = that king Dasaratha; babhau = shone; bhagavān sahasrachakshuriva = like lord Indra; amaraiḥ = surrounded by celestials.

Surrounded by the respected and humble kings sitting closely to him as well as the important heads of cities and villages, king Dasaratha shone like lord Devendra surrounded by celestials.

- - -

|| iti śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ayodhyakāṇḍe prathama sargaḥ ||

Thus completes 1st chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
