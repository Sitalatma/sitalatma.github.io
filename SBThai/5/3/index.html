Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam

Canto 5: The Creative Impetus

CHAPTER THREE

Ṛṣabhadeva’s Appearance in the Womb of Merudevī, the Wife of King Nābhi

In this chapter the spotless character of King Nābhi, the oldest son of Āgnīdhra, is described. Wanting to have sons, Mahārāja Nābhi underwent severe austerities and penances. He performed many sacrifices along with his wife and worshiped Lord Viṣṇu, master of all sacrifices. Being very kind to His devotees, the Supreme Personality of Godhead was very pleased with the austerities of Mahārāja Nābhi. He personally appeared before the King in His four-handed feature, and the priests who were performing the sacrifices, began to offer their prayers unto Him. They prayed for a son like the Lord, and Lord Viṣṇu agreed to take birth in the womb of Merudevī, the wife of King Nābhi, and incarnate as King Ṛṣabhadeva.

Translation

In the performance of a sacrifice, there are seven transcendental means to obtain the mercy of the Supreme Personality of Godhead: (1) by sacrificing valuable things or eatables, (2) by acting in terms of place, (3) by acting in terms of time, (4) by offering hymns, (5) by going through the priest, (6) by offering gifts to the priests and (7) by observing the regulative principles. However, one cannot always obtain the Supreme Lord through this paraphernalia. Nonetheless, the Lord is affectionate to His devotee; therefore when Mahārāja Nābhi, who was a devotee, worshiped and offered prayers to the Lord with great faith and devotion and with a pure uncontaminated mind, superficially performing some yajña in the line of pravargya, the kind Supreme Personality of Godhead, due to His affection for His devotees, appeared before King Nābhi in His unconquerable and captivating form with four hands. In this way, to fulfill the desire of His devotee, the Supreme Personality of Godhead manifested Himself in His beautiful body before His devotee. This body pleases the mind and eyes of the devotees.

“One can understand the Supreme Personality as He is only by devotional service. And when one is in full consciousness of the Supreme Lord by such devotion, he can enter into the kingdom of God.” (Bg. 18.55)

One can understand and see the Supreme Personality of Godhead through the process of devotional service, and not in any other way. Although Mahārāja Nābhi performed prescribed duties and sacrifices, it should still be considered that the Lord appeared before him not due to his sacrifices but because of his devotional service. It was for this reason that the Lord agreed to appear before him in His beautiful bodily features. As stated in Brahma-saṁhitā - พฺรหฺม-สํหิตา - พรัหมะ-สังหิตา (5.30), the Supreme Lord in His original nature is very beautiful. Veṇuṁ kvaṇantam aravinda-dalāyatākṣaṁ barhāvataṁsam asitāmbuda-sundarāṅgam: - เวณุํ กฺวณนฺตมฺ อรวินฺท-ทลายตากฺษํ พรฺหาวตํสมฺ อสิตามฺพุท-สุนฺทรางฺคมฺ: - เวณุง กวะณันตัม อะระวินทะ-ทะลายะตากษัง พัรหาวะตังสัม อะสิตามพุทะ-สุนทะรางคัม: the Supreme Personality of Godhead, although blackish, is very, very beautiful.

Purport

In Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา (7.7), Lord Kṛṣṇa Himself says, mattaḥ parataraṁ nānyat kiñcid asti dhanañjaya: - มตฺตห์ ปรตรํ นานฺยตฺ กิญฺจิทฺ อสฺติ ธนญฺชย: - มัตตะห์ ปะระตะรัง นานยัต กิญจิท อัสติ ธะนัญชะยะ: “O conqueror of wealth [Arjuna], there is no truth superior to Me.” No one is more attractive or more authoritative than Lord Kṛṣṇa. Consequently He appeared as the son of Merudevī in His original spiritual form, which is above the modes of material nature.

Purport

When the Supreme Lord appears or descends as an incarnation within this material world, He does not accept a body made of the three modes of material nature (sattva-guṇa - สตฺตฺว-คุณ - สัตตวะ-คุณะ, rajo-guṇa - สตฺตฺว-คุณ, รโช-คุณ - สัตตวะ-คุณะ, ระโช-คุณะ and tamo-guṇa - ตโม-คุณ - ตะโม-คุณะ). Māyāvādī philosophers say that the impersonal God appears in this material world by accepting a body in the sattva-guṇa - สตฺตฺว-คุณ - สัตตวะ-คุณะ. - สตฺตฺว-คุณฯ - สัตตวะ-คุณะฯ Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī states that the word śukla - ศุกฺล - ศุกละ means “consisting of śuddha-sattva - ศุทฺธ-สตฺตฺว - ศุทธะ-สัตตวะ. - ศุทฺธ-สตฺตฺวฯ - ศุทธะ-สัตตวะฯ” Lord Viṣṇu descends in His śuddha-sattva form. Śuddha-sattva - ศุทฺธ-สตฺตฺว - ศุทธะ-สัตตวะ refers to the sattva-guṇa - สตฺตฺว-คุณ - สัตตวะ-คุณะ which is never contaminated. In this material world, even the mode of goodness (sattva-guṇa) is contaminated by tinges of rajo-guṇa - รโช-คุณ - ระโช-คุณะ and tamo-guṇa. - ตโม-คุณฯ - ตะโม-คุณะฯ When sattva-guṇa is never contaminated by rajo-guṇa - รโช-คุณ - ระโช-คุณะ and tamo-guṇa, - ตโม-คุณ, - ตะโม-คุณะ, it is called śuddha-sattva. - ศุทฺธ-สตฺตฺวฯ - ศุทธะ-สัตตวะฯ Sattvaṁ viśuddhaṁ vasudeva-śabditam - สตฺตฺวํ วิศุทฺธํ วสุเทว-ศพฺทิตมฺ - สัตตวัง วิศุทธัง วะสุเทวะ-ศัพทิตัม (Bhāg. - ภาคฺฯ - ภาคฯ 4.3.23). That is the platform of vasudeva, - วสุเทว, - วะสุเทวะ, whereby the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vāsudeva, can be experienced. In Bhagavad-gītā - ภควทฺ-คีตา - ภะคะวัท-คีตา (4.7) Śrī Kṛṣṇa Himself says:

อัภยุตถานัม อะธัรมัสยะ
ตทาตฺมานํ สฺฤชามฺยฺ อหมฺ

“Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion — at that time I descend Myself.”

Unlike ordinary living entities, the Supreme Lord is not forced by the modes of material nature to appear. He appears dharmān darśayitu-kāma - ธรฺมานฺ ทรฺศยิตุ-กาม - ธัรมาน ทัรศะยิตุ-กามะ — to show how to execute the functions of a human being. The word dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะ is meant for human beings and is never used in connection with beings inferior to human beings, such as animals. Unfortunately, without being guided by the Supreme Lord, human beings sometimes manufacture a process of dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะ by concoction. Actually dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะ cannot be made by man. Dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะṁ tu sākṣād bhagavat-praṇītam. - ธรฺมํ ตุ สากฺษาทฺ ภควตฺ-ปฺรณีตมฺฯ - ธัรมัง ตุ สากษาท ภะคะวัต-ประณีตัมฯ (Bhāg. - ภาคฺฯ - ภาคฯ 6.3.19) Dharma is given by the Supreme Personality of Godhead, just as the law is given by the state government. Man-made dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะ has no meaning. Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam - ศฺรีมทฺ-ภาควตมฺ - ศรีมัท-ภาคะวะตัม refers to man-made dharma - ธรฺม - ธัรมะ as kaitava-dharma, - ไกตว-ธรฺม, - ไกตะวะ-ธัรมะ, cheating religion. The Supreme Lord sends an avatāra - อวตาร - อะวะตาระ (incarnation) to teach human society the proper way to execute religious principles. Such religious principles are bhakti-mārga. - ภกฺติ-มารฺคฯ - ภักติ-มารคะฯ As the Supreme Lord Himself says in Bhagavad-gītā: - ภควทฺ-คีตา: - ภะคะวัท-คีตา: sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja. - สรฺว-ธรฺมานฺ ปริตฺยชฺย มามฺ เอกํ ศรณํ วฺรชฯ - สัรวะ-ธัรมาน ปะริตยัชยะ มาม เอกัง ศะระณัง วระชะฯ The son of Mahārāja Nābhi, Ṛṣabhadeva, appeared on this earth to preach the principles of religion. That will be explained in the Fifth Chapter of this Fifth Canto.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Fifth Canto, Third Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled “Ṛṣabhadeva’s Appearance in the Womb of Merudevī, the wife of King Nābhi.”
