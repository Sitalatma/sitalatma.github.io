Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 2
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sugreeva comforts Rama, who is tormented with grief, asking him to keep away from apprehensive mind and to think of ways and means to cross the ocean.

Sloka 1

tam tu śokaparidyūnam rāmam daśarathātmajam |
uvāca vacanam śrīmān sugrīvah śokanāśanam || 6-2-1

1. shrīmān = the glorious; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; uvācha = spoke; shokanāshanam = (these) grief-removing; vachanam = words; tu = then; tam rāmam\ to that Rama; dasharathātmajam = the son of Dasaratha; shokaparidyūnam = felt miserable by grief.

The glorious Sugreeva made the following reply, which was intended to expel his grief, to Rama the son of Dasaratha who felt miserable with anguish.

Sloka 2

kim tvayā tapyate vīra yathānyaḥ prākṛtastathā |
maivam bhūstyaja satāpam kṛtaghna iva sauhṛdam || 6-2-2

2. vīra = "Oh; hero! Kim = why; tapyate = is it lamented; tvayā = by you; tathā = thus; yathā = as; anyaḥ = any other; prakṛitaḥ = ordinary man? mā bhūḥ = do not be(sorrowful); evam = like this; tyaja = abandon; santāpam = grief; sauhṛidam iva = as abandoning friendship; kṛitaghnaḥ = by an ungrateful man.

"Oh, hero! Why are you lamenting thus, as any other ordinary man? Do not be sorrowful like this. Abandon your grief, as an ungrateful man abandons friendship."

Sloka 3

samtāpasya ca te sthānam na hi paśyāmi rāghava |
pravṛttāmupalabdhāyām jñāte ca nilaye ripoḥ || 6-2-3

3. pravṛittau = "As the information (about ṣeetha); upalabdhāyām = has been procured; na pashyāmi hi = I do not indeed see; sthānam; any scope for; te = your; samtāpasya = grief; rāghava = Oh; Rama!"

"As the information about Seetha has since been gathered, I do not indeed see any scope for your grief Oh, Rama!"

Sloka 4

matimān śāstravitprājñḥ paṇḍitaścāsi rāghava |
tyajemām prākṛtām uddhiṃ kṛtātmevārthadūṣaṇīm || 6-2-4

4. rāghava = "Oh; Rama! asi = you are; matimān = a wise man; shāstravit = a knower of sacred works; prājJṇaḥ = an intellectual; paṇDitashchāsi = and a learned man; tyaja = give up; imam = these; prakṛitām = ordinary; buddhim = conjectures; arthadūshhiṇīm buddhim iva = like giving up conjectures which spoil the purpose; kṛitātmā = by one whose spirit is disciplined."

"Oh, Rama! You are a wise person, a knower of sacred works, an intellectual and a learned man. Give up these ordinary apprehensions like a man whose spirit is disciplined gives up ideas which spoil the purpose.

Sloka 5

saṃdram laṅghayitvā tu mahānakrasamākulam |
laṅkāmārohayiṣyāmo haniṣyāmaśca te ripum || 6-2-5

5. ārohayishhyāmaḥ = "we shall make our way up; laṇ^kām = to Lanka; laṇ^ghayitvā = by crossing; samudram = the ocean; mahā nakra samākulam = which is filled with large crocodiles; hanishhyāmashcha = we shall destroy; te = your; ripum = enemy.

"We shall make our way up to Lanka, by crossing the ocean filled with large crocodiles. We shall destroy your enemy."

Sloka 6

nirutsāhasya dīnasya śokaparyākulātmanaḥ |
sarvārthā vyavasīdanti vyasanam cādhigacchati || 6-2-6

6. sarvārthāḥ = "All actions; vyavasīndanti = get dissipated; nirutsāhasya = by a non-enthusiastic person; dīnasya = a depressed individual; shoka paryākulātmanaḥ = and a person disturbed with grief; adhigachchhati = (such a person) obtains; vyasanamaḥ = troubles too."

"All action get dissipated by a person who is non-enthusiastic, depressed and disturbed with grief. Such a person gets into troubles too."

Sloka 7

ime śūrāḥ samarthāśca sarvato hariyūthapāḥ |
tvatpriyārtham kṛtotsāhāḥ praveṣṭumapi pāvakam || 6-2-7
eṣām harṣeṇa jānāmi tarkaścāpi dṛḍho mama |

7. ime = "ṭhese; hariyūthapāḥ = leaders of monkey troops; shūrāḥ = are gallant; samarthāshcha = and efficient; sarvataḥ = in every way; kṛitotsāhāḥ = they are made of enthusiasm; praveshhṭum api = even to enter; pāvakam = a fire; tvatpriyārtham = for your sake; jānāmi = I know; eshhām = this; harshheṇa = from their joy; mama = my; tarkashchāpi = reasoning also; dṛiDhaḥ = is strong."

"These leaders of monkey squads are gallant and efficient in all ways. They are enthusiastic even to enter a fire for your sake. I understand this from their joy and my reasoning too is sound."

Sloka 8

vikrameṇa samāneṣye sītām hatvā yathā ripum || 6-2-8
rāvanam pāpakrmāṇam tathā tvam kartumarhasi |

8. tvam = you; arhasi = ought; kartum = to act; yathā tathā = in every way that; samāneshhye = I may be able to get back; sītām = ṣeetha; hatvā = after destroying; vikrameṇa = by an attack; ripum = the enemy; rāvaṇam = Ravana; pāpakarmāṇam = of sinful deeds.

"You ought to act in every way that I may be able to get back Seetha, after destroying by an attack the enemy Ravana of sinful deeds."

Sloka 9

seturatra yathā baddhyethā paśyema tām purīm || 6-2-9
tasya rākṣasarājasya tathā tvam kuru rāghava |

9. tvam = "You; kuru = take steps; yathā tathā = in such a way; setuḥ = (that) a bridge; badhyet = is constructed; atra = here; pashyema = and we behold; tām = that; purīm = city; tasya = of that; rākshasa rājasya = king of ogres; rāghava = Oh; Rama!"

"You take steps in such a way that a bridge is constructed across the sea and we reach that city of the king of ogres."

Sloka 10

dṛṣṭvā tām hi purīm laṅkām trikūṭaśikhare sthitām || 6-2-10
hatam ca rāvaṇam uddhe darśanādavadhāraya |

10. avadhāraya = "be certain; rāvaṇam = that Ravana; hatam = is killed; darshanāt = as soon as he is seen; yuddhe = in combat; dṛishhṭvā = and on our beholding; tām = that; laṇ^kām purīm = city of Lanka; sthitām = standing; trikūṭa shikhare = on the peak of ṭrikuta mountain.

"Be certain that Ravana is killed, once he is seen in a battle and on our seeing that city of Lanka standing on a peak of the Trikuta ṃountain."

Sloka 11

abaddhvā sāgare setum ghore ca varuṇālaye || 6-2-11
laṅkā na marditum śakyā sendrairapi surāsuraiḥ |

11. abaddhvā = without building; setum = a bridge; sāgare = across the sea; ghore = the dreadful; varuṇālaye = abode of Varuna (the god of water); laṇ^kā = Lanka; na shakyā = cannot be; marditum = crushed; surāsuraiḥ api = even by gods and demons; sendraiḥ = including Indra.

"Without building a bridge across the sea, the dreadful abode of Varuna (the god of water), Lanka cannot be defeated even by gods and demons including Indra."

Sloka 12

seturbaddhaḥ samudre ca yāvallaṅkāsamīpataḥ || 6-2-12
sarvam tīrṇam ca me sainyam jitamityupadhāraya |
ime hi samare vīrā harayaḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ || 6-2-13

12;13. upadhāraya = "Understand that; yāvat = when; me sarvam sainyam = the whole of my army; tīrṇam = crosses(the sea); setuḥ = (as) a bridge; baddhaḥ = is built; samudre = across the sea; laṇ^kā samīpataḥ = at eh vicinity of Lanka; jitam = (they will come out) victorious; ime = these; harayaḥ = monkeys; kāmarūpeṇa = who are capable of assuming any form at will; vīrāḥ hi = are indeed heroic; samare = in battle."

"Know that when the whole of my army crosses the sea as a bridge is built across it, they will come out victorious, because these monkeys who are able to change their form at will are indeed valiant in battle."

Sloka 14

tadalam viklabām buddhim rājan sarvārthanāśanīm |
puruṣasya hi loke.asmin śokaḥ śauryāpakarṣaṇaḥ || 6-2-14

14. tat = "therefore; alam = (be) enough; viklabām = of apprehensive; buddhim = min; sarvārthā nāshanīm = which destroys all purposes; shokaḥ = (because) grief; asmin loke = in the world; shaurya apakarshhaṇaḥ = diminishes the valor; purushhasya = of a man; rājam = Oh king!"

"Therefore, take away your apprehensive mind, which destroys any enterprise for grief in this world diminishes the might of a man, Oh king!"

Sloka 15

yattu kāryam manuṣyeṇa śauṇḍīryamavalambyatām |
tadalamkaraṇāyaiva karturbhavati satvaram || 6-2-15

15. avalambyatām = "cling to; kāryam tu = the practice of; shauṇDīryam = boldness; yat = which (is to be shown); manushhyeṇa = by a man; tat = It; bhavati = will produce; alamkaraṇāyaiva = competence truly; kartuḥ to the doer; satvaram = quickly."

�Cling to the practice of boldness, ought to be resorted to by a man. It will produce competence without doubt to the doer quickly."

Sloka 16

asmin kāle mahāprājñ sattvamātiṣṭha te jasā |
śūrāṇām hi manuṣyāṇām tvadvidhānām mahātmanām || 6-2-16
vinaṣṭevā ranassṭe vāśokaḥ sarvārthanāśanaḥ |

16. ātishhṭa = prevail upon; sattvam = the strength; tejasā = with alertness; asmin = at this; kāle = moment; mahāprājJṇ = Oh; highly intelligent prince!; shokaḥ = grief; vinashhṭe vā = for something lost; pranashhṭeva = or destroyed; sarvārthā nāshanaḥ = defeats all the purposes; shūrāṇām = for heroes; mahātmanām = and magnanimous; manushhyāṇām = men; tvadvidhānām = like you."

"Prevail upon the strength with alertness at this moment, Oh highly intelligent prince! Grief for something lost or destroyed consumes all resources of even the strong and magnanimous men like you."

Sloka 17

tattvam buddhimatām śreṣṭhah sarvaśāstrarthakovidaḥ || 6-2-17
madvidhaiḥ sacivaiḥ sārdhamarim jetum samarhasi |

17. shreshhṭaḥ = (you are the) foremost; buddhimatām = among the intelligent; sarva shāstra kovidaḥ = the knower of all sacred texts; tat = hence; tvam = you; samarhasi = ought to; jetum = defeat; arim = the enemy; sachivaiḥ sārdham = in conjunction with friends; madvidhaiḥ = like me.

"You are the foremost among the intelligent and the knower of all sacred texts. Hence, you ought to defeat the enemy, with allies like me."

Sloka 18

na hi paśyāmyaham kam cittriṣu lokeṣu rāghava || 6-2-18
gṛhītadhanuṣo yaste tiṣṭhe dabhimukho raṇe |

18. aham = I; na pashyāmihi = do not indeed see; kamchit = any one; yaḥ = who; tishhṭhet = stands; abhimukhe = face to face; raṇe = on a battle field; raṇe = on a battle field; te = against you; trishhu lokeshhu = in the three worlds; gṛishīta dhanushhaḥ = (when you have) seized a bow; rāghava = Oh; Rama!

"I do not indeed see any one who can withstand you in a battle filed in the three worlds, when you are armed with a bow, Oh Rama!"

Sloka 19

vānareṣu samāsaktam na te kāryam vipatsyate || 6-2-19
acirāddrakṣyase sītām tīrtvā sāgaramakṣayam |

19. te kāryam = your work; samāsaktam = duly entrusted; vānareshhu = to the monkeys; na vipatsyate = will not be spoiled; drakshhyase = you shall behold; sītām = ṣeetha; achirāt = before long; tīrtvā = by crossing; sāgaram = the sea; akshhayam = which is imperishable.

"You work duly entrusted to the monkeys will not be spoiled. You shall behold Seetha era imperishable sea."

Sloka 20

tadalam śokamālambya krodhamālamba bhūpate || 6-2-20
niśceṣṭāh kṣatriyā mandāḥ sarve caṇḍasy bibhyati |

20. bhūpate = Oh; Lord of the earth! tat = hence; alam = enough; ālambya = of having maintained; shokam = the grief; ālamba = maintain; krodham = wrath (on your enemy); kshhatriyāḥ = Kshatriya as (members of warrior- tribe); nishcheshhṭāh = who are not active; mandāḥ = are miserable; sarve = all; bibhyati = fear; chaṇDasya = the wrathful.

"Oh, Lord of the earth! Desist from this melancholy. Yield to your legitimate indignation. Unadventurous Kshatriyas (members of warrior-tribe) never win honor but all fear the wrathful."

Sloka 21

laṅghānārtham ca ghorasya samudrasya nadīpateḥ || 6-2-21
sahāsmābhirihoopetah sūkṣmabuddhirvicāraya |

21. sūkshma buddhiḥ = (you with) sharp intellect; iha = now; asmābhiḥ saha = along with us; upetaḥ = together; ichāraya = think of; laṇ^ghanārtham cha = design to cross; samudrasya = the sea; nadīpate = the lord rivers; ghorasya = which is terrible.

"You with your resourceful mind, along with us together, ponder over now a design to cross the sea, the terrible Lord of rivers."

Sloka 22

laṅghite tatra taiḥ sainyairjitamityeva niścinu || 6-2-22
sarvam tīrṇam ca me sainyam jitamityavadhāryatām |

22. nishchinu = be assured; jitamityeva = of having conquered; tatra = (once) that sea; laṇ^ghite = has been crossed; taiḥ sainyaiḥ = by that army; avadhāryatām = It is to be understood; jitamiti = of having won; sarvam = (if) all; me = my; sainyam = forces; tīrṇam = arrive beyond ( the sea).

"Know victory to be certain, once the sea has been crossed by the army. Verily when all my forces have passed over the sea our triumph is assured!"

Sloka 23

ime hi harayaḥ śūrāḥ samare kāmarūpiṇaḥ || 6-2-23
tānarīnvidhamiṣyanti śilāpādapavṛṣṭibhiḥ |

23. ime harayaḥ = ṭhese monkeys; shūrāḥ = strong ones (as they are); samare = in battle; kāma rūpeṇa = and capable of assuming any form at will; vidhamishhyantihi = will indeed blow away; tān = those; arīn = enemies; shilāpādapa vṛishhṭibhiḥ = by a shower of rocks and trees.

"These monkeys, the courageous soldiers who are able to change their form at their volition, will crush their opponents with an avalanche of rocks and trees."

Sloka 24

katham citparipaśyāmi laṅghitam varuṇālayam || 6-2-24
hatamityeva tam manye yuddhe śatrunibarhaṇa |

24. paripashyāmi = I perceive; varuṇālayam = the sea; the abode of Varuna; laṇ^ghitam = crossed; kathamchit = by any manner whatsoever; manye = I think; tam = that Ravana; hatamityeva = as definitely killed; yuddhe = in combat; shatrunibarhaṇa = Oh; exterminator of enemies!

"Once we have crossed the sea (the abode of Varuna) by whatever the means employed, Ravana is as definitely killed in my eyes, Oh exterminator of enemies!"

Sloka 25

kimuktvā bahudhā cāpi sarvathā vijayā bhavān || 6-2-25
nimittāni ca paśyāmi mano me samprahṛṣyati |

25. kim = what is the use; uktvā = of speaking; bahudhā = at length? bhavān = you will be; vijayī = victorious man; sarvathā = in every way; pashyāmi = I see; nimittāni = good omens; me = my; manaḥ = mind; samprahṛishhyati = is over-thrilled with delight."

What is the use of all these words? By all means, you will be a victorious man. I see good omens and my heard is over thrilled with joy."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvitīyaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 2nd chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
