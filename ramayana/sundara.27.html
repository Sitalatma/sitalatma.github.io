Valmiki Ramayana

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 27
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter an ogress named Trijata describes her dream. She dreams of Sri Rama triumphs and the ogre Ravana is conquered.

Sloka 1

ityuktāh sītayā ghoraṃ rākṣasyaḥ krodhamūrchitāḥ |
kāścijjagmustadākhyātuṃ rāvaṇasya durātmanaḥ || 5-27-1

1. iti = thus; uktāḥ = spoken to; sītayā = by ṣeetha; rākshasyaḥ = ogresses; krodha mūrchhitāḥ = swooned with anger; ghoram = terribly; kāshchit = some; jagmuḥ = went; ākhyātum = to tell; tat = that; rāvaṇasya = to Ravana; durātmanaḥ = the evil minded one.

ṭhus spoken to by ṣeetha, ogresses swooned with anger terribly. ṣome went to tell that to Ravana the evil minded one.

Sloka 2

tataḥ sītāmupāgamya rākṣasyo ghoradarśanāḥ |
punaḥ puruṣamekārthamanarthārthamathābruvan || 5-27-2

2. tataḥ = thereafter; rāksasyaḥ = ogresses; ghora darshanāḥ = with a terrible appearance; upāgamya = neared; sītām = ṣeetha; atha = then; punaḥ = again; abruvan = spoke; anarthārtham = (words of) evil purpose; ekkartham = with a single meaning; parushham = (which were) harsh.

ṭherafter ogresses with a terrible appearance neared ṣeetha and ten agin spoke words of evil purpose, with a single meaning and which were harsh.

Sloka 3

adyedānīṃ tavānārye sīte pāpaviniścaye |
rākṣasyo bhakṣayiṣyanti māṃsametadyathāsukham || 5-27-3

3. anārye = O ignoble one!; pāpavinishchaye = O one with sinful resolve!; sīte = O ṣeetha; adya = today; idānīm = at this moment; rākshasyaḥ = ogresses; bhakshayishhyanti = will eat; etat = this; tava = your; māṃsam = flesh; yathhasukham = according to comfort.

"O ignoble one! O one with a sinful resolve! O ṣeetha! ṭoday at this moment ogreeses will eat this your flesh according to comfort."

Sloka 4

sītāṃ tābhiranāryābhirdṛṣṭvā saṃtarjitāṃ tadā |
rākṣasī trijaṭā vṛddhā śayānā vākyamabravīt || 5-27-4

4. tadā = then; dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; sītām = ṣeetha; saṃtarjitām = frightened; tābhiḥ anāryābhiḥ = by those evil ones; rākshasī = an ogress; trijaṭā = (named) ṭrijata; shayanā = sleeping (till then); abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (these) words.

ṭhen, seeing ṣeetha frightened by those evil ones, an ogress named ṭrijata, sleeping till then, spoke these words.

Sloka 5

ātmānaṃ khādatānāryā na sītāṃ bhakṣayiṣyatha |
janakasya sutāmiṣṭāṃ sunnaṣāṃ daśarathasya ca || 5-27-5

5. anāryāḥ = O evil ones! khādata = eat; ātmānam = your selves; na bhakshayishhyatha = you will not eat; sītām = ṣeetha; ishhṭām sutām = dear daughter; janakasya = of king Janaka; snushhām cha = and daughter-in-law; dasarathasya = of king Dasaratha.

"O evil ones! Eat your selves. You will not eat ṣeetha who is the dear daughter of king Janaka and daughter-in-law of king Dasaratha."

Sloka 6

svapno hyadya mayā dṛṣṭo dāruno romaharṣaṇaḥ |
rākṣasānāmabhāvāya bharturasyā bhavāya ca || 5-27-6

6. adya = now; svapnaḥ = a dream; dāruṇaḥ = (which is) terrible; romaharshhaṇaḥ = causing the hairs to stand erect; abhāvāya = for the destruction; rākshasānām = of ogres; bhavāya = for the welfare; asyāḥ bhartuḥ = of ḥer husband; dṛiṣhṭaḥ = has been seen; mayā = by me.

"ṇow a dream which is terrible, causing the hairs to stand erect for the destruction of ogres and for the welfare of Her husband, has been seen by me."

Sloka 7

evamukṭāstrijaṭayā rākṣasyaḥ krodhamūrchitāḥ |
sarvā evābruvanbhītāstrijaṭāṃ tāmidaṃ vacaḥ || 5-27-7

7. evam = thus; uktāḥ = spoken to; trijaṭayā = by ṭrijata; sarvāḥ eva = all; rākshasayaḥ = ogresses; bhītāḥ = were frightened; abruvan = (and) spoke; tām trijaṭām = to that ṭrijata; idam = this; vachaḥ = sentence.

ṭhus spoken to by ṭrijata all ogresses were frightened and spoke to that ṭrijata these words.

Sloka 8

kathayasva tvayā dṛṣṭaḥ svapno.ayaṃ kīdṛśo niśi |
tāsāṃ śrutvā tu vacanaṃ rākṣasīnāṃ mukhācycutam || 5-27-8
uvāca vacanaṃ kāle trijaṭā svapnasaṃśritam |

8. kīdṛishī = of what kind; ayam svapnaḥ = (is) this dream; dṛishhṭaḥ = seen; tvayā = by you; kathayasva = tell (us); shrutvā = listening; vachanam = (to the) utterance; chyutam = (that) came forth; mukhāt = from the mouths; tāsām rākshasīnām = of those ogresses; trijaṭā = ṭrijata; uvācha = spoke; kāle = at that time; vachanam = (this) word; svapna saṃshritam = relating to the dream.

"Of what kind is this dream seen by you. ṭell us". Listening to this utterance that came forth from the mouths of those ogresses, Trijata spoke at that time this word relating to the dream.

Sloka 9

9;10.rāghavaḥ = Rama; shuklamālyāmbara dharaḥ = wearing white garlands and clothes; antarikshagām = passing through the sky; svayam āsthāya = ḥimself ascending; shibikām = a palanquin; gajadantamayīm = made of ivory; yuktām = tied; haṃsa sahasreṇa = with a thousand swans; āgataḥ = arrived; lakshmaṇena saha = along with Lakshmana.

"Rama wearing white garlands and clothes passing through the sky, ḥimself ascending a palanquin made of ivory tied with a thousand swans, arrived along with Lakshmana."

Sloka 11

svapne cādya mayā dṛṣṭā sītā śuklāmbarāvṛtā |
sāgareṇa parikṣiptaṃ svetaṃ parvatamāsthitā || 5-27-11

11. adya = now; dṛishhṭā = (it has been) seen; mayā = by me; svapne = in the dream (that); sītā cha = ṣeetha also; shuklāmbarāvṛitā = wearing white clothes; āsthitā = was sitting; shvetam parvatam = on a white mountain; parikṣhiptam = surrounded; sāgareṇa = by the ocean.

"ṇow it has been seen by me in the dream that ṣeetha also wearing white clothes was sitting on a white mountain surrounded by the ocean."

Sloka 12

rāmeṇa saṃgatā sītā bhāskareṇa prabhā yathā |

12. sītā = ṣeetha; saṃgatā = came together; rāmeṇa = with Rama; prabhā yathā = like the light; bhāskareṇa = with the ṣun; rāmashcha = Rama also; mayā dṛishhṭaḥ = was seen by me; ārūDhaḥ = (to be) mounted; mahāgajam = on a great elephant; chaturdraṣhṭram = with four tusks; shailasaṃkāsham = equalling a mountain; chachāra = (and) wandered; saha lakshmanaḥ = together with Lakshmana.

"ṣeetha came together with Rama like the light with the ṣun. Rama also was seen by me to be mounted on a great elephant with four tusks and equalling a mountain and Rama wandered together with Lakshmana."

Sloka 13

tatastau naraśārdūlau dīpyamānau svatejasā || 5-27-13

13. tataḥ = thereafter; dīpyamānau = shining; svatejasā = with their own radiance; shuklamālyāmbara dharau = wearing white garlands and clothes; tau = those two; narashārdūlau = best among men (Rama and Lakshmana); paryupasthitau = neared; jānakīm = ṣeetha.

"ṭhereafter shining with their own radiance, wearing white garlands and clothes, those two best among men Rama and Laksmana neared Seetha."

Sloka 14

tatastasya nagasyāgre hyākāśasthasya dantinaḥ || 5-27-14

14. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; jānakī = ṣeetha; āshritā = rested on; skandham = the upper part of back; dantinaḥ = of elephant; ākāshasthasya = in the sky; parigṛihītasya = taken; bhartrā = by ḥer husband Rama; agre = to the front; tasya nagasya = of that mountain;

"ṭhereafter ṣeetha rested on the upper part of back of elephant in the sky; taken by ḥer husband Rama to the front of that mountain."

Sloka 15

"ṭhereafter it has been seen by me that ṣeetha with eyes like lotuses has risen up from the lap of Her husband and gently touched the ṃoon and the Sun with Her hand."

Sloka 16

"ṭhereafter that best elephant ascended by those princes Rama and Lakshmana and by ṣeetha with wide eyes stood on the top of Lanka."

Sloka 17

17. kākutthsaḥ = Rama; bhāryayā sītayā saha = together with wife ṣeetha; svayam = ḥimself; iha upayātaḥ = came here; rathena = in a chariot; pāṇDurarṣhabhaḥ yuktena aṣhṭa yujā = tied with eight white bulls;

"Rama together with wife ṣeetha, ḥimself came here in a chariot tied with eight white bulls."

Sloka 18

lakṣmaṇena saha bhrātrā sītayā saha vīryavān || 5-27-18

18;19. vīryavān = ṭhe valorous; puruṣhottamaḥ = best among men Rama; saha = together; bhrātrā lakshmaṇena saha = with brother Lakshmana; sītayā = (and) with ṣeetha; āruhya = ascended; divyam = wonderful; puṣhpakam vimānam = Pushpaka aeroplane; sūrya sannibham = equalling the ṣun; jagāma = went; ālokya = beholding; uttarām disham = northern direction.

"ṭhe valorous best among men Rama together with brother Lakshmana and with ṣeetha ascended wonderful Pushpaka aeroplane equalling the Sun, went in the northern direction."

Sloka 20

"Rama with strength equalling that of Lord Vishnu, born in Raghu's dynasty together with brother Lakshmana and with Seetha has been seen by me thus in the dream."

Sloka 21

"Rama with great glory is not possible to be defeated by ṣuras or by ogres or by others like heaven not being conquerable by sinners."

Sloka 22

"Ravana also was seen by me on earth sprinkled with oil, with red clothes, with garlands tied with Oleander flowers, drinking oil and intoxicated."

Sloka 23

"In Lanka horrible with ash, all ogresses were drinking oil, laughing heartily with a great noise and also dancing a lot."

Sloka 38

kumbhakarṇādayaśceme sarve rākṣasapuṅgavāḥ || 5-27-38
raktaṃ nivasanaṃ gṛhya praviṣṭā gomayahrade |

38. ime sarve = all these; rākshsapu~ṇgavāḥ = best among ogres; kumbhakarṇādayaḥ = (such as) Kumbhakarna and others; gṛihya = taking; raktam = a red; nivasanam = cloth; praviṣhṭāḥ = entered; gomayahrade = a hole filled with cow dung.

"All these best among ogres such as Kumbhakarna and others taking a red cloth entered a hole filled with cow dung."

Sloka 39

apagacchata naśyadhvaṃ sītāmāpnoti rāghavaḥ || 5-27-39
ghātayetparamāmarṣī yuṣmān sārdhaṃ hi rākṣanaiḥ |

39. apagachchha = go away; nashyadhvam = be destroyed; rāghavaḥ = Rama; āpnoti = will get; sītām = ṣeetha; paramāmarṣhī = with a great anger; yuṣhmān ghātayet = ḥe will get you killed; rākshasaiḥ sārtham = together with (other) ogres.

"Go away! Be destroyed! Rama will get ṣeetha. With a great anger ḥe will get you killed together with other ogres."

Sloka 40

priyāṃ bahumatāṃ bhāryāṃ vanavāsamanuvratām || 5-27-40
bhartsitāṃ tarjitāṃ vāpi nānumaṃsyati rāghavaḥ |

40. rāghavaḥ = Rama; na anumaṃsyati = will not approve; bharsitām = frightening; tarjitāṃ vāpi = or threatening; priyām bhāryām = ḥis dear wife; bahumatām = who is highly valued (by ḥim); anuvratām = who followed ḥim; vanavāsam = to live in forest.

"Rama will not approve frightening ot threatening ḥis dear wife who is highly valued by ḥim, who followed Him to live in the forest."

Sloka 41

tadalaṃ krūravākyaiśca sāntvamevābhidhīyatām || 5-27-41
abhiyācāma vaidehīmetaddhi mama rocate |

41. tat = for that reason; alam = enough; krūra vākyaiḥ = of cruel words; sāntvameva = gentle words indeed; abhidīyatām = be said; abhiyāchāma = (let us) request; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; etaddhi = this; rochate = is agreeable; me = to me.

"For that reason, enough of cruel words. Gentle words indeed be said. Let us request ṣeetha. ṭhis is agreeable to me."

Sloka 42

yasyāmevaṃvidhaḥ svapno duḥkhitāyāṃ pradṛśyate || 5-27-42
sā duḥkhairvividhairmuktā priyaṃ prāpnotyanuttamam |

42. yasyām = which woman; duḥkhitāyām = while being sorrowful; evaṃvidhaḥ = such type; svapnaḥ = (of) dream; pradṛishyate = has been seen;sā = that woman; muktā = being released; vividhaiḥ duḥkhaiḥ = from various sorrows; prāpnoti = will get; anuttamam = unsurpassed; priyam = pleasure.

"Which woman while being sorrowful, such type of dream has been seen, that woman being released from various sorrows, will get unsurpassed pleasure."

Sloka 43

bhartsitāmapi yācadhvaṃ rāksasyaḥ kiṃ vivakṣayā || 5-27-43
rāghavāddhi bhayaṃ ghoraṃ rākṣasānāmupasthitam |

43. rākshasyaḥ = O ogresses! yāchadhvam = entreat (ḥer); bhartsitāmapi = who has been frightened; kim = what (is the use); vivakshayā = with desire to speak; rāghavāt = from Rama; ghoram bhayam = a horrible fear; upasthitam = has come; rākshasānām = to ogres;

"O ogresses! Entreat ṣeetha who has been frightened. What is the use with a desire to speak. From Rama a horrible fear has come to ogres."

Sloka 44

praṇipātaprasannā hi maithilī janakātmajā || 5-27-44
alameṣā paritrāruṃ rākṣasyo mahāto bhayāt |
api cāsyā viśālākṣyā na kiṃcidupalakṣaye || 5-27-45
virūpamapi cāṅgeṣu susūkṣmamapi lakṣaṇam |

44;45. rākshasyaḥ = O ogresses!; eṣhā = this; maithilī = ṣeetha; janakātmajā = daughter of Janka; praṇipāta prasannā = pleased with prostration; alam = is competent; paritrātum = to protect; mahataḥ = (from) great; bhayāt = danger. apicha = and also; na upalashaye = I do not see; asyāḥ = in ḥer; vishālākshyā = who is wide eyed; a~ṇgeṣhu = in ḥer limbs; susūskhmamapi = even a minute; virūpam lakshaṇam = misshapen characteristic; kiṃchidapi = even insignificant (one).

"O ogresses! ṭhis ṣeetha daughter of Janaka, pleased with prostration is competent to protect us from great danger. And also I do not see in Her who is wide eyed, in Her limbs even a minute misshapen characteristic, even insignificant one."

Sloka 46

cāyāvaiguṇyamātraṃ tu śaṅke duḥkhamupasthitam || 5-27-46
aduḥkhārhamimāṃ devīṃ vaihāyasamupasthitām |

46. chāyāvaiguṇya mātraṃ = (I see) an imperfection in complexion alone; sha~ṇke = I doubt; imām devīm = (that) this princess; upasthitām = who came near; vaihāyasam = an aeroplane (in my dream); aduḥkhārhām = who is not suited to sorrows; upasthitam = received; duḥkham = sorrow.

"I see an imperfection in complexion alone. I doubt that this princess who came near an aeroplane in my dream, who is not suited to sorrows received sorrow."

Sloka 47

arthasiddhiṃ tu vaidehyāḥ paśyāmyahamupasthitām || 5-27-47
rākṣasendravināśaṃ ca vijayaṃ rāghavasya ca |

47. aham = I; pashyāmi = am seeing; ardhasiddhiṃ tu = accomplished motive; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha; upasthitām = coming near; rākshasendra vināsham cha = the destruction of Ravana also; rāghavasya vijayaṃ cha = and victory of Rama (coming near).

"I am seeing ṣeetha coming near accomplishment of motive. I also see the destruction of Ravana also and victory of Rama coming near."

Sloka 48

nimittabhūtametattu śrotumasyā mahatpriyam || 5-27-48
dṛśyate ca sphuraccakṣuḥ padmapatramivāyatam |

48.nimittabhūtam = being a cause; shrotum = to listen; mahat = (to) a great; priyam = pleasure; asyāḥ = of ḥer; etat = this; spurat = throbbing; chakshuḥ = eye; āyatam = long; padmapatramiva = like a lotus petal; dṛishyate = is being seen ( by me).

"Being a cause to listen to a great pleasure of ḥer, this throbbing eye long like a lotus petal is being seen my me."

Sloka 49

īṣacca hṛṣito vāsyā dakṣiṇāyā hyadakṣiṇaḥ || 5-27-49
akasmādeva vaidehyā bahurekaḥ prakampate |

49. ekaḥ adakshiṇaḥ bāhuḥ = one left arm; t asyāḥ dakshiṇāyāḥ vaidehyāḥ = of this straight forward ṣeetha; akasmādeva = suddenly; hṛiṣhitaḥ = has become erect; īṣhat = slightly; prakampate = is trembling.

"One left arm of this strightforward ṣeetha suddenly has become erect and is slightly trembling."

Sloka 50

kareṇuhastapratimaḥ savyaścoruranuttamaḥ || 5-27-50
vepamānaḥ sūcayati rāghavaṃ purataḥ sthitam |

50. anuttamaḥ savyaḥ ūruḥ = (ṣeetha's) best left thigh; kareṇuhastapratimaḥ = equal to an elephant's trunk; vepamānaḥ = is trembling; sūchayati = indicating; rāghavam = Rama's; sthitam = is standing; purataḥ = in front (of ḥer).

"ṣeetha's best left thigh equal to an elephant's trunk is trembling indicating Rama's standing in front of Her."

Sloka 51

pakṣī ca śākhānilayaṃ praviṣṭaḥ |
punaḥ punaścottamasāntvavādī |
susvāgataṃ vācamudīrayānaḥ |
punaḥ punaścodayatīva hṛṣṭaḥ || 5-27-51

51. pakshī cha = a bird also; praviṣhṭaḥ = entering; shākhā nilayam = (its) dwelling place on a branch; punaḥ punashcha = again and again; uttamasāntvavādī = speaking best consolating words; susvāgatam vācham udīrayānaḥ = tellīng heartily welcoming words; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = being delighted; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; chodayatīva = as though impelling (ṣeetha).

"A bird also entering its dwelling place on a branch again and again speaking best consolating words, is telling heartily welcoming words being delighted again and again, as though impelling Seetha."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe saptaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 27th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic
