Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 79
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Fight again ensues between monkeys and demons, when ṃakaraksha enters the battle-field. The monkeys get frightened of ṃakaraksha's arrows and begin to run away. Rama comes in and intercepts the demons with his hail of arrows. ṃakaraksha challenges Rama to fight with him. Rama tears off the arrows hurled by ṃakaraksha and ṃakaraksha broke the arrows released by Rama. Finally, Rama breaks the chariot and its horses of his chariot, stands on the ground, takes a splendorous spike in his hand to fight and hurls it on Rama. Rama chops it off with his four arrows. When ṃakaraksha rushes towards Rama to fight with his fist, Rama hurls a mystic missile from his bow and kills him on the spot. The demons get frightened and run away to Lanaka.

Sloka 1

nirgataṃ makarākṣaṃ te dṛṣṭvā vānarapuṃgavāḥ |
āplutya sahasā sarve yoddhukāmā vyavasthtāḥ || 6-79-1

1. dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; makarākṣham = ṃakaraksha; nirgatam = having come forth; sarve = all; te = those; vānarapuṇ^gavāḥ = chiefs of the monkeys; sahasā āplutya = having jumped up at once; avasthitāḥ = took their positions; yoddhu kāmāḥ = with intent to give fight.

On seeing ṃarkaraksha come out, all those chiefs of monkeys, leaping all at once, took their positions, with intent to give fight.

Sloka 2

tataḥ pravṛttaṃ sumahattadyuddhaṃ romaharṣaṇam |
niśācaraiḥ plavaṃgānāṃ devānāṃ dānavairiva || 6-79-2

2. tataḥ = thereafter; tat = that; sumahat = very great; yuddham = battle; pravṛittam = occurred; plavaṇ^gānām = between monkeys; nishācharaiḥ = and demons; romaharṣhaṇam = which caused the hair to bristle; devānām iva = as between celestials; danavaiḥ = and demons.

Thereafter, a very great battle ensued between monkeys and demons, which caused one's hair to bristle, like the encounter between celestials and demons.

Sloka 3

vṛkṣaśūlanipātaiśca gadāparighapātanaiḥ |
anyonyaṃ mardayanti sma tadā kapiniśācarāḥ || 6-79-3

3. kapinishācharāḥ = the monkeys and the demons; tadā = then; vṛikṣha shūlanipātaiḥ cha = by hurling trees and spikes; gadāparigha pātanaiḥ = and by throwing maces and clubs; mardayantisma = began to crush; anyonyam = each other.

The monkeys and demons then, by hurling trees and spikes and by throwing maces and clubs, began to crush each other.

Sloka 4

śaktikhaḍgagadākunaistomaraiśca niśācarāḥ |
paṭṭiśairbhindipālaiśca bāṇapātaiḥ samantataḥ || 6-79-4
pāśamudgaradaṇḍaiśca nirgātaiścāparaistathā |
kadanaṃ kapisimhānām cakruste rajanīcarāḥ || 6-79-5

4; 5. te nishācharāḥ = those demons; rajanīcharāḥ = who were prowlers of might; kadanam chakruḥ = made a slaughter; kapisimhānām = of the lions among the monkeys; shaktikhaDgagadākunaiḥ = by means of their javelins; swords; maces; spears; tomaraishcha = and lances; paṭṭishaiḥ = sharp-edged spears; bhindipālaishcha = Bhindipalas (slings for throwing stones); bāṇapātaiḥ = throws of arrows; pāshamudgaradaṇ^Daiḥ cha = nooses; mallets and staffs; nirghātaiḥ = missiles; tathā = and; anyaishcha = other weapons; samantataḥ = on all sides.

Those demons, who were prowlers of might, made a slaughter of the lions among the monkeys, by means of their javelins, swords, maces, spears, lances, sharp edged spears, Bhindipalas (slings for throwing stones), nooses, mallets, staffs, missiles and other weapons and by throwing arrows on all sides.

Sloka 6

bāṇaughairarditāścāpi kharaputreṇa vānarāḥ |
saṃbhrāntamanasaḥ sarve dudruvurbhayapīḍitāḥ || 6-79-6

6. arditāḥ = tormented; bāṇanghaiḥ = with a multitude of arrows; kharaputreṇa = by ṃakaraksha; sarve = all; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; sambhrānta manasaḥ = were bewildered in mind; bhayapīDitāḥ = agonized with fear; dudruvuḥ = and ran away.

Tormented by ṃakaraksha with a multitude of arrows, all the monkeys were bewildered in mind, agonized with fear and ran away.

Sloka 7

tān dṛṣṭvā rākṣasāḥ sarve dravamāṇān vanaukasaḥ |
neduste siṃhavaddṛptā rākṣasā jitakāśinaḥ || 6-79-7

7.dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; tān = those; vanaukasaḥ = monkeys; dravamāṇām = fleeing; sarve = all; rākṣhasāḥ = the demons; dṛiptāḥ = who were arrogant; simhavat = like lions; neduḥ = roared; jitakāshinaḥ = assuming a triumplant feeling.

On seeing those monkeys fleeing, all the arrogant demons roared like lions, assuming a triumphant feeling.

Sloka 8

vidravatsu tadā teṣe vānareṣu samantataḥ |
rāmastānvāramāyāsa śaravarṣeṇa rākṣasān || 6-79-8

8. teṣhu vānareṣhu = (while) those monkeys; vidravatsu = were fleeing; samantataḥ = on all sides; rāmaḥ = Rama; tadā = then; vārayāmāsa = obstructed; tān = those; rākṣhasān = demons; sharavarṣheṇa = with a shower of arrows.

While those monkeys were fleeing on all sides, Rama then, with a shower of arrows, intercepted those demons on that occasion.

Sloka 9

vāritān rākṣasān dṛṣṭvā makarākṣo niśācaraḥ |
kopānalasamāviṣṭo vacanaṃ cedamabravīt || 6-79-9

9. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing rākṣhasān = the demons; vāritān = being intercepted; makarākṣhaḥ = ṃakaraksha; nishācharaḥ = the demon moving about by night; kopānala samāviṣhṭaḥ = engrossed in a fire of anger; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanamcha = the following words.

Seeing Rama thus interrupting the demons, ṃakaraksha, the demon, engrossed in a fire of anger, spoke the following words:

Sloka 10

tiṣṭha rāma mayā sārthaṃ dvandvayuddhaṃ bhaviṣyati |
tyājayuṣyāmi te prāṇān dhanurmuktaiḥ śitaiḥ śaraiḥ || 6-79-10

10. tiṣhṭa = pause; rāma = O Rama; bhaviṣhyati = there will be; dvandvayuddham = a dual; mayā sārdham = with me; tyājayiṣhyāmi te prāṇān = I will make your lives to quit; shitaiḥ sharaiḥ = by the sharp arrows; dhanurmuktaiḥ = discharged from my bow.

"Pause, O Rama! You will have a dual with me. I will make your life to quit, by the sharp arrows discharged from my bow."

Sloka 11

yattadā daṇḍakāraṇye pitaraṃ hatavānmama |
tadagrataḥ svakarmasthaṃ dṛṣṭvā roṣo.abhivardhate || 6-79-11

11. yat = for which reason; hatavān = you killed; mama pitaram = my father; daṇ^Dakāraṇya = in the forest of Dandaka; tadā = at that itme; tat = for that reason; roṣhaḥ = my anger; abhivardhate = is getting augmented; dṛiṣhṭvā = in seeing; agrataḥ svakarmastham = you in front engaged in such nefarious acts.

"Since you killed my father on that day in Dandaka forest, my anger is still growing violent, when I think of you engaged in such nefarious acts".

Sloka 12

dahyante bhṛśamaṅgāni durātmanmama rāghava |
yanmayāsi na dṛṣṭastvaṃ tasmin kāle mahāvane || 6-79-12

12. durātman = O evil minded; rāgava = Rama!; yat = since; tvam = you; nadṛiṣhṭaḥ asi = were not being seen; tasmin kāle = from that time; mayā = by me; mahā vane = in that huge forest; mama = my; aṇ^gāni = limbs; bhṛisham = were very much; dahyante = being fumed.

"O evil minded Rama! Since you were not being seen by me from that time in that large forest, my limbs were getting very much fumed."

Sloka 13

diṣṭyāsi darmanaṃ rāma mama tvaṃ prāptavāniha |
kāṃkṣito.asi kṣudhārtasya simhasyevetaro mṛgaḥ || 6-79-13

13. diṣhṭyā = by a good fortune; tvam = you; prāptavān = got; mama darshanam = my sight; iha = here; rāma = O Rama!; kānkṣhitaḥ asi = you are being sought; kṣhudhāvtasya simhasya iva = as a lion in hunger; itaraḥ mṛigaḥ iva = (seeks) other animal (to eat).

"By a good fortune, you came into my sight here, O Rama! You are being sought by me, as a lion in hunger seeks other animal to eat."

Sloka 14

adya madbāṇavegena pretarāḍviṣayaṃ gataḥ |
ye tvayā nihatāḥ śūrāḥ saha taiśca vasiṣyasi || 6-79-14

14. adya = now; madbāṇa vegena = by the flight of my arrows; gataḥ tvayā = (you) having gone; pretarāDviṣhayam = to the world of Death; vasiṣhyasicha = you will reside; taiḥ shūrāḥ saha = with those warriors; ye = who; nihatāḥ = were killed; tvayā = by you (earlier).

"Now, by the flight of my arrows, you will go to the world of Death and reside there with those warriors, who were killed by you earlier."

Sloka 15

bahunātra kimuktena śṛṇu rāma vaco mama |
paśyantu sakalā lokāstvāṃ māṃ caiva raṇājire || 6-79-15

15. rāma = O Rama!; kim = what is the use; uktena = by telling; bahunā = too much; atra = on this topic?; shruṇu = hear; mama = my; vachaḥ = words; sakalāḥ = all; lokāḥ = the worlds; pashyantu = will look at; tvām = you; mām chaiva = and me; raṇājire = in the battle-front.

"O Rama! What is the use, by telling too much on this topic? Hear my words. All the worlds will look at you and me, in this battle-front."

Sloka 16

astrairvā gadayā vāpi bāhubhyāṃ vā raṇājire |
abhyastaṃ yena vā rāma vartatāṃ tena vā mṛdham || 6-79-16

16. rāma = O Rama!; mṛidham = the battle; vartatām = will go on; raṇājire = in the battle-field; astrairvā = either with the weapons; gadayāchāpi = or with a mace; bāhubhyām = or our arms; yena tena vā = or by those means; abhyastam = learnt (by you).

"O Rama! The battle will proceed in the battle-field, either with the weapons, or with a mace or by our arms or by any other means as learnt by you."

Sloka 17

makarākṣavacaḥ śrutvā rāmo daśarathātmajaḥ |
abravītprahasam vākyamuttarottaravādinam || 6-79-17

17. shrutvā = hearing; makarakshavachaḥ = the words of ṃakaraksha; rāmaḥ = Rama; dasharathātmajah = the son of Dasaratha; prahasan = laughingly; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the following worlds; uttarottaravādinam = to him who was talking further and further (without interruption).

Hearing the words of ṃakaraksha, Rama the son of Dasaratha laughingly spoke the following words to him, who was still talking further and further (without interruption).

Sloka 18

katthase kiṃ vṛthā rakṣo bahūnyasadṛśāni te |
na raṇe śakyate jetuṃ vinā yuddhena vāgbalāt || 6-79-18

18. rakṣhaḥ = O; demon!; kim katthase = why are you boasting yourself; bahūni asadṛishāni = with so many unworthy words; vṛithā = in vain?; yuddhena vinā = without fighting; raṇe = in the battle-field; nashakyam = it is not possible; te = for you; jetum = to attain victory; vāgbalāt = with the strength of a mere speech.

"O demon! Why are you boasting yourself with so many unworthy words, in vain? Without fighting in the battle-field, it is well nigh impossible for you to attain victory with the strength of a mere speech."

Sloka 19

caturdaśa sahasrāṇi rakṣasāṃ tvatpitā ca yaḥ |
triśirā dūṣaṇaścāpi daṇḍake nihatā mayā || 6-79-19

19. chaturdasha = fourteen; sahasrāṇi = thousand; rakṣhasām = demons; (Khara); yaḥ = who; tvatpitā = was your father; trishirāḥ = ṭrishira; dūṣhaṇashchāpi = and even Dushana; nihatāḥ = were killed; mayā = by me; daṇ^Dake = in Dandaka-forest.

"I killed fourteen thousand demons along with Khara your father, Trishira and even Dushana in Dandaka forest."

Sloka 20

svāśitāścāpi māṃsena gṛdhragomāyuvāyasāḥ |
bhaviṣyantyadya vai pāpa tīkṣaṇatuṇḍanakhāṅkurāḥ || 6-79-20

20. pāpa = O sinful one!; adya = today; gṛidhragomāyuvāyasāḥ = vultures; jackals and crows; tīkṣhṇatuṇ^Dana khāṇ^kurāḥ = with their sharp beaks and goad-like claws; bhaviṣhyanti = will be; svāshitāḥ = satiated; māmsena = with your flesh.

"O sinful one! Today, vultures jackals and crows with their sharp beaks and goad-like claws will be satiated with your flesh."

Sloka 21

rāghaveṇaivamuktastu makarākṣo mahābalaḥ |
bāṇaughānamucattasmai rāghavāya raṇājire || 6-79-21

21. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; makarākṣhaḥ = ṃakaraksha; amuchat = discharged; bāṇaugham = a multitude of arrows; tasmaiḥ rāghavāya = on that Rama; raṇājire = in the battle field.

Hearing Rama's words, the mighty ṃakaraksha discharged a multitude of arrows on that Rama in the battle-field.

Sloka 22

rān śarān śaravarṣeṇa rāmaściccheda naiadhā |
nipeturbhuvi te cinnā rukmapuṅkhāḥ sahasraśaḥ || 6-79-22

22. rāmaḥ = Rama; chichheda = tore; tān = those; sharān = arrows; naikadhā = in many ways; sharavarṣheṇa = by his array of arrows; te = those arrows; rukmapuṇkhāḥ = which were provided with golden shafts; nipetuḥ = fell; bhuvi = to the ground; chhinnāḥ = when torn; saharashaḥ = in thousands.

Rama tore those arrows in many ways, by his array of arrows. Those arrows, which were provided with golden shafts, fell to the ground, when torn in thousands.

Sloka 23

tadyuddhamabhavattatra sametyānyonyamojasā |
khararākṣasaputrasya sūnordaśarathasya ca || 6-79-23

23. tat = that; yuddham = battle; abhavat = raged; ojasā = furiously; sametya = on their meeting; anyonyam = each other; tatra = there; khararākṣhasaputrasya = between ṃakaraksha the son of Khara the demon; dasharathasya sūnoḥ cha = and Rama the son of Dasaratha.

That battle raged furiously, on their meeting each other there, between ṃakaraksha the son of Khara the demon and Rama the son of Dasaratha.

Sloka 24

jīmūtayorivākāśe śabdo jyātalayostadā |
dhanurmuktaḥ svanotkṛṣṭaḥ śrūyate ca raṇājire || 6-79-24

24. tadā = then; shabdaḥ = the sound; dhanurmuktaḥ = emanated by the bows; svanotkruṣhṭaḥ = with a great resonance; shrūyate cha = was heard; raṇājire = on the battle-front; jīyūtayoriva = like the rumbling of clouds; ākāshe = in space.

Then, the sound emanated by the bows, with a great resonance, was heard on the battle-front, like the rumbling of clouds in space.

Sloka 25

devadānavagandharvāḥ kimnarāśca mahoragāḥ |
antarikṣagatāḥ sarve draṣṭukāmāstadadbhutam || 6-79-25

25. draṣhṭukāmāḥ = with an intent to see; tat adbhutam = that wonderful battle; sarve = all; devadānavagandharvāḥ = the celestials; the demons; the celestial musicians; kinnarāshcha = mythical beings; mahoragāḥ = and the great serpents; antarikṣhagatāḥ = arrived at the sky.

With an intent to see that wonderful battle, all the celestials, the demons, the celestial musicians kinnaras the mythical beings and ṃahoragas the great serpents reached the sky.

Sloka 26

viddhamanyonyagātreṣu dviguṇaṃ vardhate balam |
kṛtapratikṛtānyonyaṃ kurutāṃ tau raṇājire || 6-79-26

26. anyonyagātreṣhuviddham = (ṭhrough) the limbs of both were pierced by each other; balam = their strength; dviguṇam vardhate = was redoubled; tau = the two warriors; kṛitapratikṛitānyonyam = inflicted wounds on each other and returned the blows of the other; raṇājire = in the battle-front; kurutām = and performed the combat.

Though the limbs of both were pierced by each other, their strength was redoubled. The two warriors inflicted wounds on each other, returned the blows of the other in the battle-front and continued the combat.

Sloka 27

rāmamuktāṃstu bāṇaughān rākṣasastvacchinadraṇe |
rakṣomuktāṃstu rāmo vai naikadhā prācchinaccharaiḥ || 6-79-27

27. raṇe = in the battle; rākṣhasaḥ = that demon; achchhinat = broke off; bāṇaughān = the multitude of arrows; rāma muktān = discharged by Rama; rāmaḥ = Rama; prāchchhinat = snapped off; naikadhā = in many ways; sharaiḥ = the arrows; rakṣhomuktān = released by the demon.

In the battle, that demon broke the multitude of arrows discharged by Rama. Rama snapped off, in many ways, the arrows released by the demon.

Sloka 28

bāṇaughavitatāḥ sarvā diśaśca pradiśastathā |
saṃcannā vasudhā cava samantānna prakāśate || 6-79-28

28. sarvāḥ = all; dishashcha = the four quarters; tathā = and; pradishaḥ = the intermediate points; bāṇaugha vitatāḥ = were diffused with a multitude of arrows; vasudhāchaiva = even the earth; samchchhannā = was covered; samantatā = on all sides; na prakāshate = and did not become visible.

All the four quarters and the intermediate points were diffused with a multitude of arrows. Even the earth was covered on all sides and did not become visible.

Sloka 29

tataḥ kruddho mahābāhurdhanuściccheda saṃyuge |
aṣṭābhiratha nārācaiḥ sūtaṃ vivyādha rāghavaḥ || 6-79-29

29. tataḥ = then; mahābāhuḥ = the long armed; rāghavaḥ = Rama; kruddhaḥ = enraged; samyuge = in battle; chichheda = broke up; dhanuḥ = the bow (of the demon); atha = thereupon; aṣhṭābhiḥ nārāchaiḥ = by his eight iron arrows; vivyādha = struck; sūtam = the charioteer.

Then, the long-armed Rama, full of anger in battle, broke off the bow of the demon. Thereupon, by his eight iron arrows, struck the charioteer.

Sloka 30

bhittvā rathaṃ śarai rāmo hatvā aśvāama[ātauat |
viratho vasudhāsthaḥ sa makarākṣo niśācaraḥ || 6-79-30

30. rāmaḥ = Rama; bhittvā = breaking; ratham = the chariot; sharaiḥ = with his arrows; hatvā = and killing; ashvān = the horses; apātayat = made them to fall down; saḥ makarākṣhaḥ = that ṃakaraksha; nishācharaḥ = the demon; virathaḥ = bereft of his chariot; vasudhāsthaḥ = stood on the ground.

Rama, breaking the chariot with his arrows and killing its horses, made them to fall down dead. That ṃakaraksha the demon, bereft of his chariot, stood on the ground.

Sloka 31

tattiṣṭhadvasudhāṃ rakṣaḥ śūlaṃ jagrāha pāṇinā |
trāsanam sarvabhūtānāṃ yugāntāgnisamaprabham || 6-79-31

31. tat = that; rakṣhaḥ = demon; tiṣhṭhat vasudhām = standing on the ground; jagrāha = took; pāṇinā = into his hand; shūlam = a spike; trāsanam = which created fright; sarva bhūtānām = among all living beings; yugāntāgni samaprabham = and whose splendour was equal to that of fire at the end of the world.

That demon, standing on the ground, took into his hand, a spike, which created fright among all living beings and whose splendour was equal to the fire at the end of the world.

Sloka 32

duravāpaṃ mahacchūlaṃ rudradattaṃ bhayaṃkaram |
jājvalyamānamākāśe saṃhārāstramivāparam || 6-79-32
yaṃ dṛṣṭvā devatāḥ sarvā bhayārtā vidrutā diśaḥ |

32. mahat shūlam = (ḥe took) that great spike; yam dṛiṣhṭvā = by seeing which; sarvāḥ devatāḥ = all the godheads; bhayārtāḥ = were afflicted with fear; vidrutāh = and fled; dishaḥ = to different directions; duravāpam = which was difficult to be obtained; rudradattam = which was gifted by Rudra; one of the eight forms of ṣhiva the lord of dissolution; bhayamkaram = which was terrible; jājvalyamānam = which flamed violently; ākāsho = in the sky; aparam samhārāstram iva = like another weapon of destruction.

That great spike, which was difficult to be obtained, was gifted to him by Rudra, one of the eight forms of Shiva the Lord of Dissolution. That terrible spike, which flamed violently in the sky, looked like another new weapon of destruction of the world. On seeing it, all the godheads were afflicted with fear and fled to different quarters.

Sloka 33

vibhrāmya ca mahaccūlaṃ prajvalantaṃ niśācaraḥ || 6-79-33
sa krodhātprāhiṇottasmai rāghavāya mahātmane |

33. vibhrāmyacha = whirling; mahat = that great; shūlam = spike; prajvalantam = which was flaming; nishācharaḥ = that demon; krodhāt = in rage; prāhiṇot = hurled it; tasmai mahātmane rāghavāya = against that great-souled Rama.

Brandishing that flaming great spike, that enraged demon hurled it against that great-souled rama.

Sloka 34

tamāpatantaṃ jvalitaṃ kharaputrakarāccyutam || 6-79-34
bāṇaiścaturbhirākāśe śūlaṃ ccheda rāghavaḥ |

34. rāghavaḥ = Rama; chichcheda = broke; chaturbhiḥ bāṇaiḥ = by his four arrows; tam shūlam = that spike; jvalitam = which was flaming; chyutam = coming forth from; kharaputrakarāt = the arm of ṃakaraksha; āpatntam = rushing towards him; ākāshe = in the sky.

Rama broke, by his four arrows, that flaming spike coming forth from ṃakaraksha's arm and rushing towards him in the sky.

Sloka 35

sa bhinno naikadhā śūlo divyahāṭakamaṇḍitaḥ || 6-79-35
vyaśīryata maholkeva rāmabāṇārdito bhuvi |

35. saḥ shūlaḥ = that spike; divyahāṭakamaṇ^Ditaḥ = adored with charming gold; bhinnaḥ = broken; naikadhā = in many ways; rāmabāṇārditaḥ = and tormented by Rama's arrows; vyashīryata = fell scattered; bhuvi = on the earth; maholkena = like a mighty meteor.

That spike, adored with charming gold, broken in many ways, struck by Rama's arrows, fell scattered on the earth, like a mighty meteor.

Sloka 36

tacchūlaṃ nihatam dṛṣṭvā rāmeṇākliṣṭakarmaṇā || 6-79-36
sādhusādhviti bhūtāni vyāharanti nabhogatāḥ |

36. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam = that; shūlam = spike; nihatam = struck; rāmeṇa = by Rama; akliṣhṭakarmaṇā = who was unwearied in action; bhūtāni = the beings; nabhogatāḥ = standing in the air; vyāharanti = cried; sādhu sādhu iti = saying 'excellent; excellent!'.

Seeing that spike struck by Rama, who was unwearied in action, the beings standing in the air, cried, saying 'Excellent, Excellent!'.

Sloka 37

taṃ dṛṣṭvā nihataṃ śūlaṃ makarākṣo niśācaraḥ || 6-79-37
muṣṭimudyamya kākutthsaṃ tiṣṭha tiṣṭheti cābravīt |

37. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam shūlam = that spike nihatam = destroyed; makarākṣhaḥ = ṃakaraksha; nishācharaḥ = the demon; udyamya = rising; muṣhṭim = his fist; abravīt = called out; kakutthsam = to Rama; iti = saying; tiṣhṭha tiṣhṭha = "Wait; Wait!".

Seeing that spike destroyed, ṃakaraksha the demon, rising his fist, called out to Rama, saying "Wait, Wait!".

Sloka 38

sa taṃ dṛṣṭvāpatantaṃ tu prahasya raghunandanaḥ || 6-79-38
pāvakāstraṃ tato rāmaḥ saṃdadhe tu śarāsane |

38. tataḥ = then; dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; tam = him; āpatantam = rushing towards him; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; raghunandanaḥ = the delight of Raghu dynasty; prahasya = while smiling; samdadhe sharāsane = fitted to his bow; pāvakāstram = a mystic missile presided over by fire.

Then, seeing ṃakaraksha rushing towards him, that Rama the delight of Raghu dynasty, while smiling, fitted to his bow, a mystic missile presided over by fire.

Sloka 39

tenāstreṇa hataṃ rakṣaḥ kākutsthena tadā raṇe || 6-79-39
sacchinnahṛdayaṃ tatra papāta ca mamāra ca |

39. hatam = struck; tena astreṇa = by that missile; kākutthsena = by Rama; rakṣhaḥ = the demon; tadā = then; bhinna hṛidayam sat = with his heart split open; papāta = fell down; mamāracha = dead; tatra = there; raṇe = in the battle-field.

Struck by that missile by Rama, the demon with his heart split open, fell down dead then and there, in the battle-field.

Sloka 40

dṛṣṭvā te rākṣasāḥ sarve makarākṣasya pātanam || 6-79-40
laṅkāmeva pradhāvanta rāmabāṇabhayārditāḥ |

40. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; pātanam = the fall; makarākṣhasya = of ṃakaraksha; sarve = all; te rākṣhasāḥ = those demons; rāmabāṇa bhayārditāḥ = tormented by the fear of Rama's arrows; pradhāvanta = ran away; laṇ^kāmeva = straight to Lanka.

Seeing the fall of ṃakaraksha, all those demons, tormented by the fear of Rama's arrows, ran away straight to Lanka.

Sloka 41

daśarathanṛpasūnubāṇavegai |
rajanicaraṃ nihatam kharātmajaṃ tam |
pradadṛśuratha devatāḥ prahṛṣṭā |
girimiva vajrahataṃ yathā vikīrṇam || 6-79-41

41. prahṛiṣhṭāḥ = thrilled with delight; devatāḥ = the celestials; tam rajanicharam pradadṛishuḥ = (watched) that demon; kharātmajam = the son of Khara; nihatam = destroyed; dasharatha nṛipa sūnu bāṇa vegaiḥ = by the flighs of Rama's arrows; girimiva = like a mountain; vikīrṇam = shattered; vajrahatam = after struck by lightning.

Thrilled with delight, the celestials watched that demon, the son of Khara, destroyed by the flights of Rama's arrows and resembling a mountain shattered, after struck by lightning.
