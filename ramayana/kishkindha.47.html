Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 47
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction


Search for Seetha failed in east, north, and west directions. The monkey chiefs who went there have come back to inform that she is not found and they all hoped Hanuma to find her out, because he is pursuing the direction in which Seetha was taken away.


Sloka 1

darśanārtham tu vaidehyāḥ sarvataḥ kapi kuṃjarāḥ |
vyādiṣṭāḥ kapi rājena yathā uktam jagmur aṃjasā || 4-47-1

1. kapi rājena vyādiṣṭāḥ [vi ā dish] = by monkeys, king, commanded; kapi kunjarāḥ = monkeys, elephantine; yathā uktam = as, said [true to command]; vaidehyāḥ darshanārtham = of Vaidehi, for a glimpse; anjasā = with post-haste; sarvataḥ jagmuḥ = everywhere, essayed at.

As commanded by the king of monkeys Sugreeva all of the elephantine monkeys essayed everywhere with post-hast for a glimpse of Vaidehi. [4-47-1]

Sloka 2

te sarāṃsi sarit kakṣān ākāśam nagarāṇi ca |
nadī durgān tathā śailān vicinvanti samantataḥ || 4-47-2

2. te = those - monkeys; sarāmsi saritaḥ kakṣān = at lakes, river's [on riverbanks,] chambers, [creeper-pens]; ākāsham nagarāṇi ca = sky [empty space, unpeopled lands,] cities, also; tathā = thus; nadī dur gān shailān = with rivers, not, passable places, mountains; samantataḥ vi cinvanti = everywhere, thoroughly, searched out.

Those monkeys have thoroughly searched at lakes, in the pens of creepers on riverbanks, in vacant lands and in crowded cities and at impassable places with rivers and mountains. [4-47-2]

Sloka 3

sugrīveṇa samākhyātāḥ sarve vānara yūthapāḥ |
tatra deśān pravicinvanti sa śaila vana kānanān || 4-47-3

3. sugrīveṇa sam ākhyātāḥ = by ṣugreeva, well-said [explicitly, commanded]; sarve vānara yūthapāḥ = all, monkey's, commanders; tatra = in that matter; pra sa shaila vana kānanān = inclusive of, mountains, woodlands, thick forests; [sarvān = all of the]; deshān = provinces; vi cinvanti = carefully, searched.

All the monkey commanders explicitly commanded by Sugreeva, very carefully searched all the provinces inclusive of mountains, woodlands, and thick forests of those provinces. [4-47-3]

Sloka 4

vicintya divasam sarve sītā adhigamane dhṛtāḥ |
samāyānti sma medinyām niśā kāleṣu vānarāḥ || 4-47-4

4. sarve vānarāḥ = all of the, monkeys; sītā adhigamane dhṛitāḥ = in ṣeetha's, attaining, firmed up; divasam vicintya = by day, on searching; nishā kāleṣu = at night, time; medinyām = onto earth; sam āyānti sma = well [together,] come, they are.

All the Vanara-s firmed up in finding Seetha have searched nooks and corners of earth by day, and at nighttimes they used to come together onto earth at scheduled places. [4-47-4]

Sloka 5

sarva ṛtukān ca deśeṣu vānarāḥ sa phalān drumān |
āsādya rajanīm śayyām cakruḥ sarveṣu ahassu te || 4-47-5

5. te vānarāḥ = those, Vanara-s; sarveṣu ahassu [ te] = in all [those,] days; sarva ṛitukān ca = all, seasonal, also; sa phalān drumān = with, fruits, trees; desheṣu āsādya = in [those and those] provinces, on getting at; rajanīm shayyām cakruḥ = at nights [at bedtime,] bed, made.

In all those days those Vanara-s used to come to trees which have fruits of all seasons, to make those trees as their beds at bedtime. [4-47-5]

▼

Or to say in another way: drumān saphalān cakruḥ= 'trees, blessed to be fruitful...' Fruting trees will be aplenty with fruits, if monkeys visit them. Because monkeys resided on all the trees of all provinces, Rama blesses all of the trees of those and those countries through those monkeys, namely the agents of Rama, to be full of fruits. Even now allowing a troop of monkeys into orchard or groves is tolerable and sacred. But after a sumptuous meal, they start all their monkey tricks and ransack gardens, which is intolerable and then they are driven out.

Sloka 6

tat ahaḥ prathamam kṛtvā māse prasravaṇam gatāḥ |
kapi rājena saṃgamya nirāśāḥ kapi kuṃjarāḥ || 4-47-6

6. tat ahaḥ prathamam kṛitvā = that, day [of their starting,] first, on making [counting]; māse = in month; kapi kunjarāḥ = monkey, elephants; nir āshāḥ = without, hope; prasravaṇam gatāḥ = , ṃt. Prasavana, went [returned]; kapi rājena sangamya = with monkey, king, joined - met with ṣugreeva.

Counting the day of their starting as day one, those elephantine monkeys returned to ṃt. Prasavana within one month, hopeless of Seetha, and met Sugreeva, the king of monkeys, who is with Rama awaiting the return of monkeys. [4-47-6]

Sloka 7

vicitya tu diśam pūrvām yathā uktām sacivaiḥ saha |
adṛṣṭvā vinataḥ sītām ājagāma mahābalaḥ || 4-47-7

7. mahābalaḥ = great-mighty one; vinataḥ = Cmdr. Vinata; sacivaiḥ saha = 1] ministers, 2] colleagues, together with; yathā uktām = as, said [as advised by ṣugreeva]; pūrvām disham vicitya = eastern, quarter, on searching; sītām a dṛiṣṭvā = ṣeetha, not, seeing [finding]; ā jagāma = came back.

Cmdr. Vinata, the great-mighty vanara, came back after searching eastern quarter as Sugreeva had advised, but without finding Seetha there. [4-47-7]

Sloka 8

diśam api uttarām sarvām vivicya sa mahākapiḥ |
āgataḥ saha sainyena vīraḥ śatabaliḥ tadā || 4-47-8

8. tadā = then; vīraḥ mahā kapiḥ shatabaliḥ = valiant one, matchless, monkey, ṣhatabali; sainyena saha = army, along with; sarvām uttarām disham api = whole of, northern, direction, even; vi vicya = = explored; [sītām a dṛiṣṭvā = ṣeetha, without seeing]; saḥ = such as he is; āgataḥ = he came back.

Even the valiant and matchless monkey Shatabali explored whole of the northern direction along with his army, but he too came back as Seetha is undiscovered there. [4-47-8]

Sloka 9

suṣeṇaḥ paścimām āśām vivicya saha vānaraiḥ |
sametya māse pūrṇe tu sugrīvam upacakrame || 4-47-9

9. suṣeṇaḥ vānaraiḥ saha = ṣushena [father-in-law of ṣugreeva,] vanara-s, along with; pashcimām āshām vivicya = in western, direction, on searching; māse pūrṇe tu = a month, on completion, but; [a dṛiṣṭvā sītām = without seeing, ṣeetha]; sametya = came back; sugrīvam upacakrame = to ṣugreeva, nearby arrived.

Sushena, the father-in-law of Sugreeva, searching whole of western quarter along with vanara-s, but not finding Seetha there, he came back when one month is completed, and approached Sugreeva. [4-47-9]

Sloka 10

tam prasravaṇa pṛṣṭhastham samāsādya abhivādya ca |
āsīnam saha rāmeṇa sugrīvam idam abruvan || 4-47-10

10. rāmeṇa saha āsīnam = Rama, along with, one who is seated; prasravaṇa pṛiṣṭhastham = ṃt. Prasavana, on peak of; tam sugrīvam = him, ṣugreeva; sam āsādya = on reaching; abhivādya ca = on revering, also; idam abruvan = this, [all the expeditionists] spoke.

All of the expeditionists have reached and venerated Sugreeva who is sitting along with Rama on the peak of ṃt. Prasavana and spoke this to him. [4-47-10]

Sloka 11

vicitāḥ parvatāḥ sarve vanāni gahanāni ca |
nimnagāḥ sāgara antāḥ ca sarve janapadāḥ tathā || 4-47-11

11. sarve parvatāḥ vicitāḥ = all, mountains, are searched; gahanāni vanāni ca = serried, forests, also; nimn agāḥ = low, going [flowing, rivers that slope down]; sāgara antāḥ ca = ocean's, edge of [or, along the rivers that slope down into oceans]; tathā = likewise; sarve jana padāḥ = all, people's, places [inhabitations - are thoroughly searched.]

"All of the mountains, all of the serried forests, all of the riversides up to the edge of oceans, likewise, all of the inhabitations have been searched. [4-47-11]

Sloka 12

guhāḥ ca vicitāḥ sarvā yāḥ ca te parikīrtitāḥ |
vicitāḥ ca mahāgulmā latā vitata saṃtatāḥ || 4-47-12

12. sarvāḥ = all; te pari kīrtitāḥ = by you, explicitly, extolled; yāḥ = which of those; guhāḥ = caves are there; they too; vicitāḥ = thoroughly searched; latā vitata santatāḥ = climbers, entwined with, thickly; mahā gulmāḥ = immense, shrubby hutches; vicitāḥ ca = rummaged, also.

"Those caves that were explicitly extolled by you, they are all thoroughly searched, and immense shrubby hutches that are thickly entwined with climber plants have also been rummaged. [4-47-12]

Sloka 13

gahaneṣu ca deśeṣu durgeṣu viṣameṣu ca |
sattvāni atipramāṇāni vicitāni hatāni ca |
ye caiva gahanā deśā vicitāḥ te punaḥ punaḥ || 4-47-13

13. gahaneṣu = in compacted places; dur geṣu = impenetrable areas; viṣameṣu ca = asymmetrical, [lopsided lands]; desheṣu = in such provinces; ati pramāṇāni = too much, in size [colossal beings]; sattvāni = living beings [creatures]; vicitāni = are hunted out; hatāni ca = are killed [by us,] also; gahanāḥ ye deshāḥ = impassable ones, which of those, provinces are there; te = they are; punaḥ punaḥ vicitāḥ = again, again, searched high and low.

"Search is conducted in the compacted, lopsided, and impenetrable provinces, and colossal beings are hunted out and killed presuming that Ravana might have assumed those odd shapes, and the impassable provinces are searched high and low, time and again. [4-47-13]

Sloka 14

udāra sattva abhijano hanūmān
sa maithilīm jñāsyasi vānarendra |
diśam tu yām eva gatā tu sītā
tām āsthito vāyu suto hanūmān || 4-47-14

14. vānarendra = oh, Vanara king; udāra sattva abhijanaḥ = exceptionally, mighty, high-born one; saḥ hanūmān = he that, ḥanuma; maithilīm jñāsyasi = about ṃaithili, he ascertains; sītā yām eva disham gatā = ṣeetha, in which, direction, alone, has gone [taken away]; tām = that direction; vāyu sutaḥ hanūmān = Air-god's, son, ḥanuma; āsthitaḥ = resorted to.

"Hanuma is high-born and exceptionally mighty, oh, Sugreeva, the king of vanara-s, he will ascertain about ṃaithili, because the son of Air-god, Hanuma, resorted to the direction in which Seetha is taken away." Thus, monkey chiefs reported to their king Sugreeva. [4-47-14]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe sapta catvārinśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 47th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
