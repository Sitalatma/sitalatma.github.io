Introduction

Rama asks Hanuma to proceed to Shringaberapura and communicate to Guha about his welfare and also to know the appropriate path to Ayodhya. Rama also asks Hanuma to go to Ayodhya and communicate to Bharata about his welfare and also the previous happenings at the forest and at Lanka, including the abduction of Seetha and her restoration, by killing Ravana in battle. Rama also asks Hanuma to inform Bharata, about their arrival at the proximity of Ayodhya, along with Sugreeva and Vibhishana. Accordingly, Hanuma approaches Guha and Bharata in Nandigrama and informs the pleasant news about the welfare of Rama as well as his impending return to Ayodhya. Bharata is overjoyed on hearing the glad tidings from Hanuma and offers him a number of valuable gifts, for having communicated the agreeable news to him.

Sloka 1

ayodhyāṃ tu samālokya cintayāmāsa rāghavaḥ |
priyakāmaḥ priyaṃ rāmastatastvaritavikramam
ayodhyāṃ tu samālokya cintayāmāsa rāghavaḥ |
priyakāmaḥ priyaṃ rāmastatastvaritavikramaḥ || 6-125-1

1. samāloky = looking at; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; rāmaḥ = Rama; rāghavaḥ = born in Raghu dynasty; tvarita vikramaḥ = who was quick in exhibiting prowess; priyakāmaḥ = who wished to do a kindly act; chintayāmāsa = contemplated; tataḥ = thereupon; priyam = to do a kindly act.

Looking at Ayodhya, Rama, a scion of Raghu dynasty, for his part, who wished to do a kindly act and who was quick in exhibiting prowess, contemplated thereupon to do a kindly act.

Sloka 2

cintayitvā tato dṛṣṭiṃ vānareṣu nyapātayat |
uvāca dhīmāṃstejasvī hanūmantaṃ plavaṅgamam || 6-125-2

2. chintayitvā = having contemplated; dhīmān tejasvī = the sagacious and the glorious Rama; tataḥ = then; nyapātayat = cast; dṛiṣhṭim = his glimpse; vānareṣhu = on the monkeys; uvācha = and spoke; hanūmantam = to ḥanuma; plavaṇ^gamma = the monkey.

Having thus contemplated, the sagacious and the glorious Rama then cast his glimpse on the monkeys and spoke to Hanuma, the monkey (as follows):

Sloka 3

jānīhi kaccitkuśalī jano nṛpatimandire || 6-125-3

3. plavaga sattama = O the foremost of the monkeys!; gatvā = going; tvaritaḥ shīghraḥ = with all speed; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; jānīhi = find out; janaḥ kushalī kachchit = whether the people there are sage; nṛipatimandire = in the royal palace.

"O the foremost of the monkeys! Going with all speed to Ayodhya, find out whether the people there, are safe in the royal palace.

Sloka 4

niṣādādhipatiṃ brūhi kuśalaṃ vacanānmama || 6-125-4

4. prāpya = reaching; shṛiṇ^ga berapuram = ṣhringaberapura; brūhi = communicate; kushalam = about my welfare; mama vachanāt = in my name; guha = to Guha; niṣhādādhipatim = the king of ṇishadas; a wild tribe; gahanagocharam = who lives in the forest.

"Reaching then to Shrigaberapura, communicate in my name, about my welfare to Guha, the king of Nishadas, a wild tribe living in the forest."

Sloka 5

śrutvā tu māṃ kuśalinamarogaṃ vigatajvaram |
aviṣyati guhaḥ prītaḥ sa mamātmasamaḥ sakhā || 6-125-5

5. guhaḥ = Guha; bhaviṣhayti = will be; prītaḥ = pleased; shrutvā = in hearing; mām = about me; ātmasamaḥ = as well as my own self.

"Guha will be pleased to hear about me, as being safe, healthy and free from trouble. He is my friend, as well as my own self."

Sloka 6

nivedayiṣyati prīto niṣādādhipatirguhaḥ || 6-125-6

Feeling pleased, Guha, the lord of Nishadas, the wild tribe, will tell the path to Ayodhya and the news about Bharata."

Sloka 7

"Bharata too is to be asked by you, the news about his welfare, in my name. Tell him of me as having returned, accomplished of our object, with my consort and Lakshmana."

Sloka 8

haraṇaṃ cāpi vaidehyā rāvaṇena balīyasā |

"Tell him about the abduction of Seetha by the mighty Ravana, my conversation with Sugreeva and the killing of Vali in combat."

Sloka 9

"Tell him also about the search for Seetha and how she was traced out by you, after crossing the never-diminishing ocean, having extensive waters."

Sloka 10

"Tell him about our going near the ocean, the appearance of the ocean, how the bridge was constructed there and how Ravana was killed."

Sloka 11

varadānaṃ mahendreṇa brahmaṇā varuṇena ca |

11. (ṭell him) varadānam = about the bestowal of boons; mahendreṇa = by Indra the lord of celestials; brahmaṇā = by Brahma the lord of creation; varuṇena = and by Varuna the lord of waters; māma samāgamam = my meeting; pitā = with my (deceased) father; mahādevaprasādena = through the grace of ṣhiva; the ṣupreme Deity.

"Tell him about the bestower of boons by Indra the lord of celestials, by brahma the lord of creation, and by Varuna the lord of waters, my meeting with my (deceased) father, through the grace of Shiva the Supreme Deity."

Sloka 12

12. saumya = O gently ḥanuma!; nivedaya = inform; bharatāya = to Bharata; mām = of me; upayātam = as having come near; rākṣhasarājena saha = along with Vibhishana; īshvareṇa harīṇām = and ṣugreeva the lord of monkeys.

"O gentle Hanuma! Inform to Bharata, of me as having come near Ayodhya, along with Vibhishana and Sugreeva the lord of monkeys."

Sloka 13

jitvā śatrugaṇānrāmaḥ prāpya cānuttamaṃ yaśaḥ |
upayāti samṛddhārthaḥ saha mitrairmahābalaḥ || 6-125-13

13. (ṭell him) rāmaḥ = (that) Rama; upāyāti = is coming; jitvā = having conquered; shatrugaṇām = hosts of enemies; prāpyacha = having obtained; anuttamām yashaḥ = an excellent fame; samṛiddhārthaḥ = having accomplished his purpose; mahābalaiḥ mitraiḥ saha = along with his mighty friends.

"Tell him, that having conquered hosts of enemies and obtained an excellent fame, is purpose having been accomplished, Rama is coming near, with is mighty friends."

Sloka 14

etacchrutvā yamākāraṃ bhajate bharatastataḥ |

14. saḥ cha = it is also; veditavyaḥ = to be known; te = by you; ākāram = the facial expression; yan = which; bhajate = he obtained; shrutvā = after having heard; etat = this; sarvam = all; yachchāpi = syāt = that he intends to do; mām prati = in relation to me.

"It is also to be observed by you, about the facial expression which Bharata wears, after hearing all this and also all that he intends to do in relation to me."

Sloka 15

jñeyāḥ sarve ca vṛttāntā bharatasyeṅgitāni ca |

15. sarvecha = all; vṛittāntāḥ = the occurances; Jṇeyāḥ = are to be known; mukhavarnena = by the colour of his face; dṛiṣhṭyā = by his glances; vyābhāṣhitena = and by his speech; iṇ^gitāni = gestures; bharatasya = of Bharata; tattvataḥ = are to be really; jJṇeyāḥ = known.

"All the occurrences there are to be known. All the gestures of Bharata are to be ascertained through the colour of his face, glances and the speech."

Sloka 16

sarvakāmasamṛddhaṃ hi hastyaśvarathasaṅkulam |
pitṛpaitāmahaṃ rājyaṃ kasya nāvartayenmanaḥ || 6-125-16

16. kasya = whose; manaḥ = mind; na āvartayet = does not turn round; rājyam = with a kingdom; sarvakāma samṛiddham = abundantly rich in all coveted enjoyments; hastyashvaratha samkulam = teeming with elephants; horses and chariots; pitṛipaitāmaham = and which is inherited from one's father?

"Whose mind does not turn round, with a kingdom abundantly rich in all coveted enjoyments, teeming with elephants horses and chariots and which is in herited from one's father?"

Sloka 17

saṃgatyā bharataḥ śrīmānrājyenārthī svayaṃ bhavet |

17. samgatyā = having been associated (for long); rājyena = with the kingdom; bhavet shrīmān bharataḥ = if the illustrious Bharata; arthī = has a desire; svayam = for himself; raghunanadanaḥ = (let) Bharata; prashāstu = rule; sarvām vasudhām = the entire kingdom; akhilām = in one piece.

"Having been associated with the kingdom for long, if the illustrious Bharata is longing for it himself, let Bharata rule the entire kingdom in one piece."

Sloka 18

tasya buddhiṃ ca vijñāya vyavasāyaṃ ca vānara |
yāvanna dūraṃ yātāḥ smaḥ kṣipramāgantumarhasi || 6-125-18

18. vānara = O ḥanuma!; viJṇāya = understanding; tasya = his; buddhim = mind; vyavasāyamcha = and perception; arhasi = you ought; āgantum = to come; kṣhipram = quickly; yāvat dūram na yātā smaḥ = before we do not go for a long distance towards Ayodhya.

"O Hanuma! Understanding his mind and perception, you ought to return quickly, before we are not gone for a long distance towards Ayodhya."

Sloka 19

iti pratisamādiṣṭo hanūmānmārutātmajaḥ |
mānuṣaṃ dhārayanrūpamayodhyāṃ tvarito yayau || 6-125-19

19. iti = thus; pratisamādiṣhṭaḥ = commanded; hanuman = hanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; dhārayan = assuming; mānuṣham rūpam = a human form; tvaritaḥ = swiftly; yayau = went; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya.

Assuming a human form, when thus commanded by Rama, Hanuma the son of wind-god, swiftly left for Ayodhya.

Sloka 20

athotpapāta vegena hanūmān mārutātmajaḥ |
garutmāniva vegena jighṛkṣan pannagottamam || 6-125-20

20. garutmāniva = as Garuda the eagle; (flies) vegena = speedily; jighṛikṣhan = with an intention to seize; pannagottamam = a large snake; mārutātmajaḥ = ḥanuma the son of wind-god; atha = then; utpapāta = flew up; vegena = with speed.

Hanuma, the son of wind-god then flew up with speed towards Ayodhya, as Garuda the eagle would fly with speed, while seeking to catch a large snake.

Sloka 21

laṅghayitvā pitṛpathaṃ bhujagendrālayaṃ śubham |
gaṅgāyamunayorbhīmaṃ saṃnipātamatītya ca || 6-125-21
śṛṅgaverapuraṃ prāpya guhamāsādya vīryavān |
sa vācā śubhayā hṛṣṭo hanūmānidamabravīt || 6-125-22

21-22. laṇghayitvā = leaping; shubham pitṛipatham = into the auspicious sky; vhagendrālayam = the dwelling place of birds; samatītya = crossing over; bhīmam samāgamam = the terrific conjuction; gaṇ^gāyamunayoḥ = of Ganga and Yamuna rivers; prāpya = reaching; shṛiṇgaberapuram = the City of ṣhringabera; āsādya = and approaching; guham = Guha; vīryavān sah hanūmān = that valiant hanuma hṛiṣhṭaḥ = joyfully; abravīt = spoke; idam = the following words; shubhayā vāchā = in a charming voice:

Leaping into the auspicious sky, the dwelling place of birds, duly crossing over the terrific conjunction of Ganga and Yamuna rivers, reaching Shringaverapura and approaching Guha (the chief of Nishadas), that valiant Hanuma spoke the following words in a charming voice:

Sloka 23

sakhā tu tava kākutstho rāmaḥ satyaparākramaḥ |
sasītaḥ saha saumitriḥ sa tvāṃ kuśalamabravīt || 6-125-23

23. saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; tava sakhā = your friend; kākutthsaḥ = born in Kakutthsa dynasty; satya parākramaḥ = who is true in his prowess; sa sītaḥ = along with ṣeetha; saha saumitriḥ = and Lakshmana; abravīt = enquired; tvām kushalam = of your welfare.

"Along with Seetha and Lakshmana, Rama, your friend, that scion of Kakutthsa, of true prowess, has for his part enquired of your welfare."

Sloka 24

"You can see Rama here itself today, when he has been duly permitted by Bharadwaja the sage, after spending, at the instance of the sage, for a night of the fifth lunar day (of the bright half of the month of Ashvayuja)."

Sloka 25

utpapāta mahāvego vegavānavicārayan || 6-125-25

25. vegavān = that swift moving ḥanuma; samprahṛiṣhṭa tanūruhah = with his rejoicingly feather-like body; mahā tejāḥ = and with a great splendor; avichārayan = not thinking of his fatigue; utpapāta = sprang up; mahāvegāt = with a great speed.

That swift-moving Hanuma, with his rejoicingly feather-like body and with a great splendor, not thinking of his fatigue, sprang up with a great speed.

Sloka 26

26. saḥ = ḥanuma; apashyat = saw; rāmatīrtham = Ramatirtha; a place of a descent into the river (hallowed by the association of Lord Parasurama); tathā = as well as; vālukinīm nadīm = the rivers VAlukini; varūthīm = Varuthini; gomatīm chaiva = and Gomati; tathā = and; bhīmam = the formidable; shālavanam = forest of ṣala trees; bahusāhasrīḥ = and many thousands; prajāshcha = of people; sphītān = and numerous; janapadānapi = communities.

Hanuma saw, on the way, Ramatirtha, a place of descent into the river (hallowed by the association of Lord Parashurama), as well as the rivers Valukini, Varuthini and Gomati as also the formidable forest of Sala trees, many thousands of people and numerous communities.

Sloka 27

Going quickly for a long distance, that foremost of the monkeys reached trees in flowering, near Nandigrama and looking like those comprised in the garden of Kubera (known by the name, chitraratha) and frequented by women along with their sons and elders, charmingly adorned as they were.

Sloka 34

Those citizens, who were all fond of righteousness, were not willing to enjoy pleasures, ignoring that prince clad in the bark of trees and the skin of a black antelope.

Sloka 36

asminmuhūrte bhrātrā tvaṃ rāmeṇa saha saṅgataḥ |

37. deva = O Lord!; ākhyāmi = I am telling; te = you; priyam = a pleasant news; tyaja = give up; sudāruṇam = the dreadful anguish; asmin muhūrte = at this moment; tvam = you; samgataḥ = joined; rāmeṇa saha = with Rama; bhrātrā = your elder brother.

"O Lord! I am telling you a pleasant news. Give up this dreadful anguish. Within a while, you will be meeting Rama, your elder brother."

Sloka 38

nihatya rāvaṇaṃ rāmaḥ pratilabhya ca maithilīm || 6-125-38
upayāti samṛddhārthaḥ saha mitrairmahābalaiḥ |

38. nihatya = having killed; rāvaṇam = Ravana; pratilabhya = and having got back; maithilīm = ṣeetha; rāmaḥ = Rama; samṛiddhārthaḥ = having accomplished his purpose; rāmaḥ = Rama; upayāti = is coming; mahābalaiḥ mitraiḥ saha = with his mighty friends.

"Having killed Ravana and got back Seetha, Rama is returning with his mighty friends, his purpose duly accomplished."

Sloka 39

lakṣmaṇaśca mahātejā vaidehī ca yaśasvinī || 6-125-39
sītā samagrā rāmeṇa mahendreṇa śacī yathā |

39. lakṣhmaṇashcha = Lakshmana too; mahātejāḥ = who is endowed with extra ordinary energy; (is coming); samagrā sītā = intact with ṣeetha; yashasvinī vaidehī = the illustrious princess of Videha territory; rāmeṇa = and with Rama; shachī yathā = as ṣachi (the wife of Indra) mahendreṇa = would with the mighty Indra the lord of celestials.

"Lakshmana too, who is endowed with extraordinary energy, is coming intact with Seetha, the illustrious princess of Videha territory and with Rama as Sachi (the wife of Indra) would with the mighty Indra the lord of celestials."

Sloka 40

evamukto hanumatā bharataḥ kaikayīsutaḥ || 6-125-40
papāta sahasā hṛṣṭo harṣānmohamupāgamat |

40. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoke; hanumatā = by ḥanuma; bharataḥ = Bharata; kaikeyīsutaḥ = the son of Kaikeyi; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = felt delighted; papāta = and sank; sahasā = all at once; bhūmau = to the ground; upāgamat moham = and fainted; harṣhāt = through joy.

Hearing the words of Hanuma, Bharata the son of Kaikeyi, felt delighted and sank all at once to the ground as also fainted through joy.

Sloka 41

tato muhūrtādutthāya pratyāśvasya ca rāghavaḥ || 6-125-41
hanumantamuvācedaṃ bharataḥ priyavādinam |

41. tataḥ = thereupon; bharataḥ = Bharata; rāghavaḥ = who was born in Raghu dynasty; uttāya = got up; muhūrtāt = within a while; pratyāshvasya = and regaining his consciousness; uvācha = spoke; idam = the following words hanūmantam = to ḥanuma; priya vādinam = who spoke pleasant tidings (to him).

Thereupon, Bharata who was born in Raghu dynasty, got up from the swoon within a while and regaining his consciousness, spoke the following words to Hanuma, who spoke pleasant tidings to him.

Sloka 42

aśokajaiḥ prītimayaiḥ kapimāliṅgya sambhramāt || 6-125-42
siṣeca bharataḥ śrīmān vipulairaśrubindubhiḥ |

42. āliṇ^gya = embracing; kapim = ḥanuma; sambhramāt = with eagerness; shrīmān = the illustrious; bharataḥ = Bharata; siṣhecha = bathed (him) vipulaiḥ = with copious; ashrubindubhiḥ = tear-drops; prītimayaiḥ = born of delight; ashokajaiḥ = and as such; other than those born of anguish.

Embracing Hanuma with eagerness, the illustrious Bharata bathed him with copious tear-drops born of delight and as such, other than those born of anguish.

Sloka 43

devo vā mānuṣo vā tvamanukrośādihāgataḥ || 6-125-43
priyākhyānasya te saumya dadāmi bruvataḥ priyam |
gavāṃ śatasahasraṃ ca grāmāṇāṃ ca śataṃ param || 6-125-44
sakuṇḍalāḥ śubhācārā bhāryāḥ kanyāśca ṣoḍaśa |
hemavarṇāḥ sunāsorūḥ śaśisaumyānanāḥ striyaḥ || 6-125-45
sarvābharaṇasampannā sampannāḥ kulajātibhiḥ |

43-45. saumya = O the gentle one!; tvam = are you; devovā = a divine being mānuṣhovā = or a human being; āgataḥ = who have come; iha = here; anukroshāt = out of compassion?; te priyākhyānasya = to you; who have given this agreeable news to me; dadāmi = I shall give (in return); priyam bruvataḥ = for the pleasant tidings; shatasahasram cha = a hundred thousand; gavām = cows; shatam = a hundred; param grāmāṇām = best villages; bhāryāḥ = and for wives; shooDasha = sixteen; hemavarṇāḥ = golden complexioned; kanyāḥ = virgin girls; shubhāchārāḥ = of a good conduct; sakuṇ^Dalāḥ = decked with ear-rings; sunā sorūḥ = having beautiful noses and thighs; sarvābharaṇa sampannāḥ = adorned with all kinds of jewels; shashi saumyānanāḥ = with charming countenances as delightful as the moon; kulajātibhiḥ = and born in a noble family.

"O the gentle one! Are you a divine being or a human being, who have come here out of compassion? To you, who have given this agreeable news to me, I shall give in return, for the pleasant tidings, a hundred thousand cows, a hundred best villages, and for wives, sixteen golden complexioned virgin girls of a good conduct, decked with ear-rings, having beautiful noses and thighs, adorned with all kinds of jewels, with charming countenances as delightful as the moon and born in a noble family."

Sloka 46

niśamya rāmāgamanaṃ nṛpātmajaḥ |
kapipravīrasya tadādbhutopamam |
praharṣito rāmadidṛkṣayābhavat |
punaśca harṣādidamabravīdvacaḥ || 6-125-46

46. nishamya = hearing; kapipravīrasya = from ḥanuma; rāmāgamanam = the news of arrival of Rama; adbhutopamam = resembling a wonder; niṛipātmajaḥ = Bharata the prince; abhavat = became; praharṣhataḥ = over-joyed; rāma didṛikṣhayā = by a desire to see Rama; punashcha = and spoke; idam vachanam = the following words; punashcha = again; harṣhāt = with delight.

Hearing the news of Rama's arrival resembling a womnder, from Hanuma, Bharata the prince felt overjoyed by a desire to see Rama and spoke the following words again with delight:
