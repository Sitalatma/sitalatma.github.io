Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 72
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Having been informed that Atikaya, Dhumraksha, Akampana, Prahasta, Kumbhakarna and other mighty demons are killed in battle, Ravana felt anxious. He is surprised as to how Rama and Lakshmana got released from the bind of arrows, made by Indrajit, his son earlier. He feels that no demon is capable of defeating Rama, Lakshmana, Sugreeva and Vibhishana. He enjoins demons to protect the City of Lanka and even Ashoka garden, where Seetha has been kept in capture. He asks the army not to be indifferent to the movements of the monkey-warriors. After issuing the necessary instruction to his army, he enters deep into his palace and remains boarding over the loss of Atikaya, his son.

Sloka 1

atikāyaṃ hataṃ śrutvā lakṣmaṇena mahātmanā |
udvegamagamadrājā vacanam cedamabravīt || 6-71-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; atkāyam = Atikaya; hatam = killed; mahātmanā lakṣhmaṇena = in the hands of the high souled Lakshmana; rājā = the king; agamat = got; udvegam = an anxiety; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words:

Hearing Atikaya having been killed in the hands of the great-souled Lakshmana, Ravana was worried and spoke as follows:

Sloka 2

dhūmrākṣaḥ paramāmarṣī sarvaśastrabhṛtāṃ varaḥ |
akampanaḥ prahastaśca kumbhakarṇasttathaiva ca || 6-71-2
ete mahābalā vīrā rākṣasā yuddhakāṅikṣaṇaḥ |
jetāraḥ parasainyānāṃ parairnityāparājitāḥ || 6-71-3
sasainyāste hatā vīrā rāmeṇākliṣṭakarmaṇā |
rākṣasāḥ sumahākāyā nānāśastraviśāradāḥ || 6-71-4
anye ca bahavaḥ śūrā mahātmāno nipātitāḥ |

2; 3; 4. dhūmrākṣhaḥ = Dhumraksha; akampanaḥ = Akampana; paramāmarṣhī = greatly impatient; sarvashastrabhṛitām varaḥ = excellent among all the wielders of weapons; prahastashcha = Prahasta; tathaivacha = and; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; mahābalāḥ = the mighty; vīrāḥ = and valiant; rākṣhasāḥ = demons; yuddhakāṇkṣhiṇaḥ = longing for battle; jetāraḥ = who conquer; parasainyānām = the army of adversaries; nityāparājitāḥ = never conquered; pariah = by enemies; te vīrāḥ = those warriors; sasainyāḥ = along with their armies; hatāḥ = were killed; rāmeṇa = by Rama; akliṣhṭa bahavaḥ = many; karmāṇām = unwearied in action; bahavaḥ = many; anye = other; mahātmanaḥ = mighty; shūrāḥ = warriors; sumahākāyāḥ = with colossal bodies; nānāshastravishāradāḥ = skilled in various kinds of weaponry; vinipātitāḥ = were struck down.

"Dhumraksha, who was greatly impatient Akampana who was excellent among all wielders of weapons, Prahasta and Kumbhakarna were killed by Rama, who was unwearied in battle. The mighty and valiant demons longing for battle, who conquer the adversaries, who was never defeated in battle by the enemies they along with their enemies were killed. ṃany other mighty warriors.

Sloka 5

prakhyātabalavīryeṇa putreṇendrajitā mama || 6-71-5
tau bhrātarau tadā baddhau ghorairdattavaraiḥ śaraiḥ |

5. tadā = that day; mama patreṇa indrajitā = by Indrajit; my son; prakhyāta bala vīryeṇa = having renowned strength and prowess; tau = those two; bhrātrau = brothers; baddhau = were bound; ghoraiḥ sharaiḥ = by terrific arrows; dattavaraiḥ = on which boons had been conferred.

"That day, Indrajit, my son, whose strength and prowess are renowned, bound both Rama and Lakshmana, the brothers with terrific arrows, on which boons had been conferred."

Sloka 6

yanna śakyam suraiḥ sarvairasurairvā mahābalaiḥ || 6-71-6
moktum tad bandhanam ghoraṃ yakṣagandharvapannagaiḥ |
tanna jāne prabhāvairvā māyayā mohanena vā || 6-71-7
śarabandhādvimuktau tau bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |

6; 7. tat = that; bandhanam = bondage; yat = which; na shakyam = is not possible; moktum = to be released; sarvaiḥ suraiḥ = by all the celestials; mahābalaiḥ asurairvā = or by the mighty demons; yakṣha gandharva pannagaiḥ = or by Yakshas the supernatural beings or Gandharvas the celestials musicians or Pannagas the serpent-demons; ghoram = was terrific; rāmalakṣhmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; tau bhrātarau = two brothers; vimuktau = were released; sharabandhāt = from that tie of arrows; prabhāvaiḥ vā = either by their power; māyayā = or by sorcery; mohanenavā = or by stupor; na jāne = I do not know; tat = that.

"That terrific bind of arrow cannot be released by any celestial or a mighty demon or by Yakshas the supernatural beings or Gandharvas the celestial musicians or Pannagas the serpent-demons. Rama and Lakshmana, the two brothers were released from that tie of arrows either by their power or by their sorcery or by their marvel I do not know that."

Sloka 8

ye yodhā nirgatāh śūrā rākṣasā mama śāsanāt 6-71-8
te sarve nihatā yuddhe vānaraiḥ sumahābalaiḥ |

8. ye = which; shūrāḥ = valiant; yodhāḥ = warriors; rākṣhāḥ = of demons; mama shāsanāt = at my command; nirgatāḥ = have set out; yuddhe = for battle; sarve = all of them; nihatāḥ = were killed; sumahābalaiḥ = by highly mighty; vānaraiḥ = monkeys.

"All of those valiant demon-warriors set out for battle, at my command, were killed by those exceptionally mighty monkeys."

Sloka 9

taṃ na paśyāmyaham yuddhe yo.dya rāmaṃ salakṣmaṇam 6-71-9
nāśayetsabalam vīraṃ sasugrīvavibhīṣaṇam |

9. aham = I; na pashyāmi = do not find; tam = such a person; yaḥ nāshayet = who can destroy; vīram = the valiant Rama; sa lakṣhmaṇam = along with Lakshmana; sasugrīva vibhīṣhaṇam = ṣugreeva and Vibhishana; sabalam = accompanied by their army; yuddhe = in battle; adya = now.

"I do not find any demon, who can destroy the valiant Rama along with Lakshmana, Sugreeva and Vibhishana accompanied by their army in battle now."

Sloka 10

aho subalavān rāmo mahadastrabalam ca vai || 6-71-10
yasya vikramamāsādya rākṣasā nidhanam gatāḥ |

10. yasya = by which Rama's; vikramam = valour; rākṣhasāḥ = the demons; āsādya nidhanam gatāḥ = reached past destruction; rāmaḥ = (that) Rama; aho = alas!; subalavān = has a great strength; astra balamcha mahatvai = and indeed has an arrow of great power too.

"By Rama's valour, the demons were destroyed. Alas! How great is Rama's strength! How great is the power of his arrow!"

Sloka 11

apramatteśca sarvatrā gulmai rakṣyā purī tviyam || 6-71-11
aśokavanikā caiva yatra sītābhirakṣyate |

11. gulmaiḥ = by some regiments of army; iyam = this city; ashokaianikāchaiva = and even Ashoka garden; yatra = where; sītā = ṣeetha; abhirakṣhyate = is being guarded; rakṣhyātu = have to be protected; apramattaiḥ = vigilantly; sarvatra = on all sides.

"Some regiments of the army have to protect this City and even Ashoka garden where Seetha is being guarded, vigilantly on all sides."

Sloka 12

niṣkramo vā praveśo vā jñātvyaḥ sarvadaiva naḥ || 6-71-12
yatra yatra bhavedgulmastatra tatra punaḥ punaḥ |

12. Jṇātavyaḥ = It should be known; naḥ = to us; yatra yatra = wherever; gulmaḥ = a regiment of army; bhavet = is there; tatra tva = in that and that place (where); niṣhkramovā = persons are going out; praveshovā = and where persons are entering; sarvadaiva = at all times; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again.

"We should know the locations of army-regiments and also the places where persons are exiting and entering at all times, again and again.

Sloka 13

sarvataścāpi tiṣṭhadhvam svaiḥ svaiḥ parivṛtā balaiḥ || 6-71-13
draṣṭavyam ca padam teṣām vānarāṇāṃ niśācarāḥ |

13. nishācharāḥ = O demons!; tiṣhṭhadhvam = stay; sarvataḥ = on all sides; parivṛitāḥ = surrounded by; svaiḥ svaiḥ balaiḥ = by your respective armies; padam teṣhām vānarāṇām = the position of those monkeys; draṣhṭavyam cha = is to be watched.

"O demons! Stay on all sides with your respective armies. The various positioning of those monkeys are to be watched."

Sloka 14

pradoṣe vārdharātre vā pratyūṣe vāpi sarvaśaḥ || 6-71-14
nāvajñā tatra kartavyā vānareṣu kadācana |
dviṣatāṃ balamudyuktamāpatatkiṃ sthitaṃ tathā || 6-71-15

14; 15. tatra vānareṣhu = in the case of monkeys; avaJṇā na kartavyā = disrespect should not be shown; kadāchana = at any time; sarvashaḥ = in any way; pradoṣhevā = either at evening time; ardharātrevā = or at midnight; pratyūṣhevā = or at dawn; (You have to watch whether); dviṣhatām balam = the army of adversaries; udyuktam = is ready for war; āpatat = or appearing suddenly; tathā = and ; sthitam = remaining.

"Indifference should not be shown to monkeys in any way at any time, either at evening or at midnight or at dawn. You have watch whether the army of adversaries is fervently active or advancing or staying where it was."

Sloka 16

tataste rākṣasāh sarve śrutvā laṅkādhipasya tat |
vacanaṃ sarvamātiṣṭhan yathāvattu mahābalāḥ || 6-71-16

16. shrutvā = hearing; tat vachanam = those words; laṇ^kādhipasya = of Ravana; sarve = all; te mahābalāḥ = those mighty; rākṣhasāḥ = demons; tataḥ = then; yathāvattu = precisely; ātiṣhṭhan = stood by; sarvam = all that.

Hearing those words of Ravana, all the mighty demons then precisely carried it out forthwith in its totality.

Sloka 17

sa tān sarvān hi saṃdiśya rāvaṇo rākṣasādhipaḥ |
manyuśalyam vahan dīnaḥ praviveśa svamālayam || 6-71-17

17. samdishya = instructing; tān sarvān = all of them; saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; rākṣhasādhipaḥ = the king of demons; vahan manyushalyam = suffering from prickly sorrow; dīnaḥ = and depression; pravivesha = entered; svam ālayam = his house.

Having thus instructed all of them, Ravana the king of demons, suffering from prickly sorrow and depression, penetrated deeply into his palace.

Sloka 18

tataḥ sa saṃdīpitakopapahni |
rniśācarāṇāmadhipo mahābalaḥ |
tadeva putravyasanam vicintayan |
muhurmuhuścaiva tadā vyaniḥśvasat || 6-71-18

18. samdīpita kopa vahniḥ = blazed as he was with a fire of anger; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; adhipaḥ = lord; nishācharāṇām = of demons; tataḥ = then; vichintayan = thinking; tat puravyasanam eva = of that loss of his son; tadā = then muhuḥ muhuḥ = again and again; vyaniḥshvasat = sighing.

Blazed as he was with a fire of anger, Ravana the mighty lord of demons, then remained broading about the loss of his son (Atikaya) and also sighing again and again.
