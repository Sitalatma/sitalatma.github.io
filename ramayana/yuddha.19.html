Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 19
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Descending from the sky, Vibhishana seeks refuge at the feet of Rama. On Rama's enquiry, he explains about the strength of Ravana. Rama promises to kill Ravana and anoints Vibhishana. Rama sits on the sea -shore, seeking the help of the ocean god to yield a passage for his army to pass through and reach the city of Lanka.

Sloka 1

rāghavena abhaye datte samnato rāvaṇa anujaḥ |
vibhīṣaṇo mahāprājño bhūmim samavalokayat || 6-19-1

1. abhaye = (while); assurance of protection; datte = having given; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; mahāprāG^yaḥ = the highly sagacious; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; rāvaṇānuje = the brother of Ravana; samnataḥ = bent down; samavalokayat = and glanced towards; bhūmim = the earth.

While the assurance of protection having been given to Rama, the highly sagacious Vibhishana the brother of Ravana bent down and glanced towards the earth.

Sloka 2

khāt papāta avanim hṛṣṭo bhaktair anucaraiḥ saha |
sa tu rāmasya dharma ātmā nipapāta vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-19-2

2. saḥ = that; dharmātmā = virtuous; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; papāta = descended; hṛishhṭaḥ = joyously; khāt = from the sky; avanim = to the ground; bhaktaiḥ anucharaiḥ saha = along with his faithful companions; nipapāta = and alighted; rāmasya = near Rama.

The virtuous Vibhishana descended joyously from the sky to the ground along with his faithful companions and alighted in the vicinity of Rama.

Sloka 3

pādayoḥ śaraṇa anveṣī caturbhiḥ saha rākṣasaiḥ |
abravīc ca tadā rāmam vākyam tatra vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-19-3
dharma yuktam ca yuktam ca sāmpratam sampraharṣaṇam |

3. atha = thereafter; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; chaturbhiḥ rākshhasaiḥ saha = along with four demons; nipapāta = fell prostrate; abravichcha = and spoke; rāmam prati = to Rama; vākyam = the following words; yuktamcha = which were right; sāpratam = suitable; sampraharshhaṇam = and joyful.

Thereafter, Vibhishana along with the four demons fell prostrate and spoke to Rama the following words which were equitable suitable and delightful.

Sloka 4

anujo rāvaṇasya aham tena ca asmi avamānitaḥ || 6-19-4
bhavantam sarva bhūtānām śaraṇyam śaraṇam gataḥ |

4. aham = I; anujaḥ = an younger brother; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; avamāniteshcha = and was humiliated; tena = by him; sharaṇam gataḥ = I sought refuge; bhavantam = in you; sharaṇyam = who are the refuge; sarvabhūtānām = for all the beings.

"I am the younger brother of Ravana and was humiliated by him. I sought refuge in you, who are the refuge for all the beings."

Sloka 5

parityaktā mayā laṃkā mitrāṇi ca dhanāni ca || 6-19-5
bhavad gatam hi me rājyam ca jīvitam ca sukhāni ca |

5. laṇkā = Lanka; parityakta = who was abandoned; mayā = by me; mitrāṇi = the friends; dhanānicha = and the possessions too; me = my; rājyam = kingdom; bhavadgatamhi = is indeed; at your disposal; jīvitam = my life; sukhānicha = and happiness too.

"Abandoning Lanka, friends and possessions, I place my kingdom, life and happiness at your disposal."

Sloka 6

tasya tadvacanam śrutvā rāmo vacanamabravīt || 6-19-6
vacasā sāntvayitvainam locanābhyām pibanniva |
ākhyāhi mama tatvena rākṣasānām balābalam || 6-19-7

6; 7. shrutvā = hearing; tatvachanam = those words; tasya = of Vibhishana; rāmaḥ = Rama; sāntvayitvā = in soothing tone; pibanniva = while soothing to refresh; lochanābhyām = with his eyes; abravīt = spoke; vachanam = words; ākhyāhi = ṭell; mama = me; tattvena = really; balābalam = the strengths and weaknesses; rākshhasānām = of the demons."

Hearing those words of Vibhishana, Rama in a soothing tone and with a refreshing look in his eyes, spoke these words: "Tell me really the strengths and weaknesses of the demons."

Sloka 8

evamuktam tadā rakṣo rāmeṇākliṣṭakarmaṇā |
rāvaṇasya balam sarvamākhyātumupacakrame || 6-19-8

8. evam = thus; uktam = asked; tadā = then; rāmeṇa = by Rama; aklishhṭakarmaṇā = who was unwearied in action; rakshhaḥ = Vibhishana; upachakrame = started; ākhyātum = telling; uktam = a word; sarvam = about the entire; balam = strength; rāvaṇasaya = of Ravana.

Thus asked then by Rama who was unwearied in action, Vibhishana started telling about the detailed strength of Ravana.

Sloka 9

avadhyaḥ sarvabhūtānām gandharvoragapakṣiṇām |
rājaputra daśagrīvo varadānātsvayambhuvaḥ || 6-19-9

9. rājaputra = O; prince! Varadānāt = because of a special boon given; svayambhuvaḥ = by Brahma (the lord of celestials); dashagrīvaḥ = Ravana; avadhyaḥ = cannot be killed; gandharvoragapakshhiṇām = by Gandharvas; serpents and birds.

"O, prince! Because of a special boon given by Brahma (lord of creation), Ravana can not be killed by all beings, Gandharvas (celestial musicians), serpents, and birds."

Sloka 10

rāvaṇānantaro bhrātā mama jyeṣṭhaśca vīryavān |
kumbhakarṇo mahātejāḥ śakrapratibalo yudhi || 6-19-10

10. kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; bhrātā = a brother; rāvaṇānantaraḥ = born after Ravana; mama jyeshhṭhashcha = and my elder brother; vīryavān = who is valiant; mahātejā = and highly powerful; shakrapratibalaḥ = has a strength to fight against Indra (the lord of celestials); Yuddhi = in battle.

Kumbhakarna, my elder brother born after Ravana, who is valiant and highly powerful, has enough strength to fight against Indra (Lord of celestials) in battle.

Sloka 11

rāma senāpatistasya prahasto yadi te śrutaḥ |
kailāse yena sama maṇibhadraḥ parājitaḥ || 6-19-11

11. rāma = O; Rama; yena = by whom; maṇibhadraḥ = ṃanibhadra; parājitaḥ = was defeated; samare = in battle; kailāse = on the mountain of Kailasa; tasyasenāpatiḥ = that chief of army; prahastaḥ = Prahasta; shrutaḥyadi = is it mentioned; te = to you?

"O, Rama! You might have heard about Prahasta, his chief of army. He defeated ṃanibhadra in a battle on the mountain of Kailasa."

Sloka 12

baddagodhāṅgulitraśca avadhyakavaco yudhi |
dhanurādāya yastiṣṭhannadṛśyo bhavatīndrajit || 6-19-12

12. baddha jodhāṇgulitrashcha = when furnished with gloves made of the skin of an Iguana (to prevent injury from the bow string); avadhyakavachaḥ = and when clad in armor that no arrow can pierce; yaḥ = that; Indrajit = Indrajit; ādāya = wielding; dhanuḥ = his bow; bhavati = becomes; adhṛishyaḥ = invisible; tishhṭhan = standing; yudhi = in battle field.

"When furnished with gloves made of the skin of Iguana (to prevent injury from the bowstring) and when clad in armor that no arrow can pierce, that Indrajit (son of Ravana) standing in battle wielding a bow, becomes invisible."

Sloka 13

samgrāme sumahadvyūhe tarpayitvā hutāśanam |
antardhānagataḥ śrīmānindrajiddhanti rāghava || 6-19-13

13. rāghava = O; Rama! ṣhrīmān = the glorious; Indrajit = Indrajit; tarpayitvā = having propitiated; hutāshanam = the God of Fire; hanti = strikes; (the enemy) antardhānegataḥ = while remaining invisible; sangrāme = in the battle field; sumahadvyūhe = with a huge battle array (on both sides)

"O, Rama! The glorious Indrajit, having propitiated the God of Fire, strikes his enemy while remaining invisible in the battle field with a huge battle array (on both sides)"

Sloka 14

mahodaramahāpārsvau rākṣasaścāpyakampanaḥ |
avīkapāstu tapyaite lokapālasamā yudhi || 6-19-14

14. mahodara = ṃahodara; mahāpārshvā = and ṃahaparshva; rākshhasashcha = along with a demon; akampanaḥ = called Akampana; lokapālasamāḥ = who are equal to the guardians of the world; yudhi = in battle; ete = they are; tasya = his; anīkapāḥ = commanders of army.

"ṃahodara and ṃahaparsva along with a demon called Akampana who are equal to the guardians of the world in battle - they are the commanders of his army."

Sloka 15

daśakoṭisahasrāṇi rakṣasām kāmarūpiṇām |
māṃsaśoṇitabhakṣyāṇām laṅkāpuranivāsinām || 6-19-15

15. dashakoti sahasrāṇi = ten thousand crores; rakshhasām = of demons; laṇkāpuranivāsinām = who reside in the city of Lanka; kāmarūpiṇām = can assume any form at will ; māmsa shoṇita bhakshhyāṇām = and eat flesh and blood as their staple food.

"In the city of Lanka, are residing ten thousand crore demons, who can assume any form at will and who consume flesh and blood as their staple food"

Sloka 16

sa taistu sahito rājā lokapālānayodhayat |
saha devaistu te bhagnā rāvaṇena durātmanā || 6-19-16

16. saḥ = that; rājā = king Ravana; saha = along with; tataiḥ = those demons; ayodhayat = made war; lokapālān = against the guardians of the earth; devaiḥsaha = along with celestials; bhagnāḥ = were defeated; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; durātmanā = the wicked one.

"King Ravana along with those demons made war against the guardians of the earth. Those guardians of the earth along with the celestials were defeated by the wicked Ravana."

Sloka 17

vibhīṣaṇasya tu vacastachrutvā raghusattamaḥ |
anvīkṣya manasā sarvamidam vacanamabravīt || 6-19-17

17. shrutvā = hearing; tat = those; vachaḥ = words; vibhīshhaṇasya = of Vibhishana; raghusattamaḥ = Rama; anvīkshhya = examined; sarvam = all; manasā = attentively; abravīt = and spoke; idam = those; vachanam = words.

Hearing the aforesaid words of Vibhishana, Rama scrutinized them all attentively and spoke the following words:

Sloka 18

yāni karmāpadānāni rāvaṇasya vibhīṣaṇa |
akhyātāni ca tattvena hyavagaccāmi tānyaham || 6-19-18

18. vibhīshhaṇa = O;Vibhishana; aham = I; avagachchhāmi hi = indeed know; tāni = those; karmāpadāni = feats; rāvanasya = of Ravana; yāni = which; ākhyātāmi = were told; tattena = really (by you).

"O, Vibhishana! I indeed know those feats standing to the credit of Ravana, which were told by you, as true."

Sloka 19

aham hatvā daśagrīvam saprahastam sahātmajam |
rājānam tvām kariṣyāmi satyametacchṛṇotu me || 6-19-19

19. hatvā = after killing; dashagrīvam = Ravana; saprahastam; along with Prahasta; sahātmajam = and his son(Indrajit); karishhyāmi = I will make; tvām = you; rājānam = the king; shruṇotu = here; etat = this; satyam = truth; me = from me.

"After killing Ravana along with Prahasta and his son (Indrajit), I will make you the king. Hear this truth from me."

Sloka 20

rasātalam vā praviśetpātālam vāpi rāvaṇaḥ |
pitāmahasakāśam vā na me jīvanvimokṣyate || 6-19-20

20. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; pravishet = may well enter ; rasātalam = Rasātala; pātālamvāpi = or even Pātāla; pitāmaha sakāsham vā = or seek the presence of Brahma; na vimokshhyate = he will not be left; jīvan = alive; me = by me.

"Ravana may well plunge into Rasātala (the penultimate subterranean region) or even Pātāla (the nethermost subterranean region) or seek the presence of Brahma and he will not be left alive by me."

Sloka 21

ahatvā rāvaṇam samkhye saputrajanabāndhavam |
ayodhyām na pravekṣyāmi tribhistairbhratṛbhiḥ śapeḥ || 6-19-21

21. na pravekshhyāmi = I will not enter; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; ahatvā = without killing; rāvaṇām = Ravana; saputra jana bāndhavam = together with his sons; companions and relatives; samkhye = in battle; shape = I swear; taiḥ = on those; tribhiḥ = three; bhrātṛibhiḥ = brothers.

"I will not enter Ayodhya without killing Ravana together with his sons, companions and relatives in battle. I take oath on my three brothers."

Sloka 22

śrutvā tu vacanam tasya rāmasyākliṣṭakarmaṇaḥ |
śirasā vandya dharmātmā vaktumeva pracakrame || 6-19-22

22. shrutvā = hearing; vachanam = the words; tasya rāmasya = of that Rama; aklishhṭakarmaṇaḥ = who was unwearied in action; dharmātmā = Vibhishana; the right minded; āvandya = saluted; shivasā = (by bowing) his mind; prachakrama = and started; vaktumeva = to tell; (the following words):

Hearing the words of that Rama, who was unwearied in action, the right minded Vibhishana saluted him by bowing his head and started telling as follows:

Sloka 23

rākṣasānām vadhe sāhyam laṃkāyāḥ ca pradharṣaṇe |
kariṣyāmi yathā prāṇam pravekṣyāmi ca vāhinīm || 6-19-23

23. karishhyāmi = I will do; sāhyam = help; vadhe = in killing; rākshhasānām = the demons; pradharshhane = and in the attack; laṇkāyāḥ = against Lanka; yathāprāṇam = according to my strength; pravekshhyāmi = I will also enter; vāhinīm = the army.

"I will extend my help in killing the demons and in attacking the city of Lanka. According to my strength, I will also penetrate into the army of the adversary to fight."

Sloka 24

iti bruvāṇam rāmas tu pariṣvajya vibhīṣaṇam |
abravīl lakṣmaṇam prītaḥ samudrāj jalam ānaya || 6-19-24

24. rāmastu = Rama on his part; prītaḥ = duly delighted; parishhvaṇga = embraced; vibhīshhaṇam = Vibhishana; brūvāṇam = speaking; iti = as aforesaid; abravīt = and spoke; lakshmaṇam = to Lakshmana (as follows); "ānaya = Bring; jalam = water; samudrāt = from the sea"

Rama on his part, duly delighted, embraced Vibhishana who was speaking as aforesaid and spoke to Lakshmana as follows: "Bring some water from the ocean."

Sloka 25

tena ca imam mahāprājñam abhiṣinca vibhīṣaṇam |
rājānam rakṣasām kṣipram prasanne mayi mānada || 6-19-25

25. mānada = O; Lakshmana; who gives respect to others! Kshhipram = quickly; abhishhiṇcha = consecrate; tena = with that water; imam = this; vibhīshhaṇam = who is greatly intelligent; rājānam = as a king; rakshhasām = of demons; mayi = I am; prasanne = pleased.

"O, Lakshmana who bestows honor on others! Quickly consecrate as a king of demons this greatly intelligent Vibhishana with that water, so that I am pleased."

Sloka 26

evam uktas tu saumitrir abhyaṣincad vibhīṣaṇam |
madhye vānara mukhyānām rājānam rāma śāsanāt || 6-19-26

26. uktvaḥ = having been spoken; evam = thus ; soumitriḥ = Lakshmana; abhishhiṇchat = as per the royal instructions; madhye = amidst; vānara mukhyānām = the leaders of monkeys.

Hearing Rama's words, Lakshmana consecrated Vibhishana as a king, as per the royal instructions, amidst the leaders of monkeys.

Sloka 27

tam prasādam tu rāmasya dṛṣṭvā sadyaḥ plavam gamāḥ |
pracukruśur mahānādān sādhu sādhv iti ca abruvan || 6-19-27

27. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tam = that; sadyaḥ = instant; prasādam = graciousness; rāmasya = of Rama; plavaṇgamāḥ = the monkeys; prachukrushuḥ = cried; abruvancha = proclaiming; sādhu sādhu = Excellent; excellent" mahātmanām = about the high soled Rama.

Perceiving that instant graciousness in Rama, the monkeys cried, proclaiming "excellent, excellent" about the high soled Rama.

Sloka 28

abravīc ca hanūmāmḥ ca sugrīvaḥ ca vibhīṣaṇam |
katham sāgaram akṣobhyam tarāma varuṇa ālayam || 6-19-28
sainyaiḥ parivṛtāḥ sarve vānarāṇām mahaujasām |

28. hanūmānshcha = ḥanuman; sugrīvevashcha = and ṣugriva; abravīt = spoke; vibhīshhaṇam = to Vibhishana; katham = how; tarena = can we cross; sāgaram = the ocean; akshhobhyam = which is imperturbable; varuṇālayam = the habitation of Varuna; the god of water; sarve = by all of us; parivṛite = surrounded; sainyaiḥ = with the army; vānarānam = of monkeys; mahoujasām = possessing a great splendor?

Hanuman and Sugreeva asked Vibhishana as follows: " How can we cross the imperturbable ocean, the habitation of Varuna, the god of water by all of us along with our army of monkeys possessing a great splendor?"

Sloka 29

upāyair abhigaccāmo yathā nada nadī patim || 6-19-29
tarāma tarasā sarve sasainyā varuṇa ālayam |

29. yathā = In what manner; tarena = shall we ; tarasā = swiftly; varuṇālayam = the sea; nadanadīpatim = the lord of streams and rivers; abhigachhāma = we will arrive; upāyaiḥ = at the strategy.

"In what manner shall we along with the army swiftly cross the sea, the lord of streams and rivers, and arrive at that strategy?"

Sloka 30

evam uktas tu dharmajñaḥ pratyuvāca vibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-19-30
samudram rāghavo rājā śaraṇam gantum arhati |

30. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; dharmātmā = the rightminded ; vibhīshaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; pratyuvācha = replied;as follows): rājā = the prince; rāghavaḥ = Rama; arhati = is apt; gantum = to obtain; sharaṇam = refuge; samudram = from the sea."

Hearing their words, the right minded Vibhishana replied as follows: "It is apt if prince Rama seeks ocean as his refuge"

Sloka 31

khānitaḥ sagareṇa ayam aprameyo mahāudadhiḥ || 6-19-31
kartum arhati rāmasya jñāteḥ kāryam mahāudadhiḥ |

31. ayam = this; mahodadhiḥ = great ocean; aprameyaḥ = the immesurable; khanitaḥ = was excavated; sagareṇa = by ṣagara; (hence; mahodabhiḥ = this great ocean; arhati = will be inclined; kartum = to do; kāryam = the act; rāmasya = for Rama; j^ṇāte = his kinsman.

"This immeasurable great ocean was excavated by Sagara*. Hence, this great ocean will be inclined to do the act for Rama, his kinsman".

▼

*Sagara was one of Rama's ancestors. His story is told in Balakanda.

Sloka 32

evam vibhīṣaṇena ukte rākṣasena vipaścitā || 6-19-32
ājagāmatha sugrīvo yatra rāmaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ |

32. atha = then; uktaḥ = after being spoken; evam = thus; rākshhasena = by the demon; vibhīshhaṇena = Vibhishana; vipashchitā = the learned; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugriva; ājagāma = came; yatra = where; rāmaḥ = Rama; salakshhmaṇaḥ = along with Lakshmana.

Hearing the aforesaid words of the learned demon Vibhishana, Sugreeva came to the place where Rama and Lakshmana were there.

Sloka 33

tataścākhyātumārebhe vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śubham || 6-19-33
sugrīvo vipulagrīvaḥ sāgarasyopaveśanam |

33. tataḥ = thereafter; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugriva; vipulagrīvaḥ = the long necked; ārebhe = startred; ākhyātum = to tell; shubham = the auspicious; vibhīshhaṇavachaḥ = words of Vibhishana; upaveshanam = advising Rama to approach; sāgarasya = the ocean (to allow him a passage).

The long necked Sugreeva then started to tell the auspicious words of Vibhishana, advising Rama to approach the ocean (to allow him a passage).

Sloka 34

prakṛtyā dharma śīlasya rāghavasya api arocata || 6-19-34
sa lakṣmaṇam mahātejāḥ sugrīvam ca hari īśvaram |
sat kriyā artham kriyā dakṣaḥ smita pūrvam uvāca ha || 6-19-35

34;35. asya rāmasyāpi = to even this Rama; prakṛityā = who by his nature; dharmashīlasya = is of pious attitude; ārochata = that advice was agreeable; mahātejāḥ = the highly splendorous; saḥ = rama; abhāshheta = spoke; smitapūrvam = smilingly; satkriyārtham = with an intention to honor Vibhishana words; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; sugrivamcha = and to ṣugriva; kriyādakshham = who was skilled in action; harīshvaram = and the ruler of monkeys.

That advice found favor with Rama who by nature is of pious attitude. Then that highly illustrious Rama replied to Sugreeva, the ruler of monkeys who was skillful in action and who was accompanied by Lakshmana. Full of respect for Vibhishana's words, he told him as also his brother Lakshmana smilingly as follows:

Sloka 36

vibhīṣaṇasya mantro ayam mama lakṣmaṇa rocate |
sugrīvaḥ paṇḍito nityam bhavān mantra vicakṣaṇaḥ || 6-19-36
ubhābhyām sampradhārya āryam rocate yat tad ucyatām |

36. lakshhmaṇa = O; Lakshmana; ayam mantraḥ = this thought; vibhīshhaṇasya = of Vibhishana; rochate = is agreeable; mama = to me; sugrivaḥ = ṣugriva; paṇditaḥ = is a learned person; bhavām = you; nityam = are ever; mantra vichakshhaṇaḥ = wise in your thoughts; artham = the matter; sampradhārya = be decided; ubhābhyām = by both of you; uchayatām = and tell; tat = that; yat = which; rochate = is agreeable.

"O, Lakshmana! This idea of Vibhishana is agreeable to me. Sugreeva is a learned person. You are ever wise in your thoughts. Both of you decide on the matter and inform whichever is agreeable to you."

Sloka 37

evam uktau tu tau vīrāv ubhau sugrīva lakṣmaṇau || 6-19-37
samudācāra samyuktam idam vacanam ūcatuḥ |

37. tataḥ = then; uktou = having been spoken; evam = thus; ubhou sugrīva lakshhmaṇah = both ṣugriva and Lakshmana; vīrou = the valiant; ūchutuḥ = spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words; samudāchārasamyuktam = with proper courtesy.

Hearing Rama's words, Sugreeva and Lakshmana both, valiant as they were, spoke with proper courtesy, the following words: .

Sloka 38

kim artham no nara vyāghra na rociṣyati rāghava || 6-19-38
vibhīṣaṇena yat tu uktam asmin kāle sukha āvaham |

38. rāghava = O; Rama; naravyāghra = the tiger among men! ṇa kimartham = why not; vibhīshhaṇena = Vibhishana's; uktam = words; sukhāvaham = which are soothing; asmin = at this; kale = time; rochishhyati = be agreeable; nou = to us?

"O, Rama the tiger among men! Why not Vibhishana's words, which are soothing at this time of crisis be agreeable also to us?"

Sloka 39

abaddhvā sāgare setum ghore asmin varuṇa ālaye || 6-19-39
laṃkā na āsāditum śakyā sa indrair api sura asuraiḥ |

39. abaddhvā = without constructing; setum = a bridge; asmin = sāgare ghore = across the fiery ocean; varuṇālaye = which is an abode of Lord Varuna; surāsurairapi = even the celestials and demons; na shakyā = can not; āsāditum = reach; laṇkā = the city of Lanka.

"Without constructing a bridge across this fiery ocean, which is an abode of Lord Varuna, even the celestials and demons along with Indra cannot reach the city of Lanka."

Sloka 40

vibhīṣaṇasya śūrasya yathā artham kriyatām vacaḥ || 6-19-40
alam kāla atyayam kṛtvā samudro ayam niyujyatām |
yathā kālātyayam kṛtvā sāgarāya niyujyatām || 6-19-41

40;41. shūrasya = the valiant; vbhīshhaṇāsya = Vibhishana's; vachaḥ = advice; kriyatām = be followed; yathārtham = suitably; alam = It is enough; kṛitvā = of having done; kālātyayam = a waste of time; niyujyatāya sāgarāya = Let the ocean be commanded; yathā = how; gachchhāma = to go; sainyena = with the army; purīm = to the city; rāvaṇapālitām = being ruled by Ravana.

"Let the valiant Vibhishana's words be followed suitably. It is enough that we have already wasted our time. Let the ocean be commanded to yield a passage for the army to reach the city being ruled by Ravana."

Sloka 42

evam uktaḥ kuśa āstīrṇe tīre nada nadī pateḥ |
samviveśa tadā rāmo vedyām iva huta aśanaḥ || 6-19-42

42. evam = thus; uktaḥ = having been spoken; rāmaḥ = Rama; tadā = then; samvivesha = sat; tire = on the shore; nadanadīpate = of the ocean; kushāstīrṇe = covered with blades of kusha grass; vedyāmiva = as ascending an altar; hutāshanaḥ = by the god fire.

Having been thus exhorted, Rama then sat on the shore of the ocean covered with blades of Kusha grass, as the God of Fire ascends the altar.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāmḍe ekonaviṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 19th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
