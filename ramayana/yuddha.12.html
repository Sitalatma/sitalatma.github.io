Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

Introduction

Ravana instructs Prahasta, chief of his army to defend the city of Lanka carefully. Prahasta positioned the army for defense accordingly at suitable places both inside and outside the city. Ravana then narrates how he brought beautiful Seetha to Lanka from Dandaka forest, because of his growing infatuation for her. Ravana asks his companions and relatives gathered there, to suggest ways and means to kill Rama and Lakshmana. Then, Kumbhakarna, Ravana's brother first accuses Ravana of his unworthy act but assures him that he would neutralize that dishonour by killing the enemies.

Sloka 1

sa tām pariṣadam kṛtsnām samīkṣya samitimjayaḥ |
prabodhayāmāsa tadā prahastam vāhinīpatim || 6-12-1

1. tadā = then; saḥ = that Ravana; samitimjayaḥ = eminent in an assembly; dṛiṣṭvā = having seen; kṛitsnām = all; tām pariṣadam = that gathering; tadā = at that time; prabodhayāmāsa = instructed; prahastam = Prahasta; vāhinīpatim = the commander of the army (as follows):

Then, Ravana an eminent person in the assembly, having seen all that gathering, instructed Prahasta the chief of his army (as follows):

Sloka 2

senāpate yathā te syuḥ kṛtavidyāścaturvidhāḥ |
yodhā nagararakṣāyām tathā vyādeṣṭumarhasi || 6-12-2

2. senapate = "O; commander!; te = (Let) those; yodhāḥ = warriors; kṛitavidyāḥ = well-trained; chaturvidhāḥ = in four ways; yathā = how; syuḥ = (they) stand; nagararakṣāyām = in defence of the city; tathā = in that way' arhasi = you are allowed; vyādeṣṭum = to command.

"O, commander! You are allowed to issue an order to see that well-trained warriors belonging to the four divisions of the army (viz.cavalry, infantry, chariots and elephants) are entrusted with the defense of the city."

Sloka 3

sa prahastaḥ pratītātmā cikīrṣan rājaśāsanam |
vinikṣipad balam sarvam bahirantaśca mandire || 6-12-3

3. saḥ = that; prahastaḥ = Prahasta; pratītātmā = steadfast in his mind; chikīrṣan = with a desire to follow; rājashāsanam = king's order; vinikṣipat = stationed; sarvam = the whole; balam = army; bahiḥ = outside; antashca = and inside; mandire = the city.

Prahasta, resolute in his mind to follow the king's orders stationed the whole army both outside as well as inside the city.

Sloka 4

tato vinikṣipya balam sarvam nagaraguptaye |
prahastaḥ pramukhe rājño niṣasāda jagāda ca || 6-12-4

4. tataḥ = thereafter; prahastaḥ = Prahasta; vinikṣipya = keeping; sarvam = the whole; balam = army; nagaraguptaye = in defence of the city; niṣasāda = sat; pramukhe = in front; rāṇ^aḥ = of the king; jagāda = and spoke (as follows).

Thereafter, Prahasta after keeping the whole army in defence of the city, sat in front of the king and spoke (as follows):

Sloka 5

vihitam bahirantaśca balam balavatastava |
kuruṣvāvimanāḥ kṣipram yadabhipretamasti te || 6-12-5

5. tava = your; balam = army; balavataḥ = as strong (as you are); vihitam = has been stationed; bahiḥ = outside; antashca = and inside; kuruṣva = do; kṣipram = quickly; yat = what; asti = is; abhipretam = preference; te = to you; avimanāḥ = with an undistracted mind."

Your army, as strong as you are, has been positioned both outside and inside the city. Pray do quickly, with an undistracted mind, whatever is preferable to you."

Sloka 6

prahastasya vacaḥ śrutvā rājā rājyahitaiṣiṇaḥ |
sukhepsuḥ suhṛdām madhye vyājahāra sa rāvaṇaḥ || 6-6-12

6. saḥ = that; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rājā = the king; shrutvā = hearing; vachaḥ = the words; prahastasya = of Prahasta; rājya hitaishhiṇaḥ = who wished the welfare of the kingdom; sukhepsuḥ = being desirous of obtaining happiness; vyājahara = spoke (as follows); madhye = in the middle; suhṛidām = of his friends.

Hearing the words of Prahasta, who wished the welfare of the kingdom, king Ravana, with a desire to obtain happiness, spoke in the desire midst of his friends as follows:

Sloka 7

priyāpriye sukha duḥkham lābhālābhe sitāhite |
dharmakāmārthakṛccreṣu yūyamārhatha veditum || 6-12-7

7. yūyam = (when) you; dharma; kāmārthkṛichchhreshhe = are in difficult situations regarding virtue pleasure and wealth; arhatha = you are able; veditum = to have a correct notion; riyāpriye = of pleasant and unpleasant things; sukham duḥkham = of happiness and sorrow; lābhālābhe = of gain and detriment; hitāhite = and of advantages and disadvantages of things.

When you are in difficult situations regarding virtue pleasure and wealth, you are able to have a correct notion of pleasant and unpleasant things, happiness and sorrow, gain and detriment as well as advantages and disadvantages."

Sloka 8

sarvakṛtyāni yuṣmābhiḥ samārabdhani sarvadā |
mantrakarmaniyuktāni na jātu viphalāni me || 6-12-8

8. me = ṃy; sarvakṛityāni = entire; tasks; sarvadā = always; samarambhāni = undertaken; yuṣmābhiḥ = by you; mantrakarmaniyuktāni = with an act of attention; na jātu = were never; viphalāni = in vain."

"All of my works undertaken by you with an act of attention, were never in vain."

Sloka 9

sasomagrahanakṣatrairmarudbhiriva vāsavaḥ |
bhavadbhirahamatyartham vṛtaḥ śriyamavāpnuyām || 6-12-9

9. aham = I; avapnuyām = will obtain; atyartham = maximum; shriyam = prosperity; vṛitaḥ = while abiding; bhavadbhiḥ = with you; vāsavaḥ iva = as Inidra; (obtains prosperity); sa somagrahanakshhatraiḥ = while abdiding with moon; planets and stars; marudbhiḥ = as well as celestials."

"I will obtain maximum prosperity, while abiding with you, as Indra obtains properity while abiding with moon, planets, stars and celestials."

Sloka 10

aham tu khalu sarvanvaḥ samarthayutumudyataḥ |
kumabhakarṇasya tu svapnānnemamarthamacodayam || 6-12-10

10. aham = I; udyataḥ = an intent on; samarthayitum = reminding; sarvān = all; vaḥ = of you; kumbhakarṇasya tu = but to Kumbhakarna; svapnāt = beacuse of his sleep; na achodayam = I could not inform; imam = this; artham = matter.

"I am intent on reminding all of you again. But I could not inform this matter earlier to Kumbhakarna because of his sleep."

Sloka 11

ayam hi suptaḥ ṣṇmāsān kumbhakarṇo mahābalaḥ |
sarvaśastrabhṛtam mukhyaḥ sa idānīm samutthitaḥ || 6-12-11

11. ayam = this; mahābalaḥ = very strong kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; mukhyaḥ = the chief; sarvashastrabhṛitām = among the entire wielders of weapons; suptaḥ hi = was indeed sleeping; shhaṭmāsāḥ = for the last six months; saḥ = ḥe; samutthitaḥ = woke up; idānīm = now.

"This most powerful Kumbhakarna, the chief among all the wielders of weapons, was indeed sleeping for the last six months and now, he woke up."

Sloka 12

iyam ca daṇḍakāradrāmasya mahiṣī priyā |
rakṣobhiścaritoddeśādānītā janakātmajā || 6-12-12

12. iyam = this; janakātmajā = ṣeetha; priyā = the beloved; mahiṣī = wife; rāmasya = of Rama; ānītā = was brought; daṇDakāraṇyāt = from the forest of Dandaka; charitoddeshāt = the place randered; rakṣobhiḥ = by demons."

"Seetha the beloved wife of Rama was brought from the forest of Dandaka, the place frequented by demons."

Sloka 13

sā me na śayyāmāroḍhumiccatyalasagāminī |
triṣulokeṣu cānyā me na sītāsadṛśī matā || 6-12-13

13. sā = ṣhe; alasāgāminī = who walks slowly; na ichchhati = does not like; āroDhum = to reach; me = my; shayyam = bed; namatā = It is not acceptable; me = to me; anyā = any other woman; sītā sadṛishī = similar to ṣeetha; triṣu = in the three; lokeṣu = worlds.

Seetha of languid pace, does not like to reach my bed. I do not imagine any other woman similar to Seetha existing in the three worlds."

Sloka 14

tanumadhyā pṛthuśroṇī śaradindunibhānanā |
hemabimbanibhā saumyāmāyeva mayanirmitā || 6-12-14

14. tanumadhyā = (ṣhe has) a slim waist; pṛithy shroṇī = well-developed hips; sharadindunibhānanā = her face similar to antumnal moon; hema bimbanibhā = looking like an image of gold; saumyā = placid; māyeva = like an illusory image; mayanirmitā = made by ṃaya.

"She has a slim waist, well-developed hips, and a face similar to an autumnal moon. Looking like an idol of gold and being placid She appears like an illusory image created by ṃaya.

ṃaya: Architect of demons, also versed in magic, astronomy and military science.

Sloka 15

sulohitatalau ślakkṣaṇau caraṇau supratiṣṭhatau |
dṛṣṭvā tāmranakhau tasyā dīpyate me śarīrajaḥ || 6-12-15

15. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tasyāḥ = ḥer; charoṇau = feet; sulohitatalau = with a little reddish soles; shlakṣṇau = smooth; supratiṣṭhitau = evenly resting on the ground; tāmranakhau = with lightly reddish nails; me = my; sharīrajaḥ = lust; dīpyate = is kindled.

"Seeing Her feet with rose-coloured soles, smooth and evenly resting on ground with the rosy soles, my lust is kindled."

Sloka 16

hutāgnirarciḥsamkāśāmenām saurīmiva prabhām |
unnasam vimalam valgu vadanam cārulocanam || 6-12-16
paśyaṃstadavaśastasyāḥ kāmasya vaśameyivān |

16. pashyan = seeing; tasyāḥ = ḥer; tat = that; vadanam = countenance; hutāgnirarchiḥ sankāsham = similar to the flame of a sacrificial fire; prabhāmiva = (and) looking like radiance; saurīm = of the sun; enām = with her; unnasam = prominent nose; chārulochanam = her pretty eyes; vimalam = which are clear; valgu = and handsome; avasham = not being in my fire will; eyivān = I obtained; vasham = the will; kāmesya = of passion."

"Seeing her countenance similar to the flame of a sacrificial fire and looking like the radiance of the sun, with her prominent nose and the pretty clear and handsome eyes, I became unrestrained, having submitted to the will of my passion."

Sloka 17

krodhaharṣasamānena durvarṇakaraṇena ca || 6-12-17
śokasamtāpanityena kāmena kaluṣīkṛtaḥ |

17. kalushhīkṛitaḥ = I have been polluted; kāmena = by lust; krodha harshhasamānena = equally in anger and pleasure; durvarṇakaraṇenacha = making me pale; shokasantāpanityena = and is ever present in grief and anguish."

"I have been polluted by lust and equally by anger and pleasure thus making me pale and I have been ever since in grief and anguish."

Sloka 18

sā tu samvatsaram kālam māmayācata bhāminī || 6-12-18
pratīkṣamāṇā bhartāram rāmamāyatalocanā |
tanmayā cārunetrāyāḥ pratijñātam vacaḥ śubham || 6-12-19
śrānto.aham satatam kāmādyāto haya ivādhvani |

18;19. pratīkṣamāṇā = looking forward; rāmam = for Rama; bhartāram = her husband; sā = that; āyatalochanā = large-eyed; bhāminī = ṣeetha; ayāchata = requested; mām = me; samvatsaram kālam = year's time; tat = those; shubham = auspicious; vachaḥ = words; chāran netrāyāḥ = of the charmingly eyed ṣeetha; pratijJṇātam = were accepted; mayā = by me; aham = I; shrāntaḥ = am wearied; kāmāt = of lust; hayaḥ iva = like a horse yātaḥ = going; ādhvani = along the way; satatam = uninterruptedly.

"Looking forward Rama, her husband, that longish large eyed Seetha, requested me to give time for one year. I accepted the auspicious words of the charmingly eyed Seetha. I am wearied of this lust, like a horse galloping along a path continuously."

Sloka 20

katham sāgaramakṣobhyam tariṣyanti vanaukasaḥ || 6-12-20
bahusattvasamākīrṇam tau vā daśarathātmajau |

20. katham = how; vanaukasaḥ = the monkeys or; tau = those; dasharātmajau vā = Rama and Lakshmana; tariṣyanti = can cross; sāgaram = the ocean; akṣobhyam = the unassailable; bahusattvasamākīrṇam = completely crowded with sea-animals?

How the monkeys or even Rama and Lakshmana can cross the unassailable ocean, completely infested as it was with sea-animals?"

Sloka 21

athavā kapinaikena kṛtam naḥ kadanam mahat || 6-12-21
durjñeyāḥ kāryagatayo brūta yasya yathāmati |

athvā = Is it not so; ekena kapinā = by one monkey; mahat = a huge; kadanam = destruction; kṛitam = was done; naḥ = to us; kāryagatayaḥ = the consequences of their actions; durṇ^eyāḥ = are not known; brūta = tell (me); yathāmati = according to your own intellect; yasya = what is there (in your mind).

"on the other hand, a huge destruction was done to us, by a single monkey. The consequences of their acts are unpredictable. Tell me what is there in your mind, according to your own understanding."

Sloka 22

mānuṣānno bhayam nāsti tathāpi tu vimṛśyatām || 6-12-22
tadā devāsure yudde yuṣmābhiḥ sahito.ajayam |
te me bhavantaśca tathā sugrīvapramukhān harīn || 6-12-23
pare pāre samudrasya puraskṛtya nṛpātmajau |
sītāyāḥ padavīm prāpya samprāptau varuṇālayam || 6-12-24

22;23;24. nāsti = no; bhayam = fear; naḥ = to us; mānuṣāt = with human beings; tathāpi = even so; vimṛishyatām = let it be deliberated; tadā = at that time; sahitaḥ = together; yushhmābhiḥ = with you; ajayam = I conquered; devāsurayuddhe = in the battle between celestials and demons; te = those; bhantashca = of you also; me = on my side; tathā = (conquered) in the same manner; prāpya = procuring; padavīm = access; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; nṛipātmajau = Rama and Lakshama; the princes; puraskṛitya = placing in front; vānarān = the monkeys; sugrīva pramukhān = like ṣugreeva and others; samprāptau; well-reached; varuṇālayam = the ocean; parepāre = on the other bank; samudrasya = of the ocean.

"We have no fear with human beings. Even so, the matter can be discussed. Earlier, together with you, I conquered the celestials in a battle between the celestials and demons. You also conquered them. Knowing the whereabouts of Seetha, Rama and Lakshmana together with Sugreeva and other monkeys reached the other shore of the ocean."

Sloka 25

adeyā ca yathā sītā vadhyau daśarathātmajau |
bhavadbhirmantyratām mantraḥ sunītam cābhidhīyatām || 6-12-25

25. mantraḥ mantryatām = let a thought be deliberated; bharadbhiḥ = by you; yathā = how; sītā = ṣettha; adeyā = may not be given; dasharathātmajau = (and) how Rama and Lakshama; vadhyauca = may be killed; sunītam = A prudent advice; abhidhīyatamca = be mentioned."

"Let a consultation be held by you and a judicious line of course suggested, so that Seetha may not have to be handed -over to them back and Rama and Lakshmana may be killed."

Sloka 26

na hi śaktim prapaśyāmi jagatyanyasya kasyacit |
sāgaram vānaraistīrtvā viścayena jayo mama || 6-12-26

26. na prapashyāmi = I do not see; kasyachit = any; anyasya = other; shaktim = capability; tīrtvā = to cross; sāgaram = the ocean; vānaraiḥ = along with the monkeys; jayaḥ = Victory; nishchayena = is surely; mama = mine."

"I do not see any other's capability to cross the ocean even so with the monkeys. Victory is surely mine."

Sloka 27

tasya kāmaparītasya niśamya paridevitam |
kumbhakarṇaḥ pracukrodha vacanam cedamabravīt || 6-12-27

27. nishamya = hearing; paridevitam = the lamentation; tasya = of Ravana; kāmaparītasya = captivated by lust; kumbhakarṇa = Kumabhakara; prachukrodha = was angry; abravītcha = and spoke; idam = those; vachanam = words.

Hearing the lamentation of Ravana who was captivated by lust, Kumbhakarana became angry and spoke the following words:

Sloka 28

yadā tu rāmasya salakṣmaṇasya |
prasahya sītā khalu pā ihā.ahṛtā |
sakṛtsamīkṣaiva suniścitam tadā |
bhajeta cittam yamuneva yāmunam || 6-12-28

28. yadā = when; sītā = ṣeetha; rāmasya = of Rama; salakshhmaṇasya = accompanied by Lakshmana; khalu = indeed; āhṛitā = was brought; iha = here; prasahya = forcibly; tadā; then itself; sakṛit = once; samīkshhyena = by contemplating thoroughly; chittam = your mind; bhajeta = should have driven into; sunishchitam = a good decision; yāmunaniva = like filling the depression at its source; yamunā = by Yamuna River."

"You should have contemplated thoroughly that very moment when Seetha was brought here forcibly only after a single impulsive thought from the hermitage of Rama, who was accompanied by Lakshmana, even like the river Yamuna fills the depression at its source the moment it descends on earth."

Sloka 29

sarvametanmahārāja kṛtamapratimam tava |
vidhīyeta sahāsmābhirādāvevāsya karmaṇaḥ || 6-12-29

29. mahārāja = O; emperor!; ādāneva = athe beginning itself; asya = of this; karmaṇaḥ = action; apratimam = it would have been unequalled; tava = to you; yadi sarvam = (if)all etat = this; kṛitam = act of consultation asmābhiḥ saha = with us; vidhīyetayadi = was done.

"O, emperor! If all this act of consultation with us was done at the beginning of this action itself (of bringing Seetha here), it would have been worthy of you."

Sloka 30

nyāyena rājakāryāṇi yaḥ karoti daśānana |
na sa samtapyate paścānniścatārthamatirnṛpaḥ || 6-12-30

30. dashānana = O; Ravana; yaḥ = which; nṛipaḥ = king; karoti = does; rājakāryāni = king's functions; nishchitārthamatiḥ = with a mind; ascertained; nyāyenna = by justice; saḥ = he; nasantapyate = will not repent; pashchāt = thereafter."

"O, Ravana! A king who performs king's functions with a mind duly ascertained by justice, will not repent thereafter."

Sloka 31

anupāyena karmāṇi viparītāni yāni ca |
kriyamāṇāni duṣyanti havīmṣyaprayateṣvina || 6-12-31

31. karmāṇi = actions; vuoarūtābu = oervertedktl kriyamāṇāni = done; anupāyena = witout use of any strategem; duṣyanti = get spoiled; havīnshhīva = even as oblations; aprayate = not intent on devotion.

"Actions done pervertedly without use of any stratagem get spoiled, even as oblations not intent on devotion got spoiled.

Sloka 32

yaḥ paścātpūrvakāryāṇi karmāṇyabhicikīrṣati |
pūrvam cāparakaryāṇi na sa veda nayānayau || 6-12-32

32. yaḥ = ḥe who; chikīrshhati = wants to do; karmāṇi = actions; pūrvakāryāni = which ought to be done before; pashchāt = later; aparakaryāṇi = and actions which ought to be done later; pūrvamca = \ before; saḥ = he; na veda = is not aware; nayānayau = of predence and imprudence.

"He who is not aware of prudence and imprudence, wants to do actions which are to be done before at a later stage and actions which are to be done later at a stage before."

Sloka 33

capalasya tu kṛtyeṣu prasamīksyādhikam balam |
cidramanye prapadyante krauñcasya khamiva dvijāḥ || 6-12-33

33. prasamīkshhya = seeing; adhikam = superior; balam = strength; chapalasya = of a hasteful enemy (others); prapadyante = look for; chhidram = his weak point; dvijāḥ iva = even as swans; kham = (seek) the cleavage; krauṇchasya = in Kraucha mountain.

"Seeing superior strength in a hasteful enemy, some look for his weak point, even as swans seek the cleavage in the Kraunca mountain."

God Kartikeya (son of Lord Shiva) made a cleavage in Krauncha mountain with His javelin. (ṃahabharata, Salya Parva 46-84).

Sloka 34

tvayedam mahādārabham kārya mapraticintitam |
diṣṭyā tvām nāvadhīdrāmo viṣamiśramivāmṛtam || 6-12-34

34. idam = this; mahat = great; kāryam = act; ārabdham = was undertaken; tvayā = by you; apratichintitam = without proper thinking; diṣṭyā = by good fortune; rāmaḥ = Rama; nāvadhīt = did not kill; tvām = you; āmiṣam iva = even as the meat; viṣamishram = mixed with poison."

"This great act was undertaken by you without proper thinking. By good fortune, Rama did not kill you even as the meat mixed with poison would kill the consumer."

Sloka 35

tasmāttvayā samārabdham karma hyapratimam paraiḥ |
aham samīkariṣyāmi hatvā śatrūm stanānagha || 6-12-35

35. anagha = O; irreproachable brother!; tasmāt = therefore; hatvā = by killing; tava = your; shatrūm = enemies; samīkarishhyāmi = I shall neutralise; apratimam = the dishonourable; karma = act; samārabdham = undertaken; tvayā = by you; paraiḥ = in relation to your enemies."

"O, irreproachable brother! Therefore by killing your enemies, I shall neutralize the dishonourable act undertaken by you in relation to your enemies."

Sloka 36

ahamutsādayiṣyāmi śatrūṃstava niśācara |

36. nishāchara = O; ranger of night!; aham = I; utsādayishhyāmi = shall destroy; tava = your; shatrūn = enemies; aham = I; yodhayishhyāmi = shall do battle with; tau = the two brother; yadi = even if (they are); shakravivasvautau = Indra and the sun-god; yadi pāvakamārutau = even if god of fire and the wind-god; kubera varuṇāvapi = or Kubera (the god of riches) and Varuna (the god of water).

"O, ranger of night! I shall destroy your enemies. I shall do battle with the two brothers, even if they are Indra and the sun-god or the god of fire and the wind-god or Kubera (the god of riches) and Varuna (the god of water).

Sloka 37

girimātraśarīrasya mahāparighayodhinaḥ || 6-12-37

37. purandaraḥ = even Indra; bibhīyat vai = indeed will be afraid of me; girimātra sharīrasya = with a mountain-sized body; tīkshhṇadamsṭrasya = fiery teeth; nardataḥ = making a roaring sound; mahāparigha yodhinaḥ = attacking with an immense bludgeon."

"Entering the combat with a mountain-sized body and fiery teeth, roaring the while and attacking with an immense bludgeon, I shall strike terror in Indra himself!"

Sloka 38

punarmām sadvitīyena śareṇa nihaniṣyati || 6-12-38

38. saḥ = (before) that Rama; nihanishhyati = strikes; mām = me; punaḥ = once more; dvitīyena = with a second; shareṇa = arrow; tataḥ = immediately; pāsyāmi = I shall drink; tasya = his; rudhiram = blood; āshvasa = cheer-up; kāmam = freely."

"Even before Rama strikes me once more with a second arrow, I shall drink his blood. cheer up freely.

Sloka 39

vadheva vai dāśaratheh sukhāvaham |
jayam tavāhartumaham tayiṣye |
hatvā ca rāmam saha lakṣmaṇena |
khādāmi sarvān hariyūthamukhyān || 6-12-39

39. aham = I; yatiṣve = shall try; āhartum = to fetch; jayam = victory; sukhavaham = which brings pleasure; tava = to you; vadhena = by killing; dāsharadhena = of Rama; hatvā = by killing; rāmam = Rama; lakshhmaṇena iha = along with lakshmana; khādāmi = I shall eat awa; sarvān = all hariyūthamukhyān = the leaders of monkey- troops."

"I shall try to fetch victory, which brings pleasure to you, by the killing of Rama. By exterminating Rama along with Lakshmana, I shall devour all the leaders of monkey-troops."

Sloka 40

ramasva kāmam piba cāgryavāruṇīm |
kuruṣva kārvāṇi hitāni vijvaraḥ |
mayā tu rāme gamite yamakṣayam |
cirāya sītā vaśagā bhaviṣyati || 6-12-40

40. ramasva = enjoy yourself; kāmam = freely; piba = drink; agryavāruṇīm = excellent of your desire; vijvaraḥ = without anxiety; rāme = Rama; gamite = having been despatched; yamakshhayam = to the abode of death; mayā = by me; sīta = ṣeetha; bhavishhyati = will be; vashaga = at your disposal; chirāya = forever."

"Enjoy yourself freely. Drink excellent of wines to the extent of your desire without any anxiety. Rama, having been dispatched by me to the abode of death, Seetha will be at your disposal forever."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe dvādaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 12th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
