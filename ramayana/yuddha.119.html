Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 119

Introduction

Lord Shiva informs Rama that his father, Dasaratha, in an ethereal form and as a replica of his extinct personality, has arrived in an aerial car along with Indra, the lord of celestials. Shiva asks Rama and Lakshmana to pay their respects to the soul of Dasaratha and they respond in approaching him and offer their salutations. Dasaratha says he is overjoyed in seeing him, having defeated Ravana and having completed the exile for fourteen years. He asks Rama to return to Ayodhya, meet Bharata and get consecrated to the throne of Ayodhya. Dasaratha further advises Lakshmana and Seetha to continue to serve Rama, the supreme deity. Dasaratha returns to the abode of Indra, by the aerial car.

Sloka 1

idaṃ śubhataraṃ vākyaṃ vyājahāra maheśvaraḥ || 6-119-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; shubham = the auspicious; vākyam = words; etat = thus; anubhāṣhitam = spoken; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; maheshvaraḥ = Lord ṣhiva; the supreme Lord; tataḥ = thereupon; vyājahāra shubhataram vākyam = delivered the following still more beautiful speech:

Hearing the auspicious words thus spoken by Rama, Lord Shiva the Supreme Lord thereupon delivered the following still more beautiful speech:

Sloka 2

puṣkarākṣa mahābāho mahāvakṣaḥ parantapa |
diṣṭyā kṛtamidaṃ karma tvayā śastrabhṛtāṃ vara || 6-119-2

2. puṣhkarākṣha = O lotus eyed; mahābāho = long armed; mahāvakṣhaḥ = broad chested; paramtapa = the annihilator of adversaries; vara = and excellent; dharma bhṛitām = among those upholding the cause of virtue!; idam karma = this task; kṛitam = was accomplished; tvaya = by you; diṣhṭyā = thank heaven!.

"O lotus-eyed, long-armed, broad-chested, annihilator of enemies and excellent among those upholding the cause of virtue! Thank heaven! You accomplished this task."

Sloka 3

"Fortunately has the fear born of Ravana - which increased the severe darkness on the entire world has been removed by you, on the battle-field, O Rama!"

prāpya rājyamayodhyāyāṃ nandayitvā suhṛjjanam |
ikṣvākūṇāṃ kule vaṃśaṃ sthāpayitvā mahābala || 6-119-5

brāhmaṇebhyo dhanaṃ dattvā tridivaṃ gantumarhasi || 6-119-6

Sloka 7

eṣa rājā vimānasthaḥ pitā daśarathastava |
kākutstha mānuṣe loke gurustava mahāyaśāḥ || 6-119-7

"This king Dasaratha, your father in this mortal world and the highly glorious as well as the venerable person, is seated in an aerial car, O Rama!"

Sloka 8

indralokaṃ gataḥ śrīmāṃstvayā putreṇa tāritaḥ |

"Having been delivered by you, his (godly) son, the glorious king obtained the abode of Indra the lord of celestials. you offer your respectful salutation to him, along with Lakshmana, your brother."

Sloka 9

mahādevavacaḥ śrutvā kākutsthaḥ sahalakṣmaṇaḥ |
vimānaśikharasthasya praṇāmamakarotpituḥ || 6-119-9

9. shrutvā = hearing; mahādeva vachah = the words of ṣhiva; rāghavaḥ = Rama; saha lakṣhmaṇaḥ = along with lakshmana; bhrātrā = his brother; praṇāmam = offered their salutation; pituḥ = to their father; vimāna shikharasthasya = seated on the topmost part of an aerial car.

Hearing the words of Shiva, Rama along with Lakshmana his brother, offered their salutation to their father, seated on the topmost part of an aerial car.

Sloka 10

dīpyamānaṃ svayāṃ lakṣmyā virajoambaradhāriṇam |
lakṣmaṇena saha bhrātrā dadarśa pitaraṃ prabhuḥ || 6-119-10

The lord Rama along with Lakshmana his brother saw their father, who was blazing with his own splendour and clad in brilliant garments.

Sloka 11

Then the king Dasaratha the lord who was sitting in the aerial car, in an excellent seat, was filled with excessive delight to see his son (Rama), who was dearer to him than life. Placing him in his lap and embracing him in his arms, he thereupon commenced his speech as follows:

Sloka 13

na me svargo bahumataḥ saṃmānaśca surarṣibhiḥ |
tvayā rāma vihīnasya satyaṃ pratiśṛṇomi te || 6-119-13

13. rāma = O Rama!; vihīnasya = separated from; tvayā = you; svargaḥ = the heaven; na bahumataḥ = is not a liking; me = to me; sammānashcha = (nor) the respect given to me; surarṣhabhaiḥ = by the foremost of celstials; pratishṛiṇomi = I am telling; te = you; satyam = the truth.

"O Rama! Separated from you, the heaven is not a liking to me, nor the respect given to me by the foremost of celestials. I am telling you the truth."

Sloka 14

adya tvāṃ nihatāmitraṃ dṛṣṭvā saṃpūrṇamānasam |
nistīrṇavanavāsaṃ ca prītirāsītparā mama| 6-119-14

14. adya = today; prītiāsīt = there is a great joy; mama = for me; dṛiṣhṭvā = for having seen; tvām = you; nihitāmitram = by whom the enemies have been destroyed; sampūrṇa mānasam = fully satisfied in your mind; vistīrṇavana vāsam = and having fully gone through the period of your exile.

"Today, there is a great joy for me, for having seen you, fully satisfied in your mind, now that your enemies have been destroyed and you have fully gone through the period of exile."

Sloka 15

kaikeyyā yāni coktāni vākyāni vadatāṃ vara |
tava pravrājanārthāni sthitāni hṛdaye mama || 6-119-15

15. vara = O excellent; vadatām = among the eloquent!; vākyāni = the words; yāni = which; uktāni = were uttered; kaikeyyā = by Kaikeyi; tava pravrajanārthām = with an aim to send you into exile; tāni = those words; sthitāni = are (still) imprinted mama hṛidayāni = in my heart.

"O excellent among the eloquent! The words, which were uttered by Kaikeyi, with an aim to send you into exile, are still imprinted in my heart."

Sloka 16

tvāṃ tu dṛṣṭvā kuśalinaṃ pariṣvajya salakṣmaṇam |
adya duḥkhādvimuktoasmi nīhārādiva bhāskaraḥ || 6-119-16

"Seeing you in a fine fettle with Lakshmana and hugging you today, I stand completely rid of sorrow - even as the sun is freed from mist."

Sloka 17

tāritoahaṃ tvayā putra suputreṇa mahātmanā |
aṣṭāvakreṇa dharmātmā tārito brāhmaṇo yathā || 6-119-17

17. mahātmānā = O great-souled; putra = son!; aham = I; tāritaḥ = have been redeemed; tvayā = by you; suputreṇa = my worthy son!; kaholaḥ brāhmaṇo yathā = even as Kahola; a brahmana; dharmātmā = who was virtuous; aṣhṭāvakreṇa = (was redeemed) by Ashtavakra.

"O great-souled son! I have been redeemed by you, my worthy son, even as Kahola a brahmana was redeemed by Ashtavakra."

Sloka 18

idānīṃ ca vijānāmi yathā saumya sureśvaraiḥ |
vadhārthaṃ rāvaṇasyeha vihitaṃ puruṣottamam || 6-119-18

"O gently one! I recognize you now to be the Supreme person, duly enjoined here by the rulers of gods, for the destruction of Ravana."

Sloka 19

19. siddhārthā khalu = blessed indeed; kausalyā = is Kausalya; yādrakṣhyati tvam = who will see you; shatru sūdanam = the destroyer of your enemies; nivṛittam = returned; gatam = and got; gṛiham = home; ranāt = from the forest.

"Blessed indeed is Kausalya, who will see you, the destroyer of your enemies, returned home from the forest."

Sloka 20

rājye caivābhiṣiktaṃ ca drakṣyanti vasudhādhipam || 6-119-20

20. siddhārthāḥ khalu = blessed indeed; te narāḥ = are those men; ye drakṣhyante = who will see you; gatam = returned; purīm = to the City (of Ayodhya); rājyam abhiṣhiktam cha = and consecrated on the throne; vasudhādhipam = as a lord of the earth.

"Blessed indeed are those men, who will see you returned to the City of Ayodhya and consecrated on the throne as a lord of the earth."

Sloka 21

anuraktena balinā śucinā dharmacāriṇā |
iccheyaṃ tvāmahaṃ draṣṭuṃ bharatena samāgatam || 6-119-21

21. aham = I; ichchheyam = desire; draṣhṭum = to see; tvām = you; samāgatam = re united; bharatena = with Bharata; anuraktena = who has affection towards you; balinā = who is strong; shuchinā = who is honest; dharmachāriṇā = and virtuous.

"I desire to see you, re-united with the mighty Bharata, who is affectionate towards you, honest and virtuous."

Sloka 22

caturdaśasamāḥ saumya vane niryāpitāstvayā |
vasatā sītayā sārdhaṃ lakṣmaṇena ca dhīmatā || 6-119-22

22. saumya = O gentle one!; chaturdasha = fourteen; samāḥ = years; niryātitāḥ = were spent; tvayā = by you; vasatā = residing in the forest; dhīmatā lakṣhmaṇena = along with the intelligent Lakshmana; sītāya sārtham = and with ṣeetha.

"O gentle one! You spent fourteen years, residing in the forest with Lakshmana and Seetha."

Sloka 23

nivṛttavanavāsoasi pratijñā saphalā kṛtā |
rāvaṇaṃ ca raṇe hatvā devāste paritoṣitāḥ || 6-119-23

23. pratiJṇā = my pledge; puritā = was implemented; tvayā = by you; asi nivṛitta vanavāsaḥ = you completed the term of your exile; hatvā = by your killing; rāvaṇam = Ravana; raṇe = in battle; devatāh cha = celestials too; paritoṣhitāḥ = have been fully gratified.

"You completed the term of your exile. ṃy pledge was implemented by you. The celestials too have been fully gratified, by your killing of Ravana in battle."

Sloka 24

kṛtaṃ karma yaśaḥ ślāghyaṃ prāptaṃ te śatrusūdana |
bhrātṛbhiḥ saha rājyastho dīrghamāyuravāpnuhi || 6-119-24

24. shatrusūdana = O the destroyer of enemies!; shlāghyam karma = a landable act; kṛitam = has been done; yashaḥ = glory; prāptam = has been earned; te = to you (by you); rājyasthaḥ = being in a kingly role; bhrātṛibhiḥ saha = along with your brothers; avāpnuhi = may you attain; dīrgham = a long; āyuḥ = life.

"O the destroyer of enemies! A laudable act has been done and glory has been earned by you. Getting consecrated on the throne, may you attain a long life, along with your brothers."

Sloka 25

iti bruvāṇaṃ rājānaṃ rāmaḥ prāñjalirabravīt |
kuru prasādaṃ dharmajña kaikeyyā bharatasya ca || 6-119-25

25. rāmaḥ = Rama; prāṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms in salutation; abravīt = spoke; rājānam = to dasaratha; bruvāṇam = who was speaking; iti = thus; kuru prasādam = be gracious; kaikeyyāḥ = to kaikeyi; bharatasya = and Bharata; dharmaJṇa = O the knower of virtues!

To Dasaratha, who was thus speaking, Rama with joined palms in salutation, submitted (as follows): "Be gracious to Kaikeyi and Bharata, O the knower of virtues!"

Sloka 26

saputrāṃ tvāṃ tyajāmīti yaduktā kaikayī tvayā |

You will remember those words spoken by you saying, I disown you, with your son (Bharata)'. ṃay that terrific curse not touch Kaikeyi and her son, O Lord!"

Sloka 27

tatheti mahārājo rāmamuktvā kṛtāñjalim |
lakṣmaṇaṃ ca pariṣvajya punarvākyamuvāca ha || 6-119-27

Saying 'ṃay it be" to Rama who stood with joined palms embraced Lakshmana. Dasaratha again uttered the following words to Lakshmana:

Sloka 28

rāmaṃ śuśrūṣatā bhaktyā vaidehyā saha sītayā |
kṛtā mama mahāprītiḥ prāptaṃ dharmaphalaṃ ca te || 6-119-28

28. shushruṣhatā = by doing service; bhaktyā = with devotion; rāmam = to Rama; sītayā saha = and ṣeetha; vaidehyā = the princess of Videha kingdom; mahāprītiḥ = a great gratification; kṛitā = has been brought; mama = to me; dharma phalam cha = a religious merit too; prāptam = has been obtained; te = to you.

"A great gratification has been rendered to me and reward in the form of religious merit earned by you in that you served Rama and Seetha, the princess of Videha kingdom, with devotion."

Sloka 29

dharmaṃ prāpsyasi dharmajña yaśaśca vipulaṃ bhuvi |

29. rāme prasanne = Rama; being pleased with you; prāpsyasi = you will attain; dharmam = religious merit; vipulam = and extensive; yashashcha = glory; bhuvi = on earth; svargam cha = as also heaven; tathā uttamam mahimānam = and excellent power; dharmaJṇa = O knower of righteousness!.

"Rama, being pleased with you, you will attain religious merit and extensive glory on earth, as also heaven and excellent power O knower of righteousness!"

Sloka 30

rāmaḥ sarvasya lokasya śubheṣvabhirataḥ sadā || 6-119-30

"Happiness to you, O the augmentor of the joy of Sumitra, your mother! Serve Rama well. Rama forever is intent on advancing the interests of the entire world."

Sloka 31

abhigamya mahātmānamarcanti puruṣottamam || 6-119-31

31. ete = these; lokāḥ = 9three) worlds; sendrāḥ = including indra; siddhāshcha = ṣiddhas (a class of demi-gods); paramarṣhayaḥ = and great sages; archanti = pay homage to; mahātmānam = the great-souled; puruṣhottamam = Rama as a supreme person; abhigamya = on approaching him.

"These three worlds, including Indra Siddhas (a class of demi-gods) and great sages pay homage to the great-souled Rama as a Supreme person, on approaching him."

Sloka 32

"O great one! O the destroyer of adversaries! Rama has been spoken of as the unmanifest and the imperishable Brahama (the absolute), established by the Vedas, the heart of gods and the secret of all gods."

Sloka 33

Sloka 34

Thus speaking to Lakshmana, the (former) king, addressing his daughter-in-law standing before him with joined palms as "ṃy daughter", slowly and affectionately advised her as follows:

Sloka 35

"No wrath indeed should be rendered by you towards Rama, for having repudiated you. This has been done by him, wishing for your welfare and in order to demostrate your purity."

Sloka 36

Sloka 37

na tvaṃ subhru samādheyā patiśuśrūvaṇaṃ prati |
avaśyaṃ tu mayā vācyameṣa te daivataṃ param || 6-119-37

37. tvam = you; na samādhyuā kāmam = need not be instructed; bhartṛi shushrūṣhaṇam prati = about the matter of rendering service to your husband; tu = but; vāchyam = it is to be told; avashyam = certainly; mayā = by me; eṣhaḥ = he; te param = is your supreme; daivatam = deity.

"You need not be instructed about the matter of rendering service to your husband. But, it is to be told certainly by me. He is your supreme deity."

Sloka 38

iti pratisamādiśya putrau sītāṃ tathā snuṣām |
indralokaṃ vimānena yayau daśaratho jvalan || 6-119-38

38. iti prati samādishya = having instructed thus; putrau = to his sons; sītām cha = and ṣeetha; dasharathaḥ nṛipaḥ = the king; Dasaratha; rāghavaḥ = who was born in Raghu dynasty; yayau = went; indralokam = to the abode of Indra the lord of celestials; vimānena = by an aerial car.

Having instructed thus to his sons and Seetha, the King Dasaratha who was born in Raghu dynasty went to the abode of Indra, the lord of celestials, by an aerial car.

Sloka 39

vimānamāsthāya mahānubhāvaḥ |
śriyā ca saṃhṛṣṭatanurnṛpottamaḥ |
āmantyra putrau saha sītayā ca |
jagāma devapravarasya lokam| 6-119-39

39. samtuṣhṭa tanuh = with a satisfied self; nṛipottama = Dasaratha; the excellent king; mahānubhāvaḥ = of noble- mindedness; shriyā = endowed with splendour; āmantrya = taking leave; putrau = of his sons; sītayā saha = and ṣeetha; āsthāya = and ascending; vimānam = the aerial car; jagāma = went; devapravarasya lokam = to the abode of Indra; the chief of gods.

With a satisfied self, Dasaratha the excellent king of noble-mindedness endowed with splendour, taking leave of his sons and Seetha and mounting the aerial car, went to the abode of Indra, the chief of gods.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ekonaviṃśatyadhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 119th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
