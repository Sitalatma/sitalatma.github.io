Valmiki Ramayana

Kishkindha kanda


Book IV : Kishkindha Kanda - The Empire of Holy ṃonkeys

chapter [Sarga] 8
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sugreeva gives a report to Rama about his plight for fear of Vali, who made his own brother an enemy and abducted Sugreeva's wife. Rama asks Sugreeva to inform him about the real cause of enmity, so that he can decide correctly. Sugreeva narrates his woeful condition.


Sloka 1

parituṣṭaḥ tu sugrīvaḥ tena vākyena harṣitaḥ |
lakṣmaṇasya agrajam śūram idam vacanam abravīt || 4-8-1

1. ṣugreeva; tena vākyena = by that, sentence; harṣitaḥ = gladdened; parituṣṭaḥ tu = satisfied; shūram = to that valiant one, Rama; lakshmanasya agrajam = Lakshmana's, elder; idam vacanam = this, sentence; abravīt = said.

Sugreeva is gladdened and satisfied too by that sentence, and said this sentence to Lakshmana's elder brother, Rama the valiant one. [4-8-1]

Sloka 2

sarvathā aham anugrāhyo devatānām na saṃśayaḥ |
upapannaḥ guṇa upetaḥ sakhā yasya bhavān mama || 4-8-2

2. upapannaḥ = possessor of [all kinds of endowments]; guṇa upetaḥ = having good virtues, with; bhavān yasya mama sakhā = you, whose, mine, friend; [on becoming so]; aham = sarvathā = I, in everyway; devatānām anugrāhyaḥ = to gods [also]; worthy to be blessed; na samshayaḥ = no, doubt.

"When you wth all kinds of endowment and good virtues are my friend, doubtlessly I am worthy to be blessed by all gods in everyway. [4-8-2]

Sloka 3

śakyam khalu bhavet rāma sahāyena tvayā anagha |
sura rājyam api prāptum sva rājyam kimuta prabho || 4-8-3

3. anagha = oh, merited one; oh, Rama; tvayā sahāyena = with your, help; sura rājyam api prāptum shakyam bhavet = god's, kingdom, even, to achieve, possible, it will be; sva rājyam kim utaḥ prabho = one's own, why tell about, oh , lord.

"Oh, merited one, with your help it will be possible to achieve kingdom of gods, oh, lord, then why tell about one's own kingdom... [4-8-3]

Sloka 4

so.aham sabhājyo bandhūnām suhṛdām caiva rāghava |
yasya agni sākṣikam mitram labdham rāghava vaṃśajam || 4-8-4

4. yasya = to whom such like me; rāghava vamsha jam = in Raghava's, dynasty, one born in; mitram = as friend; agni sākshikam = fire, as witness; labdham = gained by; saḥ aham = such as, I am; bandhūnām = among, relatives; ca iva = also, like that; su hṛidām = kind-hearted friends; sabhājyaḥ = estimable.

"He who has gained the one born in Raghava-s dynasty as friend, to which sacred fire bore the witness, such a being like me is estimable among his relative, more so among his kind-hearted friends... [4-8-4]

Sloka 5

aham api anurūpaḥ te vayasyo jñāsyase śanaiḥ |
na tu vaktum samartho.aham tvayi ātmagatān guṇān || 4-8-5

5. aham api = I am, even; anurūpaḥ = matching; vayasyaḥ = friend; te = to you; jñāsyase = you will know; shanaiḥ = soon; aham ātma gatān gunān = I am my in soul, having [inherent,] capabilities; tvayi = to you; vaktum = to speak; na tu samarthaḥ = not, capable [inexpedient.]

"Even though I am a matching friend to you, that you will know soon, it will be inexpedient of myself to talk about my inherent capabilities. [4-8-5]

Sloka 6

mahātmanām tu bhūyiṣṭham tvat vidhānām kṛta ātmanām |
niścalā bhavati prītiḥ dhairyam ātmavatām vara || 4-8-6

6. maha ātmanām tu = for great souls; bhūyiṣṭham = generally; tvat vidhānām = in your manner; kṛita ātmanām = purified, at heart; nishcalā = stable; bhavati = will be; prītiḥ = friendship; dhairyam = courage; ātmavatām vara = among hearty ones, the best [amiable one.]

"Great souls of your kind, whose hearts are purified like yours, oh, amiable Rama, their friendship and courage will generally be stable. [4-8-6]

Sloka 7

rajatam vā suvarṇam vā śubhāni ābharaṇāni ca |
avibhaktāni sādhūnām avagacchanti sādhavaḥ || 4-8-7

7. sādhūnām = among gentle souls; rajatam vā = silver, or; suvarṇam vā = gold, or; shubhāni ābharaṇāni cha = prosperous ornaments, even; a vibhaktāni = not, in an apportioned manner; [thus] sādhavaḥ = by gentle souls; avagachanti = will be knowing.

"Gentle souls will be knowing that silver or gold, or even prosperous ornaments are not to be apportioned among themselves as this is mine and that is yours. [4-8-7]

Sloka 8

āḍhyo vā api daridro vā duḥkhitaḥ sukhito.api vā |
nirdoṣaḥ ca sadoṣaḥ ca vayasyaḥ paramā gatiḥ || 4-8-8

8. āDhyaḥ vā api = rich, or, even; daridraḥ vā = poor, or; duhkhitaḥ = saddened; sukhitaḥ api vā = gladdened, even, or; nir doṣaḥ ca = without flaw, or; sa doshaḥ vā = with flaw, or; vayasyaḥ = friend is; paramā gatiḥ = ultimate, recourse.

"ṃay he be rich or poor, saddened or gladdened, flawless or flawed, a friend is the ultimate recourse to any. [4-8-8]

Sloka 9

dhana tyāgaḥ sukha tyāgo deśa tyāgo.api vā anaghaḥ |
vayasyārthe pravartante sneham dṛṣṭvā tathā vidham || 4-8-9

9. anaghaḥ = oh impeccable one; sneham = friendship; dṛiṣṭvā tathā vidham = on observing, thus, course of [friendship]; vayasya arthe = for friend's, sake; dhana tyāgaḥ = wealth, devoting; sukha tyāgaḥ = pleasure devoting; desha tyāgaḥ vā api = country, devoting, or, even; pravartante = conducts [they don't hesitate to forgo, extend.]

"On observing the course of friendship and knowing which is true friendship, the true friends do not hesitate to extend wealth, pleasure or even their own country for the sake of friendship." Sugreeva thus extolled Rama and his friendship. [4-8-9]

Sloka 10

tat tathā iti abravīt rāmaḥ sugrīvam priya darśanam |
lakṣmaṇasya agrataḥ lakṣmyā vāsavasya iva dhīmataḥ || 4-8-10

10. rāmaḥ = Rama; vāsavasya iva dhīmataḥ = Indra, as with, sagacious one; lakshmyā = one who is resplendent; lakshmanasya agrataḥ = [such] Lakshmana's, before; priya darshanam sugrīvam = one who looks pleasant [soft-natured in his for the onlookers, to such] ṣugreeva; tat tathā iti abravīt = that, that way, only, [thus] said Rama.

"That is thus only," said Rama before Lakshmana, which Lakshmana is sagacious, resplendent and who vies with Indra, about what is said by Sugreeva, which Sugreeva is by now soft-natured in his looks, doffing off his jittery look. [4-8-10]

Sloka 11

tato rāmam sthitam dṛṣṭvā lakṣmaṇam ca mahābalam |
sugrīvaḥ sarvataḥ cakṣuḥ vane lolam apātayat || 4-8-11

11. tataḥ = then; ṣugreeva; sthitam rāmam dṛiṣṭvā = standing there, at Rama, on seeing; mahābalam = greatly mighty; lakshmaṇam ca = Lakshmana, also; lolam = rolling - bolting; cakshuḥ = eyes; sarvataḥ = around; vane = in forest; apātayat = thrown [he cast sight.]

Then on seeing Rama standing there with greatly mighty Lakshmana, Sugreeva cast his sight with his bolting eyes around that forest. [4-8-11]

Sloka 12

sa dadarśa tataḥ sālam avidūre harīśvaraḥ |
supuṣpam īṣat patra āḍhyam bhramaraiḥ upaśobhitam || 4-8-12

12. tataḥ = then; saḥ harīshvaraḥ = he that, monkeys, lord,; avidūre su puṣpam = not far off, well flowered; īṣat patra āDhyam = with some, leaves, covering; bhramaraiḥ = with honeybees; upa shobhitam = well bedecked; sālam = sala tree; dadarsha = has seen.

He that lord of monkeys Sugreeva has then seen a well flowered sala tree not far off from there, covered with some leaves and bedecked with honeybees. [4-8-12]

Sloka 13

tasya ekām parṇa bahulām śākhām bhaṃktvā suśobhitām |
rāmasya āstīrya sugrīvo niṣasāda sa rāghavaḥ || 4-8-13

13. sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; tasya = its, tree's; parṇa bahulām = leaves, many; su sobhitām = well, charming one; ekām = one; shākhām = branch; bhanktvā = broke off; rāmasya āstīrya = for Rama, spread out; saḥ rāghavaḥ = with Raghava; niṣasāda = sat on it.

And Sugreeva broke off one branch of that sala tree that is with many leaves and a well charming one, and spreading it out for Rama, he sat on it with Raghava. [4-8-13]

Sloka 14

tau āsīnau tataḥ dṛṣṭvā hanūmān api lakṣmaṇam |
sāla śākhām samutpāṭya vinītam upaveśayat || 4-8-14

14. tataḥ = then; hanūmān = ḥanuma; api = even; āsīnau = sitting; tau = those two; dṛstvā = on seeing; sāla shākhām = sala, branch; sam utpāṭya = on breaking; vinītam = modest one; lakshmaṇam = to Lakshmana; upaveshayat = sat on.

On seeing those two sitting on a branch then Hanuma also broke a branch of sala tree for the modest Lakshmana and seated Lakshmana on it. [4-8-14]

This verse and some more will look alike to verses 18, 19, 20 and some more in 5th chapter of this canto, meaning that these actions are repeated whenever Rama and Lakshmana are standing, these monkeys broke the branches of trees and spread its leaves like a leafy mat as a comfortable seat to Rama with their veneration. Some other verses dealing with the agony of Sugreeva and assurances of Rama are also there, indicating that Rama assured the doubting monkeys repeatedly.

Sloka 15

sukha upaviṣṭam rāmam tu prasannam udadhim yathā |
sāla puṣpāva saṃkīrṇe tasmin girivara uttame || 4-8-15
tataḥ prahṛṣṭaḥ sugrīvaḥ ślakṣṇayā śubhayā girā |
uvāca praṇayād rāmam harṣa vyākulita akṣaram || 4-8-16

15, 16. tataḥ = then; ṣugreeva; prahṛiṣṭaḥ = who is pleased; sāla puṣpa = with sala tree's, flowers; samkīrṇne = all over bestrewn with; tasmin girivara uttame = there, on that mountain, the best; sukha upaviṣṭam = comfortably, one who is sitting; prasannam yathā udadhim = quiet, like, ocean; rāmam tu = one who causes delight to heart; rāmam = to such a Rama; shlakshnam shubhayā girā = with gentle, gracious, words; harṣa vyākulita aksharam = in happiness, fluttering, with words; praṇayāt = in friendship; uvāca = said.

Then to him, who by now is comfortably sitting on a flowery mat laid by the bestrewn flowers of sala trees on that best mountain like a quiet ocean, to such Rama who by his very presence delights hearts, that gladsome Sugreeva said this in friendship with his gentle and gracious words that fluttered with happiness while he spoke. [4-8-15, 16]

Sloka 17

aham vinikṛto bhrātrā carāmi eṣa bhayārditaḥ |
ṛṣyamūkam giri varam hṛta bhāryaḥ suduḥkhitaḥ || 4-8-17

17. bhrātrā vinikṛitaḥ = by brother, calumniated; eṣaḥ aham = this, me; bhayārditaḥ = with fear haunting; hṛita bhāryaḥ = stolen, wife; su duḥkhitaḥ = highly, anguished; giri varam = mountain the best; Rishyamūkam = on ṃt. Rishyamuka; carāmi = I am moving.

"ṃy brother calumniated me and stole my wife, with his fear and my anguish haunting me I am moving about this best mountain Rishyamuka. [4-8-17]

Sloka 18

so.aham trastaḥ bhaye magnaḥ vane saṃbrānta cetanaḥ |
vālinā nikṛtaḥ bhrātrā kṛta vairaḥ ca rāghava || 4-8-18

18. Raghava; bhrātrā = by brother; vālinā = by Vali; nikṛitaḥ = insulted - affronted; kṛita vairaḥ = made, enemy; ca = also; saḥ aham = such as, I am; trastaḥ = scared; sambhrānta = dazed; cetanaḥ = vitality; vasāmi = living; bhaye magnaḥ = in fear, flung in.

"Affronted by my brother, oh, Raghava, I am made as his enemy, such as I am, I am living on with a scared and dazed vitality far flung in fear. [4-8-18]

Sloka 19

vālinaḥ me bhaya ārtasya sarvaloka abhayaṃkara |
mama api tvam anāthasya prasādam kartum arhasi || 4-8-19

19. sarva loka = for all worlds; a bhayam kara = no, fear - protection, giver [protector]; me = my - from my brother; vālinaḥ = from Vali; bhaya ārtasya = in fear, agonized; a nāthasya = un, protected one [I am]; mama api = to me, even; tvam = you; prasādam kartum arhasi = protection, to accord, able you are.

"I am agonized by the fear from Vali, oh, protector of all the worlds, you protect me too for I am unprotected, and able are you to accord protection." Thus Sugreeva appealed to Rama. [4-8-19]

Sloka 20

evam uktaḥ tu tejasvī dharmajño dharma vatsalaḥ |
pratyuvāca sa kākutsthaḥ sugrīvam prahasan iva || 4-8-20

20. evam uktaḥ tu = thus, said; tejasvī = resplendent; dharmajñaḥ = virtuous; dharma vatsalaḥ = righteousness, the patron of; saḥ kākutsthaḥ = he that, Rama; pra hasan iva = laughing it off, as though; sugrīvam = to ṣugreeva; prati uvāca = in turn, said - replied.

Thus said by Sugreeva, the resplendent and virtuous Rama, the patron of righteousness replied Sugreeva as though laughing off the task. [4-8-20]

Sloka 21

upakāra phalam mitram apakāro ari lakṣaṇam |
adya eva tam vadhiṣyāmi tava bhāryā apahāriṇam || 4-8-21

21. upakāra phalam mitram = helping, fruit of, is a friend; apakāraḥ ari lakshaṇam = harming, is enemy's, trait; adya eva = today, only; vadh iṣyāmi = to kill, I wish to; tam tava bhāryā apahāriṇam = him, your, wife's, abductor.

"Helping is the fruit of friendship while harming is enemy's trait, hence I wish to kill him today only, that abductor of your wife..." Rama continued thus. [4-8-21]

Sloka 22

ime hi me mahābhāga patriṇaḥ tigma tejasaḥ |
kārtikeya vana udbhūtāḥ śarā hema vibhūṣitāḥ || 4-8-22
kanka patra paricchannā mahendra aśani sannibhāḥ |
suparvāṇaḥ sutīkṣṇa agrā saroṣā bhujagā iva || 4-8-23

22, 23. mahā bhāgāḥ = oh, highly fortunate one - ṣugreeva; me ime patriṇaḥ sharā = my, these, winged [pinion to hern,] arrows; tigma tejasaḥ = sharply, sparkling; kārtikeya vana udbhūtāḥ = from Kartikeya's, forest, begotten; hema vibhūṣitāḥ = gold, decorated with; kanka patrai pari cchannāḥ = with eagle's, feathers, tied with as fins; mahendra = Indra's; ashani sannibhāḥ = ṭhunderbolt, like; su parvāṇaḥ = having smooth, nodes, even knots; su tīkshṇa agrā = very, sharply, tipped; sa roṣāḥ = with rage; bhujagā iva = snakes, like.

"These are my arrows winged from pinion of the hern, oh, highly fortunate Sugreeva, these that are decorated in gold sparkle sharply like the Thunderbolts of Indra, and they are begotten from the reed forest of Kartikeya, ends tied with eagles feathers for fins, smooth are their nodes, sharp are their tips that pierce sharply, and these will be like enraged snakes. [4-8-22, 23]

Please see ch 37 in Bala Kanda for the legend of Karthikeya and his emergence.

Sloka 24

vāli sajñam amitram te bhrātaram kṛta kilbiṣam |
śaraiḥ vinihatam paśya vikīrṇam iva parvatam || 4-8-24

24. te bhrātaram = your brother; kṛita kilbisham = who has done, malice; vāli sañjam = Vali, denotative by, named; a mitram = unfriendly - intimidating one; sharaiḥ = with arrows; vi nihatam = verily, eradicated; vikīrṇam iva parvatam = splintered, like, mountain; pashya = you may see.

"You may see your malicious and intimidating brother, someone denotative by name Vali, eradicated with these very arrows and splintered down like a mountain." Rama said so to Sugreeva. [4-8-24]

Sloka 25

rāghavasya vacaḥ śrutvā sugrīvo vāhinī patiḥ |
praharṣam atulam lebhe sādhu sādhviti ca abravīt || 4-8-25

25. rāghavasya = Raghava's; vacaḥ = words; shrutvā = heard; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; vāhinī patiḥ = monkey-force, lord of; praharṣam = happiness; atulam = much; lebhe = attained; sādhu sādhu iti ca = good, good, thus, also; abravīt = said.

On hearing Raghava's words, the lord of monkey-force Sugreeva attained much happiness and said, 'good, good...' [4-8-25]

Sloka 26

rāma śoka abhibhūto aham śoka ārtānām bhavān gatiḥ |
vayasya iti kṛtvā hi tvayi aham paridevaye || 4-8-26

26. Rama; aham = I am; shoka abhibhūtaḥ = by sadness, confounded in; shoka ārtānām = for sadly, lamenting ones; bhavān gatiḥ = you are, the recourse; vayasya iti kṛitvā = friend, thus, on making [because you are a friend]; hi = verily; tvayi aham = in you, I am; paridevaye = beseeching [I open up my woes before you.].

"Rama, I am confounded in sadness, and for those lamenting sadly you are the only recourse, and because you have befriended me I am opening up my woes before you. [4-8-26]

Sloka 27

tvam hi pāṇi pradānena vayasyo me agni sākṣikam |
kṛtaḥ prāṇaiḥ bahumataḥ satyena ca śapāmi aham || 4-8-27

27. tvam = you are; pāṇi pradānena = hand, by extending; agni ākshikam = of fire, by witness; kṛitaḥ = befriended; [me, therefore]; prāṇaiḥ bahumataḥ vayasyaḥ me hi = than life, you are dearest, friend you are, of minbe, indeed; aham = I am satyena api shapāmi = by truth, only, taking oath.

"By extending a helping hand and befriending me while sacred fire bore witness, you have indeed become my dearest friend on par with my life, and hence I take promise on truth to broach. [4-8-27]

Sloka 28

vayasya iti kṛtvā ca visrabdhaḥ pravadāmi aham |
duḥkham antargatam tan me mano harati nityaśaḥ || 4-8-28

28. vayasya iti kṛitvā ca = friend, thus, making, also; aham = I am; visrabdham = in believability [without hitches and hold-ups]; pra vadāmi = clearly, saying; tat antargatam duḥkham manaḥ harati nityashaḥ = that which, internal, sorrow is there, my, heart, stealing [rending,] always.

"By befriending you I am able to say clearly about my internal sorrow without any hitches and hold-ups, which is always rending my heart..." So said Sugreeva to Rama. [4-8-28]

Sloka 29

etāvat uktvā vacanam bāṣpa dūṣita locanaḥ |
bāṣpa dūṣitayā vācā na uccaiḥ śknoti bhāṣitum || 4-8-29

29. etāvat = up to here; uktvā vacanam = said, sentence; bāshpa dūṣitayā locanaḥ = with tears, abused, eyes; bāshpa dūṣitayā vācā = tears, abused, voice; na = not; ucCaiḥ = aloud; shknoti = able; bhāshitum = to talk.

Sugreeva said this much sentence with tears abusing his eyes and his voice is unable talk aloud since it is equally abused by tears. [4-8-29]

Sloka 30

bāṣpa vegam tu sahasā nadī vegam iva āgatam |
dhārayāmāsa dhairyeṇa sugrīvaḥ rāma saṃnidhau || 4-8-30

30. ṣugreeva; sannidhau = in the presence of; Rama; nadī vegam iva = river's, speed, like; sahasā = suddenly; āgatam = came up; bāshpa vegam tu = tears, speed of; dhairyeṇa = courageously; dhārayāmāsa = controlled.

Sugreeva courageously controlled the sudden haste of tears that came up hastily and suddenly like a river in the presence of Rama. [4-8-30]

Sloka 31

sa nigṛhya tu tam bāṣpam pramṛjya nayane śubhe |
viniḥśvasya ca tejasvī rāghavam punarūcivān || 4-8-31

31. tejasvī = bright one; saḥ = he; tam bāshpam = those, tears; nigṛihya tu = controlled; pramṛijya shubhe nayane = on wiping out, his fortunate, eyes; vi nisḥvasya ca = verily, sighed, also; rāghavam vākyam punaḥ ūcivān = to Raghava, sentence, again started to speak.

He that bright Sugreeva controlled those tears, and on wiping his fortunate eyes, but sighing heavily he again started to speak this sentence to Raghava. [4-8-31]

Sloka 32

purā aham valinā rāma rājyāt svāt avaropitaḥ |
paruṣāṇi ca saṃśrāvya nirdhūto asmi balīyasā || 4-8-32

32. purā = earlier; aham = I was; balīyasā valinā svāt rājyāt avaropitaḥ = by forceful, Vali, from my, kingdom, dismounted [ deposed]; paruṣāṇi ca samshrāvya nirdhūtaḥ asmi = harsh words, also, made to listen [spoke by him,] shoved off [banished,] I am.

"Earlier he that forceful Vali deposed me from my kingdom, oh Rama, and speaking harsh words he even banished me. [4-8-32]

Sloka 33

hṛtā bhāryā ca me tena prāṇebhyo api garīyasī |
suhṛdaḥ ca madīyā ye saṃyatā bandhaneṣu te || 4-8-33

33. tena = by him; prāṇebhyaḥ api garīyasī = of lives, even, loftier; me bhāryā ca hṛitā = my, wife, is also, stolen; madīyā ye suhṛidaḥ = my, those, sympathisers are thee; [te = they are]; bandhaneṣu samyatā = in prisons, tied down.

"ṃy wife who is loftier than my lives is stolen by him, and those that are my sympathisers they are also captivated and imprisoned by him. [4-8-33]

Sloka 34

yatnavān ca sa duṣṭātmā mad vināśāya rāghava |
bahuśaḥ tat prayuktāḥ ca vānarā nihatā mayā || 4-8-34

34. Raghava; saḥ duṣṭa atmā = he that, evil, minded one; mat vināshāya = for my, annihilation; yatnavān ca = he tried, also; bahushaḥ = many times; tat = by him; prayuktāḥ ca = deployed, also; vānarāḥ = monkeys [combatants]; nihatā mayā = are killed, by me.

"He that very evil minded one tried many times to annihilate me, and Raghava, I killed those combatants monkeys that are deployed by Vali to kill me. [4-8-34]

Sloka 35

śaṃkayā etayā aham ca dṛṣṭvā tvām api rāghava |
na upasarpāmi aham bhīto bhaye sarve hi bibhyati || 4-8-35

35. Rāghava; aham ca = I, too; etayā = with the same; shankayā tve = doubt; tvām api iha dṛiṣṭvā = you, even, here, on seeing; na upasarpāmi = not, I neared you; bhaye sarve bibhyati hi = in fear, all, will be fearsome, isn't it.

"With the same doubt I did not even near you when I saw you... in fear everything will be fearsome, isn't it... [4-8-35]

Sloka 36

kevalam hi sahāyā me hanumat pramukhāstvime |
ataḥ aham dhārayāmi adya prāṇān kṛcchra gataḥ api san || 4-8-36

36. kevalam ime hanumat pramukhāḥ = just, these, ḥanuma, important ones; me sahāyā = my, helping hands; ataḥ aham kṛicchra gataḥ api san = thereby, I am, in tribulations, gone in, though, I am; dhārayāmi prāṇān adya = I bear, my lives, even today.

"Though I am engulfed in tribulations I still bear my lives even today due to these important monkeys like Hanuma and others, who are the only helping hands of mine. [4-8-36]

Sloka 37

ete hi kapayaḥ snigdhā mām rakṣanti samantataḥ |
saha gacchanti gantavye nityam tiṣṭhanti ca sthite || 4-8-37

37. snigdhā = friendly; ete kapayaḥ mām rakshanti samantataḥ = these, monkeys, me, guarding, from all over; gantavye saha gacchanti nityam sthite tiṣṭhanti ca = while I go, along with, they go, always, while staying, the stay.

"These are the friendly monkeys guarding me all over, while I go they go along with me, and if I were to stay they too will stay. [4-8-37]

Sloka 38

saṃkṣepaḥ te eṣa me rāma kim uktvā vistaram hi te |
sa me jyeṣṭho ripuḥ bhrātā vālī viśruta pauruṣaḥ || 3-8-38

38. Rama; eṣa me samkshepaḥ = this, is my, brief account; te vistaram uktvā kim = to you, in detail, telling, why [what is the use in elaborating it]; me jyeṣṭhaḥ bhrātā = my, elder, brother; vishruta paurushaḥ = who is renowned, for his intrepidity; saḥ Vali mama ripu = that, Vali, is my, enemy.

"Rama, this is briefly my sad-story and why tell this very broadly to you as it would be too tedious... my elder brother Vali is a renowned one for his intrepidity, and he that Vali alone is my enemy. [4-8-38]

Sloka 39

tad vināśe api me duḥkham pramṛṣṭam syāt anantaram |
sukham me jīvitam caiva tad vināśa nibandhanam || 3-8-39

39. tat vināshe api = his, end, only; me duḥkham pra mṛiṣṭtam syāt = my, distress, is wiped out [end,] it will be; anantaram = later; me sukham jīvitam ca eva = my, quietude, life, also, thus; tat vināsha nibandhanam = his, destruction, subjected to.

"His end ends my distress and my later life and quietude are subjected to his destruction. [4-8-39]

Sloka 40

eṣa me rāma śokāntaḥ śoka ārtena niveditaḥ |
duḥkhitaḥ sukhitaḥ vā api sakhyuḥ nityam sakhā gatiḥ || 3-8-40

40. shoka ārtena = by grief, stricken one; me eṣa shoka antaḥ = this, misery's, end, [end for my misery]; niveditaḥ = is submitted; sakhyuḥ duḥkhitaḥ sukhitaḥ vā api = for a friend, saddened, gladdened, or, even if; nityam sakhā gatiḥ = at any time, friend, is recourse.

"Grief stricken such as I am, I have submitted as to how my misery ends, whether one is gladdened or saddened he has recourse only to his friend." So said Sugreeva to Rama. [4-8-40]

Sloka 41

śrutvā etat ca vacaḥ rāmaḥ sugrīvam idam abravīt |
kim nimittam abhūt vairam śrotum icchāmi tattvataḥ || 3-8-41

41. Rama; etat vacaḥ shrutvā = all this, words, on hearing; sugrīvam = to ṣugreeva; idam abravīt = this, said; kim nimittam = what, for; abhūt vairam = chanced, enmity; tattvataḥ shrotum icchāmi = in its reality, to listen, interested I am.

Hearing all that is spoken by Sugreeva, Rama asked, "What for chanced the enmity? I am interested to listen, in its reality...[4-8-41]

Sloka 42

sukham hi kāraṇam śrutvā vairasya tava vānara |
ānantaryad vidhāsyāmi saṃpradhārya balābalam || 3-8-42

42. vānara = oh, monkey; tava vairasya kāraṇam shrutvā = yours, of enmity, cause, on listening; bala abalam sampradhārya = strength and weakness, on deciding; ānantaryāt = after a proper thought; sukham vidhāsyāmi = easily, I will enforce [do the needful.]

"On knowing the cause of your enmity, oh, monkey, and after a proper thought, and deciding upon the strength and weakness of the cause of enmity, or of the enmity itself, I will do the needful very easily. [4-8-42]

Sloka 43

balavān hi mama amarṣaḥ śrutvā tvām avamānitam |
vardhate hṛdaya utkampī prāvṛḍ vega iva aṃbhasaḥ || 3-8-43

43. shrutvā = on hearing; tvām = you are; avamānitam = insulted; utkampī = that can flutter; mama = my; hṛidaya = heart; balavān = intense; amarṣaḥ = anger [is coming to pass, like]; ambhasaḥ prāvṛiD vega iva varthate = of waters, rainy season's, speediness, like, increasing.

"On hearing that you are insulted my intense anger that can flutter my heartbeat is intensifying like the intensifying speed of waters in rainy reason. [4-8-43]

Sloka 44

hṛṣṭaḥ kathaya visrabdho yāvat āropyate dhanuḥ |
sṛṣṭaḥ ca hi mayā bāṇo nirastaḥ ca ripuḥ tava || 3-8-44

44. mayā = by me; yāvat = before; dhanuḥ āropyate = bow, is aimed; bāṇaḥ sṛiṣṭaḥ ca = arrow, is released; tava ripuḥ nirastaḥ ca = your, enemy, become helpless [ he dies]; [within that time]; hṛiṣṭaḥ visrabdhaḥ kathaya = gladly, in belief, you tell.

"Be glad to say in belief, Sugreeva, before I take aim with my bow, and even before I release the arrow, and even before silencing your enemy." Rama assured Sugreeva in this way. [4-8-44]

Sloka 45

evam uktaḥ tu sugrīvaḥ kākutsthena mahātmanā |
praharṣam atulam lebhe caturbhiḥ saha vānaraiḥ|| 3-8-45

45. mahātmanā kākutsthena evam uktaḥ tu = by great souled, Rama, thus, when said; sugrīvaḥ = he that ṣugreeva; caturbhiḥ vānaraiḥ saha = with four, vanara-s, along with; praharṣam atulam lebhe = rejoice, very much, obtained.

Thus said by the great souled Rama, Sugreeva is very much rejoiced along with four of vanara-s available in his attendance. [4-8-45]

Sloka 46

tataḥ prahṛ^iṣṭa vadanaḥ sugrīvaḥ lakṣmaṇāgraje |
vairasya kāraṇam tattvam ākhyātum upacakrame || 3-8-46

46. tata = then; prahṛiṣṭa vadanaḥ = with pleased, countenance; ṣugreeva; Lakshmana agraje = to Lakshmana's elder, to Rama; vairasya kāraṇam = enmity's, cause; tattvam = truthfully; ākhyātum = to tell; upacakrame = started.

Then with a pleased countenance Sugreeva started to tell Rama about the cause of the enmity, it all it truthfulness. [4-8-46]

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye kiṣkindha kāṇḍe aṣṭamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 8th chapter in Kishkindha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
