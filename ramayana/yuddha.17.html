Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 17
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Vibhishana together with four of his companions reach Rama's place. Halting in the sky itself, Vibhishana asks Rama to give a refuge to him. Sugriva tells Rama that he cannot trust Vibhishana the demon. Then Rama asks for opinion of the leaders in his group. Angada, sharabha, Jambavan and ṃainda expressed their apprehensions to accept Vibhishana. But Hanuman says that Vibhishana need not be doubted because he thinks that Vibhishana understands the merits in Rama and demerits in Ravana. Hanuman further adds that Vibhishana is approaching Rama for refuge because Vibhishana is desirous of obtaining the kingdom of demons. Hanuman requests Rama to take his final decision on the matter.

Sloka 1

iti uktvā paruṣam vākyam rāvaṇam rāvaṇa anujaḥ |
ājagāma muhūrtena yatra rāmaḥ salakṣmaṇaḥ ||6-17-1

1. uktvā = having spoken; parusham = harsh; vākyam = words; iti = thus; rāvaṇam = to Ravana; rāvaṇānujaḥ = Vibhishana; ājagāma = arrived; muhūrtena = almost immediately; yatra = where; ramaḥ = Rama was; salakshmaṇaḥ = together with Lakshmana.

Having spoken the aforesaid harsh words to Ravana, Vibhishana arrived almost immediately to the place where Rama was together with Lakshmana.

Sloka 2

tam meru śikhara ākāram dīptām iva śata hradām |
gaganastham mahīsthās te dadṛśur vānara adhipāḥ ||6-17-2

2. te = those; vānarādhipāḥ = leaders of monkeys; mahīsthāḥ = standing on ground; dadṛishuḥ = saw; tam = him; merushikarākāram = resembling the peak of mount ṃeru; dīptam = and shining; shatahradām iva = like a thunder bolt; gaganastham = in the sky.

Those leaders of monkeys standing on the ground saw Vibhishana, resembling the peak of ṃount ṃeru and shining like a thunderbolt in the sky.

Sloka 3

te cāpyanucarāstasya catvāro bhīmavikramāḥ |
te.api varmāyudhopetā bhūṣaṇottamabhūṣitāḥ ||6-17-3

3. tasya = his; chatvāraḥ = four; te = those; anuchārāḥ api = companions also; bhīma vikramāḥ = of terrific prowess; (where there); teapi = they also; varmāyudhopetāḥ = were wearing armor and weapons; bhūshhaṇottamabhūshhitāḥ = and adorned with excellent ornaments.

His four companions of terrific prowess were also there with him. They were wearing weapons and armour and were adorned with excellent ornaments.

Sloka 4

sa ca meghācalaprakhyo vajrāyudhasamaprabhaḥ |
varāyudhadharo vīro divyābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ ||6-17-4

4. sa cha = that Vibhishana also; meghāchaprakhyaḥ = resembling a mass of cloud; vajrāyudhasamaprabhaḥ = the equal of the God who wields the thunderbolt; vīraḥ = and valiant man; varāyudhadharaḥ = wearing excellent weapons; divyābharaṇa bhūshhitaḥ = and adorned with wonderful jewels.

Vibhishana also resembled a mass of cloud, the equal of the God who wields the thunderbolt. He was holding excellent weapons and was adorned with wonderful jewels.

Sloka 5

tam ātma pancamam dṛṣṭvā sugrīvo vānara adhipaḥ |
vānaraiḥ saha durdharṣaḥ cintayām āsa buddhimān ||6-17-5

5. sugrivāḥ = ṣugriva; durdarshhaḥ = who was difficult to be overpowered; vīryavān = a valiant person; vānarādhipaḥ = and the king of monkeys; dṛishhṭvā = having seen; tam = him; ātmapaṇchamam = being himself the fifth one along with four others; chintayāmāsa = became thoughtful; vānaraiḥ saha = along with monkeys.

Sugriva the valiant of monkeys, who was difficult to be overpowered, having seen Vibhishana, the fifth one along with four others, became thoughtful along with monkeys.

Sloka 6

cintayitvā muhūrtam tu vānarāṃs tān uvāca ha |
hanūmat pramukhān sarvān idam vacanam uttamam ||6-17-6

6. chintayitrā = having thought; muhūrtam = for a moment; uvācha ha = spoke; idam = these; uttamam = sterling; vachanam = words; sarvān = to all; tān = those; vānarām = monkeys; hanumatpramukhān = and to ḥanuman in particular.

Having thought for a moment, Sugriva spoke these sterling words to all those monkeys and to Hanuman in particular.

Sloka 7

eṣa sarva āyudha upetaḥ caturbhiḥ saha rākṣasaiḥ |
rākṣaso abhyeti paśyadhvam asmān hantum na saṃśayaḥ ||6-17-7

7. pashyadhvam = see; eshhaḥ rākshhasaḥ = this demon; sarvāyudhopetah = possessing all types of weapons; abhyeti = coming; chaturbhiḥ rākshhasaiḥ saha = along with four demons. na = ṭhere is no; samshayaḥ = no doubt; hantum = that; (he is coming) to kill; asmān = us."

"See this demon possessing all types of weapons, coming along with four demons. There is no doubt that he is coming to kill us."

Sloka 8

sugrīvasya vacaḥ śrutvā sarve te vānara uttamāḥ |
sālān udyamya śailāmḥ ca idam vacanam abruvan ||6-17-8

8. shrutvā = hearing; sugrivasya = ṣugriva's; vachaḥ = words; sarve = all; te = those; vānarottamāḥ = excellent monkeys; udyamya = lifted; shālān = trees; shailāmshcha = and mountains; abruvan = and spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words:

Hearing Sugriva's words, all those excellent monkeys lifted trees and mountains and spoke as follows:

Sloka 9

śīghram vyādiśa no rājan vadhāya eṣām durātmanām |
nipatantu hatāḥ ca ete dharaṇyām alpa jīvitāḥ ||6-17-9

9. rājan = O;king! Vyādisha = order; shīghram = swiftly; vadhāya = for killing; eshhām = them; durātmām = the wicked ones; hatāḥ = Being killed; alpachetanāḥ = these fools; nipatantiyāvat = will drop; dharaṇyām = to the ground."

"Do you swiftly order us to slay these wicked doers, O, king? Let us strike these fools down so that they drop to the ground!"

Sloka 10

teṣām sambhāṣamāṇānām anyonyam sa vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
uttaram tīram āsādya khastha eva vyatiṣṭhata ||6-17-10

10. saḥ vibhīshhaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; āsādya = who reached; uttaram = the northern; tīram = shore; vyatishhṭhata = was stationed; svasthaheva = indeed composedly; teshhām = while those monkeys; sabhāshhamāṇām = speak together; anyonyam = among one another.

While the monkeys were speaking thus among one another, Vibhishana had reached the northern shore and indeed coolly halted there.

Sloka 11

sa uvāca ca mahāprājñaḥ svareṇa mahatā mahān |
sugrīvam tāmḥ ca samprekṣya khastha eva vibhīṣaṇaḥ ||6-17-11

11. saḥ vibhīsssaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; mahā prāG^yah = that highly intelligent; mahān = and the great; samprekshhya = seeing; sugrīvam = ṣugriva; tāmshcha = and those monkeys; khasthaḥ eva = halting in the sky itself; uvācha = spoke; mahatā = with a loud; svareṇa = voice.

That great and highly intelligent Vibhishana, halting in the sky itself, saw Sugriva and those monkeys and spoke to them in a loud voice (as follows):

Sloka 12

rāvaṇo nāma durvṛtto rākṣaso rākṣasa īśvaraḥ |
tasya aham anujo bhrātā vibhīṣaṇa iti śrutaḥ ||6-17-12

12. rākshhasaḥ = there is a demon; rāvaṇonāma = named Ravana; rākshhaseshvaraḥ = the king of demons; durvṛittaḥ = having a bad conduct; aham = I; tasya anujaḥ bhrātāḥ = am his younger brother; shrutaḥ = known; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = as Vibhishana"

"There is a demon called Ravana, the king of demons, having a bad conduct. I am his younger brother, known as Vibhishana."

Sloka 13

tena sītā jana sthānādd hṛtā hatvā jaṭāyuṣam |
ruddhvā ca vivaśā dīnā rākṣasībhiḥ surakṣitā ||6-17-13

13. hatvā = killing; jaṭāyushham = a bird called Jatayu; tena = by himself; sīta = ṣita; hṛitā = was taken away; janasthānāt = from janasthana; ruddhā = and hold captive; vivashā = against her will; dīnā = and unfortunate ṣita; surakshhitā = is well guarded; rākshhasībhiḥ = by female titans."

"It is Ravana who, having killed a bird Jatayu, took away Sita from Janasthana.That unfortunate lady is held captive against her will and she is now amidst the female titans who guard her jealously"

Sloka 14

tam aham hetubhir vākyair vividhaiḥ ca nyadarśayam |
sādhu niryātyatām sītā rāmāya iti punaḥ punaḥ ||6-17-14

14. aham = I;nyadarshayam = persuaded; tam = him; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; vivdhaiḥ = by diverse; vākyaih = words; hetubhiḥ = and arguments; iti = that ; sītā = ṣita; niryātyatām = be restored ; sādhu = smoothly; rāmāya = to Rama.

"I persuaded Ravana again and again by my diverse words and arguments to restore Sita smoothly to Rama.

Sloka 15

sa ca na pratijagrāha rāvaṇaḥ kāla coditaḥ |
ucyamāno hitam vākyam viparīta iva auṣadham ||6-17-15

15. saḥ rāvaṇaḥ = that Ravana; kālachoditah = impelled by fate; na pratijagrāha = did not accept ; hitam = the sage;vākyam = advice; uchyamānām = uttered; (by me); oushadham = (as not accepting) a medicine; viparītaḥ = by a perverted person."

"That Ravana, impelled by fate, did not receive my sage advice, as a perverted person does not accept his prescribed medicine"

Sloka 16

so aham paruṣitas tena dāsavac ca avamānitaḥ |
tyaktvā putrāmḥ ca dārāmḥ ca rāghavam śaraṇam gataḥ ||6-17-16

16. parushhitaḥ = reviled; tena = by him; avamānitaḥ = and humiliated; dāsavat = as a slave; saḥ aham; I; as such; tyaktvā = leaving; putramshcha = my sons; dāramshcha = and my wife; sharaṇamgataḥ = have come to take refuge; rāghavam = with Rama."

"Reviled by him and humiliated as a slave, I, leaving my sons and my wife, have come to take refuge with Rama."

Sloka 17

nivedeayata mām kṣipram rāghavāya mahātmane |
sarva loka śaraṇyāya vibhīṣaṇam upasthitam ||6-17-17

17. nivedayata = inform; kshhipram = immediately; rāghavāya = to Rama; mahātmane = the high soled; sarva lokasharaṇyāya = and the refuge of all the worlds; mām = of me; vibhīshhaṇam = Vibhishana; upasthitam = having come here."

"Inform immediately to high soled Rama, the protector of all the worlds, that I, Vibhishana, have come here."

Sloka 18

etattu vacanam śrutvā sugrīvo laghu vikramaḥ |
lakṣmaṇasya agrato rāmam samrabdham idam abravīt ||6-17-18

18. sugrivaḥ = ṣugriva; laghuvikramaḥ = having a swift pace; shrutvā = hearing; etat = these; rachanam = words; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; samrabdhataram = more hurriedly; rāmam = to Rama; agrataḥ = in front; lakshmaṇasya = of Lakshmana.

The swift-paced Sugriva, having heard the words of Vibhishana, spoke more hurriedly to Rama, in front of Lakshmana as follows:

Sloka 19

praviṣṭaḥ śatru sainyam hi prāptaḥ śatruratarkitaḥ |
nihanyādanntaram labdhvā ulūko vāyasāniva ||6-17-19

19. prāptah = belonging; shatrusainyam = to the enemy force; shatruḥ = an adversary; atarkitaḥ = who unexpectedly; pravishhṭaḥ hi = has indeed come; nihanyāt = to kill us; labdhvā = getting; antaram = a first opportunity; vāyasāniva = like killing the crows; ulūkaḥ = by an owl."

"Belonging to the enemy force, here is an adversary taking us unawares, who unexpectedly has come here to slay us at the first opportunity like an owl destroying crows!"

Sloka 20

mantre vyūhe naye cāre yukto bhavitumarhasi |
vānarāṇām ca bhadram te pareṣām ca paramtapa ||6-17-20

20. paramtapa = O; annihilator of enemies! arhasi = You are apt; bhavitum = to become; yuktaḥ = aware; mantre = of the design; vyūhe = distribution; naye = leading of the army; chāre = and the secret service; vānarāṇāmcha = of the monkeys; pareshhāmcha = and also your foes; bhadram = ṃay good come; te = to you."

"O, annihilator of enemies! You ought to be aware of the design, distribution, leading of the army and the secret service of the monkeys and also of your foes. ṃay good come to you!"

Sloka 21

antardhanagatāhyete rākṣasāḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ |
śūrāśca nikṛtijñāśca teṣām jātu na viśvaset ||6-17-21

21. ete rākshhasāḥ = these demons; kāmarūpiṇaḥ = can assume any form at will; antardhānagatāḥ = and can disappear; shūrāḥ = they are valiant; nikṛitij^ṇāshcha = and know how to be deceitful; na vishvaset = we can not trust; teshhām = them; jātu = at any time."

"These demons can assume any form at will and can disappear. They are valiant and deceitful. We can not trust them at any time."

Sloka 22

praṇīdhī rākṣasendrasya rāvaṇasya bhavedayam |
anupraviśya so.a smāsu bhedam kuryānna saṃśayaḥ ||6-17-22

22. ayam = he; bhavet = may be; praṇidhiḥ = a spy; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rākshhasendrasya = the king of demons. ṣaḥ = he; anupravishya = will succeed to become a member; asmāsu = among us; kuryāt = and create; bhedam = a controversy; na samshayaḥ = no doubt."

"He may be a spy of Ravana the king of demons. He will succeed to become a member among us and create differences. There is no doubt."

Sloka 23

athavā svayamevaiṣa cidramāsādya buddhimān |
anupraviśya viśvaste kadācitpraharedapi ||6-17-23

23. athavā = otherwise; sah = he; buddhimān = the intelligent; svayameva = himself on his own; āsādya = can get into possession of; chchhidram = a weak point; anupravishya = after becoming a member among us; praharedapi kadāchit = can at any time strike; vishvaste = at you having the trust."

"Otherwise he , intelligent by himself, can get into possession of a weak point in us. Having first gained our confidence by craft, he may even at any time attack us."

Sloka 24

mitrāṭavībalam caiva maulabhṛtyabalam tathā |
sarvametadbalam grāhyam varjayitvā dviṣadbalam ||6-17-24

24. mitrāṭavībalamchaiva = a contingent supplied by friends or an inhabitant of the woods (like ourselves); tathā = and; moula bhṛitya balam = those furnished by hereditary warriors or paid servants; sarvam = all; etat = this; balam = contingent; grāhyam = can be accepted; varjayitvā = leaving off; dvishhadbalam = that furnished by an enemy."

"A contingent supplied by friends or an inhabitant of the woods (like our selves), or furnished by hereditary warriors or paid servants - all these contingents can be accepted but not that furnished by an enemy."

Sloka 25

prakṛtyā rākṣaso hyeṣa bhrātāmitrasya vai prabho |
āgataśca ripoḥ sākṣātkathamasmiṃśca viśvaset ||6-17-25

25. prabho = O; Lord! Eshhaḥ = ḥe; rākshhasohi = is indeed a demon; prakṛityā = by nature; bhrātā = a brother; amitrasya = of an enemy; āgataḥ = who came; sākshhāt = directly; ripoḥ = from an adversary; katham = how; vishvaset = can we keep trust; asmin = in him?"

"O, Lord! He is indeed a demon by nature and a brother of an enemy who came directly from an adversary. How can we keep trust in him?"

Sloka 26

rāvaṇasya anujo bhrātā vibhīṣaṇa iti śrutaḥ |
caturbhiḥ saha rakṣobhir bhavantam śaraṇam gataḥ ||6-17-26

26. anujah bhrātā = the younger brother; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; shrutaḥ = the famous; vibhīshhaṇaḥ rakshhobhiḥ saha = along with four demons; gataḥ = approached; bhavantam = you; sharaṇam = far a refuge."

The younger brother of the famous Ravana, called Vibhishana along with four other demons approached you for refuge."

Sloka 27

rāvaṇena praṇihitam tam avehi vibhīṣaṇam |
tasya aham nigraham manye kṣamam kṣamavatām vara ||6-17-27

27. kshhama vatām = O;the excellent one among men knowing what is right! Avehi = know; tam = that; vibhishhaṇam = Vibhishana; praṇihitam = is being sent; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; manye = I think; tasya = of him; kshhamam = being fit for; nigraham = arrest."

"O, the excellent one among men knowing what is right! Know that Ravana is sending Vibhishana. I opine that Vibhishana is fit for arrest."

Sloka 28

rākṣaso jihmayā buddhyā saṃdiṣṭo ayam upasthitaḥ |
prahartum māyayā canno viśvaste tvayi rāghava ||6-17-28

28. anagha = O; the faultless one! ṣamdishhṭaḥ = being sent; jihmayā = with a crooked; buddhyā = intention;ayam = this; rākshhasāḥ = demon; āgataḥ = came; iha = here; prahartum = to attack; tvayi = you; vishvaste = reposed faith in him; chhannaḥ = remaining hidden; māyayā = by his witchcraft."

"O, the faultless one! Being sent with a crooked intent, this demon came here to attack when you are going to repose faith in him, (remaining hidden by his witchcraft).

Sloka 29

badhyatām eṣa tīvreṇa daṇḍena sacivaiḥ saha |
rāvaṇasya nṛśaṃsasya bhrātā hi eṣa vibhīṣaṇaḥ ||6-17-29

29. eshah vibhīshhaṇaḥ = this Vibhishana; bhrātāhi = is indeed the brother; nṛishamsasya = of cruel; rāvaṇasya = Ravana; eshhaḥ = he; sachivaiḥ = along with his ministers; vadhyatām = be killed; tīvrena = by severe; daṇdena = punishment."

"This Vibhishana is indeed the brother of cruel Ravana. Hence, let him along with his ministers be killed, by imposing severe punishment on them."

Sloka 30

evam uktvā tu tam rāmam samrabdho vāhinī patiḥ |
vākyajño vākya kuśalam tato maunam upāgamat ||6-17-30

30. vākyaj^ṇaḥ = ṣugriva; who knew how to speak; vāhinīpatiḥ = and who was the chief of army; uktvā = spoke; evam = thus; samrabdhaḥ = hurriedly; tam rāmam = to that Rama; vākyakushalam = who was skilled in oratory; tataḥ = and thereafter; upāgamat = entered; into; mounam = silence."

The chief of army Sugriva, who knew how to speak expressed thus hurriedly to Rama, who was skilled in oratory and thereafter entered into silence.

Sloka 31

sugrīvasya tu tad vākyam śrutvā rāmo mahābalaḥ |
samīpasthān uvāca idam hanūmat pramukhān harīn ||6-17-31

31. shrutvā = hearing; tat = those; vākyam = words; sugrivasya = of ṣugriva; mahābalaḥ = the exceedingly strong; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = addressed; idam = in this manner; kapīn = to the monkeys; hanumatpramukhān = in front of ḥanuman; samīpasthān = staying nearby:

Hearing those words of Sugriva, the exceedingly strong Rama addressed as follows to the monkeys in front of Hanuman staying near by:

Sloka 32

yad uktam kapi rājena rāvaṇa avarajam prati |
vākyam hetumad atyartham bhavadbhir api tat śrutam ||6-17-32

32. yat = which; vākyam = words; atyartham = which were very; hetumat = reasonable; uktam = uttered; kapirājena = by ṣugriva; rāvaṇāvarajam prati = about Vibhishana; shrutam = were heard; bhavadbhirapi = by you also."

"The very reasonable words uttered by Sugriva about Vibhishana were heard by you too."

Sloka 33

suhṛdā hi artha kṛcceṣu yuktam buddhimatā satā |
samarthena api saṃdeṣṭum śāśvatīm bhūtim iccatā ||6-17-33

33. samarthena = by an efficient; buddhimatā = and intelligent person; sadā = ever; ichchhatā = desirous; shāshvatīm = of everlasting; bhūtim = prosperity; suhṛdam = of his friends; yuktam = it is befitting; upasamdeshhṭum = to advise well; arthakṛchchhreshhu = in difficult matters."

"An efficient and intelligent person ever desirous of everlasting well being of his friends should properly advise them well in difficult matters. Hence, tell your opinion one by one"

Sloka 34

iti evam paripṛṣṭās te svam svam matam atandritāḥ |
sa upacāram tadā rāmam ūcur hita cikīrṣavaḥ ||6-17-34

34. tadā = then; paripṛishhṭāḥ = asked; etyevam = thus; te = they; atandritāḥ = free from lassitude; priyachikīrshhavaḥ = wishing to do good; sopachāram = and out of their politeness; ūchuḥ = spoke; svam matam = their respective opinion; rāmam = to Rama (as follows)

Thus asked by Rama, those monkeys free from lassitude, wishing to do good and out of their politeness, spoke their respective opinions to Rama as follows:

Sloka 35

ajñātam na asti te kiṃcit triṣu lokeṣu rāghava |
ātmānam pūjayan rāma pṛccasi asmān suhṛttayā ||6-17-35

35. rāghava = O; Rama! nāsti kim cit = ṇothing; aj^ṇātam = is unknown; te = to you; trishhu = in the three; lokeshhu = worlds; pṛichchhasi = you are asking; asmān = us; suhṛittayayā = with a friendly heart; ātmānām = to us."

"O, Rama! Nothing is unknown to you in the three worlds. You are consulting us with a friendly heart, as an honor to us."

Sloka 36

tvam hi satya vrataḥ śūro dhārmiko dṛḍha vikramaḥ |
parīkṣya kārā smṛtimān nisṛṣṭa ātmā suhṛtsu ca ||6-17-36

36. tvam = you; satyavrataḥ = are strictly truthful; shūraḥ = a valiant man; dṛiDhavikramah = of firm fortitude; parīkshhyakārī = take action proper investigation; smṛitimān = having good memory; nisṛishhṭātmācha = and committed in your heart; suhṛitsu = to your friends."

"You are avowed to truth, a valiant man and a righteous man of firm fortitude. You take action only after proper investigation. You have good memory. You are committed in your heart to your friends."

Sloka 37

tasmād eka ekaśas tāvad bruvantu sacivās tava |
hetuto mati sampannāḥ samarthāḥ ca punaḥ punaḥ ||6-17-37

37. tasmāt = hence; tava sachivāḥ = your counselors; matisampannāḥ = who are rich in their minds; tathā = and; samarthāshcha punaḥ = are moreover efficient; bruvantu tavāt = will tell; hetutaḥ = with reason; ekaikashaḥ = one by one."

"Hence, your counselors, who are rich in their minds and are moreover efficient, one by one, will tell their opinion with reason."

Sloka 38

iti ukte rāghavāya atha matimān angado agrataḥ |
vibhīṣaṇa parīkṣā artham uvāca vacanam hariḥ ||6-17-38

38. ukte = having spoken ; iti = thus; atha = then; aṇgadaḥ = Angada; matimān = the intelligent; haviḥ = monkey; vuvācha = spoke; vachanam = words; rāghavāya = to Rama; vibhīshhaṇa parīkshhartham = for examining Vibhishana; agrataḥ = at the first instance."

Having spoken thus by the monkeys, Angada the intelligent monkey told Rama to arrange for examination of Vibhishana before hand."

Sloka 39

śatroḥ sakāśāt samprāptaḥ sarvathā śankya eva hi |
viśvāsa yogyaḥ sahasā na kartavyo vibhīṣaṇaḥ ||6-17-39

39. vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; samprītaḥ = who came; sakāshāt = from the presence of; shatroḥ = an enemy; sarvathāḥ = by all means; tarkyaḥ evahi = is indeed to be doubted; nakartavyaḥ = ḥe is not to be made; vishvāsanīyaḥ = a trustworthy person; sahasā = immediately."

"Vibhishana who came from an enemy is indeed to be doubted by all means. He is not to be made as a trust worthy person so soon."

Sloka 40

cādayitvā ātma bhāvam hi caranti śaṭha buddhayaḥ |
praharanti ca randhreṣu so anarthaḥ sumahān bhavet ||6-17-40

40. shaṭha buddhayaḥ = the deceitful minded; charantihi = move around; chhādayitvā = concealing; ātma bhāvam = their peculiar nature; praharanticha = attack; randhreshhu = at weak places; saḥ = It; bhavet = will produce; sumahān = a very great ; anarthaḥ = evil."

"Deceitful persons move around, concealing their peculiar nature and attack at weak places. They will create a very great misfortune."

Sloka 41

artha anarthau viniścitya vyavasāyam bhajeta ha |
guṇataḥ samgraham kuryād doṣatas tu visarjayet ||6-17-41

41. bhajeta = one must wait upon; vyavasāyam = a decision; vinishchitya = after ascertaining; arthānarthon = the pros and cons; samgraham = acceptance; kuryāt = has to be done; guṇataḥ = according to quality; doshhatastu = but faults; visarjayet = should be given up.

"One must take a decision, after ascertaining the pros and cons of it. One should take up the action, if there is an advantage and reject it, if it is faulty."

Sloka 42

yadi doṣo mahāṃs tasmiṃs tyajyatām aviśankitam |
guṇān vā api bahūn jñātvā samgrahaḥ kriyatām nṛpa ||6-17-42

42. nṛipa = O; king! mahān doshhaḥyadi = If there are great faults; tasmin = in him; tyajatām = let him be rejected; avishaṇkitam = undoubtedly; j^ṇātvāvāpi = If we recognize; bahūn = many; guṇām = good qualities; kriyatām samgrahaḥ = let him be accepted.

"O, king! If there are great faults in him, let him be rejected undoubtedly. If we recognize many good qualities in him, let him be accepted."

Sloka 43

śarabhas tv atha niścitya sārtham vacanam abravīt |
kṣipram asmin nara vyāghra cāraḥ pratividhīyatām ||6-17-43

43. atha = thereafter; sharabhastu = ṣharabha on his part; abravīt = spoke; sārtham = meaningful; nishchitya = and decisive; vachanam = words; "naravyāghra = O; tiger among men! chāraḥ pratividhīyatām = Let a spy be sent; kshhipram = immediately; asmin = for him."

Thereafter Sharabha on his part spoke the following meaningful and decisive words: "O, tiger among men! Let a spy be sent to shadow him"

Sloka 44

praṇidhāya hi cāreṇa yathāvat sūkṣma buddhinā |
parīkṣya ca tataḥ kāryo yathā nyāyam parigrahaḥ ||6-17-44

44. praṇidhāya = by sending out a spy; parīkshhyacha = and investigating; yathāvat = suitably; sūkshhma buddhinā = by a keenly intellectual; gūDhachāraṇa = spy; tataḥ = and then; parigrahaḥ = acceptance; kāryaḥ = to be done; yathānyāyam = as per justice."

"By sending out a spy and causing a suitable investigation by a keenly intellectual spy, he can then be accepted as per justice."

Sloka 45

jāmbavāṃs tv atha samprekṣya śāstra buddhyā vicakṣaṇaḥ |
vākyam vijñāpayām āsa guṇavad doṣa varjitam ||6-17-45

45. atha = then; vichakshhaṇaḥ = the discerning; jāmbavāmstu = Jambavān on his part; samprekshhya = perceiving; shāstra buddhyā = through his leaning derived from scriptures; vij^ṇ^āpayāmāsa = advised; guṇavat = qualitative; doshhavarjitam = fault-less; vākyam = words:

Then, the discerning Jambavan on his part, perceiving the matter through his learning derived from scriptures, advised the following fault-less and qualitative words:

Sloka 46

baddha vairāc ca pāpāc ca rākṣasa indrād vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
adeśa kāle samprāptaḥ sarvathā śankyatām ayam ||6-17-46

46. vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; samprāptaḥ = came; rākshhasendrāt = from Ravana the king of demons; baddhavairāchcha = who has contracted hostility with you; pāpāt = and who is sinful; adeshakāle = at a very wrong place and time; ayam = ḥe; shṇkyatām = is to be suspected; sarvathā = by all means."

"This Vibhishana came from sinful Ravana who has contracted hostility with you and that too at a wrong place and time. He is to be suspected by all means."

Sloka 47

tato maindas tu samprekṣya naya apanaya kovidaḥ |
vākyam vacana sampanno babhāṣe hetumattaram ||6-17-47

47. tataḥ = then; maindastu = ṃainda; nayāpanayakovidaḥ = skilled in discriminating good and bad conduct; samprekshhe = observed carefully; babhāshhe = and spoke; vāchana sampannaḥ = in his perfect oratory; hetumattaram = highly reasonable; vākyam = words.

Then ṃainda, skilled in discriminating good and bad conduct in others observed the matter carefully and spoke in his perfect oratory in the following highly reasonable words:

Sloka 48

anuho nāma tasya eṣa rāvaṇasya vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
pṛccyatām madhureṇa ayam śanair nara vara īśvara ||6-17-48

48. narapatīshvara = O; king of kings! Eshhaḥ = ṭhis; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; anujo nāma = is indeed the younger brother; tasya = of that; rāvaṇasya = Ravana; ayam pṛichchhyatam = let him be questioned; shanaiḥ = slowly; madhureṇa = by sweet words."

"O, king of kings! This Vibhishana is indeed the younger brother of that Ravana. Let him be questioned slowly by sweet words"

Sloka 49

bhāvam asya tu vijñāya tatas tattvam kariṣyasi |
yadi dṛṣṭo na duṣṭo vā buddhi pūrvam naraṣabha ||6-17-49

49. nararshhabha = O; the best among men! ViG^yāya = having known; tattavataḥ = actually; asya = his; bhāvam = mind; dushhṭoyadi = whether he is dangerous; na dushhṭovā = or not dangerous; tvam = you; kanishhyasi = should act; buddhipūrvam = according to your feelings."

"O, the best among men! Having read actually his mind whether he is dangerous or not, you should act according to your feelings."

Sloka 50

atha saṃskāra sampanno hanūmān saciva uttamaḥ |
uvāca vacanam ślakṣṇam arthavan madhuram laghu ||6-17-50

50. atha = then; hanumān = ḥanuman; samskāra sampannah = who was well educated; sachivottamaḥ = the best among consellors; uvācha = spoke; shlaklam = smooth; arthavat = meaningful; madhuram = sweet; laghu = and brief; vachanam = words:

Then the well educated Hanuman, the excellent among counselors; spoke the following smooth meaningful, sweet and brief words:

Sloka 51

na bhavantam mati śreṣṭham samartham vadatām varam |
atiśāyayitum śakto bṛhaspatir api bruvan ||6-17-51

51. bṛihaspati rapi = even Brihaspati; bṛivan = while talking; na shaktaḥ = cannot; atishayāyitum = excel; bhavantam = you; matishreshhṭham = who are of an exalted intellect; samartham = powerful; varam = and the foremost; vadatām = of those who are eloquent.

"Even Brihaspati, while talking cannot excel you, who are possessing an exalted intellect. You are powerful and the foremost man among those who are eloquent."

Sloka 52

na vādān na api samgharṣān na ādhikyān na ca kāmataḥ |
vakṣyāmi vacanam rājan yathā artham rāma gauravāt ||6-17-52

52. rājan = O; king! rāma = O;Rama! ṇa: ṇeither; vādāt = for argument; na = nor; samgharshhādapi = for competition(with other counselors); na = nor; ādhikyāt = for superiority; na = nor; kāmatahcha = out of passion; gouravāt = but on account of importance; vakshhyāmi = I am talking; yatārtham = the matter in hand.

"O, king! O, Rama! I am talking neither for argument, nor for competition (with other counselors, nor for superiority, nor out of passion for debate but on account of importance of this matter in hand."

Sloka 53

artha anartha nimittam hi yad uktam sacivais tava |
tatra doṣam prapaśyāmi kriyā na hi upapadyate ||6-17-53

53. prapāshyāmi = Iam perceiving; doshham = a mistake; tatra = in that; yat = which; uktam = was told; sachivaiḥ = by your counselors; arthānarthanimittam = regarding advantages and disadvantages (accruing from Vibhishana); na upapādyatehi = It is not indeed possible; kriyā = for judicious investigation.

"I am perceiving an error in what was advised by your counselors assigned to look into advantages and disadvantages, accruing from accepting Vibhishana. It is not possible for such a judicious investigation into his character."

Sloka 54

ṛte niyogāt sāmarthyam avaboddhum na śakyate |
sahasā viniyogo hi doṣavān pratibhāti me ||6-17-54

54. niyogāt ṛite = without entrusting any work; na shakyate = It is not possible; avabodhum = to understand; sāmarthyam = his ability; pratibhāti = It occurs; me = to me; doshhavān = as a mistake; viniyogaḥ = to entrust any work; sahasā = so soon (to a stranger).

"Without entrusting any work, it is not possible to understand his ability. But at the same time, it occurs to me as a mistake to entrust any work so soon to a stranger."

Sloka 55

cāra praṇihitam yuktam yad uktam sacivais tava |
arthasya asambhavāt tatra kāraṇam na upapadyate ||6-17-55

55. yat = whatever; uktam = was told; tava savachivaih = by your counselors; yuktam = as it was befitting; chāra praṇihitam = to send spies; tatra = that; kāraṇam = action; nopapadyati = is not possible; asambhavāt = due to impracticality; arthasya = of the thing.

"What ever was told by your counselors that it was befitting to send spies to Vibhishana, that action is not possible due to impracticality of the proposition."

Sloka 56

adeśa kāle samprāpta iti ayam yad vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
vivakṣā ca atra me asti iyam tām nibodha yathā mati ||6-17-56

56. itiyat = It has been said; ayam Vibhīshaṇaḥ = that Vibhishana; samprāptaḥ = came; adoshakāle = into a wrong place and time; tatra = In that matter; asti = there is; me = to me; iya vivakshhā = a desire to tell; yathāmati = according to my mind; nibodha = Listen; tām = to it.

"It has been said that Vibhishana came into a wrong place and time. In that matter, I have to express my thought. Listen to it."

Sloka 57

sa eṣa deśaḥ kālaḥ ca bhavati iha yathā tathā |
puruṣāt puruṣam prāpya tathā doṣa guṇāv api ||6-17-57

57. eshha = this; deshhashcha = place; kālashcha = and time; iha = here; bhavati = become; yathā tathā = according to what is right; tathā = thus; prāpya = by obtaining; (in his mind) purushhātpurusham = you are superior to Ravana; guṇadoshhāvapi = and also found merits in you and demerits in the latter.

"This place and time become rightly obtained in his mind , as he has thought that you are superior to Ravana and also found merits in you and demerits in Ravana."

Sloka 58

daurātmyam rāvaṇe dṛṣṭvā vikramam ca tathā tvayi |
yuktam āgamanam tasya sadṛśam tasya buddhitaḥ ||6-17-58

58. yuktam hi = it is indeed befitting; āgamanam = to arrive; atra = at this place and time; dṛishhṭvā = by seeing; vikrmamcha = the prowess; tvayi = in you; tathā = and; dourātmyam = wickedness; rāvaṇe = in Ravana; sadṛisham = It is worthy; tasya = of his ; buddhitaḥ = judgement.

"It is indeed appropriate for him to arrive at this place and time, by seeing the prowess in you and the wickedness in Ravana. It is worthy of his judgment."

Sloka 59

ajñāta rūpaiḥ puruṣaiḥ sa rājan pṛccyatām iti |
yad uktam atra me prekṣā kācid asti samīkṣitā ||6-17-59

59. rājan = O; king! Yat = whatever; uktam = was told; saḥ pṛichchhyatām iti = that let him be questioned; purushhaiḥ = by spies; aG^yātarūpaiḥ = of unknown identity ;me = my; kāchit = certain; prakshhā = view; atra = in this matter; samīkshhitā = after due consideration; asti = is this.

"O, king! Whatever was told by your counselors that let Vibhishana be questioned by spies of unknown identity, my considered view is as follows:

Sloka 60

pṛccyamāno viśanketa sahasā buddhimān vacaḥ |
tatra mitram praduṣyeta mithya pṛṣṭam sukha āgatam ||6-17-60

60. buddhimān = a wise man; pṛichchyemānaḥ = being questioned; sahasā = suddenly; vishaṇ^kena = would be apprehensive; vachaḥ = of the talk; tatra = in those circumstances; sukhāgatam = an easily obtained; mitram = friend; pradushhyeta = becomes faithless; mithyāpṛishhṭam = due to deceitful questioning

"A wise man being questioned suddenly would be apprehensive of that questioning. In those circumstances, an easily obtained friend becomes faithless upon facing a deceitful questioning."

Sloka 61

aśakyaḥ sahasā rājan bhāvo vettum parasya vai |
antaḥ svabhāvair gītais tair naipuṇyam paśyatā bhṛśam ||6-17-61

61. rājan = O; king! Bhṛisham naipuṇyam vinā = without a high skill; pashyatām = of perceiving; antareṇa = between;bhinnaiḥ svaraiḥ = different voices; ashakyam = it is not possible; sahasā = rapidly; boddhum = to comprehend; parasya = the other's; bhāvaḥ = intention.

"O, king! Without possessing a high skill of reading his diversified tones, it is not possible rapidly to comprehend his intention."

Sloka 62

na tvasya bruvato jātu lakṣyate duṣṭa bhāvatā |
prasannam vadanam ca api tasmān me na asti saṃśayaḥ ||6-17-62

62. na dushhṭa bhāvatā = no bad intention; na dṛishyate = is seen; jātu = at all; asya = in his; bruvataḥ = talk; vadanamchāpi = ḥis face is also; prasannam = bright; tasmāt = hence; nāsti = there is no; samshayaḥ = doubt; me = for me.

"I am not seeing any bad intention at all in his talk. His face is also bright. Hence, I do not doubt him."

Sloka 63

aśankita matiḥ svastho na śaṭhaḥ parisarpati |
na ca asya duṣṭā vāgasti tasmān na asti iha saṃśayaḥ ||6-17-63

63. shaṭhaḥ = a deceitful person; na parisarpati = does not approach; ashaṇkamatiḥ = fearlessly; svasthaḥ = and confidently; asya = his; vākcha = expression too; nāsti = is not; dushhṭā = bad; tasmāt = hence; me = to me; nāsti = there is no; samshayaḥ = doubt.

"A deceitful person does not approach so fearlessly and confidently. His expression too is not bad. Hence, there is no doubt to me on him."

Sloka 64

ākāraḥ cādyamāno api na śakyo vinigūhitum |
balādd hi vivṛṇoti eva bhāvam antar gatam nṛṇām ||6-17-64

64. na shakyah = it is not possible; vinigūhitam = to hide; ākārah = expression of the face; chhādyamano. api = even if it is concealed; balata = by force; antargatam = the internal; bhāvam = intent; nṛiṇām = of the persons; vivṛiṇotyeva = certainly gets revealed.

"It is not possible to hide expression of the face, even if it is concealed. By force, the internal intent of the persons certainly gets revealed."

Sloka 65

deśa kāla upapannam ca kāryam kāryavidām vara |
saphalam kurute kṣipram prayogeṇa abhisamhitam ||6-17-65

65. kārya vidām vara = O; the eminently skillful man in work! Kāryam = an action; deshakālopapannamcha = endowed with proper place and time; kurute = transacts; saphalam = successfully; abhisamhitam = if associated; prayogeṇa = with practice; kshhipram = quickly.

"O, the eminently skillful man in work! An action endowed with proper place and time transacts successfully, if it is performed quickly."

Sloka 66

udyogam tava samprekṣya mithyā vṛttam ca rāvaṇam |
vālinaḥ ca vadham śrutvā sugrīvam ca abhiṣecitam ||6-17-66
rājyam prārthayamānaḥ ca buddhi pūrvam iha āgataḥ |
etāvat tu puras kṛtya yujyate tv asya samgrahaḥ ||6-17-67

66;67. samprekshhya = seeing; tava = your; udyogam = perseverance; mithyāvRittam = and improper conduct; rāvaṇām = of Ravana; shrutvā = and hearing; vālinam = about Vali; hatam = having been killed; sugrīvam = and ṣugriva; abhishhechitam = having been anointed as a king;; āgatam = came; iha = here; buddhipūrvam = deliberately; puraskatya = considering; tāvattu = this much alone; tasya = his; samgrahaṇaḥ = acceptance; vidyate = is understandable.

"Seeing your perseverance and the improper conduct of Ravana as well as hearing about Vali having been killed and Sugriva anointed as king, he deliberately came here, with a desire to obtain the kingdom of demons. On consideration of this aspect alone, he is worthy of acceptance by us."

Sloka 68

yathā śakti mayā uktam tu rākṣasasya ārjavam prati |
pramāṇam tu śeṣasya śrutvā buddhimatām vara ||6-17-68

68. buddhimatām vara = O; Rama; the best among the wise! Uktam = It has been told; mayā = by me; yathā shakti = according to my ability; ārjavam prati = about the sincerety; rākshhasasya = of this demon; shrutvā = after hearing this; tvam = you; pramāṇam hi = indeed are the judge; sheshasya = of the issue.

"O, Rama the best among the wise! It has been told by me this according to my ability about the sincerity of this demon. After hearing my words, you are indeed the final judge of the issue."

- - - -

ityārśe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe saptadaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 17th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
