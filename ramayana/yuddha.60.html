Introduction

Ravana returns to Lanka and asks some demons to go to the abode of sleeping Kumbhakarna, to wake him up and request him to come to him. The slumbering Kumbhakarna is roused by the demons under orders of their king. Kumbhakarna, giant in form, sets out on the high way to see Ravana and the monkeys get alarmed to see the giant demon.

Sloka 1

sa praviśya purīm laṅkām rāmabāṇabhayārditaḥ |
bhagnadarpastadā rājā babhūva vyathitendriyaḥ || 6-60-1

1. tadā = then; saḥ = that; rājā = King of Demons; pravishya = after entering; laṇ^kām purīm = the city of Lanka; rāma bāṇabhayārditaḥ = afflicted with the fear of Rama's arrows; bhagnadarpaḥ = his pride humbled; babhūva = became; vyathi tendriyaḥ = troubled in his senses.

Returning to the City of Lanka, Ravana the King of Demons was afflicted with the fear of Rama's arrows, his pride was humbled and his senses troubled.

Sloka 2

mātaṅga iva simhena garuḍeneva pannagaḥ |
abhibhūto.abhavadrājā rāghaveṇa mahātmanā || 6-60-2

2. rājā = that King; abhavat = was; abhibhūtaḥ = overcome; mahātmanā = by the high-souled; rāghaveṇa = Rama; mātaṇ^gaḥ iva = like an elephant; simhena = by a lion; pannagaḥ iva = or like a serpent; garuDena = by an eagle.

The king was overcome by the high-souled Rama, like an elephant is by a lion or a serpent by an eagle.

Sloka 3

brahmadaṇḍapratīkānām vidyuccalitavarcasām |
smatan rāghavabāṇānām vivyathe rākṣaseśvaraḥ || 6-60-3

3. rākṣaseshvaraḥ = the lord of Demons; vivyathe = was maddened; smaran = in recollecting; rāghava bāṇānām = Rama's arrows; brahma daṇ^Dapratīkānām = resembling the Rod of Brahma the Lord of creation; vidyuchchalita varchasām = and possessed of the splendour of lightning.

The Lord of Demons was maddened in recollecting Rama's arrows resembling the Rod of Brahma the Lord of creation and possessed of the splendour of lightning.

Sloka 4

Resting on an excellent and charming throne made of gold, Ravana looked at the demons and spoke the following words:

Sloka 5

sarvam tatkhalu me mogham yattaptam paramam tapaḥ |
yatsamāno mahendreṇa mānuṣeṇa vinirjitaḥ || 6-60-5

5. tat sarvam = all that; paramam = great; tapaḥ = penance; yat = which; taptam = was performed; me = by me; mogham khalu = was indeed in vain; yat = for; mahendreṇa samānaḥ = I who am equal to ṃahendra the Lord of celestials; vinirjitaḥ = have been defeated; mānuṣeṇa = by a mere man!.

"All that great penance performed by me became a waste indeed, for, I who am equal to ṃahendra the Lord of Celestials have been defeated by a mere man!"

Sloka 6

mānuṣebhyo vijānīhi bhayam tvamiti tattathā || 6-60-6

6. idam = these; ghoram = terrible; vākyam = words; brahmaṇaḥ = of Brahma the Lord of creation; iti = saying that; tvam = you; vijānīhi = know; bhayam = of the threat; mānuṣebhyaḥ = from men; upasthitam = appear (true); mām = to me; tat = that ; tathā = is so.

"The terrible words of Brahma the Lord of Creation saying, 'You know of the threat from men' appear true. That is so."

Sloka 7

devadānavagandharvairyakṣarākṣasapannagaiḥ |
avadhyatvam mayā prāptam mānuṣebhyo na yācitam || 6-60-7

7. prāptam = (ṭhe boon) was obtained; mayā = by me; avadhyatvam = for not to be slain; devadānava gandharvaiḥ = by celestials; titans or celestials musicians; yakṣarāṣasa pannagaiḥ = or sam-divine beings or demons or serpent-demons; nayāchitam = but not asked; mānuṣebhyaḥ = from men.

"I sought immunity from death at the hands of celestials, titans or celestials musicians, or semi divine beings or demons or serpent-demons, but f man I made no mention."

Sloka 8

8; 9. rāmam = Rama; dasharathātmajam = the son of Dasaratha; mānuṣam = is the man; manyee = I think; yat = of whom; purā = formerly; anaraṇyena = Anaranya; ikṣvākukula jātena = born of Ikshvaku dynasty; viditam = made me to understand; (staying); rākṣasādhama = O; the worst of demons; kulādhama = and worst of your race!; durmate = O; the wretched one! madvamshe = In my race; utpatsyatihi = will be born; puruṣaḥ = a man; yaḥ = who; nihaniṣyati = will slay; tvām = you; samgrāme = in battle; saputram = with your sons; sāmātyam = ministers; sabalam = army; sāshvasārathim = horses and charioteer.

"Rama the son of Dasaratha is the man I think of whom Anaranya born of Ikshvaku dynasty formerly spoke, saying: 'O, the worst of demons, the worst of your race and the wretched one! In my race will be born a man who will slay you in battle with your sons, ministers, army, horses and charioteer."

Sloka 10

śapto.aham vedavatyā ca yadā sā dharṣitā purā || 6-60-10

10. aham = I; shaptaḥ = was cursed; vedavatyā = by Vedavati; yadā = when; sā = she; dharṣitā = was humiliated; purā = before; sā = that; iyam = same Vedavati; jātā = is born; sītā = as ṣeetha; mahābhāgā = the highly fortunate one; janakanandinī = and the daughter of Janaka.

"Further, I was formerly cursed by Vedavati when she was humiliated by me. The same Vedavati is born as the highly fortunate Seetha the daughter of Janaka."

Sloka 11

umā nandīśvaraścāpi rambhā varuṇakanyakā || 6-60-11

11. yathā = how; umā = parvati the consort of ṣhiva; nandīshvarashchāpi = lord ṇandi the attendant of ṣhiva; rambhā = Rambha wife of ṇala-Kubara; varuṇakanyakā = Varuna's daughter; uktāḥ = told; tat = that; prāptam = has been obtained; mayā = by me; ṛiṣibhāṣitam = the words uttered by sages; na = are not; mithyam = untrue.

"What was predicted by Parvati the consort of Shiva, Nandishvara the attendant of Shiva, Rambha the wife of Nalakubara and Punjikasthala the daughter of Varunda has come to pass! The words of the sages never prove false."

Sloka 12

etadevābhyupāgamya yatnam kartumahārhatha || 6-60-12

12. etat eva = on account of all this; abhyupāgamya = and admitting it; arhathā = effort; iha = in this matter; rākṣasāshchāpi = (Let) the demons; tiṣṭhantu = be stationed; charyāgopura mūrdhasu = on the summit of charya ṃountian.

"On account of all this and admitting it, you should exert yourselves to the uttermost. Let the demons go to the summit of charya ṃountain."

Sloka 13

sa cāpratimagāmbhīryo devadānavadarpahā || 6-60-13
brahmaśāpābhibhūtastu kumbhakarṇo vibodhyatām |

13. vibodhyatām = Awaken; saḥ = that; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; apratimagāmbhīryaḥ = who is without equal in prowess; devadānava darpahā = who humbles the pride of the celestials and demons; brahma shāpābhibhūtaḥ = and on whom the curse of Brahma the Lord of Creation rests.

"Awaken that kumbhakarna, who is without equal in prowess, who humbles the pride of the celestials and demons, and on whom the curse of Brahma the Lord of creation rests."

Sloka 14

samare jitamātmānam prahastam ca niṣūditam || 6-60-14
jñātvā rakṣobalam bhīmamādideśa mahābalaḥ |

14. jJṇātvā = having known (that); prahastam = Prahasta; niṣūditam = being slain; saḥ = and that king; mahābalaḥ = the mighty one; ātmānam = himself; jitam = being defeated; samare = in battle; (that king); ādidesha = issued his commands; bhīmam = to the dreadful; balam = army (saying)

Having known that Prahasta being slain and knowing himself to have been defeated in the battle, Ravana issued his commands tot he dreadful army, saying:

Sloka 15

dvāreṣu yatnaḥ kriyatām prākāraścādhiruhyatām || 6-60-15
nidrāvaśasamāviṣṭaḥ kumbhakarṇo vibodhyatām |

15. kriyatām = guard; dvāreṣu = the gates; yatnaḥ = with care; adhiruhyatām = and ascend; prākārashcha = the ramparts; vibodhyatām = rouse; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; nidrāvasha samāviṣṭaḥ = who is slumbering soundly.

"Guard the gates with utmost care. ṃan the ramparts. Rouse Kumbhakarna who is slumbering soundly."

Sloka 16

sukham svapiti niścintaḥ kālopahatacetanaḥ || 6-60-16

16. rākṣasaḥ = Kumbhakarna; svapiti = is sleeping; sukham = happily; kālopahata chetanaḥ = his understannding vitiated by lust; nishchintaḥ = free from all anxieties; (the demons sleeps); nava = for mine; sapta = seven; dasha = ten; aṣṭaucha = or eight; māsān = months.

"Kumbhakarna is sleeping happily, his understanding vitiated by lust and free from all anxieties. The demon sleeps for nine, seven, ten or eight months.

Sloka 17

mantram kṛtvā prasupto.ayamiastu navame.ahani || 6-60-17
tam tu bodhayata kṣipram kumbhakarṇam mahābalam |

17. kṛitvā = having me; mantram = in consultation (with me); navame = on the nineth; ahani = day; itam = before; ayam = he; prasuptaḥ = has since fallen asleep; bodhayata = Awaken; kshīpram = quickly; tam = that; mahābalam = mighty; kumbhakarṇam = Kumbhakarna.

"Having deliberated with me nine days ago, he has since fallen asleep. Awaken quickly that mighty Kumbhakarna."

Sloka 18

sa hi samkhye mahābāhuḥ kakudam sarvarakṣasām || 6-60-18
vānarān rājaputrau ca kṣiprameva haniṣyati |

18. saḥ = Kumbhakarna; kakudam = the foremost; sarvarākṣasām = of all demons; mahābāhuḥ = and the mighty armed; haniṣyati = will kill kṣiprameva = quickly; vānarān = the monkeys; rājaputraucha = and the princes; samkhye = in the battle.

"The mighty armed Kumbhakarna, the foremost of all demons will kill the monkeys and the two princes at once in the battle."

Sloka 19

19. eṣaḥ kumbhakarṇaḥ = this Kumbhakarna; mukhyaḥ = the chief; sarva rakṣasām = among all the demons; param = a great; ketuḥ = mark; samkhye = in the battle; sadā = is always; shete = sleeping; mūDhāḥ = a stupid; rataḥ = being fond; grāmya sukhe = of a rustic comfort.

"This Kumbhakarna, the chief among all the demons and a great mark in the battle, being fond of a rustic comfort, is always sleeping stupidly."

Sloka 20

20. kumbhakarṇe = (If) Kumbhakarna; vibodhite = is awaken; na bhaviṣyati = there will be no; shokaḥ = grief; me = for me; abhinirastasya = who has been defeated; rāmeṇa = by Rama; asmin = in this; sudāruṇe = highly terrific; samgrāme = battle.

"If Kumbhakarna is awaken, there will be no worry for me, who stand defeated by Rama in this highly terrific battle."

Sloka 21

kim kariṣyāmyaham tena śakratulyabalena hi || 6-60-21

21. kim = what; aham kariṣyāmi = will I do; tena = with him; yaḥ = who; shakratulya = Lord of Celestials; na kalpate = cannot; sāhāyya = help; īdṛishe = in such; ghore = a dreadful; vyasane = calamity?

"What is the use of him, who possessing a strength equal to that of Indra the Lord of celestials, cannot help me in such a dreadful catastrophe?"

Sloka 22

te tu tadvacanam śrutvā rākṣasendrasya rākṣasāḥ || 6-60-22

Hearing those words of Ravana, those demons very briskly went to the abode of Kumbhakarna.

Sloka 23

23; 24. pravishya = entering; ramyām = the beautiful; kumbhakarṇaguhām = cave of Kumbhakarna; mahādvārām = with a large door; yoganāyatām = having an area of Yojana (About 9 miles x 9 miles); sarvato = on all sides; puṣpagandhapravāhinīm = bearing a floral perfume; avadhūtāḥ = (thought) shaken off; kumbhakarṇasya = by Kumbakarna's; niḥshvāsāt = breaths; mahābalāḥ = the demons; of mighty strength; yatnāt = with exertion; pratiṣṭhamānāḥ = remained stable; kṛichchhreṇa = with difficulty; pravivishuḥ = went into; guhām = the cave.

Entering the beautiful cave of Kumbhakarna, with a large door, having an area of a Yojana on all sides and bearing a floral perfume, the demons of mighty strength, though shaken off by Kumbhakarna's breaths, remained stable forcefully with difficulty and went into the cave.

Sloka 25

25. pravishya = entering; tām = that; ramyām = beautiful; guhām = cave; ratna kāṇchana kuṭṭimām = inlaid with diamonds and gold; nairṛita ryāghrāḥ = those tigers among demons; dadṛishuḥ = saw; bhīmavikramam = Kumbhakarna of terrific prowess; shayanām = who was sleeping.

Entering that beautiful cave inlaid with diamonds and gold, those tigers among demons beheld Kumbhakarna, of terrific prowess, who was sleeping.

Sloka 26

They together tried to awaken Kumbhakarna who was sleeping nastily like a spread-out mountain in a great slumber.

Sloka 27

bhīmanāsāpuṭam tam tu pātālavipulānanam || 6-60-28
śayane nyastasarvāṅgam medorudhiragandhinam |
kāñcanāṅgadanaddhāṅgam medorudhiragandhinam || 6-60-29

27; 28; 29. ūrdhva lomāṇchita tanum = his limbs covered with down which stood on end; shvasantam = breathing; pannagamiva = like a serpent; shayānam = as he slept; bhīmavikramam = shayānam = as he slept; bhīmavikramam = Kumbhakarna of irresistible valour; bhrāmayantam = brandishing; viniḥshvāsaiḥ = dreadful snores; bhīmanāsāpuṭam = his nostrils being horrible; pātāla vipulānanam = and his mouth a gaping hell; nyasta sarvāṇgam = stretched to his full length of body; shayane = while lying down; medorudhiragandhinam = giving forth an odour of marrow and blood; kāṇ^chanāṇga danaddhāṇ^gam = his limbs adorned with goldedn armlets; kirīṭena = wearing a diadem; arkavarchasam = as bright as the sun; tam kumbhakarṇam = that Kumbhakarna; nairṛita vyāghram = the lion among demons; arindanam = and the slayer of his foes; dadṛishuḥ = (they) saw.

His limbs covered with down which stood on end, breathing like a serpent, as he slept, Kumbhakarna of irresistible valour emitted dreadful snores, his nostrils being horrible, and his mouth a gaping hell. Stretched to his full length on the earth, he gave forth an odour of marrow and blood, his limbs were adorned with golden armlets and he wore a diadem as bright as the sun, thus did that lion among demons, Kumbhakarna, the slayer of his foes, appear!

Sloka 30

Those excellent demons piled up a great mass of wonderful food with the meat of deers, buffaloes and pigs.

They rubbed Kumbhakarna the scourge of his foes with the most rare sandalwood and refreshed him with celestial and fragrant garlands as well as sweet-smelling perfumes.

Sloka 36

Those demons made sounds by clapping their hands, in order to awaken Kumbhakarna and shook him too, creating a great clamour.

Sloka 37

The birds passing through the sky ran helter-shelter and fell down soon on hearing the sounds of the couches, drums, gongs, clapping of hands and leonine roars.

Sloka 38

"That which this king never suffered at the hands of Gods or Demons or Danavas, he has had to endure here from Rama, who released him from the danger to his life."

Sloka 77

sa yūpākṣavacaḥ śrutvā bhrāturyudhi parābhavam |
kumbhakrṇo vivṛttākṣo yūpākṣamidamabravīt || 6-60-77

77. shrutvā = hearing; parābhavam = about the humiliation; bhrātuḥ = of his brother; yudhi = in the battle; yūpākṣa vachanaḥ = through the words of Yupaksha; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; vivṛittakṣaḥ = rolling his eyes; abravīt = spoke; yūpakṣam = to Yupaksha (as follows):

Hearing about the humiliation of his brother in the battle through the words of Yupaksha, Kumbhakarna rolling his great eyes, spoke to Yupaksha (as follows):

Sloka 78

sarvamadyaiva yūpākṣa harisainyam salakṣmaṇam |
rāghavam ca raṇe jitvā tato drakṣyāmi rāvaṇam || 6-60-78

78. yūpākṣa = O; Yupaksha!; adyaiva = now itself; jitvā = after conquering; rāgjavam = Rama; salakshamaṇam = along with Lakshmana; sarvam = and the entire; harisainyam = army of monkeys; raṇe = in the battle; drakṣyāmi = I shall see; rāvaṇam = Ravana; tata = thereafter.

"O, Yupaksha! Now itself, after conquering the entire army of monkeys with Lakshmana and Rama on the battle-field, then only I shall see Ravana."

Sloka 79

rākṣasāṃstarpayiṣyāmi harīṇām māṃsaśoṇitaiḥ |
rāmalakṣmaṇayoścāpi svayam pāsyāmi śoṇitam ||6-60-79

79. tarpayiṣyāmi = I shall satiate; rākṣasān = the demons; māmsashoṇitaiḥ = with the flesh and blood; harīṇām = of the monkeys; pāsyāmi = I shall drink; svayam = myself; shoṇitam = the blood; rāmalakṣmaṇayoḥ = Rama and Lakshmana.

"I shall satiate the demons with the flesh and blood of monkeys and, as for Rama and Lakshmana, I shall drink their blood myself."

Sloka 80

tattasya vākyam bruvato niśamya |
sagarvitam roṣavivṛddhadoṣam |
mahodaro nairṛtayodhamukhyaḥ |
kṛtāñjalirvākyamidam babhāṣe || 6-60-80

80. nishamya = hearing; tat vākyam = those words; bruvataḥ = spoken; garvitam = with haughtiness; roṣavivṛiddha doṣam = and whose anger increased his ferocity; tasya = of him; sah = that; mahodaraḥ = ṃahodara; nairṛitayodhamukhyaḥ = the chief of warriors of demons; kṛitāṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms; babhāṣe = spoke; idam vākyam = these words.

Hearing these haughty from that demon, whose anger increased his ferocity, ṃahodara the chief of warriors of demons, having offered obeisance with joined palms, spoke the following words:

Sloka 81

rāvaṇasya vacaḥ śrutvā guṇadoṣau vimṛśya ca |
paścādapi mahābāho śatrūnyudhi vijeṣyasi || 6-60-81

81. mahābāho = O; the mighty armed!; shrutvā = when your have listened to; vachaḥ = the words; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; vimṛishya cha = and considered; guṇa doṣau = the advantages and disadvantages (of the matter); pashchāt = api = even afterwards; vijeṣyasi = you can conquer; shatrūn = the enemies; yudhi = in battle.

"O, the mighty armed! When you have listened to the words of Ravana and considered the advantages and disadvantages of the matter, even afterwards you can conquer the enemies in battle."

Sloka 82

mahodaravacaḥ śrutvā rākṣasaiḥ parivāritaḥ |
kumbhakarṇo mahātejāḥ sampratasthe mahābalaḥ || 6-60-82

82. shrutvā = hearing; mahodara vachaḥ = the words of ṃahodara; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; mahātejāḥ = of great valour; sampratasthe = prepared to depart; parivāritaḥ = surrounded; rākṣasaiḥ = by demons.

Hearing the words of ṃahodara, the might Kumbhakarna of great valour prepared to depart, surrounded by demons.

Sloka 83

suptamutthāpya bhīmākṣam bhīmarūpaparākramam |
rākṣasāstvaritā jagmurdaśagrīvaniveśanam || 6-60-83

83. rākṣasāḥ = the demons; utthāprya = having awaken; suptam = that sleeping Kumbhakarna; bhīmākṣam = of dreadful eyes; bhīmarūpa parākramam = with formidable aspect and valour; jagmuḥ = went; dashagrīva niveshanam = to the king's palace; tvaritāḥ = in haste.

The demons, having awaken that sleeping Kumbhakarna of dreadful eyes, form and valour, went to the King's palace in haste.

Sloka 84

te.abhigamya daśagrīvamāsīnam paramāsane |
ūcurbaddhāñjalipuṭāḥ sarva eva niśācarāḥ || 6-60-84

84. sarve = all; te nishācharāḥ = those demons; abhigamya = approaching; dashagrīvam = Ravana; āsīnam = who was seated; paramāsane = on the throne; ūchuḥ = said to him; baddhāṇ^jalipuṭāḥ = with joined palms (as follows):

All those demons, approaching Ravana who was seated on the throne, said to him with joined palms, as follows:

Sloka 85

kumbhakarṇaḥ prabuddho.asau bhrātā te rākṣaseśvara |
katham tatraiva niryātu drakṣyase tamihāgatam || 6-60-85

85. rākṣaseshvara = O; Lord of Demons!; asau kumbhakarnaḥ = this Kumbhakarna; te bhrātā = your brother; prabuddhaḥ = has wakened; niryātu katham = (It is your will) that he should enter the battle field; tatraiva = from there itself; drakṣyase = or do you desire to see; tam = him; āgatam = who came; iha = here?.

"O, Lord of Demons! Kumbhakarna, your brother, has wakened. Is it your will that he should enter the battle field from there itself or do you desire him to come here and see you?"

Sloka 86

rāvaṇastvabravīddhṛṣṭo rākṣasāṃstānupasthitān |
draṣṭumenamihecchāmi yathānyāyam ca pūjyatām || 6-60-86

86. rāvaṇastu = Ravana on his part; abravīt = answered; tān = those; rākṣasān = demons; upasthitam = who stood before him; hṛiṣṭaḥ = and with a glad heart; (said); ichchhami = I want; draṣṭum = to see; enam = him; iha = here; pūjyatām = let hi receive the honours; yathānyāyam = in a befitting manner.

Ravana answered those demons who stood before him and with a glad heart, said: "I want to see him here. Bring him after extending the honours due to him in a befitting manner."

Sloka 87

tathetyuktvā tu te sarve punarāgamya rākṣasāḥ |
kumbhakarṇamidam vākyamūcū rāvaṇacoditāḥ || 6-60-87

87. uktvā = uttering; tatheti = "Be it so"; sarve = all; te = those; rākṣasāḥ = demons; rāvaṇachoditāḥ = as per the command of Ravana; punaḥ āgamya = returned; kumbhakarṇam = to Kumbhakarna; ūchuḥ = and spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words.

Uttering "Be it so", all those demons as commanded by Ravana, returned to Kumbhakarna and spoke the following words:

Sloka 88

draṣṭum tvām kāmkṣate rājā sarvarākṣasapumgavaḥ |
gamane kriyatām buddhirbhrātaram sampraharṣaya || 6-60-88

88. rājā = the king; sarva rākṣasa pungavaḥ = the lion of all the dmeons; kān^kṣate = desires; draṣṭum = to see; tvām = you; kriyatām = bestow; buddhiḥ = your mind; gamane = in going; sampraharṣaya = and gratify; bhrātaram = your brother!.

"The king, the lion of all the demons, desires to see you. Go and gratify your brother!"

Sloka 89

kumbhakarṇastu durdharṣo bhrāturājñāya śāsanam |
tathetyuktvā mahāvīryaḥ śayanādutpapāta ha || 6-60-89

89. āJṇāya = understanding; bhrātuḥ = his brother's; shāsanam = command; kumbhakarṇastu = Kumbhakarna; durdharṣaḥ = the inviolable demons; mahāvīryaḥ = of great prowess; uktvā = uttering; tathā iti = "so be it"; utpapātaha = rose up; shayanāt = from his couch.

Hearing his brother's wish, Kumbhakarna the inviolable demon of great prowess, uttering "so be it", leapt up from his couch.

Sloka 90

prakṣālya vadanam hṛṣṭaḥ snātaḥ paramabhūṣitaḥ |
pipāsustvarayāmāsa pānam balasamīraṇam || 6-60-90

90. prakṣālya = washing; vadanam = his face; snātaḥ = bathing; hṛiṣṭaḥ = refreshed and delighted; paramabhūṣitaḥ = adorning himself well; pipāsuḥ = and feeling thirsty; tvarayāmāsa = (he) hastened (them); pānam = (to bring him) a drink; balasamīraṇam = which can boost up his strength.

Washing his face and bathing, refreshed and delighted, adorning himself well and feeling thirsty, he hastened them to bring him a drink which can boost up his strength.

Sloka 91

tataste tvaritāstatra rākṣasā rāvaṇājñayā |
madyam bhakṣyāṃśca vividhān kṣipramevopahārayan || 6-60-91

91. tataḥ = thereupon; te rākṣasāḥ = those demons; upahārayan- brought; (him); kṣiprameva = quickly; madyam = the wine; vividhān = and various kinds; bhakṣyāmshcha = of eatables; tatra = there; tvaritāḥ = (and who) hastened; rāvaṇāJṇayā = to carry out Ravana's commands.

Thereupon, those demons brought him quickly the wine and various kinds of eatable there, in their hurry to take Kumbhakarna with them as per Ravana's commands.

Sloka 92

pītvā ghaṭasahasre dve gamanāyopacakrame |
īṣatsamutkaṭo mattastejobalasamanvitaḥ || 6-60-92

92. pitrā = having drunk; dve = two; ghaṭa sahasre = thousand pitchers; gamanāya upachakrame = (Kumbhakarna) prepared to set out; īṣat = and slightly; samutkaṭaḥ = inebriated; mattaḥ = and flushed; tejobalasamanvitaḥ = he was exhilarated and filled with energy.

Having drunk two thousand pitchers, Kumbhakarna prepared to set out and slightly inebriated and flushed, he was exhilarated and filled with energy.

Sloka 93

kumbhakarṇo babau ruṣṭaḥ kālāntakayamopamaḥ |
bhrūtuḥ sa bhavanam gacchan rakṣobalasamanvitaḥ || 6-60-93
kumbhakarṇaḥ padanyāsairakampayata medinīm |

93. ruṣṭaḥ = the fuming; umbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; gachchhan = going; bhrātruḥ = to his brother's; bhavanam = palace; rakṣobala samanvitaḥ = along with the army of demons; babhau = appeared; kālāntaka yamopamaḥ = like Yama the Lord of Death at the end of all-destroying time; akampayata medinīm = he caused the earth to tremble; padanyāsaiḥ = with his foot-steps.

The fuming Kumbhakarna, going to his brother's palace along with the army of demons, appeared like Yama the Lord of Death at the end of all-destroying Time. He caused the earth to tremble with his foot-steps, while marching.

Sloka 94

sa rājamārgam vapuṣā prakāśayan |
sahasrarasmirdharaṇīmivāṃśubhiḥ |
jagāma tatrāñjalimālayā vṛtaḥ |
śatakraturgehamiva svayambhuvaḥ || 6-60-94

94. rājamārgam = the royal highway; prakāshayan = was illumined; vapuṣā = by his body; sahasra rashmiḥ iva = like unto that orb of a thousand rays (sund); amshubhiḥ = by its flashes; dharaṇīm = (illumines) the earth; jagāma = and he went; tatra = there; vṛitaḥ = surrounded by; aṇ^jalimālayā = a circle of demons paying him obeisance; shatakratuḥ = resembling Indra the Lord of Celestials; geham = (approaching) the abode; svayambhuvaḥ = of Brahma the Lord of Creation.

The royal high way was illumined by his body, like unto that orb of a thousand rays (the sun) by its flashes illumines the earth and he went there, surrounded by a circle of demons paying obeisance, resembling Indra the Lord of Celestials approaching the abode of Brahma the Lord of Creation.

Sloka 95

tam rājamārgasthamamitraghātinam |
vanaukasaste sahasā bahiḥsthitāḥ |
dṛṣṭvā.a prameyam giriśṛṅgakalpam |
vitatrasuste saha yūthapālaiḥ || 6-60-95

95. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; amitraghātinam = that slayer of his foes; rājamārgastham = on the royal high way; tam = that monster; girishṛiṇga kalpam = as high as the peak of a mountain; te vanaukasaḥ = those monkeys; bahiḥsthitaḥ = stationed outside (the city); yūthapālaiḥ saha = as also their leaders; vitatrasuḥ = were frightened; sahasā = suddenly.

Seeing that slayer of his foes on the royal high way, that monster as high as the peak of a mountain, those monkeys stationed outside the city, as also their leaders, were frightened suddenly.

Sloka 96

kecicharaṇyam śaraṇam sma rāmam |
vrajanti kecidvyathitāḥ patanti |
keciddiśaśca vyathitāḥ patanti |
kecidbayārtābhuvi śerate sma || 6-60-96

96. kechit = some; sharaṇam vajrantisma = gained refuge; rāmam = in Rama; sharaṇyam = who affords protection; kechit = some; patantisma = fell down; vyathitā = tottering; kechit = some; patantisma = fled away; vyathitāḥ = perturbed; dishaḥ = in all directions; kechit = and some; sherate sma = lay; bhuvi = on the ground; bhayārtāḥ = confounded with fear.

Some gained refuge in Rama who affords protection, some fell down tottering, some fled away perturbed in all directions and some others lay on the ground, confounded with fear.

Sloka 97

tamadriśṛṅgapratimam kirīṭinam |
spṛśantamādityamivātmatejasā |
vanaukasaḥ prekṣya vivṛddhamadbhutam |
bhayārditā dudruvire yatastataḥ || 6-60-97

97. prekṣya = seeing; tam adbhutam = that colossus; adri shṛiṇga pratimam = like a great peak; kirīṭinam = having a diadem; spṛishantam iva = who seemed to touch; ādityam = the sun; ātma tejasā = with his brilliance; vanaukasaḥ = the monkeys; bhayārditāḥ = were seized with terror; vivṛiddham = and had grown immensely in size; dudruvire = fled; yatastataḥ = hither and thither.

Seeing that colossus appearing like a great peak, having a diadem who seemed to touch the sun with his brilliance, the monkeys were seized with terror and had grown immensely in size, fled hither and thither.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṃḍe ṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 60th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
