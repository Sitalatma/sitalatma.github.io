Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 16
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana refuses to hear the words of Vibhishana and rebukes him with harsh words. Vibhishana gets angry and sets off, to leave Ravana.

Sloka 1

suniviṣṭam hitam vākyam uktavantam vibhīṣaṇam |
abravīt paruṣam vākyam rāvaṇaḥ kāla coditaḥ || 6-16-1

1. vibhishhaṇam = to Vibhishana; sunuvishhṭam = who was very stable; uktavantam = and who spoke; hitam = wholesome; vākyam = words; rāvanṇaḥ = Ravana; kālachoditaḥ = goaded by death; abravīt = spoke; parushham = harsh; vākyam = words; (as follows):

Ravana, goaded by death, spoke the following harsh words to Vibhishana, who was very stable and who had spoken wholesome words:

Sloka 2

vaset saha sapatnena kruddhena āśī viṣeṇa ca |
na tu mitra pravādena samvasccatruṇā saha || 6-16-2

2. vaset = one can stay; sapatnena saha = with an enemy; ashīvishheṇa = or with a snake; kruddhena = which is enraged; tu = but; na samvaset = one cannot stay; shatruṇāsaha = with an enemy; mitrapravādena = talking as a friend."

"One can stay together with an enemy or even with a furious snake, but one cannot stay together with an adversary yet avowing himself as a friend"

Sloka 3

jānāmi śīlam jñātīnām sarva lokeṣu rākṣasa |
hṛṣyanti vyasaneṣvete jñātīnām jñātayaḥ sadā || 6-16-3

3. O; demon! GYāmi = I know shīlam = the conduct; gyātīnām = of kinsmen; sadā = always; hṛishhyanti = are rejoiced; vyasaneshhu = at the calamities; g^yātīnām = of their kinsmen."

"O, demon! I know the conduct of kinsmen in all the worlds. These kinsmen always are rejoiced at the calamities of their kinsmen"

Sloka 4

pradhānam sādhakam vaidyam dharma śīlam ca rākṣasa |
jñātayo hyanamanyante śūram paribhavanti ca || 6-16-4

4. rākshhasa = O;demon; n^yātayaḥ = kinsmen; avamanyante hi = indeed insult; paribhavanticha = and even humiliate; sādhakam = an effective; pradhānam = leader; vaidyam = an educated person; dharmashīlāmcha = a person of right conduct; shūram = and a valiant man."

"O, demon! The kinsmen indeed insult and even humiliate their kinsman, who is an effective leader, a person of education and right conduct and a valiant man."

Sloka 5

nityam anyonya samhṛṣṭā vyasaneṣvātatāyinaḥ |
praccanna hṛdayā ghorā jñātayas tu bhaya āvahāḥ || 6-16-5

5. ghorāḥ = the horrible; G^yātaḥ = kinsmen; prachchhanna hṛidayāḥ = with their concealed thoughts; ātatāyinaḥ = and treacherous minds; bhayāvahāḥ = terrific ones as they are; nityam = always; anyonyasamhṛishhṭāḥ = rejoice each other; vyasaneshhu = in adversities."

"The horrible kinsmen with their concealed thoughts and treacherous minds, terrific ones as they are, always rejoice each other in adversities."

Sloka 6

śrūyante hastibhir gītāḥ ślokāḥ padma vane purā |
pāśa hastān narān dṛṣṭvā śṛṇu tān gadato mama || 6-16-6

6. padmavane = In a forest called Padmavana; purāḥ = previously; shlokaḥ = maxims; gītaḥ = were spoken; hastibhiḥ = by elephants; dṛishhṭvā = after seeing; narān = men; pāsha hastān = with nooses in hand; shṛiṇushhva = ḥear; gadataḥ = as told; mama = to me."

"Previously in a forest called Padmavana, some maxims were spoken by elephants, which saw men with nooses in hand. Hear and I shall tell them."

Sloka 7

nāgnirnānyāni śastrāṇi na naḥ pāśā bhayāvahāḥ |
ghorāḥ svārthaprayuktāstu jñātayo no bhayāvahāḥ || 6-16-7

7. naḥ = for us; na = no; agniḥ = fire; na = no; anyāni = after; shastrāṇi = weapons; na = no; pāshāḥ = nooses; na = they are not; bhayāvahāḥ = begetting fear; ghoraḥ = the terrific; jJṇātayaḥ = kinsmen; svārthaprayuktāḥ = incited by selfishness; bhayāvahāḥ = are begetting fear; naḥ = to us."

"For us, fire or weapons or nooses are not begetting fear. The terrific kinsmen, incited by selfishness are begetting fear to us."

Sloka 8

upāyamete vakṣyanti grahaṇe nātrasaṃśayaḥ |
kṛtsnād bhayājjñātibhayam sukaṣṭam viditam ca naḥ || 6-16-8

8. ete = these kinsmen; vakshhyanti = will inform; upāyam = the strategy; grahaṇe = to seize us; na = there is no; samshayaḥ = doubt; atra = in this; kṛitsnāt; out of all; bhayāt = fears; G^yātibhayam = the fear of kinsmen; sukashhṭam = is very troublesome; naḥ = for us; viditam = It is known thing."

"These kinsmen will inform the strategy to our enemies to seize us. There is no doubt about this. Out of all fears, the fear of kinsmen is very much troublesome for us. It is a well known thing."

Sloka 9

vidyate goṣu sampannam vidyate jñātito bhayam |
vidyate strīṣu cāpalyam vidyate jñātito bhayam || 6-16-9

9. vidyate = ṭhere is; sampannam = wealth; goshhu = in cows; vidyate = there is; bhayam = fear; G^yātitaḥ = for kinsmen; vidyate = there is; chāpalyam = fickleness; strīshhu = in women; vidyate = there is; tapaḥ = austerity; brāhmaṇe = in brahmins."

"There is wealth in cows. There is fear for kinsmen. There is fickleness in women. There is austerity in Brahmins."

Sloka 10

tato neṣṭam idam saumya yadaham loka satkṛtaḥ |
aiśvaryam abhijātaḥ ca ripūṇām mūrdhni ca sthitaḥ || 6-16-10

10. saumya = O;gentleman! ṭataḥ = hence; idam = this; na ishhṭam = is not of liking to you; iti yat = that; aham = I; loka satkṛitaḥ = am venerated by the people; abhijātaḥcha = that I obtained; aishvaryam = riches; sthitaḥcha = and that I am sitting tight; mūrdhni = in the head; ripuṇām = of enemies."

"O, gentleman! Hence, it is not of liking to you that the people are venerating me, that I obtained riches and that I am sitting tight in the heads of enemies".

Sloka 11

yathā puṣkarapatreṣu patitāstoyabindavaḥ |
na śleṣamabhigaccanti tathānāryeṣu sauhṛdam || 6-16-11

11. yathā = how; toyabindavaḥ = drops of water; palitāḥ = fallen; pushhkarapatreshhu = on lotus leaves; na abhigachchhanti = do not get; shleshham = clinging; tathā = in the same manner. ṣouhṛidam = friendship; anāryeshhu = in vulgar ones."

"As drops of water fallen on lotus leaves do not get clung to those leaves, so also a friendship does not cling to vulgar ones."

Sloka 12

yathā śaradi meghānām siñcātāmapi garjatām |
na bhavatyamubasamkledastathānāryeṣu sauhṛdam || 6-16-12

12. yathā = how; piṇchatām = raining; meghānām = clouds ; garjatāmapi = even if thundering; na bhaveti = cannot; ambusamsamkledaḥ = give excessive wetness with water; tathā = in the same manner; souhṛidam = friendship; anāryeshhu = in vulgar people."

"As rainy clouds in autumn, even though thundering, cannot get the earth saturated with water, so also friendship does not have tenderness of heart in vulgar people."

Sloka 13

yathā madhukarastarṣādrāsam vindanna tiṣṭhati |
tathā tvamapi tatraiva tathānāryeṣu sauhṛdam || 6-16-13

13. yathā = how; madhukaraḥ = a honey bee; vindan = getting; rasam = nectar (from a flower); na tishhṭhati = does not stick on (that flower);tarshhāt = due to eager desire (for more); tathā = in the same manner; tvamapi = you also; tatraiva = in that(category) only; tathā = in the same manner; souhṛidam = friendship; anāryeshhu = in vulgar people."

"As a honey bee, even though getting nectar from a flower, does not stick on to that flower due to its eager desire for more, you also belong to that category as friendship does not stick on to vulgar people."

Sloka 14

yathā madhukarstarāṣātkāśapuṣpam pibannapi |
rasamatra na vindeta tathānāryeṣu sauhṛdam || 6-16-14

14. yathā = how; madhukaraḥ = a honey bee; na vindeta = cannot get; rasam = nectar; atra = in it; pibannapi = even though trying to drink;kāshapushhpam = on a flower of grass; tathā = in the same manner ; sauhṛidam = friendship; anāryeshhu = from vulgar persons."

"As even though trying to drink nectar from a flower of grass, a honey bee cannot get it, so also friendship cannot be extracted from vulgar persons."

Sloka 15

yathā pūrvam gajaḥ snātvā gṛhya hastena vai rajaḥ |
dūṣayatyātmano deham tathānāryeṣu sauhṛdam || 6-16-15

15. yathā = how; gajaḥ = an elephants; dūshhayati = abuses; ātmanaḥ = its; deham = body; gṛihya = by helping itself; rajah = with dust; snātvā = after taking bath; pūrvā = earlier; hastena = with its trunk; tathā = in the same manner; souhṛidam = friendship; anaryeshhu = in vulgar persons"

"How an elephant abuses its body by helping itself with dust after taking bath earlier with its trunk, friendship gets an abuse in vulgar persons in the same manner."

Sloka 16

yo.anyastvevamvidham brūyād vākyametanniśācara |
asmin muhūrte na bhavet tvām tu dhik kulapāṃsanam || 6-16-16

16. nishāchara = O; demon; walking about by night! yaḥ = Whoever; anyaḥ = other one; evamvidham = in this manner; brūyāt = utters; etat = these; vākyam = words; na bhavet = he will not be present; asmin = at this; muhūrte = moment; kula pāmsana = O; destroyer of race! dhik = A curse; tvām tu = upon you."

"O, demon, walking about by night! If any other person utters these words in this manner, he will be ceasing to exit at this moment. O, destroyer of race! A curse upon you!"

Sloka 17

itiuktaḥ paruṣam vākyam nyāyavādī vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
utpapāta gadāpāṇiḥcaturbhiḥ saha rākṣasaiḥ || 6-16-17

17. parushham = harsh; vākyam = words; iti = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; nyāyavādī = who advocates justice; gadāpāṇiḥ = having a mace in his hand; utpapāta = soared into the sky; chaturbhiḥ rākshhasaiḥ saha = along with four demons.

Hearing those harsh words from Ravana, Vibhishana who advocates justice, wielding a mace in his hand, soared high into the sky, along with four other demons.

Sloka 18

abravīcca tadā vākyam jātakrodho vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
antarikṣagataḥ śrīmān bhrātaram rākṣasa adhipam || 6-16-18

18. tadā = then; shrīmān = the illustrious; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; jātakrodhaḥ = for whom angry was arisen; antarikshhagataḥ = after moving into the sky; abravīchcha = spoke also; rākshhasādhipam = to the king of demons; bhrātaram = his brother.

Then, the illustrious Vibhishana who became angry also spoke these words to king Ravana, his brother after moving into the sky.

Sloka 19

sa tvam bhrātā asi me rājan brūhi mām yad yad iccasi |
jyeṣṭho mānyaḥ pitṛsamo na ca dharmapathe sthitaḥ || 6-16-19
idam tu paruṣam vākyam na kṣamāmi anṛtam tava |

19. rājan = O;king! ṣaḥ tvam = you as such; asi = are; bhrātā = brother; me = to me; brūhi = speak; mām = to me; yadyat = whatever; ichchhasi = you like; jyeshhṭhaḥ = elder brother; pitṛisamaḥ = is equal to father; mānyaḥ = he is to be respected; nachasthitaḥ = even though not staying; dharmapathe = in right path; ; na kshhamāpi = I cannot forbear; ;idam = these; te = your; parushham = harsh; vākyamtu = words."

"O, king! You are a brother to me. You utter whatever you like. Elder brother is like a father. Even if he is not staying in a right path, he is to be respected. But I cannot forbear these harsh words from you."

Sloka 20

sunītam hita kāmena vākyam uktam daśa ānana || 6-16-20
na gṛhṇanti akṛta ātmānaḥ kālasya vaśamāgatāḥ |

20. akṛitātmenaḥ = those who have an ill composed mind; āgatāh = and get; vasham = conceded; kālasya = to the occasion; na gṛihṇanti = do not assimilate; sunītam vākyam = the words of prudence; uktam = spoken; hitakāmena = by a well wisher."

"Those who have an ill composed mind and who get conceded to the occasion do not assimilate the words of prudence, spoken by a well wisher."

Sloka 21

sulabhāḥ puruṣā rājan satatam priya vādinaḥ || 6-16-21
apriyasya tu pathyasya vaktā śrotā ca durlabhaḥ |

21. rājan = O;king! purushāḥ = men; priyavādinaḥ = who speak pleasing words; satatam = forever; sulabhāḥ = are easy to be obtained; vaktā = (But) one who speaks; pathyasya = of useful; apriyasya = but unpleasing words; shrotācha = and also one listens to them; durlabhāḥ = are rare to be obtained."

"O, king! ṃen who forever speak pleasing words are easy to be obtained. But one who speaks of useful but unpleasing words as also the one who listens to them, are rare to be obtained"

Sloka 22

baddham kālasya pāśena sarva bhūta apahāriṇā || 6-16-22
na naśyantam upekṣeyam pradīptam śaraṇam yathā |

22. naupekshhe = I could not ignore; tvām = you;nashyantam = being destroyed; pāshena = by the noose; kālasya = of Yama; (god of death); baddham = being tied; (around you); sarva bhūtāpahāriṇaḥ = which takes away all beings; sharaṇamyathā = as a house; pradīptam = which is burning."

"I could not ignore you, being destroyed by the noose of Yama (god of death); tied around you and which snatches away all beings, as none can ignore a burning house."

Sloka 23

dīpta pāvaka samkāśaiḥ śitaiḥ kāncana bhūṣaṇaiḥ || 6-16-23
na tvām iccāmi aham draṣṭum rāmeṇa nihatam śaraiḥ |

23. aham = I; naichchhāmi = do not wish; drashhṭum = to see; tvām = you; nihatam = killed; rāmeṇa = by Rama; sharaiḥ = by his arrows; shitāḥ = sharp as they are ; kāṇchana bhūshhaṇaiḥ = = adorned with gold; dīptapāvaka samkāshaiḥ = and resembling like blazing fire."

"I do not wish to see you, being killed by Rama with his sharp arrows, adorned with gold and each resembling like a blazing fire."

Sloka 24

śūrāḥ ca balavantaḥ ca kṛta astrāḥ ca nara ājire || 6-16-24
kāla abhipannā sīdanti yathā vāluka setavaḥ |

24. narāḥ = persons; shūrāḥ = who are valiant; balavantashcha = who are strong; kṛitāshcha = and who are skilled in weaponry; kālābhipannāḥ = overpowered by ṭime; sīdanti = sink down = (ruin) vāluka setuvaḥ yathā = like a dam constructed with sand."

"Persons who are valiant, strong and skilled in weaponry, when time comes, sink down (ruin) as a dam constructed with sand sinks down."

Sloka 25

tanarṣayatu yaccoktam gurutvāddhitamiccatā || 6-16-25
ātmānam sarvathā rakṣa purīm ca imām sarākṣasām |
svasti te astu gamiṣyāmi sukhī bhava mayā vinā || 6-16-26

25; 26. gurutrāt = as an elder brother; marshhayatu = be patient; tat = with that; uktam = spoken; yat = by which; hitam = welfare; ichchhatā = was desired by me; rakshha = guard; ātmānam = yourself; sarāksshhasām = along with demons; imām = and this ; purīmcha = city ; sarvathā = by all means; asti = Let (all) be; savasti = well; te = to you; gamishhyāmi = I am departing; bhava = Be; sukhī = happy person; mayā vinā = without me."

"As an elder brother, bear the words spoken by me, who desire your welfare. Guard this city and yourself along with demons, by all means. Let all be well with you. I am departing. Be a happy person without me."

Sloka 27

nivāryamāṇasya mayā hita eṣiṇā |
na rocate te vacanam niśā cara |
parīta kālā hi gata āyuṣo narā |
hitam na gṛhṇanti suhṛdbhir īritam || 6-16-27

27. nishāchara = O; Ravana; the ranger of the night! vachanam = my words; narochate = are not relishing; te = to you; nivāryanāṇasya = who are being restrained; mayā = by me; hitāishhiṇā = desiring welfare; narāḥ = persons; gatāyushhaḥ = whose duration of life is ended; na gṛihṇanti = do not accept ;hitam = the beneficial words; īritam = spoken; suhṛidbhiḥ = by well wishers."

"O, Ravana the ranger of the night! ṃy words are not relishing to you, who are being restrained by me duly desiring your welfare. Persons whose duration of life is to end, do not accept the salutary words spoken by their well wishers"

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ṣoḍaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 16th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
