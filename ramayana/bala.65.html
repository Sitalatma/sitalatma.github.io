Valmiki Ramayana

Bala kanda

Book I : Bala Kanda - The Youthful ṃajesties

chapter [Sarga] 65
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Oct 09

Introduction

Vishvamitra becomes Brahma-sage after a prolonged ascesis that made all the worlds to startle. All gods request Brahma to accord the supreme Brahma-sageship on Vishvamitra as he is cleansed of all mortal impurities, and Brahma accords that highest order on him. Thus Sage Shatānanda concludes his narration about the legend of Vishvamitra.

Sloka 1

atha haimavatīm rāma diśham tyaktvā mahāmuniḥ |
pūrvām diśham anuprāpya tapaḥ tepe sudāruṇam || 1-65-1

1. rāma = oh, Rama; atha mahā muniḥ = then, great-saint; haimavatīm disham = snowbound, direction - ḥimalayan area, northern side; tyaktvā = leaving off; pūrvām disham anuprāpya = eastern, direction, on arriving; sudāruṇam tapaḥ tepe = highly rigorous, ascesis, undertook.

"That great-saint Vishvamitra then leaving off the snowbound Himalayas on north, oh, Rama, he reached eastern quarter and undertook rigorous ascesis." Thus Sage Shatānanda continued the legend of Vishvamitra. [1-65-1]

Sloka 2

maunam varṣha sahasrasya kṛtvā vratam anuttamam |
chakāra apratimam rāma tapaḥ parama duṣhkaram || 1-65-2

2. rāma = Rama; varṣa sahasrasya = of years, thousand; an uttamam = un, excelled; maunam = muteness; vratam = vow; kṛitvā = on making [committing himself]; a pratimam = un, matched; parama duṣkaram = highly, impracticable; tapaḥ cakāra = ascesis, he performed.

"Committing himself to a vow of muteness, oh, Rama, he performed an unexcelled and unmatched ascesis which is highly impracticable for others to perform. [1-65-2]

Sloka 3

pūrṇe varṣha sahasre tu kāṣhṭha bhūtam mahāmunim |
vighnaiḥ bahubhiḥ ādhūtam krodho na aṃtaram āviśhat || 1-65-3
saḥ kṛtvā niśhchayam rāma tapa ātiṣhṭat avyayam |

3, 4a. rāma = Rama; varṣa sahasre pūrṇe tu = years, thousand, on completing, even; kāṣṭha bhūtam mahā munim = woodenly, on becoming, great-saint; bahubhiḥ vighnaiḥ ādhūtam = many, by barriers, blasted [at full blast]; krodhaḥ [hṛidaya] antaram = fury, in inside [heart of hearts,]; na āvishat = not, entered; saḥ nishcayam kṛitvā = he, firm determination, on making; a vyayam tapa ātiṣṭat = not, mitigated, ascesis, stood fast.

"Even on completing a thousand years, even when that great-saint became woodenly, even many barriers are at full blast, oh, Rama, fury has not entered his heart of hearts, for he stood fast in an unmitigated ascesis on making a firm determination. [1-65-3, 4a]

Sloka 4

tasya varṣha sahasrasya vrate pūrṇe mahāvrataḥ || 1-65-4
bhoktum ārabdhavān annam tasmin kāle raghūttama |
indro dvijātiḥ bhūtvā tam siddha annam ayāchat || 1-65-5

4b, 5. raghūttama = oh, Ragu-dynasty's best, Rama; tasya varṣa sahasrasya = those, years, thousand of; vrate pūrṇe = ascesis, while being completed; mahāvrataḥ = one with a rigour-pledge; annam bhoktum ārabdhavān = meal, to eat, started to; tasmin kale = at that, time; indraḥ dvijātiḥ bhūtvā = Indra, Brahman, on becoming; tam siddha annam ayācat = him [Vishvamitra's,] readily available, meal, requested.

"On one day when those thousand years of ascesis with the rigorous pledge of Vishvamitra are being completed, and when he started to eat his meal, oh, Rama, best of Raghu's dynasty, Indra arrived there disguising himself as a Brahman and requested for the readily available meal. [1-65-4b, 5]

This meal is prepared by Vishvamitra alone after a thousand years and such self-cooking of food by the ascetics is called, vaishva devam, since it is a sacred preparation anena vaishvadeva ante samāgato atithiḥ svayam upoṣhyā api svīya anna pradānena sambhojya iti sūcitam - dk

Sloka 6

tasmaiḥ dattvā tadā siddham sarvam viprāya niśhchitaḥ |
niḥṣheṣhite anne bhagavān abhuktvā iva mahātapāḥ || 1-65-6
na kiṃchit avadat vipram mauna vratam upāsthitaḥ |
tathā eva āsīt punaḥ maunam anuchchhvāsam chakāra ha || 1-65-7

6. bhagavān = godly Vishvamitra; mahā tapāḥ = great-ascetic; mauna vratam upāsthitaḥ = muteness, pledge of, one who is abiding; such Vishvamitra; nishcitaḥ = willingly; tadā = then; siddham = ready [meal]; sarvam = all; tasmaiḥ viprāya dattvā = for him, to Brahman, gave away; anne niḥ ṣheṣite = food, nothing, remained - Indra consumed everything without any leftovers; a bhuktvā iva = without, taking food [starved himself,] like; vipram kimcit na avadat = to Brahman, a little, not, said; tathā = later; punaḥ = again; an ucchvāsam eva āsīt = without, breath [with breath-control,] only, he remained; maunam cakāra ha = muteness, carried on [his ascesis,] indeed.

"Then that godly Vishvamitra willingly gave away all the readied meal to that Brahman, and as no meal is leftover by Brahman-Indra, that great-ascetic Vishvamitra starved himself. Vishvamitra did not speak a little to the Brahman in dissent as he is abided by his pledge of muteness, and he again remained in muteness and breath-control. Like that, he indeed carried on his ascesis. [1-65-6, 7]

Sloka 8

atha varṣha sahasram cha na uchchhvasan munipuṃgavaḥ |
tasya anuchchhvasamānasya mūrdhni dhūmo vyajāyata || 1-65-8
trai lokyam yena saṃbhrāṃtam ātāpitam iva abhavat |

8, 9a. atha = then; munipungavaḥ = saint, the eminent is; varṣa sahasram = years, [another] thousand; na ucchvasat = without, respiration; an ucchvasamānasya = who is - not, taking breath; tasya mūrdhni = his, from head; dhūmaḥ vyajāyata = fumes, started to emit; yena = by which [fumes]; trai lokyam sambhrāntam = triad, of worlds, startled; ātāpitam iva abhavat = seared, as if, became.

"That eminent saint remained without respiration for another thousand years, and then fumes have started to emit from the head of sage who is controlling his breath, by which fumes the triad of worlds looked as if it is searing, and this startled all the worlds. [1-65-8, 9a]

Sloka 9

tato devarṣhi gandharvāḥ pannaga uraga rākṣhasāḥ || 1-65-9
mohitā tapasā tasya tejasā maṃdaraśhmayaḥ |
kaśhmala upahatāḥ sarve pitāmaham atha abruvan || 1-65-10

9b, 10: tataḥ = then; deva ṛiṣi gandharvāḥ pannaga uraga rākṣasāḥ = gods, sages, gandharva-s, serpents, reptiles, demons; tasya = his [Vishva mitra's]; tejasā = by resplendence; mohitā = are puzzled; atha = then; tapasā = by [his] ascesis; manda rashmayaḥ = dulled, resplendence - of gods and others; kashmala upahatāḥ = all of them - blemish, marred by; sarve pitāmaham abruvan = all, to Grandparent, then, addressed.

"Then the gods, sages, gandharva-s, serpents, reptiles, demons are puzzled at the ascesis of Vishvamitra, and as their own resplendence is dulled by the ascesis of Vishvamitra, thereby they are marred by this blemish of lowered resplendence, then all of them addressed the Grandparent, Brahma. [1-65-9b, 10]

Sloka 11

bahubhiḥ kāraṇaiḥ deva viśhvāmitro mahāmuniḥ |
lobhitaḥ krodhitaḥ chaiva tapasā cha abhivardhate || 1-65-11

11. deva = oh, god; mahāmuniḥ vishvāmitraḥ = great-saint, Vishvamitra; bahubhiḥ kāraṇaiḥ = by many - all, means; lobhitaḥ krodhitaḥ caiva = [even if] allured, angered, also thus; tapasā abhivardhate = by ascesis, transcending.

" 'Even if that great-saint Vishvamitra is angered and allured in order to foil his ascetical ascendancy, by all of us and by all means oh, god, he is transcending these lures, angers, and passions by his ascesis. [1-65-11]

Sloka 12

na hi asya vṛjinam kiṃchit dṛśhyate sūkṣhmam api atha |
na dīyate yadi tu asya manasā yat abhīpsitam || 1-65-12
vināśhayati trailokyam tapasā sa chara acharam |

12, 13a: atha = now; asya = his; sūkṣmam api = imperceptible, even; vṛijinam kimcit = sin - imperfection, in the least; na dṛishyate hi = not, appears, really; asya manasā yat abhīpsitam = his, heart, which, felt [desire]; na dīyate yadi = not, given, if; tapasā = by ascetic [power]; sa cara acaram = with, mobile, sessile; trailokyam vināshayati = triad of worlds, he devastates.

" 'Now, even an imperceptible imperfection does not really appear in him, but if his heartfelt desire is not meted out, he will devastate the triad of worlds with his ascetic power. [1-65-12, 13a]

Sloka 13

vyākulāḥ cha diśhaḥ sarvā na cha kiṃchit prakāśhate || 1-65-13
sāgarāḥ kṣhubhitāḥ sarve viśhīryante cha parvatāḥ |

13b, 14a. sarvāḥ dishaḥ vyākulāḥ = all, directions, are tumultuous; kimcit na prakāshate = anything, not, brightening; sarve sāgarāḥ kṣubhitāḥ = all, oceans, are tempestuous; [sarve] parvatāḥ vishīryante = [all,] mountains, are eruptive.

" 'All the directions are all tumultuous, all the oceans are tempestuous, and all the mountains are eruptive, and nothing is bright. [1-65-13b, 14a]

Sloka 14

prakaṃpate cha vasudhā vāyuḥ vāti iha saṃkulaḥ || 1-65-14
brahman napratijānīmo nāstiko jāyate janaḥ |

14b, 15a. Brahman = oh, Brahma; vasudhā pra kampate = earth is, highly, tremulous; vāyuḥ samkulaḥ vāti = air is, turbulently, gusting; iha = now; na prati jānīmaḥ = not, towards, we know - we don't know what to do; janaḥ = people; na āstikḥ jāyate = not, theistic, they tend to become.

" 'The earth is highly tremulous, air is gusting turbulently, oh, Brahma, people tend to become non-theistic and we do not know what to do. [1-65-14b, 15a]

Sloka 15

sammūḍham iva trailokyam saṃprakṣhubhita mānasam || 1-65-15
bhāskaro niṣhprabhaḥ chaiva maharṣheḥ tasya tejasā |

15b, 16a. trailokyam = [all beings in] triad of worlds; sam pra kshubhita mānasam = with very, highly, perturbed, senses; sammūDham iva = stupefied, as though; tasya maharṣeḥ tejasā = his, of grate sage, by resplendence; bhāskaraḥ ca = sun is, also; niṣ prabhaḥ = without, lustre.

" 'All the beings in the triad of the worlds are very highly perturbed at their senses and they are as though stupefied, and when juxtaposed the sun is lustreless before the resplendence of that great sage. [1-65-15b, 16a]

Sloka 16

buddhim na kurute yāvat nāśhe deva mahāmuniḥ || 1-65-16
tāvat prasādo bhagavān agni rūpo mahādyutiḥ |

16b, 17a. deva = oh, god; mahāmuniḥ = great saint, Vishvamitra; nāshe = in total destruction - of all worlds; yāvat = before; buddhim na kurute = mind, not, going to make up; tāvat = prior to it; agni rūpaḥ= Fire-god's, embodiment of; mahādyutiḥ = great-resplendent one; bhagavān = most reverential one Vishvamitra; prasādaḥ = he is to be placated.

" 'Oh, God, great saint Vishvamitra turned out to be the embodiment of Fire-god, and before that great-resplendent and most reverential sage makes up his mind for total destruction of all worlds he is to be placated. [1-65-16b, 17a]

Sloka 17

kāla agninā yathā pūrvam trailokyam dahyate akhilam || 1-65-17
deva rājyam chikīrṣheta dīyatām asya yat matam |

17b, 18a. akhilam trailokyam = entirely, triad of worlds; pūrvam kāla agninā yathā dahyate = previously, by End-ṭime, Fire, as to how, blazed away; likewise it is; [dahyate = burnt now]; deva rājyam cikīrṣeta = of gods, realm, wants to do [be potentate of]; [yadi = if]; asya yat matam = whatever, his, predilection is; [tat = that]; dīyatām = may be given.

" 'As to how the End-Time Fire entirely blazed away the triad of worlds previously, now this sage may do likewise, hence whatever is his predilection that may be given to him, even if he wants to become the potentate on the realm of gods.' Thus, all gods appealed to Brahma [1-65-17b, 18a]

Sloka 18

tataḥ sura gaṇāḥ sarve pitāmaha purogamāḥ || 1-65-18
viśhvāmitram mahātmānam vākyam madhuram abruvan |

18b, 19a. tataḥ = then; pitāmaha purogamāḥ = Grandparent, keeping in forefront; sarve sura gaṇāḥ = all, gods', assemblages; mahātmānam vishvāmitram = to great souled one, to Vishvamitra; madhuram vākyam abruvan = harmonious, word, said.

"Then all the assemblages of gods keeping the Grandparent Brahma in their forefront appeared before that great souled Vishvamitra and said this harmonious word. [1-65-18b, 19a]

Sloka 19

brahmarṣhe svāgatam te astu tapasā sma su toṣhitāḥ || 1-65-19
brāhmaṇyam tapasā ugreṇa prāptavān asi kauśhika |

19b, 20a. brahmarṣe = oh, Brahma-sage; te svāgatam astu = to you, welcome, let there be [you are welcome]; tapasā su toṣitāḥ sma = by ascesis, much, contented, we are; kaushika = oh, Kaushika; ugreṇa tapasā = by rigorous, ascesis; brāhmaṇyam prāptavān asi = Brahman-hood, you obtained, you have.

" 'Oh, Brahma-sage, you are welcome. We are much contented with your ascesis. Oh, Kaushika, you have achieved Bahaman-hood by your rigorous ascesis. [1-65-19b, 20a]

Sloka 20

dīrgham āyuḥ cha te brahman dadāmi sa marud gaṇaḥ || 1-65-20
svasti prāpnuhi bhadram te gachchha sauṃya yathā sukham |

20b, 21a. brahman = oh, Brahman; sa marut gaṇaḥ = [I,] along with, ṃarut-gods', assemblages; te = to you; dīrgham āyuḥ dadāmi = long, life, I am bestowing; svasti prāpnuhi = blissfulness, betides you; te bhadram = to you, safeness will be there; saumya = oh, gentle [sage]; yathā sukham gaccha = as you, please, take leave.

" 'Along with the assemblages of ṃarut-Wind-gods, I bestow upon you a long life. Let blissfulness betide you. You be safe. Oh, gentle sage, you may take leave as you please.' Thus, Brahma said to Vishvamitra. [1-65-20b, 21a]

Sloka 21

pitāmaha vachaḥ śhrutvā sarveṣhām tridiva okasām || 1-65-21
kṛtvā praṇāmam mudito vyājahāra mahāmuniḥ |

21b, 22a. mahāmuniḥ = great-saint; pitāmaha vacaḥ = Grandparent's, word; sarveṣām tridiva okasām ca = of all, heaven, residents [words of]; shrutvā = on hearing; muditaḥ = cheerfully; praṇāmam kṛitvā = good devoirs, on performing [paying]; vyājahāra = said.

"On hearing the word of Grandparent Brahma and the other residents of heaven, and on paying good devoirs to all of them that great saint cheerfully said. [1-65-21b, 22a]

Sloka 22

brāhmaṇyam yadi me prāptam dīrgham āyuḥ tathaiva cha || 1-65-22
Oṃ kāro atha vaṣhaṭ kāro vedāḥ cha varayantu mām |

22b, 23a. me = to me; brāhmaṇyam prāptam yadi = Brahman-hood, befallen, if; tathaiva = like that; dīrgham āyuḥ ca = long, life [eternality,] also - if I got them; atha = then; AUṃ = Om; kāraḥ = syllable [quintessence of]; vaṣaṭ kāraḥ = vaṣaṭ, syllable [quintessence of]; vedāḥ ca = Veda-s, even; mām varayantu = me, let them patronise.

" 'If Brahman-hood and eternality have befallen on me, let the quintessence of AUṃ and vaṣat syllables, and even all Veda-s patronise me. [1-65-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

kṣhatra vedavidām śhreṣhṭho brahma vedavidām api || 1-65-23
brahma putro vasiṣhṭho mām evam vadatu devatāḥ |
yadi ayam paramaḥ kāmaḥ kṛto yāntu surarṣhabhāḥ || 1-65-24

23b, 24a. devatāḥ = oh, gods; kṣatra veda vidām = Kshatriya's, knowledge [kingcraft,] among geniuses; brahma veda vidām api = Brahman, knowledge, among knowers [scholars of Veda-s,] even; shreṣṭhaḥ = the outstanding one; brahma putraḥ = Brahma's, son [brainchild]; vasiṣṭhaḥ = Vashishta; mām evam vadatu = me, in this way, say [acknowledge]; ayam paramaḥ kāmaḥ = this, ultimate, yearning; kṛitaḥ yadi = done, if; sura ṛiṣabhāḥ = gods, the best ones; yāntu = you may leave.

" 'Oh, gods, he who is the outstanding one among the geniuses of kingcraft, and among the scholars of Veda-s as well, even that Vashishta, the brainchild of Brahma, shall acknowledge me in this way as Brahma-sage. Oh, the best gods, you may take leave if you can effectuate this ultimate yearning of mine.' Thus Vishvamitra requested the gods. [1-65-23b, 24]

Sloka 25

tataḥ prasādito devaiḥ vasiṣhṭho japatām varaḥ |
sakhyam chakāra brahmarṣhiḥ evam astu iti cha abravīt || 1-65-25

25. tataḥ devaiḥ prasāditaḥ = then, by gods, one who is besought - Vashishta; japatām varaḥ vasiṣṭhaḥ = among meditators, the best, Vashishta; [has come to Vishvamitra]; sakhyam cakāra = friendship, made; evam = thus; brahmarṣiḥ astu = Brahma-sage, you are; iti ca abravīt = in this way, also, said.

"Then the best one among meditators Vashishta has come over there when the gods besought him to come, and he made friendship with Vishvamitra. He also said in this way to Vishvamitra, 'you are a Brahma-sage.' [1-65-25]

Sloka 26

brahmarṣhiḥ tvam na saṃdehaḥ sarvam saṃpadyate tava |
iti uktvā devatāḥ cha api sarvā jagmuḥ yathā āgatam || 1-65-26

26. tvam brahmarṣiḥ = you are, Brahma-sage; sandehaḥ na = doubt, is not there; tava sarvam sampadyate = to you, everything, will accrue - in accordance with this sageship; iti uktvā = thus, on saying; sarvā devatāḥ ca api = all, gods, also, even; yathā āgatam jagmuḥ = as, they have come, went away.

"No doubt, you are a Brahma-sage and everything will accrue to you in accordance with the sublimity of this sageship,' and when Vashishta said so to Vishvamitra, all the gods have gone away as they have come. [1-65-26]

Sloka 27

viśhvāmitro api dharmātmā labdhvā brāhmaṇyam uttamam |
pūjayāmāsa brahmarṣhim vasiṣhṭham japatām varam || 1-65-27

27. dharmātmā vishvāmitraḥ api = virtue-souled one, Vishvamitra, even; uttamam brāhmaṇyam labdhvā = supreme, Brahman-hood on getting; brahmarṣim japatām varam vasiṣṭham = Brahma-sage, among meditators, the best one, at Vashishta; pūjayāmāsa = started to reverence.

"On getting his Brahman-hood even the virtue-souled Vishvamitra started to reverence the supreme among meditators and his counterpart Brahma-sage, namely Vashishta. [1-65-27]

Sloka 28

kṛta kāmo mahīm sarvām chachāra tapasi sthitaḥ |
evam tu anena brāhmaṇyam prāptam rāma mahātmanā || 1-65-28

28. kṛita kāmaḥ = effectuated, aim - whose aim is effectuated; tapasi sthitaḥ = in ascesis, abiding; sarvām mahīm cacāra = all over, earth, he ambled; rāma = oh, Rama; mahā tmanā = by great souled one; anena = by him; evam = in this way; brāhmaṇyam prāptam = Brahman-hood, is achieved.

"When his aim is effectuated Vishvamitra ambled all over the earth abiding in ascesis, and oh, Rama, this great souled sage Vishvamitra achieved Brahman-hood in this way. [1-65-28]

Sloka 29

eṣha rāma muni śhreṣhṭha eṣha vigrahavān tapaḥ |
eṣha dharmaḥ paro nityam vīryasya eṣha parāyaṇam || 1-65-29

29. rāma = oh, Rama; eṣa = he is; muni shreṣṭha = saint, best; eṣa vigrahavān tapaḥ = he is, embodiment of, ascesis; eṣa nityam dharmaḥ paraḥ = he is, always, in righteousness, obliged with; eṣa vīryasya parāyaṇam = he is, for fortitude, dwelling house stonghold.

"Oh, Rama, he is the best saint, he is the embodiment of ascesis, he is always obliged with righteousness, and he is the stronghold for fortitude." [1-65-29]

Sloka 30

evam uktvā mahātejā virarāma dvijottamaḥ |
śhatānaṃda vachaḥ śhrutvā rāma lakṣhmaṇa saṃnidhau ||1-65-30
janakaḥ prāṃjaliḥ vākyam uvācha kuśhikāatmajam |

30, 31a. mahātejāḥ dvijottamaḥ = great-resplendent, Brahman, the best [ṣhatānanda]; evam uktvā virarāma = that way, on saying, took respite; janakaḥ = King Janaka; rāma lakṣmaṇa sannidhau = Rama, Lakshmana, in the presence of; shatānanda vacaḥ shrutvā = ṣhatānanda's, words, on listening; prānjaliḥ = with adjoined palms; kushikāatmajam vākyam uvāca = Kushika's, son sentence, said to.

On narrating the legend of Vishvamitra in this way that best Brahman and great-resplendent Sage Shatānanda took respite. And on listening the narration of Sage Shatānanda said in the presence of Rama and Lakshmana, king Janaka said this sentence to Kushika's son, Vishvamitra, with suppliantly adjoined palms. [1-65-30b, 31a]

Sloka 31

dhanyo asmi anugṛhīto asmi yasya me munipuṃgava || 1-65-31
yajñam kākutstha sahitaḥ prāptavān asi kauśhika |

31b, 32a. kaushika = oh, Kaushika; munipungava = oh, sage, the eminent; kākutstha sahitaḥ = Kakutstha-s, along with; yasya = which; me yajñam = to my, Vedic-ritual; prāptavān asi = happened to come, you have; such as I am; dhanyaḥ asmi = fortunate, I am; anugṛihītaḥ asmi = much obliged, I am.

"Oh, eminent sage Vishvamitra, you happened to be here at this Vedic-ritual which is conducted by me, oh, Kaushika, that too, along with Rama and Lakshmana, the legatees of Kakutstha, thereby I am fortunate, I am much obliged. [31b, 32a]

Sloka 32

pāvito aham tvayā brahman darśhanena mahāmune || 1-65-32
guṇā bahu vidhāḥ prāptāḥ tava saṃdarśhanāt mayā |

32b, 33a. mahāmune brahman = oh, great saint, oh, Brahman; tvayā darshanena = by you, when see - by your graceful glance; aham pāvitaḥ = I am, consecrated; tava sandarshanāt = you, by [my] beholding; maya bahu vidhāḥ guṇāḥ prāptāḥ = many, kinds, of attributes [boons,] achieved [by me.]

"Oh, Brahman, I stand consecrated just by your graceful glance, and oh, great saint, I reckon that I have achieved many boons just glancing at you. [1-65-32b, 33a]

Sloka 33

vistareṇa cha vai brahman kīrtyamānam mahattapaḥ || 1-65-33
śhrutam mayā mahātejo rāmeṇa cha mahātmanā |

33b, 34a. mahā tejaḥ brahman = oh, great-resplendent, Brahman; vistareṇa kīrtyamānam = comprehensively, being extolled; [te = your]; mahattapaḥ = great ascesis; maya = by me; as well as; mahātmanā rāmeṇa = by noble souled, Rama; shrutam vai = heard, indeed.

"Oh, great-resplendent Brahman, myself and the noble souled Rama have heard about your great ascesis when Sage Shatānanda extolled it comprehensively. [1-65-33b, 34a]

Sloka 34

sadasyaiḥ prāpya cha sadaḥ śhrutāḥ te bahavo guṇāḥ || 1-65-34
aprameyam tapaḥ tubhyam aprameyam cha te balam |
aprameyā guṇāḥ chaiva nityam te kuśhikātmaja || 1-65-35

34b, 35. sadasyaiḥ sadaḥ prāpya = by congregationalists, congregation, [who have] come - who are available in; te bahavaḥ guṇāḥ shrutāḥ = your, numerous, endowments, are heard; tubhyam tapaḥ aprameyam = your, ascesis, is inestimable; te balam ca aprameyam = your, power, also, is infinite; kushikātmaja = oh, son of Kushika; te guṇāḥ caiva = your, talents, also thus; nityam aprameyā = everlastingly, invaluable.

"The congregationalists available in this Vedic-ritual congregation have also heard about your numerous endowments. Inestimable is your ascesis, infinite is your power, and oh, son of Kushika, your talents are everlastingly invaluable. [1-65-34b, 35]

Sloka 36

tṛptiḥ āśhcharya bhūtānām kathānām na asti me vibho |
karma kālo muni śhreṣhṭha lambate ravi maṇḍalam || 1-65-36

36. vibho = oh, lord; āshcarya bhūtānām kathānām = wonder, causing, of narratives; me tṛiptiḥ na asti = to me, contentment, not, is there; muni shreṣṭha = oh, saint, the best; ravi maṇDalam lambate = sun's, sphere, dangling; karma kālaḥ = ritual, time; [abhivartate = is approaching.]

"Oh, lord, there is no contentment to me while listening to your wondrous narratives, but oh nobleman, time for vespertine ritual is fast approaching as sun's sphere is dangling westward. [1-65-36]

Sloka 37

śhvaḥ prabhāte mahātejo draṣhṭum arhasi mām punaḥ |
svāgatam japatām śhreṣhṭha mām anujñātum arhasi || 1-65-37

37. mahātejaḥ = oh, great-resplendent sage; shvaḥ prabhāte = tomorrow, morning; mām punaḥ draṣṭum arhasi = me, again to see, apt of you; japatām shreṣṭha = oh, meditator, the best; svāgatam = you are welcome; mām anujñātum arhasi = me [to leave,] to permit, apt of you.

"Oh, great-resplendent sage, it will be apt of you to see me tomorrow. Oh, best meditator, it will be apt of you to permit me to leave for now." Thus Janaka sought leave from Vishvamitra. [1-65-37]

Sloka 38

evam ukto munivaraḥ praśhasya puruṣharṣhabham |
visasarja āśhu janakam prītam prītimān tadāa || 1-65-38

38. tadāa = then; evam uktaḥ munivaraḥ = that way, who is spoken to [Vishvamitra,] saint, the best; prītimān [prīta manāḥ] = pleased, at heart; prītam puruṣa rṣabham janakam = pleased, to the best one among men, to Janaka; prashasya = on praising; āshu visasarja = immediately, released - bid farewell.

When he is spoken in that way, the best saint Vishvamitra is pleased at heart, and praising Janaka, who too is pleased to meet the sage, immediately bid farewell to the best one among men, namely Janaka. [1-65-38]

Sloka 39

evam uktvā muni śhreṣhṭham vaideho mithilā adhipaḥ |
pradakṣhiṇam chakāra āśhu sa upādhyāyaḥ sa bāṃdhavaḥ || 1-65-39

39. vaidehaḥ = legatee of Videha kingdom; mithilā adhipaḥ = ṃithila's, king; muni shreṣṭham evam uktvā = to saint, the best, in this way, on saying; sa upādhyāyaḥ sa bāndhavaḥ = with, teachers, with relatives; āshu pradakṣiṇam cakāra = immediately, circumambulations, performed.

This way on saying to the best saint, the king of ṃithila and the legatee of Videha lineage, immediately performed circumambulations along with his teachers and relatives to Vishvamitra in veneration. [1-65-39]

Sloka 40

viśhvāmitro api dharmātmā saha rāmaḥ sa lakṣhmaṇaḥ |
svam vāsam abhichakrāma pūjyamāno maharṣhibhiḥ || 1-65-40

40. dharmātmā vishvāmitraḥ api = virtue souled one, Vishvamitra, even; saha rāmaḥ sa lakṣmaṇaḥ = with, Rama, with Lakshmana; maharṣibhiḥ pūjyamānaḥ = by great sages, being venerated; svam vāsam abhicakrāma = his own, camp, started towards.

Even that virtue souled Vishvamitra started towards his own camp along with Rama and Lakshmana, while being venerated by great sages who are available in the ritual-hall. [1-65-40]

...

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye bāla kāṇḍe paṃcha ṣhaṣhṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 65th chapter in Bala Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
