Valmiki Ramayana

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 50
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama bids farewell to his birth-place Ayodhya and reaches the bank of the holy Ganga. There, Nishadas offers welcome reception to Rama and others.

Sloka 1

viśālān kosalān ramyān yātvā lakṣmaṇa pūrvajaḥ |
ayodhyābhimukho dhīmān prāññlirvākvamabravīt || 2-50-1

1. yatvā = having crossed; ramyān = the beautiful; vishālān = and the extensive; kosalān = Kosala territory; ayodhyābhimukhah = and standing with his face turned towards Ayodhya; dhīmān = the wise; lakshmaṇa pūrvajaḥ = Rama(elder brother of Lakshmana); prāṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words.

Having traveled the extensive and beautiful Kosala territory and standing with his face turned towards Ayodhya, the wise Rama with joined palms spoke the following words:

Sloka 2

āpṛcche tvām purīśreṣṭhe kākutsthaparipālite |
daivatāni ca yāni tvām pālayantyāvasanti ca || 2-50-2

2. purīshreshhṭe = " Oh Ayodhya; best of cities; kākutstha paripālite = ruled by Dasaratha born in kakutstha dynasty! āpṛichchhe = I bid farewell; tvām = to you; yāni = and to those; daivatāni = deities; pālayanticha = who protect you and ; āvasanti = dwelling on your boundaries."

"I bid farewell to you, Oh best of the cities, carefully protected by Dasaratha born in Kakutstha dynasty, as well as of the deities who protect you and dwell in you."

Sloka 3

nivṛttavanavāsastvāmanṛṇo jagatīpateḥ |
punardhrakṣyāmi mātrā ca pitrā ca saha samgataḥ || 2-50-3

3. "nivṛitta vanavāsinaḥ = after getting relieved from dwelling in the forest; anṛiṇaḥ = and becoming free of debt; jagatīpate = to the king Dasaratha; drakshhyāmi = I shall see; tvām = you; punaḥ = again; sangataḥ = united with; mātrācha = my mother; pitrācha saha = along with my father."

"After getting relieved from exile in the forest and thus freed from the debt to the emperor, I shall see you again, duly getting united with my mother and father."

Sloka 4

tato rudhiratāmrākṣo bhujamudyamya dakṣiṇam |
aśrupūrṇamukho dīno.abravījjānapadam janam || 2-50-4

4. udyamya = lifting; dakshhiṇam = (his)right; bhujam = arm; dīnaḥ = and wearing a woeful look; ashrupūrṇamukho = his face covered with tears; rudhiratāmrākshhaḥ = and who was having lovely reddish eyes; abravīt = (Rama) spoke; janam = to the people; jānapadam = hailing from countryside.

Lifting his right arm and wearing a woeful look, his face covered with tears, Rama possessing lovely reddish eyes, spoke them to the people hailing from the countryside.(as follows) :

Sloka 5

anukrośo dayā caiva yathārham mayi vah kṛtaḥ |
ciram duḥkhasya pāpīyo gamyatāmarthasiddhaye || 2-50-5

5. dayāchaiva = mercy and; anukroshaḥ = compassion; yathārham = according to my worth; kṛitaḥ = have been shown; mayi = to me; vaḥ = by you. duḥkhasya = Agony; chiram = for a long time; pāpīyaḥ = is miserable. gamyatām = Let you be gone; ardhasiddhaye = for accomplishing your desired object (viz. attending to your house-hold work)."

"ṃercy and compassion according to my worth (in your view) have been shown to me by you. Keeping agony for a long time is undesirable. Let each return to accomplish your desired object(attending to your house-hold work)!"

Sloka 6

te.abhivādya mahātmānam kṛtvā cāpi pradakṣiṇam |
vilapanto narā ghoram vyatiṣṭhanta kvacit kvacit || 2-50-6

6. abhivādya = respectfully saluting; mahātmānam = the high-soled prince; pradakshhiṇamchāpi = and going round him clockwise (as a mark of respect); te narāḥ = those men; vyatishhṭanta = stood; kvachit kvachit = (rooted) here and there; vilapantaḥ = wailing; ghoram = frightfully.

Respectfully saluting the high soled prince and going round him clockwise (as a mark of reverence), those men stood rooted here and there, wailing frightfully.

Sloka 7

tathā vilapatām teṣāmatṛptānām ca rāghavaḥ |
acakṣuriṣayam prāyādyathārkaḥ kṣaṇadāmukhe || 2-50-7

7. teshhām = (while) they; vilapatām = were lamenting; tathā = thus; atṛiptānām = unceasingly; rāghavaḥ = Rama; prāyāt = passed; achakshhurvishhayam = beyond their sight; arkaḥ yathā = as the sun; kshhaṇadāmukhe = at nightfall.

While they were lamenting thus unceasingly, Rama passed beyond their sight, as the sun sinks out of view at nightfall.

Sloka 8

tato dhānyadhanopetān dānaśīlajanān śivān |
akutaścidbhayān ramyām ścaityayūpasamāvṛtān || 2-50-8
udyānāmravanopetān sampannasalilāśayān |
tuṣṭapuṣṭajanākīrṇān gokulākulasevitān || 2-50-9
lakṣaṇīyānna reṃdrāṇām brahmaghoṣābhināditān |
rathena puruṣavyāghraḥ kosalānatyavartata || 2-50-10

8;9;10. tataḥ = then; purushha vyāghraḥ = that tiger among men; rathena = in his chariot; atyavartata = crossed; kosalān = Kosala territory; dhānya dhanopetān = which was rich in grain and treasure; dāna shīla janān = inhabited by men given to charity; shivān akutashchidbhayān = benevolent and free from every danger; ramyān = pleasing; chaitya yūpa samāvṛitān = full of temples and sacrificial stakes; udyānāmravanopetān = adorned with gardens and mango orchards; sampanna salilāshayān = intersected by ponds full of water; tushhṭa pushhṭa janākīrṇān = inhabited by well nourished people; gokulākula sevitān = abounded in herds of cows; lakshhaṇīyān = which deserved to be seen by ; narendrāṇām = by all kings; brahmaghoshhābhināditān = and which re- echoed to the charity of religious texts.

Then Rama, a tiger among men, in his chariot, crossed Kosala territory, which was rich in grain and treasure, inhabited by men given to charity, benevolent and free from every danger, pleasing, full of temples and sacrificial stakes, adorned with gardens and mango-orchards, intersected by ponds full of water, populated by contented and well nourished people, abounded in herds of cows which deserved to be seen by all kings and which re-echoed to the chanting of religious texts.

Sloka 11

madhyena muditam sphītam ramyodyānasamākulam |
rājyam bhogyam narendrāṇām yayau dhṛtimatām varaḥ || 2-50-11

11. varaḥ = the best; dhṛitimatām = among the resolute;yayou = moved;madhyena = into themiddle; muditam = of a happy; rājyam = kingdom; sphītam = which is prosperous; ramyodyāna samākulam = abounding in lovely gardens; bhogyam = fit to be enjoyed; narendrāṇām = by kings.

Rama, the best among the resolute, moved into the middle of a happy and prosperous kingdom, abounding in lovely gardens and fit to be enjoyed by kings.

Sloka 12

tatra tripathagām divyām śiva toyām aśaivalām |
dadarśa rāghavo gangām puṇyām ṛṣi nisevitām || 2-50-12

12. tatra = there; rāghavaḥ = Rama; dadarsha = beheld; puṇyām = the celestial; gaṇgām = Ganga river; tripathagām = which takes a three fold course (through three tributaries) ramyām = a lovely one; shiva toyām = carrying clear waters; ashaivalām = without a green moss; ṛishhi nishhevitām = and frequented by sages.

There, Rama beheld the celestial and lovely river of Ganga with its tree tributaries, carrying clear waters without green moss and frequented by sages.

Sloka 13

āśramairavidūrsthaiḥ śrīmadbhiḥ samalam kṛtām |
kāle.apsarobhirhṛṣṭābhiḥ sevitāmbhohradām śivām || 2-50-13

13. samalamkṛitām = adorned; avidūrasthaiḥ = not very far (from one another); shrīmadbhiḥ = splendid; āshramaiḥ = hermitages; sevitāmbhohradām = with pools overflowing with water visited; kāle = at suitable hours; apsarobhiḥ = by celestial nymphs; hṛishhṭābhiḥ = thrilling with rapture.

The River Ganga was adorned with splendid hermitages within easy distance from one another, with pools overflowing with water, visited at suitable hours by celestial nymphs thrilling with rapture.

Sloka 14

devadānavagandharvaiḥ kinnarairupaśobhitām |
nāgagandharvapatnībhiḥ sevitām satatam śivām || 2-50-14

14. upashobhitām = graced; deva dānava gandharvaiḥ = by gods and demons; Gandharvas (celestial musicians); kinnaraiḥ = (and) Kinnaras; satatam = constantly;sevitām = visited by; nāga gandharvapatnīḥ = consorts of ṇagas and Gandharvas.

The blessed river was graced by gods and demons, Gandharvas(celestial musicians) and kinnaras and was constantly visited by consorts of Nagas and Gandharvas.

Sloka 15

devākrīḍaśatākīrṇām devodyānaśatāyutām |
devārthamākāśagamām vikhyātām devapadminīm || 2-50-15

15. devā krīDa shatākīrṇām = the hills serving as the playground of hundreds of celestials; devodyāna shatāyutām = embellished with hundreds of celestial gardens; ākāshagamām = coursed through the heavens; devārtham = for the benefit of gods; vikhyātam = (and which in heaven) was named; devapadminīm = "ṭhe stream of Golden Lotuses".

The well known river was hemmed in by hills serving as the play- ground of hundreds of celestials and embellished with hundreds of celestial gardens; it coursed through the heavens for the benefit of gods and which in heaven was named "The stream of Golden Lotuses".

Sloka 16

jalaghātāṭṭahāsogrām phenanirmalahāsinīm |
kvacidveṇīkṛtajalām kvacidāvartaśobhitām || 2-50-16

16. jalāghātāṭṭahāsogrām = the sound of whose clashing waves and striking with rocks resembles deep laughter; phena nirmala hāsinīm = having a smile as white as a foam; veṇīkṛita jalām = its water reduced to the shape of a maiden's plaited locks; kvachit = at some places; āvarta shobhitām = rendered beautiful by whirl pools; kvachit = at some places.

The holy river, the sound of whose clashing waves and its striking with rocks resembled a deep laughter, having a bright smile as white as a foam, its waters reduced at some places to the shape of a maiden's plaited locks, was rendered beautiful at some places by whirl pools.

Sloka 17

kvacitstimitagambhīrām kvacidvegajalākulām |
kvacidgambhīranirghoṣām kvacidbhairavanisvanām || 2-50-17

17. kvachit = at some places; smita gambhīrām = its waters were still and deep; kvachit = at some places; vega jalākulām = they were disturbed with violent agitation; kvachit = at some places; gambhīra nirghoshhām = making a deep roaring sound; kvachit = at some places; bhairava niḥsvanām = having a terrific noise.

At some places, its waters were still and deep. At some places, they were disturbed with violent agitation. At some places, they made a deep roaring sound. At some places, they were having a terrific noise

Sloka 18

devasamghāplutajalām nirmalotpalaśobhitām |
kvacidābhogapulinām kvacinnarmalavālukām || 2-50-18

18. deva sanghāpluta jalām = hosts of gods took a dip into its water; nirmalotpala shobhitām = adorned by white lotuses; kvachit = at some places; ābhogapulinām = with expanse of small islands; kvachit = at some places; nirmala vālukām = lined with white sands.

Hosts of gods took a dip into its water .It was adorned by white lotuses. It was hemmed in, with expanse of small islands and at some places, lined with white sands.

Sloka 19

haṃsa sarasa samghuṣṭām cakra vāka upakūjitām |
sadāmadaiśca vihagairabhisamnāditām tarām || 2-50-19

19. hamsa sārasa samghushṭām = rendered resonant by swans and cranes; chakravākopa kūjitām = with cackling of chakrawaka birds; vihagaiḥ = (other) birds; sadāmadaiḥ = which are in rut all through; abhisannāditamtarām = kept hovering in its middle. **-chakravaka is a species of bird; which gets disunited with its mate by night.

The river was rendered resonant by swans and cranes, was graced with cackling of chakrawaka** birds and other birds which are in rut all through the year kept hovering on its water.

Sloka 20

kvacittīraruhairvṛkṣairmālābhiriva śobhitām |
kvacitphullotpalacchannām kvacitpadmavanākulām || 2-50-20

20. kvachit = at some places; shobhitām = (the river) was adorned; vṛikshhaiaḥ = with trees; tīraruhaiḥ = growing on its banks; mālābhiriva = (encircling) like garlands; kvachit = at some places; phullotpalachchhannām = covered with widely opened lotuses; padmavanākulām = crowded with thick cluster of lotuses.

At some places the river was adorned with trees growing on its banks encircling like garlands, at some places crowded with thick cluster of lotuses.

Sloka 21

kvacitkumudaṣṇḍaiśca kuḍmalairupaśobhitām |
nānāpuṣparajodhvastām samadāmiva ca kvacit || 2-50-21

21. kvachit = at some places; upashobhitām = graced with; kumuda shhaṇDaishcha = beds of water lilies; kuDmalaiḥ = in the farm of buds; kvachit = at some places; nānā pushhpa rajodhvastām = reddened with the pollen of numerous flowers; samadām iva = as though excited with passion.

At some places, it was graced with beds of water lilies in the form of buds and at some places, it was reddened with the pollen of numerous flowers, as though excited with passion.

Sloka 22

vyapetamalasamghātām maṇinirmaladarśanām |
diśāgajairvanagajairmattaiśca varavāraṇaiḥ || 2-50-22
devopavāhyaśca muhuḥ samnāditavanāntarām |

22. vyapeta mala samghātām = having every aggregation of dirt removed; maṇinirmala darshanām = presenting a spotlessly clear appearance like a crystal; samnādita vanāntarām = the interior of the forest rendered noisy; muhuḥ = again and again; dishāgajaiḥ = by the elephants guarding the quarters; mattaiḥ = ruttiest; vanagajaiḥ = wild elephants; varavāraṇaiḥ = elephants of excellent breed; devopavāhaiḥ = used for riding by gods.

Having every aggregation of dirt removed, the river presented a spotlessly clear appearance like a crystal. The interior of the forest was reddened noisy again and again by the elephants guarding the quarters, by the ruttiest wild elephants and the elephants of excellent breed used for riding by gods.

Sloka 23

pramadāmiva yatne na bhūṣitām bhūṣaṇottamaiḥ || 2-50-23
phalaiḥ puṣpaiḥ kisalayairvaṛtām gulmaiddvijaistathā |
śiṃśumaraiḥ ca nakraiḥ ca bhujamgaiḥ ca niṣevitām || 2-50-24

23;24. vṛitām = surrounded; phalaiḥ = by fruits; pushhpaiḥ = flowers; kisalayaiḥ = tender leaves; gulmaiḥ = shrubs; tathā = and; dvijaiḥ = birds; pramadāmiva = it looked like a young woman; yat nena = carefully;bhūshhitām = decked with; bhūshhaṇottamaiḥ = the best of jewels. nishhevitām = infested with; shimshumāraishcha = dolphins; nakraishcha = crocodiles; bhujangaishcha = and snakes.

Surrounded by fruits and flowers and tender leaves, shrubs and birds, it looked like a young woman artistically decked with the best of jewels. The river was infested with dolphins, crocodiles and snakes.

Sloka 25

viṣṇupādacyutām divyāmapāpām pāpanāśinīm |
tām śaṅkarajaṭājūṭādbhraṣṭām sāgaratejasā || 2-50-25
samudramahīṣīm gaṅgām sārasakrauñcanāditām |
āsasāda mahābāhuḥ śṛṅgiberapuram prati || 2-50-26

25;26. mahābāhuḥ = Rama;the mighty armed; āsasāda = reached; tām gangām = that river Ganga; vishhṇu pādachyutām = which flows from the feet of Lord Vishnu; apāpām = which is devoid of sins; pāpanāshinīm = and dispels all sins; bhrashhṭām = and which had fallen; shankara jaṭājūṭāt = from the mass of matted hair of Lord ṣhankara; sāgara tejasā = through the spiritual power of the descendent of ṣagara(Emperor Bhagiratha) ; samudra krounchanāditām = which was rendered noisy by cranes and herons; shṛiṇ^giberapuram prati = in the vicinity of ṣringaverapura( the modern ṣingraur.)

Rama, the mighty armed, reached the river Ganga, which is devoid of sins and which dispels all sins, which had fallen from the mass of matted hair of Lord Shankara through the spiritual power of Emperor Bhagiratha, which is rendered noisy by cranes and herons, which is a consort of the ocean and which is in the vicinity of Sringaverapura(the modern Singraur).

Sloka 27

tām ūrmi kalila āvartām anvavekṣya mahā rathaḥ |
sumantram abravīt sūtam iha eva adya vasāmahe || 2-50-27

27. anvavekshhya = beholding; tām = that river Ganga; ūrmi kalilāvartām = having whirlpools covered by waves; mahārathaḥ = Rama; the mighty warrior; abravīt = said; sumantram = to ṣumantra; sūtam = the charioteer; vasāmahe = "we shall halt; ihaiva = here itself; adya = today."

Beholding the river Ganga with its waves covering whirlpools, Rama the great warrior said to Sumantra the charioteer as follows: "We shall halt here itself today."

Sloka 28

avidūrāt ayam nadyā bahu puṣpa pravālavān |
sumahān ingudī vṛkṣo vasāmaḥ atra eva sārathe || 2-50-28

28. sārathe = "Oh; charioteer! avidūrāt = ṇot distant; nadyāḥ = from the river; sumahān = there is a very big; iyam = this one; iṇ^gudīvṛikshhaḥ = the sacred fig tree; bahu pushhpa pravāLavān = with many flowers and shoots. Vasāmaḥ = (We) shall stay; atraiva = here itself."

"Oh, charioteer! Not far from the river stands this very large sacred fig tree with its many flowers and shoots. We shall stay here itself."

Sloka 29

drakṣyāmaḥ saritām śreṣṭhām sammānyasalilām śivām |
devadānavagandharvamṛgamānuṣapakṣiṇām || 2-50-29

29. drakshhyāmaḥ = "I shall see; saritām shreshhṭām = the excellent river Ganga; shivām = which is auspicious; deva dānava gandharva mṛiga mānushha pakshhiṇām = to gods; men; Gandharvas; beasts; reptiles and birds; sam mānya salilām = whose waters are respectable."

"I shall see (from here) the excellent river Ganga, which is auspicious and whose waters deserve to be respected by gods, men, Gandharvas, beasts, reptiles and birds ."

Sloka 30

lakṣaṇaḥ ca sumantraḥ ca bāḍham iti eva rāghavam |
uktvā tam ingudī vṛkṣam tadā upayayatur hayaiḥ || 2-50-30

30. uktvā = saying; bādhamityeva = �ṣo be it' and nothing more; rāghavam = to Rama; lakshhmaṇashcha = Lakshmana; sumantrashcha = and ṣumantra; tadā = then; upayayatuḥ = approached; hayaiḥ = by horses; tam = that; iṇ^gudīvṛikshham = sacred fig tree.

Saying �so be it' and nothing more to Rama, Lakshmana and Sumantra too then directed the horses to that sacred fig tree.

Sloka 31

rāmaḥ abhiyāya tam ramyam vṛkṣam ikṣvāku nandanaḥ |
rathāt avātarat tasmāt sabhāryaḥ saha lakṣmaṇaḥ || 2-50-31

31. rāmaḥ = Rama; ikshhvāku nandanaḥ = the delight of Ikshvaku dynasty; abhiyāya = reached; tam vṛikshham = that tree; ramyam = the beautiful one; avātarat = and got down; tasmāt rathāt = from that chariot; sabhāryaḥ = along with his wife; saha lakshhmaṇaḥ = and with Lakshmana.

Rama, the delight of Ikshvaku dynasty, reached that beautiful tree and got down from the chariot along with Sita and Lakshmana.

Sloka 32

sumantraḥ api avatīrya eva mocayitvā haya uttamān |
vṛkṣa mūla gatam rāmam upatasthe kṛta anjaliḥ || 2-50-32

32. sumantro api = ṣumantra too; avatīrya = got down; asmāt = from the chariot; mochayitvā = released; hayottamān = the superb horses; kṛitāṇjaliḥ = with joined palms; upatasthe = (he) seated himself near; rāmam = Rama; vṛikshha mūla gatam = at the foot of the tree.

Sumantra too dismounted from the chariot, unyoked the superb horses after which with joined palms, he seated himself near Rama at the foot of the tree.

Sloka 33

tatra rājā guho nāma rāmasya ātma samaḥ sakhā |
niṣāda jātyo balavān sthapatiḥ ca iti viśrutaḥ || 2-50-33

33. tatra = ṭhere; rājā = a king; guho nāma = named Guha; rāmasya = Rama's; sakhā = friend; ātmasamaḥ = equal to his life; nishhāda jātasya = ṇishada by birth; balavān = a strong man; vishrutaḥ = well known; sthapatishcheti = as ruler of ṇishadas.

There, a king named Guha was Rama's friend dear to him as his own life. He was Nishada by birth, a strong man and well known as a ruler of Nishadas.

Sloka 34

sa śrutvā puruṣa vyāghram rāmam viṣayam āgatam |
vṛddhaiḥ parivṛtaḥ amātyaiḥ jñātibhiḥ ca api upāgataḥ || 2-50-34

34. shrutvā = hearing of; rāmam = Rama; purushhavyāghram = the tiger among men; āgatam = having come; vishhayam = to his territory; saḥ = he; abhyupāgataḥ = approached; parivṛitaḥ = accompanied by; vṛiddhaiḥ = elderly; amātyaiḥ = ministers; G^yātibhishcha = and relatives.

Hearing of Rama the tiger among men having come to his territory, he approached the prince, escorted by elderly ministers and relatives.

Sloka 35

tataḥ niṣāda adhipatim dṛṣṭvā dūrāt avasthitam |
saha saumitriṇā rāmaḥ samāgaccad guhena saḥ || 2-50-35

35. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; dūrāt = from the distance; nishhādādhipatim = the king of ṇishada; upasthitam = coming; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; soumitriṇā saha = along with Lakshmana; tataḥ = thereupon; samāgachchhat = went forth to meet; guhena = Guha.

Seeing from a distance the king of Nishada coming, Rama along with Lakshmana thereupon went forth to meet Guha.

Sloka 36

tam ārtaḥ sampariṣvajya guho rāghavam abravīt |
yathā ayodhyā tathā idam te rāma kim karavāṇi te || 2-50-36
īdṛśam hi mahābāho kaḥ prapsyatyatithim priyam |

36. samparishhvajya = closely embracing; tam rāghavam = that Rama; guhaḥ = Guha; ārtaḥ = who felt disturbed; abravīt = spoke to him; iyam = "ṭhis city too ; yathā tathā = is in every way as ; ayodhyā = Ayodhya; kim = what; karavāṇi = can I do; te = for you? mahābāho = Oh; mighty armed! kaḥ hi = who indeed; prāpsyasi = will get; īdṛisham = such; priyam = a lovely; atithim = guest?"

Closely embracing Rama, Guha who felt disturbed, spoke to him, "This city too is as much as Ayodhya to you. What can I do for you? Oh mighty armed! Who indeed will get such a lovely guest?"

Sloka 37

tataḥ guṇavad anna adyam upādāya pṛthag vidham |
arghyam ca upānayat kṣipram vākyam ca idam uvāca ha || 2-50-37

37. upādāya = having brought; annādyam = pristine cooked rice; guṇavat = of excellent quality; prithagvidham = and other dishes of various kinds; tataḥ = (he) then; kshhipram = quickly; upānayat = offered (him); arghyamcha = water to wash his hands;uvācha ha = and spoke; idam = these; vākyamcha = words:

Having brought pristine cooked rice of excellent quality and other dishes of various kinds, he then quickly offered him water to wash his hands and spoke as follows:

Sloka 38

svāgatam te mahā bāho tava iyam akhilā mahī |
vayam preṣyā bhavān bhartā sādhu rājyam praśādhi naḥ || 2-50-38

38. svāgatam = "welcome; te = to you; mahābāho = oh; mighty armed! akhilā = All; iyam mahī = this land; tava = is yours. vayam = We; preshhyāḥ = are servants. bhavān = You; bhartā = are the Lord. Prashādhi = Rule over; naḥ = our; rājyam = kingdom; sādhu = efficiently."

"Welcome to you, Oh, mighty armed! All this land is yours. We are your servants. You are the Lord. Rule over our kingdom in an efficient way."

Sloka 39

bhakṣyam bhojyam ca peyam ca lehyam ca idam upasthitam |
śayanāni ca mukhyāni vājinām khādanam ca te || 2-50-39

39. idam = "here; upasthitam = are arrived; bhakshhyam bhojyam = dishes of various kinds; peyam = drinks; lehyamcha = and syrups; mukhyāni = as also excellent; shayanāni = beds; khādinamcha = and food; te = for your; vājinām = horses."

"Here have arrived various kinds of dishes, drinks and syrups as also excellent beds (for you to sleep on) and food for your horses"

Sloka 40

guham eva bruvāṇam tam rāghavaḥ pratyuvāca ha || 2-50-40
arcitāḥ caiva hṛṣṭāḥ ca bhavatā sarvathā vayam |
padbhyām abhigamāc caiva sneha saṃdarśanena ca || 2-50-41

40;41. guham = to Guha; bruvāṇam = who was speaking; evam = as aforesaid; rāghavaḥ = Rama; pratyuvācha ha = replied thus: architāshchaiva = "we stand honoured; bhavatā = by you; abhigamachchaiva = by your very visit; padbhyām = on foot; snehasamdarshanenacha = as well as your show of affection; hṛishhṭāshcha = and are pleased(with you)."

To Guha who was speaking as aforesaid, Rama replied thus: "We stand honored by you, by your very visit to us on foot, as well as your show of affection and are pleased with you"

Sloka 42

bhujābhyām sādhu vṛttābhyām pīḍayan vākyam abravīt |
diṣṭyā tvām guha paśyāmiarogam saha bāndhavaiḥ || 2-50-42
api te kūśalam rāṣṭre mitreṣu ca dhaneṣu ca |

42;43. pīdayam = pressing; sādhu = gently; pinābhyām = with his muscular; bhujābhyām = arms; abravīt = (Rama) spoke; vākyam = (these) words; guha = "Oh; Guha! dishhṭyā = ṭhank heaven; pashyāmi = I am seeing; tvām = you; arogam = in good health; saha bāndhavaiḥ = with your relatives;api kushalam = is all well; te = in your; rāshhṭre = kingdom; mitreshhu = (with your) friends; dhaneshhucha = and the treasure?"

Pressing gently with his muscular arms, Rama spoke these words: " Oh, Guha! Thank heaven that I am seeing you in good health with your relatives. Is all well with the kingdom, the allies and the treasure?"

Sloka 43

yat tu idam bhavatā kiṃcit prītyā samupakalpitam |
sarvam tat anujānāmi na hi varte pratigrahe || 2-50-43

43. anujānāmi = I know; idam prītya = this affection; yat kimchit = by which; tat sarvam = all that; samabhikalpitam = is extensively well- arranged; bhavatā = by you. na varte hi = I am not in a condition indeed to; pratigrahe = accept."

"I know your affection by which all this is extensively well arranged by you. But I am not in a position indeed to accept it."

Sloka 44

kuśa cīra ajina dharam phala mūla aśanam ca mām |
viddhi praṇihitam dharme tāpasam vana gocaram || 2-50-44

44. viddhi = know; mām = me; praṇihitam = as under a vow; tāpasam = to be an ascetic; kushachīrājinadharam = wearing the robes of bark and deerskin; dharma = and by piety; praṇihitam = I am determined; vanacharam = to live in the forest; phalamūlāshinam = eating fruits and roots."

"Know me as under a vow to be an ascetic, wearing the robes of bark and deerskin and by piety, I am determined to live in the forest by eating roots and fruits only."

Sloka 45

aśvānām khādanena aham arthī na anyena kenacit |
etāvatā atra bhavatā bhaviṣyāmi supūjitaḥ || 2-50-45

45. aham = "I; arthī = am the one who desire; khādanena = only food; ashvānām = for the horses; na = not; anyena kenachit = and any other else. Etāvatā = by this much only; bhavitā = I shall be; supūjitaḥ = well = satisfied; atra = at this time; bhavatā = by you."

"I desire nothing but a little forage for the horses. By being provided with this much at the present moment, I shall be duly satisfied by you."

Sloka 46

ete hi dayitā rājñaḥ pitur daśarathasya me |
etaiḥ suvihitaiḥ aśvaiḥ bhaviṣyāmy aham arcitaḥ || 2-50-46

46. ete = " these (horses); dayitā = cherished; dasarathasya rāG^yaḥ = by the king Dasaratha; me pituḥ = my father; aham = I; bhavishhyāmi = shall be; architaḥ = honored; etaiḥ ashvaiḥ = by these horses; suvihitaiḥ = being duly fed."

"These horses were cherished by the king Dasaratha, my father. I shall feel honored by these horses being duly fed."

Sloka 47

aśvānām pratipānam ca khādanam caiva so anvaśāt |
guhaḥ tatra eva puruṣāṃs tvaritam dīyatām iti || 2-50-47

47. saḥ = that; guhaḥ = Guha; tatraiva = on that spot; anvashāt = commanded; purushhān = his men (as follows); " pratipānamcha = "Let water for drinking; khādanamchaiva = and forage; dīyatāmiti = be supplied; tvaritam = promptly; ashvānām = to horses."

Then Guha on that spot commanded his men as follows: "Let water for drinking and forage be supplied promptly to horses"

Sloka 48

tataḥ cīra uttara āsangaḥ saṃdhyām anvāsya paścimām |
jalam eva ādade bhojyam lakṣmaṇena āhṛtam svayam || 2-50-48

48. anvāsya = having worshipped; pashchimām sandhyām = the evening twilight appearing in the west; chīrottarāsaṇgaḥ = with an upper garment made of bark; tataḥ = (Rama) then; ādade = took; bhojyam = for food; jalameva = only water; āhṛitam = brought; lakshhmaṇena = by Lakshmana; svayam = himself.

Having worshipped the evening twilight appearing in the west, with an upper garment made of bark(on his person); Rama then took for food only water brought by Lakshmana himself.

Sloka 49

tasya bhūmau śayānasya pādau prakṣālya lakṣmaṇaḥ |
sabhāryasya tataḥ abhyetya tasthau vṛṣkam upāśritaḥ || 2-50-49

49. prakshhālya = having washed; pādou = the feet; tasya = of Rama; shayanasya = who was lying; bhūmou = on the ground; sabhāryasya = along with his consort; lakshhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; tataḥ = then; abhyetya = came; tasthou = and stationed; upāshritaḥ = leaning against; vṛikshham = a tree.

Having washed the feet of Rama who was lying on the ground along with his consort, Lakshmana then came and stood near a tree.

Sloka 50

guho api saha sūtena saumitrim anubhāṣayan |
anvajāgrat tataḥ rāmam apramattaḥ dhanur dharaḥ || 2-50-50

50. guho.api = Guha too; sūtena saha = along with the charioteer; anubhāshhayan = conversed with; soumitrim = Lakshmana; tataḥ = and thereafter; dhanurdharaḥ = wielding a bow; apramattaḥ = alertly; anvajāgrat = kept a vigil; rāmam = over Rama.

Guha too along with the charioteer conversed with Lakshmana and thereafter wielding a bow; alertly kept a vigil over Rama.

Sloka 51

tathā śayānasya tataḥ asya dhīmataḥ |
yaśasvino dāśarather mahātmanaḥ |
adṛṣṭa duhkhasya sukha ucitasya sā |
tadā vyatīyāya cireṇa śarvarī || 2-50-51

51. yashasvinaḥ = the illustrious; dhīmataḥ = lofty minded; mahātmanaḥ = and the high-soled; asya dāsharathe = son of Dasaratha; adṛishhṭa duḥkhasya = who had never seen suffering; sukhochitasya = and who deserved all comforts; shayānasya = remained lying; tataḥ = thereafter; sā = while that; chireṇa = long; sharvarī = night; vyatīyāya = passed away.

The illustrious, lofty minded and the high-soled son of Dasaratha, who had never seen suffering and who deserved all comforts, remained lying on the ground thereafter, while that long night passed away.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe pañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 50th chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
