Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 18

Introduction

Hearing Hanuman's words, Rama says that he cannot desert Vibhishana who is seeking a refuge in him. Initially Sugreeva disagrees with Rama's opinion. But when Rama explains the different circumstances under which Vibhishana is acceptable, Sugreeva also agrees with that view. Finally Vibhishana joins as an associate in Rama's side.

Sloka 1

atha rāmaḥ prasanna ātmā śrutvā vāyu sutasya ha |
pratyabhāṣata durdharṣaḥ śrutavān ātmani sthitam ||6-18-1

1. atha = thereafter; rāmaḥ = Rama; durdharshhaḥ = the inviolable; shrutavān = and the one having sacred knowledge ; shrutvā = after hearing; vāyusutasya = ḥanuman's words; pratyabhāshhata = spoke; prasannātmā = with a clear understanding; sthitam = that which was established; ātmani = in his mind.

Thereafter the inviolable Rama who possesses sacred knowledge, after hearing Hanuman's words, spoke with a clear understanding, the following opinion that was firm in his mind:

Sloka 2

mama api tu vivakṣā asti kācit prati vibhīṣaṇam |
śrutam iccāmi tat sarvam bhavadbhiḥ śreyasi sthitaiḥ ||6-18-2

2. mamāpi = I also; asti = have; kāchit = a; vivakshhā = desire to tell; vibhīshhaṇam prati = about Vibhishana; ichchhāmi = I want; tat = it; sarvam = all; shrotum = to be heard; bhavadbhih = by you; sthitaih = who stand firmly; shreyapi = in our welfare.

"I also wish to tell my opinion about Vibhishana. I want it to be heard by you, who are firmly established in our welfare."

Sloka 3

mitra bhāvena samprāptam na tyajeyam kathaṃcana |
doṣo yadi api tasya syāt satām etad agarhitam ||6-18-3

3. kathamchana = by any means; nātyajeyam = I donot leave; tasya = him; samprāptam = who comes; mitrabhāvena = with a friendly appearance; syāt yadyapi = even though he has; doshhah = a defect; etat = ṭhis; agarhitam = is irreproachable; satām = by good men.

"By any means, I do not desert anyone who arrives with a friendly appearance, even if he has a defect. His acceptance is irreproachable in the eyes of good men"

Sloka 4

sugrīvastvatha tadvākyamābhāṣya ca vimṛśya ca |
tataḥ śubhataram vākyamuvāca haripuṅgavaḥ ||6-18-4

4. atha = then; ṣugreevaḥ = ṣugreeva; haripuṇgavaḥ = the chief of monkeys; ābhāshhyacha = closely analysed; vimṛishyacha = and examined; tadvākyam = those words of Rama; tatah = and thereafter; uvāchaha = spoke; shubhataram = most auspicious; vākyam = words:

Then, Sugreeva the chief of monkeys closely analyzed and examined the aforesaid words of Rama and thereafter spoke the following most auspicious words:

Sloka 5

suduṣṭo vāpyaduṣṭo vā kimmeṣa rajanīcaraḥ |
īdṛśam vyasanam prāptam bhrātaram yaḥ parityajet ||6-18-5
ko vāma sa bhavettasya yameṣa na parityajet |

5. kim = what matters if; eshhaḥ = this; rajanīcharaḥ = ranger of night; (demon) sudushhṭovā = is very dangerous; yaḥ = he who; parityajet = can desert; bhrātaram = his brother; prāptam = who get; īdṛisham = such; vyasanam = a calamity; tasya = for such a person; yam = who to;konāma = whom; saḥ = he; naparityajet = would not betray?

"What matters if this ranger of night is very dangerous or other wise, if he can desert his own brother at a time of peril? Whom will he not betray subsequently?"

Sloka 6

vānarādhipate rvākyam śrutvā sarvānudīksya tu ||6-18-6
īṣadutsmayamānastu laksmaṇam puṇyalakṣaṇam |
iti hovāca kākutthso vākyam satyaparākramaḥ ||6-18-7

6; 7. shrutvā = hearing; vākyam = the words; vānarādhipate = of ṣugreeva; kakutthsaḥ = Rama; satyaparākramaḥ = who was truly mighty; udīkshhyatu = looked round; sarvam = all of them; utsmyamānaḥ = smiled; īshhat = a little; uvāchaha = and spoke; vākyam = these words; iti = thus; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; puṇya lakshhaṇam = of virtuous characteristics:

Hearing the words of Sugreeva, Rama who was truly mighty looked round all of them , smiled a little and spoke the following words to Lakshmana, of virtuous characteristics:

Sloka 8

anadhītya ca śāstrāṇi vṛddhānanupasevya ca |
na śakyamīdṛśam vaktum yaduvāca harīśvaraḥ ||6-18-8

8. yat = which words; harīshvaraḥ = ṣugreeva; uvāchaḥ = spoke; na shakyam = it is not possible; vaktum = to speak;īdṛisham = such words; anadhītya = without studying; shāstrāṇi = scriptures; anupasevyacha = and without serving; vṛiddhān = elders.

"Without studying scriptures and without serving elders, it is not possible to speak such words as Sugreeva spoke."

Sloka 9

asti sūkṣmataram kiṃcidyadatra pratibhāti mā |
pratyakṣam laukikam cāpi vartate sarvarājasu ||6-18-9

9. yat = which; vartate = is; pratyakshham = clearly to be seen; sarva rājasu = in all the kings; loukikamchāpi = and occurring in even ordinary life; kimchit = a somewhat; sūkshhmataram = highly subtle thing; asti = is there; atra = ḥere; mā = to me; pratibhāti = it is appearing to the mind.

"It appears to my mind that there is however something peculiar in these circumstances which are clearly to be seen in all lives of kings and even occurring in ordinary life."

Sloka 10

amitrāstatkulīnāśca prātideśyāśca kīrtitā |
vyasaneṣu prahartārastasmādayamihāgataḥ ||6-18-10

10. kīrtitāḥ = it is told; tatkulīnāshcha = that persons of that family; prātideshyāshchcha = and rulers belonging to adjoining territory; amitrāḥ = are enemies; prahartārah = and strike; vyasaneshhu = in adversities; tasmāt = for that reason; ayam = he; āgatam = came; iha = here.

"It is told that persons of the same family and rulers belonging to adjoining territories become enemies and strike in times of adversities. For this reason, he came here."

Sloka 11

apāpāstatkulīvāśca mānayanti svakān hitān |
eṣa prāyo narendrāṇām śaṅkanīyastu śobhanaḥ ||6-18-11

11. tatkulīnāḥ = persons belonging to the same family; apāpāḥ = who are not sinful; mānayanti = respect; svakān = their own; hitān = well- wishers; prāyaḥ = Generally; narendrāṇām = for kings; eshhaḥ = even the persons; shobhanaḥ = who are virtuous; shaṇkanīyaḥ = are to be apprehended.

"Even if belonging to the same family, persons who are virtuous do respect their own well wishers in that family. Generally among kings, even a virtuous person is apprehended."

Sloka 12

yastu doṣastvayā prokto hyādāne^^ribalasya ca |
tatra te kīrtayuṣyāmi yathāśāstramidam śṛṇu ||6-18-12

12. yathāsāstran = as per scriptures; kīrtayishhyāmi = I will tell; te = you; shruṇu = hear; idam = this; yaḥ = which; dashhaḥ = draw back; proktaḥ = is told; tvayā = by you; tatra = in that matter; ādāne = of accepting; aribalasya = from the side of an adversary.

"With regard to the draw back you told me in the matter of accepting a person coming from the side of an adversary, hear from me what the scriptures say."

Sloka 13

na vayam tatkulīnāśca rājyakāmkṣī ca rākṣasaḥ |
paṇḍitā hi bhaviṣyanti tasmādgrāhyo vibhīṣaṇaḥ ||6-18-13

13. vayam = we; na = do not; tatkulīnāḥcha = belong to the same family. rākshhasaḥ = ṭhe demon; rājya kāṇkshhīcha = is desirous of kingdom; (ṭhese demons also)bhavishhyanti = may be; paṇDitāḥ hi = rather learned; tasmāt = for that reason; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; grāhyaḥ = is admissible.

"We do not belong to the same family. Further, Vibhishana the demon is desirous of acquiring the kingdom. A few of the demons also may be rather learned ones. That is why, Vibhishana is acceptable."

Sloka 14

avyagrāśca prahṛṣṭāśca na bhaviṣyanti samgatāḥ |
praṇādaśca mahāneṣa tato.asya bhayamāgatam ||6-18-14
iti bhedam gamiṣyanti tasmātpropto vibhīṣaṇaḥ |

14. sangatāḥ = kinsfolk; na bhavishhyanti = do not live together; avyagrāshcha = in a fearless mode; prahṛishhṭāshcha = and in a delightful manner; iti = hence; gamishhyanti = they get; bhedam = a split; ataḥ = for that reason; bhayam = a fear; āgatam = has come; asya = to him; eshhaḥ = ṭhis; praṇādashcha = loud appeal is also; mahān = leading; tasmāt = for that reason; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; grāhyaḥ = is acceptable.

"Kinsfolk do not live together in a fearless mode and in a delightful manner. Hence, they get a split among themselves. For this reason, a fear has come to him. This loud appeal is also major cause. That is why, Vibhishana can be accepted."

Sloka 15

na sarve bhrātarastāta bhavanti bharatopamāḥ ||6-18-15
madvidhā nā pituḥ putrāḥ suhṛdo vā bhavadvidhāḥ |

15. tāta = my dear one! sarve = All; bhrātaraḥ = brothers; na bhavanti = are not; bharatopamāḥ = like bharata; putraḥ = sons; madvidhāḥ = like me; pituḥ = to a father; suhṛido vā = or friends; bhavadvidhāḥ = like you.

"ṃy dear one! Neither all brothers are like Bharata, nor all sons of a father like me nor all friends are like you."

Sloka 16

evamuktastu rāmeṇa sugrīvaḥ sahalaksmaṇaḥ ||6-18-16
utthāayodam mahāprājñaḥ praṇato vākyamabravīt |

16. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmeṇa = by Rama; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; saha lakshmaṇaḥ = who was together with Lakshmana; mahāpraG^yaḥ = the highly wise man; utthāya = stood up; praṇataḥ = saluting; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words:

Hearing Rama's words Sugreeva who was together with the highly wise Lakshmana stood up saluting and spoke these words:

Sloka 17

rāvaṇena praṇihitam tamavehi niśācaram ||6-18-17
tasyāham nigraham manye kṣamam kṣamavatām vara |

17. kṣhamavatāmvara = O; Rama the excellent among those who are tolerant! avachi = Realiase; tam = that; nishācharam = ranger in night(demon); praṇihatam = to have been sent; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; manye = I think; tasya = of him; kshhamam = befitting; nigraham = to be taken as captive."

"O, Rama the excellent among the tolerant! Realise that Ravana has sent Vibhishana, the demon. I think of him to be worthy of being taken as a captive."

Sloka 18

rākṣaso jihmayā buddhyā saṃdiṣṭo.aya mihāgataḥ ||6-18-18
prahartum tvayi viśvaste viśvaste mayi vānagha |
lakṣmaṇe vā mahābāho sa badhyaḥ sacivaiḥ saha ||6-18-19
rāvaṇasya nṛśaṃsasya bhrātā hyeṣa vibhīṣaṇaḥ |

18; 19. anagha = O; faultless one! mahābāho = O; mighty armed! ayam rākshhasaḥ = ṭhis demon; samdishhṭaḥ = is been sent; prahartum = to strike; tvayi = you; vishvaste = who are unsuspecting; mayivā = or me; lakshhmaṇeva = or Lakshmana; vishvaste = in trust; āgataḥ = and came; iha = here; jihmayā = with crooked; buddhyā = mind; saḥ = he; sachivaiḥsaha = with his counselors; badhyaḥ = are fit to be imprisoned; vibhīshhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; bhrāta hi = is indeed brother; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; nṛishamsasya = the cruel demon.

"O, the mighty armed Rama! The faultless one! This demon is being sent to kill you who are unsuspecting, or to kill Lakshmana or me in trust. He came here with a crooked mind. He with his counselors is fit to be imprisoned. Is not Vibhishana the brother of cruel Ravana?"

Sloka 20

evamuktvā raghuśreṣṭaṃ sugrīvo vāhinīpatiḥ || 6-18-20
vākyajño vākyakuśalaṃ tato maunamupāgamat |

20. uktvā = speaking; evam = thus; raghushreshhṭam = to Rama the best of Raghu dynasty; ṣugreevaḥ = ṣugreeva; vākyaG^yaḥ = who knew the mode of expression; tatah = thereafter; pāgamat = obtained; mounam = silence.

Speaking thus to Rama, the best of Raghu dynasty, Sugreeva who knew the mode of expression, thereafter kept silent.

Sloka 21

sa sugrīvasya tad vākyaym rāmaḥ śrutvā vimṛśya ca ||6-18-21
tataḥ śubhataram vākyam uvāca hari pumgavam |

21. bhrutvā = hearing; tat vākyam = those words; sugrīvasya = of ṣugreeva; rāmaḥ = Rama; vimṛishyacha = pondered over them and; tataḥ = thereafter; uvācha = spoke; subha taram = more auspicious; vākyam = words; haripuṇgavam = to ṣugreeva.

Hearing those words of Sugreeva, Rama pondered over the words and thereafter spoke more auspicious words to Sugreeva as follows:

Sloka 22

suduṣṭo vā api aduṣṭo vā kim eṣa rajanī caraḥ ||6-18-22
sūkṣmam api ahitam kartum mama aśaktaḥ kathaṃcana |

22. kim = what if; eshhaḥ = this; rajanīcharaḥ = demon; sudushhṭovā = is very bad? asaktaḥ = ḥe is incapable; kartum = of doing; kathamchana = scarcely; sūkshhmam = a little; ahitamapi = harm ; mama = to me.

"What does it matter whether that demon is very bad or not bad? He is incapable of doing even a little harm to me."

Sloka 23

piśācān dānavān yakṣān pṛthivyām caiva rākṣasān ||6-18-23
anguli agreṇa tān hanyām iccan hari gaṇa īśvara |

23. harigaṇeshvara = O; ṣugreeva Lord of monkey troops! Ichchhan = If I wish; hanyām = I will kill; tān = those; pishāchān = devilish beings; dānavān = demons; yakshhān = supernatural beings; rākshhasanchaiva = and ogres; pṛithivyām = living on earth; aṇgulyagreṇa = with the tip of my finger.

"O, Sugreeva, Lord of monkey-troops! If I wish, I can kill those devilish beings, demons, supernatural beings and ogres living on earth with just a tip of my finger"

Sloka 24

śrūyate hi kapotena śatruḥ śaraṇam āgataḥ ||6-18-24
arcitaḥ ca yathā nyāyam svaiḥ ca māṃsair nimantritaḥ |

24. shrūyate hi = it is heard; kapotena;by a dove; shatruḥ = an enemy; āgataḥ = came; sharaṇam = for a refuge; architashcha = was received; yathānyāyam = according to rules of hospitality; nimantritashcha = and was invited for a feast; svaiḥ = with its own; māmsaiḥ = flesh.

"It is narrated how by a dove, its enemy ( a fowler) when it came for a refuge, was received according to rules of hospitality and was invited for a feast with its own flesh."

Sloka 25

sa hi tam pratijagrāha bhāryā hartāram āgatam ||6-18-25
kapoto vānara śreṣṭha kim punar mad vidho janaḥ |

25. saḥ kaptaḥ = that dove; pratijagrāha hi = indeed accepted as a guest; tam = the fowler; āgatam = as he came; bharyāhantāram = although he killed its wife; kimpunaḥ = how much more; janaḥ = a man; madvidhaḥ = like me?

"O, the excellent of monkeys! The aforesaid dove indeed accepted as a guest, the fowler as he came, although he killed its wife .How much more a man like me has to do?"

Sloka 26

ṛṣeḥ kaṇvasya putreṇa kaṇḍunā parama ṛṣiṇā ||6-18-26
śṛṇu gāthām purā gītām dharmiṣṭhām satya vādinā |

26. putreṇa = the son; purā = long ago; kanvasya ṛishhe = of a sage called Kanva; satyavādinā = a great sage; kaṇDūnā = and by Kandu by name; gathā = verses; dharmishhṭhā = inculcating virtue; gītā = were sung; shruṇu = hear them.

"Hear the verses inculcating virtue so long ago, by Kandu, the son of a sage called Kanva, a great sage and a speaker of truth."

Sloka 27

baddha anjali puṭam dīnam yācantam śaraṇa āgatam ||6-18-27
na hanyād ānṛśaṃsya artham api śatrum param pata |

27. paramtapa = O; king the torminator of enemies! ṣhatrum api = even an enemy; baddhāṇjalipuṭram = who formed a cup with his hollowed hands; dīnam; a miserable person; yāchantam = a person who is begging; sharaṇāgatam = and who is seeking refuge; na hanyāt = should not be killed; anṛishamsyārtham = with the aim of not being cruel.

"O, king the terminator of enemies! Even an enemy, who formed a cup with his hollowed hands, a miserable person, a person who is begging and who is seeking a refuge should not be killed with the aim of not being cruel."

Sloka 28

arto vā yadi vā dṛptaḥ pareṣām śaraṇam gataḥ ||6-18-28
ariḥ prāṇān parityajya rakṣitavyaḥ kṛta ātmanā |

28. ariḥ = an enemy; gataḥ = who gets; sharaṇam = a refuge; pareshhām = from others; ārtovā = even if he is oppressed; yadivā = or; dṛiptaḥ = arrogant; rakshhitavyaḥ = is to be protected; parityajya = by abandoning; prāṇān = even lives; kṛItātmanā = by one who has a well-disciplined mind.

"An enemy who comes for protection against others, even if the aforesaid enemy is oppressed or arrogant, is to be protected by one who has a disciplined mind, even by abandoning one's life."

Sloka 29

sa ced bhayād vā mohād vā kāmād vā api na rakṣati ||6-18-29
svayā śaktyā yathā tattvam tat pāpam loka garhitam |

29. saḥ narakshhatichet = If he does not ; svayā = by his; shaktyā = strength; yathānyāyam = rightly; bhayadvā = by fear; mohādvā = or by ignorance; kamādvāpi = by or desire; tat = it; pāpam = is a sin; lokagarhitam = to be reproached by the world.

"If he does not protect rightly through his strength, by fear or by ignorance or by desire, it is a sin to be reproached by the world."

Sloka 30

vinaṣṭaḥ paśyatas tasya rakṣiṇaḥ śaraṇa āgataḥ ||6-18-30
ādāya sukṛtam tasya sarvam gacced arakṣitaḥ |

30. arakshhitaḥ = having not been protected; gataḥ = being in; sharaṇam = a refuge; tasya = and if that; rakshhiṇaḥ = protector; pashyataḥ = while seeing; vinashhṭaḥ = dies; tasya = his; sarvam = whole; sukṛitam = moral merit; ādāya = having taken along; gachchhet = goes.

"If having not been protected, a refugee dies before the eyes of a man who is able to protect him, the former takes along all his moral merit and goes."

Sloka 31

evam doṣo mahān atra prapannānām arakṣaṇe ||6-18-31
asvargyam ca ayaśasyam ca bala vīrya vināśanam

31. mahān doshhaḥ = there is a great blemish; evam = thus; atra = in this; arakshhane = who take refuge; asvargyam = it does not bestow heaven; ayashasyamcha = it destroys reputation; balavīrya vināshanam = it devastates strength and valor."

"In not protecting thus the persons who take refuge, there is a great blemish involved in it. It does not bestow heaven. It destroys reputation. It devastates strength and valor"

Sloka 32

kariṣyāmi yathā artham tu kaṇḍor vacanam uttamam ||6-18-32
dharmiṣṭham ca yaśasyam ca svargyam syāt tu phala udaye |

32. karishhyāmi = I will make; yathārtham = real; uttamam = the excellent; vachanam = words; kaṇDoḥ = of Kandu; syāt = It becomes; dharmishhṭham = very righteous; yashasyam = gives a reputation; svargyam = leads to heaven; phalodayam = and rewards appear.

"I will follow the excellent words of Kandu. It becomes a very righteous thing, gives reputation, leads to heaven and the rewards appear consequently."

Sloka 33

sakṛd eva prapannāya tava asmi iti ca yācate ||6-18-33
abhayam sarva bhūtebhyo dadāmi etad vratam mama |

33. yāchate = he who seeks; prapannāya = refuge; sakṛideva = just once; iti = saying that; asmi = I am; tava = yours; dadāmi = I shall give; abhayam = assurance of safety; sarva bhūtebhyaḥ = against all types of beings; etat = this; mama = is my; vratam = pledge.

"He who seeks refuge in me just once, telling me that �I am yours', I shall give him assurance of safety against all types of beings. This is my solemn pledge"

Sloka 34

ānaya enam hari śreṣṭha dattam asya abhayam mayā ||6-18-34
vibhīṣaṇo vā sugrīva yadi vā rāvaṇaḥ svayam |

34. sugrīva = O; ṣugreeva; harishreshhṭha = the chief of monkeys! vibhīshhaṇovā = either he is Vibhishana; yadivā = or; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; svayam = himself; abhayam = an assurance of safety; dattam = has been given; asya = to him; mayā = by me; ānaya = bring; evam = him(here).

"O, Sugreeva, the chief of monkeys! Let him either be Vibhishana or even Ravana himself; I have given an assurance of safety to him. Bring him here."

Sloka 35

rāmasya vacanam śrutvā sugrīvaḥ plavaga īśvaraḥ ||6-18-35
pratyabhāṣata kākutstham sauhārdena abhicoditaḥ |

35. shrutvā = hearing; rāmasya; = Rama's; vachanaḥ = words; ṣugreevaḥ = ṣugreeva; plavageshvara = the Lord of monkeys; abhipūrita = overwhelmed; souhardena = with cordial friendship; pratyabhāshhata = replied; kākutthsam = to Rama(as follows).

Hearing Rama' s words, Sugreeva the lord of monkeys, overwhelmed as he was with cordial friendship, replied to Rama as follows:

Sloka 36

kim atra citram dharmajña loka nātha śikhā maṇe ||6-18-36
yat tvam āryam prabhāṣethāḥ sattvavān sapathe sthitaḥ |

36. dharmaJṇa = O; the one who is aware of righteousness; lokanāthasikhāmaṇe = the crest jewel among the lords of the world; kim = what; chitram surprise; atra = is there; iti yat = in thinking that; tvam = you; sattvavān = endowed with true essence; athitaḥ = and established; satpathe = in right path; prabhāshhethāh = speak; āryam = these venerable words?

"O, Rama, who knows righteousness, the crest jewel among lords of the world! What surprise is there in thinking that you, duly endowed with true essence of life and established in a right path, speak these venerable words?"

Sloka 37

mama ca api antar ātmā ayam śuddhim vetti vibhīṣaṇam ||6-18-37
anumanāc ca bhāvāc ca sarvataḥ suparīkṣitaḥ |

37. ayam = this; mama = my; antarātmā cha = inmost spirit; vetti = feels; vibhīshhanam = Vibhishana; shuddham = to be genuine; suparīkshhitaḥ = being well examined; sarvataḥ = from all angles; anumānāt = by act of inference; bhāvāt = and by appearance.

"ṃy inmost spirit also feels Vibhishana to be a genuine person, he being well examined from all angles, by act of inference and by his appearance."

Sloka 38

tasmāt kṣipram saha asmābhis tulyo bhavatu rāghava ||6-18-38
vibhīṣaṇo mahāprājñaḥ sakhitvam ca abhyupaitu naḥ |

38. rāghavā = O; Rama; ṭasmāt = ḥence; bhavatu = let; vhbhīshhaṇah = Vibhishana; māhāprājJṇaḥ = the highly wise; tulyaḥ = be equal; asmābhiḥ cha = among us together; abhyupaitu = let him get; sakhitvamcha = friendship too; nah = with us.

"O, Rama! Hence, let the highly wise Vibhishana be equal among us soon together. Let him get our friendship too"

Sloka 39

tatas tu sugrīva vaco niśamyata|
hari īśvareṇa abhihitam nara īśvaraḥ |
vibhīṣaṇena āśu jagāma samgamam |
patatri rājena yathā puram daraḥ ||6-18-39

39. nishamya = hearing; tat sugrīva vachaḥ = those words of ṣugreeva; nareshvaraḥ = Rama the king of men; abhihitam = as acknowledged; harīshvarena = by ṣugreeva; jagāma = held; samgamam = meeting; vibhīṣhaṇena = with Vibhishana; puramdaraḥ yathā = as Indra the lord of celestials; patattrirajena = had with Garuda the king of birds.

Hearing those words of Sugreeva, Rama the king of men, as acknowledged by Sugreeva, held meeting with Vibhishana, as Indra the lord of celestials once met Garuda the king of birds.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe aṣṭādaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 18th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
