Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 55
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana next sends Akampana for the battle. As Akampana along with his army sets out for the war, he visualized various bad portents. A deadly conflict ensued between monkeys and demons. Kumuda, Nala and ṃainda the chiefs of monkeys creates a great carnage among demons.

Sloka 1

vajradamra akṣam nihatam śrutvā rāvaṇo rākṣasa īśvaraḥ |
bala adhyakṣam uvāca idam kṛta anjalim upasthitam || 6-55-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; vajradamṣṭram = (that) Vajradamshtra; hatam = was killed; vāliputreṇa = by Angada; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; uvācha = spoke; idam = these words; upasthitam = to the nearby; balādhyakṣam = army general; kṛitāṇ^jalim = who joined his palms in reverence.

Hearing that Vajradamshtra was killed by Angada, Ravana spoke the following words to the General of his forces who, with joined palms, stood near him.

Sloka 2

śīghram niryāntu durdharṣā rākṣasā bhīma vikramāḥ |
akampanam puras kṛtya sarva śastra prakovidam || 6-55-2

2. bhīmavikramāḥ = (Let) the invincible; rākṣasāḥ = demons; durdharṣāḥ = of irresistible courage; niryāntu = go forth; shīghram = soon; akampanam = with Akampana; puraskṛitya = in front; sarva shastrāstra kovidam = who is skilled in use of every weapon and missile.

"Let the invincible demons of irresistible courage go forth soon, with Akampana as their head, who is skilled in the use of every weapon and missile."

Sloka 3

eṣa śāstā ca goptā ca netā ca yudhi sattamaḥ |
bhūtikāmaśca me nityaṃ nityam ca samarapriyaḥ || 6-55-3

3. eṣaḥ = this; sattamaḥ = excellent demon; shāstā = is a chastiser; goptācha = a protector; netācha = ad a leader; yudhi = in batle; nityam = ever; me bhūtikāmashcha = desiring my welfare; samarapriyaḥ = and loved war; nityam = forever.

"This excellent demon is a chastiser, a protector and a leader in battle. He ever desired my welfare and always loved war."

Sloka 4

eṣa jeṣyati kākatthsau sugrīvaṃ ca mahābalam |
vānarāṃścāparān ghorān haniṣyati na saṃśayaḥ || 6-55-4

4. eṣaḥ = ḥe; jeṣyati = will prove victorious; haniṣyati = and can kill; kākutsthau = Rama; Lakshmana; mahābalam = the mighty; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; aparān = and the other; ghorān = terrific; vānarān = monkeys; na = there is no; samshayaḥ = doubt.

"There is no doubt that he will prove victorious and Rama, Lakshmana, the mighty Sugreeva and the other terrible monkeys."

Sloka 5

parigṛhya sa tāmājñām rāvaṇasya mahābalaḥ |
balaṃ samprerayāmāsa tadā laghuparākramaḥ || 6-55-5

5. parigṛihya = taking; tām = that; ājJṇām = command; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; mahābalaḥ = the mighty; saḥ = Akampana; laghuparākramaḥ = of quick resolution; tadā = then; samprerayāmāsa = mobilized; balam = the army.

Taking that command of Ravana, the mighty Akampana of quick resolution then mobilized the army.

Sloka 6

tato nānāpraharaṇā bhīmākṣā bhīmadarśanāḥ |
niṣpetū rākṣasā mukhyā balādhyakṣapracoditāḥ || 6-55-6

6. mukhyāḥ rākṣasāḥ = those foremost of demons; bhīma darshanāḥ = of terrifying appearance; nānāpraharaṇāḥ = furnished with every kind of weapon; bhīmākṣāḥ = and fearful to look upon; tataḥ = thereafter; niṣpetuḥ = rushed into the fray; balādhyakṣaprachoditāḥ = where their general had dispatched them.

Those foremost of demons of terrifying appearance, furnished with every kind of weapon and fearful to look upon, rushed into the fray where their general had dispatched them.

Sloka 7

rathamāsthāya vipulaṃ taptakāñcanabhūṣaṇam |
meghābho meghavarṇaśca meghasvanamahāsvanaḥ || 6-55-7

7. tadā = then; akampanaḥ = Akampana; meghābhaḥ = of the stature of a cloud; meghavarṇashcha = of the colour of a cloud; meghasvana mahāsvanaḥ = whose voice resembled thunder; āsthāya = ascended; vipulam = an extensive; ratham = chariot; tapta kāṇ^chana bhūṣaṇam = decorated with fine gold; niryāti = and set out; samvṛitaḥ = surrounded; ghoraiḥ = by dreadful; rākṣasaiḥ = demons.

Then, Akampana of the stature and colour of a cloud, whose voice resembled a thunder, ascended his great chariot, decorated with fine gold and set out, surrounded by dreadful demons.

Sloka 8

na hi kampayitum śakyah suraiṛapi mahā mṛdhe || 6-55-8
akampanas tatas teṣām āditya;iva tejasā |

8. surairapi = even celestials too; nashakyaḥ hi = were indeed unable; kampayitum = to upset; akampanaḥ = Akampana; mahāmṛidhe = in a great battle; tataḥ = hence; (he) tejasā = by his splendour; ādityaḥ iva = was resembling the sun; teṣām = among the demons.

Even celestials were indeed unable to upset Akampana in a great battle. Hence, among the demons, he was shining like the sun, by his splendour.

Sloka 9

tasya nidhāvamānasya samrabdhasya yuyutsayā || 6-55-9
akasmād dainyam āgaccadd hayānām ratha vāhinām |

9. tasya = (As) he; nirdhāva mānasya = sped on his way; samrabdhasya = hastening; yuyutsayā = and eager to enter the battle; hayānām = the horses; rathavāhinām = drawing his chariot; āgachchhat dainyam = were deprived of their energy; akasmāt = suddenly.

As he sped on his way, hastened and eager to enter the battle, the horses drawing his chariot were suddenly deprived of their energy.

Sloka 10

vyasphuran nayanam ca asya savyam yuddha abhinandinaḥ || 6-55-10
vivarṇo mukha varṇaś ca gadgadaś ca abhavat svaraḥ |

10. savyam = the left; nayanam = eye; asya = of Akampana; yudhābhinandinaḥ = who delighted in warfare; visphurat = began to twitch; mukhavarṇashcha = hs countenance; vivarṇaḥ = grew pale; svanaḥ = and his voice; abhavat = became; gadgadaḥ = trembled.

The left eye of Akampana, who delighted in warfare, began to twitch. His countenance grew pale and his voice trembled.

Sloka 11

abhavat sudine ca api durdine rūkṣa mārutam || 6-55-11
ūcuh khagā mṛgāh sarve vācah krūrā bhaya āvahāḥ |

11. kāle = at a time; sudine = of a good day (marked by a fine weather); abhavat = it became; durdinam = a day of bad weather; mukṣamārutam = with a bitter wind beginning to blow; khagamṛigāḥ = Birds and beasts; ūchuḥ = uttered; krūrāḥ = cruel; bhayāvahāḥ = and fearful; vāchaḥ = cries.

At a time of a good day, marked by fine weather, it turned into a day of bad weather with a bitter wind beginning to blow. Birds and beasts uttered cruel and fearful cries.

Sloka 12

sa simha upacita skandhah śārdūla sama vikramaḥ || 6-55-12
tān utpātān acintya eva nirjagāma raṇa ājiram |

12. saḥ = that demon; simhāpachita skandhaḥ = who had the shoulders of a lion; shārdūlasama vikramaḥ = and the agility of a tiger; avichintyaiva = disregarding; tān = those; utpātān = portents; nirjagāma = rushed; raṇājiram = towards the battle field.

That demon, who had the shoulders of a lion and the agility of a tiger, disregarding those portents, rushed towards the battle-field.

Sloka 13

tadā nirgaccatas tasya rakṣasah saha rākṣasaiḥ || 6-55-13
babhūva sumahān nādah kṣobhayann iva sāgaram |

13. tasya rākṣasaḥ = (while) that demon; rākṣasaiḥ saha = along with other demons; nirgachchhataḥ = went forward; tathā = thus; sumahān nādaḥ = an immense tumult; babhūva = arose; khhobhayanmivā = that seemed to convulse; sāgaram = the ocean.

While Akampana advanced thus with his troops, an immense tumult arose that seemed to convulse the ocean.

Sloka 14

tena śabdena vitrastā vānarāṇām mahā camūḥ || 6-55-14
druma śaila praharaṇā yoddhum samavatiṣṭhata |

14. tena shabdena = by (hearing) that sound; samupatiṣṭhatām = of the coming demons; yoddhum = for the battle; mahāchamūḥ = the huge army; vānarāṇām = of the monkeys; druma shailaprahārāṇām = moving forward with trees and rocks; vitrastā = was frightened.

By hearing that clamour of the demons coming for the battle, the huge army of the monkeys, brandishing trees and rocks, was frightened.

Sloka 15

teṣām yuddham mahā raudram samjajne kapi rakṣasām || 6-55-15
rāma rāvaṇayoṛarthe samabhityakta jīvinām |

15. mahāraudram = a highly fierce; yuddham = battle; samjaJṇe = occurred; teṣām = between those; kapirākṣasām = monkeys and demons; samabhityakta jīvinām = who were ready to lose their lives; rāma rāvaṇayoḥ arthe = for the sake of Rama and Ravana.

A highly fierce battle ensued between those monkeys and demons, who were ready to lose their lives for the sake of Rama and Ravana.

Sloka 16

sarve hy atibalāh śūrāh sarve parvata samnibhāḥ || 6-55-16
harayo rākṣasāś caiva paraspara jighaṃsavaḥ |

16. sarve = all; harayaḥ = the monkeys; rākṣashchaiva = and the demons; paraspara jighāsavaḥ = who were willing to kill each other; atibalāḥ = were very strong; shūrāḥ = valiant; sarve = and all; parvata samnibhāḥ = were resembling mountains.

All the monkeys and demons, who were willing to kill each other, were very strong and valiant. All were resembling mountains.

Sloka 17

teṣām vinardātām śabdah samyuge atitarasvinām || 6-55-17
śuśruve sumahān krodhād anyonyam abhigarjatām |

17. teṣām = their; sumahān = very great; shabdaḥ = clamour; vinardatām = yelling; tarasvinām = with their strength; kopam = in their rage; abhigarjitām = making savage cries; shushruve = were heard; pamyuge = in that combat.

The tremendous clamour of the aforesaid warriors, yelling in their rage and strength, making savage cries, were distinctly heard on the battle-field.

Sloka 18

rajasḥ ca aruṇa varṇa ābham subhīmam abhavad bhṛśam || 6-55-18
uddhūtam hari rakṣobhiḥ samrurodha diśo daśa |

18. subhīmam = A highly terrible; rajashcha = dust too; bhṛisham aruṇa varṇābham = in a thick coppery colour; uddhatam = raised; harirakṣobhiḥ = by the monkeys and demons; abhavata = was found; samrurodha = and enveloped; dasha = the ten; dishaḥ = quarters.

A highly terrific dust, in a thick coppery colour, raised by the monkeys and demons enveloped all the ten quarters.

Sloka 19

anyonyam rajasā tena kauśeya uddhūta pāṇḍunā || 6-55-19
samvṛtāni ca bhūtāni dadṛśuṛna raṇa ajire |

19. bhūtāni = the beings (combatants); samvṛitāni = enveloped; tena rajasā = by that dust; kesheyoddhūta pāṇDunā = which was whitish like a piece of silk shaken by the wind; na dadṛishuḥ = did not see; anyonyam = each other; raṇājire = in the battle = field.

The combatants, enveloped by that dust, which was whitish like a piece of silk shaken by the wind, could no longer distinguish each other on the battle-field.

Sloka 20

na dhvajo na patākā vā varma vā turago api vā || 6-55-20
āyudham syandanam vā api dadṛśe tena reṇunā |

20. na = neither; dhvajaḥ = standard; patākā vā = banner; na = nor; charma vā = shield; turago.api vā = horse; āyudham = weapon; syandanova api = even chariot; (could be seen); tena = in that; reṇunā = dust.

Neither standard, banner and shield, nor horse, weapon and chariot could be discerned in that pall of dust.

Sloka 21

śabdasḥ ca sumahāṃs teṣām nardatām abhidhāvatām || 6-55-21
śrūyate tumule yuddhe na rūpāṇi cakāśire |

21. sumahān = a great; tumulaḥ = tumultuous; shabdashcha = clamour; teṣāṃ = of warriors; nardatām = making challenging cries; abhidāvatām = and rushing upon each other; shrūyate = was heard; yuddhe = in the battle; rūpāṇi = (yet in the confuison) no forms; nachakāshire = were visible.

A great tumultuous clamour, of warriors, making challenging cries and rushing upon each other, was heard on the battle-field, yet in that confusion, no form was visible.

Sloka 22

harīn eva susamkruddhā harayo jaghnuṛāhave || 6-55-22
rākṣasāś ca api rakṣāṃsi nijaghnus timire tadā |

22. tadā = then; timire = in that darkness; susamruṣṭāḥ = the greatly enraged; harayaḥ = monkeys; jaghnaḥ = killed; harīnena = the monkeys themselves; āhave = in the battle; rākṣasāḥ = the demons; nijaghnuḥ = killed; rākṣasāshchāpi = the demons themselves.

In the darkness, the greatly enraged monkeys killed the monkeys themselves and the demons killed the demons themselves in battle.

Sloka 23

te parāṃśca vinighnantaḥ svāṃśca vānararākṣasāḥ || 6-55-23
rudhirārdrām tadā cakrurma hīṃ paṅkānulepanām |

23. te = those; vānara rākṣasāḥ = monkeys and demons; nighnantaḥ = killing; parāmshcha = others; svāmshcha = and their own people; tadā = then; chakruḥ = make; mahīm = the earth; rudhirārdrām = dampened with blood; paṇkānulepanām = anointing its body with mud.

Those monkeys and demons killing other and their own people then made the earth dampened with blood, anointing its body with mud.

Sloka 24

tatas tu rudhira ogheṇa siktam vyapagatam rajaḥ || 6-55-24
śarīra śava samkīrṇā babhūva ca vasum dharā |

24. tataḥ = thereafter; trajaḥ = the dust; apagatam = wnet off; siktam = being sprinkled; rudhiogheṇa = by the stream of blood; vasundharā = the earth; babhūva = became; sharīra shava samkīrṇā = filled with bodies and corpses.

Under the stream of blood being sprinkled, the dust was laid, revealing the earth covered with bodies and corpses.

Sloka 25

druma śakti śilā prāsaiṛgadā parigha tomaraiḥ || 6-55-25
harayo rākṣasās tūrṇam jaghnuṛanyonyam ojasā |

25. rākṣasāḥ = the demons; harayaḥ = and the monkeys; tūrṇam = quickly; jaghmuḥ = struck; anyonyam = each other; ojasā = with vigour; druma shaktigadā prāsaiḥ = with blows from trees; spears; maces; javelins; shilā parigha tomaraiḥ = stones; bars and picks.

The demons and the monkeys vigorously struck each other swiftly with blows from trees, spears, maces, javelins, stones, bars and picks.

Sloka 26

bāhubhiḥ parighā ākāraiṛyudhyantah parvata upamāḥ || 6-55-26
harayo bhīma karmāṇo rākṣasān jaghnuṛāhave |

26. haryaḥ = the monkeys; bhīma karmāṇaḥ = of dreadful deeds; parvatopamāḥ = and who resembled mountains; yuddhantaḥ = fighting; bāhubhiḥ = with their arms; parighākāraiḥ = appearing like iron bars; jaghnuḥ = killed; rākṣasān = the demons; āhave = in the battle.

The monkeys of dreadful deeds, resembling mountains, fighting with their arms appearing like iron bars, killed the demons in battle.

Sloka 27

rākṣasāsḥ ca api samkruddhāh prāsa tomara pāṇayaḥ || 6-55-27
kapīn nijaghnire tatra śastraih parama dāruṇaiḥ |

27. prāasa tomara pāṇayaḥ = with darts and javelins in their hands; abhisamkruddhāḥ = the enraged; rākṣasāstu = demons; tatra = there; nijaghnire = struck; kapīn = the monkeys; parama dāruṇaiḥ = with their cruel; shastraiḥ = weapons.

The enraged demons with darts and javelins in their hands, struck the monkeys there with their cruel weapons.

Sloka 28

akampanaḥ susaṃkruddho rākṣasānāṃ camūpatiḥ || 6-55-28
saṃharṣayati tānsarvānrākṣasān bhīmavikramān |

28. susamkruddhaḥ = the extremely enraged; akampanaḥ = Akampana; chamūpatiḥ = the General of the army; rākṣasānām = of demons; samharṣayati = cheered; sarvān = all; tān rākṣasān = those demons; bhīmavikramān = by his terrible prowess.

The extremely enraged Akampana, the general of the army of demons, cheered all those demons by his terrible prowess.

Sloka 29

harayas tv api rakṣāṃsi mahā druma mahā aśmabhiḥ || 6-55-29
vidārayanty abhikramya śastrāṇy āccidya vīryataḥ |

29. harayanti api = the monkeys however; abhikramya = leaping upon then; āchchhidya = and snatching; shastrāṇ = their weapons; vīryataḥ = through their strength; vidārayanti = crushed; rakṣāmsi = those demons; mahā druma mahāshmabhiḥ = with blows from large trees and stones.

The monkeys however leaping upon them and snatching their weapons through their strength, crushed those demons with blows from large trees and stones.

Sloka 30

etasminn antare vīrā harayah kumudo nalaḥ || 6-55-30
maindasḥ ca parama kruddhaś cakruṛvegam anuttamam |

30. etasmin antare = meanwhile; kumudaḥ = kumuda; nalaḥ = nala; maindashcha = and ṃainda; vīrāḥ = the courageous; harayaḥ = monkeys; paramakruddhāḥ = in an outburst of rage; charuḥ = displayed; anuttamam = unsurpassed; vegam = rashness.

ṃeanwhile Kumuda, Nala and ṃainda the courageous monkeys in an outburst of anger, displayed unsuprassed rashness.

Sloka 31

te tu vṛkṣaiṛmahā vegā rākṣasānām camū mukhe || 6-55-31
kadanam sumaha cakrur līlayā hari yūthapāḥ |
mamanthū rākṣasān sarve nānāpraharaṇairbhṛśam || 6-55-32

31; 32. mahāvīrāḥ = the exceedingly valiant; te = those; haripuṇ^gavāḥ = chiefs of monkeys; līlayā = as in sport; chamnumukhe = in the army-front; vṛikṣaiḥ = with trees; chakruḥ = performed; sumahat = a very great; kadanam = carnage; rākṣasānām = of demons; sarve = all of them; bhṛisham = repeatedly; mamanthuḥ = crushed; rākṣasān = the demons; nānā praharaṇaiḥ = with every kind of weapon.

Those exceedingly valiant chiefs of monkeys, as in sport, in the battle-front, with mighty blows of trees, created a great carnage among the demons. All of them repeatedly crushed the demons with every kind of weapon.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe pañcapañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 55th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
