Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 56
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hanuma again visits Ashoka garden and sees Seetha once more. He consoles Seetha that Rama along with his forces of monkeys and bears will come soon and after conquering the enemies in battle, will take her back. After bidding adieu to Seetha, he ascends ṃount Arishta and enlarges his body making himself ready to leap across the ocean.


Sloka 1

tataḥ tu śiṃśapā mūle jānakīm paryavasthitām |
abhivādya abravīt diṣṭyā paśyāmi tvām iha akṣatām || 5-56-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; abhivādya = having offered is salutation; jānakīm = to ṣeetha; paryupasthitām = who was present; shimshupāmūle = at the foot of Ashoka tree; (ḥanuma); abravīt = spoke (as follows); diṣṭyā = thank ḥeaven!; pashyāmi = I am seeing; tvām = you; iha = here; akṣatām = uninjured.

ṭhereafter, having offered his salutation to ṣeetha, who was present at the foot of Ashoka tree, Hanuma spoke: "Thank heaven! I am seeing you uninjured here!"

Sloka 2

tataḥ tam prasthitam sītā vīkṣamāṇā punaḥ punaḥ |
bhartṛ sneha anvitam vākyam hanūmantam abhāṣata || 5-56-2

2. tataḥ = then; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; vīkṣamāṇā = beholding; tam hanūmantam = that ḥanuma; prasthitam = starting out (for his return-journey); sītā = ṣeetha; abhāṣata = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; bhartṛisnehānvitam = endowed with affection towards her husband.

ṭhen, beholding again and again, that ḥanuma, who had made ready for his return journey, Seetha spoke the following words, filled with affection towards her husband.

Sloka 3

kāmam asya tvam eva ekaḥ kāryasya parisādhane |
paryāptaḥ para vīraghna yaśasyaḥ te bala udayaḥ || 5-56-3

3. paravīraghna = O ḥanuma; the annihilator of enemies!; kāmam = surely; tvam = you; ekaḥ = alone; paryāptaḥ = are enough; parisādhane = in achievement; asya kāryasya = of this task; te = your; balodayaḥ = elevated energy; yashasya = is creditable.

"O ḥanuma, the annihilator of enemies! You are yourself surely sufficient single-handed completion of this task. Your elevated energy is creditable indeed.

Sloka 4

śaraistuḥ samkulām kṛtvā lankām para bala ardanaḥ |
mām nayet yadi kākutsthaḥ tasya tat sādṛśam bhavet || 5-56-4

4. tu = but; tat bhavet = it will be; sadṛisham = appropriate; tasya kākutthasaḥ = of him Rama; parabalārdanaḥ = who destroys the army of adversaries; nayedyadi = if he takes; mām = me; kṛitvā = after making; laṇ^kām = Lanka; samkulām = upset; sharaiḥ = with his arrows.

"But, it will be appropriate for Rama, who destroys the hostile forces, to take me with him, after routing Lanka with his arrows."

Sloka 5

tat yathā tasya vikrāntam anurūpam mahātmanaḥ |
bhavati āhava śūrasya tattvam eva upapādaya || 5-56-5

5. tat = therefore; tva = you; upapādaya = act; yathā tathā bhavati = in such a way that; mahātmanaḥ = the high-souled Rama; āhavashūrasya = valiant in battle; vikrāntam = (may exhibit) prowess; tasya anurūpam = worthy of him.

"ṭherefore, you act in such a way that the high-souled Rama, valiant in battle, may exhibit prowess worthy of him."

Sloka 6

tat artha upahitam vākyam praśritam hetu samhitam |
niśamya hanumān tasyā vākyam uttaram abravīt || 5-56-6

6. nishamya = hearing; tat = those; vākyam = words; prashritam = which were humble; hetusamhitam = endowed with reason; arthopahitam = and meaningful; tasyāḥ = of her; hanumān = ḥanuma; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; uttaram = in reply.

ḥearing those humble, reasonable and meaningful words of ṣeetha, ḥanuma spoke the following words in reply.

Sloka 7

kṣipram eṣyati kākutstho hari ṛkṣa pravaraiḥ vṛtaḥ |
yaḥ te yudhi vijitya arīn śokam vyapanayiṣyati || 5-56-7

7. kākuthsaḥ = Rama; vṛitaḥ = surrounded; haryṛiṣapravaraiḥ = with leaders of monkeys and bears; eṣyati = will come; kṣipram = immediately; yaḥ = who; vijitya = after conquering; arīn = the enemies; yudhi = in battle; vyapanayiṣyati = will take away; te = your; shokam = grief.

"Rama, accompanied by the leaders of monkeys and bears, will come soon and after conquering the enemies in battle, will take away your grief."

Sloka 8

evam āśvāsya vaidehīm hanūmān mārut ātmajaḥ |
gamanāya matim kṛtvā vaidehīm abhyavādayat || 5-56-8

8. hanumān = ḥanuma ; mārutātmajah = the son of wind-god; evam = thus; āshvāsya = consoling; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; kṛitvā = and making up; matim = his mind; gamanāya = to go; vaidehīm abhyavādayat = saluted ṣeetha with reverence.

ḥanuma, the son of wind-god, thus consoling ṣeetha and making up his mind to go, saluted Seetha with reverence.

Sloka 9

tataḥ sa kapi śārdūlaḥ svāmi saṃdarśana utsukaḥ |
āruroha giri śreṣṭham ariṣṭam ari mardanaḥ || 5-56-9

9. tataḥ = thereafter; saḥ = that; kapishārdūlaḥ = ḥanuma the foremost among the monkeys; arimardanah = the annihilator of enemies; svāmi samdarshanotsukaḥ = with an anxious desire to see his lord; āruroha = ascended; girishreṣṭham = (that) mountain of excellence; ariṣṭham = called Arishta.

ṭhereafter, that ḥanuma the foremost among the monkeys, and the annihilator of enemies, with an anxious desire to see his lord, ascended the ṃount Arishta of excellence.

Sloka 10

tunga padmaka juṣṭābhiḥ nīlābhiḥ vana rājibhiḥ |
sottarīyamivāmbhodaiḥ śṛmgāntaravilambibhiḥ || 5-56-10

10. nīlābhiḥ = (that mountain) was covered as it were; with dark green; vanarājibhiḥ = rows of gardens; tuṇ^gapadmakajuṣṭābhiḥ = inhabited by towering elephants with red spots on their skin; ambhodaiḥ = with clouds; shṛiṇgāntaravilambibhiḥ = hanging between its summits; sottarīyamiva = as with an upper garment.

ṭhat mountain was covered as it were with dark green rows of gardens inhabited by towery elephants with red spots on their skin and with its clouds hanging between its summits, as with an upper garment.

Sloka 11

bodhyamānamiva prītyā divākarakaraiḥ śubhaiḥ |
unmiṣantimivoddhūtairlocanairiva dhātubhiḥ || 5-56-11

11. dhātubhiḥ = by metals; uddhūtaiḥ = scattered here and there; lochanairiva = looking like eyes; unmiṣantamiva = drawing up the eye = lids; (the mountain); bodhyamānamiva = looked like being awakened as it were; prītyā = with love; shubhaiḥ divākarakaraiḥ = by the bright rays of the sun.

By metals scattered here and there, looking like eyes drawing up the eye-lids, the mountain looked like being awakened, as it were, with love by the bright rays of the sun.

Sloka 12

toyaughanisvanairmandraiḥ prādhītamiva parvatam |
pragītamiva vispaṣṭairnānāprasravaṇasvnai || 5-56-12

12. parvatam = the mountain; prādhītamiva = looked like the one who has started reciting the scriptures; vispaṣṭaiḥ = clearly; mandraiḥ toyaughanisvanaiḥ = in the form of the deep sound of running streams of water; pragītamiva = singing as it were at a high pitch; nānā prasravaṇasvanaiḥ = in the form of roaring sound of various cascades.

ṭhe mountain looked resembling the one which has started reciting the scriptures clearly in the form of the deep sound of running streams of water, singing as it were at a high pitch, in the form of roaring sound of various cascades.

Sloka 13

devadārubhiratyuccairūrdhvabāhumiva sthitam |
prapātajalanirghoṣaiḥ prākṛṣṭamiva sarvataḥ || 5-56-13

13. sthitam = (the mountain) stood; ūrdhvabāhum iva = as it were with uplifted arms; atyuchchaiḥ devadārubhiḥ = in the form of very high deodars; prākṛiṣṭamiva = and which started crying loudly as it were; sarvataḥ = on all sides; prapātajalanirghoṣaiḥ = on all sides; prapātajala nirghoṣaiḥ = in the form of the sound of its cascades.

ṭhe mountain stood as it were with uplifted arms in the form of very lofty deodars and which started crying loudly as it were, on all sides, in the form of the sound of its cascades.

Sloka 14

vepamānamiva śyāmaiḥ kampamānaiḥ śaradvanaiḥ |
veṇubhirmārutoddhūtaiḥ kūjantamiva kīcakaiḥ || 5-56-14

14. vepamānamiva = (ṭhe mountain) seemed to be trembling; kampamānaiḥ = in the form of waving; shāmaiḥ = dark; sharadvanaiḥ = antomnal groves; veṇubhiḥ = which was piping as it were; kīchakaiḥ = in the form of hollow bamboos; mārutoddhūtaiḥ = shaken up by the wind; kūjantamiva = and as though whistling.

ṭhe mountain seemed to be trembling in the form of waving dark automnal groves, which was piping as it were, in the form of hollow bamboos shaken up by the wind and whistling.

Sloka 15

niḥśvasantamivāmarṣādghorairāśīviṣottamaiḥ |
vīhārakṛtagambhīrairdhyāyantamiva gahvaraiḥ || 5-56-15

15. niḥ shvasantamiva = (the mountain) was hissing as it were with fury; āshīviṣottamaiḥ = in the form of its terrible and most poisonous snakes; dhyāyantamiva = which sat absorbed in meditation as it were; guhvaraiḥ = in the form of its caves; nīhāra kṛita gambhīraiḥ = which were deep and covered with mist.

ṭhe mountain was hissing as it were with fury, in the form of its terrible and most poisonous snakes and which sat absorbed in meditation as it were, in the form of its deep caves covered with mist.

Sloka 16

meghapādanibhaiḥ pādaiḥ prakrāntamiva sarvataḥ |
jṛmbhamāṇamivākāśe śikharairabhrāmālibhiḥ || 5-56-16

16. prakrāntamiva = (the mountain) seems to be setting out on a journey; sarvataḥ = on all four sides; pādaiḥ = with the feet of that mountain; meghapādanibhaiḥ = looking like rising clouds; shikharaiḥ = which with its peaks; abhra mālibhiḥ = having a lining of clouds; jṛimbhamāṇamiva = seemed to stretch its limbs; ākāshe = in the sky.

ṭhe mountain seems to be setting out on a journey on all four sides, with feet of the mountain, looking like rising clouds, which with its peaks having a lining of clouds, seemed to stretch its limbs in the sky.

Sloka 17

kūṭaiśca bahudhā kīrṇai śobhitam bahukandaraiḥ |
sāla tāla aśva karṇaiḥ ca vaṃśaiḥ ca bahubhiḥ vṛtam || 5-56-17

17. (ṭhe mountain) shobhitam = was embellished with; kūṭaishcha = number of summits; bahukandaraiḥ = and adorned with numerous caves; bahudhākīrṇaiḥ = scattered at many places; vṛitam = and endowed with; bahubhiḥ = many; sāla tālāshvakarṇaishcha = sal; palmyrah; karna and bamboo trees.

ṭhe mountain was embellished with a number of summits and adorned with numerous caves scattered at many places and endowed with many sal, palmyrah, karna and bamboo trees.

Sloka 18

latāvitānairvitataiḥ pṣpavadbhiralamkṛtam |
nānāmṛgagaṇākīrṇam dhātuniṣyandabhūṣitam || 5-56-18

18. (ṭhe mountain); alamkṛitam = was adorned; latāvitānaiḥ = with clusters of creepers; vitataiḥ puṣpavadbhiḥ = having plenty of flowers; nāā mṛigagaṇākīrṇam = filled with many troops of animals; dhātuniṣyanda bhūṣitam = and embellished with gushes of liquid minerals.

ṭhe mountain was adorned with clusters of creepers having plenty of flowers, filled with a number of summits and adorned with numerous caves scattered at many places and endowed with many sal, palmyrah, karna and bamboo trees.

Sloka 19

bahuprasravaṇopetam śilāsaṃcayasamkaṭam |
maharṣiyakṣagandharvakinnaroragasevitam || 5-56-19

19. (ṭhe mountain); bahuprasravaṇopetam = was endowed with many cascades; shilāsamchaya samkaṭam = crowded together with heaps of rocks; maharṣiyakṣagandharava kinnaroragasevitam = and frequented by great sages; yakshas; Gandharvas; Kinnaras and celestial serpents.

ṭhe mountain was endowed with many cascades, crowded together with heaps of rocks and frequented by great sages, Yakshas, Gandharvas, Kinnaras and celestial serpents.

Sloka 20

latāpādapasamghātam simhādhyuṣitakandaram |
vyāgrasamghasamākīrṇam svādumūlaphaladrumam || 5-56-20

20. (ṭhe mountain); latāpāda samghātam = was exquisite with creepers and trees; simhādhyuṣia kandaram = having caves inhabited by lions; vyāghrasamghasamākīrṇam = filled with a number of tigers; svādumūla phala drumam = and having sweet roots and fruit-trees.

ṭhe mountain was exquisite with creepers and trees, having caves inhabited by lions, spread over with a number of tigers and endowed with sweet roots and fruit-trees.

Sloka 21

tam āruroha hanumān parvatam pavanātmajaḥ |
rāma darśana śīghreṇa praharṣeṇa abhicoditaḥ || 5-56-21

21. hanumān = ḥanuma; pavanātmajah = the son of wind-god; rāmadarshana shīghreṇa = in a hurry to see Rama; āruroha = ascended; tamparvatam = that mountain; abhichoditaḥ = excited as he was; praharṣeṇa = with an extreme joy.

ḥanuma, the son of wind-god, in a hurry to see Rama, ascended that mountain, excited as he was with an extreme joy.

Sloka 22

tena pāda tala ākrāntā ramyeṣu giri sānuṣu |
saghoṣāḥ samaśīryanta śilāḥ cūrṇī kṛtāḥ tataḥ || 5-56-22

22. tataḥ = then; shilāḥ = the rocks; ramyeṣugirisānusu = in the charming table-land of the mountain; tena pāda talākrāntāḥ = on which the soles of his feet lied heavily; sama shīryanta = were crushed; saghoṣāḥ = with sounds; chūrṇīkṛitāḥ = and fallen to pieces.

ṭhen, the rocks in the charming table-land of the mountain, on which the soles of ḥanuma lied heavily, were crushed with noise and fallen to pieces.

Sloka 23

sa tam āruhya śaila indram vyavardhata mahākapiḥ |
dakṣiṇāt uttaram pāram prārthayaml lavaṇa ambhasaḥ || 5-56-23

23. prārthayan = desirous (of reaching); uttaram pāram = the northern shore; dakṣiṇāt = from the southern shore; lavaṇāmbhasaḥ = of the ocean; mahākapiḥ = ḥanuma; āruhya = ascending; tam shailendram = that mountain; vyavardhata = enlarged in his body.

Desirous of reaching the northern shore from the southern shore of that ocean, ḥanuma ascended that mountain and enlarged his body.

Sloka 24

adhiruhya tato vīraḥ parvatam pavana ātmajaḥ |
dadarśa sāgaram bhīmam mīna uraga niṣevitam || 5-56-24

24. tataḥ = then; vīraḥ = the valiant; pavanātmajaḥ = ḥanuma; adhirūhya = ascending; parvatam = the mountain; dadarsha = saw; bhīmam = the terrific; sāgaram = ocean; mīnoraganiṣevitam = inhabited by fishes and water-snakes.

ṭhen, the valiant ḥanuma after ascending the mountain, saw the terrific ocean inhabited by fishes and water-snakes.

Sloka 25

sa māruta iva ākāśam mārutasya ātma sambhavaḥ |
prapede hari śārdūlo dakṣiṇāt uttarām diśam || 5-56-25

25. saḥ harishārdūlaḥ = that foremost among the monkeys; ātmasambhavaḥ = the son; mārutasya = of the wind-god; prapede = entered; ākāsham = the sky; uttarām disham = towards northern direction; dakṣiṇāt = from southern direction; mārutaḥ iva = like a wind.

ḥanuma, the foremost among the monkeys and the son of the wind-god, entered the sky towards northern direction, like a wind, from southern direction.

Sloka 26

sa tadā pīḍitaḥ tena kapinā parvata uttamaḥ |
rarāsa saha taiḥ bhūtaiḥ prāviśat vasudhā talam || 5-56-26
kampamānaiḥ ca śikharaiḥ patadbhiḥ api ca drumaiḥ |

26. pīDitaḥ = pressed; tadā = then; tena kapinā = by that ḥanuma; saḥ parvatottamaḥ = that excellent mountain; pravishan = entering; vasudhātalam = the earth; taiḥ bhūtaiḥ saha = along with the various living beings; rarāsa = made a noise; kampamānaiḥ shikharaiḥ = with its shaking summits; patadbhiḥ drumaiḥ = and toppling trees.

Pressed at that moment by ḥanuma, that excellent mountain sank into the bowels of the earth along with the various living beings and made a noise with its shaking summits and toppling trees.

Sloka 27

tasya ūru vegān mathitāḥ pādapāḥ puṣpa śālinaḥ || 5-56-27
nipetuḥ bhū tale rugṇāḥ śakra āyudha hatā iva |

27. mathitāḥ = shaken; ūruvegāt = by the impetus of the thighs; tasya = of that ḥanuma; pādapāḥ = trees; puṣpashālinaḥ = endowed with blossoms; rugṇāḥ = were broken; nipetuḥ = and fell down; bhūtale = on earth; shokrāyudha hatā iva = as struck by a thunder-bolt.

ṣhaken by the impetus of the thighs of ḥanuma, trees in blossom were broken and fell down on earth, as though struck by a thunder-bolt.

Sloka 28

kandara udara saṃsthānām pīḍitānām mahāojasām || 5-56-28
simhānām ninado bhīmo nabho bhindan sa śuśruve |

28. saḥ bhīmaḥ ninadaḥ = that roaring sound; mahaujasām simhānām = of the lions of great prowess; kandarāntara samsthānām = dwelling in the midst of the caves; pīDitānām = were tormented; shushrave = (and that sound) was heard; bhindan = piercing; uabhaḥ = the sky.

When the lions of great prowess dwelling in the midst of caves were tormented and roared terribly, that roaring noise was heard as it was piercing the sky.

Sloka 29

trasta vyāaviddha vasanā vyākulī kṛta bhūṣaṇā || 5-56-29
vidyādharyaḥ samutpetuḥ sahasā dharaṇī dharāt |

29. vidyādharyaḥ = the females of Vidyadhara class of supernatural beings; trasta vyāviddha vasanāḥ = with their raiments in disorder through fear; vyākulīkṛita bhūṣaṇāḥ = and their ornaments fallen off; samutpetuḥ = went up; sahasā = (all of a sudden; dharaṇīdharāt = from the mountain.

ṭhe females of Vidyadhara class of supernatural beings, with their raiments in disorder through fear and their ornaments fallen off, went up all of a sudden from the mountain.

Sloka 30

atipramāṇā balino dīpta jihvā mahāviṣāḥ || 5-56-30
nipīḍita śiro grīvā vyaveṣṭanta mahāhayaḥ |

30. mahāhayaḥ = large serpents; atipramāṇāḥ = of immense size; balinaḥ possessing vigour; dīpta jihvāḥ = having fiery tongues; mahāviṣāh = very poisonous; nipīDita shirogrīvāḥ = with their hoods and necks severely pressed; vyacheṣṭanta = lay coiled.

Large poisonous serpents of immense size, possessing vigour and having fiery tongues with their hoods and necks severely pressed lay coiled.

Sloka 31

kinnara uraga gandharva yakṣa vidyā dharāḥ tathā || 5-56-31
pīḍitam tam naga varam tyaktvā gaganam āsthitāḥ |

31. tadā = then; tyaktvā = leaving; tam pīDitam = that tormented; nagavaram = mountain of excellence; kinnaroragagandharva yakṣavidyādharāḥ = kinnaras; ṇagas; Gandharvas; Yakshas and Vidyadharas; āsthitāḥ = ascended; gaganam = to the sky.

ṭhen, leaving that tormented mountain of excellence; Kinnaras, ṇagas, Gadharvas, Yakshas and Vidyadharas ascended to the sky.

Sloka 32

sa ca bhūmi dharaḥ śrīmān balinā tena pīḍitaḥ || 5-56-32
savṛkṣa śikhara udagrāḥ praviveśa rasā talam |

32. pīDitaḥ = pressed down; tena balinā = by the strong ḥanuma; saḥ shrīmān = that beautiful; bhūmidharashcha = mountain; savṛikṣa shikharodagraḥ = having its elevated summits crowned with trees; pravivesha = entered; rasātalam = the bowels of the earth.

Pressed down by that strong ḥanuma, that beautiful mountain having its elevated summits crowned with trees, entered the bowels of the earth.

Sloka 33

daśa yojana vistāraḥ triṃśat yojanam ucchritaḥ || 5-56-33
dharaṇyām samatām yātaḥ sa babhūva dharā dharaḥ |

33. saḥ dharādharaḥ = that mountain; dashayojanavistāraḥ = with a width of ten Yojanas (or eighty miles); trimshat yojanam uchchhritaḥ = and height of thirty Yojanas (two hundred and forty miles); babhūva = became; yātaḥ = completely; samatām = in sameness of level; dharaṇyām = with the earth.

ṭhat mountain, with a width of ten yojanas (or eighty miles) and a height of thirty yojanas (or two hundred and forty miles) got level with the earth.

Sloka 34

sa lilaṅghuyirbhīmam salīlam lavaṇārṇavam || 5-56-34
kallolāsphālavelāntamutpapāta nabho hariḥ |

34. lilaṇ^ghayiṣuḥ = with a desire to cross; salīlam = easily; bhīmam lavaṇārṇavam = the terrific ocean; kallola sphālavelāntam = whose shores were being lashed by its waves; saḥ hariḥ = that ḥanuma; utpapāta = jumped up (into the sky).

With a desire to cross easily the terrific ocean, whose shores were being lashed by its waves, which Hanuma jumped up into the air.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ṣṭpañcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 55th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
