Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 10
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Hanuma has first glimpse of Ravana in his house. He also observes various wives of Ravana sleeping in the house. He sees ṃandodari and thinks her to be Seetha.


Sloka 1

tatra divya upamam mukhyam sphāṭikam ratna bhūṣitam |
avekṣamāṇo hanumān dadarśa śayana āsanam || 5-10-1
dāntakāñcanicitrāṅgervaiśca varāsanaiḥ |
mahārhastaraṇopetairupapannam mahādhanaiḥ || 5-10-2

1;2. tatra = in that house; avekshamāṇaḥ = while observing; hanumān = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; shayanāsanam = (a portion of house with) beds and couches; upetam = consisting of; varāsanaiḥ = best couches; divyopamam = equaling those in heaven; mukhyam = best; sphāṭikam = made of crystal; ratnabhūshhitam = decorated with diamonds; dāntakāJṇchana chitrāṇ^gaiḥ = with wonderful parts made of ivory and gold; vyDūryaiḥ mahārḥ āstaraṇa upetaiḥ = covered with best beds made of cat's eye gems; mahādhanaiḥ = of great value.

In that house, while observing, ḥanuma saw a portion of house with beds and couches, consisting of best couches equaling those in heaven, made of crystal decorated with diamonds, with wonderful parts made of ivory and gold, covered with best beds made of cat's eye gems of great value.

Sloka 3

tasya ca ekatame deśe so agrya mālya vibhūṣitam |
dadarśa pāṇḍuram catram tārā adhipati samnibham || 5-10-3

3. saḥ = ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; ekatamedeshe = in a part; tasya = of that room; chhatram = an umbrella; pāṇDuram = white in colour; agryamālāvibhūshhitam = decorated with best flower garlands; tārādhipatisannibham = and resembling moon the lord of stars.

ḥanuma saw in a part of that room, an umbrella white in colour decorated with best flower garlands and resembling moon - the lord of stars.

Sloka 4

jātarūpaparikṣiptam citrabhānusamaprabham |
aśokamālāvitatam dadarśa paramāsanam || 5-10-4

4. dadarsha = (ḥanuma) saw; paramāsanam = an excellent couch; jātarūpaparīkshiptam = made of gold; chitrabhānusamaprabham = with radiance equaling that of fire; asokamālāvitatam = spread by garlands of Ashoka flowers.

ḥanuma saw an excellent couch made of gold with radiance equaling that of fire, spread by garlands of Ashoka flowers.

Sloka 5

vāla vyajana hastābhir vījyamānam samantataḥ |
gandhaiḥ ca vividhair juṣṭam vara dhūpena dhūpitam || 5-10-5

5. vījyamānam = fanned; vālavyajanahastābhiḥ = by women with fans in their hands; samantataḥ = in all the four directions; jushhṭam = obtained; vividhaiḥ gandhaiḥ = by various fragrances; dhūpitam = fumigated; paradhūpena = by the best incense.

Fanned by women with fans in their hands in all the four directions and obtained by various fragrances fumigated by the best incense.

Sloka 6

parama āstaraṇa āstīrṇam āvika ajina samvrtam |
dāmabhir vara mālyānām samantād upaśobhitam || 5-10-6

6. paramāstharaṇāstīrṇam = covered by an excellent bed; āvikājinasamvṛitam = spread by sheep skin; samantāt = everywhere; upashobhitam = shone; damabhiḥ = by rows; varamālyānām = of excellent garlands.

Covered by an excellent bed, spread by sheep skin everywhere and shone by rows of excellent garlands.

Sloka 7

tasmin jīmūta samkāśam pradīpta uttama kuṇḍalam |
lohita akṣam mahā bāhum mahā rajata vāsasam || 5-10-7

7. (ḥanuma saw Ravana) tasmin = in that; jīmūtasamkāsham = equaling a cloud; pradīptottamakuṇDalam = wearing earrings with a brilliant shine; lohitāksham = with read eyes; mahābāhum = with long arms; mahārajatavāsasam = wearing gold clothes.

ḥanuma saw Ravana in that house, equaling a cloud, wearing earrings with a brilliant shine with red eyes and with long arms wearing gold clothes.

Sloka 8

lohitena anulipta angam candanena sugandhinā |
saṃdhyā raktam iva ākāśe toyadam sataḍid guṇam || 5-10-8

8. anuliptāṇ^gam = with body smeared; lohitenachandanena = with red sandal wood; sugandhinā = with good fragrance; sandhyāraktam ākāshe toyadam iva = like a cloud in the red sky at sunset; sataṭidgaṇam = together with groups of lightening.

With his body smeared with red sandal-wood with good fragrance like a cloud in the red sky at sunset, together with groups of lightening.

Sloka 9

vṛtam ābharaṇair divyaiḥ surūpam kāma rūpiṇam |
savrkṣa vana gulma āḍhyam prasuptam iva mandaram || 5-10-9

9. vṛitam = filled; divyaiḥ ābharaṇaiḥ = with excellent jewellery; surrupam = with a good appearance; kāmarūpiṇam = with ability to assume desired form; mandaram iva = resembling mount ṃandara; prasuptam = in sleep; savṛikshavanagulmāDhyam = together with groups of trees and bushes.

Ravana was filled with excellent jewellery, with a good appearance, with an ability to assume desired form, resembling mount ṃandara in sleep, together with groups of trees and bushes.

Sloka 10

krīḍitvā uparatam rātrau vara ābharaṇa bhūṣitam |
priyam rākṣasa kanyānām rākṣasānām sukha āvaham || 5-10-10

10. uparatam = resting; krīDitvā = after having pleasure; rātrau = at night; varābharaṇabhūshhitam = bedecked with the best ornaments; priyam = lover; rākshasakanyānām = to the Rakshasa girls; sukhāvaham = causing comfort; rākasānām = to rakshasas.

Resting after having pleasure at night, bedecked with the best ornaments, he was a lover to the rakshasa girls and causing comfort to rakshasas.

Sloka 11

pītvā api uparatam ca api dadarśa sa mahā kapiḥ |
bhāskare śayane vīram prasuptam rākṣasa adhipam || 5-10-11

11. sa mahākapiḥ = that great ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; vīram = the gallant; rākshasādhipam = king of rakshasas; prasuptam = in the sleep; uparatam = resting; pītvā = after drinking; bhāsvare shayane = on a shining couch.

ṭhat great ḥanuma saw the gallant king of rakshasas in sleep on a shining couch resting after drinking.

Sloka 12

nihśvasantam yathā nāgam rāvaṇam vānara uttamaḥ |
āsādya parama udvignaḥ so apāsarpat subhītavat || 5-10-12

12. saḥ vānararshhabhaḥ = that ḥanuma; best among Vanaras; āsādya = neared; rāvaṇam = Ravana; paromdvignaḥ = became very gloomy; apāsarpat = (and) moved away; subhītavat = with great dread; niḥshvasantam nāgam yathā = as though from a hissing serpent.

ṭhat ḥanuma, best among Vanaras neared Ravana, became very gloomy and moved away with great dread, as though from a hissing serpent.

Sloka 13

atha ārohaṇam āsādya vedikā antaram āśritaḥ |
suptam rākṣasa śārdūlam prekṣate sma mahā kapiḥ || 5-10-13

13. atha = thereafter; mahākapiḥ = the great ḥanuma = āsādya = neared; ārohaṇam = staircase; āshritaḥ = sought; vedikāntaram = another dais; prekshate sma = (and) observed closely; rākshasa shārdūlam = Ravana.

ṭhereafter the great ḥanuma neared staircase, sought another dais and observed closely Ravana.

Sloka 14

śuśubhe rākṣasa indrasya svapataḥ śayana uttamam |
gandha hastini samviṣṭe yathā prasravaṇam mahat || 5-10-14

14. shayanottamam = the excellent couch; svapataḥ rākshasendrasya = of the sleeping Ravana; shushubhe = shone; mahat prasravaṇam yathā = like a great lake; gandhahastini samvishhṭhe = when a scented elephant resides (in it).

ṭhe excellent couch of the sleeping Ravana shone like a great lake when a scented elephant resides in it.

Sloka 15

kāncana angada naddhau ca dadarśa sa mahātmanaḥ |
vikṣiptau rākṣasa indrasya bhujāv indra dhvaja upamau || 5-10-15

15. saḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha cha = also saw; mahātmana = the wealthy; rākshasendrasya = Ravana's; bhujāḥ = arms; kāJṇchnāṇ^gadanaddhau = tied with golden armlets; vikshiptau = thrown apart; indradhvajoptamau = resembling flag staffs(raised in honor ) of Indra.

ṭhat ḥanuma also saw the wealthy Ravana's arms tied with golden armlets thrown apart, resembling flag staffs raised in honor of Indra.

Sloka 16

airāvata viṣāṇa agrair āpīḍita krta vraṇau |
vajra ullikhita pīna aṃsau viṣṇu cakra parikṣitau || 5-10-16

16. āpīDanakṛitavraṇau = (those arms had) wounds made by stabbing; airāvatavishhāṇāgraiḥ = from the pointed edges of tusks of Irāvata; vajrollikhitapīnāmsau = fleshy shoulders smeared with diamond; vishhṇuchakraparikshatau = hit by Vishnu chakra;

ṭhose arms had wounds made by stabbing from the pointed edges of tusks of Irāvata and fleshy shoulders smeared with diamond and hit by Vishnu chakra.

Sloka 17

pīnau samasujāta aṃsau samgatau bala samyutau |
sulakṣaṇa nakha anguṣṭhau svangulī tala lakṣitau || 5-10-17

17. pīnau = (these arms of Ravana were) corpulent; samasujātāmsau = with well grown and equal shoulders; samgatau = proportionate to body; balasamyutau = with strength; sulakshaṇanakhāṇ^gushhṭhau = with knees and thumbs of good characteristic; svaṇ^gulītalalaskhsitau = with good fingers and palms.

ṭhese arms of Ravana were corpulent with well grown and equal strong shoulders proportionate to body, with knees and thumbs of good characteristics, with good fingers and palms.

Sloka 18

samhatau parigha ākārau vrttau kari kara upamau |
vikṣiptau śayane śubhre panca śīrṣāv iva uragau || 5-10-18

18. samhatau = with strong joints; parighākārau = with a shape of club; vṛittau = round in shape; karikaropamau = resembling the trunks of an elephant; paJṇchashīrshhāuvivoragau iva = like five headed snakes; vikshiptau = thrown; subhre shayane = on clean couch.

With strong joints in the shape of club, round in shape resembling the trunks of an elephant, like five headed snakes thrown on clean couch.

Sloka 19

śaśa kṣataja kalpena suśītena sugandhinā |
candanena para ardhyena svanuliptau svalamkrtau || 5-10-19
uttama strī vimrditau gandha uttama niṣevitau |
yakṣa pannaga gandharva deva dānava rāviṇau || 5-10-20

19;20. bhujau = shoulders; su anuliptau = well smeared; parārthyena chandanena = by best sandal; sugandhina = with good smell; sushītena = and very cool; shashakshatajakalpena = resembling a hare's blood; svalankṇ^kṛitau = well decorated; uttamastrīvimṛiditau = massaged by the best women; gandhottamanishhevitau = served by the best sandal; yakshapannaga gandharva deva dānava rāviṇau = (which made) yakshas; nagas; gandharvas; devas and dānavās scream.

ṣhoulders well smeared by best sandal with good smell and very cool resembling in color to a hare's blood, well decorated, massaged by the best women, served by the best sandal and which made yakshas, nagas, gandharvas, devas and dānavās scream.

Sloka 21

dadarśa sa kapiḥ tasya bāhū śayana saṃsthitau |
mandarasya antare suptau mahā arhī ruṣitāv iva || 5-10-21

21. saḥ kapiḥ = ṭhat ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; tatra = there; bāhu = arms; shanasamsthitau = on the couch; mahāhī iva = like great serpents; rushhitau = in anger; suptau = sleeping; mandarasya antare = in the middle of mount ṃandara.

ṭhat ḥanuma saw there arms on the couch like great serpents in anger sleeping in the middle of mount ṃandara.

Sloka 22

tābhyām sa paripūrṇābhyām bhujābhyām rākṣasa adhipaḥ |
śuśubhe acala samkāśaḥ śrngābhyām iva mandaraḥ || 5-10-22

22. saḥ = ṭhat; rākshaseshvaraḥ = king of Rakshasas(Ravana); achalasamkāshaḥ = equaling a mountain; tābhyām bhujābhyām = with those two shoulders; paripūrṇābhyām = which were well built; shushubhe = and shone; mandaraḥ iva = like ṃount ṃandara; shṛiṇ^gābhyām = with twin peaks

ṭhat king of Rakshasas equaling a mountain with those shoulders, which were well built and shone like ṃount ṃandara with twin peaks.

Sloka 23

cūta pumnāga surabhir bakula uttama samyutaḥ |
mṛṣṭa anna rasa samyuktaḥ pāna gandha puraḥ saraḥ || 5-10-23
tasya rākṣasa simhasya niścakrāma mukhān mahān |
śayānasya vinihśvāsaḥ pūrayann iva tad grham || 5-10-24

23;24. māhāmukhāt = from the great face; rākshasasimhasya = of that king of Rakshasas; shayanasya = who was sleeping; nishchakrāma = came out; vinisvāsaḥ = breath; chūtapunnāga surabhiḥ = fragrant like the flowers of chūta and punnāga; vakuLottamasamyutaḥ = consisting of the best fragrance of Vakula flowers; mṛishhṭānnarasasamyuktaḥ = together with the flavor of best cooked rice; pānagandhapuraskṛitaḥ = with the smell of liquor; pūrayanniva = as though filling; tat gṛiham = that house.

From the great face of that king of Rakshasas who was sleeping, came out breath with fragrance like that of flowers of chūta and punnāga, consisting of the best fragrance of Vakula flowers, together with the flavor of best cooked rice, with the smell of liquor as though filling that house.

Sloka 25 Sloka 29

muktā maṇi vicitreṇa kāncanena virājatā |
mukuṭena apavrttena kuṇḍala ujjvalita ānanam || 5-10-25
rakta candana digdhena tathā hāreṇa śobhitā |
pīna āyata viśālena vakṣasā abhivirājitam || 5-10-26
pāṇḍureṇa apaviddhena kṣaumeṇa kṣataja īkṣaṇam |
mahā arheṇa susamvītam pītena uttama vāsasā || 5-10-27

25;26;27;28;29;30. saḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; tasya rakshaḥ pateḥ gṛihe = in the house of that king of Rakshasa's; patnīshcha = wives; pādamūlagatāḥ = at the feet; sumahātmanaḥ = a gigantic one; priyabhāryasya = with loving wives; virājitam = shone; apavṛittena makuṭena = with crown tilted to the side; muktāmaṇivichitreṇa = having strange hue with pearls and diamonds; kāJṇchanān = and of golden colour; kuṇDalojjvalitānanam = with a face made brilliant made by earrings; abhivirājitam = shone; vakshasā = with the chest region; raktachandanadigdhena = smeared with red sandal; shobhināhāreṇa = shining with a necklace; pīnāyatavishālena = wide high and corpulent; kshaumeṇa = with a silk cloth; pāṇDareṇa = of white colour; apaviddhena = falling on him; kshatajekshanam = with eyes reddish like blood; susamvītam = well tied; uttamavasasā = by a best cloth; pītena = of yellow colour; mahārheṇa = and of very best quality; māshharāshipratīkāsham = resembling a heap of black beans; nishshvasantam = snoring; bhujaṇ^gavat = like a hiss of a snake; kuJṇjaram iva = like an elephant; prasuptam = sleeping; toyānte = in the middle of water; mahati gāṇ^ge = belonging to the great river Ganga; dīpyamānachaturdisham = with all the four directions shone; chaturbhiḥ dīpaiḥ = by the four lamps; kāJṇchanaiḥ = of golden colour; megham iva = resembling a cloud; prākāsīkṛita sarvāṇ^gam = with all the parts being shone; vidhyugaṇaiḥ = by groups of lighting.

ṭhat ḥanuma saw in the house of that king of Rakshasa's, wives at the feet, Ravana being a gigantic one with loving wives, shone with crown tilted to the side, having strange hue with pearls and diamonds and of golden colour, with a face made brilliant by earrings, shone with the chest region smeared with red sandal, shining with a necklace wide, high and corpulent with a silk cloth of white colour falling on him, with eyes reddish like blood, well tied by a best cloth of yellow colour and of very best quality, resembling a heap of black beans, snoring like a hiss of a snake, like an elephant sleeping in the middle of water belonging to the great river Ganga with all the four directions shone by the four lamps of golden colour, resembling a cloud with all the parts being shone by groups of lighting.

Sloka 31

śaśi prakāśa vadanā vara kuṇḍala bhūṣitāḥ |
ambāla mālya ābharaṇā dadarśa hari yūthapaḥ || 5-10-31

31. hariyūdhapaḥ = the leader of Vanaras; dadarsha = saw; shashiprakāshavadanāḥ = (those wives of Ravana) with faces with radiance of moon; chārukuṇDalabhūshhitāḥ = adorned with beautiful earrings; amlānamālyabharaṇāḥ = wearing jewellery and flower garlands which were not worn out.

ṭhe leader of Vanaras saw those wives of Ravana with faces with radiance of moon, adorned with beautiful earrings, wearing jewellery and flower garlands which were not worn out.

Sloka 32

nṛtta vāditra kuśalā rākṣasa indra bhuja ankagāḥ |
vara ābharaṇa dhāriṇyo niṣannā dadrśe kapiḥ || 5-10-32

32. hariḥ = ḥanuma; dadṛishe = saw; nṛittavāditrakushalāḥ = (wives of Ravana) with skill in dance and musical instruments; rākshasendrabhujāṇ^kagāḥ = obtained the shoulders of Ravana; varābharaṇadhāriṇyoḥ = wearing the best jewellery; nishhaṇṇāḥ = being in sleep.

ḥanuma saw wives of Ravana with skill in dance and musical instruments, who obtained the shoulders of Ravana wearing the best jewellery and being in sleep.

Sloka 33

vajra vaidūrya garbhāṇi śravaṇa anteṣu yoṣitām |
dadarśa tāpanīyāni kuṇḍalāni angadāni ca || 5-10-33

33. dadarsha = (he) saw; kuṇDalāni = earrings; yoshhitām = of those women; shravaṇānteshhu = at the end of ears; vjravaiDūryagarbhāṇi = with embedded diamonds and cats eye gems; tāpanīyāni = and having golden colour.

ḥe saw earrings of those women at the end of ears with embedded diamonds and cats eye gems and having golden colour.

Sloka 34

tāsām candra upamair vaktraiḥ śubhair lalita kuṇḍalaiḥ |
virarāja vimānam tan nabhaḥ tārā gaṇair iva || 5-10-34

34. vaktraiḥ = with the faces; tāsām = of those women; lalitakuṇDalaiḥ = with delicate earrings; chandropamaiḥ = which were equal to moon; shubhaiḥ = (and)auspicious; tat vimānam = that house; virarāja = shone; nabhaḥ iva = like the sky; tārāgaṇaiḥ = with clusters of stars.

With the faces of those women with delicate earrings which were equal to moon and auspicious that house shone like the sky with clusters of stars.

Sloka 35

mada vyāyāma khinnāḥ tāḥ rākṣasa indrasya yoṣitaḥ |
teṣu teṣv avakāśeṣu prasuptāḥ tanu madhyamāḥ || 5-10-35

35. yoshhitaḥ = women; tāḥ rākshasendrasya = of that Ravana; madavyāyāmakinnāḥ = being tired from lustful passion and exertion; tanumadhyamāḥ = and having lean waists; prasuptāḥ = slept; teshhu teshhu avakāsheshhu = at opportunity.

Women of that Ravana being tired from lustful passion and exertion and having lean waists slept at first opportunity.

Sloka 36

aṅgahāraistathaivānyā kovalairnṛttaśālinī |
vinyastaśubhasarvāṅgī prasuptā varavarṇinī || 5-10-36

36. anyā = another; nṛittashālinī varavarṇinī = woman with skill in dancing; komalaiḥ aṇ^gahāraiḥ = consisting of delicate body postures; prasuptā = slept; tathaiva = in the same way; vinyastashubhasarvāṇ^gi = with all her body parts making the same dance postures.

Another woman with skill in dancing consisting of delicate body postures slept in the same way with all her body parts making the same dance postures.

Sloka 37

kācid vīṇām pariṣvajya prasuptā samprakāśate |
mahā nadī prakīrṇā iva nalinī potam āśritā || 5-10-37

37. kāchit = one women; parishhvajya = hugging; veeṇām = veena; prasuptā = and sleeping; samprakāshate = was shining; nalinīva = like a lotus plant; mahānadīprakīrṇa = thrown away by( the flow of) a great river; āshritā = and resorting; potam = a boat.

One woman hugging veena and sleeping was shining like a lotus plant thrown away by the flow of a great river and resorting a boat.

Sloka 38

anyā kakṣa gatena eva maḍḍukena asita īkṣaṇā |
prasuptā bhāminī bhāti bāla putrā iva vatsalā || 5-10-38

38. anyā = another woman; asitekshaṇā = with black eyes; prasuptā = sleeping; maDukena = with an instrument called madduka; kakshagatenaiva = under arm pit; bhāti = shone; bhāminīva = like a woman; vatshalā bālaputrā = (carrying) an infant boy with love.

Another woman with black eyes sleeping with an instrument called madduka under arm pit shone like a woman carrying an infant boy with love.

Sloka 39

paṭaham cāru sarva angī pīḍya śete śubha stanī |
cirasya ramaṇam labdhvā pariṣvajya iva kāminī || 5-10-39

39. bhāmini = a women; chārusarvāṇ^gī = with beautiful body features; subhastanī = with beautiful breasts; shete = slept; pīDya = tightly hugged; paṭaham = instrument called pataha; parishhvajyeva = as though hugging; ramaṇam = a lover; labdhvā = getting him; chirasya = after a long time.

A woman with beautiful body features and with beautiful breasts slept tightly and hugged instrument called pataha as though hugging a lover, getting him after a long time.

Sloka 40

kācid aṃśam pariṣvajya suptā kamala locanā |
rahaḥ priyatamam gṛhya sakāmeva ca kāminī || 5-10-40

40. kāchit = another woman; kamalalochanā = with lotus like eyes; parishhvajya = hugging; vasam = a flute; suptā = slept; sakāmā kāminīva = like a woman with lust; gṛihya = holding; priyatamam = (her) lover; rahaḥ = in secret.

Another woman with lotus like eyes hugging a flute slept like a woman with lust holding her lover in secret.

Sloka 41

vipañcaīm parigṛhyānyā niyatā nṛttaśālinī |
nidrā vaśam anuprāptā saha kāntā iva bhāminī || 5-10-41

41. anyā = another woman; nṛittashālinī = skilled in dance; anuprāptā = obtained; nidrāvasham = sleep; parigṛihyā = getting vipanJṇchīm = an instrument like veena; niyatā = (and being) in tune with it; bhāminī = like a woman; saha kānta = together with her lover.

Another woman skilled in dance obtained sleep getting an instrument like veena and being in tune with it like a woman together with her lover.

Sloka 42

anyā kanaka samkāśair mrdu pīnair mano ramaiḥ |
mṛdangam paripīḍya angaiḥ prasuptā matta locanā || 5-10-42

42. anyā = another woman; mattalochana = with lusty eyes; prasuptā = slept; paripīDyā = hugging; mṛidaṇ^ga = a percussion instrument called mridanga; anṇ^gaiḥ = with her body parts; manoharaiḥ = which were pleasant; kanakasamkāshaiḥ = which resembled gold; mṛidupīnaiḥ = and which were smooth and fleshy.

Another woman with lusty eyes slept hugging a percussion instrument called mridanga with her body parts which were pleasant which resembled gold and which were smooth and fleshy.

Sloka 43

bhuja pārśva antarasthena kakṣagena krśa udarī |
paṇavena saha anindyā suptā mada krta śramā || 5-10-43

43. kṛishodarī = another woman with thin stomach; anindyā = who was not to be blamed; suptā = slept; madakṛitasramā = due to tiresomeness from lust; padameshaḥ = together with an instrument called Padama; bhujapāshāntarasthena = between her shoulders; kakshagena = and reaching arm pits.

Another woman with thin stomach, who was not to be blamed, slept due to tiresomeness from lust together with an instrument called Padama between her shoulders and reaching arm pits.

Sloka 44

ḍiṇḍimam parigrhya anyā tathaiva āsakta ḍiṇḍimā |
prasuptā taruṇam vatsam upagūhya iva bhāminī || 5-10-44

44. anyā = another woman; āsaktaDiṇDimā = with an instrument called Dindima near her; parigṛihya = got; DinDimam = that Dindima; prasuptā = slept; tathaiva = and in the same way; bhāminī iva = as a woman; taruṇamupaguhya = hugging her husband; vastham = and also her child;

Another woman with an instrument called Dindima near her slept in the same way as a woman hugging her husband and also her child.

Sloka 45

kācid āḍambaram nārī bhuja sambhoga pīḍitam |
kṛtvā kamala patra akṣī prasuptā mada mohitā || 5-10-45

45. kāchitnārī = another woman; kamalapatrākshi = with eyes like lotus petals; prasuptā = slept; kṛitvā = making; āDambaram = the instrument called ādambara; bhujasamyogapīDitam = pressing it by her shoulders; madamoohitā = being desired by lust;

Another woman with eyes like lotus petals slept making the instrument called adambara pressing it by her shoulders being desired by lust.

Sloka 46

kalaśīm apaviddhya anyā prasuptā bhāti bhāminī |
vasante puṣpa śabalā mālā iva parimārjitā || 5-10-46

46. anyā bhāminī = another women; prasuptā = sleeping; apavidhya = felling down; kalasīm = a small pot; bhāti = shone; māleva = like a flower garland; pushhpashabalā = with the flowers of strange hue; vasante parimārjitā = made auspicious in spring.

Another woman sleeping, felling down a small pot, shone like a flower garland with the flowers of strange hue made auspicious in spring.

Sloka 47

pāṇibhyām ca kucau kācit suvarṇa kalaśa upamau |
upagūhya abalā suptā nidrā bala parājitā || 5-10-47

47. kāchit ablā = another woman; suptā = slept; upaguhyā = hugging; kuchau = her breasts; suvarṇkalashopamau = resembling golden pots; pāṇibhyām = by her hands; nidrābalaparājitā = overcome with sleep.

Another woman slept hugging her breasts resembling golden pots by her hands, overcome with sleep.

Sloka 48

anyā kamala patra akṣī pūrṇa indu sadrśa ānanā |
anyām ālingya suśroṇī prasuptā mada vihvalā || 5-10-48

48. anyā = another woman; kamalapatrākshi = with eyes like lotus petals; pūrṇedu sadṛishānana = with her face resembling full moon; madavihvalā = overcome with lust; prasuptā = slept; āliṇ^gyā = hugging; anyām = another woman; shushroṇīm = with a beautiful hip region.

Another woman with eyes like lotus petals, with her face resembling full moon, overcome with lust slept hugging another woman with a beautiful hip region.

Sloka 49

ātodyāni vicitrāṇi pariṣvajya vara striyaḥ |
nipīḍya ca kucaiḥ suptāḥ kāminyaḥ kāmukān iva || 5-10-49

49. varastriyaḥ = some excellent woman; suptāḥ = slept; parishvajya = hugging; vichitrāṇi ātodhyāni = strange instruments; kuchaih nipīDhya = and pressing them with breasts; kāminyaḥ kāmukāniva = as though lustful woman with lusty men;

ṣome excellent women slept hugging strange instruments and pressing them with breasts as though lustful woman with lusty men.

Sloka 50

tāsām eka anta vinyaste śayānām śayane śubhe |
dadarśa rūpa sampannām aparām sa kapiḥ striyam || 5-10-50

50. saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuma; dadarsha = saw; tāsām = among those women; rūpasampannām striyam = a very beautiful woman; syānām = sleeping; shubhe shayane = on an auspicious couch; ekānta vinyaste = arranged alone at a side.

ṭhat ḥanuma saw among those women a very beautiful woman sleeping on an auspicious couch arranged alone at a side.

Sloka 51

muktā maṇi samāyuktair bhūṣaṇaiḥ suvibhūṣitām |
vibhūṣayantīm iva ca sva śriyā bhavana uttamam || 5-10-51
gaurīm kanaka varṇa ābhām iṣṭām antaḥ pura īśvarīm |
kapir manda udarīm tatra śayānām cāru rūpiṇīm || 5-10-52

51;52. kapiḥ = ḥanuma; mandodarīm = (saw) ṃandodari; charurūpiṇīm = with a beautiful form; muktāmaṇisamāmuktaiḥ = together with diamonds and pearls; suvibhūshhitām well decorated; bhūshhaṇaiḥ = by jewellery; svashriya = and with her self radiance; vibhūshhayantīmiva = as though decorating bhavanottamam = that great building; gaurīm = with a fair complexion; kanakavarṇābhām = and with a radiance like golden colour; ishhṭām = who was dear to her husband; antaḥpureshvarīm = the lady of the women in that gynaeceum; shayānām = sleeping; tatra = there.

ḥanuma saw ṃandodari with a beautiful form together with diamonds and pearls, well decorated by jewellery and with her self radiance as though decorating that great building with a fair complexion and with a radiance like golden colour, who was dear to her husband the lady of women in that gynaeceum sleeping there.

Sloka 53

sa tām dṛṣṭvā mahā bāhur bhūṣitām māruta ātmajaḥ |
tarkayām āsa sītā iti rūpa yauvana sampadā || 5-10-53
harṣeṇa mahatā yukto nananda hari yūthapaḥ |

53. saḥ mārutātmajaḥ = that ḥanuma; mahābāhuḥ = with great arms; tāmdṛishhṭvā = seeing that woman; bhūshhitām = well decorated; tarkayāmāsa = in logic though; sīteti = this is ṣeetha; rūpayauvana sampadā = by the wealth of her appearance and her youth; hariyudhapaḥ = that warrior of vanaras; nananda = was delighted; mahatā harshhenayuktaḥ = together with great pleasure.

ṭhat ḥanuma with great arms seeing the woman well decorated, out of logic thought thus: "ṭhis is Seetha by the wealth of her appearance and her youth". That warrior of Vanaras was delighted together with great pleasure.

Sloka 54

āśpoṭayām āsa cucumba puccam |
nananda cikrīḍa jagau jagāma|
stambhān arohan nipapāta bhūmau |
nidarśayan svām prakrtim kapīnām || 5-10-54

54. āsphoṭayāmāsa = clasped his arms; chuchumbha puchchham = kissed his tail; nananda = he was delighted; chikrīDa = he was playful; jagau = sang; jagāma = paced; nidarshayan = showing; svām = his; kappīnām prakṛitim = simian nature; ārohat = climbed; stambāt = pillars; nipapāta bhūmau = and fell down on land.

ḥe clasped his arms, kissed his tail he was delighted, he was playful, sang, paced showing his simian nature, climbed pillars and fell down on land.
