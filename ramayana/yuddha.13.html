Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 13
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Advised by ṃahaparsva to take charge of Sita by force, Ravana expresses his inability to do so, because of a curse pronounced against him by Brahma (lord of creation) in the past. Ravana then boasts of his own prowess.

Sloka 1

rāvaṇam kruddhamājñāya mahāpārśvo mahābalaḥ |
muhūrta manusaṃcintya prāñjalirvākya mabravīt || 6-13-1

1. āG^yāya = noticing; rāvaṇam = Ravana; kruddham = enraged; mahābalāḥ = a very strong; mahāpārshvaḥ = ṃahaparsva; anusanchintya = thought; muhūrtam = for a while; prāṇjaliḥ = and with joined palms; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = these words:

Perceiving Ravana enraged, a very strong demon named ṃahaparsva thought for a while and with joined palms, spoke as follows:

Sloka 2

yaḥ khalvapi vanam prāpya mṛgavyālaniṣevitam |
na pibenmadhu samprāpya sa naro bāliśo dhruvam || 6-13-2

2. khaluapi = besides indeed; yaḥ naraḥ = which man; prāpya = after reaching; vanam = a forest; mṛigavyāla nishhevitam = inhabited by wild beasts and vicious elephants; samprāpya = obtained; madhu = honey; na pibet = does not drink; saḥ = he; dhruvam = surely; bālishaḥ = is a fool."

"That man indeed is a fool, who after reaching a forest inhabited by wild beasts and vicious elephants and having duly found honey, does not drink it."

Sloka 3

īśvarasyeśvaraḥ ko.asti tava śatrunibarhaṇa |
ramasva saha vaidehyā śatrūnākramya mūrdhasu || 6-13-3

3. shatrunibarhaṇa = O; annihilator of enemies! Kaḥ = who; asti = is; īshvaraḥ = a lord; tava = to you; īshvarsya = who are a lord of all? ākramya = stepping upon; mūrdhasu = the heads; shatrūn = of your enemies; ramasva = you take pleasure; vaidehyā saha = with ṣita."

"O,annihilator of enemies! Who is a lord to you, who are a lord of all? Setting your foot upon the heads of your enemies, take pleasure with Sita."

Sloka 4

balātkukkuṭavṛttena pravartasva mahābala |
akramyākramya sītām vai tām bhuṅiksva ca ramasva ca || 6-13-4

4. mahābala = O; ruler endowed with a mighty prowess! pravartasva = Behave; kukkuṭa vṛittena = in the mode of a cock; balata = using force against her; bhuṇkshhva = enjoy; tām sītām = that ṣita; ākramya ākramya = charging and charging; ramasya cha = and revel with her."

"O, ruler endowed with a mighty prowess! Act in the mode of a cock, forcibly against her. Enjoy that Sita, by charging on her again and again and revel with her."

Sloka 5

labdhakāmasya te paścādāgamiṣyati kim bhayam |
prāptamaprāptakālam vā sarvam pratividhāsyate || 6-13-5

5. kim bhayam = what fear; āgamishhyati = will come; pashchāt = after; labdhakāmasya = fulfilling your lust? sarvam = All; prāptum = that comes; aprāptakālam = or yet to come ; pratividhānyate = will be prevented."

"What fear will be there to you, after fulfilling your lust? All that comes or yet to come will be prevented (by us)."

Sloka 6

kumbhakarṇaḥ sahāsmābhirindrajicca mahābalaḥ |
pratiṣedhayitum śaktau savajramapi vajriṇam || 6-13-6

6. kumbhakarṇaḥ = Kumbhakarna; indrajitcha = and Indrajit; mahābalaḥ = of great might; asmābhiḥcha = with us; shaktou = are capable; pratishhedhayitum = of keeping off; vajriṇam api = even Devendra(god of celestials); savajram = wielding the weapon of a thunderbolt".

"Kumbhakarna and Indrajit of great might together with us are capable of keeping off even Devendra (god of celestials), wielding the weapon of a thunderbolt."

Sloka 7

upapradānam sāntvam vā bhedam vā kuśalaiḥ kṛtam |
samatikramya daṇḍena siddhimartheṣu rocaye || 6-13-7

7. samatikramya = having overstepped; upapradānam = the method of giving away gifts; sā stvam vā = or gentle means; bhedamvā = or showing dissention; kṛitam = being done; kushalaiḥ = by right men ;rochaye = I like; siddhim = accomplishment; artheshhu = in actions; daṇDena = through hostility."

"Overstepping the methods of giving away gifts or conciliation or even showing dissention being followed by right men, I like accomplishment of actions through hostile means."

Sloka 8

iha prāptānvayam sarvān śatrūṃstava mahābala |
vaśe śastrapratāpena kariṣyāmo na saṃśayaḥ || 6-13-8

8. mahābala = O; ruler endowed with great might! Vayam = We; karishhyāmaḥ = shall make; sarvān = all; tava = your; shatrūn = enemies; prāptān = reached; iha = here; vashe = to surrender; shastrapratāpena = by the power of our weapons; na samshayaḥ = there is no doubt."

"O, ruler endowed with great might! We shall make all your enemies reaching here to surrender, by the power of our weapons. There is no doubt about it."

Sloka 9

evamuktastadā rājā mahāpārśvaena rāvaṇaḥ |
tasya sampūjayanvākyamidam vacanamabravīt || 6-13-9

9. tadā = then; sampūjayan = appreciating; tasya = his; vākyam = words; evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; mahāpārshvena = by ṃahaparsva; rājā = king; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; abravīt = spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words:

Appreciating the words spoken thus by ṃahaparsva, King Ravana spoke the following words:

Sloka 10

mahāpārśva nibodha tvam rahasyam kiṃcidātmanaḥ |
ciravṛttam tadākhyāsye yadavāptam purā mayā || 6-13-10

10. mahāpārshva = " O;ṃahaparsva; nibodha = know; kimchit = a little; rahasyam = secret; ātmanaḥ = about me; ākhyāsye = I shall tell; tat = that; yat = which; avāptam = was obtained; mayā = by me; purā = previously; chiravṛittām = which occurred long ago."

"O, ṃahaparsva! Know a little secret about me. I shall tell you an incident, which occurred to me long ago."

Sloka 11

pitāmahasya bhavanam gaccantīm puñjikasthalām |
cañcūryamāṇāmadrākṣamākāśe.agniśikhāmiva || 6-13-11

11. adrākshham = I saw; puṇjikasthala = Punjikasthala; (by name); agnishikhāmiva = gleaming like a flame; chaṇchūryamāṇām = concealing herself (for fear of me); ākāshe = in the sky; gachchhantīm = and proceeding; bhavanam = to the abode; pitāmahasya = of Brahma."

"Once I saw a celestial nymph, Punjikasthala(by name) flashing like a flame, concealing herself in the sky and proceeding towards the abode of Brahma."

Sloka 12

sā prasahya mayā bhuktā kṛtā vivasanā tataḥ |
svayambhūbhavanam prāptā lolitā nalinī yathā || 6-13-12

12. sā = she; kṛitā = was made; vivasanā = unclothed; bhuktā = and was enjoyedmayā = by me; prahasya = forcibly; tataḥ = thereafter; prāptā = she reached; svayambhū bhavanam = the abode of Brahma; nalinīyathā = like a lotus; lolitā = which was crumled."

"She was made unclothed by me and was enjoyed forcibly. Thereafter, appearing like a crumpled lotus, she went to the abode of Brahma."

Sloka 13

tacca tasya tadā manye jñātamāsīnmahātmanaḥ |
atha samkupito vedhā māmidam vākyamabravī || 6-13-13

13. manye = I think; tachcha = it; G^yātam = was informed; tadā = then; tasya = to Brahma; mahātmanaḥ = the high soled; atha = and then; samkupitaḥ = the enraged; vedhāḥ = Brahama; abravīt = spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words; mām = to me."

"I think that the matter was made known to the high soled Brahama and then the enraged Brahma spoke to me the following words:

Sloka 14

adyaprabhṛti yāmanyām balānnārīm gamiṣyasi |
tadā te śatadhā murdhā phaliṣyati na saṃśayaḥ || 6-13-14

14. adya prabhṛti = from today onwards; gamishhyasi = if you revel with ; yām anyām = any other; nārīm = woman; balān = forcibly; tadā = then; te = your; mūrthā = head; phalishhyati = will break asunder; shatadhā = into hundred (pieces); na samshayaḥ = there is no doubt."

"From today onwards, if you revel with any other woman forcibly, your head then undoubtedly will break asunder into a hundred pieces."

Sloka 15

ityaham tasya śāpasya bhītaḥ prasabhameva tām |
nārohaye balātsītām vaidehīm śayne śubhe || 6-13-15

15. bhītaḥ = fearing; tasya = his; shāpasya = curse; iti = in this manner; aham = I; nārohye tām sītām vaidehīm = am not making that ṣita; the daughter of king Videha to mount; shube = (my) beautiful; shayane = bed; prasabhameva = hurriedly."

"Fearing the curse given in this manner by Brahama, I am not violently making Sita the daughter of Videha, to mount on my beautiful bed forcibly."

Sloka 16

sāgarasyeva me vego mārutasyeva me gatiḥ |
naitaddāśarathirveda hyāpādayati tena mām || 6-13-16

16. me = my; vegaḥ = swiftness; sāgarasyeva = is like that of an ocean; me = my; gatiḥ = movement; mārutasyeva = is like that of wind; dāsarathiḥ = Rama = na veda = does not know; etad = this; tena = that is why; āsādayati hi = he is indeed attacking; mām = me."

"ṃy swiftness is like that of an ocean. ṃy movement is like that of a wind. Rama does not know this and hence he is indeed having an encounter with me."

Sloka 17

ko hi simhamivāsīnam suptam giriguhāśaye |
kruddham mṛtyumivā.asīnam sambodhayitumiccati || 6-13-17

17. kohi = who; ichchhati = wants; sambodhayitum = to arouse; (me); āsīnam = sitting; simham iva = like a lion; suptam = sleeping; giriguhāshaye = at a place in a mountain cave; mṛityum iva = like death; āsīnam = sitting; kṛIddham = enraged."

"If not otherwise, who wants to arouse me (for a combat) as one would awaken a lion asleep, sitting at a place in a mountain cave or arouse into activity an enraged Death?"

Sloka 18

na matto nirgatān bāṇān dvijihvān panngāniva |
rāmaḥ paśyati samgrāme tena māmabhigaccati || 6-13-18

18. rāmaḥ = Rama; na pashyati = did not see; bāṇām = the arrows; pannagāniva = looking like serpents; dvijihvān = with two tongues; nirgatān = coming forth; mattaḥ = from me; tena = for that reason; abhigachchhati = he is coming against; mām = me; samgrāme = in battle."

"Rama did not see the arrows, looking like two tongued serpents, coming forth from me. That is why he is marching against me for a battle"

Sloka 19

kṣipram vajrasamairbāṇaiḥ śatadhā kārmukacutaiḥ |
rāmamādīpayiṣyāmi ulkabhiriva kuñjaram || 6-13-19

19. ādīpayishhyāmi = I shall blaze up; rāmam = Rama; kshhipram = quickly; bāṇaiḥ = by arrows; vajrasamaiḥ = resembling thunderbolts; shatadhā = in hundreds; kārmukachyutaiḥ = released from my bow; kuṇjaram iva = like an elephant; ullābhiḥ = with fire brands."

"I shall blaze up Rama swiftly with my arrows in hundreds resembling thunder bolts discharged from my bow, like tormenting an elephant with fire brands."

Sloka 20

taccāsya balamādāsye balena mahatā vṛtaḥ |
uditaḥ savitā kāle nakṣatrāṇām prabhāmiva || 6-13-20

20. vṛitaḥ = surrounded; mahatā balena = by a great army; ādasya = I shall take away; asya = his; tat = that; balam = strength; prabhāmiva = like taking away the light; nakshhatrāṇām = of the stars; uditaḥ = by a rising; savitā = sun; kāle = in dawn."

"Surrounded by great army, I shall take away that strength of Rama, as a rising sun at dawn takes away radiance of the stars."

Sloka 21

na vāsavenāpi sahasracakṣuṣā |
yudhāsmi śakyo varuṇena vā punaḥ |
mayā tviyam bāhubalena nirjitā |
purā purī vaiśravaṇena pālitā || 6-13-21

21. vāsavenāpi = even by Devendra(Lord of celestials) ; sahasra chakshhushhā = with thousand eyes; varuṇenavā = or by Varuna(king of the universe); nashakyaḥ asmi = I am not capable of being conquered; yudhā = in a battle; purā = once; iyam purī = this city; pālitā = ruled; vaishrevaṇena = by Kubera; (Lord of riches); nirjitā = was conquered; mayā = by me; bāhubalena = with the power of my arms."

"Even Devendra (lord of celestials) with thousand eyes or Varuna (king of the Universe) can not conquer me in battle. Once upon a time, this city of Lanka ruled by Kubera (Lord of riches) was conquered by me with the power of my arms."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe trayodaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 13th chapter of Yuddha Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
