Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 116
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Seetha gives a taunting reply to Rama, saying that why she was not abandoned by the latter, even at the time when Hanuma came to see her in Lanka. Then, Seetha requests Lakshmana to prepare a pile of fire for her to enter. When Lakshmana prepares a pyre, Seetha prays the fire-god and enters into it, in order toprove her conjugal fidelity.

Sloka 1

evamuktā tu vaidehī paruṣaṃ lomaharṣaṇam |
rāghaveṇa saroṣeṇa bhṛśaṃ pravyathitābhavat || 6-116-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; uktā = (the words) spoken; evam = thus; saroṣheṇa = with indignation; paruṣham = and harshness; rāghaveṇa = by Rama; roma harṣhaṇam = which caused her hair to stand on end; vaidehī = ṣeetha; abhavat = became; pravyathitā = very much perturbed.

Hearing the harsh words with indignation, spoken by Rama, which caused her hair to stand on end, Seetha became very much perturbed.

Sloka 2

sā tadaśrutapūrvaṃ hi jane mahati maithilī |
śrutvā bhartṛvaco rūkṣaṃ lajjayā vrīḍitābhavat || 6-116-2

2. shrutvā = hearing; ghoram = the terrific; vachaḥ = words; bhartuḥ = of her husband; ashruta pūrvam = which were never heard by her before; mahati jane = amidst a large gathering of people; sā maithilī = that ṣeetha; tadā = at that time; abhavat = became; avanatā = downcast; lajjayā = with shame.

Hearing the terrific words of her husband, which were never actually heard by her before, amidst a large gathering of people, Seetha stood bent low with shame.

Sloka 3

praviśantīva gātrāṇi svānyeva janakātmajā |
vākṣalyaistaiḥ saśalyeva bhṛśamaśrūṇyavartayat || 6-116-3

3. svāni gātrāṇi iva = as though her own limbs; pravishanti = were pierced; taiḥ vāksharaiḥ = by those arrow-like words; sashalyena = with pointed splinters; sā janakātmāa = that ṣeetha; āvartayat = shed; ashrūṇi = tears; bhṛisham = profusely.

As though her own limbs were pierced by those words, which were arrow-like with pointed splinters, Seetha shed profuse tears.

Sloka 4

tato bāṣpaparikliṣṭaṃ pramārjantī svamānanam |
śanairgadgadayā vācā bhartāramidamabravīt || 6-116-4

4. tataḥ = then; pramarjantī = wiping clean; swam ānanam = her face; bāṣhpa pariklinnam = which was bathed in tears; abravīt = (she) spot; dam = the following words; sharaiḥ = slowly; bhartāram = to her husband; gadgadayā vāchā = in a stammering voice.

Then, wiping clean her face, which was bathed in tears, she spoke the following words slowly, in a stammering voice to her husband.

Sloka 5

kiṃ māmasadṛśaṃ vākyamīdṛśaṃ śrotradāruṇam |
rūkṣaṃ śrāvayase vīra prākṛtaḥ prākṛtām iva || 6-116-5

5. vīra = O valiant one!; kim = why; shrārayase = are you causing me to hear; īdṛisham = such; rūkṣham vākyam = harsh words; shrotra dāruṇam = which are violent to hear; mām = for me; prākṛitaḥ iva = like a common man; prākṛitām = (speaking) to a common woman?

"O valiant Rama! Why are you speaking such harsh words, which are violent to hear for me, like a common man speaking to a common woman?"

Sloka 6

na tathāsmi mahābāho yathā tvamavagacchasi |
pratyayaṃ gaccha me svena cāritreṇaiva te śape || 6-116-6

6. mahābāho = O the long-armed one!; na asmi = I am not; yathā tathā = the one in the way; avagachchhasi = you understand; mām = me; gachha = pick up; pratyayam = a trust; me = in me; shape = I swear; te = to you; svena chāritreṇa = by my own character.

"O the long-armed one! I am not the one in the way you understand me. Have a faith in me. I swear to you by my own character."

Sloka 7

pṛthakstrīṇāṃ pracāreṇa jātiṃ tvaṃ pariśaṅkase |
parityajemāṃ śaṅkāṃ tu yadi te.ahaṃ parīkṣitā || 6-116-7

7. prachāreṇa = by the conduct; pṛithak strīṇām = of vulgar women; tvam = you; parishaṇ^kase = distrust; jātim = the entire race of women; parityaja = give up; enām = this; shaṇ^kām = doubt; yadi aham = if; I; parīkṣhitā = have been actually tested (and found trustworthy); te = by you.

"By the conduct of vulgar woman you distrust the entire race of women. Give up this doubt, if I have been actually tested (and found trustworthy) by you."

Sloka 8

yadyahaṃ gātrasaṃsparśaṃ gatāsmi vivaśā prabho |
kāmakāro na me tatra daivaṃ tatrāparādhyati || 6-116-8

8. prabho = O lord!; na it was not; me kāmakāraḥ = my willfulness; iti yat = that; gatā asmi = when I came; gātra samsparsham = into contact; (with the person of Ravana); aham vivashā = I was helpless; daivam = my (adverse) fate; aparādhyati = was to blame; tatra = on that score.

"O lord! It was not my willfulness, when I came into contact with the person of Ravana. I was helpless. ṃy adverse fate was to blame on that score."

Sloka 9

madadhīnaṃ tu yattanme hṛdayaṃ tvayi vartate |
parādhīneṣu gātreṣu kiṃ kariṣyāmyanīśvarā || 6-116-9

9. me hṛidayam tu = my heart on its part; yat tat = which was; madadhīnam = subservient to me; vartate = was abiding; tvayi = in you; kim = what; kariṣhyāmi = could I do; anīshvarī = helpless as I was; gātreṣhu = anīshvarī = helpless as I was; gātreṣhu = with regard to my limbs; parādhīneṣhu = with regard to my limbs; parādhīneṣhu = which had fallen under the sway of another?

"ṃy heart, which was subservient to me, was abiding in you. What could I do, helpless as I was, with regard to my limbs which had fallen under the sway of another?"

Sloka 10

sahasaṃvṛddhabhāvācca saṃsargeṇa ca mānada |
yadyahaṃ te na vijñātā hatā tenāsmi śāśvatam || 6-116-10

10. mānada = O bestower of honour; aham na vijJṇatā yadi te = If I could not be fully known to you; sahasamvṛiddha bhāvena samsargeṇa cha = in spite of our love having simultaneously grown and despite of our having lived together; hatā asmi = I am ruined; shāshvatam = permanently; tena = by such ignorance.

"O bestower of honour! If I could not be fully known to you, in spite of our love having simultaneously grown and despite of our having lived together, I am ruined permanently by such ignorance."

Sloka 11

preṣitaste yadā vīro hanūmānavalokakaḥ |
laṅkāsthāhaṃ tvayā vīra kiṃ tadā na visarjitā || 6-116-11

11. rajā = O king!; mahāvīraḥ = the great hero; hanumān = ḥanuma; preṣhitaḥ = was sent; te = by you; avalokakaḥ = as your search-agent; kim = why; aham = I; laṇ^kāsthā = who was still in Lankā; na visarjitā = was not abandoned; tadā = then itself?

"O king! Hanuma, the great hero, was sent by you as your search-agent. Why I, who was still in Lanka, was not abandoned then itself?"

Sloka 12

pratyakṣaṃ vānarendrasya tvadvākyasamanantaram |
tvayā santyaktayā vīra tyaktaṃ syājjīvitaṃ mayā || 6-116-12

12. vīra = O hero! jīvitam = life; syāt tyaktam = would have been given up; mayā = by me; samtyaktayā = when deserted; tvayā = by you; tadvākya samanantaram = immediately on hearing the message (conveying your desertion); asya vānarasya pratyakṣham = before the eyes of this monkey.

"O hero! Life would have been given up by me, when deserted by you; immediately on hearing the message (conveying your desertion) before the eyes of the monkey."

Sloka 13

na vṛthā te śramo.ayaṃ syātsaṃśaye nyasya jīvitam |
suhṛjjanaparikleśo na cāyaṃ niṣphalastava || 6-116-13

13. ayam vṛithā shramaḥ = this wasteful endeavour; (in the form of crossing over to Lanka and waging war against the mighty Ravana); nyasya = keeping; jīvitam = your life; samshaye = in jeopardy; nasyāt = would not have been there; na cha = nor; ayam tava suhṛijjana viphalaḥ parikleshaḥ = would have your friends have been put to such fruitless hardship.

"This wasteful endeavour (in the form of crossing over to Lanka and waging war against the mighty Ravana, keeping your life in jeopardy), would not have been there, nor would have your friends been put to such fruitless hardship."

Sloka 14

tvayā tu naraśārdūla krodhamevānuvartatā |
laghuneva manuṣyeṇa strītvameva puraskṛtam || 6-116-14

14. nṛipa shārdūla = O excellent king; tvayā tu = you; however; puraskṛitam = gave priority; strītvameva = to woman liness; laghunā manuṣhyeṇa iva = like a feeble man; anuvartatā = conforming; roṣhameva = to just an emotion of anger.

"O excellent king! You, however, like a feeble man, gave priority to womanliness, conforming yourself to just an emotion of anger."

Sloka 15

apadeśena janakānnotpattirvasudhātalāt |
mama vṛttaṃ ca vṛttajña bahu te na puraskṛtam || 6-116-15

15. vṛittajJṇaḥ = O knower of virtuous conduct!; utpattiḥ = my birth; janakāt = was from Janaka; apadesheva = in disguise; vasudhātalāt = but was actually from the earth; mama = my; vṛittam cha = sacred birth; bahu = of a high degree; na puraskṛitam = was not honoured; te = by you.

"O knower of virtuous conduct! ṃy birth was from Janaka in disguise; but was actually from the earth. ṃy sacred birth of such a high degree, was not honoured by you."

Sloka 16

na pramāṇīkṛtaḥ pāṇirbālye bālena pīḍitaḥ |
mama bhaktiśca śīlaṃ ca sarvaṃ te pṛṣṭhataḥ kṛtam || 6-116-16

16. mama pāṇiḥ = my hand; nipīDitaḥ = taken (as a bride by you); bālye = in our childhood; na pramāṇīkṛitaḥ = was not recognized; mama = my; bhaktishcha = devotion; shīlam cha = my chastity; sarvam = all; pṛiṣhṭhataḥ kṛitam = have been ignored; te = by you.

"ṃy hand taken (by you as a bride) in our childhood was not duly recognized by you. ṃy devotion, my chastity and all have been ignored by you."

Sloka 17

iti bruvantī rudatī bāṣpagadgadabhāṣiṇī |
uvāca lakṣmaṇaṃ sītā dīnaṃ dhyānaparāyaṇam || 6-116-17

17. sītā = ṣeetha; iti = thus; bruvantī = speaking; rudatī = weaping; bāṣhpa gadgadabhāṣhiṇī = and stammering with tears; uvācha = said; lakṣhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; dīnam = who was sad; dhyāna parāyaṇam = and engaged in thoughtfulness.

Seetha, thus speaking, weeping and stammering with tears, said to Lakshmana, who was sad and engaged in thoughtfulness (as follows):

Sloka 18

citāṃ me kuru saumitre vyasanasyāsya bheṣajam |
mithyāpavādopahatā nāhaṃ jīvitumutsahe || 6-116-18

18. saumitre = O Lakshmana!; kuru = create; chitām = a pile of fire; me = for me; bheṣhajam = which is a remedy; asya vyasanasya = for this trouble; aham mithyāpavādopahatā = smitten as I am with false blames; na utsahe = (I) no longer wish; jīvitum = to survive.

"O Lakshmana! Create a pile of fire, for me, which is a remedy for this disaster. I no longer wish to survive, smitten as am with false blames."

Sloka 19

aprītena guṇairbhartrā tyaktā yā janasaṃsadi |
yā kṣamā me gatirgantuṃ pravekṣye havyavāhanam || 6-116-19

19. pravekṣhyāmi = I will enter; havyavāhanam = a fire; gantum = to obtain; gatiḥ = a course; yā kṣhamā = which is appropriate; me = for me; tyaktāyaḥ = who has been jana samsadi = amidst an assembly of men; bhartrā = by me husband;aprītena = who was not satisfied; guṇaiḥ = with my traits.

"I will enter a fire, to obtain the only course appropriate for me, who has been abandoned amidst an assembly of men, by my husband who was not satisfied with my traits."

Sloka 20

evaṃ bruvāṇā rudatī bāṣpagadgadabhāṣiṇī |
abravīllakṣmaṇaṃ sītā dīnaṃ dhyānaparaṃ sthitam || 6-116-20

20. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; vaidehyā = by ṣeetha; lakṣhmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; para vīrahā = the destroyer of enemy-warriors; āpannaḥ amarṣvā vasham = who gave way to wrath; samudaikṣhata = looked; rāghavam = towards Rama.

Hearing the words of Seetha, Lakshmana, the destroyer of enemy warriors, giving way to wrath, looked towards Rama.

Sloka 21

sa vijñāya manaśchandaṃ rāmasyākārasūcitam |
citāṃ cakāra saumitrirmate rāmasya vīryavān || 6-116-21

21. vijJṇāya = understanding; manashchandam = the inclination of the mind; rāmasya = of Rama; ākāra sūchitam = hinted by the expression in his face; saḥ vīryavān = that valiant; saumitriḥ = Lakshmana; chakāra = prepared; chitām = a pyre; rāmasya mte = in deference to the wishes of Rama's.

Understanding the inclination of the mind of Rama, hinted by the expression in his face, that valiant Lakshmana prepared a pyre, in deference to the wishes of Rama.

Sloka 22

na hi rāmaṃ tadā kaścitkālāntakayamopamam |
anunetumatho vaktuṃ draṣṭuṃ vā pyaśakatsuhṛt || 6-116-22

22. na kashchit suhṛit = no one near and dear; ashakat hi = was indeed able; anuvetum = to cajole; tadā = on that occasion; atho = or; vaktum = to speak; draṣhṭum va api = or even to look upon; rāmam = Rama; kālāntakayamopamam = who appeared like Yama the lord of death at the time of dissolution of the world.

No one near and dear was indeed able to cajole on that occasion or to speak or even to look upon Rama, who appeared like Yama the lord of death at the time of dissolution of the world.

Sloka 23

adhomukhaṃ tato rāmaṃ śanaiḥ kṛtvā pradakṣiṇam |
upāsarpata vaidehī dīpyamānaṃ hutāśanam || 6-116-23

23. tataḥ = thereupon; vaidehī = ṣeetha; pradakṣhiṇam kṛitvā = doing circumambulation; rāmam = to Rama; sthitam athomukham = standing with his head bent low Rama; upāvartata = went towards; dīptamānam = the blazing; hutāshanam = fire.

Thereupon, Seetha, after doing circumambulation to Rama, who was standing with his head bent low, proceeded towards the blazing fire.

Sloka 24

praṇamya devatābhyaśca brāhmaṇebhyaśca maithilī |
baddhāñjalipuṭā cedamuvācāgnisamīpataḥ || 6-116-24

24. praṇamya = having offered salutation; daivebhyashcha = to gods; brāhmaṇebhyashcha = and Brahmins; baddhāṇ^jalipuṭā = as also having joined her palms; agni samīpataḥ = in the vicinity of the fire; maithilī = ṣeetha; uvācha = spoke; idam = the following words:

Having offered salutation to gods and brahmins as also having joined her palms in the vicinity of the fire, Seetha spoke the following words:

Sloka 25

yathā me hṛdayaṃ nityaṃ nāpasarpati rāghavāt |
tathā lokasya sākṣī māṃ sarvataḥ pātu pāvakaḥ || 6-116-25

25. yathā = as; me = my; hṛidayam = heart; nāpasarpati nityam = never moves off; rāghavāt = from Rama; tathā = so; pāvakaḥ = let the fire-god; lokasya sākṣhī = the witness of the world; pātu = protect; mām = me; sarvataḥ = from all sides.

"As my heart never moves off from Rama, so let the fire-god, the witness of the world, protect me from all sides."

Sloka 26

yathā māṃ śuddhacaritāṃ duṣṭāṃ jānāti rāghavaḥ |
tathā lokasya sākṣī māṃ sarvataḥ pātu pāvakaḥ || 6-116-26

26. yathā = as; rāghavaḥ; Rama; jānāti = apprehends; mām = me; shuddha charitām = though of unimpeachable conduct; duṣhṭām = to be spoilt; pāvakaḥ = let the fire-god; lokasya sākṣhī = the witness of the world; pātu = protect; mām = me; sarvataḥ = from all sides.

"As Rama apprehends me, though of unimpeachable conduct, to be spoilt, let the fire-god the witness of the world protect me from all sides."

Sloka 27

karmaṇā manasā vācā yathā nāticarāmyaham |
rāghavaṃ sarvadharmajñaṃ tathā māṃ pātu pāvakaḥ || 6-116-27

27. yathā = as; aham = I; nāticharāmi = have never been unfaithful; karmaṇā = in act; manasā = thought; vāchā = and speech; rāghavam = to Rama; sarvadharmajJṇam = who knows all the virtues; tathā = so; pāvakaḥ = let the fire-god; pātu = protect; mām = me.

"As I have never been unfaithful in act, thought and speech to Rama, who knows all the virtues, so let the fire-god protect me."

Sloka 28

ādityobhavān vāyurdiśaścandrastathaiva ca |
ahaścāpi tathā sandhye rātriśca pṛthivī tathā || 6-116-28
yathānye.api vijānanti tathā cāritrasaṃyutām |

28. yathā = since; bhagavān = the adorable; ādityaḥ = sun-god; vāyuḥ = wind-god; dishaḥ = the four quarters; tathaiva cha = and even so; chandraḥ = the moon-god; āshchāpi = as also the deity presiding over the day-time; tathā = and; samdhye = the twilights; rātrishcha = and the night; tathā = and; pṛithivī = the earth; anyo.api = and even others; vijānanti = know (me); chāritra samyutām = to be endowed with good conduct; tathā = so; (let the fire-god protect me).

"Since the adorable sun-god, wind-god, the four quarters and even so the moon-god, as also the deity presiding over the day-time and the twilights and the night and the earth and even others know me to be endowed with good conduct, so let the fire-god protect me."

Sloka 29

evamuktvā tu vaidehī parikramya hutāśanam || 6-116-29
viveśa jvalanaṃ dīptaṃ niḥsaṅgenāntarātmanā |

29. evam = thus; uktvā = speaking; vaidehī = ṣeetha; parikramya = walking around; hutāshanam = the fire-god; antarātmanā = mind; niḥ shaṇ^kena = free from hesitation; vivesha = entered; dīptam jvalanam = the blazing fire.

Thus speaking, Seetha walking around the fire-god, with her mind free from hesitation, entered the blazing fire.

Sloka 30

janaḥ sa sumahāṃstatra bālavṛddhasamākulaḥ || 6-116-30
dadarśa maithilīṃ tatra praviśantīṃ hutāśanam |

30. sumahān janashcha = a large gathering of men; bāla vṛiddha samākulaḥ = including children and elders; dadarsha = saw; dīptām = the shining; maithilīm = ṣeetha; pravishantīm = having entered; hutāshanam = the fire; tatra = there.

A large gathering of men including children and elders, saw the shining Seetha having entered the fire there.

Sloka 31

sā taptanavahemābhā taptakāñcanabhūṣaṇā || 6-116-31
papāta jvalanaṃ dīptaṃ sarvalokasya saṃnidhau |

31. sā = that ṣeetha; taptanava hemābhā = having a shining of fresh refined gold; tapta kāṇ^chana bhūṣhaṇā = and decked with ornaments of refined gold; papāta = plunged; dīptam jvalnnam = into the blazing fire; sarvalopasya samnidhau = in the presence of all people.

That Seetha, with the shining of fresh refined gold and decked with ornaments of refined gold, plunged into the blazing fire, in the presence of all people.

Sloka 32

dadṛśustāṃ viśālākṣīṃ patantīṃ havyavāhanam || 6-116-32
sītāṃ sarvāṇi rūpāṇi rukmavedinibhāṃ tadā |

32. sarvāṇi = all; rūpāṇi = the living beings; dadṛishuḥ = saw; tadā = then; tām vishālākṣhīm sītām = that wide-eyed ṣeetha; rukavedi nibhām = and looking like a sacrificial altar made of gold; patantīm = who was plunging; havya vāhanam = into the fire.

All the living beings saw then that wide-eyed Seetha, who looked like a golden altar, plunging into the fire.

Sloka 33

dadṛśustāṃ mahābhāgāṃ praviśantīṃ hutāśanam || 6-116-33
sītāṃ kṛtsnāstrayo lokāḥ puṇyāmājyāhutīmiva |

33. kṛitsnāḥ = the entire; trayaḥ = three; lokāḥ = kinds of people (viz. ṣages; gods and Gandharvas); dadṛishuḥ = saw; tām mahābhāgām sītām = that illustrious ṣeetha; pravishantīm = entering; hutāshanam = the fire; puṇyām ājyāhutīmiva = as a sacred oblation of clarified butter.

The sages, gods, and the Gandharvas saw that illustrious Seetha entering deep into the fire as a sacred oblation of clarified butter.

Sloka 34

pracukruśuḥ striyaḥ sarvāstāṃ dṛṣṭvā havyavāhane || 6-116-34
patantīṃ saṃskṛtāṃ manrairvasordhārāmivādhvare |

34. sarvāḥ = all; striyaḥ = the women; prachukrushuḥ = screamed; dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; tām = her; patantīm = rushing; havyavāhane = into the fire; dharāmiva = like a gush; samskṛitām vasoḥ = of clarified butter; adhvare = plunging into sacrificial fire; mantraiḥ = duly consecrated with Vedic hymns.

All the women (who were present there) screamed on seeing her, rushing into the fire, like a gush of clarified butter plunging into the sacrificial fire, duly consecrated by Vedic hymns.

Sloka 35

dadṛśustāṃ trayo lokā devagandharvadānanāḥ || 6-116-35
śaptāṃ patantīṃ niraye tridivāddevatāmiva |

35. trayaḥ = the three; lokāḥ = worlds; devagandharva dānavāḥ = the gods; Gandharvas and demons; dadṛishuḥ = beheld; tām = her; devatāmi va = as a goddess; patantīm = rushing; shaptām niraye = into a cursed hell; tridivāt = from heaven.

The denizens of the entire three worlds including the gods, Gandharvas and demons, beheld her falling into the fire, like a goddess, subjected to a curse, falling from heaven to hell.

Sloka 36

tasyāmagniṃ viśantyāṃ tu hāheti vipulaḥ svanaḥ || 6-116-36
rakṣasāṃ vānarāṇāṃ ca sambabhūvādbhutopamaḥ |

36. tasyām = while she; vishantyām = was entering deeply; agnim = into the fire; vipulaḥ svanaḥ = a loud sound; adbhutopamaḥ = which appeared strange; hā hā iti = uttering 'Alas; Alas'; sambabhūva = rose; rakṣhasām vānarāṇām cha = from both the demons and the monkeys alike.

While she was entering deeply into the fire, a loud sound, which appeared strange, uttering 'Alas, Alas' rose both from the demons and the monkeys alike.

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe ṣoḍaśādhikaśatatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 116th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
