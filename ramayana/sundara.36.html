Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 36

Introduction

Hanuma gives Seetha, the signet ring sent by Rama through him in order to strengthen her confidence in Seetha felt very happy to see the ring and praised Hanuma for his arrival to Lanka after having crossed the sea single handedly. She expresses her hope to Hanuma, saying that Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata and Sugreeva will come to Lanka with their armies so as to destroy Ravana, for her sake. Hearing Seetha's words, Hanuma consoles Seetha saying that Rama, Sugreeva and a host of monkeys will come to Lanka by crossing the ocean, so as to defeat Ravana and his army, with assurance that Rama will appear before her shortly.

Sloka 1

bhūya eva mahātejā hanūmān māruta ātmajaḥ |
abravīt praśritam vākyam sītā pratyaya kāraṇāt || 5-36-1

1. hanumān = ḥanuma; mahātejāḥ = the highly energetic; pavanātmajaḥ = son of wind-god; sītāprashraya kāraṇāt = with a reason to obtain confidence from ṣeetha; eva = again; abravīt = spoke; prashritam = modestly; vākyam = (the following) words:

ḥanuma, the highly energetic son of wind-god, with an inducement to obtain confidence from Seetha, again modestly spoke the following words:

Sloka 2

vānaro aham mahābhāge dūto rāmasya dhīmataḥ |
rāma nāma ankitam ca idam paśya devi angulīyakam || 5-36-2

2. mahābhāge = O illustrious lady!; aham = I; vānaraḥ = am a monkey; dūtaḥ = and a messenger; rāmasya = of Rama; dhīmataḥ = the wise; devi = O princess!; pashya = look at; idam = this; aṇ^gulīyakam cha = ring also; rāma nāmāṇkitam = marked with Rama's name.

"O illustrious lady! I am a monkey and a messenger of the wise Rama. O princess! Look at this ring too, marked with Rama's name."

Sloka 3

pratyayārtham tavānītam tena dattam mahātmanā |
samāśvasihi bhadram te kṣīṇa duhkha phalā hi asi || 5-36-3

3. tava pratyayārtham = for the purpose of creating your confidence; tena dattam = this ring given by Rama; mahātmanā = the high souled man; anītam = is brought; samāshrasihi = get consoled indeed; te bhadram = happiness to you! asi hi = you are indeed; kṣiṇaduḥkhaphalā = free from the object of your grief.

"I brought this ring given by the high souled Rama, for the purpose of gaining your confidence. Get consoled indeed. Happiness to you! You are now free from the object of your grief."

Sloka 4

gṛhītvā prekṣamāṇā sā bhartuḥ kara vibhūṣaṇam |
bhartāram iva samprāptā jānakī muditā abhavat || 5-36-4

4. gṛihītvā = taking; kara vibhūṣaṇam = that ring; adorned by Rama's hand; prekṣamāṇā = and seeing (it); sā jānakī = that ṣeetha; abravat = became; muditā = glad; samprāptā iva = as though she reached; bhartāram = her husband.

"I brought this ring given by the high souled Rama, for the purpose of gaining your confidence. Get consoled indeed. Happiness to you! You are now free from the object of your grief."

aśobhata viśālākṣyā rāhu mukta iva uḍu rāṭ || 5-36-5
tataḥ sā hrīmatī bālā bhartuḥ saṃdeśa harṣitā |
parituṭṣā priyam śrutvā prāśaṃsata mahākapim || 5-36-6

Then, that young Seetha, gladdened by the message of her husband, became shy and message of her husband, became shy and well-pleased. Uttering delightful words, she praised Hanuma the great monkey (as follows):

Sloka 7

vikrāntaḥ tvam samarthaḥ tvam prājñaḥ tvam vānara uttama |
yena idam rākṣasa padam tvayā ekena pradharṣitam || 5-36-7

7. vānarittana = O ḥanuma the best of monkeys!; yena tvayā = by which you; ekena = singly; idam = this; rākṣasa padam = place infested by demons; pradharṣitam = is overpowered; tvam = you as such; vikrāntaḥ = are valiant; tvam = you; samarthaḥ = are competent; tvam = you; prāJṇaḥ = are intelligent.

"O ḥanuma the best of monkeys! You have singly overpowered this place infested by demons. You are valiant. You are competent. You are intelligent."

Sloka 8

śata yojana vistīrṇaḥ sāgaro makara ālayaḥ |
vikrama ślāghanīyena kramatā goṣpadī kṛtaḥ || 5-36-8

8. kramatā = by you; who have crossed; vikrama shlāghanīyena = with your acclaimable gallantry; sāgaraḥ = the ocean; shatayojana vistīrṇaḥ = having a width of one hundred Yojanas; makarālayaḥ = and an asylum of crocodiles; goṣpadīkṛitaḥ = made it as a small puddle.

"You, with an acclaimable gallantry, crossed the ocean having a width of one hundred Yojanas and an asylum of crocodiles, making it as a small puddle."

Sloka 9

na hi tvām prākṛtam manye vanaram vanara ṛṣabha |
yasya te na asti samtrāso rāvaṇān na api sambhramaḥ || 5-36-9

9. vānarṣabha = O excellent of monkeys!; yasya te = from whom you; nāsti = there is no; samtrāpaḥ = fear; rāvaṇāt = from Ravana; na = nor; sambhramaḥ api = even bewilderment; na manye = I do not consider; tvām = you; prākṛitam = as an ordinary; vānaram = monkey.

"O excellent of monkeys! For you there is no fear nor even bewilderment from Ravana. Hence, I do not consider you as an ordinary monkey."

Sloka 10

arhase ca kapi śreṣṭha mayā samabhibhāṣitum |
yadi asi preṣitaḥ tena rāmeṇa vidita ātmanā || 5-36-10

10. kapishreṣṭha = O the best of monkeys!; asi yadi = If you are; preṣitaḥ = sent; tena rāmeṇa = by that Rama; viditātmanā = who knows the nature of his self; arhase = you are fit; samabhāṣitam = to talk well; mayā = with me.

"O the best of monkeys! If you are sent by that Rama, who knows the nature of his self, you are worthy of talking to me well."

Sloka 11

preṣayiṣyati durdharṣo rāmo na hi aparīkṣitam |
parākramam avijñāya mat sakāśam viśeṣataḥ || 5-36-11

11. durdharṣaḥ = the indomitable; rāmaḥ = Rama; aviJṇāya = without knowing; parākramam = your enterprise; aparīkṣitam = and without being tested; na preṣayiṣyatihi = must not have sent you; visheṣataḥ = and especially; matsakāsham = to my presence.

"ṭhe indomitable Rama must not have sent you indeed, especially to me, without knowing your enterprise and without testing you in advance."

Sloka 12

diṣṭyā ca kuśalī rāmo dharma ātmā dharma vatsalaḥ |
lakṣmaṇaḥ ca mahātejāḥ sumitra ānanda vardhanaḥ || 5-36-12

12. rāmaḥ = Rama; dharmātmā = the righteous man; satya sangaraḥ = who is true to his promise; lakṣmaṇashcha = and Lakshmana; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; sumitrānandavardhanaḥ = who enriches the joy of ṣumitra; diṣṭyā = are fortunately; kushalī = safe.

"Rama the righteous man who is true to his promise and Lakshmana of great splendour who enriches the joy of Sumitra are fortunately safe."

Sloka 13

kuśalī yadi kākutsthaḥ kim nu sāgara mekhalām |
mahīm dahati kopena yuga anta agniḥ iva utthitaḥ || 5-36-13

13. kākutthsaḥ kushalī yadi = If Rama is safe; kopena = with anger; kim = why; na dahati = is he not scorching; mahīm = the earth; sāgara mekhalām = with ocean as its belt; utthitaḥ yugāntāgniriva = like rising fire at the end of the world.

"If Rama is safe, with his anger why is he not scorching the earth with ocean as its belt, like rising fire at the end of the world?"

Sloka 14

athavā śaktimantau tau surāṇām api nigrahe |
mama eva tu na duhkhānām asti manye viparyayaḥ || 5-36-14

14. athavā = otherwise; tau = those two brothers; Rama and Lakshmana; shaktimantau = are having strength; nigrahe = in holding down; surāṇāmapi = even the celestials; tu = but; manye = I think; nāsti = there is no; viparyayaḥ = end; mama dukhānāmeva = to my troubles indeed.

"Otherwise, Rama and Lakshmana are capable of holding down even the celestials. But I think there is no end indeed to my troubles."

Sloka 15

kaccic ca vyathate rāmaḥ kaccin na paripatyate |
uttarāṇi ca kāryāṇi kurute puruṣa uttamaḥ || 5-36-15

15. na rāmaḥ vyathitaḥ kachchit = Is not Rama getting perturbed?; na paritapyate kachchit = is he not tormented with grief?; puruṣottamaḥ = Is Rama the best of men; kurute = doing; kāryāṇi = actions; uttarāṇi = to be done; (to annihilate his enemies?).

"Is not Rama getting perturbed? Is he not tormented with grief? Is Rama the best of men now doing the right acts to annihilate the enemies?"

Sloka 16

kaccin na dīnaḥ sambhrāntaḥ kāryeṣu ca na muhyati |
kaccin puruṣa kāryāṇi kurute nṛpateḥ sutaḥ || 5-36-16

16. nṛipate sutaḥ = is Rama the prince; na kachchit = not hopefully; dīnaḥ = miserable; sambhrāntaḥ = and confused?; nacha muhyati = nor even mistaken; kāryeṣu = in his actions?; kachchit kurute = I hope he is doing; puruṣakāryāṇi = acts which require human effort.

"Is Rama the prince miserable and confused? Is he even mistaken in his actions? I hope he is doing all acts which require human effort."

Sloka 17

dvividham trividha upāyam upāyam api sevate |
vijigīṣuḥ suhṛt kaccin mitreṣu ca param tapaḥ || 5-36-17

17. paramtapaḥ = is Rama the annihilator of enemies; apisevate = resorting to; dvividham upāyam = the two strategies; viz.; conciliatory and partaking approaches; trividhopāyamapi = and even the three strategies viz. partaking sowing dissention and open assault; suhṛit kachchit = is he kind-hearted; mitreṣu = to friends; vijagīṣuḥ = with a wish to conquer the enemies?

"Is Rama the annihilator of enemies as per necessity resorting to the two strategies viz., conciliatory and partaking approaches and even the three strategies viz., partaking, sowing dissension and open assault? Is he kind-hearted towards friends, with a wish to conquer the enemies?"

Sloka 18

kaccin mitrāṇi labhate mitraiḥ ca api abhigamyate |
kaccit kalyāṇa mitraḥ ca mitraiḥ ca api puraḥ kṛtaḥ || 5-36-18

18. kachchit labhate = Is he getting; mitrāṇi = friends?; api abhigamyate = is he approached; mitraiḥ = by friends?; kalyāna mitrashcha kachchit = I hope he is having good friends; api puraskṛitaḥ = and even honoured; mitraishcha = by his friends?

"Is he getting friends? ḥas he been approached for friendship by others? I hope he is having good friends and even so honoured by them."

Sloka 19

kaccit āśāsti devānām prasādam pārthiva ātmajaḥ |
kaccit puruṣa kāram ca daivam ca pratipadyate || 5-36-19

19. pārthivātmajaḥ = is Rama the prince; āshāsti kachchit = wishing for; prasādam = the grace; devānām = of gods?; kachchit pratipadyate = Is he (equally) aware of; puruṣakāramcha = human endeavour; adaivamcha = and the fate?

"Is Rama the prince wishing the grace of the gods? Is he equally aware of the role of human endeavour and the role of fate?"

Sloka 20

kaccin na vigata sneho vivāsān mayi rāghavaḥ |
kaccin mām vyasanāt asmān mokṣayiṣyati vānaraḥ || 5-36-20

20. vānara = O ḥanuma!; rāghavaḥ = Is Rama; na kachchit = not hopefully; vigata snehaḥ = losing affection; mayi = in me; pravāsāt = because of my staying away from home?; mokṣayiṣyati kachchit = will he release; mām = me; asmāt vyasanāt = from this evil plight?.

"O ḥanuma! Is Rama not losing affection in me because of my staying away from home? Will he release me from this evil predicament?"

Sloka 21

sukhānām ucito nityam asukhānām anūcitaḥ |
duhkham uttaram āsādya kaccit rāmo na sīdati || 5-36-21

21. rāghavaḥ = Rama; uchitaḥ = who is habituated; nityam = always; sukhānām = to pleasures; anauchitaḥ = and not habituated; asukhānām = to pains; na sīdati kachchit = not hopefully sinking into despondency; āsādya = by obtaining; uttaram = a great; duḥkham = pain?

"Is Rama, who is habituated always to pleasures and not habituated to pains, not sinking into despondency by now getting this great pain?"

Sloka 22

kausalyāyāḥ tathā kaccit sumitrāyāḥ tathaiva ca |
abhīkṣṇam śrūyate kaccit kuśalam bharatasya ca || 5-36-22

22. abhīkṣṇam shūyate kachchit = are you constantly hearing; kushalam = the news of safety; kausalyāḥ = of Kausalya; tathā = and; sumitrāyāḥ = of ṣumitra; tathaiva cha = and; bharatasya = of Bharata?

"Are you constantly hearing the news of safety of Kausalya, ṣumitra and Bharata?"

Sloka 23

man nimittena māna arhaḥ kaccit śokena rāghavaḥ |
kaccin na anya manā rāmaḥ kaccin mām tārayiṣyati || 5-36-23

23. rāghavaḥ = is Rama; mānārhaḥ = worthy of honour; kachchit shokena = suffering from pain; mannimitteva = just because of me; rāmaḥ na kachchit = is not Rama; anyamanāḥ = having his mind fixed on something else; kachchit tārayiṣyati = and will he liberate; mām = me?.

"Is Rama worthy of honour, suffering from pain just because of me? Will he liberate me from my sorrow, without fixing his mind on something else?"

Sloka 24

kaccit akṣāuhiṇīm bhīmām bharato bhrātṛ vatsalaḥ |
dhvajinīm mantribhiḥ guptām preṣayiṣyati mat kṛte || 5-36-24

24. bharataḥ bhrātṛivatsalaḥ kachchit preṣayiṣyati = I hope Bharata having a brotherly affection will send; matkṛite = for my sake; dhvajinīm = an army furnished with banners; guptām = guarded; mantribhiḥ = by the ministers; akṣauhiṇīm = consisting of ten anikinis or 21870 elephants; 21870 chariots; 65610 horses and 109350 foot-soldiers; bhīmam = and terrific.

"I hope Bharata, having a brotherly affection, will send for my sake, an army furnished with banners, guarded by the ministers, consisting of ten anikinis (21870 elephants, 21870 chariots, 65610 horses and 109350 foot soldiers) which were terrific in appearance."

Sloka 25

vānara adhipatiḥ śrīmān sugrīvaḥ kaccit eṣyati |
mat kṛte haribhiḥ vīraiḥ vṛto danta nakha āyudhaiḥ || 5-36-25

25. shrīmān = the glorious; sugrīvaḥ = ṣugreeva; vānarādhipatiḥ = the Lord of monkeys; eṣyati kachchit = will indeed come; matkṛite = for my sake; vṛitaḥ = along with; vīraiḥ = the valiant; haribhiḥ = monkeys; danta nakhāyudhaiḥ = with their tusks and nails as weapons.

"ṭhe glorious ṣugreeva the lord of monkeys will indeed come for my sake along with his valiant monkeys, who have their tusks and nails as their weapons."

Sloka 26

kaccic ca lakṣmaṇaḥ śūraḥ sumitrā ānanda vardhanaḥ |
astravit śara jālena rākṣasān vidhamiṣyati || 5-36-26

26. lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; shūraḥ = the strong man; sumitrānandavardhanaḥ = who augments the joy of ṣumitra; astravit = and who is an expert in weaponry; kachchit vidhamiṣyati = will indeed destroy; rākṣasān = the demons; sharajālena = by the multitude of his arrows.

"Lakshmana, the strong man, who augments the joy of ṣumitra and who is an expert in weaponry, will indeed destroy the demons by the multitude of his arrows."

Sloka 27

raudreṇa kaccit astreṇa rāmeṇa nihatam raṇe |
drakṣyāmi alpena kālena rāvaṇam sasuhṛj janam || 5-36-27

27. alpena kālena = within a short time; drakṣyāmi kachchit = I hope to see; rāvaṇam = Ravana; nihatam = killed; sasuhṛijjanam = along with his companions; raṇe = in the battle-field; raudreṇa; astreṇa = by terrific weapons; jvalatā = burning brightly.

"Within a short time, I hope see Ravana killed along with his companions in the battle-field by terrific weapons burning brightly."

Sloka 28

kaccin na tadt hema samāna varṇam |
tasya ānanam padma samāna gandhi |
mayā vinā śuṣyati śoka dīnam |
jala kṣaye padmam iva ātapena || 5-36-28

28. tat = that; ānanam = face; tasya = of Rama; hema samāna varṇam = with the complexion of gold; padma samānagandhi = and the fragrance of a lotus; shoka dīnam = may be looking miserable grief; mayā vinā = as bereft of me; jalakṣaye = and dehydrated; na shuṣyati kechchit = I hope it is not parched; ātapena = by sun; padmamiva = like a lotus.

"ṭhat face of Rama with its golden and with the fragrance of a lotus may be looking miserable as bereft of me and getting dehydrated too. I hope it is not parched by the sun, like a louts."

Sloka 29

dharma apadeśāt tyajataḥ ca rājyām |
mām ca api araṇyam nayataḥ padātim |
na āsīt vyathā yasya na bhīḥ na śokaḥ |
kaccit sa dhairyam hṛdaye karoti || 5-36-29

29. yasya = which Rama; tyajataḥ = left; rājyam = the kingdom; dharmāpadeshāt = as an indication of his righteousness; yasya = to whom; nāsīt vyathā = there was neither disturbance; na bhīḥ = nor fear; na shokaḥ = nor sorrow; mām chāpi nayataḥ = even while taking me; araṇyam = to the forest; padātim = by foot; saḥ = such Rama; karoti kachchit = is hopefully maintaining; dhairyam = boldness; hṛidaye = in is heart.

"Which Rama left the kingdom as an indication of his righteousness and to whom there was neither disturbance nor fear nor sorrow even while taking me to the forest by foot, I hope that the same Rama is maintaining boldness in his heart."

Sloka 30

na ca asya mātā na pitā na ca anyaḥ na |
snehāt viśiṣṭo asti mayā samo vā |
tāvadt hi aham dūta jijīviṣeyam |
yāvat pravṛttim śṛṇuyām priyasya || 5-36-30

30. asya = to Rama; snehāt = in the matter of affection; na mātā = neither his mother; na pitā = nor his father; nāsti anyaḥ = nor any body else; samovā = is equal or; vishiṣṭaḥ = more; mayā = than me; dūta = O ḥanuma the messenger!; aham = I; jijīviṣeyam = wish to survive; yāvat tāvat tu = as long as; shruṇuyām = I hear; pravṛittim = the activity priyasya = of my beloved.

"ṭo Rama, in the matter of affection, neither his mother nor his father nor any body else is equal to or more than me. O Hanuma the messenger! I wish to survive only as long as I hear the activity of my beloved."

Sloka 31

iti iva devī vacanam mahāartham |
tam vānara indram madhura artham uktvā |
śrotum punaḥ tasya vaco abhirāmam |
rāma artha yuktam virarāma rāmā || 5-36-31

31. uktvā = uttering; itīva = thus; mahārtham = the greatly meaningful; madhurārtham = and sweetly worthwhile; vachanam = words; rāmā = ṣeetha; devī = the princess; virarāma = kept abstained from talking; shrotum = so as to hear; punaḥ = again; tasya = his; abhirāmam = delightful; vachaḥ = words; rāmārtha yuktam = containing matter pertaining to Rama.

Uttering thus those greatly meaningful and sweetly worthwhile words, ṣeetha the princess further abstained from talking so as to hear Hanuma's delightful words describing the matters pertaining to Rama.

Sloka 32

sītāyā vacanam śrutvā mārutiḥ bhīma vikramaḥ |
śirasi añjalim ādhāya vākyam uttaram abravīt || 5-36-32

32. shrutvā = hearing; vachanam = the word; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha; mārutiḥ = ḥanuma; bhīma vikramaḥ = of terrific prowess; ādhāya = keeping; aṇ^jalim = his joined palms; shirasi = on his head; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words; uttaram = in reply.

ḥearing ṣeetha's words, ḥanuma of terrific prowess, keeping his joined palms on his head, spoke the following words in reply.

Sloka 33

na tvām ihasthām jānīte rāmaḥ kamala locanaḥ |
tena tvāmihasthām jānīte rāmaḥ kamalalocane || 5-36-33

33. kamala lochane = O ṣeetha having lotus-eyes!; rāmaḥ = Rama; na jānīte = does not know; tvām = that you; ihasthām = are here; tena = for that reason; tvām nānayati = he did not take your back; āshu = soon; purandaraḥ iva = as Indra the destroyer of strong-holds; shachīm = did not take you back; āshu = soon; purandaraḥ iva = as Indra the destroyer of strong-holds; shachīm = did not take back ṣhachi her wife.

"O ṣeetha having lotus eyes! Rama does not know that you are here. For that reason, he did not take you back soon, as Indra the destroyer of strong holds did not take back Sachi, his wife."

Sloka 34

śrutvā eva tu vaco mahyam kṣipram eṣyati rāghavaḥ |
camūm prakarṣan mahatīm hari ṛṣka gaṇa samkulām || 5-36-34

34. shrutvaiva tu = soon after hearing; mama = my; vachaḥ = words; rāghavaḥ = Rama; eṣyati = will come; kṣipram = soon; prakarṣan = drawing forth; mahatīm = a large; chamūm = army; hari ṛikṣagaṇa samkulām = consisting of a multitude of monkeys and bears.

"ṣoon after hearing my words, Rama will come soon, drawing forth a large army, consisting of a multitude of monkeys and bears."

Sloka 35

viṣṭambhayitvā bāṇa oghaiḥ akṣobhyam varuṇa ālayam |
kariṣyati purīm lankām kākutsthaḥ śānta rākṣasām || 5-36-35

35. kākutthsaḥ = Rama; viṣṭambhayitvā = will paralyse; akṣobhyam = the imperturbable; varuṇālayam = ocean; bāṇaughaiḥ = with his flood of arrows; kariṣyati = and make; lan^kām purim = the city of Lanka; shānta rākṣasām = extinguished of demons.

"Rama will paralyze the imperturbable ocean with his flood of arrows and make the City of Lanka extinguished of demons."

Sloka 36

tatra yadi antarā mṛtyuḥ yadi devāḥ saha asurāḥ |
sthāsyanti pathi rāmasya sa tān api vadhiṣyati || 5-36-36

36. mṛityuḥ yadi = If the goddess of Death; sahāsurāḥ = along with demons; devāḥ = or celestials; sthāsyanti = stand firmly; antarā = in the middle; pathi = of the way; rāmasya = of Rama; tatra = at that time; saḥ = he; vadhiṣyati = will kill; tānapi = them also.

"If the goddess of Death along with demons or celestials stand firmly in the way of Rama at that time, he will kill them also."

Sloka 37

tava adarśanajena ārye śokena sa pariplutaḥ |
na śarma labhate rāmaḥ simha ardita iva dvipaḥ || 5-36-37

37. ārye = O noble lady!; pariplutaḥ = being full of; shokena = grief; tava adarshanajena = caused by your non-appearance; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; na labhate = is not obtaining; sharma = happiness; dvipaḥ iva = like an elephant; simhārditaḥ = tormented by a lion.

"O noble lady! Being full of grief caused by your non-appearance, that Rama is not obtaining happiness, like an elephant tornmented by a lion."

Sloka 38 Verse Locator

dardareṇa ca te devi śape mūla phalena ca |
malayena ca vindhyena meruṇā mandareṇa ca || 5-36-38
yathā sunayanaṃ valgu bimbauṣṭhaṃ cārukuṇḍalam |
mukhaṃ drakṣyasi rāmasya pūrṇacandramivoditam || 5-36-39

38; 39. devi = O ṣeetha the princess!; shape = I swear and tell; te = you; malayenacha = by ṃalya ṃountain; vindhyena = by Vindhya mountain; meruṇā = by ṃeru mountain; dardureṇa = by Dardura ṃountain; mūlaphalena = by our food of roots and fruits; yathā = that; drakṣyasi = you will see; rāmasya = Rama's; mukham = face; sunayananam = having good eyes; valgu = handsome; bimboṣṭam = having lips with the colour of a bright-red gourd (fruit of ṃomordica monodelpha); chāru darshanam = with a charming look; uditam pūrṇachandramiva = like a rising full moon.

"O ṣeetha the princess! I swear and tell you by the mountains ṃalaya, Vindhya, ṃeru and Dardura as well as by our staple food of roots and fruits, that you will soon see Rama's face, having good eyes, handsome, having lips with the colour of a bright-red gourd (fruit of ṃomordica monodelpha) and with its charming look like a rising full moon."

Sloka 40

kṣipraṃ drakṣyasi vaidehi rāmaṃ prasravaṇe girau |
śatakratumivāsīnaṃ nākapṛṣṭhasya mūrdhani || 5-36-40

40. vaidehi = O ṣeetha!; drakṣyasi = you will see; kṣipram = soon; rāmam = Rama; prasravaṇe girau = on Prasravana ṃountain; shatakratum iva = like Indra the Lord of celestials; āsīnam = seated; mūrdhani = on the back; nāgarājasya = of Airavatha; the chief of elephants.

"O ṣeetha! You will see soon Rama on the Prasravana mountain, like Indra the Lord of celestials seated on the back of Airavata, the chief of elephants."

Sloka 41

na māṃsaṃ rāghavo bhuṅkte na cāpi madhusevate |
vanyaṃ suvihitaṃ nityaṃ bhaktamaśnāti pañcamam || 5-36-41

41. rāghavaḥ = Rama; na bhuṇkte = is not eating; māmsam = meat; na sevate = not indulging in; madhuchāpi = even spirituous liquor; nityam = everyday; paṇ^chamam = in the evening; ashnāti = he is eating; bhaktam = food; vanyam = existing in the forest; suvihitam = well-arranged (for him).

"Rama is not eating meat, nor indulging even in spirituous liquor. Everyday, in the evening, he is eating the food existing in the forest, well arranged for him."

Sloka 42

na eva daṃśān na maśakān na kīṭān na sarī sṛpān |
rāghavo apanayet gatrāt tvat gatena antaḥ ātmanā || 5-36-42

42. antarātmanā = with his mind; tvadgatena = directed towards you; rāghavaḥ = Rama; naiva apanayet = is not driving away; damshān = forest-flies; gātrāt = from his body; na mashakān = nor mosquitoes; na kīṭān = nor insects; na sarīsṛipān = nor reptiles.

"With his mind wholly devoted to you, Rama is not even driving away forest-flies from his body, nor mosquitoes nor insects nor reptiles from his body."

Sloka 43

nityam dhyāna paro rāmo nityam śoka parāyaṇaḥ |
na anyac cintayate kiṃcit sa tu kāma vaśam gataḥ || 5-36-43

43. rāmaḥ = Rama; nityam = is always; dhyānaparaḥ = thoughtful; nityam = and is always; shokaparāyaṇaḥ = engaged in sorrow; saḥ = ḥe; kāma vashāngataḥ = is enamoured with the god of love; nachintayati anyat kimchit = and thinks of nothing else.

"Rama always cogitates on something or the other. ḥe is forever engaged in sorrow. ḥe is enamoured with the god of love and thinks of nothing else."

Sloka 44

anidraḥ satatam rāmaḥ supto api ca nara uttamaḥ |
sīteti madhurām vāṇīm vyāharan pratibudhyate || 5-36-44

44. rāmaḥ = Rama; satatam = always; anidraḥ = does not have sleep; supto.api narottamaḥ = even if that best of men sleeps; prati budhyate = he keeps awake; vyāharan = uttering; madhurām = the sweet; vāṇīm = words; sīteti = "lo! Behold! ṣeetha!"

"Rama mostly does not have sleep. Even if that best of men sleeps, he keeps awake uttering sweet words like "lo! Behold! Seetha!"

Sloka 45

dṛṣṭvā phalam vā puṣpam vā yac ca anyat strī mano haram |
bahuśo hā priyeti evam śvasan tvām abhibhāṣate || 5-36-45

45. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; phalamvā = a fruit; puṣpam vā = or a flower; yadvā = or; sumanoharam anyat = any other beautiful object; bhibhāṣate = he recollects and says; tvām = about you; ha priyetyevam = "O Beloved!" bahushaḥ = many times; shvasan = and breathes a sigh.

"When he happens to see a fruit or a flower or any other beautiful object, he recollects of you and says 'O beloved!' many times and breathes a sigh."

Sloka 46

sa devi nityam paritapyamānaḥ |
tvām eva sīteti abhibhāṣamāṇaḥ |
dhṛta vrato rāja suto mahātmā |
tava eva lābhāya kṛta prayatnaḥ || 5-36-46

46. devi = O ṣeetha the princess!; saḥ = Rama that; mahātmā rājasutaḥ = magnanimous prince; nityam = is always; paritapyamānaḥ = lamenting; abhibhāṣamāṇaḥ = speaking; tvāmeva = about you alone; sīteti = thus recollecting ṣeetha; ṣeetha!; dhṛitavrataḥ = being firm in his vow; kṛita prayatnaḥ = he makes effort; tavalābhāyaiva = to find you alone.

"O ṣeetha the princess! Rama the magnanimous prince is always lamenting, speaking just about you, recollecting "Seetha, Seetha!". Being firm in his vow, he makes effort to find you only."

Sloka 47

sā rāma samkīrtana vīta śokā |
rāmasya śokena samāna śokā |
śaran mukhena ambuda śeṣa |
niśā iva vaideha sutā babhūva || 5-36-47

47. sā = that; vaidehasutā = ṣeetha; samāna shokā = who has the same amount of grief; rāmasya shokena = as that of Rama; babhūva rāma samkīrtana vīta shokā = became worn out of grief on hearing the adoration of Rama; nisheva = like a night; sāmbuda sheṣa chandrā = with moon obscured by the remnant clouds; sharanmukhe = at the beginning of autumn.

"ṭhat ṣeetha, who has the same amount of grief as that of Rama, became worn out of her grief upon hearing the adoration of Rama, like a night with the moon obscured by the remnant clouds at the commencement of autumn."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ṣaṭtriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 36th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
