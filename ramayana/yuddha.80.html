Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 80
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana instructs Indrajit to proceed to the battle field. Before proceeding t the battle, Indrajit performs ceremorial oblations into a sacred fire and obtains a capacity of going out of sight while fighting. He proceeds to the battle-field and releases a flood of arrows towards Rama and Lakshmana, while himself remaining invisible in the sky. With a network of arrows, Indrajit creates a darkness in the sky and showers a multitude of steel arrows towards Rama and Lakshmana. Sharp golden shafts arrows are then released by Rama and Lakshmana. Struck by the arrows discharged by Indrajit, monkeys in hundreds fall down dead. Then, Rama and Lakshmana reflect on the various ways and means to destroy Indrajit.

Sloka 1

makarākṣaṃhataṃ śrutvā rāvaṇaḥ samitiṃjayaḥ |
roṣeṇa mahatāviṣṭo dantān kaṭakaṭāyya ca || 6-80-1
kopitaśca tadā tatra kiṃ kāryamiti cintayan |
ādideśātha samkruddho raṇāyendrajitaṃ sutam || 6-80-2

1; 2. shrutvā = on hearing; makarākṣham = ṃakaraksha; hatam = having been killed; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; samitiṇ^jayaḥ = who had ever seen victorious in battle; kaṭakaṭāyya cha = grinding his teeth; mahatā roṣheṇa = in excessive rage; kupitashcha = and anger; chintayan iti = reflected on; kim = what; kāryam = to do; tadā = then; tatra = there; atha = and thereupon; samkruddhaḥ = instructed; sutam = his son; indrajitam = Indrajit; raṇāya = for the battle.

On hearing ṃakaraksha having been killed, Ravana who had been ever victorious in battle, grinding his teeth in rage, reflected on what to do then and there, and as greatly furious as he was, instructed Indrajit, his son to proceed to the battle-field.

Sloka 3

jahi vīra mahāvīryau bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |
adṛśyo dṛśyamāno vā sarvathā tvaṃ balādhikaḥ || 6-80-3

3. vīra = O hero!; adṛishyaḥ = either being invisible; dṛishyamānevā = or visible; (yourself); jahi = kill; rāmalakṣhmaṇau = Rama and Lakshmana; bhrātarau = the brothers; mahāvīryau = of great prowes; tvam = you; balādhikaḥ = are superior in strength; sarvathā = by all means.

"O hero! Either remaining yourself invisible or visible, kill Rama and Lakshmana, the brothers of great prowess. You are superior in strength by all means."

Sloka 4

tvamapratimakarmāṇamindraṃ jayasi saṃyuge |
kiṃ punarmānuṣau dṛṣṭvā na vadhiṣyasi saṃyuge || 6-80-4

4. tvam = you; jayasi = are conquering; indram = Indra; apratimakarmāṇam = of incomparable deeds; samyuge = in battle; kim punaḥ na vadhiṣhyasi = can you not kill; dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing (them); mānuṣhau = (those) men; samyuge = in the battle field?

"You have conquered Indra, of incomparable deeds, in battle. Can you not kill those two men, on seeing them in the battle-field?"

Sloka 5

tathokto rākṣasendreṇa pratigṛhya piturvacaḥ |
yajñabhūmau sa vidhivat pāvakaṃ juhavendrajit || 6-80-5

5. tathā = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rākṣhasendreṇa = by Ravana; saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; pratigṛihya = bowing to the command; pituḥ = of his father; juhava = poured oblations; pāvākam = into the sacred fire; vidhivat = with due ceremony; yajJṇa bhūmau = on the sacrificial ground.

Hearing the words of Ravana, that Indrajit, bowing to the command of his father, poured oblations into the sacred fire, with due ceremony, on the sacrificial ground.

Sloka 6

juhvataścāpi tatrāgniṃ raktoṣṇīṣadharāḥ striyaḥ |
ājagmustatra sambhrāntā rākṣasyo yatra rāvaṇiḥ || 6-80-6

6. juhvataḥ cha api = even as he was pouring oblations into the sacred fire; tatra = there; rākṣhasyaḥ striyaḥ = the female-demons; raktoṣhṇīṣhadharāḥ = carrying red turbans (for the use of priests); sambhrāntāḥ = hastily; ājagmuḥ = arrived; tatra = there; yatra = where; rāvaṇiḥ = Indrajit was there.

Even as he was pouring oblations into the sacred fire there, the female-demons carrying red turbans (for the use of priests) hastily arrived at the spot where Indrajit was there.

Sloka 7

śastrāṇi śarapatrāṇi samidho|a atha vibhītakāḥ |
lohitāni ca vāsāṃsi sruvaṃ kārṣṇāyasaṃ tathā || 6-80-7

7. shastrāṇi = weapons (such as a lance); shara patrāṇ = served as blades of ṣhara grass (for being spread around the sacrificial fire); vibhītakāḥ = chips of wood of Vibhitaka tree; samidhaḥ = served as wooden sticks to feed the sacrificial fire; atha = and; lohitāni vāsāmsi = red robes; tathā = and; kārṣhṇāyasam = iron ladles; sruvam = (were used) for cleaning and pouring clarified melted butter into the sacrificial fire.

Weapons (such as a lance) served as blades of Shara grass (for being spread around the sacrificial fire). chips of wood of Vibhitaka tree served as wooden sticks to feed the sacrificial fire as also red robes and iron ladles were used for cleaning and pouring clarified melted butter into the sacrificial fire.

Sloka 8

sarvato|agniṃ samāstīrya śarapatraiḥ satomaraiḥ |
cāgasya sarvakṛṣṇasya galaṃ jagrāha jīvataḥ || 6-80-8

8. samāstīrya = having spread on the ground; agniḥ sarvataḥ = around all the sides of the sacrificial fire; sharapatraiḥ = with other weapons; satomaraiḥ = along with lances; jagrāha = the demon seized hold; galam = of the neck; jīvitaḥ chhāgasya = of a live goat; sarva kṛiṣhṇasya = entirely black (and consigned it to the fire).

Having spread on the ground around all the sides of the sacrificial fire with other weapons along with lances, the demon seized hold of the neck of a live goat, entirely black and consigned it to the fire.

Sloka 9

sakṛddhomasamiddhasya vidhūmasya mahārciṣaḥ |
babhūvustāni liṅgāni vijayaṃ darśayanti ca || 6-80-9

9. tāni = such; liṇ^gāni = omens; vijayam = as betokened victory; babhūvuḥ darshayanti = appeared; vidhūmasya = in the smokeless; mahārchiṣhaḥ = fire which burst into mighty flames; homa samiddhasya = when it was fully aroused; sakṛit = by the single offering.

Such omens as betokened victory appeared in the smokeless fire which burst into mighty flames, when it was fully aroused by that single offering.

Sloka 10

pradakṣiṇāvartaśikhastaptahāṭakasannibhaḥ |
havistatpratijagrāha pāvakaḥ svayamutthitaḥ || 6-80-10

10. utthitaḥ = becoming visible; svayam = in person; pāvakaḥ = the god of fire; pradakṣhiṇāvarta shikhaḥ = who was shooting out flames towards the right; taptahāṭaka sannibhaḥ = and who shone brightly like refined gold; pratijagrāha = received; tat = that; haviḥ = offering.

Becoming visible in person, the god of fire, who was shooting out flames towards the right and who shone brightly like refined gold, received that offering.

Sloka 11

hutvāgnim tarpayitvātha devadānavarākṣasān |
āruroha rathaśreṣṭhamantardhānagaraṃ śubham || 6-80-11

11. hutvā = offering oblations; agnim = in fire; atha = and thereafter; tarpayitvā = gratifying; devadānava rākṣhasān = the gods; devils and demons; āruroha = (he) ascended; shreṣhṭham = and excellent; shubham = and splendid; ratham = chariot; antardhāna gatam = capable of disappearing from sight.

Offering oblations in fire and gratifying the gods, the devils and demons, Indrajit ascended an excellent and splendid chariot, capable of disappearing from sight.

Sloka 12

sa vājibhiścaturbhistu bāṇaistu niśitairyutaḥ |
āropitamahācāpaḥ śuśubhe syandanottamaḥ || 6-80-12

12. chaturbhiḥ yutaḥ = drawn by four; vājibhiḥ = horses; nishitaiḥ = sharp; bāṇaiḥ = arrows; āropita mahā chāpaḥ = and a mighty bow placed on it; saḥ = that; syandanottamaḥ = excellent chariot; shushubhe = looked beautiful.

Drawn by four horses, provided with sharp arrows and a mighty bow placed on it, that excellent chariot looked beautiful.

Sloka 13

jājvalyamāno vapuṣā tapanīyaparicchadaḥ |
mṛgaiścandrārdhacandraiśca sa rathaḥ samalaṃkṛtaḥ || 6-80-13

13. tapanīya parichchhadaḥ = adorned with gold; saḥ = that; rathaḥ = chariot; jājvalya mānaḥ = glittered; vapuṣhā = on account of its body; samalamkṛitaḥ = and was decorated; mṛigaiḥ = with carved images of antelopes; chandra ardhachandraishcha = full moons and crescents.

Adorned with gold, that chariot glittered on account of its body and was decorated with carved images of antelopes, full moons and crescents.

Sloka 14

jāmbūnadamahākamburdīptapāvakasannibhaḥ |
babhūvendrajitaḥ keturvaidūryasamalaṃkṛtaḥ || 6-80-14

14. ketḥ = the flag-post; indrajitaḥ = of Indrajit; babhūva dīpta pāvaka sannibhaḥ = shone like a flaming fire; jambūnada mahākambuḥ = provided as it was with large rings of gold; vaiDūrya samalamkṛitaḥ = and decorated with cats's eye gems.

The flag-post of Indrajit shone like a flaming fire, provided, as it was, with large rings of gold and decorated with cat's eye gems.

Sloka 15

tena cādityakalpena brahmastreṇa ca pālitaḥ |
sa babhūva durādharṣo rāvaṇiḥ sumahābalaḥ || 6-80-15

15. pālitaḥ = protected; tena brahmāstreṇa = by the missile presided over by Brahma; āditya kalpena = which was as efflugent as the sun; saḥ rāvaṇiḥ = that Indrajit; sumahābalaḥ = endowed with exceptional strength; bahūva = became; durādharṣhaḥ = difficult to be attcked.

Protected by the missile presided over by Brahma, which was as efflugent as the sun, that Indrajit endowed with exceptional strength, became difficult to be attacked.

Sloka 16

so|abhiniryāya nagarādindrajitsamitiñjayaḥ |
hutvāgniṃ rākṣasairmanrairantardhānagato|abravīt || 6-80-16

16. saḥ indrajit = that Indrajit; samitiṇ^jayaḥ = who was ever victorious in war; abhiniryāya = coming forth; nagarāt = from the city; antardhānagataḥ = and having acquired the capacity of vanishing from the sight; hutvā = by pouring oblations; agnim = into the sacred fire; mantraiḥ = with the utterance of spells; rākṣhasaiḥ = sacred to demons; abravīt = spoke (as follows):

Coming forth from the city and having acquired the capacity of vanishing from the sight, by offering oblations into the sacred fire, with the uttereance of spells sacred to demons, that indrajit, who was ever victorious in war, spoke as follows:

Sloka 17

adya hatvā raṇe yau tau mithyāpravrajitau vane |
jayaṃ pitre pradāsyāmi rāvaṇāya raṇārjitam || 6-80-17

17. hatvā = by killing; tau = both the princes; yau = who; mithyāpravrajtau vane = left home to become religious mendicants; roaming in the forest in vain; pradāsyāmi = I will present a gift; jayam = of victory; raṇārjitam = secured in the battle; pitre rāvaṇāya = to my father; Ravana; adya = today.

"By killing both the princes who left home to become religious mendicants in the forest in vain, I will present a gift of victory secured in the battle, to my father, Ravana today."

Sloka 18

adya nirvānarāmurvīm hatvā rāmaṃ ca lakṣmaṇam |
kariṣye paramāṃ prītimityuktvāntaradhīyata || 6-80-18

18. urvīm nirvānarām = by making the earth bereft of monkeys; adya = today; hatvā = and by killing; rāmam = Rama; lakṣhmaṇam cha = and Lakshmana; kariṣhye = I will proudce; paramām prītim = a great pleasure; iti = thus; uktvā = speaking; antaradhīyata = he vanished from the sight.

"By making the earth bereft of monkeys today and by killing Rama and Lakshmana, I will create a great spleasure". Thus speaking, Indrajit vanished from thes sight.

Sloka 19

āpapātātha samkruddho daśagrīveṇa coditaḥ |
tīkṣaṇakārmukanārācaistīkṣaṇastvindraripū raṇe || 6-80-19

19. choditaḥ = impelled; dashagrīveṇa = by Ravana; tīkṣhṇaḥ = the fiery; indraripuḥ = Indrajit; raṇe = in battle; tīkṣhṇa kārmuka nārāchaiḥ = with barbarous bow and steel arrows; papāta = swiftly came; samkruddhaḥ = enraged; atha = thereafter.

Impelled, as he was, by Ravana, the fiery Indrajit, with his barbarons bow and steel arrows, swiftly came to the battle-field enraged.

Sloka 20

sa dadarśa mahāvīryau nāgau triśirasāviva |
sṛjantāviṣujālāni vīrau vānaramadhyagau || 6-80-20

20. saḥ = that Indrajit; dadarsha = saw; vīrau = those two heroes; mahāvīryau = having abundant prowess; nāgau iva = like serpents; trishirasau = with three heads; sṛijantau = who were discharging; iṣhujālāni = a net-work of arrows; vānara madhyagau = and standing amidst the monkeys.

That Indrajit saw the two heroes, showering a multitude of arrows. Both the princes, having mighty prowess, looked like three- hooded serpents in the middle of the monkeys.

Sloka 21

imau tāviti saṃcintya sajyaṃ kṛtvā ca kārmukam |
santatāneṣudhārābhiḥ parjanya iva vṛṣṭimān || 6-80-21

Concluding that both of them were the two princes, Rama and Lakshmana and stringing his bow, he covered them with a flood of arrows, as the rainy clouds do.

Sloka 22

sa tu vaihāyasaṃ prāpya saratho rāmalakṣmaṇau |

That Indrajit with his chariot, reaching the sky and remaining invisible, struck Rama and Lakshmana with his sharp arrows.

Sloka 23

When enveloped by a stream of arrows Rama and Lakshamana, fitted arrows to their bows and revealed divine missiles.

Sloka 24

Though covering the sky with a net-work of arrows, the two mighty princes could not touch Indrajit with their arrows charged with mystic missiles and which were efflugent like the sun.

Sloka 25

sa hi dhūmāndhakāraṃ ca cakre pracchādayannabhaḥ |

Covering the sky, the illustrious Indrajit created a dark-like smoke. He made the quarters also invisible, by enclosing them with mist-liek darkness.

Sloka 26

śuśruve caratastasya na ca rūpaṃ prakāśate || 6-80-26

26. tasya = charitaḥ = while Indrajit was moving about; jyātalanirghoṣhaḥ = the sound produced by the impact of his palm on the bow-string; naiva shushruve = was not heard; na cha = nor; nemikhura svanaḥ cha = the sound of hs wheels; or the clattering of hoofs (of his horses); rūpam cha; na prakāshate = nor did his form come to view.

While Indrajit was moving about, neither the sound produced by the impact of his palm on the bow string was heard, nor the sound of his wheels or the clattering of hoofs of his horses could be heard, nor did his form come tot he view.

Sloka 27

ghanāndhakāre timire śaravarṣamivādbhutam |

In that thick darkness, that long-armed Indrajit showered hails of his steel arrows, like wonderful shower of rocks.

Sloka 28

sa rāmaṃ sūryasaṅkāśaiḥ śarairdattavaro bhṛśam |
vivyādha samare kruddhaḥ sarvagātreṣu rāvaṇiḥ || 6-80-28

That enraged Indrajit in battle, abundantly pierced Rama in all his limbs with his arrows, which were earlier granted as boons to him and which were effluent like the sun.

Sloka 29

Both Rama and Lakshmana, the foremost of men, who were being struck with steel arrows, like two mountains being hit by torrents, released sharp golden-shafted arrows.

Sloka 30

Those arrows, adorned with plumes of heron, reaching Indrajit in the sky and piercing him, fell to the ground, soaked in blood.

Sloka 31

31. dīpyamānau = shing; atimātram = beyond measure; sharaugheṇa = with a flood of arrows; narottamau = the two excellent men; vichakartatuḥ = began to chop off; tān iṣhūn = those arrows; patataḥ = which were falling; anekaiḥ = with many arrows; bhallaiḥ = called by the name of Bhallas.

Shining beyond measure with a flood of arrows, the two excellent men began to chop off those arrows which were falling on them with many arrows called by the name of Bhallas.

Sloka 32

tatastato dāśarathī sasṛjāte.astramuttamam || 6-80-32

32. yataḥ = from which side; tau = they; dadṛishāte = saw; shitān = the sharp; sharān = arrows; nipatitān = fallen; tataḥ = towards that side; tau dāsharathī = both Rama and Lakshmana; sasṛijāte = released; uttamam astram = their excelletnt missiles.

Both Rama and Lakshmana for their part directed their excellent missiles in the direction from which they saw the aforesaid sharp arrows, fallen.

Sloka 33

Indrajit for his part, who was a superior chariot-warrior and who was swift in discharging missiles, rushed on towards all sides and pierced Rama and Lakshmana with his sharp arrows.

Sloka 34

Those valiant princes, Rama and Lakshmana pierced deeply with well-made goldne shafted arrows, appeared like two Kamshuka trees in flowering.

Sloka 35

No one could perceive his rapid movement, like the position of the sun when the sky is thickly overcast with clouds, nor his form nor his bows and arrows, nor any other thing about him whatsoever could be known.

Sloka 36

Struck down by him, the monkeys were killed. The monkeys dropped dead in hundreds on the earth's surface there.

Sloka 37

lakṣmaṇastu susaṅkruddho bhrātaraṃ vākyamabravīt |
brāhmamastraṃ prayokṣyāmi vadhārthaṃ sarvarakṣasām || 6-80-37

37. tataḥ = then; lakṣhmaṇastu = Lakshmana for his part; kruddhaḥ = was enraged; abravīt = and spoke; vākyam = the following words; bhrātaram = to his brother; prayokṣhyāmi = I will employ; brāhmam astram = the missile presided over by Brahama; vadhārtham = for the purpose of killing; sarva rākṣhasām = all the demons.

Then, Lakshmana for his part was enraged and told his brother that he would employ the missile presided over by Brahma for the purpose of killing all the demons.

Sloka 38

tamuvāca tato rāmo lakṣmaṇaṃ śubhalakṣaṇam |
naikasya heto rakṣāṃsi pṛthivyāṃ hantumarhasi || 6-80-38

38. tataḥ = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; tam lakṣhmaṇam = to that Lakshmana; shubhalakṣhaṇam = who was endowed with auspicious bodily marks; (as follows); na arhasi = you ought not; hantum = to kill; rakṣhāmsi = all the demons; pṛithivyām = on the earth; ekasya hetoḥ = for the sake of a single demons.

Then, Rama spoke to that Lakshmana, who was endowed with auspicious bodily marks as follows: "You ought not to kill all the demons on earth, merely for the sake of a single demon."

Sloka 39

ayudhyamānaṃ pracchannaṃ prāñjaliṃ śaraṇāgatam |
palāyantaṃ pramattaṃ vā na tvaṃ hantumihārhasi || 6-80-39

39. tvam na arhasi = you ought not; hantum = to kill; iha = here; ayudhyamānam = one who is not fighting; prachchhannam = or one who is hiding; prāṇ^jalim = or the one who is a refugee; palāyamānam = or who is fleeing; mattam vā = or who is intoxicated.

"You ought not to kill on this earth one who is not fighting or one who is hiding or one who seeks refuge with joined palms or is fleeing or is intoxicated."

Sloka 40

asyaiva tu vadhe yatnaṃ kariṣyāvo mahābala |
ādekṣyāvo mahāvegānastrānāśīviṣopamān || 6-80-40

40. mahābhuja = O long-armed Lakshmana; yatnam kariṣhyāvaḥ = we shall try; vadhe = to kill; tasyaiva = only Indrajit; ādekṣhyāvaḥ = we will employ; astrān = mystic missiles; mahāvegān = with great impetuosity; āshīviṣhopamām = and which are equal to venomous serpents.

"O long-armed Lakshmana! We shall try to kill Indrajit alone. We will employ mystic mssiles with great impetuosity and which are equal to venomous serpents."

Sloka 41

tamenaṃ māyinaṃ kṣudramantarhitarathaṃ balāt |
rākṣasaṃ nihaniṣyanti dṛṣṭvā vānarayūthapāḥ || 6-80-41

41. dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; enam = this; rākṣhasam = demon; tam as such; vānarayūthapāḥ = the leaders of monkey-troops; balāt = will forcibly nihaniṣhyati = kill; kṣhudram = this petty demon; māyinam = who is skilled in conjuring tricks; antarhita ratham = and whose chariot remains invisible.

"On seeing this demons, the leaders of mokey-troops will forcibly kill this petty demon, who is skilled in conjuring tricks and whose chariot remains invisible."

Sloka 42

yadyeṣa bhūmiṃ viśate divaṃ vā |
rasātalaṃ vāpi nabhastalaṃ vā |
evaṃ nigūḍho.api mamāstradagdhaḥ |
patiṣyate bhūmitale gatāsuḥ || 6-80-42

42. mama astradagdhaḥ = scorched by my mystic missiles; patiṣhyati gatāsuḥ = he will fall dead; bhūmitale = on the ground; eṣhaḥ vishate yadi = even if he enters; bhūmim = the earth; divam vā = or into heaven; rasātalam vā = or into the subterranean world; nabhasthalam vā = or penetrates the vault of heaven; nigūDho.api = and remains completely hidden; evam = in this manner.

"Scorched by my mystic missiles, he will fall dead on the ground, even if he enters the earth or into heaven or into the subterrranean world or penetrates the vault of heaven and remains completely hidden in this manner."

Sloka 43

ityevamuktvā vacanaṃ mahātmā |
raghupravīraḥ plavagarṣabhairvṛtaḥ |
vadhāya raudrasya nṛśaṃsakarmaṇas |
tadā mahātmā tvaritaṃ nirīkṣate || 6-80-43

43. uktvā = speaking; mahārtham = highly meaningful; vachanam = words; ityevam = in the aforesaid manner; mahātmā = the great-souled; raghupravīraḥ = Rama; vṛitaḥ = surrounded; plavagarṣhabhaiḥ = by the foremost of monkeys; tadā = then; nirikṣhate = started to reflect; tvaritam = on the speedy means; vadhāya = of killing; raudrasya = the furious; nṛishamsa karmaṇaḥ = Indrajit; the demon of cruel deeds.

Speaking highly meaningful words in the aforesaid manner, the great-souled Rama, surrounded by the foremost of monkeys, started to reflect on the speedy means of killing the furious Indrajit, the demons of cruel deeds.
