Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 70
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Kabandha's overlong arms are cut off when he wanted to devour Rama and Lakshmana as godsend dainty morsels. Then Kabandha wishes to know who these two are and when Lakshmana informs him of Rama, that demon feels elated for his accursed state is over at the hands of Rama.


Sloka 1

tau tu tatra sthitau dṛṣṭvā bhrātarau rāma lakṣmaṇau |
bāhu pāśa parikṣiptau kabandho vākyam abravīt || 3-70-1

1. bāhu pāsha pari kṣiptau = arms, [hangman's] halter, completely stowed by; tatra sthitau = therein [within the grip,] staying [enduring]; bhrātarau = at brothers; tau rāma lakṣmaṇau = them, at Rama, Lakshmana; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing [prying]; kabandhaḥ vākyam abravīt = Kabandha, sentence, said.

On prying at those two brothers, Rama and Lakshmana, who are completely stowed in the hangman's halter-like arms of the demon, and who are enduring within it, Kabandha said this sentence to them. [3-70-1]

Sloka 2

tiṣṭhataḥ kim nu mām dṛṣṭvā kṣudhā ārtam kṣatriya ṛṣabhau |
āhāra artham tu sandiṣṭau daivena gata cetasau || 3-70-2

2. kṣatriya ṛiṣabhau = oh, Kshatriya, best ones among; kṣudhā ārtam = by hunger, anguished [miserably famished]; mām dṛiṣṭvā = me, on seeing; kim nu tiṣṭhataḥ = what for, really, you stay [cool]; gata cetasau = gone, your lives [you are almost dead]; daivena āhāra artham tu sandiṣṭau = by god, food [my gobbets,] for purpose of, only, you are sent.

"Oh, excellent Kshatriya-s, on seeing me who am miserably famished, really what for you stay cool? You are the godsend gobs of mine, and hence now you are almost dead..." So said Kabandha to brothers. [3-70-2]

Sloka 3

tat śrutvā lakṣmaṇo vākyam prāpta kālam hitam tadā |
uvāca ārtim samāpanno vikrame kṛta niścayaḥ || 3-70-3

3. lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; tat shrutvā = that, one hearing; tadā ārtim sam āpannaḥ = then [who is by now,] anguish, possessed by; vikrame kṛita nishcayaḥ = in valour [to wreck revenge on demon,] making, determination; prāpta kālam = chanced, time [expedient]; hitam vākyam uvāca = advisable, words, said [to Rama.]

Lakshmana who is possessed by anguish, and who is determined to wreck revenge on that demon, said these expedient and advisable words to Rama on hearing that demon's word. [3-70-3]

Sloka 4

tvām ca mām ca purā tūrṇam ādatte rākṣasa adhamaḥ |
tasmāt asibhyām asya āśu bāhū cindāvahe gurū || 3-70-4

4. rākṣasa adhamaḥ = demon, scurrilous; tūrṇam = quickly; tvām ca = you, also; mām ca = me, also; purā = prior to; ādatte = can take in / hauled us in; tasmāt = therefore; asya = his; gurū bāhū = lengthy, arms; asibhyām = with two [of our] swords; āshu = swiftly; chindāvahe = we hack off.

"This scurrilous demon quickly hauled in you and me to his face level, and he will quickly take us in, therefore, we will have to swiftly hack off his lengthy arms at his shoulder level... [3-70-4]

Sloka 5

bhiṣaṇo ayam mahākāyo rākṣaso bhuja vikramaḥ |
lokam hi ati jitam kṛtvā hi avām hantum iha icchati || 3-70-5

5. bhiṣaṇaḥ = gruesome one; mahākāyaḥ = gargantuan bodied; bhuja vikramaḥ = by arms, triumphing one; ayam rākshasaḥ = such, a demon; lokam ati jitam kṛitvā = world [his province,] completely, conquered [devastating,] on making; iha avām hantum icchati hi = now, us, to finish off, wishes, indeed.

"Gruesome is this gargantuan bodied demon, and triumphing over just with his arms he has utterly devastated this province, and he now wishes to end us... [3-70-5]

Sloka 6

niśceṣṭānām vadho rājan kutstito jagatī pateḥ |
kratu madhya upanītānām paśūnām iva rāghava || 3-70-6

6. rājan rāghava = oh, king, Raghava; niḥ cheṣṭānām = without, gesticulations [motionless, those that are non-aggressive]; vadhaḥ = [their] killing; kratu madhya = Vedic-ritual, in the midst of; upa nītānām pashūnām iva = to nigh, led in, animal, like [tantamount to]; jagatī pateḥ kutstitaḥ = for world's, lords [kings,] despicable.

"Oh, king Raghava, killing the non-aggressive is tantamount to the killing of animals that are led into Vedic-ritual in its midst is despicable to the kings... [3-70-6]

The text varies with other mms. kratu madhye upa nītām will change to kratu madhye apa nītām in ṃaheshvara Tīrtha's version, then it translates as 'an animal led out of the ritual half way through the ritual...' meaning that 'the animal unbefitting for sacrifice...' And that is to say 1] an animal not prearranged and sanctified right from the beginning, but brought in the middle of ritual; 2] or, one that is brought for ritual which has to yield without being aggressive pratikāra hīna shakti; 3] or, paryagni kṛitā na araṇyām utsṛijyam a himsāyā 'unsanctified or wild animals brought from wilds are to be let off, unhurt...' 4] or, one that which is not at the sacrificial post - it is not to be sacrificed...' and Lakshmana says addressing Rama as 'oh, king, you have to let off those beasts, but not this beastly demon who is aggressive and contemplating a regicide...'

Sloka 7

etat saṃjalpitam śrutvā tayoḥ kruddhaḥ tu rākṣasaḥ |
vidārya āsyam tato raudram tau bhakṣayitum ārabhat || 3-70-7

7. kruddhaḥ rākshasaḥ tu = infuriated, demon, on his part; tataḥ = then; tayoḥ = their; sam jalpitam = together, talked [conversation]; etat shrutvā = all that, on hearing; raudram āsyam vidārya = ferocious, mouth, broke open - opening wide; tau bhakshayitum ārabhat = both, to wolf down, started to.

On hearing all of their conversation that demon is infuriated, and then widely opening his ferocious mouth he started to wolf down both of them. [3-70-7]

Sloka 8

tataḥ tau deśa kālajñau khaḍgābhyām eva rāghavau |
acchindatām susaṃhṛṣṭau bāhū tasya aṃsa deśataḥ || 3-70-8

8. tataḥ = then; su sam hṛiṣṭau = very, highly, gladdened; desha kālajñau = place, time knowers of [brothers with circumspection]; tau rāghavau = those, Raghava-s; khaDgābhyām eva = with two swords, only; tasya bāhū = his, arms; amsa deshataḥ = shoulder joint, from the place of; acchindatām = mutilated.

Then both the Raghava-s are very highly gladdened as the demon is hauling them towards his eye at shoulder level, and as they are the brothers with circumspection, they instantly mutilated his arms right at their shoulder joints only with two swords. [3-70-8]

This compound su sam hṛiṣṭau is as per the text of Govindaraja and ṃaheshvara Tīrtha states this as su sam vignau meaning 'very highly agitated...' agitated at the short time available to cut off his hands, before falling into his well-head like mouth. And this expression 'very highly gladdened' is for the righteous advice given by Lakshmana, circumspectly.

Sloka 9

dakṣiṇo dakṣiṇam bāhum asaktam asinā tataḥ |
ciccheda rāmo vegena savyam vīraḥ tu lakṣmaṇaḥ || 3-70-9

9. tataḥ = then; dakṣiṇaḥ rāmaḥ = dextrous, Rama; a saktam = not, restrained; vegena = with speed; dakṣiṇam bāhum = right, arm; asinā ciccheda = with sword, hacked off; vīraḥ lakṣmaṇaḥ tu = valiant one, Lakshmana, on his part; savyam [bāhum ciccheda] = left [arm, hewed down.]

Then that dextrous Rama with an unrestrained speed hacked off the right arm, and valiant Lakshmana on his part hewed down the left arm that speedily. [3-70-9]

Sloka 10

sa papāta mahābāhuḥ cinna bāhuḥ mahā svanaḥ |
kham ca gām ca diśaḥ caiva nādayan jalado yathā || 3-70-10

10. mahābāhuḥ = overlong-armed; saḥ = he, Kabandha; chinna bāhuḥ = with mangled, arms; mahā svanaḥ = with cacophonous, voice; jala daḥ yathā = rain, giver [black-thunderous-cloud,] as with; kham ca = sky, also; gām ca = earth, also; [dasha] dishaḥ caiva = [ten] points of horizons, also, thus; nādayan = reverberating; papāta = he, fell down.

He that overlong-armed Kabandha fell down with mangled arms, reverberated the sky, earth, and all of the ten points of horizon with a cacophonous voice, like a black-thunderous-cloud. [3-70-10]

Sloka 11

sa nikṛttau bhujau dṛṣṭvā śoṇita ogha pariplutaḥ |
dīnaḥ papraccha tau vīrau kau yuvām iti dānavaḥ || 3-70-11

11. saḥ dānavaḥ = he, that demon; nikṛittau bhujau = at his dissevered, shoulders; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing; shoṇita ogha pari plutaḥ = blood, gushes, with over, flowing; dīnaḥ = self-piteously; yuvām kau = you two, who; iti = thus; tau vīrau papraccha = those, two bold ones, has asked.

That demon on seeing at his dissevered shoulders on which the gushes of blood are overflowing, he self-piteously asked those two bold ones thus as, "who are you..." [3-70-11]

Sloka 12

iti tasya bruvāṇasya lakṣmaṇaḥ śubha lakṣaṇaḥ |
śaśaṃsa tasya kākutstham kabaṃdhasya mahābalaḥ || 3-70-12

12. tasya iti bruvāṇasya = his, this way, who is speaking [asking]; shubha lakṣaṇaḥ = one with prosperous, characteristics; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; mahābalaḥ [mahātmānaḥ] = great-mighty [or, great souled]; tasya kabandhasya = to him, to Kabandha; kākutstham shashamsa = about Kakutstha Rama, informed.

When that great mighty Kabandha is asking thus, Lakshmana who has prosperous characteristics has informed him about Rama of Kakutstha, an unremitting dynasty. [3-70-12]

Sloka 13

ayam ikṣvāku dāyādo rāmo nāma janaiḥ śrutaḥ |
tasya eva avarajam viddhi bhrātaram mām ca lakṣmaṇam || 3-70-13

13. ayam = he is; rāmaḥ nāma = Rama, by name; janaiḥ shrutaḥ = by people, heard [known]; ikṣvāku dāyādaḥ = Ikshvāku's, legatee; mām tasya avarajam bhrātaram = me, his, later born [younger,] as brother; lakṣmaṇam [nāma nāmataḥ] = as Lakshmana [named one by my name]; viddhi = you know.

"He is known to people by the name of Rama, the legatee of Ikshvaku dynasty, and you know me as his younger brother, Lakshmana, by my name... [3-70-13]

Sloka 14

mātrā pratihato rājye rāmaḥ pravājito vanam |
mayā saha carati eṣa bhāryayā ca mahat vanam || 3-70-14

14. eṣa = this one; rāmaḥ = Rama; mātrā = by mother; prati hataḥ = counter, vailed; rājye = from kingdom; vanam pra vājitaḥ = to forest, bluntly, sent to; mayā saha = me, along with; bhāryayā ca = with his wife, also; mahat vanam carati = great, forest, he moves about.

"Countervailed from kingdom by mother this Rama is bluntly sent to forests, and he is on the rove in great forests along with me ad his wife... [3-70-14]

Sloka 15

asya deva prabhāvasya vasato vijane vane |
rakṣasā apahṛtā bhāryā yām icchantau iha āgatau || 3-70-15

15. vi jane vane = without, people, in forest; vasataḥ = while living; deva prabhāvasya = god [like that of,] in his leverage; asya bhāryā rakṣasā apahṛitā = his, wife, by demon, abducted; yām icchantau iha āgatau = whom, wishing [to retrieve,] to here, [we] came.

"A demon stole his wife while he whose leverage is like that of god is living in the unpopulated forest... and wishing to retrieve her we came here... [3-70-15]

Sloka 16

tvam tu ko vā kim artham vā kabandha sadṛśo vane |
āsyena urasi dīptena bhagna jangho viceṣṭase || 3-70-16

16. tvam tu = you, but; kaḥ vā = who, or; kabandha sadṛishaḥ = trunk, alike; urasi dīptena āsyena = in chest, infernal, with mouth; bhagna janghaḥ = broken, calves [legs, legless]; vane = in forest; kim artham vā = for what, reason, or; vi ceṣṭase = sprawling.

"But who are you? Your legs are broken, and your mouth is infernal, yet it is on your chest, and alike a topless, rootless tree trunk you are sprawling in the forest, how so, or, for what reason?" Lakshmana asked the demon thus. [3-70-16]

Sloka 17

evam uktaḥ kabaṃdhaḥ tu lakṣmaṇena uttaram vacaḥ |
uvāca parama prītaḥ tat indra vacanam smaran || 3-70-17

17. lakṣmaṇena evam uktaḥ = by Lakshmana, that way, when spoken; kabandhaḥ tu = Kabandha, on his part; tat indra vacanam smaran = that, Indra's, words, on memorising; parama prītaḥ = highly, delighted; uttaram vacaḥ uvāca = in reply, words, spoke.

But Kabandha is highly delighted when Lakshmana spoke that way, as the words of Indra came to his memory, and he said these words in reply. [3-70-17]

Sloka 18

svāgatam vām naravyāghrau diṣṭyā paśyāmi vām aham |
diṣṭyā ca imau nikṛttau me yuvābhyām bāhu bandhanau || 3-70-18

18. naravyāghrau = oh, manly-tigers; vām svāgatam [su āgatam] = to you, well, come; aham vām diṣṭyā pashyāmi = I am, you, providentially, seeing you [instead, you are a revelation to me];; diṣṭyā = providentially; yuvābhyām = by you two; me imau = my, these; bāhu bandhanau = arms, shackles of [or, bondage]; nikṛittau = sheared.

"Oh, manly tigers, by my providence you are revealed to me... welcome to you... and providentially sheared are these shackles, called my two arms, by you... [3-70-18]

The arms, mouth-to-stomach, and another organ are the epitomes of mortals. A mortal does everything with arms and devours anything with mouth. martyā kartā bhoktā ca Now those shackles of mortality are severed and he is ready for immortality.

Sloka 19

virūpam yat ca me rūpam prāptam hi avinayāt yathā |
tat me śṛṇu naravyāghra tattvataḥ śaṃsataḥ tava || 3-70-19

19. naravyāghra = oh, manly tiger; me = my; vi rūpam = dis, figure; yat rūpam = mine, which, form - is there; that; yathā = as to how; a vinayāt = by dis, respect; prāptam = chanced on me; tava = to you; tattvataḥ shamsataḥ = actually, while being narrated; tat me shṛiṇu = that, from me, you listen.

"Oh, manly tiger, actually how this form of mine is disfigured, of course owing to my distrustful behaviour, that you may listen while I narrate it to you. [3-70-19]

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe saptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 70th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the first Epic poem of India
