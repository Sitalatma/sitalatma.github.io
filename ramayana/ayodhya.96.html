Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 96
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Rama hears a gigantic clamour of an army nearby and asks Lakshmana to find out what it is. Lakshmana ascends a tree and informs Rama that Bharata's army is approaching towards their hermitage. Lakshmana expresses his apprehension to Rama, saying that Bharata's army may be coming there in order to kill both of them. An enraged Lakshmana says that he will kill both Bharata and Kaikeyi together with the army.

Sloka 1

tāṃ tathā darśayitvā tu maithilīṃ girinimnagām |
niṣasāda giriprasthe sītāṃ māṃsena candayan || 2-96-1

1. tathā = thus; darshayitvā = having shown; girinimnagām = the mountaneous river ṃandakini; tām sītām = to that ṣeetha; maithilīm = the daughter of the king of ṃithila; niṣasāda = sat; giriprashthe = on the hill side; chhandayan = in order to gratify her appetite; māmsena = with flesh.

Having shown ṃandakini River in that manner to Seetha, the daughter of ṃithila, Rama set on the hill-side in order to gratify her appetite with a piece of flesh.

Sloka 2

idaṃ medhyamidaṃ svādu niṣṭaptamidamagninā |
evamāste sa dharmātmā sītayā saha rāghavaḥ || 2-96-2

2. saḥ rāghavaḥ = that Rama; dharmātmā = of righteousness; āste = stayed; sītayā sha = with ṣeetha; evam = thus speaking; idam = this meat; madhyam = is fresh; idam = this; niṣṭaptam = was roasted; agninā = in the fire.

Rama, whose mind was devoted to righteousness stayed there with Seetha, saying; "This meat is fresh, this is savoury and roasted in the fire."

Sloka 3

tathā tatra āsataḥ tasya bharatasya upayāyinaḥ |
sainya reṇuḥ ca śabdaḥ ca prādur āstām nabhaḥ spṛśau || 2-96-3

3. tasya = (While) that Rama; āsataḥ = was sitting; tatra = there; tathā = in that way; reṇushcha = the dust; shabdashcha = and clamour; bharatasya sainyam = Bharata's army; upayāyinaḥ = which was approaching; prādurāstām = ascended; nabhaspṛishau = touching the sky.

While Rama was thus sitting there the dust and clamour of Bharata's army, which was approaching, ascended into the sky.

Sloka 4

etasminn antare trastāḥ śabdena mahatā tataḥ |
arditā yūthapā mattāḥ sayūthā dudruvur diśaḥ || 2-96-4

4. etasmin antare = meanwhile; trastāḥ = frightened; mahatā shabdena = by the great clamour; yūthapāḥ = leaders of animal troops; mattāḥ = in rut; arditā = were tormented; dudruvuḥ = fled; dishaḥ = (in different) directions; tataḥ = from there.

ṃeanwhile, frightened by that gigantic clamour, the leaders of elephants and other animal - troops, in rut, were tormented and fled in different directions from there.

Sloka 5

sa tam sainya samudbhūtam śabdam śuśrava rāghavaḥ |
tāmḥ ca vipradrutān sarvān yūthapān anvavaikṣata || 2-96-5

5. saḥ rāghavaḥ = Rama; shushrūva = heard; tam shabdam = that sound; sainya samuddhūtam = generated from the army; anvavaikṣatea = and saw; sarvān = all; tān = those; yūthapān cha = leaders of the animal flock; vipradrutān = running away.

Rama heard that sound generated from the army and also saw all those leaders of the animal-flock, running away from there.

Sloka 6

tāmḥ ca vidravato dṛṣṭvā tam ca śrutvā sa nihsvanam |
uvāca rāmaḥ saumitrim lakṣmaṇam dīpta tejasam || 2-96-6

6. dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tān = those animals; vidravataḥ = running away; shrutvā = and hearing; tam = that; nisvanam cha = sound; rāmaḥ = Rama; uvācha = spoke; lakṣmaṇam = to Lakshman; saumitrim = the son of ṣumitra; dīpta tejasam = of burning courage.

Seeing those animals running away and hearing that clamour, Rama spoke to Lakshmana (son of Sumitra) of burning courage as follows:

Sloka 7

hanta lakṣmaṇa paśya iha sumitrā suprajāḥ tvayā |
bhīma stanita gambhhiraḥ tumulaḥ śrūyate svanaḥ || 2-96-7

7. hanta = Alas!; lakṣmaṇa = O; Lakshmana; sumitrā = sumitra; suprajāḥ = is blessed with a noble son; tvayā = in you; iha = here; shrūyate = is heard; tumulaḥ = a tumultuous; svanaḥ = sound; bhīmastanitagambhīraḥ = as terrific as that of a roaring cloud; pashya = see (what it is).

"Alas! O, Lakshmana! Sumitra is blessed with a noble son in you. Here is heard a tumultuous sound as terrific as that of a roaring cloud. See what it is."

Sloka 8

gajayūthāni vāraṇye mahiṣā vā mahāvane |
vitrāsitā mṛgāh siṃhaiḥ sahasā pradrutā diśaḥ 2-96-8

8. gajayūthāni = ṭhe lordly elephants; araṇye = in the forest; mahiṣāḥvā = the buffaloes; mahāvane = in the great woods; mṛigāḥ = and the wild beasts; vitrāsitāḥ = frightened; simhaiḥ = by lions; sahasā = are suddenly; pradrutāḥ = running away; dishaḥ = in various directions.

"The lordly elephants in the forest, the buffaloes in the great woods and the wild beasts frightened by lions are suddenly running away in various directions."

Sloka 9

rājā vā rāja mātro vā mṛgayām aṭate vane |
anyad vā śvā padam kiṃcit saumitre jnātum arhasi || 2-96-9

9. saumitre = O; Lakshmana!; arhasi = you ought to; j^ṇātum = know; (whether); rājā vā = a king; rāja putrovā = or a prince; mṛigayām aṭate = in roaming for a hunt; vā = or; anyat = any other; kiṇchit = somewhat; shvāpadam = savage beast.

"O, Lakshmana! You ought to find out whether it is a king or a prince roaming for a hunt or some other savage beast."

Sloka 10

suduścaro giriścāyaṃ pakṣiṇāmapi lakṣmaṇa |
sarvam etad yathā tattvam acirāj jnātum arhasi || 2-96-10

10. lakṣmaṇa = O; Lakshmana!; giriḥ = mountain; ayam = this; sudushcharaḥ = is difficult of access; pakṣiṇāmapi = even to birds; arhasi = you ought; J^ṇātum = to find out; achirāt = soon; etat sarvam = all this; yathā tattvam = accurately.

"O, Lakshmana! This mountain is difficult of access even to birds. You ought to find out soon, all this in an accurate manner."

Sloka 11

sa lakṣmaṇaḥ samtvaritaḥ sālam āruhya puṣpitam |
prekṣamāṇo diśaḥ sarvāḥ pūrvām diśam avaikṣata || 2-96-11

11. saḥ lakṣmaṇaḥ = that Lakshmana; samtvaritaḥ = quickly; āruhya = ascended; sālam = a ṣala tree; puṣpitam = in flowering; prekṣamāṇaḥ = and looking; sarvāḥ = in all; dishaḥ = directions; udaikṣata = saw; pūrvām disham = towards eastern side.

Lakshmana quickly ascended a Sala tree in flowering and looking towards all directions, saw towards eastern side.

Sloka 12

udan mukhaḥ prekṣamāṇo dadarśa mahatīm camūm |
ratha aśva gaja sambādhām yattair yuktām padātibhiḥ || 2-96-12

12. prekṣamāṇaḥ = looking; udaṇ^gumukhaḥ = towards norhern direction; dadarsha = (Lakshmana) saw; mahatīm = a huge; chamūm = army; rathāshva gaja sambādhām = full of chariots; steeds and elephants; yuktām = accompanied by; yataiḥ = smart; padātibhiḥ = foot-soldiers.

Looking in northern direction, Lakshmana saw a huge army, full of chariots, steeds and elephants accompanied by smart foot-soldiers.

Sloka 13

tām aśva gaja sampūrṇām ratha dhvaja vibhūṣitām |
śaśaṃsa senām rāmāya vacanam ca idam abravīt || 2-96-13

13. shashamsa = (Lakshmana) informed; rāmāya = Rama; tām senām = about that army; ashvagaja sampūrṇām = full of horses; elephants; ratha dhvaja vibhūṣitām = and chariots decorated with flags; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these; vachanamcha = words also.

Lakshmana informed Rama about that army which was full of horses, elephants and chariots decorated with flags and spoke the following words also.

Sloka 14

agnim saṃśamayatu āryaḥ sītā ca bhajatām guhām |
sajyam kuruṣva cāpam ca śarāmḥ ca kavacam tathā || 2-96-14

14. āryaḥ = O; honourable brother!; samshamayatu = extinguish; agnim = the fire; sītācha = let ṣeetha also; bhajatām = occupy; guhām = a hiding place; kuruṣva = ṃake; chāpam = your bow; sajyam = ready with a bow-string; tathā = and; sharāmshcha = arrows; kavacham = (wear) an armour.

"O, honourable brother! Extinguish the fire. Let Seetha occupy a hiding place. ṃake your bow ready with a bow-string and arrows. Wear an armour."

Sloka 15

tam rāmaḥ puruṣa vyāghro lakṣmaṇam pratyuvāca ha |
anga avekṣasva saumitre kasya etām manyase camūm || 2-96-15

15. rāmaḥ = Rama; puruṣavyāghraḥ = the tiger among men; pratyuvācha La = replied; tam lakṣmaṇam = to that Lakshmana; anga saumitre = O; Lakshmana!; avekṣasya = see; kasya = whose; chamūm = army; manyase = your think; imām = is this?.

Rama the tiger among men replied to Lakshmana, saying: "O, Lakshmana! See, whose army do you think is this?"

Sloka 16

evam ukktaḥ tu rāmeṇa lakṣmāṇo vākyam abravīt |
didhakṣann iva tām senām ruṣitaḥ pāvako yathā || 2-96-16

16. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; rāmeṇa = by Rama; lakṣmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; ruṣitaḥ = was irritated pāvako yathā = like a fire; abravīt = and spoke; vākyam = the following words; didhakṣanniva = as though wishing to scorch; tām senām = that army.

Hearing Rama's words, Lakshmana was irritated like a fire and spoke the following words, as though he was wishing to scorch the army.

Sloka 17

sampannam rājyam igcchamḥ tu vyaktam prāpya abhiṣecanam |
āvām hantum samabhyeti kaikeyyā bharataḥ sutaḥ || 2-96-17

17. bharataḥ = Bharata; kaikeyyāḥ = Kaikeyi's; sutaḥ = son; prāpya = having obtained; abhiṣechanam = coronation; ichhan = and desirous; rājyam = (of making) the kingdom; sampannam = a perfect one; samabhyati = is coming; hantum = to kill; āvām = both of us; vyaktam = It is clear.

"Bharata, Kaikeyi's son, having obtained coronation and desirous of making the kingdom free of enemies, is coming here to kill both of us. It is clear."

Sloka 18

eṣa vai sumahān śrīmān viṭapī samprakāśate |
virājaty udgata skandhaḥ kovidāra dhvajo rathe || 2-96-18

18. eṣaḥ = this; sumahāni = very big; shrīmān = and glorious; ujvala skandhaḥ = with superb branches; samprakāshate = is shining; kovidāra dhvajaḥ = the ensign bearing the device of a Kovidara (pomegranate) tree; virājati = is shining; rathe = on that chariot.

"The gigantic and glorious tree with superb branches is shining over there prominently. The ensign bearing the device of a kovidara (pomegranate) tree is shining on that chariot."

Sloka 19

bhajanty ete yathā kāmam aśvān āruhya śīghragān |
ete bhrājanti samhṛṣṭā jagān āruhya sādinaḥ || 2-96-19

19. ete = they; bhajanti = are cavorting; āruhya = mounted; yathākāmam = comfortably; shīghragān = on their swift; ashvān = horses; ete = these; sādinaḥ = seated on elephants; bhrājanti = are bearing; samhṛiṣṭāḥ = delightedly; āruhya = mounted; gajān = on their elephants.

Among the cavalry, some are cavorting merrily on their swift horses, while others are beaming with delight, mounted on their elephants.

Sloka 20

gṛhīta dhanuṣau ca āvām girim vīra śrayāvahe |
athavehaiva tiṣṭhāvaḥ sannaddhāvudyatāyudhau 2-96-20

20. vīraḥ = O; warrior!; āvām = let us; gṛihīta dhanuṣau = take up our bows; shrayāvahai = and take refuge; girim = in the mountain; athavā = or; tiṣṭhāvaḥ = let us stay; ihaiva = here itself; sannaddhau = fully prepared; udyatāyudhau = with our weapons in our hands.

"O, warrior! Let us take up our bows and ascend the mountain or let us stay here itself, fully prepared with our weapons in our hands."

Sloka 21

api nau vaśam āgagcchet kovidāra dhvajo raṇe |
api drakṣyāmi bharatam yat kṛte vyasanam mahat || 2-96-21
tvayā rāghava samprāptam sītayā ca mayā tathā |

21. rāghavaḥ = O; Rama!; kovidāradhvajaḥ = will this flag with the insignia of a Kovidara tree; apyāgachchhet = come into our; vasham = subjugation?; apidrakṣyāmi = Can we see; bharatam = Bharata; yatkṛite = because of whom; mahat vyasanam = a great misfortune; samprāptam = is obtained; tvayā = by you; sītayā = by ṣeetha; tathā = and; mayā cha = by me.

"O, Rama! Will that flag with the insignia of a Kovidara tree come into our subjugation? Can we see Bharata, because of whom a great misfortune has occurred to you, to Seetha and to me?"

Sloka 22

yan nimittam bhavān rājyāc cyuto rāghava śāśvatīm |
samprāpto ayam arir vīra bharato vadhya eva me || 2-96-22

22. he vīraḥ = O; Warrior!; rāghava = O; Rama!; yannimittam = by the reason of whom; bhavān = you; chyutaḥ = were deprived; shāshvatāt = of an eternal; rājyāt = kingdom; ayam = such; ariḥ = an enemy; bharataḥ = Bharata; samprāpataḥ = who came here; vadhya eva = is to be slain; me = by me.

"O, Rama the warrior! By the reason of whom you were deprived of an eternal kingdom, such Bharata as a foe who is coming here, deserves to be slain by me."

Sloka 23

bharatasya vadhe doṣam na aham paśyāmi rāghava |
pūrva apakāriṇam hatvā na hyadharmeṇa yujyate || 2-96-23

23. rāghava = O; Rama!; aham = I; napashyāmi = do not see; doṣam = a mistake; vadhe = in the slaying; bharatasya = of Bharata; hatvā = by killing; pūrvāpikāriṇam = the one who has hurt another before; na yujyate = non is charged adharmaṇa = with unlawfulness.

"O, Rama! I see no sin in slaying Bharata. None is charged with unlawfulness, if one strikes down an aggressor."

Sloka 24

pūrvāpakārī bharatasytaktadharmaśca rāghava |
etasminn nihate kṛtsnām anuśādhi vasuṃdharām || 2-96-24

24. rāghava = O; Rama!; bharataḥ = Bharate; pūrvāpakārī = has hurt you before; tyaktadharmashcha = and his death is legitimate; etasmin = (while) he; nihate = is slain; anushādhi = rule; kṛitsnām = the entire; vasundharām = earth.

"Bharata is the assailant and his death is legitimate. He being dead, you will rule the entire earth."

Sloka 25

adya putram hatam samkhye kaikeyī rājya kāmukā |
mayā paśyet suduhkha ārtā hasti bhagnam iva drumam || 2-96-25

25. kaikeyī = (Let) Kaikeyi; rājya kākukā = who is craving for the kingdom; pashet = see; suduḥkhārtā = with great sorrow; adya = today; putram = her son; hatam = slain; mayā = by me; samkhye = in battle; drumam iva = as a tree; hasti bhagnam = is crushed down by an elephant.

"Let Kaikeyi, who is craving for the kingdom see, with great sorrow, her son slain by me today in battle, as a tree is crushed down by an elephant."

Sloka 26

kaikeyīm ca vadhiṣyāmi sānubandhām sabāndhavām |
kaluṣeṇa adya mahatā medinī parimucyatām || 2-96-26

26. vadjoṣyāmi = I shall kill; kaikeyīm cha = Kaikeyi too; sabāndhavān = along with her friend the hunch-back; sānubandhām = and other accomplices madinī = Le the earth; adya = today; parimuchyatām = be relieved; mahatā = of a great; kaluṣeṇa = sinner.

"I shall kill Kaikeyi too along with her friend the hunch-back and other accomplices. Let the earth today be relieved of a great sinner."

Sloka 27

adya imam samyatam krodham asatkāram ca mānada |
mokṣyāmi śatru sainyeṣu kakṣeṣu iva huta aśanam || 2-96-27

27. mānada = O; warrior bestowing an honour!; adya = today; samyatam = in the concentrated; krodham = fury; imam = this; asatkāram cha = injustice; (has kindled); mokṣyāmi = I shall scatter; shatru sainyeṣu = the enemy- battalions; hutāshanam iva = as a fire (consumes); kakṣeṣu = the dry wood.

"O, warrior bestowing an honour! Today in the concentrated fury that injustice has kindled I shall scatter the enemy - battalions as a fire consumes the dry wood."

Sloka 28

adya etac citra kūṭasya kānanam niśitaiḥ śaraiḥ |
cindan śatru śarīrāṇi kariṣye śoṇita ukṣitam || 2-96-28

28. adya = today; nishitaiḥ = with my sharpeed; sharaiḥ = arrows; chhindam = I shall slay; shatru sharīrāṇi = the bodies of my foes; kariṣye = and make; etat kānanam = this forest; chitra kūṭasya = of chitrakuta; shoṇitokṣitam = overflow with blood.

"Today, with my sharpened arrows, I shall slay the bodies of my foes and make this forest of chitrakuta overflow with blood."

Sloka 29

śarair nirbhinna hṛdayān kunjarāmḥ turagāmḥ tathā |
śvāpadāḥ parikarṣantu narāḥ ca nihatān mayā || 2-96-29

29. kuṇjarān = the elephants; tathā = and turagān = the horses; nirbhinna hṛidayān = whose breasts were laid open; sharaiḥ = with my darts; narāmshcha = and the men; nihatān = struck down; mayā = by me; parikarṣantu = be dragged about; shvāpadāḥ = by savage beasts.

"The elephants and horses whose breasts were laid open with my darts and the men too struck down by me, be dragged about by savage beasts."

Sloka 30

śarāṇām dhanuṣaḥ ca aham anṛṇo asmi mahā vane |
sasainyam bharatam hatvā bhaviṣyāmi na saṃśayaḥ || 2-96-30

30. hatvā = by killing; bharatam = Bharata; sa sainyam = together with his army; asmin = in this; mahāvane = great forest; bhaviṣyāmi = I shall; anṛiṇaḥ = discharge my debt; sharāṇām = to my arows; dhanuṣashcha = and my bow.

"By killing Bharata together with his army in this great forest, I shall discharge my debt to my bow and arrows."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe ṣaṇṇavatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 96th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
