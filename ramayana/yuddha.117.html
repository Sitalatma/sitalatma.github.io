Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 117
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Gods reach Lanka in aerial cars and approach Rama. They ask Rama why he ignores Seetha, when she is entering into the fire. Rama appeals them to describe in reality who he is. Brahma (the creator) proclaims his divinity and eulogizes him with hymns.

Sloka 1

tato hi durmanā rāmaḥ śrutvaivam vadatāṃ giraḥ |
dadhyau muhūrtaṃ dharmātmā bāṣpavyākulalocanaḥ || 6-117-1

1. shrutvā = hearing; giraḥ = the cries; vadatām = of those who were wailing; evam = in that way; dharmātmā = the virtuous minded; rāmaḥ = Rama; tataḥ = then; dadhyau = became thoughtfu; mūurtam = for a while; durmanāḥ = afflicted as he was in melancholy; bāṣhpa vyākula lochanoḥ = his eyes filled with tears.

Hearing the cries of these who were thus wailing, the virtuous minded Rama then became thoughtful for a while, afflicted as he was with melancholy, his eyes filled with tears.

Sloka 2

tato vaiśravaṇo rājā yamaśca pṛbhiḥ saha |
sahasrākṣaśca deveśo varuṇaśca jaleśvaraḥ || 6-117-2
ṣaḍrdhanayanaḥ śrīmān mahādevo vṛṣadhvajaḥ |
kartā sarvasya lokasya brahmā brahmavidāṃ varaḥ || 6-117-3
ete sarve samāgamya vimānaiḥ sūryasaṃnibhaiḥ |
āgamya nagarīṃ laṅkāmabhijagmuśca rāghavam || 6-117-4

2-4. tataḥ = thereupon; vaishravaṇaḥ = Kubera; rājā = the king of Yakshas; yamashcha = Yama the lord of death; pitṛibhiḥ saha = along with the deceased ancestors; sahasrākṣhaḥ = Indra; deveshaḥ = the lord of celestials; varuṇashcha = Varuna; jaleshwaraḥ = the lord of waters; shrīmān mahā devaḥ = the illustrious ṣhiva the great deity; shaDardhanayanaḥ = having three eyes; brahmā = brahma; kartā = the creator; sarvasya lokasya = of all the worlds; brahmavidām varaḥ = and the best among the knowers of sacred knowledge; sarve = all; ete = these; samāgamya = together; āgamya = reaching; laṇ^kām nagarīm = the City of Lanka; sūrya samnibhaiḥ vimānaiḥ = in aerial cars; shining like sun; abhijagmuḥ = and approached; rāghavam = Rama.

Thereupon, Kubera the King of Yakshas, Yama the lord of death together with the deceased ancestors, Indra the lord of celestials Varuna the lord of waters, the illustrious Shiva the great deity who bears the device of a bull as his banner and having three eyes, Brahma the creator of all the worlds and the best among the knowers of sacred knowledge all these together reaching the City of Lanka in aerial cars, shining like the sun approached Rama.

Sloka 5

tataḥ sahastābharaṇān pragṛhya vipulān bhujān |
abruvaṃstridaśaśreṣṭhā rāghavaṃ prāñjaliṃ sthitam || 6-117-5

5. tataḥ = thereupon; tridasha shreṣhṭhāḥ = those excellent gods; pragṛihya = lifting; vipulān bhujān = their long arms; shastābharaṇām = their hands decked with ornaments (as follows); abruvan = spoke; rāghavam = to Rama; sthitam = who stood (there); sahastābharaṇān = making a respectful salutation with folded hands.

Lifting their long arms, their hands decked with ornaments, those excellent gods thereupon, spoke (as follows) to Rama who stood there, making a respectful salutation to them with his folded hands.

Sloka 6

kartā sarvasya lokasya śreṣṭho jñānavatāṃ prabhuḥ |
upekṣase kathaṃ sītāṃ patantīṃ havyavāhane || 6-117-6
kathaṃ devagaṇaśreṣṭhamātmānaṃ nāvabuddhyase |

6. katham = how; upekṣhase = are you looking at; sītām = ṣeetha; patantīm = who is falling; havyavāhane = into the fire; samsta lokasya kartā = (you) who are the maker of the entire cosmons; jJṇānavatām shreṣhṭhaḥ = and the foremost among those endowed with knowledge; prabhuḥ = and an all-capable person; katham = how; nāvabuddhyase = do you not know; ātmānam = yourself; devagaṇashreṣhṭham = as the foremost among the troop of gods.

"How do you, the maker of the entire cosmos, the foremost among those endowed with knowledge and an all-capable person, ignore Seetha who is falling into the fire? How do you not recognize yourself to be the foremost of the troop of gods?"

Sloka 7

ṛtadhāmā vasuḥ pūrvaṃ vasūnāṃ ca prajāpatiḥ || 6-117-7
trayāṇāmapi lokānāmādikartā svayaṃ prabhuḥ |

7. vasūnām = among the Vasus (a class of gods; eight in number); vasuḥ = you are the Vasu; ṛitadhāma = ṛitadhāma (one whose abode is ṭruth or the Divine law); pūrvam = who was formerly; svayam prabhuḥ = self-constituted ruler; ādikartā = the first creator; trayāṇāmapi = lokānām = of all the three worlds; prajāpatiḥ = and the lord of creatures.

"Among the Vasus (a class of gods, eight in number), you are the Vasu, named ṛitadhama (one whose abode is Truth or the Divine Law) who was formerly the self-constituted ruler, the first creator of all the three worlds and the lord of creatures."

Sloka 8

rudrāṇāmaṣṭamo rudraḥ sādhyānāmapi pañcamaḥ || 6-117-8
aśvinau cāpi karṇau te sūryācandrāmasau dṛśau |

8. aṣhṭamaḥ rudraḥ = you are the eight Rudra; rudrāṇām = among (eleven) Rudras; paṇ^chamaḥ = and the fifth (viryavān by name) sādhyānām api = among the ṣadhyas (a particular class of celestial beings belonging to Gana Devata); ashvinau = the twin = Aswinis (the physicians of gods); te karṇau = are your ears; sūrya chandrau = the sun and the moon; dṛishau = constitute your eyes.

"You are the eighth Rudra among (eleven) Rudras and the fifth (Viryavan by name) among the Sadhyas (a particular class of celestials belonging to Gana Devata). The twin Aswinis are your ears. The sun and the moon constitute your eyes."

Sloka 9

ante cādau ca lokānāṃ dṛśyase ca paraṃtapa || 6-117-9
upekṣase ca vaidehīṃ mānuṣaḥ prākṛto yathā |

9. paramtapa = O the destroyer of adversaries!; dṛishyase = you are seen; ādau = at the beginning; ante cha = and at the end; lokānām = of the created beings; prākṛitaḥ mānuṣho yathā = just like a common man; upekṣhase = you are ignoring; vaidehīm = ṣeetha.

"O the destroyer of the adversaries. You are seen (to exist) at the beginning and at the end of creation. Yet, you ignore Seetha, just like a common man."

Sloka 10

ityukto lokapālaistaiḥ svāmī lokasya rāghavaḥ || 6-117-10
abravittridaśaśreṣṭhān rāmo dharmabhṛtāṃ varaḥ |

10. iti uktaḥ = thus spoken; taiḥ lokapālaiḥ = by those guardians; rāmaḥ = Rama; lokasya svāmī = the lord of creation; rāghavaḥ = who was born in Raghu dynasty; dharma bhṛitām varaḥ = and the foremost one among the protectors of righteousness; abravīt = spoke; tridashashreṣhṭhān = to those god-chiefs (as follows)

Hearing the words of those guardians of the world, Rama, the lord of creation, who was born in Raghu dynasty and the foremost one among protectors of righteousness, spoke to those god-chiefs as follows:

Sloka 11

ātmānaṃ mānuṣaṃ manye rāmaṃ daśarathātmajam || 6-117-11
so.ahaṃ yasya yataścāhaṃ bhagavaṃstadbravītu me |

11. manye = I think; ātmānam = of myself; mānuṣham = to be a human being; rāmam = called Rama; dasharathātmajam = the son of Dasaratha; bhagavān = you; as a gracious Divinity; bravītu = tell; me = me; tat = that; saḥ aham yasya = which I as such really am; aham yashcha = and why I am like this.

"I think of myself to be a human being, by name Rama, the son of Dasaratha. You, as a gracious Divinity, tell me that which I as such really am like this."

Sloka 12

iti bruvāṇaṃ kākutthsaṃ brahmā brahmavidāṃ varaḥ || 6-117-12
abravīcchṛṇu me vākyaṃ satyaṃ satyaparākrama |

12. kākutthsam = to Rama; iti bruvāṇām = thus speaking; brahmā = Brahma; varaḥ = the foremost; brahma vidām = among the knowers of Brahma the Absolute; abravīt = spoke (as follows); shruṇu = listen; me satyam vākyam = to my true word; satyaparākrama = O the truly brave lord!"

Hearing the words of Rama, Brahma (the creator), the foremost among the knowers of Brahma the Absolute, spoke as follows: "Listen to my true word, O the truly brave lord!"

Sloka 13

bhavānnārāyaṇo devaḥ śrīmāṃścakrāyudhaḥ prabhuḥ || 6-117-13
ekaśṛṅgo varāhastvaṃ bhūtabhavyasapatnajit |

13. bhavān = you; prabhuḥ nārāyaṇaḥ = are the Lord ṇarayā himself; shrīmān devaḥ = the glorious god; chakrāyudhaḥ = who wields the discus; tvam = you; varāhaḥ = are the divine Boar; eka shṛiṇ^gaḥ = with a single tusk; bhūta bhavya sapatnajit = the conqueror of your past and future enemies.

"You are the Lord Narayana himself the glorious god, who wields the discus. You are the Divine Boar with a single tusk, the conqueror of your past and future enemies."

Sloka 14

akṣaraṃ brahma satyaṃ ca madhye cānte ca rāghava || 6-117-14
lokānāṃ tvaṃ paro dharmo viṣvaksenaścaturbhajaḥ |

14. brahma = (you are) Brahma; akṣharam = the imperishable; satyamcha = and the ṭruth; madhye cha antyecha = in the ṃiddle and at the end; tvam = you are paraḥ dharmaḥ = the ṣupreme righteousness; lokānām = of people; viṣhvaksenaḥ = whose pwers go everywhere; chaturbhujaḥ = and the four-armed one.

"You are Brahma, the imperishable, the Truth abiding in the middle as well as at the end of the universe. You are the supreme righteousness of people, whose powers go everywhere. You are the four-armed."

Sloka 15

śārṅgadhanvā hṛṣīkeśaḥ puruṣaḥ puruṣottamaḥ || 6-117-15
ajitaḥ khaḍgadhṛgviṣṇuḥ kṛṣṇaścaiva mahābalaḥ |

15. shārṇ^gadhanvā = you are the wielder of a bow; called ṣharnga hṛishīkeshaḥ = the lord of the senses; puruṣhaḥ = the supreme soul of the universe; puruṣhottamaḥ = the best of men; ajitaḥ = the invincible; khaDgadhṛik = the wielder of a sword named ṇandaka; viṣhṇuḥ = the all-pervader; kṛiṣhṇashchaiva = the bestower of happiness to the earth; mahābalaḥ = and endowed with great might.

"You are the wielder of a bow called Sarnga, the lord of the senses, the supreme soul of the universe, the best of men, the invincible, the wielder of a sword named Nandaka, the all-pervader, the bestower of happiness to the earth and endowed with great might."

Sloka 16

senānīrgrāmaṇīśca tvaṃ tvaṃ buddhi stvaṃ kṣamā damaḥ || 6-117-16
prabhavaścāpyayaśca tvamupendro madhusūdanaḥ |

16. tvam = you; senānīḥ = are the leader of the army; grāmaṇīshcha = and the village headman; tvam = you; buddhiḥ = are the intellect; tvam = you; kṣhamā = are the endurance; damaḥ = the subduer of the senses; tvam = you; prabhavaḥ = are the origin; apyayashcha = and the dissolution; upendraḥ = the Divine Dwarf (the younger brother of Indra); madhusūdanaḥ = and the destroyer of ṃadhu; the demon.

"You are the leader of the army and the village headman. You are the intellect. You are the endurance and the subduer of the senses. You are the origin and the dissolution of all, Upendra the Divine Dwarf and (the younger brother of Indra) as also the destroyer ṃadhu, the demon."

Sloka 17

indrakarmā mahendrastvaṃ padmanābho raṇāntakṛt || 6-117-17
śaraṇyaṃ śaraṇam ca tvāmahurdivyā maharṣayaḥ |

17. tvam = you; indrakarmā = perform action for Indra the lord of celestials; mahendraḥ = the ṣupreme Ruler; padmanābhaḥ = and the one having a lotus in one's navel; raṇāntakṛit = and who puts an end to all in battle; divyāḥ maharṣhayaḥ = the divine sages; āhuḥ = pronounce; tvām = you; sharaṇyam = to be fit to afford protection toa ll; sharaṇam = and the refuge (for all).

"You perform action for Indra the lord of celestials, the Supreme Ruler, the one having a lotus in one's navel and who puts an end to all in battle. The divine sages pronounce you to be fit to afford protection to all and the refuge for all."

Sloka 18

sahasraśṛṅgo vedātmā śataśīrṣo maharṣabhaḥ || 6-117-18
tvaṃ trayāṇāṃ hi lokānāmādikartā svayaṃprabhuḥ |
siddhānāmapi sādhyānāmāśrayaścāsi pūrvajaḥ || 6-117-19

18-19. vedātmā = In the form of the Vedas; maharṣhabhaḥ = you are the great bull; shatashīrṣhaḥ = with hundred heads (rules); sahasra shṛiṇgaḥ = and thousand horns (precepts); tvam = you; ādikartā = are the first creator of all; trayāṇām lokām = three worlds; svayamprabhuḥ = the self constituted Lord of all; asi = you are; āshrayaḥ cha = the refuge; pūrvajaḥ = and the for bear; siddhānām = of ṣiddhas (a class of demi-gods endowed with mystic powers by virtue of their very birth) and ṣadhyas (a class of celestial beings).

"In the form of the Vedas, you are the great Bull with hundred heads (rules) and thousand horns (precepts). You are the first creator of all, the three worlds, and the self constituted Lord of all. You are the refuge and the forbear of Siddhas (a class of demi-gods endowed with mystic powers by virtue of their very birth) and Sadhyas (a class of celestial beings.)"

Sloka 20

tvaṃ yajñstvaṃ vaṣaṭkārastvamoṃkāraḥ parātparaḥ || 6-117-20
prabhavaṃ nidhanaṃ vā te no viduḥ ko bhavāniti |

20. tvam = you; yajJṇaḥ = are the sacrificial performance; tvam = you; vaṣhaṭkāraḥ = are the sacred syllable 'Vasat' (on hearing which the Adhvaryu priest casts the oblation to a deity into the sacrificial fire); tvam = you are; omkāraḥ = the mysitic syllable "Oṃ'; paraḥ = and higher; parāt = than the highest; nor your origin; na bhavān kaḥ iti = nor who you are in reality.

"You are the sacrificial performance. You are the sacred syllable 'Vashat' (on hearing which the Adhvaryu priest casts the oblation to a deity into the sacrificial fire). You are the mystic syllable 'Oṃ'. You are higher than the highest. People neither know your end nor your origin nor who you are in reality."

Sloka 21

dṛśyase sarvabhūteṣu goṣu ca brāhmaṇeṣu ca || 6-117-21
dikṣu sarvāsu gagane parvateṣu nadīṣu ca |

21. dṛishyase = you appear; sarvabhūteṣhu = in all created beings; goṣhucha = in the cattle; brāhmaṇeṣhu cha = in brahmanas; sarvāsu dikṣhu in all quarters; gagane = in the sky; parvateṣhu = in mountains; nadīṣhu cha = and in rivers.

"You appear in all created beings in the cattle and in brahmanas. You exist in all quarters, in the sky, in mountains and in rivers."

Sloka 22

sahasracaraṇaḥ śrīmān śataśīrṣaḥ sahasradṛk || 6-117-22
tvaṃ dhārayasi bhūtāni pṛthivīṃ ca saparvatām |

22. sahasracharaṇaḥ = with thousand feet; shrīmān = along with Lakshmi the goddess of wealth; shata shīrṣhaḥ = with hundred heads; sahasra dṛik = and with thousand eyes; tvam dhārayasi = you bear; pṛithivīmcha = the earth; bhūtāni = with all its created beings; saparvatām = along with the mountains.

"With thousand feet, with hundred heads and with thousand eyes along with Lakshmi the goddess of wealth, you bear the earth with all its created beings along with its mountains."

Sloka 23

ante pṛthivyāḥ salile dṛśyase tvaṃ mahoragaḥ || 6-117-23
trīn lokān dhārayan rāma devagandharvadānavān |

23. rāma = O Rama!; tvam = you; dṛishyase = appear; mahoragaḥ = as ṣesha; a large serpent; salile = in water; ante pṛithivyāḥ = at the earth's bottom; dhārayan = bearing; trīn lokān = the three worlds; devagandharva dānavān = gods; Gandarvas the celestial musicians and the demons.

"O Rama! You appear as Sesha, a large serpent in water, at the earth's bottom, bearing the three worlds, gods, Gandharvas, the celestial musicians and the demons."

Sloka 24

ahaṃ te hṛdayaṃ rāma jihvā devī sarasvatī || 6-117-24
devā romāṇi gātreṣu brahmaṇā nirmitāḥ prabho |

24. rāma = O Rama!; aham = I (Brahma); te hṛidayam = am your heart; sarasvatī = ṣarasvathi; devī = the goddess (of learning); jihvā = is your tongue; prabho = O Lord!; sarvagātreṣhu = In all your limbs; devāḥ = the gods; nirmitāḥ = have been created; brahmaṇā = by me; the Brahma; romāṇi = int eh form of hair.

"O Rama! I (brahma) am your heart. Saraswathi, the goddess (of learning) is your tongue. O lord! The gods created by Brahma are the hair on all your limbs."

Sloka 25

nimeṣaste smṛtā rātrirunmeṣo divasastathā || 6-117-25
saṃskārāstvabhavanvedā naitadasti tvayā vinā |

25. rātriḥ = ṇight; smṛitā = has been recognized; te nimeṣhaḥ = as the closing of your eye-lids; tathā = and; divasaḥ = the day; unmeṣhaḥ = as the opening of your eye-lids; tva samskārāḥ = the correct usages of your words; vedāḥ = are the Vedas; tvayā vinā = bereft of you; etat = this (the visible universe); nāsti bhavan = does not exist.

"Night has been recognized as the closing of your eye-lids and the day, as the opening of eye-lids. The correct usages of your words are the Vedas. Bereft of you, this visible universe does not exist."

Sloka 26

jagatsarvaṃ śarīraṃ te sthairyaṃ te vasudhātalam || 6-117-26
agniḥ kopaḥ prasādaste somaḥ śrīvatsalakṣaṇaḥ |

26. sarvam jagat = the entire cosmos; te sharīram = is your body; vasudhātalam = the earth; te sthairyam = constitutes your firmness; agniḥ = fire; kopaḥ = is your anger; somaḥ = the moon (constitutes) te prasādaḥ = your placidity; shrīvatsa lakṣhmaṇaḥ = you are Lord Vishnu (who bears the mark ṣrivatsa = a curl of white hair on his breast).

"The entire cosmos is your body. The earth constitutes your firmness. Fire is your anger. The moon constitutes your placidity. You are Lord Vishnu (who bears the mark Srivatsa - a curl of white hair on his breast)."

Sloka 27

tvayā lokāstrayaḥ krāntāḥ purā svairvikramaistribhiḥ || 6-117-27
mahendraśca kṛto rājā baliṃ baddhvā sudāruṇam |

27. purā = in the past; trayaḥ = the three; lokāḥ = worlds; krāntāḥ = were occupied; tvayā = by you; svaiḥ tribhiḥ vikramaiḥ = in your three strides; baddhvā = after binding; sudāruṇam = the exceptionally formidable; balim = Bali; mahendraḥ = and Indra; kṛitaḥ rājā = was made king.

"In the past, the three worlds were occupied by you in your three strides, after binding the exceptionally formidable Bali (the ruler of the three worlds) and Indra was made the king (by you)."

Sloka 28

sītā lakṣmīrbhavān viṣṇurdevaḥ kṛṣṇaḥ prajāpatiḥ || 6-117-28
vadhārthaṃ rāvaṇasyeha praviṣṭo mānuṣīṃ tanum |

28. sītā = ṣeetha; lakṣhmī = is (no other than) Goddess Lakshmi (the divine consort of Lord Vishnu); bhavān = (while) you are; viṣhṇuḥ = Lord Vishnu; bhavān = you; kṛiṣhṇaḥ = are having a dark blue hue; devaḥ = and a shining one; prajāpatiḥ = you are the Lord of created beings; rāvaṇasya vadhārtham = for the destruction of Ravana; praviṣhṭaḥ = you entered; mānuṣhaṃ tanum = a human body; iha = here (on this earth).

"Seetha is no other than Goddess Lakshmi (the divine consort of Lord Vishnu), while you are Lord Vishnu. You are having a shining dark-blue hue. You are the Lord of created beings. For the destruction of Ravana, you entered a human body here, on this earth."

Sloka 29

tadidaṃ nastvayā kāryaṃ kṛtaṃ dharmabhṛtāṃ vara || 6-117-29
nihato rāvaṇo rāma prahṛṣṭo divamākrama |

29. rāma = O Rama; vara = the foremost; dharmabhṛitām = among the supporters of righteousness!; tat = that; idam = this; naḥ kāryam = purpose of ours; kṛitam = has been fulfilled; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; nihataḥ = has been killed; ākrama = approach; divam = your divine abode; prahṛiṣhṭaḥ = with a rejoice.

"O Rama, the foremost among the supporters of righteousness! The aforesaid purpose of ours has been fulfilled. Ravana has been killed. Return to your divine abode, with a rejoice."

Sloka 30

amoghaṃ deva vīryaṃ te na te moghāḥ parākramāḥ || 6-117-30
amoghaṃ darśanaṃ rāma amoghastava saṃstavaḥ |

30. deva = O Lord!; amogham = unerring; vīryam = is your valour; te = your; parākramāḥ = exploits; na moghāḥ = are never in vain; rāma = O Rama!; darshanam = your blessed sight; amogham = is powerful; tava samstvaḥ = the songs; in praise you; amoghaḥ = never go in vain.

"O Lord! Unerring is your valour. Your exploits are never in vain. O Rama! Your blessed sight is powerful. The songs in praise of you never go in vain."

Sloka 31

amoghāste bhaviṣyanti bhaktimanto narā bhuvi || 6-117-31
ye tvāṃ devam dhruvaṃ bhaktāḥ purāṇaṃ puruṣottamam |
prāpnuvanti sadā kāmāniha loke pātra ca || 6-117-32

31-32. bhuvi = On this earth; narāḥ = the humans; te bhaktimantaḥ = with devotion towards you; bhavanti = will; amoghāḥ = never be unsuccessful; ye = those who; bhaktāḥ = are devoted; tvām = to you; devam = the preimeval Lord; dhruvam = and the eternal; purāṇam = who belongs to ancient times; puruṣhottamam = and the ṣupreme person; (they); sadā prāpnuvanti = will forever attain kāmām = their desired objects; iha loke = in this world; paratra cha = and in the next world.

"Those humans who are full of devotion to you will never be unsuccessful on this earth. Those who are devoted to you, the primeval and the eternal lord, belonging to ancient times and the Supreme Person, will forever attain their desired objects here as well as hereafter."

Sloka 33

imamārṣam stavaṃ divyamitihāsaṃ purātanam || 6-117-33
ye narāḥ kīrtayiṣyanti nāsti teṣāṃ parābhavaḥ |

33. parābhavaḥ = ḥumiliation; nāsti = will never be the plight; teṣhām ye narāḥ = of those humans; kīrtayiṣhyanti = who will recite; imam stavam = this hymn; divyam = divine; purātanam = ancient; itihāsam = history; ārṣham = sung by Brahma; the foremost seer.

"Humiliation will never be the plight of those humans who will recite this hymn in a divine ancient history, sung by Brahma, the foremost seer."

- - - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye yuddhakāṇḍe saptadaśādhikaśtatamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 117th chapter in Yuddha Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
