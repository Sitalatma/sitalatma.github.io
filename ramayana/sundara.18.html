Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 18
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Hanuma sees Ravana entering Ashoka garden with a retinue of his wives following him.

Sloka 1

tathā viprekṣamāṇasya vanam puṣpitapādapam |
vicinvataśca vaidehīm kiṃcicceṣā niśābhavat || 5-18-1

1. nishā = the night; abhavat = became; kiṃchichheshā = a little remaning; tathā viprekshamāṇasya = while (ḥanuma) searched like that; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; vanam = in the garden; pushhpitapādapam = with flowers in blossom;

A little of the night was left while ḥanuma was searching like that for ṣeetha in the garden with flowers in blossom.

Sloka 2

ṣaḍaṅgavedaviduṣām kratupravarayājinām |
śuśrāva brahmaghoṣān sa virātre brahmarakṣasām || 5-18-2

2. saḥ = ṭhat ḥanuma; shushrāva = heard; brahma ghoshhān = Vedic sounds; virātre = early in the morning; brahmarākshasām = of Brahma Rakshasas;shhadaṇ^ga veda vidushhām = well versed in six parts of Vedas; kratu pravara yājinām = who performed excellent sacrifices;

ṭhat ḥanuma heard Vedic sounds early in the morning of Brahma Rakshasas well versed in six parts of Vedas and those who performed excellent sacrifices.

Sloka 3

atha maṅgalavāditraśabdaiḥ śrutimanoharaiḥ |
prabudhyata mahābāhurdaśagrīvo mahābalaḥ || 5-18-3

3. atha = ṭhereafter; dasagrīvaḥ = Ravana; mahā bāhuḥ = with great arms; mahā balaḥ = with great prowess; prābudhyata = was woken; mangalavāditra shabdaiḥ = by the sounds of various auspicious musical instruments; shrutimanoharaiḥ = pleasing to the ear.

ṭhereafter Ravana with great arms and great prowess was woken by the sounds of various auspicious musical instruments pleasing to the ear.

Sloka 4

vibudhya tu yathākālam rākṣasendraḥ pratāpavān |
srastamālyāmbaradharo vaidehīmanvacintayāt || 5-18-4

4. rākshasendraḥ = ṭhe king of rakshasas; pratāpavān = with great might; vibudhya = woke up; yathākālam = at the appropriate time; srasta mālyāmbara dharaḥ = wearing slippery flower garlands; anvachintayat = remembered; videhīm = about ṣeetha.

ṭhe king of rakshasas with great might woke up at the appropriate hour wearing slippery flower garlands remembered about Seetha.

Sloka 5

bhṛśaṃ niyuktastasyām ca madanena madotkaṭaḥ |
na sa taṃ rākṣasaḥ kāmaṃ śaśākātmani gūhitum || 5-18-5

5. tasyām = In that ṣeetha's matter; niyuktaḥ = directed; bḥṛisham = a lot; madanena = by the god of love; madotkaṭaḥ = excited by passion; saḥ rākshasaḥ = that Rakshasa; na shasāka = was not capable; gūhitum = to suppress; ātmani = in self; tam kāmam = that desire.

In that ṣeetha's matter, directed a lot by the god of love and excited by passion, that Rakshasa was not capable to suppress in self that desire.

Sloka 6

sa sarvābharaṇairyukto bibhacchriyamanuttamām |
tāṃ nagairbahubhirjuṣṭām sarvapuṣpaphalopagaiḥ || 5-18-6

6. yuktaḥ = together with; sarvābharaṇaiḥ = all ornaments; bibhrat = wearing; anuttamām shriyam = great glory; saḥ = that Ravana; (entered) that Ashoka garden; jushhṭām = having; bahubhiḥ nagaiḥ = a lot of trees; sarvapushhpaphalopagaiḥ = with all fruits and flowers.

ṭogether with all ornaments wearing great glory that Ravana entered that Ashoka garden having a lot of trees with all fruits and flowers.

Sloka 7

vṛtāṃ puṣkariṇībhiśca nānāpuṣpopaśobhitām |
sadāmadaiśca vihagairvicitrām paramādbhutām || 5-18-7

7. (Ravana entered that Ashoka garden) paramādbhutām = a great wonderful one; vṛitām = together; pushhkariṇībhiḥ = with lakes; nānā pushhpopashobitām = shone by various flowers; vichitrām = brilliant;vihagaiḥ = with birds; sadā madaiḥ = always in rut.

Ravana entered that great wonderful Ashoka garden together with lakes, shone by various flowers, brilliant with birds always in rut.

Sloka 8

īhāmṛgaiśca vividhairjuṣṭāṃ ḍṛṣṭimanoharaiḥ |
vīthīḥ saṃprekṣamāṇaśca maṇikāñcanatoraṇāḥ || 5-18-8
nānāmṛgagaṇākīrṇāṃ phalaiḥ prapatitairvṛtām |
aśokavanakāmeva prāviśatsaṃtatadrumām || 5-18-9

8; 9. prāvishat = (Ravana) entered; ashokavanikāmeva = Ashoka garden; saṃprekshamāṇashcha = seeing;maṇikāṇ^chana toranāḥ = archways of gold and gems; jushhṭām = crowded; īhāmṛigaishcha = by artificial deer; vividhaiḥ = of various varieties; dṛishhṭimanoharaiḥ = ravishing the eyes and mind; nānāmṛigagaṇākīrṇām = filled with animals of various kinds; vṛitām = together; prapatitaiḥ phalaiḥ = with fallen fruits;samtatadrumām = full of trees.

Ravana entered Ashoka garden seeing archways of gold and gems crowded by artificial deer of various kinds ravishing eyes and mind, filled with animals of various kinds, together with fallen fruits and full of trees.

Sloka 10

aṅganāśatamātraṃ tu taṃ vrajantamanuvrajat |
mahendramiva paulastyamṃ devagandharvayoṣitaḥ || 5-18-10

10. vrajantam = while going; aṇ^ganāshatamātram = only a hundred of women;anuvrajat = followed; tam paulastyam = that Ravana; devagandharvayoshitaḥ = like Deva and Gandharva women; mahendram = (following) Indra.

While ḥe was going only a hundred of women followed that Ravana like Deva and Gandharva women following Indra.

Sloka 11

dīpikāḥ kāñcanīḥ kāścijjagṛhustatra yoṣitaḥ |
vālavyajanahastāśca tālavṛntāni cāparāḥ || 5-18-11

11. tatra = there; kāshchit = some; yoshhitaḥ = women; jagṛiḥ = bore; dīpikāḥ = lamps; kāṇ^chanīḥ = of gold; aparāḥ = some others; tālavṛintāni = (carried) chowries vālavyajana hastāshcha = (some others had) fans of Palmyra leaves in their hands.

ṭhere some women bore lamps of gold; some others carried chowries; some others had fans of Palmyra leaves in their hands.

Sloka 12

kāñcanairapi bhṛṅgārairjahruḥ salilamagrataḥ |
maṇḍalāgān brusīṃścāpi gṛhyānyāḥ pṛṣṭhato yayuḥ || 5-18-12

12. (ṣome women) juhruḥ = carried; salilam = water; bhṛingāraiḥ = in small vessels; kāṇ^chaniḥ = of gold; anyāḥ = some others; pṛishhṭhataḥ yayuḥ = followed in the back; gṛihya = taking; brusīṃshchaiva = pillows; maṇDalāgrān = with circular ends

ṣome women carried water in small vessels of gold; some others followed in the back taking pillows with circular ends.

Sloka 13

kācidratnamayīṃ sthālīṃ pūrṇāṃ pānasya brājatam |
dakṣiṇā dakṣiṇenaiva tadā jagrāha pāṇinā || 5-18-13

13. kāchit = one; bhāminī = woman; dakshinā = to the right (of Ravana); jagrāḥ = held; dakshinenaiva pāṇinā = with her right hand; sthālīm = a vesel; pānasya = of liqour; ratnamayīm = with gem stones; pūrṇām = full (of liqour)

One woman to the right of Ravana held with her right hand a vessel with gem stones filled with liqour.

Sloka 14

rājahaṃsapratīkāśaṃ catraṃ pūrṇaśaśiprabham |
sauvarṇadaṇḍamaparā gṛhītvā pṛṣṭhato yayau || 5-18-14

14. aparā = another (woman); pṛishhthataḥ yayau = went at back; gṛihītvā = taking; chhatram = an umbrella; rājahamsapratīkāsham = equalling a royal swan; pūrnashasiprabhām = with the radiance of full moon; sauvarṇadaṇDam = with a golden handle.

Another woman went at back taking an umbrella equalling a royal swan with the radiance of full moon with a golden handle.

Sloka 15

nidrāmadaparītākṣyo rāvanasyottamāḥ striyaḥ |
anujagmuḥ patiṃ vīram ghanam vidyullatā iva || 5-18-15

15. nidrāmada parītākshyo = With sleepy eyes; uttamāḥ striyaḥ = best women; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; anujagmuḥ = followed; vīram patim = mighty husband; vidyullatāḥ ghanamiva = like lightning (following a) cloud.

With sleepy eyes best women of Ravana followed mighty husband like lightning following a cloud.

Sloka 16Verse Locator

vyāviddhahārakeyūrāḥ samāmṛditavarṇakāḥ |
samāgalditakeśāntāḥ sasvedavadanāstathā || 5-18-16
ghorṇantyo madaśeṣeṇa nidrayā ca śubhānanāḥ |
svedakliṣṭāṅgakusumāḥ sumālyākulamūrdhajāḥ || 5-18-17
prayāntaṃ nairṛtapatiṃ nāryo madiralocanāḥ |
bahumānācca kāmācca priyā bhāryāstamanvayuḥ || 5-18-18

16;17;18. vyyaviddha keyūrāḥ = With displaced necklaces and armlets; samāmṛidita varṇakāḥ = with effaced cover (of sandal-paste); samāgaLita keshāntāḥ = with dishevelled hair; tathā = and also; sasveda vadanāḥ = with sweaty faces; ghoorṇantyaḥ = staggering; madasheshheṇa nidrayā cha = due to surviving trace of liqour and due to sleepiness; sveda klishhṭaṇ^ga kusumāḥ = with shrivelled flowers on perspiring bodies; sumālyākulamūrdhajāḥ = with dishevelled hair along with good garlands; madiralochanāḥ = with intoxicating eyes; nāryāḥ = women; priyāḥ bhāryāḥ = who were dear wives; anvayuḥ = followed; tam = that; nairṛitapatim = king of Rakshasas; prayāntam = who was going; bahumānāchcha = due to respect; kāmāchcha = and due to love.

With displaced necklaces and armlets, with effaced cover of sandal-paste, with dishevelled hair, and also with sweaty faces, staggering due to surviving trace of liquor and due to sleepiness, with shrivelled flowers on perspiring bodies, with dishevelled hair along with good garlands, with intoxicating eyes, women who were dear wives followed that king of Rakshasas who was going due to respect and due to love.

Sloka 19

sa ca kāmaparādhīnaḥ patistāsāṃ mahābalaḥ |
sītāsaktamanā mando mandāñcitagatirbabhau || 5-18-19

19. patiḥ = husband; tāsām = of those (women); mahābalaḥ = of great might; kāmaparādhīnaḥ = who was surrendered to lust; sa cha = that Ravana also; mandaḥ = (who was) dull witted; sītāsakta manāḥ = with his mind interested in ṣeetha; babhau = shone; mandāṇ^chita gatiḥ = with a slow and beautiful gait.

ḥusband of those women, of great might, who was surrendered to lust, that Ravana also who was dull witted, with his mind interested in Seetha shone with a slow and beautiful gait.

Sloka 20

tataḥ kāñcīninādaṃ ca nūpurāṇām ca nisvanam |
śuśrāva paramastrīṇāṃ sa kapirmārutātmajaḥ || 5-18-20

20. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; saḥ kapiḥ = that ḥanuma; mārutātjamjām = the son of wind-god; sushrāva = heard; kāṇ^chīninādam = the sound of waist-bands; nūpurāṇam nisvanam cha = and the sound of anklets; paramastrīṇām = of those excellent women.

ṭhereafter that ḥanuma, the son of wind-god heard the sound of waist-bands and the sound of anklets of those excellent women.

Sloka 21

taṃ cāpratimakarmāṇamacintyabalapauruṣam |
dvāradeśamanuprāptaṃ dadarśa hanumān kapiḥ || 5-18-21

21. hanumān = ḥanuma; kapiḥ = the monkey; dadarsha = saw; tam cha = that Ravana also; apratima karmāṇam = performer of deeds without an equal; achintya bala paurushham = with unfathomable might and virility; anuprāptam = reaching; dvāra desham = entrance region.

ḥanuma the monkey saw that Ravana also, performer of deeds without an equal, with unfathomable might and virility, reaching the entrance region.

Sloka 22

dīpikābhiranekābhiḥ samantādavabhāsitam |
gandhatailāvasiktābhirdriyamāṇābhiragrataḥ || 5-18-22

22. gandha tailāvasiktābhḥ = Wettened by fragrant oil; dhriyamāṇābhiḥ = being carried; agrataḥ = in front; (ḥanuma saw Ravana) avabhāsitam = being shone; samantāt = in all directions; anekābhiḥ = by many; dīpikābhiḥ = lamps;

Wettened by fragrant oil, being carried in front of Ravana, ḥanuma saw Ravana being shone in all directions by many lamps.

Sloka 23

kāmadarpamadairyuktaṃ jihmatāmrāyatekṣaṇam |
samakṣamiva kandarpamapaviddhaśarāsanam || 5-18-23

23. (ḥanuma saw Ravana) yuktam = together; kāmadarpamadaiḥ = with lust; vanity and intoxication; jihmatāmrāyatekshaṇam = with wide red eyes which were slanted; samaksham kandarpamiva = like ṃanmadha; the god of love in person;apaviddhasharāsanam = with bow kept at a distance;

ḥanuma saw Ravana together with lust, vanity and intoxication, with wide red eyes which were slanted, like the god of love in person with bow kept at a distance.

Sloka 24

mathitāmṛtaphenābhamarajovastramuttamam |
salīlamanukarṣantaṃ vimuktaṃ saktamaṅgade || 5-18-24

24. (ḥanuma saw Ravana) salillam = playfully; anukarshhantam = readjusting;uttamam vastram = excellent (upper) garment;mathitāmṛitaphenābham = With the lustre of churned nectar froth; arajaḥ = without stain;vimuktam = slipping; aṇ^gade saktam = being entangled with armlet; .

ḥanuma saw Ravana playfully readjusting his excellent upper garment with the luster of churned nectar froth, without stain,slipping being entangled in armlet.

Sloka 25

taṃ patraviṭape līnaḥ atrapuṣpaghanāvṛtah |
samīpamiva saṃkrāntaṃ nidhyātumupacakrame || 5-18-25

25. (ḥanuma) līnaḥ = blended;patraviṭape = in a branch full of leaves; patrapusshpaghanāvṛitaḥ = covered by groups of leaves and flowers; upachakrame = started; nidhyātum = to see; tam = that Ravana; samkrāntamiva = as though coming; samīpam = near (ḥim);

ḥanuma blended in a branch full of leaves covered by groups of leaves and flowers started to see that Ravana as though coming near Him.

Sloka 26

avekṣamāṇastu tato dadarśa kapikuñjaraḥ |
rūpayauvanasaṃpannā rāvaṇasya varastiyaḥ || 5-18-26

26. tataḥ = ṭhereafter; kapikuṇ^jaraḥ = the best among Vanaras; avekashamāṇaḥ = looking in all directions; dadarsha = saw;varastriyaḥ = best women; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; rūpayauvanasampannāḥ = full of beauty and youth;

ṭhereafter ḥanuma, the best among Vanaras looking in all directions saw best women of Ravana full of beauty and youth.

Sloka 27

tābhiḥ parivṛto rājā surūpābhirmahāyaśāḥ |
tasmṛgadvijasamghaṣṭaṃ praviṣṭaḥ pramadāvanam || 5-18-27

27. rājā = king; mahā yashāḥ = of great fame; parivṛitaḥ = together; tābhiḥ = with those women; surūpābhiḥ = with good appearance; pravishhṭaḥ = entered; tat = that garden; mṛigadvijasaṃghushhtam = resounded by animals and birds.

King of great fame, Ravana, together with those women with good appearance entered that garden resounded by animals and birds.

Sloka 28

kṣībo vicitrābharaṇaḥ śaṅkukarṇo mahābalaḥ |
tena viśravasaḥ putraḥ sa dṛṣṭo rākṣasādhipaḥ || 5-18-28
vṛtaḥ paramanārībhistārābhiriva candrāmāḥ |
taṃ dadarśa mahātejāstejovantaṃ mahākapiḥ || 5-18-29

28; 29. saḥ rākshsādhipaḥ = that king of Rakshasas; kshībaḥ = who was intoxicated; vichitrābharaṇaḥ = with wonderful ornaments; saṇ^kukarṇaḥ = with pointed ears; vishravasaḥ putraḥ = son of Visravasa; chandramāḥ iva = was like the moon; tārābhiḥ = together with stars; vṛitaḥ = together; paramanārībhiḥ = with best women; dṛishhtaḥ = was seen; tena = by that ḥanuma; mahā kapiḥ = that great Vanara; mahā tejāḥ = with great brilliance; dadarsha = saw; tam = that Ravana; tejovantam = with glory.

ṭhat king of Rakshasas who was intoxicated with wonderful ornaments, with pointed ears, son of Visravasa was like the moon together with stars, together with best women was seen by Hanuma. That great Vanara with great brilliance saw that Ravana with glory.

Sloka 30

rāvaṇo.ayam mahābāhuriti saṃcintya vānaraḥ |
avapluto mahātejā hanumānmārutātmajaḥ || 5-18-30

30. mārutātmajaḥ = ṭhe son of god of wind; vānaraḥ = Vanara; hanumān = ḥanuma; mahā tejāḥ = of great brilliance; ayam = this; mahābāhuḥ = long armed one; rāvaṇaḥ = is Ravana; iti samchintya = thinking thus; avaplutaḥ = jumped down.

ṭhe son of god of wind Vanara ḥanuma of great brilliance thinking thus "ṭhis long armed one is Ravana", jumped down.

Sloka 31

sa tathāpyugratejāḥ sannirdhūtastasya tejasā |
patraguhyāntare sakto hanumān saṃvṛto.abhavat || 5-18-31

31. tathā = thus; hanumān = ḥanuma; ugra tejāḥ sannapi = (although) of terrible energy; nirdhūtaḥ = was overshadowed; tasya tejasā = by Ravana's brilliance; abhavat = (and) became; patra guhyāntare saktaḥ = rooted behind a group of leaves; samvṛitaḥ = and was concealed;

ṭhus ḥanuma although of terrible energy was overshadowed by Ravana's brilliance and became rooted behind a group of leaves and was concealed.

Sloka 32

sa tāmasitakeśāntāṃ suśroṇīṃ saṃhatastanīm |
didṛkṣurasitāpāṅgamupāvartata rāvaṇaḥ || 5-18-32

32. saḥ rāvanaḥ = that Ravana; upāvartata = neared; tām = ṣeetha; asitakeshāntām = with black hair; sushroṇīm = with charming limbs; samhatastanīm = with well knit breasts; asitāpāṇ^gām = with black corners of eyes; didṛikshuḥ = wishing to see (ḥer);

ṭhat Ravana neared ṣeetha with black hair, with charming limbs with well knit breasts, with black corners of eyes, wishing to see Her.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe aṣṭādaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 18th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
