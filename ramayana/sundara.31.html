Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 31
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sitting on the branch of a Simshapa tree, Hanuma began to narrate in human language the story of Rama from the time he was born till his sending Hanuma to Lanka in search of Seetha. Hearing the narration of Hanuman, Seetha was overjoyed and finds Hanuma perched on Simshapa tree.


Sloka 1

evam bahu vidhām cintām cintayitva mahākapiḥ |
saṃśrave madhuram vākyam vaidehyā vyājahāra ha || 5-31-1

1. chintayitvā = thinking; evam = thus; bahuvidhām = many kinds of; chintām = thought; mahākapiḥ = ḥanuman; vyājahāraḥ = spoke (the following) madhuram = sweet; vākyām = words; samshrave = within the hearing; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha.

ṭhus reflecting on many kinds of thought, ḥanuma spoke the following sweet words within the hearing range of Seetha.

Sloka 2

rājā daśaratho nāma ratha kunjara vājinām |
puṇya śīlo mahākīrtiḥ ṛjuḥ āsīn mahāyaśāḥ || 5-31-2

2. rājā = (ṭhere was) a king; dasharathe nāma = named Dasaratha; mahāyashāḥ = who was very glorious; ikṣvākūṇām = among kings belonging to Ikshvaku dynasty; puṇya shīlaḥ = and had virtuous disposition; ratha kuṇ^jara vājimān = he owned chariots; elephants = and horses; mahā kīrtiḥ = and was very famous.

"ṭhere was a king named Dasaratha who was very glorious among the kings of Ikshvaku dynasty and had a virtuous disposition. He owned chariots, elephants and horses, becoming very famous.

Sloka 3

rājarṣīṇāṃ guṇaśreṣṭhastapasā carṣibhiḥ samaḥ |
cakra varti kule jātaḥ puram dara samo bale || 5-31-3

3. (ḥe was) guṇashreṣṭhaḥ = excellent in virtues; rājarṣīṇām = among royal sages; samaḥ = equal to; ṛiṣibhiḥ = the (seven) sages; tapasā = in austerity; jātaḥ = born; chakravarti kule = in the lineage of emperors; purandara samaḥ = and was equivalent to Indra; bale = in prowess.

"ḥe was excellent in virtues among royal sages. ḥe was equal to the seven sages in austerity. ḥe was born in the lineage of emperors and was equivalent to Indra in prowess."

Sloka 4

ahiṃsā ratiḥ akṣudro ghṛṇī satya parākramaḥ |
mukhyaḥ ca ikṣvāku vaṃśasya lakṣmīvāml lakṣmi vardhanaḥ || 5-31-4

4. (ḥe was) ahinsā ratiḥ = fond of harmlessness; akṣudraḥ = not vulgar; ghṛiṇī = compassionate; satya parākramaḥ = truly mighty; mukhyashcha = eminent; ikṣvāku vamshashcha = in Ikshvaku race; lakṣmīvān = endowed of prosperity; lakṣmivardhanah = and causing to increase prosperity.

"ḥe was keen on harmlessness. ḥe was not vulgar. ḥe was compassionate. ḥe was truly mighty. ḥe was an eminent king in Ikshvaku race. He was endowed with prosperity and was causing to increase prosperity."

Sloka 5

pārthiva vyañjanaiḥ yuktaḥ pṛthu śrīḥ pārthiva ṛṣabhaḥ |
pṛthivyām catuḥ antayām viśrutaḥ sukhadaḥ sukhī || 5-31-5

5. (ḥe was) yuktaḥ = endowed; pārthiva vyaṇjanaiḥ = with royal characteristics; pṛithushrī = was highly prosperous; pārthivarṣabhaḥ = excellent among kings; vishrutaḥ = famous; pṛithivyām = on earth; chaturantāyām = with four borders; sukhadaḥ = gave happiness; sukhī = and was happy himself.

"ṭhe king Dasaratha was endowed with royal characteristics, highly prosperous, excellent among kings and highly prosperous, excellent among kings and famous on earth with its four borders. He was happy man and gave happiness to others."

Sloka 6

tasya putraḥ priyo jyeṣṭhaḥ tārā adhipa nibha ānanaḥ |
rāmo nāma viśeṣajñaḥ śreṣṭhaḥ sarva dhanuṣmatām || 5-31-6

6. tasya = to that Dasaratha; jyeṣṭha putraḥ = (there was) an eldest son; rāmo nāma = named Rama priyaḥ = who was beloved; tārādhipa nibhānanaḥ = having a face like a moon; visheṣaJṇaḥ = knowing distinctions (in humans and others); shreṣṭhaḥ = and excellent; sarvadhanuṣmatām = among all the wielders of the bow.

"ṭhere was an eldest son named Rama to that Dasaratha. Rama was beloved having a moon-like face. He, knows the distinctions among humans and others. He was excellent among all the wielders of the bow."

Sloka 7

rakṣitā svasya vṛttasya sva janasya api rakṣitā |
rakṣitā jīva lokasya dharmasya ca param tapaḥ || 5-31-7

7. paramtapaḥ = (that Rama) who torments the enemies; rakṣitā = is a protector; svasya = his own; vṛittasya = mode of life; rakṣitā = a protector; svajanasya = of his own people; rakṣitā = a protector; jīva lokasya = of every living being; rakṣitā = and a protector; dharmatyacha = of righteousness.

"ṭhat Rama who annihilates enemies, is a protector of his own mode of life, a protector of his own people, a protector of every living being and a protector of righteousness."

Sloka 8

tasya satya abhisaṃdhasya vṛddhasya vacanāt pituḥ |
sabhāryaḥ saha ca bhrātrā vīraḥ pravrajito vanam || 5-31-8

8. vachanāt = by the words; tasya = of that Dasaratha; vṛiddhesayā = his old; pituḥ = father; satyābhisandhasya = a true speaking man; vīraḥ = that Rama; a brave man; a brave man; pravrājitaḥ = went on exile; vanam = to a forest; sa bhāryaḥ = along with his wife; sahacha bhrātrā = and together with his brother.

"By the command of that Dasaratha, his old father and true-speaking man, that Rama a brave man went on exile to a forest along with his wife and brother."

Sloka 9

tena tatra mahāaraṇye mṛgayām paridhāvatā |
rākṣasā nihatāḥ śūrā bahavaḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ || 5-31-9

9. paridhāvatā mṛigayām = while hunting; tatra mahāraṇye = in that great forest; baharaḥ = many; rākṣasāḥ = demons; shūrāḥ = who were valiant; kāma rūpiṇaḥ = and who could assume any form at will; nihatāḥ = were killed; tena = by that Rama.

"While hunting in that forest, that Rama killed many demons who were valiant and who could assume any form at will."

Sloka 10

jana sthāna vadham śrutvā hatau ca khara dūṣaṇau |
tataḥ tu amarṣa apahṛtā jānakī rāvaṇena tu || 5-31-10
vañcayitvā vane rāmaṃ mṛgarūpeṇa māyayā |

10. tataḥ = thereafter; shrutvā = hearing; jana sthāna vadham = about the killing of demons in Janasthana a part of Dandaka forest; khara dūṣaṇau cha = Khara and Dushana the demons; hatau = having been killed; jānakī = ṣeetha; amarṣāpahṛitā = was taken away with anger; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; vaṇchayitvā = by deceiving; mṛiga rupeṇa = in the guise of a deer; rāman = Rama; vane = in the forest; māyayā = and by creating illusion.

"ṭhereafter, hearing about the killing of demons in Janasthana, a part of Dandaka forest, in addition to killing of Khara and Dushana the demons, the enraged Ravana took away, Seetha, by deceiving Rama in creating an illusive image of deer in the forest."

Sloka 11

sa mārgamāṇastām devīm rāmaḥ sītāmannditām || 5-31-11
āsasāda vane mitram sugrīvam nāma vānaram |

11. tataḥ = thereafter; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama parapuramjayaḥ = the conqueror of enemys cities; mahābalaḥ = and a very stron man; hatvā = having killed; vālinam = Vali; prāyachchhat = gave away; tat = that; kapirājyam = kingdom of monkeys; sugrīvasya = to ṣugreeva.

"ṣearching for ṣeetha the irreproachable lady, that Rama met a monkey called ṣugreeva as his friend in the forest."

Sloka 12

tataḥ sa vālinam htvā rāmaḥ parapuramjayaḥ || 5-31-12
prāyacchatkapirājyam tatsugrīvāya mahābalaḥ |

12. tataḥ = thereafter; saḥ rāmaḥ = that Rama; parapuramjayaḥ = the conqueror of enemy's cities; mahābalaḥ = and a very strong man; hatvā = having killed; vālinam = Vali; prāyachchhat = gave away; tat = that; kapirājyam = kingdom of monkeys; sugrīvasya = to ṣugreeva.

"ṭhereafter, Rama the conqueror of enemy's cities and very strong man, killed Vali and gave away that kingdom of monkeys to Sugreeva."

Sloka 13

sugrīveṇāpi saṃdiṣṭā harayaḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ || 5-31-13
dikṣu sarvāsu tām devīm vicinnvanti sahasraśaḥ |

13. samdiṣṭāḥ = sent; sugrīveṇa = by ṣugreeva; sahasrashaḥ = thousands of; harayaḥ = monkeys; kāmarūpiṇaḥ = capable of assuming any form at their will; vichinvanti = are searching; tām devīm = that lady; ṣeetha; sarvāsu dikṣu = in all quarters.

"As commanded and sent by ṣugreeva, thousands of monkeys capable of assuming form at their will, are searching for that lady Seetha in all quarters of the earth."

Sloka 14

aham sampātivacanācchatayojanamāyatam || 5-31-14
asyā hetorviśālākṣyāḥ sāgaraṃ vegavān plutaḥ |

14. sampāti vachanāt = by the words of ṣampati; aham = I; vegavān = a swift monkey; plutaḥ = crossed; sāgaram = the ocean; āyatam = having a length; shata yojanam = of one hundred Yojanas; asyāḥ vishalākṣyāḥ hetoḥ = for the sake of this broad eyed ṣeetha.

"By the words of ṣampati, I a swift monkey crossed the ocean having a length of one hundred yojanas for the sake of this broad eyed Seetha."

Sloka 15

yathā rūpām yathā varṇām yathā lakṣmīm viniścitām || 5-31-15
aśrauṣam rāghavasya aham sā iyam āsāditā mayā |

15. iyam = this ṣeetha; asāditā = has been found; mayā = by me; yathā rūpām = according to the comeliness; yathā varṇām = according to the complexion; yathā lakṣmavatīmcha = and according to the bodily marks; sā = the same; aham = I; tām = that ṣeetha; ashrauṣam- had heard; rāghavasya = from Rama.

"ṭhis ṣeetha has been found by me according to the same comeliness, complexion and the bodily marks as I had heard from Rama."

Sloka 16

virarāma evam uktvā asau vācam vānara pumgavaḥ || 5-31-16
jānakī ca api tat śrutvā vismayam paramam gatā |

16. uktvā = speaking; vācham = the words; evam = thus; asau = that; vānarapuṇgavaḥ = ḥanuma; the excellent among the monkeys; virarāma = kept quiet; shrutvā = hearing; tat = those words; jānakīcha api = ṣeetha also; āgatā = got; param = a great; vismayam = surprise.

ḥaving spoken these words, ḥanuma kept quiet. ḥearing those words, ṣeetha too got a great surprise.

Sloka 17

tataḥ sā vakra keśa antā sukeśī keśa samvṛtam || 5-31-17
unnamya vadanam bhīruḥ śiṃśapā vṛkṣam aikṣata |

17. tataḥ = then; sā = that ṣeetha; klesha samvṛita chetanā = whose conciousness was enshrouded by affliction; vakra keshāntā = whose hair-ends were curved; bhīruḥ = and who was of fearful nature; unnamya = lifted; vadanam = her face; aikṣata = and looked towards; shimsupāvṛikṣam = shimshupa tree.

ṭhereafter, that ṣeetha whose consciousness was enshrouded by affliction, whose hair-ends were curved and who was of a fearful nature, lifted her face and looked towards Shimshupa tree.

Sloka 18

niśamya sītā vacanam kapeśca |
diśaśca sarvāḥ pradiśaśca vīkṣya |
svayam praharṣaṃ paramaṃ jagāma |
sarvātmanā rāmamanusmarantī || 5-31-18

18. nishamya = heaving; kapaḥ = ḥanuma's; vachanam = words; vīkṣya = and seeing; sarvāḥ = all; dishashcha = quarters; pradishashcha = and sub-quarters; sītā = ṣeetha; anusmarantī = was recollecting; rāmam = Rama; sarvātmanā = in all ways; jagāma = and obtained; paramam = a great; praharṣam = joy.

ḥearing ḥanuma's words and looking at all quarters and sub-quarters, ṣeetha was recollecting Rama in all ways and obtained a great joy.

Sloka 19

sā tiryag ūrdhvam ca tathā api adhastān |
nirīkṣamāṇā tam acintya buddhim |
dadarśa pinga adhipateḥ amātyam |
vāta ātmajam sūryam iva udayastham || 5-31-19

19. nirīkṣamāṇā = looking at; tiryak = sides; ūrdhavam cha = up; tathāpi = and; adhastāt = down; sā = that ṣeetha; dadarsha = saw; vātātmajam = that ḥanuman; of wind-God the sun; achintya buddhim = having an unimaginable intelligence; amātyam = the minister; piṇgādhipate = of ṣugreeva; sūryamiva = resembling the sun; udayastham = behind an eastern mountain.

Looking at sides, up and down, that ṣeetha saw that ḥanuman, the sun of wind-god, having an unimaginable intelligence, the minister of Sugreeva and looking like the sun behind an eastern mountain.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe ekatriṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 31st chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic
