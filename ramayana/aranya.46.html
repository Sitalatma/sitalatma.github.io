Valmiki Ramayana

Aranya kanda

Book III : Aranya Kanda - The Forest Trek

chapter [Sarga] 46
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Ravana praises Seetha's beauty by extolling every limb of hers, though he enters like a Brahman sanyāsi, a friar. He indulges in a most untoward laudation that is unbefitting for a saintly and elderly almsman. Unable to refute him, because he is in such a pious garb, she had to invite him as an untimely guest.



Sloka 1

tayā paruṣam uktaḥ tu kupito rāghava anujaḥ |
sa vikāṃkṣan bhṛśam rāmam pratasthe na cirāt iva || 3-46-1

1. tayā = by her [ṣeetha]; paruṣam uktaḥ = rudely, who is spoken to [Lakshmana]; saḥ rāghava anujaḥ = he, Raghava's, younger brother; kupitaḥ = is infuriated; bhṛisham = very much; vi kānkṣan = verily, yearning [to see Rama]; na cirāt iva = not, lately [at once]; rāmam pratasthe = towards Rama, journeyed.

Raghava's younger brother Lakshmana is very much infuriated at Seetha who spoke to him rudely, as such he journeyed towards Rama at once as though he is yearning to see Rama immediately, albeit his heart wanted him to stand guard to Seetha. [3-46-1]

Sloka 2

tadā āsādya daśagrīvaḥ kṣipram aṃtaram āsthitaḥ |
abhicakrāma vaidehīm parivrājaka rūpa dhṛk || 3-46-2

2. antaram āsthitaḥ = interval [opportunity,] taking hold of; dasha grīvaḥ = ten, throated [headed one, Ravana]; tadā āsādya = then, on getting it - opportunity; pari vrājaka = roundabout, moving [Brahman] sanyāsi [meandering friar]; rūpa dhṛik [dhṛit] = aspect, adopting [wearing]; kṣipram = quickly; vaidehīm abhicakrāma = to Vaidehi, moved towards - approached.

Quickly taking hold of intervallic time then the ten-headed Ravana approached Vaidehi adopting an aspect of a sanyāsi, a meandering Brahman friar. [3-46-2]

Sloka 3

ślakṣṇa kāṣāya saṃvītaḥ śikhī catrī upānahī |
vāme ca aṃse avasajya atha śubhe yaṣṭi kamaṇḍalū || 3-46-3
parivrājaka rūpeṇa vaidehīm anvavartata |

3, 4a. atha = now; shlakṣṇa kāṣāya samvītaḥ [tat parivrājakaḥ] = smooth [silky,] saffron, enwrapped in [cloaked in] [that sanyāsi is]; shikhī = with tufted head-hair; chatrī = with an umbrella; upānahī = with [wooden] sandals; vāme amse = on left, shoulder; shubhe yaṣṭi kamaṇDalū = auspicious, sanyāsi's shaft, handy water-vessel; avasajya = arranged [hooked - water vessel hooked to hand-shaft]; parivrājaka rūpeṇa = with meandering sanyāsi's, mien; vaidehīm anvavartata = towards Vaidehi, approached to.

That sanyāsi is now cloaked in smooth silky saffron, his head-hair is neatly tufted, sandals are wooden, umbrella is on right shoulder, and on his left shoulder a shaft of sanyāsi is shouldered, onto which his handy water vessel is hooked, and he proceeded towards Vaidehi in a mien of meandering sanyāsi. [3-46-3, 4a]

▼

There are many dress rules to a sanyasi 'the Hindu sanyāsi' where the main items are, as said by Sage Angīrasa: brahma sūtram tridanDam ca vastram jantu nivāraṇam | shikyam pātram bṛisīm caiva kaupīnam kaṭi veṣhṭitam 'sacred thread, three-shafts, saffron clothing, deerskin, handy water vessel, grass-mat, loin-cloth, girdle-cloth... by these he will be known as a real sanyasi 'Hindu sanyāsi.' Out of all these items, the shafts make a difference. Vaishnavaite sanyāsi-s handle three shafts tied into one, and the three are symbolic to cit, acit, īshvara 'animate, inanimate, and lord...' and on this ṃanu says vāk daṇDaḥ ca mano daṇDaḥ kāya daṇDaḥ tathaiva ca | yasya ete nihitā buddhau sa tridaṇDī ca ucya te the word daṇDa means 'repressing, suppression, control...' thus a sanyāsi has to control his 'speech, mind, and body... by that alone he is renowned as sanyasi... ṃanu. As such, Ravana is in the getup of an unmistakable Brahman sanyāsi, with an unbridled tongue.

Sloka 4

tām āsasāda atibalo bhrātṛbhyām rahitām vane || 3-46-4
rahitām sūrya candrābhyām saṃdhyām iva mahat tamaḥ |

4b, 5a. ati balaḥ = great-mighty - Ravana; vane bhrātṛibhyām rahitām = in forest, two brothers, without - she who is - ṣeetha; tām = her; sūrya candrābhyām rahitām = sun, moon [either of them,] without; sandhyām = [lively] eventide; mahat tamaḥ = great, darkness - overpowering an evening; iva = like; Ravana; āsasāda = reached.

As with a pitchiest gloom overcoming the sunless and moonless lively eventide, that great-mighty Ravana came over to Vaidehi in that forest, when she is without two of the brothers, namely Rama and Lakshmana. [3-46-4b, 5a]

Sloka 5

tām apaśyat tato bālām rāja putrīm yaśasvinīm || 3-46-5
rohiṇīm śaśinā hīnām grahavat bhṛśa dāruṇaḥ |

5b, 6a. tataḥ = then; bhṛisha dāruṇaḥ = extremely, sinister [planet, as well as Ravana]; yashasvinīm = glorious one; rāja putrīm = king's, daughter - princess; bālām = of young age - youthful one; tām = her [at ṣeetha]; shashinā hīnām rohiṇīm = by ṃoon, devoid of, at ṣtar Rohini; grahavat = like a [bad] planet; [yathā = as with]; apashyat = [Ravana] has seen.

As a sinister planet looks over Star Rohini when she is devoid of ṃoon, that extremely sinister Ravana then looked over the glorious and youthful princess Seetha. [3-46-5b, 6a]

▼

Rohini is the principle star and dear wife of the ṃoon will be in lunar asterism of the same name, and when she comes in the view of planets like Saturn and ṃars astrology predicts earthly havocs. Hence, Ravana is compared with such a planet and Seetha with Rohini.

Sloka 6

tam ugram pāpa karmāṇam janasthāna gatā drumāḥ || 3-46-6
saṃdṛśya na prakaṃpante na pravāti ca mārutaḥ |

6b, 7a. ugram pāpa karmāṇam = Ravana who is with - ferocity, with iniquitous, activities; tam samdṛishya = him, on observing; janasthāna gatā drumāḥ = in Janasthāna, gone in [existing in,] trees; na pra kampante = are not, in full, sway; mārutaḥ ca = wind, also; na pra vāti = not, in full, swing.

On observing ferocious Ravana whose activities are iniquitous, the trees of Janasthāna are not in full sway, nor the wind is in full swing. [3-46-6b, 7a]

Sloka 7

śīghra srotāḥ ca tam dṛṣṭvā vīkṣaṃtam rakta locanam || 3-46-7
stimitam gaṃtum ārebhe bhayāt godāvarī nadī |

7b, 8a. shīghra srotāḥ ca = rapid, with onrushes, also; godāvarī nadī = Godavari, river; rakta locanam vīkṣantam = with bloodshot, eyes, one who is looking about; tam dṛiṣṭvā = him, on seeing; bhayāt = from fright; stimitam gantum ārebhe = quietly, to go [to rush off,] started to.

On seeing him who is seeing with his bloodshot eyes, River Godavari too is frightened and started to rush off quietly, whose onrush will be rapid otherwise. [3-46-7b, 8a]

▼

The reaction of the trees, wind, and river is as said by gods to Brahma in Bala Kanda, at 1-15-10: "Surya, the Sun will not verily parch Ravana. ṃārutha, the Air will not surge at his sides. On seeing Ravana, the lord of the undulating, drifting waves, Ocean... will not pulsate either, and will hide the undulating waves, for the waves are his wives, and Ravana may abduct them... like Seetha."

Sloka 8

rāmasya tu aṃtaram prepsuḥ daśagrīvaḥ tat aṃtare || 3-46-8
upatasthe ca vaidehīm bhikṣu rūpeṇa rāvaṇaḥ |

8b, 9a. rāmasya tu = Rama's, but - some [lapse or the other]; antaram prepsuḥ = lapse - misstep, waiting for; dashagrīvaḥ = ten, throated one - Decahedron; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; tat antare = in that, meanwhile; bhikṣu rūpeṇa = sanyāsi, in the getup of; vaidehīm upatasthe = to Vaidehi neared.

In the meanwhile the Decahedron demon Ravana, who is all the while waiting for some lapse of Rama, neared Vaidehi in the getup of a sanyāsi. [3-46-8b, 9a]

Sloka 9

abhavyo bhavya rūpeṇa bhartāram anuśocatīm || 3-46-9
abhyavartata vaidehīm citrām iva śanaiścaraḥ |

9b, 10a. a bhavyaḥ = not, proper - disgraceful Ravana; bhartāram anushocatīm vaidehīm = of husband, who is thinking over, to Vaidehi; bhavya rūpeṇa = proper, in mien [graceful mien of a Brahman almsman]; shanaiḥ caraḥ = slower, mover - slow moving ṣaturn; citrām iva = to ṣtar Citra, as with; abhyavartata = made advances to.

That disgraceful Ravana made advances in a graceful mien of Brahman almsman to Vaidehi who is thinking over her husband, like the slow moving Saturn making advance to Star Citra which will be ominous. [3-46-9b, 10a]

Sloka 10

sahasā bhavya rūpeṇa tṛṇaiḥ kūpa iva āvṛtaḥ || 3-46-10
atiṣṭhat prekṣya vaidehīm rāma patnīm yaśasvinīm |

10b, 11a. kūpaḥ tṛiṇaiḥ [āvṛitaḥ] iva = wellspring, by grass, shrouded, like; bhavya rūpeṇa āvṛitaḥ = decent, in getup, enshrouded [Ravana]; yashasvinīm rāma patnīm vaidehīm = admirable, Rama, wife of, Vaidehi; prekṣya = seeing her [leering her]; sahasā atiṣṭhat = in a trice, stood - in her fore.

Like a wellspring shrouded by grass, enshrouded in a decent getup of a Brahman friar, in a trice Ravana leeringly stood before of Vaidehi, the admirable wife of Rama. [3-46-10b, 11a]

▼

This idiomatic usage of tṛiṇa kūpa which draws upon straw, is held high by Dr. Satya Vrat, which is repeated often in Ramayana. He says 'Probably Kalidasa too was inspired by this very expression of Valmiki when he put it in the mouth of Shakuntala as she was being repudiated by Dushyanta: ko anyo dharma kancuka praveshinaḥ tṛiṇa kūpa upama asya tava anukāri bhaviṣhyati - abhijñāna shākuntalam Act 5.

Sloka 11

tiṣṭan saṃprekṣya ca tadā patnīm rāmasya rāvaṇa || 3-46-11
śubhām rucira danta oṣṭhīm pūrṇa candra nibha ānanām |
āsīnām parṇaśālāyām bāṣpa śoka abhipīḍitām || 3-46-12
sa tām padma palāśa akṣīm pīta kauśeya vāsinīm |
abhyagacchata vaidehīm hṛṣṭa cetā niśā caraḥ || 3-46-13

11b, 12. tadā = then; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; rāmasya patnīm = at Rama's, wife; tiṣṭan sam prekshya = while standing, eyed; hṛiṣṭa [duṣṭa] cetā = gladdened [wicked,] hearted one; saḥ nishā caraḥ = he, night, walker [Ravana]; shubhām = at her - auspicious one; rucira danta oṣṭhīm = having fine, teeth, lips; pūrṇa candra nibha ānanām = full, moon, shiny, visaged one; parṇa shālāyām āsīnām = in straw, cottage, who is seated; bāṣpa shoka abhipīDitām = tears, lamentation, tortured by; padma palāsha akṣīm = lotus, petal, eyed one; pīta kausheya vāsinīm = ochry, silks, wearing; tām vaidehīm abhyagacchata = at her, Vaidehi, approached.

While standing before her then Ravana eyed at the auspicious wife of Rama, whose lips and teeth are fine, visage is shiny like a full moon, and who is presently tortured by her tearful lamentation and sitting in the straw-cottage, and he approached that lotus-petal eyed Vaidehi who is dressed in ochry silks, as that night-walker is gladdened at his heart for her solitude. [3-46-11b, 12,13]

Sloka 14

dṛṣṭvā kāma śara āviddho brahma ghoṣam udīrayan |
abravīt praśritam vākyam rahite rākṣasa adhipaḥ || 3-46-14

14. kāma shara āviddhaḥ = Love-god, arrows, hit by; rākṣasa adhipaḥ = demons, king of; dṛiṣṭvā = on seeing - her, ṣeetha; brahma ghoṣam udīrayan = Vedic-hymns [said by Brahma,] resonances, uttering [resonating]; [jana] rahite = [people,] without, [to one without companions, to ṣeetha in solitariness]; pra shritam vākyam abravīt = well, mannered, sentences, spoke.

He who is hit by the arrows of Love-god that king of demons Ravana, on seeing Seetha entered resonating the resonances of Vedic-hymns as precursory in seeking alms, and spoke these well-mannered sentences to her who is in solitariness. [3-46-14]

Sloka 15

tām uttamām trilokānām padma hīnām iva śriyam |
vibhrājamānām vapuṣā rāvaṇaḥ praśaśaṃsa ha || 3-46-15

15. tri lokānām = in three, worlds; uttamām = excellent one [ṣeetha]; padma hīnām shriyam iva = Divine-lotus, devoid of, Goddess Lakshmi, [just] like; vapuṣā vibhrājamānām = with body [beauty of body, ] beaming forth; tām rāvaṇaḥ prashashamsa ha = her, Ravana, acclaimed, really.

Ravana really acclaimed her who is an excellent one in all the three worlds, beaming forth with the beauty of her body, and who is like Goddess Lakshmi but for Divine-lotus as Her settle, for Seetha is devoid of her settling, namely Rama. [3-46-15]

Sloka 16

kā tvam kāṃcana varṇa ābhe pīta kauśeya vāsini |
kamalānām śubhām mālām padminī iva ca bibhratī || 3-46-16

16. kāncana varṇa ābhe = oh, one with - gold's, glitter, glittering; pīta kausheya vāsini = ochry, in silks, silkened; padminī iva kamalānām = lotus-tendril, like, of lotuses; shubhām mālām bibhratī = divine, garland [you wear]; kā tvam = who, you are.

"Glittering like the glitter of gold, silkened in ochry silks, you are like a lotus-tendril garlanded with divine lotuses as your ensemble, who are you?" Thus Ravanan started addressing Seetha [3-46-16]

▼

The beautiful outfits on this lotus-stalk like Seetha are her mukha kamala, netra kamala, kara kamala, pāda kamala... 'lotus-eyes, lotus-face, lotus-stalk like hands and lotus-feet and other limbs...' The word padmini also means a 'lake' besides the runner of lotus plant. The first compound is also read as: raupya kāncana ābhā meaning 'shining like silver and gold...' but it is in limited mms and with very limited use.

Sloka 17

hrīḥ śrīḥ kīrtiḥ śubhā lakṣmīḥ apsarā vā śubha ānane |
bhūtir vā tvam varāarohe ratir vā svaira cāriṇī || 3-46-17

17. shubha ānane = oh, rosy, faced one; varāarohe = oh, curvacious one; tvam = [are] you; hrīḥ kīrtiḥ shrīḥ = [personified] Respect, or Renown, or Resplendence; shubhā lakṣmīḥ vā = felicitous, Lakshmi, or; apsarā = or [nymphal] apsara; bhūtiḥ vā = or Benefactress, or; svaira cāriṇī = or self, moving [motivated woman]; ratiḥ vā = Rati, or.

"Oh, rosy faced one, are you the personified numen of Respect, Renown or Resplendence, or the Felicitous Lakshmi herself, or oh, curvaceous one, are you a nymphal Apsara, or the numen of Benefactress, or a self-motivated woman, or Rati Devi, the consort of ṃanmatha, the Love God. [3-46-17]

▼

These three attributes are as informed by gods to Vishnu about the three wives of Dasharatha at 1-15-20, as: hrī, Hṛillekha, virtue, modesty, uprightness... shrī, shrīkāra affluence, prosperity and also suggestive of resplendence, radiance of that prosperity or effluence and kīrti fame, renown, glory... a bhūti, vibhūti is accordance, give-all, fortune. The ash applied on forehead in worships is to be applied with a verse bhūtir bhūtikar īshvarī... and for each of these attributes, a presiding deity is there with the same name. Here the hrī is also taken to mean bhū devi 'ṃother Earth' another wife of Vishnu. The addressing varārohe etc., are the attributes of the addressees as a beautiful woman in the group of: varārohā, matta kāshinī, uttamā, vara varṇinīi - amara kosha

Sloka 18

samāḥ śikhariṇaḥ snigdhāḥ pāṇḍurā daśanāḥ tava |
viśāle vimale netre raktānte kṛṣṇa tārake || 3-46-18

18. tava dashanāḥ = your, teeth; samāḥ = are evenly; shikhariṇaḥ = have tips like jasmine buds; snigdhāḥ = smooth; pāṇDurā = whitely; netre vishāle vimale = eyes, broad, spotless; rakta ante = red, at ends; kṛiṣṇa tārake = black, starred [irises, pupils are black.]

"Your teeth are evenly, smooth and their tips are like jasmine buds, and your whitish broad eyes are spotless, reddish at ends, and pupils are black. [3-46-18]

Sloka 19

viśālam jaghanam pīnam ūrū kari kara upamau |
etau upacitau vṛttau saṃhatau saṃpragalbhitau || 3-46-19
pīna unnata mukhau kāntau snigdha tāla phala upamau |
maṇi praveka ābharaṇau rucirau te payo dharau || 3-46-20

19, 20. vishālam jaghanam = beamy, hips; pīnam ūrū = burly, thighs; kari kara upamau = elephant's, hand [trunk,] akin to; te = your; etau = these; maṇi praveka ābharaṇau = those that have - jewellery, best, ornamented; payaḥ dharau = milk, bearers - two breasts; upa citau = butting [rubbing each other]; vṛittau = rotund; sam hatau = well, bumping against each; sam pragalbhitau [sam pra valgitau] = verily, unobtainable [very, well, swinging up]; pīna unnata mukhau = brawny, jutting out, faces [of breasts - nipples]; kāntau = covetable; snigdha tāla phala upamau = smooth, palm, fruit, like; rucirau = beautiful.

"Your hips are beamy, thighs burly akin to elephant's trunks, and these two breasts of yours that are ornamented with best jewellery are rotund, rubbing and bumping each other, and they are swinging up and up, their nipples are brawny and jutting out, and they are smoothish like palm-fruits, thus they are covetable for they are beautiful. [3-46-19, 20]

Sloka 21

cāru smite cāru dati cāru netre vilāsini |
mano harasi me rāme nadī kūlam iva aṃbhasā || 3-46-21
karāntamita madhyā asi sukeśī saṃhata stanī |

21, 22a. cāru smite = oh, on who has - alluring, smile; cāru dati = alluring, teeth; cāru netre = alluring, eyes; vilāsini = oh, allurer; rāme = oh, beautiful lady [or, Rāmā, the female form of Rama]; nadī ambhasā kūlam iva = river, by water [by spate,] riverbank [eroding,] as with; me manaḥ harasi = my, soul, you are robbing; kara anta mita madhyā = hand, inside, limitable, waisted - a palmful is your waist; su keshī = good, head-hair [velvety hair]; samhata stanī asi = - jostling, breasted one, you are - breasts extrusive to each other.

"Oh, allurer, your smile is alluring, teeth are alluring, and your eyes allure, oh, beauty, your waist is palmful, your hair velvety, your breasts are jostling, and you rob my soul as a spate robs riverbank. [3-46-21, 22a]

Sloka 22

na eva devī na gandharvī na yakṣī na ca kiṃnarī || 3-46-22
na evam rūpā mayā nārī dṛṣṭa pūrvā mahī tale |

22b, 23a. mayā = by me; mahī tale = on earth's, face; evam rūpā nārī = this kind of, featured, woman [earthly female]; na dṛiṣṭa pūrvā = not, seen, earlier [hither to]; devī = a goddess; na eva = not [seen,] in that way; gandharvī na = gandharva female, no; yakṣī na = yaksha female, no; kinnarī na = kinnara female, no.

"I have not hitherto seen any earthly female with this kind of features on the face of earth; or, a goddess - no; a gandharva female - no; yaksha female - no; kinnarā female - no, none whosoever! [3-46-22b, 23a]

Sloka 23

rūpam agryam ca lokeṣu saukumāryam vayaḥ ca te || 3-46-23
iha vāsaḥ ca kāṃtāre cittam unmathayanti me |

23b, 24a. lokeṣu agryam te rūpam = in [three] worlds, excellent, by features; saukumāryam vayaḥ ca = with fragility, youthfulness, also; iha kāntāre vāsaḥ ca = here, in thick of forest, living, also; me cittam unmathayanti [unmādayati] = my, heart, highly stirring up [maddening, I marvel why.]

"I marvel why an excellent one in all the three worlds by her features, more so, fragile and youthful as yourself should be living here in the thick of forest. [3-46-23b, 24, 25a]

Sloka 24

sā pratikrāma bhadram te na tvam vastum iha arhasi || 3-46-24
rākṣasānām ayam vāso ghorāṇām kāma rūpiṇām |

25b, 26a. sā = such as you are; prati krāma = return, you go; te bhadram = to you, safety betides; iha tvam vastum na arhasi = here, to live, you, not, suitable for you [inapt of you]; ayam = this [forest]; ghorāṇām kāma rūpiṇām = for ghastly ones, by wish, guise-changers; rākṣasānām vāsaḥ = for demons, dwelling-place.

Such as you are you go back, let safety betide you, as you are unsuited to live in here, for this forest is the dwelling for ghastly demons who by their wish will be changing their guise. [3-46-24b, 25a]

Sloka 25

prāsāda agrāṇi ramyāṇi nagara upavanāni ca || 3-46-25
saṃpannāni sugandhīni yuktāni ācaritum tvayā |

25b, 26a. prāsāda agrāṇi ramyāṇi = skyscrapers, rooftops, delightful ones; sampannāni = flourishing ones; sugandhīni = richly fragranced ones; nagara upavanāni ca = city, pleasure-gardens, also - they alone; tvayā ācaritum yuktāni = by you, to saunter, befitting.

"Delightful rooftops of skyscrapers, flourishing and richly fragranced pleasure-gardens of cities alone are meetly to you to saunter. [3-46-25b, 26a]

Sloka 26

varam mālyam varam gaṃdham varam vastram ca śobhane || 3-46-26
bhartāram ca varam manye tvat yuktam asitekṣaṇe |

26b, 27a. a sita īkṣaṇe = not, white, one with eyes - oh, one with blackish eyes; shobhane = oh, beauteous one; varam mālyam = best, garlands; varam gandham [bhojyam] = best, scent [foodstuffs]; varam vastram ca = best, clothing, also; varam bhartāram ca = best, husband, also; tvat yuktam = to you, meetly; manye = I opine.

Oh, beauteous lady, I opine that only best garlands, best scents, best foodstuffs, best clothing are meetly to you, oh, lady with black pupils, hence it is meet and right to you to have a best husband.

Or

"Getting at you, the garlands, scents, foodstuffs, or clothing, they all become the best, because you are the best beauteous one, as such, he alone becomes a best person who gets you as his wife, and it is meet and right to you to choose such a husband, [aside: me alone.] [3-46-26b, 27a]

Sloka 27

kā tvam bhavasi rudrāṇām marutām vā śucismite || 3-46-27
vasūnām vā varāarohe devatā pratibhāsi me |

27b, 28a. shuci smite = oh, one with impeccable smile; varāarohe = oh, lay with best midriff - a curvaceous one; tvam kā bhavasi = you, who, are; rudrāṇām = among Rudrā-gods; marutām vā = of ṃarut-gods, or; vasūnām vā = of Vasu-s, or; me devatā pratibhāsi = to me, as goddess, you are beaming forth to me.

"Oh, curvaceous lady, who are you? Oh, one with an impeccable smile, do you belong to Rudrā-gods, or ṃarut-gods, or to Vasu-s? To me you are beaming forth like a goddess. [3-46-27b, 28a]

▼

Vividly: 'your kind of female is unspotted by me among humans, so you must be some goddess, but I have marked all gods and there I did not find you. This province belongs to demons. As such, gods, gandharva-s, kinnarā-s and others cannot enter this place, let alone the gods from Shiva's side, because Shiva is the Ultimate for demons. If so, do you belong to ekā dasha rudrā-s eleven Rudrā-s? Or, because Air can circulate here, do you belong to any of the forty-nine marut gaṇā-s, Wind-gods? Or, because earth has ancient connections with aṣṭa vasu-s, eight-Vasu-s, do you belong to any one of them?' Further, Seetha is not smiling throughout this chapter as her mind is set on her husband's safety. Nevertheless Ravana assumes that if Seetha smiles, that smile will be impeccable.

Sloka 28

na iha gacchaṃti gaṃdharvā na devā na ca kinnarāḥ || 3-46-28
rākṣasānām ayam vāsaḥ katham tu tvam iha āgatā |

28b, 29a. iha gandharvā na gacchanti = here, gandharva-s, not, they go - arrive; devā na = gods, no; kinnarāḥ ca na = kinnarā-s, also, no; ayam rākṣasānām vāsaḥ = this is, for demons, dwelling-place; tvam katham iha āgatā = you, how, to here, have come.

"This is the dwelling-place of demons, and no goddess, nor a gandharva, nor a kinnara can arrive here, but how you have come here? [3-46-28b, 29a]

Sloka 29

iha śākhāmṛgāḥ siṃhā dvīpi vyāghra mṛgāḥ tathā || 3-46-29
ṛkṣāḥ tarakṣavaḥ kaṃkāḥ katham tebhyo na bibhyase |

29b, 30a. iha = here; shākh āmṛigāḥ = tree-branch, animals [simians]; simhā dvīpi vyāghra = lions, leopards, tigers; tathā = likewise; mṛigāḥ = predators; ṛikṣāḥ tarakṣavaḥ kankāḥ [caranti] = bears, tigers, vultures, [will be moving]; tebhyaḥ katham na bibhyase = from them, how, not, frightened you are.

"Here, simians, lions, leopards, tigers, likewise other predators, bears, tigers, vultures will be moving. How you are not frightened from them? [3-46-29b, 30a]

Sloka 30

mada anvitānām ghorāṇām kuṃjarāṇām tarasvinām || 3-46-30
katham ekā mahāraṇye na bibheṣi varāanane |

30b, 31a. vara ānane = oh, best faced one; mahā araṇye = in great forest; mada anvitānām = rut, possessing; ghorāṇām = horrendous ones; tarasvinām = mighty ones; kunjarāṇām = from [such] elephants; ekā katham na bibheṣi = a lonely one, how, not, you are scared.

"Being alone in this forest, oh, lady with a lovely face, how you are unafraid of horrendous and mighty elephants possessed of rut? [3-46-30b, 31a]

Sloka 31

kā asi kasya kutaḥ ca tvam kim nimittam ca daṇḍakān || 3-46-31
ekā carasi kalyāṇi ghorān rākṣasa sevitān |

31, 32b. kalyāṇi = oh, auspicious lady; tvam = you; kā asi = who, are you; kasya = whose - to whom you belong; kutaḥ ca = from where, also- you have come; rākṣasa sevitān ghorān daṇDakān = by demons, adored, dreadful, Dandaka forest; ekā = lonely; kim nimittam carasi = for what, reason, you move about.

"Oh, auspicious lady, who are you? Whose are you? Also from where are you? And being lonely, what for you are moving in dreadful Dandaka forest that is adored by demons?" Thus Ravana spoke to Seetha. [3-46-31, 32b]

Sloka 32

iti praśastā vaidehī rāvaṇena durātmanā - mahātmanā- || 3-46-32
dvijāti veṣeṇa hi tam dṛṣṭvā rāvaṇam āgatam |
sarvaiḥ atithi satkāraiḥ pūjayāmāsa maithilī || 3-46-33

32b, 33. durātmanā [mahātmanā] = by wicked minded one, [by great-souled Ravana]; rāvaṇena = by Ravana; iti prashastā = thus, praised; maithilī vaidehī = one from ṃithila, Vaidehi; dvijāti veṣeṇa āgatam = by twice-born [Brahman,] with getup, one who arrived; tam rāvaṇam dṛiṣṭvā = him, at Ravana, on seeing; sarvaiḥ atithi satkāraiḥ = with all, [casual] guest, [affordable] respects; pūjayāmāsa = she started to revere.

When the wicked minded Ravana praised her in this way, Vaidehi the princess from ṃithila has seen him who has arrived in the getup of Brahman and started to revere him with all the respects affordable to a casual guest. [3-46-32b, 33]

Sloka 34

upānīya āsanam pūrvam pādyena abhinimaṃtrya ca |
abravīt siddham iti eva tadā tam saumya darśanam || 3-46-34

34. tadā = then; pūrvam āsanam upānīya = firstly, seat, on fetching; pādyena abhinimantrya ca = with water, invited, also; saumya darshanam = he who has - gracious, bearing; [bhiksha] siddham iti eva = [food, alms] ready, thus, only; abravīt = said to him.

She firstly fetched a seat him, then invited him with water for feet-wash, and then said to him who looked gracious in his bearing, 'alms are ready.' [3-46-34]

Sloka 35

dvijāti veṣeṇa samīkṣya maithilī
tam āgatam pātra kusuṃbha dhāriṇam |
aśakyam uddveṣṭum upāya darśanān
nyamaṃtrayat brāhmaṇavat yathā āgatam || 3-46-35

35. tadā = then; maithilī = ṃaithili; dvijāti veṣeṇa = in Brahman's, getup of; āgatam = who has arrived; pātra kusumbha dhāriṇam = a vessel [alms-bowl,] saffron-robe [or, kamaDulu, handy vessel, as the word kusumbha means doubly, robe is taken here,] wearing; ud dveṣṭum = to flatly refute [irrefutable appearance]; a shakyam = not, possible one - not a rejectable one; upāya [a] darshanān [apāya a darshanam] = idea [reason,] [not,] found [danger, not, seen = one in an unimperilling appearance]; samīkṣya = on exploring - him;brāhmaṇa vat = a Brahman, like; yathā āgatam = as, he came nyamantrayat = invited.

ṃaithili explored him who arrived with the features of a Brahman, with a saffron-robe and alms-bowl, and finding no reason to refute him flatly, as he has come like a Brahman, she invited him as a Brahman.

Or

ṃaithili explored him who arrived with the features of a Brahman, with a saffron-robe and alms-bowl, and as he came with an unimperilling appearance of a Brahman, she invited him as she would ordinarily invite a real Brahman. [3-46-35]

▼

Did she recognise Ravana as a pseudo-Brahman or not... is the doubt raised by this verse, because different texts use different compounds apāya a darshanam.... upāya a darshanam etc. It is concluded that she, for sure, identified him as a pseudo-Brahman, because she has seen many such sanyasi-s while she was in ṃithila. Hence she is said here as ṃaithili because she hails from ṃithila and her father's court was full with these sanyasi-s, yati-s, parivrājaka-s. Then it is questioned as to why she invited him, though recognised him as a pseudo-Brahman. For this Govindaraja says: kāṣāya daṇDa mātreṇa yatiḥ pūjyo na samshayaḥ | 'By spotting the saffron robe alone, a sanyasi is venerable...'

▼

Then, another doubt is raised as to how she noticed him to be pseudo- sanyasi . For this it is said rātri advānam yānam ca strī kathā laulyam eva ca | mancakam shukla vastram yatīnām patanāni Saṭ || 'ṃeandering during night, using vehicles, showing interest in episodes relating to women, and relations with women, using a cot to sleep, wearing white cloths... these six show the downfall of his sanyāsi-hood...' Ravana is indulged in strī kathā in the whole of this chapter, in admiring at her lips, breasts, thighs etc., and no usual swasti vacana, shubha āshīs 'blessings...' are uttered by him and thus Seetha could discern him to be a pseudo- sanyasi.

▼

Though knowing him as such, why should she revere him at all, is another question. What else she could do as his appearance itself is uddveṣṭum a+shakyam 'irrefutable appearance of a Brahman sanyasi...' hence, somehow some time is to be gained until the arrival of Rama or Lakshmana. Otherwise, this pseudo-sanyasi may cause any pseudo-problem that she cannot solve. For this, the reply is at the word in the last verse haritam dadarsha... 'only greenery is seen by her not Rama nor Lakshmana...' And in the next verse itself she is addressing him as a "Brahman...' but not with the titles like sanyasi or yatīshvara, paramahamsa, parivrājaka etc. A Brahman alone is eligible to take up sanyāsa 'Hindu-sanyāsi-hood...' not Kshatriya or others. Hence, she concluded him to be Brahman. When once the sanyāsi-hood is taken he is no more a Brahman and his bodily being neutralises in the Absolute, and such a sanyāsi shall drag on his body, without comfortable bodily needs, until his natural death. But Seetha is addressing him contrary to his stance.

Sloka 36

iyam bṛsī brāhmaṇa kāmam āsyatām
idam ca pādyam pratigṛhyatām iti |
idam ca siddham vana jātam uttamam
tvat artham avyagram iha upabhujyatām 3-46-36

36. brāhmaṇa = oh, Brahman; iyam bṛisī = this is, Darbha-mat; kāmam āsyatām = comfortably, be seated; idam pādyam pratigṛihyatām = here is, water, receive it; iti = thus on saying; idam uttamam vana jātam = here is, best, in forest, produce [fruits, tubers, victuals]; tvat artham siddham = for your, sake, ready; iha avyagram upabhujyatām = now, relaxedly, be dined on.

"Oh, Brahman, this is the Darbha-grass-seat and you be seated comfortably, here is water, receive it for hand and feet wash, here is the forest grown victuals for your sake... dine on them relaxedly..." Thus Seetha said to Ravana. [3-46-36]

Sloka 37

nimaṃtryamāṇaḥ pratipūrṇa bhāṣiṇīm
narendra patnīm prasamīkṣya maithilīm |
prasahya tasyā haraṇe dhṛḍham manaḥ
samarpayāmāsa ātma vadhāya rāvaṇaḥ || 3-46-37

37. nimantryamāṇaḥ = one who is being invited; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; prati pūrṇa bhāṣiṇīm = in turn, thoroughly, talking [talking civilities contextually]; narendra patnīm maithilīm = king's, wife, at ṃaithili; pra samīkṣya = on thoroughly, observing; [sva] ātma vadhāya = [his own,] himself, for destruction of; prasahya = [even] forcibly; tasyā haraṇe = in her, abduction; manaḥ dhṛiDham samarpayāmāsa = mind, firmly, started to dedicate.

Ravana thoroughly observed that wife of the king, ṃaithili, when she invited him talking civilities contextual in inviting a guest, that too Brahman sanyasi, and he firmed up his mind to abduct her even forcibly, and started to dedicate himself for destruction. [3-46-37]

Sloka 38

tataḥ suveṣam mṛgayā gatam patim
pratīkṣamāṇā saha lakṣmaṇam tadā |
nirīkṣamāṇā haritam dadarśa tat
mahad vanam na eva tu rāma lakṣmaṇau || 3-46-38

38. tataḥ = then; su veṣam = beautiful, bodied one - shapely Rama; mṛigayā gatam = for hunting, who has gone; saha lakṣmaṇam = with Lakshmana - one who ought to return with Lakshmana, or, Lakshmana who might be returning; patim prati ikṣamāṇā = husband, towards, one who is waiting for - ṣeetha; tadā = thus; nirīkṣamāṇā [vi vīkkshamāṇā] = gazing at [or, seeing all sides]; haritam tat mahat vanam dadarsha = greenery, that, great, forest's, she saw; rāma lakṣmaṇau tu = Rama, Lakshmana, but; na eva = not, thus - she saw.

Then she looked forward for her shapely husband who has gone on a hunting game, and who must be returning along with Lakshmana, and though she looked intently she could only see the green backdrop of that great forest, but not Rama or Lakshmana. [3-46-38]

- - - - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye araṇya kāṇḍe ṣaṭ catvārinśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus, this is the 46th chapter in Aranya Kanda of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic poem of India.
