Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 77
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

On the twelfth day of the death of Dasaratha, Bharata gives various presents to Brahmans. On the thirteenth day when Bharata and Shatrughna go to the cremation ground to collect the bones both of them are overwhelmed with grief and fall down on the ground. Vasista, the family priest and Sumantra raise them up and pacifying urge both of them to complete the remaining rites to be performed on the thirteenth day.

Sloka 1

tataḥ daśa ahe atigate kṛta śauco nṛpa ātmajaḥ |
dvādaśe ahani samprāpte śrāddha karmāṇi akārayat || 2-77-1

1. tataḥ = thereafter; atigate = having passed; dashāhe = the tenth day; kṛita nṛpātmajaḥ shouchaḥ = Bharata; who performed purification; akārayat = got done; shrāddha karmāṇi = ṣhraddha* rites; sampāpte = on the arrival of; dvādase = twelfth; ahani = day.

After having passed the tenth day of the death of Dasaratha, Bharata having himself purified, performed Shraddha* rites on the arrival of the twelfth day.

* Shraddha rites are in the honor and for the benefit of dead relatives observed with great strictness at various fixed periods and on the occasion of rejoicing as well as mourning by the surviving relatives. These are performed by the daily offering of water and on the stated occasion by the offering of Pindas or balls of rice and meal to three paternal forefathers i.e. Father, grandfather, and great grandfather. It is an act of reverential homage to a deceased person performed by relatives, most desirably by a son of the deceased.

Sloka 2

brāhmaṇebhyo dadau ratnam dhanam annam ca puṣkalam |
vāsāṃsi ca mahārhāṇi ratnāni vividhāni ca || 2-77-2

2. (Bharata) dadou = gave; ratnam = precious stones; dhanam = money; pushhkalam = a lot of; annam = cooked rice; mahārhāṇi = very valuable; vāsāmsi = clothes; vividhāni = various kinds of ; ratnāni = presents; brāhmaṇebhyaḥ = to brahmans.

On the occasion of Shraddha rites, Bharata gave precious stones, money, a lot of cooked rice, very valuable clothes and various other kinds of presents to Brahmans.

Sloka 3

bāstikam bahu śuklam ca gāḥ ca api śataśaḥ tathā |
dāsī dāsam ca yānam ca veśmāni sumahānti ca || 2-77-3
brāhmaṇebhyo dadau putraḥ rājñaḥ tasya aurdhvadaihikam |

3. tasya = in that; ourdhvadehikam = ceremony performed in honor of the dead; rājṇ^aḥ = king; putraḥ = Bharata the son of Dasaratha; dadou = gave; bahu = many; shuklam = white; bāstikam = multitudes of goats; tathā = and; shatashaḥ = hundreds of ; gāshchāpi = cows; dāsī dāsamcha = servants and servant maids; yānāmcha = vehicles; sumahānti = and very big; veshmānicha = houses; brāhmaṇebhyaḥ = to brahmans.

In that ceremony performed in honor of the dead king, Bharata the son of Dasaratha gave multitudes of white goats, hundreds of cows, servants and servant maids, vehicles and very big houses to Brahmans.

Sloka 4

tataḥ prabhāta samaye divase atha trayodaśe || 2-77-4
vilalāpa mahā bāhur bharataḥ śoka mūrcitaḥ |
śabda apihita kaṇṭhaḥ ca śodhana artham upāgataḥ || 2-77-5
citā mūle pitur vākyam idam āha suduhkhitaḥ |

4; 5. tataḥ = then; prabhāta samaye = at the time of dawn; trayodashe = on the thirteenth; divase = day; mahābāhuḥ = the mighty armed; bharataḥ = Bharata; upāgataḥ = who came; shodanārtham = for clearing up bones and ashes; atha = thereafter; vilalāpa = wept; shokamūrchhitaḥ = stunned by grief; shabdāpihitakaṇ^ṭhaḥ = his throat being choked; aha = spoke; idam vākyam = these words; suduḥkhitah = greatly distressed as he was; chitāmūle = having reached the foot of his father's funeral pile (to collect the bones and ashes for their immersion in the holy ṣarayu river.)

Then, at the time of dawn on the thirteenth day, the mighty armed Bharata who came for clearing up bones and ashes, wept in grief, his throat being choked of sound (because of crying) and spoke in great distress the following words, having reached the foot of his father's funeral pile (to collect the bones and ashes for their immersion in Sarayu River).

Sloka 6

tāta yasmin niṣṛṣṭaḥ aham tvayā bhrātari rāghave || 2-77-6
tasmin vanam pravrajite śūnye tyaktaḥ asmy aham tvayā |

6. tāta = O; father! Yasmin = to which; bhrātari = brother; rāghave = Rama; aham = I; nisṛishhṭaḥ = have been entrusted; tvayā = by you; tasmin = that Rama; pravrajite = having gone to exile; vanam = forest; aham = I; asmi = became; tyaktaḥ = abandoned; shūnye = wholly alone; tvayā = by you.

"O, father! ṃy brother Rama to whose care I was entrusted by you, having gone on exile to the forest, I now become abandoned wholly alone, by you."

Sloka 7

yathā gatir anāthāyāḥ putraḥ pravrājitaḥ vanam || 2-77-7
tām ambām tāta kausalyām tyaktvā tvam kva gataḥ nṛpa |

7. nṛipa = O; king!; tāta = O; father! kva = where; gataḥ = where you have gone; tyaktvā = leaving; kausalyām = Kausalya; tām = such; ambām = a mother; yasyāḥ = whose; putraḥ = son; gatiḥ = the support; pravrājitah = has been sent away from home; vanam = the forest?

"O, king! O, father! Where have you gone, leaving Kausalya the mother whose son Rama, her support, has been sent away from home to the forest?"

Sloka 8

dṛṣṭvā bhasma aruṇam tac ca dagdha asthi sthāna maṇḍalam || 2-77-8
pituḥ śarīra nirvāṇam niṣṭanan viṣasāda ha |

8. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tat = that; sthānamaṇ^Dalam = spot of collection; bhasmāruṇam = which became reddish brown in colour because of ashes; dagdhāsthi = with bones consumed by fire; pituḥ = and his father's; sharīra nirvāṇam = body extinguished; saḥ = Bharataḥ; vishhasāda = was depressed; nishhṭanan = and began to cry loudly.

Seeing that spot of collection (of bones), which became reddish brown in colour because of ashes, with bones consumed by fire and his father's body having extinguished, Bharata was depressed and began to cry loudly.

Sloka 9

sa tu dṛṣṭvā rudan dīnaḥ papāta dharaṇī tale || 2-77-9
utthāpyamānaḥ śakrasya yantra dhvajaiva cyutaḥ |

9. dīnaḥ = miserable as he was; dṛIshhṭvā = to see; (the spot); saḥ tu = that Bharata; papāta = fell; rudan = weeping; dharaṇītale = on the ground; yantradhvajaḥ iva = as a flagstaff fastened by any mechanical contrivance; chyutaḥ = would drop; utthāpyamānaḥ = while being raised; shakrasya = in honor of Indra the god of celestials.

ṃiserable as he was to see the spot, Bharata fell weeping on the ground, as a flag staff fastened by any mechanical contrivance would drop down while being raised in honor of Indra the god of celestials.

Sloka 10

abhipetus tataḥ sarve tasya amātyāḥ śuci vratam || 2-77-10
anta kāle nipatitam yayātim ṛṣayo yathā |

10. tataḥ = then; sarve = all; tasya amātyāḥ = his ministers; abhipetuḥ = quickly approached Bharata; shuchivratam = virtuous in conduct; ṛishhayo yathā = as sages; (rushed to) nipatitam = the fallen; yayātim = Yayati; antakāle = at the time of his death.

All his ministers hastily approached Bharata who was virtuous in conduct, as sages once rushed to the fallen Yayati at the time of his death.

Sloka 11

śatrughnaḥ ca api bharatam dṛṣṭvā śoka pariplutam || 2-77-11
visamjño nyapatat bhūmau bhūmi pālam anusmaran |

11. dṛishhṭvā = seeing; bharatam = Bharata; shatrughnashohāpi = ṣhatrughna also; shokapariplutaḥ = was overwhelmed with grief; anusmaran = and while recollecting; bhūmipālam = the king; nyapatat = fell; bhūmou = on the ground; visamj^ṇaḥ = unconsciously.

Seeing Bharata, Shatrughna was also overwhelmed with grief and while recollecting the king, fell unconscious on the ground.

Sloka 12

unmattaiva niścetā vilalāpa suduhkhitaḥ || 2-77-12
smṛtvā pitur guṇa angāni tani tāni tadā tadā |

12. smṛityā = Remembering of; tāni tāni = many; guṇāṇ^gāni = acts resulting from good qualities; pituḥ = of his father; tathā tathā = at different times; vilalāpa = he was lamenting wildly; (as follows) unmattaḥ = like one who has lost his wits; nishchetāḥ = bewildered as he was; suduḥkhitaḥ = and very much afflicted with grief.

Remembering the many gestures of his father resulting from his loving qualities made from time to time, Shatrughna was lamenting (as follows) like one who has lost his wits, bewildered as he was.

Sloka 13

mantharā prabhavaḥ tīvraḥ kaikeyī grāha samkulaḥ || 2-77-13
vara dānamayo akṣobhyo amajjayat śoka sāgaraḥ |

13. (we were) amajjayat = immersed; shokasāgaraḥ = in an ocean of sorrow; tīvra = which was violent; akshhobhyaḥ = and appalling; mantharāprabhavaḥ = invoked by ṃanthara; kaikeyī grāhasamkulaḥ = in which Kaikeyi; in the form of a crocodile; varadānamayaḥ = swam in its current the boons; (granted by my father in favor of Kaikeyi)

"We are immersed in a violent and appalling ocean of sorrow invoked by ṃandhara, in which Kaikeyi in the form of a crocodile swam in its current the irrevocable boons granted to her by my father."

Sloka 14

sukumāram ca bālam ca satatam lālitam tvayā || 2-77-14
kva tāta bharatam hitvā vilapantam gataḥ bhavān |

14. tāta = O; father! Kva = where; bhavān gataḥ = have you gone; hitvā = leaving; bharatam = Bharata; bālamcha = your boy; lālitam = caressed; satatam = always; tvayā = by you; vilapantam = and lamenting.

"O, father! Where have you gone leaving the lamenting Bharata, your boy always being caressed by you."

Sloka 15

nanu bhojyeṣu pāneṣu vastreṣv ābharaṇeṣu ca || 2-77-15
pravārayasi naḥ sarvāṃs tan naḥ ko adya kariṣyati |

15. pravārayasi nanu = you used to fulfill the wishes; maḥ savān = of all of us; bhojyeshhu = in the matter of eatables; pāneshhu = drinks; vastreshhu = clothes; ābharaṇeshhu cha = and jewels; kaḥ = who; anyaḥ = else; karishhyati = will do; tat = it; naḥ = for us?

"You used to fulfill the wishes of all of us, in the matter of eatables, drinks, clothes, and jewels. Who else will do it (now) for us."

Sloka 16

avadāraṇa kāle tu pṛthivī na avadīryate || 2-77-16
vihīnā yā tvayā rājñā dharmajñena mahātmanā |

16. pṛithivī = that earth; yā = which; vihīnā = is deprived; tvayā = of you; dharmajṇ^ena = who knew the righteousness; mahātmanā = a high soled man; rājṇ^ā = and a king; nāvadīryate = is not riven; avadīraṇakāle = even when it is time to be riven.

"That earth which is deprived of you, the high soled and righteous king, is not riven even when it is the time to be riven."

Sloka 17

pitari svargam āpanne rāme ca araṇyam āśrite || 2-77-17
kim me jīvita sāmarthyam pravekṣyāmi huta aśanam |

17. pitari = the father; āpanne = having obtained; svargam = heaven; rāmecha = and Rama; āshrite = having taken refuge; araṇyam = in a forest; kim = what is; jīvita sāmarthyam = the significance of life; me = for me? pravekshhyāmi = I shall enter; hutāshanam = a fire.

"The father having obtained heaven and Rama having taken refuge in a forest, what is the significance of life for me? I shall enter a fire."

Sloka 18

hīno bhrātrā ca pitrā ca śūnyām ikṣvāku pālitām || 2-77-18
ayodhyām na pravekṣyāmi pravekṣyāmi tapo vanam |

18. hīnaḥ = bereft of; bhrātrā = my brother; pitrācha = and father; na pravekshhyāmi = I can not return to; shūnyām = the deserted; ayodhām = Ayodhya; ikshhvākupālitām = where Ikshvaku used to reign; pravekshhyāmi = I shall retire to; tapovanam = a hermitage.

"Bereft of my brother and father, I can not return to the deserted city of Ayodhya, where Ikshvaku used to reign. I shall retire to a hermitage."

Sloka 19

tayoḥ vilapitam śrutvā vyasanam ca anvavekṣya tat || 2-77-19
bhṛśam ārtatarā bhūyaḥ sarvaeva anugāminaḥ |

19. shrutvā = hearing; vilapitam = the lamentation; tayoḥ = of them both; anvavekshhya = and beholding; tat = that; vyasanam = misfortune; sarve = all; anugāminaḥ = their companions; bhūyaḥ = once again; ārta tarāḥ = were disturbed; bhṛisham = very much.

Hearing the lamentation of both of them and beholding their plight, all their companions were disturbed very much, once again.

Sloka 20

tataḥ viṣaṇṇau śrāntau ca śatrughna bharatāv ubhau || 2-77-20
dharaṇyām samvyaceṣṭetām bhagna śṛngāv iva ṛṣabhau |

20. tataḥ = thereafter; ubhau = both; shatrughna bharatou = ṣhatrughna and Bharata; shrāntoucha = were wearied; vishhaṇṇou = with grief; samvyaveshṭetām = and began to roll; dharaṇ^yām = on the ground;ṛishhabhou iva = like two bulls; bhagnashṛiṇ^gou = having their horns broken.

Both Shatrughna, and Bharata were wearied with grief and began to roll on the ground, like two bulls whose horns have been broken.

Sloka 21

tataḥ prakṛtimān vaidyaḥ pitur eṣām purohitaḥ || 2-77-21
vasiṣṭho bharatam vākyam utthāpya tam uvāca ha |

21. tataḥ = then; vasishhṭah = Vasista; prakṛitimān = a noble natured man; vaidyaḥ = a family priest; eshhām = of their; pituḥ = father; utthāapya = after raising up; tam = that; bharatam = Bharata; uvācha ha = spoke; vākyam = (the following) words.

Then, Vasista, a noble man, a learned man and the family priest of their father, raised up that Bharata from the ground and spoke the following words:

Sloka 22

trayodaśo.ayam divasaḥ piturvṛttasya te vibho || 2-77-22
sāvaśeṣāsthinicaye kimiha tvam vilambase |

22. vibho = O; lord Bharata! Ayam = this; trayodashaḥ = is the thirteenth; divasaḥ = day; vṛittasya = of the death; te pituḥ = of your father; kim = why; tvam = are you; vilambase = hanging down; iha = here; sāva sheshha asthi nichaye = when the ceremony of collecting the bones is still remaining.

"O, Lord Bharata! This is the thirteenth day of the death of your father. Why are you hanging down here, when the ceremony of collecting the bones is still unfinished?"

Sloka 23

trīṇi dvandvāni bhūteṣu pravṛttāni aviśeṣataḥ || 2-77-23
teṣu ca aparihāryeṣu na evam bhavitum arhati |

23. trīṇi = the three; dvandvāni = pairs of opposites (namely hunger and thirst; joy and sorrow; birth and death) ; pravṛittāni = occur; bhūteshhu = living beings; avisheshhataḥ = without any distinction; teshhu = they; aparihāryeshhu = being inevitable; nārhasi = you ought not; bhavitum = to behave; evam = like this.

"The three pairs of opposites (namely hunger and thirst, joy and sorrow; birth and death) occur to all living beings without any distinction. They being inevitable, you ought not to behave like this."

Sloka 24

sumantraḥ ca api śatrughnam utthāpya abhiprasādya ca || 2-77-24
śrāvayām āsa tattvajñaḥ sarva bhūta bhava abhavau |

24. sumantrashchāpi = even ṣumantra; tattvajṇ^aḥ = who knew the truth; utthāpya = raising up; shatrughnam = ṣatrughna; abhiprasādyacha = and pacifying him; shrāvayāmāsa = told him; sarvabhūta bhavābhavam = about the origin and dissolution of all beings.

Even Sumantra, who knew the Truth, raising up Shatrughna and pacifying him, told him about the origin and dissolution of all beings.

Sloka 25

utthitau tau nara vyāghrau prakāśete yaśasvinau || 2-77-25
varṣa ātapa pariklinnau pṛthag indra dhvajāv iva |

25. yashasvinou = the two illustrious; naravyāghrou = tigers among men; utthitou = who had got up; prakāshete = appeared; pṛithak indradhvajāviva = like two banners severally raised in honor of Indra; varshhātapa pariglānou = soiled through rain and sun.

The two illustrious tigers among men (Bharata and Satrughna) who had got up, appeared like two banners severally raised in honor of Indra (the god of celestials) soiled through rain and the sun.

Sloka 26

aśrūṇi parimṛdnantau rakta akṣau dīna bhāṣiṇau || 2-77-26
amātyāḥ tvarayanti sma tanayau ca aparāḥ kriyāḥ |

26. amātyāḥ = the ministers; tvarayantisma = hurried up; tanaou = the princes; parimṛidnantou = who were wiping; ashrūṇi = their tears; raktākshhou = their eyes reddened; dīna bhāshhiṇou = and who were talking pitiably; aparāḥ kriyāḥ = to expedite the other rites pertaining to the thirteenth day.

The ministers hurried up the princes, who were wiping their tears and who were talking pitiably with their eyes reddened (due to excessive weeping) to expedite the other rites pertaining to the thirteenth day.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe saptasaptatitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 77th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
