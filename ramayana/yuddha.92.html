Introduction

Ravana feels distressed in hearing the news of his son's death. He gets enraged and after deeply pondering over, decides to kill Seetha, who was the root of all this trouble. Ravana with a sword in his hand, sallies forth towards Ashoka garden to make a short work of Seetha. Seetha observes Ravana rushing towards her and laments that after killing Rama and Lakshmana, he is coming to her place to kill her. Beholding the lamentation of Seetha, Suparshva, a good and upright minister of Ravana advises Ravana to desist from that dastardly act and to divert his anger towards Rama in battle. Ravana accepts the advice of his minister and returns to his assembly-hall.

37; 38. aham = I; kariṣhye = shall make; tat idam = that hoax; tathyameva = into a hard reality; ātmanaḥ priyam = which is pleasing to me; nāshayiṣhyāmi = I shall destroy; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; anuvratām = who is devoted to; kṣhatra bandhum = Rama; a bad warrior; ityevam = thus; uktvā = speaking; schivān = to his counsellors; parāmṛishat = he drew; khaDgam = his sword; āshu = swiftly.

"I shall make that hoax into a hard reality, which is pleasing to me. I shall destroy Seetha, who is devoted to Rama, a bad warrior". Thus speaking to his counsellors, he swiftly drew his sword.

Verse Locator

39; 40. samkṛiddhaḥ rāvaṇaḥ = the enraged Ravana; bhṛisham ākulachetanaḥ = who was very much agitated in mind; putrashokena = because of the grief for his son's death; utplatya = springing up suddenly ādāya = and taking; khaDgam = the sword; guṇasampannam = enriched with good attributes; vimalāmbaravarchasam = with the dazzle of a clear sky; niṣhpapāta = sallied forth; sachivaiḥ vṛitaḥ = along with his ministers; vegena = swiftly; sahasā = and quickly; sabhāyāḥ = from the assembly; yatra = to the place where; maithilī = ṣeetha was.

The enraged Ravana, who was very much agitated in mind because of the grief for his son's death, springing up suddenly and taking his excellent sword, having the dazzle of a clear sky, sallied forth from the assembly hall along with his ministers swiftly and quickly to the place where Seetha was.

Verse Locator

41. prekṣhya = seeing; rākṣhasam = Ravana; vrajantam = going; (his followers); vichukrushuḥ = emitted; simhanādam = a lion's roar; prekṣhya = seeing; samkruddham = the enraged; rākṣhasam = Ravana; āliṇ^gya = and embracing; anyonyam = each other; (they); ūchushchaḥ = spoke (as follows):

Seeing Ravana going, his followers emitted a lion's roar. Seeing the enraged Ravana and embracing each other, they spoke as follows:

Verse Locator

adyainaṃ tāvubhau dṛṣṭvā bhrātarau pravyathiṣyataḥ |

42. ubhau = both; tau = those; bhrātarau = brothers (Rama and Lakshmana); dṛiṣhṭvā = on seeing; enam = him; adya = now; pravyathiṣhyataḥ = will get perturbed; anena = by him; kruddhena = who was in fury; chatvāra = four; lokapālāḥ = guardians of the world; tarjitāḥ hi = were indeed frightened; bahavaḥ = ṃany; anye = other; shatravashchāpi = enemies were also; abhipātitāḥ = struck down; samyugeṣhu = in battles.

"Both those brothers (Rama and Lakshmana), on seeing him now in fury, will get perturbed. Indeed even the four guardians of the world had been frightened by him, who was in fury. ṃany other enemies were also frightened by him in various battles."

Verse Locator

triṣu lokeṣu ratnāni bhuṅkte cāhṛtya rāvaṇaḥ || 92-6-43

43. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; āhṛitya = having brought; ratnāni = valuable things; triṣhulokeṣu = from the three worlds; bhuṇkte cha = enjoys them; nāsti = no one; bhuvi = on earth; sadṛishaḥ = is equal; asya = to him; vikramecha = i prowess; balechaiva = and strength.

"Fetching precious things existing in all the three worlds, Ravana enjoys them. No one on the earth is equal to him in strength and prowess."

Verse Locator

teṣāṃ saṃjalpamānānāmaśokavanikāṃ gatām || 92-6-44

44. teṣhām samjalpamānānām = while the demons were thus talking; rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; krodhamūrchhitaḥ = who was excited with fury; abhidudrāva = rushed towards; vaidehīm = ṣeetha; gatām = who was present ashoka vanikām = in the Ashoka grove.

While those demons were thus talking, Ravana who was excited with fury, rushed towards Seetha, who was present in the Ashoka grove.

Verse Locator

vāryamāṇaḥ susaṃkruddhaḥ suhṛdbhirhitabuddhibhiḥ || 92-6-45

45. vārua;āṃaḥ = (ṭhough) being kept back; suhṛidbhiḥ = by his companions hitabuddhibhiḥ = whose minds were set on his welfare; susamkṛiddhaḥ = Ravana; who was very much enraged; abhyadhāvata = rushed; samkruddhaḥ grahaḥ iva = like the enraged ṃars; khe = in the sky; (would rush towards); rohiṇīm = the constellation Rohini.

Though being kept back by his companions whose minds were set on his welfare, Ravana who was very much enraged, rushed like ṃars, in the sky, which would rush towards the constellation, Rohini in fury.

Verse Locator

maithilī rakṣyamāṇā tu rākṣasībhiraninditā || 92-6-46

46. aninditā = the faultless; maithilī tu = ṣeetha for her part; rakṣhyamāṇā = who was being guarded; rākṣhasībhiḥ = by female demons; dadarsha = saw; krudham = the enraged; rākṣhasam = Ravana; nistrimsha vara dhāriṇam = bearing an excellent sword.

The faultless Seetha for her part, who was being guarded by female demons, saw the enraged Ravana who was bearing an excellent sword.

Verse Locator

taṃ niśāmya sanistriṃśaṃ vyathitā janakātmajā || 92-6-47
nivāryamāṇaṃ bahuśaḥ suhṛdbhiranivartinam |

47. janakātmajā = ṣeetha; vyathitā = was perturbed; nishāmya = in seeing; tam = him; sanistrimsham = bearing a sword; anivartanam = and who was not going back; nivāryamāṇam = eventhough being kept back bahushaḥ = in many ways; suhṛidbhiḥ = by his friends.

Seetha was perturbed in seeing him, bearing sword and who was not going back eventhough being kept back in many ways by his friends.

Verse Locator

sītā duḥkhasamāviṣṭā vilapantīdamabravīt || 92-6-48
yathāyaṃ māmabhikruddhaḥ samabhidravati svayam |
vadhiṣyati sanāthāṃ māmanāthāmiva durmatiḥ || 92-6-49

48; 49. sītā = ṣeetha; duḥ khasamāviṣhṭā = filled; with grief; vilapantī = while lamenting; abravīt = said to herself; idam = these words; ayam = ḥe; durmatiḥ = the evl-minded; abhikruddhaḥ = with fury; yathā = as; abhidravati = he is rushing; svayam himself; mām = towards me; vadhiṣhyati = will kill; mām = me; sanāthām = having a husband; anāthāmiva = as if to make me husband-less.

Filled with sorrow and lamenting, Seetha said to herself as follows: "From the manner in which this fellow himself is rushing towards me in fury, I fear the evil-minded demon is going to kill me, to make me husband-less, even though I have a husband".

Verse Locator

bahuśaścodayāmāsa bhartāraṃ māmanuvratām |
bhāryā bhava ramasyeti pratyākhyāto dhruvaṃ mayā || 92-6-50
so.ayaṃ māmanupasthānād vyaktaṃ nairāśyamāgataḥ |
krodhamohasamāviṣṭo vyaktaṃ māṃ hantumudyataḥ || 92-6-51

50; 51. mām = to me; anuvratām = devoted as I am; bhartāram = to my husband; chodayāmāsa = he incited; bahushaḥ = several times; iti = saying; mama bhāryā bhavasva = "By my consort!"; saḥ ayam = he as such; pratyākhyātaḥ = was refused; mayā = by me; dhruvam = firmly; vyaktam āgataḥ = having been evidently afflicted; nairāshyam = with despair; anupasthāne = on my refusing to wait upon him; udyataḥ = he is intent; vyaktam = surely; hantum = to kill; mām = me; krodhamohasamāviṣhṭaḥ = filled as he was with anger and infatuation.

"He incited me several times, devoted as I am to my husband, saying 'Be my consort!'. He was refused firmly by me. Having been evidently afflicted with despair on my refusing to wait upon him, he is surely intent to kill me, filled as he was with anger and infatuation."

Verse Locator

atha vā tau naravyāghrau bhrātarau rāmalakṣmaṇau |
mannimittamanāryeṇa samare.adya nipātitau || 92-6-52

52. athavā = otherwise; tau bhrātarau rāma lakṣhmaṇau = those brothers; Rama and Lakshmana; naravyāghrau = the foremost of men; nipātitau = might have been killed; adya = today; samare = in battle; mannimattam = because of me; anāryeṇa = by that vulgar demon.

"Otherwise, that vulgar demon might have killed those brothers, Rama and Lakshmana, the foremost of men, today in battle, because of me."

Verse Locator

bhairavo hi mahānnādo rākṣasānāṃ śruto mayā |
bahūnāmiha hṛṣṭānāṃ tathā vikrośatāṃ priyam || 92-6-53

53. iha = here; bhairavaḥ = a terrific; mahān = great; nādaḥ = sound; babhūnām rākṣhasānām = of many demons; (shrutaḥ hi = has indeed been heard; mayā = by me); hṛiṣhṭānām = who were merry; tathā = and; vikroshatām = roaring; priyam = about a pleasant thing.

"Here, I just heard a terrific great noise of a multitude of demons, who have been jubilant and roaring about a pleasant happening."

Verse Locator

aho dhinmannimitto.ayaṃ vināśo rājaputrayoḥ |
athavā putraśokena ahatvā rāmalakṣmaṇau || 92-6-54

"Alas! Woe is me, if that destruction of the two princes was brought about because of me. Otherwise, the terrific demon of a sinful resolve, having not been able to kill Rama and Lakshmana, is going to kill, me, because of the grief over his son's death."

Verse Locator

55; 56. tat vālua; = those words; hanūmataḥ = of ḥanuma; na kṛitam = were not acted upon; mayā = by me; kṣhudrayā = having a low mind; aham ayāsam yadi = If I left; tadā = on that day; tasya pṛiṣhṭhena = on his back; anirjitā = though not won back (by my husband); anushocheyam = I would not have grieved; adya evam = as I am doing in his manner today; bhartuḥ aṇ^kagatā satī = as I should be resting on the lap of my husband (in that case).

"Those words of Hanuma were not acted upon by me, having a low mind. If I left on that day on his back, though not won back by my husband, I would not have grieved like this today, as I should be resting on the lap of my husband (in that case)."

Verse Locator

"I think that the heart of that Kausalya will get burst, when the lady, who had only one son, hears of her son having been killed in battle."

Verse Locator

58. rudatī = while lamenting; sā = she; samsmariṣhyati = would be recollecting; janmacha = about the birth; bālyam cha = childhood; yauvanamcha = youth; dharmakāryāṇi = the righteous acts; rūpam cha = and the handsome form; mahātmanaḥ = of the great-souled Rama.

"While lamenting, Kausalya will vividly recall about the birth, childhood, youth, the righteous acts and the handsome form of the great-souled Rama."

Verse Locator

59. putre nihate = when her son is killed; (Kausalya); nirāshā = becomes despondent; achetanā = unconscious; dattvā = after observing; shrāddham = a ceremony in honour of her dead son; nūnam = and certainly; āvekṣhyate = will enter; agnim = the flame; pravekṣhyati vāpi = or even be drowned into; āpaḥ = water.

"Having observed a ceremony in honour of her deceased son and getting despondent and unconscious, Kausalya will surely enter the flame or be drowned in water."

Verse Locator

dhigastu kubjāmasatīṃ mantharāṃ pāpaniścayām |

60. dhik astu = woe be; kubjām = to the hunch-back; asatīm = and unfaithful; mantharām = ṃanthara; pāpanishchayām = of sinful resolve; yannimittam = on whose consequence; kausalyā = Kausalya; pratipadyate = will obtain; imam shokam = this grief.

"Woe be to the unfaithful hunch-back ṃanthara, of sinful resolve, on whose consequence, Kausalya will get this grief."

Verse Locator

ityevaṃ maithilīṃ dṛṣṭvā vilapantīṃ tapasvinīm |

Seeing Seetha miserably weeping in that manner like Rohini (the deity presiding over a constellation of this name), fallen under the sway of the planet ṃars, when away of the moon-god (her husband), a minister called Suparshva, a good-natured, upright and highly intelligent demon, even though restrained by other ministers, spoke the following words Ravana, the foremost of demons:

Verse Locator

"O Ravana, the younger brother of Kubera himself! How do you wish to kill Seetha, abandoning your righteousness, in a bout of anger?"

"O valiant king of demons! Why are you thinking of destroying a lady, ever since you completed the vow of celibacy essential for a study of the Vedic lore and were devoted to your own duty?"

Verse Locator

"O king! Behold at Seetha, endowed, as she is, with beauty. Together with us, release your anger, in battle, on Rama alone."

Verse Locator

Accepting that advice, endowed with justice, tendered by a friend, the evil-minded Ravana went to his palace. Thereafter, that valiant Ravana, along with his friends, again went to his assembly hall from there.
