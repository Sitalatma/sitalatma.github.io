Introduction

As per instructions of Rama, Guha gets a boat ready. Sumantra requests Rama to take him as a personal attendant to the forest, but Rama declines his offer and after reasoning with him, sends him back to Ayodhya. Rama and Lakshmana matted their hair with the latex of a banyan tree procured by Guha. On reaching the middle of the stream, Sita offers prayers to ṃother Ganga (the deity presiding over the stream) and after crossing the river, all the three halt for the night at the foot of a tree.

Sloka 1

prabhātāyām tu śarvaryām pṛthu vṛkṣā mahā yaśāḥ |
uvāca rāmaḥ saumitrim lakṣmaṇam śubha lakṣaṇam || 2-52-1

1. sharvaryām = night; prabhatāyāmtu = having given way to dawn; rāmaḥ = Rama; pṛithuvakshhāḥ = the man with a broad chest; mahāyashāḥ = (and) the illustrious; uvācha = said; lakshhmaṇam = to Lakshmana; soumitrim = the son of ṣumitra; shubhalakshhaṇam = endowed with auspicious signs.

That night having given way to dawn, the illustrious Rama with a broad chest spoke (as follows) to Lakshmana, the son of Sumitra and who was endowed with auspicious signs :

Sloka 2

bhāskara udaya kālo ayam gatā bhagavatī niśā |
asau sukṛṣṇo vihagaḥ kokilaḥ tāta kūjati || 2-52-2

2. tāta = "Oh; dear brother. ayam = ṭhis; bhāskarodaya kālaḥ = is the hour of sunrise. bhagavatī = ṭhe auspicious; nishā = night; gatā = has departed. vihagaḥ = ṭhat bird; sukṛishhṇaḥ = of dark plumage; asou kokilaḥ = the cuckoo; kūjati = is singing".

"Oh, dear brother! This is the hour of sunrise. The auspicious night has departed. That bird of dark plumage, the cuckoo, is singing."

Sloka 3

barhiṇānām ca nirghoṣaḥ śrūyate nadatām vane |
tarāma jāhnavīm saumya śīghragām sāgaram gamām || 2-52-3

3. nirghoshhaḥ = the cries; barhiṇānām = of peacocks; nadatām = resounding; vane = in the forest; shrūyate = are being heard. soumya = Oh; good brother! tarāma = (let us) cross; shīghragām = the swift-flowing; jāhnavīm** = Ganga river; sāgaram gamām = that gushes to the sea."

"Oh, good brother! Hear the cries of peacocks resounding in the forest. Let us cross the swift-flowing Ganga River that gushes to the sea."

▼

--***-The river Ganga, when brought down from heaven by the austerities of Emperor Bhagiratha, followed the latter to the subterranean regions in order to consecrate the remains of his great grand uncles. In its course, it inundated the sacrificial ground of king Jahnu, who in his anger, drank up its waters. But the gods and sages and particularly Bhagiratha appeased his anger and he consented to release the waters through his ears. The river is thereafter regarded as his daughter.

Sloka 4

vijñāya rāmasya vacaḥ saumitrir mitra nandanaḥ |
guham āmantrya sūtam ca so atiṣṭhad bhrātur agrataḥ || 2-52-4

4. saḥ soumitriḥ = that Lakshmana; mitranandanaḥ = who makes his friends happy; viG^yāya = having understood; vachaḥ = the words; rāmasya = of Rama; āmantrya = called; guham = Guha; sūtamcha = and ṣumantra; atishhṭat = and stood; agrataḥ = in front; bhrātuḥ = of his brother.

Lakshmana, the delight of his friends, having understood the words of Rama, called Guha as well as Sumantra and stood in front of his brother.

Sloka 5

sa tu rāmasya vacanam niśamya pratigṛhya ca |
sthapatistūrṇamāhuya sacivānidamabravīt || 2-52-5

5. nishamya = hearing; vachanam = the words; rāmasya = of Rama; saḥ sthapatiḥ = that Guha; tūrṇam = quickly; pratigṛihyacha = received them; āhūya = invited; sachivān = his ministers; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these words:

Hearing the command of Rama, Guha quickly received it, invited his ministers and spoke to them as follows:

Sloka 6

asya vāhanasamyuktām karṇagrāhavatīm śubhām |
supratārām dṛḍhām tīrkhe śīgram nāvamupāhara || 2-52-6

6. asya = for the sake of Rama; shīghram = quickly; upāhara = bring; tīrthe = to the landing place; nāvam = a boat; vāhana samyuktām = accompanied by a boatman; karṇagrāhavatīm = and having a helmsman in it; shubhām = beautiful; supratārām = which can easily ferry across; dṛiDhām = and solidly constructed.

"Let a beautiful boat that is solidly constructed, sails well and a helmsman in it, be brought to the bank to carry this hero across!"

Sloka 7

tam niśamya samādeśam guhāmātyagaṇo mahān |
upohya rucirām nāvam guhāya pratyavedayat || 2-52-7

7. nishamya = hearing; tam = that; samādesham = command; mahān guhāmātya gaṇaḥ = the chief minister of Guha; upohya = brought; ruchirām = a charming; nāvam = boat; pratyavedayat = ( and) reported the matter; guhāya = to Guha.

Hearing that command, the chief minister of King Guha brought a charming boat to the bank and reported the matter to Guha.

Sloka 8

tataḥ saprāñjalirbhūtvā guho rāghavamabravīt |
upasthiteyam naurdeva bhūyaḥ kim karavāṇi te || 2-52-8

8. tataḥ = then; guhaḥ = Guha; bhūtvā = became; prāṇjaliḥ = one with folded hands; abravīt = and spoke; rāghavam = to Rama: devaḥ = "Oh; Lord! ; iyam = ṭhis; nouḥ = boat; upasthitā = has arrived; kim = what; bhūyaḥ = more; karavāṇi = can I do; te = for you"

Then, Guha with folded hands spoke to Rama as follows: "Oh, Lord! Here, the boat has arrived. What more can I do for you?"

Sloka 9

tavāmarasutaprakhya tartum sāgaragām nadīm |
nauriyam puruṣavyāgra! tām tvamāroha suvrata! || 2-52-9

9. purushha vyāghra = Oh; tiger among men! amarasuta prakhya = Oh; Rama resembling a son of divinity! iyam = ṭhis; nouḥ = is the boat; tava = for you; tartum = to cross; nadīm = the river; sāgaragām = which flows into the sea. suvrata = Oh; virtuous one! tvam = You; āroha = ascend; tām = it.

"Oh, tiger among men! Oh, Rama resembling a son of divinity! Here is the boat for you to cross the river, which flows into the sea. Oh, virtuous one! (Pray) get into it."

Sloka 10

athovāca mahātejā rāmo guhamidam vacaḥ |
kṛtakāmo.asmi bhavatā śīghramāropyatāmiti || 2-52-10

10. atha = then; rāmaḥ = Rama; mahā tejāḥ = with great splendor; uvācha = spoke; idam = these; vachaḥ = words; guham = to Guha; iti = thus; asmi = "I became; kṛita kāmaḥ = one whose desire has been accomplished; bhavatā = by you. āropyatām = Let us embark shīghram = with speed".

Then, Rama with great splendor, spoke to Guha as follows: "ṃy desire has been accomplished by you. Let us embark with all speed."

Sloka 11

tataḥ kalāpān samnahya khaḍgau baddhvā ca dhanvinau |
jagmatur yena tau gangām sītayā saha rāghavau || 2-52-11

11. tataḥ = then; tou rāghavou = both Rama and Lakshmana; sītayāsaha = along with ṣita;samnahya = having put on; kalāpān = quivers with arrows; baddhvā = having fastened; khaDgoucha = the swords too; dhanvinou = and holding their bows; jagmatuḥ = went; yena = in which way; gaṇ^gām = Ganga is situated.

Having equipped themselves with a quiver each with arrows, fastening their swords and armed with their bows, Rama and Lakshmana, with Sita, proceeded towards the river of Ganga.

Sloka 12

rāmam eva tu dharmajñam upagamya vinītavat |
kim aham karavāṇi iti sūtaḥ prānjalir abravīt || 2-52-12

12. sūtaḥ = ṣumantra; prāṇ^jaliḥ = joining his palms; vinītavat = in humility; upagamya = approached; rāmameva tu = only Rama; dharmaG^yam = who knew what is right; abravīt = and spoke; iti = thus: kim = :What aham = I; karavāṇi = shall do?"

Sumantra joining his palms in humility, approached Rama who knew what is right and said, "What shall I do?"

Sloka 13

tato.abravīddāśarathiḥ sumantram |
spṛśan kareṇottamadakṣiṇena |
sumantra śīghram punareva yāhi |
rājñaḥ sakāśe bhavacāpramattaḥ || 2-52-13

13. dāsharathiḥ = Rama; tataḥ = then; spṛishan = touching; sumantram = ṣumantra; uttama dakshhiṇena = with his auspicious right; kareṇa = hand; abravīt = (and) said: sumantra = "Oh; ṣumantra! yāhi punareva = Return; shīghram = quickly; rāG^yaḥ = to king's; sakāshe = presence; bhava = (and) be; apramattaḥcha = attentive."

Touching Sumantra with his auspicious right hand, Rama then said: "Oh, Sumantra! Return quickly to the king's presence and be attentive in serving him."

Sloka 14

nivartasva iti uvāca enam etāvadd hi kṛtam mama |
ratham vihāya padbhyām tu gamiṣyāmi mahāvanam || 2-52-14

14. etāvat = "this much service; kṛitamhi = has been done indeed; mama = to me. nivartasya = Return. gamishhyāmi = I shall go; padbhyām = on foot; mahāvanam = to the great forest; vihāya = abandoning; ratham = the chariot. uvācha = (he) spoke;iti = thus.

"Go now, that your service to me has been completed. Abandoning the chariot, I shall go on foot to the mighty forest," Rama said.

Sloka 15

ātmānam tu abhyanujñātam avekṣya ārtaḥ sa sārathiḥ |
sumantraḥ puruṣa vyāghram aikṣvākam idam abravīt || 2-52-15

15. āvekshhya = seeing; ātmānam = himself; abhyanuG^yātam = dismissed; saḥ sumantraḥ = that ṣumantra; sārathiḥ = was distressed; abravīt = and spoke; idam = these words; aikshhvākam = to Rama; purushavyāghram = the tiger among men.

Finding himself dismissed, Sumantra the charioteer was distressed and spoke to Rama, the tiger among men, as follows:

Sloka 16

na atikrāntam idam loke puruṣeṇa iha kenacit |
tava sabhrātṛ bhāryasya vāsaḥ prākṛtavad vane || 2-52-16

16. vāsaḥ = dwelling; vane = in the forest; prākṛitavat = like a common man; tava = (has come) to you; sa bhrātṛI bhāryasya = with your brother and your consort. idam = ṭhis; nātikrāntam = has never been set aside; kenachit purushheṇa = by any man; iha loke = in the world.

"None in the world has had to suffer such a fate as yourself; that you should have to dwell in the forest with your brother and your consort as if you were an ordinary man!"

Sloka 17

na manye brahma carye asti svadhīte vā phala udayaḥ |
mārdava ārjavayoḥ vā api tvām ced vyasanam āgatam || 2-52-17

17. manye = I think; nāsti = there is no; phalodayaḥ = reward; brahmacharye = in leading a life of religious student; svadhīte vā = or in studying scriptures; mārdavārjavayorvāpi = or even in cultivating tenderness and strait forwardness; vyasanam chet = (when) adversity; āgatam = has come; tvām = to you."

"I think that there is no reward in leading a life of religious student or in studying religious scriptures or even in cultivating tenderness and straight forwardness, when adversity has come to you."

Sloka 18

saha rāghava vaidehyā bhrātrā caiva vane vasan |
tvam gatim prāpsyase vīra trīml lokāṃs tu jayann iva || 2-52-18

18. vīra = "Oh; heroic; rāghava = Rama! vasan = Residing; vane = in the forest; vaidehyā saha = along with ṣita; bhrātrāchaiva = and your brother; tvam = you; prāpsyase = will obtain; gatim = the same position; jayanniva = as one who has conquered; trīn = the three; lokān = worlds"

"Oh, heroic Rama! Living in the forest along with Sita and your brother, you will obtain the same position as one who has conquered the three worlds."

Sloka 19

vayam khalu hatā rāma ye tayā api upavancitāḥ |
kaikeyyā vaśam eṣyāmaḥ pāpāyā duhkha bhāginaḥ || 2-52-19

19. rāma = "Oh; Rama! vayam = We; hatāḥ khalu = are actually ruined; yena = because; upavaṇchitāḥ = disappointed in our hopes; tvayāpi = by you too; eshhyāmaḥ = we shall fall; vasham = under the sway; kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi; pāpāyāḥ = of sinful nature; duḥkha bhāginaḥ = and reap suffering".

"Oh, Rama! We are actually ruined, in that, disappointed in our hopes by you too, we shall fall under the sway of Kaikeyi the sinful woman and reap the suffering."

Sloka 20

iti bruvann ātma samam sumantraḥ sārathis tadā |
dṛṣṭvā dura gatam rāmam duhkha ārtaḥ rurude ciram || 2-52-20

20. tadā = then; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; sārathiḥ = the charioteer; iti = thus; bruvan = speaking; rurude = wept; chiram = long; duhkhārtāḥ = stricken with grief; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; rāmam = Rama; ātmasamam = equal to his soul; dūragatam = gone to a distance.

Sumantra the charioteer thus speaking, wept for a long time, stricken with grief, seeing Rama, equal to his soul, departing to a distance.

Sloka 21

tataḥ tu vigate bāṣpe sūtam spṛṣṭa udakam śucim |
rāmaḥ tu madhuram vākyam punaḥ punar uvāca tam || 2-52-21

21. tataḥ = thereafter; rāmastu = Rama; punaḥ punuḥ = again and again; uvācha = spoke; vākyam(these) words; madhuram = which were sweet; tam sūtam = to that charioteer; bāshhpe = (whose) tears; vigate = had gone away; spṛishhṭodokam = who had sipped some water; shuchim = and got purified.

Then, Rama again and again spoke these sweet words as follows to that charioteer, whose tears got dried up and who had sipped some water and got himself purified:

Sloka 22

ikṣvākūṇām tvayā tulyam suhṛdam na upalakṣaye |
yathā daśaratho rājā mām na śocet tathā kuru || 2-52-22

22. nopalakshhaye = "I do not see; suhrudam = a friend; tulyam = equal; tvayā = to you; ikshhvākūṇām = for the kings of Ikshvaku dynasty. kuru = Act; tathā = in such a way; yathā = as; rājā = king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha; na shochet = may not lament; mām = about me."

"I do not see any one who is as great a friend of the Ikshvakus as you are. (Pray) act in such a way that king Dasaratha may not lament about me"

Sloka 23

śoka upahata cetāḥ ca vṛddhaḥ ca jagatī patiḥ |
kāma bhāra avasannaḥ ca tasmāt etat bravīmi te || 2-52-23

23. jagatīpatiḥ = the king; shokopahata chetāshcha = his mind afflicted with grief; vṛiddhashcha = is aged as well. Kāmabhārāvasannashcha = (ḥe is) pressed down by a burden of passion. tasmāt = for that reason; bravīmi = I tell; etat = this; te = to you.

"The king, his mind afflicted with grief, is aged as well. He is pressed down by a burden of passion. Hence, I tell you this."

Sloka 24

yad yad ājñāpayet kiṃcit sa mahātmā mahī patiḥ |
kaikeyyāḥ priya kāma artham kāryam tat avikānkṣayā || 2-52-24

24. yadyat = what so ever; kimchit = some act; saḥ = that; mahātmā = high-soled; mahīpatiḥ = emperor; āG^yāpayet = may enjoin; priya kāmārtham = with intent to oblige the desire; kaikeyyāḥ = of Kaikeyi; tat = that; kāryam = is to be done; avikāṇkshhayā = un hesitatingly."

"What so ever act that high-soled emperor may enjoin you to do, with intent to oblige the desire of Kaikeyi, it is to be done unhesitatingly."

Sloka 25

etat artham hi rājyāni praśāsati nara īśvarāḥ |
yad eṣām sarva kṛtyeṣu mano na pratihanyate || 2-52-25

25. nareshvarāḥ = "the kings; prashāsatihi = indeed rule; rājyāani = the states; etadartham = with this end in view; manaḥ = that their will; yat na pratihanyate = may not be frustrated; sarva kṛityeshhu = in all their undertakings."

"The kings indeed rule the states with this end in view that their will may not be frustrated in any undertaking."

Sloka 26

yadyathā sa mahā rājo na alīkam adhigaccati |
na ca tāmyati duhkhena sumantra kuru tat tathā || 2-52-26

26. sumantra = " Oh; ṣumantra! kuru = Carry out; tathā = in such a way; tat = that; saḥ mahārājaḥ = the said emperor; yathā = in which way; yat = whatever; na adhigachchhati = may not find it; aLīkam = as anything unpleasing; na cha tāmyati = nor gets distressed; duḥkhena = by grief."

"Oh, Sumantra! Carry out everything in such a way that the said emperor neither finds it unpleasing nor gets tormented by grief."

Sloka 27

adṛṣṭa duhkham rājānam vṛddham āryam jita indriyam |
brūyāḥ tvam abhivādya eva mama hetor idam vacaḥ || 2-52-27

27. abhivādyaiva = "only after doing respectful salutation; rājānāam = to the king; vṛiddham = who is old; āryam = (and) venerable; adṛishhṭa duḥkham = who has never known suffering; jiteṇ^driyam = and who has subdued his senses; tvam = you; brūyāḥ = tell; idam = these; vachaḥ = words; mama hetoḥ = on my behalf."

"Only after performing respectful salutation to the old and venerable king, who has never known suffering and who has subdued his senses, you tell these words to him on my behalf."

Sloka 28

na eva aham anuśocāmi lakṣmaṇo na ca maithilī |
ayodhyāyāḥ cyutāḥ ca iti vane vatsyāmaha iti vā (maheti!)|| 2-52-28

28. naiva = "Indeed neither; aham = I; na = nor; lakshmaṇaḥ maithilīcha = Lakshmana and ṣita; anushochāmi = grieve; chyutāshcheti = for having moved; ayodhyāyāḥ = from Ayodhya; vatsyāmaheticha = or that we are going to dwell; vane = in a forest."

"Indeed neither I nor Lakshmana and Sita grieve for having moved from Ayodhya or that we are going to dwell in a forest."

Sloka 29

catur daśasu varṣeṣu nivṛtteṣu punaḥ punaḥ |
lakṣmaṇam mām ca sītām ca drakṣyasi kṣipram āgatān || 2-52-29

29. nivṛitteshhu = after completing; chaturdashasu = fourteen; varshheshhu = years; drakshhyasi = you will see; punaḥ punaḥ = once again; lakshhmaṇam = Lakshmana; mām cha = myself; sītām cha = and ṣita too; āgatān = returned; kshhipram = quickly."

"After completing fourteen years, you will once more see Lakshmana, myself and Sita too returned apace from the forest."

Sloka 30

evam uktvā tu rājānam mātaram ca sumantra me |
anyāḥ ca devīḥ sahitāḥ kaikeyīm ca punaḥ punaḥ || 2-52-30

sītāyā mama ca āryasya vacanāl lakṣmaṇasya ca || 2-52-31

30;31. sumantra = "Oh; ṣumantra! evam = this is; uktvā = what should you say; rājānam = to the king; me mataram cha = and my mother; sahitāḥ = all; anyāḥ = other; devīshcha = queens; kaikeyīm cha = and Kaikeyi. brūhi = tell; kousalyām = Kousalya; ārogyam = (about my)health; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; atha = and thereafter; pādābhivandanam = salutations at her feet; sītāyāḥ = (on behalf of) ṣita; mama cha = as well as myself; vachanāt = the words; lakshhmaṇasyacha = of Lakshmana; āryasya = the faithful man."

"Oh,Sumantra! This is what you should say to the king, my mother, all other queens and Kaikeyi. Tell Kausalya again and again that I am keeping good health. Thereafter, convey salutations at her feet on behalf of Sita as well as myself and Lakshmana the faithful man."

Sloka 32

brūyāḥ ca hi mahā rājam bharatam kṣipram ānaya |
āgataḥ ca api bharataḥ sthāpyo nṛpa mate pade || 2-52-32

32. brūyāḥ = "ṭell (our salutations); mahārajāmcha = to the emperor also. anaya = Bring; bharatam = Bharata; kshhipram = quickly. āgashchāpi = after his arrival; bharataḥ = Bharata; sthāpya = may be installed; pade = in the position; nṛipa mate = as desired by the king."

"Tell our salutations to the emperor too. Bring Bharata quickly. After his arrival, Bharata may be installed in the position, as desired by the king."

Sloka 33

bharatam ca pariṣvajya yauvarājye abhiṣicya ca |
asmat samtāpajam duhkham na tvām abhibhaviṣyati || 2-52-33

33. parishhvajya = "when you embraced; bharatam = Bharata; abhishhichya cha = and installed him; youva rājye = in the office of the Prince Regent; duḥkham = the agony;asmatsamtāpajam = caused by the remorse felt by you on our account; na abhibhavishhyati = will not overpower; tvām = you."

"When you embrace Bharata and install him in the office of the Prince Regent, the agony caused by the repentance felt by you on our account will not overpower you."

Sloka 34

tathā mātṛṣu vartethāḥ sarvāsv eva aviśeṣataḥ || 2-52-34

34. bharatasyāpi = "Bharata also; vaktavyaḥ = is to be told (thus): vartethāḥ = "ṭreat; avisheshhataḥ = without distinction; sarvāsveva = all; mātṛishhu = your mothers; tathā = with the same regard; yathā = as; vartase = (you) behave; rājani = towards the king."

"Bharata too is to be told thus: "Treat without distinction all your mothers with the same regard as you behave towards the king."

Sloka 35

yathā ca tava kaikeyī sumitrā ca aviśeṣataḥ |
tathaiva devī kausalyā mama mātā viśeṣataḥ || 2-52-35

35. yathācha = as is; kaikeyī = Kaikeyi; tava = to you; tathaiva = so let it be; sumitrācha = for ṣumitra; visheshhataḥ = more so; devī = (and) the divine kausalyā = Kausalya; mama mātā = my mother; visheshhataḥ = in particular"

"As is your affection for Kaikeyi, so let it be for Sumitra and also the divine Kausalya, my mother"

Sloka 36

tātasya priyakāmena yauvarājyamapekṣatā |
lokayorubhayoḥ śakyam tvayā yatsukhamedhitum || 2-52-36

36. apekshhataḥ = "(If you) accept; youva rājam = the princely kingdom; priya kāmana = with an intent to please; tātasya = our father; shakyam = it will be possible; tvayā = for you; edhitum = to enhance; sukham = happiness; yat = whatever; ubhayoḥ = in both; lokayāḥ = the worlds."

"If you accept the princely kingdom with an intent to please our father, it will be possible for you to enhance happiness in both the worlds (in this world and the next)."

Sloka 37

nivartyamāno rāmeṇa sumantraḥ śoka karśitaḥ |
tat sarvam vacanam śrutvā snehāt kākutstham abravīt || 2-52-37

37. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; nivartyamānaḥ = who was being sent back; rāmeṇa = by Rama; shoka karshhitaḥ = agonized with grief; shrutvā = heard; sarvam = the whole; tat = of that; vachanam = discourse; snehāt = affectionately; abravīt = spoke; kākutstham = to Rama (as follows):

Sloka 38

sa bhrātuḥ śāsanam śrutvā sarvam apratikūlayan |
āropya maithilīm pūrvam āruroha ātmavāṃs tataḥ || 2-52-76

76. shrutvā = hearing; shāsanam = the command; sarvam = wholly; bhrātruḥ = of his (elder) brother; saḥ = that Lakshmana; ātmavān = prudent; apratikūlayan = not resisting it; āropaya maithilīm = having made ṣita to ascend (the boat); pūrvam = first; āruroha = stepped into (the boat); tataḥ = afterwards.

Hearing the command completely of his elder brother, the prudent Lakshmana, by not counteracting it, made Sita to ascend the boat first and stepped into it afterwards.

Sloka 77

atha āruroha tejasvī svayam lakṣmaṇa pūrvajaḥ |
tataḥ niṣāda adhipatir guho jñātīn acodayat || 2-52-77

77. atha = then; tejasvī = the glorious; lakshhmaṇa pūrvajaḥ = Rama; the elder brother of Lakshmana; āruroha = got into (the boat); svayam = himself. ṭataḥ = ṭhereafter; guhaḥ = Guha; nishhadādhipatiḥ = the ruler of the ṇishadas; achodayat = commanded; G^yātīm = his kinsfolk (to row them across the river).

Then, the glorious Rama got into the boat himself. Thereafter, Guha the ruler of Nishadas commanded his kinsfolk to row them across the river.

Sloka 78

rāghavo.api mahātejā nāvamāruhya tām tataḥ |
brahmavat kṣatravaccaiva jajāpa hitamātmanaḥ || 2-52-78

78. ārūhya = after ascending; tām nāvam = that boat; rāghava. Api = Rama too; mahā tejaḥ = of mighty splendor; tataḥ = then; jajāpa = recited (a sacred text Daivīm nāvam etc) brahmavat = (fit for) Brahmanas; kshhatriyashchaiva = and Kshatriyas; hitam = and conducive to the good; ātmanaḥ = of his son.

After ascending the boat, Rama too of mighty splendor then recited a sacred text (daivīm nāvam etc) fit for brahmanas and Kshatriyas alike and conducive to his own good.

Sloka 79

ācamya ca yathāśāstram nadīm tām saha sītayā |
prāṇamatprītisamhṛṣṭo lakṣmaṇaścāmitaprabhaḥ || 2-52-79

79. āchamyacha = having sipped water; yathā shāstram = as per scriptures; prītisamhṛishhṭaḥ = and with extreme delight; sītayā saha = (Rama) with ṣita; prāṇamat = made obeisance; tām nadīm = to that river; lakshmaṇashcha = Lakshmana also; amita prabhaḥ = of infinite splendor (followed suit.)

Having sipped water as per scriptures and with extreme delight, Rama with Sita made obeisance to that river. Lakshmana, of infinite splendor, followed suit.

Sloka 80

anujñāya sumantram ca sabalam caiva tam guham |
āsthāya nāvam rāmaḥ tu codayām āsa nāvikān || 2-52-80

80. anuG^yāya = bidding farewell; tam guham = to that Guha; sabalam = with his army of men; sumantram = and ṣumantra; rāmastu = Rama; āsthāya = sat on; nāvam = the boat; chodayāmāsa = and directed; nāvikān = the boatmen (to move on).

Bidding farewell to Guha with his army of men and Sumantra, Rama sat on the boat and directed the boatmen to move on.

Sloka 81

tataḥ taiḥ coditā sā nauḥ karṇa dhāra samāhitā |
śubha sphya vega abhihatā śīghram salilam atyagāt || 2-52-81

81. taiḥ = through their; choditā = propulsion; sa nauḥ = that boat; karṇadhāra samāhitā = furnished with a pilot; shubha sphya vega abhihatā = obeying those splendid and vigorous oarsmen; shīghram = rapidly; atyagāt = moved across; salilam = water.

Propelled by those splendid and vigorous oarsmen, that boat furnished with a pilot, rapidly moved across the water.

Sloka 82

madhyam tu samanuprāpya bhāgīrathyāḥ tu aninditā |
vaidehī prānjalir bhūtvā tām nadīm idam abravīt || 2-52-82

82. samanuprāpya = coming to; madhyam = the middle; bhāgīrathyāḥ = of Bhagirathi; aninditā = the irresproachable; vaidehī = ṣita; bhūtvā = having been; prāṇ^jaliḥ = with joined palms; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; tām nadīm = to the said river.

Coming to the middle of Bhagirathi river, the irreproachable Sita with joined palms, spoke as follows to the said river: -

Sloka 83

putraḥ daśarathasya ayam mahā rājasya dhīmataḥ |
nideśam pālayatu enam gange tvad abhirakṣitaḥ || 2-52-83
catur daśa hi varṣāṇi samagrāṇi uṣya kānane |
bhrātrā saha mayā caiva punaḥ pratyāgamiṣyati || 2-52-84
tataḥ tvām devi subhage kṣemeṇa punar āgatā |
yakṣye pramuditā gange sarva kāma samṛddhaye || 2-52-85

83;84;85. gaṇge = "Oh; Ganga! Ayam (let) this Rama; putraḥ = the son; dasharathasya = of Dasaratha; mahārājasya = the emperor; pālayitvā = honor; imam = this (his father's); nidesham = command; tvadabhirakshhitaḥ = under your protection! Ushhya = ḥaving dwelled; kānane = in the forest; samagrāṇi = in full; chaturdasha = for fourteen; varshhāṇi = years; pratyāgamishhyati = (may he) return; punaḥ = once more (to your bank); bhrātā saha = with his brother; Lakshmana; mayāchaiva = and myself! ṣubhage = Oh; blessed; devī = goddess; gaṇ^ge = Ganga! Punaḥ āgatā = Returning; kshhemeṇa = safely; tataḥ = then; pramuditā = I; full of joy; sarva kāma samṛiddhinī = all my desires fulfilled; yakshhye = shall worship; tvām = you."

"Oh, Ganga! Let Rama, the son of the emperor Dasaratha honor his father's command under your protection! Having dwelled in the forest in full fourteen years, may he return once more to your bank with his brother, Lakshmana and myself! Oh, blessed goddess Ganga! Returning safely, with all my desires fulfilled, I shall worship you with great joy."

Sloka 86

tvam hi tripathagā devi brahma lokam samīkṣase |
bhāryā ca udadhi rājasya loke asmin sampradṛśyase || 2-52-86

86. tvam = "you; devī = Oh; goddess; tripathagā = flowing through three regions; (namely heaven; earth and subterranean); samīkshhase hi = are indeed seeing; brahmalokam = Brahma's realm (the outermost of the six spheres enveloping the earth); sampradṛishyase = and are vividly seen; asmin = on this; loke = terrestrial plane; bhāryācha = as a consort; udadhirājasya = of the ocean king."

You, Oh goddess flowing through three regions (namely heaven, earth and subterranean regions), include in your basin the realm of Brahma (the outermost of the six spheres enveloping the earth) and are vividly seen on this terrestrial plane as a consort of the ocean king."

Sloka 87

sā tvām devi namasyāmi praśaṃsāmi ca śobhane |
prāpta rājye nara vyāghra śivena punar āgate || 2-52-87
gavām śata sahasrāṇi vastrāṇi annam ca peśalam |
brāhmaṇebhyaḥ pradāsyāmi tava priya cikīrṣayā || 2-52-88

87;88. shobhane devī = "oh; charming goddess! ṣā = I; ṣita; namasyāmi = greet; tvām = you; prashamsāmi cha = and extol you too; naravyāghre = when Rama the tiger among men; shivena = has safely; āgate = returned; punaḥ = again; prāpte rājye = and regains his kingdom; pradāsyāmi = I shall give away; shatasahasrāṇi = a lakh; gavām = of cows; peshalam = soft; vastrāṇi = clothing; annamcha = and food stuff; brahmaṇebhya = to Brahmanas; priyachikīrshhayā = with an intent to please you."

"Oh, charming goddess! I, Sita, greet you and extol you too. When Rama the tiger among men safely returns and regains his kingdom, I shall give away a lakh of cows, soft clothing and food to brahmanas with intent to please you."

Sloka 89

surāghaṭasahasreṇa māṃsabhūtodanena ca |
yakṣye tvām prayatā devi purīm punarupāgatā || 2-52-89

89. devī = "Oh; goddess! Upāgata = After reaching; purīm = the city (Ayodhya); punaḥ = again; yakshhye = I shall worship (you); surāghata sahasreṇa = with thousand pots of spirituous liquor; māmsa bhūtodanena cha = and jellied meat with cooked rice; prayatā = well-prepared for the solemn rite."

"Oh, goddess! After reaching back the city of Ayodhya, I shall worship you with thousand pots of spirituous liquor and jellied meat with cooked rice well prepared for the solemn rite."

Sloka 90

yāni tvattīravāsīni daivatāni ca santi hi |
tāni sarvāṇi yakṣyāmi tīrthānyāyatanāni ca || 2-52-90

90. yakshhyāmi = I shall worship; yāmi = whatever; daivatāni = deities; santi = are there; sarvāṇi = all; tvattīra vāsini = dwelling on your banks; tīrthāni = sacred spots; āyatanāmicha = and sanctuaries."

"I shall worship all deities dwelling on your banks as also sacred spots and sanctuaries."

Sloka 91

punareva mahābāurmayā bhrātrā ca samgataḥ |
ayodhyām vanavāsāttu praviśatvanagho.anaghe || 2-52-91

91. anaghe = "oh; irreproachable one! Anaghaḥ = (ṃay) the sinless; mahābāhuḥ = and mighty armed Rama; pravishatu = re-enter; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; punareva = again; vanavāsāt = from dwelling in the forest; sangataḥ = along with; bhrātrācha = Lakshmana; his brother; mayā = (and) myself."

"Oh, irreproachable goddess! ṃay the sinless Rama (with mighty arms) re-enter Ayodhya again from the forest, along with Lakshmana and myself."

Sloka 92

tathā sambhāṣamāṇā sā sītā gangām aninditā |
dakṣiṇā dakṣiṇam tīram kṣipram eva abhyupāgamat || 2-52-92

92. tathā = then; sambhāshha māṇā = praying (to Ganga); sīta = ṣita; dakshhiṇā = the efficient; aninditā = irreproachable; abhyupāgamat = reached; kshhiprameva = rapidly; dakshhiṇam = the right; tīram = bank.

Thus praying to Ganga, the efficient and irreproachable Sita rapidly reached the right bank of the river.

Sloka 93

tīram tu samanuprāpya nāvam hitvā nara ṛṣabhaḥ |
prātiṣṭhata saha bhrātrā vaidehyā ca param tapaḥ || 2-52-93

93. samanuprāpya = reaching; tīram = the bank; nararshhabhaḥ = Rama the best among men; paramtapaḥ = and the chastiser of foes; hitvā = left; nāvam = the boat; prātishhṭhata = and further proceeded; bhrātrāsaha = along with Lakshmana his brother; vaidehyācha = and ṣita.

Reaching the bank and leaving the boat, Rama the best among men and the chastiser of foes proceeded further along with Lakshmana and Sita.

Sloka 94

atha abravīn mahā bāhuḥ sumitra ānanda vardhanam |
bhava samrakṣaṇārthāya sajane vijane.api vā || 2-52-94

94. "Be prepared for protecting ṣita in the inhabited as well as in uninhabited areas also." W.wṃ---atha = then; mahābāhuḥ = Rama; the mighty armed; abravīt = spoke; sumitrā nandanam = to Lakshmana (who heightened the joy of ṣumitra); bhava = "Be prepared; samrakshaṇārthāya = for protecting; (ṣita); sajane = in the inhabited; vijane.apivā = and in the uninhabited areas also."

Then, Rama the mighty armed, spoke to Lakshmana (who heightened the joy of Sumitra) as follows:

Sloka 95

avaśyam rakṣaṇam kāryamadṛṣṭe vijane vane |
agrataḥ gacca saumitre sītā tvām anugaccatu || 2-52-95

95. rakshhaṇam = "security; kāryam = is to be arranged; avashyam = inevitably; adṛshhṭe = and in an unforeseen; vijane = and in an uninhabited; vane = forest. soumitre = Oh; Lakshmana! Gachchha = Go; agrataḥ = in front. ṣītā anugachchhatu = Let ṣita follow; tvām = you."

"Security is an inevitable need in a forest, which has unforeseen dangers and is uninhabited, Oh, Lakshmana! Go in front. Let Sita follow you."

Sloka 96

pṛṣṭhataḥ aham gamiṣyāmi tvām ca sītām ca pālayan |
adya duhkham tu vaidehī vana vāsasya vetsyati || 2-52-96

96. aham = "I; gamishhyāmi = shall proceed; pṛishhṭhataḥ = in the back; pālayam = protecting; tvām cha = you; sītāmcha = and ṣita; purushharshhabha = Oh; jewel among men! Rakshha = Protection; kartavyā = must be accorded; iha = here; naḥ = by us; anyonyasya = to one another."

"I shall proceed in the rear, protecting you and Sita. Oh, jewel among men! We must accord protection here to one another."

Sloka 97

na hi tāvadatikrāntā sukarā kācana kriyā |
adya duḥkham tu vaidehī vanavāsasya vetsyati || 2-52-97

97. atikrānta = "an over stepped; kāchana kriyā = doing whatsoever; ṇa hi tāvat = cannot indeed be; sukarā = smoothly done again. Vaidehī = ṣita; vetsyati = will experience; duḥkham = the hardship; vanavāsasya = of staying in a forest; adya = (only) from us."

"An act which has gone out of hand, whatsoever, cannot indeed be remedied again. Sita will experience the hardship of staying in a forest only from now."

Sloka 98

praṇaṣṭajanasambādham kṣetrārāmavivarbitam |
viṣamam ca prapātam ca vanamadya pravekṣyati || 2-52-98

98. adya = " today; pravekshhyati = (she)will enter; vanam = the forest; praṇashhṭajana sambādhām = when density of people is not seen; kshhetra arāma vivarjitam = which is utterly devoid of fields and gardens; vishhamamcha = and is full of stumbles."

"Today she will enter the forest, where density of people is not seen, which is utterly devoid of fields and gardens, has a rugged surface and is full of stumbles."

Sloka 99

śrutvā rāmasya vacanam pratisthe lakṣmaṇo.agrataḥ |
anantaram ca sītāyā rāghavo raghanandhanaḥ || 2-52-99

99. shrutvā = listening to; rāmasya = Rama's; vachanam = words; lakshmaṇaḥ = Lakshmana; pratasthe = walked; agrataḥ = in front. Anantaram = immediately after; sītāyāḥ = ṣita; rāghavaḥ = Rama; raghunandanaḥ = the delight of Raghu dynasty (advanced).

Listening to Rama's words, Lakshmana walked in front. Immediately after Sita, Rama, the delight of Raghu dynasty, advanced.

Sloka 100

gatam tu gangā para pāram āśu |
rāmam sumantraḥ pratatam nirīkṣya |
adhva prakarṣāt vinivṛtta dṛṣṭir |
rmumoca bāṣpam vyathitaḥ tapasvī || 2-52-100

100. pratatam = constantly; nirīkshhya = gazing at; rāmam = Rama; gatam = who reached; ashu = soon; gaṇ^gā para pāram = the other bank of Ganga river; tapasvī = the distressed; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; vinivṛitta dṛishhṭiḥ = his vision having failed; adhvaprakarshhāt = due to the great distance; vyathitaḥ = and perturbed as he was; mumocha = shed; bāshhpam = tears (of grief over separation from Rama).

Constantly gazing at Rama, who reached soon the other bank of Ganga River, the distressed Sumantra, his vision having failed due to the long distance and perturbed as he was, shed tears (of grief over separation from Rama).

Sloka 101

sa lokapālapratimaprabhāvavām |
stīrtvā mahātmā varado mahānadīm |
tataḥ samṛddhān śubhasasyamālinaḥ |
krameṇa vatsān muditānupāgamat || 2-52-101

101. tīrvā = having crossed; mahānadīm = the great river; saḥ = that Rama; mahātmā = the high-soled; varadaḥ = the bestowal of boons; loka pāla pratimaprabhāva vān = equal in glory with the guardians of spheres; tataḥ = then; upāgamat = reached; krameṇa = progressively; vatsān = the land of Vatsa; samRidhdhhān = and the happy; shubha sasya mālinaḥ = which contained rows of beautiful crops.

Having crossed the great river, Rama the high-soled, the bestowal of boons, equal in glory with the guardians of spheres, then reached progressively the prosperous and the happy land of Vatsa; which contained rows of beautiful crops.

Sloka 102

tau tatra hatvā caturaḥ mahā mṛgān |
varāham ṛśyam pṛṣatam mahā rurum |
ādāya medhyam tvaritam bubhukṣitau|
vāsāya kāle yayatur vanaḥ patim || 2-52-102

102. hatvā = having killed; tatra = there; chaturaḥ = four; mṛigān = deer (namely); varāham = Varāha; ṛishyam = Risya; pṛishhatam = Pṛisata; mahāruru = (and) ṃahāruru; (the four principal species of deer); ādayā = and taking; tvaritam = quickly; medhyam = the portions that were pure; tou = Rama and Lakshmana; bubhukshhitou = being hungry as they were; yayatuḥ = reached; vanaspatim = a tree; vāsayā = to take rest; kāle = in the evening.

Having hunted there four deer, namely Varāha, Rishya, Prisata; and ṃahāruru (the four principal species of deer) and taking quickly the portions that were pure, being hungry as they were, Rama and Lakshmana reached a tree to take rest in the evening.

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe dvipaṃcāśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 52nd chapter in the AyodhyaKanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
