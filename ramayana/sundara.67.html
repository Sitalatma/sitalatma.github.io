Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 67
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Hanuma narrates at length an incident connected with a crow that occured during the sojourn of Seetha with Rama at chitrakuta, which was related by Seetha by way of a token of remembrance. Seetha then describes her miserable state to Hanuma and requests him to ask Rama, Lakshmana and Sugreeva to came to Lanka to destroy Ravana together with his army. Hanuma consoles Seetha, saying that Rama and Lakshmana would reduce Lanka to ashes and take her back to Ayodhya. When Hanuma further requests Seetha to give a token of remembrance to be handed over to Rama, she gives him a jewel for the head as a token.


Sloka 1

evam uktaḥ tu hanumān rāghaveṇa mahātmanā |
sītāyā bhāṣitam sarvam nyavedayata rāghave || 5-67-1

1. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; mahātmanā = by the high-souled; rāghaveṇa = Rama; hanumān = ḥanuma; nyavedayata = reported; rāghave = to Rama; sarvam = all; bhāṣitam = the words; sītāyāḥ = of ṣeetha.

ḥearing the words of the high-souled Rama, ḥanuma reported to Rama, all the words spoken by Seetha.

Sloka 2

evam uktavatī devī jānakī puruṣa ṛṣabha |
pūrva vṛttam abhijñānam citra kūṭe yathā tatham || 5-67-2

2. puruṣarṣabha = O Rama the foremost of men!; jānakī = ṣeetha; devī = the princess; uktavatī = spoke; yathā tatham = a detailed account of an event; pūrvavṛittam = that occured previously; chitrakūṭe = in ṃount chitrakuta; abhiJṇānam = as a token of recollection.

"O Rama the foremost of men! ṣeetha, the princess, spoke a detailed account of an event which occurred long ago in ṃount chitrakuta, as a token of recollection."

Sloka 3

sukha suptā tvayā sārdham jānakī pūrvam utthitā |
vāyasaḥ sahasā utpatya virarāda stana antare || 5-67-3

3. jānakī = ṣeetha; sukha suptā = who slept happily; tvayā sārtham = with you; utthitā = got up; pūrvam = before you; vāyasaḥ = a crow; utpatya = coming down; sahasā = quickly; vidadāra = tore the flesh; stanāntare = at an area in the midst of her breasts (with its beak).

"ṣeetha, who slept happily by your side (at one instance) got up before you. A crow coming down quickly, tore her flesh at an area in the midst of her breasts (with its beak)."

Sloka 4

paryāyeṇa ca suptaḥ tvam devi anke bharata agraja |
punaḥ ca kila pakṣī sa devyā janayati vyathām || 5-67-4

4. bharata agraja = O Rama!; tvam = you; suptaḥ = slept; paryāyeṇa = in your turn; devi anke = on the lap of the princes; saḥ pakṣī = that crow; punaḥ ca = again; janayati kila vyathām = began to torment; devyāḥ = the princess.

"O Rama! You slept in your turn on the lap of the princess. ṭhat crow again began to torment the princess."

Sloka 5

punaḥ punarupāgamya virarāda bhṛśam kila |
tataḥ tvam bodhitaḥ tasyāḥ śoṇitena samukṣitaḥ || 5-67-5

5. upāgamya = coming near; punaḥ punaḥ = again and again; vidadārakila = it tore (her breast); bhṛisham = severely; tataḥ = thereafter; samukṣitah = dampened; tasyāḥ shoṇitena = by her blood; tvam = you; bodhitaḥ kila = were awakened (by her).

"Coming near again and again, it tore her breast severely. Dampened by her blood, you then were awakened by her."

Sloka 6

vāyasena ca tena eva satatam bādhyamānayā |
bodhitaḥ kila devyāḥ tvam sukha suptaḥ param tapa || 5-67-6

6. param tapa = O tormentator of enemies!; devyā = by ṣeetha the princess; bādhyamānayā = who was being tormented; satatam = ceaselessly; te vāyasenaiva = by that crow; tvam = you; sukhasuptaḥ = who were sleeping happily; bodhitaḥ kila = were awakened.

"O tormentator of enemies! ṭhough lying happily asleep, you were awakened by ṣeetha the princess, who was being ceaselessly tormented by the crow."

Sloka 7

tām tu dṛṣṭvā mahābāho rāditām ca stana antare |
āśī viṣa iva kruddho nihśvasann abhyabhāṣathāḥ || 5-67-7

7. mahābāho = O Rama with long arms! dṛiṣṭvā = seeing; tām = her; dāritām = who was lacerated; stana antare = at the midst of her breasts; kruddhaḥ = you were enraged; nishvasann āshī visha iva = like a hissing snake; abhyabhāṣathāḥ = spoke (to her as follows):

"O long-armed Rama! ṣeeing her, who was lacerated at the midst of her breasts, you were enraged like a hissing snake and spoke to her as follows."

Sloka 8

nakha agraiḥ kena te bhīru dāritam tu stana antaram |
kaḥ krīḍati saroṣeṇa panca vaktreṇa bhoginā || 5-67-8

8. bhīru = O bashful one!; kena = by whom; te stana antaram = was your mid-area of your breasts; dāritam = lacerated; nakha agraiḥ = with nail-points?; kaḥ krīDati = who is playing; saroṣeṇa = with an angry; pancha vaktreṇa = five-hooded; bhoginā = serpent?

" 'O bashful ṣeetha! By whom was the middle of your breasts lacerated with nail-points? Who is playing with an angry five-hooded serpent?"

Sloka 9

nirīkṣamāṇaḥ sahasā vāyasam samavaikṣatāḥ |
nakhaiḥ sarudhiraiḥ tīkṣṇaiḥ mām eva abhimukham sthitam || 5-67-9

9. nirīkṣamāṇaḥ = looking around; samavaikṣhatāḥ = you saw; sahasā = suddenly; vāyasam = a crew; sthitam = which perched; tāmeva abhimukham = in front of her; tīkṣṇaiḥ nakhaiḥ = with its sharp nails; sarudhiraiḥ = stained with blood.

"Looking around, you suddenly saw a crow, which perched in front of ṣeetha, with its sharp nails stained with blood."

Sloka 10

sutaḥ kila sa śakrasya vāyasaḥ patatām varaḥ |
dharā antara caraḥ śīghram pavanasya gatau samaḥ || 5-67-10

10. saḥ vāyasaḥ = that crow; varaḥ = the foremost; patatām = among the birds; shakrasya putraḥ kila = was indeed the son of Indra the lord of celestials; dhara antara caraḥ = dwelling in the middle of the mountains; samaḥ = and equal; shīghram gatau = in speed; pavanasya = to wind.

"ṭhat crow, foremost of the birds was indeed the son of Indra the lord of celestials; dwelling in the middle of the mountains and equal in speed to the wind."

Sloka 11

tataḥ tasmin mahābāho kopa samvartita īkṣaṇaḥ |
vāyase tvam kṛtvāḥ krūrām matim matimatām vara || 5-67-11

11. mahābāho = O great armed one!; matimatām vara = O excellent among the wise! Kopa samvarta īkṣhaṇaḥ = with your eyes rolling with anger; tvam = you; tataḥ = then; kṛithāḥ = harboured; krūrām = a cruel; matim = intention; tasmin vāyase = against that crow.

"O great armed Rama, the excellent among the wise! With your eyes rolling with anger, you then harboured a cruel intention against that crow."

Sloka 12

sa darbham saṃstarāt gṛhya brahma astreṇa nyayojayaḥ |
pradīpta iva kāla agniḥ jajvāla abhimukhaḥ khagam || 5-67-12

12. gṛihya = taking; darbham = a blade of grass; samstarāt = from you bed of the sacred grass; ayojayaḥ = you charged it; brāhma astreṇa = with the potency of Brahma missile; saḥ dīptaḥ = that flaming blade; jajvāla = blazed forth; kāla agniḥ iva = like the fire of universal dissolution; khagam abhimukhaḥ = with its end facing that bird.

"ṭaking a blade of grass from your bed of the sacred grass, you charged it with the potency of Brahma missile. That flaming blade blazed forth like the fire of universal dissolution, with its end facing the bird."

Sloka 13

kṣiptavāṃstvam pradīptam darbham tam vāyasam prati |
tataḥ tu vāyasam dīptaḥ sa darbho anujagāma ha || 5-67-13

13. tvam = you; kṣiptavān = hurled; tam pradīptam darbham = that flaming blade of grass; vāyasam prati = towards the crow; tataḥ = then; saḥ dīptaḥ darbhaḥ = that flaming blade of grass; anujagāma = followed; vāyasam = the crow.

"You hurled that flaming blade of grass towards the crow. ṭhen that flaming blade of grass for its part followed the crow."

Sloka 14

sa pitrā ca parityaktaḥ suraiḥ sarvaiḥ maharṣibhiḥ |
trīn lokān samparikramya trātāram na adhigacchati || 5-67-14

14. partiyaktaḥ = forsaken; suraisḥ ca = by all the celestials; pitrā ca = its father; Indra; sa maharṣibhiḥ = and the sages also; saḥ = (that crows) na adhigacchati = did not find; trātāram = a protector; samparikramya = after going round; trīn lokān = the three worlds (heave; earth and the lower regions).

"Forsaken by all the celestials, Indra, its father and the sages too, that crow did not find a protector even after going round all the three worlds (heaven, earth and the lower regions)."

Sloka 15

punarevāgatastrastastvatsakāśamariṃdama |
sa tam nipatitam bhūmau śaraṇyaḥ śaraṇā gatam || 5-67-15
vadha arham api kākutstha kṛpayā paripālayaḥ |

15. arindama = O destroyer of enemies!; trastaḥ = frightened; (the crow); āgataḥ = came; tvatsakāsham = to your presence; punareva = once again; vadhārham api = though fit to be killed; saḥ kākutthsaḥ = that Rama; sharaṇyaḥ = affording shelter; paryapālayaḥ = protected; kṛipanā = with mercy; tam = to that crow; nipatitam = which had fallen; bhūmau = flat on the ground; sharaṇāgatam = and who sought your protection.

"O destroyer of enemies! Being frightened the crow came to your presence once again and sough your protection. Though fit to be killed, you on your part, affording shelter, in mercy to the crow, protected that crow which had fallen flat on the ground."

Sloka 16

mogham astram na śakyam tu kartum iti eva rāghava || 5-67-16
bhavāṃstasyakṣi kākasya hinasti sma sa dakṣiṇam |

16. rāghava = O Rama!; ityeva = because; na shakyam = it is not possible; kartum = to make; astram = the missile; mogham = a waste; bhavān = you; hinanti sma = destroyed; dakṣinam akṣi = the right eye; kākasya = of the cow.

'O Rama! ṣince it is not possible to make the missile a waste, you destroyed the right eye of the crow."

Sloka 17

rāma tvām sa namaḥ kṛtvā rājño daśarathasya ca || 5-67-17
visṛṣṭaḥ tu tadā kākaḥ pratipede kham ālayam |

14. partiyaktaḥ = forsaken; suraisḥ ca = by all the celestials; pitrā ca = its father; Indra; sa maharṣibhiḥ = and the sages also; saḥ = (that crows) na adhigacchati = did not find; trātāram = a protector; samparikramya = after going round; trīn lokān = the three worlds (heave; earth and the lower regions).

"O Rama! ṭhen, that crow, having been discharged by you, went back to its own habitation, after offering salutation to you and to king Dasaratha."

Sloka 18

evam astravidām śreṣṭhaḥ sattvavān śīlavān api || 5-67-18
kim artham astram rakṣahsu na yojayasi rāghava |

18. rāghava = O Rama!; kim artham = why; na yojayasi = are you not employing; astram = the missile; evam = thus; rakṣhassu = on the demons; shreṣṭhaḥ api = though foremost; astravidām = among the wielders courageous; shīlavān = and possessed of good disposition.

"O Rama! Why are you not employing the same type of a missile on the demons, though you are the foremost among the wielders of missiles, though courageous and possessed of good disposition?"

Sloka 19

na nāgā na api gandharvā na asurā na marut gaṇāḥ || 5-67-19
na ca sarve raṇe śaktā rāmam pratisamāsitum |

19. nāgāḥ = ṇagas the celestials serpents; na shaktāḥ = are not capable; na gandharvāḥ api = ṇor even Gandharvas the celestial musicians; na marut gaṇāḥ = nor the troops of maruts; the na sarve = nor all of them put together; pratisamāsitum = of fighting; rāmam prati = against Rama; raṇe = in battle.

"ṇeither the ṇagas the celestials serpents nor even Gandharvas the celestial musicians nor the demons nor the troop of ṃaruts the host of gods, nor all of them joined together are capable of fighting with Rama."

Sloka 20

tasya vīryavataḥ kaccin mayi yadi asti sambhramaḥ || 5-67-20
kṣipram suniśitaiḥ bāṇaiḥ hanyatām yudhi rāvaṇaḥ |

20. yadi = If; vīryavataḥ tasya = to that powerful Rama; sambhramaḥ asti = there is eagerness; mayi = in my matter; hanyatām rāvaṇaḥ = let Ravana; be killed; yudhi = in battle; kṣhipram = quickly; sunishitaiḥ bāṇaiḥ = with very sharp arrows.

"If that powerful Rama is eager to save me, let him kill Ravana in battle quickly with his exceptionally sharp arrows."

Sloka 21

bhrātuḥ ādeśam ādāya lakṣmaṇo vā param tapaḥ || 5-67-21
sa kim artham nara varo na mām rakṣati rāghavaḥ |

21. kim artham = why; saḥ lakṣmaṇo vā = even that Lakshmana; param tapaḥ = the annihilator of adversaries; naravaraḥ = the foremost among men; rāghavaḥ = born in Raghu dynasty; na rakṣhati = is not saving; mām = me; āJṇāya = with permission; ādesham = (by way of) an order; bhrātuḥ = of Rama his brother?

"Why even that Lakshmana, the annihilator of adversaries and the foremost of men born in Raghu dynasty is not saving me, by taking orders from Rama his brother?"

Sloka 22

śaktau tau puruṣa vyāghrau vāyv agni sama tejasau || 5-67-22
surāṇām api durdharṣau kim artham mām upekṣataḥ |

22. kim artham = wherefore; tau shaktau = those two competent brothers; Rama and Lakshmana; vāyv agni sama tejasau = having their splendour similar to that of wind and fire; puruṣa vyāghrau = and the excellent among men; durdharṣau.api = even though they can conquer; surāṇām = the celestials; upekṣataḥ = are ignoring; mām = me?

"Wherefore those two competent brothers Rama and Lakshmana, with their splendour equal to that of air and fire, and excellent among men, even though they can conquer the celestials, are ignoring me?"

Sloka 23

mama eva duṣkṛtam kiṃcin mahat asti na saṃśayaḥ || 5-67-23
samarthau sahitau yan mām na apekṣete param tapau |

23. kimchit = some; mahat = great; duṣkṛitam = impure act; asti = was there; mamaiva = on my part; na samshayaḥ = there is no doubt; yat = for which reason; tau = those two brothers; Rama and Lakshmana; param tapau = who torment the enemies; samarthāvapi = eventhough capable; na avekṣate = are not paying attention; mām = to me.

"ṭhere is no doubt that I had done some big impure act, for which reason those two brothers Rama and Lakshmana, who torment the enemies, even though capable, are not paying attention to me."

Sloka 24

vaidehyā vacanam śrutvā karuṇam sāśru bhāṣitam || 5-67-24
punaḥ api aham āryām tām idam vacanam abruvam |

24. shrutvā = hearing; karuṇam = the miserable; vachanam = words; vaidehyāḥ = of ṣeetha; sāshru bhāṣitam = spoken with tears; aham = I; punaḥ api = again; abruvam = spoke; tām āryām = to that venerable ṣeetha; idam vachanam = the following words:

ḥearing the miserable words of ṣeetha spoken with tears, I again spoke to that venerable Seetha the following words:

Sloka 25

tvat śoka vimukho rāmo devi satyena te śape || 5-67-25
rāme duhkha abhibhūte ca lakṣmaṇaḥ paritapyate |

25. devi = O ṣeetha the princess!; rāmaḥ = Rama; tvat shoka vimukhaḥ = has grown indifferent because of grief born of separation from you; shape = I swear; te = to you; satyena = truly; rāme = Rama; duhkha abhibhūte = being overcome with grief; lakṣmaṇaḥ cha = Lakshmana too; paritapyate = is feeling pain.

" 'O ṣeetha the princess! Rama has grown indifferent because of grief born of separation from you. I swear it to you truly. Rama, being overwhelmed with grief, Lakshmana too is feeling pain."

Sloka 26

kathaṃcit bhavatī dṛṣṭā na kālaḥ pariśocitum || 5-67-26
asminmuhūrtam duhkhānām antam drakṣyasi bhāmini |

26. anindite = O faultless one!; bhavatī = you; dṛiṣṭā = have been seen; kathamchit = somehow; na kālam = this is not time; devitum = to lament; drakṣyasi = you will see; antam = the end; duhkhānām = of your sorrows; imam muhūrtam = during this very moment.

" 'O faultless one! You can give now, a token of recollection, which Rama can recognize and which can beget happiness to him."

Sloka 27

tāv ubhau nara śārdūlau rāja putrāv arim damau || 5-67-27
tvat darśana kṛta utsāhau lankām bhasmī kariṣyataḥ |

27. ubhau tau raja putrau = those two princes; nara shārdūlau = the foremost among men; aninditau = and the faultless ones; tavat darshana kṛita utsāhau = who are eager to see you; laṇ^kām bhasmī kariṣyataḥ = can reduce Lanka to ashes.

" 'ṭhose two princes, the foremost among men and the faultless ones are eager to see you. They can reduce Lanka to ashes'."

Sloka 28

hatvā ca samare raudram rāvaṇam saha bāndhavam || 5-67-28
rāghavaḥ tvām mahābāhuḥ svām purīm nayate dhruvam |

28. varārohe = O lady with charming hips!; hatvā cha = killing; rondram = the terrific; rāvaṇam = Ravana; saha bāndhavam = together with his relatives; samare = in battle; rāghavaḥ = Rama; nayate = will take; tvām = you; svām purīm = to his City of Ayodhya; dhruvam = It is certain.

" 'O lady with charming hips! Killing the terrific Ravana together with his relatives in battle, Rama will certainly take you to his City of Ayodhya'."

Sloka 29

yat tu rāmo vijānīyāt abhijñānam anindite || 5-67-29
prīti samjananam tasya pradātum tattvam arhasi |

29. anindite = O faultless one!; tvam = you; arhasi = ought; dātum = to give; iha = now; abhiJṇānam = a token of recollection; yat = which; rāmaḥ = Rama; vijānīyāt = can recognize; prīti samjananam = and which can beget happiness; tasya = to him.

" 'O faultless one! You can give now, a token of recollection, which Rama can recognize and which can beget happiness to him."

Sloka 30

sā abhivīkṣya diśaḥ sarvā veṇi udgrathanam uttamam || 5-67-30
muktvā vastrāt dadau mahyam maṇim etam mahābala |

30. mahābala = O mighty Rama!; abhivīkṣya = having surveyed; sarvāḥ = all; dishaḥ = the directions; muktvā = and untying; vastrāt = from the end of her garment; etam uttamam maṇim = this excellent jewel; veṇudgrathitam = which was being strung together in her hair; sā = the ṣeetha; dadau = gave it; mahyam = to me.

"O mighty Rama! ḥaving surveyed all the directions and having untied from the end of her garment, this excellent jewel which was being strung together in her hair that Seetha gave it to me."

Sloka 31

pratigṛhya maṇim divyam tava heto raghu uttama || 5-67-31
śirasā sampraṇamya enām aham āgamane tvare |

31. raghūdvaha = O Rama!; pratigṛihya = taking; divyam maṇim = that wonderful jewel; tava hetoḥ = for your sake; praṇamya = and offering my salutation; shirasā = by bowing my head; āryām tām = to that venerable ṣeetha; aham = I; tvare = was seized with a haste; āgamane = to come back.

"O Rama! ṭaking that wonderful jewel for your sake from her, and offering my salutation by bowing my head to that venerable Seetha, I was seized with a haste to come back."

Sloka 32

gamane ca kṛta utsāham avekṣya vara varṇinī || 5-67-32
vivardhamānam ca hi mām uvāca janaka ātmajā |

32. avekṣya = seeing; mām = me; kṛita utsāham = who was in a hurry; gamane = to come back; vivardhamānam cha = and augmenting my body abundantly; jānaka atmajā = ṣeetha; varavarṇinī = having a beautiful complexion; uvācha = spoke.

"ṣeeing me who was in a hurry to come back and augmenting my body abundantly, ṣeetha having a beautiful complexion spoke to me."

Sloka 33

aśru pūrṇa mukhī dīnā bāṣpa saṃdigdha bhāṣiṇī || 5-67-33
mamotpatanasambrāntā śokavegasamāhatā |

33. ashru pūrṇa mukhī = with her eyes filled with tears; dīnā = looking miserable; baṣpa samdigdha bhāṣiṇī = with ambiguous words coming out of her choked throat; mama utpatana sambhrāntā = bewildered as she was that I was going to fly away; (she spoke to me as follows); shokavega samāhatā = afflicted with a gush a grief.

"With her eyes filled with tears, looking miserable, with her words ambiguously coming out of her coked throat, bewildered as she was, at the though of my flying away and afflicted with a gush of grief; (she spoke to me as follows):

Sloka 34

hanuman simha samkāśau tāv ubhau rāma lakṣmaṇau || 5-67-34
sugrīvam ca saha amātyam sarvān brūyā anāmayam |

34. hanumān = O ḥanuma!; brūyāḥ = ask; tau ubhau rāma lakṣmaṇau = those two brothers; Rama and Lakshmana; sugrīvam cha = ṣugreeva; saha amātyam = together with his ministers; sarvān = and all others; anāmayam = about their well-being.

"O ḥanuma! Inquire those two brothers Rama and Lakshmana looking like lions, ṣugreeva together with his ministers and all others about their welfare."

" 'You ought to clear up difficulties for that mighty armed Rama, so that he can help me to cross this ocean of sorrow. O best of heroes among monkeys! Approaching Rama, tell this horrible gush of my grief and the threats of these female-demons. Have a happy journey!' "O illustrious princes, the protector of men! The venerable Seetha filled with sorrow spoke these words to be conveyed to you. Comprehending this matter spoken by me, believe that Seetha is completely safe."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye suṃdarakāṇḍe sptaṣaṣṭitamaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 67th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
