chapter[Sarga] 14

Introduction

This chapter starts with Kaikeyi's entreaties to the king followed by Dasaratha disowning her as wife. The night passes and Sumantra comes to wake up the King. Ordered by Kaikeyi to bring Sri Rama to the king, he goes to Sri Rama to fetch Him.

Sloka 1

putra śoka arditam pāpā visamjñam patitam bhuvi |
viveṣṭamānam udīkṣya sā aikṣvākam idam abravīt || 2-14-1

1. sā pāpā = that wicked woman; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; ikshhvākam = to Dasaratha who was born in Ikshvāku dynasty; putrashokārditam = tormented by the anguish for his son; patitam = who was fallen; visajJṇam = unconscious; viveshhṭamāna m = tossing about; bhuvi = on the floor.

That wicked woman spoke these words to Dasaratha, tormented as he was by the anguish for his son and who was unconscious, tossing about on the floor.

Sloka 2

pāpam kRtvā iva kim idam mama saṃśrutya saṃśravam |
śeṣe kṣiti tale sannaḥ sthityām sthātum tvam arhasi || 2-14-2

2. kim = what is; idam = this ? samshrutya = after hearing; mama = my; samshravam = promise; sheshhe = you are lying; kshhititale = on the floor; sannaḥ = with grief; pāpam kṛitva ivā = as; though you had perpetrated a sithtyām = you; arhasi = ought to; sthātum = keep; sthityām = within bounds of morality.

"What is this? After hearing the promise given to me, you are lying on the floor dejected as though you had committed a great sin. You ought to keep yourself within bounds of ethics".

Sloka 3

āhuḥ satyam hi paramam dharmam dharmavido janāḥ |
satyam āśritya hi mayā tvam ca dharmam pracoditaḥ ||2-14-3

3. janāḥ = People; dharma vidaḥ = who know what is right; āhuḥ hi = indeed speak; satyam = of truthfulness; paramam dharmam = as highest virtue; tvam = you; prabodhitaḥ = are made aware of; dharmam = (your)duty; mayācha = by me; āshritya = who has taken refuge; satyam = in truth.

"People who know what is right, indeed speak of truthfulness as highest virtue. I too have taken refuge in truth and made you aware of your duty."

Sloka 4

4. rājā = Oh; king! samshrutya = having promised; svām = his own; tanum = body; shyenāya pakshhiṇe = to a bird called hawk; shaibyaḥ = ṣaibya; jagatīpatiḥ = the lord of the world; jagāma = obtained; uttamām = the highest; gatim = destiny.

"Oh, king! Having made a promise to a hawk and offering his body to the bird , king Saibya, the ruler of world obtained the greatest destiny".

Sloka 5

5. tathā = In that manne; alarkaḥ = alarka; tejasvī = the glorious man; uddhṛitya = plucking; svake = his own; netre = eyes; dadau hi = indeed gave; avimanāḥ = remorselessly; brāhmaṇe = to the brahmana; veda pārage = skilled in the veda; yāchamāne = when asked for them.

"In that manner, Alarka the glorious man plucking his own eyes, indeed gave remorselessly, to a brahmana skilled in the Veda, When asked for them."

Sloka 6

saritām tu patiḥ svalpām maryādām satyam anvitaḥ |
satya anurodhāt samaye velām khām na ativartate || 2-14-6

6. satyam anvitaḥ = Following truthfulness ; saritām patiḥ tu = ocean; the lord of rivers; samaye = at the time of flow-tide; nātivartate = does not transgress; svām = its; velām = limit; maryādām = the boundary; svalpām = (even)to a small extent; satyānurodhāt = because of compliance to truth.

"Following ruthfulness, ocean the lord of rivers even at the time of flow-tide, does not transgress even to a small extent its boundary because of its compliance to truth."

Sloka 7

styamekapadaṃ brahme satye dharmaḥ pratiṣṭhataḥ|

7. satyam = ṭruth; ekapadam = in the word; brahma = (is)Brahma; satye = on truth; dharmaḥ = is righteousness; pratishhṭitaḥ = eshtablished; satyameva = truth indeed; vedāḥ = is knowledge; akshhayāḥ = which is imperishable; satyaineva = by truth alone; param = the supreme being; āpyate = is obtained.

"Truth is one word and is Brahma. On truth is righteousness established. Truth indeed is the knowledge imperishable. By truth alone, the supreme being is obtained".

Sloka 8

8. sattama = Oh the best of men! matiḥ dhṛitāyadi = if your mind is fixed; dharme = on piety; samanuvartasva = comfirm to; satyam = truth; astu = let; me = my; varaḥ = boon; saphalaḥ = bear fruit; asiḥ = you are indeed; varadaḥ = bestower of boons!.

"O the best among men! firmly adhere to truth, if your mind is fixed on piety. Since you are a bestower of boons, let my aforesaid prayer be granted.

Sloka 9

"In the matter, yearning righteousness and to concede to my request, send Rama the son to exile. I am telling to you three times".

Sloka 10

10. ārya = Oh; the venerable man ! tvam karishhyasi yadi = if you do not implement; imam = this; mama = my; samayam = agreement; parityaktvā = having been left over; parityakshhyāmi = I will give up; jīvitam = life; te agrataḥ = in front of you.

"Oh, the venerable man! If you do not implement our agreement, it means you have abandoned me. Hence I will give up my life here in front of you".

Sloka 11

evam pracoditaḥ rājā kaikeyyā nirviśankayā |
na aśakat pāśam unmoktum balir indra kRtam yathā || 2-14-11

11. evam = ṭhus; prachoditaḥ = by Kaikeyi; nirvishaṇkayā = without hesitation; rajā = king Dasaratha; indrakṛitam yathā = as trapped by Indra; nā shakat = was not able; moktum = to untie; pāsham = the cord.

Thus compelled by Kaikeyi, who had no uneasiness in her mind, king Dasaratha could not untie the cord of plighted word that fettered him , any more than Bali could unloose the noose placed (round his body) by Indra(through his younger brother Vamana in order to deprive him of his sovereignty of the three worlds).

Sloka 12

Like a bullock throbbing between yoke and the wheel , Dasaratha got agitated in heart and became pale in his face.

Sloka 13

vihvalābhyām ca netrābhyām apaśyann iva bhūmipaḥ |

13. bhūpatiḥ = ṭhe king; vihvalābhyām netrabhyām = with bedimmed eyes; apashyanniva = was unable; as it were to see; samstabhya = stood firm; dhairyeṇa = with courage; kṛichchhrāt = and with difficulty; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words; kaikeyīm = to Kaikeyi.

King Dasaratha, with bedimmed eyes, was unable, as it were, to see. But with difficulty, he controlled himself by recourse to firmness and spoke to Kaikeyi as follows.

Sloka 14

yaḥ te mantra kRtaḥ pāṇir agnau pāpe mayā dhRtaḥ |
tam tyajāmi svajam caiva tava putram saha tvayā || 2-14-14

14. pāpe = Oh; wicked woman! tyajāmi = Iabandon; te = your; yaḥ pāṇiḥ = hand; which; dhṛitaḥ = was clasped; mayā = by me; agnau = in the presence of nuptial fire; mantrakṛitaḥ = when it was consecrated by sacred recitations; tava putram chaiva = as also your son; svajam = begotten by me; tvayā saha = along with yourself.

"Oh, wicked woman! I abandon your hand, which was clasped by me in presence of nuptial fire, when it was consecrated by sacred recitations as also your son begotten by me along with yourself".

Sloka 16

Sloka 18

Sloka 19

" Oh, king ! You are uttering words, which cannot be swallowed easily like in a painful throat disease. You ought to summon your son Rama here without any delay."

Sloka 24

ṃeanwhile, the night began to become clear, with the rising of sun. While the auspicious, moment united with lunar mansion approaching, the sage Vashishta who is rich in virtues surrounded by his disciples entered the capital city of Ayodhya briskly, having procured the requisite materials for Rama's coronation.

"These pitchers filled with holy waters of ganga , gold pitchers with sea water and an excellent seat with Udambara wood came for the purpose of Rama's installation ceremony. Sloka 42

42. tvarayasya = ḥasten; mahār ājam = the emperor; yathā = so that; rāmaḥ = Rama; avāpnuyāt = will get the kingdom; ahani = when the day ; samudite = gets started; puṇye = at the auspicious; nakshhatra yogecha = occation of constallation called pushya.

Hasten the emperor, so that Rama will get the throne when the day gets started at the auspicious moment of pushya constellation uniting with the moon"

Sloka 43

43. sūte putraḥ = ṣumantra; shrutvāa = hearing; iti = th ese; vachaḥ = words; tasya mahātmanaḥ = of Vasista; the great souled; pravivesha = entered; niveshanam = the gynaeceum; stuvan = enlogging; nṛipati shārdūlam = Dasaratha; that tiger among kings.

Hearing these words Vashishta the great souled, Sumantra entered the gynaecium along with Dasaratha, the tiger among kings.

Sloka 44

The doormen , who were wishing to do kindness to king, could not obstruct Sumantra, who came before hand who was senior and who was highly esteemed by the king.

Sloka 45

Standing besides the king and ignorant of that situation, Sumantra started praising the king with very pleasing words.

Sloka 46

Sumantra, standing with joined palms in the gynaecium of the king, eulogised the king according to the appropriate moment as follows.

Sloka 47

yathā nandati tejasvī sāgaro bhāskarodaye |
prītah prītena manasā tathānandaghanaḥ svataḥ || 2-14-47

47. yatha = ḥow; tejasvī = the splendid ; sāgaraḥ = ocean; nandati = gladdens; bhaskar odaye = at the time of sunrise; tathā = so; ānanda ghanaḥ = you filled with delight; svataḥ = by nature; prītaḥ = gladden (us); manasā = with your mind; prītena = delighted.

"How the splendid ocean gladdens at the time of sunrise, so do you by nature filled with delight, gladden us with your delighted mind."

Sloka 48

indramasyāṃ tu veldāyāmabhituṣṭāva mātaliḥ || 2-14-48

"At the very hour of sunrise, ṃatali (Indra's charioteer)praised Indra and the latter conquered all the demons. In the same manner, I am awakening you."

Sloka 49

49. tathā = ṣo; aham = I; bodhayāmi = am awakening; tvām = you; adya = now; yathā = as ; vedāḥ = vedas; sahāṇga vidyāḥ = along with the knowledge of limbs of the body; bodhayanti = guide** vibhum = Lord; brahmāṇam = Brahma; ātma ; bhuvam = self born

"As Vedas along with the knowledge of the limbs of the body guide Lord Brahma (the creator) who is self born, so am I awakening you now."

Brahma derives his knowledge of objects to be evolved at the beginning of creation from the Vedas.

Sloka 50

50. yathā = Even as ādityaḥ = sun; sahachandreṇa = along with moon ; bodhayati = awaken; shubhām = the beautiful; pṛithivīm = earth; bhūtadharām = which sustains the ; living beings; tathā = so; aham = I; bodhayāmi = am awakening ; tvām = you."

"Even as the sun along with the moon awaken the beautiful earth which sustains the beings so, am I awakening you."

Sloka 51

uttiṣṭhāśu mahārāja kṛtakautukamaṅgaldaḥ || 2-14-51

51. mahārāja = Oh; ṃajesty!; u ttishhṭhaḥ = raise up; divākaraḥ iva = like a sun ; ; meroḥ = from mount ṃeru; vapushhā = with body; virājamānaḥ = shining brilliantly; kṛita kantuka ; maṇ^gaLaḥ = and dressed suitably for the auspicious ceremony.

"Having dressed suitably for the auspicious ceremony and shining brightly with you personality, raise up. Oh, ṃajesty, like the sun from mount ṃeru."

Sloka 52

"Oh, Dasaratha born in Kakutsa dynasty! ṃay the Gods -Sun and the ṃoon Shiva and Kubera, Varuna, Agni and Indra bestow you victory!"

Sloka 53

"Oh, the best of the kings ! The holy night has gone by. Know what has been done and do what has to be done. All the requirements for Rama's coronation is kept ready" .

Sloka 55

"The venerable Vashishta himself stands waiting at the gate along with brahmanas, being saluted saluted with joined palms by citizens, village folk and merchants."

Sloka 56

King Dasaratha hearing his words which were soothing and meaningful, was surrounded with grief once more.

That king who was pious and glorious, having lost joy about his son, looked up with sorrowful red eyes and spoke those to Sumantra. "You are chopping off my vitals further more, with your words!."

"Oh Sumantra! The king, being smitten by emotional joy in relation to Rama, who tired of awakening the whole night and got subjected to sleep"

Sloka 63

"Oh, Sumantra! Hence, quickly go and bring the glorious prince Rama. Blessedness to you! Do not have any hesitation in this matter. "

"He was thinking of that auspicious occasion in heart and rejoiced. With pleasure, he set out, by the orders of the king."

Instigated by her, Sumantra thought that the righteous Rama would come there certainly for coronation.

Sloka 66

Sumantra thus thinking and dwelling in a great joy, set out with a desire to see the long armed Rama

Sloka 67
