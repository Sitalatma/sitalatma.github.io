Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 120
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

When Indra the lord of celestials asks Rama for a boon, Rama requested Indra to bring back to life, all the monkeys who had lost their life in the battle. Indra grants the boon and all the dead monkeys are restored to life. The celestials disperse and the whole army of monkeys enjoy their well-earned rest.

Sloka 1

pratiprayāte kākutthse mahendraḥ pākaśāsanaḥ |
abravīt paramaprīto rāghavaṃ prāñjaliṃ sthitam || 6-120-1

1. kākutthse pratiyāte = when Dasaratha returned; mahendraḥ = the Lord Indra; pāka shāsanaḥ = the destoryer of Paka; the demons; parama prītaḥ = was very much pleased; abravīt = and spoke; rāghavam = to Rama; sthitam = who stood; prāṇ^jalim = with joined palm.

When Dasaratha returned, the Lord Indra, the destroyer of Paka the demon, was very much pleased and spoke to Rama, who stood there with his oined palms.

Sloka 2

amoghaṃ darśanaṃ rāma tavāsmākaṃ nararṣabha |
prītiyuktāḥ sma tena tvaṃ brūhi yanmanasepsitam || 6-120-2

2. rāma = O Rama; nararṣhabha = the foremost among men!; asmākam you; darshanam = sight of us; tava = to you; amogham = should not go in vain; sma = we are; prītiyuktāḥ = endowed with delight; tena = therefore; tvam = you; brūhī = tell; yat = whatevr; īpsitam = is desized; manasā = by your mind.

"O Rama, the foremost among men! Your sight of us, should not go in vain. Therefore, tell us whatever is desired by you."

Sloka 3

evamukto mahendreṇa prasannena mahātmanā |
suprasannamanā hṛṣṭo vacanam prāha rāghavaḥ || 6-120-3

3. evam = thus; uktaḥ = spoken; mahātmanā = by the great-souled; mahendreṇa = Indra the lord of celestials; prasannena = having been pleased; rāghavah = Rama; suprasannātmā = with the most placid mind; prāha = spoke; vachanam = the following words; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = with rejoice:

Hearing the words of the great souled Indra the lord of celestials, after having been pleased, Rama with the most placid mind spoke the following words with rejoice:

Sloka 4

yadi prītiḥ samutpannā mayi te vibudheśvara |
vakṣyāmi kuru me satyam vacanaṃ vadatāṃ vara || 6-120-4

4. vadatām vara = O the foremost among the eloquent!; vibudheshva = O Indra!; te mayi prīti samutpannāyadi = if you have affection in me; kuru = make; me vachanam = my words; vakṣhyāmi = that I tell you; satyam = real.

"O the foremst among the eloquent! O Indra! If you have affection in me, I shall speak out my words. ṃake my words real."

Sloka 5

mama hetoḥ parākrāntā ye gatā yamasādanam |
te sarve jīvitaṃ prāpya samuttiṣṭhantu vānarāḥ || 6-120-5

5. ye te sarve vānarāḥ = (Let) all those monkeys; yatāḥ yama sādanam = who reached the abode of Death; parākrāntāḥ = after displaying their energy; mama hetoḥ = for my sake; prāpya jīvitam = restore their life; samuttiṣhṭhantu = and get up.

"Let all the monkeys, who reached the abode of death, after displaying their energyfor

Sloka 6

matkṛte viprayuktā ye putrairdāraiśca vānarāḥ |
tān prītamanasaḥ sarvān draṣṭumicchāmi mānada || 6-120-6

6. mānada = O the bestower of honour!; ichchhami = I wish; draṣhṭum = to see; tān sarvān = all those; vānarāḥ = monkeys; ye = who; matkṛite = for my sake; viprayuktāḥ = were removed from; putraiḥ = their sons; dāraishcha = and wives; prīta manasaḥ = delighted at heart.

"O the bestower of honour! I wish to see all those monkeys, who for my sake, were removed from their sons and wives, be delighted at heart."

Sloka 7

vikrāntāścāpi śūrāśca na mṛtyuṃ gaṇayanti ca |
kṛtayatnā vipannāśca jīvayainān puraṃdara || 6-120-7

7. vikrāntāḥ cha = (ṭhey all) displayed their energies; shūrāḥ = were valiant; na gaṇayanti cha = and did not take; into account; mṛityum = their death; kṛitayatnāaḥ = they; made their strenuous efforts; vipannāḥ cha = and died; puramdara = O Indra!; jīvaya = restore their lives; etān = to them.

"They were all valiant, proving their energies and did not take their death into account. They made their strenuous efforts and died. O Indra! Restore their lives to them."

Sloka 8

matpriyeṣvabhiraktāśca na mṛtyuṃ gaṇayanti ye |
tvatprasādātsameyuste varametamahaṃ vṛṇe || 6-120-8

8. te = those monkeys; ye = who; na gaṇayanti = did not take into account; mṛityum = their death; abhiraktāḥ matpriyeṣhu = and who were fond of doing favour to me; te sameyuḥ = let them get reunited; (with their near and dear ones); tvatprasādāt = by your grace; aham = I; vṛiṇe = seek; etam = this varam = boon (of you).

"Those mokeys - who coutned death as nothing and who were fond of doing favour to me - let them get re-united with their near and dear ones by your grace. I seek this boon of you."

Sloka 9

nīrujo nirvraṇāṃścaiva saṃpannabalapauruṣān |
golāṅgūlāṃstatharkṣāṃśca draṣṭumicchāmi mānada || 6-120-9

9. mānada = O bestower of honour; ichchhāmi = I wish; draṣhṭum = to see; golāṇgūlān = the monkeys; tathā = and; ṛikṣhāmshcha = bears; nīrujaḥ = free from pain; nirvraṇāmshchaiva = even free from wounds; sampannabala pauruṣhān = and having augmented strength and valour.

"O bestower of honour! I wish to see the monkeys and bears, free from wounds and pains, with augmented strength and valour."

Sloka 10

akāle cāpi puṣpāṇi mūlāni ca phalāni ca |
nadyaśca vimalāstatra tiṣṭheyuryatra vānarāḥ || 6-120-10

10. yatra = whereever; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; tiṣhṭheyuḥ = stay; tatra = let there be; vimalāḥ nadyashcha = rivers with pure water; puṣhpāṇi = flowers; mūlāni cha = roots; phalānicha = and fruits; akālecha api = even in an off-season.

"Wherever the aforesaid monkeys live, let there be rivers with crystal-clear water, flowers, roots and fruits even in an off-season."

Sloka 11

śrutvā tu vacanaṃ tasya rāghavasya mahātmanaḥ |
mahendrah pratyuvācedaṃ vacanaṃ prītisaṃyutam || 6-120-11

11. shrutvā tu = hearing vachanam = the words; tasya mahātmanaḥ rāghavasya = of that great-souled Rama; mahendraḥ = Indra the lord of celestials; prīti samyutam = endowed with delight; pratuvācha = again spoke; idam vachanam = the following words:

Hearing the words of that great-souled Rama, Indra the lord of celestials endowed as he was with delight, again spoke the following words:

Sloka 12

mahānayaṃ varastāta yastvayokto raghuttama |
dvirmayā noktapurvaṃ ca tasmādevadbhaviṣyati || 6-120-12

12. tāta raghūttama = O beloved Rama!; ayam varaḥ = this boon; yaḥ uktaḥ = which has been sought; tvayā = by you; mahān = is very great (is hard to gratn); na ukta pūrvam = nothing was spoken before; mayā = by me; dviḥ = twice; tasmāt = therefore; etat = this; evam bhaviṣhyati = will come to be.

"O beloved Rama! This boon, which has been sought by you is hard to grant. No offer has, however, been revised by me in the past. Therefore this will come to be."

Sloka 13

samuttiṣṭhantu te sarve hatā ye yudhi rākṣasaiḥ |
ṛkṣāśca saha gopucchairnikṛttānanabāhavaḥ || 6-120-13

13. te sarve = (Let) all those monkeys; ṛikṣhāshcha = and bears; gopuchchhaiḥ = along with long-tailed monkeys; ye hatāḥ = who have been killed; yudhi = in battle; rākṣhasaiḥ = by the demons; nikṛittanana bāhavaḥ = and whose heads and arms have been severed; samuttiṣhṭhantu = be active again.

"Let all those monkeys and bears along with long-tailed monkeys, who have been killed in battle, by the demons and whose heads and arms have been severed, be alive and active again."

Sloka 14

nīrujo nirvriṇāścava saṃpannabalapauruṣāḥ |
samutthāsyanti harayaḥ suptā nidrākṣaye yathā || 6-120-14

14. nīrujaḥ = without any ill-health; nirvraṇāshchaiva = without any and with boosted strength and valour; harayaḥ = the monkeys; samutthāsyanti = will rise again; suptāḥ yathā = as those fallen asleep (rise); nidrākṣhaye = at the end of their sleep.

"Without any ill-health andwounds and with boosted strength and valour, the monkeys will srise again, as those fallen asleep would do at the end of their sleep."

Sloka 15

suhṛdbhirbāndhavaiścava jñātibhiḥ svajanena ca |
sarva eva sameṣyanti saṃyuktāḥ parayā mudā || 6-120-15

15. sarve eva = all of them; parayā mudā = with great delight; sameṣhyanti = will get re-united with; suhṛidbhiḥ = their friends; samyuktāḥ = together with; bāndhavaishchaiva = their relatives; jJṇātibhiḥ = kinsmen; svajanena = and family-members.

"All of them, full of delight, will get re-united with their friends, relatives, kinsmen and family members."

Sloka 16

akāle puṣpaśabalāḥ phalavantaśca pādapāḥ |
bhaviṣyanti maheṣvāsa nadyaśca nalilāyutāḥ || 6-120-16

16. maheṣhvāsa = O the wielder of a great bow!; pādapāḥ = the trees; bhaviṣhyanti = will be; puṣpashabalāḥ = colourful with flowers; phalavantashcha = and fruits; akāle = even in the off-season; nadyashcha = rivers too; salilāyutāḥ = will remain (constantly) with water.

"O the wielder of a great bow! The trees will look colourful with flowers and fruits even in the off-season. Rivers too will remain constantly with water."

Sloka 17

savraṇaiḥ prathamaṃ gātrairidānīṃ nirvraṇaiḥ samaiḥ |
tataḥ samutthitāḥ sarve suptveva hariyūthapāḥ || 6-120-17
babhūvurvānarāḥ sarve kiṃ nvetaditi vismitāḥ |

17. tataḥ = thereupon; sarve hariyūthapāḥ = all those monkey-warriors; samutthitāḥ = got up; sustvaiva = as of from a sleep; samaiḥ gātraiḥ = with even limbs; idānīm = now; nirvraṇaiḥ = without any wounds; prathamam savraṇaiḥ = which were injured earlier; vānarāḥ = all the mokeys; babhūvuḥ = became; vismitāḥ = surprised; etat kim nu iti = saying to one another; "what (miracle) is this?"

Thereupon, all those monkey-warriors got up, as if from a sleep, with all their limbs completely healed of wounds. All the monkeys felt surprised, saying to one another "What miracle is this?"

Sloka 18

kākutthsaṃ paripūrṇārthaṃ dṛṣṭvā sarve surottamāḥ || 6-120-18
abruvan paramaprītāḥ stutvā rāmaṃ salakṣmaṇam |

18. dṛiṣhṭvā = seeing; kākutthsam = Rama; paripūrṇārtham = whose wish has been fully realised; sarve = all; surottamāḥ = the foremost of celestials; parama prītāḥ = with a great delight; abruvan = spoke; stutvā = praisingly; rāmam = to Rama; sa lakṣhmaṇam = and Lakshmana.

Seeing Rama, whose wish has been fully realised, all the foremost of celestials, with a great delight, spoke praisingly to Rama and Lakshmana:

Sloka 19

gacchāyodhyāmito rājan visarjaya ca vānarān || 6-120-19
maithilīṃ sāntvayasvaināmanuraktāṃ yaśasvinīm |

19. rājan = O monarch!; gachchha = proceed; ayodhyām = to Ayodhya; itaḥ = from here; visarjaya cha = and disband; vānarān = the monkeys; sāutvayasva = reassure; enām yashasvinīm maithilim = this illustrious ṣeetha; anuraktām = who is affectionate towards you.

"O monarch! Proceed to Ayodhya from here and disband the monkeys. Reassure this illustrious and devoted Seetha."

Sloka 20

bhrātaraṃ bharataṃ paśya tvacchokādvratacāriṇam || 6-120-20
śatrughnaṃ ca mahātmānaṃ mātṛiḥ sarvāḥ paraṃtapa |
abhiṣecaya cātmānam paurāngatvā praharṣaya || 6-120-21

20-21. paramtapa = O destroyer of adversaires!; pashya = see; bharatam = Bharata; bhrātaram = your brother; vratachāriṇam = who is practising austerities; tvachchokāt = through frief caused by separation from you; mahātmānam = the great-souled; shatrughnam cha = the ṣhatrughna; sarvāḥ mātṛīḥ = and all your mothers; ātmānam abhiṣhechaya = get yourself consecrated on the throne; gatvā = by going there; praharṣhaya = bring rejoice; paurān = to the citizens.

"O destroyer of adversaries! See Bharata, your brother, who is practising austerities through grief caused by separation from you, the great-souled Shatrughna and all your mohters. Get yourself consecrated on the throne. Bring rejoice to the citizens, by going there."

Sloka 22

evamuktvā sahasrākṣo rāmaṃ saumitriṇā saha |
vimānaiḥ sūryasaṃkāśairyayau hṛṣṭaḥ suraih saha || 6-120-22

22. evam = thus; uktvā = speaking; rāmam saumitreṇa saha = to Rama and Lakshmana; sahasrākṣhaḥ = Indra the thousand-eyed god of celestials; hṛiṣhṭaḥ = having been pleased; yayau = returned (to heaven); suraiḥ saha = with other gods; vimānaiḥ = in their aerial cars; sūrya samkāshaiḥ = shining like the sun.

Thus speaking to Rama and Lakshmana, Idnra the thousand-eyed god of celestials, having beenpleased, returned to heaven with other gods in their aerial cars, shining liek the sun.

Sloka 23

abhivādya ca kākutthsaḥ sarvāṃstāṃstridaśottamān |
lakṣmaṇena saha bhrātrā vāsamājñāpayattadā || 6-120-23

23. tadā = then; kākutthsaḥ = Rama; bhrātrā lakṣhmaṇena saha = along with Lakshmana; his brother; abhivādya = having respectfully saluted; tān sarvān tridashottamān = all those celestials; ājJnāpayat = instructed; vāsam = (all the monkeys) to take rest in their respective places.

having respectfully saluted all those celestials, Rama along with Lakshmana his brother, then instructed all the monkeys to take rest in their respective places.

Sloka 24

tatastu sā lakṣmaṇarānupālitā |
mahācamūrhṛṣṭajanā yaśasvinī |
śriyā jvalantīvirarāja sarvato |
niśā praṇīteva hi śītaraśminā || 6-120-24

24. tataḥ = thereupon; sā yashasvinī = that illustrious; mahāchamūḥ = and mighty army; hṛiṣhṭajanā = of rejoiced troops; tu = (for its part); lakṣhmaṇa rāma pālitā = which was protected by Lakshmana and Rama; jvalantī = and was blazing; shriyā = wth slepndour; sarvataḥ = on all sides; virarāja = shone brightly; nisheva = like the might; praṇītā = illumined; shītarashminā = by the moon.

Thereupon, that illustrious and mighty army of rejoiced troops, for its part, whichw as protected by Lakshmana and Rama, and was blazing with splendour on all sides, shone brightly like the might, illumined by the moon.
