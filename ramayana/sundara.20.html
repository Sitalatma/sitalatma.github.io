Valmiki Ramayana

Sundara kanda

Book V : Sundara Kanda - Book Of Beauty

chapter [Sarga] 20
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

In this chapter Ravana entices Seetha with wealth, gems, sovereignty over all wives of Ravana.


Sloka 1

sa tām parivṛtāṃ dīnāṃ nirānandāṃ tapasvinīm |
sākārairmadhurairvākyairnyadarśayata rāvaṇaḥ || 5-20-1

1. rāvaṇaḥ = Ravana; nyadarshayata = conveyed; sākāraiḥ vākyaiḥ = in significant words; tām = (to) that ṣeetha; parivṛitām = surrounded (by ogresses); dīnām = (who was) wretched; nirranandām = without happiness; tapasvinīm = (and) pitiable.

Ravana conveyed in significant words to that ṣeetha who was surrounded by ogresses, wretched, without happiness and pitiable.

Sloka 2

māṃ dṛṣṭvā nāganāsoru gūhamānā stanodaram |
adarśanamivātmānaṃ bhayānnetuṃ tvamiccasi || 5-20-2

2. nāgavāsoru = (You are) With thighs like the trunk of an elephant; mām dṛishhṭvā = seeing me; guhamānā = covering; stanodaram = (your) breasts and belly; tvam = you; ichchhasīva = desire; netum = obtaining; adarshanam = disappearence; ātmānam = of self; bhayāt = due to fear.

"You are with thighs like the trunk of an elephant. ṣeeing me covering your breasts and belly, you desire obtaining disappearance of self due to fear.

Sloka 3

kāmaye tvām viśālākṣi bahumanyasva māṃ priye |
sarvāṅgaguṇasampanne sarvalokamanohare || 5-20-3

3. vishālakshī = O wide eyed one!; sarvāṇ^ga guṇa sampannā = you are endowed with bodily excellencies; sarva loka manohare = pleasing to all people; tvām kāmaye = I desire you; priye = O lovable one!; mām bahumayasva = respect me.

"O wide eyed one! You are endowed with bodily excellencies, pleasing to all people. I desire you. O lovable one! respect me."

Sloka 4

neha kecinmanuṣyā vā rākṣasāḥ kāmarūpiṇaḥ |
vyapasarpatu te sīte bhayam mattaḥ samutthitam || 5-20-4

4. seethe = O ṣeetha!; iha = here; kechit na = there are no; manushhyāḥ = humans; kāmarūpiṇaḥ rākshasāḥ vā = or ogres with ability to change form; te bhayam = (Let)your fear; samutthitam = caused; mattaḥ = due to me; vyapasarpatu = be removed;

"O ṣeetha! ḥere there are no humans or ogres with ability to change form. Let your fear caused due to me be removed."

Sloka 5

svadharmo rakṣasāṃ bhīru sarvathaiva na saṃśayaḥ |
gamanaṃ vā parastrīṇām haraṇam sampramathya vā || 5-20-5

5. bhīru = O one with fear; gamanam vā = obtaining parastrīṇām = women belonging to others; haraṇam vā = or abducting; saṃpramathya = by force; svadharmaḥ = is the righteous deed; rakshasām = for ogres; sarvathaiva = by all means; saṃshayaḥ na = there is no doubt (in this).

"O one with fear! Obtaining women belonging to others or abducting by force is the righteous deed for ogres by all means. There is no doubt in this."

Sloka 6

evaṃ caitadakāmāṃ tu na tvāṃ sprakṣyāmi maithili |
kāmam kāmaḥ śarīre me yathākāmaṃ prārtatām || 5-20-6

6. maithili = O ṣeetha!; etat evam = It is like this; kāmaḥ = desire; kāmam = very much; yathā kāmam pravartatām = may behave in whatever way it likes; me = in my; sharīre = body; tu = but; na spṛikshyāmi = I will not touch; tvām = you; akāmām = without lust.

"O ṣeetha! It is like this. desire very much may behave in whatever way it likes in my body. But I will not touch you without lust."

Sloka 7

devi neha bhayam kāryam mayi viśvasihi priye |
praṇayasva ca tatvaena maivam bhūḥ śikalālasā || 5-20-7

7. devi = O queen (of my heart)!;iha = in this matter; bhayam na kkaryam = fear is not to be made; priye = O love! vishvasihi = believe; mayi = in me; tattvena = truly; praṇayasva = become with love; mā bhūḥ = do not become; shokalālasā = absorbed in sorrow;evam = like this.

"O queen of my heart! In this matter fear is not to be made. O love! Believe in me. ṭruly become with love. Do not become absorbed in sorrow like this."

Sloka 8

ekaveṇī dharāśayyā dhyānaṃ malinamambaram |
asthāne.apyupavāsaśca naitānyaupayikāni te || 5-20-8

8. ekaveṇī = single plait; dharāshayya = the bed of ground; dhyānam = meditation; malinam ambaram = dirty cloth; asthāne upavāsashcha = fasting at an inappropriate time; etāni = all these; na aupayikāni = are not useful; te = to you.

"ṣingle plait, the bed of ground,mediation,dirty cloth,fasting at an inappropriate time - all these are not useful to you."

Sloka 9

vicitrāṇi ca mālyāni candanānyagarūṇi ca |
vividhāni ca vāsāṃsi dvyānyābharaṇāni ca || 5-20-9
mahārhaṇi ca pānāni śayanānyāsanāni ca |
gītam nṛttaṃ ca vādyaṃ ca labha maṃ prāpya maithili || 5-20-10

9; 10. maithilī = O ṣeetha!; prāpya = getting; mām = me; labha = gain; vichitrāṇi mālyāni = wonderful garlands; chandanāni = sandals; agarūṇi = Agallochums; vividhāni vāsāṃsi cha = a variety of cloths; divyāni ābharaṇāni cha = and best ornaments; mahārhāṇi pānāni = the best drinks; shayanāni = beds; āsanāni cha = and seats; gītam = song; nṛittam cha = and dance; vādyam cha = and musical instrument.

"O ṣeetha! After getting me gain wonderful garlands, sandals, Agallochums, a variety of cloths and best ornaments, the best drinks, beds and seats, song and dance and musical instrument."

Sloka 11

strīratnamasi maivam bhūḥ kuru gātreṣu bhūṣaṇam |
māṃ prāpya hi katham nu syāstvamanarhā suvigrahe || 5-20-11

11. strī ratnma asi = You are a gem among women; mā bhūḥ = do not be; evam = like this; kuru = make; bhūshhaṇam = decoration; ātrshhu = on your limbs; suvigrahe = O one with a good body!; prāpya getting; mām = me; katham nu = how (will you); syyaḥ = become; anarhāḥ = unsuited (for luxuries).

"You are a gem among women. Do not be like this. ṃake decoration on your limbs. O one with a good body! Getting me how will you become unsuited for luxuries?"

Sloka 12

idam te cāru samjātam yauvanam vyativartate |
yadatītaṃ punarnaiti srotaḥ śīghramapāmiva || 5-20-12

12. idam = this; te yauvanum = your youth; chāru saṃjātam = born beautifully; vyativartate = is passing away; yat = Whichever (youth); sīghram = (is) quickly; atītam = passing away; naiti = will not come back; punaḥ = again; apām srotaḥ iva = like the flow of water.

"ṭhis your youth born beautifully is passing away, whichever youth is quickly passing away will not come back again like the flow of water."

Sloka 13

tvāṃ kṛtvoparato manye rūpakartā sa viśvasṛk |
na hi rūpopamā tvanyā tavāsti śubhadarśane || 5-20-13

13. shubha darshane = O one with auspicious appearance!; rūpa kartā = the creator of form; saḥ = that; visvasṛik = creator of the world; Lord Brahma; kṛitvā = made; tvām = you; uparataḥ = and stopped; manye = I think so; anyā = Another; nāsti hi = is indeed not; tava rūpopamā = equal to your appearance.

"O one with auspicious appearance! ṭhe creator of form, that creator of the world, Lord Brahma has made you and stopped - I think thus. There is not another who is equal to your appearance."

Sloka 14

tvām samāasādya vaidehi rūpayauvanaśālinīm |
kaḥ pumānativarteta sākṣādapi pitāmahaḥ || 5-20-14

14. vaidehī = O ṣeetha!; samāsādya = obtaining; tvām = you; rūpayauvanashālinīm = endowed with beauty and youth; kaḥ pumān = which man; sakshāt pitāmahaḥ = (even if he is) Lord Brahma himself; ativarteta = will disregard (you).

"O ṣeetha! Obtaining you endowed with beauty and youth, which man even if he is Lord Brahma himself will disregard (leave) you."

Sloka 15

yadyatpaśyāmi te gātram śītāṃśusadṛśānane |
tasmiṃstasmin pṛdhuśroṇi cakṣurmama nibadhyate || 5-20-15

15. shītāṃshusadṛishhanane = O one with face like that of moon!; pṛithushroṇi = O one with broad hips!; te = your; yadyat = whichever; gātram = limb; pashyāmi = I see; mama = my; chakshuḥ = eye; nibadhyate = is getting tied; tasmin tasmin = in that that (limb).

"O one with face like that of moon! O one with broad hips! Your whichever limb I see, my eye is getting tied in that that limb."

Sloka 16

bhava maithili bhāyā me mohamenam visarjaya |
bahvīnāmuttamastrīṇāmāhṛtānāmitastataḥ || 5-20-16
sarvāpāmeva bhadraṃ te mamāgramahiṣī bhava |

16. maithilī = O ṣeetha!; bhava = become; me = my; bhāryā = wife; visarjaya = leave; evam = this; moham = ignorance; sarvasāmeva = In all; mama = my;bahvīnām = many; uttama strrinām = best women; āhṛitānām = brought; itastataḥ = from here and there; bhava = become; agramahishhī = foremost consort; te bhadram = (let there be) auspicious to you.

"O ṣeetha! Become my wife. Leave this ignorance. In all my many best women brought from here and there, become foremost consort. Let there be auspicious to you."

Sloka 17

lokebho yāni ratnāni sampramathyāhṛtāni vai || 5-20-17
tāni me bhīru sarvāṇi rājyam caitadahaṃ ca te |

17. bhīru = O timid one!; rāni ratnāni = Whatever gems; āhṛitāni vai = (that have been)brought; saṃpramadhya = by force; tāni sarvāṇi = all those; me = my; etat = this; rājyam = kingdom; aham cha = and also myself; te = (belong to) you.

"O timid one! Whatever gems that have been brought by force, all those, my this kingdom and also myself belong to you."

Sloka 18

vijitya pṛthivīṃ sarvāṃ nānānagaramālinīm || 5-20-18
janakāya pradāsyāmi tava hetorvilāsini |

18. vilāsini = O charmful lady!; vijitya = conquering; sarvām pṛithivīm = the entire earth; nānā nagara mālinīm = together with chain of various cities; tava hetoḥ = for your sake; pradāsyāmi = I can give; janakāya = to king Janaka.

"O charmful lady! Conquering the entire earth together with chain of various cities for your sake, I can give to king Janaka.

Sloka 19

neha paśyāmi loke.anyam yo me pratibalo bhavet || 5-20-19
paśya me sumahadvīryamapratidvandvamāhave |

19. iha loke = in this world; na pashyāmi = I do not see; anyam = another one; yaḥ = who; pratibalaḥ = is equally matched (to me); pashya = see; me = my; sumahat = very great; vīryam = power.

"In this world I do not see another one who is equally matched to me. ṣee my very great power."

Sloka 20

asakṛtsamyuge bhagnā mayā vimṛditadhvajāḥ || 5-20-20
aśaktāḥ pratyanīkeṣu sthātuṃ mama surāsurāḥ |

20. surāsurāḥ = ṣuras and Asuras; bhagnāḥ = defeated; vimṛidita dhvajāḥ = with broken flags; mayā = by me; saṃyuge = in war; asakṛit = many times; ashaktāḥ = are powerless; sthātum = to stand; pratyanīkeshhu = (in an army)against met;mama = of me.

"ṣuras and Asuras defeated with broken flags by me in war many times are powerless to stand in an army against me."

Sloka 21

iccha māṃ kriyatāmadya pratikarma tavottamam || 5-20-21
saprabhāṇyavasajyantāṃ tavāṃge bhūṣaṇāni ca |
sādhu paśyāmi te rūpam samyuktaṃ pratikarmaṇā || 5-20-22

21; 22. iccha = desire; mām = me; adya = now; tava = (let) your; uttamam = best; pratikarma = decoration; kriyatām = be made; tava = on your; aṇ^ge = body; bhushhaṇāni = (let) ornaments also; saprabhāṇi = with great brilliance; avasajya = be fit; sādhu pashyāmi = I will see well; tava = your rūpam = form; pratikarmaṇā yuktam = together with decoration.

"Desire me! ṇow let your best decoration be made. On your body let ornaments also with great brilliance be fit. I will see well your form together with decoration."

Sloka 23

pratikarmābhisamyuktā dākṣiṇyena varānane |
bhumkṣva bhogānyathākāmaṃ piba bhīru ramasva ca || 5-20-23

23. varāne = O one with best face!; bhīru = O timid one! dākshiṇyena = with consideration; pratikarmābhi saṃyuktā = together with decoration; bhuṃksva = enjoy; bhogān = luxuries; yathā kāmam = according to your desire; piba = drink; ramasva cha = Rejoice

"O one with best face! O timid one! With consideration enjoy luxuries according to your desire together with decoration. Drink. Rejoice."

Sloka 24

yatheccham ca prayaccha tvaṃ pṛthivīm vā dhanāni ca |
lalasva mayi visrabdhā dhṛṣṭamājñāpayasva ca || 5-20-24

24. tvam = you; prayaccha = donate; priṛthivīm = land; dhanāni cha = and wealth; yathecchham = according to wish; visrabhdā = with confidence; lalasva = behave freely; mayi = in my matter; dhṛishhtam = fearlessly; ājJṇāpayasva cha = order also.

"You donate land and wealth according to your wish. With confidence behave freely in my matter. Fearlessly order also."

Sloka 25

matrpasādāllalantyāśca lalantāṃ bāndhavāstava |
buddhiṃ mamānupaśya tvam śriyam bhadre yaśaśca me || 5-20-25

25. matprasādāt = By my grace; lalantyāḥ = (with your) sporting; tava = your bandhavāḥ = relatives; lalantām = (will also) sport; bhadre = O auspicious one!; tvam = you; anupashya = see; mama = my; buddhim = wealth; me yashashcha = and my fame.

" By my grace with your sporting, tour relatives will also sport. O auspicious one! You see my wealth and my fame."

Sloka 26

kiṃ kariṣyasi rāmeṇa subhage cīravāsasā |
nikṣiptavijayo rāmo gataśrīrvanagocaraḥ || 5-20-26
vratī sthaṇḍilaśāyī ca śaṅke jīvati vā na vā |

26. subhage = O beautiful one!;kim = what; karishhyasi = can you do; rāmeṇa = with Rama; chīra vasasā = clad in tatters; nikshipta vijayaḥ = with lost hope of victory; gatashrī = with gone fortune; vana gocharaḥ = roaming in forests; vratī = observing austerities; sthaṇDilashayī cha = and also sleeping on floor; shaṇ^ke = I doubt; rāmaḥ = (if) Rama; jīvati = (is) alive or; na vā = not.

"O beautiful one! What can you do with Rama clad in tatters, with lost hope of victory, with gone fortune. Roaming in forests, observing austerities and also sleeping on floor, I doubt if Rama is alive or not."

Sloka 27

na hi vaidehi rāṃstvāṃ draṣṭuṃ vā pyupalassyate || 5-20-27
purobalākairasitairme ghorjyotsnāmivāvṛtām |

27. vaidehī = O ṣeetha!; rāmaḥ = Rama; na hi upalapsyate = indeed does not get opportunity; drashhṭum vāpi tvām = even to see you; jyotsnāmiva = like moonlight; āvṛitām = covered; asitaiḥ meghaiḥ = by dark clouds; purobalākaiḥ = with cranes at the front.

"O ṣeetha, Rama indeed does not get opportunity even to see you like moonlight covered by dark clous with cranes at the front."

Sloka 28

na cāpi mama hastāttvāṃ prāptumarhati rāghavaḥ || 5-20-28
hiraṇyakaśipuḥ kīrtimindrahastagatāmiva |

28. kīrtimiva = like keerthi; hiraṇyakashipuḥ = (got by) ḥiranyakasipu; indrahastagatām = from Indra's hand; rāghavaḥ = Rama; na chāpi arhati = is not suited; prāptum = to get; tvām = you; mama hastāt = from my hand.

"Like Keerthi got by ḥiranyakasipu from Indra's hand, Rama is not suited to get you from my hands."

Sloka 29

cārusmite cārudati cārunetre vilāsini || 5-20-29
mano harasi me bhīru suparṇaḥ panngaṃ yathā |

29. chārusmite = O One with a beautiful smile!; chārudati = O one with beautiful teeth!; chārunetre = O one with beautiful eyes!; vilāsinī = O charmful one!; bhīru = O timid one!; harasi = you are stealing; me manaḥ = my heart; pannagaṃ yathā = like a serpent; suparṇaḥ = (stolen) by Garuda.

"O one with a beautiful smile! O one with beautiful teeth!O one with beautiful eyes! O charmful one! O timid one! You are stealing my heart like a serpent stolen by Garuda.

Sloka 30

kliṣṭakauśeyavasanām tanvīmapyanalamkṛtām || 5-20-30
tvāṃ dṛṣṭvā sveṣu dāreṣu ratiṃ nopalabhāmyaham |

30. kliṣṭa kausheya vasanām = (You are) wearing a silk cloth in a bad condition; tanvīm = (you are) emaciated; analaṃkṛitāmapi = Even though you have not decorated; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; tvām = you; aham = I; nopalabhāmi = am not getting; ratim = happiness; sveshhu dāreshhu = in my wives.

"You are Wearing a silk cloth in a bad condition. You are emaciated. Even though you have not decorated, seeing you I am not getting happiness in my wives."

Sloka 31

antḥ puravināsinyaḥ striyaḥ sarvaguṇānvitāḥ || 5-20-31
yāvantyo mama sarvāsāmaiśvaram kuru jānaki |

31. jānaki = O ṣeetha!; yāvantyaḥ = whovever; striyaḥ = women; mama antaḥpura nivāsinyaḥ = living in my gynaeceum; sarvaguṇānvitāḥ = endowed with all virtues; kuru = have; aishvaryam = sovereignty; sarvāsām = on all of them.

"O ṣeetha! whoever women living in gynaeceum endowed with all virtues, have sovereignty on all of them."

Sloka 32

mama hyasitakeśānte trailokyapravarāḥ striyaḥ || 5-20-32
tāstvāṃ paricariṣyanti śriyamapsaraso yathā |

32. asitakeshhante = O one with black hair! mama = my; tāḥ = those; striyaḥ = women; apsarasaḥ = apsarasas; trailokyapravarāḥ = best among the three worlds; paricharishhyanti = will serve; tvām = you; shriyam yathā = like the goddess of wealth.

"O one with black hair! ṃy those women and apsarasas who are the best among the three worlds will serve you like the goddess of wealth."

Sloka 33

yāni vaiśravaṇe subhru ratnāni ca dhanāni ca || 5-20-33
tāni lokāṃśca suśroṇi māṃ ca bhumkṣva yathāsukham |

33. sushroṇi = O one with beautiful hips!; subhru = O one with beautiful eyebrows; bhunkshva = enjoy; yathāsukham = according to your comfort; yāni ratnāni = Whatever gems; dhanāni cha = and wealth; vaishravaṇe = belonging to Kubera; tāni = all those; lokāṃshcha = three worlds; māṃ cha = and me;

"O one with beautiful hips! O one with beuatiful eyebrows! Enjoy according to your comfort whatever gems and wealth belonging to Kubera, all those three worlds and me."

Sloka 34

na rāmastapasā devi na balena vikramaiḥ || 5-20-34
na dhanena mayā tulyastejasā yaśasāpi vā |

34. devī = O ṣeetha!; rāmaḥ = Rama na tulyaḥ = is not equal; mayā = to me; tapasā = by austerity; na = not (equal); balaena cha = by might; na = not (equal); vikramaiḥ = by strength;na = not (equal) dhanena = by wealth; tejasā = by brilliance; yashsāpi cha = and by fame.

"O ṣeetha! Rama is not equal to me by austerity, not equal by might; not equal by strength, not equal by wealth, by brilliance and by fame."

Sloka 35

piba vihara ramasva bhuṃkṣva bhogān |
dhananicauam pradiśāmi medinīm ca |
mayi lala lalane yathāsukham tvam |
tvayi ca sametya lalantu bāndhavvaste || 5-20-35

35. lalane = O playful one!; pradishāmi = I will give; dhananichayam = hordes of wealth; medinīm cha = and land; tvam = you; piba = drink; vihara = roam freely; ramasva = and play; bhunkshva = enjoy; bhogān = luxuries; lala = sport; yathāsukham = = according to comfort; mayi = in me; te bāndhavāḥ = your relatives; lalantu = may sport; sametya = together; tvayi = in you.

"O playful one! I will give hordes of wealth and land. You drink. Roam freely and play. Enjoy luxuries. Sport according to comfort in me. Your relatives may sport together in you."

Sloka 36

kanakavimalahārabhūṣitāṅgī |
vihara mayā saha bhīru kānanāni || 5-20-36

36. bhīru = O timid one!; kanakavimalahārabhūshhināṇ^gī = with body decorated by gold colored; pure garlands; vihāra = roam; mayā saha = with me; kānanāni = in forests; samudratīrajāni = born at sea shore;kusumita taru jāla saṃtatāni = filled with groups of flowered trees; bhramara yutāni = with humble bees

"O timid one! With body decorated with gold colored, pure garlands, roam with me in forests born at sea shore filled with groups of flowered trees and with humble bees."

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye sundarakāṇḍe vimṣaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 20th chapter of Sundara Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
