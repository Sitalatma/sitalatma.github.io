Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda

Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter[Sarga] 57
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Sumantra takes leave from Guha, who learns from his spies that Rama departed for chitrakuta. Sumantra drives back to Ayodhya. Entering the royal gynaeceum, he submits to the emperor what he earlier reported to the citizens who followed his chariot. Dasaratha and Kausalya fall into a faint to hear about Rama's departure for chitrakuta. All the inmates of gynaeceum weep loudly in anguish.

Sloka 1

kathayitvā suduhkha ārtaḥ sumantreṇa ciram saha |
rāme dakṣiṇa kūlasthe jagāma sva gṛham guhaḥ || 2-57-1

1. rāme = (when) Rama; dakshhiṇa kūlasthe = landed upon the southern shore; guhaḥ = Guha; kathayitvā = talked; chiram = for a long time; sumantreṇa saha = with ṣumantra; suduḥkhārtaḥ = and with much distress; jagāma = went; svagṛiham = to his house.

While Rama landed upon the southern shore, Guha conversed for a long time with Sumantra and with much distress, went to his house.

Sloka 2

bharadvājābhigamanam prayāge ca sahāsanam |
āgirergamanam teṣām tatrasthairabhilakṣitam || 2-57-2

2. abhilakshhitam = It was gathered; tatrasthaiḥ = by those (Guha and others) who were there; teshhām = about their; bharadvājāgamanam = coming to sage Bharadwaja; sahāsanam = about staying with him; prayāge = in Prayaga; gamanam = and about their going; agire = to chitrakuta mountain.

It was gathered by those who were there (Guha and others) about Seetha, Rama and Lakshmana coming to sage Bharadwaja, their staying with him in Prayaga and their departure to chitrakuta ṃountain.

Sloka 3

anujñātaḥ sumantraḥ atha yojayitvā haya uttamān |
ayodhyām eva nagarīm prayayau gāḍha durmanāḥ || 2-57-3

3. atha = then; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; anuGYātaḥ = allowed to depart by Rama; yojayitvā = tied; haya uttamān = the excellent horses (to the chariot); gāDha durmanāḥ = and with intense anguish; prayayau = rode towards; ayodhyām nagarīm eva = the very city of Ayodhya.

Sumantra, being allowed to depart by Rama, tied excellent horses to the chariot and with intense anguish, rode towards the very city of Ayodhya.

Sloka 4

sa vanāni sugandhīni saritaḥ ca sarāṃsi ca |
paśyann atiyayau śīghram grāmāṇi nagarāṇi ca || 2-57-4

4. saḥ = ḥe; pashyan = seeing; sugandhīni = the sweet smelling vanāni = woods; saritaḥ cha = rivers; sarāmsi cha = lakes; grāmāṇi = villages; nagarāṇi = (and) towns; atiyayau = crossed (them) shīghram = quickly.

Sumantra, seeing the sweet-smelling woods, rivers, lakes, villages and towns on the way, soon crossed them.

Sloka 5

tataḥ sāya ahna samaye tṛtīye ahani sārathiḥ |
ayodhyām samanuprāpya nirānandām dadarśa ha || 2-57-5

5. tataḥ = thereafter; samanuprāpya = reaching; ayodhyām = Ayodhya; sāya ahna samaye = in the evening-time; tṛitīye ahani = of the third day; sārathiḥ = the charioteer; dadarsha ha = saw (it); nirānandām = joyless.

Reaching Ayodhya in the evening time of the third day Sumantra saw that city, joyless.

Sloka 6

sa śūnyām iva nihśabdām dṛṣṭvā parama durmanāḥ |
sumantraḥ cintayām āsa śoka vega samāhataḥ || 2-57-6

6. dṛishhṭvā = beholding; shūnyām iva = Ayodhya as deserted; nishshabdām = in silence; sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; parama durmanāḥ = becoming very much distressed in mind; shoka vega samāhataḥ = and having afflicted with intense anguish; chintayāmāsa = surmised (as follows).

Beholding Ayodhya as if deserted in silence, Sumantra becoming very much distressed in mind and having afflicted with intense anguish, surmised as follows:

Sloka 7

kaccin na sagajā sāśvā sajanā sajana adhipā |
rāma samtāpa duhkhena dagdhā śoka agninā purī || 2-57-7

7. purī kaccin na = was not the city; sagajā = with elephants; sāshvā = with horses; sajanā = with people; sajana adhipā = and with kings; dagdhā = burnt away; rāma samtāpa duḥkhena = by blazing grief towards Rama; shoka agninā = and the fire of sorrow?

"I hope the city of Ayodhya with its elephants, horses, people and the kings was not burnt away by a fire of sorrow and blazing grief towards Rama.

Sloka 8

iti cintā paraḥ sūtaḥ vājibhiḥ śrīghrapātibhiḥ |
nagaradvāramāsādya tvaritaḥ praviveśa ha || 2-57-8

8. sūtaḥ = the charioteer; iti = thus; cintā paraḥ = lost in thought; āsādya = reaching; nagaradvāram = the city-gate; shīghrapātibhiḥ = by the fast-running; vājibhiḥ = horses; praviveshaha = entered (the city) tvaritāḥ = quickly.

Sumantra, thus lost in thought, reaching the city-gate through the fast-running horses, entered the city quickly.

Sloka 9

sumantram abhiyāntam tam śataśo atha sahasraśaḥ |
kva rāmaiti pṛccantaḥ sūtam abhyadravan narāḥ || 2-57-9

9. atha = thereafter; (seeing) tam = that; sumantram = ṣumantra; sūtam = the charioteer; abhiyāntam = approaching; shatashaḥ = hundreds; sahasrashaḥ = and thousands; narāḥ = of people; abhyadravan = ran towards him; pṛicchantaḥ = asking; iti = thus; kva = where is; rāmaḥ = Rama?

ṃeanwhile, seeing Sumantra the charioteer approaching, hundreds and thousands of people ran towards him asking, "Where is Rama?"

Sloka 10

teṣām śaśaṃsa gaṅgāyām aham āpṛccya rāghavam |
anujñātaḥ nivṛttaḥ asmi dhārmikeṇa mahātmanā || 2-57-10

10. shashamsa = he replied; teshhām = them; āpṛichchhya = bidding farewell; rāghavam = to Rama; gaṇ^gāyām = (at the shore) of Ganga; anuj~ṇātaḥ = when sent back; dhārmikeṇa = by that virtuous; mahātmanaḥ = and high-souled Rama; aham = I; nivṛittaḥ asmi = returned.

He replied them thus: "Bidding farewell to Rama at the shore of Ganga when sent back by that virtuous and high-souled Rama, I returned here."

Sloka 11

te tīrṇāiti vijñāya bāṣpa pūrṇa mukhā janāḥ |
aho dhig iti niśśvasya hā rāma iti ca cukruśuḥ || 2-57-11

11. vij~ṇāya = knowing; te = (that) ṣeetha Rama and Lakshmana; tīrṇāḥ iti = had crossed (Ganga); janaḥ = the people; bāshhpapūrṇamukhāḥ = with their faces filled with tears; nishshvasya = sighed; iti = thus; aho = Oh; dhik = fie upon us! cukrushuḥ cha = and cried aloud; hā rāma iti = thus = "Alas! Rama!

Hearing that Seetha, Rama and Lakshmana had crossed River Ganga, the people with their faces filled with tears sighed, "Oh, fie upon us! And cried aloud, "Alas, Rama!"

Sloka 12

śuśrāva ca vacaḥ teṣām bṛndam bṛndam ca tiṣṭhatām |
hatāḥ sma khalu ye na iha paśyāmaiti rāghavam || 2-57-12

12. shushrāva cha = (ṣumantra) also heard; vachaḥ = words; teshhām = of those people; tiṣṭhatām = standing; bṛindam bṛidam = in groups; iti = and telling thus; hatāḥ sma khalu = lost indeed are we; ye = who; na pashyāmaḥ = do not see; rāghavam = Rama; iha = here!

Sumantra also heard the words of those people standing in groups and telling, "Lost indeed are we, who do not see Rama here!"

Sloka 13

dāna yajña vivāheṣu samājeṣu mahatsu ca |
na drakṣyāmaḥ punar jātu dhārmikam rāmam antarā || 2-57-13

13. najātu = never; punaḥ = again; drakshhyāmaḥ = we can see; dhārmikam = the pious; rāmam = Rama; antarā = on the occasions of; dāna yaGYa vivāheṣu = bestowing gifts; sacrificial performances and marriages; mahatsu = large; samājeshhu cha = meetings.

"Never again can we see the pious Rama on the occasions of bestowing gifts, sacrificial performances or marriages or in large meetings.

Sloka 14

kim samartham janasya asya kim priyam kim sukha āvaham |
iti rāmeṇa nagaram pitṛvat paripālitam || 2-57-14

14. nagaram = the city of Ayodhya; paripālitam = was protected; rāmeṇa = by Rama; pitṛivat = as by a father; iti = in this manner; kim = what was; samartham = appropriate; asya janasya = to these people; kim = what was; priyam = congenial; kim = what; sukhāvaham = brought happiness.

The city of Ayodhya was protected by Rama, as by a father, with due regard to what was appropriate, what was congenial and what brought happiness to its people.

Sloka 15

vāta ayana gatānām ca strīṇām anvantara āpaṇam |
rāma śoka abhitaptānām śuśrāva paridevanam || 2-57-15

15. anvantara āpaṇam = while driving through bazārs; shushrāva = (ṣumantra) hard; paridevanam = the lamentation; strīṇām = of women; vāta ayana gatānām = coming forth from windows; rāma shoka abhitaptānām = consumed by anguish on account of Rama's exile.

While driving through bazārs, Sumantra heard the sounds of lamentation of women, coming forth from windows, consumed by anguish on account of Rama's exile.

Sloka 16

sa rāja mārga madhyena sumantraḥ pihita ānanaḥ |
yatra rājā daśarathaḥ tat eva upayayau gṛham || 2-57-16

16. saḥ sumantraḥ = ṭhat ṣumantra; pihita ānanaḥ = by covering his face; upayayau = reached; tat = that; gṛiham eva = house; yatra = where; rājā = king; dasharathaḥ = Dasaratha (was there); rāja mārga madhyena = going through the middle of the royal highway.

Going through the middle of the royal highway, Sumantra by covering his face, reached the house of Dasaratha.

Sloka 17

so avatīrya rathāt śīghram rāja veśma praviśya ca |
kakṣyāḥ sapta abhicakrāma mahā jana samākulāḥ || 2-57-17

17. saḥ = ḥe; avatīrya = descended; rathāt = from the chariot; pravishya cha = entered; shīghram = quickly; rāja veshma = the royal palace; abhicakrāma = and traversed; sapta = the seven; kakṣyāḥ = inner apartments of the palace; mahā jana samākulāḥ = filled with a large number of people.

Sumantra descended from the chariot, entered quickly the royal palace and traversed the seven inner apartments of the palace, filled with a large number of people.

Sloka 18

harmyairvimānaiḥ prāsādairavekṣyātha samāgatam |
hāhākārakṛtā nāryo rāmadarśanakarśitāḥ || 2-57-18

18. atha = then; avekshhya = seeing; samāgatam = (ṣumantra) coming; nāryaḥ = the women; harmaiḥ = from large mansions; vimānaiḥ = from seven-storied buildings; prāsādaiḥ = and from royal palaces; rāmadarshanakarshitāḥ = who had been emaciated in not seeing Rama; hāhākārakṛitaḥ = cried "Alas! Alas"

Then, finding the forthcoming Sumantra from their large mansions, from seven-storied buildings and from royal palaces, the women who had been emaciated in not being able to see Rama, cried "Alas! Alas".

Sloka 19

āyatairvimalairnetrairaśruvegapariplutaiḥ |
anyonyamabhivīkṣante.avyaktamārtatarāḥ strīyaḥ || 2-57-19

19. striyaḥ = the women; ārtatarāḥ = extremely confounded with grief; netraiḥ = with eyes; āyataiḥ = bright; ashruvegapariplutaiḥ = filled with a stream of tears; abhivīkshhante = looked at; anyoyam = each other; avyaktam = imperceptibly.

Those women, extremely confounded with grief with their long and bright eyes filled with a stream of tears, looked at each other imperceptibly.

Sloka 20

tataḥ daśaratha strīṇām prāsādebhyaḥ tataḥ tataḥ |
rāma śoka abhitaptānām mandam śuśrāva jalpitam || 2-57-20

20. tataḥ = then; shushrāva = (ṣumantra) heard; mandam = the feeble; jalpitam = conversation; Dasharatha strīṇām = of Dasaratha's wives; rāma shoka abhitaptānam = absorbed of anguish on account of Rama's exile; tataḥ tatatḥ = from their respective; prāsādebhyaḥ = inner apartments.

Sumantra also heard the feeble conversation of Dasaratha's wives absorbed in anguish on account of Rama's exile, from their respective inner apartments.

Sloka 21

saha rāmeṇa niryātaḥ vinā rāmam iha āgataḥ |
sūtaḥ kim nāma kausalyām śocantīm prati vakṣyati || 2-57-21

21. kim nāma = what; sūtaḥ = ṣumantra; prati vakshhyati = will reply; kausalyām = to Kausalya; shocantīm = who is lamenting; niryātaḥ = (when he) went out; rāmeṇa saha = with Rama; āgataḥ = and returned; iha = here; vinā rāmam = without Rama?

"What Sumantra will reply to the lamenting Kausalya, when he now returns here without Rama while he formerly went out with Rama?"

Sloka 22

yathā ca manye durjīvam evam na sukaram dhruvam |
āccidya putre niryāte kausalyā yatra jīvati || 2-57-22

22. yathā = In which manner; kausalyā = Kausalya; yatra jīvati = survives wherever; āchchhidya = in spite of; putre = her son (Rama); niryāte = having left; manye = I think that; dhruvam = surely; na = it is not; sukaram = easy; evam = thus; durjīvitam = a difficult living.

"In spite of her son (Rama) having left Ayodhya, Kausalya continues to survive. I think that surely it is difficult thus to live and it is not so easy to preserve it too!"

Sloka 23

satya rūpam tu tat vākyam rājñaḥ strīṇām niśāmayan |
pradīptam iva śokena viveśa sahasā gṛham || 2-57-23

23. nishāmayan = reaching to; tat vākyam = those words; satya rūpam = so credible; rāGYaḥ = of king Dasaratha's; strīṇām = wives; sahasā = (ṣumantra) quickly; vivesha = entered; gṛiham = the house; pradīptim iva = appeared like burning; shokena = with grief.

Hearing those credible words of Dasaratha's wives, Sumantra all at once entered the house, that appeared to be set ablaze with grief.

Sloka 24

sa praviśya aṣṭamīm kakṣyām rājānam dīnam ātulam |
putra śoka parimlānam apaśyat pāṇḍure gṛhe || 2-57-24

24. saḥ = that ṣumantra; pravishya = entered; aṣṭamīm = the eighth; kakṣyām = inner apartment; apashyat = (and) saw; rājānam = king Dasaratha; dīnām = the miserable; ātulam = the sick; putra shoka parimlānam = and the exhausted man; due to grief for his son; pāṇDure = in that white; gṛihe = house.

Sumantra entered the eighth inner apartment and saw in that white house, King Dasaratha the miserable the sick and the exhausted man on account of the exhausted man on account of the grief for his son.

Sloka 25

abhigamya tam āsīnam nara indram abhivādya ca |
sumantraḥ rāma vacanam yathā uktam pratyavedayat || 2-57-25

25. sumantraḥ = ṣumantra; abhigamya = approached; tam narendram = the distressed king; āsīnam = who was seated; abhivādya cha = offered his salutation; pratyavedayat = and presented; rāmavachanam = Rama's message; yathoktam = as told.

Sumantra approached that king who was seated, offered his salutation and presented Rama's message as told.

Sloka 26

sa tūṣṇīm eva tat śrutvā rājā vibhrānta cetanaḥ |
mūrcitaḥ nyapatat bhūmau rāma śoka abhipīḍitaḥ || 2-57-26

26. saḥ rājā = that king; shrutvā = heard; tūshhṇīm eva = silently; tat = that message of Rama; vibhrānta chetasaḥ = was afflicted by grief for his son; mūrchitaḥ = became fainted; nyapatat = and fell; bhūmau = on the ground.

That king heard silently the message of Rama, was bewildered, afflicted by grief, for his son, became fainted and fell on the ground.

Sloka 27

tataḥ antaḥ puram āviddham mūrcite pṛthivī patau |
uddhṛtya bāhū cukrośa nṛpatau patite kṣitau || 2-57-27

27. tataḥ = then; pṛithivī patau = the king; mūrcchite = having fainted; antaḥpuram = the gynaeceum; aviddham = was hurt; nṛipatau = (while) the king; patite = was falling; kshhitau = on the ground; chukrosha = (the inmates of gynaeceum) wept; uddhṛitya = raising; bāhū = their hands.

The king thus having fainted, the gynaeceum was hurt. While the king was falling on the ground, the inmates of gynaeceum wept, raising their hands in distress.

Sloka 28

sumitrayā tu sahitā kausalyā patitam patim |
utthāpayām āsa tadā vacanam ca idam abravīt || 2-57-28

28. tadā = then; kausalyā = Kausalya; sumitrayā sahitā = along with ṣumitra; utthāpayām āsa = lifted up; patim = their husband; patitam = who fell (on the ground); abravīt ca = and (Kausalya) also spoke; idam = these; vachanam = words.

Kausalya along with Sumitra lifted up their husband, who fell on the ground. Kausalya also spoke these words to Dasaratha.

Sloka 29

imam tasya mahā bhāga dūtam duṣkara kāriṇaḥ |
vana vāsāt anuprāptam kasmān na pratibhāṣase || 2-57-29

29. mahābhāgam = Oh; illustrious king! Kasmāt = why; na pratibhāshhase = are you not speaking; imam = to this; tasya = Rama's dūtam = messenger; anuprāptam = dushhkara kāriṇaḥ = and who has done difficult things?

"Oh, illustrious king! Why are you not speaking to this Rama's messenger who came from the forest and who has done difficult things?"

Sloka 30

adya imam anayam kṛtvā vyapatrapasi rāghava |
uttiṣṭha sukṛtam te astu śoke na syāt sahāyatā || 2-57-30

30. rāghava = Oh; Dasaratha! kṛitvā = after doing; anayam = an evil act; adya = now; vyapatrapasi = you are feeling shameful; uttishhṭha = raise up!; astu = let it be; sukṛitam = a meritorious act; te = for you; sahāyatā = help; na syāt = cannot be obtained; shoke = in grief.

"Oh, Dasaratha! After doing an evil act, you are now feeling shameful. Rise up! Let it be a meritorious act for you. You cannot get a help, when you weep like this."

Sloka 31

deva yasyā bhayāt rāmam na anupṛccasi sārathim |
na iha tiṣṭhati kaikeyī viśrabdham pratibhāṣyatām || 2-57-31

31. deva = Oh; king!; yasyāḥ = on whose; bhayāt; fear; na anupṛicchasi = you are not enquiring; rāmam = about Rama; sārathim = with the; charioteer; kaikeyī = that Kaikeyi; na tishhṭhet = is not; iha = here; pratibhāshhyatām = speak; visrabdham = fearlessly.

"Oh, king! On whose fear, you are not enquiring with Sumantra about the welfare of Rama, that Kaikeyi is not here. Speak fearlessly."

Sloka 32

sā tathā uktvā mahā rājam kausalyā śoka lālasā |
dharaṇyām nipapāta āśu bāṣpa vipluta bhāṣiṇī || 2-57-32

32. sā kausalyā = that Kausalya; bāshhpa vipluta bhāshhiṇī = speaking excitedly in tears; shoka lālasā = and entirely given up to uttered; uktvā = uttered; tathā = thus; mahā rājam = to the monarch; āshu = and soon; nipapāta = fell; dharaṇyām = on the ground.

Kausalya, speaking excitedly in tears and entirely given up to anguish uttered thus to the monarch and soon fell to the ground.

Sloka 33

evam vilapatīm dṛṣṭvā kausalyām patitām bhuvi |
patim ca avekṣya tāḥ sarvāḥ susvaram ruruduḥ striyaḥ || 2-57-33

33. sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ striyaḥ = those woman; ruruduḥ = cried; susvaram = loud voice; dṛishhṭvā = seeing; kausalyām = Kausalya; evam = thus; vilapatīm = weeping; bhuvipatitām = fallen on the ground; avekshhya = and seeing; patim cha = the husband too.

All those women cried in loud voice, seeing Kausalya fallen on the ground weeping as aforesaid and gazing their husband too (lying unconscious).

Sloka 34

tataḥ tam antaḥ pura nādam utthitam |
samīkṣya vṛddhāḥ taruṇāḥ ca mānavāḥ |
striyaḥ ca sarvā ruruduḥ samantataḥ |
puram tadā āsīt punar eva samkulam || 2-57-34

34. tataḥ = thereafter; samīkshya = seeing; tam = that; antaḥ pura nādam = noise in gynaeceum; mānavāḥ = persons; vṛiddhāḥ = aged; taruṇāḥ cha = and young; sarvāḥ = all; striyaḥ cha = the women; samantataḥ = on all sides; ruruduḥ = cried; tadā = then; puram = the city; punar eva = again; āsīt = became; samkulam = agitated.

Hearing that crying sound raised in gynaeceum, all aged and young men and women on all sides gathered around weeping. The city then again became disturbed.

- - -

ityārṣe śrīmadrāmāyaṇe ādikāvye ayodhyākāṇḍe saptapañcaśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 57th chapter of Ayodhya Kanda of the glorious Ramayana of Valmiki, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
