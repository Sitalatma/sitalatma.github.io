Valmiki Ramayana

Ayodhya kanda


Book II : Ayodhya Kanda - Book Of Ayodhya

chapter [Sarga] 38
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

Dasharatha is enraged to see his precious daughter-in-law in rags and takes Kaikeyi to task. Rama seeks blessings of his father for departure and requests him to keep his mother Kausalya, who is aging, comfortable.


Sloka 1

tasyām cīram vasānāyām nāthavatyām anāthavat |
pracukrośa janaḥ sarvo dhig tvām daśaratham tu iti || 2-38-1

1. tasyām = that ṣeetha; nātha vatyām = though protected by her husband; vasānāyām = is wearing; chīram = bark of trees; anāthavat = like a helpless woman; sarvaḥ = all; janaḥ = the people; prachukrosha = loudly cried out; iti = thus; dhik = "Fie; tvām = upon you; dasharatham = dasaratha!"

On seeing Seetha wearing bark of trees like a helpless woman, eventhough protected by her husband all the people there loudly cried out: "Fie upon you, Dasaratha!"

Sloka 2

tena tatra praṇādena duḥkhitassa mahīpatiḥ |
ciccheda jīvite śraddhām dharme yaśasi cātmanaḥ || 2-38-2

2. duḥkhitaḥ = pained; tena praṇādena = by that loud cry; tatra = there; mahīpatiḥ = that king; chichchheda = lost; shraddhām = interest; ātmanaḥ = in his; jīvete = life; dharme = religious merit; yashasi = esteem.

Pained by that loud cry there, King Dasaratha lost interest in his life, religious merit and esteem.

Sloka 3

sa nihśvasya uṣṇam aikṣvākaḥ tām bhāryām idam abravīt |
kaikeyi kuśa cīreṇa na sītā gantum arhati || 2-38-3

3. saḥ = that; aikshhvākaḥ = Dasaratha; niḥshvasya = by sighing; ushhṇam = warmly; abravīt = spoke; bhāryām = to his wife; idam = these words; kaikeyi "Oh; Kaikeyi! sītā = ṣeetha; nārhati = does not deserve; gantum = to go; kushachīreṇa = with a robe made of Kusa grass"

Dasaratha with a warm sigh, spoke to his wife these words. "Oh, Kaikeyi! Seetha does not deserve to go with a robe made of Kusa grass."

Sloka 4

sukumārī ca bālā ca satatam ca sukhocitā |
neyam vanasya yogyeti satyamāha gururmama || 2-38-4

4. mama guruḥ = my preceptor; satyam = truly; aha = says; iti = that; iyam = this ṣeetha; sukumārīcha = who is delicate; bālācha = young and; sukhochita = ever habituated to comforts; nayogyā = is not fit; vanasya = for forest.

"ṃy preceptro truly says that Seetha, who is delicate young and ever habituated to comforts, is not fit for forest-life"

Sloka 5

iyam hi kaśyāpakaroti kiṃci |
ttapasvinī rājavarasya kanyā |
yā cīramāsādya janasya madhye |
sthitā visamjñā śramaṇīva kācit || 2-38-5

5. yā = which ṣeetha; āsadya = by obtaining; chīram = a bark of tree; kāchit shramaṇīva = like a hermitess; sthitā = standing; visaṇJṇa = dumb founded; madhye = in the midst; janasya = of men; iyam = such; tapasvinī = pitiable; rājavarasya kanyā = daughter of Janaka(the jewel of kings); kimchint apakāroti = has done any harm; kasya = to any one?

"has this pitiable daughter of Janaka, the jewel of kings, done any harm to any one that, having obtained a bark of tree she is standing like a hermitess dumbfounded in the midst of men?"

Sloka 6

cīrāṇyasāsyā janakasya kanyā |
neyam pratijñā mama dattapūrvā |
yathāsukham gacchatu rājaputrī |
vanam samgrā saha sarvartnaiḥ || 2-38-6

6. janakasya kanyā = (let0 daughter of Janaka(ṣeetha); apāsyā = throw away; chīrāṇi = barks of trees; na = ṇo; iyam = such; pratiJṇā = pledge; datta pūrvā = was given earlier; gachchhatu rājaputrī = let the priness go; vanam = to the forest; yathāsukham = happily; samagrā = fully; sarva ratnaiḥ saha = with all valuable possessions.

"Seetha the daughter of Janaka need not wear these barks of trees. No such pledge was given be me earlier. hence, let this prinecess go to the forest happily fully provided ewith all valuable possesssions."

Sloka 7

ajīvanārheṇa mayā nṛśaṃsā |
kṛtā pratijñā niyamena tāvat |
tvayā hi bālyāt pratipannametat |
tnmām dahed veṇumivātmapuṣpam || 2-38-7

7. nṛishamsā = a cruel; pratiJṇā = pledge; kṛitāvat = has been made; niyamena = on oath; mayā = by me; ajīvanārheṇa = who do not deserve to survive; etat = this(providing robes of hermitess to ṣeetha); pratipannamhi = has been initiated; tvayā = by you; bālyāt = by sheer childishness; tat = that; dahet = will consume; mām = me; ātma pushhpam iva = as by its own flow; veṇum = to a bamboo.

"A Cruel pledge has been made on oath by me, who do not deserve to survive. This (providing robes of hermitess to Seetha) has been initiated by you by sheer childishness. That will consume me, as by its own flower to a bamboo."

Sloka 8

rāmeṇa yadi te pā pe kiṃcitkṛtamaśobhanam |
apakāraḥ ka iha te vaidehyā darśito.adhame || 2-38-8

8. pāpe = Oh; evil women! adhame = the mean woman! ashobhanam kṛitamyadi = (even) supposing an offence is done; kimchit = a little; rāmeṇa = by Rama; te = to you; kaḥ = what; apakāraḥ = harm; darshitaḥ = was displayed; te = to you; iha = here; vaidehyā = by ṣeetha?"

"Oh evil woman! (Even) supposing a little offence is done by Rama to you, what harm was displayed to you here by Seetha? Oh, mean woman!"

Sloka 9

mṛgīvotphullanayanā mṛduśīlā tapasvinī |
apakāram kamiha te karoti janakātmajā || 2-38-9

9. kam apakāram = "What harm; iha = on earth; karoti = can be done; janakātmajā = by daughter of Janada(ṣeetha) utphullanayanā = who has blooming eyes; mṛigīva = like those of a female deer; mṛidu shīlā = soft in disposition; tapasvinī = a practiser of penance?"

"What harm on earth can be done to you by Seetha, who has blooming eyes like those of a female deer, soft in disposition a practiser of penance?"

Sloka 10

nanu paryāptam etat te pāpe rāma vivāsanam |
kim ebhiḥ kṛpaṇaiḥ bhūyaḥ pātakaiḥ api te kṛtaiḥ || 2-38-10

10. pāpe = Oh; the evil woman! rāmavivāsanam = sending Rama to exile; etat = thus; te = for you; paryāptam nanu = is indeed enough; kim = what use; te = for you; bhūyaḥ = further; kṛitaiḥ = in being done; ebhiḥ = these; kṛipaṇaiḥ = wretched; pātakairapi = sinful acts too.

"Oh, the evil woman! Sending Rama to exile thus indeed is enough for you. What use is there for you in further doing these wretched, sinful acts too.

Sloka 11

pratijñātam mayā tāvat tvayoktam devi śṛṇvatā |
rāmam yadabhiṣekāya tvamihāata mabravīḥ || 2-38-11

11. shriṇatā = hearing; uktam = the speech; tvayā = by you; tvam = you; abravīḥ = spoke; rāmam = to Rama; āgatam = who came; iha = here; abhishhekāya = for coronation; tāvat = that much; pratiJṇāt = was acceded; mayā = by me; devi = Oh; queen!

"Hearing the speech you gave to Rama who came here for coronation, only that much was acceded by me, Oh queen!"

Sloka 12

tattvetatsamatikramya nirayam gantumicchasi |
maithilīmapi yā hi tva mīkṣase cīravāsinīm || 2-38-12

12. atikramya = transgressing; tat etat = all that; evam = you; yā = somehow or other; ichchhasi = wish; gantum = to go; nirayam = to hell; īkshhase = by perceiving; maithilīm api = ṣeetha also; chīra vāsinīm = clad in bark of trees.

"Transgressing all that, you somehow or other wish to go to hell, by perceiving Seetha also clad in bark of trees.

Sloka 13

itīva rājā vilapanmahātmā |
śokasya nāntam sa dadarśa kiṃcit |
bhṛśāturatvācca papāta bhūmau |
tenaiva putravyasanena magnaḥ || 2-38-13

13. saḥ rājā = that king; mahātmā = the high-soulded; itīva = thus; vilapan = lamenting; na dadarsha = did not see; kimchit = any; autam = cessation; shokasya = of that sorrow; magnaḥ = drenched; putra vyasnena eva = in excessive devotion to his son; papāte = to fell; bhūmau = on the gound; bhṛishāturatvāchcha = having been hurt very mcuh.

That high-souled king thus lamenting, did not see any ending to that sorrow. Drenched as he was in excessive devotion to his son and having been hurt vey much, he fell down on the ground.

Sloka 14

evam bruvantam pitaram rāmaḥ samprasthitaḥ vanam |
avāk śirasam āsīnam idam vacanam abravīt || 2-38-14

14. rāmaḥ = Rama; samprasthitaḥ = who was setting out; vanam = to forest; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; pitaram = to his father; bruvantam = who was speaking; evam = thus; avākchirasam = bowing down his head; āsīnam = sīting.

Rama, who was setting out to the forest, spoke these words to his father, who was speaking thus bowing down his head, sitting there.

Sloka 15

iyam dhārmika kausalyā mama mātā yaśasvinī |
vṛddhā ca akṣudra śīlā ca na ca tvām deva garhite || 2-38-15

15. dharmika = Oh; virtuous; deva = king! iyam = this; kausalyā = Kausalya = Kausalya; mama mātā = my mother; yashasvinī = the glorious woman; vṛiddhā = is aged; akshhudra shīlācha = not base natured; nacha garhate = will not accuse; tvām = you.

"Oh, virtuous king! This glorious Kausalya, my mother is aged. She is not of base nature and will not accuse you.

Sloka 16

mayā vihīnām varada prapannām śoka sāgaram |
adṛṣṭa pūrva vyasanām bhūyaḥ sammantum arhasi || 2-38-16

16. varada = Oh; the bestower of boons! arhasi = you are worthy; sammantum = to respect(her); bhūyaḥ = much; vihīnam = who is deprived; māa = of me; prapannām = who got; shoka sāgaram = an ocean of sorrow; adṛishhṭa pūrva vyasanām = such an affliction not being seen by her earlier.

"Oh, the bestower of boons! You are worthy of amply respection her, who is deprived of me, who is immersed in an ocean of sorrow and who has not see such an affliction earlier."

Sloka 17

putraśokam yathā narcettvayā pūjyena pūjitā |
mām hi saṃcintayantī sā tvayi jīvet tapasvinī || 2-38-17

17. pūjitā = being honoured; tvayā = by you; pūjyena = the venerable man; sā = she; tapasvinī = the pitiable woman; naṛichchhet = will not obtain; yathā = such; putra shokam = grief for her son; samchintayantī = thinking; māmhi = of me alone; jīvet = will have life; tvayi = in you.

"Having been honoured by you the venerable man, she the pitiable woman will not get such a grief for her son, thinking of me alone and she will draw breath in you."

Sloka 18

imām mahā indra upama jāta garbhinīm |
tathā vidhātum janamīm mama arhasi |
yathā vanasthe mayi śoka karśitā |
na jīvitam nyasya yama kṣayam vrajet || 2-38-18

18. mahendropama = Oh; king equal to the great Indra the ruler of gods! arhasi = you are worthy; vidhātum = to destine; imām = my mother; jāta gardhinīm = who has high affection towards her son; yathā = in such a manner; tathā = that; na vrajet = she will not go; yamakshhayam = to the house of yama the God of Death; syasya = by abandoning; jīvitam = her life; shoka karshitā = emaciated by grief; mayi vanasthe = while I am in the forest.

"Oh, the king akim to the mighty Indra the ruler of gods! You must see that my mother, who has high affection towards her son, will not depart to the house of Yama the God of Death, by abandoning her life being emaciated by grief after my departure for the forest."

- - -

iti vālmīki rāmāyaṇe ādi kāvye ayodhya kāṇḍe aṣṭā triṃśaḥ sargaḥ

Thus completes 38th chapter of ayodhya Kanda in glorious Valmiki Ramayana, the work of a sage and the oldest epic.
