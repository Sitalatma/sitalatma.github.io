Valmiki Ramayana

Yuddha kanda

Book VI : Yuddha Kanda - Book Of War

chapter [Sarga] 74
Verses converted to UṭF-8, Nov 09

Introduction

After having been struck by Brahma's missile released by Indrajit, Rama and Lakshmana became unconscious. Sixty-seven crores of monkeys fainted. Hanuma and Vibhishana, however in consciousness, roared about in the battle-front with torches in their hands, making a search of Jambavan. They find Jambavan. Jambavan requests Hanuma to proceed to Himalayas, by crossing over the ocean and to bring four important herbs, viz. ṃrita Sanjivani, Vishalya karani, Suvarna karani and Sandhani; so as to bring succour to the fainted army of monkeys. Hanuma begins his journey to Himalayas and reaches ṃount Rishabha, where the wonderful herbs exist. Hanuma searches for the four wonderful herbs. But the herbs appear invisible for him. Hanuma uproots the mountain clothed with the four celestial herbs and carries it along with him. Inhaling the odour of those herbs, Rama, Lakshmana and all the monkeys regain their normal health.

Sloka 1

tayostadā sāditayo raṇāgre |
mumoha sainyaṃ hariyūthapānām |
sugrīvanīlāṅgadajāmbavanto |
na cāpi kiṃ citpratipedire te || 6-74-1

1. raṇāgre = In the battle-front; tadā = then; tayoḥ = (while) Rama and Lakshmana; sāditayoḥ = were knocked down senseless; sainyam = the army; hariyūthapānām = of monkey-leaders; mumoha = fainted; te = those; sugrīvanīlāṇ^gadajāmbavanto api = ṣugreeva; ṇila; Angada and Jambavan too; napratipedire = could not find out; kimchit = even a little (of what to do).

Then, in the battle-front, Rama and Lakshmana were knocked down senseless. The army of monkey-leaders fainted. Sugreeva, Nila, Angada and Jambavan could not understand what to do.

Sloka 2

tato viṣaṇṇaṃ samavekṣya sainyaṃ |
vibhīṣaṇo buddhimatāṃ variṣṭhaḥ |
uvāca śākhāmṛgarājavīrān |
nāśvāsayannapratimairvacobhiḥ || 6-74-2

2. samavekṣhya = seeing; sarvam = the entire army; viṣhaṇṇam = looking worried; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; variṣhṭhaḥ = the best; buddhimatām = among the intelligent; tataḥ = then; uvācha = spoke; āshvāsayan = consoling; shākhā mṛiga rājavīrān = the warriors of ṣugreeva the lord of monkeys; apratimaiḥ = matchless; vachobhiḥ = words.

Seeing the entire army looking worried, Vibhishana, the best among the intelligent ones, spoke the following matchless words, consoling the warriors of Sugreeva, the lord of monkeys:

Sloka 3

mā bhaiṣṭa nāstyatra viṣādakālo |
yadāryaputrāvavaśau viṣaṇṇau |
svayambhuvo vākyamathodvahantau |
yatsāditāvindrajidastrajālaiḥ || 6-74-3

3. udvahantau = honouring; vākyam = the spell; svayambhuvaḥ = sacred of Brahma the creator; āryaputrau = the two sons of venerable Dasaratha; yat = for which reason; sāditau = they were knocked down; astrajālaiḥ = by a multitude of missiles; indrajitā = of Indrajit; yat = and for which reason; avashau = losing their free-will; viṣhaṇṇau = and becoming dejected; mā bhiṣhṭa = do not be afraid; nāsti = there is no; viṣhādakāla = time for despondency; atra = here.

"Honouring the spell sacred of Brahma the creator, the two sons of the venerable Dasaratha have actually lost their free will and became dejected and have allowed themselves to be knocked down by the missiles of Indrajit. Hence do not be afraid. There is no occasion for despondency now."

Sloka 4

tasmai tu dattaṃ paramāstrametat |
tsvayambhuvā brāhmamamoghavegam |
tanmānayantau yadi rājaputrau |
nipātitau ko atra viṣādakālaḥ || 6-74-4

4. etat = this; brāhmam paramāstram = excellent missile presided over by Brahma the creator amoghavīryam = with never-failing strength; dattam = was given; svayambhuvā = by Brahma; tasmai = to Indrajit; mānayantau = honouring; tat = it; rajaputrau = the two princes; nipātitau = were made to fall down; yudhi = in battle; kaḥ = what is the use of; viṣhāda kālaḥ = a time for despondency; atra = here.

"This excellent missile, presided over by Brahma the creator, with never-failing strength, was given to Indrajit by Brahma. Honouring it, Rama and Lakshmana the princes fell down in battle. This not a time for despondency here."

Sloka 5

brāhmamastraṃ tadā dhīmānmānayitvā tu mārutiḥ |
vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā hanūmāṃstamathābravīt || 6-74-5

5. tataḥ = then; hanumān = ḥanuma; dhīmān = the intelligent one; mārutiḥ = and the son of wind-god; mānayitvā = honouring; brāhmam astram = the missile of Brahma; shrutvā = and hearing; vibhīṣhaṇa vachaḥ = the words of Vibhishana; abravīt = spoke; idam = these words.

Then, the intelligent Hanuma, the son of wind-god, paying honour to the missile presided over by Brahma and hearing the words of Vibhishana, spoke the following words:

Sloka 6

asminnihate sainye vānarāṇāṃ tarasvinām |
yo yo dhārayate prāṇāṃstaṃ tamāśvāsayāvahe || 6-74-6

6. āshvāsayāmahe = let us restore to confidence; tam tam = that and that monkey; yaḥ yaḥ = whoever; dhārayate prāṇān = is surviving; astrahate = after having been struck by the missile; asmin sainye = in the army; tarasvinām vānarāṇām = of energetic monkeys.

"Let us restore to confidence whosoever is in this army of energetic monkeys, after having been struck by the missile."

Sloka 7

tāvubhau yugapadvīrau hanūmadrākṣasottamau |
ulkāhastau tadā rātrau raṇaśīrṣe viceratuḥ || 6-74-7

7. tau = those; ubhau = two; vīrau = valiant; hanūmadrākṣhasottamau = ḥanuma and Vibhishana; yugapat = together; tadā = then; rātrau = at that night; ulkāhastau = with torches in their hands; vicheratuḥ = roamed about; raṇashīrṣhe = in the battle-front.

Both those valiant ones, Hanuma and Vibhishana, together at that time of the night, roamed about in the battle-front, with torches in their hands.

Sloka 8

bhinnalāṅgūlahastorupādāṅguli śiro dharaiḥ |
sravadbhiḥ kṣatajaṃ gātraiḥ prasravadbhiḥ samantataḥ || 6-74-8
patitaiḥ parvatākārairvānarairabhisaṅkulām |
śastraiśca patitairdīptairdadṛśāte vasundharām || 6-74-9

8; 9. dadṛishāte = (ḥanuma and Vibhishana) saw; vasumdharām = the earth; abhisamvṛitām = covered; samātataḥ = on all sides; vānaraiḥ = with monkeys; parvatākāraiḥ = of mountainous forms; bhinna patitaiḥ lāṇgūla hastorupādāṇ^gulishirodharaiḥ = fallen with their tails; arms; thighs; feet; fingers and heaps of heads broken; sravadbhiḥ = with oozing; kṣhatajam = blood; gatraiḥ = from their limbs; prasravadbhiḥ = and urine flowing out; dīptaiḥ shastraishcha = as also covered with flaming weapons.

Hanuma and Vibhishana saw the battle-field, covered on all sides, with mountain-sized monkeys fallen with heir tails, arms, thighs, feet, fingers and heaps of heads fractured with blood oozing from their limbs and urine flowing out. The earth was also covered with flaming weapons fallen around.

Sloka 10

sugrīvamaṅgadaṃ nīlaṃ śarabhaṃ gandhamādanam |
gavākṣam ca siṣjeṃṇam ca vegadarśanamāhukam || 6-74-10
maindaṃ nalaṃ jyotimukhaṃ dvividaṃ panasaṃ tathā |
vibhīṣaṇo hanūmāṃśca dadṛśāte hatānraṇe || 6-74-11

10; 11. vibhiṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; hanūmāmcha = and ḥanuma; dadṛishāte = saw; sugrīvam = ṣugreeva; aṇ^gadam = Angada; nīlam = ṇila; sharabham = ṣharabha; gandhamādanam = Gandhamadana; gavākṣham = Gavaksha; suṣheṇam cha = ṣushena; vegadarshinamevacha = Vegadarshi; maindam = ṃainda; nalam = ṇala; jyotimukham = Jyotimukha; vānaram chāpi = and a monkey called; dvividam = Dvivida; hatān = who were struck down; raṇe = in battle.

Vibhishana and Hanuma saw Sugreeva, Angada, Nila, Sharabha, Gandhamadana, Gavaksha, Sushena, Vegadarshi, ṃainda, Nala, Jyotimukha and a monkey called Dvivida, who were struck down on the battle-field.

Sloka 12

saptaṣaṣṭirhatāḥ koṭyo vānarāṇāṃ tarasvinām |
ahnaḥ pañcamaśeṣeṇa vallabhena svayambhuvaḥ || 6-74-12

12. vallabhena = by the cherished missile; svayambhuvaḥ = of Brahma; the self-born creator; sapta ṣhaṣṭiḥ = sixty seven; koṭyaḥ = crores; tarasvinām vānarāṇām = of energetic monkeys; hatāḥ = were struck down; ahnaḥ paṇ^chama sheṣheṇa = in the fifth and last period* of the day.

Sixty seven crores of powerful monkeys were struck down by the cherished missile of Brahma, the self-born creator, in the fifth and last period* of the day.

▼

*The twelve hours of the day were commonly divided into five parts consisting of six Ghatikas (or two hours and twenty four minutes) each. They were known by the names of Prātaḥ (early morning), Sangava (forenoon), ṃadhyāhva (midday), Aparāhna (afternoon) and Sāyāhna (evening).

Sloka 13

sāgaraughanibhaṃ bhīmaṃ dṛṣṭvā bāṇārditaṃ balam |
mārgate jāmbavantaṃ sma hanūmānsavibhīṣaṇaḥ || 6-74-13

13. hanuman = ḥanuma; savibhīṣhaṇaḥ = along with Vibhishana; mārgate = was searching; jāmbavantam = for Jambavan; dṛiṣhṭvā = while looking at bhīmam balam = the terrific army; bāṇārditam = wounded by the arrows; sāgaraughanibham = and appearing like a tide of an ocean.

Hanuma, along with Vibhishana, began to search for Jambavan, while looking at the terrific army (wounded by the arrows) which appeared like the tide of an ocean.

Sloka 14

svabhāvajarayā yuktaṃ vṛddhaṃ śaraśataiś citam |
prajāpatisutaṃ vīraṃ śāmyantamiva pāvakam || 6-74-14
dṛṣṭvā tamupasaṅgamya paulastyo vākyamabravīt |

14. dṛiṣhṭvā = looking at; prajāpati sutam Jambavan; the son of Brahma; yuktam = who was endowed with; svabhāva jarayā = old age by nature; vṛiddham = an elderly person; chitam = looking conspicuous; shara shataiḥ = with hundreds of arrows; vīram = and a valiant with hundreds of arrows; vīram = and a valiant person; pāvakam iva = looking like fire; shyamantam = which was extinguishing; paulastyaḥ = Vibhishana; sama bhisamkramya = approaching abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the him; following words.

Looking at Jambavan, the son of Brahma, who was naturally bequeathed with old age, an elderly person, with his body conspicuous of hundreds of arrows and a valiant person looking like an extinguishing fire, Vibhishana approached him and spoke as follows:

Sloka 15

kaccidāryaśaraistīrṣṇairna prāṇā dhvaṃsitāstava || 6-74-15
vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā jāmbavānṛkṣapuṅgavaḥ |
kṛcchrādabhyudgiranvākyamidaṃ vacanamabravīt || 6-74-16

15; 16. ārya = O venerable one!; tava = your; prāṇāḥ = lives; na dhvamsitāḥ kashchit = are not destroyed whatsoever indeed; tīkṣhṇaiḥ sharaiḥ = by the sharp arrows!; shrutvā = hearing; vibhīṣhaṇa vachaḥ = the words of Vibhishana; jāmbavān = Jambavan; ṛikṣha puṇgavaḥ = the foremost among bears; abravīt = spoke; idam vachanam = these words; abhyudgivan = uttering; vākyam = the words; kṛichchhrāt = with difficulty.

ṣloka 17

nairṛtendramahāvīryasvareṇa tvābhilakṣaye |
viddhagātraḥ śitairbāṇairna tvāṃ paśyāmi cakṣuṣā || 6-74-17

17. nairṛitendra = O leader of the demons; mahāvīrya = with a great prowess!; abhilakṣhaye = I am recognizing; tvā = you; svareṇa = by your voice; na pashyāmi = I cannot see; tvām = you; chakṣhuṣhā = with the eye; viddhagātraḥ = as my limbs wee struck; shitaiḥ = with sharp; bāṇaiḥ = arrows.

"O Vibhishana, the leader of demons, with a great prowess! I am able to recognize you by your voice alone. Pierced with sharp arrows, I am not able to see you with my eyes."

Sloka 18

añjanā suprajā yena mātariśvā ca nairṛta |
hanūmānvānaraśreṣṭhaḥ prāṇāndhārayate kva cit || 6-74-18

18. suvrata = O Vibhishana; of good manners!; hanuman = (Does) ḥanuma; vānara shreṣhṭhaḥ = the foremost among monkeys; yena = because of whom; aṇ^janā = Anjana (his mother); mātarishrācha = and the wind-god; suprajāḥ = are his blessed parents; prāṇāndharayate = holding his lives; kvachit = anywhere?

"O Vibhishana, of good manners! Does Hanuma, the foremost among monkeys, because of whom, Anjana (his mother) and the wind-god are his blessed parents survive, holding his life any where nearby?"

Sloka 19

śrutvā jāmbavato vākyamuvācedaṃ vibhīṣaṇaḥ |
āryaputrāvatikramya kasmātpṛcchasi mārutim || 6-74-19

19. shrutvā = hearing; vākyam = the words; jāmbavataḥ = of Jambavan; vibhīṣhaṇaḥ = Vibhishana; uvācha = spoke; idam = these; vākyam = words; kasmāt = why; pṛichchhasi = do you ask; mārutim = about ḥanuma; atikramya = leaving unnoticed; āryaputrau = the venerable sons of Dasaratha?

Hearing the words of Jambavana, Vibhishana spoke the following words, "why do you enquire about Hanuman, passing over the venerable sons of Dasaratha?"

Sloka 20

naiva rājani sugrīve nāṅgade nāpi rāghave |
ārya sandarśitaḥ sneho yathā vāyusute paraḥ || 6-74-20

20. ārya = O venerable one!; yathā = how; paraḥ = an exceptional; snehaḥ = affection; samdarshitaḥ = is shown; vāyusute = in ḥanuma; naiva = (the same) is neither shown; sugrīve = in ṣugreeva; rājam = the king; na aṇ^gade = nor in Angada; na rāghave.api = nor even in Rama.

"O venerable one! The exceptional affection, you are showing towards Hanuma, is not being shown towards Sugreeva or Angada or even Rama."

Sloka 21

vibhīṣaṇavacaḥ śrutvā jāmbavānvākyamabravīt |
śṛṇu nairṛtaśārdūla yasmātpṛcchāmi mārutim || 6-74-21

21. shrutvā = hearing; vibhīṣhaṇa vachaḥ = the words of Vibhishana; jāmbavān = Jambavan; abravīt = spoke; vākyam = the following words; nairṛita shārdūla = O the foremost among demons; shṛiṇu = hear; yasmāt = why; pṛichchhāmi = I am asking; mārutim = about ḥanuma.

Hearing the words of Vibhishana, Jambavan replied as follows: "O the foremost among demons! Listen to me, why I am enquiring about Hanuma."

Sloka 22

asmiñjīvati vīre tu hatamapyahataṃ balam |
hanūmatyujjhitaprāṇe jīvanto.api mṛtā vayam || 6-74-22

22. asmin vīre = If this brave ḥanuma; jīvati = is surviving; balam = the (whole) army; hatamapi = even if killed; ahatam = is unhurt; hanūmati = If ḥanuma; ujghitaprāṇe = has given up his life; vayam = we; mṛitāḥ = are dead; jīvantaḥ api = even though living.

"If that brave Hanuma is surviving, the whole army even if killed, is unhurt. On the other hand, if Hanuma has given up is life, we are all dead, even though living."

Sloka 23

dhriyate mārutistāta mārutapratimo yadi |
vaiśvānarasamo vīrye jīvitāśā tato bhavet || 6-74-23

23. tāta = O dear child!; mārutiḥ yadi = ḥanuma; māruta pratimaḥ = equal to the wind; vaishvānara samaḥ = and equal to the fire; vīrye = in prowess; dharate = survives; tataḥ = then; jīvitāshe bhavet = there is a hope to live (for us).

"O dear child! If Hanuma, who is equal to the wind and the fire in prowess, survives; then there is a hope for all of us to survive.'

Sloka 24

tato vṛddhamupāgamya niyamenābhyavādayat |
gṛhya jāmbavataḥ pādau hanūmānmārutātmajaḥ || 6-74-24

24. tataḥ = thereafter; hanuman = ḥanuma; mārutātmajaḥ = the son of wind-god; upāgamya = approaching; ṇṛiddham = the elderly Jambavan; abhyavādayat = offered his salutation; gṛihya pāde = by grasping the feet; jāmbavataḥ = of Jambavan; vinayāt = with modesty.

Thereafter, Hanuma the son of wind-god approaching the elderly Jambavan, offered his salutation, by grasping the feet of Jambavan with modesty.

Sloka 25

śrutvā hanumato vākyaṃ tathāpi vyathitendriyaḥ |
punarjātamivātmānaṃ sa mene ṛkṣapuṅgavaḥ || 6-74-25

25. shrutvā = hearing; vākyam = the words; hanumataḥ = of ḥanuman; plavagottamaḥ = Jambavan; the foremost among monkeys; vivyathitendriyaḥ = with perturbed organs of senses; manyate = considered; jātamiva = to have born; ātmānam = himself; punaḥ = again.

Hearing the voice of Hanuma, Jambavan, the foremost among monkeys, with perturbed senses, considered himself as though born again.

Sloka 26

tato.abravīnmahātejā hanūmantaṃ sa jāmbavān |
āgaccha hariśārdūlavānarāṃstrātumarhasi || 6-74-26

26. tataḥ = then; saḥ = that; jāmbavān = Jambavan; mahātejāḥ = of great splendour; abravīt = spoke; hanūmantam = to ḥanuma (as follows); āgachchha = come on; harishārdūla = O foremost of demons!; trātum arhasi = you ought to protect; vānarān = the monkeys.

Thereafter, that Jambavan of great splendour spoke to Hanuma as follows: "Come on, O foremost of monkeys! You ought to protect the lives of monkeys."

Sloka 27

nānyo vikramaparyāptastvameṣāṃ paramaḥ sakhā |
tvatparākramakālo.ayaṃ nānyaṃ paśyāmi kañ cana || 6-74-27

27. tvam = you; paramaḥ = are the great; sakhā = companion; eṣhām = for these monkeys; vikram paryāptaḥ = by your copious prowess; na anyaḥ = there is no other person; ayam = this; tvatparākrama kālaḥ = is the time to show your prowess; na pashyāmi = I do not see; kamchana = any; anyam = other person.

"You are the great companion for these monkeys, by your copious prowess. There is no other person. This is the appropriate time to show your prowess. I do not see any other person to do it."

Sloka 28

ṛkṣavānaravīrāṇāmanīkāni praharṣaya |
viśalyau kuru cāpyetau sāditau rāmalakṣmaṇau || 6-74-28

28. praharṣhaya = cheer up; anīkāni = the armies; ṛikṣhavānara vīrāṇām = of warriors; kuru = make; etau = these; sāditau = tormented; rāma lakṣhmana too; vishalyau = healed of the arrow-wounds.

"cheer up the martial warriors of both monkeys and bears. Heal Rama and Lakshmana from their arrow-wounds."

Sloka 29

gatvā paramamadhvānamuparyupari sāgaram |
himavantaṃ nagaśreṣṭhaṃ hanūmangantumarhasi || 6-74-29

29. hanūmān = O ḥanuma!; arhasi = you ought; gantum = to go; himavantam = to ḥimalaya; nagashreṣhṭham = the foremost of mountains; gatvā = by traveling; uparyupari = higher and higher over; paramam = the large; sāgaram = ocean.

"O Hanuma! You ought to go to Himalaya, the foremost of mountains, by traveling higher and higher over the large ocean."

Sloka 30

tataḥ kāñcanamatyugramṛṣabhaṃ parvatottamam |
kailāsaśikharaṃ cāpi drakṣyasyariniṣūdana || 6-74-30

30. ariniṣhūdana = O annihilator of enemies!; tataḥ = thereupon; drakṣhyasi = you will see; tatra = there; ṛiṣhabham = ṃount Rishabha; parvatottamam = the excellent mountain; kailāsa shikharam = and the peak of Kailasa; kāṇ^chanam = of golden hue; atyugram = and very much powerful.

"O annihilator of enemies! Thereafter, you will see there the ṃount Rishabha, the excellent mountain and the very much powerful peak of Kailasa, with a golden hue."

Sloka 31

tayoḥ śikharayormadhye pradīptamatulaprabham |
sarvauṣadhiyutaṃ vīra drakṣyasyauṣadhiparvatam || 6-74-31

31. vīra = O valiant ḥanuma!; madhye = in the midst; tayoḥ = of those; shikharayoḥ = two peaks; drakṣhyasi = you will see; pradīptam = a blazing; atulaprabham = and unequally brilliant; oṣhadhiparvatam = herbal mountain; sarvauṣhadhiyutam = containing all kinds of herbs.

"O valiant Hanuma! In the midst of these two peaks, you will see a blazing and unequally brilliant herbal mountain, containing all kinds of herbs."

Sloka 32

tasya vānaraśārdūlacatasro mūrdhni sambhavāḥ |
drakṣyasyoṣadhayo dīptā dīpayantyo diśo daśa || 6-74-32

32. vānara shārdūla = O foremost of monkeys!; sambhavāḥ = sprouted; tasya mūrdhni = on the head of that mountain; chatusraḥ = are four; oṣhadhayaḥ = herbs; dīptāḥ = which are blazing; drakṣhyasi = you can see; dīptayantīḥ = them; illuminating; dashaḥ = the ten; dishaḥ = quarters.

"O foremost of monkeys! Sprouted on the head of that mountain, are four blazing herbs. You can see them, illuminating the ten quarters."

Sloka 33

mṛtasañjīvanīṃ caiva viśalyakaraṇīm api |
sauvarṇakaraṇīṃ caiva sandhānīṃ ca mahauṣadhīm || 6-74-33

33. mṛita sanjivanīmchaiva = (you can see) ṃrita sanjīvani (capable of restoring the dead to life) and also; vishalyakaraṇīm api = Vishalyakarani (capable of extracting weapons and healing all wounds inflicted by weapons); suvarṇakaraṇīmchaiva = ṣuvarna karani (restoring the body to its original complexion); samdhānīmcha = and sandhani (capable of joining severed limbs or fractured bones); mahauṣhadhim = the great herb.

"You can see there, ṃrita Sanjivani (capable of restoring the dead to life), Vishalyakarani (capable of extracting weapons and healing all wounds inflicted by weapons), Suvarnakarani (restoring the body to its original complexion) and Sandhani, the great herb (capable of joining severed limbs or fractured bone)."

Sloka 34

tāḥ sarvā hanumangṛhya kṣipramāgantumarhasi |
āśvāsaya harīnprāṇairyojya gandhavahātmajaḥ || 6-74-34

34. hanuman = O ḥanuma!; arhasi = you ought; āgantum = to come; kṣhipram = quickly; gṛihya = by taking; sarvāḥ = all; tāḥ = of them; gandhavahātmaja = O son of wind-god!; āshvāsaya = bring succour; harīn = to the monkeys; yojya prāṇaiḥ = by injecting lives into them.

"O Hanuma! Bring all those herbs quickly. O son of wind-god! Bring succour to the monkeys, by injecting lives into them."

Sloka 35

68. samutpāṭya = having uprooted; tam = that mountain; vitrāsya = frightening; lokān = the worlds; sasurāsurendrān = including the leaders of celestials and demons inhabiting them samstunyamānah = and being praised; anekaiḥ = by many; khacharaiḥ = aerial beings; utpapāta = (ḥanuma) sprang up; kham = into the sky; jagāma vegāt = and proceeded speedily; garuDogravegaḥ = with the terrible speed of Garuda the eagle.

Having uprooted that mountain, frightening the worlds together with the leaders of celestials and demons inhabiting them and being praised by many aerial beings, Hanuma sprang up into the sky and proceeded expeditiously with the terrible speed of Garuda the eagle.

Sloka 69

sa bhāskarādhvānamanuprapannas |
tadbhāskarābhaṃ śikharaṃ pragṛhya |
babhau tadā bhāskarasaṃnikāśo |
raveḥ samīpe pratibhāskarābhaḥ || 6-74-69

69. pragṛihya = grasping; tam shikharam = that mountain-peak; bhāskrābhan = radiant as the sun; saḥ = that ḥanuma; bhāskara samnikāshaḥ = who resembled the sun; anuprasannaḥ = having reached; bhāskarādhvānam = the orbit of the sun; tadā = then; babhau = shone; pratibhāskarābhaḥ = as a second sun; samīpe = in the vicinity; rave = of the sun.

Grasping that mountain-peak, radiant as the sun, that Hanuma who resembled the sun, having reached the orbit of the sun, shone as a second sun (as sun's image) in the vicinity of the sun.

Sloka 70

sa tena śailena bhṛśaṃ rarāja |
śailopamo gandhavahātmajastu |
sahasradhāreṇa sapāvakena |
cakreṇa khe viṣṇurivārpitena || 6-74-70

70. khe = in the sky; tena shailena = with that mountain; saḥ gandhavahātmajaḥ = that ḥanuma the offspring of the wind-god; shailopamaḥ = who resembled mountain himself; rarāja = appeared; bhṛisham = mostly; viṣhṇuriva = like Vishnu; sapāvaken chakreṇa = with his flaming discuss; sahasradhāreṇa = with a thousand edges; arpitena = holding in his hand.

In the sky, with that mountain in his hand, that Hanuma the offspring of the wind-god, who resembled a mountain himself, appeared mostly like Vishnu with his flaming discuss with its thousand edges holding his hand.

Sloka 71

taṃ vānarāḥ prekṣya tadā vineduḥ |
sa tānapi prekṣya mudā nanāda |
teṣāṃ samutkṛṣṭaravaṃ niśamya |
laṅkālayā bhīmataraṃ vineduḥ || 6-74-71

71. tadā = then; prekṣhya = seeing; tam = him; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; vineduḥ = made a noise; prekṣhya = seeing; tān = them; saḥ api = ḥanuma also; nanāda = made a noise; mudā = with delight; nishamya = hearing; teṣhām = their; samutkṛiṣhṭaravam = awfully excessive noise; laṇ^kālayāḥ = the inhabitants of Lanka; vineduḥ = roared; bhīmataram = terribly high.

Then, seeing Hanuma, the monkeys made a noise. Seeing them, Hanuma in turn roared in delight. Hearing their awfully excessive noises the inhabitants of Lanka roared still more terribly.

Sloka 72

tato mahātmā nipapāta tasmiñ |
śailottame vānarasainyamadhye |
haryuttamebhyaḥ śirasābhivādya |
vibhīṣaṇaṃ tatra ca sasvaje saḥ || 6-74-72

72. tataḥ = thereupon; mahātmā = the great souled ḥanuma; nipapāta = descended; vānarasainyamadhye = in the middle of that army of monkeys; shailottame = on that prince of mountains (ṭrikuta); abhivādya = having offered his salutation; shirasā = with his head sent low; haryuttamebhyaḥ = to the illustrious monkeys; tatra = there; saḥ = he; sasvaje cha = embraced; vibhīṣhaṇam = Vibhishana.

Thereupon, the great souled Hanuma descended on that prince of mountains (Trikuta) in the midst of that army of monkeys. Having offered salutation to the illustrious monkeys there with his head bent low, he then embraced Vibhishana

Sloka 73

tāvapyubhau mānuṣarājaputrau |
taṃ gandhamāghrāya mahauṣadhīnām |
babhūvatustatra tadā viśalyā |
uttasthuranye ca haripravīrāḥ || 6-74-73

73. āghrāya = by inhaling; tam gandham = that fragrance; mahauṣhadhīnām = of those great herbs; ubhau = both; tau = those; mānuṣharājaputrau = sons of Dasaratha; tatra tadā = then and there; babhūvatuḥ = became; vishalyau = healed of their wounds; anye = other; haripravīrāshcha = warriors of monkeys; uttasthuḥ = rose up.

By inhaling that fragrance of those great herbs, both Rama and Lakshamana became healed of their wounds then and there. Other monkey-warriors rose up.

Sloka 74

sarve viśalyā virujāḥ kṣaṇena |
haripravīrāśca hatā śca ye syuḥ |
gandhena tāsām pravarauṣadhīnāṃ |
suptā niśānteṣviva saṃprabuddhāḥ || 6-74-74

74. gandhena = by the fragrance; tāsām pravarauṣhadhīnām = of those most powerful herbs; sarve ye syuḥ = all those; hatāḥ = dead; haripravīrāḥ = monkey-warriors; kṣhaṇena = momentarily; vishalyāḥ = were healed of their wounds; virujāḥ = relieved of their pain; samprabuddhāḥ = and fully wake up; nishānteṣhviva = as at the close of night; suptāḥ = those who are asleep.

By the fragrance of those most powerful herbs, all those monkey-warriors who were earlier dead were healed momentarily of their wounds, relieved of their pain, even as those who are asleep fully get up at the close of night.

Sloka 75

yadāprabhṛti laṅkāyāṃ yudhyane harirākṣasāḥ |
tadāprabhṛti mānārthmājñayā rāvaṇasya ca || 6-74-75
ye hanyante raṇe tatra rākṣasāḥ kapikuñjaraiḥ |
hatā hatāstu kṣipyante sarva eva tu sāgare || 6-74-76

75; 76. yathā prabhṛiti = from the day; harirākṣhasāḥ = the monkeys and demons; yudhyanti = began to fight; laṇ^kāyām = in Lanka; tadā prabhṛiti = from that day onwards; āJṇayā = under the orders; rāvaṇasya = of Ravana; ye rākṣhasāḥ sarve evatu = all those demons; hanyante = who were being killed; kapikuṇ^jaraiḥ = by the foremost of monkeys; kṣhipyante = were being thrown away; sāgare = in the ocean; hatāḥ hatāḥ = as and when killed; mānārthe = merely for the sake of honour.

From the day the monkeys and demons began to fight in Lanka, from that day onwards, under the orders of Ravana, all those demons, who were being killed by the foremost of monkeys were being thrown away into the sea as and when they were killed, merely or the sake of honour (so that their number may not be known to the monkeys).

Sloka 77

tato harirgandhavahātmajastu |
tamoṣadhīśailamudagravīryaḥ |
nināya vegāddhimavantameva
punaśca rāmeṇa samājagāma ||6-74-77

71. ālambya = taking hold; rākṣhasam balam = of the army of demons; visrastakesha vasanam = whose hair and raiments were kept in order with difficulty; vimukta kavachadhvajam = and whose armours and standards were thrown away; vānarāḥ = the monkeys; paryavārayan = surrounded them on all sides.

Then, Hanuma the son of wind-god, with a great speed, carried away that mountain of herbs quickly back to the mountains of Himalayas and again joined Rama.
